Suggestions for the Relief Society NOTES TO THE FIELD meeting which is scheduled for the evening of Fast Day, March 7, 1943, have been prepared by the General Board.
These suggestions appear in the January issue of the Relief Society Magazine. New Serial THE problems and experiences of two people with different backgrounds who marry and live on a desert are graphically portrayed in a new serial, "Tomorrow's Cup," by Anna Prince Redd, which will begin in the March issue of the Magazine. 'The struggles and heartaches as well as the joys of the young heroine, are absorbingly revealed as the plot unfolds to its final solution. You will want to read every installment of this serial which has as its background the fabled San Juan country. Mrs. Redd, who has spent many years in this part of Utah, first as a teacher and later as a wife, is well qualified to picture for the reader the beauty and vastness of the region. 'This is the first time Mrs.
Redd has been represented in the Magazine in the field of fiction although her poems, articles of prose and a one-act play are already familiar to our readers. Acknowledgment HE General Board wishes to acknowledge the beautiful and colorful Christmas and New Year greeting cards which were received from many of the stakes and missions of the Church.
