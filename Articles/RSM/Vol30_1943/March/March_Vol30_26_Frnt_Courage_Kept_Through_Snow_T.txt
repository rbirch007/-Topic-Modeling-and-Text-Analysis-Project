Alice Money Bailey
Miracle!—that Tulips flood these hours
With joy.
Last night insidious cold crept south
With icy spears to stab the hearts of flowers;
Terror walked the earth to numb the mouth
Of spring.
But firm this small battalion stood.
Too long they waited in the dark for breath
Of life and blossoming, to taste the good
Of earth, of ultimate—to yield to death
Or fear. How could these valiant pygmies know
Such giant strength:
tenacity to form
A patterned life, and courage, kept through snow.
For flaunting scarlet banners after storm!
