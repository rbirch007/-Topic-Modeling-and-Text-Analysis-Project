[Vol30 | January 1943 | Sequence: 25 | Category: Poem | Title: Mabel]

I Light
Mabel /ones Gahhott
How beautiful this twilight hour! The day
Is done. In roseate blue and gold and pink
Apollo's fiery chargers seem to sink
Beyond our small world's rim and speed away.
Then softly, silently night closes in.
The stilled sweet hush of ending day is near;
Warm lights in little windows now appear,
Dispelling shadows, 'circling joy within.
And yet beyond the tinted line of sky
And earth, beyond the sunset's afterglow,
Great war clouds gather, flinging wild and high.
Their dark and somber silhouettes of woe.
Our dusk
falls peacefully and calm
this ni^ht;
God grant our sunrise may be clear and bright.
