[Vol30 | January 1943 | Sequence: 23 | Category: Article | Title: Beyond This Night Linnie Gabbott]

In roseate blue and gold and pink Apollo's fiery chargers seem to sink Beyond our small world's rim and speed away. Then softly, silently night closes in, The stilled sweet hush of ending day is near; Warm lights in little windows now appear, Dispelling shadows, 'circling joy within. And yet beyond the tinted line of sky And earth, beyond the sunset's afterglow, Great war clouds gather, flinging wild and high, Their dark and somber silhouettes of woe. Our dusk falls peacefully and calm this night; God grant our sunrise may be clear and bright.
