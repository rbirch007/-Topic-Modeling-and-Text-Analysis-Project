Though mine may be a craven soul A voice has whispered low to me: "You were not given flesh and blood And life to maim humanity." 74 - JANUARY, RELIEF SOCIETY MAGAZINE ~ADVENTURING By Amy M. Rice I do not live inside four walls; I soar with thoughts of man To peasant cot, or marble halls, Or desert caravan. I loiter in enchanted woods And take my merry way Along with fairies, knights, and kings Through paths of yesterday. I hear the echoes rise and fall On distant snow-capped hills, And pause beside a castle wall To live a thousand thrills.
When tired or discouraged, I go to Galilee To hear the gentle voice of Him Who stilled the angry sea.
