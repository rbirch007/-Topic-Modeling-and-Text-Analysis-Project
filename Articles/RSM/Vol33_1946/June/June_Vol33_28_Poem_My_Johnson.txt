[Vol33 | June 1946 | Sequence: 28 | Category: Poem | Title: My]

Where  many  smile  and  few  there  be  who  frown,
No  matter  what  my  misdemeanors  be.
Fm  glad  I  live  within  a  neighborhood,
Wliere  friendly  folk  exact  so  little  test,
Where  slander  falls  away,  and  only  good
Induces  me  to  measure  to  my  best.
Fm  glad  I  live  within  a  family  sphere
Where  folk  forgive  the  all  I  should  have  been,
Where,  when  I  fail,  they  love  me  just  as  dear
And  lift  me  up  to  tr\^  and  trv  again.
Yes,  here  I  am  the  happiest  to  be,
Among  my  own  who  strive  the  most  with  me.
