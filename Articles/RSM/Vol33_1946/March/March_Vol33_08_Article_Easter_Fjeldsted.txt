[Vol33 | March 1946 | Sequence: 08 | Category: Article | Title: Easter Fjeldsted]

Honorable Louis Ludlow of Indiana, from a speech in the House of Representatives, November 12, 1945. EASTER LILY Evelyn Fjelsted We stand in breathless awe before The Easter lily now in bloom; In silence it has long designed To glorify our living room. The bulb, so still, a secret held  A secret of a coming dawn, A proof of life renewed where once Another bloom had come and gone. "They toil not, neither do they spin," And yet a king was not arrayed Like one of these whose royalty Presents a roseate parade.
