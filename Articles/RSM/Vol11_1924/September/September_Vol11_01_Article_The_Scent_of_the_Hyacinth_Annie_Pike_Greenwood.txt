[Vol11 | September 1924 | Sequence: 01 | Category: Article | Title: The Scent of the Hyacinth Annie Pike Greenwood]

Annie Pike Greenwood e dust of the country road lies quiet and dull.

As I watch a spot stir, it waves and rises and whirls; Down the street it goes whirling in ecstatic freedom, Goes whirling and sinking and rising, It is the dust of one who has lain through the year, Yearning and dreaming until it has passioned the strength to be 'up and away. -.. *~ I, too, am dust, I, too, yearn and dream, O happy dust, when shall I rise and away?

Living dust, nothing can rise of itself, I lay here praying, expectant, awaiting "Til the arms of God, thru His breezes, Lifted me up and bade me begone A UTAH MOUNTAIN SCENE
