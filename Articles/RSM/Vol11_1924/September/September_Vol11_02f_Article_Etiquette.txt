ETIQUETTE

Dear Mrs. Manners:

I am planning to give a bridge party for twelve ladies. Will you please tell me how to arrange the tables, what refreshments to serve, and what prizes to give?

MRS. A. B.

Dear Mrs. A. B.:

For twelve ladies you will need three tables. If you have card tables, use them; if not, any small tables will do. Place them so that there is plenty of room around each one for the players to move their chairs back without bumping into anything.

Arrange the tables with four chairs at each. Place the score pads and pencils at each table before the guests arrive. You may use either regular bridge score pads or make your own by folding sheets of paper.

For refreshments, serve something simple that can be eaten easily while playing. Salted nuts, mints, and bonbons are always appropriate. If you wish to serve something more substantial, chicken salad sandwiches cut in small triangles, or small cakes are suitable. Serve coffee, tea, or a cold drink.

As for prizes, it is customary to give a first prize for the highest score and a consolation prize for the lowest. The prizes need not be expensive—some pretty little article for the dressing table, a box of stationery, a book, or something for the home. Wrap the prizes attractively.

Dear Mrs. Manners:

When I am introduced to someone, should I repeat the name?

MISS C. D.

Dear Miss C. D.:

Yes, it is a good plan to repeat the name when you are introduced. It helps you to remember it, and it shows that you have heard it correctly. For instance, when someone says, "Miss Brown, may I present Miss Smith," you should say, "How do you do, Miss Smith." This is much better than just saying "How do you do" without using the name.
