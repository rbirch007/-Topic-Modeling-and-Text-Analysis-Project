[Vol32 | November 1945 | Sequence: 11 | Category: Article | Title: November Courtney Cottam]

NOVEMBER Courtney Cottam Now, when November's melody is heard Upon the world's great rust and ambered harp.
Lingering as the call of a winter-bird In lonely looning when the winds blow sharp. It plays a threnody among the stalks Of roses, now betrayed by fall's embrace; Then strums a pagan chant on leaf-strewn walks, And hums an age-old hymn through bare twig lace.
Through my wild heart this rhapsody is sung, That throbbing, beats in unison with these. The stubble-pattern of fields; the trees " In filigree against the sky, star-flung. November's melody is bitter-bright. Yet brings a quiet, memoried delight.
