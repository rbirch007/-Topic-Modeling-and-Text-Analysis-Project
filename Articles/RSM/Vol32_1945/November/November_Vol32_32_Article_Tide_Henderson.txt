711 TIDE Myrtle E. Henderson I watch the tide come in on Monterey Bay? The tide come in from the ocean gray; It surges and leaps up over the land, Then slowly recedes with its burden of sand; But the tide leaves the seaweed, the jellyfish, crab-In colors of rose-pink, white, dark green, and drab, And there's beauty in some beyond words to compare In the sea dollar's star and the clamshell rare.
Some are fragments, broken and torn apart.
Mute tokens of beauty in nature's lost art; They are left on the beach where we frolic and play Wlien the tide has gone out on Monterey Bay. Is the tide like our lives from some ocean beyond. Fathomless, deathless, going eternally on.
Each wave a new life as it breaks on the surf And contributes its bit?good or bad?to the earth?
Some lives are mere symbols of what might have been. And others are wrecked by their burden of sin, But some lives there are with a perfect design Like my sea dollar's star, my clam's luster and Tine.
They have given their best, be it great, be it small. And unselfishly served for the good of us all; When their tides go out and their sands slip away.
There is more beauty left than I found in the Bay. Page 712
