[Vol32 | November 1945 | Sequence: 17 | Category: Article | Title: The Star Burns Bowen]
Martha could not look at him, but her mind saw him. Unshaven, blue eyes hollow in a tired face, a crooked grin. "I don't blame you for being sore, Marty, but I've got to tell you how sorry I am for dragging you in on last night. I... [had a fool notion that seeing life in the raw... You might like to live it with me."
Martha pleated the wet cuff of her glove, and was silent. The crooked grin faded a bit. "Won't you say something? I'm a hardy hulk myself. I forget that everybody doesn't dote on the great open spaces where plumbing is something out of a catalogue, and... Well, hang it all, I guess a fellow like Herbert has more to offer a lovely girl like you than a back-country pill-shooter... Here, we're at the toll station. You can call Herbert and..."
THE STAR BURNS BRIGHT
Martha was looking at him now, a bit of the light of stars in her blue eyes. 'Tete-?" the name was natural on her lips?"don't tell me ever again what you have to offer me. It's what I have to offer you, and Wolf Hill, and Lafe Higgins' kids, and the Furnstroms, and... Oh, Pete, what a narrow escape I had.
Just think if you, and that Star, 675 hadn't reached out and grabbed me and set me right?Peter, are you listening?" The words were breathless, almost pleading. A man's two arms about the girl he loves can be definitely more satisfying than any words ever spoken, but Peter risked a few. "Darling, what do you think?" was all he said.
