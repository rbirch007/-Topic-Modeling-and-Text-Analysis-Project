Suddenly she held out her arms to Julia. "Do you think Sally Sue would be happy here with me?" she asked. V.E. Day Observed PRESIDENT TRUMAN told the nation in a broadcast at 9 a.m. E. W. T., Tuesday, May 8, 1945, ?This is a solemn but a glorious hour. General Eisenhower informs me that the forces of Germany have surrendered to the United Nations. The flags of freedom fly over all Europe."
This declaration brought some comfort and hope to the mothers of the United States; however, faced with the rising fury of a continuing ' war against Japan, no mother could fully rejoice in view of the cruel and heartrending days lying ahead. President Truman also set aside Sunday, May 13, as a day of national prayer, and said that it is eminently fitting that the day of prayer should also be Mother's Day. To the liberated countries of Europe and to the United Kingdom where the terrible ravages of war have been experienced, Victory-Europe Day meant a blessed deliverance from the acts of an oppressor; but to the citizens of our nation whose shores have been free from hostilities, V-E Day was only a day of observance to commemorate the end of one theatre of war?only a marker along the path of peace whose'end cannot yet be glimpsed by mortal eyes. In the heavy hearts of Latter-day Saints, too, is the sure knowledge that lasting peace will not abide on the earth until the nations, in reality and not merely in name, become Christian nations? until repentance, entering into the hearts of men, dictates the acts of nations.
