[Vol24 | March 1937 | Sequence: 18 | Category: Article | Title: A Relief Jensen]

A RELIEF SOCIETY WORKER By Nephi Jensen In sun or rain she's just the same; In her simplicity And domesticity She's as real as gold's deepest vein. She flashes no pedantic phrase; In sadness or in mirth, Her words are words of worth; And often raised in finest praise. She has the heart of an unspoiled girl, A mother's tender face And a woman's fine grace; For she lives in her boys and girls. She is neither giddy nor gay; She shuns the pace that kills, Her thrills are the heart thrills; That come along Joy's serving way. She spurns the spot-light's dizzy glare; And the maddening swirl Of life's frenzied whirl; For she lives in the light, ever fair. She never covets the front page; She loves serenity Enjoys sublimity; And just grows young with coming age,
