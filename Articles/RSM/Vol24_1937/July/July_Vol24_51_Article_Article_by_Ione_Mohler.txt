[Vol24 | July 1937 | Sequence: 51 | Category: Article | Title: Article by Ione Mohler]

By Ione Mohler I used to think a quilt Was just a bunch of scraps, Patterned in a casual sort of way, Padded and quilted leisurely, And spread upon a bed. Day after day. I little knew the patience It took to match the blocks, That love and faith and hope Were all required.
That perseverance is a part of art And courage keeps your hands From getting tired. I used to think a quilt Was just a bunch of scraps, And Life is patterned Much the same, Perhaps.
