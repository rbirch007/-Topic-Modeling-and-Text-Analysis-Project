By Annie Wells Cannon JULY—Rich in her summer loveliness Earth gives a clarion call to her festivals. LADY NANCY ASTOR, American born Viscountess and first woman to sit in the British parliament has had conferred upon her by King George VI the Order of the Companions of Honor, for conspicuous service of national importance. QUEEN ELIZABETH is fond of dancing. Her favorite is the waltz which will be most popular during coronation year. AGNES DAVIS, who won the Atwater Kent Radio audition this year, made her debut as Elsa in "Lohengrin" at the Metropolitan last May. ELEANOR ROOSEVELT showed again her philanthropic spirit in suggesting the purchase of a wheel chair for a crippled girl with the money contemplated for flowers for herself. INEZ KNIGHT ALLEN of the Relief Society general board who died last month was a choice and valiant daughter of the Church and State, dearly loved and deeply mourned. WINIFRED HATHAWAY of New York was awarded the 1937
Leslie Dana gold medal for outstanding achievements in the prevention of blindness. NELLIE TAYLOR ROSS presided at the dedication ceremonies of the U. S.
Mint in San Francisco. "Time Marches On" and so does the status of women. LUELLA UDALL, Relief Society president of St. John's stake for 35 years, and first presiding officer over the women in the Arizona Temple died last spring. Her life was full of service and sacrifice. ELEANOR FARJEON'S latest book "Humming Bird" is an imaginative fantasy as quaint as "The Old Curiosity Shop" and as delicate as a Watteau picture.. E. M. DELAFIELD, English novelist in her new book "I Visit the Soviets," maintains her delightful style of story telling in giving the reader a true picture of her Russian travels. She also causes one to feel it is better to read about Russia than live there. WILLIE SNOW ETHRIDGE has published "I Live and Breathe" a charming southern story.
Rich with humor and amusing descriptions. OLGA MORRIS' "Windswept," Edith Roberts' "Candle In The Sun," and Queen Marie's "Marks" are some recent books for vacation reading. EDNA ST. VINCENT MILLAY'S new volume of poems called "Conversations at Midnight" will be a welcome book to the lover of poetry. MARJORIE HILLIS' "An Orchid On Your Budget Or Live Smartly On What You Have" is destined to be as popular as the best seller "Live Alone And Like It" by the same author.
