Abnormal reactions require such insight and sympathy and skill in portrayal that the novice butchers them and makes them ugly or ridiculous or unconvincing. Just one more wordas to length. Use just enough words to tell the story. Start at the initial situation, tight spang-bang in the thing that sets the story going. If it is necessary to tell what happened before this, give it in short "flash-backs" sandwiched in between bits of forward moving action. And stop when the emotional experience has been realized by the characters. Never go on to tell how it affected the next generation or the cousins in Duluth or Timbuctu. And there it isfor what it is worthand may some of those exciting and illuminating stories be written now.
