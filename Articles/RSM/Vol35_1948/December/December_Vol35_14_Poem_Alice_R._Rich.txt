[Vol35 | December 1948 | Sequence: 14 | Category: Article | Title: Alice R. Rich]

WINTER Alice R. Rich North wind, like unleashed fury, comes from bare and sodden hills, And sapless, naked trees and shrubs creak with its biting chills; Gray, lumbering clouds roll ominously, without turmoil or sound, Then loose their mist to spread a skirt of crystals on the ground.
