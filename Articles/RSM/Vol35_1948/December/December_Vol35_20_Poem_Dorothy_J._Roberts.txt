ONE LONE STAR Dorothy ]. Roberts I shall be there; I must return Tonight when one lone star will burn Across the acres of the world. Before its last faint gleam is furled I shall follow the camel's hoof; See winter silver on the roof; And set my heart attune to hear Despite the centuriesone clear Hosanna, filtered though it be, Coming from hilltops far from me. I shall breathe the scent of myrrh Blent by the kneeling worshipper; And behold the jewels spread Beside one on his humble bed. With all the hungering hosts of men, I shall partake of peace again. Before the waning year is spent I shall return for sacrament.
