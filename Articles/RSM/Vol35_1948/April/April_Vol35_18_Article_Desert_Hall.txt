DESERT CACTI, BLOOMING Lydia Hall And there is one who always passes by, As many do, in search of greening fields And buttercups beneath an azure sky; Who thinks he garners nature's richest yields Because he loves a narrow country lane Where lilacs blow; because he lingers where Sweet grasses feel the silver touch of rain And pampered roses freight the heavy air. He'll live his long life through and never know The wonder of this desert's blossoming: "These bright-winged things that are like sunset glow Or crimson flames that waken in the spring, When God, more than in any other land, Flings loveliness across the arid sand.
