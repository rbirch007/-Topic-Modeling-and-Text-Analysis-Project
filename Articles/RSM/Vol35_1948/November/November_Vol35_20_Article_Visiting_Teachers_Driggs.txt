Visiting Teachers MessagesOu" Savior Speaks Lesson 5 Judge Not That Ye Be Not Judged" Elder H. Wayne Driggs For Tuesday, February 1, 1949 Objective: To point out that it is not our place to pass judgment on another. WHEN the Savior gave us the injunction "Judge not, that ye be not judged," he set for his followers a standard which is hard to _ This is because it seems natural for all of us, in our finite state, to see the imperfections of our own. Not once, but many times did the Savior teach those who gathered he gave them Later, to the scribes and the Pharisees he made it clear that even they whose office it was to interpret the law, must not condemn the woman taken in sin, for they themselves "Let him who is without sin, cast the first stone," he said. He tells us to substitute mercy for harsh judgment. Be ye therefore merciful, as your Father also is merciful. Judge not, and ye shall not be judged: condemn not, and ye shall not be condemned: forgive, and ye shall be forgiven (Luke 6:36-37)- Because of our human frailties there will always be flaws in our natures. It is easy to sit in judgment upon another, but it is unjust to do LESSON DEPARTMENT so since we cannot know all the circumstances that may have contributed towards an act. The Savior knew that only God can render a just judgment, for he alone knows the intents of one's heart. Outward appearances and actions may be misunderstood and misconstrued. There is no living man who is all-wise and who can judge the justice of an act from its 775 inception in the mind to its final result. Albeit the Lord has given to some of his servants, including the bishop of the ward, to be common judges, and to such persons so authorized, gives them wisdom and discernment and inspiration sufficient to qualify them to act in this high place. "Judge not, that ye be not judged" (Matthew 7:1).
