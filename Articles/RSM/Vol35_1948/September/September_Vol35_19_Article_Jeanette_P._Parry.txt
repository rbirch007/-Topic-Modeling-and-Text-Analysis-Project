OMNIPOTENCE Jeanette P. Parry I saw the yellow moon above the clouds That climb into the blue of peaceful skies, A silence filled the woods like huddled sheep, Save when the crimson leaves fell down to sleep.
There was no afterglow of summer life, Only the flash of a late robin's breast; I saw the grass, frost-browned at winter's will, The rivulets that trickled cold and still. I was not troubled that skies were leaden And leafless aspens graced the frost-touched hills, A Power greater than our own bestows The miracle of crimson woods and snows.
