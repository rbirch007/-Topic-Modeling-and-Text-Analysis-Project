HAPPENINGS

By Annie Wells Cannon

The Relief Society General Board held its regular monthly meeting on November 5th, with President Louise Y. Robison presiding. All members of the board were present except Sister Amy Brown Lyman, who was in the East attending the National Conference of Social Work.

The board voted to send a telegram of congratulation to President Heber J. Grant on his seventy-fifth birthday, November 22nd.

Sister Clarissa A. Beesley, chairman of the music committee, reported that the Relief Society song books are now ready for distribution. The books contain forty-eight songs, including hymns, part songs, and action songs for children. The price is twenty-five cents per copy.

Sister Belle S. Spafford, chairman of the social service committee, reported that the Relief Society is cooperating with other organizations in the community chest drive.

Sister Amy Brown Lyman, first counselor, who returned from the East, reported on the National Conference of Social Work held in Minneapolis. She said that unemployment was the chief topic of discussion at the conference.

The board voted to continue the policy of having the Relief Society Magazine sent free to all stake Relief Society presidents and to all ward Relief Society presidents who are unable to pay for subscriptions.

Sister Robison announced that the annual Relief Society conference would be held March 31st and April 1st, 1932.

The Christmas number of the Relief Society Magazine will contain a special message from President Louise Y. Robison to all Relief Society sisters throughout the world.
