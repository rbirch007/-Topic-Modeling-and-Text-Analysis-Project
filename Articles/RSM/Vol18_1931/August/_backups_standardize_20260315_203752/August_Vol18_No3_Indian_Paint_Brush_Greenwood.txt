Indian Paint Brush By Annie Pike Greenwood
Seldom in the valley;— You blush upon the hill, And where there is a mountain You climb up higher still.
Some have called you coxcomb After garden things; So they label insects All with fairy wings.
Secretly I name you Inspiration flower: In the common daytime Comes the sacred hour; So your common green leaves Change to glowing red; No longer common verdure, You lift your happy head.
Like your green, my wild flower, Starts the dullest day; Comes my song, and blossoms To red, so glad and gay.
