[Song of the Weathervanes — Second Prize Poem Lael W. Hill]
LAEL  W.  HILL 
Second  Prize  Poem 
Song  of  the    vl/eathervanes 
Lad  W.  Hill 
The  Old  World  steeples  point  their  vanes 
High  above  hamlet,  field,  and  town- 
Does  the  wind  blow  fair  .  .  .  does  the  wind  blow  rains? 
And  the  iron  cocks  look  down. 
Page  12 
The  winds  blow  east,  or  the  winds  blow  west, 
The  plows  begin  and  the  seed  is  flung, 
And  the  storks  find  each  old  chimney-nest .... 
The  cocks  turn;  time  is  young.
