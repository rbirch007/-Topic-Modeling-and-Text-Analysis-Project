Bird,  what  are  you  singing  on  this  petalcd  bough, 
Blooming  between  the  time  of  bud  and  fruit; 
What  is  \our  song,  is  it  a  paean  of  how 
The  seed  was  planted  and  the  tree  took  root; 
Of  how  the  brown  soil  mothered  it;  and  sun, 
And  rain,  like  gentle  fingers,  urged  it  up 
Until  this  bright-leafed  wonder  was  begun— 
An  orehard,  lifting  skies  a  perfumed  cup? 
Bird,  what  are  you  singing  ...  is  it  a  song 
Of  promises  of  beauty  incarnate 
Within  the  smallest  thing  where  hope  is  strong 
Lifting  the  spirit,  long  disconsolate? 
Is  it  to  me  you  sing,  of  life  and  May, 
Upon  this  fragrant,  iridescent  day?
