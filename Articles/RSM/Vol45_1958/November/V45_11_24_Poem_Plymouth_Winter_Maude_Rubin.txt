[Vol45 | November 1958 | Sequence: 24 | Category: Poem | Title: Plymouth Winter | Author: Maude Rubin]
Plymouth Winter Maude Rubin There was no turning back, the ship had sailed. Thought quailed At silent distance, unknown forest aisles— Dark miles Where danger lurked... Yet here they planted corn. Stillborn And unvoiced, every doubt, each brief regret. But still when Maytime came—hedgerows in bloom At home— Old memory whispered, ""Now in England's spring Larks sing!" Though winter's ice had melted, snowing done, They longed for misty hawthorne— But walked forward Into sun!
