r\R.  SARA  JORDAN,  who  at 
seventy-four  begins  her  busy 
days  at  five  o'clock  in  the  morning, 
commenced  the  study  of  medicine 
at  a  few  weeks  under  thirty-three 
and  was  a  specialist  in  gastroenter- 
ology (stomach  and  intestinal  dis- 
orders) ready  to  practice  as  the  third 
member  of  the  beginning  Lahey 
Clinic  when  thirty-nine.  Except 
for  her  first  year,  she  worked  her 
way  through  medical  school,  also 
supporting  a  daughter.  She  became 
the  only  female  president  of  the 
American  Gastroenterological  As- 
sociation, and  she  now  heads  a  staff 
of  ten  doctors  in  her  field  at  Bos- 
ton's vastly  expanded  Lahey  Clinic. 
Dr.  Jordan  says  that  nobody  should 
smoke  cigarettes.  But  smoking  with 
an  ulcer  is  like  pouring  gasoline  on 
a  burning  house.
