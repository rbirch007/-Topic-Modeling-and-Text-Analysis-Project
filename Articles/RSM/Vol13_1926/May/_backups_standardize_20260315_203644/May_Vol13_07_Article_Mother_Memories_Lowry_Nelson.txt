Mother Memories By Lowry Nelson The sprightly years in quick succession pass And leave perceptive marks upon the brow; Though all integral to the Here and Now, They lose their force to follow and harrass. They dim away as fast along we speed, With eyes and minds attuned to things ahead; And pile themselves like wind-rows in the mead Upon the distant skyline of things dead. Dim, save tn sacred cells and, chambers rare, Where all-inclusive memory abides To keep the tapers of the past aflare Against the winnowing of time and tides. In such a well-lit shrine I kneel tonight, With images of her no years can blight.
