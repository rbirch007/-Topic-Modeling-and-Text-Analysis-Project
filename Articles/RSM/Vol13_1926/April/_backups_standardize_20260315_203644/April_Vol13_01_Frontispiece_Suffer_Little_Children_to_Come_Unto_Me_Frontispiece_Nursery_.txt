Nursery Rhymes By Ivy Houtz Woolley
A buttercup sat all alone in the grass, Wishing that something or someone would pass, Along came old Boss on her way out to sup, So she reached out and ate the poor buttercup up.

Gamble little lambs, In sunny grassy places, But lambies do not step into The snowy daisies' faces.

A little rock rolled down the hill, He went to look around, Instead, he rolled into a brook, And there he quickly drowned.

A bantam rooster crowed and crowed, From night until the morn, He said, "I am the finest chick, That to the world was born."
And then he toppled off the fence, And broke his leg, that day. He goes about on crutches now, And says, "Conceit don't pay."
