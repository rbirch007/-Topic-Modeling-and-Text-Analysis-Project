Bright buffalo beans in gay profusion spilling from their arms, They ran to greet their mother. I With worship at her fingertips, she took their proffered blooms, And all the glory that was spring Was captured in their rooms. In June they found the wild rose blooming — A tiny splash of color on the plains, I And clusters shy beside the road. Not minding thorns, their eager fingers plucked, And made a wreath to give her. She smelled the sweetness; put them in her hair, 4 And al! The loveliness of earth Seemed centered there. They told her when the rainbow came, After a fickie spat of rain, One parched and heavy summer day. She stopped her work, and raised her eyes to drink the beauty in, And on her cheeks the soft tears lay. "Sheer gossamer,'' they heard her raptly cry. "How like a bolt of fairy silk Suspended in the sky!"' When autumn came, and leaves ali orange and brown, Hung listlessly upon the trees, They heard the wild geese call. She stood there, with her arms around them alli, as overhead They watched the ordered flight. Her lips were silent, but her trembling arms said more, Shared freedom's wondrous pull, and urged Each sluggish soul to soar. Through ail the months, Through all the years, They brought her gifts. She took them in her ample hold. Sometimes in tears, Sometimes in smiles, And gave them back An hundredfold.
