20 Pine Knots and Irritations Mabel Jones Gabbott The door was made of the forest pine, rough hewn And varnished lightly against the rain and snow; The knots, like jewels in the sun's rich glow, Sang of its reaching like a glad tree tune. I thought of oysters on the inlet shore, Producing pearls from irritations, dust Specks in a foreign field, small hurts that must In healing make jewels in its very core. Look how a cloud can make a golden frieze Of the sun's rays as against a distant blue It splinters the ending day of brilliant life! "A growing, a becoming..."' always these Are jewels. Like the mute sea shells, I, too, Have underestimated change and strife.
