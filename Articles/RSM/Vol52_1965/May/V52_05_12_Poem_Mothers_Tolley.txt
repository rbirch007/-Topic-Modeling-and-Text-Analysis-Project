Mother's Day Patty Linn F. Tolley Sing me no anthems. Bring no fragile flowers. Just put my small one in my arms And let me hold him. 340 @ The old bell in the little church steeple tolled the half hour as Ellen Larkin kissed the youngest of her children and sent her on her way to Sunday School. Little Mary lingered in the doorway. "I wish that you would come to Sunday School with me today, Mama," she coaxed. "I would like to go with you, my darling," Ellen replied wistfully, "but you see I am not' ready, and there is dinner to prepare, and Daddy likes it promptly at noon." Little Mary's eyes opened wide as she answered, "My Sunday School teacher said that we should never work on Sunday, a& because it is God's day. Today it is Mother's Day, and God's day, too.
