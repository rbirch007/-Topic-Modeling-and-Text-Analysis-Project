We are only three miles from a hospital and a super-market. We are less than an hour's drive from the city. Our roads are good, and the mail man delivers our mail each day.

The glamor of the city is ours to enjoy when we wish, and yet we are out under a wide expanse of sky surrounded by stretches of fields and forest-fringed meadows. We enjoy again the drama of the sunrise and the sunset.

I am as filled with wonder as though I had just discovered America.

Of the Heart
Linda Clarke

[PHOTOGRAPH of roses]

A thing of beauty never dies,
For very like a rose,
When tended, watered, cared for,
Beauty blossoms as it grows.

But a rose when left untended,
Will never rise in bloom,
And a thing of beauty surely dies
If lost as in a tomb.

I own a gardenful of flowers,
I have tended every start,
For every bush was once a slip
A gift from some friend's heart.
