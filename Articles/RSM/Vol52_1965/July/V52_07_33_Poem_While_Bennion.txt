While Apart Sylvia Lustig Bennion Reason would console With words: "'Love knoweth no distance,"' It is true. And, a never-shoring ocean, Though tides are felt My love has no boundary From you. Yet, you are gone. The miles, no consequence, For just that I cannot hear Your voice, your song, Nor see your comely countenance, I am gripped with loneliness. And, here ail logic seems ill, For the heart, womb of love, Has no will. And it shall not be satisfied With words oversimplified; It Knows well the distance From you. 557 DOCTRINE AND COVENANTS Sections 88, 89, 90 (Eight Lectures) by ROY W.
