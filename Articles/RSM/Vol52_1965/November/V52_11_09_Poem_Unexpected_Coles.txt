Unexpected Phrase Christie Lund Coles The phrase came to me like a voice, Forgotten, distant; like music played again After long silence; like a rising tide Moving in craggy foam, dripping its rain, Awakening both joy and pain. The words seemed almost entering my flesh And in my blood; became living memories, As I heard the phrase incredibly sweet And was moved to tears by these Few words, "'A grove of trees.'' 818 The Steady dripping from the trees was the only sound that broke the awful stillness. "One night of fury and the dreams of a lifetime vanish in Snow from the blossomed branches. Icicles glistened like sems as the sun Streamed through the open places of 'the trees. "May Day, blossoms and frost," said Randy, Miriam's husband, coming up noisily from the south end of the orchard. "That's a strange combination, Miriam. The lined features of her pretty face, and her head drooped on her shoulders. "A hundred tons of fruit down the drain," continued Randy. Following. She Stopped by the graveled driveway near the house. "And this was to have been the new carport that Abe Steadman was to have started on next week," NOVEMBER 1965 wall. Oh, Randy, why did it have to happen, just when we were out of debt and ready to start living?" She put her head against the old porch railing. The tears came and sobs shook her slight frame. '""Your new truck, a new paint job inside and out, new drapes. Why? Why?" Sue felt Randy's big hand on her shoulder with its firm pressure. The gesture gave her a spark of courage and she raised her troubled eyes to his.
