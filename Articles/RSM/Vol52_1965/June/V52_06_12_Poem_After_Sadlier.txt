[Vol52 | June 1965 | Page 430 | Poem | After Overcast | Beulah Huish Sadlier]
(To be continued) After Overcast Beulah Huish Sadleir ce blue sky and a silvery sun, Shimmering streak on the pale water spun From bright metal threadings of gold filigree, Yet day passes slowly, how long shall I see The small floating sails at leisurely pace? This is soul's timing—God's beauty to trace.
