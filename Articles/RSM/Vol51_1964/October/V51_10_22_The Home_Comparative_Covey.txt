COMPARATIVE BRITISH AND AMERICAN MEASURES

FLOUR

Where American cake recipes ask for a special cake flour, use the best quality white flour available—3½ ozs. for every 4 oz. specified.
Graham flour: 100% wholewheat flour.

LEAVENING AGENTS

When following an American recipe:
Either use 1½ times the amount of baking powder specified in the recipe
Or sprinkle the specified amount over the mixture before the last few strokes of mixing, then bake immediately

SUGARS

In British recipes use castor (granulated) sugar except when another type is specified. The coarse-grained sugar used in most British recipes weighs more by volume than the finer sugar which is common in America.

MIXING

A recipe calling for "two minutes beating" means by electrical mixer. By hand, 150 strokes are the equivalent of one minute in a mixer.

SIZE OF TINS OR "PANS"

These are standardized in America. Those in common use have the following measurements:

Bread tin:—9 in. by 5 in. by 3 in. deep
square pan:—9 in. by 9 in. by 2 in. deep
oblong pan:—12 in. by 9 in. by 2 in. deep
round layer pan:—8 in. by 1¼ in. deep or 9 in. by 2 in. deep
