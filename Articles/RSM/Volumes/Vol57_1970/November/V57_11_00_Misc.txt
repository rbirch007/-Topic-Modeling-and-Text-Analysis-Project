INDWELLER

There is something in me

That answers your touch upon

The door, an extension of myself

Hurrying to hold you, searching

Caverns and cliffs of your being

To be sure all is well

With you.

If it finds despair,

Oh, swift it broods, comforts,

Promises planets.

If it finds gaiety,

Then rings like a copper pendulum

Across the sky to frolic with birds,

Or laugh you down to fishes in the

Secret coral.

There is something in me that runs

Across town at the smallest alarm

To stand beside you,

That prays great prayers for you,

Without time for kneeling.

If this, then, be so,

And you know it to be so,

Laugh at distance and time,

Laugh at separation and deaths,

Laugh at the destroyer himself . . . .

For this extension of ourselves

Can never perish.

Margery S. Stewart
