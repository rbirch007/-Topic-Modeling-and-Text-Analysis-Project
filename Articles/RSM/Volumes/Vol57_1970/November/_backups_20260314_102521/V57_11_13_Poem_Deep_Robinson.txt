& Friendship Memories Dora D. Flack Linnie F. Robinson One of my dear friends revels in choice memories as she works in her yard, for it is filled with plants from the gardens of her friends. Instead of thinking of the drudgery of weeding and cultivating, she remembers pleasant hours and the love of the friends who gave the plants. I have a year-round memory garden in my kitchenin a recipe box. As I contemplate making goodies for my family, I thumb through my recipe file, and my friends crowd back to visit. At the top of each card is written the name of the friend from whom the recipe was obtained. The friends are scattered far but are still a part of my life.
