ACROSS THESE HILLS I have no loom to weave this tapestry © When frost-fires spin their crimson in the clefts And lay their tawny shaw! Across the slopes. Why must. My forgetting, fickle mind Yield up the image, pale with time the hue, Or lose beyond recall the ragged way The aspens trail their gold along the creeks? For soon, too soon, as other seasons know, The year will drop its blanket on the land And I will turn heart-whole to peace of snow. The weaver flings his brilliant skeins each year, But I may never come this way again. All things pass, as this, and even now A bright leaf severs from the tree and floats To earth. The setting sun intensifies Magenta tones to purple, deepens blue, But douses light as dullness quenches pain, And.
