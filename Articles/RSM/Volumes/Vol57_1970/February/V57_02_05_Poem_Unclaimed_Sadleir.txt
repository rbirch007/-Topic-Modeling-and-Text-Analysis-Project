[Vol57 | February 1970 | Page 88 | Poem | Unclaimed Heritage, | Beulah Huish Sadleir]
 UNCLAIMED HERITAGE Did you say, '(Come back when England's winds Blow over marsh and meadow land; When hedge sparrows warble to The dawning day's command?"' Did you bid my return to sight Of houses, quaint as were the Bard's In Stratford town? Where swans afloat On Avon's placid stream, move Unaware of one man's high esteem? Yes, you know, that should I stay a fortnight At a village inn, to learn the lineal Legacy that moves my pen, no Doubt in England's marsh and meadow land I there shall find an unclaimed Heritage, a definitive future planned. Beulah Huish Sadleir SEA Never-the-same-sea Yet ever-the-same-sea Frolics on the shores, Leaps on the beaches Dawn to moonrise, Curled and folding under, Looped with lace; The green combed arch of wave, __ Never, yet ever the same. _.
