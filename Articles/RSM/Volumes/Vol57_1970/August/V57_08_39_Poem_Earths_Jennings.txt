EARTH'S RENEWAL Lyric with April, mountains reach for sky, Pearl-shadowed in the rose and gold of dawn. Above the quickening stream, a killdeer's cry Pours spring's ecstatic melody upon A fledgling breeze. As seedling freshness spills Over the meadow's fragile green and gold, Even the hint of snow returning fills The heart with more of joy than heart can hold! Though coats of storm are wrapped around the elms, (Cloud fringe and sharp twig patterns intertwined Like worn fur. Trappings), though winter overwhelms A leafy freshness with a crystal kind, No frost, returning, stills or censures long The blush of earth's renewal, nor its song. Maxine R. Jennings
