[Watercress Spring, Marilyn McMeen Miller]
WATERCRESS SPRING ' It is here: along the sluice of ditch And river touching cliff Where sun begins.... Here, springing on the blackest waters, Green unfolds across the silent cold There, after ice has thawed - And winter birds have fled the sky. The time of seasons turns And you and I, | In glad remembering of summers past And ali the bitter-fresh of taste, Return to gather memories And sheaves of watercress. And in the mirroring water When | watch the sky's reflection In your hair. The sundown crimson sunset Colors all the dusky trees. | see the winters of the future In the cliffs, and seize The moment of my spring And count it grace. There is but little time to love And gather home This earth Before we lose our summer place. Marilyn McMeen Miller
