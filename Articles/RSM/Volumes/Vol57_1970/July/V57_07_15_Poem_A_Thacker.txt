A WOMAN'S TOUCH " There was land Dry. Barren, unappealing. Demanding. And taking the very lifeblood and soul Of those who dared to claim it. Then came a woman Whose heart cried out for beauty. With precious seeds and cuttings carried countiess miles Each one a
memory of a much-loved garden. Then came beauty The glory of God's creations Partnered with the woman's tender care and dreams and hopes 'a=. Blossomed as the rose.' Theima A. Thacker
