SONG FOR SUMMER RAIN Oh, this is a lovely world the Lord has made; He must have loved us very, very much; The summer rain has gently washed each blade Of grass; the roses wear a subtle touch Of dew today. The skies are gray, But after summer showers nature hums Hymns of praises for refreshing cool, And bluer skies appear, a quiet comes And my reflection in the wayside pool Is brighter far Than a summer star, Or than the summer sun I know is there. Thanks, Lord, thanks very much that you should -care This much for me To send the summer rain so blessedly. —Mabel Jones Gabbott 421 Hazel K.
