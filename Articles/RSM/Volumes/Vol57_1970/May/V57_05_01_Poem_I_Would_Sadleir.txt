[Vol57 | May 1970 | Page 0 | Poem | I Would be Flexible Beulah | H. Sadleir]
I WOULD BE FLEXIBLE I look through veils of mist Upon a cherry tree, Wearing the name of spring. Defying cold, immune To stinging rain, flowered Branches dripping fragility, Move, as winds sweep through Them from a frothy sea I would be flexible As is this tree, Sharing fruitful blessings, Quite unknowingly. —Beulah H. Sadleir
