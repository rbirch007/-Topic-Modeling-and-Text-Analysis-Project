If Every Child By Eva Willes Wangsgard
This thought came with the waning light: If every little child at night Could have a sweet clean trundle bed And soft white pillows for his head; If love and his own mother's hand Could tuck him in and understand His wants and needs, and sympathize; If all our mother-love were wise; If he could waken every day To food and warmth and happy play In cheerful room where sunlight falls, Would we have need of prison walls?
