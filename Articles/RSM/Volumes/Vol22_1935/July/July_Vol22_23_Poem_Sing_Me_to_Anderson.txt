Sing Me to Sleep By Bryce W. Anderson
Small was I, and frightened, My step unsure; Strange the world, and far too large, Your hand alone secure. Body tired by age-long day Slow to bed would creep: "Mother, Darling Mother! Sing me to sleep."
Sure was I, unblushing, With youth's bold face; Dwindling world now far too small, Far too commonplace. Yet my wild, rebellious heart At night would weep: "Mother, Patient Mother! Sing me to sleep."
Tired am I, so tired Of life's mad swim? Spinning world I could not stop Twilight-hazed and dim. Anguished yet for silent rest My heart burns deep: Mother, Angel Mother! Sing me to sleep.
