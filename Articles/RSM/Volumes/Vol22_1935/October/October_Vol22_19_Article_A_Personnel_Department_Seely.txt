[Vol22 | October 1935 | Sequence: 19 | Category: Article | Title: A Personnel Department Seely]

His father was a professor of architecture in the School of Art in Bombay, while his beautiful, gifted mother was sister LESSON DEPARTMENT to the wife of Sir Edward Paynter and the wife of Sir Edmund Burne-Jones, distinguished Englishmen and artists.
Like all Anglo-Indian children, ?Ruddie" early learned the language of his "ayar," his native nurse, and the stories of Shir, Hari, and Mowgli. At an early age the boy was sent to England to school a lonely "Wee Willie Winkie."
