Notes to the Field," page $33 of this Magazine.) Work Meeting We are encouraged over the growing strength of the work meeting.
Even though Welfare sewing budget assignments were greatly reduced, the number of hours of work reported by stakes has increased. We hope this indicates that the sisters are finding real satisfaction in working together on projects that serve others.

In planning your work meetings, we suggest that you consider some of the following projects: quilting, mending, simple sewing for needy families, making cancer pads, preparing materials for Relief Society bazaars, making articles for the general Relief Society bazaar, and tying comforters.

We also suggest that refreshments be simple—perhaps just a hot or cold drink.

May we remind you that the work meeting is not primarily a social gathering, although there should be a pleasant, friendly atmosphere. The work meeting is a time when the sisters may work together on projects that will benefit others.
