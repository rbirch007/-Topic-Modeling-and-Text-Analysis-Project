First Snow Dorothy J. Roberts No snow lies like this first snow on the land. Bach twig and dry stalk clings tenaciously To crystal blossoms falling everywhere; White fingers gesture from the pinion tree. No ledge is left unlined with luminance; No form remains forgotten or undraped. Each pole is turbaned with an ermine crown, Each leafless weed magnificently shaped. The first snow lettered on the land, Virginal, a symphony in white.... Winter wears the face of innocence And purity has clothed the world tonight. Ch ry Sa nth emums.
