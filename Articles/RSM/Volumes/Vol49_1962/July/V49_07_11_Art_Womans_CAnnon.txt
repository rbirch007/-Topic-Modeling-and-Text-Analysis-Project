(To be continued ) Sixty Years Ago Excerpts From the Woman's Exponent, July 1, and July 15, 1902 "For THE RIGHTS OF THE WOMEN OF ZION AND THE RIGHTS OF THE WOMEN or Att NatTIons" WOMEN WHO WIELD THE PEN: Of those dear sisters and frends who have been occasional contributors to the Exponent some still lend a helping hand and some who took great delight in the work have passed behind the veil. Eliza R. Snow, Hannah T. King, Sarah M. Kimball, Louie Wells and Julia McDonald have laid aside the pen, but are not forgotten. We still have Augusta Joyce Crocheron, Ellis R. Shipp, Lydia D. Alder, Emily H. Woodmansee, Mary A. Freeze, Ruth M. Fox, Annie Wells Cannon, Romania B. Pratt and a score of others, only most of them are too busy doing other things to give us even occasional glimpses of what they think and feel... And it is long since we have heard from "'Alofa," "Hyacinth," "Hope," or dear little "Gladys," or "Homespun".
