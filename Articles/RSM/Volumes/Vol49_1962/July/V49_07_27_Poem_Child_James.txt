[Vol49 | July 1962 | Sequence: 27 | Category: Poem | Title: Child Swinging| Author: Ida Elaine James]
Just seconds ago
She was my own child
And that was enough
For me to know.
Now, a handful of wild,
Sweet air-borne fluff
Lighter than snow,
She is separate, free.
Yellow hair afly,
She is the essence of freedom,
Feet pointed to the sky
Away from me.
Her cascade of laughter
Is a bell's ding-dong;
Air-emancipated spirit
Beyond my claim,
While she is swinging —
A triumphant song
Without a name.
