Pearl Bunnel Nlewell Specializes in Knitting Sweaters and Making Hairpin al ace EARL Bunnel Newell, Orem, Utah, is mistress of many hobbies. She has made hundreds of yards of exquisite hairpin lace and many doilies of similar stitches and patterns. Her home and the homes of her relatives and friends are decorated with her handicraft. She crochets and embroiders, makes artificial flowers, footstools, wall plaques, pillow tops, and sews numerous varieties of aprons. She knits sweaters in many different patterns, and makes baby bootees and shawls. She has pieced and quilted a star quilt for each of her married grandchildren, and is busily engaged in making quilts for the cight unmarried grandchildren. Mrs. Newell is an expert gardener, and her indoor plants, including many varieties of African violets, are as lovely as her outdoor garden, where she specializes in roses. Mrs. Newell has been a visiting teacher for more than fifty years, and attends Relief Society regularly. She has pieced more than twenty-five quilts for Relief Society, and is active in all the work-meeting activities. At seventy-six, she has no plans for retirement — only plans for trying to find enough time to take care of all her regular work and all her hobbies.
