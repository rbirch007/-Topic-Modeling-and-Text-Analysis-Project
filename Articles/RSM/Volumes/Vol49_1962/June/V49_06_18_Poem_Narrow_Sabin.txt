RN H™ Narrow Valley Zara Sabin "Is it not too bad?" they said — When at her service someone read She had been born, had lived and died In one small town. Their cheeks, scarce dried, Were wet again.... "To spend her life As child, as maid, as mother, wife, In this narrow valley?" Narrow valley? Yes, it was true. The hills were close and steep, the view Cut off on either side, yet she Looked at the stars. Instinctively, Her thoughts reached up to higher things And knew that more than birds have wings, Even in a valley. She heard the first bluebird, or kept Close vigil where a sick child slept; Baked bread and cakes and sang the while; Walked her Savior's extra mile; Then, when her children all were grown, She died, and had not even known This was a narrow valley.
