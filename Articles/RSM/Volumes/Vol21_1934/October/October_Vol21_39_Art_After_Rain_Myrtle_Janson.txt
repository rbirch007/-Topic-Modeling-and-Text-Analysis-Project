After Rain
By Myrtle Janson
If Heaven e'er comes to earth,
With the caroling of birds thrown in;
It
is after a rain
Skies
so
delicately blue
Air
so pure,
so
fresh and redolent
And
fleeced with
feathery white,
You
breathe
and
breathe
and
breathe
Just
like baby's crib
again;
Waiting for the
celestial light
Gardens and lawns glorious as fairyland
Of
little golden
locks.

Relief Society Song.
OROTHER K. E. Fordham of St.
George last October wrote a Re-
lief Society song and presented
it to
Sister
Brooks,
president
of
that
Stake.
One-hundred
copies
were
mimeographed, eight to twelve copies
being sent to each ward.
They
all
used
it at their March Anniversary
and were so pleased with
it that Sis-
ter Brooks sent word to Sister Robi-
son of
its
success.
Now Brother Fordham
is going
to
get
it out
in
sheet form.
It
is
a two-part song,
entitled "Gifts of
Love."
Ten
copies
will be mailed
for $1.00, and additional copies may
be purchased
at
10c each.
Money
must accompany
the
order.
Send
order to K. E. Fordham, St. George,
Utah.
