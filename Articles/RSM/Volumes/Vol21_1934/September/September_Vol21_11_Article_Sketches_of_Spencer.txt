[Vol21 | September 1934 | Sequence: 11 | Category: Article | Title: Sketches of Spencer]

Sketches of Salt Lake By the Late Josephine Spencer A WATER COLOR The sea a scroll of indigo; A brilliant sunset's afterglow Blurring the sky?its copper haze Streaked with some lingering jets of blaze.
Afar two purple islands float; Between their banks a pleasure-boat Flashes her light?a cool, white star, Against the red horizon bar. IN OIL Over the hilltops' edge of green A blue sky, bending low; And through the pine-trees, dimly seen, A young moon's silver bow. "Below the slope a brilliant sea With shores of purple gauze," And in the west, hung dazzlingly, Some fair world's red mirage.
