Pioneer Mother By Mabel S. Harmer
She gathered her babes and her frugal store For a homeland yet to be won. With resolve in her heart and unfaltering step She walked toward the setting sun.
She left the green valleys and limpid streams For a desert bare and forlorn. But through peril, hardship and toil she kept Her eyes toward the setting sun.
She paused but to cheer a breaking heart, To comfort an ailing one, To offer a prayer to God for peace In the land of the setting sun.
FLAG OF THE STATE OF UTAH
The Relief Society Magazine
Vol. XXI July, 1934 No. 7
