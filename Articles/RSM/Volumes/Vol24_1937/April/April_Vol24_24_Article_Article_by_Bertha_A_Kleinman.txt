By Bertha A. Kleinman
Than courage for some far-off circumstance, Than fortitude for tasks remote and strange, More patience give me and more tolerance, For those I love, for those at closer range; More lenient I would be when others fail, When things go wrong that are not mine to mend, With prejudice and scorn of no avail— There would I train my verdict to suspend; Than wisdom, rather let me supplicate For patience and forbearance to condone; Than judgment in the weighty things of state More kindness let me culture for my own; I shall be wise when I can tolerate! I shall be just and all the rest can wait!
