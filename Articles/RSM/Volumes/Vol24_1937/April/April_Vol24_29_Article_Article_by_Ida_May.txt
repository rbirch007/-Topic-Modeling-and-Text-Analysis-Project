By Ida May
In fifty years I've learned—a minute Has always sixty seconds in it. Twice twelve hours every day Use them, waste them as one may. To high or low, to rich or poor 'Tis never less, 'tis never more. A kindly act of some one hour Has in its grasp undreamed of power. The petty meanness of a second Returns to be both weighed and reckoned. For human love means more to me Than all the sky and earth and sea.
SPRING RHAPSODY
