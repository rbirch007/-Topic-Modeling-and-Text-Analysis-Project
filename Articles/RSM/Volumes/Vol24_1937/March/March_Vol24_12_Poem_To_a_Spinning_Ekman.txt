[Vol24 | March 1937 | Sequence: 12 | Category: Article | Title: To a Spinning Ekman]

TO A SPINNING WHEEL By Bird Knowlton Ekman What you could tell if you could speak O Spinning Wheel!
What storied bygones that we would seek You could reveal. A century has passed and more We have been told Since you were brought from foreign shore By one then old. The years relentless, score on score Have passed you by. The feet that pressed your treadle floor Now quiet lie. Was there a golden-haired young bride Who spun the flax That grew about the country side, While husband's ax From nearby uncleared forest rung? Do you remember joyous bound Of children's feet Their cries that made the walls resound, The rhythmic beat Of rain on thatched or sodded roof? Was there a grey and dim-eyed crone Too old for tears Who bent where flickering fire-light shone Silent with fears? Of helpless age and she alone Remembering How fearlessly in days gone by Her joy would wing As swift her wheel made distaff fly While she would sing What you could tell if you could speak O Spinning Wheel.
