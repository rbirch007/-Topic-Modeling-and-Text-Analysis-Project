THROUGH THE YEARS
By Ella J. Coulam She was so gallant in declining years— Most valiantly she walked to meet old age. Her smile stood out to halt the gathering tears That seemed to threaten her determined wage Of war, on fear and doubt and discontent. She stood upon life's hill-top looking back On dreams of service and a life well spent, With head held high, she faced the unknown track. Why should we fear, in going down the years To walk thus, as courageously as she? As slowly the glory of sun-set nears, Keep an open mind to face eternity, A sense of humor for this strange new role With a prayerful mind and a reverent soul.
