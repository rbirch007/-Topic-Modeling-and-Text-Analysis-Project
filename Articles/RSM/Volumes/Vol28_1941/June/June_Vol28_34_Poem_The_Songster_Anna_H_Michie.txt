[Vol28 | June 1941 | Sequence: 34 | Category: Poem | Title: The Songster]
Out in the apple tree sits a gay songster
Tilting so lightly among the sweet blooms,
Gladness and joy he is sowing about him,
Cheering the world with his full-throated tunes.

Now, do I see why his song is so joyous,
Why he is singing with such lusty pride;
In their new nest in the branches close by him,
Happily listening, sits his young bride.

Sing, little warbler, your joy is supernal,
