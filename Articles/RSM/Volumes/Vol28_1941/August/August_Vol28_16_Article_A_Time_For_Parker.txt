Parker I love to sit by the fire While the embers are all aglow And muse in the shadowy stillness Where things but vaguely show. It's then I feel a presence So peaceful and benign Which spreads a magic beauty Over everything of mine. The room takes on a richness No palace has rivaled yet; I feel glad and oh, so grateful— There are no troubles to forget. I hear the rhythmic breathing Of my dear ones up the stair; My heart beats strong in unison— Love is vibrant everywhere.
