Christie  Lund  Coles 
• 
Hills  are  my  heritage!  My  soul  would  be 
Lost  where  they  were  not;  though  I  had  the  sea 
And  desert  each  intriguing  me  with  space 
I  still  should  need  the  mountains  with  their  grace, 
Their  monumental  spires,  their  velvet  shades, 
Their  amber  tints  when  day's  light  slowly  fades 
Into  soft  amethyst  and  liquid  gold, 
Their  darkly-phantom  shadows  that  enfold 
Their  heights,  the  fields  and  rivers  at  their  feet, 
The  forests  on  their  thighs,  each  small  retreat 
And  cloistered  valley,  sleepy  and  secure 
Within  their  sanctity.    I  should  need  more — 
Their  strength  to  cradle  mind  and  heart  of  me. 
And  lift  my  trust  in  God's  supremacy.
