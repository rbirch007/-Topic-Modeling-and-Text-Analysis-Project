[Vol28 | April 1941 | Sequence: 22 | Category: Article | Title: Misuse of Library Books]

A DISTURBING report has recently reached the Relief Society General Board regarding the misuse of books and periodicals belonging to public libraries. It is reported that books are being defaced by pencil marks, that the corners of pages are sometimes turned down, and in some instances entire pages are being removed. Many of these pages contain valuable reference material.

The proper care of books is important under all circumstances, but this is particularly true with regard to library books. Public library materials are provided for the benefit of the public. Books and periodicals are used many times by many people. Everyone is entitled to use them clean and undefaced. When a book is very soiled, torn, or when sections have been removed, it is not only unfit for use, but it must be replaced at the taxpayer's expense.

The facilities of public libraries are invaluable to Relief Society women both in their homes and in their organization work. The excellent materials which they supply and the courtesy of librarians are sincerely appreciated. The General Board requests that Relief Society women everywhere cooperate in every possible way to correct the misuse and destruction of library materials. There is no finer way to express appreciation for the valuable service rendered by our public libraries than to see that great care is exercised in the use of the excellent materials which they provide.
