[Vol40 | March 1953 | Sequence: 19 | Category: Article | Title: We Found Spring]

Hugged close to earth, we glimpsed the smallest of blooms pushing through the sod, so minute as to be unobserved when standing. Our eyes, long used to seeing ordered beds and marching rows of tulips WE FOUND SPRING and daffodils, now sought the solitary spikes of wild onion and blue beardtongue. We stepped lightly to avoid crushing the buttercups, shooting stars, and violets. Now we have a garden as wide as all the outdoors. We've held the 189 horizons in our grasp and lingered with infinity. As long as there is spring, we'll have beauty that no amount of crowded living can thwart—beauty so fleeting that every day must be held a precious thing.
