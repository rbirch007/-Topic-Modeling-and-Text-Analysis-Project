Pride
Catherine E. Berry

Too late—the August moon slips low,
And dips to some far outer land,
Too late—to tell you not to go,
That I am wise and understand.

For pride can be a bounding wall,
Holding the heart in silent pain,
Letting love slip beyond recall,
Just as an August moon may wane.
