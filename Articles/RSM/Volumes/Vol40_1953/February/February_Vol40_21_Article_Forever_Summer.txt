¥ & Forever Summer (Palm Desert Vacation) Eva Willes Wangsgaard I'll remember this butterfly as yellow As lemon rind on the ripened fruit. I'll remember these moments, warm and mellow, Petunia-fragrant, still as a root. I'll remember the bougainvillaea, climbing The trellis, blossoms cool in my hands, The slender palm tree's penciled timing Of sunlight's progress across the sands. Strange scent for winter's keeping, The fragrance new-mown grasses know! I shall remember when winds are sweeping New crests and swales in a sea of snow. a eg Progress of Man Part Ill—In the Way of Destiny Lesson 21—Destiny of the Earth and Man Elder Archibald F. Bennett (Text: Progress of Man, chapters 42 and 43) For Tuesday, May 26, 1953
