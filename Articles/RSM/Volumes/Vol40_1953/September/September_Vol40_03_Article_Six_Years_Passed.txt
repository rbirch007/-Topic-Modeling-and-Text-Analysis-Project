[Vol40 | September 1953 | Sequence: 03 | Category: Article | Title: Six Years Passed]
During the months that followed she was nervous whenever an Indian approached the house.
Although she fed a great many of them, she said she was always relieved when she saw them on their way back toward their camp. Six Yea rs, Passed Blanche Kendall McKey O little son, my little son, The bell at the school door is ringing; Across the meadow and up the street You seem to fly on elfin feet, In tune with the call of its swinging. The birds have flown from the northern hill; The trees, the house, and my heart are still. Gray-winged clouds presage a storm; O little son, with your big red book, O little son, with your frightened look, May God's love keep you warm!
