[Vol40 | September 1953 | Sequence: 15 | Category: Article | Title: September Snow]

September Snow Lael W. Hill Pause to watch the moments pass— Pause with summer in your hand: More than frost lies on the grass, More than breezes on the land. As a sleeping child may sigh, Dreaming everything grown old, So these frail, white fretworks lie And shiver in the scarf of cold. Summer, caught into a tear, Holds a later season's start: Too early snow upon the year— Too early winter in the heart.
