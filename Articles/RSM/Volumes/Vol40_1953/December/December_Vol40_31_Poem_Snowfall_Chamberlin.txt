[Vol40 | December 1953 | Sequence: 31 | Category: Poem | Title: Snowfall]

California.
Snowfall
Enola  Chamheilin
There  is  no  beauty  like  the  falling  snow —
No  loveliness  like  flakes  succeeding  flakes
Until  the  clouds  in  downy  whiteness  flow
To  earth;  and  distance,  like  a  crystal,  breaks
Before  the  eyes.    The  hills  are  lost,  the  trees,
Unpatterned  shadows  in  a  misty  world.
Could  be  great  giants  crouched  upon  their  knees,
Or  seaweed  from  Sargossa  oceans  swirled.
And  it  has  fallen,  fallen  through  the  years,
From  dusk  to  dawn  and  back  to  dusk  again,
Has  fallen  on  the  laughter  and  the  tears,
The  love  and  hate,  the  rise  and  fall  of  men.
And  now  today  once  more  quite  silently
It  brings  its  beauty  for  the  world  to  see.
