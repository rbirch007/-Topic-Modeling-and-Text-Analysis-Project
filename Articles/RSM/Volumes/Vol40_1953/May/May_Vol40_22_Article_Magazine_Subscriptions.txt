Magazine Subscriptions for 1952
Counselor Marianne C. Sharp GREAT appreciation is felt by the general board to the stakes and missions of the Church for the large increase in the number of Relief Society Magazine subscriptions in 1953. The number rose from 104,210 as of December 31, 1951 to 113,401 as of December 1952. 'There were 9,191 more subscribers to the Magazine in 1952 than in the year 1951.. The Relief Society Magazine is indispensable to the work of Relief Society, containing as it does all the lesson material, reports of Relief Society activities throughout the world, instructions to Relief Society officers, and records of historical importance. Distance is eliminated as a sister in a far country or on a distant farm in this country is enabled to keep pace with the great and enlightening work of Relief Society. To reach this greatly increased total of subscriptions has required devoted service of stake and ward officers and Magazine representatives who have performed their arduous duties throughout the year to maintain and increase their subscription lists. It has also necessitated the loyalty of Relief Society members and many non-members in support of the Magazine. It is hoped that all subscribers will read this Magazine of the women of the Church, and be encouraged and uplifted by the words of counsel and inspiration contained therein. There were fifty-two stakes in 1952 which attained a goal of 100% or over. Eighteen other stakes made increases ranging from ten to thirty-three per cent. The stake with the greatest per cent of increase was Nyssa with an increase of seventy-one per cent. In 1952 there were three missions which achieved a goal of over 100% —a new all-time record. The general board wishes to ex- ' press appreciation for the zeal of Magazine representatives, and the loyalty of Latter-day Saint women who have made it possible, in this day of rising costs, to continue the Magazine at its present subscription price. - +i Honors for Highest Ratings Stake South Los Angeles (California) 210% Magazine Representative—Nancy M.
