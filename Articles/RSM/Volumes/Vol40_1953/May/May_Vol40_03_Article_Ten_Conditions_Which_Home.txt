Elizabeth will be the thirty-ninth British monarch to be crowned in Westminster Abbey, the building of which was begun in 1050 by Edward the Confessor, who lies buried there. Preparations for coronation week have been underway ever since February 8, 1952, when Elizabeth, called home from East Africa by the death of her father, arrived in London by air and was formally proclaimed Queen. A triple fanfare of trumpets signalled her accession. In a ceremony dating back to the days of chivalry, a medieval-clad figure read the proclamation from the balcony of St. James' Palace.
