Lesson titles and objectives follow: Lesson 17—Expanding Horizons (Text, pp. 161-192) Objective: To outline how new and complex relationships within the new Nation expressed themselves through its literature. Lesson 18—Washington Irving, Polished Paradox (Text, pp. 193-220) Objective: To relate the writings to the man, and both to the dynamic beginning period of the Nation out of which they grew. Lesson 19—James Fenimore Cooper, Critic (1789-1851) (Text, pp. 221- 223) Objective: To increase Cooper's significance for us by exploring the interrelationships tying together his aristocracy, his artistry, and his Americanism. Lesson 20—Natty Bumppo, Ameuncan Iiction-Hero (Text, pp. 221- 223) Objective: To acknowledge Cooper's Natty Bumppo as creating and symbolizing the wild beauty of primitive America.
