Objective: To avoid being idle, we must be actively engaged in a good cause.
IN
this
nuclear, automated, busy
life, most of us would be shocked
if we were accused
of being
idle.
In our rush from task
to
task and
from responsibility to responsibility,
the
majority
of
us
can't seem
to
stretch
our
twenty-four-hour
days
far
enough
to
accomplish
all we
wish to do.
Could
this
commandment
pos-
sibly apply to us today?
Despite the
rush of our
lives, could we
still be
guilty of being idle? What is meant
by being
idle?
Socrates said, "Not only
is he idle
who
is doing nothing, but he that
might be
better
employed."
The
dictionary
tells
us
that
idleness
is
not the absence of action, but that
it denotes vain action.
It
is the ab-
sence of useful, effective action.
