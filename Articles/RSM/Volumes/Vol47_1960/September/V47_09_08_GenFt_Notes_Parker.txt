Photograph submitted by Beth M. Stallman
INGLEWOOD STAKE (CALIFORNIA) FASHION SHOW
March
25, 1960
Beth M.
Stallman,
President,
Inglewood
Stake
Relief
Society,
reports:
"A
most
successful fashion show was held
in Inglewood Stake, March
25,
1960, under the
direc-
tion
of
Lillian
Clifton, Second
Counselor,
Inglewood
Stake
Relief
Society,
and Nola
Langford, stake work meeting leader, with Beth Borland as commentator.
Lucille Peel,
stake
organist,
played
beautiful
music
during
the
show.
All
the wards
in
the
stake
were
represented,
and
many
pre-school
children
participated.
All
the
clothing
for
women
was made by
the
individual
models,
except
for
the
children, who
modeled
clothing made by their mothers.
"We were most pleased with
the beautiful sewing done by
our
sisters,
including
many
coats and
suits which were truly professional looking.
The
final model, Dolores
Maxwell, wore a dress 150 years old, which was made from the raw wool to the finished
product, by a member of the family.
The dress
is
still
in perfect condition."
