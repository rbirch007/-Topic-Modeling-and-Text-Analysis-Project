Design for ol caves Mirla Greenwood Thayne It's easy to imagine Till one almost believes That scores of cookie cutters First cut out the leaves. So many shapes and sizes Some fancy, others plain, With scalloped edge and etchings traced Through every slender vein. And while they wait for winter's snow To freezer pack them down, They cool from summer's searing heat,. Sun-baked a golden brown. And some crisp autumn morning, Don't be surprised to see Each crunchy, toasted, amber leaf Frosted dainty. J Would Sing Mary Gustafson
