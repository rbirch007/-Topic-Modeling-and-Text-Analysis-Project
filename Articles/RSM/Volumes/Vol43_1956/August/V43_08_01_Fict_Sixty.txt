Inviting  one-day  side  trips  easily 
accessible  in  the  family  car,  include 
a  thirty-five-mile  jaunt  to  Santa 
Elena  Canyon,  where  the  Rio 
Grande  winds  tortuously  through 
a  gorge,  eighteen  miles  in  length, 
with  rock  walls  rising  sheerly  on 
either  side  to  heights  as  much  as  a 
quarter  of  a  mile  above  the  river! 
One  wall  of  this  fantastic  gorge 
stands  in  Mexico  —  the  other  in 
the  United  States.  A  short  way 
down  river  is  Boquillas  Canyon,  of 
comparable  depth;  and  Mariscal, 
deepest  and  most  spectacular  of 
three  river-hewn  slits. 
En  route  to  Santa  Elena  Canyon, 
the  autoist  may  include  side  jaunts 
to  the  old  ghost  towns  of  Terlingua 
and  Study  Butte.  For  more  than 
fifty  years  one  of  the  nation's  major 
sources  of  mercury,  Terlingua  once 
boasted  the  largest  population  of 
any  mining  town  in  Texas.  Only 
a  few  old  buildings  and  mine  dumps 
remain.  Another  interesting  side 
trip  may  be  made  into  the  Grape- 
vine Hills,  a  weird  region  of  bal- 
anced rocks,  picturesque  stone  for- 
mations, and  strangely  eroded  spires.
