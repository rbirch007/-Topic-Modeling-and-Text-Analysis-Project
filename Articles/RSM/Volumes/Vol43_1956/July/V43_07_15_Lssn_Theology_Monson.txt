Visiting  teacher  meetings  increased  760  in  the  stakes  and  376  in  the  missions,  and 
the  increase  of  163,079  visits  to  Latter-day  Saint  families  by  4,873  more  visiting  teachers 
in  2,617  more  districts,  reflects  steady  progress  in  this  vital  activity. 
Compassionate  services  continued  to  receive  increased  attention  as  indicated  by 
12,421  more  visits  to  the  sick  and  homebound,  and  ten  more  days  care  of  the  sick, 
over  1954.  The  increase  in  days  care  of  the  sick,  while  small,  is  commendable  in  view 
of  the  present  trend  toward  hospitalization.  The  narrative  sections  of  the  reports  con- 
tained many  inspiring  and  heartwarming  incidents  of  varied  types  of  solicitude  and  help 
for  the  aged  as  well  as  for  neighbors  and  friends.  All  this  is  evidence  that  the  grand 
key  words  of  the  society  as  given  by  the  Prophet  Joseph  Smith  —  "Said  Jesus:  'Ye  shall 
do  the  work  which  ye  see  me  do'  "  —  continue  to  have  place  and  purpose  in  the  hearts 
of  Relief  Society  members. 
This  is  the  second  year  we  have  received  reports  on  the  Singing  Mothers  choruses. 
These  showed  2,005  choruses  in  the  stakes  and  missions,  an  increase  of  228,  with  ap- 
proximately 30,983  singers,  an  increase  of  3,703  over  1954.  These  are  very  encourag- 
ing increases  in  this  lovely  and  satisfying  activity  which  gives  added  unity,  spirituality, 
and  cultural  benefits  to  those  who  listen  as  well  as  to  those  who  sing,  and,  in  addition, 
brings  music  into  the  homes  of  the  participants. 
Each  year  sees  an  expansion  of  Relief  Society's  program  and  influence,  not  only 
in  the  established  stakes  and  missions,  but  in  newly  created  stakes  and  in  missions  in 
remote  lands  and  islands  not  heretofore  reached,  and  so  our  great  band  of  sisterhood 
gradually  is  encircling  the  world. 
The  general  board  gratefully  acknowledges  the  support  of  all  those  who  have  so 
unselfishly  given  of  their  time,  talents,  minds,  and  hearts  in  advancing  the  Relief  So- 
ciety program  of  raising  human  lives  to  higher  and  happier  levels.  With  the  sustained 
loyalty  of  its  devoted  workers,  Relief  Society  will  continue  to  do  so.
