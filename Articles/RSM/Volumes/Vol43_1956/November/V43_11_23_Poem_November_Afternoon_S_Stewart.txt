November Afternoon
Margery S. Stewart

What is this golden tide of life
That flows and falls
In golden shallows and the flaming leaf?

That spins and curves,
Turning and reaching on the crimson vine?

These shaken fields, harvested,
Hold as in cupped hand the invisible
Fountain and the springing air.

What is this never-ceasing miracle
That holds and molds
The autumn, yet frees it from
