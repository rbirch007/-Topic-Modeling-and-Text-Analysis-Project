Perhaps  we  discovered  that  poetry 
was  the  thing  a  certain  woman  was 
most  interested  in.  Let  her  see 
what  our  Magazine  has  to  offer 
along  these  lines.  Thumb  through 
the  pages  and  point  out  just  how 
many  poems  each  issue  contains. 
Offer  to  leave  one  of  the  Maga- 
zines with  her  for  a  few  days,  so 
she  can  more  fully  enjoy  the  read- 
ing of  the  poems. 
Appeal  to  her  in  other  ways. 
Every  woman  loves  to  receive  a  new 
and  interesting  recipe.  Choose  one 
that  you  particularly  enjoyed  and 
share  it  with  her.  If  you  have  tried 
the  Chicken  Casserole  that  is  in 
the  September  1955  issue  of  the 
Magazine  (page  596),  tell  her  what 
a  hit  it  made  with  your  family  and 
suggest  she  try  it. 
You  might  ask  if  she  is  keeping 
the  Magazine  in  a  place  where  it 
can  easily  be  picked  up,  maybe  on 
a  table  close  to  her  favorite  chair, 
where,  during  the  day,  when  she
