RELIEF SOCIETY ACTIVITIES Photograph submitted by Rita H. Stone SAMOAN MISSION, PESEGA DISTRICT, MOTOOTUA AND NAVU BRANCHES BAZAAR Rita H. Stone, President, Samoan Mission Relief Society, stands beside one of the booths of this successful bazaar.. Sister Stone reports: "The women had made hot pads, children's clothing, and pillows; they had block-printed native designs on materials to be used for dresses and shirts, and had also made luncheon cloths and napkins and various other articles. The booth in the picture was made from a few sticks and poles decorated with leaves trom the coconut trees. It was very unique and picturesque. I am very happy with the progress made in our Relief Societies. Our Singing Mothers groups are growing also." NOTES FROM THE FIELD 275 "Park Ward was the first ward in the stake to contribute its share to the building fund for the new Relief Society Building.... They obtained their funds by serving banquets to various organizations, among them, the National Governors Convention in 1947 at Aspen Grove, where governors from all the states enjoyed a lovely dinner in the open air, near majestic Mount Timpanogos. 'They expressed their appreciation for a delicious meal so efficiently served to nearly three hundred people in just twenty minutes, enabling them to keep their schedule on their tour. It was a memorable occasion for all. 'The Relief Society continued their dinner projects and furnished the Relief Society room and kitchen of the beautiful new chapel which they are now enjoying."
