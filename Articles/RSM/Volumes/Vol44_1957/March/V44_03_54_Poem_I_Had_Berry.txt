Forgotten Catherine E. Berry I had forgotten time could bring An end to every lovely thing The fairyland the snowfall made, The weeping willow's lacy shade; A candle's glow, a scarlet tree, The way your eyes once looked at me. And wept for loveliness gone past, For dreams and love that did not last. I had forgotten time can heal The wounded heart, can place its seal Upon the past, and yearly bring The cycle back again to spring.
