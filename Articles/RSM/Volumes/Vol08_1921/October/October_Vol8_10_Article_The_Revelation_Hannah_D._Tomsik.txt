THE REVELATION We look upon our neighbor in his grief, And wonder how he bears the bitter pain. In our weak way, we try to give relief;: We try to help and comfort, but in vain. We see him lay the dear ones in the dust, And bravely struggle on along life's way; We marvel at his loyalty and trust, And that he still can toil and pray. We pray with throbbing hearts and bated breath— "Oh! Spare me Lord such sacrifice, such pain! To see my loved ones lying cold in death, I could not bear—though they shall live again Yet, bowed beneath the Cross and left alone— When death takes all that makes life's living sweet, We feel God's hand clasped close within our own, And love and understanding is complete. —Hannah Deady Tomsik.
