THE GLORY OF ADVERSITY By Helen Kimball Orgill Tt seems we mortals must have sorrows, Stern trials, disappointments, too, That we may be drawn nearer Heaven And keep the narrow way in view. We're like the plant found in the garden, Which must be crushed, e'er its perfume Can fill the air with richest sweetness, Complete the glory of its bloom. Then let us not be ever striving For pleasures unalloyed, each day, Look up, be brave, both storm and sunshine Are found along the narrow way.
