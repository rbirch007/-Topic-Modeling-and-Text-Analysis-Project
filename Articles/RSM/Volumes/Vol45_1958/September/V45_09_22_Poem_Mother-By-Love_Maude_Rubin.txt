[Vol45 | September 1958 | Sequence: 22 | Category: Poem | Title: Mother-By-Love | Author: Maude Rubin]
MlotherBy-Lo ve Maude Rubin Oh, she knew the way of a young wife's heart, And gave me her son for my very own— Aware that no wife could take that part Of this lasting love that was hers alone! She lent peace and strength through the weary length Of slow-ticking hours one fearful night... When our first-born's breath hovered near to death; She dried my tears and held the light. Let no knife-sharp thrust of an ancient joke Leave its echoing ring of laughter's sound— For she was a friend who would give or lend A wing of comfort the whole year round. No phrase like mother-in-law could prove The way I feel toward my mother-by-love!
