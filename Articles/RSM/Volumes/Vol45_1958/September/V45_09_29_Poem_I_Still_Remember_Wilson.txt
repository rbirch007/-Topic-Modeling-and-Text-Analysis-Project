I Still Remember

Grace Barker Wilson

F Stull Remember Grace Barker Wilson I remember the aspen tree that stood To shade my mother's kitchen door. I remember the scent of the sweet pine wood Washed clean by a quick downpour. And barefoot errands were long delayed When our toes felt the soft mud ooze In the fresh rain pools that we stopped to wade, (We were far too rich for shoes! ) I remember the sweet, clear winter nights When the shadows fell long and deep; And the high stars winking their crystal lights, And Mother tucking me in to sleep.
