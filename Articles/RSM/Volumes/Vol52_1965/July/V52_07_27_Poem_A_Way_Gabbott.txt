[Vol52 | July 1965 | Page 542 | Poem | A Way of Peace | Mabel Jones Gabbott]
A Way of Peace Mabel Jones Gabbott Let peace be in my home, O Lord, I pray, In children's laughter, rippling clear and free, In polished wood and muted tapestry, In scent of lilac on an April day; Within my home let there be found a way Of loving, gladly shared; of empathy For one another's dreams; and let there be A gentleness in what we do and Say. And then across our threshold, past these walls, Like fragrance from a hyacinth in spring, A vibrant tone of peace will radiate, And like the rain-fresh apple bloom that falls Scattering beauty, our way of life will bring A leavening of love beyond our gate.
