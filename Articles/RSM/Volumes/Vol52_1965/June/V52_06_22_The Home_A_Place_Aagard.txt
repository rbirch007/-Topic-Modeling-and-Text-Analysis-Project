A Place tor Balls and Bats Made by Mary Aagard, Fountain Green Ward, Moroni Stake, Utah > @ For the "son in the house," a wall hamper for balls and bats attached to a clothes closet door, or on the wall of his den, will make him a champion of orderliness. The hamper must be made of strong cloth, such as denim, ticking, or sailcloth, and can be cut in the length and width most convenient, and in accordance with the number of pockets needed. Appliqued replicas of bats, balls, cap, and gloves make unmistakable labels, and are an invitation and a reminder for putting the boy's favorite equipment in the right place. An overlapping piece of material fits over a clothes hanger, or a shaped piece of wood or wire which can be attached to the wall or to a door.
