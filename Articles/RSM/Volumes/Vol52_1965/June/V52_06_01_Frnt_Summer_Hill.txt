[Summer Declares Lael W. Hill]

Summer declares uncountable small green leaves And stars with radiant eyes in the morning grasses; Yet if I whisper among them (soft as warm wind impresses) I love you 1! Love you! Love you hear not my loves But the wings of uncountable bees that sing to clover And carry the heart of sweet from field to hive; Yet if you would listen beyond, you would know how I've Said always and only one name to the season... Lover, Listen to leaves. In movings of morning air, To flowering flight from meadows gilded and scented: Hear then, and have in their hum all days uncounted, Your name breathed low, my voice at your summer ear.
