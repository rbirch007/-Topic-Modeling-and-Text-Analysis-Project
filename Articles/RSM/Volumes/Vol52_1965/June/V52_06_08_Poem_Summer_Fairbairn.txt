[Vol52 | June 1965 | Page 421 | Poem | Summer Cloud | Vesta Fairbairn]
Summer Cloud Vesta Fairbairn Out of the north a wisp of white smoke blew Over the hill, over the tops of pines, Until it grew And gathered into a cloud, A single cloud drifting Lonely in the immense clear blue lake of sky High Above the pines, the firs, The incense cedars, the mountain oaks, Drifting and lifting On an irresponsible wind, Until It disappears like magic, Sifting Away, invisible, Leaving the unshadowed mountain, The many greens of trees, And the bright day Once again To the authority of the crested jay, The bossy, beautiful, blue, blue jay.
