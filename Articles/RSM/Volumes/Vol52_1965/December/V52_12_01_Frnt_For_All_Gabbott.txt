Of Place That was an ancient year, a distant land When Magi, richly dressed with gifts in hand, Had traced the starlight unto Bethlehem, Had seen the Holy Child, and worshipped him, Yet all of earth is his, the restless dune, The steadfast mountains, and the sea and moon, The crowded city, trimmed and tinseled bright, The country roadway drenched in lunar light. Though stars are quiet now above the place Where Christ was born, his light will always fill With penetrating rays the ends of space, As surely as it crowned Judea's hill.
