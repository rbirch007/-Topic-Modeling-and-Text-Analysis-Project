[Summer Afternoon — Christie Lund Coles]

We were alone. Summer stitched the still, Taffeta afternoon; And crickets embroidered The blue-green silk. Needle-point shadows Filled the waiting squares; Gulls picked out The mistaken threads. Geese cross-stitched the sky. We were alone. Softly sewn Into this tapestry Which, somehow, bore Our laughing signature. Mount Hood and Lost Lake, Oregon
