Till Love Comes Home Rae Woolley Whited Yet a little while till roses bloom, Till buds burst forth on tree and bough That each may keep its yearly vow. Yet a little while till youngsters play All tanned by sun. Till life seems easy once again, And there is time to make a friend. As Seen Through Literature Elder Robert K. Thomas Lesson 10—Good Versus Evil—Part II (Text: Out of the Best Books, pp. 246-280, 329-360) For Fourth Meeting, November 1965 Objective: To show that problems of good and evil involve @ In this age of aggressive individuality how often do we hear the assertion "It's my own life to live.""' But should this be accepted as justification for rejecting the obligations of society? It shouldn't take much reflection on our part to help us see that, whether we like it or not, interdependence is a fact. Yet in a profound sense we all do have a very individual life to lead. This is not the life of the rebel, the abdicator of responsibility. Quite the contrary. The life we must live alone is the life of our choices. God has given to each of his children the right and necessity of choice. We may come from a poor environment; we may lack education; but, as The Book of Mormon makes clear, we can all desire to be better. In that desire is an affirmative choice. The selections of literature which we will consider this month all share a focus on personal choice, and the results of such choice.
