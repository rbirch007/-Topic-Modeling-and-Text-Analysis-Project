[Vol52 | February 1965 | Page 150 | Poem | Frost- Etched | Gladys Hesser Burnham]
Frost-Etched Gladys Hesser Burnham Beautiful upon the grass The silvered frost is laid Like spider web of tangled lace, Intricately made. Shy footprints melt their way across The lawn and try to hide, Before the prying sun can rout Their mdkers, who abide In secret places of the night To slip away at dawn And leave their telltale mark upon Frost-fretted, dewy lawn.
