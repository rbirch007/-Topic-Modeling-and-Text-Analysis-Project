Wind Ida A. Isaacson Outside the house and far up there The wind is rough and thin as air. It grapples with the earth and sky Then, disappears Ii Across the reaches When all the world It noses at the win ke a sigh. Of the night is void of light dow sill, Moves shadows that are dark and still. It tangles with a single leaf Then moves like moisture on a reef, No flight, no sound of winging bird. Far off in Space the wind is heard.
