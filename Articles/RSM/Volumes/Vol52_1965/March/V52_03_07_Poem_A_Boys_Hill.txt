A Boy's Way Is Up Lael W. Hill A boy's way is up, from the moment when He struggles to both feet and stands like men  To the hour he clambers and scrambles where He can teeter with joy on a kitchen chair  To the day his toes and his confidence Win a perilous perch on the backyard fence  To the breathless time he discovers he Is the bold master of any old tree. A boy's way is up  and that is why Space ships will rocket across our sky.
