[Vol52 | March 1965 | Page 177 | Poem | On the Heights | Miriam R. Anderson]
On the Heights Miriam R. Anderson Bend to the wind, old tree, set alone And stark along the timberline; Sturdy and gnarled the years have wrought A gallantry in your design. Bend to the wind, resilient, where The eagle feeds his lonely brood; Only the stout that bend and give Survive the tempest's magnitude. Bend to the wind  spring back again! So trees and men survive to know The meaning, through the storm Of life's demand  that we shall grow!
