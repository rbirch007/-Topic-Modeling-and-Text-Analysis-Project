[Vol9 | November 1922 | Sequence: 09 | Category: Article | Title: Be Near Me Alice Morrill]

BE NEAR ME Alice Morrill Be near, my friend, for in that hour When thy soul touches mine, Sweet inspiration erst unknown, A courage half divine, Bids me look up past gathering mists To brightening skies above. Be near me; let my sp'rit feel The magic of thy love. Stay near, my friend, go not away; My soul is knit to thine. When thou art near, there is no death But only rest sublime. There is no darkness, and no chill, But only warmth and light. Sit close, dear heart, and hold my hand I see the radiant light!
