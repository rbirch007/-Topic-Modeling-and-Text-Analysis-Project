[Vol9 | November 1922 | Sequence: 14 | Category: Article | Title: Words Grace Ingles Frost]

WORDS Grace Ingles Frost Words that might be sparkling gems To make the world more glad, Are oft but hard, unpolished stones, That make the heart grow sad. May I weld but gleaming words With links of gold between, And cast away all else besides, That would my work bemean. Then, mayhaps, some day I'll find A radiant diadem, With which to clasp my chain of words, And crown each one of them.
