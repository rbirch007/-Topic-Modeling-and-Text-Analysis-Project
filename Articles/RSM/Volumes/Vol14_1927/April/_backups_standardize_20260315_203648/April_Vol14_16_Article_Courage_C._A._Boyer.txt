Courage By Mrs. C. A. Boyer If the world seems gloomy And friends prove untrue, And hope and courage are fled; If flowers lose their fragrance And skies seem less blue, And you look to the future with dread; Don't sit at life's window Where the shadows fall, And watch with a sigh and a tear; But open the doors To brightness, and all That gives the heart solace and cheer. Should sorrows and cares Gather round like a cloud, Chasing the sunshine away; And temptation and darkness Fall like a shroud In a mist to cover the way; Look farther beyond? A light will appear, If you're careful no trouble to borrow; The storm will soon pass, The clouds disappear And the sunshine be brighter tomorrow.
