MY PRODIGAL

By Lucy Wright Snow

I had a son—my first-born, fair and strong,
The pride and glory of my youthful heart;
I dreamed for him a life of noble song,
A clean, sweet record, set from others apart.

But as the years went by, I saw him turn
From all the things I'd taught him to revere;
I watched the sacred fires cease to burn,
And saw him choose the paths I'd taught to fear.

My heart was torn with anguish and with shame,
As step by step he wandered from the right;
I could not lift my head when others came—
My son had chosen darkness for his light.

The years dragged by—long years of bitter pain,
Of sleepless nights and days of hopeless grief;
I prayed and wept and prayed and wept again,
But found no rest, no comfort, no relief.

Then came a day when, broken and undone,
He stood before me, penitent and sad:
"Mother," he whispered, "I'm your wayward son—
Can you forgive the sorrow I have had?"

Oh, joy beyond all telling filled my heart!
I took him to my breast and held him there;
No word of blame could make us two apart—
My son had come back to my love and prayer.

And now I know, as never known before,
How God must feel when we, His children, roam
In paths of sin, then, penitent and sore,
Come back to Him and to our Father's home.
