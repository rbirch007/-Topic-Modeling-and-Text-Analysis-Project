My New Year Alice Morrill
My New Year is not a fairy, nor a cherub, nor a little child, She is a womangrown up, mature, and capable. She is the counterpart of myself. She has come to be my companion. The kind of companion she will be depends upon my attitude towards her and the treatment I mete out to her.
From having had a close companionship with more than fifty other New Years, some of which were babes with me, others increasing in stature and strength as they came along in the order of their line of march, year after year, I feel that I can be sure of what I know of this one whose advent I am expecting.
I know that if I respect her, and take her at her true worth, she will see worth in me. If I recognize her power, she will give me strength. If I give to her the "best I have," she will give me love and cheer. In return for my faith in her, I shall receive that responding urge which will make for my success.
I know that if I greet her with a smile she will smile back at me. I know that if I take hold of the handle of my burden of duty and lift with a vim, she will grip firmly the other handle and take half of my load.
