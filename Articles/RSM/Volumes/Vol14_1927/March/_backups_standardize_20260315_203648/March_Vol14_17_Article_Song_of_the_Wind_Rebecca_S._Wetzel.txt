Song of the Wind Rebecca S. Wetzel
The blush of spring is everywhere, The winds no longer sigh, But chant a droning melody, That would a muse defy.
And through the fragrant orchard, Where trees are all a-bloom, You'll hear the gentle murmur,? Of the wind's enchanting tune.
I'm life to you the wind sings, I teach you how to grow, By swaying to my rhythm, Your sap begins to flow.
So when you see in springtime, The graceful swaying trees; You'll know that they are dancing To the music of the breeze.
