Brimhall-Foley Flower from the hillside, Sweet September rose, Planted by prophets In soil that they chose. Silent for ages, At length to come forth And speak as by magic. Through tongues of the earth. Rose of Columbia No hand shall destroy, Plucked by an angel And read by a boy. Flower of a nation And light to a world, With fragrance unceasing Its bright leaves unfold. The plant of its fountain Shall ever increase, Be guarded by angels "Till earth rests in peace.
