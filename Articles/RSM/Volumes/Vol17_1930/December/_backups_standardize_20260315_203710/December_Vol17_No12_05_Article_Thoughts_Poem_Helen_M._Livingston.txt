Thoughts By Helen M, Livingston There are some who live in rooms all elegant, Soft-carpeted and hung with richest tapestry. But whose thoughts, like beggars, walk deserted streets With soiled clothes, and in degraded company. There are some who live in homes of humble mein, Who know what hunger is, and want for worldly things, But whose thoughts, soft-velvet robed with supple forms, Tread stately measures oft, and march abreast with kings.
