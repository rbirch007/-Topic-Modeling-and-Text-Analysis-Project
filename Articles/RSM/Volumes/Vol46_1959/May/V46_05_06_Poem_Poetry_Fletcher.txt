Would You Write Poetry?

WATCHING

''If

''Have you

"Once,"

"but

A

If you would write
poetry you must know what to strive
for, and then
practice and practice
with the technique you have gained.
To be
a

I

first of

reader of

Read the poetry of the

the

Bible, early English literature, Shake-
speare's poetry and other Elizabeth-
an

of Victorian literature, American lit-

rent

Analyze

types

poems appearing in great variety in
modern magazines — literary publi-
cations,
women's
periodicals,

Try to find the

Poetry, you will find, makes many

it stimulates; through its rhythm and

It

It

The inspiration or idea for a poem

It must

It

Could anyone

"Does this poem have beauty?"

It

It

Emily Dickinson's choice of words

I

Nouns

''But by

Outdated words should never be

Guard

'hide, o'ei, yon, neath.
Your poem

IMAGERY

Note the use

And hollow like the dry and wrinkled fruit,

Images are always based on mem-

There are three musts in imagery:
1. Images should be

2. Images

To

Give your read-

Ask yourself, "How will the

When you

Repetition of pleasing vowel and
consonant sounds
is an effective way
to
attract
attention
to
a

a compelling,

Note the repetition of sounds

The

Above the

Bright

But do not overuse repetition
in

will be
carried only on
the
surface
of the sound.
Rhythm, we

mental to all the arts.
In poetry the

pattern consists of
vari-
ous

of
stressed

sounds.
The
meteiy
or
measurable
rhythm

line

pattern

stressed and
unstressed
sounds. A stressed sound combined

or two
unstressed
sounds
in
called

foot^ and
a
de-
scription of a line of poetry
is given

The rhythmical pattern of poetry
must be studied
to be

You
can
teach

nique.
Any good book on the

(I

If you

the feet of your poem and make

The rhyme scheme of a poem may

almost any

While rhyme

selec-
tion

Do not per-
mit the rhymes to

Be very careful

When we say meadows gay, instead

reader know^s
that we
were

Then

Omit

Obvious

Such

Jove, wing and
sing, hliss and kiss, have grown stale

Many

Poems

The

or phrasing within the poem.
