[Vol46 | May 1959 | Poem | Spring's Golden Web | Grace Ingles Frost]

Spring's Golden Web Grace Ingles Frost Spring has woven a golden web And spread it over earth; It matters not which way I look, Of it, there is no dearth. All that was lost of loveliness, To winter's chill embrace, Is re-endowed with beauty By spring's effulgent grace.
