When Deserts Bloom in Arizona

Ruth H. Chadwick

The rains had come to bathe the desert land And soothe and quench the burning pangs of thirst. The cooling raindrops washed the barren sand And gave new life to dying things they nursed. Creation's need impelling confidence To bring forth flower and seed upon the stem. The white-belled yuccas raised their hooded heads While yellow-misted Palo Verde trees, Beside the plumes of ocotillo reds, Wafted desert sweetness on the breeze. Revived, the plants in grateful recompense Stirred the inner powers God gave to them— The rains again had triumphed over gloom; A glorious sight—the desert was in bloom!
