Our

Bracing our backs
against the
tough, enduring bark
of the oak
tree, we watched
ants scurrying up and down the vertical cracks in
its trunk working
in
a frenzy of prep-
aration for the coming winter.
Silly ants!
Winter was such a long way
off!
We had
all the time in the world,
all the never-ending, lazy time in the world.
We
posed
hypothetical
problems
and
solved
them
with
wise
solemnity.
We
mapped our lives in exact detail and were absolutely certain of the map's accuracy.
From
these superior heights we shook our heads
in dismay
at "those who should have done,
but didn't."
Even our
little world
could be
upset,
though.
