[Vol46 | June 1959 | Sequence: 17 | Category: Poem | Title: Heart | Author: Jacobson]
> Heart of a House Ethel Jacobson The kitchen's the heart of a house. And all day long the kitchen The first thing in the morning Announces, "Help yourself!" Its heavenly aromas To any busy workers Are a most delightful warning Who want a lift, a break, To any laggard sleepers And to kiddies, fresh from classrooms, That blueberry buns await, So hungry that they ache. Or maybe orange muffins, And it's risky to be late. Then dinner's cheery bustle Is shared by the whole brigade. At lunchtime, steaming trayfuls The kitchen's the heart of a house— Are on the snack bar shelf. To the last lone midnight raid.
