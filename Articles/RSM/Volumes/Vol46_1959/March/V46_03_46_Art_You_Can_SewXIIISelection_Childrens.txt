 | Author: Jean R. Jennings]You Can Sew—XIII—Selection of Children's Clothes

Jean R. Jennings

CHILDREN'S clothes should be selected with the same care as adult clothing. The same principles of line, color, and design apply, but there are additional factors to consider when choosing clothes for children.

First, children's clothes must be practical. They should be easy to put on and take off, comfortable to wear, and easy to care for. Children are active, and their clothes must stand up to hard wear and frequent washing.

Second, children's clothes should allow for growth. It is wasteful to buy clothes that will be outgrown in a few months. Look for adjustable features such as elastic waistbands, deep hems that can be let down, and adjustable straps.

Third, children's clothes should be appropriate for the child's age and activities. A frilly dress is not suitable for playground wear, nor are good clothes appropriate for messy activities.

When selecting colors for children, remember that bright, clear colors are usually more becoming than dull or muddy ones. Children can wear colors that would be too bright for adults. However, very dark colors should be avoided for small children as they tend to make the child look smaller.

Patterns should be in scale with the size of the child. Large patterns overwhelm small children, while tiny patterns may look insignificant on larger children.

Quality is important in children's clothes. Well-made garments will last longer and look better throughout their wearing life. Check seams, hems, and construction details before purchasing.

Finally, involve the child in the selection process when appropriate. Children who help choose their clothes are more likely to take care of them and wear them willingly.
