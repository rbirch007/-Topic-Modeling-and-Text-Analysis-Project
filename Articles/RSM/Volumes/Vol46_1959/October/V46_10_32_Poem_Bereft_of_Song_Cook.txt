Bereft of Song

Maude O. Cook

Along a boundless highway Companioned by the sun, The birds are flying, flying, For the summer work is done. Like days without the sunshine, Are woods bereft of song; The nests hang gaunt and empty, And the hours are spare and long. The winter spreads a mantle, To hush the sad refrain, For the winds are sighing, sighing, Till the larks come back again.
