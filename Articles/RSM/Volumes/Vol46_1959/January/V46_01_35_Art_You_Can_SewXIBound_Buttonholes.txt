 | Author: Jean R. Jennings]You Can Sew—XI—Bound Buttonholes
Jean R. Jennings

Bound buttonholes are used on tailored garments and are especially attractive on coats, suits, and heavy fabrics. They may be made by hand or by machine.

HAND-BOUND BUTTONHOLES

Mark the position and length of the buttonhole with basting stitches. Cut a piece of fabric on the true bias, about 1½ inches wide and 2 inches longer than the buttonhole. Place this piece over the marked buttonhole with the right sides together.

Stitch around the buttonhole ⅛ inch from the marked line, making the stitching exactly the length of the finished buttonhole. The stitching should be perfectly straight and the corners square.

Cut through both thicknesses along the center line of the buttonhole, cutting to within ⅛ inch of each end. Then cut diagonally into each corner, being careful not to cut the stitching.

Turn the bias piece through the opening to the wrong side. Press carefully, rolling the seam slightly to the under side so that it will not show on the right side.

Fold the bias piece to form neat, even lips on each side of the opening. The lips should just meet in the center. Baste in place.

From the right side, stitch close to the edge of each lip, catching the bias piece underneath. Remove basting.

On the wrong side, trim away excess bias fabric, leaving about ¼ inch seam allowance. Overcast the raw edges to prevent raveling.

MACHINE-BOUND BUTTONHOLES

This method is quicker than the hand method and gives equally good results.

Cut two bias strips, each ¾ inch wide and 1 inch longer than the buttonhole. Place these strips on the right side of the garment, one above and one below the marked buttonhole line, with raw edges toward the center.

Baste in place. From the wrong side, stitch ⅛ inch from the center line on each side, making the stitching exactly the length of the finished buttonhole.

Cut along the center line between the two rows of stitching, cutting to within ⅛ inch of each end. Cut diagonally into the corners.

Pull the bias strips through the opening to the wrong side. Press carefully and proceed as for hand-bound buttonholes.

For a professional finish, the ends of bound buttonholes should be bar-tacked by hand or finished with a close zigzag stitch on the machine.
