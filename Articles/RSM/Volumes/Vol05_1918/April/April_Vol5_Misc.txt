Resurrection. By Lucy S. Burnham. Dearest mother, Spring is here, The birds are all returning With wondrous songs of great delight, For shame their hearts are yearning. As youthful Nature hush scarce dared To breathe, So great her joy to hear the birds, Hard clouds might make them leave. I hear their sweet notes calling, When from my bed I rise, I see the flowers peeping, I note in glad surprise The Easter Lily's petals Burst forth as if to bloom. Sweet emblem of the coming Of Christ from out the tomb. My heart so lone and saddened, Takes courage at the sight, My weary brain seemed maddened, All through the winter night. Where'er I looked, dear mother, Death stared me in the face, I could not even dream of thee Except in Death's embrace. And wondrous peace steals o'er me, A banished is the gloom. I know that thy dear body That now lies in the tomb Will come forth as these petals, To bloom in life and love, And you'll be there to greet me When I reach that home above.
