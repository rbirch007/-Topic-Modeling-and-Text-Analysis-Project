The Shepherd of the Streets
Claire Stewart Boyer

I who have seen so little yet seem to know so much
Philosophy, Geology, Philology, and such,
Saw something in the street today that made my soul stand still;
I was at once a wise man and a shepherd on a hill.

"Hallelujah, Hallelujah, 'tis the King of earth new born,
Hallelujah, Hallelujah to this wondrous Christmas morn."

I saw a child with laughing eyes take grey age by the hand,
And lead her safely through a street with Christmas traffic jammed;
I saw a vagrant set a sparrow's broken wing aright,
A cripple risk his life to save a mongrel in a fight.

All in that moment I saw—love, devotion, sacrifice,
And in that moment I beheld the image of the Christ.
Shepherd I was and wise man as I knelt before the King,
And from afar I seemed to hear the storied angels sing—

"Hallelujah, Hallelujah, 'tis the King of earth new born,
Hallelujah, Hallelujah to this wondrous Christmas morn."

I walked my way to Bethlehem and knelt before the king,
I saw the Christ, I knew him, and I heard the angels sing.
