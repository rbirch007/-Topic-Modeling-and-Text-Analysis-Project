AN OLD FASHIONED GARDEN

By Grace Zenor Pratt

I love an old fashioned garden,
With its hollyhocks tall and straight,
And its sweet peas climbing the trellis
By the little white garden gate.

I love the old fashioned blossoms—
The pansies with faces so sweet,
The mignonette, modest and lowly,
That perfumes the air at my feet.

I love the old fashioned roses
That bloom by the garden wall,
And the morning glories that twine
Round the posts of the garden hall.

I love the old fashioned garden
With its sweet old fashioned flowers,
For they bring back the dear old memories
Of childhood's golden hours.
