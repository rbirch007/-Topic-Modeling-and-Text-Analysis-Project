"About  tomorrow,  Paul."  Janet 
brought  the  conversation  back.  "It's 
twenty-three  years  ago  tomorrow 
since  we  came  to  El  Toro— togeth- 
er." She  looked  into  the  fire,  saw 
the  years  ahead  in  swift  review,  then 
she  laughed  dubiously.  "Twins  are 
a  responsibility,  Paul.  I  feel  perfect- 
ly antiquated  beside  my  amazing 
daughter.    Does  Jerry  dwarf  you?" 
"I'll  say  he  does.  I  have  to  stand 
on  tiptoe  all  the  time  to  keep  abreast 
of  him.    We  must  be  getting  old!" 
"Not  until  they  re  as  old  as  we 
are,  Paul!"  Janet  protested. 
"Twenty-three  years,"  Paul  mused. 
"We  sat  alone  by  the  fire  then,  too. 
Remember,  Jan?" 
"And    the   embers    glowed   long 
after  the  flame  was  gone  . . .  .  "
