Father said this was just a matter of opinion; that mother couldn't really understand Jinny, but only got what she said by some sixth sense that only mothers have. Nevertheless mother wasn't worried about Jinny because she couldn't talk plain. When father would make gibes—hoping to make Jinny try harder—or the other children teased her, mother would say calmly, "I had a sister who couldn't talk plain until she was eight." And let it go at that. Mother worried about Jinny for quite a different reason. Jinny had red hair, and red hair was not a 'thing to be proud of in those days.
