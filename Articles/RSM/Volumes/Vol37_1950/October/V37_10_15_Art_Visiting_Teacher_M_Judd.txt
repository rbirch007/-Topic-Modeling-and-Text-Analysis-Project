October Ts Forever Lizabeth Wall Here is a beauty for remembering: This moment-of-October, scarlet-still, This quiet time that holds no flight of wing — Nor any wind-song, wild from any hill. And I shall keep this peace to fold about Less lovely seasons I shall surely know: A young, uncertain springtime tinged with doubt, Or any winter, silent under snow. So let me etch upon my heart each leaf, Each arrowed shaft of sun upon the grass, To armor me against impending grief And warm me when these amber hours pass.
