[Vol27 | February 1940 | Sequence: 26 | Category: Poem | Title: Lincoln]

Lincoln

He was a man of common clay,
Yet touched with fire divine;
A man who walked the common way,
Yet left upon the shrine
Of memory, a deathless name
That glows with sacred flame.

He was a man of humble birth,
Yet great in heart and mind;
A man who loved his fellow earth,
And served all humankind.
His life was like a beacon light
That pierced the nation's night.

He was a man of simple faith,
Yet strong in righteous will;
A man who conquered sin and death,
And serves his country still.
His memory is a sacred trust
That lifts us from the dust.
