LESSON II.

Work and Business.

SECOND WEEK IN FEBRUARY.

THE IDEAL WOMAN.

PROVERBS 31:10-31.

References: Prov. 31:10-31; Eph. 5:22-33; Col. 3:18-25; I Tim. 2:9-15; Titus 2:3-5; I Pet. 3:1-7.

The thirty-first chapter of Proverbs gives us a picture of the ideal woman. It is a picture drawn by an inspired pen, and one which has been the standard of womanly excellence for thousands of years. Let us study it carefully, and see what lessons we may learn from it.

The chapter opens with the words of King Lemuel, which his mother taught him. She warns him against wine and strong drink, and against giving his strength unto women. Then she tells him what manner of woman he should seek for a wife.

"Who can find a virtuous woman? for her price is far above rubies." This is the opening verse of the description of the ideal woman. The word "virtuous" here means much more than mere chastity. It means a woman of worth, of excellence, of noble character. Such a woman is rare and precious.

"The heart of her husband doth safely trust in her, so that he shall have no need of spoil." She is trustworthy. Her husband can depend upon her absolutely. He knows that she will be true to him and to their home interests.

"She will do him good and not evil all the days of her life." She is loyal and devoted. Her influence upon her husband is always for good.

"She seeketh wool, and flax, and worketh willingly with her hands." She is industrious. She does not shrink from work, but enters into it with a willing spirit.

"She is like the merchants' ships; she bringeth her food from afar." She is provident. She plans ahead and makes provision for her household.

"She riseth also while it is yet night, and giveth meat to her household, and a portion to her maidens." She is an early riser, and sees that her family is properly fed.

"She considereth a field, and buyeth it: with the fruit of her hands she planteth a vineyard." She has business ability. She can invest money wisely and make it productive.

"She girdeth her loins with strength, and strengtheneth her arms." She keeps herself physically fit for her work.

"She perceiveth that her merchandise is good: her candle goeth not out by night." She takes pride in the quality of her work, and is willing to work late when necessary.

"She layeth her hands to the spindle, and her hands hold the distaff." She is skilled in the domestic arts.
