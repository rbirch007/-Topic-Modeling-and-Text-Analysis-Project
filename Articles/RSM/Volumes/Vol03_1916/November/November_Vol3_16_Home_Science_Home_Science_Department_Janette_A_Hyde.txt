HOME SCIENCE DEPARTMENT.
JANETTE A. HYDE.

THE THANKSGIVING DINNER.

Thanksgiving is a time when the American housewife has an opportunity to show her skill in preparing and serving a dinner. It is a time when the family gathers around the table and partakes of the good things which have been prepared for them.

The Thanksgiving dinner should be well planned. The menu should be decided upon several days in advance, and the marketing should be done early. The turkey should be ordered from a reliable dealer, and if possible, should be fresh killed.

The following menu is suggested for a Thanksgiving dinner:

Oyster Cocktail
Celery    Olives    Pickles
Roast Turkey with Dressing
Cranberry Sauce
Mashed Potatoes    Creamed Onions
Hubbard Squash
Parker House Rolls
Mince Pie    Pumpkin Pie
Coffee    Nuts    Raisins

The table should be attractively set. A white cloth should be used, and the best china and silver should be brought into service. The centerpiece may be of chrysanthemums or autumn leaves, or a combination of both. Small dishes of nuts and raisins may be placed at intervals on the table.

The turkey should be stuffed and trussed the night before, and should be put into the oven early in the morning. It should be basted frequently during the cooking.

The vegetables should be prepared early in the day, so that they may be cooked and ready to serve when the turkey is done.

The pies should be made the day before, so that they will be thoroughly cooled before serving time.

With careful planning and preparation, the Thanksgiving dinner may be made a success, and the housewife may have the satisfaction of knowing that she has done her part in making the day a happy one for her family.
