PrizeWinning Poems &liza Roxey Snow Memorial Poem Contest a ¥ LIZABETH WALL MADSEN First Prize Poem Wings OQver the Wrst Lizabeth Wall Madsen The wheeling arcs of gulls have etched the storm With slender, silver patterns of their flying. Curved to the wind and strangers to the warm Far earth, they seek a new horizon, crying. Theirs is a spiraled search, and there is more To weld them to the clouds than fragile feather; Other than bone and sinew lets them soar, Freed to the free sky and its ragged weather. Beyond the windless acre where I stand, Shuttered from rain in one protected hollow, They are but little space above the land, From chrysalis to flight where I might follow. How loosely tethered to the world am I When gulls are calling from a storm-wild sky!
