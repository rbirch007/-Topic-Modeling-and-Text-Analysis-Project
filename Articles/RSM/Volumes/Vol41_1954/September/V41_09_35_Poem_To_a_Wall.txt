[Vol41 | September 1954 | Sequence: 35 | Category: Poem | Title: To a | Author: Wall]
Being Put Out Lizabeth Wall Be glad to be abroad, small furry friend; The spangled world is magic to explore, The darkened alley reaches without end; The silent roadway is a foreign shore. Tonight is not a mght for housebound souls. Behind the neighbors' porches there are new And hitherto-unguessed-at cubbyholes For you to poke your questing nose into. Go run like wind across the greening lawn, Go chase elusive balls of shadow-silk, And with a bib of yellow stardust on, Drink moonlight from your cup instead of milk.
