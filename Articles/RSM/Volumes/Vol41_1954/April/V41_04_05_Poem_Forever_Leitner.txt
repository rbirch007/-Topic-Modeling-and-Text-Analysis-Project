[Vol41 | April 1954 | Sequence: 05 | Category: Poem | Title: Forever | Author: Leitner]
Della Adams Leitner This is the old home place, and here I come returning, worn with travel stains; Strangers to me possess these verdant fields, But something mine forever here remains. The apple trees in petaled fragrance stand, The sheltered garden pool in willow shade; The old stone fence, clothed in its ivy robe, Recalls my father's toil and what he made. I pause awhile, relive my carefree youth; I shall go on—perhaps return no more, But from this tryst with memory I shall take Comfort for sunset years—a treasure store.
