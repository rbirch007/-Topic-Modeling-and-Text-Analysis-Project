[An Appreciation to Florence Jepperson Madsen Vida F. Swenson]

An Appreciation to Slorence Yepperson ITladsen Vida F. Swenson How priceless—those rehearsals! What a privilege we have had To learn to sing together. Sister Madsen, we are glad That you have come among us here in this great latter-day To bless us with your leadership, in your most special way. That precious instrument, the voice, can lovely be—or queer. We're sure at times disturbing sounds, in discord, reached your ear, The title, Dr. Madsen, is appropriate, indeed. You know just how to cure our ills and help us to succeed. Directions you give clearly; and your method is unique. We can't help knowing what to do whenever these words you speak: "Relax your jaw; don't "punch each word; get quickly to the vowel. Don't spread the e; look pleasant; you're not happy, if you scowl. And sing the message, not just words. You've much improvement shown! Just solo voices take those parts and sound just like one voice. Now get your breath right under it. 'That combination's choice, Just pantomime uncertain notes; the audience can't tell. To spoil it, it just takes one voice, so mark your copies well. Drive carefully; and do keep well; and a good breakfast eat. Leave early so you'll be on time. Let's all be kind and sweet." Your patience was unlimited; your helpfulness, supreme. The type of singing you produce surpasses any dream. Unselfishly you've shared with us your time and talents, too. And we regret the time's so short that we can spend with you.
