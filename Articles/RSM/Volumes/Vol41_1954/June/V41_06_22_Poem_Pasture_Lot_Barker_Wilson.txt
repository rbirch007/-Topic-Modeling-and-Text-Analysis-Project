Pasture Lot
Grace Barker Wilson

This corner of the pasture used to be
Of smooth green velvet, hemmed by a pole fence
To keep sleek mares. I had not thought to see
The years could make so great a difference.

Now only brambles grow to make a place
Fit for a cottontail to hide. Here all
Else is deserted. Fence poles, for a space,
Shine silver, and wild hollyhocks grow tall.
