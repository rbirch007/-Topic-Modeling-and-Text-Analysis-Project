[Vol23 | September 1936 | Sequence: 02 | Category: Article | Title: Sitka City]
MICHAELS BUILT IN 1817 SITKA THE ENCHANTED CITY a wall. As we strolled up the board walk to the main street of the town on that long day in June, the shadows of the past were thick around us making Sitka no mere city of the present, but a city of the romantic past—a City of Enchantment. The little Russian church now, as ever, dominates the city.
Standing in the middle of the main thoroughfare, it acts as a breaker for the tides of tourists who, whenever a ship lands, pour up the little street.
Usually they scatter through the curio stores lining the street, into the Post Office, the picture studios, and down the side streets to the shore where the houses grace the small islands reached by long, lumber drives laid on pilings. But a goodly number always enter the church of St.
Michael to see the treasures of the years which have been garnered there.
