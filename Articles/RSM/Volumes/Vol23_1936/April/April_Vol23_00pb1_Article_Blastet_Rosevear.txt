By Bertha M. Rosevear

Oh the joy of Spring! When the balmy breeze Whispers softly to the trees Awake, awake, put on your garments pink, and white Help make the earth a glorious sight When every little robin's throat Swells with rapturous stirring note And everywhere is seen The Easter lily

Ah yes, 'twas in the Spring When nature, wakens from her Winter's sleep, And fragrant lilies bloom That Christ in majesty and power, Arose from out the tomb.

*Twas then that He whom men thought dead, Who was our ever living Head, Became Our Savior from the grave. Help us, oh God, to realize Thy love, and Thy great sacrifice. And as the birds return each year, When Easter lilies reappear We will remember The sacred joy of Spring.


VOL. XXIII APRIL, 1936 No. 4
