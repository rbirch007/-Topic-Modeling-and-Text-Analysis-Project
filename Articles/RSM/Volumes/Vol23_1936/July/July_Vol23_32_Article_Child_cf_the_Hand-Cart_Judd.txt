[Vol23 | July 1936 | Sequence: 32 | Category: Article | Title: Child cf the Hand-Cart Judd]
I see her keep watch by the dimming trail, Three days have passed and her tired eyes fill; But the faith within her will never fail, When lo?from the top of a distant hill Glimmering faint in the sunset's glow, A bright wool shawl waves to and fro.
I see her kneeling?words cannot tell, The sweet soul gratitude, given birth; Someone is singing, "All is Well," And she falls asleep on her bed of earth. Kind God, I thank Thee that she I can claim, As my mother?child of the hand-cart train.
