HPHE family group is a sacred one.
The welfare of each is the eter-
nal concern of the whole.
Each one,

in his life the happiness of the whole.
Salvation is collective as well as indi-

Our salvation is in a manner
wrapped up in our kith and kin.
As
Andrew Jackson approached the end
of his life he said to his family
: "My
dear children and friends, and ser-
vants, I hope and trust to meet you
all in heaven, both white and black."
The last sentence he repeated, "both
white and black."
He said this look-
ing at them with tenderest emotion.
A few days later, as the end obvious-
ly approached he said, "Heaven will
not be heaven
if
I do not meet my
wife there."
This is in harmony with the law of
life as we know it in mortality. Heav-
en will be natural and governed by
intelligent laws. Such hopes are nor-
mal to all earthly unions under wed-
lock sanctified by love.
No matter
if the marriage vow does
limit the
duration of the earthly relationship
established
in
the

reads: "To love and to cherish
till
death do us part." No one can ignore
the relationship which has once ex-
isted in mortal motherhood.
Bonds
of the tenderest character, begotten
offspring of the noblest sort—eternal
souls bound together by the ligaments
of human hearts are by nature
in-
tended
to
be
eternal.
Separation
would be
a
painful

God
designed them
to be together
eter-
nally.

President Jackson's hopes

They express the

Shall that which is noblest in

The destruction of personality is im-

She

Together the man and the woman,

They shall be
added upon and there shall be given

the

of- hus-
band and wife "in the Lord ;"
