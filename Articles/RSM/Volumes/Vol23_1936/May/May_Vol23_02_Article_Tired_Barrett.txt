[Vol23 | May 1936 | Sequence: 02 | Category: Article | Title: Tired Barrett]
A tired Mother
So shaky and worn
With wrinkled fingers
From tasks she has borne.
Tired shoulders
Forlornly they sag,
And tired footsteps
That wearily lag.
Those tired eyelids
Have hidden the tears

When heart of sorrow
Was burdened with fears.
Her tired body
So loyal and true,
Has suffered aching
No one ever knew.
Undaunted spirit
On God still depends
With prayers so faithful
Till Life's journey ends.
