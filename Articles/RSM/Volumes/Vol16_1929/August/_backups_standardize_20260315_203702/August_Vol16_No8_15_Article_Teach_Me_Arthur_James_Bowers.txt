Teach Me By Arthur James Bowers O God of the cold, gray dawning, O God of the wide, green sea, Teach me the language of nature; Teach this to me? The silence of sleeping waters, The hush of the starry skies, The voice of the dewy morning, As night birds cry. The radiant sun at noon-day, The waning moon of the night, The stars of a velvet darkness When they are bright. Waves as they break on the shoreline, Trees as they whine in a gale, Birds as they sing in an early dawn When skies are pale. The thundering roar of a cataract, The cry of a deer that is "done," And whimpering blasts of downy snow When winter's come. O God of the painted sunsets, O God of the silent moon, Teach me their songs, their voices, Teach me their tune.
