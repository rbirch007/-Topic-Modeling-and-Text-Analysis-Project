The Shadows By Alberta H. Christensen I like shadows; shadows that mock the heat Of August's noon And lure the weary traveler to sleep. All too soon The dusk of evening chases them away. I like the playful, transient shadowlets Capricious breeze Forms on the grass where sunrays slip Between the leaves. But sweeter far the moonlit images, Too frail to last, Like memories that haunt Or bless the past.
