ABSENT

By Camilla Woodbury Judd

I miss you when the morning breaks
In glory o'er the hill,
And all the world around me wakes
But you are absent still.

I miss you when the noonday sun
Shines bright on field and lea,
And when the day's long work is done
You are not here with me.

I miss you when the evening star
Comes out to light the way
For those who journey from afar—
But you have gone to stay.

I miss you when the night winds sigh
Through lonely hours till dawn,
And most of all when morning's nigh
I know that you are gone.

But though you're absent from my sight,
And though we are apart,
You dwell within my soul's delight—
Forever in my heart.
