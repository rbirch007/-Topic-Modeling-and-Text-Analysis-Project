Spring is Here By Jessie Sundwall
There's a lilt in the song of the robin, A tang in the sweet breezy air, And showing of green Over nature's great screen, Ere she showers her blossoms so rare.
There's a gay little perk to the bluebird, As he preens on the branch of the tree, And the lambkins at play Have a more cunning way, As they gambol about carelessly.
There's a dash in the stride of the schoolboy, His cap at an angle is set, He owns, if you please, The world which he sees, A man with his battles unmet.
There's a glow in the blue of the heavens, More glint to the sunshine at morn, This message is breathed, The whole earth is wreathed, Proclaiming all nature reborn.
