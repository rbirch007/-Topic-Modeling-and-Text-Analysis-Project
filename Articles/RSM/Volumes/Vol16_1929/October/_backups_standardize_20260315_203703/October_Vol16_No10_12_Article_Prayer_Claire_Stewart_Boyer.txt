Prayer By Claire Stewart Boyer
Prayer may ride on the Autumn wind And miss the gates of heaven; But to the kneeling one it gives A sort of leaven.
Prayer may stifle in a room Too suffocant for peace; But to the one whose lips move on It brings release.
And whether prayer sing in the heart of a king, Or whether a fool make plea, To each is left that blessed boon? A true humility.
Prayer may ride on the Winter wind, Nor reach the gates above; But to the soul that prays is born New hope, new love.
