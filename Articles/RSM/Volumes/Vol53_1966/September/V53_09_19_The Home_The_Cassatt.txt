"The Bath" (La Toilette) by Mary Cassatt (1845-1926), American (Art Institute of Chicago) Painting to be studied in connection with Cultural Refinement Lesson 3— "Humility Yields Strength.'' Commentary by Floyd E. Breinhoilt, Assistant Professor of Art, Brigham Young University The subject matter of art never changes. It is the way the artist "sees" it that changes. Thus the one who looks at a painting sees in it what he knows. Instead of saying, 'I know what I like,' he might better say, "I like what I know." Content matter chosen by an artist is usually something that is of value to him, something that has touched him deeply or has become a part of him. Mary Cassatt, perhaps America's most famous woman painter, was especially fond of painting mothers, babies, and children, and the tenderness shown in their relationships to each other. This painting, "The Bath," glorifies the humble task of the mother in bathing her child.
