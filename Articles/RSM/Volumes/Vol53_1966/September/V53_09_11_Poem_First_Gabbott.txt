[Vol53 | September 1966 | Page 668 | Poem | First Day at School | Mabel Jones Gabbott]
FIRST DAY AT SCHOOL Mabel Jones Gabbott Now her hands that loved her doll Reach for learning, and she looks With eager pleasure at the shelf And carefully selects her books. His hands, that baited worms on hooks, That lightly curved around a ball, Now must grapple with a pen To trace the letters short or tall. Be patient, teacher, guide these hands To want the feel of book and pen, To understand the worth of words, Their power and their beauty... Then World-wide doors of wonder and joy Will open to this girl and boy.
