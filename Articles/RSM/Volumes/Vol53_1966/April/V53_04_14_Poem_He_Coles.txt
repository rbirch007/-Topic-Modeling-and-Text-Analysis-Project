[Vol53 | April 1966 | Page 270 | Poem | "He Giveth His Beloved Sleep," | Christie Lund Coles]
COC ~ LD "HE GIVETH HIS BELOVED SLEEP"' Christie Lund Coles Let peace fall upon me Let me envision shade and sun Gently as first snow, As my hands grow still, Making a down-dipped night. Calm as a lost midnight Let me not doubt, but know. Moon above the hill. Let me recall briefly Let me sleep as a child A summer morning, cool, After ecstatic play, As dew and quiet water Losing the blinding glare In an amber pool. Of the dream-stilled day. Let me relive moments It is a simple thing to ask, Where stream-waters pass, A simple promise, Lord, to keep, Caressing the brown rocks, Saying the words in quiet prey And the spear-pointed grass.
