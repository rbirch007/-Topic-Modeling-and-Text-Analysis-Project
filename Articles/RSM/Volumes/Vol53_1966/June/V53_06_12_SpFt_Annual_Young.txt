(To be concluded) 419 42] 1965 Annual Report Growth, accomplishment, and dedicated service mark the achievements of Relief Societies throughout the world during 1965. Evidence of this was observed as General Board members visited every stake in the Church and some missions at the time of stake quarterly conferences. It was confirmed in the reports received at the General Board -office covering the works and activities of the 6,662 stake, mission, district, ward, and branch organizations located in 53 countries through the world. The influence of this work directed to the building of Latter-day Saint homes, to care of the poor and the sick, to the comforting of the downtrodden and bereaved, to uplifting and developing each individual member, and to the saving of souls has been felt by hundreds of thousands who have been recipients of its services, and of its cultural offerings, and participants in its meetings and activities. More than 12,000 new Relief Society members were enrolled during the year, swelling our ranks to 286,820 members of this world-wide sisterhood. Many other sisters who were already members of Relief Society have also been brought into greater activity in the program through the conducting of evening, in addition to daytime, sessions of Relief Society. These evening sessions have been appreciated by working mothers and single women who were unable to attend daytime sessions. More Relief Societies have also been organized on college campuses where intelligent, enthusiastic young girls are becoming imbued with the program of the organization. Organizations and members in foreign-speaking countries are rejoicing over the greater amount of program materials, lessons, standard record books, and instructions that are being made available to them in their own languages and which are greatly strengthening the work there. At the four weekly meetings of the Society to which increasing numbers of sisters have been in attendance, L. D. S. Women have been instructed in gospel principles, in ideas that exalt and ennoble womanhood, in truths that help them to achieve a peace in the midst of a troubled world. They have received practical instruction in homemaking skills and in the development of their creative abilities. Through the Priesthood coordinated course taught the forepart of the year in the work meeting and the latter part of the year in the social science meeting, they have received guidance on developing family unity through improved social relations in the home, and on applying the gospel in the lives of family members. In the literature meeting, now to be known as the cultural refinement meeting, the sisters have learned to share the wonders, wisdom, and delights of good reading with family members. Through the program of Relief Society as directed by the General Priesthood leaders of the Church, mothers have been helped to see their role with their husbands in the new family home evening program, and Relief Society leaders have been guided in so pointing the program of the organization as to help mothers be more effective in their functions related to the family home evening. A vast corps of more than 125,000 visiting teachers have continued the devoted service started in the early days of Relief Society in Nauvoo of visiting every L. D. S. Family ''to search out the poor and suffering." in fulfilling this sacred calling visiting teachers have brought inspiration through the messages they have discussed in the homes; they have comforted the homebound and aged sisters, many of whom are living in rest homes; and they have endeavored to cause all whom they have visited to know of Relief Society's interest in them which would give them a feeling of belonging. Through the divinely inspired program of Relief Society, nearly 189,000 devoted and dedicated leaders have continued to carry forward the work for which the organization was originally established and as presently directed by the presiding Priesthood of the Church. The leaders themselves have increased in their abilities and in their spirituality as they have rendered this service. Through the united efforts of leaders and members, Relief Society in 1965 fulfilled its function as an aid to the Priesthood in helping to build the kingdom.
