SUNLIT CANYON Ethel Jacobson Dazzle lies On leaf and stream— A dancing brilliance, A quicksilver gleam. Wherever the live oak Canopy parts, The sun's massed archers Loose their darts To splinter on eddy, Stone, and stem, Making a shattered Mirror of them, Reflecting each other Diamond-bright, A fluid, Shimmering bedight Landscape less of land Than light. WORDS Armoral Kent Words are such hasty things, Yet they may mean All that can ever be, All that has been. Words are such little things, Yet they may make Destinies totter, Centuries shake. Words are so uniform, Yet they may be Anything, everything, To you and me. COOK ELEC?
