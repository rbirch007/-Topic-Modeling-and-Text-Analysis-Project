Freeman Maramec, Oklahoma Mrs. Caroline Ringle Steed Washington Terrace, Utah Mrs. Ammar Elizabeth Pickett Bott Burley, Idaho IDLENESS In beautiful White Pass in Mount Rainier National Park, we passed Kautz Flood area where, in a huge valley, the trees have all died because the roots were smothered and could not get air. With all the necessary water and plant food, they died even as gardens sometimes do, too. The flood-packed debris around them suffocated them. There was no one to work the soil. They stand as mute evidence of an important law of life: without the work of cultivation for plants, trees, and man, the rot of idleness sets in to destroy.
