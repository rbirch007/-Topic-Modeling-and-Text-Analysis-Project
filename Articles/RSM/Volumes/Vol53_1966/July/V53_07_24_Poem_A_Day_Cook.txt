[Vol53 | July 1966 | Page 530 | Poem | A Day Like This | Maude O. Cook]
A DAY LIKE THIS Maude O. Cook The dark is blotting out another day; It soon will join the silent, long parade Of all the yesterdays. Time folds away Its failure and success; the dreams are laid Aside, to find fulfillment or defeat In some tomorrow Memories remain, Forevermore to share a safe retreat, Where days like this make recollection sweet.
