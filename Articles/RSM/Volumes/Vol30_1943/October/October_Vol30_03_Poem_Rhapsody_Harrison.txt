RHAPSODY Martha Fereday Harrison
O Life! I hold you tight in my two hands. Eagerly I greet the glory of the dawn. Running over I fill the measure of each day. When day is done, I dress myself in colored streamers From the setting sun and veil of evening's mist. Along the milky way in silver ship of clouds I sail away to bathe my soul In sea of stars and magic of the moon. Then travel down the Lethe River of the dark; To sleepto dreamto wait The coming of another morn.
