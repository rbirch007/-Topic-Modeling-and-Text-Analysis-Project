Acceptance

Irene T. Irving

SOMEONE has said, "Age is a state of mind." Age is also the total of life's knowledge and achievement. Its tempo, gauged to the needs of each individual, measures carefully every thought and effort toward the progress of oneself and the service he has rendered to others, bringing into balance all qualities of mind and character employed.

We must live life fully to give reason to our existence. Charity, love, faith, and tolerance are the essential qualities which strengthen our understanding.

Acceptance of life's problems, with a full attempt at solution, increases mental and emotional stature. Prayer, our definite link with God, will, when the time is right, bring enlightenment and inspiration.

Through the span of trial and error, we reach the "golden years," the sum of our learning to endure and the progress of our aims and desires for the benefit of ourselves and our fellow men. Thus, the covenant in our second estate has been fulfilled and eternity beckons.

Life's demands are strenuous, but God's plan told us long ago that they would be — thus the constant challenge to our existence.

The degree of grace employed in meeting our obligations will determine the sum of spiritual strength and dignity — the increased knowledge and love that we acquire through our sacrificial experience.

This will be the essence of our earthly existence, added unto ourselves, to endure through life eternal — a blessed privilege.
