Growing Old

Lizzie O. B. White

WHEN one grows old, he cannot realize that time is passing by so rapidly — that minutes, hours, days, and months are measured now by years.

Growing old should be a beautiful experience — a sort of sunset to a well-spent life. An instrument gets mellower with age, and the music it produces has a sweeter tone. So with man, a life attuned to God is sweeter and more meaningful as years pass by — provided it has not been spent in thinking only of himself instead of others.

There is an art in growing old — one should not have a child's mentality, but his humility. If one has lived unselfishly throughout the past, he need not dread life's few declining years, but welcome them to take an inventory of himself. If he finds that he has fallen short in deeds of usefulness, it is not too late to ask the Lord to keep his mind alert, and help him to see wherein he has erred in days gone by, and thus prepare himself to meet the test whenever the final summons comes.
