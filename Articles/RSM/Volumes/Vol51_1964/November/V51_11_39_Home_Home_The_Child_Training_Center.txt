As one looks back over a time of dire distress, often the good which emerged can be traced, although at the time it seemed impossible to find it. It may be long afterwards before gratitude fills the soul. The Lord decreed, ''In the sweat of thy face shalt thou eat bread."' This may be viewed as a curse — forcing men to work hard, or it may be viewed with gratitude as an opportunity to work. The understanding and attitude of the individual make the difference.
