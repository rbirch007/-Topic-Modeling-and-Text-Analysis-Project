Cottam YOU, who for years have seen the sown wheat sprout, The green growth head and ripen in the fall; You, who have known the bitterness of drought That withers grain when tender shoots are small, Know life; for you are of life's wheaten stock.
Yours were the dreams of land and sons and home, And yours the willing hands that cleared the rock And tree stumps; you it was who plowed the loam. Not only in God's earth were your seeds sown  When courage faltered in the hearts of men And faith waned, you, who had so often known Despair and failure, took up the reins again. Now, like stubble left at the harvest's end, When winter snows lie deep upon the field, You wait, assured that spring will always send New life, and autumn give its fruitful yield.
