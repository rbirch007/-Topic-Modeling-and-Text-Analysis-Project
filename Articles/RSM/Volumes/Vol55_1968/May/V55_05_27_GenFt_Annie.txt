[Vol55 | May 1968 | Page 361 | General Feature | Annie Bodily Roberts a Shining Example to All | ]
Annie Bodily Roberts, Layton First Ward, Layton Stake (Utah), is a shining example to all who know her. At a very early age, she began to do handwork, and she learned to knit with a nail at the age of five. She has been an expert seamstress ail her life. She has won top honors in national contests with her quilts and crocheting on two occasions, and her State and county ribbons are numerous. Her pride and joy is a large doily which won first prize in a national crochet contest. Sister Roberts has served in Relief Society as a first counselor and a visiting teacher. She seldom misses a Church meeting, and has been described by her stake president as an outstanding temple worker in the stake. She has done initiatory work for more than 12,000 souls and endowments for 1,300. She is the mother of eight children, twenty-three grandchildren, and forty-three great-grandchildren.
