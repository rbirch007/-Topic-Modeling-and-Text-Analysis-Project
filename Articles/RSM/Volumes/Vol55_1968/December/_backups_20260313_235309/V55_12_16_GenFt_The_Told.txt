There was Addie with the children's worn shoes before her. She was lovingly polishing each pair with the black from an overturned stove lid. Tears were streaming down her face. Pearl's heart nearly burst with love as she looked at her mother's face. Addie finished polishing the shoes. Then she set the table, putting on a clean cloth and, going to the makeshift cupboard, she brought to the table a sack of hard candy. Under each plate she placed two pieces. That, and the polished shoes, were the best she could do for the children's Christmas. But Pearl's eyes shone as she watched, and her mouth watered as she looked at the candy.
