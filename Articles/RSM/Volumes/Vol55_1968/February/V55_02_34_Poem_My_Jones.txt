[Vol55 | February 1968 | Page 127 | Poem | My Glasses Lucy | Woolley Jones]
MY GLASSES Lucy Woolley Jones I wonder why sight is not made to last As long as the rest of this frame, That glasses we need in order to read To me seems a pitiful shame! I lay them down here and I look for them there, And the minutes slip by and I bluster, ''Now where are those needful, but false, pesky orbs?'' I really am all in a fluster! I must bake a cake for our big ward bazaar And it's late and I can't find my glasses! Now what does that recipe say? I'm not sure; Could be sugar or spice or molasses? Glory be! If I didn't need glasses!
