[Vol55 | October 1968 | Page 783 | Poem | Suddenly Aware Iris | W. Schow]
SUDDENLY AWARE He steadied her arm, and their glances were bent For a long, searching moment, intense and intent Upon one perfect snowflake that lay on her sleeve, While awareness of joy come alive at the touch And the wonderful wonder of having so much To be shared and in which to believe. —Iris W.
