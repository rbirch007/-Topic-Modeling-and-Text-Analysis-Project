THE WEAVER

The woman's days are patterned as the sand
Where wind has blown.
From dawn's first coral to the evening's deep
Vermilion tone
She weaves a weft of footsteps on the land
Behind her flock
Where sun has made a moving warp of shade
From standing rock.
She sees the night, the lightning's jagged thrust
In black and white
And steps around the cacti bush whose blooms
Are flaming bright.
In burning summer sun or hogan's dusk
Of winter gloom
She deftly weaves the pattern of her days
Upon her loom.

—Alice Morrey Bailey
