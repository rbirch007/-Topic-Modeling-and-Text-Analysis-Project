I saw the New York skyline—the new world rising above the endless ocean, and Liberty holding her torch on high. I saw the great cities and the small towns of America. I crossed her plains and her prairies, and came at last to her fabled West, where jagged mountains cleft an azure sky. I came to Utah. For years I had sung, "Come, Come Ye Saints" and "O Ye Mountains High." Now I walked where the pioneers had trod, and saw the valley, and said, "This is the place." And in a quiet square, in the heart of a city, I saw the temple spires, I heard a mighty organ. I saw a prophet, and I heard him speak—and I was home.
