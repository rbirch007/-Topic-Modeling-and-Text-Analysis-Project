POET Dorothy J. Roberts He was made to lark the fields of night, Song out of silence in the birth of light. Hear him singing in the dark alone; He was made for longing deeper than the bone; Made for enduring in the root That we might, hungering, partake of fruit. He was made for shadow and weight of snow; He was made for darkness where lush things grow. I 50
