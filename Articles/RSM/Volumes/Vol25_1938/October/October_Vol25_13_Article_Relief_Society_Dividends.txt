[Vol25 | October 1938 | Sequence: 13 | Category: Article | Title: Relief Society Dividends]

She had many ways of getting them interested in Relief Society as she was RELIEF SOCIETY DIVIDENDS convinced that the courses in child guidance and homemaking would be of great value to them.
There were many foreign women in the ward and for them she planned special things.
There seemed to be no one whom she forgot. As time went on she became recognized as an outstanding woman. In her work for others she had developed her own latent abilities. She became a woman of courage and magnanimity.
People looked to her for leadership in public and civic affairs, and her services and sponsorship were sought. She was asked to be president of the Parent-Teachers' Association and other organizations. Her friends were legion and most loyal. The love of her neighbors 611 and co-workers she valued most highly. "Beautiful friendships tried by sun and wind Durable from the daily dust of life."
Thus through her choice of the Relief Society work she obtained what her more worldly friends feared she would forever lose, a development of personality and powers which made her a real leader, a force for good in her Church and in her community. She indeed went out to a "broader, nobler, happier life."
