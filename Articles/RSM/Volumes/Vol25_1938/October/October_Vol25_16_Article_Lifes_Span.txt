LIFE'S SPAN By Beatrice E. Linford How precious are the fleeting hours That swiftly pass away; I must not waste one moment of Each golden joyous day. I count each moment solely lost Wherein I fail to see Some beauty, or some music sweet, Some task held out to me. The short allotted time of man Seems such a fleeting space; Each moment of its rushing term Is but a short swift race.
Help me to grasp in this Prosi time All that I need to know Of life, of love, of sacrifice, Of beauty here below. And may I give some little might, Some smile, or bit of song, To ease the pain from some sad heart And help her way along.
This only can I do to pay My great unbounded debt, For joys I've had along the way; I'm searching for them yet.
