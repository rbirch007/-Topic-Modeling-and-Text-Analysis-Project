THE DESERT IS A-BLOOM

By Lydia Hall

The desert is a-bloom tonight
With stars like golden flowers,
And silver moonbeams weave their light
Through all the mystic hours.

The desert is a-bloom by day
With cactus bright and fair,
With yucca lifting spears of white
Into the crystal air.

The desert is a-bloom with dreams
Of those who journey here,
With visions born of solitude
And silence, sweet and clear.

The desert is a-bloom with God—
His presence fills the space
Where earth and sky in vastness meet
And man beholds His face.
