LUXURY

By Carla Wolfe

Luxury is not a thing of gold and silver,
Nor velvet soft, nor silk's bright sheen;
Luxury is not the gleaming satin
Of a rich and costly screen.

Luxury is not the sparkling crystal,
Nor the rarest, costliest gem;
Luxury is not fine linen
With a jeweled diadem.

Luxury is the quiet evening
When the children are in bed,
And you sit beside your husband
With his dear, familiar head

Resting near you in contentment,
While you darn his everyday socks,
And the old, reliable timepiece
On the mantelpiece softly talks.

Luxury is the baby's laughter
Ringing silver through the house;
Luxury is the morning singing
Of your busy little spouse.

Luxury is having plenty
Of the plain, substantial food;
Luxury is the peace and blessing
Of a simple neighborhood.

Luxury is not in having
All the costly things of earth;
Luxury is in the treasures
Of immeasurable worth—

Love and laughter, peace and plenty,
Health and hope and honest toil,
Friends whose hearts are true and tender—
These can never fade or spoil.
