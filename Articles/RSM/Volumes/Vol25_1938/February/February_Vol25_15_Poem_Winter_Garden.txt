WINTER GARDEN
By Vesta P. Crawford
Here now the snow is soft and smooth, A coverlet of broidered flakes; There is no trace of leaf or bud, No lily blooms, no rose awakes; And who can say that spring will come; With wonder of its sweet delight, With iris blade and hyacinth And pear tree bough of trembling white!
