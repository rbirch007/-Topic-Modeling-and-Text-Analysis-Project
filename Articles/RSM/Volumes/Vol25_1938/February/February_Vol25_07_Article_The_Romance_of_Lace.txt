Needle point lace is just what its name implies, being done with a needle and thread and consists of the loop stitch woven over single threads stretched from one point to another in accordance with the pattern followed. Both Flanders and Venice rank first in the making of their particular type of lace, but Venice, with all the popularity of the needle point variety, has not forsaken the ancient cut-work.
Were it not for the modern, yet delicate, lace-making machinery the cost of lace would make its use prohibitive to the average woman. It was not until after years of experimentation that the lace-making machinery was perfected. The struggle in perfecting it is no less romantic than the origin of lace itself. Today, the finest patterns in either needle point or bobbin lace can be copied without excessive cost, and makes available to every one this delicate finery.
