PROCRASTINATION
By Ella J. Coulam

Procrastination is the thief of time,
And steals away our days;
It robs us of our precious hours,
And leads us into maze.

It whispers softly in our ear,
"Tomorrow will suffice;
Why hurry with today's work?
Tomorrow will be nice."

But tomorrow never comes to us
As we had hoped it would;
The work we thought to do with ease
Is harder than we thought it could.

So let us grasp today's bright hours
And use them while we may;
For time once lost can ne'er return—
We only have today.
