JUNE CONFERENCE

The June Conference of the General Board of Relief Society was held in the Assembly Hall, June 2, 1932. The meeting was presided over by President Louise Y. Robison, with her counselors, Sister Amy Brown Lyman and Sister Julia A. F. Child.

The opening song was "Come, Come, Ye Saints," sung by the congregation, followed by prayer by Sister Clarissa A. Beesley.

President Robison spoke on the importance of Relief Society work during these trying times, emphasizing the need for increased charity and service to those in distress.

Sister Amy Brown Lyman addressed the conference on "Social Service Work," outlining the expanded program of the Relief Society in caring for the needy and unemployed.

Sister Julia A. F. Child spoke on "Education and Culture," stressing the importance of continuing the educational programs of the Relief Society even during economic difficulties.

Other speakers included Sister Belle S. Spafford, who spoke on "Visiting Teaching," and Sister Donna D. Sorensen, who addressed the topic of "Home Industries."

The conference concluded with the singing of "God Be With You Till We Meet Again" and the benediction by Sister Susa Young Gates.

The attendance was excellent despite the economic conditions, showing the dedication of Relief Society sisters throughout the Church.
