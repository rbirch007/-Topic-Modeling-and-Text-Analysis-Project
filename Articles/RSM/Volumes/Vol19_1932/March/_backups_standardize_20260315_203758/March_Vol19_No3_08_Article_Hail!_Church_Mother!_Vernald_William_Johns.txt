Hail! Church Mother: By Vernald William Johns Four score and ten are the years you have blessed us, Since by a Prophet to serve you were bade, Succor you've brought when ill fortune o'er took us, Hope when we faltered, cheered us when sad. Blessed in your labor of kindly devotion, Years have brought wisdom and grace to your brow; From every rank and from every station, Lady of Mercy, we honor you now. Called to go forth and bring aid to the suffering, Greater than dreamed was the task that you had: Oft prayers and smiles were more needed than clothing, Work was required more often than bread; Literature, science, religion, you study, Character, knowledge and growth is your aim; Youthful in vision you seek work and beauty, Lady of Mercy,: Woman of Culture, Old in experience, In vision full young,— Hail to you, Mother, On ninetieth birthday, By all your children Your praises are sung.
