Fish Lake at Night By Ethel Bean Andrew
The pale golden moon in the sky, Makes a path of shimmering light As it ripples over the wavelets, On this cool September night.
And the glittering stars in the deep, dark, blue, Seem friendly enough to speak; While the waves softly croon us a lullaby As they lap the shore at our feet.
And on the hill behind us, The cabin, painted white, With the light from its open windows Gleaming far into the night.
The quaking-aspens round us Shake their leaves as if they would cry; While across the lake, on the farther side, Goes a motor-boat chugging by.
