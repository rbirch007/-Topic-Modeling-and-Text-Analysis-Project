MISS LENAjMADESIN PHIL-
LIPS, president of the National
Council of Women,

in

devotion to the cause of the Council
and to the Chicago projects by
re-
fusing a salary of $5,000 a year for
her services, and offering to do the
work without pay. We quote a let-
ter she wrote to President Robison
as we are sure our readers
will be
glad
to know |how fine a character
they have
in their president.
"My Dear Mrs. Robison:
"I have given careful consideration to
the motion passed by the Board of Direc-
tors at the meeting held here on Decem-
ber
1st concerning my serving as Exec-
utive Director
for our Chicago projects,
with
the
understanding
that
the money
budgeted

the
salary of
such
Exec-
utive
Director
(approximately
$5000
a
year)
be paid me as

"Due to the unusual character of work
involved,
I
believe our
Executive Com-
mittee
feels
that
this work
will best be
carried by me.
It
is
difficult to
find an
Executive Director who can now
easily
take hold of our plans, touching as they
must
our
member
organizations
in
so
many ways.
I
realize
this
difficulty and

counsel and

operation

Executive
Committee,

"Appreciative as I am, however, of this

I

from the Council a salary for this work.
I accepted the presidency because of my

The best of my thinking and

"Cordially,
"(Signed)
