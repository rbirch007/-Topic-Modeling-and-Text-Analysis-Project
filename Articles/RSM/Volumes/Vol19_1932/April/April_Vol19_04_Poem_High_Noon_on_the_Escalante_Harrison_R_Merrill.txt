HIGH NOON ON THE ESCALANTE

By Harrison R. Merrill

The sun beats down with blazing heat
Upon the red-rock walls,
And silence, like a winding sheet,
Over the canyon falls.

No breath of wind stirs leaf or blade,
No ripple breaks the stream;
The very shadows seem afraid
To break the desert's dream.

The lizard on his rocky throne
Lies motionless and still,
As if the desert's heart of stone
Had crushed his restless will.

But in this land of burning sand
And silence, stark and deep,
There beats a pulse we understand—
The heart that does not sleep.

For God is in the blazing sun,
The rocks, the stream, the sod;
And when the day of toil is done,
We commune here with God.
