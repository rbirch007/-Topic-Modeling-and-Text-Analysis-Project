PLOWING

By THERON LUKE

I love to follow the plow in spring
When the earth lies brown and still,
And the furrows curl like a silver ring
Round the shoulder of the hill.

I love the touch of the good clean loam
As it crumbles rich and deep,
And the call of the meadow lark for home
When the shadows lengthen and creep.

I love the smell of the new-turned sod
In the hush of the dying day,
For I seem to walk very close to God
When I follow the furrow's way.
