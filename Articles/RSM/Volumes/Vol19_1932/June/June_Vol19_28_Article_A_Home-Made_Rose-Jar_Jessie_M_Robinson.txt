A Home-Made Rose-Jar
By Jessie M. Robinson

A rose-jar is one of the most delightful of home accessories. It brings the fragrance of the garden indoors and preserves the memory of summer's beauty through the long winter months.

To make a satisfactory rose-jar, gather the petals when the roses are at their best—just before they reach full bloom. Choose a clear, sunny morning after the dew has dried, but before the heat of the day has drawn out too much of the fragrance.

Pick off the petals and spread them on newspapers in a shady, airy place to dry. When they are crisp and dry, but not brittle, they are ready to use.

For each quart of dried rose petals, use one ounce of orris root (which can be purchased at any drug store), one-half ounce of whole cloves, one-half ounce of whole allspice, and one tablespoon of coarse salt.

Mix all ingredients thoroughly and pack in a tight jar or crock. Cover closely and let stand for six weeks, stirring occasionally.

At the end of this time the rose-jar will be ready for use. It may be kept in small bowls about the house, or in little bags to place among linens.

The fragrance will last for years if the jar is kept tightly covered when not in use.
