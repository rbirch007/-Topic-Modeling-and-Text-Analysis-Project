THE JOSHUA TREE

Standing alone in the desert waste,
Gaunt, grotesque, and weird,
With arms uplifted as if in haste
To catch what never appeared.

Yet brave and strong through the years it stands,
Though the burning sands blow free,
And it lifts to God its supplicant hands—
The lonely Joshua tree.

So I would stand in my desert hour,
When the way seems drear and long,
With faith to wait for the cooling shower,
And a heart attuned to song.
