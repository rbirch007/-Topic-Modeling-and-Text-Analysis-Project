Procrastination
By Marion Oakleigh
EMILY BROWN stopped her car at the gate and looked across the snow drifts that had piled high in the half-mile lane leading toward the little house in the middle of the field, which was the home of Selma Oleson. She let the engine purr softly as she decided on her course of action.
"I really should go see Selma," she said aloud, "but I don't believe I can make it through the drifts with the car."
"I could walk in ten minutes," she reasoned to herself, and I know I promised Selma months ago, that I'd come and I can't get rid of that lonely, hungry look she had in her eyes the day I picked her up on the road and let her out at the gate."
