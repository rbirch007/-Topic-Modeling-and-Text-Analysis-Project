Northern Hemisphere: First Meeting, January 1970 Southern Hemisphere: June 1970 OBJECTIVE: The Latter-day Saint woman strives to strengthen her testimony that Joseph Smith is a Prophet and that The Church of Jesus Christ was restored through him. INTRODUCTION One of the inspired documents of the latter days was written by President John Taylor at the time he was a member of the Council of the Twelve. (D&C 1844 ed.; Doctrine and Covenants Commentary, Hyrum M. Smith and Janne M. Sjodahl, Rev. Ed., p. 855.) During the remainder of this year, we shall have an opportunity to consider' the great truths found in that scripture, Doctrine and Covenants, Section 135. When a man speaks or writes by the Holy Ghost, it becomes scripturethe will, mind, word, and voice of the Lord, "and the power of God unto salvation." (D&C 68:4-5.) When imbued with the Spirit of the Lord a person can know the _ truth uttered by that Spirit. In this manner the truth is revealed and known by the honest in heart. Ultimately, this is the final test of religious truth. (D&C 50:19- 22.) Measured by this standard, the member of the Church may know that Elder Taylor was under the inspiration of the Holy Ghost when he _ penned the document about the martyrdom of Joseph and his brother Hyrum Smith. (D&C, Section eso. ) By his' apostolic calling Elder Taylor was qualified for this work and, also, because he and Elder Willard Richards, later to be a Counselor to President Brigham Young, were with the martyred brothers on June 27, 1844, at Carthage Jail, Illinois.
