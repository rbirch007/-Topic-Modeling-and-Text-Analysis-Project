ART "Advice to a Young Artist'? By Honoré Daumier (1808-1879) French, National Gallery of Art, Washington, D. C., Gift of Duncan Phillips. Painting reproduced in the June 1969 Relief Society Magazine, page 447. Commentary on art selection by Floyd E. Breinholt, Chairman, Department of Art, Brigham Young University. Honore Daumier at the age of seven decided to be an artist. To gain background for his chosen field, he frequented the poorer quarters of Paris and haunted the corridors of the Louvre. He studied Rembrandt and Michelangelo, nature, and the manners and customs of his period. Although he is better known as a lithographer and political cartoonist, it has been said that he was one of the greatest painters of the 19th century.
