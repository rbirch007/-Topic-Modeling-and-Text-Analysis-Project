The wisdom of the General Board in disbanding the central chorus in favor of promoting local choruses is reflected in the growth in the number and size of Relief Society Singing Mothers choruses throughout the Church. Today there are 3,612 Singing Mothers choruses, with more than 51,496 women participating. The General Board pays tribute to the outstanding choristers, both local and General Board, who, with skill and devotion, have used their talents to help develop choruses that have attracted wide attention and much _ favorable

We acknowledge with gratitude the work of the talented accompanists and the instrumentalists whose work is so important in the success of a chorus. In today's world where spiritual and cultural activities are so greatly needed, when the home needs the joyous uplift of good music, when mothers need the solace of song as they contend with the problems of life, Relief Society must press forward with its glorious program of congregaguished organizations and broadcasting stations to our choruses to present special concerts or protional and choral singing. This we know will be pleasing unto the Lord.
