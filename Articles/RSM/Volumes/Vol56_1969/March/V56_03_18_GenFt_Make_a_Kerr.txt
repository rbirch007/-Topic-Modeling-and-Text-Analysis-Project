Spring Blossoms Defy
The Winter Snow

■ Although springtime seems to arrive later and later each year, certain lovely blossoms defy the winds and snow, and, sometime in March in some areas of the Northern Hemisphere, their pretty blossoms offer hope for the new and welcome season. Many of these first blossoms appear on fruit trees, such as the apricot and quince blossoms. Forsythia blossoms traditionally bloom before the leaves. The lovely Red Emperor tulips create a radiant sight as they come into bloom long before other flowers greet the warm spring sunshine.
