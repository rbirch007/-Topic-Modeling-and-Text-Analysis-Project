And one of the greatest problems faced by the prophets has been to get the people to listen to and to follow their counsel. Since the opening of this last and final dispensation of the gospel, all of our prophets have stressed the importance of providing ordinances of salvation to ourselves, the living, and also to the dead. The Savior taught this principle, stressing the fact that no one could enter the kingdom of heaven without being born again of water and of the spirit. (John 3:5.) At the end of his earthly ministry, he placed the greatest possible stamp of authoritativeness on what he had taught and exemplified by giving up his life, not only to provide the basic requisitevictory over deathbut to open the way to eternal membership in his Father's heavenly kingdom to all who have the desire and the will to attain to it. This is his Churchand through his prophets he has continued to emphasize the great joy and rewards that come through following the path he has charted.
