[Missionaries to Dinner Joan Nelson Ahlman]

These are the unprofitable servants of the parable of the talents whose condemnation is greater than the loss of the talent. Their condemnation is greater than they would have received had they not been given the gift. There are those who reject the gifts so willingly given by a loving Father, those whose self-afflicted blindness, whose spiritual unawareness prevent their accepting a glorious opportunity to use the gift in perfecting themselves and in helping build the kingdom of God. Relief Society is a marvelous gift given by our Heavenly Father to his daughters which some of the unseeing women of the Church have not yet accepted. The loss to themselves is great and to their families, husband, and children, but the loss to the building of the kingdom of God on earth is equally great. Failure to accept the God-given gifts results in failure to live most abundantly. Each gift is spiritual if used to elevate, to build, to make the world better. No gift is a small one. Its size is determined by the receiver. All gifts are given that each might contribute to the work of the Lord. L. W. M. Verda F. Burton Myrtle R. Otson Elna P. Haymond Mary R. Young Celestia J.
