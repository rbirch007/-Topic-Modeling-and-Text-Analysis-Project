INVENTORY: A SNOWBALL TREE Soon the year will end and I shall take An inventory of all blessings sent My way. For future scantier years I'll make A special note of how the snowball bent Its thick-leafed branches toward my kitchen sink; How leaf-green newness turned to blossomed snow And lush cool shade, and lingered then, I think, Long past the scheduled time for leaves to go, Lingered while the sun poured red and rust And amber gold through once-green leaf-veins. Day After day I gloried in them, thinking they must Fall soon. For all things earthy go the way Of transient growth and bloom and then to dust. Only the memories of their beauty stay; Today my snowball tree, etched in frost, Adds to this blessing and will not be lost.
