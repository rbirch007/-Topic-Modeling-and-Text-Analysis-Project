Working With Knit Fabrics Myrene T. Alvord O) Available on the market today are lovely knit fabrics for home sewing. Knit fabrics can be purchased by the tube or the yard. Many are like sweater material, although made by machine. Knowing a few tricks in handling these materials can open up a whole new world of fashion. One of the professional touches you can add to your outfit is to edge it with embroidery and/or crocheting. To be most effective, this should be done from the Same yarn as your sweater is knit from. To do this, you must unravel yarn from your tube, which requires patience and know-how. Most knit tubes are made from four separate strands of wool or yarn, each of which has been machine locked. These must be unraveled into four separate balls. Never cut the tubing to unravel the yarn. At the top of most tubes (not the ribbing end) there is a rough edge which you must pick through before you reach the regular yarn. After this top edge has been carefully picked off, unravel one strand all around the tube and lay the small ball aside. Pick up the next until you have four small balls of yarn. Then pick up the first ball and repeat this process until you have enough yarn for the work you wish to do.
