ART ''The Blind Girl' by John Everett Millais (1829-1896) English (September 1969 Relief Society Magazine, p. 688.) Commentary on art selection by Floyd E. Breinholt, Chairman, Department of Art, Brigham Young University. (September 1969 Relief Society Magazine, p. 689.) What do you think of when you see this picture? "The Blind Girl" is not difficult to understand. It could be said to be true to nature with an obvious message. Try asking a group of people of any age to look at the painting and tell the story. Abstract the story into one word and see how often it will be "service." John Everett Millais was a precocious child. He was recognized as having great talent at age four. At seven he was drawing amazing likenesses of family and friends. At eleven he was accepted into the Royal Academy as its youngest student and was later elected to membership. Earlyin his career as an artist he and several friends formed a group known as Pre-Raphaelites, who set principles of painting somewhat contrary to the accepted manner of painting of the time.
