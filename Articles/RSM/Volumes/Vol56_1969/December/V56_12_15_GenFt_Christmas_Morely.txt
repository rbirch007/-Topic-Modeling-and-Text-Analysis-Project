I look out across our valley. Beautiful white snow covers all with its frosty coverlet, Having fallen last evening, Making everything a white fairyland. Looking to the east, I see the church spire, beautiful and tall, Reaching toward God. Inviting us to reach also, and find him, Ever willing to help us in our upward climb. Beyond the spire to the east, the magnificent Wasatch Mountains, Rising in their stately beauty and majesty, Standing tall as protecting warriors, overlooking our lovely valley. As I turn my gaze westward, I find the guarding Oquirrhs, With their array of towers and blinking lights As ever present as those on the east. Surrounded by nature's barriers, our valley is serene and wonderful Filled with loving friends and neighbors Who feel the love of Christ in their hearts and homes. We are thankful for the Christmas season, the beautiful lights, trees, and carols, Reminding us again of the light brought into this world By the birth of Jesus Christ. May we ever feel the warmth and security of peaceful homes in a beautiful valley And be able to feel this love for others Expressed in the celebration of Christmas. Marcella H.
