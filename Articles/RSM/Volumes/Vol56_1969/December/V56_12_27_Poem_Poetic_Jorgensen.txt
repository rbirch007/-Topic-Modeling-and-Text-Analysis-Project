Jorgensen 943 VISITING TEACHER MESSAGE  Truths to Live By (Correlated With the Family Home Evening Manual, 1968-69) Message 6Faith in Christ Alice Colton Smith Northern Hemisphere: First Meeting, March 1970 Southern Hemisphere: August 1970 OBJECTIVE: To know that faith needs constant nourishment. He was the ideal to many: tall, handsome, eloquent, and congenial. A mighty speaker, he persuaded many to join the Church. Then one day, someone else was given a position that many thought would be his. Gradually a sour note crept into his conversations. He grew less and less active in the Church. Long before he died the mighty voice no longer thundered of faith in God. The warm personality became an unhappy critic. His influence dwindled until most people forgot that he had ever been a great leader in his community. Many are the reasons for lost faith in God. Each has its subtle poison. I'o avoid such loss, faith, like all knowledge, must be constantly renewed and expanded. How do we nourish faith? How do we keep it alive, vibrant with spiritual health? For many, faith is buffeted by winds of doubt as well as attacked by subtle poisons. How do we protect ourselves against such evils? Who can explain this better than Alma who once worked so hard to destroy faith? 944 In a beautiful simile, Alma graphically explains faith's patternof growth.
