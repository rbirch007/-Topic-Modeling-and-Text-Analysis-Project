But the shine was soon gone when a handful of sand lit on her head and sifted through the golden curls. Angie looked around to find who had thrown the sand. Standing behind her were three Japanese children about her own age. Their dark slanted eyes looked strange to her. Angie hadn't seen anyone with eyes like theirs back in Salt Lake City where she used to live. The children's ebony hair hung straight down and was chopped off right below their ears. "Hi! What's your name?" chirped five-year-old Angie, who was happy to see someone to play with. The Japanese children didn't answer but stepped into Angie's sandbox and began sifting the sand through their fingers. In a few seconds she reappeared with an armful of brightly colored tin buckets and shovels, and some tablespoons she had _ borrowed from the kitchen. The Japanese children grinned and giggled as they each received their allotted playthings from Angie. One little girl, whom the others called Keiko, tenderly caressed Angie's bare white legs which emerged from the ruffled bottom of her pink sunsuit. The girl seemed to marvel at the skin that was at least two shades lighter than her own. Large mountains and valleys began to take form in the sand pile as the foursome repeatedly filled and emptied their buckets. A miniature car was buried and uncovered.
