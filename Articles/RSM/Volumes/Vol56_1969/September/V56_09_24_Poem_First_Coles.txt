[First Day of School Christie Lund Coles]

Ribbons of roads, made by tractors, steam shovels, and trucks, wound around the mountains and through the valleys. "Let's make a farm," cried Angie. "My uncle has a farm. I'll go get some cows." The screen door banged behind her as she skipped into the house. She was greeted with "ohs" and "ahs" when she returned with a plastic bag full of horses, cows, chickens, ducks, pigs, sheep, dogs, and cats. The girl named Keiko fingered a cow and repeated a strange word Angie couldn't understand. "No, it's a cow," Angie corrected. But Keiko continued to say the strange word. Not being able to quite understand what this meant, Angie decided to build a field for the cows. She ran to the tall maple tree which stood at the end of the long back yard. There she picked up dry twigs and sticks. When she had all her chubby hands could carry, she returned to the sand pile. One by one, she pushed the ends of the sticks into the sand next to each other. Her' three playmates joined her in the task, and soon an area about a foot in diameter was encircled by the fence. "The ducks need a pond to swim in," declared Angie as she ran for the sprinkler hose. She tugged the hose to the sand pile and carefully placed it in a ravine. Back she ran to the water tap on the side of the house. FIRST DAY OF The Universal Language As the water came on full force, the end of the hose flipped up, drenching the make-believe land in an unexpected shower. The three Japanese friends were also caught in the sudden sprinkle, but screams of delight and laughter escaped their mouths as the water cooled their skin.
