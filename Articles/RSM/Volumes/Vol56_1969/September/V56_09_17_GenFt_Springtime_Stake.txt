["Springtime in the Park"  Unique Bazaar in Indianapolis Stake]

The items were unusually attractive and included: 4 turtles (made out of round rocks and glued together and painted); 10 handkerchief dolls (top half made and decorated from felt, the bottom half was a handkerchief); 20 dress-up formals (these were sold quickly, materials were scraps of silks and satins and discarded curtains); 3 cow-girl outfits; 8 soldier banks (made from detergent bottles); 20 wall pictures; 6 large bulletin boards (shaped like animals); 4 large playhouses (big enough for little girls to play in, made from cardboard boxes, painted and with curtains added); 10 dress-up hats (made from clorox bottles and net); 12 wigs (made from yarn); 20 toy guns (cut from wood and painted brown); 6 doll houses (made from garment boxes and decorated); 20 sets of doll furniture; 15 paper dolls and clothes; 20 purses, and many other attractive articles. As a theme on Hawaii, the girls were invited inside a thatched hut (an old pasteboard box covered with strips of cut paper and painted). Each girl received a skirt, lei, and flowers for her hair. Variety of smiles and costumes in the dress-up booth.
