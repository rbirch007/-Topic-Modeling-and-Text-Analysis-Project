When a ceramic piece is chipped, it shows the white ceramic under the glaze. If bread clay, which is as hard as china, is chipped, it is not so noticeable as the clay is colored through and through with the same color as its surface. The first and absolute rule, Sister Alvord says, in working at the art of bread clay is extreme cleanliness.
