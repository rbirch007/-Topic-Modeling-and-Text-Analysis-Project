SPIRITUAL LIVING The Doctrine and Covenants Preview of Lessons for 1969-70 Elder Roy W. Doxey The Relief Society year 1969-70 brings to an end this study of the Doctrine and Covenants begun in 1957. These lessons began with events prior to the organization of the Church in April 1830, and end at the beginning of the pioneer period of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. The lessons again are written about a central idea from the revelations. It is hoped that during the year each Relief Society member will finish reading all of the Doctrine and Covenants to complete the assigned reading. The lessons for 1969-70 begin with Doctrine and Covenants Section 131 (May 1843), a part of which was studied last year with Section 136 (January 1847). Only two of the five sections for study were given to the Church by the Prophet Joseph Smith, the others were given by Oliver Cowdery, John Taylor, and Brigham Young. All of the sections in the Doctrine and Covenants from Section 1 through 133 represent the revelations and writings of the Prophet. The latest revelation is Section 132, carrying the date July 12, 1843; whereas, the last Section, 133, was received on November 3, 1831, but since it is the Appendix, it appears at the end of the Prophet's revelations. Apparently, Section 134, dated August 17, 1835, was placed in its position because it was not written by the Prophet, but by Oliver Cowdery, though later accepted by Joseph Smith. After the martyrdom of Joseph and Hyrum Smith, Elder John Taylor wrote Section 135, an historical statement regarding the martyrdom. The final section in the Doctrine and Covenants is a revelation received in 1847 by President Brigham Young, President of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles, about one year before he was sustained as the President of the Church. This fact indicates the authority and power of the President of the Twelve in the interim between the death of the President and the appointment of the new President. In this interim period, he presides over the Council of the Twelve which quorum is equal in authority to the First Presidency. (D&C 107:23-24.)

 Lessons for the year 1969-70 bring messages of faith and assurance of the divinity of the work instituted, under God, by the Prophet Joseph Smith. The greatness of his mission and the work performed by him are considered in four lessons. An opportunity is available through these lessons to know of the signs which indicate the nearness of the second coming of Jesus Christ. The final lesson of the series is devoted to the great theme of the dispensation—the building of Zion upon the earth. In another work the author of the lessons wrote the following: It is the writer's firm conviction that the privilege of participating in the building up of Zion in preparation for the glorious advent of the Lord depends upon how well we follow the divinely appointed latter-day leaders and hearken to the voice of the Spirit. He who accepts the prophets, seers, and revelators and constantly prays for the sanctifying influence of the Holy Ghost will be able to say with President Brigham Young, "I live and walk in Zion every day" and to recognize as he recognized, that '"'... Thousands of others in this Church and kingdom... Carry Zion with them." But this is not all. He who walks in Zion—in purity of heart—today shall build the city of Zion, in fact, tomorrow, or at the very least, bequeath to his posterity the qualities of godliness that will enable them to build it. (Roy W. Doxey, Zion in the Last Days, Salt Lake City; Ist ed., Olympus Publishing Company, 1965, pp. 81-82.) The 1969-70 series of spiritual living lessons have been given the following titles and objectives: Lesson 97—Eternal Marriage (Reading Assignment: Doctrine and Covenants, Sections 131:1-4; 132.) Northern Hemisphere: First Meeting, October 1969 Southern Hemisphere: March 1970 Objective: The Latter-day Saint woman should understand the truth that a marriage to endure after this life must be performed in a house of the Lord, and seek to attain an eternal marriage. Lesson 98—Events Associated with the Second Coming of Christ (Reading Assignment: Doctrine and Covenants, Section 133.) Northern Hemisphere: First Meeting, November 1969 Southern Hemisphere: April 1970 Objective: The Latter-day Saint woman will resolve to prepare herself for the second coming of the Savior. Lesson 99—Government and Laws (Reading Assignment: Doctrine and Covenants, Section 134.) Northern Hemisphere: First Meeting, December 1969 Southern Hemisphere: May 1970 Objective: The Latter-day Saint woman seeks to uphold the law and work within the law in seeking any desired change. Lesson 100—Joseph Smith Evaluated (Reading Assignment: Doctrine and Covenants, Section 135.)
