[Vol56 | May 1969 | Page 363 | Poem | Perception Grace | Diane Jessen]
PERCEPTION I walked today with my children three In the quiet land where I wished to be. I left the dishes at home in the sink, For I needed space to breathe and think. Three fragile voices sang delight In a miniature waterfall, silvery-bright, Exclaimed at leaves, falling one by one, And praised the warmth of the autumn sun. I walked today with my children three And saw things my eyes alone could not see. Grace Diane Jessen
