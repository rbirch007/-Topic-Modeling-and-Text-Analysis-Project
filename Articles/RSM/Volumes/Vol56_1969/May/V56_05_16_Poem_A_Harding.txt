A TRIBUTE I'm just a visiting teacher, I heard a sister say, But, oh, how golden was the key that she had used today. It had opened doors of comfort and doors of hopefulness For lonely hearts and troubled minds and brought them happiness. A sweetly-worded message she had humbly left inside, And every home she visited seemed sweetly purified. No idle words of gossip, no ugly thought was left to scar No wonder God was pleased as he watched her from afar. So she is ''just a visiting teacher,'' how little does she know How valuable, how welcome her monthly visits grow! As she turns the key of service that fits in every lock Of every precious sister whom she visits on her block. Bernice Cheshire Harding
