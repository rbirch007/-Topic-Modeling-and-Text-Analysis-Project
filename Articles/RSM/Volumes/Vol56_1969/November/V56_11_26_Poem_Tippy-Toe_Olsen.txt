Time for play and time for song. Later, days seemed even longer When I wished for them to pass. I yearned to be eighteen, and spent Some time before a looking glass. Days turned into years quite slowly, Then they started turning fast. Soon I wanted to restrain them As they left me in their past. Now the days are short as hours... Rapid come and rapid go. Weeks are briefer all the time, Passing by on tippy-toe. Pearle M. Country Club Drive Mesa, Arizona 85201 THE REACHES The sea rolls over rubbing a briny back on the shore. Small bubbles burst with a sigh. Wind whirls through the treetops, and full of presence leaves chatter. Thunder rips through a gather of clouds unleashing the rain.
