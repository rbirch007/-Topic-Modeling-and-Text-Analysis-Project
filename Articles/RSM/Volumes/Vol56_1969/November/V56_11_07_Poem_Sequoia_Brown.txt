And I have seen Ponderosa in rich mountain loam, Grow great in Stature monumental high. But I have also seen cedar on a desert waste, Stand firm and Proud despite the arid land. And pine trees clinging to a craggy granite ledge, Grace the Spot in beauty where they stand. And so it is with man. No preference is given. It matters not his heritage nor Place of birth.... God gives to him whose courage braves the storms, The gift to beautify the Spot assigned to him on earth. Alda Larson Brown 821 EDITORIAL / ''We Have Seen His Star." Now when Jesus was born in Bethlehem of Judaea... There came wise men from the east... Saying... We have seen his star... And are come to worship him. (Matthew 2:1-2.) @ To the wise men of the east, whose search took them over mountains and deserts to the hills of Judaea, the star was a sign of the birth -of Jesus, but they were looking, also, for a greater brightness, a more glorious illumination. With the Savior's birth, the plan of salvation was given again to the earth ''for it pleased the Father that in him should all fulness dwell."' We, too, in our time and place, have seen the star, but have we participated in its full radiance? To each and all of us there comes a spiritual search for an application in our own lives of the message of Christmas. Beset with many interests and urgent responsibilities, some of us who know the meaning of the star may have difficulty in realizing its enduring splendor.
