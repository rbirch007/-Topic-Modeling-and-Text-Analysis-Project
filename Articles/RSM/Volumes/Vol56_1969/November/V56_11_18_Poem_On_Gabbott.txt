(To be continued) ON DOORS CLOSING AND OPENING A new door does not always open wide Immediately when the familiar door clicks Shut; and so one stands lost, inside The unfamiliar loneness till the pricks Of shutters on the retina adjust And shadows form in new dimensions like Or unlike what is gone. The question: must One hurdle forward, seek another dike To satisfy this need that one be used, Or stand and wait, slowly gathering strength Until reason rises, unconfused And in perspective; and we learn at length That doors within the mind must open first Before the heart accepts a new sunburst. Mabel Jones Gabbott 848 (=a 8, FROM THE FIELD Z All material submitted for publication in this department should be sent through the stake Relief Society presidents, or mission Relief Society supervisors.
