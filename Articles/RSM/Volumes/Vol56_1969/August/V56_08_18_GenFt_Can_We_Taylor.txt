[Can We Afford Not to be Happy? Celestia J. Taylor]

I finally heard the faucet rattle and screech, and I knew the dishes had been rinsed. So I turned off the light from the television and rose from my chair, straightening out my wrinkled housedress. I met Kimberly as she sauntered into the hallway. She stopped when she saw me. The chiffon hung limply from her shoulders. Her hair streaked in loose shreds from her forehead. I hesitated when I looked into her eyes. They were narrowed and stern. I could not utter a word. My throat seemed so stopped up. "Well, I got home," she said coolly. "I'm in by midnight." She was tired. I could tell by the shadow of darkness beneath her eyes. "I hope you're satisfied." My heart pounded. "Did you have a good time?" I asked cautiously. "Yes," she turned toward the stairway. "But I'm really tired." At the stairs she stopped and looked back at me as I stood half disheveled in the hallway. We made a grand pair, I with my housedress and she with her streaking makeup and flattened ringlets. I was surprised to hear her voice soften. "Mom, I'm almost glad you didn't let me go to the party." Her limp arm reached for the banister. "I guess I'm _ really too tired to go. It's been a big day.". "Yes it has," I said softly. "Yes, it's been a big day for both of us." I was smiling.
