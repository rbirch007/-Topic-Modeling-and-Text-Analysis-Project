Jewels
A Roman queen in ancient days, 'tis told, While gazing on some treasures finely wrought, In casques antique, her courtly friends had brought: Long ropes of pearls, -and rings, and combs of gold— That from the glittering sight she turned and stood With tender arms clasped 'round two lovely boys, "These are my jewels," she said, "my precious joys— No crown so bright, so rich as motherhood." Oh Vanity! Your jewelled caskets close, Nor tempt with shining brilliants to ensnare Fair maids, but rather may they wisely choose To nestle in their arms love's gifts, and there Stand firm as Rome's fair queen, Cornelia, stood For joyous life and glorious motherhood.
Annie Wells Cannon.
