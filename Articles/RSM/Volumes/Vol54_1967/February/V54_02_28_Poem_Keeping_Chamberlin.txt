[Vol54 | February 1967 | Page 104 | Poem | Keeping Summer | Enola Chamberlin]
KEEPING SUMMER Enola Chamberlin When grapes were ripe, and purple plums, And warm winds swayed the grain and grass, I peeled and pitted, cooked and sealed The summer up in glass. And now with snow to claim the fields And cold wind barking at the door, I break the seals and summer lives In scent and taste once more. 1
