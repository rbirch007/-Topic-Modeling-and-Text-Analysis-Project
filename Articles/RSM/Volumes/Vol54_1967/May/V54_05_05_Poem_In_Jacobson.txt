[In High Country Ethel Jacobson]
IN HIGH COUNTRY

The brisk, rare
Crystal air
Of timberline is washed and dried,
Polished, burnished till it stings
Eyes and lungs, so close it brings
Shimmering distant cliffs, so near
You can see the
Granite grain and crystal clear
Lake water, blue as winter sky.

Up here the world
Is cleanly furled
In space. The mind
Can think, can find
Its way through problems
Tangled, knotted
In the lower, thicker air.
Up here, unspotted
By the world's confusion,
Thoughts can dare
To seek conclusion.

♦ Ethel Jacobson
