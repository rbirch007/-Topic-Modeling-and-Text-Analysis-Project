[Daybreak Alda L. Brown]

Yes, our garden was a lot of work, but the rewards were worth all the work, not only in our harvest of good things to eat, but in the companionship the children and I experienced by this family project. There is real happiness and feeling of individual contribution from working together. I feel the children learned a little more about responsibility and work, and learned from watching a seed grow to maturity and how to harvest it. As soon as frost turned our garden into a mass of dead vines and plants, the children were already talking about the garden we would have next year, always ending with the comment from Dean, "But, please, Mom, let's try to get the rows a little straighter."
