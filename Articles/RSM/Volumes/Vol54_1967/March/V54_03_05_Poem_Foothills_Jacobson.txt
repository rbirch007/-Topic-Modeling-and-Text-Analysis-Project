FOOTHILLS IN SPRING Ethel Jacobson What can I call you, hills, But jocund—where lark song spills And feathery branches fan Against this cloudless span Of hyacinth blue? What can I call you, hills, where jonquil laughter rings And a hidden waterfall sings? Doves coo, Rabbits kick up their heels, And a sweet thunder steals From root to sunlit crown till each out-reaching bough Is transfigured now In a thousand lilting petals. A jaunty robin settles On one limb. Noisily, in the pond, Young raccoons splash and swim. And everywhere is the green excitement of grasses Up every slope and cranny, while From wide-arching Oaks come squirrel talk and tree-frog trills.
