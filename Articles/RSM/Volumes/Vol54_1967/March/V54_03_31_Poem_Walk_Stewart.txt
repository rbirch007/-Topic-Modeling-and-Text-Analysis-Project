[Vol54 | March 1967 | Page 236 | Poem | Walk Lonely, Walk Still Margery | S. Stewart]
Walk Lonely... Walk Still Margery S. Stewart Beneath three trees In a snow-filled wood, Is the deep scar A kneeling man makes. A little way back circle Tire tracks of his car. He came, making shuffling But long strides back, Write purpose and a tall man. Questions in the snow, Jew? Christian? Moslem? I lack The sure details... I only know That a man knelt here, A little while ago.
