Where's Mother? By H. L. Reid When father comes home at the close of day, From the office, or road, or the field of hay, He puts out the team, or drives in the car, Then enters the house, leaving the door ajar, And asks, '"Where's Mother?" As the setting of the sun is drawing nigh, Sis comes home from her work at the High. She giggles and iaughs with the crowd at the gate, Then enters the house, she knows she's late, "O! Where's Mother?" Bill comes home, gaunt and slim From his work on the track, or perhaps in the Gym. He bounds in the house, leaving wide open the door, Throws his cap and his books, upon the floor, "O! Where's Mother?" Then the kiddies come honie, each in his way, As chicks to the roost at the close of day, They find father, sister, and brothers all, But yet each echoes that little call, "O! Where's Mother?" If she should chance to step out of the way, Or lie down to rest for a minute some day, Though nothing is needed, there's nowhere to go Yet everyone seems to just want to know, "O! Where's Mother 2?" When Mother's gone the "home" isn't there, Of course, there's the house and the garden fair, But amid it all there's an empty sting, The spirit of home! Unless Mother's there.
