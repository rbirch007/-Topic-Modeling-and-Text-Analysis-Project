Night By the late Margaret P. Bingham Night and faint lights quiver, Night and the day is done, Night and lights on the river, With the setting of the sun. Night and the stars are falling, Night and the children roam, Night and God still calling Scattered Israel home. Night and the wheat ungathered, Night and the tares grown strong, - Night and the storm unweathered On the mighty seat of wrong. Night and the bridegroom coming, Night and the virgins wait, Night and a herald running, Crying aloud: "Too late!"
