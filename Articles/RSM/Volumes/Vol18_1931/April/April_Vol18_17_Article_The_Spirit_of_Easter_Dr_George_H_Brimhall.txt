The Spirit of Easter
By Dr. George H. Brimhall

Easter is the greatest of all festivals. It is the festival of festivals. It celebrates the greatest event in all history—the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead.

The resurrection is the central doctrine of Christianity. Without it Christianity would be meaningless. Paul said: "If Christ be not risen, then is our preaching vain, and your faith is also vain." The resurrection gives meaning to the crucifixion, to the atonement, and to all the teachings of Jesus.

The resurrection is the guarantee of our own resurrection. Because Christ rose from the dead, we too shall rise. Death is not the end. It is but a step in our eternal progress. The grave has no victory. Death has no sting.

The resurrection is the proof of Christ's divinity. It demonstrates that He was indeed the Son of God, with power over death and hell. No mere man could have accomplished what Christ accomplished.

The resurrection is the foundation of our hope. It assures us that righteousness will ultimately triumph over evil, that truth will prevail over error, that light will overcome darkness. It gives us courage to face the trials and tribulations of life, knowing that in the end all will be well.

The spirit of Easter, therefore, is a spirit of joy, of hope, of triumph. It is a spirit that looks beyond the shadows of earth to the glorious realities of eternity. It is a spirit that sees in every ending a new beginning, in every death a resurrection, in every sorrow a joy to come.

May this Easter season bring to each of us a renewed appreciation of the great gift that Christ has given us through His resurrection—the gift of eternal life.
