O Let Me Live by the Roadside

O let me live by the roadside
Where the race of men go by—
The men who are toiling and striving
Beneath the same blue sky.

Let me live where I can hail them
And bid them be of cheer,
Where I can fight beside them
When the battle-field draws near.

Let me live where I can help them—
The loads are heavy to bear,
And a lift by the way is welcome
To the hearts that are full of care.

O let me live by the roadside
And be a friend to man,
And work for the God who made me
According to His plan.

—Ida R. Alldredge.
