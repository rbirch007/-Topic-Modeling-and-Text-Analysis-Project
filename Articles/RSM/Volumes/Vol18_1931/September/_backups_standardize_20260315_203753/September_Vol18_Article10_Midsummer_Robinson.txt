Midsummer By Jessie M. Robinson Hollyhock and dahlia, crimsoned high, Are bannering the garden's midday sky. All eagerness of wood and field will soon Have blazoned in the molten gold of noon. And summer cries in flaming joy to me With flash of wing and song of ecstasy; But I, of middle year, am slow to live, Remaining dull and cold, with naught to give.. O rouse my torpor, make my noontide yield A glory like the garden and the field!
