My Father By Estelle Webb Thomas Though years have passed since last my eyes beheld His loved form with its noble, silvered head, It seems a sacrilege—a gross untruth To say without reserve, "My Father's dead." Dead? When the single day has yet to dawn Without its thought, its memory of him! When tricks of speech, his smile, his voice, his hands— These images the years can never dim! When no decision of import be made; Without the thought, "Is this what he'd have done?" No triumph gained without the wistful cry, "Does Father know the victory I have won?" Dead? When each life that ever touched his own Some sign of that blessed contact surely gives! Not dead! In countless hearts and thoughts and deeds, Immortalized, I know my Father lives!
