[Vol50 | October 1963 | Page 796 | Poem | Open Kettle Canning | Christie Lund Coles]
Lst South / salt Lake City 11, Utah} Open-Kettle Canning Christie Lund Coles I peel the yellow peaches And put the smooth halves Into the rich, sweet syrup, In the large kettle. Then I wait for them to steam, And slowly boil. I sniff the pungent aroma, I remove the sugar scum. When each peach Is crystalline clear And tender to the fork's touch, I spoon them into The sweating, sterile bottles, And seal: the gold of summer, Caught for a brisk, sun-hungry day At our winter table. Some prefer pressure canning, Or so they say. Yet, somehow, I still enjoy Canning my peaches The old-fashioned way.
