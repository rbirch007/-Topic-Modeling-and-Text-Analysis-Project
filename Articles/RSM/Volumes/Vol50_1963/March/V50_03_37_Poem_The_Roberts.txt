Cycle Lael W. Hill After the seed, the greening, The soft root quested forth, A small stem thrust, a leaf uncurled Up from spring-warm earth. After the green, the blossom Opened to wind and sun And ministering of velvet bees Till summer's hour is gone. After the bloom, the ripeness Clustered on vine and stem, A cool sweet given, turn and taste, Down from the heavy limb. After the fruit, the sowing Of self in an hour grown late, The kernel waiting long through dark— Cycle again complete. — The Goblin Choir, Goblin Valley; Utah
