Washing Windows Zara Sabin It takes two to wash a window And do it really well — One outside and one within So that each can tell The other where there needs to be A special rub — spots they don't see. It is quicker, too, and much more fun When two can work instead of one. Then at the end there is always this: A smile that is almost like a kiss. You Cannot Win Gladys nesser Burnham My garden breaks my back to weed. The chickens grab up every seed That falls. I chase them from the shade Of lilacs and the holes they made To dust themselves. How can I win? Without them grasshoppers move in.
