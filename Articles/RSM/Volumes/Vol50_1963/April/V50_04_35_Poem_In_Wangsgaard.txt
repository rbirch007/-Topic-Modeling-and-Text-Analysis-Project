In Simple Robes Eva Willes Wangsgaard In simple robes is beauty dressed And often seeks the humblest place n which to hide, while those who quest In glamorous halls for beauty's face ay leave behind her quiet grace. In simple robes is beauty dressed, No oftener in silken case Than slumbering upon the breast In sackcloth garments; yet the best Of living yields to her embrace. In simple robes is beauty dressed. In humble hearts her fingers trace Her lineaments which interlace Where joy and happiness attest, Though filling all the realms of space, In simple robes is beauty dressed. Walk With Stephen Beulah Huish Sadleir He was a little boy Singing to his believing World, in the morn Of his growing years. Tall assurance rippled Through him in the way He held my hand and walked Unmurmuting. His songs Were his own making Until he mimicked the jerky Call of a stranger bird. On to the water's edge. The child's song changed to boats Moving in the rhythm of slow Water on a sunny day. Child of earth, of free design, Yours is the unhampered song.
