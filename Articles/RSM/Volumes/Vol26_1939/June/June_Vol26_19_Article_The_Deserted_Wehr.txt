Somehow the old lady must be made to tell him what she knew. (To be continued) ext. 9 oo DESERTED HOMESTEAD By Olive C. Wehr Little deserted house Here at the desert's edge, Browned by the desert sun, You wait forlorn? A symbol Of the unconquered? A lost hope, A broken dream, And once?a home. All day long The desert winds Cry their songs About you. Ghost-children Of a long-forgotten past, They run and play, And toss the sand By little handfuls In through broken windows. A red tomato can On a rusty stove, An old love letter On a dusty shelf, And a broken toy?
