By May of that year the capstone of the Temple was laid. When one considers that this was just eleven months after the Martyrdom, and at that time the Temple was only one story high, a clearer insight into the intense activity that pervaded the little town is gained. Evidently nothing could break apart this united people, but persecutions continued. By September of that year mobbings began in earnest and things became so intolerable that the Saints agreed to leave in the spring. The sacrifices necessary for them to make were tragic, but did they sit down and ponder upon it? No, greater activity ensued! There were wagons to build or repair, warm clothing to be made ready, food supplies to gather, etc. What a busy scene the little town presented! But spring was too long to wait for the self-appointed persecutors of the Mormons, and on a cold night in February the Saints were driven from their homes. Nine babies were born that night in the midst of ice and sleet! Yes, driven from the little city they had created and built with their own hands; and with them was driven out the Spirit of Cooperation that had given Nauvoo life and energy, and it remained behind lifeless! Like a canopy that spirit spread over the people; they believed in their new leader, they obeyed his word, they followed him to the Mountains! Oh there were a few who saw greener fields and strayed along the way, or turned back, but for the most part the main body of the Church was loyal.
