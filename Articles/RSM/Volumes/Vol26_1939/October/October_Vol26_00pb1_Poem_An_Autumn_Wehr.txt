AUTUMN SONNET
The scattered leaves, wind-drifted, flutter by— Dead whispers from a recent, living past; Their red-gold beauty spent too soon, too fast, Now paupers, scorned and brushed aside they lie. 'Tis autumn—all things must fade and die, Till stripped all naked, leaf by leaf, at last The trembling earth, aware, shivers aghast; The only answer is the wild bird's cry. 'Tis autumn—aloud my heavy heart cries out A protest that beauty and life must go, And knows again the age-old fears, the doubt; The only answer is a whisper low: When spring shall come again and buds new sprout, Can you deny what love would have you know?
—Olive C. Wehr.
The Relief Society Magazine Vol. XXVI OCTOBER, 1939 No. 10
