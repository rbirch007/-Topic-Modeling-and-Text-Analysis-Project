[Vol35 | March 1948 | Sequence: 25 | Category: Article | Title: Twilight Linford]

Everyone knows that order is heaven's first law, and we struggling housewives can but hope that the law is enforced. In the meantime, we can put "a little bit of heaven" in our homes.  TWILIGHT Beatrice E. Linford Twilight, that most solemn hour Wraps all the earth in gray, And gives all nature time to pause Before the close of day. Twilight, when the weary world Is straining every power To keep tired eyes from drooping Before the darkened hour, Is soft and gray as a blanket, Silent as a summer cloud, Soothing as a mother's palm Upon a child's head, bowed. Twilight, strange and silent time, Makes all the earth akin, And one may hear the voice of God, And feel the love therein. Page
