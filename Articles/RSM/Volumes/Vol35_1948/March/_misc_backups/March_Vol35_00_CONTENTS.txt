SPRING DUSK Christie Lund Coles The gray, madonna-hooded hills Lie still tonight, And far as eye can see, the sky Is platinum bright. The crystal air is petal-sweet, And as I pass Brief overtones wake in the trees And in the grass. The sky-born wedge of birds once more Slants near my door, While emerald green, the satin buds Leaf as before. This wild, sweet ache, Lest in this iridescent hour The heart may break. MARCH 1948 I make no sudden cry, nor voice Grace T. Kirton TULIPS The Mission of the Relief Society Mark E. Petersen Member of the Quorum of the Twelve Too Relief Society is one of the agencies in the hands of the Lord for the salvation of souls, and the women of the Church may become saviors upon Mt.
Zion as effectively through it as they can in any other way. The need to save souls was never greater than at the present time.
Probably in no other day have the forces of evil been so persistent as they are now in their efforts to weaken character, undermine the home, violate the sanctity of marriage, and destroy the very foundations of all happiness. The women of the Church have always been champions of the right. In the days of the Prophet Joseph Smith they rallied to protect his life and the lives of his family; they sacrificed to build the early temples of the Church, and when the time came to choose between the comforts of their Midwestern homes and the rigors of pioneer life as the price of religious freedom, they left those homes, crossed the plains, and with their husbands settled the valleys of the Rockies. 'They faced the dangers of childbearing without hospitals and often with no medical aid. 'They endured hunger and cold, attacks from Indians, and the affronts of the world. But, filled with faith and determination, and an understanding of their great objective, they applied themselves to the task.
What we now have in the West came in large measure through their efforts.
Without them, we never could have achieved so well.
Through all their trials, they were saints and mothers combinedlike angels of God ministering among men.
They helped the poor, nursed the sick, prepared the dead for burial, taught culture, built character, and _ established faith.
Great is their glory. In doing these things they carried out the spirit and the letter of the charge given them by the prophets of this dispensation through
