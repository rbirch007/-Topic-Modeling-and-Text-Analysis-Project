They have upheld a wonderful tradition and justified the confidence placed in them by the First Presidency, who, when authorizing us to launch the fund-raising program, said: "We have never known the sisters to fail in anything they have undertaken in the past and we feel sure they will not fail in this."
I am confident that those who have gone before us are today rejoicing with us, for this building program is not ours alone; it belongs to the past and it will belong to the future. In this magnificent achievement the women of this day have kept faith with the noble women of the past by opening the way whereby their dream may be brought to fruition. They have nobly and generously fulfilled the great special assignment given to the women of this day, making possible a lasting memorial to the women of this Church. They have set a worthy pattern of loyalty and obedience for Relief Society women of the future, and those who follow after them will remember their efforts and bless them for what they have done.
We stand humbled at the magnitude of what has been accomplished. One year ago when the fund-raising program was launched we recognized it to be an enormous undertaking. Its greatness has in no measure diminished as the year has passed.
