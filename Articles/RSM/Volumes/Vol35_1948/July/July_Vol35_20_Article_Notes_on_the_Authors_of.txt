Notes On the Authors of the Lessons
THIS year an author new to Relief Society members appears as a writer of one of the lesson series. Elder G. Homer Durham, author of the social science lessons, "Latter-day Saint Political Thought," is Director of the Institute of Government and head of the department of political science at the University of Utah. A son of George Henry Durham and Mary Ellen Marsden Durham, he was born in Parowan, and educated in the schools of Utah, receiving his B. A. degree from the University of Utah. He received his Ph.D. in political science at the University of California, Los Angeles, and served as a member of the faculties at California, Swarthmore, Pennsylvania, and Utah State Agricultural College.
He has written many publications dealing with politics and government. He is author of Joseph Smith: Prophet-Statesman, and the editor and compiler of Gospel Standards (Selections from the Writings and Discourses of President Heber J. Grant); The Gospel Kingdom (Selections from the writings and discourses of John Taylor); and The Discourses of Wilford Woodruff.
Elder Durham has long served the Church as an outstanding teacher and executive officer in various organizations, particularly in scouting, M.I.A., Sunday School, and Priesthood activities. A member of University Ward, Salt Lake City, he was appointed to membership in the Emigration Stake High Council in 1946.
Married to Eudora Widtsoe, daughter of Elder John A. Widtsoe and Leah Dunford Widtsoe, Elder Durham is the father of a son and Donalee.
Jean Ridges Jennings, author of the sewing lessons, "Value of Acquiring Makeover Skills; Makeover Possibilities," also prepared the sewing course outlines for last year.
She was born in Logan, Utah, the daughter of Dr. Alfred J. and Edna Hyde Ridges. Her maternal great-grandfather was President John Taylor and her paternal great-great-grandfather was the well-known and gifted pioneer, Joseph H. Ridges, builder of the original Tabernacle organ. Her maternal grandmother was Annie Taylor Hyde, first counselor to Bathsheba W. Smith in the general presidency of Relief Society. She was reared and educated in Salt Lake City and is a graduate of the University of Utah.
Mrs. Jennings, wife of Brenton W. Jennings, is the mother of a son and a daughter. She combines two interesting careers, homemaking and teaching at the University of Utah in the home economics department. Her hobbies are sewing and gardening.
