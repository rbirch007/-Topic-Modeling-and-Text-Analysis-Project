THE RELIEF SOCIETY MAGAZINE SUMMER DAY Beatrice Knowlton Ekman The summer day is languorous with heat, And through my window near the gable eaves There floats a fragrant incense, nectar-sweet, From honeysuckle bloom and brier leaves. The turquoise sky is mirrored in the pool And down the path beneath the elm-tree shade, The overhanging boughs make dim and cool The earth, where changing shadow prints are laid. The bees are noisy in the linden tree, A sparrow stalks a robin on the grass.

Garff, former Counselor, Relief Society General Presidency But if any provide not for his own, and specially for those of his own house, he hath denied the faith, and is worse than an infidel (I Timothy, 5:8). THE ultimate temporal success and security of the members of the Church will be determined by the diligence of the people to make themselves self sustaining in all respects. We must understand that we are not only to sustain ourselves, but also to build up the Church financially in order that the Church as an organization may help those members who have met with misfortune and find it impossible to help themselves, Everyone is a potential recipient of help within the Welfare Plan of the Church. No one with few exceptions can justify himself in expecting the Church to take care of his need continually. The Welfare Plan was given to rehabilitate and  encourage members, to bring them back to a point of self-sufficiency, The plan is not a dole. It is not a Process of continually contributing to the individual, who can (when Properly directed) be self-sustaining.
