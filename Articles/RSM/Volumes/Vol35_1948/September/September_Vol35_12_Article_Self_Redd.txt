[Vol35 | September 1948 | Sequence: 12 | Category: Article | Title: Self Redd]

SELF DEFENSE Anna Prince Redd I take the highway or the air When I, with urgent feet, must see Shops and cities  anywhere.
Just to lose myself from me. But when my heart again is still, I seek the trail, the musky loam. The woodfolk, whispering, until A lighted window brings me home. ANTICS Mary Pack Tiiplett Little regimented ant!
With what amazing skill You shovel out your tunnels And lift a sheltering hill. We respect your talent To build and colonize; You surely show rare intellect For one of your small size. Yet just one aberration Marks you the rankest sinner; You make your uninvited way To every picnic dinner.
