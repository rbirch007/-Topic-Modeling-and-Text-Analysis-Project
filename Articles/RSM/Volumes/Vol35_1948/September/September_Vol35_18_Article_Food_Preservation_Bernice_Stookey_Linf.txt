Food Preservation
Bernice Stookey Linford

Thou shalt be diligent in preserving what thou hast, that thou mayest be a wise steward; for it is the free gift of the Lord thy God, and thou art his steward (D. & C. 136:27).

CANNING is still the most widely used form of food preservation both in the home kitchen and in commercial factories. Yet each year many persons die from food poisoning as a result of poor canning methods due to carelessness or misunderstanding.

Because the American people depend so much on canned products for their daily food, the United States Department of Agriculture has prepared for distribution a great variety of canning and other food preservation bulletins. The utmost care has been taken by specialists in testing and assembling this authentic material. Complete step-by-step directions and illustrations are included, which make canning safe and simple even for the most inexperienced.

Single copies of these bulletins are free and may be obtained by contacting county extension agents or by writing the Office of Information, United States Department of Agriculture. Available publications are listed at the end of this article. Your family needs will determine the specific bulletins you send for—all are authentic and practical.

Freezer lockers and home freezers are available to many. Frozen foods, if the best quality food is selected and proper methods used, are almost the equal of fresh foods. They may be of even better quality than fresh foods which have been shipped great distances.
