See!" they said joyously RELIEF SOCIETY MAGAZINEAPRIL 1948 holding out the dolls in chubby hands. "Mother, read us a story." It was Peter, with his tongue in the vacant spot of his upper tooth row. "Please do," put in Beth, and the twins added, "Tory, tory," as if they knew what a story was. It was here Frank found them when he came home, unheard.
Julie looked up and saw joyous relief in his face. "What is this?" he said with a grin. "Daddy, daddy!" piped the twins racing to reach him first. "Mother, read us a story," announced Beth standing up and straightening her pinafore. "Yes," agreed Julie, "I was going to make an apple pie, but I read a story." "What I found tonight," - said Frank, with his eyes on Julie, "was sweeter than any story."
