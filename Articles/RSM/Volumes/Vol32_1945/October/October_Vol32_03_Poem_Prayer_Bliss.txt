[Vol32 | October 1945 | Sequence: 03 | Category: Article | Title: Prayer Bliss]

PRAYER Edwin C. Bliss To some, a prayer is mere routine, A boresome obligation. Prosaic offering of thanks. And unfelt adoration. Such praises, sung by grudging lips. Can never reach the throne; For God cares not for prayers made up Of words and words alone. A prayer must be a consecration, Heartfelt and sincere; A faith that, though the load be heavy, God is ever near; A willingness to work and wait As through the years we plod. And place our life entirely Within the hands of God.
