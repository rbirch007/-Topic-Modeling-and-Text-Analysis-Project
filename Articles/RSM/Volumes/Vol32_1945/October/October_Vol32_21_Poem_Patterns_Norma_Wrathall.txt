[Vol32 | October 1945 | Sequence: 21 | Category: Article | Title: Patterns Norma Wrathall]

PATTERNS Norma Wrathall We grasp the threads of life with unskilled hands. And start to weave the pattern of our days; The passing years bring wisdom, but the strands Are tangled by the time we?ve learned their ways. We long, we strive, to weave the brighter hues. As youthful hours pass like summer sun. But soon, unwilling fingers start to use The somber tones, where hidden shadows run. Our thoughts, our acts, our very lives remain To bind the threads, unwavering, to the end; We dare not cut or break a single skein? For strands like these, once torn, may never mend.
