[Vol32 | October 1945 | Sequence: 10 | Category: Article | Title: October Parry]

Yes, Sonny, you can have my tools when I die, but remember, we can build a building in a year, but we have to take care of it for a century.  OCTOBER MAGIC Jeanette P. Parry October, the roving gypsy. Is here in my garden again, With scarfs of exotic color To tie on the hillside and glen. The valley wears a grape-blue veil, To hide the sadness in her heart; It is hard to see October go And with her jocund spirit part.
Through dreamy, shifting, opal days The river dons a silver coat, And hosts of gold and crimson leaves Upon its placid surface float. The sun climbs down the autumn sky And leaves a flame-like afterglow, Soon winter's biting winds may sweep Across the frozen wastes of snow.
