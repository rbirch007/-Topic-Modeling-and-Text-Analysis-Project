NOTES TO THE FIELD 33 7.
Panties from shirt tails, from the bottom of underslips, or from underslips used with sheer party or formal dresses. 8.
Front aprons from men's shirts. 9.
Jerkins from soft woolen articles. 10.
Envelope handbags, lapel ornaments, half hats or flowers for the hair, and other accessories, from old bright felt hats. General Suggestions 1.
Pieces cut from old suits or coats, crocheted around and sewed together make good afghans. Old cotton house dresses, shirts and aprons cut and sewed may be used for woven throw rugs, hooked rugs, or other types of rugs.
Salt or sugar sacks sewed together and hemmed make very satisfactory dishcloths.
Flour sacks dyed and sewed together make satisfactory soft quilt linings. (It is almost impossible to buy plain materials suitable for this purpose at the present time.) Good pieces of knit underwear crocheted around make satisfactory wash cloths.
