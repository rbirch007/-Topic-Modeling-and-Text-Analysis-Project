[Vol32 | January 1945 | Sequence: 22 | Category: Article | Title: Pebbles Norma Wrathall]

When his teacher came, he chewed his pencil and thought fast about the blue lumps and about her thinking he was a mama's boy. And after school, when out he went with Billy down to the five-and-ten. PEBBLES Norma Wrathall I took some pebbles from the brook, Their colors glowing clear and bright With shades of blue and amethyst, And coral streaked with white; But as they dried upon my hand They were as dull and drab as sand.
Rare moments snatched from life's swift flow Fade as we long to hold them fast; Dear cherished dreams grow old before We catch a fleeting glimpse at last; And yet, in retrospect they seem Like pebbles shining in the stream.
