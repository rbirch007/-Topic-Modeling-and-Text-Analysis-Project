This short story contest, ﬁrst conducted by the Relief Society General
Board in 1941 as a feature of the centennial observance, was made an
annual contest in 1942. The contest is open only to Latter-day Saint women
who have had at least one literary composition published or accepted for
publication by the editor of a publication of recognized merit.
The three prize-winning stories are to be published consecutively in the
ﬁrst three issues of the Magazine for 1945. Prize-vanning stories become
the property of the General
