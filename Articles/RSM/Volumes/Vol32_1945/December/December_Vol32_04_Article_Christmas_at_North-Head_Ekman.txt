CHRISTMAS AT NORTH-HEAD LIGHT Beatrice Knowlton Ekman The rock-bound lighthouse fronts the sea, Below, the breakers roar In angry combat endlessly Where waves on cliff rocks pour. All day the gulls wing overhead, Fog hides the outflung space Where sailors' hearts are filled with dread Watching the dark sea's face.... The ocean wind blown in to shore, The cries of birds in flight, Will carol round the lighthouse door To mark the Christmas night.
