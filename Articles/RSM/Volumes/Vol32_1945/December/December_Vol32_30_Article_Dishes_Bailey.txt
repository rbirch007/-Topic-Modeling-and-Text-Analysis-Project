[Vol32 | December 1945 | Sequence: 30 | Category: Article | Title: Dishes Bailey]

DISHES Alice Morrey Bailey Take the cup, you naughty doll! Don't knock it off and break it! Don't put your whole hand in. Can't you play they aren't tin? I washed last night, and night before. My turn to wipe tonight. No, Mother. We're not quarreling. Oh, Gee! Oh, well! All right! He?someone?called me to the gate. Why can't I have some fun? We? talked?'but can I help it if the dishes aren't done? Tom and I will do them, Mother. Isn't he domestic? The way he puts them on the shelves! He really is artistic. Come, girls, and put the forks around by every shining plate.
Wash for dinner, boys. I hope your father isn't late.
Polish all the silverware. Cet out the glass and lace. It's just as well for Anna's beau to realize his place. Thanksgivings, weddings, Christmases] It's nice to get together But pots and pans are all the same in any kind of weather. Now I've learned to curb my tongue, my violent wants and wishes. Can it be I'm all alone with memories?and dishes?
