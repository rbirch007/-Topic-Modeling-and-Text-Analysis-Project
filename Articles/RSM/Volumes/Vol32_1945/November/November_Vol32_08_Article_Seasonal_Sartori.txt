Better Book Buying for the Home
Dr. Howard R. Dnggs
FAR more than just books and magazines make a good home Hbrary. It
takes intelHgent planning to create this intellectual and spiritual center of
family uplift and recreation. Nor can a lastingly attractive home library be
made in a day, a month, or a year. Time and co-operative eﬀort on the part
of both parents and children are required.
Not much money, comparatively, is necessary to possess a really eﬀective
home library. No such initial outlay as that for a piano, an electric or gas
range, or even an ordinary' stove, or for rugs and drapes, linen or china, is
necessary. Choice books may be had for a few dollars each, or even less—
and they can be purchased on the best kind of installment plan—one at a
time; pay as you go.
