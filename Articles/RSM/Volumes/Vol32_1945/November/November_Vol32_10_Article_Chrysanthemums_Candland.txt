What further seems needed is greater unity of action on the part of the different organizations to give parents and children guidance in this vital matter of building and using good home libraries. CHRYSANTHEMUMS Grace M. Candland Late autumn flower of rich and blended shades Of garnet, rust, and rose, and peacock blue. And delicate cerise and copper hue, Arranged like bits of muslin overlaid In pointed rows that circle into place Like caps atop a mass of deep sea green; Indented leaves, faced with a pearly sheen, To coalesce like knitted bars of lace.
Long months of summer sun and cooling rain Have wrought this miracle of loveliness. And held it in reserve till hill and plain Have lost all other blooms, and frosts caress Has blighted them, but these fair flowers will stay To greet the snowflakes of a winter's day.
