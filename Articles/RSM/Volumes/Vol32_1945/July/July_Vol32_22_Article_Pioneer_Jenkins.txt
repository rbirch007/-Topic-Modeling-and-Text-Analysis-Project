Synopsis
Delsa Marriott receives a letter from her ﬁance, Hugh Temple, with the
army in Australia, asking her to release him from their engagement so that
he can marry an Australian girl. Delsa is broken-hearted, and because her
family and friends have long looked forward to this marriage, she hesitates
to tell them that she has been jilted, and decides to keep this a secret until
she can make up her mind whether or not to release Hugh.

During the night Delsa had made up her mind to one thing: she would not
write to Hugh immediately. She would wait a little, think the matter over
before she told him he was free. For she had thought of their engagement as
he had done—as something as binding as a marriage vow. She had not
gone out once, not even to a town dance, with anyone since Hugh left. She
hadn't even thought of any other boy.
