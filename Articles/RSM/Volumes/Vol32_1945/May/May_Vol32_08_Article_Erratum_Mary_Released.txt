The Magazine honor roll is based on subscription reports submitted to the
General Board. However, Lethbridge and Oahu Stakes, and the British,
East Central (except North Carolina District which submitted a report),
Hawaiian, South African, and Texas Missions (of the English-speaking
missions) failed to submit subscription reports for 1944. Also, a few
districts of some of the reporting missions failed to respond—3 in the
California Mission, 3 in the Northern States, 1 in Northwestern States, and
1 in the Southern States. Reference to the annual Relief Society reports
compiled by the secretaries indicated that some of these unreported stakes
and missions had local Relief Societies with rates entitling them to a
position on the honor roll and they were, therefore, included. However,
none is included from the British Mission from which neither report had
been received by press time.

Achievements oi Stakes
Of the 57 stakes on the 1943 honor roll, all but 6 maintained this position.
The loss of these 6 stakes (Lakeview, Mt. Graham, New York, South
Sanpete, Star Valley, and Yellowstone) was more than oﬀ-set by 20
additional stakes which achieved honor-roll
ratings. We extend congratulations to the 51 stakes which maintained their
1943 status and to the following 20 newly added stakes: Ben Lomond,
Blackfoot, Bonneville, Chicago, Cottonwood, Denver, Emery, Liberty,
Nampa, Oakland, Ogden, Park, St. Joseph, Salt Lake, San Bernardino, San
Juan, Southern Arizona, Sugar House, Uintah, and Uvada Stakes. Many of
these stakes nearly made the honor roll last year but in striving for honor-
roll position this year, several recorded considerable gains during 1944; for
example, Uintah Stake which boosted its 1943 rate of 69 per cent to 101
per cent; Park Stake which rose from 74 to 103 per cent; Salt Lake from 65
to 88 per cent; Oakland from 66 to 86 per cent; Emery from 61 to 80 per
cent; Sugar House from 56 to 75 per cent; Southern Arizona and San
Bernardino both of which advanced from 71 to 89 per cent. Cottonwood
Stake, also in this list, is mentioned in the "Notes From the Field" in this
issue as having risen from a 1942 rate of 26 per cent—one of the lowest in
the Church—to 90 per cent in 1944, a noteworthy accomplishment in a
short period of time.
Achievements of Missions
All three of the missions which appeared on the 1943 honor roll-Australia,

Western Canadian, and Western States—maintained their positions in
1944. The Australian Mission still leads the stakes and missions of the
Church with a 1944 rate of 136 per cent. Three additional missions
achieved the 1944 honor roll, not barely but with good rates—South
African, 92 per cent;

Southern States, 88 per cent; Northern States, 82 per cent. The number of
mission districts (comparable in function to stakes) appearing on the honor
roll increased from 11 in 1943 to 24 in 1944. Four of these 11 districts
(Bakersﬁeld in the California Mission, North Indiana and North Michigan
in the Northern States Mission, and Great Falls in the Northwestern States
Mission) failed to maintain their honor-roll rating. The other 7 districts
which maintained their honor-roll status are: South Coast in California
Mission; Central Ohio, North and South Illinois, South Ohio, and
Wisconsin, in Northern States Missions; and Central Florida and South
Georgia in the Southern States Mission.
The 17 mission districts appearing on the honor roll for the ﬁrst time this
year are: Santa Barbara, San Gorganio, and Mojave of the California
Mission; Central Indiana, East Michigan and West Michigan of the
Northern States Mission; Northern Montana of Northwestern States
Mission; Alabama, Florida, Georgia, Mississippi, South Carolina, South
Florida, South Georgia, West Florida, and West South Carolina of the
Southern States Mission, and South Louisiana of the Texas Mission.
Suhsciiptions From Non-ranking Societies Also Appreciated
In addition to the stakes, missions, and mission districts whose local Relief
Societies attained rates suﬃcient to place the entire area on the honor roll,
63 stakes and 10 missions are represented not by a rating for the stake or
mission as a whole, but by one or more of their local Relief Societies which
attained honor-roll status. Accordingly, a total of 134 stakes (all but 14)
and of 16 English-speaking missions (all but 2) are represented on the 1944
honor roll by one or more local Societies. It is regretted that a few (Beaver,
Panguitch, South Ogden, and Moon Lake Stakes, and the Hawaiian
Mission) which were so represented in 1943 by high-ranking local

Societies are not represented by even one ward or branch this year.
Nevertheless, we are mindful and appreciative of the thousands of
subscriptions which have helped to swell the total subscription list but
which were obtained by local Relief Societies whose individual rates did
not entitle them to a place on the honor roll. It is hoped that the
accomplishments of the year 1944 will encourage these Relief Societies to
renew their eﬀorts during 1945 and match the achievements of their sister
Societies.
