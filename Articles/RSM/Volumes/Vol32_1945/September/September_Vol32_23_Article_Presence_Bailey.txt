[Vol32 | September 1945 | Sequence: 23 | Category: Article | Title: Presence Bailey]

PRESENCE Alice Money Bailey She is not gone. The rows of bottled fruit Arranged on cellar shelves still bear a mute But sharp reminder?berries, apples?keys To thrift. Her potted plants, her brooms, and these: The brightly colored rags she meant to braid. The handkerchiefs she ironed, her glasses laid With neat precision by her much-read book. No dim and fleeting wraith is hers. I look To see her laughter-crinkled face. I hear The briskness of her step, her accents clear. How can I feel the loss her absence gives. Or mourn for her whose vividness still lives? Page
