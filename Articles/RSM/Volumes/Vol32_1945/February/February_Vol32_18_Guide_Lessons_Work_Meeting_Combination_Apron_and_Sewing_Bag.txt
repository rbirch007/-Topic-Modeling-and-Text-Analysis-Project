[Vol32 | February 1945 | Sequence: 18 | Category: Guide | Title: Work Meeting Combination Apron and Sewing Bag]
ANY woman who sews, darns, mends, or does fine needlework, crocheting or knitting will appreciate an apron with pockets to hold her thimble, scissors, and tape measure, and keep her sewing clean, too.

This combination apron and utility bag is very convenient and practical.

Materials required—1 1/6 yds. Fabric, preferably heavy, washable print or solid color, 3/4 yards cord or ribbon for draw string and to tie envelope accessory holder, bias binding, two wooden rings, beads or buttons.

To make—Cut two pieces of material 16 3/4 inches by 22 inches for bag (A) (Illus. 1).

Seam the bottom and sides.

Turn down 1 3/4 inches at top for hem.

Stitch hem, then stitch 3/4 inch from edge of hem to make a 1/4 inch casing to enclose draw string.
