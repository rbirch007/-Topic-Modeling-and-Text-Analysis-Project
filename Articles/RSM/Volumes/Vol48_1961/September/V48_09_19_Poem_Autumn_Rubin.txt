[Vol48 | September 1961 | Sequence: 19 | Category: Poem | Title: Autumn | Author: Rubin]
AUTUMN ON THE PLAINS Autumn Tloon Maude Rubin The pond is shrunken, its reed-pipes dry, Its blackbird chorus still. The field is stubble, ankle high, And over the bread-loaf hill The sun hangs ripe as a yellow pear. The breath of timothy rides the air From high in its steep-roofed haymow where, Moveless, the weathercock's metal stare Surveys this bronze tranquility. One velvet bee Goes bumbling by, Dressed for some fall fiesta, Stirring the indolent gold of noon Where pond and sun and dragonfly soon Will drowse in a brief siesta.
