Down the Lanes of August

Mabel Jones Gabbott

W. M. >< Down the Saves of August Mabel Jones Gabbott The goldenrod is yellow as the sun high overhead, And the apples in the orchard are turned a redder red, The bees have drained the clover to fill their honey-trust, And down the lanes of August boys go barefoot in the dust. The corn is ripe for eating and the pumpkin's orange gold? The best days are about us, with summer getting old.
