THE AMARYLLIS Evelyn Fjeldsted e stand in breathless awe before The amaryllis now in bloom; In silence, it has long designed. To glorify our living room. The bulb, so still, a secret held A secret of a coming dawn. A proof of life renewed where once Another bloom had come and gone. "They toil not, neither do they spin"; And yet a king was not arrayed Like one of these, whose royalty Presents a roseate parade. Each lily seems to hold a scroll That tells, in sweet humility, The story written in a bulb, A treatise on eternity.
