[Vol34 | December 1947 | Sequence: 03 | Category: Article | Title: Christmas Morris]

CHRISTMAS EVE Mary J. Morris The sand has slowly and without a sound Dissolved the year. I never know it's gone Until I see the presents heaped around The Christmas tree. Again, I hear a song I've known and loved since I, too, was a child And stood beneath the star-dressed sky To sing the holy hymn.
Although self-styled In grown-up ways, my happy heart is shy And awed of such a light as shines tonight.
Inside the house the air has warm, spiced wings, And holly berries catch the candlelight That keeps within this roomthe King of Kings.
