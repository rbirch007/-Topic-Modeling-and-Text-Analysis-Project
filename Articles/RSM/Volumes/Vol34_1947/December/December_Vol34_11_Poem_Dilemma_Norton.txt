CHRISTMAS SHOPPING
In Memory of My Mother
Alice Whitson Norton
Along a gaily festooned street, I walked amid a happy throng, And with them listened eagerly To puppets sing a Christmas song.
In crowded shops I purchased gifts, Recalling wishes I once knew A watch, a rug, a silver vase, And then, beloved, I thought of you.
You always asked for little things Like thimblesscissorsmending thread; And patiently my poor heart broke Remembering that you were dead.
