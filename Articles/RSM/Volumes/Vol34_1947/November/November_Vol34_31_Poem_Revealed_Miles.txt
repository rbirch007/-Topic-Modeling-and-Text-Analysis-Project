REVEALED
Ora  Lee  Paithesius
Those  lemon-yellow  leaves,  the  last  that  cling,
Accentuate  the  darkened  symmetry
Of  barren  branches;  then,  that  fluttering
Of  color  goes;  there  is  the  poignancy,
The  innate  strength  of  every  twig  revealed
In  nakedness.  Whatever  be  the  cost —
Our  youth,  our  visions — when  the  scars  are  healed,
The  skeleton  of  faith  will  not  be  lost.
