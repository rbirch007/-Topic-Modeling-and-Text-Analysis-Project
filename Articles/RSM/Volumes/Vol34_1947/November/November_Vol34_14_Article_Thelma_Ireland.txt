[Vol34 | November 1947 | Sequence: 14 | Category: Article | Title: Thelma Ireland]

A HOME Thelma Ireland It is nice to have a house built new, According to one's wishes, And carry out the color scheme, With curtains, rugs, and dishes. But give to me an old farmhouse With the rooms built on in tiers, To accommodate a family That increases with the years; A house with family albums And old portraits here and there, And a raveled, crocheted tidy Hiding scars on some old chair. No, it may not be artistic, Sort of motley, as a whole, But give me a house that's lived in, A house that has a soul.
