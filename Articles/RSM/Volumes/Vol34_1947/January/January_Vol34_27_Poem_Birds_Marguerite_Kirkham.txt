WINTER RAIN
Marguerite Kirkham
The rain fell, cold and drizzling gray, On the garden and on the lawn; It beat, in a steady rhythmic sound, From the dusk of day to the dawn.
The dead leaves clung to dampened earth, The trees were deep in wintry sleep; And all was drab, and bleak, and brown, And all the willows seemed to weep.
But when I looked with different eyes, I saw the summer garden there, With roses smiling in the sun And blossoms scenting soft, warm air.
