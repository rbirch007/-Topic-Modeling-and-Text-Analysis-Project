A SAMARITAN HIGH PRIEST AND THE PENTATEUCH "Moreover, thou son of man, take thee one stick, and write upon it, For Judah, and for the children of Israel his companions: then take another stick, and write upon it, For Joseph, the stick of Ephraim, and for all the house of Israel his companions."—Ezekiel 37:16. The frontispiece shows a picture of a Samaritan high priest and an original roll of the pentateuch supposed to be written by Abishua, a descendant of Aaron. We are publishing it primarily that our readers may discover why the ancient scripture was referred to as a stick.
