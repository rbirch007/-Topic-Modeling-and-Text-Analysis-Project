Compensation By Ethel Allen Kilgore
'Tis sultry hot, the summer sun Rides high in cloudless sky, And scarce a breeze to sway the trees As birds go winging by. But up in the top of the poplar tree A bird gives us a treat, Singing a song so trillingly, "Sweet, sweet, sweet, pretty sweet."
A dust film settles on the flowers, The bees hum drowsily, The barn-yard fowls lift drooping wings, The dogs pant gustily. But still in the top of the poplar tree, In ecstasy complete, A yellow bird sings trillingly, "Sweet, sweet, sweet, pretty sweet."
