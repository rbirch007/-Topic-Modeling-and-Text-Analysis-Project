Mardula By Josephine Spencer
I saw a face within a frame, A quaint old picture, known to none, Its heritage a single name From Time's complete oblivion won; A thread of music in it spun? Mardula!
Her eyes were like the dark lagoons In tropic forests shyly hid, With glimmers of the Southern moons Ready to ripple 'neath each lid; Their gaze a tempting challenge bid? Mardula!
Her cheek was woven with the rose, Her ear the fainter sea-shell's hue; Her throat was whiter than the snows, And her red lips, apart, I knew Were sweet with smiles, and dawn-like dew? Mardula!
I knew that romance, like the air, Had brooded in her storied days? That lovers, bold and debonair, With crowding tread, had sieged her ways; I guessed their sighs, their moon-sung lays? Mardula!
But I may never from her life One sweet, enchanted moment claim, Though I should seek with song, or strife; She is a picture, in a frame, And yields me but one thing?a name? Mardula!
