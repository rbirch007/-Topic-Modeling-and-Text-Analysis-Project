Song By Susan T. Jennings
Singing in the orchard, happy boy! Oh, the marvel of your song! Singing through the days so long! While the beauteous birds and bees Wing through sunshine and the trees. Waft your song on summer's breeze, All sad thoughts from me decoy.
Sing beyond the orchard, happy boy! Take your song into the world, Let your glad notes be unfurled; Lift the sorrowing with rapture, Some lost soul, perchance, you'll capture. Sickness, sadness you may lure With your throbbing trills of joy.
Sing where flowers flourish, happy boy! Sing beneath a cold, white moon, Sing where sky frets out its gloom. Be your voice as chastening rod; Sing so seaman, man of sod May hear promptings of his God In your song. Sing, happy boy!
