It is the organ of the Relief Society of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. As such it keeps each of us in touch with the Relief 'Society work in general. It is a medium through which special messages may be received from the presidenc yof the Relief Society. Material found in the Magazine: A. It contains news of interest to women and particularly to Relief Society women, such as important world events as interpreted in their relationship to Relief Society work. B. Literary gems in prose and verse form part of the makeup of practically every issue of the Magazine. C. Historical events and biographies of Relief Society leaders and other women of note are preserved to us through its pages. D. The Magazine frequently contains travel articles of interest and importance sometimes written by L. D. S. Writers and sometimes by others. E. Child guidance and parent problems are often treated. F, Households helps such as budgeting are included. G. The Magazine contains the guide lessons which are indispensable to good class work. Talent is developed by the publication of the prose and poetry of our local writers. As editor of the Magazine we have a woman who has made a life sttrdy of woman's cause, keen and sympathetic with high ideals. She is preeminently qualified for the great task by inheritance and education in and out of the Church. The teacher should study thoroughly one copy of the Magazine and carry it with her that people may see it and that it may become a basis of discussion.
