Our Tree
By Merling Dennis Clyde
Somehow, when I look at our Christmas-tree,
It seems
so wise and knowing,
As
if there were one of the family
Through
all
its
tinsel showing.
It's 'way after Christmas, and
I really should
Put everything away
;
But, somehow, I just can't begin
Does
it
really beg to stay?
As
it stands dressed up in
its pretty things
Oh that
dignified symmetry!
It whispers, "Please don't disturb me now,"
With a tree's rare coquetry.

Counselor Jennie Brimhall Knight
