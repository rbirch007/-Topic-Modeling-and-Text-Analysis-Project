Substitution By Alberta Huish Christensen An English-garden picture hangs Within my breakfast room, Where hollyhock and mignonette And larkspur are in bloom; And though the skies outside be dull, The sea mists damp with gloom,  The sunshine of its garden path Illumines all the room. I have a golden memoried dream, Hung close within my heart, Where nightingale and slender moon And roses are a part; And when all life prosaic seems Like flowers without perfume, I live again the romance of A distant, perfect June.
