Gratitude By Julia Collard Baker My dreams once held a swelling germ, of high ambitious flight; And worldly Cynics smiled to note that kindling burst of light.

When whisked away by atheist's sneer, Faith made me understand.

That reaching upward I could hold that flame within my hand.

A flower I tended, watered, nursed, ah deep the joy that thrilled me; Uprooted by the storms of night, memory and longing filled me With hope of June, with perfume of the rose that blushed, Shine of the sun that spoke to me, of winter winds now hushed.

A little singing bird I found of plumage strangely fair; I pressed it close against my heart, with none its song would share: Till gladdest music from its throat swelled out upon the air And little children caught the song to heart, and held it there.

A child was laid upon my breast, on her with generous dower Was lavished Faith, the wild rose's bloom, the Songster's magic power., That radiant life was called above, by Heavenly mandate wise, Which bade me rise and claim the light of service and sacrifice.

This day a rapture stirs my soul, nor from it would I flee?

The flame of hope, the wild rose scent, song of a bird in glee.

Dear God, I thank Thee for the right to Faith, and hope, and light, And memory of the life that sleeps in peace within Thy sight.
