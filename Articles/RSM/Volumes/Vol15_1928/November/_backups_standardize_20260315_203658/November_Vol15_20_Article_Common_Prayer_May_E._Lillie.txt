A Common Prayer By May E. Lillie Oh, Father in heaven I thank Thee so? For this, our restful bungalow, For the grass in the yard where the lines swing high, Filled with clothes that are hung to dry. How clean they look, how fresh and sweet, Shirts and tablecloth, towel and sheet, And even the wholesome underwear, Fluttering high in the summer air. How glad I am for the vines that grow, Around this peaceful bungalow; Glad for the musical strains that soar Afar to Thy throne from our open door; In praise to Thee my voice is lifted For daughters dear whom Thy love has gifted, And for these others my voice I raise In lasting, joyful songs of praise.
