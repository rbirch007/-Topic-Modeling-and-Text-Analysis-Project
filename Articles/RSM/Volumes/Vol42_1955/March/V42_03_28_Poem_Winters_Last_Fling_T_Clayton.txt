[Vol42 | March 1955 | Sequence: 28 | Category: Poem | Title: Winter's Last Fling | Author: Bernice T. Clayton]
Newer least Sling Bernice T. Clayton Late winter smiled and promised spring And then swirled snow on everything. Down from the north an icy blast Gave proof that winter had not passed; An outraged nature, near to leaf, Rebelled at promises so brief. Great branches cracked as trees bent low And snapped beneath their weight of snow. Our cat, perched on a snow-piled rail, Switched disapproval with her tail, And shook wet paws as if to state Her great disgust with snow so late.
