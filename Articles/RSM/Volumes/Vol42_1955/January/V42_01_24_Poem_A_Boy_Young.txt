~_- A Boy Sylvia Probst Young A boy is adventure, noise, and fun, With a smudge of dirt, and his knees Are forever out of his overalls— He's a genius at climbing trees. He has no use for a pair of shoes Or a shirt when the days are long; Forever he's munching on jam and bread, And singing a tuneless song. A boy is a king in his own small world— A boy is exasperating— And whatever he might be doing next There is no use contemplating. But a boy holds the strings to his mother's heart, And his sudden kiss is a cure For any ill—oh, a boy is grand— I am glad that I have four. Page 4] Fler Hobbies Bring Yoy to Others Mary Elizabeth Jensen Bingham, Belvedere Ward, Los Angeles, Is a Needlecraft Artist ARY Elizabeth Jensen Bingham, at the age of eighty-nine, still gives joy to her family and her friends, and serves her Church by making exquisite handicraft articles. During the past year she has embroidered twenty pairs of exquisite pillowcases, all with crocheted edges. Also, she has made many sets of dish towels and numerous crocheted doilies. She is an expert at quilting and has designed several original quilt patterns. She has recently completed a lovely crocheted altar cloth to be presented to the Los Angeles Temple when it is finished. Mrs. Bingham's custom of giving a crocheted doily each month to the eldest sister having a birthday during that month, has given much pleasure to the members of her ward Relief Society. At an early age she was responsible for spinning the yarn for her brothers' and sisters' clothing, and she learned habits of industry and service.
