On Mleasuring Mabel Jones Gabbott When I was one and five, I stood up straight and tall, While mother marked my height In inches on the wall. Now I am one and five Times many more; it's true My reaching up was stopped At inches: sixty-two. How shall I note my growth As future years unroll— In breadth and span of mind And depth of heart and soul? New Year s Prayer Vesta N. Lukei Upon the New Year's shining scroll. Beloved, now, let me enroll Our names, our need for special care, And, with humility, my prayer To God that he protect and bless Our pure bright love with happiness; And guide us with his wisdom, clear And understanding, through this year. Yet, neighbors are neighbors, And sometimes, much more; And a woman needs one Not too far from her door, To tell her the news Of children and church, To give recipes, To aid in the search Of small child or dog Strayed from the place.
