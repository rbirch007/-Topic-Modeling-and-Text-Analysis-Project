[Vol42 | February 1955 | Sequence: 21 | Category: Poem | Title: The Unanswerable | Author: Lael W. Hill]
She Unanswerable Lael W. Hill One child is dark, with midnight eyes; One, golden as an August day. Shy is the dark one, wild and wise; The gold one, placid in her play, Flashes a smile that's honey-spun. Her solemn sister haunts you so! Which is the prettier, dearer one Of two little girls? You want to know Then go—ask wind if dusk or dawn Stirs more delight; ask the wide sea What shore is best to break upon. Question which clover suits the bee— But never, never ask a mother Which daughter is lovelier than the other!
