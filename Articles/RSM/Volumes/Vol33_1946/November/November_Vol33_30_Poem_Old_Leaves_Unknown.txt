sage.
OLD  LEAVES
Beatrice  K.  Ekman
The  leaves  from  the  sycamores,  warped  and  dry.
Blow  over  the  roof  as  the  wind  rides  by,
And  over  the  smooth  paved  city  street,
In  rustling  rows,  advance  and  retreat.
The  voice  of  the  wind  sounds  high  and  thin,
As  into  the  gutters  the  brown  leaves  spin,
Bruised  and  broken  and  drenched  with  rain —
To  be  dust  when  the  trees  are  green  again.
