AUTUMN HILLS Grace Zenor Pratt It seems but yesterday I walked across the hills And it was summer... But today a soft and purple mist Has crept across their blueness, while The blazing touch of winter's breath Has changed to bright and shining glory The path of summer's death. All this bright splendor I would somehow keep, Like some sweet, cherished dream to hold In pictured memory.
There is one hidden secret I should like to know How autumn hills can make one strangely yearn, Why autumn's magic touches heartstrings so!
