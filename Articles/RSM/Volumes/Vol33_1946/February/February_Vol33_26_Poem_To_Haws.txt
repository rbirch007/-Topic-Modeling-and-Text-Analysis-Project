[Vol33 | February 1946 | Sequence: 26 | Category: Poem | Title: To]

A  black  wind  shrieked,  but  you  and  I
Went  out  to  battle  with  heads  held  high;
And  fighting  through  it,  arm  in  arm,
Found  weird  beauty  in  the  storm.
Today  I  watch  in  sheltered  pain
Frail  flowers  whipped  by  driving  rain;
Now,  fearfully,  I  shrink  to  see
How  wild  and  bitter  a  storm  can  be.
