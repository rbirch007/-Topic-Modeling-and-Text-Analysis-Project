[Vol33 | February 1946 | Sequence: 10 | Category: Article | Title: Ring Out the Thomas]

BS Ring Out the Old Estelle Webb Thomas THINKING things over on the T front steps that afternoon, Cora decided she had better hide Miss Johnson until Aunt Coralee's visit was over. But even as she mused, a car stopped in the street, the gate clicked and Aunt Coralee came up the walk.
Cora experienced her usual sensations at sight of her. First, amazed admiration at so much beauty, then an unconscious bracing of herself for the familiar, "Ha, Speckles!" which was her aunt's inevitable greeting. Today, however, Aunt Coralee seemed different. She said soberly, "Hello, Cora!" but her quick eye took in Miss Johnson, whom Cora had hastily thrust behind her and she added, "Still playing with that disreputable old camel? We've simply got to get rid of that thing!"
