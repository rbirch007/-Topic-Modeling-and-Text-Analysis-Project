BOUQUET By Estelle Webb Thomas Dear Mid-Victorian ladies, Sweet belles of yesteryear, Whose charms Time's gentle traces Have rendered yet more dear. Though the rose in your cheeks has faded, And your eyes less brightly shine, I shall seek 'mongst your gentle numbers To find my valentine. I've a love for the old-time graces, That fragrant old bouquet That bloomed with your lovely faces, And faded so soon away. I think of my mother's garden With its phlox and columbine Where she plucked for her boy a nose-gay— My first sweet valentine!
