My Wealth

I may not have a fortune
In silver and in gold,
But I have wealth far greater
Than money ever sold.

I have the love of children
Who gather round my knee,
I have a wife whose faithful heart
Beats only, dear, for me.

I have the clasp of friendship,
I have my daily work,
I have my faith in God above—
From duty I'll not shirk.

I have the hope of heaven,
I have a conscience clear,
I have the blessed certainty
That God is always near.

So though I may not be a man
Of silver and of gold,
I have a wealth far greater
Than riches ever sold.

—H. H. Ramsay.
