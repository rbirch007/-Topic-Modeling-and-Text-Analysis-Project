With a knapsack on his back and a crude staff in his hand, He's prancing like the leader of a dress-parading band Through a bed of giant larkspurs?a blue battalion; It's summer in the mountains, and I'm hiking with my son.
The slopes are brilliant color, red and yellow, emerald green, The sky is glowing azure?nothing somber mars the scene. There's a riot 'mong the maples; how their colors blaze and run! It's autumn in the mountains, and I'm hiking with my son.
The pines are muffled whiteness, and we're both a little awed, The altitude and silence bring us mighty near to God; We draw a little closer as we trail on one by one?
