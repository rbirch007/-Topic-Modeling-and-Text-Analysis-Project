HIKING WITH MY SON
By Ada Hurst Brown

My son and I go hiking
Up the canyon trail,
Where mountain peaks are gleaming
And the winds tell many a tale.

We climb the rocky ledges,
We ford the rushing stream,
We rest beneath the pine trees
And share a boyhood dream.

He tells me of his longings,
His hopes and fears so real,
And I am glad we're hiking
Where hearts can freely feel.

The mountains seem to whisper
Of courage, truth, and right,
And we come down the canyon
With souls renewed and bright.

For hiking with my laddie
Up the mountain trail
Has brought us close together
Where love can never fail.
