[Vol34 | August 1947 | Sequence: 06 | Category: Article | Title: Remembrance Lizabeth]

'THE PLACE" Lizabeth Wall They stood for a long time, watching and wondering, These strange, proud people in a strange, new land, These gentle, kindly people, plodding and pondering, Men and women, weary, waiting, hand in hand. (The heat waves shimmered On the valley floor, And a lake shone silver On a far, gold shore . . . . ) They stood for a long time. Here was the ending, Here the beginning of a hard-fought dream; And the valley held them in a subtle blending Of sagebrush and aspen and wind-song and stream. (The heat waves shimmered And a city grew Up from the desert And into the blue . . . .) Under their feet were the hot sands, sifting, And over their heads were the wild, bright skies, But they saw a white city with tall spires lifting, And they walked toward it, singing, with glory in their eyes!
