This attitude of the press undoubtedly increased the difficulties of Mormon settlement. MORMONISM IN THE EYES OF THE PRESS The important point to remember in any evaluation of Mormon history is that these accounts were published as the views, or intentions, of the Mormons.
After careful study, I have no reason to doubt that the average citizen of New York, Ohio, Illinois, Missouri or California believed that 265 these newspaper accounts expressed the teachings of the Church leaders; the intentions of the Mormons to "take over' America and establish Zion; and to use force, if necessary, to accomplish their goal. The task of our pioneer forefathers was undoubtedly rendered more difficult by the opposition thus engendered.
