(To be continued) THE GOLDEN THREAD Renie H. Littlewood Into the tapestry of this great land Are woven, warp and woof, the noblest dreams Of many peoples, and they ever stand As beacon lights that cast refulgent gleams Athwart our paths. All through the weaving run The golden threads of humble gratefulness, Set in a pattern that was first begun By men who deemed their blessings measureless. In early days when Pilgrim Fathers gave Full thanks to their Creator's gracious hand For bounteous harvests garnered, and the brave, Undaunted folk were fed, the little band Became the first to use the golden thread That weaves a bright design repeated through The years.
