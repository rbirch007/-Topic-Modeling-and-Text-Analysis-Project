They bore children to the colony. Let us bear in our hearts veneration for the women of any Christian folk going out in the wilderness to plant a new community (Deseret Evening News, March 17, 1892). PIONEER WOMAN Alice Morrey Bailey They say she wept when first she saw the land That swept unbroken to the salten sea, The unfenced sage, the single cedar tree The shimmering heat strained taut the last frail strand Of her endurance. Wagon-choking sand Seemed bitter lottoo hard the stern decree Of wildernesstoo close in memory The empty homes of this long-driven band. Yet, pulse with his, she caught each growing surge Of hope, and stretched her courage famine-thin To bear new life, to span the cricket scourge. She bent her strength to nourish, teach, and spin, And made her faith in God a moving stream To gird the spires and temples of man's dream.
