[Vol34 | September 1947 | Sequence: 27 | Category: Poem | Title: Within]

Deep  furrowed,  was  a  story  time  had  told
Of  patient  toil  within  a  humble  place.
His  faded  eyes  were  warmed  by  memory —
Beauty  I  saw  in  silent  reverie.
The  last,  a  girl  who,  in  her  quiet  way,
Did  so  much  good,  passed  so  much  cheer  along,
While  deep  within  her  heart  a  sorrow  lay;
But  no  one  knew,  they  only  heard  her  song.
Beauty — she  wore  it  like  a  shining  dress,
A  beauty  nurtured  by  courageousness
NOSTALGIA
Myrtle  Dean
The  ceaseless  throng  moves  down  the  busy  street;
Planes,  droning  in  the  sky  with  tireless  wings,
Defy  earth  noises,  and  drown  the  sound  of  feet.
I  walk  with  them,  yet  loneliness  still  clings.
Instead  of  all  this  clamor,  I  can  hear
The  call  of  meadow  larks,  the  sound  of  rain
Upon  a  cottage  roof;  I  see  you,  dear,
Walk  down  the  path  and  take  my  hand  again.
