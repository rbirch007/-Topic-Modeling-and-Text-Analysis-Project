Where Did The Red Man Come From? By Celia A. Van Cott HE scattered groups of Americans whom our forefathers first saw upon this continent reflected a vigorous and growing civilization.
Most of the tribes were in a semi-nomadic stage with the vast continent providing their needs.
Tribal boundaries were jealously guarded from outside encroachment and if their rights were disturbed they were quick to retaliate; therefore almost incessant warfare was carried on which sometimes wiped out an entire tribe.
Each group spoke a different language and through the lack of understanding one another, they regarded all other tribes as potential and scheming enemies. Had a universal language been spoken it would have welded them together into a homogeneous group, possibly changing the early history of our nation. Archeologists, historians, _ linguists, geologists and many others spend thousands of dollars annually trying to solve the intricate problem of where these dark-skinned people came from.
