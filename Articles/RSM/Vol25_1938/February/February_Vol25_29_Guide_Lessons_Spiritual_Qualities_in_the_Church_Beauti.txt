LESSON 8

SPIRITUAL QUALITIES IN THE CHURCH BEAUTIFICATION PROGRAM

IN THE Church Beautification Program there is one phase that supersedes all tangible evidence of what is grand in architecture, of what nears perfection in the art of sculpture and in painting, or of any surroundings that may be brought into a state of loveliness to enhance a building—it is the condition of spiritual beauty through which our church was organized.

There is one note which should be sounded in order to sustain that kind of beauty in the hearts of those who are fortunate enough to live in an age when the beautification program can be launched—the humility of remembrance.

Time has done much for the Latter-day Saints; it has given the strength and the power to project this program. But time must not be allowed to diminish the grandeur of the spirit which outshone the poignantly humble beginnings of the "Mormons" in a material way.

To call this fact to the attention of the reader is the purpose of this lesson.

THE Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints came to life through beauty. The implicit faith of the lad, Joseph Smith, prompted him to seek guidance through prayer. On the morning of a clear day in spring, for the first time in his life, he sought to pray vocally. "Never before had I made such an attempt," the young boy said.
