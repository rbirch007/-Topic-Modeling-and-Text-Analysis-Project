She Makes An Art of Friendship

By E. C. C.

She makes an art of friendship. In her hands
The common clay of human intercourse
Becomes a thing of beauty. She understands
The secret springs that move the human heart,
And knows the healing touch, the kindly word
That soothes the troubled spirit. She can start
A flame of hope where despair has stirred.

She has the gift of making others feel
That they are valued, that their lives have worth.
Her sympathy is genuine and real,
Her interest in their welfare proves her mirth
Is not mere surface brightness, but springs deep
From wells of understanding. She can keep
A confidence as sacred as a prayer.

She never wounds with careless, thoughtless speech,
Nor spreads abroad the faults that others show.
Her friendship is a sanctuary where each
May come for comfort when the heart hangs low.
She builds up courage, strengthens failing faith,
And by her very presence seems to breathe
New life into the souls that seek her care.

Oh, blessed are the women who possess
This precious gift of friendship! They can bring
To earth a touch of heaven's tenderness,
And make the desert places bloom and sing.
Their lives are benedictions, and their names
Are written in the book where honor claims
The noblest servants of the human race.
