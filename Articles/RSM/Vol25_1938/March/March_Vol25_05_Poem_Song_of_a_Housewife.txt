[Vol25 | March 1938 | Sequence: 05 | Category: Article | Title: Song of a Housewife]

SONG OF A HOUSEWIFE By Florence Strong Across the way from me two women sit With idle hands and watch life passing by.
Their porch is very pleasant?they're content. They'd smile, no doubt, if they could hear me sigh And pity them because they lack my tasks. My tasks that fill my hours with quiet joy And give me sweet content when day is donc; Making a blue rug for the kitchen floor, Picking bright daffodils before the sun Has robbed them of the early morning dew.
Filling a china jar with spicy cakes To please a hungry lad just in from play; Hanging fresh laundered sheets upon a linc To billow in the wind and tug away Like white sails on the ships at sea.
Trailing a vine above the kitchen door; Teaching sweet childish lips how first to pray: Reading a verse about an April dawn, And pausing long enough to smile and say Thank God for work that glorifies each day.
