Autumnal &guinox Margaret Hyatt Let me rest here on this time-worn stile, Built to lift tired feet across the boundary rail, Dividing gold-crested mpening grain From sharp stubble left by harvesters today. Let me share the plaintive evensong Of field doves, killdeer scurrying to nests Hidden in rank grass along this selfsame fence. Though late afternoon's still warming sun Turns dust and autumn haze to gold, Something in the nsing wind Whispers warning of long winter nights. So I would tarry just a little while Before I cross the field of fallow ground To meet the purple twilight just beyond.
