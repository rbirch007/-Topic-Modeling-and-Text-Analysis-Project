[Vol56 | November 1969 | Page 832 | Poem | Winged Return | Leora Larsen]
WINGED RETURN I sent her out to Shake a rug When she was SiX and three: She watched a bird with wings of wind And never quite came back to me. One day, full-grown, she went away With love Strong for her nest. Still, when the days and duties weigh, She flies to me for rest. Leora Larsen @ Today is the most wonderful day of your life! Today is the day that you can do marvelous things! We cannot change what happened yesterday. We do not know what tomorrow will bring, but today is a gift to us from Heavenly Father! Let us make the most of this gift. Today we can teach our children and make them happy. Today we can be real neighbors and worthwhile friends. Today we can study and learn to do new things. We must stop regretting the things that happened yesterday. We cannot change any part of the day that is gone, yet, how many of us there are, who, like Lot's wife, turn to pillars of salt when we look back. In our case the pillars of salt are salt tears, whether they are shed actually, or whether we weep inwardly in our regret. All the tears in the world will not change the past, yet if we should spend the energy and the thought and the pain that we spend on vain regret in making the present all that we can make it, what a happy future ours would be! Stop regretting. If you are regretting lost opportunities of learning, start learning now. If you are mourning about something you did not accomplish, start working and planning something you can accomplish now. Some of the greatest books have been written, some of the finest paintings painted, some of the most wonderful inventions worked out by people well along in years, or seriously handicapped, or both.
