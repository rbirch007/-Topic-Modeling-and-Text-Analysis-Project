Barnes 823 s to the Field ' Relief Society Magazine Subscription Price to Be Increased It is with genuine regret we announce at this time that regardless of our efforts to continue The Relief Society Magazine at its present $2.00 subscription price, it now becomes necessary for us to increase it to $2.50 for the twelve issues per year. (There will be no change at this time in the subscription price of the Spanish edition.) This price change becomes effective January 1, 1970 and applies to all subscriptions including renewals which start with the January 1970 issue of the Magazine or any month thereafter. Subscriptions beginning in September, October, November, or December 1969, or renewals beginning in these months will be at the $2.00 price. Subscriptions for one year at a time only will be accepted. Increases in the costs of labor, printing, paper, and postage force this step upon us. Presidents, we urge you fully to explain this action to ward and branch presidencies and to enlist their support in making certain that the number of subscriptions does not decline either for the sake of the organization or for the sake of the sisters who need the Magazine's instructions, guidance, and influence. Cannon Mrs. Patricia Nixon accompanied her husband, the President, in August on his swift visits to eight countries: the Philippine Islands, Indonesia, India, Thailand, South Korea, South Vietnam, Romania, England. She was most enthusiastically received, and, in many cases, she discussed and visited welfare volunteer projects because of her husband's stress on national volunteer programs. Ladybird and ex-President Lyndon B. Johnson celebrated the former president's birthday, August 29th, as luncheon guests of President and Mrs. Nixon at the Western White House in San Clementé, California. The two first families then flew 700 miles to Redwood National Park, where the President named and dedicated a lovely area "Ladybird Johnson Redwood Grove,"' honoring Mrs. Johnson for '"'stirring the American conscience'' to preserve the beauty of the land for future generations. Some of the redwoods are 200 feet tall and 2,000 years old. Mrs. Johnson worked diligently to have the 58,000 acres of timberland dedicated for conservation purposes.
