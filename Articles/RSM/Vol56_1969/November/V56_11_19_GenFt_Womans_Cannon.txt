Cascade Stake (Washington) Holds Annual Relief Society Day May 19, 1969 Mabel M. Collins, President, Cascade Stake Relief Society, reports: ''Three hundred sisters of the Cascade Stake enjoyed a program, luncheon, displays, and a sewing demonstration at our annual Relief Society day. The program honored the visiting teachers of the stake. The dramatization, 'To Hearken and to Obey,' by Luacine C. Fox was presented. "A lovely floral arrangement made by Helen Barton, stake Secretary-Treasurer was presented to the ward with the highest percentage of visiting teaching throughout the year. Following the program, a luncheon was served. Beside her plate, each sister found a pattern for a 'Happy Coat' and a list of sewing hints. 'The fashion show was presented by women from the ward Relief Societies. Marilyn Haymond, stake cultural refinement class leader, gave the narration to a background of soft music." Virginia E. Kelson, President, Redondo Stake Relief Society, reports: ''Our annual Spring Festival was a very successful event. A chorus of ninety Singing Mothers under the capable direction of Julia R. Wentz and accompanied by Lynne H. DeSpain rendered lovely music in keeping with the theme 'Melodies of Love.' A delightful skit portraying a couple celebrating their golden wedding anniversary reminiscing over each phase of their life together from courtship through family life to old age, was written by Linda Blazer and unfolded through dialogue and song, including an original song composed by Marco Holbrook, one of the chorus members, entitled 'The Voices of Love.' 'Special numbers were rendered by the stake triple trio, a trumpet soloist, and the individual ward Singing Mothers. One of the highlights of the evening was the Gardena Second Ward Children's Chorus. Directed by Sharron Bentley, this group originated as a result of entertaining at a bazaar, and have sung together for five years, thrilling the audiences. Hyrum Stake (Utah) Conducts Successful Home Nursing Program Colleen P. Leatham, President, Hyrum Stake Relief Society, reports: ''We have been delighted with the home nursing program in our stake. It began in April of 1969 under the direction of former president Bernice B. Darley, her counselors, and the Red Cross executives, Cecilia White, Claire Sawyer, R. N., and Mary Clark, Red Cross Western Area Nursing Consultant. "To date we have had 260 sisters and four men complete the course. The classes were taught by seventeen registered nurses, nurses' aids, and specially trained sisters from our stake. It is our plan that every sister who desires this training be able to take it. The cooperation from the wards has been gratifying, and the priesthood support from the stake presidency and bishoprics has helped to insure the success of our program."
