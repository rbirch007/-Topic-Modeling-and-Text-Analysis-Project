Our calling is so important, Our calling is sacred and choice, The work is sweet and rewarding And we have cause to rejoice For we are visiting teachers, And grateful we are for this task, We walk in the steps of our Master, What more could a woman ask? Jeane Reed 809 "The Greatest )i These Counselor Marianne C. Sharp [Address Delivered at the General Session of the Relief Society Annual General Conference, October 1, 1969] @® Should we ask the sisters in the Tabernacle what is the motto of Relief Society, I feel sure we would all answer "Charity Never Faileth." We are told by Paul: Though I speak with the tongues of men and of angels, and have not charity, I am become as sounding brass, or a tinkling cymbal. And though I have the gift of prophecy, and understand all mysteries, and all knowledge; and, though I have all faith, so that I could remove mountains, and have not charity, I am nothing. (I Cor. 13:1-2.) Could we then say that the basic characteristic of an individual striving for exaltation should be charity, which Moroni defines as "... The pure love of Christ."
