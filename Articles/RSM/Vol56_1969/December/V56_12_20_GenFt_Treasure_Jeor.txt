I 932 A Thing of Beauty is a Joy A THING OF BEAUTY IS A JOYCandles of Elegance (continued from page 919) lighted with the results of this interesting and rewarding project that many additional ones were made to be given as Christmas gifts. Wouldn't you like to make one for your home or for a very special Christmas gift? Here are the instructions: GENERAL INSTRUCTIONS FOR ALL VARIETIES The bases can be purchased from a wood turning shop or most carpenters can make any design you wish. A great variety is possible. Those shown in the pictures ranged from eight to thirty-two inches in height. Fill in any visible cracks. Lightly sand base with either 00 steel wool or fine sandpaper. Apply a sealer coat and let dry for 10 to 12 hours. INSTRUCTIONS FOR WOOD STAINED CANDLESTICK 1-inch paint brush soft cloths wood stain antique glaze Apply stain with small soft cloth, allow to stand for 45 minutes. Rub off excess stain with another soft cloth. If a darker shade is desired, apply stain again in the same manner. Dry thoroughly Apply antique glaze with small brush, let stand for 2 or 3 minutes, wipe off as much as you wish for desired effect. ENAMELED CANDLESTICKS 1 small can enamel paint in color of your choice 1 1-inch brush Apply several coats, allow sufficient time between each coat to dry thoroughly. Sand lightly between each coat. As many as five coats may be needed for a satisfactory finish. These are very attractive done in decorator colors, orange, green, pink, blue, red, etc. INSTRUCTIONS FOR ANTIQUED CANDLESTICKS Flat paint in any color of your choice or small can Bisque Wax (ceramic paint) may be used in its stead. (This paint is very economical, easy to handle, and comes in many colors.) 1 1-inch paint brush small can dull varnish antique glaze Sister Ogden makes her own glaze which she finds very easy to handle and is low in cost: 3 tsp. Turpentine 1 tsp. Linseed oil 1 tbsp. Tube coloring, either raw umber, cool color, burnt umber, warm color ° Cover candlestick base with 2 coats of either the flat paint or Bisque Wax. Allow to dry thoroughly after each coat. Apply 1 coat antique glaze. Let stand for two to five minutes. With soft cloth or toilet tissue remove as much as you wish for highlighting. Klondike gold paint was used on those in picture. Let dry for 2 to 3 days. Cover with dull varnish, let dry for 12 hours. If you choose gold as the color, a special paint is needed. Klondike gold paint was used on those in picture. Be sure to stir thoroughly before using and handle with great care since gold has a tendency to lift. For decoration, cut styrofoam to desired size, carving out center to hold candle. Spray with green paint. Place atop candlestick base. Dip _ flowers, greens, or fruit stems in glue and then insert into styrofoam as you desire. Now sit back and enjoy these things of beauty.
