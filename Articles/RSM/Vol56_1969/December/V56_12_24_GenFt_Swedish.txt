Swedish Lucia Queen and Attendants (} Each year in the Swedish Branch of the Church in Salt Lake City, Utah, the Lucia festival is held. In Sweden this is known as Little Christmas, and is held on the 13th of December, the longest night of the year in Scandinavia. Lucia means Festival of Light, and is symbolic of the triumph over darkness. The Lucia Queen is the Queen of Light, and spreads cheer and light throughout the villages, with the help of her attendants. The candles are of course symbolic, and this crown is imported from Sweden. Part of the festival includes a parade through the building, and lovely music. Following the celebration, there are refreshments and a visit from Saint Nicholas.

927 Swedish Cookery For Christmas Darlene C. Dedekind Member, General Board of Relief Society L) A typical Swedish holiday meal would be aptly termed a feast. Many varieties of seafood make up the first course. This is followed by baked ham and red cabbage, which is followed by meat balls and various kinds of cheeses. The main course of the meal is Lutfisk, a_ traditional Swedish dish served with potatoes and a white sauce. Lutfisk is available in package form in many import stores. Rice pudding is the popular dessert for such a feast, and there are several interesting customs surrounding it. Among these is that one almond is included in the recipe, and the person who gets this will be married within the year. Before eating the rice pudding, each person is supposed to say a little rhyme he or she has made up, then light a candle. The first candle that burns down indicates that person has been naughty during the year. Following the feast, Santa Claus generally appears and the delightful festivities of Christmas begin.
