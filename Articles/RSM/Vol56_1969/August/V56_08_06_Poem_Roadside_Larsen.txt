FZ ROADSIDE MOMENT, SUMMER Direr the artist etched the growing world As few have paused to see it... Once, Halted by a roadside, I looked out level and deep into his summer worldat blade, stem, blossom, pod, leaf, Whorled and curved, planed and shaped, Divided, yet intricately twined and curled, (Each one an entity individually rising) Diversity in a random plat So myriad, the eye seeking thought by thought For reason, was dazzled by the shades Of light given back from so subtle a variance Of delicate greens. And these were weeds! Never, no never before had I seen Such wonders springing in so small a space... Great Moses once, in a burning bush, Saw God... I too have looked, and marveled In my small way, at his gifts of grace! 572 Katherine F. Larsen Who You Are Lillian Y. Bradshaw w At the circus, the lithe tightrope walker gracefully climbed I the pole. With breathless excitement we watched him test the wire. A hush fell upon every lip as with slippered feet he glided along the wireone cautious step after anotherhis balance measured by every thought and movement of his body. A slip here, a slip there, a quick bend of the body far to the left and then quickly to the rightwe gasped with relief as he regained his balance once again to move I swiftly forward. And though we sat taut on the bench below, inside we were keenly aware we were taking every step with him. Now the goal but a few steps morejoyfully and with obvious relief we all exclaimed "Hurrah!" as the platformon the other side was reached in safety.
