Roberts Dick Scopes Mary Scopes 561 I have read the serial ''Welcome the Task'' by Michele Bartmess (concluded in the June Magazine) and enjoyed it very much. I have also enjoyed seeing the lovely poems by Pearle M. Olsen (see the Magazine for April and June 1969, and other issues). Sister Olsen's poetry gives me inspiration to keep writing myself. Wilma Morley Despain, Centerfield, Utah I would like to tell you how much I have enjoyed my year's subscription to the wonderful Relief Society Magazine. I have been a member of the Church abouf two years. I am happy that my husband has renewed my subscription to the Magazine, and I hope I don't miss any of the wonderful stories and articles in the future. I first became acquainted with the Magazine by receiving a copy from a Latter-day Saint woman, and now I am always waiting for the next issue to come. I have never enjoyed any Magazine as much as I have this one. Jean Smith, Le Roy, Illinois Welcome Relief Society Magazine! How I have missed you the past three months. Not your fault, I know, but what a joy it was to see you back in my mail box again and to know that once more my friend had arrivedthis time three together. How wonderful our membership in Relief Society is. How blessed we are as wives and mothers to have the privilege of walking beside the priesthood of God. How good it is to be guided by the Magazine. I enjoyed the article on the new Giant System in genealogical research. Too often this work is left to a later date. To bring it to life out of the Magazine can help us to be mindful of our callings as missionaries for our dead. I find great joy in doing this work (see the Magazine for March 1969). Alvina Shore, Solihull, Warwickshire, England Thanks so much for The Relief Society Magazine. -I always enjoy it and the lessons it gives to us. I want to thank you especially for the story ''Mama and the Teddy Bear'' by Lael J. Littke, in the April issue. I am also very hard-headed about accepting help from others, and since reading this story I have been striving to overcome this fault. With the inspiration received from this story, and much help from my Heavenly Father, I feel that I am making progress. It has been a wonderful experience learning to receive, and I thank the Magazine and Sister Littke for helping me to develop this quality. Janie Cooks, Kansas City, Missouri The Relief Society Magazine has been a source of inspiration and aid to many of our young members. Many are attending Relief Society for the first time. We feel the greatest gift one could give a loved one away from home is a subscription to this gem of a Magazine. The articles are adaptable to all situations and needs. Margaret Perry, President, Junction City Branch Relief Society, Junction City, Kansas The reason I am writing is because I have in my hands a copy of The Relief Society Magazine for January 1968 which was given me by Elder Winston Palmer.
