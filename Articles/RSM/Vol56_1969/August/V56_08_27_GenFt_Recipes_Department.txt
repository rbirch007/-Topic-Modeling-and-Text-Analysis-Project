PATCH  QUILT 
Time,  brought  back, 
Cut  up  in  pieces 
And  then  sewed  down 
In  tiny  stitches 
To  warmly  cover 
Snow-white  batting. 
Scraps  of  cloth?    No, 
Dancing  daughters. 
Rowdy  sons  in  sturdy  breeches. 
The  wool  once  worn  at  skating  parties 
And  cotton  shirts  one  festive  autumn. 
The  cashmere  dress  you  wore  one  Christmas, 
The  satin  when  you  sang  that  Easter, 
And  denim  when  you  dug  at  ditches. 
Scraps  of  cloth?    No, 
Friends  returning. 
Thimbles  clicking— smiling— telling. 
Children  underneath  the  frame 
Appetite  for  play  and  pudding. 
Scraps  of  cloth?    No- 
Memories  brought  forth 
To  bless  the  home 
For  a  time  like  this. 
—Hazel  Loomis
