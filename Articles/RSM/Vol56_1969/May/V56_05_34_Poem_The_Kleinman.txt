[Vol56 | May 1969 | Page 336 | Poem | The Lowly Places | Bertha A. Kleinman]
The walks of famethe high and lofty places, Are not for methe hero ranks are few, And I must tread the lowly rutted spaces And there perfect the humble things I do. The day so big must teem with deeds so little, His imaged race must toil at tasks so small, That somewhere when he weighs each jot and tittle, His loveI knowwill justify them all. 336 Bertha A. Kleinman Pool in Springtime ) ay: Dorothy J.
