[Vol56 | May 1969 | Page 386 | Poem | Mount Rainier | Dorothy J. Roberts]
MOUNT RAINIER After long distances, decorated with green needle And amber cone, hung with veils of hemlock And set with hue of many petals, The mountain peaks emerge, summer-burdened with snow. What once-red violence, long stilled, Burst from the lifted peak A savagery now cooled by converging streams And the breath of a million leaves. PO Dorothy J.
