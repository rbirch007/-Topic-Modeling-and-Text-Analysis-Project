Mrs. Kline has recently been honored with an appointment as a member of the Board of Directors of the National Society of Arts and Letters. 103 EDITORIAL Loyalty g 'Loyalty is one of the noblest attributes of the soul.'' (President David O. McKay.) Loyalty is a most desirable virtue, praised by poets, philosophers, and men of religion; honored by the great and the humble; sought by leaders of nations, churches, and other organizations; achieved by the unselfish and truly loving. Several important traits are encompassed in loyalty. FIDELITY is a facet of loyalty. It implies strict adherence to obligations and duties as well as respect for leaders, peers, and oneself. Fidelity is the virtue which leads one to remember and to keep vows, to be true to God, to fellow men, and to the teachings of parents. Wisdom dictates that one be true to himself in order to build a worthy life. Fidelity describes the true relationship of marriage partners.
