 THE SILVER SONG Little winter sparrows, I wonder why you stay When almost all the feathered folk Seek winter holiday. You glean the seeded berries, The sparsely proffered crumbs, Weather tempered to the cold And wind's bleak, wailing drums.

When the sun obscured from sight Wears its mask of grayyou Perch upon a snowy limb, Giving your song away.
