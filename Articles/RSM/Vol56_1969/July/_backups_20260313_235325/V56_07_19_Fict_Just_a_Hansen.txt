LOVE
Is Not Veiled

Marian Gardner Nielson

[ILLUSTRATION: A pen and ink drawing showing a winding mountain road with a car driving on it, surrounded by hills, trees, and clouds]

■ When the three sisters, Gwen, Mary, and Maggie made the final decision, they knew there were still problems to be solved. Getting Gwen's three lively boys settled at Maggie's would entail planning and cajoling. Telephoning Mary's husband in New York about the change of plans was going to take some diplomacy. The real problem was getting their mother out of the hospital and then getting her comfortably and safely back home. The plan was simple on the surface. Gwen would go to Cedar Mesa on the plane with their mother. Maggie would keep Gwen's children, and Mary would drive down by car first to get the home ready for the mother's homecoming.

Gwen was washing the boys' play clothes, mending overalls, and folding and packing their Sunday clothes. She had a batch of bread in the oven. The baby's bed would be taken to Maggie's later. The trip would probably take four or five days, so she must leave plenty of clothes for the children. Gwen hoped she could get a batch of cookies made to take to Maggie. It would be well hardest on Maggie—what with four youngsters of her own—and she did look a little peaked lately. Gwen had wanted to get a baby sitter but Maggie had insisted on having the three little boys come to her place.
