Ruth G. Rothe 757 Make Your Own Ba ( y Look Grace Diane Jessen Every child treasures the stories, the pictures and mementos that tell of him alone. When these bits are bound together under one cover that can expand with the years as he grows and learns, they become doubly precious. You can make a book for your baby (or a grandchild or a friend) that will be more cherished than any book purchased in a store, because a bit of your love, your personality and creativity will remain stamped onto the pages forever. This book will be less expensive in terms of dollars and cents, but more precious to the one who receives it because you make it just for him. The following directions tell how you can make a basic baby book. You may wish to make many changes or additions to fit your own ideas, and, of course, many pages can be added as the child grows. MATERIALS NEEDED First, purchase these materials from genealogical supplies: One inexpensive ''Book of Remembrance'"' cover. (These cost about 50¢ and are available in different colors and designs.) One personal record page One personal health record page One pedigree chart One portrait pedigree chart One dozen sheets of plain heavy bond paper. (This has prepunched holes as do the other genealogy pages.) Assemble your old greeting cards, appropriate pictures from magazines, a few packages of baby and flower seals, or'a package of baby gift-wrapping paper. These items can be used as you desire to add decorative touches to the pages or youmay prefer to draw your own if you have artistic talent.
