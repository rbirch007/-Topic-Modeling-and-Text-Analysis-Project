Because most of my ancestors were of Pennsylvania Dutch extraction, the food conformed to that mode of cooking. And where could one find better? More than the traditional "seven sweets" and "seven sours" graced the enlarged tables. Wee 756 Black Walnut Gathering Black walnuts had been used in every possible way: in the apple salad with its homemade sweet dressing; in the plain and chocolate cakes and cookies. Broken nutmeats spilled from the white and dark icings and topped the pumpkin pies. After all were satiated with rich food, the men assembled walnut harvesting gearpails and gunny sacks. The older women stayed behind to do the dishes and straighten up the kitchen and dining room, and get caught up on news and recipes. The children dashed ahead, dogs barking and darting between them. When they reached the grove, they vied with each other in finding the biggest nuts, and filling pails. In no time, it seemed, sacks were full and stood in neat rows, waiting to be tied and loaded into the waiting wagon. This done, the children clambered aboard, and with much shouting, urged the horses homeward. The older members of the party walked slowly toward the house savoring the beauty of the day, the fresh air and exercise. Back in the farmyard, the children drank long draughts of cold well water, and washed the grime, but not the stain, from their hands. This would remain for weeks. It would be the identifying mark that led one schoolmate to extend his hands to another, and say, "You've been walnut hunting, too." While the men reloaded the pungent nuts in the various cars, the women scurried about, putting the leftovers from dinner on the table, where each could help himself. As the signs of evening approached, everyone made ready to leave in order to get home by dark. In the corral, cows lowed, horses neighed, and calves bawled for food. Although the red sunset of the "sailor's delight" emblazoned the sky, one could faintly see the outline of the moon. The scarlet leaf of a maple drifted by. Goodbyes were called, the cars started, and people settled back against the hard cushions. Truly, this black walnut gathering had been one to remember!
