AUTUMN REMINDS ME. Of rains washing the earth of dust to make ready her winter sleep. And mothers washing the summer's play from the necks of squirming children. Of lasting autumn flowers bursting with the giving of themselves Into full blooms of perfection, bronzed copper and scented gold. Of cold cement sidewalks F warmed by bright falling leaves, Capped with a cloudless violet sky too deep for summer's frivolity. Of the fresh crispness of young love innocent of the hardships ahead, / But filled with the courage of youth and trust in the love of God. Nola Marilyn Hansen Relief Society ({ ) VY ) CO NCEeSCT /   e = a a oo General Announcement The Relief Society General Board is pleased to announce the second contest for Latter-day Saint women in song writing (lyrics and music) to be conducted annually.
