SALT LAKE STAKE (UTAH), NINETEENTH WARD RELIEF SOCIETY
MAKES HOOKED RUGS IN PROJECT "RAGS TO RICHES"
Left
to
right:
Jeanne Wilkins; Naomi
Bliss; Antonia Van
Otten;
Alice Tolman,
instructor; Cordelia Taylor; Connie Ward.
Marjorie M. Ward,
President,
Salt Lake
Stake
Relief
Society,
reports:
"During
the year 1959 the Relief Society
sisters from the Nineteenth Ward have hooked
these
beautiful
rugs, making them
entirely from discarded woolen clothing and
old blankets,
doing
all the dyeing themselves.
They have learned
the
true
value
of
thrift,
the
real
art
of blending
colors, and
the
joy
of
doing
something
very
worthwhile.
'A
thing
of beauty
is a joy forever.'
With care, these rugs will last for many generations, a luxury
many could not
afford
if they had
to buy them.
These
rugs
cost
so
little,
only the
price of the stamped burlap and a few cents for dye."
