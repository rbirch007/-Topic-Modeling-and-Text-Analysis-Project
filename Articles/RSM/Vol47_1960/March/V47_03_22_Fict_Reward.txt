Reward of Obedience Flora J. Isgreen II at my watch, I noticed I would be on time and hurried on to meeting. How tiny but valuable a watch is, I thought. How intricately made; one part depending on the other; the hands depending on the springs, the springs depending on the service of man. If man did not wind it and give it care, it would have no value for him. The watch works by law. As I walked on to church my mind dwelt on the similarity between the watch and the kingdom of God. All the blessings of God are available for our welfare and good. His whole plan is for us, but this plan works on law. The Lord said that when we obtain any blessing from heaven it is by obedience to the law on which it is predicated. So, like the watch, the kingdom has value only to the man who makes the effort, who will co-operate, who is obedient. He is the man who receives the blessings.
