Fall Drama
Roberta L. Theobald

585 Great literature, great art, and great music enhance the joy of family living. They are gifts of the Heavenly Father to his children everywhere. 'The mother first relates the story which never grows old, she first points out the beauty of the summer day, the glory of the star-studded heavens, she croons the lullaby to the babe in her arms, and sings praises to the Lord. The time she will spend in her life in study and in the guidance of her children will be a benediction to her labors. When her children are in dire distress, easement may come to them through the memory of another's trials, through the comfort of beauty, or through the healing balm of music. 'The Prophet Joseph sought solace previous to his martyrdom in the sweet singing of Elder John Taylor. Today, and in the tomorrows, that mother who has served her children through these media will recognize that the pattern established and fostered by her and her husband continues to enrich and ennoble oncoming generations of the family as well. —M. C. S.
