Organized effort is needed to bring safety education to parents. It has been proved in campaigns against disease—diphtheria, smallpox—that when parents know and are shown how, they act to protect their children.
National statistics tell us that home accidents—over 34,000 deaths in 1948—still remain the number one killer in the accident field.
Communicable diseases have been controlled and reduced as much as sixty to ninety per cent in some fields.
Accidents have become the leading cause of death among children in the United States.
They also bring the greatest loss of productive life of any cause not only of adults but also in the potential contribution that the stricken children might have made to the world.
