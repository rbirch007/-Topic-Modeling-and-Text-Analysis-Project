[Heart-Deep in Winter by Eva Willes Wangsgaard]

Heart-deep in Winter Eva Willes Wangsgaard With you so lately gone it seemed untrue That winter had relaxed and spring was near. Half-heartedly the northern ranks withdrew Relinquishing to spring the infant year; And spring came blithely, undisturbed by death. The soil had quickened; I could feel it stir, Perfumed by hyacinths; the morning's breath Was stroking cheek and throat like rabbit fur. The autumn bulbs erupted wells of gold; The lilacs leafed, for day was April warm; But night kept lagging back with icy cold And threatened lily fringe with frost and storm. Heart-deep in winter, unprepared, alone, The night and I and newly lettered stone! Second Prize Story Annual Relief Society Short Story Contest
