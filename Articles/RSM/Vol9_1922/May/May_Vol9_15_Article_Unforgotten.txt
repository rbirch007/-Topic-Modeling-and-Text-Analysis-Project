[Vol9 | May 1922 | Sequence: 15 | Category: Article | Title: Unforgotten]

Unforgotten "T cannot tell why there should come to me A thought of someone miles and years away, In swift insistence on the memory, Unless there be a need that I should pray. "He goes his way, I mine, we seldom meet To talk of plans or changes day by day, Of pain or pleasure, triumph or defeat, Or special reason why 'tis time to pray. "We are too busy to spare thought For days together of some friends away; Perhaps God does it for us, and we ought To read His signal as a call to pray. "Perhaps, just then, my friend has fiercer fight, A more appalling weakness, a decay Of courage, darkness, some lost sense of right— And so, in case he needs my prayer, I pray. "Friend, do the same for me! If I intrude Unasked upon you, on some crowded day, Give me a moment's prayer as interlude; Be very sure I need it, therefore, pray." —Anon.
