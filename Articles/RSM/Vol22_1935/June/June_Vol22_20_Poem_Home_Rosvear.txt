[Vol22 | June 1935 | Sequence: 20 | Category: Article | Title: Home Rosvear]
What benefit can you imagine from the sun bath? Home By Bertha M. Rosvear Home is such a pleasant place, The things we prize are there, Pictures and books, a shaded lamp Beside an easy chair.
Each room holds something sacred, Prized not for worth alone, But for the joy of knowing It's all our very own.
Home is such a happy place, Our children at our side, A wealth of joy and pride they bring And peace doth there abide.
Each kindly friend and neighbor Will find an open gate, And in our homely pleasures All may participate.
Home is such a hallowed place, Our best beloved are there, Or perhaps a cherished memory Around a vacant chair. Ah!
