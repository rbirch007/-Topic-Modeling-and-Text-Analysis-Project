[Vol22 | February 1935 | Sequence: 01 | Category: Article | Title: The Rock Rainbow of Martineau]
urns
By May D. Martineau
Awarded Second Honorable Mention in the Eliza Roxey Snow Memorial Contest
In the glare of the midday sun
I came upon them
;
Adobe walls unpretentiously
Crumbling to
decay,
Grim,
ugly,
desolate,
forlorn.
I turned away.
Back on the morrow at the spot
I stood enchanted;
Old
ruins
these—an
ancient
fort,
Glamor surrounds
it.
I
see
the
thrilling
battles
fought;
Enthralled I
sit.
I met upon the
street one day
A drab old man;
Faltering of step all bent and slow,
Marked for decay;
Wrinkled, unsightly, decrepit, worn,
I turned away.
Eagerly another day I search
Among the faces;
Ah, here he comes—a pioneer
Such deeds heroic
Are marked upon his countenance.
It is magnetic.
