Relief Society Conference October 2 and 3, 1935 By Julia A. F. Lund, General Secretary The Semi-Annual Conference of the Relief Society was held on October 2 and 3, 1935, in Salt Lake City, Utah. The following is the attendance at the Officers' Meeting, held in the Assembly Hall, Thursday, October 3, at 10 a. M. Ninety-five stakes and fourteen missions were represented —Mission Presidents 14; Stake Presidents 73; Counselors 108; Secretaries 45; Board Members 384; Total 624.
There was a full representation from the General Presidency and Board for the opening day.
During the second day the presence of President Louise Y.
Robison was greatly missed.
This was occasioned by the serious illness of her husband. All the sessions on Wednesday, October 2, were devoted to the educational program of Relief Society. At the Opening Meeting, held in the Tabernacle, subjects dealing with the opportunities offered through the courses of study were discussed, and stress laid upon the spiritual values in life and teaching. In the afternoon Department Sessions were conducted with the chairmen of the various courses in charge. At all the meetings there was a large and enthusiastic attendance of women eager for help to develop the fine educational programs offered. The consultation service in the Music Department attracted many choristers and organists who received valuable instructions from Charlotte O.
Sackett and Alta B. Cassity. Glenn J.
