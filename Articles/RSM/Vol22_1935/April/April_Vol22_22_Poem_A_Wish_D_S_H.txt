A Wish

By D. S. H.

I wish that I might be a tree
That grows beside a little brook,
Where children often come to see
The minnows in each shady nook.

I'd spread my branches wide and low
To make a place for them to play,
And in my shade the flowers would grow
To make their little hearts more gay.

And when the sultry summer sun
Beat down upon the dusty street,
I'd call to every little one:
"Come, rest here in my shade so sweet."

And when the autumn days grew cold,
I'd let my leaves fall, one by one,
To make a carpet, red and gold,
For children's feet to run upon.

And when the winter winds blew wild,
I'd stand up straight and strong and true,
To guard each little sleeping child
From icy blasts and storms that blew.

Oh, if I were a spreading tree,
How happy, happy I would be!
