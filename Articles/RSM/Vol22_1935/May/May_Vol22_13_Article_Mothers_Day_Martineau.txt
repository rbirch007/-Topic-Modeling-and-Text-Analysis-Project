[Vol22 | May 1935 | Sequence: 13 | Category: Article | Title: Mothers Day Martineau]

Mothers' Day By May D. I did not know last Mothers' Day Such joy sublime. How could I when for eight long years Our home, our hearts, despite our prayers, were barren Of the gift divine? I had not felt last Mothers' Day Such joy intense.
Longing and envy filled my soul, That all but I had rightly won the tributes paid In recompense. Martineau The flowers they gave last Mothers' Day In sympathy To me were given, or so it seemed To one whose heart was sorely tried and could not keep In harmony. But Ah! The joy this Mothers' Day, My cup runs over. No more I weep; I am at peace, For tenderly within my arms I hold so close My foster daughter.  
RELIEF SOCIETY MAGAZINE
