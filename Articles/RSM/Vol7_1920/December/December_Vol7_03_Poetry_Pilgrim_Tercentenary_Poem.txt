[Vol7 | December 1920 | Sequence: 03 | Category: Poem | Title: Pilgrim Tercentenary Poem]

PILGRIM TERCENTENARY. The Pilgrim fathers find many to celebrate them in this Tercentenary year. This poem from Abbie Farwell Brown's "Heart of New England" pays tribute to The Pilgrim Mothers. Now thank God for the women Who dared the perilous sea With our adventurous ancestors, To bear them company! They sailed, they knew not whither, They came, nor questioned why, But that the men-folk whom they loved Without their care would die. Babes newly born they carried. And bairns with wavering feet;  But never a cow was there for milk, And never a stove for heat. Through icy waves they landed, They washed in frozen streams;  They shivered through the nights of dread With horror in their dreams. They had no voice in meeting, No vote in pact or law;  But of their flesh and blood is built Our strength for peace and war.
