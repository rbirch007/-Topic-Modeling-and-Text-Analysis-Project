Dawn is their tumbled music As morning moments bring The rosy voice of pigeons, Calling and answering.  Ethel Jacobson 293 Enalish I Cooker Mary A. Adams Lowestoft, Suffolk, England FISH CAKES 1 Ib. Whitefish (cod, halibut, or other fish) 1 Ib. Cooked mashed potatoes 1 onion, chopped salt and pepper, as desired 2 eggs, beaten bread crumbs Remove skin and bones from fish and flake into a basin. Mix in the potato, onion, salt, pepper, and beaten eggs. Form into patties, using flour on hands, and dip into the crumbs. Press crumbs on well so they stick to cakes. Fry in shallow fat, turning occasionally, for three to five minutes, until crisp and golden. (Makes 6 cakes.) VEAL AND HAM PIE 1 Ib. Cooked filet veal ¥ tsp. Mixed herbs 4 oz. Bacon or ham 1 gill (% pint) veal stock or water 2 hard-boiled eggs, sliced flaky pastry 2 tsp. Chopped parsley 2 egg yolks, beaten salt and pepper to taste Cut the veal into one-inch cubes, cut up bacon or ham, and slice the eggs. Put the meat and eggs in a baking dish in layers, sprinkling the seasoning on each layer. Add stock, cover with pastry. Make a hole in center. Decorate with pastry ''leaves,"' glaze with yolk of egg. Put on halfway shelf in oven, preheated to 400 degrees, and bake about 45 minutes. If uncooked meat is used, cook for 12 hours, turn heat lower after 45 minutes. Serve with potatoes and a vegetable or salad. SWEET COURSEAPPLE CHARLOTTE 4 oz.
