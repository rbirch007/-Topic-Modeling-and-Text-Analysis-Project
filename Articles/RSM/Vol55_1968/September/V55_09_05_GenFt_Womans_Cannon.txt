AUTUMNAL 
These  wild  flamboyant  colors 
The  season  has  put  on 
Have  found  my  heart  exulting 
Over  hill  and  town. 
The  aspens  on  the  pathway 
Are  wrapped  in  flame  and  gold, 
And  all  the  woods  are  crimson 
Where  maple  trees  are  bold. 
Here  willows  drape  the  ridges 
In  panicles  of  brown 
And  all  the  seeded  grasses 
With  wealth  are  bending  down. 
Oh,  fall  has  teased  the  fancy, 
Written  itself  in  rhyme- 
But  a  leaf  drifts  down  in  warning 
Lest  I  forget  the  time. 
—Linnie  F.  Robinson
