[Vol55 | July 1968 | Page 505 | Poem | Dagger Points Times Five | Kathryn Kay]
Ome arrene ae an ee = a I DAGGER POINTS TIMES FIVE Let me remember, let me gather love-brimmed thoughts culled from the shining of companioned years, small incidents to be blue-bright forget-me-nots to light my lonely dark of unshed tears. Our understanding forms a bond, a lasting tie, infrangible, which time cannot destroy. I You live still in the thoughts which diamond-stud my sky I with stars full quintupled with pain as well as joy. For I have learned that each star has jagged points, sharp, and ancipital to pierce my calm, but, also, I have learned each passing year anoints I the painful wound with time's hurt-healing balm. Each star has dagger points times five, and yet 1 let me remember—let me not forget! —Kathryn Kay - 506 v a
