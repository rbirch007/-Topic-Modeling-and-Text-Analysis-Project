CLOSE NEIGHBORS Their windows looked north into our own Through our crooked apple tree And there was room beside the fence For hollyhocks to be. Their children and ours would run and play { As back and forth they bounced, And we never worried at mealtime If some guests came unannounced. I We watched for the smoke from their chimney In the early morning light. We knew when they made their bed-time retreat In the quiet country night. —Melba S. Payne SS 598 2 c.
