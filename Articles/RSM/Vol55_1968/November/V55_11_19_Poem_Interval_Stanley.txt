INTERVAL Today I saw him sitting in the sun, His well-worn hat pulled down to shade his eyes, His shoulders bowed through years of endless toil To wrest a meager living from the soil. With trembling hands grasping a battered cane, He waits until the summer comes again. —Elena Hassell Stanley
