[Vol28 | June 1941 | Sequence: 35 | Category: Poem | Title: Let Us Sing to Love]

Let us sing to love which is from God,
For love is found in all the universe.
Love, like the still waters, is deep;
It is refreshing to the weary traveler.

Love has the strength of the mountains
And endures even as they.
The heart is uplifted by it as a bird that soars above the mountain, singing.

Love has the straight growth of a tree reaching for sunshine;
It bears fruit in rich abundance.

Love has the brightness and warmth of the sun,
The glory of its rising,
And the quiet of its setting.

Love is the shadow of a tree at noon-day
And a welcome shelter in storm.

Nowhere can be found anything like love
For it has the beauty of a moonlit valley,
And it is a bright star;
It has the delicacy of a newly opened bud,
And it fills the soul as the perfume of a rare flower.

It is wildly beating as the high-tossed waves of the sea
And as calm as a desert evening
With whisperings and the caress of gentle breezes.

In love
There are heights unclimbed,
There are depths unsounded,
There is vastness unmeasured,
And there is the intimate closeness of the drop of dew hidden in the flower's heart.

Let us sing all together in praise of love,
For love is wonderful past understanding.
It is a choice gift from God.
