The work was intended to symbolize the historic friendship between the two republics and to typify the idea of freedom and brotherhood which underlies a republican form of government. Only a few people are aware of the poetic inscription inside the pedestal of the Statue of Liberty written by Emma Lazarus: "Give me your tired, your poor, Your huddled masses yearning to breathe free, The wretched refuse of your teeming shore Send these, the homeless, the tempest-tost, To me. I lift my lamp beside the golden door." The statue stands on Bedloe Island, also known as Liberty Island, in New York Harbor.
