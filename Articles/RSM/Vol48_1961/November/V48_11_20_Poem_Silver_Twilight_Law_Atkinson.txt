[Vol48 | November 1961 | Sequence: 20 | Category: Poem | Title: Silver Twilight | Author: Mabel Law Atkinson]
Silver Swilight Mabel Law Atkinson My sun still high was darkened.

Spent with tears, I knew the blighting kiss of early frost.

Along a pathway shadowed by my fears I held the hand of God till I had crossed The bridge of hope to peace, and lifted up My eyes to see the sun. And now I sing With clearer tones; drink from a star-filled cup While beauty rims the shadows lingering.

Above the bronzing hills of truth, where still I garner dreams, I see the first white star That preludes night.

Beyond the last high hill The dawn will wake new dreams. My eyes afar, With love about me like an accolade, I walk the silver twilight unafraid.
