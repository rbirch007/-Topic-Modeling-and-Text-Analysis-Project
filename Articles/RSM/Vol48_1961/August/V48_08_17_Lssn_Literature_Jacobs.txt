SL iterature—America's Literature Comes of Age Lesson 26 — John Greenleaf Whittier, Commoner (1807-1892) Elder Briant S. Jacobs (Textbook: America's Literature, by James D. Hart and Clarence Gohdes Dryden Press, New York, pp. 400-413) For Tuesday, November 21, 1961 Objective: To enter more fully into the nineteenth century by blending together Whittier's three voices, as a Reformer, Quaker, and a New Englander. LTHOUGH not one of the greatest American authors, Whittier, next to Longfellow, was the most popular nineteenth century poet, and today remains one of the most-quoted, best-loved spokesmen for the "Party of Security."
