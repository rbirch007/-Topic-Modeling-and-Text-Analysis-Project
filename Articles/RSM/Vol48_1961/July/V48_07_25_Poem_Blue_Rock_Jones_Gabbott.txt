[Vol48 | July 1961 | Sequence: 25 | Category: Poem | Title: Blue Rock | Author: Mabel Jones Gabbott]
Blue Rock Mabel Jones Gabbott Half way up the hillside was a flat blue rock, Here we paused in climbing to rest and talk Of how the narrow railway tied a silver bow Around our little valley; how the fields below Plowed, or newly planted, or rich in harvest board, Made the valley table a king-size checkerboard, How the little houses, chimneyed snug and warm, Circled all completely, standing arm in arm, Sometimes we looked inward, and read each other's heart; Then, we walked down the hillside, lingering, loath to part.
