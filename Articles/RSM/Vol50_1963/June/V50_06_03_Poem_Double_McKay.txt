[Vol50 | June 1963 | Page 415 | Poem | Double Exposure | Elaine Stirland McKay]
Double Exposure Elaine Stirland McKay Oh, how I love the sun's first rays Upon a mountain peak— The gold surprising purple hills And playing hide and seek. While streaming down the mountainside And causing day to break Across the world... And leaving trails Of gladness in its wake. But let me watch the sun go down Upon vast level plains Where as a child I saw it set A hundred clouds to flames Above an earth which gentle hands Had smoothed ail wrinkle free, And left a solitary oak To guard tranquility.
