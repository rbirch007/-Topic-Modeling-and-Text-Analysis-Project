513 Josef Muench 514 Mount Hood and Lost Lake, Oregon Portals of Summer Ida Elaine James I pause within tall portals, slim and straight, As one who lingers in a vestibule Of long, long corridors, before I sate My being with the dim and sweetly cool Beauty promised me within the great Green vistas of the forest. Is there one Who dares to drop upon these leaves and prate Of self's abandoned hopes, of life's work done? I lean my ear on stems that sweetly bend To my tired cheek, whereunder insects lie No more encumbered in their crypt than I, And loose my stifled, harrowed soul, and blend My psalm with lullabies of birds and flowers.. A rite that cleanses me from wasted hours.
