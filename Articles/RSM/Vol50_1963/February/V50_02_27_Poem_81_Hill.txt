[Vol50 | February 1963 | Page 95 | Poem | 81 Woman With Birds | Lael W. Hill]
Woman With Birds Lael W. Hill WN With twittering small words They tell of her as ''friend'"" — Who feeds and shelters birds: \ No jay too brash to tend, \ No slum-born sparrow too slight. Ay Lp All wintered birds descend In by-the-ways of flight; ff In hungering and cold They flutter, they alight. She coaxes them, Be bold! She spreads the banquet crumbs, The suet richly doled. To shelf, to sill, there comes The motley feathered crowd, Partaking as of plums; Till having feasted, proud And preeningly in rows, They chirp their thank-yous, loud With love, through wind and snows, Whatever way she goes. 9
