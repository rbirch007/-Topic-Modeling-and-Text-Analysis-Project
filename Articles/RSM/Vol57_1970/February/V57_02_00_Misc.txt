The Cover: Frontispiece: Art Layout: IMustrations: WINTER SONG Ice is in the wind tonight glazing in the swale. I have heard a bittern bird weeping on a rail. I have watched a listless bee Crawling to a hollow tree, Its summer-swift and reaching wings Trailing, spent and useless things. In brittle light of frozen night A snow fox finds his lair On Cavern Peak, and now I seek My fireplace and my chair. I! Am snowbound. A winter sound Easing silence in my house Is midnight gnawing of a mouse. With drowsing year I hush and hear New snow sliding off my roof, Hoarfrost shattered by a hoof. Peggy Tangren Reflection of Trees and Mountains Transparency by Hal Rumel Lithographed in Full Color by Deseret News Press Winter Near Park City, Utah - Photograph by Elizabeth Simon Dick Scopes Mary Scopes
