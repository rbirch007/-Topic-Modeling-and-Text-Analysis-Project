[On Evening Wings, Dorothy J. Roberts]

EVENING WINGS All places are edges, save where you are— All, periphery and perimeter, you the hub— All places rims and horizons And spokes extending from you. Yet so gentle you are, meadowed In the matrix where my tether is staked— Buttercup-yellow, new-green and meadow-fresh. How gossamer the slender filament between us, Yet how magnet and center you are for me. Even above summits I hear you, and at sea, From sand and season and all petaled flowering. From horizoned far and purple distance Lights beckon in a glowing circle Beyond which I am not moved to remain. With evening I return To hover around the memory of you, Back to the tether of your warmth, To the valley of meanings Taller than this stony triumph over cliffs. From acquaintance with clouds I come, Down the torn edges of thunder, Always returning on evening wings. Shelter, you are to me, Yet all freedom. —Dorothy J.
