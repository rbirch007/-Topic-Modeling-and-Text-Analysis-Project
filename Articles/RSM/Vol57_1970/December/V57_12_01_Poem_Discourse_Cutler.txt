[Vol57 | December 1970 | Page 0 | Poem | Discourse on Leaves Grayce | E. Cutler]
DISCOURSE ON LEAVES = mY Consider the leaves of summer cAI Which are really of autumn a g~Y As they silently drift to lie Cea Frozen in a pool of light To become winter leaves That once were leaves of Spring green, fragile At a rough touch, snapping Under the quick takeoff Of a bird with black feathers / To fall in the light pool Beside other winters, Now brown-soaked and sunk In new wells of water, Fodder for trailing roots of old trees.
