[Vol57 | December 1970 | Page 893 | Poem | If I Should Call Them Now, | Peggy Tangren]
ae IF I SHOULD CALL THEM NOW The little ones, The timid ones, The gentle ones, Bright of eye and wary, Darting, gathering, hoarding Beneath root and rock, on limb, in hollow tree; Answering my call, ''hiyahiya,'' The squirrels and chipmunks came From cone-tipped spruce and pine, Leaping logs and stones To my reaching hands, My feeding hands, My stroking hands. If I should call them now they could not come. Deep, deep'in trance-like sleep In chambered tree, in burrowed earth, They cannot banish winter from the woods, Nor hear the murmur in my loneliness. They wait the joyful ''hiya'' of the spring. I, too, am waitingand remembering. Peggy Tangren
