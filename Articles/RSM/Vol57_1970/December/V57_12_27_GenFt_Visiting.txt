[Visiting Teaching in Alaska]

To this lowly, sinning woman Jesus gave some of the most beautiful teachings of his ministry. What was Jesus saying to us? Was he not bidding us not to judge but to look within the heart? Was he not calling us out of the world to give up its values and adopt his way of loving? Jesus was monumentally kind to sinners. Many of them he knew would be redeemed if given a chance. Should we do less? A few years ago a group of women were having lunch together. A very attractive and talented woman walked by. When she was out of hearing, one of the women asked, "Did you know that she has been married four times 'and that it is widely held that some of her children are illegitimate?" The woman went on to elaborate. As she was still speaking, one of the group interrupted: "Isn't it wonderful that this woman has learned the great lesson of repentance? She blesses all our lives through her talent. Everyone is richer because of her. She is always gracious and willing. Let us all agree to say nothing of what we have heard today so that it will never be an obstacle to her or her family." Shamefacedly, the members of the group agreed. Does our neighbor work at a very menial job? Does she speak ungrammatically? Does she do things that offend us? Do we shun her as not our equal? The good news of Jesus Christ was that he welcomed the repentant sinner. He understood the hearts of men and women and recognized their goodness when it was present. He was unhampered in his love by awkward customs, despised jobs, lack of prestige, lack of wealth, or lack of power.
