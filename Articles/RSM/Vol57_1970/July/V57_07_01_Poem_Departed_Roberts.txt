[Vol57 | July 1970 | Page 0 | Poem | Departed from Desert Dorothy | J. Roberts.]
Gold, green, and fallow-brown lie DEPARTED FROM DESERT The checkered fields, quilting all distances. The unwinking black eyes of berries Dorothy J. Roberts Coax, by the roadside. Frail mist weds the sun, And the tossing sea talks in incessant liquid Accent to the shore. Beauty drops Its easy mantle to all periphery While I remember one white lily, Determined, on the blistering hills of sand. 530
