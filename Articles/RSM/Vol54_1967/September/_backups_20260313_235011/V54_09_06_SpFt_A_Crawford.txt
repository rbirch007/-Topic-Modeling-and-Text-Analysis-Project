The years following 1868, were more than a time of restoration of the beginnings in Nauvoo and a spreading of the patterns already established—more than a time of enlarging the boundaries—they were in spirit and accomplishment a foretelling of this present time, when the sisters of many lands have found the answer to their yearnings for companionship, for comfort, for learning, for inspiration, for direction in the work of their hands, for guidance in their ministrations, for enlightenment in their homes, and for light along the pathway to eternal life.
