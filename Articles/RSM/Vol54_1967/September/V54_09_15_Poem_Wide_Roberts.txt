WIDE AUTUMN Dorothy J. Roberts Autumn stretches the landscape wide; The valleys spread, the hills retreat And stand in silence, blue and far. The golden trees repeat, repeat. There never was this width before — The tiers of mountains to the west, The circle of horizons spread — So much of harvest here compressed. Oh, spring is intimate and close And summer stops the eye with green, This layered splendor in between. The gold of stubble brightens all. Vision stretches miles to touch The bright periphery of fall. 677
