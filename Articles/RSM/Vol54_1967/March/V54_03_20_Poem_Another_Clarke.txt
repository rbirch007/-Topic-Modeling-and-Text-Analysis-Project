[Vol54 | March 1967 | Page 203 | Poem | Another Spring Linda | L. Clarke]
ANOTHER SPRING Linda L. Clarke The pines are mine, the dogwood bower, The stars, the moon, and every hour; Trails that only deer have known Are here for me to make my own. I taste sea spray upon my lips While sand runs through my fingertips And then I stand on desert land With sprigs of sagebrush in my hand. What could be more fair to see Than lilacs blooming on a tree? What could be more fair to hear Than song-birds cradled in the air? Then why is it when all is mine That I become aware of time? This one thought evades my reason, Have I gained or lost a season?
