[Vol54 | March 1967 | Page 239 | Poem | River Marsh Eva | Willes Wangsgaard]
RIVER MARSH Eva Willes Wangsgaard No life is here, No mallard phalanx, no call of loon, No splash of carp along the edge Of the still lagoon. Only old nests of sedge Stiffened and sere, Cupping hard eggs of snow Where frozen rushes swerve Over the sterile fruits, Snuggled against the roots Off reed and sedge and rush Above the windless hush Where wintry waters curve In slow, unrippled flow. Yet, well I know Some miracle will loose A rocket burst of sound To tinkle round on round Across the sky And meet the killdee's cry When blackbird heralds bring An April truce, Shrill, red-winged chorusing The green surprise of spring.
