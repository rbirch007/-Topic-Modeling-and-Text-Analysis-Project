3) 206 HAL RUMEL A Daisy Luncheon for Springtime Florence G. Williams Springtime brings the flowers—and floral table settings—to be combined with appropriate recipes for a springtime luncheon. Use your "daisy dishes" on place mats of contrasting color (blue, in any deep, rich shade, is effective) and arrange a bouquet of daisies for the centerpiece. If your dishes are some other pattern in yellow, other flowers can be attractive for a similar table setting. Use daffodils, hyacinths, tulips, marigolds or zinnias (if you have some early ones). Or, if your dishes have a pink motif, use pink flowers. However, the following recipes are planned for a yellow table setting. Orange Rolls 1 c. Warm water 14 c. Sugar 4 c. Flour 1 tsp. Salt 14 c. Shortening (butter or 3 eggs.. Substitute) 3 oranges 2 yeast cakes Dissolve yeast in warm water. Mix together all ingredients. After mixture rises a couple times, roll out thin on a board.
