At Half-Past Autumn Peggy Tangren The year is measured by a shadow's length And now it is half-past autumn. Behind small weirs of sodden maple leaves Rain waters deepen into little lakes; The cradling nest has emptied To a sky wedge of swift wings. Two months ago this forest reeled And roared defiance In wars with storm and wind And held its roots. But now, at half-past autumn, A soughing fills the pauses in the woods' deep breathing. October, tremulous and slow, Descends the mountain Down from cliff and canyon To the drowsing hills, Then waits, as an aging patriarch Who, seeing beyond his sightless eyes, A vision of resurgence, and its keeping, Stands waiting with the waiting land.
