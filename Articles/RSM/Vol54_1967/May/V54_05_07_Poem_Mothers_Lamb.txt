[Mother's Day Patricia A. Lamb]
MOTHER'S DAY

Downy soft, and cuddly, blue as a baby's eyes;
She turns the bootee gently and breathes a longing sigh.
Under the folds of tissue the other bootee lies,
Wetted once by scalding tears—now dry.

She folds them both together and puts them back to rest
In the cedar chest of treasures she loves the very best.
Someday when the time is right, when God thinks it is best,
She'll use them for the baby at her breast.

♦ Patricia A. Lamb
