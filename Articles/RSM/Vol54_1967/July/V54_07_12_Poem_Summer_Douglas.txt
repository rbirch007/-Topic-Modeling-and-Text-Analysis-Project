[Summer Senses Gilean Douglas]

I think of summer as a time of senses—
The feel of warm earth under bare brown feet,
The sound of bees among the sweet pea tendrils,
The scent of new-mown hay and clover sweet.

I think of summer as a time of tasting—
Cool water from a well, and berries warm
With captured sunshine, picked at dewy morning
Before the heat can work its wilting harm.

I think of summer as a time of seeing—
The flash of hummingbird at trumpet vine,
The lazy drift of white clouds cross the heavens,
The way the light slants golden through the pine.

But most I think of summer as the season
When all my senses, blended, seem to say
That earth is good, and life is sweet for living,
And every moment is a holy day.
