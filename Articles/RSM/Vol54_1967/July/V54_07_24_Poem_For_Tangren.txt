[Vol54 | July 1967 | Page 542 | Poem | For This I Have Wept | Peggy Tangren]
FOR THIS I HAVE WEPT Peggy Tangren For this I have wept for a single flower Dying before parturition hour Of ripened seed. Its promised worth Denied a somber yearning earth, Willing no blossom to a flowerless plain, Gone forever for the want of rain. With oceans, lakes, and rivers, still Beauty can die on a thirsty hill.
