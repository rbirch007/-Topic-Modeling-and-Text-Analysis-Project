A HERITAGE Lois M. Anderson Alone I walked in the somber dusk, Resigned to my burden of grief; Feeling akin to the winter trees And the end of a life, too brief. Stark were their branches against the gray sky, Stripped by the north wind's sweep; No awakening forces arose to transcend Their heritage of sleep. Then a shaft of sunlight pierced the clouds Like a curtain parted at night, Tinting the bare boughs above my head With a luminous, golden light; And I suddenly visioned each cycle of life Impelled in a seasonal swing; When each in its autumn succumbed to the cold— It awaited another spring! Now, beneath the snow, each golden leaf Must moulder into dust— But, oh, I will remember the glory and the green These cold limbs hold in trust! ~ 828 Ramona W.
