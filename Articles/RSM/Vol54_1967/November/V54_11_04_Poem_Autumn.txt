[Autumn Leaves Linnie Fisher Robinson The Sisters Gathered, G/7//an G. Brown-Lee 809]

Autumn Leaves Linnie Fisher Robinson So many times in years gone by, When autumn leaves were sailing high, We children ran across the grass Nor dreamed how fast the seasons pass But thought our days would all repeat. Today, as leaves in winds blow wild, I wish I were a little child, To go to bed without a care Save that I said my evening prayer, And kissed my parents life was sweet. Oh, leaves, that in the winds must play, My autumn, too, has come this day; So have my spring and summer flown And all my ties more loosely grown The cycle moves and it is fleet. The cycle moves and it is sweet Soon, soon I go on leaf, blown feet.
