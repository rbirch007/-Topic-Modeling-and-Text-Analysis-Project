INTRODUCTORY COMMENT Love is the most universal subject of literature, as well as the great principle of the gospel. Sermons and scriptures and also thousands of novels, stories, poems, dramas, and essays have been published emphasizing the values of love and exploring its many variations—romantic love, family love, God's love, our love of God, love of truth, self-love, love of friends and_ neighbors, which point out also counterfeits of genuine love—pride, lust, and greed. One of the most important things to know about love is that it benefits the one who loves as much as the one loved. Likewise, he who hates is corroded more than he who is hated. The magic, the mystery, and the miracle of love are inthe wonder of loving 952 as much as in the wonder of being loved.
