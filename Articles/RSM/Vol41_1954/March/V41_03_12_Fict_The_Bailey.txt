HE had devised a plan of sorts by the time Kirby came home for lunch, and so intent was she on it that she missed her usual pleasure in his comforting masculine presence. "Margaret Marie is coming for a visit, Kirby," she told him as he kissed her. His eyes lighted with pleasure. "No! When?" "Day after tomorrow." "How did she manage?" he asked with interest. "Fixchange teacher," Beth replied. "She's simply thrilled. Says she can visit five days before she'll have to assume her duties." "That's fine," Kirby said. "Give you girls time to catch up on the six years since you were an exchange student in England." He grinned. "After all, since then you've acquired a husband, a fiveyear-old daughter, a son with a defnite will of his own, and a rundown, poorly stocked ranch, with a view!" They both glanced toward the west wall of the dining room. Through the wide expanse of the newly installed window, the two peaks of Twin Knolls Mountain rose in silent majesty in the distance.
