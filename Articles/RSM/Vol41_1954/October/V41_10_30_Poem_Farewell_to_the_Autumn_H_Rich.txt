Farewell to the Autumn Leaves
Lettie B. H. Rich

All day the autumn leaves drop down
By silent breezes stirred;
A hush as if someone passing—
Scarcely a sound is heard.

Then twilight stars shine bright;
The moon is lost behind the cloud.
The earth grows dark as if in mourning
Wrapped in a somber shroud.

The raindrops softly kiss the leaves,
The wind sighs a tender last farewell
As the dead leaves fall on the walk.
