A GREAT CHURCH UNIVERSITY

By F. S. HARRIS
President of the Utah Agricultural College

The Brigham Young University is one of the greatest educational institutions in the West. It is great not only because of its size, but because of the character of work it is doing and the spirit that pervades the institution.

The University has grown from very small beginnings. When it was established in 1875, it had but one building and a handful of students. Today it has a beautiful campus with many substantial buildings and an enrollment of more than 2,500 students.

The growth of the institution has been steady and substantial. Each year has seen an increase in the number of students and in the scope of the work offered. The University now offers work in practically every field of human knowledge and grants degrees in the various colleges and schools.

The spirit that pervades the institution is one of its greatest assets. The students and faculty are imbued with high ideals and noble purposes. There is a spirit of cooperation and helpfulness that makes the institution a real community of scholars.

The University is doing a great work in training young men and women for useful careers. It is sending out graduates who are making their mark in the world and who are contributing to the progress of civilization.

The Brigham Young University is indeed a great church university. It is an institution of which the Church and the people of Utah may well be proud. It is making a valuable contribution to the cause of education and to the welfare of humanity.
