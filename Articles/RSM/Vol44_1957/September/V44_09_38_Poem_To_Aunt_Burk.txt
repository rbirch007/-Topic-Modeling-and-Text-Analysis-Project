~>- Jo Aunt Cetty s Old Blue Bowl Matia McClelland Burk I like to sit at evening And watch its soft blue glow. It crossed the harsh and dreary plains In a handcart long ago. It once companioned weariness And tragic loss and pain; It held the frugal sustenance Of those who crossed the plain. Then, when they reached the valley, Its lupine-colored grace Still comforted Aunt Cetty's heart In that far, lonely place. And when her heart was lifted In plea or thankful prayer, As blue as any April sky The bowl was standing there. Now still the bowl can touch my heart, And like an azure wraith, It brings back dear Aunt Cetty, With her courage and her faith. WYotes.
