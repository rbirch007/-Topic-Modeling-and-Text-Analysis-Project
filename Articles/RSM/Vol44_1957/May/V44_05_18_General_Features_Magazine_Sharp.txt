Magazine Subscriptions for 1956 Counselor Marianne C. Sharp THE callings of stake and ward, mission and branch Relief Society Magazine representatives, if faithfully performed, have far-reaching results. Not only do they render service to Relief Society today through bringing the spirit of Relief Society into Latter-day Saint homes within the stakes of the Church and in the far distant lands of the earth, but their work will not be forgotten in the years ahead. By commandment of the Lord, the Latter-day Saints are a recordkeeping people. So, today, we turn to the pages of the Woman's Exponent to read of the record of Relief Society from 1872 to 1914, as it was reported from societies in that day. And for the enlightenment of those sisters, we find our early great Relief Society leaders sharing their experiences of Nauvoo in the pages of the Woman's Exponent. 'These precious papers give us understanding and appreciation of Relief Society's work of earlier years. Had sisters of that day not acted as agents for the Woman's Exponent, these copies would not be found today. In this day The Relief Society Magazine is presenting and preservRelief Society history. 'To the Magazine representatives throughout the world, the general board expresses its heartfelt thanks for their love of Relief Society which urges them to fulfill their callings so nobly. Year by year, as the blessings of Relief Society are extended to a greater Page 328 membership of Latter-day Saint women, The Relief Society Magazine likewise enters new portals to bring inspiring and refining influences to an enlarged circle of sisters. The increase in number of subscriptions in 1956 is the largest recorded — 10,465 — the subscriptions as of December 31, 1956 were 148,562 and the number in December 31, 1955 was 138,097. When one considers the zeal and effort sometimes expended in securing one subscription, one may realize even though dimly, the great amount of work and the devotion which this great number of subscriptions represents. One year's award subscription which is given to each Magazine representative whose subscriptions equal seventy-five per cent of the respective enrolled Relief Society membership, is but a small token of regard. The reward to Magazine representatives is in the blessings which each one receives for having accepted the calling and faithfully per. Formed it, and these blessings are not confined to our life here. Recently a mission Relief Society president said that she considered The Relief Society Magazine one of the best proselyting mediums in the mission over which her husband presides. For the tenth year, South Los Angeles Stake leads the stakes of the Church with 253 per cent. 'They have 1143 enrolled Relief Society members and 2896 subscriptions. MAGAZINE SUBSCRIPTIONS FOR 1956 South Gate Ward of that stake reached 481 per cent, with eightyfive members and 40g subscriptions. In 1956 there are 222 stakes on the honor roll and 1949 wards. 'This compares very favorably with the 1955 report which had 204 stakes on the honor roll and 1739 wards and branches in stakes. 'Twenty-six stakes in 1956 had every one of their wards over one hundred per cent, which is seven less than last year. Fifteen missions are on the honor roll in 1956, an increase of one over the previous year, and 642 branches, whereas there were only 486 mission branches on the honor roll in 1955. 'he missions are to be commended for this excellent increase. As each Magazine representative goes about her calling, her interest is centered — and rightly so — upon 329 her particular ward or branch. The stake and mission Magazine representatives are concerned with larger geographic-ecclesiastical units, but the real significance of the work of the Magazine representative is approximated only when the statistics for the entire Church are studied. Then the light of the service of each faithful, devoted individual Magazine representative joined with the lights of hundreds of her sister Magazine representatives bursts into a great flame of service. It is the hope of the general board that the words of instruction and encouragement in The Relief Society Magazine may be as a lamp to help guide the sisters who read and follow the teachings of their beloved Relief Society. Then will the Magazine representatives feel their labors have indeed borne fruit. Cloner for Highest Ratings Stake South Los Angeles (California) 253% Magazine Representative—Edna C.
