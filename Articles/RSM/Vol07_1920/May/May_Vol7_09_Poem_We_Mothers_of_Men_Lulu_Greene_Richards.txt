WE MOTHERS OF MEN
By Lulu Greene Richards
For May, 1920

Where do we stand, we mothers of men?
Kisses and flowers on lip and brow!
As babes we carried them first and then
Led them as often they lead us now.
Carry and lead we as younger ones,
As elders follow our true, brave sons.

From babe to manhood, how quickly past!
A flower, a kiss, and the day is gone.
Tender emotions — how rich, how vast
Thy realm, oh love, from its eve till dawn!
And a long, bright day approaches when
Joy shall be full for mothers of men!
