Biographical sketches of Elder Blaine M. Porter and Charlotte A. Larsen follow. BLAINE M. Porter, author of the social science lessons "Spiritual Living in the Nuclear Age,' was born in Morgan, Utah, the second son of Ernest and Edna Brough Porter. He graduated from Morgan High School and Morgan L. DS. Seminary. He attended Utah State University and Weber College, where he was in the first graduating class from the Institute of Religion at Weber College. Dr. Porter served in the United States Air Force as a pilot during World War II, during which time he was awarded both US and RAF wings. He returned to Brigham Young University, where he received his Bachelor's and Master's Degrees. His Ph. D. Was received at Cornell University, Ithaca, New York. He was a member of the faculty at Iowa State College for five years, and, since the fall of 1955, he has been professor and chairman of the Department of Human Development and Family Relationships at Brigham Young University.
