Grandmothers Who Baby Sit Camilla Woodbury Judd We must remember to keep well-filled cookie jars, Some dough to fashion men of gingerbread; Treasures to cuddle, eyes joy-lit with stars For little ones who would be tender-fed. There must be eager faces at the door And loving arms to lift for an embrace, A legacy of patterned story lore And picture books to light a little face; A game of make-believe for wee pretender, Band-aids for wounds, a kiss for weepy eyes, Old curtain lace to trail in gold-heeled splendor, And rocking chairs for bedtime lullabies; License to spoil a bit, reprove when rude — Much patience and a prayer of gratitude.
