[Vol46 | February 1959 | Sequence: 02 | Category: Article | Title: The | Author: Nibley]
[The Central Atlantic States Mission Preston R. Nibley]

When we talk about juvenile delinquency, we comfort ourselves with the thought that, after all, only about six to seven per cent of all the juveniles in the United States ever get arrested. But what about those whose sins are of a kind for which arrests are not made? For instance what about the intimacies that develop in the wake of young people dating too steadily at too early an age? What about the evergrowing menace of petting, a terrible perversion which is spreading among the young people of the Nation of the high school age and up? What of the rise of drinking and smoking among teenagers? What about the rise of the gang spirit among both boys and girls? What about the ever-increasing spirit of taking a dare which leads to the breaking of most of our standards, including the loss of virtue? There is the delinquency also among young married people, some of whom engage in drinking parties in their own homes and patronize public taverns, and there violate the standards of the Church. Some of these young married people organize into card clubs of various kinds, and it is not long until there is, at least, minor gambling going on. Betting and other forms of gambling in the bridge clubs, and canasta clubs, and other types of card clubs are prevalent in the United States. One of the great difficulties with this type of partying going on among young married couples is that it often leads to dating with each others husbands or wives, leading to a breakdown of the sanctity of marriage.
