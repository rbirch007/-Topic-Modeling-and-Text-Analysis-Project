The real Jonathan Edwards

He wanted

If

even

he

aware of these weaknesses and strove
to overcome them.
Yet
so great a
power was he in their lives, that he
was
the
highest-paid
minister
in
interior
Massachusetts,
and
people

towns to pack his meetinghouse.
Born

Instead

who feared Harvard's tendencies to-
ward

This
school was
renamed Yale College while he was
still an undergraduate student.
Edwards'
precociousness
made
him a legend in his own day.
As a
child he had mastered Hebrew, Lat-
in, and Greek, tutored by his father
who
delighted
in
guiding
his

through
difficult
writers

attention

self-discipline.
He

only
a
lad when
first he
read

difficult Essay on the
Human Undeistanding.
From it he
received a delight
''greater than the
most greedy miser finds when gath-

discovered
