You Can Sew— XIII — Selection of Childrens Clothes Jean R. Jennings EWING for children of all ages is, no doubt, one of the most important phases of the home-sewing program. Fortunate, indeed, are children whose mothers sew with skill and a flair for fashion rightness. Too much emphasis cannot be placed on the importance of fashion in children's clothing. Even though there are always stable styles, such as pinafores, smocked dresses, the standard frocks with full skirts and tight-fitting bodices, there are new innovations in styles and materials which are noteworthy. Children can and should be as aware of good taste and good grooming as their elders. Early training along this line can save much unhappiness and personal difficulty in later years. 'The very young respond to what they are wearing, and this does not change as they grow older. Many behavior problems stem from dull, drab, unbecoming, or uncomfortable clothing. The selection of fabrics appropriate for children's clothes and colors that are becoming should always be a vital phase of sewing for children. Clothes can be chosen with a view to suiting the child's personality. His physical characteristics of coloring and type must be taken into consideration, as well as his habits of action. The dainty, demure little girl will no doubt look her best in clothing different from that worn by a sturdy, active type.
