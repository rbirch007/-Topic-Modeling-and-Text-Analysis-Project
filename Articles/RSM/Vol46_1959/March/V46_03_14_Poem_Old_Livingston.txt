[Vol46 | March 1959 | Sequence: 14 | Category: Poem | Title: Old | Author: Livingston]
Old Hlome Helen M. Livingston The tree grown tall holds up long arms of shade, Her leafy fingers spread against the sky. The brook runs close around the old white wall, And asters blossom in the field close by. The wide, deep-seated chairs are on the porch; The daisies still are growing in the grass. The evening mists are on the balcony; The dark-gray shadows linger there and pass. And who will sit upon the porch tonight? And who will walk upon the balcony? The moon, slow-rising, dimly lights each pane; A bird's low call pours softly, silvery. Oh, mourning dove, once nestled with your mate, Within the sheltered tree you call—and wait.
