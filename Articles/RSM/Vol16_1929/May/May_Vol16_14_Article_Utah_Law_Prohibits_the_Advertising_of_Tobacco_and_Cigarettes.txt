Utah Law Prohibits the Advertising of Tobacco and Cigarettes

Utah has a law which prohibits the advertising of tobacco and cigarettes. This law was passed by the legislature in 1921, and reads as follows:

"It shall be unlawful for any person, firm or corporation to advertise tobacco or cigarettes by means of billboards, signs, newspapers, magazines, or any other form of advertising within the state of Utah."

The penalty for violation of this law is a fine of not less than $25.00 nor more than $300.00, or imprisonment in the county jail for not more than six months, or both such fine and imprisonment.

This law was sponsored by the Relief Society and other organizations interested in the welfare of the youth of Utah. It has been quite effective in keeping tobacco advertising out of the state, although there have been some attempts to evade it.

The law does not prohibit the sale of tobacco products, but merely their advertising. This is considered a step in the right direction by those who are working for the elimination of the tobacco habit.
