Relief Society Silver Service

COLLEGE PARK WARD, WASHINGTON STAKE

This lovely silver service, which includes two punch bowls and trays of various sizes, was purchased with money earned through the sale of glass pictures (see "Painting With Glass," The Relief Society Magazine, March 1964, page 198, Article by Florian H. Thayne, transparencies by Robert Pease). The interesting and original hobby developed by Mrs. Betty Beesley Huber, ward work meeting leader, and her daughter, Sharon Huber Carter, under the sponsorship and direction of the ward and stake Relief Society, provided the funds for the purchase of the silver service, through the sale of glass pictures. The service is proudly displayed and used for Relief Society functions and for other stake and ward activities in Washington Stake, always reminding the sisters of the rewarding joy of creative activities when their individual talents are shared and become a part of the group accomplishments.

TRANSPARENCY BY ROBERT PEASE
