Baby Bonnet
Made From a Handkerchief

Opal F. Christensen

Use a handkerchief with a wide lace border, about 1½ inches. Take one side and fold up about 2¼ inches. Pin to hold. This is the front of the bonnet. On the opposite side (back) fold the lace edge under and press.

Mark the center back of the bonnet on the lower edge, then fold the bottom two corners over to the center back to make a V. (See figure 1.) Pin together at lace edges in back, then blind stitch the pleat on the reverse side of the bonnet along the two triangles where the material is double. (See figure 2.)

Place 4 ribbon and two lace rosettes which hold bonnet in place, one in back where V comes to point; one in center front; one on each side, with ribbon tie underneath. Sew rosettes through all thicknesses of the handkerchief. (See figure 3.)

To make rosettes: Use a 3-inch length of ¾ inch wide nylon lace. Gather on one edge and draw up, making a tight center. Do the same with a ⅝ inch ribbon, blue or pink, buying enough for ties, also. Sew ribbon rosette in center of lace rosette, and sew on bonnet.
