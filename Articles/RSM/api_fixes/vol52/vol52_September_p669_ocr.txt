Creek  in  Autumn 
Ethel  Jacobson 
Tumbling  from  distant  heights, 
The  creek  careens  and  lurches, 
Roistering  on    its  way 
Between  the  copper  birches. 
Where  burnished  trunks  bend   low, 
White  water  swirls  and  falls, 
And  a  tawny  chipmunk  chatters 
And  a  tanager  calls; 
And  the  spendthrift,   hurrying  stream 
Flings  largesse  left  and  right, 
When  autumn   rains  down  birch   leaves, 
Newpenny  bright.
