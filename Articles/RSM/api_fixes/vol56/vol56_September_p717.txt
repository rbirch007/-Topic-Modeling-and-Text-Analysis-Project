Emily Olsen, South Blackfoot Stake, Idaho, has used her talents to create beauty within her own home. She is an expert at making rugs, quilts, lace, and embroidered bedspreads, doilies, and other decorative items.

Her yard is also very lovely because of the tender care given to the violets and other varieties of flowers. Her vegetable gardens are rarely surpassed.

She has been active in all of the auxiliaries of the Church, having served for nine years as a Relief Society counselor, and more than fifty years as a visiting teacher. She has also rendered faithful service as a teacher and executive in the other organizations.

Sister Olson is the mother of three children and grandmother to three. At eighty-five, she still seldom misses a Relief Society meeting.

Nellie Green Howard, Portneuf Stake, McCammon, Idaho, has made many lovely articles of handwork. Her specialty is quilts, but her crocheted afghans, lace tablecloths, doilies, and bedspreads are very lovely. She started counting the quilts she had made in 1955. Since that time she has quilted 130 full-sized quilts and twenty-one baby quilts.

She has given quilts along with a pair of embroidered pillow cases to each of her five children, twenty-one grandchildren, fifteen step-grandchildren, thirty-five great-grandchildren, and four step-great-grandchildren, and to countless friends and neighbors.

Sister Howard has served as a counselor in Relief Society, and as a teacher and executive officer in the other organizations of the Church. She is eighty-four years old and still attends her meetings, does gardening, and makes quilts.
