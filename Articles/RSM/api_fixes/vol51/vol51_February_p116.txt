Easy
Valentine
Cake

Janet W. Breeze

[ILLUSTRATION: Heart-shaped cake with decorative lacy border]

A mother's baking day is always evidence of love toward her hungry family, but this treat is more than just evidence. It even looks like a token of love. The special equipment needed for this heart-shaped dessert is one eight-inch square cake tin and one eight-inch round cake tin. Use them in which to bake your favorite cake.

When the cake is cool, cut the round layer in half. These halves make the top sides of the heart. The square layer placed at diagonal angle forms the bottom of the heart.

Arrange the cake on a large plate or tray, first placing small paper doilies around the cake to form a lacy background.

Now frost the cake pink and pretty with no-cook valentine icing, topped with sprinkles of coconut, for a special family treat that looks good enough for a party.

No-Cook Valentine Icing

¼ tsp. salt
2 egg whites
¼ c. sugar
⅜ c. corn syrup
1¼ tsp. flavoring
red food flavoring

Add salt to egg whites and beat to soft peaks. Gradually stir in sugar and beat until shiny. Continue beating and add corn syrup gradually. Beat until icing stands in fairly firm peaks. Fold in flavoring and food coloring.
