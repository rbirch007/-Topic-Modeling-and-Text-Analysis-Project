[Summer Is Over by Eva Willes Wangsgaard]

Summer is over. Point southward in winging. The stubbles are under, The orchards announce it The furrows are drying With crimson boughs bending, And gone are the sea gulls With tang of sweet cider With sharp, hungry crying. And pear scent ascending. The red-shafted flicker Has silenced his drumming. Summer is over, Autumn is coming.
