["The Dying Year," by Beatrice K. Ekman,]

Dying Year Beatrice K. Ekman Where storm has laid covers of white down Over roofs and gardens silently, The bright, full moon has spread spun-silver now, And snow-wrapped hills add grave austerity.
Behind a passing cloud the round moon flees, And down the little street a cold wind blows, A sudden tremor shudders through the trees— Another year is drawing to a close. Some, on this last and fleeting old year night, Sit by the hearth in warmth of fireglow And hug their old and happier memories tight, Living again the days they used to know. They, who have grown more patient and more wise, Remembering other hearths and other fires, Look to the New Year with expectant eyes— Forsaking old regrets for new desires.
