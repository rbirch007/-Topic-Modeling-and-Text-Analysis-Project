WORK AND BUSINESS

Topic for November: "The Dignity of Labor."

References: Gospel Doctrine, pages 285-6; Doctrine and Covenants 42:42; 56:17; 68:30-32; 75:3; Discourses of Brigham Young, pages 232-3.

Questions for Discussion:

1. What is the attitude of the Church toward work?

2. What is meant by "the dignity of labor?"

3. What are some of the evils of idleness?

4. How may we make our work a blessing instead of a burden?

5. What is the proper attitude toward those who work with their hands?

6. How may we teach our children to love work?

7. What are some of the occupations that are especially suitable for women?

8. How may a woman make her home work more interesting and efficient?

9. What is the Church attitude toward women working outside the home?

10. How may we help to solve the unemployment problem?

Suggestions to Leaders:

This lesson should emphasize the gospel teaching that work is honorable and necessary. All honest labor is dignified. The Lord has commanded that we work. Idleness leads to temptation and sin. We should teach our children to work and to take pride in doing their work well. Women have many opportunities for useful service both in the home and outside. The home is woman's first responsibility, but circumstances may make it necessary or advisable for her to work outside the home. In such cases she should choose work that is suitable and that will not interfere with her home duties more than necessary. We should all do our part to help solve the unemployment problem by being considerate of those who serve us and by creating work when possible.
