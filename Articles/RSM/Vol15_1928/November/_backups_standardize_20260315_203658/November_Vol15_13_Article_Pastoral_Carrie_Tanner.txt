Pastoral By Carrie Tanner
Ah beautiful! When calm of coming evening spreads o'er summer fields, And cool deep shadows fall on western hills, Enhancing there dear Nature's dress of flowered green, While on and on low sounds the uncomplaining murmur of the rills.
Then clouds like whitest carded wool 'gainst heaven's blue lie soft at rest. Ah beautiful! The tinkling bell, the sleek and shining kine then homeward leads Through gentle waves of damp'ning grasses cool, Of meadows?velvet like?around the spring's deep, quiet pool.
The sheep 'neath shelt'ring shed lie cozy in their fold? A picture of sweet innocence and perfect peace. Ah beautiful!
