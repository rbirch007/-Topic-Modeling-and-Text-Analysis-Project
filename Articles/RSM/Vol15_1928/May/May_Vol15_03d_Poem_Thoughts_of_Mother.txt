Thoughts of Mother By a Missionary, Velma Brinkerhoff Somehow the world's not quite the same With you so far away; T sometimes dream of you at night; I think of you each day. You are the dearest pal I've had— Can I forget your face? Though many friends are round me here, Which one can take your place? When evening comes, when lights are low My memory flies to you, To all your sweet and loving ways— The things you used to do. How oft you smoothed each childish grief And helped me when I fell; And the secrets deep I've told to you— Heart thoughts you'll never tell. When my field work here has ended And from duty's call I'm free, I shall go straight home to greet the pal, Who is all the world to me!
