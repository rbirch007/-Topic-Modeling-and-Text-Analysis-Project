Fidelity

By Edith E. Anderson

Fidelity is faithfulness to duty, to trust, to one's word. It is loyalty to a person, a cause, or an ideal. It is constancy in affection or allegiance.

Fidelity is one of the most beautiful traits of character. It is the foundation of all true friendship, of all lasting love, of all worthy achievement. Without fidelity there can be no real happiness, no genuine success, no permanent satisfaction.

The faithful friend is a treasure beyond price. He is one who stands by us in adversity as well as in prosperity, who shares our sorrows as well as our joys, who believes in us when others doubt, who defends us when we are absent, who forgives our faults and helps us to overcome them.

The faithful wife and mother is the cornerstone of the home. Her love is constant and unchanging. Her devotion never wavers. Her loyalty is absolute. She makes the home a haven of rest and peace, a place of refuge from the storms of life.

The faithful worker is the backbone of every organization. He can be depended upon to do his duty regardless of circumstances. He is prompt, reliable, conscientious. He puts the interests of his work above his own convenience.

The faithful citizen is the strength of the nation. He is loyal to his country's ideals and institutions. He supports good government and opposes corruption. He is willing to sacrifice for the common good.

The faithful member of the Church is a pillar of strength to the organization. His testimony is firm and unwavering. His service is willing and cheerful. His influence is always on the side of righteousness.

Fidelity brings its own reward. The faithful person enjoys the confidence and respect of others. He has the satisfaction of duty well performed. He has the peace that comes from a clear conscience. He has the joy of knowing that he has been true to his highest ideals.

Let us cultivate this beautiful virtue of fidelity. Let us be faithful in all our relationships and responsibilities. Let us be true to our friends, our families, our work, our country, and our God. Thus shall we find happiness and peace, and thus shall we make the world better for our having lived in it.
