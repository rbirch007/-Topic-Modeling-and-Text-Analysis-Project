Mantled Tree

By Vesta Pierce Crawford

I saw a tree mantled in snow,
Its branches bent with winter's load;
Yet through the white I seemed to know
The life that in its heart still glowed.

So may it be with human hearts
When sorrow's mantle wraps them round;
Though winter's chill the joy departs,
Life's warmth within may still be found.

The tree will cast its snowy dress
When spring returns with warming sun;
So hearts that now know loneliness
May find their winter days are done.
