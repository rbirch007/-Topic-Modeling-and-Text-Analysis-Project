AN AUTUMN PICNIC

By Lucy Rose Middleton

We planned a picnic for today
Beneath the autumn trees,
Where golden leaves come drifting down
Upon the vagrant breeze.

We packed our lunch with loving care,
The children danced with glee,
As off we started for the woods
Beside the singing lea.

But when we reached our chosen spot
The sky was overcast,
And chilly winds came sweeping down
With winter's icy blast.

The children's faces fell with grief,
Their joy had turned to tears,
Until dear mother said, "Come, dears,
Let's have our picnic here."

So in the cozy living room
We spread our feast so gay,
And had the jolliest picnic
Of any autumn day.

The rain beat on the window panes,
The wind howled round the eaves,
But we were warm and happy there
Among the autumn leaves.

For mother's love had made a way
To turn our tears to joy,
And given us a memory sweet
That nothing can destroy.
