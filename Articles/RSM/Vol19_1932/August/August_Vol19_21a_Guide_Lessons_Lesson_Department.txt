LESSON 1. SOCIAL SCIENCE

Topic: "The Home and the Community."

References: "Essentials in Conducting a Meeting," by Amy Brown Lyman; "The Message of the Master," by John A. Widtsoe, Lesson 1; Relief Society Magazine, August, 1932.

Objective: To help the sisters realize their responsibility in making the home the foundation of good citizenship and community welfare.

Approach: What is meant by "community"? A community is a group of people living in the same locality and under the same government. The welfare of the community depends upon the character of the individuals who compose it, and the character of the individual is largely determined by the home training and environment.

Development:

1. The Home as the Foundation of Society.

The home is the basic unit of society. Upon its strength and character depends the welfare of the community, the state, and the nation. "The strength of a nation, especially of a republican nation, is in the intelligent and well-ordered homes of the people." (Lydia Sigourney.)

The home should be:
a. A place of love and understanding.
b. A place where children learn obedience, industry, and responsibility.
c. A place where high ideals and standards are maintained.
d. A place where the gospel is lived and taught.

2. The Mother's Responsibility.

The mother is the heart of the home. Upon her largely depends the atmosphere that pervades it. She should:
a. Create an atmosphere of love, peace, and happiness.
b. Teach her children correct principles.
c. Set a good example in all things.
d. Train her children in habits of industry and thrift.
e. Instill in them a love for the beautiful and good.

3. The Home's Influence on the Community.

Good homes make good communities. The influence of the home extends far beyond its own walls. Children trained in good homes become good citizens. They carry into the community the ideals and standards learned at home.

4. The Community's Influence on the Home.

While the home influences the community, the community also influences the home. Therefore, parents should:
a. Take an active interest in community affairs.
b. Support good government and oppose evil influences.
c. Work for better schools, churches, and recreational facilities.
d. Set a good example in community relationships.

5. The Relief Society's Part.

The Relief Society can help by:
a. Teaching mothers their responsibilities.
b. Encouraging high standards in the home.
c. Supporting community welfare projects.
d. Working for the betterment of conditions affecting the home.

Conclusion: The home is the foundation of all social progress. When homes are what they should be, communities will be strong and righteous. Every Relief Society sister has a responsibility to make her home a contributing factor to community welfare.

Questions for Discussion:
1. What are the characteristics of a good home?
2. How does the home influence the community?
3. What can mothers do to improve community conditions?
4. How can the Relief Society help in this work?

LESSON 2. LITERATURE

Topic: "Literature in the Home."

References: "Literature in the Home," by Elsie C. Carroll, Relief Society Magazine, August, 1932; "The Message of the Master," Lesson 2.

Objective: To emphasize the importance of good literature in the home and to suggest ways of developing a love for reading.

Approach: What place does literature occupy in your home? Is there a family library? Do the members of the family read together? Do they discuss what they read?

Development:

1. The Value of Literature.

Good literature:
a. Broadens our knowledge and understanding.
b. Develops our imagination and creative powers.
c. Refines our tastes and elevates our thoughts.
d. Provides wholesome recreation and entertainment.
e. Helps us understand life and human nature.
f. Brings us into contact with the best minds of all ages.

2. Selecting Literature for the Home.

In choosing books for the home library, consider:
a. The age and interests of family members.
b. The moral and spiritual value of the books.
c. The literary merit and style.
d. The practical value for reference and study.

3. Creating a Love for Reading.

Parents can develop a love for reading in their children by:
a. Reading to them from early childhood.
b. Providing good books suited to their age.
c. Setting an example by reading themselves.
d. Discussing books and stories with the children.
e. Making reading a pleasant family activity.

4. The Family Library.

Every home should have:
a. The standard works of the Church.
b. Good reference books.
c. Books of poetry and literature.
d. Biographies of great men and women.
e. Books on practical subjects.
f. Good magazines and periodicals.

5. Reading Together.

Family reading can be made enjoyable by:
a. Selecting books of general interest.
b. Taking turns in reading aloud.
c. Discussing the characters and plot.
d. Relating the story to real life experiences.

Conclusion: Literature has a powerful influence in shaping character and developing the mind. Every home should provide good reading material and encourage the reading habit.

Questions for Discussion:
1. What is the value of literature in the home?
2. How can parents develop a love for reading in their children?
3. What books should every home library contain?
4. How can family reading be made enjoyable?

LESSON 3. THEOLOGY

Topic: "By Grace Ye Are Saved."

References: "By Grace Ye Are Saved," by Nephi Jensen, Relief Society Magazine, August, 1932; "The Message of the Master," Lesson 3; Ephesians 2:8-10; 2 Nephi 25:23.

Objective: To help the sisters understand the true meaning of salvation by grace and its relationship to works.

Approach: What is meant by "grace"? How are we saved by grace? What part do works play in our salvation?

Development:

1. The Meaning of Grace.

Grace means:
a. The unmerited favor and love of God.
b. Divine assistance given to man.
c. The power that enables us to overcome sin.
d. The mercy and forgiveness of God.

2. Salvation by Grace.

We are saved by grace in that:
a. Christ's atonement makes salvation possible.
b. God's love and mercy are freely given.
c. We cannot save ourselves by our own efforts alone.
d. Divine help is necessary for our spiritual progress.

3. The Relationship of Grace and Works.

Grace and works are both necessary:
a. Grace provides the means of salvation.
b. Works demonstrate our faith and obedience.
c. We must accept God's grace and act upon it.
d. Faith without works is dead.

4. How Grace Operates.

God's grace is manifested through:
a. The atonement of Jesus Christ.
b. The gift of the Holy Ghost.
c. The ordinances of the gospel.
d. The scriptures and revelations.
e. The guidance of Church leaders.

5. Our Response to Grace.

We should respond to God's grace by:
a. Having faith in Jesus Christ.
b. Repenting of our sins.
c. Being baptized and confirmed.
d. Keeping the commandments.
e. Serving others.
f. Continuing in righteousness.

Conclusion: Salvation comes through the grace of God, but we must do our part by exercising faith and performing good works. Grace and works go hand in hand in the plan of salvation.

Questions for Discussion:
1. What is the meaning of grace?
2. How are we saved by grace?
3. What is the relationship between grace and works?
4. How should we respond to God's grace?

LESSON 4. WORK MEETING

Topic: "Practical Homemaking."

Objective: To provide instruction in practical skills that will help sisters in their homemaking duties.

Suggestions for Work Meeting:

1. Canning and Preserving.
a. Demonstrate proper methods of canning fruits and vegetables.
b. Show how to make jams and jellies.
c. Discuss storage methods.

2. Sewing and Mending.
a. Teach simple mending techniques.
b. Show how to make useful household articles.
c. Demonstrate clothing alterations.

3. Home Decoration.
a. Suggest inexpensive ways to beautify the home.
b. Show how to arrange flowers.
c. Demonstrate simple handicrafts.

4. Cooking and Nutrition.
a. Share economical recipes.
b. Discuss proper nutrition for the family.
c. Demonstrate food preparation techniques.

5. Home Management.
a. Discuss efficient housekeeping methods.
b. Show how to organize household tasks.
c. Suggest ways to save time and energy.

Note: Choose one or two of these topics for each work meeting, depending on the needs and interests of the sisters.
