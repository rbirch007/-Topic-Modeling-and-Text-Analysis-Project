Her Calling By Edith E. Anderson "I am only a visiting teacher," she said, As I asked of the calling she had: "Only a teacher," I answered and thought What cause she had to be glad.

Only a teacher, through sunshine and storm, Making her rounds of cheer, To the sick and the lonely, the burdened and sad, Bringing the Comforter near.

The true and the faithful, encouraging on; Allaying fair childhood's alarms, The weak and the wayward, the careless and proud, Enfolding in charity's arms.

What calling more potent, could anyone have?

The Master has said in His call, That those in His kingdom who would be great, Must freely give service to all.
