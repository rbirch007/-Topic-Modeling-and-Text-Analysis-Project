SENTIMENT

Sentiment is a tender thing—
A fragile, lovely flower
That blooms within the human heart
In some unguarded hour.

It may be just a faded rose
Pressed in a book of years,
A letter tied with ribbon blue
And blurred with happy tears.

A little shoe, a baby dress,
A curl of golden hair,
A picture in a silver frame—
How precious and how fair!

These simple things that others pass
Without a second thought
Are treasured in the heart of love
Beyond all gold that's bought.

For sentiment's the gentle touch
That makes life sweet and dear,
The bridge that spans the gulf of time
And brings the past years near.

—Elsie Chamberlain Carroll
