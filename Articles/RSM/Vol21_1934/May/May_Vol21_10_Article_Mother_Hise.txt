Anne went to the porch to meet Marian Welling. (To be continued ) Mother By Roxana Farnsworth Hase Your hair has turned to silver, But your heart is more like gold, Not the type that mankind barters But that from a finer mold. The gold that is burnished and mellowed With the service of many years, oe Tempered, refined, made purer, By your triumphs and your tears. Age rests on your brow like a halo Serene — for he knows your real worth, And your face grows more sweet, and more noble Every day that you live on this earth.
