Personnel. For Spring Maryhale Woolsey Spring is March—a tomboy tot, Racing through her days Recklessly, and loud and wild In a thousand ways. Spring is April, venturing Shyly up a hill— Veiled with rain, and carrying Violets to spill. Spring is May—a dancing-girl Of bright and nimble grace; Where her rhythmic slippers touch, Blossoms rise in place. Spring is eager, gay, and young— A woman-child who grows Through storms and sun to learn the charm Which lovely summer knows. BOOKS for the L. D. S.
