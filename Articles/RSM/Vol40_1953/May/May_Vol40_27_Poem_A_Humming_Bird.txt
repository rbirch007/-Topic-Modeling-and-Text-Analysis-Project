[Vol40 | May 1953 | Sequence: 27 | Category: Article | Title: A Humming Bird]

(To be concluded) > She Humming Bird Evelyn Fjeldsted From heights of a leaf in the wind, A humming bird swings through the air And, sighting a twig once again, A little bronze statue is there. Speeding with summer, he brings A kiss for a flower at dawn; Then, with a swift whirring curve, Like a bobbin with wings, he is gone.
