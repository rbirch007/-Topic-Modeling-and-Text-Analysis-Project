Ivy Houtz Woolley She stood up there; a wisp of hair Hung wet against her face, Warm drops of sweat were standing yet, Where smiles could find no place. Her wrinkled hands, like open fans Were pressed upon her hips, The tub of clothes where bubbles rose Brought murmurs to her lips. What need was there to even care If things were never clean, For Jan had gone, before the dawn, His words were harsh and mean. She would leave the tub, and never rub Another sock or shirt, Then where she stood, some other would Wash out his grime and dirt. How drab her fate! She heard the gate Drag on the graveled walk— Her Jan stood there, with smiles to share Loaves, too, and celery stock. He touched her dress, a mute caress, Her heart sang all athirst; Above the clothes, air castles rose— She prayed they would not burst.
