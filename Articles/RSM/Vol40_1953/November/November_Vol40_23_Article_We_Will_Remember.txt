[Vol40 | November 1953 | Sequence: 23 | Category: Article | Title: We Will Remember]

Will We Remember? Agnes Just Reid When wintry winds begin to shout And blow the dead leaves all about, I wonder if we'll still remember The golden sunshine of September? When snows of winter come in March And no leaf shows on birch or larch, Will we recall the calm September? Deep in our hearts will we remember? When June frosts come to blight and kill Before we're robbed of every thrill, We might just struggle to remember The cloudless skies of last September.
