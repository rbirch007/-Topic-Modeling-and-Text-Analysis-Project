It  seemed  only  minutes  after  he 
had  left  that  the  children  came  run- 
ning to  babble  about  a  car  and  a 
pretty  lady.  Steve  watched  the 
nurse  getting  out  of  the  cab,  com- 
ing up  the  walk.  She  was  in  uni- 
form, a  navy  blue  cape  over  her 
shoulders,  her  white  cap  in  one 
hand  and  a  black  case  in  the  other. 
She  walked  with  a  swift  grace  and 
proud  bearing.  Steve  looked  at  her 
with  alarm  and  dismay.  Such  a  crea- 
ture as  this  would  never  stay  in  his 
miserable  domain,  perhaps  wouldn't 
know  the  first  thing  about  taking 
care  of  a  sick  child.  Somehow  he 
had  expected— and  wanted— a  mid- 
dle-aged, motherly  woman,  perhaps 
plump,  but  certainly  comfortable 
and  capable.
