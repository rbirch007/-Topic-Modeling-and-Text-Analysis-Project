There  was  no  sign. 
No  bitter  wind,  with  sharpening  breath 
Had  scattered  scarlet  leaves  of  trees; 
Untouched,  late  flowers  glowed  beneath  the  sun, 
For  now,  enchanted  autumn  held  the  land. 
Only  the  blue-white  haze  upon  the  far  horizon 
Told  of  change; 
Only  a  wedge  of  wild  geese,  winging  south. 
Gave  warning 
That  winter's  cold,  fierce  legions  marched 
To  conquest, 
Down  from  the  northland.
