Relief Society School of Obstetrics and Nursing, 1919-1920
======================================================================

RELIEF SOCIETY SCHOOL OF OBSTETRICS AND NURSING, 1919-1920. The Relief Society School of Obstetrics and Nursing will begin its fifteenth year Monday, October 6, 1919, fourth floor. Bishop's Building. Term, eight months. This course consists of studies in anatomy and general instruction innursing, emphasizing Home Care of the Sick, with practical demonstrations in Invalid Cooking. First Aid Work, Hydrotherapy, Sterilization, and lectures on Elementary Hygiene, Public Health, Contagious Diseases, Drugs and Solutions, Prevention and Treatment of Diseases. These lecture courses are given periodically and will be given by prominent physicians, surgeons and other specialists. Each student of this course will be required to do at least fifty hours of practical field work during the term, under the direction of the school. Tuition, $25.00 cash, thirty days service charity nursing. Tuition to be paid and contracts to be signed upon registration. For further information, address Mrs. Amy Brown Lyman, General Secretary, 28 Bishop's Building, Salt Lake City, Utah.
