MEADOW FLOWERS Norma A. Wrathall Now it's May, my truant thoughts are wandering, And we have climbed a tree-lined fence, our quest Star flowers hiding ankle-deep in marsh grass Where meadowlarks call joy from nest to nest. Soft clumps of pull aparts are pungent islands For nimble feet where quiet waters seep, While sun and white-puff clouds trail endless patterns Along our pink starred paths till shadows creep. The dreaming sky, the scents of earth and flo Rise from my heart, but wander where I! Will, I searc in vain. For rosy fields, and listen Across. 'the silence for the lark's sweet trill.
