She is an expert with the crochet hook, and makes many of her own patterns. She is currently busy making exquisite edgings on ~illowslips for her ward bazaar. She has made hundreds of quilts, and the homes of her six children, her grandchildren, and most of her great-grandchildren are adorned with beautifully designed and carefully stitched quilts. Ismilda Thueson Dorchues was married to William England in 1891, and they are said to be one of the longest married couples in the United States. Sister England has been a Relief Society class leader, a visiting teacher for more than fifty years, and she served as Relief Society president of Plain City (Utah) Ward for ten years. She is skilled in all household arts, and, in addition to her handicraft skills, she is noted for her cooking and baking.
