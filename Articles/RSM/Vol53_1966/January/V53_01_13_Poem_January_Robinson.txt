[Vol53 | January 1966 | Page 29 | Poem | January Linnie | Fisher Robinson]
JANUARY Linnie F. Robinson January comes knocking at my door Where scattered fragments of the year remain, In tasks attempted or left undone. And where discouragement had grown to pain. But January pushes wide the door— A calendar of days is in his hand; My troubles fall like paper to the floor— New time is mine—the glass is full of sand. And so I take the legacy of hours And treadle at the waiting loom of day; How blithely did I use the springtime up, How prodigal of every summer way. But now these hours in new dimensions shine For I have learned to husband and to ply; The world has in it so much to learn But best of all is time again to try.
