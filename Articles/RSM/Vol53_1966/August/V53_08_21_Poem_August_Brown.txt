AUGUST
Alda L. Brown

. . . . And the days sear and scar and burn
And hang like pendants on a ripened vine:
Bright yellow orbs . . . . succulent and bittersweet. . . .
Waiting to rebuke the gatherer's hand—
To utter the futile pleading,
"Hold back the summer!"
Swift is the lash of cold scythe on proud grain,
That severs and hurts the brief hours. . . .
For I would have the skies go on
And sear and scar and burn
Forever
Rather than starve
My summer-hungry heart. . . .
