[Vol53 | March 1966 | Page 235 | Poem | My Mother's Country | Christie Lund Coles]
MY MOTHER'S COUNTRY Christie Lund Coles. I recall her speaking of Tivoli, And the cobblestone streets where her youth ran; The forests with their spires and ferny leaves; The ocean's roaring and its foam-white span; The low, thatched roofs on cottages; Bakeries with their warm and spicy scent.
