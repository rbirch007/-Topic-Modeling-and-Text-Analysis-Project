MY YELLOW BIRD Alda L. Brown Blithe canary! My yellow bird of spring! Golden is the merry note From lilted throat you sing! We share a rendezvous with May— One trysted hour with dawn. From lilac-scented bough you flay A roundelay of song! I will miss you, yellow bird, When gilded petals close. No longer will you sip From dew-filled cup. Unheard Will be your rush of wing. So silent will my morning sing When summer goes..
