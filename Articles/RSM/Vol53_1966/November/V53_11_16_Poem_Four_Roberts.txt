[Vol53 | November 1966 | Page 831 | Poem | Four A.M. Siren | Dorothy J. Roberts]
SIREN Dorothy J. Roberts A four a. M. Siren slices the pre-dawn silence With the scream of — death or birth. It is birth I prefer to dream of — Prone and peaceful, drifting back into the calm. Birth and the velvet fingers, curling, And the daisy-chain of childhood in the palm.
