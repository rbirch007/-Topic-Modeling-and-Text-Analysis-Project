GOING SOMEWHERE Linda L. Clarke Where are you going my little lass With your brown and windblown hair? Are you walking alone or with the dove? Do you know you are going somewhere? The search is beginning, it's there in your eyes. The journey is long—this I know— But follow the song of the white dove, She flies to where all rivers flow. I long to walk here beside you, Your shield from all thistles and thorns, But the call, the song of the white dove, Bids you leave on your journey alone. Sing loud, sing clear, oh, white dove, For my lass with the hair of brown. Sing loud, sing clear and tead her To the place where all waters are bound.
