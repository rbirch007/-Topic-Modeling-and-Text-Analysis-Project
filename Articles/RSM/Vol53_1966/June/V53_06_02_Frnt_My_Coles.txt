[My Town Christie Lund Coles]

The mountains form a necklace for my town, Green as amethyst or the rarest jade; Sunlight falls like gold-dust from a crown; Clouds embroider the sky of delphinium shade. Each eye is entranced when looking on this scene, Where maples border walks; where water moves Silverly humming tunes; reflecting green Of dainty willows which the memory loves. Here, sometimes, just at dusk an organ plays, Or carillon chimes peel out from some far tower; This is a moment when the spirit prays, Knowing a sacred, sanctifying hour. I thank God for this moment and this spot, So richly given and so dearly bought.
