[Vol53 | July 1966 | Page 514 | Poem | Reverie in Meadow Grass | Peggy Tangren]
REVERIE IN MEADOW GRASS Peggy Tangren This meadow is a world within a world Of little things that crawl and creep in shade Of leaf and stone. Of wings not yet unfurled Laid camouflaged against the tall green blade. The draglines of the ant have mapped their land For keeping life, or bringing back the dead Around a lake the size of my cupped hand, And under girders strung of spider thread. Deep in this meadow grass are town and shire Built to the level of an insect's eye. Hunger is here, frustration, and desire; And over them the stems rise strong and high, Their forest, as my forest is to me, Dwarfing them as redwoods dwarf us all. What fearsome moving monster must I be— I, who am a hundred grass blades tall. Will go softly through the meadow grass Breathing a benediction as I pass.
