In halls where great and wise folk meet, —But I can place a chair just right, With hassock for one's tired feet. A house palatial, servant-filled, I might not competently rule; —But I can serve a tempting meal, And make a bed smooth, clean, and cool... Lure of Something Green 
By Mabel S. Harmer OR years I have tried to alibi myself out of keeping house plants. My south windows are inadequate to care for many plants. And I always have a guilty conscience when I deprive them of sufficient sunshine by putting them anywhere else. If I go away for a vacation they are harder to dispose of than either the canary or the children and I usually end up by bribing some kind neighbor to come in and give them an occasional drink and then I worry all the time as to whether she has remembered to lock the back door after her or if I shall come home and find all the silverware missing.
