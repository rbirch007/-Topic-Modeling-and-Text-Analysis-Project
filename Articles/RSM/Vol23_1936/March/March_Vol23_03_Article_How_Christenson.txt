HOW BIG?
By Alberta H. Christensen
I cannot
tell how big
I am
'Cause things have sort-of mixed me up.
Suppose
I
take some
doughnuts,
Just
three
or four or
so
My mother says, "Enough, my son,
You're
pretty
small, you know."
But
if
I scratch my elbow
Upon the garbage
can,
Why then she says, "There, there, don't cry.
You're mother's great big man."
I cannot tell how big I am,
'Cause things have sort-of mixed me up
!
