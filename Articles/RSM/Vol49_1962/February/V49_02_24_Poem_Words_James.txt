(To be continued ) <-> Words After Snow Ida Elaine James This season of white fortitude shall melt At last into a tender time of bloom, An hour less visioned than obscurely felt, After long months of chaste and frozen gloom. Insistently the heart, cloaked in despair, Scents fragrance subtle as the breath of hope, Moving through branches, pulseless yet, and bare, Over the winter's chill and empty slope. There will come beauty from this barren hour, A peril-sweet interval when children sing Beneath the dogwood's luminous ivory flower; When faith shall come to warm awakening, To roll the stone of winter's blight and doom Away at last from spring's beleaguered tomb.
