She Spirit s Stillness Caroline Eyring Miner God's masterpiece, Iguassu Falls, demands The spirit's stillness, savoring silence, too. The mighty water thunders and commands And I stand mute. Eternity shines through, As emerald-amber streaked, the living stream Plunges in shattered glass and silver spray, Rising in mist like bride's illusive dream. Glass beads spill on the mossy footstool where Embroidered eyelet flounces endless weave Hypnotic trances of this sunlit hair So silvery bright that falling, falling leaves This memory in tropic jungle green, Where the majesty and power of God are seen.
