[Editorial: "The Multitude of the Promises" Vesta P. Crawford]

EDITORIAL Relief Society women find joy and fulfillment in strengthening themselves and yielding strength to others. At one time a woman may be a listener in the class; at another time she may be seated with her companions and deftly work her needle through the satin of a quilt; she may be at evening time in the quiet of her home studying the longlasting words of a poet whose work will be the subject of tomorrow's lesson. A Relief Society woman may go forth with her companion on errands of love and solicitude as a visiting teacher; later, the visiting teacher may become a president or a counselor in Relief Society, and 27 will possess the wisdom and devotion which her other callings have developed within her. In each and every calling, she will know that giving and receiving are but different facets of a treasure passed from hand to hand. She will know that spiritual gifts are not for one alone, but are most radiant and glorious when they are shared in the worldwide sisterhood. For us and all the world, the year is new. The commandments and the gospel, the promises, are old and everlasting. 'They are ours to receive and share as "heirs to the kingdom of God." —V. P. C. Zine. Beginning in February.
