In Summer's Wake Eva Willes Wangsgaard I stand above exotic tropic bloom Begonias yield within their sun-flecked shade, The hose relinquishing a silver plume Of liquid life to burnish leafy jade. While all about petunias climb and spread High up the fence and over moistened roots. Bright garlanded in apples overhead, The Hopa crabtree molds its crimson fruit. Yet something indefinable repeats A thin familiar warning "'Autumn's near." And deep within me smoldering protests beat A sharp denial to the waning year. Each mellow moment caught in summer's wake Tempers my joy with its peculiar ache.
