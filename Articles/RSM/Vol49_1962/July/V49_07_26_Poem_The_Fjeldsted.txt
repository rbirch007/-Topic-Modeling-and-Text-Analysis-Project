The Apple Tree Evelyn Fjeldsted Our apple tree is gone. Chalice blossoms pink and white, Will never open wide again, To hold the rains and warm sunlight. Knarled old branches shielding homing birds Brought spring perfume, inviting shade, Gifts of apples brushed with red, And autumn leaves — a gold cascade. How many trees have come and gone Since this our land began to grow — Gone with this our tree — our pioneer? Only passing winds could know. Our family tree is gone — There is no singing now at dawn.
