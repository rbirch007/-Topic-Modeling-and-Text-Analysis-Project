"You know, it's (To be continued ) > My Legacy Maude Rubin She left this needle-case, these bits and pieces Of silks and colored woolens; half a quilt. She'd made one for the others, all the nieces And granddaughters but me... Yet how the lilt Of her creaking rocking chair sings in my ears! I watched her sew through summer's amber hours, Through winter's long still evenings; no swift fears Hinted that she might leave me. Now the flowers In her window pot still bloom, their fragrance mine; Though this quilt is still undone, my legacy Is a memory of color, bright design, And this open Book she used to read to me. It was so I learned of love, of peace, of duty Which covered her years with their quilt of shining beauty.
