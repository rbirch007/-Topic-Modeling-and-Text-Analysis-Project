[June and the Rose Dorothy J. Roberts]

I want to use our tithing money this month for his present. 'Then I'll make it up in the next few months, and pay double then." "Joyce, you wouldn't do that." Melba couldn't hide her disapproval. "Don't. It is so difficult to get back on a regular schedule, after once postponing a sacred obligation." "Why, Melba, you remember telling me you did the same thing once," Joyce reminded Melba. "Yes I did, and I've regretted that postponement ever since. When we paid our tithing regularly, everything went so smoothly it was unbelievable. 'Then I got the idea that I just had to have some new curtains. Cliff didn't know about it, either, and believe me, when he found out, I know that he lost some RELIEF SOCIETY MAGAZINE—JUNE 1962 of his respect for me. After that we had one thing after another come up. First I broke my glasses, then the baby got sick, and we just kept getting further behind. I ended up regretting my decision, and I have been very prompt ever since." Melba touched Joyce's arm. "You have a couple of days before Roger's birthday. 'Think about it for awhile. Then, if you still feel that same way, Pll go down with you to buy the present, but please think about it some more. You wouldn't want your blessings to be postponed." "Well, I guess that I can wait a couple of days, but I do plan to make up the tithing," Joyce added. "Joyce, Roger gave that money to you to pay the Lord. He trusted you with it for that. If you let him down, you let yourself down, too." Melba paused, thinking that she had been too outspoken.
