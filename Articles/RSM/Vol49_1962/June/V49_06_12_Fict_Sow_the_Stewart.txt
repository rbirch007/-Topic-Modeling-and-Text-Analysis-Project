Joyce Vance moved slowly along the streets of the small college town. The campus was one of the loveliest in the entire State, and she couldn't help feeling slightly sentimental as she strolled by the buildings. "There have been many good memories here," Joyce sighed as she stopped for a moment to adjust Bobbie's cap. "Here now, dear, it isn't quite warm enough to take your cap off yet." Joyce was approaching the university apartments where she and Roger had lived for the past five years. It was home now, and they accepted it. 'They had done their best to fix up the apartment, and even though it was small, they had managed. Just the will to manage, Joyce thought as she remembered all her dreams of the house that they would live in when she married. Joyce had waited for Roger while he was on his mission. She hadn't minded really. 'The time had gone by swiftly and soon Roger was home. She hadn't objected, either, when Roger expressed his desire to go on and finish college. However, she Page 420 had wished inwardly that he would settle down to a job. She wanted a home of her own. All her life she and her mother had been moved from one city to another by her father. It wasn't her father's fault that his job had transferred him all over the state. "My goodness, Bobbie, it is almost five o'clock." Joyce quickened her pace. "We had _ better hurry home and get your father some dinner. He has to go to work tonight." Once inside the apartment, Joyce made the baby comfortable and then began preparing dinner. I wonder what it will be like not to have to economize at every turn. After five years of it, I doubt very much that I ' will ever be able to get out of the habit, Joyce thought. "Dinner ready, Joyce?" Roger called cheerfully as he picked up Bobbie and walked into the kitchen that was really only a partition from the living room. "Something smells good." Roger was tall, with dark hair and blue eyes. Joyce thought he looked tired and that the strain of school and work was taking its toll. "You say that every night, my dear husband, even though we have about the same thing," Joyce explained patiently. "It won't always be this way." Roger tried to console Joyce. FTER Roger had left for work and Joyce was finishing up the dishes, she couldn't help feeling guilty as she thought back over the way she had spoken to Roger. I shouldn't have talked to him that way. After all, it is just as difficult for him as it is for me. He is the one who has to go to school, study, and keep up a part-time job. Joyce glanced up at the calendar. Oh, well, if things worked out, Roger should have only six more months of school left. Suddenly Joyce stared at the dates on the calendar. Why it is Roger's birthday next week. I almost forgot. What will I ever do for money to buy him a birthday present? The next few days passed by hurniedly, and still Joyce had not come to a solution as to how she would be able to afford a present for Roger. Just this once she wanted to give him something really nice, something that would help him over these last few months of school. It had been such a difficult struggle, and this last winter had seemed to be worse than the others. Now their rent was due, and they had to have some repairs made on the car. There just wouldn't be any money left over at all. One evening Roger and Joyce were down town buying groceries when Roger stopped by a sporting goods shop and admired the reel and rod fishing combination displayed in the window. "Look, honey," he called to Joyce. "When I get out of school and we 421 get enough money ahead, this is what I am going to have." Roger's eyes fairly twinkled with admiration. "Isn't that a beauty?" "Too bad you can't have it for this summer, dear," Joyce remarked. "You could at least have a little enjoyment out of it now." "Oh, I can wait. I can still use father's fishing equipment. We just have to wait for these things." Joyce frowned. "I just wish that I had your patience." she strained to see the price tag on the fishing equipment. It was $19.95. Later, on their way home, Roger said, "That reminds me. I almost forgot to give you our tithing money. We will have to pay it this next Sunday." Roger opened his wallet and took out some bills. "That's right, it is fast day this Sunday." Joyce reached to take the money from Roger's hand. Then suddenly the thought struck her. Just this once it will be all night. We have always paid our tithing promptly before. Roger wouldn't agree with me for postponing it, but he doesn't have to know. I'll be able to make up our tithing in the next few months. Joyce seemed to relax now. I've found a way, she thought. Roger will have that new fishing equipment for his birthday.
