ITlintatare Plants Elizabeth Williamson INIATURE plants are not a new idea. The Japanese and the Chinese thought of _them centuries ago. However, the charm of these tiny plants and containers continues to please us, and their uses are many. 'They always delight anyone who creates them or anyone who receives them as a gift. Keep on hand very small containers and the smallest of flower pots, and when you are transplanting or making slips, put the smallest ones in the containers. Rock plants and succulents seem to adapt themselves best to miniature planting. Enliven the pots with tiny colorful figurines which you may pick up from time to time. 'These miniature plants make charming gifts and will be joyfully received. From The Field Note® Margaret C.
