[Vol43 | September 1956 | Sequence: 27 | Category: Poem | Title: Theirs to Hold | Author: Alice Morrey Bailey]
They gather summer out across the fields
When autumn purples and the year is late.

Where plowshares slip beneath the loam, earth yields
Her goodness to the harvesters. 'The weight
Of orchard boughs is loosed. The April dawn,
The pollened flowers of May are theirs to hold.
The dews of June cannot be truly gone
Where treasure lies in busheled red and gold.
And they will never let the summer go
Who capture it in bottles, row on row,
Fruit and tuber caught in shining tin,
And ripened stores in crock and pit and bin.

When coming winter blasts the land with frost
The season's root and leaf shall not be lost.
