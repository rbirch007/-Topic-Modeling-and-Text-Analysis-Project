[Vol43 | September 1956 | Sequence: 30 | Category: Poem | Title: Autumn Tidings | Author: Elsie McKinnon Strachan]
Now migrant, feathered folk make noisy talk;
Beneath my sill, their chatter
Punctuating dawn; till, flight plans made,
With a whir, they scatter.
Now crispness marks the early morning air,
And flowers fold their glory;
While shadows lengthen toward that final page
Of summer's story.
The summer sun still reigns, round-faced and high;
The crow maintains his chidings;
But down these green-aisled hours, September brings
Autumn tidings.
