[Vol43 | September 1956 | Sequence: 15 | Category: Article | Title: Little | Author: Dorio]
Notice, next, the reading position a little boy assumes while he's reading a book to himself! It's a jackknifed and two-story-sprawl position! First the book is over his head, then his head is over the book. Or the book's forward, no, it's backward, no, it's two-way animation! Now the book's an incompleted forward pass over his ears, and then it's a straight pitch, unless it could be behind the catcher! Now he slaps down the book or maybe slaps his own tummy in the tumult! So that when he groans "Oh, chohoh!" you can't tell whether it's for his tummy or for his book! 'Then he'll surprise you by settling down to the forward look: elbows anchored, book anchored, mind anchored. But don't miss that special star-spangled face a little boy wears while he's reading a book! You can observe this best, if you speak to him. "It's bedtime, Son!" you say. But he doesn't really hear you. He may look at you, all right, but through a pair of clear and sparkling marbles, and he doesn't see you at all, even in the electric light. You feel as though you're talking to a star; as though you're disturbing a new Star. There isn't a more wonderful sight anywhere than a little boy reading a book to himself!
