\  woman  of  many  artistic  gifts,  Sarah  M.  (Knowles)  Brown,  Nephi,  Utah,  still  en- 
*"*  joys  many  of  her  hobbies  although  she  was  ninety-seven  years  old  in  July  1956. 
She  has  made  many  beautiful  braided  rugs  in  unusual  designs  and  in  many  lovely  color 
patterns.  The  homes  of  her  relatives  and  friends  have  been  made  "homey"  and  attrac- 
tive by  gifts  of  her  braided  rugs,  and  Mrs.  Brown  often  says,  "You  always  braid  much 
more  than  cloth  into  a  rug,  and  you  put  more  than  thread  into  the  stitches  —  you  make 
a  pattern  of  memories  and  dreams  and  ideals  —  and  always  you  think  of  home." 
Sister  Brown,  who  remembers  well  the  pioneer  days  of  Juab  County,  has  been  a 
widow  for  more  than  fifty  years.  During  the  busy  times  of  rearing  her  seven  children, 
she  served  devotedly  in  Relief  Society  and  acted  as  counselor  to  three  presidents.  She 
also  served  as  Relief  Society  secretary  for  many  years. 
All  her  life  she  has  loved  poetry  and  has  entertained  public  gatherings  by  "reciting" 
and  giving  talks  on  poetry.  Even  now,  in  her  advanced  years,  she  is  able  to  recall  many 
poems  and  she  can  recite  them  verbatim.  To  her,  poetry  is  a  mirror  of  life,  enriched 
with  the  lasting  beauty  of  words.
