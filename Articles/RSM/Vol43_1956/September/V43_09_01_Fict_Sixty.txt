Provide  a  moist,  cold,  storage 
place  for  tulips,  daffodils,  and  hya- 
cinth bulbs  while  they  are  filling 
the  pots  with  roots.  Place  the  pots 
in  either  a  well-drained  cold  frame, 
or  in  a  shallow  trench.  Dig  the 
trench  twelve  inches  deep  and  place 
a  four-inch  layer  of  cinders  in  the 
bottom  for  drainage.  Place  the 
containers  in  and  fill  in  around  the 
pots  with  peat  moss  or  sharp  sand. 
Add  a  covering  of  sharp  sand,  and 
cover  with  leaves  or  straw.  This 
mulch  makes  it  easier  to   get  the 
bulbs  out  during  the  winter.  Twelve 
weeks  is  a  safe  period  to  allow  for 
the  root-making  processes.  Then 
bring  the  pots  into  the  house  and 
place  in  a  temperature  of  about 
fifty  degrees.  Remove  any  soil 
which  may  be  clinging  to  the  pale 
new  shoots.  Water  plants  enough 
to  keep  the  soil  moist,  and,  as  the 
growth  increases,  more  water  will 
be  needed.  Water  should  be  at 
room  temperature.  As  soon  as  the 
foliage  has  started  to  spread,  and 
the  stems  are  well  started,  move  the 
plants  into  bright  sunlight.  If  you 
can  keep  the  room  temperature  at 
sixty  degrees,  you  will  have  much 
better  results  with  the  bulbs  you 
are  forcing.  Always  turn  the  plants
