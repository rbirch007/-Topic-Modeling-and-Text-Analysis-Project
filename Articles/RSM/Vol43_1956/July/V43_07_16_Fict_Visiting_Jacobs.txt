T  has  been  said  that  frontiers  are 
now  but  half-forgotten  realms  in 
the  world  of  history,  that  their 
eager,  spiritual  beckoning,  their  call 
to  adventure  and  far  places,  their 
promise  and  their  fulfillment  will 
never  be  known  to  another  genera- 
tion. It  is  true  that  the  geographical 
aspects  of  the  world  are  fairly  well 
known,  having  been  viewed  from 
the  skies,  and  having  been  explored 
even  to  the  lonely  outposts  by  the 
brave  and  the  venturesome.  Nearly 
all  the  habitable  regions  are  occu- 
pied, and,  although  some  areas  are 
still  in  various  phases  of  the  pio- 
neering stage,  still  the  noontide  of 
seeking  the  unknown  frontier  is 
past. 
Today  we  are  not  privileged  to 
experience  the  sacrifice,  the  wonder, 
and  the  rejoicing  of  the  quest  of  a 
far  frontier.  The  wagons  are  gone, 
the  trails  but  faintly  marked  upon 
the  prairie;  the  mountain  peaks  are 
named  and  mapped,  the  course  of 
the  rivers  long  discovered.  We  may 
feel  that  in  our  generation  we  have 
lost  something  of  that  fearless  fac- 
ing of  the  future,  that  undeviating 
purpose,  that  great  fulfillment  that 
led  the  vanguard  westward  and 
turned  the  desert  into  gardens. 
Still  we  need  never  be  without 
the  heritage  of  the  frontier.  It  is 
our  great  legacy  from  the  past  to  be 
for  us  a  source  of  courage  and  buoy- 
ant inspiration,  for,  though  the 
geographical  frontiers  have  vanished,

Along  these  pathways,  there  are  still

Much  of  life  is  like  an  ever-beck- 
oning frontier.  Take  the  morning, 
for  instance  —  dawn  is  forever  a 
frontier,  the  light  of  day  on  a  far 
peak,  the  joy  of  waking  with  the 
golden  hours  unfolding.  No  one 
knows  in  the  morning  what  treas- 
ures of  enlightenment  and  under- 
standing a  day  may  bring.  Its  hours 
are  all  to  be  discovered  —  "if  I  take 
the  wings  of  the  morning  .  .  .  ."
