Sunbonnet Manijane Morris [I the women of America were ever to need a common symbol — it could be the sunbonnet. The broad-brimmed bonnets, in some form or another, were always present whenever something really important happened to America. Curiously, however, little information is available on them as a type of headdress. And yet, they have endured through the years and are still a favorite of many women. Those who make and wear sunbonnets today may not do as grandmother did, match them to their dresses, but you can be sure they know their value in warding off the sun's rays and frustrating the insects. Surely, the sunbonnet has earned more than mention in a dictionary. The pattern here is a basic one consisting of five pieces: the brim, the back, the three-fourths inch deep strip sewed to the underside back to be used to gather up the cape, and the two ties. It requires one-half yard of any kind of light-weight material, print, organdy, cretonne, etc. The brim is made double. The back is gathered to the underside of. The brim, and the right side of the brim stitched down on top, as you would attach a collar.
