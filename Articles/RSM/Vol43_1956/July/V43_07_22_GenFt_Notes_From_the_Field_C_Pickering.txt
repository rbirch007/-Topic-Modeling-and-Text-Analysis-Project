'The tables were decorated in the Relief Society colors, blue and gold. 'The centerpiece depicted the various departments of Relief Society by means of dolls carrying articles representative of the different activities. Each separate doll was attached to a center doll by white nbbons. 'The center doll represented Relief Society, with the motto: 'Charity Never Faileth.' Daffodils in blue vases were also used. Special guests were the Gooding Stake presidency and their wives and the husbands of the stake Relief Society presidency. 'These guests are seated at the head table. The party created a good feeling among the ward members and the sisters felt that their time was well spent in making preparations for the occasion. Officers of the Jerome Second Ward Relief Society are: Idella Walker, President; Virgie Packer, First Counselor; LaVerl Johnson, Second Counselor; Eva Johnson, Secretary." Clawson reports: "The Seattle Stake Relief Society Board gave a birthday and reunion luncheon March 12, 1956, honoring Jennie Kearns, Maurine Linde, and Clarine Walpole, who were resigning from the board. Special guests were Vera Leishman and Birdie Bean, former presidents of Seattle Stake Relief Society. All those who had served as stake board members since the stake was organized in 1938 were invited to attend. 'Table decorations were carried out in Relief Society colors, with dolls dressed in costumes of 1842 carrying a copy of The Times and Seasons, and dolls in modern dress carrying a copy of The Relief Society Magazine dated March 1956."
