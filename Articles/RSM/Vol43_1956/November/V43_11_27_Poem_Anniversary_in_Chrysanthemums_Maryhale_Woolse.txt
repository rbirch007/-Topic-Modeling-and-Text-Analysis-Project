Anniversary in Chrysanthemums
Maryhale Woolsey

Bright in the rain of this November day
They stand, their gold and bronze and ruby-red
A brilliant canopy above the dead
And sodden corse of summer's lush bouquet.
They glow like gems among the leaden-gray
Enshrouding mists of sunless afternoon;
The rhythm of the raindrops' tuneless tune
Is measured in their small, reluctant sway.
I watch through tears—remembering how last
You walked the paths chrysanthemums made gay:
Caressing every bloom along your way,
Smoothing its fringe of petals as you passed.
"Childish..." I smiled—not wise enough to tell
Your each touch coded parting, marked farewell.
