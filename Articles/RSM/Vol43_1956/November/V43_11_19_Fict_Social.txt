DEVELOPING  SAFEGUARDS  FOR  YOUTH 
721 
Mothers,  how  close  are  you  to 
your  daughters?  Do  you  pal  with 
them?  Do  they  share  their  prob- 
lems and  secrets  with  you,  knowing 
that  they  shall  receive  sympathetic 
understanding  and  helpful  counsel 
and  guidance?  Or  do  they  prefer 
to  go  to  young  people  of  their  own 
age  and  social  group  or  clique  to 
unburden  their  hearts  concerning 
personal  problems  and  seek  counsel 
and  advice  that  should  come  from 
the  home?  Too  many  young  peo- 
ple are  afraid  to  discuss  intimate 
matters  with  parents  because  they 
are  shrugged  off  for  lack  of  interest, 
or  time,  or  it  is  something  not  to 
be  talked  about,  when  to  youth  it 
is  important  and  maybe  a  pressing 
matter,  a  problem  with  which  they 
are  presently  faced.  A  satisfying 
answer  and  wise  direction  may  be 
needed  to  protect  and  safeguard 
them  against  pitfalls  that  might  be 
imminent  in  their  lives.  Youth  re- 
quire understanding  and  help  in 
the  process  of  growing  up,  for  it  is 
the  most  critical  and  important 
time  in  their  lives.

joyful  and  fruitful  living.  Does  the 
home  promote  love  and  affection? 
Is  there  respect  of  children  toward 
parents  and  parents  toward  chil-

about  daily  family  prayer?  Is  the 
recommended  weekly  family  home 
night  religiously  followed?  Is  the 
family  home  program  of  activities 
and  functions   occasionally  revised 
to  meet  the  changing  needs  of  chil- 
dren in  growing  up  to  enliven  inter- 
est and  promote  development  and 
progress  in  family  and  social  rela- 
tionships? Is  the  gospel  taught  and 
the  principles  of  morality  discussed 
in  language  that  will  interest  youth 
and  give  them  understanding  to 
help  them  obtain  the  basic,  whole- 
some satisfactions  in  life  which  in- 
clude deeper  fundamental  loyalties 
such  as  love  of  God,  love  of  the 
Savior,  love  of  the  gospel,  love  of 
the  Church,  love  of  fellow  men, 
self-respect,  the  attainment  of  a 
happy  marriage  and  an  ideal  family 
life,  which  provide  a  bulwark  against 
material  and  worldly  things?  Are
