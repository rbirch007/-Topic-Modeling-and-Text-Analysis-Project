sixteen  years  of  age.  Other  bap- 
tisms followed,  performed  principal-

the  island  of  Maui.  On  August  6, 
1851,  the  first  branch  of  the  Church 
was  organized  at  Kealakou,  island 
of  Maui,  by  Elder  Cannon.  It  was 
known  as  the  Kula  Branch.  By  Sep- 
tember i?  1851,  the  membership  of 
the  Church  in  the  islands  num- 
bered 220;  this  number  increased 
to  4,000  by  the  end  of  the  year  1853. 
In  1854,  while  still  on  his  mis- 
sion, George  Q.  Cannon,  assisted  by 
several  educated  natives,  translated 
The  Book  of  Mormon  into  the 
Hawaiian  language.  The  book  was 
published  the  following  year  in  San 
Francisco. 
The  work  prospered  and  a  temple 
was  built,  between  1915-1919,  near 
the  settlement  of  Laie,  on  the  island 
of  Oahu.  Two  stakes  have  been 
organized  in  recent  years,  Oahu  and 
Honolulu. 
Today,  there  are  approximately 
7,000  members  of  the  Church  in  the 
Hawaii  Mission,  located  in  forty- 
three  branches.  D.  Arthur  Hay- 
cock is  the  Mission  President. 
Forty-one  Relief  Society  organiza- 
tions, with  810  members,  were  re- 
ported in  December  1955.  Maurine 
M.  Haycock  presides  over  the 
Hawaii  Mission  Relief  Society.
