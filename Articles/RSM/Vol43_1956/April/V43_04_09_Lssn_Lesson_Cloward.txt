[Lesson From Letty Arlene D. Cloward]
"Yes, darling," she said happily, as his arms opened to receive her, "everything's all right." > She Kingbird Ethel Jacobson His court is the orchard on the hill, His throne is a peach-tree bough; A ruby winks from the sable crown That graces his royal brow. In robes of velvet, ermine-trimmed, He watches with lively eye Over his feathered colony, Watches the wide blue sky That no marauding hawk or crow May swoop with threatening wing "While a small bird sits on a peach-tree bough, A guardian and a king!
