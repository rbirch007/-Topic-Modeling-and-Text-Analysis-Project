I Need a Tranquil Hour
Wanda G. Nielson

I need a while beside a mountain stream—
No moment—but a long and tranquil hour,
Time to see the cliff with crannied flower,
The spruce and aspen, and the sun's pure beam;
Till mountain, sky, and water make a balanced whole,
And the picture has etched itself into my soul.

I need to watch the eager, plunging fall;
The churn of foam and water deep below;
The dashing against rocks, the quiet pools; the flow,
Full-bodied, swift, along the gray cliff's wall;
Till leaping, pausing, gliding, is one dramatic whole,
And the rhythm has rolled itself into my soul.

I need to hear the thunder in the roar
Of falls, the laughter of ripples thinly flowing,
The bass-viol undertones of volume growing,
And the soft lap-lapping along the mossy shore;
Till the booming, babbling, gurgling, makes one symphonic whole,
And the music has sung itself into my soul.

I need a tranquil hour in which to see
A mountain stream, and find serenity.
