[Vol43 | August 1956 | Sequence: 20 | Category: Poem | Title: Home | Author: Catherine E. Berry]
Wherever you were born, that land shall be
Home to your heart—beside the rolling sea,
In mountain country or the wandering plain,
A part of it shall stay with you, remain
To color sunrise or the day's bright close;
Your mind can feel it there, emotion knows
No other background that can give you peace,
That sure sense of belonging, heart's release.
If you should wander far from climbing hills,
Or from a rock-bound coast where water spills
The foam of mounting waves upon the sand,
If you should leave the rolling, green-spread land,
No matter where you go, it follows you,
Like treasured dreams forever breaking through
The round of time, and you will feel the lack,
The haunting need of it that calls you back.
