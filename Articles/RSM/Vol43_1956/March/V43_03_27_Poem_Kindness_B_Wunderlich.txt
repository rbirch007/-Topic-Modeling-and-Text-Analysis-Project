[Vol43 | March 1956 | Sequence: 27 | Category: Poem | Title: Kindness | Author: Jane B. Wunderlich]
Kindness Jane B. Wunderlich Like the gentle gift of shade where strong trees grow, Whose pliant green deflects gold-pinioned heat, Whose branching promise beckons dusty feet, And punctuates their wayworn come and go; Or, like the firelight's all-inclusive glow, Whose welcome conquers wind-wild cold and sleet, Whose cordial hearth enfolds to snug retreat, And beautifies the silent drift of snow; So a brief beneficence may fall, May nourish roots, deep in arid need, Releasing tenderness concealed in seed To lift its valiant foliage over all, And bloom in thumb-frayed memories, half hidden, Spilling fragrance everywhere — unbidden.
