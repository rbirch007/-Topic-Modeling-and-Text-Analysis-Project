To My Mother By Ruby Baird Anderson
Could I repay the love I owe I'd glorify thy name And heap upon thee A mantle white with purity fair
Along the dusty way Where Age must take its faltering step And Youth pass by some day.
I'd pass the spotless mantle on She won with pain and tears, And wrapped around my shoulders weak To wear throughout my years.
When sickness came across my path I'd bathe the fevered brow— And red with valor won To Him I'd meekly bow.
I'd grow sweet flowers bright and Forgetting self in service true. From battling all the faults of life Until her span was done.
In service, truth and righteousness Until her span was done.
