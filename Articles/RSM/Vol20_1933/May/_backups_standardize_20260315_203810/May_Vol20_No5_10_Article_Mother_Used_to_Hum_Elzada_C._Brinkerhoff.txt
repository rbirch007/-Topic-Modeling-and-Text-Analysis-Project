Mother Used to Hum By Elzada C. Brinkerhoff
At work, my Mother used to hum Sometimes I'd recognize a tune And shrill I'd pipe "Jee-ru-sa-lum" Or "Rock of Ages Cleft for me," I'd sing the words I knew, then hum.
She'd cross the room from stove to sink, At work, she'd hum from morn till night— Now that I'm grown I often think That by the sacred tunes she hummed She taught her child, of God, to think.
I wonder if the mothers hum The old and sacred songs today, Or do they trill ta-te-ah-tum And boopadoop for children' ears?— I'd rather hear my Mother hum.
