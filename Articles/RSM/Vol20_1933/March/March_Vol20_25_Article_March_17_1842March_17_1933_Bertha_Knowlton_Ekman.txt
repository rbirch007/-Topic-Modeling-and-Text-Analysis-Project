March 17, 1842
March 17, 1933
By Beatrice Knowlton Ekman
When Joseph organized and set apart
The women for relief work in Nauvoo
It was that they might succor the distressed
And lend their aid in want and sickness too.
"I turn the key for women!" Joseph said,
In prophecy that mid March natal day.
"If you live up to tendencies, which God
Has given you, how great—and glorious!"
And now almost a century has passed.
The hand of Time is turned to March again,
And daughters of their daughters carry on
The mighty work that little band began.
These noble women strive to make the world
A better place for all who live therein.
They do a work of service selflessly
And countless hearts and homes are cheered by them.
They symbolize a prophecy fulfilled
"Charity in its fulness
is their aim"
Vast fields for women open to their gaze
;
The key an open sesame has been.
