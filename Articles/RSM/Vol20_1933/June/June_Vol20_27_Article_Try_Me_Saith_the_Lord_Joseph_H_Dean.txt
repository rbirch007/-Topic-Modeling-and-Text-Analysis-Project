"Try Me, Saith the Lord"
Joseph H. Dean

Bring ye all the tithes into the storehouse, that there may be meat in mine house, and prove me now herewith, saith the Lord of hosts, if I will not open you the windows of heaven, and pour you out a blessing, that there shall not be room enough to receive it.—Malachi 3:10.

The Lord has made a definite promise to those who pay their tithing. He has said, "Prove me now herewith, saith the Lord of hosts, if I will not open you the windows of heaven, and pour you out a blessing, that there shall not be room enough to receive it."

This is a challenge from the Lord. He says in effect: "Try me and see if I will not keep my promise." It is one of the few places in the scriptures where the Lord invites us to put Him to the test.

Many people have accepted this challenge and have found that the Lord does keep His promise. They have found that when they pay their tithing faithfully, the Lord does open the windows of heaven and pour out blessings upon them.

These blessings may not always come in the form of material wealth, but they do come. Sometimes they come in the form of better health, greater happiness, increased wisdom, or spiritual growth. Sometimes they come in the form of protection from accident or disaster. Sometimes they come in ways that we do not even recognize at the time.

But the Lord does keep His promise. Those who pay their tithing faithfully will receive blessings from the Lord. This is His promise, and He will keep it.

The law of tithing is a law of blessing. It is not a burden, but a privilege. It is an opportunity to show our faith in the Lord and to receive His blessings in return.

Let us accept the Lord's challenge. Let us "try Him" and see if He will not keep His promise. Let us pay our tithing faithfully and watch for the blessings that will surely come.
