Autumn Happenings By Annie Wells Cannon 9' TIS harvest time. The world is full of golden things. Let the soul be full of golden thoughts. WY, ALICE REYNOLDS, professor of English literature at the Brigham Young University, Utah, has a record of 39 years' service as a faculty member. RS. GAINOR JENNINGS (Penelope Perrill), Ohio's oldest newspaper woman, died last month. For 14 years she was on the editorial staff of the Dayton Daily News. RS. MARTHA MARTIN, of Idaho, just celebrated 51 years as postmistress in the service of Uncle Sam. RS. STELLA ATKIN, a young lawyer of Savannah, Ga., was recently sworn in as assistant in the department of justice. GENORA MARCELLE DURAND, Paraguay's first lady, has been mediator between her country and Bolivia and persuaded the belligerents to hold a peace council in Rio de Janiero. GENORITA CARMENCITA CAMPOS, age 17, is said to be the youngest woman engineer and the only woman engineer on the American continent. She holds an electric engineer's license and is employed by the Pacific Railroad system of Costa Rica. "THE MISSES BURN, STRATTON and ETTRIDGE are the first women detectives on the Scotland Yard police force. They have made a thorough study of criminal procedure and law as they affect police. ANNE LINDBERGH, assisted her distinguished husband in mapping an aerial cruise over the notth Atlantic to Europe. The young couple received an ovation at Copenhagen and were entertained by United States Minister Ruth Bryan Owen. HELEN JACOBS, of California, now holds the woman's national tennis championship, having defeated the popular Helen Wills Moody, who, because of physical distress, was forced to default. DR. MARGARET G. SMITH, of Washington University, has discovered a virus which it is believed, will be a valuable aid in the battle of sleeping illness. DAME FASHION has queer notions for "Milady" this autumn, bringing back the puffs and ruffles of the "90's," while the hats and hair and makeup are entirely unique even for this "Jazz age." MARGARET AYER BARNES' novel, "Within the Present," also Mary Borden's "Technique of Marriage" and Naomi Smith's "Portrait of Mrs. Siddons," are among the new September books. Jaxer BEITH, an English girl, won the $20,000 prize in the international prize novel competition. Her book entitled "No Second Spring," is published this fall. MRS. CATHERINE PHILLIPS gives a colorful picture of early San Francisco days in her recent book ''Portsmouth Plaza." ISABEL WILDER in her novel ~ "Mother and Four" very interestingly relates a simple story of pleasant, intellectual people. Mrs IRENE MERRILL and Miss Phyllis McGinley, two Utah girls, have attained distinction in the literary field, one as a magazine contributor and one as a poet.
