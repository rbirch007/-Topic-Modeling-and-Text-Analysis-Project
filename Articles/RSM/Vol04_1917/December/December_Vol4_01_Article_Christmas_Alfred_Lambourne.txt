[Vol4 | December 1917 | Sequence: 01 | Category: Article | Title: Christmas Alfred Lambourne]

Ah! as the world awakens to the morn, The risen sun illumes the mountain domes, As comes the day on which the Christ was born, What prayer will rise from out now saddened homes? What from the sweetheart in the spring of life, With streaming eyes and words all sobbing wild? And from the trembling lips of some fond wife, To look this day upon her new-born child? Or from that one who hides her bitter woe, Who mourns her last-born son, now far away. The widowed mother with her locks of snow, Who all subdued doth bow her head to pray? Ah, Christ, in tenderness all ones above, Heal these three hearts with Thy eternal love!
