WEDDING BELLS. ! In my lap your letter lies.
Sister, Sister! Mists arise
To my vision tears are falling
On their crystals Time is calling,
Beck’ning with a trembling hand
Backward over Mem’ry’s land.
	Dear, I see a picture there,
Bright and sacred, pure and rare!
Shall I tell it? If my tearsJ
Fall upon the fading years
Do not blame me for I see
Such a fairy, Sweet, in thee!
Rosy dimples, rosy lips,
Roses to thy finger-tips;
Rosy-tinted all thy dreams!
Sunlight in thy blue eye gleams,
Sunlight nestles in thy hair,—
Sunlight roses everywhere
Ah, can e’er my heart forget
	That our babe was sister’s Det?
Has our birdling learned to soar
Field and hill and valley o’er?
Can she safely leave the nest?
Darling sister, is it best?
	Ah, I know your heart will say
That e’en brighter beams today!
Wealth he brings the lover bold ,:i
Who hath won thee pearls and gold
Deck thy fairy boat of Hope;
Benedictine skies but ope
To pour down, in sun or show'er,
Wealth of soul each passing hour	
Shall be radiant be it so—
May’st thou never taste of woe!
	He is true then trust him, Sweet
Glories sparkle at thy feet,
Laid there by his tender hand'
May he always understand,
Love and bless thee may your life
As fond husband, kind, true wife,
Glide adown the stream of time
Through a calm and sunny clime,
Peaceful, glorious in your trust
That each heart is true and just
He is noble trust him, dear,
He will love thee, bless and cheer
Through each shadow that may fall'.
Soft or dark all, overall
Let the light of love descend
Rainbow-tinted to the end!

But, my darling, while we go
Treading here the dust of earth,
Shadows startle storms and dearth
Shake our souls then while you live
	Truest wisdom ’tis to give
Of the deep gifts of thine heart
Still to Heav’n the fondest part:-! 
Trust Him best who dwells above,
Eternal God of Truth and Love!
So shall ye, from youth to age,
Read an ever bright’ning page,
Love shall fonder, happier grow,
Warm as sunshine, pure as snow:
Blessings on thy lov’d and thee
We will plead eternally:
We who loved will love thee still,
Wander wheresoe’er you will.
Dear to us the past Shall be, 
Sacred when we think of thee
To the end, do not forget,
Thou shalt. still be—“sister’s pet.”
