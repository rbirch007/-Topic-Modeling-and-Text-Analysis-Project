[WomansExponent | Vol24 | 1895 | August | Issue: 6 | Title: MY FRIENDS. | Author: E. R. S]

MY FRIENDS.

O, THOU friends of my youth, and life’s later age!
Thy names are inscribed on time’s fitful page
Indelibly written in letters of gold,
On my heart’s living tablet that never grows cold
Though the world grows apace and the cycle of
	time
Reaps the harvest of age,and e’en flowers in their
	prime,
Still enshrined in my soul with all that is dear,
Are the names of my friends in characters clear.
Ah! These friends are the brighest of jewels I
	wear,
More precious than diamonds so brilliant and rare
The very best gifts my Father hath given.
E’en beams from His presence, descending from
	Heaven.
How dreary the world e’en with all of its powers,
Its sunshine, its music, and beautiful flowers,
Without a true friend, with the magic of love,
Refreshing our souls, as the fountain above,
Refreshes our spirits with infinite peace,
Inspiring us ever in good works to increase.
	E. R. S.
