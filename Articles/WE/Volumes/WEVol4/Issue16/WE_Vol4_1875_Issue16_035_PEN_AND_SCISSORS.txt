PEN AND SCISSORS.
	DON'T write long obituaries. Save some
of your kind words for those living.
	MANY run  after happiness, like an
absent-minded man hunting for his hat
while it is on his head or in his hand.
	HE who will not take cheap advice will
have to bear dear repentance.
	WE are oftener deceived by being told
some truth than no truth.
	PLATO said the Creator has so framed his
laws that it is for the advantage of every
one to observe them.
	THE miseries of the poor are as sacred as
those of the rich; and the rich have miser-
ies in spite of their riches.
	PROVIDENCE has decreed that those com-
mon acquisitions—money, gems, plate, no-
ble mansions and dominion—should be
sometimes bestowed on the indolent and
unworthy; but those things which consti-
tute our true riches, and which are properly
our own, must be procured by our own la-
bor.
