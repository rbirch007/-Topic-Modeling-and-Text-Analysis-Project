FROM LAIE.

	The following items are from a private
letter written by Sister Jane E. Molen,
Feb. 5th, 1879.

	"I have just been preparing a mess of
string beans to cook for dinner; I think you
could enjoy our beans, raddishes and tur-
nips. We are having rains off and on every
day; our "dry Laie" is looking so green
and fresh it makes our Elders feel quite en-
couraged.	*	*	*	We are looking for
Brother Richards every day. He was well
when last heard from. The mission is
slowly progressing, the health of the Elders
usually good. Brother Alexander and wife
live with us; they are a nice intelligent
young couple. Sister Dean lives in the
yard, and we are all feeling first rate and
thankful things are so prosperous with us,
so far away from home and Zion. Brother
Molen joins in kind regards to yourself and
family, and to all friends who may inquire
after us"


WOMAN'S EXPONENT.
