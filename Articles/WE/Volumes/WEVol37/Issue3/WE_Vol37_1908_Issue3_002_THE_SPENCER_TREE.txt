[WomansExponent | Vol37 | 1908 | October | Issue: 3 | Title: THE SPENCER TREE. | Author: Unknown]

	THE SPENCER TREE.
Hand hath not traced to what far time
	The tree’s first acorn grew
Before that day when Saxon soil
	Our own forefathers drew
From foreign shores to mix their blood
	With that historic strain
Which, warmed by Persecution’s breath
	Flowed in the Pilgrim’s vein—
The blood that fired the Puritan
	For his sole Conscience sake
The sacrifice of land and home
	With ready zeal to make.
Numbered among the exile band
	That fled from native shore
Their altars on New England’s soil
	Triumphant rose once more,—
And braving famine, peril, death—
	They helped to build in time
A sturdy commonwealth which grew
	To measure every clime.
Their names were with the patriot band
	When Revolution flung
Its banners high for Liberty;—
	They heard its victory sung!
And all they suffered, all they won
	Strengthened the strain of those
Forefathers of our nearer time
	Whose blood within us flows.
Time passed, and Peace enjoined its sway;
	Till down the path of years
A greater call to Conscience came
	With trail of strife and tears;
A sign of faith, from earth and heaven—
	Met by the world’s cold scorn
Which grew to Persecution’s war
	Till deeds of blood were sown.
But still, to Faith’s old leadings true,
	The strain of bygone sires
Leapt in the veins of sturdy sons
	And lit Religion’s fires—
—To be a pillared cloud by night
	To light them on their way,
Oft, too, a mournful funeral pyre
	Which charred their hopes each day.
Old altars of self Sacrifice
	On their new way were reared—
Home, kindred, wealth were all the price
	For faith and truth revered.
Three sons—we know and love them well
	Who gather here tonight,
Three whom in memory we revere
	With token of this sight—
Heard, and responded to that call
	Which kindled Conscience blaze—
And built for us this gardened spot
	To pass our peaceful days.
Some first few branches yet remain
	Of this ancestral tree,
The sap still fresh within their veins,
	Their minds still clear and free.

[Column 2]
Each annual gathering sees each face
	And feels each active hand
A staunch supporter of the cause
	That links the family band.
There’s Theron, Claudius and George
	There’s Howard, Mary Leone,
Aurelia, too, and Katherine,
	All children who have known
Those early days of pilgrim stress
	Our valiant fathers knew
Welding for them the stronger tie
	Which this close pathway drew.
The younger branches and their sprouts—
	How can we name them all?
The list a lengthy volume’s task—
	An army’s muster call:
But midst these leaves full many a name
	May rise to worthy hight,
In lists of proud Achievement’s fame
	Or tournaments of Right’
Some there have been, who but for choice
	And love of humbler lot,
Might have drawn Fame’s triumphant prize
	With honors nobly wrought.
Edith,* we know, had she essayed
	To tread a mimic stage
Would mateh Maud Adams side by side
	On proud theatric page.
Another of that lineal branch
	Gives promise yet, to gain
Proud laurels in his new career
	In Music’s high domain.
But gifted, humble, rich or poor
	We own humility
Before the three, thrice-honored names
	Which make our family tree.
And older, younger—joined to them
	By blood or marriage link,
All render here a wish that time
	May lead us yet to drink
Some measure of the laurelled cup
	Which pledges the three names
That stand for sacrifice to Truth
	And all that Conscience claims
And if we may not win ourselves
	This prize that crowned their day,
May their high effort bring to us
	A privilege to stay
At least within some kindred place
	When Time’s last race is run—
Where we may greet them face to face—
	And hear them say “Well done!”
