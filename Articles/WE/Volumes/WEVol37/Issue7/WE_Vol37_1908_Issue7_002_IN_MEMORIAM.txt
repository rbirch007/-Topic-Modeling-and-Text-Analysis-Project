		IN MEMORIAM.
	ANNIE TAYLOR HYDE.
Read at the R. S. Conference, April 2, 1909.

A gentle spirit hers we miss today—
Her Father called—we could not bid her stay
Her work so sweetly done, now she may rest,
We still are toiling thitherward—God knows best.

And so we do not mourn, for well we know
Thou hadst thy meed of suff’ring here below,
Earth’s narrow vision widened, thou mayst see
Much that to us remains a mystery.

It is not Life to live mechanically—
We do not live while lacking charity—
For Love is All, and Love is God’s own way:
“He prayeth best who loveth most” they say.

And in that blessed Evermore we’ll see
What now, perchance, hath been revealed to thee:
God’s will be done! and Father grant us grace
To reach with her, Thine own abiding place!
