GIFTED POET, HENRY W. NAISBITT.

O sightless Poet! say not yet farewell!
But in thy nearness to the “Golden shore,”
Catching faint strains of “Life for Evermore,”
Half hearing “Mother’s voice” the “Anthem”
	swell,
Of visions like to these, let pen still tell;
Nor lay it yet aside, but as ye soar
Near the “Beyond,” thine inspirations pour
On souls aweary, and where sorrows dwell.
Thy gifted pen can soothe life’s deepest pain,
Can ease its cross, make clear its darkened way;
Then signal us again and yet again,
’Till “Unlatched door” is reached, and Endless
	day
On quickened vision bursts; there shalt thou reign
A king, a priest, a God for aye and aye.
		For the WOMAN’S EXPONENT
					NELLIE BECROFT.
