	OUTLINE, SALT LAKE STAKE.

Lectu;e VII.—The Gospel the Embodiment
	of all Good:
		(I) Truth, Honesty, Obedience, in-
	tegrity and all the beautiful in life.
	“Casting down imagination and every
	high thing that exalteth itself against
	the knowledge of God, and bringing
	into captivity every thought to the 
	obedience of Christ.”
		II Corinthians, chap. x, verse v.

Lecture VIII.—Social Home Life:
		Refinded entertainment and social in-
	struction in the home is a counter-at-
	traction and prepares the young man
	and woman to meet and judge properly
	life and influence in the world.
		(I) Mutual participation with parents
	and children in conversation and 
	amusements.
		(a) Teach order, thoughtfulness,
	obedience and politeness in the home,
	at the table and in conversation.
		(b) Encourage sports and games out-
	doors as well as in for physical devel-
	opment and mental recreation.

Lecture IX.—Education Theoretical and
	Practical:
		“The glory of God is intelligence.”
	Education is the greatest factor both in
	the business and in the social world.
		(I) It is a help both to the teacher
	and the child when parents interest
	themselves in school life.
		(a) Study the natural bent of the
	child’s nature and strive to educate to
	future needs.
		(b) Avoid a waste of brain tissue,
	bodily strength, time and means by
	cramming and training in useless
	lines.

[Column 2]
		(c) Literature and the Arts com-
	bined with manual training and domes-
	tic science make the useful and refined
	life.
		The truest and best education is that 
	which makes the man or woman the
	most useful.

Lecture X.—Spiritual Character:
			“As it is done in heaven.”
		(I) Reverence authority. Obedience
	is the first law of heaven and order is
	the result of that obedience.”
		(a) The priesthood the government
	of God upon the earth.
	“The truth will make you free” (Doc. and 
Cov. Lecture 84. verses 21 and 22.)

Lecture XI.—Marriage:
		The parents are the high priests
	responsible to God for the spirit of
	their ministry. The home is the sanc-
	tuary of the human race where each
	generation is consecrated for life’s
	work.
		(I) Teach the sacredness and glory
	of the Temple marriage compared with 
	the temporal.
		(a) Dwell upon the rights of eternal
	inheritance and warn against the dis-
	inheritance of our posterity.
		The ties that bind us here
			Are not for life alone,
		No! hold them ever dear
			In heaven they’re closer drawn,”

Lecture XII.—Patriotism:
	“We bel[-]eve in being subject to Kings, Presi-
dents, rulers and magistrates in obeying, honor-
ing and sustaining the law.”

		Every family owes the sacred duty of
	planting in the minds and hearts of lts
	growing members, enthusiasm for
	their country, love for its history and 
	its flag, obedience to its laws and rev-
	erance for its institutions.
		(I) If you wish your sons and
	daughters to make good citizens instil
	in their hearts a love and devotion for
	home and country.
		The greatest Americans have had
	patriotic mothers.
			ANNIE W. CANNON,
			CARRIE S. THOMAS,
			JULIA S. WOOLLEY,
						Committee.
