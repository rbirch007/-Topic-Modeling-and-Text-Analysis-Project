		BENSON STAKE.

Lecture I.—Divinity of Womans Mission:
		(a) First command given to Adam
	and Eve Gen i: 28.
		(b) Woman as a helpmate Gen. ii:
	28; Juvenile Instructor. Vol. 38, No.
	12.
		(c) Woman divinely chosen to be a
	mother Gen. xvii: 15-20; I Nephi ii:
	14-21.
		(d) Mission of woman further ex-
	plained: 1. Joseph Smith’s instruc-
	tions at the organization of the Relief
	Soeiety. 2. Address to the Relief Soci-
	ety, April 3, 1903, by President Jos.
	F. Smith—WOMAN’S EXPONENT, Vol.
	31: 23-34

God bless woman, for to her has been given,
To people the earth with the roses of heaven. 

Lecture II.—Home:

		(a) What constitutes a home.
		(b) An ideal home. As a pattern,
	Doc and Cov. Sec. 109: 8, 9.

“Mid pleasures and palaces though we may roam,
Be it ever so humble, there’s no place like home;
A charm from the sky seems to hallow us there, 
Which seek through the world, is ne’er met with
	elsewhere.”

Lecture III.—Arrangement of the Home:

		(a) Comfort:— Young Womans’ Jour-
	nal Vol. 13, Nos. 1, 2, 10, 11, 12.
		(b) Economy of money, time,
	strength—Young Woman’s Journal
	Vol 13.
		(c) Convenience—Young Woman’s
	Journal Vol. 13, No. 3, 4, 5, 6, 7.
		(d) Play room—Young Woman’s
	Journal Vol. 13, No. 12.

	The best conditions for securing an artistic home
are the combination of taste, good judgment, n-
genuity, with industry and little money.

Lecture IV.—Habits in the Home:

(a) Good.
		(a) Regularity of meals.
		(b) Regularity of rising and retir-
	ing, Doc. and Cov.
		(c) Order and neatness.
		(d) Word of Wisdom.
		(e) Proper language.
		(f) Courting: true, false.
		(g) Unselfishness.
(b) Bad.
	Form only good habits, that ye may have none
to reform.

Lecture V:

		(a) Respect to husbands Eph. v:
	22-25.
		(b) Respect to parents 5th com-
	mandment.

	As the wife respects the husband, so the child
respects the father.
	As Christ is the head of the Church, so man is
the head of the woman. 

VI.—Religious Devotion in the Home:

	(a) Time and place. 
	(b) Method of adopting.
	(c) Need of inspiration.


Sacred song invites the Holy Spirit. 
	Prayer invokes the blessings of God. 
	Knowledge obtained by hearing the word of 
God establishes faith in a child for Him and His 
works. 
	Faith once firmly rooted is not easily shaken. 
				—Extract, Francis M. Lyman.
