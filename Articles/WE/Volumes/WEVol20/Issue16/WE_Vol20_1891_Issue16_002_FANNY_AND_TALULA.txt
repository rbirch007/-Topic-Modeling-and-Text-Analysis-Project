[WomansExponent | Vol20 | 1891 | March | Issue: 16 | Title: FANNY AND TALULA. | Author: L. M. HEWLINGS]

	FANNY AND TALULA.

They have passed away like a gentle breath,
Their eyes are closed in the sleep of death,
In Spring and noon of womanhood,
Tender and loving, wise and good,
Ah! too good for the sinful earth,
Only the angels knew their worth,
So they called them home unto them,
Decking their brows with diadems.—
Better to smile when such true lives,
Are called home from earth's bitter strife,
Better to know their tasks are done,
Trials all ended, glory won.
Sad tears are vain when souls so bright,
Wing their way to the gates of light.—
Called away! but their sweet good will,
Like fragrant odor lingers still;
The tender lesson's mem'ry brings
Love's healing mantle o'er us flings,
We learn to follow paths they trod,
Be more like those, now gone to God.
			L. M. HEWLINGS.
[My heart was touched and moved to write, these lines
after reading the notice of those sisters departure from
earth-life in the last issue of the EXPONENT.]
