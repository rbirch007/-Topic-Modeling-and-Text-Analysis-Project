A MESSAGE FROM THE SPIRIT WORLD

Little Helen’s spirit speaks—
	List, oh mother, hearken!
“Here the sunny days and weeks,
	Nothing comes to darken.

“Tell our loved ones, on the earth,
	Not to grieve, so lonely;
In this new and heavenly birth,
	There is brightness only.

“Tell dear papa to be brave,
	Pure and noble ever;
So in life beyond the grave,
	We’ll be parted never.

“And our sweet Virginia May,
	(Precious little sister!)
While she prayed the other day,
	I drew near and kissed her.

“Tell her angels truly write,
	All she’s ever saying,
And they smile in such delight,
	When she’s humbly praying.

“Tell dear grandpa, kind and true,
	In my new position,
That to love and help him, too,
	Is, in part, my mission.

“Little children, every one,
	Aid in preparation
Of the great work being done
	For the world’s salvation.

“There were few things on the earth,
	I could do, if any,
Of real, lasting use and worth—
	Here I find so many!

“It was hard to part with you,
	We so loved each other!
But if you could have one view
	Of my new life, Mother—

“You would never wish me there,
	Dreading pain and sorrow;
Here I have no cross to bear,
	Joyous comes each morrow.

“Every day new pleasure brings
	Some new form of beauty;
We all do such happy things,
	Works of love and duty.

“Grandpas, grandmas, cousins dear,
	Hosts of friends and neighbors,
Are so kind and helpful here,
	Sweet are all our labors.

“Sweeter far than play on earth,
Blissful songs and gentle mirth,
	Work is recreation;
	Free from all temptation.

“And the joy of doing good
	Is such holy gladness;
Mother, if you understood
	You would conquer sadness.

“As you grow more reconciled,
	I shall oft be near you,
And the presence of your child
	Will uplift and cheer you.

“But through heavy clouds of grief,
	I’ve no power to reach you,
Cast them off, and find relief,
	Dearest, I beseech you!

“Sneak good words to some poor heart
	Which, like yours is stricken,
And the hope you thus impart
	In your own shall quicken.

“Let your hands be busy, too,
	Comforting some other;
And the Lord will strengthen you,
	Darling, sweetest Mother!

“Papa, mamma,—oh so dear!
	Jesus loves to bless you;
While your Helen hovering near,
	Yearns so to caress you.

“Visit oft the House of Prayer,
	Learn best ways of living;
Trust the Father’s loving care,
	Tender and forgiving.

[Column 2]
“Think not much of worldly wealth;
	Fleeting are earth’s pleasures,
But regard your strength and health—
	These are priceless treasures.

“Search the Scriptures, look aloft;
	Pray, your sins confessing;
Then you’ll feel my presence oft;
	Come with some new blessing.
				—L. L. Greene Richards.
