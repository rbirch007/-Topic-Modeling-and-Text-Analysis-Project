HOUSEHOLD HINTS.
	Following are some timely and well-
proven recipes:
	CRANBERRIES.—3 cups berries, 1 1/4 cups
sugar, 1 cup boiling water, cover and boil
ten minutes; this is not strained; jells nice-
ly, and keeps the bright color.
	FILLING FOR SQUASH PIE.—Two cups
of squash, one cup milk, 1/4 cup sugar, 1/2
teaspoonful salt, 1/4 teaspoonful cinnamon,
1/4 teaspoonful ginger, a little nutmeg and
2 eggs. This quantity makes two pies.
	DECORATIONS—In place of flowers, which
in winter time are expensive and hard to
obtain, a basket of fruit with ivy leaves
entwined makes a pretty and inexpensive
table decoration.
	MAHOGANY CAKE.—1/2 cup chocolate
grated and 1/2 cup milk boiled together, 1/2
cup of butter, 1 1/2 cups sugar, 2 cups flour,
3 eggs, 1/2 cup sweet milk, 1 level teaspoon-
ful of soda, 1 teaspoonful vanilla. Bake 30
minutes in moderate oven.
	ENGLISH PLUM PUDDING.—1 lb. brown
sugar, 1 lb. suet, 1 lb. bread crumbs, 1 lb.
raisins, 1 lb. currants, 1/4 lb. citron and lem-
on peel, 1/2 oz. each mace, cinnamon and
allspice, 1 nutmeg, 9 eggs (beat the yolks
and whites separately), 1 cup milk, enough
flour to make a nice stiff batter. Boil in
mould or bag from eight to ten hours the
first day, and four to six hours the day of
using.
