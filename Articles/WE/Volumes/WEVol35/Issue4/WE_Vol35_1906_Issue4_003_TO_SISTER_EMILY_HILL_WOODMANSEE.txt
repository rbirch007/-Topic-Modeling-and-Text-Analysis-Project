[WomansExponent | Vol35 | 1906 | October | Issue: 4 | Title: TO SISTER EMILY HILL WOODMANSEE. | Author: Unknown]

TO SISTER EMILY HILL WOODMANSEE.
Dear sister of song, we laid thee to rest,
’Mid music and flow’rs, whilst the comforting
	guest
		From the home of the angels
		Poured forth promise and blessing
		On those who were weeping,
		Thy memory caressing,
	For the life thou hadst lived.
Blessed sister of song, so modest and sweet,
So dainty in person, so friendly to greet;
		While thy spirit was welcomed
		With joy and rejoicing,
		Thine own words, so gifted,
		Here the prophet was voicing,
	And the life thou hadst lived.
Sainted sister of song, true and faithful wert thou,
And brave as the bravest of women I trow
		In defense of thy people—
		Thy pride and thy glory;
			The foes of the kingdom
			Yet shall yield to thy story,
	In the life thou hast lived.
