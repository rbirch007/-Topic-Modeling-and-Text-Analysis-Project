		      SEVENTY-ONE.
	On Wednesday evening Novenber, 20th, a
select party of friends of Mrs. M. Isabella Horne,
met by invitation at the residence of her daughter
Mrs. Clara E. James, to offer their congraulations
and celebrate with appropriate festivities the
birthday anniversary of this honored lady.
	The weather being unfavorable many were
hindered from coming, but there was a goodly
company assembled in Mrs. James handsome
parlors, and Mrs. Horne’s daughters (there were
four of them present) know how to entertain socially
so as to make every one feel at ease.
	President Wilford Woodruff and President Geo.
Q. Cannon were present, Bishop Geo. H. Taylor,
David James Esq., and other gentlemen besides
a large number of ladies. Most of the evening
was devoted to social conversation, but about nine
or half past nine, the attention of the guests was
called and Mrs. Lula Greene Richards read an
original poem, which we give below.
