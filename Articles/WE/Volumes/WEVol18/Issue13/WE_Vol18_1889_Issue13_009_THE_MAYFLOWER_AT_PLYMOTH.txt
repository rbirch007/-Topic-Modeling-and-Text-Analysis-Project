THE MAYFLOWER AT PLYMOTH.

	There has been completed recently at the
capital a painting which is attracting much
attention, not only for the artistic features of
the work but also for the historic interest
attached. The painting is the work of
Harrison Eastman, of Washington. It repre-
sents the arrival of the Mayflower at Plymoth
Rock. The picture was painted for Mrs. Le-
land Stanford, wife of Senator Stanford, of
California, and is the result of long and la
borious research on the part of the artist. It
is said that the price paid for the work is
$25,000.
