[WomansExponent | Vol18 | 1889 | October | Issue: 9 | Title: THE INFANT SISTER. | Author: Unknown]

THE INFANT SISTER.

	Let me see our Baby Sister 
Press my lips upon her cheek;  
	Isn't she a sprightly "fister"?
How her young eyes dance and speak!
	Telling us of happy Heaven,
Joyous message from above;
	As of follies all forgiven,
And of new born faith and love.

	Gently greet the youthful stranger,
Bid her welcome everywhere, 
	Help to shield from threat’ning danger,
Soothe her hours of anxious care.
	How her weary feet may blister.
In the rough and heated sand!
	Cheer her, aid her, brother, sister,
Take her kindly by the hand.

	Lead her bravely, firmly onward,
Though an infant, born to-day,
	Hopefully she reaches sunward.
For our “Y. L. M. I. A."
	Years ago was contemplated,
First, by Heaven inspired “Hope,"
	Such a birth—yet watched and waited
This fair child for strength and scope.

	When her elder sister ventured,
Eighteen years ago, to speak, 
	How, by some, her ways were censured!
Some have grown more wise and meek.
	Now the Infant and the Maiden, 
Side by side, in rosy youth,
	Clasping hands, with hearts love-laden,
Stand for ZION, GOD and TRUTH.
						L. G. RICHARDS.
