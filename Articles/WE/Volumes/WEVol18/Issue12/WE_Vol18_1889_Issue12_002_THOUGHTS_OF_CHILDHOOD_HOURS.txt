[WomansExponent | Vol18 | 1889 | November | Issue: 12 | Title: THOUGHTS OF CHILDHOOD HOURS. | Author: L. M. H]

	THOUGHTS OF CHILDHOOD HOURS.

		Thoughts of my childhood hours,
			Revel awhile for me,
		And pluck from fancy's bowers,
			Fair leaves from life’s young tree
		Sweet buds of youth and beauty,
			Growing uncheck’d and free, 
		In loving truth and duty
			Oh, bring them back to me !

		Innocence, purest flower,
			Blooming on childhood's tree,
		Revive in fancy’s bower,
			And nourish it for me.
		Bring forth each fairy color,
			As erst in morn’s young day,
		Bud and bloom unsullied,
			As in life's early May.

		Bright, lovely, vernal garland;
			O bloom for me once more,
		In fancy's fairy garden,
			On youth's bright sunny shore—
		Let songs of artless gladness,
			Now wake an echo’d lay,
		In my tried soul of sadness,
			Cold waves of sorrow stay.

		Thoughts of my childhood hours
			Priceless thou art to me,
		Revelling in the wild-wood,
			Untramell'd pure and free.
		Could aught on earth be given,
			To soothe awhile my pain,
		'Twere thoughts of early morning,
			When life was free from stain.

		Ere poison’d shafts of malice,
			Or arrow barb’d with scorn,
		Had enter’d in life's chalice,
			To leave its rankling thorn,
		Then come, and o’er this bosom,
			Thy calm influence pour,
		And the world shall never know
			My dream of love is o'er.
									L. M. H.
