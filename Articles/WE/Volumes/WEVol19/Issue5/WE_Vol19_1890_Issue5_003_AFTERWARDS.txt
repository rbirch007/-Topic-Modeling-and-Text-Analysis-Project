[WomansExponent | Vol19 | 1890 | August | Issue: 5 | Title: AFTERWARDS. | Author: Unknown]

AFTERWARDS.

When rushing swift to some vague goal
    We cast chance glances by the way,
But have not time to pause or look
    Lest we the unknown world shall stay,
We snatch quick sight of pleasant things;
    And when we find ourselves at last,
Within the land we made such haste
    To reach, and see the flowers fast
Fade in the dullness of decay,
    We think of glimpses that were flashed
From deep rich tints of summer’s bloom.
    As o’er the way we careless dashed.

Thus when we struggle to attain
    Some cherished plan, some topmost hight.
That seemed the one alluring way,
    And to that end both day and night
We hurried swift, nor noticed aught
    Of modest grace in humble ways,
Till fades the sky's enchanting blue,
    And we remember then some days,
Whose swift, sweet hours flew by like birds
    Upon the wing with merry lays.
Whether they flew to north or south,
    We heeded not till after days.

Returning late they brush their wings
    Close to our face; and then we feel
Something we lost and gave no thought.
    Till thus we’re smote in mute appeal.
And standing dizzy and alone,
    We dreaming measure years apast.
By just these hours, How strange wee birds,
    Ye should usurp the throne at last.
