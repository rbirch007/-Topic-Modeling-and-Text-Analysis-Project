A CALL.

BY RUBY LAMONT.

There's a flash of light in the midnight sky,
There's a voice of strength as the flash sweeps by,
For Oppression's doom has been sounded far,
Lovely Freedom wakes with the day-beam's star!

Shall we close our eyes to our neighbor's need?
And though we be blest shall we cease to plead
For the cause of Right, for our sister's woes,
While fair Justice droops 'neath her cruel foes?

How long, how long shall we bend and weep,
How long shall the waves of injustice sweep
Over human life, with its woe and care,
To darken a world that were else so fair?

Awaken! Arise! Let thy slumbering heart
In the march of Right take a noble part!
Thou shalt share the palm by the victor's side,
And rejoice with them who for Truth have died.

Circleville, Utah, Feb. 13, 1891.
