[WomansExponent | Vol19 | 1890 | June | Issue: 23 | Title: PEACE. | Author: Unknown]

PEACE.

God give you peace! your life will have its longings,
	I would not ask they be less keen and deep;
The soul that firmly stands upon the mountains
	Must know the footing of the pathway steep;
It must have trod the valleys dim and low
	And tented where the streams of silence flow.

For you no blank content of heart or brain
I crave, but I would have you, friend, to drain
	The cup of all wide living, feed and grow
Full rich in wealth of feeling, have you know
The strength of strong desire, and bravely face
The close temptations of our struggling race.
I would not spare you strife and weariness,
Nor beg that powers of darkness never press
	Anear your feet; yet ever do I pray
	The rest of God be with you all the way!

		A. FURBER.
