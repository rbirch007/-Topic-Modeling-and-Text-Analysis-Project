	MARY N. ADAMS.


WOMAN'S EXPONENT.


LIFE.

I watched a bud, as morning light
	Burst o'er the earth;
I saw it, as its leaves so bright
	Received their birth!

I saw it, when the noonday sun
	Shed forth its rays;
Its beauty seemed my sense to shun—
	I sang its praise!

I watched once more—the sun had set—
	Its beauty fled—
Around me nought save leaves I met
	To show 'twas dead.

And such is life—we blossom, die;
	Our acts remain,
Which, like the leaves, around do lie
	To mark our reign!
