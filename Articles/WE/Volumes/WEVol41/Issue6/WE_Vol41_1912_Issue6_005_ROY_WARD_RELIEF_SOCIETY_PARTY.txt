ROY WARD RELIEF SOCIETY PARTY.
	The sisters of Roy ward, Weber stake,
held a very interesting party and meeting on
the occasion of the seventy-first anniversary
of the Relief Society day, the 17th of March.
	Several young people helped to make the
program entertaining. Songs were sung by
Emma and Elva Robinson, Melvin Gibley
and Esther Baker; recitations by Lettie
Stoker, Etta Hammon, and Cornelia Wes-
ton, while Leo Lambert contributed a solo
on the clarionet.
	The meeting was presided over by the
president, Margaret Child, who after some
interesting remarks and speech of welcome,
was followed by Bishop Thomas Hollands,
First Counselor Diana Robinson, and Sister
E. V. Jones. All expressed splendid senti-
ments for the work of the Relief Society
and appreciation of those engaged along the
lines of charity.
