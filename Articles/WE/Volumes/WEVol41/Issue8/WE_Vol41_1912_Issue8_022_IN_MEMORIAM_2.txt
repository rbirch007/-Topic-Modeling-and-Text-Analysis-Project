		IN MEMORIAM.
		ANNIE W. LARSON.
	Sister Larson, first president of the
Emery Stake Relief Society, died at the
home of Bishop Sam Larson, Castle Dale,
Utah, May 22, 1913, after suffering many
months. Sister Larson was born October 1,
1832, and came to Utah Sept. 27, 1862.
Soon after her arrival in Utah she was mar-
ried to Christian G. Larson, a former presi-
dent of the Emery stake.
	
[Column 2]
	She acted as counselor to Sister Mary
Ann Hyde in the first Relief Society of
Sanpete stake and was chosen president of
the Emery Stake Relief Society, July, 1882,
which office she held for nineteen years.
	She has led a beautiful life, which is
worthy of imitation. She has been kind,
patient and loving to the many children in
the Larson family, who respected and loved
her as a mother.
	As a leader among her sisters in the great
Relief Society work, she will always be re-
membered.
	It was Sister Larson, when president of
Emery stake, who gave the first donation to
the fund for a woman’s building, the money
thus given having been raised by the sale
of “Sunday eggs.”
	She observed the commandments of God,
and was true to the gospel of Christ.
	We resolve in our hearts to endeavor to
be as faultless as she has been, that we may
go where she has gone. That we may be
joined with her in friendship, companionship
and in the ties of sisterly love. That we
may find the emerald gates of Paradise open
for us as they are now open for her whom
we love.
