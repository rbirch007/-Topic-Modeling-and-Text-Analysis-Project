THE GIFT DIVINE.
TO MARY.
By Maud Baggarley.
And thou wouldst write, dear child?
O, say not so!
For thou must know
And feel, and suffer
E'er the words thy pen
A grace to save, shall give to men,
And thou beside an open grave must wait;
And thou must tarry too,
Without the close shut gate,
That bars thee from some joy
Thy soul doth crave,
Perchance thy heart shall break!
But this I say again,
Full measure shall ye have of pain!
And sorrow too shall scar thy heart;
For these are part;
And parcel of the gift divine.
Then walk the common way of life,
Unheard, unknown,
And sing thy little song of love
In joyful under-tone
For God will see;
And all shall be well with thine and thee.
But if the gift were given you at birth,
Or e'er thou camest to this earth
Fare forth, with spirit bold
And keep thy vision undefiled;
And sing high songs of hope and cheer;
And do not cry aloud at loss or pain
But count them gain!
And laugh;
And priestess be of One Most High;
And guard well the sacred fire
That unto thee is given
It shall engender pure desire;
And light men back to Heaven.
