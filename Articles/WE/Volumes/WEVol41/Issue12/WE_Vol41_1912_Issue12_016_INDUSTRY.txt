INDUSTRY.

	The following essay was given at a Relief
Society meeting in 1856, by Sister Elizabeth
McLelland.
	I have chosen the subject of “Industry”
to speak upon this evening. Industry,
whether it be hard labor, or the exercise of
the mind, is that by which the great and


good of our sex have been distinguished.
Indeed, it is by the application of this prin-
ciple to their lives that they have become
truly good or truly great. It is said of Jesus
that He went continually about doing good,
hence we see industry was a prominent part
of His character, and no one can truly say
he is a faithful follower of Jesus, unless his
faith is made manifest by his works.
	I can bear testimony myself that when I
have the Spirit of God upon me, I am led
to be industrious, and my pursuits in life
though in a humble sphere, give me much
comfort and happiness. My home, or rather
my husband’s house, is my heaven, and 
when I can make it comfortable and neat,
I feel I am improving my heaven.
	It is in this sphere that the faith of those
of my sex is made manifest, for here are
their works seen.
	And how true it is that by their works
or fruits ye shall know them. No one can
hide himself, for all have their sphere to
act in. And therein will their works make
their character manifest.
