ITEMS FROM NIBLEY, OREGON.
NIBLEY, OREGON, May, 1903.
Dear Sister:
	It has been a long time since I wrote to 
the EXPONENT, but I feel it my duty to 
write and let you know of our society in
Nibley away off in Eastern Oregon. We 
have a good Relief Society and have about 
twenty names enrolled. 
	We have a new town just started here 
with about thirty-five families The town
is building up fast, as it has been settled 
about two years. There are about twenty 
new buildings besides the old ones. Nib-
ley is situated on a sandridge about four-
teen miles east of La Grande City, and about 
five miles west of Cove. All kinds of fruit 
can be raised here and it is also a good 
country for grain.
	COM.


Dr. Helene Stocker is the editor and
Fraulein Carmen Teja the assistant editor 
of a new paper for women, Frauen Rund-
schau, published at Leipzig, Germany.	Its
aim is to promote the movement for equal
rights. Dr. Anita Augspurg and Dr. Kathe
Schirmacher are among the contributors.
