THE POET SOUL.
		(Ellis R. Shipp, M. D.)
		By Grace Ingles Frost.
Borne on the eagle wings of thought ascends
The poet soul;
Transcendent o’er earth’s grovelings
In flight,
Its hand clasped close in hand of beauteous 
		love,
With face turned ever toward celestial light.

In rosy clouds of imagery doth dwell
The poet soul,
And as æolian harp, with harmony,
Grows vibrant ’neath a lithesome touch of
		breeze,
In wondrous waves of symphony doth sound
Its heart tones their seraphic melodies.

In all creation naught there is despised
By poet soul.
From nesting bird and fair-faced bloom of
		spring,
Unto the day full fraught with gloom of wrong,
From God to man, from man to insect, all
Have formed the poet’s great world-wreath of
		song.
