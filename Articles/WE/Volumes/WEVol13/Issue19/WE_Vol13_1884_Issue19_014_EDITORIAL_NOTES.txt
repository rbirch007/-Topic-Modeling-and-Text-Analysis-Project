EDITORIAL NOTES.

     ERRATA.--In the first paragraph of “Scenes
and Incidents,”in our last issue, a sentence was
omitted which rendered the remainder of the
subject almost senseless.  It was written in the
journal: “By Bro. Kimball’s request I went to
assist Shter Hyrum Smith to move up from the
river.”
	THE young ladies of Bear Lake Stake have is-
sued a small magazine entitled, “The Literary
Garland,” representing the Y. L. M. I. A. of
that Stake of Zion. Miss Libby Laker, Editor.
It is published at St. Chairles, Idaho, and bears
the motto, "Knowledge is Power.” The copy
shown us by a friend la No. 2, Vol.1, published
July, 1831, and contains 20 pages.  The editorial
is well written and contains excellent advice to
the young ladies.  The other articles of prose
and poetry are mostly from the young girls of
that Stake, and are a great credit to the Asso-
ciations.  It is a very commendable effort on
the part of the young ladies in that locality, and
we sincerely hope their enterprise will succeed.
The WOMAN'S EXPONENT offers congratulations
and would be glad to exchange.
	“THE NEW ERA,” successor to “Our Herald,”
is now  published in Chicago, Elizabeth Boynton
Herbert, well known to our readers and to the
public generally as one of the representative
women of America, is the editor.  It is in square
magazine form and contains sixty-two pages, is
published monthly.  The articles are all from
the pens of able writers; and we think it one of
the very best womens’ papers of the present
time, when there are many that are good.
These journals and magazines in the hands of
thoughtful and intelligent women are doing a
vast amount of good, more than we can enumer-
ate. They reach all classes of society and are
sowing not only in good ground, but broadcast,
much precious seed that will spring into bud,
blossom and fruit in due time for the advance-
ment of the human family.
