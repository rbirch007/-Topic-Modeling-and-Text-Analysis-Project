TIME’S DELIGHT.

For The Exponeut:—
ALL through the chilly hours of winter
Through its gloom or bright sunshine,
Springtime’s budding, or bloom of summer,
Autumn’s golden fruits and grain 
I watch and wait, and vainly linger,
For some echo, token, sign,
From beyond time’s mystic line.

So time glides on, and will not delay
Morning, noon, and eventide,
In swift review pass along their way,
Nought unchanged can here abide.
Man’s golden youth is a summer’s day,
Merging into active noon,
That stretches over a toilsome way,
Down life’s hillside all too soon.

But we’ll strive life’s duties to obey,
Content to sing our low refrains,
Time’s flight no mortal e’er can stay,
O’er all the world it rules and reigns.
Tenafly N. Y. LUCY.
