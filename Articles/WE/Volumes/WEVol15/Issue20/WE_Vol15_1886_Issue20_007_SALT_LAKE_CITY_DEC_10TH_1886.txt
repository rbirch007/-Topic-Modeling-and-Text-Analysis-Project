SALT LAKE CITY, DEC. 10TH, 1886.

	Trembling and faint, in voice and hand,
		Poor mortals we, so weak and frail!
	Yet by our Father's grace we stand—
		All power is His, we will not fail.

	We will not fail, if by His word
		We live and walk the narrow way,
	And heed the teachings we have heard
		Of truth and wisdom here to-day.

	No selfish aim, or worldly pride
		Must claim our thoughts or win our hearts;
	The lowly Jesus is our guide,
		What peace and trust His love imparts.

	Though hard and heavy oft the road,
		Yet patiently we must endure;
	Great are the promises bestowed,
		And God is Truth, His word is sure.

	That law 'gainst which the nations fight—
		Polygamy, so named of men—
	Will live, for it is true and right!
		Though trampled, crushed, 'twill rise again.

	The word of wisdom was revealed
		For a grand purpose, and to be
	To us a strength, a guide and shield—
		Let us sustain it and be free.

	Oh! blessings on this humble group
		Of sisters, pure in heart and true!
	The King of heaven doth kindly stoop
		To own and bless each one of you.

	He looks upon the labors done,
		So small, we think, of little weight,
	And yet He smiles upon each one,
		And counts her portion rich and great.

	Father, to Thee, eternal praise!
		be merciful, our sins forgive!
	And teach us in Thine own sweet ways,
		That charity by which we live.

	Inspire our thoughts, direct our speech,
		Lead us by Thine own hand, and then
	No powers of darkness can o'er-reach;
		Praise God forever more. Amen!

   Interpretation given by Sister Rida Taylor; arranged and 
written by L. Greene Richards.
