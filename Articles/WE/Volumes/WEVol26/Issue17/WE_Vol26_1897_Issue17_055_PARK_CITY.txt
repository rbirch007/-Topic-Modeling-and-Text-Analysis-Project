MARIAN, CASSIA CO., IDAHO.
	Although the Relief Society of Cassia
Stake has not been heard of through the
columns of your paper for some time, never-
theless they have been alive to their duties.
	At a conference just held at Oakley, as
also at the one held three months previous-
ly, the verbal reports of the various repre-
sentatives showed the members to be mani-
festing increased interest and diligence. A
number of the branches hold alternately
with the monthly testimony meetings what
we term a literary meeting, the exercises in
which call for more or less reading and
study on the part of the members.
	The statistical and financial reports read
at the last conference show a slow but
steady increase in both members and means,
also in the storage of wheat. There is not
much call for charitable work, some of the
wards report no poor among them.
	President Louisa Haight presided at the
conference. She and her counselors, H. E.
Tuttle and Ann Wilson, though all in poor
health, felt to praise the Lord for His good-
ness, and encourage the sisters in their work
of charity and mutual improvement. In
connection with others the meeting was ad-
dressed by Sister Avery of Castle Valley,
also by Brother Moroni Pickett of the Stake
presidency and Bishop John L. Smith of
Oakley.
LOUISA PICKETT, Rec. Sec.
	Feb. 20, 1898.
PROVO FOURTH WARD.
	THE annual meeting of the board of
managers of the Provo Fourth ward Relief
Society was held in the Society house on
Saturday February 3, 1898, at 10 a. m.,
President M. M. Watson presiding. In the
absence of the secretary Anna B. Selck was
chosen secretary pro tem.
	Minutes of Jan. 28, 1897, were read and
accepted, showing one hundred and five
officers and members enrolled, and twenty-
eight meetings held. The treasurer’s report
for the year ending February 1, 1898, was
then read, showing donations received in
cash, provisions and merchandise as follows:
Receipts $157.25; disbursments $124.25;
balance on hand $33.00; wheat 30 bushels.
	President M. M. Watson said she was
well pleased with the report and had en-
joyed her labors among the sick and needy
in the ward, and felt to encourage all to at-
tend the meetings during the present year.
	It was moved and seconded that the old
board be re-elected with the exception of
Sarah E. Cluff resigned. The officers that
comprise the board are as follows: Margaret
M. Watson, president; Mary M. Boyden,
first vice-president; Delia I. Booth, second
vice-president; Mary Boshard, secretary;
Anna B. Selck, assistant secretary; Mary
M. Boyden, treasurer; Jane B. Snyder,
Sophina Gee and Harriet E Bean, trustees.
Plans for the new year were adopted and
the meeting adjourned subject to call.
ANNA B. SELCK, Asst. Sec.
