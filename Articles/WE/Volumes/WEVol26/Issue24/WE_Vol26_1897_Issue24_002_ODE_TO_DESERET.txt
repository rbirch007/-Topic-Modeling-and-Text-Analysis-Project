ODE TO DESERET.

Thou’rt beautiful, sweet, our mountain retreat,
	There’s magic in everything here;
Tho’ the raging storm howl, and the angry sky
		scowl
	And all nature in terror appear. 
Tho’ the wind whistle shrill, and each valley and
		hill,
	Echo back to the wild music’s strain, 
Yet it chords with the heart, and doth comfort
		impart
	For it touches a sensitive vein. 
There’s a voice in the wind, which awakes in the
		mind
	Reflections naught else could have stirred,
We commune with the soul, while we try to con-
		trol
	And hush into silence each word.

’Tis a heavenly spell, no bosom can tell,
	That accords with the element’s strife,
And the loud, piercing shriek of the tempest most
		bleak, 
	Alone can awake it to life. 
Here thy tall mountains rise, and seem touching
		the skies,
	Truly wonderful, grand and sublime!
We admire to behold, so majestic and bold!
	Thou art lovely our own happy clime.

Thy ravines and thy streams, where true loveliness
		seems
	Fairer mirrored than ever before;
As we view with delight by the moon’s gentle
		light, 
	Are enough to make mortals adore.

Then thy clear silvery lake, would unconsciously
		wake
	Admiration most pure in the breast,
When the far-setting sun its day’s work hath done
	And its banners reflect from the West.

’Tis an emblem complete, of that rapture so
		sweet,
	That still lingers awhile round the heart,
When we bid an adieu, to friends constant and
		true,
	Compelled for a season to part.

Let the great and the wise, talk of Italy’s skies,
	And sing of her gay orange bowers, 
Thy skies are more fair, more salubrious thine
		air,
	More fragrant and brilliant thy flowers.

[Column 2]
Circassian maids will sink in the shade
	With Deseret’s fair daughters in sight;
Then their love for her laws, well deserve of
		applause,
	They would die in defending the right.

And then ’tis thy boast, thou canst muster a host
	Of such boys so true-hearted and brave,
Who never know fear, and when danger is near,
	Would fight till they died thee to save.

Thou art honored above all the world, we can
		prove,
	For here dwell the wisest and best, 
Who commune with the Lord and receive of His
		word,
	And an Ensign have raised in the West.

And to thy peaceful home, Zion’s children will
		come,
	And all nations shall hear of thy fame,
Highly honored art thou, to thy mandates we’ll
		bow,
	And our hearts shall exult in thy name.
								E. B. W.
Great Salt Lake City, Dec. 1850.
