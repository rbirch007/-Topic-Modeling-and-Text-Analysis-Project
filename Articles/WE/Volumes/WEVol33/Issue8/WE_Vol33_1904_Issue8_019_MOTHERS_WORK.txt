MOTHERS’ WORK.
		SUMMIT STAKE.
Lec, 1st. Woman, Her Mission:
	(a) How to attain the highest type of
womanhood.
	(b) Learn the will of God concerning
them.
	(c) Preparation for further usefulness.
	(d) Laws of health, how observed.
	(e) Word of wisdom a fundamental prin-
ciple.
	(f) Full development of physical and
mental powers.
	(g) Tell of special ambition of women of
ancient Israel.
	(h) How do women of latter-day Israel
compare with those of former-day Israel in
fulfilling the most important function of
woman’s life?
Lec. 2nd.— Wifehood.
	(a) Woman’s duty to husband and home.
	(b) Love of God, desire to fulfill His
commandments.
	(c) How to be companionable.
	(d) Use influence for good, religiously
and intellectually.
	(e) What greater privileges do women of
today enjoy than women of ancient days
enjoyed?
	(f) God’s commandment to Adam and
Eve. (Gen 1: 28-36.)
Lec. 3rd.—Parents:
	(a) Highest calling of man and woman.
	(b) Responsible to God for their ministry.
(Ref. Maeser's “School and Fireside.”)
	(c) Child’s best heritage, honorable birth.
	(d) Purposes bent to prepare heart and
mind for embryonic moulding.
	(e) Cultivation of spirituality.
	(f) Prenatal influence: example, child of
faith.
	(g) Beginning and duration of parent’s
responsibility.
Lec. 4th.—Infancy.
	(a) The child’s welcome as a heaven-sent
gift.
	(b) Cleanliness a necessity.
	(c) Fresh air and warmth essential to
well-being.

	(d) Nature’s food best, how often given.
	(e) Hygienic clothing,and plentiful mas-
sage with olive oil.
Lec. 5th.—Childhood.
	(a) Bright looks and gentle tones the
child’s birthright.
	(b) The character of the child a special
study.
	(c) Prayer taught at the mother’s knee,
and early imprsssions of faith in God.
	(d) First principles taught, obedience,
truthfulness, honesty and respect for the
priesthood and those in authority over
them.
	(e) Faith in ordination taught by pre-
cept and example.
Lec. 6th.
	(a) Co-operation of parents necessary for
future welfare of child.
	(b) Special teachings necessary when
child leaves home to attend district school.
	(c) Example set by others not always
worthy.
	(d) Care taken to guide children in choice
of companions.
	(e) Bad effects of continual fault-finding.
	(f) Parents should control themselves
before correcting child.
	(g) Cultivate spirituality and develop by
constant care. (Ref. “Fireside Talks,”
Carl G. Maeser.)

			ONEIDA STAKE.
	Lesson 1st. Marriage,
		Ye kindred spirits filled with mutual love,
		Pure as the dews descending from above,
		All hail! for you the sacred keys are given,
		To make you one on earth and one in heaven,
		Be fruitful then, and let your race extend;
		Fill Earth, the stars and worlds that never end.
							Key to Theology.
	1. Marriage is ordained of God unto
man, Doc. & Cov. Sec. 49-15. 2. The
great purpose for which marriage was or-
dained is to fulfill that first divine law given
in the beginning— “Multiply and replen-
ish the earth,” Gen. 1: 28. Heb. 13: 4. 3.
Those contemplating marriage should pos-
sess a proper knowledge of the laws of life
and have a reverence for God’s laws con-
cerning the marriage relation. Key to The-
ology, page 165 166. 4.Warn our girls
against the delusive hope of reforming after
marriage a man whose habits before were
bad. 5. Justify advice by giving example
of lives which have been wrecked in this
way. 6. Kind of marriage approved, Doc.
& Cov. Sec. 132-15-18-19. Key to Theo-
logy, page 162.
	Lesson 2nd. Personal appearance and
cleanliness.
	“Cleanliness is indeed next to Godliness.’’
	1. Importance of cleanliness in person
and dress. Doc. &. Cov. Sec. 42-40-41.
2. Effect of personal appearance in society,
in the home, and in business. 3. Clothing
of expectant mothers should be both
healthful and becoming. 4. Good effects
of early training in cleanliness and personal
appearance. 5. Fashions and frivolities
of Babylon should be discouraged. (Brig-
ham Young) Journal Discourses, Vol. 16
& 17 page 27. Doc. & Cov. Sec. 42-40-41.

[Column 2]
	Lesson 3rd. Economy and Industry.
	1. Economy and Industry are unfailing
sources of temporal prosperity. 2. Econ-
omy of strength, of time and of money is
absolutely necessary to success. 3. Thou
shall not be idle: For he that is idle shall
not eat the bread nor wear the garments
of the laborer. Doc. & Cov. Sec. 42-42.
Doc. & Cov. Sec. 72-29. Proverbs 19: 15.
II Thess. 3-10-11-12. 4. Begin early in life
to teach a child to love manual labor: the
educational value of work.
	Lesson 4th. Tithing and Fast Offerings.
	1. By the law of Tithing the land of Zion
may be sanctified unto the Lord. Doc. &
Cov. Sec. 119: 6. 2. Origin of the law of
Tithing, Lev. 27-30 to 34. Gen. 14 18-19
20. 3. Meaning of the term Tithing. Doc.
& Cov. Sec. 119, Mal, 3: 10-11, Prov. 3: 9-
10. 4. When and to whom Tithing should
be paid. 5. Promise to those who are
tithed. Doc. & Cov. Sec. 64-23. 6. Potency
of the law of Tithing. 7. Fast offerings. In
as much as ye impart of your substance to
the poor ye will do it unto me. Doc. &
Cov. Sec. 42. 30-31.
	Lesson 5th. Word of Wisdom and prepar-
ation of Food.
	1. Health, wisdom and knowledge are
promised to all those who observe the word
of wisdom and the destroying angel shall
pass them by. Doc. & Cov. Sec. 89.
Dan. 1: 6-21 inclusive. 2. The word of
Wisdom was given first as a principle with
a promise, but since that time it has been
given as a commandment. 3. Show the
injury and disadvantage resulting from non-
observance of this commandment. 4. Food
should be clean, wholesome, and well
cooked. 5. Avoid the use of fermented
liquors and strong spices in seasoning lest
an appetite be created in the youth for
strong drink.
	Lesson 6th. Review Entire Lessons.

	Lecture I. The Human Body.
	(a) The human body the home of
thought. (b) Woman’s aim a noble one
in life. (c) The human heart the pulse
beat of life. (d) A perfect being the
noblest work of God. (e) We hear no
sound of hammer but the building never
ceases.
		We are building every day,
		In a good or evil way,
		And the building as it grows
		Must our inmost thought disclose;
		Build it well whate’er you do,
		Build it straight, and strong, and true;
		Build it high, add deep, and broad
		Build it for the eye of God.
