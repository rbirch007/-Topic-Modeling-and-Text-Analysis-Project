[WomansExponent | Vol33 | 1904 | November | Issue: 5 | Title: THE FLAG AND THE CHILD. | Author: AUGUSTA JOYCE CROCHERON]

THE FLAG AND THE CHILD.

Rich at last from his mountain mines,
He said: “We will travel far,
My dearest ones shall see and know
What the world and its splendors are.”
So they went by rail and they went by steam
To the Old World far away,
But the little one sighed wearily
And paler grew day by day.

“Dearest, what is it? You’ve but to speak 
And all that you wish may claim?”
Then the dark eyes drooped and in heartsick
	tones.
The little one’s answer came.
“I know ’tis beautiful, and ’tis wrong
That I cannot happy be;
But I want to go back, our mountain home
Is better than all to me.”

So they bade farewell to splendors all, 
But train and ship seemed slow.
To the little one who gazed far ahead
For the scenes that she loved so
One day on deck some one unfurled 
Our country’s flag, and the child—
Leaped and caught it in fond embrace,  
With laughter and sobbings wild.

Hushed for a moment by the sight,
Each foreigner bowed his head;
Then one who was greatest there, stepped forth
And with reverence grave, he said:
“Great is that country, where its child
Has the soul of a patriot man!
Men of Valor! Salute with me,
The little American!”

Year after year, from her father’s mines 
The stream of gold will flow;
And through the years, the loyal child
To perfect womanhood grow.
And suitors will come from far and near 
Her beauty, and wealth to see;
But the Prince of Love who will win her heart,
An American will be.
		AUGUSTA JOYCE CROCHERON.
Erda, August, 1904.
