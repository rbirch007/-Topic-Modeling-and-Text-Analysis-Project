DEEP IN THE HEART.
A woman's heart was one day laid bare,
That others might its secrets share.
It told of deeds both great and good,
Of temptations met and nobly withstood,
Of sacrifice made, of work completed,
Of truth, and love, and despair defeated;
Of battles gallantly fought and won,
And of perfect faith in the Father and Son.

A woman's heart was one day laid bare,
That others might its secrets share.
But oh! what stories this heart contained!
Stories, so dark its readers gained—
A knowledge of sin and unbelief,
Which caused them to turn away in grief.

Both of those hearts that day laid bare,
Had each received a plentiful share,
Of faith, hope, mercy, truth and love
From the Father, with whom they dwelt
	above.
One came to earth, and her gifts did treas-
	ure,
The other thought only of self and pleasure, 
So one heart became like purified gold,
While one grew small, and cruel, and cold.
				GRACE INGLES FROST.
