[WomansExponent | Vol38 | 1909 | April | Issue: 9 | Title: TO JULIA. | Author: Unknown]

TO JULIA.
Let me tell you why I love you—
	Why we’re to each other dear:
’Tis because we dwelt together
	In a higher, holier sphere.
Reared by the same fond parents,
	Taught in the same great schools— 
E’en the colleges of the nobles,
	And observed their righteous rules.

So, when we met in earth-life,
	The same old kinship true
Brought each unto the other—
	A mystic tie drew me to you.
We see alike the Gospel plan
	Which we had known before;
And we revel in its music,
	Just as we did of yore—

When the morning stars together
	Sang that anthem sweet and grand,
We were ’mong that sacred number—
	That glorious vocal band;
And we heard the mighty shouting
	Of our brothers tried and true,
That another earth’s foundation
	Had been laid—for well we knew
That we should dwell upon it,
	In a second grand estate,
With power to win that glory
	For which all the righteous wait,
And rejoice with Father, mother,
	As in the primeval life,
If we could through this life’s wanderings
	Do good deeds with honor rife.

We were ’mong the highly favored,
	Kept back until latter days,
When the Gospel in its fulness
	Would shed its glorious rays
O’er all of earth’s dominions,
	Uplifting fallen man,
Revealing truths kept hidden,
	According to God’s plan.

Well might His sons be joyful,
	His daughters, too, sing praise,
As, looking down time’s vista,
	They saw these wondrous days,
When they should stand as saviors
	Upon Mount Zion fair,
Redeeming friends and kindred
	In the holy temples there,
That the chains which had been broken
	Might be welded once again,
For great ones thus had spoken,
	And all echoed the amen.

We are of the blood of Israel,
	A noble prince was he,
And we hope to be an honor
	To our royal family.
Tho’ born among the lowly,
	We will rise to heights unknown,
And, as saviors on Mount Zion,
	Be entitled to a throne.
					Mary A. Freeze.
