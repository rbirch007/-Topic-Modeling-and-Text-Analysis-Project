[WomansExponent | Vol28 | 1899 | June | Issue: 1 | Title: POET LAUREATE ALFRED AUSTIN. | Author: Unknown]

	POET LAUREATE ALFRED AUSTIN.

Spring time was flowering in your fair young
	face,
When first the sceptre laid on you its load—
Springtime and seed time—and how well you
	sowed,
In the rough furrows Life’s long duties trace,
Scattering with lavish hand the smiles of youth
	and grace.
Oh, what a harvest, lady, now is yours,
Empire and fame and glory, and above
Glory and fame—a universal love—
Love rooted deep in reverence that insures
Remembrance of your name as long as time en-
	dures.
Long may the Indian summer of your days,
Yet linger in the land you love so well,
And long may we who no less love you, dwell,
In the reposeful radiance of your gaze,
A golden sunset seen through autumn’s silvery
	haze.
