[WomansExponent | Vol23 | 1894 | February | Issue: 15 | Title: OUR HOME ABOVE. | Author: Unknown]

OUR HOME ABOVE.
	(Air “The Old Folks at Home.”)
WAY up among the “Many Mansions,”
			Fair, fair to see!
Where Christ, the blessed, holds our ransoms,
		There’s where we hope to be.
There once with father dear, and mother,
			Happy were we;
And with the Saviour’s King, our brother,
		There’s where we hope to be.
CHORUS.
Though our lives are glad and cheery,
	Filled with works of love;
Yet oft the heart grown faint and weary,
	Longs for its home above.
How many loved ones, true and tender,
			From earth set free,
Are dwelling now in bliss and splendor,
		There, where we hope to be.
Like ripened fruit, or leaf, or flower,
			From parent tree,
Some cherished friend is called each hour,
		Home, where we hope to be.
