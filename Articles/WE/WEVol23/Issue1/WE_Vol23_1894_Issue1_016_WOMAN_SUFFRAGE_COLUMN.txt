WOMAN SUFFRAGE COLUMN.
        UTAH COUNTY W. S. A.
	ON Tuesday morning, May first, a W. S.
A. convention was held at Provo, President
Electa Bullock in the chair.  Delegates
were present from most of the towns in the
County and the attendance was unusually
large.  A very interesting program was
carried out. a choir of beautiful young girls
from Spanish Fork, furnished sweet and
inspiring music for the occasion.  There
were some original papers read, one by
Fanny Stewart, of Pleasant Grove, and one
by Margaret Gaddy, of American Fork,
both were excellent; Mrs. Gaddy’s paper
was published in our issue of June first.
Brief speeches were made and addresses

[Column 2]
given by the Delegates and others present;
among whom we recall Mesdames Mary
John, Manila Daniels, Joanna Patten and
Ellen Jakeman, of Provo, Zina D. H.
Young and E. B. Wells of S. L. City.
Hannah Lapish of American Fork made
quite a characteristic speech telling in her
humorous style of her trip to California and,
attending some Woman’s Congresses.
  Some important business was transacted
and after the Convention adjourned, all
present were invited to partake of a
delicious lunch prepared by the ladies in
Provo for this special occasion, and the re-
sult was indeed a perfect success. The
social enjoyment was a particularly at-
tractive feature; such gatherings promote
union and call out the best attributes of our
natures.						     H.
