[WomansExponent | Vol23 | 1894 | December | Issue: 11 | Title: TO HYACINTH. | Author: Unknown]

TO HYACINTH.
For The Woman’s Exponent:—

I COME, Dear Heart, at thy behest:
	Thou wert so long in saying, "Come!”
I queried if thy soul found rest,
	Or if thy lips were sealed and dumb.
		Oh! joyous, blissful day,
		Like morn in rosy May,
		Which gives thee power  to say,
		“Return, and with me stay!’’ 

	I bring thee back the love and hope,
	Which thrilled our hearts in by-gone hours;
No more mid lonely shadows grope,
	We’ll stroll again through radiant bow’rs
		Thy true hand clasped in mine,
		While beauteous wreaths we twine,
		Of leaf and flow’r and vine,
		Where golden sunbeams shine.

Now that we understand once more,
	The longings which our hearts enfold,
Fair Peace her gladness shall restore,
	With Love’s sweet whispers, as of old.
		I’ll lead thee by pure streams,
		Where light celestial gleams;
		I’m what my presence seems
		The Spirit of thy dreams.
