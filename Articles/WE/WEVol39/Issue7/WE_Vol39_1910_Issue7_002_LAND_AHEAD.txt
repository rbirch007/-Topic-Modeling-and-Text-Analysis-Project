[WomansExponent | Vol39 | 1910 | February | Issue: 7 | Title: “LAND AHEAD.” | Author: Unknown]

“LAND AHEAD.”
Our hearts are lifted from the dark,
Our hopes are knowledge verified,
Our doubts have drifted with the bark
That went to wreck on sorrow’s tide.
The thunderous boom of breakers high,
That washed a-deck our joys, one day,
Have lapsed to soothing murmurs, nigh
The heights that lift beyond the bay.

Unruffled, calm, the waters now
Sweep all our struggles hard aside,
There's "Land ahead”—our life boat’s prow
Her straight course knows, o’er ocean wide.
Eternal lights, electric, gleam
Athwart her way, and compass true
Points on to where life is no dream:
Did we build better than we knew?

The storms may rock and winds annoy,
Wild tempests fret, and rough the way;
But, lo! they never can destroy
The passage paid to future day!
I scorn your vexing, tides of earth—
Your strength is puny, quickly spent;
Heaven is our port, and all of worth
Bides there by shores of deep content.

Already is the anchor chain
Within our grasp. See! See! The way
Is radiant, pure, without earth stain,
And leads unto the perfect day.
Ah! blest fulfillment, grief and loss
Roll backward—are forever fast:
“Drop anchor—furl the fluttering sail—
For I am safe at home, at last.”
						—Eva E. Ames.
