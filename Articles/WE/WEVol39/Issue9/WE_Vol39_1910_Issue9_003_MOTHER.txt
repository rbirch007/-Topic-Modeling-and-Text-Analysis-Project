MOTHER.
Mother, most sacred name in all the earth,
How wonderful thy power to impel
In humble cottage, palace grand or prison cell,
Thy holy name soothes comforts and doth
	love compel.

Mother mine, to kiss your lips is love divine,
To feel your warm breath on my cheek
Is rarer far than words can ever speak;
To nestle close upon your breast
Brings nature’s choicest, sweetest rest.

To hear thy voice so rich and clear,
Speak words of comfort, love and cheer,
Restores the heart of pain
To warmth and smiles again.
					—Lillian Whatcott.


In a recent address, Governor Osborn of
Michigan, said: “I believe in woman suf-
frage. I hope to see the question put to a
vote of the people. I have been a believer
in the principles of woman suffrage for
many years.”
