﻿WOMAN'S CALLING.

	During the enlightened progress of the age,
we are gaining a more elevated view of the
true mission of woman, of the dignity and im-
portance of her labors and duties, and of the
true happiness which will be the result of a
faithful performance of woman’s work, if her
good efforts are appreciated and sustained by
those holding the power of reason and justice.
At the present time there is quite an agita-
tion at large, involving many theories, and
even speculation, as to woman’s rights and
wrongs. Who can deny that there is not a
wonderful power, social and moral, in the
keeping of every good wife, every devoted
mother, and every true woman, married or
not.  This power is now seeking expression in
nearly every portion of the civilized globe.
There must be some cause for this! ’tis not a
freak or whim of one separate party, religion
or denomination, or nationality, and will one
day be universal. We, of course, must expect
failure and opposition, but our victory will be
so much the greater. To reflecting, intelli-
gent women, whose faith rests on the teachings
of Jesus Christ, this will be no drawback,
women’s calling is grand and all-ennobling, by
her mission being conducted as the great Mas-
ter intended, all mankind will be promoted to
a higher standard, and better qualified to teach
the restored Gospel as taught by Christ him-
self; thereby saving and exalting the souls of
men in the eternities.
	The calling of our sex is not duly appreci-
ated, woman’s efforts receive but little encour-
agement, and she is not given the advantage
due her; her trust is responsible, sacred and
sublime; that of bringing to earth the body
and spirit of man; of training and cultivating
the mental, physical, and spiritual make up in
the most impressible period of childhood; this
is just as important and sacred as anything
ever acquired or accorded to man, for this
alone, all advantages and honor should be
given to every true mother, and she should be
considered man’s equal, as she verily is, nor is
this the end of her world, the limit of her
sphere, her duties here do not in any wise
make her incompetent for any of the intellec-
tual pursuits of life, inasmuch as her natural
abilities and desires qualify her.
	After Eve was given unto Adam, ’twas said,
“a man shall cleave nnto his wife and they
both shall be one flesh.” Woman was created
for a helpmeet to man, we have nothing on re-
cord to make us think God made the female
inferior in any way to the male portions of
creation, ’twas not suggested that she possessed
just enough intelligence to make a very pleas-
ing plaything or toy for the sterner sex,
neither was it proposed that she should be his
slave or drudge, because her mind was not
sufficiently large to grasp knowlege of a higher
order, but ’tis written plainly woman was
created for a helpmeet, not in any particular
branch or sphere, but, a helpmeet, and that
embraces all that was to be done, every requi-
sition devolving upon mankind, as a matter of
fact woman’s labor is more closely connected
with home affairs and ’tis her duty and privi-
lege to become educated, that she may be pre-
pared to instil in the minds of her daughters
the best methods for saving labor, of using
economy, yet-producing the finest results, being
an expert in the domestic regions, need be no
detriment to one having a perfect knowlege of
any of the arts and sciences; of the laws of the
country the most beneficial regulations, and
most desirable systems of executing the same.
	There are in all communities, some who
were never destined to reign either in the pa-
rental sphere or that of wifehood, their intellec-
tual attainments are oftimes of a very superior
nature; and I argue that their calling is equally
grand, and one writer I know says, higher

than those who rule only in their own homes,
because their aims and interests are for those
who are the offpring of others, and who are
not bound to them by the ties of blood, but
their labors are given cheerfully, because of
the intense desire they possess for the onward
and upward march of God’s children; should
they be kept from the performance of their
natural incline ion and abilities? should their
minds be dwarfed to keep them on a level with
those who could not, if they would climb to 
such heights? I say decidedly, no! they should
also be remunerated as liberally as though
they were of the masculine persuasion. It
really looks, if even-handed justice was dealt
to our sisters and daughters, there would be
less enthusiasm displayed for the right of polit-
ical power which is now deemed necessary in
order to obtain the former points of equality,
although I believe women perfectly capable of
becoming acquainted with everything political,
religious or otherwise; yet I can observe noth-
ing elevating for a woman to make herself
conspicuous in a political primary meeting, or
at the polls, I know too well my opinion here
will be opposed, but I calmly submit to your
disapproval, for it is my sincere views upon the
question, and I fail to see any real benefit we
could obtain by laws and penalties, the power
of right we so earnestly crave will only be
gained by the gift of love and duty, man and
woman are either incomplete without the
other, no great thing can be accomplished if
they are separated, we can only fill our des-
tinies unitedly. We are God’s daughters,
Paul tells us, “we must be in subjection to
our husbands” also our “husbands should love
us, and not be bitter towards us,” if this admo-
nition is heeded by our liege lords, they will
consider our cause, grant us all rights and ad-
vantages, for developing womanhood, and ele-
vating our sex generally. We hold the
priesthood in connection with our husbands,
the right to rule with them, to be a helpmeet
in all things which is only obtained through
the covenant we make to "love honor and
obey.” I am an advocate of justice in every
sense being accorded to women, but I fear
the remedy will not be procured by her be-
coming a party in the fight for political power,
I think it is for our eternal good to make
the best use of our time, improving every op-
portunity to expand our minds, enlarge our
understanding, that our mental capacity may
be sufficient to contain all intelligence, for
that is the glory of God; would those who
withold our privilege, grant us the right of
equality with themselves, they would be re-
warded with wiser mothers for their offspring,
sons and daughters with finer intellectual quali-
ties and brighter crowns to adorn their brows
in that world where they will be devoid of
power to deprive us of the advantages the
great Designer intended should be ours.
