			SMITHFIELD CACHE Co.,
						March 16, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We the Relief Society of Smithfield wish
to let you know what we are doing to help
build up Zion. We are building a neat lit-
tle adobie house to hold our metings in.
We got the walls up and the roof on last
fall, and are going to have it finished as
soon as possible now. The young men and
boys hauled the materials, and the society
had two dances and picnics for them. The
boys had a fine time and were so well
pleased that they said they would help the
society again. The brethren donated some
of the work, and we have paid the rest
with quilts made by the Relief and Re-
trenchment Societies. The Retrenchment
Association has done considerable. Our So-
ciety are doing what they can in the Grain
Movement; we have gathered up some-
thing over one hundred bushels of wheat.
Next year we intend to commence sooner,
and gather in the grain in good season, and
think we can do better than we did this
winter. We will exert ourselves early in
harvest time.
		Your sister etc.
