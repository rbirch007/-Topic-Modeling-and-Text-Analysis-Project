SPLINTER'S.

	Littell's Living Age; of Apr. 28, con-
tains a literary parallel of Daniel Deronda's
mother......The most precious diamond in
the world is said to be the "Pitt Diamond,"
it is now valued at $1,000,000......The "Kin-
tergarten" publishers, New York, have is-
sued a new work entitled "The Cyclopedia
of Education."


A WESTERN paper says: "Wanted at this
office an editor who can please everybody.
Also, a foreman who can so arrange the pa-
per as to allow every man's advertisement
to head the column."


A WOMAN hearing a great deal said of
preserved autumn leaves, tried the experi-
ment by putting up a few jars of them. Af-
ter a time she said to a neighbor she might
as well have thrown away the sugar, for she
was sure they never were going to be fit to 
eat.
