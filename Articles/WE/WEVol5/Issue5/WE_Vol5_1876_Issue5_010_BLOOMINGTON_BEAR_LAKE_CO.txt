BLOOMINGTON, BEAR LAKE CO.,
	July 14, 1876.
Editor Exponent:
	Our Relief Society is prospering under
the untiring zeal of our President L. Green-
holst; we number about sixty, and I believe
a great amount of good has been done by
the sisters in this place in the past year; we
hope to be able to build a good house the
coming summer, that we may hold
our meetings whenever we wish, we
have a good attendance at our meetings,
and the sisters, many of them, feel a deep
interest in doing all in their power to help
on this good work by their faith, prayers
and also by their means; we are sometimes
favored by a visit from Pres. Rich, also
Bishop Budge, who feel a deep interest in

[COLUMN 2]

the Society, and we receive much valuable
instruction and advice from them; we were
also favored with a visit from Sister M. I.
Horne while she was visiting in Bear Lake
Valley, and I believe all who heard her
speak felt blessed and instructed, and deter-
mined to do better in the future than they
had in the past.
	Sister Horne while here organized a
Young Ladies' Retrenchment Association
which is now in good condition; our young
ladies seem to feel a great interest in their
meetings which they hold every two weeks
under the presidency of Sister Hester Dunn;
their meetings are well attended; our young
sisters are not ashamed to pray and speak,
and they feel determined to do all in their
power to forward the cause of the kingdom
of God.
	We had a good time on the Fourth of
July; our Sunday School children had a
nice Entertainment given by the President
of our Society and the Teachers of the Sun-
day School; the Bloomington band played
and the children sang some beautiful songs;
received prizes and were served with suit-
able refreshments; run races, and finished
with a dance in the evening.
	ELIZABETH LONG,
		Secretary.
