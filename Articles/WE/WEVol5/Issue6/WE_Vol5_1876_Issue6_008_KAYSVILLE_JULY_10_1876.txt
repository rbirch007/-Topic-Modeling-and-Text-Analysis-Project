	KAYSVILLE, July 10, 1876.
EDITOR EXPONENT
	The Young Ladies Retrenchment Asso-
ciation of Kaysville was organized May 16,
1876, by Sister Eliza R. Snow: officers as
follows:
	Mrs. Ada Williams, President,
	"	Maggie Allred,
	Miss Helen Hyde,
	"	Matilda Egbert		Counselors.
	"	Sarah Ann Webb,
	"	Martha Raymond,
	"	Mary Ann Layton, Secretary.
	Mrs. Celina Phillips, Assistant Secretary.
	Miss Elizabeth Williams, Treasurer.
	Seventy-five young ladies were admited
as members of the Society. Our meetings
are well attended, and all seem to take an
interest in the welfare of the Association.
	We have an energetic president who is
fast gaining the love and respect of the
young sisters. She is taking measures to
establish Home Industries, according to the
counsel of Sister Snow. At the organiza-
tion of the society, Sisters' Eliza R. Snow
and Rachel Grant gave us some excellent
instructions and advice, which we think
will leave a lasting impression on the
minds of all who were present.
	We gave a concert on the fourth of July,

[COLUMN 2]

assisted by a number of the young men,
from which we realized forty-two dollars,
which will be used for the establishment of
Home Industry.
	The Exponent is read with interest in
our meetings.
	With best wishes for the advancement of
every good work, we remain your sisters,
	HELEN HYDE,
