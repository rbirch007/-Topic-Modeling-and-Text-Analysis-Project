A GOOD MAN GONE.
	In the death of Hon. Francis Armstrong,
which occurred on June 15th, in this city,
a vast number of people in various circum-
stances of life have lost a staunch, reliable
and warm friend. When it can be said of
a wealthy man, as it was testified to of
Brother Armstrong by the several speakers
over his earthly remains, that he has been
“a friend to the poor,” it means more than
all the high-sounding encomiums that may
be paid to his ability as a clever business
man, a successful financier, a wise counselor
and strong pillar in public affairs.
	All these qualities were feelingly attribut-
ed to this good man by the earnest, honest
speakers at his funeral.
