[WomansExponent | Vol28 | 1899 | January | Issue: 15 | Title: A HYMN. | Author: L. D. ALDER]

A HYMN.

		O, Saints of the Most High,
			Who dwell on Europe’s shore,
		You’ve heard His servant’s cry,
			Low bow His thrown before. 
Praise give to Jesus King, He comes on earth to reign,
Soon will the tidings ring, give glory to His, name.

		O, keep your covenants true,
			You made beneath the wave,
		A crown He’ll give to you,
			And life beyond the grave.
A name and place in Zion, awaits you over there,
And Judah’s fierce lion, will soon your way prepare

		Proud Babylon will fall,
			Destruction o’er her spread;
		List! to the watchmen’s call,
			Have Saints to Zion fled?
The sun will darkened be the moon be turned to
	blood,
The Saints will Jesus see beyond Atlantic’s flood.

		Rejoice ye Saints of God,
			For mighty is His power,
		Hold fast the Iron Rod,
			’Tis His avenging hour.
No more rejected there past the cold finger’s scorn,
O’er the power of men, hail Church of the First
	Born.
						L. D. ALDER.
	London, Eng., Oct. 1899.
