LOVING APPRECIATION. 
My Beloved Sisters:
Our "Mother" is at rest, and while the
void her sweet presence filled still yearns
for
“The touch of a vanished hand
And the sound of a voice that is still ”
we will try to prove our devotion to her
sacred memory by living our most holy
religion as she would have us.
I recall the many acts of kindness
bestowed upon me while there with you,
and take this occasion to express my grati-
tude and love to all my kindred and friends
who remembered us as a family and myself
as an individual, who was there without
husband or children. God bless and com-
fort all, is the prayer of your grateful 
sister. 
ZINA YOUNG CARD. 
Cardston, Canada.
