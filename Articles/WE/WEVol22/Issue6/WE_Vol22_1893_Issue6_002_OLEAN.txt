ÆOLEAN.

I heard the wind and I listened low:
	The   wind from  the  South, and my heart was
		light,
And I smiled to hear it  blow and blow,
As it fanned my cheek to a burning glow,
	And my eyes were tearless and bright.
		Youth danced in the wind;
			Joy laughed in its face;
		Grief lingered behind,
			Nor found there a place.
 
I heard the wind, and my head was bent
To catch the breath of the free wild west,
It  brought as its lovely freightage sent,
The fragrance of flowers in sweetness blent;
	The perfumed flowers, I love the best
		Love came in the wind
			And entered my breast,
		Grief lingered behind;
			I deemed myself blest,

I heard the wind and I turned my face
	To feel the touch of the Eastern blast,
And I shivered and shrunk  in my smallest space
As I felt the chill of the wind-clouds trace
	And heard it hurrying past.
		Grief came in that wind
			And entered my heart
		Love loitered behind
			And sought to depart.

I heard the wind  though my head lay low
	I felt the North wind blow on me;
And I wept and moaned as I heard it blow
While its fury drove me  to and fro
	Too blinded and sad to see.
		Death came in the wind
			No power could save,
		And hope was consigned
			To the depth of the grave.
                                                        E. L. G.
