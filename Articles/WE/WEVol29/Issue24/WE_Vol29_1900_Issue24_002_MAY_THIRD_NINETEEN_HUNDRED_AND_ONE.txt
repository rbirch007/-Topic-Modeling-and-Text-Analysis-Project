MAY THIRD, NINETEEN HUNDRED AND ONE.

	The Sister Workers in the Salt Lake
Temple presenting Sister Bathsheba with a
beautiful white lace fichu, designed to be
worn on the head.

The "crown" that we bring you, our mother and
	friend,
    Is only a token of true, tender love,
But adorned with it here, your dear presence will
	lend
    A cheerful reminder of glories above.

While the beautiful "soul crown" you've chosen to
	bear,
    Is Faith unalloyed with any disparity;
Pure hope is the “soul-robe” you modestly wear,
    And your chaste, royal mantle is unfailing
	Charity.
