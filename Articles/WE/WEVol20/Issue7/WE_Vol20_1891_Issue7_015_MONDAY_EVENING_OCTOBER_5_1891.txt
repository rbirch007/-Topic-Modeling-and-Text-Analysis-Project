	MONDAY EVENING OCTOBER 5, 1891
	IN THE
	TABERNACLE SALT LAKE CITY.
For benefit of the Deseret Sunday School Uunion.

	700 Voices—40 Instruments—11 Soloists. The
whole under the direction of E. Stephens. Ad-
mission, 50 cts. Children under 12, half-price.
Doors open at 7 o’clock; Concert begins at 8.
	Programme: Invocation. Grand Chorus, “Let
the Hills Resound" Tabernacle Choir (350
voices) and Great Organ. Male Quartette, “O
World so Wondrous Fair,” Boshard and Pyne
Brothers, (Provo.) Song, “Love's Sorrow,” Mrs.
Bessie Dean Allison. Instrumental Selection,
Ladies’ Mandolin and Guitar Club, (40 Instru-
ments, Miss Ella Olsen, Conductor.) Duet,
“Flow Gentle Deva,” Tout Brothers, (Ogden.)
Grand Double Chorus, “Anvil Chorus,” Juvenile
and Tabernacle Choirs. (At this number the
Juvenile Choir will take the centre of the large
platform, the choir taking each side, the whole
force uniting in singing the second stanza, with
banners waving.) Duet, “The Sailor Sighs,”
Mrs. Agnes Olsen Thomas and G. D. Pyper.
Organ Solo, Joseph J. Daynes. Duet, Boshard
Brothers, (Provo.) Scotch Ballad, “Mary of
Argyle,” R. C. Easton. A good old time Song,
George Goddard, (76 years of age). Chorus by
everybody. Grand March, “Harlech.” (Original
words by Geo. A. Meears,) Combined Choirs
(700 voices) and Great Organ. Benediction.
	E. Stephens, Conductor. J. J. Daynes, Ac-
companist.
