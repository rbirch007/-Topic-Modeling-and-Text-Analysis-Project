IN MEMORIAM.
SARAH WOOLLEY SUTTON.
	Sister Sarah Woolley Sutton, daughter of Bish
op Edwin D. and Ellen Woolley, died March 28
1902, at Paris, Bear Lake Co., Idaho, of pneu-
monia.
	Sister Sutton was born December 27, 1847, in
Council Bluffs. She emigrated to Utah in 1848
with her parents. When a child she passed
through the early scenes of pioneer life. In 1887
she was married to John A. Sutton. Her life has
been one of usefulness and she lived a faithful
Latter-day Saint. When she died she was sur-
rounded by her husband, children, brother and
sister and aged mother.
	Sister Sutton was the mother of two children,
one son and one daughter, both of whom, with
her husband survive her.
	The funeral was held in the Paris 1st ward
meeting house The speakers were J. W. Stucki,
William L. Rich and President William Budge.


The casket was covered with flowers and a large
cortege followed the remains to their last resting
place.
	She led a quiet, peaceful life, and she was
loved, honored and respected by all who knew
her.
								COM.
