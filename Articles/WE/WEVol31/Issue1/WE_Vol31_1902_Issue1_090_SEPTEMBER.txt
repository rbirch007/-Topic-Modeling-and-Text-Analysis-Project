SEPTEMBER.
BELLE M. WEEKS.
On leaf and flower the sunlight lay
At the close of a mild, delightful day,—
Afar the gold of the sunset glowed,—
In softened beauty the landscape showed,—
Above the azure.—below the green,—
And a mystic glory of light between.

Forth through this beautiul land of ours,
Went Summer, crowned with magnolia
	flowers,—
Shod with the silver of running brooks.
Fast sped her feet through the woodland
	nooks,—
While trailing behind the Southern Queen
Was her velvet mantle of sylvan green.

A voice was heard through the echoing wood;
In the path before her, King Autumn stood,
The light that shone from the golden west
Illumed the leaves of his knightly crest,
Till its sprays of maple and birch became
A mingled yellow and crimson flame.

No word was spoken on either side;
He stretched his hands to his gypsy bride,
And the Northern King and the Southern
	Queen
Have met once more in the forest green.
Where without a tremor of doubt or fear,
They keep their love-tryst every year.

And there they meet ’neath the sapphire sky,—
A joyous welcome,—a long good-bye,—
And Nature rewards their changeless love
With a gem from the deep blue sky above, 
That sapphire stone of constancy
September’s emblem shall ever be.
				Author's Magazine.
