R. S. REPORTS.

	The meeting of the Senior and Junior
Retrenchment Associations held in the
Fourteenth Ward Assembly Rooms, June
20th, 1875, was presided over by Counselor
Mrs. Zina D. Young, in absence of the Presi-
dent Mrs. M. I. Horne. In her opening re-
marks she alluded to the sisters who were
absent South, in Sanpete Co., and remarked
though they were away from the meeting
they would not forget it, but would be unit-
ed in spirit with those who were there,
She expressed herself particularly gratified
with the minutes which had been read
there on that occasion. She then mentioned
the subject of sericulture and said it was
then the proper time to save the mulberry
seed.
	Counselor Mrs. P. Woodruff made some
most excellent and appropriate remarks.
Mrs. M. P. Young, Mrs. J. Howe, Mrs. M.
E. Watmough, Mrs. W. East, and Mrs.
Foss, all spoke impressively of the work
now in progress, and the great mission giv-
en to woman to assist in establishing the
Kingdom of God upon the earth.
