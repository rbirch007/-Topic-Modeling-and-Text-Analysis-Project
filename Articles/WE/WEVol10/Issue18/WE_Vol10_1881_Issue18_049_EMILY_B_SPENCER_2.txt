EMILY B. SPENCER


IT is only by labor that thought can be made
healthy; and it is only by thought that labor
can be made happy.—Ruskin.
IT is a great virtue to restrain the tongue, to
know how to be silent even though we are in
the right.
THOUGH flattery blossoms like friendship, yet
there is a great difference in the fruit.—Socrat[-]
