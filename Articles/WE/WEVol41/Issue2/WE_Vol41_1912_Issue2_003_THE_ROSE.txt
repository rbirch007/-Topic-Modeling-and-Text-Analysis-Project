[WomansExponent | Vol41 | 1912 | October | Issue: 2 | Title: THE ROSE. | Author: Unknown]

THE ROSE.
She wears a rose within her hair,
Whose fragrance fills me with despair.
Why should a flower of sweet perfume
Recall old memories of this room,
And one who, long ago, did wear
A rose entwined in her fair hair.
It may be, as I breathe a prayer
Of blessing on that head so fair,
A saintly form of angelic light
Beholds me as I sit tonight
Dreaming of the faded years,
Until mine eyes are filled with tears,
Longing for her at evening’s close
And the perfume of the withered rose.
					—Dolores Watts.
