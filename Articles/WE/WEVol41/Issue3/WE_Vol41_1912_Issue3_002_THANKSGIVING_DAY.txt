[WomansExponent | Vol41 | 1912 | November | Issue: 3 | Title: THANKSGIVING DAY. | Author: Unknown]

THANKSGIVING DAY.
O’er all the land, wherever waved
	Our starry flag, the call has sped,
And over calm or roughest seas,
	To continents and isles has fled,
On lightning’s wings into the hearts
	That know and love its meaning well;
No matter what the scene, or where
	Our country’s sons and daughters dwell.

Upon that day there shall arise
	As light spreads over all the earth,
Like incense, to His throne their prayers
	For this dear land that gave them birth;
And joy and gratitude for all
	Its bounteous harvest and its peace,
Its wastes that wake to industries,
	Where manhood’s worth and power increase.

Frugal and plain in dress and face,
	Those Pilgrims kept this holiday,
Remembering how His guidance brought
	Them through the trackless, stormy way;
How, from strange foes, their lives were
		spared,
	How the stern wastes their labors blessed;
And, consecrated to their God,
	Their lives as He should use them best.

And God, in answer to their faith,
	So blessed them that but prophet’s eyes,
Could have foreseen what yet should come;
	That like a beacon should arise
The government and laws inspired;
	How older nations yet should see
Its flag in every land, and all
	Should reverence its majesty.

And ’neath its mighty, sheltering care,
	Freedom to worship found at last
The true path back to that high Source
	From whence life’s wand’ring steps had passed.
Thanksgiving! for our own dear land,
	Thanksgiving! For the Truth we’ve found,
And, help us Lord, that our life’s work
	Shall make the whole earth Freedom’s
		ground.
					Augusta Joyce Crocheron.
Salt Lake City,
	Nov. 15, 1912.
