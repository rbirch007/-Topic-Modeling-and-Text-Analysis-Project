GREETING SONG.

	[Dedicated by [-]he author to Mrs. Hannah J.
Bailey, on her return home after a six months' ab-
sence on acount of her health, and sung at the
May W. C. T. U. meeting.]
How close to our hearts is our white ribbon
leader.
Our dear Mrs. Bailey, so kind and so true;
How much in our Union we lovingly need her,
How gladly today we greet her anew.
We hail her with pleasure, we thank God in
Heaven,
That He has returned her in health to us now;
Our heart’s adoration to Him should be given;
In praise and thanksgiving before Him we bow.
CHORUS.
Our dearly loved leader, our white ribbon leader,
We greet you today with voice and with song.
We mourn with the nations the death of our
Frances,
America’s queen, though uncrowned until now;
Though God takes the laborers the work still ad-
vances,
As we to His will do submissively bow.
Manhood is nobler and womanhood truer,
E’en childhood is safer because of her life;
All hearts loved her fondly, all homage was due
her,
As nobly she led in our temperance strife.
CHORUS.
Our promoted comrade, our national chieftain,
Our beloved leader, advanced to a throne.
Oh, let us be doing the work of the Master,
Remember we ne’er ca[-] again pass this way;
We’ll follow our light, with no thought of [-]isaster,
Till we, too, shall reply to the roll call one day.
And there with the ransomed, the pure and the
holy,
With Christ and the angels forever to dwell,
We’ll greet Frances Willard and dear Mrs. Bailey,
And the white ribbon sisters we all love so well.
CHORUS.
Then let us, oh, let us be faithful my sisters,
And prove ourselves worthy of leaders so true.
JENNIE AMES PACKARD.
East Winthrop, May 3, 1898.
