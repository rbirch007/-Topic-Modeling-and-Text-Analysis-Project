					NELLIE BECROFT.


In Los Angeles, the Jewish Women’s
Foreign Relief Association, of which Mrs.
Bertha Hirsch Baruch is president, has
opened an attractive new settlement house
at 738 Turner St. It is named for Moses
Mendelssohn, the famous musician’s grand-
father, who brought the light of modern cul-
ture to the Ghetto Jews in the 18th century.
The house was opened with appropriate
ceremonies, and addresses from prominent
men, Christians and Jews.
