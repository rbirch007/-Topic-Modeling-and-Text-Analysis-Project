HONORS.
	On Wednesday evening, the 6th inst., a
reception was given in honor of Sisters
Martha A Critchlow, Nancy L. Burch and
Eveline C. Brown, the retiring presidency of
the Relief Society of the Ogden first ward.
	Sisters Critchlow and Burch have been
faithful workers in the Relief Society cause
for the past thirty years, and for twenty-
eight years held the positions of President
and First Counselor. Sister Brown acting
with them as Second Counselor for the last
eighteen years.
	By their earnest and constant labors of
love and sacrifice these sisters have en-
deared themselves to the hearts of the peo-
ple of this ward, and the present officers and
members of the organization made this a
fitting occasion for expressing the gratitude
and love felt for them.


WOMAN’S EXPONENT.


The program consisted of an eloquent ad-
dress of welcome read by Assistant Secre-
tary Lillian Belnap, remarks by Bishop D.
H. Ensign, Jane S. Richards, Stake Presi-
dent of Relief Society, and President C. F.
Middleton of the Presidency, interspersed
with musical numbers.
	One of the most interesting parts of the
program was the presentation to each of the
retiring officers of a beautiful rocking chair
from the Relief Society members. These
honored guests each made a response ex-
pressing their appreciation of the sentiment
which had prompted the tribute paid them
on this occasion.
	Dainty refreshments were served during
the time of sociability that followed, and all
departed feeling that the evening would be
long held in happy remembrance.
