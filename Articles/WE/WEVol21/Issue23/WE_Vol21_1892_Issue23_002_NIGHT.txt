[WomansExponent | Vol21 | 1892 | June | Issue: 23 | Title: NIGHT. | Author: Unknown]

NIGHT.

THE twilight hours linger not,
	As in the days past by;
But with seraphic wings are caught,
	To far off realms to fly.

Between the dark and waning light,
	No soft’ning shade is found,
Before the Goddess Night,
	In robes divine is crowned.

Athwart the canopy of blue, 
	Long e’er the day has fled,
Glimmering stars of silver hue,
	Fairy-like glances shed.

And even the balmiest sigh, 
	Breathed by the zephyrs low,
Awaken in harmony nigh,
	Quietude to o’erthrow.

Far off in the ling’ring daylight,
	That seems to fainter grow,
The silv’ry sheen of the starlight,
	Is changed to golden glow.

Heaven a silent watch doth keep,
	Over the slumb’ring world;
In whose ebony vault so deep,
	Meteor lights are hurled.

Faint the gleam of a distant light,
	That breaks the darkened way,
Fair Luna, crowned with sceptre bright,
	Begins her supreme sway.

Pale Moon, resplendent Queen of Night,
	Sail on thy heavenly way;
To thine astral home where sunlight
	Ne’er sheds its shining ray.

Oh Night, sublime and beautiful,
	In mystic silence wrapt;
With awe replete, yet merciful, 
	In thine arms to be clasped. 

Peacefully rest thy weary wings,
	In calm security;
Safely, as by thee are all things
	Locked in obscurity.
					HYACINTH.
