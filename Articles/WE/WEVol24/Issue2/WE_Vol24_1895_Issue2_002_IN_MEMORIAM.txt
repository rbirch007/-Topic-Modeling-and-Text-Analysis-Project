IN MEMORIAM.

[Emily-Thornton Charles, of the Woman’s Na-
tional Press Association, died April 25, 1895.]
	One of the rare, sweet poets,
		Whose genius the pulses thrill,
	Whose inner life, with beauty rife
		Illumines the portals still.

	One of the strong, brave poets, 
		Whose soul with its love aflame 	
	With loyal youth, crowned regal truth
		In her country’s deathless fame.” 

	One of the loving poets, 
		Who the tender heart could reach
	Through rhythmic thought, that solace
			brought
		Beyond the power of speech,

	We yield our homage to thee, 
		We garland thy memory o’er,
	And loose thy hand, for angel band
		To clasp on the farther shore.
			L. A. Crandell, W. N. P. A.
Washington, D. C.					Ex.
