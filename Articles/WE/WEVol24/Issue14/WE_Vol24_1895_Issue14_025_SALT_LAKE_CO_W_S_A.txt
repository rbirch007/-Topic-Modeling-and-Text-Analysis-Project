SALT LAKE CO. W. S. A.

	MINUTES of Suffrage meeting held Dec.
17th, 1895, in the Fourteenth Ward hall,
President Groesbeck in the chair Song,
"O Come, Come Away." Prayer by
Elizabeth Price. Song, "Hold the Fort."
Minutes of last meeting read and ap-
proved. President Groesbeck read the
Constitution of the W. S. A. of Utah.
	Elizabeth Price spoke of women being
backward in going to the primaries and do-
ing their duty as suffragists, in spite of all
obstacles.
	Margaret A. Caine said now is the time
to work diligently, and encourage our citi-
zens, and teach them to understand their
rights that they may vote for good men to
fill offices and enforce the laws. Also spoke


in regard to membership dues and the
standing they give in regard to the W. S.
A. Convention in Washington.
	Elizabeth McFarlane made some very in-
structive and encouraging remarks, after
which Dr. E. B. Ferguson read a card in
reply to a telegram sent by the W. S. A. of
Utah to Mrs. Stanton on the anniversary of
her 80th birthday.
	Moved hat the list of names of members
be forwarded immediately and be sent with
the State dues to the National treasury.
	Janitor’s bill presented and ordered paid.
Executive committee instructed to prepare
a program for the next meeting.
	Meeting adjourned for one month.
