[WomansExponent | Vol38 | 1909 | September | Issue: 3 | Title: YELLOWSTONE STAKE. | Author: MARY E. BROWER,]

		YELLOWSTONE STAKE.
	The Yellowstone stake Relief Society
conference convened at St. Anthony, August
8, 1909, President Julia E. Miller presiding.
	There were present the Stake presidency,
several high counselors, Sisters Sarah J.
Cannon and Hattie B. Harker, of the Gener-
al Board, and Sisters Barnes and Baliff of
the Fremont Relief Society.
	The morning session commenced by sing-
ing. Prayer was offered by President Dan-
iel G. Miller. Four wards were reported as
being in good condition. President Julia
E. Miller reported the societies throughout
the stake as being in a thriving condi-
tion.
	A duet was sung by Sisters Amelia Barnes
and Katherine Flint.
	Sister Harker, of the General Board, spoke
to the sisters on the need of studying the
Gospel.
	Sister Sarah Barnes of Fremont Stake
spoke for a short time on forming char-
acter,
	After singing, benediction was pronounced
by Brother M. I. Kerr.
	Afternoon session commenced by singing.
Prayer was offered by President M. I.
Kerr.
	President Julia E. Miller welcomed the
sisters to the conference and reported the
officers as being strict observers of the word
of wisdom.
	A solo was sung by Sister Vilate Rigby.
	Sister Sarah J. Cannon, of the General
Board, spoke encouragingly to the teachers
and told what a noble work was theirs.
	Sister Harker spoke for a short time on
the different duties of Relief Society work-
ers.
	Solo by Sister Emily Baliff.
	Remarks were made by President David
G. Miller, Marion J. Kerr, and Joseph E.
Fogg,
	First Counselor Mary J. Miller gave a
short talk on the storing of grain.
	After singing, benediction was pronounced
by Bishop Arnold D. Miller, Sr.
				MARY E. BROWER,
						Stake Secretary.
