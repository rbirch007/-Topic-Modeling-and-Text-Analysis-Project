[WomansExponent | Vol38 | 1909 | February | Issue: 7 | Title: ARDS ON HER 87TH BIRTHDAY. | Author: Unknown]

	ARDS ON HER 87TH BIRTHDAY.

Dear heart, in this thine eventide, rest thee in
	Love’s sweet bower,
While friends draw near and weave for thee—
	chaplets for one brief hour, 
While hearts and tongues are all attuned to com-
	fort and to bless,
Until the scene breathes forth of Eden’s peace and
	happiness. 

'Tis only in mortality, speech fails us to reveal,
For angels it is said speak forth the love and
	cheer they feel,
Oft we our alabaster box pour not o’er those we
	love,
Till their Gethsemane is passed, their spirits fled
	above.

This human is; yet we, within the Gospel’s glori-
	ous light,
Oft catching glimpse and sound from yonder home
	so bright,
Would fain lisp forth our love, esteem and rever-
	ence for thee,
And at this milestone crown thee queen of
	heaven’s royalty.

Full well thou’st earned the laurels which thy
	friends to thee hath brought,
From rosy morn, through heightening sun, thy
	willing hands have wrought,
The waning of thine afternoon, no respite gave
	from care,
E’en twilight found thee at thy post, the Gospel’s
	work to share,

As child, as maiden, and as wife, as mother, sis-
	ter, friend,
In every need thy worth was proved thy life
	works all doth blend, 
In one sublime, harmonious and beautiful, grand
	whole,
That well bespeaks thee, as God planned, a noble,
	perfect soul.

Thy trials oft have been severe, thy pathway rug-
	ged, steep, 
Yet with unswerving step you marched, nor
	shrank when wavea were deep,
Thy record, penned by angel hand, awaits thee
	up above,
Where, clothed with immortality, thou’lt meet
	with those ye love.
Thy day is beautiful to view, so too thy evening
	hour,
And friends delight to bring thee flowers of love
	to deck thy bower. 

[Column 3]
And as they gather close around to greet, to cheer,
	to bless,
May this sweet season be to thee one of true hap-
	piness.
And as the twilight deepens and the shadows
	closer creep,
May the angels chant thee vespers while they their
	vigils keep,
And may thy king, thy priest, thy lord, await
	thee by the shore,
To lead thee to celestial joys with Christ forever-
	more.
January 31st, 1910.			NELLIE BECRAFT.
