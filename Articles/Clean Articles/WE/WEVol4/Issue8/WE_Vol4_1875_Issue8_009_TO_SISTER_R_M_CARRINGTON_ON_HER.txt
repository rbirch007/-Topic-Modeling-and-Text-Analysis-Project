[WomansExponent | Vol4 | 1875 | September | Issue: 8 | Title: TO SISTER R. M. CARRINGTON, ON HER | Author: MRS. G. A. ALDER]

TO SISTER R. M. CARRINGTON, ON HER
		DEPARTURE FOR EUROPE.
	[The following lines were inscribed as a tribute from the
Relief Society of the Seventeenth Ward at the presenta-
tion of a silver cup, at a Surprise Party given for Mrs.
Carrington.]
		Farewell, dear sister, but ere thou leav'st us,
			We would take thy parting hand,
		And wish thee health, and joy, and pleasure,
			In that far off, distant land.

		We have known thee many seasons,
			Many years have proved thy worth;
		And in faithful bonds of friendship
			May our days be passed on earth.

		As Friendship's offering we bring,
			The cup you now behold,
		And hope that all thy future life,
			May be bright silver, lined with gold.

		Farewell! and may the God of Heaven,
			Shield and save thee from all harm,
		And safely bring thee back to Zion,
			By his mighty, out-stretched arm.
									MRS. G. A. ALDER.
S. L. City, Aug. 25th, 1875.
