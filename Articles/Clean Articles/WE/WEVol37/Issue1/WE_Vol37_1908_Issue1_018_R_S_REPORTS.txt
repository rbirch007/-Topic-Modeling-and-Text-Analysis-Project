R. S. REPORTS.
SAN JUAN STAKE.
	San Juan Stake Relief Society Conference
was held at Monticello, Utah, May 16 and
17, 1908, and was very much blessed and
instructed through the visit of Sisters Harker
and Peters’ from head quarters. A general
stake conference was in session and the Re-
lief Society met at 4:30 p. m. both times.
President Mary N. Jones presiding.
	Four of the wards reported their organiza-
tions. There being live wards not represented.
	Sister Peters, a missionary in Salt Lake
Stake said “Let your light so shine that you
may gain respect for yourselves and lead
others to glorify the Lord. No greater mis-
sion is on earth than that of Motherhood.
I love to find daughters by the side of their
mothers; especially in household labors and
in Relief Society work.”


[Column 1]
	Sister Harriet B.Harker, who is a member
of the General Board, gave very timely in-
structions and expressed her pleasure to
meet with sisters of San Juan Stake. She
told the young mothers that the Society
needed their support and they in turn need-
ed the benefit of the Society. “Every
member is responsible for the condition of
the Society and the ward in general. Our
efforts are the source of our benefits. Of-
cers Meetings are a necessity.”
	Sister Harker also related a very practical
method of enlarging the Relief Society en-
rollment, which we hope to profit by.
				Eva L. Bayles,
					Secretary pro. term.
