THE PASSING OF THE CENTURY.

Toll, bells toll, toll, bells toll,
A century is passing away,
With its follies and woes, 
Its horrors and throes;
So swiftly ’tis passing,
Not peacefully passing.
But striving with bloodshed and sin.

Toll, toll, toll, sadly toll;
For its great contentions and wars,
For the heroes they’ve slain
Upon land and on main,
For women that are weeping,
Their lone vigil keeping—
For loved ones who never return.

Toll, toll, mournfully toll,
For the needless hunger and cold,
For those who have perished
Unloved and uncherished,
For those who were driven,
Though bravely they’d striven,
To sell their own bodies for bread.

Toll, toll, solemnly toll.
For the crime of the century dire,
For the sorrow and shame,
That shall ever remain;
For the cry that’s ascending—
Forever ascending
To the throne of the great I Am.

Toll, toll, remorsefully toll;
'Tis the blood of the martyrs so true—
Of prophets rejected,
Who were called and elected,
To emblazon the way,
With a luminous ray,
Direct from the courts above.
		*	*	*

Toll, toll, tenderly toll;
It is but a memory now:
Let the soft robe of charity,
That mantle of rarity.
Encircle the bier;
For do we not hear?
’Twas truly a wonderful age!

Toll, toll, cheerfully toll,
Good works in part may atone;
'Tis its own bereavement.
Though there be no retrievement.
The world has grown better,
Full many a fetter
Has been loosed in the century past

Toll, toll, joyously toll;
Real greatness will always remain—
For its wondrous advancement,
'Twas an age of enchantment;
Its way has been fraught,
With the marvels God wrought,
In sight of the children of men.

Toll, toll, toll, proudly toll;
As swift as the wind we go,
For the science and arts,
And the o’erflowing marts,
For the messages speeding,
Long distance unheeding,
For speech flashing over the wire.

[Column 2]
Toll, toll, wond’ringly toll!
For the electrical current unseen;
Which simply astounds us,
Its limits are boundless,
Its magical power
Is a magnificent dow’r,
To bequeathe to ages to come.

Toll, toll, gratefully toll!
For the age of liberty’s dawning,
Shall cast its bright beam
Adown Time’s running stream,
Until freedom shall reign, 
Over mountain and plain,
And the rivers join in the glad song.

Toll, toll, toll, loudly toll!
The world’s deliverance is nigh;
The angels of wrath,
Are sweeping the path,
The scourges and wonders.
The tempests and thunders
Are proclaiming “Prepare ye the way."

Toll, toll, toll yet again!
For who shall His coming abide?
In His glory resplendent,
With a host of attendants, 
He is coming again.
O’er the whole earth to reign,
And to Him shall all nations bow.
