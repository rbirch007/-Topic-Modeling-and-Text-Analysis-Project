"THE HEART KNOWETH ITS OWN
			BITTERNESS."
	BY WM. H. S. ATKINSON.
			(Proverbs xiv. 10.)
Aye, the heart knoweth—every pang of sadness,
	Each weight of anguish that we have to bear;
Knoweth the tear which drowns the smile of gladness,
	The crushing sorrow and the anxious care.

Knoweth the heavy burdens that beset us,
	The hopes that back return with crushing force,
The disappointments keen that daily fret us,
	Each sense of shame and feeling of remorse.

Knoweth full well the aching desolation,
	Which comes to us when last good-byes are said,
The failure of each tendered consolation
	To soothe us, as we kneel beside our dead.

Knoweth what hurts us more than death or sorrow,
	The wounded pride which comes with sore defeat,
The yearnings weak that die with each to-morrow,
	The rude awakenings from illusions sweet.

Aye, the heart knoweth—knoweth and remembers,
	As loves of other days become outgrown,
As we stand o'er old friendship's whitening embers—
	Each heart must bear its bitterness alone.
