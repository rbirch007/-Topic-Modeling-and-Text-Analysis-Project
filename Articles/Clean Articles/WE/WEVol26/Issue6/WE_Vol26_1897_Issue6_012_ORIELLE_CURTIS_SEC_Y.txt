		ORIELLE CURTIS, Sec’y.


IN Chicago the Civil Service Commis-
sion on July 29, posted a list of 221 per-
sons eligible for ward and street inspectors,
as a result of the recent examination.
Commissioner Winston pointed out with
pride that the candidate who got the best
mark, and was almost perfect in her ex-
amination, was a woman. This was Mrs.
A. Emmogene Paul, who was for three
years sanitary inspector of the Civic Feder-
ation.
