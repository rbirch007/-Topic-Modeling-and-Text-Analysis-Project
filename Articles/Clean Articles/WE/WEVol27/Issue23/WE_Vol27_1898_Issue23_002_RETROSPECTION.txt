RETROSPECTION.

I strolled amid the quiet charm,
Of the twilight’s placid calm;
I heard the cricket’s gruesome cry,
And the katydid’s reply;
Mine eyes beheld the sun’s last ray,
Steal athwart the lucid bay,
And kiss the sailboat’s soft, white face,
And the clinging mists efface. 

My footsteps lingered near the wall,
Over-run with rosevines tall;—
Where trellis’ bent beneath the gift,
That accrued from nature’s thrift.
Sweet flowers raised their dewy heads,
And rare fragrance round me spread;
Fair Summer’s charms were everywhere,
Nor knew Winter’s grim despair.

Withal my thoughts would wander back,
Tread again the self-same track,
That led me to the fountain clear,
Whose drops filled my heart with cheer,
And robbed the past of all its pain,
Planting hope where it had lain,
Crushing the germ of discontent,
By the magic aid they lent.

Crystal drops whose phantom power,
Spanned the depth of that short hour;
And undenied, unknown, unseen,
Opened wide a mystic screen,
And gathered to each limpid breast,
The thorn that ’gainst my heart had
   pressed,
Leaving only to mark the place,
The scar they could not efface.
					HYACINTH.
March 30 1899.
