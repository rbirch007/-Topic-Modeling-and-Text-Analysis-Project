R. K. THOMAS


Mrs. Mariana W. Chapman, President of
the New York State W. S. A., writes:
“Please say in the Journal that the thanks
of every local suffrage association in the
State of New York should be sent to
Governor Theodore Roosevelt.” His address
is Hon. Theodore Roosevelt, Executive
Mansion, Albany, N. Y.—Ex.
	Governor Pingree of Michigan made this
recommendation in his message to the
Legislature: “I earnestly recommend that
such legislation be enacted as may be
necessary, requiring the appointment of at
least one woman on the advisory board,
and of at least one woman physician upon
the staff of each State institution, charitable
or corrective, having women or girls as in-
mates.”
