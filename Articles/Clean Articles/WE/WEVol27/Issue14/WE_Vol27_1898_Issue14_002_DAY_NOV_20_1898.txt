DAY, NOV. 20, 1898.

’Tis not “too much” that reverence should be
	shown,
Where such integrity as thine is known,
Love, the “fulfilling of the law” is here.
That “perfect love” which “casteth out all fear.”
The love which faith and confidence inspire,
Which knows no guile and no unjust desire.
Lovers and sweethearts in the balmy days
Of young, sweet love, repeat each other’s prai[-]e;
And so will we; there is no higher theme
Than holy love, pure as an infant’s dream
Our “Sweetheart” of today has proven true
[-]hrough eighty years; in Kirtland and Nauvoo
She with the prophet and his people fared,
And all the trials of the Saints has shared.
So speak her worthiness whoever may,
To high a tribute none of us can pay.
Sweethearts and lovers, with rich thoughts sub-
	lime,
Oft set dear names to music or to rhyme;
And so will we in joyous measures tell
Of our great love for Mary Isabelle
’Tis not “too much,” that honor should be done,
To loyalty like thine, dear honored one
