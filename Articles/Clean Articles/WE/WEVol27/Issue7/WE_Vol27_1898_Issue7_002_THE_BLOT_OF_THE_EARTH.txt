[WomansExponent | Vol27 | 1898 | September | Issue: 7 | Title: THE BLOT OF THE EARTH. | Author: AUGUSTA JOYCE CROCHERON]

THE BLOT OF THE EARTH.

When the nation answered Spain
To her cry at last for peace,
There’s something more than the "Maine”
Or the Island’s blest release;
Not conquest, and not glory,
No matter how great they be—
There is another story
For thy Sword O, Liberty!

Where the tropic heat down pours,
And the sea sends up its moan
Forever against the shores
Grim tow’ring cliffs of stone,
’Neath the Fortress guns and walls
And the flag that proudly waves—
Hear! the human cry that calls
From countless living graves!

For the hearts that dared to beat
With the dreams of liberty,
For the lips that dared repeat
The story of the free,
For the hand that dared to raise
The banner that led men on—
Broke the tyrant’s wrath ablaze
And hope—and life—were gone!

Give tears for the famine-slain
And those who fighting fell;
Love’s eyes pitied their pain
And gave them love’s farewell;
The green earth ’neath their feet,
The wide sky over head—
O! death like that were sweet
And restful in their bed. 

But—walled alive in the rock
And bound erect with chains, 
While hate their doom doth mock
As long as life remains;
Then the hands reach up no more
For the water and the bread—
Forever is sea'ed the door
Above the Patriot dead.

For this—did the mothers bear
Fair daughters and brave sons?
O, Freedom! wheresoe’er
Old Spain’s long record runs,
The soul that dared adore
Visions of Liberty,
Hath won the hope, no more,
And died in shame for Thee!

Does America forget
The cruel Bastile’s fall,
And how brave LaFayette
From the broken, burning wall
Brought forth the great iron key,
Proud France’s blood stained one;
And sent it across the sea
To our great Washington?


Ah! may the Fortress wa[-]ls
	Of Ceuta be broken down,
While tyranny deeper falls 
	Till none shall bow to the crown.
May shackle and chain and cell
	Yield forth to Liberty,
And our children live to tell—
	America keeps the Key!
		AUGUSTA JOYCE CROCHERON.
Salt Lake City, August, 1898.
