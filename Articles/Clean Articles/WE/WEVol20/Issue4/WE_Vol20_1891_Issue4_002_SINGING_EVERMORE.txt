[WomansExponent | Vol20 | 1891 | August | Issue: 4 | Title: SINGING EVERMORE. | Author: AUGUST JOYCE CROCHERON]

SINGING EVERMORE.

Should I veil my face in passing
Thou would'st know me without sign,
Thou would'st know me still unchanged
As my own heart knoweth thine.
Though in solitude I dream on
Thou dost sit my soul beside,
Thou dost answer all my asking
O'er the distance dark and wide.

Other eyes may change their meaning
Wiser grown as years pass o'er,
But thine own rhyme with thy singing
Through the silence evermore.

All I strove to do, though failing—
Thou dost know the meaning's worth,
Waiting for the better future
To complete the hopes of earth.
Matters not though great or idle
Discords come and changes pass,
Time holds fresh as souls are deathless
What was valued in the past.

Often life may hush to silence,
All their praises done and o'er,
But thine own to me are singing
Through the silence evermore.
	AUGUST JOYCE CROCHERON.
August 9th, 1891.
