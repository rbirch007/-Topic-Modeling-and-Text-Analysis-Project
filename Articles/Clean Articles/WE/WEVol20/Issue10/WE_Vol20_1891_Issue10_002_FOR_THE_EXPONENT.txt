[WomansExponent | Vol20 | 1891 | December | Issue: 10 | Title: FOR THE EXPONENT: | Author: L. M. HEWLINGS]

FOR THE EXPONENT:
				MAN.

	What a strange medley being is Man! 
		What a mixture of good and of ill,
	And how wondrous the architect's plan 
		That hath formed such a creature of will, 
	What structure of our world can compare,
		To this perfect symmetrical form,
	There is naught of the earth, sea, or air;
		That hath so many powers combined!
	Mighty fleets of the sea he commands,
		E’en lightning, is held by his power,
	Under ocean, and over the land, 
		Flies his word on magical wire.
	He hath trained too a fiery steed,
		Who for power and strength hath no lack, 
	Like the wild winds it rushes in speed,
		Menacing to all foes on its track
	Thro’ tunnels, o’er mountains, subduing
		The weird wilderness places of earth;
	Thus turning its deserts to blooming,
		And their barrenness into new birth.
	’Neath ocean’s bed, from caverns and caves,
		Treasures are sought for: years clinging there,
	Out from many wrecked mariner's graves 
		Man’s skill hath brought rich pearls for the fair.
	Action, wisdom and glory are his,
		On achievements he builds his renown,
	Art, and science, he grapples with ease,
		And an immortal fame is his crown,
	But this being we designate man,
		With his manifold powers so great,
	With a genius so mighty and grand, 
		This structure, whom the Lord did create,
	Has deep passions, malignant, and dark,
		Base, grovelling, cruel and fierce,
	Buried oft in the depths of his heart.
		Where no arrow of justice can pierce.
	This great being, so God-like in form, 
		Is sometimes a tyrant and fiend,
	Speculating with cold selfish heart,
		In the blood of his fellows and kind.

	Most complicate wonderful man!
		Take heed to your works and your ways,
	For to answer to the wise Builder’s plan
		Ye must live to His glory and praise.
							L. M. HEWLINGS.
Atchison, Kan.
