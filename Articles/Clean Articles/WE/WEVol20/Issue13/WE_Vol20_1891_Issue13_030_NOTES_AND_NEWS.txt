NOTES AND NEWS.
	Rev. Amanda Deyo has been elected presi-
dent of the Lackawanna County (Pa.) Wo-
man Suffrage Association.
	The last number of the Australian Woman’s
Suffrage Journal reprints three of Mrs. Lillie
Devereux Blak'e’s “Suffrage Fables.”
	Montana’s World’s Fair Commission has
set aside $5,000 of the State’s appropriation of
$50,000 for the use of the women.
	At the last meeting of the Beneficent Society
of the New England Conservatory, Mrs. Rufus
S. Frost, one of the two members of the Board
of Lady Managers from Mass., spoke in behalf
of the work ot the Board.
	Mrs. Lillie Devereux Blake is to start early
in January on a tour of Southern meetings,
beginning in Maryland. Mrs. Blake was for
many years president of the New York State
Woman Suffrage Association. She is a well-
known woman, an able writer, and a ready
and experienced speaker, full mistress of her
subject, which she illustrates with a large fund
of interesting and touching stories, all of them
true.
	It is a curious fact that the total number of
men who voted last Tuesday for the Republi-
can candidate for Mayor of Boston (19,534)
was not equal in number to the women who
voted for school committee in Boston four
years ago (19,570) Had those women school
committee voters been enabled by their
victorious Republican allies in the Legislature
to vote for all city and town officers, the re-
sults of last year’s election and of this one
might have been very different.—Ex.
