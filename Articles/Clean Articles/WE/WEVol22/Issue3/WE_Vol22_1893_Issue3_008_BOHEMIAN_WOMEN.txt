BOHEMIAN WOMEN.

	We have had a very pleasant visit recent-
ly with Madame Josefa Humpal Zeman,
whom we had met at the Woman’s Con-
gress in Chicago. Madame Zeman is a
charming finely educated Bohemian. She
was accompanied in her visit here by two
others of her country-women, Miss Anna
Roesel and Miss Sleona Karla Marchova of
Prague, Miss Marchova represents the
Woman's Journal of Prague Bohe-
mia. She spoke in the Woman’s Con-
gress upon “The Position of Women in
Bohemia.” These women are traveling
across the continent unattended in fact came
from their home, Eastern Europe, across
the sea to America without a male guide a
fact which has not been believed by the
people of their country, but is now success-
fully proven.
	Madame Zeman is very bright and ex-
ceptionally gifted, she was a great favorite,
at the Woman’s Congress presided as
Honorary Foreign President at one of the
sessions in the Hall of Columbus and read a
paper on “Woman as a Social Leader,”
at the session where Madame Helena
Modjeska followed with hers upon
“Organized Development of Polish Wo-
men.” This was the same evening that the
Y. L. N. M. I. A. held their meeting,
therefore we did not hear these papers and
only saw newspaper comments.
	We shall have something more to say of
Madame Zeman hereafter when we finish
our “Noted Women of the Congress.”
Madame Zeman is a correspondent for two
Bohemian Journals Chicago Amerikan and
the Racine Slavic, and her aim is to estab-
lish a Slavonic Journal to be owned and
controlled by women.
