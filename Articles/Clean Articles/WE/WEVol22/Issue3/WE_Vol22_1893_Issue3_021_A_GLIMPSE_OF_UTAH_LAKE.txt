[WomansExponent | Vol22 | 1893 | August | Issue: 3 | Title: A GLIMPSE OF UTAH LAKE. | Author: Unknown]

A GLIMPSE OF UTAH LAKE.
A score of miles toward the west,
	The lake lies spread with sunset sheen,
A top the highest peak I rest,
	And count the hills that lie between
The valley and the mountain crest.

The changing hues of sunset die
	In misty twilight, dull, opaque;
The distant sea-gulls slowly fly,
	A crescent moon hangs o’er the lake, 
Low down a fading evening sky.
LAURA ST. VRAIN
