JUNE.

JUNE—loveliest of months,
	Fairest of all the year,
Music and garlands and laughter;
	Perhaps the gentlest tear;
You bring, in the world’s great cycle
	All treasures of true worth,
For the tears of heaven’s weeping
	Are the fountains of the earth.

Summer’s heart with all its gladness 
	Cannot earth’s completeness know,
’Till it mingles in the sadness
	With the winter’s spotless snow.
So life’s heart forever longing
	For earth’s brightness and sunshine
Must feel earth’s sombre shadows
	For its fulness most sublime.

And the songs and gentle soughing
	Of the birds and tender leaves
And the glowing of the flowers,
	And the humming of the bees,
And the children’s joyous laughter
	And the sighs and thoughts replete.
Of all the months, of all the year,
	Make June the most complete.
						A. W. C.
