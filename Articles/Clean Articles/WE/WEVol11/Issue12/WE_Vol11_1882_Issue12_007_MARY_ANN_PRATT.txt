			MARY ANN PRATT.


IT is a delightful help merely not to be
hindered.—Jean Ingelow.
THE sad consequence of defection in princi-
ple is corruption in practice.—Dickens
TO persevere in one's duty and to be silent,
is the first answer to calumny.—Washington.
AN ability and an opportunity to do good
ought to be considered as a call to do it.—
Cecil.
MRS. Livermore writes that "The newspapers
of Boston are greatly improved now that women
are employed as regular writers on the staff."
