THE MOUNTAINS.

BY MRS. VICTORIA ALEXANDRA STONE.
“THE mountains are growing old.”—Talmage.
Are the mountains old? Old, did you say?
	Aye, but when will they be stronger?
For they lift their heads as strong today
	As in the days when they were younger.
in pride they defy the lightning’s wrath,
	And the hoarsely bellowing thunder,
While they cleave a path for the tempest’s breath
	Which the storm has cleft asunder.

Old did you say? are they growing old?
	Aye, but when shall they be older ?
For time rolls on with its tale untold
	And the heart of man grows colder.
Oh, dear are the hills that our fathers trod
	Made dearer by song and story
But dearer by far are the hills of God,
	That shine ’neath the shine of glory.

Are the mountains old? If they are who cares?
	Since high in his cloud of thunder,
He sits; and the sword in his right hand bears
	That cleaves the rocks asunder.
Oh, rich is his love as the earth is broad.
	And the heart and the hand grow stronger,
To think we shall dwell on the hills of God,
	When time shall be no longer!
