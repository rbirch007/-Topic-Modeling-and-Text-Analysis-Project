[WomansExponent | Vol28 | 1899 | June | Issue: 1 | Title: THE HOUR. | Author: Unknown]

THE HOUR.

In every age there sounds the Watchman’s cry,
	Startling the dwellers in time’s narrow street
With tidings of events which Destiny
	Holds for the dawn to greet.

Now, in the silence of our listless night,
	Chasing with clarion-tone a countless brood
Of selfish dreams—the Voice proclaims the light
	Of man’s true Brotherhood.

This, not the cant-phrase of the Pharisee
	Nor empty word of all the warring creeds,
But watch-word of a Cause at last to be
	Clad in the garb of Deeds.

From countless armies whom the torch of war
	Enflames with brutish lust of strife and blood—
From hopeless women, who have seen the star
	Of empire rise through flood

Of staunchless tears; from fields whose furrows lie
	Sown with brave hearts, which, fired with com-
		mon zeal
For human right, laid every kindred tie
	At altar of that weal—

Has risen one strong prayer, and lo, the cry
	Has reached the hearts of earthly potentates;
When Might enthroned in priceless panoply,
	Behind his kingdom gates

Turns from the ancient law and puts his seal
	Of acquiescence on the gentler code,
Peace needs no mighter symbol of her weal
	There lies an open road


To that great goal to which the sage and seer
	Have prophesied through time—faintly in ages
		far—
The note was sounded, grew, dwindled again,
		and here
	Beats like full flood-tide at the bar.

In every air that broods, or breath that creeps
	From lip to lip—the word rings forth in power;
"Mankind are of one kin; a new faith leaps,
	Arise and meet the hour!"
