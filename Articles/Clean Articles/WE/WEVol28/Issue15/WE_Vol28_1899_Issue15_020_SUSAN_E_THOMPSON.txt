	SUSAN E. THOMPSON.


A National Council of Nurses has been
formed in Denmark, and has become affili-
ated with the International Council of
Nurses. The Danish nurses distinguished
themselves during the Greek war.
	MRS. HILDA MINER celebrated her one
hundredth birthday on December 29, at the
home of her daughter, Mrs. H. D. Miner,
in Fredonia, N. Y. She has seven grand-
children, nineteen great-grandchildren and
three great-great-grandchildren. She pos-
sesses a clear mind, good memory, excellent
health and a remarkably amiable and cheer-
ful disposition. Her friends think the pros-
pect bright for a continuance of her long
and useful life. There are two other per
sons in Chautauqua County who have lived
beyond their hundredth birthdays, Ambrose
R. Hanchett, of Mayville, who was a hun-
dred last November, and Mrs. Doty, of
Frewsburg, who is in her one hundredth
and fourth year.
