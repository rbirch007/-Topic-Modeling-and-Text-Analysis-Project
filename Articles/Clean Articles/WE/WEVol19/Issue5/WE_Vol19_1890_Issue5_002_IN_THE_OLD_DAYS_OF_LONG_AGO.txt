IN THE OLD DAYS OF LONG AGO.
BY LU.

In the old. old days of the long ago
	This world was a loom, it is said.
Wherein truth and error wrought together,
	Thro' the woof and the warp of its thread.
So firmly they wove that few could sever,
	Or fairly trace out the false from the true,
Only persistent, constant endeavor,
	Caught sight of the error, woven all through.
Then such beautiful things were woven in,
	Rich dazzling colors both of light and dark.
Glimmering all over its strife and din,
	That the deepest wisdom could scarcely mark
Where the truth came in; or error was placed.—
	Fashion and folly mad passion and hate,
Were wrought with truth in extravagant haste,
	Adown through the ages early and late.
Fearless and free without halt or delay,
	Backward and forward the swift shuttle flew,
Evil and good traveled along through the way,
	The woof and warp, held the false and the true.
