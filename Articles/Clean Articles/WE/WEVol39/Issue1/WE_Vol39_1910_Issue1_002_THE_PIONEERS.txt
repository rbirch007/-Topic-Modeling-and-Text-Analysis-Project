THE PIONEERS.
Oft in sermon, song and story, hear we of the Pio-
	neers;
How our sires won lasting glory writ in blood, in
	graves, and tears.
Exiles for the Truths’ sake were they, far from
	plenty’ peace and home
Leaving all for cause most worthy, in the wilder-
	ness to roam.

Days and weeks and months they traveled over
	hill and barren plain
Driving lean and hungry cattle toiling on in hope
	and pain
Trav’ling far in midnight hushes grass and water
	to obtain.
Alkali and scanty rushes, often all that they would
	gain. 

They were led by inspiration as the dreary plain
	they trod,
Western shores their destination—Where they
	hoped to worship God
Unmolested and protected by the mountain bul-
	warks grand,
When the soil so long neglected, they’d subdued
	with hardy hand. 

Yet these days were not all sad ones, for there
	often came a time
When their hearts were light and gladsome,
	fraught with feelings most sublime. 
For in converse oft they’d gather round the camp
	fires ruddy glow,
Singing songs in faith together, light of Heavens’
	smile to know. 

Hope was bright within their bosoms, they were
	searching for a land
Seeu in inspirations Vision, past the deserts burn-
	ing sand. 
Where the great dead sea was washing lonely
	shores with saline spray,
There the Gospel everlasting could send forth sal-
	vation’s ray.
