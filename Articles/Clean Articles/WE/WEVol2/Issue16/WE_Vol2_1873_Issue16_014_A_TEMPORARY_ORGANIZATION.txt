[WomansExponent | Vol2 | 1873 | January | Issue: 16 | Title: A TEMPORARY ORGANIZATION | Author: Unknown]

	A TEMPORARY ORGANIZATION
Was effected by calling Mr. Rockwood to
the chair, and electing Mr. R. L. Campbell
Clerk pro tem. A committee on credentials,
reported what gentlemen were entitled to
seats in the House; namely:
	Beaver and Piute Counties, John R. Mur-
dock.
	Box Elder County, Jonathan C. Wright.
	Cache and Rich Counties, Marriner W.
Merrill, John Nebeker.
	Davis and Morgan Counties, William R.
Smith, Willard G. Smith.
	Iron County, Silas S. Smith.
	Juab County, Joel Grover.
	Kane and Washington Counties, Lorenzo
W. Roundy.
	Millard County, Edward Partridge.
	Salt Lake County, Orson Pratt, John Tay-
lor, A. P. Rockwood, S. A. Mann, B. Young,
Jr., Joseph F. Smith.
	Sanpete and Sevier Counties, William
Morrison, George Peacock.
	Summit County, W. W. Cluff.
	Tooele County, John Rowberry.
	Utah County, Albert K. Thurber, William
B. Pace, George Halliday.
	Wasatch County, Abram Hatch.
	Weber County, Franklin D. Richards,
Lorin Farr.
	THE HON. SECRETARY BLACK
Administered the oath of office to the mem-
bers elect, after which a permanent organi-
zation was effected by elections being made
as follows:
	Speaker of the House, Hon. Orson Pratt.
	Chief Clerk, Robert L. Campbell.
	Assistant Clerk, Richard J. Taylor.
	Enrolling Clerk, Joan M. Campbell.
	Engrossing Clerk, E. W. East.
	Sergeant-at-arms, Milando Pratt,
	Door-keeper, Samuel H. B. Smith.
	Messenger, Albert C. Young.
	Watchman, John Smith.
				(TO US,
An interesting feature in the above organi-
zation, was the administering of the oath
of office—with others—to the enrolling clerk,
Miss Joan M. Campbell. We trust, and
doubt not, that she will honor the sacred
trust confided to her.)
		A COMMITTEE,
Consisting of Messrs Mann, Rockwood and
Farr, was appointed to wait upon his Excel-
lency, Governor Woods, and notify him that
the House was organized and ready for his
message, or any communication that he
might choose to offer.
		A LITTLE PARLEYING
On the subject of a Chaplain, (Congress hav-
ing annuled that office) and Zebedee Coltrin
was appointed, to be paid from the treasury
of the Territory.
			MR. MANN
Announced that the Committee appointed
had conferred with his Excellency, the Gov-
ernor, who would be pleased to communi-
cate his Message to the joint assembly, at 11
o'clock A. M., on Tuesday.
			A MESSAGE
Was sent to the Council, inviting its Hon.
Members to meet with the House on Tues-
day at the appointed hour, to receive the
Governor's Message.
			AN INVITATION
To attend the Salt Lake Theatre on Tuesday
evening was received by the Members of
the House. (One gentleman quietly insin-
uated that he did not care to go if all the
ladies were to be left out in the cold.)
