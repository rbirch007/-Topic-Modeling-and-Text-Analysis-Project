LONGING FOR HOLINESS.

O, for a holy faith, no terrors to discern,
	Oh for a clearer sight my path to trace,
Beyond all gloomy doubts of weakness or repining,
	Into the shining light of truth and grace.

Poor wearied soul, let not thy faith be shaken.
	Gaze up and onward toward that blissful clime,
Where all thy powers shall to new life awaken,
	No more to feel the chilling blight of time.

Dear Father draw to thee my lost affections back,
	Clear from mine eyes the mist that hides from view,—
Give the worn soul once more its pinions free
	To find the truer tife,—by grace renew.—

Touch and make pure and free the flame that knows not
		death,
	Bearing it up to heaven, love’s own abode,
O pity, and forgive all vain idolatry,
	And take from me I pray, this grievous load;

Chasten’d and school’d at last from worship vain I turn,
	To one whose voice alone can give me rest.
No earthly image in my heart shall sit enthron’d
	To mar my vision from the regions blest.
								L. M. HEWLINGS.
