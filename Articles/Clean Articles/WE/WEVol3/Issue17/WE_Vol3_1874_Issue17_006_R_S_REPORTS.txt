		R. S. REPORTS.
			 ——
 	WEST WEBER, January 10th, 1875.
DEAR EXPONENT:—
   I have thought many times I should like
to write a letter for your columns. I con-
sider our little paper truthfully a valuable
instructor, for I have received many good
things through reading it. I wish that all
my sisters could realize this as some of us
do, and the paper would have a larger cir-
culation. But we must do the best we can,
and always hope for the best. We have a
good Relief Society here composed of sixty-
six members. I am happy to say that the
sisters are now alive to their duties more
than ever. We have excellent meetings,
in which the spirit of the Lord is enjoyed.
We are continually desirous of adding our
mites towards building the Temple of the
Lord, for many of us begin to realize that
we are daily growing older instead of
younger; therefore we are anxious to see
the Temple finished, that the blessings
which have been promised may be fulfilled.
Our settlement has passed through many
hardships, but I think the cloud is passing
by. The President, Elder John L. Hart, of-
ten attends our meetings and encourages us
with his kind fatherly advice and words of
cheer. President Mrs. Mary Douglas is
very diligent in looking after the needy,
causing many to rejoice and gaining the
prayers and blessings of the poor.
   With many kind wishes, I am your sister
in the Gospel,				P. S. HART.
