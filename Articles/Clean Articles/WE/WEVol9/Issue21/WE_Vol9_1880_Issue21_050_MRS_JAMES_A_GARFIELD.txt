MRS.  JAMES  A. GARFIELD.

	The wife of the  twentieth President of  the
United  States  is a daughter  of  a  Maryland
farmer, Zebulon  Rudolph,  whose  uncle, tradi-
tion says, fought in the Revolution, and, going
to France, espoused the cause  Napoleon and
became Marshal  of  France,  the  gallant  and
unfortunate  Ney. * * *  She  is
fond of  housekeeping, affectionate, yet decided
in her rules of household government, and with-
al a well-balanced, sensible and interesting
woman.  She enters the White House with the
good wishes of the whole  country, and there is
no reason to doubt that she will make the Ex-
ecutive Mansion  a home  as well as a  palace,
and  inculcate in  the  private as well as the
public virtues which should adorn it.- Boston
Jaurnal.
