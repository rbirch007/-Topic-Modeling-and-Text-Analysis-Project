[WomansExponent | Vol35 | 1906 | March | Issue: 8 | Title: VOICES OF THE WIND. | Author: Unknown]

VOICES OF THE WIND.
Oh thou sighing, moaning breeze.
	Sweeping through the woody land,
Swaying grass, and graceful trees,
	Bending them with unseen hand;
Reaching e’en the ocean’s tide,
	Dashing billows mountain high,
Through the surge so deep and wide
	Waking strong convulsive sigh.

O’er the land thy might is seen,
	Desolation marks thy course,
Cities razed, and fields made clean,
	Swept from earth without remorse,
Now thy mournful monotone
	Wailing as some soul in pain,
Makes me feel a child alone
	Listening to thy solemn strain.

Whistling through the chimneys tall,
	Rattling doors, and creaking blinds,
Dashing madly ’gainst the wall, 
Oh remorseless fickle wind!
How you pierce my throbbing heart
	Wounding as with knife of steel,
Making all my pulses start
	When thy blasts I hear and feel.

In thy tempest-stricken tone
	Mournful voices greet my ears,
And my burthened spirit moans
	With the weight of by-gone years;
For my mother fond and dear
	Faded with the autumn days,
Falling with the leaves so sere
	’Neath the snow passed from our gaze.

Now while murmuring echoes ring
	’Round about my lonely bed,
Oh what memories they bring—
	Sweet beloved spirit fled!
Is there, can there ever be,
	Recompense for such a loss?
Wind again thou answerest me:
	“Where’s the crown without the cross?”
