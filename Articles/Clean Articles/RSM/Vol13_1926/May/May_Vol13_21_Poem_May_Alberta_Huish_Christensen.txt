[Vol13 | May 1926 | Sequence: 21 | Category: Poem | Title: May]

Somehow I know that glorious May is near,
Each chirping note
From feathered throat—
The bursting trills of song
That I have yearned so long,
Make my heart leap.
I know that May is here.

Over the lake a white winged boat is sailing.
On ragged shore
Ice bound no more—
Young leaves of timid green,
My eager eyes have seen
In the cool twilight breezes blowing.

A riot of perfume tells me that May is near.
I hear the bees
In honeyed trees—
Drear winter's chill is gone;
Wild hearts break forth in song!
Spring's promise is complete, for May is here.
