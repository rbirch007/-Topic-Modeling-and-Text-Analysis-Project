Presidency and General Board of Relief Society announce, with deep regret, the resignation of Miss Alice Louise Reynolds as Editor of the Relief Society Magazine. Since February 7, 1923, she has held and brought honor and distinction to this position. Miss Reynolds, who is professor of English Literature at the Brigham Young University, feels that it is best to confine her labors to the University, and the General Board has, with reluctance, acquiesced in her decision. The Relief Society Magazine, under Miss Reynolds' supervision and guidance, has not only maintained its high standard, but has increased in interest, grown in popularity, and extended its influence. ISS REYNOLDS' contacts with universities and university life both in the United States and Europe, coupled with her extensive travel experience, have given a breadth of interest to her writing which could come in no other way, attracting the attention of a class of readers who have been interested in both the national and international tone of the Magazine. Then, too, she has always been interested in the achievements of women—this, added to her contacts with leaders of women's organizations, has been another source of special interest. Miss Reynolds was reared in a home with the companionship of parents deeply interested in religion—her father a writer of Latter-day Saint books—and she has been a teacher of theology in our Church schools the major portion of her life, all of which has helped her in a special way to edit a Church magazine. Miss Reynolds has the affection and respect of the General Board, and their interest and good wishes in her future welfare. Louise VY. Robison, Amy Brown Lyman, Julia A, Child, General Presidency.
