Prosperity: An Answer to Hope As a topic of the day, no one can doubt the popularity of the word "Prosperity." The word is on the tongues and in the minds of millions daily. Some dispute its existence. Others assert it. The word prosperity has an interesting fact in its history. Fundamentally, from its origin, the word means "an answer to hope." An effective popular definition of prosperity could perhaps be phrased: "Prosperity is that condition of economic affairs which the people earnestly hope will come to pass." This origin of the word prosperity is borne out by Webster's New International Dictionary, which traces the word to the Latin prefix "pro," meaning "according to," plus the first four letters of the verb "sperare," meaning to hope.
