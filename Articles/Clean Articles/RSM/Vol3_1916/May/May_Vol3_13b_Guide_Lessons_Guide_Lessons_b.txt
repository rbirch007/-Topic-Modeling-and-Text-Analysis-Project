LESSON II.

Work and Business.

SECOND WEEK IN FEBRUARY.

THE IDEAL WOMAN.

PROVERBS 31:10-31.

References: Prov. 31:10-31; Ruth 2:2-23; 1 Sam. 25:2-42; 2 Kings 4:8-37; Luke 10:38-42; Acts 16:14, 15; Rom. 16:1-16; Phil. 4:3.

The thirty-first chapter of Proverbs gives us a picture of the ideal woman. It is a picture drawn by an inspired pen, and is worthy of our most careful study. The woman here described is not a dreamy, impractical person, but one who is thoroughly alive to all the duties and responsibilities of life. She is industrious, prudent, kind, and wise. She looks well to the ways of her household, and eateth not the bread of idleness.

Let us study this ideal woman, and see what lessons we can learn from her example.

First, she is industrious. "She worketh willingly with her hands." "She riseth also while it is yet night, and giveth meat to her household." "She girdeth her loins with strength, and strengtheneth her arms." "She layeth her hands to the spindle, and her hands hold the distaff." Work is not a burden to her, but a joy. She finds pleasure in useful employment.

Second, she is prudent and wise in business matters. "She considereth a field, and buyeth it: with the fruit of her hands she planteth a vineyard." "She perceiveth that her merchandise is good." "She maketh fine linen, and selleth it; and delivereth girdles unto the merchant." She is not afraid of business responsibilities, but meets them wisely and successfully.

Third, she is kind and charitable. "She stretcheth out her hand to the poor; yea, she reacheth forth her hands to the needy." Her heart is full of sympathy for those who are less fortunate than herself, and she is always ready to help them.

Fourth, she is wise and prudent in her speech. "She openeth her mouth with wisdom; and in her tongue is the law of kindness." She does not speak hastily or unkindly, but her words are always helpful and encouraging.

Fifth, she is a good housekeeper. "She looketh well to the ways of her household, and eateth not the bread of idleness." Her home is well ordered and comfortable, and she takes pride in making it so.

Sixth, she is honored and respected by all who know her. "Her children arise up, and call her blessed; her husband also, and he praiseth her." "Give her of the fruit of her hands; and let her own works praise her in the gates."

This is indeed a beautiful picture of womanhood. It shows us what a woman may become when she consecrates all her powers to the service of God and humanity. Such a woman is a blessing to her family, to her community, and to the world.
