Hollyhocks! Your petaled faces lift So dreamily, as though with thoughts adrift, With smothered sighs, reciting as a class You tell of lonely years since lad and lass Once climbed and romped near you so merrily. Dear Hollyhocks! Perhaps some coming year, From halls of Art, with tender eagerness And men' ties stirred, they'll come and paint your dress Of quaintly ruffled edges, soft and sheer, And fill, once more, your days with bygone cheer. Oh that my brush could portray! But I fear That since I've heard your voices sweet and low, And noted well how daintily you grow, That I can never have the great success To place on canvas e'en your soft caress, Because to-Me You Are So Very Dear.
