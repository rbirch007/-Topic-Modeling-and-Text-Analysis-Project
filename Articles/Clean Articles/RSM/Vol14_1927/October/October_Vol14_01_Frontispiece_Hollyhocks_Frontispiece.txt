[Vol14 | October 1927 | Sequence: 01 | Category: Frontispiece | Title: Hollyhocks Frontispiece | Author: Elsie E. Barrett]

Dear Hollyhocks! I hear you call to me
With faintly fluted voices as I pass
The old deserted garden tall with grass
And weeds. I pause, with heartfelt sympathy,
Beneath the full blown, unpruned apple tree
That still holds nectar for the busy bee.
Dear Hollyhocks! Your petaled faces lift
So dreamily, as though with thoughts adrift,
With smothered sighs, reciting as a class
You tell of lonely years since lad and lass
Once climbed and romped near you so merrily.
Dear Hollyhocks! Perhaps some coming year,
From halls of Art, with tender eagerness
And memories stirred, they'll come and paint your dress
Of quaintly ruffled edges, soft and sheer,
And fill, once more, your days with bygone cheer.
Oh that my brush could portray! But I fear
That since I've heard your voices sweet and low,
And noted well how daintily you grow,
That I can never have the great success
To place on canvas e'en your soft caress,
Because to Me You Are So Very Dear.
