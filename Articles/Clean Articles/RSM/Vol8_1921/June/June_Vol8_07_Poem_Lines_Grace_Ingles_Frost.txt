LINES By Grace Ingles Frost. Interlacing boughs of Color, russet, gold and crimson hue; overhead like some rare jewel, Sky with limpid depths of blue. On a branch in wide-eyed wisdom, Sits the Sage of feather tribes, As if loath to leave for Pastures here the Warmth of Summer bides. All the joy of sight is wakened
By the picture that I view.
And unto the Master Artist,
My allegiance I renew.
