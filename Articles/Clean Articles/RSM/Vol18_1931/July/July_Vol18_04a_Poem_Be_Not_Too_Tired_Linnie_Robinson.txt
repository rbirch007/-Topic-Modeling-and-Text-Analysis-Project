RELIEF SOCIETY CONFERENCE

Be Not Too Tired, Mother
By Linnie Fisher Robinson
The day and its splendor of colors is gone,
Close veiled by the velvety darkness.
The winds of the night creep softly by,
Rustling the leaves 'neath the kildeer's cry,
Wihile the lamps of the hearth are lighted.
Wee little birds are hushed and abed
'Till morn and the sunshiny glory.
Mother's wee tots are all washed and fed,
Eagerly waiting the gift of a story
;
Be not too tired, Mother
!
What shall you tell them, though fed, they are hungry
;
Hungry for light, though day has far gone.
Each little soul's hungry for beauty,
Each little pulse for manly deeds done
;
Each child wants a wee bit of heaven.
Give them truths that eternally glow;
Let each look at life through a window
Unwarped and clean, for so they shall grow,
Crooked or straight by the strength of thy story
;
Be not too tired, Mother!
