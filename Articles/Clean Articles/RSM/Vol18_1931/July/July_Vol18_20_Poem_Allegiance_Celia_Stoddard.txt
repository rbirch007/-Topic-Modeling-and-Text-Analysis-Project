Allegiance
By Celia Stoddard
You have your beauty, desert,
Gray dune upon gray dune,
And
space
enough
to
think
wide
thoughts
Beneath the low red moon.
And there be those who love you,
And think your wastes more fine
Than green spires of tamarack
Or bronze shaft of pine,
Or
blue
lakes
shining
silver,
Or clean smell of wood.
And I could love you, deseret,
Save these are in my blood.
