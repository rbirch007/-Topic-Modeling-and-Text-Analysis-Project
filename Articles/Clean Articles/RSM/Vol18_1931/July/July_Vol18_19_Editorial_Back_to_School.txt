BACK TO SCHOOL

The vacation period is over and the children are back to school. This means that mothers must again adjust themselves to the school schedule and cooperate with teachers in the education of their children.

The Relief Society has always been interested in education and has encouraged mothers to take an active part in school affairs. We urge mothers to become acquainted with their children's teachers, to visit the schools, and to attend parent-teacher meetings.

The home and the school should work together for the best interests of the child. Neither can do its work effectively without the cooperation of the other. The teacher needs to know something of the home background of the child, and the mother needs to understand what the school is trying to accomplish.

Mothers should see that children are properly prepared for school—that they have adequate rest, proper food, and suitable clothing. They should also see that children develop good study habits and learn to assume responsibility for their school work.

The Relief Society stands ready to cooperate with schools in every way possible in the great work of education.
