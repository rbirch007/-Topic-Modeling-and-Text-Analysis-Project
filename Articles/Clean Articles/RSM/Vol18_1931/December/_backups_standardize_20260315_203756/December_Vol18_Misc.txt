Remember this: Each Ton of Utah Coal displaced in Salt Lake City deprives some Utah Man of One Day's Work.

Each Ton of Coal resulted from the labor of one man for one day. Either a miner, a railroad man, a retail distributor, or some allied tradesman.
Utah Coal in 1929 provided the equivalent of 300 days work for 12,500 men.
In 1931 it is different.
Do you wish this Utah Industry destroyed?
We believe you do not.
If we are right, Burn Coal.

A well-known vocational counselor has said: "When the employment situation is bad the trained man is the one who has a steady job. It is those doing the more unskilled routine work who are laid off. The routine man's work can be done by anyone and therefore can be split up and divided among the others. Also, when business is bad, there is less routine work to be done."
"Take as an example the work of the accountant in a large firm. Not even the routine bookkeepers are capable of handling it, because it requires technical training and knowledge and therefore cannot be divided among them. But accountants are difficult to get, because there are not enough of them to meet our present day business needs."
"Learning accountancy not only enables a man to take advantage of the numberless opportunities, to secure a better position, but it is also good insurance against losing your jobs."
