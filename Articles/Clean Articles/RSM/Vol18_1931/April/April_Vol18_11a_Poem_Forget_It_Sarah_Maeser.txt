[Vol18 | April 1931 | Sequence: 11 | Category: Poem | Title: Forget it | Author: Sarah C. Maeser]

Forget It By Sarah C. Maeser Is some trait of your neighbor's distasteful to you?

Forget it.

Is he getting more favors than you think his due?

Forget it.

You have your own questions and problems to solve, Your own goal to strive for, and plans to evolve.

Don't waste your time airing his faults, but resolve To forget it.

Are you hurt by some careless or slighting remark?

Forget it.

Does hate in your soul try to kindle a spark?

Forget it.

Are you prone over wrong and injustice to brood?

Turn quickly your thoughts to the lovely and good; And should aught that's impure ever seek to intrude, Forget it.

Let no evil passion encumber your life, Forget it.

Be it vanity, jealousy, anger, or strife, Forget it.

For life's well supplied with most beautiful things, And harmony sweet through each avenue rings, If we learn to pass by what e'er dissonance brings, And forget it.
