Social Service

Lesson 33. The Relief Society and Social Service

Objective: To show the relationship between the Relief Society and social service work.

References: Relief Society Magazine, Vol. XVII, No. 12, pp. 707-715; Vol. XVIII, No. 3, pp. 193-198.

Suggestions for Lesson Development:

1. What is social service work?

2. What relationship does the Relief Society bear to social service work?

3. What are some of the social service activities of the Relief Society?

4. How may Relief Society members prepare themselves for more efficient social service work?

5. What opportunities for social service work exist in your community?

Topics for Discussion:

1. The meaning and scope of social service work.

2. The Relief Society as a social service organization.

3. Specific social service activities of the Relief Society:
   a. Care of the poor and needy
   b. Visiting the sick
   c. Caring for mothers and babies
   d. Work among the aged
   e. Child welfare work
   f. Work with delinquent girls and women
   g. Cooperation with other social agencies

4. Preparation for social service work:
   a. Study and training
   b. Understanding of human nature
   c. Sympathy and tact
   d. Knowledge of community resources

5. Community opportunities for social service.

Questions for Review:

1. Define social service work.

2. In what ways is the Relief Society a social service organization?

3. Name five specific social service activities of the Relief Society.

4. What qualifications should a Relief Society worker have for effective social service work?

5. What social service opportunities exist in your community?
