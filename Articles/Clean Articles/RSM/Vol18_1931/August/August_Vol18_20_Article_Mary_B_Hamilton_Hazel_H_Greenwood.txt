MARY B. HAMILTON

Mary B. Hamilton, who died at her home in Salt Lake City, June 15, 1931, was born in Nauvoo, Illinois, February 22, 1843, the daughter of William and Mary Burt. She was the wife of John Hamilton and the mother of eight children.

Sister Hamilton was a faithful member of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints all her life. She was active in Relief Society work and served as a teacher in the organization for many years. She was also active in genealogical work and spent much time in research and temple work.

She was a woman of strong character and deep faith, beloved by all who knew her. Her life was one of service to others and devotion to the principles of the gospel. She will be greatly missed by her family and friends.

Sister Hamilton is survived by four sons and two daughters: William B. Hamilton, John B. Hamilton, George B. Hamilton, Charles B. Hamilton, Mrs. Mary H. Cannon, and Mrs. Sarah H. Young. She was preceded in death by her husband and two children.

Funeral services were held June 18, 1931, in the Eighteenth Ward chapel, with Bishop John M. Knight conducting. Interment was in the Salt Lake City Cemetery.
