TEACHERS' TOPIC FOR SEPTEMBER
OBSERVANCE OF QUARANTINE REGULATIONS
Disease germs are the most
fatal enemies of human life to-
day.
Quarantine rules are made
to prevent,
so
far as
possible,
the destruction of human life.
The second great commandment, "Thou
shall
love thy
neighbor
as
thyself"
certainly
forbids
conduct
that
endangers
the life of neighbors.
The command, "Thou shalt not kill"
is not
restricted to killing with the sword or other weapons of violence.
It applies equally to killing with disease germs.
It
is the moral and
religious duty of every one to take
all
precautions
against contracting
disease.
If however,
these pre-
cautions
fail the duty
is equally binding to prevent the spread of
disease
to
others.
Strict observance of
the quarantine
laws
is
meant to do
this.
All
cases
of
contagious
and
infectious
disease
should
be
reported promptly to the health officer in charge of the district,
and isolation of the patient immediately established.
In case of
doubt as to the cause of
illness, the family physician or health
officer
should
be
called
without
delay.
Delay may mean
in-
creased suffering and possible death, not to the patient alone, but
to many others,
