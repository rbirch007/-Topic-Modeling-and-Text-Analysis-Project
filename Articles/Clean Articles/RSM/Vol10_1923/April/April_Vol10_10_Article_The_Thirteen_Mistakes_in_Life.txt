THE THIRTEEN MISTAKES IN LIFE To attempt to set up your own standards of right or wrong. To try to measure the enjoyment of others by your own. To expect uniformity of opinions in this world. To fail to make allowances for inexperience. To endeavor to mould all dispositions alike. Not to yield to unimportant trifles. To look for perfection in our own actions. To worry ourselves and others about what cannot be remedied. Not to help everybody, wherever, however, and whenever we can. 10. To consider anything impossible that we cannot ourselves perform. 11. To believe only what our finite minds can grasp. | 12. Not to make allowances for the weaknesses of others. 13. To estimate by some outside quality when it is that within which makes the man. —Geyer's Stationer.
