Child By Mrs. Claire Stewart Boyer, Salt Lake City Awarded Second Prize
Dreamer — Questioner Teller of Tales, You stand before me, And I see Great visions in your eyes, Visions unknown but wise— You know eternity.
But Life as yet is just a parable To ponder on, Revealing, year by year, Man's blundering To God: And you, a stranger in a foreign land As yet can't understand.
But you can take the bits of wonderment Fast slipping from your once-celestial hold, And, blending them with earth's most transient lights, Great tales unfold—
Tales that make wise men Pause And rich men stare, And both turn to the cross again In Prayer.
