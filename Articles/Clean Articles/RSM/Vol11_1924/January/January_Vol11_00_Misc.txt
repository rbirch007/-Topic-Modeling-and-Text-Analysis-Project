SARAH AHLSTROM NELSON Mrs. REXBURG, IDAHO. 14 The Dreamer By Mrs. Sarah Ahlstrom Nelson _ Rexburg, Idaho, Awarded First Prize. I sat and dreamed,. Unmindful that the day was passing on, That humble duties waited to be done; My mind on pinnacles. Of castles fair, Ambition mounting on a golden stair. And, as I wandered in enchanted lands, I neither saw nor heeded baby hands_ Outstretched in sweet and dimpled loveliness, A coaxing invitation to caress. Distasteful cares of life I bade begone. Forgetting Duty's urgent call IT still dreamed on. I sat and dreamed Now listened to the sound of pattering feet, > Now childhood's laughter, innocent and sweet. The flower-decked scene appeared not to my view, Now fleecy clouds with mountains peeping through, The murmuring brook, the droning of the bee, " Earth's sweetest songs no music held for me. Ignoring Nature's wealth of beauties rare, Self-centered, grasping at elusive-air, Still gazing toward the distant horizon, turned my eyes from love and joy And still dreamed on. I sat and dreamed And longed for power to make my dreams come true. And while my eyes sought skies of endless blue, My life seemed to become a broken stair, With more of pain than I knew how to bear, Of hopes deferred that tried my inmost soul, Of yawning chasms 'twixt myself and goal.
Till, in despair, I roused myself at last To find the evening shadows deepening fast, And drowsy birds still chirping in their trees; All things seemed peaceful and at rest but me.
How could I know that since the morning's dawn Life's sweetest gifts had passed me by While I dreamed on?
MRS. PARLEY NELSON, REXBURG, IDAHO.
