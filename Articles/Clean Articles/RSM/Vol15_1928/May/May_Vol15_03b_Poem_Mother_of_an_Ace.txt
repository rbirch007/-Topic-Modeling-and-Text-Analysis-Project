Mother of an Ace By Linda S. Fletcher
Pain, the Inquisition Demon, Stretched her on a torturing rack, In a grim, gray place of horrors— Out surged consciousness, then back;
But she fought on, ne'er despairing, Claimed at Gates of Death, a toll— Back to Life returned, triumphant, With the boon she'd won—a soul!
In that mysterious, gray Unknown, Linked was her heart with a Son—her Own!
In the silver mists of heaven, Soul undaunted—courage high, Swooping hence and yon, an Eagle Went to conquer or to die.
Did she pause, afraid to follow To the Kingdom of the Air? No; asserting their high kinship, Soared she up to meet him there.
In the solitude of the pure, high Blue, Visions knit their souls anew!
