LESSON II Work and Business (Second Week in December)
TEACHERS' TOPIC FOR DECEMBER?THE HOME
IDEALS OF A LATTER-DAY SAINT HOME
Summary
"My son, hear the instructions of thy father, and forsake not the law of thy mother, for they shall be an ornament of grace unto thy head, and chains about thy neck; bind them continually upon thy heart, and tie them around thy neck."
"Youth is the period of discipline; and discipline, true and thorough, is a blessing that lasts beyond this life."
Character building is the most important and most satisfactory work a home can do. Habits and ideals formed in the home will influence the destiny of its members. How vital is the parental problem! It is within the possibilities of the home to be so conducted that it will impart a love of righteousness and justice to all, and the every day necessity of self control. The home should be a center of culture and refinement; here the value of faith and virtue should be implanted, and an inspiration to a strong purpose and a lofty aim.
The home should disseminate amongst its members a true
spirit of helpfulness, and a willingness to perform the duties of life for which God has given them power. Within the home should be the spirit of brotherly love, to be generously used in the affairs of life, both private and public. The Word of Wisdom should be the dominant note in the health program of the home. All should be taught to know the truth and act in accordance with its dictates. By example teach the value of industry and thrift, "The hand of the diligent maketh rich." Teach and uphold the sacredness of marriage and the evils of divorce. "Man is that he may have joy:" What greater joy than to be of value and service in the community. "God has given you yourselves, now what will you do with this gift.
