An Allegory
By Merling D. Clyde

For an all-wise plan the great Creator
Breathed into a fragrant bud a human soul,
Shrouded in its compass warmth and goodness
Closing 'round a heart of purest gold.

Through the years it grew—developed—
Shed its grace on others touching near,
As the petals soft unfolded in perfection
With a wealth of radiating cheer.

In a garden of love the flower—transplanted—
Matured, unblemished, in the sun,
Sending shoots to perpetuate its own creation
Till the Master Gardener said: "Thy work is done."

At its pinnacle of gracious beauty
Came the Reaper's gently stealing hand,
Plucked that upright rose from out its loving comrades—
A destiny fulfilled as God had planned.

Took the flower, but left its sacred sweetness
Lingering, hallowed, on the quiet air,
So that they who walk the pathways feel its presence,
Inviolable to all who loved it there.

Shall we say too soon the stem is broken—
Though we'll miss it from its place each lonely day—
Or, inspired to emulate its perfect blooming,
Feel that by its holy life we'll see the way?
