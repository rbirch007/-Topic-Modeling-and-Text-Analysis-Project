At the Dawning By Grace Ingles Frost In the gray shrouding mist of the morning, When roses hang heavy with dew, When quietly throbs heart of nature, My soul reaches out unto you. And in the deep silence unbroken, Save sounds there some bird's native song, I enter the Holy of Holies, Where hallowed memories throng. Within the vast walls of that temple, Unveiled for the moment I stand Face to face with You, God of my seeking, And know the firm clasp of your hand. And when dawn flashes over the hill's acres, Her quivering arrows of gold, I greet her, grown strong for the conflict, Of all that the day may yet hold.
