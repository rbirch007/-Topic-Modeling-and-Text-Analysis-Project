Latter-Day Saint Education to Build Faith Elder Mark E. Petersen Of the Council of the Twelve [Address Delivered at the Annual General Relief Society Conference, October 3, 1951.] T' is very important that we have an educational program in the Relief Society. So many women, especially those who do not come to Relief Society, get the idea that Relief Society is only for the relief of the poor; but I am grateful that we have an educational program in this organization, because, as the gospel teaches: Man shall not live by bread alone. The glory of God is intelligence. It is impossible for a man to be saved in ignorance, The Lord has commanded that we become perfect, even as our Father which is in heaven is perfect. If we are going to keep that commandment, we must, by all means, seek for wisdom, knowledge, and light, and apply that knowledge, light, and wisdom in. Building faith, and becoming like our Father in heaven. It was surely the intention of the Lord that we carry on an educational program in the Church. He made it clear in the revelations that he has given to us, and I would like to read a sentence or two from some of those revelations. This one particularly is well known to you. The Lord says: As all have not faith, seek ye diligently and teach one another
