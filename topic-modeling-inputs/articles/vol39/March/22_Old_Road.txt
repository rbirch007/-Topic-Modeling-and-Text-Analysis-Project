Old Road Vesta N. Lukei A mountain road, abandoned long ago To summer growth of fern and winter snow, To falling needles of the pine and fir, Becomes in loneliness the lovelier. The road belongs at last to all wild things— To butterflies and birds with sun-bright wings, To squirrels, silver-gray, with rhythmic tails, And lizards making sudden dusty trails, To tattered chipmunks, little mice, and fleet, Shy deer who disappear on nimble feet. Alone I walk in quietude, bestir No leaves, but still I am a trespasser.
