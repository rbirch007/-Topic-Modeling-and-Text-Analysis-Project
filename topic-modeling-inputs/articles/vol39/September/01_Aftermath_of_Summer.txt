Aftermath of Summer Katherine F. Larsen When waning summer's richness lingers, Down bright air Float sounds like sun the leaves are sifting— Everywhere The children's voices rising, falling, Interplay Like leaves in sun and shade; their laughter Light and fey. Now summer leaves are yellowing—falling— Down blue air Lie secret empty lanes still haunted Everywhere By wraiths of laughter.
