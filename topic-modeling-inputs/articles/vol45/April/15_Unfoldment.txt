Unfoldment Amy Viau Oh, evening, I would go again along Your silent, mellowed path, expectantly; Not of some certain thing, but of all things. And if there be The cool white splendoring from light of moon, I would attend and joyously discern The bordering beauty of my path, nor fret About the turn. And in some evening garden I would pause; For roses still bloom in such shadowed holds; A thousand birds chirp in their homing trees; And peace unfolds.
