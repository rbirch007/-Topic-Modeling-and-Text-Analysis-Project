Sung in October Dorothy ]. Roberts A moment comes to earth when time stands still As autumn shadows flow into one sea Of shade at sunfall, and the amber tree, Like a lamp diffused outside the window sill, Leaves not a stain of darkness on the lawn. And though no gilded shafts of sunlight rise, No obelisks of light from earth to skies, The creeping shapes of darkness, all, are gone. Peace moves like mist above the quiet land, No pattern pulses on the sunless floor, The globes seem painted on the sycamore, A task lies folded by the resting hand. A moment comes to all when life stands still As twilight pauses on the heart and hill. The Cover: Scene on the West Coast of South Island, New Zealand Photograph by Wendell B.
