As I Pass By Margery S. Stewart Let a hundred roses lift Their velvet richness to my eyes, But if my heart and mind are closed I will go blindly past the gift. Though a symphony is played So rapturously that men are swept Beyond the bounds of space and time, But my own spirit, cold, unswayed Refuses to unfold its wings, I have not heard, though ears go deaf With cymbal clash and viol cry. Though the greatest love earth knows Pours down in splendor from his house, And I, all loveless as a sieve, Can keep no droplet as it flows, How can it bless me or my ways? Oh, let me always be aware, My mind an ever open door, My heart safe harbor for his rays. Then nothing fair in flower or clef Shall be withheld as I pass by.
