Like a Kernel Vesta Nickerson Lukei A poem is like a kernel of wheat Which needs the fragrant soil, the heat Of sun, the timeliness of rains Before the planted seed attains Completion in the heavy head Of swaying grain, husk-bonneted. - A poem, wheat-like, grows with time Into perfection — rhythm, rhyme, And words slow-winnowed from the store Of wealth that is the poet's lore.
