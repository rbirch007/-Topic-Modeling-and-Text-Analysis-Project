THE TEMPLE IN NEW ZEALAND 493 Ted Cannon THE NEW ZEALAND TEMPLE AND SURROUNDING LANDSCAPE Tuhekaramea, Near Hamilton to take the tours through the temple. We then went back to Temple Hill, turned to look over the valley, and there we saw what had always been a two-way highway packed with automobiles three and four deep, all going in one direction. This was the sight as far as our eyes could see. The parking grounds were filled and people were standing in line several deep from the Bureau of Information, across the street to the corner, and to the back door of the temple. (We went in the back door and out of the front door. ) There was a guide for about every fifteen people at first, but on the days we had 10,000 visitors, or during the Easter week end when there were 30,000, there was a guide for about every fifty to one hundred visitors. HEN I saw this vast group of people not of our faith, flocking to the temple of their own free will and choice, it reminded me of the missionary davs of Brother Mendenhall in that same area. He said that he had stood on the street corners in that city and talked night after night and hardly a_ person would stop to hear his message, and now, today, our hearts were full. Guides were instructed not to discuss the gospel doctrines with their guests in the temple but let the spirit of their Heavenly Father talk to them, his children.
