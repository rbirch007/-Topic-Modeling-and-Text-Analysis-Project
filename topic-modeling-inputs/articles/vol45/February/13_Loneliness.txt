loneliness I see The loneliness of others. It may be They're sensing, too, within each silent heart The walls eternal, locking us apart. Their eyes, alleged windows to the soul, Remain opaque — conceal their truest goal. We share our tears, our happiness, our fears, And yet remain alone through endless years. Each treads a private world, sails his own sea. On courses separate, lone, eternally. But when I call to him from humble knee My loneliness is gone, he walks with me. Second Prize Story Annual Relief Society Short Story Contest
