Ethel Reynolds Smith "And it shall come to pass that those who die in me shall not taste of death, for it shall be sweet unto them." WE believe the death angel was a welcome messenger to Sister Ethel Reynolds Smith, for it released her from long suffering. Though she only tabernacled in the flesh tor forty-seven years, she lived long when judged by her accomplishments and her good deeds. She was the devoted wife of one of earth's noble men whom she loved and honored. She appreciated his worth and his loving care of her and their family. She mothered her husband's two little girls so successfully that people have said of her, "There never was a better stepmother." She gave birth to nine children and adopted one. As wife and mother she achieved her greatest success, After working on the Salt Lake Stake Relief Society Board, she served efficiently and well as a member of the General Board for fifteen years. She was ever ready to help and to do all in her power, and she often carried more than her share of the load. She was refined and had an innate love of the beautiful. Her home and her apparel show this artistic bent. May her children prove worthy of their heritage. May they live the Gospel of Christ in their daily lives so that their parents will find joy in their accomplishments.
