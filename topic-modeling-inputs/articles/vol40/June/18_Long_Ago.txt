Long Ago Wanda G. Nielson When I was young—once on a time— My thoughts all charmed me, like a rhyme, And all I heard and all I knew, Was sweet as song, and deep as blue. Each dear object in my home Stirred with meaning, like a poem; And outside, the trees and brook Told me stories, like a book. And the sky's smooth countenance Smiled upon my fairy-dance Across the lawn to garden brown, Where blossom-maidens floated down And pansies spoke my native tongue— Long ago—when I was young.
