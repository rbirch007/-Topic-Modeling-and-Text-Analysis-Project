Christensen Old-fashioned flowers keep love's memories Too close for comfort, if the heart would fling Its dreaming to the wind. Around them cling Forgotten words; a trysting shadow flees Evasively but leaves its fragrant breath. So do not near the budding four-o'clocks Or touch the larkspur bed; the stalwart phlox Know lovers' vows that should be kept till death. But
