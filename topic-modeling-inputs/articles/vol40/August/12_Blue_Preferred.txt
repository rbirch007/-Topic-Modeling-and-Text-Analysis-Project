Blue Preferred Dorothy ]. Roberts I must have blue to keep me company, Blue of gown, of ornament, and room, With blue-winged minstrels homesteading a tree In gardens, azure with delphinium. I love blue carpets that ascend a stair; The water-cool silhouettes where shade has lain; And ribbons twining through a girl's rich hair Like sky-tinted brooks threading brown terrain. Something in me cries the color's need— Knowing blue space the soul's first corridor, With dawn's deep indigo, its chariot;: Knowing when sapphire zeniths shall have freed The last noon metered to this earthly plot, Infinitude will open a wide, blue door.
