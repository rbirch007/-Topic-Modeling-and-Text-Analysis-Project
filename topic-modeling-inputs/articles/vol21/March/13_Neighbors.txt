Neighbors
By Merling D. Clyde
A neighbor is the greatest gift That God can mortal send, When others pass unheeding by On her we can depend.
When sadness comes she stands close by With words that comfort bring; And in the midst of happiness Her friendly laugh will ring.
Though Life's routine may chain us fast, The day grow dark and drear, Yet in the very darkest hour We feel her presence near.
There is no gift so great as this. As through life's hours we labor, The God-like counterpart of Love? The friend, who is our neighbor.
