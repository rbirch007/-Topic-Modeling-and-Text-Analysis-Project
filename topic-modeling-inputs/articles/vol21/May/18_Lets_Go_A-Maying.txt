Let Us Go A-Maying By Estelle Webb Thomas
This is vagrant weather Just we two—but ever, Long ago, you and me! Love—young love and laughter Kept us company!
Let us seek the laughter— We shall find it, dear, Love will follow after— Now that May is here!
Let us go a-Maying As we used to do, Through the meadows straying When the world was new.
Let us roam at will, Hand in hand together O'er the grassy hill.
Think not such quest tragic At this distant day, Seeking life's lost magic, Youth and love and May.
In our hearts we'll find it As we used to do, For we there enshrined it, When the world was new.
