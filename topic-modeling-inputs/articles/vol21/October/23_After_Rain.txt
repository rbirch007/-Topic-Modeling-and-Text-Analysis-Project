After Rain By Myrtle Janson If Heaven e'er comes to earth, It is after a rain— Air so pure, so fresh and redolent You breathe and breathe and breathe again; Gardens and lawns glorious as fairyland With the caroling of birds thrown in; Skies so delicately blue And fleeced with feathery white, Just like baby's crib Waiting for the celestial light Of little golden locks.
