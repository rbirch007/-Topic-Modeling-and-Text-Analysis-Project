THE END My Garden By Patience Nielsen Davis Within my heart where none can see, A lovely garden grows. I wonder if each friend would know The blossom she bestowed. A loving thought, a kindly deed, From friends along the way. Are in the garden of my heart, So when the weeds of discontent Attempt to hide their grace, Or winter winds of daily Care, Their beauty would deface, A sunny smile of some dear friend, Dispels their chilling gloom. And in the garden of my heart For weeds, there is no room.
