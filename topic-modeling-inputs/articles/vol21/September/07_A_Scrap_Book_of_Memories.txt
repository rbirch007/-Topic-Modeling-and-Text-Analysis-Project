A Scrap-Book of Memories By Estelle Webb Thomas who was obliged to lie in bed for many months, I asked a friend how she managed to while away the time. "Partly with my scrap-book," she answered,. And handed me a bulky volume from a bed-side table. I examined it with interest, the backs were of the usual scrap-book order, but for me, at least, the contents had unusual appeal. At the age of ten my friend had commenced to paste in scraps of her "dress-up" and party dresses with the date of the first wearing, and a description of how the dress was made. Sometimes there was a snapshot of herself in it, or she had drawn a little sketch and the change in styles alone, made the book interesting. If the dress was a "readymade" she had written in the data and put the scrap of cloth in after the dress was past wearing. As the book progressed there were significant items such as, "Wore this the first time I went out with Bob." Or, "Wore this at Alice's fifteenth birthday party." "Wore this to the Junior Prom. Went with Charlie." A peach-colored chiffon was "Bridesmaid's gown at Patsy's wedding." One dainty, flowered organdy had this honor, "Had this on when John proposed!' One graced the sad occasion, "When John told me goodbye before sailing for France." Since John never returned from France what a sentimental value those two scraps must have! Still, the scrap-book, like any other good book, has a happy ending, for a few pages farther on are scraps of lace and ivory satin and the legend, "My wedding dress!"
