The Sego Lily By Merling D. Clyde All Hail! The Sego Lily With its creamy cup flung bold, Enticing the winged insects To find its heart of gold. Down in the meadow, violets And modest cowslips grow; While wild pink roses fringe the lane, All fragrant in a row. But the sturdy sego 'lily On hot hillsides in. June, Just sets the bees to humming there In breezy, busy tune. Glad to carpet the barren plain, Its upright bloom to blow, Content to grace to rolling hills Where purple sages grow.
