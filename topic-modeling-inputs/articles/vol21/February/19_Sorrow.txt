Sorrow By Carrie Tanner
O grief, thou burden on the human heart! Oh would that at a word thou could'st depart! Like cloud that hides the warmth of sun's bright ray Thou art. But clouds forever cannot stay.
The darkest cloud oft brings the sudden rain; And thirsting flowers, grass, and tree, and grain Receive their need, and give in swift return Their beauties rich in garden, field and urn.
Thus shadows in our lives bring shock and fears, And clouds of darkness oft bring sudden tears. But who doth know that clouds in life are vain, And that beyond there's not resultant gain?
The tears, like rain, our dying flowers give Refreshing life, and nurtured faith doth live. And strength of heart through sorrow's pain endured.
