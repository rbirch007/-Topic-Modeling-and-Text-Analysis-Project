Crawford Now the heat of searing noon Is shimmered on the valley wide, And waves of warmth are drifted On summer's flowing tide; And one who seeks the quiet road For shadows dark and cool Shall find the boughs of willows Leaning on a pool; For oh, among the lovely things God's sun and rain have made, Loveliest are bended boughs And gracious willow shade.
