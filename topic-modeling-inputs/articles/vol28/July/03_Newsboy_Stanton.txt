NEWSBOY Gertrude Perry Stanton. _ Standing on a hot street corner Summer days, just right for swimming— Shivering on an icy pavement In the frost or fog of winter; Other boys with skates on shoulders On their way to pond or river, While he watches every passer Keenly, asking, "Paper, Mister?" Urging him "Read all about it!" Clarion call of "Last edition!" In a voice that, pure and vibrant, Well might lead a vested choir. Optimist when days are gloomy, Last to whine or be discouraged; Playing fair, and not encroaching On the boundaries of his fellows; Born of enterprise and progress, Here's a business man in making— Free America in action. "Paper? Here's your paper, Mister!"
