Washington and Lincoln
WHEN President Theodore Roosevelt on February 12, 1909, laid the cornerstone of the memorial hall near Hodgenville, Kentucky, in which are the remains of the log cabin in which Lincoln was born, he linked together the names of Washington and Lincoln in a memorable address, in which he said:
"There have been other men as great and other men as good; but in all the history of mankind there are no other two great men as good as these, no other two good men as great."
