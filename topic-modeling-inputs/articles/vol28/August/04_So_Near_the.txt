SO NEAR THE HEART Eva Willes Wangsgaard They lie so near the heart, these hours before The leaves are gone, when something seems to say, "The rose with its perfume will slip away. Then gather petals. Make a fragrant core For winter thoughts." I look beyond the door On children spending happiness in play Where crimson roses let no hint betray. That earth is calling back the wealth she bore. So every busy day is like a flower With petal moments rich in rare perfume That we may save against the lonely years. A heart can never know the zero hour If happy children once have filled each room, For echoes rise from laughter not from tears.
