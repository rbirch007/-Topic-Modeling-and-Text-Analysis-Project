FROM THESE Hortense Spencer Andersen These children that, though varied, still reflect Their current day almost without defect, And like a handglass held close by, reveal The fertile, careful genius which yet guides Their eager, lusty growth toward some ideal, Will surely change as life with time collides. The years that have it in their constant power To alter and modify from hour to hour, Let them not steal the precious youthful zest, Nor dull the gallantry, nor sweep away The carefree, buoyant laughter; no, nor wrest From these the trust that lights their way today.
