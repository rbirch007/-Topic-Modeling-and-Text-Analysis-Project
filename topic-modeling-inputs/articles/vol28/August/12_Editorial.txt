'They must not stand around EDITORIAL waiting for people to speak to them, or hurry away without giving anyone a chance to speak. 'They must find pleasant things to say and kind things to do. They must make a business of friendship—study its laws, watch for opportunities, invest, dare and expect success. Those who practice the laws of friendship are 541 rewarded with an envious concourse of friends." Through cooperative effort, the spirit of "gladly meeting, kindly greeting" should distinguish all Church gatherings. Everyone attending any of our meetings should feel as did that little Norwegian girl, that "everybody has adopted" him.
