forever the Fragile Lily Blanche Kendall McKey Josef Muench The epochs and the dynasties have passed away, And yet you are as fresh this Easter day As any lily that has held the morning dew. The proud procession of the years, The yearning hearts, the boisterous cheers, Are gone; and little in their shadowed splendor Is more fair than your recurrent rendezvous. So brief your hour and yet you live forevermore, With your perfume and your whiteness and your youth! I feel the cyclic rhythm of the truth That though you cannot stay, You will come again when skies are blue, For many, and yet many, an April day! Why should one mourn lost life, lost history, When you transcend death's solemn mystery?
