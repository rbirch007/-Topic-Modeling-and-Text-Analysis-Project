Almond Blossoms Annie Atkin Tanner Pink as shells thrown by rebellious waves On white and pebbled sands, Perfumed as spices from far eastern lands; Graceful as birds, singing as they fly, Then disappear in a sea-gull speckled sky, Fragile as blown-glass rainbows, Soft as soothing winds of May, Precious as memories that come Of home and friends of another day.
