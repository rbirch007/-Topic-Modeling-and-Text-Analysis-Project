Homecoming Leslie Savage Clark With what glad tenderness the heart Turns toward home to trace Each dear familiar landmark Of that beloved place. So, surely, when the spirit mounts Some vast celestial stair It, too, will find love's welcoming And homeland there. Gdyll Mloment Marie Call Webb To my side has come my love With all the blossoms his hand can hold; The last of the roses and cosmos, Snapdragons and manigold. To my side has come my love, Most carefully and slowly came, His flowers spilling from his hand. He touched my hand and said my name. It is not strange when lovers Bring to lovers flowers, And surely these are fitting To tell of love like ours. For often has my lover come With flowers as his gift for me— I am his wife of years and years, And my love is eighty-three.
