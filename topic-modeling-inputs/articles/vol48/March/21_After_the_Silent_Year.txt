After the Silent Year Mabel Jones Gabbott Today, we met, after the silent year, And took the same path, over the hill; We said, "Remember this," >? And "it was here.. There were memories enough to fill Fach shadow's length. We found the willow tree, And crossed the brook reminiscently. "The same," we said; and waited for such speech As often flowed, freely, deep and wide, Between us; waited — but no word could reach Beyond the shallow froth, the rushing tide Of inconsequential, over-washed debris. We found that we had only memory.
