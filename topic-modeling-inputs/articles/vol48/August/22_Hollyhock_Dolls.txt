Hollyhock Dolls Christie Lund Coles She came to the door just now, Wide-eyed and eager; fair As only a little girl Can ever be; her hair Color of sun, her eyes Part of the flower-blue sky; Her voice, tremulous, asking, "Would you care... Care to buy A hollyhock doll I made?" With reminiscent ache, I saw the ruffled dress, Like a doll I used to make. I reached for the clothespin stem. Beneath the pollened flower, I saw myself a child, and more, My daughter in a summer hour. "Yes, yes. I will buy the doll." She saw my tears, not guessing, I Had been given more this summer day Than a dozen coins could buy.
