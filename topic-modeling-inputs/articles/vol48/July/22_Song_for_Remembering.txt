Song for Remembering Evelyn Fjeldsted A whistled song and a symphony, Brought music on the evening breeze. The whistled measures, glad and free, Flew up and down in changing keys. Enchanted rhythm seemed to call; The leaves like symbols clapped and swayed, And as a calm fell over all, The little song began to fade. Strains of violins on moonlight rays, Came softly through the dark of night, And deeper tones sustained the praise To summer's interlude in flight. The symphony too soon was gone. In memory the song lives on.
