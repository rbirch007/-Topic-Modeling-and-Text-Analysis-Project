Again Tonight Christie Lund Coles Across the hills of Bethlehem, | seem to walk again tonight, Accompanied by staff and star And shepherds who had seen the light, Who followed humbly, questioning This star so luminous and new, So filled with radiance and hope, That I, as they, must find it true. | go with Wise Men richly gowned Who followed, too, and proudly came With gifts of rich magnificence, Marked with his holy, holy name. 958 | pause with them beside the stall, Beside the manger's fragrant hay, And see with wonder and surprise, The place the dream-eyed baby lay. | kneel, | tremble at the scene, | whisper humble, gentle prayers; | walk with those of long ago, My spirit tremulous as theirs. My worship is as awe-inspired, My reverence is as deeply wrought. Across a long two thousand years, | find the Savior earth has sought.
