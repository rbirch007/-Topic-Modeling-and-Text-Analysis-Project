i Stood on a Hilltop Vilate R. McAllister | stood on a hilltop with autumn, And noted how far the eye Could penetrate leafless landscapes, How reachable was the sky. The sun slanted in through windows Once darkened by summer shade, And | marveled at lengthening vistas The shortening days had made. My soul had a new horizon; In height and in breadth it grew, As | stood on a hilltop with autumn, And contemplated the view.
