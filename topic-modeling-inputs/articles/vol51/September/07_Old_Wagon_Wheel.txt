Old Wagon Wheel Jeanette Swanson A wheel encrusted in an ironwood tree! How came it here, as if a hasty hand Had cast it out? Did some catastrophe Wipe out a lonely, west-bound band? Perhaps the emigrants made camp one night Beside the wash, and warriors found them there Unguarded and alone.... Only the desert moonlight Witnessed the deed; heard the cries of despair. Perhaps they paused for noontide rest In dappled shade, and carelessly threw out This wheel we find embedded in the breast Of an ironwood, now grown old and stout. We only dream. No one now can reveal How came this alliance of tree and wheel.
