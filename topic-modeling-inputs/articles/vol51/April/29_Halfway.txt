Halfway Zara Sabin | stand at the summit of Halfway Hill The road has not seemed long. With laughter and tears | have met the years Or danced to a care-free song. | pause to look back at the green-clad hill — Its sunshine and utter peace throw The brightness of hope on the upward slope Of the way that | still must go. Though rocks and thorns beset the path That | sometimes wearily trod They will be as a wraith while | walk by faith Sustained by my trust in God.
