Earth Renewed
 Grace Barker Wilson | had forgotten how the earth is fair, The miracles of birds and butterflies, Till | saw wonder shining in her eyes, And shafts of sunlight making gold her hair. We gathered nuts and left them for the squirrels, We watched the wild canaries flash and dart From tree to tree. Again | felt a part Of mystery encompassing small girls. A bright leaf fallen from a maple tree, A fluffy cloud ship floating overhead, Were newly beautiful when sunset's red Reflected all her wonderment to me. Cara floated gently, unhurried, through the anesthetic until the gleam of the delivery room ceiling brought her back to reality. "That didn't take long," she thought with drowsy relief. "You have a little girl." The doctor's pleasant voice caught her up with a jerk. "Is she all right?" She gave weak voice to the question first on a mother's tongue. "Yes," the doctor's voice reassured her. She gave in to the lassitude that plagued her, floating gently to the haven of no pain. "But," he continued, pulling her rudely back, "she has something wrong with her stomach. A hernia, or something of that order." She forced herself to show interest. "Qh?" Hernia operations were quite common. She began to drift away again, but the doctor wouldn't let her. "Let's go to the nursery so you can see her." Clara felt the table move under her and watched 276 through pleasantly clouded vision as the ceiling changed from white to cool green.
