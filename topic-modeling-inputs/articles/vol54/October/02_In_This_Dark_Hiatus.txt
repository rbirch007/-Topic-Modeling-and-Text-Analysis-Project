IN THIS DARK HIATUS Alda L. Brown October climbs the hills that summer climbed, And mourns the echoed footfall on beaten trails. Bonfire laughter now has wafted to the skies, And sifted into winds where the lonely eagle sails. And | am steeped in solitude. Like the charred burn Upon these circled stones, | too have seen The sparkling flame of summer. | mourn small hands That picked wild jungle fern in mountain glen; lean Feet that waded icy stream for colored castle-stones. My loved ones... Now a thousand wingspan miles away, Not to return till forest glades have greened again.... And in this long hiatus that looms to that far day Autumn leaves fall with infinitesimal sound, And lie like teardrops on this endless-waiting ground.... Paul's Photos
