IN HIGH COUNTRY The brisk, rare Crystal air Of timberline is washed and dried, Polished, burnished till it stings Eyes and lungs, so close it brings Shimmering distant cliffs, so near You can see the pines' dark pride� Each cedar spire, each aspen clear. All gleams, lacquered gold, Till sudden thunderheads unfold Awesomely on a granite peak Where all the demons of storm will wreak Their savage furies. Yet as swift, Comes a rift. Once mote the prismed atmosphere, Where all glitters, and far is near, Claims its fortress homeland here Where soaring height Invites the might Of unleashed forces that harry and flail But cannot�in the end�prevail.
