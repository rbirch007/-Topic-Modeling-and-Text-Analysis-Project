AND NOW IT IS MAY And now it is May, the winter seeds awaken In riotous bloom after the long, long night; Under trees of apricot, the shaken Blossoms spread a carpet, petal-white. The breezes, dewy soft with April's showers, Whisper, ''The growing season has begun, now," they whisper, "'in these fragrant hours, Bring out your dreams; re-dream them one by one." May is the month of promise, of believing; All that was hoped for can become in May; See how the peonies' red points are cleaving Damp earth and mulch and musty leaves away; May is a month of surety, of knowing Life is an always becoming, and ever-growing. The name Karen Wilcox.
