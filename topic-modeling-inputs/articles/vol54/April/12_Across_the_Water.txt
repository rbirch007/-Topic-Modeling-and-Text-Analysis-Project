ACROSS THE WATER Peggy Tangren 1 knelt in meadow rue Where sunlight, broken by willow leaves, Fell as amber beads on water Earth-sweet and cloud-clean. Cupping my hands into a chalice Around its purity, 1 lifted it up. Velvet antlered, wet lipped, A deer raised his head. Looking into each other's eyes In mute communicatioin, ancient as life, We offered water In a primeval sharing.
