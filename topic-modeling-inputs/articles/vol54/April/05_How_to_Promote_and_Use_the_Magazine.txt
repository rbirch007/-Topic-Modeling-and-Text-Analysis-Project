How to Promote and Use the Magazine In the same October issue Elder Thomas. S. Monson of the Council of the Twelve, teaches us strength through obedience as only Elder Monson can teach it. And then there is the unusual, rich offering of so-called ''fiction." I say "so-called"? Because the truths in some fiction are more precise and more meaningful than some of the things we treat as fact. And they are told in the rich idiom of the well-told story. Now the lesson departments must surely be among the bestread features in the Magazine, and I only saved them until last because I feel that the reading of the Magazine should not stop with these lessons, and I know in many instances that it does. I suspect that many women just read the lessons, and they read nothing else in the Magazine. I was especially impressed with Elder Robert K. Thomas' cultural refinement lessons. This term was a new one to me, but seemed most appropriate. Here he cites what some of our greatest English authors have had to say about the strength of humility. But this is really absurd of me. Why should I tell you about your Magazine? I am sure everyone of you here has read it from cover to cover. I won't embarrass you by asking for a show of hands. Now each of you here today, and your counterparts in all the wards and branches of the Church, are much more than representatives. As Elder Ashton said last year, in essence, you carry to the world the only periodical on earth which delivers the restored gospel of the Master in a form especially written and edited for women. You are emissaries in the great cause, and a worthy work, and I pray that each of you here today may recognize the importance of this work and find your place in it and the best way in which to carry it forward. This is my prayer, in the name of Jesus Christ. Amen. A "a
