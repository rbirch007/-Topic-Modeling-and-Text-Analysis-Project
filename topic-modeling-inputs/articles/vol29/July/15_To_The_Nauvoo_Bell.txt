Mabel Jones Gabbott
Ring out, O Nauvoo Bell, ring out once more! Too long thou hast been cold and silent, hushed? As if the closing of Time's restless door Had locked thy sacred tales within, and crushed Thy soul that would ring out melodiously The story of a people great and good, Who dared to die for that which they believed.
Too long thou hast been stilled. What history Lies hidden in thy tones?misunderstood! How often thou hast pealed in joy, or grieved!
Tell of the ancient mold where thou wast wrought Before thou braved the fierce Atlantic's roar, When to the Nauvoo Temple thou wast brought Long years ago from England's distant shore; And rescued by the Saints from charred debris, By oxcart crossed the prairies vast and wide Unto the Valley, in the mountain tops.
