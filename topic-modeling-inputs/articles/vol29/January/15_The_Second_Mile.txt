THE SECOND MILE Gertrude Perry Stanton tight, Henry, and never let me go." The first mile was not easy, Lord. The man Who asked that I should bear him company To share his burden, speed him on his way, Dealt not in kindly speech or friendly smile, No vestige of appreciation showed. His churlish manner, habit of complaint, Made long the way. Although my tasks I left, He claimed the helpful service as his due; So when the mile was past, I almost turned, Relieved, to journey home. Then came the thought Of Thy great love and sacrifice for me; Freely I had received, and thus should give. Who gives to others only what he must, Gives not at all. So I went on again; And something sweet awakening in me stirred As if it were a song within my heart; The load no longer seemed a drudgery.
