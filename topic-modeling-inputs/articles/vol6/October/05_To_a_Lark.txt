To a Lark. Heard singing near support lines during artillery service, February, 1918. By Joseph Hunt Stanford. How can you sing? 'Tis true the skies are blue, And in the air there comes a breath of spring; But hurtling death shrieks o'er the head of you — Beneath — around you — till it seems your wing Must surely brush the flying shot and shell That screams across the fields you love so well. How can you sing? The sun is warm, I know, Beyond what is this February's dew; And song's your business, whereso'er you go, 'Tis nothing more than we expect of you. But — here's my point — by what astounding chance Came you to sing in this grim part of France?
