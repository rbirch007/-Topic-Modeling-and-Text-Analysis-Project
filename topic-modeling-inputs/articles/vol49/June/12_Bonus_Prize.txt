In all phases of the work of Relief Society during 1961 success has been realized in helping to strengthen the testimonies of its members, to notably increase its membership from both Latter-day Saint and non-Latter-day Saint women, to develop existing as well as potential leadership, to give service under the direction of the Priesthood on Church welfare projects, and to the needy, the bereaved, the sick and the aged, and to strengthen the women of the Church in their role as wives, mothers, and homemakers. Through intensive membership building programs, 16,973 new members were added to the ranks of Relief Society making a total membership of 231,175 at the end of 1961. These members are distributed throughout the world in fifty-one foreign lands and each of the fifty states in the United States. Included in these numbers are many new converts to the Church and women not yet baptized. A notable increase was also made in the average attendance at Relief Society meetings. Leadership development opportunities were provided to the 152,98] Relief Society members who served as leaders in the organization, an increase of 10,076, some of whom were women who had been inactive in the Church or were recent converts.
