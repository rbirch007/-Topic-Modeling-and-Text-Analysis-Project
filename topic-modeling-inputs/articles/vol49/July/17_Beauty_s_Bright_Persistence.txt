Beauty's Bright Persistence Maude Rubin Once the wagon hauled its freight— Oak chest and box and bed, Cornmeal and axe and candlelight— Toward a far homestead. In one small earthen jar, Where darkness hoarded strength, A lilac-cutting, with its leaves, Through the dusty length Of that tortuous desert trek Through sand and storm to home. Now by this chimney's crumbled wreck, Still spills its bloom.
