In speaking of the organizational meeting, the Prophet Joseph Smith wrote: We dismissed with the pleasing knowledge that we were now individually members of, and acknowledged of God, "The Church of Jesus Christ," organized in accordance with commandments and revelations given by Him to ourselves in these last days, as well as according to the order of the Church as recorded in the New Testament (DHC I:79). To Latter-day Saints today the growth of the Church seems phenomenal, yet to the Prophet Joseph and succeeding prophets of the Lord this growth was to be expected, for a vision of the destiny of the Church has been theirs. In 1843, the Prophet Joseph declared: Take Jacob Zundell and Frederick H. Moeser... And send them to Germany; and when you meet with an Arab, send him to Arabia; when you find an Italian, send him to Italy; and a Frenchman, to France; or an Indian that is suitable, send him among the Indians. Send them to the different places where they belong. Send somebody to Central America and to all Spanish America; and don't let a Page 256 single corner of the earth go without a mission (DHC V:368). From and even before the time of the organization of the Church, the gospel net began to draw in its own. Converts in this country and Canada and in the old countries, aflame with the spirit of gathering, sailed the oceans and rode the rivers; ox teams plodded and saints walked laboriously the one thousand miles of dry land to their resting place in the Rocky Mountains. Before his martyrdom the Prophet Joseph foretold that the saints would become a mighty people in the midst of the Rocky Mountains. Isaiah of ancient times had declared, And it shall come to pass in the last days, that the mountain of the Lord's house shall be established in the top of the mountains, and shall be exalted above the hills, and all nations shall flow unto it (Isaiah 2:2). The saints have become a mighty people in the midst of the Rocky Mountains, and the gathering continues but, still obedient to the voices of the prophets, the gathering of the saints in this day is in stakes, some far removed from Church headquarters, which are designated as places of gatherings for the saints, and where the greatest blessings of the sealing power have now been made available. The Savior is the head of the Church, and his mind and will are EDITORIAL revealed to the Church members through his chosen prophets. The members must have listening ears and accept the counsel, admonitions, and warnings of the present leaders and follow them implicitly. Joel spoke of the day when the Lord said, "T will pour out my spirit upon all flesh" (Joel 2:28). The wonderful progress taking place in the Church could be in fulfillment of that prophecy. As the Church spreads over the earth, so the organization of Relief Society spreads, that it may accomplish its divine purpose among the sisters and for the blessing of the Church members generally. 'The present prophet, President David O. McKay, has declared "Every Church member a missionary." If Relief Society is to keep pace in its designated place in the Church, then members will heed this injunction, 257 will be obedient to the Priesthood in their homes, teach and train their sons and daughters, as called, to go on missions, teach righteous principles and themselves lead exemplary lives. If every Relief Society member is a missionary — by precept when called and always by example — each one will then be participating in the blessings described in holy writ as "How beautiful upon the mountains are the feet of him that bringeth good tidings, that publisheth peace" (Isaiah 52:7). This is a glorious day in which to be permitted to live, and glorious accomplishments are expected of those who are living now. May Relief Society live up to its blessings and great opportunities in this day of phenomenal growth and expansion. —M. C. S.
