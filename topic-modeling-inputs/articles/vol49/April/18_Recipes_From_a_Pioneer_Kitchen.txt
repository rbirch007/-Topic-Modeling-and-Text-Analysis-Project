Recipes From a Pioneer Kitchen Anne McCall PICKLED RED CABBAGE 1 or more firm red cabbages, as: desired (shredded) vinegar to cover cabbage 2 tbsp. Black peppercorns (to each quart of vinegar) 2 tsp. Allspice (to each quart of vinegar.) salt Wash cabbage well. Put into earthenware vessel and sprinkle with salt. Cover and let stand two days. Add vinegar, black peppercorns, and allspice. Boil until well flavored. Cool and strain off remaining liquid. Place cabbage into wide-mouthed bottles and fill up with vinegar. Seal airtight. Leave for one month before using. It is the hardy one, and it has seeds that may grow tall and strong and straight, bringing beauty down the generations.
