Where I was born the grass was deep, The nights were cool, and the sea could keep The earth back with a cove and sand, Marking division of salt and land. Where I live now the plains are wide; They almost tuck the world inside. The roads stretch out and I can see One hundred miles in front of me. Where I live now, the world is high And mountains reach to split the sky. One world was neat and citcumspect, But here life is the architect.
