She tried to wipe her face surreptitiously, but Tomas thrust his handkerchief into her hand. "My bank tells me I am a very rich man now," he said, "and it is true that I have been able to buy many things but never tears that are shed in love." He rose abruptly and walked away down the beach. After awhile Joseph became impatient. "Somebody will eat our cookies. Let's go back." Tommy had taken his fins and gone home, but Tomas waited for them beside the beach towel. "It's time to go," Nina said. They worked together in silence, folding the beach towels, packing away the sun tan lotion and the cookies and thermos bottle. Nina took Joseph's hand, and the three of them plodded across the sand and climbed the palisade where the car was parked. She had wondered if 'Tomas would take Joseph now. But he helped Joseph into her battered car. Nina sighed with relief. She clambered quickly under the wheel, feeling her spirits rise. OMAS came around to her side and leaned on the car door. "When I was a child," he said, "my world collapsed. My father and my mother quarreled frightfully and often. I overheard my mother's cries of anguish. She blamed my father because they must sell so much of the land. I began to imagine things... Dreadful things... -Lhere was no one to assure me. 'The day came when I could endure it no longer.
