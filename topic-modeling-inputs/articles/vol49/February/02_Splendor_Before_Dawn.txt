Getting the washing equipment back was only a drop in the bucket. The landscaping, the costly draperies, and the furniture for the elegant rooms, and all the other expenditures would in all probability have the same consequences as the washing equipment. John was right. They had to learn the hard way. Phil did not make that much money. Mary looked back over the years. John's thrift and organization in his life was like a religion to him. As far back as she could remember, he had saved for a rainy day. Small wages, small savings, but the habit was there. He had always been-an opportunist, too, in a small way — food bargains, little deals that filled the locker for winter. There had always been plenty, always a sure way to meet the emergencies that came along. 'Thrifty, yet no one could say John was stingy. 'That winter when Mary's father had been hospitalized in the depression days, John had come to the rescue. The little bank account was wiped out completely, and he had started in again ungrudgingly. Yes, John had helped many people — the Andersons with the large family. He had instructed Mary then to supply them with food and clothing. He had supplied them with money, too, and they had gotten by until a job was in sight. And yet, what an adventure life had been. What fun they had building their own home, making additions, improving, renovating, and making the old pieces serve their purposes. The things that mattered — music, education, a vacation, new dresses for the girls, when an event was something special — all those things John stood behind. Life was lived leisurely and joyously. Mrs. Moss is a talented and enthusiastic writer. She is a member of the League of Utah Writers, a former member of the Barnacles, a writers club, and is a member of a well-known workshop group in Salt Lake City. She is the wife of Don W. Moss. They have three daughters: Joyce and Mary Sue who are teachers in the Granite District; and Mrs. Carol Donna Voss of Pomona, California. They have one grandchild. Mrs. Moss was born in Brooklyn, New York, and came with her parents at an early age to Logan, Utah. She received her education at Brigham Young College and Utah State University.
