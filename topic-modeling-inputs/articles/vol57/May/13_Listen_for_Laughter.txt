The city is man's world, but country life is of God.
(William Cowper)

The road curls westward through lyric hills
(The city's gray walls are behind me now.)
A gypsy wind catches a splinter of song
And toys with it under a willow bough.
There is a sudden turn in the ribbon of road,
And daisies crowd toward the lake's blue edge.
I have come back to my childhood home,
To the barley field, to the wild rose hedge.
I listen for laughter to lace the air
As an aspen does when the sun is high—
Are village boys frolicking on the green?
Are girls hanging dreams on the fence to dry?
Then loneliness gathers like a fog—
House windows are boarded, no longer fair!
The people have followed some silvered dreams;
Now silence is caught in the village square.
