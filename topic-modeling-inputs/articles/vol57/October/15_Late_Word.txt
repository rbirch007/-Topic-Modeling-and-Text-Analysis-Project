When the garnet and the gold of October yield 
Leave that final gray word of all dead fire, 
With sinking to ember in a red leaf caught in the stubble, 
When all twigs blur in a mist of mauve, 
I have seen birds leave earth for the glory of the sky, 
Where clouds gather the memory of all seasons, 
And swirl it into a rainbow turbulence over death, 
A vast hosanna of hues, a concert of color in the sky. 

Dorothy J. Roberts
