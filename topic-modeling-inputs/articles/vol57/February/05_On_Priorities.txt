Let me lean on your love tonight;
Tomorrow's crowded moments might
Find me frantic with the need
To frost small cakes with poppy seed
For eager Scouts, to soothe the cares
Of wee ones tripped by treacherous stairs.
Perhaps, awhile, a child again,
I'll stay my aging father when
His loneliness spills over....

All day long, whatever task or call,
A sister's sorrow, a daughter's tears,
A lesson written on the pioneers,
Whatever has priority—
Mother, daughter, wife—I'll be.
But I am a woman first, my dear,
Frayed with uncertainty and fear,
So let me lean on your love tonight;
Then tomorrow will be blessed and bright.
