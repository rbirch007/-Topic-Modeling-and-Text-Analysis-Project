Just give me a little sprig of green
That I may weave it in between
The twilight hour and the purple dark
Between layers of winter preceding the lark.
A philodendron cutting will do,
Its slender leaf spear reaching through
Step and sheath in an endless chain
Reaching, and leafing and reaching again.
I do not ask for blossoms of red
Or flowering pink in a window bed.
Leaf green is enough to urge me on
In faith through the shadows before the dawn.

Alice Brady Myers
