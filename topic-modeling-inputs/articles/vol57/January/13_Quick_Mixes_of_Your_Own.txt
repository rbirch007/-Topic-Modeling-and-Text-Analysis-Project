8 c. Sifted cake flour 
5 c. Sugar 
1-1/2 c. Double-acting baking powder 
4 tsp. Salt 
2 c. Shortening

Mix dry ingredients. Drop shortening by spoonfuls onto dry ingredients. Blend at low speed of electric mixer or cut in with pastry blender till consistency of corn meal, scraping bowl and beaters frequently. Makes 14 cups, enough for 4 two-layer cakes.

To store, keep in tightly covered container at room temperature. Do not refrigerate. Cake mix will keep 6 weeks in your cupboard; several months if stored in cool, dark place.

To measure: Do not sift cake mix. Spoon lightly into measuring cup; do not pack. Level with spatula.

Bake at 375° about 25 minutes. Cool in pans on rack 10 minutes then turn out on rack. SPICY APPLESAUCE CAKE
Use two 8" round pans. Line with plain paper (not wax), do not grease pans.
Have ingredients at room temperature.
3-2 c. Homemade cake mix 
1/2 tsp. Soda 
1 tsp. Cinnamon 
1/4 tsp. Cloves 
1/4 tsp. Allspice 
1/8 tsp. Nutmeg 
1 c. Canned applesauce 
2 eggs

Stir together cake mix, soda, and spices. Add slightly over half the applesauce. Beat 2 minutes at medium speed of electric mixer (or 300 strokes by hand). Add remaining applesauce and eggs. Beat 2 minutes more. Turn into prepared pans. Bake at 375° about 25 minutes. Cool in pans on rack 10 minutes, then turn out on rack.

Have ingredients at room temperature.
Heat oven to 350°.
Topping: Melt 3 tablespoons butter or substitute in 8" x 8" x 2" pan. Cover evenly with 1/4 c. (packed) brown sugar. Over sugar arrange 5 or 6 well-drained pineapple slices and maraschino cherries.

Make batter:
2-43 c. Homemade cake mix 
1/2 c. Milk 
1 tsp. Vanilla 
1 egg

Combine milk and vanilla; add half to cake mix. Beat 2 minutes at medium speed of electric mixer (or 300 strokes by hand). Add remaining milk and egg. Beat 2 minutes more. Pour over fruit in pan. Bake about 40 minutes. Cool in pan 3 or 4 minutes, then turn out on serving plate.

Follow recipe above, making these substitutions:
In topping, use canned peaches instead of pineapple.
