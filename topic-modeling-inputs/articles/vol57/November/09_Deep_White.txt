Oh, swift the passing of the golden hours.
And summer lies beneath a deep, deep white;
Only the trees stand motionless and bare
Naught else remains but what is out of sight.

Yet, here upon this northern mountain steep,
The magpies and the blue jays come for food;
With cedar waxwings in great clouds of grey,
And on each tiny twig their soft breast brood.

They find the apples that escape men's eyes,
All frozen now to feed each hungry bird;
They wake the landscape with their grace of flight,
And with flashing colors my heart is stirred.
