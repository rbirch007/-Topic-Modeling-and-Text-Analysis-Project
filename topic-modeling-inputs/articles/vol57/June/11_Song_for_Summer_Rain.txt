Oh, this is a lovely world the Lord has made; He must have loved us very, very much;
The summer rain has gently washed each blade
Of grass; the roses wear a subtle touch
Of dew today. The skies are gray,
But after summer showers nature hums
Hymns of praises for refreshing cool,
And bluer skies appear, a quiet comes
And my reflection in the wayside pool
Is brighter far Than a summer star,
Or than the summer sun | know is there.
Thanks, Lord, thanks very much that you should
Care this much for me To send the summer rain so blessedly.

—
