His Father's Keys

His father takes my little son On all the errands he must run—the two of them walk side by side.

My little son is filled with pride To be on every one of these, Entrusted with his father's keys. At first I longed to rescue them For purse or pocket once again. My mind could only visualize Their loss. But now I recognize The wisdom of his father's act, The existence of a simple fact: Many doors require keys; And faith and love are two of these.

Carolyn K. Dickson
