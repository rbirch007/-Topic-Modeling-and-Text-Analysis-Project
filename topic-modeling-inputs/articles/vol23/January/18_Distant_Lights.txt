DISTANT LIGHTS By Mary Hale Woolsey As night comes down vale, And drops a darkening robe of gray And hid from view way What matter that the way seem dark More safe and clear. From this far hill? This thought keep I, within my heart: That there will be Always, though dimmed by fog and storm, A light ahead for me. Here linger I, upon a hill, To cover vale and town. For these I watch: the friendly lights That one by one appear To make the travelers' homeward Last night, a storm swept o'er the All lights except the ones close by.
