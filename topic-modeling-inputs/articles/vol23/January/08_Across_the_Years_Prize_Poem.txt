Though long ago my eyes were dried, I hear your voice across the years And all the garner for my pride Is memory that time endears. When days are drab and nothing cheers, If you within my thoughts abide, I hear your voice across the years And peace folds down at even-tide. To hedge-rows, pheasants run and hide. In stubble fields, the winter nears.
