Dangerous Toys
PARENTS should be sure that the toys they purchase for their children are safe, and they should remember that it is desirable to give toys that will be educational and inspirational in their nature. The Wright Brothers played with balloons; Edison played with tools, and thus they developed themselves along lines that later made them famous. Guns are dangerous and should not be purchased. A measure was introduced to the League of Nations Disarmament Conference in 1932 that from 1935 on all countries prohibit the manufacture of war-like toys. This was killed in the committee, but would have been a most desirable measure. Merchants buy what the public demands. If this year these dangerous toys and those that are war inspiring rather than peace provoking are not bought, next year the merchants will not stock up with them.
