EDITORIALS 779 A Benefactor to Mankind DECEMBER 23rd is the 131st anniversary of the birth of the Prophet Joseph Smith. His work follows him in increasing power. The lives of thousands now dead, the lives of thousands now living, and the lives of countless thousands vet to tabernacle in the flesh have been and will be transformed through his ministry. His ministry like that of the Savior's was brief. He suffered a martyr's death when he was but 38 years old, yet in a few short years he was instrumental in restoring the Gospel with its priestly power, organizing a church that has challenged the attention of the world, translating the Book of Mormon, the Pearl of Great Price and giving to man the divine revelations and commandments embodied in the Doctrine and Covenants. He also organized the Relief Society for women, which traces its origin back farther than any other woman's organization now extant. Not only those who have accepted the restored Gospel have been the beneficiaries of his teachings. All over the civilized world the light of truth restored by him has shone, and people have basked in its radiance not realizing their debt to the American Prophet. And so as we think of his life, his teachings, his marvelous work, we thank the Father for sending this Prophet and inspiring him so abundantly in his ministry.
