Patriotism By Edith E. Anderson When you see that flag of beauty Watfted toward the vaulted sky Do you feel an urgent duty To uphold its standard high? Do its bars impel your valour To defend its purity? Do its stars inspire your heart-throbs E'er to beat in constancy? Think no word of pledged devotion Can alone prove loyalty. They who gave this glorious banner Earned their right to liberty. Would you emulate their courage And sustain your country's cause You can give your true allegiance By obedience to its laws.
