>———=1- ego MOTHER-FRIEND By Edna J. Gardiner God gave me a friend that I might see Something of His Divinity. A friend where grief and joy are one, To have, to share, till life is done. God gave me a friend so sweet and mild Who could open her arms to a little child; A friend to heed the age-old plea Let little cihidren come to me. God gave me a friend whose charm and grace Lent halo to the commonplace. A friend whose kindly thought and deed Was manna in my time of need. God gave me a friend in life's dark hour, A staff, with all sustaining power; A friend for whom my prayers ascend God make me worthy of my friend.
