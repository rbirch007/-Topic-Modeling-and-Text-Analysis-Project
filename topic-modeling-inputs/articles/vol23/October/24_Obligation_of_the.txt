OBLIGATION OF THE CHURCH
"THE present situation throughout the United States of America relative to the beverage alcohol problem constitutes a major challenge to the Church. It is probably safe to say that at no time in the history of the American temperance movement has the demand upon the Church in connection with that movement been more definitely linked with responsibility for the future of both the temperance reform and the Church itself.
If the Church is to influence society today as it has in the past, it must set itself against all forms of evil and must hold to its purpose regardless of any different attitude on the part of influential members in the pews or other influences. The task of the Church is the redemption of the world and the establishment of the kingdom of righteousness. It can take no middle course.?Ernest H. Cherrington in The American Issue.
