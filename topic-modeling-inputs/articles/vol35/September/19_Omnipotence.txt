esert Rain
Eva Willes Wangsgaard

A day like this engenders gratitude. You hear the rain plink-plank a great guitar; you watch it fall where growing ponds include each singing drop to make a silver star. This is a day for walking. You can feel the rain's smooth fingers on your hands and face, and earth's resilience underneath your heel, where grasses spilling silver interlace. You swing off down the pathway, and the rain hangs silver fringes round you everywhere. Each stream-filled gutter is a silver lane as you stride onward breathing silver air. You rise above the city's sordid crowd and pace the silver lining of a cloud.
