APACHE BASKET Irene Rayner Storey Crisscross went the weaving rush, While her eyes traveled far; Infolded were the swallow's flight, And the gleam of metal star. Interlaced with warm, red grass And quail tuft's woven brim, The primordial flush of dogwood And the spruce's evening hymn. In primitive grace and Indian art She coiled the flowing stream, In polished bits of abalone, The glacier lily's dream. In chippa twigs and cedar roots, In zigzag line and turn, The rain cloud and the thunder bird In strangest lore may bum. P
