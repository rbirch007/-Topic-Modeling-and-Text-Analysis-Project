REFACE TO: WINTER Jessie M. Robinson Thready weeds in buff and brown, Brittle, silvered grasses, Milkweed pods of fairy down; A crying blackbird passes. Meadows rimed with pearls of white, And oaten stubble trembling Like a wave of copper light; Honk of geese assembling. Sulphur sunset burning low Past boughs of late. November; Indian summer's warm red-gold Is now a graying ember.
