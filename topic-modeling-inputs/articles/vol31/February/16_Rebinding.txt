REBINDING Alice Morrey Bailey Let's take our book to the binder? Too many pages are lost; Some remaining are bitter, Others are stormy and tossed, It has been carelessly handled, Trampled with thoughtless feet, Left in the garden, neglected? Wide open to wind and sleet. We learned so late what was precious, The cover is old and torn; But we still have the day that you kissed me, And the day that our son was born.
