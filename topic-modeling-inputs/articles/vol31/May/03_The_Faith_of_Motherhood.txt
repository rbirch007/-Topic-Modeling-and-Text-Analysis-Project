THE FAITH OF MOTHERHOOD Ann Young Hughes Her window frames A golden star tonight, And valor wreaths a hero with its light. She marked the star That guided him from birth Through joyous years Of sweet disordered mirth, And does not cry Because he found a nobler way to die. Twin agonies of birth and death have swept All laughter from her lips, but she has kept The faith of motherhood, Because her son Competed for a golden star—and won.
