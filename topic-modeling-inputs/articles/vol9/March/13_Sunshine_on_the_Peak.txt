Sunshine on the Peak Nina B. McKean The storm brooded low o'er the valley, Enveloped my soul as a shroud: The city was covered with shadows, Close crept round my spirit the cloud. But e'en as the rain clouds grew blacker, Round my heart grew still thicker the pall, I lifted my eyes to the mountains, To the peak reaching highest of all. My heart leaped high with rejoicing, For there on the calm mountain sod, Was a patch of most radiant sunshine: And I called it a token from God. And ever in memory I see it, When my heart falls again in despond, That sunshine that lay on the mountain Brings a promise of light from beyond.
