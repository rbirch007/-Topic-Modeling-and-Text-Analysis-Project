Mother Mine By Eunice Jacobson Miles No matter, Mother, where you are beyond my human view, I'd give the best of all I have for a friendly talk with you. Long are the years since last I saw your face, aglow with cheer; My cup of joy would surely over-flow if you were near. I've journeyed far, oh Mother Mine, adown life's crowded street, But never have I chanced upon a face than yours more sweet. For careless word and calloused deed I yearn so to atone,? The Mother-heart I cherish,?now I've nestlings of my own. And some time when the close of day brings happy evening smiles, And time and change have had their way, and life no more beguiles, We'll meet again, oh Mother Mine, beyond the boundless blue, We'll live and love, and I shall have that longed-for talk with you.
