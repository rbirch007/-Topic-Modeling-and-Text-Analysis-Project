An Angel By Ann M. Bennion Just three score years ago and ten Her hair is turning silver now, An angel came to earth. Her eyes are fading, too. While of the highest rank, Yet her love and faith will ne'er grow Yet was she of lowly birth. Old? Such angels are but few. She came to make all things seem gay, Oh, I should like you all to know And make the earth more bright. There is not such another. She came to give us happiness, Now, do you know or could you guess, And give the earth more light. This angel is my mother?
