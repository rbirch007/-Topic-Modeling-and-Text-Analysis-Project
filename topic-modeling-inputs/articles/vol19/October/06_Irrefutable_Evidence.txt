'One group of very tiny creatures comprises the well known chickadee and its allies the bush-tit, the creeper, the nuthatches, and the kinglets. These birds save the Northern forests every winter. They do this by taking the insect eggs of caterpillars—eggs that the mother moth has cunningly hidden beneath bark scales on the underside of branches of forest and timbertrees. These eggs, invisible to our eyes, and wholly beyond our power to destroy, are of numbers inconceivable.
