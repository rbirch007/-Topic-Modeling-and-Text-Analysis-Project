A mission was established in Brazil by the First Presidency in FebTuary 1935, and Elder Rulon Howells of Salt Lake City was chosen as the president. On his arrival he established headquarters in the city of Sao Paulo. Prior to that time no missionary work had been carried on among the native Brazilian people. President Howells presided until , when he was succeeded by John A. Bowers; President Bowers was succeeded im by William W. Seegmiller; President Seegmiller was succeeded in by Harold M. Rex; President Rex was succeeded in by Rulon S. Howells; President Howells was succeeded in by Asael 'T'. Sorensen, who presides at the present time. In there were twenty-three branches of the Church in Brazil, with 1,103 members. Fifteen Relief Society organizations were reported in , with 143 members. Ida Lorene M. Sorensen is now president of the Brazilian Mission Relief Society.
