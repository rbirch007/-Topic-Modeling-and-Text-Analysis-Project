Prelude to Winter Miranda Snow Walton The purple grapes hang heavy on the vine, A pumpkin patch lies bursting with its load: The pasture fence is blue with columbine, And goldenrod grows tall beside the road. The whir of combine and of reaper calls Across the ripened fields, and sheaves of wheat Stand waiting, while the hazy sunlight falls Upon a land whose harvest is complete. But when the ground lies barren in the snow, And ghosts of roses only dream rebirth, When frost has seared the fields, and wet winds blow, What recompense can winter bring to earth? The answer comes with each succeeding day: The joy of work well done, the quiet peace Of softly falling snow, the hearth fire's ray, And faith which gives a saddened soul release. Yes, he who sows good seed, need have no fear When harvesting is done and winter near.
