Golden Windows Dorothy J. Roberts In new pride I polish from my window pane The wavy rivulets where rain has been. Once, far away, I watched a splendor come Upon my own as on some hill-wide home. And I recall the sheets of flaming gold Each sash, against the dark and distance, held. The final fingers of the sunset reached Across the valley till these panes were touched And rooms were filled with molten gold that fell, A burning atras, to the window sill. Rayed in rose and distance, mine was no less Than the greatest treasure behind a window-glass. I shine to luster every crystal square For though I may not see it, gold is there,
