November Day Beatrice K. Ekman A cold day, a gray day, Wind and rain and sleet; Sullen clouds that do not break, Shroud-mist on the wet, paved street. The rain drips from the shingle eaves In dreary monotone; With gusty force against the pane, A leafless branch is blown. The sodden leaves cling to the grass, Bare trees loom stark above... I kindle fire in my heart... In memory of my love. —_-
