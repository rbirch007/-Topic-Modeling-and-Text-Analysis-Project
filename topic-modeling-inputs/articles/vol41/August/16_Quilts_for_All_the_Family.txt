Quilts for All the Family Creresta M. Crark, Teton, IpAno, Makes QuiLTs FOR HER CHILDREN AND GRANDCHILDREN INETY years old in April, mother of thirteen children, grandmother to thirty-one, and great-grandmother to sixty-seven, Mrs. Clark keeps house for one of her sons, and still does beautiful quilting for the 'Teton Ward Relief Society. At the age of eighteen, two important events happened in Mrs. Clark's life—she was married to Hyrum James Clark, and in that same year she began her long carecr as a Relief Society visiting teacher. She has been a widow for twenty-six years, but her loneliness has been lessened by active interest in hobbies — quiltmaking and rugmaking. She has made and quilted a quilt for every one of her daughters and daughters-in-law, a quilt for every grandson, and a set of blocks for every granddaughter. The double wedding ring pattern is Mrs. Clark's favorite design, but she loves to make her own patterns and to experiment with color combinations and quilting patterns. Mrs. Clark has lived in Teton City, Idaho, since 1889, and in addition to her Relief Society activities and other Church work, she served for many years as a counselor in Primary, and has been a guide and an inspiration to three generations of people in the Teton country.
