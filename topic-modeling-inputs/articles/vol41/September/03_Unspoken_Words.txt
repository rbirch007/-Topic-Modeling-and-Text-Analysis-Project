Nelson FISHER TOWERS, UTAH Red Fills in Utah, Lydia Hall I must go unto these vermilion hills Often these days, and mark how bright they are When winter's sunset points their painted sills, I must note how the first pale summer star Makes silver magic here. And walk in spring Where sun-drenched dunes and rocks and knolls are gay With paintbrush; where white sego lilies cling And desert mallow marks a lovely way. I must look to these hills, for well I know The scenes of life may change relentlessly For me, that on some morrow I may go To where there are no cliffs that call to me; No well-loved place where I can walk apart And borrow sunshine for my hands and heart.
