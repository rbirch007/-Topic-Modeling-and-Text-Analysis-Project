Farm Shopping Iris W. Schow When we were little, on the first cool night, Our father sat beside a table spread With order blanks and pens, ink at his right; He was the scribe. Our mother clearly read Descriptions from the catalogue aloud: "Side pockets," "Placket closing in the back," "All of new wool," "The wearer can be proud," "Drawers ankle length, in choice of cream or black." Were these for me? Oh, joy—my brother's size! "'Wide-collared sweater, worsted, in deep rose.' The lamp was rubbed. Next week before our eyes The genie (Postman Jones) brought all they chose Straight to our farm. Outpourings of delight Held sway: "How pretty!" "Mine?" "Look, what finds!"' And the desire to learn to read and write Stirred wistfully in our unfolding minds. <_<
