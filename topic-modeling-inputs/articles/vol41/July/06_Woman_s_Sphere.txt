Woman's Sphere Ramona W. Cannon HE Woman's Home Companion has named as the six most successful women of 1953 the following: Mary Pillsbury Lord, America's delegate to the U. N. Commission on human rights; Dr. Helene Wallace Tolan, cancer research scientist; Mildred Carlson Ahlgren, President, General Federation of Women's Clubs; Dr. Frances Horwich, Ding Dong School teacher; Marianne Moore, poetess; Peggy Wood, actress. Each was given the Companion's Silver Distaff award. BEY ERLY JONES, eighteen-yearold, Ogden, Utah, high school girl, received national honors as the best cherry-pie maker in America. In contests supported locally by 4H clubs, Future Homemakers of America, and other youth organizations, more than fifty thousand girls competed.
