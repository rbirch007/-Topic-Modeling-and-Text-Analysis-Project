CHRISTMAS IS FOR HAPPINESS SYMBOLIZING that early time when three wise men, guided by a star, came from Chaldea and brought gifts of gold and frankincense and myrrh and laid them at the feet of the Babe born in the manger, we exchange gifts at Christmas time. Some of the most treasured gifts received are things you cannot hold in your hands. One of the richest gifts a chil can receive, is a love for good books, for reading. The Christmas season furnishes a worthwhile opportunity for the family, as a group, to go-over each member's favorite Christmas stories. A Christmas scrapbook, in which the best Christmas stories and poems clipped from magazines are pasted, can be added to and referred to each year with delight. Some of the most popular stories in our family have come from the pages of old, bound volumes of the Church magazines. Charles Dickens, Kate Douglas Wiggin, and Henry Van Dyke have given us some unforgettable Christmas scenes. But there is always one 823 story that is best on Christmas Eve, no matter how many times before or after Christmas we read it. That is the story of the birth of a Child who showed the way for Peace on Earth, and good will toward all men. The visit of Jesus Christ to the Nephites, as described in the Book of Mormon, also deserves a place on the Christmas reading list. Christmas is for happiness. All of our plans and activities are slated toward the greatest enjoyment for everyone. It is impossible to be happy if we let gift giving become a burden, both mentally and financially. It is a pity to undertake more shopping and partying than we are physi-. Cally able for, so that we become too exhausted to participate on Christmas day in the family pleasure. Christmas is for happiness. A deeper, sweeter Christmas is ours when we also include humble acknowledgment of God's greatest gift to mankind, His Son. ??
