AN OLD LADY PRAYS
By Thyrza R. Berrett

I always used to pray
Let me go first,
Nor leave me lonely
Mourning loved ones gone.

I am no longer selfish, Lord,
Take first, please, John.

He never seems to know
Where things are kept;
He can't find socks,
Nor handkerchiefs nor ties.
I read his paper to him nights
To save his eyes.

I always hurry home
From town or club;
John wants me there when he
Comes in at close of day.
He'd be so lost and lonely, Lord,
With me away.

No one else can make
The pies he likes.
I spoil him, sure,
It's been so very long
Since there was any one to please
But me and John.

So let me stay till he
Has gone before,
Lest lonely waiting
Be too hard for him to bear.
Then please to call me quickly, Lord.
He'll need me there.
