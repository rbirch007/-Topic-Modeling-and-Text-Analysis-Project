Acknowledgment
The office has received many communications from different sources expressing appreciation and commendation for the contributions of the Singing Mothers to our recent Conference. We acknowledge these with gratitude, gratitude for the talented and well trained women, gratitude for the thoughtfulness which has prompted so many kindly expressions. The following is typical of the letters received: "I cannot refrain from letting you know how I appreciate the marvelous contribution of your Singing Mothers to the recent Conference. I have never seen a more beautiful sight or heard finer music, and to all I extend my congratulations." RESPONSE By Mabel L. Gleason You're giving me praise today For reasons I know not why; For love I've shown my children For whom I'd gladly die. Don't think I've minded the long hours I've tended, watched and prayed; These are golden links in my memory, The only ones I'd hoped to have stayed. The days I've watched o'er the cradle The little ones lying there; Don't thank me for care I've given, Care I'd not have been willing to share. Now they've grown tall?these babies of mine Grown to manhood,. Womanhood too; And I long for the old days And those lost little jobs to do. I ask no praise for my life's work, Their love is all that I need; Just knowing they're filling their niche in life Is honor and praise indeed.
