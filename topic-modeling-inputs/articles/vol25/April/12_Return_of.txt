RETURN OF SPRING By Lottie Hammer Singley
My Robin friends came back again, As greying dawn burst into light They settled on my window sill And rested, weary from their flight.
Their rest was short; their aim was plain. They builded well. And at their ease, With perfect faith, through dripping rain Nested in my apple trees.
I, too, was weary from life's flight, Dark gripped my soul; no spring could be. Their fortitude, their lilting song Surrounded and enveloped me.
And through the message of a bird I see more plainly life, my part; I'll trust, rebuild, I'll try again; Springtime has come into my heart.
