If We Could See the End. Annie D. Palmer. Ah, me, if only we could know the way, The thing to do and the word to say. That would help our fellows and do the good That with all our hearts we wish we could! There's many a blunder and stinging wrong Would be kept from our lives as we plod along If we the end could but see. Sometimes a gift with keenest delight We prepare for a loved one with all our might, And instead of pleasure it gives unrest That is poorly hidden within the breast, And we never can look or think or care For the misplaced gift, for regret is there. Oh, the end is far to see. It may be a word of praise we give Hoping to help someone to live In a happier mood, and by some mischance Of occasion or wording or tone or glance Our meaning is lost, and counted a sneer.
