I'll Go Where You Want Me to Go." 
By Hazel Washburn

"I'll go where you want me to go, dear Lord," 
She sang in unconscious glee, 
For the day was so bright, and so clear was the path 
In the way that seemed marked out to be; 
And life was so joyous, and love was so sweet 
That scarcely she noted the thorns at her feet, 
But sang as she went in a wonderful chord, 
"I'll go where you want me to go, dear Lord."

"It may not be on the mountain side 
Or over the stormy sea;" 
But oh, 'twas a woman that sang it now 
With many babes at her knee; 
And the manifold burdens of mother and wife 
Were bringing the lines of care.
