To a Little Girl 498 Dorothy J. Roberts Once I thought a dark-eyed child The dearest in the world, But now I have an added boon Around that small child curled: For I see more than the camera, Which catches your tilted eye, Dimples at mouth corners where A smile is passing by. I cannot see you as you were — That princess in your face, The dainty childhood fingers, for A girl stands in their place — A little girl with brand new teeth, A shining golden braid — The reaching outward of a grace, A lady being made.
