Walk With Stephen Beulah Huish Sadleir He was a little boy Singing to his believing World, in the morn Of his growing years. Tall assurance rippled Through him in the way He held my hand and walked Unmurmuting. His songs Were his own making Until he mimicked the jerky Call of a stranger bird. On to the water's edge. The child's song changed to boats Moving in the rhythm of slow Water on a sunny day. Child of earth, of free design, Yours is the unhampered song.
