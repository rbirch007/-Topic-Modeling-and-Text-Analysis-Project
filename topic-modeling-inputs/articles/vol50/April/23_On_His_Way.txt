On His Way Rose Thomas Graham Proud as a peacock he walks down the street, his head held high. He deigns to smile if he chances to meet a passerby But he never turns to the left nor right; do you suppose He thinks he belongs to the mighty-might? He tilts his nose And purses his lips with a haughty air; He's on his way. It makes no difference if people stare. It's his birthday. But isn't my precious three-year-old cute Out for a walk in his blue-buttoned suit?
