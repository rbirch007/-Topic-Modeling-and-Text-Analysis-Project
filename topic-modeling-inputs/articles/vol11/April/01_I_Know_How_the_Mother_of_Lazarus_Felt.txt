I know how the mother of Lazarus felt. And so all day long I watch him at play; I beg him for kisses, and fondle him close; The cup that he broke, the crumbs that he strewed, The milk overturned, the magazine torn,? How can I scold him and think of last night When lifeless he seemed to lie limp in my arms? How easy it is to forgive and caress When I think, what if this were his burial ay? Oh, I know how the mother of Lazarus felt!
