To My Mother
By Camilla W. Judd
You are sitting by the fire, Dear, sweet mother of mine, And your heart sings like the lyre, In your love of life divine; The firelight glows and dances, As it shines upon your face, That is fairer than a lily, And lovely in its grace.
Upon your brow of splendor, Shines your hair of silver gray; And your eyes have grown more tender, As the years have passed away; Years of love and pain and gladness, Years of service, kindly given, And your dear strong heart grows lighter As you climb the steps to heaven.
May God comfort you, and bless you, And guard you for all time, That your days may all be happy, Oh, gentle mother mine.
