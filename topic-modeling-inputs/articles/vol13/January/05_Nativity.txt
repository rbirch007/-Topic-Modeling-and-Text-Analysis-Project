NATIVITY
Mrs. Maud Chegwidden, Murray, Utah. Awarded Second Prize in the Eliza R. Snow Memorial Contest.
I never knew what pain could be Before this hour; I could not guess Its bitter, blinding agony, This breathless fight, this black distress.
How can I bear it? Welcome death Would be—but no, I cannot die; I can but suffer, while my breath Comes faster and the dark draws nigh.
Oh Mary Mother, now I know The agony that tore thee so!
I never knew what love could be Until from out the mists of pain I heard that cry. Such ecstasy, Such joy then flooded heart and brain!
A thousand torments now are nought; No more is pain, no more is grief. This miracle that love has wrought Brings love again beyond belief.
Oh Mary Mother, was it thus The Christ Child held thee rapturous?
