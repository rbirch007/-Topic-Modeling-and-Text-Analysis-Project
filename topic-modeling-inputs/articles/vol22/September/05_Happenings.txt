Happenings By Annie Wells Cannon
SEPTEMBER—Nothing lightens sorrow nor assuages grief like work. It is the secret of resignation and brings happiness.
THE Catholic Daughters of America and the National Association of Business and Professional Women held notable conventions in Seattle this past summer. The first was marked by strongly worded resolutions against birth control, salacious entertainment, communism and atheism. The second by forceful pleas for equal opportunity in every line of endeavor, while discrimination against married women in different fields was condemned.
PRISCILLA SAWYER RANDOLPH, of Los Angeles, was elected president of the National Association of Women Lawyers. Mrs. Randolph states that an appalling number of women teachers are divorcing their husbands in order to hold their jobs. In view of this it is refreshing to learn that the great city of London has removed the ban on married women teachers.
MLLE OSAKA, age 22, of Paris, has recently been declared a wonder girl. The metaphysical Institute of Paris has announced, after several tests, that she has the most accurate mathematical memory on record.
