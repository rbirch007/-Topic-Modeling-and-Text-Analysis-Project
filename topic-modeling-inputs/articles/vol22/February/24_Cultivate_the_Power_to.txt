Cultivate the Power to Appreciate TT has been said people who appreciate us can do almost anything with us. Christ had the seeing eye, the understanding heart that could detect the good, the beautiful in everyone. He always emphasized the loveliest thing in man or animal or nature. There is a legend that at one time when he and his Disciples were leaving a city, they saw a crowd assembled around a dead dog. One said, "Look at his bleared eye." Another, "How mangy is his fur." Another, "How crooked are his legs." Christ gave one look and _ said, "Pearls cannot rival the whiteness of his teeth." He picked out the one beautiful thing this mongrel dog possessed. He made people respect themselves, and they held their heads higher, feeling there was hope in life. He loved those who were outcasts, because he could see something in them worthy of love. Itisa fascinating worthwhile experience to use His method to search out and. Emphasize the good that is often buried so deep that few see it. There is a divine spark in each human breast. Blessed are they that fan it into a flame and one of the most successful fuses is appreciation of the good.
