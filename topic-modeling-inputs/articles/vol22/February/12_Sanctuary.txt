Sanctuary By Rachel G. Taylor Awarded Third Honorable Mention in the Eliza Roxey Snow Memorial Contest Beneath high, massive, vaulted domes Where men in somber robes move slowly by And glistening tapers, starlike, shine In shadows dimmed by opal glass With organ's deep accompaniment, Thy children kneel And whisper prayers to Thee. On crowded curbs where ragged children play With roaring motors swiftly clanging by; In murky street-lights' flickering gleam, Mid scoffers' smiles, With blatant, drum accompaniment. Thy children lift their heads And sing soul-songs to Thee. Oh, that such, might go Far up on mountain heights Where stands a temple of Thy handiwork Untouched by art of man. Its columns tall of silver satin bark Are arched above by stirring, shining leaves; Long shafts of golden sunlight gently slant Thro' skylight openings of the trees, Its aisles are carpeted with rich design Of trailing fern and columbine. As they would enter in with reverent step, From their shoulders there would fall The wornout cloak of creed; From their hearts there would be lifted The heavy burden of disturbing doubt; For there, where breezes like faint organs roll, Within the peaceful confines of Thy beauteous solitude, Comes faith and benediction to the soul.
