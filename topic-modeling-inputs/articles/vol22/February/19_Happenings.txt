Happenings By Annie Wells Cannon
FEBRUARY — Patriotism need not be severe nor gayety foolish.
MRS. CAROLINE O'DAY of New York is the only new woman among the feminine members of Congress this session. There are five other women in the House, 3 democrats and 2 republicans, and one woman in the Senate—Mrs. Hattie Caraway of Arizona.
MRS. S. F. YOUMANS is mayor of the town of Oak Park, Ga. All members of the city council are also women.
MISS LUVERNE BROWN of Kansas City has been appointed a federal court bailiff. She is the first woman court bailiff in a United States district court.
MRS. ALICE M. FRENCH, founder and first president of the American War Mothers, died this winter.
MRS. ELBERT GARY bequeathed her wardrobe valued at $350,000 to be sold "for improving the condition of the poor."
REBA CRAWFORD SPLINALO has succeeded Amy Semple McPherson, resigned, as pastor of Los Angeles Temple.
CROWN PRINCESS JULIANA of Holland is the only European princess who offers a throne to her future consort. Court circles are wondering where there is a worthy noble for this beautiful princess.
EMPRESS MARIE LOUISE cherished her letters from Napoleon. Now 300 of them have recently been sold to the French government. They brought $75,000 and are said to furnish a most important addition to the knowledge concerning Napoleon.
MRS. NORMAN NEVILLE of Utah is the first woman to navigate the San Juan river canon where it enters into the Colorado through a gorge of wall 1500 ft. High. The trip was made by a party of three to take pictures.
CORA STERLING of Seattle is the first aerial policewoman. She patrols the skies for air regulation offenders.
THE Martin Johnsons have prepared a series of pictures of their trip into dark Africa. The pictures to be shown in the air over New York City.
HELEN JACOBS, amateur tennis champion, denies she has accepted an offer to become professional. She is now playing in Egypt.
MATHILDE EIKER has written a new psychological novel, "Heirs of Mrs. Wellingdon," which is said to be "delightfully written and never dull."
DORIS LESHIS' new book, "Full Flavor," is of the Victorian period. A choice book of kindly satire which if not quite authentic is none the less entertaining.
MARY KIDDER PARK has published her own story in a novel called "A Cowman's wife." It is a humorous record of Arizona ranch life.
