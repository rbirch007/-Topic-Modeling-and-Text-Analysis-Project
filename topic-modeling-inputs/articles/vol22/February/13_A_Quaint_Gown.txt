A Quaint Gown By LaRene King Bleecker My friend has a quaintly fashioned gown, Designed for a princess, demure. Romance lurks in its shining folds, And intrigue and dainty allure. Gay its sheen, as the sunset clouds, Or the red of a velvety rose. Fragrance of musk and lavender, Waken mem'ries of long, long ago. Each year in mood reminiscent, She wears it to Pioneer Hall. Her years fade away to magical youth; Once again she's the belle of the ball. Suitors in broadcloth and velvet, Pay homage in courtliest style; While troopers 'and gay caballeros, Bow low to the charm of her smile: Bow low at the feet of my Princess, To clasp the fringe of her gown; Or press a cheek in its shining folds, Though the seams are fraying and brown; And all its glamored fragrance breathes Of flower gardens, scented and old, And musky paths where young love walked Through moonbeam's latticed gold. Then back in its wrappings of tissue and silk, In fragrance of musk and sachet, In an old oaken chest in the attic The quaint gown is folded away. There are mists of tears, and sighs long-drawn, For the mem'ries of yester-years, When youth was gay and love was new, In the hearts of the Pioneers. Oh, sweet to know that once each year, In her gown of shimmering glow, My Princess walks in her garden, With her friends of the long, long ago.
