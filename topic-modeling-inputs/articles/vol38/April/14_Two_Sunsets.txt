Two Sunsets Juliaetta B. Jensen THE CRUCIFIXION I saw a sunset from my hill So dark and drear, foreboding, All my heart with fear was still, Black clouds frowning from beneath, above, 'Shut out all hope of peace and love, One thin, red streak as if of blood, _ Was all I saw in that dull, dark, western sky, ' One moment, and even that was gone. My path blacked out, and with a bitter cry, I fell upon the shaking earth, and wept alone. THE RESURRECTION The next night, I saw another sunset From my hill, so different My heart was still, and lifted up With joy and hope and peace. It was as if some artist from on high Had dipped his brush into the rainbow dew And painted all the western sky With pastel colors of every hue; That shouted to the heavens about, above, Of One who gave his all for love That you and I might never die.
