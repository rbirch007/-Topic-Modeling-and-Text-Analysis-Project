The heart will find it, if it has not yet, The heart, grown glad, will learn it—and be wise. Then shall beauty have no death nor end, For when the autumn fades there is the dream, The lucent amber threaded on the mind, The coral heaped as by the canyon stream. Memory can bridge the winter's white abyss And stir the flame still embered in its sight, The little space till glory shall return In some new guise, and all the land be bright. ~The Cover: Cabin at Jackson Hole, Wyoming, Photograph by Ray Loomis.
