Benediction By Mary Anderson Methinks the loveliest hour of all Is the magic hour when twilight falls. Life's petty cares are all effaced; The world stands still a little space, Receiving benediction. The sunset sky with rose is flushed, The feathered minstrel's song is hushed, And flowers in their garden beds Their petals fold and droop their heads, Receiving benediction. And little children, ere repose Shall over-tired, white eyelids close, All kneel beside a mother's chair, And for their loved ones ask, in prayer, A Father's benediction. May we, when our last sunset glows, Before we seek a last repose, Look back upon a life so spent That we may ask, with confidence, A final benediction.
