MAMA'S RETIREMENT "We're going to like that. There's a first time for everything. Aren't we going to like it, Joey?" I asked, as he and his grandfather came back into the room. "Grandpa is going to give mea dump truck for his birthday," Joey cried, all aglow. "And I'm never never never going to leave it in a driveway again. Not even our own." Mama's eyes were closed. But there was a gentle smile around her mouth. I knew she was thinking somewhat along the same line, but with doing too much, rather than too little. Order. Order in our households, our personal lives, and in our spiritual lives. It was a good thing, but we had to go about it in an orderly manner. "Just one thing more, Mama," I said. '"You're going to have to get along with my lemon cream pie." Mama moved restlessly there on her pillows, opened her eyes a bit, then closed them again. For one long moment I feared she was going to renege on the whole thing. But then she opened her eyes and smiled, though a trifle wanly. "Everything is going to be all right," she said. And suddenly I knew it would be.
