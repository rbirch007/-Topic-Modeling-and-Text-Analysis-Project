Remembering Celestia J. Taylor | walked along the water's edge, And everywhere upon the sand lay glistening stones Like scattered jewels upon a silken spread. Here and there, where hurried eddies swept the sand, Were left deep frosted swirls of rich burnt brown. My feet were bare; the chocolate mud oozed round my toes, And velvet coolness bathed them as they sank. | stooped and gathered, till my hands could hold no more, The colored gems. "These | will take and put inside my house, And when | look upon them, | will see again this place Of sand and sun and jeweled stones Upon a pebbled shore." But when | took the stones away from sun and sand and laving wave And put them in a silver dish or copper bowl, Or even when | set them in a place apart, | saw, not glistening jewels, but pallid rocks Like those which lay outside my door.
