So Shought Unfolds Maude Rubin As cereus petals open in the night, So thought unfolds in solitude and peace; Its flowering need not wait the early flight Of morning larks — but, held in quiet's lease, Its fragile beauty, opening serene And pure as alabaster, soft as dove, Illumes the problem darkness with a sheen Gentle as whispered prayer, as bright as love! CARLOTA DE YALIBAT
