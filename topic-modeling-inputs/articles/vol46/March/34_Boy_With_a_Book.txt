Boy With a Book Christie Lund Coles We saw him sitting on the country porch, Alone and still, a book between his hands, Oblivious to us, the twilight's torch Against the western sky; the nearby stands Where fruit was sold, and people paused—as we— To touch and purchase. For a moment, only, I Jooked upon him, in his jeans, to see If he apart there from us, could be lonely. Then, suddenly, I knew it was not true, For all the magic and the priceless joy Of books that I had read, returned. I knew They were incarnate now within this boy. Oh, to be as young as he was there and then, And for the first time, read each book again. —>+ Shey Sell Tle
