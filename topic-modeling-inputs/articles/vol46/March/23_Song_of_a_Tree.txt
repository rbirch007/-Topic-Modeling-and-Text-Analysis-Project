Song of a Sree Dorothy J. Roberts Through the eyes' small wicket enter, Tall tree garbed in the robe of spring. Tell with the leaf-cut symbol and the swaying Stamens hung with a distant death; Speak in the kindest tongue of earth; Whisper the wakening wonder, birth. Murmur of the green shoot in the breath; The rhythm, known, of spent and yearn— Green voice singing in the choir of wood, Till the song be bird on bough and understood.
