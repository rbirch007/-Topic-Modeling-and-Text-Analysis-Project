Mother'' Claire Stewart Boyer Unknown creator of our lives art thou, When on the brink of this our world-to-be, With heritage thou only couldst endow, Thy children start their furtive destiny. Then slowly as the dark slips from our eyes, We see thee watching o'er us tenderly, And every care bestow Athena-wise, And so we learn to watch and call for thee. And as the days make years, our thoughts take wing, On words that we have mastered with thy aid, We turn to thee with all our questioning, And when we pray, our prayer for thee is made. But youth is always headstrong in the fight, Self-confident we need no counselor, Believing that we know the test of right, We shun thy truths and warnings more and more.
