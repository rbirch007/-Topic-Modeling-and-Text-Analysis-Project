Thy Gift Alberta Huish
Thy gift to me, great Prince of Bethlehem? I cannot know the mercies infinite That thy great sacrifice has given me; Yet power to love, to pity and to see, I know are gifts from thee.
Remembering thy cup of bitter dregs, My trembling grasp is strengthened, and I quaff More bravely and more readily, my share, From my life's cup, though filled with pain and care. This is thy gift to me.
I hear the cry from out Gethsemane: "A debt is paid;" the price was thy life's blood Which makes men gods potential, gives them breath, And power to see beyond the wall of death. These are thy gifts to me.
