A Prayer By Lucy M. S. Carter
Oh, Thou who knoweth all Hear Thou my plea. Speak that I may hear Thy voice And follow Thee, My Father.
Dark are the nights of doubt, I cannot see; So many sounds I hear Confusing me— My Father.
Striving to push my way I stumble so. Seeking with hands outstretched Fumbling I go My Father.
Life asks such heavy toll I dare not pay. Many I meet who though Seeking the way Are broken.
Here in the dark I pray For them and me That I may find a way And bring to thee A token.
