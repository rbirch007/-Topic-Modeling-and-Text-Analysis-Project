But autumn sun still beamed benignly warm, There was no time to waste in vain regret; The same Good Giver brings the sun and storm— Death is the thing, in life, we must forget. We cannot shadow now with things to come, We must press on to happiness ahead. Tomorrow is the day for which we sigh, We must forget the past, the flowers dead. For well we know, despite the bitter pain, That everything that dies will live again.
