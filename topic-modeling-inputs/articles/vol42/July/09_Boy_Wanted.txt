Boy Wanted Elsie McKinnon Strachan I have need for a boy in my kitchen, To approve of my culinary art, To butter my mornings with questions, To put spice in my middle-aged heart; Anticipatory, impatient, A boy eager and ready to cat; With a grin hiding under a doughnut— Unimpressed with today's cost of meat. I have need for a boy in my kitchen, With an appetite deep as a wish, With a finger to salvage loose frosting, And, especially, to clean out the dish. Page 443
