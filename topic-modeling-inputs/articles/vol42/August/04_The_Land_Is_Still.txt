The land is still: no wakeful winds confer With wayside shrubs; no mockingbird lets ring His sweet arpeggio. Naught save the whir Of tires gives voice to sound unraveling. Far, far I ride, where silence beds the earth, Where I, alone, have not succumbed to sleep; And yet, through hush, I sense the pulse of birth, Know day will bring fresh fruits for man to reap. In faith I watch dawn's color-promenade, And know anew the glory that is God. Page 50]
