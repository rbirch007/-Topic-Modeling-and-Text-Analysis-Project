Autumn Zara Sabin A prescience of winter is felt these days— A knowledge from past years, not quite forgot; A hushed expectancy that lures our gaze To half-bare elms, or dim secluded spot Where blooms the hardy sweet-breathed violet, Or sheltered more, a clump of mignonette. Bare fields lie dreaming in the cooling breeze; Blackbirds wing higher, swifter than before, Or, holding convocation in the trees, They seem to teach their young new nature lore In preparation for their southward flight... I wonder, do they plan to go tonight?
