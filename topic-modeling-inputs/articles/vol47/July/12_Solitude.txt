Solitude Catherine B. Bowles The silence of Cumorah's hill Stole over the place, quiet and still. Through the woodland I wandered alone With trials, troubles, a cross of my own. A tranquil spirit soothed my fears, Calmed my heart and dried my tears. Through nature's garden I walked unseen, Feeling his presence, peaceful, serene. \Cronce, Women Christie Lund Coles Into the too-small wagons, loaded deep, (With things most precious from the young years spent Acquiring treasures that the heart would keep) She puts the most valued, those that are meant To last a lifetime in an alien place. Tokens, perhaps, to bring back memories Of warmth and comfort, each familiar face Left in a world of happiness and ease. Across the miles she rides as stoically As one born to the hardship and the tears, Rather than to a mild gentility, And loving assurance through her tender years. Upon her lips she tastes the gray of dust, Yet faces proudly everything she must.
