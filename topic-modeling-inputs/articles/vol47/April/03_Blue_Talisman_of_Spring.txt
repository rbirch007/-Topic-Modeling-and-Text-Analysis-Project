Blue Salisman of Spring Dorothy J. Roberts Something breaks the monotone of seasons Edged with the ragged ermine of the snow — A sapphire jewel glinting on the landscape Where a pool holds part of heaven here below. And I recall the brave, blue tint of promise— The aqua sphere beneath the robin's wing, Blue courage of the hyacinth and crocus, Bare willows where an azure bird will sing. I think of dawn's pale preface to the morning Where the cold, black weight of midnight had been pressed— How the turquoise swells and spreads above the valley And crowds the waning darkness from the west. Revived, I leave, the bright brooch of the water Glistening on the dullness of the fen, And turning from the darkness and the winter, I walk the waiting land with faith again.
