Shis Sun-Walled Hour Maude Rubin Though greener green once painted distant pastures And ocean's pulsing song sang siren rhymes, Today, my wander-urge, becalmed in brightness Hears children's laughter, delicate wind-chimes Above this sun-walled hour... Let tides keep calling, Let gull cries splinter night, no smell of brine Can drown this rose-drenched air—for, oh, my darling, I've found my land at last, your home and mine!
