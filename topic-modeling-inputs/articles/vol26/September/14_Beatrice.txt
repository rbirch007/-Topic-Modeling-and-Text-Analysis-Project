SHUT IN By Beatrice Knowlton Ekman
A wind from the western prairie Swept into the town today, Strained at the doors and the windows, Ran over the roofs and away.
It stirred my heart with yearning As it tossed and swayed the trees, For a lonely stretch of sky-bound plain Where beard grass blows in the breeze,
For scarred and lifted mountains Where the eagles soar and dip, For the churning foam of the ocean In the wake of an outbound ship.
I wanted to rise and follow, But only my winged thoughts soar; Cloistered I watch and remember I will run with the wind no more.
