AUTUMN IN THE SOUTHWEST By Sadie Ollorton Clark
This is the time, the season of the year When earth yields bounty from her treasure horn; As far as eye can see, distant or near, Is ripe red fruit, brown leaves and yellow corn.
Gone are the dusty, shrieking winds that tore Frail branches from the bowed and naked trees. Gone is the driving heat that summer bore, The withering, arid heat the desert sees.
The field is mellow, as the setting sun Pours out his golden rays on grain and flower; Trees, spreading out their leafy arms, have won First right to lavish beauty in this hour.
All is so calm. Afar the ancient hills Have clothed themselves in dignity and peace. Their quiet falls upon my head and fills My soul with gratitude that will not cease.
