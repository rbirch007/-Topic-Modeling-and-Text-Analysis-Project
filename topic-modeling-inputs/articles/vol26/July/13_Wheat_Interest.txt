Wheat Interest
Ward Relief Society presidents are advised that wheat interest checks for this year will be mailed to the bishops. Checks will be made payable to Relief Society presidents, as always, but are mailed to the bishops in order to facilitate the handling of the immense amount of mail in the Presiding Bishop's office. Presidents will kindly contact the bishops. Checks are to be used by the Relief Society as formerly in the interest of maternity and child welfare.
Upon the advice of the First Presidency and the Presiding Bishopric, approximately one-fourth of the wheat fund has been used in buying wheat to be stored for need. The General Presidency of the Relief Society are in perfect harmony with this action. Due to this purchase, wheat interest checks will be smaller this year, as interest will be paid only on the funds remaining with the Presiding Bishopric.
The Relief Society appreciates the generosity of the Presiding Bishopric in paying a 5% interest rate in view of the greatly reduced interest rates being paid by banks and other institutions. However, in all probability the rate will be lower next year.
Wheat interest money has been the means of accomplishing much good in a special field. We commend the Relief Society presidents for their diligence and wisdom in putting this money to use in the past and trust that the same efficiency will characterize its use this year.
