The sounds of the radio banged into Cally's ears as she tried to remember what came next in her pancake recipe. The baby was crying to be picked up, and her husband was calling for a clean towel. The rain beat a heavy tempo against the window; gray clouds pressed a damper on any cheerful thoughts that might have entered her mind. Over the din she heard the radio announcer presenting the local weatherman for his report and forecast.

"It's a beautiful day in Fremont County!" he began.
"At 7:00 this morning, 45 hundredths of an inch of precipitation...."
Click! Cally couldn't bear hearing anyone being cheerful about this muggy weather. "He must be some kind of nut," she muttered. At least now with the radio off there was less competition for her attention.

She finally got her husband out the door, the baby fed, the dishes done, and the bed made. She had an awful temptation to climb back into bed, but wisdom and good sense beat back her heavy eyelids, and she smoothed the bedspread over the pillows.

"A beautiful day indeed!" she muttered as she settled into her daily routine.

The phone rang.
"Is this the Glen MacMillan residence?" a man's voice asked.
"Yes—this is Cally MacMillan."
"Mrs. MacMillan, your husband has been in an automobile accident and has been injured. The ambulance is taking him to City Memorial Hospital now. Can you meet us there?"
