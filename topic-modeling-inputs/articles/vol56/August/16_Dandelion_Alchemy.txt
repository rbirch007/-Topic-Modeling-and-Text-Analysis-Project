From melting snow and first bird song
Till summer is long past,
You only smile at scorching sun,
And scorn the icy blast.
Men curse you on their broad terrain,
While women sigh and yawn,
To see your merry face a-glow—
Triumphant—on the lawn.

Gay dandelion, flaunt your curls!
Pursued—decried—who knows
What inner magic stirs your heart
To wink at all your foes?
For in warm hands held up to me
With words of love, untold,
Small field-grown flower, glorified,
Your petals turned to gold.

—Norma A. Wrathall

Chapter 3 HEART ROOM FOR HOME

Alice Morrey Bailey

SYNOPSIS: Rosemary Freenian has stormed out of the living room of their Los Angeles home after a scene about her wedding, planned for the coming June, which she had expected her mother to finance. Her parents, Kim and Mary Ann Freeman, have brought her aged grandfather, convalescing from a broken hip, home to care for him, rather than sell his home in Mayville, Utah, and put him in a nursing home. His home is beloved by all the family.

Rosemary's mother must give up her job of teaching in order to take on this new responsibility, and it will cut the family income in two. There was nothing for Mary Ann to do but leave, but she was near tears when she came out of Rosemary's room, not only at the rebuff, but at her inability to reach Rosemary and at her own question. Kim came to meet her, to put his arm around her.
