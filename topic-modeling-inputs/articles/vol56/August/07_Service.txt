Lilly Peter, a past-seventy poet, photographer, and fulltime farmer, recently mortgaged 4,000 acres of her Arkansas plantation to bring the Philadelphia Symphony Orchestra, with Eugene Ormandy, to play for Little Rock's one hundred and fiftieth celebration of Arkansas' territorial status. She wanted her fellow citizens to hear the best in music, paying $60,000 for this privilege.

"Miss Lilly" commissioned, for $8,000, the Pulitzer-prize-winning "Homage to Haydn," which the orchestra premiered. It also played selections from Wagner, Debussy, and Brahms.

Rose Mary Woods has been President Nixon's intelligent and efficient secretary for seventeen years. Mary Wells, wife of the head of Braniff Airlines, is the head of a large New York advertising agency. Renata Adler, twenty-nine years of age, has become the highly regarded film critic of the New York Times. Mrs. May West, of Pleasant Grove, Utah, in the Memorial Day Regatta on Utah Lake, piloted her own boat, purchased and tuned to perfection by herself. She was the only competitor in the exciting races to win two events, thus winning the regatta. Her husband, Don, holds some world's records, but a mischance disqualified him from the regatta.
