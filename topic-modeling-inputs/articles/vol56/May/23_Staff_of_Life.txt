Place it in a large bowl or pan, cover it with a damp cloth, set it aside in a warm place (try a tray table set next to a wall heater) and let it alone. It will rise until "double in bulk." Usually, this takes about two hours. Plunge your fist into the dough and watch it deflate! Sort of mound up the dough, turn it over, and let it rise again. This time it will take a shorter amount of time, around one to one and one-half hours.

If the recipe makes three loaves, cut the dough into three parts and roll each with your hands to fit into a greased bread pan. If you don't have bread pans, use small hunks of dough about the size of a plum, and place them side by side in a 9x15 greased pan. You could make hamburger buns by rolling out tangerine size balls to a flat pancake. Place these discs on a baking sheet and let them rise. Bake any of the above according to the recipe you are using. You can also save some of the dough, let it rise again, then tear off plum-size pieces and deep fat fry them. When light brown, remove, drain, and dip in sugar. My family calls them "spindles" and can eat a whole recipe at one sitting. This is a good Family Home Evening treat.

Bread baking is fun, homey, simple, therapeutic, and you can do it yourself easily.
