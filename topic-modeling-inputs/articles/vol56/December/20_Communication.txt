"Of the Dust"

Low, out of the dust atoms of lost cities,
Low, out of the dust the gentle, obedient men.
Thus in leaping tongues of flame old rebellions, passions, fires and the slain children.
Low, out of the dust he old kings, Jabin broken with years
From his tower. Gleaming on the city wall.
Weled from without and within, yet valiant.
Cry from the dust lost and heroic people, Holy men and evil...
Trumpet me to repentance and believing.
And on this golden morning
Shining on the land that once held him.
Pierce me with the sweetness
Behind him thorns and he shadowed cross,
And the rising on the third day...

Other sheep I have, which are not of this fold: them also must bring...
Torments of darkness, hundreds of destructions,
And then the still small voice And the descending Son.
Hark, dark and lovely children of those who once saw him here...
Have kept in your legends
The eternal promise...
