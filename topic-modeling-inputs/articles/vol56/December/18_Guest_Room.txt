GUEST ROOM Here, as a child I came to read Into its quiet before day's end, The great old Bible, too heavy to lift, With copied engravings and all it contained; I read, and reread, each of the prophets— My tears blurred the pages where Christ was arraigned. Now the home must go for mother's away, This beautiful room where a friend could stay; Lesson Department God be thanked for the knowledge he has given us of all this. (Immortality and Eternal Life, Vol. 2, lesson 3.) SUMMARY Thus in our existence here, we are carrying out the plan which was made for the great celestial family of which we are a part; we are going forward as the children of our God and fitting into the pattern he made for us. The place we shall hold in God's household, in God's family, in our heavenly and eternal home, whether it shall be in the inner family circle, or outside in the halls and anterooms, depends wholly upon what we ourselves do here. (Ibid., lesson 1.) Question Could a Latter-day Saint woman, after reading and discussing the foregoing essentials of the gospel plan, doubt the importance of her inherent role? For Home Doing Appreciate, in your own thinking, the enlightenment which the gospel gives us regarding the purposes of life. Put forth effort on at least one aspect of family life which will forward the family unit toward its high destiny. But the guests in the book were the ones we loved best, Isaiah, and Matthew, and all of the rest. Linnie F. Robinson
