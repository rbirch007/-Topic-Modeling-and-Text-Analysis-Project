Flowers Are For Christmas
Arrangement by Jean Rigby Hawthorne Ward, Sugar House Stake (1)

A predominating color for the arrangement was selected for use with harmonizing tones and tints. In this arrangement, purple, rose, blue, and green have been chosen, with gold for accent. The artificial flowers made of various types of plastic and other materials (and which may have served previously for other purposes and been discarded) were spray painted with either lacquer or enamel and thoroughly dried. A hemp rope, dyed purple, was used as a foundation for the flowers and balls, which were attached with florist's wire. In this arrangement, an antique brass scale was used for making the attractive and graceful design. The candles pick up the deeper shades of color, and the wreaths add to the effectiveness of the design placed against avocado colored walls. TRANSPARENCY BY J M. HESLOP

Refinished Furniture � More Beautiful Than New
Refinishing by Virginia Lattin Bodell Balli, President, East Mill Creek Relief Society (Cl)

The furniture in the picture was first sanded down to smoothness, a coat of base flat paint applied, then the color coat, and, finally, a finishing coat of antique enamel. Decals, free-hand painting, or other decorative features should be applied before the final finish, which may require more than one coat for effective protection and lasting beauty.

For other references on refinishing and repainting furniture, see The for 1968: "Antiqued Articles," March, page 195; ""Rooms for Improvement," March, pp. 197-201; "An Old Trunk Can be Rejuvenated," June, page 447. TRANSPARENCY BY J M. HESLOP
