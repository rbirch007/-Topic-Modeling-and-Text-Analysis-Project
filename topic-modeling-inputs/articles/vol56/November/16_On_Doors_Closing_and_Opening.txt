A new door does not always open wide
Immediately when the familiar door clicks shut;
and so one stands lost, inside
the unfamiliar loneness till the pricks
of shutters on the retina adjust
and shadows form in new dimensions like or unlike what is gone.

The question: must
one hurdle forward, seek another dike
to satisfy this need that one be used,
or stand and wait, slowly gathering strength
until reason rises, unconfused
and in perspective; and we learn at length
that doors within the mind must open first
before the heart accepts a new sunburst.
