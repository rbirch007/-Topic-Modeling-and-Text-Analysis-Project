I am as a rushing stream Flowing over these rough stones, my poems. From far places where I have been— From the rugged mountains of learning, From the desert of maturing insight, From the forest of deep appreciation, From the valleys of experience, I have gathered abrasive sand and silt, Carrying all along with me. Each time that I, so laden, Touch one of these my poems, Its roughest corners fall away, And it takes on an encouraging bit of polish. Possibly, if I continue long enough, Some of these will become smooth, round pebbles, Shining and beautiful— Such as children delight to find—and keep! Violate R. McAllister
