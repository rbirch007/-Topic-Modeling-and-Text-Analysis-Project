To Martha, overly busy with many things, he said, "But one thing is needful: and Mary hath chosen that good part, which shall not be taken away from her." (Luke 10:42.) To one puffed up with her own importance, he says "... Whosoever will be great among you, shall be your minister; And whosoever of you will be the chiefest, shall be servant of all." (Mark 10:43-44.) The Savior was long-suffering and kind in his hour of deep anguish and loneliness. He longed to have his three beloved disciples watch and pray with him during his agony in Gethsemane. Not once, nor twice, but three times he returned where they were, only to find them asleep. Even an angel came and strengthened him, but the human companionship failed him. With the greatest charity Christ only said, "Sleep on now, and take your rest: it is enough, the hour is come." (Mark 14:41.) Whatever aspects of our characters need strengthening, we will find help to improve through prayer and study of the scriptures. Then as beloved Relief Society gives us the opportunity to act according to the sympathies God has planted in our bosoms, we will not only perform the outward act but it will be accompanied by the inward grace. I pray each Relief Society member in the coming year may learn the truth "And now abideth faith, hope, charity, these three; but the greatest of these is charity." (1 Cor. 13:13.)
