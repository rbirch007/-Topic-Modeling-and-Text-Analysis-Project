Leaves wind-lost they fell upon a waiting earth retaining veins of summer's green: they lay as other years of leaves have lain released from parent stem, they knew the early light of Spring, the summer strengthening, emblazoned color, and the fall. As leaves do, children will outlive the parent tree many seasons after.

Rome spread over 62,000 acres. It has about 2,000 restaurants. The delicious oversized pears, apples, peaches, and grapes served to us with our meals for dessert were a delight to see and eat. We saw the famous "Spanish Steps" before we left and a street with magnolia trees on either side just about to bloom. We learned that Rome is a little farther north than the city of New York, and we reluctantly left the magnificence of this amazing city and hoped that some day our road would lead us back to Rome.

In 1850 the Italian Mission of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints was opened and in 1854 it was combined with the Swiss Mission. The Swiss-Italian Mission was divided in 1966 with the Italian Mission headquarters in Florence and is progressing among those wonderfully gifted, warm, and friendly people. A new era is opening for those who will listen to the gospel to show them the way to peace and salvation.

Parent-taught, they have learned to lean against the winds nor fear the wrath of storms, aware of radiance wrought by morning's sun and the regenerative power in the cycled harmony of birth.
