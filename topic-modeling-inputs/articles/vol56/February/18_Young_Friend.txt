Young Friend Ida A. Isaacson

I met a young friend of mine at leadership meeting. She brought her bicycle to a full stop as we met. She is the mother of five lovely children. She had a basket on the front of her bicycle, and it held her , and her Out of the Best Books, Volume Four, a notebook, and her purse. It was gratifying to see her, and I was happily surprised at her mode of travel. It indicated youthfulness, economy, and a method of traveling that was faster than walking those blocks. And it denoted good judgment.

After the meeting was over, I am sure that she mounted her bicycle and arrived home a little earlier to greet her children and to make plans for dinner and attend to the multiple things that a young mother and wife must do. I admired her.
