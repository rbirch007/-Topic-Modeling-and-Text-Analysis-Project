Mrs. Mara K. Culp, twenty-seven, of Newport Beach, California, a small blond native of Riga, Latvia, who has lived in America the last twelve years, won the twenty-third annual Powder Puff Derby, women's cross-country air race, last July. She has two thousand flying hours to her credit and flew from San Diego to Washington in eleven hours and fifty-seven minutes.

Mrs. Sharon Sites Adams, whose husband is a sailing instructor, has become the first woman to sail alone the six thousand miles across the Pacific Ocean (from Yokohama, Japan, to San Diego, California). During the 75-day crossing in a 31-foot ketch, the 37-year-old housewife weathered severe gales, lost her rotator (which drags in the water and measures distance), to a shark and was becalmed for one week. Her radio transmitter became inoperative.

Letitia Baldridge has been social secretary to Ambassador and Mrs. David Bruce in Paris, to Ambassador Clare Boothe Luce, and to President and Mrs. John F. Kennedy. She became the first woman executive in Tiffany's history, and is currently public relations consultant for Chicago's Merchandise Mart, and for Sears, Roebuck. She is married and is a mother.
