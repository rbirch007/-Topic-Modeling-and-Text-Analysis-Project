The canvas of my day is yet empty.
And the pallet is clean.
My beloveds still slumber—
And God awaits my prayer.

I offer my hands this day unto their Creator,
To fashion a thing of beauty
From these virgin hours awaiting color and form.
Strike away the temptation to blotch and blurr, 
Lest they be lost—these priceless pearls of purity and purpose holy. 

Know ye not that thy place is at the side, and not below,
This companion to whom thou art given of the Lord?
Thou woman exalted, thou first to forgive and last to forsake,
Thou priestess, queen,
Thank thy God who made thee thus,
That thou wast born
A woman.

That the canvas may be vibrant and clean
With the work of loving hands.
Hands that will comfort, protect and perpetuate.
Hands that will discipline and adhere,
That the work may form in order and beauty.
Hands that will build, give, and guide,
Strengthening each fiber of importance,
And shunning the rest.

When the canvas is completed
In detail and deed,
May I hopefully seek my Lord again. 
Then, in humility and with thankfulness,
May I ask for his refreshment
And begin anew with these hands he has given me.
—Mary Jo McEwan
 
Fayola R.
