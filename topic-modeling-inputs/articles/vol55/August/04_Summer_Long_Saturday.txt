SUMMER-LONG SATURDAY We summer together in nature's long Saturday. It is the day of the whispered secret:
There is a hummingbird nest In the crotch of an elm bough Above a banquet table Spread petunia-red.
It is the never-alone time: Many hands unite over rake and spade, Over jam kettle and sweetened ladle; Many eyes finger plump cherries in glass cages; Many small arms Swing brown and free.
