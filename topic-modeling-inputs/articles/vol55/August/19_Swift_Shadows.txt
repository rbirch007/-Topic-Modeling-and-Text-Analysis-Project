3 I see the shadows on the sunny hill,
Patterns that the drifting clouds have made.
They slide across each gentle rise and fill
The chasm's cup with pools of sudden shade.
They trace their path along the windy arc
That pours its freshness on the valley floor;
All this day the earth has borne a mark
For every buoyant thing the heavens wore.

Summer splashes all we see with light,
Then underlines this joy with blue and green,
Balancing the darks with tones more bright,
An ever glad, and ever changing scene.
A Lucille R. Perry
