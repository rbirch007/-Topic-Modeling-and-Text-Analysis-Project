HIGH LINK FENCE No sound is in this country square, But quiet throbs as I survey The high link fence and sturdy gate That keep without the grazing stray. The fence encloses long-marked graves Of early dead who made and heard The active life on the hamlet hill, Abandoned now to beast and bird. The high link fence and sturdy gate Were not too difficult to buy When someone knew and cared enough That sleepers here could occupy Their place upon this silent hill Where winds are clean and flowers grow Between the shrubs and pale headstones That mark the high link-fenced plateau. —Pearle M.
