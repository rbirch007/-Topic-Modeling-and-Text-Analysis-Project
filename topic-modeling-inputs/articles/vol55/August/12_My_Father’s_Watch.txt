I opened the ornate dresser drawer
And, after many years, again I saw my father's gold, initialed watch,
Saw it with sudden, aching pain.
I saw his thoughtful brow as each night
He reached to wind the clock upon the wall,
Matching the hands with those upon his watch,
That moved in rhythm summer, spring, and fall.

Once more, I seemed to hear its ticking
Against my ear, held within his hand,
As firm and steady as the wheels that he
Showed to me though I did not understand.
The stem wound now the same as in the past,
The sound began as though long overdue,
Telling of time that shall go on and on,
(As he) and you.
