You see, Kitten, she was the one who gave it to Grandma in the first place." He took my hand, and together we walked slowly to the house. Inside it was cosy and warm, and the Christmas turkey was beginning to spread its fragrance from the oven. There was Momma, her eyes shining as she stood against the sink peeling potatoes. Marcia was gathering the wrapping paper and ribbon from the living room floor. Fred and Wayne were arguing (quietly yet) over the electric train, and there was my gift, hanging on the wall in the exact spot it had hung for so long, ever since I could remember.
