THE GRAIL OF THE FIELDS. Maud Baggarley. With heads down-cast the horses stood Beside the fragrant upturned sod; In some dim way I knew life good, Desirable, and beautiful, and felt the peace of God. And as I dreamed The brooding spirit of the fields Unto me spoke — Submerged, or sleeping, instinct woke — I looked upon a world illumed: The sky is blue — that breath of eglantine is sweet — IYon peach tree's flame is like the morn! cried And gazed in ecstasy — a soul new born.
