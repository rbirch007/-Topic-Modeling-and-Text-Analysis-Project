Grace Ingles Frost.
Some there are who look on life as on a puppet show,
They early don the cap and bells and with the pageant go:
In tinsil, tawdry make-believe, with empty hearts and brains.
They greet the day with dancing feet, a trip to garish strains.

Some there are who see in life a melodrama mild,
With cadence rising not above the pathos of the child;
For them is but the transient cloud that brings the summer showers
Which end in shine and song of birds and sweeter breath of flowers.

And some there are who view life as the stage of a solemn scene,
When lights burn low and music weeps and moans the acts between;
They know the treacherous deeps and shoals, the chastening pain.
The bitter of the cup so of experience must drain.
