HOW WONDERFUL Lela Morris How wonderful that | have eyes to see Pale mauve and shell pink skies at break of day When dancing aspen leaves are faintly musical, With errant fingers of pine-scented wind; When robins call to neighbors in their nests, And all the waking birds make twitterings. And far above, in the domed roof of earth, Clouds drift on farflung measures of blue; Day wanes and blazing fire of western skies Casts glory on majestic, snow-capped peaks And after dusk, black velvet of the night, Studded with galaxies of shining gems. How wonderful that | have eyes to see Breathtaking splendor he creates for me.
