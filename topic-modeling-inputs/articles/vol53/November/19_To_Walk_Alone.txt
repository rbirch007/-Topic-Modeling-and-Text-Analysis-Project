TO WALK ALONE Mabel Jones Gabbott All that | wanted was this for you That you could tie or buckle your shoe. At night when the stars blinked loftily down At us and the lights in our little town, You read to me, | read to you Of the Little Red Hen and Little Boy Blue, And all | wanted was that you should need To feel this urgency to read. And all | wanted when the noon sun shone Was that you could waik to school alone. Sometimes the wind tossed leaves in our path; You would leave my side and chase them and laugh; | watched how the breeze tossed your red-gold curls As you dropped my apron to run with the girls; All that | wanted was that you should decide The turn of your sails with the outgoing tide.
