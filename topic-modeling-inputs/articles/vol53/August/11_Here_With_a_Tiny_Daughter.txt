HERE WITH A TINY DAUGHTER Mabel Jones Gabbott It is not very far from where I've been: Out in the playhouse playing with dolls, Under a pear tree reading a book Or at the neighbors throwing balls. It doesn't seem long, but it took some time To climb through digits, quotients, and sums, To run around predicates and proper nouns, And investigate land maps and why the bee hums. It is not very far, from primping and proms, From balancing toe shoes and basking in sun, From looking at moons with star-dusted eyes And falling in love with dozens—then one. It is not very far from where I've been, But now you are here, we can do it again.
