Vat v Get Acquain If you know of a meadow that is fed by little streams, you are more than likely to find watercress growing. You will also find it in the produce section of your market, where it comes from farms which grow it commercially. This peppery-tasting and many-leaved plant adds zest to salads and hors d'oeuvres. It also adds to the nutrition in the diet because it is very rich in minerals, and particularly high in vitamins A and C. The people of the past, the Persians, Greeks, Romans, and Chinese recognized this plant as an important part of their diets. They knew that it improved their health, but they didn't analyze it in terms of vitamins or minerals. The Chinese make a delicious soup using watercress.
