Third Prize Poem Forming Adorning Delighting 14 PRELUDE OF PROMISE Pearle M. Olsen The boundless ocean groaned! Its heaving strength Moved restlessly with bidding will of moon And wind, in ever-changing tidal length! Waves whipped in furious widening typhoon While roots of future islands, being born In darkest layers rupturing earth's core Spewed high again! Only the ocean, torn And timeless, knew the forming on its floor! Relentless effort, claiming right to be— With inch by painful inch of straining birth; Volcanic base, fringed coral filigree— A subterranean chain of writhing earth! The child of violence, risen from the deep— Mature and willing, luring nature's leap! The youthful surfaces of earth's marred shell— Nude islands, raw and waiting, empty-armed, Were swept by winds without a parallel And pounded by the force of rain's alarm! Their milling acts, on lava newly born, Made fertile soil for growth of migrant seed Let fall by halting bird, long weather-worn, Into a niche where sprouting could proceed. A few insects, wind-borne from distant land, With fragmentary green, wilting from strife— Found ways to give sweet growth to bare, unscanned And pregnant soil's potential—beauteous life!
