SEA GULL MONUMENT: Dorothy J. Roberts "Bid " on 'the bough of winter, Call back the century, the summer day. _ Snow on straining wing, - Eternity of granite sphered beneath your airy tread, Paint on the leaden wind To the fear of winter-hunger. Then, answer— Above the creeping black stain on their isolation, 'The wheeling cloud of all-consuming birds, _ The fluid arc of wings which rose and fell, again, Bird on the bough of evening, west, DOROTHY J.
