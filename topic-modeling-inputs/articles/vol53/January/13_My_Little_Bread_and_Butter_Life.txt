MY LITTLE BREAD AND BUTTER LIFE Catherine B. Pratt Age eighty-three | love my bread and butter life Nor would!| change it for another. I'm just an average sort of wife, An ordinary sort of mother. | feel that fancy things are vain Like caviar on gold-trimmed dishes, Contentedly, | find my plain Old bread and butter is delicious. For me there's no monotony Because of one-meal repetition, And | look forward gratefully To each meal's pleasure and nutrition. | know that others yearn for more And find my bread and butter meager; But, often, all they're looking for Leaves them somewhat more bored than eager, And | have friends that | love dearly, Whose lives are bread and butter, too. We share our simple tastes and clearly Old-hat, old-fashioned point of view. So let those who desire their pheasant With its accompanying strife, Have all they want. What | find pleasant? My little bread and butter life!
