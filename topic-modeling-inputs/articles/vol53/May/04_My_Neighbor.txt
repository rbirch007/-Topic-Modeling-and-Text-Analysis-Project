MY NEIGHBOR Dorothy J. Roberts Each spring | fear no furrow May stripe his field again, Age let the ground lie fallow, Spring be a stranger, then. Each spring | scan his garden— Where the fall was neatly burned— Praying his palms may harden And a furrow again be turned, Knowing that sometime winter Will walk a wanton way, And the striving seed of summer No longer line the day. Then | see fresh earth toppling, White gulls a queue on wing, And on his plow, triumphant, My harbinger of spring!
