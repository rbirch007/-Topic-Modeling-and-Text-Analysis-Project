SUNLIT CANYON Ethel Jacobson Dazzle lies On leaf and stream— A dancing brilliance, A quicksilver gleam. Wherever the live oak Canopy parts, The sun's massed archers Loose their darts To splinter on eddy, Stone, and stem, Making a shattered Mirror of them, Reflecting each other Diamond-bright, A fluid, Shimmering bedight Landscape less of land Than light.
