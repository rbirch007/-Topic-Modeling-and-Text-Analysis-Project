Thought Martha, gazing at the papers spread in front of her on the kitchen table. You put your signature on a few official-looking documents, and then the land you'd lived on and worked and loved for so many years was no longer yours. By a few scribbles of a pen she had transferred to someone else a house, most of which they had built themselves, a house in which they had brought up the family, a house whose every corner was full of memories. "T believe I understand everything now, Bert,' she said to the man who stood by the table. Her voice was calm in spite of the thoughts which tore at her heart. "The lawyer explained everything pretty well the other day when we signed these papers."' Bert Randall nodded. "I guess that's all then, Mrs. Nelson. Those are your papers. I've got mine at home."
