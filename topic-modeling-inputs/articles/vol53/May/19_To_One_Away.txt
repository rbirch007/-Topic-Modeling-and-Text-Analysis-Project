TO ONE AWAY Enola Chamberlin There is remembering in my heart tonight— Not just of spring as winter winds blow cold; Not just of summer with its warmth and light, Nor of the autumn with its bronze and gold. But recalling in my heart of other days When other winters lay along the land; Exploring feet were walking other ways, And to my hand there clung another hand. You are not lost completely but the miles Are long and far that keep our hands apart, And loneliness like wind-blown snowdrifts piles Its bitter cold around my yearning heart.
