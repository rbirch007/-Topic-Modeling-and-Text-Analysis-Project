IN DEFENSE OF VALENTINES Olive W. Burt Now let the messages of love be spoken. Let no one hesitate to say the word, To send the flower, or verse, or some small token That gives his love a voice that can be heard. For hate is vocal. Hate sends its hurtful message Straight to the anguished heart that must give heed; And lust and greed are noisy and derisive— Into the mangled air they shriek their creed. But love speaks softly, and is often silent, Trusting frail threads of thought too swiftly broken. So in this day when much is mad and violent Let love's sane messages be clearly spoken.
