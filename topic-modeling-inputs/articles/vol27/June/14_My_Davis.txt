MY TASK By Irene R. Davis I should not be too critical! Nor say, "This child is dull, his intellect is slow." I cannot see beyond the years when he Shall have arrived, and judge me, too. Abilities and unsuspected talents dwell Buried deep, beneath confusion of an adult world, Striving mightily to manifest themselves to light As black clouds of doubt and Uncertainty are furled It is not given me to make, create a mind, Nor mine to say, nor judge its worth— Mine the task to train and mold the clay Such as it is. To give a bit of inspiration birth!
