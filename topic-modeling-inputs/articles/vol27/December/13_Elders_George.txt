The leadership of Washington, the eloquence of Pattick Henry, the culture of Hamilton, and the realism of Jefferson, as these qualities are stressed in the novel, were mentioned. The relationships between some of the fictional characters to these and other historical figures were pointed out. The chief members of the Howard family, together with their main traits, were presented. PHASES OF THE NOVEL NOT PREVIOUSLY CONSIDERED A novel is usually considered from the points of theme, setting, characters and plot. Liberty, its beginning and early development in America, the theme of this novel, is kept constantly before the reader. The setting, that is, the time and place of the action, has, of course, a direct bearing on the theme in this particular novel; and the characters and plot are likewise what they are because of the author's theme or purpose. The novel, in all these related aspects, is particularly significant today, when we need to know what the liberty we talk so much about really is, and (if we would preserve it) how it came into being. This book should help us to understand liberty and to comprehend our relationship to it.
