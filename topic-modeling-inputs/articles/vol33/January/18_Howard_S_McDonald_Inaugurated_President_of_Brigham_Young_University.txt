Howard S McDonald inaugurated President of Brigham Young University Wednesday, November 14, 1945, Howard S. McDonald was inaugurated president of Brigham Young University. Against a background of snow-clad peaks, the academic procession marched across the upper campus and entered the stately Joseph Smith Memorial Hall for the traditional ceremony. The principal address, delivered by Dr. Edwin A. Lee, dean of the school of education of the University of California at Los Angeles, stressed the theme?"The Glory of God Is Intelligence'"?and urged the young people of today to develop spirituality and brotherhood. Elder J. Reuben Clark of the First Presidency, in giving the charge to President McDonald, emphasized the fact that spiritual values must always take precedence over secular values, for the spiritual life is based upon eternal truth. President McDonald, in accepting this charge, said that he visioned the Bigham Young University as a great institution with students from all sections of the earth coming to learn the teachings of their Church as well as to acquire knowledge in the regular academic field. Relief Society women throughout the Church are grateful for the establishment of the Brigham Young University and for the great and good men who have been called to guide its work. We are grateful that many of us and our sons and daughters have been privileged to partake of the spirituality and the vision which have characterized our Church university. President Belle S. Spafford represented the Relief Society at the inaugural ceremonies. 40 ?
