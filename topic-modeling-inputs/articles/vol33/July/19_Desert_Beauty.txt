DESERT BEAUTY Ethel VanHook Hopper Driving through a desert town Where growing things were scorched and brown, I saw beside a heaped-up load Of sagebrush on a dusty road, A woman in the heat and glare, With faded dress and wind-blown hair; She clutched white blossoms in her hand? Where had she found in this bare land Such beauty? Could the desert give These flowers that helped her soul to live I smiled she glanced with pleased surprise; What gratitude in those pale eyes!
