Silent Wings Gene Romolo Time, passing, wafts its way on wings, On silent wings and fleet, And varying events it brings Speed or retard our feet. Though we would halt the wings of time, Some joyous hour to hold, We cannot; there is not a clime Where wings of time may fold. AN AFTERNOON WITH MOLLY An Afternoon With Molly (Continued from page 165) "And you didn't forget the resolution you made in your heart, Mollie, with the first visit?" Julia asked eagerly. "No," Mollie answered, "you don't forget how good life is—when you make a practice of visiting shuting?" "T'm already seeing things in a different light," Julia said softly, "and somehow, of a sudden, I seem to know things have a way of happening for the best. That trip to Florida won't even be missed, because," just for a minute the speaker paused, then a broad smile wreathed her face, "I've just decided John's mother is coming to live with us." x x % of Four years of worthwhile living have slipped by since Julia Dutton made her first visit to the shut-ins, and today she has endeared herself to many invalids in the city that shelters her; and the light that glows in her beautiful eyes is a clear revelation of the joy that comes to those who give happiness to others.
