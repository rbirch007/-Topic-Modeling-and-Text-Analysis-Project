C. IP -— Color of Autumn Beatrice K. Ekman Below the mountain road the farms have spread A colored pattern. Autumn has begun, And native currant bushes have turned red. Brown cockleburs, made brittle by the sun, Sound harsh rustlings by the road, as wind shifts Veils of dust across their sharpened edges. From crumpled milkweed pods, soft, white down drifts To the brown pastures and tawny sedges. Loosed from their anchored roots, the tumbleweeds Drift with the wind, light, silent ghosts at play; Sowing, as they drift, their spiny seeds Across the fields and prairies faraway. Wind weaves smoke through September grasses And purple haze veils the mountain passes... The languorous summer is done.
