Once again the hillside leaves are turning, The harvest crops are safe in pit and barn; Sumacs have begun their annual burning, And crisping air has drifted in to warn Of coming frost. Then chestnut burs release Their shining seed, the orchard yield will fall, The song of bird and insect chirp will cease, Squadrons of geese repeat their parting call.
