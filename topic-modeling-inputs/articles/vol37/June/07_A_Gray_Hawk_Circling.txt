A Gray Hawk Circling Marvin Jones A gray hawk circles where the endless sage Of desert silver merges with the sea— And circling, does the gray hawk sense the age Of desert silver— or infinity? What prompts his reaching wings to challenge wind, What wisdom lifts his wild heart to the sky, And in what measure is his living thinned By desert silver or a hurt, gray cry? Gray as the phantom of relinquished springs, I stood and reached to sky and sea and sand, Reached to the wind and its imaginings To find a greater desert in your hand
