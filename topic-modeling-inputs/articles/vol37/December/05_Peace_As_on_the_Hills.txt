Peace, as on the Hills Dorothy J]. Roberts I long for heart as was the shepherd's heart who, guarding The small gray mounds that were his sleeping flock, Lay still, his back against a sheltering rock, And received the sudden glory of the song. Peace, as on the hills, was in his breast, Ready for the angel voice, to pour The glad, white message, the celestial score Into the vessel. Fashioned by his faith. I long for heart so pure it need not search And comb the continents of earth for light. I long for thought, so quiet that the night Would part again with carol and with star. ~
