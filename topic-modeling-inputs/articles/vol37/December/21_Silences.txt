Silences LaVerne ]. Stallings I have known a purple silence Hung with sunset fires Resting far beyond a canyon's ledge. I have languished in the cool, green silence Where a ferny wood Grows beside a tranquil water's edge. Danger treks have lured me on Through sunswept desert lands, With tawny, golden silence over all; And I have pressed my face upon A crystal windowpane To watch the silver-silent rains that fall. Quietudes of gray slow-moving On a sea's new dawn Have stirred me from the stilling deeps of night; But now I come to a high Norn world Where softly gleaming snows Cover all the silences in white.
