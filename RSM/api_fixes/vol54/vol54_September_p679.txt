Helpful Hints for Success

1. Always hold objects upside down first when painting or glazing, finishing right side up.
2. Cover thoroughly with the first coat of paint. Give two or three coats, if necessary, to very porous surfaces. Let dry completely.
3. When applying adhesive, work in for about five minutes after covering.
4. Adhesive will disappear from sight when "tacky" — about one hour is right.
5. Pick up gold leaf sheets and apply one at a time. Sheets will tear, but that is all right. Smooth with a soft brush and fingers. Work into small places with a small brush. Patch spots with small pieces. Let a certain amount of the red show through, as it gives that "old world" look. Don't apply the gold leaf in a draft. Brush off excess.
6. After sealing the gold leaf, let article dry completely.
7. Do your glazing outdoors or in the garage. The amount of glaze you leave on depends on how you want the object to look. Rub harder in some spots to "high-light" the area.
8. Always wash the adhesive brush with water immediately. Clean the glaze brush with paint thinner.
9. If some spots do not take gold leaf, apply more adhesive to these and do over, between steps 4 and 5.
