Mabel
Harmer

Third Prize Winner
The Relief Society
Poem Contest

Mabel Harmer, who placed second in the Relief Society Short Story Contest in 1942, and was an award winner in the story contest also in 1952 and 1955, is well known to readers of the Magazine. Her articles and short stories have appeared in the Magazine since 1933, and her serials have included "The Lotus Eater," "Love Is Enough," and "Turn of the Wheel."

With her poem "Naomi to Ruth," Sister Harmer is a first-time winner in the Relief Society Poem Contest. "I have tried my hand at almost every type of writing," she tells us, "stories, plays, books, but very little poetry, so I am especially delighted to be a winner in the Relief Society Poem Contest. I have had eleven books published, one a Junior Literary Guild selection. I am affiliated with several writers groups, and currently am serving as Utah State President of the National League of American Pen Women. I also teach a class in creative writing at the Brigham Young University Center for Continuing Education in Salt Lake City, Utah. My husband is Earl W. Harmer, and our five children are Mrs. Oren (Marian) Nelson, Dr. Earl W. Harmer, Jr., Mrs. Charles R. (Patricia) Spencer, John, and Alan. There are also thirteen grandchildren."
