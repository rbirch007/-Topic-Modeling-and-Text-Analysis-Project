Deep Summer

Vesta Fairbairn

This mountain pool of sunlight,
Where no wind stirs
The fragrant air,
Is laden with scent of pine,
Of cedar and fir,
And midday warmth.

Suddenly
A crested jay, pert bird
With flash of blue,
Disturbs
This golden pool
With brief, bright
Flight.

Through the scented air
A feather flutters
Down,
And I grow aware
Midsummer
Is now.
