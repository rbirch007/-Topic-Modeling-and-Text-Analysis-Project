MAY 1966

We were shelling peas when I told her about my sixth grade "crush" and my ninth grade "heartbreak," and still later of my adolescent plans and aspirations.

Nowadays most mothers say that they can't "get through" to their daughters. Girls who have confided in me say that they wish they could talk with their own mothers. Because of this lack of communication, mothers and daughters, too, go through the days and weeks and years never understanding each other, never really discovering how to make their lives touch. Because they cannot tolerate this distance between them, or because things go wrong, mothers and daughters, too, seek family counselors, psychologists, analysts, psychiatrists. Perhaps all of this expensive counseling is helpful, perhaps, sometimes, it is necessary. But maybe what mothers and daughters really need is a quiet hour with three pounds of garden-fresh green peas that need four hands to do the shelling.

SPRING DREAM SALAD DRESSING
Ida A. Isaacson

½ c. vegetable oil                    ½ c. light vinegar
⅓ c. sugar                           dash paprika
sprinkle of pepper                    1 green pepper (medium sized, finely
1 can tomato soup                       cut)
1 tsp. salt (or less for individual taste)  1 onion (size of small egg), minced
1 tsp. mustard (prepared)             ½ c. finely cut celery

Blend in your blender or mixmaster, or shake well in a bottle, all ingredients except the green pepper, onion, and celery. When the other ingredients are blended, toss in the cut green pepper, the minced onion, and the cut celery.

Keep in a covered jar in the refrigerator. This wonderful dressing is delicious with lettuce and sliced tomatoes, or lettuce alone. Any combination of salad vegetables is delectable with this dressing.
