HOME ACADEMY

[ILLUSTRATION]

What are you going to teach, Mother? Leora Larsen

■ School is out and the children are relieved and delighted. Or are they? Greg, who had just been promoted to the Fourth Grade, couldn't find anything to do, Gay, a pretty teenager, was collapsed on a chair in the kitchen with her hand in the cookie jar.

Where were their usually alert minds?

I could hear other children in the neighborhood. They were running and shouting outside. But my little boy had asthma, and my quiet teenager had a heart block that limited her activity. I could not watch them become unhappy.

"How would you like a school of your own?" I asked. "First, we will need a janitor to vacuum the carpet and set out chairs."

"I will!" said Greg.

"We should have music and a thought to start with."

Gay came from the kitchen. "We had Seminary first, and I really liked it last year."

"Why don't you be the teacher?" I asked.

"I will teach math," Greg offered. I smiled, for this was his difficult subject.

"Could I be the school cook, mother, and fix anything I want?" Gay asked. "And don't forget recess!" Greg shouted.
