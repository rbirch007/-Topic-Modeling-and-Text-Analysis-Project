[ILLUSTRATION: Drawing of an elderly man sitting in a large armchair with a dog lying at his feet]

"Mum, I have an idea." John turned suddenly to his mother. "Why don't we adopt Colonel Watkins as our Grandpa, if he will let us? We have only one and he lives a long way from us, and the colonel is all alone."

They all looked at the old man inquiringly. Suddenly he had turned rather red. His cold had come back it appeared as he blew hard into his handkerchief.

"And you will be our very own Grandpa, and tell us stories?" Peter's voice was shrill with delight. "Oh, boy!"

Trub, hearing the excited chatter, came bounding into the room to investigate, skidded to a halt by the colonel's chair, and laid a large paw on his knee.

"It seems that even Trub agrees," laughed the old man

with misty eyes. "Good old Trub, for without him, we might never have become friendly. With four mischievous boys and a delinquent dog, I'm certainly going to have my work cut out.

"Yes," he continued more seriously. "I would like to be your Grandpa and I'll never forget the honor you have paid me today. I only wish my dear wife was here beside me to have a second chance at happiness."

"That's settled, then," Jean said briskly, with a slight sniff. "Come on, chaps, there's work to be done and Grandpa's fire is burning low."

They all laughed, and with a contented sigh Colonel Watkins settled further down into his chair with Trub stretched out at his feet.
