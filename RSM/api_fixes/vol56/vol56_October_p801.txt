October 1969

BANNOCK STAKE (IDAHO) SINGING MOTHERS PRESENT
"LET MUSIC FILL THE EARTH," April 26, 1969

Front row, left to right: Audrey Patterson, chorister, Bannock Stake Relief Society; Florence Panier, pianist; Edsel Prescott, organist.

Ann P. Lloyd, President, Bannock Stake Relief Society, reports: "Our Singing Mothers presented the lovely musicale 'Let Music Fill the Earth,' by Lucelle R. Taylor. Narrating was Vivian Simmons, and the Statue of Liberty was portrayed by Donna Harris.

"The Singing Mother selections were highlighted by flute accompaniment, piano and vocal solos, violin and vocal duets, girls, children, and male choruses, a Cub Scout chorus, and a male quartet.

"We had much support in this production from the stake and ward officers, and this will be long remembered as an outstanding cultural event in our stake."

LIBERTY STAKE (SALT LAKE CITY, UTAH) RELIEF
SOCIETY HAS GOOD HOME STORAGE PROGRAM

Presidents of the wards and branches of Liberty Stake Relief Society, seated, left to right: Cleora Smith, South Harvard Ward; Ruth VanDerMayden, Ninth Ward; Edith Crosley, Harvard Ward; Thelma Olsen, Liberty Ward; Mary Bendsten, Danish Branch; Winifred Gran, Norwegian Branch; Michi Mano, Dai Ichi Branch; Catherine Romyn, Eighth Ward.

Back row, left to right: Marjorie Shepherd, Liberty Park Ward; Gwynne Smith, Second Ward; Nellie Hansen, stake Homemaking Counselor; Sarah McKee, Education Counselor; Deniece Starich, President.

Sister Starich reports: "We have been carrying forth a home storage program in our stake for several years. We encourage our sisters when shopping to 'buy a bag for themselves and a bag for the shelves.'

"We use the theme 'Plan to Can' regarding that which is in season. The Church cannery may be rented by any group, and they will order the produce or the group may bring its own. This group canned fresh pineapple from Mexico, and we have found that working together unites us in love and sisterhood and greatly aids the program."

OLYMPUS STAKE (UTAH) SINGING MOTHERS PERFORM AT
CONFERENCE, May 11, 1969

Front row, third and fourth from left: Lucile M. Salisbury, organist, Olympus Stake Relief Society; Carroll B. Smith, chorister.

Iola J. Peterson, President, Olympus Stake Relief Society, reports: "Our Singing Mothers were delighted to have the opportunity of singing at our stake quarterly conference. We deemed it especially appropriate since it was Mother's Day. Eighty-two sisters performed, singing 'The Lord Is My Light,' and 'Prayerful Hour.' "
