TEACHERS' TOPIC FOR JANUARY

THE RELIEF SOCIETY AND CHILD WELFARE

The Relief Society has always been interested in child welfare. From the very beginning of the organization, the sisters have been concerned with the care and protection of children. This interest has manifested itself in many ways—through the establishment of nurseries, the care of orphans, the promotion of better health conditions for children, and the education of mothers in child care.

In recent years this interest has taken on new phases. The Relief Society has cooperated with other agencies in promoting child welfare legislation. It has supported the establishment of juvenile courts, the passage of child labor laws, and the creation of agencies for the protection of dependent and delinquent children.
