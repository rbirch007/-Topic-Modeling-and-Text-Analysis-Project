Bless't are those numbered in this band So willing, tireless, steady; Who, like colonial minute-men By day or night are ready; A call may come of grief or pain Some heads bowed low by "Death, the Lurker," No matter what, she'll always go, Blest "Relief Society worker." And when she goes to her reward, - As all must do sometime or other, She'll find a crown bedecked with stars By those who loved her as a mother. The Father'll say, "This mansion bright We kept for those who never shirk; The faithful sisters here reside, Who did Relief Society work." i  Vol. IX APRIL, 1922 No. 4
