			IN MEMORIAM.
		“He giveth His beloved sleep.”
	Mrs. Emily B. Spencer departed this life July
3rd, 1906, aged 72 years, at Cannonville, Utah.
She was the wife of George Spencer, who de-
parted this life 1872. She was the mother of nine
children, four only survive her. Among them is
her oldest daughter, Emily, who married Prof.
Miles, now of Idaho, and Julia, who married Wil-
ford Woodruff, Jun, oldest son of the late Presi-
dent Woodruff, she died some years ago.
	The last years of her l[-]fe were sp[-]nt at Cannon-
ville, where she was an earnest worker in the
Sunday School, also secretary for the Relief
Society. She was beloved by all the sisters of the
society, and is greatly missed. She possessed the
gift of po try, and wrote many verses, most of
which have been published She was very strict
in keeping the Word of Wisdom, using neither
pork nor drinking hot drink-: she was widely
known in Southern Utah. Her nature was angelic
and especially to those that were cast down; her
religion was to her the dearest thing of her life;

[Column 2]
she retained her bright and noble mind to her
death; her memory was remarkable, also her eye-
sight, never using spectacles; she was one of the
most remarkable of women; she died as she had
lived, a faithful Latter-day Saint, and her light
will shine before her children both in time and
eternity. Her genial disposition won for her the
love of all with whom she was associated; God
grant that all may strive to emulate her.
				LETITIA M. SPENCER.

	Mrs. Louise Eddins Hales, wife of our esteemed
and veteran citizen George Hales, departed this
life on Tuesday, April 17, of general debility and
old age. She was born at Cradley, Herefordshire,
England, June 1st, 1834, arriving at Nauvoo the
same year. Here the family remained until the
exodus from that city in 1846. They remained in
Florence, Winter Quarters until the spring of
1848, when they proceeded on their journey,
reaching Salt Lake the same year.
	Mrs. Hales was married to George Hales about
1852, since which time she has been a resident of
Beaver. Sister Hales has endured many trials
and priv[-]tious since the early settlement of Utah,
and has ever remained true to the faith she
espoused; she was a kind and loving wife, a de-
voted mother and a good citizen. She was the
mother of eleven children, five of whom survive
her, she has twenty-four grandchildren and six
great grandchildren living.
	Funeral services were held in the L. D. S.
chapel, Thursday 19th inst. at which consoling re-
marks eulogistic of the life and character of the
deceased were made by Elders John Lang, George
Pace and R Maeser.—Beaver Weekly Press.
	Resolutions of respect to the memory of Annie
M. Rouche by the Relief Society of the Melville
Ward
	Whereas our Heavenly Father in His infinite
wisdom has seen fit to call home our beloved sis-
ter. and
	Whereas, the members of this association do
feel a personal loss in the death of our beloved
secretary, Annie M. Rouche, and regret that she
should be called home so soon.
	Whereas, her passing is as fruitful and as beau-
tiful as was her living, as she honored God, hon-
ored His holy priesthood, and so honored herself
and enjoyed to the fullest degree the spirit of her
ca ling, so frail, and yet so powerful in defence
of right, therefore be it
	Resolved that we strive to emulate her unwaver-
ing faith, her fidelity to purity and truth and her
devoti n o her home and religious duties and her
humi ity and respect and obedience to proper
au hority.
	Resolved, that we extend our heartfelt sympa-
thy and love to the bereft husband and children
and all other dear ones and sincerely pray our
Heavenly Father that He will fill their wounded
souls with the comforting influence of His Holy
Spirit that they may realize that she has only
gone before to enjoy the rest and reward of a
well sp[-]nt life That a copy of these resolutions
be given to the esteemed husband one to be sent
to the son “Les” who is fulfilling a mission in
New Zealand, one to the Womans Exponent and
one to be placed on our record.
	One less at home The charmed circle broken.
A dear face missed day by day from its accus-
tomed place. But purified and perfected by grace,
one more in heaven.
	President E. A. Farr, Counselors A. P. Ander-
son and M. A. Anderson, Assistant Secretary
Olive Scott.
