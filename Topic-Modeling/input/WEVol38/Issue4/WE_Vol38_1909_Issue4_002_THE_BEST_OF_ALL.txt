[WomansExponent | Vol38 | 1909 | October | Issue: 4 | Title: THE BEST OF ALL. | Author: Unknown]

THE BEST OF ALL.
Oh mothers remember when children are young,
While you list to the prattle of innocent tongue,
As you patiently answer the unending call,
That now is the happiest season of all.

Oh list to the voices so tender and sweet, 
Oh heed the soft patter of tottering feet,
Soothe gently the bruises that come from a fall,
And remember these days are the noblest of all.

When you hush them to sleep with your lullaby
	song,
Those moments of joy sweet mother prolong,
For time with its changes may come as a pall,
When they’ve slipped from your arms beyond
	your recall.

Then hold them secure in clinging embrace,
In the folds of your love to ever have place
Through sorrow, e’en sin, whatever befall
Encompassed by mother-love, constant through
	all.

For ah there will come, as moments flit on,
A time when the bliss of these days will be gone.
Fond memory only, all you can recall.
Of these hours that will e’er be the dearest of all.

Then breathe a soft prayer as you lull them to
	sleep,
That Angels their watch-care forever may keep,
He hears e’en the ravens—He heeds every call,
His love the sublimest divinest of all!
						ELLIS R. SHIPP, M. D.
