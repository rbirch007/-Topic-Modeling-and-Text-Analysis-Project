SHE HATH GONE.
	(Lines dedicated to Sister Lydia Clayton
Thatcher, deceased, by Grace Ingles Frost.)
	She hath gone, and though we miss her.
		Still we know she liveth on,
	In that land beyond the shadows,
		Where we all shall meet ere long.

	She hath gone, hath left all sorrow,
		Laid her heavy burdens down;
	Gone to meet her loved ones waiting.
		She hath triumphed won her crown.

	“Dead!” we sang, but, oh, ’tis error,
		She hath just begun to live,
	Called unto a higher mission,
		There her loving help to give.
	Shall we grieve o’er her, our sister?
		Death for such can have no sting.
	“She is with the ransomed numbered,”
		Hark! the Hosts of Heaven sing.

	She hath gone, our well beloved,
		Gone to forge within the chain, 
	One more link more strong to bind us
		To that life which knows not pain.


Let her then enjoy her blessings,
	Ah! drive not her light by tears,
She hath passed from woe to gladness,
	Laid aside all earthly fears,

“Victory! victory!” she singeth;
	Over death and o’er the grave,
Like a faithful beacon shining,
	Lives she on, the strong and brave.
