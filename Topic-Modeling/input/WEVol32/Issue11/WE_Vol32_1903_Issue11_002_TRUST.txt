TRUST.
	To be content,
		That is the best!
	Not to be indolent
		And simply rest,
But having done what duty calls to do,
And having been to your soul-impuls true,
Then worry not because you do not see
Results. Leave these unto the time to be.
Do what you can. Trust God for the event,
	And be content.
	To be content!
		Who in his soul
	Learns this accomplishment
		Has gained the whole.
He who can master self alone is great;
He who can work and likewise can wait,
Greatly deserve, and weary not the Lord
By constant intercessions for reward,
Know all will be returned that you have lent,
		And be content.
		To be content
			Is wisdom true!
		Know what for you is meant
			Will come to you.
Cry up the heights your motto, 'Onward, on!"
Then climb nor pause to count the distance
	gone.
Think not of self; but if some other soul
Is faint, assist and point him to the goal.
Thus mount and help until life's day is spent,
	And be content.
	To be content,
		This is the best!
	Life's sun and storm are blent,
		And both are blest
All glory love and joy that soul secures,
Who strives, and overcomes and who endures,
For God is all, and you with Him are rife-
Know this, and you have gained the goal of
	Life.
Take thou this blessing that is heaven-sent,
		And be content. 
						J. A. Edgerton.
