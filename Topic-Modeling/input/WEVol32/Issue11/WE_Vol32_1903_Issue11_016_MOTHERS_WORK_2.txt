MOTHERS' WORK.
PAROWAN STAKE.
Lecture I.—Pre-existence of spirits.
	Sing Hymn, “O My Father.” Give a
talk explaining the doctrine expressed in
the hymn. Speaker read the hymn, as proof
that we have a Father in Heaven. Prove
that all existed with God and His only be-
gotten Son before we came to this earth.
What was our condition in our pre-exis-
tant state?
	References.—John, 1 14. John 16, 28.
John 17th, 5. Heb. 12, 9. Matt. 6. 9. Job
38, 4 7. Num. 27, 16. Ecc 12, 7. John 3,
2. Jer 1, 4-5. Rev. 12, 7-9. As set forth in
“Woman who art thou, What is thy Mis-
sion, By Pres. John Taylor. Added upon.
Jude 6.
	Questions.—1. What proofs have we
that we have a Father and Mother in
Heaven? Read explanation on marriage in
Compendium, also Gen. 1, 7. 2. Had we
developed as thoroughly as we could in the
spirit world? 3. Did we all keep our first
estate?
Lecture 2.—Object of existence.
	(a) Whence we came, (b) Who we are,
(c) Where are we going? (d) Object is to
benefit humanity and to gain eternal life.
	References.—(a) Pearl of Great Price,
page 32. Alma 13, 3-7. Eph. 1, 3-4-5.
Tit. 1, 2. (b) John 3, 2. Heb. 12, 9. (c)
I Nephi, 28, 8. Doc. and Cov. 43, 44-45.
Ecc. 12, 7. Pearl of Great Price, page 22.
Rev. 20, 6. Doc. and Cov. 29, II. (d)
Multiply and replenish the earth. Gen. 1,
28.
	Questions.—Will all get back to our
Father’s home? Doc. and Cov. 29, 9-14-21.
What was the first commandment given to
Adam and Eve? When was it given? How

can we benefit humanity? How may we
gain eternal life.

Lecture 3.—Divinity of Parents Mission.

	Love and attributes as God planted them
in the human heart for a holy purpose. Mar-
riage a divine covenant for the propagation
of the race. Woman as a helpmate.
	References.—Read articles on marriage
(Com) John 5, 42. Tit. 3, 4. 1 Cor. 11, 11.
Doc. and Cov. Sec. 132. Sec. 49, 15-18.
1 Cor. 11, 3-12. Gen. 2, 24. Doc. and Cov.
Sec. 23. Gen. 2, 18.
	Questions.—Why is love implanted in
the human heart? 2. Is the mutual attrac-
tion of the sexes proper? 3 Enumerate the
ways in which women can be truly help-
mates. 4. When was woman divinely
chosen to be a mother? 5. What is truly
woman’s mission?

[-]ecture 4.

	(a) As mothers in Israel what manner
of women should we be? (b) Very prayer-
ful. (c) A sound mind in a sound body.
(d) Deportment to husband and children.
(e) Seek knowledge from all good sources,
truth, wisdom and the Holy Spirit. (f)
Motherhood a gift from God.
	References.—Doc and Cov. The prayer-
ful life by A. O. Woodruff, Young Wo-
man’s Journal Jan. 1[-]04. Eph. 5 chapter
22-25 vs.
	Questions.—1. What are some of the qual-
ifications necessary to be a true mother in
Israel? 2. Was the 25th Sec of the Doc.
and Cov. given for Emma Smith only? 3.
Should mothers devote their time entirely
to their home? 4. Why is it necessary to
keep our bodies sound and healthy? 5.
Why should we be careful in our deport-
ment around our home? 6. Is Motherhood
a gift from God?

Lecture 5—Prospective Mother.

	What shall we do to have our babies
healthy and happy? A life in harmony
with the gospel is the perfect example.
Discuss the physical needs of the body.
Freedom of dress, out door exercise, clean-
liness, food, fresh air and sunshine. Power
to guide and control oneself. Study well
one’s character before undertaking the grave
responsibility of Motherhood.
	Questions.—1. Give experiences what ef-
fect certain tendencies of the mother have
upon the child. 2. Does our temporal con-
dition effect our children? 3. To what ex-
tent should women exercise?

Lecture VI.

	Mental development, choice of literature.
The social life of the mother Spiritual de-
velopment, following the steps of the Sav-
ior. Parents may have the kind of spirits
they are worthy of.
	Questions.—1. What course should a
mother take in order that her child may
have the proper development? 2. What do
you think of mothers going into society
during this period? 3. Did the Roman and
Grecian nations honor prospective mothers?
4. Were children welcome by the Jewish
nation?

Lecture VII Care of the Infant.

	The baby’s wardrobe. How to care for
the infant. Discuss ventilation and sleep-
ing apartments. The baby’s food, when to
feed and quantity. Baby’s bath, baby’s ills
and how to treat them.
	Questions.—1. How should baby be

[Column 2]
dressed? 2. Why have flannel next to the
skin? Should the clothes be long or short?
3. Do you think a child should be fed at
regular intervals? 4. Why should a child
be bathed and how often? 5. Should a
baby learn to creep before it walks? 6.
Should a child be given water often? 7.
When the mother’s milk is insufficient,
what artificial food do you think best?

Lecture VIII.—First Years of the Child.

	Obedience is heavens first law. As the
twig is bent so the tree inclines. “Too
much indulgence has spoiled many a child,
too much love not one. Love brought the
child into the world and the more love we
give them the more we have for ourselves.”
“Love begets love.” Home is the child’s
world. Keep it uppermost in our
minds. One of the first steps is to gain
the child’s confidence. Habits are formed
in infancy.
	Questions.—What lessons of obedience
should a child learn in the first five years of
its life? 2 Illustrate love and indulgence.
3. In what way would we have more love
if we gave more? 4. Should we gratify the
child’s wishes about pleasure and outings?
5. How can we gain a child’s confidence?
6.What habits are formed in infancy?

Lecture IX.—Physical Development.

	Teach the child proper attitudes. Observ-
ance of the Word of Wisdom is essential to
physical development. The rights of chil-
dren in the home. Pay strict attention to
questions and give suitable answers. To
put children off or to send them away, what
effect does it have on the child’s character
	Questions.—1. What are proper attitudes.
2. How shall we entertain our children in
the home? 3. Should each child have some
part in keeping up the home? 4. What
effect has responsibility on character? 5.
How far should the freedom of a child ex-
tend in the home? 5. Why are idle mo-
ments dangerous to the child?

Lesson X.—Mental, Moral and Social
	Development.

	Overcome evil with good. Encourage
virtue and vice will disappear. Selection of
good books and companions should be
taught early in life. A mother’s influence.
The part a mother takes in the forming of
character cannot be over-estimated. It is a
social duty to be cleanly of speech, person
and dress.

	True politeness is to do and say,
	The kindest thing in the kindest way.

	Questions.—1. How can we overcome
evil with good? 2. Illustrate how we can
encourage virtue? 3. Name some good
books suitable for children of all ages. 4.
llustrate how a mother helps to form a
child’s character. 5. Why should we be
clean of speech, person and dress?

Lecture XI.—Spiritual Development.

	Each day should properly begin with
prayer. Peace, love and contentment come
from within. Christ the true ideal. One
life which embodies all the virtues, should
be made the example in the life of all. Give
special religious training in order that hon-
or for the priesthood may be fostered. Why
should the lesson of obedience be one of the
foundation stones of character building.
How is obedience connected with our spir-
tual progress?
	Questions.—1. Why should each day begin
with prayer? 2. Do favorable temporal sur-

[Column 3]
roundings make us contented and happy?
3. How do parents constantly teach morals
good and bad to their children? 4. How
soon does a child have a moral sense? 5.
What special religious training should be
given in order that children may obey or
honor the Priesthood?

Lecture XII.— The Development of the
	Child to Man and Womanhood.

	Teach the child the sacred laws of life be-
fore he gains the knowledge from evil
minds. Be sure you have his confidence
and then pay strict attention to every need.
Assist the children in choosing suitable
companions and books. During the devel-
opment period the boy and girl should be
tenderly and wisely guarded.
	Questions—Do you think children should
be taught the sacred laws of life, and how
soon? How can we gain their confidence,
and what are their needs? How can we as-
sist our children in choosing their compan-
ions and books? To what extent should
our boys and girls be allowed to participate
in dancing and other exciting pastime?
