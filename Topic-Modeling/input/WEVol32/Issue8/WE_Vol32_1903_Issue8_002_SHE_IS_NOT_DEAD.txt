SHE IS NOT DEAD.
Lines commemorative of the centennial birthday
		of Eliza R. Snow Smith.
	Born January 21, 1804—Died December 5, 1887.

She is not dead—the tunefnl bard who sung
	For Zion’s children in these latter days
She is not dead, whose harp the angels strung,
	Whose lyre resounded with immortal lays.

She is not dead, whose words of living fire
	Enthuse and keep so many hearts aglow,
Whose melodies awaken pure desire
	And help the seeds of righteousness to grow.

She is not dead, whose utterance was replete.
	With timely counsel for the old and young.
Whose wise reproof, blent with compassion sweet.
	Warmed and revivified the faltering one. 

She is not dead, who toiled for Zion’s cause,
	Truth’s advocate! endowed with noble zeal,
Truly her fixed determination was
	To do on earth our heavenly Father’s will.

The cheering strains her tuneful soul outpoured.
	Pure streamlets, fresh from inspiration’s spring,
Delight and consolation oft afford.
	Joy to appreciative souls they bring. 

Just now her friendly face we cannot see,
	But none the less fond mem’ry holds her dear,
She lives! She lives and sings rejoicingly.
	With many loved ones in a loftier sphere.
						EMILY H. WOODMANSEE,
Salt Lake City.
