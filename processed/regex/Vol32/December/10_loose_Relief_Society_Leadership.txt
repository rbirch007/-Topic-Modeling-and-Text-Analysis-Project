A change in general Relief Society leadership occurred April 6, 1945, with the release by the First Presidency of Sister Amy Brown Lyman and her General Board. The same day, Belle S. Spafford was sustained as the new General President of Relief Society.
New Name for Relief Society
We begin the work of this new era with a new name being officially adopted for the Society. By action of the General Board of Relief Society, June 27, 1945, the name of the Society was officially changed from "National Woman's Relief
Society" to "Relief Society of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints."
Restoration of Union Meetings
This is also a day of restoration for Relief Society. Union meetings have been restored to us allowing stake officers to once more function normally. Visits to stakes by General Board members have once more been authorized by the First Presidency.
We urge that in the conduct of union meetings every possible effort be made to make them as beneficial to ward workers as possible. Let your preparation for these meetings be thorough and keep in mind the two great purposes of union meetings; namely, to aid wards toward fine performance in all phases of Relief Society work and to assist in developing ward leadership. Stake leaders are cautioned to do nothing that will hinder the ward in standing on its own feet.
New Recommendations
The General Board recommends that except when a stake counselor is needed in the department over which she has general supervision, or unless she feels a special need to conduct a department for ward counselors, that she attend the department conducted for presidents. Ward counselors should also attend the presidents' department unless a special department is being conducted for them.
