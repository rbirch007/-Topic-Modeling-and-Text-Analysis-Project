Woman's Sphere Ramona W. Cannon WITH the dawning of 1945 come the greatest opportunities of the ages for women. Peace-planning must be as careful and exhaustive as warplanning has been, or the world's tears will not cease to flow. It has always been woman's role to heal wounds; given the chance, she might do much to prevent them. Today's world needs tenderness, patience, the human mother-touch. But it can also use the intellects and skills of its countless, highly trained women to carry out the mandates of their hearts and their consciences. A recent visitor in the United States was Madame Tsai Kwei, national secretary of China's Y. W. C. A. Graduate of Ginling College, Nanking, Master of Arts from Columbia University, New York, author, editor of the Chinese national magazine for the Y. W. C. A., translator of H. G. Wells' Short History of the World, into Chinese. She is a leader in the Chinese-Christian movement, and in relief work for refugees.
