Woman's Sphere Ramona W. Cannon A host of relatives and friends mourn the passing of Flora Ridge Hicks, who died August 24, 1945, in Scottsdale, Arizona. Of English birth, Flora came to America in her late teens already a prominent singer. Ten years ago Sister Hicks accompanied her husband to Scottsdale, where he presided over the Indian (Papago) Ward. Flora taught the Indians hygiene, nutrition, cooking, nursing. A soloist, accompanist, composer, and director, she organized quartettes, choruses, and a ward choir. Her service to the Church was outstanding and ardent. PROFESSOR EDWARD E. THORNDIKE, psychologist of Columbia University, after extensive studies in comparative ratings of states along many lines, says that Utah and Massachusetts take the lead in producing the most smart men and women per million population. Many Utah women help to bear out the above statement. MRS. DONNA EARLE PARKINSON, who has returned to Salt Lake to make her home, has been for some years an instructor in the department of speech and dramatics in Vassar College, also director of the radio workshop there. She has filled important dramatic roles on national radio networks and is NOW appearing in radio programs originating in Salt Lake. MRS ALENE DALTON wrote a song for her sorority, "Mom Says Not to Worry." Mrs. Dalton also wrote the lyrics for My Picture Book of Songs, in which Myriel Ash-ton and Erla Young, also Utah girls, collaborated with music and illustrations. Donahue of Chicago, publisher of Raggedy Ann and Raggedy Andy, is bringing out the volume. MISS RUTHELLA WADE, University of Utah alumna, is editor of Dance Magazine, published in New York. TOP Utah moving-picture stars are Loretta Young, Laraine Day, Jean Sullivan. MRS. ELNA JEAN ANDERSON " " HANSON of Salt Lake, only twenty-three, was in great demand as a Powers model in New York, and last season, while understudy to the leading lady in "Mexican Hayride," had many opportunities to sing the role. Audiences were enthusiastic. MISS HELEN FOSTER OF Cedar City, while writing in the Orient, married foreign correspondent Edgar Snow.
