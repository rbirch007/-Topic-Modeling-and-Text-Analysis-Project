Subscriptions—1948 _ The subscription figure for December 31, 1948 (77,740) shows a loss of 3,238 from the subscription figure of December 31, 1947 (80,978). However, more than half of the subscription total for 1947 represented subscriptions taken out at the old subscription price of $1, and the total as of December 1948 reflects subscriptions at the new price of $1.50. Therefore, the general board feels that the Magazine representatives have worked exceptionally hard during 1948 and are deserving of special credit in having so. Successfully established the Magazine at the new price. Page 320 Because of carrying so many subscriptions during 1948 at the old subscription price, the general board regrets to report that the Magazine showed a small deficit at the end of 1948. However, it is confidently expected that one year from now the subscription figure will reveal a substantial increase and that the Magazine will be entirely self-supporting. For the full realization of these expectations, the general board enlists the continued efforts of the officers and Magazine representatives during the year 1949.
