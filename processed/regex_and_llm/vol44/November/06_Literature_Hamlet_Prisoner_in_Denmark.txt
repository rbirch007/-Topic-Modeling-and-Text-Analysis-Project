Only the written word gives students the solid background that underlines all real culture. | Besides reading low books that injure our young people's morals, there are other problems which influence them against leading a clean life. Three groups in Philadelphia in 1951 decided to solve those problems before they had a good start. These groups are: First, parents, Second, students, and Third, the school personnel. The problems are: (1) Unreasonable late hours, (2) drinking alcoholic beverages, (3) abuse of car privileges, (4) lack of parental responsibility im chaperoning parties in the homes, (5) a series of home parties after late school functions, (6) party crashing, (7) iappropriate dress, (8) a general disregard for the rights and property of others, (9g) parental acceptance of newer RELIEF SOCIETY MAGAZINE—NOVEMBER 1957 and unsafe freedoms as a trend of the times. After deep study by these three groups of these nine problems, the result was a booklet called "We Can Agree." Every family has one, also every new student. Conditions are improving especially in the area of appropriate dress and party hours. These ideas of agreement in improvement of the young are now spreading to other states and the P. T. A. Is becoming interested. May these ideas be adopted by the whole United States of America so that our morals may be something of which we may be proud. These movements are good, but there is nothing so efficacious as confidence between a wise mother and a willing - to - listen - and - learn sweet young girl. Mother, get close to your daughter. Gain her confidence so that she will have a desire to talk to you after the show, after the dance, after the ride. A few words of wisdom on such occasions may change her desires to read the wrong book, to associate with the wrong boy, to make the wrong marriage. Begin very early with your little girl to build a strong character that she may be able to resist uncleanliness whether physical, mental, or moral. Then, if gentleness, kindness, love, cleanliness, and morality exist in the home, she can look upon the wickedness of the world in disdain and disgust at the low thoughts on the screen and in publications and they will influence her character not one whit. Teach her that according to The Book of Mormon "... Neither can filthiness or anything which is unclean be received into the kingdom of God " (Alma 7:21).
