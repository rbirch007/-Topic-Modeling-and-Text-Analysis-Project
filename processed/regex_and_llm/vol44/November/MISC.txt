Thanksgiving Song Margery S. Stewart For all the fields from east to west That poured their goldness into bread, For all the rivers and the brooks, That sent their beauty down to me, For men and women whose quiet hands Wove warmth and cover against storms, For doctors I will never see Who nightly strained their hearts and minds Uncovering secrets that will be Armor against tomorrow' ills, For all these, Lord, my thanks to thee. For men in mines and men in planes, For men who govern this free land, For nurses rustling in the dark To where an ailing baby cries, For those whose songs fulfilled a need, For teachers holding high their lamps Who do not shrink nor compromise, I give my brimming gratitude, My love for each high enterprise.
