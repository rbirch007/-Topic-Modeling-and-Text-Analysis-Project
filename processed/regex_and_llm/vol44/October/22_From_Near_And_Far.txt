Yost's "Society Sonnets" I was indeed pleased to see Edna Day's fine article "A Nursery Will Be Maintained" in the June issue of the Magazine. I especially lked the sonnet ending. I also enjoyed Frances Yost's story "Society Sonnets" in the August issue. I am grateful to Edna Day for many things. It was she and another friend and neighbor, Fern McClellan, and, of course, my old friend Shakespeare, who nudged me to go to Relief Society. Edna is the literature teacher, and her lessons are especially apt. She had us make Shakespeare scrapbooks with some _pictures and mimeographed "bests" from the plays as we studied them. Then we went into sonnet writing, and some of us wrote both Christmas and Easter sonnets. 'The sonnets were added to our scrapbooks, too, because, of course, we had a sonnet section. —Mrs. Adah Lempke Idaho Falls, Idaho As a Relief Society literature teacher, I wish to express appreciation for Frances in the August Magazine, and the editorial "Pen in Hand" by Sister Crawford in the May issue. She also used many of the excellent poems published in the Magazine. —Elsie C.
