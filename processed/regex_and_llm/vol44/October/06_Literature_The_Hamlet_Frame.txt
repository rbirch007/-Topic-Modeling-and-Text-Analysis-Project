Music in the home is of vital importance and the Family Hour can be the culmination of talents and interest in this delightful medium—who doesn't like to sing? Just a good family song-fest from "Here's a Ball for Baby" to part singing of loved and familiar songs is a wonderful way to spend an hour at home. Children should be encouraged to lead the singing and also to teach new songs to the group. Many special Primary, Sunday School, M. I. A., and Relief Society songs can be taught to the entire family for all to enjoy. Folk songs and ballads, as well as songs of other nations, all have universal appeal. For example, plan a Family Hour centered around an opera, and aim to tread the story all members of the family with it. Study the life of the composer and then listen and seek to capture the emotional character of the music. The simplest of all dance action is portrayed in the little singing games common to children. Most homes can push the chairs back and make room for this type of delightful activity. One Family Hour evening could be spent in which the children might enjoy folk dances and singing games. It may be spent in teaching family members to foxtrot, waltz, tango, etc., and nothing is more spirited and heartwarming than the revival of square and round dancing. 'The older members could profitably teach the younger some of the old-fashioned dances, and the younger generation could take pride in reciprocating by teaching Mom and Dad some of the modern dances. 'The fine dance music now recorded makes the phonograph serviceable for social dancing in the home where there are older children. Visits to art exhibits, the library, radio station, newspaper plant, poultry farm, sugar factory, soft drink bottling plant, potato chip factory, observatory, museum, or a picnic in the park take on a new interest when all the family join in. Encourage your family to take advantage of these opportunities to spend a delightful Family Hour away from home. A few missionaries were sent to assist President Evans, and for four years the work of proselyting was carried on in a rather limited manner, with the Evans home in Shiprock serving as the mission headquarters.
