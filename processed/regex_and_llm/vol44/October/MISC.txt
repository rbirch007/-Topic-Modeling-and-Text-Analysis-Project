Enola Chamberlin Let us speak softly in this autumn air, Lest we disturb the orchard's loose-held leaves, Or send to ground the last late-hanging pear, Or fright the swallow clinging to the eaves. Lest we disturb the robin now at rest Before his flight to reach the summer south; Disturb the chipmunk on his acorn quest, The squirrel, a pilfered nut within his mouth. Let us speak softly, corn is in the shock; The yellow pumpkins piled across the field, The frost has drawn back his hand to knock, To hammer loudly on the summer's shield. Let us speak softly, nature waits to know If all the earth is ready for her snow.
