But my heart leaps beyond the logical, the human, the intellectual, the social, or even the moral, to recognize the divinity of the record of the Nephites and the Jaredites. Upon Mount Sinai, Moses received two tables of stone written on both sides by the finger of God. 'Though actually engraved by human prophets, the ancient American plates just as surely had divine endorsement, for I know that Nephite and Jaredite fingers were guided in their writing by the inspiration of the Holy Spirit; yet the ancient American historians, in deepest humility, more than once expressed their feelings of 821 inadequacy for the tremendous task assigned them. Moroni, in_ particular, acknowledged his imperfection as a literary man: his awkwardness of hands in writing, his stumbling in the placing of words. Fools may mock, as Moroni feared, but they shall surely mourn. 'Though inscribed by fingers, faltering at times, because of the Christ-like humility of the writers, 'The Book of Mormon rings forth with the living word of God. This knowledge of the divinity of The Book of Mormon came to me neither easily nor miraculously. As the result of an earnest desire to know, as the result of patient study to gain understanding, and as the result of fervent prayer for guidance and enlightenment, the revealing power of the Holy Ghost was gradually exercised in my behalf. One blessed day, I knew that in the early nineteenth century an untutored farm boy (doubtless chosen before the world was, for his tremendous mission) had been raised up by the Lord to restore to the earth the gospel of Jesus Christ in its purity and truth. I knew, as I know that I live, that the ancient Nephite record translated into English by Joseph Smith and published as 'The Book of Mormon was in very truth another witness for Christ. Since that happy day when a sure testimony entered my soul, I have felt flooded with light, light sufficient to brighten my way as I walk through the darkness of mortality, sufficient to cast a reassuring gleam along. The pathway toward eternal life. Nor do I regret that neither mitaculous manifestation nor startling 822 RELIEF SOCIETY MAGAZINE—DECEMBER 1957 discovery proved to be the foundation of my testimony. I can testify to the truthfulness of 'The Book of Mormon far more surely than if one of the Three Nephites had borne witness at my door; more surely than if, somewhere in the Americas, a stela had been unearthed bearing carved figures and decipherable inscriptions which read as follows: Lehi, Sariah, Laman, Lemuel, Sam, Nephi; more surely than if recently in the Hill Cumorah in New York there had been accidentally discovered some gold plates, curiously engraved with characters which scientists had identified as ancient Egyptian. Yes, I have a testimony far stronger than that founded upon miracles or upon the corroborating support of external evidence. 'Through the enlightenment of the Holy Ghost, I feel as if a testimony has permeated every fiber of my being—a testimony that Jesus of Nazareth is the Christ, that Joseph Smith is a true prophet of God, that The Book of Mormon is the Biblical stick of Joseph. Now, the stick of Judah and the stick of Joseph are one in my hand and one in my love. May the Lord graciously look beyond my inadequate words to read the gratitude written in my heart for the testimony which I have gained! Well do I understand the wise words of King Benjamin. Though I should thank the Lord and serve him all the days. Of my life, I should still be an unprofitable servant because of the magnitude of the gifts which he offers me. I can only bow in humble devotion before the glory and the wonder of it all. With the strengthening of my testimony has come a greater peace of soul than I had ever previously enjoyed. A calming realization has dawned. Disappointments and failure to achieve goals set by human ambitions and desires will continue to test my faith, from time to time. But, if I sincerely and prayerfully attempt to obey, all the laws and ordinances of the gospel of Jesus Christ, I have faith that I shall live to fulfill the mission of my creation in the divine plan of God. More I ask not.
