Polio and the Ilarch of (Dimes Basil O'Connor President, The National Foundation for Infantile Paralysis HE March of Dimes wants to finish the fight against polio. To do this, we must have your help — just as we had it in reaching the present hopeful position. It's hopeful because final victory is now in sight. Thanks to the Salk vaccine, which was financed with March of Dimes funds. Tomorrow's children will be spared the tragedy of polio. Thousands of today's children. However, still suffer from this crippling disease. There are many thousands for whom the victory is not so quick, and not so easy. These people still suffer from polio, and they need much more than an inoculation. They need mechanical substitutes for arms and legs and lungs. They need help i in rebuilding shattered lives. Most of all, they need the hope which comes from knowing that they do not fight alone. We can't quit—we know you would not want us to quit — until these youngsters have had a chance to run and play again; a chance to grow up as strong and useful citizens for the vears ahead. The money in your pocket will help children to walk without braces. And, what is more, vour dimes and dollars will help train the minds and hands of the professional experts so desperately needed in the treatment of crippled patients. Tens of thousands born too soon for the vaccine still need your help, and your help is needed to help finance research to perfect the vaccine.
