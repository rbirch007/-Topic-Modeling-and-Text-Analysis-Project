The women picked up the fiber and wire, the stems and leaves, and started to fashion the flowers they had learned to make during a winter craft class. They had scarcely begun, however, when the door opened and May came bustling in, cheery and efficient, ready to do her full share of the task. Helen, seeing her this way, recalling what a good worker she was, felt her throat swell with pain at the thought of what lay ahead. Mercifully, though, she knew that May was unaware of their plan.
