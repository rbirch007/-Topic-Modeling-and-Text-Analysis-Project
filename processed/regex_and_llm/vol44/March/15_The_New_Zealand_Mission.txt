Ballif VIEW OF THE GREEN HILLS OF NEW ZEALAND The plan met with success, and, in 1883, a branch of twenty-seven members was established in the Waotu settlement, with Harn T. Katera as president. The Book of Mormon was translated into the Maori language in 1887 by Elders Ezra F. Richards and Sonda Sanders, assisted by several educated natives. At the close of 1887 there were 2,573 members of the Church in New Zealand, 2,243 of whom were Maoris. The Australasian Mission was divided in 1897 and New Zealand was made a separate mission. At the end of 1930 there were 7,256 members in the mission. In 1913 an Agricultural College was established by the Church in 167 New Zealand, which has been of great benefit to the young Maori members. President David O. McKay, the first President of the Church to visit New Zealand, announced after his return to Salt Lake City in February 1955 that a temple would be built in that land. Today the New Zealand Mission is in a prosperous condition. 'There are 14,630 members, located in sixtyeight branches. Ariel S. Ballif is the mission president. In December 1955, seventy Relief Society organizations were reported with 998 members. Arta R. Ballif presides over the New Zealand Mission Relief Society.
