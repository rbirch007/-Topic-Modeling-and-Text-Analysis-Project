Work ITleeting—Living More Abundantly (A Course Recommended for Use by Wards and Branches at Work Meeting) Lesson 3—Living Economically Elder William F. Edwards For Tuesday, December 10, 1957 Objective: To understand that wise people, whether their income is relatively large or small, learn that it is good to live economically, because it is a good way of life. HE previous lesson discussed the importance of budget planning. We have all observed in others, or have been tempted sufficiently to learn by experience, that: One sure way to make life miserable is to live in a manner that you can't afford (Richard L. Evans). On the other hand, we have also come to realize that: If there is any one thing that will bring peace and contentment into the human heart, and into the family, it is to live within our means (Heber J. Grant). The Importance of Economy Living economically is a_ basic fundamental for happiness and success that is logically related to budgets, and it is equally important for all, rich and poor alike. The writer will always remember his first trip with members of his family to Victoria, Canada. In the government building in this beautiful city these words are on display: Without economy none can be rich; With it none can be poor. Two ancient proverbs re-emphasize this important principle of successful living: Eat and drink, but waste not by excess, for God loveth not the wasters (Abrabian Proverb). No gain is more certain than that which proceeds from the economical use of what we have (Latin Proverb).
