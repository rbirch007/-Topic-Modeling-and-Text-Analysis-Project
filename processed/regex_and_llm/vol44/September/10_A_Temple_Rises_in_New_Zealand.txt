From my apartment, I could hear singing from the early morning hours until long after the stars and the moon were shining brightly at night. How these people love to sing as they work! Yes, they are a chosen people. THE temple is located on the North Island of New Zealand. It is about three miles west of the town of Frankton Junction, which is near the city of Hamilton. It is on the most prominent site of the Church property, and the main axis of the building extends east and west and is parallel with the main highway running on the west side of the temple, which is the Pirongia Highway. According to Edward O. Anderson, architect, entrance to the temple grounds is by way of 'Temple Drive, which extends off Pirongia Highway at a ninety-degree angle. The drive has a rise of twenty feet from the highway, and the ground level of the temple is thirty-eight feet above the Drive, on a beautiful green rolling knoll, with native woodland nearby. 'This gives the temple a very imposing setting and makes it the most important and impressive building in the area. The temple is of reinforced concrete and concrete block construction with cast stone trim, most of which was manufactured on the site by the native missionaries. It is built in three main floor levels, which floors are expressed in the elevation design.
