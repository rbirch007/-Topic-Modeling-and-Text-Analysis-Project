While Apart Sylvia Lustig Bennion Reason would console With words: "'Love knoweth no distance,"' It is true. And, a never-shoring ocean, Though tides are felt My love has no boundary From you. Yet, you are gone. The miles, no consequence, For just that | cannot hear Your voice, your song, Nor see your comely countenance, | am gripped with loneliness. And, here ail logic seems ill, For the heart, womb of love, Has no will. And it shall not be satisfied With words oversimplified; It Knows well the distance From you.
