Combing a Granddaughter's Hair Christie Lund Coles Like red-brown wings, the touch. Transports me backward to When | combed your mother's hair, How it lifted, flew Like softest milkweed fluff Blown by the wind, in sun, So warm, so soft, oh, child, Thus is a memory begun: Her young impatience, too, Her fair face lifted, turned; Her cheek close to my own. Such is a memory learned. And then, your smile of pride, Your kiss with love distilled; Your arms encircling me Thus is a hope fulfilled.
