To an Eight-Year-Old Dorothy J. Roberts Isn't it ridiculous, Your being so far away — A river and a chain of hills To part me from your play? Jt is unbelievable, A lake, a field of grain, A desert and a row of elms Can cause a year of pain. Isn't it incongruous, With jets to shrink the hours That we are separated by A line of gold sunflowers, A duty and a dollar and A cloud that may bring snow — When | long to see you And to hold you, so!
