Sharp Statistics on the number of subscribers to The take on meaning as letters come constantly to the General Board from subscribers who express appreciation for what the contents of the Magazine mean to them. Every phase of a woman's life which the General Board seeks to strengthen through the pages of the Magazine, comes in for praiseworthy comment. The spiritual, intellectual, practical, and beautifying values of articles receive individual commendation. In addition, the worth of the Magazine as a proselyting and. Fellowshipping means is hailed. In the stakes and missions, past issues of the Magazine have been gathered and distributed to nonsubscribers and nonmembers as an inviting introduction to the world-wide sisterhood, with excellent results. It is well to reflect that behind every subscriber is the faithful and devoted work of a representative and the encouraging guidance of a Relief Society president in the wards, stakes, missions, and on the General Board. President Spafford, who was the editor of the Magazine from 1937 to 1945, as the General Manager, keeps a careful watchcare over all phases of the Magazine-production from the initial planning to the colorful printed Magazine. The devotion on the part of the editorial staff is dedicated as is the devotion of the more than 6,800 Magazine representatives in the Church who go in good and bad weather as faithful emissaries of the Relief Society to spread the work of Relief Society through the printed word. The Magazine representatives go both to the crowded city dweller and to the lonely outpost of the bush. They serve in such places as New York and London, to the wives of servicement in Okinawa, Germany, Spain, and elsewhere, and to South Africa. The Magazine is welcomed by the elderly as well as by the young mother surrounded by her growing children, by the bride, and by the expert housekeeper.
