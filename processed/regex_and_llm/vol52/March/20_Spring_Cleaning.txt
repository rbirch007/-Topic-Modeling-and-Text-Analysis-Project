Spring Cleaning Viola Ashton Candland March winds are sweeping the hills Today, cleaning up the snow Still packed in crevices And hollows, loath to go. March winds are sweeping the hills, Leaving them bare and cold, Huddled behind the fields Of faded stubble-gold — Naked, weatherbeaten hills Caught shivering between A February blizzard And an April breath of Spring. Just waiting for the sun's long Fingers to coax the unseen Dormant life to wake and stretch And lift pale, folded heads of green, Seeking the comfort of warm sun Caressing bud and leaf. This year, Mother Nature, Could your cleaning time be brief?
