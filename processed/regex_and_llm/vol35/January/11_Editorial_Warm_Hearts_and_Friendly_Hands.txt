"Warm Hearts and Friendly Hands"

Loyalty to the teachings of the gospel shown by one's daily conduct, loyalty to one's family, loyalty to friends and those placed in Church positions of authority, bring to a person the blessings of heaven. In the midst of trials and afflictions, how sweet, how solacing is the loyalty of friends. When the Prophet Joseph Smith was a prisoner in Liberty Jail, the Lord spoke these comforting words: "Thy friends do stand by thee, and they shall hail thee again with warm hearts and friendly hands. Thou art not yet as Job; thy friends do not contend against thee neither charge thee with transgression as they did Job (D&C 121:9-10)." It is a priceless gift to be able to rely on the loyalty of one's associates, to be certain that they will remain staunch and true in adversity. Every president of the Church has been surrounded by associates who would have been loyal to the death. That is an attribute of the Lord's apostles.

In any Church position to which a woman may be called, she owes full loyalty to those placed over her and to those with whom she labors. One of the reasons that the Relief Society is different from every other woman's organization on the face of the earth, is that the leaders are called by men bearing the Holy Priesthood, and seekers of positions are not called. When a woman is chosen for a position, she may expect the full loyalty of all the women with whom she works.

It is a sorrowful thing for a woman to be disloyal to those placed over her, or to be disloyal to those whom she professes to befriend. Those who would dwell in the presence of the Lord and belong to the Church of the Firstborn "see as they are seen, and know as they are known." A sincere woman shuns associating with women whom she knows, from past experience, are disloyal to friends and associates. She knows that each person will have to answer! For every idle word spoken. Said Jesus, "But I say unto you, that every idle word that men shall speak, they shall give account thereof in the day of judgment" (Matt. 12:30).

As a woman increases in years she should increase also in all righteousness. Her appearance, more and more, comes to mirror the spirit within. Sweetness and goodness tend to illumine and refine. At the beginning of this new year, each Relief Society member will earn the approbation of the Lord if she keeps a stricter curb on her tongue, builds up her sisters, and proves her loyalty to friends and principles through her daily living.

M. C. S. Yotes
