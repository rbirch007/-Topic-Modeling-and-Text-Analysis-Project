Magazine Subscriptions for 1947
Counselor Marianne C. Sharp Hanks and gratitude are extended to the officers, Magazine representatives, and to all subscribers of The for the excellent work which has been done throughout 1947 in obtaining a subscription of 80,978. Faithful Magazine representatives have been blessed for the long hours of work which this record has required.

Increase in Magazine Subscriptions
Particular appreciation is felt for the way in which the number of subscriptions has been maintained and even increased by 420 subscriptions over 1946, in view of the increase in subscription price to $1.50 as of July 1947. Once more Relief Society members have demonstrated their loyalty to the Society and appreciation for the Magazine.

Improvement in Content
The general board is constantly endeavoring to improve the content of the Magazine and to give to Latter-day Saint women the opportunity of improving their writing skills, and have their work preserved through the pages of the woman's magazine of the Church. It seeks to impart a message of spirituality, so much needed in the world today, to promote the work of Relief Society and publish Relief Society lessons, as well as offering reading enjoyment and articles of special interest to the homemaker.

New Rates for Some Foreign Missions
In order to put the Magazine on a sound financial basis, it was found necessary to increase the subscription rate to $2 for Australia, Great Britain, Europe, New Zealand, and South Africa, because of postal rates.

This is true of Australia which has been in the forefront for so many years.

The general board sincerely regrets the circumstances which have necessitated the increased rate of $2, and is sympathetic to the increased burden thus placed upon them.

Modernizing of Mailing
There has been a marked improvement in the last six months in the mailing of the Magazine to subscribers.
A modern system is being installed and prompt delivery from the printers has resulted in the Magazines being in the mail before the month of issue.
This has greatly decreased the amount of correspondence and resulted in improved relationships between the representatives and subscribers.

Percentages for 1948 to Be Based on January Enrollment
Effective for 1948, instead of the membership figures on which the Honor Roll percentages will be based being counted as of December 1948, they will be counted as of January 1948.
This will allow the Magazine representatives to obtain from the secretary, at once, the membership figures of January 1948 on which her Magazine percentage for 1948 will be figured.
In this way a Magazine representative will know all through the year the number of subscriptions she must obtain in order to be on the 1948 Honor Roll.
