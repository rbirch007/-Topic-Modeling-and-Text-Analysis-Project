ALPINE STAKE (UTAH), AMERICAN FORK SEVENTH WARD RELIEF SOCIETY RAG BEE, January 29, 1948 Sister Alice Carlisle, President, Alpine Stake Relief Society, has submitted an account from the American Fork Seventh Ward which well illustrates the zeal of Relief Society workers: "The room was a beehive of activity as thirty members assembled for a regular old-fashioned rag bee. First, a fashion show of the clothing that was to be cut or torn in strips for the rugs was held. Needless to say, there was a great deal of merriment. The coats of the women were safely hung on hangers on a rack so that none of them would meet the fate of the coat belonging to the lady from Lehi, who, when weaving rugs for her own ward, discovered that her own coat had been cut up into strips. In all, thirty-one balls of rags were sewed, as well as a large box of rags torn, which were later sewed by the sisters in their homes. In keeping with the occasion, homemade cinnamon rolls and grape juice were served as refreshments by Sister Froelick and Sister Wall. Cleone Cleghorn is ward Relief Society president, with Violet Varney and Lena Storrs as counselors, and Jennie Greenwood as secretary. Photograph submitted by Cleona W. Hedenstrom
