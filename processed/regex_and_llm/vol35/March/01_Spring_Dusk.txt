SPRING DUSK Christie Lund Coles The gray, madonna-hooded hills Lie still tonight, And far as eye can see, the sky Is platinum bright. The crystal air is petal-sweet, And as I pass Brief overtones wake in the trees And in the grass. The sky-born wedge of birds once more Slants near my door, While emerald green, the satin buds Leaf as before. This wild, sweet ache, Lest in this iridescent hour The heart may break.
