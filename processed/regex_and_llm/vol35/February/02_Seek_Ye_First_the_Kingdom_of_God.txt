SEEK YE FIRST THE KINGDOM OF GOD" You can send a message around the world in one-seventh of a second, yet it may take years to force a simple idea through a quarter-inch of human skull. Our safety in the future requires us to keep in tune with our Heavenly Father's wishes so that his inspiration can penetrate our beings and move us to noble achievements. Our conduct as individuals is what influences world movements and we could have heaven on earth the minute we all would follow the second great commandment: "Love thy neighbor as thyself." Consider the blessings that can be ours by obedience to the laws decreed in heaven. In 1 Corinthians 3:9 we read: Eye hath not seen, nor ear heard, neither have entered into the heart of man, the things which God hath prepared for them that love him. Therefore, "Seek ye first the kingdom of God, and his righteousness and all these things shall be added unto you." FOR SOME APPOINTED REASON Berta Huish Christensen Time will not wait; for some appointed reason, It moves with swift precision and is lost. The rose that bares its beauty beyond season Will find its velvet brittled by the frost. Though we would stay the falling petals, keep The harvest bough that leans on autumn's gate, Or healing moments at the edge of sleep, As solace for our tears, time will not wait. But love will stay through the long years turning, From the first frail bud to the severed leaf, Shielding its flame to a constant burning Through the winds of loss and the snows of grief. And only love will wait with patient breath, Beyond the somber interim of death. Spiritual Security for the Family Leone G. Layton Member, Relief Society General Board [Address Delivered at the Annual Relief Society General Conference, October 2, 1947] Dear Sisters, it is good to partake of the spirit of this great gathering of the sisterhood of the Relief Society. As Relief Society workers, we are all vitally concerned with building the home and family. Latter-day Saint homes should be the happiest in all the world, homes where the spirit of our Father in heaven can abide and his influence direct. Latter-day Saint mothers are all anxious to foster such conditions in their homes, knowing the peace, happiness, and security they bring to the family, for, like Father Lehi, we desire to share with our families the fruits which we have found good. He tells us in the Book of Mormon of his dream-vision of the tree whose fruit "was desirable to make one happy. And it came to pass that I did go forth and partake of the fruit thereof; and I beheld that it was most sweet, above all that I had ever tasted.... And as I partook of the fruit thereof it filled my soul with exceeding great joy; wherefore I began to be desirous that my family should partake of it also...." We all know the story, how Sariah, Sam, and Nephi came and partook and Laman and Lemuel refused to heed the call of their father; how, in looking for them, he saw the river leading to the tree, the narrow path beside it, and the rod of iron which secured the way for those who clung to it through the mists of darkness. We are fortunate that Nephi was given the interpretation of this dream, for through him we know the sweet fruit was a representation of the "love of God which sheddeth itself abroad in the hearts of the children of men," and that the iron rod represents the word of God. We who are striving to ensure the future security and happiness of our families today, have ready access to the revealed word of the Lord. We have no need to go far to grasp this iron rod of safety, but can, in our own homes, direct our children to the straight and narrow path leading to spiritual security and happiness, and set their feet thereon. We are fortunate, also, in having certain definite markers given us by which we may be sure our course is right, and by which we may judge our progress. I would like to point out a few of these markers which are of special importance to us as wives and mothers. "Search the scriptures; for in them ye think ye have eternal life: and they are they which testify of me" (John 5:39). As mothers, then, is it not our place to search the scriptures that we may teach them to our children so they will love them and learn to turn to them for guidance in their daily lives? How many great men have told of learning to love the Bible at their mothers' knees! We would none of us think of planning a long journey without consulting the guidebooks and road maps to be sure of the best way to reach our destination. How important it is, then, that we should be conscious at all times of this marker and "search the scriptures" that we may learn of our Heavenly Father, our heavenly home, and the way we must take to reach it.... Love him and serve him, the only living and true God.... If we love our Heavenly Father, we will keep the commandments which he has given us, and we will delight to serve him by doing his work here in his earthly kingdom. This marker is of special importance to the homemaker, for it is in the home that patterns of living are set. Little things sometimes influence future attitudes greatly, and a mother who sets aside her desire to see a certain movie or other entertainment that her husband may be free to fill his ward-teaching assignment may be giving her children a lesson in service values that will stay with them throughout their lives.

What understanding support comes to a husband from having a wife who honors the Priesthood and its calls. The sons of such a mother will never take the possession of the Priesthood lightly. Surely, if we place first in importance in our homes obedience to authority and a desire to do all we are called upon to do, we shall have marked the way to loving service for our families and set in motion those forces which will bring to them eternal happiness and satisfaction. 79 "Pray always, lest you enter into temptation...." As we search the scriptures and serve our Father, we appreciate anew the part prayer plays in dispelling the mist of darkness and helping us cling to the rod. This is the very first marker we are conscious of giving our little ones. How soon they sense the security of a kind Father watching over them as they sleep at night! Then, as they kneel with the family, and, in turn, voice thanksgiving for blessings received and ask for those now needed, a sense of "belonging," of family solidarity, is born within them. Family prayers are, of course, the responsibility of the Priesthood, but I am sure that in homes where the mother sees clearly the many strengthening values of the family praying together, this commandment is never forgotten. I remember one of the brethren saying once, "We could never forget family prayer in our home. When we came to breakfast we found our chairs all turned away from the table, and, without a word, we were reminded that the first order of the day for our family was to approach our Father in heaven, to thank him for his many blessings and to ask for his protecting care and guidance through the day." "And that thou mayest more fully keep thyself unspotted from the world, thou shalt go to the house of prayer and offer up thy sacraments upon my holy day." Was there ever a time when mothers of the Church were more concerned with keeping their families "unspotted from the world"? Here, in this marker, we are told plainly the way to accomplish our desire. In the 50th section of the Doctrine and Covenants, we read of the Sabbath: And on this day thou shalt do none other thing, only let thy food be prepared with singleness of heart that thy fasting may be perfect, or, in other words, that thy joy may be full.... And inasmuch as ye do these things with thanksgiving, with cheerful hearts and countenances... The fulness of the earth is yours.... I always feel that this revelation is directed to women, especially this part. We manage the homes. We can plan our food for the day with singleness of heart and go with the family to Sunday School and sacrament meeting, and partake of the spirit with them. I know of nothing we can do to bring a more united family spirit than appreciating the privilege of partaking of the sacrament together. I know of no way that we can bring security and happiness to our families more than to set them in the way of obtaining the "fulness of the earth." Sisters, we have our Father's word that "the herb, and the good things which come of the earth, whether for food or for raiment, or for houses, or for barns, or for orchards, or for gardens, or for vineyards," are promised to those who observe his day as he would have them observe it. Read this section often. The promises given herein are explicit. The earthly and spiritual security which we so desire for our families is clearly set forth, and the way pointed out whereby they may be learned. In today's world of uncertainty it seems that, truly, mists of darkness do cover the path ahead, and yet, even as we grope, our hearts are made glad, for we see the light from the fountainhead and testify afresh that by clinging fast to the rod of iron spiritual security for our families will be won and, together with them, we shall partake of the fruit of the tree, which is sweet above all other fruit, which fills our souls with exceeding great joy.
