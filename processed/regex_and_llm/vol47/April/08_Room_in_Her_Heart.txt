His abilities as a physician and surgeon could not help her with her coming ordeal, however, although Ann was deeply happy in her motherhood and the opportunity to bring another soul into the world, the criticisms of others were sometimes hard to take smilingly. Ann was grateful for the early evening breeze that refreshed her as she stepped out of the downtown medical building, joining the tide of homeward-bound shoppers and office workers. As she walked towards her bus corner, her mind was a whirlpool. Ann could see her mother's face and hear her say, "Oh, Ann, not again. Susie is only a little over a year old. You will never live to rear them at this rate." And no amount of reassuring on Ann's part would make her mother stop worrying and realize, as with each of the other children, that this eighth one was wanted. And Ann knew what Beth, her neighbor on the west, would say. "Oh, really, dear, what can you be thinking of?" And she knew what Beth would be thinking. Happiness is always contagious, she thought. She knew that when she walked through the door, they all would ask their usual questions: "Where have you been, Mommy?" "Why did you have to go to the doctor?" "Don't you feel well?"
