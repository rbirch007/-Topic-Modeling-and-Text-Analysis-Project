April Road Katherine F. Larsen A brown road calls me In the tender spring, To leave accustomed homeways; For when the blackbirds sing My wayward feet would follow Paths meandering Through buttercups and violets, Up an old wood road That winds through white-limbed aspen trunks Whose slender branches fling Fresh-minted glinting leaflets In sunlight shimmering. Lucien Bown TEAPOT LAKE IN THE UINTAH MOUNTAINS, UTAH
