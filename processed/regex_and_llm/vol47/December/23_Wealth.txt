And yet, I thank thee, too, O Lord, For hours when the road was dark; When gloom engulfed our path, and hop: Succumbed to fear, enormous, stark; When sorrow gnawed its aching way Into our being, and we fought To maintain soul tranquility. With such as these new strength was bought, Assuring us that night would rise To morning and its splendor, bright; That every dark, prophetic cloud Wears its antithesis of light. 1 thank thee, Lord, for all thy good, And all is good at long, long last.
