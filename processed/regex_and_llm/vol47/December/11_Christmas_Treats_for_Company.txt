The trees of springtime, with their delicate blossoms of pink and white, are harbingers of the bright luscious fruit of autumn days. Whether trees are the silver-trunked aspens of the mountains, with their trembling leaves, or the dark, velvety sentinel pines of the forest, nature has made them beautiful to behold, and man stands in wonder at God's handiwork. As Yuletide approaches, the season during which the Christian world celebrates Christ's birth, a tree becomes a symbol. Nations, countries, communities, and families have their own traditions about Christmas trees, and throughout the world grandparents and parents pass on to their children and grandchildren, customs, stories, decorations, and ideas which keep alive the warmth and glow of the Christmas tree. The Christmas tree truly becomes a family affair, as little ones, with father and mother, pop corn and string chains of the white kernels, or string cranberries on branches or hang rock candy, frosted cookies, Or peppermint canes on the soft green boughs. Whether the decorations are precious old-fashioned ones, gay, new icicles smoothed and hung just so, or the modern exotic balls of colored glass, or those made of velvet and sequins, the decorating becomes a joyous pastime when each and every member of the family takes a part Page 824 Big grow in woods, in orchin creating a tree of loveliness and beauty. The tree the family makes becomes not only a symbol of Christmas, but an expression of family mutuality and solidarity. In Florence Williams' family is an example of the old axiom that a family which works and plays and laughs together stays together, and is bound by lasting ties of loving affection, spirit, and interests. As Christmas draws near, Florence goes to the homes of each of her children laden with materials to help with ideas, to work with them, and to give guidance, for she has had years of expericnce in decorating and making unusual Christmas articles. At the home of son Richard and his wife, Marion, at Christmas time, there will be a most unusual Christmas tree on their front door with two glamorous ball trees at either side of the doorway as pictured. Each and every member of the family helped in one way or another to create these trees. Mother Marion and Grandmother Florence assembled all the materials. When everything was in readiness the family all gathered in the work room, and the fun of making the family tree began. Father was supervisor and consulting engineer and really did most of the hard, intricate work. First, he cut a piece of styrofoam in the shape of a perfect Christmas tree. Ricky, aged eighteen, aided by Florence, drilled small holes through hazel, Brazil, almond, pecan nuts, walnuts, and peanuts. 'This was accomplished with a very fine dll. The materials were all ready at last and everyone helped by dipping the florist wire pieces into glue, then quickly slipping them through the holes in the nuts and into the styrofoam tree. The spaces left exposed were filled in with bits of artificial green foliage. Finally, the styrofoam tree was covered completely with nuts, pine cones, rosebuds, and soft greens. The tree was then planted and held with wire in the golden halfbasket filled with the moss — but before the family could take the tree upstairs to hang on the door, the tree balls to stand on either side had to be made. Into perfectly round balls of styrofoam was inserted a_ brush handle which had been sprayed with gold paint. 'These round handles were planted in flower pots filled with plaster-of-Paris, then placed in old-fashioned milk buckets. 'The handles were inserted into the pots while the plaster was moist and held very straight until the mixture solidified. 'This process didn't take long. The styrofoam balls were then covered with the nuts, cones, and greens in the same manner as the tree for the door was made. Under each ball Anne tied a glistening bow of three-inch-wide gold metallic ribbon. The trees were beautiful and would give perfect balance to the front entrance of the house. When all was in readiness, the family followed Father and Ricky as they carried the tree to the front porch. They watched as Ricky hung the tree with a wire attached to a hook at the top of the front door and then he secured it to the door knocker with another piece of wire. The girls placed the round trees, just so, on either side of the doorway, and father adjusted the spotlight so it would shine brightly on the decorations when dusk descended. With the green door as a background, all the rich tones of the brown nuts and dark foliage, the bright gold of the ribbon bows, handles, and baskets, seemed to radiate in the darkness. We're grateful for the lovely trees created by nature that beautify our land, and we're thankful for the family spirit, the traditions, the joy of having ideas which grow into unusual and picturesque family trees at Christmas. Florence has been blessed with a rare gift of being able to create, to make her ideas materialize into things of beauty, but her greatest blessing is the quality of wanting to share with others her talents and abilities. As she enters her sons' and daughter's houses, on Christmas she will have the satisfaction and great joy to behold the beautiful "Trees the Family Made."
