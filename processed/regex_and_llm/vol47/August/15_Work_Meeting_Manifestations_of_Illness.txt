Work Meeting) Lesson 2 — Manifestations of Illness Maria Johnson For Tuesday, November 8, 1960 Objective: To learn to recognize, observe, and report symptoms or signs of illness. To learn how to take and record the body temperature, pulse, and respiration. Symptoms symptom such as headache, pain, or nausea is not a disease, but it may be an important warning that something is wrong and may be most helpful to the doctor in determining the cause of the illness. The doctor depends upon the individual attending the patient to report to him the symptoms she observes and those of which the patient complains. Just as a child puts together the various shapes and colors of a puzzle to make the picture complete, so the doctor puts together the symptoms that are evident. One symptom may be of little importance by itself; but, when associated with others, the group of symptoms may be very significant. Any of us might have a fever today, yet the disease or cause of the fever could be different in each case. Other symptoms that accompany the fever will be important in determining the cause. Warning signals that any one of us can recognize are numerous, for example, headache, dizziness, nausea, swelling, bleeding, rash. Different diseases usually have a different combination or grouping of symptoms, so we can sce how important our observations are to the doctor who is to make the diagnosis and treat the disease. In addition to the symptoms observed or reported, he may need X-rays or laboratory tests. Page 548 Reporting to the Doctor When you call a doctor, let him feel you need help and that you are not just a panicky woman. Describe the symptoms you have observed. For example, pain in the abdomen is Sharp, comes and goes; the patient draws up her legs when the pain comes; it started an hour ago; or you might report the baby pulls at his ear and screams. Whatever you report, give the location, the character, when first noted, etc. If you have a thermometer, take the temperature and report it along with other observations. When the doctor comes ask him to write his orders for you, or you may write them. In either case go over them with the doctor and see that there are no misunderstandings. Never hesitate to ask for further explanation, if it is needed.
