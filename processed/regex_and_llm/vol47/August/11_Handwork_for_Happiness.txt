I'V'TING quietly in the living room that evening, Margo's thoughts automatically returned to the events of the day. She was so excited and happy over her first Relief Society mecting that she could hardly restrain herself. She had completely lost her feeling of being alone from the moment that she had stepped into the meetinghouse. 'The warm, cheerful room was made even more warm by the friendly greetings that she received from the women present. When they asked her to return, she couldn't have been more pleased. She glowed happily as she walked from the meeting with Evelyn. In their discussion after meeting, Evelyn had learned how adept Margo was at sewing. "I'm work meeting leader in the ward, Margo, and if you wouldn't mind, I'm sure that some of the sisters would be pleased to learn how to make some of these hot pads. Would you mind coming and demonstrating to us?" Margo looked timidly at Evelyn. "Pd like to try, Evelyn, but [ve really never done anything like that. Besides I don't believe that they're good enough." "Nonsense, they couldn't be more clever. You really have a talent, Margo. Now, how about joining us for supper tonight? Ld and the children would love to have you." Margo sighed contentedly. "Yes, it has been a good day. It was so wonderful the way that the women accepted me; just as if I were one of them. 'They were all so swect." When Robert came home that week end and Margo had told him about the events of the week, he couldn't have been more pleased. He hadn't seen Margo looking quite so happy in years. Saturday evening, Margo and Robert dined with the Christensens, and Robert, too, found himself captivated by their open friendliness. It was comforting to Robert to know that when he left Margo during the week, she would now have someone to visit. In the weeks that followed, Margo found herself becoming more and more engrossed in Relief Society activities. She had more friends than she had ever had _ before. She was included in every thing. Days that were once lonely, were now filled with friends dropping in to pass the time of day. She and Evelyn did their shopping together, and often they would call on other neighbors. Evelyn only casually mentioned that Margo and Robert go to Sunday School with them some Sunday, but that was all that was necessary. Robert thought that anything that had made Margo so happy was worth investigating. They had thought that their marriage was worthwhile, but now it had taken on an entirely new perspective. When Robert arrived home each week end, he would find an enthusiastic wife awaiting him, happy and anxious to share the events of the past week with him. It was without any misgivings that Margo and Robert joined the Church. 'They had found the one thing in their life that could make it complete. They enjoyed serving ONE OF THEM others. Margo in turn felt needed. Their only regret was that they hadn't joined the Church sooner. We OKO T'S hard to believe that I've been a member of the Church for seven years, Margo thought as she dressed for Relief Society meeting. Just as she finished combing her hair, the doorbell rang. "Hi," Evelyn called as she walked in. "Ready for Relief Society? Oh, Margo, I want you to meet Sister 531 Brown. She just moved into the ward last week. And, Sister Brown, I'd like you to meet our ward Relief Society president, Sister Williams." Margo took Sister Brown's hand warmly. "I'm happy to know you." As Margo walked to Relief Society meeting, her heart was full, remembering that first meeting for her seven years ago.
