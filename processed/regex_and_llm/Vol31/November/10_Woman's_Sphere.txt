Woman's Sphere Ramona W. Cannon DESPITE determined efforts by many women's organizations to push through the Equal Rights Amendment giving women equal rights with men, it is still in Congress, where was introduced in 1923. Before it can become law, two-thirds of the House, of the Senate, and then of the forty-eight states must approve it. Before it could become effective five more years must clapse to give states time to make their laws conform. More than one thousand state laws discriminating against women now exist.
