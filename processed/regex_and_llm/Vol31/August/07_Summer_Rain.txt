SUMMER RAIN Louise Larson Cornish How I like a summer rainstorm When it comes with And a blinding flash pounding drops of lightning After which the thunder pops! And I like the big wet splashes That are made upon the ground, With a fragrant damp-earth odor Comes acreeping all around; And the cooling swish of water, And the dripping of the eaves, And the clean-washed out-of-doors That a shower always leaves. Yes, I think it most exciting! And I get a mighty thrill! When. There's thunder mixed with lightning As the clouds begin to spill! '
