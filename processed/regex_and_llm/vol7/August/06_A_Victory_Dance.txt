A VICTORY DANCE. Alfred Noyes. The cymbals crash. And the dancers walk With long, silk stockings And arms of chalk, Butterfly skirts. And white breasts bare, And shadows of dead men Watching 'em there. Shadows of dead men Stand by the wall. Watching the fun Of the Victory Ball. They do not reproach. Because they know, If they're forgotten. It's better so. Under the dancing Feet are the graves. Dazzle and motley. In long, bright waves. Brushed by the palm fronds, Grapple and whirl Ox-eyed matron And slim, white girl. Fat, wet bodies Go waddling by. Girded with satin. Though God knows why; Gripped by satyrs In white and black. With a fat, wet hand On a fat, wet back.
