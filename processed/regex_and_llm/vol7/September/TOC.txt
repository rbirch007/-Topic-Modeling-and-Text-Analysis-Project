THE NEEDS OF YESTERDAY. Bertha A. Kleinman. Unbowed of years nor yet of pungent sorrow, Unsmarted of doubt that probes its avid way, I yet have need of grace and a tomorrow To right the many wrongs of yesterday. The many hours I have not to filling. The wasted days of empty little wiles, The crying tasks that have not found me willing The span of cloud that needed but my smiles;  The fleeting beats of chance so easy squandered, The thousand little precious waits of time — And all the ways of quest I have not wandered, Where timid ones might cling to hands of mine. Ah me! the little zest and care expended. The meager, little, momentary zeal — To others' wrongs that might have been amended. And rasping little hurts that I could heal! O Thou who knowest every throb of sorrow. Bear still with me, for I would know the way, That somewhere in the might of Thy tomorrow, I may amend the needs of yesterday! Mrs. Mary Wright Sewell. THE
