FLOWERS OF EARTH AND HEAVEN. John M. Chamberlain. I know a garden fair to see, Where sweetest flowers grow; Red roses bloom with sweet perfume, And He’s white as snow. The pansies, too, they smile on you. With modest violets; The columbine our hearts entwine, With fragrant mignonette. Then come, oh, come, the flowers call. With voices so sweet, in our hearts they meet, They call for one, and all. Go where you will, by land or sea. No place can compare with flowers so fair They call, "Oh, come to me." In heaven there's a garden fair, Where flowers immortal grow. Angelic love that blooms above, • Pure as the driven snow. And faith, and hope, sweet charity, They are the blessed three That bind our hearts no more to part, Through all eternity.
