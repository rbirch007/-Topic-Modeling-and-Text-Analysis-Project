Mine for Keeps Ida Elaine James Two portable blossoms Sprang up in my yard — Two brightly ginghamed children Looking at me hard. I went on with my raking, Gave them a casual smile; One fumbled in her pocket - A little while For a gaudy egg she had colored, Held it close to me. Praisefully 1 nodded: "Pretty as can be." Leaned nearer just to please her — "Why, it's the very blue Of your pretty little apron; Is it new?" The egg slipped in my pocket — They hippety-hopped on air. It was then I stopped my raking, To say an Easter prayer.
