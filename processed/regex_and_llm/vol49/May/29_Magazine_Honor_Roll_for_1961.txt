Magazine Honor Roll for 1961 NCE, again the General Board takes opportunity to express its gratitude and appreciation to the dedicated and devoted women who have been instrumental in placing The in so many of our Latter-day Saint homes throughout the world, and also in the homes of other lovely women who appreciate the good and beautiful in life. Especially do we express appreciation to the Magazine representatives in stakes and wards, missions and branches, for their zealous and untiring efforts, and to the Relief Society presidencies and other officers and teachers who have given their loyal support and encouragement. Surely the Lord is pleased with his faithful daughters who have so diligently magnified their calling. Thanks must also be expressed to our thousands of sisters who subscribe to the Magazine, thereby evidencing their appreciation of Relief Society itself, and of the Magazine, the "voice" of Relief Society which brings so much knowledge and pleasure, beauty and spirituality into our lives. In expressing appreciation, we must not forget the many gift subscriptions so thoughtfully and generously provided the General Board for distribution to the missions of the Church by individual sisters and by stake, ward, and branch organizations. Sisters, we thank you Page 372 for these subscriptions. We are sure you will feel well repaid for your generosity and kindness in the knowledge that many hearts have been touched by the messages and inspiration contained in the Magazine, and that a number of conver-: sions to the gospel have resulted. You will be happy to know that the total number of Magazine subscriptions in 1961 was 183,236, compared with 171,002 in 1960, for an increase of 12,234. 'There were 303 stakes on the Honor Roll, an increase of nineteen over the 1960 total of 284. The mean of all the stakes was ninety-three per cent, which is an increase of one per cent over 1960. The highest stake was again the South Los Angeles, for the fifteenth consecutive year, with a percentage of 226. It also had the largest number of subscriptions, with a total of 1,641. Of the ten highest stakes, the first four places were taken by stakes in California. Others in the top ten included one stake in Arizona, two in Idaho, one in Canada, one in Nevada, and one in Utah. There were also 2,344 wards and 61 branches in the stakes on the Honor Roll. In forty-one stakes all of the wards achieved one hundred per cent or over. In 1961 there were 24 missions on the Honor Roll, an increase of four over 1960. Three of these are new MAGAZINE HONOR ROLL FOR 1961 missions and are to be congratulated for doing so well. Highest honor goes to the British Mission with 143 per cent. Second is the Western States Mission, with 129 per cent; third, the Canadian Mission, with 124 per cent; and fourth, the North Central States Mission, with 111 per cent. In total number of subscriptions the Western States led with 1,075. The mean of the missions on the Honor Roll rose to ninety-five per cent, for an increase of three per cent over 1960. Sisters, we rejoice with you over these outstanding achievements in Magazine subscriptions. They represent many hours of effort by thousands of Magazine representatives throughout the Church, and they are a reflection of loving and devoted SEIVICE. Relief Society women the world over are grateful for our , with its beautiful covers and its many pages filled with beauty, culture, refinement, knowledge, faith, inspiration, and love.
