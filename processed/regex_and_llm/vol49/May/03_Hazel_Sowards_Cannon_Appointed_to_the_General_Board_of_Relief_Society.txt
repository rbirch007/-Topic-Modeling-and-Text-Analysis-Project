Hazel Sowards Cannon Appointed to the General Board of Relief Society Luella F. Okeson Instructor of English, Granite School District, Salt Lake City, Utah HAZEL SOWARDS CANNON AZEL Sowards Cannon was appointed to the General Board of Relief Society February 21, 1962. She has accepted the responsibility in a spirit worthy of a devoted Latter-day Saint woman. Among her many qualifications for the position are a truly humble attitude, a sincere love of her fellow beings, and a vital, innate graciousness of manner. Sister Cannon is the daughter of Harmon S. And Ida Rebecca Jensen Sowards. These devout Latter-day Saints settled in Vernal, Utah. Fer father is a civic- -minded, friendly, generous man with a distinguished record of service in his community and Church. Her mother, now deceased, was also a devout Church and civic worker; a genteel woman, sensitive to the needs of others, and creative in every phase of her life. Page 320 Sister Cannon graduated from Uintah High School in Vernal. Later, she received a degree in home economics from Utah State University in Logan, Utah. She taught at Granite High School for two years. On August 30, 1934, she was married to Hyrum P. Cannon in the Logan Temple. 'They have two children. Harmon S. Cannon has fulfilled a mission to the Eastern States and, with his sister, Ida Christine, is now attending the University of Utah. Sister Cannon has created a lovely, distinctive home for the family, and has proved that real homemaking is an art. 'True to her early training, she has always — been devoted to serving her Church.
