Third Prize Poem Recess — School for the Deaf Eva Willes Wangsgaard Here children play denied the joy of sounds, Where thunder falls as silently as snow. They've never heard the milkman on his rounds, The clink of bottles, hurried steps tiptoe. They cannot hear a hymn or lullaby, A mother's voice wrapped softly round a name, A night train's whistle or a coyote's cry, For town or country silence is the same. The thud of ball on bat, the lilt of laughter Received in silence colorless as frost Are subtle arguments for what comes after, Some heaven where no joy is ever lost, Where man's equality is not belied And heaven grants each child what earth denied.
