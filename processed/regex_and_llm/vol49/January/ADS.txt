Unlimited Possibility of Growth In the Doctrine and Covenants, Section 93:36, it says "The glory of God is intelligence..." and in Section 131:6, it says: "It is impossible for a man to be saved in ignorance." The quest for knowledge, faith, and love is equally as important to the woman as to the man who holds the Priesthood. She, too, is given her free agency and placed upon this earth to gain experience and progress toward perfection. Her understanding of life here and hereafter is of vital importance in giving intelligent direction to her family and for her own exaltation. Here again the Priesthood is responsible for and is the source of blessings. The Call to Service One of the richest blessings to a woman comes through the privilege of service in the Church. Every officer in the Church is called - by proper authority and commissioned by this authority. In the ward the bishop calls and commissions the worthy persons to their particular responsibilities. Every office andcalling in the Church is important, LESSON DEPARTMENT and to the individual receiving the assignment it is the most important assignment in the Church. No one else can do the work you are called to do while you are in the office. Your effective performance of the duties and responsibilities of your office gives strength to the whole organization. 'The privilege comes through the Priesthood, and _blessings come from your devotion to duty. We have stated before that the government of the Church is the Priesthood in action. Every office in the Church is therefore, to a degree, Priesthood work. As_ the Church has grown there has been a need for auxiliary help. As the term implies, the auxiliaries are assisting organizations to the Priesthood, but subject to, and under the direction of the Priesthood. The officers and teachers in the auxiliaries play an important part in the teaching of the gospel. But, as has been pointed out, teaching the gospel to all the members is a major responsibility of the Priesthood quorums. There are assignments to the women in the work that is assigned to the Priesthood. Women work on the genealogical committees and do both research and temple work for the dead. The missionary work which is to every nation, kindred, tongue, and people also uses the women of the Church. They are an effective part of the missionary program in teaching the gospel to people of the Many nations. Service in the Church is a blessing in and of itself. Through Church work you are serving the Lord by serving your fellow men. It tends to Teduce selfishness, and by doing 69 things for others it reduces tensions in your own life. We tend to love whom we serve. 'The Lord emphasized this to Peter when he asked i lovest thou me.. ' and Peter's answer was "yea " Then he said "Feed my sheep.' ' This he repeated, giving emphasis to the connection between, and the importance of service and love of God (John 21:15-17). Women's Service, a Strength to the Church The activity of the women in the Church program is a manifestation of dedication to a great cause. This is demonstrated by a situation in the mission field where three wonderful Maori ladies, old in years, but vigorous in the love of the gospel, held a little branch together for many years until the Priesthood holders were regenerated. These faithful sisters did everything but the ordinances, and they guided the young Aaronic Priesthood members in the care and administration of the sacrament. Many times the women of the wards and stakes have provided the activity necessary to the success of Priesthood projects. 'Through the efforts of the women in the home, the men are constantly built up and encouraged. They care for the children, assist their men in _ projects and programs, and provide leadership for the auxiliary organizations. The blessings in store for the women of the Church are limited only by the efforts they put forth to build the kingdom.
