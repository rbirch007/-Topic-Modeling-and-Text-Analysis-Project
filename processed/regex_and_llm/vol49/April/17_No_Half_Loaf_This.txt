No Half Loaf, This Virginia Newman Friendly were the words you said, (I'd like the recipe some day), Tendering the loaf of bread, Oven warm and savory; But let me ask you if I may, How much that gesture meant to me, How you acquired the finer art Almost a stranger, lonely too, Of nourishing the hungry heart? And gladdened by the sight of you. I never had the knack, somehow. I would repay you if I could. Oh, yes, the bread was extra good. (I'd like that recipe right now).
