Enough Hazel Loomis I want no more than corn to grind — Or sheep to tend. Enough, if by a scrubby tree, I sit the whole day long As mother, weaving, Hums a song. Enough, to sit for just an hour Beside my father's hogan fire — Enraptured artist, color rising in his face, Pounding silver Into lace. I need no pillow for my head, A pallet of clean sand, my bed. Where hearts glow warm — where smoke leaps higher, Enough, the peace Of a hogan fire. —— Attitudes Even the desire to do our best brings hope and the beginning of gladness. Tich, then, shall the harvest be. How —Pauline M. Bell
