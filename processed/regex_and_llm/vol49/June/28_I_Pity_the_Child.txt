LEON SMITH RICE Farmington, Utah Page 472 | Pity the Child Christie Lund Coles I pity the city child Whose heart has not grown still At sight of a river singing, Or a country-summer hill; Before star-flowered night, And the morning's chorale; And the magical secrets Fields or orchards can tell. A city's ways are narrow With mortar, brick, and stone; But country lanes grow wider For a child to walk alone.
