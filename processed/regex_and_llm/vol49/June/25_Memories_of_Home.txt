Geneva H. Williams I think of them there in the twilight When the night shades begin to fall, Father and Mother in the dear, old home That has known and sheltered us all. The house seems quiet and lonely, now, Since the children are grown and gone, But the bees still hum in the orchard bloom, And the birds sing the same sweet song. The evening star hangs over the mountain, The whippoorwill calls to its mate. The rising moon makes a path of gold On the shimmering blue of the lake.
