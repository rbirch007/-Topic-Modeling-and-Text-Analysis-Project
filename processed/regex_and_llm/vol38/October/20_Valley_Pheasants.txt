Valley Pheasants Evelyn Fjeldsted The sunshine's reach was valley-wide, Its warmth as soft as milkweed down, And pheasants gleaning in the field Came to visit near the town. Then golden stubbled lands were dimmed, The shadowed pheasants, terrified, Before the endless searching squads Fled further up the mountainside. The first snow found an obscure nest The base where fledglings learned to fly. The fields were lonely, desolate, Beneath a disapproving sky. Notes From The Field 4 Margaret C.
