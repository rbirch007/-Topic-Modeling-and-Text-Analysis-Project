I saw her in her crimson velvet shoes, I saw her golden hair, pretentious, bright; She seemed so proud, so exuberant, until She turned and paused a moment in the light; And then I noted ravages that time, And frost, and sudden, stinging cold Had left upon her, and I knew that though Her dress was taffeta, her heart was old. I knew that she was tired being gay, Being so colorful for our delight. I knew how ready she would be, at last, To sit beside the fire in noiseless white.
