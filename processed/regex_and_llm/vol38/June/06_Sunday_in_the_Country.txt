Sunday in the Country Christie Lund Coles Sunday in the country was a day Quite set apart, even by the way The sun shone, more luminously bright Between the shadows cool and dark as night; Sunday was a day of worship, rest, Of dressing in our stiffly-ironed best, Walking the graveled pathway to the church Set amid poplars and the lean, white birch, And flanked by yellow roses that were bent In rich abundance, giving the air a scent That wakes us to homesickness even yet. And there was organ music. We can forget Much, but not that, nor our child-world, then Simple as life will not be again.
