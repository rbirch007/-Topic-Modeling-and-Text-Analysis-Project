Lehi Wakes Again Dorothy J. Roberts Lehi wakes again in these deep eyes, For dawn unraveling his shroud of night Reveals arroyos where his red dust lies, His fables crumbling in the mesa's light. He walked in his decreed immunity Spared, when his fair kin met oblivion's waves; Through sinful eras, saved by chastity, He built his hogan on the fair ones' graves. Penanced with clouded sight and darkened skin, He roamed, lost courier of the distant dead; Stalking the centuries in moccasin He left few symbols save the arrowhead. Lehi looks again toward the promised land From persecution's wide and bitter sea. Calm is the spirit tried on trackless sand Where patience hardens into majesty. His fair, white kinship to its source will trace Through blood remembered, when his mind is freed. Lehi lives in this long-chastened race To fill the covenant upon his seed.
