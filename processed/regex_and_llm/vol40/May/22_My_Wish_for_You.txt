My Wish for You Bernice T. Clayton I would not wish you sunshine, dear, Without some blessed rain; I could not wish you happiness Devoid of grief and pain. For deserts form in sunny lands, Where rain can never fall, But God, who hears our sorrowing, Can heal and comfort all. Deep love, compassion, tenderness, We buy at sorrow's cost; We know the joys of being found When we have once been lost. God give you strength to drain life's cup But still find sweetness there, For joy will come when once you learn The great release of prayer. —oe Mother Emily Carey Alleman No longer does she run On eager dancing feet To greet the promise of dawn As it tiptoes down the street.
