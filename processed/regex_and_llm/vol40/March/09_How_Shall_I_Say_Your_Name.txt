How shall I say your name With its full meaning? Once I stood by a stream Where an old tree, leaning Above a quiet place, caught And sifted its flowing Into cool syllables. But I stood unknowing. I walked slant, twisted trails Past red cliff-faces Where the blaze of noon spills Through time—sculptured spaces To green pines, and ferns That rustle and glisten. Warmth echoed down tall stones. I did not listen. Somehow I even missed High, thin air singing Your name-song. It is lost As the first bird's winging— Even now, in this breathless: time Of holding you nearest.
