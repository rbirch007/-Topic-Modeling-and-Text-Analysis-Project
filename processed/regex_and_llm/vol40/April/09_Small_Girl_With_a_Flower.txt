Small Girl With a Flower Genevieve Groen The yellow of the curled petal Matches the twining tendrils of her hair. The gold of their twin-shining, Repeats the sun-warm perfection of the air. The leaf and stem are green-envy For the sky-color of her wondering eyes, When she finds the scented hilltop Where the heaven-designed daffodil lies. The mated softness of cheek and blossom Becomes a born-belonging no thought can undo, When her small nose presses gently, And one innocent kiss of fragrance weds the two.
