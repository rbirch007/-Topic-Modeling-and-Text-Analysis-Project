Jacobs For Tuesday, February 2, 1954 Objective: To encourage the cultivation of humility. "THROUGHOUT the scriptures, mankind has repeatedly been admonished against pride and its degenerating effects. Oft quoted from Proverbs is: "Pride goeth before destruction, and an haughty spirit before a fall" (Proverbs 16:18). Ruskin said, "In general, pride is at the bottom of all great mistakes." From the pages of history, we learn that pride has been the initial step in the downfall of many peoples and nations. Particularly in Book of Mormon history is this evident. 'The rise and fall of different peoples chronicled therein, closely parallel the absence or presence of' pride. Wars and tribulations have been preceded by this pernicious evil. Typical of many similar quotations are these: And now, in this two hundred and first year there began to be among them those who were lifted up in pride (4 Nephi 24). They persecute the meek and the poor in heart, because in their pride they are puffed up (2 Nephi 28:13). And also the people who were called the people of Nephi began to be proud in their hearts.... And from this time the disciples began to sorrow for the sins of the world (4 Nephi 43, 44). Pride is the forerunner of many other evils. It is a contributing facPage 770 tor in the downfall of many societies. _How may one's soul be destroyed by pride? Pride has always been a 'stumbling block in the way of. Righteousness. When it infects the human heart, the very essence of Christianity is crowded out, which is the true love of one's fellow men. Pride leads one to become selfish, and selfishness is not righteousness. When one is proud, he is inclined to show preference for social prestige, wealth, the worldly acclaim of men, and to set aside the things of God. Thus pride cankers the soul. Pride leads some to exercise unrighteous dominion over those less fortunate, to be lifted up in their own estimation above others, to expect special privileges and consideration. Such a spirit is not in harmony with the gospel of Jesus Christ. "Think of your brethren like unto yourselves" (Jacob 2:17), said Jacob, in The Book of Mormon. Elder Albert E. Bowen said, "There is no aristocracy with the Lord except the aristocracy of righteousness." Pride is a destructive force, while humility fosters and builds righteousness and nobility of character. Humility induces one to give credit and thanksgiving to his Maker for LESSON DEPARTMENT every blessing received and to accept, with fortitude, the trials that inevitably come. It induces one to seek out the suffering and downtrodden and render loving service, 771 with an eye single to the glory of God. "Be thou humble; and the Lord thy God shall lead thee by the hand" (D. & C. 112:10). WVork Meeting—Family Money Management (A Course Recommended for Use by Wards and Branches at Work Meeting) Lesson 5—Spending the Furniture Dollar Rhea H. Gardner (For Tuesday, February 9, 1954) HE amount of money a family spends on furniture is not necessarily a measuring guide to its beauty or the satisfaction derived from its use. Wise and careful planning, before buying, can save hundreds of dollars and countless regrets. Before deciding to buy new furniture, consider what you can do to modernize what you have, or _ what you can make yourself. Much of the modern furniture is easy to copy if there is a handy man or woman in the family. In many homes there are treasures of priceless value in old pieces of furniture. The real beauty may be hidden by numerous layers of varnish, but this can easily be removed. Know exactly what you need and want and the price you can afford to pay before going shopping. You will want the new piece of furniture to seem a part of all your other room furnishings. It need not be of the same period style, but should blend with the rest of the furniture. It is important to observe the size scale, taking into consideration the space available and the comfort and convenience of the persons who will use the piece of furniture. Take with you, on your shopping tour, space measurements and, if color is involved in the purchase, samples of the colors already in the room. When you shop, go to several stores. Shoppers often pay too much because they do not shop enough. Compare quality, styles, comfort, and prices. Ask questions, examine the furniture on all sides, and read the labels. You may soon find a piece that pleases you very much. The size, style, and color may all seem just right, and even the price may be one you can afford, but how about the construction? Is it sturdy, well built, and able to take the kind of wear you will expect of it? If it is a piece that will get hard and continuous wear, it is economy to get the best you can afford. Furniture construction comes down to two items—the quality of the materials used, and how these materials are put together and finished. The way wooden pieces are fitted and fastened together is most we RELIEF SOCIETY MAGAZINE—NOVEMBER 1953 important. There are two important and equally satisfactory types of corner joints. They are mortise and tenon and dowel joints. Dovetailed corners are indications of sturdy drawer construction. In very inexpensive pieces nails and glue are used to hold the drawer together. Fitting and grooving of wood panels contribute much to strength. In. The best drawer construction, bottoms are fitted into matching grooves on the four sides of the drawer. Center drawer guides and dustproof panels between drawers are further marks of high-quality construction. Never buy a piece of upholstered furniture without knowing what materials are used on the inside. Strong interlaced webbing, hightempered steel springs—held securely in place with strong twine, have long marked the best quality upholstered furniture. Over these is placed such padding materials as curled hair or moss. 'Today, foam rubber is also being used in a most satisfactory way, in most cases. Also, today, woven webbing is being replaced by flexible metal strips and other devices which lessen the amount of hand work required. This means lower prices and better quality. The outside covering of a piece of upholstered furniture represents a high percentage of the total cost, but high quality top covering is a poor substitute for good under construction. Make sure that it is of a quality that will serve your needs. Long threads will pull and wear quickly. Cotton and rayon soil more readily than wool and do not wear as well. Many rubberized plastics stretch and tear along stitching lines where there is extra strain. Heavily textured weaves are currently in high style. With the great simplicity of line in contemporary furniture, texture of both wood and fabric is relied on for interest more than in the past. Today's fashion permits the choice of individual pieces that blend instead of match. A spring and mattress on legs can be much less expensive than when the frame is purchased. 'The money saved might well go into a better set of springs or mattress. 'The headboard may be a piece of plywood padded and covered with quilted glazed chintz or other suitable material. Or, you may want to build a functional headboard of shelves and storage space above for books, a lamp and radio. Do not be mislead by labels regarding the kind of finish. "Walnut finish" means that a less expensive wood has been stained to look like. Walnut. Most of the fine furniture available in stores today is made with veneered plywood construction. Excellent bonding materials make the veneer of today the most durable of all furniture woods. 'They are about eight per cent stronger than solid wood. Veneering also adds immeasurably to the beauty of the wood grain. Tubular steel, wrought iron, plastic, and metals molded at the factory into drawers, magazine racks, and end tables, are fast replacing materials and methods that have involved the use of much expensive hand labor. The result should be better made, less expensive furniture. LESSON DEPARTMENT Regardless of all you can do to learn sound buying practices, you must trust somebody.
