President Spafford: Members of the First Presidency, General Authorities, Dear Sisters, and Friends of Relief Society, I think this is the happiest day of my life, and I am grateful for your presence. I welcome you to this ground-breaking ceremony. We are greatly honored to have with us today President David O. McKay, President Stephen L Richards, President J. Reuben Clark, Jr.; our advisors in the Council of the 'Twelve: President Joseph Fielding Smith and Elder Mark E. Petersen; as well as other General Authorities and their wives and special guests. We are very happy that we have with us today our dear former president, Sister Amy Brown Lyman. We will begin these services by the invocation being offered by Flder Joseph Fielding Smith. Invocation: President Joseph Fielding Smith Our Father who art in heaven, we have assembled here this afternoon for the purpose of removing earth to begin the foundation for a building for the Relief Society members of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. And at this time we present ourselves unto thee, grateful in our hearts for all the accomplishments of this great organization founded in Nauvoo on the 17th day of March, 1842, and given by revelaPage 798 tion to the Prophet Joseph Smith. We thank thee for all the great performances, the untiring labor, the continued success, faithfulness, and obedience on the part of our sisters who have been connected with this organization from those days in Nauvoo. We pray thee, Father, that thy spirit may be present with us on this occasion to bless those who shall speak, to be with those who take part in the exercises of removing earth for the commencement of the Building in which our sisters may assemble in matters pertaining to the welfare of the Relief Society of the Church. We bless them for their integrity and their faith. We pray that the accomplishment of this Building may not be retarded, but that it may go forward to be completed; that it may serve all the purposes for which it is intended as a home for the Relief Society where they may hold their meetings, engage in all their exercises, where they may look after the interests of the society and conduct its affairs from this point throughout all the courses of Zion. Let thy peace be with them and thy blessings upon them, and we pray thee that all pertaining to this Building may be in perfect harmony with thy purposes, and we ask it in the name of the Lord Jesus Christ. 'Tens of thousands of Relief Society women throughout the world, learning of this event, will rejoice with us. And it is my conviction that today women of the past are mindful of us and are looking upon us aor sharing our joys.:: We are deeply grateful for the goodness of our Heavenly Father to his daughters in opening the way whereby we might have a home of our own. We remember with gratitude the kindness of President George Albert Smith and his counselors President Jr., and President David O. McKay when, in 1945, they sanctioned the Building program and, in 1947, authorized us to go forward with the fund-raising program. We are very grateful today to our present First Presidency—President David O. McKay, President Stephen L Richards, President Reuben Clark, Jr., for their understanding of our needs and our desires, for their personal interest in Relief Society, for the wisdom of their counsel, for their support in our many problems and our many activities. We are grateful for their generosity in being willing to help us financially as they do the units of the Church. We are grateful for this beautiful building site which has granted us. 'This site fulfills the dreams and the expressed design of our sisters of the past, that our Building should be in the shadow of our great temple. It is the opinion of the Relief Society presidency that no more choice site exists for a Relief Society home in all the world. J. Reuben Clark, Many, many prayers have been offered and countless hours of time have been spent in perfecting the plans for our Building. We acknowledge with great appreciation the part of many in the perfecting of these plans. We thank all who have taken part in the study or the preparation or the drawing of the plans. ° Particularly at this time, we wish to mention our architect, Bishop George Cannon Young. The Building will include the administrative offices of the general board, a beautiful general board room, with committee rooms for the board members.
