Beyond the Strength of Rock Eva Willes Wangsgaard The mystery of summer found a seed Not only under earth, but stone-encased Where men had laid a walk and feet had paced Upon their busy way. No one gave heed To what might lie beneath. An inner need Demanded light and all else was effaced By that desire for growth. A course was traced, A dungeon door gave way, and life was freed. A blade of grass has forced an ancient lock. Although more frail than any flesh or bone It used a strength beyond the strength of rock. Here is a triumph marked and plainly shown, A miracle the doubting cannot mock, For fragile-fingered grass has rent a stone.
