PEAR BREAD OR FRUIT BREAD Pears (dry and sliced) 16 oz., raisins 5 oz, currants 5 oz., walnuts (chopped) 5 oz., sugar 4 oz., cinnamon 2 teaspoons, cloves 1 teaspoon, anise 1 teaspoon, brandy or cider 4 oz. Mix well and leave stand over night in a stone or glass jar. In the morning make sponge: 1 qt. water, 1½ lbs. flour, 3 cakes Fleischmann's Yeast, 1 tablespoon sugar. When light (about 1½ hours) mix the fruit in the sponge, add about 1½ lbs. flour, make a medium stiff dough, let rise until about double size, make into loaves, let rise to double size and bake. If served with cheese is delicious. This bread will keep for weeks, and improves with age. The fruit may be prepared days or even weeks ahead of baking. It will improve in flavor. THE FLEISCHMANN CO. I thought you sisters in the West perhaps would like to hear About the fine Relief Society that is established here. Our city, named Detroit, has many sisters striving, Who toil with might and main to make the work more thriving. Two lady missionaries us instruct, in ways and modes of living, Theology and Genealogy before our minds are bringing. From far and near the sisters come, some tiny babies bringing; With zeal we aim to make this year the best with all attending. We think and plan in various ways to help the work along, We sew, knit, crochet, hold bazaars to make the treasury strong, And, (when our country called our boys to France to fight for freedom), Helmets, sweaters, caps and socks we felt quite sure they'd need them. All kinds of food we do conserve, such tasks we'd never shirk, We rally to our Country's needs, so we took up Red Cross work. With courage bold we'll face our tasks; Truth's banner we will wield To those around us lend a hand, while in the mission field. Written by Emma Schrimshaw. Detroit, Mich.
