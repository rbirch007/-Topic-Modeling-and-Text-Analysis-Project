Magazine Honor Roll For 1957 Counselor Marianne C. Sharp THE General Board congratulates all those responsible for the record of Magazine subscriptions made during 1957. In spite of the necessity of raising the price to $2, as of August 1957, still the number of subscriptions reached an all-time high of 150,988 — with an increase of 2,426 number of subscriptions over 1956. The substantial increase attests the devotion of Magazine representatives — representatives who walked through storms to obtain renewals so that no issue would be lost by subscribers; representatives who called on newcomers and who in addition to selling them a subscription, let them know that Relief Society was vitally interested in their welfare; sisters who, although homebound, made telephone contacts for Magazine representatives; representatives who used their own cars to reach the sisters living far away; representatives who returned two and three times to a home to obtain a subscription. These and other services were performed by ward and branch Magazine _ representatives who honored and magnified their callings. Stake and mission Magazine representatives spent many hours in giving vital suggestions at union meetings, giving training in salesmanship, keeping Magazine records for the stake or mission, and enthusing and encouraging ward and branch representatives. The General Board attaches great significance to the calling of a Magazine representative. It is an unselfish service of great benefit to Relief Page 324 Society throughout the world. Presidencies who support their Magazine representatives also contribute to the well-being of Relief Society through having their sisters subscribe to the Magazine. Why is the Magazine vital to the work of Relief Society? It is a messenger which carries the spirit of Relief Society into every home it enters. It instructs on new policies and rules and sets forth in both print and pictures the activities and programs of this world-wide sisterhood. It records the history of Relief Society. It teaches approved, outlined courses of study to bring mental and spiritual development and progression. The Relief Society Magazine brings joy to readers through creative writings of poetry and prose by Latter-day Saint women. It suggests ways of improving homemaking and ties together the sisterhood of the Church. It indeed seems an essential part of Relief Society and the work of the sisters of the Church. For the eleventh consecutive year, South Los Angeles leads the stakes of the Church with a percentage of 251%. South Gate Ward of South Los Angeles Stake again leads the wards with 520%. 'The Central States Mission leads the missions with 113%, with Northern California just 1% less. 'The Central States Mission also has the highest district—West Kansas District with 163%, and the highest mission branch, Oklahoma City Branch with 3507. There are eighty-eight stakes MAGAZINE HONOR ROLL FOR 1957 whose percentage is 100% or over; there are 220 stakes on the honor roll; and twenty stakes in which all the wards achieved 100% or over. There are 1,854 wards on the honor roll. 'There are sixteen missions on the honor roll in 1957 and 559 branches. Ever since 1872 the women of the Church have had their own publication, and over the years the work of placing the publication in the homes of Latter-day Saint women has been entrusted to faithful sisters 325 who have willingly accepted this strenuous and exacting but satisfying call. Today The Relief Society Magazine representatives give unselfishly of their time, of their talents, and of themselves to further their important work for Relief Society. The General Board extends its heartfelt thanks to them, the presidencies, and to the subscribers who help make a publication for women a continued reality in the Church.
