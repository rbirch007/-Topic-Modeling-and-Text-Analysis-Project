Bird On an Orchard Bough Christie Lund Coles Bird, what are you singing on this petaled bough, Blooming between the time of bud and fruit; What is your song, is it a paean of how The seed was planted and the tree took root; Of how the brown soil mothered it; and sun, And rain, like gentle fingers, urged it up Until this bright-leafed wonder was begun— An orchard, lifting skies a perfumed cup? Bird, what are you singing is it a song Of promises of beauty incarnate Within the smallest thing where hope is strong Lifting the spirit, long disconsolate?
