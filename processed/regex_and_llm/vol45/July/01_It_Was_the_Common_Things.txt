It was the common things which gave them strength, Whose courage cradled Freedom's lusty birth. No crown was their's, no carpet's royal length, But virgin land, and brawn, and valor's worth. The yield of corn, the hearth of hope and trust, These shaped their lives as good words shape the hymn; The pail of milk, bread, coarse but gold of crust, Drew prayerful thanks around each table rim. And we, in turn, give thanks each time we pause To treasure common things, deep-rooted ways, Nor let our chrome-bright progress dim their cause, But hold their triumphs emblem-high with praise: The right to speak, to worship as we please— Our country suckled life from such as these.
