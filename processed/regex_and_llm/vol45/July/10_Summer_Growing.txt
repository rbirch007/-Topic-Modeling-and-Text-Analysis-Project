Summer Growing - Linnie Fisher Robinson The sun beats down its long and steady rays Urging a greater strength of twig and root; Purple begins along the plum's green mm And cherries load the tree with luscious fruit. From denser shade I note the birdlings' cries As food is brought to meet their mounting needs; And I turn water to a thirsting tree And pull away the clinging, choking weeds. I seek the shade wherein my child is sleeping, Almost too large for his small crib and toys, With apple blossom glow upon his face, For summer days are growing days for boys. Oh, may I never quench his inward growing And keep the weeds away from God's bestowing.
