A Pink Shawl Mabel Jones Gabbott Knit for me a shaw] of pink, Pale threads and deeper in loop and link, To wear when I am sad. Catch in one stitch a primrose blush, And somewhere the tone of the rose-throated thrush; Oh, let the center mirror the dawn When the day is new and night just gone, And lost the dreams we had. Edge it with white-pink of apple bloom, And touch of peach blossom if there is room, Blend the fringe from the budding rose To the dusky glow that a sunset knows; And I shall walk by the water's brink Warmed by your love and my shawl of pink, And evermore be glad. Yotes.
