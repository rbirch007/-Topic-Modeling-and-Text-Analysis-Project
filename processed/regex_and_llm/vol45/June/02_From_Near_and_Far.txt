From Vlear and Sar I enjoy The Relief Society Magazine very much. Many of the covers are especially lovely. I save them all, and have read the stories and articles and poems many times. In this way I feel that I know many of the contributors. —Elsie McKinnon Strachan Santa Ana, California My mother was delighted when I read to her an item from the April issue of the Magazine in the Sixty Years Ago department concerning a San Juan Stake Relief Society Conference at Bluff. President Martha Hammond, wife of President Francis A. Hammond, was my foster grandmother. Then President Elnora (Nora) Hammond, as reporting the Mancos, Colorado, Relief Society is my mother, who will soon be ninety-five years old. She presided over the Mancos organization for twelve years, riding side saddle to the meetings. Mother is still alert in her mind and it is a joy to converse with her. | —Mrs.
