SARA JORDAN, who at seventy-four begins her busy days at five o'clock in the morning, commenced the study of medicine at a few weeks under thirty-three and was a specialist in gastroenterology (stomach and intestinal disorders) ready to practice as the third member of the beginning Lahey Clinic when thirty-nine. Except for her first year, she worked her way through medical school, also supporting a daughter. She became the only female president of the American Gastroenterological Association, and she now heads a staff of ten doctors in her field at Boston's vastly expanded Lahey Clinic. Dr. Jordan says that nobody should smoke cigarettes. But smoking with an ulcer is like pouring gasoline on a burning house.
