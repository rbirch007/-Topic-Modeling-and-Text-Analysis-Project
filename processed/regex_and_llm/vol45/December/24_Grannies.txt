Grannies Ethel Jacobson Grannies have sort Of a restful look. They smell like the violets By the brook, And their skirts kind of rustle When they pass Like the breeze that whispers Through meadow grass. Grannies know stories Of long ago. They can hum old hymns So soft and low, And their laps are always A just-right size For a small tired child With dream-filled eyes.
