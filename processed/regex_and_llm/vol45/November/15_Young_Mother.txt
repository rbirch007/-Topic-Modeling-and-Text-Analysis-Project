Young Mlother Elsie McKinnon Strachan I knew her when her face was springtime-growing— A periwinkle ribbon in her hair; Exchanging talk with birds and flowers blowing, Her words were April patter on the air. And now she's summer! Young and lovely summer! The willow's lullabies, the robin's wings, And, oh, these shining, rose-blown hours become her Who folds her small one heaven-close and sings.
