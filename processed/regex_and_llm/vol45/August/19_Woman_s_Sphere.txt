Woman's Sphere Ramona W. Cannon RS. AMINA EL-SAID is an Egyptian journalist and editor of Eve, a magazine with a circulation of 100,000. She also broadcasts over the radio and writes a column called "Ask Me" for Al Mossawar, a political and social weekly dealing with contemporary problems of international scope. Married to a science educator at Cairo University, Mrs. Said is the mother of two sons and a daughter. RS. GLADYS MICKELS, who has been a Morse Code telegraph operator for more than forty years, recently retired. One of the few remaining women in this profession, Mrs. Mickels' career began in Bancroft, Idaho. One of the first messages she received was "The Armistice has been declared — November 11, 1918." NVELL MURBARGER, a contributor to The Relief Society Magazine, whose home is in Costa Mesa, California, has recently published a new book Sovereigns of the Sage, which relates the fascinating life stories of miners, ranchers, hermits, and philosophers of the Southwest. Well known as "The Roving Reporter of the Southwest," Mrs. Murbarger in a previous book, Ghosts of the Glory Trail, related the history of towns and cities which are now only ruins in the desert country.
