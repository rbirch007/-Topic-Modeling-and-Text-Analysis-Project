To meet the indebtedness, the mother may frequently leave her children to themselves while she finds employment out of the home. Irregularities in the home follow: service to God is disregarded, tithing is neglected, prayers become less frequent, persons begin to feel separated and apart from God and church.

The most common reasons for serious financial problems which result in family indebtedness are:

1. Lack of careful and adequate planning.
A. Careless, unplanned day-to-day spending.

2. Spending in excess of the income.

3. Failure to establish future, long-range goals.
A. No reserves for future expenditures.
B. Lack of incentive as a curb to spending.

4. Emotional immaturity—lack of self-discipline.
A. Allowing wants to take precedence over needs.
B. Buying impulsively and heedlessly.
C. Demanding unwarranted luxuries.

5. Too much installment buying.

6. Overuse of credit cards.

7. Unexpected illness requiring hospital and doctor care.

8. Loss of employment.

1. We can avoid going into debt by first eliminating whenever possible the foregoing reasons for it.
2. We should become informed in regard to wise buying and spending. (Consult previous "Homemaking" discussion.)
3. We should avoid so-called easy credit plans.
