A Bazaar Designed Especially for Children
Helen M. Dall, President, Kearns North Stake Relief Society Karen Sorensen, President, Seventeenth Ward Relief Society @ Our whole bazaar was especially focused on items of interest for children and this plan turned out to be a great success. A special booth was prepared to illustrate helps for Family Home Evening. Assignment charts, flannel boards, plaques, lap boards, and activity sheets were prepared. Items for children were attractively displayed. The items for sale in the various displays were all donated. Sister Sharon Seamons was chairman. She had made up a sample item and then assigned several sisters to make the desired number of each item. Over thirty sisters were involved in one single booth, and all seemed willing to help. Several sisters who do not sew or do handwork were especially willing to help with decorating the booths and arranging the displays. Many sisters who are a little timid seemed glad when they were shown what to do.

The items were unusually attractive and included: 4 turtles (made out of round rocks and glued together and painted); 10 handkerchief dolls (top half made and decorated from felt, the bottom half was a handkerchief); 20 dress-up formals (these were sold quickly, materials were scraps of silks and satins and discarded curtains); 3 cow-girl outfits; 8 soldier banks (made from detergent bottles); 20 wall pictures; 6 large bulletin boards (shaped like animals); 4 large playhouses (big enough for little girls to play in, made from cardboard boxes, painted and with curtains added); 10 dress-up hats (made from chlorox bottles and net); 12 wigs (made from yarn); 20 toy guns (cut from wood and painted brown); 6 doll houses (made from garment boxes and decorated); 20 sets of doll furniture; 15 paper dolls and clothes; 20 purses, and many other attractive articles. As a theme on Hawaii, the girls were invited inside a thatched hut (an old pasteboard box covered with strips of cut paper and painted). Each girl received a skirt, lei, and flowers for her hair. Variety of smiles and costumes in the dress-up booth.
