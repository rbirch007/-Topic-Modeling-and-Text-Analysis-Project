British Women Make A Union Jack Design In Knitting
Submitted by Norinne R. Callister, Former Supervisor, British Mission Relief Society

This unique "Union Jack" blanket was knitted by Sister Louisa Ridley (in the picture) and Sister Eda V. Longbone for Elder Dean Orton who took it home to his mother. It took the two women three weeks to make the blanket, working three to four hours a day, and lining the blanket took six hours. Sister Ridley and Sister Longbone belong to the Eastbourne Branch, Sussex, England. Joyce H. Walker is president of the branch Relief Society. Dorothy J. Roberts
