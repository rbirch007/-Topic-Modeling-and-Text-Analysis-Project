Today as I look from my window at half-bare trees, 
at crimson and gold, gay-dotting the landscape over, 
I give grateful thanks to God on bended knees 
For flowers and fruits, for sun and succulent clover; 
That "While the earth remaineth, seedtime and harvest, 
And cold and heat, and summer and winter, and day 
And night shall not cease." The owl and the bluebird shall nest. 
We shall be gladdened alike by the sun's golden ray 
And storms, be they gentle or wild; that age follows youth, 
For we who are a part of it all, treasure each truth 
As seasons come and go and light illumines the world.

—Zara Sabin
