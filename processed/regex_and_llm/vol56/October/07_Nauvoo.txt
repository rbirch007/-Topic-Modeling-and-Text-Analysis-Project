NAUVOO -Nauvoo was a young man's town,
When they braced the fearful fury of the foe.
You can feel it. But those young men, facing west,
Built for shelter—not for yearning,
Did they feel themselves oppressed?
Planned for worship and for learning.

When the band played, and they danced
If these ancient walls could whisper
In the snow? They would reveal it.

Nauvoo is an old, old town.
Nauvoo was a city they could leave—
Time is slow,
Tears on cheek—mouth a-quiver,
Since the frightened children stumbled
As they walked that final track,
While the Carthage cannons rumbled,
Eyes ahead, no looking back,
And the Temple spire crumbled,
There would be no mound of salt on that cold,
Long ago. Icy river.

Surely old men would have wept,
Full of woe,
Sadie O. Clark
