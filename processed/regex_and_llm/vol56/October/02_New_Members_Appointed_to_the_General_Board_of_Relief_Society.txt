New Members Appointed to The General Board of Relief Society

Amy Young Valentine, Provo Thirteenth Ward, East Provo Stake, Utah, is a daughter of Cecile Virginia Skousen and W. Ernest Young. She was with her parents in Argentina for three years while her father was president of the mission. In 1952 her husband Lee B. Valentine was called to Argentina to be mission president. Sister Valentine served as the Relief Society supervisor.

A graduate of Brigham Young University, Sister Valentine has been active in education and community affairs and has been a teacher of music and languages. She now teaches Spanish at Brigham Young University and served as president of BYU 7th Stake Relief Society at the time of her call.

Sister Valentine, now a widow, has four children: Mrs. Colin L. Corbin (Amy Lee); Robert Y. (married to Shauna Burgon); Christina Ruth, working on a Master's Degree at BYU; and Angela, a high school student. There are two grandchildren.

Marian Louise Richards Boyer, Monument Park Ward, Monument Park Stake, Salt Lake City, Utah, is a daughter of Ina Ashton and Elder LeGrand Richards. She is the wife of Harold R. Boyer and mother of five children: three married daughters, Mrs. O. Don Ostler (Barbara), Mrs. J. Fielding Nelson (Carolyn), Mrs. John S. McMaster (Janet); one married son, Roger (married to Sara Stohl); and Richard, who is a student at the University of Utah. There are seventeen grandchildren.

She has served in all the women's auxiliaries of the Church and was Relief Society stake president of Monument Park at the time of her call to the General Board. She is skilled in homemaking arts and many crafts—knitting, sewing, candle making, home decorating, and many types of needlecraft. Sister Boyer is a hard worker and inspirational leader, well qualified for her new responsibilities.
 
October 1969

Marjorie Merrill Reeve, Kansas City First Ward, Kansas City Stake, Missouri, is a daughter of Idetta Ellsworth and Nathan D. Merrill. Her husband is Wayne L. Reeve. Sister Reeve is a graduate of Brigham Young University and has served in several positions of leadership in Seminary, and in the auxiliaries. Her devotion to Relief Society has made her an outstanding leader as teacher and ward president.

For the last eight years she has served as Relief Society stake president of Kansas City Stake. She has endeavored to teach her children the basic principles of Latter-day Saint family living. She has encouraged her sons in their scholastic and athletic interests and her daughters in music. The Reeves have two married daughters, Mrs. David Wade (Kathryn), Mrs. Lynn Southam (Lee Ann); a son Dan on a mission in Japan; two sons, Gerry and Craig; and a daughter Merrilee at home.

Ada Jones Jones, Chandler Second Ward, Mesa Stake, Arizona, is a daughter of Sarah Jane Hurst Jones and John Barker Jones. Her parents were colonizers in Mexico and Virden, New Mexico. Her husband is Archie Lavar Jones. They have six children—two married daughters, Mrs. Norman H. (Linda) and Mrs. Robert E. (Martha); two sons, David and Richard; and two daughters, Mary and Jane.

Jackson (Ada Ruth) and Mrs. Charles Carpenter (Sherilyn); three sons: Keith L., D. Lynn, and Gordon W.; and a daughter Kathleen who is a student at BYU. There are four grandchildren.

Sister Jones is a graduate of Gila Junior College and attended New Mexico State Teachers College. She has held teaching and executive positions in the auxiliaries. She has been a ward Relief Society president and was president of Mesa Stake Relief Society at the time of her appointment to the General Board. Devoted to her home and family, Sister Jones has encouraged her children in Church activities and educational pursuits. Helen Gygi Lach, Monument Park Eleventh Ward, Monument Park Stake, Salt Lake City, Utah, is a daughter of Liesetta Reidelbauch and Rudolph Gygi. Her husband is John Edward Lach. They have a son, David L. (married to Kathryn Lloyd), and two daughters, Mrs. Stephen C. Oswald (Bonnie) and Kathleen. Sister Lach has served in several capacities in the Church auxiliary organizations. She served in the ward Relief Society presidency in two different wards and has been an outstanding sewing instructor, quilting chairman, and homemaking leader. She is especially skillful in all the homemaking arts and crafts, including interior design and decorating. She has imparted her gifts and talents in sewing, needlecraft, doll making, designing gifts and articles for bazaars to many Relief Society women, who, in turn, have beautified their homes and served Relief Society.

Sister Lach was a member of the Tabernacle Choir for eleven years.

Orlene Jones Poulsen, Monument Park Third Ward, Monument Park West Stake, is a daughter of Louie Orlene Thomas and V. Ashton Jones, and was born in Cedar City, Utah. Her husband is George Poulsen Jr. They have four daughters, Lou Ellen and Lana Rae attending the University of Utah, Rosalie and Sharon.

Sister Poulsen is a graduate of the University of Utah and has been a school teacher. Her Church work has included teaching in the auxiliaries. She has been a class leader in spiritual living, cultural refinement, and social relations, as well as serving in Relief Society while her husband was Hawaiian Mission president. She was serving as Relief Society president of Monument Park West Stake at the time of her call to the General Board. She is international president of Lamba Delta Sigma (the Church organization for women missionaries). Her special interests are her home and family, and she enjoys homemaking, sewing, reading, and music. October 1969 Aline Rawson Pettit, Monument Park Seventh Ward, Monument Park West Stake, Salt Lake City, Utah, was born in Provo, Utah, a daughter of Lydia Knudsen and William K. Rawson. Her husband is Lynn A. Pettit, and they have four children, Mrs. Ole Dunn (Kathleen), Mrs. Robert Bohn (Peggyanne), Lynn A., recently returned from the Southwest Indian Mission, and Marilyn.

Sister Pettit is a graduate of the University of California, and has studied three years at the University of Utah as a graduate student. She is a professional librarian and has worked in several libraries. She is a former member of the General Board of the Primary Association, and has served as a cultural refinement class leader in two stakes and also in Libya, North Africa, for the wives of Air Force personnel at Wheelrus. Sister Pettit has been active in the field of literature, teaching adult education classes.

Johna Mary de St. Jeor, Holladay Eighth Ward, Holladay Stake, Salt Lake City, Utah, is a daughter of Melva Adamson and Wallace Bramwell de St. Jeor. She is a graduate of Brigham Young University and has studied in the graduate schools at the University of Mexico and the University of Utah. She has been stake speech director in San Bernardino Stake, attendance secretary in Mexico City Stake, and mission MIA board member in Montevideo, Uruguay. She served as Relief Society coordinator and interpreter in Uruguay. She has been employed as an airlines reservations agent and as a social worker. Her special interests are sewing and many different homemaking and decorating crafts. She is widely traveled and speaks and reads several languages and is interested in music and art. She has enjoyed working with the Indian people and other peoples of Mexico and South America. She is a teacher of history at the present time. Beverly Jensen Pond, Monument Park Thirteenth Ward, Monument Park Stake, Salt Lake City, Utah, is a daughter of Edith A. Hoagland and David Jensen. Her husband is Farrel Smith Pond, and they have five children: one married daughter, Mrs. Samuel E. Burggraaf (Farelyn), and returned missionary son Nicholas, and three other sons, Marden, Kevin, and Talmage. There are two grandchildren.

Sister Pond attended the University of Utah and graduated from the McCune School of Music. She has been organist and chorister in all the ward auxiliary organizations, and has been a stake Relief Society chorister and ward president. She is a professional piano teacher and a charter member of the Utah Teachers National Association, accredited with the Music Teachers National Association. She is a devoted homemaker and extends her many gifts and talents to her family, her Church, and the community. October 1969 Inez Tolman Waldron,
