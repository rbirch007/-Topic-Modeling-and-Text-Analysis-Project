The snow sneaked in on us last night
On downy, padded feet.
So still it is as flakes float down
All muffled in the street.
The cars creep past like shrouded ghosts.
No children shout aloud.
The sky above is quiet, too;
One massive, great gray cloud.

How peaceful and serene earth lies
Under its coverlet of white.
That softly, silently drifted down
During the tranquil night.

Lucy W. Jones
