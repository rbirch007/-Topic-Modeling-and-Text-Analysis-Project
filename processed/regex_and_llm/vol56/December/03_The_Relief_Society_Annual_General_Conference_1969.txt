@ "The fields of compassion have not all been plowed," said Elder Bruce R. McConkie as he addressed the opening session of the 1969 Annual General Conference of the Relief Society held October 1 and 2 in Salt Lake City. A colorful assembly of Relief Society women from various parts of the world heard revered priesthood and Relief Society leaders in numerous sessions give words of comfort and faith, of admonition and instruction to the Society.

"Religion must not become an isolated and uninfluential afterthought," but its "driving power must be harnessed" in order that the "needs of today be met." So

This group of Lamanite Singing Mothers performed in the Presidencies Department of the Annual Relief Society General Conference, October 2, 1969. Their singing was under the arrangement of Margaret E. Dahl, Supervisor, Northern Indian Mission Relief Society.

Spoke President Hugh B. Brown in the General Session of the conference. President Joseph Fielding Smith, Elder Mark E. Petersen, and Elder Marion G. Romney gave inspiring addresses urging women to use their powers (as women) to better the world, to strengthen their homes, to encourage their husbands; to place their confidence in the priesthood, to teach their sons to understand and honor their priesthood, and to "save souls" as they were originally instructed to do by the Prophet Joseph Smith.

The messages of the priesthood leaders and those of the Relief Society General Presidency and other participants of the conference together with the moving, and at times, ethereal music rendered by Singing Mothers choruses combined to make this conference, according to many, the "best ever."

New emphasis was given to excellence in music by the rendition of an hour-long choral presentation together with a narration during the Stake Board session in the Tabernacle. The combined Singing Mothers choruses of the Cottonwood, Mill Creek, Murray, and Wilford Regions, under the brilliant musicianship of Sister Ellen N. Barnes of the General Board, provided the conference with a new dimension in this phase of Relief Society work. The chorus was accompanied by Roy M. Darley, a Tabernacle organist.

The galaxy of nine departmental meetings held simultaneously produced ideas designed to assist in the various facets of Relief Society work.
