Mrs. Antoinette Concello has been a circus performer since 1929 when she joined a flying trapeze act. She retired for a time when her son was young, but now that he is in law school, she has reinvested her valuable experience to further the future of the circus and now serves Barnum and Bailey Circus as director of the aerial ballet.

Women Pilots in the United States number seventy who hold air transport ratings from the Federal Aviation Administration.

Approximately another 1700 hold commercial licenses, while an additional 500 are licensed flight instructors. The airlines do not hire women as air transport pilots. Mrs. Roxa Wright, Woman's Day needlework editor and herself a needlework designer, collected for the New York Hallmark Gallery's spring exhibit of America's needlework: past and present, many rare and breath-takingly beautiful items. Some, lent by museums and historical collections date from early eighteenth century. Embroidery, appliqué, elaborate petit-point, crochet, all kinds of handwork embellished utilitarian objects, and clothes demonstrate touchingly the skills of many countries and a depth and breadth of aesthetic feelings. Mrs. Lois Arnow and Mrs. Mary B. Kadesch are "plant librarians" at the University of Utah Herbarium, a plant reference library. The library furnishes information on many species of plants, identifies poisonous plants, edible and non-edible plants, and supplies data for medical research, as well as aiding air pollution experts and police officers and others in the identification of narcotic specimens.
