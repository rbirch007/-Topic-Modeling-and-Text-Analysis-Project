Swedish Cookery For Christmas
Darlene C. Dedekind Member, General Board of Relief Society (L)

A typical Swedish holiday meal would be aptly termed a feast. Many varieties of seafood make up the first course. This is followed by baked ham and red cabbage, which is followed by meat balls and various kinds of cheeses. The main course of the meal is Lutfisk, a traditional Swedish dish served with potatoes and a white sauce. Lutfisk is available in package form in many import stores. Rice pudding is the popular dessert for such a feast, and there are several interesting customs surrounding it. Among these is that one almond is included in the recipe, and the person who gets this will be married within the year.

This person is not to tell MODEL NANCY ANN JOHANSSON Swedish cookery for Christmas that he or she has it, but the excitement runs so high among the single girls, it is nearly impossible to keep it a secret. Before eating the rice pudding, each person is supposed to say a little rhyme he or she has made up, then light a candle. The first candle that burns down indicates that person has been naughty during the year. Following the feast, Santa Claus generally appears and the delightful festivities of Christmas begin. 1 lb. Ground beef 
1 tsp. Nutmeg or 1/2 tsp. Allspice 
1/4 c. Fine bread crumbs 
2 tbsp. Shortening 
1 egg 
2 tbsp. Butter or margarine 
2-3 tsp. Salt 
2-3 tbsp. Flour 
1/2 tsp. Pepper 
1/4 c. Beef broth or consommé

Combine ground beef (the Swedish put the beef through a meat grinder three times), bread crumbs, egg, salt, and pepper, and nutmeg or allspice (optional). Mix well and make into 1-inch meat balls with firm hand-pressure. Let stand in bowl one hour. Brown meat balls in shortening, remove from pan. Add butter or margarine to the pan in which the meat balls were browned, and when butter has melted add flour. Blend. Add beef broth or consommé and bring to a boil. Add meat balls, cover pan, and simmer over low heat for 1-1/2 hours, or may be put into a baking dish and cooked in a 325°F oven for 1-1/4 hours.

CHRISTMAS GINGER SNAPS (Pepperkakor)
2/3 c. shortening 
2/3 c. Sifted all-purpose flour 
3/4 c. Light brown sugar 
1 tsp. Baking soda, firmly packed 
1 tbsp. Cinnamon 
2 tbsp. Molasses 
1/4 tsp. Cloves, powdered 
3 tbsp. Water 
1 tsp. Ground cardamon seeds 
1 tsp. Grated lemon rind

Cream shortening and sugar thoroughly. Add molasses, water, and lemon rind. Sift flour with baking soda and spices. Add to creamed mixture a little at a time. Blend well. Cover bowl and chill. When thoroughly chilled, flour hands and turn dough onto a floured surface. Roll very thin and cut with floured cookie cutter into desired shapes. Bake on a greased cookie sheet at 350°F for 8 to 10 minutes. Makes approximately 5 dozen cookies.

By adding more flour (3/4 to 1 c.) to the cookie recipe, the dough can be rolled into balls in the palm of your hand, then rolled in granulated sugar, an almond placed on top, and baked. If desired, spread the warm rolled cookies with the following icing.

FRUIT SOUP (Fruktsoppa)
1/2 lb. Seedless raisins 
1/4 c. Cinnamon candies or 1/2 |b. Prunes 
1 stick cinnamon 
1/2 lb. Dried apricots 
1/4 c. Sugar 
2 apples 
8-10 cooked plums 
2 slices lemon 
2 cooked (canned) peaches 
2 slices orange 
3 quarts of liquid (water and any 4 tbsp. Tapioca juice from the canned fruits)

Wash raisins, prunes, and apricots and soak overnight in the water and juice. To this add apples, peeled and sliced, lemons and orange slices, tapioca, sugar, and broken cinnamon stick or cinnamon candy. Simmer for 1 hour. Add cooked fruit which has been sliced. Let stand for 3 to 4 hours to mellow. Serve hot or cold, with or without cream.

Step 1. Select box (remove any knobs on the box)
a. If old finish, sand to smoothness ready for new finish.
b. If new wood, sand lightly, apply 1 coat of lacquer to seal the wood.

Step 2. Apply lace using a good quality glue. Move lace around until you have a pleasing design.
Step 3. Apply several coats of gesso.

The purpose is to fully permeate the lace and make it hard enough not to absorb further coats of finish. Be sure to dry thoroughly between coats.

Step 4. Apply color. A high grade satin finish lacquer that takes about 20 minutes to dry is best. Mix with artist's oil paints to the desired shade. Prepare sufficient paint to cover the surface at least four times. Apply two to three coats to whole box, lace and all, allowing time for thorough drying between each coat.

Step 5. Following directions for gold leafing, apply gold to lace area. When size is almost dry (about 40 minutes) apply the gold leaf and make no attempt to remove the loose excess gold until dry (overnight). Next day remove extra gold with a clean dry brush, seal with one coat of lacquer, smooth the edges of gilt area with a light touch-up of color.
