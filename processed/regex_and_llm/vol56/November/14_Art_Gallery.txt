Art treasures are here along this quiet street as in the corridors of a great gallery. A still life "Old Man in the Sun," picture of serenity. A landscape "Autumn Gold" depicts a weeping birch dripping bright tears. Lovers hand in hand "Portrait of Young Love." Even I, framed in this moment of time, title "Walk Along a Country Lane."

Art treasures are here, painted in living colors, signed by the Master.

Dorothy R. Cox

Her most recent undertakings have been making miniature and regular sized hats for tourists. She has averaged making twenty quilts a year for more than twenty-five years. Her family of eleven children, thirty-six grandchildren, and twenty-eight great-grandchildren have received many items of clothing and quilts from her. She is a generous and faithful Relief Society member, and still is an active visiting teacher who is welcomed in all homes because of her lovely spirit and happy outlook on life.

Ina Wilson Hummell, St. George Stake, Utah, has filled the lives and homes of those close to her with many lovely handmade articles, ranging from exquisite quilts to delightful children's toys. She has a talent for painting, and makes the faces of dolls and other toys truly come to life. Sister Hummel is the mother of five children and there are nine grandchildren to add to her joy. All of them are appreciative of her talents, but more so of her wonderful spirit and the things she teaches them.
