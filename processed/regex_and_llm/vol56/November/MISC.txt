Subscription Price to Be Increased
It is with genuine regret we announce at this time that regardless of our efforts to continue The Relief Society Magazine at its present $2.00 subscription price, it now becomes necessary for us to increase it to $2.50 for the twelve issues per year. (There will be no change at this time in the subscription price of the Spanish edition.) This price change becomes effective January 1, 1970 and applies to all subscriptions including renewals which start with the January 1970 issue of the Magazine or any month thereafter. Subscriptions beginning in September, October, November, or December 1969, or renewals beginning in these months will be at the $2.00 price. Subscriptions for one year at a time only will be accepted.

Increases in the costs of labor, printing, paper, and postage force this step upon us. Presidents, we urge you fully to explain this action to ward and branch presidencies and to enlist their support in making certain that the number of subscriptions does not decline either for the sake of the organization or for the sake of the sisters who need the Magazine's instructions, guidance, and influence. We suggest that ward presidents take a few moments of time in the regular Relief Society meeting 'to call attention to some feature of the Magazine, thus creating interest in it and deepened appreciation for it.

Tall, slim, and beautiful at eighteen, she gazed discontentedly at her mother. "I don't have any special gift," she complained. "I have only weaknesses." Have you shared, with this teenager, an overwhelming sense of inadequacy? When you wanted to make a musical instrument sing, have you ever struck a wrong note? When you wanted to share a particularly radiant sunset, have you responded less than eloquently with, "It's beautiful," having no words to match your emotion? Have you wished that you were more able? In such moments of inadequacy it helps to remember, among others, Moroni, Moses, and Paul. Moroni fearing the mockery of the Gentiles "... Because of awkwardness of our [his] hands" (Ether 12:24) complained, "... When we write we behold our weakness, and stumble because of the placing of our words." (Ether 12:25.) The Lord answered him saying, "I give unto men weakness that they may be humble; and my grace is sufficient for all men that humble themselves before me; for if they humble themselves before me, and have faith in me, then will I make weak things become strong unto them." (Ether 12:27.) As we read the later words of Moroni, we know the truth of God's promise, because Moroni expressed some of the greatest concepts given to man. The Apostle Paul whose magnificent gospel teachings make up the great bulk of the apostolic writings of the New Testament said: And lest I should be exalted above measure through the abundance of the revelations, there was given to me a thorn in the flesh, the messenger of Satan to buffet me, lest I should be exalted above measure. Most gladly therefore will I rather glory in my infirmities, that the power of Christ may rest upon me. Therefore I take pleasure in infirmities, in reproaches, in necessities, in persecutions, in distresses for Christ's sake: for when I am weak, then am [ strong. (II Cor. 12:7-10.) 863 November 1969 Moses said unto the Lord, "... I am not eloquent but I am slow of speech, and of a slow tongue. And the Lord said unto him.... I will be with thy mouth, and teach thee what thou shalt say." (Exodus 4:10-12.) The great promise of the Lord is to all of us. We grow through struggle. If we humble ourselves and have faith, our weaknesses will help us to grow strong.
