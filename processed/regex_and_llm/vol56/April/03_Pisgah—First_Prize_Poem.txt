Ina J. Hobson

"Here, take my hand," I said but yesterday, and now you stand so tall, The need has passed, and you walk on alone. The promised land of all your life's tomorrows— Where I may not go— Is beckoning in the golden distance now, and lies, Bright as a Midas kingdom, in the shimmering air. It seems to your enchanted gaze a land Of milk and honey Flowing from rich reservoirs of ages past. And so, in truth, it is. But know this, too: Beyond the river's leafy, sunlit edge The world's dark wisdom waits. The hills and valleys seen so fair will soon Or later make demand of all your powers. Hear, dear child of light: obedience and sacrifice Are still the law As anciently they were. And God will have The broken heart and contrite spirit now as then.

Give me your hand once more; I go out Lonely upon the hills to escape your going.
