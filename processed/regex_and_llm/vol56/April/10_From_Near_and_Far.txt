One sister writes she is not interested in the fiction but enjoys the lessons. Others write in goodly number expressing thanks for the family situations found in the short stories saying that solutions for their own problems were suggested through the fictionalized situations. A young mother wishes to have help given her in rearing her young family. A sister especially appreciates the recipes, and many express thanks for the handicraft instruction articles. Others tell of their delight in the four-color sections (run four times a year) and tell of framing the art reproductions.

The mail gives a cross-section of the talents and interests of Latter-day Saint women. Some write expressing disappointment at missing an issue because the subscription renewal was not received in time. It is hoped that Magazines which expire in June and July will be renewed promptly by May 1 and June 1, respectively, so no issues of the lessons will be lost, since back numbers cannot be supplied.

The lessons for October in the Northern Hemisphere and the following March for the Southern Hemisphere begin in the July issue with the previews found in the June issue. The General Board is doing all in its power to maintain the subscription price at $2 which has been in effect since November 1957. It seeks to be provident in all phases of publishing the Magazine, and for that reason orders only the number of Magazines for which there are paid subscriptions.

Since all costs have gone up in the past eleven years, with no increase in subscription price, and since the advertisements are held to a minimum, it is essential that the increase in subscriptions continue satisfactorily. The calling of a Magazine representative is therefore one of importance, needing a spirit of dedication. The General Board expresses appreciation to the dedicated Magazine representatives, to their presidencies, and to all Latter-day Saint women who are subscribers to The .

It is hoped that bound volumes are being preserved in ward and stake libraries for future reference and historical value. For the twenty-third year South Los Angeles Stake leads the Church in having the highest per cent of subscriptions, with 192%. The highest mission award goes to Western States, with 121%. The missions are to be especially commended in that nine had 100% and over.

Glendale Stake had the highest number of subscriptions, with 1456, and the East Central States Mission had the highest for the missions_1504 subscriptions. There were 260 stakes over 100% in 1968, an increase of 80 over the previous year. In 88 stakes, moreover, every ward was 100% and over.

The total subscriptions, in English, for 1968 were 277,386. Spanish-speaking sisters in the United States, Mexico, and Central and South America rejoice in the opportunity of having the contents of The available to them in Spanish. The circulation of the Spanish edition has reached 6000, making a grand total of 283,386 for The in both languages.
