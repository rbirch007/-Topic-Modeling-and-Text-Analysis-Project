I'm sitting in my big arm chair 
Passing the time away, 
Nothing seems to change 
It's the same from day to day. 

I'm sitting quietly by 
Counting the hours till night, 
There's nothing I can do 
But wait for the waning light. 

My strength is almost gone | can hardly leave my chair, 
Will I rise tomorrow To wait by the window there?

118 "~~ ee Grandmother (Mada W. Stanger) and the Stockings

I remember Grandmother's visits! How we looked forward to her coming! These visits were not too frequent, and only once in awhile did she stay all day or overnight. Grandma is just a little woman, but her love and sweet personality are just the reverse of her size. We loved to see her come, because of her love and kindness. No grandmother could have shown more love for grandchildren than she showed us.

Now the years have rolled along, and Grandmother is getting older. Her little body is getting frail, and she is not as active as she used to be. But as long as I live I shall remember Grandma and the stockings. There were eight growing, active, healthy children in our family. We lived on a farm, and there was always plenty to be done. All the girls were taught the art of homemaking at an early age by our sweet mother, as we cooked for hay men, put up fruit.
