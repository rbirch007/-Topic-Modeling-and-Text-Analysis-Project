Little winter sparrows,
Wonder why you stay when almost all the feathered folk seek winter holiday.
You glean the seeded berries, the sparsely proffered crumbs,
Weather tempered to the cold—and wind's bleak, wailing drums.
When the sun obscured from sight wears its mask of gray—you
Perch upon a snowy limb, giving your song away.

Thank you for your presence in the spruce tree old and blue,
Thank you for your silver song that changes daytime's hue.

Beulah Huish Sadleir
