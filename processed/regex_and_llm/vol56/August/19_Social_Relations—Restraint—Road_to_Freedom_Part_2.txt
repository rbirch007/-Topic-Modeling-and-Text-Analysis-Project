Lesson 2: Restraint—Road to Freedom, Part

Northern Hemisphere: Third Meeting, November 1969
Southern Hemisphere: April 1970

To point out that understanding the difference between immutable, spiritual and physical laws and the regulations which apply to them, may influence one's attitude toward the regulations.

Our Heavenly Father, with infinite wisdom and understanding, seemingly reveals admonition and warning to his earth children when that counsel and commandment may be most needed and rewarding. How beneficial the divine counsel may be depends upon our willingness to accept and follow the revealed commandment. In lessons 6 and 7 of the current Melchizedek Priesthood course of study, President Clark points out several occasions when this divine counsel and warning have been given to us. (Other examples might be cited.) This correlating lesson considers these occasions and also distinguishes between the principle involved in the law and the rules and regulations which make possible the enforcement of the law.

Again, much will be quoted directly from the Melchizedek Priesthood course of study since President Clark's clarification of the subject is both logically and interestingly expressed.

Consider the example of ancient Israel to whom the law of the Lord was given, by way of restraint, in the affirmative commandment of the Decalogue, "Thou shalt have no other gods before me." (Exod. 20:3.) How needed this commandment was at that particular time is evident when we recall that Israel, newly emancipated from Egyptian bondage, was spiritually weak as a result of having lived for generations in an idolatrous land. The liberated people were now moving into a land (Canaan) inhabited by a people who likewise were idolators, worshipping many gods, and practicing human sacrifice. These environments were not compatible with the life the chosen people were to live if they were to carry out the work which God had planned for them. The idolatrous practices were not consistent with the building of a personal moral life nor with the building of stable homes upon which a Lesson Department righteous and sound society is dependent.

President Clark states: God's work could not then, and cannot now, be maintained in partnership with a worship which calls for the heathenish practices of human sacrifice and grossly immoral rites. Our own civilization cannot be preserved in the presence of such heathenish practices, for a moment's reflection will show how human sacrifice would outrage every feeling of humanity that generations have fostered.... In our time when the Western world has come to a worship of one God only, the need for such a commandment, as among ourselves, is no longer necessary, unless it is to be applied figuratively. (It may be parenthetically observed that such an application might not be a strained one, for modern man-chas, in a too large extent, made materialism his god.) (Immortality and Eternal Life, Vol. 2, lesson 6.)

Thus we see how important it was for this restraining commandment to be given at that particular time.

Another example of restraining counsel given in a time of special need is the law of health known to us of today as the Word of Wisdom. We discuss it here:

1. As a restraint or forewarning given at a time of special need.
2. As an example that truth, although relating to the physical body, is as unchanging—unless some other law intervenes—as the immutable laws governing other areas of the physical universe.

Although the Word of Wisdom was revealed to us before scientific research had given us the confirming facts of which we now are aware, modern science supports the claims of this law of health. An interesting point of view is clarified by President Clark:

It is demonstrated that nicotine, alcohol, tannin, and caffein exert their effects upon the body, and the Lord and modern science say these effects are harmful. These effects are visited upon us whether we wish the effects or not; whether we consent to them or disprove them. These noxious things enforce their own mandates against the body, no other agency is necessary or used. This is the law.

Some complain about the Word of Wisdom as if it were a rule of conduct imposed upon them by the Church authorities and so a rule that could be changed by the Church authorities. In accordance with this idea certain persons have suggested changes or modifications of the Word of Wisdom. But the Word of Wisdom is not a rule of conduct; it is a law—the Lord's law—of health. It was promulgated by him. The law existed before he told it to us; it would exist if the revelation were blotted out from the book. The Church authorities have nothing to do with the law. God, speaking through the forces of the physical world, has prescribed it, and so long as those forces exist, the law will remain.

It is therefore the foolish ignorance of a child to assume that the First Presidency can issue a rule that will permit the use of any of these injurious things without their harmful effects. It would be an easy and, in one sense, a pleasing gesture, as satisfying Church members who wish to use these harmful substances to declare the Word of Wisdom no longer existent. But such a declaration would be no more efficacious than a declaration that the law of gravitation no longer operates.

(Ibid., lesson 7.)

For Discussion
1. Perhaps you are one who has wished that the Word of Wisdom might be modified or withdrawn. If so, does the foregoing approach to this law of health add a new dimension to your thinking?
2. How may the attitude toward the Word of Wisdom presented by President Clark improve one's thinking relationship to the Church authorities?
3. Would young people be influenced more favorably toward acceptance of this divine admonition if they were taught from the beginning that it is not merely a restraint imposed upon them by the Church but an unchanging law of health?

It is relevant to recall that the Lord also gave a law of health to ancient Israel during the days of wandering in the wilderness.

It was a dietary and sanitary code, given through Moses, and was founded on scientific principles. One of the prohibitions of that code was that the Israelites should not eat sea foods unless they had fins and scales. President Clark states: Today we eat many wholesome seafoods that do not meet this ancient requirement. Again it must be said that it is not for me to attempt to suggest the reasons why the Lord gave this great law, nor the reasons why he made this and other dietary distinctions. But looking at the matter from the point of view of human knowledge, one reason why the Lord forbade such things to ancient Israel might have been the extreme perishability of certain sea foods which would not be found either in the wilderness or in Palestine, seafoods which rapidly decay, and which, in decaying, generate poisons destructive of health and, indeed, of life itself. Ancient Israel had no such rapid transportation as would enable the delivery of such food materials while they were still fresh, and no refrigerating processes by which they might preserve such sea food materials pending the time they were to be eaten. Under such conditions, even a wise human lawgiver would today enact such a law as was given to ancient Israel, and for the reasons I have suggested. But in our time, with our rapid transportation, our efficient refrigeration, such seafoods may be properly preserved and, so preserved, seem as wholesome as other sea foods that have fins and scales; the Lord has not forbidden these foods to us and we eat them.

By way of comparison it may be observed in passing that the difference in the character of the two codes of laws given and of the things proscribed in each is an important illustration and example of how God speaks to a people in accordance with their stage of knowledge, their culture, and their surroundings. I do not pretend to say why the Lord gave to Israel some commands and withheld others. But this much may be said. It was seemingly unnecessary to forbid the use of tobacco, tea, and coffee to ancient Israel, for apparently they knew nothing of these things; and alcoholism though forbidden to the priests under certain conditions and discountenanced to the people, had not taken hold of them. It was unnecessary in our day to give the intimate instructions of the Mosaic code concerning sanitation and diet because science was revealing to us the laws relating to such things and we were generally obeying them. But our science had not yet reached, when the Word of Wisdom was given, accepted conclusions upon drugs and narcotics, nor upon the effects of alcoholism which had become almost a besetting sin. In this situation the Lord revealed his modern law of health. So God spoke to each people in the language and terms they understood and in accordance with their needs.
