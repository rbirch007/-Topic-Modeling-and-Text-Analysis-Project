Fisher Towers (Continued from page 517) off the bottom of Fisher Towers, on up toward the LaSal Mountains, in Forbidden Valley. Forbidden Valley is so named because it is so difficult to reach. In Onion Canyon the roadbed and the stream bed are the same for about a mile. The canyon is narrow, only ten feet wide in one place, and not much more all the way, with the walls rising four hundred feet. These walls are the same deep red as the Fisher Towers, being part of the same formation. The whole Moab area is full of places of great beauty and strange sights, towers and gorges, cliffs and canyons. Yet even here Fisher Towers are unique and deserving of the visitor's highest praise.
