Where Drift Logs Lie Mary Gustafson The smoke curls lazily upward From the beach where drift logs lie, Curling beyond our seeing, Disappearing into the sky. I rest on the pillowing seaweed 'Spilled from the going tide, Etching the scene with pictures— Completely self-satisfied. I search through a million pebbles, For one of a different hue, Drifting them past lax fingers As a questing child might do. The driftwood sinks to ashes, The sun is an embered coal, And I am filled with a restfulness That irradiates my soul. Page 14] 142 RELIEF SOCIETY MAGAZINE—FEBRUARY 1952
