Lets Stuff a Rug Thalia Black oo strips of material three inches wide, and sew together end to end. Cut strips of cotton batting one-inch wide and fold inside the strips of material. Stuffing can be made from old blankets, cotton from a worn-out mattress, or even old overalls will do. Sew edges of material together on the machine, holding stuffing firmly inside. Braid tightly. Lay the rug flat on the table on floor while sewing the braids together, so that it will lie flat when finished. Sew with heavy linen thread doubled, or according to instructions given in The Relief Society Magazine, May 1949, page 317. This is good for an evening of family fun and works up quickly for a good-sized rug in a single evening.
