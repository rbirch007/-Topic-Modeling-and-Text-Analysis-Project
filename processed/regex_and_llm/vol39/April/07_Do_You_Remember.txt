Do You Remember? Grace Sayre The rain-wet dogwood bursts along the land, Its tinted blossoms opal as the day; Beside the stream, the silver, slanting rain Repeats its song of spring. Yet again The reaching ferns unroll their fronds of gay, Soft feathers for the trillium's white bells Unfolding for the advent of new spring; Mark now the flash of bluebirds on the wing. Do you remember how the soft, sweet rain Opened the showy balls of white pear bloom? The rain that dripped in silver on each pane, Washing away the darkness and the winter— Do you remember? Do you still recall Its cleansing heart and soul from dark and gloom? Page 236 e
