Lost Season Eva: Willes Wangsgaard That year the frost was victor over gardens. In their old feud he conquered every bout. Frost was a sleek white panther every morning And left dark tracks when he was driven out. I'd never thought to see the lilacs frozen, | A summer without lilacs could not be. ~My heart could not believe, the buds unopened, No fluted flasks of perfume on the tree. Incredible that there should be a summer Rose-trimmed and scented tripping down the year To meet the sorrel-mounted, red-caped autumn While I still wait for lilacs to appear!
