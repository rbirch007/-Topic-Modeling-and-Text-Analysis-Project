He ran through fields, like a puppy in his play; He found the underneath of sun: warm stones, A world of quick new creatures... Day on day Such learning filled his eyes and stretched his bones; But not one singing line was ever penned To capture his delights when he was small. Regretful, now I search my thought's frayed end. (David is eight years old, and very tall.) His little self is vanished now, Like petals blown from an apple bough. And his mother marvels, and sighs, and sings Only shadow-songs of rememberings.
