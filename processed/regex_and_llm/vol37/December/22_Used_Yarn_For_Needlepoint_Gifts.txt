Used Yarn for Needlepoint Gifts Rachel K. Laurgaard Illustration by Elizabeth Williamson HERE is more yarn than you think in those old sweaters and knit suits. And, if you are a needlepoint addict, you will make good use of it! Unravel the garment, and wind the yarn into loose hanks. Rinse these hanks in cool water to smooth out the kinks, and hang them over a towel rack to dry. | If the yarn is finer than ordinary tapestry wool, use two or more strands in your needle. Stitching diagonally across the canvas, dovetailing each row with the next, will make a firm, evenly padded back of basket weaving, increasing the wearing qualities of the piece and making it easier to block. One two-piece, plum-colored knit suit yielded enough yarn for six fifteen-inch square dining-room chairs—and it was necessary to use three strands in the needle!
