We here today are possessed of these two greatest of all gifts which God can bestow upon man, life and the right to make that life what we choose. The use we make of any gift determines its value to us. We may be given a precious book, one with an elegant binding, containing the world's greatest wisdom within its printed pages. If we put that book away, and fail to ever bring it out to admire its beauty or to partake of the wisdom of its contents, it is of no more value to us than an empty scratch pad. As a general rule, we are anxious to put the gift of choice to good use, we are anxious to improve our conditions, and to improve our minds, anxious to strengthen our will to do good and to overcome evil. But the great difficulty with us is that we know so poorly how to evaluate what is good and what is bad for us. It is easy for us to accept the gospel in principle, but to live those principles every day of our lives is more difficult.
