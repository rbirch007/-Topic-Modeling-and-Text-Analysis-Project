Edith turned and tossed until she was thoroughly miserable. So long had she been wrapped in the cocoon of her own tragedy and misery that her thoughts and emotions had all turned inward. To think, even momentarily, of the problems of others, as she had been forced to do in the last few days, was painful, had made her ill. Mr. Lewis' vaguely worded concern about his daughter's coming wedding, his mother's wish for death, 22 even Amanda's dissatisfaction, had made inroads on her concern, but this was different.
