Hobbies for Happiness At left, Nellie M. Clark, holding a china pitcher which she decorated; at right, Luella R. Adams, holding one of her water color paintings. These two Relief Society women of Parowan, Utah, now in their "'seventies,"' have found much pleasure in their hobbies which have developed into real accomplishments. Sister Adams began painting with water colors at the age of seventy-five. Her paintings, which reveal much beauty in design and color harmony, were exhibited at the Relief Society spring fashion show. For many years Sister Adams "'had a hunger for art work, but living in a small community and rearing a large family, she could not, until her later years, satisfy this longing." Now she enjoys her painting each day. Sister Clark began piecing quilts before she was five years old, and has designed and made many articles of almost every type of needlework. She is an expert at flower making and has sold ninety baby quilts, many chair sets, and numerous rugs, aprons, and handkerchiefs through the Mormon Handicraft Shop. Last winter she joined a class in ceramics. "I have always been thrilled to see the ceramic articles in the shops," says Sister Clark, "'so when the opportunity came to me I was glad to try my hand at the work—and I surely love it." Sister Clark is doing exquisite work and making use of the native clay found in the Parowan hills. As Relief Society workers, both Sister Adams and Sister Clark have long records of varied service.
