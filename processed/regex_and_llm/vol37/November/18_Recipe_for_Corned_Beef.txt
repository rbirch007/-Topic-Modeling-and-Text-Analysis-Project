Close of the Apostolic Ministry— The Revelation Through John No definite date has been fixed when the last apostle chosen in Christ's dispensation ceased to minister unto the people. John, the Beloved, was evidently the last. Paul declares about thirty years after the ascension that the gospel had been carried to every nation. His words are: "Preached to every creature which is under heaven" (Col. 1:23, also verse 6). This probably means the world known to Paul. However, Eusebius, a writer of the fourth century, says: "Thus, then, under a celestial influence and cooperation, the doctrine of the Savior, like the rays of the sun, quickly irradiated the whole world." There is no doubt that, under the inspired apostles and those who assisted them, the message of the gospel was taken rapidly to the known world. Branches of the Church were organized in many of the cities and towns. Those ancient worthies were wonderful messengers for the risen Lord.
