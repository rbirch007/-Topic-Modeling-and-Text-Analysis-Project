Hemmed Placket This placket is used most frequently on baby dresses to finish the back neck opening. It is also a simple and practical type for gathered skirts. Slash the center back of dress or skirt on the grain of the material, to the desired length. For the under side of the placket, stitch a narrow hem, by hand or machine, on the raw edge, tapering off to nothing at 107 HEMMED PLACKET the bottom. Faced Underarm Placket A faced or a faced and hemmed placket gives a flat, smooth closing with snaps or hooks. It may be used on light wools or silks in situations where zippers are not practical or not desired. It is also frequently used on the bottom of tight-fitting sleeves. In making this placket, both front and back edges are faced with a lengthwise strip of matching fabric. Stitch, press, and edge finish the side and waistline seams then: 1. Cut two strips on the lengthwise grain 14% inches wide and 2 inches longer than the opening. 2. Place the strips on the front and back placket edges with right sides together. Using a % inch seam allowance, and leaving one inch above and below placket ends, place the strip for the front edge so that it can be stitched on the seam line of the garment. 'The strip for the back edge can be stitched at least % inch from the side seam line. Stitch the strips to the garment, backstitching at the ends. 3. Steam press the placket seams open and trim allowances on facings to Y% inch. Allowances on a dress may be left wider for possible alteration. 'Turn facings to the wrong side and press well to insure thin edges along the opening. 4. Finish raw edges to prevent fraying. 5. Anchor seam tape behind front facing through which to sew snaps and hooks and eyes. Fasten front facing to dress with an invisible hemming stitch. 6. On the back edge, clip through seam allowance at top and bottom so they will turn forward under front edge. Hand stitch to front at top and bottom. Overcast raw edges. If seam allowances are wide enough, one or both raw edges may be hemmed back instead of faced. In this case edges must be reinforced with seam tape to prevent stretching.
