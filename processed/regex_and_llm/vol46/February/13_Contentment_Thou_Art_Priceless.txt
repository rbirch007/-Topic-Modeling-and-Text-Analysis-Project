"See how the wild roses twine themselves around the place," said Alice. "They seem so very appropriate for children's graves, don't they?" Marian was deeply moved and she contemplated seriously the meaning of life. "What is true greatness, Jess?" she finally asked, voicing the thought that had come to her in that moment of contemplation. A long pause ensued, then her stalwart brother lifted his head and answered, "A good father and a good mother are great. I am sure Grandfather and Grandmother were great people. If a man and woman are honest and dependable, if they love others and are loved in return, if they sincerely try to keep the commandments of God, helping their neighbors, then I think they are great. If they rear a fine family that is a worthy contribution to society, they are great. And to be content with what the Lord in his mercy metes out to us is greatness." The grandchildren stood for some time in silence around the two graves, engaged in as solemn and important thoughts as they had had for many a day. "T'm so glad we came here today," said Marian at last. "I think I have been needing this for a long time." That night, as she and George prepared for bed, she turned to him, and out of the fulness of her heart, she said, "George, my dear, you are a truly great man. And [ am content."
