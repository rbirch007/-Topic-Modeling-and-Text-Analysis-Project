Strange Reward Ida Elaine James Where we conspired together here I work alone, Remembering your wishes, dear, How you were prone To salvage questionable weeds: "Perhaps next year Some strange reward from magic seeds!" A falling tear Now damps this dark and stony spot When I think how You gardened life's haphazard plot, And then, as now, Your valor led me, lighting hours Whose barren yield Would spoil a lesser faith in flowers In life's hard field. Hulda Parker, General Secretary-Treasurer All material submitted for publication in this department should be sent through stake and mission Relief Society presidents.
