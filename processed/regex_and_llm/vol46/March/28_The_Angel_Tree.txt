She Angel Sree (For a Baby Announcement Party) Helen Spencer Williams HAT could be sweeter for a child's room or a baby announcement party than this little angel tree? This tree is a rustic branch of scrub oak. Most any kind of tree with tiny branches will do, and Florence Williams broke hers from a tree out in her garden, then she sprayed it with white, quick-drying enamel, and while the paint was Page 188 drying she found a flower pot and filled it with plaster of Paris then placed the tree in it while the plaster was still moist. Then the tree was all ready for its branches to hold baskets, baby dolls, and little angels. She had shopped at the five and dime stores for baby dolls about two and a half inches long and tiny baskets with handles. Next she THE ANGEL TREE went to a display store for angel hair and for the smallest of small flower lights with white wire to light the tree. The little baskets were sprayed with gold, and when _ thoroughly dry, she lined them with the downlike angel hair. 'Then she nestled a baby doll in each basket and hung it on the tree. Over each one she placed an angel doll with arms outspread as if to protect and guard the babe sleeping so innocently in the golden basket cradle beneath. The tree is lighted with little white lily globes which give a soft 189 white glow and illuminate the golden baskets. The angel trees stands in a bed of angel hair which catches the highlights from the tree and flower globes, giving an ethereal feeling as if the tree, babes, and angels were resting on a heavenly cloud. The angel tree, whether it is on a table, mantle, or in a child's room brings forth an irresistible "oh" and "ah" from young and old alike, for there is nothing sweeter in the whole world than a little babe asleep in a golden cradle with a guardian angel hovering near.
