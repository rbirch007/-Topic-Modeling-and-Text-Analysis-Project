Woman's Sphere Ramona W. HE Fine Arts Committee of Mesa, Arizona, choosing from among devotees of all the arts, has named writer Mrs. Orson C. (Bertha) Kleinman "Artist of the Year" for 1958. She received a very handsome plaque and the most flattering of citations. Now eighty-one, Mrs. Kleinman is remembered among Latter-day Saints for her pageant "Message of the Ages." She has since written ten other pageants and is author of a great number of poems, hymns, short stories, and dramas. CAMILLA KOFFLER, the famous photographer of animals known as Ylla, was the author of Animals in India, just off the Harper press. The volume includes a diary of her recent trip in India, with entries recording what she saw and photographed up to two days before an accident which killed her —a fall from a jeep while she was photographing a bullock-cart race. RS. TORA SELANDER NELSON, 'Teasdale, Utah, has changed an abandoned schoolhouse into a museum. 'Thirty-three years ago, Mrs. Nelson, who was then a citizen of Sweden, visited Utah's flaming canyon country, and was so impressed that she made up _ her mind to return and make her home in the colored cliffs. Now she has a home and museum filled with art treasures from all over the world, including an Eskimo blanket made of Arctic duck down, and shawls from Kashmir. Cannon HRISTIE LUND COLES, Provo, Utah, a contributor to The Relief Society Magazine, and prizewinner in several Relief Society literary contests, has published seventy-six of her recent poems in Some Spring Returning (Faucette Publications, Provo, Utah.) The poems are representative of the lyrical gifts and heartfelt understanding of this well-known poet. GERTRUDE MacLEAN, London, England, in 1921, organized the "Universal Aunts," a helpful society offering to do "anything for anybody at any time." They tend babies, find employment for people, locate houses and_apartments, reserve seats at theaters and do research work. They do washings, wave hair, hang curtains, read to the ill or the aged, assist with hobbies, and do catering for parties; they arrange travel tours, buy trousseaux and layettes, act as companions and nurses, and meet trains, alrplanes, and busses. "Token" prices are charged for these services, plus a 35c registration fee from all customers. PRISCILLA J. MILITANTE is the first woman of Philippine ancestry to enter the field of geology. She received Bachelor's and Master's Degrees from the University of the Philippines, and at present is working towards a Doctor's Degree in Geology at Stanford University.
