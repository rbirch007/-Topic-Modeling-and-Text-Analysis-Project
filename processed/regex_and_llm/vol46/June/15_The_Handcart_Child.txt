She Handcart Child Orvene B. Holman Oh, wagon girl, you cradled dolls of rag, With button eyes and colored yarn for hair. And, wagon boy, with dust you played your tag, And kept the pace of wheels when skies were fair. You snuggled on your quilts outside the carts, And whispered, even laughed, when day was done, I wish I knew what dreams were in your hearts On nights when stars and desert seemed as one. Josef Muench
