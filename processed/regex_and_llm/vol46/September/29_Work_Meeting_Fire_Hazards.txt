Larsen For Tuesday, December 8, 1959 Objective: To show that the best fire control is prevention. ByVERY day 800 homes are struck by fire. Don't permit your home to become one of the 800. Most fires in the home are caused by carelessness; usually it's a thoughtless act or a forgotten precaution — just little things — but too often they lead to destruction and sometimes loss of life. Nearly all fires in the home can be prevented, if the simple and common-sense methods are followed. If you are on your guard and do not let these hazards occur, it will go a long way in eliminating fire in your home. Major Causes of Fire Matches, smoking, and misuse of electricity, account for forty per cent of all fires of known origin. Their real danger cannot be emphasized often enough.
