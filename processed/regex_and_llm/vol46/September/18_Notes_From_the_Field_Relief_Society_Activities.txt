EFORE closing, I'd like to relate briefly two experiences which have strengthened my personal testimony concerning the value of the Relief Society organization and The Relief Society Magazine. The first one is a personal experience. When I was a young boy living on a farm, one of my duties was to herd cows during the daytime. 'This job was not particularly hard — and it gave me plenty of time to read. Many weeks during the summer I would read as many as twelve books a week, and although much of the material I read was worthless, I read many other things which have had a great influence on my life. Among these worthwhile things were all of the standard works of the Church and every Relief Society Magazine which had been published up to that time. My grandmother had subscribed to the Magazine since it was first started and had faithfully saved every single issue. We frequently talk about the influence of other people on our lives — but frequently that influence is received vicariously through reading of the beliefs and actions of others. I am grateful for the testimony of the divinity of the gospel which I have and for the help the articles and stories in The Relief Society Magazine have given mc in gaining this testimony. I feel that we as parents — and grandparents — have a responsibility of placing desirable reading materials before our children to help them grow and develop - in the gospel. The final experience I'd like to relate happened this week. When the Brigham Young University Stake was organized three ycars ago, I was called to serve as a member of the stake high council. As many of you know, this stake is comprised almost entirely of students at the B. Y. U., and now has twenty-four wards completely organized with Priesthood, Relief Society, Sunday School, and Mutual groups. My particular assignment on the high council is to serve as advisor to the stake mission. In this capacity I have noted that in proportion to the number of non-Latter-day Saint boys and non-Latter-day Saint girls on the campus, that our missionary efforts have been much more successful among the girls. Undoubtedly there are many reasons for this, but I'm sure one reason is the fine examples set for the non-Latter-day Saint girls by the Relief Socicty sisters in our stake. One of the girls who has been serving as Relief Society president in her ward the past two years found herself living within the boundaries of a different ward when she returned to school last week, and therefore had to give up her position as Relief Society president. Our stake mission president knew some THE REAL WORTH OF THE RELIEF SOCIETY MAGAZINE 593 of the sterling qualities of this girl, and so asked me to interview her concerning the possibility of calling her as a stake missionary. It was a thrilling experience to observe the poise and social maturity of the exRelief Society president. 'm happy to say that yesterday she received her call to serve as a missionary in the B. Y. U. Stake (she will not be twenty-one until December). As long as you have Relief Society presidents of this caliber throughout the Church, I have no fears for the continued growth and development of your great organization. In closing, Vd like to wish you the best in your Magazine campaign this year. You are engaged in a marvelous work and are selling a great product. I hope you can go forth with the spirit, enthusiasm, and dedication of a true missionary. If you are not as successful as you would like to be in getting your subscriptions, may I make one brief suggestion. Every failure has a cause — and the cause for failure must be removed before you can succeed. If you are not successful in placing a subscription in one of the homes to which you are assigned, encourage your potential subscriber to list the things she considers to be the most important in her life; then point out how The Relief Society Magazine will help her in realizing and achieving these goals. The objective of the Church — and of the Relief Society — is to prepare a people to live with Christ on the earth during the millennium, and to live with God in the celestial kingdom in the eternities to come.
