Magazine Honor Roll For 1958 Counselor Marianne C. Sharp HE General Board congratulates stake and ward, mission and branch Relief Society Magazine representatives, with their respective presidencies, for their sustained efforts in 1958, which resulted in an all-time high of 157,070 subscriptions to The Relief Society Magazine. This is an increase of 6,182 over 1957. This large increase reflects earnest and zealous work on the part of Magazine representatives and an awareness on the part of Relief Society members of the worth of The Relief Society Magazine in their lives and the desire to have it in their homes to be read by their family members. Letters constantly come to the General Board in which young mothers state that their mothers always had the Magazine in their homes and how much it means to them now to have the Magazine in their own homes. With over 193,000 members of Relief Society, we find that the subscription number equals 81% of the membership. However, since many non-Relief Society members subscribe to The Relief Society Magazine, the percentage of Relief Society members is probably not that high. 'There is a great difference in the growth that can come to two Relief Society members living side by side who attend Relief Society meetings together where one reads and studies the Magazine contents and the other does not. There is a whole area of influence and knowledge which remains closed to the one sister. It is a closed book. Not only does she deprive herself of valuable knowledge, enjoyment, and growth, but she also deprives her family, for as a mother gains saving knowledge, the benefit is felt by her family in her enlightened attitude and the added information she imparts to them. There is a great responsibility attached to the calling of a Magazine representative to convert Latter-day Saint women to subscribe and read the Magazine. In addition to selling a year's subscription, the Magazine representative may continue her interest in the sister to see that she is receiving and enjoying the Magazine. With new subscribers, especially, she can inquire if they have read certain articles and show a continued sisterly interest. With all the means of instruction and entertainment offered today, a Latter-day Saint woman must be selective and Relief Society has the responsibility to point out the benefits to be found in The Relief Society Magazine, to bring an awareness that the entire contents reflect and are in harmony with the principles of the gospel. The Relief Society Magazine combines material on the varied aspects of the life of a Latter-day Saint woman as does Relief Society itself. As Relief Society is a unique organization in all the world, so the Relief Society Magazine is unique among magazines for women. As in the homes, so in stakes, certain patterns are set. In some stakes high goals of achievement are set and attained. 'The realization of Page 325 326 them becomes the pattern of the stake. 'This is attested by the fact that year after year many of the same stakes remain with the highest percentages. Some new stakes attain high goals from their beginnings. Others show remarkable gains and maintain their increased _percentages. 'The General Board watches the progress of the Magazine subscriptions in the stakes with keen interest, feeling that if members subscribe, read, and study the contents it is some indication of the progress and strength they and their beloved Relief Society are gaining. For the twelfth consecutive year South Los Angeles has the highest percentage—222%. South Gate Ward of South Los Angeles Stake again leads the wards and has a percentage of 323%. The Western States Mission leads the missions with 114%. The highest percentage of a mission district was achieved by the West North Dakota District of the West Central States Mission with a percentage of 127. Sidney Branch of the Scottsbluff District RELIEF SOCIETY MAGAZINE—MAY 1959 of the Western States Mission achieved the high percentage of 220. Special commendation is extended to these organizations. In 1958, there were 72 stakes which had 100% or over, and 19 stakes in which all wards achieved 100% or over. 'There are 247 stakes on the honor roll which is an in- | crease of 27 over last year. 'There are 1,912 wards on the honor roll, an increase of 58 over last year. There are 16 missions on the honor roll and 538 branches. The General Board extends its warmest thanks and appreciation to every Magazine representative whose unselfish service has enriched the lives of her sisters. Magazine representatives will be blessed for their efforts, for The Relief Society Magazine may exert a great persuasive power for good with its readers and, through magnifying their callings, Magazine representatives have performed a service and been the means of bringing enlightenment into countless lives.
