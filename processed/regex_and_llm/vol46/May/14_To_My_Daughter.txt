So My Daughter Camilla Woodbury Judd I have grown old, my daughter, since you went away, Missing the radiance of your shining hair, Missing your face of heavenly sweetness, cameo chiseled; Your skin like velvet flower petals, Your laughing eyes, your lips so tender; The saucy tilt of your nose, the little dent in your forehead; The long expressive hands, so deft and beautiful; Your arms about me — your rare, sweet confidence. Missing all these so much, My heart grows young again at your returning.
