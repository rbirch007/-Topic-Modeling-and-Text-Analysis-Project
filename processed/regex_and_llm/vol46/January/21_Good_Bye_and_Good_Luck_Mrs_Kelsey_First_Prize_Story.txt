Nibley Assistant Church Historian HE first members of the Latter-day Saints Church to arrive in California were 230 men, women, and children who had sailed from New York City in the ship "Brooklyn," on February 4, 1846, and who, after rounding Cape Horn, arrived in San Francisco Bay on July 31st. Six months later, in January 1847, the members of the Mormon Battalion, consisting of about 300 men and a few women, arrived at San Diego, after an overland journey. In 1851 a large colony of Latter-day Saints, approximately 500 in number, settled at San Bernardino. 'Thus the Latter-day Saints were prominent in the early history of California. Missionary work was carried on by them to a considerable extent, under the leadership of Parley P. Pratt, Amasa M. Lyman, Charles C. Rich, George QO. Cannon, and other Authorities of the Church; however, in 1857, at the time of the coming of Johnston's Army, President Brigham Young counseled the members of the Church to return to Utah. The great majority of them responded to this call. Actual missionary work in California was not resumed again until 1892, when Elder John L.
