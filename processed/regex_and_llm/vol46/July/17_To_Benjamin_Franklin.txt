So Benjamin Franklin Elsie McKinnon Strachan I'm thinking of you, Benjamin, today. I'm wondering, were you here, what would you say About our ventures into outer space, Our findings that exalted heights erase The twinkling of the stars? How would you view The tracing of the jet plane's pearly quill Above the crimson slope of evening's hill? What signs would crowd Poor Richard's Almanac— What pithy proverbs, wisdom-sharing knack, Would cushion air-borne hearts who probe the new? As one "who seized the lightning" from the sky With kite and string, how would you simplify Our plans to reach where we've not been before— To sound our knuckles on the moon's still door?
