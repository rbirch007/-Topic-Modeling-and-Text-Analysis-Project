More Precious - Than Rubies HE strength and the beauty of virtue have been treasured and found priceless over the long years of earth history — even from the time of the commandment in stone, the "words the Lord spake unto all lin the mount out of the midst of the fire, of the cloud, and of the thick darkness, with a great voice. " Like all of the commandments of our Heavenly Father, the requirement of chastity results in many blessings and much comfort and peace of mind. Freedom from regret — the joy of obedience — reflect upon the personality and are indeed jewels of enduring radiance. The possessor of such treasure, though humble, may walk as a_ proud woman all the days of her life. Unhampered by feelings of guilt or disobedience, the way of her life becomes more blessed and more beautiful, and all her words and all The heart of her acts, in following the path of integrity, make of her life an undivided pathway to a far-reaching goal. And this goal of a woman's life is not hers alone, for none of us can truly say that her life belongs to herself alone and that her actions have no effect beyond the results which come to an individual. We know that we live in a family setting. We arc daughters with ideals in the circle of home, and with responsibilities toward our parents and our brothers and sisters. We share in the family honor, and we contribute to that integrity. And the woman in her own home, in that place of love and trust —where husband and children look to her for their ideals and their life pattern — there the jewels of her virtue are as a shining light unquestioned by her loved ones who safely trust her counsel and her example. For Latter-day Saints who understand that earth life is more than an interval between the premortal existence and the time until mortal death — for those who realize that they lived before their time upon the earth, and that thev will live forever afterwards, then the standards of the Church and the commandments of the Lord have amplified and eternal meaning. Even as Paul, the apostle, spoke to the Corinthians: "And whether one member suffer, all the members suffer with it; or one member be honoured, all the members rejoice with it" (I Cor. 12:26).
