Yet, long since you came, homeless, stripped of treasure, I still count blessings you earned for me here. Never a summer deepens in the valley, With loud light breaking over window sills, But you emerge from time and covered wagon To part the circle of these western hills. July can never arch in azure zenith, But the lengthening shadow of your height Monograms our city with your stature, And I kneel solemn, prayerful in the night. Never this festive time held in your honor Moves unseen down my calendar of days; Against the sacrifice that marked your coming I test again the tenor of my ways. "W
