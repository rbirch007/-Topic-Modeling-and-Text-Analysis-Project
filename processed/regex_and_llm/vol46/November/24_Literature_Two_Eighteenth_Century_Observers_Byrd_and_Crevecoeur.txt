A request has been made for a clarification of the recommendation of the General Board regarding the holding of socials and outings as work meetings during the summer months. 'The General Board has always regarded social activitics as an integral part of the program of Rehef Society, and the promotion of the social well-being of the sisters as a function of the work meeting. Therefore, summer outings, and soclals or luncheons, even when held in places other than the mectinghouse, if arranged under the direction of the Relief Societv president, and open to the general membcrship, and conducted in conformity with regulations set down by the General Board for the conduct of work meetings, may be regarded as work meetings. 'This would take into account such regulations as prayer, either prior to leaving for the function or sometime during the activity, a recording on the roll of those in attendance, the recording 734 RELIEF SOCIETY MAGAZINE—NOVEMBER 1959 of minutes, and the financing of the activity in line with established policies. 'This statement is not intended to imply that summer meetings should be exclusively, or even preferably, outings or — socials. Whether this type meeting is held rests with the ward Relicf Society presidency, with the decision based on circumstances and the interests and needs of Relief Society members within the ward. Singing Mothers Our Singing Mothers choruses continue to grow in number and their beautiful singing wins high commendation everywhere. At the close of 1958 there was a total of 2,564 wards and branches with Singing Mothers choruses, an increase Over 1957 of 166 choruses. There were 38,896 singers participating in the choruses, an increase over the previous year of approximately 3, o00 singers. 'This ls a gratifying record of a unique and cultural program. "Union Meeting" Name Changed to "Stake Relief Society Leadership Meeting" You will be interested to know that hereafter, by action of the General Board, the meeting presently referred to as the "Union Meeting" will be designated as the "Stake Relief Society Leadership Meeting." Ward Officers and Class Leaders Meeting Established; Ward Preparation Meeting Discontinued We also announce that by recent action of the General Board, the ward preparation mecting, heretofore recommended, now will be discontinued. In its stead, ward Relief Societies will hold, periodically, a ward officers and class leaders meeting as needed. 'The frequency of holding such a meeting will be determined by the ward presidency. The General Board suggests that perhaps two such meetings a year would be advisable — one just prior REPORT AND OFFICIAL INSTRUCTIONS to or soon after the opening of Relief Society in October for the educational year; and a second early in the new year after the compilation of the annual report. 'The purpose of this meeting will be to consider matters of common concern to officers and class leaders, to keep the group united in support of pro- 735 grams which affect the Society as a whole, and to promote generally the well-being of the Society.
