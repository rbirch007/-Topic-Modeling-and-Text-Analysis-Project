Zippers and hooks and eyes are taboo as they are too difficult for tiny fingers. Gripper fasteners are good as the child gets a little older. All garments should have as few fastenings as possible. Pajamas or nightgowns are made on the same principle as daytime clothes. 'They should be sturdy, with firm fastenings, be easy to get in and out of, and easy to launder. They should be made in comfortable fabrics that wear well. Fashion should be important in night clothes as in day clothes. Children can look adorable in their night things if fabrics are attractive and well chosen and the styles becoming. Cotton crepe for summer wear and cotton flannel and jersey for winter are ideal fabrics. 'They are easily washed and need little or no ironing. Because infants' and _ toddlers' clothes get hard wear and need frequent laundering, plain seams are not satisfactory for use in their construction. 'I'wo sturdier-type seams that are most frequently used are the French seam and the flat fell seam. For infants' and little girls' dresses, slips, and gowns, the French seam is best. For play clothes and pajamas, the fell seam serves well. To make a French seam lay the fabric edges together, right side out. Pin or baste, then stitch, taking out half the seam allowance. 'Trim raw edges, then press the seam open or to one side. Turn to the wrong side and crease on the line of stitching. Stitch along the seam line and so enclose the raw edges. To make a flat fell seam begin by pinning or basting the seam from the right side and stitch. Press flat. Trim away one edge to within 1/4 inch of the seam line. Turn the other edge under and pin or baste flat so the raw edge is covered. Stitch close to the fold.
