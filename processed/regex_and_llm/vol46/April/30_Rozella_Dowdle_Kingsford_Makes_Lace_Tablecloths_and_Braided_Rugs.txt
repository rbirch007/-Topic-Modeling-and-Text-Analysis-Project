Rozella Dowdle Kingsford Makes Lace Sablecloths and Braided Rugs Roza Dowdle Kingsford, Cove, Utah, has been busy with needles and crochet hooks since girlhood. She has made more than fifty braided rugs and many articles of exquisite crocheting. For the Relief Society room in her ward chapel she crocheted a lovely lace tablecloth and a lace cloth for the top of the piano. She also crocheted hundreds of articles which were sold and the money given to help fnance the building of the ward chapel. Mrs. Kingsford's ''double hobby," as she calls it, is gardening. She raises raspberries and strawberries, as well as a vegetable garden, and her flower garden is the pride of the valley — presenting a picture of continuous bloom from early spring until late fall. Her habits of industry and thrift were acquired early, for Rozella Dowdle lost her mother when she, the eldest daughter, was only twelve. Soon after the mother's death, Rozella's father said to her, "Be sure to have the bushel of wheat ready for the Relief Society sisters when they call, even though your mother is not here.' Relief Society has been a beloved companion to Mrs. Kingsford for many of her seventy-eight years, and she has been a visiting teacher for more than forty years.
