Day of the Spelling Match Maude Rubin Like ten-pins most of the boys went down, Leaving only me and that city girl. She tried the hard word, then with a frown Flipped to the bench with a flowery twirl Of her wild-rose skirts. I spelled it loudly, CINNAMON. The prize was a pencil, Bright red, sharp-pointed. Whistling proudly, I carried triumph enough to fill All Gold Hill Gulch. No road too steep — I conquered the mountain in one great leap! 109 110 WHITE SANDS, NEW MEXICO
