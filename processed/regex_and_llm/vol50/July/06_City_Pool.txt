City Pool Eva Willes Wangsgaard The rain ceased falling, and while walking I came upon a lonely pool Imprisoned in a stony hollow — The pavement warm, the water cool. A boy swung happy toes across it And later robins came to sport, But there was only sky above it, No leafy branch of any sort. Never to climb a birch tree fountain, Never to be one with grass or clover; Only the sun's hot rays as outlet, Then make the long, slow journey over! ME: OLGA PEARSON ENGDAHL, of Omaha, Nebraska, was selected as American Mother of the Year from among fifty successful State candidates, in New York City in May. A queenly woman, she has given countless days of service as a volunteer hospital worker and in other humanitarian efforts, in addition to being the successful mother of six sons and one daughter. RS ORA NELSON ANDERSON, Brigham city, Utah's Mother of the Year, was one of five mothers to receive a special citation. Hers was for "extraordinary skills in the art of homemaking." She has _ wallpapered, painted, and tiled her home and made almost all her children's clothing. She received her award in a handsome black suit made by herself.
