Praise Is a Prayer Margery S. Stewart The stars hold conversations in the night, Singing of delight For being, finding it awesome to exist, Much less to move in silver-winged flight. The gulls are glad for their brief Soarings, the fragile leaf For seasons, fields hold in greening palm The tender sheaf. Small things praise by acts, the ant, In the green comfort of the plant Moves in minute obedience. There is a gratitude in the sea's deep chant. They seem to be a part of the days I like to remember, and I hate to see them go, ugly as some of them were. One by one, the old fences have gone, and it is like old memories being burned alive—a very unhappy experience. After long years of absence from my home town, I returned one day in spring, to find many things had changed. The place seemed half undressed with so many friendly fences gone. The houses that used to look serenely down at me over tall fences, now seemed to be boldly staring. I walked down the once familiar sidewalk which led me to my old home. It appeared strangely stark and lonesome and a little bit apologetic without the weathered, browngray, picket fence which, in other days, seemed to warn any intruder not to enter. As a barefoot child I ran along the narrow plank to which each 218 picket was nailed.
