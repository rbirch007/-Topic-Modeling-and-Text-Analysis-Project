The Moon Is Full Evalyn Sandberg How sweetly sleeps the world tonight All wrapped in luminosity And patterned shade. How sweetly from the thickets Soprano-chirping crickets Are raising repetitious serenade. In soft, legato cadences A song of surcease lifts And fills the ear. And peace, made nearly visible — A presence almost palpable — Reposes here.
