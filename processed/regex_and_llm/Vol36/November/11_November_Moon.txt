November Moon Margaret B. Shomaker The blood-red moon hangs low upon the hill Against the darkened arch of indigo, And on the night a cry of whippoorwill, The low and doleful tone is long and slow. I bend my knees to ease and follow her As she wends her way upon the ground. But where? I do not see the grasses stir; Listening long, I hear no other sound. Slow over stubble field I move with care; The cornstalks, stripped of foliage, dry and still, And earth, man-scarred, is waiting for repair. The breeze foretells a winter white and chill; Across the field new hope in this November, My heart drinks full a beauty to remember. Page 752
