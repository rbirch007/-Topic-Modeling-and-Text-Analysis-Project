Magazine Subscriptions for 1948 Counselor Marianne C. Sharp HE general board takes this T opportunity to express heartfelt thanks and gratitude to the Relief Society officers, Magazine representatives, and subscribers who have demonstrated their loyal support of The Relief Society Magazine during the year 1948. The devotion with which the Magazine representatives throughout the Church have placed the Magazine in the homes of Latter-day Saints is indeed gratifying. Also a matter of joy and pride to the general board is the success of the representatives in placing the Magazine in the homes of many who are not members of the Church, but who may thereby learn of the ideals and accomplishments of Latter-day Saint women and partake of their spirit of service and enrichment of life. Subscriptions—1948 _ The subscription figure for December 31, 1948 (77,740) shows a loss of 3,238 from the subscription figure of December 31, 1947 (80,978). However, more than half of the subscription total for 1947 represented subscriptions taken out at the old subscription price of $1, and the total as of December 1948 reflects subscriptions at the new price of $1.50. Therefore, the general board feels that the Magazine representatives have worked exceptionally hard during 1948 and are deserving of special credit in having so. Successfully established the Magazine at the new price. Page 320 Because of carrying so many subscriptions during 1948 at the old subscription price, the general board regrets to report that the Magazine showed a small deficit at the end of 1948. However, it is confidently expected that one year from now the subscription figure will reveal a substantial increase and that the Magazine will be entirely self-supporting. For the full realization of these expectations, the general board enlists the continued efforts of the officers and Magazine representatives during the year 1949. Honor Roll Percentages Continuing the policy established in 1948, the membership figures on which the Honor Roll percentages for 1949 will be based, will be counted as of January 1949. This allows each Magazine representative to obtain from the secretary, at once, the membership figures of January 1949 on which her percentage for this year will be figured. In this way a Magazine representative may know all through the year the number of subscriptions she must obtain in order to be on the 1949 Honor Roll, New Features and Services The editorial department of the Magazine strives constantly to improve the quality of the Magazine and to add new features of special interest to Latter-day Saint women.
