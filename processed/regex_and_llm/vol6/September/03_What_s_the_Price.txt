What's the Price?
Mary Foster Gibbs.
"Mother, O, Mother, Austin has cheated me out of my fifty cents. Make him give it up!" The child's voice, shrill, and full of distress, startled the white-haired woman who sat on the porch mending a fine lace collar. As she looked up from her work she saw the twelve-year-old girl fling out her hand and scratch the face of her younger brother with vicious haste.
"Vilate, Vilate, I say, what are you doing?" The little girl swung about. "Aunt Elizabeth, he's got my money, and he won't give it up. Make him give it to me."
"She said I could have her old fifty cents, if I would find her old ring." "Well, and he hasn't found it, and he can't, but he has stolen my money, anyway."
