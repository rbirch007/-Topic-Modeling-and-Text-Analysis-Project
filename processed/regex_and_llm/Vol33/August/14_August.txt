Freedom of worship, freedom to share in the responsibilities of their own governing, freedom under self-made laws, were the fundamentals for these colonial builders of early America. In all the literature that truly voices the heart of these varied groups of earnest folk, this love of liberty�orderly liberty�is expressed. It was that for which they sought a home in the New World, for which they endured hardships and dangers. For this precious boon they lived; and for it they were ready to give their all.
