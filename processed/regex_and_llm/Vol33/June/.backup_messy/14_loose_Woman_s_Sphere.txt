EVANS (Mrs. Frank Evans) of the Relief Society General Board has been re-elected to the post of corresponding secretary of the National Council of Women of the United States. Mrs. Evans was a delegate from the Relief Society to the national organization six years ago when she was serving as a missionary in the Eastern States Mission over which her husband presided. At that time she was made corresponding secretary and has held that office ever since. She will serve two more years. She accompanied President Belle S. Spafford and Edith S. Elliot of the General Board of Relief Society to the inter-continental congress of the National Councils of Women of the United States, Canada, and some of the Latin-American countries held in New York City, May 6, 7, and 8, 1946. MARGARET WEBSTER, daughter of Dame May Whitty, and Eva La Gallienne are two of the leading spirits in a group which will establish an American Repertory Theater. They will produce the plays of all countries which reveal the profounder aspects of society. Public subscriptions to support the movement will amount to $300,000. The first season six plays will be produced, three different ones being presented each alternating week. The companies will tour America from coast to coast.  WALTER A. KERR is the Utah State director of the Global Thinkers, an adult educational society, non-sectarian and non-partisan, the purpose of which is to establish woman's place in the new world. 'The society discourages drinking and smoking among women and girls, and stresses personality, intelligence, and charm. The society, incorporated in 1943, has grown rapidly and sends out four times a year, for discussion among its members, literature of a high order educating people to every phase of these habits. Men, as well as women, belong and take an active part on the programs. Each adult member may sponsor a junior member, beginning with girls in the ninth grade. RS. YSABEL F.
