Neighbor Child Margery S. Stewart She does not wait to be asked in, Rings the bell... Darts under my arm, Skips like a sparrow around The minutes, picking up brief worms Of my greeting. | love you, she says. The words are three sickles. We stand waiting. She must see all the windows at once, Ask all the questions in a tumble, Put the pale tongue of her finger On everything she passes. | love, she says, Everything becomes still... - Waiting.... Like a pond for another pebble perhaps? No. Like the kitten for another leap From the long dead cicada? No. | look at the child and she looks At my mouth... Willing it to form Words. | love, she says; The teacher waits in her eyes. | love you, | say unhappily. She takes the three stones, Blesses them, And departs.
