Summer Song Linnie F. Robinson The apple trees are dappled silver This afternoon of waning spring; The cherries hang in shining rows And all the birds have come to sing. The dahlias push up mighty shoots Where giadiolas knife the loam; And mountain shadows beckon me To follow if | chance to roam. | marvel that | view this day, New as when light was first unrolled; This morning's red and fiery shafts Turn evening lowlands into gold. And all around where | can see — Such beauty makes me sing a song; | hear it in the harp-like trees And in the grasses, lithe and long.
