On Seeing a Son off to School Evalyn M. Sandberg His hand leaves mine. He saunters down the hill, All dignity and new-found poise, And he is only five. He turns and waves. Alone — and yet not quite alone. Still held Upon that silver strand By which a mother claims her child. |! Would not have him walk alone; Yet walk alone he must. Dear God in heaven: Regard my child.
