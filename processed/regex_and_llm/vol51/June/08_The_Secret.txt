The Secret Eleanor W. Schow | learned a little secret, and this it proved to be, A tiny seed of regret for one scarce known to me, | knew its power at first sight; This little seed was dynamite. | saw fear of deep trouble within her eyes, and doubt; The heart must burn as stubble when gossip flies about; And so within the heart of me | hid that seed where none could see. Then with apparent blindness | left it very still, And fed by loving kindness it throve and grew until One day it bloomed, and all could see Sweet fragrant flowers of love for me.
