After a time the moon rose. It was enormous and 185 MARCH 1964 deep red in color. Somewhere on the mountain there was a forest fire! The car became very still, with only an occasional, cryptic remark from one of our parents to break the silence. Suddenly, without any need for consultation, we girls joined hands and bowed our heads in prayer. We loved to sing and did so almost every evening. To this day, five minutes after any two or more of us get together, the house is likely to be bursting with song. My oldest sister was very popular with the boys. She was very popular with me, also, as she was almost never without a box of candy, often the kind with Brazil nuts in the middle. She had a difficult time, though, in many ways, because our parents being still young and tense, were determined to do their full duty by her. Any small misdemeanor found Plet sitting, cringing, on a chair while the law was explained to her in no uncertain fashion. Today Plet is a very special person, lovely to look at and to talk to, capable and womanly always. All I can say is — scoldings and chocolates must be magic therapy!
