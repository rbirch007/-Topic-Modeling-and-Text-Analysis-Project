On Spring and Love Mabel Jones Gabbott It was the budding season of the year; The brown grass turned and drank of sun and rain, Each smallest spear, refreshed, grew taller then: And folded petals on the elm tree, near The garden gatepost where we talked, in clear Denial of the winter's grasp, began To yawn and stretch and open up again. Why does this first spring green disappear? It doesn't really; like love's first fragileness That still remains a part of love fulfilled, Spring is absorbed in summer's richer sheen; But after sudden showers when storms are stilled, Our frail first love and hints of new spring green Color all the aftermath of stress.
