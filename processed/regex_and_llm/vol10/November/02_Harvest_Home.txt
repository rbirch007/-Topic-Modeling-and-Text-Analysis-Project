Harvest Home
By Alfred Osmond, Head of the English Department of the Brigham Young University, Provo, Utah.

 Bind up the sheaves. The heavy heads of grain Are filled wiih extracts from the soil and air. The Summer sunshine and the Autumn rain Have nourished them with tenderness and care. The golden crowns of glory that they wear Are heralds that the harvest time is here, Inviting all to come and freely share Their ample blessings which will charm and cheer The cold and cruel days that poverty would fear, The brushwoods, on the brown and purple hills, Adorn themselves in scarlet robes of flame, As Nature with her frigid hand distils The liquor that from juicy rootlets came. Mistake it not.
