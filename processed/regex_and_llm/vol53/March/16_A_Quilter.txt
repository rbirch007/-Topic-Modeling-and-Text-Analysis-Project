A QUILTER Caroll Orton Once upon a special day | met a quilter sweet and gay. She taught me things that | should know— The do's and don'ts of how to sew. A grandmother she was, with fingers nimble (And | hadn't learned to use a thimble.) She seemed most adept at stitchery And someday a quilter like her | hope to be. Biographical Sketch of Lael J.
