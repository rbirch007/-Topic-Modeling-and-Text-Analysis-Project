MORNING BY THE SEA Sylvia Probst Young On softly rolling water, morning sun Flecked golden, gently rides toward the land, And white fringed wavelets, fingering the shore, Leave etchings traced in silver on the sand. Far in the distance, where the blue meets blue, A ship's hull rises, phantom like... The cry Of soaring seagull breaks the solitude, In this vast world of sand and sea and sky; This quiet world where fear and vain regret Are lost to me—at this lone oceanside, | walk and feel a power, infinite, With time eternal—constancy of tide, And life beginning new at each sunrise— Beyond the reach of sea | lift my eyes.
