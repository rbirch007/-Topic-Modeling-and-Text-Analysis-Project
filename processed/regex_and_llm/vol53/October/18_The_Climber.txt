THE CLIMBER know. If he went over the top of that seat, he would plunge a distance almost twice his length headfirst onto the floor of the car. Since he did not know what was beyond his immediate goal, he must have thought me unreasonably opposed to his having what he wanted. I, too, have tried to climb. I have used all of the capabilities I have developed in a lifetime of trying. I know what my goal is and how to reach it. I am willing to try over and over again, and I am even patient in trying to remove the obstacles that impede my progress. But still I do not often reach the goal I'm striving toward. Does the hand that impedes my progress belong to one who knows more than I about what is beyond the immediate goal I long for with my finite mind?
