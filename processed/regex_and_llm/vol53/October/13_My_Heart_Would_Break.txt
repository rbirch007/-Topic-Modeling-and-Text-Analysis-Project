MY HEART WOULD BREAK Maude O. Cook | stand beside an open door. Through it | freely pass To breathe the fragrant air of spring, To walk on velvet grass. Surrounding me on every side Are paths that mark the way To meadows, hills, or far-off lands Where |! May choose to stray. If this same door were locked and barred, Another held the key, And four bare walls made up my world— Were all that | could see— Remembering the orchard-blooms, The moonlight on the lake, The hillside rich with autumn-glow— | fear my heart would break. The firelight is warm and golden As | sit here alone; But each room is empty, silent Until you come. Then when |! Hear your whistle, And your footstep at the door, This place becomes alive, Happy, and secure. For by your very presence At evening when you come, The empty quiet of each room Becomes the peace of home. OCTOBER 1966 CINNAMON BISCUITS Place one package prepared biscuits. In greased pan. Put a dab of butter or margarine on top of each. Cover biscuits with brown sugar, and sprinkle cinnamon over this. Bake as directed on package. STRING BEANS WITH MUSHROOM SAUCE 1 can French sliced green beans 14 can mushroom soup Heat beans in saucepan. When liquid has boiled for a few seconds, drain it off and add undiluted mushroom soup. Allow this to begin bubbling, stir, and serve. If you have time on Saturday to sliver a few almonds, they make a delightful addition to this.
