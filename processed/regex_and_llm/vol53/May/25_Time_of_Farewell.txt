Time of Farewell Leora Larsen Brenda Jean wasn't even a year old when her mother left her with me for two weeks. For a long time afterward, when she came with her parents to visit me, and it was time to go, her tears were copious. She refused to kiss me goodbye because she hated to go. Then, tonight I saw her waving and smiling, as their car drove away. Such a smile! Almost as if she knew her return would be soon and home was a fine place to be going. Little girl, tonight you taught me something. Life is like this. We approach it with quiet fear, Learn that it is wondrous good and, when all its Splendor is yet warm, we must learn to smile and Wave goodbye. If our cry is loud, if fear for The end makes us frantic, we must still learn to Smile and wave goodbye. JS
