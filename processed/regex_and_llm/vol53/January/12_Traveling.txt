TRAVELING Zara Sabin Oh, | have been far-traveling Unto a foreign land; Took pictures of the public squares And buildings tall and grand; Stood countless hours in galleries, Silent at beauty's shrine, Storing my heart and brain and mind With color, blend and line. | loitered in the little shops That dot each thoroughfare And fingered linens and fine lace— Exquisite and rare; Then strolled about the market-place And down the avenue, Sought signs that told of language known And thought of home, and you. Yes, | have been far-traveling, But hope to make it plain The best of all the journeying Was coming home again. 40 the Home inside and out
