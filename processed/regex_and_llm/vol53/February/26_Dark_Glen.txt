DARK GLEN Shirley M. Howard The leaves are thick The branches low In this dark glen Where | must go. The path, if one Can call it so, Refuses grass Yet nettles grow. A foot can trip, A body fall To disappear In weeds grown tall. Some say In venturing a call The words will make No sound at all. At most they rise Where branch has thinned And blend as Whispers in the wind. Rather, in this World of dark, | think there waits A gleam, a spark, A thread of sun, A single ray Of gold to hold The light and say, "Here is the way, Here is my way."
