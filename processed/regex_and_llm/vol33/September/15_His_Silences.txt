HIS SILENCES Blanche Kendall McKey He wears deep in his eyes an alien look? This changeling who has closed an open door, Yet smiles to touch his desk, his pen, his book, Nor does he fail to cherish as before. A note cries from his voice sometimes at night That halts me, leaves me stranded and alone. What lies unburned, guarded from my sight, To shut him from me by a shaded tone? I did not sleep with pain and halt with death, Nor rise from blows because a dream shone clear, Nor shout above the flames with pulsing breath Because my God had stilled the beat of fear. I may not touch the latchstring of that gate Which holds his silences inviolate!
