Clusters of mock Willard Luce 359 orange, white with yellow centers, and the intense green of the dainty ferns also have decorative qualities all their own. Besides the flowers and the blossoming shrubs, there are three varieties of trees found in the Monument: western juniper, Juniperus occidentalis; limber pine, Pinus flexilis; and quaking aspen, Populus tremuloides. All of which fails to make the Craters of the Moon National Monument into a city park; but, at the same time, keeps it from being quite the barren waste described by Washington Irving.
