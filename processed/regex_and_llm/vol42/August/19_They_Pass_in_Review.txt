Carlyle, Ruskin, and Rossetti Endeavored through their art and prose To find a cure for England's woes. They aimed at those, whose petty Hates and shams caused misery and pain. With them, Christina, poet sister of Dante, Whose humility was a lesson to humanity, Had no thoughts of any worldly gain. Next followed, with heartbreak in her wake, The mystic, Emily Bronte, whose sad fate Struck pity, as fickle fame came late. Trials she bore, to make the stoutest quake. By way of change, in lighter vein, Came the humorists, who did berate With satiric nonsense, to deflate The nation's leaders; then with disdain Made fun of politics and aristocracy.
