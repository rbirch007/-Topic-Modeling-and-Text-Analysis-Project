So Long As Springtime Comes Mabel Jones Gabbott Some springtimes enter like a warm green breeze, Through budding elm and oak and maple trees, A slender grass stem pushing earth apart, A low, round violet leaf, a tulip dart. And spring sometimes in yellow is arrayed, Wide fields with dandelions overlaid, Forsythia that frames my neighbor's walk, And sunshine in the sky, in smiles, in talk; And often spring will beckon all in pink, Glowing like children's cheeks, or dawn's wide wink, Peach blooms, and white-pink apple witchery, Rose-throated robins in the cherry tree; Oh, I care not what colors first appear, So long as springtime comes year after year.
