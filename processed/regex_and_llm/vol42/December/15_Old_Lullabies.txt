Old Lullabies Maude Rubin I rock my little grandson In the same old rocking-chair I used to rock his mother in. The same old creak's still there, The same worn cushion. (I should make A new one of cretonne.) I rock and sing and dream a while Of olden days long gone. I hold him close, for one more song, Glad rocking babies isn't wrong; And somehow those old lullabies Still make me want to close my eyes But dreams of those days I'll forsake— This blessed baby's wide awake!
