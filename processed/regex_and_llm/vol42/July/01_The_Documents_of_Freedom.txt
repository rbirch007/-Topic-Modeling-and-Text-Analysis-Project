In reflecting upon the celebration of this historic day, it is natural to contemplate the events that brought it about. It took a revolution to break the bonds with the Mother Country; not the type of revolution we hear so much about today. Historians tell us that if any pattern can be discerned in the revolutionary movements of modern times, the American war for independence does not conform to it. The American Revolution did not result from serious economic discontent. In point of fact, the depression which followed the French and Indian War had, within a few years, been succeeded by a period of considerable prosperity and even business expansion. 'True, the final retaliatory measures of the British government—notably the closing of the port of Boston—caused some localized unemployment, but few people in the colonies were ever hungry in 1775. The amazing thing to learn is that the American Revolution did not array class against class, and particularly it did not conform to what is known as a Communist or Marxist pattern. 'The recruits for the revolutionaries' cause, or patriot cause, came from all classes and economic groups. 'The landed aristocrats, the merchants engaged in transatlantic and Caribbean trade, Page 420 the local shopkeepers, the professions, the mechanics, and the laboring classes, all were engaged in this great cause for freedom. The American Revolution was definitely not a class war. 'The leadership of this great movement was drawn from the upper and middle strata of society, which explains why it stopped short of a reign of terror, and why, for example, no single royal governor lost his life in the conflict. It was entirely different from the French Revolution. The American Revolution was accomplished without the extremists seizing power, without resort to dictatorship, and without any violent reaction. The Fourth of July was the day on which the Declaration of Independence was signed, a new country came into being, "The United States of America." It was in the Declaration of Independence that this name was first and formally proclaimed to the world, and to maintain its verity the War of the Revolution was fought. The Declaration of Independence was written by Thomas Jefferson. He was selected by his fellow committeemen to write this immortal document. In a letter 'Thomas Jefferson wrote to James Madison in 1823, he makes the following statement:? The committee of 5 met, no such thing as sub-committee was proposed, but they unanimously pressed on myself alone to undertake the draught. I consented; I drew it; but before I reported to the THE DOCUMENTS OF FREEDOM committee I communicated it separately to Dr. Franklin and Mr. Adams requesting their corrections... And you have seen the original paper now in my hands, with the corrections of Dr. Franklin and Mr. Adams interlined in their own handwriting. Their alterations were two or three only, and merely verbal. I then wrote a fair copy, reported it to the committee, and from them, unaltered to the Congress.. FE are told that Jefferson, without consulting a book or pamphlet, wrote in a half day's time our great national symbol, The Declaration of Independence. The rough draft may now be seen in the library of Congress. Jefferson was a young man, only thirty-three years of age at the time. He had already gained a reputation in the fields of science and literature. He was acclaimed by the men who knew him best as an able lawyer, scientist, and philosopher, and a writer of some repute.
