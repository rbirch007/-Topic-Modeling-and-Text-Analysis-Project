The Where Lilacs G rew Alice Morrey Bailey Lilacs pioneered this western place Before begonias and delphinium. Nurtured in the wagon's premium space They rode beside the apple and the plum. Down across the sage-locked valley floor They sent a line of waxen green to grace A cedar fence, a gate, a rustic door With alien lavender and perfumed lace. And many hearts that ached with homesick grief Were salved by bits of home, transplanted here, For courage grew in thrusting root and leaf, And triumph waved in lilac's scented spear. The coyote's wail, the hard, unyielding clay, Were robbed of strength where lilacs led the way.
