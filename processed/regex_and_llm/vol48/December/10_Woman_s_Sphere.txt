Woman's Sphere Ramona W. Cannon R. CONNIE GUION, at the age of seventy-nine, practices medicine from 7:30 A. M. To 6 P. M. In New York City. She is also a gifted teacher, a noted research worker, and a much-in-demand public speaker, with a delightful sense of humor. The vast new hospital under construction in New York, which will house most of the city's eighty-six clinics, bears her name, the first time a woman has been so honored in America. ILLIE PAT DANIELS set the American girls' mark in the 400 and 800-meter races last summer at the American tryouts for the Olympic games, in Corpus Christi, Texas. Miss Daniels is now a student at Brigham Young University in Provo, Utah. In the 1960 Olympics she. Fell and did not finish. She hopes to compete in the next Olympic games. She is a native of Utah. EARL BUCK, Pulitzer and Nobel prize winner, continues her writing of fiction which stirs the heart to kindness and wide appreciation.
