Hu m il it y Louise Morris Kelley Grandeur? Sometimes. But give to me The loveliness of minute things. Thus intertwine my symphony With solo parts for flute or strings. When ocean lures, as flame the moth, My soul to revel in its roar, Let orphaned bubbles of sea froth Remain — my treasures on the shore. Let me recall as from the crest Of mountains I survey this land: Not only mountains has he blessed But, too, this quartz grain in my hand.
