She didn't want to go back to the noise and chaos that usually lasted long into the evening. It wasn't that she resented Edith, Charles, and the children who had come to live with her. She wanted her own friends again, in the privacy of her own home. She often wished she could be left to read by 101 herself, sew by herself. She wanted to prepare a small repast and talk with a friend. In short, Martha once more wanted her own stove and her own table. And then she shuddered, when she thought of the big house with no one in it but herself. Suddenly she realized how far she had come. She had passed the point of the mountain. But she drove on.
