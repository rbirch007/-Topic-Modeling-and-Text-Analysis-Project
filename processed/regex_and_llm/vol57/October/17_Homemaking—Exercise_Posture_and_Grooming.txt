HOMEMAKING — A Lovelier You Discussion 4 — Exercise, Posture, and Grooming

Northern Hemisphere: Second meeting, January 1971
Southern Hemisphere: Second meeting, June 1971

OBJECTIVE: To emphasize that proper exercise, posture, and grooming improve appearance, health, and self-confidence.

INTRODUCTION Women have great potential power and unrealized possibilities if they maintain their vitality and energy. One great hindrance to reaching our full potential in life is poor health. We should make an effort 'to eliminate illness and fatigue when it is possible. For maximum physical fitness, the importance of exercise, deep breathing, correct eating, and relaxation cannot be overemphasized.

Exercise is important because it tones the muscles and encourages good posture and deep breathing. Good posture and also erect carriage add distinction to our appearance. It is desirable to have good posture because it gives an appearance of confidence and it builds good health by allowing the inner organs to function properly.

Discussion Questions 1. What are some poor posture habits? 2. How can we improve our posture? 3. Name some causes of poor posture.

Daily exercise and balanced eating are two "musts" if we are to obtain maximum bodily health. If you have checked with your doctor and have no serious health problem, then you are free to embark on a regular exercise program to improve both health and posture. As we grow older we sometimes resort to becoming melancholy and moody. We try to escape by succumbing to watching television, when walking in the fresh air can be far more beneficial. Gardening is a wonderful exercise for the body as well as uplifting to the soul. Working with the soil in fresh air and in the sunshine contributes greatly to relieving tension and to developing peace of mind. Walking, gardening, and swimming are some of the best exercises for women of all ages, if started in moderation and gradually increased. These exercises help tone muscles and thereby give better control to the body. A strong body promotes courage and self-confidence. It enables us to sit and arise from a chair with more grace and ease. Exercises will help us to be more agile and better able to take care of ourselves.

Your appearance reveals at a glance the general state of your health and mind. If you are neatly groomed, it indicates you care about yourself. It also indicates that you care about others by showing them the courtesy of looking your best for them. Clean and attractively arranged hair is a beauty asset. Observance of the rules of good dental hygiene will enhance your appearance. It is important to keep your skin clean and conditioned, using creams to counteract dryness when necessary. We can protect our hands with gloves when we're doing heavy work. We should give attention to our feet so that we wear properly fitting hose and shoes. No woman can look beautiful and relaxed when her feet hurt. If it is painful to walk, it affects her posture, her disposition, her facial expression, and makes it difficult for her to be pleasant and congenial with her family.

The human body is the handwork of God, marvelous and sacred. If we treat it with care, it will serve us well. As mothers, let us emphasize to our young people that a body that is clean and strong, agile, and well groomed, is a source of joy. Keeping our bodies vigorous and healthy helps us maintain enthusiasm for life, and as long as we have enthusiasm we have youth. 'A woman looks years younger when she smiles, when she walks with head held high, shoulders back, and a brisk gait which indicates maximum body health. Regular exercise clears the mind, relieves nervous tension, and tones the muscles, thereby creating a happier and lovelier you.
