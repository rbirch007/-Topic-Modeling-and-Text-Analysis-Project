She goes about her daily tasks,
Dusting here and there;
No childish laughter at her door,
No light brown hair to brush and comb,
The window pane now shining bright—
No finger prints are there.
She sighs; her eyes grow moist,
The postman passes, the phone is silent,
No knock upon the door.
Then day is cupped
Within the shadows of the night.

She kneels, please bless my child,
She softly says Her room.
And silence fills—
Margaret B. Shomaker
