REFLECTION Even after all this time they still remain, The deep quiet pools of sadness. Secluded, forgotten, until stirred By a ripple of haunting melody, Or plumbed by a remembered gesture, The turn of a phrase. I see your face in their mirrored depths, And remember how, in later years, we smiled At the memory of tears shed for the short separations Of our youthful love. Perhaps the days of our separation will be soon spent, Your tender, teasing smile again will chide me For this grief. Slowly the waters settle and are still once more. —Sandra Lundquist
