A Rug For Long Wear and Easy Care
Edythe K. Watson, Member, General Board of Relief Society

Rug making is a fascinating and ancient art. Women down through the ages have designed and created many varieties of handmade rugs. Homemakers from nearly every land have left a rich heritage of rug making. Our Mormon pioneer women, because of the need, braided and hooked their own rugs for floor coverings. Some of you may still remember those used by your grandmothers.

Rug making at home is no longer a necessity, but Relief Society women throughout the Church still take great pleasure in this craft. Some rugs are made solely for service and durability, while others are chosen for beauty and comfort.
