Mama Was a Heroine! These I noted in one swift look before my eyes found what they sought: Papa. Papa, riding Eagle, bareback, and leading Prince along behind. I never have been able to remember what words ran through the joyous bedlam that followed. I do remember Mama asking, when Papa and Elmy had reached our gate together and stopped, "Where's the wagon?" in an alarmed tone, and Papa's reply, through a wide grin, "On the other side of the creek. The horses could wade, but the wagon has to wait till the water goes down some." After awhile, a sudden hush fell around us. Papa was asking—repeating something he had already asked but said again because Mama was stammering so. "I said, who hung my new lantern down in that tree last night?" Why... I did... I was bit by bit, we 'told Papa all about it, while Papa just sat and stared as if he couldn't believe it. It was Elmy who said at last, "You're a brave girl, Libbie. A brave girl if I ever saw one. What a woman! Jack, do you know how lucky you are, having a wife like her?" Mama's face was all pinky, and Papa's was even pinker. He said after a long silent time, "She'll do, I guess." I sat still, thinking the great thought our Mama was a heroine, brave as any in a book! I looked out the window, amazed. The sun was shining! The bad storm was quitting, and Papa was safe at home again, and Mama our own Mama had become a heroine!
