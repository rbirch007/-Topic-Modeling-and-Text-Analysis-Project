An Heirloom Quilt JoAnne Baker
I call this an "Heirloom Quilt" because it has my husband's parents' life story on it. The center block is a map of where they were born, later moved, and now live. Just above is a picture of Samantha and Claud and their hobbies. Each block represents something in their lives such as a story about a watermelon or a schoolhouse. They canned many fruits and vegetables and raised horses and cattle. On the bottom are representations of each of their children and their professions. The front is made of one square 31½" x 28⅜" and twenty squares 16⅛" x 14⅜". The back is two pieces 36" x 90" sewed together. I used 7½ yards for back and front edges, tore four pieces 5" wide, and the rest of this material makes four blocks for the front. Figures were appliquéd on small blocks, put together, and then quilted.
