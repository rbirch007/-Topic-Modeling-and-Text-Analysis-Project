Children in the Night

What do children speak of in the night,
Clucking like downy chicks in the soft dark?
Telling childish secrets, cuddling close,
Watching for the first star's golden light.

What do children whisper!
Near windows where leaves murmur, small birds peep,
Wrapped in the blanket of pure love and peace,
Before their prayers ascending merge with sleep?

—Christie Lund Coles
