Just So Just so, he stacked the whisper and the rustle Of the parchment corn Heaping hubbard, and banana And the pumpkin at its side. And so, the blue mountain wore Its thin turban of snow And the sky was A turmoil of departing glory. All remain, repeating the eternals Of the ending year Now to other hands Their yield to another hoe.

Dorothy J. Roberts
