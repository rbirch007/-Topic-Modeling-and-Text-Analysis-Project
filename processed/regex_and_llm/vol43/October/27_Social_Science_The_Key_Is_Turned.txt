Social Science—Latter-day Saint Family Life Lesson 3—The Key Is Turned Elder John Farr Larson For Tuesday, January 22, 1957 Objective: To show significant steps in the organization of the Relief Society and the opportunities for service found in its objectives. And I now turn the key in your behalf in the name of the Lord, and this Society shall rejoice, and knowledge and intelligence shall flow down from this time henceforth... (Joseph Smith, April 28, 1842, D. H. C., IV, page 607). HE organization of the Relief Society, under the hand of the Prophet Joseph Smith, was in fulfillment of a revelation of the Lord. That its organization was inspired is evidenced by its accomplishments over a century of service. Within its stated objectives are found unlimited opportunities for personal improvement and service. The year 1842 was one of great progress for the Latter-day Saints. It is described, in part, by the Prophet Joseph Smith. On March 1, 1842, he wrote his now famous "Wentworth Letter" (Ibid., page 536). In response to a request of Mr. John Wentworth, editor of a Chicago newspaper, the Prophet sketched the history of the Church, and, in summarizing our beliefs, set forth our "Articles of Faith." After describing the terrible mob attacks of 1838 he wrote:. We arrived in the state of Illinois in 1839, where we found... A people who were willing to be governed by the principles of law and humanity. We have commenced to build a city called "Nauvoo"... We number from six to eight thousand here, besides vast numbers in the country around We have a city charter granted us, and charter for a Legion... For a University, for an Agricultural and Manufacturing Society, have our own laws and administrators, and possess all the privileges that other free and enlightened citizens enjoy (D. H. C.,, IV, page 540). Brief review should now be made of the last lesson in which is described the status of women in the early part of the igth Century.
