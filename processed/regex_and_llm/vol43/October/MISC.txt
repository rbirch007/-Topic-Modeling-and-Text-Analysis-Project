VOL. 43 NO: 10 Lessons for January Magic Call Catherine E. Berry How many autumns past my heart has yearned With restless hungering for any trail That wandered off where maple fires burned, Beneath October's amethystine veil. This urge to go, though tempered in the spring, And lulled to sleep when summer walks the land, Besets my heart with constant bickering The moment autumn shows its golden hand. No hill that lifts a torch against the sky But I would climb the bronze trail to its height, No winding lane where windblown leaves now lie But I would follow it in happy flight. There is no cure for wandering, I know, Except to heed its magic call—and go.
