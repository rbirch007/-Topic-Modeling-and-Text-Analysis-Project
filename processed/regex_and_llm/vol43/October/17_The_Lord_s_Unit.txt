C.'s there would have been recognition in your eyes. You may think that it is a new unit. It is, here on the Bngham Young University campus. 'This is its third year, but I am surprised that you haven't heard of it, for it has been in existence since 1842 and is known all over the world! Yes, I mean world, for there are few countries and languages which do not know Rho Sigma. It is not a religious unit, or a service unit, or a departmental or social unit, but is a well-balanced combination of all four. In fact, we may say that the Thirteenth Article of Faith is our creed: "We believe in being honest, true, chaste, benevolent, virtuous, and in doing good toallmen " Indeed, true charity is the soul of our unit "If there is anything virtuous, lovely, or of good report or praiseworthy, we seek after these things." In this unit I have found those things which every true Latter-day Saint seeks: a strengthening of my testimony through reading and studying the Scriptures, and an appreciation and deepening of my testimony through hearing the testimonies and experiences of others. Have you ever heard of a unit which converts others to the Church? Mine has converted many. One of our members had her Page 678 testimony strengthened and _renewed by attending our meetings. She enjoyed our unit and used the principles to such good advantage that her roommate who was not a member became curious and was impressed to ask more about the Church. After attending some of our meetings and being thrilled by what we were doing, she gradually became better acquainted with the Church and soon joined it. But this is more than a religious unit, we offer service, too. In fact, service is emphasized at all times. We are interested in the welfare of the individuals in our group and in helping them we strengthen the group as a whole, for we are not just a name but an active organization. 'Those who receive as well as those who give in this unit are benefited morally and spiritually. UT, in addition to these praiseworthy characteristics of religion and service, we offer a departmental program. We _ better ourselves through the great literature, sociological, and domestic knowledge of the world. Over a period of time we cover the world's best literature. In the field of sociology we have studied mtan's problems, psychology, politics, and our relations with others. We prepare ourselves to be better wives and mothers through the best ways to manage a household and understanding what constitutes a real home. We were given an example one day in meeting THE LORD'S UNIT of how an economical, practical, and versatile wife can be a profit to her husband. He would have to invest $40,000 in order to receive in dividends each year the money his wife could save him. 'That shows how important a capable wife is. ES, here we learn of those things virtuous, lovely, and of good report. But what of some recreation and social life? Doesn't all work and no play make Rho Sigma a dull unit? Oh, but we have a social side, too. Almost any day of any week we can join with members of the Phi Epsilon unit in making and preparing articles for those who are less fortunate than we. We have an organized singing group which sings at parties and programs. We have dinner dances, buffet suppers, bake sales, and exchanges with the PhiEpsilon unit. So, you see this unit combines all the good of the social, departmental, religious, and service units into one well-balanced whole which keeps the unit always interesting, yet always changing. This well-balanced combination of social activities, service, and knowledge is not just for my benefit here at school. I can belong to my unit and benefit from it the rest of my life. It will broaden my spiritual and mental capacity, and thus make me a fitting partner for my companion and help me become a worthy Latter-day Saint. It. Has been said, "When you educate a man, you educate an individual, but when you educate a woman you educate a family." Men need to 679 learn specialized work to enable them to support their families, but we women should learn all we can in the most important fields, if we want to take our part well and strengthen our husband's place in the community. We are the hub of the home, and our duties are to rear families, to teach our children correct principles to live by, and to give them broad backgrounds and strong foundations with which to meet the world. We ate prepared for this very well by my unit, for it gives us many opportunities to become well-rounded individuals. Through my affiliation with this unit, I will fnd members wherever I go during my life, for we can belong all our lives wherever we are, and can receive instructions from headquarters and induce others to join. One of our members, after graduation, was married to a man whose work took them to a foreign country. They didn't know anyone and had no connection with the community except through the husband's work. The wife was very lonesome and bored, and you can imagine her joy and surprise, when members of the unit called on her and invited her to join them in the same effective, uplifting program she had known during her college days. Surely, such a comprehensive unit with all these benefits, should be very selective and limit its members. It doesn't. Every girl on campus can belong. 'There needs be no selection, for this unit appeals to only the best girls. There is no initiation; the lack of money is no bartier; in fact this unit is the least expensive of any unit or sorority in 680 the world. A half dollar will pay for all the activities in one year. There are no "secret sisters'; all will be "helpful sisters," who will help me be my best and not hinder me because they want the higher positions. There will be no falling away because of the demands of families or because we grow old and do not resemble so much the former coeds; no disappointments at college reunions that others have grown so old and uninteresting or seem to be pursuing their lost youth. For those who grow old in this unit gain wisdom and knowledge and hidden stores of spiritual beauty which grow more lovely and desired with the years. The unit does not weaken at graduation with a few alumni meetings which become fewer and fewer with the affiliation becoming RELIEF SOCIETY MAGAZINE—OCTOBER 1956 smaller. Instead, it grows stronger and stronger with the years, and the membership grows with it. It grows into a true sisterhood, with love and charity for all, and those who remain true to this unit remain true to the ideals of Christ. You can see I really have something! A unit which is a combination of social growth, education, religion, and service. It is so beneficial that its effects will be with me forever, and every woman in the Church can belong to it everywhere. Where else would you get a combination just like this, to interest a girl in her teens and a wife and mother in her later years? 'To promote the common good and personal welfare of all?
