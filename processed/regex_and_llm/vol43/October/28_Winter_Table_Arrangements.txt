Winter Sable Arrangements Elizabeth Williamson OW is the time to think about making winter arrangements and dried bouquets for your home. Try to get out before the heavy rains begin and the snow covers the dry summer weeds and grasses. Gather armfulls of materials, tie them in bundles, and hang them from the ceiling of your storeroom for future selecting. Look for unusual and interesting pieces of dead wood. If you are near the sea, pick up driftwood. If you are not entirely inspired by nature, drop by the lbrary and look through the imnumerable and beautifully illustrated books on dried flower and plant arrangements. Some of the recent Japanese arrangement books are helpful. Simplicity is the key word. Remember this, and you will select from your harvest a few choice specimens and arrange them so that they become part of your home and your personality. You will find the effort to be worth the field trips. Often in your home among your possessions you will find choice pieces of china, pottery, or glass which may be used as accessories with your arrangements. A tiny blue horse with a piece of gray weathered driftwood and stalks of dried barley or wheat in an old glass pitcher are unusually attractive. Try different effects. The colors of dried plants, wood, and grasses are neutral, so they will blend harmoniously with many color schemes.
