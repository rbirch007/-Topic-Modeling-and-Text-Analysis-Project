In 1851 Elder Taylor established a publication in Paris named L'Etoile du Deseret. Branches of the Church were organized in Paris, Boulogne-sur-Mer, and Le Havre. Ata conference held July 24, 1853, at St. Heliers, Isle of Jersey, nine branches of the French Mission were represented with a combined membership of 337. The French Mission did not prosper as much as had been hoped, due to severe restrictions and other factors, and in 1864, it was made a district of the British Mission. This condition continued until 1912, when the French Mission, consisting of parts of Belgium, Switzerland, and all of France, was reorganized, with Elder Edgar B. Brossard as president. 'The work of proselyting has been actively continued since that time. 'There are today thirty-three branches of the French Mission with approximately 1,300 members. Elder Harold W. Lee is the president, with headquarters in Paris, where the Church has purchased a beautiful mission home. Twenty-seven Relief Society organizations were reported in December 1956, with 231 members.
