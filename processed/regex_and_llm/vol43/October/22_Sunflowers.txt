Sunflowers Luella N. Adams Along the highways and byways Blooming in profusion gay, The cheery, sturdy sunflowers Brighten each summer day. Their faces aglow with sunshine, These common yellow flowers Expect no tender care, they flourish Through drouth or freshening showers. In summer, nectar for honey bees; Come autumn, their precious seeds Provide a feast for chickadees; In winter, squirrels feed.
