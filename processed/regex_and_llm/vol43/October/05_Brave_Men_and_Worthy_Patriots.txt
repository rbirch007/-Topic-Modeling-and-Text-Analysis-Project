Soon a hundred grim messages were dumped upon the already loaded desk of the President of the United States. As the President read them, the furrows in his dark brow deepened. And, here's another: April third: "Yesterday a tribe of Blackfeet under Pocatello massacred an immigrant train south of Fort Hall!' " "Why that's terrible, Mr. President," the adjutant said. The President's mouth settled into a hard, firm line. "This situation must be controlled immediately!" "But, Mr. Lincoln," the adjutant sighed, "we can't send men to the West. Already the Southerners outnumber our forces from Kansas to the Potomac." "True," the President replied. "Nevertheless, serious consequences will result, if we lose communication with California and the territories out there." "I wish the Westerners would protect themselves, Sir." "Because it is our duty as Americans to obey when our country calls," he said, "I think General Wells should recruit two companies at once, one for Captain Smith to lead to the North Platte immediately, and the other for Colonel Burton to train so that it may protect the area near our territory."
