Cooking apples jelly 1 c. Melted butter whipped cream sweeten to taste vanilla dried bread crumbs Peel, core, and quarter apples. Simmer, covered in a little water, until soft. Force through a sieve and sweeten to taste. Add vanilla. Butter a fireproof dish, put layers of bread crumbs and layers of applesauce into the dish alternately, letting the bottom and the top layer be of bread crumbs. Pour the melted butter down through the apple cake, and bake for 30 minutes in a hot oven. 'Turn on to a plate and decorate with jelly and whipped cream. Additional whipped cream may be served in a separate dish. Serve the cake either hot or cold. KRINGLE (Knot-shaped Cake) 300 grams flour 2 eggs 300 grams butter or margarine 60 grams dry yeast 200 grams sugar egg and sugar for coating Mix flour with margarine or butter. Stir yeast with a little sugar and put it in the flour together with eggs. Roll the dough at once in two bars. Stir butter with sugar and place it on the bars. Form the bars as a knot, place them in the pan, and let them rise from three to four hours. Coat with egg and sugar. Bake in a hot oven (375°) for about 20 minutes. Note: About 28% grams equal an ounce in avoirdupois weight. For purposes of cooking measurements, it would be reliable to figure about 28 grams to an ounce. Add lemon juice and grated peel. Stir soaked, melted, and warmed gelatine in the eggs. Place in a cool place. When it thickens, mix carefully the stiff whipped egg whites in it.
