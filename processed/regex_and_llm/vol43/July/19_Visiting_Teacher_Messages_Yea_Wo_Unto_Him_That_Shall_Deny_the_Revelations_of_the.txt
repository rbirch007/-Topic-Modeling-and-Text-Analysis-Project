FXECUTIVES, choristers, organists, Magazine representatives, and work meeting leaders should also be aware of the value of good visual aids and make use of them in their work. The executives of stakes and wards are being given suggestions in the conventions for visual aids in publicizing the March and November Fast Sunday night meetings. Visual aids can also be used to improve membership and attendance. Magazine representatives are urged to make use of attractive posters and charts to stimulate interest in their work. Work meeting leaders can make their plans known to members by the use of informative, attractive posters. Music leaders will find material in the text, Fundamentals of Conducting, by J. Spencer Cornwall, which can be used as visual aids in their department. Also much of the work in Sister Madsen's lessons in The Relief Society Magazine, 1950-51 and 1951-52, can be made more effective by good visual aids devised by stake and ward music leaders. The general board again urges leaders in all departments to make the best possible use of audio-visual aids in carrying out their various assignments.
