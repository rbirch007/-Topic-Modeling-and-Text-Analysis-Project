Annual Report 1955 REV Society continued to advance in nearly all phases of the program during 1955, its 113th year, according to the activities and achievements reflected in this report compiled mn the annual reports of 223 stakes and 42 missions. Many worthwhile and inspiring activities, particularly in compassionate service, were disclosed by the narrative sections of the reports. There were 7,213 members added to Relief Society dung the year, bringing the total membership to 163,513—a considerable number being young women living in foreign lands to which their husbands have been assigned in the armed forces. These young women not only enjoy the benefits of the Relief Society program themselves, but are introducing them to their neighbors among whom they are temporarily living. While the growth reflected in 1955 is commendable, it is less than 1954, and so our goal— every Latter-day Saint woman a member of Relief Society—should continue to receive constant, serious consideration. In 1955, 103,467 Relief Society members participated in leadership activities, bringing opportunity for development to 6,269 more women than were reached by these activities in 1954. The overall average attendance at regular meetings showed a slight decrease, though there was a slight increase in the missions. Theology and testimony meetings continued to enjoy the largest attendance, followed, in order, by work, literature, and social science meetings. We are particularly gratified to see that work meeting, through its varied program designed to meet the needs of all homemakers, old and young, is bringing into the society many young women interested in making their homes attractive and happier. Visiting teacher meetings increased 760 in the stakes and 376 in the missions, and the increase of 163,079 visits to Latter-day Saint families by 4,873 more visiting teachers in 2,617 more districts, reflects steady progress in this vital activity. Compassionate services continued to receive increased attention as indicated by 12,421 more visits to the sick and homebound, and ten more days care of the sick, over 1954. The increase in days care of the sick, while small, is commendable in view of the present trend toward hospitalization. 'The narrative sections of the reports contained many inspiring and heartwarming incidents of varied types of solicitude and help for the aged as well as for neighbors and friends. All this is evidence that the grand key words of the society as given by the Prophet Joseph Smith — "'Said Jesus: 'Ye shall do the work which ye see me do' " — continue to have place and purpose in the hearts of Relief Society members. This is the second year we have received reports on the Singing Mothers choruses. These showed 2,005 choruses in the stakes and missions, an increase of 228, with approximately 30,983 singers, an increase of 3,703 over 1954. These are very encouraging increases in this lovely and satisfying activity which gives added unity, spirituality, and cultural benefits to those who listen as well as to those who sing, and, in addition, brings music into the homes of the participants. Each year sees an expansion of Relief Society's program and influence, not only in the established stakes and missions, but in newly created stakes and in missions in remote lands and islands not heretofore reached, and so our great band of sisterhood gradually is encircling the world. The general board gratefully acknowledges the support of all those who have so unselfishly given of their time, talents, minds, and hearts in advancing the Relief Society program of raising human lives to higher and happier levels. With the sustained loyalty of its devoted workers, Relief Society will continue to do so. Mangerut 0. Pietsrimg General Secretary-Treasurer Page 458. ANNUAL REPORT. 459 MEMBERSHIP — 1955 334,906 L.
