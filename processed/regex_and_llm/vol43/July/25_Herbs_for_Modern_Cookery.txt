Herbs for Modern Cookery FENNEL (FoEnicuLum Dutce) Elizabeth Williamson WEET fennel is an annual herb, not usually included in the kitchen herb gardens, but once started it becomes an old standby, often seeding itself. Fennel needs a sunny exposure and will grow tall (two to four feet high) and feathery. It makes a pretty background in the garden. Onginally fennel came from the Mediterranean area. The Romans used it as a vegetable. 'They also dried the leaves and extracted the juice from the stalks. The juice was used for eye medicine and for stomach disorders. Fennel is still commonly used in Italian cookery. The early English used the extract in drinks supposed to make obese people lose weight and become thin. The most common American use of fennel is for a sauce to be used with boiled fish. It provides a sharp and unusual flavor. If you have never tried this herb, you will experience a new thrill in herb cookery. It is also good with white beans, salads, soups, and sauces. The dried seeds are used in seasoning pickles, cake, and cookies. The stalks are good for eating raw.
