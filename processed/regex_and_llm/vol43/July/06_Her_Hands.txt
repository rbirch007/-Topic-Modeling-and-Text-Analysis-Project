Kind hands that eased our childish pain We know that we will clasp again. Old Sucson Willard Luce LD Tucson isn't nearly as old as it sounds—or looks. Its crumbling adobe walls seem to have weathered centuries of desert wind and rain. Its warped lumber seems to have been burned brown by the desert sun and sucked dry of all moisture. Its hitchracks appear to have tethered a thousand horses. But all this is make-believe. Old Tucson was born in 1940, created by Hollywood for the movie "Arizona." Built in the Tucson Mountains a few miles west of Tucson, Arizona, the set was constructed to represent the old Spanish settlement of Tucson during our Civil War days. When the film was finished, the movie company moved out and the Junior Chamber of Commerce moved in. 'They kept the set repaired as a show place for tourists and as a location for their annual 'Tucson Daze celebration held each autumn. Since 'Tucson ls a square dancing center with a dozen or more clubs meeting regularly, the J. C.'s inaugurated square dancing at Old Tucson. 'These weekly get-togethers are well attended by visitors and natives alike. Since 1940, the movie companies have moved back to Old Tucson on several occasions. In 1954 they came back for the Cinemascope production of Greer Garson's "Strange Lady in Town." They completely remodeled Old Tucson. They covered some of the adobe Page 432 walls with boards. They added second stories to some of the buildings. 'They built hotels, livery stables, saloons, and a dozen other establishments until you just wouldn't know the old town. And that is quite as it should be since the setting for this new, full-color film was Santa Fe, New Mexico, 7 9) just before the "Gay go's. Most of these buildings are complete — not just walls and false fronts. They are colorful, authentic, and a tourist attracter of the first magnitude. 'Today, however, Wards Saloon dispenses soft drinks, postcards, and other tourist subplies. 'There are other business houses which open and do a landoffice business during "Tucson Daze" and the height of the tourist season. It is said that the set will soon be reconverted back to the original "Old Tucson." Set on a valley floor in the Tucson Mountain Park, the make-believe town is surrounded by giant saguaro cactus. On the east and south the rugged mountain peaks hover over it. There are picnicking areas in the Tucson Mountain Park, and there is the Trailside Museum. 'This museum features live exhibits of the fauna and flora of the ArizonaSonora desert.
