Nountain Vigil Ethel Jacobson The last light pales on the loftiest rim Long after the valleys are veiled and dim, Then heaven bends in a starry arc Spreading a mantle of tranquil dark. The wind lies hushed in the silvery firs, On soundless wings the great owl stirs As, in infinity's embrace, Poised constellations wheel in space. The spheres tread out their measured dance The stars give way. 'The sun's swift lance Touches the topmost peak anew At a moment preordained, on cue, Set long before the tremulous light Of dawn first quickened primordial night. Faith needs no sign for all reborning Beyond this miracle of morning. Page 425
