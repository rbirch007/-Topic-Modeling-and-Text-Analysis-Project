Fences Thelma Ireland There is character in fences Shown in purpose and designs. Some are weighted with the beauty Of gay honeysuckle vines; Others strut around the premises, So very pompously, And some roam aimlessly like one Absorbed in memory. Some look neat, clean, prim, and tidy, White and scrubbed, each board in place. Others sag dilapidated, Bowed and cowed, bent with disgrace. Some formidable, unfriendly, Guarding vast estates with pride, But without a squeaking, swinging Gate that some wee tot could ride. Best of all, I like the fat ones, Fat with robins, girls and boys; Those not built to fence out friendship, But to fence in homely joys.
