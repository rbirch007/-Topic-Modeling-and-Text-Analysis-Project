Nibley HE East German Mission, which comprised the provinces of Mecklenburg, Saxony, Brandenburg, Pommern, Schliesen, and East and West Prussia, was formed in August 1937, with Elder Philemon Kelly as president. President Kelly presided until January 1, 1938, when he was succeeded by Elder Alfred C. Rees. President Rees presided until July 30, 1939, when he was succeeded by Elder 'Thomas E. McKay. President Joseph Fielding Smith and Mrs. Smith visited the East German Mission in July 1939 and held meetings in Konigsberg, Berlin, and Chemnitz. Ominous threats of the great World War II, in August 1939, caused the First Presidency to request that all American missionaries should leave the East German and West German Missions. 'The supervision of their exodus, of their gathering at Copenhagen, Denmark, and their subsequent return to the United States, was the task of President Joseph Fielding Smith, who established headquarters at Copenhagen. During the great war, which broke out in all its fury in September 1939, the activities of the East German Mission were directed by 'Thomas E.. McKay, by correspondence from Salt Lake City. His work was largely that of bringing relief to the suffering members of the Church, many of whom were behind "the Iron Curtain." In November 1946, the East German Mission was again organized, with Elder Walter Stover of Salt Lake City as president. He established headquarters in Berlin and began the work of reconstructing the mission, amidst the ruins of war. Eight districts were formed: Erzgebirge, Zwickau, Berlin, Thueringen, Dresden, Leipzig, Schwerin, and Chemnitz. Much difficulty was experienced by President Stover in visiting the members in the Russian Zone. President Stover was released in June 1951 and was succeeded by El der Arthur Glaus. President Glaus was succeeded in November 1953 by Elder Herold L. Gregory, who presides at the present time. Ninety-seven Relief Society organizations were reported in December 1955, with 3,727 members. Mary Ethel E.. Gregory presides over the East German Mission Relief Society.
