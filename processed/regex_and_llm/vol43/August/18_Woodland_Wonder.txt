Woodland Wonder Elsie McKinnon Strachan In the wooded park on the canyon's shoulder, They found each other, the doe and the lad; Each the appraiser, neither the bolder— The doe's eyes, a question; the boy's, all-glad. With new-found patience the boy moved closer; The doe, still alerted, held its stand; Then heaven stood guard in that woodland wonder While a doe nibbled leaves from a small boy's hand. Summer ide Beatrice K. Ekman The glittering sun has climbed the noonday sky, On hills and fields its searching heat is turned. From leafy branches plaintive robins cry And on the mountain trails, white dust is churned. In the near pasture young lambs seek the shade Of cottonwoods beside the dwindling stream Where tame geese with their wobbly goslings wade And through the sunlit shadows gleam. Now the full tide of summer overflows The fruited orchards and the beaded grain. To honeysuckle and the velvet rose The droning bees are trafficking again. The land is thirsting for the cleansing rain To freshen leaves where dust too long has lain. Sal al _ N DEPARTMENT
