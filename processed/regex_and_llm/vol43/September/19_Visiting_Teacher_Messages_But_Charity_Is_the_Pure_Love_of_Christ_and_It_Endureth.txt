President Nettie P. Smoot reports this historic event: " "The Spirit of God Like a Fire Is Burning' has served as the opening song for two historic Relief Society meetings in Nauvoo. In 1842, this grand hymn was sung as the opening song of a meeting in which the Relief Society was first organized under the direction of the Prophet Joseph Smith. And now, many years later, it has again been used to open a meeting of Relief Society sisters. 'This event is deeply significant, for it makes the first time in over a hundred years, yes, the first time since that cold winter evening when the Mormon settlers were driven from their Nauvoo homes, that there has been a Relief Society organization in this historic city. "At this time eleven sisters gathered and, with the approval of President Isaac A. Smoot and Nettie P. Smoot of the Northern States Mission, they organized the Nauvoo Branch Relief Society in the old John Taylor home." Photograph submitted by Ethel E. Stephens is president of Lorin Farr Stake Relief Society. BIG HORN STAKE (WYOMING) VISITING TEACHERS CONVENTION, Jume 5, 1956 Helena D. Belnap, President, Big Horn Stake Relief Society, reports an unusually successful convention which featured several talks on the various phases of the work of the visiting teachers. Music was presented by the Singing Mothers and a special scriptural reading was given by Plet Avery, President, Basin Ward Relief Society. Sister Vada Tirrell, stake visiting teacher supervisor, in her address of appreciation, honored a number of the visiting teachers who had served for many years: Julia Despain (fiftynine years); Malita Jolley, Elizabeth Snell, Salley Griffin, Ann Meeks, and Myra Allphin (fifty years). 'Thirteen sisters were reported as having served between forty and fortyeight years, and forty sisters as having served from thirty to thirty-nine years. NOTES FROM THE FIELD 623 Sister Belnap reports: ''As a special recognition to the sisters who had served from forty-five years through fifty-nine years, we presented each one of them with the official Relief Society pin; and a white carnation was presented to those who had served from forty to forty-four years. The song "How Lovely Are the Messengers," sung by our Singing Mothers, was dedicated to all of our visiting teachers. "We paid tribute to all our visiting teachers and to all of the Relief Socicty sisters for their love and devotion to this work.... Each of the wards exhibited some of the loveliest handwork that we have ever seen. It was wonderful the way each of the wards responded to our request for such displays, and it was simply grand to be together in this convention." ~—_
