The number of converts grew slowly; in November 1890, the combined membership of the Church was thirty-five, including three elders and two priests. President Ezra Taft Benson of the European Mission visited Finland in July 1946 and at Larsmo rededicated the land to the preaching of the gospel. Finland remained a district of the Swedish Mission until it was organized as a separate mission on September 1, 1947, with Henry A. Matis as president. President David O. McKay and party visited the Finnish Mission in June 1952, and held a mission-wide conference at Helsinki. President Matis presided in Finland until December 1954, when he was succeeded by Phileon B. Robinson, who presides at the present time. On March 31, 1956 there were 613 members of the Church in Finland, located in fourteen branches. In December 1954, thirteen Relief Society organizations were reported, with 221 members. Hortense B. Robinson presides over the Finnish Mission Relief Society.
