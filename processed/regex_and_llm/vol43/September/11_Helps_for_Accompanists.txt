Helps for Accompanists Roy M. Darley Assistant Tabernacle Organist [Address Delivered in the Music Department Meeting of the Annual General Relief Society Conference, September 29, 1955] HIE subject given me to discuss this morning is of vital concern, for it is universally important in every meeting of the Church. The music rendered at the beginning of a Church service influences the spiritual level of the meeting that follows. The organist is the first to indicate the commencement of the Church service with the organ prelude and is also the last to exalt his listeners with the postlude. As a Relief Society organist, you hold a position as important as that of any other organist in the Church, and your preparation should be as careful and thorough as it would be for any other meeting. Keeping in mind that the prelude creates the spiritual setting for the meeting, use great care to select and master appropriate music. Use the prelude to improve your talent; make it an opportunity to perfect technique and registration, so that it becomes a song of praise to our Father in heaven. Careful consideration in selecting repertoire for your meetings will result in your raising the standards of your musical endeavor and, in turn, raising the standard of our Church music. You could use your monthly leadership meeting to good advantage by having the organists from each ward bring their very best preludes to leadership meeting and exchange suggestions for good prelude music. Eight organists conPage 598 tributing two prelude suggestions in leadership meeting would result in organists of that stake having sixteen preludes; in other words, enough preludes for four months' use. 'Title, composer, and publisher are important items in furnishing adequate information. As an organist, you are the righthand aid of the chorister. Remember, there can be only one head to any organization, and in Church music that is the chorister. Wise is the conductor who will bring the organist into her planning of the music. 'There is a joy in working together. On occasion, you will have the opportunity to play an organ solo. Use this as a challenge, a new goal to reach, an achievement to accomplish. Have the organists bring two or three favorite solo suggestions to leadership meetings. The organ solo is something very special and should have melody, beauty, and should fit into the spint of the meeting. Avail yourselves of any and all opportunities to improve your talents. The Church provides excellent training through Church organ classes — a Course I and a Course II at $5 per course. Avail yourselves of this opportunity to join a class, for you can accomplish a great deal in this period of study. In working on preludes, solos, and hymns, the registration chart which follows has been designed to help HELPS FOR ACCOMPANISTS you with registration on the Baldwin, Connsonata, and Hammond pipe organ. As you will notice, a soft, mezzo forte (medium loud), and forte (loud) registration are given for all of these organs. 'This chart 599 is designed to give general helps and suggestions and in no way does it mean there are not other excellent registrations to be found. We trust they will guide you in your study of the instrument.
