The Joy of Early Rising Mildred Higgins E are told in the Doctrine and Covenants that we should arise early, that our minds and bodies may be invigorated. I am, by nature, an early riser. To slip quietly out of bed while the rest of the family lies sleeping in the early morning hours, is the privilege of a mother. It is my privilege, and I am eternally grateful for it. I dress hastily and walk through the back door into our yard. The air is so filled with the songs of birds that they seem to be trying to outdo each other in praising their Creator. 'The green leaves are lightened by the far-reaching rays of the sun which is not yet above the horizon. The air at this hour is so fresh that I can scarcely breath deep enough to do it justice. At their very best are the roses — suspended perfectly in the crosscurrents of rising dew, descending sunlight, and the crisp but gentle breath of the morning itself. They, like the honey- THE JOY OF EARLY RISING suckle, send their lovely aroma heavenward in praise of their Creator. It seems as if the earth itself is saying; "This is my most perfect hour — drink of it, for it is a tonic which will carry you through the heat of the noonday with a light step and a joyful heart. Now go into the midst of those who love you and minister to their needs, rejoicing and starting this day night!' Looking upward at the clear blue of heaven, my heart holds a prayer of gratitude and petition that I might not mar the remainder of a day that begins so perfectly. That, instead, I may be granted the wisdom to enhance it somewhere along the way. Much has been spoken and written about the place of the woman in a home —to make her home a haven of rest for her returning husband and children. It seems to me that something may be said about the joyful responsibility that a woman has in the morning — to help her family face the day with high courage and a splendid faith. Many hundreds of years ago King Lemuel had something to. Say about this in his praise of a virtuous woman. One of his statements reads like this: "She riseth also while it is yet night, and giveth meat to her household. (Proverbs 31:15). <_<
