School Again Lael W. Hill Beyond the heavy doors, the stolid walls, The last of summer lingers lonely-hearted On paths where even now a bright leaf falls, Whence even now the robins have departed. Where once the children's gamin laughter pealed, Where once their dusty feet and freckled faces Starred summer ways like daisies in a field, Approaching autumn threads her frosty laces. Within the stolid walls, the heavy doors, Hands pattern restlessness, whispers run swishing, Rows of small feet fret across worn floors; Each wistful pair of eyes, remembering, wishing, Turns from the leaf-like rustle of a book To give to summertime one last long look.
