Spafford, General President of Relief Society; Fanny S. Kienitz, President, University Stake Relief Society. President Kienitz reports a successful and happy work meeting: "All eight wards Page 618 NOTES FROM THE FIELD 619 were represented. The day began at ten a. M. Invocation was offered by Grace Colton of the University Ward presidency. Each ward planned its own work, which included: quilting, textile painting, crocheting slippers, making flowers, hot pads, animal toys, etc. The stake board displayed articles which could be made by the Relief Societies, if they desired. There was also a display on food preparation. Book binding was under the direction of the stake board. Stake chorister Annie Jensen played her accordion and led the women in community singing while they worked during part of the morning hours. We were pleased to have Blanche Stoddard of the general board of Relicf Society with us in the morning. General President Belle S. Spafford met with us at the lunch hour, and also our Stake President, W. W. Kirton Jr. The stake board served lunch to approximately 225 women.... After lunch the women of the University Ward sang a song they had composed about the shirts they were sewing for welfare. 'T'wo numbers, one a novelty song, were presented by the West Twelfth Ward trio, Violet Reese, Lillian Holmberg, and Rosa Phillips. Sister Holmberg then played two violin selections. "President Kienitz presented President Spafford with an orchid and a check for $370 to be used to help furnish the new Relief Society Building. We were honored to have President Spafford tell us some of her experiences in regard to the building." Photograph submitted by Della H.
