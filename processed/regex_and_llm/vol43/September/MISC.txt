Where plowshares slip beneath the loam, earth yields Her goodness to the harvesters. 'The weight Of orchard boughs is loosed. The April dawn, The pollened flowers of May are theirs to hold. The dews of June cannot be truly gone Where treasure lies in busheled red and gold. And they will never let the summer go Who capture it in bottles, row on row, Fruit and tuber caught in shining tin, And ripened stores in crock and pit and bin. When coming winter blasts the land with frost The season's root and leaf shall not be lost. Courtesy Z. C. M. I. Photo Reflex Studio PRESIDENT DAVID O. McKAY wil Happy Birthday to President David O MleRay Mildred C. McKay ig is a pleasure to wish President David O. McKay a happy birthday, and an honor to be asked to pay a tribute to a father-in-law whom I love. I realize his greatness, his life of service, and his dedication to the Church, but I shall not write of these. Instead, I want to mention some of the little things that endear him to our family. Even a few words about President McKay must include Sister McKay because to us, as Papa Dade and Mama Ray, they exemplify ideal parenthood. There is graciousness in the way they include their sons-in-law and daughters-in-law in the family. We are all their children. It is just a little thing, but flattering, to have Papa Dade say, "Have you met our daughter Mildred and our son David L.?" They both love children, and the greatest event in the family is a new baby. When they visit the hospital to welcome a new grandchild, Papa Dade, with a twinkle in his eye, always greets the new mother with, "Many happy returns of the day." President McKay's thoughtfulness includes all ages. Our daughters are tall, and when they were at that adolescent age of all arms and legs, their grandfather never said, "My, how tall you are getting," but always, "What a queen you are becoming!" "A happy home is a heaven on earth" is one of President McKay's favorite comparisons. We all feel the love in his home, and know that he wants that same sweet spirit in the homes of his children. In the family gathering, Christmas Eve, after the carols are sung, Papa Dade always says, "And now sing 'Love at Home.'"' I believe that he asks us to sing that song so that we will remember "Kindly heaven smiles above, when there's love at home." Page 573
