Fueling the Flame of Testimony Bertha S. Reeder President, Young Women's Mutual Improvement Association fame secured an old battered lamp of magical powers. By its aid he acquired great wealth. He bought a magnificent palace and became influential in his country. A wicked magician determined to gain possession of the lamp. He knew human nature very well; he bought bright new lamps and went to the palace, offering "new lamps for old." Aladdin's wife, knowing nothing of the magical powers of the old lamp, gladly exchanged it for the new one. Aladdin's palace, wealth, and influence vanished ovetnight. Each youth in the Church has within himself, waiting to be lighted, the fire of testimony, worth far more than any Aladdin's lamp. Once it is lighted, no one except himself can take its powers or blessings away from its possessor; but unless it is kept trimmed and fueled it may go out — because the owner has been careless. How this fire is lighted is a serious and difficult matter. Any fire to be lighted and made to burn has a pattern that must be followed. There must be tinder, kindling, fuel to keep the fire going, and a match to fire it. The tinder for a testimony must be laid by the parents, neighbors, teachers, and officers of the various units in which the young person moves. Oliver Wendell Holmes said that if you want to educate Page 574 A LADDIN of Arabian Nights hedrin, a man of wisdom. Someone you should begin with his grandmother. 'Thus heritage plays an important part in the life of anyone who wishes to gain a lasting testimony. Other things _ being equal, parents with a testimony impart that feeling for belief to their offspring. But we cannot stop with inheriting a good background in which to grow and develop a testimony. The tinder of inheritance mav be there, but fuel for the flame must be added by the person who wishes to gain a testimony. 'There is no substitute for hard work and study in order to gain knowledge and truth. No magical powers can give youth a testimony, no Aladdin's lamp can make it glow. There have been only one or two cases where a testimony came as a bolt of lightning, bringing conviction instantaneously. 'The conversion of Saul of 'Tarsus was one such instance. Yet—as we consider all of the facts in the case, were there not preparation and study and work on Saul's part before conversion came? Saul, both a Jew and a citizen of Rome, born in cosmopolitan 'Tarsus, studied under Gamaliel, one of the SanIt was Gamaliel who said when Peter and the apostles were called before the council:... Even if misguided Saul fought against the Church, his training had been such that he recognized the truth of the miracle when it occurred. The youth of Zion also have been taught correct principles — the tinder has been laid on which they may build. When eight years of age, they have been baptized and confirmed members of the Church. With the confirmation came a great blessing: the Holy Ghost was given:. Even the Comforter, which showeth all things, and teacheth the peaceable things of the kingdom... " which manifesteth all things which are expedient unto the children of men (D. & C. 39:6; 18:18). HE Holy Ghost affords the match which will light the testimony of young people. But they must be taught the power of this Personage of Spirit; they must have strong leaders who realize the importance of being true followers of God the Father and Jesus Christ his Son. 'True leaders will add to that which parents teach their young folk. Both will teach the true meaning of prayer, because it is through prayer that we come in close touch with our Eternal Father. Prayer becomes the compass that charts a true course back to our Father in heaven. Leaders help young people realize that our Father is a Personage such as we are, 315 and that he is interested in our welfare and eager to give us our blessings, if we but ask them from him. As Jesus said: If ye then, being evil, know how to give good gifts unto your children, how much more shall your Father which is in heaven give good things to them that ask him (Mt. 7:11). A boy in the service felt ashamed to kneel in prayer before his companions. He felt that they would deride him. So he would crawl into bed and then offer his prayers while lying down. One night he could stand it no longer. He knelt down by his bed and offered his heartfelt prayers to his Father in heaven. When he arose, he expected that the others would mock him. Instead, his companion said, "I am glad you are a boy who believes in prayer as I do." One must live with prayer to gain a testimony of the truthfulness of the gospel. 'The first thing in the morning a youth should offer up a prayer for the day ahead; and the last thing at night he should offer up his prayer of gratitude for the day's activities. He should pray without ceasing, if he wishes his life to reach the heights. He should add to his prayer, works. Thousands of our young people know that the Church is true, but sometimes they feel to follow the example of Aladdin's wife: they trade old lamps for new. They sell their precious heritage for a "mess of pottage," even as Esau sold his birthright to Jacob for food. A testimony is a precious thing; it grows by constant feeding, just as a flame must be constantly fueled in order to continue to live. It is interesting to watch a testimony grow in a young person's heart. When World War II was at its height, a chaplain in the service was assisted by a young woman who had been appointed his aide. Every Sunday morning he brought her to Sunday School. It wasn't long before a spark began to kindle. She attended sacrament meetings and firesides, upon invitation. Soon she began to seek the truth and ask questions. Arrangements were made for the missionaries in the area to hold classes with her. Since the young woman's mother had passed away several years before, the girl seemed to desire companionship and understanding. A year of study and investigation passed, and then she came one night to the mission headquarters and applied for baptism. She was very humble and had a firm understanding of the gospel, which she had acquired by study and prayer. She was bap- FUELING THE FLAME OF TESTIMONY tized and confirmed a member of the Church. Since that time she has been married in the temple, is doing the work for her kindred dead, and living a good Latter-day Saint life. F I were a young girl or boy at the very threshold of life, I would resolve to have a plan or goal to follow in building that life; no great building or bridge or organization has ever been built by blind opportunism. I would not want to leave to chance the kind of life I wanted to build for myself. Although I could conceivably draw the plans for my life, after all, a person in his teens is not very well qualified for such a task. Surely such an important job would require the hand of a master architect, and I would want the very best, one who knows more about me and my possibilities than anyone else. Is such an architect available to young people in the Church? One such Architect is our Heavenly Father. He has drawn up for all a plan of salvation: the gospel of Jesus Christ. Obeying the blueprint of the principles of the gospel: attending sacrament meetings, paying tithing, keeping the Word of Wisdom, keeping the Sabbath day holy, serving 577 others, living by prayer, I would learn to know and trust in my Heavenly Father. Obeying these principles, I would go forth confidently to build my life, realizing as any builder must, that the end product will depend upon no one else but me, the builder, I would guard my actions, my thoughts, my time, so that I would build a good life, one in which a testimony could flourish and burn brightly all the days of my life. I would be alert to grasp every opportunity to improve myself, seeking help from many sources. 'T'o do less than my best in building my life according to the gospel plan revealed in these latter days, would be to let down myself, my parents, and my Father in heaven. The real goal in mortality is the achievement by nghteous living of an eternal life in our Father's kingdom. By living a clean, chaste life through prayer, by study, by observation of the principles of the gospel, I would build a testimony that will endure through all the eternities to come. I would not exchange old lamps for new—but would find the fire of testimony lighting my path into a lifetime of happiness here and everlasting life hereafter. Flygare, President of the Scandinavian Mission, with headquarters in Copenhagen, wrote a letter to Albert Carrington, President of the European Mission, at Liverpool, under date of November 20, 1875, in which he stated that two Elders had been sent from Sweden, to preach the gospel in Finland. "Two Swedish Elders have been sent to Finland, to open the gospel door for that nation. I have had very encouraging news from them; they have had a chance to hold meetings and the power of God has been greatly manifested in their behalf" (Millennial Star, XXXVII, 798). These two first missionaries to Finland were brothers, Carl A. And John E. Sundstrom, members of the Church from Sweden. Converts were made and the first baptism was performed on May 7, 1876, "in the vicinity of Nikolaistad." Four persons were baptized during the year 1876, and in December a branch was organized at Nikolaistad — the first branch in the mission. Other elders were sent to Finland in the years following and the missionary work was extended. Great difficulty was experienced by the missionaries on account of the severity of the laws.
