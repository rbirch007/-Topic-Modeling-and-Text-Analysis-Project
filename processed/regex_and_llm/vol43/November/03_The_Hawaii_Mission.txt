On August 6, 1851, the first branch of the Church was organized at Kealakou, island of Maui, by Elder Cannon. It was known as the Kula Branch. By September 1, 1851, the membership of the Church in the islands numbered 220; this number increased to 4,000 by the end of the year 1853. In 1854, while still on his mission, George QO. Cannon, assisted by several educated natives, translated The Book of Mormon into the Hawaian language. The book was 725 published the following year in San Francisco. The work prospered and a temple was built, between 1915-1919, near the settlement of Laie, on the island of Oahu. 'Two stakes have been organized in recent years, Oahu and Honolulu. Today, there are approximately 7,000 members of the Church in the Hawaii Mission, located in _fortythree branches. D. Arthur Haycock is the Mission President. Forty-one Relief Society organizations, with 810 members, were reported in December 1955. Maurine M. Haycock presides over the Hawaii Mission Relief Society.
