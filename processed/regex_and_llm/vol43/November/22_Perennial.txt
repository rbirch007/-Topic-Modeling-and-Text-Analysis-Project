Perennial Dorothy J. Roberts We move with reticence of mien Now taking each a separate way Around this barrier between Formed by surmise. No word to say, Our substance hidden from the ear. All the glory and the grave Tumult waning with the year. No sound, no syllable to save The beauty from the dark debris. I often grieve the times we pass So mutely by the changing tree, The amber apple in the grass, And issue inward, false decree, With gavel in uncertain hands, Committing you, arraigning me, Whom neither knows nor understands.
