Let it not be for present delight nor for present use alone; let it be such work as our descendants will thank us for (From Seven Lamps of Architecture, by John Ruskin) HE Relief Society Building now stands completed, "a splendor in the sun." Dedicated October 3, 1956, with prayers of gratitude and devotion, hallowed with music, and blessed by prophetic words, this building represents the world-wide sisterhood — their home — and the symbol of the ideals and accomplishments of Relief Society women everywhere. * Built in the beloved valley of Deseret, circled by the high hills and towered mountains, the building rises as a fulfillment of promise 'an the shadow of the temple." Strength and beauty are evident in the simplicity of outline and in the shining surface of the polished stone. 'The steps are wide and inviting, the halls spacious and welcoming, the offices commodious and tasteful, yet expressing in their appointments refinement and immaculate order. The building is designed to meet the needs of an ever-growing society, expressing the ideals and purposes of the many women who believe that "charity never faileth" and strive to live according to the requirements of their high mission. In all the world there is no other building planned by women, constructed in large part by the offerings of women, built for the work of women, yet directed by prophetic counseling of the Priesthood. As Relief Society members, we are grateful to be of the house and of the lineage of eternal builders. As the present guardians of a dearly loved heritage, we stand today in the brief, bright moment of accomplishment, rejoicing in this time of fulfillment. We are as gleaners who have harvested from the past, and we are as sowers who plant the seeds for tomorrow's reaping. We honor and remember those devoted sisters who accepted with humility and thanksgiving the key which the Prophet turned for them. Nauvoo, the barrier plains, the desert valleys, the faraway frontiers— these are the pictures of the past. We look forward to the future, when our daughters and our granddaughters shall be Relief Society builders and shall receive from our hands the precious gifts of sisterhood and service. In that time, in another era, they shall say: "This house our mothers built for us." We are that golden link in time which binds the historic days to the *The proceedings of the dedication of the Relief Society Building will appear in the December issue of The Relief Society Magazine. We know that many of the builders of this edifice will never see its shining walls, its spacious rooms, its lovely gifts and adornments which have been sent from afar, but all Relief Society women everywhere will feel the joy of the builders, and the spirit of the house will flow forever into all the stakes and missions, into the far-off branches to the great cities and to the towns — to all places where faithful women meet in the name of the world-wide sisterhood — in all those places where devoted women build the structures of their earthly lives for an everlasting purpose. The replicas of sheaves of wheat which adorn the marble panels on the outside walls of the building are more than decorative motifs. The sheaves are as a joining of large and small treasures, harvested, perhaps, from abundant fields, or smaller garnerings from regions of small 739 harvests. Yet all the sheaves are rich in fulfillment. Even as the faithful sisters once gleaned in the desert fields and stored their wheat for a time of need, even so the women of Relief Society joyfully through all the years have gathered their sheaves of spiritual sustenance and have stored them for a time of need. The sheaves of wheat are a symbol of fulfillment, and they are like the precious grain which will be for the seeding of other fields in years to come. 'The service and the joys of Relief Society are everlasting; they are forever a reaping and a planting. This includes all departments except the Mormon Handicraft Gift Shop, located at 21 West South Temple Street. Dedication of She Reliof Society Building to Be Featured in December Mlagazine The complete proceedings of the dedication of The Relief Society Building will be featured in the December Relief Society Magazine. Included will be a cover picture of the building in four colors, and many other illustrations inside the Magazine, some of them in color. President McKay's dedicatory prayer and addresses by the First Presidency and the general presidency of Relief Society will be presented. Relicf Society Souvenir Plate Available The Relief Society Building souvenir plate may now be purchased at the general offices of Relief Society, 76 North Main Street, Salt Lake City 16, Utah, for $3.50, which includes mailing costs. The plate is made of Old English Staffordshire Ware and was especially designed for Relief Society. The center part of the design is a replica of The Relief Society Building. Photographs of the oil paintings of the nine general presidents of Relief Society are circled on the plate, and the whole pattern is bound with replicas of sheaves of wheat—an emblem long dear to Relief Society women. The motto of the society—"Charity Never Faileth'"—is engraved on the plate.
