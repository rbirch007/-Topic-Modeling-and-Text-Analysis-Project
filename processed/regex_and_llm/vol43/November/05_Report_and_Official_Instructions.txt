Report and Official Instructions President Belle S. Spafford [Digest of an Address Delivered at the Officers Meeting of the Annual General Relief Society Conference, October 3, 1956] NE of the important purposes () of the general Relief Society conference, is to present a brief report of the status of Relief Society as a whole as shown in the annual report, and to give to you sisters who are guiding and directing the work in the stakes and missions of the Church, a few instructions to aid you in the conduct of the work in the units over which you preside. Figures from the 1955 annual report are, in most instances, gratifying to us, as I am sure they will be to you who are responsible, in such large measure, for them. General Board The general board has a present membership of twenty-four sisters, devoted to the work of Relief Society and tireless in their efforts to fulfill their many assignments. Since our last general conference we have lost by death, one of our number, Sister Mary Grant Judd, who served as a member of the general board for sixteen years. We mourn her passing, we miss her association and services, but we express our gratitude for her life and for her magnificent contribution to Relief Society. Organizations and Reorganizations We are gratified by the steady increase in the number of Relief Society organizations. At the close of Page 726 1955 there was a total of 223 stake Relief Societies. There were forty reorganizations in the stakes and seventeen in the missions. We acknowledge with appreciation the services of the sisters who have been released. We welcome those now called to these positions, and wish for them success and joy in their calling. Missions The work of Relief Society is progressing in the missions of the Church. Leadership among the local sisters is being developed. Effort is being made to standardize the work. Relief Society members are gaining in testimony and are happy in the work, according to reports from mission Relief Society presidents and others. Membership Figures for 1955 show a membership of 163,513, an increase over 1954 of 7,213. 'This was a somewhat smaller increase than was shown on the previous annual report. Our total enrollment represents only 48.82 per cent (less than half) of the potential membership. Average attendance at regular meetings for members represents 35.86 per cent of the enrolled members, a slight decrease under 1954. Membership and attendance are giving us some concern. REPORT AND OFFICIAL INSTRUCTIONS Educational Program I am sure we all recognize that the quality of the regular Relief Society meeting depends, in large measure, upon the quality of the lesson work. Courses of study are carefully planned to meet the needs of Relief Society women and to accomplish the purposes set for the educational program by the general board and the Church. Generally speaking, the more than 13,000 Relief Society class leaders throughout the Church are doing excellent work. The general board notes that an increasing number of classes dealing with Relief Society subject matter are being conducted by persons and organizations other than the general board. While we are happy to have class leaders increase their knowledge of subject matter through study and instruction, we remind you that attendance at special classes is entirely an individual matter, and not a requirement of the Relief Society.. This should be made clear to class leaders. Directives to be followed in teaching the Relief Society lessons come from the general board, approved by the Church Publications Committee, and should not be set aside in favor of instructions from other sources. No instructions should receive consideration over those given by the general board. Subscription to Instructor Not Required There is some misunderstanding with regard to the necessity of Relief Society class leaders subscribing to the Instructor. This is an excellent publication and one that would 727 be helpful to class leaders and profitable reading for all of us. However, it is not required that Relief Society class leaders shall be subscribers to this publication. Book of Mormon Reading Program The theology lessons for 1956-57 mark the conclusion of a six-year course of study on the Characters and Teachings of The Book of Mormon. It was the aim to have every Relief Society member read The Book of Mormon during this period of study. A record has been kept each year of the number of women who, during the year, read the section of The Book of Mormon covered by the year's lessons. The reports on 1955-56 show that in 1956 there were 49.7% of the sisters who read 'he Book of Mormon assignment—an increase of 51%. Next year you will be asked for two reports on The Book of Mormon reading program as follows: 1. The number of women who have read during the year the section of The Book of Mormon covered by the lessons for the year. 2. The number of women who during the six-year period or any portion of it have read the book in its entirety. This coming year is the last year to achieve our goal of having every Relief Society member read the entire Book of Mormon during the period of our study of it. We urge you to lend your earnest efforts toward the realization of this goal. Work Meeting Your efforts in behalf of the work meeting continue to bear fruit. Visiting Teaching Visiting teaching figures for 1955 show an increase in family visits of more than 163,000 over 1954. Close to three million visits were made— an average of 8.35 per cent of our goal of twelve visits to each Latterday Saint family annually. 'This is an excellent record. Welfare Compassionate Services: The report of the compassionate services shows that careful attention is being given to these basic services of Relief Society. While there was an increase of only ten in the number of days care of the sick, it is attributed to the present-day trend of hospitalization for the sick. 'There was a decrease of twenty-two in the number of dressings for bunal. 'This, too, is in line with decreasing requests for this service. Visits to the sick and homebound, however, showed, as we would expect them to do, a substantial increase of 12,421 Or a total of 247,878 visits. Relief Society women are giving continued support to the Church Welfare Program, participating as they are called upon in sewing, in food processing, and in working on other welfare projects. Presidents are assisting the Welfare Program by taking storehouse inventories. Ward presidents made a total of 38,611 initial and follow-up visits to families in need under the direction of their respective bishops. It is important that the Society shall continue to meet its responsibilities in this important program. New Record Book Provisions A new Record Book will go into use in January 1957. With regard to recording participation in the Welfare Program, the new Record Book will call for some new information. In the space provided in the minutes of the monthly work meeting, you are asked to record the number of hours contributed to the Church Welfare Program by all females upon assignment by Relief Society. In crediting hours, any activity, the end result of which is for the General Church Welfare Program, should be counted; 1. E., fund-raising events, sewing, food processing. (Hours spent at welfare meetings or in custom canning should not be counted.) Count as one hour any portion of an hour that is thirty minutes or more. 'Time less than thirty minutes should not be counted. The work director counselor should be made responsible for reporting to the secretary monthly, the total number of hours contributed. This information may be obtained at the completion of an assignment from the chairman or supervisor of the activity, or from the person filling the assignment. In past years, minutes of the last regular meeting in May, all summer work meetings, together with muinutes for the October theology and work meetings, have been read at the October literature meeting. This infringes upon the time of the literature lesson in October and does not tend toward precise recording of minutes, since it is difficult for persons attending a meeting to retain details of it in mind for a long REPORT AND OFFICIAL INSTRUCTIONS period of time. 'Therefore, the new Record Book is set up to allow for the reading, at each meeting, of minutes of the meeting immediately preceding. It is recommended that at work meeting, minutes be read following the opening prayer. March and November Sunday Night Programs The program to be presented later in this meeting will be sent to you to distribute to the wards for optional use on the Fast Sunday evening of March 1957. Instructions for presenting the program will be included. 'This will allow Latterday Saint women everywhere who have been unable to attend the Dedicatory Service of The Relief Society Building, to sense the significance of it and to partake of the spirit of this historic event. We hope the program will be enjoyed by the sisters everywhere who have so loyally supported the building program. Magazine We are grateful for the excellent services of the Magazine representatives. As of December 31, 1955, there was a paid subscription list of over 138,096, an increase of 8,218 over the previous year. 'There were 204 stakes and 1,453 wards on the Magazine Honor Roll; fourteen missions and 486 branches. Music We are happy to report that there was at the close of 1955 a total of 2,005 Singing Mothers choruses in the stakes and missions. This is a delightful and satisfactory activity which brings with it spiritual and cultural benefits. The general board lends its full support and encouragement to Singing Mothers choruses. Correspondence Sisters, the individual general board members are anxious to be of help to you in your callings. However, we will appreciate having you address any correspondence related to Relief Society work to the general board and not to individual board members. Following _ this procedure places in our files a record of your requests and also of our answers to you. 'This is very important in the conduct of our work. We appreciate your service; we recognize your capabilities; we love you for your devotion to Relief Society, and we pray for you continually that the choice blessings of our Heavenly Father may be with you in your responsible callings.
