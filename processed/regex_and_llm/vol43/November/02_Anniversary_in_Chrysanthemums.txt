Nibley HE work of preaching the gospel in the Hawaiian Islands began in the fall of 1850, when ten elders, who were temporarily located in California, were called to open a mission for the Church in what was then known as "the Sandwich Islands." 'These elders were: Hiram Clark (designated as the mission president), Henry W. Bigler, Thomas Morris, John Dixon, William Farrer, James Hawkins, James Keeler, Thomas Whittle, Hiram Blackwell, and George OQ. Cannon. The missionaries sailed from San Francisco on November 22, 1850, and arrived in Honolulu harbor on December 12th, after a voyage of twenty days. The day following their arrival they climbed to the top of "Punch Bowl Hill," an extinct volcano near the harbor, where they built an altar of stones and dedicated the land to the teaching of the gospel and the establishment of the organizations of the Church.
