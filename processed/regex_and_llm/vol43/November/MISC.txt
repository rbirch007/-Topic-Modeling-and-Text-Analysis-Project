Stewart What is this golden tide of life That flows and falls In golden shallows and the flaming leaf? That spins and curves, Turning and reaching on the crimson vine? These shaken fields, harvested, Hold as in cupped hand the invisible Fountain and the springing air. What is this never-ceasing miracle That holds and molds The autumn, yet frees it from Chains and boundaries and labor? Shocks in the field murmur In wind-whispers, pumpkin carries Its own secret of being, The oak tree shakes down tomorrows. This beautiful moving power Builds and breaks And will not have done with beauty, But burns on hills a thousand Candles, shimmers in nooks, leaps Purple in the evening bracken, moves Through the day-shine, pushing Aside leaves to begin building April.
