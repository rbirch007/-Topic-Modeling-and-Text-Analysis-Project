Jackson reports the success of this unusual bazaar: "The bazaar presented an excellent display of handwork and other items. 'The sisters made several hundred dollars, and the bazaar included everything from wonderful New England recipe books made by Relief Society members, to fresh honey from the hives belonging to one of the members. The unique thing about the bazaar, I felt, was the way the sisters worked to get ready for it. 'They decided in advance what they wanted the bazaar to include. They then divided the work into as many months as there were left until the time of the bazaar. They mimeographed copies of their plans and gave a copy to each member. Each work meeting they worked like bees, beginning their quota for that month. Then, at the close of the work period, each member would take some part of the unfinished work home and bring it back to the next meeting. They would then start on the next month's quota. The plans were most perfect in organization and development." May Dyer, President, Central States Mission Relief Society, reports the activities of this devoted organization: "The sisters of the Liberty, Missouri, Branch always gather together with a special feeling of humility. They are privileged to meet each week in a hallowed spot, over the Liberty Jail, where the Prophet Joseph Smith suffered as a prisoner for many months. This branch Relief Society was begun in March 1955, with fourteen members. Under the able leadership of Sybil Rayner, Jewel Mackesty, and Alice Tolman, the members have made an enviable record, in spite of the fact that many of them have to drive a distance of fifty to seventy-five miles from their homes to meeting and back. Although they have to drive 110 miles a month to get their visiting teaching done, Wanda Rogers and Ocean Dick, and others who have helped, have always achieved a one hundred per cent visiting teaching record. The branch has also scored one hundred per cent on The Book of Mormon reading project. Last November the women conducted a very successful bazaar in Liberty. Although it was very stormy weather, the women brought 160 articles which they had made. In addition to the bazaar, they had a bake sale at the same time. At the spring Relief Society fair and fashion show, in Independence, the Liberty Branch again submitted many articles and received three first-place awards. Millar, President, Boise Stake Relief Society, reports an unusually successful social at which the visiting teachers were honored: "The enclosed account of the closing social of the Boise Fourth Ward Relief Society will enable you to catch the spirit of some of the fine work being done in our stake. I was present at this closing social, and the inspiration that Sister Emma F. Davis had in organizing this program was unique and exceptional." The details of the planning for the social and the working out of the plans are reported by Sister Davis: ""When I was asked to prepare the program for the luncheon honoring our visiting teachers, I felt at a loss, as it seemed we had already used every idea there could be. I prayed earnestly for inspiration, and it came to me very distinctly —- why not have the sisters who are visited each month do the honoring? That was the answer. I asked two sisters in each district if they would like to pay tribute to their visiting teachers. 'Their response was one of the most heart-warming experiences I have ever had. The plans were to be a surprise for the visiting teachers, so we seated them at small tables by districts with the two members who were to pay them tribute. As each visiting teacher arose when her name was called, the sister in her district paid her a lovely tribute. Many original verses and sweet sentiments were expressed, which brought tears of joy and warmed the hearts and delighted the souls of our visiting teachers. Even two sweet non-members had enjoyed their visiting teachers very much, and were appreciative of the privilege of attending the social. Every officer and teacher of our Relief Society was also honored that day, to show our appreciation for their untiring efforts in Relief Society work. We feel that the visiting teachers in our ward now know how very much they are appreciated."'
