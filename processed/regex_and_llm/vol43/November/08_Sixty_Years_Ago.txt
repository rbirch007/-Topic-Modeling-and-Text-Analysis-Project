Sixty Years Ago Excerpts From the Woman's Exponent, November 1, and November 15, 1896 "For THE RIGHTS OF THE WOMEN OF ZION AND THE RIGHTS OF THE WomeEN OF ALL NATIONS" CLARA BARTON'S WORK IN TURKEY: If you ask of our mission what we found and what we left, there is really but this to say: We found want, desolation and suffering, and relieved them so far as we were able. In spite of all, these are still there, and they have grown since. Winter is approaching and unless the open hand of charity, not of this country alone, but of all civilized countries, is reached out to them and access be secured, hunger and cold will gather the victims in by the tens of thousands, and bury them like the fallen leaves beneath the snow. —cClara Barton, President of the American Red Cross THE SPIRIT OF SLEEP He seals the eyes that are wet with tears, And those where gladness beams, And by a wave of his scepter-wand We soar to the land of dreams In the silent gloom of the midnight hour His standard is unfurled; With his misty crown he reigns supreme The monarch of the world. —Julia Bowring Maltese QUEEN VICTORIA: Queen Victoria has been queen of Great Britain during the administrations of Van Buren, Harrison, Tyler, Polk, 'Taylor, Fillmore, Pierce, Grant, Hayes, Garfield, Arthur, Harrison and Cleveland. —Selected THE EXPONENT: The prestige the Exponent has given the women of this Church in the outside world is little realized; it has broken down mountains of prejudice, it has gone into the homes of the rich, and the famous, poets, writers, philosophers, people of rank and title; besides it affords opportunity to the Elders who are out preaching the Gospel to allay much prejudice they have to meet —KHditorial BOOK OF POEMS: The book of poems to be issued by Mrs. E. B. Wells is now in course of publication... More than one third of the pages have been prepared, and it is expected the book will be ready for subscribers before Christmas. —FEditorial Note THE NATIONAL COUNCIL OF WOMEN: The Executive meeting of the National Council of Women of the United States will be held this year in Boston on December 2, 3, and 4th. Notices have been sent to each society belonging to the Council to send delegates, but this year Utah organizations cannot conveniently send representatives on account of the great distance and the scarcity of means. —Editorial Note Page 736 LLEN C. HENDERSON, Salt Lake City, Utah, is the author of You Can Teach Your Child That Reading Can Be Fun, a new book which includes many of the excellent techniques which Mrs. Henderson has developed during a long and distinguished teaching career in the speech arts. MILDRED (BABE) DIDRICKSON ZAHARIAS, forty-two, greatest American woman athlete of all time, died in 'Texas, September 27, 1956. An Olympic recordholder, she excelled in many sports and was also noted for feminine accomplishments. Her marriage to George Zaharias was a classic story of romance and devotion. LBERTA H. CHRISTENSEN, a member of the general board of Relief Society, was awarded first prize by the League of Utah Writers at their annual Round-up in September, for her poem "To the Framers of the Constitution," which was published in The Relief Society Magazine, September 1955. Mrs. Christensen also won the second prize in the unpublished poetry division. Vesta P. Crawford, Associate Editor of The Relief Society Magazine, placed first in the unpublished poetry division, and was awarded a citation.
