Anderson EAST LOS ANGELES STAKE (CALIFORNIA), MISSION PARK WARD RELIEF SOCIETY MEMBERS MAKE MULTI-COLORED HOOKED RUG FOR RELIEF SOCIETY ROOM Left to right: Grace Laidlaw, President; Carma Clark, Counselor and designer of tug; Fairie Sessions, Counselor. Genevieve L. Anderson, President, East Los Angeles Stake Relief Society, reports this interesting and worthwhile project: " "Tis a rug of many colors, like Joseph's coat of old. Loving hearts and hands have made it, through hours of work untold." "These lines from a poem composed by Joanne C. Duncan, and dedicated to Counselor Fairie Sessions, very well describe the multi-colored hooked rug lovingly and patiently made by Relief Society members. The exquisitely hooked piece, which was two years in the making, consists of forty-four sections and will cover an area fifteen by thirty feet. It was hooked by over sixty women for the Relief Society room in the new chapel, for which ground was broken in 1954. In January of that year, Sister Sessions proposed that the organization hook a rug for the Relief Society room in the new chapel. Unanimous approval of her suggestion gave impetus to the project. A talented designer, Carma Clark suggested a pattern that would be effective, yet not too difficult. Thirty colors were used, and each section consists of fifty-four squares, each five and one half inches square. Special mention should be made regarding the devoted efforts of two sisters, each over seventy years of age, who donated far more than their share of work hours: Josie Alleman and Amanda Bassett.
