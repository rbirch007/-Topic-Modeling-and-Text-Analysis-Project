My Garden Catherine E. Berry My garden wears upon its face The bloom of poppies, Queen Anne's lace To frame its locks of iris blue, And zinnias of every hue To star its flowered Sunday gown, With marigolds to make a crown. And Canterbury bells will ring Whenever winds of twilight sing, Pale moonflowers open in the night When four-o'clocks have closed up tight; And when the dawn has tinged the skies My garden opens rosebud eyes.
