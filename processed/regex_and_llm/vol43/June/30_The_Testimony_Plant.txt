She Sestimon y Plant Nancy M. Armstrong Wt all know that a testimony of the gospel of Jesus Christ is a living, growing thing. All living things need nourishment. When we have a particularly beautiful or rare plant in our gardens we give it extra care. We see that it has sunlight and water, cultivation and plant food. In the garden of life, what is more beautiful than a testimony of the gospel? It is indeed entitled to the choicest spot; to the best care and finest nourishment we can provide for it. A testimony draws its nourishment from activity in the work of the Lord, and in keeping his commandments. John Keats expressed the idea that "A thing of beauty is a joy forever." Surely this is true of a testimony. It is a source of joy to the one who possesses it, and a thing of beauty to all who come im contact with it. When a testimony is in full flowering, it bears many lovely blossoms of good deeds. The more bouquets one gives away to others, the more abundantly the testimony blooms. Like othe: growing things, a testimony can lie dormant for a period of time, but, if it receives no nourishment, it may wither away. Our first obligation is to see that our own testimonies are receiving all the sunlight and nourishment they need to keep them in full flowering. Our next duty ls to feel the responsibility to reach out to some fine sister whose testimony is lying dormant and help her bring it into bloom, so that her garden of life will be rich with the perfume "peace of mind"; the fragrance of every true testimony.
