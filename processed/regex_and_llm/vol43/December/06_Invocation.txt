PETERSEN Of the Council of the Twelve OC) Heavenly and Eternal Father, as thy humble children, we have gathered here this afternoon to worship thee and to thank thee for thy bounteous blessings to us. We have met this afternoon at the conclusion of a great enterprise undertaken by the Relief Society sisters of thy great Church. And at this time, we honor them and thank thee for them. We are grateful for the mighty mission they carry forward here in the earth. In all humility, we thank thee for their faith and their devotion and for the outstanding influence they have here in the earth, in their families and homes, in the Church, and also in the communities in which they live. They are, indeed, thine handmaidens here in the earth. They have with them a divine power. We acknowledge it; we thank thee for them. We thank thee for the great Relief Society organization, and for the women who have headed it and who carry on in the various wards and stakes and missions and in the general board. We thank thee that thou didst inspire the great Prophet, Joseph Smith, to organize this society, and we are grateful that it has carried on over the years and fulfilled such a great mission. Our Father, we this afternoon are to conduct this program in connection with the dedication of the beautiful new headquarters of the Relief Society; and thy great servant, President McKay, has come here to dedicate this great building — The Relief Society Building. We humbly ask that thou wilt inspire him as he does so. We pray that thou wilt be with all who take part in this dedicatory service, that all may be done under the direction of thy Holy Spirit. We are thankful for all of the women who have contributed for the construction of this Building and for its beautification. And now, Father, we pray that as the Building is about to be dedicated, that we may all be dedicated in our hearts to further service in thy great cause. And we ask thine especial blessings on the entire Relief Society organization, that it may be prospered, that it may grow, that it may bring a message of conversion, conviction, and of happiness and of comfort to the many, many thousands who will be the recipients of the labors of the sisters. Father in heaven, we now pray that thou wilt be with us during these services, guide all that is done, accept our gratitude for all thou hast given, and we do it all in the name of the Lord, Jesus Christ. The Singing Mothers chorus sang "If Ye Love Me, Keep My Commandments." Report of Relief Society Building Activities Counselor Marianne C. Sharp T has been eleven years since || Sister Spafford, with the approval of the First Presidency, proposed to the members of Relief Society convened in the Relief Society general conference of 1945, that they undertake the erection of a Relief Society Building. 'The proposition was enthusiastically approved. In 1947, two years later, at the general Relief Society conference, a financial plan, approved by the First Presidency, was presented and adopted whereby the sisters within a year would raise half a million dollars for the Building, with the understanding that the Church would match this amount. Every member of Relief Society in the stakes was expected to contribute five dollars—for this was to be an undertaking of the many. In addition, each stake board was asked to contribute half of its funds on hand as of January 1947. No quotas were given to the missions by the general board. Participation was left entirely to the discretion of the respective mission presidents. 'The European missions, in the aftermath of war, were not expected to contribute at all. When the sisters gathered for the 1948 Relief Society general conference, one year later, they came rejoicing. All stakes in the Church (at that time numbering 172) were recognized for filling their quotas. As of October 1948 there was contributed by the membership of Relief Society in stakes over $457,000; from stake board funds over $36,000; with a few very generous contributions from individuals. 'The missions outside Europe, under the direction of their mission presidents, had entered into this undertaking REPORT OF RELIEF SOCIETY BUILDING ACTIVITIES 793 with great enthusiasm also, and a total of nearly $48,000 was contributed within that year by the missions of the Church. 'The great goal of half a million dollars had been reached and exceeded by nearly $70,000. In the years which have elapsed since October 1948, contributions in money have come in from European missions. Many missions located in foreign countries have also sent a beautiful object of art representing the respective countries. These now beautify the building. And, during the past few months, many stakes have presented lovely and useful gifts to add that woman's touch to make The Relief Society Building truly the embodiment of the culture, tasteful furnishings, and bounteous living which we, as Latter-day Saint women, enjoy in our own individual homes. The Church had agreed to match Relief Society funds to half a million dollars. The million dollars was all needed for the erection as prices had gone up and there still remained the Building to be furnished. 'The general board, you will recall, had contributions of over a half million dollars. Back we went to the First Presidency. Would they now be so generous as to match collected funds, including accrued interest, and reserve funds to a total of $100,000 — thus allowing $200,000 for furnishings and equipment and to include any overrun on the Building? HE, First Presidency were most considerate and generous and agreed to match this additional $100,000 dollars. 'The costs here related do not include the value of the site. The site, "in the shadow of the temple" which was our first choice for a site, was granted to us by the First Presidency in October 1952. Ground for the Building was broken on October 1, 1953. 'The cornerstone was laid on September 30, 1954. In it were placed materials of historic interest and the names of all contributors to The Relief Society Building. A copy of these names is today bound in two volumes in the Building. A third volume will contain pictures of gifts which have been contributed with the names of the donors. And so today, in the hearts of all Relief Society members, there is a feeling of humble gratitude for the Church, for its understanding of the needs and desires of the women of the Church. All Relief Society members throughout the lands of the earth are with us today in spirit as they have been with us in contributing of their means with sacrifice for The Relief Society Building. In deep humility we thank our Heavenly Father for numberless blessings and we do so in the name of his son, our Savior, Jesus Christ. PRESIDENT BELLE S.
