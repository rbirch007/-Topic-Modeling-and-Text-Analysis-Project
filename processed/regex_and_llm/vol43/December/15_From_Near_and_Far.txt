From Near and Sar I must mention how much I enjoyed Mildred McKay's article on President McKay in the September Magazine. It is such personal glimpses that enrich our understanding and knowledge of our loved prophet. I especially wish to express my appreciation for the following numbers in the October Magazine: "Our Cabin Had a Porch," by Nell Murbarger—country-reared, I grew nostalgic as I read her beautiful and vivid descriptions; "My Guiding Light," by Lucille Tournear — what a lovely tribute to her mother; "Helping Hands," by Flo Whittemore — a beautifully clear portrait of the "muinistry of hands'; and "So Dear to My Heart," by Ruth Young—I shed _ tears, remembering the little steepled church of my childhood. I love the poem, "Night Incident," by Lael W. Hill. —Mabel Law Atkinson Dayton, Idaho The Magazines are coming out very beautifully, and we appreciate them. They are certainly filling the place in our Church they were meant for. —Nina N.
