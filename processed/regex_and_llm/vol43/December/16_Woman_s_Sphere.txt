SPAFFORD, General President of Relief Society, in October, attended meetings of two national groups in New York City. She first attended meetings of the American Mothers Committee, the organization which selects the American Mother of the Year. The meetings stressed the importance of a more spiritual observance of Mothers Day and mote effective religious training in the home, in harmony with the slogan of the Committee: "A Bible in Every Home." At the biennial meetings of the National Council of Women, of which President Spafford has been second vicepresident for the past nine years, she was elected a member at large of the Executive Committee. President Spafford reported on the activities of Relief Society and of the American Mothers Committee. She opened the second day's meetings with prayer, presided at the luncheon, and paid a tribute to Mrs. Charlotte Leyden, retiring President of the Council, who was also elected a member at large. MILY H. BENNETT, First Counselor, Young Women's Mutual Improvement Association, also attended the meetings of the National Council of Women and presented a report of the activities of the Y. W. M. I. N Cairo, Egypt, today, many women, hiding behind veils not long since, are as modern in dress, living conditions, and outlook as are American women. Leading feminist educators, such as Madame Nahid Sirry, President of the Feminist Union, and Madame Karima Said, of the Egyptian Ministry of Education, have labored assiduously for these improved conditions. Women are doing much excellent social welfare work throughout Egypt, are holding more and more government offices, and are numerous employees in the Ministries of Public Health, Education, and Social Affairs. A larger-than-life-size bronze statue of Eliza R. Snow, the great pioneer woman leader, second General President of Relief Society, who gave so untiringly of her fine mind and talents to the women of the Church, now stands in the garden of the Daughters of Utah Pioneers Memorial Building, 304 North Main Street, Salt Lake City, Utah.
