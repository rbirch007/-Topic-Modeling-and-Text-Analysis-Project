After the removal of the Latter-day Saints to the West in the fall of 1847, most of the members in Canada emigrated to Utah. Missionary work there was carried on by the Eastern States Mission until April 1919, when the Canadian Mission was organized, with Nephi Jensen of Salt Lake City as the first president. A house was secured at 36 Ferndale Avenue, 'l'oronto, which for many years served as the mission headquarters. 'In 1930 there were 1,232 members of the Church in the Canadian Mission. In December 1955 this number had increased to 3,309, located in 34 branches. J. Earl Lewis is the present president of the Canadian Mission. 'There were twenty-two Relief Society organizations reported in December 1955. Leah Henrichsen Lewis presides over the Relief Society of the Canadian Mission.
