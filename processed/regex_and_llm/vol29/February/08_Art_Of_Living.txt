ART OF LIVING Gertrude Perry Stanton I loved the works of master minds In music and in art; Reveled in poetry sublime— A farmer won my heart. What leisure has a farmer's wife Such tastes to gratify? I wrote my symphony in soup, My poetry in pie. I found great songsters with the birds, And sculpture in the hills, Most gorgeous paintings in the west, And rhythm in the rills. I lived an opera every day— Adventure, ecstasy; My children sang the solo parts, The chorus rang from me. My jellies showed the colors rate That painters love to use; I modeled in my bread and rolls— And thus I wooed my muse. "
