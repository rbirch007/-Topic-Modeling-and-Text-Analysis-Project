AND TELL OF TIME Mabel Jones Gabbott Were you not born within this span of time, These hurried years that mark our life's estate, Had I not too been chosen for this clime, Had I been born a century too late, Had we not each in our own plan and way Walked at some time the path that led us here, Had we not met and known at once that day That we at last had found each other, dear.... Had we not met, how bleak the world would be! How drear and meaningless this life of mine. Yet it is rich and full of ecstasy, A thing of beauty, sweet and true and fine. I've lived and loved and learned to feel and see Because you made your life a part of mine.
