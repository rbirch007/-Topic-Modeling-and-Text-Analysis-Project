other's Prayer Dorothy Clapp Robinson
Our boys are men: Across the too-quick years they have Leaped to the full stature of manhood. Their limbs are straight; their cheeks against ours No longer warm velvet. They search for Reason; and have marked the world For their conquering.
Yet, as the virgin tints of dawn linger Even as it is quickened into day, So about their mouths and in their eyes Loiters that glimpse of divinity That was there when first we held them against our hearts.
Please, God, let not their sacrifice be in vain.
