Life and Death For Mrs. Margaret Erickson Tenderly, gently, we'll lay her away, To a rest of serenity sweet, Where pain and affliction can never disturb A slumber sublime and complete. No more shall the ills, to mortals akin, Torture and vex and annoy, The form lying here so peaceful at rest In the realm of Death's bountiful joy. Tt was Life and not Death that brought suffering keen To the body that rests on this bier, And held it bound down in agony's throe So long, for year after year. It was Life that offered no help or relief As the days, months and years went by; From the tortures of Life the only relief For this suffering soul was to die.
