The Form of a Little Child Hannah Deady Tomstk I do not care for the milk-white pearls On the neck of the withered dame; I do not care for the diadem That helps the queen to reign. As I stand, in the shifting sands of time When the winds of life blow wild. Let me hold in my arms, close to my heart, The form of a little child. What care I should a kingdom rise— What care I should it fall— When I look in my babies' trusting eyes Then I am queen of all. And the treasures of Sodom are not for me, I am not by the world beguiled, When I hold in my arms, close to my heart, The form of a little child.
