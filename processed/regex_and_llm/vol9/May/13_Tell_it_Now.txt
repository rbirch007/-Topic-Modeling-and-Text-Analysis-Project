TELL IT NOW. Bertha A. Kleinman. There's so much I know about you, Let your other neighbors doubt you, O, so much that makes me glad to be your friend, Yet I know, and you—throughout you— That a day may dawn without you, And how little it will count to tell you then. There are days when you have blundered, When your other friends have wondered, And I know so well the dregs that strew your cup, When your peace is shorn asunder By the trials that maul you under, Yet I haven't spared a hand to help you up.
