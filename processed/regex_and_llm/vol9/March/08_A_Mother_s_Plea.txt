A Mother's Plea Louise Gulbransen For twelve long years I was a wife With mate to shield me from all strife, Blest with the gift of motherhood, The greatest gift bestowed by God, Those rosy, bright-eyed boys and girls, To cheer me with their wiles and curls. Then came a tempter to our home, Upon destruction bent, And called to aid some fiends on earth, And willing hands they lent. Without remorse they planned and fought To overrule. What God had wrought, And knowing this, there still are some Condone the work that they have done. No friend unto the rescue came, As undisturbed they played their game, Love, honor perished, reason died, As my companion left my side.
