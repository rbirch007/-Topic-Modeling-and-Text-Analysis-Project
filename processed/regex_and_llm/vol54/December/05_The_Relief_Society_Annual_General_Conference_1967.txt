President Belle S. Spafford conducted all of the Tabernacle sessions of the conference, supported by her Counselors Marianne C. Sharp and Louise W. Madsen and forty-seven other General Board members. In attendance at various sessions of the conference were three members of the First Presidency, President Hugh B. Brown, President N. Eldon Tanner and President Joseph Fielding Smith, who is an Advisor to Relief Society, and also the other two Advisors to Relief Society, Elder Harold B. Lee and Elder Marion G. Romney and Bishop Robert L. Simpson of the Presiding Bishopric. Each of these brethren addressed the conference, giving to the sisters timely and inspired messages enlarging their vision regarding their responsibilities as wives, mothers, and homemakers, and also as leaders in Relief Society. For the first time the proceedings of the two Wednesday sessions and the Thursday afternoon session were carried over television and in color on _ station KBYU which serves the major portion of northern and central Utah. Delayed television broadcasts of the afternoon sessions each day were carried in the evenings. For the second year, excerpts from the Tabernacle sessions were broadcast in English and some in Spanish over the short-wave, Church-owned radio station in New York, Station WNYW, to an estimated twothirds of the surface of the world. Never before have the inspired messages directed to the needs 898 and interests of women of the Church been aired to such a vast listening audience. Provision was also made for foreign-speaking, representatives in attendance at the conference to hear the proceedings through simultaneous translation in Spanish, Tahitian, German, French, and Portuguese. The international character of the conference was enhanced by participation of sisters from many countries. Heartwarming remarks were given in the opening session by Delfina de Torres, Stake Relief Society President of the new Guatemala City Stake, and by Angelia F. De Giuliani, Stake Relief Society President of the new Buenos Aires Stake in Argentina. Their messages were given in Spanish and translated for the congregation in English. Mavis B. Draper, President of the new Sydney South Stake Relief Society in Australia offered the closing prayer in the first session and Lucrecia Sua rez Vda. De Juarez, Stake Relief Society President of the new Mexico City North Stake, offered the benediction at the concluding session of the conference. In the Presidencies Department on Thursday morning a chorus of seventeen Tahitian Singing Mothers from the French Polynesian Mission sang. They also entertained at the reception held on Wednesday evening in the Relief Society Building. Music for the opening session of the conference was provided by the West Boise Stake Singing Mothers conducted by LaRue R. Campbell with Roy M. Darley at the organ. A large combined Singing Mothers chorus from Alberta, Canada, consisting of The Relief Society Annual General Conference about four hundred voices under the baton of Florence J. Madsen, with Ellen N. Barnes conducting one number, presented music in the general session on Wednesday afternoon and also at both of the Friday sessions of the General Conference of the Church. Brother Darley was also at the organ on Wednesday afternoon. The stakes participating in the chorus were Alberta, Calgary, Calgary North, Edmonton, Lethbridge, Taber, and Taylor Stakes. All in attendance rejoiced with the members of the Church from Canada which is commemorating its centennial year. Alexander Schreiner was at the organ at the Thursday morning Tabernacle session of the conference and Robert Cundick on Thursday afternoon. Specific guidance and directives relative to carrying forward the program of Relief Society were given to stake and mission leaders by President Spafford in her Report and Official Instructions on Wednesday morning. That afternoon she and _ her Counselors addressed the sisters with moving and impressive messages. Sister Mary V. Cameron also spoke, representing the General Board. Wednesday evening approximately 3,000 Relief Society leaders from stakes and missions were welcomed by the General Board at a beautiful reception in the Relief Society Building. Detailed instructions were given in separate departmental sessions conducted by General Board committees on Thursday morning for presidencies, secretary-treasurers, music leaders, Magazine representatives, homemaking leaders, visiting teacher message leaders, and also spiritual living, social relations, and -culture refinement class leaders. On Tuesday preceding the conference, a display of thousands of beautiful homemaking articles provided practical and delightful suggestions for homemaking leaders and members of stake and mission presidencies who were invited to view them. Messages of lasting influence were conveyed to those in attendance through impressive presentations as well as music and inspired speaking. One presentation entitled, "As a City on a Hill," written by Alberta H. Christensen, was given in both the social relations and the Presidencies Departments. It demonstrated how the moving message of a social relations lesson can be portrayed in a Christmas dramatization. At the concluding session of the conference, the presentation, "The Call to Benevolence," written by Luacine C. Fox, stirred the hearts of all present to greater compassion and charity toward their fellow men. Relief Society leaders from most of the stakes and missions in the United States, Canada, and Mexico; representatives from Australia, New Zealand, South America, Central America, and Tahiti, from South Africa, Switzerland, France, and England, departed from conference with renewed conversion of the divinity of the restored gospel which encompasses unsurpassed light and knowledge regarding the place, challenges, and opportunities for women in the gospel plan.
