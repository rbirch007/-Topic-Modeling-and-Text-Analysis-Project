MY DAY OF DAYS IS NOW > Bertha A. Kleinman The olden days are not for me to keep; A wraith are they to lull me as | sleep. From wilted shard and ashen leaves consigned, | turn to face the newer tasks assigned. These days are mine, no matter what my age, Enriched, rewarding, turning page on page, Fulfilling and amending what is past. My days are now to live and learn and last, For all they are, nor questing why or how. Y These days are mine. My day of days is now. J)
