Magazine Honor Roll for 1966 Counselor Marianne C. Sharp @ As Relief Society grows among the daughters of Zion, The spreads its message to ever-increasing numbers. The General Board is gratified by the increase of 11, 625 subscriptions in 1966 making a total of 252,902 subscriptions. The year 1966 is also a notable one in the history of The because a Spanish edition commenced publication in June 1966. As of December 31, there were 5,590 subscribers. Many letters are received at the office of the General Board from Spanish-speaking sisters of South America, Central America, Mexico, and the Philippines expressing their gratitude. More Latter-day Saint sisters speak Spanish than any other language, except English. The grand total of subscriptions, at the conclusion of 1966 is 258,492. The large subscription to the is an important factor in keeping the price of the English subscription down to $2 a year. In the true spirit of sisterhood, those concerned with the Magazine at Relief Society Headquarters, and the presidents and Magazine representatives throughout Relief Society organizations give unstintingly of their time and talents in producing and selling this great and vital means of promoting and binding the world-wide sisterhood. The spirit of Magazine representatives is exemplified by this letter: To Sisters� Dear Sisters: This will be my last order for the Magazine. After twenty years and eight months, | have to give this privilege to a younger sister as | am so crippled | cannot help myself. | have to depend on the other sisters to take my walker or my wheel chair whenever | go out. Most of the cars are too small to hold them, and it is too much for the sisters to handle them, so | am not able to get to the stake meetings. | am grateful for having had this privilege and will do all | can to help the dear sister who will take my place. February 27th | will have my 81st birthday and cannot get around to contact the sisters as | should. It makes me feel very sad to have to give it up. | am grateful to my Heavenly Father for all the blessings he has given me, and for the many friends | have, both old and young. Sincerely, Sister Frances Kolarik The calling of a Magazine representative requires dynamic and unselfish service. She is enthusiastic over the worth of the Magazine, keeps abreast with its contents, seeks out the Latter-day Saint women, and is persuasive in selling its value to the sisters. She knows that any woman who reads and studies it will be better prepared to fulfill her great functions as a woman and as a Relief Society worker. Heartfelt appreciation of the General Board goes to all those who have accomplished this outstanding record in 1966. There were 371 stakes on the Honor Roll, and 180 having 100 per cent or more subscriptions. Twenty-four missions were on the Honor Roll, and four missions achieved 100 per cent or more. For the twentieth year, South Los Angeles Stake led the Church, with 198 per cent, and this year the North Central States Mission was highest, with 110 per cent. The General Board extends to every faithful, devoted Magazine representative, in all the stakes and missions, wards and branches, its heartfelt thanks and appreciation for their united efforts and their outstanding achievement.
