It was only the second summer after that Halloween, when the pipe line for the power plant broke in the night, and the washout caused a slide that completely blocked off the road, about two thirds of the way down the canyon. Ren was doing more and more of the farm work, now. The afternoon following the washout, Ren was finishing mowing the seventeen-acre field, and Grandpa, Wes, and I had walked out there to see if the hay had dried enough that Wes could start raking it. The mowing was just finished, when something startled the horses, and somehow Ren, who had mowed for years, got in the way and had practically all of his left foot cut off. Well, everybody did the very best they could, including Dr. Browne, who clambered over the rocks and mud with his bag. Ren's life and leg were saved. He soon became an expert with his crutches, as we all knew he would, and, eventually, when 278 medical skills were better, he had an artificial foot. From that day on, a mildly warm feeling existed between Mrs. Maysprite and nearly everyone in Verona. At last the children began to let her treat them with her pans of goodies, like the ones she must have been preparing for them on Halloween for years, to no avail. The sisters exchanged recipes and embroidery ideas with her sometimes. Occasionally, one or two accepted a lift in her automobile on the way to the store with eggs to sell. She even helped the Daughters of the Pioneers with the flower arrangements at members' funerals, a couple of times. And the brethren sometimes sent their big boys to clean her irrigation ditch. I should like to be able to say that Mrs. Maysprite was taught the gospel in Verona, and that, eventually, she was baptized. But the fact is that when she grew too old to live alone, her nephew or cousin and his wife came and moved her away somewhere to share their home. I was at college then and had lost track of affairs in Verona, somewhat. I guess none of us ever thought of saying anything to Mrs. Maysprite about religion, for in those days we did not ask the outsider any golden questions. So many years have passed that she has surely been called home by now, but she has never been brought back to Verona to share Mr. Maysprite's burial lot and the direction of his grave. So, when the time comes, Mr. Maysprite will have to rise up and look down the canyon all alone, rejoicing in the confirmation of his firmly abiding belief in the resurrection. But Ren's little wife Marj and I are partners in searching to find out where Mrs. Maysprite went, and all the other necessary facts concerning her and Mr. Maysprite. For their love for each other was true love.
