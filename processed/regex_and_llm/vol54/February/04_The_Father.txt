THE FATHER Dorothy J. Roberts Sabbath was a beloved word— Peace lent him a pillow Coming on velvet feet— And sieep—he kept the law, To bring a father home again Wheat in bin and furrow From the furrow and the wheat And for his cattle, straw. He had his fields, his family, Tithe and Sabbath offering, The Sabbath and '"'The Rod," Bathed and in his best, The brother and the sister Never once to deviate Akin by blood or God. Or break the day of rest... Sabbath, more than anything— Coming on velvet feet— Can bring a father back again From the golden years of wheat.
