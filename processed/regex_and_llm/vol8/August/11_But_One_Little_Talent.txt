But One Little Talent Janey Haefeli
If you have but one little talent And its light is dim and small, If you polish it and use it Where 'twill do a little good It will grow to be a great light As the Maker meant it should.
If you dig a hole and hide it, Guard its hiding place with care, From unuse it will grow tarnished, Its existence come to naught, Though it might have been a beacon, If it had not been forgot.
Is better than neglect. God never made a little star To shine brighter than the sun. Nor did he give the talents Just alike to every one.
Each talent should be useful. And each little light should shine. He doesn't set an infant To accomplish like a man.
