Departed Spirits. By Laura Moench Jenkins.

Softly the vesper bells, ringing at eve, Called the fair spirit daughters to prayer. Silently glided each form to its place, Joining sweetly the requiem there. "Stay daughter Magdalena! Why art thou downcast?" inquired Mother Barbara as they two followed the retiring throng from the vesper hall. Tears sprang to Magdalena's eyes at the sympathetic words of the aged matron. "Nay, speak out, daughter. Dost thou yearn for freedom from this prison home, or cravest thou the companionship of thy husband and sons?" "O Mother Barbara!" sighed the unhappy woman.

"How many many years we have been incarcerated here; waiting, watching, and pleading with our heavenly Father for deliverance.
