Zion. Maud Dobson. The Wasatch mountains proudly stand Enrobed in snow and pine. Their mighty peaks on every hand Crowned with a light divine. As The first hills appeared to Moses' band of Palestine. Safe sheltered at the mountain's feet The busy city lies, Nature drowses in soft heat, Nor heeds how swift time flies; Man fills the busy marts with noise and merchandise. And to and fro with mystic ways Flit guides we cannot see, They keep a vigil on our days And guard us silently, That all who dwell here by His grace, May nobler, purer be. O ancient hills! O inland sea! O town, fair as a bride! O temple, where shall never be Deceit nor hate nor pride. Dearer to me one stone of thee Than all the world beside.
