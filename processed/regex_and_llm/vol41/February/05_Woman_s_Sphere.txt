Woman's Sphere Ramona W. Cannon ton of Provo, nineteen years old, was one of eight to win $300 scholarships from Sears Roebuck Foundation. Her national prize was for home improvement — making a basement storeroom into an attractive playroom. Among many improvements, she upholstered furniture, made plywood walls, refinished a piano, made bookcases, tables, a radio case, and a lamp shade. Wanda Lee Peacock of Price received a United States Savings Bond from the Kellogg Company, Battle Creek, Michigan, as a health-improvement award. Helen Werner Slocum bean a business with $2.50, which now grosses $250,000 a year. With her only resources a secondhand trailer, she began hauling boats in it from one state to another, near and far. Now she has many employees and handles a fair share of the nearly 5,000,000 small boats in this country, when they require dry-land transportation.
