From Near and Sar It is always a stimulation and a special treat to find a travel article by Willard Luce. To my way of thinking he is onc of the ablest travel writers in the West today. After reading one of his articles, I am always inspired to see the place he talks about and have frequently done just that. These articles add color and variety to a wonderful publication, a very complete publication. —Manjane Morris Corinne, Utah It truly was a thrill for me to sce the picture of my beloved mother, Martha H. Porter, in the March issue of The Relief Society Magazine. She was_pictured with other dear sisters who have been Relief Society presidents in my home town of Orderville, Utah. Along with years of service in the Church and community, mother has given birth to thirteen children (I am number 12), and I would like to take this opportunity to extend my thankfulness and appreciation to the Relief Society, which has certainly been an influence for good in our family life. —Laura Porter Brew Sacramento, California I enjoy the poetry printed in our Magazine very much, and it is my hope that eventually I'll be able to get my thoughts across in the wonderful way that Eva Willes Wangsgard does. Such a_ truly beautiful mind! —Charlene Scott Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania I have had the Magazine in my home for many years, way back in the beginning when it was called The Woman's Exponent. Due to my ninety-six years of age, I cannot be active, so the coming of the Magazine each month is like a visit from dear friends, and it keeps me up on the work and happenings of the great Relief Society. I enjoy the splendid articles, stories, lessons, and poetry. —Dessie N. Middleton Los Angeles, California We think that The Relief Society Magazine is one that has no comparison for its size. We look forward to it each month and read it from cover to cover.
