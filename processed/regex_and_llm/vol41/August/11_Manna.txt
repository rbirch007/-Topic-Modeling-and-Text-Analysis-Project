Manna Mabel Jones Gabbott And when the dew that glistened all around Had lifted from the wilderness of Sin, The manna lay like hoar-frost on the ground, White as snow, where snow had never been. They gathered it at morning, fresh as cress; It was their strength, their faith, their bread to eat; Each took his daily need, no more, no less; ' Like wafers made with honey, it was sweet. _So is your love, my dear, a gift from heaven, My staff of life, my sustenance, my trust; As fragile as the dawn, love is the leaven Of each day, and I like Moses' children must Refresh it often—gladly give, glad-given, Lest hoarded love, like manna, turn to dust,
