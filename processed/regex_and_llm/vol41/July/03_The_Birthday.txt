In spite of the natural fatigue from the long hours of walking over the dusty, heat-shimmering plain, her toes tingled with the urge to dance, and she hummed softly while she worked. She gathered the younger children into a circle for family prayer, and then helped Lizzie serve up bowls of hot corn-meal mush and sizzling slices of salt pork. After they had eaten, she started the nightly ritual of scrubbing the young ones clean and tucking them in bed, while Lizzie washed the dishes and cared for Mother and the new baby in the wagon. All the while a part of her consciousness was focused on her father, trying to impress him with the competence and mature ability of an added year. She crawled out from under the wagon with a final warning to the boys, and stood by the inside front wheel. They reached the creek, and, tucking up her skirts, Emily waded out to where the stream was swift and clear, before filling her bucket. The water and sand were soothing and cool to her feet. She turned back and sat on the bank and let the water lave them with rippling gentleness.. The bushes behind her rustled softly in the night breeze, and here and there she could hear the soft call of the night birds. Lizzie, taking advantage of the darkness, had unfastened her bodice and begun to scrub herself industriously. Emily's tears welled up afresh, as she caught the faint gleam of her sister's white skin in the darkness and thought of Lizzie dancing gaily, while she waited alone in her bed under the wagon. She felt unloved and incredibly lonely. Suddenly their father was beside them. The pressure of his fingers on Emily's shoulder was at once a warning and a command. She arose quickly and walked with him, noting that his other hand was guiding Lizzie. They walked swiftly and without a sound until they reached the circle of wagons. Safely inside, Emily slowly released her pent-up breath and her troubled eyes searched her father's face. Except for a small tightness around the lips, she could see no difference.
