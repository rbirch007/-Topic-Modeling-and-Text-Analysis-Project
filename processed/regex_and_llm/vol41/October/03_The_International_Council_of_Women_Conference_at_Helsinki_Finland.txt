The International Council of Women Conference at Helsinki, Finland, June 1954 President Belle S. Spafford T is with pleasure that I bring to Relief Society members a report of the recent International Council of Women Conference with a brief mention of my other very delightful experiences in Europe. Finland, far to the north, with its limitless forests, blue inland lakes, long, bright summer nights, and modest, hospitable people, is an interesting land. Helsinki, Finland's beautiful capital city, with a population of 400,000 people, has preserved much of its old character, yet amazes one with much that is new and modern.
