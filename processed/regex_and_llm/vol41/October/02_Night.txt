The adage referred to material things. Frugality and thrift have been a part of Latter-day Saint ways. Economy is considered a virtue. And so it is, if it pertains to things of the world and not of the spirit. It may be necessary to make a dress from less material than would be desirable. It may be necessary to use top milk when cream would be better. But it is never necessary to skimp on things of the spirit. We only have to ask, and it Shall be given to us, we can ask for the Lord's Spirit and expect it. Peace and joyousness can be ours. Prayer, scripture reading, partaking of the sacrament, keeping good company, and Church activity, all help to insure an abun- 'dance of the Holy Spirit. Let's not skimp on spirituality.
