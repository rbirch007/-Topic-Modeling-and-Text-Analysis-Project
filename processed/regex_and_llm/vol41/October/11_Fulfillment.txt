Wilson, as of September 1, 1954, due to the appointment of her husband, David J. Wilson, as a judge of the Federal Customs Court in New York City. Sister Wilson was appointed to the board in January 1947, and has served for almost eight years. During those years she has won the love of the sisters of Relief Society who have been privileged to 1 irtake of her sweet spirit of humility and love for her fellow man. No little attention which she could give to an individual secking understanding has been withheld because of personal duties or her diversity of interests. _As a member of the general board, Sister Wilson has served with zeal and faithfulness in all her many and varied committee assignments. She is a granddaughter of Zina D. H. Young, who was general president of Relief Society from 1888 to 1901. Attributes which characterized the noble life of Zina Young are also found in Sister Wilson and appreciated by her fellow board members. The general board is happy in the honor that has come to Sister Wilson's husband, and extends to Sister Wilson its love and best wishes in the new responsibility which will be hers. Woteds.
