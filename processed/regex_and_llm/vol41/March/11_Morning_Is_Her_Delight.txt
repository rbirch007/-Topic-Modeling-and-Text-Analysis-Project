Morning is her delight—she wakens smiling, Barefoot, she dances over the dawn-cool floor; She runs from one room to another, eager, calling In formless words, finding new day everywhere. Still in her small white gown, she slips out softly; Sky is no brighter than her sky-bright eyes. Only the breeze can touch a leaf more deftly; Sparrows turn toward her laugh, lawn tickles her toes. Captured at last into shoes and a dress for playing, Given the usual toys (soon scattered and spilled) She watches for sun through windows with curtains blowing.... Morning is her delight, she is morning's child. 202 RELIEF SOCIETY MAGAZINE—MARCH 1954 i.
