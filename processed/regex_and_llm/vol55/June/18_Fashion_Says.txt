Maxine Grimm
Fashion says knees but we extend pleas To cover the knees, please!

As I started to write this article, I could not help but remember the complaints of Brigham Young concerning the length of women's dresses. He was lamenting the fact that they were so long that they gathered up the dirt from the streets. He suggested that they might be shorter—"... Do not be extravagant and cut them so short that we can see the tops of your stockings—[surely a prophet was speaking! ]—but bring them down to the tops of your shoes and have them so you can walk and clear the dust and do not expose your persons." (Journal of Discourses, Brigham Young, 12:299.)

Here I am, a century later, trying to design a dress to cover the knees when you sit down. At least in one respect Brigham Young would condone my efforts. This dress is handloomed, and he always stressed weaving and sewing and creative work. "Let the beauty of your adorning be the work of your hands." (Lbid., 19:75.)
