What rapture fills my heart
When strains of half-forgotten melody
Take me back across the years
To where its start awakened,
In my untried youth, the luring hold of beauty,
And captured, spellbound, my mind to soothe
In dreams of ecstasy and charm.

Now youth has left the shore
Of carefree, reckless phase.
The tune is sweeter than before,
For memory wraps the notes
In softer tones that reach to penetrate
With stronger thrust, and thus remain
Immortal, beloved, never ceasing to inflate
My soul with quiet peace.

Margaret Read Norwich, Norfolk, England
