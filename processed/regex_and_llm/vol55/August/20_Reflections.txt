REFLECTIONS) Did I, in my childhood, miss the music of the years Swiftly melted like waves upon a rock, Did time so quickly, then, conceal the tears, Of sorrows counted easily as minutes of a clock, Whose face wears the same impassive mood? Where, then, were the words flowing in golden rivers, 610 Ne Norwich, Norfolk, England Through my mind, now, to torment my pen that idle stood For want of waking? Cold grows my hand as thought quivers Like a thousand beams of light into a darkened room— Words!
