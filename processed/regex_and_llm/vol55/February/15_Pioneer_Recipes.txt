Are you becoming tired of the same old treats for parties or family nights? Try these old, old recipes for something new and delicious.

Pioneer Wheat

Place a cup of clean, dry wheat in a pre-heated (medium heat) heavy pan, containing just enough vegetable oil to cover the bottom. Stir about 5-10 minutes, until kernels are popped (they will pop without stirring, but scorch easily and taste burnt). Sprinkle with popcorn salt, stir, and spread out on a paper towel to cool. Keep small children's hands away until it does cool, as it can blister an overeager hand.

Golden Parched Corn

Dried kernels of sweet corn can be cooked the same way. They may take a trifle longer to pop, however.

Note: For those interested in trying these recipes, wheat and corn can usually be purchased from farmers, food stores, flour and feed mills, or health food stores.
