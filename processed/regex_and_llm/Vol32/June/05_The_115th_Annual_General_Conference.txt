The 115th Annual General Conference Elder Joseph J. Cannon First Assistant General Superintendent, Young Men's Mutual Improvement Association THE one hundred fifteenth anniversary of the Church was celebrated with the smallest conference but most potent Priesthood representation since the saints came to the Valley nearly a century ago. If President Heber J. Grant, sitting in his nearby home with a special wire carrying the proceedings to him, could be reckoned as being there, and every man in the conference felt very close to him, then the general authorities could be counted as being all present. The weather was favorable. Spring gave three beautiful days and relapsed into winter only after the conference was over. This gathering represented the largest membership the Church has had. Nearly a million persons (954,004) are on its books. The rapid growth is understood when we take into account the conversions (5,493) and the astonishing ratio of births to deaths (5.65 to 1). During 1944, the Church distributed in missionary work, allowances to stakes and wards, welfare, education, maintenance and renovation of buildings, genealogical and other work ($8,270,653).
