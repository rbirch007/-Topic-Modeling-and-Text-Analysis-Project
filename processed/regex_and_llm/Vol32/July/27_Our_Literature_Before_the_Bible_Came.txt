Our Literature Before the Bible Came A study reaching back into the legendary past?when our days were named ? and songs and sagas in vigorous, alternative lines were created. Typical selections from this folklore will be used in portraying the background of primitive thought, feeling, and ideals which were later to be illumined, shaped, and spiritualized through the influence of the Bible. Lesson 2. How the Bible Is Woven Into Our Literature A study of both the outward and inward phases of the shaping of our literature through the influence of the Bible. Such more tangible aspects, as Biblical allusions and expressions, as well as those more spiritual in nature. Biblical themes and ideals, will receive attention in this lesson. Lesson 3. Bible Influence as Revealed in Our Lyric Literature A study of typical songs inspired by stories and living truths of the Bible. Selections to impress the depth and breadth of its influence in the creation of our lyric literature will be made from the classic carols on through the years down to the present. Some precious lyrics will be included in the lesson for special consideration. Lesson 4. Bible Influence on Our Epic or Narrative Poetry This study will center attention on certain outstanding poet story-tellers who were markedly influenced by the Bible in their creative work. Chaucer, Milton, Tennyson, Longfellow are among these. A limited selection of their well-known story poems will be used to make the message of this lesson clear and concrete. Lesson 5. Bible influence in Our Drama This study, beginning with brief attention to the old plays created to impress Bible stories, will specifically deal with other dramas through the years centering around Biblical characters and themes. Attention will also be given to the influence of the Bible in shaping and spiritualizing dramas that grip and hold the hearts of humanity. Lesson 6. Bible Influence on Our Fiction This study naturally, like those preceding, must be severely limited to consideration of a few typical and worthy novels whose themes or central characters are essentially Biblical. Its range will be from tested earlier fiction to present creations which reveal classic qualities. Brief attention will be given to the more general spiritual influence on other worthy fiction. Lesson 7. Bible Influence on Essays and Oratory Again the scope of the lesson will be restricted to the study of a few inspiring types of the essay and to certain outstanding orations that show plainly Bible influence. These will be taken from past and recent literature of these two important kinds of expression. Reliance of great writers and speakers on the Book of Books will be made clear. Lesson 8. Bible Influence in Literature for Children A forward-pointing study to help parents get closer to the book companions of their children and grandchildren. Children's literature, except for Mother Goose and Fairy Tales, is mainly of more recent creation. What of the Bible influence is revealed in most of the books produced for children today? In this lesson, directed opportunity will be given to make some selective research to answer this question, and out of it to bring to our children more well-written, Bible-inspired literature of living appeal for them. Text and References First, the lessons themselves are to be the guiding text. Each of these units should be carefully studied by teacher and members of class. Questions for study should be thoroughly considered before the lesson is given; and the suggested activities carried through, both before and after the lesson. Second, for general references the following will be found most helpful: The Bible?King James Version (also Modern Reader's Bible ?"by Moulton). General reference books may be added later. Note: Specific references will be given with each lesson.
