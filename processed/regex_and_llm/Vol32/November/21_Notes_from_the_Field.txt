SPECIAL PROGRAMS (Continued) Tooele Second Ward, Tooele Stake (Utah) "UNDER THE DIRECTION of the Stake Relief Society Board, the Tooele Second Ward sponsored a work meeting, Tuesday, September 12, 1944, at 10 a.m. All wards of the stake were invited to participate and a good representation from eight wards attended. "A very fine display of handwork was shown by each ward, along with the welfare assignment for the stake, which is entirely complete. A luncheon was served to ninety-four guests at noon, and at one o'clock a meeting was held, where the changes in the program of the work meeting for the coming winter were explained by stake board members. *'Several demonstrations were given in rug making, bias cutting, hose repairing, quilting, and handwork. We feel that an enjoyable and a profitable time was had by all who attended." Photograph submitted by Stake President Emma Hanks, Tooele, Utah Page  SPECIAL FEATURE of the Mothers' and Daughters' Day of the Tooele Second Ward Relief Society was the special honor given to twenty-six members who had served in the capacity of an officer, teacher, or visiting teacher for twenty-five years or more. The total number of years amounted to 905, or very close to an average of thirty-five years each. "A tribute to the courage, steadfastness, and faithfulness of these sisters was given by Mrs. Wilhelmina Hess. Each lady was presented with a corsage. Stake officers were in attendance. 'The daughters officiating at the meeting were: Rose Marie Mascon, president; Lorraine Bell and Zella West, counselors; Irene Sainsbury, secretary; Ruth Bird, organist; Bonnie Christiansen, chorister; Mildred Mercer, class leader. A musical program was given, and, in conclusion, refreshments were served and an hour was spent socially. Beardall, Kolob Stake Relief Society President Fifth Ward. Kolob Stake (Utah) ZELMA P. BEARDALL, President of Kolob Stake Relief Society, reports a very effective presentation of 'The Gate Beautiful' a pageant by Mae Fridal and Maud O. Cook of Tremonton, Utah, which was published in The. "It was very beautifully and inspirationally presented March 4, 1945, by the Fifth Ward Relief Society of Kolob Stake in Springville, Utah, under the direction of President Alice Davis, Counselors Barbara Francis and Elen Erdmann, and Secretary-Treasurer Helen Robbins, with lighting effects by Ned Childs. The characters were chosen from every organization in the ward "Appropriate music was rendered by: Russel Palfreyman, violin solo; John Hardy and Floss Phillips, vocal duet; Kay Weight, tenor solo; Melba Wheeler and Evelyn Boyer, vocal duet; Ilean Bird, violin solo; Edgel Leigh ty, trumpet call. The accompanist was Wilda Oakley, with Carol Rawl assisting. "The ward was organized in January 1944, and was made up from part of the First Ward and part of the Fourth Ward. Since that time the Relief Society has earned through various projects, and presented to the building committee $800.00 towards the new meeting house which is to be erected on a site already purchased, as soon as priorities and materials permit."
