Woman's Sphere Ramona W. Cannon A host of relatives and friends mourn the passing of Flora Ridge Hicks, who died August 24, 1945, in Scottsdale, Arizona. Of English birth, Flora came to America in her late teens already a prominent singer. Ten years ago Sister Hicks accompanied her husband to Scottsdale, where he presided over the Indian (Papago) Ward. Flora taught the Indians hygiene, nutrition, cooking, nursing. A soloist, accompanist, composer, and director, she organized quartettes, choruses, and a ward choir. Her service to the Church was outstanding and ardent. PROFESSOR EDWARD E. THORNDIKE, psychologist of Columbia University, after extensive studies in comparative ratings of states along many lines, says that Utah and Massachusetts take the lead in producing the most smart men and women per million population. Many Utah women help to bear out the above statement. MRS.
