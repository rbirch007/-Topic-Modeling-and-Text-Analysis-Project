MOUNTAIN SONG Ivy Houtz Woolley My heart is singing wild, a mountain song; The mountain tops are singing back to me! The gay-toned melodies now wing along, And murmur, as they pass, that I am free. Yes, free to dance upon the world's wide rim. And free to soar into the changing skies. My feet keep time to this tumultuous hymn, My blood through every secret channel flies, My throat bursts forth in a melodious strain. The crags ring back an echo, and I hear A flood of sound, swept in like breeze-tossed rain; A thousand symphonies play in my ear. Sing! Mountain, sing! Nor miss a single beat?
