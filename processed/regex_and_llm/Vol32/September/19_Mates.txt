MATES Edward R. Tuttle The fabric of their love was never one Of fragile texture, made with silken twine And weft of dreams that later might be done, Somehow, into a workable design; But, was the homespun of a pent-up street Where pain-worn dwellers scarcely knew the soil. Expectancy had seldom turned their feet From an accustomed rendezvous with toil. They had preferred to let a slower growth Mature the rugged fibers in their care, Until a loom of years could furnish both With proper raiment for enduring wear. The mute concessions of this man and wife Had given each the condiments of life.
