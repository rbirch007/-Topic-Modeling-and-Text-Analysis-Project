TIBBS, Utah poet, who was ninety years old, October 26, and her sister, Miss Nellie Morris, who was eighty-three in March, were honored at an open house Sunday, October 28, at the home of Mrs. Tibbs' daughter, Mrs. Josephine T. Saville. Nearly three hundred guests called to greet the sisters. THE August number of Theillustrates the headdresses of Navajo girls. Sister Maggie Nielsen of Bluff, Utah, recently made a wedding dress for a Navajo bride, in which the skirt had twenty-two gores, each approximately one yard wide at the bottom. AT atea on November 5, honoring Utah's 8,000 Minute Women, Governor Herbert B. Maw presented each guest with a certificate of merit bearing the State seal. Especially honored were Mrs. John T. Wahlquist, State Chairman, and Mrs. L. A. Stevenson, Salt Lake City Chairman, unusually intelligent and dynamic organizers and leaders. Working on thirty-two salvage and conservation programs, the women have salvaged for example, six and one-half million pounds of tin, seven million pounds of waste paper, and forty-six and one-half million pounds of rags and old clothing. Miss Helen Blakenhorn of the national War Production Board said: Utah has the very best woman's organization for salvage in the United States. Mr. E. C. Seawell, national chairman of fat salvage said, "Utah has done the best salvage job in the United States. There is alive today a vivid imprint of the pioneer. Utah has retained the realities. She has combined qualities visible and invisible to set an example." VARIOUS professions in which Utah women have excelled are: Law?Judge Florence E. Allen, also poet, musician, and instructor in Greek, who has served on the Ohio Supreme Court Bench, and on the Circuit Court for Ohio, Michigan, Kentucky and Tennessee. Engineering: Young Inez Hansen was the third engineer hired at the Geneva Steel plant, and is now working on a great airport being built in San Francisco. Dentistry: Dr. Rose By water Valentine was Utah's first woman dentist, practicing forty-five years ago. She is now president of the Women Dentists Association of the Pacific Coast. Anthropology: Mrs. Huberta Berg Robison, a young Latter-day Saint girl, formerly of California, now of Utah, is employed by the Carnegie Institute, studying Mayan characters and culture, and making expert graphs.
