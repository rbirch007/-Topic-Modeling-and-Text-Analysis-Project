GOOD WILL TOWARD MEN-1945 Eva Willes Wangsgaard I Now Christmas lights may burn without restraint, For only friendly wings disturb the skies; And quiet snow clouds under wind's complaint Explode in crystal flak before our eyes. The moonlight silvering dark avenues Will bring no fears to make night overlong; Rime-jeweled firs will flash prismatic hues And turn our hearts to joy, our tongues to song.
