Homemaking Skills Developed Through the Work-Meeting Program Velma N. Simonsen Member Relief Society General Board WHEN the Prophet Joseph Smith organized the Relief Society, he stated that the purpose of the organization was "to care for the needy, and to save souls." Through the wisdom of our leaders, a day has been provided whereby both of these aims may be served. This is the work meeting. This day is comparable in its cultural value to other prescribed courses, and may require more thorough preparation than any of the other courses to make it one of the best departments in the Society. It seems that there are two real objectives for each work meeting-first, to plan a very vital program, and second, to get the women to participate in that program. Sisters, the day is yours. Plan it thoroughly and wisely, and don't be guilty of planning something that will just "take up the time." The greatest strength of the Relief Society lies in the fact that direction and guidance are given it by the priesthood of God.
