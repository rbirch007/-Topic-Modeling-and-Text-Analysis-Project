In Simes of War Christie Lund Coles What Shall the Poet Sing What shall the Poet Say in these dark days ith war's fierce shuttle straining at the loom Of life and hope, entangling the old ways Our feet have gone as in a well-known toom; What shall the Poet say, how can he sing With beauty like a torn and broken lute, here can he find tranquillity to bring Heart to the muse that lies disparaged, mute?
