The Modern Family and Spirituality Achsa E. Paxman Member, Relief Society General Board OME is the first and most important school in life, and religion should be the foundation of its education. Everything we want our Church and community to be, we must begin to teach in the home, our first aim being manhood and womanhood.
The most powerful and sustaining force in helping us to meet the realities of life is religion. It helps us to face danger, disappointments, and sorrow. Religious faith helps us to put our trust in the Lord and must be acquired early in life and exercised throughout life. It is a great influence and blessing in every Latter-day Saint home.
A mother is responsible for the atmosphere of the home. If our children are to do their part we must do ours. One of our leaders has said, "I am not afraid of the youth of this Church. What are you parents doing? Are you doing your part in every way?" Unless we are more capable wives and wiser mothers who teach by example the principles of honesty, dependability, and righteousness, we have fallen short of our goal in spite of all our accomplishments.
