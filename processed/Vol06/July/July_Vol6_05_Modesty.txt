Modesty
======================================================================

MODESTY. When every pool in Eden was a mirror That unto Eve her dainty charms proclaimed, She went undraped without a single fear or Thought that she had need to be ashamed. 'Twas only when she'd eaten of the apple That she became inclined to be a prude, And found that evermore she'd have to grapple With much debated problems of the nude. Thereafter she devoted her attention, Her time and all her money to her clothes; And that was the beginning of convention, And modesty, at least, so I suppose.
