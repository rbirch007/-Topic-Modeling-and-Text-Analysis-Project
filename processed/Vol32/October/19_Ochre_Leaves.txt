OCHRE LEAVES Ruby Baird Andersen In zeal, my neighbors heaped their leaves in mounds Then watched them go, in curling, smoky spires; And smugly thought: "How neat?my grounds." Not I, to burn such gold in sacrificial pyres. What though the flame of autumn soon expires, Remembered still are buds that April knows, And sound of wind on summer's leafy lyres. Deserving now, these leaves, of some repose, Intimate with earth and kindly fallen snows.
