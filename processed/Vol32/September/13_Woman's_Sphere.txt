Captain Hyde flies with her chief to hospitals and camps, where, through contact with the soldiers, problems that face their families are brought to light, recorded and, later, taken care of. MRS. DESLA BENNION of Spokane, Washington, formerly Edna Hull of Salt Lake City, has been elected to membership on the city school board. A Spokane newspaper points out that she was sponsored by the women of the University, of the Rotary Club, and of the Parent-Teachers Association; that she is the mother of five children, granddaughter of a Mormon woman who pushed a handcart all the way across the plains, and wife of a former Latter-day Saint mission president. In her campaign speeches Mrs. Bennion used her experience in recreational and other Church activities as a talking point. FILIPINO women were the first orientals to win suffrage. Mrs. Pilar H. Lim, a former president of the Philippine Federation of Women's Clubs, was largely responsible for this and many other achievements, such as higher literacy, lower mortality rate, diet clinics and factory nurseries for working mothers.
