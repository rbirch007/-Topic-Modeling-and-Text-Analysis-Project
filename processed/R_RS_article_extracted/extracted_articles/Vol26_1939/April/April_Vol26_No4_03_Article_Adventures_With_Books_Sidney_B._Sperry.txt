AS a member of the Library Committee at Brigham Young University I have numerous interesting adventures with books, sometimes on a large scale. Last spring I examined a catalog
226 - APRIL, RELIEF SOCIETY MAGAZINE
from a British book concern in London that had for sale several rare or valuable sets of books dealing with archaeology. One set, Archaeologia, published by the Society of Antiquaries of London, contained 107 volumes running from 1770 to 1930. Another set was the Proceedings of the Society of Biblical Archaeology and still others were the Proceedings of the Cambridge Antiquarian Society and The Survey of Western Palestine.
I realized that these books would be of immense value for research and reference purposes in the fields of Bible, Book of Mormon, English, archaeology, etc. We are always especially desirous of getting fundamental works in the field of religion because of the importance of the latter at Brigham Young University. Even a layman can see the value of having such fine sets at the Church university. The prices placed on these valuable works were so low as to surprise me. I went to President Franklin S. Harris and asked if it would be possible to send immediately for them. He was toiling over the University budget, and no funds could be spared at the time; but he promised that if possible he would send for them later. I thought to myself, "These books will be snapped up by some institution before our order ever gets there." However, late in the summer I was happily surprised by the arrival of all the sets in good condition. Our buy was a splendid one and will add materially to the religious sources available in the Heber J. Grant library.
