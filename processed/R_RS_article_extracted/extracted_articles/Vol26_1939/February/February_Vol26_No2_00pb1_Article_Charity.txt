ROADS
I have often wondered about roads... Traveled by myriad feet— Travelers who come and go, Some happily, some sadly, some with hope...
Some roads lead to high and wooded hills With purple shadows and sylvan dells, And waterfalls... Green glades and pines And crystal atmosphere and peace—
And others toward a desert where the sun Is ever ardent, where cacti is monotony; Where the wild things seem too lonely— Where life moves futilely. Others hopeless.
There are other roads, smooth roads which lead Through fertile plains, o'er daisied slopes Toward golden sunsets.
I have often wondered about roads And the mystery of their travelers...
Grace Zenor Pratt

The Relief Society Magazine Vol. XXVI FEBRUARY, 1939 No. 2
