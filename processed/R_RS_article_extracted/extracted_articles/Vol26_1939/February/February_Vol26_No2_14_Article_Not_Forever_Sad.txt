NOT FOREVER SAD The trees once green, then red, then brown, stand naked on the hill, The stream that whispered as it ran, is frozen at the rill, The field where waved the golden grain, is naught but stubble now, The gentle wind that sang in May, is but a dismal sough; The lad that gathered daisies in a meadow far away Is lonely, oh, so lonely! Now his golden hair is grey. But the trees are getting ready to bud again in spring, The stream below the ice has not forgotten how to sing, The field where waved the golden grain is resting for a day, And the wind that howls like a fiend will sing again next May; The lad that gathered daisies, in his soul is still a lad, There's nothing in this life can make a man forever sad. —Terrence Sylvester Glennamaddy.
