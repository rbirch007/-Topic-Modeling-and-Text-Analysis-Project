Amid the hardships attendant upon the building of homes and communication in a new world, there was gradually and painfully taking a form a new order of things. Not the least significant, as a forecaster of things that were to be, was the setting up in 1639 of the first printing press?a very primitive hand operated device?on this continent. And as we observe the season of Thanksgiving this year, it is stimulating to ponder the blessings that have accrued to all of us in this new and free land as a result of the setting up of that first printing press in New England, and the setting up, upon an ever-broadening horizon, of countless other presses, many times more efficient in operation, but not more unfettere3d in their right to print the thoughts of just men and to give wings to the aspirations of a free people.
