OF INTEREST
New Serial
DO you have a problem? Then you will want to read about Carolyn Evans, married to harsh, brilliant Turner Evans, whom she bores and who has long ago left her behind. Their unequal development, with its attendant conflicts and disagreements, is in a fair way to ruining the life of her son Robert, a tall, powerful lad, in love with cultured, refined June Strauch, but who will not tell her of his love because of his home condition. The home situation is affecting Carson, the second son, in quite another way. In his search for security he is setting his feet upon the wrong path. All these things Carolyn senses. Then, there is her own heart to consider and the love of another kind, good man, Kane Holland. Shall she try to build a life for herself? Shall she for her children's sake try to bridge the slough of fifteen years' mental stagnation? Is divorce the answer? Read how Carolyn worked it out and perhaps you will see something that will help you with your problems in the story, CATHEDRAL OF PEACE, the new serial by Dorothy Clapp Robinson, beginning in this issue of the Magazine. Mrs. Robinson needs no introduction to our readers. Her contributions to the Relief Society Magazine as well as other local publications have long since established her as a fiction writer of merit. Her appreciation of home life as interpreted by Latter-day Saints enables her to bring to us an important message in her story CATHEDRAL OF PEACE.
