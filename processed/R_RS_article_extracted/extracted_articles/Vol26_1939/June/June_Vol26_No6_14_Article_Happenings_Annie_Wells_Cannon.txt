HAPPENINGS By Annie Wells Cannon
MAY—the world brimming over with fragrance, beauty, and song is a testament of God's beneficence.
QUEEN ELIZABETH is her own stylist, and English women take notice. She shunned the extremely short skirts in her wardrobe for the American tour. Did American women take notice?
RUTH BRYAN OWEN RHODE is said to be as eloquent and magnetic as her distinguished father on the lecture platform. Blood will tell.
ELEANOR ROOSEVELT, versatile and accomplished, is a patron of art and never happier than when displaying some of her collections, among them her Goya etchings of which she is especially proud.
LAURA INGALLS, most daring aviatrix since the ill-fated Amelia Earhart, lost somewhere in the South Seas, has planned a solo distance flight record across the Pacific.
MADAME CHIANG KAI-SHEK, touring America in behalf of Chinese refugees, was severely injured in a plane crash. Regaining consciousness she said, "I would gladly die for the cause."
ANNA SCHETININA is the first of the Soviet seagoing women to achieve the rank of Captain, and there are 8,800 women sailors besides over 2,000 women running river boats. Besides the Russians, women in England and other lands are organizing to take over naval jobs in case of war.
MARJORY KINNAN RAWLINGS received the Pulitzer prize of $1,000 for the most distinguished novel of last year, "The Yearling," a pastoral story of a Florida boy.
IDA M. TARBELL has delighted her many admirers by printing "All In The Day's Work," an autobiography. It is the story of a great American who has fearlessly approached life and stood for liberal thought and action. "My Days of Strength" is another interesting biography by Anne Walton Fearn, an American woman doctor who spent forty years in China.
ELIZA A. MUNSON, 91, of Circleville died last month. She was a member of the Daughters of the Mormon Battalion and Daughters of Utah Pioneers and a devoted Latter-day Saint.
THRU L. PARTRIDGE of Utah has recently written a novel "Adventures With A Lamp," the story of a nurse. Elsie C. Carroll is a contributor to "Women Of The West" and Anna Prince Redd's Relief Society contest prize poem "No Beauty Is" appears in "North American Book of Verse," while other anthologies of 1939 are recognizing a number of Utah women poets, among them Nellie Parker, member of the Relief Society General Board, and Mary Leona Jolley, postmistress at Tropic.
