George H. Dern
COME to the State over which he had ably presided as Governor for two terms and from which he had gone to serve in the President's Cabinet was brought the body of George H. Dern to rest near the bodies of his beloved parents and infant daughter. The services in the historic Tabernacle were most impressive. Never before had there been gathered there so many leading men of the nation, the state, the city, and various churches. His honesty, integrity, fidelity and kindliness were praised. His was a democratic spirit. He could mingle with and enjoy the companionship of the great and the lowly. He has left a record of integrity and devotion to duty that is inspiring. While he was Governor, some of Utah's finest social legislation was enacted. As Secretary of War, he earnestly studied the problems that came to his attention and was sincere and honest in the discharge of his duties. May his loved ones be blessed with peace and his children emulate the noble life of their father.
