In the previous lesson of this series we learned that Christ's ministry was foretold by the prophets One might surmise, without any knowledge of scripture, the need of knowing the reasons for His advent. It would be useless to predict the coming of a Savior if the plan of salvation was withheld from men. The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints teaches the somewhat unique, but nevertheless true, doctrine that Christianity—the Gospel, if you please—was taught from the earliest times, thousands of years before the Redeemer came in the flesh. Hence, the title of this lesson: Christianity Before The Christ. Many persons not of our faith may exclaim, "If the Gospel, as understood by you, was in the world before the advent of Christ, why does not the Bible indicate it? We hasten to say that it does in part. Unfortunately, for reasons to be considered, the Bible does not portray as clearly as we should desire, the point in question. Ina prophecy of Nephi the following remark is made: "Because that ye have a Bible ye need not suppose that it contains all my words; neither need ye suppose that I have not caused more to be written." (II Nephi 29:10.) That statement is one key to the difficulty. Other revelations of the Lord, as: mplied above, are necessary to make up for the lack of clearness in the Bible record. An angel of the Lord on one occasion gave Nephi some information respecting a book to come in the latter days into the hands of the Gentiles. The book spoken of is our Bible. Nephi was informed that following the ministry of the Savior many parts of the Bible, originally plain and easy to understand, were taken therefrom. "For beheld, they have taken away from the gospel of the Lamb many parts which are plain and most precious; and also many covenants of the Lord have they taken away." (I Nephi 13: 26.) The implication is, of course, both the Old and New Testaments were similarly treated. The result, to use the words of the angel, was to "pervert the right ways of the Lord.. Blind the eyes and harden the hearts of the children of men." (Read carefully I Nephi 13:19-2°.) 2.
