Minerva Richards Knowlton
MINERVA RICHARDS KNOWLTON, mother of our Board Member, Marcia K. Howells, passed to the great beyond May 28, 1936. For years Sister Knowlton served efficiently as secretary, counselor, then as president of the Relief Societies of Davis County. Her work was most effective. Sister Wells said, "Sister Knowlton can always be depended upon to see the right way and do the right thing." All who knew her recognized how lovely was her personality, how unselfish, kind and self-sacrificing she was. She was a wonderful mother. Not only did she rear her own children but several of her husband's children whose mother had died and several grandchildren. Sister Knowlton knew the conditions of Pioneer life. She was always a hard worker. As a young girl she milked cows, churned butter, scrubbed floors, washed and ironed, carded wool for quilts, made rag carpets and in addition did quantities of fine art needle work. She loved life deeply and got the most enduring values out of it. She made many wise remarks. We quote three of them:
"I have found that not only children but even grown people feel more comfortable when they are well and appropriately dressed."
"When I was unhappy, I always tried to do something for someone else, the doing of which brought me happiness in return."
"To have a true friend one must be a true friend and the same is true regarding neighbors."
Sister Knowlton's last years were spent as a temple worker, where her gentle sweetness endeared her to all. In her death the grave has won no victory, her fine character, her excellent example, and her lovely personality, which have so enriched the lives of others, will not be forgotten; and her numerous good works will live as a monument to her memory and an inspiration to others. She has but passed from mortality to immortality where she will enjoy her reward and the fulfilment of her highest desires.
