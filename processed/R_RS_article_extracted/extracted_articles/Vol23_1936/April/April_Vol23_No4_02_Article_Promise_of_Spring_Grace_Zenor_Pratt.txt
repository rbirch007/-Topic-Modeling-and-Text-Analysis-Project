PROMISE OF SPRING By Grace Zenor Pratt I watch the distant cottonwoods turn silvery green? The wheat fields like an emerald carpet spread; The glorious promise of another spring When winter has betokened all things, dead. The hyacinths along the garden wall Thrust up their waxen blossoms from the earth? Blue, rose and lavender and purest white;. A daffodil springs golden, from the turf. A wild rose now appears on swaying stem, The sky is blue with fairy floating clouds; An orchard, fragrant in its rosy mist, A field with upturned sod, but newly plowed.. So many springs beheld with wondering eyes, So. Many miracles of sun and shower. With each new promise, we behold anew? And faith returns in God's creative power.
