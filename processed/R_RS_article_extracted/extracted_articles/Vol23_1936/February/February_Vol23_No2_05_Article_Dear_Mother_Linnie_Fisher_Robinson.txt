DEAR MOTHER By Linnie Fisher Robinson Dear Mother, here's a valentine So many years in making; It's tied and dyed in things I've learned~ And hallowed with hearts' waking. It's decked with buds, those other days You pointed to eternal flowers; And now, because of you, my ways Lead on through fragrant bowers. It wears a heart?-my own to you? You taught me how to give; There's so much joy in life, because, You taught me how to live. And last of all it holds a thought, "To one I love," in gravest manner, How many times the thought that you Love me, has been my banner.
