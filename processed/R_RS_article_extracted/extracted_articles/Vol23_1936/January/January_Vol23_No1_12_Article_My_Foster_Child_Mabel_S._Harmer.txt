Kkk MY FOSTER CHILD (Awarded Second Honorable Mention in the Eliza Roxey Snow Poetry Contest) By Mabel S. Harmer Oh, who shall say I do not love thee more Because thou art not of my flesh a part, Or that the tie which binds us is less strong Because thou wert not nurtured 'neath my heart? Gladly would I have borne the pangs of birth; Such are but of the body—'tis the soul That suffers with an unrewarded pain When robbed by bleak frustration of its goal. What though I yearned to give thy life to thee; Fulfillment need not be by this alone— Another bond as tender holds us close; My spirit claims thee for its very own!
