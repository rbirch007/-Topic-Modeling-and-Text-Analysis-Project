*>—_A10 G07 1>_—_<- OLD-FASHIONED THINGS By Viola Stone Card Afraid to be old-fashioned, Lest a modern world should sneer, Lest you lack sophistication, Which the gay crowd holds so dear? Know this: the oldest fashioned things, Whose lustre time cannot dim— Those priceless virtues, eternal Will be—and have always been! For what is older than courage— To row against the current While the crowd drifts with the stream? Or what is older than simple Truth, Or Faith, or Sincerity? Don't be afraid of ridicule, But dare your best self to be. For those who achieve true greatness— Whose lives the years applaud— Receive of the angels these old-fashioned gifts Which are as ancient as God.
