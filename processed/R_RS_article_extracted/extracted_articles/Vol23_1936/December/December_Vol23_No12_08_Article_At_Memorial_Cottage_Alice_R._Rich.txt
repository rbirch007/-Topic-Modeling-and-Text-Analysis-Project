AT MEMORIAL COTTAGE By Alice R. Rich
A sacred lovely calm Pervades the place, And memories, Sweet as the breath Of meadow flowers, Come to us Through the years.
'Twas here that Lucy, Favored Mother of our race, Welcomed to her waiting arms The infant seer.
The old hearth stone, 'Round which the young lad played, Rests now before The glowing fire.
With love of God Within our hearts, We stand before the humble shrine, And to our souls There comes A reverence mild, Akin to that Which came direct from God To Israel's liberator And Guide:
"Take off thy shoes, For sacred is the ground On which you stand."
