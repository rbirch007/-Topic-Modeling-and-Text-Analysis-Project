"The Soul's Fire" By Jeremiah Stokes
"THE SOUL'S FIRE" is a most dramatic portrayal of the drivings, and persecutions of those who first accepted the restored Gospel. Since the historical events are portrayed without exaggeration, it gives a vivid picture of what it cost to establish the great Latter-day work. The heroes and heroines stand out in their divine and unswerving faith, their wisdom, courage and undying devotions to the great cause. Driven from place to place, robbed of their possessions, they remained true to their convictions. Neither mob fury nor suffering nor death of loved ones turned them away. They knew the work was true, that Joseph Smith was a Prophet and the work he established was of God. A beautiful part of the book is the love story of Ann Northrop who depicts the character of Mrs. Roberts, an English convert, while the experiences of Garry portray the boyhood of Brigham H. Roberts. The book holds its readers' interest from beginning to end. He gets an unforgettable picture of the truly great men and women who not only established a great religious work but also pioneered the West and made its barrenness yield plenty. Price, $2.75, Deseret Book Company, 44 East So. Temple, Salt Lake City, Utah.
