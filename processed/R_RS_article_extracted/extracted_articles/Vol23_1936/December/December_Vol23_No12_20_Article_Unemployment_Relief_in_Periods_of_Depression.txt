"Unemployment Relief in Periods of Depression"
THE Russell Sage Foundation has just issued a volume valuable to all interested in social work?Unemployment Relief In Periods of Depression?"A study of measures adopted in certain American cities, 1857-1922," by Leah H. Feder. It is pointed out that "with each depression emergency measures are embarked upon?and the results generally forgotten. This study recovers and records significant experience in previous depressions for its bearing upon present and future policies." It has taken much time and careful research to obtain the information contained in the volume, for it was found stored away in "widely separated dust-covered files and out-of-print publications." The careful authentic work that is evidenced by each publication of the Russell Sage Foundation is again shown in this volume. It is a source book of information that should give social workers great help in meeting future depressions. Price, $2.50.
