TREASURE FROM THE EAST By Carlton Culmsee
Out of the East has rolled Wealth that is strange and fair? The sunset of jewels, the rainbow of silks, and rare Perfumes and fruits unknown to men of old.
But the fairest and strangest of all Was a book with a golden tale Of a Prince who wore no sword or coat of mail, Won no rich land, and seemed in shame to fall, But gave the world a breathing hope?and a Call!
This treasure from the East, This tale of stable-birth And the lad who rose from it to mold the earth, Is childhood's heritage and wonder-feast.
Let not a Christmas board Be lacking in this food, In the beauty and awe of the Manger and the Rood; Let every child of the nation's plastic horde Grow hale with faith in a glad, much-giving Lord.
