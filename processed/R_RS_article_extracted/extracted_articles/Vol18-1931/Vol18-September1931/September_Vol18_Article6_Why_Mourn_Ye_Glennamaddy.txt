Why Mourn Ye? By Terrence Sylvester Glennamaddy It shall not be an everlasting night; For, behold! The day cometh with the dawn; Then shall the righteous stand forth in His light; Then shall the redeemed be like the newborn. Oh death! Where is thy sting to reborn man? Oh, grave! Where is thy victory today? This body in corruption lives its span; But evil never can the spirit stay. When mortal dust shall have slept out its time, When this earth shall have run its course through space, Then shall our God's holy fire refine; And resurrected men stand face to face. Why mourn ye then, oh thou spirit of man? We live in hope and lay us down in trust. Our faith in Christ has broken every ban, A perfect one did rise from mortal dust.
