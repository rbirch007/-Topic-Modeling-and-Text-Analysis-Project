Mary By Helen M. Livingston
She did not mind the dusty road, The donkey's gait so slow, The chill wind of the winter night, The weary way to go. A holy peace filled Mary.
She did not mind the crowded town, No place for her to stay, She made a manger sanctified, Bore Jesus on the hay. And holy peace filled Mary.
She did not pry the future years And see his body rent, But pillowing the little head She knew a sweet content. And holy peace filled Mary.
