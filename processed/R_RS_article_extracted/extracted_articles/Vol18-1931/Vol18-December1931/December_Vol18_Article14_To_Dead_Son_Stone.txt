Christmas Greetings 1931
25th ANNUAL SEAL
To a Dead Son By Ivy W. Stone
At first we mourned and questioned why He could not live life's span; It seemed so wrong that he should die As he became a man.
With folded hands we nursed our grief And chafed against God's will; We gave no neighbor kind relief Nor solaced any ill.
But now we know the Lord has need For some of earth's fair flowers; They bloom between sear stalk and weed And that's why He chose ours.
Our boy is safe within God's sheaf, We do not mourn his fate; With kindly deeds we conquered grief And trust in God and WAIT!
