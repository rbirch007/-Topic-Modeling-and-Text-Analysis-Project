Maytime By Merling D. Clyde Noontime and sunshine, A breath of flowers on the breeze, A song of love and loveliness Murmuring among the trees; Gay little buds in springtime green Swinging to and fro, Awaiting the touch of the sun's caress Their full blown beauty to show. Down in the grass a shy white flower Sheds a fragrance in the air, Close where a baby's feet may pass And find it growing there. By the garden gate a winsome maid Waits for her lover so true, Hoping he'll come when no one's about And there'll be a kiss for two.
