A SHAWL FOR BABY Caroline Eyring Miner A handmade shawl of fluffy wool—no more You think—pale pink and white; the design Of stitches, little shells piled by the score Overlapping each other in even line. Around its edge a long and filmy fringe, Illusive as the uncertain clouds of dawn; A shimmering mist of silver with tinge Of pink—here now, another moment gone. Hour after hour of precious time I spent Making the shawl—voices of friends I hear; Faces and memories that came and went While I worked, appear and disappear. This covering of shells and mist and cloud And memories and dreams will warm my child. 

 —DECEMBER, 1941
