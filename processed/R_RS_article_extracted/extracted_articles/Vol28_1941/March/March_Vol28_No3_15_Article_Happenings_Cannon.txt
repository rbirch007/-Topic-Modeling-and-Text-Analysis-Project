CHILD WELFARE PROVISIONS OF THE SOCIAL SECURITY ACT 195 HAPPENINGS Annie Wells Cannon Today I saw the flash of a bluebird's wing, There was snow on the hills, Yet I knew it meant spring. THE Greek women, like the Amazons of ancient times, are defending their home land both on the battlefield and by keeping roads and streets clean in the cities. COUNTESS MORAG ZANORYSKA, a Polish refugee, is relating her tragic experiences during her flight after the fall of Warsaw, in American cities. ALICE MARBLE, with "no more worlds to conquer" as an amateur tennis player, is making victories in the professional field. MARY FIELD GARNER, 105 years old, of Utah, still interested in world affairs, makes sage comments on conditions. Another remarkable Utah centenarian is Emily Hodgetts Lowder, whose one hundredth anniversary was celebrated by all Iron County. ROSE McMULLIN, famed donor of blood, was awarded a medal by the Forty and Eight Society of the American Legion, with the words, "The bravest woman in America." Thirty out of forty-one sufferers helped, recovered. DOROTHY WRIGHT LEEBES, California artist, has turned from the paint brush to the loom and is now making a fortune weaving her dream pictures into rugs and tapestries. LADY RUSSELL, author of "Elizabeth and Her German Garden," "Enchanted April," and other novels, and, just recently, the charming story, "Mr. Skeffington," under the pen name, "Elizabeth," died last month. SIGRID UNSET, Norway's' famous novelist, now a refugee in the United States, has been informed that the Quisling Government has ordered all her books and manuscripts burned. ELLEN GLASGOW was recently awarded the William Dean Howells medal for fiction by the American Academy of Arts and Letters. Her latest novel, "In This Our Life," is now ready. Pearl Buck's "Today and Forever," and Josephine Lawrence's "No Stone Unturned," are also new novels of February, while Alice Duer Miller's beautiful story in verse, "The White Cliffs," is attracting much attention. DEANNA DURBIN, piquant movie star, donated money for six huge mobile canteen units, to provide first aid to civilian war sufferers. This was her Christmas gift to her English friends. MY JOHNSON MOLLISON, Britain's greatest aviatrix, was drowned in the Thames estuary while flying for the air transport auxiliary. EMMA BENNION LINDSAY, Rachel Baliff Campbell, Amy Adams Thomas, and Lydia Spencer Clawson are among the notable Utah pioneer mothers who died in the late winter months.
