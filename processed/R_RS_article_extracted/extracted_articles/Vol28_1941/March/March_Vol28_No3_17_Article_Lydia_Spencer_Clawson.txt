Lydia Spencer Clawson THE March, 1939, Relief Society Magazine contained an article entitled "Women We Should Know ? Lydia Spencer Clawson, Wife and Mother." This was the concluding article in a series which presented the lives of outstanding Latter-day Saint women in various fields of endeavor. From this article we quote: "Sister Clawson chose the best of all careers?that of a true woman, wife and mother, whose price is still 'above rubies.' " The Relief Society General Board extends its sincere sympathy to President Rudger Clawson and the members of his family in the passing of their beloved and devoted wife and mother, Saturday, February 1, 1941. Sister Clawson's life has been rich in experience and fruitful from every point of view. She has met trial and mastered it. She has known good times and met them with zest and delight. She has been blessed with beauty, quick wit, and a pleasing, kind disposition which has won her innumerable friends. She has also been blessed with nine sons and daughters, five of whom survive her. Her devotion to the Church has been evidenced in the many sacrifices she has made for it and in the spirit with which she has accepted the many calls it has made upon her husband. She has been ready at all times to relieve him of responsibilities and make the way clear for him to do the work which has been his as an apostle in the Church which they both loved. There is no doubt that her life of sacrifice, service, and devotion will be amply rewarded in the Kingdom of our Father. We sincerely pray that His peace, comfort and blessings will attend President Clawson and his family in their hour of sorrow.
