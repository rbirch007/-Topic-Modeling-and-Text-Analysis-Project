PARTY DRESS Lucille Waters Mattson
There now, house, You're all dressed up for company. But remember, house, And wear your dress nonchalantly.
Be careful not to show The mending in the tapestry Or let them know Your silent dignity Is all veneer.
Don't for a moment, let them guess The sighs and tears, The agony and happiness You've held inside.
And when the party's over, then We'll laugh And be ourselves again.
