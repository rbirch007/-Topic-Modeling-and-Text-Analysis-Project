She should study the homes in her district and should not neglect to visit the homes of new neighbors. The world needs friendliness, and she has the opportunity to establish many fine friendships. Many women find their lives far from satisfactory. They need courage to meet the problems of every day—to meet life's tests. Perhaps something has happened to take the sweetness and joy out of their lives. Visiting teachers can, by their friendly visits and by encouraging activity in Relief Society work, help them to find their way back to happiness. The Magazine left in the homes of such women can be a real missionary. Visiting teachers must be messengers of peace. Many hearts and homes in our Church are saddened because of the conflict in Europe. Many have dear ones over there. Let us be understanding and sympathetic as we go into the homes of people of different nationalities. Let us never forget that no matter what country was the homeland, all Latter-day Saints are entitled to the same courtesies and the same Christ-like service. We must have faith in ourselves and faith in each other, faith in our leaders in the Church and Government, and, most of all, faith in our Heavenly Father. The Lord has been kind to us. Let us show our gratitude by the joy we take in our work, whatever it -may be, and the happiness we can bring to others. PRAYER'S SOLACE Jasmine Romney Edmunds It is the hour of my evening prayer, Alone and bowed I kneel within my room. Deep is the wish and great the gift I dare To ask of Him this day of deep'ning gloom. Released, the mighty yearning of my soul Pours upward from the pain within my heart; With rushing words it trembles towards its goal, Beseeching Him to hear and help impart: "Dear Lord, I feel the strength of Thy great pow'r, I trust in Thy deep love and mighty skill; Oh, hear my aching plea in this sad hour, And grant it—if it be Thy will!" Words cease.
