REMEMBERING LINCOLN Vesta P. Crawford
There is no marble edifice can reach The measure of this man who stood apart, No carven words of praise can ever teach The grandeur and the kindness of his heart; No troubled years are long enough to dim A deeper tracery than words in stone. There is no monument too high for him To whom the humble paths of earth were known.
For Lincoln was a simple man and plain Who loved his neighbors and the prairie space And walked at evening in the weedy lane With transient wind and shadows on his face; Perhaps the quiet peace within his eyes Was mirrored from the dusk of country skies.
