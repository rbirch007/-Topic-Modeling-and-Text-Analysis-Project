LOVE'S MIRACLE  May Booth Talmage Each year in cherry-blossom time, The flood-tides of my soul o'erflow, When from my upper casement I behold The miracle of glory wrought below. No bare brown, lifeless-looking limbs— Unsightly—cheerless now before me rise, But massive perfume-laden clouds Of snowy loveliness bring glad surprise. Are human lives like trees to outward view, Now cold and bare, congealed by winter's gloom? Anon love's warmth and sunlight filter through And lo! Life's greatest miracle—a soul in bloom!
