"The Mitten" (in Monumental Valley) Anna Prince Redd
Eternity is here. In this dim region, light When once invoked began to burn. Eons ensued. Age after age in anonymous turn, Dawn, noon, and breath-held night Looked down upon the solitude. Nothing here save water to invade The spacelessness. No motion but in waves blown High, rent, Curled backward to meet new waves grown Till land set up her monument. Ere "THE MITTEN"—Man now calls it— In this far, strange world knew season, A tree it knew, A cedar tree that girthed beyond all reach or reason Until by its own force it split: Yet still the jagged outline grew. Blistered. Splintered, limbs gone bare, It burned its heart to ring-flint, New each year. Beside the monument. Together they imprint God's time. Eternity is here.
