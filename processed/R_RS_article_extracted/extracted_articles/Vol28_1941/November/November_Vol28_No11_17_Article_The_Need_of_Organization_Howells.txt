The Need Of Organization Counselor Marcia K. Howells AS our Relief Society century draws to its close, we marvel at what has been accomplished. There is power and strength in organization. We were organized by a prophet of God. A later prophet, President Joseph F. Smith, said of Relief Society: "I expect to see the day when

 RELIEF SOCIETY CONFERENCE this organization will be one of the most perfect, most efficient and effective organizations for good in the Church. It stands for the people of God, for nghteousness, for justice, for good and more good all the time." Our women, guided by the Priesthood, have tried to meet needs of all kinds. With an earnest desire and an adequate organization the work of the Society has progressed. Relief Society women have gathered and stored grain, made clothing for the Indians, made, laundered and mended clothing for temple workers, grown mulberry trees and helped with silk culture, and promoted home industry. It was about 65 years ago that President Brigham Young made a special call on the Relief Society to help establish home industries. Sister Eliza R. Snow eagerly supported the making of straw hats at home. These were to cost from 50c for men's hats suitable to wear in the field, to $1.75 for hats suitable for fine wear. A letter from a Relief Society in Cache Valley stated, "We are doing all in our power to sustain home manufacture. We have sent two ladies to Brigham City to learn braiding, millinery, et cetera, and we hope with our own exertions and the means we have on hand, to build a house for the Society and to establish a school for young ladies to learn to make hats, bonnets and many of the articles we are now importing.
