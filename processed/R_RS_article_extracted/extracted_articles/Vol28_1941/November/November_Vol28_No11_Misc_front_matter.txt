1 Relief Society Conference October, 1941 Vera White Pohlman, General Secretary-Treasurer "Let us not be weary in well doing: for in due season we shall reap, if we faint not."—Galatians 6:9. This was the theme of the semiannual general conference of Relief Society which was held in Salt Lake City, Wednesday and Thursday, October 1 and 2, 1941. Counselor Donna D. Sorensen, member of the General Presidency in charge of educational work for the Society, was chairman of the October conference committee, and was assisted by eleven other members of the General Board who served on this committee. This conference featured especially the educational work of the Relief Society, but also gave consideration to plans for the observance of the Relief Society centennial in 1942, and lent impetus to the closing months of the Relief Society's fouryear membership campaign. The Relief Society greatly appreciated the added strength given its membership campaign by the General Authorities who, at two sessions of the general conference, urged the women of the Church to affiliate with the Society, and suggested that stake presidents and bishops cooperate with their respective Relief Society officers in the furtherance of this campaign. Schedule of Meetings On the first day of the conference, two educational department meetings were held simultaneously in the forenoon and also in the afternoon, and in addition, a music department was also held in the afternoon for stake choristers and organists. Both the forenoon and afternoon department sessions were preceded by a short officers' meeting for all stake and mission officers and board members. Counselor Donna D. Sorensen, chairman of the conference, conducted both of these general officers' meetings. The department sessions were conducted by their respective chairmen, as indicated in the ac- © companying schedule, each of whom gave a preview of the coming season's work.
