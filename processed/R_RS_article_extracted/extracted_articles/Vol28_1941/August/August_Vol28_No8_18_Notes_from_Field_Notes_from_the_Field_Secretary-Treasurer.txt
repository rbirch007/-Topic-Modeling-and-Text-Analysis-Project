Nevada Stake (Ely, Nevada) HOPE BROADBENT, first counselor and stake centennial chairman, has submitted the following details of an activity planned to serve both as a memorial to the recently retired stake Relief Society _ president, Anna M. Aljets, and as a nucleus for future stake and ward anniversary programs on March 17, and for the coming centennial observances in 1942. 'The plan for this activity developed from the general desire of the wards to join in presenting a token of appreciation to Mrs. Aljets, from her interest in the preparation and preservation of the Relief Society histories of the wards, and from the desire of the stake board to have recorded the impressive and beautiful faith-promoting stories of Relief Society women. The plan developed by the stake board was for the wards to prepare two loose-leaf scrapbooks—one for presentation to Mrs. Aljets and one to be retained by the stake board, and added to periodically. In arranging for each ward to prepare its individual pages for these two scrapbooks, the stake board also suggested that each ward prepare and continue to preserve its own ward Relief Society history. The stake board provided each of the wards with pages 11% inches by 151% inches in size for use in preparing their individual material. The tentative plan of contents outlined by the stake board for each ward was as follows: (1) Organization—date of organization and reorganizations, pictures and names of officers, biographical histories, achievements and personal stories: (2) History—relating to the ward Relief Society both before and NOTES FROM THE FIELD after inclusion in the Nevada Stake, (3) Activity — such as successful parties, Magazine and membership activities, bazaars, dinners, beautification and other special activities sponsored by the ward Relief Society, to be accompanied by drawings and pictures; (4) Welfare work—both Relief Society activities, as such, and assistance by the Society in the interest of the Church welfare program; (5) Reminiscence—including references to visits, experiences, and associations with ward and stake Relief Society workers; (6) Faith-promoting stories —collected especially from the older members of the Society and signed personally by them wherever possible. The wards adopted the plan wholeheartedly, using their own ingenuity in collecting and arranging the material, with the result that each ward record for this scrapbook represented the work of many individuals and included stories, experiences, original verse, songs, pictures and illustrations. The completed and assembled scrapbook for Mrs. Aljets was presented to her on the evening of March 8, 1941, at a well-arranged program honoring all past officers of the Nevada Stake Relief Society, when special tribute was paid to each of the three past presidents and a pledge of support was given to the present president, Mrs. Nellie C. Brunson. The continuity of the tribute to the past officers was adapted from "My Relief Society Tapestry," by Mary Grant Judd, Relief Society Magazine, March, 1940, page 165, with the poem, "Your Tapestry," by Lucy H. Nielson, Relief Society 599 Magazine, October, 1938, page 683, forming the introduction. A beautiful verse was selected for tribute to each president, portraying her outstanding virtue, followed by an appropriate hymn, also emphasizing this virtue, sung as a solo. During the musical tribute, a lovely corsage made by the stake work-and-business leader was presented to each president in turn. The audience, which filled the auditorium, was profoundly impressed by the beauty and appropriateness of the program. Representatives were present from all but three of the most distant branches, some of the women having traveled over 200 miles to attend. The success and value of this project are summarized in the following words from Mrs. Broadbent's statement: "Certainly the compilation of our scrapbook histories measured up to our expectations. Never have we experienced such united effort and such deep joy and satisfaction in the results of our efforts. The scrapbook is lovely to look at, but beneath the appearance is a heart and soul that stirs the reader to great depths because it represents the life of the Nevada Stake Relief Society up to the present time. Before we start on the second century in the life of the Relief Society, we have the satisfaction of knowing that our record up to date is well written and preserved." She walked with the train of pioneers across the desert 561 and mountain paths to Salt Lake." In order to be with her brother, she returned to California in about 1875, after the death of her first husband and her son, and resided there for the remainder of her life.
