Restoration Elsie F. Parton (Awarded Third Prize, Eliza R. Snow Poem Contest)
The once green breast of the outraged earth Lay bare and black and scarred. The stiff gaunt trees stripped of leaf and branch Stood naked, worn and hard.
A gaping hole from a bursting shell Was strewn with mangled dead, And structures fell 'neath the battering guns Where smouldering waste was spread.
The crimson dawn lit the eastern sky When day was born anew. A single bloom curved its petals bright To cup the morning dew.
A small bird hopped on a broken spar To chirp its roundelay: An infant crawled from a stiff cold arm And toddled on its way.
