3 LOVE YOU, SNOW Edna S. Dustin I love you, snow; you, too, I've truly missed, For you were near the day I met her there; You melted on those lips I never kissed And clung like diamonds in her hair. And you were present when she went away, And for her grave you wove each lacy flake Into a counterpane where flowers lay,. And walked with me, sensing my heart must break. And you are here again. I love you, snow; My waiting cheek now feels your lambent touch; It freshens tender memories—long ago, And fills a heart with joy that's ached too much. I never say that clouds are silver lined For in each somber cloud, there you I find.
