Mary Grant Judd
MARY GRANT JUDD, although the only child of President Heber J. Grant and his wife Augusta Winters Grant, was not reared alone. It was in the early nineties that Sister Grant took to her heart the six motherless children of her husband, and Mary doesn't remember the day when she was not one of a large family. It so happened that just at the time Augusta Grant came into the home her husband had lost everything he had in the world in a financial way. That "Aunt Gusta", as the children called her, managed to keep the family budget (including clothes for herself and the children, food for ten and some of the operating expenses of the house) within the sum of seventy dollars a month is still to be marveled at. The house at 14 Second East Street, which President Grant had built many years before for his widowed mother, was added to as the family grew, until there were fourteen rooms. The only help that could be afforded in this large home was that contributed by a school girl who worked for her board. But the mother had not spent ten years of her life in school teaching to no purpose.
