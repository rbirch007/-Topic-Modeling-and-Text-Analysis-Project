~ADVENTURING By Amy M. Rice I do not live inside four walls; I soar with thoughts of man To peasant cot, or marble halls, Or desert caravan. I loiter in enchanted woods And take my merry way Along with fairies, knights, and kings Through paths of yesterday. I hear the echoes rise and fall On distant snow-capped hills, And pause beside a castle wall To live a thousand thrills. When tired or discouraged, I go to Galilee To hear the gentle voice of Him Who stilled the angry sea. Each day I joy in living, For someone's magic pen Will take me where I want to go Adventuring again.
