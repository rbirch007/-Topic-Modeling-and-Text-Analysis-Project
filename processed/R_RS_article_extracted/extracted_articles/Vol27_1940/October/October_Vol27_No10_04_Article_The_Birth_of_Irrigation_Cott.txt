THE BIRTH OF IRRIGATION
Celia Anderson Van Cott
In the soil of the sun-parched valley, Sown by the Pioneer's hand, Seeds of grain lay cupped within The dry and wind-swept land.
Hot waves lashed the salty shore, The water holes burned dry; Cattle stood in huddled groups, No rain fell from the sky.
Across the dusty barren plain, Released from the river's flow, Water was brought to the arid land So their withered crops could grow.
Where yesterday was naked soil, Through toil and inspiration, Green shoots of grain appulse with life Are succored by irrigation.
