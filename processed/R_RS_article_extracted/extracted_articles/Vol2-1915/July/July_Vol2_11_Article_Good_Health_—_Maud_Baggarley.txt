But though much has been accomplished in the real work of enlightening and training people in general, there is yet much to be done. Many beautiful children have had their lives marred in the beginning, even though it might be sometimes by circumstances over which parents or guardians had no control. Much illness and many accidents, however, may be averted by fore-knowledge and wise care: And so the little one may have his life well begun and happily influenced by escaping many of the unhappy experiences so apt to occur in childhood. The wise physician, as well as the thoughtful mother or careful nurse, does everything possible to prevent sickness, and in case of epidemics of disease, to limit the contagion as much as possible. Recent years have shown wonderful advancement in medical knowledge and in surgical skill; but in no direction has greater gain been manifest than in the prevention of disease.
