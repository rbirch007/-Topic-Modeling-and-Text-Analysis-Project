8 ex A Glimpse of Loveliness By Glen Perrins It was with a gasp of delight that I came upon picturesque San Dimas canyon in California. It is a glimpse of ~ loveliness. Nestled high in the Pomona valley, I sat down upon the grassy bank of the stream which I had been following. Alone with Nature in the out-of-doors! And in such a wonderland! One glorious hour I spent in this spot, watching the lazy sunlight flicker through the tree tops, and listening to the murmuring of the water as it splashed against the rocks. It was near here that the pioneer settler of Pomono valley first pastured his herds. His name, they told me afterward, was Ignacio Palomares. The Indians, however, so frequently made off with his cattle that the settler decided to quit the beauty spot and his hacienda nearby. He named the spot San Dimas after the thief who repented on the cross—because Palomares had himself repented choosing such a place as pasture-land. One cannot, however, blame Palomares for picking just such a place. Who would not want to settle down in this beauty spot? My hour in San Dimas canyon was a contented, restful and refreshing one. I wager that Palomares — back after a time—just to feast upon the beauty of the place.
