BLIZZARD
By Alice Pierce Willardson
Skies are distant, cold, and gray; Winds are hissing, raging, moaning Through a mist of icy turmoil; Snow in clouds is driven upward, Whipped from quiet into chaos, Dancing now in crazed confusion, Rests again in heaps and mounds. Winter, wild and lost and wailing, After all your moods and madness Shall we find the summer mild?
