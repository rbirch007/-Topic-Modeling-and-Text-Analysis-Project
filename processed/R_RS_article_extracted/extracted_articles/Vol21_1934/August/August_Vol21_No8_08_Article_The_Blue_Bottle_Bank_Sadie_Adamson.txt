The Blue Bottle Bank By Sadie Adamson AS I seated myself in the living room of a friend of mine, I gazed in admiration upon a lovely plant in a candy bucket. "You like it?' She smiled in appreciation. "I grew blooms nearing tea-cup size at one time, thirteen in number." "It's the rarest plant I have seen in years," I replied. "It's most too good to be real. You must love flowers, even daisies and clover blossoms in the rich green grass," I ventured as I looked out over a stretch of lawn. "I can't smell them when I'm gone," she breathed. At the porch of the house next door two little girls were placing dandelions in its crude holes. A part of the porch was decorated with yellow blooms., "Note how busy they are," I commented. "Yes, they are only little ones," she replied, as she handed me ten dimes for a renewal subscription to our Relief Society Magazine. She recorked a small blue bottle from which she had taken the money and THE BLUE BOTTLE BANK placed it high on a china closet shelf.
