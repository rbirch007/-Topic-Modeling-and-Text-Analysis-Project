Pictures By Estelle Webb Thomas
I like to see a willow tree Adroop 'gainst a brick-red wall; The leaden gray of a winter day, And a pine tree straight and tall.
I like the sheen of a window screen, A flow'ry curtain a billow, A snowy bed where a little head Has made a nest in the pillow.
But the dearest sight is a fire-lit night, And my boys—one dark, one fair, All freshly tubbed, and pinkly scrubbed, Just kneeling to say their prayer.
