'Treasures By Eunice J. Miles My little children's faces, How dear they are to me, Abrim with shining wonder at all they hear and see. My little children's questions, How quaint they are, and queer, More curiousness they hold than could be answered in a year. My little children's voices, I'd hear them call to me E'en were I poised on the far-flung brink of a vast eternity.
