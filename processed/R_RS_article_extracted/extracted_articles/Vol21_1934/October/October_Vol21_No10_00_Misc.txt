When Buying Mention Relief Society Magazine

Relief Society Magazine
At this time, more than ever before, when changed schedules have lessened business hours, the problem of how to occupy leisure time is of paramount importance. Confronted. With more free time, we are still handicapped for money, so the wise buyer seeks the best, at the lowest possible cost.
Entertainment and relaxation are imperative to sound living. What, therefore, can possibly meet the existing demand of what to do with our leisure so satisfactorily as a good magazine?
In the Relief Society Magazines subscribers are offered a study course in Theology, Literature, Social Service and Teacher Training. This, along with the opportunity for discussion in class work each Tuesday, is an invaluable asset in building character and rounding out our lives.
The contents of the Magazine also range from the best in Poetry, Fiction, Biography, Ethics, Home. Decoration, Cooking, Civic Problems and articles of current interest to all.
The cost is within the reach of all. For only two cents each week a year's accumulation of pennies will bring the magazine right to the door, providing recreation and education through the best in magazine reading. Just one carfare saved each month will meet the cost.
In the cooking recipes, accuracy and careful selection of ingredients lessen the cost of food, thus demonstrating that a penny saved is a penny earned.
Can any Latter-day Saint woman afford not to accept the privilege of enrolling among the many enthusiastic readers? Being self sustaining, through service offered its readers, our magazine is virtually without cost and is friend, teacher and entertainer.
EDNA J. GARDINER Magazine Representative of Granite Stake
