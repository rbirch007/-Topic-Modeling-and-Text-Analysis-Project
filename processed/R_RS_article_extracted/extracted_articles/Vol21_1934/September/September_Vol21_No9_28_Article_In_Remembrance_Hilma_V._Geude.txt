In Remembrance By Hilma V. Goude Dedicated to the memory of the first Relief Society held in Nauvoo, March 17, 1842 I would that I my voice could raise And sing a song of love and praise To the band of women, tried and true, That met that day in old Nauvoo. Methinks, I see them gathered there, Some golden-locked, some with graystreak'd hair, With willing hearts and eager minds, | They plan to aid all human kind. And then I see a stalwart form, Like a Rock of Shelter, in time of storm, He speaks, and lo! The shackles fall And crumble like a mouldering wall. "T turn the keys and set you free, Henceforth, an era new you'll see Where womanhood shall honored be And gain her rightful high degree." Now many, many years have fled And they lie numbered with the dead But like a monument still stands Their faith and works throughout the lands. So, may their memory ever live And hope and courage to us give That we, like they, our goal may win And to His presence enter in.
