Indian Summer By Helen Kimball Orgill Earth's at her loveliest, Fold upon fold, Distances gleaming Purple and gold Asters are paling, Fields rapture know, Smoke wraiths are drifting Maples aglow. Ah, would I linger By purpling vine There in the meadow Blueberries shine. Hillsides are changing Weaving bright dreams, Vision ethereal. Opalescently gleams. Deep in the tenderness That slowly turns Green into golden Heart fires that burn. Dear is the knowing All things, though wild Join one glad chorus By love beguiled. Joyous fulfillment Crown all the days, Indian Summer— Parting of ways!
