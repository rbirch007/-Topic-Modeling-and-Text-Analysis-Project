The Vagaries of an Autumn Day By Lettie B.H. Rich The sun shone bright in the morning, Then the blue sky turned to grey, Black clouds rolled across the sky The lightning flashed and played, The thunder pealed forth and rumbled, Big raindrops began to fall The wind roared, and scattered autumn leaves Dancing over the old rock wall.
As the sun peeped from behind the clouds, The rain began to cease, The west was a blaze of color, A rainbow appeared in the east.
All nature was resplendent with beauty, As the sun gilded the mountain tops, The earth had received a baptism With glittering soft raindrops.
