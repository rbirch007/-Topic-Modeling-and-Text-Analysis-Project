Music By Ivy Williams Stone
I have heard the note of the lean coyote As he stalked the purple night; And the rapid click of the miner's pick, When he sensed his goal in sight.
I have heard the lark at the end of dark Trilling his wondrous song— And the rhythmic lave of the foamy wave Shifting the sands along.
But the world around, I have heard no sound As sweet as an infant's cry; For my babe's first wail, as he ends travail Is music from God on high!
