The hyacinths along the garden wall Thrust up their waxen blooms from the dark earth, Blue, rose and lavender, and purest white.  A daffodil springs golden from the turf. A wild rose now appears on swaying stem; The sky is blue with fairy floating cloud, An orchard fragrant in its rosy mist, A field with upturned sod but newly ploughed. So many springs beheld with wondering eye, So many miracles of sun and shower, With each new promise we behold in spring Our faith returns to God's creative power.
