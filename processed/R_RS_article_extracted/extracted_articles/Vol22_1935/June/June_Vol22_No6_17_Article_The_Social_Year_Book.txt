EDITORIAL
The Social Year Book
THE Russell Sage Foundation has just published its 1935 "Social Work Year Book," a description of Organized Activities in Social Work and in Related Fields. This treatise of social work is published biennially. Mrs. Russell Sage established the Russell Sage Foundation in 1907, "for the improvement of social and living conditions in the United States of America." The Foundation maintains a staff that has among its duties to conduct studies of social conditions "where new information, its analysis and interpretation seem necessary in order to formulate and advance practical measures aimed at improvement. From time to time the Foundation publishes the discoveries of these studies in book or pamphlet form." This year book will play a vital part in informing all interested in social work of the status of all things social at the present time. The previous volume was published in 1933, just six weeks after the Roosevelt Administration was ushered in. The preface tells that "the year book is not a manual of what ought to be; it is a record of what is." The volume aims to give social workers up-to-date information concerning fields outside their own, to assist those who are entering new areas of service and for the information of related professions. The compilers are proud of the reference literature furnished in this volume. The list includes 1,502 books and pamphlets and 386 magazine articles 'constituting as a whole the most up-to-date work in existence." The contributors are outstanding in their fields of endeavor, and the range of subjects is wide. We congratulate the Russell Sage Foundation on this excellent book that will be so illuminating to those specializing in Social Work. Price $4.00. Russell Sage Foundation, 130 East 22nd Street, N. Y.
