Clyde I came into your town, dear friend, A stranger feeling blue; The people passed me coldly by, Then I one day met you. Something seemed to blossom Like a flower, kissed by the dew, When the sun comes up a' smiling Giving promise ever new. The months go by so swiftly, We our diverse paths pursue; Meeting sometimes, always knowing That the bond is holding true. There's a gentle smile in passing, A handclasp when we meet; The knowledge of your presence Brings a blessing that is sweet. A cheery word of greeting, A wish I know is true, I'm on my way rejoicing The gray skies turned to blue. God never made a precious gem So wondrous rare or fine As human hearts lit by the flame Of friendship's fire divine. So I'm glad I came a' straying Into this town of thine, For I have a priceless treasure In your friendship, friend of mine. L. D. S. Training Doesn't Cost?IT PAYS!
