IF YE DO IT UNTO THE LEAST OF THESE 35 Bon Abu By Sarah A. Farr ON ABU was a descendant of Abraham by his wife Katurah, whom he married after the death of Sarah. He was an Arab and seemed possessed of the wanderlust, leaving all his tribal kindred, who were idol worshipers, he roamed the desert alone. He was not an idol worshiper but was seeking for some supreme being that he had failed to comprehend. My story will begin as Fairy Tales do. Once upon a time, nineteen hundred and thirty-four years ago, Bon Abu sat in the door of his tent listening to the breeze, as it sang its evening vespers through the palm trees, and watching the shifting, drifting sands of the desert. As he gazed he saw the last glinting of the sun rays on the far distant hills.
