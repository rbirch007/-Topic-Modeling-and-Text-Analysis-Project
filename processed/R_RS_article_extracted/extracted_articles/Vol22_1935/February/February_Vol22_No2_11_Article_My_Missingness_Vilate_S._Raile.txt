My Missingness By Vilate S. Raile Awarded First Honorable Mention in the Elisa Roxey Snow Poetry Contest Because I'd watched pain wear her thin, The bit of heaven that dwelt therein Should not be mourned! To comfort me they say, I should not cry; For no one knows so well as I How weary she had grown. I should be glad to see her empty chair. And know she's no more there To wait for death. I should be gay, enjoy each care-free day In new-found freedom. Go my way Unhindered by her care. Unhindered!' Freedom! God Forgive, They know not what they say— They have not sensed my missingness— They did not hear me pray to Thee, dear God, For three—or two-—or only one more Living, giving day.
