Love Divine By Carrie Tanner Dear mother earth lies gray and bare and scarred, The browning leaves swirl in hollows drear, The biting cold creeps o'er her wounded breast, And wailing winds rush through the naked trees. Then lowering clouds of night come very near And yield their comfort in the falling snow, A mystic peace o'er spreads the painful sight, The gift of love speaks through the glistening white. So, too, the human heart is pained and scarred, Unsheltered oft from cold, from blows, from wounds Of disappointment keen and blighting woes, And numbing pain of grief when love's bereft. But like the mystic peace of falling snow, Though dark the night of fitful dream, Comes peace of God the wounded heart to heal, And covers all with soothing love divine.
