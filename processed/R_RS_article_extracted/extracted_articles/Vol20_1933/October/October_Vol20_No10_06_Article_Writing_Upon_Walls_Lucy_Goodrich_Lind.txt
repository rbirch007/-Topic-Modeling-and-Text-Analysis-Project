Writing Upon Walls By Lucy Goodrich Lind In each heart is an inborn desire To leave footprints, time will not efface, Hence the pictographs carved on canyon cliffs By a savage Indian race. But on walls of worth and beauty Some pictures do naught but deface For some, misguided, follow wrong Just to be known, in life's race. Some write but where need is, for right's sake: Thus in type that the world will ken Their names are through kindness engraven On the hearts of their fellowmen.
