Sometimes we fail to realize how wide is the work of the world. Among the numerous professions for which college helps to prepare may be mentioned the following: the various fields of business, including stenography and secretarial positions, librarian, recreational director, home economics expert, journalist, interior decorator, advertisement writer, the various fields of research, in addition to the many professions that women are pioneering, such as medicine, law, science, statesmanship, etc. It is much better to direct the college course toward the achievement of some worthy occupation, but even if your daughter is not successful in accomplishing this aim, she is even then better prepared to more quickly acquire the information necessary for making a living. The greatest tool of all is a "furnished" mind—a mind capable of weighing facts without prejudice or superstition, a mind capable of adjustment to new ideas.
