Wait By Bertha A. Kleinman Sodden with the winter's raining, Has the summer found decay? Wait—beneath the rust and staining Time is spinning buds for May. Thwarted amid doubt's disaster, Blinded are the ways you go? Wait—Time is the only master And Truth is his ultimo. Are the battle trenches gory, Stretching on beneath the stars? Wait—Time turns it all to glory, Poppies heal the battle scars. Are there mounds where memory hovers— Blinding out the hope beyond— Wait—a lichen veil he covers Where the deepest grave has yawned. Never feud or grudge or grievance, Never mystery or doubt, But awaiting Time's allegiance Shall at last be blotted out.
