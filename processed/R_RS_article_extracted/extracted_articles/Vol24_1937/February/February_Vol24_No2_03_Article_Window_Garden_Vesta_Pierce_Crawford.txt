WINDOW GARDEN
By Vesta Pierce Crawford
Outside the winds of winter blow And blizzards gild the window pane, Yet here where yellow flowers grow, The colored summer comes again.
Oh narrow is the garden row, But wide and long enough to hold This primrose fringe of petaled glow And daffodils of bended gold.
Here now the red geraniums stand, Defiant of the outer gloom, And who can see the frozen land Beyond this flame of window bloom!
