JESUS WALKED By Vesta P. Crawford
As Jesus walked among his native hills He saw the way of seasons on the land, How welcome rain falls upon desert sand And drenches waiting seeds to life and fills Lone gullies with a swathe of waving grass; But though earth flaunts her yellow fields of grain, The glory of her blooming time must pass And turn to arid measure once again.
He knew that life is like a desert land, And His all-knowing heart could understand That multitudes seek not for life alone, But for some ancient comfort to atone For change and loss and all our futile tears— An everlasting gleam across the years!
WINTER CREEPS ON IN THE ROCKIES
The Relief Society Magazine
