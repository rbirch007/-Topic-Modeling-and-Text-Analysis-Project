Idaho Stake "THE above picture represents the Relief Society of the Tuttle Ward in Idaho Stake. These sisters have made a very enviable record in Relief Society activity. Among such was the idea of securing subscriptions for the Relief Society Magazine in order to achieve the one hundred percent. Many of the sisters could not afford to take the Magazine, so a plate supper and dance was held. The members of the Relief Society furnished the materials for the supper, and helped prepare it. The proceeds of this were then used to subscribe for the Magazine. In this way the sisters earned their subscription and went over the top. In the Church Security program more than nine thousand cans of fruits and vegetables were provided. Another very splendid accomplishment was the clinic which was held for children from thirteen to eighteen years of age. Tonsils and adenoids were given attention, and more than two hundred and sixteen patients have been aided. Lyman Stake A VERY excellent report of the Lyman Stake Relief Society day was sent to the office. Relief Society members from all the Wards of the Stake were in attendance, many of the sisters coming a distance of over one hundred miles to be in attendance. The first meeting began at 10:30. This took the form of a general assembly where the President, Sister Martha Field, gave a report of the year's work. This was supplemented by some of the officers who gave accounts of A RELIEF SOCIETY WORKER particular activities. Notable among these was the fine work of the Singing Mothers, who had practiced some of the songs suggested by the General Committee, and had learned these and furnished their beautiful music. Every Ward in the Stake had a part in the exhibit of all kinds, which included embroidery, cut work, crocheting, knitting, tatting, flowers made from paper and wood fibre, quilts of many designs, braided, woven and hooked rugs, luncheon sets, layettes and hand-painted pictures of various kinds, too numerous to mention, made a display long to be remembered. In addition to the exhibition of finished work a demonstration of rugs from bias strips of woolen material, crocheted with carpet warp, was given by Mrs. Hannah 177 Blackner. This rug has the appearance of chenile, and is very beautiful. At noon a delicious luncheon, served cafeteria style, was provided for all the guests, and' at two o'clock there was a re-assembling of the sisters when a demonstration in Magazine binding was given by trained leaders. This useful art was greatly appreciated as it enables the sisters to take proper care of their magazines and repair books which may need such service. A dance in the evening concluded the fine day's activities, and all felt that it had been one of the most successful ever held in the Lyman Stake. The credit for this was undoubtedly due to the Stake President, her Board and the fine cooperation of the Wards.
