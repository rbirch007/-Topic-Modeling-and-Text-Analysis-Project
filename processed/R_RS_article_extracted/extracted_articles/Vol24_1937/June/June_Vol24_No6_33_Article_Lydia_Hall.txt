HOME By Lydia Hall Adventurers, who sail through skies of blue On eagle wings beyond the horizon, Your clear eyes seeking unknown lands where you May view strange wonders 'neath a foreign sun; O, brothers of the restless heart who hear And list to the call of the seven seas It must be fine to rove year after year Discovering all of earth's mysteries, But O, the strongest call to me today, The one that fills my fondest dreams is home, Beneath the old roof. Tree I want to stay, I love the fireside too much to roam, I am content to be where joy is still, A small, brown wren beside the window sill.
