Green I wish my past misdeeds could be Completely cast aside like garments old, Nor leave a stain, nor spot on me, Nor on my future life still keep a hold. I wish my selfish heart could yield To others all that's rightly theirs, And not uphold its own ungodly ways, But lose them in unselfish prayers. I wish my loved ones and my friends Could cover all my wrongs with charity And only see in me my better self And understand me as I try to be. I wish that I could live a life so sweet, So truly lovely and so undefiled That every act or thought might safely be Exposed unguarded to a little child.
