THE DUST STORM By Sadie Ollorton Clark
The hills are hidden from my eyes today, Great clouds of dust enshroud their barren sweep. The rocks are grimed, the cactus, too, is gray; Oh rust hued sky, is there no cause to weep?
There is no sign of spring in this drear land, The dust destroys the blossoms 'ere they form, And every stream where water flowed, lies sand. There is no grass to hold the dew at morn.
My thoughts turn backward to a spring I knew, High mountain peaks, snow capped, beneath a sky Of purest azure. Clean winds swept on high, And racing clouds were feathers on the blue.
The yellow clouds still roll, yet I can sing, For I still keep the mem'ry of that spring. This booklet will tell you why.
