TO THE DAUGHTER OF A PIONEER By Claire Stewart Boyer
O if the daughter of a pioneer Can treasure such a homespun loveliness, And pass it into lives she holds most dear Without the loss of its bright stateliness, Then can a heritage of thought and way Be greater than the lands of homestead pride, And sons and daughters in a future day Will need no fairer, no diviner guide. For in their quest, the fervent, rhythmic beat Of your nobility will shape their goals; It will be music for their eager feet; It will be power for their yearning souls. And through the years your homespun loveliness Will weave a broidery of deep content; Each dawn will be your signal tenderness, Each night of stars the truth your words have lent. O if the daughter of a pioneer Can leave this boon to her posterity, Then can she raise her eyes to God in praise; For this, her share in immortality.
