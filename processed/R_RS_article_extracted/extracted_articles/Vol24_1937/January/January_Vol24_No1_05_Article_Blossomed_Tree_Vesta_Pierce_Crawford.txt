BLOSSOMED TREE
By Vesta Pierce Crawford
(Awarded Second Prize in the Eliza Roxey Snow Poetry Contest)
VESTA PIERCE CRAWFORD
Once more the orchard treads at her loom And finest threads the early weavers ply Are wefted in this plum tree's gauzy bloom With tracery of branches lifted high;
Oh make no echoed sound, walk softly now To see these petals glossed and satin pale, To see the flowers etched upon a bough As broidery upon a wedding veil.
Yet even now the blossoms fall and drift, Too brief are April's opalescent ways, The splendor of the trees exceeding swift; In this ephemeral hour springtime stays
I seek to hold the season's transient gift And garner beauty for unflowered days.
