THE CROSS. I made the cross myself, whose weight Was later laid on me; This thought is torture, as I climb Up life's steep Calvary. To think my own hand drove the nails, I sang a merry song, And chose the heaviest wood I had To make it firm and strong. If I had guessed, if I had dreamed Its weight was meant for me, I would have made a lighter cross To bear up Calvary. ?Selected.
