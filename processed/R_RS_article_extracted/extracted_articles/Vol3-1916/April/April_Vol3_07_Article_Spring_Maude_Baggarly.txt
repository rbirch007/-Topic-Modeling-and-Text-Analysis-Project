SPRING.
By Maud Baggarly.
Hark ye not the voice of spring In the high, clear note of joy That insistently doth ring From the meadow where the lark, Drunk with life, doth ceaseless sing?
Every bough with life's a-thrill The pregnant sod in prescience stirs With rhythmic harmony, that fills With its cadences sublime, Valleys cupped among the hills.
Rosy clouds of blossoms bright Lilting song the whole day long: Over all supernal light And the evening with the hush Of the first created Night
Thus the spring! hark and hear Earth's old love song newly sung Sound unto thine ear Sweet as when the world was young!
