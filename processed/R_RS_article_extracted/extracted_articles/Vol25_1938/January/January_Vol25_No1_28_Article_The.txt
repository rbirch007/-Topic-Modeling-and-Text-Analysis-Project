Finally, a defeatist attitude in the mother is contagious. She should generate confidence and success by her own life. She should not shift her frustrations on to the child and expect him to succeed way beyond her level of achievement. Setting the child's sights toward a goal that is too high in order to gratify a mother's unfulfilled ambitions is a frequent source of forced failure and inferiority feelings in the child.
Problems for Discussion
1. Give an example of how a sense of failure can spread from one task to another.
2. Show how one success contributes to the next success, and one failure contributes to the next.
3. How does a parent's or school's failure to provide the child with tasks difficult enough to call forth effort but easy enough for him to see his way clear to accomplishment, contribute to laziness? What would you suggest as a treatment for the lazy child?
4. Discuss three concrete steps a parent can take to prevent or correct inferiority feelings in her child.
