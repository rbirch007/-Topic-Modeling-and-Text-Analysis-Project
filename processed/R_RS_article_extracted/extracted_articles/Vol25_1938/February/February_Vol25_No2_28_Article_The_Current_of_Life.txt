THE CURRENT OF LIFE By Celia A. Van Cott Rippling and flashing the silvery brook Rambles along in the sun Through clover bloom to the deepening stream Where the turbulent waters run. The river rolls to the surging sea Where the heaving breakers roar And crash in frothing flakes of foam On the sands of the rugged shore. Swiftly swirling the ebbing tide Carries us far out to sea To a haven of rest on the ocean's breast The peace of eternity.
