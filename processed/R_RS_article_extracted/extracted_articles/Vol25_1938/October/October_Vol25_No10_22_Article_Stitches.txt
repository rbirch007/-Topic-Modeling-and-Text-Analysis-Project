Stitches
By Ida May Rees
Mending, patching, darning all day, Old things thinning and wearing away, New clothes ripped in a too rough play, Children's lives must be joyous and gay.
Work all day and nothing to show, Nothing that any but I would know; Back to the shelves the same clothes go, Respectable, orderly, row on row.
Maybe with stitches and method and thrift Something will somehow in character sift, Into their lives with a mild uplift, Into their hearts as a fairy gift.
