KINDLINESS
By Adeline R. Ensign
There never was an unkind word
That did not leave a sting,
No bitter thought or selfish deed
That did not sorrow bring;
For though they may seem trifling things
True happiness they mar.
There never was a real heartache
That did not leave a scar.
And since there's so much suffering
O'er which we have no power,
Then let the things we do and say
Enrich each passing hour;
For pleasant words and kindly deeds
Make love in triumph reign,
It's what we sow in kindliness
We reap in love again.
