PULSE OF PROGRESS
By Sadie Willes Adamson
WHILE strolling one day my eyes fell upon a wee tot climbing a rude ladder, trying in vain to reach a white rose that nodded and swayed in the sunlight and gentle breeze. There were numerous buds in reach but the tempting white rose seemed elusive to the little hand. Secure from sight I stood transfixed as the mother of the child came into view. There was no hint of alarm in her quiet voice, although the tot had climbed to quite a height as she said, "Steady son, climb higher." Instantly the little hands gripped the ladder, stepped a round higher and reaching steadily once more, obtained the rose.
Years later I met the tot now grown to manhood. His achievements were wonderful. I asked, "How do you account for your success?" I waited but a moment for the answer. "My mother was my inspiration, she constantly encouraged me to climb higher."
Encouragement is the pulse of progress. Thus we grow.
