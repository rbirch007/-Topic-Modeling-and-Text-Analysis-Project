A Spring Song Mrs. R. J. Green. _ Hear the laughter and the singing That the infant Spring is bringing: In her wake! As from the snowy bed she merges, E'en the willows, oaks and birches New life take! Every bee and bird and flower, In each greening, budding bower, All astir. At the earliest peep of day Each one busy in its way Just for her. See the glorious rosy morn Of her drear and coldness shorn Spread her rays! Beauteous blossoms?every hue, Bathed with perfumed drops of dew, 'Homage pays! And the fairy leaflets dance Baby eyes look on askance; All things new! Birdlings twitter, chirp and sing, Dainty cherub?lovely spring, All for. You.
