A Weird Hush of Beauty By Glen Perrins
Sunset cliffs! Hollow grottoes filled with the roar of the ocean! Dismal caves opening into the dazzling sunlight of the Pacific Coast. Weird formations that have been washed out by the action of the sea during ages past. Famous are these curious formations of many beautiful colorings. These caves?the Seven Caves of La Jolla (which is from the Spanish, meaning the gem, or jewel, and so pronounced "La Hoy-Ya") are found near San Diego, California, and the Sunset Cliffs near by. Both have been used in the motion pictures time and time again. The cliff of caves is a haven for hosts of black cormorants or sea birds, who rest themselves on the many crags and points, after their long flights to sea. These birds are expert divers and live entirely on small fish. As the sun sinks over the broad expanse of water, one looks on the cliffs and caves, brilliantly colored in golden lustre, and marvels at the weird hush of beauty which falls over the spot.
