Listening to Beethoven's Fifth Symphony
By Annie Pike Greenwood
What heaven was locked within his precious brain That never was revealed to any man; What harmonies of joy, what notes of pain, Even a faint surmise is all we can.
He must have heard things greater far than this There in his soul, or else this could not be. At least he had this most transcendent bliss Of knowing all of music's ecstasy?
More than is possible for us who hear This that he left us when he went away? Away where he can make his music clear? Clearer than ever in this mortal day:
Somewhere in God's great universe is he Creating greater than this symphony.
