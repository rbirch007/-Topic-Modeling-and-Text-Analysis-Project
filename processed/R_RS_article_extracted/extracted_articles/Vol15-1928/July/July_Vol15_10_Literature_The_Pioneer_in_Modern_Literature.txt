The Pioneer in Modern Literature We are pleased to be able to present to our readers a brief criticism of the outstanding pioneer literature of our period. In the May issue of the Magazine we published an interesting review of Giants of the Earth, by O. E. Rolvaag, which is a story of the Norwegian American immigrant. In the July issue we are presenting Carl Sandburg's Life of Abraham Lincoln. These reviews are written by Mrs. Lais Vernon Hales, a student of literature who is keenly interested in studies of pioneer life. We are sure that our readers are interested in new and good books in any line; and as pioneering forms so much of the background of American life and is so close to our Western life, we shall all be particularly interested in books that interpret it. Behind us is a veritable mine of rich material that will in the future be worked from many angles by writers of fiction. We are gratified at the present output, for it argues much for the future.
