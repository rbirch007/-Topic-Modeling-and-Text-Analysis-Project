Infant Mortality Decreasing
It is a matter of deep gratification to be able to record that infant mortality continues to decrease in the United States. This news comes to us from the American Child Health Association through the medium of its annual report. In 1918 there were one hundred deaths per thousand, or the relatively high rate of ten percent. In 1926 the death rate had been reduced to 73.7 per thousand. Last year, 1927, it had fallen to 64.4 deaths for each one thousand births. This is certainly a notable decrease in less than a decade. If the good work continues, it will be a very short time before infant mortality among children of the United States will be cut in two. To reduce infant mortality is one of the aims of all Relief Society workers and other organizations that have gone forward in the name of the Shepherd-Towner bill. It is one of the aims of all organizations having in mind the welfare of children.
