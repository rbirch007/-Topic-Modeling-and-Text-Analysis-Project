A LAY OF CHIVALRY By Josephine Spencer Close where the Winter's bivouacked reserve Holds its last trenches, pitching stubborn tents In tangled forests where some shadowed curve Fosters the cause with rebel insolence,? A troop of buds?the Spring's young chivalry? Silently creep, where sluggish outposts lie? Frost sentinels asleep with sun-drugged sense? And at some dawn, before the swallow's view Has stumbled on the Spring's thin trail of blue, Over the crumbling bastions of the camp? So shortly since a regnant field of snow? With glow of daffodil and crocus lamp, Swiftly they clamber, whilst the routed foe Fly with mist-banners trailing worn and low.
