VALENTINE’S DAY.
	According to some ecclesiastical writers,
St. Valentine was a bishop, according to
others a presbyter. He was beheaded at
Rome in the reign of the Emperor Claud-
ius, 270 A. D., and was early canonized.
Wheatley says: “He was a man of most
admirable parts, and so famous for his love
and charity that the custom of choosing
valentines upon his festival took its rise
from thence.” Others derive the custom
from birds being supposed to select their
mates on this day; others from a custom
practised in ancient Rome at the festival of
the Supercalio, during the month of Feb-
ruary, when among other ceremonies, the
names of young women were placed in a
box, from which they were drawn by men
as chance directed, with the same merri-
ment that valentines are exchanged to-
day.
	The pastors of the early church, finding
it impossible to extirpate this heathen cere-
mon changed the form. [---] he cus-
tom on the eve of St. Valentine to have
the names of a select number of one sex
put into some vessel by an equal number
of the other, and thereupon everyone drew
a name which for the time being was called
his or her valentine.
	The custom of choosing valentines exist-
ed very early. Presents of gloves, garters
and jewelry were common as valentines.
						C. J. T.
