For native-born women to be ruled by the
men of their own nationality—their own
civilization—is quite humiliating enough:
but to live in subjection to the men of
foreign countries, void of the first principles
of our free institutions, is surely a degrada-
tion too dire for the Daughters of America
to submit to without an earnest protest. 
	The fiat has gone forth, there is no es-
cape from universal manhood suffrage, and
the one and only antidote for all its ills is
universal womanhood suffrage. There can
be no going backward. All men, of all
classes and all nationalities, have the right
to vote and all women must have it. There-
fore, the question now is, when shall the
crowning right of American citizenship be
placed in the hands of women, whom men
love to call the better half of the people?
	That you, the Daughters of America,
may give the weight of your influence to
hasten the coming of this good time is the
