MARIA CROMPTON.
	Sister Maria Crompton, wife of Robert Cromp
ton died at her home in American Fork on
Thursday, October 13, 1898, after a long and pain-
ful illness of nearly two years, the cause being
dropsy superinduced by heart trouble. She was
born April 8, 1834, at Withyham, Sussex, Eng-
land, joined the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-
day Saints in New York City and came to Utah in
1861. She was married to Brother Crompton in
1862 and has made her home in American Fork
ever since. She was the mother of seven chil-
dren six of whom survive her, three sons and
three daughters, also a number of grandchildren.
	Sister Crompton was a teacher in the Relief
Society for a number of years, always w[-]lling to
do her part in the reat work that devolves
upon the sisters in this calling, was faithful in at-
ending her meetings as long as she was able and
rejoiced in the testimony of Jesus. She was pa-
tient and long suffering during her illness and had
unbounded faith in the ordi[-]ance of the Church
for the healing of the sick, always obtaining re-
lief whenever she was administered to by the
Elders. She was a devoted wife and mother, al-
ways working diligently to set a good example
for her children and to educate them in the prin-


ciples of life and salvation as well as to fit them to
battle with the trials of life. Great credit is due
to her children who worked so untiringly in her
behalf during her illness, never wearying in their
labor of love. May the Lord support them and
their father upon whom the loss falls so heavily,
and may He help them to realize that all is well
and though her presence will be sadly missed in
the home circle, yet she may be a link in the
chain that binds them to the Eternal Father.
	EMMA FEATHERSTONE,
	JANE C. HINDLEY,
	SYLVIA MISENER,
				Committee.
