EDITORIAL NOTES.

	There will be an entertainment given at
the handsome new residence of Mr. and
Mrs. O. J. Salisbury in this city in the inter-
est of the woman’s department of the George
Washington Memorial Fund. Utah was
given its proportion, a thousand dollars,
and nearly that amount was raised in small
donations from the school children. A lit-
tle over a hundred dollars is lacking to
make up the thousand and a unique pro-
gramme has been prepared by the commit-
tee who have had the work in charge,
which will be more than compensation for
the price of the tickets. It will not be a
social affair, but there will be music and
other pleasing features and a talk by Cap-
tain Ducat who was in the battle at Santi-
ago de Cuba.
	The meetings of the National Woman’s
Relief Society held this month have been
fraught with much interest and a general
good feeling has been manifested in the
charitable and philanthropic work of this
oldest organization; and the instruction
given by President Zina D. H. Young and
her counselors and assistants has been of a
nature calculated to bind together and
strengthen the work in the various locali-
ties, and to increase the interest of the Soci-
ety in the several lines of work necessary
to the accomplishment of the purposes de-
signed when the organization was effected.
To elevate and uplift the condition of those
who need assistance temporally or spiritual-
ly, to provide employment for the idle and
to secure the wheat that has been gathered
and held in trust as well as add to the quan-
ty now while bounteous harvests are being
garnered. These and many other matters
have been discussed by those residing in
this centre Stake of Zion and by those who
have come from a distance, until it would
seem these topics must be thoroughly
understood.
