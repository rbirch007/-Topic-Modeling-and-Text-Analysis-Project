MARY ANN WALLACE WOODWARD.

	On the 14th of May 1898, after a lingering and
painful illness, this our beloved sister passed the
bourne whence none return. The cause of her
death was pneumonia complicated with disease
of the heart.
	Sister Woodward was the daughter of Neverson
Grant Wallace and his wife, Eleanor Carns. She
was born in Erie Co. Penn., on the 17th of Nov-
ember, 1841. Her mother died when she was
quite young. She came to Utah in 1856 with her
father, brother and her uncle and his family, in
Abraham O. Smoot’s company. She was bap-
tized in the winter of 1856, during the Reforma-
tion. On March 27, 1857, she was married to
Elder George Woodward and came with him to
Dixie at President Young’s call in 1861. She
carded and spun a five pound bunch of cotton
yarn and took to the fair in Salt Lake City in 1863,
probably the first raised in Utah. When the Re-
lief Society was organized in 2nd ward, she was
appointed a counselor to the president, Margaret
Moody. She was called to labor in the ordinances
of the St. George Temple, March 1, 1877, a posi-
tion she has occupied ever since up to the time of
her death. For many years she has occupied the
responsible position of treasurer of the Relief
Society of this Stake and has filled it with honor
and ability. In her death we realize that we have
lost a staunch friend, a faithful Saint, and one
whose usefulness and integrity cannot be gain-
said. Yet we rejoice that she is relieved from
suffering, and only hope that when our turn
comes we may be as well prepared.
