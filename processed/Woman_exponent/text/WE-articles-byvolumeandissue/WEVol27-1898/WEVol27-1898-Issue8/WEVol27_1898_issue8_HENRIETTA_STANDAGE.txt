		HENRIETTA STANDAGE.

	Died at Mesa City, Oct. 11, 1898, af er an illness
of some days, Henrietta Standage, wife of Henry
Standage one of the Mormon Battalion, and
daughter of Noah Rogers, one of the first mis-
sionaries to the Sandwich [-]sl[-]nds,and his wifeEda
Rogers. Deceased was born May 30, 1832, at
Shelbyville, Portage Co , Ohio. Born at so
early a period she passed through every principal
evolution of the Church  experience from the
day of her birth until the day of her death. She
came to Utah in 1850 and was married to Henry
Standage in 1851, with whom she shared in the
privations and toils of Utah’s early pioneers, and
to whom she bore a family of ten children. In the
year 1880 she emigrated from Utah to Arizona.
On her arrival in Mesa, Arizona, with her family
she joined the Relief Society in Alma ward, and
in 1883 she was voted in a first counselor in the
Relief [-]ociety of which she has been a faithful
member attendin every duty devolving upon her
and always on hand to help to relieve the sick or
those in distress; and as stated by her Bishop and
each who spoke at her funeral, Sister Standage
was a live member of the Church, was faithful to
every duty and calli g. From age, experience
and good works she has gone to her rest as a
veteran in the Church of God, leaving a veteran
husband with eight living children, forty-four
[-]randchildren and four great grandchildren to
bear her name in honor and blessing through all
generations of time.
	A long cortege of loving kindred and friends
followed her remains  to the grave to await a re-
union of loved ones in that sweet bye and bye.
