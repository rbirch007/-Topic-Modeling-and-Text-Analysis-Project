AN INTERESTING PARTY.
	At the residence of Mrs. Amelia F. Young
in her handsome parlors on Tuesday after-
noon, November 15, there were assembled
a company of ladies who represented at least
three of the principal families of early Utah,
Brigham Young, Heber C. Kimball and
Daniel H. Wells. Of President Young’s
family there were present seven wives and
sixteen daughters, including the two eldest
born before the Church was organized in
the State of New York. Elizabeth the eld-
est had come from Idaho five or six hun-
dred miles to attend this gathering, having
to travel by team part of the way. It was
a goodly assemblage and we were forcibly
reminded of the gatherings in the Social
hall in the days now departed when we were
accustomed to see those heroic representa-
tives of the Church and the State, Brigham
Young, Heber C. Kimball, George A.
Smith. Daniel H. Wells, John Taylor, Or-
son Hyde, Wilford Woodruff, Orson Pratt
and many others well known as founders of
this commonwealth. Brother Fulsom,
father of Mrs Amelia F. Young, and his
wife were also of the company, and after
general conversation, partaking of a deli-
cious repast in the handsome dining room
of the hostess, remarks were made by some
of those present mostly of a reminescent,
congratulatory character. Meantime Bishop
Clawson came in and was called upon to
offer a benediction as some of the guests
were ready to leave; the Bishop asked per-
mission to make a few remarks and told of
his experience on the stage, his intimate
acquaintance with Joseph Smith and men-
tioned the Prophet’s love for the idealities,
the drama etc., as well as for practical and
athletic sports and games. It was a happy
reunion of old friends who are occupied
with daily duties to the exclusion of that
sweet intercourse which makes all friends
feel akin to each other.
