EDITORIAL NOTES.

	We are just in receipt of a postal card
from Jerusalem a souvenir of the visit of
the Emperor and Empress of Germany, on
which Madame Mountford writes among
other items, “Greetings to all friends in
Utah.”
	As Christmas is approaching it is well to
remind the public that we have for sale at
this office the book of poems “Musings and
Memories” by E. B. Wells, which is a
suitable holiday gift-book and should be in
every library of the Y. L. M. I. A., cloth
cover $1.25, dainty binding $1.50, moroco
$2.00. This book is handsomely bound and
contains a steel engraving of the author.
	A new book for the holidays will soon be
ready entitled “Aunt Em’s Stories” includ-
ing “Hephzibah,” which was enthusiastic-
ally received when published as a serial ten
years ago, also “Aunt Esther’s Sweetheart,”
“Some Old Love Letters,” “Emily Hamil-
ton’s Christmas,” “He Cometh Not,” and
several others equally as interesting and all
founded on facts.
	President Franklin D. Richards and his
wife, President Jane S. Richards, organized
a Relief Society in Omaha on Tuesday,
October 25, at the residence of one of the
local Elders. There were present also the
presiding Elder of the mission, Elder R.E.
Yeates and Elder Hatch, both of Logan,
Utah, also Bishop Barton and wife of Kays-
ville, and Mrs. Josephine R. West, one of
the General Board of the Primary Associa-
tion, and Mrs. E. B Wells who arrived just
before the meeting closed. Full particulars
will be published.
