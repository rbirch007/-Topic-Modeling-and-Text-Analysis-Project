U. W. P. C.

	On Friday evening, Sep. 30, 1898, the
Press Club met as usual in the “Exponent”
office. Called to order by President Ro-
mania B. Pratt, Mrs. E. B. Wells made the
opening prayer. Roll call was answered
by sentiments from Shakespeare. Minutes
of previous meeting read and approved.
The business of incorporating the club was


brought up. A motion was made by Mrs.
E. B. Wells and seconded by Lydia D.
Alder that the Press Club be incorporated;
carried. Moved by Mrs. E. W. Hyde and
seconded by Ellis R. Shipp that the presi-
dent appoint a committee to attend to it;
carried. The president appointed Mrs. E.
B. Wells and Mrs. Margaret A. Caine.
	A letter was read by the corresponding
secretary, L. D. Alder, from the editors of
Woman’s Work thanking the club for their
splendid report, also asking for meritorious
papers written by club members; also read
a letter from the secretary of the State
Federation in regard to annual dues for
the coming year. It was moved by Mrs.
E. B. Wells and seconded by Ruth M. Fox
that the dues be paid; carried.
	What shall we do at our initial meeting?
was then discussed. Dr. Ellis R. Shipp
moved that we meet at Mrs. Bunnell’s home
125 First St.; carried. It was also decided
that the club have an open discussion on
the late queen of Denmark, the Dreyfus 
case and Hallowe’en, Dr. Ellis R. Shipp,
Helen V. Kimball and Ruth M. Fox lead-
ing in the respective topics.
	It was decided that we hold a business
meeting, Monday, October 10, at 7 o’clock
p. m., at the “Exponent” office.
	A most interesting and instructive paper
on the Empress Elizabeth of Austria was
read by Helen V. Kimball, and meeting
adjourned to meet at Mrs. Bunnell’s, Oct.
