EVOLUTION.

	“Blessed are they that hunger and thirst after
righteousness for they shall be filled.”
I walk in the radiant glory of a new-born, golden
	day,
As full of calm, fresh sunshine as the cloudless
	skies of May,
And my steps are light as Zephyr, for my hope is
	bright as morn,
And the glow of the day shall brighten in the
	light that is yet unborn.

In the vistas of ages vanished, in the tears and 
	groans and strife,
In the turbulent pulse [-]f the cosmos, in the 
	panting toil of life.
In the chaos all but formless, in the cloud that
	holds a rod,
In the dreary tread of ages I behold the hand
	of God.

Upward and ever upward His radiant beacons
	mount,
And the heart of the world is quickened, by the
	infinite sun it fount,
The Fount of the Infinite Father, so gentle and
	true and kind, 
Patiently striving and giving Himself to the
	darkened finite Mind.

For ages before man’s knowledge, I see from the
	lowly sod,
A white, wan face uplifted in a broken prayer to
	God;
So the prayer hath touched the heart of Him who
	is Joy and Love and Light,
And the sweet response lifts the lowly Heart up,
	up fiom the depths of Night.

Still on in the dim prospective, in the solemn
	march of time,
We read the story of Light and Love struggling
	up to a height sublime. 
Eternity looms before us: shall Humanity’s dream
	come true?
Ah, yes! For the trend of the Soul mounts on
	with deathless Life in view!

Though here in the shadows of earth-life our
	human strength grows faint,
Through sorrow and sin and darkness the crushed
	soul moans comp[-]aint,
Yet Truth never sleeps on the Tower, God’s
	forces of love march on,
And we shout our joy to the great wide world as
	the next black shade has gone.

Though our hearts may be chilled in the night-
	shades, where the saviors of men have stood,
Ever battling with souls undaunted, for the up-
	ward trend of God,
’Tis sweet to know that the irid east for the,
	famished heart holds more
Than even the glorious, purpled morn hath
	promised to hold in store.

And so I walk in a fairy dream with my spirit all
	aglow,
And joy in the light of a day that will come with
	the shadows all laid low.
For the Father’s hand is upon the helm and the
	heart of man will yield,
And evil shall cease for the soul of Christ shall
	garner the whitened field.
					RUBY LAMONT.
Circleville, Utah, Sep. 6, 1898.
