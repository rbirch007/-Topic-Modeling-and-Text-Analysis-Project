AS EAGLE

	Denver is one day nearer Chicago to-day than
it was yesterday. This is the actual practical
effect of the fast mail trains inaugurated by the
North-Western and Union Pacific.—Chicago
Times Herald, Feb. 8, 1898.
“The Chicago Special”
		Leaves Denver, 	4:30 p m today.
		Arrives Omaha,	7:00 a m tomorrow.
		Arrives Chicago,	8:45 p m tomorrow.
	To the Chicago, Union Pacific and North-
western belongs the credit of inaugurating the
new fast train service between Denver and
Chicago.
	This new train is a triumph of modern rail-
roading. It consists of six magnificent cars:
Buffet, smoking and library car, with barber
shop, etc. Two splendid palace sleeping cars.
Reclining chair car. Dining car, meals a la 
carte. Baggage car.
	This is the only train between Denver and
Chicago having the luxurious
. . . . . . . . Buffet, Smoking and Library Cars.
	Westbound, “The Colorado Special” leaves
Chicago 10:00 a m, arrives Omaha 11:55 p m, to-
day, and Denver 1:30 p m, tomorrow.
