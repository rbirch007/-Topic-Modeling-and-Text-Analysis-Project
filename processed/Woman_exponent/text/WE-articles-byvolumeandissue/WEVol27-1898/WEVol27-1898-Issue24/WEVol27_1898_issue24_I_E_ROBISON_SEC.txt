				I. E. ROBISON, Sec.


Mrs. Frances H. Howard, of the New
England W. P. A., read a characteristically
trenchant paper on “Our Critics,” at the
last literary meeting of the association.
The paper will be printed entire in the
Club Woman.

	Lady Tennyson, the wife of the new
viceroy of South Australia, is an Irish wo-
man. Her name when she married the
laureate’s son, the Hon. Hallam Tennyson,
in 1884, was Audrey Florence Boyle, of the
famous fighting family of Cork. She is the
mother of three sons two of whom remain in
England.
