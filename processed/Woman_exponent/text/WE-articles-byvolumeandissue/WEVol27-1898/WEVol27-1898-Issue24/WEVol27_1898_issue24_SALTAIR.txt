SALTAIR.

	The Young Family Association con-
templates having the grandest celebration
this year, on “The First of June,” that
has been known in Utah for a long time.
Something like Pioneer Day in early Utah
times, when everybody was invited, seems
to be in progress. All friends of the great
Pioneer, President Brigham Young, and all
others who are interested will be cordially
welcomed to participate in the enjoyment
of a real gala day at Saltair. Committees
have been appointed to attend to all the
necessary arrangements, and a splendid
program is in preparation.
	It will be the ninety-eighth birthday of
the honored and beloved leader of the peo-
ple who first settled Utah, and President
Lorenzo Snow, with the other Church
authorities, enter heartily into the idea of
observing in the elaborate way suggested, as
being appropriate thus to keep in memory
one of the greatest men of the present cen-
tury.
