who have many daily cares and duties, and
one who is thoughtful of these good sisters
whose devotion through long years of toil
and personal sacrifice, and who in a beauti-
ful home can open her doors and her heart
in generous hospitality, is in the true sense
of the word a benefactress.
	One of the ladies present at that party
has already passed behind the veil, Harriet
Taylor, daughter of Lucy D. Young and
sister of Mrs. Rossiter. How little we
know of the future; on that day her sweet
face and gentle manner reminded the writer
of her sainted mother, whose presence in
days gone by we were wont to see at many
social parties.
	Dear Sister Taylor died suddenly with-
out a word even to those she loved best on
earth, but as the poet said, so we believe—


Those who go—
Feel not the pain of parting, it is they
Who stay behind that suffer.
