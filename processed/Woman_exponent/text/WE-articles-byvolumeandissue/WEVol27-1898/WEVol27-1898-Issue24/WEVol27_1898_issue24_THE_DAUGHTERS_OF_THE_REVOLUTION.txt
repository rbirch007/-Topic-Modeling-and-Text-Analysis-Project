THE DAUGHTERS OF THE REVOLUTION.
	This organization was incorporated April
3, 1899, and has for its object, as set forth
in the articles, which were filed in the
county clerk’s office, the perpetuation of
the patriotic spirit of the men and women 
who achieved American independence, by
commemorating prominent events connect-
ed with the war of the revolution; the en-
couragement of the study of American his-
tory and first, last and all the time to instil
into the hearts of the young the spirit of
liberty that made American independence
an accomplished fact. 
	The incorporators are Emmeline B. Wells,
Sarah F. Smith, Ella W. Hyde, Lucy E.
W. Smith, Isabel M. Whitney Sears, Mary
A. H. Cannon, Maria Y. Dougall, Myra
Y. Rossiter, Caroline Y. Cannon, Susa
Young Gates, Julia P. M. Farnsworth,
Julia Almira Farnsworth, Almira H. Mur-
dock, Emily H. Cannon, Phebe Y. Beattie,
Clarissa S. Williams, Catherine Wells,
Alice S. M. Horne, Claire Clawson and
Minnie Maebelle Snow.
	The officers are: Regent, Mrs. Emme-
line B. Wells; first vice regent, Mrs. Susa
Young Gates; second vice regent, Mrs.
Clarissa S. Williams; recording secretary,
Julia A. Farnsworth; assistant secretary,
Maebelle Snow; corresponding secretary,
Mrs. Lucy Woodruff Smith; treasurer,
Mrs. Mary A. H. Cannon; registrar, Mrs. Phebe
Young Beattie; historian, Mrs. Alice Smith 
Merrill Horne; chaplain, Mrs. Maria Young
Dougall. The State council is composed of
Mrs. Cannon, Mrs. Seats, Mrs. Farnsworth,
Mrs. Smith, Mrs. Rossiter and Mrs. Hyde.
