ANNIVERSARY AT HADEN, IDAHO.

	On Friday, March 17th, the members of
the Haden ward Relief Society held an
entertainment in commemoration of the first
Relief Society organized.  The members of
our society being very limited in number,
it became necessary, in order to make a
success of the occasion, to invite the whole
ward.
	At 7:30 p. m. the company commenced
gathering at the ward meeting house,
which was soon well filled. When all was
quiet opening prayer was offered and appro-
priate remarks were made by President
Martha Little. Then came the reading of
the minutes of the first Relief Society held,
after which came dancing interspersed with


songs, recitations and speeches. Refresh-
ments were served by a committee of young
ladies. All present seemed to partake of
the spirit of the occasion, and manifested a
lively interest in the proceedings.
	After the program had been rendered and
the dancing ceased the entertainment was
dismissed by prayer, and all returned
feeling well satisfied with the evening’s
amusement.
	Our Relief Society has not been able to
hold meetings regularly during the winter
on account of the extremely cold weather
and deep snow, which has been unusually
tedious the past winter.
	Our community has had its share of
sickness and some deaths have occurred,
but with the approach of spring the health
of the people is improving; and seldom has
spring in all its glory been more eagerly
welcomed than at the present time by the
inhabitants of Teton Valley.
		MARTHA LITTLE, President.
		N. G. SMITH, Secretary.
