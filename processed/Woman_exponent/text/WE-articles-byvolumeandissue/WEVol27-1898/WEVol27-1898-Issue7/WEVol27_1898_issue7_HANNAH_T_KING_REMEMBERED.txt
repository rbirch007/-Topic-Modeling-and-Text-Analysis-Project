HANNAH T. KING REMEMBERED.
A very enjoyable little meeting was held
in memory of Sister H. T. King on the
afternoon of August 15, 1898.
	The quiet, pleasant home of Sister Sarah
M. Kimball was the place selected for the
gathering. As the affair was suddenly
thought of and hastily arranged, but a
small matter was anticipated and made of
it. Only a few of the intimate friends and
warm admirers of Sister King could be
called in, previous engagements and ab-
sence from the city preventing the atten-
dance of quite a number who would other-
wise have been present.
The conversation carried on informally
was largely of a religious and spiritual
character, Sisters Zina D. H. Young and
S.M. Kimball giving many gems of wis-
dom, strength, comfort and encouragement
to the younger participants.
Reminiscences of the days of the proph-
ets, Joseph Smith, Brigham Young, Heber
C. Kimball and others of their associates, in
connection with memoirs from the life of
Sister King and quotations from her in-
spired writings all contributed to the form-
ing of a beautiful memorial offering to the
gifted lady in whose honor the meeting
was called.
A suitable repast was daintily served by
Misses Lizzie Kimball and Miss Granger,
(Sister Kimball’s granddaughter,)and much
enjoyed by the company.
Although in her eightieth year and deli-
cate in health, physically, Sister Sarah M.
Kimball’s mind and memory are wonder-
fully acute and active, and her colloquial
powers such as to ensure a rich treat and
valuable points of instruction in some lines,
to any one who may have the happiness to
spend a half hour in her presence. Among
those who were thus favored and who con-
tributed to the pleasure of the occasion on
the day above mentioned, were Sisters John
Alford, Harriet T. Badger, Lydia D. Alder,
Persis Y. Richards, Mary Gray and L. L.
Greene Richards.
