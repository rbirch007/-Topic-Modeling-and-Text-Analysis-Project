BIRTHDAY PARTY.

	Monday, August 8, was the birthday of
the President of the Woman’s Press Club,
Dr. Romania B. Pratt, and the members of
the club planned a surprise for the auspi-
cious occasion. Dr. Pratt is a great favor-
te with her associates not only in the Press
Club but in all places because of her many
fine qualities of head and heart, and withal
a genial manner. Her presence in the sick
room is like a healing balm to the suffering
and this in itself is a boon worth possessing.
	There were not many attended the party
as the club had adjourned previously and a
number were absent from the city; but
there was a sufficient number of brilliant
conversationalists to make the informal
party enjoyable. The ladies of the club
presented their president with a beautiful
picture, (medalion) the subject “Queen
Louise Among Her People,” representing
philanthropy, an appropriate gift for the
doctor. Many fervent congratulations and
good wishes for happy returns of the day,
etc., were showered upon the amiable
president, and gifts of flowers, bouquets of
roses, carnations and other fragrant floral
offerings came in abundance as tributes of
esteem and affection.
	During the evening a discussion of the
most eminent woman (now living) in the
United States was entered into and many
prominent in good works were mentioned,
but finally two who seemed to be the most
conspicuous and eminent in the largest
field of uplifting and greatest achievements
were decided upon, Susan B. Anthony and
Clara Barton, the voting was almost a trib-
ute, Miss Anthony after all having the larg-
er number of votes.
	A delicious repast was delicately served
to the guests, and the ladies and gentlemen
present felt it had been a most delightful
evening.
