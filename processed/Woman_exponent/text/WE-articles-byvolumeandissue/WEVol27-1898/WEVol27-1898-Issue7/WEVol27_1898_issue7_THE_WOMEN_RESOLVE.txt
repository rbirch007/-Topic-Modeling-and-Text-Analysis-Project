THE WOMEN RESOLVE.
	The Woman's Suffrage Association of
Utah county held a meeting August 30, in
Provo. About forty or fifty ladies were in
attendance, four towns outside of Provo
being represented, and regrets were received
from Springville ladies, who were unable
to be present on account of the funeral of
Mrs. Geo. Gifford. The following resolu-
tions were adopted:
	Whereas, An informal understanding has
been sought to be effected between the two
political parties eliminating women from
the state and county tickets at the next
election; and
	Whereas women, have been named as
a weakness upon the ticket and charged
with lacking executive ability and being
the enemy of women refusing to vote for
them, therefore be it
	Resolved, That we the suffragists of
Utah county, in convention assembled, in
view of these publications and utterances of
the press, repudiate the sentiments attri-
buted to women and protest against the
measures proposed.
	Resolved, That we demand a more com-
plete and just recognition and representa-
tion in some measure adequate to the num-
ber of votes which we cast.
	Resolved, That heretofore in consequence
of women relying upon the honor of their
parties for proper and just recognition and
representation, have desisted from active
work for the advancement of women, out-
side of political parties, and whereas we
have been charged with being the enemy
of our own sex, we hereby pledge ourselves
to refute such charges by active work and
public expression, believing that eternal
vigilance is the price of liberty; and be it
further
	Resolved, That we are ready to meet the
official records of women holding office now
provided the records of men elected at the
same time be as closely scanned and placed
parallel.
