THE CANTEEN’S WORK.

My boy! my boy! and is it thus,
Thou to thy mother hast come back,
To thus disgrace thyself and her?
A drunkard and disorderly,
Thou who were e'er thy mother's pride.
O, God! it surely cannot be!
My boy cannot be so debased;
For he was ever pure and brave
And never drank or smoked or swore,

When just a few short months ago,
To serve his country, he left me,
Declaring he would bravely fight
For God and for humanity.
This drunken youth, is it my boy ?
My boy who was so pure and true,
It surely, surely cannot be,
My country e’er would send him thus
Back to his mother’s arms again.
He, for his country went to fight,
And would she tempt him go astray.
And let canteens destroy his soul,
When her protection I should trust,
And give my boy into her care?
Could she betray my confidence
And not look after my dear boy,
When from my care I let him go
To honor and to serve her name?
When mother’s gave their sons to her
Pure, innocent and free from shame,
Would she allow temptations stand
In places where our youth would go?
Would she not try to save their souls,
When human lives they offered her,
To help their country win her cause?
How could such degradation be,
If she protected as she should?
And oh! what misery may come
From her allowing careless ways
To those whose mothers are not near,
To shield their boys from Satan’s wiles.
What sorrow, for the after years,
One lost soul, in a home, may bring,
Far worse than many dear lives lost
In honoring their country’s name;
They are not lost, but only gone
To wait for us at heaven’s gate;
But the lost soul, what misery
‘Twill cause the mother’s heart to feel,
What disappointed hopes and grief
it brings to every one that loved.
How could a country ask for men
And send back drunkards in their place?
O, Father! comfort mother’s hearts
That have been broken as mine is,
He was my only, dear, sweet child,
Now worse than childless I shall be.
Would for his country’s sake he’d died,
And onward sped his soul to God,
To wait till I should be called home,
And meet him in the spirit land.
But now it breaks my heart to think
His soul forever may be lost.
Oh! Father, pray reclaim him yet,
His heart, I know, cannot be bad,
’Tis only vile temptation’s rule
That drags his soul down in the mire,
Oh! strengthen, shield him with thy arm,
That ever has the power to save.
Oh!  hear his mother’s earnest prayer
And save my boy from moral death.
