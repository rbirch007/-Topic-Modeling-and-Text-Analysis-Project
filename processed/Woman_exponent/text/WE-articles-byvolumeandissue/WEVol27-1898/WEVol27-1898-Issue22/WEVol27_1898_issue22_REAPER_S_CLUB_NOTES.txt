REAPER’S CLUB NOTES.
	
	The Reaper’s Club met in the Woman’s
Exponent office, Templeton, on the 20th of
March, 1899.
	An interesting paper was read by Mrs.
Mary L. Morris, on the History of Cortez.
She gave a description of the conquest of
Mexico by the Spaniards under this leader,
mentioned the cruel way in which Monte-
zuma, a native king, had been treated, and
other items of history.
	Mrs. Lucy B. Young related some inci-
dents of her trip to the East, and spoke of a
pleasant visit which she and others had
made to the Kirtland Temple. She said
that after permission had been given the
brethren and sisters sang two hymns. The
first selection was “The Spirit of God like
a fire is burning.” She also alluded to
other meetings she had attended, she en-
joyed these visits very much.
	Current events were given by other
members of the Reaper’s Club.
	Adjourned for two weeks and dismissed
with prayer by Mrs. E. J. Stevenson.
			I. A. DRUCE, Cor. Sec.
