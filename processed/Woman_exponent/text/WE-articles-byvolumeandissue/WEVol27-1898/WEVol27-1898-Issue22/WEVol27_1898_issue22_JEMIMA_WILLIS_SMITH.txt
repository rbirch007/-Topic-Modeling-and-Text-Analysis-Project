	JEMIMA WILLIS SMITH.
	We feel it a duty to record the death of
one of our beloved sisters, Jemima W.
Smith, who departed this life in 1899,
at her home in Snowflake, Arizona.
	We have known her faithfulness, wit-
nessed her sacrifices and works of charity,
her humble and true demeanor, her devo-
tion to her aged husband and her labors in
Relief Society work for many years, and
her good deeds have been inspiring unto
us. For twenty five years she never
missed a sacrament meeting or gathering of
the Relief Society unless detained by sick-
ness. Her work for the dead was not
neglected and for years, when her health
would allow, she went in person to the
Holy Temple. When too feeble she sent
the names of her dead, and always paid for
the work being done. She has been Stake
treasurer for the Relief Society for a num-
ber of years, and we her colaborers offer
these resolutions of respect as a slight
token of our love:
	RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.
	Resolved, That inasmuch as our Stake
treasurer, Jemima W.Smith, has been called
from our midst, we wish to express our
appreciation of her worth.
	Resolved, That we emulate her nobility
of character and unfaltering devotion to the
truth.
	Resolved, That as she has died childless
we desire by some means to perpetuate her
memory.
	Resolved, That these resolutions be re-
corded in our Stake record and a copy pub-
lished in the Woman’s Exponent.
	Emma S. Smith, Sarah Griggs, Mary J.
West, Lucy H. Flake, Frances Wells.
