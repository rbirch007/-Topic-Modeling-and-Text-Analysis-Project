ANOTHER RELIEF SOCIETY.

	Persuant to previous arrangements a fair
representation of ladies of this city met at
the Saint’s Church for the purpose of
effecting the organization of a Relief
Society at St. John’s, Kansas, Jan. 8, 1899.
	Elder Wm. J. Jack was present, and after


the opening exercises stated briefly the
object of the Relief Society, explaining the
benefits to be derived and extended to
others in a social and benevolent way.
	Sister M. E. Taylor and Belle Copening,
of Utah, also spoke encouragingly of the
proposition, and related their experience as
members of similar associations out west.
	In a few well chosen remarks all the 
ladies present signified their willingness to
take hold and do their best to make the 
society a success.
	Officers for the society were nominated
and elected as follows: Mrs. Lucy Brecken-
ridge, president; selection of first and 
second vice presidents postponed until next
meeting; Mrs. Margaret Budge, secretary;
Mrs. Mary J. Bryant, treasurer. The
assembly then selected four ladies as the
visiting committee, Mrs. B. K. Oden, Mrs.
C. Shiller, Mrs. Alice Gooing and Miss
Georgie Oden, after which the society ad-
journed to meet at the same place, Thurs-
day 12th, at 2 p. m. Benediction was pro-
nounced by Mrs. Belle Copening.
	The names enrolled were Lucy Brecken-
ridge, Margaret Budge, Mary J. Bryant,
B. K. Oden, Joanna Miller, Alice Gooing,
Georgie Oden, Annara Baker, Ida Brecken-
ridge, Mary Dixon, Mary E. Taylor, Belle 
Copening, Sister Cooper, Sister Lawhead.
