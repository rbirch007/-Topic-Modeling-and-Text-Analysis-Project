WOMAN’S WORK IN WAR.

	Nowadays one has daily the war and the
news pertaining thereto with all its varia-
tions of battles fought and unfought on sea
and on land. To publish a paper that con-
tained no war news at all would be an ex-
ception to the general rule, for we have
war news the first thing in the morning
and the last thing at night, and one dreams
of the war waking or sleeping; and yet to
give anything like a summary of reliable
war dispatches in a little paper only pub-
lished at intervals of two weeks or more is
out of the question.

mighty faith it is in view of the fevers pre-

weather. When one reads that Gen. Shafter
