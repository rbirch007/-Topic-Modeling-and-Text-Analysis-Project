PIONEER PARK.

	On Monday, July 25, fifty-one years after
the arrival of the Pioneers in the valley of
the Great Salt Lake, the square on which
the first human habitations were built in
this desert land (now a flourishing state)
was publically dedicated with appropriate
and impressive ceremonies, conducted un-
der the auspices of the Mayor and the City
Council. President Wilford Woodruff, the
veteran pioneer now ninety-one years of
age, was seated upon the platform, also
President George Q. Cannon, who came in-
to the valley a few months later, and a few
members of the old City Council of long
ago and other citizens, also a few promin-
ent women, among the number being Mrs.
Zina D. H. Young, Mrs. Jane B. Taylor,
Mrs. Maria L. Nebeker; the two last named
came in 1847. The patriotic music was
inspiring, the singing excellent, opening
prayer by Dr. T. C. Iliff and address by
Mayor Clark both particularly well adapt-
ed to the occasion, the Mayor rehearsing
the most notable events. Our revered
President made the speech of the day, heart-
felt and replete with memories of the time
alluded to.
