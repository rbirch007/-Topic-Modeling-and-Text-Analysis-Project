SAD AND SORROWFUL.

Ah, the sweet young rose of hope is dead—
	'Twill never bloom again
And the tears I shed for the beautiful dead,
	They fall like the desolate rain.

	The sorrow that has fallen so heavily up-
on Brother and Sister Gates of Provo through
the terrible accident on the evening of
July 21, fills all hearts with grief, who know
the family. One stands overwhelmed in
such an hour, and it is only by seeking the
Lord one can find consolation at all, He
has promised and His word cannot fail of
fulfillment:

	“When thro’ fiery trials thy pathway shall lie,
	My grace all sufficient shall  e thy supply.
	The flames shall not hurt thee I only design
	Thy dross to consume and thy gold to refine.”

	Little Beulah Gates, seven years of age,
was a child of promise and of faith in
God, exemplified in the last hour by her
kneeling to pray after (though she knew
it not) she was past all human aid. What
a tribute to her mother was this child’s
dying prayer! and may the beautiful les-
son be impressed upon the parents far and
wide; “teach the children to pray.”
	The funeral services were excellent, we
are told, and great consolation was offered
to the bereaved parents. We know
“God moves in a mysterious way,” and
we must acknowledge His hand in all
things however hard the lesson may be

	And if the ordeal seems cruel
		The father will make it right
	And the brighter thy precious jewel
		Will shine in t e realms of light.
