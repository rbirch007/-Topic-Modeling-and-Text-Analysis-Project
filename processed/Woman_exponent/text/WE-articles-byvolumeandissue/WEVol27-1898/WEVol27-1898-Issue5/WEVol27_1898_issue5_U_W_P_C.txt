U. W. P. C.

	The regular meeting of the U. W. P. C.
was held in the "Exponent" office Tuesday
evening, May 31. The president being ab-
sent the vice-president, Mrs. R. H. Doolan,
presided. Opening prayer Mrs. Phebe C.
Young. The secretary called the roll, re-
sponses being given with original patriotic
sentiments. Mrs. E. B. Wells proposed
the name of Mrs. Kate B. Anderson as a
member, and she was unanimously elected.
Under unfinished business the Curfew law
was discussed with no definite result. The
resignation of Mrs. Miller as alternate to
Mrs. Brown was accepted and Mrs. Rhodes
was appointed in her place.
	Mrs. Luella Rhodes read a carefully pre-
pared historical paper on the "Royal Fam-
ily of Spain and the present Generals."
Mrs. M. J. Lambert kept the members
deeply interested in her eloquent paper on
“Current Events,” including letters from
women’s organizations concerning the war,
and a mother’s letter whose son was killed
on the Maine. General discussion of papers
followed.
	On motion of Mrs. Schiller the meeting
adjourned. Closing prayer by President R.
B. Pratt, after which volunteer refreshments
were thoroughly enjoyed by all present.
