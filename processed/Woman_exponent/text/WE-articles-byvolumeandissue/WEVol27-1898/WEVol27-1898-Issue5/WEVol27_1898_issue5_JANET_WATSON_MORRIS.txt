	Sister Janet Wa son Morris was born in Ren-

with her parents when a child to Glasgow, where 
in 1864 she embraced the Gospel through oppo-
sition on the part of her family. In 1866 she left 
her home and her native land and emigrated to 
Utah, coming by ox team, and endured the dis- 
comforture of the journey with her characteristic 
cheerfulness and willingness to embrace all the 
principles the Lord revealed. 
	She was married to Robert Morris, now Bis op 
of the 11th ward, on December 21, 1867. She was 
the mother of six children, two sons and four 
daughters, her two sons having preceded her to

	A few years ago she visited her native land 
where she succeeded in allaying much prejudice 
concerning Mormonism  She was a first and 
wise counselor in the 11 h ward Relief Society, 
which position she was appointed to fil on the 
7th of June, 1894. She was afflicted for years 
with rheumatism but bore her suffering cheerfully 
She bore a strong testimony to the truth of the 
work of God and was ever ready to do her duty
and to stand up for the principles of right. She
