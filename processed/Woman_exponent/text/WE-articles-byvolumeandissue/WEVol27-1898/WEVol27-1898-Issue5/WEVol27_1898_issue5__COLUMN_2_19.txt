At length with a slow, stately motion,
	The stranger a step drew more near,
And Leah her startled eyes lifted,
	All darkened and heavy with fear.
He opened his arms to enfo[-]d her.
	The pall drew aside from his brow,
And lo! the dear face of her father
	Smiled lovingly over her now.

What glorious flash no[-] illumines
	The sweet face so pallid and worn!
What luster those heavy eyes brightens,
	Light sunshine above a dark storm!
“Papa!” cried the sweet voice, and lifting
	Frail hands like the wings of a bird,
“Goodbye, dear mamma,” low she whispered,
	And sprang to his arms at the word

There were sighs from the lone hearted mother,
	Caressing the pale, lovely clay;
There were tears from the sister and brother,
	For Leah had vanished away;
But the smile of her joy softly lingered
	Around the cold, beautiful face,
That seemed to her dear ones remaining
	A token of heavenly grace.
