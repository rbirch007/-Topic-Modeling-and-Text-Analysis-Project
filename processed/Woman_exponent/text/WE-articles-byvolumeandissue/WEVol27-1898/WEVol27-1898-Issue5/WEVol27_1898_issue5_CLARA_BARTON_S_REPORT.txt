CLARA BARTON’S REPORT. 

	New York, July 16.—A telegram re-
ceived today by the executive committee of
the Red Cross from Miss Clara Barton dated
Playa Del Este, July 15, is as follows:
	Santiago has surrendered. I came from
the front yesterday in a pouring rain.
Fever is suspected here. Siboney is burned.
Dr. Lesser and Mrs. Lesser and all their
nurses are in the fever hospital two miles
distant. I saw them today and all are doing
well. Four army surgeons of the St. Paul
are with us, there being no other provision
for them.
	We are feeding the refugees of Sibony,
and many thousands at Eurmisea, by rail.


Elwell is feeding them at Caney by army
wagons, and 20,000 by pack mules, 15
miles travel.
	Commander McCalla of the Marblehead,
called yesterday for 100,000 rations, medi-
cines and clothing for the refugees in the
woods in the country surrounding Guan-
tanamo. Nothing is known of boats or of
aid of any kind from any other source.
State of Texas has fed all the wounded at
the front, and is helping them home.
	All members of the Red Cross are in per-
fect health, thoroughly organized.
	A telegram has been received from Red 
Cross Agent Kent at Jacksonville who
states that Col. Maus, surgeon-in-chief,
has just returned from Miami and reports
much sickness there, with a great need of
milk and ice. The Red Cross launch
Moyner has been repaired and will be
ready to leave Jacksonville Monday, for
Key West.
	Advices just received from Key West in-
dicate that the Red Cross will reach Santia- 
go Tuesday next with the much needed
supplies and surgeons.
