	The Woman’s National Cuban League 
at Washington, which was an active factor 
in furthering the Cuban cause last fall, has 
reorganized, adopted the old charter and re-
elected its former director-general, Mrs. 
Clara Bell Brown, and its vice director-
general, Mrs. Rosa Louisa Townsend, a 
great granddaughter of one of the signers 
of the Declaration of Independence. The 
purpose of the reorganized league is to give 
aid and comfort to American soldiers and 
sailors. 
	The army hospital ship, Relief, which 
has been fitting out at New York during 
the past month, is the most perfect vessel 
of its kind in the world. As compared 
with the Solace, the navy hospital ship, the 
Relief is superior in structure, speed, ac- 
commodation and equipment. Its berths
for sick and wounded number 1,300, and 
nearly a hundred additional patients can be 
cared for in an emergency. The ship will 
carry a crew under contract, not belonging 
either to the army or navy. The nurses 
will number ten male and eight female
The former are from Mills Training School 
in New York City, and the women from 
John Hopkins Hospital Training School at 
Baltimore. Ex.
