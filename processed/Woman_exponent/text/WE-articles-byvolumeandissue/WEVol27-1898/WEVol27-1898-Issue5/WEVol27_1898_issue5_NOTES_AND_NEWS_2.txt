NOTES AND NEWS.

	The bill raising the age of protection for 
girls in the District of Columbia to eighteen 
passed the U. S. House of Representatives 
before adjournment. 
	Until the remonstrants produce a second 
Clara Barton, it will remain true that the 
most effective type of patriotism has come 
from the ranks of the suffragists. 
	On “Club Woman’s Day,” at the Trans-
Mississippi Exposition, Mrs. Kate Tannatt 
Woods of Salem, read a paper on “Club
Courtesies,” and Mrs. Ellen A. Richardson 
of Boston, spoke on “The Keystone of the
Educational Arch.”
	A Law Committee consisting of Mrs. 
Halsted Smith, Rome; Mrs. Frances Akin, 
Cartersville; Mrs. Walter Hill, Macon; and 
Mr. E. Cameron Reed, Atlanta, have fin-
ished their arduous task of making extracts 
from the code of Georgia upon the law of 
the State regarding woman’s legal status. 
The work is well done and is receiving uni-
versal praise wherever the pamphlet is re-
ceived.
