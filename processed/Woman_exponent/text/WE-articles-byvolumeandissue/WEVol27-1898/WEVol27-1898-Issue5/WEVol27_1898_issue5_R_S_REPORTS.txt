R. S. REPORTS 
		MARICOPA STAKE. 
	Report of Relief Society Stake conference 
held June 16, 1898. The sisters compris-
ing the Maricopa Stake Relief Society held 
their quarterly conference in the Society 
hall with First Counselor Francelle E. Rob-
son presiding. President Mabel A. Hakes
was with us but was in very feeble health, 
also Counselor Sarah N. Johnson who was 
very poorly in health, also Stake President 
C. R. Hakes and Counselor Henry C. 
Rogers and other leading sisters 
	Meeting was opened with the usual ex-
ercises. All the wars were represented in 
good condition generally, showing a good
spirit among the sisters. The topics dwelt on 
were for our general good, storing grain 
now that we are just reaping a bounteous 
harvest, leading exemplary lives, and many 
testimonies were borne by the sisters. Two 
Lamanite sisters bore strong testimonies to 
the truth of the Gospel, which was very in-
teresting to all. These sisters, of whom 
there are quite a number, were very sincere, 
they felt glad to have their names in rec- 
ord, as they saw the secretary taking notes 
of what was said. They speak through an 
interpreter. President Hakes and Counsel-
or Rogers exhorted the sisters to come out 
to meeting oftener, also spoke of the duties 
of the Relief Society, said we should first 
seek the Kingdom of God and all things 
will be added. All felt that we had had a 
good time together. 
	Meeting adjourned for three months. 
Benediction by H. C. Rogers.
