A SUCCESSFUL CELEBRATION.

	The minutes of the 24th ward Relief
Society, March 17, 1899, Margaret Night-
ingale presiding. The entertainment given
by the members of the 24th ward Relief
Society in honor of the first organization
which was organized by the Prophet Joseph
Smith, was a success in every way. Opened
by singing “The spirit of God like a fire
is burning.” Prayer by Brother Allen.
	The following sisters bore their testi-
monies to the truth of the work of God;
Sisters Nightingale, Duncanson, Water-
fall, Attley and Keller. Brother Gill spoke
upon the good work the society was en-
gaged in, that the Lord would bless
one and all in their noble work.
	Delicious refreshments were served by
Sisters Gill and Ames with their numerous
aids. All seemed to enjoy themselves
and a good spirit prevailed. Twenty seven
new names were given in to become mem-
bers of the society. About $8.00 was
taken in, the admission being ten cents
$6.00 went to the General Board to help
defray traveling expenses of delegates to
the National Council and other neces-
sary expenses and obligations pertaining
to Relief Society work. The members
of the society were all there.
	MAGARET NIGHTINGALE, Pres.
