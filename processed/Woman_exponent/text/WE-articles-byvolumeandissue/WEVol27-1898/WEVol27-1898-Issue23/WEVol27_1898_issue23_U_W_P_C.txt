U.W. P. C.

	The regular monthly meeting of the
U. W. P. C. was held in the “Exponent”
office, Friday evening, March 31, 1899.
Prayer by Mrs. Doolan, after which the
secretary called the roll, responses being
given by quotations from Cowper. By re-
quest of Mrs. Ruth M. Fox, she was per-
mitted to read her interesting paper on Cur-
rent Events previous to the business. The
subject of having one special evening for
the subject “Cliff Dwellers” was discussed,
but it was decided to have Mrs. Lawler
read Mrs. Rhodes’ (who was out of the
city) paper that evening. It proved of
great interest to all present, discussion fol-
lowed. The secretary then read a com-
munication from the free traveling library
committee acknowledging the receipt of one
dollar from the Press Club; one from Mrs.
Lulu Lee Stiehl suggesting the appoint-
ment of a delegate to the Mothers’ Assem-
bly. Mrs. L. D. Alder was elected a dele-
gate. Mrs. Margaret A. Caine was re-
appointed a delegate to the Council of
Woman’s Clubs. The regular program
was postponed for an evening with the
“Cliff Dwellers,” the time and place to be
determined later. The meeting then ad-
journed.
