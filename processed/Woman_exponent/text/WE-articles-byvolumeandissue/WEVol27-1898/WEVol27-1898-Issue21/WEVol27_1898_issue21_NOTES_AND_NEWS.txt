NOTES AND NEWS.

	Progressive Chinese women have started
a weekly publication called the Feminine
Magazine. The staff consists of women only.
	Women have more sense about machin-
ery in a minute, says Edison, than men
have in a lifetime, and to confirm his belief
he has two hundred women on his pay roll.
	The newly elected president of Sorosis
who has been acting president since the
resignation of Mrs. Mary Dame Hall, Mrs.
D. T. Dennison, is sister to Mrs. May N.
Knaggs, president of the Michigan Wo-
man’s Suffrage Association.
	Mrs. Fanny Humphreys Gaffney, presi-
dent of the National Council of Women,
has been reelected president of the New York
City Society for Political Study. The
society meets every Tuesday afternoon at
226 West 58th St.
	The Countess Schimmelman, who has
given up a royal palace to preach to the
poor and unfortunate, spoke to the convicts
at Joliet, Illinois, on March 12, making an
earnest appeal which affected many of the
men to tears. Later she addressed the wo-
men prisoners.
	Immediately after the Speaker’s gavel
dismissed the 55th Congress, some one on
the floor of the House started up “My
Country ’Tis of Thee.” Immediately others
joined, the galleries caught up the strain
and all the people rose and joined in the
national anthem until the walls echoed to
the sound. At the close General Hender-
son started “Auld Lang Syne” which was
taken up as eagerly; then J. M. Mitchell
led the voices in the “Red, White and Blue.”
Then after cheers for Dewey and Wheeler
and the army and navy heroes generally,
House and galleries grew quiet as they
joined in singing the Doxology.
