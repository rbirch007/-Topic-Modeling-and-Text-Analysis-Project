ANNIVERSARY DAY AT DENVER.
	By special invitation the Denver branch
of the Relief Society of the Church of Jesus
Christ of Latter-day Saints met at the home
of Sister Jane W. Manning Skolfield at
1205 Evans St., to commemorate “Anni-
versary Day.”
	The meeting was called to order at 2:30
p. m., by President Eva E. Haley, ten
members responding to roll call. The
guests of the afternoon were Sisters Rosa
Koch of Manassa, Dollie Russell of Cone-
jos, Mamie Dihgee and Mrs. J. T. Jones of


Denver. The meeting opened with sing-
ing “How great the wisdom and the love.”
Prayer by Sister Skolfield. Singing,
“Though deepening trials throng, your
way.” The recording secretary, Sister
Mary W. Ensign, read the minutes of the
previous meeting which were accepted.
She also read the minutes of the first Relief
Society organized at Nauvoo and repub-
lished in the “Exponent,” and afterwards
a statistical and financial report of our
society since its reorganization in Septem-
ber 1898, showing a great deal of valuable
work done by the few active members.
	The corresponding secretary then read a
letter from Mrs. E. B. Wells, the official notices
regarding Anniversary Day and an extract
from the Deseret News of the Utah ladies’
visit to Washington, D. C.
	Sisters Sophia Luce, Mary Ensign and
Florence Benjamin sang as a trio, “O, my
Father.” A very instructive and carefully
prepared paper by Sister Hannah E. Long
was read, entitled “How the Home and
the Mother Influence our Lives.”
	At the conclusion of the program light,
refreshments were served, by the hostess,
assisted by Sister Hannah E. Long. The
house was tastefully decorated in pink and
white, the blinds were drawn to shut out
the dismal, stormy day, numbers of lamps
were lighted, shading from red to white.
	The meeting closed with a vote of thanks 
to the hostess for the pleasant afternoon and 
singing “The time is far spent.” Prayer
by Sister Ellen Rosenvall.
