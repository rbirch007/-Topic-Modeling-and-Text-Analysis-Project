	On the afternoon of the tea party among
the many guests were quite a number
whose ancestors participated in the original
tea party. Among those was a Miss Treat,
whose grandmother, Helen de St. Pry, was
a god-daughter of Lafayette. She married
Lieut. Treat who was stationed at Castle
island from 1776 to 1798. Another guest
was young Edmund Sears who celebrated
his birthday by attending the reception, for
his ancestors had 125 years before helped to
throw the tea overboard. Other guests
whose ancestors participated in the historic
affair were Mrs. Walter Tower of Melrose,
a descendant of John Spurr; H. G. Hewes
of Braintree, descendant of George Robert
Twelves Hewes; Mrs. J. D. Ronimus, des-
cendant of James Starr, all of the “party”
and Mrs S. Agnes Sprague, whose ances-
tor was of the same notable company.
	Many of these told pleasing anecdotes of
their ancestors, which had been handed
down in the families, and of which they are
deservedly proud. The Fadette orchestra
played.
	There was no attempt at any formal
speechmaking. In fact it would have been
almost impossible to do so on account of the
crowded state of the rooms. Miss Hunt
did say to a little circle surrounding her 
that she believed in the patriotism of work-
ing for one’s country, especially along the
line, of good citizenship, and that next to
the work of the home and the church should
come that of the patriotic societies, which
strive to keep alive the memories of the his-
toric past to inspire the youth to noble ef-
forts and the highest type of life in the
future.
