TO SISTER MARY A FREEZE.

How oft as we journey on through life,
Does the heart grow weak from its toil and
	strife;
How oft we feel that the heavy load,
Is all too much for the long, long road! 
But a cheering voice in the stillness heard,
Sends strength through the ambient air,
Or a tender thought from a sister’s lips
Makes lighter the cross we bear.

How many eyes in the flight of years,
Are always moist with bitter tears,
Till the fountain of sorrow at last is dry,
And only the pent up soul can cry;
But an earthly angel whispers hope,
Though it seems but an idle dream,
And that hopeful whisper paves the way, 
Through which heaven’s light may stream.

How many discouraged ones there be
Who the sequel of life can so dimly see,
Who faint or falter at every turn,
Finding courage and faith so hard to learn!
To such a beautiful word is given,
A lesson of holy love,
And they who for earth will not brave the
	storm
Will try for the life above. 

There are many others whose stalwart forms
Are bowed and bent with life’s heavy storms;
Of toil they have always had their share,
They are weary now of the part they bear,
To the hearts of these tried, feeble ones,
There's a source of joy untold
In whispers of the deeds of worth
They have done in days of old.

Oh, these words of love! how much they do
To make us better—our hearts more true!
How much they help on the weary road!
How much they govern our last abode!
Yet oft in selfish hearts confined
Are the thoughts which thus might bless, 
And they never render the world more bright,
Nor make its sorrows less.

But the world of hope in which you live,
The words of comfort you often give,
The kindly letters your hand has penned 
To son or brother or distant friend,
Not only brighten the jeweled crown
Which you in heaven will wear,
But help to swell the throng who hope
To meet and love you there.
