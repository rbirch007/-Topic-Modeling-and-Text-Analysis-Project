RELIEF SOCIETY IN OMAHA.

	The members of the Omaha branch of
the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day
Saints met Oct. 25, 1898, at 3 p. m., at the
home of Sister Clara M. Fredricksen, 1604
No. 29 St., Omaha, Nebraska, for the pur-
pose of organizing a Relief Society. There
were present Apostle Franklin D. Richards,
Sister Jane S. Richards, first counselor in
the general presidency of the Relief Society,
Emmeline B. Wells, general secretary,
Josephine Richards West, Bishop Peter
Barton and Ellen A. Barton of Kaysville,
Utah, Elder J. L. Hatch, president of
Omaha branch, Elders R. E. Yeates and
Daniel Mackay, Sisters Mary M. Miller,
Mary E. Blixt, Addie S. Stone, Ida A.
Peterson, Anna K. Gregerson, Caroline C.
E. Peterson, Mary M. Weeks, Clara M.
Fredricksen, Elizabeth Hover, Caroline S.
Tromler and Anna B. Olson.
	Meeting was called to order at 3 o’clock
p. m. by Elder J. L. Hatch. The hymn
which begins as follows was sung: “How
firm a foundation.” Prayer by Elder R. E
Yeates. The hymn which begins “Now
let us rejoice” was sung. The meeting
was then presided over by Apostle Richards.
	Sister Richards expressed her joy and
satisfaction in being present with us today.
