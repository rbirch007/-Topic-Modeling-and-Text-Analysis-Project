Teach them virtue and self denial. Sister

necessary that mothers should attend the
Sabbath school as much as anything else,
by blessing we are blessed. Our children
may be careless and wayward, but if we do
our part. they will yet come back, God will
keep a watch care over them. Be careful
how we spend our lives. By careful
thought we progress We must not lose
sight of the fact that it is necessary to have
trials, if we staid in the same place there
would be no advancement. If it is hard
for us today do as well as we can for it may
be harder for us tomorrow.
	Elder L. John Nuttall spoke at some
length on the needs and necessities of the
Relief Society. Said that each president
should take a pride in her society and make
it the best, the Prophet Joseph said the duty
of this society was to save souls, gave
several ways how this could be done. It
was the duty of the sisters to learn the
Gospel that they might be able to convert
others, many women would be called upon
missions and it was necessary to inform our
selves that we could do credit to the church
to which we belonged. He read extracts
from the minutes of the first organization
and commented upon it. Meeting closed
by singing “Now let us rejoice in the day of
salvation.” Prayer by Bishop Robertson.
	An officers meeting was called just after
the afternoon session. Elder Nuttall there
explained many things that were of great
importance to the sisters.
	An evening session was held, the choir
sang "Behold the Savior comes,” prayer by
Elder C. R. Johnson. Sang “Ye wander-
ing nations.” Elder Nuttall occupied most
of the time in a very instructive and in-
teresting manner; spoke of the first or-
ganization of the Relief Society its aim and
object, how it had been a great help to the
church, it being the first organization of
women on the earth and that the Prophet
Joseph had said he had turned the key for
women, how many organizations of women
had been effected since. and the good we
could do providing we never lost the spirit
of the gospel, we should all declare to our-
selves that “As for me and my house I in-
tend to serve the Lord.’’
	Sister Estella Colard sang “My Mountain
Home.”

Choir sang “Behold the Mountain of the

	Saturday, 9:30: Annie Larson presiding.
Choir sang “Sweet is the work my God my
King,” prayer by Elder Samuel Rowley.
Sang, “Are we yet alive.”
	Sister Alder spoke of the inventions
that are in our day. It was a day of ad-
vancement, if we were not careful we
would be left behind. Urged the sisters to
cultivate themselves; we had no time to
lose. We should brighten up our mind
and make ourselves useful; explained
clearly how much good the Exponent had
been to the sisters, was anxious that the
members of the Relief Society should sus-
tain it. Advised the sisters to make a
study of the Book of Mormon. It was
necessary for the president to study the
members and help them to cultivate the
talent that was in them: we should make
confidents of our daughters and draw them
nearer to us that they might in return
make confidents of us, give them some-
thing to do in the Relief Society.
	Elder Nuttall was the next speaker. He
endorsed the remarks of the former speaker,
spoke of the difference in early times and
now educationally, read some of the min-
utes of the Relief Society held in the days
of the Prophet Joseph, said the sisters had
the right to lay hands on the sick.
	Stake Counselor Pulispher was pleased
with the conference and very thankful the
visitors had met with us.
	Stake Gounselor Josie E. Childs pre-
sented the general and stake officers of the
society which were unanimously sustained.
President Annie Larsen said the next con-
ference would be at Ferron Sang “Joy to
the world” Benediction by Elder Thomas
