			EMERY STAKE.
	Minutes of the Relief Society Conference,
of Emery State. Held at Huntington,
Oct. 31st, 1898. Annie U. Larsen presid-
ing. Meeting commencing 10 a. m. by
the choir singing, “Come, Oh Thou King
of Kings,’’ under the leadership of Profes-
sor Hardie. Prayer by Brother Hardie.
The choir sang, “The happy day is rolling
on.” President Annie U. Larson, spoke
at some length, gave a report of the Stake.
Said, she with her counselors, had made a
visit to all the branches since our last con-
ference had found the sisters anxious to
adhere to counsel. Many had and were
building halls and granaries and storing
up wheat for time of need. There was a
steadily growing interest in silk culture.
Many had planted trees and cuttings and
some had raised a few worms; she had on
exhibition some beautiful cocoons and a
twist of silk that had been raised in Emery
County; she was pleased that the sisters
were willing to profit by the advice of the
stake board. After which Huntington,
Cleveland, Castle Gate, Orangeville, Castle
Dale, and Ferron were represented. Each
president substantiating the report of
President Larson. Bishop Johnson,of Hun-
tington spoke to the sisters, a short time en-
couraged them to lay up grain and hoped
the brethren would aid them what they
could.
	Congregation sang, “We thank thee O
God for a Prophet.” Benediction by Brother
M. E. Johnson.
