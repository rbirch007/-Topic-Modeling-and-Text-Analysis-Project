LETTER FROM SANTA ROSA.

	I have taken the “Exponent” from the
beginning of its existence, and I shall be
glad to know how much is due.
	By a letter received today I learn of the
death of Sister Staines, Brother Goddard
and Brother C. M. C[-]nnon. The latter I
did not know personally, but presume he
is a young man. The other two I can re-
joice to know that like our dear Sarah M.
Kimball they are freed from the suffering
of this mortal body.
	I seem to hear the echo of the Halleujah!
The hearty, joyous welcome! The shouts
of victory of the tried and true as they pass
one by one to the realms of eternal bliss.
	I sincerely trust that every one will put
hes shoulder to the wheel and save the
friend of woman, the dear “Exponent”
from shipwreck from the carelessness of its
officers, or the subordination of its crew.
	I have a large chest full of “Exponents”
which I hope to have bouud some day.
Of course there are many other choice selec-
tions and letters there, too, which I hope to
haul over and glean from if life holds out
and other cares do not accumulate.
	They may spoil me here, as grandma’s
are scarce and all hands are anxious to help
make my stay pleasant, and profitable to
themselves from the questions asked.
	I very much regret that there is not a
branch of our Church here, but I am
pleased to find friends whom I met long
ago who have a spirit of inquiry, and have
called to welcome me back again.
	President Woodruff must have been in-
spired when he presented Bancroft’s His-
story of Utah to the free library of Califor-
nia. It seems like an arrow of light
through the thick darkness, and has the
effect of dispelling the ignorance of many
people and their sympathies are aroused
for the injustices done our people, how far
this extends I do not know as all classes are
not in a condition to reach this knowledge;
but I find that those who are feel that this
is a reliable work.
	Trusting that you may receive timely
aid, I remain,
			Your loving sister,
