DR. ROBERT’S NURSING CLASS. 
	The nurse’s class taught by Dr. Margaret 
C. Roberts in the Templeton for the last 
six months or more, held its graduating ex-
ercises on Tuesday Dec. 20, Counselor An-
nie T. Hyde presiding in the absence of 
President Horne. Dr. Roberts gave the in-
structions free in the interest and under the 
auspices of the Relief Society in this Stake 
of Zion. The members of the class acquit-
ted themselves admirably, and the Dr. her-
self read a very able and highly instructive
paper, (which we would gladly have pub-
lished if space would permit.) Congratula-
tory remarks were made by President B. 
W. Smith, E. B. Wells and L. D. Alder, 
also Counselors Clara C. Cannon and 
Annie T. Hyde; Elder C. W. Penrose oc-
cupied a few moments. Sister Hyde in be-
half of the Stake Board presented to Dr. 
Roberts in a neat and happy speech a hand-
some rocking chair in appreciation of her 
generous labors, and the class through one 
of the students, Sister Jones, presented a 
silver cake dish filled with lovely flowers. 
Other gifts and tokens of love suitable to 
the happy occasion were made and the good 
spirit manifested itself very perceptibly. If 
the graduating nurses will be as conscien-
tious in their duties as the Dr. has been in 
giving them instruction the benefit to the 
society will be productive of great good.
