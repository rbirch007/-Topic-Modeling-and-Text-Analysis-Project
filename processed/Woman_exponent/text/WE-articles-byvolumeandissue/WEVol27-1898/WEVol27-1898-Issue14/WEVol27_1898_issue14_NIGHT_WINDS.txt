NIGHT WINDS.

The cold night wind is moaning
	And sighing amid the trees;
It flies o'er the wooded highland
	And sinks o’er the downy leas.
It catches the sails of the vessel
	That ploughs through the ocean wide;
And wafts its streamers landward
	With the strong and rolling tide.
But O, how it sobs at the window,
	And wails as it rushes by;
Like cries of the souls of the lost ones,
	Who trembled and feared to die.
Far away in the dreary distance
	I hear it sighing still,
And my heart but echoes t[-]e sadness
	That nothing of heart can fill.
And now it groans like the living
	When life’s faint spark has fled;
As they bow in deepest sorrow
	Around the newly dead.
And now it sounds like music
	From a harp of a thousand strings;
And then like sweetest melody
	That earthly chorus sings.
Now it sounds like a living thing
	That is haunted and full of pain;
And then it weeps for the doomed ones,
	Whose sins arise again.
And then it laughs in derision,
	And mocks at human woe;
Then rushing it reaches the mountains,
	Then over to valleys below.
And now like a saddening memory
	It calls up the troubled Past;
A harvest of hopes that were blighted,
	Too radiant and sunny to last.
Now again it cries like an infant,
	And now is mad with glee;—
O, God save the father fishermen
	Far out on the stormy sea.
This wind is playing with heartstrings,
	As it roams abroad tonight;
O, pray that the storm-tossed mariner
	May see the morning’s light.
Down by the open fireplace
	Sits Fear, so pallid and cold;
While the wind is rattling the window,
	In its dance of the demons bold.
But O, when it sobs like the human
	I think my heart will break!
Then out in the deepest darkness
	Steals a prayer for the wanderer's sake.
Again it sounds like an army
	Marching and tramping along;
Now the thunder of heavy cannon,
	Is heard with the victor’s song.


Then over the hills in the distance 
	The sound is lost and gone;
While brightening far to the eastward 
	Comes the morning pale and wan.
Now its moans are fast receding,
	As it softly goes to sleep;
In the rock-bound, foaming caveens
	Of the mighty ocean’s deep.
