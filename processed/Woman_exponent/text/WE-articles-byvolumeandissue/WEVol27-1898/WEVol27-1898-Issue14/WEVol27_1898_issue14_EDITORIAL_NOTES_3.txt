EDITORIAL NOTES.

	We have been shown today the drawing
for a very artistic map of the the pioneer
route of the Mormon people journeying
from the city of Nauvoo to the valley of
the Great Basin. The work is to be litho-
graphed and placed upon the market. It
will be suitable for every home and for
public buildings. A pocket edition is also
to be prepared and issued.
	The agents of the “Exponent” in all the
several wards and Stakes of Zion, and also
individual subscribers indebted for the
paper are urgently requested to settle all
old accounts at the earliest date possible.
This becomes an absolute necessity on ac-
count of the change that takes place in the
management of the Deseret Evening News.
The “Exponent” will not be sent to any
subscriber after January 1899, unless pre-
paid. All delinquents will as soon as pos-
sible receive written notices of bills due, in
addition to the printed matter. 
	One of the notable features of the past
year, 1898, and one in which women are
greatly interested is the document of the
Czar of Russia toward a proposed treaty
for universal peace, and coming as it did
from the most autocratic of rulers it does
seem indicative of a world-wide movement
for the arbitration of all serious difficulties
among nations, and a general tendency to-
wards the amicable settlement of hostilities,
and finally the result may be a ceasation of
warfare altogether, for which good time
coming all who desire the true welfare of
humanity should be grateful.
