EDITORIAL NOTES.

	The "Exponent" acknowledges the gift of
a picture group of beautiful women, the


General Board of the Primary Associations
of Zion. Not only were we delighted with
the gift in itself, but knowing each one
personally and the individual grace of soul
that shines out from the eyes, it is doubly
dear, and as we gaze and admire we recall
the many lovely traits in each one’s char-
acter, and incidents in each one’s life that
are admirable. The likeness, too, is very
good and the picture altogether does credit
to the association. They are certainly faces
that denote intelligence and intense spiritual
power. Every Stake board in Zion who
can afford one should purchase one of them
to hang where the children can become ac-
quainted and familiar with the founders of
the Primary work.
	Among the handsome displays for Christ-
mas the new book store of George Q. Can-
non Sons and Company has certainly one of
the very finest, and perhaps equal in quality
of goods in their line to any establishment
in the city, if it does not excel. The goods
are all in style and the prices are on an
average with the quality. In fact they do
keep an excellent line of stationery and
materials in that line. This year they have
fancy china ware, rare vases and such orna-
ments as are suitable for Xmas gifts and a
fine assortment of pictures, principally
medallion, subjects new in style. The “Ex-
ponent’’ can safely recommend their goods
and also the attention one receives in being
served. The new store itself is very
attractive and fitted up in excellent taste,
and everything seems in keeping within
and without.
