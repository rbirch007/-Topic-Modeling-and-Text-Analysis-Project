	President James E. Steele said he was
pleased to meet in Relief Society conference.
He always liked to be on time so as to hear
the petitions that go up to the Father and
partake of the spirit that we may be in pos-
session of it in our conference. It is neces-
sary that we should come together that the
people may be instructed and encouraged.
The Lord sees the needs of His people and
thus we have these privileges. He could
see in meeting with those who did not at-
tend these conferences and did not partake
of the spiritual food, that they were not so
energetic. It looked as though the re-
sponsibility of this work were resting upon
a few. An object in coming to meeting
should be to be fed spiritual food and in-
structed. The reward is to those who are
faithful all the day long. Spoke upon the
storing of grain; hoped the sisters in this
Stake would add two thousand more bush-
els this season to their grain. Urged the
sisters to use their surplus quilts in blessing
the poor and keeping them from suffering
with the cold. Said the more he became
acquainted with the organization the broad-
er and grander it was, prayed God to bless
us that we might enjoy this conference.
	President E. J. Bennett wished there had
been more present to hear the words of
President Steele and the spirit they were
given in, said we were making a record for
time and all eternity.
	Meeting then closed by singing “Lord
dismiss us.” Benediction.
	Afternoon session opened by singing
“The time is far spent.” Prayer was of-
fered by Patriarch O. Harmon. Singing,
“O, ye mountains high.” Minutes of the
afternoon session of the previous conference
were read and accepted, after which Sister
Kate Bennett was released as Stake treasur-
er of the Relief Society, and sustained and
set apart as assistant secretary, and Sister
Alice Boomer was released as assistant
treasurer, and sustained and set apart as
Stake treasurer to the Relief Society. Re-
ports were then called for and the Ammon
society was reported by President Richard-
son. She said meetings were not attended
so well of late on account of sickness. They
had built a granary, had 235 bushels of
grain.
	Counselor Dabell reported the Grant soci-
ety. Said they had paid out fifteen dollars
of late for charitable purposes.
	Labelle society was reported by Sister
Clifford, secretary; Rudy society, by Presi-
dent Clark.
	Emma C. Rushton, Stake counselor,
thought we had good reports in today,
but they could be improved. Noticed the
sisters who attended these meetings were
generally on hand, it was those that did not
attend to these duties that needed waking
up. Felt we had none too much time and
we should try and visit those who were
neglectful.
	Stake Secretary E. S. Brunt thought we
did not study sufficiently so as to be able to
instruct our children in the Gospel, let us
keep good books in the way of our children,
and not the light literature of to day. The
Lord desired to see our children saved and
He was sending fishers and hunters to
bring them in the path of their duty.
