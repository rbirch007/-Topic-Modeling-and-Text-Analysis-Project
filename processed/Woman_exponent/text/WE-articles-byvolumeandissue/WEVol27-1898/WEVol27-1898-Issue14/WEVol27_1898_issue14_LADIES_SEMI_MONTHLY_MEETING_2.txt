LADIES’ SEMI-MONTHLY MEETING

	Semi-monthly meeting held November,
26th, 1898, in the 14th ward hall, President
M. I. Horne presiding, opened by singing,
“Let those who would be Saints indeed;”
prayer Sister Brown; singing “Zion stands
with hills surrounded;” Minutes read and
approved. President M. I. Horne said,
“this is a proving day and a pruning day;
we have our liberty, our choice, our agency,
we can serve God or not; if we attend to
our prayers and our duties we shall not go
astray, so said Joseph Smith, “Satan has
great power in deceiving and leading the
people away.” Exhorted all to be careful
of seducing spirits. Spoke of the suffer-
ings of Paul the Apostle how he had been
tried, said she felt well and thanked all for
the love expressed by her sisters on her
birthday, she thanked the Lord that He
had blessed her in raising her children and
begged all young mothers to ask God con-
tinually for His support to help them fill
their missions as wife and mother. Spoke
of the good seed we might sow day by day,
if we live as God wished us too. An-
nounced that the first Saturday every three
months would be Relief Society conference
and next Saturday was the appointed day.
Sisters Peterson, Liddle, Waterfall, Mary
P. Silver, Jones, Barton and Irvine bore
testimony of God’s goodness to them and
said many good things, in appreciation of
the blessings of the Saints. Congregation
sang, "Arise O glorious Zion.”
	Counselor Bathsheba Smith spoke of the
pernicious habit of smoking, thought we
might influence boys we meet, to avoid the
use of the tobacco, felt parents should rule


with kindness, and love. Sisters Ellis,
Cooper and others each spoke a few
words and bore their testimony.
	President Horne said we must expect
persecution, our Savior told us this, but we
should all be thankful that the knowledge
of the Gospel cannot be taken from us.
Meeting adjourned for two weeks. Sang
Doxology, Benediction.
