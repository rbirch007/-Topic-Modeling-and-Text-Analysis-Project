	Another incident is recalled in which a
father figures and a teacher (without the
least disrespect to the office of a teacher.)
One evening while sitting reading an inter-
esting article in the Juvenile, the gentleman
in question called for my husband to assist
him in his rounds as a teacher. While
waiting for him to make ready he spoke of
the Juvenile, as he noticed I had been read-
ing in it.
	“Yes,” he said, “it is a nice little maga-
zine, but I am not taking it now, can’t af-
ford it. My children are reading a nice
continued story in an Eastern paper we are
taking. They can hardly wait for the time
for it to come.”
	Oh! let us as mothers in Israel choose
good reading for our children, that we may
do nothing that will in any way tarnish
their characters; and remember the beauti-
ful poem which says,
“Build well the temple ye mothers of men;
For know ye are builders for glory or shame,
Know ye are building for weakness or might,
Know ye are building for darkness or light.”
