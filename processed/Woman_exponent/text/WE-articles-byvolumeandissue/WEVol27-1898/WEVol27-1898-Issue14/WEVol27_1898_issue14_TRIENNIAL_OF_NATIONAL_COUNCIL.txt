TRIENNIAL OF NATIONAL COUNCIL

	The National Council of Women of the
United States will hold its triennial in
Washington, D. C., at the Universalist
church, corner of G. and Tenth streets,
meetings commencing February 12 to 18,
1899. There will be ten public sessions
and two very important business meetings,
besides Sunday services.
	At this triennial of the National Council
new officers will be elected for the next
term of three years, and the subject of the
International Council in London, England.
which is to be held the last week in June
of this year, will be an important feature of
the work to be considered.
	The National Council of Women of the
United States is composed of the great organ-
izations of women in the United States and
naturally such organized membership makes
the council a powerful agent in any work it
undertakes for the good of the country.
	Ladies intending to visit Washington
during the winter should go down in time
to attend the meetings of the triennial of
the council.


Gov. Roosevelt has ridden rough-shod
over the susceptibilities of the New York
“Antis.” This is the first time the Governor
of New York has recommended woman suf-
frage in his message, but in Massachusetts
it has been a frequent occurrence. Secre-
tary of the Navy, Long, was Governor of
Massachusetts for several terms and always 
recommended woman suffrage in his mes-
sage to the Legislature. Among other Mass-
achusetts Governors who have done so
were Govs. Claflin, Washburn, Talbot,
Butler, Ames and Greenhalge. These
gubernatorial recommendations do not have
much influence on the action of the Legis-
lature but they call attention to the subject
and stir up much wholesome discussion.
