EDITORIAL NOTES. 
	On Sunday July 31, 1898, Apostle J. 
W Taylor, Elders Horace Ensign and Fred
Graham with Susa Y. Gates organized a 
Relief Society in Pueblo, Colorado, Sister
Gates presiding at the meeting. Apostle 
Taylor told the sisters they had the privi-
lege of choosing their own president. They 
selected Mrs. Elizabeth Hart who chose 
for her counselors Amanda Coleman and 
Emma Simmons, Ella Branch, secretary 
and Turner Johnson treasurer. The soci-
ety seems to be flourishing and meetings 
are well attended, the sick, the poor and 
needy are attended to according to their 
needs. They have a little money on hand 
and are making a quilt, so we learn from 
a letter recently received. 
	We have been notified by letter of the 
change of officers made in the Relief Soci-
ety of Sevier Stake. The president, Sister 
Elizabeth Bean, and her first counselor, 
Sister Thurza Thurber, had presided since 
the first organization nearly twenty years 
ago, but on account of disadvantageous cir-
cumstances preferred to resign. The con-
ference was held at Glenwood and Elder 
Joseph Horne, counselor to the president of 
the Stake, was present and the resignations 
were accepted and a new Board of officers 
elected, Christina Sorenson, president;
Mary Magleby and Sister Tueson, counsel-
ors; Mary Swindle, secretary, and Caroline 
Bowman, treasurer. Sisters Bean and 
Thurber are satisfied with their labors and 
feel they have the love and confidence of 
the sisters. It was a great surprise to the 
General Board as they knew nothing of the 
change of officers.
