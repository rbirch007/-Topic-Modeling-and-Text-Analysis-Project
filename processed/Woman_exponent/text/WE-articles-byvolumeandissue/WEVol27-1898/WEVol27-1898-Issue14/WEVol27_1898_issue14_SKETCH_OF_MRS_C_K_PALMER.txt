SKETCH OF MRS. C. K. PALMER.
	Catharine K. Palmer, wife of Mifflin L.
Palmer, passed away at 3:20 p. m., Janu-
ary 12, 1899. She was the daughter of
Abram and Catharine King Dolbey, and 
was born in Chester Co., Penn., on the 6th 
of April, 1817. She was the mother of
eight children, five of whom survive her;
they are Mrs. Rebecca W. Harper of Read-
ing, Penn., Mrs. Phoebe Shaffer and Mrs.
Eliza Frost of this city, Mr. A. Morris
Palmer of Taylorsville and Mrs. Bethula
Sharp of Vernon; and she was also the
mother of the late Mrs. Selinda D. Bring-
hurst, wife of Bishop W. A. Bringhurst of
Toquerville.
	Sister Palmer joined the Church of Jesus
Christ of Latter-day Saints about 1840, and 
with her husband and part of her family
came to Utah in 1861. She settled in the 
8th ward of Salt Lake City, and with the
exception of a few months has resided there
ever since. She joined the Relief Society
of that ward at its organization in 1866, and 
was appointed a teacher. Since then she
was treasurer and afterwards counselor, and
in 1893 she was appointed president of the
society, which office she held at the time of
