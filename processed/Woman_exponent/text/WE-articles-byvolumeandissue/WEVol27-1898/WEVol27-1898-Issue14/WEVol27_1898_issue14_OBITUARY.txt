OBITUARY.
			ELIZABETH WEILER.
	It becomes our painful duty to record the death
of our beloved president, Sister Elizabeth Weiler,
who departed this life January 10, 1899. She was
born January 25, 1819, making her the ripe age of
eighty years within fifteen days. She
was president of the Relief Society of the 3rd
ward for thirty-one years on the day of her funer-
al, which was held on the 13th day of January,
1899.
	Sister Weiler was beloved of all her associates
for her gentleness, her integrity, her love of right
and her staunch faith in the Gospel of Christ. She
was a friend to the poor and the widow, and she
exhibited great wisdom in all of her counsels.
	During the thirty-one years of her presidency
in connection with her sisters she worked in per-
fect harmony. We recognize the death of our be-
loved president as the loss of a faithful, energetic
worker, a friend to those in need and a comforter
to those in distress. We esteem her faithful and
untiring labors and devotion to the interest of
Zion worthy the emulation of every member of
the society.
	RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.
	Resolved, That we sympathize with the be-
reaved children, grandchildren and great grand-
children in the loss of a loving parent and a dear
friend.
	Resolved, That these resolutions be given to
her children, also that they be inscribed in the
records of the Relief Society, and that we pub-
