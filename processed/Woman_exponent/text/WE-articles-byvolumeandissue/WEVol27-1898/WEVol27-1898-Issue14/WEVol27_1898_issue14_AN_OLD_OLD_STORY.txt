AN OLD, OLD STORY.

	This story has been told but not written,
of that I feel tolerably certain; and as I
could not from experience tell such a fine
story myself, I can only write one told to
me by a school friend a very long time ago.
	She was a famous little tory and all the
rest of us were pure, blue-blood Yankees,
but she told us such wonderful tales that
we looked upon her as a veritable fairy.
We were quite unwilling then to think
these things were all true, and yet since
those days I have many a time and oft
heard stories far more extravagant, and can
easily believe now that Pamela Kingsley
told the truth as she had heard it from her
governess about her maiden Aunt Dilli-
way with whom she lived. She was her
great aunt, the sister of her grandfather
Dilliway, who was a “royalist to the back-
bone,” Pamela told us. And really she
used to sort of queen it over us, in a way,
because she knew all about Xmas and we
barely knew the name. Aunt Dilliway lived
in Boston and her niece, Miss Pamela
Kingsley was sent up in the country to get
fresh air, new milk and the scent of wild
flowers and new-mown hay. That was
how we come to know her, and as we
adored Boston girls we all fell in love with
her before we knew she was a tory.
