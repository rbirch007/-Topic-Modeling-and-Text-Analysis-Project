M. V. N. HALL.


New and hygienically constructed tele-
phone stations have been provided in Vien-
na, and with them has come a reform in
the dress of the telephone girl. Experience
has proved that the dust lodging on the
clothes of the girls gets into the instruments
and interferes with the effectiveness of the
service. To avoid this the girls are now
required to wear a uniform during office
hours. On their arrival they doff their
street clothes, even their boots, and substi-
tute dresses provided by the government.
The dresses are made of stuff like that most
used for dust cloaks. They are dark in
color, and on the sleeves are yellow stripes,
in imitation of the national colors. Chicago
has adopted the same method to reduce
dust in the operating rooms.
