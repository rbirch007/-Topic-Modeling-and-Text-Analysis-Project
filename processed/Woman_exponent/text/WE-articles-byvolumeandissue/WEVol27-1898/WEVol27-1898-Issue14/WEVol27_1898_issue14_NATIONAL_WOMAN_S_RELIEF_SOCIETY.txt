NATIONAL WOMAN'S RELIEF SOCIETY.

	Afternoon session, President Zina D. H.
Young presiding, Prof. C. J. Thomas lead-
ing the singing by the Temple choir.
Meeting opened with “Now let us rejoice.”
Prayer by Elder C. J. Thomas. Singing
“Oh Golden Day,” very beautifully rend-
ered by Maggie C. Hull and members of
the Temple choir.
	President Artemissia Lugmidler reported
Kanab Stake, referred to the lack of mem-
bers attending meetings and said that in
listening to other reports she found it to be
a general complaint. In regard to laying
up wheat said theirs was not a grain pro-
ducing locality, but she had urged the sis-
ters inasmuch as there was an appearance
of a bountiful harvest this year to lay up
what wheat they could; felt although they
were a small Stake they were diligent and
proving themselves faithful over a few
things.
	President Ann C. Woodbury of the St.
George Stake, told the long distance they
had to travel by team to visit the branches
of the society in their Stake, some places
were four hundred miles away and it
was quite an arduous undertaking to go all
the way around. “We are just getting a
good start in the silk industry. We were
to have had timber for a mill and the boys
were going to start off with their teams to
haul it when we received word the mill
was burned. Still we have every prospect
of success this year, all have plenty to do.
Our children have not applied themselves
to industrial pursuits in the past as they
should, we must insist upon industrious
habits in our children and the silk industry
is one pursuit that will be helpful in this
respect, and there is a good prospect of suc-
cess.”
