DISCUSSIONS AND REPORTS.

	The women from Utah who went to
Washington in February to attend the
Triennial sessions of the National Council
of Women of the United States, have had
an interesting experience to say the least,
especially those who went as presidents
(proxies,) delegates and members of the
Resolution Committee. As every one knows
who has been connected with such great
national bodies, the hard work and really
most important is done in committees.
	Speech making is comparatively easy, more
especially so when prepared beforehand,
which the addresses certainly should be on
any given subject, and not written at odd
moments or on the way as some very busy
people are apt to do in the hurry that
comes from overwork. If one accustomed
to public duty might be permitted to sug-
gest the advice would be to take the ut-
most pains with the papers to be given as ad-
dresses to an audience of cultured people.
Random shots come in occasionally, but it is
the steady, deliberate firing that accom-
plishes the objects seriously undertaken.
	The Resolution Committee consisted of
one member from each organization belong-
ing to the Council and numbered twenty-
five persons, representing various shades of
opinion on many vital subjects, Rev. Anna
Garlin Spencer, Providence, R. I., chair-
man; Ann M. Cannon, Salt Lake City,
Utah, secretary.
