REORGANIZATION.

	The quarterly conference of the Relief
Society of Box Elder Stake convened in the
Tabernacle at Brigham City, Dec. 13, 1898,
President Susannah P. Booth presiding.
Singing “Know this, that every soul is
free.” Prayer by President Mary Jansen.
Singing “Sweet is the work my God, my
King.” Minutes were read and approved.
	Sister Theone Michelson reported the
Scandinavian society, said, “We can com-
fort and strengthen one another when we
possess the right spirit.” Sister Charlotte
Boden of the 2nd ward said, “We are all
laborers in the same cause. therefore, we
trust one another. I feel proud to be one
with such good sisters.”
	Sister Christina Jensen of Mantua said,
“We have buried one of our most faithful
sisters lately and one last winter, we miss
them so much. I think we couid not get a
better president than Sister Booth.” Sister
Mary Harding of Willard said, “Let every
mother instil in her daughter’s mind to live
a pure life, obedient to those placed over
her.
	Sister Mary Watkins of the 4th ward said,
“I cannot help feeling a little lonely today.
I miss our beloved president, Sister Olivia
Widerborg, but she is happy, my only de-
sire is to live to be worthy to meet my par-
ents and friends who have passed away.
