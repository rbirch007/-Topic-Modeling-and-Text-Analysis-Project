NEW ORGANIZATION.

	Minutes of the organization of the Relief
Society held in the 22nd ward branch, Dec.
8, 1898.
	Bishop Solomon stated that they deemed
it proper to organize a Relief Society in this


part of the 22nd ward, and for that reason
they had called this meeting prior to the
organization.
	Instructions and advice were then given
the sisters by Bishop Solomon and his
counselors, Arthur Winter and John Nebe-
ker. Bishop Solomon then gave the names
of the following sisters to act as officers:
Sister J. S. McKean, president; Elizabeth
Lawrence, first counselor; Nancy Shaw,
second counselor; Octavia H. Walgren,
secretary; Elvira Winberg, treasurer.
	The sisters then expressed their willing-
ness to fulfill their calling to the best of
their ability. Brother Solomon advised the
sisters to meet together as often as possible,
and to seek the spirit of the Lord at all times.
	The sisters were then set apart by the
Bishop and his counselors. Bishop Solo-
mon made a few remarks, exhorting the
sisters to be faithful at all times, and asked
the blessing of the Lord upon all. Bene-
diction was pronounced by Niels Nelson.
						O. H. W.
