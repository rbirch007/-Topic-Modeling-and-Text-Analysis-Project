U. W. P. C.
	The regular meeting of the U. W. P. C.
was held in the “Exponent” office Nov. 30,
1898, the new president, Ella W. Hyde, in
the chair. Mrs. E. B. Wells offered prayer.
Roll call was ahswered by miscellaneous
sentiments. Minutes of annual meeting
read and approved.
	The new president then made a happy
little speech, thanking the ladies for the
honor conferred and expressing a desire for
the future advancement of the club. Under
unfinished business the matter of incorpor-
ating came up and was again laid over.
Under head of new business a note was
read from the secretary of the General
Federation asking members to subscribe for
the Biennial report of the General Federa-
tion for 1898. Ruth M. Fox was nomina-
ted by E. B. Wells and seconded by Lucy
A. Clark for director to the State Federa-
tion; carried.
	Miss Julia Farnsworth then gave a very
interesting and instructive paper on the
Phillipines, followed by discussion. A con-
cise and pithy paper on Current Events was
given by Mrs. L. M. Rhodes. A pretty
original poem entitled “Life” by Phebe C.
Young finished the program.
	Mrs. I. Cameron Brown kindly invited
every member of the club to meet at her
home, 201, E. South Temple St., at 2
o’clock on December 9.
	The program for next meeting was read,
and after benediction by Dr. E. R. Shipp
the club adjourned to meet at the rooms of
Dr. E. R. Shipp, Constitution Building,
for the December meeting.
	RUTH M. FOX, Asst. Sec.
