THANKS.

Editor Woman’s Exponent:
	It seems to me that Ada Patterson de-
serves the heartfelt thanks of the women of
Utah for the fair-minded and sensible way
in which she has explained the present em-
barrassing situation we are placed in by the
election of B. H. Roberts to Congress. I
think, too, we should acknowledge the
liberality of the Mecca, a Denver magazine,
in publishing Miss Patterson’s fair state-
ment concerning us.
	She gives us credit for at least standing
by our rights, and of maintaining such
heroism as should be sufficient to stop the
voice of unthinking people who would have
it appear that to the imbecility of the wo-
men of Utah may be charged the election
of the man who fiercely opposed their being
allowed the privilege of voting.
	Miss Patterson says: “The unembroid-
ered truth is that the women of Utah were
opposed to Mr. Roberts. A large majority
of the woman vote was against him. The
minority voted for him because they were
loyal to Democracy, rather than to Mr.
Roberts. * * *
	He opposed permitting women to vote.
* * He lost as every one knows; but the
women never forgave him. Had his elec-
tion depended upon the votes of the women,
he would have met a Balaklava on election
day."
	Thus our friend, Miss Patterson, has
nobly spoken out in behalf of the wo-
men of Utah. And as one of them I feel
to return her sincere thanks.
