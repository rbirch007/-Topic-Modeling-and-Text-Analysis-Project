EIGHTIETH YEAR NOV. 1898.

Dear Mother

	Through the blessings of a kind, loving
Father, whose children we are, you have
been permitted to remain in mortality to
the ripe age of four score years. Eighty
years of probation in this world of care,
sorrow and trouble have proven your integ-
rity and womanhood to be equal to all
emergencies.
	Born in a foreign land, fostered under an
alien government, but brought under the
royal emblems of liberty and peace in early
womanhood, you have proven your fidelity
to your country and its constitutions under
the most trying ordeals of life. True to
your convictions of right you have not
swerved from the truth as it came to your
heart through the light of the Gospel of the
Lord Jesus Christ.
	Drivings, mobbings, the trials and suffer-
ings incident to exile and pioneer life have
failed to weaken your trust in a divine
work, but have caused you to remain firm
to the last.
	We hail you as our mother, and sincere-
ly hope that the remaining years of your
stay with us will be your happiest, best
ones on earth.
	As one to whom you have given birth in
the flesh. I must respectfully bow in hom-
age to my mother, and tender a son’s filial
love and gratitude. To you and your kind,
motherly affection and interest in me in my
young days I owe much of the little good
that is within me.
	Ever solicitous for the welfare of those
whom God has intrusted to you, and wish-
ing all your children to follow paths of
honor and righteousness, you have careful-
ly guarded us against the wiles and snares
of the ungodly.
