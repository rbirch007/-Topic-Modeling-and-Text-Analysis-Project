SISTER HORNE’S BIRTHDAY.
	Sunday, November 20, the 80th birth-
day of our beloved and respected Stake
president, Sister M. I. Horne. The day
is quite emblematic—the snow coming
down so pure and white is like what her
grand life has been. To me it was very
pleasing to see so large a congregation as-
sembled at the Assembly Hall yesterday
to celebrate the birth and do honor to one
who has done so much good, and to listen
to the remarks made by the different
speakers. 
	It will be forty-four years next month
since I first met Sister Horne in company
at the home of President John Taylor’s
father, many notable persons being present;
but when introduced to Sister Horne I was
forcibly struck with her motherly appear-
ance and kindly countenance.
	Having lived within a block from her
from that time until she moved from
the Fourteenth ward, I have had ample
opportunity to become acquainted with her.
