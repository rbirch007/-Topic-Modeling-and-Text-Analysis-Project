ST. JOSEPH STAKE.
	Believing our sisters would like to know
what the Relief Society sisters are doing in
far off Arizona, I will give you a short ac-
count.
	Our conference convened on Dec. 3, 1898,
President Elizabeth Layton presiding.
There were present of the priesthood
Apostle Abraham O. Woodruff, President
Andrew Kimball, Counselors William D.
Johnson and Charles M. Layton.
	The speakers touched on many impor-
tant subjects, viz. storing of grain, building
a Stake granary, as we have on hand 995
bushels of grain; on tithing, fast donations,
supporting the Church school, necessity of
attending to prayers, looking after the poor
and needy, comforting the widows and or-
phans.
	It was decided that on the evening of
Dec. 6, we have a Stake reunion in
the form of a dance and repast. There
were over three hundred present and all
partook of a sumptuous supper. Apostle
Abraham O. Woodruff and Elder Seymour
B. Young were present to add to the dig-
nity of the occasion. The dancing began
at seven p. m., and closed at twelve p. m.
The proceeds of the party which were
$65.00 were used to defray the expenses of
two sisters who were in Salt Lake City
taking a course in obstetrics under Dr. Ellis
R. Shipp.
	The health of the people in general is
good, though this has been the coldest
winter witnessed in the last fifteen years.
The weather at present is delightful.
		SELENA PHILLIPS, Stake Sec.
	Thatcher, Ariz., Jan. 7, 1899.
