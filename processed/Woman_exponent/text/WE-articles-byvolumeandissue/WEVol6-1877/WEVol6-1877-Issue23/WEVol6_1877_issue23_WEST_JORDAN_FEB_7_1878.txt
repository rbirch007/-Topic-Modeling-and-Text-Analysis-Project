		WEST JORDAN, Feb. 7, 1878.
	Organization of the Young Ladies' Mu-
tuul Improvement Association, under the
direction of Sister Eliza R. Snow, with J.
J. Williams, Secretary:
	Sister Delila Gardner was chosen Presi-
dent of the Y. L. M. I. A.
	Marinda Bateman,
	Rachel Hill,
	Mary A. Cooper,		Counselors,
	Ellen Jensen,
	Polly Egbert,
	Adeline Williams,
	Elizabeth Cutler, Secretary.
	Rebecca Gardiner, Assistant Secretary,
	Elizabeth Cooper, Treasurer.
	The nominations were carried unani-
mously. Sixty names were then enrolled
as members. Sister Snow then gave some
instructions to the mothers, and exhorted
the members to be punctual in their duties.
	Sister E. Davis remarked she believed
"Obedience was better than sacrifice;" ex-
horted the members to always respond
when called upon to do anything by their
President. Spoke very highly of the WO-
MAN'S EXPONENT; said it was the means
of helpiug the Elders when on missions.
Also said Dr. Romania B. Pratt was about
to open a class to instruct the sisters in the
medical profession, which she hoped would
receive the earnest attention of our sisters.
	Sister MacGregor said she felt there was
a great responsibility placed upon the sis-
ters to-day, but if they would give heed to
what Sister Snow had said, the Lord would
bless them.
	Sister Snow said that when she first came
into the Church, she made up her mind to
do anything and everything she was called
upon to do, however awkwardly she might
do it.
	Brother John Hill said he felt good in
attending the meeting, and if the sisters
would start a cocoonery, he would assist
them all he could, and was sure the Bishop
and Brother Turner would do the same.
	President Delila Gardiner said the Y. L.
M. I. A. would meet at this place next Sun-
day. Singing by the choir. Benediction
by J. J. Williams.
