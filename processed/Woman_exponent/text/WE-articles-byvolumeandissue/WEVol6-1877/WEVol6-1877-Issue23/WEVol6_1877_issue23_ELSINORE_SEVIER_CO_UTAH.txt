	ELSINORE, SEVIER CO., UTAH,
		March 29, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	In accordance with appointment, the sis-
ters of this place gathered at the School-
house on the 9th of December, 1877, to or-
ganize the Relief Society. Present, Bish-
op J. W. Sylvester and his Counselors, J.
C. Jensen and Jas. I. Jensen, and a majority
of the sisters. The following sisters were
unanimously elected as officers:
	Johanna Marie Nielsen, President,
	Christine Sylvester,
	Christine Sodenberg,	Counselors.
	Juger Jensen, Secretary,
	Nicoline Hansen, Treasurer.
	Bishop Sylvester and Counselors gave in-
structions and made encouraging remarks
to the congregation; advised them to
gather up wheat for storing, and to do so
without delay, as there may come a day
that it will be needed.
	Forty-two members have been enrolled,
which, with few exceptions, includes all
the sisters of this place. We hold our
regular meetings once a month. Since the
organization of the Society there has been

[Column 3]

gathered fifty-seven bushels of wheat to
lay up, besides other means which are on
hand.
	We cannot but express our satisfaction
with the feelings of the sisters; all seem to
take a lively interest in the organization,
and we hope, by the blessings of God and
the united efforts of the sisters, to be the
means of doing some good in the interest
of the kingdom of God.
	Elsinore is a young settlement of forty
families, but prosperous, and all is peace
and union; we pray that this may so con-
tinue. Several of the sisters desire to sub-
scribe for the EXPONENT, and we send
with this for four copies as a beginning.
	Respectfully;
JOHANNA M. NEILSEN, President,
	INGER JENSEN, Secretary.
