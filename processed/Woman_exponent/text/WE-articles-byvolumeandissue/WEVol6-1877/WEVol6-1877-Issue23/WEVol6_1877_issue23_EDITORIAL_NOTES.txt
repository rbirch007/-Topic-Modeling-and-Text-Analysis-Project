EDITORIAL NOTES.

	WE have several articles and poems
from our contributors laid over, also re-
ports from Fourteenth Ward General Re-
trenchment Meeting, Clarkston, Miners-
ville, Pleasant Grove, Peoa, Mona, Heber and
Bloomington, and one from Rockville, stat-
ing their success in sericulture. They have
plenty of silk-worm eggs and have put out
10,000 mullberry cuttings, and planted a
quart of mulberry seed.
	THE Quarterly Conference of Weber Co.
Relief Society will be held in Ogden, Thurs-
day May 2, when it is expected a full re-
port will be made of the condition of each
branch of the Relief Society of that Stake
of Zion. Several sisters from this city have
been invited to attend. Miss E. R. Snow,
Mrs. Zina D. Young, and Mrs. E. Davis
will leave Ogden on the morning of the 3rd
of May to make a tour of Malad Valley and
settlements in that vicinity, holding meet-
ings with the Societies in the various set-
tlements and talking up home-industries.
especially sericulture, the storing of grain,
co-operative enterprises, encouraging the
sisters in their organizations, urging them
to obtain naturalization papers, and exert
ing their utmost influence in promoting the
interests of the Kingdom of God. These
sisters expect to be absent a week or more.
