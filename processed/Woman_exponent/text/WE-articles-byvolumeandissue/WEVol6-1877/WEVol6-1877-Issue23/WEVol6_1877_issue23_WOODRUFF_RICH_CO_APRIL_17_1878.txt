	WOODRUFF, RICH CO., April 17, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The Relief Society of this place was or-
ganized on the 28th September, 1874, with
the following officers:
	Harriet A. Lee, President,
	Ruth C. Cornia,
	Tryphenia Fairchild,	Counselors,
	Annie Cox, Secretary,
	Aurelia Lee, Treasurer.
	The Society was organized by Sister Eliza
R. Snow, and has been steadily increasing
ever since, both in membership and finances,
and the prospects are flattering for accom-
plishing a good work the coming summer.
The climate here has, so far, prevented the
successful raising of grain, consequently
our granaries and store houses are at present
"To let;" still we have not been idle, the
sisters have been busy making quilts, &c.,
for Temple donations and other purposes,
and we have on hand at present date seven-
teen head of stock, and as opportunities of-
fer we intend selling them to our more
fortunate neighbors in the Stake for wheat.

[Column 2]

	Our meetings (every two weeks) are well
attended, and an excellent spirit is mani-
fested, and the earnest working and untir-
ing efforts of the sisters is conclusive evi-
dence of their desire to make the Society
an organization of permanence, and to ac-
complish that good for which they were set
apart.
	Hoping the Spirit of God may rest upon
us and every organization of the Church,
and desiring the welfare of the great work
in which we are engaged, we remain your
sisters in the Gospel,
	HARRIET A. LEE, President,
		ANNIE COX, Secretary.
