	MEADOWVILLE, March 24, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT.
	The young ladies of Meadowville were
organized as a Retrenchment Association
June 3d, 1875, under the direction of Sister
Prescinda Kimball; our officers were as fol-
lows:
	Ann M. Eldredge, President,
	Elizabeth Townsend,
	Charlotte Tufts,		Counselors,
	Lathilla Kimball,
	Lizzie Eldredge, Secretary.
	On January 30th, 1878, the name, was
changed to Young Ladies Mutual Improve-
ment Association.  Two additional Coun-
selors and an Assistant Secretary were se-
lected by Sister Eldredge, viz:
	Janet Moffat,
	Christine Kimball,		Counselors,
	Ellen Kimball, Assistant Secretary.
	There are but twenty-four families here,
and most of them are young and have fami-
lies of little children; owing to this our
Association is not as well attended as we
would like. The sisters all seem to feel
well, and readily respond to any call that is
made on them for donations, etc., for any
purpose.
	On the 14th inst. we gave a picnic, and
an entertainment consisting of songs, recita-
tions, dialogues, essays, etc. We were ably
assisted by the Y. M. M. I. Association.
Evergthing passed off pleasantly, and we
had a very agreeable time. We have
twenty members in our Society. Wishing
the EXPONENT success, I remain your sister
and friend,	LIZZIE ELDREDGE,
				Secretary.
