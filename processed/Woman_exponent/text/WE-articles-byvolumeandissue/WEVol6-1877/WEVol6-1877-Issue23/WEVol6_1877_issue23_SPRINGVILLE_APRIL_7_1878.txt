	SPRINGVILLE, April 7, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The Y. L. M. I. A. of Springville was re-
organized March 15, 1877, under the direc-
tion of Miss E. R. Snow, assisted by Mrs.
Zina D. Young and Mrs. Prescinda Kimball,
with the following officers:
	Caroline Whiting, President,
	Calista Boyer,
	Ellen Sumsion,
	Abby Whiting,
	Clara Bringhurst,		Counselors,
	Maria Mendenhal,
	Elizabeth Bird,
	Rettie Bringhurst, Secretary,
	Kate Houtz, Corresponding Secretary,
	Eliza Haymond, Treasurer,
	Mary Whiting, Superintendent.
	Our first Annual meeting was held in the
Meeting House, March 15, 1878, Caroline
Whiting presiding. After the exercises of
singing and prayer, the programme for the
evening consisted of songs, essays, decla-
mations, etc. The Constitution and By-
laws and the laws to govern the circulation
of the library were read by the Secretary.
The Financial report of the Association
during the past year was read by the Trea-
surer. Also a report of the working de-
partment read by the Secretary. After
which Elders Wm. Bromley and Wm. Kel-
sey and Bishop Bringhurst made some very
encouraging remarks to the young ladies;
exhorted them to press forward with a new
will, and assured them that a decided im-
provement was manifest in many ways.
The President then presented the officers
of the Association, who were unanimously
sustained.
	Our first Annual meeting was a time of
real pleasure, and ended with expressions
of gratitude and a determination in the fu-
ture to become zealous of good works.
	We also wish to inform you that our little
Association has now made a permanent
step in the direction of silk-culture, as we
have accomplished the clearing of four
acres of benchland, and a setting out of a
small orchard of the mulberry cuttings.
The young sisters manifest the greatest
willingness to assist in starting a work
which we hope will prove a benefit and
blessing in some future day.
	KATE HOUTZ, Cor. Sec'y.
