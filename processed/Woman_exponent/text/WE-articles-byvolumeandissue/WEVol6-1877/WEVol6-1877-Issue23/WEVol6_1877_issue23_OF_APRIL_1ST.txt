OF APRIL 1st.

Thy piteous "questionings" touch an answering chord
	Within my soul, which fain would breathe reply,
If I perchance by some low, gentle word
	Might help to soothe thy spirit's yearning cry;
For well I know that doubts bring dire unrest
	And naught in common should they have with us,
For how by faith in God, can we be blest,
	Unless we yield to Him in perfect trust.

That man has carped, and warped a woman's sphere,
	To suit his selfish needs, is too well known,
Yet galling though it be to woman's ear—
	We must admit the fault is part her own,
For has she not with free and gracious will,
	Such service rendered to the ones beloved,
Nor counted costs to future ages, till
	Man thinks her lack of agency is proved.

But sister, do not shrink from Heaven's decree,
	'Twas Christ the man-child came the world to save,
Thine Elder Brother died for you and me,
	That we might rise triumphant from the grave.
Methinks that he who brings the clay to life
	Mnst not be far removed from Christ's Estate
To claim from death his sister, friend or wife
	And pilot them,through the Celestial gate.

Should he to whom thy love and truth is given
	(And his to thee) should he I say aspire
To be as perfect as the Lord in heaven
	And call thee forth from death, wouldst then seek
		higher?

O breathe no thought of doubt in thy babe's ears
Nor tell her thy chafed heart's bewildering fears,
Break not the magic charm of infant trust,
Nor sow the seeds of canker, moth or rust,
But gently plant love's own immortal germ,
Too soon hard lessons she will have to learn,
And teach her trust in God's unfaltering will,
That her earth-mission she may nobly fill.

Though tardy justice may be long delayed,
	Our cause no longer lingers in the rear,
And Woman's onward course can not be stayed,
	Though man, reluctant, turns her plea to hear,
And in that life where we may hope to gain
	A respite from this world of pain,
Methinks where all is holy, pure and free,
	"Our Rights" will sink in Perfect Liberty.
