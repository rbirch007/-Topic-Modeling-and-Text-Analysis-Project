OBITUARIES.

	Sister Jane Miles, wife of Edwin R. Miles,
departed this life March 14th, in her 38th year,
after a few days illness, leaving a husband and
children to mourn her loss. Sister Miles was
much beloved and respected by all who knew
her, faithful in her duties as wife and mother.
She has spent her whole life in this Church,
and lived and died a faithful Latter-day Saint,
much esteemed and beloved. It can be truly
said of her, she rests in peace, in sure and cer-
tain hope of a glorious resurrection with the
just.				MARTHA WILLIAMS.
	Smithfield, Cache Co., March 19, 1878.

	Our beloved sister, Cecelia Peterson, depart-
ed this life Friday, Feb. 22d, after a lingering
illness of four months. She died as she had
lived a faithful Latter-day Saint, and true to
every principle of the Gospel as it was presented
to her. She has been a member of the Relief
Society since its organization, in 1868, and a
Teacher; was ever faithful to her duties, and
always on hand and ready to help whenever
she was called upon. Her sufferings have been
great for a long time: she died of dropsy. Her
faith in the ordinance of laying on of hands
was steadfast to the last. She was one of the
first to enter into the business of sericulture
and was most successful. In Sister Peterson
we have lost a faithful member and an earnest
and indefatigable worker. She leaves a sor-
rowing husband to mourn her loss, with whom
we deeply sympathize.
	She was aged 64 years, and was a native of
Denmark.				M. F. C. MORRISON.
	Mt. Pleasant, Feb. 26, 1878.

						For the EXPONENT,
	Died, at Lake Town, Rich Co., Sunday,
March 17, 1878, Eveline, beloved wife of Jo-
seph G. Williams, and daughter of Willard
and Rosamond F. Green, leaving a baby three
weeks old, and two little girls, besides a large
circle of relatives and friends to deplore her
loss. She died in full faith of a glorious resur-
rection with the Saints.
