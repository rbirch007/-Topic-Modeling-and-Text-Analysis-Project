EDITOR EXPONENT:

	The Relief Society was re-organized at
this place Dec. 6, 1877, under the direction
of Bishop G. A. Murdock. We had an ex-
cellent meeting, and much good instruc-
tion was given. The election of officers
was as follows:
	Mary J. Davis, President.
	Lucinda E. Murdock,	} Counselors.
	Sarah A. Ross,		}
	Lydia L. Wells, Secretary.
	Martha A. Wells, Assistant Secretary.
	Floria A. Russell, Treasurer.
	We meet together once in two weeks,
and our meetings are well attended; we
number twenty-nine members, but expect
our number will continue to increase. We
have stored thirty-nine bushels of grain;
we have a lot and expect to fence it this
coming summer, and then we shall set out
mulberry trees. We are striving to become
united in doing all the good we can towards
building up Zion. With best wishes for
the prosperity of the EXPONENT, your sis-
ter, &c.,				L. L. WELLS, Sec'y.
