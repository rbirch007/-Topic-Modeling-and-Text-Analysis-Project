	BY HANNAH T. KING.

The year is passing, do I weep
	To see it sink into the past?
No, no, I have not one regret
	Of all its days, this is the last,
Had it no charms?–a very few,
	And yet mid all I have been blest,
But what a struggle life has been
	To gain one Oasis of rest!
One spot whereon to cast my Soul,
	And say to all, now peace, be still,
Let me in joy and sunshine bask,
	For one brief moment take my fill;
Yet life! I love thee! life alone
	is God's own spirit unto all,
And 'tis so rich, so full, so good,
	We need the wormwood and gall
To keep us in the narrow way,
	The plodding, sterile road of life,
To hold the lamp above our head
	To save us from much woe and strife.
Then pass old year. I bow you out
	With all respect, and grateful love
For well I know Time is a boon
	A censer swayed from hands above!

And though the thorns blend with the rose,
	And wheat and chaff together blend.
And shade and sunshine strew our path,
	In all a blessing God doth send.

And every day the eternal pile
	But numbers wealth, our own to call,
In the grand Future of our lives
	When God with us is All in all.

S. L. City, Dec. 1877.
