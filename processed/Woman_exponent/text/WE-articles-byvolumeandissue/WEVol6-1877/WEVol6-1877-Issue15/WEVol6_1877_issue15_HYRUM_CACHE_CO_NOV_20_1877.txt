	HYRUM, CACHE CO., NOV. 20, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	On Oct. 25th, we were favored with a
visit from Sisters E. R. Snow, Z. D. Young
and E. Davis; our Bishop and his Counsel-
ors, besides a number of brethren and sis-
ters from Paradise, were present.  Our
meeting was one of great interest, and the
Spirit of God rested upon both speakers and
hearers. It is the first visit we have had
from Sisters Snow and Young, but we hope
it will not be the last.  Among other ex-
cellent instructions, they urged home in-
dustries and silk culture, and also exhibit-
ed specimens of Utah silk, which were very
beautiful, showing that our climate is suit-
able for that branch of industry.  They
also spoke in favor of the EXPONENT, and
urged the sisters to sustain it.
	The members of our Society, as a major-
ity, are alive to their duties, both in look-
ing after the needy and helping to build
Temples; we are goig to take the counsel
of our sisters and open a store for our
home products, and hope to have it started
before Christmas.
	CHRISTINA LILJENQUIST, President,
	N. M. NEILSEN, Secretary.
