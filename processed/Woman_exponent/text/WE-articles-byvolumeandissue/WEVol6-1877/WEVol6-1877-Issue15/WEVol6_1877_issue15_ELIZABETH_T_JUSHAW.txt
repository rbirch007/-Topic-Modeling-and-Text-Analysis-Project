	VERNON SETTLEMENT, Dec. 14, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The sisters of this little settlement were
organized into a branch of the Relief Socie-
ty, January 26th, 1877, with the following
sisters as officers:
	Mrs. Jennet C. Sharp, President.
	"	Mary Hokensen
	"	Mary Ann Bennion,
	"	Elizabeth Bennion, Secretary,
	"	Lula Sharp, Treasurer.
[The following is typed to the right side of "Mary Hokenson" and "Mary Ann Bennion"]
Couselors.
	Our settlement is somewhat scattered,
and several of the sisters cannot speak our
language, yet our meetings are well attend-
ed, and we are increasing in unity of feel-
ing and are trying to do something toward
rolling on the great work.  We meet every
two weeks and always feel the influence of
the Spirit of God to be with us.  We have
thirty-two members enrolled, the young
ladies meeting with us, as we are so few in
number.  We have bought material and
have built a granary to store our wheat in,
of which we have thirty-nine bushels safe-
ly stored, in good condition.  Our desires
are for the success of all those who are
helping to build up the kingdom of God.
We remain your sisters in the Church,
	JENNET C. SHARP,
