	SMITHFIELD, Dec. 13, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT.
	On the 29th of October we had a visit
from Sisters E. R. Snow, Zina D. Young,
and Elizabeth Davis, from your city, who
spoke to us words of comfort and gave us
good instruction in the various missions
that women are expected to perform in
this kingdom; may God give us wisdom
and strength to carry them out, and may
these noble sisters live long to speak words
of comfort and encouragement to the sisters
of Zion.  In November, our esteemed and
worthy President, Elizabeth T. Morehead,
resigned her office as President of this Re-
lief Society on account of poor health, we
were very sorry to lose her for she has been
a mother, counselor, and friend to us all,
ever since the first organization of the So-
ciety in this place, the sisters presented her
a testimonial of their love and respect, we
also got up a surprise party and gave her
a number of useful presents.  Since then
our Society has been reorganized with the
following officers.  Adaline Barber, Presi-
dent, Cordelia M. Morehead, and Mary Jane
Roskelly, Counselors; Elizabeth T. Jushaw,
Secretary, Margaret Story, Treasurer.  We
have built a small but comfortable house to
meet in, and we had it dedicated on the
10th of this month.  We have stored up
some grain, not so much as we should have
done had the grasshoppers not destroyed
so much this season; we have a store start-
ed which we hope will encourage the sis-
ters to wear home-made articles, and thus
do away with importations from Babylon.
We are trying to keep pace with every
good work that comes along.  That we may
be enabled to continue is the wish of your
sister and co-worker in Zion.
