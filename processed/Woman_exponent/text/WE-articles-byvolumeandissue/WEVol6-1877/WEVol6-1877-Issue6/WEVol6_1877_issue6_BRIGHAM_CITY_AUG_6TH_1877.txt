	BRIGHAM CITY, Aug. 6th, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Thinking a few lines from the Young
Ladies' Improvement Association of Brig-
ham City would not be uninteresting to
our progress as a Society.
	We were organized, under the direction
of Miss E. R. Snow, on the 31st of July,
1875, with the following officers:
	Mrs. Minnie Jensen Snow, President,
	"	Annie Madsen,
	Miss Ida Snow,
	Mrs. Lottie Huntsucker,	Counselors.
	"	Lucy Jensen,
	Miss Esther Smith,
	Mrs. Jane Johnson,
	Miss Eugenia Snow, Secretary,
	"	Fannie Grachl, Assistant Secretary.
	Mrs. Annie Madsen, Treasurer.
	During the first year our meetings were
very well attended, and a lively interest
manifested in responding to every require-
ment. Last year we contributed by dona-
tions two hundred and fifity-five dollars
($255) to be applied on our new Tabernacle,
now in course of erection, and have already
this year over one hundred dollars ($100)
more to be devoted to the same purpose.
We have also assisted in purchasing the
second organ for public use, to be placed in
our new Social Hall, and we do whatever
we can for the Temples and other local calls.
	We continue to have good and lively
meetings, once in two weeks, with an aver-
age attendance of about fifity members. We
feel well and enjoy the spirit of the Gospel,
and desire to improve our talents and pre-
pare ourselves to be usefnl in promoting
the welfare of Zion, by desisting from all
we know to be evil, and doing all we can
to elevate and imporve our minds, and live
up to the glorious principles of the gospel
of Christ.
	Wishing success to your valuable paper,
we remain your sisters,
		Minnie Jensen Snow, President,
	Eugenia Snow Pierce,	Secretaries.
	Fannie Graehl,
