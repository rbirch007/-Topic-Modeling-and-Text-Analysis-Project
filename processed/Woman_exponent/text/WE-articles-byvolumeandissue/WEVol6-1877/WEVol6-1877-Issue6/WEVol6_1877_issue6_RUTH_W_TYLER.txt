						RUTH W. TYLER.

	The 20th Ward branch of the Relief So-
ciety has lately been reorganized, and is
now in successful operation, and, we trust,
CO-OPERATING with all other branches of the
Society, whose motives and efforts are to
accomplish good and assist the great Latter-
day work. We hold our meetings once in
two weeks—a working meeting and a
speaking or testimony meeting alternately.
Our teachers are energetic, earnest women,
and we have a goodly number of lively,
diligent members in our Society. Our ob-
ject is to make ourselves useful in looking
after and relieving the wants of the poor,
and also to aid in every enterprise which
the women of the Latter-day Saints are
called to engage in, such as assisting the
brethren in saving means for building Tem-
ples, storing up grain for times of scarcity,
promoting home industries, sustaining

[Column 3]
home productions and all these great co-op-
erative schemes. On the 13th of April last
we had a feast prepared for the poor, the
aged and infirm of the Ward, and a most
enjoyable time was spent by all who partici-
pated. Circumstances prevented our Bish-
op, Brother John Sharp, from meeting with
us, but his counselors, Brothers Dunbar and
Allen, and also Brothers C. R. Savage and
C. W. Stayner favored us with their pres-
ence, and assisted with appropriate hymns,
songs and music in cheering the hearts of
our aged and afflicted brethren and sisters.
The meeting was adjourned for one year.
In the evening a pleasant and very sociable
party was given for the younger members
of the ward.
