	MONROE, SEVIER Co., July 19, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	There is a Relief Society in this place in
pretty good condition, under the presiden-
cy of Mrs. Clarinda Washburn, who is alive
to the duties of her office. Although we
are a long way from the seat of govern-
ment of the Church, yet we rejoice in the
same truths of the Gospel; are very glad to
hear the good tidings the servants of God
bring from time to time. We have just
had a two days' Conference in this county,
received much good counsel, and been more
fully organized in the priesthood, and trust
under the blessings of God to be able to do
more in the future than in the past for the
building up of the kingdom of God. We
are a small community in this place, but
when the counsel of our venerable and be-
loved president came, for the sisters to store
up grain, we went to work with a will and
cast in our mite. We are also trying to
do the best we can to help roll on the great,
grand cause in other ways. Please pardon
us for not communicating with you before,
but will strive to keep you better informed
in the future. The instructions and gen-
eral information we get through the EXPO-
NENT we hail with delight .
	We remain yours truly,
			C. WASHBURNE, President,
					C. COOPER, Secretary.
