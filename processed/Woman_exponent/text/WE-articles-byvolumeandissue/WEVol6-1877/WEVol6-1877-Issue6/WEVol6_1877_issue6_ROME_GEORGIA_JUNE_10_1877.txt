	ROME, GEORGIA, June 10, 1877
Mrs. E. B. WELLS:
 My Dear Sister:—I have just received
one of your papers this morning; it comes
regularly to hand and is a most welcome
visitor.  I prize it above all other papers
that I get.  I can readily realize the great
work you are striving to accomplish in re-
moving prejudice, and helping to build up
the Kingdom of God in the last days.  I
consider it a great and glorious work to be
engaged in.  My father and all the breth-


ren here are trying very hard to sell all
their possessions to the best advantage in
order to emigrate this fall to Utah.  I sin-
cerely hope they will be successful in their
efforts; I will hail the day with great joy
when we can all say farewell to Babylon
and gather with the saints in Zion.  I have
placed all my hopes in the Lord knowing
that He is able to accomplish all things.
My prayers ascend continually for the
speedy success of our gathering out of Bab-
ylon and being placed where we can enjoy
our religious beliefs, and assist in building
up the great kingdom which is now being
established upon the earth.  I think it the
duty of every Latter-day saint to do all
that the Almighty has given them power
to do.  Brothers Morgan and Leizenbee
preached to us their last sermon at this
place on the night of the 19th of May; on
the 20th they left in search of a new field of
labor; but they will return in due time to
attend to the emigration.  My prayer is
that they may be successful in all their un-
dertakings.  I remain your sister in the 
true gospel,
