				CONCLUDED.

	I wish ladies would give this subject the at-
tention it deserves, and adopt, if not these ar-
ticles named, some others well devised, that
will, if not perfectly, partially meet the want.
I am convinced that if ladies only knew the
detrimental results or effects of weight and
heat in this region of the body, the spine, hips,
and abdomen, they would hasten to devise
some means of removal. I am not alone in
my convictions as to the great need, but have
the indorsement of the best medical authority
throughout the land.
	Every article of dress should fit loosely, and
tight, girting bands of any kind or form, be
wholly discarded, as they hinder or impede
free respiration, free circulation of blood, and
even easy, graceful movements of the body.
	When the ill effects of these stays and bands
that injuriously compress, are known, what
lady of even ordinary knowledge, will con-
tinue their use?
	Even gaiters, gloves and shoes, should be
sufficiently large so as not to confine either the
hands limbs or feet; and the latter, shoes,
should have always low broad heels to insure
comfort, not to say health, as the injurious ef-
fect and uncomfortable feeling of a high nar-
row heel, is well known to those who have
given the matter an earnest thought.  The
high, narrow heel, prevents elasticity of step,
and with any attempt to step, the body re-
ceives a jar or jolt, very injurious to females
that have worn them, and in consequence been
obliged to discard them, as many can attest.
	Leather belts are decidedly injurious and
should never be worn. It would indeed seem
that style, fashion and present custom, all
strive to invent and offer for sale articles of
dress especially injurious, and that we blindly
adopt and continue their use in view of all the
alarming consequences. This should not be,
let us rather throw off the shackels which fash-
ion would impose, and think and act in the
future for ourselves.
