TREASURES.

I saw the miser counting greedily his gold.
His hands were shriveled—eye and heart were cold;
I saw him turn the poor man from his door,
With frown, nor give one penny from his store

I've seen in childhood's bright and fairy land,
Bright treasures gleam along the golden strand:
And heard the merry shout of children in their play,
Each seeking gems along the pleasant way.

I saw a maiden silent, thoughtful stand,
And gaze upon a jewel on her fair white hand,
And heard her murmur gently, soft and low—
"The pledge of all the joys my soul can know."

I saw a mother stand beside a cradle bed,
And gently smooth the ringlets on the pillow spread.
And heard her pray—"O bless my darling here"—
Earth holds no other treasure half so dear.

I looked again—she clasped a broken toy,
The plaything once of her departed boy;
As such more prized by far than golden store—
Those little hands now beckon'd from the other shore.

I've read the tale a tiny flower did hold;
The hopes and fears within a letter's fold;
Know all that clasp of hand in hand can tell,
And in the heart how words of love and kindness dwell.

No living soul but hath some treasure hid
From mortal eye beneath its casket lid;
Some deeply hid 'neath cold and sordid gain
And some 'neath waves of cruel bitter pain.

And yet how slight a thing will touch the spring,
And forth to light the hidden treasures bring—
A strain of music known in days long gone,
Or simple words of some remembered song.

O human heart what mystery is thine!
And who can judge thee but the "Power Divine?"
In that great day His eye will read aright,
The story hidden deep from mortal sight.

	HOPE.
