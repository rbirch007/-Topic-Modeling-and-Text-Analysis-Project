					M. A. HARDY.

	HOOPER, WEBER Co., June 14th, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Our Relief Society numbers one hundred
and thirty-eight members and although we
may have been slow in writing to your val-
uable and interesting paper, we have nev-
ertheless been actively engaged in the good
works of duty and benevolence. The poor
and sick have received every attention in
our power to bestow. It is well known that
our settlement extends over a large breadth
of country, consequently our people are
scattered, yet notwithstanding this our
meetings are well attended and we enjoy
ourselves at these assemblies. Mrs. Ade-
line Belnap is President, ably assisted by
Mrs. Margaret Manning, and Mrs. Mary
Jargensen, her counselors, Mrs. Elizabeth
Guilliam, Secretary, and Mrs. Mary Stevens
Treasurer. Unity and a good spirit prevails.
The health of the people is good, the bless-
ings of God are abundantly bestowed upon
us.
	Asking our heavenly Father to bless you
in your noble work among your sisters, and
that the EXPONENT may always be as it is
now a welcome visitor.
	Respectfully, your sister in the Gospel,
