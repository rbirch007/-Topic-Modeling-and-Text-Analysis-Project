NOTES AND NEWS.

	THE KING of Spain has decorated the
Marchioness of Francos with the cross of
the "Noble ladies of Marie Louise."
	A GRAND wedding is to take place soon
at Hyderabad, India. The bridegroom is
one of the most promising men at present
in that vicinity; a few months ago, during
the absence of his uncle in Europe and
Delhi, he officiated as Prime Minister. He
is Mawab, Mookram-ool-dowlah. The bride
is his cousin, the daughter of Sir Salar
Jung.
	VICTORIA OF England, Augusta of Ger-
many, and Marie of Russia are presently to
receive, from the American Women's Cen-
tennial Executive Committe, elaborately
bound copies of "The New Century for
Women," "Reports of the Philanthropic
and Charitable Institutions of the World,"
"Worthy women of our First Century,"
and "Final Report of the Women's Centen-
nial Executive Committee." These books
are records of women's work at the Centen-
nial, and are enclosed in cases made of
Florida red cedar, lined with Vermont
bird's-eye maple. They are to be given in
recognition and acknowledgment of the
courtesy bestowed by these sovereigns up-
on the Women's Centennial Executive
Committee.
