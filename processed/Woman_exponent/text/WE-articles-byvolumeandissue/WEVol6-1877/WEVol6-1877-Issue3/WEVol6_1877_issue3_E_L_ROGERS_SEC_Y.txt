					E. L. ROGERS, Sec'y.

			EPHRAIM, June 5th, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:	The sisters in Ephraim had a very pleas-
ant and profitable time on Thursday, May
31st. There were present, besides our own,
Bishop E. Peterson and one of his counsel-
ors, Elder George Taylor, and other breth-
ren, Mrs. Zina D. Young from Salt Lake
City, Mrs. Mary J. Young and Mr. and Mrs.
Madsen from Gunnison, and Mrs. Christo-
pherson from Manti. Mrs. S. A. Peterson
presided; after the usual opening exercises,
Mrs. Zina D. Young gave us a most excel-
lent address on home industries and silk
manufacture; she showed us specimens of
silk fringe, thread, and handkerchiefs, all
of home material and manufacture. She
recommended Mrs. Jane Haddon to teach
the art of making pillow lace, in which she
is proficient; exhorted the sisters to be
faithful and obedient, and to be strictly
mindful of their duty to their dead friends.
On Friday evening she addressed the young
ladies and gentlemen; her instructions were
almost invaluable, truly full of the Spirit of
God. On Friday, the 1st of June, the 76th
birthday of President Brigham Young,
there was a dinner party given in honor of
the day, at which Mrs. Zina D. Young was
present. May God spare him and his for
many years.
		Your sister,
