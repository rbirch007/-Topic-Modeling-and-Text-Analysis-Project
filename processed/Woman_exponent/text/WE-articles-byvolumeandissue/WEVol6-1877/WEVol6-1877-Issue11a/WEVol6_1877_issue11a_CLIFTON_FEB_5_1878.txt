			CLIFTON, Feb. 5, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	It is with a feeling of thankfulness to
God for the blessings and privileges we
enjoy I write, feeling I must give expres-
sion, through your valuable paper, to the
sentiments of my heart. I realize my in-
ability to write my thoughts properly, ex-
cept by the Spirit of God. I do feel thank-
ful for the good instruction the EXPONENT
contains, and grateful to God for the intelli-
gent women in our midst, who are not
afraid to stand up for woman's rights, and
speak in her defense. I feel interested in
woman having all the rights that belong to
her. Why should she not have the privi-
lege to expand the intelligence that God
has given her, in something more than
household duties and dress? Why should
she not have the privilege of voting for the
officers that govern her, as well as her sons
and husband? I feel to say God bless the
noble women who are laboring for the rights
of woman! Why did God give her intel-
lect equal to man's, if she must not use it


for the welfare of her fellow-beings, and to
promote good in a broader sphere than
housewifery affords?  I think she ought to
have all the rights and blessings she is ca-
pacitated to enjoy and honor, that she may
be better qualified to bring up her sons to
make wise and useful men in the land; and
to teach her daughtersaright that they may
be the wise mothers of a future generation,
that we may keep increasing in intelligence,
until we become a noble people, zealous of
good works, and full of wisdom and under-
standing: this is just what the Lord wants
His people to be.  Ever praying for the
prosperity of all good works, and all who
labor in building up Zion.	ELIZA.
