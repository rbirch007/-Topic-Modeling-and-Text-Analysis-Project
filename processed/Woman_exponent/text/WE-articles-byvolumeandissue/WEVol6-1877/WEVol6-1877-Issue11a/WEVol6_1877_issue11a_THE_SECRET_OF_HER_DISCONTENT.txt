THE SECRET OF HER DISCONTENT!

	It is this: she has only the external em-
blems of happiness. Of the only true con-
tentment that makes a life in the humble
cottage, with garments of the plainest ma-
terial, peaceful and happy, she was utterly
ignorant. Her only idea of happiness is
embodied in fine houses, costly equipages,
elegant furniture and magnificent toilets,
whose splendor shall throw every assem-
blage which she enters into a fever ef ex-
citement, astonishment and envy. As she
is not always successful in producing the
required amount of the latter article, it
follows that she is often unhappy.
	There are, I am sorry to say, many Mad-
ame Kut-a-dashes in the world, and their
pernicious example has a grievous influence
on the minds and hearts of numbers of the 
"coming women." For the sake of the
peace and contentment, as well as the truth,
modesty, and good sense of these "future
mothers of the race," I pray that their
tribe may "grow smaller by degr[-]es and
beautifully less," until not one specimen is
left as a libel on true womanhood.—Mis-
sion Mirror.
