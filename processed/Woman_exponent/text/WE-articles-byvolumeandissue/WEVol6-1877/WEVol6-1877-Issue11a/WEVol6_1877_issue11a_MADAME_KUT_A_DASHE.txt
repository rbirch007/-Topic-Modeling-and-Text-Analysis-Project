MADAME KUT-A-DASHE.

	It was at the opera I saw her. The first
act of "Don Giovanni" was nearly through
when she came sweeping in, with that ob-
sequious usher (who is always so polite to
well dressed people who have reserved
seats, and so curt to those unfortunates who
have none) clearing the way before her to
her place in the dress-circle, and her equally
obsequious husband bringing up the rear
and bearing her ladyship's opera-cloak, bou-
quet, fan and lorgnette.
