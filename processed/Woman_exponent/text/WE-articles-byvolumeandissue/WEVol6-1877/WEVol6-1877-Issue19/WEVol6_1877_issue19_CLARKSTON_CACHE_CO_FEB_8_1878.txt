	CLARKSTON, CACHE CO., Feb. 8, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We held our Annual Meeting on Monday,
the 4th inst. The report of the last year's
doings was read, which showed we had
paid $133 to help build a new Meeting
House, $27 paid in shares to Logan Woolen
Factory, and $19 to the Logan Temple, be-
sides assisting the poor and needy. Means
on hand:—Cash $3,100 lbs of flour, $45 in
Woolen Factory, $89 in Co-op Store and $12
in sheep, 44 bushels of wheat. We have
not been able to get much wheat, as our
crops were mostly destroyed by grasshop-
pers, but we hope to save some this year,
as the grasshoppers all left without deposit-
ing their eggs, and I think we have a fair
prospect to raise a good crop.
	Our Annual Meeting was well attended by
the sisters, and some of the brethren hon-
ored us with their presence; we received
some valuable instructions from our worthy
Bishop and some of the other brethren,
also by our much esteemed President, Mary
Griffin; others of the sisters spoke as well.
We had a good meeting and the Spirit of
God was with us. In the evening we had
a dance and picnic, and all present seemed
to enjoy themselves. There are about forty
members in our Society.
	Desiring the welfare of the great work
in which we are engaged, we remain your
sisters in the Gospel,
	MARY GRIFFIN, President,
	JANE GODFREY, Secretary.
