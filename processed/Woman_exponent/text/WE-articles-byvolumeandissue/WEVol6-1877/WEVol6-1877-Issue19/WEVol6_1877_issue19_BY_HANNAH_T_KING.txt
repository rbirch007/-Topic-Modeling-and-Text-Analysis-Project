BY HANNAH T. KING.

Angels still are on the earth,
	I have seen them, felt their breath,
In the chill simooning dearth,
	In the atmosphere of death.

Angels? yes, they hovered round
	When affliction laid me low,
Placing me on holy ground,
	Leaving Heaven where they go.

Angels! they have bronght to me
	All that I had strength to bear,
Through the gloom they made me see
	Visions, glorious, bright and fair.

Angels! heaven is in the name—
	On my track they've ever been,
If one left, another came,
	Faithful through life's changing scene.

Angels! some stand by me now,
	Firm and faithful by my side,
Last, but first of all below,
	Treasured with a holy pride.

Last, but oh! my most approved!
	Brothers, sisters, helpmeets mine!
Trying scenes thy love has proved
	And my heart is ever thine!

Last, but truer than the first,
	Bound by links that form a chain
Time, nor change can ever burst,
	Through Eternity's domain!

Brothers, sisters, leave me not,
	Let me never more know change—
Thou canst never be forgot
	Through Eternity's broad range.

When, oh! when shall my rapt soul
	Clasp your hands in realms divine?
Bound by laws the Gods control,
	Then each will whisper Mine and Thine!

S. L. City, Feb. 6, 1878.
