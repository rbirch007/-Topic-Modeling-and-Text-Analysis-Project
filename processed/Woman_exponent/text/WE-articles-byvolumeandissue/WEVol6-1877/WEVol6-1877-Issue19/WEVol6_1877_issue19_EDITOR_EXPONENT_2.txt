EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I send you a contribution from the pen
of a young friend of mine, who has been
called early from a circle of loving relatives
and acquaintances.
	MISS Agatha Allula Perry, was born in
Provo, Feb. 28, 1863, and died Nov. 1877,
of typhoid fever. She was a Normal stu-
dent in the B. Y. Academy, and took great
pride in fitting herself for a teacher, being
respected by all from the principal to the
pupils. She was also first counselor in the
Mutual Improvement Association of the
First Ward. Endowed with mind and
manners calculated to win the esteem of
those with whom she associated, with in-
dulgent parents and every prospect of hap-
piness in long life, she was called, at the
early age of fourteen years and eight
months, to join the holy angels in the beau-
tiful land of peace and love, while her sor-
rowing friends can only say "Why is it?"
God knows best. Before her last illness she
wrote the following piece, which so pleased
her teacher that she expressed a desire of
sending it to the Exponent, and I take
pleasure in complying with her wish.
	Provo.	M. J. Tanner.
