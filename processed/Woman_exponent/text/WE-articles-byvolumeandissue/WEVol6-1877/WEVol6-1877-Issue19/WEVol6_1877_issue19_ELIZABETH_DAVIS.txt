	ELIZABETH DAVIS.

	LEVAN, Dec. I1th, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT.
	Thinking a few lines from the Young
Ladies' Improvement Association of Levan
would not be uninteresting to your readers,
we venture a brief report of our Society.
	We were organized, under the direction
of President George Teasdale, on the 3rd of
November, 1877, with the following offi-
cers.
	Miss Cenie Peterson, President.
	Mrs. Olive E. Wilbeck,
	Sarah Francis,			Counselors.
	Miss Sarah D. Pierce, Secretary.
	"	Eliza Smith, Assistant Secretary.
	"	Martha E. Morgan, Treasurer.
	We meet once a week; our Society num-
bers thirty members, who take a great in-
terest in trying to improve their minds.
	We met Dec. 10, at Pres't. Teasdale's, and
he and other brethren were present and

[Column 3]

gave us some very good counsel and in-
struction. A good spirit prevailed through-
out the meeting.
	Hoping that God will bless our Society
and labors, and the labors of all who are
working for the advancement of truth and
righteousness upon the earth, we subscribe
ourselves,
	Your sisters in the covenant of peace.
		GENIE PETERSON, President,
		SARAH D. PIERCE, Secretary.
