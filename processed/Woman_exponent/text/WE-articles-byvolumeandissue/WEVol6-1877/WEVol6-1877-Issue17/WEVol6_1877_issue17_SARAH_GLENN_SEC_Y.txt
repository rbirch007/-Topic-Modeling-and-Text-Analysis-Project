				SARAH GLENN, Sec'y.

		CEDAR CITY, Jan. 10th, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The Relief Society of this place has been
storing grain, in accordance with the coun-
sels of our late President Brigham Young,

[Column 2]
which I consider a great enterprise and an-
other of his fatherly thoughts for the bene-
fit of the poor.
	Our beloved bishop kindly granted us a
room in the Tithing Office, for the storing
of grain, and we have sacked up there one
hundred and sixty bushels of wheat; we
have other property that we intend to turn
into grain as soon as an opportunity offers.
	I am happy to inform you that our worthy
President, Sarah Uric, and her counselors
are alive to their duties, teaching right-
eousness and exhorting the sisters to be one
in all things—in feeding the poor, in pay-
ing our offerings, in donating to build Tem-
ples, and in everything that is calculated to
save and exalt, and raise us in the scale of
intelligence.
	The New Year has come in, and the old
and the young have enjoyed themselves in
the dance; good order and peace prevailed.
The parties break up in good season, re-
membering the counsels from head quarters.
	I am proud to say that the brethren gen-
erally feel like going into co-operation and
bring out the many facilities that we have
around us to make us self-sustaining and
independent of Babylon.
	The young ladies of the Retrenchment
Society feel the necessity of improving
their minds and laying aside the follies of
Babylon, which is advisable to mothers as
well as daughters.
	God grant that we may be just, and our
walk and conversation of that nature that
will claim respect from the righteous.
						A. E. W., Sec'y.
