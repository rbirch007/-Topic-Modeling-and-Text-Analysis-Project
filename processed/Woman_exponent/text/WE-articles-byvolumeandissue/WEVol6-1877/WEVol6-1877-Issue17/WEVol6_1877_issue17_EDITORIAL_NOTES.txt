EDITORIAL NOTES.

	The Municipal Election passed off more har-
moniously than appearances seemed to indi-
cate. The People's Ticket carried by a large
majority.
	The "Deseret News" of Tuesday 12 inst. con-
tains among other good items, an interesting
letter from Washington, by C. A. S., an inter-
view between Hon. George Q. Cannon, Dele-
gate from Utah, and the reporter of the Wash-
ington Post. In this encounter Br. Cannon
seems to have spoken very plainly and in lan-
guage not to be misunderstood.
	Dispatches tell us in reference to the death
of Pope Pius IX, that he was attacked with a
sort of suffocation or difficult breathing at 8
o'clock on Wednesday evening, the 8th inst.,
at 11 o'clock he was very much worse; then he
took the crucifix from under his pillow, and
blessed those around him. He afterwards
blessed the Sacred College at the request of a
Cardinal who was present. The Cardinals in
attendance relieved each other in praying by
the bedside. At 3 40, the corpse was livid.
Solemn masses and requiems for the Pope are
being held in large cities throughout the dif-
ferent countries where Roman Catholic Church-
es are established and great pomp and ceremony
are manifested on his account.
	The official report of the physicians who at-
tended the Pope, report the cause of his death
"Paralysis of the lungs." The Pope was to lie
in state three days in the hall of the con[-]i[-]tory,
after the preliminary congregation, which
took place immediately after his death. The
body will lie in the Basilics of St. Peter await-
ing the last ceremony. It is expected the cer-
emonies will continue nine days, after which
the conclave will close.
