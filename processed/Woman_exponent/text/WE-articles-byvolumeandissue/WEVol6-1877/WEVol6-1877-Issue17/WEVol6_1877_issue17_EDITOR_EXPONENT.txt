EDITOR EXPONENT.

	Sister Wignall wishes to know if any in-
struction could be given through the EXPO-
NENT for giving silk a gloss. Such items
are beneficial to the sisters. The editorial
in your columns on that NEW YEAR, I think
is splendid;also Mrs. Hannah T. King’s poe-
try entitled the “Vale,” true it is that each
year brings its bitters and sweets. Many
in this place have had to mourn the loss of
fathers, mothers, husbands, wives and child-
ren. We know the separation is severe, es-
pecially to the carnal world, but it is not for
us to murmur at Providence, the separa-
tion is only for a time. I feel sorry for those
that take no comfort in reading, they lack
the food for their souls. Sister Snow’s
Hymns, poetry, and writings, what solid
comfort I have received from them, I feel
to say all the time God bless her, every
word she speaks of writes seems to me like
inspiration. Her second volume of poems
just published is beautiful. My husband
bought her likeness for me last summer
knowing that it would be prized by me. I
love to see the noble works of my sisters
and hope some day to be worthy of being
closer associated with them. When I was
at President Young's funeral, I looked at
the grand assembly and thought what a 
meeting! What will it be at the resurrec-
tion? It seems to me that it would be
grand beyond description.
	I wish you and all the readers of the EX-
PONENT A Happy New Year, and many of
them, and I pray God to bless and prosper
you in your labors.
	With love, your sister,
