	BEAR RIVER CITY, Dec. 28th, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
This is the financial report of the Ladies' Re-
lief and Young Ladies Mutual-Improvement
Societies for the year 1877:
Received							$44. 40
Disbursed to the poor				17. 25
In the Treasury					27. 15
Logan Temple						25. 00
	We have 155 bushels of wheat stored. We
have also a $275 share in the Co-op Store. The
elder and younger sisters here are trying to
unite themselves and carry out the counsels of
our late beloved President Young in regard to
storing grain, for we fully comprehend the im-
portance of this mission. The two Societies
are in a prosperous condition; we have been
reorganized lately by our Bishop, W. Neely,
and the following are the names of officers for
Relief Society; Christine Albertsen, President
Karen Christiansen and Trine Abrahams[-]n,
Counselors; Mary Hansen, Secretary; Karen
Christiansen, Treasurer. We have good, active
teachers.
	The officers of the Young Ladies Association
are: Anna Hansen, President; Christine Neeley
and Mary Hansen, Counselors; Mary Ipsen
and Julia Hansen, Secretaries; Mary Chris-
tiansen, Treasurer. We have very good meet-
ings, and the Lord blesses us abundantly in
our meeting together, and we all feel more and
more the important mission we have to per-
form as mothers and daughters in Zion.
	Wishing success for the EXPONENT and all
good enterprises, we remain your sisters in the
Gospel,
	MISS MARY HANSEN, Sec'y R. S.
