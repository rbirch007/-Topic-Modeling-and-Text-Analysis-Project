				ADELAIDE PAXTON, Sec'y. 

		WALLSBURG, Jan. 9th, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We send you a few items in regard to our
Relief Society in this place. Since writing
to you Apr. 18th, 1877, we have added to our
store of wheat twenty-six bushels and six
pounds which makes in all for the year 1877
sixty-six bushels and six pounds; and we
intend to do all we can in regard to saving
and storing grain, although wheat is scarce
here owing to the early frosts last season.
The sisters have donated very liberally ac-
cording to their means. At present we
have no place of our own to put our wheat
in, but one of our good brethren has kindly
loaned us a bin in his granary until we are
able to build one of our own. We continue
to hold our meetings twice a month which
are generally very well attended. The ma-
jority of the sisters are willing, and anx-
ious to perform all the duties required of
them.
	Hoping we may do better this coming
year than we have done in the past, and be
more punctual in reporting, we remain your
sisters in the Covenant,
				NANCY WALL, Pres't.
