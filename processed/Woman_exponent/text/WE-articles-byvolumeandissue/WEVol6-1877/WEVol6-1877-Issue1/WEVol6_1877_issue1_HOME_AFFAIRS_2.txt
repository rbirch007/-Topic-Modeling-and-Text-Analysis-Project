HOME AFFAIRS.
	WE RETURN sincere thanks to Mrs. Emma
Wagstaff for two very elegant bouquets of choice
and beautiful flowers, received recently; these
are kind remembrnaces, most particularly wel-
come to those sitting all day at the desk, with-
out the opportunity of leisure to go and inhale
their refreshing fragrance in the gardens, or
wildwoods.
	DIED AT Huntsville, Weber Co., June 6th,
1877, of heart disease, Mary Jane Dilworth,
wife of Bishop F. A. Hammond and President
of the Relief Society in that place.  She went
with her husband as a missionary to the Sand-
wich Islands in 1850, where she taught the
native children during six years and three
months and was much beloved by them, so
much that they gave her the title of mother.
Her  name will ever be remembered there with
honor and gratitude.  Her death has cast a 
gloom over the whole community where she
resided.
