EDITOR EXPONENT:

 I would like to address a few lines to the
girls through the EXPONENT, if I may be
permitted to do so.  I am myself a girl, and
therefore take an interest in the improve-
ment of the young sisters; I begin to real-
ize that there is a great work for us to do 
in helping carry on the work that our par-
ents have commenced.
 The Retrenchment Associations are, I 
believe, for us to gain much knowledge,
and prepare us for the accomplishment of
a good life-work.  Then girls let us attend
our meetings regularly, and try to improve
ourselves in all things that will tend to
make us useful women in the Kingdom of
God; let us give heed to the counsel and ad-
vice of our presidents; they are placed over
us as teachers, and we should always up-
hold them with our prayers and works.
These associations are calculated in a meas-
ure, to unite the young sisters.  I can see
quite an improvement in this respect among
the girls in this place, since the organiza-
tion of our society, one year ago; and I
trust that this improvement may increase
in every society in Zion.
 If we pray earnestly to God for strength,
He will surely hear our prayers and we will
be able to perform all that the Gospel re-
quires of us.
