EDITORS EXPONENT:
	Thinking you might like to hear what
we are doing in Hyde Park, we determin-
ed to report ourselves.  Our society is pro-
gressing, not rapidly, but as fast as possible;
we have nearly seventy bushels of wheat
securely stored and intend adding to it.
We have given our mite to assist with the
temple and factory.  We intend trying to
raise our own silk, and make our own wear-
ing apparel as much as possible.  As an as-
sociation we enjoy the spirit of the Lord
in our midst, which prompts us to try to
overcome our weaknesses and help in all
good works.  We have a Retrenchment As-
sociation here, presided over by Mrs. Sar-
ah Daines, it is in quite a prosperous con-
dition.  The EXPONENT is always joyfully
received and much information is gained by
perusing its columns.  We have had a great

[Column 2]
deal of sickness and many deaths; there
are few families but what have had to part
with one or more of their little ones.  We
acknowledge the hand of God in all things
and desire to serve Him and ever have His
spirit to guide and strengthen us.  If Miss
E. R. Snow, or Mrs. Z. D. Young, or any
of the sisters who take delight in encour-
aging each other, should be called by duty
or pleasure to Cache Co., we should be
most happy to have them pay us a visit.
That the women of our most holy faith
may have power to do the good they de-
sire, to exalt themselves above the follies
of mankind, is the prayer of your sisters,
	MARY L. WOOLF,
	President.
	MARY E. GRIFFETH,
	Secretary.
