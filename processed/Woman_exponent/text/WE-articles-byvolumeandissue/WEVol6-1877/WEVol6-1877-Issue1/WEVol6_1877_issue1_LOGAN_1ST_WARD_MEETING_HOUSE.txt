	LOGAN, 1st WARD MEETING HOUSE,
							Dec. 5, 1876.
	MINUTES of the meeting for the organi-
zation of the Young Ladies Retrechment
Society.
	Mrs. Elizabeth Benson was appointed to
act as the president of the meeting, and
Miss Kennie Baliff secretary pro tem. Af-
ter the opening excercises Mrs. Benson said
that she was very thankful that the sisters
had come to visit and to organize the
young ladies, and hoped that after their or-
ganization they would be united. By re-
quest Mrs. Elizabeth Howard said that the
time had come for the daughters of Zion to
turn their attention to many things per-
taining to the kingdom of God; counseled
the girls to keep the word of wisdom and to
read good books, gave many good instruc-
tions. Miss E. R. Snow said that before or-
ganizing she wanted to know if the young
ladies wished to be organized, asked them
to make it manifest, and all were desirous.
It was moved, seconded and carried that
Miss Ellen Ricks be appointed President,
Mrs. Sarah A. Card, and Miss Caroline Olsen,
Counselors,  Miss Lydia Crockett, Secre-
tary. Miss Snow said that as the president
was not present we would not elect the oth-
er four counselors, as it was her privilege to
elect them herself; said also, it would be 
proper to have a treasurer; gave excellent 
counsel and explained the many ways in
which we might make ourselves useful in
helping to build up the kingdom of God.
Exhorted us to be punctual in attending our
meetings and to be united. Said that the
sisters of the Relief Society would visit us
and do all they could to aid us. Mrs. Jane
S. Richards, spoke very encouragingly; ad-
vised the girls to be kind and obedient to
their mothers and to do all they could to
help them.
	Mrs. Zina D. Young said that the time
had been well spent and she did not wish to
detain us, but would like to counsel the
girls to set out mulberry cuttings and to 
raise silk worms.
				KENNIE BALLIF,
					Sec'y pro tem.
