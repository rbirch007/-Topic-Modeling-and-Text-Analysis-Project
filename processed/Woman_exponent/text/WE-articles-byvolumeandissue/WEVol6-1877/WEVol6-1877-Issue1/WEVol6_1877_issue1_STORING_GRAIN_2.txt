STORING GRAIN.

	RICHFIELD, SEVIER Co., April 5th, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I will endeavor to give some information
concerning the progress of our Society; we
assure you that the sisters of Richfield are
not altogether dead, although so long un-
heard from. We read with surprise the in-
structions, when first given through the
EXPONENT, for the Relief Societies to Store
up Grain, and our hearts were filled with a
a true desire to obey the call from the ven-
erable leader of our church. It is a proof of
his confidence in the sisters, and we ought
to rise up in our might, yet with all humil-


ity, to engage in this noble enterprise, re-
membering that thereby we shall be able to
make restitution for past wrongs, for had
we, as a general thing, been obedient and
wise, more could have been accomplished
than has been, towards building up Zion.
