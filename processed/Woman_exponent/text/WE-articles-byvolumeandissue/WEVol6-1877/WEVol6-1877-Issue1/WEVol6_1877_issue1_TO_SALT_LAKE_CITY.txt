TO SALT LAKE CITY.

O City of the wilderness,
     Made beauteous by untiring toll;
Industry have thee lovliness,
     And fertilized they barren soil.
When sunshine gilds the western skies,
     Enwreathing thee in dazzling green,
Greeting the travelers' weary eyes
    Thou seem'st the desert's queen.

Thou'st made thy throne the eternal hills,
    Whose strength is freedom's guarantee,
Whose treasured snows, the mountain rills,
    And canon torrents bear to thee.
When "Sol" in radiance on us beams,
    And freshness given to all things here,
What robes of green 'mid sparkling streams
    Thy glorious summer's wear.

Made great by precious trophies won, 
    Of mind and skill, from many lands
Where want and sin their courses run
    And error gilds her strengthened bands.
Won in the power of truth divine--
    Here is the truth of God adored,
Not dragged in chains in grief to pine,
    Not gathered by the sword.

Shall slavery ever drag thee down?
    Not while they mountains kiss the sky,
While round thee craggy strong holds frown
    True liberty shall never die,
Nor avarice plant unholy bowers
    To desecrate the sacred soil.
Want's rude despoiler raze they towers
    To mock the sweat of honest toil,

Already worth's engraved thy name,
    Undimmed with blood, on history's page;
Thy genius and they new-born fame
    Rebuke the spirit of the age-
Wrong rampant, virtue in the shade, 
    Wealth lending might power to ill,
Religion mean and servile made, 
    To speak wealth's sovereign will. 

Dear sought out refuge of the meek, 
    Be thou the home of liberty!
With truth and wisdom may we week
    To light thy future destiny.
Science, industry, faith and lgiht
    Have come to worship from afar,
To hail thy birth and heaven-born right,
    And be thy guiding star.
				R. SMITH.
15th Ward, Salt Lake City
