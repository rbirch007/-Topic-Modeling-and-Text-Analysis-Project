EDITOR EXPONENT.

	It is our sad duty to record the death of our
dearly beloved sister Lavina B. Mitchell, who
died in Kamas March 22nd, 1878, of inflama-
tion of the lungs. She had been a member of 
the church for many years, and had passed
through many of the persecutions of the
Saints. She had embraced the principle of
Celestial Marriage in which she lived a true
and noble life. Rearing children and teaching
them the true principles of the gospel, was a
duty that she dearly loved and cheerfully per-
formed. One orphan child she took at the age
of three months and nursed him as her own,
the maternal love was scarcely discernible
from that which she felt for her own offspring,
and deeply and truly was her love returned. She
bore twelve children five of whom died in in-
fancy One, her eldest daughter had died two
months previous to her mother's decease. This
was a dreadful blow to the fond mother, she
had often expressed herself satisfied with life
but now she prayed for life and strength that
she might help to care for the five motherless
children that her daughter had left. But God
had ordered otherwise and she has gone to
meet the reward of the noble deeds done in
the body. Her kind affectionate disposition
made her beloved by young and old. She
leaves a large circle of relatives and friends to
mourn her loss.
