INSCRIBED TO BELLE AND MAGGIE.

	Methinks the hand of angels traced
	The Picture which our window graced;
	Design so perfect and so fair,
	No power of chance could paint it there.

	'Twas Christmas morn a year ago,
	Our eyes beheld, with morn's first glow,
	This picture, lovely, clear and plain,
	In frost-work on the window pane.

	A broken wall, with draw-bridge thrown
	Across a moat with briars o'ergrown;
	Within, in desolation wild,
	A castle in deep ruins piled.

	While rising o'er the ruins high,
	A cross was pointing to the sky;
	And o'er the cross, resting in air,

	Oh, emblem of our earthly day,
	Life's ever-changing, fitful way.
	The Moat I view as time's swift tide,
	The Bridge the years o'er which we glide.

	The Castle of our faded dreams,
	A perfect emblem truly seems;
	While Faith's the Cross that points above,
	Guiding us on to heaven and love.

	The Picture did not long remain,
	But left its stamp on heart and brain;
	Our bright hopes may in ruin lie,
	God's promises can never die.

	We gazed in silence, 'til at once
	We all exclaimed, 'Tis meant for us,
	A lesson on Christ's natal day,
	To cheer and help us on our way.

	Help us our Father well to learn,
	Help us the Crown of life to earn,
	The Cross to bear—"Thy will be done;"
	Guide us, we pray, 'till Heaven is won.
