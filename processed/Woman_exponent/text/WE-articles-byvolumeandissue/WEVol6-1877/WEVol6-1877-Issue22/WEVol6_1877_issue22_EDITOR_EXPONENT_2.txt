EDITOR EXPONENT:

	I send you a few words from the Young
Ladies' Mutual Improvement Association

[Column 2]
of this Ward. Our Society was organized
on the 8th of January, 1878. The Sisters
met pursuant to the call of Bishop Stuart,
who, in company with Elders McKinnon
and Rex, met with the sisters. The Bish-
op stated the object of the meeting, and
presented the following names for officers,
which were unanimously sustained by the
sisters:
	Jane Brough, President.
	Agnes Smith,	 } Counselors.
	Rebecca Quinney. }
	Annie Christianson, Secretary.
	Augustine McMinn, Assistant Secretary.
	Jeanette Cameron, Treasurer.

	The young ladies responded to the call of
the Bishop to organize, and thirty enrolled
their names as members. Elders McKin-
non and Rex addressed the meeting, and
gave much good instruction to the young
sisters. President Jane Brough and her
Counselors also made a few remarks to the
sisters and encouraged them to press on-
ward in well doing. A collection was tak-
en up to defray the expenses of procuring
books, stationery, etc., for the use of the
Society.
	Since our organization we have met
weekly, and a great desire is manifest
among the young sisters to improve the
talents which God has blessed them with,
and we are increasing in numbers and good
works. We hold two general meetings for
testimony, etc., and then one working
meeting, where the young sisters are taught
useful branches of industry.
	Ever praying for the welfare of the
daughters of Zion, we remain your sisters
in the Gospel.
			JANE BROUGH, President,
