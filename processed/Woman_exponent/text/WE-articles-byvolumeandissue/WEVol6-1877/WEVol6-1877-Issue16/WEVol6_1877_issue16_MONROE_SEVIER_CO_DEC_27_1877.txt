	MONROE, SEVIER CO., Dec. 27, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT.
	Thinking a few lines from the Young La-
dies Improvement Association of this place
would not be uninteresting to your readers,
we venture a report of our progress since
we were organized as an Association.
	We were organized under the direction
of President Franklin Spencer, Sept. 13th,
1877, with the following officers:
	Mrs. Julia Larsen, President.
	"	Ann Sidd,		}	Counselors.
	"	Mary Ann Jones,	}
	"	Francis Collings, Secretary.
	Miss Minnie Cooper, Treasurer.
	Since our organization we have raised
ten dollars in money, with which we have
purchased shares in our new Co-operative
Store. We have a Y. M. M. I. A., which
is in good condition. We with them are
combining our efforts to procure a library
for our mutual benefit.
	We hold meetings once in two weeks,
which are lively; in them we feel well and
enjoy the Spirit of the Gospel. We desire
to improve our talents and prepare our-
selves for usefulness, in obedience to the
commandments of God, for the building up
of His kingdom and promoting the welfare
of Zion, by refraining from that which we
know to be evil, by trying to elevate and
improve our minds, and by living accord-
ing to the requirements of the glorious
principles of the gospel of Christ.
	We are also raising some donations for
the Temple at Manti.
	Wishing success to your valuable paper,
which we consider a blessing to all the sis-
ters of the Church of Jesus Christ of Lat-
ter-day Saints, We remain your sisters in
the Gospel,
		JULIA LARSEN, President,
