SCIENTIFIC NOTES.

THE nebular hypothesis, one of the grand-
est conceptions of the human mind, has be-
gun to be questioned by eminent astrono-
mers.  Facts, which at first were thought
to be favorable to it, now seem to many to
be fatal to it.  Marked exceptions have
been found to the rule that the movement
of all of the bodies of the solar system is
in the same direction round the sun.  The
satellites of Jupiter, for instance, is retro-
grade.  Again, the satellite of Mars nearest
the planet has a much more rapid motion
than Mars itself—a fact which, on the nebu-
lar hypothesis, would be impossible.  Lastly,
the new "star" in Cygnas, months ago was
exceedingly bright, but now is very dim
and has all the appearance of a nebula; but
on this nebular hypothesis the course should
have been exactly the reverse.  It should
have developed from a nebula to a star.
However, it is too soon to weep or rejoice--
as one may be disposed--over the supposed
collapse of this theory.  It has passed
through grave dangers and apparent contra-
dictions before this, and a fuller acquaint-
ance with the phenomena which now puz-
zle or perplex astronomers may add strength
to the theory.—Graphic, Oct. 1877.
