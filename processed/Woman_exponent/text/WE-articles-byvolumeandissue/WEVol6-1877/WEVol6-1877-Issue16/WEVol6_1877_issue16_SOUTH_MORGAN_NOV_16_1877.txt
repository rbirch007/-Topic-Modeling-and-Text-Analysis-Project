		SOUTH MORGAN, NOV. 16 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Thinking a few lines would not be out of
place just to let you know what we have
been doing here, I subjoin the following:
	The sisters in this place feel desirous to
do all in their power for the furtherance of
this great and glorious work and in car-
rying out the instructions of our beloved
President in storing grain. Several have
had grain sown in the hope of getting a
good crop, but owing to grasshoppers and
frost it is not so good as it otherwise might
have been, but still by the blessing of our
Heavenly Father, we have raised in this
place fifty-six bushels which is carefully
stored in a bin in the Co-op store, placed
there by the society that it might be kept
separate and distinct from any thing else;
as to our future prospects we intend to do
all we can both in raising wheat and by do-
nations. We have a sewing meeting once
a week, and a business meeting once a
month, when the teachers give in their re-
ports; these meetings are well attended,
and each member seems desirious to do all
the good they can, in counseling and guid-
ing the young in the paths of truth and
virtue.
	That every effort that is being made for
the good of this work may be crowned with
success is the prayer of your sister in the
gospel,				S. A. RAWLE, Sec'y.
