					MARY ANN KENT.


BLOOMING.—A little daughter had watch-
ed with interest the unfolding of flowers
in a garden, seeing a rose-blossom change
from a bud to a full-blown rose in a short
time. One day she saw a turkey-rooster
suddenly expand his tail into a fan, in the
act of strutting, when she ran to her moth-
er and exclaimed: "Oh, mother, I have
just seen a turkey bloom out."
