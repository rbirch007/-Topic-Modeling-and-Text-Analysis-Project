	GRANTSVILLE, TOOELE, July 16, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The sisters of this branch of the Relief
Society are trying to keep up with the
times. There are no poor uncared for; the
teachers are faithful in the discharge of
their duties. Retrenchment from every
evil is taught in our meetings; backbiting
is condemned. We have nearly three hun-
dred bushels of grain stored away and in-
tend to still take care of all we can get.
The counsel of President Young to supply
our own wants is being carried out to a
great extent. We shall endeavor to plant
out a lot, that the brethren has given us, in
mulberry trees next spring, for the purpose
of raising silk-worms. We are striving to
become united in doing all the good we
can. The young ladies are also doing well.
	With best wishes for the prosperity of
the EXPONENT we remain your sisters in
the Gospel, M. A. Hunter, President; Jane
Eastham, and Sarah Hale, Counselors.
				M. A. HOUSE, Secretary.
