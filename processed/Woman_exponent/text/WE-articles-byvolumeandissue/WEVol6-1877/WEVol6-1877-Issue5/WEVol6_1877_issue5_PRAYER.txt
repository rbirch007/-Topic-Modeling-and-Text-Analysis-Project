			PRAYER.

	I often think of the word prayer and its
great efficacy-indeed, without prayer how
could we endure trails? It fills the heart


with gratitude and love to approach the
throne of grace by supplication; it soothes
the weary; it puts the mind to rest; it com-
forts us through sorrows, when we feel that
we must give up, if we cannot find help.
When we see our loved ones taken from us,
one by one, and we feel ofttimes that God
has forgotten us; those we have thought
our true friends forsake us, and our neigh-
bors slight us; we have no friends and
nothing worth living for; disappointments
befall us, and a chastening hand is upon us
and our trials seem more than we can bear;
it is then, if we will humble ourselves and
say Father, forgive me my sins, and teach
me to do thy wiil, that we realize the words
“Ask of me in my name and you shall re-
ceive.” Then we will find consolation in
prayer; we begin to feel like taking up
our cross and jogging along and going about
our duties, saying, “Blessed is the name of
the Lord.”
	Dear sisters, do you ever feel like this?
if you do you cannot get such comfort in
any other way as you may through prayer.
Then how necessary it is for us to pray.
we should keep it in our minds continually,
that all we do and say, every action of our
lives, should be the subject of prayer.  Our
minds may be filled with good thoughts for
each other, that we may grow better day
by day that we may be a comfort to those
that are around us, that when we lay our
bodies down to rest we shall be missed, and
be worthy to meet those loved ones that
are gone before us.  Then let us always
pray.	
							A SISTER.
	Alpine, Utah Co., June 20, 1877.
