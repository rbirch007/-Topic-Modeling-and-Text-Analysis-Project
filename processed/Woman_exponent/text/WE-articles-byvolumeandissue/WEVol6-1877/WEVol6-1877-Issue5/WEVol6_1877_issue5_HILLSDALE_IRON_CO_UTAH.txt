		HILLSDALE, IRON CO. UTAH.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	A Relief Society was organized at this
place June 3, 1877, under the direction of
Elder Seth Johnson, president of the
branch, and several other brethren; we had
an excellent meeting and much good in-
struction was given. The election of offi-
cers were as follows: For president, Mrs.
Mary A. Johnson; counselors Mrs. Martha
A. Wilson, and Mrs. Julia D. Wilson; Sec-
retary, Mrs. Susanna D. Pinney; Assistant
Secretary, Miss Martha A. Wilson; Treas-
urer Mrs. Mary J. Johnson. We live in a
somewhat scattered condition on account of
many having large farms but we meet to-
gether and enjoy ourselves in our meetings
very much, at the present. We number
fifteen members, but expect that number
will continue to increase. We are making
preparations for gathering and storing grain
according to the counsels of President
Young, and intend to try to keep up with
the times in every good work. We have a
Young Men's Mutual Improvement Asso-
ciation at this place which is doing much
good among the young. Our general Sab-
bath meetings are well attended and the
spirit of union and good feeling prevails

[Column 2]
and all seem interested in trying to carry
out the counsel of the priesthood of God
upon the earth, and although the wicked
may howl and strive to utterly destroy the
Kingdom of God, our faith is that it will
triumph, and the wicked receive their just
reward.
	Hoping that prosperity may attend all
the branches of the Relief Society through-
out the Territory, I remain your sister in
Gospel,
			SUSANNA D. PINNEY,
						Secretary.
