			R. S. REPORTS.

	The General Retrenchment meeting in
the Fourteenth Ward Assembly Rooms
Saturday, July 21, was exceedingly inter-
esting. President Mrs. M. I. Horne re-
marked that the minutes of the last meet-
ing were full of instruction and showed the
great improvement being made. She gave
good advice to mothers in respect to the
care of their children and especially con-
cerning their diet. Remarks were made
by several of the sisters who attended the
recent meeting of the Weber Relief Socie-
ties inOgden, in regard to the good in-
structions and wise counsel given there to
women by President Young and other
brethren. Many good things were said on
the subject of being self-sustaining and the
practice of economy. The study of medi-
cine or more particularly obstetrics was
commented upon, and the idea of encour-
aging our girls to make themselves profi-
cient in the study and practice, and learn to
obey all the laws of life. These meetings
are replete with good instruction, and ex-
ercise a powerful influence for good in the
midst of the saints.
