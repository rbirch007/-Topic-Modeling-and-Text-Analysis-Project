				WAITING.

	How sad it is to see a pale weary face
pressed against the window-pane or stand-
ing on the threshold for hours, waiting for
some loved one who does not come, and
how sad to hear the weary sigh of disap-
pointment when, after hours of waiting, one
must turn away for we have waited until
twilight has shut out all objects and the
lamps within are lighted, the curtains
drawn, and we must not look any longer for
we cannot discern any object in the gather-
ing darkness. Now we can only sit and
wait to hear the sound of the footsteps that
have grown so familiar; but it does not
come and perhaps may never come again.

		"Ah! how many wait forever
			For the steps that do not come,
		Wait until the pitying angels
			Bear them to a peaceful home."


Sometimes we grow weary—of life wait-
ing. Waiting for promises that are never
fulfilled; for dreams that are never real-
ized, and hopes that are never gained. Our
lives are made up of waiting, from the 
cradle to the grave; the child waits for the
privilege of being a man, and the man
waits for his rest in heaven, for life has
enough trials and vexations to make us
sometimes wait impatiently for the end
when we can rest. When we are in poverty
we are waiting for prosperity, and when
we gain it, if we ever do, we then wait for
happiness, which wealth alas! does not al-
ways bring. I think though, that there
are a few sweet happy souls who can meet
disappointments bravely, who have learned
that cruel lesson of waiting patiently and
long; and when adversity overtakes them,
wait with a loving patience that is truly
noble, for the blessed sun of prosperity.
But alas! how many go down to the grave
waiting. We must all learn to wait, pa-
tiently if possible, always trusting in the
Alwise Father who overrules all things for
our good.					JUNE.
