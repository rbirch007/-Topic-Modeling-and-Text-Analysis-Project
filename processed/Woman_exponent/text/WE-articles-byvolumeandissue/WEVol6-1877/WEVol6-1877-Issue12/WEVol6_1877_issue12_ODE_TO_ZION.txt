ODE TO ZION.

Afflicted Zion, rise and shine!
	For thy deliverance now draws near;
The time has come—the chosen time—
	That thou must gloriously appear.

The Lord has brought thee by His hand,
	And sheltered thee beneath the rock:
While all His judgments heavy fall
	On those who at His Gospel mock.

Thy bleating flocks in pastures green,
	In flowery meadows make their bed;
While bounteous sheaves of golden grain
	For very fulness droop their head.

And as the hills around thee stand,
	Thy towering Temples soon shall rise,
And incense burn, and chorus ring
	Continual praises to the skies.

The Lord in thy assemblies waits;
	Shonts of a king amongst them are;
In power and greatness He shall sit
	And judge the nations from afar.

Lift up your heads, ye gathering Saints,
	And sing your great Redeemer's praise:
That you upon Mount Zion stand,
	Amongst the Saints of Latter-days.

Salt Lake City.	ELL[--] LEES
