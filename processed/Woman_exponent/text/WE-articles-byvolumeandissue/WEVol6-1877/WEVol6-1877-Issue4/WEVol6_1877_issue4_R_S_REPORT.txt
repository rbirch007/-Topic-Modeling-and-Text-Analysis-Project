R. S. REPORT.

		SMITHFIELD, June 14th, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
 I send you a report of our annual meet-
ing, held May 12th, 1877. It was well at-
tended and we had a good time. The re-
port for the year past is as follows:
Property on hand, donations, dividends in
	store, and sundries.................$695 42
Disbursements to poor, school, and
	on Society house..................... 303 35
Property belonging to Relief Soci-
	ety, vouchers on Utah Northern
	R. R, shares in Co-op store, knit-
	ting machine, quilts, sundries..327 13
	Bishop Samuel Roskelly gave us good in-
structions in regard to home manufacture,
wishing us as a Society to  engage in every
branch of home industry that we can; es-
pecially to plant mulberry trees and go to
raising silk,  and thus help to fulfil the
mission given to the women of Utah. Our
climate is rather cold for the mulberry, but
we intend to obey counsel and leave the re-
sult with the Lord.
	President E. T. Morehead gave us some
good counsel upon our duties as a Relief
Society; said in looking back to the time
when we were first organized she could see
so much improvement, that it gave her
great joy, and she hoped we would con-
tinue to progress in the future.
	We have almost finished our Society
House, and then we expect to have more
means to help build Temples, and for any
other good work that may be needed.
May God bless the EXPONENT and every
good work that is for the building up of
Zion.
