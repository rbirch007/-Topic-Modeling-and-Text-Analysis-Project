THE FOURTH IN ST. GEORGE.—The Fourth
has passed off very quietly, I am sure there
must have been a rejoicing in heaven to-day
over the freedom given to one hundred and
fifty of our dead. It was not thought advisa-
ble to close the Temple to-day, and all who
spent their Fourth in that Holy House, felt to
rejoice and give glory to God, that they were
permitted to unloose the bands and give free-
dom to some of their frieuds who have passed
behind the vail. One hundred of the brethren
and sisters who went through to-day in behalf


of the dead, volunteered to act for and in be-
half of Elder Wilford Woodruff, and made him
a very agreeable surprise; as he has the gene-
ology of between four and five thousand dead
relatives to attend to, he cannot hope to do
the work without help. 
	The weather is quite warm in the middle of
the day, but we have cool, pleasant nights.
The crops are in a most flourishing condition.
The brethren and sisters are all feeling the
blessing of having a Temple in their midst,
and the interest in the redemption of our dead
continues to increase.	
	Hoping I have not over-taxed your valuable
time,allow me to wish for the continued success
of your able and instructive paper. I do not think
it is right for any sister to be without the
WOMAN'S EXPONENT, if she can possibly pay
for it; better to do without the new dress and
make the old one answer. I consider it neces-
sary,as it makes us acquainted with each other’s
feelings, views and desires, and really helps us
to live our religion.
			Your sister,
