			R. S. REPORTS.

	The General Retrenchment Meeting in
the Fourteenth Ward Assembly Rooms,
Saturday, July 7th, was one of unusual in-
terest. Counselor B. W. Smith presided
in the president's absence, and after the
usual exercises and reading of minutes
from junior societies, addressed the assem-
bly in an able and efficient manner upon
the duties of saints and the future destiny
of the young. Counselor Z. D. Young and
S. M. Kimball followed her and spoke upon
silk-culture and home institutions, the op-
portunities for education and for self-educa-
tion; referred to the Medical Class now
about to resume their meetings for instruc-
tion, and many other important items.
Mrs. E. Pixton from Leeds, spoke of her
having been absent one year from the meet-
ings, of the privileges and blessings en-
joyed by the sisters here, of diamonds in
the rough and diamonds polished etc.
Mrs. E. S. Barney, Mrs. R. Jones, Mrs. M.
E. A. Watmough, Mrs. Hannah T. King,
Mrs. W. East each addressed the meeting
in a pleasant and happy manner upon the
subjects which concern the temporal and
spiritual welfare of the saints. These meet-
ings strengthen encourage and edify the
sisters in the spirit of the Gospel, and a
very great improvement is apparent in
those who are stepping out in the develop-
ment of the talents in their possession.
The home industries are increasing and im-
proving and there is a spirit manifested
by the sisters who attend these meetings to
promote all home industries.
