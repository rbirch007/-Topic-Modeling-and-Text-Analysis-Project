	ESCALANTE, IRON Co., June 20, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I will proceed to tell you about this beau-
tiful little valley; it is situated fifty miles
east of Panguitch, and has a mild climate;
we have no mail running here yet, but re-
ceive our mail matter from persons travel-
ing to and from Panguitch. Some of the
wheat has been headed out here for two
weeks; wild flowers of every color and var-
iety grow in abundance, giving the valley a
very beautiful appearance. There are about
fifty families in the settlement. On March
13th, 1877, our Bishop, Andrew Schow.
and several of the brethren organized a
Relief Society and a Retrenchment Asso-
ciation; officers of Relief Society as follows:
Mrs. Mary Ann P. Schow, President; Mrs.
Susannah Heaps, Mrs. Lydia C. Adams,
Counselors; Mrs. Jane S. Coleman and Miss
Mary Coleman, Secretaries; Mrs. Vesta Lu-
cretia Twitchell, Treasurer. We have teach-
ers and president of teachers appointed;
they visit the sisters once a month. We
hold semi-monthly meetings alternately to
speak and work. Our president is an ener-
getic woman and means to take hold and
go into the silk-industry, and have mul-
berry trees set out in the Relief Society
lot. We desire to be co-workers with the
rest of our sisters in assisting to build up
and develop Zion. Money is scarce here,
it is fifty miles to the nearest market.
	The Report of the Escalente Retrench-
ment Association organized March 13, 1877,
is as follows: Mrs. Catherine Dewel, Presi-
dent; Misses Sarah May Coleman, Lydia C.
Adams, Mrs. Mary E. Alvey, Misses Mary
Alice Barker, and Mary Coleman, Counsel-
ors; Mrs. Mary Ann P. Schow, Superin-
tendent; Misses Mary Alice Barker and
Harriet Larrmie, Secretaries; Mrs. Eleanor
Barker, Treasurer.
	JANE S. COLEMAN, Secretary of
					Relief Society.
