	KIRTLAND, OHIO, Nov. 15, 1877.
DEAR AUNT EM:
	Your favor of the 4th inst. came to hand
a day or two ago, and, as usual, pleased me
with its interesting contents. The very
topic so dear to you, but which you present
to me with an apology for so doing, is the
one which engaged my closest attention; so 
you see, in a measure, I am not the same
being as when frivolity was a ruling char-
acteristic of my nature. But I do not mean
to play the egotist—all I wish to say is that
I too claim a deep and abiding, a growing
and earnest sympathy with all that pertains
to the upbuilding of Zion. Her destiny is
mine, for I am a part of her; her name is 
dear, for it is a theme of which I never
weary; her people are my people, her God
my God. I am but a feeble portion of the 
great and increasing whole, a slender piece
of the stupendous frame; but in the hands
of the Almighty, who takes the weak things
of the world to accomplish His purposes,
who can say what I may not do to be useful
to the cause of Zion? Moreover, "we are
