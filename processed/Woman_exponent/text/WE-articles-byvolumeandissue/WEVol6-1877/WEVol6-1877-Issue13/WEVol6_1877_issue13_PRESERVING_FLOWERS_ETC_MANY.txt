	PRESERVING FLOWERS, ETC.——Many
flowers and leaves may be beautifully pre-
served by covering them in every part with
melted sperm. The best way is to cut a
candle into pieces and melt it in an old sau-
cer on the back of the range—it must not
be hot enough to smoke. First dip in the
stems, the sperm will harden almost at
once. so that the stem may be held while
immersing the flower or blade of the leaf.
Dip but once and that quickly. The pro-
cess is too severe for delicate petals, melt-
ing them as it were together, of others
heat will change the colors. Variegated
leaves, such as those of the Pelargoniums,
Maples, Ivies and fancy-leaved Caladiums
will perhaps give the best satisfaction.
When arranged into bouquets or wreaths
they are less liable to curl if protected by
a glass globe, like those used for wax flow-
ers; still, if not so protected, with a little
care in a coolish atmosphere they will re-
tain their form and colors for months.
