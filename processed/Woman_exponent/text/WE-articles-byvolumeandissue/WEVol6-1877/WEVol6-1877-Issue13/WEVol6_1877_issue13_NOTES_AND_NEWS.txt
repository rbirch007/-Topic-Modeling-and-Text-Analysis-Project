NOTES AND NEWS.

	THE LADIES of Missouri sent a Delegate to
the funeral of Senator Morton, with a shaft of
laurel and ivy leaves, surmounted by a white
dove, from whose beak depended a wreath of
smilax and ivy, and the motto, "A slight tes-
timonial of the gratitude we bear to one who
was the unswerving advocate of Woman Suf-
frage."  The offering was made by Mrs. Fran-
ces Minor and Mrs. Phebe Cozens, of Missouri.
	FRANCE.—The resignation of the ministry
has at length been accepted by President Mac-
Mahon.  They will, however, retain their port-
folios until their successors in office are quali-
fied.  In the Chamber of Deputies, after stormy
speeches by Duc de Broglie and M. Gambetta,
the Electoral Inquiry Bill was voted by 320 to
203.
