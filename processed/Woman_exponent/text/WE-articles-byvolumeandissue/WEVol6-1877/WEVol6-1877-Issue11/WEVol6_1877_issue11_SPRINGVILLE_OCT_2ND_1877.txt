	SPRINGVILLE, Oct. 2nd, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Ever feeling desirous to mingle our
voices, and unite our efforts with our sister
societies, we the Relief Society of Spring-
ville send you these few lines. We are
trying to do all in our power to help the
cause of truth, to minister to the wants of
the poor, and the needy among us, to help
to build Temples, and in our feeble way as-
sist in every good work. The EXPONENT
is ever a welcome messenger among us, and
while reading the many interesting ac-
counts of the various works in home in-
dustries, the silk culture, and other de-
partments, in the different societies, we al-
ways feel to renew our diligence in relation
to every thing towards practically sustain-
ing ourselves; and feel like asking our
Heavenly Father to hasten the day when
the Latter-day Saints are entirely free,
when the last fetters shall be loosed that
bind us to Babylon.
	We remain your sisters in the Gospel,
		MARY E. WHITING, Sec'y.
		MARY BATE, Assistant Sec'y.
