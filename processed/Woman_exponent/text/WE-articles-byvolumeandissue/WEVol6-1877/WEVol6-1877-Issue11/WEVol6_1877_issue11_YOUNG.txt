YOUNG.

Sleep for the weary eyes,
	Peace for the throbbing breast,
We all must follow bye and bye!
	Then rest, beloved rest.
Beloved of thy people, sleep!
	No cares disturb thy breast,
Well hast thou earned thy calm respose
	Why should we grudge thy rest?

All grief is selfish, and we mourn
	Our councelor and guide;
The poor man's friend, the friend of truth,
	The Leader, proved and tried.
Thank God! He sleeps in peace;
	The last glance of his eyes
Rested on those he loved; his own
	Recieved his latest sighs.
Farewell! 'tis evening now
	Soon will the darkness flee.
How sweet to meet in the dawning light
	From sin and sorrow free.
