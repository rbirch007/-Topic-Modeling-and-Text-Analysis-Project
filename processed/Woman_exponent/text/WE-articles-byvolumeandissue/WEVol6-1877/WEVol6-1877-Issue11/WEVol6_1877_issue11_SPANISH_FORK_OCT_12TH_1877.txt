	SPANISH FORK, Oct. 12th, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT.
	By request I send you a few items of re-
port in regard to storing grain by our Re-
trenchment Association in Spanish Fork.
We were called out by our President Mary
J. Morrison, Sept. 18, to glean, and thereby
save a portion of the grain that would have
been wasted. Seventy-five girls volunteer-
ed to go, we were organized into compa-
nies of ten, with a team to each company;
Bros. G. H. Brimhall, Z. Coltrin, E. A.
Wilson and John Beckstun, of the Y. M.
M. I. A. assisted with teams. It being
so late in the season the grain was rather
dry, however we succeeded in saving
twenty-three bushels and a half. Our ob-
ject was to save, in accordance with our de-
parted President's counsel. We would all
have willingly donated our peck; but that
would have been taken from the abundant
harvest already secured, this we saved of
what would have been left in the fields.
Bishop Snell very kindly let us have room
in the Tithing Office for our grain for the
present. In the evening the City Hall was
at our service for a party; although feeling
too tired to dance much, we felt happy that
we had been working for a good cause for
Zion. We feel well as a society and wish
at all times to be dictated by the Spirit of
God that our efforts may prove successful.
	Wishing success to the EXPONENT, I re-
main,
	Your Sister,
		E. P. LITTLE, Secretary.
