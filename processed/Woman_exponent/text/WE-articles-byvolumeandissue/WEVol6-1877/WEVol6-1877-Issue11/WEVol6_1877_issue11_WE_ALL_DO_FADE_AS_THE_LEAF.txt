WE ALL DO FADE AS THE LEAF.

Oh! see but the leaves that around us are falling
From boughs, where so late they were blooming and gay.
And tell me if they are not silently calling
On us to prepare for a like coming day.

Oh! I weep pretty leaves, that thy doom is recorded,
That death, cruel Spoiler has robbed me of thee:
But the lesson thou breathest shall not be unhoarded
But stor'd in my heart shall thy whisperings be.

Adieu too, ye flowers—in love were ye tended,
And repaid me in sweetness in beauty and health,
But now to the tomb are thy beauties descended,
And I mourn as a miser the loss of his wealth.

But let me reflect on the lesson you're giving.
That the summer of life passes quickly away
And the plants that are human and beauteous, and living
Must soon feel the blights of a wintry day! 

And that we like the leaves, and the flowers, must die
And repose for a time in the bosom of earth,
But rise when the Spring that's eternal on high
Shall summon the just to a heavenly birth.

In that glorious Land is the "Tree of Life" waving
And the rose without thorns undecaying shall bloom,
There man—(his Redeemer's atonement all saving)
Transplanted shall be from the jaws of the Tomb.
