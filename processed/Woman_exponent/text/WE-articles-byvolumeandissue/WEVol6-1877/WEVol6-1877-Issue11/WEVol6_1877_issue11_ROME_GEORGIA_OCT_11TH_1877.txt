		ROME, GEORGIA, Oct. 11th, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I wish to address a few words to you, as a
sister in the everlasting Gospel. I am still
a true believer of the same Gospel that I
have been converted to through Brother
Morgan's preaching. I have been hoping
to see the day when I could leave Babylon;
and I am still in hopes, and think that the
Lord will open up the way, but the pros-
pects for immigration are very gloomy as
yet; the brethren have not been successful
in selling their land so far, it seems that
they can't do much towards emigrating
at this time, in consequence of being poor
and not being able to do anything much for 
themselves. There are several of the
brethren who cannot help themselves to
emigrate, if they can't sell their land.
There is a vast multitude of peoble in our
midst who have been plunged into great
poverty, in consequence of the late war,
and my father and family are among that
unfortunate number. They have only the
sale of their land to depend upon, and find
no sale for it. I tell my parents that I do
not think that I can stay here; I do not
think I will have to remain a great while,
or at least that is my feeling now; and as it
is my greatest desire to gather with the
saints in Zion. I do earnestly hope that I
may be able to accomplish my desire, and I 
feel that the Lord will render his aid unto
me.
	I remain as ever your sister in the true
and everlasting Gospel,
