R. S. REPORTS.
	The General Retrenchment Meeting in the
Fourteenth Ward Assembly Rooms, held their
semi-monthly meeting on Saturday, Aug. 18,
which was well attended, and a lively interest
manifested on all the various topics of interest
which animate and inspire the Latter-day
Saints. The President, Mrs. M. I. Horne, be-
ing absent on a visit through Bear Lake Coun-
[---] Counselor B. W. Smith presided over the
meeting. The minutes of the Senior and Ju-
nior Associations were read, and speeches were
made on the principles of the Gospel, the home
industries, the gleaning and saving of grain,
by Counselors B. W. Smith and E. Howard,
and by Mrs. W. East and several other ladies
in the congregation. Elder Zebedee Coltrin,
Jun., who had recently returned from a mission
to the States, gave a sketch of his travels and
labors, and alluded to the ridicule and ill-treat-
ment to which the elders were subjected when
out proclaiming the truths of heaven.
