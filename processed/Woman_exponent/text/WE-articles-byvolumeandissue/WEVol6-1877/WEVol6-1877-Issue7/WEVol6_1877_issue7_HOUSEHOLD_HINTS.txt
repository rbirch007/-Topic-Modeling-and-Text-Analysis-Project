HOUSEHOLD HINTS.

	Dried corn and especially sweet corn is an ex-
cellent vegetable for winter when there is not
the variety of other seasons.  In many places
corn does not ripen on account of the early frosts
and if there are ears sufficiently ripe to cook, it
would be good economy to preserve them for the
winter. The process is simple and requires no
particular skill only a little care.  Remove the
husks and put the corn in boiling water and
scald it just enough to start the milk, then take
it out, and cut it from the cob, and spread it on
sheets to dry.  For small quantities it may be
dried upon plates.  Some persons prefer cooking
it thoroughly.  We know a lady who dries large
quantities for sale, and in this way some of our
sisters who live in country places might get pocket
money besides making a delicious dish for home
use.  This year there is an unusual scarcity of all
kinds of fruits, and dried corn will no doubt be in
good demand.  The lady referred to also informs
us that she saves all the water in which she boils
the corn, and with a little yeast, and a few hops
she makes a very good article of home-brewed
beer, which answers for the men when working
in the harvest field, and is much better for them
than a stronger drink.
