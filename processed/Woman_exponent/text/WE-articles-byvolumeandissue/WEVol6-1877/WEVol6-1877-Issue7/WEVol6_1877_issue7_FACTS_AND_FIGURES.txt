	SMOKY STATISTICS.—The Boston Post
says that 8,000,000 cigars are smoked in
this country every day. When we consid-
er that there are over 8,000,000 voters in 
this country, and there are probably 15,000,
000, males from 16 to 90 years of age, we
are inclined to think that the estimate is
not too large. For every man who does not
somke at all we may estimate one who
smokes more than one cigar a day. At ten
cents each, these 8,000,000 cigars would 
cost $800,000 a day. Throwing out Sundays
and holidays (though a cigar is occasionally
smoked on Sunday evenings), the total cost
for cigars yearly would be $240,000,000—
enough to pay the national debt in nine
years.
