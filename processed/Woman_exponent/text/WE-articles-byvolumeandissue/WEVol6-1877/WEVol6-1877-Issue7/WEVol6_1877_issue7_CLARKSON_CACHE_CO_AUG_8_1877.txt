  CLARKSON, CACHE Co, Aug. 8, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
 The people here are enjoying the bless,
ings of health and general prosperity, for
which we feel thankful to God the Giver of
all good gifts. We have a new meeting
house, 56 feet by 134, 16 feet in the height
of ceiling; it is finished sufficiently so we
can meet in it. Our Bishop, John Jardine,
and the brethren have been diligent in their
efforts to build it, and the sisters of the Re-
lief Society have done a noble part towards
it, selling their butter and eggs to pay the
cash part of the expenses. We have a good
Sunday School, the children are now prac-
tising for the Jubilee, which is to be held
in Logan on the 25th of August. The sis-
ters of the Relief Society meet twice a
month to hold their meetings; they speak
words of comfort and encouragement to
each other, and try to accomplish all the
good possible, and take care of the poor and
sick, and help in every good work. We
number about thirty in our society at
present.
 Praying the Lord to bless each and every
one of us, that we may gain an exaltation
in the Celestial kingdom, we remain
	Yours in the Gospel,
	   MARY GRIFFIN, President,
		JANE GODFREY, Secretary.
