CLIFTON; ONEIDA Co. Aug. 4th, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
Our Society is in  flourishing condition.
The grain question was again brought be-
fore the sisters in our last meeting and
there was a unanimous vote carried, that
we exert ourselves to the utmost ability in
obtaining grain to lay up. The hoppers
have damaged the crops very badly but we
intend to work with the hope in view that
God will help those who help themselves.
 Our President has a fine son born on the
22nd of July. Mother and son are doing
well. Household duties occupy my time
pretty much but when tired and perplexed,
I find it a great comfort to spend a few mo-
ments in reading the EXPONENT. The ex-
cellent advice and hints to mothers should
not escape the notice of any mother in Zion,
for who is there among us that does not
need to learn much in relation to the rear-
ing of a family? 
 With best wishes I remain a sister.
