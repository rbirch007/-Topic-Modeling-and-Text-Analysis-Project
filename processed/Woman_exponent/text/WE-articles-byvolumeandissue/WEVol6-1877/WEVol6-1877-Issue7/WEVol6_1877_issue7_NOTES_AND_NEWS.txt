NOTES AND NEWS.
A NEW discovery has been made of two
heavenly bodies thought to be satellites of
the planet Mars.  The discovery was made
on Thursday Aug. 23, at 11, p. m. by the
aid of the great telescope, by Prof. Hall of
the Washington Government Observatory.
Commodore Rogers has notified the astro-
nomical world of this new achievement of 
science.
 IT IS said that Russia has succeeded in
inducing Servia and Greece to simultane-
ously declare war against Turkey, also that
the Russians are concentrating on the river
Lomer, and that an attack on Kayrad is
thought to be imminent.  Mehomet Ali is
fortifying the place and has 40,000 men to
defend it.  Dispatches of Aug. 27, say that
from the manner in which the Russians
are pushing forward reinforcements to
Schippa Pass they must be confident of be-
ing able to resist any advance from the riy-
er Lomer.
 ON SATURDAY morning Aug. 25, about
3. 30, during a terrific rain and wind
storm the Union Pacific bridge at Omaha,
Neb. was so seriously damaged that it is
not possible to repair it in less than two or
three months, it is supposed to have been a
cyclone which struck it at the Eastern end
and about 500 feet of it was precipitated
into the river.  The piers are left standing
uninjured.  The loss is estimated at $350,
000.  Trains will probably have to transfer
passengers and freight by way of Platts-
mouth, Nebraska, and Missouri Valley.  It
has been proposed to put in a Howe Truss
over the break in the bridge.  If this can
be done successfully trians will be running
over the bridge inside of three months.
The debris is being rapidly cleared away.
