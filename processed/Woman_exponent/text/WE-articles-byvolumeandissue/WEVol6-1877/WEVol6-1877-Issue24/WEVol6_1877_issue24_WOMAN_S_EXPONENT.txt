WOMAN'S EXPONENT.


THE TEMPERANCE Reform and The Great
Reformers," is the title of a work on the sub-
ject of Temperance by Rev. W. H. Daniels M.
A., with an introduction by Rev. Theo. L.
Cuyler, D. D. The book is divided into nine
sections and gives an outline of all the great
temperance movements in America, with
sketches of the different societies organized to 
assist in the work and pictures of celebrated
workers, men and women. It is printed upon
fine tinted paper in clear readable type and
contains 600 pages. The book is sold by sub-
scrip[-]io[-] only; price $2,00 Mrs. Hewlings,
(widow of Rev. G. R. Hewlings lately deceas-
ed,) is agent in this Territory, and is now can-
vassing through this city. We recommend
her to the courtesy of our sisters, and wish her
success in her noble efforts for good. Her no-
ble heart is in the cause of Temperance and
Reform. She has lectured on that and other
subjects in some of the Eastern and Western
States, and has been an efficient practical
worker in the cause. Mrs. Hewlings has visit-
ed Inebriate Homes, Asylums and Prisons in
the interest of the Temperance Cause. This
lady is about to visit the southern settlements
of this Territory and intends canvassing in the
villages and towns upon the way. God speed
the cause of Temperance in this and other
lands and, and bless those who devote their
energies to so laudable a work.
