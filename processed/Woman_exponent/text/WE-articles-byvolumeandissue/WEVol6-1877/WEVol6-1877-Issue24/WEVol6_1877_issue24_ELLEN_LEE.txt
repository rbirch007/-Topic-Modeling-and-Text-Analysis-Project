	ROCKPORT, May 4th, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Our little Society was organized Septem-
ber 20, 1873 with twenty members, by Bish-

[Column 3]

ops' Atwood and Harder from Kamas, with
the the following sisters as officers:
	Ann Bryant, President,
	Elizabeth Vernon,		Counselors,
	Alice M. Malin,
	Sarah Staker, Secretary
	Charlotte Marchent, Treasurer.
	There has been donated to our Society
since that time..............................$	77 80
	Disbursed to the poor...........	23 80
	Salt Lake Temple................	23 80
	Indian Clothing..................	  9 70
	Wheat for storing..............	  3 00
	Centennial Flag...................	 13 00
	Silk Worms..........................	  3 00
	Lumber for wheat bin...........	  2 00
		Total				77 80
	Have now in Treasury in quilts
		and sundries...................	10 50
	Cash....................................	  7 25
		Total				17 75
	This last sum was obtained by making
articles and selling them.
	The Society raised twenty-two bushels of
wheat on a piece of land belonging to Bish-
op Edwin Bryant. After furnishing seed
last year we had on hand bought and donat-
ed, eight bushels, making on hand in bin,
thirty bushels.
	We held our yearly meeting April 1st,
1878. Teachers reported our members feel-
ing well. After the usual exercises the
sisters spoke to one another, exhorting to
good words and works. We are trying to
do the best we can and wish to be one with
other branches of the Relief Society in
Zion.
	Ever praying for the welfare of the
Daughters of Zion, we remain your sisters
in the Gospel,
	ANN BRYANT, President.
	SARAH STAKER, Secretary.
