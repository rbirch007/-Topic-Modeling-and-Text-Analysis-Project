	FAIRFIELD, UTAH CO., April 16, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT.
	We the Young Ladies' M. I. A., have
long wished to have a few lines in your lit-
tle paper to show our sisters and friends
that we have a welfare in Zion. Having
been reorganized we will let you know
what we are doing. Our society is very
small, our former president and a number
other sisters having moved away, leaves us
but few in number, although we feel deter-
mined to be united and do all in our power
to forward the great and mighty work in
these Latter-days. We have begun storing
grain and feel it a duty that all the Saints
should take interest in. We have had a
great deal of the opposite spirit to contend
with, although by adhering to good coun-
sel, also having faith and unity, we are
gaining ground, and feel greatly encourag-
ed, and know that there is encouragement
for all who seek faithfully for the paths that
our Savior trod. We meet every Tuesday,
and hold a conjoint meeting once a month.
Our prayers are fervent for all who seek af-
ter righteousness. We take the EXPO-
NENT which is a welcome friend, and wish
it success.
	Your sisters in the Gospel,
		Zilpha R. Young, President,
		Marintha Carson,
		Sophy Beesinger,	Counselors
		Sophy Beesinger, Secretary,
		Caroline Carson, Treasurer.
