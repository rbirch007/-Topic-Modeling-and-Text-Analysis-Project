	PLEASANT GROVE, Apr. 24th, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Not having been represented through the
columns of your much esteemed paper, for
some time, we herewith send a report of 
our progress. At our last monthly meeting
we were favored with a visit from a num-
ber of sisters from American Fork, who
spoke interestingly on experience gained
in this church, and bore faithful testimony
of the divinity of the Latter-day work; the
Spirit of God was present with us, and we
were mutually benefitted.
	Our Annual Report ending Jan. 6th, 1878,
reprensented the Society in a prosperous

[Column 2]

state financially. We have stock in Co-op
Store and Provo Factory, three city lots well-
fenced, where we have planted mulberry
and fruit trees, all these with property in
treasury amounting to $ 1,075. 10.
	The aged and infirm are cared for and
supplied with necessaries. We have not
been idle in the good work of storing grain;
we own a bin in the United Order Granary,
a substantial rock building just completed,
capable of storing one thousand bushels of
grain, having one hundred and fifty bush-
els on hand at present.
	We, as a society are striving to increase
our efforts for good and wish ever to be
found valiant in the cause of Zion.
	P. E. Brown, Sec'y.
	F. E. Stuart, Ass't.
