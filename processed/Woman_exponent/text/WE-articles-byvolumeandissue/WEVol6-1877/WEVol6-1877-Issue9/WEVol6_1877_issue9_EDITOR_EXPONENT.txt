EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We here send a short account of our An-
nual Meeting held June 7th. Our accounts
being read showed that we had disbursed
to the poor $6. 23; to the building of a
meeting-house, $15. 00; and to the Logan
factory $25. 00, leaving us an amount on
hand of $12. 00. Our Bishop Orson Smith
and his Counselors H. C. Jackson and H.
A. Shaw gave much good instruction con-
cerning our duties as members and teachers
of the Relief Society, and as mothers in
Israel, and encouraged the sisters in their
endeavors to do all the good possible for
them to do. It is our ambition to be wor-
thy of being numbered with our sister so-

[Column 3]
cieties in the great work of improvement
and restoration of all things.
	Knowing the encouragement your paper
gives the women of Utah, and the good it
does, we pray for its extended circulation
and influence, and remain your sisters in
the Gospel,
		L. B. REMINGTON, President,
		SINAH BISHOP, Secretary.
