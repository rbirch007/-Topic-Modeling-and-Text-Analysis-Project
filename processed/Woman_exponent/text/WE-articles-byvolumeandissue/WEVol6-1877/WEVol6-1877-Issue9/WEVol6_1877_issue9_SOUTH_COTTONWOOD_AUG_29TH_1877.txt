	SOUTH COTTONWOOD Aug. 29th, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Our president and all interested in our
Relief Society are feeling well and alive to
their duties. We are obeying the counsels
of President Young as near as possible.
We have nearly two hundred bushels of
wheat on hand, and expect to have much
more; we hope to get the brethren to assist
us in building a bin. We have always been
willing to respond to the calls of those
placed over us. The Society has donated
liberally to the St. George Temple and to
the Temple in S. L. City, as well as assist-
ing the brethren in their missions, besides
attending to the wants of the poor and
needy in the ward. There seems to be a
spirit of unity among the sisters of this
place, and a desire tn be pushing ahead.
The Counselors, Mrs. Lucy Bullock, and
Mrs. Mary Rawlens with a large number of
the teachers of the society gave our Presi-
dent Mrs. Janette Young, a very pleasant
surprise last Saturday, it being her fifty-
seventh birthday. The sisters united to-
gether and gave her a nice present. They
took with them quantities of good things
for picnic both for dinner and supper.
Bishop Rawlens joined the company at sup-
per time. We found our President in very
poor health, but she felt considerably bet-
ter when we arived. All present seemed
to enjoy themselves very much, and a good
spirit prevailed in our midst.
		Yours in the Gospel
			F. E. TURNER, Ass't.
							Sec'y.
