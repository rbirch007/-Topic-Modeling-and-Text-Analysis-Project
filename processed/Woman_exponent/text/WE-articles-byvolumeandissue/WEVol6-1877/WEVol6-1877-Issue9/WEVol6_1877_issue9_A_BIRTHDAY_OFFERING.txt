A BIRTHDAY OFFERING.

Through the forest of thought I've been roaming
	Piercing deep through its inmost recess,
If perchance I might cull from her treasures.
	A bright garland—the fairest and best,
Unfaltering I've pressed through the thicket,
	Never once heeding brier or thorn,
That stood by as a vanguard or picket,
	Thus to turn my poor efforts to scorn.

I have peered into dark cell and grotto,
	Traversed yawning ravines and deep glade,
I have crossed over wild roaring torrent,
	Wandered far in the thick jungle's shade.
And I paused near the brink of a river
	Flowing onward through time's golden sands,
Murmuring of the mystic forever,
	That awaiteth the pure of all lands.

I have mused over moss-covered emblems;
	As they whispered of glory long past,
Of their broad spreading branches and tendrils
	[---] had withered and chilled in his grasp
I gathered a pearl from the [---]
	That now cover a stately old elm,
And I've borne it 'mid trials and crosses,
	As a charm for thy bosom dear Em.

It will tell thee youth's morning is fleeting,
	Like the dew of the flower its day,
To seek ever true knowledge and wisdom
	That will live when all else pass away.
A bright diamond of truth I have woven.
	In a wreath of fair laurel and bay,
Let it grace thy pure brow lovely maiden,
	To the end of life's devious way.

Like the rainbow its light shall illumine,
	Thy pathway through this vale of tears,
Guide thee safe to that brighter dominion
	Where they never more count up the years.
I have come with my offering dear Emma,
	O do not now reject it I pray!
Keep it sacred and wear it forever;
	In memoriam of thy birthday.

S. L. City, Sept. 10, 1877.
