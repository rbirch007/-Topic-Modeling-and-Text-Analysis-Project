			R. S. REPORTS.

	On the evening of September 4th, 1877,
the Retrenchment Association of the Twen-
tieth Ward celebrated its second anniver-
sary, by holding a meeting to which all the
ward was invited. A number of the pa-
rents of the young ladies availed themselves
of the opportunity, and were present. The
exercises of the young ladies, after prayer,
singing, and reading of minutes and yearly
report, consisted of a select reading, "The
Righteous never Forsaken," by Miss Mary
A. Sansom; an original song suitable for
the occasion, composed by Mrs. Lula Greene
Richards, was very sweetly sung by Misses
Alice and Lizzie Sansom; an essay on
"Friendship" by Miss Mary Culmer; a reci-
tation, "Beautiful Snow," finely rendered
by Miss Thora Tweede; and an essay on
"Retrenchment" by Miss Hulda E. Wilcox.
Our Bishop, Brother John Sharp, was not
present on account of other business, but
we were favored with the presence of one
of his counselors, Brother W. [-]. Dunbar;
also Brother C. W. Stayner and Sisters E.
R. Snow, Z. D. Young, B. W. Smith and
M. R. Thompson were present as visitors,
and all made cheering, instructive and
highly appropriate remarks. The meeting
was a gratifying one in all respects, and
much credit was offered both to officers and

[Column 2]
members of the Association by the breth-
ren and sisters who spoke.
	MRS. SARAH HOWARD, President,
