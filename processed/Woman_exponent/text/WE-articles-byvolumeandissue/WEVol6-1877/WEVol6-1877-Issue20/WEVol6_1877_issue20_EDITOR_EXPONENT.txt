EDITOR EXPONENT:

	Samaria is situated on the east side of
the Malad Valley, and it is a lovely thriv-
ing settlement, and is increasing yearly in
numbers and prosperity. On Nov. 1st, we
were favored with a visit from Mrs. Zina
D. Young and E. Davis of Salt Lake City,
who spoke words of comfort and gave us
good instruction in the various missions
that woman is expected to perform in this
kingdom. May God give us wisdom and
strength to carry them out, and may these
noble sisters live long to speak words of
comfort and encouragement to the sisters
of Zion. They organized a Young Ladies'
Mutual Improvement Association which is
proving a great benefit to the young girls
here. The meetings are well attended and
a good spirit prevails. We have a Relief
Society in this place who are trying to
carry out the counsel of our late President
Brigham Young in storing grain and other
duties required of us.
	With prayers for the success of the EX-
PONENT and all who assist to carry on wo-
man's work, I remain your sister in the
Gospel.					M. T. H.


In the "Congressional Record" of March
