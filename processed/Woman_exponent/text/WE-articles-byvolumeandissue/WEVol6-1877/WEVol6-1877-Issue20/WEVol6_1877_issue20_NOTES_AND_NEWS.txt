NOTES AND NEWS.

	HON. GEORGE B. Loring spoke at the
Middlesex Club dinner, recently.

	THAT EXCELLENT paper the "Jewish
Messenger," lately celebrated its twenty-
first birthday.

	IN THE Congressional record of Feb. 12,
1878, mention is made of the suffrage peti-
tions from this Territory. They were pre-
sented by Mr. Dorsey and Mr. Hoar in the
Senate and referred to the Committee on
privileges and elections.

	THE GREAT powers of Europe have de-
cided npon a peace-congress to convene at
Vienna, Austria, on the 28th inst., to decide
upon the policy to be pursued in future
emergencies, and methods of reconciliation.
Dispatches say Germany will claim the
presidency by proscriptive right whether
Bismarck attends or not.

	THE "Woman's Suffrage Journal" is an
English monthly edited by Lydia E. Beck-
er. It contains full information of the pro-
gress of the movement for removing the
Electoral Disabilities of Women in England.
It also records and discusses other questions
affecting the welfare of women—such as
Education, Employment, industrial or pro-
fessional, and Legislation affecting their
property and personal rights. Price for one
copy monthly (post free for one year), two
shillings. Communications for the editor,
and orders for the "Journal," to be ad-
dressed 28 Jackson's Row, Albert Square,
Manchester, or Messrs. Trubner & Co., Pa-
ternoster Row, London, England.
