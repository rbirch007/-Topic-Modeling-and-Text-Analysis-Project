Snow, Mrs.
M. Isabella Horne and Mrs. Zina D. Young
have been making a tour through the north-
ern settlements in Davis, Cache and Box El-
der Counties, in the interest of Relief Socie-
ties and Y. L. M. I. A., laboring to awaken a re-
newed interest in all the departments of wo-
man's work which pertains to the building up
of Zion.  These sisters with many others are
earnestly seeking to promote home industries
among the people, to give employment to the
poor and make the saints more self-sustaining.
One of the principle objects now in view is to
establish sericulture and make it a successful
enterprise; and in order to do so it requires unit-
ed efforts. Almost every Relief Society in
Utah can, if they make an exertion, lend a 
helping hand to the general interest of this
special industry.  It is expected now that there
will soon be suitable machinery for manufac-
turing, and meantime the mulberry trees must
be growing, so the silk-worms can have food,
and a large quantity of cocoons will be wanted
to supply the silk reelers, and the sisters who
understand reeling should have employment,
and stimulate others to learn  It is a trade
easily learned, and will be a remunerative one,
for future benefit, as it is now a well-known fact
that the climate of Utah is adapted to silk cul-
tivation, and the enterprise is in the hands of
those who will not let anything daunt them
from pushing the work forward.  Yet of course
it will depend upon the people, whether much
or little shall be accomplished, for if there are
not many mulberry trees, there will not be
many cocoons, and consequently there will not
be material for the loom.  The Relief Society
and Y. L. M. I. A. Presidents should not forget
this important mission given them at the Con-
ference in the St. George Temple less than a 
year ago, by one, now no more in our midst to
counsel and instruct.  This labor is not the
sole work of a few individuals, but the people,
and the result will be such as is ever the re-
ward of diligence and industry in a commui-
ty— wealth and prosperity.
 Pamphlets, giving definite details of the 
work of sericulture in all its stages, are for sale
at the Commission Store for Home Manufac-
tures, in the Old Constitutiou buildings, at ten
cents each.  The sisters should bear in mind
that this store, established through their united
energies, by counsel of President Young, still
needs their patronage.  It is increasing in va-
riety of goods, and the utmost pains should be
taken to make it the most flourishing institu
tion in Salt Lake City, if this people ever ex-
pect to become independent of Babylon.
