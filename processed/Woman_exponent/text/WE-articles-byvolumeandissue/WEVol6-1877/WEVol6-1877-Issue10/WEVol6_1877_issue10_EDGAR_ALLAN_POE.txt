EDGAR ALLAN POE.

	The following poem, attributed to Edgar
Allan Poe, and first published by the Koko-
mo (Ind.) "Dispatch", was discovered by
the editor of that journal on the fly-leaf of
an old book. Attracted by the initials "E.
A. P.," at the bottom of the page, he in-
quired the author's name, when the owner
of the book, who seemed through lack of
education unable to appreciate the treasure,
informed him that his grandparents, who
gave him the book, kept a country hotel in
a small village called Chesterfield, near
Richmond, Va. One night a young man
rapped at the door, and asked if he could
stay there until morning. He was shown
a room, and that was the last they saw of
him. When they went to call him to
breakfast next morning, they found he had
gone away and left the book, on the fly-leaf
of which were written the lines we give
below, "verbatim." The poem is written
in Roman characters, without erasure or an
interlineated word, and is almost as legible
