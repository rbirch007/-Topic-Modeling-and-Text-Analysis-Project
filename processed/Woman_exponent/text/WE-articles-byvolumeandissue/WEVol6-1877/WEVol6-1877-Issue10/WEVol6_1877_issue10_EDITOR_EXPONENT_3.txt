EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I thought I would say a few words through
your valuable paper, praying the Lord to guide
my thoughts with His holy spirit, that I may
say something that may be of interest to your
readers, especially to the young ladies of the
Improvement Associations. I attended one of
their meetings last Monday in the 11th Ward.
I was pleased to see the little girls rise from
their seats and read their selections, and speak.
I thought how lovely and beautiful they looked;
and I feel sure that God and the angels were
pleased with them. I pray the Lord to bless
them with wisdom and knowledge that they 
may learn and understand the things pertain-
ing to life and salvation, and as they grow in
years they may grow in grace and favor with
the Lord; for it is the youth that the greater
portion of this Latter-day work will rest upon.
I pray that they may go on from one degree of
true learning to another, until glory will
crown their labors. When I was a little girl
I was very fond of the little hymn on page 36
in our hymn book.

		Be it my only wisdon here.
		To serve the Lord with f[---]al fear,
			With love and gratitade;
		Superior sense may I display
		By shunning every evil way
			And walking in the good.
									J. BROWN.
20th Ward, S. L. City.


NEVER affect to be witty, or jest so as to
wound the feelings of another.

	SPEAK with calmness and deliberation on
all occasions especially of circumstances
which tend to irritate.
