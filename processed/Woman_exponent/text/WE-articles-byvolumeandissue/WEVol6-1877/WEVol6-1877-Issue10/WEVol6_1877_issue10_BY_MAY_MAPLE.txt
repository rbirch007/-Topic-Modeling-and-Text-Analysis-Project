BY MAY MAPLE.

No star in yonder sky that shines,
 Can light like woman's eye impart;
The earth holds not in all its mines,
 The gem so rich as woman's heart.

Her voice is like rich music sweet,
Poured out from e'en a harp alone;
Like that, when storms most loudly beat,
It yields a clearer, richer tone.

And woman's loves a holy light,
 That brighter, brighter beams for aye,
Years cannot dim its radiance bright,
 Nor ever falsehood quench its ray.

But like the star of Bethlehem
 Of old, to Israel's shepherds giv'n,
It marshals with its steady name,
 The erring soul of man to heav'n.
