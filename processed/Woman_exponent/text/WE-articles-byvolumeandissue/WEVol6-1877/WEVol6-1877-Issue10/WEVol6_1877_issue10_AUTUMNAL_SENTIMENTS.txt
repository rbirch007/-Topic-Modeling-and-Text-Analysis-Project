		AUTUMNAL SENTIMENTS.

		"Now garnering gray October's sober gains,
		Now Christmas hollies pile our loaded wains."

	All poets, or poetical prose (not prosy,) writ-
ers who are of the descriptive class, who delight
to dwell upon scenery, seasons, birds, flowers,
waterfalls, rivulets and sunshine, who assim-
ilate their sentiments and subjects to nature's
varied and changeful moods, revel in the po-
etical inspiration of falling leaves, faded flow-
ers, gorgeous colored forests all aflame with
beauty, brown hills and royal-purple sunsets.

	"But see the fading many-colored woods,
	Shade deep'ning over shade, the country round
	Embrown."
