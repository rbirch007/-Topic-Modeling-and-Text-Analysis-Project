EXTRACTS FROM A PRIVATE LETTER.

	SUNSET, ARIZONA Sept. 13th, 1877.
	I received your very kind letter, and
thank you for the good sentiments expressed
therein.  It does me good to known that
you feel interested in our mission; we some-
times are a little lonesome, there being so
few of us, but the brethren and sisters of
our camp are united, and when we consider
that we are here to try to do the will of
God and carry out the counsel of President
Young (in our weakness), it inspires us
with hope that our Heavenly Father will
give us strength to overcome out imperfec-
tions and learn to discharge our duties
faithfully in the United Order.
	I am happy to hear you are engaged in
the Ladies' Store; I think it will encourage
others to try to follow your example, and 
heartily wish you success in the enterprise.
I am aware it must be quite a tax upon
your physical strength.
	I have been teaching school a short time
for the same kind of remuneration.
	The labors of the brethren here have
been much blessed this season; they have
raised some good wheat- enough for bread
and seed; the corn and sugar cane look
splendid.  We have also some good vege-
tables of almost all kinds, which we can
appreciate after the failure of last year.
We have one corral for our cows, one field,
one stock-yard, and on treasury.
	We are so far from Utah that we get but
little news.
	May God bless, comfort and strengthen
you in all your labors, is the prayer of
	Your sister in the Gospel of peace,
							A. W.
