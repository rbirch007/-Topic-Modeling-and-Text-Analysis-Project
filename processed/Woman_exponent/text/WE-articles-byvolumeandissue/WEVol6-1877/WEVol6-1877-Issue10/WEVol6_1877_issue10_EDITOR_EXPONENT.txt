EDITOR EXPONENT:

	Believing your readers,have an interest
in the Belief Society of Peoa, Summit Co.,
and having recently returned from that lo-
cality, I submit the following:
	The brethren have enclosed about three
hundred acres, which they planted in
White Touse wheat, that looked beauti-
ful when matured, standing from four to
five feet high; of this the Belief Society
has 9ix acres. The brethren donated the
raising and harvesting of this wheat to
the Society. While, the brethren were at
work the Society furnished supplies for
subsistence, and all had an enjoyable time.
The general health of the people is good.
Your paper is a welcome visitor. Wishing
it perfect success.
		I remain yours;
					M. A. Rockwood.
	S. L. City Sep. 25, 1877.

		ST. CHARLES Sept. 10th 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT.
	On the 29th Aug. we received a visit
from our much beloved sisters Mary I.
Horne and Phebe Woodruff, from whom
we received much valuable instruction
which if carried out in our lives will be of
great benefit to us. On the following
morning we received the sad news of our
beloved President's death which cast a
gloom over the entire settlement, yet we
realize that our loss is his gain, and feel to
say "Thy will be done Father and not ours."
Help us Father to so live that we will be
worthy of his society in the world to come
	With best wishes for yourself and your
valuable paper, I remain, 
	Your sister in the Gospel,
