	INSCRIBED TO MRS. E. B WELLS.

A river winding on and on,
	Ne'er still nor resting by the way,
By verdant hills 'neath forest shades, 
	Through silent night or burning day.
Anon in valleys gently flows, 
	Sings there in lazy undertone; 
Its current slow, no ripple's crest
	Is foaming in the pathway's lone.

Defined its course by foliage
	More dense or sparse as rich the land; 
Or like unto a co'ling thing, 
	It shimmers o'er the golden sand.
Through mountain pass it rushes wild, 
	Then thund'ring dashes down below;
O, awe inspiring its descent
	Amid a phosphorescent glow!

A little brook or murm'ring stream,
	Would babble on unto the end;
But crystal springs send rivulets,
	Mingling they in volume blend.
No life can be lived in a day,
	No time for love or tenderness;
No flowers growing by the way,
	To thrill beneath the wind's caress.

But like the harp so famed of old,
	A thousand strings of melody--
That quiv'ring 'neath a gentle touch
	Are tratic--fierce struck ruthlessly,
To peace are stilled its throbbings wild,
	Its chords vibrating tune regain;
Divinely sweet the songs it sings--
	It trembles with the loved one's name.

All devious is the way ofe life,
	And other lives are in it wound;
So beautiful of it a part,
	By ties eternal firmly bound.
All wise the destined course of life,
	To gather gems of priceless worth;
From one to multitudes unknown,
	For an inheritance of earth.
