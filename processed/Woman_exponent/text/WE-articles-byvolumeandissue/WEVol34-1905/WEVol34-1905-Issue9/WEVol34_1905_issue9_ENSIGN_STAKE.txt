		ENSIGN STAKE.
		Mothers’ Work.
			Lecture 1.
	(a) The ideal mother.
	(b) The over-indulgent mother; the
self-indulgent mother.
	(c) What about the father?
	(d) What is most desirable in a
mother?
	(e) Are our girls being prepared for
ideal motherhood?

			Lecture 2.
	The Ideal Mother (continued.)

[Column 2]
	(a) How much may Church or society
expect of mothers of young children?
	(b) Co-operative motherhood. Let
mothers occasionally take care of children
of their friends; giving opportunity for
meeting going, special work, sewing,
etc.
	(c) Each mother responsible for con-
duct of all children in her home.

			Lecture 3.
	(a) Plan carefully each day’s and
week’s work.
	(b) Stated times for sleeping and eat-
ing.
	(c) People should not be needlessly
disturbed during sleep.

			Lecture 4.
		Economy in the Home.
	(a) Economy of time.
	(b) Use of baskets and trays in carrying
things from basements, store-rooms and
tables.
	(c) Economy in buying.
“He who buys what he does not want,
Will sometimes want what he cannot
	buy.”
	(d) Advantages and disadvantages of
wholesale buying.
	(c) As much judgement required in
spending as in earning money.

			Lecture 5.
	Economy in the Home (continued.)
	(a) The sinfulness of waste.
“Wilful waste makes woeful want.”
	(b) The example of the Savior in the
Feast of the Loaves and Fishes.
	(c) Small savings in the fuel, lights,
food, clothing, etc., aggregate much.

			Lecture 6.
		Neatness in the Home.
	(a) Personal appearance.
	(b) Taste in dress. Do not wear visit-
ing or street dresses for house work. Per-
cales or wash goods are suitable for home
work.
	(c) The advantages of aprons and work
sleeves.
	(d) Table manners.
	(e) Selfishness and unselfishness at
table.
	(f) Handle no food but your own.

		Program Committee.
			Lecture 1.
		Children God’s Best Heritage.
	(a) Illustrate from the lives of Sarah,
Rebecca and Rachel.
	(b) Their great desire for children.
	(c) Posterity—Abraham’s reward for
his faithfulness.
	(d) Marriage should be between people
of the same faith.
	(e) Give Bible references. Isaac and
Jacob.

			Lecture 2.
			Moses.
	(a) His birth. Pharaoh’s cruelty in
destroying Israelitish children.

[Column 3]
	(b) God’s preservation of Moses through
the agency of his mother, sister and
Pharaoh’s daughter.
	(c) Barbarous destruction of children at
the birth of Christ and of Moses.
	(d) Compare with prenatal destruction
of children at present time.

			Lecture 3.
		Moses (continued.)
	(a) Egyptian civilization and the splen-
did education of Moses.
	(b) His discipline in the wilderness as
a preparation for his future work.
	(c) His call to be leader of Israel.
	(d) The laws of today—the outgrowth
of the laws of Moses.

			Lecture 4.
		Moses (continued.)
	(a) The Ten Commandments.
	(b) Treat each one separately, making
application to our daily life.

			Lecture 5.
		Moses (continued)
Sanitation, Hygiene and Foods of Mosaic
			Laws.
	(a) Read Leviticus chap. XI.
	(b) Talk on Hygiene, selection of prop-
er food and good cooking.
	(c) Read Leviticus, Chap. XIV, and
note the care taken to prevent the spread
of leprosy.
	(d) Talk on sanitation and the preven-
tion of contagious diseases.

			Lecture 6.
		Moses (continued.)
	(a) Read the song of Moses Ex. Chap.
XV.
	(b) Read Ex. Chap. XVI, the Lord
provides quails and manna for the chil-
dren of Israel.
	(c) Compare with the miraculous pres-
ervation of the Saints on their journey
across the plains and in the early days of
Utah.
	(d) Incidents of the quails, gulls and
crickets.
	(e) Relate other incidents of a miracu-
lous nature.
