HONOR TO A VETERAN WOMAN!
	The Relief Society sisters of the Sandy
ward gathered at the home of Sister Har-
riet Wilson, in honor of her 92nd birth-
day, July 9.
	The heart of the aged veteran was
made to rejoice with the honors that
were bestowed upon her by those good
friends and neighbors; her long life has
been spent in usefulness and in activity;
she still possessse every faculty of mind
and body, having retained her excellent
eyesight so long, never wearing specta-


cles, can see to thread her needles, and
read fine print.
	Speeches were made and hymns were
sung, and there was a bounteous supply
of the good things of the earth to satisfy
all who were present and make the oc-
casion memorable. The guest of honor
pronounced the blessing upon the food,
her words were filled with inspiration. A
short sketch of her life work and her de-
votion to her religion, mentioning some
of the sacrifices she has made for the
Gospel, was given by her daughter; this
closed the happy meeting of a time long
to be remembered, the guests vieing with
each other in wishing the honored lady
joy and peace as long as she remains in
the flesh; these blessings only come
from our heavenly Father through our
obedience to His commandments. H. W.
	The EXPONENT extends loving congrat-
ulations to this dear lady whose life has
been lengthened out and whose days are
full of peace and gladness.
