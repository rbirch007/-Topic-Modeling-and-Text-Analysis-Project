ENSIGN STAKE.
The Relief Society conference of En-
sign stake convened on Friday, June 30,
1905, the first annual conference and
was held in the 12th ward chapel with a
large crowd of workers in attendance.
	Members of the stake presidency also
of the General Relief Society Board were
present.
	The musical numbers were very fine.
Instrumental selections by Sisters Kate
Romney Stewart and Lizzie Thomas
were much appreciated, as were the vocal
solos of Margaret C. S, Hull. Elma Young
and Olea shipp. 
Sister Augusta W. Grant gave an excel-
lent talk on Japan, after which Sister E.
B. Wells spoke of the magnitude of the
Relief Society work, including nurses
work, etc. Counselor Annie T. Hyde
talked about the Relief Society bureau of
employment for the protection of young 
girls coming from the country to work.
	Remarks were made also by Dr. M. C.
Roberts, Hon. W. W. Riter, President
Richard W. Young, Counselor Joseph S.
Wells, and many others. The speakers
eulogized Relief Society work and the
value of organized effort for charity.
	A substantial luncheon was served to
the congregation, between sessions by the
stake officers.
