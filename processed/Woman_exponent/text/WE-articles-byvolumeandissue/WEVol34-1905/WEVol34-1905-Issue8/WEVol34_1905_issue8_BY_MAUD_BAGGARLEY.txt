	BY MAUD BAGGARLEY.

“Mine eyes are weary” the hermit sighed,
“From gazing on mountains; and desert wide.”
“My only solace is yon towering pine,
Whose life is as useless and lonely as mine.”

“Yet—from storms it shelters the birds of the 
	air,
And hath stood for ages a sentinel there;
A guide to those on the desert astray,
A shield to all from the sun’s fierce ray.”

“Father, the pine hath taught to me—
All things are useful created by Thee.
I shall not repine when Thou dost give
The sorrows that teach me how to live.”
