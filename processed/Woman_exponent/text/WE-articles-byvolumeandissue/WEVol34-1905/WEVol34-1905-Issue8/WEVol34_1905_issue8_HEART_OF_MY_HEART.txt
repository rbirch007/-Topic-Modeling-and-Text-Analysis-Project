HEART OF MY HEART.
	Ellis Meredith, a noted newspaper wo-
man and journalist, also a fine speaker,
who has traveled considerably to speak
on the suffrage question, has recently
published a book on motherhood relating
to children, in which she embodies some
original ideas of her own, the story is
exquisitely told.
	In her very first chapter she says, after
having opened with the creation, “We
prate wisely of Nature and her processes,
but after all, what do we know of the
mystery of life! We may speak glibly of
blood and brain, bone and sinew, but how
or why certain elements build tissue and
others nerves, none of us can tell. We
only know that it is as it was in the begin-
ning. She was wiser who said, ‘My soul
doth magnify the Lord.’”
	She touches deeply and beautifully up-
on the joys of maternity, the relations of
father and mother towards their offspring
before and after birth; true and false
conditions, and brings in many wise and
helpful sayings that stir the soul to
thoughts of high and lofty life-purposes,
though they are simple, homely things
that all women can understand if they
will. It is unlike any book we have ever
seen and must meet a want in the pa-
rents’ classics that no other book we have
seen ever has. We may certainly call it
rare among books. She speaks of liking
the old Greek idea that made men bow
before a woman approaching maternity.
Here is one paragraph with which I will
close this brief notice:
	“Child of my heart, lying under my
heart, with my relationship to you has
grown a new relationship to your father:
he is more than lover, husband and king;
high priest of the Most High; love has
become sacred and holy beyond words,
almost beyond thought. I have been
singing low to myself that beautiful solo
from ‘The Messiah’:
	‘He shall feed his flocks like a shepherd,
and gently, gently leadeth those that are
with young.’”
