PARTY AT PLEASANT VIEW.
	The sisters of Pleasant View, Weber
Co., met at the home of Mrs. M. A. Cra-
gun, January 18, 1906, to celebrate the
thirtieth anniversary of their organization
and surprise our president, Mary J. May-
cock, who has served in this capacity
since our organization.
	After partaking of a sumptuous repast,
we held a splendid meeting, where Bishop
Charles A. Hickenlooper spoke of the
good work the sisters had performed,
and a number of others made timely re-
marks.
	Pres. Maycock and her first counselor,
Sister Eliza P. Rhees, who has served in
this capacity the greater part of thirty
years, were each presented with a beau-
tiful vase as a token of our love and es-
teem for their long labor in this society,
	The following song, composed by Sis-
ter Eliza P. Rhees and sung at our
last annual day program, was rendered
during the meeting;
	(Tune: Home, Sweet Home.)
In the year of our history eighteen forty-two,
In the beautiful city of the Saints called Na uvoo,
There Joseph the Prophet, inspired from above,
Ordained our dear sisters their labor of love.
			CHORUS:
	Come, sisters, come;
		We’ll all be united, and ever be one.
He said, “You’re appointed and now set apart
To labor in Zion to comfort the hearts
Of the poor and the needy, the sick and depressed,
The widow and orphan in lonely distress.
			Chorus,
Look well to the orphans, and teach them the way
From  paths of true virtue never to stray;
And counsel the wayward, where’er they may be
To overcome evil and from sin to be free.
			Chorus,
By feeding the hungry and clothing the poor,
Never turning the needy away from our door,
With benevolence true and charity mild,
We’ll endeavor to bless every woman and child.
			Chorus.
			M. A. CRAGUN,
				Assistant Secretary.
