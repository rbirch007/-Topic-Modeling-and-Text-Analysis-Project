RELIEF SOCIETY REPORTS. 
RELIEF SOCIETY REORGANIZED. 
	Agreeable to the request of Joseph E.
Robinson, President of the California
Mission, and Justus Swenson, President of
the San Francisco Branch, a special meet-
ing for the reorganizatiofi of the Ladies'
Relief Society of this city was called
November 8th, 1905, Sister Mary G.
Hughes, the President, having tendered
her resignation previously and same hav-
ing been accepted.
	President Robinson presided and called
the meeting to order. He addressed the
sisters upon the subject of the Relief
Society and its importance as an aid in
the Church.
	Sister Elizabeth Rawlinson was unani-
mously chosen President of the Society,
with Sisters Anna Stormfieldt and Eliza-
beth Hooper as Counselors. Sister W.
P. Wolfinger was unanimously sustained
as Secretary.
	President Rawlinson addressed the sis-
ters asking their assistance and faith that
they might have a Relief Society in very
deed. Sisters Stormfieldt, Hooper and
Wolfinger followed.
	Testimonies were borne by many
present and a good spirit prevailed
throughout.
	W. P. Wolfinger, Secretary.
