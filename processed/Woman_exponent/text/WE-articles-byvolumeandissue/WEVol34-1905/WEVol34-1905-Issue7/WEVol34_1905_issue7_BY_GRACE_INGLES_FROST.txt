BY GRACE INGLES FROST.

Christmas bells are chiming––
	“Peace on earth good will to man,”
Glory to God in the highest,
	And unto the great “I Am,”

Great tho’ so humble His advent,
	Not a place could he find for His head,
Save a manger—the meanest of cradles, 
	Where oftimes the cattle had fed. 

Great, tho’ through life with the lowly He
		walked,
	Scorned, insulted, rejected by men, 
The least in the kingdom of heaven,
	Accounted more worthy by them.

And great, ah, so great! were His teachings.
	That down thro’ the ages they roll,
Bringing joy, peace, long suffering and meek-
		ness
	To those who His doctrine extol.

Ring on Christmas bells!
	Sound your praises?
To Christ and His Father above.
Who gave us poor erring creat[-]res,
	The heavenly message of love.
