TRULY A MOTHER IN ISRAEL.
MARY ISABELLA HORNE.
	It is always painful to record the death
of those we love, and especially those
whose lives have been waymarks for
others to follow; and whose associations
have been so closely mingled and inter-
twined with those they have left behind
them through many long years; all these
sentiments make the feeling of sadness
more profound, and it is the more diffi-
cult to write what the heart would dic-
tate.
	Of the honored veteran women of the
Church it has fallen to the lot of the
writer not only to write biographies of
them while living, but to prepare obitu-
ary notices for a very large number. We
recall a score and more of these noted
women who knew the prophet Joseph
Smith, and who were counted among
those greater women of the early days of
the Church; but only a very few remain
with us now.
