MOTHER'S WORK
		KANAB STAKE.
				I.
	(a) Self-culture for mothers. (b) What
the result will be upon the children.
				II.
	(a) Child helplessness. (b) Necessity of
care and patience.
				III.
	(a) Child confidence. (b) How it should
be rewarded.
				IV.
	(a) Child helpfulness. (b) What trait
of character may it not develop?
				V. 
	(a) Child's love for stories. (b) What
kind of stories to be told. (c) True
stories preferable.


VI. 
(a) Child's desire for knowledge. (b)
Asking questions. (c) Parents should
seek to answer all questions to the under-
standing of the child. 
			VII. 
(a) What to teach our boys. (b) How
to be pure in their lives. (c) How to 
treat the opposite sex. 
			VIII.
(a) What to tell our girls. (b) To
have confidence in mother. (c) How to 
conduct themselves in the presence of 
young men.
			IX. 
(a) Peace making. (b) Value of peace 
at home and with neighbors. (c) Love
your neighbors—do unto them as you 
would have them do unto you. (d) Ev-
ils of backbiting.
	Cleanliness in person and the home.
			XI.
		INDUSTRY.
(a) Value of moments. (b) Economy
of time and means.
			XII
		PUNCTUALITY.
(a) Always on time. (b) Cultivate hab-
its of punctuality.
		PERSIS A. SPENCER, Sec.
Kanab Relief Society.
