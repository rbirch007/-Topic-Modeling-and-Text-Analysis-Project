NATIONAL COUNCIL OF GREAT BRITAIN.
	The annual conference of this council
will be held in Edinburg. The program
shows the names of their very large com-
mittee, on which there are twenty-four
vice presidents, and about twenty other
members. This council has between thirty
and forty branches Among the subjects to
be discussed will be “University Education
for Women, its Effects on Social and In-
tellectual Life,” “The Present Position
of Woman’s Suffrage,” “Public House
Trusts,” “Wage-Earning Children,” “The
Outlook of Women at Home and in the
Colonies,” “The Permanent Care of the
Feeble-Minded,” “The Relation of Amuse-
ments to Life” “To Health,” “To Educa-
tion,” “Popular Amusements for Working
Girls.” Papers on rescue work among
women and children, papers on organiza-
tion and committee work.
