WAYNE STAKE.
	Relief Society conference of Wayne
Stake, held at Loa, July 2, 1902, President
Jane S. Coleman presiding. Visiting sis-
ters, E. J. Stevenson and M. A. Caine.
	President Coleman in her opening re-
marks expressed her pleasure in having
missionary sisters, the Stake presidency
and many sisters from the wards. Re-
ported union, promptness and good feeling
in the Stake.
	Presidents reports. Harriet Staples, Loa;
Louisa J. Taylor, Fremont; Orilla Chapel,
Lyman; Sarah G. Meeks, Thurber. All
gave favorable reports. Counselor Sarah
S. Forsyth spoke upon living exemplary
lives.
	Sister Stevenson spoke on many elevat-
ing subjects pertaining to the home and the
Relief Society, and urged the sisters to
help along the work of the Lord.
	Sister Caine spoke upon obedience to the
priesthood, duties and responsibilities of
mothers, on exercising patience with chil-
dren and gaining their confidence, etc.
	At the close of the morning session an
officers’ meeting was held where questions
were asked, and answered by the visiting
sisters and good instructions given.
	Afternoon session. Sister Mary E.
Hanks spoke on the duties of mothers and
the training of children.
	Sister Caine addressed the conference and
made a specialty of the silk industry; also
spoke upon motherhood and prayer.
	Sister Stevenson spoke well upon the
grand mission of motherhood, the watch
care over children and cultivating faith; al-
so of economy, cleanliness, the Word of
Wisdom and urged the sisters to belong to
the Relief Society.
	President Willis E. Robison, Patriarch
E. H. Blackburn, Counselors H. M. Han-
son and G. S. Bastian each endorsed the
teachings of the visiting sisters, and ap- 
preciated their visit and teaching. Presi-
dent Coleman was grateful for the excellent
spirit that had been enjoyed.
	Conference adjourned for six months.
Benediction by Elder Lazenby.
