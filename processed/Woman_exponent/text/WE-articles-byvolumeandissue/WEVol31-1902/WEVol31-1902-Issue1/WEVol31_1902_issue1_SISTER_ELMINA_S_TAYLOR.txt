	SISTER ELMINA S. TAYLOR.

	My sisters I am sorry they have called
upon me to speak because my voice is so
poor. I am suffering from the effects of a
bad throat. If you cannot understand me
you will make allowance for it.
	I am proud today to be called upon to
make a few remarks in regard to our be-
loved Sister Horne  I have lived on the
same block with her for nearly forty years.
I have associated with her in her home and
in her labors among the sisters of Zion.  I
have served under her as a secretary for our
Relief Society in the 14th ward for many
years, and I have traveled through the
cities of Zion with her, and I can truthfully
say that I have never known a better
woman, one who is more full of the spirit
of inspiration and love of the Gospel and a
desire to do good on the earth while she
may live upon it. I can never remember
any circumstance where Sister Horne has
done anything that we should censure, but
has always been, to our knowledge, for the
advancement of the kingdom of God and of
the daughters of Zion. Her heart has been
filled with this and she has desired to per-
form the work of God in every way that
she could. We have enjoyed some of the
most lovely times in her home that we
have ever had anywhere. I presume be-
cause of that love and sympathy and the
spirit of the Gospel. She has been a model
for the daughters of Zion to take as an ex-
ample. And may God bless her, and may
her remaining days upon the earth be sweet
and peaceful, and may the love of God be
with her. May her children be preserved
and may they still continue to follow in the
paths of their parents is my prayer in the
name of Jesus. Amen.
