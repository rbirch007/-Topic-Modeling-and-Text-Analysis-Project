	EDITORIAL NOTES.

	The Kansas State Grange have petitioned
the Legislature of the State of Kansas to
support the bill giving to women the right
to vote for the President of the United
States in 1904.
	The Stake secretaries will please remem-
ber the annual reports for 1902 from Janu-
ary 1, to December 31, and prepare and
send them in as soon as possible. Missions
and branches outside of Stakes and missions
will also be expected to make proper re-
ports. Address Mrs. E. B. Wells; Temple-
ton, Salt Lake City, Utah.
	We have been requested by the Cor.
Sec’y of the National Council of Women of
the United States to announce officially
that the next Executive Session of the
National Council will be held in New
Orleans, Louisana, in March 26 to 29, in-
clusive, at the St. Charles Hotel. These
meetings will follow the Convention of the
National American Suffrage Association.
	It would seem there is great activity
manifest at least in our own country in re-
gard to public matters. Women are look-
ing critically into Civil Service Reform in a
systematic way and in our own State the
Kindergarten and Domestic Science meas-
ures are being looked after by committees
appointed from all the active women’s
organizations, and they will make a vigor-
ous campaign in the interest of these much
needed educational interests.
	The State Historical Society, composed
of both men and women, is also beginning
to feel the need of some assistance from the
State to acquire and preserve valuable relics
and historic pioneer mementoes that other-
wise would be lost to the coming genera-
tions, and which certainly should be a
part of the history and sociology of the set-
tlement and growth of this Rocky Moun-
tain and desert region. It is strange there is
little interest is this matter when one con-
siders what pains have been taken and
what means expended to collect and care
for antiquities from other countries.
