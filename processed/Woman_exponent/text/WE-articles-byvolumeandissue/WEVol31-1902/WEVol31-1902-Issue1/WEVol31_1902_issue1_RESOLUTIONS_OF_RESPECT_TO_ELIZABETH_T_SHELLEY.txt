RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT TO ELIZABETH T. SHELLEY.

	Whereas it has pleased our Heavenly Father
to call from us on May 24, 1902, our associate and
colaborer, Sister Elizabeth T. Shelley, who, al-
though having laid helpless on her bed for three
months with that dreadful disease, cancer, was
patient and cheerful to the last. She was first
vice-president in the 4th ward Relief Society of
American Fork, a sincere and earnest worker in
the cause of truth, a virtuous and loving wife,
a kind and faithful mother. She was the mother
of twelve children, six of whom survive her.
	Resolved, That we, the officers and members of
the society, feel the loss of such a faithful worker
in the cause of truth and that we humbly acknowl-
edge an over-ruling Power who doeth all things
well.
	That a copy of these resolutions be sent to the
husband and family and one be sent to the
“EXPONENT” for publication. 
ALICE G. STEELE,
NANCY B. FORBES,
SARAH H. SHELLEY,
	Committee
Relief Society, 4th ward American Fork.
