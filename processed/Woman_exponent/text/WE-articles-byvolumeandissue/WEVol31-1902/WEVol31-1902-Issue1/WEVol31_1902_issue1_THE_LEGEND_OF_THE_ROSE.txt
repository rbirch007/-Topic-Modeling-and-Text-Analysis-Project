THE LEGEND OF THE ROSE.

The white rose blossomed first in Paradise,
	And shed her fragrance on the balmy air,
And Eve smiled on it with her sinless eyes,
	When all the world was pure, and sweet and
		fair.

But when an exile from those heavenly bowers,
	Sad Eve went forth amid earth’s thorny
		ways, 
She bade good bye to all the lovely flowers
	She knew and cherished in her happy days.

Then bending low she kissed the snow-white rose.
	When, lo, its pure, pale petals changed to red,
As though it blushed to hear of sins and woes,
	As Eve knelt weeping by the garden bed!


Her falling tear-drops shone like pearly dew
	Upon its petals as she sobbed farewell,
And deeper still its ruddy color grew,
	As, one by one, the burning tear-drops fell.

And ever since the blood-red roses blow
	In sultry hours, and speak of joys and pain,
And lovers pause to pluck them as they grow,
	While nightingales chant on their soft refrain.

Perchance at times they feel the piercing thorn,
	Which ever guards the roses stem, ’tis said,
Since that long vanished hour in Eden’s morn,
	When sad Eve kissed the white rose petals
		red.
							M. E. S.
