NOTES AND NEWS

Mrs. Squeirs, wife of the American min-
ster to Cuba, is trying to organize a society
for the prevention of cruelty to animals.
	King Edward’s coming to the throne has
been celebrated in India with gorgeous
magnificence. The report read like a chap-
ter from the Arabian Nights. The expense,
of course, has been lavish and enormous.
At the same time the newspaper dispatches
report a terrible famine in Finland—thou-
sand of brave and intelligent people are on
the verge of starvation—and in Russia a
frightful famine of still wider extent, en-
volving fifteen million hungry peasants and
untold thousands of starving horses and
cattle.                                       Ex.
