THE NEW YEAR.

[Read at the New Year’s party, Cannonville,
Utah.]

	Good bye to the old year that has dis-
appeared for aye, never more to come
again. It has dealt kindly with us and we
will enshrine it gratefully in our memories.
	Few shadows lie on its receeding path-
way, and for the sunshine, the happiness
and pleasures of the past we may well be
thankful
	The hours that are past and gone we can
never recall, but in the future, as it comes,
every mistake can be corrected, and the
 hours be made to bubble over with useful-
ness and good.


The days of the new year will come
marching on, and may we improve the
shining moments that hare been designated
the “gold dust of time.”

New Year ! New Year dawning on us here,
Bring us many joys through this glad New Year,
May it hourly bring us peace and plenty too,
To the ones around us, though we number few.

New Year ! New Year ! may thy numerous days,
Glisten, ever glisten with joyous, happy rays;
May each flitting day a blessing to us be,
Until the last day of the year is but a memory.

New Year! New Year! may the months pass by,
Gliding smoothly for us in their coming nigh;
And our hearts beat happy till the welkin ring,
With another New Year’s praises, which we joy
	ous sing.

New Year! New Year ! we happy greet you here.
May we have no sadness, shed no bitter tear,
May our glad hearts raise in prayer, and praise
	and love,
To our loving Ruler in the heavens above,
