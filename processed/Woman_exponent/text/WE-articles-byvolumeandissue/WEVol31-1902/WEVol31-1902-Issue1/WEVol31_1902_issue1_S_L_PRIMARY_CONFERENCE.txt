S. L. PRIMARY CONFERENCE.

The Salt Lake Primary held a most in-
teresting cenference in Barratt Hall, on
the 20th of December last. All the mem-
bers of the Stake Board were present on
the platform and they were most agreeably
surprised at the splendid turnout of the
little folks, for never before had there been
so well attended a conference. There were
830 children; besides the officers and par-
ents that were present, and the spirit and
enthusiasm of the conference were beauti-
ful and inspiring. Sister Camilla Cobb
presided in her customary genial and pleas-
ant way. The roll call was very entertain-
ing, each ward responding with a senti-
ment, after stating the number present.
A most interesting program was rendered,
each number being very much appreciated
by the little folks and deserving of special
mention, but two little girls, Rhea Little-
wood, of the 29th ward, and Lucile Snow
Critchlow, of the 8th ward, were cun-
niug in their recitations and the little
boys’ band, of the 8th ward, furnished
most pleasing music.
One of the features of the conference
was the sketch of the life of President Geo.
Q. Cannon, by his little granddaughter
Emmeline, and if at the conferences this
idea is carried out and the children give simi-
lar sketches of the lives of our beloved lead
ers, it will not only be instructive, but will
keep in mind their good deeds and their
devotion to the Church, and we take great
pleasure in publishing the sketch the little
girl gave with the thought that it will be
not only pleasant reading, but an example
for future use.                  A. W. C.
