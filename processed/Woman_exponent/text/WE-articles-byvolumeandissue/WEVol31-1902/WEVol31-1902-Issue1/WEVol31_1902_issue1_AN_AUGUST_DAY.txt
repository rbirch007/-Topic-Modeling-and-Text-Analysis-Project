AN AUGUST DAY.
’Twas long ago—the golden light
	Of fair, unclouded August days
Had bathed the world in radiance bright,
	The garden beds were all ablaze
With asters, purple, red and white.
	That nodded in the sunshine’s rays.

White daises starred the velvet grass,
	With tiny, gleaming, golden eyes,
Tall weeds waved o’er the dark morass,
	Blue harebells shamed the azure skies,
And meadow-sweet—a fragrant mass—
	Set of the loosestrife’s purple dyes.

The sunflow’r raised his golden head.
	The lily pale was fair to see,
And robed in white and deepest red,
	The d[-]hlias blossomed royally;
The bees hummed o’er the flower-bed,
	The fruit was blushing on the tree. 

I met you as the sun sank low
	To rest within the Western sea,
Your lovelv face was all aglow.
	Your lips like cherries on the tree,
Your brow was pure as drifted snow—
	And you were all the world to me !
