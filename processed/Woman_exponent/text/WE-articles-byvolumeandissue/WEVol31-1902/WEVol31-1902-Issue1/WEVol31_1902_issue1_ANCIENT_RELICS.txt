		ANCIENT RELICS.

	One of the richest finds of relics of pre-
historic man is that unearthed by Captain
Francis Tuttle of the revenue cutter Bear
during her recent trip in the Arctic seas.
The investigations and discoveries were
made at the instance of Captain D F. Toz-
ler of the revenue cutter Grant, senior
officer of the revenue cutter service on the
coast. A house built by human beings
probably thousands of years ago was found,
and Captain Tolzer now has some of the
utensils used by the people who made it a
habitation.
	Two years ago a missionary at Point Bar-
row told Captain Tuttle of a remarkable dis-
covery the natives of the vicinity had made.
It was the framework of a house which
was partially embedded in a cliff fronting
on the ocean fifteen miles southwest of
Point Barrow. At the point where the
house was discovered protruding the cliff
rose sheer seventy feet from the ocean.
Forty feet above tidewater one end of the
house was exposed. The Indians first
noticed the end of the house nine years ago,
and year after year as the cl[-]ff crumbled in-
to the ocean the framework of the house
became more plainly visible. The house
was built somewhat after the plan of
structure of the primitive habitations of
America. The ribs of an enormous whale
or of some animal now extinct were used for
a framework, and over these, skins had
evidently been stretched. No trace of the
skins, however, could be found, but the
framework of the house was intact.
