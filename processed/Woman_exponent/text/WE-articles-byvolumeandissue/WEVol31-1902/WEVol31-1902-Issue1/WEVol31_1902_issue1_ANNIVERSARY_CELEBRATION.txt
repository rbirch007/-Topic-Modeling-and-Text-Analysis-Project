ANNIVERSARY CELEBRATION. 

	We wish to inform you that our little
village of Freedom held its anniversary 
meeting on Monday, March 18, 1902, to 
commemorate the organization of the 
Relief Society at Nauvoo in 1842. 
	On account of the loss of our beloved
sister, Hannah Crompton, our gathering
was partly to pay our tribute of respect to
her as well as the anniversary.
	The meeting was called to order by
President Mary E. Simonsen. Singing,
"Come ye children of the Lord." Prayer
by Brother Joseph Draper. Singing,
"Weary not." Roll was called, minutes
read and approved Welcome speech by
President Mary E. Simonsen, who said we
had met in commemoration of the organiza-
tion of the Relief Society by the Prophet
Joseph Smith, and read a few lines from
his teachings. She spoke in high esteem
of our departed sister, Hannah E. Cromp-
ton.
	Song by Sister Mattie Draper and com-
pany. Select reading from the WOMAN'S
EXPONENT by Sister Florence Lowry.
Reading by Sister Phoebe Draper. Reading
of resolutions of respect in memory of
Sister Hannah E. Crompton, by Sister
Emily Taylor.
	Brother John Crompton spoke to the
sisters, encouraging them to continue in 
the good work; also read a selection en-
titled "The Grove." Recitation by Sister
Minnie Lowry. Song by Sister Jane
Hunt.
	Bishop William Lowry spoke very en-
couragingly in behalf of the Relief Society,
also in kind remembrance of Sister Cromp-
ton.
	Brother Almon Draper also felt well in
being privileged to meet with the society.
Remarks by Brother Joseph Draper. 
	Meeting adjourned for one year. Sing-
ing. Benediction by Brother Almon
Draper.
		EMILY TAYLOR, Sec. pro tem.
