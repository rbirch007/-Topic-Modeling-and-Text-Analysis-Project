HANNAH E. CROMPTON.

	A token of respect and remembrance of our be-
loved sister, Hannah E. Crompton, who departed
this life March 14, 1901. Sister Crompton was
born in Olden, Lancashire, England, 1815. She
came to this country in 1844, and was married
to Brother John Crompton in Council Bluffs, 
March 1, 1850. She held the position of second
counselor in the Relief Society in American Fork
for eighteen years. As a teacher, wife, mother
and friend her example was worthy of imitation.
She was one of the noblest of God’s handmaidens


and was faithful through all trials and tribulations 
that beset her pathway. And we, as members of 
the Relief Society of the Freedom Ward of San-
[-]ete Stake of Zion, form the following resolutions: 
	RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT. 
	Whereas God has seen fit to call from this life 
our beloved sister, Hannah E. Crompton,
	Resolved, That we mourn in her death the loss 
of a faithful worker, a comforter in the hour of 
trouble, a devoted wife and mother and a true 
Latter-day Saint. 
That we esteem her faithful labors worthy of 
emulation by every member of the society, that 
we sympathize and mourn with the aged husband, 
the children, grandchildren, great grandchildren 
and friends. 
That we send a copy of these resolutions to the 
WOMAN'S EXPONENT for publication, that a copy 
be kept in the society and that one be given to her 
husband, Brother John Crompton. 
MARY E. SIMONSEN, 
FLORENCE LOWRY,
