	MILLARD STAKE.

Lecture 1st.—Motherhood:

	A being breathing thoughtful breath,
	A traveler between life and death,
	The reason firm, the temperate will,
	Endurance, foresight, strength and skill;
	A perfect woman nobly planned,
	To warn, to comfort and command.

The great mission of woman predestined.
Nature and Character study.
Natural intuitions and environments.
Cultivate the good and evil will vanish.
Faith, hope and charity the only true
	guides.
Improve the present by studying the
	past.
“While it is good to walk among the liv-
	ing, it is good to live with the wise,
	great, good dead.”

Lecture 2nd.—Prenatal Influence.

What influences the first of life be-
	comes a part of its being.
As ye sow, so shall ye reap.
Seek wisdom, self-control, happy dispo-
	sition.
Physical exercise, home duties; moral
	and mental development.
Study the life of Christ, “The fear of
	God is the beginning of wisdom.”

Lecture 3rd.—Relationship of Mother and
	Child:

“Of all earth’s affections, pure, undefiled,
Is the love and devotion of an innocent child.”

The child a sacred trust or gift from God.
The purest love exists between mother
	and child.
Innate principles the governing influ-
	ence.
The tenderest influence felt in the one
	word—mother.


[Column 1]
Mother’s influence in its daily life.
 
	Speak gently, it is better far, 
		To rule by love than fear, 
	Speak gently, let no harsh word mar,
		The good we might do here.

Lecture 4th.—Growth and Development of
	the Child:

	“As the twig is bent the tree inclines.”
	Teach the importance of self-activity,
		self-reliance, self-control as essential
		elements to proper growth.
	Free, outdoor exercise, proper food,
		cleanliness, orderly habits build up a
		healthy body.
	Reverence for parents and the Deity are
		spiritual needs.
	The Lord’s prayer, etc.

“Let love through all your actions run,
Yet over-indulgence spoils the little one,
By love and firmness with wisdom wrought.
Many a soul from sin is brought.”

Lecture 5th.—Boyhood and Girlhood:

	Train up a child in the way he should
		go and when he is old he will not de-
		part from it.
	To build up a healthy body: outdoor ex-
		ercise, physical training, purity of
		thought and action.
	Obedience to the Gospel. Teach the
		Articles of Faith.
	Teach that there is good in every one,
		and that it must grow from the inside
		not without until a man is all full of
		goodness.
	Teach the relation between the desire to
		be good and the practice of being
		good.

	“The good we do in life,
		Springs from an inward grace:
	The mission of evil is strife,
		Each good act to displace;
	To build a character firm and strong,
		Encourage the right and denounce
	the wrong.”

Lecture 6th.—Development of the Youth:

	Seek wisdom from good books. Emulate
		the acts of wise men.
	Cultivate pure thoughts and actions.
	The mind should control natural desires.
	Growth obtained through self activity.
	Scientific truth’s bring us to a higher
		plane of civilization.
	The life of Christ the embodiment of all
		that is desired to make us perfect even
		as He is perfect.
	“The glory of God is intelligence,”
		should be an incentive to all who desire
		to become like Him

Lecture 7th.—Marriage and Sacred Coven-
	ants:

	“Likings may come and pass away
	’Tis love that remains to our latest day,
	Our Heavenward way is holy love,
	And will be our bliss with saints above.”

	Love is an attribute of God implanted for
		a pure and holy purpose.
	Marriage is a divine covenant instituted
		for the propagation of the human race.
	The life of Christ embodies all the vir-
		tues.
	Our obligations to God and our sacred
		trust.
	He is richest who enriches mankind.

Lecture 8th.— Wife and Mother:

	To be a true wife and mother be true to
		your covenants.
	Peace, love and contentment come from
		within and make our environments.

[Column 2]
	Self-control and sacrifices are enobling.
	Poverty no restriction to a happy home.
	Study the lives of great and noble minds.
	Prayer is the key to success.
	Impart of our substance if we would be
		truly great.
	“It is better to give than to receive.”

Lecture 9th.—A Mother’s Love and In-
	fluence:

	A mothers love soothes the aches and
		ills of life.
	Every mother should be imbued with the
		love of God.
	Its imprints may be traced to the unborn.
	Great men attribute achievements to
		mother’s influence.
	Strength of mind imparts will power,
		and crushes the evil that would under-
		mine our best endeavors.
	“The hand that rocks the cradle rules
		the world.”

Lecture 10th.—Diseases of Women and
	Children:

	Every mother should study the laws of
		health.
	The obligations of wife and mother can-
		not be discharged without some knowl-
		edge of hygiene and medicine.
	Study the nature of diseases and sim-
		ple remedies.
	Healing by prayer and faith. 
	Word of Wisdom.

Lecture 11th.— Value of Moments:

	The mind a receptacle or storehouse and
		is never idle.
	It advances or retrogrades.
	Each day adds its might for good of
		evil.
	Youth is the golden age.
	Improve the present. There is no to-
		morrow.
	Men cannot be saved in ignorance.
	The glory of God is intelligence.
	Seek ye out of good books words of wis-
		dom
	A moment lost cannot be recalled.

“Count that day lost whose low descending sun,
Views from thy hands no worthy action done.”

Lecture 12th.— Woman’s Sphere:

	Woman know thyself and to thyself be
		true.
	Mental and physical organization.
	Moral and social condition.
	Her influence upon society,
	Her natural religious inclinations.
	Quick perception and capablities.
	Susceptible to influence but conscientious
		of wrong doing.
	Progress in science and literature.
	The woman of the 20th century.

	“Oh not for wealth, fame or power.
		Have we poor mortals striven.
	But even as the growing flower
		To make of earth a heaven.
	And in her garden of the sun,
		Heaven’s brightest rose shall bloom,
	For woman’s best is just begun,
		Her advent yet to come.”
