WHEN ROSES DIE.

When roses die, all crimson glow
	The fading leaves that soon shall lie
Upon the forest pathway low.
When roses die
	The winds of Autumn sadly sigh.

The waves are crowned with foamy snow,
	And shadows cross the changing sky,
And rain-filled streamlets swiftly flow,
	The fickle swallows southward fly,
My thoughts go back to long ago,
	When roses die.
