A BOOK FOR THE HOLIDAYS.
Eleven years ago and more, the writer
obtained the copyright of a book of stories
she had written for publication. Circum-
stances prevented bringing out the book at
the time, and although repeated efforts
to publish have been made since none have
succeeded. The writer has now decided to
publish by subscription if a sufficient num-
ber of persons can be secured who are will-
ing to pay beforehand, so as to give the
publishers a guarantee to undertake the
work. The book will be entitled “Aunt
Em’s Stories of Long Ago.” It will con-
tain “Hephzibah,” which caused quite a
sensation when it appeared as a serial in
1889’ “Aunt Esther’s Sweetheart,” “Some
Old Love Letters,” “He Cometh Not,”
and other short stories. The stories are all
founded upon facts—of course the names
are fictitious, necessarily.
	Any one desiring further particulars may
obtain them by writing to this office. If
possible to raise the money the book will
be on sale at Christmas and during the
holidays. Any money taken in will be
receipted for and returned if the book is not
issued.
