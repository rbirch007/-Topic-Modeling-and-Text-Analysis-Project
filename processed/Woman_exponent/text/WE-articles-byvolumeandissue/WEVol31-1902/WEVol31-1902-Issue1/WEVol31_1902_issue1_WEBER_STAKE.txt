	WEBER STAKE.

First Talk. The Infant. Its First Year:

	(a) The bath. Different modes of
bathing the young child. Cleanliness
next to Godliness, etc.
	(b) Dress. For comfort, regardless
of style.
	(c) Food. Natural or artificial; reg-
ularity.
	(d) Sleep. Sleep and grow. Let
nature wake the baby.
	(e) Creeping. The benefits.
	(f) Teething.
	(g) Education begins at the cradle,
whether we will or not.

Second Talk. Mother's Medicine Chest:

	(a) No drugs should be given to
baby.
	(b) The chest. Its contents.
	(c) Simple remedies. Nature and
use.
	(d) Teething disorders, colds, croup,
burns, canker, punctured wounds.
	(e) Prevention is better than cure.
To be forewarned is to be forearmed,
etc.

Third Talk. The Child from Second Year
	to School:

	(a) Its growth physically. Light
and sunshine as necessary to the
growth of a child as to a plant.
	(b) Its growth morally. The power
of singing. Prayer and pleasant eve-
ning talks. Stories true to life.
	(c) Obedience and order. The first
laws of heaven.
	(d) Respect for age and places of
worship.
	(e) Sabbath for little children. Plato
says “The most important part of edu-
cation is in the nursery.”

Fourth Month Talk. Child's Duty at Home
	and at School.

	(a) Why do children lie, steal, pro-
fane, or use slang ?
	(b) Methods of counteracting evils.
	(c) Late hours: effects. Children
on streets or away from home at
nights.
	(d) Respect for the rights of others,
public property, etc.
	(e) Teach household duties to girls
and boys. Compare cultivation of
childhood to the cultivation of flowers.
The neglect to the neglected flower
bed.

Fifth Month Talk. Youth and Maiden:

	(a) Health of the young girl. Spec-
ial care now required.
	(b) Need of mother's sympathy.
Gain their confidence and keep their 
secrets.
	(c) Special religious training in
order that the boy may honor and de-
sire to hold different degrees of Priest-
hood.
	(d) Selecting companions and books
suitable for them.
	(e) Special training in cooking,

[column 2]
sewing and general housework.
	(f) Courting and marrying in the
Church, so as not to be deprived of
blessings in the Gospel. Incidents of
healing by laying on of hands.
	(g) The importance of the Priest-
hood for our sons.

Sixth Month Talk. Wife and Mother. As
	Mothers in Israel What Manner of
	Women Should They Be?

	(a) Sound mind and sound body.
	(b) Good housekeepers.
	(c) Deportment to husbands and
children.
	(d) Exemplary lives, as Caesar,
said, above reproach. Studying the
principles of the Gosoel. Following
its teachings will give her the inspir-
ation needed for her varied duties as
wife and mother.

	Praying God to bless each and all of our
sisters, and help them to fill the measure
of their creation in an acceptable manner
before Him, is the humble prayer of,
	Your sisters,
	C. E. DRIVER,
	R. E. MOENCH,
	Committee.
	J. S. RICHARDS,
	President.
