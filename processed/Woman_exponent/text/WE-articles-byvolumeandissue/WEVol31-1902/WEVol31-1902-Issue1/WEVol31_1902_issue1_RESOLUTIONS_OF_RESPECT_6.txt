RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.
Whereas our Heavenly Father has seen fit in his 
wisdom to call from among us one of our colabor- 
ers, we deeply feel her loss. She was a model 
wife and mother and endeared herself to those 
around her by her kindness in times of sickness. 
She possessed an affable, pleasant nature and 
won the esteem and good feelings of her many ac-
quaintances, She leaves a loving husband, who 
mourns her departure, and eight children, besides 
several motherless children whom she cared for in 
childhood, who all feel the loss of a loving mother, 
until that joyful time when they will meet again 
and enjoy her society in a state far more happy 
and glorified. Therefore be it 
	Resolved, that we, her colaborers in the great 
work of love, express our appreciation of her ex- 
ample. 
	That we emulate her noble traits of character, 
of faith and charity and her devotion to her re- 
ligion.
	That we insert a copy of these resolutions in our 
records, that one be sent to the WOMAN'S EXPON-
ENT for publication and that one be presented to 
the bereaved family. 
SUSANNA PICKERING,
MARTHA BATES,
FANNY Z. SIMMONS,
Committee.
