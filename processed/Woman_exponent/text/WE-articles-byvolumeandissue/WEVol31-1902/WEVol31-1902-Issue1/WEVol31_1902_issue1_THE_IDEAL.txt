THE IDEAL.

Think not thou ever shalt attain
	To all that highest seems,
Hope not at any time to reign
	The monarch of thy dreams.

If at a step thy foot should feel
	The height that lures thy soul,
That height would other heights reveal
	Above its longed-for goal.

And should an angel lend thee wings
	To soar from peak to peak,
Thy furthest flight would show the things
	Far off and still to seek.

But though thy journey never cease—
	From light to greater light—
Would’st change it for a sluggish peace,
	With earth alone in sight?
						W. W.
