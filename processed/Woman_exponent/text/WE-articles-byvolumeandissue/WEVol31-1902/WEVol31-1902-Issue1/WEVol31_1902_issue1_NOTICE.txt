NOTICE.
	A booklet has been issued by the Gener-
al Relief Society, to which the following
note has been added since its publication.
It is inserted in the “EXPONENT” for the
information of persons who received copies
of the pamphlet previous to the insertion of
this addition:
	Note to page 77.—It is due to Dr.
Margaret C. Roberts to add that while the
importance and benefits of the establish-
ment of classes for instruction in nursing
were thought of and talked about for many
years before they were organized in this
Stake of Zion, the practical work and con-
duct of these classes was first undertaken
by her, and she is largely entitled to the
credit of the success that has attended
them under the auspices of the Relief
Society.”
	All inquiries in reference to the Relief
Society Mother’s Classes should be directed
to Ida S. Dusenberry, Provo, and all cor-
repondence relating to the Nurse Class or
Nurse Work, should be addressed to Annie
T. Hyde, 40 West, North Temple Street,
Salt Lake City, Utah.
			BATHSHEBA W. SMITH,
			ANNIE T. HYDE,
