OBITUARY.
	Ellen Redman Hutchinson, born at Luddington
Yorkshire, England, May 17, 1833.
	Emigrated to Utah in ox teams, enduring cheer-
fully the trials and privations incident to such a
trip. She with her husband settled in Salt Lake
City, afterwards moving to Deseret, Millard Co.,
being among the first settlers of that place; taking
her share of trial and privation, as well as Indian
troubles with the rest of her brethren and sisters.
At one time an old Indian came to her house ask-
ing for food and everything else which he could
see. When Sister Hutchinson refused to give the
things to him, he pulled out his knife. Sister
Hutchinson took up the fire shovel with a good
supply of fire on it, and ran after the Indian. He
ran away as fast as possible, and did not come
back.
	For many years she was an officer in the Des-
eret Primary Association, also a teacher in the
Rellef Society both in Deseret and Hinckley, to,
which place they moved from Deseret She was a
kind mother and great friend to the needy, depriv-
ing herself of needful food many times to give to
others. For five years she suffered from paralysis,
which she bore without a murmur. She finally
was seized with la grippe, and died at the resi-
dence of her daughter, Mrs. James Garrett, at
Nephi.
			NAOMI R. THEOBOLD,
				President Hinckley R. S.
