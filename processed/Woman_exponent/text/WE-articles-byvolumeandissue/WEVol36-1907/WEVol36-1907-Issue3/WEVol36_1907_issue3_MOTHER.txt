MOTHER.

Respectfully dedicated to Professor Kent.

The sweetest word in human lore,
Repeated most and thought of more,
Its music rings and means the same
Whene’er you speak that holiest name.
In spheres afar, in heaven above.
Where’er is heard the song of love!
It blends with motherhood in b iss, 
Its smiles, caress, or lingering kiss,
I here salute and bend the knee,
My mother, thou art all to me!	
And though removed from me afar,
Thou art for e’er my guiding star!
Thou art my hands, my feet, my whole,
The guardian of my childish soul,

		Chorus:

My mother dear, I hail thy name,
In every stage, from youth to fame, 
And I thy blessing oft implore,
When heart is weary, sick or sore,
My mother! oh, my mother!

Into thy presence-chamber, I,
In life’s mad rush oft freely fly;
Thy counsel lit my earliest years,
And oft I ask it now in tears.
Brave, true and steadfast, always right,
When mists obscured my clouded sight,
I turned to thee, as unto God,
His agent on earth’s rugged sod;
And often wish that thou wert nigh,
My sainted, sinless mother, here I sigh;
Come teach my wandering feet to tread
The path that you so bravely trod;
I know it leads, though weary, sore,
Back to the Father’s unlatched door.

My hair is whitening now, like hers,
And thought full oft my bosom stirs,
Shall I find welcome at her door,
When called to leave life’s changing
	shore?
Can I be free from stain of sin?
And hear her voice say, “Come in! come
	in!”
“I’ve longed to hear your voice—your
	song;
I’ve waited here and waited long
To hear you, with the choir above,
That anthem called 'Redeeming Love,'
Which makes the dome of heaven ring,
And every angel doth it sing;
You, too, have worked and toiled for
	this—
The acme of eternal bliss.”
