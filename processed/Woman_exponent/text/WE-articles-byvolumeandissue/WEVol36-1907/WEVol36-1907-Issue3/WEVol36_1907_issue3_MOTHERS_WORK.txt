MOTHERS’ WORK.
		PANGUITCH STAKE.
	1st. Life and its responsibilities, why
are we here? Unity, love and loyalty in
home.
	2nd. Have an aim in life, that of mak-
ing the home happy. Good cooking an
important factor. Order, cleanliness
environment.
	3rd. Hygiene and sanitation. Preven-
tion of contagious diseases. (1) Plenty of
sleep and fresh air. (2) Care of bed-
rooms, (a) boys, (b) girls. (3) Proper
care of cellar, yards, etc.
	4th. (1) Industry necessary to success.
(2) How to teach children habits of indus-
try. (3) Benefits derived. Idlers shall
not eat the bread of the laborer. D.
C. Sec. 42.
	5th. Obedience (a) Willing. (b) Blind.
Do not expect more from children than
we are willing to do ourselves. Children’s
rights to be respected.
	6th. Courtesy, children should be taught
to be courteous. (1) How? (2) Why?
Courtesy is uplifting as it leads each to
render respect to another.
	7th. Cheerfulness, how obtained. (1)
Show gratitude for blessings. (2) Count
your blessings, not your trials. Do not
always bring another’s faults before
them. Commend their good deeds so
their minds may build upon good rather
than evil.
	8th. Moral training, our girls and boys,
what they shall be taught, and who shall
teach it. Associates; show the evil effect
of going into society too young. Re-
tain the confidence of your children.
How? (1) Share their joys and sorrows.
(2) Never betray a child’s confidence
even to another member of the family.
	9th. Punishment. Is punishment nec
essary and what is its object? What
faults in the parents often result in pun-
ishment for the child? Give examples.
	10th. Reverence for sacred things. (1)
Reverence for Deity. (2) Reverence for
the house of worship. Irreverence comes
mostly from thoughtlessness and not
from desire.
	11th. Give plan for serving. (1) Break-
fast. (2) Dinner. (3) Supper. Give menu
for each.
	12th. Social duties. What claim has
society and the Church on the time of
young mothers? What rights have chil-
dren to entertain in the home? (1) Sab-
bath visiting, and (2) Sabbath night so-
cials.
	HANNAH A. CROSBY, Pres. R. S.
