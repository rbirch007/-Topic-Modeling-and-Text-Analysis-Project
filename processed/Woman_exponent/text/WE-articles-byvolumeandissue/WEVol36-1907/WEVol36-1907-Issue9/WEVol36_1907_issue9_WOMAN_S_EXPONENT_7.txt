WOMAN’S EXPONENT.


The program consisted of an eloquent ad-
dress of welcome read by Assistant Secre-
tary Lillian Belnap, remarks by Bishop D.
H. Ensign, Jane S. Richards, Stake Presi-
dent of Relief Society, and President C. F.
Middleton of the Presidency, interspersed
with musical numbers.
	One of the most interesting parts of the
program was the presentation to each of the
retiring officers of a beautiful rocking chair
from the Relief Society members. These
honored guests each made a response ex-
pressing their appreciation of the sentiment
which had prompted the tribute paid them
on this occasion.
	Dainty refreshments were served during
the time of sociability that followed, and all
departed feeling that the evening would be
long held in happy remembrance.
