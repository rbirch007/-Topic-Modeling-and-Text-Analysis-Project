	A DELIGHTFUL RECEPTION.
	Our sisters generally throughout the entire
Church in all parts of the world are aware
now through reading the EXPONENT and
other publications, that the birthday of our
General President Bathsheba W. Smith oc-
curs on May 3, and that the Relief Society
or the family of this illustrious woman, one
or both, aim to celebrate it and make it an
occasion of rejoicing. This year the real
anniversary came on the Sabbath day and
it was the Fast Day; therefore no demon-
stration could be made on that day. Sister
Smith however chose Saturday, May 2, 1908,
and herself decided to given an open recep-
tion at her own home to her friends, and
to her co-workers in the Relief Society and in
the Temple.
	This idea was carried out and the affair
was in every way most enjoyable. The ar-
rangements were most tasteful and artis-
tic in color and design, the parlor was in
pink and white, pink and white roses, (the
May blossom colors) here and there in pro-
fusion; old fashioned portraits adorned the
walls of Father John Smith the Patri-
arch and his wife, and an excellent picture of
President George A. Smith, husband of the
hostess, whose death occurred many years
ago hung near a sofa, where the hostess was
receiving; the dining room was in green and
the softest lace curtains at the windows
helped to complete a pretty picture of ar-
tistic decorating that blended so harmoni-
ously with the entire furnishings of the
home, that a quiet serenity pervaded the at-
mosphere of the house. The central figure
of course was the hostess herself, arrayed in
