CAROLINE E. PARTRIDGE LYMAN.
	To the memory of our beloved Sister
Caroline E. Lyman, President of the Oak
City Relief Society since its organization in
1875, until 1906, when she was honorably
released.
	She was born at Painesville, Ohio, Jan. 8,
1827, and died May 5, 1908.
	She was associated with Joseph Smith,
the Prophet, passed the early persecutions
of the Church. Her father, Presiding Bishop
Edward Partridge, was mobbed, tarred and
feathered, and otherwise mistreated for his
religion.
	She was the mother of five children, all
members of the Church. She was a pioneer
of both Utah and California.
			RESOLUTIONS.
	Whereas, God has taken unto Himself our
beloved sister, friend and mother, therefore
be it
	Resolved, that in the death of Sister Ly-
man, Oak City Relief Society has lost one of
its stanchest supporters, the poor a gener-
ous and true friend, her children a tender,
devoted mother.


Resolved, that though we miss her from
our association and home, we can truly say
of her, “Blessed are the dead who die in
the Lord; she rests from her labors and her
works do follow her.
	Resolved that these resolutions be copied
in our record and a copy sent to the Wom-
an’s Exponent for publication.
			VERSES.
Mother, mother, true and tender,
	Though your form is bent with age,
Though your steps were slow and faltering.
	And your life a well-filled page—
Yet we miss you, dearest mother,
	None on earth your place can fill.
Your dear old room, your vacant chair
	Seemed hallowed with your presence still,
May we meet you in God’s kingdom.
	When our life on earth is done;
There to dwell in joy and gladness,
	With the Father and the Son.
			MARY M LYMAN.
			LYDA L. FINLENSON,
							Sec’y.
