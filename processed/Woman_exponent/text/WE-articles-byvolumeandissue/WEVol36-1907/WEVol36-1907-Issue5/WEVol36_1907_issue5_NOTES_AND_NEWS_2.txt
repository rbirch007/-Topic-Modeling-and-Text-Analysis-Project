NOTES AND NEWS.
	Mrs. Ida H. Harper has completed the
third volume of the “Life and Works of
Susan B. Anthony.” She expects that
it will be out by February, Miss Anthony’s
birth month.
	Delegate Taylor of Kalamazoo has in-
troduced in the Michigan Constitutonal
Convention a proposal to allow women
taxpayers to vote on all measures which
include the expenditure of the public
funds.
	In Holland, a royal decree emanating
from Minister Kraus has abolished the
rule promulgated three years ago which
forbade women employed in the postal
and telegraph offices to marry.
	Mrs. Kenward, who has just celebrated
her 88th birthday, has been postmistress
for 61 years at the quaint old postoffice
at Wivelsfield, near Hayward’s Heath,
England. In addition to the work of the
office Mrs. Kenward attends to her own
household duties, and is a great reader of
the newspapers.
