IN MEMORIAM.
	PRISCILLA SMITH TAYLOR.
Come to the bridal chamber, Death!
Come to “the mother when she feels
For the first time, her first-born’s breath,”
	It seems only yesterday, as it were,
that we published the marriage of the
young woman whose demise we now
feel it a duty to record. Her married
life was so brief, yet so sweet, one
cannot but feel that in such a short space
of time, one may experience the heights
and depths of human joy and sorrow.
	Sister Priscilla Smith Taylor was a
happy bride on October 25th of last year
(1906), and on November 3rd of this
present year (1907) she passed away to
the “better land,” leaving an infant
daughter six weeks old; her husband,
Elder George S. Taylor, and her widowed
mother, Sister Susan E. Smith, and others
of her family were almost inconsolable
at the loss of one so precious and so
lovable, and whose circumstances gave
promise of so much happiness, These
losses to those who remain seem irrepar-
able, and only time can in part assuage that
poignant grief; but to those who have an
implicit faith in the hope of a resurrec-
tion there is always a consolation: and
the spirit of the Gospel sheds its rays up-
on the heart, of all who bow in meek sub-
mission to the dispensations of divine
Providence.
“And this we call a loss! O, selfish sorrow
	Of selfish hearts! O, we of little faith!
Let us look round some argument to borrow,
Why we in patience should await the morrow
	That surely must succeed this night of death.
