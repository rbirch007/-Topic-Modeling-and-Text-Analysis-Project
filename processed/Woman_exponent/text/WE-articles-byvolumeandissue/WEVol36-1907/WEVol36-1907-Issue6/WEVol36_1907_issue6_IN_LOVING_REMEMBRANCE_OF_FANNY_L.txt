IN LOVING REMEMBRANCE OF FANNY L.
				HARPER.
	On the 16th of September, 1907, there passed
away from earth one of the noble spirits of this
dispensation, Sister Fanny L. Harper, beloved
wife of Prest. William T. Harper, of the Cassia
stake presidency. Sister Harper was the daugh-
ter of Chester and Rosanna Loveland, and was
born at Call’s Fort, Utah, January 9, 1863. In
1884, in connection with her husband: she moved
to Albion, Cassia county, Idaho, where in meek-
ness of spirit she won the respect and love of all
who associated with her. In the summer of 1900
her husband was called to the presidency of Cas-
sia stake of Zion, and it became necessary to
move to Oakley, the headquarters of the stake,
and from that time on she was an ever ac:ive
worker in the Relief society.
	She was president of the First Ward Society
until that ward was divided and the Fourth
Ward was created, at which time she was made
president of the new ward society, which position
she held until a month befere her death. All
were reluctant to release her, hoping and pray-
ing she would recover from a serious illness of
heart trouble that had overtaken her a year be-
fore. She had the greatest desire to live and
raise her children, and could not be reconciled
until a few days before she passed away, when
too weak to speak above a whisper, those at her
bedside overheard her beseeching her Father in
heaven for strength to do His will, and from that
time sweet peace pervaded their home? She was
the mother of ten children, six of whom survive
her, the eldest being on a mission to New Zealand.
