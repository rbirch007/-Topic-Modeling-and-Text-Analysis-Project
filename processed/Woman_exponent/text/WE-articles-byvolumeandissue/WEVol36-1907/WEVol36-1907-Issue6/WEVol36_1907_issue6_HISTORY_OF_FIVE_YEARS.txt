	HISTORY OF FIVE YEARS.
	The tenth annual gathering, was unique 
and really bewildering, for we found our-
selves on this occasion among the ladies
of the White house, the first ladies of the 
land.
	Entertained by the retiring president,
Mrs. C. C. R. Wells, the members were
dressed in costume, each representing
some lady of the White House, each giv-
ing a short sketch of the lady represented.
Some choice musical selections were
played, and Mrs. Lydia D. Alder read a 
history of the club covering in brief its
first decade.
	Mrs. F. S. Richards wore the costume
of Martha Washington previous to her
marriage to General George Washington.
Mrs. Doolan, Martha Washington at fifty-
seven years; E. B. Wells, Abigail Adams;
Mrs. C. C. R. Wells, Mrs. Hayes; Mrs,
John McVicker, Mrs. Fillmore; Mrs. E-
Jenson, Martha Pattin; Mrs. Margaret
Caine, Mrs. John Quincy Adams; Miss
Neff, Letitia C. Tyler; Mrs. Lucy A.
Clark, Mrs. Andrew Johnson; Mrs. Grace
Anderson, Mrs. Ulysses S. Grant; Dr.
Romania B. Pratt, Martha Jefferson
Randolph; Mrs. Lydia D. Alder, Mrs.
Andrew Johnson; Mrs. Ella Hyde, Mrs.
Garfield; Mrs. Lizzie Shipp, Mrs. McKin-
ley.
	The officers elected at this meeting
were: President, Mrs. Lydia D. Alder
First Vice President, Mrs. Ella W. Hyde;
Second Vice-President, Mrs. Grace L. 
Anderson; Treasurer, Mrs. R. H. Doolan;
Recording secretary, Emma H. Jenson
Corresponding Secretary, Ellis R. Shipp.
	The death of Mrs. Phebe C. Young, a
talented and valued member of the club,
occurred in December, 1901.
	At the November meeting Mrs. Anna
C. Widtsoe was received as a member of 
the club. At this meeting President Mrs.
Alder spoke of the death of Mrs. Sarah
Carmichael Williams, who for thirty
years had been out of her mind. Paid a
high tribute to her wonderful gift of
poetry, and spoke of the sad ending of 
such a brilliant, gifted writer. Mrs. E.
B. Wells also spoke ot the dead poetess.
	She was attired in the richest black
velvet, with real lace collar and cuffs,
as she laid in a casket of deepest black, a
fleshy, portly woman of over sixty years.
“Who are these arrayed in white?” how
vividly the words come to mind, here 
before the pall of death!
