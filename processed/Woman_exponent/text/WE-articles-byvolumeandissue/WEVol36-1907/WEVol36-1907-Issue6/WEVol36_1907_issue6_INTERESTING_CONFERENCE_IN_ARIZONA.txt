INTERESTING CONFERENCE IN ARIZONA.
	Relief Society Conference of St. Joseph
Stake was held in Thatcher, December 23,
1907, in honor of the 102nd anniversary of
the birth of the Prophet Joseph Smith, Pres-
dent Elizabeth Layton presiding. After
singing, and prayer by Patriarch Samuel
Claridge, First Counselor Salina Phillips
made some opening remarks and welcomed
all present.
	President Layton stated the object of the
meeting, commenting on the event of the
birth of the Prophet.
	President Andrew Kimball gave a de-
scriptive narrative of a visit to the cities of
Nauvoo, Independence, and adjacent places
of interest to the Saints.
	Roll call showed eight stake officers pres-
ent, and eight society organizations repre-
sented.
	Emma B. Coleman of the Stake Board
gave a detailed account of the organization
of the first Relief Society, quoting the
Prophet many times during her remarks.
	Congregation sang “Redeemer of Israel.”
when the conference was adjourned until 2
p. m., during which time all repaired to the
hall below, where luncheon was served and
the noon hour spent in social chat, and re-
lating of events in the early history of the
Church.
	Afternoon session opened by the assembly
singing the hymn sung by the Apostle John
Taylor just prior to the martyrdom, “A poor
wayfaring man of grief.”
	Statistical and financial report of the
Stake was read, and the names of the offic-
ers presented by the secretary and unani-
mously sustained.
	John F. Nash of the Stake Presidency
spoke on the life of the Prophet. The
speaking was interspersed with quartet,
solos and recitations, all appropriate selec-
tions for the occasion, with also an original
poem by Samuel Claridge.
	The social feature of the conference was
an old-time, daneing party, which was
greatly enjoyed by all who participated-
			SARAH WEBB, Cor. Sec.
