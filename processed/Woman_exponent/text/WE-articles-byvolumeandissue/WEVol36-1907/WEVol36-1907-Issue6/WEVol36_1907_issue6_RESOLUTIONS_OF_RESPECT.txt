RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.
To the Memory of Sarah B. Layton, President
	of the Kaysville Relief Society for Twenty-
	four Years.

	Sister Sarah B. Layton was born July 6, 1826,
in Bedfordshire, England, and departed this life
September 13th, 1906. She was the daughter of
William and Elizabeth Barnes and the wife of the
late Christopher Layton. She was the mother of
six children, five of whom survive her.
	The officers of the Kaysville Relief Society of
the Davis Stake of Zion have prepared the follow-
ing resolutions:
	Whereas, our eternal Father has called home
one of his most earnest workers in his cause,
therefore be it
	Resolved, That we, the officers and members
of this Society, offer our earnest sympathy to the
bereaved family.
	Resolved, That we do humbly testify that we do
greatly appreciate her noble works. The leading
traits of her character were charity, love, and
humility. She died as she had lived, a faithful
Latter-day Saint, and her light will shine before
us in time and in eternity. We realize that she
is enjoying the rest of the faithful and she will
come forth in a glorious resurrection crowned
with eternal life.
	Resolved, That these resolutions be recorded on
our Relief Society Record, also published in the
Woman’s Exponent.
	In behalf of the Kaysville Relief Society,
	Sarah H. Sheffield, President; Elizabeth Lar-
kins, Susannah Robins, Counselors; Alice Foxley,
Secretary.
