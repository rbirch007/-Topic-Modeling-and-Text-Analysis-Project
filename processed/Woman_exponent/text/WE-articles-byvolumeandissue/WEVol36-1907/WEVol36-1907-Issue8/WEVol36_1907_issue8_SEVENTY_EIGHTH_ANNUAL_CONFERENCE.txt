SEVENTY-EIGHTH ANNUAL CONFERENCE.
	The General Conference of the Church
held this month was very numerously at-
tended and the spirit of the discourses of the
Presidency, Apostles and other brethren
were characterized by a lofty religious and
spiritual fervor that was most helpful and
uplifting in its tendency. The music was
very inspiring and melodious, and altogether
there was a general feeling of unity, and con-
cert of action in all matters pertaining to
the upbuilding of the kingdom of God upon
the earth, and the furtherance of the spread
of the Gospel in all lands, that must give en-
couragement to those who are consecrating
their lives to the cause of truth and right-
eousness. It was a glorious sight to behold
the assembly in the large tabernacle and to
note the oneness of spirit and feeling, the
desire to promote the best interests and wel-
fare of the Saints and of all humanity.
	One can easily perceive the awakening
among the Saints, during the past few years
and since some of the old landmarks have
been re-established and interest manifested
in improving and beautifying the birthplace
of the Prophet Joseph Smith, and the Temple
district in Independence. The fact seems
so perceptible and is so gratifying, that those
who watch the signs of the times cannot
help feeling that the prophecy that the whole
Western continent is indeed the land of
Zion is being well-established in the minds
of those who have eyes to see and hearts to
understand. The progress of the Latter-day
work in far-off lands is strong testimony in
itself of the truth that pervades the hearts


of honest men and women in whatever land
or clime they dwell, or whatever their calling
or the customs of the country they inhabit.
They know for themselves when the Gospel
comes the spirit bears witness to the earnest
and upright in heart, and they are made to
rejoice in finding the plan of salvation as
laid down in the Scriptures, has been again
restored as promised by men of old.
	This last Conference should be a convinc-
ing proof to the young people in Zion of the
magnitude of the mighty work in which the
Saints are engaged.
