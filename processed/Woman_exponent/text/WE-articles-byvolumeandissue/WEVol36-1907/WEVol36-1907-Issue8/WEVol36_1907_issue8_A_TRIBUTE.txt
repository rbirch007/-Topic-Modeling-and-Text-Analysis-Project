A TRIBUTE.
To the memory of Isadora Jardiue Thompson,
	who departed this life February 9th, 1908.
	Deceased was 49 years 9 months and 19 days
old, was the daughter of James and Isabella Jar-
dine, West Weber, Utah. She was born April 21st,
1858, in Jackson, Illinois. She moved with her
parents to Wellsville, Utah, and from there to
West Weber, Utah. She was married to Hyrum
C. Thompson in July, 1873. Brother and Sister
Thompson and family moved to Wilford, Idaho,
in May, 1887, where they lived till St. Anthony
was organized. Sister Thompson was Secretary
for the North Wilford Sunday School for a num-
ber of years. Also Counselor to the Relief Society
for a long time. And was First Counselor to the
President of the Relief Society of St. Anthony at
the time of her death. She was the mother of 14
children, 12 of whom survive her. She leaves a
devoted husband, 9 sons and 3 daughters, 4 sisters
and two brothers to mourn her loss.
	The funeral services were held in the L. D. S.
Church February 13th; Bishop Jos. E. Fogg con-
ducting.
				RESOLUTIONS.
	Be it resolved that we the sisters of  the Woman’s
Relief Society of the St. Anthony Ward do most
deeply mourn the loss of so faithful a sister.
	And we do feel it is hard to become reconciled
to her death. But we are willing to bow in humble
submission to our Father’s will.
	Be it resolved that we extend our heart felt
sympathy to her husband, children, parents,
brothers and sisters, and ask God to bless them
and make them feel that he is their friend.
	Be it further resolved that these resolutions be
entered in the minutes of the Local Relief So-
ciety a copy given  to the bereaved family of the
deceased and a copy sent to the WOMAN’S EX-
PONENT.
			SARAH J. STIMPSON, President.
			LOVISA THORNTON, 2nd Counselor.
			SARAH R. TAYLOR, Secretary.
