	I am intensely interested in all accounts
of our Church meetings, conferences, etc.
and read with joy every item I can find in
Deseret News. A few copies of WOMAN’S
EXHONENT have come to me from time
to time. I am always interested in the
Relief Society reports and especially in
your words. I was so glad to see your
picture in May number and I feel to
want to send you my heartfelt wishes
and I am at last obeying the promptings
of my heart. I would love to meet a
woman who had known our beloved
Prophet Joseph. I hope you will send me
a few lines of your testimony as to His
divine mission from one who has known
him will be valued by me. The elders
who have labored in this city have been
so true and earnest and I count it a bles
sing to have met such pure, good, young
men. We missed Elder Cannon, he is
indeed a sweet Christian, so few respond
to the message. We number only six
in E. L. I long to be in Utah where I
could enjoy the services and engage in
work of all kinds and see the lovely
temple. In becoming a “Mormon” I know
I shall have some thing to bear. I already
feel a coolness of some friends toward me
and my sensitive nature shrinks from be-
ing hurt and pained, but the Father will
make me brave to defend our faith that is
so misunderstood. My mother and I long
to go to Utah and it is our daily hope and
prayer that the way may be opened for
us to go. My dear good father died eight
years ago, had he lived, he had resolved
to take us all back to Utah. Oh, how we
miss him, and mother has never been the
same since that terrible grief. Letters
from our sisters in Utah do cheer us. I
know you are a busy woman and your age
as well, but I hope and pray for a few
words from you as soon as you can. I
shall esteem it a favor. Hoping this finds
you in good health. Mother joins me in
love. Think of me as your sister in the
Gospel.			Susie M. Morgan.
