OCTOBER GENERAL CONFERENCE.
	The General Conference of the Church of
Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints this year
was one of the largest if not the very largest
in attendance of people ever held. It is not a
remarkable fact when we consider the growth
of the Church not merely at home by means
of family life, but the spread of the work in
other lands and in our own country, which
necssarily means numbers of Saints gath-
ering to Zion. The fact remains, how-
ever, that the crowds at the conference meet-
ings could not find standing room in the
large tabernacle; and other places also were
filled to overflowing on Sunday, October 6,
1907. Indeed, when all the halls available
near the Temple were filled, the streets were
crowded with people; therefore, it is safe to
say Zion is growing, but when we give the
true interpretation of Zion, “the pure in
heart,” will it apply then?
	The sermons delivered during the confer-
ence were of the very best, sound, practi-
cal advice, wise and judicious reasoning,
though plain and clear, easily comprehend-
ed by all who heard the inspired words of
the speakers. The sacred music was of the
highest order, uplifting the soul and mellow-
ing the feelings of the human heart. There
was really nothing to mar the harmony
that prevailed in the large and cruwded as-
semblies, and there was much food for
thought that will benefit all who heard the
words of eternal truth uttered by the in-
spired men of God who addressed the
people with such sublime eloquence as to
appeal to the higher nature of the vast mul-
titude of men and women.
