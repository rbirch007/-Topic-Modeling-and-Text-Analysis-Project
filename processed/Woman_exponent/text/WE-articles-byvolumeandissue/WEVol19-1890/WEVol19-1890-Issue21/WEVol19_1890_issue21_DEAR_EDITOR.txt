	DEAR EDITOR:
	Thinking that a few lines about our Pri-
mary would be interesting to you and the
readers of the EXPONENT, I will try to tell


you something about it. We have enrolled
fifty-five members and our meetings are well
attended.
	On December 30th, we held a Primary Fair
which was a credit to the children and all
concerned. A nice assortment of things were
on exhibition, as every child made something.
After the people had viewed the things on ex-
hibition they were entertained by listening to
songs and recitations from the children who
afterwards enjoyed themselves in dancing.
	We held our Stake Primary Conference
Feb. 23rd. And on account of the stormy
weather none but the Bluff children were
present to take part in the exercises, yet, a
lengthy programme, which was instructive as
well as entertaining, was well carried out. At
the suggestion of President Hammond we met
again in the afternoon and received words of
instruction and encouragement from President
Hammond and others of the brethren. After
closing remarks by Stake President Julia Butt,
conference was adjourned for three months.
A short intermission was taken then all met
together for dancing and refreshments.
	We do not have to make the complaint that
is so common about the non-attendance of boys.
Our boys attend as regular as the girls and
seem to be just as willing to take part in the
exercises.
	Our President, Sister Josephine Wood, is a 
good, kind woman, and is greatly interested in
the duty assigned her.
