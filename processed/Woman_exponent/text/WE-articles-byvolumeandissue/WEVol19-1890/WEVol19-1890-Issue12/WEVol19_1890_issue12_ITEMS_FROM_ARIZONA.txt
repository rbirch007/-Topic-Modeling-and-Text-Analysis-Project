ITEMS FROM ARIZONA.
EDITOR WOMAN’S EXPONENT:
	Dear Sister: Thinking perhaps a few lines
from this far off place would not be amiss, I will
endeavor to write you, knowing that most every
one has a peculiar dread of the name of “Arizona”
I should like to enlighten the readers of this
valuable paper a little concerning this isolated
country—but will just say that we have some good
saints in our settlement, I think as true as any in
Zion. We had a visit from Sister Willmirth East
the president of the Relief Society of the St.
Joseph Stake. She came here in Oct., with 
Patriarch P. C. Merrill and wife who had been on
the Gila to attend quarterly conference; they
arrived home on the fiftieth anniversary of their
wedding and their relatives and friends were
pleased to see them home and wish them "many
happy returns of the day."  Sister East stayed
about a fortnight and spent a very enjoyable time
visiting with the sisters and giving advice when
needed. We all feel better in spirits since her
visit and hope she will soon come again, she does
all in her power to do good and help the needy,


and we all desire that she may live long and still
continue in her labors. We had a visit from
 the stake presidency on the 8th of Nov., they
gave good counsel and we are striving to treasure
it up, and live better lives in the future than we
have in the past.
