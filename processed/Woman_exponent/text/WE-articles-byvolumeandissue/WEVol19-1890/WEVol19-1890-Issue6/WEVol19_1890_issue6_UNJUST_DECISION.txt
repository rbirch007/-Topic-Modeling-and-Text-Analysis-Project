UNJUST DECISION.
	The decision recently rendered in regard to the
rights of children born in Polygamy to any in-
heritance in the father’s estate was to say the
least very surprising. “Polygamous children can-
not inherit,” so says Judge Henderson and Judge
Zane concurs.
	Now we ask why? Because you are such a
just monogomist and say they cannot, must it be
so? Have you or has any one a right to make
laws to reach back half a century, and power to
make a law where there was none to bear on all
cases not under your jurisdiction? I have been
reading a decision rendered by this high tribunal
on the same case.
	“That the law of 1862 debarred all children, born
after this law was passed, from inheriting their
father’s estate.” How about the children born be-
fore there was any law made against their inher-
iting, or before there was any law against polyg-
amy. The decision of this Judge is wonderful in-
deed, to extend away back to the time when
there was no law at all upon the subject.
