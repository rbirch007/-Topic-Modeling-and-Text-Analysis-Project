THE MYSTIC BED.

The chamber is low and dark and deep,
here many a one goeth down to sleep;
And the pilot who openeth the entrance door,
Closeth it never to open more;
And the occupant drest in a robe of white,
Looketh ne'er again on themoming light.

The bed is so narrow and hard and cold,
The pillow is covered with damp and mold;
And frozen mist in the clear starlight,
Are the sheets so thin and strangely white,
 And the counterpane rests, with a heavy fold,
On the sleeper there, so still and cold.

No window to let in the sweet moonlight,
To shine on the face so calm and white,
Or nestle like pearls, in the golden hair,
Where lieth a rose all withered there.
Not even the fragrant breath of the flowers
Can enter therein on the morning hours.

And down upon that mysterious bed,
Sometime I shall lay my weary head.
Whose gentle hand in the bright summer hours,
Will gather for me sweet beautiful flowers?
What loving friend will hallow my rest,
And place the white rose upon my breast?
