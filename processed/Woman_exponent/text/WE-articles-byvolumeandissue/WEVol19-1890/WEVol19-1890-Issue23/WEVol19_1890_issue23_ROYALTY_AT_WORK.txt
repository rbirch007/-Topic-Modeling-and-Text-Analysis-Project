ROYALTY AT WORK.

	The daughters of the Princess of Wales,
says Lady Elizabeth Hilary in The Ladies'
Home Journal, are sensibly educated. They
know how to sew so well that they can make
their own gowns, and their knowledge of every
art taught them is thorough. They can go
into the kitchen and cook—cook well; they
understand the art of breadmaking, and if
they were ever thrown upon their own re-
sources would be able to take care of them-
selves. And this has been done not only as an
example to other mothers in the kingdom, but
because her Royal Highness thought it right
for her daughters. I wonder how many of the
daughters of American gentlewomen could
make butter, sew, paint, are good musicians,
have a knowledge of sculpture and can read
and speak three or four languages? And yet
this is true of the daughters of the Princess of
Wales, who was herself, while thoroughly edu-
cated, taught all the industries that would be
a part of the knowledge of a daughter of ordi-
nary gentlefolk. Sweet-faced, healthy-looking
girls, they are always.
