INVENTED BY WOMEN.
Without Mheural Nisa the world would
never have had Cashmere shawls.
 Yao, the wife of the fourth Emperor of
China, bears the honor of having invented spin-
ning.
	Harriet Hosmer first introduced to the
world the permanent magnet as a motive
power.	
Miss Betsey Metcalf made, in 1798, the
first straw hat ever manufactured in this
country.		
The invention of the first submarine teles-
cope was made by Sarah B. Mather, of New
York.	
The exquisite perfumery called “Atyr of
Roses” was the product of Mheural Nisa’s in-
ventive genius.
	A patent mower and reaper was invented by
Elizabeth Smith, an enterprising woman of
New Jersey.
	Si-Lang-Chi is thought by the Chineese to
have discovered silk, and is called: “The god-
dess of silk-worms.”
Pamphile was a woman of Cos and the in-
ventress of silken gauze, her fabric being
known as “woven wind.”—Chicago News.
