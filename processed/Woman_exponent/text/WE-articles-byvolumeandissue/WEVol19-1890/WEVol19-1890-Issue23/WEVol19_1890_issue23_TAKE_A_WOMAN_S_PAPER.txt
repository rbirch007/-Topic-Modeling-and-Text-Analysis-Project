TAKE A WOMAN'S PAPER.

	It will be found by those who care to in-
vestigate, that for men there are upwards
of 17,000 papers issued, while for women there
are not more than fifty. Yet the excuse most
frequently given by women, when asked to
subscribe to a woman's paper is, "My husband
takes so many papers that I do not feel that I
can afford to take this one." Would it not be
better for the husband to do with one paper
less, and let the wife have a chance to learn
what women are doing in the world?" If he
requires as a man to know what men are do-
ing, does not she, as a woman, need like infor-
mation concerning the work accomplished by
her sex.—Woman's Chronicle.
