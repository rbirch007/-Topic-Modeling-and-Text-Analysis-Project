THE SINGLE SIN.
 
He walked for years in ways of righteousness
	Good deeds unnumbered dropping from his hand;
Then, lo! a single sin he must confess,
	That bruited was far up and down the land;
And all the people drew a solemn face,
	And all called him trickster; whispered his disgrace,

'Twill be a wondrous world, to heaven full nigh,
	If ever men whose common life is sin,
Shall do one act of good whereat a cry
	Of saint shall fill the air with ready din;
The people quick forgetting all the past,
	And glad to see God's image show at last.
