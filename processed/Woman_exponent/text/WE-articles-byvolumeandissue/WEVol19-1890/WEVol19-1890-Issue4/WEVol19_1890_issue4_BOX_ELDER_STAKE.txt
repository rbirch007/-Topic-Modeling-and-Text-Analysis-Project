BOX ELDER STAKE.

	The quarterly conference of the Relief
Society, Box Elder Stake, convened on the
10th of June, in the Third Ward Meeting
House, Brigham City.
	Meeting was called to order by counselor 
Susannah P. Booth.
	Singing: "Once more we come before our 
God."
	Prayer by Bishop Tingey.
	Singing:  "Know this that every man is 
free."
	Counselor Booth was the first speaker. “I
am thankful I am here this morning, but it is
with peculiar feelings that I arise."  Spoke
feelingly about our departed president, also of
her own sister who died suddenly.  Said: we
should always be prepared to leave this exis-
tence, I trust I may not yet be called, I am
not prepared, I have a desire to fill my mis-
sion.  The Lord has power to do all things,
let us pray that He will not place more on our
shoulders, than He will give us strength to
bear.  Let us pray for the youth of Zion, I
want to do a mother's part, whether my chil-
dren or others do theirs or not.
	Mrs. Hubbard, Pres. of Willard:  "I am
pleased to be here, but we all feel to mourn,
still our loss is sister Harriet's gain, let us live
so we may meet those who have gone before
us.  We have great reason to rejoice, that we
can meet in peace.  We must be faithful, we
must be true, and love one another."
	Mrs. Jane Perry, Pres. Three Mile Creek:
It is a year to-day, since I had the privelege
to meet with you, and perhaps this is the last
time.  How thankful we should be that the
Lord has permitted us to receive this Gospel, it
is more than my meat and my bread, think of
the many in the world longing to come to
Zion.  We are drifting too much apart, when
we leave this life we will take nothing with us
but our knowlege.  Sister Hariet has laid her
work down after having performed it well,
she has lived up to the principles of the Gos-
pel; she is rejoicing now.  This is the work of
God.
