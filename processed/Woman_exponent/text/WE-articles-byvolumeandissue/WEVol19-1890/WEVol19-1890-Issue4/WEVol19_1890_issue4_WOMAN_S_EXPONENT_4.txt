WOMAN'S EXPONENT.


Mrs. Kimball then announced the close of
the exercises, and that refreshments were next
in order also dancing; the band playing enliven-
ing strains of music. The tables were hand-
somely decorated with flowers, the refreshment 
committee deserve the utmost praise for the
effective work done.  Dancing and animated
conversation was kept up until midnight, when
the guests dispersed.
	The lawn fete was certainly a brilliant 
affair, and the several committees worked with
a will to ensure its perfect success. They
realize most fully the truth of the saying,
"there is no excellence without labor."


We call the attention of our readers to the
article, entitled Woman's Calling, hoping to hear
from other ladies on the subject.
