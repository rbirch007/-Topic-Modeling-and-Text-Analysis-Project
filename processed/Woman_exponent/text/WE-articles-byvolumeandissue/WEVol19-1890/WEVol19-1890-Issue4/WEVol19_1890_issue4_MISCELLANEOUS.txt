MISCELLANEOUS.
	Forbearance is attended with profit.
	To persecute the unfortunate is like throw-
ing stones on one fallen into a well.
	You can outlive a slander in half the time
you can out argue it.—Uncle Esek.
	One ray of moral and religious truth is
worth all the wisdom of the schools.  One
lesson from Christ will carry you higher than
years of study under those who are too en-
lightened to follow this celestial guide.—
Channing.
	As far as possible dwell on the good side of
human things.  There are family boards
where a constant process of depreciating, as-
signing motives, and cutting up character goes
forward.  They are not happy places.  One
who is healthy does not wish to dine at a
dissecting table.  There is evil enough in  man
God knows!  But it is not the mission of every
young man and woman to detail and report 
it all.  Keep the atmosphere as pure as pos-
sible, and fragrant with gentleness and char-
ity.—Dr. John Hall.
