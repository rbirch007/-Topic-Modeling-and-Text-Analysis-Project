NOTES AND NEWS.
	The House agreed July 8th, to the Senate
amendment to the bill for the admission of
Wyoming, and the President signed it July
10th, delaying his signature until half past
five, to give Delegate Carey opportunity to
finish up pending business, as the signature of
the President reduces him to the ranks, and
leaves Wyoming without representation.—Ex.
	The Idaho bill passed the Senate and re-
ceived the signature of the President July 3rd.
The President consulted Delegate Dubois
whether he would rather have Idaho come into
the Union on the Fourth, or have its star
added to the National flag on that day, as the
law provides that the star of a new State is
added on the Fourth of July succeeding its ad-
mission.—Ex.
	Wednesday the 23rd of this month is the
day when Wyoming celebrates its statehood.
The magnificent flag to be presented to the
State by the women as a token of their appre-
ciation of their citizenship is now having the
forty-fourth star added to it. The hearts of all
who love fredom, will send an electric current
of sympathy and congratulations to this
youngest and fairest daughter of the Union.
—Ex.
