A RESOLUTION.

	At the Annual Convention of the National
Woman's Christian Temperance Union held in
Atlanta, Ga., in November, the resolution follow-
ing was offered by the newly elected Superin-
tendent of the Department of Mercy, Mrs. Mary
F. Lovell of Bryn Mawr, Pa., and adopted:
	Whereas we believe vivisection to be cruel. un-
necessary, and demoralizing, tending to a de-
terioration of the human soul utterly out of pro-
portion to any known benefit to the human body
gained thereby, the practice of it thus reversing
the teachings of Christianity,
	Resolved, That we will do all in our power to
develop sentiment against it, and because we do
not believe in half measures, nor in licensing any
evil, we will do this with a view to securing legis-
lation for its total prohibition.
