TRUST AND DUTY.
Oh wrestling heart when sick and weary,
	Bowed beneath life's weight of woe;
In meekness thou hast learned thy duty,
	And chose to walk the narrow way.

But yet how prone to shrink from duty.
	E’en when the voice of Father calls,
In His love and tender mercy,
	To prove and purify thy soul.

He chooses those who're true and willing, 
	To keep the strait aud narrow way,
Where gentle dews from heaven are falling,
	Lighting sorrow's cloudiest day.

The back He fits to bear its burden
	The wounds He heals, the heart makes glad,
Unfathomed love is ever shedding,
	Peace on those who trust in God.

Its been decreed that all that’s earthly,
	Must meet with change and pass away;
Through toil and pain that we’re made holy,
	To gain a crown of endless day.

All such are safe whate'er betide them,
	Sheltered in the Savior’s flock,
'Neath that fount whence peace is flowing
	Cleaving close the Smitten Rock.
