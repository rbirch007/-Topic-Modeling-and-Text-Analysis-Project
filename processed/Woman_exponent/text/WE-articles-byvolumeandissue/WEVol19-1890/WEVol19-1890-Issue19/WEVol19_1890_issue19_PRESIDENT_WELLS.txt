PRESIDENT WELLS.

A brave true heart, that wore no worldly mask,
	Nor ever shrank from any proving test,
A strong, kind hand, ne'er known to shirk its task.
	Now calmly rest.

Rest from their labors, but the light they shed,
	Shines now from earth to heaven,and will not dim;
The works of this, our Friend, the blessed dead;
	Do follow him.

Ye Saints of God, whose love for him was great,
	Mourn for your loss, yet deem not this complete;
Bring high resolves his work to emulate,
	As offerings mete.

Remember what sweet charity he's shown,
	Forgetting self in care for others' need;
And try to make such Christ-like love your own,
	In word and deed.

Eternal One, this Friend hath served Thee well;
	Thy mercies to his family still increase;
For love like his, Thine only can excel,
	Grant them its peace.

From first to last, e'en that lone, absent one,
	The favored child of his declining years,
Help them to truly say, "Thy will be done,"
	Amid their tears.

And help Thy Saints, oh,Lord, who labor still,
	Thy laws t' uphold, Thy name to glorify;
Like this great man, life's duties to fulfill,
	Like him to die.
