EDITORIAL NOTES.

	The Semi-Annual Conference of the Relief
Society was held in the Assembly Hall in Salt
Lake City, Oct. 7 1890. Mrs. Zina D. H. Young
presiding. There was a forenoon and afternoon
session, and a large congregation almost entirely
women assembled. Reports were heard from the
sisters who preside over the Relief Society in the
several Stakes of Zion, and the meeting was also
addressed by President Zina D. H. Young who
has only recently returned from a visit to Canada
also her Counselor Bathsheba W. Smith, the
Secretary Sarah M. Kimball, and Pres. M. Isa-
bella Horne, Minutes of the Conference will be
published in our next issue.


The first Annual Conference of the Primary
Associations was held in the Assembly Hall in
this City Sunday evening Oct. 5, 1890 commenc-
ing at 7.30. Mrs. Louie B. Felt presiding. Her
Counselors Lillie T. Freeze and Clara C. Cannon
were both present. The house was crowded to
overflowing with brethren and sisters. The
teaching was such as would instruct both old and
young. The speakers were Pres. Louie B. Felt,
Coun. Lillie T. Freeze, Pres. Z. D. H. Young,
Sister E. B. Wells. Report from Cache Stake
P. A. by Sister Jane E. Molen and from Utah
Stake P. A. Sister Sarah E. Cluff. Sister Cornelia
H. Clayton offered her resignation as Secretary
which was accepted, and Miss May Anderson
chosen to fill the vacancy. The meeting was an
interesting one throughout, and must be a source
of great satisfaction to those who are laboring so
diligently for the children of Zion to feel that the
work is gaining ground.
