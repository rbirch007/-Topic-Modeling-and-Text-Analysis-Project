And as he sang, and looked for leaves the while
His eyes sought mine with arch and tender smile.
What joy can e'er again my heart beguile—

Since Death has done to me this cruel wrong !
Has hushed the beating of a heart so strong,
And silenced evermore the voice of song ?

O precious leaves ! “For love—and thee and me?"
Amid the pages old they still may be,
But seeing them, again I seem to see

The loving face; the far off day seems near;
The voice I loved in song again is clear,
And once again those old-time words I hear;
