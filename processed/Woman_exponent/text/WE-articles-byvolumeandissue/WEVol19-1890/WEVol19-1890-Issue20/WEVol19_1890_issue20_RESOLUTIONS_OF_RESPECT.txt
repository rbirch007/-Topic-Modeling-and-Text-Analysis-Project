RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.
Relief Society Twenty-first Ward.
	Whereas, in His divine wisdom, it hath
pleased Almighty God to take out of her long
affliction our dear Sister, Counselor H. J.
Allen, whose unwearied devotion to the duties
of her office, whose self-sacrifice and prompti-
tude under all circumstances command our
admiration and esteem. Be it therefore
	Resolved—That while we feel keenly the
loss of our friend and fellow-laborer, we
humbly acknowledge that He who doeth all
things well hath done this also, and without
murmur we bow to His righteous decree,
praying Him to give us His grace, that we,
like her, may remain steadfast unto the end,
and having finished our work may enter into
the rest prepared for the faithful; and be it
further
	Resolved—That, we tender our most pro-
found and heartfelt sympathy to the bereaved
husband, our beloved Bishop, and our sincere
condolence to the family.
	Also that a copy of these resolutions, with
the foregoing memoir, be presented to them,
and published in the WOMAN'S EXPONENT,
and the resolutions be entered upon the
secretary's book.
	Signed in behalf  of the Officers and
Members of the Society March 1891.
