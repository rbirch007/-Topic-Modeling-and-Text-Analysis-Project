WOMAN'S EXPONENT


Mas. Elizabeth Cady Stanton writes from
abroad that she does not expect to return to
this country before next June.

	The Youth’s Companion will give, during
the coming year, four practical, helpful articles
on what a girl of sixteen years can do when
thrown upon her own resources, by Mrs. Mary
A. Livermore, “Jenny June,” “Marion
Harland,” and Mrs Amelia E. Barr.

	The annual fair and flower sale of the
Beneficent Society of the New England
Conservatory of Music was held Nov. 6, in the
conservatory parlors. In the evening a fine
concert was given in Sleeper Hall. This
society loans to needy but talented pupils funds
with which to pursue their musical education.
Contributions are solicited from friends and
members of the society.
