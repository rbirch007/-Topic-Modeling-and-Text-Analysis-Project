U. W. P. C.

	THE U. W. P. C. held its regular
meeting April 30, 1897, at the office of the
WOMAN'S EXPONENT. President Fox
called the meeting to order at 8 p. m.


After prayer by Mrs. Lydia Alder, the roll
was called members responding with orig-
inal sentiments. Minutes of last meeting
were read and approved.
	Under the head of business was an in-
formal discussion in regard to change of
time of annual meeting of Federation of
clubs. At the request of the Federation
Entertainment committee the names of
those who will assist in entertaining Feder-
ation guests were given. Mrs. Dickinson
being unable to act as delegate from the
Press Club, Dr. Curtis was appointed in
her place. Resignation of Miss S. L.
Munroe as a member of the club was read
and accepted.
	Program of the evening:—“Life of Lin-
coln,” Mrs. Phebe C. Young. ‘‘Social
Life in Washington,” Mrs. Brown.
	The next meeting of the club will be
May 29, 8 p. m. at usual place. The war
between Greece and Turkey will be the
topic for discussion. Sentiments from
N. P. Willis.
