MARY WAREHAM JACKSON.

	DIED, at her home in American Fork, Oct. 7,
1897, our beloved sister, Mary Wareham Jackson.
Sister Wareham was born Nov. 2, 1825 in York-
shire, England. Embrace the Gospel in Shef-
field, and emigrated to Utah in 1855 with her
husband, George Wareham. She has been a
teacher in the Relief Society from its first organ-
ization and filled her office with credit to herself
and satisfaction to her coworkers. In connection
with her husba d she entered into the principle
of Celestial marriage, and after his death was
married again to Brother W. W. Jackson, by
whom she had two sons, one of whom survives
her, also four grandchildren.
	She was a good neighbor, warm-hearted and
liberal, always on hand to assist a friend or help
the poor and needy. Her departure was sudden.


though having been sick for some time no one
seemed to think the end was so near, as her
faith in the priesthood was strong and she always
revived after the administration of the Elders.
Her testimony was unwavering, and she died as
she had lived a faithful Latter-day Saint, and in
the sure hope or a glorious resurrection.
				MARY S. HINDLEY,
				JEMIMA DURRANT,
				EMMA FEATHERSTONE,
							Committee.
	In behalf of the Relief Society.
