EDITORIAL NOTES.

	MRS. CHARLES HENROTIN of Chicago, 
President of the Woman’s Clubs. is ex-
pected to attend the meeting of the State
Federation of Clubs, and will probably
make an address on Wednesday evening
May 26,—Mrs. Platt, Mrs. Thather and
Mrs. Gale of Denver will also be present and
they also will address the Federation.
	THE Annual Convention of the State
Federation of clubs will be held May 26
and 27 in the Congregational Church, corner
1st South and 4th East in this city, Mrs.
Martha B. Jennings President. Meetings
will commence 10 a. m. and 2 p. m. and
on Thursday evening, May 27, at 8 o’clock
there will be a reception at the residencee of
Mrs. W. A. Nelden, 1145 E. First South.
Papers by women of literary culture will be
given and questions of importance to club
women as well as to the public will be dis-
cussed. It is hopefully expected that it
will be interesting and instructing in the
best sense of the word.
	THE Hall of Relics to be erected immedi-
ately on the dear old Council House corner
will be a great improvement to the appear-
ance of the locality. The place is con-
spicuous and will give a good opportunity
for display. The plans were drawn by
Dallas and Hedges and the building will
be a reproduction of the famous temple of
Zenus at Olympia, and if the plan is car-
ried out will be “a thing of beauty” as
the Corinthian columns will give an ap-
pearance of strength and the Greek style
in building is the admiration of all lovers
of art in architecture. The outside is to be
dazzlingly white, while the inside will be
decorated in the high colors of the Jubilee.
Then the pedestal in front of the building
will be a reproduction of Dallin’s "Signal
of Peace,” the same as that which appear-
ed at the World’s Fair in Chicago; this is
appropriate too as Dallin was Utah born
and of course the State may claim some
honor of his achievements.
