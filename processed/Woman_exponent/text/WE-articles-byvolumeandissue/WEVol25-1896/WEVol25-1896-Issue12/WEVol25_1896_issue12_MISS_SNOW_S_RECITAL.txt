MISS SNOW’S RECITAL.

	THE Hotel Templeton parlors were
crowded on Monday, November 23 ult, to
hear Miss Snow’s Recital. The audience
was highly appreciative. Miss Snow be-
gan with the early Christian Art as seen in
the Catacombs in Rome. The magnificent
Mosaic decorations in Venice; the Byzan-
tine school of art in Constantinople, closing
with Giovanni Cimoline, the Father of mod-
ern painting.
	Pictures were shown illustrating the dif-
ferent subjects. Following the lecture was
a talk on the Delsarte System of Expression,
Harmonic Gymnastics, gracefully illus-
trated. Excellent music was rendered by
Miss Oliver and Miss Della Daynes.
	Miss Snow is now giving a series of
Lecture Recitals in the Hotel Templeton
parlors every Wednesday afternoon at 3.30 p.
m., subjects, Art and Artists, illustrated by
pictures of great Masters. Each lecture is
followed by music and Delsarte Society
Gymnastics. 
	After the holidays Miss Snow will
begin another series of Lectures. For fur-
ther information see Miss Snow at her stu-
dio, Brooks Arcade, opposite Hotel Knuts-
ford.
