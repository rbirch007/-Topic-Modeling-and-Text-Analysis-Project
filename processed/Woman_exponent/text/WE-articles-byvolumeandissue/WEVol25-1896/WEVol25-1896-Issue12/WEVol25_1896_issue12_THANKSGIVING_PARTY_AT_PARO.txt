THANKSGIVING PARTY AT PARO-
WAN.

	WE are justly proud of our R. S. organ-
ization; a more earnest, united band of
sisters would be hard to find; their efforts
on Thanksgiving day only served to
strengthen our good opinion of them.
	Our society gave what is known to us by
the homely name of “a wood hauling
party,” the brethren (especially the young
portion) hauling wood for a Thanksgiving
dinner and dance ticket. Thiry-three loads
of excellent fuel was received and distribut-
ed amongst the needy, and many hearts
were thereby made glad and more thankful
than ever.
	Beside the wood haulers and ladies, all
over sixty-five were invited. Our tables
were arranged in our new Social hall, (plates
laid for one hundred and sixty at each
sitting,) and were filled three times. One
feature that added to the day’s success our
Bishop assisted from the beginning with a
will, both by work and financial aid; one
thing in particular, he personally paid for
all the turkeys for our feast, which was one
indeed, ’twas utterly surprising where so
many good things came from after such a
trying summer, not only these present
shared that bountiful repast but the sick
and the motherless children were richly
remembered.
	We had large committees of both breth-
ren and sisters who worked hard for three
days, yet all feel fully compensated when-
ever they think of the great amount of
good wrought by their labors.
	Our association is energetic and faithful,
we have our meetings regular and well
attended, our membership is one hundred
and twenty-five. We have been wondering
if there would be any change in the day of
our testimony meeting, as the general fast
day is on Sunday, would it not be allowable
for us to hold ours some evening? It would
be more convenient for every mother whose
children are in school, and more of the
young sisters would be apt to join us. God
bless the beautiful cause.
			{S. A. ADAMS, Pres.
