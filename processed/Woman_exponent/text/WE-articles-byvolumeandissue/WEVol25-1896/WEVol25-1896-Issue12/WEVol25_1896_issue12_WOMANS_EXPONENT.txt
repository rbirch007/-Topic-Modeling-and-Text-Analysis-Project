WOMANS EXPONENT


A FLAG reception was held at the Parker
House, Boston, the afternoon before the
election, under the auspices of the Ameri-
can Woman’s Sound Money League. Mrs.
Marion McBride presided and read a report
from thirty-one states in which women
have been working for the cause. Mrs. J.
Sewall Read spoke for the Woman’s Relief
Corps. Mrs. Julia Ward Howe also spoke
for the women and recited her poem on the
flag written during the war. Prof. Augus-
tus Bedford delivered a lecture on the de-
velopment of the American flag, and
llustrated it with miniature flags. Among
the other speakers were Rev. E. A. Horton
and Rev. A. A. Berl.
