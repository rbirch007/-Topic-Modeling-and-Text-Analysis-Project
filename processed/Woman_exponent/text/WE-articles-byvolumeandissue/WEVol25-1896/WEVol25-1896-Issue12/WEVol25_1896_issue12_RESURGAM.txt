RESURGAM.*
	I SHED not a tear
	O’er the dying old year!
I mourn not to see him depart—
	He has answered an end
 	Been my Mentor and friend,
And his Cenotaph hangs in my heart.
	I calmly look on
	As the requiem song,
Floats over my memory’s ear!
	Let the old year depart
	No regret’s in my heart,
And lachrymal cells hold no tear!
	My heart is at rest.
	And I fear not the test,
With which Time tries the children of Earth
	My all’s in the bank 
	Where the Saints ever rank,
And I fear not the desert or dearth.
	There is One up above,
	Whose Spirit is Love!
And through life I’ve experienced His care
	And a sure gurantee
	Shall the past be to me,
That of good things I’ll still have my share.
	But should the good things
	Be what poverty brings!—
May hope trim the lamp in my heart;
	For ’tis said those He loves,
	He most certainly proves,
To see if from Him they’ll depart. 
	Then let the year pass
	With his scythe and his glass,
Yet to Time—dear old Time, I owe much,
	He has ever been kind
	To my heart and my mind,
And has gently on each laid his touch.
	But the time has now come 
	For the year to go home,
And calmly we’ll lay him away—
	And hail the new year,
	With a welcoming cheer, 
And start for the years’ race today!
					HANNAH T. KING.
S. L. C., Dec. 31, 1876.
*Unpublished, found among some old papers
but remember its having been left over at the time
and think it will recall to mind one of our dear
departed sisters whose name often appeared in
the EXPONENT.
