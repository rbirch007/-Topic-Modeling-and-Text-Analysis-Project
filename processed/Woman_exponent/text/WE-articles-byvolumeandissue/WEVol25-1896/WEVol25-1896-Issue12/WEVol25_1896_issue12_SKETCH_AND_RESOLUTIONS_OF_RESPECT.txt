SKETCH AND RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.
	[To the memory of Sister Martha Jasper who
	departed this life Sep. 8, 1896.]
	DECEASED was born Feb. 14, 1821 in Lambeter,
Breckenshire, England. She embraced the
Gospel between forty and fifty years ago,
emigrated to Utah twenty-three years ago last
July and settled on a ranch near Utah Lake where
she resided until four years ago last May, her
husband having died a few months previous, she
was left a lone widow. Her friends persuaded
her to sell the ranch and move into Goshen
where she has resided with Mrs. Elizabeth Davis
until her demise.
	She has been a very faithful and energetic
teacher and member of our Relief Society for the
last four years, and a liberal donater. She was
very zealous in working and having work per-
formed for her progenitors in the Manti Temple,
and she spent a great deal of means in that direc-
tion, and if she had made a proper will the princi-
pal part of her property would have gone to the
Manti Temple.
	WHEREAS, It has pleased our Heavenly Father
to take from our midst our sister and co worker,
we realize our loss is her gain. [-]herefore be it,
	RESOLVED, That we emulate her energy in the
performance of her duty as a teacher, and her
zeal in laboring for her dead, for her whole soul
was in the work of God. Therefore be it,
	RESOLVED, That we record a copy of th
resolutions upon our records and send one to
WOMAN'S EXPONENT for publication
						ELLISON GOURLEY,
						AMELIA GOURLEY, 
						LUCY TAYLOR,
								Committee
