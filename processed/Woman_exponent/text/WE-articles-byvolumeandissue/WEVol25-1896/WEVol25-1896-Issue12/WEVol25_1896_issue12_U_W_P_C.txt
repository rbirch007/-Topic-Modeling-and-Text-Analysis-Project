U. W. P. C.

	NOV. 30, 1896 the U. W. P. C. was call-
ed to order at 8.30 p m., E. B. Wells in
the chair. The first business of the eve-
ning was the installation of the new officers;
before taking her seat as President, Mrs.
Fox made a motion, that a vote of thanks
be extended to Mrs. Wells for her efficient
work as president; the motion accepted
unanimously, and Mrs. Wells was made
Past President by society’s vote. Mrs. Fox
then accepted the presidential chair for the
coming year. After a short address by the
new President, the programme for the eve-
ning was given. A poem “Thanksgiving
Thoughts” by Dr. Shipp. A paper,
“Slavery as a National Problem” by Mrs.
Arthur Brown. Reading from the col-
lection of Poems, “ Shadows and Whisper-
ings” by Mrs. E. B. Wells. Paper
“Titian” by Mrs. Syntitha Dickinson.
Reading “Symmetrical Womanhood”
by Mrs Rachel Miller. A poem Pro
tanto” by Dr. Curtis. After the comple-
tion of the programme the motion was made
and carried that Mrs. McVicker be retained
as critic. Another was also carried that
Mrs. May R. Taylor be admitted as a mem-
ber of the club. 
   Time for the meeting of the club was
changed to 7.30 p. m. Adjournment was
in order.
