WEDDING BELLS.

[WRITTEN in honor of the sixtieth anniversary
of the marriage of Brother and Sister Tyler.]

RING out! Ye joyous wedding bells
Let your sweet music fill the air.
A wedding festive is at hand
So extol it everywhere.
Let your notes be clear as crystal
Laden with joy, peace and love,
With heart-felt thanks and gratitude,
To the great God above.

For three score years ago today
You sent forth a happy peal
While modest maid and buoyant youth
Did at the sacred altar kneel.
’Twas then your notes were filled with hope
Thought was found there blending,
But now life’s fiercest storms are past,
And in paths of truth they still are wending.

Tho’ sorrow oft has filled their hearts,
And bitter grief has left its traces,
The sun’s shone brightly thro’ the clouds
And still illuminates those faces.
Blessed are you, dear aged friends
Who launched your bark on life’s stormy sea
So many, many years ago
To be preserved this day to see

With fond hearts still aglow with love,
Both for each other and the truth,
And the vows at the altar given,
So long ago in your happy youth;
So now ring out! Ye merry bells!
Let not one shadow your tones bedim,
But let your rich notes gladly tell
Of the richer qualities within.

For our Patriarch and his winsome bride
Who still are working hand in hand
In the great cause of righteousness
Among Zion’s most faithful, stand.
Ring out, let the angels catch the strain
And carry it heavenward I pray,
And there in endless peace and joy
May they sing the eternal wedding day.
