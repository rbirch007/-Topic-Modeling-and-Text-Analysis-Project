TEASDALE, WAYNE CO.
				October 6, 1896.
Dear Sister Wells:
	I FORWARD our Conference Report; we
just held one afternoon session of Stake
Conference. My counselors could not
attend, Sister Forsyth had sore throat in
her family and three of her children died;
it was the first time she had failed to attend
an appointment. She is my second coun-
selor and has been a great help to me in
my labors. We enjoyed an excellent spirit
in our conference though short, much was
spoken for our general good and received in
an appreciative manner. We had a number
of young ladies join the Relief Society.
The Stake President of the Young Ladies
Mutual Improvement Association advised
the young sisters to join and attend the
monthly testimony meetings, and I am
pleased to note they heeded her counsel;
there are three associations where all have
joined the Relief Society. I enclose the
names of the presidents and secretaries as
desired. We are always pleased to receive
the WOMAN’S EXPONENT and profit by its
hints and suggestions on many subjects
pertaining to women. With kind love and
wishing you health and success in your
many labors.
			Yours respectfully
