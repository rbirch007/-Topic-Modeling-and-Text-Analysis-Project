U. W. P. C.

	SEP. 30, 1896, the Woman’s Press Club
met at the office of the WOMAN'S EXPO-
NENT. Because of the absence of Presi-
dent Wells, Dr. R. B. Pratt presided. The
opening prayer was offered by Phebe C.
Young. Shakespearean Sentiments were
given in answer to roll call. The minutes
were read and with one correction were ap-
proved. Mrs. Phebe C. Young should be
mentioned as having read, at the last meet-
ing, “Margaret Queen of Louis of
France.” Miscellaneous business being
next in order Mrs. Brown moved, and it
was carried, that the treasurer be empower-
ed to buy the necessary books and paper
for the club.
	Dr. Curtis gave an interesting account of
her summer vacation. 
	She was glad to return to our ideal city
after her peaceful respite during the heated
term. At the present time there are sani-
tariums where the panacea for all ills is
found in absolute rest, must not read or write
and as far as possible abstain from thinking.


She had found in the wild woods and peace-
ful rivers of Washington such an ideal sani-
tarium. She had left all care, worry and
political issues at home and had returned a
weightier woman—at least physically.
Rest is fine, and so is work grand. She
felt she had returned not as a stranger but
as one of the press household.
	Mrs. Mary Lambert followed with a
beautiful poem on the Island of Hawaii,
after which the members all joined in an
animated discussion of the paper.
	Mrs. Lambert by request spoke in the
musical language of the Hawaiian.
	After a lengthy and interesting discussion
of the religious, social and moral status of
the Hawaiians and society in general, the
meeting adjourned for one month.
	ELLIS R. SHIPP, Sec. pro tem.
