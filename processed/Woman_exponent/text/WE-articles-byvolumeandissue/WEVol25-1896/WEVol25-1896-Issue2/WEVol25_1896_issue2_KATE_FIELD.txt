KATE FIELD

	THE tidings of the demise of Miss Field
in far off Honolulu, seemed very sad when 
one remembers her brilliant career before
the public for so many years, as well as
her extensive acquaintance here and
abroad. To die alone in a foreign land,
without a loving friend near to say one
tender word, or drop one tear upon the
couch of suffering is saddening. To be
alone in the world, apart from family ties,
seems to us an affliction. Miss Field went
to Honolulu to regain her health, she suf-
fered from overwork and depression,
brought about by financial embarrassments. 
She was an accomplished journalist, and
her paper “Kate Field's Washington” was
a reflex or a mirror of her pronounced
opinions upon all subjects.
	“Brave minds, how e’er at war are secret friends, 
Their generous discord with the battle ends; 
In peace they wonder whence dissension rose,
And ask how souls so like could e’er be foes."
