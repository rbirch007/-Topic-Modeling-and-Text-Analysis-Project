WOMAN S EXPONENT


MINUTES of meeting held in WOMAN'S
EXPONENT Office, May 19, 1896, Mrs.
Kate Groesbeck in the chair. Sang, “Help
the working women.” Prayer by Mrs.
Oswald. Sang’ “Columbia’s daughters.”
Minutes of last meeting read and approved;
after which the secretary read a lecture on
townships from Kirke’s Civil Government.
The president made some remark on Civil
Government and spoke about holding parlor
meetings, thought that the ladies could ex-
press their views better in an informal meet-
ing than they could in the regular meeting.
Mrs. J. Fewson Smith made some remarks
on common law. Mrs. Grames read from
Roberts rules of order. C. Newman
