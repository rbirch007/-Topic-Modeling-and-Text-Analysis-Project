OBITUARIES.

	DIED April 14, 1896, in Castle Dale, Emery Co.
Utah, at her residence, Sister Annette Johnson
Larson at the age of fifty four years, nine months
and one day.
	Deceased was born in Sweden on the thirteenth
day of July, 1841. Received the gospel at the
age of fifteen and emigrated to Utah in 1861;
lived in Brigham Young’s family for some time,
after which she went to Spring City, Sanpete Co.,
on a visit to her sisters who resided there, while
there she became acquainted with Elder Chris-
tian G. Larsen and in 1864 was married to him in
Salt Lake City. She ever proved to be a faith-
ful wife and loving mother. She was blessed
with six children, two of whom have passed away
before her; the other four were at her beside in
her last moments and still live to mourn her loss.
	Her life has been an eventful one. She having
borne testimony of the truth of the Gospel to
many in her native country as well as in Zion.
For many years she was President of the Primary
Association in this place and as such won the
love and respect of all the children under her
charge; but owing to her failing health she was
forced to resign her office and for the last two
years was confined a great deal of the time to her
room, and much of the time to her bed. During
all her sickness and affliction she never mur-
mured, but bore her troubles bravely. Through
the mercy of the Lord she was able to go to the
Temple at Manti nearly two years ago and there
received all the blessings that can be obtained in
the house of the Lord in connection with her
husband and family. Thus she is fully prepared


to go into the presence of our Heavenly
Father with a record to be proud of.
	On account of her being a faithful member of
the Relief Society of Castle Dale, we the mem-
bers of said society
	Resolve, to mourn her loss and sympathize
with her grief stricken family.
