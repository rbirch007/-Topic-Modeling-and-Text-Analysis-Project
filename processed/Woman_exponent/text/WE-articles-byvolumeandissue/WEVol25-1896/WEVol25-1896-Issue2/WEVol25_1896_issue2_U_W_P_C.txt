U. W. P. C.

	THE Utah Woman’s Press Club met at
the office of the WOMAN'S EXPONENT,
Wednesday evening, June 3rd, 1896,
President E. B. Wells in the chair. Opened
with prayer by Mrs. C. C. R. Wells.
Minutes of previous meeting read and with
two exceptions approved. The exceptions
were that Miss Barton president of the Red
Cross society be mentioned by her full
name Clara Barton. Also that Mrs. Brown
who wrote the club, the interesting letter
from Washington D. C. be mentioned by
her full name Isabella Cameron Brown. A
visitor to the club for the evening was Miss
Sybil Stewart. Mrs. Emma J. McVicker
was unanimously voted a member of the
Club. Ruth M. Fox moved that Dr. R.
B. Pratt be chairman of a new program
committee and select her aids. Motion
carried. Dr. Pratt chose Dr. E. B. Fergu-
son and Phoebe C. Young as members of
the Committee.
	Phoebe C. Young read an interesting
paper on “The Armenians,” which will be
published.
	Remarks on the subject were made by
Mrs. E. B. Wells, Dr. Ferguson and others.
Meeting adjourned until June 30th, 8 p. m.
Closing prayer by Dr. R. B. Pratt.
L. S. WILCOX, Sec. pro tem.


Program for June 30th, 8 p. m,— Senti-
ments from Sir Walter Scott. Poem by
Dr. E. R. Shipp. Mrs. McVicker—verbal
account of visit to Denver. Mrs. Lizzie
Wilcox to read a chapter of Parliamentary
Law. Discussion.
