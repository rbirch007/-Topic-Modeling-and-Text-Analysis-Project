ADDRESS TO RELIEF SOCIETY.

	To the Relief Society of Centerville. I
ask you to pardon me if I may appear to be
obtrusive in the liberty I have taken, by ad-
dressing you in this manner. While at the
Relief Society union today I was impressed
with a very pleasant feeling of pleasure,
which is characteristic of this organization.
It was not only a repetition of what I al-
ways enjoy at these meetings, but while
there I was led to reflect upon the object of
the Relief Society and what it has done, I
think language will fail me, but still
enough can be said, to encourage the sisters
in that noble work. In the language of
scripture, to feed the hungry, clothe the
naked, to nurse and administer to the sick
embraces the highest point of the moral
law that can be expected of humanity; the
fact of which, is so well acknowledged by
all sects, all creeds, and all people whether
Christian or barbarian; and woman, with
her feminine nature and her sympathetic
feelings has so well adapted her for the
position that she has done her work so well
that it really appears to be one of the great
est essentials to human happiness. She has
labored for 6000 years in the good work
and no wonder that the Prophet Joseph
Smith was inspired to organize the Relief
Society, as he saw how much more good
could be done in a united capacity; may
the organization always have that support
that they deserve, and now the franchise
has been given her, it is to be hoped her
new obligations may not lessen her useful
ness in her acts of mercy nor burden her
with life’s cares, that she may not share in
all that pertains to human happiness. I be-
lieve with suffrage, woman will be able to
extend her usefulness in helping to improve
the civil affairs of society in other ways,
beside relieving the needy, and so far as
patronage in civil government she will find
every place for which she i[-] by nature and
qualification adapted.
