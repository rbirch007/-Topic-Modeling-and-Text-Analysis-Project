A TRIBUTE TO SISTER PETERSON

Dear Sister Wells:
	AGAIN I feel it my painful duty to say a
few words in regard to the departure of my
beloved friend and counselor, Sister S. A.
Peterson, so closely following our mutual
friend and co-laborer Sister M. W. Snow
whose death she mourned and wrote me her
deep sympathies for my bereavement.
Little did I then suppose she would so
soon leave me, to stand alone!
	Sister Peterson was true and faithful
under all circumstances and ever ready to
assist the poor and comfort the afflicted and
her loss will be felt by many. She was
devoted to her husband President C. Peter-
son and their numerous family who love and
reverence her memory, besides being a
public benefactor and visiting all the settle-
ments in the stake once or twice a year, feel-
ing an interest in every one’s welfare.
	I cannot express the desolation I felt in
being deprived of this my valued friend
and only remaining counselor. I feel to
acknowlege the hand of the Lord in this
His dispensation, for He doeth all things
well, "Blessed be the name of the Lord."
MARY A. P. HYDE.
May 27th, 1896.
