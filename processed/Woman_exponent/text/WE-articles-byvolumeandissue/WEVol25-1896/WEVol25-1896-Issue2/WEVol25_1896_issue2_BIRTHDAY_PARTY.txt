BIRTHDAY PARTY.

	ON Wednesday the seventeenth day of
June, our beloved friend and co-laborer,
Sister Julia C. Howe, reached her seventy-
third milestone.
	By invitation a few of her friends as-
sembled at her lovely home in the Seven-
teenth Ward Salt Lake City, and spent a
few hours in social communion. The
hostess in her loving amiable way, made
every one welcome and at home. Time
has gently touched Sister Howe, and it may
be said of her, “She grows old gracefully.
But truth to tell Sister Howe, will never be
old. Her love for the children and young
people of the Saints, and her many years of
association with them, and her great earnest
love for the Gospel, reflects youth and
beauty in her countenance. After partak-
ing of the delicate bounties spread for the
feast, and wishing Sister Howe life as long
as desired, or as her Patriarchal Blessing
says, until she shall behold the closing
scenes of this Dispensation, the guests de-
parted.
	The party was graced by the presence of
our venerable Presidents, Sister Zina D. H.
Young and M. Isabella Horne, also Sisters
L. M. Kimball, M. E. Kimball, Annie T.
Hyde, Clara C. Cannon, E. J. Stevenson,
and others who have been long associated
h the Lord’s work with her.
	O, how we love the old friends,
	Who have been fond and true;
	And we’ll not forget the old friends,
	Although we love the new. 
	O, how we love the old friends,
	Whose hair has silver grown;
	Though Time has touched them lightly,
	As swiftly it has flown.
	O, how we love the old friends;
	And friends of days of yore;
	Who now. their watch are keeping;
	For us on the "Other Shore.’’ 
June, 1896. L.D.A.
