	THE Y. L. M. I. A. of the Seventh Ward
held its annual meeting on the evening of
June 16th, at the Ward meeting house,
Mrs. Carrie S. Thomas presiding. The
house was beautifully decorated with roses
and other flowers. and a delightful perfume
was wafted through the house. The exer-
cises were of a most choice and interesting
character. During the winter the girls
had been studying the life of Joseph Smith,
and an exceedingly interesting review of the
subject was given by Miss Nellie Wallace,
who was quite original in her style and
manner of presenting the theme. Other
exercises were exceedingly well rendered,
even to the original play written by Miss
Blanche Thomas and given by four or five
of the girls. 
	Remarks were made by Sister E. B.
Wells who gave personal remembrances of
the Prophet Joseph, and by President
Elmina S. Taylor and Lucy W. Smith,
Mrs. Nellie C. Taylor and Bishop
Thorne. Mrs. C. S. Thomas deserves great


commendation for the successful method in-
troduced which have succeeded so admira-
bly in promoting the best interests of the
Association, spiritually and in a general
way, creating and keeping alive the work
in that locality. She has able assistants in
the young ladies of the ward, but has none
the less proven herself an efficient leader.
