HONOR TO A VETERAN.

Dear Sister Wells:
	According to previous appointment a few
sisters, thirteen in number, met at the
residence of Sister Howd Aug. 24, to com-
fort and cheer her in her affliction. You
have heard, perhaps, how she met with an
accident on June 7, which laid her upon a
bed of sickness and deprived her of the use
of her right limb. She has now so far re-
covered as to be able to sit up, but cannot
use her limb to walk. The sisters surprised
her completely, and she felt pleased and
grateful that we had thought of her. By
request of Sister Howd we held a little
meeting, Sister R. W. Tyler presiding.
Opened by singing and prayer, after which
all the sisters expressed themselves as
happy and thankful in meeting with Sister
Howd and seeing her so far recovered.
Sister Howd testified that it was only
through the power of God and the prayers
of the faithful brethren and sisters that she
had been restored thus far, and she felt that
He would continue to bless her until she
could walk. Sister Tyler said whomsoever
the Lord loveth He chasteneth, and afflic-
tions cause us to draw near to our heavenly
Father.
	At the close of the meeting a bounteous
picnic was served in an adjoining room.
Sister Howd was carried in her chair and
placed at the head of the table, and all had
a feast of good things as they had previous-
ly enjoyed a feast of the Holy Spirit.
	A sweet and heavenly influence prevaded
during all the proceedings, and we left
Sister Howd feeling much better. It is the
intention to visit in a similar manner, other
sisters who are confined to their homes, as
we cannot honor too much these aged ones
who, having spent their lives in humbly
serving God, are now drawing close to the
other side.
	Respectfully your sister in the Gospel,
						   L. W. JONES.
