U. W. P. C.

	IN response to the kindly invitation of
Mrs. Rachel Miller, the members of the
Press Club met at her residence, July 31,
1896 President E. B, Wells called the
meeting to order and prayer was offered by
Mrs. Lizzie Wilcox. The roll was called
and presence signified by poetic sentiments.
Lengthy minutes of the interesting June
meeting were read and approved. Mis-
cellaneous business being next in order the
president reported the completion of pay-
ment to the Federation, and proposed the
name, of Mrs. Mary J. Lambert, and Mrs.
R. S. Miller the name of Martha Miller as
members of the U. W. P. C. The motion
was put and carried. In accordance with
our president’s oft repeated wish that our
club should have a critic, Mrs. E. J. Mc-
Vicker was proposed by Miss S. L. Monroe
as critic. The motion was carried
unanimously. The nominee very modest-
ly expressed thanks for the honor, and said
sheould try to exercise discretion in this
duty, and desired all to feel free to criticise
the critic. A discussion then followed on
the pronunciation of the word “program”,
and all were advised to “look it up.” Mrs.
L. D. Alder then read an original poem en-
titled “Left unfinished.” President E. B.
Wells then read a letter from Susan B.
Anthony advising the Utah women as to
their duties in voting, and the advisability
of their holding office. Mrs. I. C. Brown
entertained the club with reminiscences of
Washington life, dwelling upon the social
and domestic qualities of President Cleve-
land and his beautiful wife and lovely
children. Mrs. L. S. Wilcox, by request,
read a poem written by Dr. Orielle Curtis
entitled “Friends of the Press Club.” The
meeting was then adjourned for one month
and closed with prayer by E. W. Hyde.
	After this our hostess and her fair
daughters served most delicious refresh-
ments, flitting in and out of the brilliantly
lighted parlors, in their airy summer cos-
tumes, making a picture long to be remem-
bered.
