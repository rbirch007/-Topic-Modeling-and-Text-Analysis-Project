U W. P. C.

	THE Utah Woman’s Press Club met at
the EXPONENT Office, Aug. 31, 1896, Presi-
dent E. B. Wells in the chair. Prayer was
offered by Mrs. Lizzie Shipp. The roll
was called, members responding with or-
iginal sentiments; minutes of the July
meeting were read and approved. President
E. B. Wells said she had received a card


from the Ladies Literary Club, inviting the
members of the Press Club to be present at
their opening day exercises, which would
include a reception to the retiring president;
also a book containing their plan of work
for the year. Miss Monroe moved that a
vote of thanks be tendered the Literary
Club for their invitation; the motion was
carried unanimously. Dr. R. B. Pratt read
a poem from Jean Ingelow entitled, “The
Dead Year,” followed by a sketch from the
life of Margaret, Queen of St Louis of
France. Dr. E. B. Ferguson gave an in-
teresting account of the Democratic conven-
tion held at Chicago to which she was sent
as a delegate. Meeting was then adjourned
for one month. Closing prayer by Mrs.
Mary Morris; after which delicious refresh-
ments were served, and pleasant chat en-
gaged in.
		ELLA W. HYDE, Cor. Sec.
