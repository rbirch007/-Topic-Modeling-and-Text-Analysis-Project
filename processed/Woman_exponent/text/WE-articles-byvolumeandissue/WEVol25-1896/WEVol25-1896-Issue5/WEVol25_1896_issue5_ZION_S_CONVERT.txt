					ZION'S CONVERT


THE Chicago Civic Federation has an-
nounced its intention to concentrate its
efforts on two important objects—the prose-
cution of city employees who are systematic-
ally robbing the city treasury, and the election
of State legislators who will give Chicago
new reform measures.
	THE twenty-fourth Congress of Women
will be held in St. Johns, New Burnswick,
Sep. 16, 17 and 18. Members of the A. A.
W. expecting to attend should write at once
to Mrs W. H. Tuck, Orange Street, St.
Johns, N. B., in order to receive names and
addresses of their hostesses.


“Mrs. Harriet Beecher Stowe never
ceased to believe that women were needed
in government as truly as in the family,
and ought to be clothed with the same polit-
cal rights and charged with the same polit-
cal duties as men.”
	Western Womanhood of Fargo, North De-
kota edited and published by Mary A. Whe-
don comes out in a new and attractive form
with its third volume. It is a general wo-
man’s paper—confined to no single hobby,
but concerning woman’s work in the home
and out—probably the only journal in the
West giving an outlook over such a broad
field. W. C. T. U., Equal Suffrage,
Home, Children, School Room, Sunday
school, all have their part and place. The
leading page contains a delightful picture
and sketch of Mrs. Ella Knowles Haskell,
of Montana, the first and only lady Assist-
ant Attorney General in this country.
