NOTES AND NEWS.

	THE Chicago Political Equality League
closes its public meetings for the season
with an all day meeting Saturday, April
3rd, at the Woman’s Club Hall. Forenoon
session at 10.30; afternoon at 2.30.
Among the speakers will be Mrs. Florence
Kelly, on Women and Children in Fact-
ories; Mrs. W. H. Rand, on the Women’s
and Children’s Protective Agency; Rev. J.
A. Rondthaler, D. D., on Woman Suffrage;
Mrs. Jennie C. Hutchins, on Current Suf-
frage Events; Mrs. Helen E. Starrett, on
Asking for her Share of Power; Mrs. Mary
Knowles Bartlett, on The Woman Question
in Mrs. Ward’s “Marcella” and “Sir
George Tressady;” Dr. Julia Holmes
Smith, on The Woman Question in Mere-
dith’s “Diana of the Crossways.”    —Ex.


THE Woman’s National Press Associa
tion transacted a good deal of routine busi-
ness at its meeting, March 19, and appoint-
ed Clara B. Colby and Carrie Harrison a
committee to draft a memorial to the Presi-
dent, asking his good office in securing the
impartial execution of the civil service laws,
and that the chiefs of divisions be no longer
allowed to discriminate against women in
the selection of clerks.
