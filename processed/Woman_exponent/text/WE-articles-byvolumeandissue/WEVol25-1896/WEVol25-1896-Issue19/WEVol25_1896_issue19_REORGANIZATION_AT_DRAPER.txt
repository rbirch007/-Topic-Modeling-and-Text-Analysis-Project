REORGANIZATION AT DRAPER.

	ON Thursday, Feb. 6, 1897, at 10 a. m..
a meeting was held at Draper, Salt Lake
Co. at the Ward house for the purpose of
reorganizing the Relief Society, it being
disorganized by the death of our beloved
President, Sister Hannah Rawlins.
	The meeting was presided over by Bishop
William C. Allen. Singing “How firm a
foundation;” the opening prayer was offer-
ed by Lauritz Smith Sen.; continued by sing-
ing “Behold the Savior of mankind.”
The Stake Presidency of the Relief Society
were present. President Horne gave some
excellent advice with regard to this organ-
ization. The speaker urged all the sisters
to be enrolled as members. Counselors
Annie T. Hyde and Clara C. Cannon en-
dorsed what the previous speaker had re- 
ferred to; spoke to some extent on the
duties of the teachers of the Society; many
incidents were related about the Prophet
Joseph Smith and the instructions he gave.
	President M. I. Horne was chosen to
preside during the election of officers, and
Sister Sarah Burnham to act as secretary.
The articles of the Association were read
by the Secretary and the result of the
election was, Mrs. Sarah Ann Snow, Presi- 
dent Laura Jane Allen and Caroline
Rowbery, Vice: Presidents; Sarah Burnham,
Secretary; and Nancy Ballard, Treasurer;
Trustees, Mary Emma Terry, Ellen S.
Staker and Maria Comfort Goff.
	After the organization was completed
President Horne and her Counselors and
our worthy Bishop, W. C. Allen, and Elders
Thomas Staker and P. N. Goff gave some
excellent advice, The speakers urged those
who had been elected to magnify their call-
ing, also spoke upon sustaining those who
are placed over us; many things were made
mention of concerning the early rise of the
Church. A good spirit prevailed during
the exercises. Meeting closed by singing
“Softly beams the sacred dawning.” Bene-
diction by Elder Peter M. Goff.
		SARAH BURNHAM, Secretary.
