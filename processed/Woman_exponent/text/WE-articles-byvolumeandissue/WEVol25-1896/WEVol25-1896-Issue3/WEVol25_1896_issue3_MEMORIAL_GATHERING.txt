MEMORIAL GATHERING.

	ON Friday, June 19, a number of inti-
mate friends of the late Sister Hannah T.
King, met by invitation of her daughter,
Mrs. Louisa Spencer, at the residence of
Mr. and Mrs. Jacob Spencer in this city, in
memory of the gifted and much loved sister.
This pretty idea of social gathering to keep
her memory fresh and green, and to honor
her name and fame, was the idea of Mrs.
S. M. Kimball and Mrs. J. Fewson Smith,
and has been continued, yearly since her
death. The conversation is usually upon
Mrs. King’s characteristics, her writings,
her letters, and reminiscences of her event-
ful life and remarkable personality.
	The gathering was in the season of
roses and the perfume of the flowers was
delightful in the house, and the table dec-
orations were very fine. The ladies
present were M. Isabella Horne, Sarah M.
Kimball, Rachel Grant, Helen M. Whitney,
Julia C. Howe, Eliza V. Smith, Margaret
Alford, Lydia D. Alder, Emily H. Wood-
mansee, Kate Brockbank, Louisa Spencer
and the writer.
	The conversation at table, though there
were no toasts or set speeches, was very
delightful and very like old-fashioned tea
table talk, and the spirit of it was quite re-
freshing to the soul, and the delicacies
partaken of were of the best and daintiest
of the season.
	If Mrs. King could have been present in
person, she would have been satisfied with
the perfect taste and order of the arrange-
ments.
						E. B. W.
