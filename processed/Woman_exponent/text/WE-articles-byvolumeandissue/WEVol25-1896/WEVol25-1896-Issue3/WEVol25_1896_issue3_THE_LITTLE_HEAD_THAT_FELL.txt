THE LITTLE HEAD THAT FELL.

I arose for household care
	When scarce shone forth the light of day,
And still, tho’ close into the night,
	A lot of sewing there did lay.
Yes all around me, everywhere
	The undarned socks and baby stuff,
The tiny dress, the little cap,
	The fluffy lace, and dainty ruff,
And other duties after that
	That mothers know they must obey.
All this I pondered on that night,
	The end of that long weary day;

Not knowing where to first begin
	So dazed was my brain with all,
When midst it came a childish voice—
	“Mamma, mamma” my boy did call,
Then rushing in with face aglow
	And eyes so bright and dimples deep,
Thro’ merry laughter ringing out,
	And roguish looks he could not keep—
"Oh Ma" he cried and danced about
	Until his curls did upward fly,
"I've such a funny dream to tell
	Just listen mamma dear—oh my!”

The night so hot the work undone,
	All then a burden seemed to me
That quickly in a rage I turned,
	And sharply cried “O let me be.”
Scarce had I spoke the bitter words,
	When all at once my careless gaze
Upon him rested thoughtlessly,
	But still I saw though in a daze,
Astonishment was on his face,
	That he the dream could not me tell.
His dancing stopped and in amaze
	The little head it fell.

Then I worked on till all complete,
	The night far gone I sat and thought
How hard my lot, how tired my feet,
	When I grew unconscious, aye was naught,
When came a sudden shining light
	And then a cloud obscured it all,
Then again it grew so bright,
	I heard a harp or baby call,
Celestial sounds came then to me,
	The light was bright the music near,
A flower was midst the star-lit air.
	An angel shining did appear.

[Column 2]
“Your lot is hard” she then did say
	“Hard work upon your spirits tell,
I come to take upon my breast,
	The little head that fell.”

Quick—with a cry of anguish wild,
	I then awoke—what was it all!
What was it all—that dream, my child!
	My brain confused I could not think,
Oh strength to rise oh power to know,
	To clear my head that’s crowded so.
Was that a dream—upon her breast
	To take my heart, my life, my joy,
Has she—Oh God, has she my boy?
	Did I repine oh— let me think,
What did the angel mean to tell
	The little head that fell.

Like one that staggered ’neath a blow,
	And strengthened up when strength re-
		turned,
I sprang upon my chamber floor,
	And fled while my poor brain yet burned,
Up with one bound the long dark stairs,
	My arm stretched out to feel the door,
Then turned the knob so silently
	My brain still by that angel tore.
There stood the cot—I could not move,
	My heart stopped beating, nor could I tell
What brought me there, nor could I see
	The little head that fell.

Then—then was the light; Oh clear,
	The cloud was gone—I was dreaming then not,
The angel then did appear,
	Smiling on me, and in the cot,
My heart cried out, I screamed with joy,
	Such joy that none but mothers tell,
And to my breast I lifted up
	The little head that fell.
