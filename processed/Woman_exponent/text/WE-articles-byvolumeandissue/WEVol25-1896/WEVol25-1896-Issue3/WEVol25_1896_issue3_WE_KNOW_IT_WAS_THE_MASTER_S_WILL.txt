We know it was the Master’s will,
And joy His wishes to fulfill.


Ye erring mortals cease to grieve,
“Though he were dead yet shall he live,”
But join in our triumphal song,
That one so valiant and so strong—

In faith, and righteounsess and worth,
Was for a moment lent to earth,
An almost perfect light to shed,
That in its rays your feet may tread. 

Then being all too good to stay,
Like some bright star he leads the way;
Hosanna! this our song shall be,
Death’s swallowed up in victory.
							R. M. F.
