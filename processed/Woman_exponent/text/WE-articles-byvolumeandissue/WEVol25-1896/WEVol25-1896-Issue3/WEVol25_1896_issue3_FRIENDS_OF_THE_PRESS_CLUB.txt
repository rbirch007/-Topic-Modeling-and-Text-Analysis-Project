FRIENDS OF THE PRESS CLUB. 
	WHILE you pore,
Over themes profound and vast,
Studying legendary lore,
Culling old world fancies o’er,
Tracing dim mysterious score,
Palimpsest from secret store
Cloistered in the whispering past;
Or perchance with thought more wise,
Pondering what directly lies
Under your observant eyes,—
Problems of the hour and day:--
How the brave balloonist tries
Task severe and high emprise,
Bravihg cold tempestuous skies;
How the searching Roentgen ray
Still new wonders brings alway;
Or how ’neath hypnotic sway
Minds from sentient thoughts astray,
Laws occult and strange obey;
Or, while patriotic zeal
Bids you seek the nation’s weal.
*         *         *         *         *         
While in your accustomed places,
You discuss with serious faces,
Themes of pith and weight like these,—
Pause—and place a dash or colon
in the essay good and wise;
Let me show you through my eyes,
Cedars tall and streams that roll on,
Hurrying to distant seas
With their wild sweet symphonies.

Tenderly fingers of evening were folding
Mantles of mist over valley and hill,
The eye in a rapture of beauty beholding
The red bloom of sunset fade softly and still,
When away from fair Utah, where sultry winds
	blow,
We sped as the arrow is shot from the bow,
As the eagle soars swift o’er the meadow be-
	low;—
Where the shriek of the steam sounded wild-
	ly and shrill.
On and still onward: in Eastern skies glowing
The saffron haired Dawn opened portals of
	flame,
Till through their bright arches fresh largess
	bestowing,
The Day King in splendor and majesty came.
Aquiver with gladness earth saw the light rise,
The flowers dried the dew in their tremulous
	eyes
And rang their soft joy-bells in grateful surprise,
Still westward we rolled over mountain and
	plain,

Here canons deep-shaded with crags beetling
	o’er us.
There, pink bloom of orchards that scented the
	breeze;
Now full bosomed rivers shone sparkling before
	us,
Or gleamed through the verdure of wide spread-
	ing trees.
We rushed through fair hamlets, we skirted broad
	plains,
With their rustling of grasses and waving of
	grains,
And paused where fair cities spread wide their
	domains,
Till we hailed the tall ships anchored safe from
	the seas.

How softly Pacific thy blue waves are glancing,
Mid moss vestured islands and silence serene!
So fair and so peaceful in liquid light dancing.
White crested Atlantic rolls billows of green,
What prairies all rustling with corn and with
	wheat,
What hills.where the amber grape clusters grow
	sweet,
What streams tripping seaward with silver shod
	feet,
What orchards and meadows lie smiling between!

Thine, Oh my country, these princely dominions,
Thy treasure-vaults mountains of wealth giving
	ore;
Thy tall masted ships spread their starry-crown
	pinions
And waft their rich gifts to Earth’s uttermost
	shore.
Shall the “land of the brave and the home of the
	free”
England’s vassal and appanage willingly be?
For a fetter of gold shall her statesmen find plea?
Then thy stars shall lead onward and upward no
	more.

So I mused as I sailed o’er the soft silken waters,
Where Puget Sound sings round her velvety
	isles,
And I asked shall the wisdom the ages have
	taught us.
Be vain to protect us from tyranny’s wiles?
But the waves rippled on in their musical glee,
And mirrored the grace of each vine-covered
	tree.
And beauty again seemed new born from the sea
Till thought grew entranced by earth’s magical
	smiles.

And now mydear friends in the hot dusty city,
I wish I could paint you the sylvan retreat,
Where I write at my ease and remember with
	pity
The tired throngs that swelter in fierce summer
	heat,
Think of trees just as far as the vision can go,
Reaching up to the skies in a numberless row,—
Spruce, cedar and fir,—leafy maple below,—
Vines and moss running riot with soft changing
	feet.
Embosomed in beauty imagine a clearing,
With mossy log fortalice circling it round,
Its cabin and garden securely ensphering,—
Here your Press correspondent cool refuge has
	found.
Birds, squirrels and bees join the Skykomish
song,
As the river flows bubbling and laughing along,
Green wave chasing wave in continuous throng,
With the soft grace of motion, the music of sound.

Here lightly the wild duck skims over the
	waters,
Or laves with their coolness her full-feathered
	breast.
From the snow mantled mountains the river has
	brought us,
Their soft dewy breezes, their blessings of rest.
Here awhile it is good the world’s strife to forget;
Its jostling and bustle, its envious fret,
Here nature’s sweet peace soothes life’s pain and
regret,
And hopes Paradise Bird in the heart builds her
	nest.

But friends of the Press Club, I gossip too long,—
Forgive the intrusion, I keow it is wrong;
The curb on my Pegasus needs to be strong,
Or he ambles on ground that to others
	belong.
I read your high Carnival 'neath the green
	shade.
And your glorious 4th with its pomp and
	parade,
With the beauty of Utah in splendor arrayed.
The miners and ranchers and railroad men
	here
Must perforce be content with very plain cheer.
*         *         *         *         *         
Success to the cause and the Press Club as
	well,
Remember with kindness your friend Orielle.

To Curtis I’ve hunted in vain for a rhyme, 
So l leave the last name for more leisurely
	time.
