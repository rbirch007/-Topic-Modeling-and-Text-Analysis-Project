IN MEMORIAM.
		SUSANNAH MORTENSEN.
	RESOLUTIONS of Respect, by the officers and
members of the W. H. P. R. class of Brigham
City, to the memory of our departed sister,
Susannah Mortensen, who died January 29, 1896,
at the age of 39 years.
	Whereas, The hand of the Almighty has seen fit
to take to Himself our beloved sister and class-
mate, Susannah Mortensen, who was always
faithful in the cause of right, full of charity
and humility and long suffering. Therefore be
it
	Resolved, That we deeply mourn the loss of a
sincere worker, and tender our sympathies to the
children and relatives of the deceased.
	Resolved, That in our natural sorrow for the
loss of a devoted laborer, we find consolation in
the knowledge that it is well with her for whom
we mourn. Indeed though we miss her we can
but say—she was prepared to enter the bright
Celestial Shore.
	Resolved, That her loss is not only felt by the
W. H. P. R. class, but also the Relief Society,
Sunday School, Primary and all the associations
with which she was connected.

She is not dead—she dwells on that bright shore
	where angels tread
That bear our best loved things away, and then
	we call them dead.

	Resolved that these resolutions be published
in the WOMAN'S EXPONENT and also recorded in
the records of the W. H. P. R. Association, and
that one copy be tendered to her children.
	Maggie Rohwer, Mary Nelson, Melina Peters,
Emeline Watkins, Committee.
