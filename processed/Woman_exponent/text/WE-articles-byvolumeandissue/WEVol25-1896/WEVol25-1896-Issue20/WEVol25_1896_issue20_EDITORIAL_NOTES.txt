EDITORIAL NOTES.

	Madame Mountford delivered her
new lecture “The Life of Jacob” in the
Tabernacle April 8. The rendering of this
subject was exceptionally fine.
	We desire to explain to our subscribers
and patrons that the Book of Poems is nearly
ready, the steel engraving has come, the
covers are finished and the remaining pages
will soon be printed. The delay has been
caused more on account of the revision and
copying of poems written long ago, many
o them scattered here and there that never
had been published, had to be collected and
corrected.
	Mrs. Emma J. McVicker gave a Ken-
sington tea to a party of ladies on Thurs-
day, April 8, in honor of her sister, Mrs.
Morgan, who is here on a visit. The after-
noon was very enjoyably passed in social
conversation, the tea was served in the
daintiest style, and the refreshments were
delicious. Mrs. McVicker is a most genial
hostess and knows how to bring the right
people together. These social entertain-
ments are certainly very pleasant and it
does seem a renewal of friendship to meet
and “break bread” together, and is re-
freshing to those who amid the hurry and
bustle of a busy life occasionally stop to
greet the friends they love, but seldom
ever see. And so we are particularly grate-
ful to Mrs. McVicker and other ladies who
have remembered us when making up a
social gathering.
