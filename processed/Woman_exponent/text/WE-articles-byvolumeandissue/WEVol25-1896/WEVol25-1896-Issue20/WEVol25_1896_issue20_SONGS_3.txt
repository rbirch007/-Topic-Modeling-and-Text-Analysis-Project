	Nay, I canna’ sing the old songs,
		There comes a rush of tears,
	And my voice fails to reach the notes,
		Soar’d o’er in bygone years. 
	I may not touch a broken lute,
		Nor strike a jangled string,
	Nor strive with tones forever mute,
		Those dear old songs to sing, 
	My harp is on the willows hung,
		Its strains of music o’er,
	Those dear old songs remain unsung,
		That oft thrilled hearts of yore.
	“Sweet highland Mary,”—“Bonnie doon,”—
		“Ben Bolt’’—and Nelly Bly,”—.
	“Raking the hay in merrie June,’’—
	And “Cornin’ thro’ the rye.’’—
	“O there’s ne’er made sic’ anither,”—
		“Annie Laurie,”—“ Ivy green,”—
	“Sweet Home” “The last rose of summer—”
		“Bonnie blue-eyed Kathleen,”—
	“Kate Mavourneen,”—“Rory o’Moore,”—
		“Jessie flower o’ Dumblane,”—
	“Young Mary across the moor,”—
		“When shall we meet again,”
	“Old oaken bucket,”—“Lochinvar,”—
		“Bide ye darlent, wi' me,”—
	“Pledged to union in peace or war,”—
		“Land of the brave and free,”—
	*“Now launch the boat upon the wave,”—
		“Do they miss me at home?”—
	“Here’s health bonnie Scotland to thee,”—
		“Westward our footsteps roam.”
