ANNIVERSARY PARTY.

	ON the 23rd of December 1896, the
anniversary of the birthday of our beloved
Prophet Joseph Smith, was celebrated by
the Saints of the Fourth ward of Spanish
Fork. Meeting convened in the new
Society hall at 2 p.m., under the auspices
of the Fourth ward Relief Society, Pres’t.
Mary Baxter presiding. An excellent pro-
gram was rendered; the music by the choir
under the leadership of Morgan H.
Thomas was exquisite, and the themes
chosen appropriate and beautiful. Prayer
and opening address by Bishop A. E. Nel-
son; Brother Charles Monk gave a most in-
teresting account of the birth, life and labors
of that great and noble man, and related
many interesting and thrilling episodes of
his life and character, and held the audience
spell-bound by the vivid delineations of the
power and glory of this great and last dis-
pensation, and of the love and condescen-
sion of our Father and His Son Jesus
Christ, our great Redeemer.
	Counselor August Swenson and others
made appropriate remarks interspersed with
music by the choir and essays and recita-
tions by the young ladies of the M. I. A.
Benediction by Robert Holmes; and all
went home feeling that they had had a
feast in very deed.
		MARY BAXTER, Pres’t.
		MARY S. CHRISTENSEN Sec’y.
Spanish Fork, Jan. 18, 1897.


REV. OLYMPIA BROWN, president of the
Wisconsin W. S. A., has a son and daugh-
ter who are two of the most brilliant
young people in Wisconsin.
	ON motion of Mrs. Belva A. Lockwood,
Mrs Alice A. Minnick, of Beatrice, Neb.,
was admitted to the bar of the Supreme
Court of the United States on Jan. 18, 1896.
