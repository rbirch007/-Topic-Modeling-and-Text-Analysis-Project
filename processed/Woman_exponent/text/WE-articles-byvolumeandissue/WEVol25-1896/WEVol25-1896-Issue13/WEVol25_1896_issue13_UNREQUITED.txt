UNREQUITED.
METHOUGHT my love was perfect sweetness,
   Milk of human kindness running o’er,
With all the virtues in completeness,
   Held too in ample bounteous store.
Alas, 'tis but a sad delusion,—
   A fleeting phantom,—fate must deplore,
Apart, alone in still seclusion,
   I mourn for love that returns no more.
Yet one ray in my vale of sorrow,
   From hope's bright star cheers the path I tread
Guides upward towards a blissful morrow,
   Where eternal life restores the dead.
                                                            L. M. H.
