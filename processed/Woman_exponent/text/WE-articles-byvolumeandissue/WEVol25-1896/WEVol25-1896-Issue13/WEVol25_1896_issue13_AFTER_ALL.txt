AFTER ALL.
AETER the day has sung its song of gladness.
	After the night has told its tale of woe,
After the bird's sweet notes have turned to sad-
ness,
After the breeze has breathed its music low,
		Think of me, pray for me,
		And of thee, only thee,
		My thought shall be,
		After all, after all.
After the flow'rs refuse to yield their fragrance,
	After the dew no more its mantles spread,
After the sun has ceased to shed its radiance,
	After the joys of springtime long have fled,
		Leave me not, pain o'er fraught
		For I've sought, only sought.
		Thy sincere thought,
		After all, after all.
After the bloom of youth's forever banished,
	After the hours have lengthened into years,
After my hopes like melting snows have vanished,
	After my heart has dried its well of tears,
		Still trusting, and hoping,
		Still waiting, and loving,
		To thee I cling,
		After all, after all.
			HYACINTH.
New Year's Day, '97.


LADY HENRY SOMERSET has sent a
London lady doctor and two trained nurses
to Bulgaria to help to care for the Armenian 
refugees, who are in great need.
