U. W. P. C.
	Dec. 39, 1896, the U. W. P. C. held its
regular meeting at the office of the WO-
MAN'S EXPONENT. The meeting was
called to order at 7.30 p. m., President
Ruth M. Fox presiding. After prayer was
offered by Dr. Shipp, the secretary called
the roll, each member responding with a
sentiment from Mrs. Hemans. Minutes of
last meeting were then read and approved.
A motion was made by Mrs. E. B. Wells
and carried by vote of club that Mrs. Emily
S. Richards be made a member. The pro-
gramme for the next meeting was presented
by Mrs. L. D. Alder and the program com-
mittee dismissed. Mrs. E. B. Wells, Mrs.
Ella W. Hyde and Mrs. P. C. Young were
appointed by the President as the members
of the next program committee. Under
the head of new business, Pres. Fox spoke
in regard to the necessity of the club paying
for light, heat and room for their meetings.
No motion was made but the matter was
left for the consideration of the club. A
letter from Mrs. Julia McDonald to the
U. W. P. C. was read by the Sec’y. The
evening’s program was as follows: Paper
“ Pilgrim Women of New England,” Miss
Monroe. Poem “ Year of Jubilee,” Mrs.
Fox. Paper “John Ruskin,” Mrs. Wil-
cox. Dr. Pratt was excused from giving
her paper on account of sickness.
	On motion made and carried that the
critic’s report be given next meeting and
that the critic be allowed unlimited scope
in her criticisms. Adjournment was now
in order. Benediction Mrs. Margaret A.
Caine.
