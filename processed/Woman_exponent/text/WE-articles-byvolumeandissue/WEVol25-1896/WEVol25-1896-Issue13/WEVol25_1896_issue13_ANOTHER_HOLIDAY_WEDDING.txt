	Miss Edna Lambert and Mr. Eugene
M. Cannon of this city were united in mar-
riage at noon on Friday, January 1, 1897,
in the Salt Lake Temple, President Angus
M. Cannon of the Salt Lake Stake of Zion
and father of the bridegroom, performing
the solemn ceremony.
	The bride is the daughter of George C.
Lambert Esq. of the Lambert Paper Co.,
and a very sweet and amiable young girl;
the young husband has just recently re-
turned from filling honorably a mission of
three years to the Society Islands Their
courtship, if we may be excused the term,
dates back to their childhood, and judging
from appearances they have always been in
love with each other.
“But they were young; Oh! what without our
	youth,
Would love be?—what would youth be without
	love?
Youth lends it joy, and sweetness, vigor, truth
Heart, soul, and all that seems as from above.”
	A wedding reception was given at the
bride’s parents in the evening (New Year’s
night) a most auspicious time, and surely
everything indicates a long and happy life
for this very young couple, who have start-
ed out so well and under such favorable
conditions.
	The guests were very numerous though
nearly all relatives, and congratulations
were even more numerous than the
assembled guests, for many were sent by
those who could not be present in person.
Delicious refreshments were served on
tete a tete tables and there was joy and
merriment and nothing at all to mar the
happiness of the party.
	Again the EXPONENT extends heartfelt
congratulations and hopes and believes
these devoted young people who have start-
ed out to begin life together will be worthy
all the blessings mortals are permitted to
enjoy in this world.
