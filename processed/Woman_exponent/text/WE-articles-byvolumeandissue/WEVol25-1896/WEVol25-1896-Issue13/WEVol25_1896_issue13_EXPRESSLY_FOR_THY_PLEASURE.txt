	Expressly for thy pleasure;
Speak thou the word—and earth shall yield
	Of stores full bounteous measure.
And we will pour it at thy feet
	That thou may’st feast thy vision
On wealth and products of that soil
	Men once held in derision.
For scarcely fifty years ago
	These broad and fertile valleys
Were sterile, drear and desolate
	None dwelt here but the savage.
Come clamber up the mountain side
	View hamlet, field and garden
Look up the treasures of the hills
	Our pride then thou wilt pardon.
Glance now thine eye on yonder lake.
	Behold that sunset golden
Where fleecy, crimson-tinted clouds
	The snow-clad peaks enfolding.
O, wondrous change the years have wrought
	Since our gallant pioneers 
Braved countless dangers fearing not
	But with the eye of Love
They saw the barren wilderness,
	Would blossom, bloom and flourish;
With cattle on a thousand hills,
	The meek and poor to nourish.
Here they unfurled the stars and stripes
	An ensign to the nations,* 
Then bowed in reverence to Him
	Whose arm was their salvation.
And now in this their jubliee,
	We witness their predictions
Fulfilled, applaud them for their faith
	And honor their convictions.
All blessing on their silvered heads
	Though few with us remaining,
A mighty monument they’ve reared
	Our lasting homage claiming.
Ring the bells for ninety-seven
	Let’s have a great rejoicing;
Forgiveness, love and peace abound
	The ancient custom voicing.
Praise Him who led us to these vales,
	With song and acclamation,
And give those grand old pioneers,
	A glorious ovation.
							R. M. F.
*Ensign peak.
