WOMAN’S EXPONENT.


MRS. OLIVE C. BUTLER Mrs. Evange-
line Heartz and Mrs. M. A. B. Conine, of
Denver have been elected members of the
Colorado House of Representatives on the
Populists fusion ticket. One of these
legislators-elect, Mrs. Butler, is entitled to
especially cordial congratulations from
Massachusetts, since she is a Massachusetts
emigrant, a Boston woman, born under the
very shadow of Bunker Hill Monument,
and educated in the public and private
schools of Massachusetts.
	THE Vermont Senate, last Thursday,
Nov 5, passed the Municipal Woman Suf-
frage bill by an almost unanimous vote,
only one Senator voting against it.
	MISS GRACE E. PATTON of the Colorado
State Agricultural College, has just been
elected State Superintendent of public in-
struction, to succeed Mrs. Angenette M.
Peavey.
