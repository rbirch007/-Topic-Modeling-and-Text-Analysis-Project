DEGREES FOR WOMEN.

	CAMBRIDGE, Eng., March 2.—The syn-
dics appointed by Cambridge university to
consider the question of granting degrees to
women has presented its report. While it is
not prepared to recommend that women be
admitted to membership, the syndics recom-
mend that the title of B. A. be conferred by
diploma upon women who have already
passed or hereafter pass the final tripos.
The Syndics further recommend that the
women may proceed in one course to the
M. A. degree and that the university be
empowered to grant degrees in arts, law,
letters, sciences and music to women who
have not fulfilled the usual conditions but
who have been recommended for such titles
honorias causa, by the council of the senate.
