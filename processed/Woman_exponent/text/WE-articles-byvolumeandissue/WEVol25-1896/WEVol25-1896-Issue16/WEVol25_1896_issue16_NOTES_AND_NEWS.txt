NOTES AND NEWS.
	THE Committee on Elections has reported
adversely on presidential woman suffrage
in Kansas.
	THE fact that a man gives up his seat to
a woman in a street car is no evidence that
he does not let his wife bring in the coal.—
Syracuse Post.
	IN Lexington, Ky., Mrs. Allen Harrison
has been president of the board of education
for a year. Her administration has been
most successful. The board is composed of
nine men and three women.
	THE Iowa Capital says: “One of the
actions of the joint committee on elections
will greatly please the distinguished women
who visited Des Moines in January, and
that is the adherence to the recommenda-
tion of the code commissioners that at least
one trustee of every State institution hav-
ing children in its charge must be a
woman.
	THE Constitutional Convention of the
State of Delaware, on Feb 16, voted 17 to
7 against striking out the word male from
the section of the constitution relating to
general elections. Those voting in favor
of striking out were all Republicans.
Seven Republicans and ten Democrats voted
against striking out. Not a Democrat
present voted for equal suffrage.
	EN route from the Convention Miss
Anthony went to visit Mrs. Sewall at In-
dianapolis; Mrs. Catt and Miss Hay attend-
ed a meeting of the Chicago Political Club,
and on Mrs. Catt’s earnest presentation of
the needs of the organization work, over
two hundred and fifty dollars was con-
tributed by the members present.
