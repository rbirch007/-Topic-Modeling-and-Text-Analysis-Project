SOCIAL GATHERING.

	ON Friday afternoon Feb. 19, a very
small but pleasant social dinner party was
given by Sister Zina D. H. Young, Presi-
dent of the Relief Society. The guests
felt greatly honored by the presence of
President Wilford Woodruff, the venerable
patriot and pioneer, to whom this people
owe so much as prophet, seer, revelator,
leader, Apostle, missionary and friend.
	Presidents George Q. Cannon and Joseph
F. Smith, Counselors in the First Presi-
dency were also present, Apostle F. D.
Richards, President C. O. Card of Alberta
Stake, Elder Oliver B. Huntington, Elder
H. A. Naisbett, Hon Joseph S. Kimball,
the hostess herself and her daughter Sister
Zina Young Card, Sisters Emma Woodruff,
Julina L. Smith, Jane S. Richards, Bath-
sheba W. Smith, Emmeline B. Wells and
Lathilla Kimball.
	The dinner was a perfect success and
social conversation most enjoyable; the
coming together socially of old-time friends
and acquaintances was most refreshing to
one’s spirits. Aunt Zina, as we love to
call her, can congratulate herself on the
success of the delightful feast she gave to
her dear friends brethren and sisters.
	The First Presidency have so many im-
portant duties and grave responsibilities
resting upon them to which they devote
their almost entire time, that it is quite a
rarity to meet them socially, and must be
somewhat restful to them as well. Sister
Zina Young was indeed fortunate in having
them gathered beneath her roof. It was an
especial privilege to see and listen to them,
knowing how wide have been their
opportunities for knowledge, and with
what pearls of wisdom their minds are
stored.
