	Counselor Jane Holt said she loved to
hear the sisters bear their testimony who
lived in the days of Joseph. “If we live
true, faithful and prayerful lives we will be
blessed. I feel to bear my testimony to
what has been said. May our heavenly
Father help us to be true and faithful.”
	Pres’t. Hess said, “I have experienced a
great many things in this Church and I
want to tell you the Latter-day Saints were
never on so dangerous ground as they are
today. The evil one is trying to have the
people give way and not trust the Priest-
hood, those who feel that way should re-
pent. Some told Joseph Smith they were
willing for him to advise in spiritual things
but thought they knew as much in tempor-
al things. This influence is now here and
a great many are influenced by it. I feel
my brethren and sisters we have been
silent long enough and it is time we should
rise up and warn the people. I have the
right as a citizen to speak my mind, let us
sustain ourselves and the Priesthood.”
	Referred to the remarks of the sisters,
said they were good. “Now sisters you
have just as much influence as the men and
use your influence for good. I ask the
Lord to bless you all.”
	Meeting adjourned for three months to
be held at Centerville. Singing Doxology.
Prayer by D. O. Willey.
			SUSAN GRANT, President,
			PHEBE C. SESSIONS, Secretary.
