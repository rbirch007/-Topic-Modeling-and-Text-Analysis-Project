WEDDING ANNIVERSARY.

Dear Sister:
	FRIDAY the 11th inst. being the sixtieth
wedding anniversary of Patriarch D. Tyler
and wife, members of the High Priest’s
Quorum of which Brother Tyler is presi-
dent, and their wives, also members of the
Relief Society of which Sister Tyler is pres-
dent, and their husbands with members of
the family and other invited guests, met in
the Assembly Hall at 10 o’clock p. m. to
celebrate the occasion.
	The hall was tastefully and artistically
decorated and draped with flags, bunting,
evergreens and flowers. The afternoon exer-
cises consisted of speeches, recitations, instru-
metal and vocal music, an essay, verses com-
posed for the occasion and reminiscences by
the worthy couple, after which an excellent
repast in picnic form was partaken of.
After all had feasted to their hearts content
(and their appetites also) order being re-
stored, toasts and sentiments were indulged
in for a short time.
	I will also remark that Stake President
Sister Lucinda Houd, who has been confin-
ed to her room for some time and to her
bed for some time from a stroke, was
brought in a buggy and assisted to an easy
chair on the stand where she was heartily
welcomed by those present.
	The afternoon’s proceedings being con-
cluded all returned to their homes, being
however, invited to return in the evening
and participate in the dance a few hours,
which they did, all seeming to enjoy them-
selves to the utmost. The aged couple
were each presented with an easy chair
besides other presents.
		Very Respectfully
			ALICE M. T. TANNER.
