FOREST DALE RELIEF SOCIETY.

	ON the 22nd of September 1896, at 4
o’clock p. m., a meeting was held in the
Forest Dale meeting house in Salt Lake
Stake for the purpose of organizing a
Relief Society. 
	Bishop James Jensen and Counselor
Royal B. Young were present. Sister M. Isa-
bella Horne, President of the Relief Society
of Salt Lake Stake was the first speaker.
She referred to the time when the Society
was first organized in Nauvoo by the
Prophet Joseph Smith, which was very
interesting and instructive. The following
officers were chosen and set apart: Cor-
nelia P. Driggs, President, Louisa Steven-
son and Addie Morris Cannon, Counselors,
Sarah Strock, Secretary, Agnes Rumel,
Ass’t. Secretary and Telitha Hendry
Treasurer.
	There were twenty-one members whose
names were enrolled.


DR. GRACE KIMBALL in an interview in
the London Daily Chronicle, describes the
recent disturbance in Constantinople, of
which she was an eye-witness. Dr. Kim-
ball was standing on the steps of the Otto-
man Bank on the day it was seized, a short
time before the attack took place.
	MRS. S. V. ROOT, of St. Paul, Minn.,
has been appointed by Mayor Moran as a
special police officer, possessing full power


to make arrests. Mrs. Root will not patrol
a beat, yet she will wear a star. She desir-
ed the appointment to aid her work in con-
nection with the rescue home, an institution
for the reformation of fallen women, with
which she has long been connected.
