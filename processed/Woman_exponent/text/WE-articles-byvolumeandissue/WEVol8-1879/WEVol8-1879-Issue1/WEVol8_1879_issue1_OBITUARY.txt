OBITUARY.

	Died at South Cottonwood, Salt Lake Co.
April 16, 1879, Lucy C[-]ayton Bullock, born at
Farrington, Lancashire, England, 26th of
Ma[-]ch, 182[-]. She was baptised into the
Church of Jesus Christ in 1837, being one of
the first Latter-day Saints baptised in Eng-
land, and came to Nauvoo in 1842.
	She received the ordinances in the Temple
at Nauvoo, and married Elder Thomas Bul-
lock, January 23, 1846
	Was the only woman who saw the blood of
the Patriarch, Hyrum Smith, after his mar-
tyrdom in Carthage jail; and was driven from
Nauvoo by the mobbers, in Sept., 1846; moved
to Winter Quarters, where she lived through
the winter of 1847-8; emigrated to Salt Lake
Valley, where she arrived September 22, 1848
	She was set apart as first counselor to the
president of the Relief Society of South Cot-
tonwood ward, and officiated as a midwife
successfully for ten years.
	During the last th[-]ee months she suffered
greatly from dropsy and other complaints, and
died on Wedne[-]day, April 16, at 12 45 p. m.
without one struggle, or a groan; surrounded
by her husband, their child[-]en, and a few of
her nnmerous friends: being "worn out" by
her labors, aged 59 years and 26 d[-]ys, and
having a glorious hope of the coming forth in
the morning of the first resurrection, to re-
ceive her crown of reward, and eternal life
with the sanctified, redeemed by the Lord
Jesus Christ.—Com.
