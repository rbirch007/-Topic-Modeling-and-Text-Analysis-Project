	SMITHFIELD, Oct. 6, 1879.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I believe the sisters of Smithfield are not
often heard from, not because we are not
trying to do our duty, for we are; we are
caring for the poor and the sick, and we
are trying as a band of sisters to be united
and work for the good of all.
	We had a three days visit recently from
Sisters' E. R. Snow. M. I. Horne, S. M.
Kimball, and S. M. Heywood, when on 
their visit North; and we had a glorious 
time, one that will not soon be forgotten by
us, and I hope we will try and put in
practice the instructions we then received,
and may they live long to comfort and
encourage the sisters in their labors,for they
are growing more important and arduous
every year, and if every sister only real-
ized the importance of the mission we all
have to fill, there would be less slothfulness,
less caring for the things of the world.
The Sisters of the Latter-day Saints would
read the revelations and study the princi-
ples of the Gospel more and strive to clothe
the mind with intelligence and fit and
prepare themselves for the great and im-
portant duties that await all who will strive
to live the lives of Latter-day Saints. May
God bless all who are working for the ad-
vancement of WOMAN.
	Your sister and Co-worker in the Gospel.
