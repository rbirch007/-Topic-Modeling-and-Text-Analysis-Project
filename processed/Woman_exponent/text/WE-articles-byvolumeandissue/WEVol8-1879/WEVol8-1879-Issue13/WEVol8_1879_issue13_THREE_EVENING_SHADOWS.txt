THREE EVENING SHADOWS.

Creeping down, creeping down,
Shadows with a golden crown;
Shadows in the valley lying,
Gold upon the mountain dying;
Creeping down, gently down,
Shadows grey, with golden crown.

'Tis thus the shades of evenin fall
In youth; when Trustfulness o'er all
A tender beauty throws;
Holding without its trembling veil,
Dark hours that droop, with sorrow pale,
With tears, vain hopes, and woes.

Falling down, falling down,
Shadows with a sable crown;
Shadows on the heart and valley,
Clouds on the hills in blackness rally,
Falling down. dimly down,
Shadows deep, with death-dark crown.

Thus, when the veil is rent apart,
And trouble gathers round the heart,
And the pale hours press on;
Each with its weary weight of pain,
Till doubt and anguish cloud the brain,
And trust and peace are gone.

Falling down, gently down,
Shadows with a snowy crown;
Trembling hands are weaving now,
Flowers pale for painless brow.
Shadows 'round death's solemn bier,
Rest to the heart so troubled here.
Thus, when the ills of life are past,
And that far shore is won at last,
Where shadows never fall;
And many are the eyes that weep,
Watching across life's stormy deep,
Waiting the Angel's call.
