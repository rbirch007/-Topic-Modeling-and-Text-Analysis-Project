HOME AFFAIRS.

	Sisters Eliza R. Snow, M. I. Horne and
Zina D. Young visited Santaquin Friday Nov.
14th and met with the Relief Society and ad-
dressed them in their Annual meeting which
was largely attended. Saturday at 10, o'clock
an Annual Conference of the Primary Associa-
tion, afterwards a picnic dinner at 12 o'clock
and dance in the afternoon. The affair was
specially enjoyable. On the following morn-
ing, Sunday the party reached Goshen in time
to attend the Sabbath School and attended the
ward meeting in the afternoon. Monday there
was a meeting of the Relief Society at 10
o'clock largely attended, also of the Primary
Association at 2 o'clock, Young Ladies and a
general meeting in the evening. Tuesday
morning the party went over to Payson held
three meetings there; At one o'clock P. M,
Relief Society at half past four o'clock with
the Primary Association Young Ladies and
general attendance in the evening.
	Wednesday the party visited Salem and
met with the Relief Society at 10 o'clock A.
M. Afternoon Sisters Horne and Young went
to Spanish Fork to fill an appointment and
were joined there by Sister M. T. Smooth.
Sister Snow remained and organized a Prim-
ary Association at Salem. The next morning
Sister Snow joined the party at Spanish Fork
where they held three meetings which were
most numerously attended. The Primary
Association at Spanish Fork numbers over
three hundred children. From Spanish Fork
the sisters went on and visited Springville
where they held two meetings. Friday the
sisters came on to Lehi, where Sisters Snow
and Horne met with the brethren and sisters
and also the Primary Associationsn Satur-
day morning. Sister Zina Young returned to
Salt Lake Friday evening; Sisters Snow and
Horne on Saturday evening. Everwhere the
ladies were received cordially, and the breth-
ren seemed to enjoy the Sisters' meetings and
hearing the ladies speak quite as much as the
sisters.
