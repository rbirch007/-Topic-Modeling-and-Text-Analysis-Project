LITERARY AND PRACTICAL.

	The Young Ladies' Mutual Improvement
Associa[-]ion of Spanish Fork was organized
March 31, 1875, with seventeen members,
including officers; it now numbers one hun-
dred and fifty-one. We have a weekly
manuscript paper, called "The Young La-
dies' Herald of Union," which was estab-
lished with the organization of the Society,
treating upon a variety of subjects, princi-
pally theological, interspersed with poetry,
original and selected, changing its editors
each week.
	We have seventy bushels of wheat that
was gleaned by the young ladies of the
Association; have donated fifty-eight dol-
lars to missionaries, and sixty-five dollars
to Temple fund.
	Our Society and the Y. M. I. A. of this
place hold monthly conjoint meetings, also
have a small library in common.
