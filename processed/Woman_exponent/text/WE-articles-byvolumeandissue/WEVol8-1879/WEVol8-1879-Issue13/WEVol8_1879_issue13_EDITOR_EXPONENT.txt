EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Thinking a few items from Willard
would be interesting to your numerous read-
ers I send the following. The sisters in
general are alive to their duties always be-
ing ready to respond to any call that is
made on them, they are storing grain and
building a Granary, and Meeting Hall,
which are nearly finished. Our President
Sister Mary Ann Hubbard truly magnifies
her office always being full of faith and
good works and has the love and confidence
of all her sisters. On Aug. 12, being the 
anniversay of her sixty-third birthday, the
sisters got up a surprise party to celebrate
the day. At one o'clock the committee of
arrangements went to the residence of Bro.

[Column 2]

Charles W. Hubbard and invited Brother
and Sister Hubbard to accompany them to
the school house where their family and a 
great number of the brethren and sisters
had assembled in the lower room to receive
them. After being seated a song of wel-
come was sung, prayer by Bishop G. W.
Ward and singing again. A beautiful
wreath of hair flowers taken from the heads
of her family had been made unknown to 
her and was presented by seven young
ladies, afterwards short speeches, toasts,
and singing &, etc. At four o'clock the 
company adjourned to the upper room
which was tastefully decorated with mot-
toes and pictures; and above all the tables
were richly spread with the bounties of life.
The company of about one hundred sat
down and did ample justice to the feast.
Short speeches,toasts, songs, and recitations
were kept up until about seven o'clock
when the company was dismissed with
prayer, every one grateful for the enjoy-
ment they had had in each others society.
	HANNAH COOK,
Secretary.
