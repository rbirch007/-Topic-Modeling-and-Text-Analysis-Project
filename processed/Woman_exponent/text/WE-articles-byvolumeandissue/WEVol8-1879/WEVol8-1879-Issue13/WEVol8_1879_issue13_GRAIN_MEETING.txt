GRAIN MEETING.

	The Woman's Annual Territorial Grain
Meeting was held in the Council House in
this city Saturday, Nov. 15, at two o'clock
P. M., Mrs. E. B. Wells presiding. meet-
ing opened by singing; prayer by Mrs.
Phebe Woodruff; singing again. Then the
report of last meeting was read by the Sec-
retary, Mrs. Elizabeth Howard. After the
minutes were accepted, the Secretary read
the reports from various parts of the Terri-
tory, gave an account of the work accom-
plished by the sisters in the storing of grain
and building granaries. The meeting was
devoted specially to business, suggestions
and remarks relative to the work. The
thoughts of the sisters seemed to concen-
trate upon the movement, and many things
were said which will no doubt tend to in-
crease the interest already awakened upon
this subject. The meeting was addressed
by Sisters E. B. Wells, S. McGregor, S. M.
Kimball, B. W. Smith, E. H. Goddard,
Presendia Kimball, Anna Savage, Phebe
Woodruff, S. M. Heywood, H. T. King, So-
phia Taylor Nuttall of Kane Co. Closed
with singing, "The Spirit of God like a
fire is burning." Prayer by Mrs. Presendia
Kimball.					E. HOWARD.

	As the reports have not all been received,
the general report for the year cannot yet
be made. There have been a great many
granaries and bins built by the sisters in
various parts of the Territory. We shall
be glad to receive reports as early as possi-
ble from all who have not yet reported.
							E. B. W.
