CHESTER SANPETE Co., Aug. 3, 1879,
DEAR EDITOR;
	As I was reading a letter from Nellie in
the far off golden state of California I could
not help but be struck with the comparison
between her home, surrounded with vice
and iniquity and our little peaceful vale sur-
rounded with joy and happiness.
	The morning is beautiful. There is no
sound of noisy vehicles and passing feet but
all around is quiet. The little meadow lark
chirps out her sweet tune as a herald to the
Sabbath morning. The robin and the red-
breast harmoniously join their mat in song
with hers making a most delightful chorus.
My six little ones cluster round me, for the
morning kiss, ere their departure for the
Sabbath school; And as I look at their
beaming faces and gladsome smiles I cannot
help but think I am indeed blest, and not
all the riches in the world could entice me
away from my humble but happy home.
Here are taught principles of virtue and
truth; and the golden rule is the motto of
this little village. There is no swearing,
thieving, murdering, &c. going on here and
I feel to thank my heavenly Father for the
privilege of being gathered with the saints
in this vale of peace and rest. Long may
the sisters of the Latter day saints live to
bless the hour of the revealing of the gospel
to the prophet Joseph. Success to your pa-
per, may it live long to cheer and bless the
sisters of Zion is the prayer of your sister.
