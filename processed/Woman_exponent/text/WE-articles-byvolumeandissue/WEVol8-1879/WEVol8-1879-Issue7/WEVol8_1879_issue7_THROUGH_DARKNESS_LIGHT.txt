THROUGH DARKNESS, LIGHT.

I wonder what's amiss this morning—
	Where has the light of summer fled?
It seems a week since early dawning—
	A month since yesterday was sped.

Time's leaden feet, that move so slowly,
	Are not more heavy now than mine;
These busy hands lie limp and lowly,
	As under wizard spell malign.

A dread, chill gloom broods o'er my spirit,
	A sense of loss and emptiness—
Not agony, but something enar it—
	A joy in sorrow's sable dress.

The fount of tears is dry and burning,
	My hear almost too numb to ache-
Had I a dream before the morning,
	From which I do not dare to wake?

It does not seem a dream of sorrow,
	But rather joy I may not know-
Enshrined in some too distant morrow,
	Or yesterday too long ago.

And through it baby eyes were glancing,
	So violet dark, so melting blue,
And silken baby locks all dancing,
	As summer airs breathed softly through.

Oh speaking eyes! Oh, murmured music,
	From baby lips so rosy sweet!
Oh, half unconscious, soft endearments,
	How charmed they hours, alas! too fleet.

Oh, sheen above those shining tresses,
	Like aureoles by angels worn!
Oh, dimpled baby loveliness,
	Which I too fondly dreamed my own!

A shining angel, won from heaven,
	Enshrined in loveliest human mould,
To motherhood and me was given—
	A gift too rare for earth to hold.

How empty now these arms which bore it;
	How bleak this world in which it smiled;
How heavy now this heart which wore it—
	Oh, tender Christ, give back my child!

Nay, little one, I dare not murmur
	That God loves you as well as I,
But that he lent you one brief summer,
	I will give thanks until I die;

For, love, it made you mine forever—
	How great reward for little grief!
Eternity all ours together,
	Rejoice, my heart, that time is brief!
