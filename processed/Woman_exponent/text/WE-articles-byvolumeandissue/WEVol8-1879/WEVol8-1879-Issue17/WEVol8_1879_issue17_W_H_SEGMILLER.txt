MRS Duniway says: Twenty-five years
ago it would not have been possible for any
woman to become the principal in a public
school; and any woman who presumed to
aspire to such a position was as loftily
sneered at by silly and ignorant opponents
of woman's enfranchisement, which alone
opened the way, as she herself now affects
to sneer at women who are removing fur-
ther obstacles in their way.

	WASHINGTON, 23.—Mrs. Wallace, of In-
diana; Lucinda B. Chandler, Pennsylvania;
Susan B. Anthony, and other delegates to
the Woman's Suffrage Association, made
arguments to-day before the Senate com-
mittee, in. favor of the Sixteenth Amend-
ment to the Constitution, to enfranchise
woman.
