NOTES AND NEWS.

	THE National Woman Suffrage Association
will hold its Twelfth Annual Convention
in Masonic Hall, Corner of Washington and
Tennessee Sts., Indinapolis, Ind., Tuesday
and Wednesday, May 25th and 26th,1880.
	All letters and contributions for the Con-
vention should be addressed to Mrs. May
Wright Thompson Sec. E. S. Society,561. N.
Alabama St. Indianapolis Ind.
	Notice of preliminary meetings will be
given hereafter,
	On behalf of the National Woman Suffrage
Association.
MATILDA JOSLYN GAGE,Chairm'n Ex. Com.
SUSAN B. ANTHONY, Vice-Pres't-at-Large.
SARA ANDREWS SPENCER, Cor. Secretary.


REPORTS from the various branches in the
Scandinavian Mission are very favorable.
Relief Societies and Young Men's Improve-
ment Associations are now organized in the
various conferences. Our Sunday mission-
aries, who are appointed to go from family
to family with pamphlets and invite the
people to our meetings,have heen very suc-
cessful; and the sisters have been doing
good in the same labor. The Relief Society
has truly been a blessing this winter to the
sick, the poor and needy.—Millennial Star.

	THE Home Dramatic Club are preparing
to present the new play of "Ours."
