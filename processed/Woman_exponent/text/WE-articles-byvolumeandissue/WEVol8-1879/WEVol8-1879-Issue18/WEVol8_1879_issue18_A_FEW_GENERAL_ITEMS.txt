	A FEW GENERAL ITEMS.

	Condensing from Quarterly Reports, we find
that each and every Association, feels a worthy
pride and interest, in its own and Sister Asso-
ciations, and sincere  love  for  the  Church of
Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints at large; and
a desire  to  improve, progress  and  become a
united and solid band of  laborers in the cause
of truth and right, after the pattern afforded by
their worthy and revered leaders.
	Their general exercised have  been nearly
similar, consisting of prayer, singing, addresses;
readings, recitations,  (select  and  original)
poems,  religious, historical,  biographical, and
and other standard literature.
	Their labors have been such as their locality
admitted, some directing  their  attention  to
mulberry planting, and silk  culture; some have
labored as far as able in the interest  of  grain,
storing; others have made large donations to
the Temple Fund, the Emigration  Fund, or
sent means to our Missionaries abroad.  Many
have aided the poor of their Wards. assisted in
building Ward Meeting Houses  or  Relief  So-
ciety Halls; paid tuition  of  orphan children,
furnished Association libraries for themselves,
subscribed  for different  Church  papers and
books; besides several of them editing  maun-
script Association papers.		A. J. C.
