A FEW OF MY THOUGHTS.
			    -----
	What are women thought of?  I think
sometimes very little; but when I contem-
plate  what they have to pass through, I
think them  noble;  and  when  they  stand
firm to their calling and office in this king-
dom there is a great and mighty work to
do. It is their special work to raise up their
family in the fear of the Lord, and not only
to teach them, but to set an example worthy
of imitation, for they watch those who are
set to lead them;  therefore  they should be
a light unto  them.  It is a difficult  thing,
with the many besetments surrounding us,
to train up our children in the way they
should go; but we must perservere and do
our part, and those who will not be obedient
must suffer the consequences.
	I think and realize the Primary Associa-
tion is doing much good in this place; there
are  many  truly  noble  spirits among the
younger class, who have a desire for spir-
itual food, and they do enjoy their meet-
ings; and the officers are learning as well
as the little ones—we find there can be
much learned from children; and when we
are doing good to others we are doing good
to ourselves.
	My dear sisters, how thankful we ought
to be that we are honored to live in this day
and age, when we can raise our voices and
proclaim the principles of the Gospel, and
when our words may be published to the
nations of the earth.  We can do this be-
cause we know this is the work of God.
This Anti-Polygamy movement will do us
more good than harm; the Gentile ladies
do not know they are spreading the Gospel
and the principle of Celestial Marriage will,
not die away as they think it will.
	I want to say a word or two about danc-
ing.  The young people engage in that
pastime too much, it seems to me, when
they might be improving their minds on
Gospel truths.  Our young folks  should
have recreation, but not indulge in it to
excess: for I think it is not pleasing in the
sight of the Lord.
Goshen.                    SARAH ROBERTS.
