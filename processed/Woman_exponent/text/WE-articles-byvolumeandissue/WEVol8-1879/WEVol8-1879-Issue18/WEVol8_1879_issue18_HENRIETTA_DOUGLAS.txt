					HENRIETTA DOUGLAS.

DIED suddenly, at Kanarra, Kane Co., Feb.
1, 1880, after a three months illness, Lauraette


Parker, fourth daughter of John D. and Sophia
Roundy  Parker, aged fifteen years.  Her sick-
ness was supposed to be  the  result  of  taking
cold, which settled in her eye and throat.  She
was considered much better, ate  hearty din-
ner, and within  half  an  hour after  her  spirit
had taken its flight.
 She was calm  and composed  through all her
sickness,  and  was,  indeed, possessed  of  that
sterling integrity and virtue, that  really  char-
acterizes the Saint of God.  She was a faithful,
loving daughter to her parents, kind and affec-
tionate to  her brothers  and  sisters, steadfast
and true, to her young associates,  and beloved
by  all who knew her.
	May God, the Eternal  Father, comfort her
bereaved parents and friend, and may her sis-
terd and young companions  emulate her vir-
tues that it may also be well with them.
Lauraette  is  not  dead,  but  gone  before, and
her works do follow her.
