NOTES AND NEWS.

	NEVER has one person forgotten his pure,
right-educating mother. On the blue
mountains of our dim childhood, toward
which we ever turn and look, stand the
mothers who marked out to us from thence
our life.

	SIR ROWLAND Hill had a stately funeral
in Westminister Abbey the other day.
After the ceremonies were over the Abbey
was for a long time crowded with visitors,
some of them poor people, come, as a poor
little street boy said, "To see the grave of
Sir Rowland Hill, who made the first penny
postage."

	PLUCKY WOMEN.—The above is the title
of a lecture now ready to be delivered by
Prof. Sheppard, of Alleghany College. The
lecture has been heard with great approval,
and both it and the speaker are highly
praised by Francis E. Willard, who thinks
this lecture well adapted to help women to
break their own fetters. His agent is
George H. Drew, 120 Tremont street, Bos-
ton.							L. S.

	THE Annual Convention of the New York
State Woman's Suffeage Association will be
held in ASSOCIATION HALL, Poughkeepsie,
Friday, October 24th.
	Among the speakers will be Lillie Deve-
reaux Blake, Helen M. Slocum, Clemence
S. Lozier, M. D., Amanda Deyo and Matilda
Joslyn Gage.
	A convention will also be held in New
York city about Oct. 28, for the purpose of 
ratifying the nomination of those candi-
dates who have expressed themselves as
favoring Woman Suffrage.
