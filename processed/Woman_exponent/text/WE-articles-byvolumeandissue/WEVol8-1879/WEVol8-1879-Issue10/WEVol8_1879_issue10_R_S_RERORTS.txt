R. S. RERORTS.

	Relief Society report of the Salt Lake
Stake, for quarter ending Sept. 27, 1879.
Statistical: Teachers, 409; members, 1,815;
total officers and members, 2,283; meetings
held, 187; average attendance, 587; Church
papers taken, 144; WOMAN'S EXPONENT
342. Financial; On hand at last report,
cash, $587.85, property, $6,116.37; receipts
for quarter, cash, $545.97, property, $357.01;
Disbursed, to needy, cash, $377.47, proper-
ty, $217.44; emigration, cash $22.15; Tem-
ple, cash, $31.95, property, $40.86; home
industries, $185; books, $38.90; Missionary,
$12.75; building, cash, $55.25, property,
22.05; on hand at date, cash, $410.35, pro-
perty, $6,193.03; 2463 bushels of wheat,
and 1,600 lbs of flour. The following wards
not reported; 3d, 4th, 10th, East Mill Creek,
Granite and Mountain Deil; no Relief So-
ciety in the 11th Ward.
	Report of Quarterly Conference, convened
in the 14th Ward Assembly Rooms, Friday
morning, Sept. 26, 1879. On the stand
were President M. I. Horne, her counselors,
Mrs. E. S. Taylor and Mrs. S. M. Heywood,
and Eliza R. Snow. Zina D. Young, Anna
Ivins, B. W. Smith, P. Woodruff, E. B.
Wells, S. M. Kimball, and many other dis-
tinguished ladies. Music and singing con-
ducted by Miss Maggie Meads. Opening
prayer offered by Mrs. Zina D. Young.
	After singing, Secretary Mrs. E. Howard
read the minutes of last Conference. Re-
ports were then read from the following
wards and branches; 1st, 2d, 4th, 5th, 6th,
7th, 8th, 9th, 12th, 13th, 14th, 15th, 16th,
17th, 18th, 19th, 20th, 21st, Farmer's Sugar
House, Mill Creek, Big Cottonwood, South
Cottonwood, Union, Fort Herriman, South
Jordan, West Jordan, North Jordan, Plea-
sant Green, Draper and Brighton.
	Afternoon Session: Pres. Mrs. Ho[-]ne
called on the presidents to come to the
stand and represent their respective socie-
ties; nearly all responded. Conference ad-
journed till Saturday, 27th inst, at 2 a. m.
	Saturday morning, Sept. 27, occupied by
the Young Ladies' Mutual Improvement
Associations.
	Saturday afternoon: After opening ser-
vices, reports were read from the Primary
Associations of the 2d, 10th, 11th, 12th,
13th, 17th, 19th, and 20th Wards by Miss
Maggie Freeze. Some of the presidents
of the Primaries reported verbally of the
success they are meeting with in gathering
the children together, and feel assured of
the lasting benefit that will result from
these organizations. After a few remarks
from Mrs. Horne, Elders J. E. Taylor and
A. M. Cannon addressed the congregation
in an interesting and instructive manner.
	Mrs. M. I. Horne was set apart as Presi-
dent of the Relief Societies of Salt Lake
Stake of Zion by Elder A. M. Cannon, Mrs.
Elmina S. Taylor as First Counselor by
Elder J. E. Taylor, Mrs. Sarepta M. Hey-
wood as Second Counselor by Elder A. M.
Cannon, and Mrs. Elizabeth Howard as
Secretary by Elder J. E. Taylor.
	Conference adjourned until the 21st and
22d of December, 1879, which will be the
Annual Conference, and Secretaries will
please govern themselves accordingly.
				E. HOWARD, Sec'y.

	Minuets of the quarterly Conference of
the Relief Societies of Utah Co. Held in
Provo City August 2, in the first Ward
School House. Pres't. Mrs. M. T. Smoot,
presiding, with the different officers of the
board. Meeting opened by singing and
prayer as usual. Reports were read by

[Column 2]
the Sec'y. after which a few remarks were
made by Sister M. T. Smoot commending
the sisters for their diligence, and the spirit
of unity that exists among the sisters of the
various Societies. Felt thankful to be in
the midst of tho daughters of Zion, and be
called a handmaiden of the Lord. Ex-
horted the sisters to hold each others' chara-
cters sacred and ever speak of one another
the best we can. Counseled the sisters on
home industries and various other matters;
after which the Presidents gave verbal re-
ports of the well doing of those over whom
they presided.
	After singing and prayer, meeting ad-
journed till 2 o'clock p. m.
Afternoon Session.—The Presidents who
did not give in their reports in the fore-
noon were called upon to do so. We
had a very enjoyable time and we could
truly say the Lord was in our midst.
	After the Pres'ts. had given in their ver-
bal reports a great many sisters bore their
testimonies to the truth of this work, and
spoke of the goodness of God which had
been manifested towards us as a people
and we all felt to rejoice in the name of
Israel's God.
