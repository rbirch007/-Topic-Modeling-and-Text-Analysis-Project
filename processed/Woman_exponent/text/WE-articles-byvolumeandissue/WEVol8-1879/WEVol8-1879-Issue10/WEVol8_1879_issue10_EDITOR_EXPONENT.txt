EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Our Relief Society is in a flourishing condi-
tion, and has been a positive relief and bless-
ing to our little place. At the County Fair
held here in August, the sisters' work was well
represented, both useful and ornamental; also
a fair specimen of silk in all its stages, from
the worm to the skein.
	September 29, we were called upon to mourn
the loss of our beloved sister, Ann Highbee,
who departed this life. She was an exemplary
wife and mother, and a faithful Latter-day
Saint. She was one of the presiding officers in
our Society for some years, also Secretary for
our store. Her loss is deeply regretted by all
her relatives and friends, but we know that
her loss is our gain.
	That the Lord will bless you and strengthen
you in your good work, is the wish of your
sisters,
		ELIZABETH KLIENMAN, Pres. R. S.
		ROSELIA SPILSBURY, Sec'y.
	Toquerville, Oct. 7, 1879.
