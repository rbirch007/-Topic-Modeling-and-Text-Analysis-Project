IN MEMORIAM.

"A lovely bud, so sweet and fair?
	Called hence by early doom,
Just sent to show how sweet a flower,
	In paradise would bloom."

	DIED, in Salt Lake City, May 16, 1879,
Franklin J. Alder, beloved son of George A.
and Lydia Dunford Alder, and grandson of
Bishop George Dunford: and Mrs. Sarah Dun-


ford. Deceased was born in this city, Oct. 8,
1877; he was one year, seven months and eight
days old. Frankie was an exceedingly inter-
esting child, possessing in a large degree those
affectional attributes that so closely entwine
themselves around the chords of the human
heart, and when called to part with the dear
ones, it tears asunder the very heart-rtrings, leav
ing an utter loneliness and desolation in the
heart and in the home.

	Lorenzo-Maeser Richards, only child of Lo-
renzo M. and Mamie Dunford Richards, died
at Ogden Sept. 18, 1879, aged two years and
thirteen days. Deceased was born in Ogden,
Sept. 5, 1877, and was the grandson of Apostle
F, D Richards and Mrs. Jane S. Richards, of
Ogden, and of Bishop George Dunford and
Mrs. Sarah Dunford of this city. Maeser was
tenderly beloved, he had endeared himself to
every heart, and his death cast a gloom over
all the household that only time and the Holy
Spirit can dispel. May our Heavenly Father,
who is full of compassion, heal up the wound-
ed hearts and give them faith and courage to
say, "Thy will, O Lord, not mine be done."
