THE DAUGHTER OF ZION.

The daughter of Zion is chasten’d, 
	Her sons are brought low in the dust— 
The Lord will confound her oppressor,
	And avenge her deep wrongs, for he must.
We trust in His arm of salvation,
	We bow to His will every day;
Will he leave us to perish unnoticed?
	Ey’ry power of our being says, Nay.

Nay, nay! we believe that out Father
	Has knowledge rnorp perfect than they;
Nay, nay! we believe that our Father
	Can match with the wisest each day.
We've honor'd and bow’d to His mandate,
	Forsaken our countries, our homes;
And stricken we wait for His coming,
	And hunted as lion that roams.

Crowd not the strong limb to the wall,
	it may reach you, 'though guarded and strong;
Despair his a weapon in nature—
	Chafe not the proud lion too long.

We always sustain our file-leaders,
	Always cling to the iron rod;
Are willing to work for Christ’s kingdom,
	Our holy religion, our God.

The more that they work, against Zion,
	The more will her glory appear;
The more that they seek to oppress her,
	The sooner redemption is here.

The key of knowledge now is turn'd,
That for which our hearts have burn'd.—
Not to us, but to the Father,
And His holy Son forever, 
Do the glory aud the power, 
For tbp triumph of the hour.
									A. Q.
7th Ward, S. L. City, May, 1879.
