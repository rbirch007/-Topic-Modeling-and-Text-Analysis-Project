LITERARY WORK FOR YOUNG
LADIES.
	WALLSBURG, June 23, 1879.
	Our Association was re-organized Feb. 7, with
the following officers: Lucina Boren, President,
Alice Cook and Julia Bigelow Counselors, Dora
Bigelow Secretary, Ada Glenn Treasurer. We
have edited one paper, “The Young Ladies’
Adviser.” It was edited by Izora Boren and
Ada Glenn. We intend to issue it monthly.
The articles arranged as follows: Introductory,
Summer, Improvement, Kindness, Faith,Hope
Charity, Improve Your Talent, Slang Talk, Be
Kind to the Poor, Obedience, Backbiting,
Home,Remember Our Sisters That are Sick,Do
Right, Truth, Honor and Always be Kind.
Contributors, Lucina Boren, Alice Cook, Dora
Bigelow, Ada Glenn, Izora Boren, Martha
Clifton, Louisa Kirbey, S. I. Boren.
	IZORA BOREN, Ass’t Sec’y.


﻿PRINCESS LOUISE contributes to the
Grosvenor Gallery a pretty statuette portrait
in terra cotta of her friend, Miss Violet
Lindsay. The figure is represented as seated
with the head turned slightly from the
spectator. The young lady holds a basket
in hor slender hands and two dainty slip-
pered feet peep out from under her gown.—

﻿QUEEN VICTORIA has just instituted a new
order which does her as much honor as its
recipients. It is to be bestowed upon those
trained female nurses who, by their skill
and conduct deserves high recognition, and
they will be called “St. Katharine’s Nurses.”
Each St. Katharine’s nurse will hold her
appointment for three years, receiving dur-
ing that time $250 a year in additton to the
salary paid her by the institution to which
she belongs, and will be permitted to wear
for life the royal badge or armlet of the
order.—Ex
