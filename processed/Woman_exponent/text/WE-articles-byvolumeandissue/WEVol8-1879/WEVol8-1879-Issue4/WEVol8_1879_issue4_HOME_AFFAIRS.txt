HOME AFFAIRS.

 MRS. ZINA D. YOUNG and Miss Susa Young 
left this city Tuesday July 1, for a short visit to 
San Francisco and the Sandwich Islands. A
note from Mrs. Z.D. Young, received on the 
10th inst., informs us that they embarked on
board a steamer from San Francisco July 7,
and were in good health and spirits and send
kind love to friends. We wish them a pleasant
and safe voyage, and trust Mrs. Young's health
will improve during her journey. The breth-
ren and sisters of the mission on the Sandwich 
Islands will no doubt give them a hearty wel-
come.
	SISTERS ELIZA R. Snow, M. Isabella Horne,
Sarah M. Kimball and Sarepta M. Heywood
left this city on the morning train Monday,
July 7, enroute for Bear Lake valley and Soda
Springs. They will visit settlements upon the
route and hold meetings, and wherever it is
practicable organize Primary Associations.
They expect to be in Paris at the Stake Con-
ference on the 16th and 17th of this month,
when it is expected a presidency of the Relief
Society and Y. L.M.I.A of that Stake of
Zion will be chosen. These sisters will be ab-
sent some weeks and intend to visit as many 
settlements as possible.
	A LITTLE book entitled "The Deseret Sun-
day School Reader," is before us. It has been
arranged with great care by a committe of the
Sunday School Union, and published at the
office of the "Juvenile Instructor." It con-
tains several woodcuts, including the Nauvoo
Temple. The lessons in poetry and prose are 
easy, the words short and simple. it is en-
tirely homemade, all the articles for the book 
furnished by well-known writers. It will sup-
ply a want long felt for a work of this kind,
and is deserving  the partronage of the Saints
in all parts of the Territory. This is the first
of a series of books which we trust will ere
long be produced at home for the benefit of the
children.
