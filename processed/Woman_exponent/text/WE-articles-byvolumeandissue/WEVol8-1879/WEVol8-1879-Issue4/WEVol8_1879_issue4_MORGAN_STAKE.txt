MORGAN STAKE.

	The Third Quarterly Conference of the
Morgan Stake Relief Society convened at
Morgan City on the 24th of April, 1879, at
10 a. m., Pres. Lydia Rich presiding.
There were present of the presiding priest-
hood of the Stake, Pres. Willard G. Smith
and Counselors Richard Fry and Samuel
Francis; also a good representation of the
presiding priesthood of the various wards.
The nine societies of the Stake were all
represented by their Presidents, or other
officers, as being in good condition spirit-
ually and temporally, there being an in-
creasing interest manifested among the
sisters in attending to their meetings and
other duties. The grasshoppers are very
numerous here this season and threaten to
destroy our crops, which makes the sisters
feel to engage as much as possible in va-
rious pursuits of home industry, thus econo-
mizing what they have and making many
of the things they need. They also feel
much interested in the spiritual work of
the Lord. There were many faithful testi-
monies borne to the truth of the Latter-day
work in which we are engaged, by the sis-
ters who spoke, and the Spirit of the Lord
was with us. The subjects mostly dwelt
upon were Co-operation, Home Industries,
United Order, Faith, Charity, our duties
one toward the other, and the principle of
Celestial Marriage.
	Our brethren present gave us much valu-
able instruction concerning our duties and
positions. They also made many strength-
ening and encouraging remarks which were
highly appreciated by us.
	Our Statistical and Financial report is as
follows for the quarter ending April 10,
1879:
	Statistical: Teachers, 39; members, 214;
officers and members, 253; meetings held
last quarter, 58; average attendance, 114;
WOMAN'S EXPONENT taken, 32.
	Financial: On hand January 10, 1879,
in cash, $62 45; in property, $1,048 64;
wheat, 592 bu., 53 lb. Receipts last quarter,
cash, $32 91; property, 65 22; wheat,
28 bu. 24 lb. Disbursements: To the poor,
in cash and property, $43 85; Temple,
$17 25; home industries, $17 64; books,
$6 26; missionaries, $7 40; building $94 40.

	Annual report of the Mt. Pleasant Relief
Society, from May 11, 1878, to May 11,
1879: Donations for poor, $174 56; dis-
bursements to the poor, $169; donations to
Welsh emigration, $20; to the European
mission, $30; in the Treasury, sundry arti-
cles for poor, $21 70; cash, $5 40; stock in
Co-op institutions, $50 73; total, $691 59.
Wheat stored away, 419 bu., 14lb.
	The sisters here are very willing, and I
feel to encourage them; and may God bless
them and give them a double portion of
His Holy Spirit, that they may gain wis-
dom, knowledge and understanding to as-
sist them in the performance of so great
and so glorious a cause.
	It is truly gratifying to be able to give a
good report of our young ladies; they are
really taking a lively interest in their
meetings, and by their energy and perse-
verance are accomplishing much good, and
the more they exercise the ability God
has given them the better they will be
able to convey their thoughts to each other,
and by prayerfulness and humility their
understandings will become enlarged, so
that they can more fully comprehend the
great and important work that is before
them:
	We also have a meeting every Thursday
for the juveniles, who appear to be quite
elated at the prospect of soon being organ-
ized into an Association of their own. May
God bless the dear children and all those
who take an interest in trying to encourage
them and do them good.
	Our agent for the Silk Association is tak-
ing quite an interest in her labors, and in-
tends, by energy and perseverance to make
it a success.
	May our Heavenly Father bless us in our
labors of love in which we are engaged,
that it may be like bread cast upon the wa-
ters—be found after many days—is the
prayer of your sister in the New and Ever-
lasting Covenant,
	MARGARET F. C. MORRISON, Pres't,
