WOMAN'S VOICE.

	Sisters, we are called to a noble and great
work. Do we appreciate the privileges
that are given us by the Almighty, to lead
out with our husbands in this great work
of reformation? Do we realize the respon-
sibility resting upon mothers towards their
children, to train their young and tender
minds to righteousness and peace, that
when they come to maturity they will not
depart from it? Let us keep in the path of
righteousness, and seek to know the mind
and will of our Heavenly Father, and our
children, seeing our good works, will follow
in our footsteps, and be noble sons and
daughters in the kingdom of God. Let us
put away all malice, envy and pride, for all
these things separate us from the love of
God, which is spread abroad in our hearts.
As our father Abraham imputed righteous-
ness through faith and not the law, let us
go on unto perfection.
	We are the children of light and not of
darkness, doing not the things of the world,
but seeking to know the will of God and
doing it, for that is the work that is given
us to do in this our day. The time is fast
approaching when Zion will be fully estab-
lished "in the tops of the mountains," and
who will be ready to meet their Lord.
	Let us be faithful in all things in the
kingdom, that our joy may be full, and
that we may reign with our King, the Sa-
vior of the world.				M. P.


MRS. Gen. Banks takes an ardent interest
in woman suffrage. The General's daugh-
ters have both registered, and will vote,
and they are influencing the young women
of Waltham of their "set" to follow their
example.


A YOUNG lady just returned from board-
ing-school, being asked at table if she
would take more, replied, "By no means,
madam; gastronomical satiety admonishes
me that I have arrived at the ultimate of
deglutition consistent with the code of
Æsculapius."

	A COUNTRY woman stopped some ten
minutes in front of a story in Springfield to
gaze at a patent fly trap in operation, which
was pretty well filled, and after studying
the placard, $2, intently, moved on, after
piping out, to the great amusement of the
bystanders: "Tew dollars! I wouldn't give
tew cents for all the flies in Springfield."

	AN old Scotch lady, who had no relish
for modern church-music, was expressing
her dislike for the singing of an anthem in
her own church one day, when a neighbor
said, "Why, that is an old anthem. David
sang that anthem to Saul." To this the
old lady replied, "Weel, weel, I noo for the
first time understan' why Saul threw his
javelin at David when the lad sang for
him."
