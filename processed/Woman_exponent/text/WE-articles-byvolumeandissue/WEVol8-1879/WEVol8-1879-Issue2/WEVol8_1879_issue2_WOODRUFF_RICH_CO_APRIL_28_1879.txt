	WOODRUFF, RICH CO., April 28, 1879.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I am happy to forward you a satisfactory
report of the doings of the sisters at this 
place. Our Relief Society is in a flourish-
ing condition, considering the very adverse
circumstances with which we are surround-
ed. We are not so fortunate as a great
many of our sisters in older settlements
where the congeniality of the climate is
conducive to raising grain, sericulture, &c.;
we are in the center of a vast orchard of 
sagebrush, which seems determined to re-
sist all efforts at removal, still the brethren
are exercising every effort to raise grain,
and quite a large tract of this land is under
cultivation this year.
	The sisters deserve great praise for their 
indefatigable exertions to maintain their
place as a Society, and I am happy to state
their efforts have been crowned with suc-
cess. Not raising any grain here the atten-
tion of the brethren has been turned to
raising stock, and we have taken advantage
of it, in becoming possessed of calves, year-
lings, &c., to the number of twenty-five
head, which we propose to sell next fall
and buy wheat, that our granary may have
the appearance of plenty and a reign of 
peace.
	I feel to encourage the sisters in their 
efforts and to say, May God bless and pros-
per you in the good work, and attend
strictly to every requirement, and the Lord
will recognize in your works a desire to
love and serve Him; obey strictly the words
of wisdom, and your precept and example
will be sure of a reward. I feel to extend
this counsel to the sisters of other wards
similarly situated to us, as I know the value
and benefit of encouragement. And I feel,
in behalf of my sisters, to thank the EX-
PONENT for its marked encouragement and
counsel given from time to time, and the 
cheering words of comfort of which we
stand so much in need; and I feel like say-

ing, May our Heavenly Father bless you in 
the good work in which you are engaged,
that under good counsel and guidance the
Relief Societies may ere long become an
active and not a latent force in Zion, that
the result of their labors may be felt
throughout the length and breadth of the
land when Zion shall "arise and put on her
beautiful garments, and the glory of God
be made manifest throughout the earth."
	May God bless you and all my sisters, is 
the prayer of your sister in the New and
Everlasting Covenant,
