OBITUARY.

	During the past week quite a gloom has been
cast over the sisters here at Paris through the
death of Sister Matilda Price, wife of Bishop
Robert Price of the 2d Ward of this place. On
Sunday last she was in her usual health until
the evening of that day, when she was sud-
denly taken sick, and in less than twenty-four
hours expired, surrounded by her family and
many friends who deeply feel her loss.
	The funeral took place on Tuesday, when
Presidents C. C. Rich and Geo. Osmond gave
some very encouraging and comforting words
of instruction to the many friends who had as-
sembled to pay their last respects to the re-
mains of Sister Price—and also an infant son of
Bro. John T. Rich of this town, who was
buried at the same time.
	Sister Price was born on the 25th of March,
1833; embraced the Gospel in England about
1850; married in the fall of 1853, and in the
same year emigrated to Utah. She has been
identified with the Relief Society of this place
ever since it has been organized, and at the
time of her death held the office of President
of the Teachers of the 2d Ward Relief Society;
and, from the time of her embracing the Gos-
pel, has lived a good and faithful Latter-day
Saint.						E. M. S.
