IN MEMORIAM.

	DIED, at Clarkston, Cache Co., U.T., on the
17th of July, 1879, of cold and inflammation of
the lungs, Levisa Eveline, youngest daughter of
Edmund and Susan Eveline Greene Homer.
She was born the 22d of July, 1876, at Spring-
dale, Kane Co., Utah.
	Oh! fair, sweet flower of southern birth,
	Too delicate and frail for earth,
	Though lost a moment to our sight,
	We'll find thee beauteous till, and bright.
	Clothed in immortal life and light.
		LULA.
	DIED, at Vernon, Tooele Co., June 8th,
in childbirth, Ada Augusta Dawson Rendall,
wife of Wm. Durant, deeply regretted.
	Sister Ada was ever ready to help in teach-
ing the little ones, making the Sunday School
interesting; she was Secretary of the Y. L. M.
I. A. at Fairfield, where she formerly resided;
was ordained Secretary of the Relief Society
here, and always ready to do good—her great-
est pleasure seeming to be in training the
young. Her purity of thought and language
were worthy of imitation. Beloved and re-
spected she has gone to her rest, leaving a
husband and three little boys (the youngest
but four hours old), and many friends to mourn
her loss.
	She was born at St. Johns, Newfoundland,
March 9th, 1845.
	PROVO, June 22d, 1879.
MRS. WELLS:
	Dear Sister:—I thought I would send you a
few lines concerning Sister Charlotte Hume
Clive, recently deceased, as I have been
acquainted with her personally ever since the
year 1851, and if she had been my own sister
in the flesh I could not have thought more of
her. She came to Salt Lake City in the year
1852, and shortly after her husband took an-
other wife. She was ever willing to bear her
part under all circumstances that she had to
pass through, and honored the principle of
plural marriage. She was a kind mother and
an affectionate wife. She has left a son and a
large circle of friends to mourn her loss.
	Your Sister in the Gospel,
