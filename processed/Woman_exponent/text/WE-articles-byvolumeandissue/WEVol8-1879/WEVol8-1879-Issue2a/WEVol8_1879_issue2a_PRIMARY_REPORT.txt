PRIMARY REPORT
	Report of the  12th, Ward  Primary As-
sociation Mrs.  Ellen  Clawson  President.
Our interesting little Association  is still in
progress.  Average attendance over  thirty
the majority of whom are very young child-
ren,  therefore  advancement  is  somewhat
slow but  steady.  Many  of  them  manifest
considerable self-possession when called up-
on to open and dismiss meetings &c.
	Recitations, select  readings,  essays &c.,
are carried on  in  good style,  interspersed
with short  lectures,  selections  from  good
books  and questions  on the same, from the
President and her counselers.
	Our contributions  to the Temple  for the
last two terms have  been  so small  that our
President deemed it wiser to wait until the
annual meeting in April  before  handing it
in.  The  amount  on  hand  to date,  is
seven dollars and ninty cent.
	We cordially  invite  our friends here  to
visit us by way of encouragement.
		Secretary, Master Samuel Barratt.
			Per. Assistant, Edna Clawson.
	March 19, 1880.
