PROGRAMME OF CONCERT.
	The Primary Association of Farmington
gave a Concert at the Meeting House March
6, 1880, Commencing at 7, o'clock P.M


Prayer,  by  Bishop J. W.  Hess; Opening
Song, "Songs of the heart,"by eight boys and
eight  girls; A welcome by Joseph Robinson;
Song, by S. J. Robinson and  E. Palmer;
Dialogue, The  Hardscrabble meeting, by
ten boys; Song, "Mother I have heard sweet
music," by Lucy and Mamie Robinson;
Recitation, by Hector Haight, entitled "Not
So Easy," Dialogue, The  Flower children,
by fifteen girls and three boys; Song, "Little
shoes and stockings," by Mary Haight and
Clara Leanard; Dialogue, "Great  Lives,"
by  six boys and six girls; Song, "Homeless
To-night," by O. Smith  and  Kate Cotterall;
Dialogue, "Kitty's Funeral," by eight little
girls; Music on the melodeon, by little Miss
Bourne; Song, S. J. Robinson; Dialogue,
"Out of Debt, and Out of Danger," by two
girls and three boys; Speech, by John Clark,
Dialogue, 1776 and 1876,  by Nathalia Miller
and Bell  Vanfleet;  Verse  by  Frank  Hess;
Closing song, by eight boys and eight girls;
Dismissed by prayer by Elder Arthur Stay-
ner.
"The number of  children  that took  part in
the Concert was, 63.
