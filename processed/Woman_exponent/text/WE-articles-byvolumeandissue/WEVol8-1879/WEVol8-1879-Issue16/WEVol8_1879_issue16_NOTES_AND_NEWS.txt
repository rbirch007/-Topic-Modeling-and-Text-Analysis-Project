NOTES AND NEWS

	WADE Hampton, Jr., is dead.

	MRS. Myra Clark Gaines has just lost by
death her only child, a daughter.

	THE highest number of votes received
by any candidate on the London School
Board was by Miss Muller, in Lambeth—
18,860.

	MRS. James, the widow of G. P. R.
James, the novelist, is living at Eau-Claire,
Wis. She is now a woman of eighty years,
and is cared for by her sons.

	THE eleventh annual meeting of the
Massachusetts Woman Suffrage Association
will be held in Wesleyan Hall, Bromfield
street, Boston, beginning on Wednesday
evening at 7½ o'clock, the 28th inst., and
continuing on Thursday afternoon at 2½
o'clock and evening at 7½ o'cleck. Thurs-
day morning at 10½ o'clock will be devoted
to business.
	The Suffrage clubs throughout the State
have done excellent work, and from each of
these a report of work and of plans is cor-
dially invited.
