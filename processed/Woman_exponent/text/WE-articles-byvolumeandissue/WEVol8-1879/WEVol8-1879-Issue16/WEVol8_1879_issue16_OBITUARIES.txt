OBITUARIES.

	DIED. At Rockville, Kane County Dec 18th,
1879. in childbirth, Maria De-Grey; beloved
wife of Bishop Chas. N. Smith Aged 39, years,
Deceased was a daughter of John and Maria
De Grey, born in Dudley, Worcestershire,
England, Nov. 21st 1840. became a member of 
the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day
Saints at the age of 12 years. Emigrated to
Utah, through the exertions of a widowed
mother, walking most of the distance, in 1857.
Adopted with gladness the holy order of
Celestial Marriage, and became the mother of
eleven children all now living to emulate the
virtues, and cherish the sacred memory of a
devoted Mother.
	Sister Maria was First Counselor in the
Rockville Relief Society, and ever cultivated
a profound regard for Zion and her institutions.
And although she was snatched as it were in
a moment from her family and friends in the
prime of womanhood, and a life of usefulness
so suddenly terminated, yet we will humbly
submit to the decree of Him who ordereth all
things well.						C. M. S.

	DIED, At Clifton Idaho Nov. 12th 1879.
After an illness of 18 hours Lucretia Maria


beloved wife of Wm. N. Marier sen., and
daughter of Samuel and Lydia Gates of Bing-
ham Fort, Weber Co. Utah. Deceased was
born Oct. 7th 1837. in Levan Wayne Co.
Michigae. She emigrated to Utah with her
parents in the year 1852; was the mother of
14 children of which 9 are left to mourn her
her loss. She became a member of the Church
of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints while
very young was a true and faithful wife a kind
and affectionate mother. She was First
Counselor in the Relief Society of Clifton and
was a live and active woman in the position
She died as she lived a faithful Latter-day.
Saint in the hopes of a glorious resurrection
with the just.
