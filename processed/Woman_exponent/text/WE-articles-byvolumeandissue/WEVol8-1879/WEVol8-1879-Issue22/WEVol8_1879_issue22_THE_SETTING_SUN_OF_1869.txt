THE SETTING SUN OF 1869.

In the West the sun is sinking,
	With a radiance grand, sublime;
And I'm thinking, sadly thinking,
	'Tis the last of sixty-nine.

On the mount his rays still linger,
	Loth, it seems, to say farewell,
But old Time's resistless finger
	Bids the parting words to tell.

Oh, how many hopes and pleasures
	He has seen on earth decay—
Radiant dreams and countless treasures,
	In the short year passed away

Bitter scenes of pain and sorrow,
	That would chill the strongest heart;
Souls that have no bright to-morrow,
	Pierced to deep by misery's dart.

Life, oh, life! how much of bitter
	In thy purest cup of joy!
Even thy brightest flowers wither-
	Sin and death will still destroy.

But the sun's last rays are lending
	To the vales a golden glow,
And with twilight beauties blending,
	They a heavenly power bestow;

And I hear the angels singing
	Of the great and glorious day;
Louder yet their anthems ringing,
	Christ the power of sin shall slay.

Haste, oh! haste the glorious beaming
	Of the great Millennial day,
When the sun in splendor teeming,
	Finds but joys that he or decay.

Written Dec. 31, 1869.	HOPE.
Sarah E. Rumel
