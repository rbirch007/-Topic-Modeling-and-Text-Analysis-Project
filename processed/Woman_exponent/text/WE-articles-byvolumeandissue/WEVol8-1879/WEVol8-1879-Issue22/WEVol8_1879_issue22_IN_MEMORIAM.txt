IN MEMORIAM.
	Harriet Fairbanks was the  daughter  of  Jo-
seph  Fairbanks  and  Polly  Brooks  Fairbanks
and was born March 28, 1819, at  Sandy Hill,
Washing[-]on on Co., New York. She moved to the
State  of New Jersey, Mead's  Basin [-]a[-]se near
Patterson, with her parents in early childhood.
She heard the Gospel and received its principles,
and was baptized in the  spring of 1842.  Soon
after and preparatory to  her  emigration  she
resided in New York City, and was married
there to Dr. Henry I.  Doremus  May 12,  1844.
In the Fall  of the same year with her husband
arrived at  Nauvoo;  where  she  shared  in  the
persecutions of the L. D. S., and  in  their  ex-
odus from that place, arriving in Winter Quar-
ters(Florence)in 1846.  In  the following Spring
commenced  journeying across the plains with
the first company  of Saints  who  followed the
Pioneers, and reached the  Valley on  the  first
day of Nov. 1847.  Ever since that time she had
resided  in  Salt  Lake  City.  Her death oc-
curred March 10, 1880.  Sister  Doremus  was
a faithful  Latter-day  Saint,  her  record  is
irreproachable.  She was the  mother  of  four
children, two of whom died in childhood.  One
son Abraham  F. and  one  daughter  Ann  now
the wife of Willard B.  Richards of Mendon are
still living.
	Dr. Doremus husband  of  the  deceased  is
widely and favorably known  among this com-
munity as one of our teachers  in day[-] gone by
in which profession  his  wife assisted him very
efficiently.
 She has gone before him, but her gentle man-
ner, her  amiable  deportment, and pure life
will live in the recollection of her family  and
friends  forever.  We  extend  our heartfe[--]
spmpathy to the bereaved husband and family.
