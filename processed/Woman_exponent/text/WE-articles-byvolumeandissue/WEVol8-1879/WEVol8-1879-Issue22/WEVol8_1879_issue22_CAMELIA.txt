	A great deal has been said  about wo-
man's mission," and yet the subject seems
inexhaustible. It is certain, that her indi-
vidualtiy is becoming more  distinct, more
clearly  defined.  Once she was content to
 stand behind her husband's chair, and serve
him at table, but now we find  her  out  in
the great  field of endeavor,  strengthening
her faculties like a man.
	We contend that  woman  was  not born,
merely to do the work between  four walls.
The office of  wife  and  mother  is  a  most
noble  one.  There  are  certain  duties  in
special circumstances of life which she can-
not  ignore;  but  why  confine  all  to  the
special sphere of taking care of home.  A
true woman-- especially one  who  is  ac-
quainted with the design of God in the last
days, and who understands so well the glory
of being a mother-- that woman, we expect,
will  never never neglect  her  family,  to obtain
public honor.
	A great many get this question  very
much mixed up.  We feel  a  sincere  pity
for those who  are  so  much  prejudiced  by
traditionary ideas,  they  cannot  treat it with
a clear unprejudiced mind.
	We are  acquainted  with certain gentle-
men,  whom  it  worries  very  much.  They
fear it is going to subtract  from  their  own
dignity, for women to hold office, or get in-
to very remunerative employment.  Superi-
ority is such a sweet  thing.  Some people
take and  suck it  like a  sugar  plum.  A
good  woman  will  give  honor  where it is
due; never fear that.
	Woman is not losing sight of the object of
her creation, when she steps forth  to engage
in pursuits  and  profesions  distinct  and
apart  from  home.  This is  a  momentous
age.  Art and science  are making  wonder-
ful strides.  Every  thing,  it  would  seem,
which can benefit and  interest  the human
family, is being developed, and improved.
Woman  cannot  help  feeling  the impulse
given to this genereation. It  is  the  Spirit
of God, and  woman  shares  the  influence.
She is bound to rise, and no human power
can stop her progress.
	What if sometimes the floor  goes unscrub
bed, or the knives will not vie in brightness
the looking glass. It would  not  hurt  to
delay some minor duties. Woman  should
of course, be wise  enough  to draw  a line,
between the important, and unimportant.
	Many a woman  stands  idly  looking  on.
She has so long  confined talent  and energy
within a  certain  "sphere,"  she  does not
realize how much  natural  force  God has
given her.
	Give woman a home: poor  woman!  She
has been brow beaten long enough: put her
in possession of all  the  happiness she was
created for: let her  reach  out after a more
intelligent life; there  is no  danger  of her
forgetting that she is a woman.
FLORA S. HILL.
Beaver.
