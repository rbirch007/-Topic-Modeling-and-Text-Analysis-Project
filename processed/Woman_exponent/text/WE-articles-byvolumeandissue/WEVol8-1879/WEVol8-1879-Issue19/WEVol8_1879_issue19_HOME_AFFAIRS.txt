HOME AFFAIRS.
	The Salt Lake Assembly Hall is being hand-
somely frescoed  by Morris & Co., painters, un-
der  the  direction  and  supervision  of Elder
Henry Grow, the designer and architect.  Over
the  stand  is  a picture  repre[-]enting  the  Bee
Hive,  the emblem of Deseret, embowered in
vines and flowers, on which the bees are feast-
ing; above the hive is the All-seeing eye watch-
ing over all.  The Nauvoo and Kirtland Tem-
ples are painted one on each side of the bee-
hive.  Next on the south side of the ceiling
are Joseph and Oliver  receiving  the  Aaronic
Priesthood from John the Baptist, which took
place on the banks of the Susquehanna  river,
in Pa.  Opposite, on the north side are Peter,
James and  John  bestowing  the Melchizedec
Priesthood upon Joseph, who is kneeling  be-
fore them.  The St. George and Manti Temple
on the south tide, and the Salt Lake and Logan
on the north, make a complete and charming
picture.  Over the gallery is the representation
of the hill Cumorah, and Joseph receiving the
plates at the hands of the angel.  The work is
tastefully designed and neatly  executed, and
will add greatly to the appearance of the inte-
rior of the building.  The pictures being histori-
cal will give tone to the building, and recall
the mind to the subjects they represent in the
most pleasing way.  It is a new feature of Mor-
mon Tabernacles, original in some respects,and
calculated to create a sublime and lofty admira-
tion for the places and incidents thus pre-
sented.
