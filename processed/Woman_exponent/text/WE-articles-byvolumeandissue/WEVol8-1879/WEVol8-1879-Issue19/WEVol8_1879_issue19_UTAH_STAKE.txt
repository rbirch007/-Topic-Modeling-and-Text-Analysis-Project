                     UTAH STAKE

	Minutes of the Sixth Quarterly Confer-
ence of the Relief Societies of Utah Stake
of Zion, held in the Meeting House, Provo
City, Feb. 13, 1880. Present on the stand,
President Margaret T. Smoot, Counselor
Mary John, with the other members of the
board. Visitor from Salt Lake City, Sister
Zina D. Young; brethren, David John, S.
S. Jones,Bishop Johnson and several others.
J. C. Graham, Editor “Enquirer,” was also
present.
	Meeting opened by singing and prayer.
Minutes of former meeting read by the
Secretary. The reading of the reports
for the quarter then followed, after which
verbal reports from the several presidents
of the Relief Societies of the Stake were
called for.
	Sister John made a few remarks, encour-
aging the sisters in faithfulness and the
discharge of every duty.
	Pres. M. T. Smoot followed with a few
remarks, after which verbal reports were
given by all th[-] presidents present. Our
meeting was very well attended, consider-
ing the inclemency of the weather and the
cars being blockaded by snow so they could
not run on time.

[Column 3]
	Sister Zina D. Young made some very
interesting remarks on various subjects, all
tending to the upbuilding of the kingdom
of God on the earth. Spoke on silk culture,
and exhorted the sisters to be united and
love one another.
	Our afternoon meeting was better attend-
ed than the morning; a number of the
brethren spoke. Bro. John, Bishop John-
son and S. S. Jones each made some very
spirite[-] remarks, eulogizing the sisters for
what they were doing. Spoke of the object
and aims of the societies and the help they
were to the Bishops in the care of the po[-]r

and her mission. Each speaker’s remarks
were fraught with good instruction.
	Our Statistical report is not as full as it
should be, as some of the societies failed to
send in their Statistical report, taking it for
granted that having sent once there was
no need of sending them again, making no
allowances for removals, or deaths, or in-
crease of numbers. Our Statistical and Fi-
nancial reports are as follows:
