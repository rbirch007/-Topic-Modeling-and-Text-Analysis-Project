TO ONE LOVE.

		(Published by request.)
For all that thou hast been to me,
Of light and joy and melody;
What can I offer love to thee?
What can I plead thy life to hold
But fair success and honors told?
'Tis true I only breathe one prayer
God's love go with thee every where,
His guidance and his tenderest care.
I only know that by thy side.
My life for me is glorified,
And heavenly peace is flowing wide.
For all the kindness, thou hast shown
For every tender word and tone
[—] cious than before I’ve known.
I thank thee even in her name,
Who now beneath the snow is lain,
Unmoved by sunshine or by rain.
O'er whose sweet rest the breezes blow,
O’er whose unbroken slumbers low,
Our tears unheeded still must flow.
When other arms than mine shall fold
Thee tenderly, in sheltering hold,
More strong, and true and self controlled.
Remember then dear, how my prayer,
My tenderest thoughts, my sweetest care,
Go with thee, dearest, every where.
Thou art the rest, the [-]anguar sweet,
Thou my desire, thou my retreat,
I consecrate my heart to thee.
I hope thou’it in eternity
Come into me and shut the door,
So fast that none can enter more.
Fill all my soul with sweet delight
My thoughts are with thee, day and night.
						S. L.
Salt Lake City, Jan. 22.1880.
