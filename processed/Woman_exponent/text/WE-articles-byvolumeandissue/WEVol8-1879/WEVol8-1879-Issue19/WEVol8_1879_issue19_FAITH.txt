FAITH.
	O world unknown!  O world unseen!
	In which, my spirit, soon will be,
	Could I but pierce the veil between
	This slowly fading world and thee!

	Could I but catch, though faint and dim, 
	One glimpse of thine immortal shore, 
	One note of that celestial hymn
	That angels sing forever more!

	Could but one voice the silence break, 
	That doth encompass, thee around; 
	And in the breathless stillness wake
	The faintest echo of a sound!

	If in the darkness I might trace,
	-Bright with a glory borne of thee.
	The shadowy outline of a face,
	Or a dear hand stretched out to me!

	Catch but one whisper, one pale gleam;
	Of light eternal, just a touch
	Of some one gone before - twould seem
	To my poor soul so much; so much!

	But Faith alone, with brow serene,
	Stands by my side and points the way;
	And all the drifted clouds between
	Grow bright and luminous as day.

	Her voice through all the spaces rings,
	A triumph o'er death's awful calm;
	And my glad soul responsive sings,
	An answer to her joyful psalm.

	Her strong arm lifts me far above,
	Into a realm of purer air; 
	I feel a sweet, sustaining love,
	That will go with me everywhere. 

	O heavenly Faith, with me abide!
	Forsake me not, thou truest friend;
	Do thou but lead, O fearless guide!
	And I will follow to the end.

	For thou hast wide the portals thown, 
	And through thy blessed eyes I see
	The glory of that world unknown.
	Where my freed spirit soon will be.
