	SAMARIA, MALAD VALLEY.

	I believe the sisters of Samaria are not
often heard from, but it is not because we
are not trying to do our duty; we care for
the poor here, and are trying, as a band of
sisters, to be united and work for the good
of all. We are trying to carry out the coun-
sel given to store up grain. We take the
EXPONENT in our society, which is read
with much interest by the sisters. The
teachers visit regularly, and their reports
are favorable. We have thirty-two mem-
bers, and our meetings are well attended,
considering the weather and the distance
we have to travel. Our President, Mary
D. Price, got up a Leap Year party on New
Year’s night, for the benefit of the Society.
The ladies bought the tickets and furnished
supper for the gentlemen. The young la-
dies made the Meeting House look a picture
of beauty and cleanliness, which every one
admired when they entered the door. We
had the best music in this valley, and every
one enjoyed themselves famously; every-
thing was peaceable and quiet, and, in
short, our dance was a success.
	We feel like improving intellectually as
well as spiritually, and hope we may indi-
vidually and as a society add our influence
and unite with the Saints in building up:
the kingdom of God.
		MARY H. TOUT, Sec’y.
	Jan. 15, 1880.
