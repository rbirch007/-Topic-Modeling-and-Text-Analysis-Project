		NEW TUFTON, NEAR CHESTERFIELD,
DERBYSHIRE, ENG., Nov. 21, 1879.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Dear Sister—On the 28th of October, 1878,
the Tufton Relief Society was organized
by Elder M. B. Wheelwright, one of the
missionaries from Utah, with the following
officers, viz.:  Susannah Stacy, President,
Elizabeth Griffin and Ellen  Bailey, Coun-
selors, and Eliza  Clark,  Secretary.  Not-
withstanding the hard times, we have been
enabled to render valuable assistance to the
poor Saints in our midst.  We take great
pleasure in the good work, and sincerely
hope that the time may speedily come when
we shall all have the privilege of gathering
to Zion, and have the pleasure of co operat-
ing with our dear sisters there.  The EXPO-
NENT  comes to hand regularly, and is
always hailed as a welcoming messenger.
	On the 10th of this month  Elizabeth
Griffin was released as First Counselor, and
Eliza Clark appointed in her stead. This
appointment left a vacancy in the secretary-
ship, which was filed by appointing Mary
Clark.
	The sisters of the Society a few days
since formed themselves into a surprise
party and visited an old lady, who is desig-
nated as "Granny Warren."  It was the
eighty-seventh anniversary of her birthday,
and I can assure you it was a pleasant sur-
prise to her.  We gladdened the dear crea-
tures heart by a few of the good things of
this earth, and spent a very happy and
pleasant time together.
	Not wishing to infringe too much on
your valuable time, I will conclude, praying
God to bless you in the noble work you are
engaged in, and believe me your sister in
the Gospel of Christ,
