EDITORIAL NOTES.

	THE National Woman Suffrage Association
will hold their Convention in Washington, D.
C., commencing Jan. 21, at 10:30 A. M. It is
expected there will be an unusual number of
delegates in attendance, as well as new and
talented advocates of woman's enfranchise-
ment. Entertainment will be provided for
officers of the Association, speakers and ac-
credited delegates during the sessions of the
Convention, upon applying to the correspond-
ing secretary. Delegates who do not wish to
accept this hospitality, may be supplied with a
comfortable home at one dollar per day. All
delegates are requested to report at the Riggs'
House, corner 15th and G. Streets, upon arrival.
	MRS. E. C. Stanton's daughter; who gradu-
ated the last year from the Boston school of
oratory, will speak (it is expected) on the plat-
form at Lincoln Hall during the Convention.
	A DELEGATE from each section of the coun-
try will be heard before the Committees of
the House and Senate, to whom petitions will
be referred. This promise was given the Sec-
retary, Mrs. S. A. Spencer, during the extra
session, even unfriendly members pledging
themselves to be present.
