OBSERVE THE GOLDEN RULE.

   I have thought  for some  time  that I should
like to mingle my voice with my sisters through
the columns  of our little  paper.  There are but
a few of  here in Eden it being a small place
still we have some good metings. And we take
a deep interest in trying to  do  right. For my
part I  think  that  we  should  be  united  and
strive  to live  righteously  and  improve  our-
selves and stop all backbiting;  if we try to live
up to our duty we have somthing of  more  im-
portance to occupy our time and we should im-
prove in our manners and  learn  to  guard  our
tongues and our tempers, and strive to do right
and learn to keep  the  golden  rule  "To  do  to
others as  you  would  that  they should do to
you.” I think if we would  observe  the golden
rule more than we do we would be more united
improve more rapidly and enjoy a better spirit.
Oh,-what happiness  there  would then be;  and
if we would be more prayerful and humble; and
try to live up to every principle of our religion,
Satan  could have little or  no  power, for I be-
leive he is using his utmost endeavors and try-
ing to bring destruction upon the earth if we
would listen to the  good instructions we hear
and put  them  into  practice  we  would  enjoy
more true happiness than  we can in any other
way.
								E. J. F.
