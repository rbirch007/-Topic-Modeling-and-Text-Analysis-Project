OBITUARIES.

	DIED: at Mendon, Cache County April 10th,
1880, Mary  Ann wife of the late John Gardiner
aged 74 years,she was born in Glocuester-shire,
England and was baptized  into  the Church of 
Jesus Christ of L. D. S., in the Chalford Branch
of the Cheltenham Conference by Elder James
Stephens on the 22nd, of May 1845.  And emi-
grated with her family to  Utah  in  1853.  She
died  as  she  had  lived  a  faithful  Latter-day
Saint, rejoicing in the hope of a glorious resur-
rection.  She leaves a  numerous  progeny  be-
hind her who we trust will strive to remember
and emulate her virtues. 
									COM.

	DIED: at Hoytsville of  erysipelas, Mrs. And
Lowe, born at Stole, Sumerset-shire,  England
December 13th, 1811, died Feb.  9th, 1880, aged
68 years,  one  month  and  twenty-six  days.
Funeral  services  were  held  at  the  meeting
house Feb  11th. the house being crowded  by
friends and relations.
	Remarks were made by Bishops Winters  of 
Hoytsville and Roundy of Wanship.
	Mrs Lowe was a woman beloved and respected
by all who  knew  her and  was ever a  faithful
Latter-day Saint,after joining the Church Nov.
6th 1845.  After moving to  Hoytsville she was
president of the  Relief  Society  for  five  years
but at the end of that time she resigned on ac-
count of poor health.  From a family  of eight
children four are left to mourn her loss.
