MOTES AND NEWS.

	A TRACT is in circulation, showing that
in the United States alone $700,000,000 are
expended for liquor, and only $95,000,000
for education.
	A LADY has been admitted to the prac-
tice of law in the Supreme Court of Cal-
ifornia, Mrs. Mary Josephine Young, of
Sacramento. It is said "she passed the ex-
amination with credit."
	THE WOMEN of Massachuetts have suc-
ceeded in obtaining the privilege of voting
on school committees. The law in that
State requires registration. Meetings have
been held to consider their duties as voters;
speaches made on the subject, etc.
	MRS. PRESIDENT HAYES, on Decoration
Day, placed on the "unknown" tomb on
Arlington Heights, an elegant placque of
flowers, and sent  150 bouquets to the
cemetery.  The President himself placed a
lovely wreath of choice flowers on the top
of the column.
