	WALLSBURG
Editor Exponent:

	We held our annual meeting July 2lst,
Sister Nancy Wall, resigned her position as
President, on account of moving away: we
were reorganized as fellows; Elizabeth
Clifton, President; Polly Meacham, and
Almira Duke, Counselors; Sarah Glenn,
and Lucina Boren Secretaries; Mary Kerby,
Treasurer.	
	Our Society numbers fifty two members.
We are trying to keep up with the spirit of
the times and perform our duty.
 	We hold our meetings semi-monthly.
The grass-hoppers have destroyed our crops
here. We may not be able to add much to
our store of wheat this season. We have
built a good, substantial granary, worth
forty dollars, we have stored one hundred
and twenty six bushels and twenty four
pounds. We own eleven shares in the Co-
op store $55[-]00, other property on hand $40
[-]00. Total $95[-]00, Disbursed, $8,05.
	With success to all who labor for the
cause of Zion we remain your sisters in the
Gospel.
		SABAH GLENN.
		LUCINA BOREN, 	}Secretaries.

Editor Exponent:	
	The fifth Quarterly Conference of the
Davis Stake Relief Society, was held at
Farmington July 17, 1879, Pres. Sarah I.
Homes presiding. Present, Elders' Israel
Barlow, of East Bountiful, James Loynd,
E. T. Clark, and Freeman Leonard of Farm-
ington. The afternoon was entirely oc-
cupied with the written and verbal reports
of the different Societies; including the
Y. L. A's of the Stake; the P. A’s of East
Bountiful and Farmington All were repre-
sented as doing well.
	The afternoon speakers were, Sisters C.
M. Barlow, Zelnora Glover, Aurelia S.
Rogers, and Mary L Clark. Also Brothers 
Israel Barlow, and Jas. Loynd. Pres. S. I.
Homes made the closing remarks.
	The speakers dwelt upon the subjects, of
plurality of wives, humility, prayer, union,
confidence, etc, etc. A general good feel-
ing and spirit prevailed and the rich instruc-
tions given were received with marked
attention.
