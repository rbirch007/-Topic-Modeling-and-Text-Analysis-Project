EDITOR EXPONENT:
	SNIDERVILLE, PARLEY'S PARK.
		July 9th, 1879. 
	Our Relief Society, have had a very en-
couraging visit from President Sarah S.
Richards. and her Counselors, Sisters
Reynolds and Roundy. After singing and
prayer Sister Richards wished to here
from some of the sisters present. Sister
Harriet Chapman and Sister Wright, made
some appropriate remarks. Coun. Reynolds,
then bore her testimony to the truth of
this work, and exhorted the sisters to be
faithful and live for the kingdom of God,
and righteousness. For what are riches of
this world compared to eternal life.
	Coun. Roundy said she was surprised and
very much pleased to see so many sisters;
she did not think there were so many in this
place. She spoke of the care mothers
should have over their children, they
should avoid using bad language before
them, and must teach them to pray that
they may be guided aright. She told the
sisters not to let little things keep them
away from their meetings, and counseled
us to pay our donations freely and to teach
our children to pay donations top.
	Pres. Richards said she hoped the sisters
would be prayerful and be united in their
Society and she felt that we should do
much good;she asked the sisters to be care-
ful what they said of one another and if they
couuld not say any thing good, say nothing
at all. She promised to come in a month
and organize us. We also expect to have a
Young Ladies’Society organized at the same
time.
	Our beloved Bishop, then made a few
remarks said he was well pleased with
the sisters meeting and hoped the Saciety
would prosper in Snyderville, remembering
“it is better late than never.” And he
would always be on hand to help us all he
possibly could.
	Brother Bradbury and Brother Snyder,
followed with very encouraging remarks
and were well pleased with the meeting.
	Ever praying God to abnndantly bless
you, is the desire of your sister in the 
Gospel of Christ;  
		Zelnora Chapman.
			Acting Sec’y.
