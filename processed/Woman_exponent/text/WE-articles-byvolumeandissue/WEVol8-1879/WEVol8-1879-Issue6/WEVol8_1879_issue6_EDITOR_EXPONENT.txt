EDITOR EXPONENT:

	It is with pleasure I take this opportunity
of writing to you. We are far away, but
we do not want to be quite forgotten by the
folks in Utah. We are trying to live our
religion and do something in our Relief
Society, but it does not seem as though we
had done much, but we look for better
times. We do not raise any grain here, so
that we cannot do anything in the way of 
storing wheat.
	We held our Relief Society Annual Meet-
ing on the 16th of May; we had a good
time and a good spirit prevaiied. Our re-
ports were all read, showing that we had
received during the year $22 67. Disburse-
ments for the last year, for the needy, $7 05;
emigration, $6 00; leaving a balance in the 
Treasury of $9 62.
	The health of our company is good at
present. We have had so much sickness
that it has taken up nearly all our time and 
strength, but the Lord has blessed us ex-
ceedingly in sparing our lives; we have
only lost one of the company that came
from Utah (that was Bro. A. O. Williams,
who died last fall) and one little babe with
all our sickness; some of the folks have
the chills yet.
	The sisters of this place feel to heartily
endorse the movement of the sisters
throughout Utah with regard to the Anti-
Polygamy Societies and Memorials. We
are always glad to get the EXPONENT, and
it is ever read with pleasure.
			CYRENA MERRILL, Pres't.
