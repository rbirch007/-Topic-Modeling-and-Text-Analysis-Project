SABBATH DAY THOUGHTS.

	As I sit down in my quiet, little home,
resting my mind from care and labor on this
Sabbath day, set apart for rest and reflection,
some thoughts, Oh, happy thoughts come
to my mind; and I conclnded if our editor
would find room for them in the EXPONENT
it would do no harm for its patrons to read
and ponder upon. As I have been chosen
by the young ladies of this settlement as
president of the Y. L. M. I. A. until we are
reorganized by higher authority, I feel to
take a deeper interest in the welfare of our
young girls, and wish to make every exer-
tion in my power to encourage them to keep
up their meetings, and to donate our little
means to the work upon the Temple, as I
do not feel hardly capable of teaching or in-
structing my sisters in the knowledge of
the things of God, or indeed in much else.
	I have been meditating upon what I
might do to promote the most good and
what improvement I could make in myself,
that I might have the best influence. My
thoughts were to keep the spirit of the
Gospel, to pray always, and to read the
Church books; not to spend time and means
in novels and the light literature of the
world, but store my mind with that true
knowledge which will prove of lasting ben-
efit. I am happy to say I have never spent
a cent for novels or any such trash, and
have spent but a few hours of my life in
reading them, and intend to spend no more
in such a frivolous way. The teachings
and spirit of the Gospel is far more precious
to me than all worldly knowledge, or its
fashions and enjoyments.
		CAROLINE DAY.
Hamblin, Utah.
