VOL. 8.		SALT LAKE CITY, UTAH, AUGUST 15, 1879.		NO. 6.


[For the EXPONENT.
GLEAMS OF LIGHT.
There are moments, bless God, when Life's burdens
From our hearts are roiled softly away;
When we feel from our trials uplifted
As the sun o'er a cloud-darkened day.

When we feel deep within us upspringing
The fountain of gladess anew;
Its bright healing waters o'erflowing
Revive life's parched flowers like dew.

We catch the deep heart of the sunshine
to warm our chilled hopes to new life,
With quickened sense hearken the music
With which Nature's voices are rife.

We see blessings strewn all around us,
Where all seemed so barren and gray;
Where only dead leaves seemed to rustle
Our pathway with flowers is gay.

Enraptured we catch a new music
In our little ones' laughter so sweet,
And dearer than tinkling of fountains
Their restless and pattering feet.

Unnoted and unvalued blisses
We brushed like the cobwebs away,
Now dimple and smile through the darkness
In which we had thrust them away.

And even the burdens we carry—
The burdens so heavy and sore-
Finding these contain costliest treasures,
We feel they'll be heavy no more.

The language of praise springs unbidden
To lips sadly used to complain-
we think if we may be forgiven
We never will murmur again.

Sweet moments, we give you glad welcome
And longer entreat you to stay;
Glad beams from shining Hereafter
Like Angel hands beckoning away.
