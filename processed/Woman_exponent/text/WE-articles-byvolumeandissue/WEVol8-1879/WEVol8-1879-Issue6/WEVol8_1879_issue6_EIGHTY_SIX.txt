EIGHTY SIX.

	Quite a number of the friends of Bishop
Shadrach Roundy, deceased, met at the res-
idence of his widow, Mrs. Betsy Roundy,
Sixteenth Ward, on Sunday, June 29th, to
celebrate her eighty sixth birthday.
	Presiding Bishop, Edward Hunter, and
lady, Bishops J. C. Roundy and F. Kesler,
with sisters E. R. Snow, Mother Whitney as
we lovingly call her, Sarah D. Rich, Mercy
R. Thompson and daughter and many
others were present, manifesting thereby
their love and respect for our aged sister,
whose heart and hands have ever been
open to comfort the afflicted and provide for
the poor. She has also taken seven mother-
less children and cared for them as her own.
	Her husband, Bishop Shadrach Roundy,
was baptized at the earliest rise of the
Church, and served as life-guard to the Pro-
phet Joseph Smith, and whose superior will
and determination overawed the mob of
two hundred strong, under the leadership
of William Law, who came at midnight,
pretending they wanted to see the mum-
mies, but really to kidnap the Prophet Jo-
seph. Brother Roundy, with a hickory
cane, forced them back as they were enter-
ing, and fastened the gate.
	Sisters Thompson and Mathews brought
the little girls of the Primary Association
who sang several sweet songs, that gave
great pleasure to our aged sister and the
company present.
	We had a very pleasant time, and realiz-
ed that in blessing our beloved sister, we
all were truly blessed.	E. DAVIS.
