of Summit Stake of Zion was held in the
Meeting House at Coalville. Pres. Sarah
S. Richards and other officers of the organ-
ization were on the stand, as also Pres.
Eliza R. Snow and Mrs. E. Howard of Salt
Lake City, Mrs. Lydia Rich of Morgan
Stake, and a majority of the presidents of 
the Ward Societies; Pres. Wm. W. Cluff of
Summit Stake, Bishop Salmon of Coalville,
Bishop Roundy of Wanship, and other
Stake officers. The Conference was opened
with the usual exercises. The Statistical
and Financial reports of the various 
branches of the stake, except Coalville,
Parley's Park and Upton were read by the
Secretary. The present Stake organization
consists of thirteen branches, viz.: Coal-
ville, Henneferville, Echo, Hoytsville, Wan-
ship, Rockport, Peoa, Kamas, Morrell,
Almy, Evanston, Parley's Park and Upton;
all of which, except three, were represented
verbally. They were reported as being gen-
erally in improving conditions. The Almy
and Fvanston branches being some distance
from this place, it is not always convenient
for the officers to attend all of our confer-
ences.
	Pres. E. R. Snow addressed the meeting.
She spoke at length upon the duties of par-
ents to their children, and referred to the
too frequent neglect of the rising genera-
tion, in not bringing them up in habits of 
care, thrift and industry. If we wish the

[Column 2]
generatien that succeeds us to be better,
wiser and more intelligent than the present
one, we must commence in earnest, educate
our children intelligently, inculcate true
and correct principles into their minds,
teach them to be wise and good, and to as-
sist them in qualifying themselves for the
duties that will in the future be required of
them.
	Afternoon session: Mrs. M. J. Brim, Sec-
retary of the Y. L. M. I. A. for the Stake,
read reports from some of the branch asso-
ciations. Mrs. Eliza Rhead, Pres. of the
Stake Association, expressed her regret
that they had not more complete reports to
present to the Conference. Mrs. M. J.
Brim, President of the Primary Associa-
tion, reported very favorably of its condi-
tion. Mrs. Rich of Morgan, Mrs. M. W.
Reynolds, Counselor and Mrs. E. Howard,
each occupied a short time in speaking upon
subjects of interest.
	Pres. E. R. Snow again occupied a few
moments.
	Pres. Wm. W. Cluff spoke encouragingly
of the labors of the societies, and referred
briefly to the necessity of having the re-
ports made out punctually and correctly.
The Conference adjourned to the last Fri-
day in April 1880.
	On Saturday a meeting of the Primary
Association was held, Sisters Snow, How-
ard, Rich and others were present. The
meeting was particularly interesting, the
questions put to the little ones being an-
swered in a very intelligent manner, and
gratifying to those concerned.
			SARAH S. RICHARDS, Pres.
