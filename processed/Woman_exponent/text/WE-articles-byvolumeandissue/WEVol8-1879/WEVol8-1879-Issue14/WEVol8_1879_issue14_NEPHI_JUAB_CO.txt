		NEPHI JUAB Co.
	The Quarterly conference of the Juab
county Stake organization of the Relief
Society was held in the Nephi Tabernacle
on the 17th, of Oct. 1879, commencing at
10 A.M. On the stand were the officers of
the Stake, and some of the branch officers;
also the officers from Levan and Mona.
Of the brethren on the stand were Pres.
Teasdale, Counselor K. H. Brown, Bishop
Sperry, Patriarch J. G. Bigler and Bro.
George Kendall. After the usual opening
exercises, Pres. Goldsbrough made a few
very appropriate remarks, said she with
some of her sisters had visited Levan, and
Mona, several times since the last quarterly
conference, and, had tried to discharge her
duty in counseling and advising the sisters
to the best of her ability, in every good
word and work, exhorting them to per-
severe in promoting practically the cul-
ture of silk and in the establshing and 
carrying on every branch of home industry
that came with in their reach, that we might
become self sustaining and independent:
and to be diligent in STORING GRAIN. Plead
with the sisters to be united and to sustain,
uphold and defend each other's characters
that the evil one might have no power
over us.
	The rest of the forenoon was nearly all
occupied with reading reports from the
Stake and branches, also the Young Ladies
and Primary Associations, which were very
encouraging. The afternoon was devoted
to remarks from the brethren and sisters,
which were calculated to encourage and
instruct all that have a desire to see the
work of God roll on.
	Our financial condition is not so prosper-
ous as we would wish, but, we are trying to
do the best we can. We have a granary
nearly done, so near that we are storing
grain in it.
	The Choir sang "Glory to God on High".
Benediction by Patriarch J. G. Bigler.
