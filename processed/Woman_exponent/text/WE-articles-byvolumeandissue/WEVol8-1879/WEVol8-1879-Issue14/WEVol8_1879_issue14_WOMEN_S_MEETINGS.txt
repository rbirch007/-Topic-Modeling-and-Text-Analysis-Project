WOMEN'S MEETINGS.

	SISTERS Eliza R. Snow and Jane S. Richards
have been making a tour through Weber Co.,
holding meetings in the different se[--]lements.
Tuesday, Dec. 4, at North Ogden;  Wednesday
the appointments were not filled, on account
of the bad state of the roads.  Thursday after-
noon the City Hall in Ogden was actually
packed with sisters and a few brethren.  Fri-
day they visited Slaterville, and held meetings
there; Saturday went to Plain City, Sunday
afternoon in Riverdale,, Monday at Huntsville,
Tuesday in Eden.  The sisters' Conference in
that Stake of Zion will be held on the 18th and
19th of this month.  The sisters who travel in
the interest of the organizations of women and
the Primaries are doing effective work, and are
a powerful lever for good.
	SISTERS S. M. Kimball and S. M. Heywood
attended  the  sisters'  Conference  of  Tooele
Stake, which was held Friday and Saturday
in Grantsville.
	The Box  Elder  Stake  Conference  of Relief
Society, Young Ladies' and Primary  Associa-
tions was held in Brigham City on the 9th and
10th of this month! We acknowledge our
thanks for the kind invitation extended us to
attend these Conferences,  and  should  cer-
tainly have accepted, if circumstances had not
prevented, It is very pleasant to be remem-
bered by one's friends.
