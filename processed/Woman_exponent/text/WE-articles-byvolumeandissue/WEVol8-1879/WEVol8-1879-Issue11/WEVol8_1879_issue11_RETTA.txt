								RETTA.

	DIED at the Nephi House, Nephi, June 1879,
Sarah Jane aged sixteen and Alvin aged seven
the son and daughter of Henry and Susanna
Goldsbrough.
	They have gone to their rest spotless and
pure. They are safely sheltered in the bosom
of Him, who said "Of such is the kingdom of
heaven." They were too good for the rude
blasts of earth.  O, how we all prized them.
The silvery tones of their voices, their sunny
smiles, and their numberless acts of kindness,
live in remembrance, and we weep because
they are gone from us, we feel it is hard to
give them up, so good and gifted, so tender
and true.  But "death has all seasons for its
own," and we have no power to stay its hand.
Death came among us an "unbidden guest and
an unwelcome victor.  But they shall hunger
no more for the Lamb shall feed them and
shall lead them unto the fountains of living
waters and God shall wipe all tears from their
eyes.
It is fitting we should place upon our reccords
some small tribute of our regard. To the s'rick-
en ones to whom the loss seems irreparable
those to whom these beloved ones were nearest
and dearest, we tender our best sympathies
and yet we know we can offer no palliation.
He Who is too wise to err, too good to be un-
kind, knew when it was best to take them to
Himself. Sister Sarah Jane passed away from
earth in peace without a struggle.  In the last
hours of her life she bore a strong testimony,
to the power of Jesus's grace to sustain her and
the precious comfort of the Holy Spirit to her
soul
Who, who would live alway away from his God,
Away from yon heaven that blessed abode
Where the rivers of pleasure flow o'er the high plains
And the noontide of glory eternally reigns,
