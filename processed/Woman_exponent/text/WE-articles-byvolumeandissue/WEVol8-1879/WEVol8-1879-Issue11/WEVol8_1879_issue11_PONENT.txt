PONENT.

	I have just read (not for the first time) a
poem put into my hands,entitled "SHINEHAH"
The subject is a glorious one! and I rejoice
that we have a young man among us (and a 
Poet of no mean order) who can enunciate
such noble sentiments, and delineate Truth in
such beautiful and appropriate language. It
is a decided fact in my mind, that henceforth
we have a Poet of Our Own.
	I would like to elaborate my veiws of the
worth of this Home Production "which, make
it dear and valuable to every Latter-day Saint
but at present will conclude in the words of
Lord Chestefield on returning Mallet's poems to
Lord Bute, who had sent them to him for
criticism:
		"It has no faults, or I no fault can spy:
	It is all beauty, or all blindness I,"
		H. T. K.
Salt Lake City
