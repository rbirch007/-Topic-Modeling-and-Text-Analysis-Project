A SINGLE WORD.

	The following touching story, was re-
cently told by a gentleman in Boston in
delivering a temperance address.
	A mother in the Green Hills of Ver-
mont, was holding her son sixteen years of
age by the right hand, as he was about to
leave her, "mad with the love of the sea."
As she stood thus by the garden gate
on a sunny morning she said: "Edward,
they tell me—for I never saw the ocean—
that the great temptation of a seaman's
life is drink. Promise me, before you quit
your mother's hand, that you will never
drink." "And, said he, for he told me the
whole story, I gave her the promise and I
went the broad globe over—Calcutta, the
Mediterranean, San Francisco, the Cape of
Good Hope, the North and South pole I saw
them all in forty years, and I never saw a
glass filled with sparkling liquor, that my
mother's form by the garden gate on the
green hill-sides of Vermont, did not rise be-


fore me; and to-day, at sixty, my lips are
innocent of the taste of liquor. Was not
that sweet evidence of the power of a sin-
gle word? Yet that was but half. "For"
said he, "yesterday there came into my
counting room  a young man of forty, and
asked "Do you know me?" "No." "Well,
I was once brought drunk into your pres-
ence on ship-board; you were a passenger,
the Captain kicked me aside; you took me
to your berth, and kept me, till I had slept
off the intoxication. Then you asked me if
I had a mother: I said I never knew a word
from her lips; you told me of yours at the
garden gate, and to-day I am the master of
one of the finest packets in New York; and
I come to ask you to call and see me."
	How far that little candle has thrown its
beams! God be thanked for the Almighty
power of a single word.
