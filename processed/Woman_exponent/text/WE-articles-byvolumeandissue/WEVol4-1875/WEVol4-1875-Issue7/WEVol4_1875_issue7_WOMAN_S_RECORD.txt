WOMAN'S RECORD. 

	MRS. William C. Peck of New Haven,
sued that city for damages to the amount of
$5,000 for injuries received in consequence
of a defective side-walk.
	LADY Bulwer is spoken of as having very
beautiful violet eyes with black lashes, and
the most childishly innocent expression; and
to her is attributed the inspiration of many
of the beautiful poems of her husband
"Owen Meredith."
	Mrs. Lucy Stone has suggested that the
President of the United States, shall at the
Centennial, on the 4th of July 1876 lead on-
to the platform Mrs. Lucretia Mott, Lydia
M. Child, and Frances D. Gage, under a
black flag—commemmorating the fact that
women are ranked with idiots, felons and
ignorant men.
	MISS Mason and Miss Hamilton of
the graduating class in Vermont Universi-
ty have been elected to membership on ac-
count of their superior scholarship to the
Phil Beta Keppa Society. Professor Pea-
body of Chicago, President of the Alumni
Association hails this event as the
beginning of a new era; and he remarked
they had shown themselves thinkers, and
referred to the Alumni as their brothers
and warmly welcomed them to a place
among the Alumni. He congratulated the
institution on this progressive step.
