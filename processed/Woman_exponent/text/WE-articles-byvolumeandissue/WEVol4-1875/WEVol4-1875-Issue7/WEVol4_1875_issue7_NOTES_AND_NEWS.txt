NOTES AND NEWS.

	THERE IS talk of a woman's paper being
started at Larrmie, Wy. That is right, la-
dies, step forward and persevere in every
good work.
	GEN. SHERIDAN, who lately visited this
city arrived in San Francisco on the
23rd of Aug., where he was received with
great demonstrations of military honor, the
streets being crowded with people anxious
to witness the reception.
	THE AMERICAN rifle team that visited
Europe recently, arrived safely in New
York city on the 21st of August.  They have
been feted and feasted everywhere abroad.
They have gained considerable notoriety
away, and return home to find their friends
ready to welcome them with loud cheers and
deomonstations of honor.
	KING KALUKA held a meeting at his
palace in Honolulu recently, to urge upon
his people and particularly all about his pal-
ace, to reform in the use of liquors.  He had
seen the evil influences resulting from the
use of ardent spirits, and hoped, by begin-
ning in his own palace to protest against it;
the good influence would spread throughout
the land.  He did not wish to command
any, but desired to see this evil checked;
a pledge was prepared and all were invited
to sign it.
	GEORGE W. WILLIS, a son of the poet N.
P. Willis, was recently married to Miss Em-
ma Miles, whose father, Mr. William H.
Miles, is a well known resident of this city.
The ceremony took place in Brooklyn, N. Y.
and was pronounced by its reverend pastor
Henry Ward Beecher.  It was noticed that
the bride was required to give her promise
to "love and honer" but the "obey" was
omitted. Beecher is said to make this 
omission always in performing the marriage
ceremony.  He says he knows the women
would not keep that promise if they should 
make it.
