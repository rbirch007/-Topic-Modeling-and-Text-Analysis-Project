WOMAN'S RECORD.

	KATE Harrington, one of the poetical
contributors to the "Woman's Journal," is
about to publish her poems in book form.
Her real name is Mrs, R. S. Pollard; she
resides in Keokuk, Iowa.
	MRS. Batemen, manager of the Lyceum
Theatre, London, has the exclusive right to
perform Mr. Tennyson's drama during five
years, both in England and America; pay-
ing the author a stipulated sum per night.
	MISS Ella Dietz is giving readings in
England, from all the most popular poets;
she is commended highly by the press there.
She is also taking the leading part in a
comedy taken from the French and adapted
by herself. The piece is interspersed with
songs, which she herself has set to music.
She must certainly be a lady of uncommon
genius as well as superior culture and attain-
ments.
	AT the commencement, in Cazenovia,
New York, of an old Methodist School which
has just celebrated its semi-Centennial An-
niversary, Miss Jerusha Loomis took the
prize of scholarship of one hundred dollars;
and the four scholars next in rank were
girls. This is noticeable in a school where
the majority of pupils are boys.


WE are usually capable of greater things
than we perform; we are sent into the
world with bills of credit and seldom draw
to their full extent.
