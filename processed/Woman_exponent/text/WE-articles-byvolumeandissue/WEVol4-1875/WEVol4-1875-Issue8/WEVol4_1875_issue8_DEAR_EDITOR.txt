DEAR EDITOR:
	I have been so much interested in our
meetings and Sunday schools lately, that I 
really want to say something to my young
friends about them.
	Boys and girls, I do not believe that you
have all got as good fathers and mothers as
mine. I am sure you cannot have, or some
of you would not do as I see you. My par-
ents teach me that to keep the Sabbath day
holy, as the Lord has commanded us to do,
means not to play nor work on that day; but
to prepare our food with as little labor as
possible, and to go to the house of the Lord
to pray and worship in union, with the good
people who gather there, and then to rest at
home and read good books and think about
God and His wisdom and kindness.
	Now I know some of you laugh and talk
about picnics and parties, and your pretty
dresses and all such things in Sunday school;
and I presume you do the same in meeting
if you go there.  And you play and romp at
home and at your neighbors, and laugh and
talk loud on Sunday, for I hear you. All
this is wicked and I wish not one of you
would ever do so any more.
	Perhaps you will think, "what right has a 
little girl only ten years old to talk to us of 
our faults? What does she know?" I can
tell you, I know of these things because my
father and mother have told me, not be-
cause I am smarter or better than you, or
half so smart as some of you. But dear
friends, smartness will take none of us to
Heaven, except we use our knowledge for
good.
