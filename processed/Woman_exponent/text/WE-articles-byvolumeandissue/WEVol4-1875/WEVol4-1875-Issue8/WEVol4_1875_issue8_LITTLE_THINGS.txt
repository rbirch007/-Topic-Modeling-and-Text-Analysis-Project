LITTLE THINGS.
	Little words are the sweetest to hear; lit-
tle charities fly furthest and stay longest on
the wing; little flakes are the stillest; little
hearts the fondest, and little farms the best
tilled. Little books are the most read, and
little songs the dearest loved. And when
Nature would make anything especially rare
and beautiful, she makes it little—little
pearls, little diamonds, little dews. Agar's
is a model prayer, yet it is but a little one,
and the burden of the petition for but little.
The sermon on the mount is little, but the
last dedication discourse was an hour. Life
is made up of littles; death is what remains
of them all. Day is made up of little beams
and night is glorious with little stars.
