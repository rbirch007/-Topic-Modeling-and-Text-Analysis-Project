			RESTITUTION.

	The word restitution has dwelt upon my
mind of late until I felt impressed to say a
few words upon the subject, for it seems to
me that people are heedless and negligent
on this point, and do not look upon it as
seriously as they should. It seems to me
in speaking of re-baptism and renewal of
covenants, we should be careful to straight-
en up some of the old differences which ex-
ist between families and one another. How
many are there who from time to time go
to the table of the Lord's supper with feel-
ings of hatred towards one another, where-
as St. Paul says, "If ye have aught against
thy brother go first and make it right, lest
otherwise ye eat and drink unworthily."
	How often evil reports get circulated
where there is no foundation for them; and
they are circulated from one to another, and
cause trouble, unhappiness and bad feelings;
and how little is thought of the sin of injur-
ing another. Will not sincere and true re-
pentance seek to make reparation for wrong?
Debts left unpaid often cause bad feelings;
it makes us lose confidence in one another.
Going down into the waters of baptism will
not avail us much if we do not seek to
make right all our wrongs, but if we hon-
estly and candidly confess the wrongs we
have done to those who have been the suf-
ferers, then we can start afresh, believing
fully that God will be merciful to us.
	Dear Editor, do not think I am presuming
to teach, I only wish to present to your read-
ers a few ideas which have been upon my
mind, and I trust I have not offended any;
if you have a spare corner for it in your val-
uable paper, I trust you will believe me sin-
cere in my desire to use an influence for
good.
