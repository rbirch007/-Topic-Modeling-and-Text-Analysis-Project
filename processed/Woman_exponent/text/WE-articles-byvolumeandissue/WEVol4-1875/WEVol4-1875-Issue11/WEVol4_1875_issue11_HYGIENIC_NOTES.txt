HYGIENIC NOTES.

	 About Sleep.—The sleep of perfect health is
dreamless—a kind of sleep not procured by lauda-
num, chloral, or any other drug. Do healthy
work enough to weary you, and you will sleep a
healthy sleep. Strange as it seems, sleep is the
greatest need of man, next to air. He can live
on a very little food and drink, but any continuous
reduction of his hours of sleep, even in moderate
proportion; results in insanity or death. We have
said that this is strange; and is it not so—that the
greatest boon and the greatest need in life should
be absolute oblivion?—that to enjoy, indeed to en-
dure, two-thirds of life we must pass one-third, or
nearly that proportion of it, in a state of absolute
unconsciousness? For sleep to be perfectly re-
freshing should be dreamless.
	At this season of the year it is one of the most
important things to avoid taking cold; a little care
will often prevent a cold that will cause much suf-
fering. The Herald of Health says one of the
means to prevent a cold is a daily bath in a warm
room with much friction, but in no case to let the
body become chilled; use most friction over the
chest and throat, and snuff a little of the water in-
to the nostrils warmed to a comfortable tempera-
ture. Next after the bath take daily exercise in
the open air, but not too much exposing one's self
to the cold and the sun for a short time, but not to
exhaustion. People who are not very vigorous
should avoid over exertion, and keep the strength
up to the highest point. One preventive of cold
is to take plenty of sleep. One cause of cold is
eating too heartily after a hard day's work, when
there are not forces enough to digest the food and
keep up the circulation. A cold in its early sta-
ges may often be broken up by a hot foot bath,
with warmth to the body, especially a hot pack or
hot bath in the middle of the day. It is not ad-
visable to take a hot bath at night in such cases.
