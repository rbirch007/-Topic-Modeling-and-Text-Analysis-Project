WOMAN'S VOICE.

	My sisters, I am thankful that we have
the opportunity of addressing you through
the EXPONENT, for in no other way could
we so effectually reach so many hearts, and
extend so wide an influence by our words.
	We all have the opportunity given us of
speaking often to one another in our socie-
ties, but then we can only reach, compara-
tively, a few of the sisters of the great
household of faith. The time has come
now when woman can bear an active part
in assisting the kingdom of God. If union and a
concentration of effort can be effectual,
surely we shall prevail. The work which
has begun is not going to be arrested in its
progress; the Lord will mark out our path,
He will show us our work, He will guide
our efforts if we are careful to move in the
proper channel and strengthen, comfort and
build up each other. None need be afraid
of lack of opportunity to assist in this great
undertaking; every faculty bestowed upon
woman should be carefully cultivated and
guided to make it a practical benefit to its
possessor, but more especially to be used for
the universal good of renovating the human
family. When so much depends upon wo-
man, how careful each sister should be to
sustain each other; and herein lies one great
and essential item, for slander and backbit-
ing and all their consequences must be done
away with, ere peace and union can exist,
and perfect confidence be established in the
hearts of all who would combine together to
work in unison.
	Words will avail little if the heart is not
interested, we may say we will not do any
evil, but unless we watch as well as pray,
and keep the Holy Spirit in our hearts, we
are in continual danger of being overcome;
for Satan is always ready to suggest some-
thing, and many spirits and influences are
abroad in the world that are as catching as
disease, and therefore we need be continu-
ally watching our words, our thoughts, our
actions, lest we drink in from an impure
fountain, instead of the clear, living waters
of the Gospel of salvation. Be careful not
to partake of the spirit and influence of those
whose faith has become darkened; I know
that many say they are not afraid, they are
strongly fortified, nothing ever shakes their
faith; but the Saviour said, "Let him who
thinks he standeth take heed lest he fall.”
						EMILE.
	S. L. City, Nov. 10, 1875.
