EDITOR EXPONENT:

	The Tenth Ward Retrenchment Associa-
tion is in a prosperous condition at present,
and the young ladies who attend take a
lively interest in the meetings although, I
am sorry to say, the average attendance is
small compared with the number of names
enrolled, but those who do attend are alive
to their duties and seem to have a deter-
mination to improve their minds. At our
little meetings we read essays and selections
from good books and papers; the EXPONENT
is a welcome visitor among us and many
spicy bits are read from it. We have a
Theological Class organized, composed of
the members of our Association, we meet
on Monday evenings and read chapters from
the Bible, Book of Mormon and Doctrine
and Covenants; this class proves a great
benefit to the Association, for we can speak
upon, and quote from the chapters read. A
few weeks ago the girls prepared what we
called a "Re-union Supper;" it was arranged
in the meeting house, on two large tables
reaching from one end of the hall to the
other. Although flowers were scarce, the
tables were beautifully decorated with all
kinds of lovely fall flowers, which added
greatly to the enjoyment of the feast, thanks
to the committee on bouquets. Among the
invited guests were the bishop and his
counselors, the heads of the Relief Society,
and the Deacon's Quorum of young men.
After the supper was over, the gentlemen
of the Deacon's Quorum presented our As-
sociation with a handsomely bound Record
or Minute book; after which all enjoyed
themselves in the amusements of the dance,
which the young men had arranged to finish
up with. A good spirit prevailed.
	Very respectfully,
		MRS. M. J. PIERCE, Pres't.
				M. H. PAUL, Sec'y.
