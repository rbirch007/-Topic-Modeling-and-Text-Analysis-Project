R. S. REPORTS.

	The meeting of the Senior and Junior
Co-operative Retrenchment Association held
in the Fourteenth Assembly Rooms, Oct.
30th, 1875, was largely attended. The
sisters were spirited and earnest in their
remarks, many subjects of interest being
brought before the meeting. Miss E. R.
Snow informed the sisters that they would
have the opportunity of representing them-
selves at the Centennial next year. It was
to be a national affair, and not a religious
one, and every creditable specimen of
home-work from a collar to a carpet would
be accepted; she alluded to what Joseph
Smith had said concerning the great of the
earth coming to Zion to purchase the finest
fabrics, for they would be manufactured by
the Saints.
	Mrs. W. East gave a sort of farewell testi-
mony to the sisters, previous to going East
with her husband upon his mission; said
she had President Young's permission to
accompany him as a missionary. She had
promised the Lord if He would give her a
testimony of this Gospel and of plural mar-
riage she would bear a faithful testimony to
others. Her prayer was answered, and this
would be an opportunity to faithfully dis-
charge her obligation to her Heavenly
Father. Several other sisters spoke and
the diversity of spirits which actuate dif-
ferent persons was apparent; but the Presi-
dent, Mrs. M. I. Horne, manifested great
caution and wisdom, and all must have un-
derstood the Spirit of God was the prevail-
ing power.
