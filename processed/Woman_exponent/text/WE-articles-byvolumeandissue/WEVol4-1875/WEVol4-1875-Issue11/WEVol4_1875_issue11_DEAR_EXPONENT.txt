DEAR EXPONENT:

	I have expected to see a report of our Re-
lief Society in your valuable paper before
this date, but as you have not yet received
it, I would say that the Relief Society and
Young Ladies Association are both doing
excellently well. The sisters old and young
are alive to the importance of their duties,
as mothers and daughters in Israel; more es-
pecially since the good time we had when
visited by Sister E. R. Snow and her asso-
ciates, while on their visit South last sum-
mer. We have been called upon to mourn
the loss of one of our most faithful members
and teacher in our Relief Society. Sister
Mary Banks, aged thirty-six years. She
leaves a husband and seven children to
mourn her loss; she has been a member in
the Spanish Fork choir twenty years. She
was a faithful Saint and a virtuous careful
wife and mother, a dutiful daughter a sister
dearly beloved. Her sudden death was
quite a shock to her many friends and rela-
tives.
	Yours in the Covenant,
