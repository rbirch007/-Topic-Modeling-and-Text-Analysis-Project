LITTLE LETTER.

[The following came to hand very late; but
it will do to consider the old proverb, "bet-
ter late than never," as well in this case as
in thousands of others. Ed.]

	Special to the EXPONENT. 
		SPRINGDALE KANE Co. U. T.
						July 27th, 1875.

	Since the 22nd the thermometer has
ranged from 68 to 100 in the shade. Last
Friday the heat was intense.
	We have our Sunday meetings and schools
regular here. The boys have the privilege
of talking too. Some of them can speak
very well.
	The apricots are all gone; the peaches,
apples and figs are getting ripe; the green
corn has grown too big to eat; the small
grain is harvested; the grape crop will be
heavy this year.
	With good wishes to the Northern peo-
ple, I remain your servant,
