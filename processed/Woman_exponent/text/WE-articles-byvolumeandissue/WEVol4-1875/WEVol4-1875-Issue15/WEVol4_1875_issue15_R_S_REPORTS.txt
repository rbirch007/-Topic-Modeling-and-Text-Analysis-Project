R. S. REPORTS.
	The meeting of the Senior and Junior
Co-operativd Retrenchment Associations
held in the Fourteenth Ward Assembly
Rooms Saturday Dec. 11, 1875, was one of
special interest and importance. After the
reading of minutes of the previous meet-
ing and from the junior societies, President
Mrs. M. I. Horne arose and expressed her
pleasure in the improvement manifested by
the minutes from the different societies of
the young ladies. After making some ex-
cellent remarks to the elder sisters as well
as the younger, she called upon those sis-
ters who had been visiting the different set-
tlements north and south to address the
meeting. Accordingly Mrs. E. Davis arose
and gave a brief account of her visit through
Cache, Malad, Morgan and Summit coun-
ties; said she had passed the time pleasantly
and had been kindly treated everywhere,
and that she had held in all fifty-three meet
ings.  She said that everywhere she had
advised and encouraged the sisters to do
something towards representing Utah at
the Centennial.
	Mrs. E. S. Barney also gave an account
of her trip south, she had visited many set-
tlements in Sanpete and Sevier counties; al-
so Utah, Millard, Beaver, Iron and Wash-
ington counties, had held about forty-five
meetings, and had been well received and
entertained everywhere. A spirit of refor-
mation was among the people, and the de-
sire to awake and commence anew, was the
spirit of the people almost universally.
Coun. Miss E. R. Snow then made a few
spirited remarks in regard to the improve-
ment of the young ladies, and also spoke
earnestly of the interest all should feel to
have the women of Utah well represented
at the centennial. Miss Rebecca Manning
was proposed and accepted as Assistant Sec-
tary of the meeting.
MISS AMY ADAMS,
Secretary.
