WOMAN'S RECORD.

	MRS. F. W. Gillette recently delivered a
sermon before the Michigan State Conven-
tion of Universalists.
	MRS. Rice of Wilbraham, Mass., who is
ninety-one years old, gathered bushels of
cranberries and chestnuts for sale this year;
she walks regularly twice a week, one mile
to church winter and summer; she takes
care of herself and lives alone, and in all
probability will live ten years longer.
	EMMA Brainard Ryder, M. D., who has
attained an extensive and superior reputa-
tion as a lecturer on Physiology, is residing
in New York for the winter, at the residence
of Dr. Clemence L. Losier. This talented
lady recently lectured before the students of
Elmira College and Cornell University.
	REV. Mary Thomas Clark of Dublin,
Wayne Co., Indiana, was the first woman in
the West invested with power to solemnize
marriage. Mrs. Clark has been for many
years an active worker for Woman's Suf-
frage. She is an Fngiish woman of very
superior intellect and attainments, has read
much, and possesses all the advantages of
fine culture. She is a minister of the Uni-
versalist Church.
	QUEEN Victoria followed the remains of
John Brown, father of her faithful body-
servant, to the grave. She and the Prin-
cess Beatrice returned to the house, after
the hearse had departed for the grave-yard,
and helped to comfort the bereaved widow.
