HOW TO APPRECIATE BLESSINGS.—It has
been remarked often, and very truly, that
people are incapable of appreciating any-
thing until they have been deprived of it.
This applies even to our most common bless-
ings. We do not know how refreshing and
delicious pure cold water is especially from
"the old oaken bucket that hangs in the
well," until we happen to be in some place
where the water is brackish or hard, and we
cannot obtain any fit to drink. For several
weeks during most summers, the prevail-
ing feeling is that there is nothing else in
the world so delightful as a breath of fresh
cool air. But when we have a cool comfort
able summer, we think little of it. So it is.
The poor prisoner feels that the liberty
to which the multitude outside seem so in-
different, is a boon of priceless value. The
hungry appreciate food, and the thirsty
drink.
