	THE NAME, JESUS.

I think upon that sacred name,
	And in that name, I pray;
It kindles in my soul, a flame
	That cheers me day by day.

The music of that precious name,
	Above all minstrel's art
Wakes in the mind, a nobler aim,
	And motive in the heart.

I feel a virtue in that name—
	The name in which we pray:
That holds, in faith's eternal chain,
	The powers of hell, at bay.

That name embodies strength and pow'r,
	Prolonging mortal breath;
And often in the trying hour,
	Averts the hand of death.

I boast the merit of that name—
	I venerate its power—
Its matchless potency I claim
	As bulwark, shield and tower.
							E. R. S.
