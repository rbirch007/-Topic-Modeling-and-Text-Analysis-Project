	CEDAR CITY, IRON CO., NOV. 20th '75.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Sister Barney arrived here yesterday, and
held a meeting at eleven o'clock, and start-
ed for Parowan at 3 p. m. my husband and
myself accompanying her, we had a good
time together, also an excellent meeting,
truly enjoyable.  Sister Barney carries a
good influence with her and truly has the


spirit of her mission. We think if two or
three sisters from head quarters could come
along occasionally it would arouse the sisters
to their duty in regard to their religion,
both practically and spiritually. I had not
thought that she or any other woman in
Utah, could talk so long and so well to an
audience as she did. I am rejoiced to find
they are coming out and declaring them-
selves to be sincere in what they profess.
The people both here and in Parowan felt
well and thought she had come just at the
right time when Reformation was needed.
We are pleased to hear of John Henry
Smith being ordained Bishop.
	Yours in the faith of the Gospel.
								J. B.
