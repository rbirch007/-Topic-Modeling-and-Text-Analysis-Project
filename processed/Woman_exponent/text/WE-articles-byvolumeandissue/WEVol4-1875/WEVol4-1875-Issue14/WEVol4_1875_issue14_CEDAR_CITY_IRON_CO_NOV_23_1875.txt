	CEDAR CITY, IRON Co., Nov. 23 1875.
DEAR EDITOR:
	We have had such a feast to-day in this
beautiful little city of ours. Sister Barney,
from Salt Lake visited us and held a gener-
al meeting. Her discourse was spirited and
full of principle; it ran chiefly upon the
UNITED ORDER and HOME MANUFACTURE
in its various branches. Her heart was full
to overflowing, and it was as she said, when
she looked at the faces of the daughters of
Zion, whose bright eyes beamed with intel-
ligence, it was an index to the heart that
the spirit of God was there.
	She cautioned the young ladies against
forming any acquaintance or being united
with the wicked and corrupt outsiders; in
fact I think she talked of everything that
was beneficial and necessary.
	She said "I try to be as charitable as I can
in my feelings towards my enemies, know-
ing they are the children of our Father in
heaven, and if they would but repent of
their sins and redress our wrongs and let us
worship our God according to the revela-
tions which He has given us, and according
to the declaration of independence, then the
will of our Father might be done on the
earth as it is done in Heaven."
	Your sister in the gospel of peace,
