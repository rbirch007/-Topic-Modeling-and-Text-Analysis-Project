		HARRISVILLE NOV. 21st, 1875.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I think it a duty, as well as a pleasure to
send you a report of our Society, we are
still improving and endeavoring to do good;
our meetings are attended with a good
spirit, peace and harmony prevails. We
received a visit from Pres. Eliza R. Snow,
on the 11th of Nov. and also Mrs. Harriet
Brown and Mrs. Frodsham from Ogden.
The house was well filled a goodly number
of brethren. both young and aged.
	Pres. Eliza R. Snow arose and addressed
us at some length, upon the most beneficial
principles of the Gospel, which we wish to
appreciate as we ought, for it was a source
of great pleasure to hear instruction from
her lips. She then proceeded to organize a
Young Ladies' Retrenchment Association,
and gave them instruction in the line of
their duties. Then followed Sisters Brown
and Frodsham and also some appropriate
remarks by our Pres. Melissa Shirtliff.
	The Presidency of the District expressed
themselves pleased with the instruction
that had been given.
	Ever yours in the Gospel:
		MRS. MARY ELIZA TAYLOR,
								Sec'y.

	The Young Ladies Retrenchment Asso-
ciation in the Twelfth Ward, Salt Lake City,
was organized September last by Miss Eliza
R. Snow, assisted by Mrs. Zina D. Young,
and Mrs. Priscilla Staines (President of the
Twelfth Ward Relief Society). Mrs. Ro-
mania Pratt was chosen President, with
Counselors, etc. The Society has increased
in numbers considerably, and a good feeling
prevails universally among the members.
A spirit of mutual improvement is apparent,
and all seem desirous to appreciate their
blessings and privileges. Essays and select
readings, with speaking, praying and sing-
ing are the prominent features of our meet-
ings, and we hope to improve in every good
word and work.
			ELIZABETH KINGSBURY.
						Ass't Secretary.
	Dec. 8th, 1875.
