DEAR EDITOR:
	Knowing that you feel a decided inter-
est in our advancement, I send you a report
of the Thirteenth Ward Retrenchment As-
sociation, and of the progress made since
our organization in March 13th, 1875. Our
society then numbered twenty three mem-
bers not including the officers. Now there
are seventy-five belonging and numbers
steadily increasing.
	In August last our President Mrs. E.
Thomas, opened a knitting school for the
young ladies of the society, teaching all
kinds of wool knitting, principally jackets,
intending to be ready in the fall to fill or-
ders for them.
	The young ladies were successful in learn-
ing, but have not been able to obtain yarn of
colors and quantity to suit them. There is a
lively interest taken by the President, as-
sisted by some of the members in preparing
articles for the Centennial. Last week she
visited the Provo Woolen Mills and expects
to receive from there a good assortment of
colored wools.
	There is great interest taken, by the
members in improving and gaining a know-
ledge of their religion; all seeming to real-
ize the advantages offered by meeting to-
gether and being instructed by those who
are spending their time for the good of
Zion.
	Most Respectfully Yours,
			L. A. Ashby,
				Secretary.
	Thirteenth Ward, Jan. 26, 1876.
