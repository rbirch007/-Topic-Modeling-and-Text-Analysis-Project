	WELLSVILLE, CACHE COUNTY, May
						20th, 1875.
DEAR EDITOR:
	We held our Monthly Meeting on Thurs-
day the 6th inst. Bishop Maughan with
several brethren and a great many sisters
were present.
	Bishop Maughan motioned that we adopt
President Young's counsel in regard to
round dancing; upon which a unanimous
vote was carried in favor of abandoning
them; also that the sisters of the Relief So-
ciety organize a Ladies Retrenchment As-
sociation which was also unanimous.  We
have commenced the summer season of
sewing meetings for the benefit of the
needy, in which some of the sisters take
great interest. On Saturday the 8th inst.,
the ladies made a picnic party out of respect
to our worthy Bishop who has been called
on a mission to Europe.  Two hundred and
sixty persons partook of the bounties of life
with which the tables were spread, and the
glasses of pure water added greatly to the
scene as tea and coffee were entirely dis-
pensed with.  The brass band kindly ren-
dered their services and enlivened all with
their cheering music.  After the tables
were cleared; short and appropriate ad-
dresses were delivered by some of the
brethren and sisters, with singing and dan-
cing.  All who were present enjoyed them-
selves and agreed it was the most pleasant
party we ever had in Wellsville.  We take
pleasure in forwarding subscriptions for
your valuable paper, and hope it will in-
crease as we feel it deserves to do.
			Respectfully,
					E. WHEATON, and
