	TO MY MOTHER ON HER BIRTHDAY.

Dear Mother! hear the knell of time
Proclaim once more with measured chime
The day of joy, that gave thee birth
Among the noblest of the earth;
When prophets and inspired men
The Priesthood hold on earth again.
To Joseph Smith the angels brought
The self-same truths thy fathers sought.
To them this privilege was denied;
Without the Gospel they have died.
For thee has been reserved the right,
This precious boon of Gospel light,
Ordained before this world has sprung
From chaos, other worlds among,
A great and glorious work to do
For kindred dead. Oh then be true
To all the cov'nants thou hast made?
Thy life has been a mixture more of shade
Than sunshine. Yet for  everlasting truth
Of heaven, embraced by thee in youth,
The afflictions of the saints thou hast shar'd
Their sorrows thou hast seen and heard.
When driven forth by human fiends,
Among their number thou wert seen
Rejoicing in the God of love,
Who suffered this, His Saints to prove;
For He had said "they must be tried
As gold when seven times purified."
Husband and child were buried too,
In the fair city of Nauvoo;
And there their precious forms will lay,
Until the resurrection day
Brings forth the spirits of the just,
Those who in Christ have placed their trust.
When trying scenes thou wert passing through
Thy faith most firm, still stronger grew
That God in power and mercy would
These trials over-rule for good.
And O what blessings are in store
For thee with many thousands more
Of faithful saints who yet may stand
Upon Mount Zion's favored land.
No eye hath seen or mortal heard
What Jesus hath for those prepared,
Who've humbly sought His will to do
And kept the faith loyal and true.
May'st thou, dear Mother live to know
Many more pleasant years below.
May thy posterity increase
In goodness, purity and peace,
And in the great Millennial Morn
Radiant as stars thy crown adorn.
			YOUR LOVING DAUGHTER,
									  M. A. F.
