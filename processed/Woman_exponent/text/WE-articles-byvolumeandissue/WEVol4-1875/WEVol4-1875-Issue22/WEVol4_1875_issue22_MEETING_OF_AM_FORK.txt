			MEETING OF AM. FORK.

	My young sisters it has fallen to my lot
to present before you a few of my thoughts
in writing. In endeavoring to compose the
same, I feel my lack of experience and
ability to prepare anything that will be
adapted to the occasion. We are engaged
in a very good and great work, that of im-
provement, hence I have selected that as a
subject, and although much has been writ-
ten upon it, still I think there is room for
more.
	Improvement is a very comprehensive
word and applies to all the relations and du-
ties of life. I will speak more particularly
of our condition and circumstances; we are
mostly young, and in many respects lack
the experience of our parents and older
persons. Thus it is necessary for us to lis-
ten to their advice and instructions, and by
their experience, we may avoid much that
is evil. We need to improve our minds,
our persons, our habits, and our language.
We should improve in true politeness, so-
cially and morally. As members of the
church and professors of the Gospel, we
should improve in the spiritual graces and
qualify ourselves to adorn the profession we
make, and thus bear testimony to all, that
we love virtue and truth. If we faithfully
carry out the purposes of our Association
we will be diligent in our duties, in attend-
ing the meetings, and in listening to the
instructions given us. With the blessings
of the Lord we mean to improve.
