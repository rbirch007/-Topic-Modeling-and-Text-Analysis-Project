		OBEDIENCE TO PARENTS.

	It seems to me that parents should, at all
times, and in all things, command respect
and love from their children and live in
such a manner that their children would al-
ways honor them. Some children when
they arrive at the age of maturity get high
notions into their heads, and consider them-
selves far superior to their parents, who
have cared for them in sickness and health,
and provided for them, perhaps depriving
themselves for their sakes. All children
should, when they are old enough consider
it their duty to help sustain, support and
comfort their parents in their declining
years, and let them know that their child-
ren's hearts are filled with gratitude for the
kind care they have bestowed upon them
in their youth.
	We are all children in one sense of the
word, and we should all strive to please our
earthly parents, and greatest of all conduct
ourselves in such a manner as will be pleas-
ing in the sight of our Heavenly Father.
							A. E. M.
	Pine Canyon, March 17, 1876.
