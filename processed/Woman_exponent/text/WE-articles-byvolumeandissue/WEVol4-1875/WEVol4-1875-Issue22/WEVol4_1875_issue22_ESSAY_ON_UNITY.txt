ESSAY ON UNITY.

	By the united efforts of the people na-
tions rise, and become powerful; by devia-
tion and discord they fall and crumble to
pieces. Jesus prayed that his disciples,
might be one with Him, as He was one
with the Father. Thus we see it is neces-
sary for the people of God to be united to
carry on the work of God. It is by the
unity of the Latter-day Saints that they
have been able to stand the persecution of
the enemy, and build up the kingdom of
God in these the latter-days. By their
united efforts, and the blessing of God at-
tending them, they have made Utah which
was once a barren desert, to become a beau-
tiful field, all that has been accomplished
has been by the Saints united faith and con-
fidence in God. If God was to suffer their
union to be broken, they could not accom-
plish anything.
	Dear sisters, what can we do to help build
up the kingdom of God? We can do a
great deal if we are united. God has in-
spired His servants to cause these associa-
tions to be organized in order that we young
girls may be trained according to His will,
and forsake the pride and vanity of Baby-
lon. Then let us with one heart and one
mind unite together and carry out those
instructions which are calculated to benefit
and bless us.

	How joyful and pleasant when sisters agree,
	Bound closely together in sweet unity-
		S. L. City, March 29, 1876.
