		A. OF AM. FORK, Feb. 6, 1876.

	Dear sisters I am happy in meeting with
you to-night on this the first anniversary
of our organization. I have chosen the
subject of obedience and have tried to write
a few words on this great and glorious prin-
ciple. Believing what the Psalmist David
said to be true, namely; that "obedience is
better than sacrifice, and to hearken than
the fat of rams," I have endeavored to
comply with the request made, to exchange
a few thoughts in this way, with you my
young sisters. I think to-night would be a
very good time for each one of us to take
into consideration how much, and how far,
we have been obedient during the past year?
Has my father or mother asked anything of
me that I have not complied with? Have
my leaders in this society required anything
of me that I have refused to try and do?
Have my sisters or brothers sought a kind-
ness at my hand that I have denied, which
would have resulted in good? Or has the
Priesthood given any general instruction
during the past year that I have not felt
willing to carry out? If such is the case
with any of us, we had better cultivate the
spirit of obedience, for if we cannot obey
in little things we shall fail in larger ones.
If the maxim is true; that "the boy makes
the man," I maintain that the girl makes
the woman, and if the girlhood is charac-
terized by virtue and obedience, the wo-
manhood will be rewarded, at least by a
happy reflection of the past, and bright
hopes for the future, when justice will be
meted out to all. Then dear sisters, let us
be encouraged to obey all commands that
are for our good, remembering that with-
out obedience we can never return to the
immediate presence of our Heavenly Fath-
er. One of our best poets says, "obedience
is the common lot of all. No being is ex-
empt. Not even God." And if that
glorious, powerful Personage who holds the
Universe in His hands is not free from the
law of Obedience, I ask is poor mortal im-
perfect man? No Verily No.
