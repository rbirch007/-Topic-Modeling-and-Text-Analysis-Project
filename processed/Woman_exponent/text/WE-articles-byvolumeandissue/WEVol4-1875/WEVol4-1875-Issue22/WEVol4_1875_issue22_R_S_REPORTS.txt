R. S. REPORTS.

	Meeting of the Senior and Junior Co-op-
erative Retrenchment Association held in 
the Fourteenth Ward Assembly Rooms,
Saturday, April 1st, 1876. Owing to the 
rather severe illness of President Mrs. M.
I. Horne, Miss Eliza R. Snow took charge
at the meeting. After the usual prelimi-
nary ceremonies, singing, prayer, etc., min-
utes were read by the young secretaries
from the various wards, which were ex-
ceedingly interesting, and showed percepti-
bly the marked improvement in these socie-
ties, and fully illustrated the benefit which
is being derived from the organizations. It 
is producing a unity and good feeling
among the people, and developing in them
gems of thought and expression, which are
elevating and inspiring and cannot but pro-
duce good results. Miss Eliza R. Snow,
Mrs. Zina D. Young, and several other able
and intelligent sisters addressed the meet-
ing their religion. The silk culture and 
straw business were largely descanted upon.
There was a very good specimen of a silk
handkerchief shown, which had been man-
ufactured here from the raw material. The 
attendance was not so large as at some pre-
vious meetings, but a good spirit prevailed,
and all present were benefitted, comforted
and instructed.
