SALT LAKE CITY, March,— 1876.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The 11th Ward Relief Society is progress-
ing and doing much good; the poor in the 
ward are cared for and the sick attended to.
We meet together to make warm clothing
for the aged, the poor, and the Lamanites.
We do not want any to suffer, whether Jew
or Gentile. The Teachers look after the 
poor and see that no one suffers. Our Bish-
op and Counselors meet with us occasion-
ally and give us a word of encouragement,
and ask God to bless us in the good work,
and tell us that we are a great help to them
in looking after the needy.
	The society has helped to build the 11th
Ward New Meeting House, and when calls
are made for missionaries or anything else,
we are ready to aid in helping. A great
number have been baptized and renewed
their covenants. We feel the spirit of the 
Lord in our meetings and great love for 
each other. Our sisters met and made
clothing and bedding etc., and gave more
than one hundred dollars worth to the sis-
ters and children who have gone to Arizona
on missions.
	I know this is the work of God, and feel
happy and proud to be a member of the 
Relief and Retrenchment Societies for they
are blessed and doing much good.
	I will give you a report of the Relief So-
ciety disbursements from the 19th of April
1869, to Dec. 31, 1875.
Relief to the poor.................... $ 1,431 26
Donated to New School House.... 381 18
Emigrating the poor..................... 145 90
Missionary donations....................	32 75
Share in Co-op Store.....................	80 00
Land bought for the Society......... 251 19
	Total............   ................. $2,259 19
