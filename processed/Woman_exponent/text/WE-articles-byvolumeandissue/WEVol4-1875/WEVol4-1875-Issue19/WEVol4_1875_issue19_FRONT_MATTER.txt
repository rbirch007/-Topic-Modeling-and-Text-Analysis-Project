VOL. 4.		SALT LAKE CITY, UTAH, MARCH 1, 1876.		No. 19.


[For the EXPONENT.
	PRAYER
How sweet to the soul is prayer's holy spell,
Its heart-soothing powers no language can tell,
As up from earth's shadows the soul takes its flight
To dwell for a season in heaven's bright light.

When joy fills our bosoms how sweet 'tis to say,
We thank thee dear Father for life's pleasant way.
For sunlight and moonbeams and all the fair flowers,
That dock with such splendor life's beautiful bowers.

We thank Thee for morning's bright glorious beam
That scatters the shadows from night's weary dream.
For cool shades that screen from the noon's piercing ray
And for the sweet rest of the calm closing day.

We thank Thee for home, for the dear ties that bind
Our hearts in one chain with the good of mankind,
For fond words that cheer amid sorrow and pain,
Like murmuring streams in the desert's wild plain.

And oh when the heart is in anguish laid low,
When moonbeams and sunlight's all shrouded in woe,
'Tis then—"Oh my Father" ascends to the skies,
And weak hands are lifted for help to arise.

And the one in Gethsemane's garden bowed down
Who first bore the Cross and now weareth the Crown,
Lifts up His afflicted, guides on through the night
'Till safely they're moored in the Haven of light.

We thank thee our Father for this precious gift,
While such boon is ours not wholly adrift;
As the compass and star is the mariner's guide,
So prayer and the Savior when sorrows betide.
			HOPE.
