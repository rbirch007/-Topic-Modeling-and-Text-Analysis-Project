A WORD TO THE GIRLS.

	In a previous number of the EXPONEET,
there was a few words of advice to mothers,
to warn their daughters. I have often
wished that I could express my views on
this subject. There are none who can
more fully realize this advice than those
who have grown up daughters. The ques-
tion has often presented itself to my mind,
do our girls realize the precipice on which
they stand, or the danger they are in, when
they encourage the attentions of those who
are not of us? I never see or think of
them but I feel to say girls beware of the
outside elements, and shun them as you
would serpents in your path, for it is not
love they bear you, or yours, but destruc-
tion they would like to see come on the
people of God; and destroy that which some
of our best men have shed their blood for.
	Girls obey your parents in all things; re-
member they have had more experience in
the world than you have; never allow false
ideas to hold you from seeking your moth-
er's counsel, or confiding in her; you will
find all through life, she is the best and tru-
est friend you can have, for your joy will
be her joy, and and your troubles will be
her troubles.
				M. J. R. HAMPTON.
	Salt Lake City, Feb. 15th, 1876.
