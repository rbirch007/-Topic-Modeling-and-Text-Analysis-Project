	BEAR RIVER CITY, Feb. 8th, '76.
EDITOR WOMAN'S EXPONENT:
	I don't know that you ever have had any
report from us here in our little Ghald[-]a,
(the land between two rivers), and as such I
hope that a few lines will not be without in-
terest to you and your readers
	We have been organized for quite a
length of time into a Relief Society and
enjoy our Association very well. But as
the Spirit of the Lord has moved upon the
presidency of the church to call more es-
pecially upon the young and rising genera-
tion, we had the joyful privilege to enter
into the Retrenchment Society on the 3rd,of
January, A. D. 1876. President William
Neely attended to the organization. Mrs.
Anna Hansen was elected President and
Mrs. Christine Neely and Mrs. Mary Han-
sen first and second Counselors, and Mrs.
Mary Petersen Secretary.
	We are twenty-nine in number, have a
comfortable Meeting-house to meet in and a
meeting every second week and enjoy our-
selves first-rate, and shall all the time do
our best to be worthy of the name of a Re-
trenchment Society in the very spirit and
meaning thereof.
	The EXPONENT is read with great inter-
est among us.
	Yours in the Gospel,
