A GOOD BEGINNING.

	There are many good and noble resolu-
tions made by the great majority of people
very frequently, but the effort of putting
them into operation is delayed from time to
time until they are forgotten or all interest
is lost in them, and therefore no good is ev-
er derived from them. Some persons, there
are too, who continually have so many good
resolutions in theory but fail to put them in-
to practice because they attempt to perform
them all at once, and reach no real benefi-
cial results from any. But the greatest im-
portance in all things is to take the first step,
to make a beginning. Remember in all
things, if you do not begin, you will never
come to an end. The first weed pulled up
in the garden, the first seed in the ground,
the first shilling put in the savings bank,
and the first mile traveled on a journey, are
all important things; they make a begin-
ning, and thereby a hope, a promise, a
pledge, an assurance that you are in earn-
est with what you have undertaken. How
many a poor, idle, erring, hesitating outcast
is now creeping and crawling through the
world who might have held up his head
and prospered, if instead of putting off his
resolutions of industry and amendment, he
had only made a beginning!
	If you intend to reform an obnoxious
habit, make a firm beginning; if you wish
to do a good act begin at once, for no one
can be too early in a good work.
