HOME AFFAIRS.

	OUR TERRITORIAL Legislaters have occa-
sion to feel more satisfaction over the results of
their labors this year than they enjoyed over
the last former session, when Gov. Woods had
the privilege to veto all their enactments. Gov.
Emery is apparently very well pleased with
their proceedings, and approved most of them.
	THE "JUVENILE Instructor" has entered
upon its eleventh year; it is the only children's
paper published among the Latte-day Saints;
and is really an INSTRUCTOR in the true sense
of the word, and should be read by every child
in Utah. It would be no loss of time for par-
ents to read the little paper too, and intelligent
mothers should take pleasure in writing for it.
	THE ENTERTAINMENT by the Sunday School
Musical Union, on the evening of Feb. 21st,
was a most gratifying success. But the farce
"As like as two peas," which concluded the
entertainment, although well rendered by the
performers, was certainly unhappily chosen.
In our opinion, something more amusing, more
instructive and less demoralizing in its nature
should always be placed on the boards at the
Salt Lake Theatre. But more especially should
this subject be carefully considered on such an
occasion, when it is expected that the greater
portion of the audience will be composed of
children.
