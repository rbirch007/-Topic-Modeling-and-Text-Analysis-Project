			JOHANNA PETERSEN LUND. 

			S. L. CITY, June 11th, 1875.
DEAR EDITOR:
	I am only a little girl and have never
written many letters, but mamma wants
me to write to the EXPONENT,  to encourage


her little girls to express their feelings;
I like to read the "Little Letters" I see
published, it makes me feel as though all
little girls everywhere, talked and acted
something alike.  When I was a very little
girl indeed mamma called me her little Gay;
because I was such a merry little creature;
but I grow so fast I am almost ashamed, be-
cause I am not as good as I ought to be. I
want to be very good indeed; I go to Sun-
day School, and try to understand all that is
said there, and attend our Retrenchment
Meetings, and feel so happy in them. I
hope now that the little girls in the country
will write nice little letters to the paper, and
tell us all about their pleasant homes, and
the woods, groves, canyons and beautiful
streams of water.
	Wishing all the little children in Utah
may grow up to be good and useful, I will
say					Good Bye.
						From LULU
