FACTS AND FIGURES
	ACCORDING to the last census in England
and Wales, the females of the population
outnumber the males by 500,000; but above
the age of twenty-five the males exceed the 
females in number. While there were 400,
000 widowers, there were 873,000 widows.
Above the age of ninety, females number
two to every male.
	AT the annual meeting of the Young
Women's Christian Association of Boston,
recently held,  the receipts for the past year
were stated to be $55,763, including a loan
at $35,000. The amount of $48,686 had
been paid on the home; the expense of car-
rying it had been $11,283.
	AT a recent municipal election in Eng-
land, out of 1000 women on the register,
516 went to the polls, which was but 48 less 
than the proportional number of men. And
out of 27,929 women registered where a con-
test occurred, 14,416 voted. Of men there
were 166,781 on the register, and 90,090 at 
the polls.
	FRANCE, in 1770, had a Landed Feudal
Nobility, she had 164,000 land holders.
England had a Landed Feudal Nobility
with about 156,000 land holders. To-day,
France has 5,000,000 land holders. Eng-
land less than 63,000.
	The people of France are growing rich,
the people of England are fast becoming
paupers. Three-quarters of them are al-
ready so.


In France women control and regulate
opinion. It has long been an aphorism in
France. "L'home fait le lois." "La Femme
fait le moems.
	In England, My Lord makes laws, man-
ners, opinions and wealth; for aristocracy
and manufacturing control, absorb and en-
slave England.
	The reason France has broken her fendal
chains, rooted out her law of primogeniture
and distributed her land among 5,000,000
land holders, is solely attributable to the in-
fluence of woman. Woman in France is
the encouraging element, and France gives
social customs, manners, science, and opin-
ions to the world.
	Thus it is that just in proportion as jus-
tice is done to woman; has liberty, inde-
pendence and elevation followed. England
still regards the wife as the property of the
husband, the idiot, the slave, the nonentity
of legal existence. England has receded.
France has advanced.
