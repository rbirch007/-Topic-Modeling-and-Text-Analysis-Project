	HUNTSVILLE, OGDEN VALLEY, May. 
							   10th, 1875. 
DEAR EDITOR: 
	I am a little girl eleven years of age.
We get along very well in Huntsville. We
have had a hard winter, but now spring has
come again, the trees and grass are green,
and men are working in the fields and 
planting gardens.  We have a beautiful
little valley surrounded with mountains.
We had a large funeral yesterday of a very
respected brother, Christian Jensen; he was
fifty years of age; there were thirty teams
that followed him to his grave.  His death
caused a great mourning over our place. 
	My aunt likes your paper very much; it 
gives her such good instructions, and she
delights in reading it.
	We have a young girls' society, and we
are braiding some straw and piecing up a
quilt. 
	Wishing your valuable paper success I 
am, 		Yours Truly:
