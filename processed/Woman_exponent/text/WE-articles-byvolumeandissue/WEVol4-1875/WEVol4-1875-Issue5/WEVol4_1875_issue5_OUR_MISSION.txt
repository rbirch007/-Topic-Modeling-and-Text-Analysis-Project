		OUR MISSION.

	To be or not to be is not the question, for
we are.  But to be and not know the
wherefore of that being. To think there
must be a future, yet scarce can catch a
glimpse of what that mighty future will
unfold.
	How many of us are there that realize
who we are, from whence we came, and
what is our mission here?
	We come into this world; we grow to ma-
turity, then decline to old age and make
our exit from this stage of life, and to all
appearance, we are as if we had not been.
But is it so.  No; we are the children of a
Supreme Being, fashioned after His own
image, immortal souls, full of life and light.
We come as spirits from the eternal worlds
the dwelling place of the Gods.  To do
what?
	To become like those that sent us forth to
work out for ourselves a destiny.  To go high-
erand higher in the scale of intelligence, un-
til we reach our Father's presence. Shall
we then say we have no aim in life, let us
eat, drink and make merry for to-morrow
we die? Or shall we rise up in the strength
of our manhood, and work out for ourselves a
destiny that shall be worthy the approba-
tion of Him who called us into existence.
	Let onward be our motto, and let no day
pass but we can look back to with pleasure and
feel that we have done some good. Some
may say, what can I do? I have no money
to give to the poor, no time to visit the sick,
it is work, work, nothing but work.
	There is something even for them to do.
There is the kind word of comfort to the
husband, or wife; the smile of approval to
the child, the cheery good morrow to the
neighbor, and the thousand and one ways
in which the poor can do as much good, and
realize as much happiness as the rich.
	Time well employed is the poor man's
weath; and how much of that wealth is
squandered in idle talk, and foolish jests, in
back biting neighbors, and in doing any


thing but working out that great problem,
OUR DESTINY.
				      M. E. TULLEDGE.
	Little Kanab.
