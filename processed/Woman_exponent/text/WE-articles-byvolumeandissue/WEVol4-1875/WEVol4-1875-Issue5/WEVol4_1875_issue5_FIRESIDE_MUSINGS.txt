FIRESIDE MUSINGS.

I am dreaming alone by the fire to-night,
As I sit by the hearthstone so radiant and bright,
And I see in the blaze of the coal's gleaming glow
An emblem of this, our strange life below.
The blaze is the wild dancing visions of youth,
So beaming with bright hopes, of glory and truth,
Chasing sorrow and shadowy doubt all away
As the darkness of night fades, with mornings bright ray.

Now the blaze in its glory is sinking so low,
That shadows hang over the coals steady glow,
As fade our bright visions of life's morn away,
They too cast a shadow o'er all our pathway.
Our hearts may still glow with warmth and delight,
But our hopes for the future are seldom so bright,
As in the fresh morning of beauty and love,
Ere dark clouds obstructed the suns rays above.

But lo, as I ponder on life as it seems,
So mixed with deep sorrows and joys fitful gleams,
Now pleasant the pathway, now rugged and hard
And weary the feet that each pitfall must guard:
The fire is fading, gray ashes o'er all
Makes me think with a sigh of the shroud and the pall,
Yet this sorrow is half of its bitterness shorn,
As I catch a bright gleam of Eternity's Morn.
										Hope.
