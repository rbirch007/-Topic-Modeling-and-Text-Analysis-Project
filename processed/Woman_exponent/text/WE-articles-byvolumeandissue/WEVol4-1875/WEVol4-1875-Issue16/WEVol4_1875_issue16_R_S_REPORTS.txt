		R. S. REPORTS.

	Meeting of the Senior and Junior Co-op-
erative Retrenchment Association held in
the Fourteenth Ward Assembly Rooms,
Dec. 18th, 1875. After the usual formula
and reading of minutes, etc., President Mrs.
M. I. Horne addressed the meeting, and
gave some valuable instruction. Coun. E.
R. Snow, in her remarks, said the integrity
of the saints would be their password into
the celestial kingdom; spoke of the great
influence women could use in this kingdom
if they were awake to their duties, and also
of the importance of cultivating the abili-
ties with which God has endowed us for the
good of each other. Coun. Zina D. Young,
Mrs. Martha Young and others gave excel-
lent instructions, and bore earnest testimo-
nies.
	Meeting of this Association at the same
place, Jan. 9, 1876. President Mrs. M. I.
Horne in the opening address wished the
sisters, one and all, "A Happy New Year,"
and hoped that all present might be faithful
in keeping the covenants they had just re-
newed. The meeting was one calculated to
deeply impress the sisters with a sense of
their obligations to God and to each other.
It being very stormy the attendance was
not as large as usual, but they proved the
Saviour's words true, that where two or
three are gathered together in His name He
will be there to own and bless them.
