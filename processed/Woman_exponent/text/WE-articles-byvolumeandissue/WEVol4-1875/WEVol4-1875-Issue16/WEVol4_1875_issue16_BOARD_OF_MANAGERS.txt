BOARD OF MANAGERS.

	A meeting of the ladies forming the ex-
ecutive committee was held at the office of
the territorial board of managers, 105 Kim-
ball block, Jan. 4th, 1876, at 11 o'clock a.m.
Most of the committee were present. The
business transacted was principally in rela-
tion to the flags. President Miss E. R.
Snow alluded to the want of sufficient time
to accomplish anything really satisfactory
or creditable, and said that if arrangements
had been made one year earlier it would
have been a great advantage. A specimen
of very fine lace work was shown, made by
a lady in Morgan city. Maj. Goodspeed
(who was present) said he thought Utah's
principal attraction would be the great va-
riety of mineral specimens and domestic
productions; in those he believed Utah
would exceed any other state or territory.
	It was resolved that meetings should be
held weekly at the same hour and place, for
the transaction of such business as should
be deemed requisite, until further notice.
	Circulars containing all necessary instruc-
tion have been mailed to the different coun-
ties and almost all the towns, either to presi-
dents of the relief societies or the bishops,
requesting prompt and decisive action in
these matters. In the different wards in
this city persons have been selected to visit
and report in regard to those who are will-
ing to contribute their work, etc. Some
have reported themselves as already em-
ployed upon articles of taste and beauty for
exhibition.
EMMELINE B. WELLS,
Salt Lake City.   Secretary
