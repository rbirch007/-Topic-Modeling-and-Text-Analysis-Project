HYGIENIC NOTES.

	For Babies Teething.—Two ivory martingale
rings tied together with a short string are prefera-
able to rubber, which contains mineral liable to
injure the little teeth.

	Poison or Canker.—Pure Olive oil (sweet oil)
administered freely is an excellent remedy for
either of these ills. It is also one of the safest
and best remedies for diarraehea with children.

	For Catarrh.—To take a wide-mouthed glass
bottle fill loosely with cotton and pour into it a
spoonful or two each, of spirits of turpentine,
camphor, ammonia and hemlock, inhale frequently
through the nose and now and then into the mouth,
gives great relief from suffering and will also cure
effectually. The same ingredients mixed without
the cotton, and with oil to lessen the intensity of
the spirits, make one of the best lineaments for
sore throat, swellings &c.

	Sun Bath.—This is the most refreshing and
life-giving bath that can possibly be taken. Liv-
ing and sleeping in a room into which the sun
never shines is a slow and miserable form of sui-
cide.
