A GOLD MINE IN EMBRYO.

	Here are free to all the elements from which
can be obtained all that man need to enjoy.
But his existence depends upon the purity of
the air he breathes, his happiness depends up-
on the society he mingles in, and his society to
him is a nucleus in embryo, that will tell its
tale in after years. If we wish to enjoy a hap-
py result we must bear in mind that all de-
pends upon the right selections.
	Selection in reading, in thinking, in labor 
and of everything that will result in the great-
est good. And as causes produce effects, and
there can be no effect without a cause, it is
quite necessary that our care and attention
should be directed to the cause, while in em-
bryo, that it may be remedied prior to maturi-
ty. Well says one, the nucleus, if there is one
being formed, is so small, that I cannot see it.
	Well in this case there is greater safety in
counsel with those that have a clearer vision.
There must be a nucleus or an effect appar-
ently seen to produce action, which is based
upon two principles, independent or dependent
vision.  If independent we labor by the
promptings of our  intuition, but as there
is no reliable independency secured to man on
earth, we are virtually dependent for every
thing enjoyed, or hoped for, and we labor upon
the principle of faith; and we may call faith
the cause, and knowledge the effect.
	I will here cite my readers to one fact based
upon a mulberry seed, and another upon a silk
worm seed. By faith and works dependent up-
on the blessing of God, the mulberry seed be-
came a forest; and the silk worm seed in the
care of a delicate, faithful, persevering woman
who, prompted by faith, had wandered to this
forest, and with her labor and care, this little
seed became a million; and the million is now
winding its silk.
	For proof dear reader, go and see for yourself,
prior to the silk being exchanged into gold, at
President Young's Cocoonery.
