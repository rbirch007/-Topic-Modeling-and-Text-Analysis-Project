ESSAY ON PRAYER.

	What is prayer? What is the use of
praying? What good does it do us? These
are, no doubt, a few of the questions which
present themselves to the minds of my
young sisters, when they kneel down and
pray before going to bed. Now, in order to
do right and be happy, it is necessary that
we should pray, for our Father in heaven
likes to have us think of Him, and he is
very much pleased when He sees little girls
kneel down and ask Him to bless them; He
loves them and wants to bless them all the
time if they will only ask Him, but if they
don't pray He thinks they do not care any-
thing about Him, and he is grieved and
withholds His blessings from them. Prayer
serves to keep open the channel of commu-
nication between us and the heavens, there-
fore, my young sisters, let us never forget
our prayers, and we will find the more we
pray, the more we will love it; but if we
miss saying our prayers one day, the next
day we feel less inclined and it seems
harder for us to do so. We are surrounded
by evil spirits and influences, and it requires
an exertion on our part, at all times, to
overcome them, and being weak of our-
selves, we cannot do so without the help of
our Father in heaven. We should not only
pray in the morning and at night, but we
should pray in our hearts, all the day long,
that our hearts may always be turned to-
wards God. Let the praise and gratitude
of our hearts ascend unto the Lord by day
and by night, that the channel may be
kept open, and the road between us and our
heavenly Father may always be broken,
that we may have constant access unto Him,
and in doing this we shall ever feel a glad
and happy spirit, and we shall eventually
be numbered with the wise virgins spoken
of in the Bible.
		MRS. JANE E. VAN TASSELL,
				1st Ward, Salt Lake City.
