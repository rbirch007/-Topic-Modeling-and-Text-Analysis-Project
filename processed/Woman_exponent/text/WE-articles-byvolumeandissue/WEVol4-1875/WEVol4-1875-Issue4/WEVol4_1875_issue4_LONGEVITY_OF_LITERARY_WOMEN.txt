LONGEVITY OF LITERARY WOMEN.

	It is really remarkable how many cases of long-
evity occur among women who make authorship
their occupation in life.  Miss Martineau, Miss
Bremer, Mrs. Howitt, Mrs. Strickland, and Mrs.
Browning—all either living or not very long de-
ceased—were born almost at the beginning of the 
century:  Madame de Stael lived to be fifty-one,
Mrs. Bradstreet fifty-nine, Ida Pleiffer sixty-one,
Miss Mitford sixty-nine, Lady Wortly Montague
seventy-two, Miss Edgeworth eighty-two, Miss
Opie eighty-four, Madame d'Arblay eighty-five,
Mrs. Sommerville ninety-one and Madame de
Genlis ninety-six.—"Hearth and Home."
