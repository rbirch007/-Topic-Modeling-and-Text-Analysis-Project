PEN AND SCISSORS.

	BITS of frontier life—Scalps.
	THE war of races—races for office.
	THE largest room in the world is the
room for improvement.
	What word is always pronounced wrong,
even by the best of scholars?—Wrong.
	THE article most commonly sold at fancy
fairs is the visitor.
	WHY is coal the most contradictory arti-
cle known to commerce? Because, when
purchased it goes to the cellar,
	THERE is a time for all things. The time
to leave, is when a young lady asks you
how the walking is.
	WHY should the last boy born to a family
be named Doxology?  Because he is the
last of the hims.
	"TWO soles that beat as one," remarked
the boy to his mother, as she was dealing
with him for his sins with both slippers at
once.
	CHARLES Lamb, when speaking of one of
his rides on horseback, remarked that "all
at once his horse stopped, but he kept right
on."
	WHEN a woman stands at the hymeneal
alter with her intended, you may know she
is about to draw her beau into a knot.
	A COUNTRY girl wants to know where
the laugh comes in about spelling matches.
She knows of but one way.
	THE most bashful girl we ever heard of
was the young lady who blushed when she
was asked if she had not been courting
sleep.


"YOU'RE a fraud,"  is a favorite expres-
sion with some young ladies. Well, nine
cases out of ten they're correct, and if
they look in the glass they'll see another.
	It is as dangerous to inform a man of his
faults without giving offence, as it is to tel
him of his good qualities without flatter-
ing him.
	CAN you inform me," said a student to a
book-seller, "whether I can find anywhere
the biography of Pollock?" "Yes I dare
say you will find it in the "Course of Time."
	SOME ingenious observer has discovered
that there is a remarkable resemblance be-
tween a baby and wheat, since it is cradled,
then thrashed, and finally becomes the
flower of the family.
