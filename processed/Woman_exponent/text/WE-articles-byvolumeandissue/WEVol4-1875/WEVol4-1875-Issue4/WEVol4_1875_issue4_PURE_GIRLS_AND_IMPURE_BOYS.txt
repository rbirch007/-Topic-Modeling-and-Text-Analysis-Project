PURE GIRLS AND IMPURE BOYS.

	Girls, in treating dissipated young men as
their equals, do a wrong they can scarcely
realize. Such men should be made to feel
that until they walk with correctness and
honor in the paths of right, good people
stand aloof from them. Girls who respect
themselves will not be seen with such men,
and will decline to receive them on the fa-
miliar footing of friendship. It is a mis-
taken kindness to poultice when caustic is
needed, and I  inclined to think that a
little sharp decision on the part of the girls
of to-day would go far to correct the gener-
al looseness of morality among young men.
—Woman's Journal.
