DEAR SISTERS:
	It has been my lot to be called from your
mist, but I go cheerfully knowing that if I
can but do my duty, I will have peace and hap-
piness, no matter where I am. Sisters, I could
not go without showing you my friends and
associates in the Society, that I shall carry a
remembrance of the many happy hours we
have passed together, through all time. I love
you dear friends, as though you were in reality
the name I so love to call you, sisters. The
way in which we have been associated with
each other, has seemed to link us closer togeth-
