LAUGH AND GROW FAT.

	The late Duchess of Devonshire grew at
forty very stout, like many other English
women, yet she retained her beauty. "How
have you kept your complexion so pure, my
dear duchess?" asked one of her fellow-la-
dies-in-waiting at the court of Queen Vic-
toria. "By dressing at ease and keeping
my temper," said the handsome duchess.
In France, where women have a natural
taste for what is becoming in dress, a
large woman wears ample draperies and
loose fitting garments, which make her look
and feel at her ease. No labored breathing,
no unusual redness, no fear of suffocation.
She is simply a large beautiful object, in-
stead of a pillow tied in the middle, with a
general air of asphyxia. Tight dressing
never makes a large woman look smaller.—
