			HOME POLITENESS.

	This is a subject that should be carefully
studied, taught and practiced in every fam-
ily, for a home without it cannot be perfect-
ly happy. How many mothers there are
who complain of their children being rude
and disobedient, and yet they never seem
to think they are in part to blame. Instead
of speaking kindly and pleasantly, they are
so harsh and stern that in time the child be-
comes hardened and heedless. If moth-
ers were to speak in a cheerful and
pleasant tone when they ask the child to
perform some small errand, the ready re-
sponse would, be yes mother, and the little
heart would be filled with joy instead of
pain.
	Children have rights that should be re-
spected and held just as sacred as older per-
sons. After a child has performed any duty,
it is far better to say that's a good child, or
thank you my dear.
	People say: "oh my c[---] learn so much
bad language outside, and are getting to be
so rude."
	If all would try to make home pleasant
and attractive, children would love to stay
at home, and would not have the opportu-
nity of learning so much bad language else-
where. If we would treat children polite-
ly they would be more apt to treat each
other politely. As Latter-day Saints we
should endeavor to rear our children in a
way and manner so as to fit them for impor-
tant stations in life. It is the young and
rising generation that must bear off this
Kingdom. No person can succeed in any
position of importance unless they under-
stand the little courtesies that make life
pleasant.
							M. R.
Clifton March 9th, 1876!
