		AND YOUR COUNTRY.

	He who changes a waste place into a
blooming garden or fruitful field, he who
plants a tree, shrub, or vine, has performed
a lasting benefit upon mankind. These
may gladden the eye and heart of thou-
sands, may give food to the hungry, and
shade to the weary, and such acts are mon-
uments to their industry. He who pro-
duces the means to feed, clothe, or give
comfort, has done a good work, and thus far
has carried out one of the objects of his be-
ing. The man who creates one dollar from
the crude and otherwise useless material of
nature is more to be honored than he who
conquers a kingdom, and one who produces
from the soil wherewith to feed the hungry
and clothe the naked is greater in the scale
of usefulness, than he who by the profits of
trade and extortion becomes a millionaire.
Young men and old, be wise and seek out
wisdom and become proficient in all useful-
ness, but rather seek to create, than craftily
take a profit and living from what others
make from natures' elements.—Utah Pomo-
logist.
