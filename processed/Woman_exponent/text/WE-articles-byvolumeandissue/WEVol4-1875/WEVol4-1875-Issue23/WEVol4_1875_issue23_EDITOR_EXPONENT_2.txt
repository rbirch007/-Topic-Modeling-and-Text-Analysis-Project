EDITOR EXPONENT:
	It is some time since you have heard from
I'll Try, so I thought I would drop a line to
your many readers and tell them of a call I
made at Mrs. Rumells', one block east of the
theatre; I had heard considerable said of the
"Straw Hat Establishment," that started one
year ago, and I was happily surprised to see
the marked improvement, and the variety of
hats, so tastily trimmed and all made from
braid manufactured by the ladies of Utah.
	In the working apartment the braid by the
hundreds of score of all kinds, and variety,
was being passed from hand to hand, through
the different branches until it was finished by
the trimmer, for the sales-room, and her slen-
der fingers seemed to travel over the work with
much dispatch and taste.
	In the sales room, I saw a variety of hats de-
signed for both sexes, also very tasteful and
elegant bonnets; ladies of taste and refinement
were there purchasing hats and bonnets for
their own wear.
	The trimmings were principally imported,
but in course of time as our silk raising de-
velops we will be able to have our own ribbons
etc.
	I could not help asking myself the question
are there any Latter-day Saints that are so
whimsical and so lost to reason and sound sense,
that they would rather be seen wearing im-
ported millinery and thereby support a foreign
people, and leave our HOME MANUFACTURES
unsupported, and deprive our own people of
labor and self-sustenance, than to wear arti-
cles manufactured in our own Territory.
	My dear readers, who of you are willing to
patronize, and thus help in sustaining this great
movement, HOME INDUSTRIES.
