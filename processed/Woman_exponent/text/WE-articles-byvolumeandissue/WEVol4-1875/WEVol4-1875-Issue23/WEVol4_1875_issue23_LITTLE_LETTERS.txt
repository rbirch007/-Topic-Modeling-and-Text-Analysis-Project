LITTLE LETTERS.

		GOSHEN, UTAH, March 25, 1876.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I am a member of the Young Ladies' Re-
trenchment Association, and it is improv-
ing very fast. I am not fourteen years old
yet, but I think that these Associations are
the best things that was ever thought of
for the young girls and boys, and the young
people here take great interest in sustain-
ing them.
	I return my older sisters many thanks for
encouraging us. If we would obey those
who are placed over us we would become
useful and honorable women and pleasing
before God. I know I could be far better
than I am, and I mean to try, and as we
grow up our minds will become more
established. Many young people run the
streets and go to all the dances &c., and that
is all they think about, but we will try to
reform from all these things and do better
now. We "the young girls" ought to be the
Deseret humming-bee and work with one
accord to assist the Prophet of the Lord.
	Let us strive with all our might to be
pleasing in the sight of heaven.
		Yours truly,
