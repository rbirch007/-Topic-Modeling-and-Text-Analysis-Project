				LAST DAYS.

How simple some words! yet we cannot deny
They strike with a force, 'tis in vain to defy—
To the fond, faithful heart there are few that convey
Such a host of reflections as crowd the last day.

'Tis the knell of bright joy—of deep grief, of pale fear,
Times' only brief portion, we KNOW will appear,
And rushing through childhood, maturity, age!
We forget to observe the last days in each stage;

So progressive is life! and so noiseless, it steals,
The transitions are made without any appeals,
We pass by each stage as the links in a chain,
Forgetting, we ne'er shall behold then again.

Oh! my heart palpitates, and my pulses beat quick,
My blood rushes thrilling—my breathing is thick,
As I musingly dwell on a many last day
Of gushing delight, that has long past away!—

Who does not remember with heart-aching thr[--]
The last days of happiness! last days of woe?
The last day of friendship, endearments, and love?
The last day on earth of the dear ones above!

The last day of school—of the nursery—of youth,
Are all deeply engrav'd with a pencil of truth
And the last day we spent in our childhood's dear Home
'Ere we leapt into life through its mazes to roam[-]

How pure was the heart! and how clear was the brain,
We dreamt not of sorrow, we dreamt not of pain,
But many last days have since taught us to know
What it is to feel agony, parting and woe!

And how soaring so ever my spirit may be
And how glided soever hopes' summits I see
My hearts dial glows with no sun beaming ray
If friendship, or love, darkly points their last day.

Oh! Earth!—thou enchain'st us with many a spell[-]
And we love thy bright fetters endearingly well—
But let us remember with holy bow'd soul
That 'ere long there will come a last day to the whole.

And that Time—though he moves with inaudible tread
Is lining our features and bleaching our head;
But on let him come, if we truly can say
I'm prepared for the last—aye the very last day!
