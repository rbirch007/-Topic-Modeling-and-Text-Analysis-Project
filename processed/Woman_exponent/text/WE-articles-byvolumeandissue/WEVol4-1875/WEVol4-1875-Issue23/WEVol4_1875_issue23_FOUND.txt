FOUND.

Found in the river at break of day,
A lifeless body, floating away,

Floating away to the wild, wild lake,
Where the rough-ribbed waves roll and break

O'er the white bleached bones of many a score
Who never were found and brought to shore.

Out from the current space that rolled,
Out from the waters so dark and cold,

Out from the refuge for shame and sin,
Dripping and lifeless, they brought her in.

Down to the Morgue, without a groan
Or shudder, they brought the fair unknown:

There in the chamber of death to wait
For friends to claim if they learned her fate.

But oh! for the friends so far away,
She left one dreary and dismal day;

Left —yet a mother whose hairs were gray,
Clung round her at parting to plead her stay.

How oft in that drear dark life must she
Have looked to the home of her infancy;

How oft when the far-off stars looked down
Upon her, wandering about the town.

Must her alpine cottage have sprung to view,
Away 'mid the rough-ridged rocks of blue;

Despised, forsaken, at last she came
Down to the river to hide her shame.

Only a moment upon the bank,
She coiled her hair, with the night dew dank;

Only a moment she heard the roar
And tumult around her —then all was o'er.

Found in the river at break of day,
A lifeless body floating away.

While in her pocket a note all torn
Was found, to send to her friends forlorn.
—Golden Dawn.
