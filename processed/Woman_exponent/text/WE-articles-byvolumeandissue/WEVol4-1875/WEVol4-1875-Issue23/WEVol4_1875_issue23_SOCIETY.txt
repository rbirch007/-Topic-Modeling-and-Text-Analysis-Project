SOCIETY.

	How can we express our ideas of a noble
woman's influence in society, or a good
mother's influence in the family circle? It
must prevail over the minds and hearts of
all those with whom she is associated.
What is it that first caused that inspiration
of goodness and greatness in men, which
we often read about, and who live to-day?
Almost invariably it was the great and good
influence of their mothers. A good wo-
man's influence in society, is greatly needed,
whether truly appreciated or not. Her val-
ue at many times is very much under esti-
mated, yet in some instances highly appre-
ciated.
	Our Woman's Cause, as we term it, is a
just one, our object is a noble one, and we
must seek to aspire to that laudable ambi-
tion for which we have started; and re-
member, where there's a will there's a way,
we have every encouragement to go on and
progress and improve. There is great space
for improvement, not only socially and re-
ligiously, but in our daily life; there are
none so good or great but can find room for
improvement; "Yet judge not that ye be
not judged," but may our great aim and ob-
ject in life be something that is worth all
our time and attention, and when we have
gained that great blessing, I think we will
be amply rewarded for our sacrifices as we
call them here below. And we will then be
prepared to enjoy the SOCIETY of our Fath-
er in heaven, which I hope will be our hap-
py lot.
					E. CROMPTON.
	Am. Fork, March 29th, '76.
