	MONA, JUAB Co.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We have read with great pleasure, the re-
ports of the different Societies, and we feel
much interested in their prosperity, and
wish to be one with them in their efforts for
Mutual Improvement. On the second day
of March the officers of the Relief Society,
assisted by President John Haws, and sev-
eral others, organized a Retrenchment As-
sociation. The following officers were elect-
ed: Miss Maggie Somerville, President,
Mrs. Eliza Somerville, and Mrs. Mattie
Haws, Counselors Mrs. Susie Tibbs, Sec-
retary, Mrs. Hannah Somerville, Treasur-
er. Our resolutions are similar to those
adopted by other societies of the same na-
ture. We hold meetings semi-monthly.
Our object is to enlarge the mind by reading
useful books, and cultivating our moral and
intellectual natures, realizing that this is
the only means by which we can enjoy
true happiness, either in this life or in the
life which is to come. We desire to over-
come evil in its various forms, and to fulfill
all obligations required of us as accounta-
ble beings, destined for eternity. We have
abandoned round dancing, and are trying to
make a more judicious use of our time than
we have done in the past.
	Our Relief Society was organized in 1868,
with Sister Eliza Somerville as President,
she is remarkable for her energy and perse-
verance. She has had disadvantages to
contend with, mainly arising from the
members being dilatory in attending meet-
ings; but I am pleased to say that her un-
tiring efforts have been blest, and that the
sisters seem inspired with new ardor and
zeal, and we hope to accomplish much good.
Our business transactions have been on a
limited scale, yet we have administered to
the wants of the sick and needy, in every
instance that has come under our observa-
tion.
	With an earnest desire for the success of
the EXPONENT,
	I Remain, Respectfully,
