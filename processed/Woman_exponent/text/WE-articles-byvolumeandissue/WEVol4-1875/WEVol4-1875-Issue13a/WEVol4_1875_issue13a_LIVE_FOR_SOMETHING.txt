		LIVE FOR SOMETHING.

	Thousands of women are born, breathe,
live, move and pass off the stage of life,and
are heard of no more; why? They did no
good to the world while in it, none were
blessed by them; none could point to them
as their benefactors, not a line they ever
wrote, not a word they spoke, not a good
deed they performed, could be recalled, and
so they perished from the memory of their
cotemporaries. What value is such a life?
Its possessor has suffered the golden days of
her probation to pass by unimproved, and
her light has gone out in darkness.
	My sisters, let not this be the case with
us, but let us seek to do good, and benefit
each other, and let us do all that ever lays
in our power to build up the church and
kingdom of God.
	My young sisters you have life before you,
do not squander it foolishly; make up your
minds while young to live for something,
understand that you have a mission given
you, and be determined to fill it worthily;
and always reccollect that you cannot be
truly great unless you are truly good; and
make it your constant study to do all the
good you can. You may be inexperienced
and not very widely known, but you can
extend the circle of your acquaintance and
influence by good deeds, and wherever you
go, you can carry an influence with you that
will inspire others with good resolves and
determinations to follow your example. Do
this, and you will write your names on the
hearts of your generation, in letters that
will never perish, and you will enter upon a
career of ever-increasing happiness and
glory.
				JANE E. VANTASSEL.
	First Ward.
