			NIGHT.

When the little feet cease their patter, the tongue
		their clatter.
	Hands and eyes so softly close,
Instead of riot, all is quiet
	The dear ones sink in sweet repose.

And then poor "Query" feels so weary,
	Mind and body, both are tired,
So gathers flowers, from other powers
	And sips the honey so much required.

Oh! gift of Heaven, to mortals given
	The gift to read and understand;
Oh! greater gift, the power to sift
	The truth from error at command.

Oh! blessed treasure, the power at pleasure
	Our thoughts and ideas to portray,
Be mine to power these gems to gather
	To beautify my life each day.

Taylorsville Nov.14th 1875.
