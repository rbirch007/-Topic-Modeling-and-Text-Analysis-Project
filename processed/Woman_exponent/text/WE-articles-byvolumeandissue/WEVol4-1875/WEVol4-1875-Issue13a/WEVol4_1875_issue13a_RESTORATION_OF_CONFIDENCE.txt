RESTORATION OF CONFIDENCE.

	If through the medium of the great reforma-
tion, now in progress among the Latter-day
Saints, the lost confidence can be restored, in-
deed a most desirable object will be attained.
But going to meeting, praying and singing, it
must be remembered, will not accomplish it.
	Through slothful habits into which the peo-
ple have fallen, carelessness in business con-
cerns, want of punctuality, failing to discharge
important duties—for all duties should be con-
sidered important ones—through these open-
ings the confidence which existed among the
Saints in the early times of the latter-day
work, has slipped away. And only by carefully
and conscientiously making all possible repara-
tions, and evincing a steadfast determination
to deal cautiously and justly in future, can that
confidence he won back or the broken bars of
its receptacles be mended.
	There is no danger of any individual trusting
the Lord too far, for He never disappoints
those who fully trust Him, never fails to fulfill
every obligation on His part, never forgets His
promises to His children be they great or small.
But men and women have not reached that
standard of completeness, and are liable to dis-
appoint themselves and each other to forget
their promises, or promise more than they can
perform. Hence the necessity of so much care
and exactness in all the relationships of life.
