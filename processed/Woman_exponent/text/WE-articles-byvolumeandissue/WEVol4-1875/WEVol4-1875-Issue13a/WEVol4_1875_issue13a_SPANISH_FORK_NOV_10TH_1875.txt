	SPANISH FORK, Nov. 10th, 1875.
DEAR EXPONENT:
	Sisters Snow, Howard, Barney, and Sister
Horne visited us last July, and held meet-
ings, giving some excellent instructions up-
on the duties of the sisters of the Relief
Society; showing the necessity of our be-
coming a self-sustaining people; encour-

[Column 2]
aged all to faithfulness in the kingdom of
God. The Relief Society having been or-
ganized in two branches, it was thought
best to organize them in one Society, which
was done, by electing Sister Mary Ferguson
President, Sisters Margaret Miller and Isa-
bel Rockwell Counselors, Alexandrine Snell
Secretary, and Caroline Pace Treasurer. Af-
ter the organization, Sister Snow spoke
very encouragingly, giving many instruc-
tions. We had a general time of rejoicing,
the spirit of God being abundantly mani-
fest upon the speakers and hearers, and we
have since felt our faith strengthened, and
a renewed determination to do all we can to
forward the work in which we feel so
much interest.
	Financially our Society is progressing,
and there seems to be a good spirit among
the people, and we earnestly pray that the
blessings of our Heavenly Father may rest
upon our efforts to do good. July 2nd, the
time of our uniting, the amount in the treas-
ury of both Societies, there was $263.80.
Received by donation $45.89; Profit on la-
bor $20.11; disbursed to assist the poor
$45.45; leaving a balance in the treasury of
$284.35.
