THREE YEAR'S OLD.

	To-day the EXPONENT enters its fourth year.
Upon the supposition that its friends will be
glad to learn concerning its welfare, we cheer-
fully submit the following.
	If at present our journal is not strengthened
by as large an amount of money in the treas-
ury as those immediately connected with its
interests could desire, it is perhaps, as well off
in this respect as many of its flourishing neigh-
bors. And it has more certain capital than
even ready money, indispensable as that arti-
cle is to progression.
	It has for its credentials the good wishes and
faith of wise men and women who favor and
seek the promotion of all useful enterprises.


For its upholders it has willing and earnest
workers whose hopes for its future, supported
as they are by evidences of the past, are bright,
and who consider its prospects full of promise.
	If our kind patrons are satisfied with our ef-
forts, let each consider, as one sister has writ-
ten, that the best way of expressing apprecia-
tion for WOMAN'S EXPONENT is by now sub-
scribing for volume four.
