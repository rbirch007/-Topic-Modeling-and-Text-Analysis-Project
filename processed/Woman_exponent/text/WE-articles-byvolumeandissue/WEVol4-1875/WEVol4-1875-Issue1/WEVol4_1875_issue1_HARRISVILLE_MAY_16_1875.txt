		HARRISVILLE, May 16, 1875.
DEAR EXPONENT:
	It is with feelings of inability that I en-
deavor to write a few lines to mingle my
voice with others of my sisters in promot-
ing the cause of truth: I can think of much
that is good, but how difficult to put it on
paper; I greatly appreciate the EXPONNET
for the instruction it contains and most es-
pecially the wise counsel how to guide our
children aright, for it is a constant fear to
me that I shall not do my duty by them. I
consider the Lord has placed a great re-
sponsibility upon me in giving me children;
therefore it is my daily prayer for wisdom
that I may do a mother's part in teaching,
instructing and governing them aright. I
see many times where I fall short of my
duty to them, but I can see where I am im-
proving, in reflecting upon what I have
read in the EXPONENT concerning these
duties, also many more useful things. I
know this work is true for myself, for the
Lord has been mindful of me and mine in
sickness, and answered my prayers many
times, and healed my children when nigh
unto death, for which I give the praise un-
to Him, the Giver of all good. I have a


good husband who is faithful in doing his
duty both to his family and to his God, for
which I am truly thankful.  I think we are
the most blest women now on the earth. I 
will close by saying may God bless our paper
and those who labor to make it truly val-
uable.
	Yours in all good work,
							M. T.
