TO MY MOTHER.

As water to the parched ground,
As gurgling brook in desert found,
As beacon light to sailor's eyes,
As grasps the hand a long-sought prize,
As meet dear friends who've parted long,
As moves the heart a gentle song:
This is a mother's love to me—
A rich, a heaven-sent legacy.

The acme of impartial love,
The portraiture of that above:
The affection drawn from nature's well,
The love that doth all love excel--
Spontaneous warms a mother's breast,
And ever shines, what'er the test;
Evincing watchful, tender care,
To shield its hope from every snare,

This, kind mother, sweet and mild,
A tribute from thy youngest child.
