FACTS AND FIGURES.

	IN Havanna for $475 you can purchase a
thousand oranges.
	PARIS has 1,500,000 French men and 420,
000 foreigners.
	ENGLAND imports 550 tons of ivory an-
nually, of which she retains for home con-
sumption 270 tons.
	THE amount voted by Parliament for de-
fraying the expenses of the British Arctic
expedition this year is £98,620.
	THE INCREASE of specie in the Bank of
France has been very considerable; re-
cently in one week the increase amounted
to sixteen million frances.
	DURING April the aggregate losses by
fires throughout the United States and
Canada amounted to $10,178,400, aggregate
losses to insurance companies $5,476,400.
	For male teachers in Rhode Island the
average salary is $83 per month, female
teachers $43 the ratio of male to female
teachers is one to four.
	THERE are in Boston 7,214 women tax-
payers whose aggregate taxes alone, annu-
ally amount to $1,296,693 and these very
women are not allowed the privilege of
suffrage.
	IT is asserted by President Abbot of the
Michigan Agricultural College, that forty-
two per cent of the living graduates of that
institution, are engaged in farming or gar-
dening pursuits.
	THE issue of postage stamps of all kinds
by the post-office department during April
amounts to a little over $2,000,000 an in-
crease of about 20 per cent on the corres-
ponding month of last year.
	AN Illinois jury has given a verdict of
25,000 against W. J. Story editor of the 
"Chicago Times" for the publication of an
article assailing the character of Alice A.
Early of Rockford.
	IN Illinois the highest salary paid to male
teachers is $300 per month; to female teach-
ers $220 per month; the ratio of male to fe-
male teachers is 3 to 7.  In Mass. the aver-
age salary per month for men is $94.34, to
women $34.34, the ratio of male to female
teachers is 1 to 8.
