	SALT LAKE CITY, Jan. 24th, 1876.
	The Relief Society of the Eighth Ward
met on the 6th of Jan. 1876 for their Annu-
al Meeting. After the opening of the meet-
ing the receipts and disbursments of the
year were read and showed that considera-
ble had been done in this ward. Our Presi-
dent Mrs. Leaver and Counselors Needham
and Steel are faithful in looking after the
poor and sick. The Spirit of the Lord is
with us, and we have good meetings. Many
are renewing their covenants and are de-
termined to live up to the principles of the
Gospel. After the business of the meeting
was attended to, Sisters Young, Howard,
Goddard and Park addressed the sisters and
gave good instructions. Sister E. S. Bar-
ney read a circular from the Ladies Depart-
ment, Territorial Board of Managers, solici-
ting the energetic co-operation of the Re-
lief Societies in preparing specimens of
HOME INDUSTRIES both useful and ornamen-
tal for the National Centennial, and that a
committee be appointed in each ward for
the purpose of accomplishing as soon as pos-
sible the articles to be forwarded to the 
TREASURER. Sister Annie Cross and Esther
B. Fletcher were appointed as Committee
for the Eighth Ward Relief Society. Bishop
Sheets desired the sisters to retrench in all
things, and to live their religion, and try to
be faithful to the end and left his blessing
with us. 
	ESTHER B. FLETCHER,
	Secretary.
	SPRING CITY, SANPETE Co. Jan. 10[-]76.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The sisters here are alive to their duties,
they are striving to do all they can to for-
ward the work they are engaged in; the
most of them have been re-baptized. Our
meetings are well attended, a good spirit
prevails. We have excellent instructions
from our President Mrs. M. A. P. Hyde.
The people in general enjoy good health,
much more so than they have done in pre-
vious winters.
	We have had an excellent Teachers Meet-
ing to-day, in which the Spirit of God was

[Column 2]
greatly manifested. Pres. Mrs. Hyde pre-
sented the CIRCULAR which had been sent
to the Society concerning the Centennial.
A committee was called to prepare some-
thing to be sent from here to the Centennial
Exhibition. It was remarked to-day that
it had been seven years since our Relief
Society was organized, and there had never
been a discord, or a dissenting voice in all
that time; and every call had been com-
plied with readily, as far as it was possible.
	MRS. P. E. BENSON,
	Secretary.
	ST. CHARLES, Jan. 19th, 1876.
DEAR EXPONENT:
	The Young Ladies' Retrenchment So-
ciety of St. Charles, Bear Lake Co., Idaho
Territory, was organized September 6th,
1875, by Sister Presinda Kimball.
	Miss Amy E. Laker, President,
	Mrs. Nancy E. Pugmire,
	"	Sarah Allred,
	"	Eliza W. Brewer,
	"	Orissa A. Williamson,	Counselors.
	Miss Mary Jacobs,
	Mrs. Laura A. Johnson,
	"	Nancy Hunt,
	"	Maria M. Beers, Superintendent,
	Miss Lizzie M. Pugmire, Secretary.
	Twenty-five young ladies were admitted
as members of the Society. Our meetings
are well attended and all take a great inter-
est in the welfare of the Society. Some of
the young ladies gathered straw last sum-
mer and we are preparing to have it manu-
factured into hats. Sister Presinda Kim-
ball gave us some excellent instructions and
advice while here, and I think it will have
a lasting impression on the minds of those
who were present. The EXPONENT has the
best wishes of the society for its advance-
ment.
	LIZZIE M. PUGMIRE, Sec'y.
DEAR EXPONENT:
	I feel the many friends of the Relief So-
cieties would be glad to learn through your
valuable paper how we are progressing in
Ogden. During the last year we have not
only gained in numbers but I trust in the
Spirit of the Lord also. May the blessings
of God rest upon Sister E. R. Snow for the
seasons of rejoicing we have had through
her meeting with us, also the other sisters
that have at times accompanied her. The
sisters here seem anxious and willing to do
all they can to assist our worthy and much
loved President, Mrs. J. S. Richards
who is ever ready to comfort the sick
and assist the needy. On the 16th of De-
cember 1875, we held our Annual Meet
ing it was 8 years since the Relief Society
had been organized here in Ogden. Presi-
dent F. D. Richards met with us. In the
evening we had a good old-fashioned old
folks dance; all expressed themselves that
it was the best dance they ever attended.
By counsel we have arranged to choose our
officers once a year, or sustain those in office
according to the feelings of the sisters.
The same officers were chosen by a unani-
mous vote. During the last year we have
started the Straw Business and proved it a
success; we have considerable braid on hand
ready for spring.
	We have disbursed over $1,011,50 to straw
work and to the poor; we have on hand
property amounting to over $250. May
God prosper and bless all the Relief Socie-
ties that they may accomplish that for
which they were organized.
	A. M. FRODSHAM,
	Secretary.
