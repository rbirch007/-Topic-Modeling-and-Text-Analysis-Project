RETRENCHMENT.
	There are many subjects that all can improve
upon and likewise many things; it is not
dress alone but we can retrench in speaking
evil and in others things that are as necessary
as those of which I have spoken; we need to
retrench in backbiting above all other things.
There are also many words we use when talk-
ing that are not very becoming; many of us
think it makes us look smart, but it does not I
assure you. I am not as perfect as I should
be, but I can improve with the rest of you; let
us retrench in wasting our time; many times
we say that we have not time to do thus and
so; but we must try to use our time to better
advantage than we do; I do not mean to say
that we are all like this, but if we have a piece
of work to do, that we could do in one hour, and
should take two, that is what we should re-
trench in, and it will teach us to keep our minds


on what we are doing, and how to save time
and never leave for to-morrow what should be
done to-day.
	Let us improve our time in learning to make
our clothing, that our apparel may be the work
of our own hands; and by so doing we shall find
our time well improved indeed.
		ELIZA VILATE ASHWORTH.
	First Ward.
