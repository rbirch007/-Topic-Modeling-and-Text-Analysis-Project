HOME AFFAIRS.
	THE CHOIR and young people of the Four-
teenth Ward under the direction of Mr. A. C.
Smith gave a concert for a benevolent purpose
in the Fourteenth Ward Assembly Rooms,
Monday evening Jan. 17th, which reflected
great credit upon their musical talent and elo-
cutionary powers. The selection of songs was
in good taste and the execution fine and artis-
tic. The songs of the young ladies particularly
were full of exquisite pathos and melody.
	A VERY interesting entertainment was given
by the ladies of the Relief and Retrenchment
Societies of the Twelfth Ward at their ward
school-house on Friday afternoon Jan. 1[-]. The
tables spread for the occasion were literally
loaded with good things, and arranged in an
inviting manner. Bishop Hunter and several
of the Twelve were pre ent, and many other
invited guests beside the members of the ward.
After dinner Bishop L. W. Hardy opened the
entertainment by addressing them upon the
object of these gatherings and spoke most en-
couragingly to all those who take part in these
Societies. There were anthems by the ward
choir, and songs and reci[-]ations all performed


excellently well, especially the rendering of the
recitations by J. C. Graham, and J. Midgeley.
Some of the songs were very finely executed.
In the evening the young people enjoyed them
selves in dancing. Such Reunious' are bene-
ficial to the people young and old, bringing
those together who are full of the spirit of the
work of the Latter Days, and assimilating the
spirit of benevolence with that of sociability
and enjoyment.
