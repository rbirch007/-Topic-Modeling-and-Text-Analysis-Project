MATTIE GOLDFISH'S HISTORY, OBITU-
ARY, FUNERAL SERMON AND BENEDIC-
TION.
		HISTORY.
	George and Mattie Reed occupied a warm
place in the friendly corner of my heart;
so when they brought me a globe contain-
ing two pretty gold fish I at once christened
them George and Mattie.
	OBITUARY.
	One morning as the accustomed meal was
being scattered Mattie swam unsteadily, and
I saw the shadow of approaching death.
The globe was more frequently filled and
the cracker more finely pulverized, but all
availed nothing.
	After three days sickness Mattie folded
her gills closely and sank to the bottom of
her crystal world; while George circled
above apparently unconscious that anything
sad had occurred.
	FUNERAL SERMON.
	My text, or leading thought, may be
found in a choir p[-]em recently published in
the "Morning Herald:"
	"I breathed naught but love on my flowers."
		H. T. K.
	I have no congregation of our pet's peers,
(shoals of fish) to address, if I had they
would not understand my language.
	But to boys and girls, large and small, I
say, tenderly regard all life even that of
birds, and fish and flowers, for they will all
live again, and in the again they will un-
derstand our language, and we will under-
stand theirs. Let them have no cause to
accuse us then of thoughtless disregard.
	A goldfish's spirit is as undying asthat of
Earth which furnishes us an abiding place,
and yields us sustenance, as is Mrs. King's
cherished flowers, or as is the immortal
spirit of man.
	This being true, we infer that when we
come in possession of our portion of resur-
rected or renewed earth, "wherein dwelleth
righteousness," and receive the keys of our
Eternal Mansion, we may confidently look
amid our surroundings to find all specimens
of life that we have encircled within the
radius of our love.
