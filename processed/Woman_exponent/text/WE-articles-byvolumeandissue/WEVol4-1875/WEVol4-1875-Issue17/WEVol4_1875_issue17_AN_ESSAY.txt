AN ESSAY.
	READ BEFORE THE YOUNG LADIES RE-
TRENCHMENT ASSOCIATION OF THE THIR-
TEENTH WARD.
	I think we all realize that God in placing us
upon this earth had an object in view, a work
for us to accomplish, that was planned and
commenced in the ages that have passed, to be
finished in the coming future. It was but a
short time since, that we were in His presence,
wrapped in the holy light of His look, with joy
and gladness worshipping our Creator; and to
earn a right to the place which we held and
show our obedience to His will, we are request-
ed to go and do His work.
	Our eyes are opened; we see the paths that
we must walk, the burdens that must be car-
ried, the struggles and temptations to be resist-
ed before the end is reached; the briars and
thorns by the wayside, that when His light is
withdrawn, we in our blindness shall grasp un-
knowingly until made aware of our mistake by
the sharp sting of regret which will follow.
Still His work lies there and we full of hope
and faith are eager to begin, that we may the
sooner enjoy the rest.
	Our knowledge is taken from us, we step
forth into this life, and the one which we
have left becomes a blank; God's light is with-
drawn except one small ray faith. which He
in His mercy allowed to remain; and if we live
as we should that ray will become a stream of
light to guide us back to our Eternal Home.
	Are we living to receive this light? living to
earn the positions we have left? or surrounded
as we are by the temptations of this world, its
joys and pleasures, have we forgotten the ob-
ject which brought us here?
	Let us keep in mind the glorious rest which
is awaiting us, after the toil and labor is over.
And as each day passes, drawing us nearer our
home, make it our aim to live the next day
more faithful and true to the principles that
will lead us to Life Eternal.
		L.
	S. L. City.
