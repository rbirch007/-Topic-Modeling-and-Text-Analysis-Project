HOUSEHOLD HINTS.
	A teaspoonful of powdered borax, dissolved in
a quart of tepid water, is good for cleaning old
black dresses of silk, cashmere or alpaca.
	A Nice Cream Pie.—One cup white sugar, two
thirds of a cup of cream, two eggs; two cups flour
one teaspoonful cream of tartar, half teaspoonful
soda. This will make seven thin cakes, between
which spread the cream.
	Apple Fritters.—Make a batter, not very stiff,
with one quart of milk, three eggs and flour to
bring in the right consistency. Pare and core a
dozen apples, and chop them to about the size of
small peas, and mix them well in the batter. Fry
them in lard as you would doughnuts. For trim-
mings use powdered white sugar.
	To Iron Velvet Ribbon.—Dampen the under
side slightly and draw it back and forth over a ho
stovepipe until the velvet is quite dry. A still
better plan—though in winter not always conveni-
ent—is to lay a wet piece of cotton cloth on a hot
flat-iron placed upside down, and while the steam
is rising from it draw the underside of the velvet
lightly backward and forward over the wet cloth.
