DEAR SISTER:
	On Thursday the 2nd inst. Prest. Jos. F.
Smith learned by Cable-gram the sad news of
the death of Prest. George A. Smith on
the 1st of Sept. This great loss has cast a
fearful sadness over our hearts. Oh! what a
harvest death is gathering from among
God's Earthly Veterans.
	It seemed to me we could not spare Uncle
George, but God is with us and can raise up
another as he did when the Prophet Joseph
was taken. Times seem so precarious just
now that we need every good man, and could
spare traitors and slinks by the host. I thank
Heaven that reformation is commenced in
Zion, for there was perhaps, never a time
when it was more needed. How well I re-
member that Jedediah went from the re-
formation of "57" and now Uncle George
has gone from the reformation of "75" and
will report to the Prophet and hosts who are
faithful with him in that better land. Oh!
that I may live as Uncle George has lived,
the personification of truth, integrity and
unflinching fidelity to the work of Salvation;
loved by God and all the Saints, and when
I depart this life that I may go where he is,
and be numbered with the champions of
eternal truth.
	Our hearts are full of sorrow; but God will
comfort His people; and the fallen has not
fallen, but has stepped into another sphere
where he can do more for us, ten fold than
he could while present with us. He is out
of reach of the puny arm of persecution,
he has conquered and vanquished every foe,
God blessed forever.
	With great respect, I remain your broth-
er in the Gospel,
					F. M. Lyman.
