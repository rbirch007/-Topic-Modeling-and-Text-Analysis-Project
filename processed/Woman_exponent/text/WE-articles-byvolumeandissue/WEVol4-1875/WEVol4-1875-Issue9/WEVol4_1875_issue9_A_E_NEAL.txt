	A.E. NEAL.

	WEST WEBER, Sept. 13th, 1875.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Being a constant reader of your interest-
ing paper, and seeing the reports from the
many different societies—I wish to give you
a brief report of our small branch. Our
settlement is very scattering, and our Presi-
dent, John I. Hart, thought it would be best
to give the sisters at the East end of the 
settlement a chance to have a small branch,
so that they could do something for the
building up of the kingdom of God. As
they are about four miles from the main
part of the settlement, he went, accompa-
nied by Pres. Mary Douglas and several
other sisters, and organized a branch society.
A good spirit prevailed among them, and
they all felt that they would like to put their
mites in for the building up of this king-
dom. Our society is doing pretty well. We
meet once a week to make quilts, or do any
other work that may be needed, and once a
month as a society meeting. A few attend,
but the weather has been very dry and hot,
and as most of the sisters have small chil-
dren, that has been the [---] on of the non-at-
tendance. I think the spirit of the times
is sufficient to awaken us all up, for there is
more need of our living our religion than
ever; when I reflect and [---] the way that
we are going on I think it is displeasing in
the sight of our heavenly Father, for pride
and wickedness are on the increase, and it
becomes us as wives and mothers, to be alive
to our duties, and help to put away all that 
would be grieving to the Spirit of the Lord.
	Wishing you success, I am most respect-
fully yours.
