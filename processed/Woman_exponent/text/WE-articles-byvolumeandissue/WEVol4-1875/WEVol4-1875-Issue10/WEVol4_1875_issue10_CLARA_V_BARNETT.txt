				CLARA V. BARNETT,

				KANAB, Sept. 23, 1875.
DEAR EXPONENT:
	I could not let the opportunity pass with-
out giving you some idea of the good time
we have had during the visit of Pres. D.
H. Wells and party. He was accompanied
by Brothers Lorenzo Snow, F. D. Richards
and Erastus Snow of the Twelve. Also
Brothers R. T. Burton, A. M. Musser, J. E.
Taylor, R. McQuarrie, D. D. McArthur and
Bishop L. J. Nuttall.
	They spoke much on the UNITED ORDER,
and gave good counsel on various other sub-
jects.
	They said the time had come when we
must live our religion and obey the com-
mands of the Lord given by His servants,
that we should know for ourselves the true
way to live, and live in that way with all
our might and strength. Also that no one
could put in the plea of ignorance, for the
Prophet Brigham had given us enough good
counsel and instruction to save all who
would listen and obey.
	Elder Junius F. Wells spoke at consider-
able length to the young people. He said
their privileges were great in being born
and reared in the gospel, by parents that
never told them to do wrong. He plead
with them to live up to their privileges,
improve their minds, become what their
parents had been, and more.
	Would that we had more such young
men, who could stand up with boldness and
humility before their young brethren and
give them such good counsel.
	Brothers Levi Stewart and John R. Young
resigned their offices; the first as Bishop of
the Ward, the latter as President of the
United Order.
	Brother Nuttall from Provo was installed
Bishop of the Kanab Ward. He staid a
few days after the brethren left, and bap-
tized over seventy of the brethren and sis-
ters in the new covenant of the United
Order. After Conference Bishop Nuttall
will make his home with us.
	There seems to be a good spirit among
the people, and I think the future prospects
of Kanab are bright and promising.
