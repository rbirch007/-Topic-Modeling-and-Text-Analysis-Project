DEAR SISTERS OF THE RELIEF SOCIETY:
	I feel myself highly honored with a name
among you, and for the privilege of address-
ing you through the the EXPONENT.
	I consider I am addressing the Daughters
of Zion, children of the living and true
God.
	The world may boast of her high ranks
of nobility, her ladies, duchesses, queens,
and empresses, although their apparel and
equipage are the most beautiful of the
productions of earth, adorned with gold,
silver, pearls and diamonds, and they are
honored by the whole world, and almost
worshipped by their subjects, their minds
are filled with care and anxiety: they know
if they are most fortunate, they can live but
a few short years before they must lay down
their mortal bodies, and with them all the 
glory and honor which, through the bless-
ing of God, has been conferred upon them
by mortals, and lie as low as the lowest.
	Not so with the rank, glory and honor of 
the daughters of Zion; it is not conferred
on them by mortal man, but by God their
Father, as a reward for their obedience to
His requirements; which glory, power and
honor is everlasting in the presence of God
their Father and His Son Jesus Christ.
	Yes, and with them we shall inherit the 
earth with all its riches, and live under the
peaceful reign of Christ, "when righteous-
ness shall cover the earth as the waters do
the face of the deep." Let us praise God


for His loving kindness to us, and for His
tender mercies over all the works of His
hands. While we humbly compare His
dealings with the human family (His own
offspring formed in His own image, pos-
sessed of faculties like His own in embryo,
yet through disobedience alienated from
his presence) I say, let us compare the love
of our heavenly Father, as evinced in his
dealings with the human family, and ex-
plained by divine revelation, with our love
for, and our dealings with our own offspring
in the flesh.
