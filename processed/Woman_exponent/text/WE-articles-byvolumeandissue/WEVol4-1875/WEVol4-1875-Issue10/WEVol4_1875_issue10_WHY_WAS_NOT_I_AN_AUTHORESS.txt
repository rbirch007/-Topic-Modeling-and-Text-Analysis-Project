WHY WAS NOT I AN AUTHORESS

	I am sure I've tried hard enough. But
I am subject to such strange flights of fancy
it is almost impossible for me to write two
sentences connectedly on any particular sub-
ject. Many and many a time have I sat
down with pen, ink and paper, resolving
that "this time, I will write something in-
teresting." But alas! for my resolves; it
has proven as fallible as all former efforts;
before I have written two lines on my sub-
ject, I find myself a hopeless wanderer in a 
labyrinth of ideas, without being able as
some of my companions are, of bringing
order out of chaos. At other times I have
written a lengthy article, and after having
read it carefully, decide in my own mind,
that "this will do, if I can only find a name
for it;" but there it is again,! not being on
any particular subject, I find it very difficult
to obtain a suitable title for my productions.
Who will wonder then, that after having
passed through all these trying circumstan-
ces, I have almost resolved in my own mind
to write no more, but to select some occupa-
tion better adapted to my temperament,
