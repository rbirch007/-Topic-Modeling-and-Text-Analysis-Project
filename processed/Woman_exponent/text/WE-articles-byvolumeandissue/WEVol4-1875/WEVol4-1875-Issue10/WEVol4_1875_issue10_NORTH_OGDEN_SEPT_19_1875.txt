	 NORTH OGDEN, Sept 19, 1875.
DEAR EDITOR:
	We, your sisters, thought we would inter-
est your readers with a few items from our
Retrenchment Association. We are trying
to improve and live our religion to the best

[Column 2]
of our ability. The young ladies of this
place seem to be seeking after truth more
diligently than they have done heretofore.
We have a meeting once a week; they are
generally well attended; we enjoy ourselves
while we are together and feel happier than
we did before our meetings commenced.
We do not feel quite as timid to get up and
speak now, as we did at first. Two weeks
ago, in a meeting for the young people,
fourteen young men and young ladies stood
up and bore their testimony to the truth of
this work.
	It being the wish of President Young that
the Latter-day Saints should refrain from
round dancing, a vote was called to see if
we were willing to try and refrain from it,
and the vote was unanimous.
	We feel to sympathize with President
George A. Smith's family and the saints in
their great bereavement, but we do not
mourn as those without hope. Our desire is
to live so that we may be worthy to meet
him when we are called away from this
stage of action.
	Your sisters in the Gospel,
				ISABEL M. CRANDAL,
				SARAH JANE WADE,
				OLIVE P. FERRIN,
