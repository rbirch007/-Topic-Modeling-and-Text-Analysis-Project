		SANTAQUIN, Sept. 25, 1875.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The Retrenchment Association of this
place held their second Annual meeting on
the 17th of Sept., 1875. After the usual
exercises and reading of the minutes, es-
says, compositions, and selections from the
EXPONENT and "Juvenile" were read by
the members. After which Bishop George
Halliday, who was present by invitation, ad-
dressed the sisters; he expressed his plea-
sure and satisfaction with the proceedings
of the Association, and instructed and en-
couraged them to go into HOME MANUFAC-
TURE as much as possible.
	Sister Zina D. Young being present ad-
dressed the sisters, instructing them to be
faithful and prayerful; said she was very
much pleased with the proceedings of the
sisters in this place and spoke at some length
upon the propriety of the sisters dressing
plain and neat; also upon Sericulture, etc.
	President Mary A. Stickney made some
very appropriate remarks, encouraging the
sisters to faithfulness.
	The house was plainly but neatly decorat-
ed for the occasion, and all seemed to enjoy
the Spirit of the Lord.
	Our Association is in a very flourishing
condition; we have our meetings every Fri-
day afternoon at 2 o'clock. Your most
valuable paper is read with interest in our
meetings, and we wish it may have an ex-
tended circulation.
	Your sister in the Gospel of Peace,
