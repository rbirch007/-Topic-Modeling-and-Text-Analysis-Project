	R. S. REPORTS.

	The meeting of the Senior and Junior
Co-operative Retrenchment Association con-
vened in the Fourteenth Ward Assembly
Rooms, Sept. 25th, 1875, was replete with
teaching and instruction in accordance with
the spirit of reformation, which is rapidly
spreading among the saints. After the
usual exercises and reading of the minutes
by the secretaries of various wards, Presi-
dent Mrs. M. I. Horne among other in-
structions gave some counsel to the moth-
ers in regard to the clothing of their chil-
dren, and the attention to be paid to them
at this particular season of the year, when
the weather is so changeable and fruit so
abundant, that children require more than
ordinary care and watching.
	Miss E. R. Snow then gave an account of
the trip North, taken recently by herself
and Mrs. M. I. Horne, and the work going
on in the settlements they had visited.
	Mrs. S. M. Kimball, who had been absent
nearly a year in California, expressed her
pleasure in meeting the sisters again, and
said her health had greatly improved; gave
some account of her visit and the feeling
existing towards this people in that country.
Mrs. P. Woodruff, Mrs. Madsen, Mrs. W.
East, Mrs. R. Pratt, Mrs. E. S. Barney,
and Mrs. Watmough each made excellent
remarks after which Miss E. R. Snow said:
"We expect to petition the Legislature to
let us have a Female Medical College, and
we shall want some lady qualified to super-
intend it; it is for this purpose we are try-
ing to raise means to send Mrs. Pratt East
that she may qualify herself for this posi-
tion."
