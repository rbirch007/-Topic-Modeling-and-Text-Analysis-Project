WOMAN'S RECORD.

	MISS Charlotte Cushman is said to be so
feeble as to require constant assistance and 
support.
	MISS Bessie Egglesfield, a young lady law
student, was permitted to practice in the
Circuit Court at Terre Haute, (Ind,) 8th ult.
	THE "airy, fairy Lillian," enshrined in
verse by Tennyson, is now Mrs. Rawnesley,
and the happy mother of seven children.
	MRS. Gould, the founder of the Italio-
American school at Rome, is dead. Her re-
mains have been forwarded to America.
	MRS. Mary Mapes Dodge has returned
home from her summering in the Adiron-
dacks with her health entirely restored.
She has resumed her editorial duties on the 
St. Nicholas.
	MISS Annie Oliver, the girl preacher, of
whom much has been said and written re-
cently, was born in New York and graduat-
ed at Rutgers Female College. She says
she preaches because she loves to, and be-
cause she feels she has been called to the
ministry. In the crusade against liquor
in Ohio she commenced at the Temperance
meetings as she says, to talk to knots of
men and soon gained confidence to address
a throng. After making up her mind to en-
gage in this work she concluded she must
prepare herself by study like the other sex;
she applied first to the Congregational semi-
naries (which was her own church) then to


the Presbyterian but they were all too dig-
nified and exclusive. She says she applied
to fourteen in all, at last she found a univer-
sity in Boston which accepted her as a stu-
dent. Oberlin College allowed her to study
the languages, and the professors she says
thought they were doing a great thing for
her and said no other church would do as
much.
