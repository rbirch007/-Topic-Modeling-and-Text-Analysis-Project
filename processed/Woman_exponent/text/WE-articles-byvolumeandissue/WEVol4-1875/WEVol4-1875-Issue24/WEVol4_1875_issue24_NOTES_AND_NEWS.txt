		NOTES AND NEWS.

	IT IS fully expected that Woman Suffrage
will shortly be debated in the Senate of the
United States.

	ON SUNDAY, May 6th, a terrible tornado
and rain storm passed over Leavensworth,
Kansas City and Chicago; the destruction
was great, and some lives were lost.

	MR. WILSON and Rev. Mr. Gledstone
have come to America as envoys from the
"British and Continental Federation, for
the abolition of Government regulation of
Prostitution;" to assist in preventing such
legislation in this country.

	THE QUEEN of England has now received
the title Empress. It is the opinion of
many that this fancy of the Queen was a
foolish one. The majority ruled; yet, it is

[Column 2]
said upon good authority that the minority
represented the highest intelligences, and
the truest greatness really, of the English
nobility.

	THE CORNWALL Times, N. Y., the Or-
ange Journal, N. J., and the New York
Observer, have each published strong edi-
torials, against the licensing of the social
evil. The New York Tribune has publish-
ed "Memorial" by Mr. Powell, and a letter
from a correspondent in St. Louis, showing
the evil that such license has caused in that
city.
	These writers receive the heartiest
thanks of all who are interested in the pre-
venting of vice and crime, and should re-
ceive the highest commendation from all
who love virtue; and desire to see a better
condition of society.
