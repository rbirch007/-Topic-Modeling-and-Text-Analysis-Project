R. S. REPORTS.

	The meeting of the Senior and Junior
Retrenchment Association held in the 
Fourteenth Ward Assembly Rooms, Apr.
15, 1876, was well attended and a spirit of
interest manifested by those who spoke on
the subject of home-manufacture, improve-
ment and self-culture. President Mrs. M.
I. Horne had been absent for some time
from them and remarked how happy she
felt to be once more with those who had be-
come so dear to her through these organiza-
tions. There were two Elders present who 
bore testimony to the good spirit manifested
and their faith in the power and influence
of woman for good.
	On the 30th of April the same Association
convened per adjournment at the same
place. The meeting was one of strong tes-
timony and general interest. The minutes
of the different Junior Associations, read
by the young secretaries were a good speci-
men of the great improvement rapidly be-
ing made among the young. The meeting
was dismissed abruptly on account of a 
threatening storm.
