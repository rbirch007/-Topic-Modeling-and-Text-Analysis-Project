FLORAL HINTS.
	The landscape gardener of Hartford,
Conn. says the management of lawns in
America require a deeper and richer soil
than England to maintain a continued
green through the summer. Deep tillage
and manuring are the foundations of suc-
cess; the soil should be uniform in texture
and quality to prevent spots. Sow in
freshly mellowed soil, and if there are any
annual weeds sow in autumn that the win-
ter may kill them. Roll in Spring old or
new lawns. Avoid coarse and lumpy man-
ure, and apply as a surface dressing finely.
powdered compost, or a mixture of guano
and superphosphate of lime. Keep the
grass cut short from the first and it will
cause it to grow thick and dense. Never
allow the grass to grow tall to cut for hay.
