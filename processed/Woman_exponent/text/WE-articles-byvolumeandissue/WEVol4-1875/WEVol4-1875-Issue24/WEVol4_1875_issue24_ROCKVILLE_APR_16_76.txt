ROCKVILLE, Apr. 16, '76.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The Retrenchment Association in this
place meet once a week and the young sis-
ters take great interest in attending meet-
ing and Sunday School, we have good times
and enjoy the spirit of the Lord; we take
one volume of the EXPONENT and appre-
ciate it very much. We intend having an
Excursion Party on May-day; the girls are
diligent in learning pieces and songs for
the occasion.
	We are organized the 2nd of last Feb-
ruary with thirty-eight members. Officers

	Our resolutions are as follows:
	1st. Resolved: That we each take an ac-
tive part in our meetings and do that which
is required of us, and by our example show
that we really wish to become useful and in
telligent.
	2d. That we not only reform in dress, but
in everything that will tend to make us
true and noble women; by keeping good
company, choosing our words and practis-
ing politeness.
	3rd. That we will reform in our language,
by dropping all slang phrases, and words
that originate from the low and vulgar
class of people, and use that which will be-
come the daughters of the Latter-day
Saints.
	4th. That we strive to set an example
worthy of imitation by respecting the aged,
both by language and conduct, also respect
ourselves, and all who are worthy of it.
	5th. That we strive to be true Daughters
of Zion by observing the Word of Wisdom,
and informing our minds by reading useful
and instructive books.
	6th. That we will attend meetings and
Sunday Schools, and assemblies where good
instructions are given, that we may pre-
pare ourselves for the various duties that 
devolve upon us and help bear off the great
work in which we are engaged.
