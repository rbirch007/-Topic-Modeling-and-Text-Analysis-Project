WOMAN'S VOICE.

	I feel grateful to my Father in heaven for
His preserving care over me unto this day.
I know that my Redeemer lives, and will
bless all who come unto Him. I know the
promises of God are to the faithful. I de-
sire to make myself useful in helping to
build up the Kingdom of God in the last
dispensation.
	Our Relief Society, here in Moroni is
prospering; the sisters all seem alive to
their duties. We have done a great deal
for those who could not help themselves.
We also have a Young Ladies' Retrench-
ment Association. They seem to have a
desire to do good and to improve in all good
works.
	When I look back and think what I have
endured the thirty-three years that I have
been trying to live the life of a Saint, I
must say that it has been a good school of
discipline for me. When I was in Nauvoo,
and could sit under the sound of Joseph
Smith's voice, I thought it was grand, and
so it was; but we knew but little then in
regard to what would transpire in the last
days.
	Sisters, I exhort you to be faithful in the
church and kingdom of God.
	I remain your sister in the Gospel of
Christ.
						SARAH JOLLY.
