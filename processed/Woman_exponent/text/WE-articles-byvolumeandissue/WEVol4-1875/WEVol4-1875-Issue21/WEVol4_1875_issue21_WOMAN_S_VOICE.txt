WOMAN'S VOICE.

		OXFORD ONEIDA CO., March 5th '76.
	When our dear little children are pros-
trated with sickness, we are fearing and
trembling lest they should not recover, es-
pecially if we are alone with them. It ap-
pears to be natural, but dear mothers it is
not the right way.
	When you see your child is sick, do not
get frightened, and your nerves all upset
so that the Evil One who is always waiting
near, can have power over you and your
child, but go to your Heavenly Father with
a humble heart and ask Him to forgive you
of your sins; for who of us are without sin,
and strengthen your faith that the Adver-
sary may be rebuked. Then go to your
child with strong faith and lay your hands
upon the little head, and rebuke the de-
stroyer in the name of Jesus Christ &c.


Our children are given to us dear sisters,
and we must keep our hearts prayerful each
moment we live and ourselves free from
sin so that we can hold them with firm
faith; they are committed to our care, and
we should if possible, be equal to every
emergency. Is it not important then that
we should live near unto God and carry
with us thankful hearts all the year round?
What mother cares for this world's riches
and vain glories in comparison to the life
and welfare of her children. Therefore
dear sisters, let us all try and live our re-
ligion from hour to hour and day to day, so
thatwe may not be found wanting is my
desire in the name of Jesus.
