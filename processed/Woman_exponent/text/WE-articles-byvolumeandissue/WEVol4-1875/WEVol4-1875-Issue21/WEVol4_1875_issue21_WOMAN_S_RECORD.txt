WOMAN'S RECORD.

	MRS. Sallie Joy White is a contributor to
the Boston Sunday Times, editing a column
under the heading of "Woman's Kingdom."
It savors of a strong mind, a vigorous in-
tellect, and smooth and harmonious adapta-
tion of the author to the subject.
	MISS Susie Dugan, daughter of Mr. T.
H. Dugan, formerly of Boston, took the
prize, the Avery gold medal, for the best
oil painting, at the exhibition of the School
of Design, in San Francisco, of which Mr.
Virgil Williams is professor.
	QUEEN Victoria opened Parliament in
person on Feb. 8th, an event which it is
said has awakened great interest and ex-
citement among her subjects. The Houses
of Parliament were filled with a distinguish-
ed throng. The Queen was attended with
various members of the royal family and
nobility. The Lord Chancellor read the
speech in behalf of the Queen. The impos-
ing ceremony lasted but a few minutes.
