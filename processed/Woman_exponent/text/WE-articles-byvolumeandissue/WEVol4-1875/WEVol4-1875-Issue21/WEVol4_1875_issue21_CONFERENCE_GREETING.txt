		CONFERENCE GREETING.

				April, 1876.

Again from North to South, from East to West,
	The Saints of Latter-days in conference meeting,
To praise the Lord, to bless and to be blest,
	Fast congregate, with peaceful, happy greetings.
Friends meet with friends, and cordial hands are shaken
	As one by one, or groups are gathering in;
Now silence! Now sweet strains of music 'waken,
	And lo! the conference services begin.

Why do they gather at this Gospel Feast,
	These thousands, 'neath this spacious dome's broad ceil-
		ing?
Father, Thy gifts and blessings have not ceased—
	And shall Thy children fail in their good feelings?
We come to hear Thy words in plainness spoken,
	That we more faithfully henceforth may live:
If in the past some trusts with Thee were broken,
	Lord, we are penitent, do Thou forgive.

We listen, purpose well, but soon forget—
	Help us to be more watchful and more guarded!
Prolong Thy mercies, we are children yet,
	O! leave us not as faithless ones discarded!
If with vain things our hearts have been encumbered,
	Yet prize we Thy dear words above them all;
If we in car[-]ess ease have slept and slumbered,
	We would awake at Thine enlivening call.

We trust Thy promises and feel secure;
	To follow Christ as our divine Preserver;
Help us all tests and chastenings to endure,
	And to pursue Thy canse with brightening fervor.
With faith and covenants renewed and strengthened,
	We worship, Father, and implore Thy grace;
O! be our follies purged, Thy mercies lengthened,
	Till we may see and know Thee face to face.

Until Thy temples shall o'erspread the land,
	Until the burden of Thy cause is lifted,
Thy cov'nant people working hand to hand,
	And seeing eye to eye, divinely gifted,
Pour out Thy spirit Lord in rich effulgence,
	Quicken our memories and our mind expand,
That as Thy servants speak, through Thine indulgence,
	Thy Saints may hear aright and understand.											LULA.


[Column 1]
					For the Exponent.
