HOUSEHOLD HINTS.

	Boiled Celery:—Take four or five roots of cel-
ery carfully washed and trimmed and boil in well
salted water for nearly half an hour. Drain them,
place on a hot dish, and pour melted butter, or a
white sauce flavored with some favorite seasoning,
over them.
	Aunt Mary's Pudding:—Butter a tart-dish,
sprinkle the bottom with finely minced candied peel
and a very little shred suet, then a thin layer of
light bread, and so on until the dish is full. For
a pint dish make a liquid custard of one egg and
half a pint of milk, sweeten, pour over the p[-]d-
ding, and bake as slowly as possible for two hours.
	Broiling Meats:—When meats are broiling on a
gridiron over hot coals, the sudden heat sears the
outside, which shuts in the juices, and the rapid
application of heat soon cooks the meat through,
if in [--]oderately thin slices. It is then tender and
palatable. Those who never broil their meat, fish,
or poultry, do not know the excellence of a prop-
erly-cooked dish of animal food.
	Condensed Eggs:—A process has been devised
in Europe for drying, by a gradual heat, the con-
tents of eggs, both white and yelk. The granular
powder thus produced only requires mixing with a
sufficient quantity of water to produce ready-beat-
en eggs capable of being used for puddings, cakes,
etc. A factory has already been established in
Bavaria for the manufacture, and it is expected the
product will soon be in the market.
