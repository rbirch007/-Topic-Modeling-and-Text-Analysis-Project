	HOW TO WATER HANGING BASKETS:—
Set in the middle of the basket or suspend
over it a deep vase or broken goblet, which
should be kept full of water. In the water
place one end of three or four cotton or
worsted cords, and drop the other end of
them on different parts of the top of the
basket. The capillary attraction will cause
a constant flow of the water through the
strings, the number of which may be in-
creased or diminished according as more or
less moisture is needed. The vase, of
course, must be fastened so as it will not
tip and spill the water. It may also be
filled with "Wandering Jew," or anything
that will grow in water, and thus add to the
beauty of the basket. The many advanta-
ges of this arrangement are obvious.
