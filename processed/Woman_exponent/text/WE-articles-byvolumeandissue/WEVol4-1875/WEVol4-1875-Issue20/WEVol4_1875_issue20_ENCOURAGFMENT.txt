		ENCOURAGFMENT.

	I thought I would say a few words to the
Retrenchment girls about writing to the
EXPONENT. I know that many of them
though they cannot express their feelings
in their meetings could do so on paper. A
great many think they are not capable of
writing for a paper, they think it is above
their abilities. I know that we have in our
Retrenchment Associations girls who have
ideas and sentiments which would benefit
others. It only requires careful thought
and calm reflection, and any girl who loves
her religion and possesses purity of heart,
would at least benefit herself, and improve
mind.
	I saw a piece in the EXPONENT a few
months ago from a little girl ten years old.
I think it is an indication of courage and of
an earnest desire to improve, when one so
young undertakes that which we that are
older feel our incompetency to do; but I
would not be without the Exponent for
anything, and after I have read it I send it
to Scotland, where it is read with great in-
terest and pleasure. May God bless us, and
prosper us in every good wish is the desire
of your sister in the Gospel,
					Sarah McKean.
	S. L. City, Jan. 4th, 1875.
