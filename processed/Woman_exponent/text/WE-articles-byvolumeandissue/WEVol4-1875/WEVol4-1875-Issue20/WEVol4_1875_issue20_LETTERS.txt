LETTERS.

		COUNCIL BLUFFS, March 4th, 1876.
EDITOR WOMANS EXPONENT:
	The women of Utah and their condition, are
topics that seem to greatly interest the people
East of that point, if I may judge from the
very many questions that are propounded as
soon as it is known that one is from Utah.
The general impression is, that you are abused,
downtrodden and degraded; that the elective
franchise granted you, was only a blind to de-
ceive the outside world, and that you are to all
intents and purposes slaves. I have taken
great pleasure in defending you and explain-
ing your true position socially, and morally and
really believe I have become more deeply in
love than ever with my subject. The women
of Utah must be known to be appreciated and
I feel satisfied that the time is not far distant
when the downtrodden women of monogamic
Christianity will be proud of the association of
their Mormon sisters.
	You have need only to glance casually at
the Christian world to discover that their sys-
tems are a decided failure, and all attempts by
them at this late day to ameliorate the condi-
tion of humanity signally fail, also, socially,
morally, physically, religiously and politically.
The prospect is gloomy indeed and I am satis-
fied that nothing short of God can grasp the
the evils of the age, and provide the necessary
remedies. But as He is altogether ignored by
them there is a terrible future and that too
not very far off.
	Let the women of Utah arouse themselves,
lay aside all follies, pride and vanity, and sanct-
ify themselves before God, to prepare to
stand in their place, to receive the thousands
of their own sex, who will yet come and be
willing to accept the salvation that these de-
spised people have to offer, for if the power to
save is not with the Latter-day Saints, it is 
not on earth.
							J. E. T.
