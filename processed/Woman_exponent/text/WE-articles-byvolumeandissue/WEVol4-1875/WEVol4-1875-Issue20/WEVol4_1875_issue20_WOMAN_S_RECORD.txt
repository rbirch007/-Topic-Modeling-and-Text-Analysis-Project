WOMAN'S RECORD.

	MRS. ANTOINETTE Brown Blackwell is
writing a book called "The Physical Basis of
immortality." It is to be offered to the
Ladies' Department of the Centennial.
	MISS CELIA Elizabeth Harris, of Nebras-
ka, a native of South Bend, Ind., leads the
medical class of Boston University, which
graduated Wednesday, March 1st. 
	EX. EMPRESS EUGENIE lives in a plain
and unpretending manner at Chiselhurst;
the late Emperor's place at table is daily
decked with a bunch of fresh violets.
	MARY NORTON, about 1669, gave the land
on which the old South Church of Boston
stands, "for the erecting of a house for their
assembling themselves together to worship
God and for no other intent, use or purpose
whatever." 
	JENNIE JUNE writes concerning Miss
Alcott that she is not small, as the impres-
sion which her books give, but tall, graceful
and charming in appearance, utterly free
from nonsense, shams and pretences, a quick
shrewd and intelligent observer, and ex-
tremely kind and loving to children, who
in turn adore her.
	MISS LUCY Hale, the oldest inhabitant of
Plymouth, Mass., died on the 5th of Febru-
ary, in the 99th year of her age; she leaves
a brother and sister whose ages are respect-
ively 93 and 95 years. Until within a few
days previous to her death, when she had a
fall, she retained possession of her faculties
to a wonderful degree.
