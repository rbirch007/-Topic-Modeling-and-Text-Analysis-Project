		CONGRESS AND POLYGAMY.

	Since Congress has been working so hard
to do something towards abolishing polyg-
amy, it has caused me to reflect, and I think
if they knew that it only tended to strength-
en our faith, make us more zealous, and
bind us more closely together, instead of
feeling to give it up, they would surely
adopt some other method. But it is not for
polygamy alone that they hate us; when the
church was first organized they persecuted
us just as bitterly, even mobbing and ston-
ing the Elders who attempted to preach.
Their cry was then, "you believe in new
revelation;" but the words of our Savior to
his deciples fully explains it. "If ye were
of the world the world would love you &c."
	I do not feel one par[---] of anxiety as to
the result, for I think the Lord is able to
fight his own battles, and we need only be
true to ourselves and religion as Latter-day
Saints. It is the Lord and Congress for it.
	The Sisters of our Relief Society are very
busy just now making a carpet for our new
Tabernacle. It is quite an undertaking, as
it requires nearly four hundred yards, but
we have united our efforts and think we
can accomplish it by March. The Temple
looks beautiful in its pure white, at first the
plastering did not improve its appearance,
but it does now that it is finished, it will
look so cool in the summer. We have warm
delightful weather and dusty streets.
	Lest I weary you I will conclude by wish-
ing you continued success.
	Your Sister in the Gospel,
							E. BENTLEY.
	ST. GEORGE, Feb. 21st, 1876.
