EDITOR EXPONENT:
	By request of our President, Sister Lil-
jenquist, I send you a short report of our
doings since I last wrote you: We meet
every fast day at 2 o'clock, and converse
freely concerning our spiritual and tempor-
al welfare, the necessities of the poor, &c.
	A good spirit seems to prevail among the
sisters and many of them are disposed to
carry out the counsel of President Young.
We have instituted a straw-braiding school,
and are preparing to manufacture our own
hats and bonnets. We have also made elev-
en bed quilts—we also, pursuant to your

[Column 2]
counsel, made a bed-spread and table-cover
for the Fair. We had a very enjoyable par-
ty on the 25th ult. It being leap year, the
sisters bought the tickets, and under the
arrangement of our worthy President, our
party was conducted according to the coun-
sel given in the Deseret News—commencing
at 2 p. m., and at 5 o'clock refreshments
were served; we continued our dancing,
with songs occasionally and a speech from
the Bishop, until 10 o'clock, all feeling first-
rate. Our Hall was very tastefully decor-
ated, by Bro. Hansen, with evergreens, a
large wreath extending around the Hall,
portraits of the First Presidency, several of
the Twelve, our Bishop, and many others,
also the banners of five nationalities. We
also exhibited our specimens of home man-
ufactures, which all seemed to fully appre-
ciate.
	We are always glad to receive the Ex-
ponent, but feel ashamed that there are so
few subscribers in Hyrum. Your agent
here frequently appeals to the sisters for
their patronage, but in consequence of the
scarcity of money, they are unable to take
more at present.
					Yours Respectfully,
