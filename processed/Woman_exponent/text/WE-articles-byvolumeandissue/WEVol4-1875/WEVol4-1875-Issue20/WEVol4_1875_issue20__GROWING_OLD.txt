"GROWING OLD."

I have watched the radiant coming
	Of the sun's unfettered rays,
Which with rich and glad effulgence
	Bathed the loveliest of days;
And at eve I saw their splendor
	Melting calmly into gold,
And I thought that nature's beauty
	Faded not in "growing old."

But a spectacle more touching
	Than the tints of eve impart,
Is the pleasant, peaceful twilight
	Of a manly, noble heart—
When the lofty aims of early days
	Their deeds of good have told,
And silver hairs life's sunset crown,
	Tell them of "growing old."

The spring is beautiful, indeed,
	With balmy air and showers,
The glad return of singing birds,
	The budding fruit and flowers;
But not until the roses fade,
	The leaves are changed to gold;
And the richest fruits of all the year
	Come when 'tis "growing old."

So when the flowers of youth are gone,
	And life's September's come,
If still there's sunshine in the heart
	There need be nought of gloom.
The retrospect of days well spent,
	And gifts improved tenfold,
Well may that heart be filled with peace,
	Happy in "growing old."
—Washington Sunday Herald.
