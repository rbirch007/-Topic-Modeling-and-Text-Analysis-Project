DEAR EDITOR:

	As I am a little girl I know that I cannot in-
struct anybody and yet I want to tell what I
am thankful for, and what we all ought to be
grateful for.  In the first place we should be
thankful for having a Heavenly Father who
has given us life and placed us here on earth to
gain an exaltation for ourselves, and to have
the privilege of having the Gospel preached to
us by the Elders of the Church of Jesus Christ
of Latter-day Saints, and of reading holy
books, and how Christ came on this earth
teaching unto the multitudes by pa[-]ables and 
healing all manner of sickness.
	And we should be thankful that we can
serve God, and that he has given us good par-
ents, food to eat, water to drink, and clothes
to wear; and that we have trials and tribula-


tions in this world, so we can gain our crown
in the next world if we are worthy of it; and
that we can build temples unto the Lord, and
can be baptized for the remission of our sins,
that we can have oil to heal the sick, in
faith, and holy brethren to administer these
ordinances and to teach the principles of the
Gospel and showing us how to gain our
salvation, that we can work for our Savior and
help bring His children into the kingdom of 
heaven, for Christ said "suffer litttle children
to come unto me, for such is the kingdom of
Heaven."
	We ought to appreciate our nice homes and 
comfortable beds to sleep in, and fires to warm
us; and we ought to be thankful that if we
pray for faith we may have it, and believe that
we will have it, so that if we are sick and the
Elders lay their hands upon our heads we will
be healed, but without faith we could not get
well. And we should be grateful that we have
the sun to give us light and warmth by day,
and the stars and moon to give light by night,
and herbs, and fruits, and plants for our use;
that He has given us rain so they might live.
We ought to be grateful for these things and a
great many more. And I hope we all will.
