EDITOR EXPONENT;
	I thought I would send you a few items
pertaining to us as a Relief Society, know-
ing the interest you take in our welfare.
We held our re-organization meeting on the
17th of Oct., 1875, and the following sisters
were nominated as officers: Mrs. Jane M.
Howell, President; Mrs. Lucretia M. Marler
and Mrs Elizabeth Howell, Counselors; Mrs.
Kittie Dixon, Treasurer; Mrs. Jane Smith,
Secretary, and Mrs. Martha Rasicot Assist-

[Column 3]
ant Secretary; Mrs Mary Thomas President
of teachers, Mrs. Sarah Howell, Amelia
Dixon, Kitty Dixon, Lydia A. Howell,
Sarah A. Larsen, teachers; and all the mem-
bers feel as one with the officers in doing
their duty and in sustaining righteousness
among us; we are quite a way north up here
in Idaho, but still we are one with all Israel,
and feel to be one with the Saints and those
who intend to do what is right.
	Ever feeling desirous for the welfare of
Zion, and the promotion of truth;
			We remain your sisters,
		JANE SMITH, Secretary
