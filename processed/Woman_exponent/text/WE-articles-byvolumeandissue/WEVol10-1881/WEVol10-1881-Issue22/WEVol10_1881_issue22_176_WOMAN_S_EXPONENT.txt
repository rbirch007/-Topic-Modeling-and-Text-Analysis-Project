176 WOMAN'S EXPONENT.


WOMEN were employed and commended as
compositors as early as 1481. The first notable
female printer was Charlotte Guillard, who was
in business for fifty years in the city of Psris.
This pioneer of feminine followers of Gutten-
berg was celebrated for the correctness of the
works issued from her press. She first entered
the typographical ranks in 1506.—Ex.
