				EPHRAIM, MARCH 1882.
EDITOR WOMAN'S EXPONENT:
	Dear Sister—After the close of the Legisla-
tive session, on the return of our brethren,
President Canute Petersen, and Counselor
Henry Beale, there was a social party in the
Sisters' Hall, in honor of the occasion, and as
many of the oldest settlers as could be com-
fortably accomodated were invited to partic-
ipate.
	We were also favored with the presence of
Sister M. A. Hyde, the County President of
the Relief Society. Bishop L. S. Anderson, of
the North Ward, Bishop C. C. N. Dorius, of
the South Ward, and their Counselors were
present. The music was excellent, the spirit of
peace and happiness seemed to fill every heart.
I wish you had been there to partake of the
pleasures and enjoyment that every-one seemed
to feel, surely we have reason to thank God
that his Holy Spirit can pervade the social
dance as well as the more serious meeting
of worship, the dance was opened and closed by
prayer, and all went home in peace and good
order at twelve o'clock. I must say the sisters
are united in all good works, and doing the
very best they can. With my best wishes for
your happiness, I subscribe myself respectfully
your Sister in the Gospel.
