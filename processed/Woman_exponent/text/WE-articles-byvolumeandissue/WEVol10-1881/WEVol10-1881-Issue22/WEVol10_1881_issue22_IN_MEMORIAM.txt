IN MEMORIAM.

	WILLIAMS,—In Provo, on Sunday morning,
March 12, 1882, of pleura pneumonia, Eliza
Helen, wife of Nathaniel Williams, and daughter
of Wm. M. and Nancy Wall; aged 38 years, 5
months, and 16 days.
	Deceased leaves a husband, seven children and
many relatives to mourn her departure. She
was a devoted wife and mother, was a promin-
ent member of the Fourth Ward Relief Society,
and always foremost in ministering to the neces-
sities of the afflicted and needy. She died as
she had lived, a faithful Latter-day Saint, with
an unshaken faith in a glorious resurrection.
	The funeral services were held in the Provo
Meeting House, and a large cortege followed the
remains to the cemetery.
	AT grantsville, Rachel Stailey; wife of James
Worthington, in her 79th year, her death was
caused by falling on the ice, breaking her thigh
and injuring her spine; she lived six weeks after
her fall, in great suffering.
	Deceased was born October 4th, 1803, in
Franklin County, Pennsylvania; joined the
Church in Beaver County, Penn., under the


preaching of Bro. Orson Pratt, in 1836; passed
through the mobbings of Missouri and Illinois,
and gathered with the Saints. Her husband
and herself were among the first settlers of
Grantsville. She died February 24th 1882; she
lived and died a faithful Latter-day Saint, was
the mother of 8 children, 5 of whom are living,33
grand-children and 2 great-grand-children.
