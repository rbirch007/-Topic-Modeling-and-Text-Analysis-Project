THE VIOLET'S INTERCESSION.

			Thou lovely flower!
That hold'st with magic spell my longing eyes,
			Plucked in the hour
In which thine evanescent beauty dies;
	Go! tell the love that thrills within my heart,
To her for whom that heart doth wildly yearn.
	Thy beauty, eloquent beyond all art,
Must move her tender soul to some return.

			Lowly thy sphere,
Thou modest emblem of a painless joy;
			Sweet as a tear.
But brief thy life, as pleasant thoughts are coy.
	Yet with the gentle fragrance of thy breath,
Stealing her soul, thou may'st o'ercome her fears
	Then might her pure heart know that, come e'en
		death,
The love I feel will breathe through endless years.
