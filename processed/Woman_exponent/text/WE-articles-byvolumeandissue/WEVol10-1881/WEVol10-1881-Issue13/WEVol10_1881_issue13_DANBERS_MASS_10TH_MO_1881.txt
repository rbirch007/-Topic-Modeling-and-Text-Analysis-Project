DANBERS, MASS., 10th Mo., 1881.

	MY Dear Friend, Lucy Stone:—I am
glad to learn by thy note that a Suffrage con-
vention is to be held soon in Louisville, Ky.,
and l hope the women of the Southwest will
take a lively interest in it. If any considerable
number of the intelligent women of Ken-
tucky show themselves earnest advocates of
impartial suffrage, there can be no doubt that
the best men of the State will accede to their
wishes. The complete success of the cause is
only a question of time. Every intelligent ob-
server sees this; and there are many indications
that its advocates will not have long to wait
			Thy friend,
