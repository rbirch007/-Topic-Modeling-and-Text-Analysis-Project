HANNAH T. KING;

Sweet Friend: In reading o'er with interest
	Your "Codicil," I scarcely can refrain
To ask, the very first of that behest,
	You will recall, or change, or please explain.

"No flowers"! What reason can my sister give
	For this exclusion? Pure and precious things,
So choice, so cherished over while we live,
	In death are they not hallowed offerings?

What emblem more befitting that repose,
	To which your lines refer, and mark the scene,
Than the full open, whitest, sweetest rose,
	In death as well as life our floral queen?

And best if briers on the stem be found—
	('Tis my own fancy, may be, or a whim,
But sacred still—) One Head with thorns was crowned,
	And here, as ever, we would think of Him!

Think of, and imitate that one divine,
	The Sovereign always of your "mystic three,"
Whose love alone, to souls like yours and mine,
	E'en when bercaved, sots faith's fair pinions free,
S. L. City, Sunday April 23rd, 1881.
