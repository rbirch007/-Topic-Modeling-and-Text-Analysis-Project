BY HANNAH T. KING.

The young May Moon! Ah! well might Moore
	And such as he, thine advent sing,
How many human eyes—before
	Whose vision tho[-] dost Heaven bring!

How many hearts have bowed beneath
	Thy mystic charm of crescent beam,
Surrounding us as with a wreath
	Of glory from its wondrous sheen!

How many hearts are raised from earth
	While gazing on thy placid face,
Yes thou hast oft dispell'd the dearth
	That hung o'er our abiding place;

We may not kiss our hand to thee
	Nor yet the knee in worship bend,—
But thou shalt be a voice to me,
	And I thro' thee will whisperings send

To Him, who hung thee up above,
	As One of His Omniscient Eyes!
As on the Altar of my love
	I lay my votive sacrifice!

Oh! glorious concave! as I stand
	And thro' thy golden vistas gaze,
My spirit eye beholds the Hand
	Of God—silent, fond amaze.
The starry vault, a whispering tongue
	By every listening ear is heard,
That tells of Heaven on Earth begun,
	A lamp by which to read His word.—
The young May Moon, again I turn
	To gaze upon its silent beam,
And thoughts within my bosom burn
	But language fails to paint the theme.

The young May Moon has come and waned—
	Spring flowers come forth, then fade and die
And knowledge thus is daily gained
	That life and death around us lie!

Types of the resurrection—these—
	Mankind start forth with beauty ripe
Then pass away by fell desease
	By life and death in mortal strife!

Father! withdraw the awful veil,
	The grand Beyond that we may see—
Let dream or vision tell the tale
	When we shall dwell indeed with Thee!
Salt Lake City, May 1881.
