"ITS all very fine to have a ready-made rich
man, but may happen he'll be a ready-made
fool; and its no use filling your pocket full of
money if you've got a hole in the corner. I
allays said I'd never marry a man as had got
no brains; for where's the use of a woman hav-
ing brains of her own if she's tackled to a gock
as everybody's laughing at? She might as well
dress herself fine to sit back'ards on a donkey."
—GEORGE ELIOT'S writings.

	AT Madrid, Spain, April 30, the superior
council of education, after a considerably spir-
ited debate of several weeks, decided to admit
women and girls to lectures and degrees of
universities and faculties. Spain has been
looked upon as the very highest type of old
conservatism, but she is in advance of some of
the countries of Europe celebrated for their
learning, in recognizing woman's higher educa-
tion. America, with all her recognition of wo-
man's equality, has not advanced so far as Ma-
drid in making them eligible to degrees.
