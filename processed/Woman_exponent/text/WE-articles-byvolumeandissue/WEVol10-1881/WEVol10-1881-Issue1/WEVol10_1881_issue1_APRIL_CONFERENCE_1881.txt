APRIL CONFERENCE, 1881.

	[The Relief Society report was read and ac-
cepted as follows:]
	Officers of the Relief Society central organiza-
tion Eliza R. Snow Smith, President; Zina D.
Young and Elizabeth Ann Whitney, Counselors;
Sarah M. Kimball, Secretary; M. Isabella Horne,
Treasurer.
	Stake Presidents who have sent in their er-
ports: Bear Lake, Julia P. Linsey; Davis, Sarah
Holmes; Juab, Amelia Goldsburgh; Kanab,
Elizabeth Nuttall; Millard, Belinda M. Pratt;
Morgan, Lydia Rich; Parowan, Ellen W. Lunt;
Salt Lake, M. Isabella Horne; St. George,
Minerva W. Snow; Summit, Sarah S. Richards;
Tooele, Mary Ann Hunter; Utah, Margaret T.
Smoot: Weber, Jane S. Richards; Sevier, Eliza-
beth Bean; Box Elder, Harriet A. Snow.
	The following organized Stakes not reported:
Beaver, Cache, Wasatch, Arizona, Little
Colorado and Eastern Arizona.
	Total of teachers, 1,530; members, 9,650; of-
ficers and members, 12,288; meetings held, 1,532;
average attendence, 3,834.


WOMAN'S EXPONENT taken, 960.
	On hand at date of last report: Cash, $3,342.21;
property, $24,099.23; wheat, 9,859 bushels.
	RECEIPTS—In cash, $3,279 28; in property,
$3,513.20; in wheat 670 bushels.
	DISBURSEMENTS—To the poor, $3,468.31; emi-
gration, $210.43; temples, $1,214.11; home in-
dustries, $689.97; books, 143.13; missionary, $98.
70, buildings, $1,617.47; Indians $66. 45.
	Totals on hand in cash, property and wheat,
$36,822.24.
	President Cannon passed a high encomium on
the neat and comprehensive report the ladies
had presented, and also on the labors that the
sisters have so zealously performed, and consid-
ered there can be no one at all acquainted with
the good being accomplished by them but must
highly appreciate them.
