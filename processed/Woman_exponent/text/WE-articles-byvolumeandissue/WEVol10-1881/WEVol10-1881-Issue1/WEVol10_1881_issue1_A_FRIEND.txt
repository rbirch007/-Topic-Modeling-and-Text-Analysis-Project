								A FRIEND.

	Whereas, By the dispensation of Providence,
Sister Hannah T. Booth, the First Counselor of
the Young Ladies' Mutual Improvement Associ-
ation, of the Fourth Ward, Provo; daughter of
Edith and George Billings, and wife of our be-
loved Bishop, John E. Booth, has been called
from our midst by death; and,—
	Whereas, we realize that the labors of our
beloved sister, in our association, have always
tended to set us a praisworthy example to
strengthen us in our faith, and lead us in the
path of truth, virtue and eternal life.


Be it Resolved, That we, the young ladies of
this Association, mingle in sympathy our sor-
rows with the bereaved husband, parents and
family of our dear departed sister, and express
our united prayer and sincere hope, that the
Lord, in his mercy, will comfort them by re-
doubled blessings of His holy spirit, and
	Be it further Resolved,. That a copy of these
resolutions be furnished the husband and par-
ents, be published in the Enquirer, WOMAN'S
EXPONENT and spread on the records of the As-
sociation.
				EMILY G. CLUFF, Pret.
