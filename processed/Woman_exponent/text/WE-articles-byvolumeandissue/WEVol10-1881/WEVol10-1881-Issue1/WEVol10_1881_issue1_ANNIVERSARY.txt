ANNIVERSARY.

	The Primary Association of the 10th Ward
held its second annual meeting on Friday. May
20th commencing at one o'clock, in the Ward
meeting house, Mrs. Jennie McLean presiding.
Bishop Adam Spiers and Counselor James
Woods were present, also many brethren and
sisters of the Ward. There were many visitors
on the platform—Sisters E. R. S. Smith, B. W.Smith, S. M. Kimball, M. I. Horne, M. P.
Young, E. S. Taylor, Agnes Park, Mary Gray,
Louie Felt, E. C. S. Clawson, and E. B. Wells,
also Pres. George Teasdale, of Nephi. The
meeting opened with singing, "Open the door
for the children." Prayer by Mrs. E. B. Wells,
Singing, "Dare to do right." The children re-
cited in concert the "Articles of Faith;" Birdie
Paul and Hannah Sanders sang "Jesus loves
me;" Theodore Nistrom recited the "Ten com-
mandments;" children sang "Nay, speak no
ill;" Oscar Nistrom [---] the "Lord's Pray-
er;" Mandie Paul, recitation: dialogue by six
little girls; Brigham [---] "I'll never use to-
bacco;" Edith Braby, recitation; Arthur
Woods; solo; Phil[---] Woods and two other girls
sung and played an accompaniment; children
sang, "Joseph Smith's first prayer;" Joseph
Pool and Alma Strong sang; Willie Harry, reci-
tation; Lottie Paul, song composed for the occa-
