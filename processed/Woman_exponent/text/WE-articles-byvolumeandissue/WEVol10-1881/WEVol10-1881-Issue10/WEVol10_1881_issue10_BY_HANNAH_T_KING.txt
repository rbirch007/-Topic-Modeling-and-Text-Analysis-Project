BY HANNAH T. KING.

Oh! death! when thou art sent to call.
	My lagging Soul away,
Come not enwrapt in funeral pall
	And all thy dread array;

But friendly, softly, gently come,
	And whisper in mine ear—
I come to bear thy spirit Home
	Fly with me! never fear!

Enwrape thy form in sweet disguise,
	Thy voice, in sweetest tone—
Stand not before my startled eyes
	In thy gaunt case of bone?

But if I must thy form behold
	In palpable array,
Drop one by one, each screening fold.
	And fear shall flee away;

So, kindly, gently draw my breath,
	Point upward to the throne
Of Him, who laid Him down in death,
	And in the silent Tomb—

Why should I fear? Since He has died
	To rob thee of thy sting?—
Come hither Death what e'er betide
	I'll soar beneath thy wing!

Thou art His Voice to call me hence
	His Spirit guides thy Hand,
His power alone thy shafts dispense,
	Thy Mission—His command!

No more I strive—I'll go with thee,
	Gaunt Spectre! bear me hence,
My passive Soul would gladly flee
	To rest—and love intense.
