BISHOP EDWIN DILWORTH WOOLLEY.

	DIED, at his residence in the 13th Ward of
this city, just past midnight, Friday, Oct. 14,
Bishop Edwin Dilworth Woolley, aged 74 years,
3 months and 16 days.
	Bishop Woolley was well known and highly
respected among the Saints. He was born at
West Chester, Chester Co., Penn., on the 28th
of June, 1807, and was raised a Quaker. He
was baptized into the Church of Jesus Christ of
Latter-day Saints at Rochester, Ohio, in 1837,
emigrated to Quincy, Illinois, in 1839, and re-
moved to Nauvoo in 1840. Shared in the perse-
cutions of the Saints at the time of their expul-
sion from that ill-fated city, leaving on the 5th
of June, 1846, and proceeded with his family to
Winter Quarters. He came to Utah in Presi-
dent Brigham Young's company in 1848, and 
has ever since resided in S. L. City.
	In November of 1853 Brother Woolley was or-
dained Bishop over the 13th Ward, which office he
held at the time of his death. Deceased leaves a
large family to mourn the loss of a kind father
and friend; he was a most excellent husband and
father, honest industrious, and upright in all


respects. Bro. Woolley has filled several impor-
tant missions in the Church at different times,
and has served several sessions as a Representa-
tive in the Legislative Assembly of Utah. He
was an intimate friend of the Prophet Joseph
Smith and his integrity to the Gospel was un-
questionable. We deeply sympathize with his
bereaved family in the loss of his society and
counsel, but he has gone to join the companion of
his youth and many dear friends on "the other
shore" where there is no more parting forever.
The faithful and honest man, the persevering en-
ergetic and prudent Bishop has gone to rest.
"Peace to his ashes;" May the remembrance
of his upright and honorable life inspire his
children and others to follow in his foot-steps.
His funeral will be held in the large Taberna-
cle, Sunday morning Oct. 16, at 10 o'clock A. M.
