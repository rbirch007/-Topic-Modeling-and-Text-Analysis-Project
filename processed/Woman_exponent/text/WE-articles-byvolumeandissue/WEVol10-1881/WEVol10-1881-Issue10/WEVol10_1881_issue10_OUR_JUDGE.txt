OUR JUDGE.

So many different lives, have different phases
	Wherefore we cannot tell, but this we see—
To some, "the lines have fallen in pleasant places"
	While others, day by day, must martyrs be.

Who is so perfect, who so free from guile—
	To judge another, every way aright?
God only sees, each circumstance and trial,
	And knows the warfare that His children fight.

"Tis well, the lives of all, are understood—
	By One! whose judgment, is both wise and even.
If unto some, "tis easy to be good—
	Unto the sorely tried is much forgiven.

But none of us are privileged to ignore—
	Our sense of Right, the monitor within;
The duty being nearest to our door
	We must perform, although no praise 'twill win.

E'en though misunderstood, or blamed by others,
	We must be ever to our conscience true
And from the heart, this ceaseless prayer must offer—
	What is it Lord that thou wouldst have me do?

And when to heaven, with trusting hearts and tender—
	We guidance seek, and tread the path we see—
Not unto Man, excuses need we render,
	Who reads his brother, so imperfectly.

It matters not, if high or low our place is;
	Nor even if one sky be black or bright;
Sufficient 'tis to know that One! most gracious,
	Doth in compassion, judge us all aright.

God bringeth down, He also lifteth up;
	"Promotion! cometh not from East or West"
If sweet or bitter our appointed cup—
	Shall we not drink it? trusting it is best.

Be sure of this, where much is freely given—
	There much will be expected, much, required,
But who 'gainst fearful odds has bravely striven—
	Shall be rewarded, with the bliss desired.
