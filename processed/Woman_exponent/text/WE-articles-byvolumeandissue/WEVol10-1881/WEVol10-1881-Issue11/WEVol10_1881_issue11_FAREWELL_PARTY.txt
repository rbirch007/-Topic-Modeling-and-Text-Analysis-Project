FAREWELL PARTY.

	On Thursday, November 3, the Sisters of
Tooele had a very pleasant party, to pay their
farewell respects to Sister Rebecca Williams,
President of the Primary Association, who is
going to move to Malad to live with her chil-
dren. The party was held at the residence of
Sister Jeanette Delamare. President of the Re-
lief Society. There were present the Bishop
and Counselors and Brother Delamare, and all
the officers and teachers of the Relief Society,
about twenty-seven in number. Our party
commenced with singing and prayer, after
which all the sisters expressed their good feel-
ings and respect for Sister Williams. She
was presented with a gold ring and a very
nice quilt, and Sister Emily Warburton was
also presented with a gold ring, as tokens of
respect for their faithful labors and good works.
Sister Warburton has been our Secretary ever
since the organization of the Relief Society.
After all had expressed their good wishes, the
party sat down to a bounteous repast, and
spent the evening in singing the songs of Zion
and in social intercourse. All went as pleasant
as a marriage feast, and all present felt it was
a time not to be forgotten.
					ANN TATE, Cor. Sec'y.
