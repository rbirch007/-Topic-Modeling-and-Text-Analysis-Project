HOME AFFAIRS.

 PRESIDENT George Q."Cannon, wife and niece,
Miss Mina Cannon, returned from the southern
part of the Territory on Sunday morning train.
THE last company of emigrants for this season
arrived on Thursday, the 10th inst. well, and
having had a safe and prosperous journey.
PRES. Taylor and party are all enjoying the
best of health, and are doing much good among
the people wherever they travel. They are pro-
bably at Rockville to-day, on their return trip.
SISTERS M. Isabella Horne and E. Howard
have been attending the Sisters’ Conference of
Wasatch Stake, which was held at Heber City
on Friday, the 11th inst. They have not yet re-
turned home.	
 THE President of the Grain Committee ur-
geently requests full reports as soon as possible
from all the different localities where grain is
being stored for Zion; and if they have grana-
ries, what dimensions and material, and at what 
cost they have been built. Direct to Mrs. E. B.
Wells, Salt Lake City, Utah.
THE prize essay affair, about which consider-
able curiosity has been manifested of late, has
at last been settled, Mr. R. W. Sloan, of the “Salt
Lake Herald,” being the lucky winner. The
prize essay on “Utah and her Resources,” is
published in full in the Salt Lake Herald of
Sunday, Nov. 6, and is certainly a very excellent
one. We sincerely congratulate our young friend,
upon his success, and are very glad the prize was 
won by one of the Mormon boys, raised and
educated in Utah; in fact, Mr. Sloan is almost
entirely self-educated, having had very little op-
portunity to attend school, as he has been from
childhood employed in the “Herald” Office in
this city. The essay comprises under its several
sub-headings, Agriculture, Horticulture, Stock-
raising, Mining, Manufactures, Attractions, and
it will bear reading more than once. Two of
the other essays are to be published, and perhaps
all of them may be; no doubt they are all good.
The prize awarded was a check for fifty dollars,
given by Mr. Meears.
