DEAR SISTER SNOW:

	I promised to write to you, and now I do so.
We are getting along splendidly with our Pri-
mary and now we have a good attendance, be-
cause there is not much work to do.   Last
Saturday we went up to East Porterville to the
Fair, and it was splendid.   The boys made
rakes, knives, forks, lounges, whips, rolling-
pins, ox-yokes, &c.   The girls made pies and 
cakes, worked tidies, and one of the girls, about
twelve years old took a piece of every one of
the Primary children's hair, and the Presi-
dent's and her two Counselors'.   Then she
put the boys' on one side of the hair work and 
the girls' on the other side, and the President's
and Counselor's above worked in hearts.
   Sister Porter, the President, takes great 
pains with the children.   She teacher them to
sing, and at the Fair they sang very sweetly.
I hope you will be permitted to come and visit
us again next summer, for the Primary chil-
dren would like to see you now.   My mother
sends her very kind love to you, and hopes
you are well.   Accept the same from myself.
		Yours affectionately,
