			SARAH GLENN, Sec'y.

	DEAR EDITORS—The first Annual Meeting of
the Glasgow and Parkhead R. S., was held at 15

[Column 3]
Moir Street Glasgow, Scotland on the 19th,
Dec. 1881, of which we submit a report for
your inspection, hoping that you may favor us
with a few words of counsel towards the ar-
rangement of the Society, as it is the first that
has been successful in Glasgow. And we ten-
der our sincere thanks to Elder David C. Dun-
bar for his energy, patience, and wise counsel
given during the commencement of our organ-
ization.
	The following is a statistical and financial
report.
	Organized by Elder David C. Dunbar, with
Sister Flora Crawford President: Sisters Jane
Wilson and Flora Picken 1st and 2d Coun-
selors; Sister Francilda Burnside Secretary,
Sister Jane Wilson Treasurer; and eight mem-
bers enrolled; since then six members have been
added: five have emigranted to Utah leaving a
total of thirteen members and officers. The
money collected during the year amounted to
