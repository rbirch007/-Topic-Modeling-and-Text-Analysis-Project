 BY HANNAH T. KING.

	The river of life
	With variety rife,
Bearing Vessels of every kind—
	Bound to deliver,
	To the Great Giver
The freightage none can leave behind:

	Often rough is it,
	Often cold is it,—
On! On! flowing river of Time!—
	Arks float upon it,
	Barks float upon it,—
On! On! to Eternity's clime!

	Alas! wrecks are there,
	The great and the fair,
That were sailing along in their pride!—
	Yet many find rest
	In a Haven so blest
Yes, the Haven where rest doth abide.

	I love thee great river!
	I thank God the giver,
That I sail on this river sublime!—
	God keep the argosies,
	With their rich merchandise,
All safe in the heavenly clime.

	Pray for them pray for them,
	Ferfently say for them,
May the great Pilot decree,
	Though deep in the Gulph stream
	O'er whelm'd it would surely seem,
At last in His Haven they'll be'!

	All hail to the river!
	Life's deep, mighty river!
The bold rushing river of Time!
	May each vessel be found
	Sea-worthy and sound,
Bearing freightage for Heaven's own clime!

	Our God is above
	The sweet God of love,
His spirit broods over the river?
	Row down life's stream
	Beneath His bright beam,
In the light and the love of such Giver!

Salt Lake City Aug. 3rd. 1881.
