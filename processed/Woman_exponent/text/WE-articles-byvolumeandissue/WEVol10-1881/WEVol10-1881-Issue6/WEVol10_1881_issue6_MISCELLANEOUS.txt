MISCELLANEOUS.

	WE are given to understand that the Princess
Louise of Lorne is the composer of the new set
of valses on old Scotch airs, published under
the name of Louise Campbell.
	M'DLLE SARAH BERNHARDT already gives
out that she intends to make a second tour in
America by-and-bye, when she hopes to play to
the Americans in their own language. She is
now hard at her English studies.
	THE Rev. Timothy Flint used to say that,
"If this world is ever to become a better and
happier world, Woman, properly enlightened,
aware of her influence, and disposed to exert it
aright, must be the prime mover in the great
work."
	Is M. Gambetta about to declare on the side
of Woman's Rights? It would seem so, from a
speech of his at the Trocadero in which the
following passage occurs:—"Without exaggera-
tion, it is certain, gentlemen, that reforms ought
to be introduced into our legislation which
shall give to woman all her prestige, all her
authority, all her rights, and the power to ex-
ercise them."
