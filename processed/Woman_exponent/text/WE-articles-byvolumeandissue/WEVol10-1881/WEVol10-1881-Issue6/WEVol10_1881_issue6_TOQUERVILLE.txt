		      TOQUERVILLE.

	We had a visit at Toquerville last
spring from Sisters E. R. Snow Smith, Zina D.
Young and Minerva Snow. They gave us
some good instructions and organized a Pri-
mary Association, which I think will be the
means of much good. About that time we had
a visit from sister Mary Nebeker from Bear
Lake, she attended our meetings and seemed
to enjoy the visit very much; she has a sister
in this settlement. The sisters in our society
one and all express a desire to live their re-
ligion and do all they can in assisting to build
up the Relief Society and other organizations
in the kingdom of God. We have sent for a
Church Organ using the money we had in the
treasury for that purpose, thinking it might
be a benefit to our Primary Association in en-
couraging the young to attend meeting. The
weather is oppressively warm at present; we
are anticipating a good time on the 24th.
	Ever wishing for the success of the EXPON-
ENT, I remain your sister in the everlasting
Gospel.
