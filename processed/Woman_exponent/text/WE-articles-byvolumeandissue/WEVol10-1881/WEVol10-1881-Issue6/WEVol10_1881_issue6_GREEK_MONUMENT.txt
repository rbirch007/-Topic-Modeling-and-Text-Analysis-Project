GREEK MONUMENT.﻿

	A London paper, "The Ladies Pictorial"
announces in its last issue, something of the
following:—Probably it is well known that the
Heart of Lord Byron was buried at Missi-
longhi, where he died, and now on that very
spot a monument is being erected to the im-
mortal Poet! It is to consist of a Statue
covered in Pentelic marble by the eminent
Sculptor Vitalis of Syracuse; a large sum of
money has been secured for the purpose and as
the monument is intended to be a memorial of
Hellenic gratitude to one who gave his song, his
life for Greece—so only Greeks have been
allowed to contribute to the fund.
	So Harold ends in Greece his last Pilgrim-
age.	H. T. K.
