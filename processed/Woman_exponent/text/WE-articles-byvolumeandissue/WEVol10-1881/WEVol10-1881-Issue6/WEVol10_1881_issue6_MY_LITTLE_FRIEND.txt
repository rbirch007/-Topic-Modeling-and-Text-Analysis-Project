MY LITTLE FRIEND,

				-----
	My little friend, plainly dressed in black
and white, comes to see me twice a month.
How the visit of this tiny one is desired by my
children and by myself.  Eyes grow brighter,
as feet fly faster, to anounce her coming.  Im-
patiently, the moments fly until a respite in
work, gives time for us to sit and hear the
choice words stored up for our hearing.  Her
coming is usually when the sun has sunk into
the legendary "hole in the ground," of the red
man, of the mountains, and twilight comes the
mellowest time of the day.  The hot air of the
south is cooled, and birds dart hither and
thither after their insect prey.
	Darkness comes on, the lamp is lit, and
gathering around our friend, the friend of all,
the expounder of principles of life and salva-
tion, speaking in poetry, and prose, and eloqu-
ently giving us the words of our sisters that
are far away, we listen.
	Little friend, how looms up before me faces
that are distant, and the echoes of voices that I
hear not.  Silent is she, until she finds some
one to give a voice to the innermost expressions
of her heart, her noble thoughts, and sympa-
thetic words.  As she visits me so do her numer-
ous sisters visit other families at the same hour,
laden with the same instructions, counsel, love,
and pity.  Who is she?  What is her name?
