OBITUARY.

	The Y. L. M. I. A. of Marriott Ward has sus-
tained a great loss in the death of Mary A.,
daughter of Silas H. and Almina Tracy, who
was born in Weber County April 21, 1857, and
was baptized in the year 1867. Her life was one
of usefulness; she was ever ready to do good,
assisting her parents, helping the poor, and
obeying the counsels of those placed over her in
the Lord. She has been circumspect in all the
walks of life, and consequently above reproach.
She held the office of Assistant Secretary of our
Association, was also an efficient teacher in the
Sunday School, which offices she faithfully filled
until her sickness (consumption) forbade her
attendance. She had been through the House
of the Lord, and received her blessings therein.
Also had been baptized for about thirty of her
relatives. Her life was filled up with purity and
kindness. She endured her long and severe
sufferings with great fortitude, and passed peace-
fully away at her home on the 8th of July, 1881,
aged 24 years, 2 months and 17 days. Her fu-
neral was largely attended, and her remains
were followed to their last resting place in the
Ogden cemetery by a numerous concourse of
relatives and friends. The young ladies decked
her coffin with beautiful wreaths of flowers, of
varied hue, as a token of their respect and high
esteem for their departed friend.
