RESOLUTIONS

Of Respect, to the memory of Sister M. Brim-
	hall, who died in Spanish Fork, Sept. 9th,
	1881; aged 15 years, 8 months and 17 days.

	Adopted by the members of the Y. L. M. I. A.
of Spanish Fork, Sept. 12th, 1881.

	Whereas, Our Father in heaven has seen fit
in the dispensations of His Providence to call
from our midst our much beloved and respected
friend and sister, Prudence M. Brimhall.
	Resolved, That we, the members of the Y. L.
M. I. A. have sustained the loss of a valued
friend and co-worker, who had become endear-
ed to us by her faithfulness, genuine piety,
and gentle, unassuming deporment.
	Resolved, That we sincerely condole and sym-
pathize with her afflicted family in their bereave-
ment.
	Resolved, That these Resolutions be spread
upon the record of the Society, and a copy be
sent to the Woman's Exponent, and our Sister's
sorrowing family.
	Resolutions of respect and condolence were
also adopted in the Sabbath School.
			MRS. MARY MORRISON, Prest.
