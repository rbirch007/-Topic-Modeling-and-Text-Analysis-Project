	PROVO.

	Minutes of the Quarterly Conference of the 
Relief Society of Utah Co., held in Provo City 
May 27th 1881, Present on the stand, Pres-
ident Margaret T. Smoot, Counselor Mary-
John, Secretary and Corresponding Sec'y. Sis-
ter E. R. Snow, from Salt Lake City, was also 
with us, and as usual a few of the brethren of
the Priesthood met with us. Meeting opened
by singing and prayer. President Margaret T-
Smoot said, I am pleased to meet with you
again and see so many familiar faces. I am 
thankful that we are able to meet together, 
and that we are favored to-day with the pres-
ence of our beloved Sister Snow, for we all 
claim her; she is the head of our Relief Socie-
ties to counsel advise and guide us. I have 
been used to call on the brethren to speak first,
when there were any with us, but Sister Snow
says it is proper for us to speak first, and let
 the stronger follow the weak, that if we say 
anything that needs correcting it can be cor-
rected.
