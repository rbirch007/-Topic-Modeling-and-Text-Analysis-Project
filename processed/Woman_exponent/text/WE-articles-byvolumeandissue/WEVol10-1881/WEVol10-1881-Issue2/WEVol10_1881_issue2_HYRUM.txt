		HYRUM.

EDITOR EXPONENT—Thinking a line from
Hyrum would not be amiss, we submit our re-
port. We held our Thirteenth Annual meet-
ing on Monday, the 9th ult; On the stand
were Bishop O. N. Liljenquist and Counselors,
Sister Benson, President of Stake, her Coun-
selors and Secretary. Sister C. Liljenquist
presiding. After the usual exercises and read-
ing reports the sisters from Logan addressed
us, and spoke words of encouragement. Said:
“Your reports show that you are united in
ministering to the poor and building temples.”
Exhorted us to continue in well doing.
Bishop Liljenquist gave good counsel on the
subject of obedience to the Priesthood and the
order to be maintained in our organizations.
Counselors G. Unsworth and J. McBride made
a few remarks. Sister Liljenquist exhibited a
fine specimen of reeled silk from Sister J.
Neilson, of Hyrum. The officers were then
elected, after which meeting adjourned. Bene-
diction by Bro. J. C. Thorison.
After meeting the sisters sat down to a boun-
teous repast that was prepared in the Hall.
In the evening a party was given, in which the
young folks joined, the profits to go to emigrate
a poor boy from Denmark.	
Praying for the success of your valuable
paper and for every noble enterprise, we sub-
scribe ourselves,
L. Liljenquist, Pres’t..
N. M. Neilson, Sec’y.
