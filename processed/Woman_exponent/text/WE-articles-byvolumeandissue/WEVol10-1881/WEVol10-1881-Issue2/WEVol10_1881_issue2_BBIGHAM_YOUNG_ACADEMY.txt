BBIGHAM YOUNG ACADEMY.
PROVO CITY UTAH, MAY 30, 1881.
EDITOR WOMAN'S EXPONENT:
	Dear Sister:— Permit me to express through
the medium of your esteemed paper the thanks of
this Faculty for the interest shown and efforts
made in behalf of the Brigham Young Academy
by Sisters Eliza R. Snow, and Zina D. H. Young,
and as  result of which the sum of seventy-three
dollars and sixty cents, donated by various Relief
Societies south in Scipio and in Washington and
Kane Counties has been placed at the disposal of
our Treasurer, to assist in procuring the neces-
sary apparatus for our chemical laboratory.
	Not only is this donation a substantial aid
financially to our hard struggling institution,
but also a testimonial of sympathy with, and
faith in our efforts to prepare the youth of Zion
for that great, future in which they are destined
to fill so conspicuous a part.
	The Board, Faculty, and Students  of this
Academy will, with the help of God, endeavor
to deserve always the confidence and support of
the Authorities of the Church and of the Saints
generally, and thus show their appreciation for
all acts of kindness, of which they are in so
many ways of late the happy recipients.
	With kind regards, your brother in the
Gospel.
