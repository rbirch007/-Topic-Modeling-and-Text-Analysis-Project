THE COMET.
				----
 It is needless to announce the appearance of a
comet in the heavens, as it is the talk of the
town at present, and it seems to be greatly ad-
mired. It is indeed very beautiful as well as
wonderful. Notwithstanding the researches of
astronomers and scientists, there is still some
mystery about the appearance and disappear-
ance of these heavenly bodies. They are very
erratic in their course, and one’s ideas and theo-
ries on the subject are very likely to be upset, if
positive calculations are made of their coming
or going. Anciently comets were looked upon
as sure precursors of dire disaster by land or sea,
but people have become so matter-of-fact now-
a-days, that it is unpopular to express a belief in
any of these old fashioned signs. But certain it
is the scripture says: “There shall be signs in the
heavens,” etc.
