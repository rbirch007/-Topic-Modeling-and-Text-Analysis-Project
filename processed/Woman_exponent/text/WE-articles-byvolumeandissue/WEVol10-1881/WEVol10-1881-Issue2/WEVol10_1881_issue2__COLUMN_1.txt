PEN SKETCH OF AN ILLUSTRIOUS
			WOMAN.
		ELIZA R. SNOW SMITH.
			CONTINUED.
	As Sister Eilza was returning home from
her travels abroad, having seen much of life in
different countries, the extremes of wealth and
poverty, she appreciated more and more the
blessings enjoyed by the Latter-day Saints in
their peaceful mountain homes; and her heart
seemed to reach out after her dear friends who
were awaiting her coming.
	From Vienna,where we left the party in our
last chapter, they went to Berlin. The wea-
ther was cold and stormy, but one sunny day,
which they improved by sight-seeing. Sister
Eliza mentions one principal promenade called
Linden Street, from the street being lined on
both sides with four rows of lime trees. She
also speaks of an extensive public ground
called the Thier Garten, with artificial forests.
shrubbery and delightful avenues, walks, etc.
Here were multitudes of people of all classes.
