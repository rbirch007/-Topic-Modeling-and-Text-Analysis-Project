BIRTHDAY PARTY.

	Sister Mary A. Frantzen, Pres. of the R. S.
of Spring City with a number of the sisters of
the Society, met at the residence of Mrs. M. A.
Hyde July 1, at 8 o'clock P. M. and gave her a
surprise party, many expressed their thankful-


ness and appreciations of her most beneficial
counsel.
	Bishop Frederic Olsen was present and blessed
and encouraged the sisters with all the energy
of his soul. Pres Hyde was too overcome by
her feelings to say much, but made a few grate-
ful remarks in love and appreciation of the con-
fidence which the sisters showed for her. Most
earnestly wished she might prove herself worthy
of the implicit confidence with which they con-
fide in her.
	A number of nice presents were made her as
tokens of love and respect. We have also been
favored with a visit from Sister Sarah M. Kim-
ball, and S. M. Heywood, who met with our
Primaries in conference. Their report showed
that under the management of their Pres. their
Society is in a flourishing condition and is doing
a good work among them. They addressed the
children giving them much instruction which
will be like bread cast upon the waters. They
afterward met with the R. S. and addressed a
crowded audience of both sexes. Much good ad-
vice was given which if put in practice will prove
a lasting benefit to all those who were present.
Bishop Olsen heartily endorsed all that had
had been said, and asked God to bless and
strengthen them. 
	Success to the EXPONENT it is ever a welcome
visitor, and to all who are working for the estab-
lishment of truth is the wish of your sister in
the covenant.
