GATHERING AUTUMN LEAVES.
"So, so; you've been wandering over the hills,
To catch the music of rippling rills,
As they wind through the crooked and rocky ravine,
Like glistening ribbons of satin and sheen.

"Take care, little woman! to you it seems fine;
To me, you have only been wasting your time;
It would bring us, I think, more stockings and shoes,
It you helped in to gather the corn from the mews."

"Our time is our money, I've heard mother say,
In wasting our time we throw money away;
To waste not and want not was always out rule--
'Twas a copy-line written me when I was at school."

To the loving wife's face came a look of surprise,
The tears came unbidden into her brown eyes--
"Dear John, do not blame me," she said as she kissed
His hand with a grace that he could not resist

"I have hurried all day to be able to go,
I can make up the time after supper, you know;
But I wanted to gether some leaver and some moss,
And see the sun  in his cloudlets of floss.

"And then, as I gathered my autumnal leaves,
(Did you ever behold such beauties as these?)
I prayed to the Lord that our autumn of life
Might always be free from confusion and strife."

"That out lives, dear heart, may be glowingand bright, 
With a halo of love and heavenly light,
As the setting sun and these autumn leaves, 
Which I've gathered beneath the grand old trees.

"And John, I prayed that our boys and our girls
Might be as pure as these mountain pearls,
As clear and as bright as the rippling rills--
'Tis a glourious thing to go to the hills."

"John's voice was strangely soft and low
As he answered, "Dear Katie, next time you go
I'd like to go too, I think that I can,
You have made me think I'm a child again.

"But Katie, the heavens are full of days,
And I do not think it always pays
To work all day and at night work late,
So put it away as once, my Kate."
							E.A.B.
