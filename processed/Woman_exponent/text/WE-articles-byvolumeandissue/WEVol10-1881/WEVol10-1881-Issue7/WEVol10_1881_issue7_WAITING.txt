WAITING,
	PRESIDENT GARFIELD, so long a severe suf-
ferer, and seemingly near his appointed time for
release from suffering, still lingers on the border
of the shaowy land. The physicians at the
present express considerable hope, and Mrs. Gar-
field, a firm faith in his recovery, a con-
viction that in his case a "a miracle will
be wrought." The sympathies of
the nation are with her in this trying
hour, and it must be a source of satisfaction in
the midst of such a fiery ordeal to know that all
the people are interested and thousands sending
up petitions to Him who rules and overrules and
holds the destiny of mations in His hands, that
He will sare the life of this great man, the
Chief Executive of the American Republic. Mrs.
Garfield's courage and fortitude in the midst of
her earnest solicitude for the welfare of her hus-
band is another proof of woman's strength of
endurance under more than ordinary affliction.
A few more days at least will determine the
important question, whether President Garfield
has vitality sufficient to rally after so great a
struggle with the grim monster death. Mean-
time, the nation is waiting in anxious and pray-
erful suspense, for that the people loved him is
evident on every hand.
