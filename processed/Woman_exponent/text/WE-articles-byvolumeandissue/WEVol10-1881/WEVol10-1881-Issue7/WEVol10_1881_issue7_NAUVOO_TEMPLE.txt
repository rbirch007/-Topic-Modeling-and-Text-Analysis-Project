NAUVOO TEMPLE.

	Nauvoo, or "The beautiful," as it was some-
times designated, was formerly called Commerce
and sometimes the "City of Joseph." October
3rd, 1840, at a general conference it was resolved
to build a "House of the Lord" there, to com-
mence it within ten days of that date and to ap-
propriate every tenth day to the building of the
sacred edifice. April 6th, 1841, the first stone
was laid amidst the most straightened circum-
stances of the Saints. In the Spring of 1843, the
Temple was raised fifteen or twenty feet above
the level of the ground. October 6th, 1845, it
was so far finished as to allow a General Confer-
ence of the Church to be held in it. At this
conference the subject of the evacuation of
Nauvoo was canvassed before the Saints and
committees for the sale of houses, farms, lots,
etc., appointed. Sunday, the day before, 5,000
saints met in it for the first time. May 1st,
1846, public services were held in the Temple,
the amount said to have been expended on it by
the Saints exceeds $1,000,000. November 10th,
1848, it was fired by an incendiary, the light of
its fire was visible for 30 miles. May 27th, 1850,
a tornado blew down the north wall and so
shook the building that the Icarians who had
been engaged in rebuilding the edifice for their
use deemed it advisable to pull down the east
and south walls leaving only the west wall.
									A. F.
