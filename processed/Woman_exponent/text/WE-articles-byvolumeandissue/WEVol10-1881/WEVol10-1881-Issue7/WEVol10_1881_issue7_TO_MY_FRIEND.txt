TO MY FRIEND.

Into my bosom dropped a star,
From out the Heaven's vast firmament,
And there so pure and bright it lay,
I scarce believed to me 'twas sent.
I clasped it close with wild delight,
I bathed its crimson rays with dew,
I wondered much, for love was blind,
But now I know, dear friend, 'twas you.

Into my bosom breathed a song,
From spheres celestial, pure and clear,
Its harmonies my soul entranced,
And life stood still its strains to hear.
My saddened heart it filled with joy,
Breathed o'er my spirit strength anew,
I wondered much, for love was dumb,
But now I know, sweet friend, 'twas you.

Into my bosom crept a flower,
Entwined around my heart to kiss,
Its glorious beauty charmed my eyes,
Its fragrance steeped each sense in bliss.
Perfumes of Araby the blest,
Exhaled from out its petals blue,
I wondered much, till love confessed,
That star, and song, and flower were you.
	ANONYMOUS.
SALT LAKE CITY, February 10, 1881
