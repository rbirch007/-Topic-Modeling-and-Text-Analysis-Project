A PLEASANT SURPRISE.

			MILL CREEK, August 5th, 1881.
	Quite a number of relatives and friends, of
Sister Jeanette Park, assembled at her residence
in Mill Creek Ward, July the 20th to welcome
her home after a three months visit South.  At
about 5 o'clock P.M., the party assembled await-
ing the arrival of the Southern train, which was
to bring Sister Park home.  When it did arrive
her surprise and joy at meeting so many friends,
can better be imagined than expressed.  At 6
o'clock the party sat down to tables sumptuously
loaded, and partook of a repast that was elegant
to the eye as well as delicious to the taste.  There
were about 100 persons present.  Sister Park re-
ceived some very useful and elegant presents
well worthy of mention.  After supper was over
various amusements were engaged in; such as
music, dancing, singing etc., which lasted until
about 12 o'clock at night, when the happy as-
semblage dispersed, feeling they had enjoyed a
very happy time.


Sister Park likes the Southern country very
much, and while there she had a very nice time,
especially working in the St. George Temple for
her dead, and she feels to bear her testimony of
the Holy Spirit that surrounds the Temple, caus-
ing such a peaceful good feeling to prevail in and
around the Temple, and throughout the place.
							O. S.
