AT REST.

	Another dear one has departed from this to a
bettter state of existence; our aged sister Patsy
Ann De Berry. She was familiarly known in
the 10th Ward, Salt Lake City, where she re-
sided as "Grandma Smith," having been sealed
to the late Samuel H. Smith, brother to the
Prophet Joseph Smith.  She was born February


4th, 1808, in Simpson County, Kentucky, and
was the youngest child of William Lowe and
Margaret Fare.  She was desirous of joining
herself to some church in her youthful days, but
her father desired her not to do so, as he said the
true church was not upon the earth, but that it
would be before long; he might not, he added, live
to see it, but she would, and become identified
with it.  She was baptized into the Church of
Jesus Chirst of Latter-day Saints, March 10th,
1835, by James Emmett.  She never gathered
with the Saints until the fall of 1875, owing to
her husband not having embraced the gospel,
she having lived with him until his death.  She
was well posted in all the affairs of the Church
in the mountains, as well as to a great extent
in the nations of the earth, as she took all the
publications of the Church as they were publish-
ed from time to time.  She lived the life of a 
true Latter-day Saint, full of faith and good
works, keeping very faithfully "the Word of
Wisdom."  I believe she never tasted tea, coffee
or intoxicants of any kind, not even home-made
wine.  She went to her rest at the ripe old age
of 73 years, 6 months and 9 days, on Saturday
afternoon, August 13th, 1881, at 2 p. m., with
the assurance of coming forth in the morning of 
the first resurrection.  "Peace to her ashes."
								T. C. J.
