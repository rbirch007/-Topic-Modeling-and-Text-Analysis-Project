AUCKLAND NEW ZEALAND

	EDITOR EXPONENT.—This branch of the
Relief Society was organized by Elder George
Batt, Dec. 22, 1880. Sister Isabel Lucy ap-
pointed President, and Sister Day and Fox her
Counselors, Sister Williams, Secretary, Sister
Miles Treasurer. We commenced to accumu-
late a fund which was cheerfully subscribed to
by most of the sisters, some giving money,
others articles of clothing, etc., un[-]il at the date
of writing, our books show[-] the sum of £10.14,0
as having been received, and disbursed, £105,0
to the poor, and remainder to assist the mis-
sionaries in their labors in this land; we feel to
thank God that we are considered worthy to
assist in the great work of redemption.
	In June 9, we bade farewell to our beloved
President and her counselors, viz., Sisters Lucy.


and Day, they leaving this place for the land
of Zion, whereupon Sister Hannah Cox was
called to preside and was sustained by unani-
mous vote of the Society, which now numbers
15 members, we are anxious to do our duty,
and are assisted in this at times by Elder
Romley meeting with and occasionally instruct-
ing us, in relation to the same; his motto is "if
we do but little, let us do that little well," which
motto we adopt and hope to be able to carry
out. The Society meets semi-monthly—once for
sewing and once for business purposes, we read
with pleasure in the EXPONENT of the efforts
of our sisters at home in Zion to assist in rolling
on the work of God, and anxiously look for-
ward to the time when we will be privileged to
be with you in person as we now are in spirit,
for with the poet we truly say,

"I know it is the promised land;
My home, my home is there."

	That God may bless you in your efforts to do
good and all who favor Zion and her interests,
is the prayer of your sisters in the Gospel of
Christ.
				HANNAH COX, Pres't.
