					ELIZA R. S. SMITH.

	Sister Eliza, as has been stated, like other
native-born American women, prided herself up-
on the Constitution, believing it gave religious
liberty to all of whatever creed, sect or de-
nomination. Her forefathers fought for liber-
ty and freedom, and she is strongly endowed
with the spirit of true patriotism. That deci-
sion was to her a disappointment, and she could
but give expression to her outraged feelings in
scathing sarcasm. It seemed impossible that
those wise and judicious minds, from whose
judgment there is no appeal save to One above,
should render such a decision, and one cannot
help exclaiming, "How are the might fallen!"
