EDITORIAL NOTES.
				     -----
	We respectfully ask our subscribers, to excuse
the delay of the Woman’s Exponent this issue,
as it was in consequence of the illness and death
of dear Mother Whitney.
	The Ladies’ Lecture Committee appointed at
the Young People’s Conference, Jan. 22, are
prepared to attend ta any calls made for lectures
in the different Associations.
	The Legislators are diligently busy at work,
notwithstanding Congress talks of putting this
Territory into the hands of five men, who will
probably undo all their proceedings.
	The address of the Secretary of the General
President of Relief Society is, Mrs. Sarah M.


Kimball, P. O. Box 785, Salt Lake City, Utah.
General President Y. L. M. I. A., Mrs. Elmina
S. Taylor, P. O. Box 625, Salt Lake City, Utah.

	On Monday, Feb. 6, a Primary Association
was organized in the Fifth Ward of this city,
with Mrs. Myra Winters, President. Sister
Eliza R. S. Smith officiated in the organization,
giving much good instruction, and afterwards
Sister M. I. Horne addressed the parents and
children upon the benefits resulting from these
institutions.


﻿ON Wednesday evening, Feb. 8, a Social Pic-
nic Party convened in the Lion House, this city,
under the auspices of the 18th Ward Relief So-
ciety, Ellen Barton, President, President John
Taylor and ladies, also Apostle Erastus Snow,
Presidents E. R. Snow Smith and M. I. Horne,
invited guests, were present. At about 7 o’clock
p. m., from one hundred and fifty to two hun-
dred people were seated around tables laden with
the bounties of the earth; after which the eve-
ning was spent in a truly social manner, in con-
versation, interspersed with instrumental solos,
songs, speeches and recitations. The spirit of
love and union prevailed, in which all partici-
pated, until 11 o’clock p. m., when the assem-
blage was dismissed with prayer by Elder John
Nicholson
