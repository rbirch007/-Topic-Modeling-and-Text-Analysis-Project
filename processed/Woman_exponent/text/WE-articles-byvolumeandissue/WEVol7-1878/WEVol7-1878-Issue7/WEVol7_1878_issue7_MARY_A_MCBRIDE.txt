	MARY A. MCBRIDE.

	[From "Deseret News."]
	DIED, In Coveville District, Cache Co,, at 8
p. m., Tuesday, Aug. 20th, from the effects of
severe injuries received, both internal and ex-
ternal, by the falling and rolling of a horse,
William Frederick, son of Mark and the late
Ellen Comish Preece.
	Deceased was born at Franklin, Oneida Co.,
Idaho, November 19th, 1867.
	At Alpine City, Utah Co,, Utah Territory,
on Saturday morning, August 24, 1878, princi-
pally of heart disease, Matilda, wife of Bishop
Thomas J. McCullough, of Alpine, aged 37
years and 10 months.
	Sister McCullough had been more or less af-
flicted for five or six years past, a portion of
that time suffering intensely, and for the last
three months her sufferings have increased un-
til it has been almost surprising how a human
being could bear it, although every attention
and care that could be has been used by both
family and friends for her re[-]ie[-].


The funeral services were held at Alpine
meeting-house on Sunday, when very excel-
lent remarks were made by Bishop L. E. Har-
rington, of American Fork, Elders Wm. H.
Kelsey, of Springville, and Wm. J. Strong, of
Alpine. The singing of the combined choirs
of American Fork and Alpine, was both beau-
tiful and appropriate. The citizens, en masse,
of Alpine, as well as many from Salt Lake and
the neighboring settlements, showed their
grert respect for the deceased and bereaved, by
their presence and expressions of sympathy.
The services were concluded at the cemetery
by a dedicatory prayer by Father Zebedee Colt-
rin of Spanish Fork. The sympathy for Bro.
McCullough and family in their bereavement
is unanimous.
