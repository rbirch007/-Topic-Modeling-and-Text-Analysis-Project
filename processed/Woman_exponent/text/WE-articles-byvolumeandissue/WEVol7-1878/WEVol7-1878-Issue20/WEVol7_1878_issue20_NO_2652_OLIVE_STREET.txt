			No. 2652 OLIVE STREET.
		St. Louis, Mo. Feb. 20th, 1879.
MRS. EMMELINE B. WELLS : — DEAR
	MADAM:
	I have been authorized and requested to
give notice that the annual meeting of the
National Woman Suffrage Association will
be held at St. George's Hall, corner of 7th
and Locust streets, St Louis, on the 7th,
8th and 9th days of May next; on which
occassion you are most cordially invited
and requested to be present.
	It is believed that the time is auspicious
for making this the most important meet-
ing of the Association ever held, for which
purpose no efforts will be spared to make
the attendance as large and influential as
possible, so that it may prove to be a veri-
table "Ecumenical Council" of the friends
of Woman's enfranchisement. I am as-
sured that delegates will be in attendance
from every section of the country, more
especially from the South, and what is term-
ed, the Great North-West
	I have every reason to believe that the
Convention will be well worth your attend-
ance, and do much to speed the cause we
have so much at heart.
	Every attention will be paid to delegates,
and accommodations secured for them, as
far as possible, in private families.
	I trust you will give this notice as wide
a publicity as possible, and inform me
whether or not, you will attend.
	With much respect,
			Yours Truly,
				VIRGINIA L, MINOR,
			Vice President for Missouri
