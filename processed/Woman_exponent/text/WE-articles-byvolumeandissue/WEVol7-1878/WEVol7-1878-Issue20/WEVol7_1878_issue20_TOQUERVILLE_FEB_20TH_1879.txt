			TOQUERVILLE, Feb. 20th, 1879.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The annual meeting of our Society was
held Jan. 18, 1879. The report of the year
1878, was as follows:
Donated to needy....................................... $40 55
Donated to Sanpete Temple cash............   6
Capital Stock in Co-op store......................    30
Capital Stock in Washington Factory            25
Cash on hand..............................................    20
Sundry articles.............................................    94 80
	We have bought a lot, to raise mulberry
trees, for which we paid $50. The brethren
turned out "en masse" and fenced the lot
entire and made other improvements. The
sisters arranged a picnic and surprised the
workers, which they received thankfully
and had a good time. The Society gave
the workers a party and supper; over one
hundred and fifty persons partook of the
supper, "and all went merry as a marriage
bell." We had a very pleasant call from
Sisters Minerva Snow, Anna Ivins, Lizzie
Calkins, Elizabeth Bently and Eyring from
St. George. They gave us some excellent
counsel and spoke very encouragingly to
the sisters, which was highly appreciated
by us.
	Wishing prosperity to the "EXPONENT."
		Your Sisters in the Gospel
			ELIZABETH KLIENMAN Pres.
