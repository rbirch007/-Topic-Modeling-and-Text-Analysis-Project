RECEPTION GIVEN TO GEN. D. H. WELLS.

	Never has there been such a grand public
demonstration by this people as that given to
Gen. Wells on his release from the Peniten-
tiary, Tuesday, May 6. The people were in-
dignant—words are too weak to express the
feelings that were aroused in the hearts of the
Latter-day Saints by his sentence to two days'
imprisonment and $100 fine—for not betraying
that which he held more sacred than his life.
His words in the Court Room on that momen-
tous occasion will be handed down to posterity,
and his name will forever be cherished by all
who reverence and honor integrity to principle.
"It has ever been and is a principle of my life
never to betray a friend, a brother, my country,
my God or my religion."
	At a large meeting of the brethren and sis-
ters held in the Council House, Sunday, May
4, after the services in the Tabernacle, commit-
tees were appointed to arrange a programme
of the proceedings: Mayor Lit[-]le, Hon.
John Sharp, Col. Theo. McKean, Hon. Wm.
Jennings, Col. John R. Winder, Marshal A.
Burt, Geo. Crismon, Esq.
