EDITOR EXPONENT:

	We do not wish to be forgotten, and there-
fore will endeavor to give a short sketch of
our progression. Although we are back-
ward in reporting ourselves we are in a 
thriving condition. We were reorganized
last May by Sister Eliza R. Snow and E.
Davis. I think it aroused the sisters and
caused them to realize more fully their po-
sition in the kingdom of God, for we have

[Column 2]
a better attendance at meeting, and all
manifest a desire to press onward in the
work that we are engaged in. We feel
truly thankful to our beloved sisters for
their visit and good instructions; would be
pleased to see them oftener. We appreciate
their good counsel and feel to say God bless
them in their labors. Our officers are as
follows:
	Elizabeth Fox, President,
	Sophia Meechan,	} Counselors,
	Mary Hawks,	}
	Susie Parnell,	} Secretaries.
	Annie Hatch		}
	Agnes Durant, Treasurer.
	Quite a number of Teachers and the
names of fifty-two members enrolled; thirty
average attendance. Our meeting house,
that has been in contemplation so long, is
now completed with the exception of seats,
which will be finished soon. It was dedi-
cated Dec. 12, 1878. We had a supper and
ball given the evening before on the occa-
sion. Pres. Moses Thatcher and Counselor
Hammond, accompanied by Bro. Ormsby,
were present. We had a time of feasting
and rejoicing, in our prosperity not forget-
ting the poor. The brethren gave much
good instruction in regard to the poor,
storing grain, and raising our children,
which if we regard will prove a great bene-
fit to us. We hold our meetings semi-
monthly. We send our annual report end-
ing June 18, 1878.

	With our estimable President at our
head we are trying to do our duty and keep
up with the spirit of the times. We are
always glad to receive your valuable paper,
and fully endorse the remarks of the sisters
contained in its pages. I remain
		Your sister in the Gospel,
