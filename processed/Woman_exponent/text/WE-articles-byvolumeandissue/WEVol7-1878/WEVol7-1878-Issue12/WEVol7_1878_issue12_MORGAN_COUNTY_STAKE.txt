MORGAN COUNTY STAKE.

	The first Quarterly Conference of the Re-
lief Societies of the Morgan Stake of Zion
convened on the 24th of October at ten A.
M., President Lydia Rich presiding.
There were on the stand Sisters Eliza R.
Snow and Elizabeth Davis from Salt Lake,
also the Presidents of the nine Societies in
the Stake. There were present of the pre-
siding priesthood, Pres. W. G. Smith and
Counselor S. Francis, Bishops Chas. Turner,
John Hopkins, John K. Hall. Although
the weather was unfavorable there was a
good attendance. The meeting was called
to order by Pres. Lydia Rich, and after the
singing and prayer she arose and stated the
object of our meeting.

	Sister Snow then addressed us in a very
encouraging and instructive manner, call-
ing our attention to a great many labors
and duties which we as Saints and daugh-
ters of the Most High, living in the eve-
ning of Time, have to perform. Said our
organization in the R. S. would be of great
benefit to us, if we would honor our posi-
tions. Wished all the Saints to step for-
ward and share in the blessings of the So-
ciety. She regretted to see that here, as
in many other places, the average attend-
ance is so small, and feared if a few did all
the work they would also receive all the

[Column 3]
reward. She made many valuable remarks
upon the principles of love and union,
which should exist in our midst, without
which we could not live the Gospel of Jesus
Christ, as that Gospel knit and bound to-
gether; spoke against the pernicious prac-
tice of evil speaking and backbiting, also
against listening to tale-bearers. Said we
must not look for perfection in each other,
but sustain each other and the priesthood
of God upon the earth; and if we did this
our enemies would be powerless in our
midst.
	Sister E. Davis said she was much pleased
to meet with the sisters in Conference ca-
pacity. Exhorted us to treasure up the
good instructions we had received and carry
them out in our lives, that we might rea-
lize a benefit from the same.
Pres. W. G. Smith made a few remarks
upon the organization and condition of the
Relief Societies, also upon the principle of
co-operation, which is a stepping-stone to
the United Order. Wished the sisters to
be united in their efforts to build up the
Kingdom of God. He referred to the in-
structions given us at our late General Con-
ference at Salt Lake by the presiding priest-
hood of the Church.
	Meeting then adjourned till 2 o'clock p.
m. The Choir sang, and benediction.
	At 2 p. m. meeting convened as per ad-
journment. Opened with singing and 
prayer. The Presidents of the nine Socie-
ties each gave a verbal report of the Socie-
ties over which they presided, which was
very satisfactory; after which Miss E. R.
Snow made a great many very interesting
remarks upon various subjects; said we
should spend our time usefully, and try to
strengthen and encourage each other by
good words and kind acts. Spoke of the
constant warfare between the flesh and the
spirit: Said Pres. Young gave the sisters
a mission, in connection with the brethren,
to lead out in Home Manufacture and the
Storing of grain; wished the sisters of this
County to make themselves eminent in
some particular branch of industry; recom-
mended us to make all the hats and bonnets
necessary for our use, and wished them
made beautiful; suggested that the means
thus saved be spent in purchasing machin-
ery. She bore a faithful testimony to the
truth and divinity of Celestial Marriage;
said she was very thankful that she could
truly say she had never utttered a word
against it. She exhorted us all to be hum-
ble and faithful, and always keep the chan-
nel of communication open between us and
our Heavenly Father.
	Remarks were then made by Mrs. E.
Davis, Bishop Turner, Counselor Francis
and Pres. Lydia Rich. Conference was
then adjourned for three months. Singing
and benediction.
