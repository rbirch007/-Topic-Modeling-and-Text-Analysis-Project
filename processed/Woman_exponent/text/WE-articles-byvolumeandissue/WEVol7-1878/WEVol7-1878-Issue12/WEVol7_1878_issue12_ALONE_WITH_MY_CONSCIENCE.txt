ALONE WITH MY CONSCIENCE.

	I sat alone with my conscience
		In a place where time had ceased,
	And we talked of my former-living,
		Where endless years increase.

	And I knew I should have to answer
		The questions it put to me:
	And to face the questions and answers
		Throughout an eternity.

	The ghosts of forgotten actions
		Came floating before my sight,
	And things I thought were dead things
		Were alive, with terrible might.

	And the vision of my past life
		Was an awful thing to face,
	Sitting alone with my conscience,
		In that solemn and silent place.

	And I thought of a far-away warning,
		Of a sorrow that was to be mine,
	In a land that was in the future;
		And now is the present time.

	And I thought of former reflections,
		What the judgment day would be,
	But sitting alone with my conscience
		Seemed judgment enough for me.

	And I wondered as to the future,
		In this land beyond the grave;
	But no one spake an answer,
		No one came forward to save.

	Then I felt that the future was present,
		And the present would never go by;
	For the awful weight of my past life
		Became an eternity.

	Then I woke from my timely dreaming,
		And the vision passed away;
	And I knew the far off warning
		Was a warning of yesterday.

	And I pray I may not forget it;
		In this land before the grave,
	That I may not call in the future,
		And no one come to save.

	And so I have learned a lesson
		I ought to have known before;
	And although I learned it dreaming,
		I hope to forget no more.
								MARY WRAY.
S. L. City, Sunday; Oct. 20, 1878.
