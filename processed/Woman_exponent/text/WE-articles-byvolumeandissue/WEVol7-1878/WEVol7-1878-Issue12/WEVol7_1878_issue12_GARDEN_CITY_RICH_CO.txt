	GARDEN CITY, RICH CO.,
	Oct. 13, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We have a most beautiful place here,
with an excellent view of the beautiful
Bear Lake; our city is situated on a rolling
plain, with the gently sloping hills sur-
rounding it on the south, west and north,
with a plenty of fine grass for stock.  Our
place is newly settled with about twenty
families, mostly young people.  The boys
have been energetic in getting out the
water; they have made a ditch about six
miles in length.  Some of those who moved
in last year have raised beautiful crops of
wheat, oats, corn, potatoes, cabbages, mel-
ons, cucumbers, squashes, pumpkins, and
almost every variety of vegetables and
garden plants that can be mentioned, and
we hope  the blessings of the Lord to
raise a plenty of fruit in a few years; Pres.
Rich says we shall if we are faithful.
	We have no Bishop here yet, we have a
Priest to take charge of the place; I hope
we will have the privilege of being organ-
ized into a branch, so we can have meet-
ings regularly.  And I hope we may have
a Relief Society; I have been a member of
one for ten years, and always loved our Re-
lief Society meetings in St. Charles; now
I miss them very much. It was also a 
member of the Y. L. M. I. A, and of the
Sabbath School.  Perhaps you can imagine
my lonely feelings in being absent from all
of those dear associations.  We hold meet-
ings once in a while, the Sabbath day seems
very long and lonely without them.
	We have a Post Office established here
now, and I hope to get my papers regu-
larly.  The EXPONENT is every a very wel-
come guest.  If every woman loved to
read it as well as I do, they would not
be without it; it helps to pass the Sabbath
day very pleasantly.
	May our Heavenly Father bless every
undertaking of the sisters in righteousness
is my desire.
	Your sister laborer in the covenant of
Christ,	PHEBE.
