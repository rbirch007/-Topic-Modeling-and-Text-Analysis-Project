	UPPER KANAB, Oct. 18, 1878.
DEAR SISTER WELLS:
	I have recently returned from a visit of
a month's duration to our Dixie; the cli-
mate there at this season is delightful.
	I enjoy myself excellently well when
circumstances are such that I can be liber-
ated for a time from our ranche home, in
visiting among the Saints and attending
the meetings of the sisters both old and
young. In my opinion, a great lack is
visible on the part of many mothers in re-
gard to the young.  Many of us surely can-
not realize the responsibilities that rest up-
on us, nor the bearing our influence has
upon our children for good or evil, or we
would shape our lives differently.  Every
mother in Zion should take and carfully
read the EXPONENT. October 1st is at
hand; "Women as Educators," "Woman's
Love," and "Memory and Observation" are
worth the price of the paper.  Extracts
from the teachings of Sister E. R. Snow
and other faithful workers are invaluable.
A vast amount of good is being done by
the EXPONENT.
	God bless you in your untiring zeal for
the welfare of Zion.
	Your sister in the Gospel,
