			MILLVILLE, May 2d, 1878.

	Meeting called for the re-organization of
the Relief Society. Meeting opened with
singing and prayer. Minutes of the former
meeting and the Quarterly report were
read. Bishop Pitkin spoke of the condi-
tion of the Society, of the first organization,
&c.; wanted to see the Society sustained by
the sisters of the ward; encouraged the
sisters to do right. Brothers King and
Laird made a few remarks on the changes
made since the first organization, and gave
some good instruction. Sarah A. Pitkin
spoke of her resignation, after which the
following nominations were made and
unanimously sustained:

	Susannah Hovey, President,
	Mariah Pitkin,	} Counselors,
	Mary Yates,		}
	Annie Anderson, Assistant Secretary.
	Sarah A. Pitkin, Doctress.

	The above named Sisters were then or-
dained and set apart by Bishop Pitkin and
Counselors.
	Several of the sisters bore their testimo-
ny to the truth of the Gospel, &c. A vote
of thanks was returned to Sarah A. Pitkin,
the former President. Benediction by
George O. Pitkin.
			L. HOVEY, President,
			Matilda Pitkin, Secretary.
