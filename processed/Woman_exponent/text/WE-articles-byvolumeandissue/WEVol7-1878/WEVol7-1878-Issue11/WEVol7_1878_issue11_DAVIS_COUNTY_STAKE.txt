		DAVIS COUNTY STAKE.

	The Quarterly Conference of the Relief
Societies a[-]d Y. L. M. I. A's of Davis Stake
was held at Farmington, Oct. 17, 1878,
commencing at 10 a. m.; Pres. Sarah I
Holmes presiding. There were present on
the stand Pres. M. I. Horne and her Coun-
selors, E. S. Taylor and S. M. Heywood, of 
the Salt Lake Stake, Pres. S. I. Holmes and
Counselors, Mary S. and Nancy A. Clark, of
the Davis Co. Stake.
	After singing and prayer, the minutes of
the preceding meeting and also the reports
of the various Societies in the County were
read by Miss M. E. Richards, Secretary.
The reports show—
Receipts since the organization	$10200 00
Disbursements				8957 00
On hand in the Treasury			1243 00
Wheat stored				57 bu., 21 lbs
	Sisters Horne, Taylor and Heywood occu-
pied the remainder of the forenoon, ex-
horting the sisters to be diligent in the
performance of their duties; home indus-
try, storing grain, unity and punctuality
were the chief subjects, upon which they
spoke with considerable earnestness.
	The afternoon meeting was presided over
by Mrs. Nancy A. Clark, President of the
Young Ladies' Associations of the Stake.
	Secretary Miss Wealthy Richards read
the reports of three Associations, showing
that they are awake to their duties.
	Sisters Horne, Taylor, Heywood, Holmes
and Clark gave some excellent instructions
and encouraged the young ladies to con-
tinue in well doing, and to use their influ-
ence to bring about a reformation in the
young men.
	Mrs. Aurelia Rogers gave a verbal report
of the Primary Association of Farmington,
of which she is President. Manifested a
great interest in the mission assigned her.
A good spirit prevailed, and prompted
each speaker. Meeting adjourned until the
16th of January, 1879.
