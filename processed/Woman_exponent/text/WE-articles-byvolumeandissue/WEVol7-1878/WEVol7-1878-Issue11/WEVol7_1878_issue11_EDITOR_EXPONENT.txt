EDITOR EXPONENT:

	We, the sisters of Panacca Relief Society,
would like to represent ourselves in the
WOMAN'S EXPONENT. We were re-organ-
ized June 22d, 1877, by President McCal-
lister and Counselors, when the following
named sisters were elected:

	Pamela E. Thompson, President,
	Christiana Syphus,	} Counselors.
	Ann Burgess,		}
	Mary A. Syphus, Secretary,
	Elizabeth Wadsworth, Treasurer.

	There are fifty-one members enrolled; we
hold meetings semi-monthly, and a spirit
of union seems to prevail. The sisters are
willing to do all in their power for the ad-
vancement of the work in which we are
engaged. We have a sewing machine, and
also own a half share in the book entitled
"The Women of Mormondom." We own
a town lot here, and have the adobies ready
for building a house, which the brethren
intend to build for us this winter. The
most of the members here take the EXPO-
NENT, and we feel greatly benefitted by the
valuable instruction contained therein.
Wishing you success, we remain, &c.,
		PAMELA E. THOMPSON, President,
		MARY A. SYPHUS, Secretary.

			WELLSVILLE, Aug. 20th, 1878.
