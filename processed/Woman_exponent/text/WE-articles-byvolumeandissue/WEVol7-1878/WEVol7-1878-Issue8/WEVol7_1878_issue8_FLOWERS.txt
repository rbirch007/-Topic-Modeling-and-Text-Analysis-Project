FLOWERS.

	MINISTERS are not like Plato and Aristo-
tle, the originators of their own doctrines,
or the teachers of the doctrines of other
men, but simply the dispensers of the
truths which God has revealed.—Hodge.
	MORALITY without religion is only a
kind of dead reckoning; an endeavor to
find our place on a cloudy sea by measuring
the distance we have to run, but without
any observation of the heavenly bodies.—
Longfellow.
	THE pursuit of something for which we
are not fitted is fruitless; it fails to bring
either happiness or success. If one has
special tastes, or talents, she or he will find
most happiness and do the most real good
by cultivating such faculties, or gifts.-Emile.
	TRUE politeness in every-day life is a
charm that is always becoming. It smooths
out many a wrinkle in human life, it en-
riches the dwelling of the peasant and
gives tone to the home of the lowly.-Emile.
