64		WOMAN'S EXPONENT.


WE SEE by the "National Citizen and
Ballot Box," that the Sunday following
the Third Decade Convention at Rochester,
Rev. A. H. Strong, D. D., President of the
Rochester Theological Seminary, preached
upon "Woman's Place and Work." He
took for his text Genesis ii, 18: "And the
Lord said, it is not good for man to be
alone; I will make him an helpmeet for
him." The editor, in taking up the cud-
gel, quotes the earliest account of the crea-
tion contained in Genesis, i, 27: "So God
created man in His own image, in the im-
age of God created He him; male and
female created he them." After some very
pertinent allusions to the creation, the
writer comments upon Mr. Strong's sermon
on other testimony than Scriptural, and
where he speaks of Cornelia's "jewels" the
writer reminds him that he forgot to say
that Cornelia delivered public lectures upon
philosophy in Rome, and that Cicero paid
the very highest tribute to her learning
and genius. He deems it "too hazardous"
to allow women to vote. "Bad women
would vote." The writer says, "Well,
what of it? Have they not equal rights
with bad men to self-government? Bad is
a relative term." This and much more is
said in defense of the arguments used by
Mr. Strong, whose strong arguments she
has most strongly contradicted.
