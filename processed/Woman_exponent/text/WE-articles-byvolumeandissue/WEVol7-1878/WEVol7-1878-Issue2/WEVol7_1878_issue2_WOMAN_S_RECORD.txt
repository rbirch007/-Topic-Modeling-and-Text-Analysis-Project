WOMAN'S RECORD.

	THE "Woman's Journal" tells us of a
lady lecturer on Communism, who held
forth on Pennsylvania Avenue in Washing-
ton, D. C., May 5th. She had all the argu-
ments of Butler and Voorhees at her 
tongue's end, and declared the American
Commune was already strongly organized.
	MISS Catherine Esther Beecher died at
Elmira, New York, on Saturday, May 11th,
at the residence of her brother, Rev.
Thomas K. Beecher. She was born at East
Hampton, Long Island, Sept. 6, 1800. The
death of Prof. Fisher, of Yale College, to
whom she was betrothed while young,
changed the whole course of her life, and
she never married. She taught for many
years in seminaries and institutions of learn-
ing, and devoted herself to the develop-
ment of plans for the education of women,
in writing and teaching. Miss Beecher has
written some books that have been widely
circulated. One of her later works, enti-
tled "Common Sense Applied to Religion,"
was the subject of much comment in reli-
gious circles, differing widely from the old
Calvinistic theology. Her personal influ-
ence has been very great. Her brother,
Rev. Henry Ward Beecher, in referring to
her life, said, "Few persons, even in the
ministry, had influenced as many minds as
she had, because she reached the mother
mind, and whoever carries the mother
mind moves the world. Although she
was not a mother, she had reached and in-
fluenced millions."
