EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Our Young Ladies' Mutual Improvement
Association was organized Feb. 16, 1876,
under the direction of Mrs. M. I. Horne,
with the following officers:
	Hetty Dunn, President,
	Emma Thornick, } Counselors.
	Mary Jane Bee,
	Clara Walker,
	Harriet Thompson,
	Christine Jacobson,
	Mary Patterson
	Josephine Hart, Secretary,
	Elizabeth Hart, Assistant Secretary.
   We hold our meetings once in two weeks.
Our exercises consist of select readings
from the Church works and other good
books. Our Association numbers in all
seventy-two members, mostly small girls.
We held our first anniversary Feb. 16, at 1
o'clock p.m. Bishop Wm Holmes and
some other brethren were present and gave
us some good instructions. In the evening
the young ladies gave an entertainment,
which consisted of songs and recitations;
the Y. M. M. I. A. assisted the young la-
dies; the performance was a credit to those
who aided in the entertainment. We want
as soon as it is practicable to make our own
hats and bonnets.
	The spirit of improvement is manifested
among our young people to a great degree,
and they seem desirous to carry out the
counsel and instruction of our beloved
President, Brigham Young, in all respects.
Our sincere desire is for the prosperity of
Zion.
		Your Sisters, &c
				HETTY DUNN, President,
				JOSEPHINE HART, Secretary.
