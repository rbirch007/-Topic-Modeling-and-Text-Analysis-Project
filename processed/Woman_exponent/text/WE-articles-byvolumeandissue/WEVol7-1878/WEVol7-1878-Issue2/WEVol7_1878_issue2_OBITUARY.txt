OBITUARY.

	DIED, in Mill Creek Ward, Salt Lake Co.,
June 3d, of Puerperal Fever and diseased lungs,
in her 44th year, Charlotte Rebecca Murphy,
wife of James D. Murphy. Deceased was born
in Uuion Dist., South Carolina; went with her
parents to Georgia when quite young; was
baptized into the Church of Jesus Christ of
Latter-day Saints May, 1808, by Elder Jesse
E. Murphy; emigrated to Utah in March, 1870,
where she continued to reside, faithfully per-
forming the duties of wife, mother, and Saint,
until the Lord called her to another field of
labor. Deceased was a member of the Relief
Society, and performed all the work in the
House of the Lord that time and opportunity
would admit of. She died in full faith, with
bright hopes of a glorious resurrection; truly
can it be said of her that she fought the good
fight, kept the faith, and there is a crown of
life and immortality in store for her. Truly
we mourn her loss, but not as those who have
no hope, for "Blessed are the dead who die in
the Lord, for their works do follow them.
	COM.


QUEEN Victoria was fifty-nine years old
on the 24th ult., and the anniversary was
made a holiday in Canada, while flags were
displayed on the British vessels in New
York.
