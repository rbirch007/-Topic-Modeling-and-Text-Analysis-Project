CORRESPONDENCE.

				LONDON, March 20th, 1878.
	From the ladies of the Relief Society of the
White Chapel Branch, London, to the ladies of
the Relief Society of the Fifteenth Ward,
Salt Lake City, Utah.
DEAR SISTERS:
	We return you our sincere thanks for the
WOMAN'S EXPONENT which we have received
from you, and we find much valuable infor-
mation in its columns and we trust that by the
Spirit of God we may use the information we
gain to the blessing of the poor, and the build-
ing up of God's kingdom. Pardon us for not
writing before, as we were about to celebrate
our Fourth Anniversary we thought you
would like to hear something about it. It
took place on the the 6th of March, we cele-
brated it with a festival. President Cook and
Elder Burton were with us, and all things were
very pleasant. We have made about six hun-
dred visits among the poor saints in the last
year, and we have given among them, 9£. 0s.
8d; out of 9£, 5s. 2d., colected, leaving us in
hand 4s. 6d.
	Trusting that the Spirit of God may ever be
with you, and us in our labors of love and that
it meet your minds to continue favoring us
with "WOMAN EXPONENT," we remain your
sisters in the Covenant of Peace.
	Relief Society Officers as follow; Elizabeth
Young, President; Susan La Page, Ann H
Perry, Counselors; Ann H. Perry, Secretary;
Susan Whi[-]pen, Alice House, Jane Lower,
Eliza Farns, Elizabeth Nankvell, Teachers
			ANN H. PERRY, Secretary.
