EAST BOUNTIFUL, May 17, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Our Young Ladies' Improvement Asso-
ciation was re-organized February 19, 1878,
by Bishop Chester Call and his counsel,
Mrs. Elizabeth H. Barlow, President of the
Relief Society, and her counselors; officers
as follows:
	Nancy Willey, President.
	Jane Holt, Vice President,
	Mary A. Call,
	Eliza Dalrymple,
	Regina Stoker,		Counselors,
	Sarah C. Sessions,
	Mary Willey,
	Jane A. Hale,
	Alice J. Call, Secretary,
	Huldah Stoddard, Assistant Secretary,
	Mary Woolstenholme, Treasurer.
	The Society numbers seventy-two mem-
bers, including officers. Meetings are held
every two weeks; the exercises consist of
lectures and readings from the Church
works, and testimonies. We have a maga-
zine, entitled "The Young Ladies' Com-
panion," which is issued monthly. We
are striving to do our best in this great
and glorious work in which we are engaged;
hoping that we may continue faithful to
the end, I remain,
	Yours very respectfully,
