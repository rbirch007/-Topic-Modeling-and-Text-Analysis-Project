EDITOR EXPONENT:

	Thinking a few lines from our Y. L. M.
I. Association would be interesting to your
many readers, I venture a brief report; we
have thirty-six members enrolled, and have
a very good attendance; we meet weekly;
semi-monthly for working, and semi-
monthly for instruction, and edit a paper
entitled "The Young Ladies' Advocate."
Our former President was called away to
Arizona, and Mrs. Sarah Ann Spilsbury
was appointed as President. We held our
annual meeting April 18th and after the
usual exercises the report for the last year

[Column 2]
was read, showing creditable marks of in-
dustry. We quilted a quilt and prepared
some straw for braiding, and made a
couple of tidies, and had a picnic during
the day, and at night gave a ball for the
benefit of the Association; the evening was
a most enjoyable one, the time was spent in
dancing and a dialogue was recited by
fourteen of the young ladies, we also had
singing. We have a quantity of straw and
purpose making all our own hats this sea-
son, and also various kinds of embroidery.
We, in unison with the daughters of Zion,
take sincere pleasure in reading the EXPO-
NENT, and gratefully acknowledge the ben-
efit we derive from its pages. May success
attend its efforts to advance the cause of
righteousness, is the desire of your sisters.
		SARAH ANN SPILSBURY, Pres't.
