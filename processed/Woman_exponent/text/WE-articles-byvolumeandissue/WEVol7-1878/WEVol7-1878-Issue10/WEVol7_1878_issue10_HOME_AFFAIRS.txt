	CLASS IN OBSTETRICS.—Dr. Romania B.
Pratt will commence her class in Obstetrics,
Puerperal Diseases and Diseases of Women
on Oct. 14th, at 4 p. m. Lectures and recita-
tions held in her office, over Drs. Richards'
drug store, nearly opposite Z. C. M. I. Lec-
ture and Recitation Class twice a week for six
months. Terms, $5 00 per month. Manakin,
Instruments, Plates, etc., for instruction of
class. Office hours 10 a. m. to 6 p. m. For fur-
ther particulars call at office.
	GRAND Sunday School Union; The Sunday
School Union held a mass meeting in the Theatre
in this city, Sunday evening at 7 o'clock p.m.
The Tabernacle Choir and Union Glee Club
were present by invitation. On the platform
was Pres. John Taylor, and several of the
Quorum of the Apostles, Elder George Q. Can-
non, General Superintendent of Sunday Schools
for Utah Ter., Geo. Goddard Assistant Gen.
Supt. for Utah Ter., and Superintendent for
Salt Lake Co., with his assistants W. Willis
and S. L. Evans, also Superintendents for al-
most every Stake in the Territory. The house
was crowded to overflowing, and hundreds of
people went away that could not be accom-
modated with seats or standing room. Elder
Jones of Provo made the opening prayer;
Elder George Q. Cannon made general remarks
upon the the object of Sunday Schools, the con-
dition of them here at the present time, his in-
terest in the children of Zion, and many other
things in connection with this subject. Most
of the Sunday Schools of the various Stakes
were verbally represented by their respective
Superintendents. Choice selections were sung
by the choir and Union Glee Club, which
added greatly to the enjoyment of the evening.
Elders George Goddard and Wm. Willis, sung
"Utah's Best Crop." The sight presented by
the large assembly was charming, and spoke
well for Utah's Sunday School organization.
The meeting was dismissed with prayer by
Elder Joseph F. Smith.
