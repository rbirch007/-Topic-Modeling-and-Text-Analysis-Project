THE FAIR.

	The Territorial Fair held in this City,
under the auspices of the D. A. and M.
Society, commenced Wednesday, Oct. 2, in
the City Market, and was open four days,
and each day was crowded with visitors. It
was undoubtedly the finest display of all
the varieties of exhibits that there has
ever been in this Territory. Those who
had the management of the several depart-
ments and the awarding of prizes deserve
great commendation for their tact, courtesy
and appreciation. The leather and shoe
department cannot be too highly praised,
and the excellence of the home manufac-
tures throughout deserve special mention.
The drawings, paintings, ornamental and
fancy work were worthy a place, and con-
vey the idea in a tangible form that there
is a love of the beautiful among the young
people, which deserves encouragement and
patronage. It is impossible to mention all,
or specify works of merit, the awarding
committee have already determined that
fact, but we will say the live stock on ex-
hibition was superior in beauty and quality.
Altogether it was a fine display, and it will
create a growing interest in the people of
Utah to excel and produce the very best
quality of fruits, flowers, manufactures of
all kinds, and live stock.


WOMEN VS. GIRLS.—Women ought to be
of more importance to society than girls,
but who does not know that the case is ex-
actly the reverse? How many women do 
we all know who shrink from society, give
their whole time to family duties, bury
themselves up at home, and seem to be of
no other use in the world than to dress their
girls for parties, and keep the house in or-
der for their beaux. Of course the chil-
dren grow up with the idea that mother is
of no consequence and don't know the 
world. They go abroad for their opinions,
and spurn all home influence.
