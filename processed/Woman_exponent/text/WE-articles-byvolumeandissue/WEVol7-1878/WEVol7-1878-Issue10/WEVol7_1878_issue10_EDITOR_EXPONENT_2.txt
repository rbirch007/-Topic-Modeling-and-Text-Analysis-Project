EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I think we are improving a little; as a
Society we number seventy members, about
forty average attendants.  Our meetings
are on the first Thursday of every month.
	The 24th of July was celebrated here in
a very pleasant manner; we had a dinner
provided at the schoolhouse, superintended
by the president of the Relief Society, Mrs.
Sarah Greenhatch, and the teachers, when
I believe every family in Bloomington were
invited to partake of the good things pro-
vided.  We had a meeting in the forenoon,
and were addressed by Presidents J. H.
Hart and George Osman, and Bishop
Hulme and others.  The Sunday School
children enjoyed themselves in the dance,
and we had a very good time.
	We have done very little about storing
grain yet; we have about thirteen bushels
of wheat and four hundred pounds of flour,
and intend, if we have health and strength,
to have more in a little while.  Our Presi-
dent has appointed a committee to purchase
wheat and store it up until a time of need.
A number of the young ladies of the Im-
provement Society, as well as some of the
older ones, go to the field and glean wheat
three days in each week, and they feel
blessed and happy in their work.  Bishop
Hulme called on the brethren to help the
gleaners by taking them in their wagons to
and from the field, and bringing their grain
home for them.
	Our financial condition for the past year
is as follows:

Disbursed to the Logan Temple............... $36 00
To the poor..................................................   28 60
Shares in Co-op store.................................   70 00

	The Lord has blessed the people in Bear
Lake Valley this year with a beautiful har-
vest.
	Praying that the blessing of God may at-
tend you, and that your beautiful little pa-
per may be more widely circulated, that
many others may rejoice in its good teach-
ings, we remain,
		SARAH GREENHATCH, President,
		ELIZABETH LANG, Secretary.

				SANTAQUIN, Sept. 22d, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The Relief Society of this place held
their Tenth Annual meeting Sept. 5, 1878.
Our reports were all duly read by our Sec-
retary showing that we had received dur-
ing the year in donations, labor, &c., $210 82;
disbursed during the year to the Temple,
missionaries, the poor, and for charitable
purposes, $102 90.  A report of the past ten
years was also read, commencing Sept. 1st,
1868, ending Aug. 31st, 1878; showing that
we had received during the ten years, in
donations, dividends and labor, $1896 83;
disbursed during the ten years to the Tem-
ple, missionaries, the poor and for charit-
able purposes, $1092 29.
	Our meeting was well attended; we had
sisters present from Salt Lake City, Provo,
Goshen, Benjamin City, and Spring Lake.
The meeting was addressed by Mrs. M. T.
Smoot, Lucy B. Young, Pres. Stickney, and
our Bishop, George Halladay, all of whom
expressed their entire satisfaction at hear-
ing our accounts read, thinking for this
small place we were doing well, and alive
to our duties.  Our Schoolhouse was neatly
decorated for the occasion with articles of
home industry, consisting of quilts, cover-
lids, shawls, tidies, homespun cloth, and a
towel made of sweet clover, by Sister Mar-
garet Arleen, and also nearly three pecks
of cocoons were displayed for the inspec-
tion of our brethren and sisters, which ex-
cited a great deal of curiosity.
	We are striving in our weak way to do
all the good we can to help build up the
kingdom of God.  Wishing you great suc-
cess, we remain yours in the new and ever-
lasting covenant.
				E. J. STICKNEY, Pres't.
				E. CARTER, Sec'y.

			GLENWOOD, Sept. 2d, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Although slow to report ourselves, we are
not behind in the good work;  We number
nearly fifty members, average attendance
between twenty and thirty.  Within the
last four years we have donated.

St. George Temple................................ $25 75
Meeting House......................................  34 75
Welsh Emigration Fund........................  25 00
Manti Temple........................................  17 50
In Co-op Store.......................................  55 00
To the poor............................................ 7 00
	We have stored 161 bu. of wheat.  We
are endeavoring to do the best we can; this
is a new country, and there are no rich
among us, yet we are not poor.  The sisters
generally feel well.  We have the "Women
of Mormondom," and we take the EXPO-
NENT, which we find very interesting.
		Your sisters in the Gospel,
