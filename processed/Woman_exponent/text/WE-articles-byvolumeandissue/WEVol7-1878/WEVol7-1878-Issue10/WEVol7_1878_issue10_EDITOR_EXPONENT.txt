EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The Branch Relief Society of Wanship
is in a prosperous condition, with twenty-
eight members.  The reorganization took
place Dec. 18, 1876.  Bishop Atwood and
Bishop Roundy being present, also several
others of the brethren, Mrs. Louisa J.
Roundy was appointed President, who has
been very active and persevering in her
duty up to this time, Mrs. Mary Reynolds
having previously resigned her office, being
called to remove from Wanship for a sea-
son.
	Our meetings are well attended, peace
and love dwell in our midst.  We have the
"Life of Brigham Young" and the "Wo-
men of Mormondom" to circulate and read.
There are several who take the EXPONENT,
and in doing our duty we feel well and en-
joy the spirit of the Gospel.  Some of our
sisters are good speakers, and all are gener-
ally on the improve in faith and good works.
We hope to be able to withstand the
scourges which await Babylon, wherever
she is found.
 Our Financial report for the past year is
as follows:

Paid to the Temple............................... $31 90
Disbursed to those in need.................  40 00
Wheat stored, 34 bushels.
Donations for general purposes........    54 00
Cash in Treasury...................................   20 50
Remaining in Society in goods.............   63 60

	We feel it important to be up and doing,
as we look at the significant signs of the
times, and we know that we must be hum-
ble and faithful to be able to stand.
	Very respectfully,
		Louisa J. Roundy, President,
		Sarah S. Richards, } Counselors,
		Marla Horton,
		Phebe M. Hatch, Secretary,
		Alice Roundy, Assistant Secretary,
		Hannah Crook, Treasury.
