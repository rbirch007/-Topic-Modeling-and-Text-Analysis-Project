EDITOR EXPONENT:

	We have an organization here with about
seventeen members. We are a small ward
and somewhat scattered, on account of some
living on their farms and ranches, but we
hold monthly meetings for instruction, &c.,
and some working meetings, and we are
trying to do what we can to forward the
great cause. We have very good meetings;
our Bishop and others of the brethren meet
with us from time to time and give us
much good instruction. We were organ-
ized just about one year ago now, and we
can see where we have made some im-
provement, and hope to be able to make
more during the next year. The EXPO-
NENT is a source of great comfort and in-
struction to us, and if every sister prized
it as I do there would be many more copies
taken by the people than there are at the
present time. We feel very much inter-
ested in the storing of grain, believing the
time will come when we will need it, as we
have so often been told by Pres. Young and 
others. May God bless the interests of 
Zion and all the faithful.

	As ever your Sister,
			LYDIA A. JOHNSON,
					President R. S.
