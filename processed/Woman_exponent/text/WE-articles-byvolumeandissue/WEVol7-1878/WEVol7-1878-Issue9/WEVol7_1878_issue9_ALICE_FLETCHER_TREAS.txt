ALICE FLETCHER, Treas.

DIED, in the First Ward, S. L. City, July 3d,
1878, of inflammation of the bowels, Miriam
C. Hawkins, wife of Creighton Hawkins, and
daughter of the late Sisson A. Chase and of
of Miriam G. Chase.
Deceased was born March 3rd, 1849, in Potta-
watamie Co., Iowa; came to Utah in 1853.  She
was a faithful Latter-day Saint, and a Coun-
selor in the Y. L. M. I. A. of the First Ward,
who deeply reget her loss, and who feel that
she was in every way worthy of their respect
and esteem.  She leaves a husband and four
children with whom we all feel to sympathize
for the loss of so good a wife and mother.
“Thine own will miss thee as they onward tread,
In the rough path where thy dear feet hath led;
Friendship will miss thee, but another tie
Is formed to guide until we meet on high."

 DIED, at Mona, Juab Co., Aug. 11th, 1878, of
typhoid disentery, Samuel, the son of Mr.
and Mrs. Ostler, aged 5 months and 5 days.
 This dear one, our first-born, was called early
to the arms of Jesus, who gathers flow-
ers from every garden.  Jesus says, “Suffer lit-
tle children to come unto me, for of such is the
kingdom of heaven.” Children are an herit-
age from the Lord."
