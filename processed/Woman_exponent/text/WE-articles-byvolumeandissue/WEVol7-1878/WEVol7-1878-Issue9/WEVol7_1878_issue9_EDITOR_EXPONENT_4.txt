EDITOR EXPONENT:

	We were organized into a Relief Society
Aug. 9th, 1877, by Bishop Ishmael Phillips
and Counselor Marion H. Brady, other
brethren being present. After the opening
exercises, singing and prayer, the follow-
ing officers were unanimously sustained:

	Elizabeth Richards, President,
	Lucy Brady,		} Counselors,
	Ann Griffiths,	}
	Mary Ann Smart, Secretary,
	Sarah Ann Griffiths, Assistant Secretary,
	Sarah Morrel, Treasurer.
	Since then our Treasurer has moved
south, and her office is filled by her sister,
Mrs. Lucy Brady, Acting Treasurer, with a
corps of teachers and the names of thirty-
two members enrolled. We hold our meet-
ings semi-monthly. Our Annual Report is
as follows:


Donations received..................$171.94
Disbursed, to Temple............... 37 80
To the Poor............................... 45 48
For Sacramental Service.......... 4 90
For Stake Book, &c.................. 2 75
3000 Shingles........................... 9 75
Cedar Blocks............................. 3 50
Calico......... .............................. 2 00
For sundry purposes................. 11 86
Remaining in Treasury, Cash,.... 34 15
Flour and Sundries.................... 9 75
The 24th of July was celebrated here by
the brethren turning out in a lively and
interesting manner, to harvest the seven
acres of wheat belonging to the Society,
the land being lent  us by Br. Brady.  While
the brethren were harvesting, the sisters
were none the less busy preparing a sump-
tuous dinner, which all were invited to
partake of freely.  After which the Sun-
day School children marched through the
settlement, headed by the string band,
banners, &c. Songs, recitations, speeches
and dancing cloes our memorable day, all
feeling well, and grateful for the blessings
and bounties that an all-wise God had
blessed us with.
We expect to build a granary this fall.
May the Lord bless all that labor with so
much zeal for the cause of truth and dis-
eminating the principles of our most holy
religion.  Your sisters,
	ELIZABETH RICHARDS, President,
	MARY ANN SMART, Secretary.
