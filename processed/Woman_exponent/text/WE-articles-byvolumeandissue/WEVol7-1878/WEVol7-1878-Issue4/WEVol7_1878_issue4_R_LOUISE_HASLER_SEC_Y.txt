	HOYTSVILLE, June 24th, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We the sisters of Hoytsville would like
to represent ourselves in the WOMAN'S EX-
PONENT. We were reorganized December
6th, 1877, by President W. W. Cluff, and
Bishop Alonzo Winters. The following
named sisters were sustained as officers:
	Parthenia A. Holt, President,
	Sarah Mills,		Counselors.
	Ann Mills,
	Mary E. Holt, Secretary,
	Elizabeth Crittenden, Treasurer.
	Since that time Sister Ann Mills has re-
signed her position, and Sister Edith Hob-

[Column 2]

son has been appointed to fill the vacancy.
We have enrolled as members,twenty-three
names; we meet together once in two
weeks, and our meetings are very well at-
tended, considering the scattered condition
of the sisters. We have under cultiva-
tion four acres of wheat, and have fifty-five
dollars and eighty-five cents in the tannery.
We are striving to become united in doing
all the good we can towards building up
Zion.
	Wishing your valuable paper prosperity,
&c.,
	MARY E. HOYT, Secretary.
