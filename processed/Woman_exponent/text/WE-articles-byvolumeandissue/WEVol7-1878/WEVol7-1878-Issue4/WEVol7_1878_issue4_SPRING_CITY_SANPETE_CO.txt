	SPRING CITY, SANPETE CO,
		May, 19, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We held our Annnal Meeting May 2d,
and herewith send you our report:
Distributed to the sick and needy..$122. 22
Wheat stored..........................246 bu., 18 lb.
For the Temple...............................$ 95. 65
Cash on hand..................................   52. 90
Sunday articles...............................   11. 32
Share in Co-op Store....................   210. 00
	The health of the people in general is
quite good; and all the sisters show a lively
interest in the Society and the upbuilding
of the kingdom of God.
	We deeply sympathize with you and the
bereaved family of Counselor Wells, and
pray God to bless and comfort you all in
the loss of so amiable a daughter and sis-
ter, and hope the young girls who read the
representation of her character in the EX-
PONENT, will strive to follow the example
she has set before them, and assist as she
has done to build up the kingdom of God,
that when they are called to go they may
leave a like name.
	P. E. BENSON, Secretary.

	FRANKLIN, June 1st, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We give a brief report from our Young
Ladies' Association. We were organized
under the immediate direction of Sisters
Zina D. Young and E. Davis, Oct. 30, 1877.
The following officers were then unanimous-
ly elected:
	Mary A. Hawks, President,
	Clara Hatch,
	Sarah A. Smart,
	Sarah Thomas,		Counselors.
	Agnes Durrant,
	Mary J. Doney,
	Betsy Low,
	Sarah A. Smart, Secretary,
	Ruth Hatch, Assistant Secretary,
	Annie Hatch, Treasurer.
	Our meetings have been well attended
during the winter; the young sisters have
manifested a great desire to be benefitted
by attending the same. We are desirous
of laboring in the cause of truth, and we
know of no better source of developing
those talents which God has given us, than
by striving strictly to attend to those du-
ties which are enjoined upon us in these
institutions which have been framed for
our mutual benefit.
	We were highly favored with a visit
from Sisters Eliza R. Snow and E. Davis
on the 10th of May last. A meeting was
held in the afternoon for the Relief Society;
Sister E. R. Snow addressed the meeting
at considerable length, showing the sisters
their duties and privileges, and encourag-
ing them to faithfulness. In the evening
there was a meeting for the Y. L. M. I. A.,
and many others, old and young, assembled

[Column 3]

with them. We had an interesting time
listening to our honored elder sisters, E.
R. Snow and E. Davis, who gave us such
counsel and advice through the Spirit of
the Lord, that we need not go astray from
the truth. Owing to the sterility of the
climate we have not as yet raised any mul-
berry trees, therefore we have not turned
our attention to sericulture, but are en-
deavoring in our weak way to encourage
all home industries, and be co-workers in
doing all the good we can, in connection
with our sisters elsewhere. Wishing your
very valuable paper success, we remain, &c.,
	MISS MARY A. HAWKS, Pres't,
