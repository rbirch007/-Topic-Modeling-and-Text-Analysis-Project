EDITOR EXPONENT.

	Feeling interested in the welfare of Zion,
and also in the young and rising generation,
I offer a few of my thoughts on the subject
of training children; having a family of
children of my own and desiring that they
may become useful men and women in the
kingdom of God, and thinking it is every
mother's wish for their sons and daughters
to become useful in the kingdom, I have re-
flected much on the best course to pursue.
Sisters let us teach our children to be watch-
ful and prayerful and truthful, and keep the 
company of good Latter-day Saints; let us
as parents set them good examples, such as
will be worthy of imitation, in things both 
temporal and spiritual. Although our tri-
als may be great let us try and overcome so
that we may gain the prize in store for the
faithful. I know for one that the adver-
sary is over on the alert to tempt us to do
wrong and lead us in by and forbidden
paths. How happy had we ought to feel
to think we have wise men to counsel us
who hold the Holy Priesthood. Let us
obey them and put in practice what we are
taught; let us teach our daughters to dress
modestly and as becomes Latter-day Saints,
and not allow ourselves to talk against any
of the principles of the Goepel, for we know 
that they are pure and holy, and calculated

to save us in the Celestial kingdom of
heaven.						J. G.
