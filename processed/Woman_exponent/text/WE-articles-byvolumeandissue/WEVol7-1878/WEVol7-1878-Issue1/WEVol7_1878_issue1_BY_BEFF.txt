BY BEFF.

		Ah, my noble, native mountains,
		Still the music of thy fountains
[-]urmurs sweetly in my ears though, I am far from thee.
		And thy torrents dashing, gleaming,
		As they down thy sides are streaming,
		In the sunshine brightly beaming,
Seem a song of invitation to be singing unto me.

		It may be a supersition,
		But before my mental vision,
Scenes of home, my mountain home, so oft appear to rise,
		Scenes that to my heart are dearest,
		And in imagination nearest
		To the Vale, where shines the clearest
Sight that beams on happy spirits, praising God in Para-
	dise.

		Yes, to me it seems like heaven,
		Does my mountain home at even,
When the sun in his effulgence sinks behind the Western
	hills.
		Or when Luna sheds her silver,
		Over valley, lake and river,
		And the leaflets gently quiver,
In the breeze so softly laden with the song of distant rills

		Noble mountains! 'neath thy shadows,
		Falling over farms and meadows,
Live a people, of all people, blessed, rich, and most con-
	tent.
		While woes follow those who drove them,
		God and good men all will love them,
		O the sun that shines above them,
Sees no people whom the Father greater blessings ever
	sent.

		Yes, I hasten to thee gladly,
		For my heart yearns for thee sadly,
And I long to flee from Bab'lon and return to thee, sweet
	home. 
		Grand old mountains! piles stupendous!
		Thou seem'st to stand there to defend us
		From the judgments so tremendous,
And the desolating scourges, that upon the earth must
	come.

		And when war spreads consternation
		Through this land, and desolation
Stalks abroad, with form so gaunt, throughout the na-
	tions of the earth,
		Then thy peace shall princes envy,
		And thy valleys teem with plenty,
		For thy people, who will then be
In enjoyment of that peace, of which the world knows
	not the worth.

Council Bluffs, May 18, 1878.
