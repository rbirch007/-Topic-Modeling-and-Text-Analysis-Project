SERICULTURE.

	ROCKVILLE, KANE Co., April 20th, '78.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I write to inform you of the progress of
silk culture in this place. When the coun-
sel of President Young was given for the
Relief Society to assume the responsibility
of raising silk, the society which had been
formed was merged into the Relief Society,
and their first efforts were directed to build-
ing a house which was accomplished by
subscription in time to use last year, but in
consequence of the cold damp Weather,
very few worms hatched, but those that did
were raised with hardly an exception.  W[-]
have plenty of eggs, having raised five doz-
en. They are just commencing to hatch
and if they do well we shall have some
worms to spare besides some silk we raised
last year.
	The society owns a lot and have planted
10,000 mulberry cuttings and a quart of seed
in it, (the same lot on which the silk
house is situated), they are doing well at
present.
		Respectfully Yours,
					BERTHA OLSEN,
	Superintendent Rockville Silk Society.
