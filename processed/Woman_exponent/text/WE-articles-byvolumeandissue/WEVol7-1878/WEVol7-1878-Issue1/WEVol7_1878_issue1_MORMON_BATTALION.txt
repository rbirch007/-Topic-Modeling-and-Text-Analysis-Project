MORMON BATTALION.

	DEAR SISTERS: Out of about [-]wenty of your
number who were engaged in that arduous
campaign of 1846-1847, I have fourteen or fif-
teen names. I have thought best in the nar-
rative I am writing to "let woman's voice be
heard," and to insert the names of those who
accompanied the command. I tnink it a sim-
ple act of justice on my part. If you will at
your earliest convenience, give me a b[-]ief nar-
rative of your trials and privations as well as
other interesting incidents of travel, I will give
you all the space that the nature of the work
will allow. This applies to all who went with
the army and those who remained behind and 
were bereft of husband or others on whom
they depended for support or comfort.
	The Church Historian has very kindly al-
lowed me to have copi[-]s from his office of all
that will be needed for general information.
Several of the brethren have also allowed me
the perusal of their journals, besides private
individuals have contributed items of public
interest, and I have my own knowledge of
facts worthy of note.
	I trust when I say that the work bids fair to 
be a valuable link in the great chain of the 
history of the Church of Jesus Christ of Lat-
ter-day Saints, I shall not be considered egotis-
tical. Such are my honest convictions. What


will add to the importance of the narrative is
the fact that nothing will be published ex-
cept what is fairly authenticated.
	Your brother and fellow laborer in Zion's
cause.
	Beaver, Beaver Co., May 20, '78.
