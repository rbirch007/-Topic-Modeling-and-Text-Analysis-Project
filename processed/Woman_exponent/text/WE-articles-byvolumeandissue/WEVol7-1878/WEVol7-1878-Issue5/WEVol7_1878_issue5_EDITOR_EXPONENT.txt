EDITOR EXPONENT:

	I expect you have never heard of Hold-
en Relief Society, or if you have it has
not been through any proper channel. I
thought it would be a good plan to try and
represent ourselves in a small degree. I
hope you may find some excuse for our
long silence, for I have none to make, but I
feel to begin now; the old adage "better
late than never" will apply to us very well.
	Our Society has not been dead, although
not represented; we have done considerable
in our way, such as relieving the poor,
when and where it has come to our knowl-
edge that such relief was needed, and also
in helping to build Temples and ga	ther the
poor from the nations as far as we have had
means to do so. The sisters all seem to
feel well, and almost without exception de-
sire to do their duty. We feel that the
Lord has blessed and prospered us in our
undertakings. We have a house in pro-
gress of erection, that will be an honor to
our Society when completed. We also
have a small Society Store in operation; it
has been running a little over one year; it
was started under Bro. Callister's counsel,
and so far it has proved a success. We are
also trying to obey President Young's coun-
sel to lay up wheat, but as this is not a
grain raising district we have our wheat to
buy, consequently the work goes on slowly,
yet we feel determined to do all we can in
this direction.
	We hold our working meetings twice a
month and our Sunday meetings once a
month. They are generally well attended,
and a good spirit prevails among us. Our
President and her Counselors are active and
energetic in the performance of their duty.	Hoping that we may be more punctual in
the future in reporting ourselves and in do-
ing all the duties that are required of us,
we pray that the blessing of God may rest
upon all the Relief Societies in these moun-
tains. We remain your sisters in the Gospel
of peace.
		FANNY N. JOHNSON, Counselor,
		JOANNA C. TEEPLES, Treasurer.
