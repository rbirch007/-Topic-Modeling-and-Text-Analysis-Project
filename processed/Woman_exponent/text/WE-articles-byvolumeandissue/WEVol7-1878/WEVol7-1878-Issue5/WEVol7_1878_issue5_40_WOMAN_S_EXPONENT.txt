40		WOMAN'S EXPONENT.


THE WOMEN of New Orleans held a pub-
lic meeting in the Spring to devise methods
of disposing of stock for the contemplated
Southern Across-the-Continent Railroad,
which in the hands of the men seemed
likely to fall through. The Women of
Virginia contemplate a greater step. They
have formed a society for the purpose of
paying the State debt; have elected officers,
appointed committees, and hope to get
every woman of the State engaged.
	This is the usual way; men get into
trouble, then the women must help them
out. But we object to following this plan
any longer. It is after the fashion of wo-
men paying church debts, only on a larger
scale. The men have danced, now let them
pay the fiddler.—Ex.
