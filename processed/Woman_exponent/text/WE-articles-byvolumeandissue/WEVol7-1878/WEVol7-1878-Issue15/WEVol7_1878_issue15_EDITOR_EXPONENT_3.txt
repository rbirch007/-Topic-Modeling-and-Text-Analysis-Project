EDITOR EXPONENT.
	Our Relief Society is in a flourishing condi-
tion; we have assisted in the emigration to the
amount of $57.19, have made a number of
quilts, and are also saving grain. Our Bishop
and Counselors visit our meetings and give us
good counsel. We are striving to live our re-
ligion and do what is required of us both in
word and deed.
	We are proud of our paper (the WOMAN'S
EXPONENT), its pages are fraught with wisdom,
and its influence is being felt abroad. Our de-
sires are to be co-workers with our sisters in as-
sisting in all good works.
	MARY ANN P. SCHOW. Pres't.
	JANE S. COLEMAN, Sec'y.
Escelenta.
