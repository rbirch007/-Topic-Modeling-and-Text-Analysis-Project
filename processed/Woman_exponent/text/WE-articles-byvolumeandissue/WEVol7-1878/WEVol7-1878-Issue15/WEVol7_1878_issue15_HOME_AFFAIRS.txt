HOME AFFAIRS.

	THE FIRST An[-]ual Conference of the Relief
Society of Salt Lake Stake, Mrs. M. I. Horne
presiding, was held Friday and Saturday, Dec.
20 and 21. The Young Ladies' M. I. A. of
Salt Lake Co. having been recently organized
in a Stake capacity handed in their first re-
ports from date of each organization, making
the Conference a conjoint one of Relief Socie-
ties and Young Ladies' Mutual Improvement
Associations. There were as many persons
present as could be seated comfortably. Elder
Joseph F. Smith and Pres. A. M. Cannon at-
tended Friday morning session to hear the re-
ports from Relief Societies and encourage the
sisters in their labors. They each made most


excellent remarks, of which we shall give a
synopsis in the minutes which will be pub-
lished in the next issue of EXPONENT. Friday
afternoon was devoted mostly to verbal reports
from the presidents of the various branches of
Relief Society in Salt Lake County. There
were present on the stand Miss E. R. Snow,
Mrs. Zina D. Young and many other ladies
who are devoting themselves almost exclusive-
ly to public duties in the interest of the Latter-
day Saints. Mrs. E. Howard, Secretary for the
County organization, read the reports in the
morning, Mrs. E. B. Wells in the afternoon.
We will publish in full the statistical and
financial report when it has been carefully
made out.
	Saturday morning Mrs. Mary A. Freeze pre-
sided over the meeting. Mrs. Augusta J.
Crocheron, Secretary, read the reports from va-
rious parts of the county. Saturday afternoon
presidents of Y. L. M. I. A. and Primary Asso-
ciations made verbal reports. Miss E. R. 
Snow and Elder Joseph E. Taylor occupied the
remainder of the time. The Conference ad-
journed for three months to the first Quarterly
meeting of the second year.
