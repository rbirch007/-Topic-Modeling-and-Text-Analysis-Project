EDITOR EXPONENT:

	The ladies of Grass Valley wish to repre-
sent themselves as a Relief Society, we
were organized August 1st, 1878. by Bishop
Joe. H. Wright and Counselors, the follow-
ing ladies were chosen and set apart:
	Margrethe Anderson, President'
	Eliza Rust, 
	Sabra Hatch        Counselors,

Secretary and Treasurere were also elected.
The ladies seem to take a great interest in
the work of the Society and being awake to
their duty; we are all new beginners and
need all the instruction we can get realiz-
ing that your valuable paper is what we
need to keep up with the times. We re-
spectfully ask you to send to us "WOMANS
EXPONENT" one club that we may glean
instruction from that paper, we also hope
that some of our sisters from headquarters
will be kind enough to come and visit us,
we live a little to one side of the world as it
were, but that does not hinder us from en-
joying the spirit of God; we have seventy
four members enrolled: we hold meetings
semi monthly, and they are well attended,
and we feel that the spirit of God is with
us.
	Wishing continued success to the EXPO-
NENT, We Remain,
		Margrethe Anderson, President,
		Mary Jenson, Secretary.
