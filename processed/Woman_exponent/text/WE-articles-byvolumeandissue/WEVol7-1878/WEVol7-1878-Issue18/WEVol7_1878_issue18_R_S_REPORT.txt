R. S. REPORT.

	Quarterly Conference of the Relief Socie-
ties of the Juab County Stake, held Jan.
20th, 1879. Members of the Board all
present, also Patriarch J. G. Bigler, Bishop
Andrews and Elder Kendall. Meeting
opened by singing and prayer. Minutes of
last Conference read and accepted, also Fi[-]
nancial reports. The officers of the Stake
were presented and unanimously sustained,
also the local officers of Nephi, Mona and
Levan.
	Pres. Amelia Goldsbrough said: "I am
thankful for the opportunity of meeting
with you and am pleased to see so many of
my sisters here; I am also pleased that the
brethren have favored us with a visit. I
with my sisters am trying to the best of my
ability to promote the interest of the Soci-
ety. The sisters are doing a great work
feeding the poor, comforting and waiting
on the sick, clothing the living and the
dead, and I feel to bless them. I wish my
sisters who understand silk culture would
speak on that subject." Solicited the aid
of the brethren in building a granary to
store grain in. "I pray God that I may be
counted worthy to enjoy a celestial glory;
it was for that object that I came to this
land and am trying to work out my salva-
tion in connection with you all, which may
God grant for Christ's sake. Amen."
	Mrs. Eliza Sommerville, Pres. of Mona
Society, said: "I am pleased to meet with
you, and gladly embrace the opportunity of
representing the Mona Society. Since the
new organization of the Society by Pres.
Teasdale we are doing well; our meetings
are well attended and a good spirit pre-
vails."
	Bishop Andrews spoke very encouraging-
ly, and said, he would do all in his power to
aid in building the granary. Bros. Bigler,
Kendall and Allen gave some excellent in-
structions.
	Singing. Patriarch J. G. Bigler blessed
the Society, by virtue of his office, and dis-
missed the meeting.
		A. GOLDSBROUGH, Pres.
