TRIBUTE TO SISTER AMANDA SMITH.

Dear Sister Amanda, we meet here to-day,
With hearts full of love, a small tribute to pay
To your worth and nobility, knowing full well
That the whole of the truth only angels could tell.

		*		*		*		*		*		*

You have honored God's laws 'mid oppression and strife,
When mobs in their fury were threat'ning your life,
When husband and loved ones were slain by your side,—
You bore all in meekness, for God was your guide.

		*		*		*		*		*		*

Repeatedly driven from the homes they possessed,
You have willingly shar'd with the sorely oppressed'
Rejoicing that you were deemed worthy to go;
You said "They're my people and my faith I will show.

"I'll follow them even 'mid deserts to roam;
Where'er their abiding place there is my home."
And bravely you traveled o'er mountain and plain.
Determined through all things the right to maintain.

Then yon struggled and toiled to create a new home
Where the wild beast and savage were erst wont to roam,
Rejoicing the while that the Saints were made free
And could worship the Lord in such sweet liberty.

A blessing you've been to the daughters of light,
For your influence ever 'has been for the right;
With words of encouragement cheered many hearts,
The memory of which will never depart.

Your children you've trained in the ways of the Lord,
By example and precept you've taught them His word;
Now they're beacons in Zion, an honor to you,
Beloved by the good for integrity true.

And multiplied thousands will yet speak your praise,
For your trust and obedience to God and His ways;
Like Jesus, your motto has been and is still,
"Not my will be done, but O Father Thy will.

And now we all pray that the blessings of heaven
May rest down upon you "from morn until even,"
May your life be prolonged 'til your work is all through,
Then you be crowned queen with the righteous and true.

				With Love Sincere,
