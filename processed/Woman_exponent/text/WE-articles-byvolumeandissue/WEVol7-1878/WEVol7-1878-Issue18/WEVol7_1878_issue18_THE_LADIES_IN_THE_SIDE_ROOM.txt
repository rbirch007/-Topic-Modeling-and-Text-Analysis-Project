THE LADIES IN THE SIDE ROOM.

	It is true that the papers inform us that
after the material elements were disposed
of a few ladies were admitted into a side
room, where they remained throughout the
evening, being regarded, no doubt, by the
gentlemen present, as they sat there with
their elegant dresses and fair faces, as an
agreeable ornament of the occasion, like the

[Column 2]
flowers and the banners. How kind and
generous it was, by the way, to permit these
ladies to come "after the dinner was all
over." Doubtless, those wives and daugh-
ters of the men present eat a simple meal
at home, while their husbands and fathers
were gorging themselves with dainties,
and then meekly accepted not even the
crumbs that were cast from the table, not
even one glass of wine, but were permitted
to sit, hungry and silent, while the men
vaunted themselves and their ancestry.
