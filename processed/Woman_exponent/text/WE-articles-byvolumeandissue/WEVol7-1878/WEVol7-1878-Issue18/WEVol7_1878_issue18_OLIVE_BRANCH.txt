						OLIVE BRANCH.


BAYARD TAYLOR, born of Quaker parent-
age, in Chester Co., Pennsylvania, January,
11, 1825, died at Berlin, Germany, Decem-
ber 19, 1878--printer, poet, traveler, jour-
nalist, lecturer, novelist, diplomat--man!
In the last character he was greatest of all;
but Bayard Taylor was essentially a poet,
and had he devoted his tremendous ener-
gies solely to the development of his mar-
velous gifts in that direction, would have
been the greatest American poet of the
century. - Ex.
	MR. CUTLER has introduced a bill in the
House of Representatives to repeal section
4708 of the Revised Statutes, and give pen-
sions to widows of soldiers who are re-mar-
ried, or may re-marry.  It also provides
that pensions paid to dependant mothers or
sisters shall continue to be paid whether
they are married or not.  Mr. Cutler wisely
thinks--and thousands of others think with
him--that the pension acts which provide
that pensions shall cease upon the re-mar-
riage of the pensioner are contrary to pub-
lic policy.  The records of the courts in
every great city show it to be true.  We
are glad Mr. Cutler has introduced this bill,
and hope it will pass.--EX.
