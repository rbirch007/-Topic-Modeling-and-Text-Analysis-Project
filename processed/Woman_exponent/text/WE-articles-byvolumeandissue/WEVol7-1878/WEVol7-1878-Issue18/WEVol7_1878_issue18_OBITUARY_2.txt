OBITUARY

	At Marriottsville, Weber County, U. T. Feb.
4th, 1879. of Typhoid fever. Annie daughter
of Simon F. and Turbor Halvorson aged 17
years. Sister Annie was a girl of amiable dis-
position, possessing excellent traits of charac-
ter, she was a teacher in our Sunday School,
and an efficient member of the Y. L. M. I. A.
She was above reproach, and was respected by
all. The funeral services were conducted by
Bishop James Richie and Counselors at the
residence of her parents in a very impressive
manner, after which almost the entire ward
followed her rem[-]ins to their last resting
place, in Ogden City Cemet[-]ry. Her death
has cast a gloom [-]ver our neighborhood. May
she rest in peace.—Com.

	Died at Marriottsville, Weber County, U. T.
19th of Jan. 1879. Mrs. Martha Wayman aged


89 years 7 months and 11 days. Deceased was
born June 8th, 1789 at Fedstanton Hunting-
don shire England. She was baptized in the
year 1850, and from that time her house was
ever open to the Elders of our faith, feeding
and lodging them, and assisting to the utmost
of her ability to advance the cause of truth.
She came to Utah in 1868, and has finished her
earthly career in good faith, and buoyant hopes
of a glorious resurrection. Her remains are en-
tered in the Ogden City Cemetery.

	DIED, at her residence South Cottonwood,
of consumption, after a lingering illness, Mary
Jane, wife of Robert Maxfield; and daughter
of Jane Spencer and Daniel S. Cahoon. De-
ceased was born in Nauvoo, Ill., on the 8th da[-]e
of February A. D. 1845, and died on the 17th
of February, 1879, leaving a husband, two chil-
dren and numerous relatives and friends to
mourn her loss. She was a member of the Re-
lief Society, a faithful wife, an affectionate
mother, and a true saint. In the family circle
her example was worthy of imitation, and her
name will ever be held in honorable remem-
brance by all who knew her.
