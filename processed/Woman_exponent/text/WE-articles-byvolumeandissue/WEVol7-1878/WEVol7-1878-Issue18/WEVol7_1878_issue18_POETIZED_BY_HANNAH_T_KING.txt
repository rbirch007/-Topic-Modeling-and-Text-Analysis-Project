 POETIZED BY HANNAH T. KING.

	Oh! bless thee, bless thee, mother dear,
In love's endearing name
I bless thee, love thee, and revere
Through all--unchanged, the same

	Mother, the mighty waters roll
		Between thy son and thee,
	But still he owns thy sweet control,
		And still thy form can see.

	Supreme of women, thou dost reign
		Within his f[--]al heart-
	And all his joys and sorrs fain
		Would to thine ear impart.

	Yes, think not, O my mother dear,
		I have forgotten thee,
	No! Memory must vacate her sphere,
		And love no longer be,

	Ere I forget thee, mother dear,
		Thou peeress of my heart!
	The thought of thee can wake the tear,
		And sweetest joys impart;

	The thought of thee can win me back
		To Virtue's own bright road,
	If e'er I wander from her track,
		Or from her pure abode.

	Oh! may this sweet, magnetic chain
		Through life extended be;
	It soothes my soul in every pain,
		And holds me true to thee!

	And next to God my mother stands
		Before my mental eye;
	When I obey her sweet commands
		I feel that God is night!

	Then think of me and pray for me,
		By day, by night-in joy,
	In sorrow-I implore of thee
		Recall thine absent boy!
Salt Lake City, Jan. 9, 1879.
