	NOTICE TO SECRETARIES.—The Relief Socie-
ty is increasing in importance and usefulness
in the work of the Latter days, and for this
reason it should be the ambition of every sec-
retary in every ward in this Stake to prepare
and furnish to the Relief Society Secretary of
of the Stake, proper and business-like reports
invariably four days before the Quarterly Con-
ference, so as to enable her to prepare and cor-
rect her report to present before the general
Quarterly Conference. It seems impossible to 
do so when several of the wards are not heard
from, which has been the case heretofore.
	It is very important that the Secretaries at-
tend to this responsible duty. They will please
send their reports for the last quarter address-
ed to the Relief Society Secretary for the
Stake, in care of Mrs. Wells, Editor of WO-
MAN'S EXPONENT, before the 15th of March.
						E. HOWARD, Sec'y.

MISS ELIZA R. Snow, President of Latter-day
Saint Women's Organizations left Salt Lake
City Wednesday morning, Feb. 5th, to visit
the different organizations of Weber Co. and
assist in organizing Y. L. M. I. A. and Pri-
mary Associations in various places.
	Ogden has recently been divided into four
wards, and Miss Snow, while there, assisted
the Bishops in their respective wards and Mrs.
Jane S. Richards, President of the Relief Socie-
ties in Weber Co. to organize Y. L. M. I. and 
Primary Associations in each of these wards.
Miss Snow and Mrs. Richards afterwards visit-
ed Lynne, Marrlott, Riverdale, Mound's Fort,
Huntsville and West Weber; and assisted the
Bishops in organizing a Primary Association
in each of these respective settlements. At
Riverdale these sisters also assisted the Bishop
in organizing a Y. L. M. I. A. Miss Snow 
was absent over two weeks, and held during
that time, seventeen meetings, at all of which
she occupied considerable time in speaking,
exhorting and giving instruction to the sisters
in their various duties and callings; her visits
are of great benefit, and her age, experience,
and her illustrious example of Christian life
strengthen the force of her advice and have a 
bearing upon all her labors, which 
makes it more effective. Our sisters should
uphold and sustain "Sister Eliza" as she is af-
fectionatly designated, that she may live long
to direct and counsel the mothers in Israel, and
daughters of Zion. On the 21st day of Jan.
she attained to the advanced age of seventy-
five, her life stretching over three quarters of 
a century, still active mentally and physisic-
ally, waits upon herself, keeping no servant,
choosing to do her own work. She has ad-
ministered as Preistess for many years in the
"Lord's House." She travels from place to 
place, as duty calls her to attend the meetings

[Column 2]
and conferences of the sisters in all the various
localities in the Territory, never refusing
when it is po[-]sible for her to go. We feel con-
fident our sisters will join with us in publicly
expressing our gratitude to this dearly beloved
sister, and wishing her many more years of
usefulness and active work as a leader among
the women of the Latter-day Saints.

	ON THURSDAY, Feb. 27, we attended the joint
Conference in Farmington of Young Men's
Mutual Improvent Associations and Young
Ladies' Mutual Improvement Associations
of Davis Co. Stake, in company with Pres.
Eliza R. Snow and Elder Junius F. Wells.
The young men's reports were made in the
morning. Elder Roberts, who presides over
the Y. M. M. I. A. in that Stake, conducted
the meeting; there was not a large attendance.
After singing and prayer reports from the va-
rious branches of the Association were read by
the Secretary. The presidents of the several
branches reported verbally the condition of
their respective Associations, and then Elder
Junius F. Wells, President of Central Com-
mittee of Y. M. M. I. A., occupied the time in
an interesting discourse, giving instruction
and exhorting the young people. Elder Wells
is a very pleasing speaker, has an excellent
voice, a good flow of language, in short he is
winning, attractive and graceful in speaking.
In the afternoon Mrs. Nancy A. Clark, Super-
intendent of the young ladies of Davis Co.,
presided. County report;
