R. S. REPORTS.
	   PROVO CITY, NOV. 21st, 1878.
Minutes of Meeting of the Relief Socie-
ties of Utah Co., held in the meeting House,
Provo City, Sept. 27th, 1878.  Present, the
Presidency of the Stake, Sister Eliza R.
Snow and Zina D. Young, with many of
the officers and members of Relief Socie-
ties of the Country.
	President Abraham O. Smoot announced
the purpose of the call to be an organiza-
tion of the Relief Societies of the County;
therefore the business of the meeting would
be to elect officers of the Stake organization,
consisting of a President, two Counselors,
Recording Secretary and Corresponding
Secretary.  On motion of Pres. Smoot, Sis-
ter E. R. Snow was elected President of
the meeting, and Elder Albert Jones, Sec-
retary.
	After remarks from Sister E. R. Snow
showing the power of organization of the
Sisters to be vested in the Priesthood, as
example, by the action of the Prophet Jo-
seph in the first organization of the Relief
Societies formed by him in Nauvoo, the fol-
lowing organization was effected:
Margaret T. Smoot, President.
Mary John,
Margaret A. Cluff,       Counselors.
Caroline Daniels, Recording Secretary.
Lucy W. Kimball, Corresponding Sec'y.
After the setting apart of the sisters to
their offices by the Presidency of the Stake,
Pres. Smoot briefly addressed the meeting
on the duties devolving upon the members
of the Society, showing that with prompt
and energetic action success would attend
their efforts.
	Pres. Margaret T. Smoot and her counsel-
ors then followed with oppropriate remarks.
Pres. M. T. Smoot announced that the first
Quarterly Conference of the Relief Socie-
ties of Utah Stake of Zion would be held
in the Church, Provo City, Saturday, the
9th of November, 1878.  Meeting then ad-
journed.
	The first Quarterly Conference of the
Relief Societies of Utah Stake of Zion con-
vened on Saturday, Nov. 9, at 10 a. m.,
Pres. M. T. Smoot presiding.  On the stand

of the presiding-Priesthood, Pres. A. O.
Smoot and Counselors, with a number of
the Bishops and other brethren.

the opening prayer.  President Mrs. Smoot

pleased to see so many of the sisters pres-

minutes of former meeting, which were

The organization of the Tooele Co. Stake
Relief Society took place on the 20th of
Sept., 1878; the first Conference was on
Saturday, Feb. 8, 1879; convened at 10
o'clock a. m. President F. M. Lyman called
the meeting to order.  After the usual
opening exercises, reports were read from
the several wards of the Stake, showing a
very fair condition of the different branches.
There were present on the stand, from Salt
Lake City, Mrs. M. L. Horne, Mrs. Zina D.
Young and Mrs. E. S. Taylor; the meeting
was largely attended by sisters from Grants-
ville, E. T. City, Lakeview, St. Johns and 
other places.
	Mrs. Horne gave some instructions to the
Secretaries, and spoke at some length upon
the necessity for progression in woman, and
the value of these organizations in learning
us to use our faculties for the good of our-
selves and others; advised the sisters to lay
a foundation at home in the hearts of their
children for all that is good and honest and
virtuous, seek to possess the Spirit of God
under all circumstances, that we may not
be overcome, for the enemy is always on
the alert.
	Mrs. Jennette Delamare; President of
the Tooele ward, spoke of the unity that
prevailed amongst the sisters, and of the
good advice given by Pres. F. M. Lyman.
Mrs. M. A. Hunter of Grantsville, Mrs.
Anne Steel of Lakeview, and Mrs. Cowley
of E. T. City, each reported her ward.
Mrs. Zina D. Young said the sisters re-
minded her of mothers, who generally
think their own children the best in all the
world. She said we are learning to be or-
derly, so that in time we can go back into
the presence of our Father.  We come
down here and learn by contrast what
heaven is; we have pure spirits committed
to our charge, and if there was ever a time
that we need to be humble and diligent it
is now.  Made some beautiful remarks up-
on our being united and seeking to perfect
ourselves in every good word and work,
and to so train our children that in the
morning of the resurrection we may meet-
them with joy.
 Mrs. E. S. Taylor felt that there is a
marked progress in the young ladies who
attend to their meetings, and that they
will soon be competent to stand side by
side with a companion qualified and fitted
to lead them back into the presence of
God.
	In the afternoon the Young Ladies Asso-
ciation occupied part of the time in reading
reports, after which the ladies expressed
themselves highly pleased with the condi-
tion of affairs, and gave some instructions
for our future observance.
 On Sunday morning the sisters attended
the Sunday School.  In the afternoon, after
singing, prayer and admistering the Sacra-
ment, Pres. Lyman read the memorial that
has been presented to Congress by Mrs. E.
B. Wells and Mrs. Zina Young Williams;
then Counselor James Ure read an account
in the "Herald" of the good work those
ladies had done.  Reports were then read
of the Primary Association, which were
very encouraging.  President Lyman then
nominated a President for the Stake, with
two Counselors, and voted in other officers.
This business being done, Mrs. Zina D. Young
spoke with great power and wisdom upon
many things very interesting to the Saints,
suited to both men and women - things to
be in the present and the benefits to be de-
rived in the future, by which we can in-
crease in wisdom and intelligence, and
draw down the blessings of heaven upon
our heads.
	Mrs. M. I Horne rejoiced in seeing so
many of our brethren meet with us, and
felt that we all have the same object--to
build up the kingdom of God.  She spoke
of home industry, the storing of grain, and
a great many other duties binding upon the
Latter-day Saints.  Spoke of Plural Mar-
riage and the need of our minds being
stored with spiritual food, and exhorted us
to set our mark high, and to labor diligently
to attain to a Celestial exaltation, for we
shall most assuredly have to earn it.
 Mrs. E. S. Taylor advised all the young
folks not to depend on father and mother's
testimony, but not to rest until they knew
for themselves that this work is of God.
Mrs. Elizabeth Clegg said this had been a
feast indeed; hoped the sisters would prac-
tice and carry out in their lives the good
things they had heard.  M[--] M. I. Horne
sang a song of Zion.  Pres. F. M. Lyman
appointed the Second Conference to be held
in Grantsville, on the second Saturday and
Sunday in June, and desired to give God
the praise for the very remarkable good
time we had enjoyed together.
Mrs. Mary A. Hunter, of Grantsville, was
set apart for President of the Relief Society
of the Stake; Mrs. Elizabeth Clegg, of
Tooele, and Mrs. Agnes Rich, of Grants-
ville, Counselors; Mrs. Mary A. House of
Grantsville, Secretary; Mrs. Ann Tate of
Tooele, Corresponding Secretary.
