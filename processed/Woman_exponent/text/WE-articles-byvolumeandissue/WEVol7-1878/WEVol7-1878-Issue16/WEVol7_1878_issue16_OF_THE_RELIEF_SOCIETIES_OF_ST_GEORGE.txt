OF THE RELIEF SOCIETIES OF ST. GEORGE.

	President Minerva W. Snow, Presiding.
After singing, and prayer by M. W. Snow,
Secretaries were appointed and voted in.
Eliza Smith Calkins as Secretary for the
Stake, and Elizabeth P. Bentley as Corres-
ponding Secretary.  Reports from the va-
rious Societies were then read, with a few
exceptions.  From a misunderstanding the 
reports were not as clean as they should
have been, and in consequence a correct re-
port cannot be given; some going back ten
years.  The presidents of St. George socie-
ties gave a verbal report of their wards.
	Pres. M. W. Snow then addressed the
assembly, and gave some good instruc-
tions.  Many excellent remarks were made
by the different sisters, concerning the du-
ties of mothers, sickness, &c.
	We had a good meeting; the Spirit of
the Lord was with us, and we felt assured
that our next Conference would be better,
this being our first attempt.  Singing,
Prayer by E. S. Calkins.
MINERVA SNOW, President,
E. P. BENTLEY, Cor. Secretary.
St. George, Jan. 5, 1879.
