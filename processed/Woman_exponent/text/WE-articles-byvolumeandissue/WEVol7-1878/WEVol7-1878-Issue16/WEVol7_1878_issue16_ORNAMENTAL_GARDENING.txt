	ORNAMENTAL GARDENING.

	At one of the monthly Sunday School
Union meetings, your correspondent was
interested in a proposition made by Coun-
sler Evens, viz. that the Sunday School
Union devise some plan to stimulate in
teachers and scholars the cultivation of
flowers, the speaker dwelt on the various use
and pleasures that a properly systematised
plan of floral culture would afford and
seemed to leave the subject open for fur-
ther suggestions, the subject has so many
important branches that it should not be
lost sight of. The cultivation of flowers by
the young leads to industrial habits to ac-
quaintance with the powers and qualities of
Mother Earth, inspires health, devotion,
refinement and affection. Permit me to
suggest that a floral central committee con-
sisting of gentlemen and ladies be elected by
the Union whose duty it shall be to collect
seeds, roots, shrubs &c. for distribution and
exchange and to collect and disseminate
information on the best methods of orna-
mental gardening. Sub committees can be
extensively appointed by whose coopera-
tion a branch of beautifieing and happifie-
ing, labor can be greatly improved and ex-
tended, the write hopes suggestions will
be presented until some practical action is
taken on the subject.
