	JOHNSON, KANE Co., UTAH,
		Dec. 15, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Our Relief Society was organized Janu-
ary 25th, 1878.
	Jane C. Johnson, President,
	Mary Ann Johnson
	Louisa Shunway, 	Counselors.
	Contradenia A. Johnson, Secretary,
	Ettie A. Johnson, Assistant Secretary.
	We have 28 members enrolled, 18 aver-
age attendants; all seem to be alive to their
duty, and feel an interest in the good cause
in which they have enlisted. We meet
for working meeting every two weeks, and

[Column 2]

have meeting for spiritual improvement
once a month. We have been piecing
quilts, making gloves, knitting socks, also
quilting, and sewing carpet rags. We all
have the spirit of storing wheat and beans;
we have but little on hand at present, but
hope soon to have more. We have in trea-
sury, Sundries, $15; cash, $4.45; wool 10 lbs;
wheat 5 1/2 bu. All feel the importance of a
Relief Society, and with the help of our
Heavenly Father we hope to keep His Spir-
it and prosper in our labors.
	I had the privilege of attending a wo-
man's Conference for the first time; we had
a very pleasant time and all felt well, and
hope to be able to attend many more such.
	In reading of the Woman's Mass Meet-
ing held in the Theatre it made my heart
rejoice to read of the good and wise doings
of our tried sisters, and believe it will have
a great influence with Congress. We know
that God is at the helm and will reign su-
preme.
	Praying the Lord to bless all the sisters
in their good works, I remain as ever
your sister in the Gospel,
