R. S. REPORTS.

	PANGUITCH, Dec. 20th, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	On Wednesday, Nov. 6th, some of the
members of our Relief Society met at
their Hall to enjoy a picnic party, the ob-
ject being a surprise to our President, Mrs.
Myria Henrie, as they had prepared a birth-
day present for her, consisting of a nicely
wrought inscription on card board, which
read as follows: "Nov. 1st, 1878. A birth-
day present from Panguitch Relief Society
to their President, Myria Henrie, born Nov.
1st, 1803," and a suitable verse.
	After its presentation, Sister Henrie arose
and expressed her gratitude to the sisters
for this unexpected token of their respect.
Said her only object in wishing to live was
to do good. Told us she had 77 grandchil-
dren and 16 great-grandchildren.
	Encouraging and appropriate remarks
were then made by Bishop G. W. Sevy and
Elder J. W. Crosby, Jr.
	The party was a success; all seemed
pleased with the entertainments of the oc-
casion.
	On the 17th inst. the Young Ladies' Re-
trenchment Association was re-organized
with the following named officers:
	Alice Dickensen, President,
	M. E. Steele,
	Sarah Marshall, 	Counselors.
	Mamie Heywood, Secretary.
