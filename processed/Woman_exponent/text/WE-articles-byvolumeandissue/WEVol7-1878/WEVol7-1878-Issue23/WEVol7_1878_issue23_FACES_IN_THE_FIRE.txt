		FACES IN THE FIRE.

	Memory's tide is backward pressing,
		As I sit before the fire,
	In an easy chair reclining;
		And the glowing flames rise higher.

	And within the rosy furnace
		Faces come and faces go—
	Friends, companions of my childhood,
		Of the long, long time ago.

	As they quickly come and vanish,
		I remember all full well—
	There is little toddling Alice,
		And our little rosy Nell.

	Both are now above in heaven,
		But their faces in the fire
	Seem to beckon my soul onward
		To a something, nobler, higher.

	Where all mortals who were faithful
		In their earthly mission here,
	Dwell in love and light eternal,
		And no pain or sorrow fear.

	Yes, my babes, for your sweet sakes
		I will bear my cross along,
	And will meet you in "the morning,"
		With a pure and happy song.

Salt Lake City.					ELINOR ELMWOOD.
