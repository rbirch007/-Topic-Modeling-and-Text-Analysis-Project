CARLETON.

	Mrs. Marindia J. Carleton, wife of Mr. Geo.
H. Carleton, cashier of the Georgetown Na-
tional Bank, departed this life March 15, 1879,
at her residence in Georgetown, Mass. The
deceased was the beloved niece of Sister Vienna
Jacques of this city, and Mrs. Boynton of
Bountiful is the sister of Mrs. Carleton. Hav-
ing made her acquaintance, with that of her
worthy husband, on my recent mission to my
native State, Mass., and becoming conversant
with her blameless and holy life, I could not
do justice to my feelings did I not testify in
some public manner to her worth as a consis-
tent and devoted Latter-day Saint. Truly, in
her daily deportment as a wife, daughter, sis-
ter and frien[-], all the virtues and beatitudes
were exemp[--]led and shone pre-eminently.
	Moving in a circle of society that was inimi-
cal to her religious principles, she never faltered
or hesitated to declare her professed faith in
the restoration of the Gospel, when it was ex-
pedient and wise so to do. Nor in words only
did she manifest a vital interest in the great
latter-day work, but in her generosity freely
contributed to the varied interests of the king-
dom of God. To the Temple fund alone she
donated ten dollars monthly, preferring to lay
up treasures in heaven, rather than to expend
the means in undue personal adornment. The
Elders who were laboring in that portion of
the Lord's vineyard always received a hearty
welcome, and never departed without receiv-
ing something substantial to help them in
their mission. She ardently desired to gather
to Zion, and in her last letter to her Aunt Vi-
enna, dated Feb. 22, she expressed herself as
being strongly impressed of the nearness of the
great calamities that await the nation, and
hoped that she would be gathered out of Baby-
lon before the time came that "He that would
not take up his sword to fight against his
neighbor must needs fly to Zion for safety."
Truly has the Lord garnered the pure spirit of
Sister "Rinnie" as a choice jewel to the Zion
above, to mingle with the Saints of all ages
and there enjoy sweet rest until the time shall
arrive when Zion below shall be perfected and
prepared for the coming of the Lord.
	To the bereaved and loving husband, all who
have had the honor of his acquaintance will
sincerely condole in the great loss he has sus-
tained; and may he be soothed by the cher-
ished memory of the many virtues and beauti-
ful life of his deceased companion, and may
the recollection of his untiring devotedness for
her happiness be as a healing balm to assuage
the poignant grief of the present, and endeavor
to look forward to a happy reunion in the fu-
ture, in that peaceful realm where sorrow and
parting shall be known no more, is the sincere
desire of his well-wisher,
						GEORGE HAMLIN.
	Salt Lake City, April 13, 1879
	We have read the tribute dedicated to Mrs
Carleton in the "G[-]orgetown Advocate," and
will here quote one paragraph, attributed to
"a lady who had known her from childhood."
	"She was the b[-]st woman I ever knew. Gen-
tle, quiet, charitable, conscientious, generous
and kind. I would not exchange a single trait
in her character if I could." What a glorious
epitaph!
