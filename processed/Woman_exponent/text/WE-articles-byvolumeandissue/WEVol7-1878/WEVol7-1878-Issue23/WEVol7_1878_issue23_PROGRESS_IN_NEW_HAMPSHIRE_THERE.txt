PROGRESS IN NEW HAMPSHIRE.—There
is light and hope. The world moves. On
Saturday evening last, at a district school
meeting, which district comprises most of
the inhabitants in a town of 4000 persons,
the district elected Miss Mary Gordon, one
of the Prudential Committee. She is a
lady worthy of such a phsition, and a high-
er one. It is a victory for Woman Suffrage.
The town is three to one Republican, as
against Democrats and Greenbackers. Of
course, Republicans, like all in power, are
inimical to Woman Suffrage; but the law is,
and is destined to be supported by the wo-
men. Women are timid at first, but bold and
brave afterwards, and are seldom wanting
in strict integrity and unalloyed sympathy
with right and justice.				H.
	Exeter, March 17, 1879.—Ex.
