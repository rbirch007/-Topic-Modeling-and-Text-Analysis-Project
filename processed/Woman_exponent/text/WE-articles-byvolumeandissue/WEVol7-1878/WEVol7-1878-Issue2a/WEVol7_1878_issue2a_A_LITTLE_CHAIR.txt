A LITTLE CHAIR.
Oh! the children, happy children,
Smiling everywhere I go;
Some as brown as Autumn's berries,
Some as fair as Winter Snow.

Some with eyes as blue as morning,
Some with eyes as dark as night,
Some with faces strangely thoughtful,
Some with faces strangely bright.

And the while I gayly greet them
Back my heart returneth where,
In my room amongst the flowers,
Stands a baby's little chair.

But, alas! the laughing darling,
That in precious by-gone days
Sat there, never more will cheer me,
With his pretty naughty ways.

Cunning feet placed on the table--
When his noontide meal was e'er--
Cup of milk poured on the sugar,
Spoon andlfork dropped on the floor.

Then when "mother's" face grew cloudy
Little arms around me thrown,
Little hands my cheeks caressing,
Sweet lips pressed upon my own.

Oh! the children, happy children--
Yes, I meet them everywhere:
But at home amongst the flowers,
Stands a baby's empty chair.
