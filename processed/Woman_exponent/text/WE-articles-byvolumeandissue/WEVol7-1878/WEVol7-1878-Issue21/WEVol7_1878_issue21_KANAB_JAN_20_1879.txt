					KANAB, Jan. 20, 1879.

	The Annual meeting of the Relief Socie-
ty of Kanab was held in the Schoolhouse,
Dec. 5th, 1878, at 2 p. m., Mrs. Harriet
Bunting presiding. After the usual exer-
cises and reading of minutes the Financial
report was read as follows:

	Mrs. Elizabeth H D. Solomon resigned
her office as Treasurer, on account of mov-
ing to Arizona, and Mrs. Lovina A. Farns-
worth was appointed Treasurer, and Mrs.
Sabina A. Oliphant Assistant Secretary.
	President Bunting spoke upon the neces-
sity of duty and obedience and of listening
to the still small voice, and said the plan of
life and salvation is well defined. Sisters,
let us walk therein!
	Pres. Elizabeth Nuttall exhorted the sis-
ters to duty, and they would have faith
given them to rebuke sickness in the name
of Jesus. Coun. Lucy A. Johnson spoke
upon secret prayer.
Bishop W. D. Johnson, Jun., said we were
placed here to beautify the earth and to do
good. No blessing is oblained without la-
bor. The sisters, through their faithful-
ness, can do a vast amount of good. Let
them seek for the Spirit of the Lord, and
they will have faith to obtain any blessing
they need. There had been fifty cases of
Typhoid fever in Kanab, and only one
death; the Lord had blessed the people
through the administration of the Elders.
Let us be very thankful for these blessings,
and give God the glory.
	After singing and prayer meeting ad-
journed.
	The sisters of Kanab have just com-
menced to store up grain. We have lumber
to make a temporary granary, and hope the
coming season to do much more.
	We appreciate the privilege of living
where we have no contaminating influences
to contend with. We fully endorse the
sentiments and resolutions of our sisters
North, which we have seen published. We
are determined to defend our rights and
uphold the principles of our holy religion,
including Plural Marriage.

				KANAB, March 18, 1879.

	The Quarterly Conference of the Relief
Societies of Kanab Stake of Zion was held
in the Kanab Schoolhouse, March 7, 1879,
Mrs. E. Nuttall presiding. The presidents
of the different wards were present, and
each gave a good report. Various subjects
were spoken of by the sisters, and we had
a very good meeting; the Spirit of the
Lord was in our midst.
	By the counsel of Pres. L. J. Nuttall, Sis-
ter Mary Judd, of this place, planted quite
a number of mulberry trees, and she has at
the present time fifteen ounces of cocoons

[Column 2]
and five ounces of eggs; we intend to do
what we can to start sericulture here.
	Wishing our valuable paper success in
every sense of the word,
		Your sister in the Gospel,
