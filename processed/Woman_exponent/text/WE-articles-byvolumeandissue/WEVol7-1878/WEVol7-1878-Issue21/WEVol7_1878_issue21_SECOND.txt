SECOND:—
Their spirits? Ah no, for the Gospel of heaven
	A promise of glorious destiny gives—
Time's comforing balm and eternity's leaven—
	Which tells thee, sad mourner, thy dear one still lives,

'Tis only the clay that now slumbers beneath us,
	For death o'er the spirit no sceptre can sway;
And both shall unite yet again to bequeath us
	The joy of re-union at some future day.

For the mortal but rests on a motherly pillow
	While its spirit mate shines like a jewel on high,
E'en as glimmering stars in the depths of the billow
	Have their twin fires sparkling aloft in the sky.

To the future then, heart, turn for ne'er ending gladness,
	And music lorn soul for the sweetness of yore,
Where the warm light of hope drys the fountains of sad-
		ness,
	And life is all spring and the spring evermore.
									ANONYMOUS.
	S. L City, Sept. 10, 1878.
