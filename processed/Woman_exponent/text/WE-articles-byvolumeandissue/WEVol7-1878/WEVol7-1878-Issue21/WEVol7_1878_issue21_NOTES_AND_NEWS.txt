NOTES AND NEWS.

	GEORGE ELIOT is in poor health, and will
probably write no more novels.—EX.
	JANE EYRE dramatized has been present-
ed upon the stage in Lowell, Mass. by Gen-
evieve Ward.
	IT IS said Queen Victoria has expressed
a great desire to visit Canada. Report
says her son the Prince of Wales is encour-
aging her to do so and also to visit the
United States.
	IN Philadelphia, March 13, at the annual
commencement of the Woman's Medical
College, the degree of Doctor of Medicine
was conferred on twenty lady graduates
from various States in the Union.—EX.
	PRINCESSE LOUISE MARGARET, of Prussia
was married, March 13th, to Queen Vic-
toria's son Arthur, Duke of Connaught, at
Windsor Castle. The affair was exceeding-
ly brilliant, and the English papers dovote
column after column to the dresses of the
ladies and the elegant paraphernalia of the
whole affair.
	THE bill for Woman Suffrage in Colorado
passed the Senate, and in the House the
vote stood seventeen to tweny-four, and
not fifty-four as the Denver "Tribune" re-
ported. So that there was only a majority
of seven against it. Judge Haynes cham-
pioned the bill and earned the thanks of all
women, as did others who rendered good
service.—EX.
	ANNA METZ Byland's "Women of Pro-
gress" a neat little paper of four pages is
now in its first volumn. We have just
received a copy the first we have ever seen
of it, it is full of interesting reading matter
and certainly deserves patronage. One of
its mottoes is; If the public want firstrate
newspapers they must pay for them liberal-
ly. We should be pleased to have the pa-
per added to our list of exchanges, as we
have no WOMAN'S paper published in the
City of New York.
