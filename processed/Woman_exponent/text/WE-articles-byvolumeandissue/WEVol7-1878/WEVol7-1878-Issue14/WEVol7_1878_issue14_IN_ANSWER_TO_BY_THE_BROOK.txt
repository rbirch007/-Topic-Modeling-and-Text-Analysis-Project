IN ANSWER TO "BY THE BROOK."

'Tis strange that Nature, with gentle seeming,
Should whisper words of noble meaning 
	To mortal minds;
That lightning's flash and thunder's mutter,
Brooks, flowers and breezes should sometimes utter
	Truths most sublime.

I, too, have found a friend, my sister,
A rippling brook, whose quiet whisper
	Oft soothes my soul,
As by its side I sometimes wander,
O'er "knotty points" to think and ponder,
	Past my control.

And thus, one day, as erst perusing
They tender plaint, I fell to musing
	By my brook side.
"Shall wrongs go unredressed in Zion?
Is there no power we may rely on?
	No hand to guide?

"Can man thus crush a woman's power,
And tear from her, her rightful dower—
	To guard her own,
To bless the life she gave another,
And prove the holy love of Mother
	Her right alone?

I had supposed that Woman's mission
'Neath Gospel sway found full fruition,
	And she might feel
That, be he husband, friend, or brother,
Man can not trample on another
	Without appeal.

As thus I mused, a gentle murmur,
As soft and low as breeze of Summer,
	Fell on mine ear;
The sunbeams through the branches glistened,
As silently, enrapt I listened
	These words to hear.

"Some men may err, and others falter,
But God's designs they cannot altar,
	His words prevail.
Though He may deem it wise to chasten,
O soon His loving hands will hasten
	To lift the vail.

"He leads His servants as He willeth,
And through them His designs fulfilleth,
	Then why repine?
For though ye may not know His meanings
Behind the clouds His smiles are beaming,
And soon thy pathway will be streaming
	With light divine.			QUERY.
