Y. L. M. I. A. REPORT.

			ROCKVILLE, Nov. 30th, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We would like to be remembered in your
columns once more. We are situated in a
little, isolated place, but nevertheless we
feel it our duty to strive to become one with
our sisters in adding our mite to build up
the Kingdom of Heaven, and perform our
part in benefitting each other, and do that
which becometh daughters in Zion.
	We are trying to fulfill our mission; and
much praise is due some of our members,
who are the mothers of several small chil-
dren, for their regular and punctual attend-
ance; we think they are weaving for them-
selves a bright future, and "in that day
when the Lord comes to make up his jew-
els" they will be accepted. We enjoy our-
selves in meeting together to talk of the
blessings of our Father in heaven, for a
good spirit prevails and we feel that we are
obeying His commands, and that gives joy
and satisfaction to the faithful. We have
united with the young men in donating for
a library, consisting of about thirty volumes
suited generally to our wants. We hold
our meetings weekly in winter, and during
the summer semi-monthly for working and
semi-monthly for reading and speaking.
We have been afflicted a great deal this
summer with sickness, which has com-
pelled us to postpone our meeting together
for a season, but as the Lord overrules all
things for our good, we hope it will be the
means of waking us up to a sense of our
duties, and learn to live nearer the Lord
and serve Him more diligently. Your
paper is always a most welcome visitor and
is read with increasing interest.
	Praying that the blessing of God may
attend your efforts to publish peace and
good will we remain
		Your sister,
