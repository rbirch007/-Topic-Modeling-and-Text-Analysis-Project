OBITUARY.

	DIED, at her residence, North Ogden, on
Thursday, Nov. 14, at the age of 66 years, Su-
sannah Hurst Maycock. Deceased, while vis-
iting her granddaughter on the Monday pre-
vious, accidentally fell, receiving injuries which
caused her death. She emigrated from Eng-
land in 1849, and came to Utah in 1852. She
was an earnest worker in the Relief Society
and Sunday School, being a faithful teacher in
both from their first organization in this place.
She was truly an able advocate and firm sup-
porter of the principles of the Gospel.
			JULIA A. WADE, Sec'y R. S.
	North Ogden, Hot Spring District.

			HOYTSVILLE, Dec. 1st, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT.
	Miss Emma Gunn died at Hoytsville, Nov-
14, 1878, of Epilepsy, aged 15 years and 6
months. She was a member of the Young
Ladies' Mutual Improvement Association of
Hoytsville. She was a faithful teacher, and
loved and respected by all who knew her.
She was an amiable young lady, full of energy,
and ever willing to do anything that was re-
quired of her. We all (members of the Asso-
ciation) feel to mourn her loss, and to sympa-
thize with the bereaved parents. She died in
full faith of a glorious resurrection.
