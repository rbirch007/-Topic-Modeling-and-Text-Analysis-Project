RELIEF SOCIETY JUAB CO. STAKE

Held at Nephi Meeting House, Oct. 9th,
1878.  All members of the Stake Board
present, also Pres. G. Teasdale, Apostle
Orson Pratt, Patriarch J. G. Bigler, Bishop
John Andrews and Bro. George Kendall.
After singing, prayer and reading of
minutes of the former meeting, Pres.
Amelia Goldsbrough said: “It is with a
thankful heart that I stand here.  I am
proud of the opportunity that I have to
represent the society and the sisters of this
place, and am very much pleased with the 
energy of my sisters.  I wish to speak con-
cerning our little granary.  I wish it could
have been finished in time to receive the
grain donated by the sisters.  I think we
should have had much more grain stored;
solicit the brethren to assist us in this mat-
ter.  I am glad the sisters are alive to their
duties in waiting on the sick, clothing the
dead, etc. I feel to repeat what I said last
Conference to the teachers, to go as com-
forters and make the people feel that their
friends are coming to see them.  I am
happy to say Elder Orson Pratt is here and
will speak to us.  I feel to bear my testi-
mony to the truth of the work we are en-
gaged in, and to do whatever I am called
upon to do.  May God bless you all. Amen.”
 Pres. Serena Aagard, from Levan, said:
“I am pleased to say the members of the 
Relief Society of Levan are alive to their
duties in blessing and comforting the poor.
There is a good spirit prevailing in our
meetings. We are getting material  to-
gether to build a house to meet in, the
brethren are doing all in their power to
help us in this matter.  I pray God to help
us to perform our part in the great work
in which we are engaged.  Amen.
Pres. Goldsbrough asked if there was any
one present to represent Mona, but there
was not.  She then said she was pleased
with the sisters of Mona branch; they are
doing all in their power to promote the in-
terest of the Society.
Elder Orson Pratt said: “I have not been
accustomed, only on very few occasions, to 
speak to Relief Societies, but do not wish
to shrink from any duty that devolves upon
me. I feel glad that the Lord, through
His servents, has seen fit to organize more
extensively the Relief Societies, which
were first organized in Nauvoo by the
Prophet Joseph.  The sisters are not called
to preside over Church Conferences and do
the work the brethren are called to do, but
they can labor in their own sphere and ac-
complish all that the Lord designs them to
do. I have no doubt the Prophet Joseph
was inspired of God to organize these so-
cieties.  God in His wisdom considered
that man could not perform all the duties
that were necessary to be performed alone;
the Lord designed that they should work
together for the mutual good of both. I
have no doubt but the Prophet Joseph saw
that an organization of this kind was nec-
essary in order that the abilities of woman
may not lie dormant.  Woman does not 
perform all her duties by attending to her
household affairs: the Lord designed that
they should have opportunities to do all the
good their hearts desire to do.  There is a
greater object than laying up grain (which
is very commendable both in the brethren
and the sisters to do), but this is a small
thing compared to the greater ones.  When
the Lord intrusted to you the children, sent
from the presence of God, first intrusted to
your care that they may be raised in the

fear of God and be brought up as they
should be, remember they need training
in their infancy!  This duty devolves on
the mother; she has all the responiibili-
ties of their early training, the father is
away attending to other duties.  Sisters, as
your children come to years of accounta-
bility you have the responsibility of instill-
ing the principles of eternal life in their
hearts; teach them the object of their be-
ing; teach them that it is necessary, when
they choose a companion to live with to all
eternity (not merely for time, but for all
eternity), that they should be dictated by
the Spirit of God.  God has decreed from
before the foundation of the world that
eternal union in marriage is necessary.
The men that are in our midst, who will
flatter and try to lead you away, my sisters,
or marry you, what can they offer you even
in marriage?  What can you expect outside
of this kingdom?  Sisters, it is your duty
to instruct the young in the principles of
our holy religion; if they are cared for and
properly trained very few will go astray.
The Apostle Paul said the man is not with-
out the woman in the Lord, neither is the 
woman without the man; we must enter
the Celestial world together; we can not
obtain Celestial glory unless we are joined
together by proper authority.  The Lord
intends there shall be an eternal relation-
ship between husband and wife.  We must
begin our work here and not go into the
spirit world ignorant.  If man alone per-
formed all the duties, how could they be
suitable companions for each other?  He
has permitted woman to be a helpmate to
man, and has continued male and female
upon the earth up to this time, and evi-
dently designed that both should perform
their part.  He calls Apostles to preside,
and Seventies and High Priests, &c., but
this does not relieve woman from her du-
ties.  In our spiritual existence before we
came here, we understood what the privi-
leges of these spirits were; there was quite
a difference between the spirits that came;
we understood that the First-born would
come and be the Redeemer.  We looked
upon our Savior as the only person that
could come and redeem the world. Our
