			FOR MOTHERS.

	"Young ladies of the present day are not
so good as their mothers were when they
were girls."
	This and many other similar remarks are
often made by both men and women.
	Permit me to ask one question--Where
does the fault lie?  True, there are outside
influences which have their effect upon the
minds of the young, but I think that if
mothers started in time to give their daugh-
ters the training that Mormon girls should
have, that the girls of to-day would not
only be as good, but a great deal better than
the girls of any other period.
	What are you Mormon mothers thinking
of when you let day after day pass without
ever offering a word of prayer up to that
God who gave you your children, who you
say are dearer to you than your own life
(excuse me, but I sometimes doubt it), to
thank Him for all His tender mercies, and
to ask His blessings to rest upon you, to
guide you in all your labors?
	How many of you take your little ones
at bed-time, and teach them to call upon
our Heavenly Father to bless them, and to
guide them in the paths of love, truth and
virtue?
	If you do it, may God bless you in your
labors, and He will.  If you do not, can
you with a clear conscience, at any time;
call for the blessings of the Father to rest
upon you and yours?  Can you, when your
little ones are sick unto death, expect God
to spare them, as you term it?  He will in
His mercy take them from you, for they
were choice flowers entrusted to your care.


with the hopes of your leading them in
the paths of truth.
	Mothers, let the cultivation of the minds
of your daughters ever be uppermost in
your thoughts.  Let them be Mormons
through and through.  You will save them
a great deal of sorrow, and reap for your-
selves blessings of untold riches.  May the
blessings of God attend your labors.
Mayfield, July 31, 1878.              N.
