	MILLVILLE, CACHE CO., Aug. 6, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I do not think Millville has ever been
represented in your paper.  Our Relief So-
ciety is in a flourishing condition; we have
lately been re-organized, with Sister L.
Hovey President; she is truly alive to her
duties, and the sisters all seem willing to
assist in doing all the good they can.  We
have not much grain stored, on account of 
grasshoppers last year, but hope to do bet-
ter this year, as the harvest is abundant.
	We meet once a month to bear testimony
to the things of the kingdom, and talk over
our affairs in general; we also have work-
ing meetings once a month, to sew for the 
sick and helpless, and make quilts, etc. for
the poor.  We have a Young Men's and a
Young Ladies' Mutual Improvement Asso-
ciation.  I believe that a great deal of 
good will come of these societies.  We
have just had the pleasure of a visit from
the young Elders, J. F. Wells and M. H.
Hardy, and it was truly a feast to both old
and young.  Hoping we may ever follow
the counsel and instructions of those placed
to preside ovor us, and improve in all good
works, is the desire of your sister in the 
New and Everlasting Covenant,
