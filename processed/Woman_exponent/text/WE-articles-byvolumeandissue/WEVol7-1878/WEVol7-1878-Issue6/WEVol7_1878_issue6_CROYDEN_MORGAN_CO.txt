			CROYDEN, MORGAN CO.,
						July 27, 1878.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The Relief Society of Croyden was re-
organized Oct. 22, 1877, by Bishop John
Hopkins and Counselors. The following
named sisters were then unanimously
elected as officers:

	Helen Thackeray, President,
	Mary Hopkins,	}	Counselors.
	Fanny Swann,	}
	Elizabeth Blackwell, Secretary,
	Hannah London, Treasurer.

	We have eighteen names enrolled as
members; and all manifest a co-working
spirit in this great cause. We assist the
poor in whatever way we can; have do-
nated liberally to the Temple, and are us-
ing our uttermost exertions in storing
wheat.
	The 24th of July was duly observed and
celebrated here. During the day we had
a pleasant and social time, and in the eve
ning a dance, which was designed and man-
aged throughout by the sisters, and proved,
financially, a success. The musicians,
Father John Toone and J. and W. Brown-
ing, donated their services for the occasion.
	Wishing your valuable paper much suc-
cess, &c.
		HELEN THACKERY, Pres't,
