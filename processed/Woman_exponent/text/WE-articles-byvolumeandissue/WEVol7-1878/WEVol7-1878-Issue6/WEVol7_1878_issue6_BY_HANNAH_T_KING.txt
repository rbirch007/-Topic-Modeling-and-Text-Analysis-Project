BY HANNAH T. KING.

The harp within my heart of a thousand strings
Is all unstrung,
Or only just a few vibrating chords
Alone are sung,
For death again has broken up my heart,
And made me mourn,
The sister much beloved has  pass'd away
To that last bourn!
Youth and its sunny hours and friends are gone
Their voices mute,
And only memory's aid can wake again
The heart's sweet lute;
Yet have we friends and oh! they love us well,
And we love them;
But youth's sweet poem, once so proudly sung
None sing again.
And do I pine that I can sing no more.
What once I sang?
That I the past can never more recall?
Gives this a pang?
No! no! the past is past, and I would not recall
Even one day,
But on! on! higher, higher yet
My heart doth say.
Yes, let it go! the stream of life flows on
With noiseless roll,
And day by day, and night by night, we do approach
The final goal.
My much loved sister thou hast reach'd the goal
The dark, dull river
Is crossed, and thy enfranchised soul
Is with the Giver!
The great, grand Giver of the boon of life
My God, and thine;
Free from all sorrow, from all care set free
In realms divine!
Dear innocent, cheerful, gay and loving Mary
Loving guileless Mary,
Angels of highest order close around
My sister Mary.
Suppress the sigh, press back the starting tear
We give thee joy!
Thou dost behold thy Father face to face,
Without alloy.
And in our heart thy cenotaph we place.
S. L. City, July, 1878.
