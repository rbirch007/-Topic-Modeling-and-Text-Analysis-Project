SOCIAL GATHERING.

	The poem published above was sung at
the residence of Elder C. J. Thomas, where
a few of the relatives and friends of Elder
John Nicholson had met in recognition of
their affection and respect for him, as he
intends leaving home shortly on a mission
to Europe. There were present his aged
parents, all his own immediate family, Br.
John Barton and family, Miss E. R. Snow
and officers of the Relief Society of the
18th Ward, and other particular friends.
It was a complete surprise for Br. Nichol-
son. The poem was sung by Mrs. Elizabeth
Thomas, Mrs. Fannie Thatcher playing the
accompaniment on the organ. Br. Nichol-
son expressed himself in a few words of
grateful acknowledgement appropriate to
the time and circumstances. The guests
then sat down to a bountiful repast, which
had been previously arranged, and the
time was spent in agreeable and social con-
versation. The sentiment expressed by
Miss Snow in the chorus will no doubt be
fully verified during the absence of Br.
Nicholson, and we trust he will realize he
has the faith and prayers of the Saints-
and especially those who know him inti,


mately. We say, God bless Elder Nichol-
son and keep him in His holy care, that he
may accomplish great good in disseminat-
ing the principles of truth.
