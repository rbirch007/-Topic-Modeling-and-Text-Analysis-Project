		ELIZABETH BLACKWELL, Sec'y.


CONNECTICUT has at last gained one point
towards “Woman Suffrage.” The women
will be permitted to vote in school meet-
ings, and the men no doubt think they
have made a wonderful concession to allow
women the privilege of electing the men
who regulate affairs for the education of
their children, boys and girls, teachers’
wages, and so on, in a State where a heavy
percentage of the teachers are women with
poor salaries. However, part of a loaf is
better than no bread at all, and a judicious
use of this privilege may pave the way for
future independence for women.
