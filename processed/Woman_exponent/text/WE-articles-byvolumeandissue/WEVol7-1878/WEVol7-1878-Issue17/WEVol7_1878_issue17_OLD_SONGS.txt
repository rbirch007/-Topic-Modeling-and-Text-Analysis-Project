OLD SONGS.
Air, "I'm leaving thee in sorrow, Annie"
Oh! how a simple song can waken
Forgotten old-time scenes,
That come with pathos nearly breaking
The heart that on them leans,
Old songs! old songs!  I cannot hear them,
But stealing to my heart
Come memories once breathing near them,
That will not thence depart.
CHORUS.
Old songs, lie silent in the bygones,
Ye only waken pain;
For joys now past beyond recalling,
Or woes awake again.
Oh! faded hopes, once bright and shining,
Upborne by wings of prayer:
Oh! faded love, with hope entwining,
Pure as the sunlit air,
Ye waken from the long, long sleeping
At the sunset Luriel call;
And worldly eyes again are weeping
Beneath sons music thrail.
