	FILLMORE CITY. Jan. 8, 1879.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We the sisters of the first ward of Fill.
more Relief Society were organized Nov.
14, 1877 by Pres. J. N. Hanckley and Coun-
selors. When the following named sisters
were elected:
	R. M. Pratt President
	Sarah L. Partridge	Counselors.
	Jean Holbrook
	F. M. Brown Secretary.
	Martha Robison Treasurer.
	There are sixty six members and officers
enrolled, we hold our meetings three times
a month, our general meetings are held the
first Thursday in each month.
	FINANCIAL REPORTS.

All seem willing to do all in their power
for the advancement of the work in which
we are engaged, and a spirit of love and
union seems to prevail. We feel it impor-
tant to be up and doing, as we look at the
spirit of the times, and we realize that we
must be humble and faithfull to be able to
stand.
	Very respectfully.
			B. M. Pratt Pres.
		F. M. Brown Sec'y.


The Herald of Health records the fact that
Harriet Hosmer, the eminent sculptress and may-
be inventor, is the only surviving member of a fam-
ily of six or seven children.  She was the young-
est child in a family of strong hereditary consump-
tive tendencies.  When all the other children had
died of this disease the father took his last one,
Harriet from school and encouraged her to en-
gage in vigorous out-door sports.  Thus she was
saved to a life of usefulness; and the same plan
would save many others who die of slow con-
sumption.
