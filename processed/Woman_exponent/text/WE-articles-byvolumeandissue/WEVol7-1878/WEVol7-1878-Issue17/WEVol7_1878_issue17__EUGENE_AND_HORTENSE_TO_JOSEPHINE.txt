"EUGENE AND HORTENSE TO JOSEPHINE."


THE committee of the National Woman's
Suffrage Association, appointed to wait upon
the President were received very cordially by
him. After the memorial had been presented
by the suffrage ladies, the two ladies from Utah
addressed the President, urging him not to
sign the bill now pending in committees of the
House and Senate.
	The entire interview was conducted by the
ladies in a calm manner, and distinguished
especially for the brevity each speaker observed
in presenting an array of facts, with a determi-
nation to occupy as little of the valuable time
of the President as possible. Before they rose
to leave, he asked them if he should bring Mrs.
Hayes in to see them, this was joyfully accept-
ed, and after the President withdrew,
leaving his wife with them, a pleasant talk was
had with her by the Mormon ladies. Mrs.
Hayes, when the ladies rose to go, showed
them all the bedrooms in the House, and had
them conducted through the conservatories.
Our Utah delegates have been before the Judi-
ciary committees of both the House and Senate,
and have presented to them the facts of the
Mormon case in writing.
