R. S. REPORTS.

	Kanab Jan. 6, 1879.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	June 9, 1878.—The Relief Society of the
different wards were organized into a 
Stake Society, under the direction of Pres.
L. John Nuttall, E. Nuttall was appointed
president, H. Bunting and L. A. Johnson
Counselors, M. E. Little Secretary, L. A.
Johnson Assistent Secretary and E. H. D.
Solomon Treasurer. Since then Pres. Nut-
tall and her Counselors have organized
Relief Societies in three other wards Glen-
dale, orderville and Pahreah. The first
Quarterly Conference of the Relief Socities
of the Kanab Stake of Zion, was held in
the Kanab School House Dec. 6, 1878. Pres.
E. Nuttle presided. There were also pres-
ent Pres. H. Bunting of the Kanab ward,
and Pres. J. Johnson of Johnson ward.
There were present by invitation Pres. L.
J. Nuttall and Bishop W. D. Johnson Jun.
The meeting was called to order by Pres.
E. Nuttall. After singing and prayer, the
Secretary read the statistical and financial
reports of the six ward societies in the
stake, the table is as follows.
No. of members enrolled...............258
Average attendance........................123
No. of meetings held......................  72
Total of receipts........................$ 88 69
Disbursments:
To the poor...............................        .25
To Manti Temple.....................$   53 50
For sundries.............................$     8 60
Total disbursments...................$   62 35
We have in the Treasury:
Cash.........................................$  10 00
Wheat, 22 bu...........................$   63 00
Beans, 35 lbs...........................$     1 40
Merchandise............................$   26 90
Sundries..................................$  116 24
Total in the Treasury...............$  217 54
Sister Nuttall said she wished the sisters
to speak as the spirit dictated. Pres. John-
son gave a good report of her ward, and
said the sisters were trying to do all the
good they could. Pres. Bunting made
some timely remarks in reference to home
manufacture, the storing of grain, raising
of beans seri culture &c. She earnestly ex-
horted the sisters to speak well of plural
marriage, for it is a pure and holy principle
and should not be spoken of lightly. The
Glendale ward was represented by Sister
Stuart, the President of the teachers. Pres.
L. J. Nuttall spoke of the great responsi-
bility of mothers in raising their children.
He said the impressions made in childhood
were seldom, if ever erased. We remem-
ber the sayings and doings of our mothers,
when we forget all others. Let the father
help the mother, and the mothers teach
the children to emulate their fathers, in all
good works. He told mothers not to keep
their daughters in ignorance of things they
ought to know, from a sense of mock mod-
esty. He made other excelent remarks, on
the Woman's Mass Meeting in Salt Lake
City, home manufacture, storing of grain
and other matters of interest.
	Bishop W. D. Johnson expressed much
pleasure in meeting with the sisters, there
was such a quiet peaceful spirit in their
meeting. He gave them great credit for
the help they had been to him. He wished
them to treasure up the good counsels they
had received.
	Pres. E. Nuttall said she was thankful
for the good counsel from Brothers Nuttall

[Column 2]

and Johnson, she hoped the sisters would
carry the good spirit home with them, and
so speak of this meeting that it will be an
incentive to the sisters not present, that at
the next conference there may be a full
attendance. Sang "How firm a Founda-
tion." Benediction by Sister Bunting.
	Our Pres. E. Nuttall and her Counselors
H. Bunting and L. A. Johnson are energet-
ic live women, their greatest desire is to
help build up the Kingdom, benefit their
fellow creatures and work out the problem
of life and salvation.
	M. E. Little Stake Sec'y.

	Minutes of a meeting celebrating the
first anniversary of the Y. L. M. I. A. of
the 17th Ward, held at the residence of
Mrs. Elerbeck. Jan. 16th, 1879.
	There were present, as visitors, Sisters
M. Hyde, Z. D. Young, B. Smith, M.
Freaze, H. T. King and others. The meet-
ing was opened by singing "The Spirit of
God like a Fire is Burning;" prayer by Sis-
ter Zina D. Young, followed by singing.
	The Yearly Report being called for, was
read and accepted, after which a short pro-
gramme was carried out, consisting of
piano solos, recitations,essays, songs &c. On
invitation from the Pres. Sisters Hyde,
Zina D. Young; Mary Freeze and others ad-
dressed the association for a short time.
much instruction and excellent advice were
given; the general tone of which urged the
necessity of exercising faith, relying on
God for assistance, however small the un-
dertaking, and as all young ladies look for-
ward to becoming wives, they should pre-
pare themselves for the holy office of
mother-hood, becoming proficient to teach
those principles that lead to the exaltation
and happiness of the human family. The
time being well spent, the meeting was
brought to a close by singing and prayer.
The company repairing to the supper-table.
where a bounteous repast was enjoyed by
all.
	Sedenia Pack Sec'y.
