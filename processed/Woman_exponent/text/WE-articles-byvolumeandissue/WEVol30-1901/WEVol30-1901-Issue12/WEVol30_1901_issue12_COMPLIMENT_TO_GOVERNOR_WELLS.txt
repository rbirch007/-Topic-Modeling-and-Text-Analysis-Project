COMPLIMENT TO GOVERNOR WELLS
	Washington, D. C., March 4, 1902.
Mrs. Emmeline B. Wells:
	MY DEAR MRS. WELLS:—I was so unfor-
tunate as not to see you to bid you good
bye before you left and your little package
of papers which I meant to return is still
here. I will enclose it in this, and I want
to say that the letter of your nephew is the
most concise body of arguments in favor of
Woman Suffrage and against its opponents
that I have ever read. In fact, it leaves
nothing to be said, excepting “Why don’t
you do it then?” I shall long remember
my pleasant days with Utah in Washing-
ton, and hope for a repetition of them on
the home soil.
	With love to any friends whom you may
see, I remain,
			Affectionately yours,
