FOURTEENTH WARD.
	The Fourteenth Ward Relief Society
had a pleasant social gathering of the
members of the ward in honor of the
sixtieth anniversary of the organizotion of
the Relief Society by the Prophet Joseph
Smith, in Nauvoo, March 17, 1842.
	The opening hymn, “Praise to the man
who communed with Jehovah,” was sung
by the congregation. Prayer was offered
by Counselor T. E. Taylor. Singing, “We
thank Thee, O God, for a prophet.” A
brief address of welcome by President M.
W. Wilcox. Solo by Miss Ida Due. A
sketch of the life of Emma Smith, Sister
Caroline Smoot. Recitation by Miss Vida
Fox. “The Seer” was sung by Brother
Thomas Poulter. Interesting remarks
were made upon the life of the Prophet
Joseph by Brother Samuel W. Richards.
Solo by Sister Maggie Hull. Recitation
by Miss Frankie Young. Remarks by
Brother M. E. Taylor. Recitation by
Brother Thomas Hull. Selection by the
choir led by Brother Butler. Benediction,
Brother J. E. Fox.
	Refreshments were then served and a
pleasant social time was spent by all
present.
