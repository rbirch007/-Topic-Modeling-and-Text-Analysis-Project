AUTOBIOGRAPHY OF SARAH B. LAYTON. 
(Concluded.)

	I never forget the night before he died. I 
went into h[-]s room before I retired to wish
him good night. His very look pierced my 
heart, and before I had time to speak he 
said, "O, my girl, you will meet me on 
the other side, wont you?" I answered
"I guess so," and turned and left the room 
quietly. I never bade him good night 
again. The next day he was under the in-
fluence of drugs all day, for the pain was 
too great for him and he passed away. 
	I sometimes think that if I had my life 
to live over again, I would try to overcome 
myself even more than I did, but I do not 
know. I am very thankful, however, that 
he did come home, through the blessing of
the Lord, to lay his body down with his 
family who preceded him. 
	When we went to have our endowments 
in 1856, Brother Kimball was talking to 
those who were going through, and he 
spoke at length on confidence. He en-
treated the brethren and sisters, husbands
and wives never to allow themselves to 
lose confidence in each other. He said, 
"I will promise you if you do, it will 
never be restored in this world, and I am 
afraid the eternity cannot restore it."
