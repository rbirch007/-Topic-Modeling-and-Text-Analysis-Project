SARAH E. CARMICHAEL.
A REQUIEM FOR THE DEPARTED UTAH POETESS
A requiem for the dead—
	The dead of many years!
Her brilliant spirit faltered, failed and fled,
	To “ashes” and unavailing tears.

The gifted singer faded.
	Her soul was hushed to silence here.
Too tense the strain for earthly casket, jaded,
	A broken lyre, those strings that thrilled our
		ear.

One of the grand immortals—
	Peers were but few and they unknown:
Her genius opened wide those shining portals
	Forever barred save to the touch they own.

There dwell the spirit singers,
	Whose songs and music were of old.
To earth they came with well-trained vibrant
		fingers,
	“Lest we forget” and perish by earth’s cold.

When silent dead, or dying
	’Mid chill of circumstances testing,
That inspiration all uncrushed is trying
	Wherever it may be, for final resting.

The dear one now—the sainted,
	Will sing again with gift supernal, 
Forgetting that on earth she ever fainted
	Among the poet singers crowned eternal!
