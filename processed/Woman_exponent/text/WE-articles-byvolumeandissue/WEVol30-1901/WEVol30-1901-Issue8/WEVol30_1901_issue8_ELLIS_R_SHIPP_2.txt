					ELLIS R. SHIPP.


Mrs. Livermore at the last meeting of
the Massachusetts Woman’s Suffrage
Association spoke emphatically on the duty
of women to keep their engagements, and
not to let themselves be appointed on a com-
mittee to do something and then neglect to
do it. “Make yourself responsible for one
thing, and then do it if you die for it,” she
said. “I did not want to come today, (it
was a very inclement afternoon) I was fol-
lowed to the door of my house with prophe-
cies of pneumonia, but I was advertized to
speak and I am here. If I have any repu-
tation, it is for keeping my promises.”
