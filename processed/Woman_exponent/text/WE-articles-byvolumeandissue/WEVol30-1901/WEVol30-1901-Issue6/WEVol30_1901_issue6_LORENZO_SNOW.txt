		LORENZO SNOW.

Another President has passed from earth, 
	And left a host of loving friends behind;
“A man of God” who prized Truth’s wondrous
		worth.
	And sensed its import unto human kind.

Like Apostolic men of olden times,
	Preachers of Righteousness unpraised, unpaid,
He scattered light afar in many climes
	And many converts, by God’s help he made,

In danger oft, upon the land and sea;
	A noble captive, doom’d for conscience sake,
Caged was his body, but his soul was free,
	Such sterling Saints a heaven of hell can make

Refined and sensitive and true as steel,
	The sacred law of heaven was his delight;
Nothing could shake his faith, or quench his zeal
	Nothing could force him to renounce the right.

From bondage freed, by God’s o’erruling hand.
	Snatched from the furnace, tried as purest gold,
He filled the greatest role in all the land
	Wise guardian of “The Master’s” flock and
		fold.

Shall Zion’s children Saints of latter-days,
	Who knew his purity of heart and mind
Shall Zion’s bards neglect to sound his praise,
	Whose peer, whose equal, we shall rarely find?

Full of good works, of ripe old age he died;
	Not in a murderer’s malice stricken down,
Yet by uncounted ordeals, sanctified.
	Lorenzo Snow has earned a martyr’s crown.
