LETTER.
	Glen Echo, Md., April 16, 1902.
Mrs. E. B. Wells:
	MY DEAR MRS. WELLS:—Your package
of beautiful photographs is here. I have
never seen finer. If Salt Lake is as beau-
tiful as that, it is sufficient to make every-
one want to go and I am living in the hope
that one day I will. You have probably
seen that I am to go to Russia, sailing the
first day of May, hoping not to be gone
over six weeks.
	I remember every day the beautiful visit
we had in Washington and cannot feel that
that is the last of it.
	Please give great love to all the sisters
and relatives there in Salt Lake and waft
one of the kindliest breezes of love over to
Susa Gates.
	You will naturally infer that I am doing
everything hastily in these days with so
much immediately before me, and I must
even sign this letter in haste and love,
				Yours,
