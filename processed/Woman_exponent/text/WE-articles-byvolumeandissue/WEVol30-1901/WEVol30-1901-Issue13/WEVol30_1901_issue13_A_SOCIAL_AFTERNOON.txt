A SOCIAL AFTERNOON.
	A social gathering or party was given
by Sisters Sarah J., Martha T. and Carlie
Y. Cannon, on Tuesday, April 22 ult., to
about fifty ladies at the residence of C. Y.
Cannon. The handsome hall, parlors and
dining room were beautifully decorated
and the company was of a social nature,
conversation enlivening, interspersed with
music, songs, etc.
	Little Miss Ann Cannon gave some
humorous recitations, Miss Vera Cannon,
who has a fine voice, sang, in her best
style, songs old and new. Miss Hester
Cannon, a skillful musician, favored the
company with some choice piano music
and Mrs. Annie Wells Cannon gave a
favorite recitation and the hours flew
swiftly by.
	A delicious luncheon was served in the
spacious dining room, large enough to
seat all the guests. Sister M. Isabella
Horne, the oldest lady present, pronounced
the blessing. The tables were prettily
decorated with flowers and a happy feeling
pervaded the company, who all seemed to
to enjoy the appetizing viands.
	Such hours of social intercourse are
good for all, and and endear to each other
those whose time is so occupied in
everyday pursuits that they have little op-
portunity to meet and greet neighbors and
friends. To bring congenial friends to-


gether for social enjoyment is to confer
a real benefit as well as pleasure. 
Hand grasps hand, eye lights eye in good friend-
	ship,
And great hearts expand, and grow, one in the
	sense of this world’s life.
