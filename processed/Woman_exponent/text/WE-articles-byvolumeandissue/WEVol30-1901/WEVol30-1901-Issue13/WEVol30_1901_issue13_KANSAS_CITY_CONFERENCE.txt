KANSAS CITY CONFERENCE.
	Minutes in brief of the Relief Society
of Kansas City, Jackson Co., Missouri,
which was held September 27, 1902, in the
hall at 1107 East 19th Street, with Presi-
dent Estella Milligan presiding. Services
began with singing, “Do what is right.”
Prayer by Jas. G. Duffin, president of the
Southwestern States mission. Singing,
“We thank Thee, O God, for a prophet.”
	President Estella Milligan made a few
appropriate remarks, said she felt very glad
to meet with the sisters of Utah, as Sister
Sarah J. Cannon, Annie T. Hyde and her
daughter Jean were present, having come
to visit the different branches of the society
in the mission.
	Sister Annie T. Hyde was then intro-
duced. She spoke of the organization of
the Relief Society by the Prophet Joseph at
Nauvoo, Illinois, March 17, 1842, with
Sister Emma Smith as President. Gave
instances in her own life of blessings re
ceived by doing her duty in the Relief
Society work Gave statistics pertaining
to the growth of the work, and said many
interesting and gratifying things, which
were highly appreciated by all present.
	Sister Jean Hyde read the address de-
livered by President Lorenzo Snow to the
Relief Society at Saltair, July 9, 1901.
	Sister Amelia B. Carling, missionary
from Orderville, Utah, bore a faithful test-
imony to the truthfulness of the Gospel.
Was glad to have the privilege of defend-
ing God’s work in the land of promise.
	President Jas. G. Duffiin was the next
speaker. He said he was very much
pleased to have the sisters of Utah with us
Spoke at some length encouraging the
sisters in the work. Bore his testimony
that the organization of the Relief Society
was inspired of the Lord. Spoke of the
Woman’s Building at Salt Lake City and
encouraged the sisters to contribute to the
same.
