ENTERTAINMENT AT BEE HIVE.
	Wednesday, April 8, 1902, Sister Julina
L. Smith entertained the officers and Board
of Directors of the National Woman’s
Relief Society and a few invited guests at
the Bee Hive House, assisted by a com-
mittee from the Board: Sisters Emily S.
Richards, Julia P. M. Farnsworth and
Phebe Y. Beatie.
	A delicious luncheon was served and the
social intercourse was delightfully pleasant.
	Among the guests who were not mem-
bers of the General Board were Sisters
Martha Smith Harris, of Provo, Sarah
Richards Smith and Edna L. Smith, of
this city. All the members of the Board
of Directors were present excepting Helena
E. Madsen, of Gunnison, Harriet M.
Brown, of Kanab, Martha A. Cannon,
Washington, D. C., and Lucy S. Cardon,
of Logan, Utah.
	An enjoyable afternoon was passed and
the ladies were happy in having the oppor-
tunity of social conversation and inter-
change of ideas, which helps to create
union of effort in philanthropic work.
	Thanks to Sister Julina L. Smith for the
happy occasion.
