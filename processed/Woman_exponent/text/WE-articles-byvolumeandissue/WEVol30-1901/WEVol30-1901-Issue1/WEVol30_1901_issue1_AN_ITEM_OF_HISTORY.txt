AN ITEM OF HISTORY.
	Last Sunday, May 26, 1901, at a fast
meeting in the Temple, we were talking
about the various trials some people had to
pass through in this life, but many times
seemed to prove to be blessings in disguise.
	I mentioned visiting Sister Eliza R.
Snow when she was sick in Nauvoo in an
upper room in Col. Markham’s house, a
room so low that she could almost reach
the rafters as she lay in bed; without car-
pet on the floor, perhaps a chair or two, a
small trunk, a stand or table, perhaps a
candlestick and a tallow candle. I have
been told it was in this room she composed
that beautiful hymn, “O my Father.” If
she had had a splendidly furnished room
with every comfort, perhaps she would not
have been inspired to compose that hymn.
	President Snow said it was likely she
would not.
