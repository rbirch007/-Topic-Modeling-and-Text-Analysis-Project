SCHOOL EXAMINATION

	An examination of some of the advanced
classes in Miss Mary E. Cook's school, at the
close of the last quarter, April 16th, was very
entertaining, and commendatory of the efforts
and abilities of both teacher and scholars.
	The reviewing of a class in American History
was particularly interesting.  Apparently every
scholar in the class excelled in promptly and
correctly answering a long list of questions on
the laws and constitution of the United States;


and in giving names and dates of celebrated
persons and memorable events. The names of
the most praiseworthy, however, are as follows:
Emeline Wells, Amy Colkins, Ida Felt, Hor-
tense Lange, Anna Lynch, Isabella Pugsley,
Wealthy Richards, George Taylor, Anna Wells,
Nettie Wells, Luella Miles, Julia Stringham,
Ida Young, Elizabeth Best, Georgia Spencer,
Messrs W. Van Cott and A. Gabbott, although
not present at the examination, having left
school a few weeks previous, formerly belonged
to the class, and came in after the close of the
review, to show, by the ready manner in which
they could repeat the "Constitution," and an-
swer all questions that could be propounded
upon it, that their interest in the study had
not collapsed. 
	Select readings and recitations by younger
pupils, contributed much to the pleasantness
of the occasion; which was also enlivened by
sweet music, vocal and instrumental, from
some of the young ladies of the school.
	President D. H. Wells, Elder J. E. Taylor
and a few lady friends were present.
