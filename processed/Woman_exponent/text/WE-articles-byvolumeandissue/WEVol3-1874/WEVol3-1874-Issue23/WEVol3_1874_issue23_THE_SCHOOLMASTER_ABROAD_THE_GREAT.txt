THE SCHOOLMASTER ABROAD:—The great
spelling epidemic has reached California at
last, and San Jose, San Francisco and other
places are having considerable merriment
at their spelling matches, as people in the
East have had before them.  An exchange
says that in St. Louis, recently, a number of
well-known citizens contended in a spelling
match. Reavis, the capital removal man,
went down on the word "capitolian;"
Knapp, editor of the Republican, was
crushed by Laocoon," and sat down amid
shouts of derision; Carl Schurz collided fa-
tally with "pianissimo;" Henry T. Blow
was vanquished by "tempestuous;" Cartz
Brown was floored by cosmographical;" ex-
Congressman Standard succumbed to "may-
orality;" and Captain Eads, who knows all
about digging out the Mississippi, couldn't
spell peerless.
