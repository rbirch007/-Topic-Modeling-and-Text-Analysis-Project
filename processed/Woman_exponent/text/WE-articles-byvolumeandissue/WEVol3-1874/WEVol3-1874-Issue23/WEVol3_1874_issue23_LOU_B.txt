							LOU B.

	From St. GEORGE, April 15, 1875.
DEAR EDITOR:
	I am a little girl ten years old. I do not
know how to address you as I ought. I am
going to school to sister O. M. Everetts in
the first ward school.  The little girls in
our school being anxious to do something
for the temple, commenced to make a quilt;
and in about ten days after we commenced
it was finished. they gave us ten dollars
credit on the temple.  Sister M. Mustard
kindly proffered to cut the pieces out for us:
we all felt pleased to think we were able to
do something to help build the temple.
The little children are all anxious to do
something that their names may be record-
ed for good. Wishing you great success
with your valuable paper, I subscribe my-
self,		Yours Affectionately,
