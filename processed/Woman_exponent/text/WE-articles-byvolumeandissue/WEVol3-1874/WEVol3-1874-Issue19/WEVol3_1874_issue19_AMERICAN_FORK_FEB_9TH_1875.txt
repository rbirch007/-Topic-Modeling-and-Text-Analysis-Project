		AMERICAN FORK, Feb. 9th, 1875.
DEAR EDITOR:
	The Young Ladies Mutual Improvement
Association of this city, was organized on
the 6th inst., under the superintendance of
Mrs. I. Durrant and Mrs. E. Eastmond. Miss
Mary Eastmond was chosen for President;
Miss Emily Abel, Mrs. Phebe Petersen, Miss
Jane Harrington, Miss Esther Hindley,
Miss Susannah Alston and Miss Henrietta
Henriot for Counsellors; Miss Elvina Cromp-
ton, Secretary; Miss Clara Wrighty assistant
Secretary, and Mrs. E. Durrant as Treasurer.
It was thought advisable to have a small
Fund for the purchasing of books, &c., for
the use of the Association.  There were
sixty-five names enrolled that day, and there
are others who will join that could not at-
tend then.  Bishop L. E. Harrington, Bros.
Hindley, Wm. Jackson, and others, gave
some good instructions on the object of the
Association, encouraging the young ladies
to go on in the good work, and set an exam-
ple to the young men worthy of imitation.
An address by Miss E. R. Snow, to the
young ladies of Provo, was then read from
the EXPONENT, being very appropriate to
the occasion.  Meetings are to be held each
alternate week.  We hope to be able to say
in the future a good work has been done,
