			YEARNINGS.

	The years go by, the years go by,
	I see them pass without a sigh.
	For thou, my love, art cold and dead,
	The earth heaped o'er thy bright young head.
	What now is all this world to me
	But tasteless, dull monotony?
	
	The years pass on, the years pass on,
	And soon—full soon they will be gone.
	I have no time or tears to weep,
	So swiftly by the moments sweep;
	But haste to fill life's fleeting hours
	With duty's pale yet fragrant flowers.

	My love, I soon shall reach the strand—
	I almost see thy beckoning hand;—
	Though wild and dark, life's water's roar,
	Yet swift and soon, they waft me o'er.
	I dare not think upon the peace
	That comes when all these waitings cease;
	When thou my true, true heart shall know
	And doubt no more as years ago.

									A. E. T.
