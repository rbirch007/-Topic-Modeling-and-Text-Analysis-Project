EDITOR EXPONENT:
	In the vale of Avon, within sight of and
on the grounds of the castle that stands on
the margin of that classic river where the
immortal Shakespere first drew breath, I
received the life that has been preserved for
near sixty years, and intelligence respect-
ing that future life to which we are all hast-
ening. The faith then implanted in my
bosom by a mother's teachings, has grown
into a closer acquaintance with God than
faith implies; for this I thank my mother—
as well as my Father who is in the heavens!
Hence my first effort, in responding to your
kind invitation, is naturally influenced by
remembrances of her teachings, and no
name seems so appropriate to be known by
as that of the place where I received my
first inspirations.
	Dear Sister, most respectfully I subscribe
myself,				AVONDALE.

					For the EXPONENT.
