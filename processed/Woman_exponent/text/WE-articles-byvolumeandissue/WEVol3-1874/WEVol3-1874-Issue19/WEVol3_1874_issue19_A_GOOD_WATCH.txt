A GOOD WATCH.

	In repairing the house of Mrs. Wrisley,
wrecked by the Mill River flood, a little be-
low Haydenville, recently, her gold watch
and chain were fouud just under the floor,
hanging to a piece of board. The house was
upset by the flood, and the watch had stop-
ped at the time the flood came along. When
shaken it had commenced running again,
apparently as good as ever. This watch
was once stolen, for which offence a man
was convicted and is now serving out his
sentence.


BARNUM'S Great Roman Hippodrome will
close soon to give the performers and ani-
mals time to recruit for the summer travel-
ing campaign. The establishment will trav-
el by rail, using 125 cars, and will exhibit
in tents 800 feet long. It will embrace 600
men, women, and children, 800 horses and
ponies, beside elephants, camels, buffaloes,
&c. How is that for stupendousness?
