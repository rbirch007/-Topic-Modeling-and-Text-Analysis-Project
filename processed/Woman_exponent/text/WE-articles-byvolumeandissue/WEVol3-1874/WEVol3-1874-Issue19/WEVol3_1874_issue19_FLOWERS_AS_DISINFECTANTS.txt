FLOWERS AS DISINFECTANTS.

	A writer in Saturday Review states that:
"Professor Mantegazza has discovered that
ozone is developed by certain odorous flow-
ers. A writer in nature states that strong-
smelling vegetables, essences such as mint,
cloves, lavender, lemon and cherry laurel
develop a very large quantity of ozone when
in contact with atmospheric oxygen in light.
Flowers destitute of perfume do not devel-
op it, and generally the amount of ozone
seems to be in proportion to the strength
emanated. Prof. Mantagazza recommends
that in marshy districts, and places infested
with noxious exhalations, strong-smelling,
flowers should be placed around the house,
in order that the ozone emitted from them
may exert its oxidizing influence. So
pleasant a plan for making a malarious dis-
trict salubrious only requires to be known to
be put into practice"—For a long time we
have been made acquainted with the fact
that sunflowers are of value as disinfectants,
the reason of this has not even yet been
fully determined. It does not follow because
flowers are beneficial in a sanatary sense
round out houses that they are so in our
houses. Plants at night give off noxious
exhalations, so do flowers. It may be ta-
ken as a rule nothing like fresh air with-
out any odor whatever. It is without doubt
true that perfume from flowers produces
that change in oxygen which converts it
into ozone. Ozone is produced in many ways
by natural processes so as to purify the air.
