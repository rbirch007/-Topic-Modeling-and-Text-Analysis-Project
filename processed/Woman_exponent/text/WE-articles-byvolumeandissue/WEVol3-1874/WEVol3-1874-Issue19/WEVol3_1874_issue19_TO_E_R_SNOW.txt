		TO E. R. SNOW.

Seventy-one! can this be so,
And thy heart still throb with so fresh a glow?
Thy step so firm, and so warm and bright
The kindly beam of thy dark eye's light.

Hand ever ready the weakest to guide,
Drifting along in life's billowy tide;
Voice lifted high in the conflict for right,
And whispering peace to the soul in dark night.

Time has not frozen the fountain of love,
Pure and unfailing it flows from above;
And freely 'tis given earth's children to bless,
In times of rejoicing or deepest distress.

Priceless the seed which in this life you sow,
And priceless the fruits which thy harvest shall know;
Seed that will spring forth in glory and might
In the souls of the sons and the daughters of light.

Zion would bless you as onward you go,
Still bless and be blessed as you journey below,
Still sow the germs that will blossom so bright
In a crown for thy head in the mansions of light.

A crown all unfading, eternal, divine.
Where thorns never more with the roses will twine,
Where never more bitter will mingle with sweet,
Nor rough be the pathway, nor weary the feet.

Thy works all await thee on heaven's bright shore,
The blessings of Zion have too gone before.
The thanks of the hungry which thy hand hath fed,
The prayers of the souls which thy counsel that led.

May you live till God's people are ransomed and free,
Live till the wicked from Zion shall flee,
'Til the warfare is ended, the victory won.
Then hear the glad tidings—"Thy work is well done."
										HOPE.
