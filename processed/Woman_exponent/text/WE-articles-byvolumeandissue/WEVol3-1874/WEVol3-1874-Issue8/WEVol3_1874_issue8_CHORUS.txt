		CHORUS.

Then cheer again for Utah! the land we love so well:
Thanksgiving for our Mountain Home in truthful num-
	bers swell;
It is the home our God has given where we in peace
	may dwell.

We came into this mountain land all travel-stained and
	worn
But thankful to our fathers' God who gave us thus a home;
And here we dwelt, contented well, from all the world
	apart,
In a land once thought a barren wild, completely deso-
	late.
But Labor has a potent charm to make the desert smile,
Change desolation into bloom and barreness beguile.
Neat little villages spring up beneath its magic sway,
Contentment and prosperity attend its happy way.

		Then cheer again, &c.

We gladly till the willing soil and raise the golden grain,
With orchards fair and gardens gay we clothe the smil-
	ing plain:
We build our homes and make them fair, we drive the
	saw and plane,
The vise and anvil never rust, we make them ring again;
Millstones revolve, and spinning mules and busy shuttles
	fly. 
While sewing machines and fingers too the glittering
	needles ply.
"HOME MANUFACTURE!" is our cry, "Let's independent be
"Of those who drove us years ago and laughed our woes
	to see!"

		Then cheer again, &c.

We thank our God who brought us here for every bless-
	ing given—
First for this rugged mountain home, a refuge and a
	haven;
We thank him for the sunshine bright and for the win-
	ter's snow,
And the Mountain barrier He has set 'tween us and many
	a foe:
For life and health, for kindred ties, for raiment, food
	and fire,
And myriad, precious, unnamed gifts which thankful
	hearts inspire. 
He is our help in every need, our Father, Guide and
	Friend;
Oh! let us worthy children prove and serve Him to the
	end.

		Then cheer again. &c. 
								LU. DALTON.
Beaver, Beaver Co., U. T., July 24th, 18—.
