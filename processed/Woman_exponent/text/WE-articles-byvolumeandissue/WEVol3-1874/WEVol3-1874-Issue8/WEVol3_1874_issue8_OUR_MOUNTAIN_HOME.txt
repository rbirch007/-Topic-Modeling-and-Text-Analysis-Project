			OUR MOUNTAIN HOME.

A home was ours when Nature wore for all a smiling face:
Where we with feeble virtue strove to serve the God of
	grace; 
But enemies of truth arose, and burned our peaceful
	homes,
And drove us to the wilderness, alone and lost to roam.
Forlorn, fatigued, and sore distressed, and pinched with
	want and cold;
With foes rejoicing in our woes, God did our grief behold,
And over deserts, hills and plains, He led our trusting
	feet
To Deseret, dear Deseret, a sweet, secure retreat.
