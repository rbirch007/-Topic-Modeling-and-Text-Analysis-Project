"JUST THIS ONCE."

"Just this once," the tempter said,
	With smiling lip;
Thus enticed, the victim took
	The fatal sip;
And years rolled on, his doom is wed,
Death glooms above the drunkard's bed.

"Just this once," the tempter said,
	With luring voice.
Tremblingly the victim threw
	The rattling dice;
And years rolled on; the river's wave
Rolls darkly o'er a gambler's grave,

"Just this once," the tempter said,
	No one shall know,"
The victim, thoughtless, gave his soul
	To ceaseless woe;
And years rolled on: oh, fearful scene!
The death bed of a libertine.

"Just this once," the tempter said,
	"My faith I pledge."
The victim dreamed not that her deed
	Was ruin's edge; 
The years rolled on: ah, 'tis the same!
A suicidal death of shame.
