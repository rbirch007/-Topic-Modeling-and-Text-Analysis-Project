EDUCATION.

	There is no subject of more importance to a
community or a nation than is the subject of
Education. On it depends the future destiny
of nations, kingdoms and empires; their rise
and progress, or fall and dissolution, in propor-


tion to the intelligence of the people. Under
these considerations, we would suggest the
policy of organizing a co-operative system of
education that will afford facilities for all who
wish to become students and are not able to do
so for want of means; that they be furnished
with manual labor, and learn trades that will
enable them to pay for their tuition. This we
consider far preferable to a free school system;
as the females as well as males may be taught
how to be self-sustaining. Shall women al-
ways be dependent? or will mothers wake up
to their duties and teach their daughters the
importance of learning trades and that the
true results of education are to produce what
they consume and to do something, not only
for themselves but for others. It has been a
long established custom in the training of fe-
males to teach them that they are dependent
while the males are thrown on their own re-
sources, this is all right but let woman do her
part, so that a man may know that when he
takes a wife he does not take a parlor ornament,
to waste all that he can earn, in extravagance. 
There is certainly a great responsibility resting
upon wives and mothers in regard to the health
aud longevity of their families. Teachers and
parents cannot attach too much importance to
the subject of diet and every woman should
understand the art of cooking. Never, until
we thus popularize this accomplishment, can
we expect to see healthy modes of living and a
people free from disease. 			W.E.
