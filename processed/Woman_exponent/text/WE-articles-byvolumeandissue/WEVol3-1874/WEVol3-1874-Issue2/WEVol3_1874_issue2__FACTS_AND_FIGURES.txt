	FACTS AND FIGURES.

	THE ten hour bill for manufactories has
passed the Massachusetts Senate.
	TWO hundred and nine wild ducks in one
day, is the boast of an Illinois sportsman. 
	THE “Tichborne” number of the London 
Graphic reached a sale of 200,000 copies.
	PENNSYLVANIA stands first in the list of
 coal producing states, Ohio second, and Illi-
nois third.
	A JOVIAL party of five Indian skeletons
sitting in one grave round a succotash ket-
tle has been disinterred at Niantic, Conn.
	COAL has been struck at Princeton, Ind.,
at a depth of 200 feet. Underneath the
vein is a stratum of fire-clay seven feet
thick.
	THE Scientific American predicts that the
time will soon come when ice will be manu-
factured in all our great cities at a dollar a
ton.
	A SPANIEL named curly is the regular
mail carrier between Lake of The Woods,
Dakota, and the Minnesota line, twelve
miles away.
	THE mercantile navies of the world com-
prise 61,429 vessels, and a total tonage of 18,
514,029. Of these Great Britain has 3,061
steamers and 20,832 sailing vessels; the
United States coming next, with 403 steam-
ers and 6,786 sailing vessels.
	IN the population of nations, China ranks
first, with 425,213,152; the British Empire
second, with 199,817,000; and Russia third,
with 82,172,022. In density of population
Belgium stands first, with 451 per square
mile; England is next, with 389.
	COMMODORE Vanderbuilt has donated an
additional $100,000 to the university bear-
ing his name at Nashville, Tenn., to be used
as a building fund, in order that the univer-
sity buildings may be completed in accord-
ance with the plans and specifications here-
tofore approved by him.
	SUEZ CANAL.—120 vessels passed through

statement shows the increase in the receipts
compared with former years.—In March,

l,333.434f. In March, 1873, 202,495 tons;
receipts, 2,246,278f. In March, 1874, 229,
