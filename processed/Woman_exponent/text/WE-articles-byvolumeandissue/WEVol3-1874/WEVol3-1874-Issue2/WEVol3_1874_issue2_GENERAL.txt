	A Concord boy poured the contents of a
bottle on his leg for sport; the fluid was vit-
riol and the little fellow was so badly burned
as to be crippled for life......A bill for an
amendment to the Constitution, striking out
the word male, was lost in the Massachusetts
Senate, but the Chairman, Hon. Henry S.
Washburn made a grand speech in favor of
the bill......The Senate of New York has
passed a bill which provides for the compul-
sory education of children......The Digger
Indians, it is said, scorn to be grave diggers,
and burn their dead......Paris papers an-
nounce that Madame Bazine is now actually
installed in prison with her husband......The
British Government furnishes free passage
to emigrants to New Zealand, where wages
are high and living expenses low.


AN Aberdeen minister who was catechis-
ing his young parishoners before the con-
gregation, put the usual question to a stout
girl whose father kept a public house,
“What is your name?” No reply. The
question having been repeated, the girl re-
plied, "Nane o' your fun, Mr. Minister, ye
ken my name well enough. D’ye no say
when ye come to our house on a night, ‘Bet,
bring me some ale?’ ”
