	CEDAR CITY, IRON CO., May 19th, 1874.
	Our Relief Society is prospering; liberal
donations have been made to the building
of the St. George Temple; also to gathering
the poor. The sisters generally are self-
sustaining, very few cases of real necessity
have been known, and those have been at-
tended to. We had an excellent meeting
last Thursday, some good remarks from
John M. Higby, Thomas Thorley and Yens
Neilson—the Presidency of the United Order
in this place—followed by a short address
from Brother J. P. Jones; the chief subject
being, classification of labor. A good feel-
ing prevails among the Saints and all seem
willing to do their part.
	With kind regards, I remain your sister,
