EDITORIAL NOTES.

   THE NATIONAL congress of brewers pro-
test against the doings of the temperance
movers, which attempt to prove that malt
liquors produce immortality, pauperism and
crime; and they pledge united effort to in-
fluence public opinion in favor of the same,
as wholesome, nutritious and necessary.
They direct a lengthy address to the Presi-
dent and people of the United States, in
which they denounce the movement of the
crusaders as being merely an effort of de-
signing men for personal agrandizement.
The crusaders are evidently waking up
some indivdiuals. Let them proceed.
   REV. DR. John Hall of New York speaks
of the crusade of women in Ohio and else-
where as probably, similar in its effects to
the temperance movement of Father Mat-
hews which some years ago, swept like a
tidal wave over Ireland and portions of Great
Britain and America, and for the time be-
ing, greatly limited drunkenness; and thou-
sands of living, prosperous men, date their
prosperity from that movement. “As a
means of suppressing drinking," says the
Rev. Dr,. “you may call it a failure; as a
means of unspeakable good to all these, you
must call it a success.” There is no doubt
but he is correct in supposing that the pres-
ent temperance movement will also be a
means of salvation to many, though it may
not affect all.
   THERE ARE ninety-nine newspapers pub-
lished in Michigan. Of these, seventy-six
have declared in favor of woman suffrage.
The other twenty-three will doubtless soon
be ashamed to appear against it, and the
woman’s cause will have triumphed in that
State.
