		WOMAN'S RIGHTS.

	A woman's truest empire is her home;
Nor should she idly from its precints roam;
Nor in her neighbors seek some fault to find;
But strive her own affairs alone to mind.
	
	High as a good man's place will always be.
As high is a true woman's destiny.
Quietly working at the side of man,
Both striving to do all the good they can.

	'Tis woman's right to lead in virtue's way
The opening mind, from childhood's earliest day.
Teaching to ardent and aspiring youth,
Self sacrifice, pure love, unshaken truth.

	With loving zeal to charm the cares of age,
The pangs of want and the sickness to a[-]suage:
To be a comforter when trials pain,
In sorrow and adversity, sustain.

	To seek the homes where striken sisters lie,
To teach the trembling sinner how to live or die;
Eager and glad each holy work to aid,
To share the burdens upon others laid.

	By gentle words win erring hearts from wrong,
And show what power to Woman does belong.
Nor turning from small deeds which must be done,
And by which must of lasting good is won.

	May we each strive our duties to fulfill,
With earnest purpose and determined will;
Helpmeet for man, not rivals, should we prove;
Our empire home, our "Rights" maintained by love.
