WOMAN’S EXPONENT.


For the EXPONENT.
		THE RELIEF SOCIETY.
We will toil on bravely, through sunshine and shade,
For our Heavenly Father will still lend us aid;
Though lowly our sphere yet our labors of love,
He honored on earth and still favors above.
“I was an hungered, ye fed me,” once kindly he said,
"The poor of my people likewise ye have fed;
As ye gave unto them, unto me ye have given,
Enter into the joy of your Father in heaven.
“The winter winds chilled me, my raiment was old,
Warm clothing ye gave, to protect me from cold;
My blessing was all that to you then was given,
Your treasures were hid in the Kingdom of Heaven.
“I was thirsty, when lo! from the river's pure brink,
Of the crystal fount springing ye brought forth my drink.
“I was sick and in prison, ye came unto me—
"No longer my servants, but friends ye shall be,”
Then why should we linger or cease from our toil?
The Bride is not ready, all lamps have not oil;
And the watchmen in Zion unceasingly cry,
“Prepare ye, make ready, the Bridegroom is nigh.
								S. B. R.
	Logan.
