WOMAN’S VOICE.

Editor Woman’s Exponent:
	We are still alive in Providence and en-
joying a goodly flow of the Spirit of God
and of our religion. We have had a good
time during the past winter; have not had
many dances, but have had good and well
attended meetings, especially our Society
meetings! I never enjoy myself better
than when attending them. I think if all
the sisters would attend these meetings and
carry with them the Spirit of God, knowing
that they are trying to do their best for
each other and to serve God, it will assur-
edly result in good. Through the past win-
ter I have been reading some of the early
history of the Church. Among other things,
concerning Parley P. Pratt’s imprisonment
and escape. Surely the brethren must have
had great faith in God and He was always
mindful of them. He heard their prayers
and answered them. I feel that He is just
as mindful of us in these days. We cannot
but acknowledge the Hand of God in the
deliverance of Brother Ricks from impris-
onment. We can see that the devil cannot
have it all his own way; especially if the
brethren and sisters are united in offering
up their prayers for the welfare of the
kingdom.
	The Lord will not forget His children as
long as they do not forget Him.
	I will mention that our Relief Society
is arranging for a party some time this
month, the proceeds to be given for the
benefit of our new Hall. I will conclude,
praying God to bless and prosper you in the
noble work of disseminating knowledge.’
	I remain your sister in the Gospel,
					Elizabeth N. NYE.
	Providence, Apr. 5. 1875.
