			BATHSHEBA W. SMITH.

	SUGAR HOUSE WARD, March 27th, 1875.
DEAR SISTER RICHARDS:
	I fear you are beginning to think the sis-
ters out in this ward are going to sleep.—I
wish to say, it is not so; they are doing the
best they can, with their surroundings; re-
sponding to all the calls made upon them,
visiting the ward, taking care of the sick,
and donating a little to the St. George Tem-
ple. The brethren and sisters of the ward
have donated nearly six-hundred dollars in
labor and produce towards building a new
house for the Society.
	With kind wishes I am yours kindly,
			SARAH WHEELER, Secretary.
