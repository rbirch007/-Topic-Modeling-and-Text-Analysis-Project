MRS. L. G. RICHARDS:
	I send you a note written by the Bishop of
the 1st Ward, S. L. City, and request its pub-
lication as an advertisement of the specified
movement of one very useful branch of Home
Industry.
	I consider each successful Branch of Home
Manufactures, an additional stone in laying the
foundation for the upbuilding of Zion—in our
becoming a self-sustaining people. To make
it successful, it must be supported by the influ-
ence and encouragement of the saints; and this
cannot be done by waiting with folded hands
and doubtful looks, to see whether it proves a
failure or success. May the latter be the result
of this worthy effort of the saints in the 1st
Ward.
	S. L. City, April 5. 			E. R. SNOW.

	SALT LAKE CITY, March 23rd, 1875.
MISS ELIZA R. SNOW,
	DEAR SISTER:—The 1st Ward Branch of the
United Order commenced about the 1st of Feb-
ruary, to knit women's and girls' stockings and
men's and boys' socks, and other wear. We have


one machine working and when we have pat-
ronage enough we will have more to supply the
demand, and we expect to supply a better arti-
cle, and as cheap as any imported. Will you
please use your influence among the sisters to
sustain us in our Home Industry and in carry-
ing out the counsels of President Brigham.
Young?
		Your Brother in the Gospel,
					J. WARBURTON,
					Bishop of 1st Ward.
