		SALT LAKE CITY. APRIL 15, 1875


[Column 1]
			SPLINTERS.
			PERSONAL.
	President Grant is expected to arrive in
Boston on the 17th, as the guest of the 
Commercial Club......Josephine Mansfield
has recovered judgment against the estate
of James Fisk Jr. for $25,000, an effort is
being made to secure a new trial......Gen.
Ben. Butler is the attorney in a case, pend-
ing in the treasury department under very
peculiar circumstances, the case is a claim
for $50,000 for property seized in New Or-
leans for the use of The Northern Army by
Gen. Butler in command.  Butler now ap-
pears as counsel against the acts of Butler
as General......Smiler Colfax has made $60,
000 lecturing this season.....A box at
Patti's benefit in St. Petersburg was sold for
only $250.  At the conclusion of her recent
engagement in that city, she was presented
with a magnificient diadem of diamonds
and sapphires.....Mc. Carter, chief of the
bureau of engraving and printing has ten-
dered his resignation......Andrew Johnson's
speech on the 22d. on the Lousiana question
was at least three hours long......The Presi-
dent has signed the commission of John C.
New to be treasurer of the United States,
to take effect from June 30. 1875......The
Chinese have been celebrating the accession
of the new Emperor....The fourth son of
Bret Harte was born at Morristown N. Y.
on Monday March 15., his eldest is about
thirteen.  The death of his mother has
been recently announced.
