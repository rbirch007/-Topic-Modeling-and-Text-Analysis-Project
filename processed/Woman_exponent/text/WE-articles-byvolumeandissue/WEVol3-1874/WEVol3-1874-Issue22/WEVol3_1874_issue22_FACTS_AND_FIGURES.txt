FACTS AND FIGURES.

	DR. Glenn, said to be the largest farmer
in California sold his last year's crop of
grain for nearly $500,000, gold coin.
	THE consumption of ice has greatly in-
creased in this country during the past five
years. As illustrative of this fact, it is sta-
ted that in 1854, 300,000 tons of ice met the
demands of the people, while now one firm
has put up 880,000, and other parties have
packed as much more.
	BOSTON has been panic-stricken on ac-
count of the scarcity of water, and not with-
out reason, for the water in Lake Cochitu-
ate has been 14 feet below the highest wa-
ter mark, and several inches below the out-
let. The city got water only through the 
agency of two powerful steam pumps run at
the expense of $800 per day.
