HOME AFFAIRS.

	HON. DAVID P. Lowe, Chief Justice of Utah,
arrived Apr. 10, and is stopping at the Walker
House.
	PROF. KARL G. Maeser is to deliver his inter-
esting lecture, "Characteristics of youths of
different ages and nations," under the auspices
of the 12th Ward Literary Association, Friday
evening, Apr. 16. Free admission.
	AT A MEETING of the stockholders of Z. C.
M. I. Saturday afternoon, Apr. 10., a vote was ta-
ken which resulted in Hiram B. Clawson and
Theodore McKean being elected as superin-
tendents of the institution.
	A DENTAL College and infirmary has been
established in this city by Dr. W. H. H. Sharp.
Students desiring to learn dentistry will be
carried through a two years' course of instruc-
tion.
	WE ARE pleased to note the safe return to
her home and friends in this city, of Mrs. Ro-
mania B. Pratt, who has been in attendance at
the Free Medical College for Women, and the
Eye and Ear Infirmary in New York City, for
fourteen months. It was a long time to be ab-
sent from the loved ones, and we hope she finds
them all well, particularly the "darling baby!"
	THE SUBJECT of free schools is vehemently
discussed at present by the "Philomathes" in
this Territory. Whether the establishment
of free schools should prove the result or not,
we hope to see liberal instructions disseminated
throughout the land, wise measures taken and
true principles taught, by which good, thor-
ough, practical educations  may be placed with-
in the reach of all.
	THE LATE general conference, which was
held six days in succession instead of three,
was one of peculiar interest in some respects.
President Brigham Young was present at each
meeting, looking and feeling well. He presid-
ed, and also addressed the conference on sever-
al occasions, one discourse in particular, being
given chiefly for the benefit of the sisters.
Notwithstanding the first four days of the
conference were stormy and disagreeable, there
was a good attendance at the Tabernacle.
