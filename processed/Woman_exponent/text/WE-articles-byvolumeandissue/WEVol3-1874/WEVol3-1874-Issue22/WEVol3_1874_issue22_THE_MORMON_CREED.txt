		THE MORMON CREED.

	It is many years since I came into these
mountains; but in those by-gone days I fre-
quently heard what was called the "Mor-
mon Creed" alluded to.  But in these days
of calumny and ill report, I almost think the
memory of it is fast dying out.
	On hearing a scandalous story not long
since, I said I can't believe it without more
confirmation. And yet, how often such
stories are caught up and sent broad cast
through society to the ruination of a wo-
man's reputation; she is looked upon as a
low character, and sometimes treated by
those who ought to be her friends, as though
she were a fit inmate for a jail.  All this,
when perhaps she  committed no crime,
broken no law, not even done an intentional
wrong. Yet it may take a woman years to
live down such an influence.  How much
suffering and evil it would save, if we only
would stop to think "there are always two
sides to a story." Or if we would remem-
ber that the surest way to find out the truth,
is to go to friends for information, and not
to enemies. Then perhaps we should learn
to practice more the "Mormon creed," To
mind our own business.
