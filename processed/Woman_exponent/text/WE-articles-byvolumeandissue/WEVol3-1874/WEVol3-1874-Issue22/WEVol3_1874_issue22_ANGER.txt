ANGER.

	I think we have a very good subject for
our meeting this evening, and it is one
which we can all realize deserves our atten-
tion. I get angry very easily, but am try-
ing to overcome my ill temper, and striving
to correct my faults. If we do not cultivate
ourselves while we are young, it will be
much harder to accomplish when we are
older. We can begin by being more pa-
tient with our little brothers and sisters,
and speaking kindly to those at home.
Seek to cultivate a pleasing manner towards
all, and when we go into society, we will
be respected and esteemed by those with
whom we are associated.
	I heard the remark recently that it was
not always the prettiest girl that was most
attractive, or most beloved, and it is true.
The manners and conduct of a young lady
will secure the respect and esteem of those
at home and abroad, if she is careful in her
behavior and truly respects herself. No
true lady will cultivate a proud, haughty
disposition. We should pray continually
that we may be truthful for we are told in
the Bible that they who speak falsely have
their portion assigned to them, and they
cannot be trusted, for we cannot lock things
up from them as we do from thieves.
	May we have power to overcome our evil
propensities and walk in the path which
leads to eternal life. 		ROSEBUD.
	S. L. City, April 1st, 1875.
