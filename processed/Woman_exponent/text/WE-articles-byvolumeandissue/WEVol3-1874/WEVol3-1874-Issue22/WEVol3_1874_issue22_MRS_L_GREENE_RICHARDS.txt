MRS. L. GREENE RICHARDS:
	I feel that I should like to mingle my
voice with my sisters, through the columns
of your valuable paper.
	I take great pleasure in reading the EX-
PONENT, but when I undertake to write, it
is with feelings of timidity that I venture
to pen a few lines for your consideration.
	The United Order is a subject that occu-
pies a considerable portion of my thoughts
and reflections. I am a member of the
United Order, and I truly believe the time
has come for us to begin to live in and prac-
tice it. And I think the brethren and sis-
ters who are working together here, feel to
live humbly, and strive to begin to love
their neighbors as themselves. How long
it will take to arrive at that state of perfec-
tion, I am not prepared to say, but it will
not be until we do away with a consider-
able amount of selfishness.
	Some seem to think when they join the
Order they can throw off all responsibility
and that the United Order will take care of
them and theirs.
	I do not look at it in that light; I think
every person should strive to build up and
do all in their power to make it a success,
work just as faithfully and diligently for the
support of a United Order family as they
would for their individual family.
	If it does not prove a success, the fault
will be with the people, and not with the
principle. I often think of a very little re-
mark of Brother George A. Smith, which
expressed a great deal to me. He said:
"Live humbly, very, very humbly." I
think we should all do this, and seek the Lord
diligently in prayer that we may not be led
into temptation, for it is my belief that the
enemy of righteousness will yet try harder
than ever before, to overthrow the Kingdom
