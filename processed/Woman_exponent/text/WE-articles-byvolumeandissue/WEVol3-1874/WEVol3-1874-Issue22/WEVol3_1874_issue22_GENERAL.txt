			GENERAL
	A Baltimore beggar has retired from bus-
iness with a fortune of $26,000.....Delaware
has appropriated $10,000 to the centennial
exhibition......The International Department
will send a competent geologist to examine
the Black Hills and ascertain accurately their
mineral wealth......As late as 1750 in Mass.
a colored woman was burned at the stake for
poisoning her master......Ex-Gov. Hale of
New Hampshire is recovering from his ill-
ness......Prof. G. W. Atherton of Rutgers
College has been tendered the presidency of
Howard University Washington D.C......Dr.
Van Courtlandt a geologist and physician
of great repute died at Ottawa Canada on
Thurs. March 25......Boston proposes to cele-
brate the one hundredth anniversary of the
battle of Bunker Hill June 17th by a grand

[Column 2]
parade of the entire state militia...... Sev-
enty millions of dollars is said to be an un-
exaggerated estimate of losses by fire in the 
United States in 1874......Mrs. Pratt of San
Francisco has applied for a divorce with a 
modest alimony of 1,000,000......In 1874 it
is asserted that San Francisco sent $1,400,000
in gold east to buy pork and lard......Major
Gen. John Williams Ex-Mayor of Rochester
a former member of congress and treasurer
of that City for the last two years died on 
Thurs. March 25th at the age of 68......The
new postal law doubling the pastage on pa-
pers etc. to non-subscribers is now in force
......Prof. Zaba lately from Europe is into-
ducing a new method of teaching History
by means of a chart......Assistant Attorney-
General Hill of Washington has offered his
resignation......There have arrived in Cal.
since Apr. 1, 1874, 33,197 emigrants by rail
......In Nevada there are 900 licensed hous-
es where liquors are retailed the population 
is 55,000......The construction of the Panama
railroad cost 82,000 human lives from death
by malaria.
