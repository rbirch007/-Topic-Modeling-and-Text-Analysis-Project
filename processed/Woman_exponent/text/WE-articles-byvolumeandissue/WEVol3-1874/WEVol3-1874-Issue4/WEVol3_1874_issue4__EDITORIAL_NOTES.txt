EDITORIAL NOTES.
	A NEW submarine cable for communica-
tion between England and America, is com-
pleted. The Great Eastern is to commence
laying it during this month.
	THE POPE in early youth joined the Free
Masons. Charges were preferred against
him in 1865 which he declined meeting and,
recently, he was expelled from the Order
of the Grand Lodge of Italy. Victor Emanu-
el, as grand master of the Orient of Italy
signed the order of expulsion.
	FELONS ARE of so common occurrence
that we deem the following clipping wor-
thy of notice. “A woman in Lapeer, Mich.,
took her husband’s knife a few days ago to
open a felon; shortly after her husband used
the knife to extract a sliver from his hand,
and inoculated himself with the virus of
the felon; his hand began to swell, and in
four hours he was a raving maniac, and in
less than forty-eight hours he died.”
	MR. BRISTOW, the new Secretary of the
Treasury, commenced his duties with a dili-
gence and thoroughness that at once created
a prestige in his favor.  And now it is said
of him that he not only positively refuses
all presents but also declines to resort to the
old ruse of allowing his wife to accept them.
This we think is a noble course if consis-
tently pursued, but will not be very likely
to prove contagious at the seat of Govern-
ment.
