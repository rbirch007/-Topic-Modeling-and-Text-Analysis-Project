THE PEOPLE’S TICKET.

	A meeting composed of prominent citizens,
Mayor Wells presiding, was held at the City
Hall, on the 10th, to nominate candidates for
the territorial and local officers to be elected on
the f[-]rst Monday in August next. The merits
of the different persons nominated were freely
canvassed. The discussion resulted in the fol-
lowing candidates being accepted for the "Peo-
ple's Ticket:"
	For Delegate to Congress, George Q. Cannon;
Commissioners to locate University lands, John
Rowberry, John Van Cott, Lewis S. Hills,
Representatives to the Legislative Assembly,
Orson Pratt, John Taylor, A. P. Rockwood,
Thos. Taylor, Albert Carrington, John Sharp;
Probate Judge, Elias Smith; Selectman, Reu-
ben Miller; Sheriff, Salt Lake County, Stephen
W, Taylor; County Surveyor, Salt Lake Coun-
ty, Abr'm F. Doremus; Superintendent of Com-
mon Schools, for Salt Lake County, Mary E.
Cook; Precinct officers, First Precinct, Salt
Lake City, Adam Speirs, J. P., George Hilton;
Constable; Second Precinct,E. W. East, J. P.,
W. S. Burton, Constable; Third Precinct, Jeter
Clinton, J. P., Milando Pratt, Constable;
Fourth Precinct, A. C. Pyper, J. P., B. Y.
Hampton, Constable; Fifth Precinct, Jacob
Gibson J. P., Wm. Clark, Constable.
	Since the above nominations were made Mr.
J. Clinton has resigned and Mr. A. H. Raleigh’s
name substituted by the People's Territorial
Central Committee which has been organized
as follows: John Sharp, chairman, D. O. Cal-
der, John R. Winder, J. P. Freeze, John T.
Caine, Feramorz Little, J. M. Benedict, E. F. 
Sheets, A. M. Cannon, George Crismon, mem-


bers. A M. Musser, corresponding secretary
and Theodore McKean, secretary.
	These names are now before the people for
the acceptance or rejection of the majority.
We shall support this ticket and we commend
it to the lady voters of this territory.
