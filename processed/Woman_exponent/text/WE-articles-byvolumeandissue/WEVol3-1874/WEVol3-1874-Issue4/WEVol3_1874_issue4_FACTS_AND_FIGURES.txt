FACTS AND FIGURES.

	CHARLESTON, S.C., is to have ice of home
manufacture at $1 a hundred weight this 
summer.	
	A LOT, containing 2,210 square feet, in 
the midst of the business part of London, 
was recently sold for over $160,000 or $72. 
50, gold, per square foot.	
	A CURIOUS and beautiful effect was pro-
duced by an ice-making machine in Phila- 
delplia. It was a cake of manufactured 
ice, in the center of which, completely in-
closed in the translucent material, was a 
bouquet of fresh flowers. Every leaf and 
flower was perfectly visible, while the
brilliancy of the colors was enhanced by the
refraction through the ice.
	IT is stated that the effect of heat and
cold, in expanding and contracting the iron
of the dome of the national Capitol, is such 
that the collossal statue, surmounting it in- 
clines four and a half inches to the west in
the forenoon, and the same distance to the
east in the afternoon. This fact has been
ascertained by fixing a plumb line to the
statue and dropping it to the rotunda be-
low.	
	ST. Augustine, Florida, is the oldest Eu- 
ropean settlement in the United States. It
was founded by the Spaniards, and a large
portion of the inhabitants are of Spanish
descent. With its convents, ruined gate-
ways, quaint cathedral, and houses of Span-
ish build, their balconies overshadowing the
narrow streets, it still resembles some Old
World towns. The remains of the fort of
St. Mark’s, overlooking the sea, crumbling,
and verdant with vegetation, have much 
the appearance of the ruined castles of the
middle ages.
