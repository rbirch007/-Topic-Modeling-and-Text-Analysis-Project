DELICATE GIRLS

	Some very wise and learned people have
made the discovery that idleness, and the
"ennui" consequent upon it, is ruinous to
health. "The nerve forces are as self-de-
structive as an unrestrained locomotive en-
gine." Chronic cases of hysteria and many
other bodily ailments have been cured by
properly directed energies and efforts in
suitable employments. How much better
to be constantly and agreeably employed
than to be forever on the Doctor's hands.


The new College at Northampton is likely to
advance light upon the problem of higher educa-
tion for women.
	Miss Susan B. Anthony, called a Woman Suf-
frage Meeting in New York, May 11th. at Union
League Hall. Mrs. Elizabeth Cady Stanton, Car-
rie T. Burnham, Phobe W. Couzons, Matilda
Joslyn Page and Rev. C. B. Fotheringham were
the speakers on the occasion.
	South Boston Woman Suffrage Club held its
second meeting Tuesday May 4th. at Mrs. Lin-
coln's residence, corner Atlantic and Thomas
Streets. Informal addresses were made by Mrs.
Lucy Stone, and Henry B. Blackwell. Rev. E. N.
Rand, W. W. Doherty Esq. and Charles D Page
editor of the South Boston Gazette made very ap-
propriate and timely remarks.  It is anticipated
this Club will exert political, social and beneficial
influence upon the future of that community.
