	NO cross, no crown.
	OUT of debt, out of danger.
	FAR from court, far from care.
	TAKE away fuel, take away flame.
	COVETOUSNESS brings nothing home.
	THE master's eye makes the horse fat.
	HE that goes borrowing, goes sorrowing.
	ALL are not hunters that blow the horn.
	AN obedient wife, commands her husband.
	LET your face and hands, like the town
clock, tell how your inner works are going.
	LEISURE is sweet to those who have
earned it, but burdensome to those who get
it for nothing.
	THE habit of being always employed is a
great safeguard through life, as well as es-
sential to the culture of every virtue.
	TO find friends when we have no need of
them, and to want them when we have, are
both alike easy and common.
	ONE in a million do a heroic action; but
the little things that make up our life come
every day and every hour.
	IF ever God had failed one who trusted
him, you might doubt; but he never has,
therefore you should be confident.


If you love others, they will love you.
If you speak kindly to them they will
speak kindly to you. Love is repaid with
love, and hatred with hatred.
	Say the strongest things you can with
candor and kindness to a man's face; and
make the best excuse you can for him, with
truth and justice, behind his back.
	By six qualities shall a fool be known:
Anger without cause; speech without mo-
tive; inquiry without an object; putting
trust in a stranger; and wanting capability
to discriminate between a friend and a foe.
	To complain that life has no joys while
there is a single creature whom we can re-
lieve by our bounty, assist by our counsels,
or enliven by our presence, is to lament the
loss of that which we possess, and is just as
rational as to die of thirst with the full cup
in our hands.
	Help somebody worse off than yourself,
and you will feel that you are better off 
than you fancied. You may subdue an an-
gry man by bearing with him, but not by
answering him; that is as if the steel should
try to make the flint leave off giving sparks
by striking it again.
	He is a wiser man who sees his own im-
perfections than he who discovers a planet,
or is able to plan a campaign or build a 
fort. He is more a conqueror who quells
the baser passions within, and brings the an-
imal nature into subjection to the moral,
than he who subdues the world. He is a
stronger man who moulds society mentally
and morally, who is able to direct the for-
ces of the mind and soul, than he who suc-
cessfully fights with bulls and bears, or over-
comes a giant in mortal combat. He is
more a brave man, a hero, who dares to do
right, to brave popular indignation, than he
who stands unappalled at the cannon's
mouth; the one is as far above the other as
the mind and soul are superior to the body.

	THE designs for the twenty cent silver
piece were selected and approved lately by
Hon. H. R. Linderman, director of the
mint. The adverse design contains a sit-
ting figure of Liberty, with the word "Lib-
erty" inscribed on a shield, the whole sur-
rounded by thirteen stars.  Beneath the
figure is the date 1875.  On the reverse
side is the figure of an eagle, with the
words "twenty cents." The edge of the
coin will be perfectly smooth, in order to
distinguish it from the twenty-five cent
coin.
