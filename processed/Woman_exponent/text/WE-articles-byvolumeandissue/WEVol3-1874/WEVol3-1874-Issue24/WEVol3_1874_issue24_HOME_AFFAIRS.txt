HOME AFFAIRS.

	PROFESSOR W. H. Bremer, of Yale College,
recently visited Salt Lake.
	THE MAY term of the Third District Court
commenced Monday morning, May 3rd, Judge
Lowe presiding.
	THIS ISSUE ends the third volume of the
WOMAN'S EXPONENT. Send in your subscrip-
tions, friends, for the fourth volume.
	THE UTAH Western Railway Company have
received a new locomotive, the "Edward Hun-
ter;" also new passenger and freight cars.
	PROF. JOHN M. Coyner, Ph. D., principal of
the Salt Lake City Collegiate Institute, expects
to issue a monthly periodical devoted to educa-
tion.  The first number will appear about the
middle of June.
	ON THE first of May most of the schools and
many private parties indulged in rambles over
the hills and through the vallies, around the
city. There was one excursion of children and
young people to American Fork Canyon.  The
Mason's Relief Fund Excursion, composed of
about three hundred persons, went to Lake
Point. Wetzel's band in attendance. They
had a sail on the Lake in the steamer. The
entertainment and accomodations at Clinton's
Hotel are highly spoken of.
