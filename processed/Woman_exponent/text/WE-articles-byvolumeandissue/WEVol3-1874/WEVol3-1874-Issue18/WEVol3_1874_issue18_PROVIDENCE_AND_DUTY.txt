PROVIDENCE AND DUTY.

     People of the world are sometimes impressed 
with what are called “special providences,” not
realizing that our Father orders all things;
even our trials and what we call our misfor-
tunes, which are many times our greatest bless-
ings. We are taught as Latter-day Saints, that
by our course of conduct in a prior existence,
we predestinated ourselves to the positions 
which we occupy here; and by our course of
conduct here, we will be entitled to certain po-
sitions hereafter. Then, as children of a wise
Father, who have been privileged to receive
light and intelligence from Him, in a greater,
degree than many others of His creatures, how
faithful and diligent should we be in the per-
formance of every duty that is revealed to us 
from heaven.  Laboring earnestly in this King- 
dom will enable us to increase in that intelli-
gence which comes from God until we will fi-
nally arrive at such a state of perfection that
we can do His will here on earth, as it is done
in heaven by the angels.				C.R.
