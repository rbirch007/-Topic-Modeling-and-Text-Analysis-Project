FLORAL HINTS.
				St. Geo. Jan. 31st, 1875.
DEAR EXPONENT:—
	Your flower-loving readers have doubtless
considered our advice about their house
plants, and find them probably thriving and
doing finely—If they are not, let them stir
the soil around the plants and fertilize with
liquid manure, and once a week, put about a
teaspoonful of aqua ammonia in the water
used for a good sized plant; also give each
pot a little fine charcoal, which will cause
the bloom to be of brighter color and more
lovely.—
	If you have seeds to plant, use a little
warm water, with a small amount of spirits
of camphor poured into it. In this soak the
seeds a few hours, which will so fertilize
them, that even old seeds, and almost life-
less, will grow.
	If any one should desire to have a beauti-
ful vine trailed in the window, let them
take the tuber of a maderia vine, plant in a
pot in the usual way and they will soon
have abundants of the finest glossy, wax-
like foliage, which will need sticks to wind
on and care to keep it from injury, as the
young runners are very tender and easily
broken.
	If you would have bulbous flowers in the
time, your Tulips and Hyacinths should
now be planted. 			FLORA.
