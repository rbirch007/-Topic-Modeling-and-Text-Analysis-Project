HOME AFFAIRS.

	AN ITEM worthy of attention is that dried
peaches from Utah are worth from four to five
cents more in the States than those raised and
cured there.
	TRAINS ON the Utah Western rail road now
run daily between this city and Lake Point,
Clinton’s landing. And work on the Utah
Southern is progressing so finely, it is antici-
pated that early in the coming summer, the
cars will run as far South as Nephi.
	Mr. Haynes and son, of this city—lately from
Wales—have commenced the manufacture of
chain, such as is used in logging, &c. We are
always pleased to note any advancement in the
scale of progression; and one particularly in-
teresting feature in this branch of industry,
new to our Territory, is that the Messrs Haynes
can manufacture a better article at less cost than
can be obtained by importation.
	THE SALT Lake Fire Brigade have chosen
the anniversary of Washington’s birthday, for
a grand dress ball, to be given at the Salt Lake
Theatre. That the occasion will be a most
entertaining one, cannot be doubted, so well
known and generally appreciated are the noble
efforts of our Firemen, whose energies are so
liberally employed for the public good. We
acknowledge the favor of a complimentary in-
vitation.
	HIS EXCELLENCY, Governor Axtell, among
the first of his official proceedings as governor
of Utah, has shown good judgment and an
honorable purpose by straightway performing
some of the duties uselessly omitted or will-
fully neglected by his late predecessor, ex-Gov-
ernor Woods. Prominent among these official
acts was the issuing to Hon. George Q. Cannon
the certificate of election as delegate to Con-
gress from this Territory.
