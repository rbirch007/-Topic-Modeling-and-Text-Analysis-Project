R. S. REPORTS.
			TOOELE CITY, Feb. 2nd, 1875
Dear Editor :—
	I have been a subscriber to the EXPONENT
since its commencement, and feel now that
I could not do without it. To me, one beau-
tiful feature is, that through its columns we
see the advancement of our sisters in the
Relief and Retrenchmeet Societies through
the territory. And as we are all striving
for the same high object,expecting to obtain
and share it all together finally, we feel
great interest in each others welfare. The
Societies in Tooele are doing well, and I
think, are accomplishing great good. The
President and Counselors in the Relief Society
are alive to their duties and the Teachers look
well after the wants of the poor. And we
all feel blest not alone in performing our
temporal duties, but also in meeting often
together to speak to each other of the good-
ness of God. The young ladies’ association
is also prospering. They take great delight
in attending their meetings and trying to
live worthy of being called Daughters of
Zion. The Exponent has not told us of
late, about the Fourteenth Ward General
Retrenchment meetings. I cannot believe
they (the sisters) have given up those re
freshing meetings. Pray let them know
that their sisters love to partake with them
of the good things they feast upon through
the EXPONENT.
	Your Sister in the Gospel,
