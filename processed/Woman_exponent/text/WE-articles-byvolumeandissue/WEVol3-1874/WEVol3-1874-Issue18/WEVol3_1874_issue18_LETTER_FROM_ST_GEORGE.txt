LETTER FROM ST. GEORGE

					Jan. 23rd, 1875
DEAR EDITOR:
	We are enjoying ourselves here in this de-
lightful climate. My husband's health has
not been better for years. President Young's
health is pretty good; he rides out almost
every day to the temple to advise and coun-
sel the workmen. There are so many things
to look after it keeps him, my husband, and
many others, on the look out continually,  to
keep so many men at work advantageously.
There are about three hundred in all. We
are very comfortably situated here, at the
St. George House, where we have taken
rooms and board. The sisters of the Relief
Society have donated to the Temple, one
hundred dollars since we have been here.
On the last Fast day, there was over fifty
dollars in money, in fifty-cent offerings, do-
nated by the brethren and sisters. We have
had another gentle rain of three or four
days, which hindered the workmen on the
Temple, but gladdened the hearts of the
farmers. The Temple walls are expected to
be ready for the roof, by the first of April;
there are twenty feet of masonry yet to be
laid. They have here, Sunday Schools in
the forenoon, and Sacramental meetings in
the afternoon. The brethren are giving
historical lectures once a week, the young
men's Historical Club meets once a week
and they are having theatres twice a week.
The weather is pleasant again. May pros-
perity attend your valuable paper. We ex-
pect to start for home about the tenth of
February next.
