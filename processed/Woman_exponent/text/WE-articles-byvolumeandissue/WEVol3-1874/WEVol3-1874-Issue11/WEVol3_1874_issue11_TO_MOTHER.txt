TO MOTHER.

	Yes mother you were tired; the day had
brought perplexing cares, and press of work,
and you were nervous. But your little
daughter had her troubles too, her sorrows
were as deep as she could bear; and before
she went to God, she came to you. She told
you of temptation which overpowered. She
had been conquered in the fight - for there
had been a fight— and she wanted confes-
sion and forgiveness, for her relief. How
did you fill her need? Forgetting yourself
did you enter into her feelings and give her
the help over the hard place, which will
bring her the next time to you with renew-
ed confidence that mother is the best friend,
even when sin has been committed—that
telling mother makes it easier to pray about?
Or did you forget the full table of forgive-
ness to which God has treated you, and deal
out to the hungry little one, the cold crums
of reproach and condemnation. God forgive
you, if you did,-and-help you set a better
table in future. God help you to keep a
cheery house in your heart for the children
in all their moods, in all their failings. Con-
sidering thyself, lest thou also be tempted
like them.—"The Christian at Work."


"Dear George said an Indianapolis young
woman, "I am willing to marry you if we
have to live on bread and water."
	"Well," said the enthusiastic George,
"you furnish the bread, and I'll skirmish
around and try and find the water."
