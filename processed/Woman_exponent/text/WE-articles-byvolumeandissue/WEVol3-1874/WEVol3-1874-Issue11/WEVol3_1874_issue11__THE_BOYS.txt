THE BOYS.
	Do their mothers know where they are or
what company they keep? A great crowd of
them, mostly small ones, gathered on the
street in front of the city market, on Saturday
last, while a large herd of cattle was going
past. Some of the little fellows ran heedlessly
in amongst the stock which was jaming, crowd-
ing and hurrying along, as if realizing no dan-
ger, until one of them was struck and knocked
down by the foot of a horse upon which one of
the cattle drivers rode. Fortunately, the boys
all scampered out of the way then, and no fur-
ther mischief was done. The one that was
struck limped a little, but showed no signs of
having been seriously hurt. He has kind
Providence to thank, however, that he was not
trampled to death in the midst of the rushing
herd.
