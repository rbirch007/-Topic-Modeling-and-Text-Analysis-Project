COURTING.
	With more than ordinary interest we have
watched for some time past the proceedings of
United States officials in this Territory. And
some of them, we conclude, have hearts and
minds of sufficient magnitude to enable them
to court any body and every body; no matter
who, provided they can steal a chance where
there is a prospect of something to be gained.
	Whether their purpose is to amuse the people
in general, or to instruct them concerning par-
ticular duties and privileges, is not clearly de-
monstrated by their acts. But certain it is,
that the constant diatribe which they carry on
against the leading men in the communities,
is not calculated to engender a spirit of peace,
or to bring enlightenment to the minds of the
people.
	The query might well be propounded, what
is their business? Are they in the midst of
the people to preserve the peace, or as disturb-
ers of the same? If the former, they and their
assigners have mistaken their abilities. The
peace and good feelings which existed among


the people in Utah, prior to the interception of
out-side influences prominent among which
may be marked the doings of certain govern-
ment appointees, have been greatly disturbed
rather than increased by the subjugation
which these individuals have attempted. Yet,
from the official appellations by which they are
known, it would be supposed that they are
placed among the people as guardians of their
rights, arbitrators by whom the peace would 
be most heartily sustained, instead of watch-
ers for iniquity, to stir up strife and create
troubles that they may have causes of offense
to examine and judge. But the people can only
continue to watch patiently for final results.
