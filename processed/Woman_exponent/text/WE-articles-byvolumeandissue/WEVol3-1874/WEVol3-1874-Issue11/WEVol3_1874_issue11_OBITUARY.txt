OBITUARY.
	Died, in this city, at her residence in the
14th Ward, at 8 P. M., on the 25th inst.,
Eleanor Jane Pratt, wife of the late Elder
Parley P. Pratt, in the fifty-seventh year of
her age.
	Sister Pratt was born in Wheeling, Va.,
on the 29th of December, A. D. 1818. Was
baptized into the church of Jesus Christ of
Latter-day Saints in San Francisco by Elder
McBride, May 23rd, in the year 1854. Since
which time she has been known as a woman
of integrity and much zeal in the cause
of truth, a faithful and true Latter-day
Saint to the end of her mortal career.
	"Blessed are the dead who die in the
Lord, for they rest from their labors and
their works do follow them."
	Sister Eleanor emigrated to this city in
1855, 19 years ago. Since that period the
writer has been intimately acquainted with
her. She was a woman of sorrow; well
acquinted with grief. Of her it can be tru-
ly said, "She came up through much tribu-
lation."
	She taught school on Thursday, and pre-
pared her breakfast Friday morning. After
which she was suddenly taken with ap-
oplexy; sent for Bishop Taylor, conversed
freely with him in regard to her business
affairs, leaving everything in his charge;
he administered to her and she went to bed
and sank into a deep sleep from which she
never awoke in mortality. But she awakes
in the world of spirits, clothed in white
robes, with palms of victory in her hands,
where with the blest she can shout, "Oh
death where is thy sting! oh grave where
is thy victory!"			W. E.
