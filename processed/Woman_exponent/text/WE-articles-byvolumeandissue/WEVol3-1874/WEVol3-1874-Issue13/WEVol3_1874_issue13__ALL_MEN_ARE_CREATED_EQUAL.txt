ALL MEN ARE CREATED EQUAL.

	Many men, many minds—many judges,
many judgments. In Illinois, the judges
in the Supreme Court held that the maxim of
independence, "all men are created equal,"
does not extend to women, and that by vir-
tue thereof, or of anything else, they have
no right of suffrage. In the same State,
another Supreme Court decides that this
maxim does apply to vagrant children, so
that a statute providing for the rescue of
such "little wanderers," and the commital
of them to a reformatory school is unconsti-
tutional, and a "tyrannical and oppressive"
infringement upon the liberties of the citi-
zens. In effect, therefore, juvenile va-


grancy receives judicial sanction, and the
State is powerless to protect and save desti-
tute minors and orphans! We thought
"Salus populi suprema lex."—Canada Law
Jrournal.
