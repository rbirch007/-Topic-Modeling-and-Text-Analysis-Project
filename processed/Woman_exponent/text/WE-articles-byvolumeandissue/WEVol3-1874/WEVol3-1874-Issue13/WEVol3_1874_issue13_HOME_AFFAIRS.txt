HOME AFFAIRS.

	AT HAMPTON'S Station, Box Elder Co., the
postoffice has been discontinued.
	A FREE University Library and Reading
Room is open at the University Building every
evening—Sundays excepted we suppose—from
6 till 10.
	MR. JAMES Dunn has recently been appoint-
ed superintendent of the Provo Woolen Fac-
tory; Bishop Myron Tanner having resigned
that position.
	IN CACHE county, nearly all the young men
are appointed home missionaries, to visit in
turns the different settlements—generally in
company with elder brethren—to bear testi-
mony to the truth of the Gospel and learn to
illustrate its principles. Exceedingly favorable
results are apparent from the adoption of this
plan.
	AN INTERESTING account is given of a con-
ference for Morgan and northern Summit
counties, lately held in Morgan city, Morgan
Co., Utah. The reports of Bishops from the
various settlements which they represented
were very satisfactory, showing that in general,
the spirit of the Saints in those parts was to
seek unitedly the interests of the Kingdom of
God. Bishop W. W. Cluff presided, and Sam-
uel Francis was clerk of the Conference.
