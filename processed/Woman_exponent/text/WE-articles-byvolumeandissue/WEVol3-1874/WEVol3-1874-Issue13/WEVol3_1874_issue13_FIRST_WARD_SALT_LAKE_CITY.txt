	FIRST WARD, SALT LAKE CITY,
		 			   	Dec.	15th	1874.
DEAR EDITOR:—
	We are improving, as we hope to con-
tinue doing, through the blessings of the
Lord in whom we trust, as a Relief Society
and as individuals. We endeavor to be al-
ways on hand to assist the poor or to aid in
any good undertaking. We had a general
meeting on Thanksgiving Day, and had our
sisters from the city, Miss Eliza R. Snow,
Mrs. M. I. Horne, Mrs. Zina D. Young, Mrs.
P. Woodruff, Mrs. E. Howard, Mrs. W.
East, Mrs. S.M. Kimball and Mrs. M. Young.
The principles of unity, Celestial Marriage
and the training of children were the chief
subjects spoken upon. Besides the sisters
named, remarks were made by our Presi-
dent Mrs. M.G. Chase, and her Counselors
Mrs. Julia Adams and Mrs. Sidney Thayne;
also by our Bishop, Joseph Warburton. A
good spirit prevailed and we felt that it truly
was a Thanksgiving Day.
		MRS. JANE E. VAN TASSELL,
							Secretary. 
		
	The Relief Society of Smithfield, Cache
county, is reported by the Secretary, Mrs.
Jane S. Coleman, to be still faithfully striv-
ing to comfort and bless the poor in their
midst, and to promote the interests of the
Kingdom of God. They have lately made
four bed quilts for a donation towards build-
ing the Temple.
