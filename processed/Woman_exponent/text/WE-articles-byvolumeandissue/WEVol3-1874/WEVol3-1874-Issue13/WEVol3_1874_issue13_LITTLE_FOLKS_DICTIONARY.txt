LITTLE FOLKS' DICTIONARY.

	A writer in the Schoolday Magazine has
gathered together the following dictionary
of words as defined by certain small people
here and there:
	Back-biter—A flea.
	Bed-time—Shut-eye-time.
	Dust—Mud with the juice all squeezed
out.
	Fan—A thing to brush the warm off
with.
	Fins—A flshe’s wings.
	Ice—Water that stayed out in the cold
and went to sleep.
	Monkey—A very little boy with a tail.
	Nest-egg—The egg the old hen measures
by to make new ones.
	Pig—A hog’s little boy.
	Salt—What makes your potato taste bad
when you don’t put any on.
	Snoring—Letting off sleep.
	Snow—Rain all poped out white.
	Stars—The moon’s eggs.
	Wakefulness—Eyes all the time coming
unbu toned.
	A Small Catechism—Kittenchism.
