MRS. L. G. RICHARDS:-
	Although the enclosed is a private com-
munication, I think its publication may be
of much suggestive benefit to the Young
Ladies' Associations generally. With this
view, I forward it to you, and trust that the
young ladies of Farmington will pardon
this liberty.	Yours,
					E.R. SNOW.
		FARMINGTON, Nov. 27th, 1874.
DEAR SISTER SNOW:—
	According to your instructions we have
formed a set of resolutions, of which we
send you a copy. The Bp. has read them,
and thinks they are just what we need. We
have also presented them to the Associa-
tion and they are determined to try to keep
them. We are now having meetings once
a week, and always have very good ones.
The sisters respond promptly when called
upon to speak or perform any other duty, and
we realize that the meetings are doing a
vast amount of good.
	Our association numbers one hundred at
present, and increases in number every
meeting.
	Hoping to hear from you when you can
make it convenient, and praying God to
bless you in all your endeavors, the young
ladies all join in sending love.
	Yours Truly,
			Pres. LUCY A. CLARK.
		Sec. MINERVA E. RICHARDS.
	P. S. The young men have also been or-
ganized, are holding meetings once a week
and forming Resolutions.
					M. E. R. Sec.
