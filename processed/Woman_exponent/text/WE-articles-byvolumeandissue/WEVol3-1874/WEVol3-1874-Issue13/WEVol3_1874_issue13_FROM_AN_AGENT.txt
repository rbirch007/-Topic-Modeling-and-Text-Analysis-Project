		FROM AN AGENT.

			BEAVER, Dec. 5th, 1874.
DEAR SISTER RICHARDS:
	I have just returned from Minersville,
where I went to visit, and also to canvas
for the EXPONENT. The sisters of the Re-
lief Society all seem in good faith, and anx-
ious to have the paper, but only a few were
able to subscribe more than one dollar. I
advised them if they could do no better to
send for half the year; they seemed pleased
with that suggestion, and accordingly, I re-
ceived five names, with one dollar each.
When that has expired I will see to the re-
newals. Sister Tyler is sick with inflamma-
tory rheumatism and was not able to accom-
pany me; when she recovers we shall en-
deavor to visit the other settlements, and
obtain what we can.  So you will please to
send to those five subscribers for six months.
I shall get more names from that place in
time.  I told the sisters I wished them to
bear in mind that the EXPONENT was a pa-


per edited and sustained by women, and for
that very reason we could not afford to let
it fail; they seemed fully to coincide with
my sentiments; besides they admire the
character of the matter contained in the
paper.
			Very restpectfully yours,
						L. B. Pratt.
