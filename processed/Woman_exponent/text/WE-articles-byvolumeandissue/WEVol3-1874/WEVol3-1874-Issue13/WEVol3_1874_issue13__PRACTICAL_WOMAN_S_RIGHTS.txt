PRACTICAL WOMAN’S RIGHTS
	We have found a woman’s rights woman
in a practical sense, one who puts into prac-
tice the theories of the strong-minded, with
a strong purpose. We refer to Mrs. Bower
of Ottawa. Left a widow with two small
children on her hands, she at once concluded
to take care of them and herself. She en-
gaged in selling musical instruments and in
the short space of four years has established
a business of twenty thousand dollars a year.
She has never allowed a note to go to pro-
test or failed to pay promptly all her liabili-
ties. She employs four men with teams and
wagons which she owns, to sell instruments.
She has lately added sewing machines to
her stock. She buys in all cases for cash, and
sells nothing on commission. Her business
now affords a profit of $4,000 per year above
expenses. Here is a woman of whom nerve-


less, worthless, silly simpering women, may,
if they will, learn very valuable lessons in
self-reliance and self-support.—"Earlville
Transcript."
