HOME AFFAIRS.

	WE HAVE been requested to announce that
on and after Friday, April 2nd, mulberry out-
tings can be obtained at Mrs. Zina H. Young's
residence, 13th Ward.  They will be prepared
for setting out, and will be placed in the ground
for preservation until called for. Of course the
earlier they are removed the safer and better
for them. The sisters who are desirous of pro-
curing a supply, are therefore requested to call
as early as possible.
	GOVERNOR AXTELL issued a proclamation,
March 22nd, assigning Associate-Justice Emer-
son to the Third Judicial district, the removal
of Chief-Justice McKean having left the bench
of this district vacant until the arrival of 
Chief-Justice Lowe.
	CAPTAIN SILVA, successor to Major Overton,
as receiver at the Land Office in this city, re-
cently arrived.
	THE FOURTH term of the 20th Ward Semina-
ry commenced Monday, March 22nd. Professor
Karl G. Maeser, Principal. The Professor has
been delivering lectures on highly interesting


subjects, in several of the different Wards late-
ly, which have drawn full houses and been par-
ticularly instructive and entertaining.
	ELDER ORSON Pratt preached to a crowded
congregation in the Thirteenth Ward Assembly
Rooms, March 21st. His subject was the
Priesthood.		
	THE GOOD work of grading is progressing on
South Temple Street, east of the State Road.
	THE POSITION of Penitentiary warden in this
city has been accepted by Mr. John Hunt, Mr.
A. G. Paddock having sometime since resigned.
	AMONG THE deaths of recent occurrence are
those of Sister Openshaw, who fell dead with-
out a moment's warning, in this city on the
24th ult.; and the well-known father Kay of
Kaysville, who quietly departed on the morn-
ing of the 25th. Both were in good standing in
the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day
Saints, of which they were old members.
	MR. J. H. Beatty has been appointed to the
office of register in bankruptcy for this judicial
district.
