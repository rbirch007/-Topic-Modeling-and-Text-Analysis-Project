WOMAN'S EXPONENT.


For the EXPONENT.
		RESIGNATION.
Out in the frozen night,
	Moonbeams falling round,
Chilling moonbeams, wan and white,
	Asleep on the dreary ground.
One brillant, lone, bright star,
	Seems resting gently now—
Where a dark line meets the open sky,
	On the mountain's rugged brow.
So near to the verge it seems,
	Looking up from where I stand,
That a child would think, could it gain that spot.
	It might grasp it with outstretched hand.
And the thought comes over my soul,
	With a bitter, painful start,
It is even thus with the brilliant hopes
	That rest in the human heart.
They seem so near to our lives;
	So firm in their beauty clear,
That we dream not their source is far out in heaven
	While only the light falls here;
'Till the clouds of this care-worn world,
	Drifting up in their gloom and wrath,
Hide the beautiful rays, and we find no more,
	Their tracings along our path.
And shadows creep over the soul,
	Their presence pierce eye and brain
With voiceless cries for the vanished light,
	'Till the heart grows still with pain.
And sometimes out from that silence deep,
	Breaks a whisper low and light,
Chasing the sorrow from eyes that weep,
	Changing the gloomiest night.
And our souls are filled with that holy love
	Coming down from Eternity's throne,
As we murmur with patient and pleading hearts
	"God's will is above our own!"
Out in the solemn night,
	Moonbeams falling round,
Beautiful moonbeams, warm and bright,
	Veiling the frozen ground.
And the mountains bleak in their rugged might,
	Are a covert for peaceful walls;
The star looks down with a tender light,
	A tear-drop starts—and falls.
