				JANE E. MOLEN.

	From the First Ward, we have received
full reports of the meetings of the Young
Ladies' Retrenchment Association since Jan.
20th, 1875. That they are making great
progress is plainly apparent by their min-
utes. One of their principle subjects has
been faith, which has been ably commented
upon by their President, Mrs. Sarah Gra-
ham, also Supt. Mrs. Elizabeth Higgs and
others. Obedience was another subject
touched upon, and also the fulfillment of
duty, especially in each one bearing her
testimony, the improvement which those
had made, who had been willing to do so,
and the blessings of the Spirit of God
which had attended them in their efforts.
They were exhorted by members of the so-
ciety in regard to visiting the sick, com-
forting those who were tried, and helping,
by their united efforts, to strengthen each
other. A general spirit of improvement,
union and mutual benefit is manifested by

[Column 2]
them, and their aim is to advance in the 
knowledge of those things which pertain to
life and salvation. They receive encour-
agement from the presence and good words
of Mrs. Margaret Steel from time to time.
Miss Sarah Smith, the Secretary, shows
rapid improvement in the position which
she holds.
