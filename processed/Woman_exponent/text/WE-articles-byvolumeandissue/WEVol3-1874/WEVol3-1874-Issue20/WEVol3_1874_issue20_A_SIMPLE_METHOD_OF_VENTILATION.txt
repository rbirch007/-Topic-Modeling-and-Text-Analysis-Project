	A SIMPLE METHOD OF VENTILATION.—


A piece of wood, three inches high and ex-
actly as long as the breadth of the window;
is to be prepared.  Let the sash be now
raised, the slip of wood placed on the sill,
and the sash let down closely upon it.  If the
slip has been well fitted, there will be no
draft in consequence of the displacement of
the sash at its lower part, but the top of the
lower sash will overlap the bottom of the
upper one, and between the bars perpendic-
ular currents of air, not felt, will enter and 
leave the room.
	SCARLET FEVER.—Upon the recommen-
dation of Schneeman, the anointing of the
body with fat in cases of scarlet fever has 
been extensively practised in Germany for
a number of years.  Cocoa butter is recom-
mended for this purpose. In severe fevers,
the entire surface of the body should be
rubbed every hour or at least once in four
hours.  The same treatment is recommend-
ed for typhoid fever,and other inflammatory
diseases, especially inflammatory rheuma-
tism, and tuberculosis.
	S. C. HALL, a distinguished English
writer, whose wife is equally well known as
an authoress, says in a letter to the Herald
of Health: "Thank God, though most three
score and ten—I by three years and my
wife by not much less—we enjoy good
health; are still at work.  I published a book 
in 1820, and Mrs. Hall began to write in 
1825, soon after our marriage, the 20th of
September, 1824.  We have a happy retro-
spect, for which we give God thanks, and a 
prospect that we shall be together, grateful
and prayerful to Him through eternity.  To
temperance we owe our health, to faith and
trust in God our many blessings.
