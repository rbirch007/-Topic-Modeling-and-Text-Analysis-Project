	UNITED ORDER:

	We solicit your united influence in sus-
taining home straw manufacture. Our ob-
ject in this is to assist in establishing home
industries in accordance with the require-
ments of the Priesthood; to contribute our
mite to the great enterprise of our becoming
a self-sustaining people. We want the sig-
nature of those who feel willing to sustain
this movement by purchasing our hats,
which, if we can get sufficient encourage- 
ment, we shall make for men and boys, wo-
men and girls at prices averaging from about
fifty cents—which will be suitable for men’s
every day wear in the field—to one dollar
and seventy-five cents for fine wear.
	We hope to supply this city in this de-
partment. 
	E.R. Snow,				E.S. BARNEY,
	BATHSHEBA SMITH,		A RUMELL,
	RACHEL GRANT,		E. RUMELL,
P. S.—If any of the sisters have braid on
hand, we wish to purchase. Second house
west of St. Mark's church, 13th Ward.
