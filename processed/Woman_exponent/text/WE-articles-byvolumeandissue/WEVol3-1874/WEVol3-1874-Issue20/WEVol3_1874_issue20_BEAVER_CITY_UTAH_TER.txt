			BEAVER CITY, UTAH TER.,
						March 16, 1875.
DEAR EXPONENT:
	The Young Ladies' Retrenchment Asso-
ciation of this place held a party in the Re-
lief Society house on Saturday, 13th inst.
At two o'clock p.m. the young ladies met
to hold their regular meeting, which con-
tinued until half past four, when there was
an intermission of two hours. Then, the
parents and friends of the members having
arrived, the exercises—which consisted of
songs, speeches, original essays, readings
and declamations—were commenced. At
eight o'clock the company partook of a sup-
per provided by the young ladies. Good
instructions were given by Presidents
Fotheringham and Tyler, and Brother Mes-
senger. The party was dismissed by prayer
by Brother Wm. Flake.
	The Association has sixty-four members;
the meetings, which occur once in two
weeks, are well attended. Last month we
donated sixteen dollars to the St. George
Temple.
		Yours Respectfully,
				ELIZA E. PARKINSON,
							Secretary.
