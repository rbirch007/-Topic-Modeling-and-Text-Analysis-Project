	     WOMAN, HER SPHERE.

	In the first place, woman is to be a help-
meet to man. This sentence alone comprises
a great deal; it is not to be expected that
she will do a man's work but she should not
feel degraded in performing her household
duties as far as lays in her power; by doing
this she can become a great help to her hus-
band. Another very important thing; she
should improve her mind by the aid of every
good and useful book, for this is a duty she
owes to herself and also to her posterity.
We can scarcely realize what a benefit it is
to children to have a mother who is well
educated and who understands her religion
for herself as is her privilege. They are
taught correct language and true principles
from the beginning, consequently they grow
up in the right path and when this is the
case they are not likely to depart from the
truth. On the other hand it is reprehensi-
ble in the highest degree for a mother to
waste her time reading novels or any of the
foolish literature with which the world
abounds. In regard to fashion; there are
higher and nobler things that should occupy
her thoughts. She should not dress to look
odd and different from every body else but
she can dress with neatness and taste with-
out taking pattern from any one, or racking
her brain for hours; and perhaps days, to
take or obtain something she has seen
somebody else wear. In fine she should
strive to be a Saint in every thought, word


and deed, and this will teach her at all times
the right course to pursue.
