A HOUSEHOLD ORNAMENT.—"A small
wire basket lined with various colored tis-
sue paper, nicely fringed hands in one cor-
ner of the room; a bunch of gay Southern
moss hangs in wavy flakes around the bas- 
ket. In the basket sets a two pound oyster 
can, filled with water and a sweet potato.
It has sent out a vine several feet in length,
guided by strings over the clock, around
picture frames, and twining through man-
tle ornaments, adding beauty, and cheerful-
ness to the room. It may not be generally
known that with a vessel kept full of clear
water in a warm room a sweet potato vine
will grow all winter.  It is not necessary
that the sun shines upon it."
