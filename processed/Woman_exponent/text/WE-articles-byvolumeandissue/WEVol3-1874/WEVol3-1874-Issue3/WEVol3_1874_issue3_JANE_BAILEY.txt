					JANE BAILEY.

		SPRING CITY June 9th, 1874.
Dear Editor :—
    We held our annual meeting June 4th.
It should have been May 4th, but President
Mary A. Hyde wished to attend conference,
and the sisters thought they would defer
it until her return. The transactions of the
year were reviewed, the Secretary’s, and
Treasurer’s report read, showing the Society
to be in good condition. We have done
more in the past year than in any one year
before, and all the sisters manifest a spirit
to continue in the work. President Mary
A. Hyde called a vote to see how many of
the Society were willing to join the United
Order. They were all willing to join it and
to do all the good they could. We are not
organized in it yet, as a society but will be
as soon as practicable. We are enjoying
general good health and have excellent
meetings; and hope God will bless all who
desire and seek to do good.
				P. E. Benson, Sec’y

     The Secretary writes from Fairview, San-
pete Co. The Relief Society of this place
met on the 13th of June, to celebrate their
6th anniversary. After the customary pre-
paratory proceedings, Bishop A. Tucker
expressed his satisfaction in having an op-
portunity of meeting with the society, spoke
in praise of their labors and financial con-
dition and said they had proved a blessing
to the people here. Mrs. Mary A. Prichett
was unanimously sustained as President,
Mrs Rebecca Tucker, first counselor. Mrs.
Hannah Cheeney was honorably released
from acting as second counselor on account
of old age, and Mrs. Peterson was elected in
her stead. Mrs. Tilitha Avery was sus-
tained as Secretary and Mrs. Sarah Terry as


Treasurer. A selected essay from the WO-
MAN'S EXPONENT was then read followed
by poetry, remarks from the Bishop, solo on
the Organ, a general attraction to pies and
cakes, toasts and the announcement of a
dance for the evening. Meeting closed
with singing and prayer.
			MARY A. PRICHETT, Prest.
