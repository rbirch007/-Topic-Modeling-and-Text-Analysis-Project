DEAR EXPONENT:—
	In reflecting on our position as the chosen
people of God, I sometimes think there are
many, especially among the young, who do
not realize the importance of living our re-
ligion strictly. If we did, fashion and pride
would have less foothold in our midst and
instead of being lifted up with pride be-
cause of our worldly possessions, the greater
our blessings the more humble we would be.
We see many of our people associating
with, and seeking the friendship of the
pleasant, agreeable gentlemen(?) who come
in our midst with the avowed intention of
doing all in their power to pull down "Mor-
monism." Some of the girls even get mar-
ried to them. Oh! girls where is your faith
in the religion you were born in or have
embraced? You know that you are casting
aside almost all hopes of an exaltation in
the Celestial Kingdom of our Heavenly
Father. You may plead the excuse that
they intend to join the Church after a while.
Then let them do so, and prove themselves
worthy of you before you risk your exalta-
tion in their hands. Better, far better be
the second wife of a good, faithful Latter-
day Saint, one that would exalt you, than
the only wife of a young man that is op-
posed to our religion, or any principle of it.
Let us not imagine, my young brethren and
sisters, that there is nothing for us to do be-
cause we are young, for such is not the case,
we will find all we can do, if we are prayer-
ful and seek to build up the Kingdom of
God. We have no time to lose in idleness;
it will take all the few years allotted to us,
to obtain an exaltation, and we should be
thankful if we can obtain it by a lifetime
devotion.					P. A. M.
	Richmond, Cache Co.
