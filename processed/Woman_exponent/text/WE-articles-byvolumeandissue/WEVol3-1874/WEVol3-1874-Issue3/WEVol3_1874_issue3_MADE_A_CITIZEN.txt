MADE A CITIZEN.

	We learn from the Salt Lake "Herald" that
Mrs. Elsa Johnson—a Swedish lady—was nat-
uralized in the Third District Court, on the 17th
ult,. and according to that spicy paper, "The
lady answered the questions in Judge
McKean's polygamic catechism without flinch-
ing."
	This, so far as we are aware, is the first in-
stance of the naturalization of a lady in this
Territory, but we see no reason why women
should not take advantage of the naturaliza-
tion law as well as men, when they would be
benefited by so doing.
