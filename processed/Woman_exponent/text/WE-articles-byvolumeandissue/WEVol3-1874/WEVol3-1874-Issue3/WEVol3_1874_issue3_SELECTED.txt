		   SELECTED.

	A row of bright little faces,
		A patter of dear little feet
	And loving arms closely entwining,
		Mother's warm kisses to meet.
	Three little cups on the table,
		Three little chairs by the wall,
	Three little curly heads nestled,
		Each on its pillow so small.Ah! who cannot read me this riddle of joy?
They're mother's own darlings, each dear little boy.

	Three little tear-stained faces,
		Little bare unwashed feet,
	Shrinkingly, timidly stealing,
		In terror, lest blows they meet.
	Bare dry bread on the table,
		Cold hard bench by the wall,
	Straw, and a scanty torn blanket,
		That will hardly cover them all.
No kisses to-night on each little brow;
God pity and help them! They are motherless now.
