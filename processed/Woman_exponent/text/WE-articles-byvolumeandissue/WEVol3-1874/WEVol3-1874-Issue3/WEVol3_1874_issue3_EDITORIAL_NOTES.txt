EDITORIAL NOTES.
	THE PRINCESS Bismarck, now past sixty
superintends her farm work.
	REV. MRS. Vancott, believed to be the first
woman ordained a minister by the Metho-
dist church, is a speaker of great power and
earnestness.
	THE LONDON University has decided, by a
vote of 83 to 65, that "it is desirable that
women should be permitted to take degrees
in the University."
	THE UNIVERSITY of Vermont decided
thatnothing in its charter or history would 
justify it in excluding ladies from any of 
its advantages enjoyed by men.
	THE IDEA of a famine doesn't trouble an
Iowa clergyman since a recent donation
party supplied him with beans sufficient to
last thirty-seven years.
	THE LOUISIANA flood has, it is calculated,
caused direct suffering to not less than 70,
000 people; and overflowed about 12,565,000
acres in the states of Louisiana, Mississippi
and Arkansas. We trust the benevolence
of the people will be equal to the needs of
our unfortunate countrymen.
