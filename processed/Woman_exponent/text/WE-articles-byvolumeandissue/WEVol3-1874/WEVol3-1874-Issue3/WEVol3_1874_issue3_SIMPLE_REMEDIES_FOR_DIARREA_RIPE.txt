	SIMPLE REMEDIES FOR DIARREA:—Ripe
fruits and berries, slightly acid, will remove
the ordinary diarreas of early summer.
	Common rice, parched brown like coffee,
and then boiled and eaten in the ordinary
way, without any other food, is, with per-
fect quietude of body, one of the most ef-
fective remedies for troublesome looseness
of the bowels.
	Some of the severest forms of that dis-
tressing ailment called dysentery, when
the bowels pass blood, with constant desire
and vain efforts to stool, are sometimes en-
tirely cured by the patient eating at the time
a heaping tablespoonful of raw beef, cut up
very fine, and repeated at intervals of four
hours, until cured, eating and drinking
nothing else in the meanwhile.—Halls'
Jour. of Health.
