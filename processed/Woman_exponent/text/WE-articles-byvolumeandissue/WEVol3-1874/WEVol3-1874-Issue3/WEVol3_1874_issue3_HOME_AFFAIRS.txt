HOME AFFAIRS.

	THE UNITED Order is said to be working well
in Beaver county and in the southern part of
Utah.
	ALL MAIL matter for Cache and Box Elder
counties is now carried by the Utah Northern 
Railroad.
	THE LADIES of Provo have petitioned the
city council to prohibit the sale of intoxicating
drinks except for medicinal purposes. 
	ELDERS DAVID McKenzie, John Henry
Smith, L. John Nuttall and Peter St. Clair
left on the 29th ult. to fulfil missions in Europe
	WE LEARN from the Deseret News that
"Bro. Horace S. Eldrege has a large quantity
of rye straw, which Relief Society ladies and
others desiring to braid can obtain by calling
upon him, free of charge."
	AN EARTHQUAKE, though not a severe one,
was plainly felt by many in this city on the
night of June 17th. The shock, though
rather light, was also felt in Weber and Summit
counties.
	A GRANITE POST, prepared by Morris and
Evans, is to be placed in the Great Salt Lake 
near Clinton's Hotel about a mile from Black
Rock. Its purpose is to denote the rise or fall
of the lake which is said to have become con-
siderably higher since the country was first
settled.
	THE OFFICES of City Water Master and Su-


pervisor of streets have formerly been held by
one person, but the duties of each having
grown so numerous and important as to require
the attention of one person alone, Mr. Wm.
Hyde has been appointed supervisor, Mr. Isaac
Groo retaining the office of general water mas-
ter.
