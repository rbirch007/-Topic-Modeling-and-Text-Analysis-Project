TO HUSBANDS.
	Women, like children, do better when praised 
and encouraged than they can when censured
and found fault with. Husband, if you enjoy
life at home better when your wife is sweet
tempered and wears a smiling face than you do
under the influence of fretful words and a
cloudy countenance, don’t be afraid to speak
to her in a pleasant way and even to address
her with endearing words.  She should be
beautiful to you when her face bears the impress
of a cheerful and loving soul; and you need
not consider it beneath your dignity to speak
to her of these things. They are things of im-
portance. Cheerful words and looks are the
sunshine of the heart, and they enliven and
gladden it as the sunshine of summer does the
verdure of earth. If kindly encouraged to do
so, will not a wife learn to cherish a mild, sweet,
spirit that she may be pleasing to the husband
she loves, even if she were naturally given to
cross irritable ways? We think she certainly


would in ninety-nine, cases out of every hun-
dred. And she will do more even than this
for your comfort if you will but let her know
that you appreciate her efforts. Praise her en-
deavors to make a home-heaven for you, though
she may not succeed so very well at first, and
with renewed courage and strengthed faith in
her own abilities to please you, and confidence
in your appreciation of her efforts she will
never fail to study your wishes and to exe-
cute them as far as possible.
