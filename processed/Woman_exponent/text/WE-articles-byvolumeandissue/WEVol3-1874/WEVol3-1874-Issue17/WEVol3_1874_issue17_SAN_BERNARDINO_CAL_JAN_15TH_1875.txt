	SAN BERNARDINO, CAL., Jan. 15th, 1875.
MRS. L. G. RICHARDS,
	DEAR SISTER:—Allow me one moment of
your valuable time, while I wish you all the
happiness you so richly deserve through the
New Year, now so young and blooming with
the many blessings of kind Heaven.  How
many there are who see no blessing in the
cold blasts of winter's frost and wind, or in
sickness, sorrow and death.  How few who
have the WILL to look for the "silvery lining
of the dark clouds." But I, with the "Stock-
ton Leader," do believe that if we only read
the WOMAN'S EXPONENT and try to live up
to the moral and intellectual teachings there
given, we shall learn to be all that our most
ardent desires can anticipate.  And with
such earthly blessings, we need have no
fears for the "other shore."
	Please continue to send EXPONENT to my
little namesake in Beaver City.  She writes
me that she learns to read many good and
beautiful pieces in her paper, and hopes to
be as good as they teach her to be.
	We are lonely since our dear old mother
went "home" to rest.  The last reading she
heard was from the EXPONENT, which she
appreciated very much.  Pardon the lengthy
form this letter has assumed; I thought to
detain you but a moment, yet I cannot close
without mentioning the death of another
one of the good old mothers.  "Old Mother
Pine" often enjoyed reading your paper,
spoke of you in terms of sisterly regard and
wished me to remember her kind love to
you.  It was herself and husband who
brought the grandmother of the Prophet Jo-
seph from the East to Kirtland, Ohio. She
died full of faith, and had entire confidence
in the work of the Lord. 
	May prosperity smile on your noble efforts,
is the prayer of your sister,
