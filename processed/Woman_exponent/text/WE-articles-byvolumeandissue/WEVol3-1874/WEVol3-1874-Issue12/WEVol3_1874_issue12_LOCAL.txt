				LOCAL.
	Work on the street railway from the Val-
ley House corner eastward is being dili-
gently prosecuted......Risley’s panoramic
mirror of England deserves better patronage
than it obtained here......A road to the newly
discovered coal mines, thirty miles from St.
George, is under way......The first real snow
storm of the season, in this valley, occurred
on the night of the 6th inst......The last
company of immigrants for this year arrived
in Ogden on Wednesday the 4th......There
has been a considerable emigration to
Dixie this season, from this and the north-
ern portion of the Territory......The prices 
for fish and meat in some of our city mar-
kets have lately been reduced.
		       POLITICAL
	Democracy will carry the country this
season by a large majority......China pays
Japan an indemnity of half a million tales
and settles the difficulties which have ex-
isted between them......The United States
internal revenue receipts for this year
amount to more than thirty five and a half
million dollars......The State Legislature of
Alabama has a Democrat majortiy of twen-
ty-nine; that of Missouri, eighty-three......
General Banks will probably be elected as
Speaker of the next House of Representa-
tives......Jubilations have been held by the
Democrats in different parts of the country
in honor of their victories.
		         PERSONAL
	The President of the Woman’s Congress
for the nsueing year, will be Miss Maria
Mitchell, the lady astronomer of Vassar
College......Garibaldi is to receive an annu-
ity of six thousand dollars from the muni-
cipality of Naples......Hon. W. Head of
Tennessee, who had just been elected to Con-
gress, died on the ninth inst......Queen Vic-
toria lately danced for the first time since
her widowhood......Adelina Patti is to re-
ceive a salary of $50,000 in the Paris opera
this season......Rosa Bonhuer is fifty-two,
unmarried and lives with a sister and sev-
eral brothers......Mrs. Logan, mother of
Senator Logan, recently died at Murphys-
boro, Illinois.
