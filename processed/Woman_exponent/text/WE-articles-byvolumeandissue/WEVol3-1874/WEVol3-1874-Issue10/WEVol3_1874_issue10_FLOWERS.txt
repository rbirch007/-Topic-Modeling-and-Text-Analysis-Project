FLOWERS.

	REMEMBER that success is more in the
man than in the country.
	GREATNESS lies not in being strong, but
in the right use of strength.
	THE certain way to be cheated is to fancy
one's self more cunning than others.
	STRIVE to be as kind, forbearing, and for-
giving as you can, both to friends and foes.
	HE that follows the Lord fully will find
goodness and mercy following him continu-
ally.
	IT should not discourage us if our kind-
ness is unacknowledged; it has its influence
still.
	LIFE is a voyage, in the progress of
which we are perpetually changing our
scenes.
	MANY run about after felicity, like an
absent-minded man hunting for his hat
while it is on his head or in his hand.
	IT is of no advantage to have a lively
mind if we are not just. The perfection of
the pendulum is not to go fast, but to be reg-
ular.


A VERY rich merchant who had an only
son, made his will, by which he gave all his
wealth, which amounted to 300,000 francs,
to certain monks, leaving them to give his
son such a sum as they wished. The mer-
chant died. The monks took all to them-
selves without wishing to give anything to
the heir. He complained to the Viceroy,
who, having seen the will, asked the monks
what they offered to the son. "Ten thou-
sand francs," they replied. "You wish,
then, to have all the rest?" "Yes my lord:
we demand the execution of the will." That
is just," said the Viceroy; "but you do not
understand it properly. It is said that the
son shall have that which you wish; you
grant ten to the son; it is then 290,000
francs that you want. Ah, well! following
the clause of the will, this sum is set apart
for the son. I order you to give it to him;
the 10,000 francs remaining are therefore
yours." They were obliged to submit.
