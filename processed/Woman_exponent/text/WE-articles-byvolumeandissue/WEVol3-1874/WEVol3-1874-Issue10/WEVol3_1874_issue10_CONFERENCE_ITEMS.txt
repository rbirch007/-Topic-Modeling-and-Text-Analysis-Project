CONFERENCE ITEMS.

	Never was a more glorious Indian Summer
known than the Saints were favored with dur-
ing the time of their late fall Conference. Not
even a gust of wind or a shower of rain occured
on either of the five days appointed for the
gathering together of the people, to prevent
even invalids f[-]om venturing forth. And those
who took advantage of the opportunities grant-
ed, for gaining instruction in the ways of li[-]e
and salvation, were uniformly blest inasmuch
as they treasure the good things offered from
the elders on the stand, and seek to live ac-
cording to the directions given.
	The health of President Young rendered it in-
consistent for him to speak to the congregation,
but he showed by his example, being at meet
ing each day, that he still holds to the doctrine
he has often preached in public, that it is good
for the Saints to congregate, and mingle in
prayer and praise, and the diffusing of knowl-
edge concerning heavenly things.
	During the Conference, discourses were deliv-
ered by President Geo. A. Smith and Elders C.
C. Rich, B. Young Jr., J. Taylor, W. Wood-
ruff, A. Carrington, F. D. Richards, O. Pratt,
G. Goddard, C. W. Penrose, G. Q. Cannon and
J. Van Cott.
	The instructions and exhortations given
were, as they have been in times past, calculated
to encourage oneness among the Saints; in or
der that they may the more readily become an
independent, self-sustaining people, hasten the
work of rearing temples to the most high God,
and in every way speed the progress of His
Kingdom upon the earth.
