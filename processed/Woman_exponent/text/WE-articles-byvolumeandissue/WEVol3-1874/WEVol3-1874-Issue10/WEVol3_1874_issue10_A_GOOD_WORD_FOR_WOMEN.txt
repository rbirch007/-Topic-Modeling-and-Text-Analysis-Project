A GOOD WORD FOR WOMEN.

	Let those who fear that the ladies of the 
land will be dragged down from their high
womanly position by a contact with politics,
that woman will not exercise the privilege
wisely, that she will not be true to her coun-
try, herself and her friends, come and wit-
ness an election day in Utah. Let them
compare it with the election days in other
places, and I will venture the assertion that
in this respect, at least, although living
among the mountains, they will admit we
are ahead of the age. The presence and
voice of woman purity and cheer; before
her, vice shrinks away, and its votaries are
sometimes so ashamed at her approach that
they resolve en masse not to visit the polls.
Whatever else is false, woman is always 
true. 
	     "Not she with traitorous kiss 
	       Her Savior strung, 
	   Not she bettrayed him 
	      With unhappy tongue. 
	   She, when Apsotles 
	     Shrank, did danger brave: 
	   Last at the cross
	     And earliest at the grave."
