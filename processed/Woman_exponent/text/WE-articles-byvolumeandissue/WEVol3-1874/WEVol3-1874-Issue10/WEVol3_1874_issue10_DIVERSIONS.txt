DIVERSIONS.
	What is that which a person likes to have
and to get rid of as soon as possible? A
good appetite.
	The provisions in the ark did not give
out. They had as much Ham at the end of
the voyage as when they started.
	There is a word in our language of five
letters, and if two of the letters are removed
only one will remain, and that word is stone.
	It is sometimes the privilege of the lover
to be, at one and the same time, in two sit-
uations. When beside his mistress he may
also be beside himself.
	A lady promised to give her maid $25 as
a marriage portion. "Why, Mary, what a 
little husband you have got!" "Dear me!
what could you expect for $25."
	An exchange having advised its readers
to plant peas "in the new moon," the Nor-
ristown Herald asks, "But how are they go-
ing to do it? They can't reach up. The
ground is a better place to plant peas any-
how."
	A call to start a society for the study of
science was responded to by five persons.
The next morning sixty-seven people were
standing in front of the Wooster House
wholly absorbed in watching two men set a
hitching post.—Earlville Transcript.
	Thackeray tells of an Irishwoman beg-
ging of him, who, when she saw him put-
ting his hand in his pocket, cried out, "May
the blessings of God follow you all the days
of your life!" but when he drew out his 
snuff-box, quickly added—"and never over-
take you."
	The Boston "Traveler" says when they
want to find out in the country if a girl
is courting or not, an old lady steps in and
remarks; "I say! there ain't no one sick in
this here house or nothin' is there? I see a
light burnin' nigh onto 12 o'clock last night,
but I don't smell no camphire or nothin'
round."
