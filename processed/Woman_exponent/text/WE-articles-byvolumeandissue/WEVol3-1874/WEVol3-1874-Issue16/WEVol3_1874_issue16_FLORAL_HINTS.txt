FLORAL HINTS.

	A TABLESPOONFUL of guano in a gallon
of soft water, administered once in three or
four weeks, is an excellent fertilizer for
house plants.
	FLOWERS in winter time are cheap luxu-
ries. Now is the time to start a few bulbs
for winter bloom in room and windows.
Write to a seedsman for a catalouge which
will inform you all about management, va-
rieties, prices, etc., and then try a little—or
much, as you may incline—indoor winter
gardening.
	A CORRESPONDENT of the Boston Tran-
script gives the following way of preserv-
ing autumn leaves, vis.: Iron them fresh
with a warm (not hot) iron on which some
spermaceti has been lightly rubbed. This
method preserves perfectly their lovely
tints and gives a waxy gloss which no other
one secures. The process is very rapid and
very agreeable, and no lady who ever tried
the tedious and uncertain experiment of
pressing will ever again resort to it after
once trying this new and better way.
