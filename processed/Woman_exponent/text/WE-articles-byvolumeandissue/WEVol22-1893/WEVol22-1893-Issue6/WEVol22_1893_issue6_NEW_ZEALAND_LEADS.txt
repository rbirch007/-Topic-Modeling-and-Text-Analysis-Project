NEW ZEALAND LEADS.
	NEW ZEALAND has shown great political
liberality in giving women equal suffrage
and has set an example worthy of imitation
by all other countries. It is a triumph for
the cause of equal rights and will doubtless
be accentuated by public celebrations in
many other lands demonstrating their ap-
proval of the New Parliament. Slowly
but surely the shackles are falling, rusty
from long usage these time worn chains of
bondage fall off gradually as the new and
brighter civilization advances and the
larger humanity takes preceedence of old
theories and superstitions, and so the world
moves on to its greater needs, and a new
element is infused by woman’s help into
the governments of the world.
	For three years past the New Zealand
House of Representative has passed
bills giving women the privilege of voting
equal with men,but this year the bill to en-
franchise women passed both the lower
and upper House, and has received the
formal assent of the Governor.
	This victory is even a greater one than
the enfranchisement of the women of
Wyoming, because New Zealand has
sovereign powers and is in itself in a certain
sense an embryo nation. New Zealand
will be the first to vote for a real Parliament,
although the fact must not be lost sight of
that women on the Isle of Man have
Parliamentary suffrage, nor must it be for-
gotten that the municipal suffrage is en-
joyed by unmarried women and widows in
England, Scotland and some Canadian
provinces.
	The EXPONENT in behalf of the women
of Utah offers its most sincere and fervent
congratulations to these fortunate women,
who have been endowed with equal rights
and privileges, and may the day speed
quickly on, when the women of Great
Britain and the United States of America
may be able to celebrate on their own ac-
count a similar victory.
