	TO  E. B W.	

SOMETIME we’ll meet again dear,
	We cannot know just when,
But once again you will be near
	And I must wait till then.
We’ll joy as when we’re young dear
	And bask in summer smiles,
Nor think of winter cold and drear
	While flower and sun beguiles.

You’ll greet me as of old dear
	And I’ll remember you, 
Your presence shall uplift and cheer 
	The heart that’s ever true, 
And though the world is cold dear
	The tie which binds us two,
Shall stronger grow each year dear
	And link my soul to you.

We’ll bravely lift our eyes dear
	To future’s page unrolled,
Believing that there lies dear 
	Beyond, the Gates of Gold.
We’ll patient be and strong dear
	And hopeful every day,
We know the hour is drawing near
	And God will find the way.
