EDITORIAL NOTES.

	THE Relief Society Conference minutes
will be concluded in the next number of the
paper, and the story ‘‘IN Rural England’’
continued. We have much matter laid
over, the minutes of several Conferences in
different places, organizations and re-
organizations of branches of the Relief
Society and meetings in different localities,
all these will be given as soon as con-
venient.
	OUR readers will be delighted to know
that Utah has taken three medals for her
silk exhibit, and it is ardently hoped by the
patrons of home industries here that this
success will give an impetus to sericulture,
and that another season will show some
good results in this direction in all parts of
the country where there are mulberry
orchards, and that the people will not be
slow in planting out mulberry trees, and
trying to make this industry remunerative.
	THE Chicago tragedy resulting in the
death of Mayor Harrison has cast a gloom
not only over the closing of the Fair, but
the whole city; all anticipated festivities are
postponed temporarily if not permanently.
After the wonderful brilliant pageantries of
the world that have been enjoyed by the
people during the Exposition, that all
should close with a pageantry of sorrow and
woe seems almost unaccountable, but such
things prove how uncertain are all the
calculations made by men; that there is a
spirit of destruction abroad in the world
seems a foregone conclusion.
