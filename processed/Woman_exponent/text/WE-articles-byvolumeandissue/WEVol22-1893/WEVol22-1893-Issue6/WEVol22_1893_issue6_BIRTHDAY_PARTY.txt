BIRTHDAY PARTY.
Editor Exponent:
	ON the afternoon of Sept. 19th 1893, a
party composed of relatives and friends,
met at the beautiful residence of Mrs.
Charles L. Flake in Snowflake, Arizona, to
pay honor to the natal day of dear Grand-
ma Hunt.
	She was born in Lexicon Co, Kentucky
Sept. 19th 1805, making her 88 years of
age.
	We found her hale and hearty filled to
overflowing with the spirit of the gospel;
she gave us such a hearty welcome that we
said in our hearts ’tis good to honor the
aged.
	After dinner the party repaired to the
parlor where she related to us many inci-
dents that had transpired in her eventful


life, while traveling with the Battalion and
in the early days of Utah. She possesses
a wonderful memory, is full of faith, in all
her life she has taken no medicine, when-
ever sickness overtakes her, she follows the
law given in the ‘‘Book of Covenants,’’ her
life and age are a proof to us, that blessed
are those who believe and rely upon the
Lord.
	She testified to us that she knew that
Eliza R. Snow and Zina D. Young were
women of God, they had blessed her and
their promises had been verified. She re-
joices that in all her life her substance has
not been witheld but has been freely given
or a portion of it for the establishing of our
Father’s kingdom; she has spent a number
of years, in California visiting her three
daughters there.
	She has left her testimony with many
there who would perhaps not have heard
the sound of the gospel only through her.
	The music rendered by Mrs. Nellie
C. Hunt was most beautiful. Some
lines composed for the occasion by her
granddaughter Mrs. Annie Hunt Kertchner
were read and brought tears to grandma’s
eyes.
	As we gazed at the tall erect form of her
who had lived so long, we were led to con-
clude that all of the severe trials of mor-
tal life have failed to make her bow to
them, neither have they caused the brain to
weaken, she hath proven herself stronger
than all.	MARY J. WEST.
