SUMMERLAND.

(Suggested by reading Book of Alma 353rd, page.)

	'Tis a land where the waters of life,
		Gently flow through the vales of the blest,
	Where the fainting and thirsty may drink,
		There the Souls that are weary find rest.

		We shall meet—we shall greet—
	All our loved ones in fair Summerland,
		We shall meet—we shall greet—
	All our loved ones in fair Summerland.

	'Tis a land where the fairest of flowers,
		Deck the vales with their sweet Eden-bloom;
	Where the angelic petals shed forth,
		Everlasting celestial perfume,
							We shall meet &c.

	In this beautiful land of the blest,
		Where sorrows and tears never come,
	There is sweet rest for souls that are faint
		In that glorious, bright, beautiful Home.
							We shall meet—

	’Tis a land where the Pilgrims find rest—
		When their trials and troubles are gone:
	Where the bright Angels welcome them home,
		To this land of the sanctified ones.
							We shall meet—
								C. L. W.
