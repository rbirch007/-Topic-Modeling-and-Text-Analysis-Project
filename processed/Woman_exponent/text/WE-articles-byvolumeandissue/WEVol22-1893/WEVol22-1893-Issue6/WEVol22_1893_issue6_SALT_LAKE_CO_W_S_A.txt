SALT LAKE CO. W. S A.

	THE regular monthly meeting of the W.
S. A. of Salt Lake Co. convened in the
13th, Ward Assembly Rooms, Sep. 19th,
1893. President Nellie Taylor in the chair.
opened by singing “Why do we seek" &c.
Prayer by Vice President E. M. Price, sang.
“O, ye fair ones” &c. Roll called,
minutes of the previous meeting were read
and approved. President Nellie Taylor
announced that four delegates were to be
appointed for the convention to be held in
Oct. it was moved and carried that the
President appoint them accordingly Vice
President E. M. Price, Julia Druce, Vice
President E. Mc. Farlane, and President
Taylor were appointed. Vice President E.
M. Price read a very instructive and in-
teresting lecture upon silver.
	Dr. M. H. Cannon gave a recitation en-
titled “The Sioux chiefs daughter,” which
was much appreciated by those present.
	Dr. E. B. Ferguson felt willing to add
her voice in the important question of suf-
frage, that is near and dear to every wo-
man’s heart. Many evils that now exist
would certainly be quicker and more easily
remedied if once woman had the franchise.
	Mrs. E. B. Wells thought more should
interest themselves in this great and impor-
tant question, made mention of the Woman’s
Congress at Chicago, and said that suffrage
was surely advancing. Let us not allow
this subject to lie neglected and forgotten.
Meeting adjourned until the third Tuesday
in October.
