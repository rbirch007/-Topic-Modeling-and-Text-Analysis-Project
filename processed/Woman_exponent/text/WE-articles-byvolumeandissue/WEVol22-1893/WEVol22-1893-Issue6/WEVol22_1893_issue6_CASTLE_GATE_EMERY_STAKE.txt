CASTLE GATE EMERY STAKE. 
Editor Exponent:
	THE Bishopric of Castle Gate Ward
organized the Relief Society on Sunday
Sept. 24, 1893, there were present of the
Stake Presidency of the Relief Society
Sisters A. U. Larsen and Ann Pulsipher.
The following sisters were set apart to act
as officers.
	Sebelia Evans, Prest. Jane Cox and
Elizabeth Young, Counselors, Lucy Reese,
Sec. Annie Kimber, Treas.
	Remarks were made by Sisters Larsen
and Pulsipher showing the duties of officers
and detailing their work, so that by follow-
ing out the instruction given all would go
hand in hand to do good and build up the
Kingdom of God.
	The Primary Association was also or-
ganized on the same day, there were present
of the Stake Presidency of the Primary
Association, Sisters Mary A. Washburn and
M. Howard, the following sisters were set
apart to fill the necessary offices.
	Annie Kimber, Prest. M. R. Cox and
Catharine Goold, Counselors, Elizabeth
Young, Sec. H. I. Cox, Treas.
	Remarks were made by Sisters Wash-
burn, Howard, Pulsipher and Larsen, en-
couraging all to attend, and see that the
children were sent regularly to Primary
that their young minds could be improved
with the principles of the Gospel.
	The remarks of the Sisters in general
were full of good instruction timely and
edifying. Bishop Lamph concluded the
meeting by invoking the blessing of God to
be upon the organizations and upon the
officers.
	T. L. REESE, Clerk Pro. Tem.
