MY DAUGHTER'S BIRTHDAY.

SAY, where is my little baby
	Of twenty years ago!
My beautiful, brown-eyed darling
	All dimples from top to toe,
Many long years, I’ve nursed her
	And others have filled her place, 
But, still on my memory graven
	Is that lovely, baby face.
Her smile was like an angel’s, 
	Thrilling my soul with love,
While the halo sweet encircling her,
	Was pure, from her home above,
Her little feet so merrily
	Would dance to tune and time,
While we all gazed in rapture
	On our baby so sublime.
Her eyes! what pen could describe them?
	So lustrous, so bright and clear,
’Twas heaven to look within them
	And her cooing voice to hear.
Ah, dear to our hearts was our cherub,
	She seemed like an angel guest,
Who had wandered from realms of glory
	So we felt divinely blest.
But this little winsome creature
	Slipped out of my arms, away;
From infant to merry childhood,
Developing day by day, 
Then up to charming woman-hood
With its witching, mystic spell,
’Till now, she’s a wife, this baby 
That I fondled and loved so well.
