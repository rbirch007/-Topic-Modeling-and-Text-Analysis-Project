When science began to shed its light,
	Over kings, and men more discerning,
God chose from the city of Genoa,
	A brave youth of humble birth,
His lands beyond the sea to explore;
	Lands of good and princely worth.
That youth, scarce higher than mother's knee;
	Treading adversities rugged path,
Pledged his life to the “Ocean Sea,”
	Contented with its love or wrath,
Imbued with the love of science,
	And thirst for geographical knowledge,
He placed in “Holy writ” his reliance,
	Surprising learned men of Pava’s College.

In the imagination of his youth,
	He pictured scenes of a world sublime;
The grandeur of which proved the truth,
	Of a scheme that grew with time,
For years, twice nine and more,
	While sunny Spain lay half asleep,
He toiled for the Western Shore;
	The mystic lands beyond the deep,
Kings wondered, wise men scoffed, 
	At the bold Explorer’s great theme
Of discovery, the whole world laughed
	As if it was some mad dream.
Cast down by poverty’s long defeat,
	He wandered, hungry and forsaken,
From Courts, to La Rabida’s Retreat,
	His faith in foreign lands unshaken,
Proving to his cause true and just,
	From the fair Isabella of God elected, 
He received, at last the needed trust,
	By foolish kings, so long rejected
Seeing afar, with wondrous knowledge,
	The truth of “Colons” scheme, she declared,
“For love of Castile, my jewels will pledge.”
	Thus the brave Queen, discovery’s hope shared
And with womanly courage untold,
	Drew from Aragon’s drained coffers
The needed thousands of precious gold,
	That silenced forever, Columbus scoffers,
When the explorer’s youth was dimmed,
	By the frost of half a century’s winds,
The Santa Maria, was fitted and trimmed,


For discovery’s voyage over the brines, 
Thus, with souls an hundred and more,
	The ships brave and proud commander,
Sailed the quiet or stormy seas o’er,
	Trusting, in a new world to land her,
Near the lands of wealth uncoined,
 	In Salvador’s early dawn of dew,
The last link of discovery’s chain joined,
	Binding the old world to the new.
						   L.S. Dickinson.
Salt Lake, July 4th, 1892.
