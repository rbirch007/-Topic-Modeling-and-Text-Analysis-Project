		WASATCH STAKE.

	THE Semi-annual Conference of the
Relief Society of Wasatch Stake was held
in the Stake House at Heber on Thursday
September 28th, at 10-30 o’clock a. m.
and 2 o’clock p. m., President Emma
Brown presiding; Sister F. D. Richards al-
so being present. After opening exercises
of singing and prayer, the minutes of the
last conference were read and accepted.
The statistical and financial report for the
six months ending August 31st, was also
read and accepted. Sister Brown made a
few opening remarks expressing pleasure
at being able to meet with the sisters in
Conference and to have the company of
Sister Richards with us.
	The various branches were reported as
being in a favorable condition, except one
which had lately been disorganized; many
good remarks were made by those sisters
who reported. Sister Richards occupied a
good portion of time both morning and
afternoon, during which time many
valuable instructions and much good coun-
sel was given. President Abram Hatch
said he had listened with pleasure to the
good counsel given, and the testimonies
borne to day; said the brethren should
come in and bless the sisters with their
presence said we have many happy homes
here and much to be thankful for; felt
to heartily thank all who had taken part in
making the conference interesting. Sister
Brown adjourned the conference and called
for a vote of thanks for Sister Richards,
and all who had taken part in the con-
ference, which was unanimously given.
Singing “Behold the Saviour comes.”
Benediction by President A. Hatch.
