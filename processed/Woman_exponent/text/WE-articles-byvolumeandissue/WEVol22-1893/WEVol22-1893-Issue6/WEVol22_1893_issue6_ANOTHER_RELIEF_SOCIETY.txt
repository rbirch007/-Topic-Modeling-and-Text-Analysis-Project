ANOTHER RELIEF SOCIETY.

	MINUTES of the organization of the
Relief Society of South Taylorsville held in
the School house Oct. 31st, 1893.
	Bishop Heber Bennion presiding. Meet-
ing opened by singing, “Praise to the man.”
Prayer by Bishop Heber Bennion. Singing
“Now let us rejoice.”
	Bishop Bennion then spoke upon the
necessity of organizing a Society in this
part of the ward, and upon the duties and
strength of the Relief Society, in the
several wards.
	Sister Pixton made the motion that Sis-
ter Mary Parker be appointed president;
and a unanimous vote was taken.
	Sister Parker was then set apart by
Bishop Heber Bennion.
	Sister Jane Bringhurst was selected as
first counselor and set apart to that position
by counselor William Panter.
	Sister Mary Palmer was chosen second
counselor and set apart by Bishop Heber
Bennion.
	Sister Sarah E. Bringhurst was selected
as secretary and set apart by Bishop Ben-
nion.
	Sister Alice Newbold was chosen treasur-
er and set apart by counselor William Pant-
er.
	There were present, Bishop Heber Ben-
nion and counselors, Sisters Julia C. Howe
and Lydia D. Alder, of Salt Lake City,
Sister Elizabeth Pixton, Sister Mary Ben-
nion, Sister Webster, all of whom spoke
very instructively to the Sisters.
	Services closed by singing “The Spirit
of God,” benediction by Elder William
Parker.
