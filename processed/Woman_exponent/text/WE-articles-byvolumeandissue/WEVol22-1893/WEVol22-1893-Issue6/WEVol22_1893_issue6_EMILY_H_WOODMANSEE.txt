		EMILY H. WOODMANSEE.

A loyal friend, most worthy of respect;
	A faithful wife, a mother true and fond,
A gentle women, one of God’s “Elect”—
	Has left us for the bright and blest “Beyond.”
No more on earth, we’ll see her genial face—
	The index of her soul’s unconscious grace.
Her’s was the charm of friendly earnestness,
	Above formality, or proud display;
Her ready hand, full oft relieved distress—
	But never in an ostentatious way;
Sweet sympathy, and modest worth combined—
To form a nature lovable and kind.
Fate, fitted her with qualities to share—
	A pleasant home, with all “the Boon” implies,
Whose generous tone, reveals the father’s care,
	The children’s tasks, the mother’s forethought,
	wise,
Alas! where mutual love held gentle sway,


The household Queen is sadly missed today.

’Tis well, 'tis well when true companions go,
	If ne’er we grieved them, with intent unkind,
'Tis well, when, howsoever deep our woe,
	No stinging self-reproach distracts our mind,
’Tis well, when keen remorse, is out of place,
	So happily as in this special case.
