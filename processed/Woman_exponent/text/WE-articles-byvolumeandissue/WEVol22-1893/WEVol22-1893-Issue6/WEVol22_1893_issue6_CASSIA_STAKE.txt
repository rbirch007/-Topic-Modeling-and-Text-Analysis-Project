CASSIA STAKE.
The conference of the Relief Society of
Cassia Stake convened at Oakley Aug. 18,
1893. On the stand were Prest. Louisa


Haight and counselors with the ward
presidents.
	The conference was opened by singing
and prayer; every ward represented and re-
ported in a favorable condition. Counselor
Helen E. Tuttle addressed the assembly,
spoke about forgiveness, and the necessity
of prayer. An article from the Exponent
was read “The World’s Congress of
Women.’, Coun. Ann Wilson said we
should do our duties td the best of our
ability, she felt to press on by the help of
the Lord. Brother Cyrus Tolman spoke in
regard to our doing work for the dead, and
preparing ourselves to be worthy to meet
those who had gone behind the vail. Ad-
journed until 2 p. m.
	Afternoon session. Opened with the
usual exercises and reading of Semi-
Annual reports of the branches of the Re-
lief Society. Sister Ann Bird expressed
herself in regard to our financial condition,
trials were to purify us, we must have charity
and love for each other. Brother Moroni
Pickett addressed the conference, spoke of
the organizations of women, and their
duties women have been secondary until
the present, they are to walk side by side
with their husbands what is man’s interest
is woman’s interest.
	Sister Sarah Worthington bore a faithful
testimony. Prest. H. D. Haight said. It is
encouraging to see so many here and to
hear the instructions we have had, spoke of
our financial affairs.
	Prest. Louisa Haight spoke of hard times
and the necessity of storing up grain.
General and Stake officers sustained.
Conference adjourned for three  months. 
  	Lucynthia Robbins, Sec.
