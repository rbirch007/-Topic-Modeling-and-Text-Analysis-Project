DIED at Georgetown, Kane Co. Utah. July 4th,
1893, Sarah Elizabeth Stewart wife of Joseph A.
Stewart. She was the daughter of William F.
and Mary Ewell; was born April 18th, 1841, in
Livingston Co. Missouri and was baptized into
the Church of Jesus Christ of L. D. S. when
eight years old; came to Utah, walking the great-
er part of the way. In 1862 she was married to
J. A. Stewart at Salt Lake City. She lived in
several places in the Territory and at one time
was president of the Relief Society in the Pahreah
Branch of the Kanab Ward which office she
faithfully performed. She was the mother of
six children all of whom were fondly attached to
her. She was always kind and good to her family
and to all,and died in full faith in the gospel. She
seemed to have a great desire for her children to
be faithful Latter Day Saints to study the gospel
and to live it.
Lydia A. Johnson.


DIED at Brigham City, Utah June 6th, 1893—of
cancer and tumor, Sine Peterson, beloved wife
of I. C. Peterson of Snowville. Born March 18th,
1836 in Denmark, was baptized in Dec. 1874, and
emigrated to Utah the same year: was married to
J. C. Peterson in January 1875, was called to the
office of Teacher in the Relief Society in Brigham
City before it was divided into Wards in 1876,
moved to Snowville  in 1884 ,and was called to
the same office in the Relief Society of Snowville
which office she held at the time of her death and
filled honorably and faithfully, as long as her
afflictions would allow her so to do. She lived and
died a faithful Latter-day Saint and the Relief
Society of this Ward feel that in her death they


have lost a faithful sister and co-laborer but that
our loss is her gain.
	Your sister in the Gospel of Peace.
