WOMAN’S EXPONENT.


The commissioners met at Ghent and
concluded on the 24th, December, 1814.
Ratified by Congress on February 17th,
1815.
	The news was brought by an English
sloop of war “Favorite” under a flag of
truce, and was hailed with the greatest
joy.
	As the president expressed it “Peace was
peculiarly welcome just then.
	March 30th following was set apart as a
day of rejoicing throughout the nation.
	Friends of the administration became
lovers, enemies were silenced and thus
closed the war of 1812.
