U. W. P. CLUB.

	THIS interesting organization held its
regular meeting at the EXPONENT parlors
Wednesday evening, August 31st. Prest.
E. B. Wells in the chair; prayer by Lillie
T. Freeze. After due attention being
given to general business the President
stated the nature of our program for the
evening which was to be devoted entirely
to poetical readings and discussions, giving
a pleasant diversion from the regular
routine. Mrs. Mary A. Freeze led with a
very appropriate selection from our honored
poet Eliza R. Snow. Ruth M. Fox read
an original poem entitled “The Moon’s
Answer’’ which had a tendency to awaken
many new thoughts.
	Miss Patterson recited in a very effective
tone and manner the beautiful poem
“Sometime.” A. J. Crocheron read
the “Lost Bower” from Mrs. Brown-
ing. C. C. R. Wells read E. B. Wells’
beautiful poem “A Fragment.” Dr. E. R.
Shipp read an original poem in two parts,
“Our Inspiration and Our Poets.” Mrs.
Lizzie Wilcox recited, very impressively,
“O, My Father.” E. W. Hyde read
“God Knows Best” from Ella Wheeler
Wilcox and L. T. Freeze read a beautiful
selection from the same author. Mrs.
Lydia Alder read “The Golden Curl” from


Mrs. Browning. The President then
charmed the club with her feeling rendition
of Jean Ingelow’s beautiful poem “Seven
times Seven.” Mrs. Lawler read the
“Deserted Mining-camp” which awakened
an animated discussion of the silver question
and Kate Field's unjust criticism of the
Women of Utah.
	The evening's program was interspersed
with sweet singing by Miss Pearl Russell
and piano music by Miss Olea Shipp,
thus blending the divine arts music and
poetry in a manner to lift one’s soul above
the stern realities of life to the idealistic of
the beyond.
	After refreshments and arranging of
program which was to be another evening
with the poets, selections from the opposite
sex exclusively, meeting was adjourned
for one month, benediction pronounced by
Lydia D. Alder.
E. R. SHIPP, Rec. Sec. pro tem.
