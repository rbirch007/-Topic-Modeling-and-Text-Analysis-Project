	MARY E. IRVINE, Sec.


IT is announced from Washington that
the Secretary of the Interior has resolved
to abate the smoke nuisance in his depart-
ment, and that henceforth smoking by the
official force during office hours is to be
prohibited. The clerk devoted to the
cigarette, cigar or pipe must decide whether
his smoke or his job is the more valuable to
him. This praiseworthy precedent by the
Secretary of the Interior might well be fol-
lowed in all the government departments.
—National Temperance Advocate.
