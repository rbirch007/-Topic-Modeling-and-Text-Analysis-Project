WOMAN’S RIGHTS.

	AS wives and mother’s have they any? Oh
yes. Innumerable rights. Thousands of
them have rights in the kitchen, at the
wash-tub, at the sewing machine, and num-
berless other rights. Is it right it should
be so? Certainly. But should her rights
end there? No. Does she need no change
from the monotonous round of her daily
tasks? There may be some few (though it
is doubtful) who feel satisfied with this
never ending round, while many long and
crave for something more, higher, intellec-
ual. They do not feel that time is lost by
an hour or two of reading, or attending a
lecture, but rather that they had gained
something. Women throughout the world
have cause to rejoice for a new era has
dawned, for her an epoch been added.
What advancement woman has made in the
past twenty years, and in the future twenty,
what may she not accomplish? If the
younger generation follow in the footsteps
of the many noble leading women of today,
the earth will be revolutionized. Women
will not be the dependent beings they are
at the present time, for they will be fitted
to fill all honorable positions that their sex


will allow them to occupy. Consequently
society will be improved. There will be
fewer fallen women, less saloons, and man,
that all important mortal, will have begun
to realize that women can maintain their
own rights, morally, socially; and politic-
ally.
