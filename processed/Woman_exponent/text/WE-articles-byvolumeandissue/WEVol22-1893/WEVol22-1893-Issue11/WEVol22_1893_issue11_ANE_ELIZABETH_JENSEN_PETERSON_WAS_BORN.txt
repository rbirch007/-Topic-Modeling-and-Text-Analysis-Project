	ANE ELIZABETH JENSEN PETERSON was born
in Haien Veile Denmark, September 28, 1819.
	She was baptized Oct. 7, 1860. In ’62 emigrated
to Utah and has ever been a faithful Latter-day
Saint She died December 5, 1893.
	Sister Peterson was put in as a teacher in the
Relief Society at Ephriam shortly after its
organization and held that position until her
death. She began the silk work industry in 1875,


and was very zealous and took great interest in
it, although she labored under great disadvant-
ages, she produced some of the finest cocoons in
Utah. For about 41 years she was unable to lie
down to rest and was obliged to use crutches for
15 years, when in 1888 she went to the Manti
Temple and received blessings therein and from
that time discarded her crutches and could lie
down and enjoy her rest. She was a woman of
great faith, no sacrifice was thought too much
for the kingdom.
