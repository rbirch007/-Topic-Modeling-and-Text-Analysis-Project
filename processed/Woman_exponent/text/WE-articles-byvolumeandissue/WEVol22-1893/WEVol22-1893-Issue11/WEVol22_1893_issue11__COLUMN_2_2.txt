and sometimes four generations would be
present at these happy reunions. Visiting
New England only a few years ago I
found that this custom of primitive
times was still observed and with far more
favor among the Puritanical families
than Christmas, for though they are be-
coming more and more English in manner,
if you go into the villages especially, the
plain country people will tell you without
the least reservation, that keeping
Christmas with all sorts of jolly festivities
is Roman Catholic, and they hate their
ways and all their forms and ceremonies.
But we do not have too many holidays or
too much pleasure with Christmas, Thanks-
giving, 4th of July, New Year’s and all
the other national days Washington’s
birthday and all our own birthdays, and
the 24th, of July our Pioneer Day.
