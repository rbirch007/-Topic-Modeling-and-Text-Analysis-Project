A WELCOME TO THE OLD FOLKS.

YE honored veterans! ye aged sires!
We bid ye welcome in our midst today,
Prophets, Apostles, God's own noblemen,
With grand dames by your sides, we welcome
	all.
Full three score years and ten you've sojourned
	here,
You've reached the alloted time to mortals
	given,
And still through Father's goodness ye are here
To bless us with your smiles, as rays from heaven.
Ye have borne the heat and burden of the day,
Have braved life's battles with true faith in God;
Stern duty for your motto; ye have proved
That ye could bow beneath His chastening rod.
'Twould fail my humble pen to tell all ye have
	wrought;
To tell what ye have not done, would be easier
	far;
But the crowning glory of your lives sublime,
Lies in the rearing of our Temples, four.
Like Simeon of old, ye now can say,
"Let us depart in peace" for we have seen
The Temple of our God 'mid mountains reared,
And swelled the hosannahs to his holy name.
O noble fathers, mothers ye have set
For us to emulate, examples grand;
Thy children's children, walking in thy steps,
Shall ready be to obey all God's command.
And were it not that death is passed on all,
How gladly would we always keep ye here,
Not still to plant and labor, but to bless,
And in our days of trial give us cheer,
But soon you'll pass beyond the shadowy river,
Reunion hold with friends who once were here;
And as you meet with hand clasps, still rememb-
	er
The Old Folk's parties on this earthly sphere.
And we too shall join you in that circle,
	Just kindly take us by the hand, and say:
You'r welcome to our King's Grand Party;
	What recompense! for welcomes given to day.
July 18th, 1893.
