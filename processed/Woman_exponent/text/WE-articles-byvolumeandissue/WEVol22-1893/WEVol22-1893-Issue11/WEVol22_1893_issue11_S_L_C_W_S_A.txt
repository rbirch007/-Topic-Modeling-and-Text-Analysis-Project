S. L. C. W. S. A.

	MINUTES of W. S. A. held Dec. 19th,
in 13th, Ward Hall President McFar-
lane in the chair. “Sang “Proud state
of the Union,” prayer C. C. R. Wells.
Sang “Come, come away.” Roll called
and minutes of November meeting read
and approved. Discussion followed as to a
place of meeting. Mrs. McCune offered
her parlor, which was accepted with the
hearty approval of all. Vice President
Druce was interested in this work and
would like to see the franchise extended
woman at an early day, thought we ought
to educate ourselves so that we could vote
intelligently, read from The Woman’s
Tribune a good article on Woman’s
Movement. Vice President Price made
some very intelligent remarks on fallen
women, wished something could be done
to help them to a better life, also spoke of
the needs existing at the present time in
consequence of so many unemployed.
Vice Prest. Eardley thought we ought to
be more parliamentary in our meeting
referred to some club of boys and girls
she had listened to, was surprised at the
intelligent manner in which they conducted
their meeting, referred to the subject that
Mrs. Price spoke upon, thought many of
them could be reclaimed if they were
dealt with kindly. President Mc. Farlane
followed on the same subject, would like to
see women get the ballot then we could
work better for all, had noticed a great im-
provement in women, some men can say
the right thing at the right time, John G.
Carlyle was one of this kind; he was
possessed of tact and if he had to refuse a
request, one went away feeling as good as
if it had been granted, we should try to do
good in a quiet way, read an article from,
The Inter Ocean. C. C. R. Wells sug-
gested that each one inform herself upon
the qualifications of a voter. Moved to
adjourn until the third Tuesday in January
at Mrs. McCune’s, 270 West South Temple
—at 2 p. m.
