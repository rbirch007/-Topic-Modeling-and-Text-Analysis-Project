	MARGARET A. CAINE, Sec.


MRS. ANNA AUSTIN was recently elected
Mayor of Pleasanton, Kan.
	THE full enfranchisement of women in
New Zealand has stimulated the friends of
woman suffrage throughout Australia. A
very demonstrative meeting was recently
held at Sidney. It appears that the chair-
manship of Sir Henry Parkes did not pre-
vent the rowdy element from interrupting
some of the men speakers to whom they
were politically opposed. The women ora-
tors, Mrs. Wolstenholme, Mrs. Lawson and
Mrs. Summerfield appear to have had no
trouble and their splendid speeches were
complimented by Sir Henry, who said that
he had never in parliamentary debate
heard three speeches on one side to equal
theirs.
