CONVENTION NOTES.

	THE Riggs Housewill be the Conven-
tion Headquarters, where the Correspond-
ing Secretarywill be glad to receive any 
persons arriving in Washington on
Wednesday, February 14, and desiring to
call for special information.
	State Presidents and Members of the
Executive Committee Meeting, Thursday,
February 15, at 9 a. m., in Metzerott’s
Hall. (Small Hall second floor.)
	Complimentary tickets for the evening
sessions have been reserved for officers and
delegates, and will be furnished to each
State President for her delegation, im-
mediately at the close of the Thursday
morning session of the Convention.
	Among the distinguished local speakers
who will take part in the sessions of the
approaching Convention in Washington,
are the District Commissioner Ross, who
will welcome the Convention at the open-
ing session, and the well known Hebrew
Philanthropist, Hon. Simon Wolf, who will
speak on Saturday evening.
	The President’s Evening a new and very
attractive, feature of the coming Washing-
ton Convention, will be Monday, Feb. 18.
Each State president will be limited to
seven minutes and “Time” will be called.
So that everyone who attends the Conven-
tion on this evening will be sure of having
no opportunity to sleep.
