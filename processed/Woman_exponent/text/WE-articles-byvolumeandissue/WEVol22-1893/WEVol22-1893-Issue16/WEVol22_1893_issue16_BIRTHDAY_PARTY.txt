BIRTHDAY PARTY.

	A VERY enjoyable affair took place here,
at Kamas, on the twelfth inst., in honor of
one of the pioneer sisters, Sister Ruth M.
Pack, Counselor to the President of the
Relief Society of the Stake, the occasion
being the lady’s seventy-second birthday.
About 7 p. m. a large number of relatives
and friends assembled at Pack & Rambert’s
hall, bearing with them the many good
things necessary for a grand feast.
	Two long tables were quickly laid and
spread with substantials and dainties in
abundance and as the aged lady was
escorted into the midst of her friends,
Kamas Brass Band burst into a lively air,
and the many friends of “Aunt Ruth”
crowded about her with good words and
congratulations. After the supper, to
which a hundred and seventy-five sat down,
speeches were made, in which a glowing
tribute was paid to the sterling qualities
and faithfuluess of the honored lady; to
which she replied in a few earnest words of
thanks. Several nice presents were also
presented to her, after which dancing was
participated in by young and old ’till a late
hour.
	Altogether the affair was a success and
the Relief Society ladies who had the affair
in charge, did themselves proud. The
company separated,hoping that Sister Pack
would see many, many happy returns.
