NABBIE.

I KNEW thee, fair spirit in life’s early bloom,
	Ere thoughts of life’s sorrows or death’s
		silent tomb
Had entered thine innocent heart.
	When father and mother and kindred so true
Sought fondest devotion to lavish on you
	And purest affection impart.

And I knew thee again as queen of thy home,
	That altar celestial of histories tome
Where hopes find truest fruition;
	Thy being enshrined in the heart of thy hus-
		band,
And calling thee blessed the voices of children
	Making of earth an elysian.

I saw  thee again—O, wondrous transition
	Passing from earth to immortal condition—
In raiment of ermine and roses.
	Thy beautiful form so cold and so silent,
The casket was here but the jewel was absent,
	In Heaven thy spirit rejoices.
								E. R. Shipp.
