(TO MY ESTEEMED FRIEND, ANNA B. J.)
YOUR birthday, dear friend! I wish you joy,
And though your life’s cup is mixed with alloy,
Think of the gladness your dear presence lends
To parents, to husband,dear ones and friends.

Dost remember the palace where primeval you 
	strayed,
The gardens of grandeur where soft breezes
	played? 
Or Father or Mother in whose courts you were
	reared, 
With love past revealing—and nothing was 
	feared? 
Where visions of beauty were old to the sight—
Where the glory of God so wondrous—so bright,
Encircled His children as in arms of love,
And rapture supreme reigned below and above.

Did the green sward of earth entice thee to
	come,
To leave joys supreme in that distant home?
Or was it the mother love drawing you here
Away from the life in yon glorious sphere?

Dear friend a mission was given to thee,
’Twas said, “Make a home and you shall be free”
But choose to do well, we will help you in love,
Will whisper of Heaven wherever you rove.”

Then when the steep pathway your tired feet
	tread,
And pitiless storms beat fierce o’er your head,
O, think of the love that is waiting for you,
When Heaven is gained and you have been true.

Then cheered be thy heart! And joyfully
	borrow,
The sunshine of hope, for lo, on the morrow
The clouds will all pass, and Heber will come,
With songs and rejoicing to you and his home.
						 LYDIA D. ALDER. 
January 26, 1894.
