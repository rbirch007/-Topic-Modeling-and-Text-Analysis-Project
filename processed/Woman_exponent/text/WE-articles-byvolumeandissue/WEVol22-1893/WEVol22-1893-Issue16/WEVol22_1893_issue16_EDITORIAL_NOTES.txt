EDITORIAL NOTES.

	WE have been reminded of the
typographical errors in the poem, “When
the summer comes again,” on the first page
of the EXPONENT of April first, in the first
verse twelfth line “rocks” should read
“nooks,” and in the second verse “these”
in seventh line should read “there”—and
worst of all the line “And in pristine beauty
blooming, the word “justice” was used
for “pristine.”
	The “Industrials,” as they are misnamed,
have been passing through Utah and mak-

ing something of a sensation, Gov. West
and others getting somewhat worried over
the affair, however at last the party that
come in from Southern California departed
in peace, and a contingent of about two
hundren men made their appearance later,
who are still waiting for something to be

ceeded in reaching Washington, what the

less one had the gift of prophecy.
	THE April Conference was a very pro-
progressive one indeed in many respects.
The inspirational sermons by Apostles and
especially the teaching of President Wood-
ruff in reference to the work for the dead,
proves that this people are not to be kept in
darkness concerning the course to be per-
sued, but that the Lord will reveal Him-
self according to the people’s needs, and
that divine revelation direct from our
Heavenly Father, is and will be given from
time to time through His servants who are
called to preside and lead his people, and
to carry on his work upon the earth.
	THE silk industry must not be permitted
to languish for want of encouragement,
some silk-worm eggs have been distributed
already, and we expect to have more soon,
reelers are being looked up to take hold
when the cocoons are ready, which will be
later in the season; and in view of render-
ing advice and all the assistance possible to
those anxious to take hold of this branch
of home, industry a Central Board has
been formed and articles of association pre-
pared and signed, the name to be Utah Wo-
man’s Silk Association, and meetings are to
be held fortnightly at this office. We
shall be able to give fuller particulars in
our next issue.
