S. L. CO. 
	MINUTES of W. S. A. held March 19th,
at Mrs. McCune's parlors. President
McFarlane in the chair. Exercises con-
ducted by second Vice President E. M.
Price. Sang "Do what is right." Prayer
by Louisa Badger, sang "Columbia's
Daughters." Roll called, minutes of
previous meeting read and approved.
	Speech by President McFarlane, "our
text is Suffrage," suggested that we make
friends of the men, "it will be through 
them that we get our rights. Ter. First
Vice President Emily S. Richards, gave an
eloquent lecture, on Woman Suffrage,
showing that it is only right and just that
we have equal rights with man, she also
read a very excellent article entitled, "Why
our daughters should be educated." Mrs.
McCune then favored the meeting with a
very pleasing recitation. Ter. President
E. B. Wells then addressed us. Thought
the Legislature, had done well in some
respects. They had tried to favor the sik
industry but the Governor refused to sign 
the bounty bill. The Governor had signed
the memorial to Congress asking that we
have the franchise restored, also a memorial
asking that the Woman's Industrial Home
be given the women of Utah for a Wo-
man's Hospital. Thought some were very
willing to help the ladies, many ladies have
not the moral courage to face opposition,
would like to see the meetings held in the
wards, had a message sent to her that there
would be a plank placed in the statehood
platform for woman suffrage. Dr. Ferguson
read a clipping explaining women's voting
in New Zealand, when women have the
suffrage they will not trade their votes.
Song by Miss Fay McCune, excellently
rendered, encored and she sang again.
Meeting adjourned 'till April.
