NOTES AND NEWS.

	MISS ANNE WHITNEY, the sculptor, has
completed a bust of Keats in marble,
which is to be placed in the parish church
of Hampstead London, as a memorial from
the American and English lovers of the
poet.
	MISS JULIA KEMP WEST, of Richmond,
Staten Island, N. Y.,  has just entered upon
her duties as school commissioner. She
has made Miss Nellie M. Ford her private
secretary.
	MISS JOSEPHINE RAND, of Boston, has
composed a “Song of Labor” and set the
words to the music of “Marching Through
Georgia.” It will be sung in Boston
for the first time at the reunion of the
Working Girl’s Clubs, in May.
