BRIGHAM YOUNG'S ANNIVERSARY

	THIS the first day of June is quite a red-
letter day among our people, on account of
its being the anniversary of the birthday of
Brigham Young, who presided over the
Church so many years and was also truly a
Moses unto this people, a pioneer and leader
into these mountain vales, where he found-
ed towns and cities, and established order
and maintained peace. It is useless to at-
tempt to recount his good deeds, his enter-
prise, his great leadership, his power and
ability in organization, extending into every
department of progression.
	Let his memory be kept ever fresh and
green by our children and children’s child-
ren.
	His family have issued invitations to all
the relatives of the illustrious departed
President to gather in the parlors of the
historic Lion House and perpetuate his
memory and the family traditions by as-
sociating themselves together, and becom-
ing better acquainted with each other, and
the history of their ancestry. It is a beau-
tiful tribute to the departed hero and vener-
ated Saint.
	The children of this people should be
taught to observe his birthday anniversary,
and those who have charge of the young
as instructors might arrange fitting amuse-
ments and testimonials to be observed, and
keep the day in remembrance.
	The Primary Associations have some of
them made it a holiday instead of May day,
because of the weather being more
propitious for out-door enjoyment, and also
as an ovation (so to speak) to one to whom
we are all of us more or less indebted for
the great pioneer work, that made this
country habitable for us and a desirable
place to dwell in. The illumination of the
Salt Lake Temple in honor of the day was a
part of the program, and music, speeches,
song and recitations were rendered by the
family. In our next issue of the paper
some further notice will be made of the
proceedings.
