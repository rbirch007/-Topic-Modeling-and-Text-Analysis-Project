POVERTY and want are abroad in the land,
	The widow and orphan are here,
There’s a chance to do good for the liberal,
		hand,
	And to dry up the sad mourner's tear.
The Savior said, when here on the earth, 
	And his words I most firmly believe,
I esteem them of value, I deem them of worth
	“Tis more blessed to give than receive.”
If fortune has lavished upon thee her wealth,
	Thou art bless’d in thy “basket and store,”
With plenty to eat, enjoyment of health,
	Forget not to succor the poor.
Thy neighbor may be, in his poverty,
	More wretched than thou would’st believe.
Remember if he asks a favor of thee,
	’Tis more blessed to give than receive.
O, I have not enough, some will say,
	To keep up appearance and style,
If I give this away, I may need it some day,
	So I think I shall keep it awhile.
This is not the spirit of Jesus I trow,
	They who think thus themselves do deceive,
For the Savior he taught in his mission below,
	’Tis more blessed to give than receive.
Remember these precepts, and by them do live,
	Let charity dwell in thy breast,
Be thou ever ready and willing to give
	To the needy, the sick and distressed,
If the beggar should ask for bread at thy door, 
	Be sure that his wants you relieve,
He lends to the Lord who gives to the poor,
	’Tis more blessed to give than receive.
