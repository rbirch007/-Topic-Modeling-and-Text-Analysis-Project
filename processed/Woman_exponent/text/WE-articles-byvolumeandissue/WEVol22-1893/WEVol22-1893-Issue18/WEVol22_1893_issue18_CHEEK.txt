Of robust physique florid cheeks, and clean
	shave,
Awaited without, with his carriage fine,
For me to accompany him to dine,
Or fill an invitation to take wine;
In less than a quarter, by the clock,
I had myself clad in silken frock
And was speeding along the street paved with
	rock.
Beside the man of my mind’s own choice, 
So near his pure heart I might well rejoice,
in a carriage fine of his own invoice.
But, as a corner we turn’d sharp and spry,
The hideous steam roller met the steed’s eye,
Quick he became frighten’d, began to shy.
Just then the frightful thing a sharp puff blew out
Instantly the horse leap’d, to reverse his route, 
With the same turned suddenly about: 
My heart leap’d up into my mouth with fright,
For dear life I to my escort clung tight—
Clung to his sinewy arm with all my might;
Another puff the engine gave—the steed,
With a wild leap of most vicious greed,
Plunged farther, bent from the noise to be freed.
