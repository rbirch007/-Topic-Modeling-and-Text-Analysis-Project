UNITED.
[To Mr. and Mrs. Earnest White, from their
sincere friend.]
Sparkling and clear, at the head of the mountain,
Rise two little streams, each from its own foun-
tain;
Yet gradually nearer, as onward they go,
Now in one common course together they flow.
Environed by banks that will not break o’er,
’Till at last they have found the ocean’s far shore,
Each lost in the other, now they are one;
United together, till life’s-work is done.
Two beautiful lives on the roadway of time,
Beginning so early round each other to twine,
Growing dearer and fonder, year after year,
Sharing each pleasure as well as each tear;
At the altar of faith, united and sealed,
“Unto life eternal”—by powers revealed! 
Their hopes, their misson, their lives are now
one:
United even after this life is done.
In love, with a blessing, we fervently pray,
That their aims ne’er diverge, nor their love go
astray;
Reinforced by each other’s ne’er ceasing aid
Ever faithful and true in sunshine and shade!
May these lives, as those streams, ever flowing
together,
Remain undivided, in clouds and fair weather,
Together move onward to rapture divine,
Eternally joined in the ocean of time.
ELLIS R. SHIPP.
Oct. 1893, Salt Lake City.
