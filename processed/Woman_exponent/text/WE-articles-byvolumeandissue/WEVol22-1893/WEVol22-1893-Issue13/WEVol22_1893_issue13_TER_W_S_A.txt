			TER. W. S. A.

	AN important meeting in the interest of
suffrage was held Feb. 3rd, 1894, in the
14th, Ward Assembly Hall. The territorial
president Mrs. E. B. Wells called the meet-
ing to order and presided. Miss Maude
Pratt and Miss Pearl Russell sang "Battle
Hymn of the Republic,” the audience
joining in the chorus. Prayer was offered
by Mrs. E. S. Barney. The congregation
sang ‘‘New America.”
	Brief and interesting were the speeches 
by Dr. Hasbrouck, Mrs. Zina D. H. Young,
Samuel Richards, and Mrs. Lucy A.
Clark. Able addresses were delivered by
Messrs John E. Booth and Dr. M. Hughes
Cannon in which the speakers showed con-
clusively that equal suffrage should be en-
joyed by both sexes.
	A Recitation by Miss Babcock, songs by
Mrs. Bessie Dean Allison and George D.
Pyper were the artistic features of the oc-
casion. Meeting adjourned subject to the
call of the president.
