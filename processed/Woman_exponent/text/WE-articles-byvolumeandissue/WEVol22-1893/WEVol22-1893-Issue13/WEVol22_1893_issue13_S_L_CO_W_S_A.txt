			S. L. CO. W. S. A.

	The Salt Lake Co. W. S. A. convened
at the residence of Mrs. Elizabeth A. C.
McCune, Jan. 16th 1894, President Eliza-
beth McFarlane in the chair.
	Opened by singing, ‘‘Come let us anew”
prayer by Mary E. Bassett. Minutes of
the last meeting read, corrected and ap-
proved. Names of new members taken.
The program for the meeting was carried
out by several of the ladies giving the
necessary qualifications to enable foreigners
to become voters in the United States,
which disclosed the fact that in about six-
teen of the states, aliens could vote with-
out having declared their intention of be-
coming citizens, by conforming to the laws
of either of those states which do not re-
quire that voters should be naturalized.
	Several speeches were made by ladies
who did not enjoy taxation, without
representation, and the Universal Sentiment
seemed to be that if they ever did get the
franchise again (which we have full faith
that we will do) they would not be so
particular about voting for parties, as they
would for men: The ladies then listened
with much attention, to the matchless pro-
duction, “Oh! my Father” by that great
exponent of woman’s rights Eliza R. Snow,
beautifully rendered by Bro. Thomas
Ashworth with piano accompaniment.
For an encore Bro. Ashworth gave two
other songs after which a vote of thanks
were given him, and the meeting stood ad-
journed until the third Tuesday in Feb., at
the same time and place, which concluded
one of the most delightful meetings the
association has ever had. Mrs. McCune
has kindly thrown open her beautiful
parlors, with ample room for all, in which
we hope the ladies will feel a renewed
interest in meeting together and contribut-
ing to make the association a grand success.
		C. C. R. WELLS, Sec. pro tem.
