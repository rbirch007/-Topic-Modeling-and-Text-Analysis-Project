			A TRIBUTE OF LOVE.
[To the memory of our dear Sister and Counselor
Maria D. Chambers.]

Tenderly fold the weary hands,
	Carefully robe the sainted clay,
Lovingly smooth the silken hair,
	Gently, peacefully lay her away.

Her work is finished—death is passed,
	Her mission on earth is o'er,
Henceforth for her brow is the victor's crown,
	In a home where death is no more.

How nobly, how bravely she labored,
	How well her work is done;
Her life, its duties, seemed all complete
	Ere the setting of life's sun.

How tender she was at the couch of pain,
	How willing her sisters to bless;
The poor felt her liberal hand in theirs;
	Her works were of righteousness.

Her counsels were fraught with wisdom,
	Her teachings were full of love,
Her example will bear fruition
	That will follow her above.

In her death we are all bereaved
	And our tears of sorrow shed,
As we bring our floral offering,
	To place o'er the sainted dead.

Today there is sorrow and mourning,
	For the loss of a mother's love;
But list to the greetings yonder?
	Husband, Wife, reunited above.

Be patient ye sons and daughters,
	Tread the path your parents have trod,
And your circle again shall be complete,
	In the mansions of our God.
			MARIANNA STRATFORD, President.
			NELLIE BEECROFT, Secretary.
