WOMEN OF THE WEST.
		BY EMILY H. WOODMANSEE.
HEROIC women of the West,
	Intrepid as illustrious man, 
When Progress, westward forced a way,
	The gentler sex was in the van.
Stout-hearted helpers—mates and peers
	Of Braves who faced the setting sun; 
Companions of the pioneers—
	What have not Western women done?

Privations man could hardly bear,
	Which taxed his utmost fortitude,
Weak woman suffered, and withal,
	Endured the cares of motherhood.
Too often in the early days
	Comforts were scarce or, out of reach,
Yet what the wealthiest had was shared
	Fraternal offerings unto each.

Courage and faith and self denial,
	Were cultivated day by day.
Good nature conqured many a trial,
	That fell in Western women’s way.
But well they loved the mountain air, 
	The peaceful vales seemed sacred ground;
And grateful hearts expanded, where
	Souls never need be conscience-bound.

Hail! western wives! ye’ve reared a race,
	A nation might be proud to claim;
Daughters and sons of noble sires,
	And mothers worthy of the name;
Whose treasured babes from living founts
	Imbibed the love of right forsooth,
The staunch integrity that counts
	For freedom, equity and truth.

The seeds of liberty, for years,
	Round western altars have been sown;
Nourished by hopes, or fed with tears,
	Surprisingly has freedom grown.
Columbia’s glory, strength and boast
	The constitution broad and grand,
Is honored by a countless, host;
	Throughout this spacious Western land.

The East can boast of dauntless sires—
	Of patriotic mothers true,
But liberties effulgent fires,
	Are kindled in the West, anew,
The Western women wield, for good,
	A mighty and a growing power,
And with the world’s brave sisterhood,
	Will win their royal rightful dower.
Salt Lake City 1893.
