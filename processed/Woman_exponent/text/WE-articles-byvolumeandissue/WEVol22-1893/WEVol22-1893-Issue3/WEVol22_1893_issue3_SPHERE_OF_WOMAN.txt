SPHERE OF WOMAN.
WOMAN'S SPHERE! I hesitate for I can scarcely
	hope
That I may courage find, with this great theme to
	cope,
Yet who more sure to comprehend her hope than
	woman,
And with true sympathy her rugged path illumine

Yes ’tis the right of woman to let her light shine
	forth,
So all may see its beauty, and emulate her worth,
Not by aggressive words, and clamoring for fame
But by her noble actions she wins without ac-
	claim.

Woman with her brother-man a heritage hath
	gained,
The earth, the sea and elements contained
Are theirs, to use for good,all for a worthy end;
With many other gifts our Father’s mercies send.

So she should wiser grow, and seek to cultivate
The powers of her mind,and ev’ry heavenly trait,
Intelligence and truth should fill the pliant mold;
Extending to its utmost; Possibilities untold!

’Tis her right to raise her voice a just cause to de-
	fend;
Let kindness, love and mercy in all her actions
	blend,
To aid the weak, soothe the sick and cheer the
	sadden’d heart,
And pure refining virtues to every soul impart.

Again it is her right to aid in forming laws
By which her sex is judged, in the one great com-
	mon cause;
To cast her vote for honest men in national affairs
Thus purifying politics, while she the burden
	shares.

But of all God-given rights that womankind pos-
	sesses
Is that of wife and mother! Think how many
	homes she blesses
With her tender love. ’Tis her lot to live for
	others!
Divine unselfishness! Ah! these noble wives and
	mothers.

Just one more right I’d mention on this auspicious
	day,
The right to love each other, and kindly tributes
	pay.
Engage in social converse, enjoy a sweet com-
	munion,
Let heart come close to heart, and angels join
	our union.
					ELLIS R. SHIPP.
Salt Lake City Aug. 14, 1893.
