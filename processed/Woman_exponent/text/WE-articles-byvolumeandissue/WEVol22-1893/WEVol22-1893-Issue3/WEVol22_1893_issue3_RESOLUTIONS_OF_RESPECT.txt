RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.

(To the memory of Sister Annie Smith Crane.)

	Who departed this life April 22nd, 1893. Sister
Crane was born at the village of great Staughton
Huntingtonshire England on Dec. 5th 1857. Was
married to Geo. Edward Crane Oct. 13th 1881,
in Salt Lake City by President D. H. Wells. Was
the mother of five children, two girls and one boy
survive her, and also a loving husband to mourn
her loss. She was a devoted wife and mother.
	At a meeting at the Fourth Ward R. S.
Provo the following Resolutions were adopted.
	Whereas God has seen fit in his Alwise Provi-
dence to call from our midst our beloved Sister
Crane who has been a faithful teacher in the 4th,
Ward, a devoted friend to the poor and a wise
counselor in her family, and
	Whereas, it is fitting that she be held in honor-
able rememberance, therefore be it
	Resolved; by the Sisters of the Relief Society of
the 4th Ward that while we mourn not as those
who have no hope yet realize that we have lost
a faithful friend and sister who died as she had
lived in the hope of a glorious resurrection.
	Resolved; that the resolutions be spread upon
the ward record of the Relief Society, and that
copies be sent to the Woman's EXPONENT for
publication.
MARGARET M WATSON, President.
Committee.		JANET HARRISON,
				ADELAIDE MELDRUM,
