MISSCELLANEOUS.

	THE way to do a great thing is never to.
neglect any little thing.
	TEMPERANCE is a tree that has content-
ment for its root and peace for its fruit.
	WE attract hearts by the qualities we
display; we retain them by the qualities
we possess.—Suard.
	PERFECT valor consists in doing without
witnesses all we should be capable of doing
before the whole world.—Rochefoucauld.
	POETRY is the breath and finer spirit of
all knowledge; it is the impassioned ex-
pression which is the countenance of all
science.—Wordsworth.
	A FATHER inquires whether his boy
can construe Homer, or understand Horace,
but how seldom does he ask, or examine or
think whether he can restrain his passions, 
whether he is grateful, generous, humane,
compassionate, just,and benevolent.—Lady
Hervey.
