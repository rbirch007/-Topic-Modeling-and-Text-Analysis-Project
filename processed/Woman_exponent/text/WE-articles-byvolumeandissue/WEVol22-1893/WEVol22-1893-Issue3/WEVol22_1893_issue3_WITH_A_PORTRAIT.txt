WITH A PORTRAIT.
ACCEPT this shadow of your absent friend,
	As a peace offering, and a Christmas token
The lips are dumb—would they might speak for
	me!

	For words are jewels that are fitly spoken. 
If, in the years to come, when pleasures wain
	You think of me with but one fond regret
That thus we part, ’twill pay the debt of pain
	That fills my heart that we have ever met.
								E. J.
