LETTER TO THE EXPONENT.
Editor Exponent.
	THE editorial in the last EXPONENT set
me to thinking quite seriously. It is cer-
tainly true that the little cost of the paper
cannot compare with its value to the wo-
men of Utah. No one can ever claim to
have read in the EXPONENT, words of ex-
pressions other than pure, elevating and
sensible. True it contains few of those
worthless fashion hints and house-keeping
and rearing children recipes and remedies;
feeling a mother’s and a wife’s own
instincts and circumstances can better guide
her in those duties, but it contains pages of
useful information pertaining to woman’s
work both at home and abroad. Its little
pages contain some of the sweetest songs
our home poets have sung, and some of the
brightest thoughts our authors have penned,
beside rare selections and articles and stories
of merit and interest. Nor do I extol the lit-
tle semi-monthly visitor too highly when I
say it is invaluable to the women of Utah,
and I wonder do they half realize what an
educator the EXPONENT has been to them
these many years. Perhaps we appreciate
our papers and books like our friends. The
more when we have lost them, but I hope
not, and I hope for the sake of the women
of Utah we will not have to lose our friend
the EXPONENT, for it is a bright little mes-
senger to our homes bringing us newsy and
pleasant thoughts, and binding us closer
and friendlier in our social industrial and
charitable work.
E. A.
