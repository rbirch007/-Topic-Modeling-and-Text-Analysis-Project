U. W. P. CLUB.

	THE U. W. P. Club met at the EXPO-
NENT parlor Saturday evening of July 
30, the president in the chair prayer by
Lillie T. Freeze minutes of Juue meeting
read and approved. General business was
next in order. The names of Mrs. Zina
D. H. Young Mrs. Elmina S. Taylor, Miss
Ada Patterson and Miss Benson were pro-
posed as members, motion put and accepted
unanimously.
	A letter was then read from the secretary
desiring to be excused for several weeks
absence. Mrs. M. A. Freeze also sent an
excuse for unavoidable absence.
	Miss Maude Pratt favored the club with
Zempa from Arnold and a selection from
Il-Trovatore the music being executed in
fine style and charming every listener, a
hearty vote of thanks followed. Lula
Greene Richards recited an original poem
entitled the “Eloquence of Action.” The
president said, “these meetings are simply 
for literary  improvement and devoted to
the press and similar things, and not for the
discussion of religion or politics as we can
secure these things in other places. No
subject should be presented here upon
which we are likely to conflict or that
will create unpleasantness.
	An animated discussion of the Sixth
Sense was followed by refreshments. The
President suggested that our next meeting
should be an evening with the poets, each
member to either write an original poem or
select something from her favorite author,
the author to be a woman.
	Meeting adjourned for one month,
benediction by Phebe C. Young.
		ELLIS R. SHIPP, Sec. pro tem.
