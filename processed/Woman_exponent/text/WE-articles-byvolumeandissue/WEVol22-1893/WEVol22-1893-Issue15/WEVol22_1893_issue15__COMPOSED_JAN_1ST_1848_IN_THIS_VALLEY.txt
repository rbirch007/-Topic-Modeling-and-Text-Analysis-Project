[COMPOSED JAN. 1st, 1848, IN THIS VALLEY.]

	Oh Ellen! my sister raise thy drooping head,
And welcome the New Year, the old one has fled:
With its joys and its sorrows alike it has flown,
And left us to think they were once our own.
It has fled and gone, yet we treasure the past
As sacred to memory: and long may it last,
Each blessing a prize as sent from above,
With hearts full, of gratitude joy, peace and love.
Yet in sadness thou thinkest of friends left behind
Whose hearts have been faithful, and acts very
	kind,
In visions we’ve seen them in griefs dark array,
Be thou only faithful and continue to pray.
Yes continue thou faithful, and be of good cheer,
For blessings await thee, and are very near:
When thy heart shall rejoice, and voice cry aloud
Glory, and honor, and praise to the Lord.
But Ellen since sorrow is mingled with joy,
Let us cheer one another, and our time we’ll em-
	ploy,
In seeking for wisdom, for its paths are peace
Its crown is immortal and joys never cease.
	M.E.K.
