TEN YEARS OLD.

	[Lines addressed to my little niece and name
sake Alta Wilbeck Brown who resides at Manti-
Utah.]
		Ten years old my little girl:
		For a moment stop this whirl,
		Till in eye of blue,
		I can prove it true,
		Like April day yourself,
		A sad, then laughing elf;
		Tear-drops come down
		Then joy all around.
		How soon the bud is blown,
		Its folded beauty shown,
		May the blossom grow
		Till ripe fruit it show!
		Then years from to day
		If we on earth shall stay
		When maiden brow I kiss
		My little girl I’ll miss.
		Should you live, and Auntie sleep,
		A place for me in memory keep 
		And know that noble woman-hood,
		I sought for you as chiefest good,
		God in Heaven bless this child,
		When the way is lone and wild.
		Or where still waters glide
		Always near her abide.
AUNT ALTA.
West Coxsackie N. Y. Jan. 31st, 1894.
