			EXCURSION.

	THE excursion to California under the
auspices of the Woman’s National Press
Association, leaves Washington Sat-
urday, April 14th 2 p. m. It arrives in
Cincinnati Sunday morning and St. Louis
Sunday evening. The round trip rates
from Washington are $83. From points
farther East the same with local fare added.
From St. Louis the round trip rates are $47.
and at points all along the route parties can
secure a proportionately reduced rate by
writing to Mrs. Joanna W. Turner, 906
Mass. Ave., Washington D. C. and being
enrolled as a member of the excursion
	The sleepers are chartered at a price
which makes them one third less than usual
and reclining chair cars are free from St.
Louis. To engage a whole berth in the
sleeper, parties must send name and ten
dollars as collateral. Arrangements are
made for reduction on meals also in Dining
Car and Buffet, and lunch baskets are ad-
mitted, but must have soft handles to slip
under seats. Can refill them where stops
are made.
	Receptions will be given at Los Angelos,
San Francisco, Salt Lake, and no doubt at
Denver and Chicago. Tickets may be ex-
tended to July 1st, but the party in a
body will have such a grand return sight-
seeing trip that few will want to linger
longer than the 8 or 10 days at San
Francisco.
	Eastern people will confer with agents
nearest their starting points who represent


the Penn. R. R. who run in connection
with the Chesapeake and Ohio. Write to
Mr. McConnell, Agent, at 378 Broadway,
N. Y. Wherever you apply show the
itinerary of Chesapeake and Ohio, which
can be had of their agents or of Mrs.
Turner, and ask the lowest rates to join the
train at Washington—the grandest
Continental trip over our country.
