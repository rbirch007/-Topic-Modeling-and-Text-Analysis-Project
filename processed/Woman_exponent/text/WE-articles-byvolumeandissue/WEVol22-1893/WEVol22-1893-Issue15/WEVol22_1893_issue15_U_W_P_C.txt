U.W. P. C.

	THE U. W. P. C. met by special re-
quest on the evening of the 16th of March,
at the office of Dr. E. R. Shipp. After
prayer by Mrs. H. A. Badger, the minutes
were read by the Secretary and the roll call
responded to with selections from the poets.
	The names of Dr. Ellen B. Ferguson,
Miss Maude Pratt, Miss Pearl Russell, Miss
Olea Shipp and Mrs. Fannie Stewart, of
Pleasant Grove, were proposed for member-
ship and voted in as members by the club.
All members voted in favor of having our
next meeting on the usual date, the last
evening of the month.
	The Secretary read an article from Mrs.
Stewart entitled “The Mission of Today.”
	Mrs. E. B. Wells spoke of the coming
meeting of the Federation of Clubs, to be
held in May, at which a representative of 
the U. W. P. C. will be expected to speak
extemporaneously. Mrs. Wells read from
the writings of Hannah T. King a poem
entiled “Life.” A pleasant discussion
followed in which all the members took part.
	While refreshments were being served,
Miss Gladys Woodmansee rendered some
music.
	The programme for the next meeting to
be as for the last meeting with the addition
of an article on “Love,” by Mrs. M. E.
Shipp, a poem by Mrs. N. C. Taylor, and
quotations from the Psalms by the members.
