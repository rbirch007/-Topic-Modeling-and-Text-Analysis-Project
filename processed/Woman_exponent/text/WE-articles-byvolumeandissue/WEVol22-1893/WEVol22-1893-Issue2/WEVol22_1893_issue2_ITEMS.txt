ITEMS.

	Mrs. WILLMIRTH East President of the
Relief Society of St. Joseph Stake in
Arizona is spending the season in this City
and may be found at the residence of her
son, 642, East, First South St. Sisters
who wish to communicate with her will
please note the address. Sister East will
be pleased to visit any of the branches of
the Relief Society, Y. L. M. I. A. or Pri-
mary, as she has been familiar with these
organizations from the beginning here in
the valley; she has leisure and would be
pleased to visit among the associations.
Mrs. East is a good strong advocate of
Woman Suffrage and thoroughly patriotic.

	IT is not possible for an editor to please
every body, and we find it difficult to pub-
lish all our subscribers think proper. Some-
times articles are rejected because nnsuit-
able, and it is not possible to make the ex-
planations public. In reference to death
notices, it is almost impossible to keep up
with the number sent in for publication.
We try to make a complete record of those
sisters who have been efficient and faithful
in the Relief Society, as the EXPONENT is
the official organ of that Society. If oc-
casionally one is inadvertently missed, it is
certainly not intentional, but one cannot pub-
lish more than a column or two of obituaries
in one issue.
