		BEAR LAKE STAKE.

	THE Relief Society Conference of Bear 
Lake Stake convened at St. Charles Idaho
Saturday May 27, 1893, at 10 a. m  Presi-
dent Lindsay presiding.  The choir sang,
"What though the sky be starless,"  prayer
Elder M. Allred.  Singing "When the 
heart is pressed with care." Roll called
and minutes of the former conference read
and approved.  Opening remarks appropri-
ate to the occasion made by Prest. Lindsay. 
	The branches of the society were then
represented as follows, St. Charles Prest.
J. C. Gallaway,  Fish Haven, Frances 
Howell, Bloomington, Sarah Patterson,
Paris, 1st Ward, Margaret Sutton, Mont-
pelier, Prest. Patrea Hogansen.  All were
reported as in good condition.  The statisti-
cal and financial report was then read.
Some very interesting and instructive re-
marks were made by Prest. Lindsay and
Counselor Hart, after which Prest. Lind-
say explained that she was in need of a
corresponding sec'y, and it was moved and
carried that Miss Rose Hillier act as Cor.
