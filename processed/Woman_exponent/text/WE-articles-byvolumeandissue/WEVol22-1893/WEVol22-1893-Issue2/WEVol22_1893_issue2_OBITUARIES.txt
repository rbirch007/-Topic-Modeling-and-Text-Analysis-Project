OBITUARIES.

Editor Woman's Exponent: 
	WITH profound sadness we record the death of
another of God’s noble women, Sister Janet Lowe
Dowdle, the beloved wife of Robert Dowdle, and
daughter of Thomas and Eliza Lowe.
	Sister Dowdle was born April the 20th 1852 at


Fifeshire Scotland and died April the 26th, 1893,
at Coveville, Cache Co. Utah.
	She emigrated to Utah with her parents when
she was three years old, and grew to womanhood
among this people, was married to Brother Rob-
ert Dowdle of Franklin, Idaho,October 3rd, 1868,
removed to Coveville in 1876, where she resided
’til her death. She was the mother of sixteen
children ten of whom died in their infancy, and
six are left to mourn her loss, the youngest a
babe at the time of her death; she suffered much
but was true and faithful to the last. She was
one of those who have come up through much
tribulation, who washed their robes in the blood
of the Lamb. She has finished her work and
fought the good fight and now rests from her
labors, and her noble example and integrity to
the truth will long be remembered by those who
knew her; she died in full faith of a glorious
resurrection, surrounded by her family, brothers
and sisters, all but her two youngest brothers who
are both absent on missions.
