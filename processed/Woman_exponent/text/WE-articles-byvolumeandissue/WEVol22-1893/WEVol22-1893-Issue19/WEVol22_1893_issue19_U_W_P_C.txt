U. W. P. C.
	MEETING of the U. W. P. C. held at the
office of Dr. E. R. Shipp, May 31st, 1894,
President E. R. Shipp in the chair.
Prayer by E. B. Wells. Roll call responded
to with quotations from Pope. Minutes of
previous meeting read and approved. Miss
Gladys Woodmansee gave a lecture upon
the subject of psychology, M. E. Shipp
gave a vocal selection. A poem written by
E. R. Shipp was read by Mrs. C. Louise
Boydon, Dr. R. B. Pratt read a paper on
theosophy. A recitation was given by
Ellis Shipp. President Shipp referred to
the visit of the U. W. P. Association and
some items of interest she had gleaned
from them. Dr. E. B. Ferguson, club
critic gave criticism upon the various
numbers of the evening program, said
she was sorry that the lecture on Feeling
had not been given before the Will where
it belonged, as Will is the king faculty of
the mind and had it been given as the
climax, we could better realize its true
position in the mind.
	After benediction, refreshments were
served and the usual informal chat was in-
dulged in until time for adjournment.
	Program for June 30th: Woman’s Press
Clubs, L. S. Dickinson; Pen Women, E. B.
Wells; Thirty minutes discussion on
Theosophy, by the members; Music, C. L.
Boyden; Original poem, Mary A. Freeze;
Quotations from the writings of Emerson.
