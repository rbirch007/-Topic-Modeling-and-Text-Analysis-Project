		GLADYS WOODMANSEE, Rec. Sec.


W. S. A.
	MINUTES of the W. S. A. held June 19,
in the Fourteenth Ward Assembly Hall.
First Vice President Adella W. Eardley in
the chair, Sang, “Come Away.” Prayer
by E. M. Price. Sang “America.” Roll
called, minutes of April and May read and
approved. Mary Lois Morris read E.
R. Snow’s poem, on “Nationality.”
Speech from Zerviah T. Eardley, had
her name enrolled in the organization,
was a firm believer in Woman’s Rights, we
inform our minds upon all subjects. Wo-
man’s influence is felt, was connected with
Young Ladies and the Relief Society, but
thought home duties should come first,
with management we can spare time for
many things, women should have the right
to vote, and when doing the same work as
man should have the same pay; then read a
poem entitled “What woman makes man.”
Third Vice President Julia Druce made
some excellent remarks. Second Vice
President E. M. Price read from the Wo-
man's Journal, a piece on Elections. Dr.
Ferguson referred to an article read, and
explained many points in regard to what
can be done through legal quibbles; referred
to the actions of some Governors who had
been elected for the Populist Party, also to


the large amount of Italian dagos who are
being brought to this country who are tak-
ing the work from honest workmen. Utah
is likely to become a state and should go in
on the platform of Equal Suffrage.
Ruth M. Fox read an excellent piece show-
ing the equality of sex from the Bible.
	President MacFarlane showed from the 
Bible many instances where destruction had
come from not respecting women. We
like strong, true reliable men, let us as-
sociate with no other. Adjourned until
July. Benediction M. E. Irvine.
