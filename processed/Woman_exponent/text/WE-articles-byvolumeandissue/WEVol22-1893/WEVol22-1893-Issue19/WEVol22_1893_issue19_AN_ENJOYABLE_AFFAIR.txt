AN ENJOYABLE AFFAIR.

	IT has been a long time perhaps, since
you have heard any thing from this little
town and it may not be out of place to say
that Mrs. Louisa Sanders, Mrs. Mary E.
Marble, Mrs. Eunice Bleak Sanders and
Mrs. Helena Kempe Naegle, were appointed
a committee to get up a surprise, which
took place here on the 19th of May, to
celebrate the birthday of the wife of our
worthy Bishop C. I. Kempe. Sister Olena
Kempe who is counselor in the Kelief
Society and President of the Primary.
Sister Kempe being absent the sisters
thought to surprise her when she returned
at dusk; all was still, she opened the door
of her beautiful parlor and was greeted
with a loud burst of music and song. She
stood transfixed with astonishment.
	Bishop Kempe called the house to order
and offered up a fervent prayer. The young
ladies’ sang, “Oh my father thou that
dwellest.”
	Bishop Kempe stepped forward and said
the young people all liked novels and he
would tell them one, he then related the
early history of his wife, her conversion
to our faith, and her trials, and troubles in
leaving her parents, and her wealthy home in
Norway her native country. A few lines
were then read composed by Mrs. M. P.
Killian telling of Sister Kempe’s many
virtues, describing her worth and goodness,
her kindness to the poor and needy, and to
the sick and suffering.
	A song was then sung by Misses Minnie
and Hattie Bilby. Lines were read com-
posed by Miss Maggie Green. Mrs. Kempe
made a few remarks and welcomed the
company to her home. Mrs. E. I. Pulsipher
sang, “Daughter don’t let mother do it.’’
A recitation by Miss Anna Green. A song
by Mrs. May Pulsipher, Organist. Mrs. E.
Sanders read a piece entitled "Over worked."
Miss O. S. Kempe and George Cheney
