BIRTHDAY ANNIVERSARY.
	ACCORDING to promise given in last
issue, something more is here presented of
the Young Family Gathering on the First
of June.
	Sister Elizabeth Ellsworth, eldest child
of President Young, was present, from
Idaho. Her Sister Vilate Decker, was born
on her father’s birthday, and on this oc-
casion, sat at the head of the table with
President George Q. Cannon, celebrating
her sixty-fourth birthday.
	After a delicious dinner, which was ar-
ranged, and partaken of in groups, formed
by different branches of the family conven-
ing around tables prepared for the occasion,
a program was given, which consisted of
recitations, singing, music, etc. The origi-
nal articles offered were, an excellent poem
of welcome and greeting, written and read
by Miss Arretta Young, and an appropriate
poem by Lula, recited by her little son,
Heber Greene Richards. A song by
Edward Young, of Provo, youngest son of
the late President Joseph Young, was
rendered in a style which proved the young
man’s rich, baritone voice to be in keeping
with the musical ability of the family of
which he is a member. Other songs and
recitations were given from members of the
Family Association; also an impromptu ad-
dress by Seymour B. Young jun. At 10
p. m. the exercises closed, and benediction
was pronounced by Elder Evan F. Greene.
	After many kind words of parting,
with hopes of meeting again, perhaps in
one year more, the party dispersed and the
old Lion House was again left in the quiet
state which of late years it usually assumes.
