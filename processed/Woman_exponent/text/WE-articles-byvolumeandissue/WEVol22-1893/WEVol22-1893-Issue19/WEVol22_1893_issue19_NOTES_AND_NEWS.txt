NOTES AND NEWS.
	MRS. A. R. TEMPLE is president of the 
First National Bank, of Lexington,
Nebraska, and Miss Temple is vice-presi-
dent.
	MISS MARIE OUTHWAITE, of De Pere,
Wis., has passed an examination as an ex-
pert drummer before the musical jury ap-
pointed by Theodore Thomas. She is only
eight years old.
	THE trustees of the estates of Lady
Henry Somerset are petitioning the courts
for an injunction to restrain her from clos-
ing all the saloons on her property, which
she is seeking to do.
	DR. SARAH HACKETT STEVENSON re-
ceived the degree of Doctor of Science, last
week, from the University of Pennsylvania.
It was conferred at the same time upon
Prof. John Fiske, William T. Harris and
Gov. Pattison.
	THE Countess of Aberdeen made about
$100,000 from the Irish village at the
World’s Fair. It will be used to promote
domestic industry among Irish peasants.
	AT the convention of the State Federa-
tion of Woman’s Clubs of Massachusetts, 
in annual session at Marlboro, recently,
the reports showed 718 women’s organiza-
tions in the Commonwealth, with a total,
membership of 50,000. If these 50,000
intelligent, high-minded women had votes,
