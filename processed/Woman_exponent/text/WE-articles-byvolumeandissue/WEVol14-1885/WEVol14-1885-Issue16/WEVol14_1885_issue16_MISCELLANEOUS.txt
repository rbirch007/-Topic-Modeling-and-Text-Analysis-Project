MISCELLANEOUS.

	Women jump at conclusions and generally
hit; men reason things out logically and general-
ly miss it.
	A woman always carries her purse in her
hand so that other women will see it; a man
carries his in his inside pocket so that his wife
wont see it.
	If instead of a gem, or even a flower, we
could cast the gift of a lovely thought into the
heart of a friend, that would be giving as the
angels give.—Geo. MacDonald.
	An old proverb says: If a person dies without
the services of a doctor, then a coronor must
be called in and a jury empanelled to inquire
and determine upon the cause of death; but if
a doctor attended the case; then no coroner
and jury are needed, as everybody knows why
the person died.—Medical Herald.
	A well known dealer in pictures, a candidate
in the Radical interest for one of the divi-
sions of Lancashire, was lately making a
speech, and in the course of it drew the usual
Radical caricature sketch of the relative
achievements of the Liberal and the Tory party.
After proving to his own satisfaction that
everything was good, from the solar system
and the precession of the equinoxes down to
cheap calico and school-boards, had been in-
vented by the Liberals, and that all the evils
of the universe arose from the incursions into
power of the Tories, he wound up by saying,
"Look on this picture and on that!" At this
a man in the hall, who had listened imp atient-
ly to the catalogue of good and evil, cried out,
"None of your blooming shop! Talk politics."
