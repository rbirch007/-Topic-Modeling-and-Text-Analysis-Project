EDITOR EXPONENT:

	I thought that a few lines from this isolated
place might perhaps be of interest. We are
but few in number, but feel to do our part in
the great work that we, the Latter-day Saints,
are engaged in.
	The Relief Society is in a good condition.
Since the death of our beloved president, Sister
Salenda E. Huntsman, which occurred June
10th, 1884 Sister E. Truman has presided.
Sister M. M. Holt presides over the Primary,
and all our little ones are greatly interested.
