		ST. GEORGE STAKE

The Relief Society and Y. L. M. I. A. of St.
George Stake held their quarterly conference
in the Lyceum, St. George, on Friday, the
12th of June ult. There was a good attend-
ance. The reports from the various settle-
ments were very encouraging. There is evi-
dently a great awakening among the Saints, a
closer union, and firmer faith in God our
Heavenly Father. The sisters are on hand
for the fulfillment of any duty, and desire to
promote and uphold the interests of the King-
dom of God; they have no fears as to the final
outcome of the persecutions by which the
faithful are at present being tried; nor do they
feel in the least disposed to retreat in their obe-
dience to any of the principles which have been
revealed from heaven.

﻿	The young ladies are improving, and some
are gaining for themselves testimony of the
truth of the Gospel as revealed to the Prophet
Joseph in these last days.
	The Primary Conference was held on the
13th. Quite a number of the parents, as well
as the children, were present, and were ad-
dressed by President John D. T. McAllister,as
well as the Stake President of the Primary As-
sociation, Sister Julia H. Ivins, and others.
The children seem to appreciate the instruct-
tion given to them in their Primary meetings,
and take a lively interest in preparing their
exercises and rendering them in a creditable
manner.
	May the Lord bless the youth of Zion, and
give those to whose care their training is en-
trusted the faithfulness and perseverance neces-
sary for their important duties.
