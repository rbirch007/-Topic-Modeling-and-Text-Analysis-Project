SALT LAKE STAKE.
	The Relief Society Conference of Salt Lake
Stake was held in the Fourteenth Ward As-
sembly Rooms, June 26th, commencing at 10
a.m., Mrs. M. I. Horne presiding. After sing-
ing and prayer by Coun. E. S. Taylor and sing-
ing, the minutes of the March Conference were
read and accepted and roll of Presidents called.
Twenty-nine war is were represented, all of
them as being in good working order, the
general feeling being to improve in wisdom
and knowledge, and to increase in faith and
works of charity and usefulness.

[Column 2]

	Prest. Horne made a very able address to
the officers, giving specific and timely instruc-
tion, and exhorting all to prepare for the great
events that are about to transpire. Conference
then adjourned till 2 p.m. Singing. Benedic-
tion by Mrs. B. W.Smith. 
	Afternoon session:	Singing. Prayer by
Secretary E. Howard. Singing. Remarks 
were made by Mrs. Minerva W. Snow, Prest.
of the Relief Society in St. George Stake.
She spoke upon the spiritual progress of the
daughters of Zion who are in the path of duty
and who testify of the goodness of God; said
such were bound together by the Holy Spirit.
She was followed by her Counselor, Mrs. Annie
Ivins, who referred to the labors of the sisters
of St. George, and the blessings that attended
their labors, and the peace, quiet and union
that existed there.
	Coun. E. S. Taylor, Secretary E. Howard,
Mrs. Sarah M. Kimball, Secretary of Central
Board of Relief Society, Dr. Ellis R. Shipp,
Dr. Maggie C. Shipp, Dr. E. S. Barney, each
made a brief, but interesting and edifying ad-
dress, and Prest. Horne made closing remarks,
reviewing what had been spoken of by the sis-
ters, and exhorting all to diligence and to
practice economy. Among the subjects treated
upon by the sisters was the storing of grain
and donating to the Deseret Hospital.
	Conference adjourned for three months with
singing and benediction.
