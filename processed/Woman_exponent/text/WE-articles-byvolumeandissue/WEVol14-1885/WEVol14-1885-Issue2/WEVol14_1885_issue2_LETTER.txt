	LETTER.

	The extracts published below are from a letter
written by a maiden lady, who resides upon the
Isle of Wight, to her cousin, an English gentle-
woman, one well known and respected among this
people, and very highly respected by all who have
the pleasure of her acquaintance, in this city.
The lady is one of high social standing, and es-
pecially interested in educational interests.—ED.]
	“I have not yet acknowledged
three enclosures of papers—one containing two
of the WOMAN'S EXPONENT, and a magazine
containing the little book—all so beautifully and
firmly done up, that it seems quite an art to show
such expertness.
	“As to Miss Faithful,I have already heard of
her in a way that somewhat surprised me, though
of small import in comparison with matters of
real moment; but in the absence of any explana
tion of her conduct, as represented in the Novem-
ber number of the EXPONENT, one is at a loss to
understand the ungracious and ungrateful part of
receiving hospitality, kindnesses and courtesies
from strangers, all the more worthy of considera-
tion on that very account, and then making so 
base a return! Bearing fully in mind that she
might conscientiously differ on some points of


great importance, she might well have justified
the expectation, suggested in the article on the
subject, in the said number, that she would at
leest deal honestly and fairly in the matter, and I
should have thought, also, have warmly acknowl-
edged the kindness shown to herself.
	“Of course the passage, in which she speaks or
'the keen eye of the Prophet' as discerning his op-
portunities in reference to the 'fertile spot,'
showed great ignorance of what she is writing
about, as those who read the leading papers and
journals know that it is an accepted fact that the
change from a mere wilderness into a fertile spot
—indeed I think I have seen it called an earthly
paradise—was due tothe talent, energy, persever-
ance and industry of Brigham Young and his fol-
lowers, and as I have read the credit of it was
fully accorded to them, and that without any pre-
tence of sympathy with their religious views. I
am a dear  lover of fairness and straight-forward
dealing.”
