	LINES TO THE MEMORY OF CLARA.

Dear, faithful Clara’s left us,
	She’s mingling with the blest—
Gone from this world of sorrow
	To a happy home of rest.

She will no longer suffer pain,
	As while on earth she stayed,
And she'll gain eternal glory,
	For in God her hopes were laid.

Yes, her earthly toils are over,
	She’s free from care and pain;
She's sleeping with the blest
	'Til the Savior comes again.

Then should we mourn for her, dear friends,
	Or to despair be driven?—
For she has been a faithful child,
	And we know she is in heaven.

Though 'tis hard to part with those we love,
	And loved she's always been,


True to her parents and her friends,
	And free from every sin.

Then let us all live faithful,
	That when we sleep ‘neath the sod,
Our names may all be written	
	In the autograph of God.
