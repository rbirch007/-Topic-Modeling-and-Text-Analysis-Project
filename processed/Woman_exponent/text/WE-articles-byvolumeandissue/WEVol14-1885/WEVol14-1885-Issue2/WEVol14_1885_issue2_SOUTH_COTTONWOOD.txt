SOUTH COTTONWOOD.

	The eighteenth annual meeting held at
South Cottonwood, May 18th, 1885, President

[Column 2]
Janette Y. Cahoon presiding. Singing. Pray-
er by Bishop Rawlius. Singing. Minutes of
last annual meeting and yearly report read
and approved.
	The President, on account of having moved
away from the ward and not being able to at-
tend her meetings, wished to resign her office.
Sisters M. I. Horne. E. S. Taylor and the
Bishop being present, it was motioned and
unanimously sustained that we accept her
resignation. A vote of thanks was rendered
for her long service and faithfulness in the dis-
charge of her duties, and her many good ex-
amples set forth for us to appreciate and follow
after. Therefore the Society was necessarily
reorganized. Counselor Mary Rawlins was
nominated Presient; Ann Wheeler and Phebe
Boyce; Couselors; Harriet A. Walker, Secre-
tary; Ann Boyce, Assistant Secretary; Ellen
Wheeler, Treasurer, in the place of Francis
Litson, deceased, who faithfully performed that
office from 1871 until her demise, Feb. 17th,
1885. All the officers were unanimously sus-
tained by the assembly.
	Our meeting was well attended, and we re-
ceived excellent instructions from those who
addressed us, the principal speakers being Sis-
ters Horne and Taylor. Our meeting was
adjourned until the 17th of next May. Dis-
missed with singing and prayer.
