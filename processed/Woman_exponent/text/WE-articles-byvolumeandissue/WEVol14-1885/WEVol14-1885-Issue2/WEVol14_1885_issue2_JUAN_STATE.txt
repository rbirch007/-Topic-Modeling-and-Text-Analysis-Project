			JUAN STATE.

	The Relief Society held its quarterly confer-
ence at Levan in the new R. S. Hall, on the
13th inst. The reports, both written and ver-
bal, were very encouraging and spoke in high
praise of the vast amount of labor that had
been accomplished during the past quarter.
	The sisters seem to be awake to the sense of
their duty, and feel a deep interest in the wel-
fare of each other, and desire to obey the
principles of their holy religion, and serve
their God with one accord.
	Words of instruction and counsel were given
by the brethren and sisters who presided, which
was listened to with marked attention, and I
trust we will long remember the timely ad-
vice and counsel given.
	The sisters of Levan, with the help of the
brethren, have recently completed a very com-
fortable hall, which will be used for a meeting
house, for the present, as well as for the Relief
Society of that branch.
	The branches of the Society throughout the
Stake have been active, both in donating and
administering to the wants of the needy, and
comforting the sick. Their efforts to do good
to their fellow creatures have been crowned
with success, and the blessing of the Lord
has attended their labor of love.
	The Relief Society of Nephi celebrated the
anniversary of its organization on Tuesday,
the 23rd inst., and had a very enjoyable time,
listeing to addresses, songs and recitations,
given by the brethren and sisters.
		M. HAYTE, Stake Secretary.
