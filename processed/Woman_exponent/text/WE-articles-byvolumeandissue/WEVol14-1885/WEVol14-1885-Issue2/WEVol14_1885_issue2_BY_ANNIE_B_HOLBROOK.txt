BY ANNIE B. HOLBROOK.

	"Your labor is not in vain in the Lord.

O patient hearts, that silent wait
	Through weary, fruitless years,
For tardy recompense and late,—
	A rainbow in the mist of tears,—
The blest, old promise shall remain,
	In Him, ye labor "not in vain."

O weary hands! with toil borne down,
	And grasping only leaves,
With eager search in stubble brown,
	Where stand the golden sheaves,
Whate'er ye lose, this guerdon gain,
	In Him, ye labor "not in vain."

O laboring feet! returning late,
	Though rougher grows the way,
Climb upward, where through glory's gate
	Soft shines the smile of the day,
Its presage traced on hill and plain,
In Him, ye labor "not in vain."

Ah, "not in vain" the secret prayers
	The sacrifice complete;
Before his face some angel bears
	The rising incense sweet,
And unseen choirs repeat the strain,
	In Him, ye labor "not in vain."	Ex.
