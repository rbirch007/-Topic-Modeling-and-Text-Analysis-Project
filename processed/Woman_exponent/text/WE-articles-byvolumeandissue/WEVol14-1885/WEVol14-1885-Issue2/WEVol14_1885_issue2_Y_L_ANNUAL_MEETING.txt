Y. L. ANNUAL MEETING.

	The first annual meeting of the Y. L. M. I.
A. of the Seventh Ward since its reorganiza-
tion, was held in the Relief Society
room May 7th, 1885, Sarah Eddington
presiding. Opened by singing, "We'll sing
all hail to Jesus' name." Prayer offered
by Mrs. Minnie James. Singing, "Glori-
ous things of thee are spoken." Roll
called, showing thirty-eight members present,
and minutes of previous meeting read and ap-
proved.
	The programme for the evening was then
taken up and carried out as follows: Remarks
upon Section 123 of the Doctrine and Coven-
ants, Annie Blazzard; Recitation, Alice Mc-
Lachlan. Entitled "Kate Maloney; Question
answered by Amelia Woods, "Why is present
revelation necessary?" The tenth number of
the Young Ladies' Journal was read by Emma
Rose; a song was then rendered by the Associa-
tion. "Joseph Smith's First Prayer." This
concluding the programme, remarks were then
made by Sisters Dougal, Richards, Taylor,
Horne and Colebrook; seemingly directing
their remarks adapted to the present time—
the persecutions of the Saints, patronizing
home industry, and of young people marrying
those not of our faith.
	The following named officers were then sus-
tained in their positions: Sarah Eddington,
President; Carrie Thorn and Emma Rose,
Counselors; Millie Foster, Secretary; Alice
McLachlan, Assistant Secretary; Nellie Mc-
Lachlan, Treasurer.
	President Sarah Eddington was then pre-
sented with six volumes of the Speaker's Gar-
iand. Programme for next meeting then read.
Singing, "Love at Home." Benediction by
Mrs. M. A. Lambert.
	MILLIE FOSTER, Secretary.
