			NEPHI.
	The quarterly conference of Juab Stake Re-
lief Society convened in the Nephi Tabernacle
at 10 a.m., April 9th, 1886, Prest. Mary
Pitchforth presiding. The branches were re-
ported by their Presidents in a favorable and
commendable manner; the members were alive
to their duties, and striving to prove them-
selves loyal to the principles of truth.
   Prest. Mary Pitchforth, Prest. Marinda By-
ran, Coun. Amy Bigler and others offered in-
teresting and spirited remarks, filled with ad-
vice and counsel to the sisters, to live their
religion, keep a strict watchcare over their
children, and to educate them and prepare
them for usefulness in the kingdom of God.
   Coun. Charles Sperry, Bishop David Udall
and several other brethren were present, and
took part in praising God for the many bless-
ings He has bestowed upon us as a people, and
counseling and encouraging the sisters in their
good work of providing for the needy and
comforting those in distress. The speeches
were interspersed with songs and recitations,
making in all a very pleasant and interesting
time. After singing and benediction, the
conference adjourned for three months.
   Owing to a visit from the diphtheria fiend

[Column 2]
in  the month of March, our meetings  and
schools were suspended for few weeks; our
conference was also postponed until the present
date.
