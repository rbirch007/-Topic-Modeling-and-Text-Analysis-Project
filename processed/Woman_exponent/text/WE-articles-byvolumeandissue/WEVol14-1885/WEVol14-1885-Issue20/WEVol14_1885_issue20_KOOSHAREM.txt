		KOOSHAREM.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The young ladies of this place held their
annual meeting February 10th, 1886. The
house was decorated with the pretty and use-
ful work of the young ladies, the time was
spent in listening to songs, essays and recita-
tions by the members of the association, and
we also received some good instructions from
the leading brethren of our settlement; they
were well pleased to see the young ladies do-
ing so well; the spirit of the Lord was with us
and all seemed to feel well. On the 12th of Feb-
ruary we had a very pleasant time; a few of
us gathered at the house of sister Barbara.

[Column 2]

Bailey, while she was out at her daughter's
for a short time, it being her 70th birthday
It gave her a surprise on her return home to
find her table spread with the good things of
the earth. There were some small presents
given to her, and the boys turned out and
hauled her 3 loads of wood and cut it all up
in the afternoon. We spent the afternoon
with her and all went home rejoicing and will,
no doubt, always remember the pleasant time
they had.
		MARY A. HATCH, President,
		JOSEPHA O. WRIGHT, Secretary.
