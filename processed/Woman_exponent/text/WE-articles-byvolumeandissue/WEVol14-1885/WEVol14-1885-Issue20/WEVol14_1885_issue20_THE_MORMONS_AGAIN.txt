THE MORMONS AGAIN.

	Senator Edmunds' bill, confiscating the
church property of the Mormons, has not had
a tendency to bring out the best and most
Christ-like principles of that people.  It was
an act unworthy of the great nation; there is
no question that some of the Mormons are as
honest and conscientious in their belief in
polygamy as Senator Edmunds is in his religi-
ous belief.  It is well known by all that there
is a large class of Mormons who are earnest
in, and upholders of, the Mormons religion,
with that single exception of the doctrine of
polygamy; yet this latter class must suffer with
their more extreme brothers.  Putting this all
aside, Edmunds’ bill cannot be considered the
proper way to deal with this question.  If it is
a crime, it is at the same time a religion, and
the extremists who condemn it the most would
condemn Shakerism, and that is a religion
also. We have scanned very closely the
columns of the WOMAN'S EXPONENT, a paper
published in Salt Lake City, and we find it
teeming with a pure,ennobling,fervent,and for-
giving spirit that is not excelled, if equaled, by
any religious paper among our exchanges, and
that is what called forth this article, for we
expected to see a less Christian spirit exhibit
itself under this new attitude of our govern-
ment.—Peabody Reporter.
