ST. JOSEPH STAKE.
	The ladies' quarterly conference of the St.
Joseph Stake of Zion convened at Pima, 
March 19th, 1886, President Willmarth East
presiding. On the stand of the Stake authori-
ties were Prest. C. Layton and Counselors,
also Bishop John Taylor of Pima.
	After the usual opening exercises, the
minutes and reports were read and approved.
Verbal reports of the following wards were
given by their respective presidents; Pima,
Curtis, Graham, Central, Thatcher and Layton,
all of which were reported in good running 
order. 
	Prest. East and Coun. Ransome addressed
the conference, the leading subjects spoken up-
on being the persecutions of the Saints at the

[Column 2]

present time as compared with facts of ancient
history. The speakers referred to the Ladies'
Mass Meeting held in Salt Lake City recently,
and were proud to hear the mothers and
daughters of Zion stand forth in defense of
the most sacred principles ever revealed for
the exaltation of the human family. We
were urged to be humble and prayerful before
the Lord, attend our meetings, and to be ever
ready to respond to the calls made by those in
authority over us, that we may attain to that
degree of glory that is in store for the faithful.
The time was very limited, the afternoon being
devoted to the Primary, in giving reports and
receiving instructions. An excellent spirit
prevailed, and all felt well paid for and great-
ly blessed in attending. Conference adjourned
for three months.
