EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Our Society is in good running order; the
sisters are all willing and on hand to fill any
call, no matter what it may be, and a good
feeling prevails.
	We held our annual meeting September the
16th, 1885, in the cedar grove, Prest. Margaret
Anderson presiding. Singing, "O my Father."
Prayer offered by Prest A. K. Thurber, Sing-
ing, "Did you think to pray?" Minutes of
previous meeting read and approved; and also
the statistical and financial report. We had
the pleasure of having our Stake President,
Sister Elizabeth Bean, and her First Counselor,
Thirza Thurber, and also our Stake Secretary,
Sister Hunt, and our leading local brethren,
from whom we received much good instruction
in regard to our duties and privileges. The
bowery was well decorated with handiwork and
articles of home industry from the sisters.
	The afternoon was spent in singing, dancing,
etc. The day passed off pleasantly, and all
returned home feeling we had spent an enjoy-
able and instructive day.
	We hold our meetings semi-monthly, and
our working meetings once a month. The good
attendance shows the desire of the sisters to be
in doing what we can to help along the king-
dom of God; storing grain, by raising it, and
in other ways. Our Society owns a number of
sheep, the wool being worked up in cloth and
other articles.
	We are blessed with pretty good health, in
general. Our Heavenly Father has seen fit to
leave us in peace, thus far, but we deeply sym-
pathize with our persecuted brethren and sis-
ters, and our prayers are ascending up to
heaven daily in their behalf. We feel that a
day will come when all will get their just re-
ward, and we desire to be firm in the faith to
the end, for we know that the Lord is on our
side, as long as we will keep His command-
ments. We remain,
	Your sisters in the Gospel,
		MARGARET ANDERSON, Prest.,
