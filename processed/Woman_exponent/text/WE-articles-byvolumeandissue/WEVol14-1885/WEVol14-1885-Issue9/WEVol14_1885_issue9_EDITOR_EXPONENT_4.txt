EDITOR EXPONENT:
  On the 26th inst., in company with Sisters
Sarah M. Kimball and Elizabeth Townsend, I


attended an annual meeting of the Y. L. M. I.
A. in Providence, and we had a very pleasant
and interesting time.
  Mrs. Lowe, a daughter of the late Seth M.
Blair, has faithfully and efficiently presided
over the Association ever since its organization,
but her many increasing cares and responsibili-
ties rendering it advisable, with the consent of
the Bishop of the ward, who was present, she
tendered her resignation, and Miss Emily
Theurer was chosen to fill the vacancy, with
Mary A. Marler and Emma Hammond as
Counselors.			     E. R. S. S.


"Mothers and teachers do not take much
pains to train their children and pupils into
good habits of enunciation. They are carefully
taught to sing, but they are not carefully
taught to read and speak; yet more than half
the charm of all social intercourse depends
upon the agreeable use of the voice."
