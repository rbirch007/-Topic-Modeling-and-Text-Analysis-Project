"AT REST!"

Through mossy tombstones, old and grey,
That near a ruined abbey lay, 
	Pensive I strayed;
So dim th' inscriptions on the stones,
Unknown the names of those whose bones
	Beneath them laid.
Unnoticed, came the ev'ning sky;
Unminded rose the unconscious sigh
	Up from the breast,
(Dreaming, perhaps, of brighter days,)
When, full beneath my wond'ring gaze
	I saw "At Rest!"
Two words, there, full upon the sod,
As if, with faces turned to God
	In calm repose;
No tombstone near, no fresh-made monnd,
No tear-dimmed eye, no ling'ring sound
	That spoke of woes.
But, like a daisy in the grass,
Unseen of thoughtless as they pass,
	(Half hid in green,)
Mayhap by loving eyes were traced,
By loving hands on tomb were placed
	These words, I ween.
Nor was it dreamed these words, by chance,
Might stay the careless, slow advance
	Of wayward feet;
Nor thought they from that tomb should fall
And, to a thoughtless mind recall
	That life is fleet,
That days are few; that hours speed fast;
That, though we strive from first to last
	With steadfast strain,
Life's worthy battle ne'er is won--
Something that's needful to be done
	Will yet remain.
Some wayward habit to abjure;
Some ling'ring thought, not wholly pure,
	A joyless hate
That from the heart must needs be torn;
From some friend's path to pluck a thorn--
	'Tis ne'er too late.
Thus striving on, life must be spent,
Still something to forgive, repent,
	Ere yet we're blest;
Ere yet the soul speeds unto God;
Ere yet beneath the freshen'd sod
	We're laid "At Rest!"
"At Rest!" At Rest!" O solemn thought!
With deep, with soulful quest'nings fraught,
	Thou fill'st the mind;
And joy too bring'st. What can express
The rapture spirits feel that press
	On, unconfined!
To think the madden'd, fever'd brain
Shall rest--life's never ending strain
	At length must cease,
To feel the bleeding heart no more
Shall bleed, to know the test is o'er!
	Ah! This is peace!
Ere yet the spirit leaves this coil,
But once to gaze back on the toil
	Of weary life,
And, seeing, know (while weak and frail,
While much remains o'er which to wail--
	Unconquer'd strife,)
Thou still hast won th' approving smile
Of Him o'erwatching all the while,
	Then art thou blest!
Bound, Spirit! at thy ransomed birth!
Sleep, Body, in the waiting earth!
	And sigh "At Rest!" --Millenial Star. 
	R. W. Sloan.
