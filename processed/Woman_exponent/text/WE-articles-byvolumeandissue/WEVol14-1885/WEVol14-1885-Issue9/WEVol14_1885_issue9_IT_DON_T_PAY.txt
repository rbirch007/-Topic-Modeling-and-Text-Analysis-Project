IT DON'T PAY.

	It don't pay to have one citizen in the coun-
ty jail because another citizen sells him
liquor.
	It don't pay to have one citizen in the luna-
tic asylum because another citizen sold him
liquor.
	It don't pay to have fifty workmen ragged,
to have one saloon keeper dressed in broad-
cloth and flush with money.
	It don't pay to have ten smart, active, intelli-
gent boys transformed into thieves to enable
one man to lead an easy life by selling them
liquor.
	It don't pay to give one man, for $15 a
quarter, license to sell liquor, and then spend
$5,000 on a trial on another man for buying
that liquor and committing murder under its
influences.—Christian Secretary.
