LOA, PIUTE CO.

	Anniversary of the Relief Society of the
Fremont Ward, of the Sevier Stake of Zion,
held June the 19th, 1885. Commenced at
10 a.m. Singing by the choir. Prayer by
John Ellet. Reports of last year read by the
secretary. Remarks by Bishop C. H. Black-
burn; An Address by Prest. Darton; Speech
by J. W. Young: "I think it is forty-two
years since I first saw Joseph Smith; he was
then talking to the Relief Society; it has been
kept up ever since, and the sisters should teach
the young the right way, and not to follow the
fashions of Babylon. There are not many so-
cieties that can meet to-day, as we do, in peace,
and none of their husbands or fathers in prison.
The sisters should be very thankful for this
privilege." a Song, by Minnie Morrell and
company; a Speech, by Mary Ellet, Remarks,
by V. L. Blackburn; Song, by C. M. Young
and daughter; Speech, by Sarah J. Morrell;
Speech, by Father Allen Taylor.
	The officers of the Society were then put
before the meeting and sustained. Singing by
the choir. Dismissed by John Ellet. We
had dinner at two o'clock, and had an enjoy-
able time together in the afternoon, and a
party at night. All having enjoyed them-
selves, we retired at twelve o'clock, well satis-

fied. Alice Darton, President; Mary Ellet
and V. L. Blackburn, Counselors; Sarah J.
Blackburn, Secretary; Hannah Blackburn,
Assistant Secretary.
	Re-organization of the Loa, Fremont Relief
Society, September 3rd, 1885: Sister Margaret
Taylor, President; Nancy Blackburn and
Sarah J. Forseith, Counselors; Sarah J. Black-
burn, Secretary; Hannah Blackburn, Assistant
Secretary; Alice Darton, Treasurer.
		S. J. BLACKBURN, Secretary.
