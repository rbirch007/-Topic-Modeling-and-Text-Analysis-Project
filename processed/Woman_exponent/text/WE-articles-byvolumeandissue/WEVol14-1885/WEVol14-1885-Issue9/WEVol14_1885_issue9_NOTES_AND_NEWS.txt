NOTES AND NEWS.

	"Mrs. Marietta M. Bones is deputy clerk of
the district court of Webster, Dakota."
	"Kate Greenaway will offer the holiday sea-
son a new book, called 'The Marigold Gar-
den.'"
	"Miss Cleveland is a successful author. Her
book has already run through ten editions, and
has been translated into several languages."
	"Lady Dufferin has formed in India a Na-
tional Association for supplying 'female medi-
cal aid,' i.e., women doctors to attend the
wives of the natives in sickness."
	"Mlle. Sinay has been awarded by the jury
of the Paris Conservatoire a first prize for
violin-playing; Mlles. Gauthier, Dautin, and
Boutin a premier accessit, and Mlle. Magnier a
second accessit."
	"Mrs. Kingsley Harrison, a daughter of
Charles Kingsley, is the author of the novels
'Mrs. Lorimer' and 'Colonel Enderby's Wife,'
under the pen-name Lucas Malet. For a long
time her first book was supposed to be the
work of a man."
	Without a doubt, the mother's superior or
inferior intelligence is the most deeply im-
printed upon the child.—Horatio Yates, M. D.,
Professor Queen's University.
	"Colonel Higginson's 'History of the United
States,' most of which has appeared in Harp-
er's Magazine, is soon to be printed in book
form."
