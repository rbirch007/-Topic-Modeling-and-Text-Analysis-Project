EDITORIAL NOTES.

	SISTERS Sarah M. Kimball and Elizabeth 
Howard and Dr. Romania B. Pratt attended the 
Sisters' Conference in Tooele, on the 12th and 
13th insts. 

	THE Primary Fair of this Stake will open in the 
Social hall on Thursday, Sept. 17th, under the 
supervision of Mrs. Ellen C. Clawson and her 
counselors, Mrs. Lydia Ann Wells and Mrs. 
Camilla C. Cobb. 

	SISTER Eliza R. Snow Smith returned to Logan 
on the 11th inst., to resume her labors in the 
Temple, where she had previously been laboring 
for some time.  Though so far advanced in years, 
she is enjoying good health, and her mind is  as 
bright and active in years gone by.

	THE Relief Society Conference of this Stake 
will be held in the Fourteenth Ward Assembly 
Rooms of Friday, Sept. 18th; and on Saturday, 
19th, the Primary Conference will be held in the 
Salt Lake Assembly Hall, commencing at 10 
a. .m.; and the Young Ladies Conference will 
convene at 2 p. m. in the Fourteenth Ward. 

	ON Thursday, the 3rd, we attended a Primary
meeting in the 21st Ward, Mrs. Mary Davis pre-
siding.  The children were orderly, and rendered 
their exercises very creditably, and speeches 
suited to the occasion were made by the visitors 
present, among whom were Mrs. Hannah T. King,  
Dr. Ellen B. Ferguson, Mrs. Emma Toone, Mrs. 
Ellen C. Clawson, and others.  The singing exer-
cises were specially fine.  Sister Davis and coun-
selors deserve great credit for their energy and 
diligence in advancing the children, and promoting 
their spiritual improvement.
