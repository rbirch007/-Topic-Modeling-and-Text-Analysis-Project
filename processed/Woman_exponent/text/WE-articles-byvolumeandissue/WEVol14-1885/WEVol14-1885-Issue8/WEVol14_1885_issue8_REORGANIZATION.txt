	REORGANIZATION
	A meeting of the First Ward Relief Society 
was held in the First Ward meeting house on
Thursday, Sept. 3rd 1885.  There were pre-
senr, besides the officers and members of the
Relief Society, Bishop Joseph Warburton and
his counselors, Joseph Booth and Alexander
Steel.  The President, Sister Miriam G. Chase,
resigned her office as President on account of
old age, and the treasurer, Sarah J. Sement
also.  It was found necessary to reorganize the
Society, whereupon the following officers were
elected, and were set apart by the Bishop and
his counselors.  Margaret F. Steel, President;
Annie Young and Jane E. Van Tassel; coun-
selors; Annie S. Musser, secretary; Mary W.
Musser, treasurer; Caroline Grouch, President
of the Teachers.
