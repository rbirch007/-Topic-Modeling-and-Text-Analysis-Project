EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The Y. L. M. I. A. of the Fourth Ward,
Ogden, held a grand reunion on Wednesday,
August 26th, 1885.  It was in honor of the
parents of the members of the Society, and was
held in the meeting-house and in the pavilion
on Lester Park. At the meeting in the fore-
noon some excellent exercises were given in
the meeting-house. Sister Ruthinda E. Monch,
who has for six years been the President of the
association and given the utmost satisfaction,
resigned her position, which was accepted. At
the close of the forenoon meeting one of the
finest feasts ever given in Ogden awaited the
people in the pavilion. It was prepared by the
members. Three hundred persons feasted from
the good things, and there was much left which
was given to the poor. Among the prominent
persons present were: President L. W. Shurt-
liff and counsel; Sister Jane S. Richards, Stake
President, of the Relief Society; Sister Sarah
A. Herrick, Prest. Y. L. M. I. A.; Hon. Joseph
Stanford and wife, Hon. Jos. A. West and
wife, and presidents of the city societies. After
dinner a fine programme was carried out in the
pavilion, which had been beautifully decorated
for the occasion. The exercises were of the
highest order. Speeches were made by Presi-
dent Jane S. Richards and several of the
brethren. Sister Monch received deserved
credit for her diligent labors in behalf of the
association. In the evening nearly one hun-
dred couples enjoyed themselves greatly in a
pleasant party in the pavilion. The kindliest
feeling prevailed throughout. We give below
a well written essay on “The Object of the
Reunion,” written and read in the afternoon
by Mrs E. H. Anderson.
