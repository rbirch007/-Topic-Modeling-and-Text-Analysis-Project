EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The gay and festive Primary Fair was held
last Thursday, under most favorable circum-
stances, as to the products of the little men
and women of the association, most delightful
weather and the pleasing presence of Sister
Helena Madsen, Prest. of the Stake Primaries
and her two counselors.
	At early morning deft fingers were busy
hanging up those ingenious patch works, called
quilts, tidies, shawls, socks, dresses, etc., till you
were surprised to see so much labor and skill.
The boys came trooping in with their works
of agricultural implements, pigeons, plymouth
rocks, etc., etc., while a table cried "enough,
enough of the culinary department." The
little cooks were consciously proud.
	One little fellow, by the way, a cripple by
paralysis, Ernest C. Candland, showed his
taste by some drawings of animals.
	After feasting the eye on the useful and
ornamental, the feast of reason was introduced
—recitations, dialogues, songs, remarks etc.,
made pleasurable impressions. All were so
wisely chosen and rendered, we cannot mention
all, hence all was excellent.
	The programme filled, that other part that
man loves so much, the sure road to his pocket,
and blessing the viands prepared and waiting,
then filled with the good things of earth, the
Bishop, President Madsen, her Counselors, the
Stake Primary Clerk, Prest. Emma Heyward
and her Counselors prayed heaven's choicest
blessing on the little ones and we all said amen.
