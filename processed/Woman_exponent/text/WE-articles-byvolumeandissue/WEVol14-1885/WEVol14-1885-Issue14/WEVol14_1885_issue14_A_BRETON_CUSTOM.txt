A BRETON CUSTOM.

	A Vienna letter to the San Francisco Alta
says: On Michaelmas day all the young
women in a district of Brittany who desire to
enter the married state assemble on a particu-
lar bridge, tricked out in all their finery, and
take their seats on the parapet. The eligible
young men from Penze and the neighboring
parishes presently make their appearance.
They scan the features of the seated damsels,
and when at length a swain has fixed his choice
on a particular nymph, he appraoches and
offers her his hand to help her to the ground.
If she accepts his hand for this particular ser-
vice she accepts it for life. The parents are
never very far off on these occasions, and if
they raise no objections, the young couple
shake hands, and the wedding soon follows.
