IN MEMORIAM

	Of Mary Elijah Etta Haskell, daughter of Thales H.
and Margaret J. Haskell, who departed this life Nov. 3rd,
1885, in Salt Lake City, aged fourteen years and thirteen
d
	Her home is in Bluff City, San Juan Co., Utah. On
the 11th of last July she left her home and parents to visit
her sister who lives in the Twelfth Ward, Salt Lake City.
While there, she was taken with the typhoid fever, and
died, leaving her parents almost broken-hearted. She was
a member of the Relief Society, also the Y. L. M. I. Associa-
tion of condolence:
	Whereas, Providence has seen fit to call from our midst
our much esteemed sister and co-laborer in the cause of
truth to finish the labor so nobly begun on this earth in a
higher sphere, we, the members of these Associations, offer
our heart-felt sympathy to the bereaved parents and friends
of the deceased, and although her loss is keenly felt, both
as a faithful member of the Associations and a devoted
daughter, we trust the parents will feel reconciled, know-
ing she was taken from a world of sorrow by a just and
merciful God.
	Resolved, That a copy of these Resolutions be placed
on the record of these Associations, and that one be sent to
the EXPONENT.
			JANE M. WALTON, President R. S.,
			MARY N. JONES, President Y. L. M. I. A.

Dedicated to the memory of Miss Mary Haskell, who
		departed this life Nov. 3rd 1885.
