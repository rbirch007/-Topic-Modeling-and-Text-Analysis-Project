EDITORIAL NOTES.

	THE Territorial Legislature meets on Monday,
the 11th of January.
	ON Monday, the 14th inst, the Supreme Court
of the United States decided the appeal taken by
Angus M. Cannon on writ of error from the Third
District Court of this Territory. The definition
of the term cohabitation as laid down by Judge
Zane was the point in argument, and it was affirmed,
with Judges Miller and Field dissenting. 
	THE new Relief Society Hall of the Seventeenth
Ward, Salt Lake City, was dedicated on the 8th
inst., by prayer from Apostle John Henry Smith.
The building is of brick, is 20x30 ft. in size and
cost, with the piece of land on which it stands
and some little incidentals, $1,484.50, all of which
is paid for. Remarks were made on the occassion
by Bro. Nathan Davis, who was Bishop when the
society was organized, Apostle Smith, who suc-
ceeded him in that office, Bishop Tingey, the
present incumbent, and Bro. Alonzo E. Hyde, his
counselor. A report of the society's workings
was also read by the secretary, in which the highly
creditable showing is made that since its organiza-
tion $5,431.83 has been contributed to the poor.
An agreeable entertainment at the house of Sister
Joseph Bull concluded the ceremonies of the
day.
