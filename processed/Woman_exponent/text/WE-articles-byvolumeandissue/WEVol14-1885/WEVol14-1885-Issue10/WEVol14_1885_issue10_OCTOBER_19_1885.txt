	  	OCTOBER 19, 1885.

Another year has passed! again we find you,
	Still patiently content to watch and wait,
And relatives and friends come to remind you
	The day returns, and you are seventy-eight.

Aged and young and infants pass before you,
	Into the beautiful, the blest abode;
While your dear angels lovingly watch o'er you,
	And gently lead you on in life's rough road.

How many cherished ones are fondly waiting,
	To welcome you with joyous, rapturous bliss,
In that dear home which you, anticipating,
	Are almost wedded to e’en more than this.

How many faithful ones on earth will mourn you,
	When thro' the small, dark vale that lies between,
Kind angel messengers have softly borne you,
	Into the purer life, the fairer scene.

So shall it be, for kindness winneth ever
	Such love and friendship as are felt for you;
Whether in life or death, your heart will never
	Fail to receive its measure, full and true.

			Affectionately,
									LULA.
