DEAR EDITOR:
	Notwithstanding the machinations of the
evil one, who seeks to annoy, destroy and tear
down what God has decreed shall be established,
we rejoice in our knowledge of the Gospel, and
feel to lay hold on the prophecy of old, that
Zion will be redeemed, and those who live
near to the Lord can feel the peace, joy and
satisfaction promised to all who serve Him
and acknowledge His hand in all things.
Though we may be called to pass through
deep waters of affliction, still He is near, and
that for to bless and sanctify to us our deepest
distress. To us it is a great privilege to live
and see the hand dealings of God amongst the
nations and with His people, for surely the
Lord is at work, and who can stay His hand?
not man, with all his imaginary greatness that
quickly vanisheth like a cloud of smoke, or a
puff of wind.
	Our meetings are well attended, and a good
spirit prevails. The sisters are filled with a
spirit of prayer and testimony, and the song
of praise ascends to the Most High from our
gatherings, and with one accord. How pleased
and blessed were we. We strive to help the
poor and needy, the sick and afflicted in our
weak way, as the times are hard, and as a
consequence we are far from being rich, except
in the riches of eternity; if we keep the narrow
road in that we have boundless riches.
	Our annual meeting, on the 4th of June, was
satisfactory and encouraging. We were
blessed with our Stake President, Sister Libbie


Benson, and our beloved sister, Adaline Barber,
together with our Bishop and several of the
brethren, and indeed we had a rich treat—a
feast of fat things long to be remembered.
	President and counselors desire to be remem-
bered to all the good sisters of the Relief So-
ciety, and with them I desire to mingle my
voice, and say, "God bless and comfort all the
faithful in God's kingdom."
		Yours in the covenant,
		BETSY M. BROWER, Secretary.
