			RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.

	Resolved, That we, the sisters of Box Elder Stake,
do acknowledge the hand of God in the death of our be-
loved sister, and feel that we have lost in her a wise coun-
selor, an energetic leader and a true friend, whose comfort-
ing words and cheerful spirit have often brought consolation
in the hour of trial and sorrow to those who now mourn
her loss. 
	Resolved, That we, in behalf of the different Associa-
tions, desire to pay this tribute of respect to our departed
sister, and tender our deep felt sympathy to the bereaved
family, and pray that our Heavenly Father, who doeth all
things well, may comfort and bless them with strength to
walk in the footsteps of their departed dear one, and that
that spirit, that brings peace and consolation, may always
be with them in the hour of sorrow.
	Resolved, That these Resolutions be read in our stake
conference, and in the ward where she presided, and also

[Column 3]
that a copy thereof be presented to the family, and sent to
the EXPONENT for publication.
			HARRIET A. SNOW, Prest. of Stake,
			SUSANNAH P. BOOTH, First Coun.,
			OLIVER	WIDERBORG, Second Coun.,
			EMILIA	D. MADSEN,	Cor. Secretary.


Prof. A. Andre has issued a small pamphlet
containing the titles of his musical compositions,
also testimonials of his ability as a teacher of
music and languages, and some of his essays, and
a story in English. The story is entitled “Celes-
tine,” and all the articles are well written.
