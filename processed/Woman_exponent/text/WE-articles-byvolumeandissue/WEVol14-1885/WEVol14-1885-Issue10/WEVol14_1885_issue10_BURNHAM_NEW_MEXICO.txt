	BURNHAM, NEW MEXICO.
MRS. E. B. WELLS:
	A few weeks ago some of the good brethren
and sisters of Bluff City paid us a visit and
organized our Relief Society, Primary and Y.
M. M. I. A., the Y. L. having been previously
organized. Miss Dellie Stevens was chosen
President of Primary, Mrs. Mariette and Mrs.
Aggie Stevens, her Counselors; Miss May
Stevens, Secretary; Miss Mamie Burnham,
Treasurer. Mrs. Aabgail Stevens was chosen
President of Relief Society, and Mrs. Melissa
Slade and Mrs. Mary A. Farnsworth, Counsel-
ors.
	We had a most enjoyable time while the
brethren and sisters were here, and were en-
couraged to keep up our meetings as much as
possible, though we have little more than the
skeleton of each organization.
	The few little ones that are here are deeply
interested in their meeting together, and are
anxious to help to make it interesting. I neg-

[Column 2]

lected to say that Mrs. Mary T. Bigler was
chosen as Secretary for the Relief Society, and
Mrs. Elizabeth Stevens, Treasurer.
	The health of the people is generally good,
and thus far we have been left in peace, but
feel it is necessary to be cautious to be on the
safe side. Part of some families have to re-
main away, which is quite trying, as our num-
bers are so small, and we are still hoping and
praying for help.
	The weather, the past few weeks, has been
most delightful. We have been visited the
past summer by our worthy President, F. A.,
Hammond and his Counselors, Brothers Hall
and Adams; also Bro. Allen, of Bluff; we hope
to see them again soon, and they assure us that
we are not forgotten by them.
	Ever praying for the welfare of Zion, I am,
		Your sister,
