				OBITUARIES.

	DIED in Malad City, September 5th, 1885, Rachel Ann
Thomas, beloved wife of Benjamin L. Thomas, and
daughter of Richard and Mary E. Jones of South Wales,
England. Deceased was born in Brigham City, May 30th,
1855, and was thirty years, three months and five days old.
She leaves a devoted husband and two sons, one seven
years, and the other four hours old, with her parents and a
large circle of friends and relatives to mourn her loss.
Quiet and unassuming, she was beloved by all who were
acquainted with her. She had been through the Temple of
the Lord, and had done work for the dead, and now free
from sorrow and pain, has gone to meet with those that
will welcome her for the good work she has done for
them.
	She was a faithful member of the Y. L. M. I. A., always
on hand when wanted, either in work or means. Was also
First Counselor to the Primary Association; and although

[Column 2]
we miss her company among us, we know it is her gain.
May her virtues be emulated by those of the Associa-
tion she has left behind. We sympathize with the be-
reaved family, and we pray God our Eternal Father to
comfort their hearts and dry their tears.

	Rest, in sweet and peaceful slumber,
		Till the trump of God shall sound,
	Till the earth, with joyous echoes,
		Waking millions all around.  

	Then arise with joy to meet Him,
		Crowned as queen among the guests.
	Having on the wedding garment,
		Entering into promised rest.

	Though thy labors here were shortened, 
		And thou wert called from us away,
	Thou art laboring with the faithful,
		With those that trod the narrow way.

	And we shall again behold thee,
		Blooming with immortal life—
	Farewell sister, mother dearest,	
		Noble, loving, faithful wife.
