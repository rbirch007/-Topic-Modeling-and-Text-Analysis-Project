EDITOR EXPONENT:
	As members of the Burrville Relief Society, it is our
painful duty to inform you of the death of our beloved
sister and esteemed fellow laborer, Sarah Jane Burr Clow-
ard, who departed this life on the 14th of July, 1885. De-
ceased was born on the 18th of July, 1855, in Salt Lake
City, Utah; was the daughter of Charles C. Burr, and
Sarah Sloat Burr, and wife of George A. Cloward.
	Born and reared in the Church, she was faithful and true
as daughter, wife, mother, and as a Latter-day Saint. She
was the mother of six children, three of whom are liv-
ing.
	The following Preamble and Resolutions were unanimous-
ly odopted by our Society. 
	Whereas, our beloved sister, Second Counselor, Sarah
Jane Burr Cloward, in consequence of the falling in of the
roof of a house at Fish Lake, on Tuesday, June 30th, met
with injuries which resulted in her death on the 14th of
July, therefore, be it
	Resolved, That we deeply mourn her loss as a wise
counselor, an affectionate sister and a faithful, devoted and
consistent co-laborer with us in Zion's cause; but while we
mourn her loss we, by our faith and confidence in God,
have the assurance that our loss is her gain, and that by
virtue of her course as a daughter of Zion, she will rise in
the morning of the first resurrection to enjoy the blessings,
promises, powers and glory of those who, having kept the
faith, have endured to the end. 
	Resolved, That a copy of this Preamble and these Reso-


lutions be forwarded to Sister Cloward's parents, and to
her husband, and that they be forwarded to the EXPONENT
for publication.	Faithfully yours,
				SARAH BURR, President
				ANNA ANDERSEN, 1st Counselor,
				EVA STARR, Secretary.
