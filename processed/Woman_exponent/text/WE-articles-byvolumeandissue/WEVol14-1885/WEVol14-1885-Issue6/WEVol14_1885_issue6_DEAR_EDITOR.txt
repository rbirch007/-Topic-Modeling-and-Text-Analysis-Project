DEAR EDITOR:

   If I am not intruding too much I would like
to add my testimony to that of Lizzie B's
"Thoughts," in the EXPONENT of May 1st.
If any of my young sisters are about to marry
a Gentile, and have not read her "Thoughts"
--either in Zion or elsewhere--I entreat them
in all earnestness to get the article and study
it, and turn back before it is too late. If they
do not they will have cause to repent in sack
cloth and ashes.
   My dear young Sisters, these are not
sketches of the imagination, but the living
truth, and I would that you could all be saved
from the heart-aches and terrible agonies that
none on earth know except those who have
trod the same path of disobedience.
   Ever praying for the welfare of Zion and
her people, and those who have fallen by the
wayside, that their eyes may be opened, so
that they may rise up with renewed strength
and determination to have their lamps trimmed
and burning, before the bridegroom comes and
shuts the door and to them says, "Depart, I
know ye not,"
				I am Yours truly,
							ANN HARDY.
	Council Bluffs, Iowa.


The first women's hospital in China has
been opened at Shanghai. It is called the
Margaret Wilson Memorial Hospital.
