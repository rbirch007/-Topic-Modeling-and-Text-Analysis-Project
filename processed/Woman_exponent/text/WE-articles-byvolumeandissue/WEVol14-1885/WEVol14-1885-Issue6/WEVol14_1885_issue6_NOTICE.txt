NOTICE.
				 ---------
	As the time is near for drawing up reports for
the semi-annual conference, a few words of en-
couragment and instruction may be of benefit.
The great difficulties encountered by secretaries
keeping their books in a well balanced state is
realized, still, through perseverance and diligence,


it can be done, and much to our credit.  The branch
Secretary will better know how to settle her own
difficulties than the stake secretary will for her, and
they are advised to be very particular that their
reports accurately balance, even if forced, as it
must be brought to that condition at last, and the
evil will be less the earlier corrected.
	We wish to mention our feelings of satisfaction
and encouragement in the improvement observ-
able in the reports.  No secretary will ever re-
gret the time spent in learning to be a first class
book keeper.
	We have taken upon ourselves the trouble and
expense of sending to each stake president, at
least a short supply of new blanks, hoping to fa-
cilitate matters somewhat.  Many have not remit-
ted, and are requested to do so at earliest conveni-
ence, as more blanks are now needed.  We have
no central fund, and work on expectations of re-
turns.  Those desiring blanks after this will please
send to Dr. R. B. Pratt, over. Godbe and Pitts',
drug store; office hours from 1 to 6 p.m.
	We earnestly solicit every stake to be prompt
in sending in its report- at latest by the 20th of
September.
