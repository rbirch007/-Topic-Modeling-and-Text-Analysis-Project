ANNOUNCEMENT.

	The first term of Dr. Ellis R. Shipp's school in
midwifery will begin Oct. 1st, 1885, at her office,
No. 18, Main St. with greatly improved facilities
for education in this important branch.
	The already invaluable opportunities of  this
school are greatly enhanced by the late addition
of three complete sets of new charts, exemplify-
ing most accurately the subjects treated upon.
	The practical advantages are also increased by
the managers of the Deseret Hospital kindly con-
senting to Dr. E. R. Shipp's class making a
weekly visit to that institution, there receiving
clinical, or bedside instruction on the nursing, care
and treatment of the sick and injured.
	In addition several prominent physicians of
Salt Lake have signified their willingness to give
a number of lectures upon obstetrics and kindred
subjects.
	The members of this class will meet one even-
ing in every week to read medical periodicals, and
discuss chosen subjects, that they may thus be
kept posted on the medical literature of the day.
One main object kept in view is to utilize every
moment of time, in order to gain all the
knowledge possible in the time devoted to this
study. 
	The railroad officials have shown their benevo-
lence in granting students half fare to those ladies
attending this class.
	A full course of study consists of three terms of
twelve weeks each- the time being lengthened
from ten to twelve weeks, in order to give time
for the increased opportunities.  Two lessons will
be given every day with the addition of the oppor-
tunities above enumerated.
	Terms:  Twenty dollars for twelve weeks.
Books, ranging from twelve  to nineteen dollars,
according to the quality desired.  Those wishing
to join this class should communicate with Dr.
Shipp at their earliest convenience, it being neces-
sary to send east for books.
