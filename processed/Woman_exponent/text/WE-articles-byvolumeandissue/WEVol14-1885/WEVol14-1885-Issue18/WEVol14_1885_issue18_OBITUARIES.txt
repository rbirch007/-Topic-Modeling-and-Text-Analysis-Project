OBITUARIES.

	Sister Betsy Crane, of Provo, Utah, departed this life on
the 29th of December, 1885. Deceased was born March
29th, 1830, at Andover, Hampshire, England, and emi-
grated to Utah in 1882. She was a faithful member and a
teacher in the Fourth Ward Relief Society, which, on her
demise, passed oppropriate resolutions.

	DIED, in Centerville Ward, Davis Co., Utah, January
4th, 1886, of consumption, Thirza Jane, the eldest and
dearly beloved daughter of Alwood and Ann Elizabeth
Brown, born at Walton, Derbyshire, England, September
13th, 1870. With her parents, came to Utah in 1872.
Modest and unassuming in her manners, she was truly be-
loved by all who knew her. Though greatly afflicted from
her infancy, she bore all without a murmur. She died in
full faith of the Gospel, and in the hope of a glorious
resurrection. The Primary Association passed resolutions
of respect.
		OLIVE CLEAVELAND, Secretary.

	DIED, at Richfield, Severe Co., on the 5th of December,
Augusta J. Westman, eldest daughter of Peter E. and
Johanna M. Westman, aged fourteen years, eight months
and twenty-four days. She was on extraordinary girl of
her age. Her greatest ambition was to gain knowledge
and do good to others She acted as a leading member in
the Primary Association since its organization, and was
Assistant Secretary. In the Y. L. M. I. A. and Sunday
School she was a most energetic member, as well as Secre-
ary of the Silk Association. Resolutions were adopted,
expressing, in the highest terms, the worth in which de-
ceased was held by her associates.	M. A.
