UTAH STAKE CONFERENCE.

	The quarterly conference of the Relief So-
ciety of Utah Stake met according to adjourn-
ment, November 27th, 1885, Sister John pre-
siding. After singing and prayer, Sister John
made a few remarks, requesting the presidents
of the different branches of the Relief Society
throughout the county to give reports of each
society over whom they were called to preside.
After singing and prayer, conference adjourned
till 2 p.m.
	The afternoon session convened at 2 p.m.
There were present Presidents A. O. Smoot, D.
John and H. H. Cluff, also Bishop Johnson.
After the opening exercises, some very instruc-
tive remarks were made by Counselor Marilla
Daniels and Anne K. Smoot. Prest. Smoot
then spoke to the sisters, giving a great deal
of good counsel and advice on many subjects
of interest to the Saints. Presidents D. John
and H. H. Cluff followed with remarks and
counsels calculated to benefit all, if lived up to.
Bishop Johnson spoke of the donations to the
Stake Tabernacle, and said we should esteem
it a privilege to help build up the kingdom of
God.
	Sister John hoped we should all be benefited
by what we had heard. She felt that the
Spirit of God was with us. After singing,
conference adjourned for three months. Bene-
diction by D. John.
	C. DANIELS, Secretary.
