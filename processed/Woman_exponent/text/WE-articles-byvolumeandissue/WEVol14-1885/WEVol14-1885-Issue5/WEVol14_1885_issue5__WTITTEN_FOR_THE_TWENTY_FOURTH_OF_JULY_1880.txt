[WTITTEN FOR THE TWENTY FOURTH OF JULY, 1880]

In eighteen hundred forty-seven,
	The Saints were led by Brigham Young;
And guided by the God of heaven,
	In Utah's valleys found a home.

Their Patriarch and Prophet slain—
	Driven from their homes by wicked men—
They wandered on the desert plains,
	Nor thought to see Nauvoo again,

But the great God of heaven led
	Them by the right way to this land,
And, by His wondrous power, He fed
	And guided them by His right hand.

And on July the Twenty Fourth
	They reached the vale of Great Salt Lake;
And here their leader said "We'll rest,"
	And build up some of Zion's stakes.

They planted here the "stars and stripes,"
	Upon the soil of Mexico,
And in the valleys hoped to rest,
	No more pursued by cruel foe.

The desert, by their hands redeemed,
	Soon blossomed like the beauteous rose;
And plenty in their homes abound,
	While they had rest from cruel foes.

Not long do they in peace possess
	The blessings by sore suffering gained;
Their foes pursue, with false pretense,
	To drive them from their homes again.

But God o'er-ruled, and, by His will,
	The scourge designed a blessing proved;
And He protects and guides them still,
	Tho' earth and hell with rage are moved.

Their homes extend from north to south.
	Throughout these vales, on every hand;


Their noble sons with joy go forth
	To preach the truth in every land.

They gather in the honest poor,
	To share the blessings God has given,
And be prepared their Lord to meet,
	When He shall come in clouds of heaven.

They will obey the laws He gives,
	In spite of earth and hell combined;
Even the law of plural wives,
	That they eternal lives may find.
*	*	*	*	*	*	*	*
We meet, to celebrate this day,
	With joy and praise to Jesus' name,
Who led us forth by the right way,
	That we our mountain homes might gain.

Then hail! all hail! the Twenty Fourth,
	With love and joy let each heart sing,
And tune their voices long and loud,
	With praises to our God and King.
		T. L. H.
