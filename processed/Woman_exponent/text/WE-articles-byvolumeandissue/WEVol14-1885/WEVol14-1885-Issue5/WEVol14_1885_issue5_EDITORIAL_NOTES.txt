EDITORIAL NOTES. 

	THE Secretaries of R. S., Y. L. and P. A. in
this Stake of Zion should remember that written
reports are expected from each branch by the res-
pective stake secretaries immediately. 

	THE address of Mrs. Elmina S. Taylor, Prest. 
of the Central Board of Y. L. M. I. A., is at No. 
158 W. Third South Street, Salt Lake City, Utah. 
She gives her address because so many mistakes
have been made in the correspondence with the
Associations. 

	WE regret that we are compelled to leave, until
next issue, an announcement of a class in mid-
wifery by Dr. Ellis R. Shipp, which is to com-
mence Oct. 3rd. Those wishing to join should
communicate with her verbally, or by letter; as
soon as possible. 

	THE conference of the Relief Society and Y. 
L. M. I. A. of Sanpete Stake will convene Sep-
tember 11th and 12th, at Manti. Mrs. Mary Ann
Hyde, President of the Relief Society in that
stake, desires that the secretaries will please for-
ward their reports not later than the 1st of Sep-
tember, to the Stake Secretary, Mary Hyde, Spring
City. 
	
	SYNOPSIS of semi-monthly meetings in the 14th
Ward Assembly Rooms, annual meeting of Y. L.
M. I. A. in the 19th Ward, including the very able
address by Coun. Underwood, reorganization of
the Y. L. M. I. A. of the 8th Ward and a notice
from one of the Primary Associations of the death
of two of its members, also editorial matter, left
over for want of space, but will appear in the
issue of August 15. 
	
	MONDAY, August 3rd, is general election day, 
and we hope and trust the largest number of votes
will be polled that have been cast in this Territory. 
The women of Utah who are entitled to the fran-
chise ought to make a good showing at this im-
portant period in the history of the Territory. Let
nothing hinder you from depositing your ballot
for the People's Party. Be sure and vote the
right ticket. No doubt the Liberals and Demo-
crats will hand their tickets out freely.
