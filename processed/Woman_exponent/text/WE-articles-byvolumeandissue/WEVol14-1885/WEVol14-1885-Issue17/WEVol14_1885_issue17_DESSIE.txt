				DESSIE.

	With sadness we mention her name, for she is
gone—passed from earth away—our sweet fair sis-
ter. Death has taken one we dearly loved, and
though in sorrow we mourn and long to see her
once again, still must we feel that she is happier
far, for her suffering now is ended, and we pray
that He who wipeth the tear from every eye will
look in tenderest mercy upon those whose hearts
are grieved—the doting husband, the little child,
the bereaved mother, the exiled father, and


the brothers, sisters and friends who held her ever
in fondest endearment.
	The possessor of a bright intellectual mind, a
fine, sensitive nature and a most amiable and
pleasing disposition, Dessie ever held the highest
place in the affection of her chosen friends, a place
now to be filled only with sacred memories.
	Her life, from childhood to the grave, was
simple, pure and beautiful; she sought no public
praise, but in a retired, quiet way fulfilled life's re-
quirements, and ever charmed with delightful
conversation and delicate acts of kindness those
whom she loved. Now that she has left us, we
tenderly recall the days her presence here has
brightened, and as we turn the golden pages in
memory's book, our eyes are dimmed with tears
and our hearts filled with sad yearnings, for we
know those days are gone never to return. So in
volume even as she closed the book of life, and,
like her, look hopefully forward to a brighter,
purer life in heaven.				A.

	DESERET WELLS READ, the daughter of Daniel
H. and Martha H. Wells, and wife of Charles
Read, was born on the 18th of April, 1853, and
died on the 15th of Jan., 1886.
