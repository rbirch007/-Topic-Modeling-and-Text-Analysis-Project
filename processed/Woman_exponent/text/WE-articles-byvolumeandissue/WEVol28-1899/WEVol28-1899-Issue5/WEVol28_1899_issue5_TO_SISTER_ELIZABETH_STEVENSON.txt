TO SISTER ELIZABETH STEVENSON.
Blessed are they that mourn for they shall be 
comforted.

	Courage, brave soul, they grief allay!
		Though life seems dark and drear,
	It is not so, to cheer thy way
		The angels hover near.

	We know thou'st drunk the bitter cup,
		Aye, drained it o'er and o'er;
	But thou hast laid thy treasures up,
		Where sorrows are no more.

	And thou art faithful; thou art true;
		As years roll swiftly by,
	E'en this great grief thou wilt not rue,
		Nor pain, nor tear, or sigh.

	And, O, thou art so greatly blessed,
		Great joy should fill thine heart,
	That thou art given through righteousness,
		In His great work a part.

	Yea, even that thy voice is heard
		In Zion's stakes afar,
	Proclaiming by thy deeds and word
		The Christ—our guiding star. 

	And couldst thou ask a greater boon,
		Than—called by love divine—
	To work for those beyond the tomb?
		O, what a lot is thine!

	That God will give thee light and trust
		According to they need—
	Thou knowest all His ways are just—
		Thy sisters can but plead.

	Blessed are they that mourn, He said,
		And blessed are the pure;
	In me they shall be comforted,
		My word is strong and sure.”
					RUTH M. FOX.
July 7, 1899
