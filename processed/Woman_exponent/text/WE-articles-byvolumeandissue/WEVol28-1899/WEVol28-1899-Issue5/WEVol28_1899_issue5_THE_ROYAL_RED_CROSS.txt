THE ROYAL RED CROSS.  
	“The coveted honor of the Royal Red
Cross has been bestowed by Her Majesty
upon Miss Leonora Maxwell-Muller, wom-
an superintendent of the Indian Nursing
Service in the Madras Presidency, and upon
Miss Annie Gill Mark, superintendent of
the Army Nursing Service, and Miss Ger-
trude Mary Payne, of the Army Nursing
Service.
	In the case of Miss Mark and Miss Payne
the honor is in recognition of their services
in tending the sick and wounded. In that
of Miss Maxwell-Muller it has been in rec-
ognition of her competency in the discharge
of her duties and in the care bestowed in
training British soldiers and army hospital
corps attendants in nursing duties. 
	Miss Maxwell-Muller was trained in the
General Hospital, Birmingham, and after
holding various appointments in the Royal
Scotch Nursing Institute and in the Guards’
Hospital, London, she entered the Indian
Nursing Service in 1889. She became
deputy superintendent, and in 1893 was
appointed woman superintendent of the
Madras Presidency. Miss Maxwell-Muller
was at one time a student of the Ear Hospi-
tal, Glasgow. She is also a registered
member of the Royal British Nurses’
Association.”
