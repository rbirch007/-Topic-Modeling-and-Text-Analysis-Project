TOAST TO MRS. STANTON. 
	The following toast to Mrs. Elizabeth
Cady Stanton was offered by her son,
Robert Livingston Stanton, on a birthday
anniversary of Gerritt Smith Stanton, Sep.
18, 1893. It was the custom to celebrate
these occasions in Mrs. Stanton’s family
with toasts and original verses:

				I.
		Here is a toast
		We all can boast;
	She’s mother, sister, “Aunty Lee.”
		She raised us all
		In many a squall,
	With the help of dear H. B.
				
				II.
		She raises cake
		And bread and ‘sich.’
	From Jersey up to Maine;
		But her favorite raise
		Is not from maize,
	’Tis when she’s “raising Cain.”

				III.
	Germany has her Bismarck,
	England Gladstone, forsooth;
	Poland, Kosciusko; Hungary, Kossuth;
	America had her Washington:
		Columbia, Bolivar:
	But we have got the best of all,
		Our darling, dear old mar!

				IV
	Then let us hope and always pray
		That full many a year may pass
	Before we have a vacant chair
		Long side the apple sass;
	For search we may through tale and
		song,
		We'll never find another
	With so many virtues all in one
		As are found in our dear Mother!


MR. WALTER H. PAGE has resigned the 
position of editor of the Atlantic Monthly.
