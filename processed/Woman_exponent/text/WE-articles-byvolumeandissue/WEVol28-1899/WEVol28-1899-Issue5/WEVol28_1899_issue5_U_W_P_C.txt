U. W. P. C. 
	The regular monthly meeting of the U.
W. P. C. was held in the EXPONENT
office, Friday, June 30, 1899.
	The opening prayer was offered by Mrs. 
Ruth M. Fox, after which the secretary
called the roll, responses being given by 
quotations from Rudyard Kipling. 
	Mrs. A. M. Bradley, as representative of 
the Press Club, in behalf of the Federation
of Clubs, offered tickets to all for Field day 
at Saltair, Thursday, July 6th, the pro-
ceeds to be used for the free traveling
library.
	Mrs. Brown moved that the Press Club
spend a social day at Saltair, Thursday,
July 12th, which was seconded by Mrs. 
Fox: carried. Mrs. Aimee C. Schiller and 
Edith A Lovesy were appointed a commit-
tee of two to perfect all plans for the day.


A motion by Mrs. Bradley, seconded by
Mrs. Brown, that the club adjourn until
September, was carried.
	This being an evening with Kipling,
Mrs. I. Cameron Brown, the first on the
program, read a most interesting paper on
the life and poems of this well-known writ-
er. Mrs. Maria Francis followed with the
reading of one of Kipling’s masterpieces.
The latter was beautifully read, and proved
of great interest to all present. The pro-
gram closed with an interesting and care-
fully prepared paper on Current Events, by
Mrs. Rebecca H. Doolan.
	There being no further business, the
meeting adjourned.
