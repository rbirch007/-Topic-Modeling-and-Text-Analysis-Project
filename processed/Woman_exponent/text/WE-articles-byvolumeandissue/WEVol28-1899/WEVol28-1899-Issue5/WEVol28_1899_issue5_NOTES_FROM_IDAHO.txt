NOTES FROM IDAHO.

Editor Woman's Exponent:
	Thinking a little sketch of our society
yould be interesting to the readers of the
EXPONENT, we forward the following:
	This branch of the Relief Society was or-
ganized August 7, 1898. There are ten
wards in the Stake, each having a branch
of the society. We have visited every
branch twice and held two conferences and
will hold another on the 21st of August.
In making the circuit of the Stake we have
to travel about two hundred miles. We
were received and treated well by the peo-
ple, and found the society in good condi-
tion, considering the scattered condition of
the people.
	Dr. E. R. Shipp is here in Pocatello,
teaching a class in obstetrics and nursing.
Eight from this Stake are in attendance.
	Ever praying for the success of the Re-
lief Society work, I remain,
	Your sister in the Gospel,
	ANNIE BIRD, Cor. Sec.
