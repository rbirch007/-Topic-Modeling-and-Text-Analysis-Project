SISTER ELIZABETH STEVENSON.
While sympathizing friends and loving kindred
	dear
With clasping hand, tones subdued, and gently
	falling tear—
Draw so near to thee, with tenderest helpfulness,
In deep affliction’s hour, thy soul to cheer and
	bless—
I, thy loving friend, so many miles away,
Can only look above, and for thy comfort pray.

O, blessed, holy bonds of love and faith divine!
That draw us ever near that high and sacred
	shrine,
E’en while to mortal life we ever fondly cling,
This constant faith subdues its bitterness and
	sting.
Our burdens at His feet, our trust with Him
	above,
While aching hearts receive His blessing and His
	love.

Beloved friend, through many changing years,
Together we have known this precious hope that
	cheers;
That heavenly inspiration, which ever beams so
	bright
Shall now descend upon you, to cheer you with its
	light,
To bless as thou hast blessed, the sorrowing and
	bereaved,
Till with implicit faith, the victory is achieved.
