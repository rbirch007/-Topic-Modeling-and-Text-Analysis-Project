THE EVERLASTING DAY. 
(Sung by Miss Ella Walker at the Women’s
Meeting on  International Arbitration.) 
I stood on a lofty mountain 
And I gazed over land and sea,
And thought of life's wondrous fountain, 
Its source, and Eternity. 
I thought of God's mighty secrets, 
And how His Myst'ries blend, 
The past without beginning, 
A future without end. 
I looked on the boundless splendor 
Till its marvels dimm’d my sight, 
Then slept in a calm amazement, 
And dream'd with a strange delight;
I dreamed of a Heav'nly City 
Where soul with soul will blend,
Where time has no beginning, 
Where bliss will never end. 
The dream was a revelation, 
A vision of vanquish'd strife, 
A sweet imagination 
Of pure and hallow'd life:
A vision of God's great bounty, 
When sin shall have passed away, 
Of souls from sleep awaking 
To everlasting day.
