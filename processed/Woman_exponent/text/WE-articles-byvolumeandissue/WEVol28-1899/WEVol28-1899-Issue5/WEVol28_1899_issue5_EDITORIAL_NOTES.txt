EDITORIAL NOTES.
	THE Relief Society Conference of this
Stake of Zion, will convene in the Assem-
bly Hall, Saturday, September 2, 1899,
meetings commencing at 10 a. m. and 2 p.
m. It is confidently expected there will be
a large representation from all branches of
the Stake.
	WEDNESDAY evening, August 30, a
lawn party was given at Mrs. Amelia F.
Young’s residence, by her niece, Miss Hat-
tie Wallace to the Young Ladies Chorus
conducted by Madame Swenson, the leader
and teacher of about forty young ladies.
The grounds were prettily decorated with
flags and bunting, and lighted profusely
with every variety of Chinese lanterns,
which, among the trees and heavy foliage,
gave the place a fairy-like appearance, the
bright young girls in soft, airy summer cos-
tumes, many in white, and a few in bright
colors, flitting about here and there, added
greatly to the beauty of the scene, and with
their merry conversation and the delightful
arrangement of the entire affair, gave one
that charming sense of rest in recreation
that is so helpful to those who grow weary
with daily toil and need some sort of
change. There were a number of young
gentlemen present, by invitation, for of
course the girls must have escorts, and we
noticed one or two, perhaps three, of the
volunteers who recently returned from
Manilla. The tables were spread under
the shade of the trees, with dainty refresh-
ments and decorated with a variety of
lovely flowers and viewing the occupants
from available nooks here and there, the
effect was very pretty. Mrs. Young and
her two sisters, Mrs. Wallace and Mrs.
Brown, and a few other matronly women,
including Mrs. Adams, of Parowan, (whose
daughters were among the chorus) seemed
to enjoy the party as much as the younger
people. The evening was beautiful, the
sky clear and the stars brilliant, and every-
thing seemed propitious to the happy oc-
casion.
