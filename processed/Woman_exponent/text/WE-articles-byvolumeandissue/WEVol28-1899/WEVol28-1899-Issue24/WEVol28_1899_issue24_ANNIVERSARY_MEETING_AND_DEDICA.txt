ANNIVERSARY MEETING AND DEDICA-
TION.
	The members of the Freedonia Relief
Society met on the 17th of March to cele-
brate the fifty-third anniversary of the first
organization of the Relief Society and to
dedicate the hall which they have just
finished for the use of the Freedonia Relief
Society.
	Services commenced at 12 noon, Bishop
D. B Stewart presiding. Meeeting opened
by the choir singing, “Come dearest Ford
descend and dwell.” Dedicatory prayer by
Bishop D. R. Stewart. Singing, “This
house we dedicate to Thee.” Opening
remarks by Bishop A. A. W. Brown, sub-
ject, the benefit the Relief Society is and
can be List of amounts donated was
then read by Bishop D. R. Stewart. Presi-
dent Thomas Chamberlain, of the Kanab
Stake, then offered the dedicatory prayer.
Singing, “The star of victory.” Remarks
were made by Second Counselor Elizabeth
Pugh, of the Stake presidency.
	Speeches were then in order by the
visiting sisters. President Chamberlain
made some excellent remarks. Singing,
“O, be joyful in the Lord.” Benediction
by A. W. Judd.
	Our society was organized February 27,
1893, with fifteen members. They were
Emma S. Brown, president; Rose Cooper,
first counselor; Caroline Foremaster,
second counselor; Frances Crosby, secre-
tary and treasurer, The sisters have been
greatly blessed and our members increased
to thirty-four, members all alive to their
duties.


Since the first organization Sister Cooper
has moved away, also Sister Crosby. Sis-
ter Bethuna Hart has been put in Sister
Cooper’s place, and Sister Gedska Henrie
in Sister Crosby’s, Eva Hart, treasurer.
	I thought a few lines from this out of
the way place would be of interest and
encourage others. Where there is unity
there is strength. Those that help them-
selves, the Lord will help.
						E. S.
