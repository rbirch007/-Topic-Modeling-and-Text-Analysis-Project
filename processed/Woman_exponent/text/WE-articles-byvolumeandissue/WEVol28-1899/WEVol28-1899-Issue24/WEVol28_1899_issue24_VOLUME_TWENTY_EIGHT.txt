VOLUME TWENTY-EIGHT.
	This number of the paper closes Volume
28 and our patrons and friends of the dear
little home paper are reminded that it is a
good time to renew subscriptions, or to
pay np whatever is due on the old and
help the new volume, now to be com-
menced, to begin on a sure basis financial-
ly. The women of Zion more especially
are greatly indebted to the EXPONENT for
aid in their undertakings in organizations
and industries and many other ways too
numerous to mention, and of which we be-
lieve they are well aware, but do not al-
ways remember that a paper needs financial
support, and when lacking in that, cannot
be promptly issued. Instead of feeling
that it is for want of the needful the sisters
think it is the fault of the management.
But the editor most solemnly assures them
to the contrary. There is no good reason
in these days of prosperity why the sisters
should not patronize their own paper. One
dollar a year; they would never feel it, it
does not amount to ten cents a month, nor
yet two cents a week; and yet the dear sis-
ters who do appreciate the paper often say
there are single articles published that are
worth more than one dollar to them. In
view of the real and positive benefits ac-
crueing to the women of Zion, we hope
and trust that the presidents of all branches
of the Relief Society everywhere will feel
it a duty and responsibility devolving upon
them, to see that the respective ward where
they preside is well canvassed and subscrip-
tions solicited now at the beginning of the
new volume twenty-nine.
