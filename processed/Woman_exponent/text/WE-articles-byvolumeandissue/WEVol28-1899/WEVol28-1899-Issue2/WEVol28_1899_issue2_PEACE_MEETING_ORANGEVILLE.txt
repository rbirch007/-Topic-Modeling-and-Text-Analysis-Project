PEACE MEETING, ORANGEVILLE.
	The ladies of this place joined their forces
in a peace meeting the evening of May 15th
according to request, and rendered the fol-
lowing programme:
	Congregational singing, “The Star Span-
gled Banner.” Prayer by the chaplain,
F. W. Young. Singing, “America.” The
“Appeal to Utah Women,”' in the Des-
eret News, read by Mrs. Maria Guymon.
An address by Mrs. Sarah A. Fullmer on
“The Importance of Arbitration.” A speech
by Mr. Jasper Robertson. A paper by J.
C. Childs on “The Importance of Peace.”
Song by Mrs. Maggie Cox and company,
“Break the News to Mother.” Speech by
J. K. Reid. Speech by B. F. Luke on
“The Educational Side for Peace.” Song
by Mrs. Hattie Reid and Mrs. Amanda Tut-
tle. Resolutions by Mrs. R. E. Robertson
Instrumental music by the Johnson band,
which was encored. Speech by U. E.
Curtis. Recitation by Kate Van Buren,
“The Returned Soldier.” Prayer by the
chaplain.
	The evening was a success. The hall
was artistically decorated with flags and
bunting, with an attentive audience that
joined in singing the closing piece, “Colum-
bia the Gem of the Ocean.”
