OBITUARY.
	MYRA CLAYTON WINTER.
	It is with feelings of sorrow that we record the
death of Sister Myra Clayton Winter, which oc-
curred at Georgetown, Idaho, June 22, 1899.
	Deceased was born in Mansfield, Nottingham-
shire, England, November 3, 1822. She em-
braced the Gospel at an early age, and emigrated
to Salt Lake City with her sister Sarah, in the fall
of 1853. In July of the following year she be-
came the wife of Thomas W. Winter, then Bishop
of the 5th ward. She became the president of
the first Primary Association organized in that ward,
which position she faithfully filled until her duties
as a nurse compelled her to resign.
	Since the death of her husband in 1882, she has
spent most of her time with her children in Rex-
burg and Georgetown, Idaho.
	Sister Winter was a very kind and loving
mother and a true friend, and her death will be
mourned by all who knew her. She was the
mother of six children, four of whom are still liv-
ing: Bishop T. J. Winter and Mrs. Sarah Rands,
of Rexburg, and Harriet Smith and Celena
Leveridge, of Georgetown.
	Sister Winter was buried, according to her re-
quest, at the place of her death. She died as she 
had lived, in full faith of the Gospel, and in the
hope of a glorious resurrection.
