OLD FOLKS' DAY AT PAROWAN.
	Parowan, July 3, 1899.
Editor Woman's Exponent:
	Under the auspices of the Relief Society
of this ward, "Old Folks' Day" was cele-
brated on the 29th of June; and we believe
our aged fathers and mothers will long
cherish the remembrance of that worthy
event.  At 2 p. m. a feast of choice variety
was enjoyed;  immediately after a creditable
program was rendered, which seemed to
immensely please those very aged and en-
feebled, who seldom get out to public
gatherings.
	Badges were presented by the Relief
Society designating the time spent by the
wearer in this estate.  Each furrowed
countenance would involuntarily relax into
a smile as the souvenir was fastened upon
his breast, so gratified were they at every
recognition of their old age. 
	At the close of the program, cake and 
lemonade were served, when all those
honored veterans were treated to a carriage
drive.  As usual in our little country
towns the affair was finally crowned with a 
ball in the evening, where refreshments
were again in order.  All over sixty were
given complimentary tickets, and it was
really surprising to see how many who had
passed the third score milestone were
yet able to enjoy the pleasures of the ball-
room.
	Thus ended a happy day, one that had 
created new joys in many hearts who had
perhaps thought they were "unhonored
and unsung." Truly we cannot devote
too much time in making happy days for
our aged ones.  There are none who better
appreciate our efforts, and none to whom
we owe a greater debt.
