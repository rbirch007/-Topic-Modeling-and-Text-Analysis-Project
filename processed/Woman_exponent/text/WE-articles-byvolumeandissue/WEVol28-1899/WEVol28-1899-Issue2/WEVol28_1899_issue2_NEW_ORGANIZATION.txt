NEW ORGANIZATION.
PROVO, UTAH, MAY 30.
Editor Woman's Exponent:
	Myself and Sister Daniels visited Knights-
ville to organize a Relief Society in that
branch on the 28th inst.  We had a lovely
visit with our old friend from Provo who
resides there; also had a good time with the
Saints at meeting.  Elder Wm. McCul-
lough and counselors met us at the depot. 
	Knightsville is a pretty little nook in a 
rich mineral district.  The people have
nice homes, and there are many of Provo’s
old settlers who have had extensive ex-
perience in the Church, and will succeed in 
the Relief Society work.
	The ride in the railroad car was superb !
The scenery in the cañon was grand ! It
was my first visit in the Tintic district.
	The following names were nominated
and unanimously sustained as officers of the 
Relief Society:  Mrs. Mary Jane Wilkins,
president;  Mrs. M. A. McCullough and 
Mrs. R. Haws, counselors; Mrs. Louie Boyle, 
secretary; Mrs. Borak, treasurer. 
	With much love, and wishing you suc-
cess on your tour to England and return, I
remain,
Yours Sincerely,
