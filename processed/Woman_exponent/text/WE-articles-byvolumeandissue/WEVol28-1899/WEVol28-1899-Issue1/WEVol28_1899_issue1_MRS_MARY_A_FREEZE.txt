MRS. MARY A. FREEZE.

	Mrs. Mary A. Freeze was the last speak-
er. She made a very few remarks in which
she said she felt that we, as Christians,
ought to do all in our power to help on the
cause of peace. “Women are opposed to
war,’’ declared the speaker. “They are
unwilling to give up their dear ones. So
in behalf of all the women of the world,
who are assembled in the interests of peace,
I say: All hail, peace conference.”
	Mrs. E. B. Wells offered a vote
of thanks to all who had assisted the wo-
men in the work of arranging a peace
meeting. Her motion included those who
assisted with the music, Held’s band, whose
services had been donated for the occasion,
all committees who had worked for the
cause, and especially the press of the city.
	Mrs. Wells referred to the fact that in
seven other cities of the State the citizens
had assembled for the same purpose, and
deplored the fact that America had not
been the first nation to call for peace.
	The meeting closed with the singing of
‘‘America” by the entire audience.
