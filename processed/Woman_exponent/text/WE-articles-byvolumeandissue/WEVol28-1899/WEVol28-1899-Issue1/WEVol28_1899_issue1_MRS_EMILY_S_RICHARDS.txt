MRS. EMILY S. RICHARDS.

	Mrs. Emily S. Richards was the next
speaker. She said: “The peace question
is one of the most important problems that
this generation has met. Whenever it is
settled finally, the world will look to
America to take a part, and an active one.
Years ago much was said about an inter-
national tribunal for the arbitration of mat-
ters that involved the great nations of the
world. The indecision of the advocates of
this plan lay in the fact that they were not
agreed as to how cases should be conducted.
A permanent mixed tribunal would insure
fairness, but this is not exactly the idea of
those who see the necessity of forming a
a definite plan. There is an old saying
that Jesus by disarming Peter, disarmed
the world for all time. I am one of those
who hope the time is not far distant when
this will be the case.”
