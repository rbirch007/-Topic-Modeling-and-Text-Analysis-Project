	Behind his kingdom gates

Turns from the ancient law and puts his seal
	Of acquiescence on the gentler code,
Peace needs no mighter symbol of her weal
	There lies an open road


To that great goal to which the sage and seer
	Have prophesied through time—faintly in ages
		far—
The note was sounded, grew, dwindled again,
		and here
	Beats like full flood-tide at the bar.

In every air that broods, or breath that creeps
	From lip to lip—the word rings forth in power;
"Mankind are of one kin; a new faith leaps,
	Arise and meet the hour!"
