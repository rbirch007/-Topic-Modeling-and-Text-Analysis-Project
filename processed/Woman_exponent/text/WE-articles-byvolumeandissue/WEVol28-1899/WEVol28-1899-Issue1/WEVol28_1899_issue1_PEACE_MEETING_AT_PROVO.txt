PEACE MEETING AT PROVO.
	A peace meeting was held in the Taber-
nacle, Mrs. Mary John presiding.
	Services were commenced by the congre-
gation singing "America." President Ed-
ward Partridge led in prayer, after which
Eva Ward and Walter Whitehead sang
"Peace to the Dwelling."
	Mrs. John spoke a few words at the com-
mencement of the meeting, expressing her
desire that we shall soon have peace. 
	Mrs. William J. Knight read the peace
resolutions.
	President Reed Smoot from this point
took charge of the meeting. He gave a
brief address reviewing the call of peace.
	Mrs. Marilla Daniels, representing the
Relief Society, responded to the sentiment,
"Universal Brotherhood of Man."
	A duet, "Life's Rosy Morn," was
rendered by Messrs. George Burles and J.
J. Johnson. Mrs. Mary Boyden, represent-


ing the Woman's Christian Temperance
Union, answered to "Who Hath Wounds
Without Cause?"
	"The Principle of Non-resistance," was
spoken of by Mrs. L. H. Cannon, of the
B. Y. A.
	The Rev. J. E. Kirby responded to
"Will Peace Diminish Patriotism?"
	A quartette, "Just as God Leads," by
Misses Etta Farrer and Fern Cluff, and
Messrs. Ray Clayton and Walter Whitehead.
	Miss Alice Reynolds responded to "Is
Universal Peace Feasible?"
	"International Arbitration" was the sub-
ject of a talk by Miss Josephine Kellogg, of
the Nineteenth Century Club.
	"May Boys Fight or Must They Arbi-
trate?" was discussed by Mrs. Ida Dusen-
berry, of the Mother's Club, and Superin-
tendent Rawlins.
	The Rev. H. S. Goodwin gave the senti-
ment, "The Slaughter of Innocents."
	"Through Strife to Peace" was handled
by Mrs Sarah E. Eggertson, of the Sorosis
Club.
	The meeting closed by singing the song,
"Gloria."
