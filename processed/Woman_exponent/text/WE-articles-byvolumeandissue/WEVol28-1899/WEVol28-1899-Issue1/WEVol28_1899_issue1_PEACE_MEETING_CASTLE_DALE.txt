PEACE MEETING, CASTLE DALE.
	The women of Castle Dale, Emery Co.,
met in the Social Hall, May 15, 1899, for
the purpose of responding to the call made
through the EXPONENT in regard to the
general peace movement.
	Mrs. Caroline Larsen, president of the
Relief Society, took charge of the meeting,
with Mrs. Kathinka Anderson as secretary.
	The object of the meeting was stated,
appropriate speeches were made in favor of
the peace movement. A committee was
chosen to draft a resolution, and the follow-
ing resolutions were read by the secre-
tary:
RESOLUTIONS.
	Whereas there has been an appeal made to the
women of Utah to participate in the Czar’s In-
ternational Peace Measure, which he suggested
through his letter to the nations’ rulers, and who
are to meet May 18th at The Hague, Holland, in
conference, for the purpose of establishing peace
among the nations by arbitration in place of rush-
ing to arms to settle difficulties among the nations
of the earth, therefore, be it
	RESOLVED, That we, the women of Castle Dale,
County, of Emery, fair State of Utah, heartily
endorse this measure, and pledge ourselves to use
all our influence in support of this move. Be it
further
	RESOLVED, That we do not endorse the present
procedure of settling difficulties among the nations,
because it destroys life, it causes sorrow and dis-
tress and leaves widows and fatherless children,
who mourn the loss of those who are slain; and
not only that, but very often pestilence and
famine follow in the wake of this carnage and
strife.
	The resolutions were unanimously sus-
tained by the vote of the house.
	A general good feeling prevailed.
CAROLINE LARSEN, Pres.,
