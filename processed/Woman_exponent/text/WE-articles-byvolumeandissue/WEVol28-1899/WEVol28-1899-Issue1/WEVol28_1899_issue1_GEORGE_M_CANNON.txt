GEORGE M. CANNON.

	George M. Cannon was next introduced
by Governor Wells. “War is horrible,
but there are worse things or war would
not exist at all. To eradicate the evil we
must strike at the root.” The speaker re-
ferred to the sentiment so often expressed
by loyal Americans, “One country, may
she ever be right, but right or wrong, one
country,” and said that in his opinion this
is not a platform on which a man can stand.
	“If we would have permanent peace, we
must teach peace to our children. We
must insist that they draw the line be-
tween legitimate and illegitimate sport,
and make them understand that they are
not to enter a conflict where they will in-
jure their opponents. If we are to have
permanent peace, let us remember the
words of our first president, ‘hold out the
hand of friendship to all.’ By this plan we
can hold our place among the nations.
And in conclusion I will leave with you a
better sentiment than the oft quoted one
above: ‘Our country, may she ever be
victorious, but that she may let her always
be right.’ ’’
