MOTHER AND DAUGHTER.
Last spring I saw them seated side by side,
	In the bright ending of the long bright day—
One old, who still her busy needles plied,
	One young, who let her happy dreams have way,
“How fair the day has been, and life is fair!”
	Murmured the maiden. “But, thank God, it
		ends!”
Replied the mother, on her daughters hair
	Laying her hand a moment “and life wends!”
The young girl, smiling, let her rosy dreams
	Of long glad years to come unchecked flow on,
As through the golden sunlight streams
	That the blue cloudless skies gaze down upon.
The aged woman looked towards the hill 
	By the old church where lay the dead at rest,
And sighed her shining needles growing still—
	Life for the young; death for the old is best.”
Later I sauntered by the cottage gate,
	And lo, the white-haired mother sat alone
Bearing life’s sorrows still, while—bitter fate!—
	The maiden to her last long rest had gone!
