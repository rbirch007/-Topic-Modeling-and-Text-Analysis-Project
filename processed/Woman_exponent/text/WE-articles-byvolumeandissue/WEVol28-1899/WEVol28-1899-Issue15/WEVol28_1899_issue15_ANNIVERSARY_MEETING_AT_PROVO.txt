ANNIVERSARY MEETING AT PROVO.
	The members of the Relief Society of the
four wards of Provo City appropriately
celebrated the fifty eighth anniversary of
the organization of the Relief Society, in
Nauvoo, 1842.
	A meeting was held in the Stake Taber-
nacle on Saturday, March 17, at 2 p, m., at
which Sister Mary John presided. After
singing and prayer, a very interesting pro
gram was carried out as follows:
	An address on the organization of the
Relief Society, by Sister Marilla Daniels.
	Items of interest in her life were given by
Sister Martha Bullock.
	Brother James E Daniels sang “Daugh-
ters of Zion”
	Sentiment, “The Relief Society,” by
Sister M. A. Till
	Address by Sister Bennett, subject “The
Organization of Women.”
	Song, by Miss Julia Ramsey.
	Reminiscences of the life of the Prophet
Joseph Smith, taken from the diary of the
late Dr John Riggs, by Sister Melissa
Riggs Stewart.
	An address by Sister Deborah Billings.
	Song by Brother George Buries, “Let
Bygones be Bygones Forever.”
	An address by Sister Sarah Partridge.
	There was a good attendance at the
meeting, and there were ten sisters present
who had seen the Prophet Joseph Smith.
	After singing the Doxology, and prayer
by Brother A. J. Stewart, the meeting was
adjourned for one year.
