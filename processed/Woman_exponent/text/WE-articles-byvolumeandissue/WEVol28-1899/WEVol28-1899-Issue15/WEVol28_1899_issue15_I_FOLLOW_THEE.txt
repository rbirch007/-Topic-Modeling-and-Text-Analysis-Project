I FOLLOW THEE.
I follow thee, although thou canst not hear
My footsteps stealing soft beside thine own;
Though all the day and all the night-time lone,
Groping, I see thee, counting it good cheer
If I may walk beside thee, tho’ unknown,
Till love to love responsive shall be grown
And I may touch thy hand and feel thee near.
I follow thee I let my spirit steal
All palpitant and wistful unto thee;
I ask no more but that I still may feel
Thy wide, sweet presence, moving graciously;
I follow thee, and yearning, patient bide
Till thou shalt turn and see me at thy side.
