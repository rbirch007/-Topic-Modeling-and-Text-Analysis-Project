PIONEER WOMAN.
With seventy-five years of life behind her
and another well nigh completed, and with
many a hardship bravely borne, Mrs.
Elizabeth Murphy McLelland, a pioneer of
’48 and widow of Thomas McLelland,
second Bishop of the Seventh Ward, of this
city, passed from mortality at her home in
Farmers Ward, Thursday, April 12.
The . deceased was born in Manahan,
Ireland, in Ausust, 1824. Having em-
braced the “Mormon” faith she sailed from
her native land in 1844, and went to Nau-
voo where she resided until the expulsion
of the Saints. During her residence there
she became well acquainted with the
Prophet Joseph Smith and was a firm be-
liever in him and the doctrines he taught.
Mrs. McLelland was among the first
“Mormons” who departed from Nauvoo,but
remained in the various camps in Iowa un-
til the fall of 1848 when she came on to


Utah one year after the arrival of her hus-
band’s team.
    Seven living children, numerous grand- 
children and a host of friends mourn Mother
McLelland’s demise. Her children are, in
the order of their ages: Mrs.Thomas A.
Starr, of Cassia County, Idaho, who arrived
in Salt Lake a few days before her mother’s
death; Mrs. Margaret Palmer; Thomas
McLelland, of Farmers Ward; Edward
McLelland, ex-deputy sheriff; Sarah Lisle,
of San Mateo, California, and Mrs. Horace
Stayner, of Salt Lake.
