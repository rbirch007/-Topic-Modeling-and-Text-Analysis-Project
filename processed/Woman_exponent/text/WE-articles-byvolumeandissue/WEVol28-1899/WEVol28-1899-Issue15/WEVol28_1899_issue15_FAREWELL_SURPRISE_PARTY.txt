FAREWELL SURPRISE PARTY.
	Though it is for the benefit of the people
that the new wards are being organized, it
causes a feeling of regret and sadness to
part with some of the members who are
thus moved to start in the fresh field in
which they are called to labor.
	It was with such feelings that the mem-
bers of Willow Creek Ward met on the 1st
of March to give a farewell party to Sister
Thompson, who besides being an officer of
the Stake Y. L. M. I. A., was president of
the same association of the ward, and a
teacher in our Relief Society, and was also
secretary of the Sunday School.
	In a neat little speech Sister Ida Barratt,
in behalf of these associations, presented
our sister with a wreath of flowers, made
from the hair of the members of the afore-
said associations, as a token of respect and
appreciation of her labors among us.
	In response Sister Thompson said she
was sure the ward had been united and suc-
cessful in getting up this surprise, and in
their efforts of showing their appreciation
of her labors, and that enough money could
not be laid at her feet to buy this wreath of
flowers that had so affectionately been pre-
sented to her.
	After a continuation of the program all
went home, feeling that they had done
their duty, and all felt happy.
