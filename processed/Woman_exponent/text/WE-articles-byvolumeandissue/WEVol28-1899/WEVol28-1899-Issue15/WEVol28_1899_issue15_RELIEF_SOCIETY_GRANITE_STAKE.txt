RELIEF SOCIETY GRANITE STAKE.
	The people who attended the conference
at Mill Creek ward were honored with the
presence and wise instructions of Apostle
Francis M. Lyman, Anton H. Lund,
Abraham Owen Woodruff, Seymour B.
Young, of the Seven Presidents of the
Seventies, and others of the authorities of
the Church January 28, 1900, for the pur-
pose of organizing the Granite Stake of
Zion.
	Sister Emma Woodruff was chosen Stake
president of the Relief Society, and Sisters
Mary P. Young and Katharine H. Brock-
bank, as counselors. President Emma
Woodruff was set apart in the afternoon by
Apostle Anton H. Lund.
	The following Saturday at Farmer’s
Ward, February 3, 1900, the counselors
were set apart by Apostle Francis M. Ly-
man, Abraham Owen Woodruff, Bishop
John R. Winder and President Frank Y.
Taylor. Sister Celestia A. S. Young was
chosen and set apart as secretary, and Maria
B. Winder, assistant secretary and treasurer
both, until the other office could be filled.
Celestia. A. S. Young, Sec.
