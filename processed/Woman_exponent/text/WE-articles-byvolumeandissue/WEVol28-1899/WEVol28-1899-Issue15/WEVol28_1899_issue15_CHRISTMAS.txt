CHRISTMAS.

	Of all the old festivals that of Christmas
awakens the strongest and most heartfelt
associations. There is a tone of solemn and
sacred feeling that blends with our joys and
lifts the spirit to a state of hallowed and ele-
vated enjoyment. Christmas commemor-
ates the religion of peace and love. It has
been made the season for the gathering to-
gether of families and the drawing together
again of those bands of kindred hearts; of
calling back the children who have wan-
dered from the home, once more to gather
around the paternal hearth, there to grow
young again among the loving mementoes
of childhood.
	There is a charm about Christmas that
fills our hearts with mute but exquisite
delight. Our friendly sympathies are


aroused. We feel more sensibly the charm
of each other’s society, and are brought
more closely together by dependence upon
each other for enjoyment. Heart calleth
unto heart, and we draw our pleasures from
the deep wells of loving kindness which lie
in the quiet depth of our hearts.
	Christmas is a day upon which to rejoice
and be happy; a mirthful day, full of inno-
cent pleasures. It throws open every door
and unlocks every heart, and brings all to-
gether in one warm, generous flow of love
and kindness. The presents, the good
cheer, the tokens of love, the evergreen
distributed about the church or home, the
emblems of peace and gladness, all awaken
fond memories and kindle benevolent sym-
pathies.
	How delightedly we turn from the cares of life and mingle in the festivities of the
day! Every sound is full of melody and I
every countenance bright with smiles,
transmitting to others a supreme and ever
shining benevolence, which constitutes a
merry Christmas and reminds us of “Peace
on earth, good will to men.”
