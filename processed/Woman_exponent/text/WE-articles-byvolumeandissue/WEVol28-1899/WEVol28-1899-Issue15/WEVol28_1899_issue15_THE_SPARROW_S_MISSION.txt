THE SPARROW’S MISSION.

Seated alone at my window
	Gazing down on the street, 
I watched the people passing,
	And heard the dull tramp of their feet.
But my heart was o’erwhelmed with sorrow,
	As I cried in my agony,
“Father amid these many millions,
	Tell me, canst Thou know of me?

“Can every one of thy children,
	Claim a share of Thy love?
Can a prayer of mine ascend
	To Thy home in the realms above?”
And my heart was o erwhelmed with longing,
	So I cried in my agony,
“Father, amid these many millions,
	Tell me, dost Thou care for me?”

Just then a sparrow descended,
	Perched on my window sill,
And chirped as he flew away,
	His mission of love to fulfill.
But the bird had taught me a lesson,
	A message from heaven to me,
The Father who cares for the sparrows,
	Does know and will care for thee.
