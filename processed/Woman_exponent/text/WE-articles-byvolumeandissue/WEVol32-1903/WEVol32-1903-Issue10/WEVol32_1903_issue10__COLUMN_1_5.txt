MOTHERS' WORK.
CASSIA STAKE.
Lecture I.—Honesty.
	“The honest man though e’er so poor,
	Is king o’ men for a’ that.” Burns.
A. Truthfulness.
		1. Children are prone to exaggerate.
	2. Fear of punishment leads to pre-
	varication. 3. Truthfulness in children
	may be encouraged, if due diligence
	be exercised by parents and teachers.
	(Ref. Doc. and Cov. Sec. 93:1-40;
	Prov. 12:19-23; Prov. 13, 5-6).
	“Lies may be light and accidental, but
they are an ugly soot from the pit, and it
is better that our hearts should be swept
clean of them, without our care as to which
is the largest or blackest.” Ruskin.
B. Stealing.
		1. Envy leads to stealing. 2. Par-
	ents should provide for the reasonable
	wants of children. 3. The only atone-
	ment for theft is confession, and resti-
	tution where possible. (Ref. Exodus
	20:15; Doc. and Cov. Sec. 83:4)..
Lecture II.—Reverence.
	“Fear mingled with respect and esteem.”
A. Reverence for God.
		1. The name of God not to be used
	in vain. (Ref. Exodus 20:7). 2.
	Silence during prayer in home and
	church.
B. Reverence for Priesthood. (Ref. Doc.
	and Cov. Sec. 107:4; 64:34-37;
	Numbers 16:1-36).
		Courtesy to the Church officers.	
C. Reverence for Holy Things.
		1. Behavior in church. 2. Gospel
	topics should be spoken of reverently.
	3. Reverence for the aged.
“Speak to them gently, treat them with care
Give them the fireside’s easiest chair,
They wept for you once parental tear,
Don’t let them weep now, smooth the gray hair.”
Lecture III.—Discipline in the Home.
	“True devotion melts distrust,
	As the sun dispels the snow,
	And too much fierce and ardent heat
	Will dry the fullest fountain too.”
A. Devotion in the Home. (Ref. Doc.
	and Cov. Sec. 93:40-45; an article
	written by President Jos. F. Smith
	published in Era, Dec. 1903, page
	135).
B. Disobedience. (Ref. I Samuel 15;
	Genesis 4:8-15; Book of Mormon
	II Nephi 5:20:25.
C. Obedience. (Ref. Eph. 6:15).
“And e’er before him who in faith goes,
The manna falls, the sea divides,
The desert blossoms as the rose
And all things good his Lord provides.’
	1. How obedience may be obtained
in the home. 2. Punishment is occa-
sionally necessary. 3. Rule in the
home does not mean tyranny.
D. System.
	1. Routine duties of parents to chil-
dren’s growths. 2. Idleness to be dis-
couraged. 3. Habits of neatness and

[Column 2]
cleanness to be formed. 4. Responsi-
bility of a portion of home life should
be placed upon each child.
Lecture IV.—Evil Habits in Children.
A. Profanity. (Ref. Matt. 5:34-37;
	Exodus 20:7).
	1. Children should not be allowed to
associate with profaners. 2. Parents
should set proper example.
B. Observance of Word of Wisdom. (Ref.
	Doc. and Cov. Sec. 89).
	1. Self respect in children. 2. Self
respect as to body, to dress and con-
versation.
Lecture V.—Courtship and Marriage.
A. Courtship.
	1. Early courtship and prolonged
courtship to be discouraged. 2. Chil-
dren should be instructed by parents,
previous to marriage as to the duties of
married life. 3. Parents should exer-
cise great wisdom in assisting their
children in the choice of life partners.
B. Marriage. (Ref. Doc. and Cov.
	Sec. 132.
	1. Early marriages to be encouraged.
Lecture VI.—The Nursery.
		1. Children should have a room in
	the home where they may indulge
	freely in their play. 2. Acts of selfish-
	ness in the nursery should be checked.
	3. Mothers should plan the games of
	children. 4. Energy of children should
	not be checked too much but directed.
	5. Mothers strive to make home the
	most pleasant place in the community.
	6. When punishment is necessary it
	should be tempered with kindness.
			LOUISA HAIGHT, Prest.,
