TO EMMELINE B. WELLS.

Oh methinks in worlds afar, sweet sister spirit
	mine—
We knew and loved each other with tenderness
	divine;
That hand in hand together we roamed Elysian
	grove.
Heart beating close to heart with pure supernal
	love.

As children of one Father, our great eternal One!
We waited there His pleasure, we lingered near
	His throne;
Until at length He called us, angel spirits to this
	earth.
To obtain our mortal bodies, thus to gain im-
	mortal birth.

How infinite His wisdom, how great His good-
	ness too.
That still we can together His wondrous works 
	pursue.
That we congenial spirits may labor in His
	cause,
Thus gain eternal blessings by obeying sacred
	laws.

When life’s mission we have finished—our weak-
	nesses o’ercome.
With affection undiminished, may we meet again
	at Home.
May our crowns of endless glory with unceasing
	lustre shine
As reward for this probation, O, sweet sister spirit
	mine.
						ELLIS R. SHIPP.
February 29, 1904.
