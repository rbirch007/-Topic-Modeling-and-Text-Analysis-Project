MY TREASURES.
		BY RAY HULBERT.
I am standing today on the evergreen bank
	O’erhanging the River of Time;
In the distance behind me are memories rank—
	Before me the future sublime.

About me are twining the affections of old,
	Like tendrils of new-grown moss;
While pictures of silver and threads of gold
	Are gleaming amid flakes of floss.

As I stand on the banks of this beautiful shore,
	Now washed by the Waters of Life,
I find fresh among the bright treasures of yore,
	The pearls of the present are rife.

And my heart wells upward, toward the Great
		White Throne,
	As I glean of these rubies so bright,
And I offer to him who sitteth enthroned,
	My treasures for His World of Light.
