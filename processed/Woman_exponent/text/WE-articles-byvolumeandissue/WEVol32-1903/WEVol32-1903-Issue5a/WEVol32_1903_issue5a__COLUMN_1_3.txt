		WOMAN’S COUNCIL
	NATIONAL AND INTERNATIONAL
	Perhaps only a few women, comparatively
speaking, comprehend the objects and aims
of the great Councils of women that hold
their sessions triennially and quinquennially
in the United States and other countries.
	If, in brief, a correct idea of these two
great organizations can be given, it would
be an advantage to us all, perhaps, and, at
any rate, increase our knowledge of what
women are doing, and striving to accom-
plish for humanity.
	Most of our sisters know a great deal
about suffrage, having heard it so many
years, and also having had practical exper-
ience, and object lessons on the subject.
