HONOR TO APOSTLE H. J. GRANT.
	A very pleasant company gathered by in-
vitation Monday evening, Oct. 12, at the
Old Cannon Home on Cannon Farm, by in-
vitation of General John Q. and Mrs. Can-
non in honor of the distinguished guest,
Apostle Grant, and his wife. The evening
was pleasantly passed in social chat and some
very interesting reminiscences of Brother
Grant’s sojourn in Japan, his experiences in
various ways, his descriptions of the habits,
and customs of the people, their manners,
their language and economy, his ideas con-
cerning the future of the mission there, all
entertainingly given, was very new and edi-
fying to those who had so little knowledge of
this great people about whom we hear so
much and know so little.
	Some choice songs by Mrs. Carol Y. Can-
non, accompanied by Miss Grace Cannon,
and negro melodies and whistling by Bishop
Whitney; and by urgent request he sang the
old song “No Irish Need Apply.”
	Refreshments were served by the hostess
assisted by Miss Grace Cannon and the
daughters Louise, Margaret and Emme-
line. The guests were Apostle Heber J.
Grant and wife, Governor Heber M. Wells
and wife, Mrs. Sarah Jenne Cannon, Bishop
O. F. Whitney and wife, Mrs Lydia Ann
Wells, Elder Rulon S. Wells and wife, Mrs.
Eliza T. Cannon, Mrs. Susan H. Wells, Mr.
H. L. A. Culmer and wife, Mrs. Isabel M.
W. Sears, Elder Sylvester Q. Cannon, and
Mrs. E. B. Wells, together with the numer-
ous houseful of little Cannons, including the
wonderful twins.
