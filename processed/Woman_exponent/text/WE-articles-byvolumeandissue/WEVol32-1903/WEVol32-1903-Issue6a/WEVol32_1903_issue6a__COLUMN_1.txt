MOTHERS' WORK.
		WASATCH STAKE.
	"Be a woman, on to duty,
		Raise the world from all that’s low, 
	Place high in the social heaven
		Virtue’s fair and radiant bow, 
	Lend thine influence to each effort 
		That shall raise our nature human;
	Be not fashions gilded lady
		Be a brave, whole-souled, true woman,”

Lesson I.—Harmony in the home:

	 (a) Seek Divine aid and cultivate
	patience. (b) Mutual interests the
	greates[-] bond. (c) Cheerfulness and
	smiles essential in the home.

Lesson II.—Proper amusements in the home:
		(a) The value of good books. (b)
		Music and its influence. (c) How
		shall we entertain our children at home.
		(d)Evil effects of card playing.
			SENTIMENT.
	"No matter what his rank or position may be,
the lover of good books is the richest and happiest
	of the children of men."

Lesson III.—Honor the Sabbath Day.

		 (a) Six days shalt thou labor and do
	all thy work; etc. (b) The great evil
	of pleasure seeking on the Sabbath.
	(c) Late hours, its effects, evils of
	children on the streets or away from
	home at night.

Lesson IV.—Respect for the priesthood.
		(a) Irreverence one of the perils of
	our day. (b) Deportment to husband
	and children. “Women must honor
	and obey their husbands as the hus-
	bands do their God and the Priest-
	hood. Thus we teach by example the
	first law of heaven—Obedience.”
Lesson V:
	“Honor, to women! to them it is given
	To gladden the earth with roses of heaven"
	True meaning of motherhood. 
	(a) Better a just mother than an un-
selfish one. (b) Foundation of charac-
		ter laid in the first few years of a
		child's life.
Lesson VI.—Honor thy Father and Mother.

			(a) Respect for the aged, (b) The
		companionship of parents and children
		both for the good of the children and
		parents.
