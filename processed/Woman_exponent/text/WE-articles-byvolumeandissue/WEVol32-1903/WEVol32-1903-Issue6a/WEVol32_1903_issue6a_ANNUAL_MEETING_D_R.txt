ANNUAL MEETING D. R.
Daughters of the Revolution State Society
met on Monday, November 16, 1903, at the
residence of Judge and Mrs. W. H. King,


Military Avenue, Salt Lake City, the
handsome parlor and library were adorned
with patriotic pictures and emblems and
stars and stripes were hung in the most
conspicuous places; flowers were artistically
arranged with exquisite taste and altogether
the entire effect was quite up to date
aesthetic. The Regent Mrs. Isabel M. W.
Sears presided, and after the opening exer-
cises and some miscellaneous business, and
an announcement of its being the annual
meeting, the paper of the day by Mrs.
Julia Farnsworth Lund on Paul Revere
was given. It was delightfully written and
graphic in style, and so feelingly read that
if Paul Revere himself had been present he
must have felt honored by the recital of his
deeds of valor and heroism.
Mrs. Alice Merrill Horne read most
pathetically “Panl Revere’s Ride,” by
Longfellow, and one could almost see the
the picture before them, so realistic was
the rendering of that magnificent poem.
The origin of Thanksgiving and descrip-
tion of the New England Thanksgiving
celebrations in early days, and in these
later times was given by Mrs E. B. Wells,
who knows what Thanksgiving means to
Puritanical New Englanders.
Little Miss Romola King gave some very
sweet selections on the piano. After the
close of the exercises a delightful hour
passed in enlivening conversation and the
daintiest refrishments were served in the
most artistic style, which of course added
to the genuine enjoyment of the occasion.
The ice cream in an original design the
head of GeorgeWashington, and his hatchet,
the hostess was profusely complimented on
her originality. Besides the members
present then were several distinguished
guests. Mesdames F. M. Lyman, B. W.
Smith, Reed Smoot. A. H. Talbot and
niece, David Evans, Hanson and others.
