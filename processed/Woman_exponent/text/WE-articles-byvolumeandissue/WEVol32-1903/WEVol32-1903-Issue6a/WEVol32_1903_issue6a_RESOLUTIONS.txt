RESOLUTIONS.

	We, the females of the North Canyon
Ward, whose names shall hereafter be at-
tached to this article, ever feeling a lively
duty in our Redeemer’s kingdom, and for
the general welfare of Zion’s cause, do mu-
tually unite ourselves together for the bene-
fit of the poor, and all other charitable pur-
poses wherein we can prove ourselves use-
ful to our fellow creatures. Under the
name of the Female Relief Society of North
Canyon Ward.
	Whereas for the better organization of
our Society, would appoint the following
officers who shall hold their office during
the pleasure of said society.
	First a president who may choose two
counselors, a secretary who shall keep a
correct account of all the transactions of
said society; a treasurer who shall keep an
exact account of means that may be paid into
her hands, and also paid out and to whom.
And make a full report of receipts and ex-
penditures once in three months or oftener
if said society shall require it. And further
all laws and by-laws that shall be made for
the regulating of said society shall be in
force until repealed by said society. And
we in our united faith will ever pray that
the blessing of the Holy Spirit may ever
attend our every duty.
	Elizabeth Barlow was sustained as presi-
dent; Louisa Grant as first counselor; Han-
nah Hollbrook as second counselor; Phebe
C. Sessons as secretary; and Lucina Sessions
as treasurer.
	There were seventy-nine sisters became
members. We held fourteen meetings and
did a good deal of work for the poor. One
poorfamily received $60 68 in the short
time we held our meetings. On account of
the move South these meetings were dis-
continued.
