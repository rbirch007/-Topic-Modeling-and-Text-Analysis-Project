KANAB STAKE.

Lecture I—The first great command: 

Womans first mission to bear child- 
dren, be fruitful, multiply and replen-
ish the earth, a helpmate and compan-
ion.

Lecture II-—Home Rule:

 (a) The husband and father honored.
(b) Love and confidence established.
(c) Parents be what you expect your
children to be. (d) The rights of all
respected.

Lecture III.—The preparation of motherhood:

(a) The thoughts and acts of the
mother, reproduced in the child. (b)
The necessity of home environments
being pleasant and agreeable.

Lecture IV.— The care and training of the
child:

(a) Physical—a healthy body. (b)
Moral. (c) Temporal. (d) Spiritual.

Lecture V.—Home Culture:

(a) Home best place on earth. (b)
Cultivate a love for home. (c) Polite-
ness and proper respect. (d) The re-
sult of over indulgence, and dhe result
of self sacrifice

“Make home beautiful,
Make home pleasant,
Emblem of that home above.”

Lecture VI.—Home Culture, continued:

(a) Industry and economy, (b) Par-
ents know where your children are.
(c) The company they keep: the books
they read. (d) Parents to accompany
children to places of amusement.

Lecture VII.—Home Culture:

(a) Have confidence in the child;
love and trust them. (b) Get their
confidence and love, and keep it. (c)
Respect for old age.

Lecture VIII—The effect of early training in
the Gospel, by example as well as precept:

(a) Respect for the Priesthood and
reverence for sacred things. (b) Ob-
servance of the Sabbath day. (c) Pay-
ing of tithes, and offerings.

Lecture IX.—Transition from childhood to 
maidenhood and boyhood:

(a) Care of health. (b) Clothing.
(c) Suitable books and companions.


Lecture X.—Hygiene in the home:

(a) Diet; food, and preparation. (b)
Ventilation. (c) Cleanliness. (d) Ex-
ercise.

Lecture XI.— Word of Wisdom:

(a) The result of obeying the word
of wisdom in the home. (b) The re-
sult of non-observance.
