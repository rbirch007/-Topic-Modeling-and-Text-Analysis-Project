A PLEASANT SURPRISE.
	The officers and members, of the two
branches of the Relief Society of the West
Weber Ward joined in a surprise given to
President Frances B. Dance on her seven-
tieth birthday. So Tuesday, May 26, the
sisters and a few brethren met at the ward
meeting house, prepared and set tables with
the bounties and luxuries of life, then
awaited her coming, it being the regular
meeting day of the society. As she opened
the door the congregation sang “We thank
Thee, O God, for a prophet.” She was
then escorted to her seat at the table. There
were between seventy and eighty persons
who partook of the bounteous feast spread
before them. After all were satisfied, the
tables were cleared and the meeting com-
menced.
	After the usual opening exercises, Sister
Sarah J. Wilson read an original poem in
honor of President Frances B. Dance. Her
counselor, Sister Alvard, in behalf of the
sisters of the West Weber Society, presented
her with a token ot respect, and Sister S. J.
Wilson in behalf of the members of the
Taylor Relief Society, gave her a present to
show the love and esteem in which she is
held by all the people of the ward. Ever
since she left her native land, England, she
has made her home in West Weber, about


thirty years. She has been faithful in at-
tending to all the duties required of her.
	The program rendered consisted of songs
and short speeches, given by the brethren
and sisters, all wishing her many happy
returns of the day, each one felt that the
time had been well spent in showing her
that her labors were appreciated. This
happy event dosed by all singing the Dox-
ology, and benediction was pronounced by
Sarah J. Wilson.
