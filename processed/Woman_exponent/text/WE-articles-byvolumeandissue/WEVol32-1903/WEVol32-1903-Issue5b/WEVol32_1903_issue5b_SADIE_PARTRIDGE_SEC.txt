		WAYNE STAKE.
Lesson I.—Noblest Mission of Woman. 
	a. Every woman should aspire to be-
	come an honorable wife amd mother,
	the first commandment “Multiply
	and replenish the earth.” b. Pre-
	paration for this honorable calling,
	study every part of domestic science
	that home may be a pleasant place
	for husband and children. c. Order,
	economy and thrift.
Lesson II.—Are Parents Responsible for
	the Character and Disposition of Chil-
	dren?
	a. Education should begin before the
	birth of the child. b Mothers should
	control their thoughts and actions,
	have noble, pure and beautiful
	thoughts, keep in mind that which
	is most desirable for your child.
Lesson III.—The Care of Children.
	a. Nursing, bathing, pure air, and
	sunshine. b. Pleasant occupation,
	keep them occupied with something
	useful and instructive. c. As soon
	as old enough let them do something
	for their parents, have some respon-
	sibilities, that they may feel they
	are a necessity to home.
Lesson IV.—Reverence and Worship in
	the Home.
	a. Prayer, singing, proper examples.
	(Ref. Editor’s Table, Era No 2. vol. 
	7.)
Lesson V.
	a. Use good language. b. Teach the
	third commandment. c. Avoid slang.
Lesson VI.—Government in the Home.
	a. Rule with love and firmness, b.
	Obedience the first law of heaven.
Lesson VII.—Rights and Privileges of
	Children.
	a. The Apostle Paul says “Provoke
	not your children to wrath, b. Re-
	spect children, teach them to honor
	and respect their parents. (Ref. 5th
	commandment.) c. Teach them to
	honor the Priesthood and be obedient
	to counsel from proper authority. d.
	Respect teachers and elderly persons.
Lesson VIII.—Keep Children Home
	Evenings.
	a. Parents make the happiness
	and welfare of the children your in-
	terest, join with them in their child-
	ish games, sing with them, tell them
	moral stories calculated to elevate,
	reading in the home circle. b. Socials
	and entertainments, young and old
	together.
Lesson IX.— Word of Wisdom.
	a. Ref. Book of Doctrine and Coven-
	ants, Sec. 89. b. Observance of
	same. Its effects on the home.
Lesson X.—Moral Training.
	a. Self-reliance and self-control. b.
	Truthfulness, honesty. c. Kindness
	to friends and animals. d. Cultivate
	pure thoughts and actions.

[Column 3]
Lesson XI.—Development of Youth. 
	a. The mind should control natural
	desires. b. Growth obtained through
	self-activity. c. Emulate the acts of
	good men and women. d. Stamp
	upon the minds of the youth the
	principles of revealed religion, which
	are true pure and exalting. e.
	“The glory of God is intelligence,”
	this should be an incentive to all
	who desire to become like Him.
Lesson XII.
	Review of lessons.
