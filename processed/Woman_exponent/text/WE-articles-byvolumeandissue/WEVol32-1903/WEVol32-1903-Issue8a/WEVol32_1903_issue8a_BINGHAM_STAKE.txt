Lesson VIII.—Results of Disobedience.
	(Ref. Mosiah 11th and 12th Chapters).
	We cannot plan and build for ourselves, 
if we do we will be like the Jews of Old, as
the Prophet says: “Have hewn out cisterns,
that will hold no water.” We must submit
to the ordinances of the House of the Lord.
Lesson IX.—Home.
	Study to meet all your requirements as a
wife in righteousness. Give personal ex-
perience as “The Mother of the Home.”
The mother and daughters [-]eed to realize
that woman’s life and liberty does not con-
sist in the multitude of avenues that lead
from home to the outside world; but in
the uumber of avenues from which she may
bring the best, from the outside world, into
the home.
Lesson X.—Duties of Parents to Children.
	Parents be just to your, children in the
least, as well as in the greatest thing. The
home without children is lonely, and want-
ing in the love element. Every child born
into this world is entitled to the best rear-
ing.
Lasson XI.—Amusements for the Home.
	The woman who takes into her heart, her
own children, may be a very ordinary 
woman, but the woman who takes into her
heart, the children of others, is one of God’s
Mothers.” It is natural that we should
love our children, but every child in the 
community is ours, in so far as our influence
makes him a better or worse citizen.
