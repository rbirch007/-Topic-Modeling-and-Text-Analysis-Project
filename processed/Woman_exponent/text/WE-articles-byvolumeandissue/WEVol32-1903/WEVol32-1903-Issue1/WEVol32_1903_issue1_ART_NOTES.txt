ART NOTES.
	Mr. J. T. Harwood, of this city, now in
Paris, has recently sold his water color en-
titled “The Young Mechanic.” The buyer
is John Wanamaker, of Philadelphia. It is
very unusual to sell a picture out of the
French Salon, and Mr. Harwood is to be
congratulated on his merited good fortune.
	Mrs. Harwood is painting some studies in
brass and copper, Miss Louise Richards,
Miss Rose Hartwell, and Miss Sawyer, Mr.
A. B. Wright, Lee G. Richards and Honri
Young are to be in the country districts
near Paris for the summer.
	Mr. Harwood and Mr. Richards (son of
L. L. Greene Richards) and Honri Young
exhibited in the recent French Salon, of
which we are very proud. It is good to see
Utah’s sons and daughters go to the art
centres and add to their native ability, the
culture and training of the Old World.
