		FANNY CLAYSON QUIGLY.

	To the memory of Fanny C. Quigly who died
December 2, 1902, of consumption.
	She was born at Payson, August 1, 1867, and
lived a faithful life in the Church of Jesus Christ
of Latter-day Saints. She was married to Brother
John Quigly in 1894, who preceded her to the
spirit world in 1894. She was the mother of two
sons, the youngest dying in infancy, the eldest
survives her to mourn the loss of a loving mother,
	She was a member and teacher of the Payson
First Ward Relief Society, in which she was an
earnest worker among the sick and afflicted.
	Whereas it has pleased our Heavenly Father to
call from our midst by the hand of death our be-
loved sister and friend, therefore be it
	Resolved, that we recognize in her death the
loss of a true friend and colaborer in the Payson
First Ward Relief Society.
	Resolved, that these resolutions be entered in the
Relief Society record, also a copy be presented to
the bereaved son and one printed in the WOMAN’S
