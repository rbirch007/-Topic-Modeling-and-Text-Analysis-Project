	ALPINE STAKE.

Lesson I.—Whom Shall our Daughters
	Marry?
	(a) Whom shall our daughters
	marry?
	(b) At what age shall they marry?
	(c) Marriage and its advantages.
	(d) The law of choice.

Lesson II.—The Young Wife:
	(a) Life without health a burden.
	(b) Health essential.
	(c) Means to promote health.
	(d) Pleasure in moderation necessary
	to health and happiness.
	(e) Evil effects of fashionable dress-
	ing.
	(f) A wife’s work.

Lesson III.—Pre-existence of Spirits:
	(a) Read and dwell on the beauties
	of the hymn “O my Father, Thou that
	dwellest.”
	(b) Refer to Job, 12th and 38th
	chapters; Heb. 12: 9; Rev. 12: 7-12;
	Eccl. 12: 7; also to the Compendium,
	page 179; Doc. and Cov. sec. 93.

Lesson IV.—Pre-natal Culture:
	(a) An important subject. Why ?
	(b) Effect of influences upon the un-
	born child.
	(c) Mothers should know themselves
	and be wise in all things—diet, bathing,
	clothing, exercise and sleep.
	(d) The right to be well born.
	(e) A God-like privilege.

Lesson V.—Infants and How to Care for
	Them:
“A rose with all its sweetest leaves yet folded.”
	(a) Care a vital question.
	(b) Cleanliness an incentive to
	health.
	(c) Bathing, clothing, diet, exercise
	and sleep. Teach baby to go to sleep
	naturally.
	(d) Effects of frightening the child
	into going to sleep, etc.

Lesson VI.—Nursing and Weaning:
	(a) Effects of wrong diet on the
	milk. Avoid all stimulants.
	(b) Regularity in nursing and its re-
	suits,
	(c) Why should the baby not nurse
	in the night?
	(d) Time and method of weaning.

In preparing these lesson, reference can
be made to the following books: Miss Har-
rison’s “Child Nature,” Riddell’s "Child
Culture,” the Latter-day Saints’ Church
History and the “Child’s History of Joseph
Smith the Prophet.”
