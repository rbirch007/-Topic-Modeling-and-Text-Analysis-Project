			ELIZABETH MAYHO.

	Died at her residence, Fourth Ward, Provo City, Utah
May 30, 1903, Mrs. Elizabeth Mayho, beloved wife of
Henry Mayho, and daughter of William and Ellen Oller-
ton Houseon.
	Deceased was born in Preston, England, May 2, 1859,
emigrating with her husband and family to Utah in 1897,
residing in Nephi for six months, removed to Provo where
they have since resided. She leaves a husband, two sons
and two daughters to mourn the loss of a most exemplary
wife and kind and affectionate mother. She was baptized
into the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints in the
year 1885, in Preston, England, since which time she has
labored earnestly and faithfully for the advancement of
the kingdom of God, and to establish truth and righteous-
ness upon the earth, always ready to bear her testimony to
the divine mission of the Prophet Joseph Smith.
	Her house was always open to the traveling Elders and
missionaries as many brethren testified at her funeral ser-
vices, speaking feelingly of her as their “English Mother in
Israel.”
	As a teacher in the Relief Society of Provo, Fourth
Ward she was ever at her post of duty, visiting, counseling
and admonishing the sisters, deeming no labor too arduous
