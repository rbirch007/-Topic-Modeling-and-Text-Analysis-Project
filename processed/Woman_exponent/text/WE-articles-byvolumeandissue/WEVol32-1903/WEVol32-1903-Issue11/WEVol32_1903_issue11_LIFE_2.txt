Lecture 5—Prospective Mother.

	What shall we do to have our babies
healthy and happy? A life in harmony
with the gospel is the perfect example.
Discuss the physical needs of the body.
Freedom of dress, out door exercise, clean-
liness, food, fresh air and sunshine. Power
to guide and control oneself. Study well
one’s character before undertaking the grave
responsibility of Motherhood.
	Questions.—1. Give experiences what ef-
fect certain tendencies of the mother have
upon the child. 2. Does our temporal con-
dition effect our children? 3. To what ex-
tent should women exercise?

Lecture VIII.—First Years of the Child.

	Obedience is heavens first law. As the
twig is bent so the tree inclines. “Too
much indulgence has spoiled many a child,
too much love not one. Love brought the
child into the world and the more love we
give them the more we have for ourselves.”
“Love begets love.” Home is the child’s
world. Keep it uppermost in our
minds. One of the first steps is to gain
the child’s confidence. Habits are formed
in infancy.
	Questions.—What lessons of obedience
should a child learn in the first five years of
its life? 2 Illustrate love and indulgence.
3. In what way would we have more love
if we gave more? 4. Should we gratify the
child’s wishes about pleasure and outings?
5. How can we gain a child’s confidence?
6.What habits are formed in infancy?
