LOVING WORDS
O, loving words, speak loving words,
	The world is full of care
Speak loving words, O, loving words
	And with them breathe a pray’r,
Speak loving words, such loving words,
	The path is rocky-steep
So oft we stumble, lose the way,
	The road is hard to keep.
Oft times the fair day ends in gloom
	Fierce storm clouds break o’er head
The sun goes down, e’en at its noon,
	Its glory—warmth all fled
Life’s storms cross o’er and o’er our way,
	Such bitter tears are shed
As in the chamber lone we weep,
	Alone but for our dead.
Speak loving words, speak loving words
	They soothe the heart, the tear,
It is the way the Master trod
	E’en was the sparrow dear.
The sad, distressed and weary bless.
	They unto God are dear;
For lo, the Savior waiting stands
	To wipe away each tear.
Speak loving words, speak loving words
	The broken heart, bind up,
So in the trial you are blessed
	Though ’tis a bitter cup.
Some day, your Savior you will meet,
	His glorious face will see;
He wept in anguish on the sod;
	Prayed in Gethsemane.
Then loving words He’ll speak to you.
	Who helped the suff’rer live,
And crowns and kingdoms wildering fair,
	To you He yet may give.
Then feed the poor, the needy aid.
	Speak loving words so sweet,
Thus endless blessings will be yours,
	Love twines them round your feet.
