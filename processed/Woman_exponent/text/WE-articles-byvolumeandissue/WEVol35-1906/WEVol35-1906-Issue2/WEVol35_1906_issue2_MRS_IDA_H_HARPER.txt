MRS. IDA H. HARPER.
	There is no agency so effective for tak-
ing the bumptiousness out of the Ameri-
can woman as the International Council
of Women. This might apply to those of
other countries also, but their egotism is
not of so colossal proportions. It is a
good thing from every point of view for
the women of Australia, for intance, to
counsel for a week with those of Austria,
the women of Canada with those of Swe-
den. These four countries are ably rep-
resented here, as are New Zealand, Hun-
gary, Italy, Switzerland, Germany, Nor-
way, Belgium, Holland and Great Britain.
Of the twenty countries belonging to the
Council, the only ones which are not rep-
resented are Denmark and Argentina.
