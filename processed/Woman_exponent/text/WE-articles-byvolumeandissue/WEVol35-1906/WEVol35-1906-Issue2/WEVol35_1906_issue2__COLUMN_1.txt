PASSING THOUGHTS.
	RELIEF SOCIETY OUTING.—The four
Stakes, Liberty, Pioneer, Salt Lake and
Ensign, joined in an outing to Saltair,
on the 19th instant, and the day was one
long to be remembered. As characteris-
tic of the Relief Society many of the
wards bought tickets and prepared lunch
for their poor and their aged, and the
long tables decorated with flowers and
laden with delicate and delicious viands,
looked very tempting indeed. Our gener-
al president, dear Aunt Bathsheba, Coun-
selor Ida S. Dusenberry, and general
treasurer Sister Williams, were guests of
the 17th Ward, while the general secre-
tary, Sister E. B. Wells, was the guest of
the 18th Ward.
