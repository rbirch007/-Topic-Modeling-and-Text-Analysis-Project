WHAT MIGHT HAVE BEEN.

As down the vista of departed years
We see amid the mists of falling tears,
The many opportunities now flown,
In self reproach we sigh and vainly moan.

We think of tender words we might have said,
	Of  loving, noble deeds we might have done,
Of wayward lives to right we might have led
	Of sins we might have checked when just begun.

We know foundations strong we might have laid,
	For grander structures here we might have
		reared,
Time-lasting friendships too we might have made,
	By bonds of sacred fellowship endeared.

We might have planted too, fair rosy bowers,
	Wherein to rest in age the weary form.
And scattered on the way sweet-scented flowers,
	And plucked from wounded hearts the pieicing
		thorn

We might have soothed the weary, troubled soul,
	Implanted seeds of holy faith and trust,
Uplifted hearts to pure diviner goal—
	To reach the shrine of One forever just.

Now to our yearning souls may God reveal.
This nobler life—sublime and grand ideal;
To seek for aye, eternal righteousness.
Not all for self—but other lives to bless.
