ONEIDA STHKE.
	The semi-annual conference of the
Oneida Stake Relief Society convened in
the First Ward meeting house, Preston,
President Louisa B. Benson presiding.
	Counselor Solomon H. Hale, Patriarch
S. R. Parkinson and four members of the
stake board present.
	Singing, “Praise to tne man,” etc.
Prayer by S. R. Parkinson. Singing,
“Let the lower lights be burning”—Miss-
es Weaver and Benson,
	Pres. Julia S. Roper, of Preston first
ward, spoke of the good being derived
from “Mother’s Work,” also on ‘Grati-
tude.”
	Secretary Phoebe A. McNeil read a
paper on Mother’s Influence in the
Home.
	Solo, “Know this, that every soul is
free”—Mary E. McNeil.
	Prest. Esther Rogers, of Preston fisrt
ward, spoke on the blessed privilege of
bearing children, rearing them, gaining
their confidence, etc., also of unity in R.
S. work.
	Dora Geddes gave a lecture on “Cha-
ty.”
	Counselor Sol. H. Hale addressed the
conference, touching briefly the subjects
of organization of R.S. work, noble call-
ing of mother, and patience; advised
wives to prudently encourage their hus-
bands to do good.
	Singing, “Guide us, O thou great Jeho-
vah.” Benediction by Bishop Bodily, of
Fairview.
	Afternoon Session.—Singing. Prayer
by Bishop Benson, of Whitney. Singing.
	Pres. Julia Jenson, of Preston second
ward, spoke on the importance of moth-
erhood, also mother’s influenced pow-
er of prayer.
	Solo, “I know that my Redeemer liv-
eth”—Florence B. Jonasson.
	Pres. Sarah J. Fjelsted, of Preston
fourth ward, spoke on diminishing our
troubles by doing good to others, and ad.
vised all to be cheerful and contented,
	Linda N. Benson gave a lecture on
Prayer,
	Duet—Eva Johnson and Eleanor
Thomas. 
	Counselor Jos. S, Geddes spoke on
Faith of Latter-day Saint women, Zion
leading educationally as well as spirit-
ually, and regretted the growing evil of
profanity.
	Recitation by Ellen Henderson.
	Pres. L. B. Benson spoke of sacrament
meetings and slack attendance at same,
etc.; advised all to grasp every oppor-
tunity for learning; urged the sisters to
care for the sick and needy.
	Conference adjourned after singing “O
ye mountains high.” Benediction by
Bishop Mecham, of Riverdale.
		PHEBE A. MCNEIL, Secretary.
