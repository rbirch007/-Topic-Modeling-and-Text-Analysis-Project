		BIG HORN STAKE.
I. Language in the Home.
	He that hath a perverse tongue falleth
into mischief.” Give meaning of ‘‘Moth-
er Tongue.” The necessity of correct
language in the home. Impure language
displeasing unto the Lord. Read James
3; Matt. 12: 36.

II. Home Influence.
	As the twig is bent, so the tree is in-
clined.” What is our attitude as mothers
to our children, as Primary, Sabbath
School and M. I. A. members. How may
we create an influence at home typical of
heaven. Ref. Young Woman’s Journal,
August, 1905, Juvenile Instructor, October
1, 1904, October 15, 1901, Nov. 1, 1901.

III. Mother’s Duty to the Primary and
		Sabbath School.
	Have children ready on time. Encour-
age their telling what the teachers tell
them. Assist them in home preparation.
Age of Primary children. Ref. Juvenile
nstructor. January 15, 1906, Young
Woman’s Journal, May, 1902.

IV. Loyalty.
	To those in authority over us. To our
co-workers. To our brethren and sisters,
To our Creator. Be loyal citizens. Ref.
January and July Improvement Era,
1905.


V. Self Control.
	Tbe secret to successful life. Mother’s
must obtain it. Teach children self-con-
trol. Ref. Y. W. J. May, 1900.
VI. System.
	“There is not an hour of youth but is
trembling with destinies.” Use of spare
moments. Have rest and sports to some
purpose. “The great majority of youths
who go to the bad are ruined after supper.
Ref. Improvement Era, 1906.
	VII. Unity.
	“Where there is unison, there is
strength.” Necessity of union among as-
sociate officers, teachers, members. What
is the greatest weapon wielded against
union. How may this evil be avoided. How
shall we train our children that the spirit
of fault-finding may not develop. Ref.
Juvenile Instructor, Jan. 1, 1904.
VIII. Obey Counsel.
	“Obedience is better than sacrifice.”
Sustain those in authority over us by
obeying their instructions. Honor those
whom God honors. Ref. Improvement
Era, Feb. 1906. Deseret News Feb. 6,
1906.
IX. Habit.
	How formed. How shall we form good
habits. How shall we teach our children
the value of good habits. Read Improve-
ment Era, March 1903, p. 377. Juvenile
Instructor, March 14, 1900. Y. W. J. Oct.
1906.
X. Respect.
	What is respect? How obtained. How
retained. To whom is it due. How
shown. Illustrated by contrast. Read
Improvement Era, Nov. 1906.
XI. Dress.
	How shall we dress? Its physical effect.
Its moral effect. Read Y. W. J. Feb.
1906.
XII. Pioneering.
	Conditions incident to pioneer life.
Develops strength of endurance. Enum-
erate advantages in pioneer life. Read
Y. W. J. July, 1902.
			LUCY F. GRANT, Pres.
