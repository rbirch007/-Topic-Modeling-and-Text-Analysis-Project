Mrs. William Grey, the founder of the
Maria Grey Training College for Women,
at Brondesbury, England, has lately
died, aged ninety. Woman and Progress
(London) says: “Mrs. Grey was one of
the earliest advocates of the higher edu-
cation of women, and took a prominent
part in founding the National Union for
Improving the Education of Women, an
organization out of which the Girl’s Pub-
lic Day School Company developed.
Mrs. Grey was also a well known sup-
porter of the women’s suffrage move-
ment.”
