IN MEMORIAM.
	Sister Ellen Bullock Adamson died at her home
in Pleasant Grove, Nov. 4th. 1906. She was the
daughter of Alexander and Emily C. Bullock;
born in Salt Lake City. October 8th, 1862; leaves
a husband and nine children, the youngest three
weeks old.
	Impressive services were held in Clark’s Hall
on the 7th inst., where a large congregation of sor-
rowing relatives and friends assembled. The
floral tributes were beautiful and appropriate. In-
structive and consoling remarks were made by the
speakers, Brother J. C. Thorne, J. D Wadley.
Sisters Sine Chipman, and Annie C. Hindley, of
the Relief Society Stake Presidency, brother
J. L. Brown, of Provo, President James Clarke,
and Bishop S. L. Swenson.
	The following resolutions of respect were
adopted by the Pleasant Grove Relief Society, of
which she was the assistant secretary for many
years, and for three years the secretary.
			RESOLUTIONS.
	Whereas our Heavenly Father has called from
our midst our beloved sister, Ellen B. Adam-
son,
	Resolved, that we deeply mourn the absence of
an active and faithful member of the society one
who was ever ready and willing to do her part
with especial promptness and regularity. She
was a devoted wife and mother, and a fervent
Latter-day Saint, loved and respected by all her
associates.
	Resolved that we extend our heartfelt sympathy
to the parents, the bereaved husband and mother-
less children and ask God to bless them and cause
them to feel that he is their friend and that their
sorrow is not greater than they can bear.
	Resolved that these resotutions be entered in our
minutes, a copy presented to the bereaved family
and one forwarded to the Woman’s Exponent for
publication. 
				LOUISA HALLIDAY, President.
				ROSE B. HAYES, Sec. pro. tem.
