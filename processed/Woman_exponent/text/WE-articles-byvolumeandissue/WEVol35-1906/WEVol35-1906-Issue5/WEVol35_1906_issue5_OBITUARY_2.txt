OBITUARY.
	At Hinckley, Millard County, August 11th, 1906
there passed from earth one of our Father’s choice
spirits, Clarinda J. Stout, wife of Hosea F. Stout.
She has been a great sufferer for some time, from
what finally proved to be cancer of the stomach.
	Sister Clarinda was first counselor in our Relief
Society for five years, and has faithfully done her
duty in this office, responding cheerfully to every
call
	We, her co-workers, miss her cheerful voice and
smile, yet we feel it is well with her and
	Whereas, it has pleased our Heavenly Father in
His great wisdom to call unto Himself our dear
sister. 
	Be it therefore resolved, that while we, as of-
ficers and members of this Relief Society deplore
her loss, we yet bow in humble submission to the
will of our Father and we recognize in her de-
parture the loss of a faithful and energetic worker
in our society, a devoted wife and mother, and a
true Latter-day Saint. We also recognize in her


faithful labors an example worthy of emulation by
all.
	Resolved, that we, her sisters, sincerely sympa-
thize with her bereaved husband, Hosea F. Stout,
and her family.
	Resolved, that these resolutions be placed on
our records, and that they be published in the
Woman’s Exponent also that a copy be sent to
the family of our dear sister and friend.
	True and tender, self-denying,
		One of truth’s disciples brave—
	Let her sleep, she needs to slumber
		In the quiet, peaceful grave,
	Shall we mourn for one who’s left us?
		Yes, our tears we needs must blend
	Love’s our offering, this we owe thee,
		Faithful mother—faithful friend.
	President Naomi R. Theobald; Catherine A.
A1lred, Pheobe Meacham, Committee.
