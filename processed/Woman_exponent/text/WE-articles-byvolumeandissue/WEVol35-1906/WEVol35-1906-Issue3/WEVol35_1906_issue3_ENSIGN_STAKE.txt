		ENSIGN STAKE.

The Relief Society conference of En-
sign stake was held in the Eleventh ward
meeting house July 13, 1906, commencing
at 10 a.m. Pres. Margaret A. Romney
presiding. Sister Ethel Conelly organist.
After the exercises of singing and prayer,

[Column 2]
a quartette was rendered by Brothers
Maxwell and Foster and Sisters Hull and
Odell, which was a pleasing feature of the
morning session.
	After roll call and minutes, the secre-
tary read the statistical and financial re-
ports for the six months ending June 30.
Pres. Romney commented on the large
attendance and expressed her gratitude
over the accurate reports that had been
offered.
	A young ladies trio sang “One hundred
years ago.”
	The seven presidents of the wards re-
ported verbally their condition.
	The representative of the General
Board, Sister Alice Merrill Horne, re-
quested the secretary to read the report
of the San Francisco relief fund—the
amount contributed by the Ensign stake:
Cash, bedding, clothing,$451.85; one case
of tomatoes; four hundred loaves of bread.
	Sister Horne then talked about the San
Francisco calamity and Relief Society
topics.
The quartette sang, “Who are these ar-
rayed in white?”
	Counselor Minnie H. James spoke on
general Relief Society work; her remarks
were timely, practical and beautiful.
	Sister Carrie S. Platt next addressed
the conference.
	Solo, “Only remembered by what we
have done,” by Margaret C. Hull.
	Sister Rachel D. Davis and Sister Han-
nah Lapish talked for a few minutes.
	After singing and prayer, meeting ad-
journed until 2 p.m.
	Luncheon was served to all assembled
during the noon hour.
	Afternoon session opened with singing
by quartette, “I know that my Redeemer
liveth.”
	Counselor Rebecca E. Little presented
the general and local authorities of Relief
Society to be voted upon—there was no
dissenting vote.
	Sister Alice M. Horne spoke on singing,
entertainments, protection of the young
people.
	Bishop Thomas A. Williams spoke on
“Economy of work.” A beautiful solo,
“Babylon,”—Sister Elma Young.
	Pres. Joseph Wells addressed the con-
ference. Bishop Frank Platt made a few
remards.
	Brother Wm. H. Foster sang, “Provi-
dence is over all.”
	Bishop Geo. Romney eulogized Relief
Society work and expressed the gratitude
he felt towards the organization. Brother
Livingston, of the Eleventh ward, also
spoke.
	Singing, Doxology, Benediction by
Annie Kay Hardy.
		Annie Kay Hardy, secretary.
