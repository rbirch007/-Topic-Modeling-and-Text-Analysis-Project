		BEAR LAKE STAKE.
	We held our Conference of the Bear
Lake Stake Relief Society, June 24th,
1906, which consisted of 2 meetings Of-
ficers meeting held in Tabernacle Vestry
10 o’clock Sunday morning, and through
the chilly weather we hold our general
meeting in the Paris 1st, Ward Meeting
House at 2 p. m. President J. P. Lindsay
presided at both meetings.
	We had with us Sisters Margaret Caine,
and Hattie B. Harker of Salt Lake City and
had a very pleasant time together. After
the opening exercises, wards were report-
ed by Presidents or Counselors, and the
conditions of the wards were better under-
stood, and corrections and advice given
where needed.
	Our beloved President J. P. Lindsay
considering having past her 82nd birth-
day, is very bright and continually, with
the help of her counselors exhorts the
sisters to be up and doing; but there is
always room for improvement, a good
spirit prevailed during Conference and a
desire to progress.
	President William Rich met with us in
both meetings and gave us much instruc-
tion that will be of great benefit if put
in practice.
	Sister Caine spoke considerable on
Temple work during Officers meeting,
and exhorted the mothers to teach their
children the sacredress of their religion
and Sister Harker said the President
of the Society of each ward is the mother
of the sisters, and we should all try to do
our best in every way possible to help the
work along. Benediction by Nancy Allred.
	LOUISE TUELLER, Cor. Secretary.
