	ONEIDA STAKE.

	Conference of the Relief Society of
Oneida stake convened in the Academy
at Preston, Idaho, June 13, 1906, 10 a.m.,
President Louisa B. Benson presiding.
There were present of the General Board
Counselor Annie Taylor Hyde and Julia
P. M. Farnsworth, of the stake board five
members. After the opening exercises
and reading of minutes, the financial and
treasurer’s report were approved. Solo,
“Not half has ever been told,” by Flor-
ence B. Jonasson.
	General and stake officers unanimously
sustained.
	Pres. Benson said this meeting was for
the officers. All the branches of the
stake present reported work as being in
good condition; Mothers’ work especially
doing much good. Franklin, Weston,
Treasureton and Glendale were not fully
organized. Preston first ward has a room
in new meeting house to meet in. Ma-
pleton built a new granary during the
year. Pres. Benson advised a review of
outline for Mothers’ work, also taking
the lessons on keeping the Sabbath day.
	Counselor Annie Taylor Hyde spoke of
blessings obtained through Relief Society
work, also through earnest prayer, also of
the exalted calling of mothers, of the Sab-
bath day, explained that our organizations
were a blessing, but did not come before
care of children, advised the appointment
of a committee to visit and welcome new
families.
	Sister Julia Farnsworth brought greet-
ings from Pres. B. W. Smith, of the Re-
lief Society, spoke of all Gospel workers
having one aim, told of our people as-
sisting the sufferers in San Francisco.
	Pres. Benson announced that meetings
throughout the stake would be held on
the first and third Wednesdays of each
month, also advised all to buy a volume
entitled “School and Fireside,” by
Karl G. Maeser. Reported the Stake
Relief Society as being in good condition,
the board were united, supported by local
presidents and stake presidency.
	After singing and prayer, meeting ad-
journed until 2 p.m.
	LINDA N. BENSON, Cor. sec.
