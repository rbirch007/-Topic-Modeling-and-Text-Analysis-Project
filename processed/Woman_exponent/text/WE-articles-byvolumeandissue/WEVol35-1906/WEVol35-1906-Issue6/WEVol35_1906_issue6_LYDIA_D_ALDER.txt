						LYDIA. D. ALDER.


Mother Bickerdyke, the mother, nurse
and confessor of the army boys of the
Civil War, gave thanks for just being use-
ful. When she might have entered Wash-
ington in honor at the grand review of
Sherman’s and Meade’s armies, she pre-
fered to ride with her troops, wearing a
calico dress and a sunbonnet. Then, soon
leaving their ranks to wait upon them
with food and water, she said: “I never
considered myself ornamental or worth
making a show of, but I can be useful,
and that’s all I want.”
