MEMORIAL SERVICE.

	Meetings were held in honor of Sister
Elizabeth Stevenson, a member of the
General Board of Relief Society, by the
Relief Society branches of Emery
Stake, May 7, 1906. The memorial meet-
ing held at Castle Dale, May 7, 1906.
Singing, “Who are these arrayed in
white.” Prayer by Carrie O. Larsen.
Singing, “Sister, thou wast mild and
lovely.” Reading items of the life, death
and funeral of Sister Elizabeth Steven-
son, by Stake Secretary Annie Maria Al-
len. Singing, “Do what is right.”
	Stake President Mary A. Sorensen
spoke feelingly of the noble character of
our dear sister, of her strong faith and
fidelity to the Gospel, of her great desire
to talk to the sisters, and referred to in-
structions given to the sisters of this
stake by this dear sister on former visits,
as being instructions from a woman of
God, the same would never be forgotten.
	Solo, “Shall we gather at the river,”by
Lucy Seely.
	Sister Clarissa Jane Seely, of Mount
Pleasant, said she had been acquainted
with Sister Stevenson since the year 1858,
and knew her to be a faithful woman who
always tried to do right, to live uprightly
before the Lord; hoped that when she
passed from this stage of action she
might die in the harness as Sister Steven-
son had done,
	Prest. Mary J. Seely thanked the sisters
for their hearty response and for the
beautiful po ted plants and cut flowers
which they had furnished on this acca-
sion for decoration.
	Singing, “Thou dost not weep to weep
alone.” Benediction by Annie J. King.
		ANNIE MARIA ALLEN,
					Stake R. S. Sec.


Mrs. Frances C. Monken, of East St.
Louis, III., who is ninety-three years old,
is well and strong. She reads and writes
without glasses and plays on the piano.
