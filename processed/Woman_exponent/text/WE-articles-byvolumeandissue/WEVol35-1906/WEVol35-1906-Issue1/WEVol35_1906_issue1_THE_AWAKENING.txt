THE AWAKENING.
	GRACE INGLES FROST.
All nature hath awakened
	From the long winter’s sleep
The leaden clouds have vanished,
	The skies no longer weep;
The long, gray days are over,
	And trees that then were bare,
Are laden now with blossoms
	Whose fragrance fills the air.
Gay silver-throa'ed birdlings
	Are with us once again,
They sing from morn ’til evening
	Their thankful, joyous strain.

And stars in azure setting

While Luna’s beams are bathing
	The land with light of love.
