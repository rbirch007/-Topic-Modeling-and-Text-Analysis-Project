	  MOTHERS’ WORK
	SNOWFLAKE STAKE.
			    1
			Prayer.
“Prayer is the simplest form of speech
	That infant lips can try;
Prayer the sublimest strains that reach
	The majesty on high.”
	(a) Teach children to pray as soon as
they are old enough to talk. Mat. 7: 7—
8.; Doc. & Cov. sec. 103: 36.
				2.
			Obedience.
	(a) Necessity of obedience.
	(b) Blessings that attend obedient peo-
ple. I Nephi 3: 6, 7; 4: 24.
	(c) Sorrows that attend the disobedient.
I Samuel 15: 22; Improvement Era vol. 5,
page 410.
	(d) Willing obedience the most accept-
able to our Heavenly Father. 
				3
			Character.
	“Dare to have a character of thine
own, and let it be an honor to thy name.”
	(a) Choose good companions and read
good books.
	(b) Guard our thoughts as well as
words and actions.
	(c) Have pure desires as well as noble
aims.
	(d) “There is no excellence without
labor.”
	(e) “The basis of intellectual knowledge
is to know, the basis of moral cul-
ture is the desire to do right.”
	“How long we live, not years but ac-
tions tell.”
			    4
		  Mothers’ Talk.
	(a) Mothers should have the confidence
of their children.
	(b) Children should be taught the sa-
credness of life’s mission.
	(c) Self sacrifice.
	(d) Importance of cleanliness.
	(e) Plenty of sleep and pure air neces-
sary.
	(f) Good seeds bring forth good fruits.
	(g) Anything worth doing is worth do-
ing well.
	 “So close is glory to our dust,
		So near is God to man,
	When duty whispers low, ‘Thou must,”
		The youth replies, “I can.”
			    5
		    Associates.
	“An author is known by his writings, a
mother by her daughter, a fool by his
words, and all men by their companions.’’
	(a( We are creatures of imitation.
	(b) Better be alone than in bad com-
pany.
	(c) More of our education is received
by example than by precept. I Cor. 5:
9—13.
			    6
			Idleness.
    “Defer not till tomorrow to be wise.
    Tomorrow’s sun may never rise.”
