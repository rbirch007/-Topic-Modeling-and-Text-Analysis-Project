GENERAL RELIEF SOCIETY CONFERENCE.
	The Annual Conference of the Relief
Society was held in the Salt Lake Assem-
ble Hall, Thursday, April 5, 1906, com-
mencing at 10 a. m., President Bathsheba
W. Smith presiding. Prof. Charles Kent
rendered as a solo the beautiful hymn,
“I know that my Redeemer lives,” Mrs.
Kent accompanying him upon the organ.
The effect of the singing was very uplift-
ing. Prayer was offered by Sister Susan
Grant. Congregation sang, “Now let us
rejoice in the day of salvation,” etc.
Sister Annie Wells Cannon, secretary
pro tem, called the roll of General Offic-
ers and Presidents of Stakes. There was
an excellent representation from the sev-
eral stakes and a very large congregation
for a morning session.
	President Bathsheba W. Smith wel-
comed the congregation in a few words of
encouragement and greeting, and signified
her wish that the Presidents of Stakes
report, as there were many present, and
called upon those from a distance. Snow-
flake was reported by Sister Jeannette
M. Smith, treasurer. The settlements in
that stake are scattered, considerable dis-
tance to travel, the presidency visit all
the wards regularly, the average attend-
