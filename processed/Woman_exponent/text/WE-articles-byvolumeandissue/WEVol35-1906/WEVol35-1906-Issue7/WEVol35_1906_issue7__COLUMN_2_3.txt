Lesson 8—Preparation for the Future.
	We owe a duty to our children’s future
mates. Teach the boys to put their cloth-
ing, books, etc., away neatly, and thus
they will learn to be helpful, rather than
hindrances not only to their mother, but
to their wife when they get one; and
teach both boys and girls to use reason,
judgment and common sense in all things.

Lesson 9—Our Bodies are Temples of the
		Living God.
	Teach the children this, that they may
learn to keep them clean and pure. Teach
them the value of deep breathing, fresh
air and exercise, and of standing straight.
We are made in the image of God, and
should keep that image as perfect in form
as possible.

Lesson 10—The Sin of Gossip.
	“Judge not, that ye be not judged; for
with what judgment ye judge ye shall be
judged.” (Matt. 7:1.) Be not hasty in
condemning others. Speak of their good
qualities rather than their misdeeds. We
might do things that would be just as
wrong if we were placed in similar cir-
cumstances. Have some subject for dis-
cussion at sewing bees and work meet-
ings.

Lesson 11—Parable of the Ten Talents
		(Matt. 25: 14-30.)
	What talent do I possess, and do I cul-
tivate it? Patience, faith, cheerfulness,
the power to instruct or comfort others,
or anything that helps to make life better
and brighter is talent which should be
cultivated.

Lesson 12—Place of Worship is Holy.
	Teach the children to be quiet and or-
derly in the house of God. It is wrong to
let the little ones run and play in the
aisles, and for the older ones to whisper
and make a disturbance during services.
These things are displeasing to our heav-
enly Father and an offense to our friends
who visit our places of worship.
