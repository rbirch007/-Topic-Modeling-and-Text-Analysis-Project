MOTHER’S WORK. 
		SAN LUIS STAKE. 
	Lesson 1. Unity in the home! What 
constitutes union. Results of union. Re-
sults of disunion. Religious unity. 
	Lesson 2. Parental influence. What 
should be the condition of a woman’s
mind in pregnancy. Contentment. Self 
control. Pure and holy thoughts etc. 
Also practice the strictest honesty, truth
fulness—having also the best environ-
ments that can be given. 
	Lesson 3. Moral development. Favor-
able environments—call forth kindly emo-
tions, and suppress evil thoughts; kind 
companions do most, as love begets love. 
Cleanliness, Good literature such as Juve-
nile Instructor, Youth’s Companion, Chil-
dren’s Friend. Ref. 1st chapt. of Prophet 
Joseph Smith by his mother, Subject, 
Home influence and example. 
	Lesson 4. Social Purity: “Blessed are
the pure in heart for they shall see God,”
Matt. 5 chap. 8th verse. Give your ideas 
in regard to Juvenile parties. The neces-


sity of Theatres, dances, etc. of a moral 
and elevating nature. Mothers should be 
their children’s confident in social pas-
times. Ref. Young Womans Journal Sept. 
1904, Doc. and Cov. Sec. 42-22-26.
	Lesson 5. Hygiene: What we eat and 
drink, Water as a food, Hints about wear-
ing clothes, Sleep and how to get it, How 
to keep well. 
	Lesson 6. Preparation for marriage: 
A girl should have a physically strong and 
well developed body, A knowledge of 
the preparation for good and wholesome 
food, some knowledge of what to do in 
case of emergencies, of cleanliness and 
economy in the household, and of her 
matrimonial duties and rights, etc. Ref.
Doc. and Cov. Sec. 89.
	LAURETTE M. PETERSON, 
			R. S. Stake Secretary.
