IN MEMORIAM.
	Another earnest, faithful worker in the Relief
Society has been called home to her rest. After
suffering for some time with pneumonia, Sister
Ellen Blackhurst McLelland passed peacefully
away on the morning of March 18, 1907, lovingly
ministered unto by her three daughters, who were
with her in her last illness.
	Sister McLelland was born in Longton, Lanca-
shire, England. November 10, 1837. Her mother
died in Winter Quarters, leaving the little girl of
eight years of age to be a mother to two young
brothers—one a young babe; and lovingly and
truly did she perform her part. On the arrival of
the family in Salt Lake City in the early fall of
1847, they made their home in the Seventh ward,
where she still resided at the time of her death.
While assisting her father in the Big Field to drive
away the crickets, her baby brother, who was her
constant charge, toddled to’ the creek and in an
attempt to obtain a drink, fell in and was drowned.
	Later she married Thomas McLelland, at one
time Bishop of the Seventh ward, and passed
through a full share of the trials and difficuties of
those early days. With ever a cheeful smile and
a kindly word, she gained the love of all acquaint-
ed with her. She always saw the good qualitie-
of others, and overlooked or did not seem to diss
cern their failings.
	A member of the Relief Society since early
times, she was always to be relied upon in her
duties as a teacher, and endeared herself to her
companions and associates, and the Relief Society
of the Seventh ward feel they have lost a stalwart,
faithful member and loving sister.
	The funeral services were held in the Pioneer
Stake house March 20, where the speakers paid
worthy tributes to her unassuming, patient, cheer-
ful life in all circumstances and conditions.
			ELLEN BROADBENT, President.
			GERTRUDE SAMPSON, Secretary.
