THE BABY.

WHERE did you come from, baby dear?
Out of the everywhere into here.
Where did you get your eyes of blue?
Out of the sky as I came through.
What makes the light of them sparkle and spin?
Some of the starry spikes left in.


Where did you get that little tear?
I found it waiting when I got here.
What makes your forehead so smooth and high?
A soft hand stroked it as I went by.
What makes your cheeks like a warm, white rose?
I saw something better than anyone knows.
Whence that three cornered smile of bliss?
The angels gave me at once a kiss.
Where did you get this pearly ear?
God spoke, and it came out to hear.
Where did you get those arms and hands?
Love made itself into hooks and hands.
Feet, whence did you come, you darling things?
From the same box as the cherubs wings.
How did they all just come to you?
God thought about me, and so I grew.
But how did you come to us, you dear?
God thought about you, and so I am here.
	When six months old to baby Julia, from
President Brigham Young. Beaver, 1875.
	AT the suggestion of two of President
Young’s wives, we send the following
pretty little poem to the EXPONENT for
publication, believing it will be appreciated
by all young mothers, who read our paper,
as it was a selection of our Honored
President, presented to our first baby
daughter by him, when she was six months
old, in the year 1878 at Beaver, Utah.
