Sister Howard, was a pioneer in woman’s
work,I feel to bless you all, and desire that
we may live near to the Lord.   We all have
our individual lives to live.  The scriptures
teach us that Jesus trod the winepress
alone, so our troubles are not known to
our friends, only to us and God.  We have
our individual sorrows and joys.  In our
hearts we feel a compensation for our
sorrows, and this brings a blessing, to help
us to a higher life.  In sorrow we draw
nearer to God, He knows us best and
understands our life works.  Sister Kimball
closed by giving her blessing to her sisters.
  Sister L. D. Alder felt to encourage the
sisters in their great life work, pictured out
the necessity of living near to the Lord
that we might enjoy the communion of the
Holy Ghost, which leads into all truth and
light, desired that we might all be faithful,
that in the end we might enter into His
presence.
  President Horne added a few closing re-
marks.  Desired to see fathers and mothers
take note and be wise in cultivating their
children, and restrain them all in their
power, gather them around their knees and
teach them, of God, His requirements, and
his laws, and read them the stories of old
in the Bible.  President Horne also warned
the parents to pay strict attention to the
genealogies of their families. The meeting
was adjourned for four weeks, on account
of the conference.  President Horne an-
nounced the Stake Conference to meet in
the Assembly Hall, June 15, 1894.  De-
sired all the sisters to attend.
	Sang, “We thank thee O, God for a
prophet.”  Benediction, Sister J. C. Howe.
