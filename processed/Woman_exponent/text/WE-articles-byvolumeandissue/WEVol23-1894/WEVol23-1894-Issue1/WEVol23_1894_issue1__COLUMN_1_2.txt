A FIRESIDE TALK WITH MY
	SISTERS.
	SURROUNDING circumstances, every
day occurrences and present conditions in
society have called forth, and aroused dor-
mant sentiments which have for the past
number of years remained expressionless
in my own home circle, but which ever
since I have been old enough to think and
reflect for myself, I have most strongly
supported which as wife and mother, I can
plainly see the necessity for adoption and
advocacy in our communities, as Latter-day
Saints, as true women, and refined modest
sisters of humanity.
	Why is it that among our intelligent
gentlewomen, so many employ and prefer
men, as Physicians, instead of those of
our own sex? Not only in contagious
diseases but obstetrics and as doctors for
their babies in all infantile troubles, every
trivial ailment, children are subjects for
medical treatment and advise. Well says
one lady "men are so much more thorough,
they have so much more time to study and
practice than women,” (we do not believe
all this.)  Yet, allowing part as true, let us
review—some of the many disadvantages,
and hindrances placed against her in this
field.
	In the first place there always has been a
strong prejudice against women in medical
colleges of the past; many not allowing her
to enter as a student, and even to day,
she is prevented, debared from many
privileges that justly belong to her.
	Next she is not paid near as large a
salary as man, and she is expected to do a
great deal more work.  I feel that this is a
gross partiality a cruel wrong.  Conserva-
tism, custom still holds an influence in
civilization; yet,we must not worry over it,
for time, intelligence and woman’s own
ability will eventually right these condi-
tions, and that which will aid and bring
about a reform the most speedy will be the
firm, united support of women by women.
