IN MEMORIAM.

	FROM the Utah County Board of officers of Silk
Association, who desire to express their deep
respect, love and appreciation of the life and
labors so exemplary verified in our beloved Sis-
ter Sarah B. Saunders, who departed this life in
Provo, March 31st, 1895, aged sixty four years,
and nine months, after an illness of three weeks
caused by paralysis, and who held the position of
Vice President in this association for eight years.
	Resolved that we honor and cherish her many
virtues and Christian spirit for truly peace and
charity covered her whole being as a mantle and
beamed from her pure countenance and in her
death we lose an indefatigable and clever work-
er in silk. In the incipiency of this organization,
she persistently persevered in weaving handker-
chiefs from very crude material upon a home
improvised loom her last effort was in weaving
over forty yards of silk from which our flag is
made, she was very desirous this industry should
be successfully and permanently established and
lent her best energies to the same. And while
we shall miss her we realize she has gone to her
reward after having led a good and faithful life, to
meet those gone before, may those who remain
follow her worthy example and ever remember
the love she evinced for them.
	Resolved that we publish this tribute of esteem,
send a copy to the family and place it upon our
record.
					M. A. TILL, President.
	SARAH GOODMAN, Vice Prest., in behalf of
the board.
