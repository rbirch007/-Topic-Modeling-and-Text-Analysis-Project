SALT LAKE CO. W. S. A.
	MINUTES of the Woman Suffrage meet-
ing held February 5th, 1895, in the 14th
Ward Hall. Dr. Ferguson in the chair.
Sang “Come away,” prayer Prof. Stewart,
sang “Oh ye fair ones.” Dr. Ferguson
introduced Prof. W. M. Stewart, who in
his usual able and eloquent manner gave
his reason for being in favor of Suffrage for
women. Dr. S. B. Young was then intro-
duced by the president, he also gave some
excellent reasons why the ladies should
vote. Closing his remarks with the poem
entitled “The new star of Wyoming.”
Brother Adams of North Dakota was in-
troduced to the audience, and made a short
speech, thought all good women would
favor temperance. Committees were then
formed for the entertainment to be given on
Susan B. Anthony’s birthday. Adjourned
for two weeks.

	Minutes of the Woman Suffrage meeting
held February 19th, in the 14th Ward Hall,
Dr. Ferguson in the chair. Committees
were chosen to wait upon the delegates to
the Convention. Report read of the enter-
tainment, and other business transacted.
Adjourned for two weeks.
	
	Minutes of meeting held March 5th in
the 14th Ward Hall, Dr. Ferguson in the
chair. Sang, “In twilight’s hour.” Prayer
B. Midgley.  Sang, “Hail the sound.”
Roll called, minutes read and approved.
Dr. Ferguson called for reports from those
appointed to interview delegates. Ruth
M. Fox had called three times but failed to
see the gentlemen. E. M. Price found all
favorable. M. E. Irvine’s companion D.
W. Eardley had been sick, they had not
visited. M. P. Silver said all she had
visited were in favor. Dr. Ferguson had
talked with a number, advised the ladies to
visit the convention, read a letter from
Rachel Foster Avery, acknowledging
twenty dollars sent by Salt Lake County
for Susan B. Anthony’s birthday fund, also
read an address entitled “Woman,” read
at the woman’s Convention. Adjourned
for two weeks.
