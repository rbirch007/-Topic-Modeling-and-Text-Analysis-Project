A PLEASANT REUNION.

	MARCH the 10th being the 17th anniver-
sary of the organization of the Summit Re-
lief Society, of the Parowan Stake of Zion
it was duly celebrated on the 11th, com-
mencing at 2 p. m. The meeting was
called to order by President Lette Dailey.
Singing by the choir, prayer by Bishop 
Joseph B. Dailey. An interesting pro-
gramme was rendered by the sisters of the
Relief Society interspersed with remarks by
Patriarch James Dailey, Wm. Dailey, S. C.
Hulet and Joseph H. Armstrong. An
essay by Sister Susie Armstrong, entitled
“Woman’s Influence,” being worthy of
special mention. At the close of the meet-
ing President Lette Dailey wished all the
local and visiting members of the Relief
Society with their husbands to adjourn to
her home as she wished to talk to them.
What was their surprise upon arriving
there to find three large tables spread with
the comforts of life of which seventy of the
brethren and sisters partook. After which
all returned to the hall to enjoy themselves
in dancing, refreshments being served dur-
ing the evening. All went home feeling
that the day was one long to be remember-
ed.					A. M. DALLEY, Sec.
