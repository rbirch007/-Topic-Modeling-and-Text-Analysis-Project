TESTIMONY OF MRS. BASSETT.

	AT the Semi-monthly meeting of ladies
held in the Fourteenth Ward Hall, Salt
Lake City, Sister Mary E. Bassett, presi-
dent of the Second Ward Relief Society,
in referring to the prevalent views of pro-


fanity and intemperance, which have so in-
creased of late in our midst, related her
early experience in the cuhrch.
	She said she was born in Missouri and
was a little girl when the Saints were driven
from their homes by an infuriated and
and bloodthirsty mob. She afterwards with
her parents found a home at Nauvoo and
remained in that city and vicinity until
again by mob violence compelled to seek a
home farther west. Among the earlier
emigrants she crossed the great desert
plains, and after suffering from want and
privation, settled in this, then, wild and
barren valley, during all those trying ex-
periences, and while encountering all these
trials and difficulties, she never once heard
the name of Deity profaned, and not until she
had grown to womanhood had she seen an
intoxicated man or woman.
