EQUAL SUFFRAGE SECTION.

[In the Convention, April 5th.]
	RICHARDS of Salt Lake, moved to adopt
the original section, and parliamentary
sparring and fillbustering and attempts to
adjourn at once commenced, but the gentle-
man from Salt Lake was successful and se-
cured a roll call vote on the passage of the
equal suffrage clause. It was as follows:
	Ayes—Allen, Anderson, Barnes, Boyer,
Brandley, Buys, Call, Cannon, Chidester,
Christainsen, Clark, Coray, Corfmar, Crane, 
Creer, Cunningham, Driver, Eichnor,
Eldredge, Emery, Engberg, Evans, of
Weber, Farr, Francis, Gibbs, Goodwin,
Hammond, Hart, Halliday, Heybourne,
Howard, Hughes, Hyde, Ivins, Johnson,
Kimball, of Salt Lake, Kimball, of Weber,
Lambart, L. Larson, Lemmon, Lewis,
William Lowe, Peter Lowe, Low of Cache, 
Maeser, Maloney, Maughan, McFarland,
Miller, Morris, Murdock of Beaver, Mur-
dock of Wasatch, Murdock of Summit,
Partridge, Peters, Peterson, of Grand,
Peterson, of Sanpete, Preston, Raleigh,
Richards, Ricks, Robertson, Robinson, of
Kane, Robinson of Wayne, Shurtliff, Snow,
Squires, Strevell, Symons, Thompson,
Thoresen, Thorne, Thurman, Wells,
Whitney—75.
	Noes—Haynes, Hill, Page, Pierce, Rob-
erts, Ryan, Sharp, Spencer, Stover, Van
Horne—14.
	Absent—Adams, Evans, of Utah, Jolley,
Kearns, Kerr, C. P. Larsen, Moritz,
Nebeker, Thatcher, Varian, Warrum,
Williams—12.
	Excused—Bowdle, Button, Cushing,
Green, James—5.
	The Convention then adjourned until
Monday at 10 a. m.
