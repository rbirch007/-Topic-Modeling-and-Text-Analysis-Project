IN MEMORIAM.

	WHEREAS, In the dispensation of Divine
Providence our esteemed co-laborer, one of
the Vice-Presidents of the Territorial Wo-
man’s Suffrage Association, Caroline E. Dye
departed this life February 26th, 1895, we
deem it expedient that we in some public
manner express our appreciation of her
many good qualities and of her efficient ser-
vices Therefore be it:
	Resolved: That the Utah Woman’s Suf-
frage Association commend the self-sacrific-
ing devotion of our loved friend to the cause
of purity, equal justice, and religious in-
tegrity. Mrs. Dye was ever discreet,
liberal and progressive. She was eminently
social, pleasing and considerate in manner.
	Resolved, That we deeply condole with
the family and with the community of which
Mrs. Dye formed so lovable and important a
part.
	Resolved, That we venerate her mem-
ory, and commend the amiable and helpful
example of our loved friend and sister.
					Sarah M. Kimball.
