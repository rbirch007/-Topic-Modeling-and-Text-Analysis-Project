WASHINGTON CO. W. S. A.
	WE are progressing fairly well with our
Woman’s Suffrage, of course we have the
usual amount of prejudice and erroneous
ideas to contend with that all such move-
ments have, still I am in hopes that by be-
ing cautious and prudent and going slow
at first, we will be able to overcome these
prejudices. We have had two very interest-
ing and instructive lectures having called
special meetings for that purpose. One by
Hon. E. G. Woolley, “Fundamental
Principles of Government. One by A.
M. Tanner when he was down here a few
weeks ago, on “Public Opinion.” The
Washington Co. W. S. Association has some
very able women both as officers and mem-
bers, and must certainly result in a great
deal of educational work among the
people.
