with a number of the members, met the

the drag “Utah,” accompanied them in a
drive over the principal parts of the City.
These ladies were otherwise entertained by
the Club.
	These visitors made honorable mention
of the kindnesses received in Utah, after
their return to the East.
	Aug. 31st, Dr. E. R. Shipp, read an able
paper on Heredity. Dr. Ellen B. Ferguson,
followed with an interesting lecture on the
same subject; many deep thoughts and
earnest reflections have been awakened, al-
so some discussion has been indulged in,
on the subjects which have been so
thoughtfully presented to the club.
	Many original poems have been read, al-
so many selected gems of both prose and
poetry.
	Some beautiful recitations have been
given by C. Louise Boyden and others.
	Sentiments from the Poets, both men and
women, have been quoted, also some from
the book of Psalms. Last but not least,
we have had rare music rendered, both
vocal and instrumental, by some of the
members.
	President E. R. Shipp, and E. B. Wells,
have won all hearts by their courteous lov-
ing manner, and now at the close of the
year, we feel that we have indeed been
benefitted, by the organization, and desire
its success in all future years, and that it
may ever be composed of thoughtful
earnest workers.
