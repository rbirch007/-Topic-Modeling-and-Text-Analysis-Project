OBITUARY.

	DIED, Sept. 27th 1894, Orderville Utah, Ann
Carling Chamberlain wife of Thomas Chamber-
lain and daughterof Isaac W.and Asenath Brown-
ing Carling, was born April 7, 1859, at Fillmore,
Utah. She was the mother of eight children one
of the number having passed away, her suffering
for four days was past severe with inflamation of
the bowels, then our Heavenly Father relieved
her of her suffering. She was a dutiful wife and
an affectionate mother. Deceased was a mem
of the Relief Society and also of the Y. L. M. I.
A., the bereaved husband and children have our
heartfelt sympathy.
	We miss thee from our home dear mother,
		We miss thee from thy place;
	A shadow o’er our life is cast,
		We miss thy smiling face.
	
	We miss thy kind and willing hand.
		Thy fond and earnest care,
	Our home is dark without thee,
		We miss thee everywhere.
						CLARISSA WILHELM.
Young Ladies Journal—please copy.

	A noble woman’s gone to rest
		A gentle mother’s voice is still.
	Sweet children and a husband kind
		Bow humbly to the Father’s will.

	A guileless life our Sister led
		Esteemed and honored by us all;
	A modest flower which sweetly bloomed
		Must by the keen destroyer fall.

	The loss we feel is Sister’s gain
		A Heavenly bliss is her reward:
	For while on earth she ever strove
		To faithfully obey the Lord.

	And those who mourn for mother, wife,
		May they find peace from God above;
	And if they lead as pure a life
		Again they’ll know a mother’s love.
