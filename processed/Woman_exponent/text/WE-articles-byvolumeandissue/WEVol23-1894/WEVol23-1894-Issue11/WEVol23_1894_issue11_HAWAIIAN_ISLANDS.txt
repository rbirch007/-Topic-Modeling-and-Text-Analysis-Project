HAWAIIAN ISLANDS.

	Relief Society Conference. Hawaiian
Islands: The morning air at Lanihule on
October 5th was fanned by a variety of
neatly made banners and flags bearing the
inspired expressions of Joseph the Prophet
relative to the labors of the sisters in this
church.
	More than one hundred sisters formed in
line and marched to the meetinghouse fully
enthused with the importance of the event,
viz, their general conference.
	After the usual opening exercises, read-
ing of reports etc., in which twenty dif-
ferent societies were represented; Sister
Noall made a few opening remarks appro-
priate for the occasion. Twelve of the
societies were represented verbally by their
presidents or their representatives. Inter-
spersed between these were songs by the
native sisters and a duet by sisters Laura
Fisher and Phoebe Scholes. Words of in-
struction and encouragement were given by
sisters Alice Harmon and Laura Fisher.
Secretary Ruth Kauhine spoke on the sub-
ject of a testimony of the truth and one of
the aids—Kaluapalaoa spoke on the sub-
ject of the laying on of hands for the heal-
ing of the sick. Sister Lizzie Brown gave
words of counsel. Sister Makanoe read a
letter of condolence containing resolutions
of respect for Sister Maulani Kea, one of
our central officers here, who departed this
life September 25th. Sister Noall spoke of
the object of the society, the duty of its
officers, members etc., referred to those
who were not so zealously engaged in this
work as formerly, and encouraged all to a
renewal of diligence in keeping God’s
commandments.
	The report from twenty societies, (ten
not being represented) shows, members
494, meetings help 333, cash on hand at
date of last report, $167,85, cash received
$208,40, disbursed for charitable purposes
