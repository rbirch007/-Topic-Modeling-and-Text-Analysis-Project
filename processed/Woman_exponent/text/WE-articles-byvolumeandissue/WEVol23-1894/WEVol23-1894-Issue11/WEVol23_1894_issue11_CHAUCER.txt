			CHAUCER.

	LIKE a new star did the genius of
Chaucer burst upon the English world of
the fourteenth century. A star which, by
its mellow radiance, revealed to that world,
beauties and pleasures it never dreamed of
before. A heralding luminary shedding its
friendly light down to the brilliant con-
stellation which was to follow in the dis-
tance. 
	 As the “Father of English poetry” he
has claimed the honor of all succeeding
generations; as the first to fix the clear
form of literary English, welding the two
tongues, struggling for supremacy, into
harmonious oneness and rhythm, thus
making it a true medium of poetic thought,
he must ever claim our gratitude; and, as
the first English artist, who, overflowing
with joy and love for all about him, war-
bled forth the gladness of his heart to de-
light the solemn world, we cannot with
hold from him our admiration.
 	A true child of nature—he sang of her
beauties and opened to the less observing
multitude, the door to a new creation.
