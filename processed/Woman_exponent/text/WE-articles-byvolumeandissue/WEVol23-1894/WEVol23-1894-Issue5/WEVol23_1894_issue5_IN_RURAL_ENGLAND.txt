IN RURAL ENGLAND.
XLI.
	NATURALLY Mrs. Bland was very lonely
after the Waltons had left America and she
could scarcely wait for news of their safe
arrival. She busied herself as best she
could with household duties, and the train-
ing of her little son the one object of her
especial care and devotion. He was grow-
ing to be a tall boy, and so very like his
father, in both appearance and disposition.
She had many plans in her mind concern-
ing his future, but was very seldom think-
ing of her own. Occasionally the absent
cousin Clair, and the companion of his
wanderings would come to her mind, but
she would not allow herself to form any
day-dreams even concerning what might
