EDITORIAL NOTES.

	RELIEF SOCIETY matter also W. S. A.
minutes are crowded out of the present
number of the paper to make room for the
Christmas story, but will come in after the
holidays.
	THE new blanks for Relief Society Re-
ports and also new books for keeping ac-
counts properly, are now ready and for sale
at the Deseret News Office. Every Society
should have these new books, which are so
perfectly adapted to the use of the Society
and have been prepared expressly for that
purpose. Send and get them at once.
Remember that each Society should report
the entire year 1894 from January 1—to
December 31—and the Secretaries of each
Stake prepare one for the General Secretary
to reach here before the middle of February
1895.
	MISS PHOEBE COUZINS, of St. Louis, the
famous lawyer and lecturer of National
reputation will be in our city now very
soon and will probably give one or two
lectures while here if satisfactory arrange-
ments can be made; Miss Couzins is a silver-
ite and has decided views of her own in re-
gard to general public questions, and is
fearless in expressing her opinions, that is
she has the courage of her convictions.
The women of Utah owe her some grati-
tude for speaking publicly against their
disfranchisement and at a time when our
people were most unpopular. We hope the
ladies of Salt Lake will show to Miss
Couzins during her stay in this City they
have not forgotten her able defense of the
Utah women in times past, and will show
her kind attentions while she tarries in our
midst, that she may carry away with her
pleasant recollections.


MISS AMELIA E. BARR is in Europe,
where she will spend the winter.
