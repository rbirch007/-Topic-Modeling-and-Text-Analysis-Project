R. S. REPORTS.

	The Annual District Relief Society Con-
ference on the East side of Bear River was
held at Montpelier Feb. 24th, 1895, Stake
President Julia P. Lindsey presiding.
Meeting opened by the choir, singing the
hymn, “Come, come ye Saints.” Prayer
by David Osborn. Singing, “Now let us
rejoice in the Day of Salvation.”
	President Lindsey, made a few remarks,
stating the purpose for which we had met
together, after which the various wards
were favorably represented by their respec-
tive presidents whose names are as follows.
	President Hagansen of Montpelier. Presi-
dent Vanormam of Bennington. President
Fretwell of Georgetown. President
Dalymple, of Preston; President Dalton
of Thomas Fork. President Sparks of
Dingle, as there was no one present from
Soda Springs, that ward was not reported.
	President Lindsey was pleased with the
reports given also thankful for the good
condition of the Relief Society on this side
of the river as this shows the sisters are
improving. Said we have a great labor to
perform, as daughters of Zion, and there is
great blessings in store for us if we are
faithful, we should remember the teachings
of our Savior and try to follow his worthy
example and be kind and charitable to-
wards all, not allowing ourselves to indulge
in selfishness, for our Heavenly Father is
not pleased with us when this is the case,
we should be thankful for the trials we are
called to pass through, for they are calcu-
lated to strengthen and purify us, and to
prepare us for a higher sphere. President
Lindsey gave much good and timely in-
structions, especially in regard to the stor-
ing of grain, she bore a faithful testimony
to the truthfulness of this great Latter-day
work, and closed her remarks by encourag-
ing us to make good use of the time and
talents which God has given us that we
may be able to give a good account of our
stewardship.
	Meeting adjourned until 2 p. m. Sing-
ing, “Come listen to a Prophet’s voice.”
Benediction by Charles Bridges.
