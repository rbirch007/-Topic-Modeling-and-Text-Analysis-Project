			ACROSTIC. 
[Written on the Seventy-fifth Anniversary of the
birth of Rnth W. Tyler.)
		Round another year has rolled,
		Unto us its tale has told,
		Tears, and laughter, joy, and pain,
		Have been number’d in its train.

		While its changes we recall,
		Each leal heart has thanks for all, 
		Leaning on our God, and Friend,
		’Tis His love all blessings send. 
		Once again we honors pay, 
		No regrets, this happy day. 

		To our friend we wish much joy, 
		Year by year, as they go by,
		Long may pleasure wreathe her brow, 
		Every happiness below, 
		Rest at last in Heaven.
	Respectfully, Your Brother, 
						W. G. BICKLEY
Beaver City Utah. Feb, 25th, 1895.


“THE California Senate, on March 14,
voted in favor of a constitutional amend-
ment extending full suffrage to women.
The amendment had already been adopted
by the Assembly. Three cheers for Cali-
fornia.”
