U. W. P. C.

	MINUTES of the U. W. P. C. held in Dr.
Ellis R. Shipp’s parlor Aug. 31st, 1894,
President Shipp in the chair. Prayer by
Mrs. Lydia D. Alder. The Secretary being
absent Ruth M. Fox was elected Sec. pro
tem. Roll responded to by sentiments
from Bryant; Minnie J. Snow and Melise
Wilson were accepted as members. A very
pathetic original poem was read by Nellie
C. Taylor. An interesting and instructive
paper on heredity was then read by Dr. E
R. Shipp followed by a very able address on
the same subject by Dr. Ellen B. Ferguson
C. Louise Boydon favored the club with a
choice selection on the piano and Mary A.
Freeze read one of her own poems, after
which a motion was made and carried that
we devote twenty five minutes next meet-
ing to the discussion of heredity. E. B.
Wells then gave us an entertaining and
much appreciated paper on Pen Names.
The same lady read current events, Miss
Patterson not being present. A letter was
read from Nevada V. Davis a member, who
is now in the East, engaged on the New
York Herald. It was then moved and car-
ried that five ladies viz. Mesdames E. B.
Wells, Minnie J. Snow. Phebe C. Young,
Lydia D. Alder and Eliza Bennion, prepare
themselves for the discussion next meeting.
Syntithia Dickinson suggested that another
time we discuss the subjects the same even-
ing they are given. The program for next
meeting was read as follows, Discussion on
Heredity. Indian traditions, Syntithia
Dickinson. Poem Ruth M. Fox. Reci-
tation Ellis Shipp. Sentiments from
Elizabeth Barrett Browning. Dismissed by
Minnie J. Snow. Refreshments and the
usual good time followed.
				R. M. FOX, Sec. pro tem.


MISS ANNE WHITNEY, the sculptor, has
completed a bust of Keats in marble, which
is to be placed in the parish church of
Hampstead, London, as a memorial from
the American and English lovers of the
poet. The bust is pronounced a triumph of
artistic genius.—Ex.
