U. W. P. CLUB.

	THE Utah Woman’s Press Club met at
the office of Dr. E. R. Shipp.  Thursday
evening Jan 31st.  The president in the 
chair.  Opening prayer by Mrs Maria W.
Wilcox.  The roll was answered by poetical
sentiments.  Minutes of previous meeting 
read and approved.
	A paper on Milton by Mrs. Nellie C.
Taylor was read by the secretary.  The
reading was followed by a discussion on the 
life of the great, blind poet by members and
visitors. 
	A paper on Rubenstein, original and
edifying, was given by Dr. E. B. Ferguson.
	Miss Claire Ferguson favored the club
with a piano solo.
	It was then announced by the president
that there is to be a meeting of the Federa-
tion of Woman’s Clubs in this city Feb.
27.  A representative should be sent from
the Press Club, and a subject chosen for her
to speak upon. 
	Miss Ada Patterson of the Herald sug-
gested that the club at some time discuss
the subject “Women in Journalism.”
	Dr. Ferguson suggested that we also dis-
cuss “The Best Method of Carrying on a 
Club for Working-girls.”
	Miss Patterson also suggested that we 
some evening devote the time to John
Ruskin.
	Adjourned until Feb. 15th, closing prayer 
by L. S. Wilcox.
	As it was entertainment evening, after
the close of the regular session, an hour was 
spent in social conversation and the enjoy-
ment of refreshments.  Although the at-
tendance was light, members and visitors
parted feeling they had been repaid for
coming. 	
			L. S. WILCOX, Rec. Sec’y.


THE Idaho Legislature has voted to sub-
mit at the next general election a consti-
tutional amendment giving full suffrage to
women. The vote for it in the Senate was
unanimous. The figures of the vote in the
House have not reached us. The Idaho
Republicans and Populists both declared for
equal suffrage at their last State conventions
