MISS ANTHONY’S GREETING.
	“Good Friends—This is the first national con-
vention of the Woman’s Suffrage Association that
I have ever had the honor to call to order south
of Mason and Dixon’s line. (Applause.) I am
delighted to stand here in the city of Atlanta and
open this twenty-seventh annual session of the
association. (Applause.) We have represented
here not only the sunny southland, but the states
of the far northwest—Oregon, Wyoming and
Colorado,—and even the Dominion of Canada.
(Applause.) I hold in my hand the gavel that
called to order the legislature of Wyoming that
gave to women the privilege of voting in this
country. (Loud Applause.) It is fitting that
America’s first republic—first in fact as well as
in name—should be planted on the heights of the
Rocky mountains. They indicate the eternal
principles on which the suffrage movement is
founded—the principles or equal rights to all
mankind. (Loud applause.) I hope the good
men of Atlanta will understand that we do not
claim to be the best half of creation, but we do
claim to be half: (Laughter.) I am sure
that our meeting in Atlanta will be a pleasant one
and that we are all glad of the privilege of meet-
ing in your beautiful city. Let us now proceed to
business and show the people of Atlanta that wo-
men know how to run a convention. (Laughter
and applause.)
