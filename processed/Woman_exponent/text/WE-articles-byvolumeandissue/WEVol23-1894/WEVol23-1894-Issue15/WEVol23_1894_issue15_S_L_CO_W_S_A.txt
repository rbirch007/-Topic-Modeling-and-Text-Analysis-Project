S. L. CO. W. S. A.

	Minutes of the W. S. A. held January
22nd 1895, in the 14th Ward Hall. Presi-
dent Dr. Ferguson in the chair, Sang
“Hark the myriad voices” Prayer Mary
E. Bassett, Sang, “Come, come away.”
Roll called, minutes read and approved.
President Dr. Ferguson briefly related about
a meeting she had attended, where she
found many who were not in favor of suf-
frage, thought if all could write in this
cause they would be able to do more efficient
work. There will be very few that will
not work for purity, truth and right.
	Ruth M. Fox asked if we had the right
to discuss the petition referred to in the
minutes. Motion made and carried that
further discussion be allowed.
	J. E. Macfarlane, Ella Hyde, Julia Druce,
Ruth M. Fox, Mary L. Morris were in
favor of the petition being laid over till next
meeting. Mary P. Silver, Adella W.
Eardley, M. E. Irvine, E. M. Price were in
favor of the petitions being circulated.
After much discussion a motion was made
and carried that we do nothing till next
meeting. Reference was then made to the
entertainment to be given on the 15th, Feb.
committees chosen to act for the benefit of
the same, adjourned till Feb. 5th.
					M. E. Irvine, Sec.
