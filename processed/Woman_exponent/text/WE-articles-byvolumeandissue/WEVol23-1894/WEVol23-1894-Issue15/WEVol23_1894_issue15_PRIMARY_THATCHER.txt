PRIMARY THATCHER.

	THE people of Thatcher assembled at 
the Academy hall on Thanksgiving day to 
witness the exhibits of a conjoint fair, viz,
the Relief Society, Young Ladies and Pri-
mary. President Christopher Layton being
present expressed himself that the fair was
a credit to those who presided over it. It
was a grand sight to see the different speci-
mens of needle work, and all kinds of
fancy work. It was surprising to see how
many things were presented at the fair,
with such fine taste; and it was beyond any
thing that was anticipated for a newly
settled place. There was a dress made by
a little girl seven years old, that would be
a credit to older ones. There were many
beautiful things made by the little hands,
it would be impossible to name them all.
There was a beautiful selection of fruit for
tbe time of year. There were no animals
but some canary birds that were raised by
Primary girls. There were delicious cakes,
pies and jellies made by the little girls.
There were cupboards, clock shelves, cor-
ner brackets, maps drawn, in fact they
were too numerous to mention by our Pri-
mary boys. There were nine presents
awarded in the Primary department for the
best work. The Primary was presided
over by the presidency of the Primary.
