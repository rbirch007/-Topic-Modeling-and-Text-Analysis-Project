			LIBERTY STAKE.
	The semi-annual conference of Liberty
Stake Relief Society was held in the 9th
ward meetinghouse June 24, 1909, at 10 a.
m. and 2 p. m.. President Annie S. Musser
presiding.
	Sister Rebecca Nibley and Alice M. Horne
of the General Board, also Elders Milando
Pratt and Charles W. Symons, of the High
Council of Liberty Stake were present. All
of the Stake Board were present, and repre-
sentatives from each ward.
	Miss Lillian Branting was the soloist.
	At the morning session the statistical and

[Column 2]
financial report for the year ending Dec.
1908 were read and accepted. Reports were
made by the presidents of the various wards
and the society in each ward was reported as
progressing favorably. Sisters Rebecca Nib-
ley and Alice M. Home made some timely
remarks and gave some good instructions,
encouraged us in our work, and thought we
had always been a great help to the priest-
hood. 
	At the afternoon session the members of
the General Board of the Relief Society, also
of the Stake Board were unanimously sus-
tained. Sisters M. Pratt and Martha Morris
reported for the Relief Society special mis-
sionary work as progressing and a good
work being performed.
	Sister Jennie Lloyd delivered a lecture on
the life of Miss Francis E. Willard, also
spoke for a short time on the purity of our
society, of our homes and children.
	Sister Horne again addressed us, speaking
words of encouragement to help us in our
every day life. Elder Symons also spoke
for a short time.
	President Musser made a few closing re-
marks, thanking all who had helped to make
our conference a success. After closing
exercises conference adjourned for six
months.
