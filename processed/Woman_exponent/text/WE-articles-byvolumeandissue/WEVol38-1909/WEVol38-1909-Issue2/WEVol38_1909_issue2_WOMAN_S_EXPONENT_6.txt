OUTLINE FOR MOTHERS WORK.
	MORGAN STAKE.
Lesson 1. The Sin of Gossip.
	“Nay speak no ill, a kindly word,
	Can never leave a sting behind.”
	(a) Of the many beautiful precepts taught
by Jesus none is more important or less re-
garded than “Judge not that ye be not judg-
ed” (Matt. 7: 1). “A good tree cannot
bring forth evil fruit” (Matt. 7: 18).

Lesson 2. What Influence has Evil Speak-
	ing.
	(a) Does it harm the speaker (Psalm 34:
13; I Peter 3: 10, 11). Its Influence on chil-
dren. (b) Explain how it may mar the life,
or place a dark blot on the character of the
one spoken of, also the influence it has on
the public, (James 3: 5, 6, 8; Prov. 25: 23—
26: 18, 20).
	“Be not the first to speak it
	If you a fault should see.”

Lesson 3. Parable of the Ten Talents
	(Matt. 25: 14-30).
	What talent do I possess and do I culti-
vate it. Patience, Faith, Cheerfulness, the
power to instruct or comfort others, or any-
thing that helps to make life better and
brighter are talents that should be cultivat-
ed.

Lesson 4. Language in the Home.
	Give meaning of Mother Tongue, “He that
hath a perverse tongue falleth into mis-
chief.” Impure language displeasing unto
the Lord read James 3; Matt. 12: 36. Cor-
rect language necessary at all times and in
all places.

Lesson 5. Habit.
	How shall we form good habits? How
shall we teach the value of good habits to
our children (read Improvement Era, March
1903, p.377; Juvenile Instructor, March 15,
1900; Young Woman’s Journal, October
1906).

Lesson 6. Proper Home Ideals.
	The love of the beautiful is a handmaid
of civilization, “For as a man thinketh in his
heart so is he” (Prov. 23: 7). “Be ye per-
fect even as your Father in Heaven is per-
fect,” (Matt. 5: 48; II Samuel 22: 31). Aspi-
ration to excel in everything that is good or
praiseworthy should be instilled,encouraged
and developed in our children. Why? (Ar-
ticles of Faith 13). The love of order and
cleanliness should be so highly developed
that every member of the family would be
uncomfortable when conditions were other-
wise.

Lesson 7. Social Purity.
	“To be socially pure one must store the
mind with pure enobling thoughts.” “Bles-
sed are the pure in heart for they shall see
God (Matt., 5: 8).
	(a) Avoid slander, vulgar language and im-
politeness.
	(b) Overcome selfish tendencies.
	(c) Cultivate truthfulness, integrity, good-
ness and pure thoughts (Eph. 5: 3, 4).

Lesson 8. Affection.
	1. Love of parents for each other.
	2. Of parents for children.
	3. Of children for parents.

[Column 2]
	Aim: Love in the home is necessary to
gain and maintain the highest ideal in home
government. General truth—Love induces
patience, gentleness, long suffering, etc.
	Suggestions: 1. If we love one another
God dwelleth in us, and His love is perfect-
ed in us, (John 4: 13). Thou shalt live to-
gether in love (Doc. and Cov. 42, 45; see
Doc. and Cov. 121: 41-46). Love may be
encouraged by a proper consideration of one
anothers’ feelings.
