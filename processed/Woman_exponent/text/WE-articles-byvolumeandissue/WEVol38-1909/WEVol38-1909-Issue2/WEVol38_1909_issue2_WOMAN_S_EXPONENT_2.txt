WOMAN’S EXPONENT.


[Column 1]
		THE CITY OF THE SAINTS.
					I.
Among the Rocky mountains whose grandeur
	poets sing
Where streams of living water from hidden foun-
	tains spring
And crags resound the echoes of fiercest eagle’s
	scream,
In wildest, awe-inspiring mood Nature’s glories
	teem,
There raging, foaming torrents dash madly down
	below,
The mountains grandly tow’ring are capped with
	crystal snow,
As bulwark on the eastside, unbroken there they
	seem.
As sentinels they mutely stand, catch the sun’s
	last gleam,
That falls in crimson glory amid their purple
	haze—
As burnished gold reflecting its glitt’ring fading
	rays.
The panorama changes—a mountain sunset
	grand
Is glinting far and near—shadows deepening o’er
	the land.
The dying glory circling, in clearest liquid blue,
Faces in the Western mountains, with varying
	color’s hue,
Their many frozen peaks seem to glisten mid the
	gold,
And challenge admiration of all who may behold.

					II.
The lake like molten fire, in a burning limpid
	sea,
Is blazing at their feet—sleeping in tranquillity.
Then suddenly they cease, and as wid’ning open
	gate
They let the glory through—on the other side then
	wait
The clouds catch up the radiance, diffusing it on
	high,
And paint the gorgeous colors across the arching
	sky.
Now Ensign smiles approval and dons the crimson
	hue—
Our flag floats there, its many stars in their field
	of blue—
And ours the proudest of them, though new
	among them all,
Partakes of freedom’s spirit, among these moun-
	tains tall.
’Tis here Salt Lake is builded, a city on a hill—
And rising o’er the world, will her destiny fulfill.
By ancients she was seen, all her wondrous beauty
	known, 
Amid prophetic, glowing light is her history
	shown.
The world knows of her Temple—Moroni’s garb
	of gold,
And of her courts and mansions so long ago fore-
	told.

					III.
Her wide laid streets are shaded from Summer’s
	burning sun,
On either side slow murm’ring streams o’er their
	courses run.
Proud Salt Lake City sits enthroned, a Queen in
	the land,
And gazes o’er the nations from her exalted
	stand.
On all their borders discord, war’s crimson tide
	o’er flows;
And widow’s cries and orphan’s moans, telling of
	their woes,
Arise in clouds like incense—float on the heavy
air—
And darken e’en as night clouds, the scene so
	passing fair.

					IV.
A Watchman guards thy fortress, what says he
	of the night?
Do inky clouds lower, shutting out the noon day 
	light?
Must nations ever rise and fall ’mid their armies
	slain?
When will the Great Messiah begin his promised
	reign?
Serenely on the mountains thy glory blazes yet;
And reaches wide as all the world, lovely Deseret.
One smiles upon thy future, e’en like thy sacred
	past,
O’ershadowing with his watch care, and ever it
	will last—
Then shine thou on in splendor, where stern the
	mountains rise,

[Column 2]
The landscape far outstretching fairest ’neath the
	skies—
’Mid this grand scene, thy triumph soon will 
	be; Christ thy King,
Who comes from courts of glory thy diadem will
	bring.
