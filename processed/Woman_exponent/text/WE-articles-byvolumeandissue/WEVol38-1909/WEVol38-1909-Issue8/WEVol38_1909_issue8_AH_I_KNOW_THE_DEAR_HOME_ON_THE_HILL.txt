Ah, I know the dear home on the hill,
	Where she sat as she leaned o’er this page,
And traced the one name on its whiteness;
	That is dimmed now, and yellow with age.

And I seem to be gazing once more
	On the beautiful face of my sister;
For, perchance, I was standing near by,
	While the forest winds tenderly kissed her.

Let me draw back the veil of the years
	And recall the dear home scenes once more;
Though the trees of the forest are bare,
	And snow drifts lie deep at the door,


There is warmth and contentment within, 
	There is hope—there is love for each other; 
And brown eyes and blue turn with pride
	To our star, the fair face of my mother. 

Here, hid in the “Gospel of John,”
	I find a pressed spray of sweet clover, 
The white blossom faded and sere, 
	And I pause ere I turn the page over. 

Was I there when her white fingers plucked it? 
	Did it grow in some bright forest nook? 
Or in some sunny spot in the garden, 
	Was I there when ’Twas placed in the book? 

And here, in the sweet Psalms of David, 
	Is a book mark in letters or blue; 
A token of love wrought by “Hope,”
	In her youth time, so wondrous and new. 

The “Book of Life” little she dreamed 
	That her life’s book so seemingly fair, 
Would unfold many dark, tear stained pages, 
	Many leaves marred with struggle and care. 

O memory, how sadly you whisper,
	“These are only the phantoms of brightness 
Long past; let the veil fall again
	O’er the shrouded years, robed in youth’s white-
		ness.”

O my soul, look aloft from these tracings, 
	By a hand long since lifeless and cold; 
For in heaven the sweet name of “Mary,”
	Is written in letters of gold
