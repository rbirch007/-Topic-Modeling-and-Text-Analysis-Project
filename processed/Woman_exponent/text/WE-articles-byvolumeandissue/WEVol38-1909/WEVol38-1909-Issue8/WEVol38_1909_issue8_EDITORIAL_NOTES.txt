EDITORIAL NOTES.
	The birthday anniversary of President
Wilford Woodruff was appropriately cele-
brated by his numerous family on March
1st, in the Fourteenth Ward chapel. Music-
al exercises and reminescences by children
and grandchildren of this illustrious chief-
tain were the principal features of the gath-
ering.
	The daughters of Sister Emma S. Wood-
ruff gave a birthday party for her on Thurs-
day, March 3, at the handsome residence of
her daughter, Mrs. J. J. Daynes, Jr., on
Fifth East Street. About a hundred ladies
were present to honor the occasion. The
rooms were very beautifully decorated with
choice flowers, vines and palms, and the
most exquisite music, vocal and instru-
mental, was rendered. Several of the ladies
present made brief remarks, expressing the
high appreciation of the work Sister Wood-
ruff had performed, both in the Salt Lake
Temple and the Relief Society, and wishing
her many happy returns of her birthday,
which occurs on March 1st, the same day as
that of her distinguished husband. Many
gifts and tokens of love and esteem were
presented. During the afternoon each lady
was asked to write a couplet or verse and
read to the company, and a prize of a very
pretty book was given to the winner. This


made a little diversion. Sister Sarah Mc-
Donald won the book. A number of old-
fashioned songs were sung by some of the
company. Delicious refreshments were
served by the daughters and grand-daughters
of the honored guest of the occasion.
