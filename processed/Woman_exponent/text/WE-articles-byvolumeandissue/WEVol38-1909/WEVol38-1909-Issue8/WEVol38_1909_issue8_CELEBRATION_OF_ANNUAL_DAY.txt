CELEBRATION OF ANNUAL DAY.
Editor Exponent:
	The Relief Society of the St. Joseph
Stake celebrated Annual Day, Masch 17, at
Thatcher, beginning at 11 a. m., in the
Amusement Hall, Pres. Elizabeth W. Lay-
ton, presiding. A program was rendered,
in which a speech of welcome and response,
vocal and instrumental music, and recita-
tions were given by members of the differ-
ent wards. These numbers all had a bear-
ing on the original progress and results of
this organization given to women sixty-
eight years ago, by the Prophet Joseph
Smith.
	At the close of the program a sumptuous
repast was partaken of by all the brethren
and sisters present, numbering about two
hundred.
	At 2.30 the Bazaar opened. Articles from
all the booths were in such demand that
they readily sold, and by 6 p. m., the bazaar
closed. An amusing program was rendered
at different periods during the afternoon.
	An entertainment by the Home Dramatic
Company was given in the evening, for the
benefit of the Society, and the house filled
to its utmost capacity. The day and eve-


ning were a decided success both socially 
and financially and will long be remem-
bered by the good sisters of this stake. 
	JOSEPHINE KIMBALL, Secretary.
