IN MEMORIAM.
	In memory of our beloved sister, Annie R.
Stringham, who departed this life, October 26,
1909, at the age of 46 years.
	We, the members of the Relief Society are
bereft of our Beloved and ever faithful co-worker.
Our intimate association with her in the perform-
ance of her duties in the ward, has so endeared
her to us that we feel it befitting to express our
appreciation of her labors in the Relief Society.
Sunday School, and Primary Association. She
has been a stay and strength to the people with
whom she mingled. A guardian angel among the
sick and afflicted, a prudent, conscientious and
careful counselor to those who were in distress and
needed counsel.
	She was an exemplar of ihe ideals we hold best
in our lives. A genuine, devoted wife. A loving,
earnest and thoughtful mother. Her life work
has been the uplifting and betterment or her kind.
Few can be found who have so faithfully fulfilled
the duties and responsibilities that have rested on
her. Be it
	Resolved, that we extend our heartfelt sym-
pathy to her husband and children, and ask the
Lord to bless and comfort them, and cause them
to know he is their friend.
	Resolved, that we send a copy to the Woman’s
Exponent and give a copy to the family.
			ELIZABETH GLEDHILL,
			LILLY BELLE GLEDHILL,
			EMMA POFF,
				Committee on Resolutions!
