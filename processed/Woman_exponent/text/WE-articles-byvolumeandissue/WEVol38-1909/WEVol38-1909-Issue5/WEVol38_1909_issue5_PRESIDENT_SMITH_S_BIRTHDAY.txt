PRESIDENT SMITH’S BIRTHDAY.
	The birthday anniversary of President
Joseph F. Smith, which occurred on Nov.
13, 1909, was fittingly celebrated by a family
gathering including beside his immediate
family, his elder brother John Smith, the
Patriarch, and his sister Martha Harris of
Provo, also his Counselors President John
R. Winder and wife, and President Anthon
H. Lund and wife, and general President
of the Refief Society, his Aunt, Bathsheba
W. Smith, the most honored woman in the
Church. The party was given at the Bee
Hive House, the President’s residence, and
the banquet was served in the famous Lion
House, with due ceremony befitting such a
happy occasion. Afterwards the younger
children made merry in dancing, games and
other childish pastimes.
	Letters and messages of congratulation
also many beautiful gifts were received,
testifying to the high honor in which he is
held by his friends and the people generally.
	The EXPONENT joins with his other near
and dear friends in offering sincere congrat-
ulations, and desiring for him many happy
returns of the day, without a shadow of
change in his appearance, and that the pros-
perity which he so richly deserves may be
showered upon him in abundance, and that
he may ever enjoy the loyalty and fidelity of
the people over whom he has been called
and chosen to preside. May our Father in
heaven protect him from his foes and en-
circle him in the arms of his tender, loving
care, that he may be a light to the world
that will ever shine pure and bright.
