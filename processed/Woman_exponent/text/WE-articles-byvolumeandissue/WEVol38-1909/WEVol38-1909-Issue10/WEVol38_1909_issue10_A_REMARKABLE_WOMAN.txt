A REMARKABLE WOMAN.
	MARGARET F. C. MORRISON.
			(Concluded.)
	Brother and Sister Morrison started on
their journey for Utah in the spring of
1856, via Omaha from St. Louis.  They
were in camp, living in tents, at Omaha six
weeks waiting for the company to get ready.
On the 26th of June the company of sixty
wagons started on the journey to cross the 
plains, Captain Canute Peterson in charge.
Sister Morrison testifies that he was a wise
and most efficient leader.  During the jour-
ney their cattle stampeded five times. A
young man from Denmark was accidentally
run over and instantly killed.  It was the 
only accident upon the journey.
