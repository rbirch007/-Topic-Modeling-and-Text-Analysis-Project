NOTES AND NEWS.
	Miss Helen Taft, daughter of President
Taft, has returned to Bryn Mawr and
entered upon her sophomore year.
	Miss Ida Lewis, keeper of Lime Rock
Lighthouse, and known as the Grace Darl-
ing of America, received Christmas gifts
from all over the country.
	Lady Frances Balfour and Mrs. H. J.
Tennant have been placed on the Royal
Commission appointed by King Edward to
inquire into the law of divorce and its ad-
ministration, especially with regard to the
poorer classes. Lady Frances Balfour is a
sister of the Duke of Argyll and greatly in-
terested in the woman suffrage movement.
She married Eustace J. A. Balfour. Mrs.
Tennant is the sister-in-law of the Prime
Minister, Mr. Asquith. It is curious that
one of the women Royal Commissioners
should be sister-in-law to the leader of the
Opposition, and the other sister-in-law to
the head of the government.—Ex.
