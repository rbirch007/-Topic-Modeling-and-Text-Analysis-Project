LIBERTY STAKE.
	Semi-annual Conference of Liberty Stake
Relief Society was held in the 9th ward
assembly hall, December 9th, at ten a. m.,
and 2 p. m., President Annie S. Musser pre-
siding.
	We had visiting us, Elder C. W. Symons
and Milando Pratt of the High Council of
Liberty Stake, and Sister Sarah J. Cannon
of the General Board.
	The soloists of the morning session were
Miss Kingsbury and Mrs. Eva M. James.
	After the opening exercises and greetings
by President Musser, reports were made by
the 9th, 10th. 31st, 33rd, Liberty and Immi-
gration wards; the organizations were reported
in a flourishing condition, and all the workers
in the line of their duty.
	Sister Cannon encouraged us to store
wheat, invoked the sisters to cultivate a spir-
it of cheerfulness when making their visits,
and remember the poor and needy.
	Elder C. W. Symons felt to endorse the
work of the society, encouraged the sisters
to continue in the good work, and not be-
come weary of well doing.
	After the closing exercises, adjourned un-
til 2 o’clock.
	Conference reconvened at 2 o’clock, and
after opening exercises, Doctor M. H. Can-
non delivered a lecture on “Heredity and
Environment,” which was instructive and
very much enjoyed.
	Sister Eva James favored us with a
solo.
	Elder Milando Pratt said that the words
of the prophets were being fulfilled and felt
that this society was performing a grand and
noble work.
	Elder C. W. Symons knew that we were
organized by inspiration, also knew that the
Lord was with us and would bless us.
	Sister Sarah J. Cannon said, let us per-
form our work in the right way, and ever
be ready to do our duty.
	President Musser made a few closing re-
marks, and after the closing exercises, con-
ference adjourned for six months.
