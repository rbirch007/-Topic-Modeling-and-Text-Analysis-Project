EDITORIAL NOTES.
	The illness of President John R. Winder
has kept the people in suspense for some
time past, yet the Saints have been hopeful
of his recovery and exercising their utmost
faith in his behalf, for the people love him
devotedly, and now that there is almost a
surety of his soon being well enough to re-
sume his duties, and his place in the Salt
Lake Temple, where he has so faithfully
officiated for so many years, the Saints
are rejoicing at the prospect of seeing his
cheerful countenance again. President
Winder is very dear to the hearts of this
people, and there will be a warm welcome
everywhere for him when he can come
among the people. It seems very apparent
that the Lord has yet a great work for him
to do here upon earth; his life altogether has
been a most remarkable one. rich in good
works and full of prophetic significance.
	The visit of Bishop David A. Smith to
the Hawaiian Islands, whither he went so
soon after his recent illness that the people
interested in him scarcely knew he was out,
has been a very pleasant trip, both on sea and
on land. His object in going was to bring
home his brother Wesley, who had been fill-
ing a mission the last two years and more,
and had been seriously ill of late with ty-
phoid fever; and it was thought advisable to
bring him home. The mother, Sister Jul-
ina L. Smith, accompanied her son David,
and as she is familiar with the language,
she will be sure to help the Relief Society
over there, and at any rate give them advice
and encouragement, although we are aware
they are really not much behind us at home,
even if they do labor under great disadvant-
ages. The home coming of Sister Julina
Smith and her two sons will be a joyful
event at the Bee Hive House, and no doubt
they have enjoyed their little respite from
home cares and business.
