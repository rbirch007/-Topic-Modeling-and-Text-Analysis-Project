REORGANIZATION.
	The 25th Ward Relief Society of Pioneer,
Stake was reorganized on Tuesday, Feb. 1r
1910, Catherine White presiding. After the
usual opening exercises Bishop Carl A. Ek,
was present, also Stake President of the Re-
lief Society Annie Wells Cannon and her
Board, Clara Clawson, Louie Giauque and
Amelia Cutler, were there for the purpose of
reorganizing the Relief Society. Catherine
White, former president, and Flora P. Niel-
son, First Counselor, were honorabl released,
having been faithful workers since the 25th
Ward was organized, eight years ago.
	Sister Edna Sullivan was chosen presi-
dent and Sister Julia Robinson, formerly
Second Counselor, was chosen for First
Counselor, and Sister Eliza Lockyear was
chosen for Second Counselor; Sister Emma
Andrus was retained as Secretary, and Sis-
ter Susie Strubeck as Treasurer.
	Bishop Ek then spoke to the sisters for a
few moments, followed by President Annie
W. Cannon, Louie Giauque and Amelia
Cutler, all praising the work of the retiring
officers. The new officers were then set
apart by Bishop Ek, and a rising vote of
thanks to the retiring officers was called for,
which was unanimously responded to.
	Sister White then spoke a few moments,
expressing her good will to the sisters, and
said her heart was in the work and her de-
sire was to see the Relief Society grow
and prosper.
	The new officers each spoke a few mo-
ments, after which the meeting was ad-
journed by singing and benediction by
Bishop Carl A. Ek.
