NOBLE WOMAN SUMMONED.
	Sister Julia A Bingham, president of the Gar-
land. Relief Society, wife of Sanford Bingham,
Jr., and one of the most highly respected and in-
fluential women of this community, passed peace-
fully away, Saturday, September 18, 1909, the eve 
of her wedding day, having been married just
forty-one years. The cause of her death was can-
cer of the stomach.
	Impressive services were held at the Garland
Hall, Tuesday, September 21, at 2:30 p.m. A
large crowd was in attendance. The Relief Soci-
ety Stake Boards of Box Elder and Bear River
Stakes, also representatives from the Relief
Society of six wards and a host of relatives of
deceased being present.
	Numerous beautiful wreaths and bouquets of
flowers were in evidence, and the stage curtain
was draped in white, with the words
			“At Rest,”
tastefully arranged in twigs and green boughs. 
The large, handsome casket was literally covered
with floral offerings.
	Bishop A. R. Capener presided. Singing by the
ward choir, “Resting, now, from care and sor-
row.”
	Prayer by Thomas E. King.
	Solo. “When we Meet on that Beautiful Shore,”
Mrs. Maggie Wixom.
	The following speakers spoke words of comfort
to the bereaved family, and paid a high tribute to
the character and exemplary life of the de-
ceased: Susana P. Booth, Louise Peters, and Emilia
D. Madsen, Presidency of the Box Elder Stake
Relief Society; Julia A. Richards and Esther
Evans of the Bear River Stake Relief Society,
Celia M. Grover, Margaret W. Manning, Laura S.
Rice, W. L. Grover, Joseph Jensen, D. E. Man-
ning and Bishop A. R. Capener.
