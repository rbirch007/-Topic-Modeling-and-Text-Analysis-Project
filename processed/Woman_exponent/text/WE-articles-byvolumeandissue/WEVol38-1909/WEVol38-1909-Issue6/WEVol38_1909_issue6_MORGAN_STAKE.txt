MORGAN STAKE.
	The Morgan Stake conference was held
December 11, 1909, in the vestry of the
meeting house; it convened at 10 o’clock
a. m. There were in attendance from the
General Board Sisters Susan Grant and
E.S. Wilcox. President Daniel Heiner
and several other leading brethren were in
attendance. Reports were given of the Re-
lief Society work in the Stake by several
presidents. They reported the work about
as usual. There are some active members
but there are many who are not taking any
part, making our attendance smaller than
it should be. There were some encouraging
remarks from the sisters of the General
Board. After singing and benediction meet-
ing adjourned till 2 p. m.
	At 2 p. m. the conference assembled in
the main building with a fairly good attend-
ance. After singing, prayer and sacra-
mental service a talk was given by Malissie
Heiner, subject, “Judge Not.” Some fine
ideas were given.
	Sister Susan Grant is one of the few who
was associated with the Prophet Joseph
Smith. She bore a strong testimony to the
divinity of his mission.
	Sister Wilcox referred to the beauties in


nature; how true she is to all the details of
her laws. We have a good lesson in the
fidelity of nature to her laws.
	President Daniel Heiner made a few re-
marks, had been interested in our confer-
ence, and he desires to assist us in good
works.
	We have fine sleighing and as a rule the
people are able to enjoy it.
			ANNIE W. CLARK.
				R. S. Stake Secretary.
