	Every reader of the WOMAN’S EXPONENT
knows Sister Richards as a pioneer of Relief
Society work and will join with us in wish-
ing for her as many birthdays as she may
wish to see. She was a member of the as-
sociation in Nauvoo and from the early days
of Utah has stood in responsible positions,
bringing her life work before the people,
who well, know of her sterling worth, her un-

[Column 2]
deviating, unselfish integrity to the cause of
Zion and who love her for what she has done.
For 31 years (from 1877 to 1908) she was
Stake President of the Relief Society of the
Weber Stake, being honorably released as
acting, but sustained as honorary president
on the division of that stake. President
Brigham Young had great faith in Sister
Jane, as he was wont to call her, and many
times talked over Relief Society work with
her, in fact he took her with him on several
occasions when traveling through the coun-
try and organizing the Relief Society in new
wards and places, realizing in her a master
mind.
	She was First Counselor to Aunt Zina D.
H. Young, the General President of the Re-
lief Society, and has traveled not only
through the Stakes of Zion, but several
times visited the east on important business
pertaining to the welfare of the people she
loved so well and among whom she had cast
her lot. Since Aunt Zina’s death she has
been senior member of the General Board,
where she is loved, honored and respected by
every member of that noble body of workers.
Nellie B., Ogden, Utah.
