A GENUINE SURPRISE.
	A party of Relief Society sisters of the
Pioneer Stake, about forty in number, des-
cended upon the Cannon home, at Cannon
farm, the first Monday in December, with
the best intentions and the most delicious
picnic in baskets and boxes, all neatly
wrapped in tissue paper, and a good supply
of lovely flowers for decorating. Unknown
to Sister Annie W. Cannon, President of
the Relief Society of that Stake of Zion,
who was at the time busily engaged in her
household affairs, they entered without
ceremony, but with glad hands and smiling
faces, wishing her many happy returns of
the day.
	Sister Cannon was almost too bewildered
to appreciate the situation at first, but
there was no alternative other than to submit
gracefully to so delightful a compliment.
	As an expression of their love and esteem
the sisters presented their President with an


exquisite cameo pin and a handsome leather
satchel.
	Sister Claire Little Clawson, First Counsel-
or in the Relief Society of that Stake made
the presentation, in a few choice words ex-
pressing the good feelings of the sisters who
are associated with Sister Cannon in the
Stake and ward work.
	Sister Cannon responded in a short
speech of gratitude and appreciation, after
which refreshments, dainty and substantial,
were served, and a most delightful after-
noon was spent by those present. The sis-
ters who participated in the surprise were
the officers of the Relief Society of Pioneer
Stake organization and of the several wards
included therein.
	Sister Cannon has presided over the Relief
Society in that Stake ever since it was
organized.
