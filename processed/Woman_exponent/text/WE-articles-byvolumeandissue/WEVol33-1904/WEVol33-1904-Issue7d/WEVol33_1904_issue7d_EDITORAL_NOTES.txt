EDITORAL NOTES:
	The anniversary of the birthday of Sister
Eliza R Snow was fittingly commemorated
by the Daughters of t[-]e Pioneers at their
regular meeting in January, at the residence
of Brother and Sister W B. Dougall. Re-
marks were made by the president, Alice
Merrill Horne, who gave her childish recol-
lections and interesting reminiscences were
given by President Bathsheba W. Smith, a
life-long companion, and by Sister Margar-
et P. Young, Phebe Y. Beatie, Edna E.
Smith and the writer.
	Susan B. Anthony’s birthday on the 15th
of February of this year will be a sort of
gala-day throughout the United States,
Societies and clubs have decided to celebrate;
and elaborate preparations are being made
to do honor to this champion of woman’s 
political equality and civil rights. Miss
Anthony eminently deserves the loyalty of
women for the stand she has taken and
maintained; she has had much adverse cri-
tism, but she has risen superior to it all, and
is today the centre of attraction in any com-
pany in America or Europe.
