		PANGUITCH STAKE.
			LESSON VII.
			Childhood,
	“Happiness is the only food on which the child
can be fed with profit.”
	(a) Physical Training. 1. Food—Kinds
—Why? 2. Clothing—Uniformity.—Why?
3. Exercise—Regularity in Work—Play.
4. Sleep—Amount—Regularity—Why? 5.
Bathing.
	(b) Mental Training. 1. Obedience—To
God, to authority of Church and State. 2.
Honesty—In thought and action. How
taught. 3. Cleanliness of thought, of ac-
tion in language. 4. Choice of Companions
—Who shall choose—parents or children.
Why?
	(c) Spiritual Training: 1. Reverence—
persons, places, things. 2. Responsibity of
parents, see Doc. and Cov. Sec. 68.
			LESSON VIII.
			Boyhood.
	(a) Character Building: 1. Teach him
his responsibility in forming his own char-
acter and his preparation for manhood and
that the body is the temple of the soul. 2.
Good Habits—Cleanliness of his person and
dress—Work—Amusements—Choice of oc-
cupation — Language. 3. Bad Habits—
How avoided?—Use of Intoxicants—Stimu-
lants—Idleness—Sec. 68—Doc. and Cov.—
Rivalry—Language.
	(b) Domestic Relations: 1. His Rights,
To expect parents confidence. To share
the best—To be equal with his sisters—To

[Column 2]
have a place for and a right to care for his
personal property—To expect a reasonable
amount of spending money—2. His Privi-
leges—To entertain his companions occas-
ionally —3. To express his ideas and con-
victions—To pursue his studies without in-
terruption.
			LESSON IX.
			Girlhood.
	“O, thou child of many prayers
	Life hath quicksands, life hath snares,
	Age and care come unawares.”
	(a) Character Building: [-]. Teach the
girl her divine origin. 2. Her capabilities
and her mission.
			LESSON X.
			Etiquette.
	As charity covers a multitude of sins before
God, so does politeness before men.”
	(a) Home: 1. Always remember the
small sweet courtesies—2. Don’t reserve
your kindest words and sweetest smiles for
strangers—3. Table etiquette—4, Morning
and evening greetings—5. Conversational
manners.
	(b) Church: 1. Be orderly in entering
—2. Avoid conversation during services—
3. Remain until after dismissal—4, Obey
all the rules—5. Avoid loud laughter or
conversation in building or on grounds.
	(c) Street: 1. Greetings—2. How to pass
—3. Introductions—4. Conversations—5.
Dress—6- Lady and her escort.
	(d) The Ball Room; Y. W. Journal 1902.
			LESSON II.
			Chastity.
	So dear to heaven is saintly chastity, that when
a soul is found sincerely so, a thousand liveried
angels lackey her.
	(a) Chastity: (1) What is it? (b) Neces-
sity of example—of parents. 1. Selection
of associates. (c) What to teach our boys
and girls: 1. That thought and action in-
fluence the body—2. Vulgar conversation
one of the chief causes of vices that ruin
body and soul. 3. Effect of unchastity on
the physical, mental and moral being. (4)
Unchastity contrary to laws of God and of
man.
			LESSON XII.
			Marriage.
	And again I say unto you that whoso forbid.
deth to marry is not ordained of God unto man-
Doc. and Cov. Sec. 49.
	(a) Preparation for marriage: 1. Ability
to make and care for a home. (2.) True
love—3. Courtship—how conducted—Par-
ents, responsibility. (4) Assurance through
prayer that the union would be approved
by God. (5) Worthy to be married prop-
erly. (b) Kinds: 1. For time. 2. For
Eternity—Compare.
	(c) Object of Marriage: 1. Compliance
with divine commandments. 2. Propaga-
tion of the human family. 3. Companion-
ship.
