BETSY ROSS AND THE FLAG.	
	BY SUSA YOUNG GATES.
’Twas a woman that made the lovely flag—
	A woman with soul divine,
The man may have thought, but the woman
		wrought,
	The cover for liberty’s shrine.
The sun had risen, and the sun had set,
	On our flag with the English cross;
When the thirteen bars and the thirteen stars,
	Were fashioned by Betsy Ross.
For the dragon and cross surmounted a crown,
	And freedom was but a story,
When the breeze unfurled to a saddened world
	The flag with its dear-bought glory.
On the high seas first, the flag flashed out
	Its welcome challenge for peace.
Paul Jones sailed faster than England’s dis-
		aster,
	To win America’s release.
Then out from each fortress and seaport town,
	There gleamed the flag with its stars.
The mountains and vales, the hills and dales
	Were bright with its crimson bars.
O, red was the blood that was shed for you,
	But white was the pure desire,
And in heaven’s own hue, the stars shone
		through,
	And kindled sweet liberty’s fire.
So, up with the splendid stars and stripes,
	There’s a gain for every loss.
Honor the men who fought, and the women
		who wrought
	And honor brave Betsy Ross.
