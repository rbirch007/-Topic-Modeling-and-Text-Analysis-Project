	Delicious refreshments were served to all
guests by daughters and granddaughters
of the veteran bridegroom, who had, not
long since reached his fourscore years. The 
remainder of the evening was pleasantly
spent in social chat and conversation.
Among the distinguished guests was Mrs.
Bathsheba W. Smith, General President of
the Relief Society, who had known Brother
S. W. Richards from his coming to Nanvoo
with his father's family in 1842. His
brother, Elder Henry P. Richards and wife,
and his sister, Maria W. Wilcox were pres-
ent to participate in the honor shown their
brother.
	Of the original Richards family who
came from the Berkshire hills in Massachu-
setts to Nanvoo, these three are
 the only
remaining ones. Their father, Phineas
and his wife Wealthy Dewey Richards,
passed away some years ago, also Apostle
Willard Richards, uncle of Samuel, who
became one of the First Presidency after the
martyrdom of the Prophet Joseph, and
died in 1856. A sister Rhoda Richards
and brother Levi passed away here.
Apostle Franklin D. Richards was of the
same family who came to Nauvoo in 1842.
These have all a numerous posterity in
Zion, except Aunt Rhoda Richards, who
never married, although she lived to be
over ninety, and was a remarkable woman
in many ways. The Richards family is
counted among the famous families in
Church history, along with the Smiths, the 
Youngs, and the Kimballs.
