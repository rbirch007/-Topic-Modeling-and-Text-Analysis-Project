RELIEF SOCIETY ORGANIZED.
RELIEF SOCIETY OF LEIPZIG AND REPORT
		OF ITS ORGANIZATION.
	By invitation of Chas. J. Thomas, presi-
ding: Elder of Leipzig Branch, the sisters
met in the L. D. S. hall, no. 2 Universitat
Str. on Sunday, August 28, 1904, to con-
sider the subject of the organization of a Re-
lief Society. After singing and prayer,
Elder Thomas moved and Pres. Benson
seconded the motion that Sister Alder be
temporary chairman. Brother Franz Schulze
was elected secretary. Sister Alder stated
the object of the meeting to be the advisa-
bility of organizing a Relief Society. It was
moved by Elder Geo. A. Alder to have a
Relief Society. Elder Thomas seconded the
motion. It was unanimously voted that an
organization be effected. Elder Thomas
moved that Sister Selma Gunther be presi-
dent. Elder Alder seconded the motion,
As first counselor Sister Maria Kretschmor
was elected and as second counselor Sister
Ida Loffler was elected. Emma Homberger
was elected secretary and Lydia Piusch
treasurer. Frederieke Poitz, Marie Hiller,
Louie Wolf and Auguste Low were chosen
teachers. These sisters were all set apart
for their various offices, by the following
brethren, Alder, Benson, Schulze, and
Thomas.
	President Gunther took the chair by re-
quest of Elder Thomas, Sister Alder gave
them instructions in regard to their duties
as officers and members and spoke of the
first organization in Nauvoo. Sister Gun-
ther called an officers’ meeting the following
evening at the house of Sister Hausdorf for
the purpose of further instructions. On
motion, meeting was adjourned to time and
place specified. Benediction was offered by
Brother Friedrich Gunther;
