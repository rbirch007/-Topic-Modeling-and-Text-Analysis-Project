IN MEMORIAM.
	Resolutions of respect to the memory of Sister
Emma Welch who departed this life Jan. 24, 1905.
	Whereas the Lord in his Providence has seen fit
to call home one of his most earnest workers leav-
ing to us the dear memory of a sister and a friend:
Therefore be it
	Resolved that we the members of the Relief
Society of the Cowley ward offer our earnest sym-
pathy and mourn with the husband, mother and
children. We appreciate the help she was in our
ward as organist, and to our children as teacher
and leader in their amusements, and an aid to the
Young Ladies as well as President of the Relief
Society, and we esteem her faithful labors as
worthy of emulation by all. To know her was to
love her.
A living example of a true Latter-day Saint, she


always had a prayer in her heart and a kind word
on her lips.
	She was greatly loved by all her many friends
who admired her sterling qualities; but we know
if we are faithful we shall meet her again in that
beautiful home where sin and death cannot enter.
	We ask the blessings of the Lord on all those be-
reaved ones, may He comfort their hearts in this
hour of their separation.
	Be it resolved that we send a copy of these Res-
olutions to her mother, the EXPONENT and that a
copy be spread upon the minutes of the Cowley
Relief Society.
						Committee,
						SARAH M. CROSBY,
						ALVILEA DICKSON,
						CLAUDIA KETCHUM,
						SARAH PARTRIDGE,
