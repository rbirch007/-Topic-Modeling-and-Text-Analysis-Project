MOTHERS’ WORK.
		SUMMIT STAKE.
Lec, 1st. Woman, Her Mission:
	(a) How to attain the highest type of
womanhood.
	(b) Learn the will of God concerning
them.
	(c) Preparation for further usefulness.
	(d) Laws of health, how observed.
	(e) Word of wisdom a fundamental prin-
ciple.
	(f) Full development of physical and
mental powers.
	(g) Tell of special ambition of women of
ancient Israel.
	(h) How do women of latter-day Israel
compare with those of former-day Israel in
fulfilling the most important function of
woman’s life?
Lec. 2nd.— Wifehood.
	(a) Woman’s duty to husband and home.
	(b) Love of God, desire to fulfill His
commandments.
	(c) How to be companionable.
	(d) Use influence for good, religiously
and intellectually.
	(e) What greater privileges do women of
today enjoy than women of ancient days
enjoyed?
	(f) God’s commandment to Adam and
Eve. (Gen 1: 28-36.)
Lec. 3rd.—Parents:
	(a) Highest calling of man and woman.
	(b) Responsible to God for their ministry.
(Ref. Maeser's “School and Fireside.”)
	(c) Child’s best heritage, honorable birth.
	(d) Purposes bent to prepare heart and
mind for embryonic moulding.
	(e) Cultivation of spirituality.
	(f) Prenatal influence: example, child of
faith.
	(g) Beginning and duration of parent’s
responsibility.
Lec. 4th.—Infancy.
	(a) The child’s welcome as a heaven-sent
gift.
	(b) Cleanliness a necessity.
	(c) Fresh air and warmth essential to
well-being.
