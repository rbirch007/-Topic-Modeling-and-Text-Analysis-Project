Lecture IV.— What to tell our Girls.
	It is one thing to possess knowledge and
quite another thing to impart it to others.
We should teach our girls to read good books
and choose good companions, warn her
against vulgar stories she may hear; ask
her to tell you everything her playmates may
tell her, then you will have a chance to cor-
rect errors and warn her against impure
teachings and harmful associates, as well as
lay a foundation for future confidence and
close companionship. They should be
taught to keep both mind and body clean
and pure. Teach them to be good home
makers in the true sense of the word, and
