WOODRUFF STAKE.

Lecture VII:—Infancy.
	a. Tell the influence a mother has upon
the Infant through the nursing period.
Dressing and care. Warn against drugs
and patent medicines. b. Necessity of
Kindergarten training in the home. Moth-
er should avoid unnecessary baby-talk, use
plain, simple language.

[Column 2]

Lecture VIII.—Childhood.
	“Train up a child in the way he should
go, and when he is old he will not depart
from it.”
	a. The key to success is love, wisdom,
faith and obedience. b. The growth of a
child physically, spiritually, and moral[--]
intellectually.

Lecture IX.—Childhood (continued).
	a. Teach the necessity of being com[-]an-
ions to our children, and joining the[-] in
their amusements, at home and other
places b. Teach that “too much indulgence
has spoilt many a child, too much love, not
one.”

Lecture X.—Home Training.
a. Teach the power of prayer, forgiveness
and charity for each others’ imperfections,
b. Honesty, faithfulness and purity.
Lecture XI.—Reverence.
	a. Reverence for the Priesthood and re-
spect for the aged. b. Study and teach the
fifth commandment.

Lecture XII.—Duties.
	a. Benefit derived from having our chil-
dren attend the juvenile organizations. b.
Reverence for houses of worship and good
behavior therein.
