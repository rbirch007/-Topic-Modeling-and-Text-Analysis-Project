BEAR LAKE STAKE.

Lec, 1st. Home, Family Organization.
	(a) The father, his authority, his position
and rights. (b) The mother, her position
and rights. (c) The children, their posi-
tion and rights.

Lec. 2nd. Prayer.
	(a) Its power and influence. (b) Belief
and faith through the same. (c) When
shall children take part in family prayer?

Lec. 3rd. Physical Development.
	(a) Methods of proper bathing. (b)
Building a healthy body. (c) Proper food
and sleep.

Lec. 4th.—Moral Development.
	(a) Reverence for Deity. (b) Reverence
for places of worship. (c) Respect for pub-
lic property.

Lec. 5th. Domestic Economy.
	(a) What is it? (b) How may it be ap-
plied in the home? (c) Distinguish economy .
from stingness.

Lec. 6th. Marriage.
	(a) Love and courtship. (b) Continuality.
(c) The ability to make a home and care
for the home. (d) Marry one of your own
faith.

Lec. 7th. Social Purity.
	(a) How shall we create a higher moral
standing? (b) The aim of parents should
be to arrange and control the environments
of the young people, cure their faults, culti-

vate their talents and develop their virtues.

Lec. 8th. Sacredness of honor.
	(a) What does it mean? (b) How to get
the children’s confidence. (c) How to up-
root evil by planting good.

Lec. 6th. Dress and habits.
	(a) Dress for comfort and health. (b)
How habits are formed? (c) Man a bundle
of habits.

Lec. 10th. Obedience.
	(a) Parents should set the example by
being obedient to their Heavenly Father.
(b) Children have rights which are often
called, “disobedience.” (c) All are not
gifted in the same line. (d) Do not force
children to learn something they have no
inclination or talent for.

Lec. 11th. What does the Mother read.
	(a) Mothers lack of knowledge. (b)
Mothers reading cheap novels. (c) Mother
as teacher to her children.

Lec. 12. Books as Companion and Teacher.
	(a) Minds are made up largely of what is
put into them. (b) What books should our
children read? (c) The influence of bad
literature.
	NANCY PUGMIRE, MARIA L. BROWN, A.
