		NEBO STAKE, 1905.

Lesson I.— Youth.
	“As the twig is bent the tree inclines.”
a. Honor thy father and thy mother. Exod.
10:12. b. In our youth the corner stone of
our lives is laid. c. How does prayer effect
the young? Doc Cov. sec. 68 v. 68. d.
Environments, habits, prayer, truthfulness.
Doc. Cov. sec. 93: 23 to 30. e. Respect. f.
Rights of others, g. Amusements for the
children.

Lesson II. Honesty.
	“Thou shalt not steal.” a. Be honest
with the Lord Doc Cov. sec. 119. Have an
honest heart. Luke 8:15; Cor 8:2; Rom.
12:17. How to teach children to be honest.
b. What does dishonesty lead to? To be
honest is a good foundation to build upon.
c. Should we tell a child he is dishonest? d.
Honest courage is worth more than wealth.

Lesson III.—Companionship, Fellowship, As-
sociation.
	a. Parents should find proper associates
for their children. b. Teach them the nec-
essity of keeping good company. “My son
if sinners entice thee, consent thou not.”
Prov. 1:10. “Forget not my law, but let
thine heart keep my commandments. Prov.
3:1. c. Should children be out after dark?

Lesson IV. Moral Purity.
	a. To be morally pure one must store one’s
mind with happy and pure thoughts, with
pleasant memories of the past and reason-
able hopes for the future. b. Guard
against impure language among the youth.
Avoid obscene pictures. c. Is there a lack of
confidence between parents and children? d.
Don’t let children be away hours at a time
and not know what they are doing. c.
Every child should be taught the laws of
personal purity. f. Do good manners lead to
good morals? Purity is better than ele-
gance.

Lesson V.—Home Influence.
	a. At home children learn their first and
lasting impressions. b. Our home should
be as pleasant as it can be made. Pleasures
and amusements. c. We cannot doubt, our
mothers knew it. Book of Mormon, Alma
56: 47, 48. d. Home influence, moral, so-
cial, religious. e. Teach the neeessity of
prayer and keepi[-]g the word of wisdom
and train in all the principles of the Gospel.

Lesson VI.—Obedience.
	Obedience is heaven’s first law. a. Obed-
ence produces confidence. Journal Dis.,
vol. 4, page 247. Obedience to God. to
parents and teachers. b. Take the greatest
care that our children obey while they are

Lesson VII.— Character.
	“Train up a child in the way he should
go, and when he is old he will not depart
from it.”—Solomon. a. Character is a struc-
ture we are all building. Honesty, faithful-
ness, sincerity and purity are the corner-
stones. Our minds are given us, but our
characters we make. b. Are parents re-
sponsible for the character of their children?
Youth is the springtime of character. Ref.
Exponent, Nov. 1904.

Lesson VII.—Charity.
	a. Charity for each others’ faults. Thou
shalt not bear false witness against thy
neighbor Exod. 20: 16. b. Do to others
as you would that they should do to you.
c. Love to our neighbors. Mark 28 to 30. 
d. Alms-giving. Prov. 16-17.

Lesson IX.—Rights.
	a. The rights of family members. “Honor
thy father and thy mother.” b. Provoke
not your children to wrath. Ephes. 6: 1-4.
b. As much as parents have children. Doc.
Cov., sec. 68-45, 28 v. Doc. Cov. sec. 88-4.
d: Obligations of children to parents. Juv.
Inst., vol. 4, number 2.

c. Is singing hymns and reading the Bible
considered part of the family worship? d.
Does daily family prayer have any influence

Lesson XI.—Courtship.
	a. Its aim and effect. b. To better un-
derstand each others’ characteristics and dis-
positions. c. Teach the boys and girls that
the body is the temple of the spirit and they
must learn to keep it pure. d. How shall
they learn? Doc. Cov. 88-124-120. e. Teach
the necessity of exercise, cleanliness and
care of the body. Doc. Cov 88-124-121-123-
125. f. If fathers neglect to teach their
sons the important lessons of life the moth-
ers must. g. When shall our girls marry?
h. When is physical development complete?

Lesson XII—Marriage.
Marriage ordained of God. Doc. Cov. 49-
15 16-17. a. Necessity of marriage by the
new and everlasting covenant. Doc. Cov.
132-19. Should we obey the command?
Be fruitful and multiply. Gen. 1-27. b.
What is the object of marriage? Earthly
covenant of marriage not in force after
death. Doc. Cov. 123-15-16. d. Whom to
marry. “Be ye not unequally yoked with
unbelievers.”—Paul. e. Why should we
avoid intemperate companions?
