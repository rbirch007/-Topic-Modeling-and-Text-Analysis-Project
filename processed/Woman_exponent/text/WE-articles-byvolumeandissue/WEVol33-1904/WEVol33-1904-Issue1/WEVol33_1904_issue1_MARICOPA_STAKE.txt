MARICOPA STAKE.

	The R. S. conference of MaricopaStake was
held at Mesa, June 2, 1904, President Mabel
A. Hakes presiding. After the usual open-
ing exercises, President Hakes addressed
the sisters, giving them a hearty welcome 
and then called for reports from the several
wards in the stake. Three out of five were
doing fairly well, two had been discon-
tinued on account of deaths, and one the
president had moved away. Considerable
wheat had been stored, average attendance
was good, and the teachers as a rule were
active in their duties. They have a lecture
from the Doctrine and Covenants each tes-
timony meeting, and had begun to see the
good results of the same.
	All the stake had not been visited this
time on account of sickness.
	President Colins R. Hakes was pleased
to hear of the good that had been done to
so many poor and sick and those in need of
encouragement. Bishop J. M. Horne spoke
of care of the mind and the body in humili-
ty for the work to be performed. Several
sisters spoke on missionary work, Mother’s
Work and influence in the home circle.
	Afternoon session was better attended,
and after the usual exercises, the Spirit of
the Lord was made manifest, and many en-
couraging words were spoken. Primary
work, punctuality, the effect of children’s
prayers, Relief Society work a labor of
love, and when all are united much can be
accomplished. The Lamanite mission not
reported, was greatly regretted as that is
truly a labor of love. Conference adjourned
for three months, feeling we had truly a
spiritual feast.
