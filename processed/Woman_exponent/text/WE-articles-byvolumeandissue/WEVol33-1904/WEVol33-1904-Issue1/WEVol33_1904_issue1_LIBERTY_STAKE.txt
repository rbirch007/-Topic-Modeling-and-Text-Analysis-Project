LIBERTY STAKE.

	The first Relief Society conference of the
Liberty Stake convened June 4, 1904, at 2
p. m. in the Second ward meetinghouse,
President Annie S. Musser presiding.
meeting opened by singing “Our God we
raise to Thee,” prayer by President Eliza-
beth H. Paul of the Tenth ward. Singing
“Come, come ye Saints.”
	After a hearty welcome by President
Musser the presidents of the several wards

[Column 3]
in the stake gave verbal reports, showing
all to be in a flourishing condition.
	Counselor Mary E. Hodgson presented
the names of the general officers of the Re-
lief Society and of Liberty Stake; all were
unanimously sustained.
	Sister Maggie Hull rendered a solo
which was a pleasing feature of the meet-
ing.
	Counselor Zerviah Eardley gave some good
advice, thought it was God’s chosen daugh-
ters who worked in the Relief Society;
urged all to be humble and prayerful and
the Lord would bless them.
	Sister Louisiana Heppler, President of
the Primary Associations, Sevier Stake for
twenty-five years, spoke beautifully to the
sisters, felt the Spirit of the Lord was in
the meeting, encouraged the young moth-
ers to attend the Relief Society, thought
the Prophet Joseph blessed the sisters by
organizing this society. The speaker said
happiness comes from within and we could
make our homes, if only a cottage, the hap-
piest place on earth. Sister Maggie Hull
thought we were working in a good cause,
and we would receive great blessings. Sister
Emma Empey spoke at some length about
the Relief Society nurses class.
	President Musser was well pleased with the
conference and thanked all the sisters who
had helped to make it a success, adjourned
the conference for three months, after sing-
ing “O, say what is truth,” benediction by
Counselor Mary E. Hodgson.
LIZZIE ROBBINS, Asst. Sec’y.
