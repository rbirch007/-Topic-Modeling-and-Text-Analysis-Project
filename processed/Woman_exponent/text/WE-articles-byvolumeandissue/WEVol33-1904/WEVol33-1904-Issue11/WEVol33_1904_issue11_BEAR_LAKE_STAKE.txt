		BEAR LAKE STAKE.

Lecture I.—Home.
	Family Organization. (a) The father,
his authority, his position, his rights. (b)
The mother, her position and rights. (c)
The children, their position and rights.

Lecture II.—Prayer,
	(a) The power and influence. (b) Be-
lief and faith through the same. (c) When
shall children take part in family prayer?

Lecture III —Physical Development.
	(a) Methods of proper bathing. (b)
Building a healthy body. (c) Proper food
and sleep.

Lecture IV.— Moral Development.
	(a) Reverence for Deity. (b) Reverence
for places of worship. (c) Respect for pub-
lic property.

Lecture V.—Domestic Economy.
	(a) What is it? (b) How may it be ap-
plied in the home? (c) Distinguish econo-
my from stinginess.

Lecture VI.—Marriage.
	(a) Love and courtship. (b) Continu-
ality. c) The ability to make a home and

[Column 2]
care for the same. (d) Marry one of your
own faith.

Lecture VII.—Social Purity.
	(a) How shall we create a higher moral
standing? (b) The aim of parents should
be to arrange and control the environments
of the young people, cure their faults, culti-
vate their talents and develop their virtues.

Lecture VIII.— Sacredness of Honor
	(a) What does it mean? (b) How to get
the children’s confidence. (c) How to up-
root evil by planting good.

Lecture IX.—Dress and Habits,
	(a) Dress for comfort and health. (b)
How habits are formed. (c) Man a bundle
of habits.

Lecture X.—Obedience.
	(a) Parents should set the example by
being obedient to their Heavenly Father.
(b) Children have rights which are often
called “disobedience.” (c) All are not
gifted in the same line. (d) Do not force
children to learn something they have no
inclination or talent for.

Lecture XI.—What does the Mother read?
	(a) Mother’s lack of knowledge. (b)
Mothers reading cheap novels. (c) Mother
as teacher to her children.

Lecture XII.—Books as companion and
teacher.
	(a) Minds are made up largely of what
is put into them. (b) What books should
our children read? (c) The influence of
bad literature.
	Nancy Pugmire, Marie L. Brown, A. C.
Stucki, Committee.
