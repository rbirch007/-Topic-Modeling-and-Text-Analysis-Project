WOMAN'S VOICE.

Testimony of Lucy Diantha Morley
Allen, daughter of Isaac Morley and Lucy
Gunn Morley, born in Kirtland, Geauga
Co., Ohio, October 4th, 1815.
I was well acquainted with the Prophet
Joseph Smith. He came from New York
to Kirtland at an early date. He lived in
our home seven months and afterwards
in a house built for him by my father near
our home.
I was married to Joseph Stewart Allen in
Lima, Hancock Co., Ill; passed through
many of the persecutions and mobbings of
the Saints; saw my father, with others,
taken by the mob to be shot because they
were “Mormons,” but they were delivered
by the power of the Lord.
I came to Utah in 1848, in Brigham
Young’s company. I have helped to build
up many places in Utah, was called to the
Muddy mission and lived there three years.
I was a member of the United Order at
Orderville, until it was broken up.
I am the mother of five sons and seven
daughters, I have seventy-eight grandchil-
dren, one hundred and four great grand-
children, and nine great, great granchil-
dren.
I was promised by Apostle F. M. Lyman
that none of my posteritv should be lost; a
promise that is a great comfort to me. I
want to bear my testimony that Joseph
Smith was a true prophet of the Lord, also
a grand and noble man,
