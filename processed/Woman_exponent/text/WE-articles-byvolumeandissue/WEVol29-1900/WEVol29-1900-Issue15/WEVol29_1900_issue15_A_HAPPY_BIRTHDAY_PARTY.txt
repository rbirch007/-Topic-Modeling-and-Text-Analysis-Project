A HAPPY BIRTHDAY PARTY.
	Mrs. Elizabeth J. Stevenson invited a few
friends to her new residence on Friday,
February 8, to celebrate her birthday,
though the party was really given in honor
of Sister Zina and the General Board of the
Relief Society of which she is a member.
Congratulations and hearty good wishes
were showered upon Sister Stevenson and
her pretty home was radiant with glowing
coal fires in the bright new grates[-] and
though the day was not so pleasant without,
rather blustering, yet within was a beautiful
spirit of peace and love and good will.
	A sumptuous dinner had been prepared
and the table was handsomely decorated
with carnations and roses. Covers were
laid for fourteen and a happy party it was
indeed who sat down to partake of the good
things. Sisters Zina D. H. Young, Jane S.
Richards, Bathsheba W. Smith, Emmeline
B. Wells, Julia C. Howe, Emma S. Wood-
ruff, Maria W. Wilcox, Romania B. Pratt,
Maria Y. Dougall, Julina L. Smith, Emily
S. Richards, Julia P. M. Farnsworth, Annie
T. Hyde and the hostess herself.
	Sister Zina asked the blessing upon the
feast. Conversation was general and en-
livening and the time passed so swiftly that
the ladies could scarcely believe they had
lingered so long over the dinner table chat-
ting and complimenting the hostess, until it


was nearly time to take leave of their genial
friend and sister.
	The EXPONENT adds its best wishes.
A long life, happiness and prosperity for
Sister Stevenson and many more happy
birthdays with old friends and new ones to
cheer and gladden her heart.
