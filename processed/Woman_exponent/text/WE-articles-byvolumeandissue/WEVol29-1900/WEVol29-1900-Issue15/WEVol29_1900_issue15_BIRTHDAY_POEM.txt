BIRTHDAY POEM. 
	To President Jane S. Richards.
Pause for a moment and backward, turn back
O’er life’s strong ocean of time,
	Its billows and roar,
	And tempests it bore,
To a scene of grandeur sublime.

’Tis our Father’s dwelling, the home of our God,
Who dwelleth in mansions above,
	Where zephyrs so calm,
	Bear only the balm,
The balm of true heavenly love.

The Father called forth from his palace of peace,
A daughter so trusty and true,
	“Because of your worth,
	A mission on earth,
Is given my daughter to you.

“Go forth in your calling, be humble and brave.
Act nobly and wisely your part,
	The cross you must bear,
	If the crown you would wear,
And reign with the righteous at heart.”

In a neat little cottage a baby was born,
’Twas “only a girl” it is true,
	So tiny and weak,
	With no power to speak,
Or tell what she came here to do.

All, all was forgotten of that blest abode,
That life of exquisite bliss,
	With music and song,
	From heavenly throng,
All changed for the sorrows of this.

But often there hovered around that dear child,
A spirit of heavenly ray,
	Which helped her along,
	Through evil and wrong,
By whispering, “This is the way.”

She heeded the whisper and shunned many snares,
Through childhood and also through youth,
	When a message came,
	In our Father’s name,
And brought her the Gospel of truth.

Twas the “Pearl of Great Price,” she gave up
		her all,
That one precious pearl to possess;
	She knew ’twas the key,
	To life’s grand mystery.
Which opened the door to success.

From that very moment her life work began,
Each weakness she sought to subdue,
	The cross she would bear,
	The crown she might wear,
For she knew that the Gospel was true.

We can but begin to portray the good work,
That her willing hands have done,
	But this we must say, 
	By her unselfish way,
Our love and respect she has won.

And now to our bountiful Father in heaven,
We earnestly, fervently pray,
	So loved and so true,
	Aunt Zina and you,
May have happy returns of the day.
