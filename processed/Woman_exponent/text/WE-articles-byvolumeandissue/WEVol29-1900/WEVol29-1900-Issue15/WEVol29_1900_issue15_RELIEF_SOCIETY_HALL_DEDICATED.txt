RELIEF SOCIETY HALL DEDICATED.
	The Provo Third Ward Relief Society
dedicated their new and beautiful hall on
Friday, March 15, 1901, it being just one
year from the time of commencement until
the dedication.
	The services were held at 2 p. m., Presi-
dent Eliza N. Taylor presiding. After
singing by the ward choir and prayer was
offered, short speeches were made by the
president of the ward society and her
counselors, some of the Stake officers, also
the presidency of the Stake. President
David John offered the dedicatory prayer,
A duet by John Johnson and Emma Knud-
sen. The Bishop of the ward, F. W. Taylor,
spoke in praise of the great work accom-
plished by the sisters and a song was sung
by James E. Daniels.
	After the closing exercises the congrega-
tion adjourned to the Assembly Hall, where
a sumptuous dinner was in waiting, about
four hundred partaking thereof. In the
evening a fine program was rendered.
This completed the day, which will long be
remembered by all.
