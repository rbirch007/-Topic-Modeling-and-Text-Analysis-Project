			LUCY W. KIMBALL.

	In summing up the many wonderful
events of the marvelous nineteenth century
now closing, every Latter-day Saint will
instinctively place as the leading headlight
in the list, the birth, mission and death of
Joseph Smith.
	When his work on earth was completed,
the Prophet Joseph, sealed his testimony
with his blood, as did Christ and many of
His disciples anciently. But in this century
the light of the Gospel of Jesus Christ was
ushered in to remain and continue to in-
crease forever.
	Joseph proclaimed the utmost freedom for
all men; and following his appeal for uni-
versal liberty, slavery was abolished in the
United States of America. At a fearful
cost, it is true; but had the government
listened to the inspired words of the young
prophet, it need not have cost one drop of
blood, nor a tithe of the money expended.
	He declared when he organized the
Sisterhood of the Church into the Relief
Society, that he “turned the key in favor of
woman.” Since that time what a noble
work has been accomplished in woman’s
favor by hundreds of heroic women in this
and other nations, including many of the
Society which the Prophet organized.
	With the progress of light darkness has
wildly striven to keep pace, or out do. But
on the 6th of April, 1893, an advance step
was taken by the over-ruling power of light,
which will never be quite approached by
the shadow of darkness.
	At that time the Prophet Wilford Wood-
ruff declared that the key was turned in
favor of the Saints, and was in the hand of
Jesus Christ; and that from that time
righteousness would have greater power
and wickedness less in the world. With
this proposition in view, what may we not
anticipate for the coming twentieth century?
To be brief, ere its close, Satan may be
securely bound and Christ may reign over
the earth.
