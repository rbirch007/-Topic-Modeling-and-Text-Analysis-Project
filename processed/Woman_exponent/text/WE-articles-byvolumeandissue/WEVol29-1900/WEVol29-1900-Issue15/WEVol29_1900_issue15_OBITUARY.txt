OBITUARY.
SISTER SARAH S. HARDER.
 	Sister Sarah S. Harder died at her residence in
Kamas, Summit Co., October 28, 1900, after a long
and painful illness and severe suffering for five
months.
	She was born in Portsmouth, England, March 3,
1840, and was the daughter of Stephen and Eliza-
beth Biddlecome Spicer, and the wife of [-]illet S.
Harder. She leaves a husband, two married
daughters, seven grandchildren and many friends
to mourn her loss. Two sons preceded her to the
great beyond. She was a faithful member of the
Kamas Relief Society for many years.
	Sister Harder was set apart as assistant secretary
on March 7, 1881, and appointed secretary of the
same society on June 10, 1895, which position she
held until her death. Her Relief Society work
was well and faithfully done and stands to her
honor. She came to Utah in 1863, and crossed the
plains in Rosel Hyde’s company.
		M. L. WOOLSTENHULME, Stake Sec.
