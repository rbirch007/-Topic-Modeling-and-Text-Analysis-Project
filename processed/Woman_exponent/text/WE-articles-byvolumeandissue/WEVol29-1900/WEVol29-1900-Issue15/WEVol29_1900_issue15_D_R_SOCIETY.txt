D. R. SOCIETY.
	The Annual meeting of the Utah State
Society Daughters of the Revolution was
held Saturday, March 16, 1901, in the
WOMAN’S EXPONENT office, Templeton
Building, and was an enthusiastic gather-
ing. An excellent program was rendered
and followed by the election of officers for
the present year: Mrs. Clarissa S. Williams,
Regent; Mrs. Julia P. M. Farnsworth and
Mrs. Mary A. H. Cannon, vice-regents;
Mrs. Sarah F. Smith, Rec. Sec; Mrs. Lucy
W. Smith, Ass’t. Rec. Sec.; Mrs. Julia F.
Lund, Cor. Sec.; Mrs. Myra Y. Rossiter,
Treasurer; Mrs. Mary E. F. Webber, Regis-
trar; Mrs. Susa Y. Gates, Historian; Mrs.
E. B. Wells, Chaplain. Committee on
Program and Entertainment, Mrs. J. F.
Lund, Mrs. E. W. Hyde and Miss Claire
Clawson.
	The next meeting will occur on April 19,
as that day is the battle of Lexington. The
next year’s work includes the following:
	April I.—Colonization: (a) Growth of
Colonies; population. (b) Nationality; from
what class of society. (c) Language; religion;
industries. Miss Katherine Wells.
	May—II. Government of Colonies: (a)
New England; middle colonies; south. (b)
Charter; proprietary; royal province. (c)
Leading cities. Miss Mabelle Snow.
	June—III. Great Political Parties: (a)
Whigs and Tories in England and America.
(b) Famous leaders. (c) Their influences.
Miss Claire Clawson.
	September—IV. Causes of the American
Revolution: (a) Rights of Englishmen;
Magna Charta; establishment of the
House of Commons; petition of rights
(Charles I.); bill of rights 1688, etc.;
rights of people under such acts, (b) Re-
lation to American history, (c) Oppres-
sive laws and acts and their effects on
colonies. Mrs. C. S. Williams.
	October—V Important events of 1875
and 1876: (a) First Continental Congress;
place of meeting; purpose of president;
noted delegates, etc. (b) Beginning of the
war; Lexington; Bunket Hill. (c) Survey
of opposing forces, officers, etc. (d) Siege
of Boston. (d) Declaration of Independ-
ence. Mrs. Hyde.
	November—VI: From 1776 to 1783: (a)
Geography of the American Revolution;
outline the battles; movements, etc., and
locate important historic spots from Lex-
ington to Yorktown. Mrs. Lucy Smith.
December—VII. Government of the
colonies during the Revolution: (a) Con-
gress, articles of Confederation and the
State government. (b) Great leaders.
Mrs. Mamie Cannon.
	January—VIII. Close of war: (a) Win-
ning of independence. (b) The definitive
treaty of 1883. (c) The extent of terri-
tories ceded to the colonies. (d) Our com-
mission in Paris. Mrs. Webber.
	February—IX. Romantic events con-
nected with the Revolution: (a) Boston
tea party; Nathan Hale; death of Mont-
gomery; Arnold’s treason; Andre; Lee at
Monmouth; surrender of Cornwallis, etc.,
etc. Mrs. A. S. M. Horne.
