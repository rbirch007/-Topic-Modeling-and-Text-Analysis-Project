THOUGHTS ON THE CLOSING CENTURY.

	What strides have been made in all
directions, in science, particularly, what
great discoveries have been made. What
mighty wars have desolated the land. But
God rules over all, and from the ashes of
battle arises a better state of feeling, a
nearer fellowship, a drawing together of
nations. In no department do we observe a
greater change than in the religious world.
Such an intolerant spirit as was manifest
between the different sects and denomina-
tions of forms of religion within my remem-
brance. The Master’s leaven of Love is
working as we come to know Him better, to
have His spirit pervade our hearts. We
see less faults and more to admire in those
people of other faiths, different from ours.
	And I believe the good work will go on,
slowly but surely, until Christ’s kingdom
shall be eatablished in every land.
	But, ah, the wickedness that still exists,
and greatest of all is caused by the liquor
traffic! What an awful curse it is, what
misery and degradation it causes! If my
faith were not strong in the final triumph
of good over evil, I should be in despair;
but the world moves and I hope
and trust towards the right. The growth
must be upward. God goes forward ever,
oven amidst our tears and fears, and He will
prevail.
	This century will bring about great
changes, it must be for the better. The bad
must die, the good lives on forever, ah, yes,
forever!
						P. E. C.
