ZION'S MIGHTY KING.
Upon the cross the Savior hung,
A crown of thorns pressed on his brow,
With anguish deep His heart is wrung;
Aloud he cries, “ ’Tis finished now.”

His slayers taunt with cruel scorn,
"Come down, if Son of God Thou be.”
The world’s Redeemer He was born,
From sin to set His people free.

In agony He bowed His head,
With broken heart and moans he died;
All nature groans, her light is fled,
God’s sinless Son is crucified.

These emblems eat, remem’bring Him, 
His body broken for our sake,
That we may life eternal win,
When in his likeness we awake.

Now of His blood so freely spilt,
Drink and make thy covenants new,
Then from the dreadful stains of guilt,
Thy trembling soul is purged anew.

O, solemn thought, the Savior’s slain!
But here we’ll testify of Him,
Till He shall come to earth again,
To reign as Zion’s Mighty King.
	Lydia D. Alder.
