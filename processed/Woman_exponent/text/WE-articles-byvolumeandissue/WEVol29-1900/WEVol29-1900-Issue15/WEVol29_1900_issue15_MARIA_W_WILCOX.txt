			MARIA W. WILCOX.

	The signs of the times are fraught wtih
deep interest to the Latter-day Saints. We
see the fulfillment of many prophecies of
the prophets in both ancient and modern
times. In my soul I heartily endorse the
sentiments expressed and the testimonies
borne by our prophets, seers and revelators,
the Watchmen upon the walls of Zion, who
are ever alert, whose voices are ever heard
in defence of the revelations given through
Joseph Smith, the Prophet, to this gener-
ation, for the uplifting and redemption of
the whole race of mankind, both the living
and the dead. Since the year 1885 my life

[Column 3]
work has been in the Temples of God. I
have had great joy in my labors. The
Prophet Joseph declared this to be the
most important work the Latter-day
Saints could be engaged in.
	I do indeed feel proud of the distinction
of being chosen as a bosom companion of
the Prophet Joseph Smith. That I am a
living witness of his divine mission and I
bear testimony to all the world that the
foundation of the everlasting hills are not
more firmly set or established than the
knowledge I have received, or the witness
that has been given me of the spirit, con-
cerning the divine origin of the great
Latter-day work.
	May the new century bring peace, joy
and happiness to all seekers after truth.
