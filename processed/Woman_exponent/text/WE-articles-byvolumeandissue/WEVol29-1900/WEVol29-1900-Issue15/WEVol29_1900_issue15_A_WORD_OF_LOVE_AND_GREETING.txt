A WORD OF LOVE AND GREETING.

Beloved Sisters:

	Agreeable to our promise, and also in ac-
cord with our most heartfelt desires, we
again address you. It is our most earnest
wish that we may commence as though we
were actually in meeting with you and
could feel your presence with us; and by
the influence of the Holy Spirit we may
understand your inmost thoughts and needs
and be able to offer such suggestions as may
meet the necessities and fill the require-
ments of the sisters who are anxiously
striving to learn and do their full duties in
the Relief Society.
	Again we will remind you of the
holy and grand work for woman to
which you are called in this important
organization. As formerly stated, we will
also repeat that to be a good member of the
Relief Society, and especially to hold office
therein means work. There is always
business of importance to be looked after,
and it is highly essential that a correct
understanding of all the business of the
society be sought for and obtained, so that
all the work may be done in order and in
harmony.
	How necessary it is then that the Holy
Spirit be cultivated, as it is impossible to
carry on the work acceptably without it.
While with its aid and support, even the
weakest of us need not shrink from any duty
to which we may be called by proper
authority. And here we will say, that
when we are in the full line of our duty we
will be in accord with the priesthood.
	One great lesson for us to learn if we
have not already done so, is to speak no evil.
Study Christ’s Sermon on the Mount and
practice its teachings, especially remember-
ing ‘‘Blessed are the peace makers, for they
shall be called the children of God.”
	We are interested in the welfare of our
sisters, the young as well as the old; and we
invite you all to attend the Relief Society
meetings and conferences. Young mothers
would be greatly benefited and blessed by
the instructions of older sisters who have
gained wisdom by lives of toil and experi-
ence. The mission of a mother is a holy
calling, which is attended with many cares
and anxieties. And every mother needs
the Spirit of God to assist her.
	The more you attend your meetings, the
more the spirit of your callings will rest
upon you, and your burdens will become
lighter and your daily tasks be easier to
perform, and your life will be sweeter and
brighter. Teach your children to pray, to
observe the Sabbath, to have regard for the
authorities of the Church, their parents,
aged people, places of worship and all such
things. Teach them to be orderly, obedi-
dient while they are young, and their
minds are pure. Be awake, sisters, get the
Spirit of God, and don’t let the cares of life
and worldly things prevent you from at-
tending to the duties which you owe to God
and to each other. You have all received
talents from on high. Don't fold them in a


napkin and lay them away, but bring them
forth to be used for the benefit of mankind.
Let your light shine to help lighten the
world, that all may glorify our Father
which is in heaven.
	How rich were the blessings showered
upon the Saints at our late General Confer-
ence! Let us not forget or lose sight of
them. And how encouraging to us were
the great gatherings of the sisters at our
Relief Society Conference on the 8th of
April, 1901. Seldom before had we so large
an attendance at a sisters conference. Those
of you who had the privilege of being pres-
ent will, we trust and believe, try to fully
appreciate the point which was that day so
clearly and practically illustrated regarding
the necessity of our having a building of
our own suitable for our general conferences
and all needful business. And let the spirit
of progression, the influence felt at our con-
ference, be diffused throughout our entire
society, that every one may be profoundly
interested in the erection of our Woman’s
Building, and use all legitimate plans for
raising the necessary means to accomplish
this very desirable end. Talk up this
building, sisters, in all your assemblies and
in your conversation until the matter is ac-
complished, which we dare to anticipate may
be at an early day. But while talk and
agitation are essential we must keep in mind
that intelligent planning and energetic labor
are the agencies, after all, by which great
works are accomplished. 
	Let us show our appreciation of the mag-
nificent gift which the presidency of the
Church have bestowed upon us in the form
of a beautiful building site, by erecting
thereupon a building worthy of the situa-
tion; a house that shall stand as a monu-
ment to do honor to the women of Zion.
	Beloved sisters, we have hopes of seeing
the contemplated building completed in our
time, which, according to the common
course of mortality, may not be many years
longer in the flesh, and that President
Lorenzo Snow in his time may have the
privilege of dedicating it. We hope to hold
at least one conference with you in our own
building, and as many more as the Lord
may, in His infinite goodness, see fit to al-
low.
	Now, as always, we pray God to bless
you all in your loving and charitable duties,
and that His Spirit may guide and aid you
throughout your lives.
	Your sincere sisters and co-laborers,
ZINA D. H. YOUNG.
JANE S. RICHARDS. 
BATHSHEBA W. SMITH.
