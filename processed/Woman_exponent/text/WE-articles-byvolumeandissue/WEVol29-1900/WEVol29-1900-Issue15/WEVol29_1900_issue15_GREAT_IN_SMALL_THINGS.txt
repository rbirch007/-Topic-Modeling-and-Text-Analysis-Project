GREAT IN SMALL THINGS.
HOW TO FORGIVE.
Keep the picture of Christ being crucified 
before you, remember these words "Father 
forgive tbem, for they know not what they 
do," or if you have been injured, you know
that you are happier than they who have
wronged you, for it is better to suffer 
wrong than to do wrong. If you were 
acquainted with the circumstances that in-
duced this person to do wrong, you would
undoubtedly say, "I am afraid that I could 
not have done so well had I been in his 
place." Tender him your sympathy and 
be thankful that you are stronger-minded 
and able to combat with temptation better
than he or she, or you have received the 
light of the Gospel, which he has not.
Criticising and finding fault will have its 
effect. When you meet that person again,
whom you have spoken against, there is a 
feeling of dislike, not love or leniency,
which we should have had.
We are all God's children, with all our 
faults and failings, and very liable to yield 
to temptation. If we are not able to do a 
great work, can we not do good in little 
things? always having leniency one with 
another, selecting the good from a person's


character and letting the bad alone; filling
our lives with so much good that the evil
will have no place whatever. By doing
this it will be said of us, as of all good peo-
ple, “I like to be with them, it does me
good, I feel better and happier in their
company, they exert a good influence.”
	‘‘Judge not that ye be not judged.”
Leave it to a higher Judge who is better
able than we are to know how to judge.
Our religion is broad and by studying these
principles of our religion we can become
great in small things. Our influence will
be felt wherever we go, we will be loved
because we have charity and live for others
