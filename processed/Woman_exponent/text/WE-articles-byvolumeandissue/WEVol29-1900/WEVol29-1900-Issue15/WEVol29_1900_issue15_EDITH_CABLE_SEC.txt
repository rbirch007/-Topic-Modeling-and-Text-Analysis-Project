			EDITH CABLE, Sec.


MRS. CARRIE CHAPMAN CATT, president
of the National Suffrage Association, spoke
in behalf of equal suffrage at the debate
held by the League for Political Education
on January 11, in New York City. The
League invited the Anti-Suffrage Associa-
tion to send a speaker to present the nega-
tive, but the “Antis” had no one who was
willing to meet Mrs. Catt in argument.
Mrs. Lillian W. Betts, a member of the
League, supported the negative from the
floor. The vote stood in favor of suffrage
33 to 3.
