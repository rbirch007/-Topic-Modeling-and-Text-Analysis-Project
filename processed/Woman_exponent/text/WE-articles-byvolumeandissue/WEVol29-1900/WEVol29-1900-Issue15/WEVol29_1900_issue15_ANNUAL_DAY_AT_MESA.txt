ANNUAL DAY AT MESA.

The Relief Society of Mesa Ward, of
Maricopa Stake, held annual day exercises
on Monday, March 18,1901, at Relief
Society hall, sixty members present.
President Laura Rappleye presided. Sing-
ing, “Praise to the man. ’’Prayer by Stake
President B. R. Hakes. Singing, “We
thank Thee, O, God, for a prophet.” The
minutes of the last annual day were read.
Opening address by Stake President M.
A. Hakes; also read the poem, “Threads of
Thought.” Minutes of the first Relief
Society in 1842, read by Sister S. M.
Pomeroy. Song, Barbara Allen. Remarks
by Stake President E. R. Hakes, who spoke
of the importance of Relief Society work,
and of suffrage organization.
Refreshments were served and the after-
noon session began at 2 o’clock by singing
“O, ye mountains high.” Prayer by
Sarah M. Johnson. Singing, “Redeemer
of Israel.”
Program continued with an address of
welcome by Counselor Sarah M.. Johnson.
Recitation “Annie Laurie,” Dora Pomeroy.
Song, Dora Gilispie. Essay, “Procras-
tination,” Annie E. Fuller. Song, “Bring
Back My Loved one,” Mary Pomeroy.
Congregational singing, “O, my Father.”
Testimonies by Ida Johnson, Alice Rich-
eus, Mary Ann Horne, Melissa Babbitt.
and Etta Pomeroy. Song, May Fuller.
Adjourned by usual exercises, the fes-
tivities of the day being closed by a grand
ball in the evening, participated in by
old and young.
BELLE POMEROY, Sec. pro tem.
