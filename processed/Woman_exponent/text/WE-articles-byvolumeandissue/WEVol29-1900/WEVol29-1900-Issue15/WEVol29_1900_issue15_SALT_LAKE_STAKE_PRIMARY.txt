	SALT LAKE STAKE PRIMARY.

	Minutes of Primary conference of the
Salt Lake Stake, held in the Assembly Hall,
March 15, 1901, President Camilla C. Cobb
presiding.
[Column 2]
	Meeting opened by singing “Gladly
meeting." Prayer was offered by Master
Sterling Talmage. Singing, “I think
when I read that sweet story of old.” Roll
called, twenty-two assistants present, each
responding with sentiments in concert.
Minntes of previous conference read and
accepted.
	The following program was carried out:
	Song, “In our lovely Deseret,” by all
of the children. Song, by four little girls
of the Eleventh Ward. Recitation by
Margaret Taylor, of the Second Ward.
Organ solo, by Ethel Reynolds, of the
Twentieth Ward. Questions and answers
on the first principles of the Gospel, by
members of the Seventh Ward. Recita-
tion about Jesus, by Elsa Knott of the same
ward. Song by five little girls of the
Seventeenth Ward. Sister Amanda Done
told an interesting Thanksgiving story,
showing how happiness can be derived from
unselfishness. Another song by four little
girls of the Eleventh Ward. Recitation by
Wanda Lyon, of the Thirteenth Ward.
Song by a number of little girls of the Six-
teenth Ward.
	President Cobb said she was pleased with
the program and complimented the children
on their punctuality. Told them we would
not have Sister Morris with us very long,
as she had resigned her position as counselor
in the Stake Board, on account of ill health.
Felt to ask the Lord to bless her, said it
was by the blessing of the Lord that she
herself was able to be with us, as she had
been very sick.
	Sister L. G. Richards, of the General
Board, said she loved the children and was
pleased to be with them. Asked them to
say God bless Sister Morris, also Sister 
Cobb and Sister Felt, and prayed the Lord
would bless the children.
	Meeting closed by singing “Around the
throne of God.” Benediction by Sister
Julia C. Howe.
