	CELEBRATIONS, MARCH 17, 1901.
		POCATELLO, IDAHO.
	Tuesday afternoon the Relief Society
of Pocatello celebrated the anniversary
of its first organization by the Prophet
Joseph Smith. A meeting was held in the
afternoon, and a program was rendered
consisting of speeches, songs and recita-
tions, and a paper was read by President
Harrison giving the history, rise and pro-
gress of the society. In the evening an
elegant supper was served, after which a
dance was enjoyed by the old and young.
			MORONI.
	Our Relief Society has just held its an-
nual celebration of the organization of the
first society of its kind, by the Prophet
Joseph Smith. Nearly all the married
folks participated, and a very en-

[Column 2]
joyable time was spent from noon until
midnight. A suitable program of
songs, recitations and addresses was
rendered. A fine repast was provided and
much appreciated. The light fantastic
was duly tripped; indeed it was just such a
time as our wives and mothers know best
how to make.
		ELEVENTH WARD.
	The Eleventh Ward celebrated the fifty-
ninth anniversary of organization of the
Relief Society in their ward hall, on Mon-
day evening the 18th, when the following
program was rendered.
	Selection by the choir. Prayer, Charles
Livingston. Solo, Laura Madsen. Read-
ing, Alexander M’Master. Duet, Olive
Cushing and Alice Wickens. Recitation,
Erma Felt. Soprano Solo, Emma Arm-
strong. Recitation, Ethel Allen. Soprano
solo, Nellie Penrose Whitney. Sketch of
Society Work, Maria Bidgood. Recitation,
John Knight. Solo, Sperry Lawson. Reci-
tation, Josie Morris. Song, Henry Hul-
bert. Remarks by Bishop Morris. Bene-
diction, Isaac Sears.
	A very enjoyable time was spent.
		SARAH H. G. M’MASTER, Pres.
