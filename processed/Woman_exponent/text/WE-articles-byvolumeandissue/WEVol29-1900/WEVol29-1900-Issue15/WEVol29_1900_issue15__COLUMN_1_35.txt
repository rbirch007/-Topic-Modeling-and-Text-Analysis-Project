IN THE WILDERNESS OF MEXICO.
Camping on the lonely prairie, with a brilliant star-
	lit sky,
In the distance weird and weary, listening to the
	coyotes cry—
He is fierce and wild and hungry, watching at this
	midnight hour,
Howling, barking in a frenzy, seeking whom he
	may devour.
In this wilderness so quaint, nature’s children roam
	at will,
Savage tribes now run at riot, over plain, and
	dale and hill.
Through the grass so tall and slender, reptiles
	drag their length along,
In their nests the birdlings tender long have
	hushed their vesper song.

Craggy rocks the precious metals, like unwilling
	prisoners hold—
Flowers, too, have closed their petals, holding
	dewdrops in their fold,
Like sentinels the prickly cactus, rear their tower-
	ing forms on high,
As afflicted Saints in anguish, turn their faces to
	the sky.
Mascall, so heavy in her juices, dot the hillside
	thickly here.
Born to pamper man’s abuses, oft to cause the bit-
	ter tear!
Like so many of God's mercies, destined for a
	higher plain—
But by evil minds perverted, bringing endless grief
	and pain.

Even through the winter hours, oose is green—
	while graceful vine
Makes luxuriant fair bowers, in the treetops where
	they twine;
Mistletoe like faithful brothers, clinging in the
	limbs above,
Just as true, devoted mothers, never loose their
	bonds of love.
While the stralight soft and mellow, forms the
	covering of my bed,
Mother earth my welcome pillow, Father watch-
	ing overhead—
Wondering brain, in wakeful vision, wandering to
	former days—
Ancient peoples and their mission, what their
	habits, hopes and ways.

Now from out the yawning caverns, o’er the cliffs
	so high and steep—
Where they dwelt in fear and trembling, dusk[-]
	faces seem to peep—
Forms seem flitting from each crevice, voices
	whisper soft and low,
Echoing o’er hill and terrace, thrilling tales of
	long ago!
When the ancient tribes of Lehi sought to find the
	promised land,
Thither led by righteous Nephi, through the Lord’s
	inspired command.
He who took the wise position ere to live by holy
	light,
By example and monition, sought to guide his
	brothers right.
Yet though brothers of one mother, angry jealousy
	and strife,
Oft arose with one another, through their wild and
	rambling life.

Through repeated generations, peaceful days were
	far between.

[Column 2]
Though they laid such grand foundations, relics
	now of which are seen,
Mounds and terrace in profusion, strongholds in
	the mountain peak,
Where they fled in wild confusion, peace and safe-
	ty there to seek.
Records, too, were often hidden ’neath the strong,
	cemented wall.
Where the pine trees now unbidden, rear their
	branches thick and tall.
Smooth Matatti, broken oyer, hyroglyphics on the
	stones,
And uncovered there before you, bleaching,
	mouldering human bones.

And it seems opposing forces, met in warfare’s
	dread array.
For we find their battle axes, even at this distant
	day.
Ancient structures long since buried, modern
	science [-]as revealed
Pomp and crime, to one grave hurried—raging ele-
	ments concealed,
Was it when the blessed Savior gave His life for
	mortal sin?
That in penitent behavior, earth and sea their
	faces hid?
Beauteous plains and peaceful valleys, in convul-
	sive natures throe,
Wildly threw themselves together burying cities
	far below.

Closing o’er their wretched victims—silenced all
	their songs of war,
Stilled the voice of hopeful peacetime—final set-
	ting of life’s star—
No more to rise, till generations long have slept
	and passed away,
While their old corrupt traditions, with their bodies
	find decay
Heaven’s Truths are never vanquished! Writ on
	pages golden leaved—
Though long buried, while men languished, is
	again to earth revealed!

Telling of those ancient races, how they struggled
	with the right.
Till before their frightened faces, beamed the
	Everlasting Light!
Darkened natures to illumine, by God’s sacrifice
	for sin.
How His Son with passions human, came redemp-
	tion to begin.
How He taught the Jewish nation principles of
	righteousness,
Speaking words of sure salvation, all mankind to
	save and bless.
But rejected was the message, by the many on the
	earth!
Little knew they of the blessing, that would bring
	celestial birth.

For the world for many ages, long in stagnant
	darkness slept,
As we find on history’s pages, yearning men and
	women wept,
Heresies and false traditions, blinding faith, ob-
	scured desire—
Only lowered man’s condition, smouldering the
	dying fire,
Till a youth of humble station, destined by a
	power on high,
Had angelic visitation, telling where the records
	lie,
That restored again the Gospel, in its fulness to
	the earth,
Bringing to all tongues and peoples, promise of
	Eternal birth.
ELLIS R. SHIPP.
Colonia, Jaurez, Chihuahua, Mexico.
