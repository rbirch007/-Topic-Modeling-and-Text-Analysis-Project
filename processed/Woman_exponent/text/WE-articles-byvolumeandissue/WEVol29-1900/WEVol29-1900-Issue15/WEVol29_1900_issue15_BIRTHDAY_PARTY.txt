BIRTHDAY PARTY.
Victor, Idaho, October 17, 1900.
Editor Woman's Exponent:
	On September 22nd the members of the
Relief Society of Raymond Ward, with
some of the brethren, met at the ward meet-
ing house in the capacity of a surprise on
Sister Telitha Avery, first vice-president of
the association, who had just reached
the 76th milestone in life. She has always
taken an active part in religious affairs, was
one of the members of the Relief Society in
Nauvoo and has acted as president in some
of the wards in Utah. A nice program was
carried out, every number being worthy of
special mention. Two little girls, great
granddaughters of Sister Avery, sang very
nicely, and Sister Margaret Rice, seventy-
one years of age, sang sweetly a Nauvoo
hymn.
	After the program a nice lunch was par-
taken of and all joined in wishing the lady
many happy returns of the day.
	Our Relief Society is progressing nicely.
We have purchased a lot on which to build
a meeting house and granary in the near
future.
A.
