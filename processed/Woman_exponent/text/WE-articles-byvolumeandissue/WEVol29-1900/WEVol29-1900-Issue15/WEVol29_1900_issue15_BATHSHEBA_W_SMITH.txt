BATHSHEBA W. SMITH,

	These are my views of the century just
passed.
	I consider it the most important
and wonderful since the days of Christ. The
rapid strides which have been made in
every branch of science are marvelous to
hmman understanding. But the greatest
and most important to the whole human
family is the mission of the young prophet
Joseph Smith. When in the sincerity of his
soul he besought the Lord to show him
which of all the religious denominations was
the right one, God the Father revealed
Himself unto him with His Son, Jesus
Christ, beside Him, and told Joseph to join
himself to none of the sects then existing, as
he had a great work for him to do. The
Lord afterward opened up to his under-
standing the great plan of salvation for the
children of men, and restored the Everlast-
ing Gospel with all its power and authority.
	Especially to the government of the
United States has it been eventful and
profitable. The opening up of the Western
intermountain region, with its untold
wealth, through the influence and perse-
verance of the Latter-day Saints, has added
materially to the strength and importance
of this nation.
	The whole century has been replete with
the fulfillment of prophecies of the ancient
prophets. The signs of the times to which
the Savior referred have been vividly mani-
fested, and will become more marked dur-
ing the centuiy that is before us. The wars
and rumors of wars, the famine, flood,
pestilence and other calamities that were
promised in the latter days, are of daily oc-
currence, and are destined to continue until
the coming of the Savior to reign upon the
earth.
