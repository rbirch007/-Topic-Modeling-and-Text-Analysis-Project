CANNONVIL[--] ANNIVERSARY.
Anniversary of the Relief Society of
Cannonville, Utah, held March 18, 1901.
As the 17th came on Sunday, President
Deba S. Dunning decided to hold the anni-
versary on Monday night, the 18th of
March, 1901, The meeting was opened by
singing “Come listen to a prophet’s voice.”
Prayer by Bishop James N. Anderson.
Singing, “All hail the glorious day.”
Sister Alvira A. Henderson made a few
remarks as follows; “I feel to rejoice in
the work of the Relief Society, where-
ever I have lived. I have lived here for
twenty-three years, and have helped in the
society here. Fifty-nine years ago the first
Relief Society was organized. The prophet
called on the sisters to relieve the poor.”
Recitation by Mrs. Harriet G. Clark,
“The Great Man at the Gate of Heaven.”
Then came a song by Mrs. Anna Davis and
Sister Sarah Henderson. Sister Margaret
E. Willis read a humorous composition on
early times in Utah. “A tribute to the
Sisters of the Relief Society, was read by
President Deba Dunning. A song, “The
End of the Way,” was sung by Sisters
Lydia M. Dunning and Letitia M. Spencer.
Song, “Kind Words,” by Myra Heward
and Janette Allen. Reading by Martha S.
Johnson, “Childish Wisdom.” Essay on
woman, by Laura J. Davis. Song, by

[Column Two]

Dora Fletcher and company, “The Narra-
gansett Bay.” Recitation, “The Gambler’s
Wife,” by Annie Dutton. Song, by Sisters
Elizabeth Fletcher and Harriet G. Clark,
“Gently Down the Stream of Time.” Reci-
tation, “The Beggar and His Day,” by
Lucy Henderson. Song, “Sweet Bye and
Bye,” by young girls. Recitation, “The
Soldiers Message,” by Cora Henderson.
A dialogue. “The Sewing Bee,” by Deba
S. Dunning, Harriet G. Clark, Lydia D.
Henderson, Laura Davis, Cora Henderson
Lucy Henderson, Frances Henderson and
Sarah Henderson. Singing, “Up, awake
ye defenders of Zion.” Dismissed by
Bishop’s Counselor Owen Clark.
