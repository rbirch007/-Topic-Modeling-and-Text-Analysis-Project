OBITUARY.
	Again we are called to report the death of a be-
loved sister and friend, Sister Ann Methly Abel,
who died at her home in American Fork, January 
7, 1901.
	Sister Abel was born June 27, 1824, in Sheffield,
Yorkshire, England. With her husband and chil-
dren she emigrated to Utah in 1863, and settled in 
American Fork, where she continued to reside un-
til her death. She was the mother of eight chil-
dren, five of whom survive her; also twenty-two 
grandchildren and three great-grandchildren. She 
was an affectionate wife, a devoted mother and a 
good friend, always willing and ready at any 
time, day or night, to help in sickness or death, and 
by her industry setting an example worthy of emu-
lation.
	She was a faithful and energetic teacher in the 
Relief Society from its first organization until
about five years ago, when her health failed and 
she very reluctantly had to give up the work which 
had been a labor of love with her. She was in 
very deed a true Latter-day Saint, and through all 
her sickness was patient and cheerful, her faith in 
he ordinances af the Gospel never faltered.
