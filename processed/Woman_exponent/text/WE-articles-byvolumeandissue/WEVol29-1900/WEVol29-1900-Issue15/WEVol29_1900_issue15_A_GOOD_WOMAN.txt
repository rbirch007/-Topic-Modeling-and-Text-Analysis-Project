			A GOOD WOMAN.

	One who on her bed of sickness was, as
she always had been, thinking of others,
had learned that an old book keeper was
out of employment, which worried her.
The Elders were called in to administer to
her. After they had gone, she said, “I am
so sorry, but I was feeling so bad while
they were here that I forgot to ask them if
they could give this brother employment.”
	The second time when they were called
in she thought of it. The employment was
not found but it was taken under advise-
ment and the brother was helped.
	She would also mention some good that
others had done her, which they undoubted-
ly had forgotten. She could most likely
have found a great deal of evil to say of
them had she been looking for it.
	Love begets love, and this was the dis-
position of this good woman, this true Saint
of God, who had all her life been gentle,
kind, sympathetic and forgiving, even to
those who had been thoughtless. Her life
was a lesson to all those who came in con-
tact with her, and they must have felt the
sweet influence of the spirit of meekness
and forbearance. To do good to others was
ever her motto in life. She has passed from
our midst, but her memory is still fresh and
green in the hearts of her children and
grandchildren and all her family, and she
will always be held in honorable remem-
brance among neighbors and friends.
	‘‘Blessed are the dead who die in the
Lord from henceforth, for their works shall
follow them.”
							R. A. S.
