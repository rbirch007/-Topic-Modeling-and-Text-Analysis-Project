LADY SOMERSET'S COLONY.
	The Viscountess Horncastle gave a
garden party August 20, at Taymouth
House, Hackney Downs to acquaint her
friends with the work at Lady Somerset for
inebriate women at the colony she has es-


tablished at Duxhurst. Rev. C. F.
Wiliams was in the chair and introduced
Mrs. Graham, who is connected with the
work at Duxhurst. She said that there
was originally six cottages, each of which
could receive six patients. Two more have
been added, one by the voluntary sub-
scription of sailors, the other by the town of
Hastings.
	Under the Act of 1898 magistrates are
empowered to send inebriate women to a
home after a third conviction, and the good
that Duxhurst can do is only limited by the
number it can house. As many as three
thousand cases have had to be refused in
one year.
