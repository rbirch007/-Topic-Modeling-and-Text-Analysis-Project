A CAPTIVE OF TWO WARS.
I live in prison, though my open door 
	Leads out upon the sunny fields and glens;
I feel the clank of chains that evermore
	Forged by the cruel fates beyond my ken,
The spirit chafes, the heart keeps sad and sore.

Burdens of sorrow might imprison me.
	Though leaving windows light and open door;
I live alone with tasks of Liberty,
	And weep for Fillippino and the Boer;
But I would up, and striving, helpful be!

Comes the dread message of the winds and waves,
	From tropic shores the wail of anguish riven
From desperate souls that will not be the slaves
	Of any tyrant ’neath the vault of heaven;
The cry of mothers by ensanguined graves!

Tears, though of blood, full of swift heart appeal—
	Would dazzled eyes see only fields of gold:
While such as I can only think and feel, 
	Who listens to Great Freedom’s mandates old?
What strife victorious would relent to heal?

I am not free while they my dark, skinned brothers
	Their rightful freedom nobly strive to win;
While sordid greed their aspiration smothers,
	The patriot’s aim denounce as mortal sin;
Who dares be happy through the loss of others ?

’Neath prison walls I wait the gleaming token
	Of blest deliverance eye and heart shall greet;
The healing touch upon the heart nigh broken,
	God’s messenger of peace with hallowing feet!
Who brings the ringing word of Freedom spoken!
					CORA WILBURN,
	EX.
