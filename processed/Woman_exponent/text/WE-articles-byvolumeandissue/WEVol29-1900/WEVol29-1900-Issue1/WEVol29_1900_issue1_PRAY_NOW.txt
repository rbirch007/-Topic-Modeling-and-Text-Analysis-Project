PRAY NOW.
When by thy busy tasks oppressed,
	There riseth in thine hour of care,
A whisper “from thy labors rest,
	And lift thy soul to God in prayer,”
Delay not till thy work is done;
	To cross the line that separates,
Earth’s climes from heaven; for they are one;
	In faith pray now, thine angel waits,

Earth’s greatest works, by sea or land,
	Are set to time, from place to place,
And in obedience strictly planned,
	The mighty forces run their race.
But no mischance or accident
	E’er wrecks the course, or e’er belates,
His work or messenger He sent,
	To thee, pray now, thine-angel waits. 

For wasted chance, for faith too slow,
	Life’s harvest yields, but what we earned;
And desolate at last will know
	Where we from waiting blessings turned.
	In weakness, need, or triumph’s hour, 
Though trials crush on joys estate;
	Near us His watchful, loving power;
Fear not, pray now, thine angel waits.
	AUGUST JOYCE CROCHERTON.
a, July 6, 1900.
