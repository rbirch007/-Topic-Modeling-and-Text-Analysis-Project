‘WHAT IS SO RARE AS A DAY IN JUNE."
To Mrs. Pallas Eliza Clark.
BY L. M. H.
Fond greetings and congratulations,
Sister, beloved, my own!
On this anniversary,
That counts thee eighty-one.
This day of bright, glad summer,
Radiant, “Merrie June,”
Arrayed in pristine beauty,
Laden with sweet perfume.
On mountain, hill and valley,
Queen June is holding sway,
All nature’s forces rally,
In gorgeous array,
Grand musicales are given,
In melody of song, 
That echoes are repeating,
And joyously prolong. 
Hearts now are thrilled with music,
Wakened anew to praise. 
The magical enchantment,
Of glorious June days,
Out from her groves and bowers,
With offerings we have come,
Of fairest, sweetest flowers,
To crown thee eighty-one! 
We wreathe thy brow with garlands, 
Perennial in bloom,
Fadeless flowers from gardens,
Of love’s eternal June.
Time lead thee kindly onward,
Through twilight’s eventide, 
Into a perfect morning,
Of love-light glorified.
