			GRACE ANDERSON, Sec.

	At the meeting of the U. W. P. C.,
Monday evening, July 30, the president,
Mrs. Aimiee C. Schiller, being absent,
Honorary President E. B. Wells was called
to the chair by unanimous request. Prayer
was offered by Dr. Romania B. Pratt.
Miscellaneous business was considered,
Field Day being one of the items and Mrs.
Wells by permission made a few remarks
regarding women having suitable repre-
sentation in political matters and on the
State tickets, especially in educational
affairs. She also thought the club should
have current events each month, consider-
ing the present condition of the world.
	Mrs. Rhodes stated that the Press Club
was the only one of the clubs that took any
part in the Domestic Science class recently.
Mrs. Ruth M. Fox read a beautiful poem of
Eliza R. Snow’s. Red Pottage, by Mary
Cholmondley was discussed by the club,
Mrs. Emma J. McVicker opening the dis-
cussion.
	Mrs. Ella W. Hyde was appointed to
give current events at the meeting in
August. Papers will be given by mem-
bers upon the Chinese situation and
Woman’s International Congresses at the
Paris Exposition.
	Adjourned until August 30. Dismissed
with prayer by Mrs. McVicker.
			RUTH M. FOX, Asst. Sec.
