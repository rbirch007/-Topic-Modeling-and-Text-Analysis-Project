CANADA.
	The Alberta Stake Relief Society met in
conference at Cardston, May 12, 1900,
Stake President Mary L. Woolf presiding,
Meeting opened by singing “Come, come
ye Saints.” Prayer by Counselor Jane
Hinman. Singing, “How firm a founda-
tion.” Roll called, fourteen officers present.
Minutes of forenoon session of previous
conference were read.
	President M. L. Woolf rejoiced to see so
many young mothers with their babes
present. Felt that the minutes just read
were full of inspiration. We must culti-
vate the spirit of God continually, that our
influence may always be felt. Knew the
Lord would bless us and we would feel
doubly paid for the little sacrifices made to
attend our conference.
	President Sarah B. Daines, of Cardston,
felt the work of the Relief Society was a
noble mission for the daughters of Zion.
Reported the Cardston society. The sisters
are united and faithful in their duties, but
the attendance at meeting was not so large
as she desired.
	President Elizabeth Webb, of the
Mountain View society, reported their
society as being in good condition. The
sisters are united and trying to do their
duty. She had enjoyed her visit in Utah
very much, had seen the hand of the Lord
made manifest to a great extent in the heal-
ing of the sick.
	Counselor Eva Hansen reported the
Aetna society as being in an improved con-
dition, although scattered. The sisters are
united and energetic in their duties.
