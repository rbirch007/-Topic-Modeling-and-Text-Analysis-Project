A WONDERFUL INVENTION
	“Man’s work is from sun to sun
But a woman’s work is never done.”
	Woman has been called the weaker vessel
and yet comparing her labors with those
her companion man, most people
agree that she has the greater burden to
carry. When he starts for work in [-]he
morning, hers has already begun; when he
returns at night, hers has not ended. And,
therefore, we think that any one who can
make life less arduous to her is bene-
factor to the world at large. One of our
young sisters, Mrs. Dora B. Rancher, has
invented a wonderful system of dress cut-
ting which entirely obviates the nec[---]ity of
measurements. This simple little instr[--]
ment is adjusted on the form in less ti[---]
than measures can be taken in the ordinal
way. It is then removed and placed on the
goods to be cut out. No drafting of figur-
ing is required whatever. Some of the
leading dressmakers in Salt Lake City have
used it and are delighted with it.
	This talented little lady is the daughter of
Brother Hyrum Bigelow and Sister Martha
Meecham Bigelow, formerly of Provo,
Utah. For nearly two years she has labored
indefatigably to perfect her invention,
which will soon be upon the market. The
Adjustable Dressfitter cannot fail to make
a mark in this age of progress.
	Our sisters will do well to give it their
careful consideration when they are visited
by her or her agents. Orders can be left at
the Woman’s Coop, 44 Maine St.
