CHRISTMAS REVERIES. 
1900.

The tremulous pulse of the universe
Throbs with "peace and good will to man," 
The millionaire opens his swollen purse,
Bestowing with bounteous hand,
While "Aids” and "Charities” swarm about,
In this noble work for the poor,
And we hear the merry childrens’ shout,
As they bound from the schoolhouse door, 
Off to their homes for a week of rest,
No studies nor books to annoy—
Entering sports with vigorous zest,
Think only of holiday joy.

The rich and the poor alike now plan
For their childrens greatest delight,
Their bank account or wages scan
While weary mothers work at night—
That none may be found that joyous morn
Without a stocking more than filled;
The table wel spread, the hearth made warm,
For the hungered, tired and chilled.
Many mysterious parcels hid
With warnings and shaking of head,
Of that trunk none must open the lid;
Each child must go early to bed.

How many dainty stitches enwrought
On trifles, the best one can give—
Interwoven with purest, most loving thought 
Which make the inanimate live,
Breathe to the heart more eloquent words
Than pen or tongue ere could express!
Music that’s sweeter than songs of birds,
Benedictions ever to bless—
Making us feel the greatest and best
Of all of God’s mercies to earth
Is Friendship and Love! frankly expressed,
How holy and grand are their worth!

While the tremulous pulse of the universe
Is throbbing with "peace and good will,”
Thus my soul is replete with reveries sweet,
Brightest hopes my being to thrill;
That the waning days of this century
May carry all doubt from the world;
That love among men forever may reign,
With the banner of peace unfurled,
The Millennial year be ushered in, 
That thousand years of love and peace,
When earth made clean, and free from mortal sin,
And souls from pain have sweet release.
