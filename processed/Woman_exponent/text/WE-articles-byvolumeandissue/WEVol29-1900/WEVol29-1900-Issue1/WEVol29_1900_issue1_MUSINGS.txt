MUSINGS.
I stood in the Valley of Mesa, 
	Neath palm and clustering vine,
Then gazed on the beauties of nature,
	My thought most near the Divine; 
With orange groves breathing their fragrance
	Roses exhaling their sweets
The chirping of birds in the distance,
	Making earth’s gladness complete.

My being was thrilled with the splendor,
	My soul enrapt with an awe,
Earthly joys I seemed to surrender,
	To Heaven’s superior law;
And e’en while I lingered in musing,
	A glorious mantle was spread,
O’er the world, a halo diffusing,
	As promise to those long since dead.

That glow that comes after sunset,
	Ere shades of night shroud the earth;
Those pale purple mists, soft and mellow,
	Assure new day and new birth.
Our hearts keep time in perfection,
	In rhythm to nature’s sweet song,
We dwell on the great Resurrection-
	Ask why this struggle prolong ?
