R. S. REPORTS.
	The Stake presidency of the Relief Soci-
ety of Bannock Stake, President Mary A
Call, Counselors Sarah M. Call and Rosa
B. Knowles, and Secretary Hannah C.
Hatch, have made their annual visit through
the Stake, their starting point being the
Lund ward. President Letitia Banks re-
ported the society as being in a flourishing
condition, the teachers making their regu-
lar monthly visits without fail and keeping
an accurate account of the articles received,
so that in any case their books could be re-
ferred to as being reliable.
	The visitors each bore a faithful testi-
mony and gave some excellent advice, the
main subjects being the storing of wheat,
fasting and prayer and also a few words to
mothers on the rearing of their children.
	We were entertained at Sister Bevins.
	The two next wards visited were
Thatcher and Cleveland. The attendance
in both wards was very slim owing to the
prevalence of a contagious disease
among the people, although the disease is
almost abated, there being no new cases.
We held meeting in both wards and gave
some good instructions to the attending
sisters. The next meeting was held at
Mound Valley. The spirit of God was
manifest in the meeting. The visitors
were kindly treated and spent the night at
Sister Barbara Williams.
	The next place visited was Trout Creek,
where we lunched with President Mary
Jensen, then held meeting in the meeting
house, where the good instructions that
were given were kindly received.
	The next place was Grace. We traveled
late to get there, in wind and dust. We
were kindly entertained by the Relief
Society president, Sister Rich. We had a
better attendance there than in the previous
