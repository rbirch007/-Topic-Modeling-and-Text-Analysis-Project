A QUIET WEDDING IN JUNE.
	On Wednesday, June 27, 1900, a very
quiet wedding was solemnized in the Salt
Lake Temple, Bishop John R. Winder
officiating. The bride and bridegroom
were Miss Mabel Tamson McBride, formerly
of Fillmore, and Mr. John Phillip Tate,
of Tooele. The only friends present ex-
cept the official witnesses, were Mrs. E. B.
Wells and Mrs. E. J. Stevenson.
	Miss McBride had been with the writer
for some months and had rendered valuable
assistance in the office work of the
EXPONENT, making herself quite familiar
with its details. Previously she had taken
the training for the kindergarten and had
been quite successful in the work be-
fore coming to Salt Lake. Here she
taught for the L. D. S. College in the
Social Hall, but the school being discon-
tinued on account of unavoidable circum-
stances, she sought other employment.
	The young couple became acquainted
while attending school at the Brigham
Young Academy, in Provo. When the
country’s call was made for troops from
Utah, Mr. Tate was enrolled as a member
of Battery C. and remained with it until
the company was mustered out by orders
from headquarters. He is a young man of
excellent character and habits and good
business ability. Mr. and Mrs. Tate ex-
pect to reside permanently in Tooele.
	The EXPONENT offers its heartiest con-
gratulations to these young people just
entering upon the matrimonial voyage, may
the choicest of heaven’s blessings attend
them on their way and their bark glide past
the danger shoals and dark places in safety
and be guided always wisely in the direc-
tions to secure peace and the riches of
eternal life.
	I do believe who love hath known,
	Or sublime friendship’s purest, highest tone,
	Hath tasted of the cup of ripest bliss,
	And drank the choicest wine life hath to give.
	Hath known the truest joy it is to live,
	What blessing rich or great compared to this?
