U. W. P. C.
	The regular meeting of the U. W. P. C.
was held in the EXPONENT office, April 30,
1900, the president in the chair. Opening
prayer by Dr. Romania B. Pratt. Quota-
tions from Ruskin. A communication say-
ing that badges could be obtained by mem-
bers of the club was read; also a communi-
cation from the General Federation con-
cerning our dues. It was decided by the
club that since we have withdrawn from
the General Federation our dues will not be
sent. A communication was read concern-
ing our delegate to Milwaukee. The club
decided to send no delegate. The presi-
dent reported from the club council. The
council asked the club ladies of the city to
pay ten cents each to bring some noted
lecturer here to the next Annual State
Federation to be held in October. Mrs.
Emma J. McVicker spoke in favor of hav-
ing some one come. Dr. R. B. Pratt moved
we pay the ten cents; Ruth M. Fox
seconded—carried. The subject of estab-
lishing a free Kindergarten was also re-
ferred to, after which the program was
carried out as follows:
	Bessie Shirley gave a talk on mines and
mining and a chain discussion was entered
into by all members present on the best way
to obtain obedience from children.
