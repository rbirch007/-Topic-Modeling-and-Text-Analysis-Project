			SARAH G. B. SNOW.

    Again the grim reaper, death, has been in our
midst to summon one of the pioneer women of
Utah to her reward, Sister Sarah Gledhill Broad-
bent Snow, departed this life October 2, 1900. at
the residence of her son-in-law, Bishop John Das-
trup. The cause of death was heart failure, super-
induced by stomach trouble.
    Deceased was the daughter of Edward and Petty
Gledhill and was born March 31, 1833, in Oldham,
Lancashire, England, She embraced the Gospel


about 1850, and married Edward Broadbent in
1853. They emigrated to Utah in 1855, shared
with the Saints the hardships of the “grasshopper
war” and other times of scarcity incident to early
life in the valley. In 1863, while living at Gunni-
son, Sanpete Co., her husband was drowned in the
Sevier river, leaving her with two small children,
one of whom soon followed the father. In 1865
she married Bernard Snow, by whom she had two
children, one of them dying in infancy. Her
career has been a varied one, she living in many
towns of the State, where she has won friends for
herself by her integrity to the principles of the
Gospel, She came to this ward in 1879, and has
since been connected with the labors of the ward.
She was president of the Primary Association, she
also held the office of treasurer of the Relief Soci-
ety, and head teacher of the same. In 1896 she
was chosen counselor to President A. M. Dastrup,
of the Relief Society, which position she held at
the time of her demise.
    She leaves two children, seven grandchildren,
one sister and three brothers, besides numerous
other relatives and friends to mourn her deaʇh.
