OBITUARY.
SARAH WILLIAMS DAW.
It is with feelings of sorrow that we record the
death of Sister Sarah Daw, wife of Isaac Daw,
and daughter of George and Mary Williams, who
peacefully passed away at her home in Cedar
Fort, Utah Co., Utah, April 11, 1900, of Bright’s
disease, aged sixty-six years and twenty days.
She embraced the Gospel in England and emi-
grated to Utah in the year 1856, crossing the plains
in Captain Millen Atwood’s handcart company.
Deceased was married to Isaac Daw the same
year that she arrived in Utah, they lived at West
Jordan for six years, then settled at Cedar Fort,
where she resided till the time of her death. She
was the mother of seven children, two sons and
five daughters and thirty-five grandchildren. Sis-
ter Daw held the office of director and teacher in
the Cedar Valley ward Relief Society at the time
of her death. 							to
We her colaborers deem it a pleasant duty
testify to her faithfulness in the Relief Society.
RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.
Whereas, God has seen fit to take unto Himsel
our beloved director and teacher, Sister Sarah W.
Daw,
Resolved, That we mourn in her death the loss
of a faithful worker, a comforter in the hour of
trouble, devoted wife and mother and a true
Latter-day Saint.
Resolved, That we esteem her faithful labors
worthy the emulation of every member of our
society.
Resolved, That we sympathize and mourn with
the aged and bereaved husband, and the children,
grandchildren and friends.
