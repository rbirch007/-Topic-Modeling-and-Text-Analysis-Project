    By this dear day’s fair guest, now Seventy-nine.

Beloved Bathsheba, our sister, mother,  
    ’Tis not for any worldly rank you claim;
But for a greater cause than any other—
    You follow Christ and magnify His name.

You do His works and have done strictly, purely,
    These seventy-nine long years, your whole life
	through;
And we your sincere friends are honored surely,
    In doing honor unto one like you.
-----
	*A shower of rain continued all day.

[Column 2]
Not only we who have this priceless pleasure,
    Of greeting you with greetings true and fond,
But all the Saints your name revere and treasure,
    Not here alone, but in the world beyond.

Joseph and Hyrum are anticipating,
    With thousands more, the happy, joyous scene,
When President George A. Smith now calmly
waiting,
Shall, welcome home his bride, his priestess,
	queen!

But, oh, “come down,” we’ll not go off in
	raptures;
    This world is good enough for you to stay, 
Long, long, ere that dear hand your spirit cap-
	tures—
