ANNIE CHRISTINA ZUNDEL. 
Sister Annie Christina Zundel was born July 31,
1811, at Reibach, Oberent, Denburg, Hessen,
Darmstadt, Germany. She was the daughter of
John and Margaret Latenshlager. She came to
America about the year 1830, located in Pennsyl.
vania, was married to John Zundel in 1831, em-
braced the Gospel in the year 1840, and was bap-
tized in Nauvoo. She also received her endow-
ments there, she. with her husband being so
privileged, to be one of the first eight couples
chosen to have their endowments in the Nauvoo
Temple.
She was a benevolent woman who loved to give
to those that were in need. She left Nauvoo with
the Saints at the time of the expulsion. She
traveled with the Saints and shared in their perse-
cutions. Soon after leaving Nauvoo her health
failed. During her travels she laid four of her
young children to rest, some of them by the road-
side. In 1850 she and her husband gave their
property, consisting of one wagon, two yoke of
cattle and some monev for the emigration, and
they stayed and worked for another team.
She came to Utah in 1852, her husband was sick
the entire journey. They came to Salt Lake City
in September and he passed behind the veil in
November, leaving her with a family of eight
children, the eldest being eighteen years of age
and the youngest six months.
She came to Payson at the time of the move in
1858, where she has lived since. She always loved
to converse on the principles of the Gospel. She
was ever true and faithful to the gospel of our
Lord and Savior, and is now gone to rest. She
passed away on the 7th of February, 1901, being
89 years, 6 months and 7 days old.
