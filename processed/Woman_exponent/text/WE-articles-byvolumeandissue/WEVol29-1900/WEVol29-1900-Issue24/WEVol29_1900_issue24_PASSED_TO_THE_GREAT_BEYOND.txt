PASSED TO THE GREAT BEYOND.

Mrs. Annie Barlow, the wife of Patriarch Israel
Barlow of Wood’s Cross, died April 26, 1901, of
jaundice and liver trouble, after an illness of many
weeks. She was born in England August 8, 1843,
and was baptized into the Church of Jesus Christ of
Latter-day Saints, while in her teens, and came to
the valley of the mountains, shortly after, crossing
the plains in wagons with ox teams, and passed
through manv trials of early pioneer life.
She became acquainted with Israel Barlow, and
they were married on April 26,1863. She
therefore died on the anniversary of her thirty-
eighth wedding day.
She was a loving and faithful wife and devoted
mother and leaves a husband and ten children,
four sons and six daughters to mourn her departure,
all of whom are married but one daughter and two
sons, one son being on amission at the time of her
death.

[Column 2]
The sisters of the Relief Society feel it a great
loss, for she was an honest, energetic, faithful
president and it will be hard to fill her place.
The funeral services were held at the West Ward
meeting house, Monday, April 29, 1901. The
house was beautifully decorated in white with
potted flovers fora background. The floral tributes
were many and beautiful.
The speakers were Brothers C. R. Jones, J.
Argyle, Sr., Patriarchs Edward Powel and Tole-
man, President Hyrum Grant and Bishop L. M.
Grant. They all spoke highly of our beloved sister
and president and encouraged her children to fol-
low in her footsteps, be faithful and true to their
covenants,and they would meet their mother again.
