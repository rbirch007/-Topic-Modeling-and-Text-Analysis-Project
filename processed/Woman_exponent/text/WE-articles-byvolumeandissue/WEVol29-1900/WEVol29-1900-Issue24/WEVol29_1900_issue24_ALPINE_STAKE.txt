		ALPINE STAKE.

	The first quarterly conference of the
Relief Society of Alpine Stake was held in
the meeting house at American Fork, on
Friday, April 26, 1901, President Jemima
Durrant presiding. On the stand were
President Jane S. Richards, Sister Julia P.
M. Farnsworth and Rebecca Standring, all
of the General Board. The officers of the
Alpine Stake were all present and all the
presidents of the different wards.
	Meeting opened by singing, “Come,
come, ye Saints.” Prayer by Emma J.
Featherstone. Singing, “Redeemer of
Israel.”
	The secretary read a report taken at the
General Conference of the Relief Society.
	President Jemima Durrant made some
opening remarks. Said her mission had
hitherto been with the young ladies, but
was assured that now the Lord would bless
her in her present duties and calling. Vice-
President Martha Webb reported American
Fork Society. Pleasant Grove was re-
ported by President Sarah Foutz; Linden
by President Martha Woolley. President
Rebecca Standring was always proud to re-
port Lehi. They have 302 members and
more joining, all willing to work in this
great cause. They paid their ten cent fund,
they also have 1,687 bushels of wheat, hold
their meetings every two weeks, and some-
times take their meetings to the scattered
Saints. Prayed that all might be prepared
to give a righteous account.
	President Jane S. Richards said she was
pleased to be with us today and to see so
many present. Hoped the sisters would be
paid for coming, for it is the Lord’s work
we are engaged in and we agreed to come
and do it. We are here to stay and the
Lord has a care over us. He has made the
country and everything beautiful and if we
have a work assigned us we should try to
do it in the best possible manner. One of
