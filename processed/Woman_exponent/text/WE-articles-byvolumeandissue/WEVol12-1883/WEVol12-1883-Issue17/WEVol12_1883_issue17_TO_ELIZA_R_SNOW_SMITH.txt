TO ELIZA R. SNOW SMITH.

What offering shall we bring
	That shall be fitting to this day!
Not merely ceremonial words
	That chill the warmth in what we'd say.

Nay, time hath caught away the veil
	Custom oft weaves as a disguise,
She better loves the simplest speech
	Wherein the heart's true meaning lies.

Earth—jewels rich and rare?
	The gift would fail its sweet intent;
To win those that in heaven shine
	Her whole life's labors have been spent.

Then shall we bring to her
	A wreath of fame, her hymns to praise?
Nay, this were little, for her brow
	So long hath worn the poet's bays.

Could some one wreathe in song
	A loftier theme than e'er was sung?
Nay, she hath heard harmonies roll
	Too rapturous sweet for mortal tongue.

A people's blessing, then,
	Upon her well loved name.
Yes, bring her this, for well she knows
	Her Father gives the same.

His people's greeting comes
	Throughout our land, from o'er the tide,
From out the hearts of faithful Saints
	Responsive echoes far and wide.

Thousands whom she hath met
	But [---], within a sacred place
Hold in sincerest, fondest love
	Remembrance of her name and face.

The cycle of her years,
	Like a rich golden cord, doth hold
Those precious works, whose value are
	Only by tongues celestial told.

Peace, joy, attend her path!
	May this day, through returning years,
Find every link in friendship's chain
	Around her in immortal spheres.
