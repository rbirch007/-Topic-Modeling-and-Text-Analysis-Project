AN OVATION.

	On Monday, January 21st, Sister Eliza R.
Snow Smith, President of the Latter-day Saint
Women's Organizations, received such an ova-
tion as is seldom accorded to a woman. The
party met in the Social Hall at 5 p.m , and the
exercises began about six o'clock. President
John Taylor made some preliminary remarks
suitable to the occasion, and congratulated Sis-
ter Eliza on having arrived at the age of four
score years. A good spirit seemed to rest upon
all assembled to pay this tribute of respect to a 
veteran mother in Israel, and the widow of the
Prophet Joseph Smith.
	Prest. George Q Cannon took charge of the
meeting. Singing by the company, "O, My
Father." Prayer by Prest. George Q Cannon.
Address of welcome, by Sister Zina D. H.
Young; Reading, an original poem, by Mattie
Horne, composed for the occasion by Emily H.
Woodmansee; Song, by Bishop O. F. Whitney,
and remarks by Apostle Erastus Snow and Wil-
ford Woodruff. President A. M. Cannon, Prest.
Joseph F. Smith and Prest. George Q. Cannon
interspersed with songs and readings. A basket
of the choicest and most delicate flowers 
and vines was presented to the honored lady
from the ladies of the committee, and a hand-
some and fragrant bouquet from Sister M. M.
Barratt.
	We publish in this issue, the poem read by
Miss Mattie Horne, and also the poem by Sister
Crocheron, ready by Mrs. Susa Young Gates, and
the address by Dr. Romania B. Pratt.
	We add our sincere congratulations to that of
her many friends, and trust that she may live
until she is satisfied with life, in the full posses-
ion of every faculty of mind and body.
