WHAT IS LIFE?
				-------
	What is life, is it all sunshine, is there any
who can say, "I know no sorrow?" Some live
to dress, eat, drink and be merry, but life to us
would be dreary with doubts of an eternity.
Some may possess wealth, yet it cannot keep
the loved ones from the grave, or make children
loving and obedient, or dispel the dark clouds
that are destined to pass over their horizon. If
wealth will not fill our lives with sunshine then
should we murmer at our lot? Should we sigh
for that we cannot get, and fill our lives with
discontent, and employ all our time and talents
in following the prevailing fashion, and let our
hearts grow full of pride and vanity, and for-
get what life is for? No! Life is a school, as
many express it. Then let us try with as much
energy as if we were studying to get an educa-
tion, try to train ourselves in the fear and ad-
monition of the Lord. If there is anyting
that will bring us life eternal, and make this
life pleasant, it is the Gospel; wealth cannot
purchase it, yet it is within the reach of all.
Life is too short to be wasted in frivolity. We
should improve our time while we do live, and
do all the good we can, for if we waste our time
and talents we will regret it in the life to come.
Life is a stage, and men and women are actors
on it, says Shakespeare, and when we pass off
from this stage of action, we will appear on an-
other; and happy shall we be if we have per-
formed our part well while here. May the
Lord help us so to do. Amen.           D.C.
	Springville, 1833
