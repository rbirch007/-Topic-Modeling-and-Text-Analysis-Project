MORAL COURAGE.

	What is moral courage? Knowing what is
right and daring to do it in the face of all the
world, is what I call moral courage. And the


person who lacks this is lacking in that princi-
ple that makes the man or woman. For with-
out the courage to calmly pocket the sneers of
evil-doers we will never amount to anything.
The persons that can do this and remain true
to themselves have one of the noblest elements
of heroism.
	That independence based upon thoughtful-
ness is needed by the young people of to-day
more than anything else. We, as young people,
are not stern enough. We lack the moral
courage to say, no, in the proper time and
place. We are too easily influenced to do
what we know to be wrong. There are some
people who say and do (and I believe think)
as the majority, because they have not the cou-
rage to investigate for themselves. I have no
patience with such a class of beings. If we
are intelligent beings and have any brain, let
us use it, and by using it improve it; in short,
let us think and act for ourselves. We as indi-
viduals are held accountable for our acts and
doings (not the "majority"). Let us first find
out what is right and then have the moral cou-
rage to do it at all times and in all places, ir-
respective of the opinions of the "majority"
of the world.		CONSTANCE WILDWOOD.
	Nephi.
