ANOTHER FAITHFUL ONE DEPARTED.

 DIED, at Logan, October 21st, 1883, Sister Ann H.
Davis, wife of David W. Davis. Deceased was born at
Pendaren, South Wales, 5th of March, 1823; was
baptized into the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day
Saints January 1849; emigrated to Utah 1854; moved
from Bountiful, Davis County, to Logan at its early
settlement in 1859. Although disabled in her limbs
she was always an active worker in the Relief Society
in the ward she lived in from its organization, and al-
ways ready to assist the sick and afflicted in time of
need. A faithful wife and mother in all the trials
and privations of the early settlement of this valley,
and to her death a faithful Latter-day Saint. She was
never known to complain of her situation in life, al-
though her family were depending, to a great extent,
upon her industry with her needle, yet like a true Saint
she would lay aside her own work and attend to that of
the Relief Society, which she did for many years. Sister
Davis was greatly loved by all who knew her.
	Her funeral was attended by a large number of friends
and acquaintances. Although the Relief Society has
suffered a great loss, yet it is her gain, for she fought a
good fight and kept the faith.
Sister Davis leaves a husband, three daughters and eight 
grandchildren, also a large number of friends to mourn
her loss.				CATHERINE WATTERSON.
								President R.S.
	3rd Ward, Logan, Oct. 25th, 1883.
