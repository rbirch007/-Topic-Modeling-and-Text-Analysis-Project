						SARAH J. TARBET.

	It is with a feeling of regret that we are called upon
to record the death of Sister Jennette Rollins. She was
born April 9th, 1860, and died at her father's residence,
Bishop James McKnight, in Minersville, Beaver County,
Oct. 25th, 1883.
	Deceased had been residing at the Cave Mine during
the summer, and was moving home; her little brother
was driving the team, the horses became frightened and
were unmanagagble; she was thrown on to a rack stake,
which penetrated her right leg above the knee, and
came out  her thigh; after six days of intense suffereing
she expired leaving three small children, father,
brothers, sister and a large circle of relatives and friends
to mourn her loss.
	Whereas, God has seen fit to call from our midst our
sister, who was greatly loved and respected by all for
her virtuous, true and noble character, and her faithful
and untiring labors in the Y.L.M.I Association.
	Therefore, be it resolved, that we, the members of 
the Y.L.M.I.A. of Minersville Ward, do deeply
mourn the loss of our beloved sister, Jennette Rollins
and that we condole with her children, father and rela-
tives in their great loss.
	Resolved, that these resolutions be spread upon our
records, and that a copy be presented to the bereaved
family, also a copy be sent to the WOMAN'S EXPONENT
for publication.    (Signed)
				LYDIA BLACKBURN, President.
				IDA HAMBLIN, Secretary.
