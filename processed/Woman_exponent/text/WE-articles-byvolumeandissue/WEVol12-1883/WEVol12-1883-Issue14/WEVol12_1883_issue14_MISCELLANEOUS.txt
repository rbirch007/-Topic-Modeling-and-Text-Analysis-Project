MISCELLANEOUS.

	Still I am learning.—Michael Angelo.

	We are shaped and fashioned by what we
love.—Goethe.

	Many a woman who does not know even the
multiplication table can "figure in society."—
Yonkers Statesman.

	Religion is reproached with not being pro-
gressive; it makes amends by being imperish-
able.—J. Martineau.

	The women of Washington Territory can
vote, and are equal before the law with their
husbands and brothers. There is great rejoic-
ing in consequence of the passage of the bill
granting the right of suffrage to women. A
grand ratification meeting was held. There
was "booming of cannon and ringing of bells."
Addresses of congratulation by noted people.
and letters and telegrams were read congratu-
latory of the success. The affair ended with a
magnificent banquet.
	We add our most sincere congratulations
also, and believe it is a decided step towards
the advancement of the Territory.
	Governor Newell, of W. T., signed the bill
granting the suffrage of women at 11:40 a. m.,
Nov. 22nd, 1883, with a gold pen, presented to
him for the purpose by the ladies of Olympia.
