LONDON.

	By the emigration of the late President,
Elizabeth Young, and one of her Counselors,
Susan Witpin, it became necessary that this
society shonld be reorganized, President Gar-
ner having notified the Conference President,
E. H. Nye, of the position of affairs, and he
attended the meeting of the branch on Sunday,
Oct. 21st, 1883, when a reorganization of the
society was effected. The following Elders
were likewise present and assisted Elder Pic-
ket: viz. Elders Clawson and Denney.
	Towards the close of the afternoon meeting
Prest. H. Garner made a few remarks relative
to the proceedings about to take place. Prest.
E. H. Nye then followed with a brief but in-
structive address appropriate to the occasion.
He said, sisters appointed to leading positions
in the church should believe in and teach all
the principles of the Gospel, not simply be-
lieving in some of them, and rejecting others.
	Elder Picket then followed in a similar
strain and pointed out that the sisters about to
be chosen to fill these vacancies should be the
best women in the branch. President Garner
then proposed Sister Mary Garner to be Presi-


dent of the Relief Society; Sisters Mary Wa-
terfall and Harriet Heath, Counselors; Sister
Ann Gill to continue to act as Secretary. This
was seconded in each case, put to the vote and
carried unaaimously. Sister Mary Garner was
set apart under the hands of Prest. E. H. Nye,
Sister Waterfall under the hand of Elder Pic-
ket, and Sister Gill under the hands of Elder
Clawson, Sister H. Heath not being present.
Prest. H. Garner said it gave him great satis-
faction and even joy that the several names
he had submitted had receieved the entire ap-
proval of Prest. Nye and the rest of the breth-
ren. He then proposed that the society thus
organized should hold their first meeting Oct.
28th, seconded by A. R. Clark, carried unani-
mously.	ALEXANDER R. CLARK,
	Branch Secretary.
