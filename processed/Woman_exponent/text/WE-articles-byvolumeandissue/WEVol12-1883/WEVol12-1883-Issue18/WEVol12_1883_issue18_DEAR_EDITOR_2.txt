DEAR EDITOR:
	In answer to yours of Nov. 30th we wish to say
we were very pleased to hear from you in con-
nection with Sister E. R. S. Smith, also to find
that we are not entirely forgotten in this far off
country, as we have almost thought sometimes.
Not hearing anything in the Exponent con-
cerning the Relief Society in this part; there-
fore we are pleased to get a few words of
counsel and instruction from you.
	It will be almost beyond our expectations to
have the pleasure of seeing any of the sisters
from head-quarters visit us here, as we are so
isolated that had it not been for the Deseret
News and our excellent little paper, the Ex-
ponent, we should almost fancy ourselves sep-
arated from the Church, but, however, we feel
like attending to our duties as best we can, and
as a rule union prevails in our midst, and all
are trying to do their part, as we wish our

[Column 3]
name in every good cause. You of course
know nearly our circumstances in hearing that
we are comparatively a poor people, and most-
ly young, but we are not forgotten by our
Heavenly Father, as we are blessed with
health and strength in our midst. We hope
we shall not have to send any patients to that
excellent institution the Hospital. We sent
two quilts, there one year ago last conference.
However, we feel like doing all we can in do-
nating for the Hospital, for we think it will
prove a great blessing to us as a people.
	We all join in sending our best wishes to
those who are interested in the welfare of Zion.
		Your sisters in the Gospel,
 		JANE M. WALTON, President,
		IRENE U. HASKELL, Secretary.
