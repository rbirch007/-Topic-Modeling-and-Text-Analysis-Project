CORRESPONDENCE.

	ST. JOHNS, Apache Co. Arizona, Jan 1884.
EDITOR WOMAN'S EXPONENT.
	I believe it is a long time since you have
heard from the sisters in this far away part of
the land. I think we are endeavoring to do the
best we can considering the circumstances
which surround us.
	Sister Lucinda Richey, President of our So-
ciety, is a good, kind, motherly woman, ever
on hand to  good in a quiet way. Our So-
ciety gave a social picnic party in the Assembly
Hall, on the evening of the 18th ult, which
was a complete success; all who participated en-
joyed themselves immensely. It was especially
planned for the enjoyment of the aged and
those who did not care much about dancing, as
there were other amusements, including sing-
ing, recitations, etc; altogether it proved to be
one of the most enjoyable events of the season,
owing to the untiring efforts and zeal of our
efficient committee, viz., Sisters Ella Udall,
Helena Jenson, Eliza Tenney and Emma Pat-
terson.
	Our Primary is progressing under the able
management of Sister July Bingham, who la-
bors unceasingly, in its interest; she, with the
help of her assistants, is doing a good work
among the children. They are preparing to
give an entertainment at the end of this quar-
ter. The Young Ladies’Association is also
well managed and prosperous.
	The Ladies Quarterly Conference of this
stake convened in this place on the—of
December, presided over by our Stake Presi-
dent, Sister Emma Smith, upon which occas-
sion we received much  good instruction from
President Jesse N. Smith, and others of the
leading brethren of the stake, as well as many
of the sisters, and we felt well paid for meeting
together.
	With best wishes for the continued success
of the EXPONENT, I subscribe myself,
	Yours truly.						K.
