ADDRESSED TO BISHOP O. F. WHITNEY.

Bright harbinger of Summer, scented May,
	Spring's fairest month, thrice welcome thou,
For thee the mavis tunes her sweetest lay,
	The linnet warbles on the hawthorn bough.
List, list, what music greets my ear,
	What sound reverberates through the dell,
Is it the crow of the chanticleer,
	Or matin chime of village bell?
Wake, wake, my harp, and join the song,
	And thou, my lute, assist the lay,
For Pheobus drives his steeds along
	To herald in the joyous May.
Bright Sol illumes the mountain crest,
	And morn her wings outspreads,
The swallow peers from out her nest
	Beneath the sheltering leads.
The dew from off the skylark's wing
	Like diamond sparks are thrown,
The rising makes the welkin ring
	With music all her own.
The buttercup with golden crest,
	The modest primrose pale,
And violet in azure drest
	Bedecks the fragrant vale,
And like the daisy, greets the day,
	Who wide her petals spreads,
As conscious of the breath of May
	They rear their perfumed heads.
Gay Flora's bud crowned nymphs are seen
	To scatter flowers with lavish hand.
Pomona come in mantle green
	To strew her fruit bloom o'er the land.
Gay Momus dons his cap and bells,
	And youth is decked in ribbons gay,
For mirth, and joy, and pleasure tells
	That this is blitbesome, smiling May.
Haste, haste, my friends, and join the throng
	For Pan has tuned his pastoral reed,
Terpsichore leads the dance along
	And trips it lightly o'er the mead.
Behold, 'tis Venus' chariot comes
	Drawn by a pair of turtle doves:
She pays a visit to our homes
	With all her train of dimpled loves.
And see, from out yon busy town,
	The boisterous crowd come rushin on,
The sternest face has lost its frown
	And bade dull care awhile begone:
The pole is hnng with garlands sweet,
	For lasses choose their queen to-day,
While youth and age together meet
	To celebrate the first of May.
