	RICHFIELD.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Report of R. S. conference of Sevier Stake
of Zion, held at Richfield, March 17th, 1883,
at ten a.m., Sister Elizabeth Bean presiding.
Singing. Prayer by A. K. Thurber. Singing.
Minutes and report of previous conference read
and accepted. Sister Bean made a few open-
ing remarks pertaining to our religion etc.
Seven branch Societies were reported as doing
well, being in a prosperous condition consider-
ing their circumstances. Sister T. Thurber
spoke of her visit among relatives and of doing
her work in the Temple, of the heavenly spirit
therein; bore a faithful testimony to the truth
of the Latter-day work. Sister Tyler, of Beaver, 
who is helping her husband in canvassing for
the History of the Mormon Battalion spoke;
mentioned that this, the 17th of March, was
the aniversary of the first organization of the
Relief Society, effected by Joseph Smith, in
Nauvoo, Ill. and said there was a great im-
provement manifest. Brother Thurber en-
couraged the societies, and said they were a
great help to the Bishops and Presidents; said
we must give God the praise for our progress.
Conference adjourned to two p.m. Singing.
Prayer by Bishop J. Wright.
	Afternoon session. Singing. Prayer by
President F. Spencer. Singing. Verbal re-
ports of six more Societies were given.
	Sister Bean, President Spencer, Bishop
Wright and Sister Heywood encouraged and
praised the sisters of the Relief Societies for
the good they had done; exhorted them to re-
main faithful and improve in all things. Gave
very good instruction and prayed the Lord to
bless us all. Sister Bean announced that Con-
ference would be adjourned till June 15th.
Choir sang, "Oh! my Father, Thou that dwell-
est." Benediction by Bro. Perkins.
	C. A. HUNT, Secretary
	A. L. BEAN, Assistant Secretary.
May 10th, 1883.
