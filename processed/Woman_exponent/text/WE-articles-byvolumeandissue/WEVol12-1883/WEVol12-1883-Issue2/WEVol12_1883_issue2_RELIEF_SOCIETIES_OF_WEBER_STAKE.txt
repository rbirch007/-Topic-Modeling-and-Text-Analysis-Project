RELIEF SOCIETIES OF WEBER STAKE.

	We had the pleasure of attending the confer-
ence in the Ogden Tabernacle, on Friday, June
8th, 1883. There was a large congregation pres-
ent. Mrs. Jane S. Richards, President of the
Society in Weber County, presiding. Mrs. Hat-
tie C. Brown, Mrs. Sarah Herrick, Counselors,
present. Mrs. Monta Harris, Secretary, Mrs.
Emily S. Richards, Mrs. Sarah S. Richards, Mrs.
Emily M. Shurtliff, Mrs. M. Critchlow, Mrs.
Adeline Belnap, Mrs. Mary Bird, Mrs. Martha
Bingham, Mrs. M. A. Wallace, and other officers
of the Societies. Among the gentlemen were
Hon. F. D. Richards, Probate Judge of Weber
County, Hon. L. W. Shurtliff, of the County
Court, Hon. D. H. Peery, Mayor of Ogden City,
Hon. F. S. Richards, member of the Constitu-
tional Delegation of Washington, D. C., Joseph
Hall, Esq., editor of the OGDEN HERALD, W.
G. Child, Esq., senior member of the mercan-
tile house of Child and Son, Bishop Sanford
Bingham, Bishop Robert Gale, Bishop E. Strat-
ford, Superintendent W. R. R. Stowell. There
were also present H. J. Newman, Simon Barker,
Joseph Robinson, William Barker, Samuel Glas-
gow, John Manning, Esqs., and other leading
citizens.
	After the usual exercises of singing and prayer,
short speeches were made by Mrs. Jane S.
Richards, Apostle F. D. Richards and Mayor
Peery; the last gentleman alluding to his visit
to Washington as a member of the Constitution-
al Convention, and his acquaintance with Mrs
Lockwood, the eminent woman lawyer.
	Mrs. Emily S. Richards then addressed the
assembly for a few minutes, and spoke eloquently
of the eminent lady lawyer, Mrs. Lockwood,
and gratefully of her labors in the suffrage work,
and in behalf of the women of Utah, after which
she read the address of Mrs. Lockwood.
