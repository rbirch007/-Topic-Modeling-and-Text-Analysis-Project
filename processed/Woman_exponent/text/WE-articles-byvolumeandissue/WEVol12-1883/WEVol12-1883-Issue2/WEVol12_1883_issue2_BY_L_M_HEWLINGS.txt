		BY L. M. HEWLINGS. 

[Affectionately inscribed to Dear Sisters and Friends
	who abide in the Beautiful Valley of Salt Lake.]

My thoughts they will go roaming
	With mixed sadness and delight--
On Fancy's wings they take me
	Over the mountain's height.
Far over the weird and fearful,
Over the strange and new, 
Till I reach the smiling valley
	Where hearts for me are true.

Fresh in its pristine beauty,
E'en as Eden seeming fair, 
With the same sweet odors laden, 
	Through all the balmy air.
Most glorious in its gleaming
	Of fruit and flower bloom,
While birds of brightest plumage
	With nature join in tune. 

Ten thousand rills are bearing
	Richest blessings as they go
Leaping down the cloud-capped mountains
	White with eternal snow.
Girdled 'round with mighty hills,
She sits a very Queen;
Polished by art--glad nature
	Has adorned with living green.

Thou peerless, charming valley!
From a desert wild brought forth;
O would that I could now recount
	The praise of toillng worth.
Could tell of those who toiled in faith,
With dauntless courage brave.
Until they found a resting place
	within the quiet grave.

waters * bitter to the taste 
Caill frosts and buring sands,
With persevering labor brought
	Fruitage from barren lands.
Where once roamed beasts and savage hordes
	O'er all the desert plain,
They tilled, and ploughed, and sowed the seed,
	That now yields golden grain.

Fierce plagues oft tried their hearts full sore 
Famine stalked through the land, 
Yet reared they huts * from out the earth
	Where now proud mansions stand. 
High places level'd, depths filled up,
Gorges and chasms dread
A mighty highway overleaps,
On which the nations tread. 

Thro' this once howling wilderness
	Of dreariness and blight,
They wrought by faith inspired by Him,
Who said, "Let there be Light."
By faith pre-visioned, oft they viewed
	This smiling  valley o'er, 
As Moses on Mount Nebo stood
	And saw fair Canaan's shore. 

They saw the solitary wastes
	Filled with abodes of men,
From north to south, from east to west,
All nations gather in. 
Cities and towns dot all the vale,
And commerce makes her way
O'er pulsing wires, with lighting speed,
Throughout the night and day. 

O wondrous change!  and passing strange
	That e'en thro' travail caome, 
This living light of beauty bright,
That covers all the plain!
I gaze enraptured on the scene
	With awe-inspired delight;
I meet and greet dear friends and true,
On this, my airy flight. 

Some dear forms lie there to rest
	Beneath the emerald sod,
While their freed spirits walk the hills
	And gardens of our God.
Softly the angels whisper,
Of things I cannot reach,
And thoughts float on in language
	Too grand to clothe in speech.  

Yet the joys of fancy's roaming,
Friends that I meet and greet,
The valley's bright adorning
	Forms a picture, passing sweet.
Atchison, Kansas, May, 1883.

* Alkaline.
* Small adobie houses.
