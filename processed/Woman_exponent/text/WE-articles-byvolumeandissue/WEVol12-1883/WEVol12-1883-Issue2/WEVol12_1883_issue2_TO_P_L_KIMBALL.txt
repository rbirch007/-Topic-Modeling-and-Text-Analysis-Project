TO P. L. KIMBALL.

I fain would plant one little flower,
	One immortelle for you,
To bloom within affection's bower,
	Dear friend so tried, so true.

Planted in gratitude and love,
	In memory of the past,
And may the light of heaven above
	Guard it unto the last,

That chilling blight may never fall
	To dim its luster bright—
But may it live until the call
	To bloom in realms of light,

Then find a place within the crown,
	Wrought by the hand divine,
To take the place of cross laid down
	At the feet of the Master, Thine.

'Tis enough for you and for me to know
	The links that together bind,
How we found them where bitterly teardrops flow,
	And dark clouds the sunshine blind.

How we found them too in the love of God,
	In the sanctified spirit of peace,
That comes when we meekly pass under the rod
	That is raised for the souls increase.

Then accept my flower though plain it be,
	We will call it the "Love of a heart,"
Brighter and gayer ones you may see,
	But its fragrance will never depart.

	HOPE.
