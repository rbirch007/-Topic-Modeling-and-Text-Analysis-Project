MAYDAY PARTY.
		PINE VALLEY, MAY 7TH, 1883.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Tuesday being the first of May the members
of the Primary Association met at the meeting
house with some of their parents, also the Bish-
op and Counselors, and spent a few hours in
singing and reciting. The President, S. M. 
Burgess, her first Counselor and the committee
made the affair a very pleasant one. The Presi-
dent called upon Bishop  F. W. Jones to
make a few remarks, to which he responded,
heartily expressing himself as well pleased
with the entertainment and also thought the
Primary Association was a great benefit in the
training and entertaining of the young. He
was followed by his second Counselor Wil-
liam Gardner, who spoke very encouragingly
to the children. The party then partook of a 
bounteous repast and all tendered their heart-
felt thanks to those who had labored so assidu-
ously to make their affair a pleasant one. Danc-
ing then commenced for the children, closing
at seven o'clock.	BENNILLA E. GARDNER,
				Secretary.
