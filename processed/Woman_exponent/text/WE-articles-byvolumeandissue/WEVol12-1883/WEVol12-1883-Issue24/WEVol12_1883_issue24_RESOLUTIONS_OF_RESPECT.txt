	SARAH C[-]OOK, 			ROSETTA STOCK, Secretary.

	It becomes my painful duty to announce the death of
a beloved sister and fellow laborer in the Relief Society
of Samaria, Oneida Co., Idaho. Her death took place
on the 21st of Feb., 1884.
	Hester Edwards, beloved wife of Dr. Edwards, and
daughter of John E. and Ruth Price, who was born Oct.
30th, 1843, in Aberysgyr Parish, Breconshire, South
Wales, was baptized at eight years old; immigrated to
this place March, 1875, after which her husband was
baptized into the Church. She filled the office of a 
teacher in the Relief Society for three years, was ap-
pointed Assistant Secretary, which office she filled
worthily. Her parents were the first settlers of this
settlement. 
	Her funeral procession was the largest of this place;
there were also many from Malad City. She was very
highly respected and beloved by all who knew her.
	She leaves a husband, a daughter fourteen years of
age, a sweet, baby girl three weeks old, relatives, and
host of friends to mourn her loss.
					  MARY D. DAVIS, President.
