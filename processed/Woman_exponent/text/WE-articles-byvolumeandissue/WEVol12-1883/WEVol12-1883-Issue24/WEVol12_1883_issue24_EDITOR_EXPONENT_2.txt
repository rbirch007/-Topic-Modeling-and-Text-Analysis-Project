EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I thought, perhaps, a few lines from this out of the
way place might find space in your valuable paper, as I
feel interested with my sisters who are laboring in the
cause of Zion.
	We are located on Goose Creek, a tributary of Snake
River, and have a thriving settlement. A great many
good Latter-day Saints live here. The health of the
people is good. We have had some very cold weather
this last winter, but very little snow in the valley.
	We had a Relief Society organized here Sept. 24th, 1882,
by Apostles Lyman and Smith, with the following
named officers: Sarah A. Snedaker, President; Louisa
Haight and Janett Whittle, Counselors: Prescilla Wor-
thington, Secretary; Breta Felt Treasurer.
	We are striving to do the best we can in our isolated
condition. By the proceeds of a picnic party, given by
the Relief Society, and other donations, we have been
able to send twenty-five dollars to the Logan Tem-
ple.
	Last spring the bretheren very kindly donated land,
and also planted it with grain. We have 200 bushels
of wheat on hand, which is but a little, but we all
have to creep before we can walk. We are trying to be
alive to our duties as Latter-day Saints.
	Every praying for the success of the EXPONENT, I re-
main, your sister in the Gospel of peace,
