DEATH OF SISTER EARL.

	Lois Caroline Owen was born Jan. 8th, 1818, Town of
Milton, Benningtion Co., Vermont. Moved with her
parents to Illinois 1826. Recieved the Gospel the first
time she heard it preached; was baptized by Sylvester
II, Earle 1838, whom she married Jan 28, 1839. They
gathered with the body of the Church, and shared in the
mobbings and drivings of the Saints, were with them
when expelled from Nauvoo: and shared in the hard-
ship of the journey to Winter Quarters, where she


took care of her three children, while her husband ac-
companied the pioneers to hunt a resting place for the
Saints of God, to which she came in 1848.  Remained in
Salt Lake City until 1861, when they moved to South-
ern Utah, settling in Pine Valley.
   Sister Earl was appointed President of the Relief So-
ciety of that place, which position she faithfully filled
as long as her health would permit.  Her deeds of ben-
evolence, self sacrifice, and integrity to the principles 
of the Gospel will ever furnish a pleasing thought to
those who cherish her memory.  She was the mother of 
ten children, had passed through a great deal of sick-
ness and trouble, having buried her husband and six
of her children.
   During the last few years of her life her health was
gradually declining, until she was prostrated, in which 
condition she lingered thirteen months; at times her
suffering being intense, until the 9th of March, 1884,
when she quietly passed off with a peaceful smile,
characteristic of her former life.  She leaves four child-
ren, several grandchildren, and a host of friends to
mourn her departure.					COM.
   Bunkerville, April 23rd, 1884.
