							SARAH B. LAYTON.

	DIED at Fish Haven, Bear Lake Co., Idaho, April
25th, 1884, of inflammatory Rheumatism, Huldah Hen-
rietta, beloved wife of J. W. Edwin Stock, in the thirty-
second year of her age. Deceased was born May 1st,
1852, at Provo, Utah, and was the daughter of George
B. and Huldah Teeple of Holden, Utah. She was the
mother of six children, three of whom are living, the
youngest being two weeks old at the time of her death
She leaves a large number of relatives and friends to
mourn her loss.
	Sister Henrietta was President of the Primary,
teacher in  Relief Society, and First Counselor in the
Young Ladies Association of this place, and has al-
ways been a faithful and concientious laborer in the
Church and different societies to which she belonged,
and all who knew her deeply feel her loss. She was a sin-
cere and true friend, a loving and affectionate mother,
and a noble and devoted wife.

       Dearest sister, though your pathway
	    Has been strewn with many thorns
	You have nobly won the victory,
	     To your rest you now have gone.

          Father, children, kindred friends,
	You will miss her lovely form;
	But a bright future lies before us
	At the resurrection morn.
