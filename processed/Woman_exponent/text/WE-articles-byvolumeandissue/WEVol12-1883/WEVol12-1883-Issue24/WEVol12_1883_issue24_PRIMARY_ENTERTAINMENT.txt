PRIMARY ENTERTAINMENT.
	The Primary Association of Plain City gave
an entertainment in the school house of that
place on the evening of April 18th, 1884. Af-
ter the usual opening exercises were over the
remainder of'the evening was spent in singing
songs, rendering recitations and dialogues.
Some of the most interesting exercises were
two recitations, entitled, “Don’t run in debt,”
and “Love, drink, and debt.” Also two dia-
logues, one entitled, “Brandy Drops,” and the
other representing a discourse between a Mor-
mon Elder, and. a minister. The evening’s en-
tertainment was brought to a close by the per-
formance of a short drama, entitled “Boots at
the Swan.” Altogether it was a pleasing af-
fair, and credit is due to those who arranged it.
It was gotten up for the purpose of helping a
little child to emigrate from the old country to
Utah
	The admission being small we were not able
to clear more than $10.50. We have very in-
teresting meetings, and have an average atten-
dance of about forty.
		SUSANNAH ROBINSON, President. 
		JOSEPHINE IPSON, Secretary.
