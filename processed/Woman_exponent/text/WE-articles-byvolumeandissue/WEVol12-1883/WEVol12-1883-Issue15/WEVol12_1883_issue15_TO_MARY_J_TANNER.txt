TO MARY J. TANNER

Lay not aside your harp, dear friend,
Its musical air waves afar,
Trem'lous with its low, sweet sound,
And other sad hearts stir
To nobler purpose, truer love.
If there's no bay for you now,
Yet angels, in their home above,
Weave a garland for your brow.

The poet merged in the woman, yes
A true poet still, I ween,
While a dear woman's tenderness
In the poetry sweet is seen.
And the light that was just ahead
Is reflected bright afar,
Lighting a path for others to tread,
Aye, the light of a guiding star.

A morning star for youth's glad spring,
To pilot a voyage of life;
A beacon light to the wayfaring,
Seen above sin and strife.
Your heart is heavy with life's great care,
Cherished hopes shattered and lost;
But should one crush a diamond rare,
He would treasure the diamond dust.

Those fragments of hope may brightly shine,
Like atoms of diamond dust,
When you have reached the hand divine,
And given to others a trust.
Then lay not aside your harp, dear friend,
Tho' you travel down life's hill,
'Twill cheer you on to your journey's end,
And vibrate for others still.
