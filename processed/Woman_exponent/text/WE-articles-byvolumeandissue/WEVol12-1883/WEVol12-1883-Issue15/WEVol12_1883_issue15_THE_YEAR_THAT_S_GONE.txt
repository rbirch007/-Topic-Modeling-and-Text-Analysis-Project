THE YEAR THAT'S GONE.

It has passed away to the other side
Of life's swift river's rolling tide,
With its cargo of smiles and bitter tears,
Of joyous hopes and trembling fears.

It has passed away and the freight has placed
Where the names of its owners are clearly traced,
Shall we blush to own it, oh, pause and think,
It awaits our coming; time's ships ne'er sink.

It has passed away, and, brothers dear,
What have ye sent with the bygone year?
Were deeds of honor and mercy thine,
Does charity's mantle thy stores entwine?

It has passed away, and, sisters mine,
What have we sent on the wings of time,
What have we stored in the harbor wide
On the other side of time's swift tide?

It has passed away, did it bear false pride
And vanity on to the other side,
With selfish deeds and slander vile
That turns to tears the angel's smile?

It has passed away, and we welcome now
The new born year with song and vow,
Of faith received and friendship strong—
May they remain while time flies on.

It has passed away, oh! may we strive
Our treasures to send that they may arrive
On eternity's shore and await us there
To encircle our brows with laurels fair.

	HOPE.
