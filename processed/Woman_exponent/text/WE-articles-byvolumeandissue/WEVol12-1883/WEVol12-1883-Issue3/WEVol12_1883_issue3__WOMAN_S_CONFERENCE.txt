WOMAN'S CONFERENCE.

	The quarterly Conference of Relief Society
of the Salt Lake Stake convened in the Four-
teenth Ward Assembly Rooms, in this city,
Friday, June 22nd, at ten a.m. and two p.m.,
Mrs. M. I. Horne presiding. After the usual
exercises verbal reports were given by the Presi-
dents of the several societies, and remarks suit-
able to the occasion were made by Sisters E. R.
Snow Smith, Zina D H. Young, M. I. Horne,
E. S. Taylor and others, calculated to comfort
and encourage the sisters in gaining spiritual
knowledge and intelligence.
	Saturday morning at ten a.m. the Conference
of the Primary Associations was held in the As-
sembly Hall, Mrs. Ellen C. Clawson presiding.
A large number of children from the several
wards were in attendance, and some exercises
rendered. The several Presidents reported
progress in their respective Associations. The
singing was particularly good.
	The Y. L. M. I. A. Conference of this Stake
of Zion was held in the Assembly Hall in this
city, Saturday afternoon, June 23rd, 1883, Mrs.
M. A. Freeze presiding. After singing, prayer
and singing inutes of previous conference
were read and accepted, and verbal reports
given from fifteen Associations. President
Freeze made a few enconraging remarks, fol-
lowed by Mrs. E. S. Taylor and Counselor Mat-
tie Horne. Sister Jane E. Molen of Cache
Stake spoke a short time, also Sisters Louie Felt
and E. B. Wells. Sister Maria Y. Dougall was
sustained by the Conference as Counselor to


President Freeze in place of Clara Y. Conrad,
deceased. Conference was adjourned for three
months. Singing and benediction.
