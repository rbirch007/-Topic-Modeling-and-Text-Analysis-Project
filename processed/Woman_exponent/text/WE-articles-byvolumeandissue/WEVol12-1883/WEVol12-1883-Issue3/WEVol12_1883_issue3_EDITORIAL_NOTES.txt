EDITORIAL NOTES.

	MRS. SARAH M. KIMBALL writes from
Belle River, St. Clair County, Michigan, that
she is well and finds much of interest there, but
she can better tell it after she returns
home than write it. However, she means
to write us a letter from New York, the home
of her childhood, after arriving there. Mrs.
Kimball has been to Nauvoo, at which place
many recollections crowded upon her. She
expects to visit Kirtland, where she was mar-
ried in the early days of the Church.


﻿THE History of Salt Lake City is to be writ-
ten by Edward W. Tullidge, by authority of
the City Council, at the request of a number
of influential citizens, both men and women.
The history is to be published in parts, magni-
ficently illustrated with steel plate portraits of
men and women of the Territory. It will also
be published in Tullidge's Quarterly Maga-
zine.
	THE Englishwoman's Review, for June, con-
tains a number of very interesting articles:
Preparations for the Reform Bill, Hospital
Management, Hysterical Old Maids, Second
Reading of the Deceased Wife's Sister Bill, Un-
derground Russia by Stepniak, Protection of
Girls, London School of Medicine for Wo-
men, Governess Association of Ireland, Club
for Working Girls in Soho, Hygienic Dress,
and other interesting matter.
