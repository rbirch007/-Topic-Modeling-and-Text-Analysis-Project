GOSHEN PRIMARY.
				GOSHEN, Sept. 22nd, 1883.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We held our fair on the above date, which
was a great credit to the children. They took
an interest in making many beautiful things
to adorn their persons and houses, and I think
it wonld be well to mention some of the arti-
cles made by our little ones, and also give you
an idea of the proceedings. Basket made by
Margaret Poulson, aged 10 years, rake, John
Poulson, 8 years; quilt, Elizabeth Burson, 11
years; bracket, Elizabeth Higginson; mat,
Annie Jaspersen, 8 years; bracket, Lydia Jas-
persen, 10 years; cushion, Eliza Dedds, 10
years; comb-case, Janet Jenkins, 10 years; veg-
etables, Hardy Jenkins, 8 years; hair switch,
Mary Laird; straw braiding, Josey Laird, 9
years; brackets, Elizabeth Standfiely, 10 years;
map drawing, Maggie Morgan; two cakes
made by two little girls about 8 years, also
needle work, flowers. knit and crochet lace, ti-
dies, a beautful nubia, nice mats of various
styles, and many beautiful things too numer-
ous to mention.
	There were also songs and recitations given
by the children, and we had a very pleasant
and interesting time together.
		SARAH ROBERTS, President.
		JULIA M. JASPERSEN, Secretary.
