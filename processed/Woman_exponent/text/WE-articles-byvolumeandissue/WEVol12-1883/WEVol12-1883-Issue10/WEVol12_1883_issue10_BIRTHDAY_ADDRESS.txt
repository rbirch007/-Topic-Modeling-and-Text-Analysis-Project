BIRTHDAY ADDRESS.
TO A YOUNG BROTHER ATTAINING THE AGE OF
			TWENTY-ONE.
	Another year has passed away.
And brought again your natal day;
And ended all minority
By ushering in majority.

The rolling years of life have run
And brought you up to twenty-one!
So now you enter life’s great mart
Summon'd to play your allotted part;
	And not in domino to play it,
But in your proper self essay it!
And truly you have well begun
The race or life, yes, well you’ve run.
You have been blest with parents kind,
Who've wisely trained your heart and mind,
And taught you how you might attain
That wisdom, which ’tis wealth to gain;
And God has had His eye on you,
And daliy watches all you do;— 
Lie ever passive in His hands,
Obeying all His wise commands;
Present your heart and brain to Him
To have, to hold them free from sin;
To strengthen, fertilize, and make
Them quick to bend, but never break.

You’ve entered by the Gospel door,
As Christ, your Savior, did before,
And now you may expect to meet
Not always roses at your feet!
For e’en the beauteous, gorgeous rose
Finds on its stem there ever grows
Thorns clustered round it thick and sharp,
A symbol of the light and dark:
To good and bad comes joy and woe,
A chequered life to all below:
But to a Saint of Latter-days
The sunshine comes in many Ways;
E’en in the storm there shines a beam,
A rainbow, with its prism gleam!
That comes so straight from God’s bright throne
It makes its brightness all our own.

The" Rod of Iron,” grasp it tight,
’Twlll put temptations all to flight;
Shut ears and eyes when such shall find you
And bid the Tempter get behind you!
Whatever shape he may appear in,
He does not always make us fear him
But as an angel fair and bright
He puts onr vigilance to flight.

Walk sotly in a prosp'rous day,
In Sol’s meridian burning ray
The shadow of a “Rock” we need,
And ’twill be there in very deed
If we will keep our, heart and eye
Upon the “Polestar” of the sky, 
That star so fixed will ne’er decline,
But in the darkest night will shine
Upon each Saint of Latter-days
Through all-the devious means and ways,
That often as a net is thrown
Around the feet of Heaven’s own!
And every Saint must surely meet
These serpent colls around their feet;
But silence keep—the fervent prayer
Will disentangle every snare:
And when’tis past—in looking back 
Along the woeful, dreary track,
In heart and soul you’ll kiss the rod
And say, “I see the hand of God!
And He it was who bore me up,
While drinking from that bitter cup!
Yes, God was with me, now I know,
Or I had sunk beneath that woe!
I rise from all a stronger man,
And heaven’s programme I can scan;
I’m wiser than I was before,
My mind is stronger than of yore;
I feel, and see, and know I am
A wiser and a better man I—”

I have not pictured blissful days,
You’ll meet them, sure, in many ways;
There’s much of good in store for you
If you are wise in al you do;
Pray, pray form wisdom!-- may it be
The ones great gift bestowed on thee!

For many a mind, both great and bright,
Has sunk involved in endless night
For lack of wisdom’s lamp to night
Her mighty halo round their head!
Wisdom in thought, in speech, in act,
To make success in life a fact!
All this you’ll need, my youthful friend,
Until life’s warfare has an end!

May heaven hold you by the hand,
That firm and faithful you may stand,
His son, His soldier, servant, priest;
Then in each one you will be blest;
And may this day return each year
Laden with joys your heart to cheer,
And friends and dear ones round you be,
Such are the prayers I pray for thee.
Accept the tribute I now send,
From one who is your faithful friend.
						HANNAH T. KING.
Salt Lake City.
