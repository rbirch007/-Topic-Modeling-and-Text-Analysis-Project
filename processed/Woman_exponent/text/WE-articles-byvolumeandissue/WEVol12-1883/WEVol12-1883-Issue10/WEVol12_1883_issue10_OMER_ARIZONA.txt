	OMER, ARIZONA.
DEAR EDITOR:
	We were first organized as a Relief Society
under the direction of P. J. Christofferson,
Jan. 1st 1879. We were then reorganized with
the assistance of Sister W. East, Stake Presi-
dent of Relief Society, Aug. 26, 1879, with
Sister Cecelia Hamblin, President; Mary
Skouson and Polly Greer, Counselors; Julia
Johnson, Secretary. A change was made in
the officers Aug. 24, 1881, with Sister Emily
Burke, President; Mary Skouson and Margrett
M. Dewit, Counselors; Mary B. Eagar, Secre-
tary; Ada A. Earl, Assistant Secretary; Celia
Jenson, Treasurer; and 29 members enrolled.
	The Society is now in a more prosperous con-
dition than ever before. Our Bishop and the
other brethren have kindly given us a lot, and
we have paid for the lumber to fence it; we
want to plant some mulberry trees, to see what
they will do in this country. We anticipate
building a house during the summer; we have
a very healthy climate and a good people; they
seem to be trying to live their religion and
build up the waste places of Zion. We are on
very friendly terms with our Mexican neigh-
bors; we can see a great difference in their
dress and customs since we came among them,
although none of them have embraced the Gos-
pel, yet there are some believing it.
	The EXPONENT comes to us like a dear old
friend to cheer and bless us; we prize it very
much in this far off land. Ever praying for
our sisters who are spending their time and
energies for the welfare of Zion, we remain
your sisters in the Covenant, E. J. Burk, Presi-
dent; Mary M. Eagar, Secretary; Ada A. Earl,
Assistant Secretary.
