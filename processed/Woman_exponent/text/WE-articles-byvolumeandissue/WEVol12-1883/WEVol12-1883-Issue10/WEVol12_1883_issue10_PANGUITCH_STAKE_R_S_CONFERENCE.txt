PANGUITCH STAKE R. S. CONFERENCE.
	The Quarterly Conference of the Panguitch
Stake Relief Society was held Aug. 31st,
1883, President Barbara Myers presiding.
There were present on the occasion Apostle
Erastus Snow, John R. Murdock, Prest. of the
Beaver Stake, Prest. J. W. Crosby and several
of the leading brethren.
	After singing and prayer minutes of the last
conference were read and approved, and verbal
and written reports were given. President
Barbara Myers said it was a very busy time,
but was glad to see so many out; circumstances
had prevented her visiting all the Stake. Ex-
horted the sisters to do all they could to teach
their children the principles of the Gospel.
Spoke of the storing of grain.
	Apostle E. Snow said he was pleased to meet
with the sisters, and also the brethren; spoke
of training the youth, felt to encourage sing-
ing and music to make home cheerful and en-
tertaining; not to give way to evil speaking,
but be ready to hold up the hands of the weak,
and those that are afflicted to give them joy.
	Prest. Murdock said it was with feelings of
gratitude he stood before us; did not know what
was calculated to do more good than these
meetings of the Relief Societies; felt to admire
the prospects we had before us; said it was the
small things that counted in the Kingdom;
that the sisters were the refining part of the
community; if we had intelligence it should be

[Column 2]

used; that the sisters had as much to do in
their sphere as the brethren, and one was not
without the other in the building up of the
Kingdom of God.
	Prest. J. W. Crosby Jr. spoke a few encour-
aging words; urged the secretary to get blanks
and get their reports up in proper shape; to be
careful how we censured others, and be gener-
ous in our feelings.
	Conference was adjourned for three months.
Singing. Benediction by Joseph L. Heywood.
	SARAH P. CROSBY, Secretary.
