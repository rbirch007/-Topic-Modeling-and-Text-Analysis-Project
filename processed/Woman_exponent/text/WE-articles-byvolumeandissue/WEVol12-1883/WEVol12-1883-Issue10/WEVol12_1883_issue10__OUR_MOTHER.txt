"OUR MOTHER."
AFFECTIONATELY INSCRIBED TO SISTER MAY.
   Sister dear, we know our mother,
With her crown of golden hair,
Soon will leave, us for another
world more fair.

   A world that is not full of sorrow
And every haunting care,
Even in that blest tomorrow,
In that world more fair. 

In that fair world she'll meet our father.
WHo has left and gone before,
He has crossed the stygian river.
And safely reached the shining shore.

The let us strive to wear with honor,
in "due time"our crown of grey,
Also let us strive to join her
In that fairer world, dear May
			B.A. Bush.
Cloverdale Farm, Tooele Co., Oct. 2nd, 1883
