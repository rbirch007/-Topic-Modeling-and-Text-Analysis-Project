THE WATCHER'S NIGHT THOUGHTS

Little crickets, so blithe and merry,
	Singing your night songs, gay and cheery,
Do you know you comfor the sad and sick,
With your "zr-r, zr-r, clip-cl-click"?

O, owl, in the tree, your "hoot-hoo-h-o-o-ty."
SOunds like a sentinel's "cry on duty!"
Telling the watcher she is not alone
In her vigils sad, by the dying one.

O, shining moon, as you go away,
You seem to tell us, 'tis almost day,"
That though your going may darken the skies,
The sun in his brightness, will soon arise.

Glittering stats, you stay all night,
Shining in brightly till morning light
Like bright rays of hope, high overhead,
Though all things earthly seem silent -dead.

The cricket, the owl, the moon and stars,
Begulling the silent, midnight hours,
Tell of a Creator everywhere,
And prove His power and loving care.
