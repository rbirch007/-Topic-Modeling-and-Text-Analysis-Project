THE TOMB!

	"Pale death, with impartial step, knocks at the hut
of the poor and the palace of kings. They are all com-
pelled to take the same road: from the urn of death
shaken for all, sooner or later the lot must come
forth."—HORACE

How sweet and peaceful is the tomb!
	How calm and tranquil there!
As naked came we from the tomb,
	So naked we repair!
There all life's tumults sink in peace,
	Fade all earth's gilded joys;
The wicked from all troubling cease,—
	Their rage no more annoys.

There ev'ry weary pilgrim rests,
	There sleep the great and small;
There lay the tyrants and oppressed,—
	Death's sleep reigns o'er
There servant, master, foe and friend,
	Partake the same repose;
And there, in peace, the ashes blend
	Of those who once were foes

There lays the sinner steeped in vice,
	Hands red with human gore,
Who learned by crime's most foul device
	All virtue to abhor.
And there in peaceful, tranquil sleep,
	The holy saint does rest,—
Bright angels round him vigils keep,
	To make his slumber blest.

There lays the babe in swaddling clothes—
	No stain of guilt its share—
Calm and at peace, in death's repose
	It slumbers free from care.
The parents, too, lay 'side its form,—
	Their battle fought and won,—
They've stood the blast of life's rough storm:
	Now shines eternal sun!

The haughty voice, the despot's tone,
	The persecutor's sneer,
All lay there hushed, still as a stone,
	Silenced from their career.
The slave aside the tyrant sleeps,
	The young rests with the old;
No more the woeful widow weeps,—
	Death comfort does unfold.

The child, the boy, the parent stern,
	The tot'ring, feeble sire,
All to the grave their steps must turn
	When God their souls require;
Then, death, where is thy sting?
	O grave, thy victory?
Show me, when life its close shall bring,
	Thy painful jubilee!
