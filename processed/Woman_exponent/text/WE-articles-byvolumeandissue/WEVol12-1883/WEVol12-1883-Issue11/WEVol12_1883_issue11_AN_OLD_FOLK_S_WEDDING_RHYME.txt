AN OLD FOLK'S WEDDING RHYME.
	THEIR FIFTY-SEVENTH WEDDING DAY.
	One summer morn in early life
		Sweet bells rang on the air,
	Light footsteps hurried to and fro
		Around a happy pair.
	For on the good ship Union
		The captain, Christopher, stood,
	And by his side his fair young bride
		With courage true and good.

	Bound for a long, long journey
		Around the world so wide,
	Well freighted down with love and faith.
		For calm, or tempest's tide.
	And during fifty seven years,
		Captain and mate have sailed
	In safety over rock and reef,
		Through many a heavy gale.

	And every passenger they took
		They brought in safety through.
	And twice they stopt in heaven's bay
		And left there, dear ones, too.
	Long may the good ship Union sail!
		And their last journey o'er--
	May Captain Christopher andwife
		Anchor on heaven's shore.
