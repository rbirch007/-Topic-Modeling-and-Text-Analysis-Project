EDITOR EXPONENT;

	It becomes my painful duty to announce the death of
Sister Karen Westenskow, beloved wife of Brother
Hans Westenskow, chief musician of this place. She
was born March 21st. 1839, died March 2nd, 1884.
	The departed sister had acted as a teacher for about
four years, and as treasurer for the last two years in the
Relief Society in this place; she was faithful in all her


duties, and died in the full faith of the Gospel. Peace
be to her ashes.
				Your sister in the Gospel,
						ANNIE R. KELLER, Secretary.

	DIED, at Curtis, Arizona, March 7th., 1884, Martha
Curtis, wife of Bishop Monroe Curtis.
	Sister Curtis was first counselor to the president of
Relief Society of the Curtis Ward, and died as she had
lived, in the faith of the Gospel and the hope of a glor-
ious resurrection. She had obeyed and honored the law
of celestial marriage, and no doubt will come forth to
enjoy a celestial glory. We condole with the bereaved
family.								W. EAST.

	DIED, in Salt Lake City, March 18, Celeste C. Riser,
daughter of Elder George C. and Christina Riser, born
in this city, September 18, 1860.
	Deceased was a very gentle and amiable girl; quiet
and unassuming in manner, and was dearly beloved by
her friends and relatives. Her early and, as it were, un-
timely death, has almost crushed her father, who loved
her very tenderly, and as she had lost her mother many
years before, he felt a more special nearness to her.
	It is always sad when the young and beautiful die,
and yet we do not always know, but they are taken in
mercy from evil to come. The funeral services were
conducted by Bishop Kesler in the 16th Ward Meeting
House; President A. M. Cannon and Joseph E. Taylor
addressed the large congregation assembled to do honor
to the dead.
