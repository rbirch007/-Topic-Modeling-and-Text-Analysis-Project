SHORT AND SWEET.

	Taking Friday evening's train, Sister Louie
Felt, Mary J. Cluff and myself sped on to San-
taquin, where we were met by Grandma Stick-
ney and Sister Wall, the Primary president.
Arriving at the meeting house, our ears were
saluted with sweet sounds from within. On en-
tering we found the hall filled, children in
front, parents occupying the seats that were
remaining. Such order! such an outpouring
of the divine influence that every heart was
filled with joy.
	Bishop Halliday moved that Sister Felt, be-
ing a missionary's wife, have her fare paid; the
hands flew up and the money came down; a
vote was called for those who did not keep the
word of wisdom to rise; not one of the officers
or members moved; and then for those who
were observing this commandment to rise, we
all rose; this gave us great joy.
	Saturday. Held meeting in Payson at 10
a.m. had a full house, good spirit and warm
friends, which made the time go like magic.
	At 3 p.m. held meeting in Salem; "all went
merry as a marriage bell;" children clean,
sweet and orderly.
	Returned to Payson, where we were the re-
cipients of Brother and Sister Douglas' hos-
pitality; enjoyed a delightful evening, and re-
turned by the morning train healthier, happi-
er and wiser for trying to do our duty to the
children of the Saints.
