	BY HER HUSBAND, J. CROSBY.

Farewell, my kind and faithful friend,
The	partner of my youth,
Forty-nlne long years we have been
United in the truth.

And now my work on earth is done,
And I am called away,
So I must go, my time has come,
I can no longer stay.

I go because my Father calls,
His voice we must obey;
Farewell, my kindred, friends, and all,
You, too, must past away.

This is the doom that’s fixed for all
The hosts of Adam's race;
They must put off this mortal coil 
To see the Father’s face.

Then we can rest in peace and wait
Till Michael’s trumpet sounds,
Then with the faithful Saints awake
To glory and a crown.

Be faithful then, beloved friends,
And do not weep for me,
In faith hold on until the end,
And great our joy will be.

When Jesus in the clouds descends
With hosts of angels, too,
Then we'll arise with all our friends
In celestial glory too.

Oh! the joy, no tongue can tell
The pleasure then so sweet;
Husbands and wives their loved ones hail,
And will each other greet.
