NOTES AND NEWS.

	The Annual meeting of the American Woman
Suffrage Association will be held in Brook-
lyn, N. Y., on the 9th and 10th of October, in
the Academy of Music.

	Mrs. Margaret W. Campbell has devoted
most of August to Northwestern Iowa, in Clay,
Dickinson, O'Brien, Monena, Ida, Sac and
Carroll Counties.

	At last the will of Mrs. Eddy, of Boston,
has been approved. This generous lady left
$32,000, to be equally devided between Lucy
Stone of Mass. and Susan B. Anthony of New
York, to be used in the interest of "Woman's
Rights Cause." She has also left some money
to Wendell Phillips.

	If the men who devote themselves with pa-
thetically unprofitable zeal to exposing the
weaknesses of women would only spend half
as much time and ingenious effort in curing
these weaknesses, women would soon be Sam-
sons in intellect and morals.—Mrs. May Wright
Sewall.

	Miss Willard's California campaign "netted"
the W. C. T. U. of that State over one thou-
sand dollars, resulting from the collections
taken at her meetings. This she was strenous-
ly urged to take and use for her own necessi-
ties and work, but preferred to leave it behind
her to continue the ministration so well be-
gun.—Ex.

	Our Herald, published in Lafayette, Iudi-
ana, comes this week in a new form. It con-
tains 20 pages of matter, and is in size suitable
for binding. It will be issued the first Satur-
day of each month, and continues to be the
organ of the N. W. S. A. as it was when
printed weekly. It is always full of news,
fresh and interesting. The editor, Mrs. Helen
M. Gougar, is one of the brightest shrewdest
women in America, good looking and
pleasing in her manner, tasteful in dress, and
one of the finest speakers on the platform.
