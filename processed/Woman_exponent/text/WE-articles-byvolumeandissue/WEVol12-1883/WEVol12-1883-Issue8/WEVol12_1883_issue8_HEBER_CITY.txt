HEBER CITY.

Conference of the Relief Societies of Wasatch
Stake of Zion convened in Heber City Hall,
August 11th, 1883. Present on the stand were
President Emma Brown and her Counselors,
also the Presidents of the several societies.
Minutes of the last Conference read and ap-
proved. Reports were read by the secretaries,
showing the spirit of improvement. President
Brown made a few opening remarks; the sev-
eral presidents then represented their wards
in a very favorable condition. Sister Lucy
Young from St. George then addressed the
conference upon the principle of celestial mar-
riage, and related some of her experience in it.
Conference adjourned until two o'clock. Sing-
ing. Benediction by Bishop Foremann.
	Afternoon session. Meeting opened with the
usual exercises. Sister Young spoke to us
again in the afternoon. Spoke of the great
responsibility resting upon the fathers and
mothers in raising and training our children.
Our Father in heaven knows of our appoint-
ment, of our coming together to serve Him,
and He will be there by His Spirit to comfort
and instruct. That we should put our entire
dependence on our Father in heaven, both in
adversity and prosperity. He gives ns all we
enjoy, and we should say, "Thy will be done
in all things." Sister Young gave us very wise
and excellent counsel; urged the great necessi-
ty of going to the Temple to redeem our dead.
	President Hatch spoke a short time; was
pleased to know the Relief Societies were in a
good condition, and asked the blessing of the
Lord on all Israel. Conference adjourned for
three months. Singing. Benediction by
President Hatch.
			MARY BRONSON, Secretary.
			AVIS M. BRONSON, Ass't. Sec.
