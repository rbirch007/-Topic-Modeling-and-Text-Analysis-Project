LEMUEL, NOT SOLOMON.
DUBUQUE, Iowa, August 7th, 1883.
	"H. B. B.” recently contrasted Dr. Ham-
mond's idea of women with the description of
a virtuous woman found in Proverbs, and at-
tributed its authorship to King Solomon. But
this wisdom did not come from any love-sick
Solomon. The words are those of King Lem-
uel;—“the prophecy that his mother taught him.”
Apparently she “jumped at this conclusion;” a
wise one, considering her "neurotical condi-
tion."
	It is the abnormal, hysterical women who
usually put themselves under the care of male
physicians. Then these physicians judge all
women by these invalids. Doctors, like the
Hebrews, see a "fall," whenever women, Eve's
daughters, desire progress.
