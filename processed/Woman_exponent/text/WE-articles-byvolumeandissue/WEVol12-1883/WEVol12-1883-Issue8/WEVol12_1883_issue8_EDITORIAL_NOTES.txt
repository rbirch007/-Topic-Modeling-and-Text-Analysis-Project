EDITORIAL NOTES.

	The P.A. will be held in the Salt Lake As-
sembly Hall, Saturday, at 10 a.m., and
Y. L. M. I. A. at 2 p.m. at the same place.
	THE Primary Fair for the Associations of
Salt Lake Co. will open on Wednesday, the
10th inst., in President Young's schoolhouse,
just north of the Eagle Gate.
	THE Woman's Conference of this Stake will
be held Friday and Saturday, Sept. 21st and
22nd. The Relief Society meetings will con-
vene in the 14th Ward Assembly Rooms, com-
mencing at 10 a.m. and 2 p.m.
	Evan Stephens and his juvenile classes will
give an entertainment in the Salt Lake Theatre,
on or about the 22nd inst. It is anticipated
that the different nations will be represented
in costume and characteristic songs, similar to
those given on the 24th of July in the Taber-
nacle. The concert will be entirely for the
benefit of Mr. Stephens' classes. He deserves
to have a full house, and everyone who loves
music will get the worth of their money.
	The picture, "Representative Women of
Deseret," spoken of elsewhere, and the accom-
panying book of "Biographical Sketches," des-
igned by Mrs. A. J. Crocheron is something
original in its character. The picture repre-
sents the three distinct organizations, viz.:
Relief Society, Young Ladies Associations and
Primary Associations, not only by the portraits
of the officers, but by characteristic symbols.
Theology, Medicine, Literature, Art and Music
are also represented in the same way. The
drawing is by the lady herself, lithographed in
New York. Mrs. C. has displayed considerable
talent for drawing artistically, and the work
should certainly meet with encouragement on
that account, as well as perpetuating in the
memory the institutions and labors of the wo-
men in the Church.
