"WE AIM AT NOBLE WOMANHOOD."

	What is noble womanhood? Is it to dress,
sing, play upon the organ or piano, or make a
fine appearance in company, all of these ac-
complishments are very well in their places,
but they do not constitute a noble woman. The
mind may be well trained, but unless the gar-
den of the heart is cultivated, and good seed
is sown we will never, no, never make noble
women. There are many people who are well
educated, who are anything but noble in char-
acter.
	Let us set our aim high and then reach it.
We aim at noble womanhood. Let us become
then tru, noble-hearted women wherever we
may be found, whether in the kitchen, garden
or parlor. It does not detract form the lady
to be able to make a nice loaf of bread or cook
a meal of victuals, in fact there is no real lady,
in my estimation, but that thoroughly under-
stands everything pertaining to household af-
fairs. Therefore if we expect to come up to,
our aim, and be true, noble women, we must
understand all these things. There is no place
that makes a young lady appear more lovely
and truly lovable then when looking tenderly
and lovingly after her younger brothers and
sisters, doing everything for the comfort and
happiness of father and mother and never re-
membering self, except so far as dignity of
character and self-respect are necessary to a


noble life. Such a young lady is generally be-
loved by all who know her, she makes sun-
shine wherever she goes, and is what I would
term a noble woman.
	Let us all keep our motto in view, remem-
bering that we are the daughters of God, by
keeping His commandments, being chaste and
pure in every thought, and in the end we will
be worthy the title of "A Noble Woman."
		AUNT HANNAH.
	Manuscript paper, Young Ladies' Journal,
Nephi, Utah.
