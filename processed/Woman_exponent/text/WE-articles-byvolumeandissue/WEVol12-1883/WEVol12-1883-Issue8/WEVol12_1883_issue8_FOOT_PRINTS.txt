FOOT-PRINTS.

I was musing one day as I passed along
A thoroughfare trod by the daily throng,
In their calls to labor to and fro,
Or on missions of love or pleasure go.

Musing and reading the story found
In the language of footprints upon the ground,
A language few study or stop to read,
As they rush along at a lightning speed.

Some are here yet that I've watched for years,
Watched with trembling hopes and fears;
As I view the dangers that lie between
The present and future so dimly seen.

Some have passed on to the other side,
And the foot-prints have faded 'neath time's swift
	tide;
And some indelible stand to-day,
As milestones to guide the stranger's way.

I have watched in the days your four little feet,
Your little forms in beanty complele;
Sweet names we called them—May and Gay—
But in the bright spring-time one passed away.

And we wept for the lost and the one still left,
For her heart was broken, her life bereft,
But the little hands beckoned beyond the tide,
And now they're together the other side.

Foot-prints, oh, foot-prints, the tales you tell,
How many feet faltered, how many fell;
Some we thought firm as the Rock of God,
In an hour unguarded let go the Rod,

The Iron Rod that must guide our feet,
Till we gain the victory grand, complete.
Oh, Christ in heaven, the weak make strong,
And guard in this hour of sin and wrong.

Here are tiniest feet just entering in,
Here is youth and manhood strong to win,
Here's the footsore and weary whose journey's near
	done,
Whose crown for eternity's lost or won.

Here's the school-girl and maiden with bright dreams
	untold,
Here's the fallen whose virtue's been bartered and sold;
The strong and the feeble, the sin-stained and pure,
All mark the same thoroughfare, who will endnre

And keep safe their birthright life, endless life,
While making their journey 'mid trials and strife?
Again—oh our Father—let Thy love descend,
And guide us in safety e'en unto the end.

HOPE.
