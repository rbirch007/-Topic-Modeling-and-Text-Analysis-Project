L. D. S. WOMEN'S MEETINGS.

	The Ladies' Conference at Provo commenced
August 30 ult., at 10 a.m., in the meeting
house. Mrs. Zina Young Williams, President
of Primary Associations of that Stake presid-
ing. Mrs. E. S. Taylor and Mrs. H. M. Whitney
of Salt Lake were present and addressed the
Conference.
	The Y. L. M. I. A. held their Conference in
the afternoon and evening, Mrs. Jane H. Tan-
ner presiding. President E. S. Taylor addressed
the meeting on subjects pertaining to their pro-
gress and advancement, urged them to live pure
and upright lives and keep the commandments
of God.
	August 31 the Relief Society Conference con-
vened at the same place at 10 a.m., Mrs. Mary
Johns, Counselor to Mrs. M. T. Smoot, presid-
ing, Sisters Eliza R. Snow Smith and E. B.
Wells were there to attend the meetings and ad-
dressed the sisters for a short time after the re-
ports had been given from all parts of the coun-
ty. These reports showed the Relief Society to
be in an exceedingly prosperous condition fi-
nancially. And each of the local sisters, those
who spoke, seemed to be growing and improv-
ing in spiritual things.
	In the afternoon Counselor John was neces-
sarily absent and by her request President E.
R. Snow Smith presided. Sisters Whitney,
Taylor and Wells each made a few remarks,
and then Sister Eliza R. S. Smith addressed the
congregation on various subjects of importance.
Sister Daniels made some remarks, and was fol-
lowed by Bishops Johnson and Madsen and
Mother Duke. During the afternoon Sister H.
A. B[-]ebee, President of Utah County Silk As-
sociation, gave an interesting account of the
progress made in silk culture. Sisters E. S.
Taylor and H. M. Whitney visited all the set-
tlements of Utah County south of Provo, hold-
ing meetings at Springville, Spanish Fork, Sa-
lem, Payson, Santaquin and Goshen. They
were absent nearly two weeks, and held twen-
ty-one meetings with the people in the county.
	On Wednesday, September 5, the Relief Socie-
ty Conference of Summit Stake was held at
Kama, Mrs. Mary J. Attwood presiding. Mrs.
E. Howard from Salt Lake was present and
addressed the meeting. The Y. L. M. I. A.
was held on the 6th, Mrs. Mary Rhead, Presi-
dent of Y. L. M. I. A. of Summit Stake, presi-
ing, Mrs. Howard giving instructions and en-
couraging the youg in the path of duty. Sept.
7 the Primary Conference was held, Mrs. Louie
Felt of the Salt Lake City, President of all the
Primary Associations, was present, and her re-
marks to the children were excellent and glad-
ly received. Mrs. Felt also accompanied Mrs.
Lovisa Roundy, the President of the Primary
Associations of Summit Stake, to one or two

[Column 2]
other places where they held meetings and
transacted some business in the interest of the
children.
	Thursday morning, Sept. 6, President E. R.
Snow Smith left the city on a visit to Box El-
der Co., and to attend the Ladies Conference
to be held in Brigham City. She will probably
stay at her brother's, Apostle Lorenzo Snow's,
two or three weeks. Sister Margaret P. Young
accompanied Sister E. R. S. S. to Brigham
City.
	Saturday and Sunday the Relief Society, Y.
L. M. I. A. and Primary of Tooele County held
their Conference at Tooele. Sisters E. Howard
and Rachel R. Grant of this city attended and
had an interesting time. The meetings were
presided over by their respective presidents.
Relief Society, Mrs. M. A. Hunter: Y. L. M. I.
A., Mrs. Ann Tate; Primary Ass'n, Mrs. Sarah
Hale.
	Tuesday, Sept. 11, a special meeting of the P.
A. of Union, S. L. Co., was held. M. I. Horne,
Lydia Ann Wells and E. C. Clawson of this
city were present. Bishop Phillips presided
over the meeting. A reorganization of the As-
sociation was effected.
	Wednesday the Relief Society of Union held
their annual meeting, Mrs. Elizabeth Richards
presiding. Present from the city, Mrs. M. I.
Horne and Mrs. M. W. Wilcox, both of whom
addressed the meeting.
	Friday morning, Sept. 14, Mrs. M. I. Horne
and Zina D. H. Young started for Sanpete to
meet with the sistesr of that Stake in their quar-
terly Conference at Ephraim on Saturday and
Sunday, 15 and 16. They will probably visit
Manti, Mount Pleasant and some other parts
of the county.
