EDITOR EXPONENT:

	On the 21st of last September our stake
president, Willmirth East, with her counsel-
or, M. K. Ransom, also Sister Carpenter, of the
Young Ladies' Society, came over to this place,
and we spent the evening very pleasantly.
	On the 22nd called a meeting and organized
a Relief Society. Sister Anna Jorganson,
President; Janet Anderson and Mary Petter-
son, Counselors; Mary E. Skinner, Secretary;
Mazibea M. Anderson, Treasurer; Lorina Wil-
son, President of teachers.
	The sisters gave us good counsel and advice.
Our meetings have not been so well attended
on account of sickness, as we have all been af-
flicted the past season with whooping cough,
malarial and intermittent fevers; also fever and
ague, and some have had very sore eyes, but
thank God
		"We are yet alive,
		And see each others' face,"
although some of them are rather long and
pale yet. We realize we are living in a day of
trials, and we do not expect to miss our por-
tion.
	Ever praying for the welfare of Zion and
the success of the EXPONENT,
		We remain, your's truly,
				ANNA JORGANSON, Pres.
