TO THE POET-BISHOP.

When next the fabled reins you grasp,
	And mount above these earthly bars,
O Poet! lower guide thy flight,
	Lest we shall lose thee 'mong the stars.

Yet poet-prophets, poet-kings,
Wrought in such matchless charm the Word—
No later pen hath reached their power,
None e'en such eloquence hath heard.

Then dip thy pen in that rich stream—
	The holy spirit's themes divine,
And may thy songs, O poet-priest!
	With sacred, ancient psalms entwine.

And when the Zion old shall meet
	The Zion of latter-days,
May'st thou with prophets, psalmists, bring
	Thy people's offering of praise.
					AUGUSTA JOYCE CROCHERON.
Feb. 6th., 1884.
