ORNAMENTAL WORK.

	We have seen some very handsome speci-
mens of natural flowers from the funeral of the
late Presiding Bishop, E. Hunter, preserved
for the family by Mrs. E. D. Roundy, 1[-]th
Ward. Flowers preserved by her retain their
perfectly natural appearance and color; they
can be arranged in any shape or design re-
quired at about one fourth the usual cost of
flowers embalmed; an ordinary frame is all that
is necessary; they cannot be injured by extreme
heat or cold, and will retain their beauty and
color for fifty years or more.
	Specimens can be seen at her residence, 422
W., First North Street, Salt Lake City.
