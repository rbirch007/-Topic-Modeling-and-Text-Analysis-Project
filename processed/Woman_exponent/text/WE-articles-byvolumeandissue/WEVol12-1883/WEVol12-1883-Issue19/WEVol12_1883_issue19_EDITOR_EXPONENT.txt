EDITOR EXPONENT:

	The Primary Association of this place was
joined by the Primary of Duncan's Retreat and
held a very pleasing little fair on January 8th.
It was opened with prayer by Charles Work-
man, one of our little Primary boys, at ten
o'clock a.m. One hour and a half was then
spent in viewing the many little articles the
children had made. These displayed all kinds
of work, both by boys and girls, from the com-
ical Punch and Judy, made from wood and
dressed by two little boys, to the delicate lace
and flowers. One piece of work, deserving
special mention, was the piecing of blocks for
a quilt, by Minnie Burke, a little girl of six
years old; the work spoke well indeed of one
so young. All the work showed that the little
folks had been very patient and persevering to
accomplish so much. There was a very nice
quilt made by the Association, which we are
going to donate to the Deseret Hospital; there
were also pies, cakes and bread made by both
boys and girls that were well worthy of praise.
After viewing the articles all were seated, the
house being hardly able to hold all that were
there. They were then entertained with songs,
dialogues, recitations, speeches, etc, by the
children until one o'clock, when a sumptuous
picnic was served to all in the house. Prizes
were then awarded to the children.
	At three o'clock the house was cleared, and
the children enjoyed themselves until five
o'clock in the dance, during which they were
regaled with candy. All expressed themselves
well satisfied with their day's enjoyment.
	Wishing for the success of your valuable lit-
tle paper, and the welfare of the Primaries,
			We remain your sisters,
				MINERVA PRATT, President,
				ELEANOR OWENS, Counselor.
