DEAR EDITOR:
*		*		*		*		*		*
	On June 4th we held our Annual Meeting; we
convened in our new Relief Society Meeting
House, which was  also dedicated at the same
time. Many of the brethren met with us on

[Column 2]
﻿this occasion. Bishop M. M. Merrill offered
the dedicatory prayer, which seemed to include
every blessing asked that could be desired to
be poured out upon not only those who had
been most energetic in its building and
rendered aid, but all the surroundings
thereof.
	The brethren praised our efforts and bid us
be united, thanked us for our aid in helping
the poor and visiting the sick, and for every
good word and work, that we should and had
performed. The financial report was read and
found to be satisfactory; inasmuch as we
have a building and appurtanances thereto,
worth $1,300, and our indebtedness on the
same something over $200, which we are now
trying to liquidate as fast as possible; besides
striving to fulfil our duty as helps to the needy.
President Merrill with her counselors, ever
desires the welfare of our valuable WOMAN'S
EXPONENT. Please accept our kind regards. 
		We remain, yours in the Gospel,
				A. J. MERRILL, President,
				B. BROWER, Secretary.
	July 22nd, 1883.
