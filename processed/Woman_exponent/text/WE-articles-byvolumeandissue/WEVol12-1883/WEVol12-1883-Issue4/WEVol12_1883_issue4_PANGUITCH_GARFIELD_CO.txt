PANGUITCH, GARFIELD CO.

	Minutes of the Quarterly Conference of the
Relief Society of the Panguitch Stake of Zion,
held in the Relief Society Hall, June 1st, 1883.
On the stand, President Barbara Myers, Coun-
selors Alvira J. Clark and Sarah P. Crosby,
and several of the brethren. Conference
opened with the usual exercises of singing and
prayer, reading of minutes and reports, and the
presidents of the different societies gave verbal
reports, and also bore their testimonies to the
truth of the Latter-day work.
	President Myers made opening remarks; said
the Stake was so scattered, did not expect a
representative from every ward, but wanted
the sisters to be on hand to attend to their du-
ties as much as possible. We had a great work
to perform to put down iniquity; should set a
good example before the young, to be kind and
generous in our feelings, and not be quarrel-
some with our neighbors; wished the brethren
to meet with us, needed their counsel.
	President J. W. Crosby said he was sorry
there were not more representatives present,
urged the secretaries to get their reports up in
proper shape, said we had the ability, all we
needed was practice; regretted there was not
more interest with the young; said our societies
were for mutual benefit; wanted the sisters in
traveling round the Stake to urge the neces-
sity of storing grain, it being President Young's
counsel for years; said he wanted us to be on
our guard and cease evil speaking, and try to
entertain everything that was good and pure.
	Brother M. M. Steele said mothers should
be careful about the first impressions made up-
on their children; told us what our societies
were for, and to live up to their privileges, and
not withold the knowledge we were in posses-
sion of, but try and reclaim the erring.
	After singing and prayer, conference ad-
journed for three months.
					SARAH P. CROSBY,
								Secretary.
