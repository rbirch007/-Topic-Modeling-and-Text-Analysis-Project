HARRISVILLE, WEBER CO.
EDITOR WOMAN'S EXPONENT.
	Our settlement extends some five or six
miles from south-east to north-west: in consid-
eration of the scattered condition of the
Saints it was considered advisable to divide or
rather organize another Society in the western
part of the ward, the two societies to be desig-
nated as East and West Harrisville. The or-
ganization of the Society of West Harrisville
took place on April 28th, 1881. The Stake
President, Sister Jane S. Richards, and Coun-
selors were present, also the Bishop and his
Counselors, we were also favored with the

[Column 2]
presence of Apostle F. D. Richards, who gave
us some excellent advice and instruction in re-
gard to our duties as Societies and Saints. The
following persons were elected as officers: Mary
Hegstead, President; Mary Taylor and Eliza C.
Larkins, Counselors; Christina Hegstead, Sec-
retary; and Eliza Jane Rawson, Treasurer.
Since our organization the sisters have taken a
lively interest in their meetings and in doing
what they could by way of relieving the wants
of the poor and comforting the sick, and doing
what they could to forward the interests of
the Kingdom of God.
	Our President takes an active part in all our
meetings, and leads out in every thing that
we have to do, and to show our appreciation
of her many virtues as an energetic and lively
worker, and the kindly feeling the sisters en-
tertain towards her, we surprised her with a
cashmere dress pattern and trimmings com-
plete. This was at our second annual meet-
ing, May 24th, 1883.
	We all enjoyed this meeting, and will long
remember the good spirit that was manifested
by all who spoke. We hold our meetings
every two weeks.
