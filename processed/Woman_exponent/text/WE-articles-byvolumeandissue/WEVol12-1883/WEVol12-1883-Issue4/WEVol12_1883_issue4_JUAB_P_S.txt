JUAB P. S.

	At two p.m. of the same day and at the
same place, the Juab County Stake organiza-
tion of Relief Society, held their quarterly Con-
ference by appointment, Sisters Elizabeth Ken-
dall and Jane Pixton, Counselor of our late
President, presided over the conference.
	Financial and statistical reports were read,
and verbal reports given by the presidents of
the different branches. All went to show that
the Society was in a good condition, and that
the sisters are alive to their duties, and striving
to do all they can to help on the good word.
We all sorely feel the loss of our late President
Goldsbrough, but realize that the Lord doeth
all things well; let his will be done; yet many
a teardrop fell from those over whom she had
presided.
	Our conference being held on the 15th anni-
versary of the organization of our Relief So-
ciety, the Nephi Society met in the Pelief
Hall, in the evening. President Paxman and
several of the brethren met with us, and the
Spirit of God was manifest in our meeting.
Sister McClean and Harvey were here on a
visit, and spoke to the sisters, and bore testi-
mony of the good spirit and influence they en-
joyed in visiting with us.
	Altogether we had a time of rejoicing, and
felt to thank ou Heavenly Father for the
great blessings we enjoyed.
				AMY L. BIGLER,
							Stake Secretary R. S.
