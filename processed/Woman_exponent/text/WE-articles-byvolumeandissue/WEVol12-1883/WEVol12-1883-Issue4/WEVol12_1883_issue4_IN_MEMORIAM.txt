IN MEMORIAM.

	Resolutions of respect to the memory of Ann Shipley,
who departed this life June 16th, 1883.
	Whereas, an alwise Providence has called from us by
the hand of death our beloved sister,
	Resolved, that we recognize in the death of our sister,
Ann Shipley, the loss of an able, efficient and worthy
teacher in the Relief Society of South Weber.
	Resolved, that we esteem her past faithful labors and
devotion to duty as worthy the fellowship and highest
respect of us all.
	Resolved, that we condole and deeply sympathize
with the bereaved family and friends, in the loss of a
kind wife, affectionate mother and a good and respected
citizen.
	Resolved, that these resolutions be spread upon our
records, and that a copy of the same be furnished the
bereaved family, also the WOMAN'S EXPONENT for pub-
lication.				Yours, respectfully,
						JEANETTE COOK, President.
						MARY M. FIRTH, Secretary.


Our sister now has gone to rest,
	She has gone to dwell on a brighter shore;
She has gone to meet the ones she loved,
	Whom death had parted long before.

And may the ones that hero remain,
	Though cruel fate should bid us part,
Still in our memory to retain,
	The friend that from us did depart.
	- AGNES H. COOK.
South Weber, July 7th, 1833.

	DIED, in the Tenth Ward, Salt Lake City, June 13th
1883, of pnuemonia, Laura Maria, daughter of John L.
and Regina Ness, born in Salt Lake City, September 21st,
1869.
	Also at the same place and time, June 13th, of pneu-
monia, Joseph Alfred, son of John L. and Regina Ness,
born August 24th, 1874, in Salt Lake City.
