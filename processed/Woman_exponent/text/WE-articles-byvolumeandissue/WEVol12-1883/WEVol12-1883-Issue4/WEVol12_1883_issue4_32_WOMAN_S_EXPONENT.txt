32	WOMAN'S EXPONENT.


JUNE.

The queen of Summer—month of June,
	Is here with joy and song;
And oh, the roses and the posies
	So sweet, she brings along!

Cheery June days, warm and bright,
	So full of blissful life,
Give purest joy, without alloy,
	Nature with love is rife.

Her angels—butterflies, hover 'round,
	Fair types of the "new birth;"
And the white clover spreads all over
	Like snow, to cool the earth.

Love, fragrance, beauty everywhere;
	Around, below, above;
The bright birds sing, the welkins ring
	And echo, "All is love."

We, who love nature and her God,
	In sympathy can meet;
The birds are ours, and the flowers,
	Through them, in love, we greet.

We see the same sky overhead,
	The white clouds on its blue,
Or gemmed with stars—they too are ours;
	 A bond of friendship true.

Leaf, bud and blossom, fragrance, song,
	The bright blue sky above,
Our hearts atune, in merry June,
	To melody and love.

Love to God who giveth all—
	The blossom, bud and leaf;
Praise we'll sing to Natures King,
	The God of life and death.
							EMILY SCOTT.
June 23rd, 1883.
