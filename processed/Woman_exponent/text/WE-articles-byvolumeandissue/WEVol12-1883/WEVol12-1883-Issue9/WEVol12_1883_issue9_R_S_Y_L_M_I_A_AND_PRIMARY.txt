R. S., Y. L. M. I. A. AND PRIMARY
		    REPORTS.
	WILSON WARD, WEBER STAKE.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Our Primary Association of Wilson Ward
at present is in a flourishing condition.  We
held a Conference June 1st, 1883, President
Alice E. Stephens presiding. There were
present on the stand, a number of the sisters,
the Bishop and also the Sunday School Super-
intendent. A short time was spent in singing
and reciting, the President then called upon
Bishop B. H. Bingham to make a few remarks,
to which he responded, expressing himself as
being well pleased and entertained with the
exercises, also thought the Primary Associa-
tion a great benefit to the young.
	He was followed by Superintendent S. H.
Nielson who spoke very encouragingly to the
children.
	Conference adjourned for three months.
Singing and benediction.
					GENEVA MARTIN,
							Secretary.
