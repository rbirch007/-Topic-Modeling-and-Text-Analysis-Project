WOMAN'S VOICE.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We had our conference, as you are aware, the
second and third of August. We had a good
time together, the Spirit of the Lord was with
us, and many good instructions were given from
the sisters as well as from the brethren who
spoke. We were very much disappointed in not
having you come to visit us; in particular the
Primary children; their little hearts rejoiced
when we told them that you were coming to
speak to them, and after that they had heard
that the railroad was washed away, yet when
we went to our Primary meeting Saturday after-
noon, my little boy asked me if the sisters from
the city would not be there to speak to them.
I feel in my heart to ask God to bless the Prim-
ary children; they are but few here in Castle
Dale, but they are bright and intelligent children,
and they have improved greatly in the short
time since they were organized. It seems the
time passes along very swift, and every moment
ought to be precious to us; a moment is but a
small fraction of time, yet moments make up
the threescore years and ten allotted to mortal
existence, and few reach that period; they drop
all the way along, from early time down to old
age; though to some there are a few more years
allotted, but when the last day comes, whether
early or late, it is useless to plead for more time
to close up unfinished business; therefore let us
use the time and the talents the Lord has given
unto us to help to build up His Kingdom; and
listen to the small voice of the angel of time,
that follows us from our early days and whispers,
prepare! Let us live our religion and honor the
name of Latter-day Saint.
	May God bless all my sisters who work for the
advancement of the Kingdom of God. I wish
success to your paper, that it may be circulated
among the Saints to cheer and bless the sisters
of Zion.			CAROLINE A. LARSON.
	Castle Dale, Aug. 12, 1883.
