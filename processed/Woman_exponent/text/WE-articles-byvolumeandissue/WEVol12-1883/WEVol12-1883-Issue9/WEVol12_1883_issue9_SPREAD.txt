SPREAD. 

"Night unto night showeth the knowledge of God;
the heavens declare His glory and the firmament show-
eth His handiwork; there is no speech nor language
where their voice is not heard."-DAVID.

When heaven's sable garb is spread,
And all is calm and still,
When, bright stars, glittering overhead,
The firmament do fill,
When full of majesty the moon
Mounts high upon her throne,
Then, oh I love my soul to tune,-
Jehovah's care to own!

I do not know why I should dwell
More on the things of God
When darkness, like a firm-bound spell,
Reigns overearth’s dark sod:
Yet then it seems a solemn, grand,	
Lends presence to the view,
And oh‘ I then do love to stand,
The works of God pursue!

Oft when the day of toil is done,	
And Nature is at rest,
When cares sink with the setting sun,
And all around seems blest,
I stand and gaze in yonder sky,
Where glitter orbs of light,
And feeble lips do fail when I
Attempt to tell the sight. 

Talk of man's works, inventions, schemes, 
And of his mighty power.
They are but baseless, empty dreams—
A fleeting, passing hour—
Compared with the eternal round,
The never-falling scene 
Of God’s grand works, that do surround
And everywhere are seen. 

Lo, take a single grain of .wheat,
Scan wrell its size and form,
Then with thy wisdom try complete
One like it, - uniform.
Or, ere the morning's sun does rise,	
Lo, take the sparkling dew,
And tell me if thou canst devise
A drop to match it true. 

You cannot, though your wisdom great
Seems wondrous here to man:
God’s works no one can imitate,
Though hard they think and plan.
Then as the sable garb of night
Each time the heaven spreads
I'll in Jehovah's works delight
Till life this body sheds.
					JAMES H. WALLIS.
Salt Lake City, Utah, Sept. 25th, 1883.
