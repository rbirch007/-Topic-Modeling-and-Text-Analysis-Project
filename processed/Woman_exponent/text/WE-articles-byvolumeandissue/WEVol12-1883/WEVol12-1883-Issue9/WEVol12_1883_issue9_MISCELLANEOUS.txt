MISCELLANEOUS.

A picture of a ruined castle on the Rhine,
painted by a blind girl, is on exhibition in
Paris, and attracts a good deal of attention.
	Mrs. Mary A. Livermore has collected in a
volume her Lectures on Women, and it will
soon he published by Lee & Shepard, under
the title of "What Shall we do with our
Daughters?—Superfluous Women, and other
Lectures."
	The man was dust, refined, but the woman
was dust double-refined,—one remove farther
from the earth. The woman was made of a rib
out of the side of Adam,—not made out of his
head to top him, nor out of his feet to be tram-
pled upon by him, but out of his side to be
equal with him, under his arm to be protected,
and near his heart to be beloved.—Matthew
Henry.
