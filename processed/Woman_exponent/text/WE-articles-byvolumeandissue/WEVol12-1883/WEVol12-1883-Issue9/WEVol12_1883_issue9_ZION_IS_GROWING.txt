ZION IS GROWING.
Conference is just at hand, and the city is al-
ready teeming with visitors and people hurry-
ing to get their trading and business of various
kinds done before the 6th of October. No doubt
it will be one of the busiest, as well as one of
the most pleasant conference gatherings that we
have had. The city is growing in beauty and
importance, and the Church itself, notwith-
standing all the powers arrayed against it, is
steadily, yes, rapidly advancing in power and
influence; in fact Zion is becoming established
in the tops of the mountains, and people from
all nations are coming to see and to hear and to
learn of that which they have heard every-
where spoken evil of.
Here in the midst of the Saints are those most
opposed, and most bitter against the progress
and advancement of the work, God has sought to
accomplish in this day and time. Every inven-
tion Satan is capable of manufacturing seems to
be used to destroy the Church, and bring the
Saints into disrepute. No slander is too infam-
ous, no falsehood too malicious to be published
and circulated against this people. And yet
they pass by it almost invariably in silence, for
to attempt to contradict these wicked misrepre-
sentations would occupy mere time than any of
this bnsy people can afford. Thore is too much
labor on hand to assist in rolling forth the King-
dom, to take any precious time to refute false
statements, unless it was positively necessary to
do so. The Mormons will live and prosper, and
no power on earth can hinder the Lord’s work
that He has set His hand to do. It is this im-
plicit faith and trust in divine power that unites
this people and gives them that confidence in
Jehovah’s protection that no amount of persecu-
tion, drivings or death itself can destroy.
