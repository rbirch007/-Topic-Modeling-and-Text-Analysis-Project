A HAPPY AFFAIR.

	By invitation of Mrs. Angeline Mc. Allister, a
few of the friends of President Mc.Allister and
his family met at the old homestead in the
Eighth Ward in this City, on Wednesday, April
2nd, the occasion being a happy one for Sister
Angeline, who has bought back the home,
which had for some time past been in the hands
of others. It was something of a reunion, a very
pleasant affair. There were present, the Bishop
of the ward and members of his family, also
Gen. Wells, H. Dinwoodey, I Brockbank, J.
Needham, and James G. Bleak, of St. George,
and several others, and a number of ladies.
	A bounteous repast had been prepared by Sis-
ter Angeline, which the guests thoroughly en-
joyed. and the delightful music, vocal and in-
strumental, rendered by President Mc.Allister
and his children was a most enjoyable feature
of the evening.
