RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.

	Whereas, By the decree of an alwise Providence, our
friend and associate, Lillie May Murdock, has been
called from our midst,
	Resolved, That in our sorrow for a faithful member,
we find consolation in the thought that it is well with
her for whom we mourn, though it is hard to part with
one before whom life was apparently all happiness.
	Resolved, That we condole with the bereaved parents,
relatives and friends, in this their great loss.
	Resolved, That these resolutions be spread upon the
records of the Association, and a copy be sent to the
WOMAN'S EXPONENT for publication.
								BELLE GILLIES,
										Secretary.

	DIED, at Hyde Park, Cache Co., March 7th, 1881, Or-
son Charles, Infant son ef Samuel and Permelia Matkin,
born Sep. 19th, 1883.

Our sweet little Orson has left us
	To dwell in a holier sphere;
With all our loved ones bereft us,
Oh, rest thee, darling, there!

Thou art free from earthly trials,
	From all sorrow, pain and sin;
Thy spirit pure is dwelling now
	Where no evil can enter in.

Sweet be thy rest, thou lovely child!
	But 'tis hard indeed to part
With the little gem of our household,
	With the darling of our heart.

Then rest thou darling baby,
	While grief our hearts doth fill,
For we know that God hath called thee
	And we bow to His holy will.

'Tis true the trial was severe
	That tore him from your breast,


But do not wish him back again,
	For he has gone to rest.

Father, mother, kindred, friends,
	May miss his lovely form;
A bright future lies before us
	At the resurrection morn.

In the glorious resurrection
	The faithful will come forth;
Then sweet will be our meeting,
	And we will know his worth.

Thou dost not weep to weep alone,
	For on all the sorrow seems to fall;
It is not felt by thee alone,
	He was beloved, beloved by all.
