A WORD FROM MILL CREEK.

	February 25th a number of the sisters of
the Relief Society of Mill Creek met at the
residence of Sister Araminta North, it being
her 65th natal day. There were also a num-
ber of the brethren present. Almost the en-
tire company were there at the appointed hour,
which made it quite surprising to her, not hav-
ing the slightest idea of the proceedings. A
beautiful arm chair was presented to her by
the sisters and the following lines were com-
posed and read by one of the sisters: "We, the
sisters of the Relief Society of Mill Creek
ward, have come together with your family to
give you a surprise on your 65th birthday, ap-
preciating your labor and care as our treasur-
er, we present to you this chair as a small tok-
en of respect, and hope that you may live
many years to enjoy it.
	It is with great pleasure that we have met
here this afternoon to congratulate you on this
occasion; and when we reflect back we realize
that you have spent over forty years in this
Church, during which time, together with your
husband and family, you have shared with the
saints in much joy and persecution, left your
home, friends and relatives that were near and
dear to you, but by so doing you have not lost
all; you have here in this land many friends, a
comfortable and happy home, surrounded by
your children and numerous grandchildren;
you have been a member of the Relief Socie-
ty of the Mill Creek Ward ever since its first
organization, and have also been the treasurer
for over seven years. Always willing to help
and cherish the sick and needy, you have our
best wishes in the future. May you yet spend
many happy days and do all the good your
heart desires, be a comfort to your husband and
children, also the grandchildren that are always
ready to greet you on all occasions; also still
continue to aid us as a band of sisters in this
glorions work in which we all are engaged.
May health, peace and prosperity attend you
the remainder of your days, is the earnest de-
sire of your sisters of the Relief Society of this
ward."
	Acting Bishop A. Hill and counselor W.
Lemmon made some excellent remarks, also
Bros. John Morgan, H. Bowden and Levi
North spoke a short time. Sister Araminta
North returned her thanks to the sisters for
the handsome present and their good will to-
wards her.
	A bounteous supper was partaken of, which
added much to the comfort and pleasure of the
party. A good spirit prevailed and all enjoyed
themselves first rate.
	In conclusion, it was a day long to be re-
membered by all present.		A. J. N.
