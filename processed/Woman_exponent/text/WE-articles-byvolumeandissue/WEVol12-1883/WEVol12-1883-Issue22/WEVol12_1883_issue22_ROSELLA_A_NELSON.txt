							ROSELLA A. NELSON.

	It becomes our painful duty to record the death of
our beloved and esteemed friend, Sister Emma Maddi-
son, who departed this life March 9th, 1884, of child
birth, wife of John F. Maddison, and daughter of Wm.
and Ann D[-]ll, born in London, Dec. 25th, 1851; aged
thirty-two years, two months and twelve days. She had
five sons and one daughter, but only two survive her.
	Sister Maddison led a life worthy of imitation, she
was a devoted wife, a kind and affectionate mother, and
a friend to all. Her smiling countenance and cheerful
words immediately won the love and respect of all who
had the pleasure of enjoying her society.
	She was Second Counselor to the Prest. of the Y. L.
M. I. A., and diligently performed her duties, her kind
advice and pious instructions always seeming to sink
into the hearts of those who came under the sound of
her voice.
	She leaves a husband, two small children, and numer-
ous relatives and friends to mourn her loss. May she
rest in peace-
					PARTHENIA LOWE, President,
					ELIZA SMITH, Secretary.
	Providence, Cache Co., Utah, March 20th, 1884.
