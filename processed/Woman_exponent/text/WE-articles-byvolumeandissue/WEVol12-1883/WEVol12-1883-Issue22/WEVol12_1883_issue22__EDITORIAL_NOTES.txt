EDITORIAL NOTES.

	The music book prepared for the use of Sun-
day Schools contains some new pieces by home
authors and is well worth the price, 50 cts.

	"Madame Anna Bishop, the celebrated song-
stress and linguist, died of apoplexy at her
home in New York, on the 25th ult."

	"Recitations and Dialogues," is the title of
another little work which contains suitable
pieces for speaking in children's entertainments
etc., and helps to make a pleasing variety for
the exercises of the little people. Price, 25
cts.
	Mrs. Miriam G. Chase, President of the Re-
lief Society of the First Ward in this city, has
gone to the State of Vermont to visit rela-
tives there, and gather up genealogy.

	The Juvenile Instructor Office has lately
published several valuable works. "Orson
Pratt's Works," a book of 314 pages, is a very
useful one, containing authoritative testimony
presented by the late esteemed Apostle Orson
Pratt, whose writings have ever been full of
intelligence and light. Price, 75 cts.

	"Of the 26 women graduates from the Wo-
men's Medical College in Philadelphia, recent-
ly, Miss Emma Atkin one of our Utah born
girls, stood second on the list. She is a very
bright, attractive young lady, and will no
doubt become successful in her profession.
