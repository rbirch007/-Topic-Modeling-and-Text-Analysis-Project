PICNIC PARTY!

	A conjoint picnic party of the Second
Ward Relief Society and Primary Associa-
tion of Logan took place on Saturday, 11th of
August, at Johnson's new bowery. All the
pleasure seekers who were too old or too
young to walk were conveyed to the grounds
in carriages and other vehicles. When all
had arrived the opening exercises commenced
at 10:30 a.m., Mrs. R. Nibley presiding. Sing-
ing by the Primary. Prayer by Sister M. Bal-
lard. Singing. Then followed questions and
answers on the Bible by the Primary Associa-
tion, also songs and recitations, which were
very good
	The remainder of the time was occupied by


the Relief Society, and the recitations and
songs of the members were rendered in a con-
cise and pleasing manner. All present partook
of the bounties of life prepared for the occa-
sion. After refreshments, swinging, skipping
the rope and other sports were engaged in by
all. On the arrival of Sister Elizabeth Ben-
son, President of the Cache Valley Stake Re-
lief Society, her counselors and others (that
were invited but could not attend earlier) the
president, Sister M. Ballard, called the people
to order, and some choice songs and recitations
were given for the benefit of Sister Elizabeth
Benson and the other sisters. Sister Benson
made a few remarks; encouraged all to continue
in well doing; she said it would be a day long
to be remembered, especially by the little folks.
Thinking that they had been rusticating long
enough, President, Sister M. Ballard, thought
it would be well to close the day's enjoyment.
Singing by the Primary. Prayer by Sister
Mary McAllister at 3:30 p.m., when all re-
turned homeward, feeling well satisfied with
the pleasures of the day.
	EMELINE E. JAMES,
		Secretary R. S.
August 13th, 1883.
