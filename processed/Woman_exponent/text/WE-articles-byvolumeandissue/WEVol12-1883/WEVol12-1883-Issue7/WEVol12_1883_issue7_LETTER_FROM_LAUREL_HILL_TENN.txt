LETTER FROM LAUREL HILL, TENN.

	Aug. 14th, 1883.
EDITOR WOMAN'S EXPONENT:
	Allow me space in your neat little paper to
thank some kind person for sending me some
copies of papers from Salt Lake. They were
old copies, but were gladly received and highly
appreciated by all that have read them, except
some that have become so steeped in Method-
ism that their minds have become so contracted
that they will not expand for anything but the
M. E. C. Y. I have seen a great deal of Mormon-
ism lately, and will have to acknowledge that it
is quite a different doctrine to what I expected.
Jacob F. Miller and Brigham A. Holbrook, of
Bountiful, Utah, have been stopping with me
often; I find them perfect gentlemen in every
sense of the word, and my door is ever open for
them or any other strangers from Utah, preach-
ers or not, that come and conduct themselves as
Elders Miller and Holbrook. If God would hear
me I would say, God bless all such people and the
Mormon cause if it is as they preach it, and
condemn all such creeds and isms as I have been
raised to believe in.
	May you make the EXPONENT one of the best
papers in the world, that it may help woman
to have her full rights with man.
	I subscribe myself a friend,
