EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We had a very pleasant affair on Friday,
the 3rd inst., gotten up by the officers of the
Relief Society in their new hall for the widows,
widowers, missionaries wives, newcomers, and
a few old people, numbering over seventy. Pre-
cisely at one p.m. dinner was served. After
dinner, speeches were made by John C. Naile,
J. Beck, Counselors W. H. Winn and Andrew
R. Anderson and Bishop T. R. Cntler, inter-
spersed with songs and dancing until five p.m.,
and all who participated felt it had been a
time of rejoicing and one long to be remem-
bered.			R. STANDRING, President,
