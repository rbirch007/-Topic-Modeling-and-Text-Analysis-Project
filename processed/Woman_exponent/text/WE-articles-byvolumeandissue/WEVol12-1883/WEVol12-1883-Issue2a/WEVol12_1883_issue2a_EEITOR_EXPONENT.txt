EEITOR EXPONENT:
	I should miss the Exponent very much were
I to get behind, and I feel to appreciate your la-
bors in behalf of the women. We live so far out
of the way we would know but little that is go-
ing on were it not for the papers. Still we enjoy
our quiet work, little or no outside influence, no
drunkeness or rowdyism, but plenty of work to
keep us out of mischief; more than usual on
dams and ditches this spring, owing to con-
tinued high water. The crops are looking well.
Small vegetables, such as raddishes, lettuce and
asparagus grace most of the tables now.
	Our Relief Society and Primary are in a good
condition. Our Young Ladies' meetings have
been discontinued for some time, due, partly, to
sickness and other causes. With best wishes
for the EXPONENT and kind regards to the Edi-
tor,				I am, yours truly,
					HANNAH A. CROSBY.
	April 23rd, 1884.
