MORMON MISSIONARY LIFE.

The Elders when called to go forth to preach
	Have trials to meet every day.
The Gospel of Jesus we're willing to teach
	To all that will hear what we say.

We meet many people, while far from our homes,
	Who care not for "Mormons" at all;
Like Talmage they say, "We should pick up our guns,
	And feed them some powder and ball."

Such cases are rare, yet they can be found
	In great Christian nations to-day.
When met by such people in traveling around,
	We Elders just go on our way.

Some people are anxious to hear Mormons preach
	Their doctrine so strange to the world;
They say, "Let us hear this new Gospel you teach,
	There's many a slur at you hurled."

The Elders then set forth the Gospel of Christ,
	As taught by both Peter and Paul,
These truths are for all those who wish to do right;
	The Gospel is free for us all.

We find many people who to us are kind,
	Though they are not seeking for truth.
There's many false notions imbibed in their minds,
	Which they have been taught from their youth.

Sometimes we find those who are seeking for truth,
	They are praying to God night and day.
In order that they may their talents improve,
	They strive God's command to obey.

When shown the right way by the servants of God
	To serve Him and keep His commands,
The honest will turn from the paths they have trod;
	They long on "Mount Zion" to stand.

The shepherds are gathering the sheep to the fold,
	Where they may be taught in God's way.
We gain an experience worth millions of gold,
	May God give us strength as our day.

O Father in Heaven, be kind to us all,
	May we this great mission fufil,
Be faithful and true till thou shalt recall
	They servants to "Mount Zion's Hill."
