THE SEX.

	JULIA GARETSON is taking the lead among
the Grange orators, and is said to be very
popular.

	JENNE COLLINS provided a Christmas
dinner for a large number of girls at Boffin's
Bower, Boston.

	MISS GIFFORD, formerly of Syracuse, N.
Y., is studying art in Florence, and is en-
gaged on a bust of Rev. Samuel J. May.

	REV. PHOEBE A. Hanaford, the pastor of
the Universalist Church at New Haven, ac-
cepts her call to Jersey City, but she will
not leave her present church, over which
she has been settled three years, until
April. 

	FOUR WOMEN in France have received
decorations: viz. Madame Dunbar, Superior
of a Convent at Nancy; Rosa Bonheur,
Lady Pigot and Mlle. Berthe Rocher of
Havre, who have lately founded a new
hospital.

	TWO OF the most successful and best paid
editors in New York are women––Miss
Mary L. Booth, of the "Bazar," who re-
ceives $4000 a year, and Mrs. Mary E. Dodge,
of the "Hearth and Home," who has a sal-
ary of $3000. They are both very indus-
trious and capable.
