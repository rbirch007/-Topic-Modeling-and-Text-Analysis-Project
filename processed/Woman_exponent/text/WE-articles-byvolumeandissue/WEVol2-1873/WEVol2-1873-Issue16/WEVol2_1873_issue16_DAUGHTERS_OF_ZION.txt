		DAUGHTERS OF ZION.

Daughters of Zion, up, up and awake
From the slumbers that bind you, their strong fetters
	break!
Up, trim your lamps quickly, and let your light shine
Amid earth's deep darkness with splendor divine.

Let the power of the Gospel, life's dross all consume,
That its precious sweet truth in your souls may have
	room:
And work—for the Harvest is ripe for the floor,
And the storms of destruction are nigh at the door.

There is famine on earth for the pure "bread of life,"
And its children are sick of the pain and the strife;
But they wander in darkness, the blind lead the blind,
And evil's strong fetters its weak victims bind.

Oh daughters of Zion! there's a great work to do—
Will you bear a brave part with the good and the true?
Will you help to redeem this fair earth from sin,
And from its deep sorrows her frail children win?

Let this be your study, that Zion be pure,
That no evil passions her light may obscure;
That the Word may go forth with great power and might
And her people be strong in the battle for right.

Think not to sit down by the wayside to rest,
'Till the haven is won in the land of the blest;
But keep your lamps bright as you journey along—
Each may guide some poor soul in the wandering throng.
										HOPE.
