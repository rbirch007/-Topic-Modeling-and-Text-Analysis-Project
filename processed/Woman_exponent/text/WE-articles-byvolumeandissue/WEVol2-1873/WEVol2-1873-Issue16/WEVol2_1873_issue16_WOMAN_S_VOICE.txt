WOMAN’S VOICE.

	Cedar City, Iron Co., Dec, 28th 1873.
EDITOR WOMAN’S EXPONENT,—
	DEAR SISTER:
	Though personally unknown to you, I feel
as if we were old acquaintances. Your val-
uable paper comes as a friend, and is wel-
comed as such; I take great pleasure in read-
ing it, and try to carry out the good instruc-
tions contained therein.
	The sisters throughout the Territory,ought
to feel grateful that we have the privilege
of communicating with, and expressing our


feelings to each other, through its columns,
How many have formed friendships in days
gone by, whose paths in life have widely
separated, and like Martha of old, they are
“troubled about many things," therefore can-
not find time to correspond with all; and
what a comfort for such to learn of each
other occasionally through the paper. Accept
my kind regards, and heart-felt wishes for
the onward progress of the EXPONENT.
