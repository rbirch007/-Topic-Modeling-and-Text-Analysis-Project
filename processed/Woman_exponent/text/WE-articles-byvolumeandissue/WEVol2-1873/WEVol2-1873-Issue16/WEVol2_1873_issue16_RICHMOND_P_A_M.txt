	RICHMOND.				P.A.M.


﻿“AUNT PHEBE,” in the “Rural New
Yorker,” attributes the cross looks and fret-
ful words of wives generally, to want of
sleep and rest. We believe with her, that
most working women, while rearing fami-
lies indulge in this, the best of all stimu-
lants, far too little. A quiet hour or even
half hour’s nap, would do them more good,
if possible to be obtained, than a dozen cups
of tea.
