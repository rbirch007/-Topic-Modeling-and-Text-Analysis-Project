SPLINTERS.

	Cherry trees are in bloom on the Pacific
slope....A plastic cast of Prof. Agassiz' face
was taken by Preston Powers....Miss Antoi-
nette Sterling, an American girl, is singing
in London, it is said with great success......
A paper lately started in New York, devoted
to the interests of the Patrons of Husband-
ry, bears the name and motto of "The
Scythe," "to mow down monopoly and ex-
tortion"......Iowa City in order to invest in
a theatre, has sold its public library at auc-
tion......A Derby, Conn., farmer has had the
good fortune to have a lead mine discovered
on his farm......California produced 1,000
bales of cotton last year........A brother of
Mrs. President Grant, Judge Louis Dent, is
seriously ill...A nephew of Victor Emman-
uel, the Duke of Genoa, lately arrived in
San Fransisco......A list of the creditors of
Jay Cooke & Co., has been published...Short-
ly before his death, Prof. Agassiz wrote to
Newfoundland to gain full particulars about
a "devil-fish," which but recently appeared
on that coast......New York city is to have a
new theatre to be known as the "Bucking-
ham," which will cover 13,342 square feet
......The Common Council of Poughkeepsie,
N. Y., is composed, with a single exception,
of temperance men.....Since 1871, nearly
2,500 enlisted men have deserted from their
regiments in the Pacific States and Territo-
ries......Wheat sells for less, by 33 percent.,
than potatoes, bushel for bushel, in Moline,
Ill......The United States Consul at Copen-
hagen, Hon, G. W. Griffin, has been elected
Fellow of the Denmark Royal Association of
Northern Antiquities...It is reported that a
Vicksburg girl has gone crazy because her fa-
ther would not let her appear in a certain per-
formance on the stage.....Seth Adams who
died recently in Boston, left an estate valued
at 1,700,000.....An ear of corn with 1,816
grains has been produced in Franklin town-
ship, Pennsylvania......A distemper among
cats is prevalent in Lawrence, Massachusetts
......The law requiring the printing of pub-
lic documents for general distribution, has
been repealed by the Senate......Agassiz'
physicians are said to be greatly puzzled over
the nature of his disease......There is a pet-
rified hog in Dunbarton, N. H., that died
and was buried 15 years ago......The schools
of St. Louis are giving great satisfaction
with the Kindergarten system......Raleigh,
N. C., has no public school......Again the
Kentucky Legislature is considering a con-
stitutional convention......Bishops in Ger-
many are hereafter required to swear alle-
giance to the State......During the year of
1873, $4,493,000 have been given or pledged
to American colleges....In the public schools
of Tennessee, there are 178,076 pupils, of
whom 24,325 are colored.....Caleb Cushing
is to address the survivors of the Mexican 
War at Washington to-day, Jan. 15th.....Mr.
Hatch, Republican Senator from Hale Co.,
has been expelled from the Alabama Senate.
