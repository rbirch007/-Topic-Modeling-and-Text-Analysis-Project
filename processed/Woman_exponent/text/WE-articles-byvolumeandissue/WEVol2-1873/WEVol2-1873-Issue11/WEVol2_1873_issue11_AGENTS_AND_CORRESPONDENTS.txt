     AGENTS AND CORRESPONDENTS.

	Like most journals of distinguished aims for
good and useful ends, the EXPONENT has met
with difficulties in struggling to establish a
permanent basis, by awakening the sympa-
thies and securing the co-operation of those
who should feel most interest in its behalf.
Yet, notwithstanding all the besetments it has
had to encounter, its course so far has been un-
usually successful, and its present prospects
are bright and encouraging.
	The following list of names should imply
that already its friends are not few. It is de-
sired however, that others be added, until at
least one lady in each settlement in the Terri-
tory, shall be known as an acting agent for the
WOMAN'S EXPONENT. Officers of Relief Socie
ties, where no agents, or insufficient numbers
have yet been appointed,are politely solicited to
select capable,active women, who will be pleas-
ed to act and forward their names as agents.
Bishops, postmasters, and all good men, will
please remember and consider that an especial,
respectful and standing invitation is extended,
for them to use their influence to increase the
circulation and assist the progress of this little
journal.
	Should mistakes appear in the following list,
corrections will be cheerfully received and
published.
