	HOME AFFAIRS.

	AT A meeting of the EXPONENT Committee
of Consultation, held October 21st, Mrs. Ma-
rinda Hyde and Mrs. Phebe Woodruff were
chosen members of the Committee; also a num-
ber of ladies were appointed to act as agents.
	THE FINE hotel, known as the Clift House,
was burned on the night of the 24th ult. A
defective flue is said to have been the cause.
At ten minutes past ten the fire bell, at the
City Hall, rang out the alarm which speedily
brought fire engine, firemen and spectators to
the scene. The flames had already made so
much progress that though the firemen worked
nobly through the night, they barely saved
the walls from falling. The total loss was
supposed to be about $70,000.
	AMONG THE recent deaths in this city, is
brought to our notice that of little Julia Horne,
infant daughter of William S. and Julia Horne
Burton, which took place on the 24th ult.
Not quite a year since, we chronicled the death
of its mother, which occurred but a few hours
subsequent to the birth of the child. Now, as if
in answer to a call from her sainted mother, the
little one has thus early fled the scenes of morta


life. The sympathies of a large circle of friends
are newly awakened for the bereaved husband
and father and other near relatives, in conse-
quence of this second sad loss.
