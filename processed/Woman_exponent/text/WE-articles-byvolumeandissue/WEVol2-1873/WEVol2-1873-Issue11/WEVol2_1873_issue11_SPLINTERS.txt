SPLINTERS.

	In Savannah, Georgia, they are experi-
menting with female street car conductors.
......It is said that base-ball professionals are
better paid than nine-tenths of the minis-
ters......In Lake Minnetonka, Minnesota, a
valuable guano island has lately been dis-
covered......The hair is worn very plain
and low on the neck by ladies just returned
from Europe and it is said to be a style "all
the rage” in Paris......The Agricultural
College of Ohio has one female student......
Great varieties of game abound in the fields
of Louisiana, and more abundant this season
than at any previous time since the war......
Lately, two babies were sent by express
several hundred miles in Oregon, arriving
all safe......The Emperor of Brazil is cred-
ited with standing six feet, four inches, in
his boots......President Grant and three
Generals, Sherman, Sheridan and Hooker,
narrowly escaped being run over by a coal
train in Pittsburg the other day......General
Beauregard met with a slight accident by
his horse falling under him, while riding
over the Bull Run battle field recently, for
the first time since the war......A woman in
Paris keeps ball dresses to rent out by the
day, week or month, and makes money at
it..........Queen Victoria and ex-Empress
Eugenie have been visiting together at
Inverlock Castle and the summit of Ben-
Nevis......The yellow fever is said to be
subsiding at Memphis Tennessee.........A
grand evangelical conference of religious
men has been in session in New York; all
Protestant sects, and all lands where Chris-
tianity has been preached were represented.
......Immense amounts of gold have been
sent to America from the Bank of England.
......Strong hopes of a monarchy are enter-
tained in France......The total number of
paupers in London on the last day of Au-
gust of the present year, was 98,707......
There is a singular natural curiosity in a
Lake in Vermont, consisting of one hun-
dred and fifty acres of floating land, which
is covered with cranberries and has trees
growing upon it fifteen feet high.........The
Paris man "Modiste," Worth, employs
twenty women to make toilets; he never
cuts, fits or sews himself, but merely super-
intends......Russian female students are
refused the right of pursuing the study of
medicine......The “Daily Globe," of Boston,
as discharged all its female compositors,
nd is now, for the first time, run exclu-
sively by males......Spain demands of
England the Vittoria and the Almanza......
Thirty women are employed on the daily
press of New York.
