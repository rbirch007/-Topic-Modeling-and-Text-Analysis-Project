TO THE TEACHERS OF THE RELIEF SOCIETY.

	Toil on, toil on, co-workers here,
	Though your lot be in the lowliest sphere;
	Toil on, toil on, though your task be hard,
	Let the end and aim be heavenward.

	Toil on, toil on, with heart and mind,
	Feet to the weary, eyes to the blind;
	Seeking fresh courage to impart,
	Searching the depths of the human heart

	Toil on, toil on ye cannot tell.
	The many souls ye may win from hell,
	Lead from the depths of darkness and night,
	To a wor'd of everlasting light.

	Toil on, toil on, doubt not, nor fear,
	Speak words of comfort, sweet words of cheer;
	Scatter abroad the precious seed,
	That touches the heart, in its greatest need

	Toil on, toil on, let each passing day,
	Its record bear of its happy sway 
	O’er the young, in teaching the true aim of life. 
	How to live maiden, mother and wife.

	Toil on, toil on, ye shall yet be strong;
	To gather the good, as ye pass along,
	Rich stores of knowledge, wise forethought,
	That poets have sung, and sages have taught.

	Toil on, toil on, 'till your tasks are oer,
	And earth greets your weary steps no more.
	And a radiance effulgent gleams from on high,
	O,er the earth worn spirit, and death dim'd eye;

	And heavenly messengers ear away.
	The spirit freed from its casket of clay,
	To a world where the shadow of death is unknown,
	Nearer, yet nearer, our Father's throne

	There to take up the endless chain,
	With sorrow no more, nor toil nor pain,
	No more of earth's battle, nor care, nor strife,
	Learning pure systems of heavenly life. 
										A. B.S.
Beaver City.
