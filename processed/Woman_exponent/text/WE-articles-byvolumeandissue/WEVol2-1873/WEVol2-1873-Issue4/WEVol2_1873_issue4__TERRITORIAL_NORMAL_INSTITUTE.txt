TERRITORIAL NORMAL INSTITUTE.
The happy idea of taking advantage of the
long summer vacation, which occurs yearly
during the months of July and August, to im-
provise a Normal School or lnstitute seems to us
to be fraught with great good to our educational
interests. There is no direction in which labor
can be spent more profitably and advanta-
geously to the rising generation than in teach-
ing our young and inexperienced teachers how
to preside and direct in the school room.
	When we reflect that Utah has had to use in
the school room as teachers, many whose op-
portunities for acquiring a thorough education
have been very limited indeed, we hail the
inauguration of the Institute with much
gratification, and only wish that our circum-
stances were such as to allow us the pleasure
of spending a portion of time daily in attend-
ance.
	We are pleased to learn that forty-eight
pupil teachers are in attendance from Salt Lake
County, with a few from Weber, Sanpete, Juab,
Morgan and Beaver counties; total seventy-
eight.
	We learn incidentally that the Superinten-
dent of Schools, has shouldered the burden of
engaging Dr. Park, Miss M. E. Cook, Professors
Maeser and Dusenberry to conduct the Insti-
tute; and in this matter it is to be hoped,
his efforts will be sustained, financially, that
he may have satisfaction in his labors.
