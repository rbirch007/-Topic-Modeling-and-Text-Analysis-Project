	TO THE YOUNG LADIES OF UTAH.
MY DEAR FRIENDS:—
	Allow me, through the columns of the
EXPONENT, to offer you a few words.
	It is always pleasing and gratifying to me,
to be able to have my weak voice heard in
relation to the work in which we are en-
gaged.
	We are not, generally, I am afraid, aware
of the responsibilities resting upon us as
daughters of Zion.
	Although young, we have missions to
perform here on the earth, as well as those
good and honorable women who have lived
before us, and those who are yet living to
instruct us in the way in which we should


walk. Our first duty is to listen and learn.
	We should remember that we are yet
young, and liable to be led astray by the
evil influences with which we are daily sur-
rounded.
	Do not, I beg of you, young ladies,let the
“evil doer”get possession of you.
	Remember, that once in his power,you are
utterly lost, as it were; and that you will be
cast out of the society of those who have
loved you. You are no longer respected by
those with whom you were associated. They
do not wish to be in your company. Seek
after the GOOD things of the world, and let
the evil things go to the wind and be swept
from your midst. Strive to live daily and
hourly nearer and nearer to that great Be-
ing, who gave you life, that you may merit
His smiles and blessings, and have His good
spirit to lead and guide you aright continu-
ally; that it may be said of you when your
worldly career is ended “Well done thou
good and faithful servant, enter thou into
the joy of the Lord." 		HETTIE.
