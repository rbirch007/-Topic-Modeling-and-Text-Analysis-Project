BIRTH-DAY GREETING.

	The 21st of Jan., 1874, was the seventieth an-
niversary of the birth-day of Miss Eliza R.
Snow.
	Early in the evening of that day, she was
called upon by a goodly number of the sisters,
who met in honor of the day—to pay their res-
pects to her, and offer congratulations. Although
a surprise, the reception and greeting were most
cordial and happy Miss Snow expressed her
gratification and recognition of the high honor
conferred by her sisters, in those renewed testi-
monies of regard in their kind remembrances
on that her seventieth birth-day. She said that
the day had been a pleasant one, and, to her
that anniversary was the most satisfactory of
any within her remembrance. It was a pleas-
ing reflection that she was really seventy years
of age.
