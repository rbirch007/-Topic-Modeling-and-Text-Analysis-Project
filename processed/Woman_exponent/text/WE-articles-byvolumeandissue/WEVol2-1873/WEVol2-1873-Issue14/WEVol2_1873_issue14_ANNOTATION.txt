ANNOTATION.

	In the present issue of the EXPONENT, will
be found a lengthy report of the doings, for the
past year, of the American Woman Suffrage
Association. We give place to it, believing
that it will be perused with interest by our
readers.
	The fact that the women of Utah enjoy, in
many respects, more freedom than is granted to
women in other parts of the world, does not
give to the intelligent ones among them any
disposition to boast of the advantages they
hold over the less favored of their sex. But
rather creates within them a sympathetic de-
sire to see all others possess as many valuable
privileges and blessings as are enjoyed by them-
selves. We would also have it understood that
the women of Utah are not unappreciative nor
ungrateful for the interest which was mani-
fested in their behalf by the American Woman
Suffrage Association, in its active efforts to de-
feat the Frelinghuysen bill, which was intended
to deprive them of the right of suffrage. Al-
though the action on the part of the Associa-
tion was made in the common cause of women
throughout the Union, yet its immediate bene-
fits were none the less realized by the ladies of
this Territory in the maintaining of their right
to the elective franchise. We consider the ac-
tion displayed a liberal and unsectarian spirit,
strangely in contrast to many who would either
manifest indifference, or, what is still worse,
take active part in seeking to deprive their fel-
low citizens, by unjust legislation, of their con-
stitutional rights.
	In concluding we offer, in behalf of those inter-
ested, our cordial acknowledgments, both, to
all the ladies who so truly befriends the wo-
men of Utah, and also to Senator Sargent for
his able opposition to the gross wrong intend-
ed.
