ACKNOWLEDGMENT.

	In a large assembly of the Senior and Junior
Co-operative Retrenchment Association, on the
6th of December, 1873, the following resolu-
tions were read and unanimously adopted:

	Resolved, That inasmuch as Mrs. Lambert
(late Miss Mary A. Jenkins,) has cheerfully,
faithfully and efficiently discharged her duties
as Secretary of this Association, we deem this
acknowledgment due as a just tribute to un-
assuming merit.
	Resolved, That while we manifest our appre-
ciation of her services, which were only with-
drawn on her departure with her husband on a
mission to the Sandwich Islands, we also ex-
press our commendation of this young lady's
amiable deportment, which has been in every
respect exemplary, irreproachable and worthy
of imitation.
	Resolved, That we unite our faith and
prayers in behalf of this, our young sister, her
husband and all with whom they are associ-
ated as missionaries.

	On motion it was decided that the foregoing
resolutions be published in WOMAN'S EXPO-
NENT and a copy forwarded to the Islands.
	MRS. M. I. HORNE, President,
				MISS ANNA SMITH, Secretary.
