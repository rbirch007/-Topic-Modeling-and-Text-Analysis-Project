THE LOVE OF BEAUTY.

	O flower! the sunshine loves to kiss,
	Speckled or streaked, or bright or dull!
	What can we say of thee but this?—
			So beautiful!

	O grass! that has with glory decked
	The land that else were bare and dull,
	This for thy title we elect—
			Most beautiful!

	O star! that gildest all the sea.
	And fill’st the air with splendor full,
	What is there to be said of thee—
			Save beautiful?

	O child! with tresses spun of gold,
	And eyes with heavenly light o’er full,
	What word has half thy beauty told—
			Like beautiful?

	In truth, when any thing is sweet,
	The grass we tread, the flower we cull,
	The star, the child, we must repeat—
			How beautiful!
