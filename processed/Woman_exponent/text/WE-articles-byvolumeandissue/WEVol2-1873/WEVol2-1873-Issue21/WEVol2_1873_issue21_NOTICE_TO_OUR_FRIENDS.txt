	     NOTICE TO OUR FRIENDS.

	In our last issue we published a few hints to
correspondents, which we trust will be received
kindly, and be productive of the beneficial ef-
fects, for which they were intended. Will our
friends please read them, and cease to send us
anonymous contributions? As an article can-
not receive attention except we are in posses-
sion of the author's real name.—We wish also
to state that obituary poems in memory of pri-
vate individuals cannot be given space in the
columns of the EXPONENT, as there are so
many received of that character, that it would
be inconsistent to attempt their insertion in
our limited space. The time draws near which
brings our second volume to a close; and we
hope to find our friends still in readiness to as-
sist in carrying on the undertaking which we
have engaged in for the general good. As many
subscriptions as can be obtained, should be
sent in before the first of June, at which time


the EXPONENT commences its third year. So
that we may be prepared to issue a sufficient
number of copies to meet the demands from
the beginning of the volume. Many calls have
been made for back numbers of the present
volume, some of which we have been unable
to meet, not having risked the uncertain in-
demnity of issuing a large number of surplu
copies. This disadvantage both to the journal
and to its subscribers we wish to guard against
in future; and therefore give timely notice, in
order that those wishing to secure the full vol-
ume may do so by sending in their subscrip-
tions before the time arrives for issuing the first
number of volume third.
