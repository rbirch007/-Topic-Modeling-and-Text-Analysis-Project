A MODEL DAIRYMAID.

	Charles Nordhoff, in his California travels,
found some model dairy farms. In most of
them men did the milking and also the
butter making; but, alas! he was captivated
with one pretty face he met by chance:
	“On one farm I found a pretty Sweedish,
girl superintending all the in-door work


with such skill and order in all departments
that she possessed, so far as I saw, the
model dairy on the estate. Here, said I to
myself, is now an instance of the ability of
women to compete with men, which would
delight Mrs. Stanton and all the woman's
rights people. Here is the neatest, the
sweetest, the most complete dairy in the
whole region; the best order, the most shin-
ing utensels, the nicest butter-room—and
not only butter, but cheese is also made,
which is not usual; and here is a rosy-faced,
white-armed, smooth-haired, sensibly-dress-
ed, and altogether admirable, and to my
eyes beautiful Sweedish lass presiding over
it all, commanding her men servants and
keeping every part of the business in order.
Alas! Mrs. Stanton, she has discovered a
better business than butter-making. She is
going to marry—sensible girl that she is;
and she is not going to marry a dairy-far-
mer either. I doubt if any body in Califor-
nia can make such nice butter as this pretty
Sweed. Certainly every other dairy I saw
seemed to me common-place and uninterset-
ing after I had seen hers. I don't doubt
that the young man who had the art to
persuade her to love him ought to be hang-
ed, because butter-making is far more im-
portance than marrying. Nevertheless, I
wish him joy in advance; and hereby give
it as my belief that the pretty Sweed is a
sensible girl."
