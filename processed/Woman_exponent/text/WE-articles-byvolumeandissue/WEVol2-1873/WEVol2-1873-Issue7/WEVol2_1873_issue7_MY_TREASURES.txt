			MY TREASURES.

	My Mountain Home, dear, honored place,
	  My heart doth fondly love thee;
	I love the light of nature's face
	  Dispensed around, above me.

	The snow hills sunk to murmuring floods,
	  The grave, surrounding towers,
	A floating field of gorgeous clouds,
	  And slopes of waving flowers.

	Ye all have notes that reach my ear,
	   With soft, mellifluous wooing.
	But through my spirit's silent sphere
	  Is music deeper flowing.

	Eternal truth has waked my soul,
	  And chants with sacred pleasure,
	Though nature's charms rare gifts unroll,
	  Faith is my holiest treasure.
									KENT.
