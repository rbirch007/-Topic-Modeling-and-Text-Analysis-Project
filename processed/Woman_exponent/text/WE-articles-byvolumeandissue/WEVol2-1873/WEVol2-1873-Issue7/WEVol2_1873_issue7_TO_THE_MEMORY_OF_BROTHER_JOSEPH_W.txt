   TO THE MEMORY OF BROTHER JOSEPH W.
				 YOUNG

	He lives in the hearts of the people he loved,
		In the hearts of the orphan, and widow, yes; 
	In the hearts of the aged, the lonely, and poor--
		He lived but to comfort and bless.

	Oh Friend! in the days when friends were so few
		The day of thy rest, and glory has come;
	How “Blessed the dead who die in the Lo d,”
		And their labors do follow them home.

	Though the mantle of sorrow falls heavy and dark,
		And thy loved ones must mourn for their Head,
	God pity them! grant them that Spirit of thine, 
		That will sweeten the tears which they shed.

	For the good of the Kingdom his life has been given,
		'Twill be said, “though his body lies low,
	  In the battle of Truth against error he fell
	With his face toward heaven, his f et to the foe !"
	
	Oh true tender heart! farewell for awhile, 
		We all the dark Valley must tread.
	And the humble and true shall meet thee again
		Through the merits of Jesus our Head.
					Agness S, Armstrong.
Ephraim June 15th 1873


[For the Exponent.
