UNHAPPY HUSBAND.

	Perhaps it is your own fault. Too many
men take all the love their wives can
give them as a matter of course, never
thinking to return it till the wife gets tired
of looking for it and ceases to bestow affec-
tion. Court your wife, just as you did in
your wooing. It won't hurt you, and de-
pend upon it, you can win her back in no
other way :– We have known husbands to
never once bestow a word or look of love
for years, and when at last their disap-
pointed wives are caught gaily laughing and
chatting with other company in the very
vein of humor which first enchained these
exacting lords, they have become as cross
and repellant as hornet-stung bears. Don't
be such a noodle, we beg. Women are not
angels, and they can't always endure every-
thing. A man is foolish who expects to
retain the warm, girlish affections of his
wife by indifference when he gained them
only by most attentive wooing. We repeat,
court her. Let her see that you care for
her, that her comfort is essential to your
enjoyment, and if she don't forgive and love
and bless you we know nothing about
women, that's all.—"New North West."
