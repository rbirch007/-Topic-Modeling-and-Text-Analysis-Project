FACTS AND FIGURES.
	The total length of railroads in Germany,
at the close of 1859, was 7,949 miles.
	The first trial by jury in California is said
to have taken place, among a party of Mor-
mons at Yerba Buena, 1846.
	Dr. M'Cosh says that there are 20,000
Presbyterian congregations in the world,
embracing a population of 34,000,000.
	Iron boiler tubes in a steamship rust out
very fast when the vessel is laid by, they
endure five times longer when in constant
use.
	The large iron pipes employed in the
Glasgow (Scotland) Water-works were orig-
inally, four feet in diameter, 1 [-] of an inch
thick, and sustained a pressure from about
300 feet of head.
	A Major Colborne backs himself for £100
to walk fifteen miles in three hours on the
Maidstone road, England. He is to carry a
brick weighing six and a half pounds in
each hand, so as to touch his sides.
	Christopher Shearer, a fruit famer near
Reading, Pennsylvania, has probably the
largest refrigerator in the country. It is
fifty-five feet square, and holds, when filled,
about one hundred and forty cartloads of ice
and four thousand bushels of fruit.
	The Ipswich (England) "Journal" states
that on the 12th of May, at Lowestoft, Lady
Smith, aged 100 years, gave a dinner to 107
old people, whose united ages amounted to
8,228 years, or about an average of seventy-
seven to each.
	One-fourth of a share in a London water
company was lately sold for £16,000. The
face value of an entire share is only £1! It
is one of the wealthiest corporations in the
world, but its projector lost his fortune and 
died in the workouse. Its name is the
New River Company.
