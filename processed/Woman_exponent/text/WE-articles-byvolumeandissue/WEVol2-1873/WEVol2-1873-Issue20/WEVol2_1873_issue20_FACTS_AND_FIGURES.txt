FACTS AND FIGURES.

	Of the new trade dollars 1,400,000 have been
coined.
	Iowa grew more wheat in 1873 than any other
state.
	There are forty Protestant places of worship in
Rome.
	Boston has twenty-one Baptist churches, with
7,579 members.
	The Buffalo (N. Y.) Young Men's Christian As-
sociation is 38 years of age.
	Coaches or carriages were first used in England
in the year 1530. They were introduced from Ger-
many by the Earl of Arundel.
	In New York in winter, the price of a handsome
basket of flowers is from five to fifty dollars. Bou-
quets can be made at from three to twenty-five dol-
lars.
	A "fossil" egg, said to be larger than that of an
ostrich, has been purchased for the St. Petersburgh
museum, at a cost of seven hundred and fifty dol-
lars. It was found in one of the Russian provin-
ces.
	Extensive farming.—The Colusa Sun states that
Mr. H. E. Glenn, of that county will put in 40,000
acres of wheat this year. It is calculated the yield
will be 1,000,000 bushels, and worth, at least, $1,000
000.
	The "Publishers Circular" (England) states that
during the past year there were published in that
country, 3,463 new books, 1,286 new additions of
former works and 242 reprints of American publi-
cations; making a total of 4,991. According to the
classification, tales, works of fiction, etc. were the
most numerous, while works on law were the few-
est in number.
