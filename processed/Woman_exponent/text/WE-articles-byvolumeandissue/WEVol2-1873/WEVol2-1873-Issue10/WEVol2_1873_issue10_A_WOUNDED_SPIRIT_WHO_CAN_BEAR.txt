A WOUNDED SPIRIT WHO CAN BEAR.
	
	In reflecting upon the wrongs which
woman is subjected to in the world, and the
evils now existing in consequence of her not
being allowed the choice of a husband in
aocordance with her affections, I have
thought, how appropriate for the considera-
tion of man in these days is the sweet ap-
peal of the poetess, Mrs Felitia D. Hemans:
		"Oh! cast thou not
		Affection from thee in this bitter world,
		Hold to thy heart that only treasure fast;
		Watch, guard it, suffer not a breath to dim
		That bright gem purity."
