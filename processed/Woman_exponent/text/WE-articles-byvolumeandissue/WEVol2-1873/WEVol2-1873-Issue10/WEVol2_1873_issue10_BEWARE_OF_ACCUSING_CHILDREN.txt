BEWARE OF ACCUSING CHILDREN

	Under this heading perhaps a few words
may not be deemed inappropriate. Years 
and experience have given the writer op-
portunity to observe the influence prevail-
ing over children, that more or less help to 
mold their characters for the future iuflu- 
ence very much to be deprececated and most
heartily to be condemned; as often the re-
sults are lamentable.
	Living in a fsst age comparatively, very 
little consideration, is given to these things,
with all the boasted knowedgc of modern 
times. Children are expected to know
more, which, in fact, they do at a very ten-
der age, than in former days; children were 
children then, until really old enough to 
have some strength of mind and under- 
standing; parents were more cautious, in
respect to conversation in their hearing,
which now seems almost totally done away 
with. Careless habits have gained ground;
for as the world stands, no great or good 
things are advanced without more or less
evil to bring up the rear; which often proves 
almost an overbalance of power, counter-
acting, in a great degree, the good attained.
Thus it is only by the most strenuous efforts
and the exercise of exceeding watchfulness
and care, on the part of those engaged in
God's vineyard—to uproot and drive forth
the little evils, which like the moth, silent- 
ly, ceaslessly and most certainly work sor-
row and woe- that anything can be done.
Among the minor evils, one that carries 
great sway with the generality of people
and families, is that hasty accusation:
Children are often corrected in anger and
haste; more often than in calmness, or with
due deliberation. Because a certain piece 
of mischief is done, some child did it;
and perhaps the child, perfectly innocent of
the mischief said to be his or her doing, is
punished severely for it no matter how 
much he or she may say and protest
