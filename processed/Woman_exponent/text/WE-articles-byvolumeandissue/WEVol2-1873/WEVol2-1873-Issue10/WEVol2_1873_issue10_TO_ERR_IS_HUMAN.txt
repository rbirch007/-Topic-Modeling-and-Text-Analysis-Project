TO ERR IS HUMAN.

	Truth reveals that mortals are all erring,
and one time or another every one is guilty
of that which memory would rather blot
from the record.
	It sounds well for people to aver in lofty
style that they never did anything of which
to be ashamed. There is not, and probably
never was, a person so perfect that to a great-
er or less degree he or she has never done
wrong, wilfully or ignorantly. In either case
a blush of shame is but a slight token that
the spirit has realized higher and better
things, and has risen above the possibility
of committing the same error again.
