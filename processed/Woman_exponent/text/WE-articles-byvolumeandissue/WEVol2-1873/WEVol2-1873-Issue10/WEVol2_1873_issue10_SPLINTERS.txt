SPLINTERS.

	New Orleans has a swimming school for
ladies.	......$20,000 is said to have been
made by the "Graphic" balloon enterprise.
......The Grand Vizier of the Shah has been
sent to prison.	    ......Barnum stops at Sara-
toga while his great show goes round.
......William Penn's house in Philadelphia
has been made into a beer saloon.	......Pres-
ident Grant's Secretary, Gen. Babcock, and
wife, have gone to Europe.	......Vienna
has sixty splendid orchestras, all full.
......Gen. McClellan's summer residence is
at Maywood, N. J.	......Chestnut timber,
five hundred years old, in Westminister
Abbey, is still sound.	......Within three
weeks, four persons have dropped dead on
one street in Atlanta, Georgia.	......The
British navy has lately had three new war
vessels added, and there are twenty-five
others being built.	......The frequency of
criminals committing suicide in their cells,
is attracting attention in Switzerland.
......In one day, duties amounting to $15,000
were collected on Canadian wool at St. Al-
bans, Vt.	     ......Eighty-six women in Mich-
igan, have entered the University this
term. 	......Quincy, Ill., has set its organ
grinders to sweeping streets. 	......Great
destruction has been made among the grape
crops of Portugal this season by vine dis-
ease. 	......A celebrated brigand in Southern
Italy, known as Manzi, has lately been cap-
tured and his band extirpated.		......Mad-
am Nilsson and her husband, Sig. Salvini,
were lately visiting in New York.	......The 
Pope has recovered from his recent indispo-
sition.	......Joaquin Miller has been passing
a season with Minister Gladstone in Wales.
......A widower in Michigan, who has forty-
eight children, recently married his fourth
wife....... 	A New York writer gives an arti-
cle on "Intelligent Mules," and it is believed
if he can prove there are such, that women
will no longer 	denied the ballot. 	......A
new cave has been discovered near "The
Cave of the Winds," at Niagara Falls, which
promises to be one of the wonders of Amer-
ica.
