HOLD STILL.

	In a private communication from Beaver,
mention is made of the cowardly acts of some
men in seeking to wrest from the rightful own-
ers portions of land in that locality. Our cor-
respondent says: "It can only be styled at-
tempted mobocracy when individuals from
other parts of the country come here, no doubt
scapegraces, escaped from justice for theft or
something worse, and claim the land which
our people have subdued and tilled for years.
Some of which is almost the entire dependence
of poor men, and widows and orphans." If pro-
fanity wasn't against the principles of the EX-
PONENT, we might think of something horribly
bad in contemplating this representation.
