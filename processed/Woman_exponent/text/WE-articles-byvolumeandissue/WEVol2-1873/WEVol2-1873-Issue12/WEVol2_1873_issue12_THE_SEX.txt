THE SEX.

	“We need woman’s influence in govern-
ment, to aid in the suppression of vice and
intemperance.”—“Bishop Simpson.”
	THE girl of this age is unfit to be wed
who don’t understand  how to make nice
sweet bread. And the man is a dunce who
a partner will take, who don’t undersand
how to wash, knit and bake.
	AN Illinois girl having six lovers offered
to marry the one who should “break up”
the most prairie in three days. The result
was she got a smart husband, and her father
found his farm ready for planting free of
cost.
	WITH woman’s influence with us, we
shall have no such word as fail. Her in-
fluence is for good, and we are glad to know
she will exert it in behalf of those who are
nearest and dearest to her.”—Coleman’s
“Rural World.”
	A MARRIED friend of ours said he would
always have remained single, but he
couldn’t afford it. What it  cost him for
“gals and ice cream,” was more than he
now pays to bring up a wife and eight chil-
dren. Bachelors should think of this.
	IN TURKESTAN a traveler relates, if the
husband of any woman go away upon a
journry and remain away for more than
twenty days, as soon as that time is past the
woman may marry another man, and the
husband also may marry whom he pleases.
