HOME AFFAIRS.

	A LARGE iron manufacturing company has
been lately organized with a capital of $250,000,
the works of which will be located in Ogden.
	WE WOULD again call attention to the list of
agents which appears in our columns, and again
solicit additions to it, from settlements not re-
presented therein.
	BRO. ISAAC Laney died on the 31st ult., the
thirty-fifth anniversary ot the massacre at
Haun's Mill, Missouri, where he was brutally
wounded by the mob.
	AN APPEAL to the Territorial Teacher's As-
sociation, which has appeared in the daily
papers, has unintentionally been crowded out
of our present issue, but will be given space in
our next.
	SEVERAL SERIOUS accidents have lately oc-
curred in and about some of the mines in this
Territory; among which we note with much
regret, the loss of an eye, and other severe in-
juries to Mr. Ben Hampton, while he was
blasting in the Chicago mine in Davis kanyon.
	ANOTHER PETITION in the form of a memo-
rial has been produced by the "Mormon Eat-
ers," praying Congress to pass laws for the
correcting of evils among the "Mormons." An-
other death and funeral will likely follow, and
then another resurrection in some other form.
	UTAH HAS never known a more glorious In-
dian Summer, than that which has hung about
her hills and valleys for the last three weeks, and
if ever her majestic beauty and graceful loveli-
ness, were calculated to inspire the artist or the
poet with "thoughts sublime," it has been since
the middle of last month until the present
time. Travelers who visit this Territory dur-
ing this delightful season of the year, may con-
sider themselves exceedingly fortunate in their
choice of time for "doing" our "Mountain
Home."
