		NEW YEAR'S GREETING.

	SONG. Tune, "The Mistletoe Bough:"
The glad New Year—the glad New Year!
In varied guises it doth appear:—
Eyes of beauty and eyes of light;
Looks of gladness and love unite;
While many a form in reverence bows
To hail old Time with his fresh young spouse;
And the sorrowing heart, and the anguished tear,
All greet the glorious, glad New Year.
			CHORUS: O! The happy New Year—
					      Joyous glad New Year!

The mother meets fondly her new-born's breath,
And kindred weep o'er the bed of death:
There are sounds of mirth 'round the festal board,
With luxurious dainties in fulness stored;
While the miser, unmoved, hears the plaintive calls
Of famishing souls by his grim, cold walls;
But the blest and the wise with their bounteous cheer,
Forget not the poor on the glad New Year.
			Chorus: O! The happy New Year!etc.

The clattering hoof and the chiming bell,
Of out-door merriment boldly tell;
In sportive haste glide the skaters by,
Bowing and smiling as eye meets eye;
'Mid blithsome music and dance and song,
The young folks are speeding bright hours along;
And the laughter of little ones gleeful and clear,
Greets gaily the dawn of the glad New Year.
			Chorus: O! The happy New Year, etc.

But of all good things, of all things blest,
Thanks and praise to our God is best!
No other light can the day impart,
T' compare with the bliss of the grateful heart;
O! dearest hope, He will guide us still,
O! sweetest work to perform His will!
His Spirit and blessings and gifts are here—
O! praise ye His name on this glad New Year!
			Chorus: O! The happy New Year, etc.
										LULA.
