	ST. GEORGE, UTAH, Dec. 18th, 1873.
EDITOR WOMAN'S EXPONENT:
	This beautiful morning, I take occasion,
according to promise, to address you a few
lines.
	We left Salt Lake City, Nov. 28th, and
arrived here Dec. 15th; had a prosperous 
journey. There were storms and cold wea-
ther after we left Nephi. We were detained
one day at Kanarra on account of wind and
snow.
	Our party consists of Prest. Young, his
wife Amelia, three teamsters and two young
girls; Bro. A. M. Musser and teamster;
my husband and son, and your correspon-
dent. We held twenty-five meetings on
the way. I met with and addressed the
sisters at Nephi, Ephraim, and Richfield;
the audiences were large. At Ephraim, the
members of the Relief Society were build-
ing a fine hall; they had also donated one
thousand dollars towards gathering the poor
Saints from Scandinavia; they are a splen-
did band of sisters, very energetic in all
their movements; they take about thirty
numbers of the WOMAN'S EXPONENT and
calculate to increase the number of subscrib-
ers, although many cannot read English.
In Richfield the same difficulties occur, many
not being able to read English. Richfield is
a new settlement, but enterprising; I had
a good time with the sisters there.
	The new road from Richfield across the
mountain to Cove Creek Fort, thirty-five
miles, passes through some very romantic
scenery. This road is a great improvement.
	Sevier County contains five hundred en-
terprising families; though the settlements
are all new, the irrigating canals are on a
scale that would do credit to the oldest coun
ties.  The red soil of this valley is very 
rich and is capable of sustaining a large
population.
	Sanpete County has sent ninety-three men
to assist in building the St. George Temple,
finding their own supplies. They com-
menced work to-day.
	Success attend your enterprise.
