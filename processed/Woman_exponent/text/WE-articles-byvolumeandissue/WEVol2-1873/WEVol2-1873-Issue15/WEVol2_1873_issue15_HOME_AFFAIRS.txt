HOME AFFAIRS.

	THANKS, FOR kind complimentaries on
Christmas, from friends in the 1st Ward,
and from Mrs. Mary L. Morris of the 15th
Ward.
	TO DAY, a Sabbath School and Ward pic-
nic is held in the 14th Ward Assembly
Rooms. Elder John Taylor and Miss Eliza
R. Snow honor the company with their pre-
sence. We shall be able to give more par-
ticulars concerning the pleasantries and
good times of the day in our next.
	THE UTAH Northern R. R. which was
blockaded a short time since between Logan
and Hampton's Station, has, through the
energies and perseverence of the President
and men of the road, again been rendered
passable. Along a considerable length of
the track, a snow fence has been made, and
where it is necessary, sheds are being put
up. The trains  run now on time.
	THE SALT Lake Fire Department advances
rapidly in the line of progression. On the
26th ult., a new company, consisting of more
than one hundred men, was organized by
chief-engineer McAllister. Its station will
be at, or near the depot. This makes the
sixth company belonging to the department.
Last evening we met with a most agreeable
company at the Fire Brigade Ball, held in
the 13th Ward Assembly Rooms. The hall
and withdrawing room, which were fitted


up by the Company, with articles mostly
belonging to itself, were as neatly and com-
fortably furnished as any we have had the
pleasure ot entering this season. Works of
art of various kinds, from their library,
which graced the tables of the withdrawing
room, spoke well for the good taste and love
of refinement which is cultivated and en-
couraged by the officers and members of the
department. The ball is to be continued
this evening,so as to give all the firemen an
opportunity to participate in the festivities,
and enjoy themselves.
