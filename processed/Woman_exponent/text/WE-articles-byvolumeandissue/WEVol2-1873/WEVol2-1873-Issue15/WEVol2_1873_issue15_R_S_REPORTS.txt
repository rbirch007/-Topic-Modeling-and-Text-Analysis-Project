R. S. REPORTS.

	Mrs. Christina Lilienquist, President of
the Relief Society at Hyrum, Cache Co.,
writes from that place under date Dec. 9, 1873:
And informs us that the Society there is do-
ing a good work in caring for the sick; and
that the Teachers are very faithful in visit-
ing from house to house. They appreciate
and prize the EXPONENT, and realize that
it is a great help to them as a Society. Their
financial affairs are in a very good condition.

	Secretary Mrs. J. Rickard, of the Second
Ward in this city, reports a meeting of the
Relief Society, Dec. 11th, 1873, which was
attended by Miss Eliza R. Snow and Mrs.
Zina D. Young. After much good instruc-
tion had been given to those assembled in
meeting, a young ladies' association was or-


ganized according to the proper form; with
Miss Maria Petersen as President; Miss
Maria McGregor, Miss Josephine Johnson,
Miss Mary Lun, Miss Emma Hicks Counse-
lors, (two other counselors to be afterwards
chosen,) and Miss Mary Jensen Secretary.

	A highly interesting report of a special
meeting, held in the Sugar House Ward,
Dec. 10, 1873, is forwarded by Secretary
Mrs, Sarah Wheeler. The aged, and much
respected and beloved Mother in Israel,
Sister Elizabeth A. Whitney, was present
as a visitor, in company with Miss E. R.
Snow, Mrs. Z. D. Young and several others.
The sweet singing and precious words given
by Sister Whitney, formed a very pleasing
and characteristic part of the meeting.
