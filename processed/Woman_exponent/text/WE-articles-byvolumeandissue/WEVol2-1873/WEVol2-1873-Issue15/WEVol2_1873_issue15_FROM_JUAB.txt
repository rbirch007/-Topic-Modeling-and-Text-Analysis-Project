FROM JUAB.
		NEPHI, Dec. 15th 1873.
Dear Sister Greene Richards:
	I will not say Editor WOMAN'S EXPO-
NENT, because I never learned to write for
the press, neither can I put my ideas on pa-
per in a manner to please myself.  But if
you can, at any time, cull an item from my
writing to you, that will be of any use to
your paper, you are at liberty to do so.
	Two weeks ago yesterday, Prest. Young
and party arrived here, and stayed over Sun-
day. During the time, we had much valua-
ble preaching. Prest. G. A. Smith, wife
and son stopped with us. We had a fine
time visiting with Sister Bathsheba. She
requested me to write a few lines about her
visit here. We had a meeting called of the
Relief Society. There was a general turn
out, to listen to the good advice and whole-
some counsel of Sister Smith. She spoke
on the subjects of Sunday Schools and the
interest we as mothers, should take in get-
ting our children ready to go, in teaching
them to pay tithing, to help build temples
and every other good work. Said the sis-
ters should sustain their husbands in every
good thing; they should be careful to ob-
serve the words of wisdom. She spoke to
the young ladies on retrenchment, and on
educating themselves, that they might be
able to fill important stations in the King-
dom of God.
	Prest. Smith also gave us some good
teaching, also Br. Musser, Sister Hartley
Prest. of the Relief Society at Levan, and
also our own Prest., Sister A. Goldsbrough,
who felt so much blessed at having the privi-
lege of meeting with Sister Smith and the 
other visitors, in which I felt to heartily join.
Taking it all together, we had a glorious
time.
	I believe it is a general time of health
here, at present, although I am not quite
well myself. The weather has been very
unfavorable of late. Money is very scarce.
	Wishing you success in every laudable
pursuit, I subscribe myself
		Yours sincerely,
