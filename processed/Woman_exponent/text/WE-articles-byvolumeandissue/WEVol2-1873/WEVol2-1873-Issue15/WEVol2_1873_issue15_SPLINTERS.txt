SPLINTERS.

	A hundred and sixty young ladies attend
the Cincinnati Wesleyan College............A
daughter of the poet Burns, Mrs. Thomp-
son died at Crossmyloof, near Glasgow, re-
cently, aged eighty-four years............Miss
Yonge is writing a Child's History of Eng-
land......Dr. Elizabeth Pope of Oneonta, N.
Y., has an extensive practice in that village
......Mrs. John P. Croser sent $5,000 as a
gift for the endowment fund of the Baptist
House for aged women at Philadelphia,
which was opened November 8th............
Volunteers for Cuba are organizing in
Louisiana......It is estimated that but half a
crop of anything has been raised in Mon-
tana this year......Persimmmon seeds are used
in Georgia as a substitute for Coffee.........
Wendell Philips lectures on "Labor, Tem-
perance and Woman"......A restriction of
Chinese immigration is favored by Geo
Booth of California.....Zuleika, a Persian
actress, is soon to make an appearance in
London......The hat, cloak and cane of Lord
Byron were lately exhibited at a Scottish
Fair......Watchel, Stanley and Parepa Rosa
return to America next season.......The indi-
go plant is indigenous to Florida......Mrs.
H. M. Tracy Cutler is spending the winter
in Paris......The town of Milton, California,
was recently almost entirely destroyed by a
tornado.
