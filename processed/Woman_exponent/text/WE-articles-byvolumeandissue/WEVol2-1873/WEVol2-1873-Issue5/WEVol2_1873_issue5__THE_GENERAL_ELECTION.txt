THE GENERAL ELECTION.

	A Convention composed of gentlemen and
ladies was held in this city on the 19th ult., for
the purpose of nominating candidates for Coun-
selors to the next Legislative Assembly,
and also other officers to be elected at the forth-
coming general eletion. A committee of
nine, comprising seven gentlemen and two
ladies, after consultation, presented for candi-
dates the following names which the Conven-
tion unanimously accepted.
	For Commissioners to locate University lands:
John Van Cott, Lewis S. Hills and John Row-
berry.


﻿For Counselors: Wilford Woodruff, William
Jennings, William H. Hooper and John T.
Caine.
	For Selectman for Salt Lake county. E. M.
Weiler.
	For Coroner for Salt Lake county: George J.
Taylor.
	Appropriate speeches were made by Messrs. E.
L. Sloan and George Q. Cannon. We do not go so
far on the woman suffrage qeuestion as does Hen-
ry Ward Beecher; that is, to say that it should be
not only the right but the duty of women to
vote, and if any woman does not want to vote
she should be compelled to do so. But we
think those women of Utah act wisely, who
seek to understand, and show their appre-
ciation of the rights granted them. And on
Monday next, the 4th inst., which is the day
appointed for General Election throughout the
Territory, every woman who has arrived at
the proper age specified for voting, should go to
the polls, taking care she does not get tricked
by any plan which may be laid for the decep-
tion of the inexperienced and unsuspecting,
and vote the People’s Ticket.
