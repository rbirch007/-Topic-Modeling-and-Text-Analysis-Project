R. S. REPORTS.

	Report from Lehi, July 11th 1873: Our
Society is still prospering; the sisters are
united, not only in helping the poor at home,
but alsp doing their best to assist the poor
brethren and sisters in other lands, that
they may come and enjoy some of the bless-
ings we enjoy in these peaceful valleys.
The EXPONENT is a welcome visitor among
us, and we wish more of the sisters could
realize its value. 
	REBECCA STANDRING, Secretary.

	The following comes to hand from Rich-
mond, bearing date July 3rd, 1873; The
Richmond Relief Society was organized
June 4th 1868. We held our annual meet-
ing June 4th the present season and found
our donations to the poor had been liberal,
besides donations to the P. E. Fund and
other purposes, in the last year. We have
"working bees" in Richmond as well as
quiet, listless ones who only attend to their
own household duties and leave the cares of
the Society to the few who are willing to 
work for their own interest and that of the
Kingdom of God. Accept, sincerely our
best wishes in behalf of your worthy enter-
prise
			SARAH ANN MERRILL, Prest.
			BETSY BROWER, Secretary.
