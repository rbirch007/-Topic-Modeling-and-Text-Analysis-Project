					﻿GRIM DUTY.

		Duty's pathway leads away
		Toward the rising sun;
		The misty morning shadows gray
		Enfold the mountins dun;
		Their hoary peaks long shadows throw,
		Quaint echos dwell among,
		Deep hidden vales send from below
		Wild waters’ ringing song.

		But though the way seems lone and rough,
		'Tis but to waken z al
		In those whose love is warm enough
		True ardor’s fire to feel.
		Though clouds may lower and thunders roar 
		The timid to affright,
		Fa r faith breathes calmly evermore, 
		“Byond this all is light.”

		And Duty’s self, so seeming grim
		Looks ever to the sun;
		Her face is not by pilgrims seen
		Until the goal is won;
		Kike carven stone she pointing stands,
		Unheeding anguished cries;
		The rod she holds in ridgid hands, 
		Affrights the pleading eyes.

		Imagination flutters near,
		Each plain thing to distort,
		To change high courage into fear,
		To smite each heavy heart,
		To paint all things in darkest shade,
		To magnify all ills,
		To bid all hopeful visions fade
		And break the bravest wills. 

		The pearly shades of infant morn 
		Appear foreboding gloom;
		The joyous echoes, sound forlorn
		As wailings o’er a tomb;
		The deeptoned thunder’s mighty peal—
		Majestic voice of power,
		The false one saith are raging fiends
		Wayfarers to devour.

		The gurgling waters in the vales.
		She saith are roaring floods;
		The softly sighing upland gales,
		Storms not to be withstood;
		The gentle slopes appear too steep
		For weary feet to tread,
		And shadows sweet, by mounds asleep,
		Like precipices dread.

		The rustle cool of smmer leaves
		seems like a serpent’s hiss.
		The kind caress which friendship gives,
		A very Judas kiss.
		She weaves her spell for human woe, 
		To turn them from the way,
		That they may not triumphant go
		Through to the land of Day. 

		And busily she flits between
		Weak human eyes and faith
		In duty, who, if fairly seen,
		Is not a mocking wraith
		Delighting in her foll’wers pain
		And smiting with her rod,
		But promising eternal gain
		And pointing up to God.

			And those who follow her sure guide
		Unto the utmost height,
		Behold, entranced, the prospect wide—
		The source of living light,
		The risen sun, the perfect day,
		The glorious halo thrown
		Around the toiler who can say 
		“My work is nobly done.”

		And Duty’s face, with smiles aglow,
		Now meets his ’raptured gaze—
		A “scepter” is the rod she bore!
		And pleasant look the ways
		Which had before so dreadful seemed
		As faintly he toild on;
		The full light sparkles on the streams
		Which sang through misty dawn,
		How vain! how needless were his fears!
		How blind his groping eyes!
		How false that everything was drear
		Which wore sweet duty’s guise!
		A shining angel, there she stands,
		Endowed with beauties rare,
		And points the way with tireless hands,
		To those who struggle there.

		O, Pilgrims! heed the faithful guide 
		Who will so kindly lead
		Your footsteps up the mountain side 
		From fancied terrors freed.
		The mornig breaks, the sun appears,
		No gloom before him dwells;
		Be steadfast through the night of tears
		Which mornig light dispels.
