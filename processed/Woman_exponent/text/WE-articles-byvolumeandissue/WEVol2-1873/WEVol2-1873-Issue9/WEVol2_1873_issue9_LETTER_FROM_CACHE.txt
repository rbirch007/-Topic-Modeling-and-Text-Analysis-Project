LETTER FROM CACHE.

			RICHMOND, Sept. 9th, 1873.
EDITOR WOMAN'S EXPONENT:—
	Perhaps a few items from this place
would not be uninteresting to your readers.
	Sunday the 24th ult., a boy named Abra-
ham Eskelson was struck by lightning on the
right shoulder, which passed over his bowels,
down his left leg severely bruising him; his
left heel was so badly torn that Dr. Ormsby,
who was telegraphed for, considered ampu-
tation necessary. He died on the 27th.
The same day on which the above stated
accident occurred, Bp. Lunt and wife were
thrown from a wagon, his collar bone was
broken, and her face considerably bruised.
Dr. Ormsby jr. attended. The Sunday fol-
lowing, a boy named David Adams, who
lived across the street from Bro. Eskelson's,
was struck dead by lightning, and five
other boys were knocked down, but none
of them were injured. I have heard of
several other accidents by lightning having
recently occurred in this county. There is
considerable sickness in this place, but
through the blessings of God, very few
deaths. 
						P. A. M.
