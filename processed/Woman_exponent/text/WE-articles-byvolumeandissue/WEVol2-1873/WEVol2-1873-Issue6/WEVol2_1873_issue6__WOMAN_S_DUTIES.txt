WOMAN'S DUTIES.

	What a vast amount has been said and
written on the duties of woman, and how
severely she is censured for the non-per-
formance of those duties. We will admit
there are many who never learn what their
duty is, or learning it, neglect it most
shamefully.
	On the other hand, there are thousands
who bravely battle with the trials of life,
seeking to render their homes comfortable
and attractive, striving to assist and benefit
their partners in life, but whose career,
shut out from the public ken [-]y the seclu-
sion of home, is never known beyond the
retirement of the home circle, and conse-
quently never receives its just meed of
praise. The name of the conqueror—the
statesman—may illuminate the pages of
history, while self-sacrificing, woman sinks
into obscurity, and her name is forgotten.

	How much better we’d get along in this
sphere of ours were we less prone to neglect
our own business to attend to that of our
neighbors! But I suppose that is to
happen in the Golden Age, which looks
as though it never would arrive. There is
a great deal we can do one another in the
way of true, genuine kindness, but it is too
often left undone, because it is so much
easier to do evil than good, and to say
mischievous, spiteful things, than pleasant
and charitable ones. Fault-finding comes
handier than praise, but it isn’t half so good,
if you did but know it. Come now, let’s you
and I see if we can’t all join hands, and say
we won’t mind anybody’s business except
our own, but will work for others’ good, and
not evil.
