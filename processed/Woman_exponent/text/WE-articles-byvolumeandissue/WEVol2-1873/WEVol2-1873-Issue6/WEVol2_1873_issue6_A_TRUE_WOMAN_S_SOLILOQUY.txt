	A TRUE WOMAN’S SOLILOQUY.

		"And they twain shall be one flesh."
	Shall we indeed be one! one in mind, in
thought, in action one—to consumate the
great, the grand, the eternal will of Him
who said—“and they twain shall be one
flesh?” Though two in material, corporeal,
and personal identity, yet one in spirit—in
love, in union one: being formed to affiliate,
to harmonize and co-operate, we become one
in spirit, in essence one, and one in repro-
duction—the spirit and true aim of all
congugal affections;—The purpose of our
creation:—The end to mature eternal lives:—
	My husband and I are one.
							Axiom.
