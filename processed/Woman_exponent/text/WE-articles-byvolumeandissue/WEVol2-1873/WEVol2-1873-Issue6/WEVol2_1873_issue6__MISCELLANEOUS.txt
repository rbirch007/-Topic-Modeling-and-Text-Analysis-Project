MISCELLANEOUS.
					
	A ROOT of a tree containg a brick is the
latest addition to Egolf's museum.
	AN English jury assessed the value of a
mouthful, taken out of a man's leg by a dog,
at $250.
	KEEP your store of smiles and your kind-
est thoughts for home, give to the world
only those you have to spare.
	THE habit of looking on the best side of 
every event is worth more than a thousand
pounds a year.—“Dr. Johnson.”
	The Chinese have recently been organiz-
ing their army, which now numbers 300,000
men armed with the Enfield, Remington
and Colt rifles, and with a full compliment
of rifled artillery.
	TEMPLE'S comet has been observed by
Herr Pechule, at Hamburg, and by several
other observers. It may now be considered
to some extent a permanent member of our
system. Its next return will be looked for
early in 1879.
	AN Illinois magistrate has fined a man
$10 for cutting off his wife's back hair.
Under what statute such an offence is
ranked we are not informed. Perhaps
under the head of barberous treatment and
shear brutality.
	THE name of England, commonly sup-
posed to be a corruption of the word Auglos,
is in reality derived from a village near
Sleswick in Denmark, named Angleu, whose
population was the first to join the Saxon
freebooters in their invasion of Great Britian.
	AN Irish physician was called to examine
the corpse of another Irishman, who had
been assassinated by some of his country-
men. "This person," said he, after inspect-
ing the body, "was so ill that if he had not
been murdered, he would have died half an
hour before.”
	A BACHELOR friend is in trouble. He
has a pet calf that entered his room the
other day during his absence, ate a dozen
cakes of perfumed toilet soap, chewed into
tatters all his Sunday shirts, swallowed his
last pair of socks, knocked down all his
furniture in the room, and then quietly took
a nap on the floor until his return. He
thinks the cool impudence of that calf is
incomprehensible.
