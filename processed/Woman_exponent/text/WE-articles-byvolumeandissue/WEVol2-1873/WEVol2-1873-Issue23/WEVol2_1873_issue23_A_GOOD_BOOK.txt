A GOOD BOOK.

	"Hill's Manual" has been handed to us for
consideration, by Mr. James England of Plain
City. After a brief inspection, we can say that
we view it as one of the best books of its kind
that has ever been brought to our notice. Its
object is to enable the writer, sitting easily and
gracefully at the desk, to express thought
plainly, rapidly, elegantly and correctly; and
the great variety of subjects upon which it of-
fers information, is calculated to aid materially
in this very desirable accomplishment, as well
as in many other respects. The book may be
procured at reasonable rates from the above
named gentleman, who is agent for it; and
doubtless, all who subscribe for it, when they
come to test its real value, will consider the
price of obtaining it, so much money well
spent.
