OFFENCES VERSUS TRUTH.

	"Thy Word is Truth."—Jesus.
	"It must needs be that offenses come but wo, to him
by whom they come"

While facts are true, we needs must rue—
	That oft they are offensive;
But Truth—the Eternal word of God—
	Is pure, as comprehensive.
His Truth is pure, it must endure,
	For Right it sanctions ever:
His law we deem the Truth supreme—
	'Tis just, and wrongs us never.

Too much alas! for Truth doth pass,
	Man's soul it oft embitters;
The good inclined, are greived to find,
	"All is not gold that glitters."
Perchance we trust, to men unjust,
	And find them much too clever;
And so, forsooth, we blame the Truth,
	But Truth betrays us never.

And not a few, whose hearts are true—
	Whose simple word suffices,
By sharpers bold are caught and sold,
	Betrayed by mean devices.
Pretentions and pretenders too,
	Should be combated ever;
Fraud, should be sifted through and through,
	But Truth, defrauds us never.

'Tis well, 'tis wise, to scrutinize
	Their plans who feign to bless us;
'Tis braver far, with wrong to war
	Than to aid what would oppress us.
Truth! S[-]ck and hold as purest gold,
	That men from dross dissever,
Is aught amiss? be sure of this,
	By Truth, we're injured, never.

Why should we choose, to Truth abuse,
	To good by evil measure?
	If others lie, shall we deny
The Truth? In our displeasure?
	Truth makes us free, and yet we see—
	The wheat and tares together;
Alas! these tares, exist as snares—
	But Truth, ensnares us never.

These tares must with the wheat remain
	Until the harvest season;
Why 'tis ordained, Christ has explained,
	And potent is the reason.
When God doth reap, no tares He'll keep,
	They'll all be burnt together;
But the garner'd wheat, all clean and sweet
	Like Truth, will perish, Never!

Lo! God is pledged to wisdom give
	Most liberally and surely
To every one, upbraiding none,
	Who ask Him for it purely.
Armed with this key, both you and me
	Offences can out-weather;
'Tis wisdom's plea—the just will be
	By Truth offended, Never!

What-ever others say or do,
	Or friend, or foe, or brother,
We must be to our conscience true—
	We're judged not for another.
For strength each day, 'tis well to pray,
	To help our true endeavour
Offences lead the weak astray,
	But truth misleads us never.
