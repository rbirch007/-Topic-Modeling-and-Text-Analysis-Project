WOMENS CONFERENCE.

	The Ladies' Conference of the Salt Lake Stake
of Zion was held in the 14th Ward Assembly Hall,
Friday and Saturday, March 25th and 26th,
commencing Friday morning, 10 a. m., Mrs. M.
I. Horne presiding. Meeting opened by singing
"Now let us rejoice in the day of salvation."
Prayer by Mrs Diana Reid, sung "On the Moun-
tain Tops Appearing." Minutes of last Confer-
ence read and accepted. Reports from branch
Societies R. S. were read by Mrs. E. Howard.
	Mrs. Horne made some remarks to the Sisters
and expressed her readiness to comply with any
invitation which might be extended to her to
visit the branches of Relief Society in this Stake.
Mrs. E. S. Taylor and Mrs. S. M. Heywood
each made some encouraging remarks Choir
sang "Lord dismiss us with Thy blessing."
Benediction by Mrs. M. P. Young.
	Two p. m. met again and after usual exercises
of singing and prayer, Sisters M. I. Horne, P.
Woodruff, B. W. Smith, S. M. Kimball, M. A.
Wilcox, M. Carrington, S. Gibson, D. Reid, E.
Woodruff, M. P. Young, E. Howard and E. S.
Taylor each made some remarks touching upon
almost every subject, essential to the work in
progress. Elder W. Willis and Father Graves
occupied a short time in speaking and Prest.
Joseph E. Taylor delivered quite an interesting
address, touching upon the duties of parents in
training and guiding their children, the neces-
sity of attending meetings and keeping the
spirit of God wirth us and listening to the whis-
perings of our guardian angel,thought we should
put away all enmity and ill will towards each
other and prepare to dwell together in eternity.
	Mrs. Horne made some closing remarks and
the Conference was adjourned. Singing Doxol-
ogy. Benediction by Elder J. E. Taylor.
	Saturday 10 a. m. Primary Conference Mrs.
Ellen C. S. Clawson presiding. There were
crowds of children in attendance, minutes and
reports were read and an excellent time of re-
freshing was enjoyed by all. The singing was
very inspiring and it was a sight to gladden the
eyes, to see so many children assembled to hear
the good teachings of the Sisters. All Societies
reported were in a prosperous condition, and the
outlook for spiritual cultivation in the children
of Zion is much more auspicious than at any
previous time.
	Afternoon Session, Conference of Y. L M. I.
A. Mrs. M. A. Freeze presiding. Opened by
singing "O My Father, &c," Prayer by Mrs.
Hannah T. King. Singing "Arise, O, Glorious
Zion." Mrs. Crocheron read the minutes of the
last Conference and reports from 1st, 2d, 9th,
10th, 11th, 15th, 17th and 19th Wards, also from
Herriman and Draper. Mrs. Cooper gave a
verbal report of the 7th Wards, Miss Gardner,
West Jordan, Miss Carlisle, Mill Creek, Mrs.
Thompson, South Cottonwood. Mrs. Freeze,
Mrs. L. Felt, Mrs. C. Y. Conrad, Mrs. E. S.
Taylor, Dr. R. B. Pratt and Dr. E. B. Ferguson
addressed the meeting upon subjects calculated
to benefit and improve the young ladies warn-
ing them of the dangerous a[---]roments of the
world and exhorting them to live for a purpose.
Conference was adjourned until June 17 and 18.
Singing "O say what is truth," Benediction by
Judge Z. Snow. E. B. W.
