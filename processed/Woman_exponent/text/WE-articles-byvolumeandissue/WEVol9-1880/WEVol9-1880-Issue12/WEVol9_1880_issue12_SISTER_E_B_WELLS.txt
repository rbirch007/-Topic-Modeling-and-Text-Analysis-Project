SISTER E. B. WELLS:

	In passing the Twelfth Ward Assembly
Rooms yesterday, I was accosted by the Presi-
dent of the Ladies Relief Society and invited
in. I found quite a number of ladies present,
working on two quilts. Finding that they re-
quired a needle threader, they petitioned me to
become a candidate for the office. The quilts
I believe were for the Temple hands, or some
other such worthy object.
	I thought if the Relief Societies of each
Ward of this Stake would do the same for like
purposes, there would be seventy quilts for
seventy men now engaged on the Temple. If
every Stake in this Temple District would
follow suit could not the families of the two
hundred Temple hands be supplied,and assist in
making their families comfortable and happy.
How many Relief Societies will engage to do
this?								R. C
