IN MEMORY OF—

	George W.	Dunford, son of George and Eliza
Snow Dunford. died in Salt Lake City Dec. 13,
1880, at ten minutes to five o’clock a. m. Little
George was born Jan. 1, 1878, being nearly three
years old at the time he died. He was an ex-
ceedingly interesting child, was very large for
his age, and his physical as well as mental de-
velopment was quite remarkable. Sister Eliza
R. Snow, in a letter to the mother (her niece),
said: “It did not surprise me as much when I
was informed that the dear, precious jewel had
departed, as it would have done had I not real-
ized that the dear little George always seemed
too good for earth. That mighty, noble spirit
was too much to be long encased in a mortal
casket. It must be great comfort to you that
God has blest you with such a beautiful, noble
child, and that by faithfulness to the Gospel you
can secure it, and receive it again as you laid it
down, full of intelligence. That child is sure of


a celestial glory; had it lived it might have been
contaminated, now it has secured its eternal ex-
altation." Other comforting remarks followed,
and the poem which we publish below, from the
gifted pen of Zion's Poetess. We also extend
our sympathies to the bereaved parents, for we
too have sorrowed, and know how to appreciate
the tears which flow when we are called to part
with those whose affections are entwined around
our hearts, but remember this, "God knoweth
best."
