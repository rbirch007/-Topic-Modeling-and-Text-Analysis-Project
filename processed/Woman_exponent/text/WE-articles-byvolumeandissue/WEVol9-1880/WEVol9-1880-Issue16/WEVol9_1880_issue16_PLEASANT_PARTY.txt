PLEASANT PARTY.

	A pleasant  surprise  party was gotten up on
Thursday, Dec. 16, by some of the sisters of the
Thirteenth Ward Relief Society, in honor of the
70th anniversary of our  much  esteemed Sister
Melvina  Snow's birthday.  The  party was held
at the residence of her  daughter, Mrs. De Long,
with whom she was invited to dine, in order to
make the surprise complete.  On arriving  there
she was  greatly suprised to find a room  full  of
warm friends to greet her, numbering more than
thirty  persons, who  sat  down  to a table  spread
with  a  variety  of  good  things.  Among  the
guests  were  Bishop  E.  D.  Woolley,  Judge  Z.
Snow,  Brother  Millen  Atwood,  and  Willard
Snow, son of Sister M. Snow, with their  wives,
and quite a number  of  the  sisters who had been
acquainted for many years  with  our worthy sis-
ter, most  of  whom  made  a  few  remarks  after
dinner.  Mrs.  Rachel  Grant,  President  of the
Relief Society, spoke of the faithfulness of Sister
Snow  as  a  Teacher  in  the  Society.  All  ex-
pressed  their  appreciation  of her  good  qualities,
tendering  heartily  their  congratulations,  with
many  good  wishes  of happy  returns  of  these
celebrations, her  son expressing his  great  plea-
sure  at  the  kindly feeling  manifested  towards
his  aged  mother.  Judge  Snow also referred to
Sister Melvina, as  being  acquainted  with  her
from  childhood, and  as  a  neighbor  and school-
mate, speaking  in  the  highest terms of her in-
tegrity  and  devotion  to  her  religion.  Brother
Atwood also spoke of the worthiness and exem-
plary life  of Sister  Snow, having  lived  a  near
neighbor  to her  for  many years.  The time was
spent  most  agreeably,  the  Bishop  being  well
pleased with the gathering, and left his blessing.
The company  retired  at  an  early  hour,  feeling
well  paid  that  they had  made her heart happy
in her old age.
