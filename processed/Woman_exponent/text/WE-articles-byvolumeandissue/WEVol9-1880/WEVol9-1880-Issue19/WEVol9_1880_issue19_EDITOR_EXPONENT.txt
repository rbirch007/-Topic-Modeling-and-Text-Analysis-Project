EDITOR EXPONENT:

	We have a Relief Society and Y. L. M. I.
Association in Pine Valley, and we are all
striving to do what we can for the upbuilding
of the Kingdom of God. We have lately had
a visit from our worthy sisters, E. R. Snow
Smith, Zina D. Young and Minerva W. Snow.
They held three meetings in this place, giving
the sisters some very good instruction, which
will long be remembered by them, and was
highly appreciated. We do not enjoy the so-
ciety of such talented women often. While
here they organized a Primary Association,
which I think will be the means of doing much
good in implanting the principles of the Gos-
pel in the young and tender minds of the chil-
dren, that they may grow in the love and ad-
monition of the Lord; for "as the twig is bent
so does the tree incline."
	Ever wishing for the success of the EXPO-
NENT, I remain your sister in the Gospel,
	C. S. GARDNER.
	Pine Valley, Feb. 6, 1881.
