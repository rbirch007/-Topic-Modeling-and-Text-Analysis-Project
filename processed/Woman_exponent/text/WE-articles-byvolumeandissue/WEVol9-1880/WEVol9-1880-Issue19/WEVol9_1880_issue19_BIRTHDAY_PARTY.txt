BIRTHDAY PARTY.

	Feb. 9, the sisters of the Relief Society in
Payson, held a party in commemoration of Pa-
triarch Hyrum Smith, under the able manage-
ment of Pres. Jane B. Simons and Counselors
Agnes Douglas and Mary Morse. At half past
six p. m., the sisters, with their husbands, as-
sembled in the City Hall. After singing, prayer
was offered up by Counselor David Sant. Sing-
ing, "We thank Thee, O God, for a Prophet."
	An interesting speech was then delivered by
Elder I. M. Coombs, referring to the many inci-
dents in the life of Hyrum Smith. Dancing by
the company. Then followed a recitation by H.
Hamill (son of Prof. Hamill), reading, Prof. J.
L. Townsend, and a song by James Reece, when
intermission for a short time was taken. Justice
was done to the viands prepared by way of a
picnic, after which a song by John Quigly.
Dancing was then resumed. Speeches by
Bishop J. S. Tanner and Counselor D. Sant, who
read the names of the eight witnesses to the au-
thenticity of the Book of Mormon, and Henry
Jacobs, who was personally acquainted with the
Prophet and Patriarch. Songs then followed by
James Reece, George Hancock and S. E. Daniels.
Sen. Pres. Jane Simons briefly tendered thanks
to the company for their presence, and to all
who had contributed to the evening's entertain-
ment; also said that she was personally ac-
quainted with Joseph and Hyrum Smith. "The
Resurrection Day," composed by Wm. Clayton,
was then sung by the assembly.
						M. A. HARDY, Sec'y.
