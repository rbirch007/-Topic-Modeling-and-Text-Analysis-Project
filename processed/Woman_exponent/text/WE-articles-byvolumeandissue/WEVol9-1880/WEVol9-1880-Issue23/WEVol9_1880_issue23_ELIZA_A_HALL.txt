						ELIZA A. HALL.
	West Portage, Box Elder County.

	Died, at Alpine City, March 23, 1881, Cather-
ine Nash, in the sixty-eighth year of her age.
Deceased was born July 23, 1813, at Ashton
Common, Wiltshire, England; emigrated to
Utah in 1854. She had suffered for several
weeks with rheumatism, and upon the night of
the 21st was taken with a paralytic stroke, which
caused much pain and deprived her of the use
of her left side, and affected her speech.
	Sister Nash has for many years held the office
of teacher in the Relief Society, which office
she well maintained up to the time of her death,
and died in full faith of the Gospel, beloved by
all who knew her. Her remains were followed
to their last resting place by a long procession of
her friends from this and surrounding settle-
ments in carriages and wagons. The sisters of
the Relief Society walked in procession, followed
by the Young Ladies' and Primary Associations.
She was loved by all, both old and young. She
leaves two sons and three grandchildren, who
deeply mourn her loss; also a numerous circle of
friends.
	We miss her, but feel assured that our loss is
her gain.					MARIA ADAMS.

	Died, at Taylorsville, S. L. Co., Utah, January,
1881, Jane Knopp. Deceased was born at Rich-
bury, Essex, Eng., May, 1798; emigrated from
Sockett Heath, Little Thurrock, Essex, in 1855;
stayed in Philadelphia, and emigrated to Utah
in 1860. Sister Knopp was an exemplary wo-
man, a true Latter-day Saint, and an active
member in the Relief Society. She resided for
some time in Wasatch County, and wherever
she was on all occasions delighted in her religion,
as a humble follower of Jesus. She died in full
faith of the Gospel.
