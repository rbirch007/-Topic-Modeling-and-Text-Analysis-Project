A SURPRISE PARTY.

	On Wednesday, Nov. 8, a number of ladies,
friends of Mrs. Augusta Joyce Crocheron, met
at her house in the 18th Ward, for the purpose
of expressing their respect and regard for her,
and their appreciation of her labors as Secretary
of the Y. L. M. I. A. of Salt Lake Stake of Zion.
After an interval of pleasant conversation, mu-
sic, &c., the ladies sat down with Sister Croche-
ron’s family to a bountiful repast; after which
Sister Mary A. Freeze, President of the Y.L.
M.I.A. of Salt Lake Stake, presented to Sister
Crocheron, in the name of the sisters present
and others who were unable to attend, a hand-
some winter shawl, accompanying the gift with
a few well chosen and happy remarks, bearing
testimony to Sister Crocheron’s faithfulness in
her official capacity, and her usefulness in the
Church and in society.
	Sister Crocheron, who was still more surprised
than she had been at the invasion of her home,
replied with much feeling and after a mutual


interchange of good wishes the meeting ad-
journed, all declaring that they had spent a few
hours pleasantly and profitably.
