TWILIGHT MUSINGS.

All the high-way grows dim in the twilight,
	And people pass on to and fro,
Little ones soothed into quiet,
	Have started for dream-land I know.
I would follow and ramble there with them
	Had I but the power to go.

'Tis an hour since sunset has faded,
	I gaze through the tremulous air,
Far away to the beautiful temple,
	And long, Oh, I long to be there!
With glad glorified spirits to worship,
	Ever free from sorrow and care.

O, there I shall throw off earth garments,
	Soiled, travel-worn wrinkled and old,
To be clothed in white purified raiment,
	Exceeding in lustre fine gold.
And my soul shall rejoice wflh the angels
	The King in His beauty behold.

Here I stretch out my hands through the darknes
	Far over the silence of night,
And my heart cries throughout the dim twilight
	For forms that have vanished from sight.
And I read precious names on the marble,
	Through tears that are blinding the light.

Between me and that glorious City,
	Rise darkly the graves of my dead;
Veiling over the bright gate of glory,
	Through which the storm-driven have fled.
And in weakness I mourn for ties riven,
	Though sad tears, THEY never more shed.

Yet I watch through the dim evening's twilight,
	And list to the wild breakers roar,
Mark the swift flowing tide of the river,
	That sends back the weird, "nevermore,"
And I sigh for a glimpse of that Country,
	Away on the "Evergreen Shore."
