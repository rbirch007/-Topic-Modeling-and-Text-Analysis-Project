SURPRISE PARTY.

  The officers and some of the members of the
Relief Society  of  South  Cottonwood,  with
many members of the family, met  at the resi-
dence of our aged and beloved Sister (Mother)
Maxfield, in a surprise party, spending a happy
and agreeable time conversing on the things of 
God and the early rise of this Church and peo-
ple, their  mobbings  and  persecutions, bringing
tears  to  the  eyes  of  many,  filling  each  heart
with a stronger determination  to  live  humbly
before the Lord.  Truly was  it  a  feast  to me
to meet  and  converse  with  some  of the aged
veterans who were personally  acquainted with
the  Prophet  Joseph.  The  Lord chasteneth
those whom he loveth.  Mother Maxfield has


been numbered with this people from the early
rise of this Church, having  raised  a  family of
nine boys and one girl.  A poem  in  honor of
the lady, composed by Mrs. Martha G. Young,
was read  at  the  table.  May her  days  end in
peace.             M. G. Y.
