BEAVER STAKE.

	The Ladies Conference of Beaver Stake met
at Minersville, October 15th, 1880. President
Lucinda Howd, Sister Zabriskie, President of
the Relief Society of Minersville, with Counselors,
Coun. McDonough, Sister Sarah M. Dell, Sister
Lydia Blackburn and many active and useful
members of the Relief Societies were present.
A number of brethren met with the sisters,
among whom were Bishop McKnight, of Min-
ersville, and Brother William Robinson, of Bea-
ver. Meeting was called to order by President
Lucinda Howd. Sung, "O God our help in ages
past." Prayer by Brother John Ayers. Presi-
dent Howd spoke of the various and important

[Column 3]
duties which rested upon the daughters of Zion,
and made many other interesting remarks.
	Reports from the Relief Societies, Y. L. M. I,
A. and "Primaries," were read, which bore un-
mistakable evidence of the lively zeal realized
by the Saints of this section. Greenville and
Adamsville were not represented.
	Much good instruction was given during Con-
ference by Brothers Robinson, Ayers, Wood and
Bishop McKnight, and by Sisters Howd, Mac-
Donough, L. Skiner, Zabriskie, Dell, Blackburn
and others.
	Sister Gillis, President of the Primary Associ-
ation of Beaver, spoke very encouragingly of the
progress the children were making. She felt a
deep interest in their improvement. Sister
Goodwin, President of the Y. L. M. I. A. of
Minersville was released from that position on
account of poor health. Toward the close of
Conference a few recitations and songs were
rendered by members of the Primary Associa-
tion.
	Adjourned till January 15, 1881.
