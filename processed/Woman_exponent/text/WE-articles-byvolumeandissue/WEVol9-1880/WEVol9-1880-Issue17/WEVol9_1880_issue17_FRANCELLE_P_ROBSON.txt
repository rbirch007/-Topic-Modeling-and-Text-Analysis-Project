				FRANCELLE P. ROBSON.

	We have about twenty-five families here in
our little settlement of Pleasant Green, Brighton
Ward, and we are trying to live up to our duties,
to the best of our knowledge. Our Relief So-
ciety was organized March 9, 1879, with Sister
Charlotte Hirst, President; Ann Bertoch and
Francis Hardman, Counselors. The death of
our dear president left our society disorganized.
It was re-organized August, 1880, with Sister
Alvira S. Hirst, President, Ann Bertoch and
Nancy Dearden, Counselors. From our first or-
ganization up to the present time we have do-
nated to the temple $10; emigration, $9.50; to
the poor, $3.75; for books $1.90. We have on
hand at present, cash, $7; property, $20. Our
society does not own any grain, but we expect
to in the future. We celebrated Joseph Smith's
birthday by getting up a party for the benefit of
the schoolhouse, to get some new benches.
			ALVIRA S. HIRST, President.
			FANNY JENKINS, Secretary.
			ALICE COCKRILL, Ass't Secretary.

	EDITOR EXPONENT:—The sisters of Rich-
mond are striving to do their part in the great
work that is laid out for them as handmaidens
of the Most High, ever willing and ready to im-
part to the needy, comfort the distressed. At
the present time there are but few who are not
able to help themselves. The Lord has blessed
us with a good harvest, there is no sickness of
any moment, for which we strive to be thank-
ful. Your paper is ever a welcome visitor among
us. Please accept our kind regards, in which
our president truly joins.
	Your sisters in the Gospel.
				A. M. MERRILL, Pres't.
