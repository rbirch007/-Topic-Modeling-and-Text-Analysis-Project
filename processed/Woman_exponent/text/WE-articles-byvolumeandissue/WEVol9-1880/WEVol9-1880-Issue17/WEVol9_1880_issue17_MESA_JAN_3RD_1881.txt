					MESA, JAN. 3rd, 1881.
DEAR AUNT EM.—
	My health is not very good but I enjoy my
Arizona home very much, I would not exchange
it for one in Salt Lake if I could, although I
would like to see you all. We have the most
beautiful climate the heat is at first an objection
but one soon gets accustomed to it, and it is
rather more agreeable than otherwise. We are
having reinforcements from the North almost
daily now; this is the emigration season for this
country on account of having Deserts to cross.
Bro. David Kimball says Mesa is destined to be
the second city of Arizona,it certainly is growing
rapidly. We are thirty miles from the Southern
Pacific Rail Road, five days and a half from Salt
Lake. I live in hopes of seeing my Salt Lake
friends here to spend our delightful winters.
	Zulie has just returned from Phoenix our
county seat where she went to pass her ex-
amination as a teacher, she got a first class
diploma and will soon open school at Jonesville.
We have excellent laws regarding schools in
this territory.
	We have enrolled 64 children on our school
record. It has been estimated that we of Mesa
receive almost as much school money from the
county as the tax on our property. We have a
good school-house just finished and another is
already being talked of.
	Everybody that has not got their small grain
sown is hurrying to get it in, there is already be-
tween five and six hundred acres in, and grow-
ing nicely. If planted now it has the advantage
of ripening before the weather gets too warm
for harvesters, and besides it does not need so
much irrigation; then also there will be time
for other crops when the wheat is harvested.
There are quite a number of orchards and vine-
yards started which are doing well; this place
seems specially adapted to vineculture. We had
several hundred pounds of grapes last year as
did others.
	Kind regards to all enquiring friends.
