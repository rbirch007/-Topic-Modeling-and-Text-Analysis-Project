FRIENDSHIP.

	I have just been reading some of our dear
sisters' thoughts in our excellent paper. I
would like to say a few words on friendship,
that, to me, is the greatest blessing of life;
apart from our own hearthstone. A true friend
in all life's phases is truly a blessing; one who
can see your imperfections (as none are exempt)
sometimes feel them, but true as steel a friend
in childhood, a friend in old age, a friend in
all life's vicissitudes one to whom you can lay
open your heart's secrets good or ill, still your
friend to do you good; reproof from such a
one is sweet to the ear. Such a friend is price-
less. Oh! my sister let us cultivate true friend-
ship like Damon and Pythias, be true to the
death, with no thought of reward, no guile.
My old friends are dear indeed, tried and true.
God bless all those who seek to live upright
before him who dwelleth in the heavens. Ever
be faithful and true.
								B. M. J. B
