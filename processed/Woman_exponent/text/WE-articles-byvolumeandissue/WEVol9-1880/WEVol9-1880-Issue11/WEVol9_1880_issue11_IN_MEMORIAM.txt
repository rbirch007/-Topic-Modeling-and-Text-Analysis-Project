IN MEMORIAM.

	Subscribed by the Relief Society of Beaver
City, to the memory of Mrs. Louisa B. Pratt.
	Our departed sister and fellow-member, who
died at Beaver Sept. 8th, 1880. aged 77 years, 9
months and 29 days; she was respected by all
who knew her, was devoted to her religion and
her God, and ever manifested a willingness to
aid the progress of His work.
	Resolved: That we extend to the bereaved
family our heartfelt sympathy, hoping they will
follow an example so worthy, and that while
mourning her loss, they may realize that,th[-]ugh
suddenly called from this earth, she has gone to
continue the labors that old age partially pre-
vented while here.
	Resolved, that while her departure saddens
our hearts it arouses our desires to continue more
faithful, that our last days, like hers. may be
blessed by the hope of a glorious resurrection.
	Resolved, that a copy of these resolutions be
tendered to her children, also sent to the WOM-
AN'S EXPONENT, for publication, and placed on
the record book of the Secratary.
		MARY E. ASHWORTH, Sec'y. R. S.
	Beaver.

	Damsel Lee, daughter of Wm. ,Lee and Jane
Lyon, born at Grantsville, April 28th 1875, died
September 29, 1880.
	It has pleased our Heavenly Father, in his in-
scrutable purposes to call from our midst, our
respected and beloved sister. We the members


of the Grantsville Mutual Improvement Associa-
tion desire to express our love and esteem to her
memory; therefore:
	Be it Resolved: That we mourn her loss but
not as those without hope, that we deeply sym-
pathize with her parents and relations. She was
an active member of our Association and in every
way gave promise of a useful life.
	That the above be placed upon record a copy
of the same be given to her parents, and pub-
lished in the WOMAN'S EXPONENT.
		MARENTHA A. STODDARD, President.
		LUCY A. CLARK,
		MAY L. RICH,		Counselors.
