	H. M. W. POULTER, Sec'y.

	Y. L. M. I. A. Quarterly Conference of We-
ber Stake of Zion was held in the Ogden Tab-
ernacle, Sept. 17, 1880, commencing at ten A.
M.; Mrs. Sarah Herrick presiding. Opened
with singing and prayer. The reports were
read, with good showing from the several asso-
ciations. Bishop McQuarrie then addressed
the young ladies, exhorting them to lay aside
the follies of the world and cling closer to their 
religion and their God; to improve in all the
principles pertaining to the Gospel, not only
spiritually, but temporally. He spoke encour-
agingly of many things for the good of the
young, and was listened to with great interest
by the congregation. He was followed by
Mrs. M. J, Tanner, of Provo, who bore a faith-
ful testimony to the work of God; she was
pleased to see so many young ladies engaged in
the work of mutual improvement; knew that
they would one and all be blest in striving to
keep the laws of our heavenly Father, who
witholds no good gifts from his children if they
will earnestly seek to do his will and keep his
commandments. Bishop Hammond of Hunts-
ville spoke in a very kindly and encouraging
manner to the young sisters, he was followed
by Brother Wm. Wright. The afternoon meet
