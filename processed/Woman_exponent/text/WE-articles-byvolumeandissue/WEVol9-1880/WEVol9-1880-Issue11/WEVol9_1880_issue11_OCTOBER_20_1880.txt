							OCTOBER 20, 1880.
	DEAR SISTER WELLS: The sisters here are all
alive to their duties as a Relief Society and de-
sire to do their part in rolling on this mighty
work in building temples, clothing the needy, or
anything required of them. According to coun-
sel from our beloved Sister E. R. S. Smith, we
have made a small beginning in silk culture.
One of our faithful sisters J. Nielson has raised
this season about 10 lbs. of beautiful cocoons for
which she received a Diploma at our fair,
which encouraged her to "try again." We feel sure
that the climate is favorable for raising silk.
We look anxiously forward to the time when we
shall be blest with a visit from some of our be-
loved sisters from Salt Lake City, and receive
some instructions and encouragement from
them. We are pleased to read in Our Paper the
different ideas of our sisters with regard to polit-
ical rights and say God bless them for so nobly
defending the franchise. We feel to coincide
with them. Why are we not organized in a
Stake? What have we done that we should be
left out in the cold?
	Very Respectfully Your Sisters in the Gospel.
					C. LILJENQUIST, Pres.
