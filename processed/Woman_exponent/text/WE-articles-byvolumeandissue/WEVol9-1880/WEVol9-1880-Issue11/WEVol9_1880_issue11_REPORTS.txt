REPORTS.

Fountain Green.
	The Conference of the Primary Association
of Fountain Green was held in the schoolhouse,
October 4, 1880. There was a good attendance
of the Association, and of the parents. Bish-
op R. L. Johnson and his Counselors, Martin
Lund and Reuben Carter, were also present.
	Meeting was called to order by Sister Har-
riet Huggins, President of the Association.
Sang, "We thank Thee, O God, for a prophet."
Prayer by William Adams, Jun., aged six
years. Children sang. Minutes of the first
and last meeting were then read and accepted.
The programme was then presented to the au-
dience, which consisted of select readings, reci-
tations, essays, repeating the articles of faith
in concert, and other exercises; after which
Bishop R. L. Johnson spoke to the children
and sisters most encouragingly. Bro. Martin
Lund also addressed the audience, expressing
great pleasure. Bro. Reuben Carter followed
in a similar strain.
	The financial report for the year was then
read: Received from the members, $11,28.
Disbursements for the year: two quilts pieced
by the children and donated to the Manti Tem-
ple, valued at $14; also $4 in cash to the Manti
Temple; for cards and stationery, $1,60. Prof-
its on home industries, $8,32.
	Candy and other presents were then distrib-
uted among the children, which pleased them
exceedingly.
	HARRIET HUGGINS, Pres't,
	ANNIE ANDERSON, Sec'y.
	ELIZA CALLARD, Ass't  Sec'y.
