ENFRANCHISEMENT.

	For us as women of America, and Mothers in
Israel, the glorious right of the ballot has meant
strength to those who have our confidence, and
a bulwark of defense in the cause of law and
equity.
	In the statues of our now famous Territory,
and the framing of such laws as our noble Legis-
lators have made for the governing of the same,
can plainly be seen the power of an Allwise and
just God, who never deserts his own. 
	Now let those who believe we have joyfully
exercised and fully appreciated this right, come
to the front and do what they can for the cause
of woman, for not only do we suffer but all
women who have raised the standard for their
own emancipation, have a firm plank in the plat-
form which is being constructed by them for
their greater freedom. 
	How palpable is the plan of our opposers, "dog
in the manger" style, they raise their manly (?)
voices against "weak women" as they please to
term us, to carry out their own mean design to
disable our husbands and fathers from having a
voice in the ruling of the country which their
own industry has made. Don't you know, you
one horse politicians, that the dishonorable sub-
terfuge to which you are resorting, is palpable to
every honorable man and woman who is not
eaten up by spite and avarice? Bah! the very
name of the one chosen for this great and mighty
conspiracy means failure. He has told the story
of "This little pig went to market, and this little
pig stayed at home" to the nation ever so many
times, and everybody knows his sanctity means
"loaves and fishes."
	We may my sisters, lose the right of suffrage,
but let us meet this infringement upon our rights
in solid phalanx, and show that we have the
power and the will to wave our banner of "Trust
in Israel's God" in the face of all the world.
