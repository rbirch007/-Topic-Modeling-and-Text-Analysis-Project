TO SISTER H. T. KING.

	Thanks. The article on "Babyhood," which
appeared in the last EXPONENT, seems to me
one of the sweetest and best your ready pen
has ever produced. I can believe that in
thanking you for it, I express the senti-
ments of thousands of young mothers. How
good it is to have some one who can help us to
think and plan for our priceless darlings, our
babies, while they are still unable to think for
themselves, and know no more and do no bet-
ter, often, with all their heavenly attributes,
than merely to confuse and perplex us. How
thankful we should be for our "Mothers in Is-
rael;" and—I speak now from my own experi-
ence—our wise-thinking "Mothers-in-law" also.
									LULA.


It is  a  remarkable  fact  that  two-thirds  of 
the church members in this country are women,
to  one-third  men;  while  on  the  other  hand,
nineteen-twentieths  of the  criminals  are men
and only one-twentieth women.
