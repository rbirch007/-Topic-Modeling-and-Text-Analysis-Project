		ST. GEORGE NOV. 10, 1880.
MRS. E. B. WELLS:
	Dear Sister: Before leaving St. George I
thought I would drop a line to inform you of
my probable return home after an absence of
two months. I expect to start for Milford to-
day I have had the satisfaction of working in
the Temple for several days in behalf of our
dead, and have had a delightful visit and time
of refreshing with my dear sister, relatives and
friends of this much talked of City.
	Yesterday was the anniversary of the birth-
day of Pres't. Erastus Snow and we were the
participants of a most agreeable and interest-
ing party on the occasion, at his residence,
where his wives and sons and daughters had
prepared a sumptuous entertainment, inviting
a number of friends including Pres't. Angus
M. Cannon and company, who are on a visit to
this place, also Mrs. Tate from Toole, probably
in all, family and guests, numbering eighty
persons.
	The evening was spent in pleasing converse
and reunion of old associations, together with
vocal and instrumental music rendered by
members of his interesting family.
	I should not omit to mention the perform-
ance of a Juvenile brass band outside which,
considering their extreme youth, was very
good and promises, by perseverance to become
proficient.
	When the guests were about to take their de-
parture Pres't. Snow closed with a heart-felt
prayer. With best wishes for your continued
prosperity. As ever,
				Yours truly,
