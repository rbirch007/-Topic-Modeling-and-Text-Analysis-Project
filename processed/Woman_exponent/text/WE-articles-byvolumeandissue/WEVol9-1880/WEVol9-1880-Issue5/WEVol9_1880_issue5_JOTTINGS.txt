JOTTINGS.

	The EXPONENT continues to come to us here
in Upper Kanab, freighted with its usual and
ever-welcome store of interesting news. Hav-
ing read the article entitled "Wildwood Leaves
and flowers," I feel as though I had had a visit
with the dear author—a friend of bygone
years. And "Seek a Reward," enables one to
take up her burthen and trudge along with
renewed diligence.					E. B. C.

	The efforts of the EXPONENT are to lead the
minds of the daughters of Zion in a channel
that will prepare them for positions they are
qualifled to fill, we know by its influence. Our
desires are to live purer and better lives. Our
hearts beat with true pride that we are num-
bered with noble women; yes, handmaids of
the Lord, who are willing to endure all things
for the truth's sake. And who, knowing their
rights and privileges, dare maintain them. We
feel to ask the blessings of the Lord upon all
faithful Saints, and especially the sisters who
are striving to do public and universal good.
We remain your sisters in the Gospel of Peace,
