CORRECTIONS.

	In the minutes of the Provo Relief Society
Conference, Elder Woodruff is made to say that
he was never at home with his family but one
summer in his life, and one of his children was a
year old before he saw him. Instead of this, he
said that he had not been at home with his fam-
ily but one summer since he was married, until
he came into these valleys of the mountains and
one child, born after he left home, was nearly a
year old before he saw him. These glaring mis-
takes show the importance of persons having the
privilege of correcting what is reported for them
before it is put in print.
