					MARY J. JOHNSON,

	EDITOR EXPONENT:—Your valuable little
paper comes to hand, and is a very welcome
visitor to us here away out in Colorado. I
think as soon as the sisters here become ac-
quainted with it, you will have many subscrib-
ers from this place. I will show my paper to
as many as I have an opportunity, for I think
no woman should be without it. I think it
will not be long until we will have as good Re-
lief Society meetings here as you have in Utah.
Our town is growing fast; everything bids fair
for a beautiful city in the future. I like the
country here quite as well as Utah, and believe
if we do right the Lord will bless it for His
Saints. We are living close neighbors to the
Mexicans here, who are very kind to us. They
have many customs which are very strange for
a while, but we soon get acquainted with them.
I suppose our ways of living are quite as
strange to them.
	Ever wishing success to your valuable paper,
I remain your sister in the Gospel,
								S. T. B.
