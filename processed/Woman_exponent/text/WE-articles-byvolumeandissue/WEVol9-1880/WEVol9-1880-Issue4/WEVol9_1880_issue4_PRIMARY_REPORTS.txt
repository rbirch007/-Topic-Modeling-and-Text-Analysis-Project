			PRIMARY REPORTS.
				
	Minutes of the Seventh Quarterly Conference
of the  Relief Societies of Utah Stake, held in the 
Provo  Meeting-house,  May  28th,  1880.  Meet-
ing  opened  by singing and  prayer. Minutes of 
preceding meeting read  and  approved.  The re-
ports  for the quarter  just ended  were read, also 
Primary reports. 
	President Mrs. M. T. Smoot said:  Although it
might seem  to some tedious to listen to so many 
reports, still they were of great importance. That
was what had called  us  together,  to  hear and 
learn of the progress  of  all.  The  Primary was 
a part of ourselves.  Time was far spent and pro-
fitably spent.  She  was  thankful that  she was 
able to meet with the societies again.  Although
her body was feeble, yet her spirit was vigorous. 
Realized that  God  was  very  good  to us.  Her
happiness was  increased  in  seeing so many  to-
gether,  and  as  we  were  favored  with having 
Brother Woodruff,  Sister  Phebe  Woodruff  and
Brother Teasdale with us, would hear the various
verbal reports  from the presidents of the differ-
ent wards and  hasten  our  business, in order to 
hear from  our  visitors.  The  presidents  gave
very interesting  accounts  of  the  doings and la-
bors of their wards,  showing zeal in the cause 
they have engaged in.
	Counselor John  was  very  much  gratified in 
listening to  the  progress  made  by her sisters.
Thought  the reports spoke  well  for  the  settle-
ments.  The speaker said we should be thankful
that we could by our feeble efforts do something to 
promote the welfare of Zion.  Prayed God to bless
all who took  an active part in Zion's cause.
