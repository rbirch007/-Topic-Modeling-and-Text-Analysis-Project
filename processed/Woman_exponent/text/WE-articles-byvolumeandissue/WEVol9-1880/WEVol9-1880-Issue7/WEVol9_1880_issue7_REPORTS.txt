REPORTS.

	The Primary Association of Woodruff, held
their first anniversary June 19, 1880. Present
were several of the brethren, President of the
Relief Society and her Counselors, and many of
the parents of the children. After the opening
exercises Brothers Putman and Cornia, ad-
dressed the children encouraging them to be
punctual in attending meeting after which
there were songs and recitations by the children.
Dancing was then engaged in for a short time.
Picnic was passed around when they were dis-
missed, all feeling they had enjoyed themselves
very much.
	We have a small settlement and very much
scattered, that very many of the children are
not able to attend, but all are trying to do the
best they can.
	During the year cards of merit have been
distributed, and the children take great intrest
in the meetings. They have pieced a very nice
quilt for the temple, and have nearly pieces
enough for another.
	The following are the officers. Ruth Cornia,

[Column 2]
President, Clarissa Putman and Adeline Harri-
son, Counselors; Elizabeth Lee, Treasurer.
