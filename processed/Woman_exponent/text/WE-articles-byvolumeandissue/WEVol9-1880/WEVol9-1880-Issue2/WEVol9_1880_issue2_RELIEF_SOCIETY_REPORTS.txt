RELIEF SOCIETY REPORTS.

	A brief report of Portage Relief Society, Box
Elder County. We held our annual meeting on
the 15th of May. The following officers were
elected and unanimously sustained: Mrs. Lu-
cinda Hoskin, President; Mrs. Julia A. Gibbs
and Mrs. Margaret John, Counselors; Jane H.
John, Secretary; Eliza A. Hall, Asst. Sec'y;
Jane Hall, Treasurer. There are about forty
members enrolled, we meet every two weeks
and enjoy a goodly portion of the Spirit of the
Lord. Our society is in a flourishing condition,
our meetings are well attended and we are often
blest with the encouraging remarks of our worthy
Bishop, O. C. Hoskin and his assistants, and we
feel like doing all in our power to held build up
the Kingdom of God. The sisters of our Society
are trying to carry out the counsel of the autho-
rities of the Church in storing grain, for we
know it will be our salvation in days to come.
Our crops for the last two or three years have
been cut off by frost, grasshoppers and crickets,
and it is rather a hard matter to lay up grain
where there is none, but we intend to try, try
again; and hope in time to succeed. The breth-
ren are now at work on a water ditch, which,
when completed, will be a great benefit to this
settlement, as water is very scarce in this part of
the valley. We have a week day and Sunday
School, a Young Men's and Young Ladies' Mu-
tual Improvement Association. We are also
called upon to donate means to assist in build-
ing the Temple in Logan, and it is our desire to
re[-]pond to every call made by the servants of the
Lord. In conclusion, I will say that the Expo-
nent is an ever welcome visitor and is highly ap-
preciated; we read it in our meetings and are
greatly benefitted by its valuable instructions.
May God bless the noble women that spend their
time and talents in helping to roll forth this
mighty work, is the prayer of
			Your sister in the Gospel,
