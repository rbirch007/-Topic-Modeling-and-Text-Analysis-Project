PRIMARY REPORTS.

	The first annual meeting of the Primary As-
sociation of Samaria was held Thursday evening,
May 6th, 1880. A large audience convened—
several of the Priesthood of the ward were
among the number. The evening entertain-
ment consisted of songs, recitations and dia-
logues. Six of the little ones, under ten years
of age, gave a very nice dialogue on Spring, four
others gave a dialogue entitled, "The Bound
Girl," and one little girl, eight years old read
the chapter from the New Testament, which
speaks of the widow's mite, and, although the
house was crowded every word was distinctly
heard to the other end of the room. We have
also made a commencement in the straw busi-
ness and several of the little girls have learned
to braid straw and they wear hats braided by
their own hands, and I am happy to say that
several of the children have taken Sister Eliza
Snow's counsel—when they had five cents
given them they have put it into the Association
and by that means have raised enough to buy
blocks for the straw they braid. they are
anxious to attend their meetings, and when I
am unable to attend through sickness, the old-
est of the little girls will carry on the meeting,
and feels glad to do so, and when the minutes of
that meeting have been read I have been truly
gratified to hear them. I often look back to the
time they were organized and feel grateful to

[Column 2]
my Heavenly Father for the progress they have
made. It is true it seems slow work with the
little ones while laboring with them, but I feel
thankful to the Lord for the assistance of his
Holy Spirit enabling us to persevere in this
great work, realizing the time will come when
these little ones will stand forth battling for
truth against error; then will their petitions as-
cend to God our Heavenly Father for blessings
upon those that organized them and those who
have labored with them, teaching them the
laws of God, preserving them from wolves in
sheeps' clothing who seek to draw the young
from the fold, and who try to bring destruction
upon the people of God. To mothers I would
say, encourage your little ones to attend their
meetings, attending them also yourselves; do
not think it too much trouble, they will repay
you in the future, becoming bright and shining
lights, crowned with immortality and eternal
lives: may our Heavenly Father bless all that
labor with the young and rising generation is
the prayer of your co-laborer,
