LITTLETON.

	Our Third semi-annual meeting convened on
the 22nd Jan. 1881. The presiding authorities
of the Stake several bishops, and others of the
local priesthood were present. Also Pres. Ly-
dia Rich and Counselors, and a very good at-
tendance of the presiding officers of branch
societies and members. The Relief Societies
were well represented statistical, financial
and verbally. And all seemed to be doing well
and feeling well in the Gospel. The only com-
plaint seemed to be a slackness in general at-
tendance at meeting. We have built, this last
season, completed and had dedicated a very
nice substantial, concrete granary for the

[Column 2]
Stake R. S. And the most of our grain is in;
its dimensions are 18 by 24 feet.
	The Y. L. M. I. Association and Primary
held their Conference the 23rd Jan. were well
represented and seemed to be in a thriving con-
dition. Our Home Industry Association is in
good runing order and turns out very nice hats,
bonnets &c. &.
	Our Statistical and Financial reports are as
follows, for the half year ending Jan. 10, 1881.
	STATISTICAL: Teachers, 31; members 156;
officers and members, 235: meetings held, 43;
average attendance, 93; WOMAN'S EXPON-
ENT. 16.
	FINANCIAL: On hand July 10, 1880, receipts,
to Jan. 10 1881, cash, $140,50; property,
$1,099, 64; Wheat lent out, in cash $35, 97;
property $106,74; wheat 533, bush. 9 lbs.
