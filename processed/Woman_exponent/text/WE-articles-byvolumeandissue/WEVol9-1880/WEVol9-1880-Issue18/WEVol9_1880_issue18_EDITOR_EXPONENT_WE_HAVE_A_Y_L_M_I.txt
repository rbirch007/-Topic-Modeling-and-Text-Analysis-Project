EDITOR EXPONENT: We have a Y. L. M, I.
Association with twenty-one officers and mem-
bers, with an average attendance of about fif-
teen, which is very well considering that there
are only seventeen families in the setlement.
	We meet every other Tuesday and the last
Sunday evening in every month with the Y.
M. M. I. Association which gives us about
three meetings monthly.
	We make out a programme every meeting
day for the next meeting, which consists of
select readings, essays, recitations and songs,
and I can truly say the girls do well in all;
they also arise and bear their testimonies when
called upon and I feel the Lord is blessing
them.
	The boys and girls gave me a very pleasant
surprise, on the twenty-second of December.
They met at the residence of Mrs. Louise
Hanson, our school teacher, and at six o'clock,
they burst in upon me not only themselves, but
with baskets well filled, not only with picnic,
but with many pretty and suitable gifts.
	We spent the evening very pleasantly in
songs and games until about half-past eight,
when refreshments were passed round after
which we had more songs, games, and a little
dancing, the company retired about ten o'clock.
The Y. M. M. I Association is in good runing
order and I believe the boys are all trying to
do right in every way.
	There has been scarcely any drinking here
during the holidays, which is very gratifying.
	We have a day-school with twenty-six
names enrolled, which is doing well.
	May the spirit of God rest down upon us all
that we may remain faithful to the end is the
prayer of,
					ELIZABETH MILLER,
	President Y. L. M. I. A.
