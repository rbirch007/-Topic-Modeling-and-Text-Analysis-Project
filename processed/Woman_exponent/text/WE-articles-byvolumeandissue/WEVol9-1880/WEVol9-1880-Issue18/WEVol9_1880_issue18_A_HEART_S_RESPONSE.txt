A HEART'S RESPONSE.

The trav'ler on the barren desert-waste
	Would soon grow faint and weary, and despair,
Were there not cooling draughts for him to taste,
	Upspringing from some green oasis there.

And even so in life, when fortunes frown,
	When hearts are sad, and brains are tempest-toss'd
When joy and hope, e'en faith is almost gone
	And worthy aspirations too seem lost—

How sweet to know, indeed sublime the thought
	That friendship's link remains unbroken still;
What nectar sweet to mortal lips is brought—
	Ecstatic bliss. Our cups of joy o'erfill!

E'en pain grows lighter 'neath a loving stroke,
	Wild grief, in time assuaged by friendship true;
Ambitions fire enkindled, and the cloak;
	Of woe exchanged for dress of brighter hue.

Upon my hand there sparkles, fair and bright,
	Thy gift, this ring, an emblem of thy trust;
A talisman of good 'twill be, a beacon light,
	Revealing names and faces pure and just.

The day is past! But oh, the memory.
	Live on, and ere will live throughout all time;
Yes, longer still, through bright eternity
	It will be written on this heart of mine.

Loved ones, for all the honor you have shown,
	As recompense what can I say or do?
My heart is full, and fain would make it known,
	That love and gratitude abound for you.

Ah, yes, my future aim in life shall be
	To live more worthy of thy trust and love;
Be true in joys and through adversity,
	And join you in that better world above.
