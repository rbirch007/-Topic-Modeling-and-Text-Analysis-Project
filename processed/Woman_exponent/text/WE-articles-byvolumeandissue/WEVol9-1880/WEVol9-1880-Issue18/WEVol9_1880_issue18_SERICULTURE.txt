SERICULTURE.

	Report of the Silk Association of Sevier Stake.
The Silk Association of Sevier Stake was or-
ganized May 26th 1879, with the following of-


ficers: President Eliza Ann Ogilvie, Counselors
Ann Marsh and Annie Hansen, Secretary Mary
Marsh, Treasurer, Martha L. Peterson. Since
we were organized we have been doing all we
could, to promote the interest of sericulture in
this stake, more especially in Richfield, we have
purchased some mulberry trees of three and
four years old and planted them on a quarter
lot belonging to the Relief Society. This winter
we have purchased a lot, of seventy-five dollars
($75) cost on which we intend to plant mulberry
trees. We have several hundred cuttings ready
when the season comes to plant them. Some of
the settlements of this Stake are doing very
well in this branch of home-industry.
