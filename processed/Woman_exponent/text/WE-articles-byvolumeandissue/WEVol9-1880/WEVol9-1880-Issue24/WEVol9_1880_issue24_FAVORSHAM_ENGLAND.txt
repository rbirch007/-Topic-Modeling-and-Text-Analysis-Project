FAVORSHAM, ENGLAND.

	Extract from a letter received by Secretary S.
M. Kimball,  from  Elder  Edward King, Kent,
England:

	"The  Favorsham  Relief  Society is  a live in-
stitution, its  members  are  energetic and trying
to live as becomes Latter-day Saints;  the Society
consists of twenty officers and members.  The
sisters have done a good work the past winter in
hunting up and relieving the poor.  The official
members  visit  among  the  Saints,  instructing
them  to  be  prayerful and live near to God, ac-
cording to the knowledge they have received.
	On the 21st ult. the Society held a semi-annual
Conference;  much  of  the  time  was occupied 
with reports, a  synopsis  of which  was  sent to
the London  Conference.   The  President, Sister
Ann Cornford, is a woman  worthy  of  the  confi-
dential  support  of  her  sisters  in Zion.  At the
close of the  afternoon meeting a social tea, pro-
vided by the President and her Counselors, was
enjoyed, and in the evening  the  Relief Society 
held a joint session with the Priesthood of Fav-
orsham  Branch, Brother  J. A. Jennings and I
taking part.   Bro. Jennings is doing well in the 
ministry; I can truly  say the Spirit of the Lord
was with us on this occasion, and I feel sure that
the result of the meeting will be increased unity
among the Saints here.


[Column 1]
R. S., Y. L. M. I. A. AND PRIMARY
