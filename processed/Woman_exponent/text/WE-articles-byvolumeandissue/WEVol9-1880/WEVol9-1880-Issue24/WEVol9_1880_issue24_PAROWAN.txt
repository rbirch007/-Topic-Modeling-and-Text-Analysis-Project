PAROWAN.

	The first yearly Conference of the Parowan
Stake was held in Cedar City Feb. 13, 1881,
President Ellen W. Lunt presiding. After
opening exercises; minutes of previous Confer-
ence were read by the Stake Secretary, also sta-
tistical and financial reports from the different
branch societies. The representatives gave
very encouraging verbal reports of their prog-
ress, also very good counsel and much good
general instruction.
	Elder Thomas Durham spoke in a very in-
teresting manner on the subject of Music.
Hoped to see the time when it would be more
extensively cultivated in all our institutions.

[Column 3]
	President Henry Lunt and Bishop C. J.
Arthur said they were greatly pleased to hear
and see the marked improvement our sisters
were making, and gave us some very excellent
instructions. Referred to the exhibitions
given by the Primary Associations as being
very pleasing and conducive of much good.
Urged the sisters to subscribe more liberally
for the EXPONENT.
	Statistical and Financial report for the Stake
R. S., for the year ending Feb. 12, 1881.
	Statistical: Teachers, 51; members, 326: total
officers and members, 377; meetings held, 86;
average attendance, 72; subscribers for EXPO-
NENT, 16.
	Financial: On hand at date of last report,
$43.05; Property, $1,834.25 1/2; wheat, 116 1/2 bu.
Receipts, cash, $85.60; property, $118.80; wheat,
40 bu. Disbursements: poor, cash, $49.37;
property, $48.50; emigration, cash, $21.30;
Temple, cash, $36.75; property, $32.25; home
industries, $14.25; building purposes, cash;
$69.40; property, $10; on hand at present date,
cash, $40.50; property, $1,945.56; wheat, 377
bu., 32 lb.
