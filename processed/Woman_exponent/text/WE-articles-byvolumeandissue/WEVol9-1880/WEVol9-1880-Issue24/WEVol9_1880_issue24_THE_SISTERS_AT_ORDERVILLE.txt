THE SISTERS AT ORDERVILLE.

	Sisters E. R. S. Smith and Zina D. Young, ac-
companied by Sister Minerva Snow of St. George,
visited Orderville, arriving on the 16th of Feb-
ruary, and stayed until the 21st; during which
time they held five meetings, organized three
Primary Associations, and said a great many in-
structive and encouraging things to the people.
We are truly thankful for the visit of our sisters
and for the Primary Associations which they
organized. We have long felt the need of some
means of getting our children more completely
under our control. Their visit has been benefi-


cial also in the way of awaking the sisters of the
Relief Societies to a sense of their duties as
Mothers in Israel and helpmeets to man in his
great work of saving souls. Our children will
long remember the kind words of Sisters Eliza
and Zina, and we all hope to meet them soon
again.
			LOUISA C. SPENCER, Pres't R. S.
