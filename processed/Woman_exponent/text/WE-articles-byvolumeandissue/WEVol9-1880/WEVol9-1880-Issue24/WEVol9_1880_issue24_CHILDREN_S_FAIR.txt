CHILDREN'S FAIR.

	In September there will be a Children's Fair
held in this city, under the auspices of the Presi-
dency of the Primary Associations of Salt Lake
Stake. The Presidents of the different branches
in this Stake are expected to take an active part
in aiding to make the FAIR a success. All kinds
of articles, useful and ornamental that can possi-
bly be made by the little folks, they should be
encouraged in making. There is no doubt it will


be a grand affair. The Fair at Farmington last
year surpassed anything which had been antici-
pated, but we expect this will be much larger as
the children have already had some few lessons
in getting up bazars in their respective wards.
