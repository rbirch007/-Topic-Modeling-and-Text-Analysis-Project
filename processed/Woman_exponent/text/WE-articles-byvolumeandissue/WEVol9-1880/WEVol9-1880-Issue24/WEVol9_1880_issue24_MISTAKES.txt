MISTAKES.
*		*		*		*		*		*
Who does not make mistakes? If there is one
Who feels no vain regrets for ought he's done,
Who would not fain recall some things he's said,
Or joy to unravel some entangled thread,
I'd like to know him, and become his friend,
And in his company some seasons spend,
That I might learn the laws which he hath kept,
And strive henceforth to step where he hath step'd.

I have this sample in the Son of God,
And He has plainly marked the path He trod;
I need not err, the pattern is complete,
'Tis my own weakness where I failures meet.
But oh! what charity should here prevail,
Since mortal strength is ever prone to fail;
How constant, too, should be the fervent call,
Lord, help us! save us, or we perish all!
*		*		*		*		*		*
									LULA.
