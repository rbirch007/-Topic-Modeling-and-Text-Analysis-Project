OBITUARY.
Departed  this  life, at  11 P.  M. July 17, 1880.
Sabina A.  Dollinger  Olaphant,  beloved wife of
Charles H. Oliphant.  Sister Oliphant was born
in the City of New York, Oct. 7, 1839.  She em-
braced the Gospel in 1861. She left all her rela-
tions and a good  home, and  gathered  with  the
Saints in 1862.  With the exception of her cous-
in,  Mrs. A. C. Pyper, she is the only  one of her
family in the Church.  In  1862 she became  the
wife of Brother C. H. Oliphant, was the mother
of one  child  who died in infancy.  For  some
years she was a member of the Tabernacle Choir.
and also of the Theatre Choir in Salt Lake City.
The last ten  years  she  has  been  a  resident  of
Kanab in which place she has made herself very
useful.  She  was  Secretary  to  the  Stake  Sun-
day  School,  assistant  secretary,  to  the  Stake
Relief Societies.  She was also a Sunday  School
Teacher, a member  of  the Ward Choir and  the
Dramatic  Association.  Her great love for, and
knowledge of music,made her a competent teach-
er, and the people  of Kanab  are much indebted
to  her  for  their  present  advancement  in  the
knowledge of music.
Many years previous to her death, she suffered
from  a  very  severe  form  of  neuralgia,  but  her
ambition was so great that  she led a very active
life.  For  two  months  before  her  death  her
disease took the form of dropsy of the heart.
Although  apparently dying  for five days, her
last hours were very peaceful.  Our sister is very
much missed in Kanab.  Her  brilliant  wit and
sparkling manner made her a very  pleasant as-
sociate.
