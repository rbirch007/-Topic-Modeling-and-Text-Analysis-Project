ON OUR FIRST BAPTISMAL ANNIVERSARY.

Hail! my Sister!—ever dear,
Hail! my Sister of a year!—
Through God's Spirit Sister mine,
By my spirit Sister thine!

God of love! and God of us
Father of the universe!
Look upon the compact--we
Sealed by covenant with thee!
Let thy blessing on it rest,
For its sake may we be blest!
We desire to do thy wil!,
Thro' good report and more—thro' ill;
Help us ever to obey
Thy righteous laws from day to day.

Came we not from worlds afar
Where thou wert our guiding star?
Came we not with willing mind,
Forgetting all we left behind?
Came we not to do a work
Which none but coward hearts will shirk?
Came we not to build, and plant
Land bestowed by God's own "grant."
Came we not to plant the vine
For the Sacramental wine?—
Came we not the seed to spread
For the Sacramental bread?—
Came we not with joy to gain
A new and everlasting name?
Came we not to win our dead
Through the blood of Christ our head?
Came we not to gain a crown
By a cross—by thee laid down?
Came we not to rule and reign
O'er the subjects we might gain?
Came we not with willing hands
To obey all thy commands?
Came we not all this to do
And a thousand others too?
Father! we are ever thine
Through the changing scenes of time!
Keep us faithful unto thee,
Such our souls desire to be!
Bless us every step we take,
Love us for thy mercy's sake;
Keep us faithful to the end,
Father! God! Redeemer, Friend!
						HANNAH T. KING.
Salt Lake City.
