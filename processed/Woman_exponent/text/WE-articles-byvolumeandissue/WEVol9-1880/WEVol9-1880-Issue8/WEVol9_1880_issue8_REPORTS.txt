REPORTS.

	The Relief Society of Sanpete Stake held
their annual Conference at Mt Pleasant Aug.
15th, 1880. The usual opening services were
offered, after which the reports were read: the
condensed reports stated that in this stake there
were 224 teachers; 1721 members 2011 total
officers and members; 101 meetings held; 625
average attendance 109 copies of the WOMAN'S
EXPONENT taken.
	The Financial report states that since the
organization of Relief Sociities in 1868, they
have received $74, 973.67 in cash and property;
and 2. 945 bushels of wheat, most of the wheat
is loaned to the brethren, disbursements to the
poor amounts to $58. 348 22; emigration $295
60; temples $515 92; home-industries 918 19;
books $44. 40; missionaries $379.15; building
897. 79; total $399. 34. And have on hand
$121. 74; in cash; property $13. 392. 59.
	The Conference passed off pleasantly, and
almost every one who addressed the audience
made appreciative mention of the refreshing
visit the stake had enjoyed just previous to
Conference, from our much beloved sisters E.
R. S. Smith and E. B. Wells, who truly added
new warmth and glow to the flame of zeal and
energy which inspires the hearts of the sisters
in assisting to forward the grand principles of
God. Much excellent and appropriate instruc-
tion was given by those who spoke especially
President M. A. P. Hyde and Counselors who
are ever blessed with a rich flow of the Spirit of
God. They urged the sisters to be energetic in
planting mulberry trees and raising silk. Also
reminded them of the great responsibility rest-
ing upon mothers in the training of their child-
ren. Told them to be very careful in the ex-
amples they set before them,to be exemplary in
their manners,promises and talk,that they might
preserve their full confidence and be enabled to
instill into their minds the principles of the
Gospel, and not let them grow up in infidelity.
Several of the brethren spoke approvingly of
the proceedings of the Conference. Singing.
Benediction by J. Frantzen. Conference ad-
journed until November 15th 1880.
