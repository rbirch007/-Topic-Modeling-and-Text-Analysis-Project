PAPANUI, CANTERBURY, N. Z.,
					June 19, 1880.

	DEAR EDITOR:—It is with feelings of pleas-
ure, that I am able to bear my humble testi-
mony to this work, and I thank our Heavenly
Father that He has given me a knowledge for
myself, that I know it to be the work of God.
I am also very happy to think we can keep up
to our sisters in Zion as far as possible, and by
the help of God we will carry it through.
Pray for us, sisters in Zion, for we need your
faith and prayers, and may the Lord bless us
in all our undertakings, that we may be able
to carry them through, and by the help of God
we will.
	We have also established a small Sunday
School here, to instruct the minds of the chil-
dren in the Gospel of life and salvation, as we
know that the influence of the Christian
world's false traditions must be guarded against,
or else their minds would be turned against
the true Gospel. We hope, by the help of the
Almighty, that we may be able to keep the
young minds pure until a way can be opened
to gather home among the Saints of God in
the valleys of Zion, and out of wicked Baby-
lon. We are very few in number, but still we
do our best to live up to our most holy reli-
gion, and gather up to Zion as soon as possible.
There are quite a number of Saints going home
by this mail, which will make our number
still less; but I hope to see the day when we
shall all be able to gather up to the land of
God's choice. May we who are here put on
the whole armor of righteousness, and battle
bravely to overcome all impure desires, and to
establish a reign of peace on the earth, that
we may receive all the blessings there are in
store for all the faithful. My whole desire is
to keep true and faithful to the covenants
which I have made with God, and to the work
in which we are engaged; and I pray God to
bless all his children in the earth, from the
highest to the lowest,it is the fervent prayer of
your Sister in the New and Everlasting Cove-
nant,
