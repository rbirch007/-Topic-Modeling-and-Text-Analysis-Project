EXCURSION TO SALT LAKE.

	On Tuesday Aug. 10th, the Relief Society of
the Third Ward of Ogden City, went on a
pleasure trip to Lake Shore. About 200 peo-
ple of both old and young, including a few in-
vited guests our worthy Bishop and Counselors
not forgetting the poor of the ward, all
participating in the affair. On arriving at the
shore,the recreation commenced; some repaired
immediately to the Lake to bathe in its briny
waters, others enjoyed themselves in a large
and commodious bowery. The happy smiling
faces of the veteran fathers the aged mothers,
and middle aged; and youthful sons and
daughters, demonstrated to the observer that
all were happy. After bathing tables were


spread with dainties, fit for a king, of which
all partook freely: an hour or so was spent
very pleasently in speeches and songs. Our
old friend Father Thomas Richardson in behalf
of the Mormon Battalion, followed by our fel-
low citizen Aaron F. Farr, made a few approp-
riate remarks,on the Pioneers, contrasting 1847
to this present day. These speeches were fol-
lowed by others, from Bishops Winslow Farr
and Counselors, congratulatory of the happy
occasion. Mr. John P. Williams and others,
sang a song, "Hard times come again no more."
also Brother John Hastings 77 years old sang
an old Mormon song much to the amusement
of all. 
	There is much credit due our respected Presi-
dent Sister Dinah M. White for the able man-
ner in which the whole affair was conducted
and the great interest taken by her, for the poor,
and the aged and in fact all. She exerted her-
self to make every thing pleasant and agreeable,
and we think that such parties are calculated
to bring union and good feeling in our Society.
Ever praying for the welfare of our worthy
paper, I subscribe myself,
		Yours Respectfully
