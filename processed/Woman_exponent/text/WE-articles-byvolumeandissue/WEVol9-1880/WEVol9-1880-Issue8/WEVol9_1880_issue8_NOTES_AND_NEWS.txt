NOTES AND NEWS.

	FROM Martinique comes the news of an-in-
teresting antiquarian discovery, in the shape of
the anchor of the ship in which Columbus sailed
on his third voyage to the new world. It is
well known that in 1498 his little fleet came to
anchor at the south-west extremity of the islrnd
of Trinidad, called Arenas Point, and that dur-
ing the night the ship encountered great dan-
ger from a tidal wave, caused by the sudden
swelling of one of the rivers that empties itself
into the Gulf of Paria. The only damage
suffered, however, was the loss of the Admiral's
anchor. This has recently been found by Sen-
or Agostino, while excavating in his garden at
Point Arenas. The anchor weighs 1100
pounds, and was at first supposed to be of
Phoenician origin, but careful inspection re-
vealed the date 1497 on the stock. The geol-
ogical conditions of the ground in which the
discovery was made bear out the conclusions
that the anchor is a relic of Columbus.—Ex.
