PARLIAMENTARY ELECTION  IN  THE  ISLE  OF
MAN.--THE  WOMEN'S VOTE,--The first  con-
tested election in the Isle of Man for members of 
the  new  House  of Keys  took  place  in Acre
Sheading to-day.  There  were  polling  booths
in the villages of Andreas, Bride, and  Lez-Ayre,
and in the town of Ramsey.  There was four can-
didates for three  seats, Messrs. Farrante, Martin,
and Cowell, nominated by the Church and Wes-
leyan party, and  Mr.  R.  Teare, the nominee of
the extreme Nonconformists.  There was much
excitement during the day.  Party colours were
freely dispalyed and  business  was  suspended.
The first vote was recorded by one of the newly-
enfranchised women.  The result  was  declared
about  5  30,  as follows:-- Farrant; 333; Cowell,
326; Martin, 295, Reare, 255.--Manchester Guard-
ian.
