GRATITUDE
	Having been, greatly  benefitted  and blessed
by the impulses recurring  from  gratitude, I in-
dulge in a few reflections  upon  such a manifest
boon, and  which  I am proud to  say it is one of
the choicest  component  parts  of  my  nature.
Had I the ability to do justice to its goodness, I
would wield my pen loudly  fraught with  its
praise.  I value  it as a noble  trait of  the mind
when we frame  our  hearts  to  give it free exer-
cise. It inspires  alike  the  old and young, the
rich, the poor, the generous aud the good, and to
cultivate which is much to our interest such a
heaven-born sentiment enriches  and exalts.  It
is an influence which should  be  fostered,  for  it
impresses  and  strengthens  us  in  our daily la-
bors;  its  happifying  features  are ever welcome
to the honest in heart. It calls forth our best
emotions  and  has  a large claim upon our finer
feelings; also  as  consoler and comforter,
giving new and invigorating  life  and content-

We
would lose much were our hearts  devoid of such
a treasure.  Let us train, then, and not careless-
ly crush  such  a  heavenly attribute, for it glad-
dens  the  heart  and makes  cheerful the home,
but ever tender our sincere thanks to our Father
in  Heaven for so precious  a jewel.  May it al-
ways retain a prominent place in our bosoms and
flow with  our  prayers for the mercies extended
towards us.
	To me it is a constant  companion,  my  heart
teems with gratefulnes when  I  behold the
works of our  Creator, and  am  led many times
to exclaim, "thank God!" when viewing nature's 
scenery or in hearing the  warbling  of the birds
or the murmuring  of the  rill,  the  snow,  the
sunshine, the rain, the  dew drops, all posses a
charm for me, and I cheerfully  give thanks for
my daily  bread, and  for  my children, who I
pray may realize the words, "I am the bread and
the life," and that they  may praise their Maker
while they've breath Let  our  prayers ascend
for the continued  advantages  we have in these
peaceful  vales, and not  only  realize, but show
our gratefulness for  the priveliges extended to
us in this dispensation,  which  I can  sense; we
are truly living in  very  eventful  times;  surely
the judgments of God are at our very doors  and
we are told  commence  at  the  house  of  the
Lord.   We have been afflicted here for months
with  that  terrible  disease diphtheria, which I 
am thankful to say is abating.   The  people  ac-
knowledge  the  chastening  hand  of the Lord,
we knew He desires us to live  nearer to Him,
but whether we will do so remains  to be seen.
The hearts of those who  have been  called to 
part with their little ones have been sorely tried,
but when the Lord calls we  must submit.  We
are taught, which is a great consolation, that he
is reserving a choice seed unto himself.
	I feel thankful for  the  priesthood,  and  that
those at the head of the Church have  been so
generously inspired in this the Year  of  Jubilee,
and hope the Lord's  poor  will  feel  to  rejoice in
the goodness  and  mercy of  God, and  offer up
their gratefulness in thanks; and what a blessing
the storage wheat  will prove to many, and may
the sisters not slacken in their energies to carry
out the counsel of  the  servants of  God.  I am
also thankful for the organizations of the sisters;
I know  the  members thereof enjoy a  greater
portion of the Holy Spirit since they were insti-
tuted, and have a better  understanding  of  the 
Gospel plan of salvation,  the  good  counsel  and
instruction which we receive  in  these Societies
are of a nature to  cause  us  to  reflect, and the
teachings are not entirely lost upon us but as-
sist us in sustaining each other.  I am  digress-
ing from my subject, and I fear lest I  tire  you,
but in conclusion  let  me  say I believe  in the
principle of plural marriage, and when or where


I may be will aid its progress.  I have been much
interested in reading the  intelligent speeches of
our sisters upon political  questions,  and  hoped
they will soon  be  looked  upon  not  only as  co-
workers, but co-equal with man, in all that per-
tains to the welfare of society.  I pray our father
in heaven to abundantly bless you sisters in your
labors of love, and may we continually strive to
live our religion,  is the wish of,  Your Sister in
the Church of Christ		M.	A. TILL
	Goshen, May 11, 1880.
