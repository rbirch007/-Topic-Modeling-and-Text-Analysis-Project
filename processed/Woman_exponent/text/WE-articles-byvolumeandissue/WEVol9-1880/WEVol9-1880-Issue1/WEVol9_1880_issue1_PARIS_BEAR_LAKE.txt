			PARIS. BEAR LAKE.

	The members of the first and second ward
Primary Associations of Paris met and held
their first anniversary in the Paris first Ward
meeting house, on Tuesday at one o'clock, May
18, 1880.
	Present by invitation Apostle C. C. Rich,
Bishop H. J. Horne, Elder R. S. Horne, Sister
Julia Lindsey, President, and Sister Maria
Rich, Counselor of the Relief Society of this
Stake and quite a number of the parents of the
children.
	Meeting called to order by Sister H. B.
Humphrey's. Song by a company of little girls
entitled "Welcome to all," Brother R. S. Horne
playing the accompaniment on the organ.
Prayer by Bishop H. J. Horne. Singing, "We
thank thee O God for a prophet." Sister Julia
Lindsey then addressed the children for a short
time, after which there was a reading, recitations,
dialogues and songs by the children.
	The first number of the paper entitled "The
Primary Monthly," (first ward) edited by Mary
A. Rich, was then read by her.
	Sister Maria Rich then spoke a short time to
the children, and urged mothers to be diligent in
sending the children to the meetings.
	Brother C. C. Rich occupied a short time ex-
horting the children to obedience, etc..
	Singing, "Do what is right." Benediction by
Brother R. S. Horne. The children then en-
gaged in dancing for a short time. Picnic being
provided by the parents was then passed
around. Dancing till six p.m., when all dis-
presed feeling that they had enjoyed themselves
very much. Number of children present 97.
	Names of the officers, first ward, President,
Elizabeth Nye; Alvira Ricks, Mary A. Rich,
Counselors. Second ward—H. B. Humphrey's,
President; Alice Athey, Sarah Clifton, Coun-
selors; Ellen Price Secretary.
