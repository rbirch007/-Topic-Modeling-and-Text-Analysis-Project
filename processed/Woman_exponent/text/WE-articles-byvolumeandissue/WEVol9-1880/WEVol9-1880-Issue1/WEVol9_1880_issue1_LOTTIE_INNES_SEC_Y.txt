					LOTTIE INNES, Sec'y

	The second Quarterly Conference of the Relief
Societies of Wasatch Stake of Zion was held at
Heber City, April 30th, 1880, Mrs Emma Brown
presiding. After the usual opening exercises,
the minute of the last conference were read and
accepted; the reports of the several wards were

[Column 2]
also read, also reports for the Y. L. M. I. and
Primary Associations; all the reports, both ver-
bal and written, showing each respective society
in a prosperous condition. There was a good at-
tendance, many of the brethren were present,
from whom we received much good and valu-
able instructions. The sisters all seem to be
alive to their duties and callings and always
ready to look after the poor and to comfort and
relieve the sick, which is the first duty of the
Relief Societies. Many of the sisters spoke on
the training of our children, that we should
teach them the principles of the Church both
by precept and example, that they may thereby
become good citizens and horoable members in
the Church and Kingdom of God.
	President Hatch spoke a short time. Said the
Relief Societies were helps to the Priesthood, and
if counsel was needed, to go to our Bishop. He
wished the sisters to use their influence to help
establish a High School, that our young men
and ladies might be educated at home, and to
strive and put down drunkenness and other
evils which exist in our midst.
	A vote was taken to sustain a co-operatieve mil-
linery store, to be located in Heber City, which
was carried unanimously.
	President Brown addressed the sisters, coun-
seling them to be faithful in attending to all
their duties, that we may thereby enjoy the
blessings promised to the faithful.
			EMMA BROWN, Stake Presdent.
			MARY BRONSON, Secretary.
