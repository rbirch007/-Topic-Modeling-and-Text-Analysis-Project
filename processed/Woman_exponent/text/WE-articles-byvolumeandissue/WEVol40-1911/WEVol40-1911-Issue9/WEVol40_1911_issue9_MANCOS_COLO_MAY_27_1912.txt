			MANCOS, COLO, May 27, 1912.
Mrs. Emmeline B. Wells,
	Salt Lake City.
	I write to inform you of the change made
in this the San Juan Stake.
	May 21, 1912, there were two stakes or-
ganized.
	The west side is called the Platt Stake
in honor of the late Platt D. Lyman, and
Sister Mary N. Jones is president of the
Relief Society.
	The east side of the stake is called the
Young Stake in honor of Brigham Young,
Jr., and Sister M. M. Halls, the daughter of
F. A. Hammond, is appointed president of 
the Relief Society of the Young Stake. I
have been president of the Mutual Improve-
ment Association for twenty-six years,
though I have also labored in the Relief
Society.
	I would like to receive any instructions
you have to write, as I will not be able to
see you until October conference.
	What is your idea of teachers visiting and 
holding a little meeting with the mother and 
girls, that is to have order about it, and talk 
Relief Society work? 
	My idea is to have the sisters offer a 
prayer before going that the Lord may help
them. If there are any young girls at the
home, call them to sit down with the mother
and listen to the counsel of the teachers.
And then have the mother and girls ex-
press themselves in regard to Relief Society
work. I think that would instill in the
hearts of the young girls the importance of
the work.
	For my part I think there is no grander
association in the Church than the Relief
Society.
	There is a great and noble work to do and
we want the young mothers to learn this 
great truth. 
	I desire to work faithfully in this office
and work up the importance of the Relief
Society. I pray God to help me to do so.
	May the Lord bless you, my dear sister,
and strengthen you for every labor you have 
to do.
		I remain your co-laborer,
