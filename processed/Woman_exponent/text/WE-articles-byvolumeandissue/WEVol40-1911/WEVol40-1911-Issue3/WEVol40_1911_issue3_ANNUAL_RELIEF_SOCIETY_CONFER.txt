ANNUAL RELIEF SOCIETY CONFER-
			ENCE.
	The Conference of the General Relief So-
ciety was held in the Assembly Hall, Salt
Lake City, Utah, October 4, 1911. Pres-
ident Emmeline B. Wells presided.
	Morning session, 10 a. m.—After singing
“Come, come ye Saints.” opening prayer
was offered by Elder William Burton. Sing-
ing, “Guide Us, O Thou Great Jehovah.”
A contralto solo was given by Mrs. Agnes
Olsen Thomas.
	President Emmeline B. Wells gave a lov-
ing and sympathetic welcome to all to the
conference, and encouraged all to continue
in the great work being accomplished by the
Relief Society, both at home and abroad.
	Mrs. Minerva R. Knowlton, of Davis
Stake, responded to the greeting, express-
ing the love and reverence felt by their ap-
preciation of her efforts in assisting local
and stake organizations.
	Mrs. Lizzie Thomas Edward sang a
soprano solo, “O Loving Father.”
	Counselor Julina L. Smith was the next
speaker, speaking in appreciation of the
work being done by the members of the Re-
lief Society. Referred to the interest now
being taken in working for the dead, relat-
ing several instances in which this work had
been started because of lost ones coming
to make the request in dreams.
	Mrs. Rebecca Standring, President of Al-
pine Stake, gave a favorable report of the
work of the Relief Society in that stake.
Told of the unity of their Board and of the
good work being accomplished by the mis-
sionaries appointed to visit the delinquent
members and the young women and urge
their attendance at the meetings of the Re-
lief Society.
