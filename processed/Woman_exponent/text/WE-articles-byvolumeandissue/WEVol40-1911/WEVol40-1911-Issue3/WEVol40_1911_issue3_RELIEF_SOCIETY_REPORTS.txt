RELIEF SOCIETY REPORTS.
		BEAR RIVER STAKE.
	A well attended and extremely interesting
conference of the Bear River Stake Relief
Society convened in the Garland Hall, Wed-
nesday, June 14, 1911, at 10 a. m. and 2 p. m.
Stake President Julia A. Richards, presid-
ing.
	The visitors of the general bord were
Sisters Clarissa S. Williams and Romania B.
Penrose of Salt Lake City. There were also
present members of the stake presidency,
high councilmen, bishops of wards and other
brethren. Members of the Relief Society
Stake Board present.
	The forenoon was devoted to hearing re-
ports of the different wards, and listening to
the interesting addresses made by the visit-
ing sisters. Morgan Miller of Penrose Ward
rendered a fine solo.
	During the noon hour the Garland sis-
ters served excellent refreshments to all
present.
	Afternoon session.—Sisters Williams and
Penrose delivered splendid addresses. Re-
marks were made by Stake President Mil-
ton H. Welling. Bishop J. M. Stokes and
Elder J. P. Christenson.
	Solo, Margaret W. Manning.
