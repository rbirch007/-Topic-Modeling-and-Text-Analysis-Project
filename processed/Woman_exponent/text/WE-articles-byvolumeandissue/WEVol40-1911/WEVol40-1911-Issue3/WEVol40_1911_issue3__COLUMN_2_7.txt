	Conference adjourned until 1:30 p. m.
	Afternoon session commenced by singing.
Prayer led by Brother William Southwick.
Continued by singing, “Now Let Us Re-
joice.” President A. J. Evans compli-
mented the sisters on their good looks.
Your faces bear evidence to me that you are
living lives of Latter-day Saints. Spoke to
us about keeping strict guard over our chil-
dren; see that they are not out late hours,
buggy riding unprotected. They are pre-
cious to us even more than some of us re-
alize. Sister Ruby Thornton, another of
our returned missionaries from the Northern
States, felt that she was blessed with many
great privileges and opportunities. Her re-
sponsibility in the Relief Society work in
the mission field had shown her the great
need of these organizations, it is wonder-
ful how the Lord will bless us when we
humble ourselves. The testimonies this
morning have given me a greater deter-
mination to live a good life, to do some of
the good things these sisters have done. I
want to be remembered for the good thingsdone in my life. Sister Susa Y. Gates said,
“These enthusiastic speakers, the lady mis-
sionaries carry with them the only Spirit
that can bring reform among the immoral-
ity of the world. Let us not forget the rev-
erence due the Priesthood and appreciate
our mission as women and never depreciate
being born a woman. It was said of Pres-
ident Young that he once predicted that no
man that ever faltered in the performance
of his duties will ever stand at the head of
this Church, and I feel that I can predict
the same of our women in aspiring to be-
come the elect lady. The Genealogical So-
ciety has been organized to help our people.
If you will set your hearts to do this work
you will find ample time to attend to your
family duties. This has been given to me
as a mission and to teach it to the people
and urge them to see to it, there is so much
of this work to be done and so few that are
doing it.
	A reading entitled “Only a Girl.” given
by Sister Justesen of the Third Ward of
American Fork. Singing by Sister Fern
Vance, “The Songs My Mother Used to
Sing.” We were privileged to have our
beloved President Sister Wells talk to us
again Her remarks continued on the days
of Nauvoo and of the lives of the great
men and women of our Church. There
are very few left who knew the Prophet.
The women who served and waited upon
him are few and far between. We honor
their memories. She eulogized the beauti-

ful characters of Emma Smith, Mother
Whitney, Eliza R. Snow, Zina D. Young,
and our late President Bathsheba W. Smith,
having been personally acquainted with
them all. We find very little written about
the women. They do not figure as largely
in history as do the men, but then good
deeds are recorded in the book of life. She
heard the Prophet Joseph deliver all of his
last sermons, remembered distinctly the time
that he was being guarded from his ene-
mies, the terrible cloud that hung over the
city the time of the martyrdom. I pray that
the Lord will bless you to work for the dead
as well as the living and live lives that are
worthy the name of Latter-day Saints.”
	President Standring bore testimony to
that which had been said. “I feel thankful
that my Heavenly Father has strengthened
me this day and that I have been permitted
to partake of the Spirit of this Conferenec.
I love this work and can safely say that I
have never opposed any principle of the
Gospel or any man in authority. I want
to use the power God has given me to do
and say the things that will exalt and purify
us all. May the Lord bless you, my dear
sisters, and spare your lives to meet again
in another Conference.”
	Sister E. J. T. Roberts thanked the Sis-
ters of American Fork for the nice lunch-
eon served in the building at the noon hour,
making it much easier for the sisters to at-
tend Conference.
	President Chipman rejoiced in the testi-
mony borne this day, for the (beautiful
singing, readings and everything that had
been said. Let us trust in the Lord and
He will guide us in our paths.
	After the closing hymn, benediction was
pronounced by Counselor Harriet Jacobs
and conference adjourned for three months.
