			LECTURES. 
	(1) We therefore suggest that each Relief
Society President of Stake, (aside from the
lectures given by missionaries from the Gen-
eral Board), shall provide for at least one
lecture by local talent on research which
shall pertain to either our religion, art,
invention, discovery, history, exploration or
travel, and also one lecture or a series of
lectures, by local talent, on a practical sub-
ject that is, or could be made to be, intensely
vital to the well-being of the people of that
Stake. We would suggest such subjects as
public health, scientific methods applied
to poultry raising, how to make the farm
pay, use and utility applied to house-
building and house furnishing, demonstra-
tions in cooking, sericulture, the best
adapted and most improved scions of fruit
for the locality in which they are to be
grown, methods by which large and small
weedy tracts, unused land, may be utilized,
the culture of the English walnut and al-
mond in our mountain climate, and the ne-
cessity of a vegetable garden. These lec-
tures may be given at the regular or special
Relief Society meetings in the Stake or
Ward.
