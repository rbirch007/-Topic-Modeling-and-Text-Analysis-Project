			BALLOT.
	(2) We believe that a proper apprecia-
tion of the privilege of the ballot and its
sacred responsibility should be shown by ac-
tive use of the franchise in the interest of
better government, clean politics, the elimi-
nation of temptations and a more practical
education, which latter should be secured
by leaving out impractical routine lessons
and replacing the same by work to develop
individual gifts, for in this change we be-
lieve would be found the solution of the
large number of pupils now classed as
failures and would have a salutary effect
upon reform work that present conditions
make necessary. Why not direct the in-
terest and energy of the child who cannot
be made to fit the mould which in systems
of education all pupils are expected to fill,
toward something which he can do, some-
thing within his scope and power and some-
thing in the doing of which he will delight?
