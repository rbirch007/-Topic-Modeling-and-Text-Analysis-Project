			SENTIMENTS.
	The following were sent for the Birthday
number of the Exponent:
	Oriental birthday greetings.
	There are three crowns, of the Law, the
Priesthood and the Kingship; but the
crown of a good name is greater than them
all.
	LYDIA M. VON F. MOUNTFORD.
	A Chinese proverb says, “A big heart is
better than a big house.”
	My mother’s home was not large enough
to take in all the world, except through the
sympathy of her big heart.
	Everything for the uplift of humanity
has, ever and always, claimed her ardent
sympathy and capable, skillful co-operation.
	To raise the lowly, cheer the downheart-
led, give freely of her substance to the poor,
comfort the bereaved, has been to her
through life a work of love.
	Such works are “quick and mighty,” good
fruits have and shall follow such sowing,
for the LAW of LOVE reigns King of all.
				MELL. C. WOODS.
	Wallace, Idaho, March 8, 1912.
	Over many difficulties triumphant; amid
the checquered scenes of life showing true
fortitude; possessed of the great charac-
teristic principle that brought Abraham
blessings, Faithfulness; above all, an un-
ceasing believer and worker in the Church.
Such is “Aunt Em” Wells.
		MARGARET A. ROMNEY,

					March 3rd, 1912.
“Aunt Em” is a woman!—And to be
A woman meaneth flower, star, queen—
And more, much more besides.
				MAUD MAY BABCOCK.
	Not often are we privileged to congratu-
late a friend who has at the age of 84 only
seen 21 birthdays. But how swiftly the
years have flown with their changes of glad-
ness and sorrow, burdens and happiness,
care and tranquilities! But on looking
back, may you only see the hilltops of joy,
forgetting the vales of trouble and dis-
quietude.
		OFFICERS OF JUAB STAKE R. S.
	OUR DEAR SISTER WELLS: Among the
many emblems of love and esteem that will
reach you, on this your natal day, we wish
to place ours.
	We would add, too, our weak words of
appreciation of your worth, and sincerely
wish you every good thing a life so exem-
plary as yours deserves.
	In years you are past the allotted time,
and if it, indeed, be true “that how long
we live, not years but deeds tell,” you have
truly lived a long time.
	Your life has been a rich one, and the
noble deeds so characteristic of you will
remain never failing memorials of your

[Column 2]
labors among women. With a feeling of
pride we look to you as leader of our great
Relief Society organization, and trust that
for many years you will still be our beacon
light.
		ONEIDA STAKE RELIEF SOCIETY.
		ST. JOHNS, ARIZ., Feb. 29, 1912.
	DEAR AUNT EM: In memory of the life-
long friendship which existed between your-
self and my dear grandmother, Sister Ad-
dison Pratt, and her daughters, I beg leave
to offer my love and congratulations on
this, your twenty-first birthday.
	In thinking of your long and useful life,
I am reminded of the beautiful words of
Ella Wheeler Wilcox:

“The prayerful heart breathes through the face
	Like perfume through the rose.
No art can imitate the grace
	Of spiritual repose.
The heart that talks with God in prayer
	Unconsciously imparts
Some portion of the truths learned there
	To other minds and hearts.”
