		MOTHER

[Column 1]
Speechless I stand before her shrine,
Enough to know that she is mine.
Garlands of love let me entwine,
A chaplet for a soul divine.

[Column 2]
Whilst others costly gifts may bring,
And loud and long her praises sing,
And honors rare are proffering,
Love is my only offering.

[Column 3]
Perhaps some time we’ll fondly trace
In memory’s book her wondrous grace.
Ah! Then my gift will claim its place
Love—hallowed time can ne’er efface.
