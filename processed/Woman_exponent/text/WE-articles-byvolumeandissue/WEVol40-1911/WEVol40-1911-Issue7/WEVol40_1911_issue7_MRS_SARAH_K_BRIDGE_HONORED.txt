MRS. SARAH K. BRIDGE HONORED.
	One of the most enjoyable events of the
month occurred at the Eleventh Ward
Amusement Hall on March 8th when one
hundred and twenty-five members and
guests of the Relief Society assembled to
do honor to their president on the occasion
of her fiftieth birthday anniversary.
	The affair took the form of a surprise
party, and Sister Bridge was visibly affected
when she was led into the brilliantly lighted
hall, where the tables were spread with
viands fit for a queen, and decorated with
clusters of carnations, and potted plants.
Among the guests were the ward Bishopric
and the stake Relief Society officers, Sisters
Margaret A. Romney, Minnie Horne James,
Sarah E. Langford, Cecil Sharp Barker
and Belle Hawkins. In behalf of the soci-
ety Bro. Arthur Brown bade the guests
welcome to the feast and eulogised the
labors of Brother and Sister Bridge. Grace
was said by Sister Margaret Romney. While
the banquet was being served, the following
program was rendered: Recitation, Sister
Barker; solo, Mrs. Jos. Wirthlin; song,
Beatrice Sherwood.
	After the dinner Sister Lewis in a neat
little speech presented the guest of honor
with a beautiful umbrella, which Sister
Bridge accepted in a very graceful little
talk.
	Sister Amanda Done gave a reading and
Sister Lillie T. Freeze read an original
poem, dedicated to Sister Bridge, and en-
titled “Fifty Links.”


The medal—the “first gold decoration of
learning”—the highest in its order, has re-
cently been conferred upon Madam Ali
Kuli Khan, wife of the charge d’affaires of
the Persian legation in Washington by the
Shah of Persia. She is one of the few
women in the world so honored.
