				IDA HUNT UDALL.

	The Relief Society sisters of the Utah
Stake of Zion join in sending greetings to
their honored leader and companion on her
twenty-first birthday.
	Dear Sister Wells, you have built up a
character so lovely as to draw unto you
friends in every walk of life.
	Marden says, “Labor is the parent of all
the lasting monuments of this world, wheth-
er in verse or in stone, in poetry or in pyra-
mids.” By your industry you have proven
that labor conquers all things.
	By your devotion to every small duty in
your pathway, you have taught us that
“trifles make perfection, but perfection is no
trifle,” as Michael Angelo says. Your life
thus far is fraught with good deeds, actu-
ated by a noble purpose.
	Having reached your twenty-first anni-
versary, you are only now beginning the
summer time of your life. And yet it would
seem that all of your spring time had been
a summer day, so strong and radiant your
every act.
	Some one has said, “A merry heart is a
continual feast to others besides itself,” and
such you have been to us.
	Through the coming years you need have
no regrets, for you have scattered your
flowers as you went and their beauty and
fragrance will live in the hearts of the mul-
titudes that love you.
	In short, we may say with Longfellow,
“A smile of God thou art.”
	Last, but not least, you have shown that
faith in God and obedience to His author-
ized servants is the noblest aim in life.
