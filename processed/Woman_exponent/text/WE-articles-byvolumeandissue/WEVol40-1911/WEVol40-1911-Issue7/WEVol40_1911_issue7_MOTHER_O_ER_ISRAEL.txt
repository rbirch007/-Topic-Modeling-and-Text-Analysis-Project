MOTHER O’ER ISRAEL.
To Mrs. Emmeline B. Wells, Feb. 24, 1912. 
Mother o’er Israel, Handmaid of God,
Saints arise, call thee blessed where’er thou
	hast trod.
Thy voice like a clarion for truth has pro-
	claimed,
Since e’er thou hast spoken. Our youth are
	inflamed
By thy noble deeds to strive for the like,
And, like thee, for Christ and His kingdom to
	strike.
Thou knewest the dawn of this, God’s last,
	time;
Hast prayed, borne and taught with a cour-
	age divine.
Father of ours, in the heavens above,
Keep thou her, our mother, enshrined in Thy
	love.
Her presence on earth a halo has been;
Where she lives or breathes, even thinks, is
	no sin.
Her brow is now crowned with the snow of
	four score;
Thy Saints would yet hold her long years, ever-
	more.
When Thou callest her Thou wilt say, “Well
	done;
A place in my love, like in theirs, thou hast
	won.”
					Edward P. Kimball.
