A MESSAGE FROM THE SPIRIT WORLD

Little Helen’s spirit speaks—
	List, oh mother, hearken!
“Here the sunny days and weeks,
	Nothing comes to darken.

“Tell our loved ones, on the earth,
	Not to grieve, so lonely;
In this new and heavenly birth,
	There is brightness only.

“Tell dear papa to be brave,
	Pure and noble ever;
So in life beyond the grave,
	We’ll be parted never.

“And our sweet Virginia May,
	(Precious little sister!)
While she prayed the other day,
	I drew near and kissed her.

“Tell her angels truly write,
	All she’s ever saying,
And they smile in such delight,
	When she’s humbly praying.

“Tell dear grandpa, kind and true,
	In my new position,
That to love and help him, too,
	Is, in part, my mission.

“Little children, every one,
	Aid in preparation
Of the great work being done
	For the world’s salvation.

“There were few things on the earth,
	I could do, if any,
Of real, lasting use and worth—
	Here I find so many!

“It was hard to part with you,
	We so loved each other!
But if you could have one view
	Of my new life, Mother—

“You would never wish me there,
	Dreading pain and sorrow;
Here I have no cross to bear,
	Joyous comes each morrow.

“Every day new pleasure brings
	Some new form of beauty;
We all do such happy things,
	Works of love and duty.

“Grandpas, grandmas, cousins dear,
	Hosts of friends and neighbors,
Are so kind and helpful here,
	Sweet are all our labors.

“Sweeter far than play on earth,
Blissful songs and gentle mirth,
	Work is recreation;
	Free from all temptation.

“And the joy of doing good
	Is such holy gladness;
Mother, if you understood
	You would conquer sadness.

“As you grow more reconciled,
	I shall oft be near you,
And the presence of your child
	Will uplift and cheer you.

“But through heavy clouds of grief,
	I’ve no power to reach you,
Cast them off, and find relief,
	Dearest, I beseech you!

“Sneak good words to some poor heart
	Which, like yours is stricken,
And the hope you thus impart
	In your own shall quicken.

“Let your hands be busy, too,
	Comforting some other;
And the Lord will strengthen you,
	Darling, sweetest Mother!

“Papa, mamma,—oh so dear!
	Jesus loves to bless you;
While your Helen hovering near,
	Yearns so to caress you.

“Visit oft the House of Prayer,
	Learn best ways of living;
Trust the Father’s loving care,
	Tender and forgiving.

[Column 2]
“Think not much of worldly wealth;
	Fleeting are earth’s pleasures,
But regard your strength and health—
	These are priceless treasures.

“Search the Scriptures, look aloft;
	Pray, your sins confessing;
Then you’ll feel my presence oft;
	Come with some new blessing.
				—L. L. Greene Richards.

	RELIEF SOCIETY REPORTS.
		WASATCH STAKE.
	The Wasatch Stake Relief Society Con-
ference was held in the Stake Tabernacle,
July 14, 1911.
	Morning Session, 10 a. m.—President
Joannah E. Jensen presiding. After sing-
ing and prayer. A chorus. German sisters
of the Midway Second Ward.
	Sisters Thomas and Edna May Davis
of the General Board were present, also
President Murdock and Jensen, High Coun-
selors; Robert Lindsay and Henry L. Mc-
Mullen and Patriarch Thomas Hicken pres-
ent.
	The general and Stake Relief Society offi-
cers were sustained by a unanimous vote.
Words of welcome from the President,
Joannah E. Jensen. Heber Third Ward re-
ported as being in a good condition, by the
president, Elizabeth Hicken. Midway First
Ward reported as being in an average con-
dition, by first counselor Emily Coleman.
The Stake reported as being in good con-
dition by the second counselor, Sophia Luke.
	Duet, “Love at Home,” Sisters Jenta
Murdock and Gladys Davis of Heber Sec-
ond Ward.
	Mother’s Work Lesson, “Home Influ-
ence,” given by Sister Mary L. Willes,
Heber Third Ward.
	Sister Anna R. Duke spoke on Relief So-
ciety work, trials of the Saints in early
days and the great blessings all are enjoy-
ing at the present time.
	President James C. Jensen spoke on the
duties of Relief Society mothers and the
eternity of the marriage covenant.
	Sister Edna May Davis, one of the new
members of the General Board, said she was
pleased to be with us, always responded to
the call of authority placed over her. We
each have a mission to perform. Be hum-
ble and prayerful. Spoke on duties of daugh-
ters to parents. Pure thoughts. Her wel-
come in our midst and love to all.
	President Joannah Jensen requested the
three Heber Wards, to care for the outside
wards and see that all had dinner. After
singing, “Marching Homeward,” and bene-
diction by Brother Robert Lindsay, session
adjourned.
	Afternoon Session, 2 p. m.—Singing,
“Nay Speak no Ill.” Prayer, Patriarch
Thomas Hicken. Singing, “Come let us
Anew.” Reading, “Call of the Relief So-
ciety,” by Sister Isabell Penrod of Walls-
burg.
	Remarks of encouragement given by
Brother Henry L. McMullen.
	Vocal Solo, “‘Vale of Dreams,” Sister
Jennie Ross of Midway.
	Lecture, “Possibilities of Relief Society
Work,” Sister Annie Stevens.
	Singing, “If there is Sunshine in your
Soul.” Reading, “The Lost Word,” Sister
Lizzie McDonald, of Heber First Ward.
	Sister Carrie S. Thomas of the General
Board said she was pleased to be here.

[Column 2]
Wanted all to appreciate the Relief Society
work and the advantages we enjoy at the
present time. Relief Society work is an all
round education within itself. Teach our
children the history of the Latter-day Saints.
Love and reverence for the pioneers. Home
life and its responsibilities. Be good wives
and mothers, for that is the greatest mis-
sion of woman.
	Duet, “There is Hope Beyond the Vale,”
by Sisters Lettie Jensen and Arbelia Har-
vey, of Center Ward.
	Timely and encouraging remarks by Pres-
ident Murdock. Sister Jensen thanked all
who had helped make the conference a suc-
cess.
	Singing, “Our God we raise to Thee.”
Benediction, Brother Robert Lindsay.
				Carlie C. Tidwell, Sec.
