UTAH WOMEN AT CHICAGO.
	Mrs. Emmeline B. Wells, General Presi-
dent of the Relief Society, Mrs. Amy B. Ly-
man, Assistant General Secretary, and Mrs.
Ardella W. Eardley of the Y. L. M. I. A.,
were in attendance at the executive session
of the National Council of Women held in 
Chicago on the 13th and 14th of Novem-
ber. The Utah ladies received marked at-
tention and were most active both in com-
mittee work and in the deliberations of the
general assembly. The meetings were in
the Auditorium and were attended by rep-
resentative women from local councils and
organizations throughout the land. 
	A memorial service was held in honor of
the late president, Lillian M. Hollister. The
first session was presided over by the first
vice-president, Mrs. Kate Waller Barrett,
who after these exercises was taken violent-
ly ill and not able to preside at any more
of the meetings, much to the sorrow and
regret of those present. However, the cor-
responding secretary, Mrs. Flo Jamison
Miller, carried the work along and presided
most ably during the remainder of the ses-
sion. 
	Following the adjournment of the council
Miss Jane Addams of Hull House enter-
tained the delegates at luncheon and they
were afterwards shown through that insti-
tution and its different departments were
explained to them. In the city of Chicago
the greatest possible interest is shown in the
welfare of children, and every possible 
means is being taken to preserve life and
give proper care and training to the child.


Many excellent resolutions for the better-
ment of women were passed and splendid
work was outlined for the future.  The
Utah visitors had an enjoyable though stren-
uous time, and returned in good health to
again resume their work at home
						A. W. C.
