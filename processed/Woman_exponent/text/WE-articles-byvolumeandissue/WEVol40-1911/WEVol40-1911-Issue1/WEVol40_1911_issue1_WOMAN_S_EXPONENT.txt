	RELIEF SOCIETY REPORTS.
		ONEIDA STAKE.
	The Relief Society Annual Conference of
the Oneida Stake was held at Preston, May
27, 1911. President Louisa B. Bennion pre-
siding. All members of the board were
present. Singing, “Now let us rejoice.”
Prayer, Miller Golightly. Singing—Solo,
“Beyond the gates of Paradise,” Mrs.
Packer.
	An address of welcome was given by
Counselor Lucy D. Parkinson. A verbal
report of the stake was given by Counselor
Hannah Mickleson and denoted the organi-
zations in a good healthy condition, with the
mother’s work accomplishing more than had
ever been known before.
	Lesson, “The Father and Mother in the
Home,” was given by Lucy Cutler.
	A paper on a “Model Home” given by
Ella M. Hull.
	Elder Geo. C. Parkinson addressed the
conference on the subjects of home, our
life’s mission, and the importance of living
up to the professions we make by calling
ourselves Latter-day Saints, explained how
a good clean influence was felt everywhere.
	Sister Mary Hawkes added her testimony
and urged all to grasp the opportunities, and
privileges we all are blessed with.
	Singing, “The time is far spent.”
	Benediction, Esther Rogers.
	Afternoon Session—The Stake Presi-
dency members of High Council were pres-
ent.
	Singing, “High on the Mountain Top.”
	Prayer, Gretta Rollison.
	Singing, “O say what is truth?”
	Roll call denoted 19 branches represented,
160 present.
	President L. B. Benson addressed the
conference, “The mother’s work is doing
better than ever before.” Spoke of the or-
ganization of the Relief Society and in-

[Column 2]
structions given by the Prophet, also the la-
bors of Eliza R. Snow, Zina D. Young and
Bathsheba W. Smith, and the work of the
local workers in the Oneida Stake.
	Duet, “Gently lead us,” Leonard and Ma-
bel Moore.
	An able paper on “How, when and what
to read,” was given by Chloe M. Howell.
Recitation, “The shadow on the blind,” Ire-
ta Rollison.
	Solo, “The dear home songs,” Mrs. Lar-
son.
	Paper, “The benefits derived from moth-
ers’ work,” was given by Rae Hawkes.
	Stake Counselor Taylor Nelson said he
rejoiced in the gospel of truth and indi-
vidual effort, spoke on the joy and bless-
ings that would follow energetic labors in
the same.
	President Jos. Geddes spoke of the light
abroad in the earth, and what it means to all
people.
	Counselor James Johnson endorsed what
had been said and done and encouraged all
to profit by the same.
	Elder J. R. McNiel spoke on duty and
honoring the same.
	Elder John Larson said they were proud
of the Relief Society workers and of the
good they accomplished.
	President L. B. Benson made closing re-
marks.
	Singing, “On the Mountain Top Appear-
ing”
	Benediction, Chas. D. Goaslind.
						Stake Secretary.
