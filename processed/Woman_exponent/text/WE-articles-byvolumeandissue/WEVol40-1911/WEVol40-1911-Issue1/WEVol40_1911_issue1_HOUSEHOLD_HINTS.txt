HOUSEHOLD HINTS.
	At the approach of warm weather the
housewife begins to lay away the heavy
wraps and bedding used in the cold weather.
One of the best means to protect the furs
and woolens from moth is the use of gaso-
line. Dip the whisk broom in the gasoline
and sprinkle it lightly over the clothing be-
fore folding, then if the articles are tightly
wrapped in newspaper and laid in your
boxes or on the closet shelves you need
have no fear of moth, of course the cloth-
ing thus placed away should first be
brushed and aired.
	Fine china plates are sometimes cracked
and broken by placing too many together.
The weight is likely to break the lower
ones in the stack.
	A little salt and gasoline sprinkled on the
velvet or brussels carpet cleans and bright-
ens it wonderfully. But one must always be
careful not to have any fire around when
using gasoline.
	For hoarseness. The juice of a lemon
and two table spoonsful of glycerine mixed
with a liberal quantity of honey will cure
hoarseness and is very good for coughs and
colds.					A. W. C.
