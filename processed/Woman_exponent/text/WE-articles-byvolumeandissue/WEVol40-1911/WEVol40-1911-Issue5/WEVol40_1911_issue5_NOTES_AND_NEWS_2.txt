NOTES AND NEWS.
	“The ballot needs woman more than
woman needs the ballot.” This was the
keynote of the address given by Dr. Roy K.
Flannagan, director of sanitary inspection
for the Virginia State Board of Health, be-
fore the Equal Suffrage League of Nor-
folk, Va., the other day.
	During a recent debate on woman suf-
frage in Hampton, Iowa, the speaker for
the negative, in making the good old “wom-
an-do-not-want-it” argument, asked every
woman in the room who would use the vote
if she had it to rise. To his manifest dis-
comfiture, practically every woman present
instantly sprang to her feet.
	Mrs. Joseph Johnson of London, Eng-
land, declared that women would not throw
their whole hearts into Christian service un-
til they were recognized by men on a com-
mon equality.
	The Ohio Woman Suffrage Association
is soon to open headquarters in Columbus,
at the Chittenden Hotel. Mrs. Rachel Fos-
ter Avery will speak for the Columbus suf-
fragists in January.
	More women’s votes were cast in Los An-
geles at the recent election than the whole
number of votes cast at the last presidential
election in the State of Mississippi. Mis-
sissippi has about five times the population
of Los Angeles.
