IN MEMORIAM.
	We, the sisters of the Granite Stake Relief
Society, have been bereft of our beloved coun-
selor, co-worker and friend, dear Sister Mary
P. Young.
	Her life was an inspiration—a life devoted
to the good of others, to the up-building of
those with whom she labored. She was an ex-
emplification of all that is pure and beautiful
the sunshine of every circle in which she min-
gled. A glorious woman, a noble soul.
	The memory of her sweet life, her deeds of
faithfulness, will ever linger with us.
	Resolved, that we extend our heartfelt sym-
pathy to her bereaved husband and children,
and pray our heavenly Father to bless them,
and comfort their hearts in this hour of afflic-
tion.
	Be it further resolved, that we present a copy
of this memoriam to the bereaved family, that
a copy be recorded in the minutes of our Relief
Society, and that one be sent to the Woman’s
Exponent for publication.
	“Sister, thou wast mild and lovely.
		Gentle as the summer breeze,
	Pleasant as the air of evening,
		When it floats among the trees.
	“Dearest sister, thou hast left us.
		Here thy loss we deeply feel;
	But ’tis God that hath bereft us,
		He can all our sorrows heal.”
				Emma S. Woodruff,
				Lenora T. Harrington,
				Katherine H. Brockbank,
					Stake Presidency R. S.
			OBITUARY.
	Sister Mary Pratt Young departed this life
at Salt Lake City, Oct. 25, 1911. Funeral serv-
ices were held at the Forest Dale chapel on
Sunday, Oct. 29, at 1 p. m., Bishop James Jen-
sen presiding. Appropriate musical selections
were rendered by the choir and Bro. Charles
R. Pike, who sang feelingly, “I know that my
Redeemer lives,” and “The Rosary.”
	Remarks were made by Elders Nephi Jen-
sen, Geo. M. Cannon, President Frank Y. Tay-
lor and others, who testified eloquently to the
love and esteem in which the deceased was
held. The floral offerings were numerous and
beautiful. Interment in city cemetery.
