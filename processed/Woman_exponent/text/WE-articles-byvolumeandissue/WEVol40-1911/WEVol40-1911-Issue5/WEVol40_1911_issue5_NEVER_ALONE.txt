NEVER ALONE
We are never alone among Saints who are
	true,
Who’ve sincerely accepted “the Covenant
	New;”
Relationship tender and near we can claim
Because we have taken upon us His name.
For He, our Redeemer, when here upon earth,
Once was told she would see Him who gave to
	Him birth;
He wondrously answered—Oh, blest be His
	grace!—
Words whose comfort and promise time ne’er
	shall efface:
“He that now doeth my own Father’s will,
The same is my brother, the law to fulfill;
My mother and sisters also are they”—
For the soul’s sure contentment what more
	could he say!
If we keep His sayings He’ll own us and bless;
We’re His sisters and mothers, we cannot be
	less;
Oh, blessed relation to Him, our great King!
With hearts full of gladness His praises we’ll
	sing.
No title e’er given to beings of earth
Could mean the great splendor, the beauty
	and worth,
Of that divine kinship which He thus declared
With those who are worthy for “mansions pre-
	pared.”
Look up, sister, brother, be glad and rejoice,
For He hath made plain by His own precious
	voice,
That we are all kindred—then why feel alone,
Since we hope soon to meet Him and kneel at
	His throne!
					—Mary A. Freeze.
