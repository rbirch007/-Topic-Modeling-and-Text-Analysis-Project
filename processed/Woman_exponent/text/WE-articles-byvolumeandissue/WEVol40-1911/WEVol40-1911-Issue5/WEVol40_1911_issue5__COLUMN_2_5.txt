	Elizabeth J. Davis was devoted to her
husband, home and children, as well as to
the Relief Society of the Twenty-second
ward, in which capacity she labored over
twenty years. The members of the ward
feel in her taking off a personal loss. The
ward meetinghouse was therefore filled to
overflowing at her funeral, and loving trib-
utes were paid to her memory by Bishop
Alfred Solomon and others. A powerful
discourse was delivered by Judge Booth of
Provo. Bishop Alvin Beesley gave a touch-
ing expression of his great appreciation of
the life and works of the deceased in be-
half of the distressed, and urged the mem-
bers of the ward to emulate her generosity
and interest in the feeble, sick, and poor. Mrs.
Alice Merrill Horne represented the Gen-
eral Board of Relief Society and the Twen-
ty-second ward organization and Mrs. Net-
tie D. Bradford spoke for the stake offi-
cers. The music was choice and touching.
Mrs. Nellie Druce Pugsley sang “Ave
Maria” and Mrs. Lizzie Thomas Edward
rendered, “I know that My Redeemer
Lives.” Bishop Alvin Beesley acted as ac-
companist.
	The Relief Society officers had charge
of the funeral services. 
		MARTHA J. WILLEY, Secretary.
