	"	Ursulia Pomeroy, Secretary.
	We hold our meetings once in three
weeks; they are always well attended, and
a good spirit prevails; all the young ladies
take a lively interest in the society. We
select pieces from the EXPONENT and other
good papers and books and read them which
proves to be a great benefit to us all. The
sisters of the Relief Society meet with us
occasionally, also President Rich and Bishop
Budge, they give counsel and advice which
is very encouraging. I really think that
most of the young ladies take more pleas-
ure in attending their Retrenchment meet-
ings than they do when attending balls or
theatres. For the past winter round-danc-
ing has been entirely abandoned in this
place; they had been favorite dances with
the young folks, it was hard for them to give
them up entirely at first; but the knowl-
edge that we were obeying counsel amply
repaid us for any regrets we might have
felt.
	We shall always remember Sister Horne
with respect and gratitude for her kind in-
structions and good advice while here, for
she truly had the interest and welfare of
the young at heart. The EXPONENT has
the best wishes of our society for its suc-
cess.
