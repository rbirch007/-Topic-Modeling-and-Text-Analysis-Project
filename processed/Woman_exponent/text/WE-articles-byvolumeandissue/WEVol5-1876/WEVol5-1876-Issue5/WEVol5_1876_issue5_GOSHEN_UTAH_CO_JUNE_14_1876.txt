GOSHEN, UTAH, CO., June 14, 1876.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Report of the Annual meeting of the
Relief Society on the first of June, in the
School House of this place.
	There were present on the stand Bishop
William Price, the president of the teach-
ers, and other brethren, also the choir led
by Elder James Jenkins.
	The house was lined with quilts and or-
namental work done by the Sisters of the
Society.
	Names of the present officers:
	Mrs. Mary Johnson,	President,
	"	Eleson Gurley,
	"	Mary Dodds,			Counselors.
	" 	Louisa Taylor,		Secretary,
	"	Christina Higginson, Treasurer.
	After the usual exercises and reading of
minutes, the following report of the year
ending May 31st, was read by the Secre-
tary.
	Donations received....................	$140 18
	Disbursed to sick and needy...... 	94 39
	Clothes for Lamanites................	23 88
	Cash invested in Co-op store,
and Provo Factory..............................	150 55
	Cash in Treasury........................	51 00
	Property on hand, consisting
of quilts, socks, burial clothes
and other articles................................ 	110 25
	Our meeting was excellent and a good
spirit prevailed, there was a large attend-
ance. President Mary Johnson remarked,
she was pleased to see the willingness of
the sisters to sustain HOME INDUSTRIES.
Prayed the Lord to bless each member of
the society that they might have a spirit of
oneness; she desired the bishop and breth-
ren present to give their views regarding
the house we anticipate building this year.
	Bishop Price spoke very encouragingly;
thought the members of the society were
making every effort to aid the sick and
needy; was pleased to see their determin-
ation to have a house of their own, said it
would not only prove a comfort to use, but
the Lord would also bless us in our under-
taking. Said the Sisters were a great help
to the Priesthood. He also spoke of the in-
fluence of WOMAN for good, and he felt to
bless the society and all its members.
