EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I wish to inform you of the condition of
affairs touching the progress of our Society.
I have had opportunities not only in public
but in private to familiarize myself with
the spiritual condition of its members, and
I must confess that the evidences are many
that their hands and hearts are in the great
work of reformation, manifesting on all oc-
casions a disposition and willingness to up-
hold and sustain by kind words and com-
mendable deeds, our highly esteemed and
worthy President Sister Margarette Ran-
dall whose untiring efforts to promote not
only the interests of the Society but the
general good of the kingdom of God, are
too well known to need comment. Suffice
it to say that at our annual meeting of re-
cent date, and largely attended, a spirit of
devotion to the cause which we have es-
poused seemed to pervade the assembly;
and some able, timely, and interesting re-
marks by Brother N. T. Porter added new
fuel to the spiritual conflagration already
kindled in the souls of all convened.
	With these hasty remarks, still praying
for the present and ultimate success of your
valuable paper, and the general good of
humanity; I subscribe myself your Sister
in the Gospel.
	CAROLINE DALRYMPLE.
		Centreville, July 1st, 1875.
