EDITOR EXPONENT:
	After spending many days at the great Cen-
tenial Exhibition in Philadelphia, of course
admiring the dif[--]rent specimens of the inven-


tive genius of ages, I was much surprised on
visining the Centennial Exhibition on Main
St. Salt Lake City, to find very many articles
comparing favorably with the best specimens
on exhibiiion at Philadelphia, though formed
out of the elements here in Utah. I have not
time to describe one of a hundred of these
truly fine and finished specimens of Utah in-
dustry, but would recommend any one doubt-
ing the comparison to go and see.
						W. O. CLARK.
	Ogden, July 17, 1876.
	"THE LIFE of Brigham Young, or Utah and
Her Founders," by E. W. Tullidge, is now com-
pleted and it is expected it will be for sale this
present month. We have had the pleasure
(through the courtesy of the author) to peruse
many of the proof sheets, and we believe it
to be a work that will counteract much of the 
prejudice now existing in regard to this peo-
ple. "One story is good, until another is told"
is an old adage, and Mr. Tullidge: has plainly
delineated the other side of the dark represen-
tations now extant concerning this people.
To those who really want to know the truth
we would recommend its perusal.
