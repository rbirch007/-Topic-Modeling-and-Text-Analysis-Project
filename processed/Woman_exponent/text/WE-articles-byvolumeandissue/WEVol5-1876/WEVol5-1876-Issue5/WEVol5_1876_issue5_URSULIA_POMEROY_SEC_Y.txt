	URSULIA POMEROY, Sec'y.

	Eighth Annual Meeting of Sixteenth
Ward Relief Society.
	Sixteenth Ward School Rooms, S. L.
City, June 15, 1876. Meeting opened by
singing Arise O glorious Zion—Prayer by
Bishop F. G. Kesler. Singing, The Spirit
of God—Minutes of the previous meeting
read and accepted. Annual report as fol-
lows; beginning June 15, 1875, ending June
15th 1876:
Cash received............................. $ 106 75
Mdse. and produce.....................	69 75
Disbursements to poor, cash......	61 75
Mdse. and produce.....................	57 25
	Many good instructions were given and
Dianna Reid was ordained President of the
Society as we had been called to part with
our former President by death. Benedic-
tion by Geo. C. Riser.
	The ladies of the Relief Society had pre-
pared a splendid supper, and the young
ladies of the Retrenchment Association
waited on the tables; over one hundred par-
took of the supper, and it was indeed a
feast of spiritual as well as temporal food.
	Donated by the Society commencing June
15, 1868, ending June 15, 1876, $2000,47,-
95.
	PRESENDIA L. KIMBALL.
		Sec'y.
	Since our Annual Meeting we have had
a Widow's Party at the Sixteenth Ward
School Rooms; we gathered all the widows
in the ward and had a pleasant interview.
Bishop Kesler addressed us and we would
his words were written, for they were in-
deed like jewels. After we had partaken of
refreshments and all had been bountifully
supplied we scattered our picnic among the
poor of the ward, there were many baskets
full and it was a day of rejoicing for all
We were indeed much blessed in coming
together, and felt we were mutually bene-
fitted by each other's society.
