WOMAN'S RECORD.

	A BOOK entitled "Woman Created an
Image of God," is now being published by
Miss Aasta Hausteen, who is an enthusias-
tic worker for woman's rights in Norway.
She is a daughter of the celebrated Prof.
Hausteen.
	MRS. Emma Malloy of Indiana deliver-
ed an eloquent and spirited address to a
numerous assembly of people in New Era
Hall, 176 Tremont Street, Boston on July
4th, at the convention of the Suffolk County
Good Templars.
	HARRIET Martineau, an English writer
of great celebrity and whose works have
had an extensive circulation in America,
died at her own home in England, the first
week in July last, at the age of seventy-
four. She was a powerful writer, and was
often spoken of as masculine in her vigor
of intellect, and clearness of perception.
She was in the strictest sense of the word
a woman of superior mind and attainments.
	MRS. M. A. Ford, an author and poet,
who died recently, has been spoken of in
terms of the highest praise, as a woman
possessing many loveable and aimable
qualities and attractions. She was one who
labored assiduously for the elevation of her
sex. She wrote under the "nom de plume of
"Una," and has been highly complimented
by Longfellow, who is by most people "con-
sidered an authority.
