R. S. REPORTS.

	The meeting of the Senior and Junior
Retrenchment Association, held in the Four-
teenth Ward Assembly Rooms, Saturday,
July 22nd, was one of increased interest,
and plainly indicated the zeal and enthusi-
asm of the sisters in promoting the HOME
INDUSTRIES.
	Counselor Mrs. Phebe Woodruff presided,
President Mrs. M. I. Horne not being able
to attend on account of ill-health. After
the usual opening exercises and reading of
minutes of the young secretaries from the
several wards represented, Counselor Zina
D. Young gave a synopsis of her journey
recently taken into the country, reported
the success of the silk culture in the places
she had visited, and the energy and mag-
nanimity manifested by the sisters to pro-
gress and develop in all the different depart-
ments of home manufacture. Counselors
Mrs. E. Howard and Mrs. P. Woodruff
spoke with considerable power and earnest-
ness, exhorting the sisters to use great wis-
dom in all their movements. Mrs. E. S.
Barney bore a strong testimony to the gos-
pel, and several other sisters arose and ex-
pressed their views and feelings. All was
in perfect order and harmony.
