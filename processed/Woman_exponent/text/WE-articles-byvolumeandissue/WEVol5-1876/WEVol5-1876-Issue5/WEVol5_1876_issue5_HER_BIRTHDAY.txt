		HER BIRTHDAY.

	Again your natal day awakes my lyre,
		And bids me sing
	A song of genuine friendship unto thee,
		Which here I bring.

	The harp within my heart of thousand strings
		Is tuned for thee!
	The dispason clear and full rings out
		To you from me.

	How many years have fled since first we met,
		In thrilling scenes!
	And memory indellby has left
		Her waking dreams!

	The vision yet in all these years fades not,
		I see as clear
	All that then I saw—all I then beheld.
		As tho' 'twere near.

	Since then the sea of life has o'er us rolled,
		Wave upon wave;
	But through God's help we've stemmed the rolling tide,
		HE stood to save.

	Yes, let it go—the stream of life flows on
		With noiseless roll,
	And day by day, and night by night, we do approach
		The final goal!

	But from the subject that I had in hand
		I have digressed—
	To wish you all that's good on this bright day,
		To wish yon blessed

	With all your heart desires, is what I wish;
		I full well know 
	You nothing crave but that a Saint may ask
		Or God bestow.

	Then be you blessed my sister ever kind,
		And with pure health;
	For health of body, and a mind that's sound;
		Is untold wealth.

	And if some wish above all others dwells within
		That heart of thine,
	Then may kind Heaven that boon on you bestow.
		So prays my rhyme.
