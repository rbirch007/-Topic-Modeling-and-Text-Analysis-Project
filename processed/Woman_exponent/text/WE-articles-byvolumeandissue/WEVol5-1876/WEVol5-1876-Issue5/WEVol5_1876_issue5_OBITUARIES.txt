OBITUARIES.

				TOOELE CITY, July 27th, 1876.
	SISTER REBECCA DEW, wife of Elder Thomas
Dew, departed this life the 24th of June, 1876.
She was born Feb. 15th, 1812, in Wiltshire,
England, was first baptized in 1843, emigrated
to Nauvoo in the spring of 1844, and was driv
en out of Nauvoo with the rest of the Saints in
1846; she was faithful and patient in all
the trials she was called to pass through.
In 1852 she removed to Tooele, where she re-
sided until her death. She suffered many years
with a cancer in her breast, and for the last ten
months was confined more or less to her room.
She lived and died in full faith of the Gospel.
Sister Drew was second counselor in our Relief
Society, and rejoiced very much in the society
of the Saints and the principles of the gospel.
She was always on hand in every duty or en-
terprise necessary for the building up of the
kingdom of God. A large procession of the
members of the Relief Society followed the re-
mains to the cemetery. and in accordance with
her request, that beautiful hymn of Miss E. R.
Snow's' "Oh My Father," was sung over the
grave, and she was laid away in peace to await
the morning of the resurrection.

HARRIET McEVER, born on the Isle of Mott,
Lake Champlain, State of Vermont, Sept. 3d,
1809; was married in 1828 to Horace Roberts in
the State of Illinois. Was baptized into the
Church of Latter-day Saints in 1838 with her
husband. They joined the Saints at Nauvoo,
1839, where they lived until their expulsion.
She came with them through all their subse-
quent journeyings. She came to Utah in 1850,
and settled in Provo, was one of the first set-
tlers. She passed through many trials. Indian
wars, grasshopper wars, and all the privations
attendant on the early settlement of this
country.	Her husband died Dec. 25, 1858, af-
ter an illness of nearly three years. She rais-
ed nine children, six of whom are now living.
	She died Feb. 27th, 1876, after an illness of
fourteen months, with tumor in the stomach.
	She was a woman of iutelligence and good
judgment, loved and respected by all who
knew her, a kind friend and faithful wife and
mother, charitable and upright in all her in-


tercourse, ever ready to minister to the sick,
or comfort the sorrowing. She was humble in
prosperity and uncomplaining in adversity.
She died in full faith of the Gospel and in
hopes of a glorious resurrection.
							M. J. T.
	Provo, July 11th, 1876.

	AT OXFORD, Oneida Co., Idaho, Julia,
daughter of Martha Eliza, and Benjamin
Boice, died May 11th, 1876, of consumption,
aged 16 years, 1 month, and two days. Her
sister, Alnora Eliza, died June 11th, 1876, of
the same disease, aged 8 years and 28 days.
	Their mother died of consumption three
years prior to their death.
