GENERAL.
	Georgia has forty cotton mills running
full time and paying handsome dividends
........Jersey Day at the Centennial was cele-
brated by twenty thousand citizens of New
Jersey........Fish of all kinds are scarce in
the East, and bring a very high price........
Large forest fires are reported as having
raged fiercely in several places in the States
.........The Boston Museum opened its 34th
regular dramatic season Monday evening,
Aug. 28th, with Victorian Sardou's play
entitled "Ferreol".......Boston Theatre, on
the same evening as the Museum, opened
with "The Streets of New York," Frank
Mayo personating Tom Badger........The
Globe has just opened with the drama
"Pique." This play run last year eight
consecutive months in New York at Daly's
Fifth Avenue Theatre.......The small pox has
been raging in Chili, attended with great
loss of life.......The standard of the prophet
was raised recently in Turkey; the emblem
is black, and is only displayed in times of
great peril........The World's Homeopathic
Convention, recently held in Philadelphia,
will assemble again for the same purpose in
1881........Normal schools have become ex-
ceedingly popular in Canada........Prepara-
tions are being made for building a hand-
some granite vault over the remains of
Wm. B. Astor........Musical promenades were
very popular in London the past summer
......They talk now of keeping the Centen-
nial Exposition open until December......
Gilmore's Band will begin to play in New
England in December......The Musical Fes-
tival at Philadelphia will begin Sept. 20.
