HYGIENIC NOTES.

	For Prickly Heat:—One oz. cream of tartar and
one oz. flour of sulphur, mixed in half a tumbler
of water; take a teaspoonful three times a day, use
vegetable diet and cold or tepid bathing.
	For Poison:—A safe and simple remedy, if one
has swallowed poison, is a heaping teaspoonful of
common salt and the same of ground mustard
stirred well together in a teacupful of water and
swallowed quickly. It will immediately cause
vomiting, and lest any remnants of the poison re-
main in the stomach after the vomiting, beat up
the white of an egg with sweet oil, or butter or
lard, several spoonfuls, and take it; these com-
com articles will counteract many virulent poisons.
	The Wet Sheet Pack:—The advantages of this
r[-]medy in sickness and ill-health and especially in
colds, is incalculable, and as there are perhaps
some of our readers who are unacquainted with its
use, we will give them the best method in a few
words: First put the blankets on the bed, then
take all clothing off the patient and wrap him or


her in a wet sheet, covering the body closely with
blankets; if the feet are very cold a warm brick
or bottle of hot water can be used; bathe the head
of the patient while in the pack with cold water
if there is fever or heat in it, and give them cold
water to drink or some warm herb tea if preferred;
the patient should remain in the pack from half an
hour to two hours, or until there is a sense of heat
or oppression. After the pack is over the patient
should be quickly washed off by sitting in a tub
while water, either cold or tepid, is poured over
the body, or if the patient is not strong, rub off
vigorously with a dry towel or with liquor. It is
better and safer than any kind of drugs or medi-
cine, and will very often prevent the need of send-
ing for a doctor.
