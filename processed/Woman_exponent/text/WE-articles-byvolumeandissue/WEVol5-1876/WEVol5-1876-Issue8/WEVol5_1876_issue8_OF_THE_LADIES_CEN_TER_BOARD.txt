	OF THE LADIES' CEN. TER. BOARD.

	The Directors of the Ladies' Centennial
Territorial Fair which was opened on the
Fourth of July and closed on the 2nd of Sep-
tember, before relinquishing their responsibili-
ties, avail themselves of this opportunity to
express their sincere thanks to all those who
either furnished specimens for the Fair, or in
other ways assisted in the enterprise.
	The interest manifested by those who have
visited the Fair has been very gratifying, and
constitutes the most desirable PRESENT success.
We have taken pride in exhibiting from day
to day the beautiful articles of taste and util-
ity, (many of them produced entirely from the
elements of Utah,) manufactured by the skill
and industry of the people of our Territory,
and humbly trust that our united efforts in
our Centennial Exhibition will prove instru-
mental in stimulating to greater energies and
be productive of much good in encouraging
home-industries.
	Through the gratuity of Pres. Young and
the generosity of the City Fathers, we are en-
abled to meet our heavy expenses incurred
through the condition in which we found the
Fair Building, occasioned by the late terrible
explosion, but after satisfying all demands,
shall have a small surplus, which will be de-
voted to some charitable purpose.
	On our own part we have spared no pains,
and have cheerfully devoted our time, efforts,
and labors gratuitously, feeling satisfied that
they were in the right direction. The only
financial success we have had in view, from
the commencement, beyond meeting our ex-
penses, is in prospective, when through the ex-
tension and general establishment of Home-
Industries, we shall as a people, become self-
sustaining.
	In conclusion we wish to be understood that
all articles of exhibition which have not been
taken away, will be safely deposited in the
Lion House, where they will remain until
called for.
				ELIZA R. SNOW, Pres
				LOUISA A. ASHBY, Sec.
	S. L. City, Sep. 9th, 1876.


For the Exponent.
