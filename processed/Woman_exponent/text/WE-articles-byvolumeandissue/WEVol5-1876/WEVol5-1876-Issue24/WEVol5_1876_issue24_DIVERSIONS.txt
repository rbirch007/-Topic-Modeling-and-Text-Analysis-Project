	BOOK-KEEPING, said an old lady, is not a
sedative employment, because, she added
thoughtfully, they must get so much exer-

	A PHYSICIAN wrote a prescription and
as the patient left the room said to him, "I
wish you would let me know if it does you
any good, for I have been troubled with
rheumatism myself recently."

	A Spaniard and an American dining to-
gether, the former passing a dish of brains
to the latter said, "What you lack." The 
American offering the spaniard a plate of
tongue answered "What you have a surplus
of."

	THERE are two things people in this age
need to learn--When to say nothing, and
when anything is said  say it well. Bless-
ed is the man or woman who knows enough
to be silent.
