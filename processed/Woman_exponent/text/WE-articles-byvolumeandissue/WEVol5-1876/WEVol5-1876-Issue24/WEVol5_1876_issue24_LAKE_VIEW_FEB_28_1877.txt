			LAKE VIEW, Feb. 28, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I send you a few words from our little
place, to let you know how we are getting
along with our meetings. We have com-
bined the Young Men's Meetings and our
Retrenchment Meetings together and the
young men take charge of the meetings
one Sunday night and the young ladies the
next; we all enjoy the spirit of the Lord,
all get up and bear a strong testimony to
the work of God, and the holy spirit rests
upon us in our meetings, and we all take
an active part in making them interesting
and we know that it is our duty to help
bear off the kingdom of God. How proud
our parents would be to have their sons and
daughters grow up to be mighty men and
women in the church of Jesus Christ; and
if we do not improve ourselves while we
have a chance we will have to endure the
consequences, for we will never have the
opportunity to come back and retrace our
footsteps, either before or after we pass
away from this earth. I never felt more
like doing good than I do at the present
time, and we trust that all the young men
and women may persevere in the good
cause.
