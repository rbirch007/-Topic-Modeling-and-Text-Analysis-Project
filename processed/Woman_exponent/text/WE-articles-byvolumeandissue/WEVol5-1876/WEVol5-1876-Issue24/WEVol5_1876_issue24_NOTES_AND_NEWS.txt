NOTES AND NEWS.

	VASSAR COLLEGE is doing a good work
for future generations by giving opportuni-
ties to women of obtaining not only a high-
er education for themselves but these wo-
men will bequeath to posterity the impress
of their work.
	A MONTHLY magazine is published in
London called the English Woman's Re-
view, which is devoted to social and indus-
trial questions. The "Woman's Journal"
says, it is a perfect mine of facts and infor-
mations on the Woman question.
	THE FRENCH Societi du Deoit des Fum-
mes" held an interesting meeting lately in
Paris in the Salle Lax, which was presided
over by M. Armard Duportal, Deputy for
Toulouse; supported by M. Brisson, mem-
bers of the Municipal Counsel of Paris.
One of the questions brought before the
meeting and upon which papers were read
and speeches were made was "la recherche


de la paterniti." The French law forbids
every attempt to make known the paterni-
ty of illegitimate children. Statistics were
presented showing the number of these
fatherless children, who either die in the
first months of their existence, or live to
people the prisons and galleys.—Ex.
	It seems fully time that women were per-
mitted to speak in their own defense where
such cruelty and oppression exists to
wards the unfortunate. If the women of
America obtain Universal Suffrage it is
probable they may in many ways assist wo-
men of other countries in the protection of
their liberties and rights. Would such laws
as these exist if woman was allowed a voice
in the matter? No! the love of offspring is
too strong with woman, and she bears too
much the impress of divinity in her na-
ture.
