NOTES AND NEWS.

	ON THE 28th of February, Prof. Mercy B.
Jackson delivered the valedictory address
to the students of Boston University School
of Medicine, in Tremont Temple.
	THE FREE Dormitory for women in New
York, is said to be besieged nightly by
homeless women applying for shelter. Only
destitute women of respectability are ad-
mitted. The general police captains of the
city have been furnished with tickets good
for one night's lodging, and these are dis-
tributed among the poor women whom they
deem worthy of relief.—Ex.
	And yet these are the people who talk so
much about the terrible condition of affairs
in Utah, and especially in Salt Lake City.
In that very city there are hundreds of For-
eign Missionary Boards and so forth for the
benefit of the heathen, etc. Truly ought
Charity to begin at home. In conversation
with strangers from New York, Chicago
and other large cities, we have always tak-
en especial pains to make reference to the
thorough systematic management of the
Relief Societies organized among this peo-
ple for the benefit of the sick, the aged,


and the poor, and of the intention to pro-
vide work for all who are not incapacitat-
ed for labor, instead of giving without com-
pensation. The home-industries which
this people are laboring to establish, will
eventually furnish occupation for the labor-
ers of both sexes.
