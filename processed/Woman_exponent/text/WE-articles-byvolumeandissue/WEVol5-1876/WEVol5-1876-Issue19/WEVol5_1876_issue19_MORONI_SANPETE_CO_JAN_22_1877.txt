	MORONI, SANPETE Co., Jan. 22, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I wish to add my testimony with that of
others in this good work that we as a band
of sisters in Utah are engaged in, for the
building up of the kingdom of our Father
in this last dispensation. I know the Lord
has revealed His will again to the human
family, to all that will obey His Gospel. We
the Sisters of Moroni feel to respond to the
counsel of our prophet, and feel thankful
that we have one who is able to warn us of
what is coming to pass. Sisters I know
what it is to be without a leader, I realized
it in Nauvoo for a short season, when our
prophet was slain in Carthage Jail; I was
an eye witness to those terrible scenes at
Nauvoo. O sisters, what a time that was
to look upon. Then we had to seek a home
in the mountains, where nobody else would
live, but thank God it was prepared for His
people. I rejoice that I have a home with
them, although I stand almost alone, only
my children with me, my husband died
when I came out from Nauvoo in 1847 but
I thank God that I am here if I am alone.
Well sisters let us do what we can in the
Grain Movement. The sisters of the Mo-
roni Relief Society are doing what they can
this year, in that direction, and if the Lord
assists and blesses us with another good
crop we will feel exceedingly thankful.
We have made a good beginning and our
Bishop is very kind and has given us a
place to store it in the Tithing Office until
we can build, and the brethren will, I hope,
help us, according to President Young's
suggestions. We feel strong as a band of
women, and hold our meetings once in two
weeks, a good spirit prevails and all seem
alive to their duties. It has been my lot to
do hard work more than writing, all my
life.
	I hope the Lord will add His blessing un
to us all; and may prosperity attend Israel,
is my prayer in the name of Jesus Amen.
