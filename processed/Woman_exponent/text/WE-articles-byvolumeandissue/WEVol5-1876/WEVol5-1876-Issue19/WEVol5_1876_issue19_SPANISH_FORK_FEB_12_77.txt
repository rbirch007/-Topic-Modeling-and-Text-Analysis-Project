	SPANISH FORK, Feb. 12, 77.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I have watched and read with interest
the various reports of the Relief Societies,
concerning the storing of Grain; and it gives
me much pleasure to see and hear of the
success which has attended their efforts
while endeavoring to carry out the counsel
of our beloved president and father; the
success which has attended the sisters in
their laudable enterprise, is consequent, no
doubt upon their earnestness in the work,
and their faith in the Gospel and in the
counsel given; being fully assured from a
long experience, that in following and car-
rying out the counsels of the Servants of
God from time, to time, the people have
been exceedingly blessed, both spiritually
and temporally. When I read the counsel
conveyed to me through the pages of our
bright luminary the WOMAN'S EXPONENT,
I did truly rejoice, and when it was read
and proposed that we begin to lay up
grain, it was acccepted and received with
joy and thankfulness by the sisters of our
Society, and we felt to thank the Lord for
the inspiration that prompted this counsel,
knowing assuredly that famine and pesti-
lence will desolate the land, and that as
Joseph was sold and banished from his
home and kindred to bring about the pur-
poses of God in saving his brethren and
kindred, so have the Latter-day Saints, even
the seed and royal blood of Joseph, been
banished from their homes, friends and in-
heritances to work out the divine purposes
of God, who maketh even the wrath of
men to praise Him; and as Joseph filled the
granaries of Egypt, so may we fill the
granaries of Utah; thereby proving that we
are noble daughters of a noble Sire.
	We had lumber on hand that we received
for quilts and socks, and have had a bin
made, in which we have over one hundred
bushels of wheat, and still keep adding, and
intend to lay up all we can. Our Society
is in a prosperous condition, and whether

we meet to work, or to exhort each other,
the spirit of the Lord is with us, and the
spirit of love and unity abounds; and es-
pecially is this spirit manifest in the Teach-
ers; at our meetings they bring in good re-
ports, no contentions or backbiting; and as
a general thing all feel well and desire the
prosperity of the Society.
	Ever praying for the welfare of Zion, I
am Yours,
