			R. S. REPORTS.

	The General Retrenchment Meeting held
in the Fourteenth Ward Assembly Rooms,
Saturday afternoon, Feb. 17. was one of in-
tense interest. The Minutes of the Junior
Associations in the different wards were par-
ticularly interesting and showed the next
improvement in the young ladies. Presi-
dent Mrs. M. I. Horne expressed herself
pleased to see so large an assembly, and
said that if the numbers continued to in-
crease that it would be necessary soon to
adjourn to a larger building. She made
many other appropriate remarks on the sub-
ject of Storing Grain; spoke of the signs of
the times—of wars and rumors of wars, and
Babylon's fall, and said this people were
well aware that if Babylon fell now and the
judgments came, they were not prepared to
sustain themselves. Mrs. Bishop, Mrs. Dy-
er and others bore faithful testimonies to
the truth of the gospel. Mrs. E. S. Barney
spoke particularly on the necessity of rais-
ing mulberry trees. Mrs. Clara J. Young
testified of her enjoyment in trying to live
in the United Order, and earnestly entreated
the Latter-day Saints to prepare their minds
for it, that they might avoid the failures
that had been experienced. Said that her
interests and sympathies were with this
people, and not with the outside world.
Counselor Miss E. R. Snow spoke expressly
upon the subject of home industries; said one
great aim was to supply work for those who
desired to labor, and also to be able to make
the entire community self-sustaining. Said
that the time seemed too short for anything
aside from the absolute duties. By request
of the President, Mrs. Horne, Bro. Wm.
Smith made a few remarks on subjects con-
nected with woman's work, and woman's
influence, and the progress he had noticed
since he last met with them. He gave a
powerful testimony on the subject of heal-
ing.
