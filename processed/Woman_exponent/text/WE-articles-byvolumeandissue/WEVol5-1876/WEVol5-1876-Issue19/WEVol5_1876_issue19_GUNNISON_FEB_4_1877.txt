	GUNNISON, Feb. 4, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	According to the instructions through the
EXPONENT, given by counsel of President
B. Young, the Relief Society of this place
has commenced to gather grain. By the
help of the Lord and our brethren, we have
at this date got three hundred and forty-
four bushels of wheat in our bin, to which
we anticipate adding weekly, monthly and
yearly, as long as the Giver of all good per-
mits plenty to abound. The brethren as
well as the sisters are wide awake to the
importance of the Grain Movement.
	Our Relief Society contemplates building
a hall and granary, to the erection of which
many of our husbands, sons and brothers
have already kindly and encouragingly of-
fered their aid; and the feeling is prevalent
that the grain gathered, and yet to be gath-
ered, is sacred to the Lord's purposes, and
can only be disbursed when the Lord's own
time comes.
	We feel, that while our husbands are
toiling yearly to raise the "staff" and nec-
essaries of life, it is but consistent, that we
should be as frugal and industrious as possi-
ble; that, far from being encumbrances, we
may be a source of genuine comfort and
strength unto them.
	The EXPONENT is ever welcome, and
ever replete with what we want.
