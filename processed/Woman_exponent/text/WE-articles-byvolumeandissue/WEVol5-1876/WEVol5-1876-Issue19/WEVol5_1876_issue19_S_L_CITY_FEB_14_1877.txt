			S. L. CITY, Feb. 14, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The Eighth Ward Relief Society held
their Annual meeting on the first of Feb.,
instead of Jan. the usual time, on account
of the illness of the president; There were
present at the meeting, President Mrs. Lea-
ver, and her Counselor, Mrs. Sarah Need-
ham; also Bishop Sheets, and Counselors
Brockbank and Royal B. Young. The
Bishop said we had done considerable for
the poor this year; that our receipts and dis-
bursements showed that means had been
liberally donated and disbursed to assist
those who were sick and needy, which he
was pleased to see. Said that as we had
commenced storing grain he hoped that we
would continue to persevere; he believed
that the sisters would accomplish what the
brethren had failed to do in storing grain.
Bro. Brockbank made some very excellent
remarks on "Woman's Sphere" observed
that much had been said on that question,
that it was truly a great mission she had to
perform and that great responsibilities rest-
ed upon women as wives and mothers.
Bro. R. B. Young made a few remarks; said
he was pleased to see the Relief Society do-
ing so much good, felt to sustain them;
thought there seemed to be a lively interest
taken by the sisters of the Society to
do their duty in whatever sphere they were
called to act. The sisters of our society
feel to respond to all the requirements of
those in authority over them, and to carry
out the counsel of President Young in the
Storing of Grain and in the building of
Temples; and feel willing to do all they can
in their humble way to build up the king-
dom of God upon the earth.
