OBITUARIES.

	DIED IN Spanish Fork, Jan. 28, 1877, after
a long and painful illness, Mrs. Jemima Jex,
wife of William Jex. She lived and died a
true Latter-day Saint.
	Died, in the Sugar House Ward, Jan. 13th,
1877, of puerperal, after an illness of ten days,
Martha Ann, beloved wife of Bishop Wm, C.
A. Smoot, daughter of Perriguene and Julia
Sessions, born Sept. 20, 1834. The deceased
came to the valley in 1847, and was married to
Wm. C. A. Smoot January 29, 1852, by Prest.
B. Young.
	She was a loving, kind and faithful wife and
mother, devoting her whole life to the welfare
of her family. Her worth cannot be too high-
ly appreciated, and her early death is deeply
mourned by her loving, bereaved husband,
children and friends, who know her worth.
	She was the mother of fourteen children—
five sons and nine daughters—three have gone
before her; the youngest, a daughter, fourteen
days old at her death, the eldest, Elder Wm.
C. A. Smoot, is on a mission to England.
	She lived and died a full believer in the Gos-
pel of Jesus Christ; attended to all the ordi-
nances which will insure her Eternal life in
the presence of her Father in Heaven, and in
the Morning of the Resurrection, when the
faithful Saints of God shall be brought forth
from their graves, clothed with immortality,
we will meet our beloved and enjoy her society
through an endless eternity.
	"She is not dead, but lives in Christ, the Res-
urrection."	M. T. S.
	Provo, Feb. 23, 1877.
