			GRANTSVILLE, Feb. 21, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENTS:
	The sisters of Grantsville are trying to
obey the counsel of President Young in re-
gard to storing grain. They are also trying
to retrench from everything that is likely
to injure them. The Relief and Retrench-
ment Societies are doing well. The former
has over two hundred bushels of wheat
stored away, besides being determined to
help their husbands keep all they can in
their bins at home by doing without every-
thing in the way of useless finery. In our
sewing meetings we always introduce sub-
jects of conversation pertaining to matters
of interest to us as a body of people, and
no gossip is ever allowed or introduced.
	The dancing parties out here are closed
at ten o'clock in the evening; we mothers
think this an excellent arrangement as it
will save the health of our young girls, and
very likely in many instances their lives.
	There is also a Young Men's Mutual Im-
provement Society here. This last effort
to awake our youth to a sense of their
importance in these last days, for they must
be the ones to carry off the responsibilities
of this church and kingdom, receive the
earnest prayers of every thinking mother.
	Our City Fathers are trying with all their
might to keep LIQUOR STORES out of
Grantsville, this of course receives our
hearty approval. We are all trying to do
as much good as we can, not forgetting the
Temple by any means.
	Still desiring to improve, we remain
your sisters in the Relief Society,
	Mary A. Hunter, President,
	Jane Eastham,	}	Counselors.
	Sarah Hale,		}
					M. A. HOUSE, Sec'y.
