				HUNTSVILLE, WEBER, Co.
								Feb. 13, '77.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I take pleasure in writing a few lines to
you as a member of the Relief Society of

[Column 2]
Huntsville, which town is located some
twelve miles east of Ogden. It is a flour-
ishing little town with a goodly number of
inhabitants mostly from Denmark and
Sweden. Our leading men are ever alive
to the interests of Zion as also our Relief
Society. Our worthy President Mrs. M. J.
Hammond and Counselors, and sisters in
general are doing all they can in storing
up grain for the time of need. The pro-
ceeds of our last working meeting will be
turned into sheep and grain. We think it
wise to provide working material for our
elderly sisters, as many of them can spin
and knit and are very willing to assist in
whatever way they can.
	The Young Ladies' Retrenchment Society
are doing all they can in the way of making
quilts, and braiding straw, and ever mani-
fest a willingness to assist in the good
cause.
	Your interesting paper is always appre-
ciated, and hoping that it may continue to
instruct, and cheer the hearts of the sisters
is the prayer of your sister,
