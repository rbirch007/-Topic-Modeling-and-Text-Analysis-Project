	PORTERVILLE, MORGAN Co.,
		Jan. 17, '77.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I take the privilege of conveying a few
lines to tell you how we are getting along
in this little branch. There is a good Re-
lief Society here and we are striving with
our amiable president and her counselors to
do what good we can in carrying out the
counsel given us and trying to be one with
the other societies throughout the Territory.
	In our last meeting the Grain Movement
came into consideration, and the sisters ex-
pressed their willingness to do all in their
power to secure and store away grain. We
have accepted a bin, kindly offered us by
Brother Sanford Porter, and sincerely hope
we will soon see it filled. Two of the sisters
visited around last week, and quite a num-
ber of bushels of grain were donated a foun-
dation to build upon.
	We also have a Young Ladies' Retrench-
ment Association. We are progressing
slowly, and feel that a little instruction
through your valuable paper will be kindly
accepted. We feel that our work in Re-
trenchment must be otherwise directed
than in dress, for our ball-room and Sunday
dresses are manufactured at home, that is,
generally speaking.
	Yours Truly,
