EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Our Retrenchment organization has been
called to part with one of its brightest orna-
ments, by the death of our well beloved Secre-
tary, Sister Martha Ann Murray. She was the
daughter of John and Martha Baugh of this
place. Deceased was born in Salt Lake City,
January 22, 1853; her parents moving with her
to Wellsville in 1860, where she resided up to
the time of her death, which occurred on
Wednesday Jan. 24th, 1877. She was a most
exemplary young woman, a true Latter-day
Saint, an obedient daughter, an affectionate
sister, a valuable friend, a faithful wife, and
a useful member of society. For many
years our young sister held the position of
school teacher in our midst, in both day and
Sunday school, her talents and aimable bear-
ing rendering her an object of love to all who
came in contact with her. She was also a
prominent member of the Wellsville choir for
several years, and was elected Secretary of our
Retrenchment Society at its organization,
both organizations sustaining a severe loss at
her removal to a more glorious sphere of ac-
tion. Sister Murray was married Jan. 27th,
1877.
	The whole people of Wellsville followed the
corse to its last resting place and consigned it
to mother earth with many tears and regrets.
Her troubles were short on this earth, may her
happiness be eternal. A singular coincidence
in connection with the birth, marriage and
death of sister Martha, is that they all occur-
red, in the month of January.
	M. E. W.
