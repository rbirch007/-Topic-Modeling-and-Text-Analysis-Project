HYRUM, CACHE Co., Aug. 17, 1876.
Editor Exponent:
	The members of our society are still alive
to the interests of the kingdom and al-
ways ready to lend a helping hand to the
poor and the needy, and to give a word of
comfort to the downcast. We held our 8th
Annual meeting on the 7th inst. There
were present on the stand several of the
brethren of this place and Sister Maughan of
Logan. Our report was laid before the
meeting which showed we had done many
good deeds in assisting the poor, donating
to the emigration, missionaries and Temple.
The brethren gave very good instructions
and encouraged us to faithfulness and per-
severance.
	Sister Maughan addressed the meeting a
short time, said our balance sheet was a
great credit to us; exhorted us to continue
in well doing. President Christina Liljen-
quist felt thankful for the good instructions
we had received, and asked the blessings
of the Lord upon the Saints. We had a
good time and the Spirit of the Lord was
with us; all seemed to feel to renew their
exertios to do good and to sustain our
President and those in authority. We
hope to build a house of our own next sea-
son and feel sure the Lord will bless us in
our endeavors. All is peace and quiet in
our little town and the Lord has blest us
with a bountiful harvest which the breth-
ren are very busy gathering in.
	Wishing success to your valuable paper
I remain your Sister,
