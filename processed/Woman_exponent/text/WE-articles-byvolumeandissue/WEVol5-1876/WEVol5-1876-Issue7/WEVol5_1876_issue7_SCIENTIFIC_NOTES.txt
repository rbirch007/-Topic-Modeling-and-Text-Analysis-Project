SCIENTIFIC NOTES.

	STEEL which has received the hardest
temper takes the strongest magnetization
	A BOILING Lake is reported to have
been discovered in the Island of Dominica,
in a mountainous region, 2,500 feet above
the level of the sea.
	M. JAMIN, treating upon magnets, says
he has proved that a bundle of thin steel
plates is stronger than a single steel bar of
the same dimensions, the proportion in-
creasing indefinitely with the number of
plates.
	MR. Lamont, the Arctic sportsman, in
speaking of the transmission of sound by
water, says "I am perfectly sure that vibra-
tions of a boat striking against the ice-edge
are conveyed a distance of two or more
miles. Directly the boat comes in contact
with the ice every seal for miles around
raises his head and is on the alert.
	MULTIPLEX TELEGRAPHY: Recent ex-
periments have proved it is possible for two
messages to be simultaneously passed
through a single wire called "duplex tele-
graphy;" and experiments have also been
made in "quadruplex telegraphy;" in one
instance an experiment was successfully
carried out, whereby eight messages were
sent simultaneously from each end of a
wire two hundred miles in length.
