WOMAN’S RECORD.

	CHARLOTTE Bremer, the only sister of
the celebrated Frederika Bremer, and sharer
in her literary labors, died at Stockholm,
July 8th. She was born in 1799.
	THERE were more than two thousand
years ago women missionaries, one the
daughter of an emperor who, at her own
expense went to Ceylon and introduced
Buddhism into that island.
	MADAME Christine Neilson has recently
given over one thousand pounds to the
Committee of Diseases of the Throat. This
is the second gift from her generous hand
to the same institution, and was the pro-
ceeds of a concert given in May.
	MRS. Thomas, widow of a Baptist mis-
sionary, has been made superintendent of a
diocese in Burmah, which includes thirty-
nine native Karen pastors besides other
evangelists, schools, etc. The annual re-
port of the Baptist Missionary Union, speaks
of her new plans and labors, and her emi-
nent fitness for the position.
   MRS. L. H. Green, M. D. writes from
the Women’s Dispensary, Barielly, India:
"People of all castes come to the Dispensa-
ry. Ladies of the higher classes spend so
much time in retirement that they are glad
to go out under any pretext that may seem
to their friends respectable.
	MISS Jane Bay, of Baltimore, who died
recently, bequeathed over $200,000 to the
"Jane Bay Home for Boys." We agree in
the sentiments expressed by the Woman's
Journal. "How much better if she had re-
membered the still more destitute orphans
of her own sex, also." This institution
was incorporated by the last Legislature for
educating and training orphan and poor
boys.
	THE widow of Frederic VII of Denmark,
the Countess Danner, left her property val-
ued at $4,000,000, for the maintenance of an
institution for orphan and deserted girls of
