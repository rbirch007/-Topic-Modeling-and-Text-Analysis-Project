	ED "THE WOMEN OF MORMONDOM."
The women of Utah they are going to tell
Of their hardships, and griefs and virtues as well?
They have toiled long and nobly, have fought the good
	fght,
Against sin and Satan, and to maintain the right.
In Missouri when the saints withont mercy were driv'n
And ties that were closest in anguish were riven,
The sisters were heroines, unflinching and brave,
Though their fondest and dearest were laid in the grave.
And many a mother when the tempest was wild,
In the storm without shelter, gave birth to her child,
Like the mother of Jesus as we often have read,
Who was glad in a manger to make her poor bed.
And in this freer country where churches abound
Little pity for Latter-day Saints has Been found.
The women of Utah, yes, their lives should be told,
And placed upon record in letters of gold.
Those mothers in Israel, (some now pass'd away,)
They have borne well the burden and heat of the day,
And their names should be honor'd while time shall en-
	dure
Their reward in the future, awaiting, is sure.
Talk of heroes that fought and the victories won
The mem'ries emblazoned on tablets—in song,
They fought but for country and they won the proud day
And rose from oppression, and a tyrants dread sway.
But the women of Utah a victory will claim
That will place their rank foremost in annals of fame;
Forc'd to leave home and friends to these mountains to
	fly; 
God will watch over them, while His wrath passes by
Their deeds are on record and remember'd will be
In this world through all time, and in eternity.
But my pen it would fail, and these pages would swell
If their self-denial and worth I should tell.
The women of Utah let their names be enrolled
On the brightest escutcheons the future shall hold
And their mem'ry forever engraven shall stand
'Mong the noblest and fairest of every land. 
	C. DANIELS.
Provo Jan. 22, 1877,
