WELLSVILLE, CACHE Co., Feb. 18 '77.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
 We wish to inform you that the sisters
in Wellsville feel to respond to the call,
of President Young in regard to storing up
wheat.  Our worthy Bishop, Wm. H.
Maughan with several of the brethren were
present at our last monthly meeting; and
our Bishop gave us some excellent instruc-
tions in regard to STORING GRAIN, as well
other good and wholesome counsel in try-
ing to be self-sustaining and the implic-
it obedience the Lord requirs of us.  Oth-
ers of the brethren encouraged us to go on
in doing good; our president, Ellen Whit-
on exhorted the sisters to be one with the
Priesthood in all things that we may be co-
workers together.  Counselor Margaret
Woodward and several others bore faithful
testimonies to the truth of the gospel, and
manifested a sincere desire to be united in
all good works.  We have commenced stor-
ing up wheat since our last meeting, and
esteem it a great blessing and privilege to
have a prophet to lead us, that we may es-
cape, by listening to counsel, the impending
calamities that will befall the disobedient.
Success to the Exponent, we peruse its
pages with great interest.
              LUCY HASLAM, Sec'y.
