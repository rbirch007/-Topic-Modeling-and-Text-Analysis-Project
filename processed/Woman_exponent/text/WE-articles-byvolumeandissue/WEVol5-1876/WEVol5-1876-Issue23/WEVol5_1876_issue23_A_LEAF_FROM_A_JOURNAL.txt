A LEAF FROM A JOURNAL.

One day I searched an ancient cabinet
And found a musty book; I have it yet—
A journal of the good year'twenty-nine,
Kept by a long departed friend of mine;
And from its leaves grown brown with age,
I take a story and a single page:

	"Roses, oh Roses,
		Handing passion-red,
		Crimson warmth o'cried,
		Thus hath he said,
			'Wait, wait, I come!'

	"Violets, oh Violets,
		Guileless wonder hearts so true,
		Faithful little souls of blue,
		I whisper it to you:
			'Wait, wait, he comes!'

	"Daisies, oh Daisies,
		Snow-white things of purity,
		A little patience teach me.
		I blush to whisper thee:
			'Wait, wait, he comes!'

	"Wil!ow, oh Willow.
		Willow drooping tenderly,
		Hear me softly say to thee,
		These words, his words to me:
			'Wait love, I come!'

	"Heart, oh heart,
		Bless this dear, dear day
		That brings from far Bombay,
		These words that to thee say:
			'Wait love, I come!"

This old-time love-song beareth date
Of June, the month of roses and of fate,
The twentieth day;
	And just below, one sees
The story's end, in words like these:
"In flowery, far-away Bombay,
Died—lit June, the twentieth day."
