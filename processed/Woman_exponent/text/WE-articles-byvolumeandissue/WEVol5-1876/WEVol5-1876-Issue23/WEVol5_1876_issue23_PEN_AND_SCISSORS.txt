PEN AND SCISSORS.

 DIOGENES on being asked,  "Of which
beast is the biting the most dangerous?"
answered,  "If you mean wild beasts, 'tis
the slanderer; if tame, the flatterer."
	"LET every man" said Sydney Smith,
"be occupied, and occupied in the high-
st employment of which his nature is
capable, and die with the consciousness that
he has done his best."  If the highest em-
ployment is not to be found in our daily av-
ocations, let us seek it in our leisure.
 THE affection which shrinks from pub-
licity, seeks to be invisible, and avoids cer-
emony, is more true and deep than that
which finds its solace in that outward dis-
play which invites the comment of the
world at large.
