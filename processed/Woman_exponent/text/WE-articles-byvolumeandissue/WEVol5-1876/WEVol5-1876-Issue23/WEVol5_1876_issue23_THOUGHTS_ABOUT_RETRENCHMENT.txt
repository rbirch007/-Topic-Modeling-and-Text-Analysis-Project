THOUGHTS ABOUT RETRENCHMENT.

	These are a few of my thoughts, sisters.
We were organized into societies to re-
trench from everything that is evil; not
only in extravagance of dress, but every
thing that is not of the Lord. We are coun-
seled not to backbite nor speak evil of each
other or our neighbors; and we are not to 
slander or use bad language, whether in
company or at home, and always and every-
where be modest, gentle, kind and polite,
and treat everybody with due respect both
old and young. Girls, I think prayer is one
of the greatest blessings we can enjoy; by
faith and prayer the sick are healed; and I
believe the plan of healing as taught by the
gospel is better than any other way that has
been introduced; and girls, if any of you
are in trouble first call on the Lord and He
will hear and answer your intercession.
	My young sisters let us now go forth and 
help to build up the kingdom of God that
we may come forth in the morning of the
First Resurrection, among the Saviors on
Mount Zion.
				CLARA E. SNEDAKER.
	Mill Creek, Apr. 4. 1877.


THE Empress of Japan visited the shools,
factories, and public offices at Kioto recent-
ly. Great interest was taken in the visits,
this being the first occasion of any Japanese
Empress taking part in public proceedings.
Eastward the star of empire takes its way,
perhaps, for woman."—Ex.
	From the above and many similar occur-
ences, which have and are transpiring from
time to time, it is easy to perceive there is 
a new era opening for woman; and that she
is gradually partaking of the spirit and 
feeling which permeates throughout the 
length and breadth of the land.
