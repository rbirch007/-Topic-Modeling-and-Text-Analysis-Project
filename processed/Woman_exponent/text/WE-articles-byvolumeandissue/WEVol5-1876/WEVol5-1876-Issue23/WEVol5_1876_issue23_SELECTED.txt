				SELECTED.

	The scriptorial drama of "Joseph and his
Brethren," says the "Northern Echo," has 
been performed in the hall of the Mechan-
ics' Institute, Darlington, before a large and
appreciative audience, by an amateur com-
pany of Primative Methodists. The scrip-
torial narative has been divided into nine
scenes, which were gone through with
much spirit by the performers, who were
all dressed in character. Pharaoh's throne,
and crown, Joseph's coat of many colours,
Bejamin's sack with the king's cup, to-
gether with the necessary Oriental cos-
tumees, were all represented with remark-
able fidelity. At intervals, between the
scenes, appropriate pieces of music were
rendered with much vigor by the brethren,
who, whether singing or acting, entered
heart and soul into their respective roles.
The representation was received with much
favor, and it will shortly be repeated.
