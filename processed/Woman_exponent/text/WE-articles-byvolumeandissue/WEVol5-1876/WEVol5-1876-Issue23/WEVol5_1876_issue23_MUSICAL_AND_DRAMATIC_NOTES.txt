 The Boston Music Hall was open for its
Annual Concert Apr. 11, 1877. The enter-
tainment was quite a remarkable one in
character, and some of the most famous ar-
tists America can boast participated in it;
the programme was miscellaneous and as
Boston is the metropolis of America in mu-
sic, it will not be out of place to reprint the
bill in full, as it might be a model for other
musicals.

	We have read the reviews of this enter-
tainment in the Boston papers and publish
here some extracts:
	“Madame Essipoff was heard in the best
music which she has ever played in Bos-
ton.  *  *  The technical difficulties of the
composition were simply forgotten under
Madame Essipoff's treatment.  *  We do
not think it would be possible to find a bet-
ter interpreter than Madame Essipoff of
such a fiery product of Listz’s peculiar gen-
ius.
 “Miss Carey was in wonderful voice, and
sang Gluck’s “Cara fu solo a me” with grand
fulness of tones and appropriate gravity, of
style.” Upon being recalled she gave
Beethoven’s Arietta, “In gusta tombo,”—
“O don fatale” from Verdi’s Don Carlos.”
She also gave “Summer Evening Memor-
ies." Miss Kellogg interpreted Ophelia’s
mad scene with genuine dramatic life and
discrimination.  Miss Kellogg also display-
her vocal fluency and her exact and easy
skill as an “executante” in the Rolero from
“Sicilian Vespers.” In response to an im-
perative ENCORE she sang “The Little
Shepherdess.” Madame Essipoff politely
yet firmly declined to repeat any of her
numbers though repeatedly called back to
the stage.
 ANNA E. Dickinson made her debut the
second of April at the Eagle Theatre, New
York.
 MME, Ristori gave a performance at the
Apollo Theatre in Rome recently, for the,
benefit of the Working Men’s Co-operative
Society. The piece was “Elizabetta d' In-
ghilterra,” and the great actress was ably
seconded by the Morelli Company, which
came expressly from Siena for the repre-
sentation.
 MME. Adelina Patti has appeared with
the greatest success in “Semiamide," a part
the lady has not heretofore sustained in
Europe. 
 AT Liszt's recent concert at Vienna, the
city of Vienna presented him with the
large gold “Salvator medal.” The Viennese
critics dwell on the piano-forte performances
of Dr. Liszt as equal to those of his best pe-
riod. The instrument was covered with
crowns and flowers. He accompanied Mme.
Gomperz-Bettelheim in the Scotch airs of
Beethoven.
