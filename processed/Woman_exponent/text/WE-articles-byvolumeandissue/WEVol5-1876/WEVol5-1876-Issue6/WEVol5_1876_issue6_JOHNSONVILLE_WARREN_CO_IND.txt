	JOHNSONVILLE, WARREN Co., IND.
					        July 3, 1876.
EDITORS EXPONENT:
	DEAR LADIES:—The second number of
your excellent paper was received, and I
was well pleased with its high moral tone.
It was not just what I had expected to see,
coming from your city. I am more than
surprised at the interest manifested by
your women on the subject of reform, and
the up-building of the cause which you
have espoused, together with your co-opera-
tive system of doing business. I can but
admire the spirit evinced in the good
works.
	I have been reading some of your books:
"Voice of Warning," by Parley P. Pratt,
and two sermons by Orson Pratt, one of
which was highly instructive, and am now
perusing the Book of Mormon. I shall be
better posted by and by, particularly if I
could have the Elders to teach and explain
the Scriptures according to your views. I
might after a while be constrained to say—
"Almost thou persuadest me to be a Mor-
mon." The women of your community
cannot be much oppressed, when they have
the right of suffrage, and a paper entirely
devoted to their interests. That is more
than we can say, for we are not allowed to
vote even on questions directly concerning
us.
	In conclusion I will remark that, If you
carry out the doctrines taught and preached
by your Elders, you cannot be a bad people.
	Let me hear from you again, at least
through the columns of your paper, if you
cannot find the time to write.
	I shall be very happy to have your paper
for the rest of the year.
	Wishing you success in your enterprise
I remain				Very truly yours,
							M. L. Johnston.
