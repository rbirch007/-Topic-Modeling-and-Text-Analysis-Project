WOMAN'S RECORD.

	MRS. CARABELLO, who died recently at
Canelones, Banda Oriental, had lived to the
extraordinary age of 120 years.
	MRS. JULIA N. Gott, of Ann Arbor, died
July 7, aged sixty years. She established
the first Young Ladies' Seminary in Ann
Arbor in 1840, and was herself a teacher in
the institution three years.
	MISS EMELINE Shepherd has on exhibi-
tion at Philadelphia a cambric handkerchief
the work of two years. It is embroidered
with a "fac simile" of Independence Hall,
a liberty cap and other national emblems,
valued at $500.00.
	SENORITA DONA Rosario Acunary Ville-
nueva has written and given to the public
in Madrid, a drama, founded upon "Rienzl
the Tribune, which has been very success-
fully played." The young lady is yet in
her twentieth year.
	MRS. ELIZA Esdaile, recently deceased,
was the only surviving daughter and child
of the poet Percy Bysshe Shelley by his ill-
fated wife Harriet Westbrook. She was
born in 1813, and could have no recollection
of her father; having been one of the three
children taken away from him by Lord El-
don, which circumstance called forth the
celebrated "Curse" to the Lord Chancellor.
	HON. MRS. Talbot of London, England,
has attained considerable celebrity in Wo-
mon's Work, by devising and practically
starting the "Parochial Mission Women."
She began this enterprise with three wo-
man, whose great and untiring labor in
ministering both to the physical and spirit-
ual wants of the poor in their own houses
were sufficient guarantees of the good faith
and earnestness with which Mrs. Talbot
forwarded the scheme. There are at present
183 of these co-workers, sincere and devot-
ed to the cause of carrying comfort to the
poor and unfortunate, and instructing them
concerning health and the management of
homes and children. "Mrs. Talbot is an in-
stance of the way in which all the great
questions of social politics are being met
and worked out by women, at the present
day, in England."
