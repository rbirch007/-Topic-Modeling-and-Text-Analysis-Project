NINETY-TWO.

	Miss Rhoda Richards celebrated her birthday
on Tuesday the 8th inst. She was ninety two
years old; and notwithstanding the heavy
rain fall, which commenced about mid-day,
two o'clock found a number of sisters assem-
bled at her humble dwelling, to offer congratu-
lations and kind wishes. A quiet little bless-
ing meeting was conducted, in the midst of
which the following pleasant note was receiv-
ed:
		Salt Lake City, August, 8th, 1876,
Sister Rhoda Richards,
					City;
My dear Cousin:
	The condition of the weather is such that I


do not think it wise for me to leave home. I
shall therefore be debarred the pleasure of call-
ing on you this afternoon and offering my con-
gratulations and blessings on this the anniver-
sary of your birthday. Permit me however,
by means of pen and ink to express my kind-
est feelings and regards, with the hope that
abundant blessings may continue to abide with
you to the latest days of your life upon the
earth, and increase with you throughout a
never-ending future of joy, happiness and ex-
altation. Such is the heartfelt prayer of your
Brother in the New and Everlasting Cove-
nant.
					BRIGHAM YOUNG.
	After choice blessings had been asked for and
pronounced upon the aged hostess by all pres-
ent, she said she did not wish to take all the
blessings to herself, but desired that her sisters
should also be blessed. Accordingly a bless-
ing was pronounced upon each in turn by the
others. At the close of this sacred and joyous
interview, the company sat down to a well-
filled table, and feasted bodily as well as spirit-
ually. When the repast was finished, Sister
Zina D. Young, in a sweet and beautiful pray-
er, returned thanks to the Giver of all good,
for the blessings of the day, and asked for a
continuance of God's mercies to His people.
Sister Rhoda gave an invitation for all to
be present at the next anniversary of her
birthday, and with affectionate adieus the com-
pany dispersed.


For the EXPONENT.
