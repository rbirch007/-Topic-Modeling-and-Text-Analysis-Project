R. S. REPORTS.

	The General Retrenchment Meeting, held
in the Fourteenth Ward Assembly Rooms,
Saturday, Oct. 28, was well attended. The
minutes read on that occasion from the dif-
ferent societies deserve praise; they were
full of wise and choice remarks from the
young members of the different or-
ganizations. The blessing, which have al-
ready accrued from these institutions can-
not be estimated. President Mrs. M. I
Horne spoke upon the grain question princi-
pally, suggesting to the sisters the propriety
of entering into some understanding that
would facilitate the accomplishment of the
object. Counselors E. R. Snow and Sarah
M. Kimball both spoke on this subject and
other general ones—the principles of eter-
nal life, the increase and multiplicity of
cares and burdens; said that those who were
not living in the line of their duty would
scarcely be able to endure all that was laid
upon them to bear. Counselor E. Howard
spoke in reference to her recent journey
among the Relief Societies, and the great
desire they manifested to hear instruction,
and the courtesy shown by Bishops and pre-
siding men in the places she visited. Coun-
selor B. W. Smith spoke of the woman's
store, of the great good to be derived from
an institution of home manufactures; if
carried out upon a systematic plan for the 
public weal.
