			FAYETTE, SANPETE Co.,
						March 21, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I send you a brief report of our Relief
Society which was organized Sept. 12, 1875
by President John Bartholomew, and Elder
John Metcalf, as follows: Mrs. Jane A.
Brown, president; Mrs. Eliza R. Bartholo-
mew, and Mrs. Polly Bartholomew coun-
selors; Mrs. Charlotle E. Miller, Secretary;
Mrs. Emma Bartholomew, Assistant Sec'y.;
Mrs. Amy Miller, Treasurer. There are
twenty-one members in the society and we
meet every first Thursday in the month to
give in our reports also to receive counsel
and instruction. We also have our sewing
meetings which are well atteuded and the
spirit of God prevails in our midst. We
have not much means at our command, but
still are in a prosperous condition, the Lord
blesses us in all our undertakings to serve
Him. He also blesses us with health and
strength, and willing hearts to perform all
that is required of us.
	We feel that the storing up of grain is of
the Lord, we have on hand quite a quantity
and will add to it as fast as we can. By the
courtesy of our President John Bartholo-
mew, our wheat is stored in his granary till
we can get one built for our own use.
There is a good rock meeting house in this
place for which we, (the R. S.), have made
matts and carpets, we also purchased a
stove and the window furniture, so you see
beloved sisters, that we can always find
something to do; I contemplate introduc-
ing the silk culture.
	Last January on the 22d, Sisters Williams
and Madsen paid us a visit which was a
real feast to us; our desire ever since then
has been for the sisters who have a large
experience to visit us whenever they can
make it convenient.
	Your sisters in the Gospel,
			JANE A. BROWN, Pres't.
				AMY MILLER, Treasurer.
