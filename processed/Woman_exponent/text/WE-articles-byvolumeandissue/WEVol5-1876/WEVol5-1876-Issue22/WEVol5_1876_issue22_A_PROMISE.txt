		A PROMISE.

Mine eyes! mine eyes! rain happy tears!
For ye shall in your mortal life
Look on your Saviour, on your God.
O blessed eyes! that live to-day—
Weep all your sorrow, shade away,
Ye shall look on the living Lord;
Mine eyes! mine eyes! look only up
To heaven's pure and sinless height.
O that no vision dark, profane,
Of stained earth, might intervene
To leave its blemish-touch upon
Their mirror gaze. O blessed eyes!
Weep happy tears! let morning's pure,
And silent night's cool, holy breath
Waft far away all earthly scenes—
For thou shalt look upon thy Christ.
O might it be, that when His eyes
Read through mine own, the heart below,
They shall be clear as pebbled stream
Through wh[-]ch we see the flow.

O blessed hands! and shall ye lie
In the pure clasp of Christ's thy Lord?
Thrill, blessed hands, with holy flame,
That Christ the pure, the victor King,
O're mortal trial, mortal sin,
Who unstained passed,
Alone and weak, this all we meet,
Shall look in mortal eyes and smile,
And bless thee by His own pure touch,
O blessed hands! be pure thy touch,

O happy feet! tread lightly on
O'er thorny paths, tho' weak and sore,
For ye shall tread the blessed earth,
That Christ's own feet shall press upon.
O feet, keep firm and pure thy ways,
On dust too sacred for e'en lips
To press, your steps shall fall.

Thrill eager heart and brain and ears,
Thrill, flesh and blood, and thirsting soul,
Which ne'er shall die,
For ye shall in His presence stand,
And see His face, and hear His voice.
Fear, mortal frame! lest in the light
Of His divine and beauteous eyes,
Which read all life, ye trembling fall,
And burn away beneath their glow.
