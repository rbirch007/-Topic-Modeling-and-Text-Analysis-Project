WOMAN'S RECORD.

	AT a recent meeting of the Citizen's Suf-
frage Association of Philadelphia, the fol-
lowing resolutions were passed:
	Resolved, the women of this nation can-
not consistently rejoice in the success of the
Republican, or deplore the defeat of the
Democrats, both parties being indifferent,
if not opposed to the advance and estab-
lishment of equal rights.
	Resolved, that the women of Philadel-
phia have no good reason to expect that
their rights will be regarded by either of 
the late candidates for Mayor, neither hav-
ing uttered a public word in their behalf.
	Resolved, that this society rejoices in the 
fact that women in England have equal
rights with men to vote, with one excep-
tion, the election of members to Parliament,
which, through the persistent efforts of wo-
men and men having that work at heart,
is likely soon to be removed.
