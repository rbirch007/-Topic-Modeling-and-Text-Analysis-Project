speak his worth.
	THEY are the wise whom God esteems
wise. They are are the wise whom God
makes wise.
	WARS have been a disgrace to that Chris-
tianity which the nations profess.—Lord
John Russell.
	THERE is no war among men but what
arises from some vice—from covetousness,
or ambition, or an immoderate love of glory.
—Plutarch
	WITH time and patience the mulberry
leaf becomes satin. What difficulty is
there at which a man shonld quail when a
worm can accomplish so much from the leaf
of the mulberry.
	KEEP your conscience tender—tender as
the eye that closes its lid against an atom
of dust, or as that sensitive plant which you
have seen shrink and shut its leaves not
merely from the rude touch of a finger, but
at the breath of a moth.
