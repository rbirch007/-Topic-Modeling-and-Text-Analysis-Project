GEORGE.

	Previous to the General Conference a two-
days meeting was held according to appoint-
ment in the St. George Temple. President
Brigham Young, and his Counselors, eleven of


the Quorum of the Twelve, presiding Bishop,
Edward Hunter, and many High Priests, El-
ders and Bishops, and a large congregation of
the saints were there assembled to worship
God in a Temple erected to His Most Holy
Name.
	The spirit of the Lord was poured out upon
them in rich abundance, and the sermons de-
livered upon that occasion were replete with
wise instructions and counsel, suited to the
condition of the people at the present day, and
great power rested upon those who spoke and
prayed, and good order prevailed. Elder J. T.
D. McAllister was elected president of St.
George Stake of Zion by unanimous vote.
The saints in that country have had much to
endure, but the Lord is blessing them above
others, in that they have a Temple completed
into which they may enter to worship God,
and call upon Him, and where they can minis-
ter for the living and the dead.
	We are indebted to the "Deseret News" for
information in regard to these meetings, and
the Conference at St. George.
