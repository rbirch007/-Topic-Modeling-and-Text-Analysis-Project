WOMEN PHYSICIANS.

	Association Hall, Fifteenth and Chestnut
streets, was thronged on the 15th of March,
by a brilliant and interested audience, it be-
ing the Twenty-fifth Annual Commence-
ment of the Women's Medical College of
Pennsylvania.
	The degrees of M. D. were conferred by
the President, T. Morris Perot, upon the
following graduates:
	Franes N. Baker, Martha M. De Graw,
Elizabeth W. Smith, and Mary A. Young,
from Pennsylvania; Josephine G. Davis,
and Henrietta B. Woolston, of New Jersey;
Caroline S. Pease and Ella M. Whitaker, of
New York, Cornelia L. Boardman, of Mass-
achusetts; Elizabeth E. L. Forbes, of Dela-
ware; Elizabeth A. Follansbee, of Califor-
nia; Romania B. Pratt, of Utah; Elvira
Bainfer and Elizabeth T. Richards of Mich-
igan, and H. Josephine Varnum, of Maine.
	The spring course of 1877 commenced on
Monday, March 19, and will continue ten
weeks. Its chief purpose is to procure for
the students of the Women's Medical Col-
lege of Pennsylvania, all the advantages
of those improved methods of study in-
troduced into medical institutions in this
country the last few years. The essential
features of these are : First, a progressive
course of study; second, an increased
amount of demonstrative teaching, with
practical work on the part of the students.
—Woman's Words.


EARLY IN the month of May the cere-
mony of unveiling the statue of Fitz
Green Halleck will take place in Central
Park, New York. Whittier will give a
poem, and William Gullen Bryant will de-
liver an address. This statue is the first
ever raised to an American poet, and will
be near those of Shakespeare and Walter
Scott.
