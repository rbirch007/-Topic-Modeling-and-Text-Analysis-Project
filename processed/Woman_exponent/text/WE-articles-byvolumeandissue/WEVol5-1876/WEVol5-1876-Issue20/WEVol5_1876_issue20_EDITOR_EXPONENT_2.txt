EDITOR EXPONENT:

	In accordance with your wishes I write
to inform you that the Relief Society of
this place have responded to the call in re-
lation to storing grain. A good spirit pre-
vails among the sisters, and all feel willing
to carry out the counsel of President Youug,
in regard to that subject. Our Bishop has
kindly offered us a place in the Tithing
Office to carefully store away our grain,
which we have gladly accepted. The sis-
ters have liberally given according to their
circumstances; and we have at present,
nearly one hundred bushels of wheat on
hand. The brethren here have a good
United Order farm, and the Bishop has
promised to give the Relief Socifety an in-
terest in it; so we hope that by the help of
our brethren, we will be able to add more
to our stock from year to year. Our be-
loved President, Mrs. E. A. Willie is ear-
nest in exhorting the sisters to become unit-
ed, and to live their religion in the truest
sense and spirit. Your excellent paper is
read with great interest, and the best wish-
es are expressed for the advancement of
every good work for we know that those
who keep the commandments of God will
receive His blessings, and those who do
not will have His judgments upon them.
	May God bless you and all who are striv-
ing to do right, is the prayer of your sister
in the Gospel.
						J. A. HUGHES.
	Mendon, Cache Co., Utah.
