MINERSVILLE, BEAVER, Co.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	By request of our President, Polly Ann Ze-
briskie, I address you, to inform you that we
are still trying to honor our calling as a branch
of the Relief Society, and to do all in our pow-
er to assist the needy. We are one with our
sisters in Utah to follow out the counsel of our
honored President Brigham Young, with re-


gard to storing up grain. Our Bishop, James
McKnight, gave us the privilege of using part
of the Tithing-house, where we have had a bin
made, and commenced storing up grain
against a time of need. On the 14 of January
the sisters met and had a sewing bee and pic-
nic. The supper was tastefully arranged and
the bounties of nature supplied in abundance.
We held our annual meeting and transacted
business on Feb. 22; the books showed our so-
ciety to be in a prosperous condition. Our
Bishop attended the meeting, and gave us 
much valuable instruction pertaining to our
duties.
				P. A. ZEBRISKIE, Pres't.
