EDITOR EXPONENT:

	By request of our President, Mrs. Mary
J. Hammond, I send a written report of
the Relief Society of Huntsville for publi-
cation. The Society is in a prosperous con-
dition. We meet twice a month and are
united and ever ready an[-] willing to re-
spond to every call made by our president
who take great interest in striving to
bring every good and useful principle be-
fore her sisters.
	The Society numbers about one hundred
and fifty members. There are but very few


poor in our midst and they are well provid-
ed for. The Society has funds on hand
amounting to two hundred and eighty-five
dollars. We have bought a few head of
sheep and are striving to become self-sus-
taining. We have shares in the Co-opera-
tive Store amounting to one hundred and
forty dollars, and shares in the Relief So-
ciety Store in Ogden City, amounting to
thirty-eight dollars. We have a commit-
tee appointed to buy wheat and store it, to
be used in the future for the benefit of the
saints according to the counsel which may
be given through the prophet of God upon
the earth. We have a very interesting
Sunday School under the direction of El-
der Charles Wright; both old and young
seem to take great interest in attending
regularly, and in gaining useful knowledge
pertaining to our religion. The EXPONENT
is very much appreciated by those who
have subscribed for it; we are only sorry
that there are not more numbers of this
valuable paper taken in our midst.
	Praying the Lord to bless the EXPONENT,
and all who are interested in it, and hoping
you will excuse this my first attempt, I re-
main your sister, 
	MARY B. HALL,
		Sec'y
