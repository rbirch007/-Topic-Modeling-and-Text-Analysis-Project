WOMAN'S RECORD.
	THE "Bosten Post" calls Miss Kate Field
a busy body indeed. At the same time she
is permanently engaged upon the staff of
the London Examiner, she is contributor to
the "Hornet, and is filling a theatrical en-
gagement in London, has recently written
a play for Herman Vezin, and a blank verse
poem which have been warmly praised.
	THE REV. Mrs. Maggie Van Cott has
been holding a series of revival meetings
in New York City, and the "Woman's
Journal" tells us that, notwithstanding the
fact that some think women have no right
to preach, she has made 1,200 converts the
past winter.
	GEORGE ELIOT is at work again on a
new novel. It is said she feels very much
disappointed that Middlemarch is consider-
ed better than "Daniel Deronda." She
herself regards this novel her master piece.
She produced her first original work 18
years ago, and the whole product of the
sale of her books is estimated at $165,000.
