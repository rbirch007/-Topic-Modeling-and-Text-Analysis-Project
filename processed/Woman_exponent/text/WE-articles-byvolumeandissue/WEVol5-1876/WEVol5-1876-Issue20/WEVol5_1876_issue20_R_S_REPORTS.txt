R. S. REPORTS.

	The General Retrenchment Meeting held
in the Fourteenth Ward Assembly Rooms,
Saturday, March 3rd, was especially inter-
esting. The day was a very stormy one, but
all who were there seemed filled with that
good spirit, which cannot fail to make glad
the heart. There were very few minutes
from Junior Associations, and the time was
therefore devoted to speaking. The Presi-
dent, Mrs. M. I. Horne, gave some of her
experiences of many years since, and relat-
ed incidents of note in the life of Joseph
Smith, which had come under her direct
observation. Counselors Mrs. Z. D. Young,
Mrs. P. Woodruff, and B. W. Smith,
all spoke upon the duties and particular
mission of the sisters. Mrs. Woodruff
spoke of her anticipated visit to St. George,
and exhorted the sisters to faithfulness and
to ask the Lord continually for wisdom.
Mrs. E. B. Wells, Mrs. W. East, Mrs. L. A.
Wells and others spoke upon those subjects
which are immediately connected with the
practical work under the supervision of the
sisters, exhorted to caution and diligence,
and to beware of false spirits. President
Mrs. M. I. Horne sang a "song of Zion."
These meetings really seem to be spiritual
feasts for those who are seeking to unite
their efforts in assisting to bring about a
universal reform, and preparing for those
great events which are about to transpire.
