	PAYSON, Jan. 4, 1877.
Editor Exponent:
	At a meeting held by the Relief Society
Dec. 6, 1876, B. J. Simons was chosen
President of the Purchasing Committe,
Mrs. Martha Simons, Mrs. Susan Fair-
banks and Mrs. Clarissa Moore to solicit do-
nations and purchase grain, Mrs. Ann Mat-
tison and Mrs. Sarah Ann Reece Assistants,
Mrs. Agnes Douglas Treasurer, Mrs. M. A.
Hardy Secretary.
	We have on hand nearly one hundred
bushels of wheat with a prospect of more;
the sisters feel with our President and
Counsel that the move is a grand one; and

[Column 3]

they all desire to be one with their sisters
in Salt Lake City and throughout Utah in
aiding, with all their might and strength,
to roll on the great work in which we are
all engaged; they join with me in love,
wishing you a happy New Year.
	I deeply sympathize with our dear sister
Mrs. L. Greene Richards in the death of
her little girl, having buried five children
myself. How sweet were the words that
flowed from her pen in the Exponent of
Nov. 15th; may our Heavenly Father bless
and comfort her
	Your sister &c.,
