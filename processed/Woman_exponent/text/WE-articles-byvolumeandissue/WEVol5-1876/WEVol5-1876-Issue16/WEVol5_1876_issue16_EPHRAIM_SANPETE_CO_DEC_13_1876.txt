	EPHRAIM, SANPETE Co., Dec. 13, 1876.
	By request of our President, Mrs. S. A.
Petersen, I send you a report of what the
sisters in Ephraim are doing about the
"storing of Grain." The Sisters of the re-
lief Society went out in the harvest field
one day each, to glean; they gleaned fifty
bushels. They have also made cheese and
and jeans which they have sold; and pieced
up patch-work quilts and obtained one hun-
dred and seventeen bushels more, making
one hundred and sixty-seven bushels be-
longing to the Society.
	In addition to this our President has pro-
posed a plan, that as many sisters as choose
to co-operate together and take shares, one
bushel of wheat being a share, the wheat to
be untouched until a time of famine. This
wheat to be earned by the sisters them-
selves not troubling their husbands about
it at all; and strive to increase the amount
of their shares until famine puts a stop to
their exertions in that line. Several sisters
have taken shares, some one, some two,
some five, according to their ability. The
shares already taken amount to one hun-
dred and thirty-three bushels; so we have
altogether, three hundred bushels. The
Bishop has promised us a bin in the new
store-house when it is finished. The sis-
ters are alive to their duties in the Gospel,
and in all the labors pertaining to the
kingdom.
	I will close with best wishes for your
happiness.
