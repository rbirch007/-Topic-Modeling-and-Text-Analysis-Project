			BEAVER CITY, Jan. 3, 1877.
EDITORS EXPONENT:
	I wish to add to the testimony of my sis-
ters a few thoughts and suggestions in re-
gard to the interests in which the women of
Utah are engaged. I feel a great interest
in the EXPONENT; I would rather miss a
meal of victuals daily than be without it.
Times are dull and money scarce, but I know
the feelings of the greater number are that
the EXPONENT should be sustained as well
as to attend to every other call made upon
us now—the laying up of grain, assisting
msssionaries, caring for our own poor, the
Woman's Book, and sundry other duties
and responsibilities. We have built a gran-
ary sufficiently large to hold four or five
hundred bushels of grain, and have placed
a small quantity of grain in it, and we in-
tend to add to it as fast as it is in our power,
and also to build a larger building when
needed for our increased supplies.
	Your sister &c.,
