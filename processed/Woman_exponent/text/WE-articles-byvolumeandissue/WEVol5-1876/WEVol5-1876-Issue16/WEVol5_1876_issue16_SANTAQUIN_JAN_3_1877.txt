				SANTAQUIN, Jan. 3, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	In accordance with your wishes, I write
to inform you that the Relief Society of
this place has responded to the call in rela-
tion to storing grain. A good spirit pre-
vails among the sisters, and all feel willing
to carry out the counsel of President Young
in regard to storing up grain.
	Bro. August Tietgen has kindly given
us the use of one of his wheat bins, until
our good brothers can build us a granary.
The sisters have nobly and liberally given
according to their circumstances, and have
donated at present sixty-five bushels, and
the stream still continues. We feel willing,
with the help of our Father in heaven, to
do all we can in storing up grain for our
temporal salvation; and, dear sisters, when
we have secured the grain, let us be faith-
fnl stewards over the same, and, putting
our trust in God, leave the event with Him.
	I remain your sister in the Gospel,
