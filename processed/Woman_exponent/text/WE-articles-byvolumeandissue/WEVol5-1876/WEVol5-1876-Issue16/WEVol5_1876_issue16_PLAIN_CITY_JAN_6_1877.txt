			PLAIN CITY, Jan. 6, 1877.
EDITORS EXPONENT:
	We have just had our first meeting, to
talk about the call there was made on us to
store up grain as helpmeets in the kingdom
of God. We are all willing to do the best
we can with the little means we have; our
President says we can afford to buy fifty
bushels at ninety cents per bushel. We
have got a good bin to store it in, and hope,
by the help of God and of our brethren,
we will still add to it an abundant supply
in the future. Thare is no sickness in our
place, nor starving poor.
	God bless you and all who try to do right
is the prayer of your sister in the Gospel,
