EDITOR EXPONENT:

	We the sisters of Tooele would like to repre-
sent ourselves in the WOMAN'S EXPONENT. I
can say that peace health and prosperity pre-
vail, for which we are thankful to our heaven-
ly Father. We meet in the new meeting
house every two weeks, where we enjoy the
society of one another and get our spiritual
strength renewed. We hope soon to have our
new hall finished, it is progressing slowly; we
want to get it covered in as soon as possible so
that we can give parties in it this winter in or-
der to raise means to help defray the expenses.
Our President, Sister Micklejohn is most un-
tiring and energetic in her labors and has the
full confidence and love of all the sisters. The
spirit and blessing of our heavenly Father is
with us. Our new hall is for a Library and
Reading Room and one room is expressly for
the Relief Society. Sister Delamare and my-
self raised a few silk worms this summer for a
beginning in Tooele, and hope that this will not
be the last of it, as there are plenty of mulberry
trees here and plenty of little girls that could
lend a helping hand. We had a very pleasant
visit from Sister Howard two weeks ago at the
Young Ladies Retrenchment Meeting. She
gave some very valuable instructions to the
young ladies; but was very sorry to see so few
in attendance. We highly prize the EXPO-
NENT for it is full of the purest gems of light
and love; we get a feast when it comes. I am
thankful that I am numbered among the Lat-
ter-day Saints, and it is my humble prayer that
we may all endure faithful to the end.
	I remain your sister, etc,
