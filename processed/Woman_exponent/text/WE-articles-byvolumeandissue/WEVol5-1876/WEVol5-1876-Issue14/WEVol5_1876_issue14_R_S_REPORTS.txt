			R. S. REPORTS.

	The General Retrenchment Meeting held
in the Fourteenth Ward Assembly Rooms
Saturday, Nov. 25, was one of special in-
terest to all who are interested in the ad-
vancment and progression of WOMAN.
Counselor E. R. Snow presided, and after
the general exercises she addressed the au-
dience in her able and earnest manner.
She contrasted the position of the sisters
now, with what it was ten years ago, and
expressed her gratitude at the change which
had been effected, and gave much excellent
counsel; her words were "as apples of gold
set in pictures of silver." Counselors Zina
D. Young and Bathsheba W. Smith, and
Mrs. E. B. Wells each expressed their feel-
ings in regard to the labors to be performed
by the sisters, and exhorted all present to
diligence. They also gave their views upon
the Grain Movement and the Woman's
Book. Some other sisters bore similar tes-
timonies. Elder Junius F. Wells then ad-
dressed the sisters for a few minutes. He
related some incidents that had come under
his observation while upon his mission, and
congratulated the sisters upon the superi-
ority of their knowledge, and their favor-
able condition viewed through the under-
standing they have of the Gospel. He bore
a strong testimony to the truth of the Gospel
revealed in this "the last dispensation of
the fulness of times."
