EDITOR EXPONENT:

	Perhaps there would be no harm in tell-
ing you how we are getting along in this
little village as a branch of the Lord's
church, and a few of my thoughts. We
have no Relief Society here yet, but the

[Column 2]
young folks' Mutual Improvement Associ-
ation is alive, and there seems to be a de-
sire for improvement in our midst, and to
march along with the kingdom; yet in my
experience I have seen it necessary that
every individual, before doing any good in
this kingdom, must humble themselves be-
fore their Maker, acknowledge their un-
worthiness without His guidance and help,
and it seems to me that unless we have the
Spirit of Christ to guide us in our thoughts,
words and actions, we are of no more use
than the moth or worm. But if we seek to
imitate His life we will know of His doc-
trine. And may we as sisters seek to be
guided by Him that we may help to bring
about peace, union and love is the prayer
of your Sister &c.,
