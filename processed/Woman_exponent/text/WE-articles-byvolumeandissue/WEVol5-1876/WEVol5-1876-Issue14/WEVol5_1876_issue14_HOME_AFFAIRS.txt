HOME AFFAIRS.

	CHRISTMAS IS coming, and in anticipation of
its arrival the indefatigable Mr. James Dwyer,
whose efforts to cater to the tastes of the citi-
zens and children of Salt Lake City, have
hitherto been so eminently successful, has open-
ed his holiday stock of goods making the most
brilliant and elaborate display of his style of
goods which has ever been made in this city,
in honor probably of the Centennial Christ-
mas of America. On the evening of the open-
ing Dec. 11, the store and side-walk in front of
it were crowded so that it was almost impossi-
ble to obtain egrees. He has a full stock of all
the standard poets, novelists and historians,
and works upon science and art. He has also
many choice works upon all subjects, and a
full supply of the school books now in use;
every variety of paper pencils etc. Russian
leather goods of all descriptions and beautiful
designs. Perforated card board with or with-
out stamping. Ladies' fine cutlery, elegant
card cases, writing desks and work-boxes of
nearly every variety from the most elegant,
down to the plainest workmanship. In short,
he has got in his assortment any and every
article of fancy goods suitable for Holiday
gifts. Go and examine for yourself and you
will be sure to find something suited to your
taste and resources.
