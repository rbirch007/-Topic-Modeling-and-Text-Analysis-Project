EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The 19th Ward Relief Society held their
annual meeting Sept. 26. Sisters E. R.
Snow, M. I. Horne, Zina D. Young, B. W.
Smith, Presendia Kimball, Mercy Thomp-
son and Bishop A. H. Raleigh addressed
the meeting. Their remarks were replete
with words of instruction and encourage-
ment, and the counsel given, would, if fol-
lowed, aid us in bringing up our children in
a righteous manner, and help us to live
more faithful to our religion and to each
other. We were cautioned as mothers in
Israel never to speak lightly of the holy
and eternal principles of Plural Marriage,
for by that we are to gain our exaltation in
the Celestial Kingdom of our Father and
God. Our duty at the polls, the opening of
a store for the sale of home-industries, and
seri-culture, were also spoken of as matters
claiming our present and earnest attention.
	The yearly report of the society was read,
also the amount of donations and disburse-
ments of the society since the organization
of the society in 1869, which was as follows:
	Donations.......................... $ 2,138 61
	Disbursements..................    1,747 71½
	Balance...............................       390 90 ½
	For loan of Temple clothes        96 55
	There were others present, that we were
sorry time would not permit us to hear
from. At the close of the meeting, all pres-
ent were invited to partake of a picnic pre-
pared by the sisters of the Ward, and they
accordingly adjourned to the tables spread
with the bounties of the earth.
	With best wishes for the success of your
valuable paper—"OURS," as we consider
it,
	We remain yours, etc.
				A. A. PRATT, Pres't.
					A. E. NEAL, Sec'y.
