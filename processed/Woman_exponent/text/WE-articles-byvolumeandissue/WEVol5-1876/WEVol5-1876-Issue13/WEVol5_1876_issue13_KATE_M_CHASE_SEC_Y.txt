				MANTI, Nov. 12, 1876.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	In accordance with your expressed wish-
es, I take the present opportunity of inform-
ing you that the Relief Society of this place
has responded to the call in relation to stor-
ing grain.
	We have about fifty bushels on hand for
that purpose, and we intend to continue in
well-doing.
	We have a small store, organized last
April, with a capital of $200,000, which is in
a flourishing condition, notwithstanding
hard times, opposition, and other adverse
circumstances. We paid a dividend of 34
per cent for our first six months, ending
Oct. 21st. We have increased our capital
considerably by taking new shares, and hope
by wisdom, economy and the blessing of our
Heavenly Father to continue to prosper. We
also have ample means on hand to assist the
needy and destitute whenever we are call-
ed upon; and all things considered, our so-
ciety was never in a more prosperous con-
dition than at present.
	A good spirit prevails in our meetings,
and we feel that we are greatly blessed in
our endeavors to do good.
	Wishing you success in your laudable en-
deavors to arouse the sisters to a realizing
sense of the great responsibilities devolv-
ing upon them, I remain,
				Very Respectfully,
					A. L. Cox, Sec'y.
