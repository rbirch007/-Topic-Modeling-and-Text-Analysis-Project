			SENATE OF ROME

	There is a man among us who is called
Jesus Christ. He is accepted of the people
as a prophet. His own disciples say he is
the Son of God. He heals the sick, restores
sight to the blind, hearing to the deaf, and
raises the dead. In conversation he is mild
and amiable; his carriage is graceful and
modest; in reproving he is terrible, yet gen-
tle and forgiving. In person he is tall and
comely; his nose and mouth are beautifully
formed; his chin a model of Grecian Sculp-
ture underlaying a heavy beard; his eyes
like diamonds, indicating great wisdom
and penetration, comprehending everything
at a glance; bespeaking great compassion for
the poor and unfortunate. His hair waves
in flowing curls over his shoulders in orien-
tal style after the manner of the Nazarites.
He has not been known to laugh, but fre-
quently weeps; in short his whole form and
carriage are without blemish and his exte-
rior beauty surpassing all the sons of men.
