	CENTERVILLE, DAVIS Co., Nov. 20, 1876.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Last Wednesday, the 15 inst., Sisters Eliza
R. Snow and E. Barney visited our settle-
ment and organized a "Young Ladies' Re-
trenchment Association." They met with
the ladies of Centerville at 2 o'clock, p. m.,
and appointed the following officers for the
association:
	Mrs. Kate Dalrymple, President,
	Mrs. Frankie Randall,	}
	Miss S. L. Smith,		} Counselors
	"	Hester Ford,		}
	"	M. E. Smith,		}
	"	Kate M. Chase, Secretary,
	"	Mary Parish, Assistant Secretary,
	"	Addie Woolley, Treasurer.
	Sister Wadoup was appointed Superin-
tendent.
	Our first meeting under the new organ-
ization was held at 2 o'clock on Saturday,
the 18th. There were present at the meet-
ing, beside the members, Mrs. M. Randall,
President of the Relief Society, and her
Counselors. Excellent and appropriate re-
marks were made by the President, Super-
intendent, Counselors and others. A lively
interest was manifested by all in the so-
ciety, and it promises to mature into a pleas-
ant and profitable institution.
	Forty members answered to roll call,
which is a fair indication of the desire felt
by all to assist in the progress of all such
enterprises as tend to instruct us, and facil-
tate the advancement of divine intelligence
among us.
			Yours Respectfully,
