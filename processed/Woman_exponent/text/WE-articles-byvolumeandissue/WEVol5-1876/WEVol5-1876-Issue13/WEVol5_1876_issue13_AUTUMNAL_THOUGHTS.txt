AUTUMNAL THOUGHTS.

	The sad days come
	When to the tomb
Are borne the sweetest flowers,
	Which when in bloom,
	Threw rich perfume
Around this home of ours.

	It seems they're given
	As types of Heaven,
Those fragrant, beauteous flowers!—
	My soul's delight
	Is the cheering sight
They shed round summer hours.

	They have a voice,
	A silent voice,
That whispers to my heart,
	And to my brain
	In sweetest strain,
From which I sigh to part.

	How much we meet
	Sublimely sweet,
That's never told or written!
	But folds us round
	In bliss profound,
By heavenly breezes smitten!

	Now birds and bees
	Have ceased their glees,
All nature seems reposing;
	A moment, I
	Reflect and sigh
On the young eyes I've seen closing.

	And one stands chief,
	In bold relief,
Departing with the flowers!
	We laid her down
	In her floral crown
And returned to forsaken bowers.

	But spring will again
	Bring life in her train,
And the flowers their beauties resume,
	And the lov'd will arise
	With their glorious eyes,
And go forth in eternal bloom!

	When the resurrection
	Restores perfection—
Oh! day of consummate beauty!
	For it we can wait
	Yes, at any gate
With pleasure—and not "on duty."
