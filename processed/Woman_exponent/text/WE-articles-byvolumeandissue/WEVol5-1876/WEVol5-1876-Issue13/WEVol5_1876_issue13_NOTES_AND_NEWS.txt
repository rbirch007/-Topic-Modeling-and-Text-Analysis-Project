NOTES AND NEWS.

	IN SOME of the places where women are
not permitted the right of the ballot, they
have made public demonstrations of their
sentiments in political matters by "flinging
to the breeze" flags bearing the names of
the candidates for the presidency, of the
party they wished to sustain. In one or
two places, they held political meetings and
discussed the subject and the probability of
its success.
	THE GREAT CYCLONE.—This terrific
wind storm that recently devastated a large
tract of country in the East Indies, com-
pletely submerging three large and four or
five small islands, is one of the most fearful
calamities ever known; and should be an
effectual warning to the living. The loss of
life is estimated by some at about a quarter
of a million. The destruction of property
is incalculable, but added to all this, and
the most heart-sickening, is the famine
which inevitably follows such unlooked-for
occurrences. The atmosphere, too, near
where the dead bodies are, is so polluted
that pestilence is almost sure to result.
