AUTUMN WINDS.

		Air—Autumn Leaves.

Autumn winds, autumn winds,
	That sweep so sadly,
Autumn winds, autumn winds,
	How hollowly ye sigh!
		Your ghostly, mournful music rolls
			Across the stubble lea,
		Like solemn notes the death-bell tolls
			When grand lives cease to be.

Autumn winds, autumn winds,
	Why must ye always mourn,
Autumn winds, autumn winds,
	In wailing billows borne?
	
Autumn winds, autumn winds,
	My spirit with you grieves;
Autumn winds, autumn winds
	Your tale my soul believes—
		This green-robed earth must naked be,
	And life of hopes be shorn,
		And broken hearts as lone as ye
	Thus bitterly must mourn;
Autumn winds, autumn winds,
	Like you of heavenly birth,
Autumn winds, autumn winds,
	They find no home on earth.
