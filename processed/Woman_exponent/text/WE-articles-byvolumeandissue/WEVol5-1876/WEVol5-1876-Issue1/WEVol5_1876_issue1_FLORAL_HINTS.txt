FLORAL HINTS.

	IT is a decided mistake to suppose that
plants are unhealthful in sleeping apart-
ments or sitting rooms. Of course, if the
flowers exhale a strong perfume—such as
that of tuberoses, hyacinths and daphnes—


they make the air too odorous to be desirable
at night; but healthy, growing plants absorb
the carbonic acid in the atmosphere, and
keep it pure and agreeable. They are, in
fact, the best disinfectants that can be em-
ployed.
	ONE of the chief attractions to make a
home really beautiful and refined, and to
speak to the heart, is by means of flowers;
if one cannot have expensive plants, have
those that are simple and easily tended.
Flowers are pleasant to the eye and to the
sense, and inspire the most elevating emo-
tions. There are always happy thoughts
suggested by an association with beau-
tiful flowers. How pleasant it is when sad,
burdened with grief or sorrow, to find the
peace and satisfaction one may always expe-
rience by walking in the garden or grove
where the trees, the shrubs, vines, plants,
the tender sprays and blossoms seem to
soothe and cheer and speak a silent lan-
guage direct to the heart, something thor-
oughly preceptible but yet inexpresssble in
language.
