THE LANGUAGE OF FLOWERS.

A Rose Leaf, I will not trouble you any
	more.
Balm of Gilead, May you be healed.
Basil, I hate you.
Balsam, I am impatient.
Blue bell, Don't bother me.
Candytuft, I am indifferent.
Currant oush, You may kiss me.
Clover, I wish you much joy.
Dandelion, You are a flirt.
Daisy, I will share your sentiments.
Dahlia' Your charms I cannot resist.
Fern, I am sincere.
Geranium Leaf, I am lonely.
Grass, Useful, but not very handsome.
Grass Pink, I wish to gain your affections.
Honeysuckle, I will make you happy.
Hollyhock, you are too sanguine.
Ivy, I can only be your friend, nothing
	more.
Iris, You will please answer this.
Juniper or Cedar, I will protect you.
Lucerne, May you live long and be happy.
Lilac, You are my first and only love.
Morning Glory, Love me.
Marigold, Jealousy: Be very careful.
Mignonette, I love you more for not being
	more handsome.
Nasturtium, I love another better than you.
Oleander, Beware! I watch your conduct.
Peach Blossom, I shall never love another
	as much as you.
Plum Branch, I am independent.
Pansy, Think of me often.
Peppermint, High esteem for you.
Rosebud: White, I am too young to marry.
Rose, Damask, I love, but am too bashful to
	tell you.
Rose, White, Silence! Do not speak of our
	love.
Snapdragon, You are made up of deceit.
Shrub, You shall have justice done you.
Sage, I esteem you highly.
Sweet pea, You are very fickle.
Tansy, I declare war against you.
Violet White, I am innocent.
Violet Blue, I will be faithful till death.
Water Cress, I resign your love—You are
	free.—Ex.


It has been discovered by a French savant that
the rising of cream is quicker, and its volume
greater, the nearer the temperature is to that of
freezing water; and that the yield of butter is
greater, and the skim milk; butter and cheese are
all of better quality under like conditions.
