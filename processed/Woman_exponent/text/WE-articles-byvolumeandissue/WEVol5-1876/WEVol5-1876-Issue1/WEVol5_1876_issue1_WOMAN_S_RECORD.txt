WOMAN'S RECORD.

	MISS Harriet Hosmer, will personally
superintend the placing of her own statues
in the Woman's Pavilion, Philadelphia.
	ANNIE Louise Carey will sing at Bowdo-
in and Bates Commencements, and give
concerts at Boston in July; after which she
will sail again for Europe to fulfill an en-
gagement of one year in St. Petersburg.
	MRS. Secretary Robison is said to be one
of the most highly cultivated singers in
Washington. As a young girl her voice
was one of her chief attractions, and dur-
ing her married life she has applied herself
closely to the cultivation of this accomplish-
ment.
	MISS Blanche Tucker, the Chicago prima
donna, who recently made her debut in opera
in London; and Miss Clara C. Smart, who
made her first appearance in oratorio with
Mlle. Titiens at Boston, Mass. April 12th,
were both formerly of La Crosse, Wiscon-
sin.
	MRS. Caroline B. Winslow M. D. of Wash-
ington, D. C., editor of the Alpha, a paper
in the interest of moral education and so-
cial elevation, has established a Homoepath-
ic Pharmacy, and has a large medical prac-
tice in her profession. Does [-]ot this prove
that woman is capable of performing an im-
mense amount of real physical and mental
work?
	A LADY in Albany has wrought a mag-
nificient banner, which is said to be one of
the most handsome specimens of woman's
work in the Centennial Exposition. It is
seven feet in length and five in width and
worked throughout in a variety of shades
of silk and chenille. The design selected,
was the great seal of the United States,
adopted Jan. 20, 1782; and is said to be
faithfully reproduced with the needle.
