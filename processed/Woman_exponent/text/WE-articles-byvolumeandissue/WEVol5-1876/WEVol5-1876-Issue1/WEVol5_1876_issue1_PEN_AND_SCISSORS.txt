PEN AND SCISSORS.

	A GERMAN paper called the "Fortschritt,"
is pubiished weekly in New York city. It
is edited by a very clever and energetic
German lady, Anna Metz Bylond.
	IF you don't want your wife to bother
you every day with the remark, "Don't for-
get that worsted," just mention to her the
pretty girl who tends the worsted counter
where you got the last.
	"YOU didn't laugh at my stupidity before
we were married; you always said I was a
"duck of a lover," grumbled a complaining
husband. "Yes, that's so," replied the
wife, "and a duck of a lover is almost sure
to make a goose of a husband."
	MRS. Attorney-General Pierrepont wears
pale lemon-colored silk under 'illusion' dot-
ted with small snow-flakes of gold," says a
fashion writer. Society at the capital seems
to have been all illusion of late years, and
flakes of gold dazzling dishonestly have
carried sorrow and shame over the whole
country.
	A CLERGYMAN who was recently discours-
ing upon the alarming increase of intem-
perance astonished his audience by exclaim-
ing: "A young man in my neighborhood
died very suddenly last Sunday while I
was preaching the Gospel in a beastly state
of intoxication." Moral—clergyman should
study grammer.
