A HUSBAND OF THE PERIOD.

Yes, love, I'm breakfasting quite late
	But then, you know, it's Sunday,
And we who work for six long days
	Deserve our comfort one day.
You're rather early home from church;
	I hope you liked the sermon.
That velvet cloak, dear, by the way,
	Looks charming with your ermine.

A chilly morning out of doors:
	I'm glad you didn't wake me/
(Oh, native cutting, mother-wit,
	Pray do not yet forsake me!
She heard me stumble up to bed
	At three o'clock—I'll swear it!)
Dark blue is just your color, dear;
	You always ought to wear it.

I've eaten very little? Yes,
	It's nearly time for dinner:
This cutlet, too, seems rather flat.
(The truth, as I'm a sinner!)
A bloodshot look about my eyes?
	My fingers in a flutter?
All fancy, love—I feel superb.
	(That nauseating butter!)

You waited up last night till twelve?
	For that, dear, I should scold you
Of monthly meetings at the club
	How often have I told you?
This waiting—had I not a key,
	Perhaps I shouldn't mind it;
But having one—By Jove, I took
	An hour, last night, to find it!

I left the hall door open? Who
	Insinuates that slander?
It's falsehood base and infamous,
	If you'll excuse my candor.
Why, love, you trust mere servants' talk
	I cannot well conjecture,
(She seats herself! The storm has burst!
	Now comes my curtain lecture.)
