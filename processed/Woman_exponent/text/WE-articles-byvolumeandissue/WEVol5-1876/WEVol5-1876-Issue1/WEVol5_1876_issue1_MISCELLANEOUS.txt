MISCELLANEOUS.

	IT is a remarkable fact that every day in
the week is observed by some nation for the
public celebration of religious services.
Sunday is devoted by the Christians, Mon-
day by the Greeks, Tuesday by the Persians,
Wednesday by the Assyrians, Thursday by
the Egyptians, Friday by the Turks, Satur-
day by the Jews.
	THE London Pall Mall Gazette, speaking
of the American girl in England, says:—
Like the wind, she goeth where she listeth;
but every man knoweth whence she com-
eth. She arouses the hostility of English
matrons the moment she sets foot on British
soil. Few of them are aware that she has
been purposely trained to self-reliance and
self-assertion, like her brother, from infan-
cy. Such a training shows itself in her
manner, which grates as harshly on British
feelings as her voice too often does on Brit-
ish ears.
