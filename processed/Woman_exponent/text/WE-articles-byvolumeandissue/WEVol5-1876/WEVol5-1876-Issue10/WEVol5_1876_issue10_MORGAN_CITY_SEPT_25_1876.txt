MORGAN CITY, Sept. 25, 1876.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Allow me through your columns, to add
my mite to the already large stock of news
and interesting reports you deal out to us
semi-monthly.
	Yours is a noble mission indeed, to carry
the expressions of love and gratitude from
thousands of hearts to each other, all over
Utah.
	Our little city was honored, on the 19th
inst., by a visit from two "bright stars"
from the Relief Society Constellation: Sis-
ters E. S. Barney and E. Howard, who
spoke words of instruction and comfort.
The Lord poured His Spirit on them in
such abundance that they fed our hungry
souls with the food they most needed. Their
texts were: Education, watch-care of our
girls, plural marriage, care of the sick,
word of wisdom, womanly dignity, the im-
portance of availing ourselves of the right
of the ballot, folly and extravagance of the
fashions of the day. Showing to us that
the mission of the Relief Society is not only
to relieve the poor, but to introduce and
cultivate everything that tends to the ex-
altation of woman and the saving of souls.
Seri-culture, straw-braiding, and all kinds of
home industries were highly recommended.
	We feel to bless them for their kind and
cheering words; and I say God bless our
dear Sister E. R. Snow, who presides over
these institutions.
	Your Sister, etc,
		ESTHER E. E. FRANCIS,
			Sec.
