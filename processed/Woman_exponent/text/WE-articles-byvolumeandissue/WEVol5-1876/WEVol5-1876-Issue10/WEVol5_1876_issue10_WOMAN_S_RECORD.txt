WOMAN'S RECORD.

	MRS. D. W. Rogers is superintendent of
schools in Danby, Conn., and is the first
woman that ever held public office in that
town.
	MISS Helen Potter, the reader, is doing an
excellent business professionally, her in-
come for the season it is said will be about
$15,000.
	MRS. A. J. Duniway, of Portland, Oregon,
and editor of the "New North West," de-
livered an earnest and characteristic speech
at the Woman Suffrage Convention at Phil-
adelphia on taxation without representation
and other subjects.
	MISS Abbie G. Ketchum of Marshall,
Michigan, sailed from San Francisco Oct. 1,
for China, to commence missionary work in
that country. She had the congratulations
and earnest blessings of t[-]ousands to follow
her as encouragement in her labors of de-
votion and self-sacrifice.
	MISS Lydia Becker, the London advocate
of Woman suffrage, in a recent speech at
the excursion of the British Association,
gave a happy description of the visible
change and marked improvement in regard
to the position of women at society gather-
ings. On the last occasion when the British
Association had visited Glasgow, the gen-
tlemen had been invited to luncheon and
the ladies left outside, the gentlemen had
pretended to have more pleasure in toasting
the ladies in their absence, but it was real-
ly more satisfactory to respond to these
toasts in person, and contributed more to
the enjoyment of the c[-]mpany.
