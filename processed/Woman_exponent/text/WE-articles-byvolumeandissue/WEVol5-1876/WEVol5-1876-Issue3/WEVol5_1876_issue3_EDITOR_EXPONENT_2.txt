EDITOR EXPONENT:
	This letter is from the fifteenth son of
Parley P. Pratt, deceased, and as I read it I
was reminded of the EXPONENT, and
thought that the contents might interest its
many readers.
	Some years ago when this lad was a daily
listener to my instructions, I told him he
was then qualifying himself for a mission-
ary, like unto his father; and with what
pleasure I have watched him developing
into manhood and nobility.
	How pleasant it is for a teacher to see the
results of her arduous labors as she mingles
in the society she has helped to form!
	That germ implanted by the teacher in
the heart of her pupil, is reflected like the
dew shed upon the foliage; it is refreshed
and beautified, and lends its enchantment
to the eye of the beholder.
	Then, my fellow laborers, do not be dis-
couraged, but continue in assisting to im-
plant the germ of true usefulness in the
hearts and heads of the rising generation,
and you will reap your reward, for seed
sown upon good ground will increase an
hundred fold.
