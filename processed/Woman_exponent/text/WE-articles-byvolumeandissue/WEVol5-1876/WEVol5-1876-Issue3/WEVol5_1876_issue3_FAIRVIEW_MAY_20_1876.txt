			FAIRVIEW, May 20, 1876.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The Young Ladies' Retrenchment Asso-
ciation in Fairview, was organized August
7th, 1875, with the following officers:
MISS MARY JONES, President,
"	LEVEE T. TERRY,			}
"	JULIA SANDERSON,		}
"	SELINA STEWART,		} Counselors.
MRS. R[-]BECCA TERRY,		}
MISS TRYPHENA COX,			}
" 	ELLEN ALRED,			}
MISS MARGARET ANDERSON,	} Secretaries
"	ELISE TUCKER,			}
	We enjoy ourselves at our meetings,
which we hold once a week. We have
girls from 10 years of age and over and
all get up and speak. The younger girls
seem to take more interest in our meetings
than the older ones. We remained under
the above organization until our beloved
president was called on the Arizona mission.
It was with the greatest regret that we
parted with her, but she had a greater mis-
sion to fulfil.
	We were re-organized Feb. 13, 1876, with
the following officers;
	MRS. JULIA RIGBY, President,
MISS NANCY SANDERS,		}
"	TRYPHENA COX,			} Counselors,
"	SELINA STEWART,		}
"	LEVEE TERRY,			}
MISS MARGARET ANDERSON,	} Secretary,
"	ELISE TUCKER,			}
	We take the EXPONENT in our society
this year, and it is a great help to us, and is
read with much interest. We are pleased
to hear from other associations, and thought
perhaps you would like to hear from us.
We are sometimes visited by some of the
members of the Relief Society, and they
always give us good instructions. We have
formed no Resolutions as yet, but all are
striving to do right; and I hope and pray
that all the Retrenchment Associations will
accomplish the end for which they were or-
ganized:
		I remain
			Your Sister in the Gospel,
