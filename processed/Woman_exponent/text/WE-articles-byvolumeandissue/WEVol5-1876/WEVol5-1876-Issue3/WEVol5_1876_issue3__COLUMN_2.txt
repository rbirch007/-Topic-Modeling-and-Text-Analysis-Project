[COLUMN 2]

	Brothers Barry Wild, I. M. Coombs, J.
Forsgreen, J. L. Townsend, Bishop Tanner
and Bishop Halliday applauded the sisters
for their talent and ingenuity; the needle
and straw work, it was thought, could not
be excelled. All acknowledged there was
talent in Payson.
	There were different kinds of fancy work,
knitted, netted, and crochet tidies, bed-
spreads, crochet and woven, besides ele-
gant quilts. A beautiful specimen of pen-
manship, by J. L. Townsend, also an eagle
and other birds preserved by him.
	Brother Townsend desired an interest in
the sisters' prayers, as he left Payson for
St. George the following morning, being
called upon to assist the carpenters on the
Temple.
	The Annual Report was read by the sec-
retary, which showed that we had received
during the year $131.51, and disbursed
$156.63.
	A piece composed by Mrs Anna Taylor
was read by President Jane Simons. A
piece by Mrs. Jennie Cowan and one by
Mrs. Rebecca Daniels were read by the sec-
retary.
	A good spirit prevails. We have good
meetings, and good faithful sisters in our
midst, who are always ready to be one with
our President and her counselors. In our
meetings the sisters often thank God that
they are privileged to live in this day; real-
izing that a great and important time is
near; hoping to keep humble and prayerful
that we may endure and be numbered
with the faithful. We realize the truth of
Brother Hallidays words, that Eliza R.
Snow's instructions were true, that our mis-
sion was not only to administer to the poor,
but strive to save souls.
	The EXPONENT is a welcome visitor
among us; we pray for its success, and may
God bless and aid you ln your labors.
	Your sister in the gospel,
