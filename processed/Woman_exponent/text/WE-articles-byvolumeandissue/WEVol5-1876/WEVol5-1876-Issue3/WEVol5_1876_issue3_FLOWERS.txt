FLOWERS.

	THE PEBBLES in our path weary us, and
make us footsore more than the rocks, which
require only a bold effort to surmount.
	TAKE ALL sorrow out of life, and you
take away all richness and depth and ten-
derness. Sorrow is the furnace that melts
selfish hearts together in love.
	THE MOST important lesson in life is to
know how to be happy with our own home,
our own comforts, and to share our affections
with those of our own household, not to re-
fine away happiness by thinking that that
which is good might be better.
	THERE ARE none living who do not, in a
greater or less degree, have an influence
over the happiness of others. The sense of
contributing to the pleasure of others aug-
ments our own happiness. Unselfishness,
Christian charity, and loving kindness are
sunbeams of the soul.
