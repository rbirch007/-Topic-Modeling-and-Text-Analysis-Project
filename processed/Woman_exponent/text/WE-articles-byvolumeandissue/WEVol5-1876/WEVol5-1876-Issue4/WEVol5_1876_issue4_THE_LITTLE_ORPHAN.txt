THE LITTLE ORPHAN,

	As I bent myself to tuck the bed-clothes
more closely over an orphan child, a thrill
passed through my mind—do it well, for
you do it for another whose hands are fold-
ed to return to her mother dust. Speak
kindly, for those lips are sealed, and that
heart beats not who would wish you so to
do; that precious mother lives in the sun-
shine of God, for she was a Saint. The
father, far away and weary, is perhaps
wrapped in the arms of sleep; he too is
dead to the wants of the little ones. I stand
alone to act for them both. May God give
me wisdom to do it well. I looked at the
lone one sleeping and thought: You too are
dead at this moment to all sorrow! Absent
father! Departed mother! I live—precious
moment—striving to do all things well, and
tuck carefully the frail wrappings of the
bed-covering, but take care to place gently
but firmly the garments of charity and
kindness around the orphan, for his soul
will live, when earthly wrappings are no
more needed.
					DIANTHA BAKER.
