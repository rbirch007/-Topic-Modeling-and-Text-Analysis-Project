	MORONI, SANPETE Co., May 29, 1876.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We held a meeting on the 28th of April,
in connection with the Retrenchment So-
ciety. Our Bishop and Counselors attended
our meeting and gave good instruction.
Bishop Bradley said that we had a great
work to do and a very short time allotted us

[Column 2]
to work out our salvation. The world know
that there is something the matter, but
they do not know that the Lord is going to
set up his kingdom on the earth, and some
of the Latter-day Saints do not realize it;
he thought the sisters had done exceedingly
well, as money is very scarce in this part.
Brothers Swenson, Hardy and Christiansen
gave good instruction to the young sisters;
said they ought to be very careful in their
choice of husbands, or they may be carried
away from the right path.
	Our Society was organized January, 1868.
Donations in all to January, 1876:
	To Emigration........................  $165.24
	To the Poor............................... 480.00
	To the Temple.......................... 125.40
	To help the missionaries........    65.00
				Total...................  835.64
Besides owning shares in the Co-op. Insti-
tution.
	Our meetings are well attended, we take
great interest in them; we meet every two
weeks and bear our testimony of the latter-
day work. We are thankful that we hear
the words, through a prophet, of salvation,
exaltation and eternal life. Jesus has said,
"I am the Good Shepherd, I know my
sheep; a stranger they will not follow, but
will flee from him." It is our earnest de-
sire to oppose every evil influence that comes
in contact with the Holy Priesthood of
God, and show by our walk and conduct
that we have received from our Father in
heaven that Holy Spirit that will guide us
into all truth.
	With an earnest desire for the success of
the EXPONENT, I remain
			Yours truly,
