NOTES AND NEWS.

	A CHART of the moon six feet in diameter
is soon to be issued in lithograph in Berlin.
The original chart was drawn by Dr.
Scmhidt, Director of the Astronomical Ob-
servatory at Athens, and is the result of
thirty years' labor. Thirty astronomical
observations were in some instances requir-
ed to complete a small fraction of the chart.
The Prussian government purchased the
drawing for $10,000.
	THE GREAT Spanish orator Emilio Caste-
lar, accompanied by Dr. Alberto De Abnis-
quita and Jose Guarandi, will arrive in this
country to visit the Centennial Exposition
about the end of this month, as representa-
tives of the Spanish press. Castelar has
promised to deliver an oration in Philadel-
phia to his countrymen.
	SECRETARY SEWARD began an auto-biog-
raphy but stopped at 1834. His son, Fred-
eric W. Seward, has completed the work


by giving a full account of his father's life
after that time. The book will probably
possess great historical interest, and will be
published this summer.
	THE "WOMAN'S Hotel," in New York,
designed by Mr. A. T. Stewart for the ac-
commodation of working-girls, is rapidly
approaching completion, and will probably
be ready for occupancy early next spring.
The building has five hundred rooms, which
will be neatly furnished and supplied with
gas and steam heat. It is the intention to
have good meals furnished at cost prices,
that is about fifteen cents apiece.
