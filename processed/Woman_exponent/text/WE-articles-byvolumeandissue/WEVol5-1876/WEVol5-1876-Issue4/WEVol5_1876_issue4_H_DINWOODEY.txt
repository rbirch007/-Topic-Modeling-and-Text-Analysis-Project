BABIES' LEGS.—Bow-legs and knock-
knees are among the common deformities of
humanity; it is generally supposed these
results are in consequence of the child
having been put upon its feet too soon. An
eminent physician in Massachusetts thinks
differently; he attributes bow-legs to the
habit some babies delight in of rubbing the
sole of one foot against that of the other;
he says he has noticed some babies go to
sleep with the soles of their feet pressed to-
gether, it is particularly when their feet
are naked that they enjoy this; they sel-
dom attempt it when in socks or shoes; the
remedy, then, is to keep the baby's soles
covered. Knock-knees the doctor ascribes
to the childish habit of sleeping on the side
with one knee tucked into the hollow be-
hind the other; he says he has found that
where one leg has been bowed in more than
another, the child has always slept on one
side and the upper member has been the
one deformed. The preventive would be
to pad the inside of the knees so as to keep
them apart, and let the limbs grow freely
their own way.
