			KANOSH CITY,  May 28, 1876.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We are not a very large community, but
as we read so many encouraging letters in
the Exponent, it seems to inspire us to
emerge from our obscurity and "let our
light shine."
	Our Relief Society is small, compared
with many, but there is a good spirit pre-
vailing among the sisters, and all seem
willing to assist in pushing forward this
glorious work. We hold meetings once
every fortnight, working meetings and tes-
timony meetings alternately, and although
we seem to progress slowly, yet I trust
surely.
	Our President, Sister Adelie Kimball,
is very zealous aad persevering, and we all
like her very much.
	It cheers us to read so many encouraging
letters in the EXPONENT, from the different
settlements, and it is clearly evident that a
good spirit is manifesting itself among our
sisters in their different fields of labor, and
that all are striving to assist in building up
the kingdom of God, which kingdom is to
be established, nevermore to be thrown
down.
	Praying that the blessing of God may at-
tend the earnest endeavors of all who strive
to live their religion, believe me to remain
		Your sister in the Gospel,
					ADELAIDE TRIPPETT,
								Secretary.


VANDERBILT University, at Nashville,
Tennessee, has received another gift from
Commodore Vanderbilt of $500,000. The
sum total given by him to that institution
is $1,000,000.
	OF the 16 University Races, Harvard has
won 9, Yale 3, and other colleges 4.
