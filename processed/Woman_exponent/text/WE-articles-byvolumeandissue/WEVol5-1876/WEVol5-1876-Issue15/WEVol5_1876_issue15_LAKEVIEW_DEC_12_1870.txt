			LAKEVIEW, Dec. 12, 1870.
EDITORS EXPONENT:
	The members of the Relief and Retrench-
ment Societies are generally all alive to
their duties, we each have our meetings
every two weeks; we have made a com-
mencement towards the laying up of grain.
The sisters of our ward held a social party
last Friday evening, nearly all the members
of our ward attended. We all partook of
supper at four o'clock which had been pre-
pared for the occasion; after the tables were
cleared away, we enjoyed ourselves in danc-
ing, singing and also had some very ap-
propriate speeches from our invited guests;
among whom were Sister Mary Micklejohn,
President of the Relief Society of Tooele,
Bros. Robert Micklejohn and Abel Parker,
who are acting presidents of Tooele in place
of our much respected Bishop who has gone
on a mission; also Bro. Hugh Gowans who
is acting as Bishop, and Bro. Delamare.
We all enjoyed ourselves very much, and
the party closed at 11 o'clock. We had no
round dances, never have had any since our
Retrenchment Association was organized
by Sisters E. R. Snow, M. I. Horne and
Elizabeth Howard, nearly three years ago.
						I. G. M.
