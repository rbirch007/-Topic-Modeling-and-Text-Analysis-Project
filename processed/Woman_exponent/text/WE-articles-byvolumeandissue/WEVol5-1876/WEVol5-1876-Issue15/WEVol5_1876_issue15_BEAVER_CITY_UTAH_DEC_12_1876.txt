		BEAVER CITY, UTAH, Dec. 12, 1876.
EDITORS EXPONENT:
	We enjoyed a rare treat during the recent
visit of Mrs. M. I. Horne. She held three
meetings in this place which were well at-
tended by both young and old of both sex-
es. Her instructions, principally, were to
the ladies of the Relief Societies and Re-
trenchment Associations, but were of a
character to benefit all who heard them.
	The subjects most spoken upon were the
necessity of laying up grain, sustaining the
EXPONENT, assisting in the publication of
the Woman's Book, and patronizing home-
manufacture. She advised the sisters to in-
terest themselves in silk-culture, and gave
good counsel on many subjects pertaining
to our every-day duties.
	Mrs. Caroline Shipp, also from Salt Lake
City, was present and made a few excellent
remarks.
	There was a change in the organization
of our Relief Society last June, occasioned
by the resignation of the President, Mrs.
Lucinda Houd. The organization now
stands as follows:
