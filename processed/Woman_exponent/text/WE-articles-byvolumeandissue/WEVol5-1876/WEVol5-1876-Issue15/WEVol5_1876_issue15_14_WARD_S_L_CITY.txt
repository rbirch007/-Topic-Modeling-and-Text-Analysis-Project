			14 WARD, S. L. CITY,
					Dec. 22, 1876.
EDITORS' EXPONENT:
	Our esteemed Pres. Mrs. M. I. Horne has
requested that I send you a brief report of
our ninth annual meeting which was held
at the Society Hall, Dec. 13, 1876.
	More than the usual number of sisters
were in attendance, and we were also hon-
ored with the presence of Bishop Thomas
Taylor, and Counselor G. H. Taylor; after
singing prayer, and roll call, the Secretary
read the Annual Report as follows:
		Donations during the year.
	Cash............................................ $ 46 55
	Sundries.....................................     61 37
	Articles sold...............................      42 60
	Loan of Temple Clothes...........      38 70
	Rent of part of our building......     55 15
	Interest on money invested......     25 50
								  ———
	Total.............................................   269 87
			Disbursed during year.
	To the needy................................ 159 78 1/2
	Materials for work, etc...............	33 50
	Washing Temple Clothes...........	17 90
	Repairing Hall after Explosion,           14 60
	Book, "Life of Brigham
		Young"...............................	          3 00
	Wheat Central Committee.........	  5 00
									———
	Total.......................................... 233 78 1/2
	Balance on hand.......................	36 08 1/2
	We have donated to the Temple in quilts,
	stockings, shirts, clothing etc...... $	77 25
	To Woman's Commission Store..	 7 65
	We are also making preparations to in-
vest seventy-five dollars in the purchase of
wheat.
	After the Report had been unanimously
accepted, Prest. Mrs. Horne addressed the
meeting in her usual earnest and energetic
manner, upon storing our grain, the Wo-
man's Book, and other subjects of business.
	Bishop Taylor being called upon, ex-
pressed himself pleased to meet with us, al-
so pleased with our reports and method of
doing business. Thought the storing of
grain a very wise movement, and proffered
room in the basement of the Ward Hall for
our bins. Said we had his best feelings and
confidence and that he would assist us all in
his power. Counseled us to be temperate
in all our movements, even in our sympathy
for the poor, but to help and bless all who
are really needy.
	Elder G. H. Taylor and Sister W. East
also made some timely and interesting re-
marks.
	The same spirit of unity and good feel-
ing prevailed which has always character-
ized the meetings of our society.
	That the blessing of God our Heavenly
Father may be upon you to assist you in
your high and holy calling, is the prayer of
your sister &c.
				ELMINA S. TAYLOR,
								Sec'y.
