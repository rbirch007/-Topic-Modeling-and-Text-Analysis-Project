			R. S. REPORTS.

			ALPINE CITY, Dec. 19th, 1876.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Desiring to be one with our sisters, I
write a few lines to the EXPONENT, that
you may know the intentions of our Relief
Society in regard to the saving and storing
of grain. The sisters are responding to the
call by donating liberally of the small
amount of means at their command. It is
the intention of the sisters, as fast as means
can be obtained for this purpose in the So-
ciety, to use the same for buying grain.
The sisters are interested, and desire to
make the business a successful one.
	We remain your sisters in the covenant,
	Margaret McCullough, Prest., Ann Wal-
ton, Angela Vance, Counselors.
