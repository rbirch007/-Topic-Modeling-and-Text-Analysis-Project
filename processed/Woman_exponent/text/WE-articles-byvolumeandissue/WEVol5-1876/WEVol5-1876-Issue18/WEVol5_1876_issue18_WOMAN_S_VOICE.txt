WOMAN'S VOICE.

	Through your columns I desire to say a
few words to your readers, and more espe-
cially to the young, for upon them will rest
a large portion of the Latter-day work. I
pray that a portion of the Holy Spirit may
accompany my words that they may reach
the hearts of those who shall read them.
	Young people, be obedient to your teach-
ers and leaders, and I pray the Lord to
bless you with wisdom and power to bear
a faithful and earnest testimony to the


cause you have espoused, before all people
whenever you may have opportunity given
you; and our heavenly Father will bless
you with more wisdom and knowledge, to
live your holy religion purely before God;
and angels will rejoice over you. I am a
mother, and I feel to offer a prayer for the
rising generation, and that they may more
fully realize the importance of the days in
which we are living. Great events are
about to transpire, for which it is necessary
we become prepared, temporally and spirit-
ually. My dear young friends, let us strive
to live so that we may be prepared to be-
hold our Redeemer with joy, for the time
of His coming draws near. O what man-
ner of a people ought we to be in name and
in works? Truly Latter-day Saints.
	JEMINA BROWN.
S. L. City, 20th Ward.
