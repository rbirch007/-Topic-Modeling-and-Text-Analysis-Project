	CEDAR CITY, IRON Co., Jan. 18, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT.
	In compliance with your request to the
Relief Societies, I feel it my duty to for-
ward a report of the steps taken by us in re-
gard to the grain question. We have not
made much progress so far. A committee
of three sisters have been appointed who
will no doubt faithfully discharge the du-
ties devolving upon them in this cause,
feeling as we all should the responsibility
of doing our utmost in complying with the
call made by the president of our church.
Our Bishop has kindly allowed us the priv-
ilege of storing what grain we can get this
year separately under our own care in the
Tithing Office, until we can build a store-
house of our own, which we intend doing
as soon as practicable. Upon the resigna-
tion of our beloved President Mrs. Whitta-
ker, whose death a few months ago was so
deeply regretted by all, and her counselors
a year ago last May; the Society was re-or-
ganized of which you were duly informed
at the time; under the present officers, viz.,
Mrs. Sarah Uric as President; Mrs. Mary
Ann Lunt as first, and Mrs. Sage Jones as
second counselor, the Society is in a pros-
perous condition, the members being alive
to their duties and trying with God's help
to forward His work to the best of their
abilities.
	Wishing prosperity to the EXPONENT and
a "Happy New Year" to all.
			JANE H. PARRY. Ass't.
							Sec'y.
