				OBITUARY.

	DOLLY ANNIS, one of the faithful mothers
in Israel, died on the first instant, in the Six-
teenth Ward of this city of general debility
and old age. She was born June 10, 1800; be-
ing seventy six years, seven months, and
twenty-two days old at the time of her death.
Sister Annis was born in Vermont. Her
maiden name was Green. When the Gospel
of Jesus Christ first found her, she was living
with her first husband whose name was Dan-
iels, and who was the father of her children.
She received and embraced the truth, but Mr.
Daniels rejected it. In consequence of this
difference of opinion, Sister Annis suffered
much persecution; and finally for the sake of
gathering with the Saints, she left her home,
companion and children, subsequently mar-
ried Brother Annis who soon after died a
faithful member of the church. Afterwards
working her own way and paying her own ex-
penses; she came to Utah to finish her days
among the people she loved. She was a warm
friend, a true philanthropist and an energetic
advocate of the principles of truth.
