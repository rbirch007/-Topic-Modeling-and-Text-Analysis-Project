SAMARIA. MALAD, Jan. 22nd, 1876.
	Our Society is doing well. We had a
special meeting on Saturday last, for the
purpose of donating towards building the
Temple, and the sisters have promised one
hundred and forty six dollars, and two hun-
dred and fifteen lbs of butter to be paid
this year, and I consider they have donated
liberally. The brethren have done well too;
on Sunday, the 14th inst., they had a meet-
ing, Bishop Daniels presiding. They donated
for the Temple one thousand dollars. Forty
families are in this settlement, so I think
we have done very well, we have also
built a new meeting house this winter, so
we are progressing as fast as circumstances
will permit. On Feb. 14th we will have
been organized two years; we have forty-
two members in our Society.
	MARY D. PRUE,
		Secretary.
