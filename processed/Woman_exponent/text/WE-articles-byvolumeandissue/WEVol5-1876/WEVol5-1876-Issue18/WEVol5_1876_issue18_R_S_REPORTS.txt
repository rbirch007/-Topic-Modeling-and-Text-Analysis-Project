			R. S. REPORTS.

	At the General Retrenchment Meeting
in the Fourteenth Ward Assembly Rooms,
Saturday, Feb. 3, 1877, there was a very
good attendance. President Mrs. M. I.
Horne presided. After the usual exercises,
the reading of the minutes from the various
wards concluded, Mrs. Horne addressed the
assembly; her remarks were wise and judi-
cious, and appropriate to the times in which
we are living. She referred to the position
in which prayer should be offered, thought
it was always most appropriate to kneel, but
it was not at all times convenient, yet the
head should be bowed and the thoughts
concentrated and fixed upon the Lord while
the blessing has invoked. She also spoke
of partaking of the Sacrament in a proper
manner, which should certainly be observed.
Counselor Miss Snow spoke upon the sub-
jects of home-industries, the spiritual culti-
vation of the young and gave many impor-
tant and interesting ideas. Counselor Mrs.
Young spoke upon the silk-culture and its
adaptability to the climate of Utah and the
circumstances of the people; that it would
be a resource for means which could be
made profitable, if once it could become
thoroughly established. Mrs. E. S. Barney,
Mrs. W. East, Mrs. M. A. E. Watmough
spoke upon things and principles which per-
tain to the building up of the kingdom, and
many of the sisters present bore a faithful
testimony. An excellent spirit pervaded
the minds of all.
