FLOWERS.

	Every human soul has germs of some
flowers within, and they would open if they
could only find sunshine and free air to ex-
pand in.
	BEAUTY is admired, talent adored, but
virtue is a woman's crown. With it the
poor are rich; without it, the rich are poor.
It walks through life upright, and never
hides its head for high or low.
	IT cannot be too often repeated that luxu-
ries not necessities, bring ruin. We are
ruined, not by what we really want but by
what we think we do; therefore never go
abroad in search of your wants, if they are
really wants, they will come home in search
of you, for he who buys what he does not
want, will soon want what he cannot buy
	IN a world like the present, one of the
grandest occupations is that of giving con-
dolence. We ought all of us to study this
holy science of imparting comfort to the
troubled. There are many who could look
around upon some of their best friends who
wish them well, and are very intelligent
and yet to be able to say to them in days of
trouble, "miserable comforters are ye all."
