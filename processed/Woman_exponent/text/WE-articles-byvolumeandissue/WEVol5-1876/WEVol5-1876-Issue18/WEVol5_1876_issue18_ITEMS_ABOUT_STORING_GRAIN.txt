ITEMS ABOUT STORING GRAIN.

			MILL CREEK, Feb. 1st, 1877.
EDITOR EXOONENT:
	We as a branch of the Grain Department 
desire to unite our efforts in gathering and
storing grain. Mrs. Mary McAlister has
been elected Chairman, and Mrs. Elizabeth
Carlisle Secretary, and as we are widely
scattered a visiting committee of eight wo-
men have been appointed. We formed this
Grain Committee December 19th 1876, and
now have on hand nearly one hundred
bushels of wheat and thirteen dollars to
buy more with. The Young Ladies, Re-
trenchment Association donated eleven dol-
lars. Considering everything we think we
have made a good beginning, and pray that
we may continue to persevere. Bishop
Miller has given us great encouragement
and good counsel; he gives us a bin in his
mill for to store our grain securely, and we
can't help but go forward and do our duty,
when we have the approbation and help of
our Bishop, and having a testimony within
us that we are doing the will of our Heav-
enly Father
	Praying for the prosperity of the Grain
Department and the unity of every good
brother and sister in this Latter-day work,
I remain yours etc.
