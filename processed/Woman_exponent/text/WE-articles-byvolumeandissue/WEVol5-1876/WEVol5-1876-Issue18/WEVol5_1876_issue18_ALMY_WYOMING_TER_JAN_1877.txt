		ALMY, WYOMING Ter. Jan. 1877
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Knowing that yourself and readers feel a
kindly interest in the welfare and progress
of the women of our church, I write a few
lines to inform you that directed by proper
authority, Bro. Samuel Pike, the president
of the branch has organized a Relief So-
ciety in this place, over which sister Emma
Pike is selected to preside; Sisters Beve-
ridge and stoddard as counselors, and sister
Crowther as Secretary and Treasurer, to be
assisted by Sister Stoddard. This place is
not a permanent home for the Saints, but
the Coal Mines are here and those who
come here are only migratory; this is why
we have not had a society started before.
There are a great many families here and of
course a good number of good women
among them and we think much good can
be done by bringing them together, mak-
ing them better acquainted with each other
and developing talents of various kinds as
well as providing a fund for the safety of

[Column 3]
the poor, sick, and aged. Quite a number
intend to take your excellent paper.
	The duties of the Society are new to
most of us but we intend to do the best we
can to fulfill them.
	Desiring an interest in the prayers of our
sisters, we are,
			Respectfully Yours,
				EMMA PIKE Pres't.
