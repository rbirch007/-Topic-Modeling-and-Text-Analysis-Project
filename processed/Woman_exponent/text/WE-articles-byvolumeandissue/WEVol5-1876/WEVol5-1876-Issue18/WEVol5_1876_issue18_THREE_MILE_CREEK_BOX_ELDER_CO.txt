THREE MILE CREEK, BOX ELDER CO.,
	Jan. 27, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I will endeavor to give a brief report of
our Society here. We were organized into
a branch of the Relief Society, July 29th,
1875, by sisters Eliza Snow, Harriet Snow
and Pres. A. O. Perry, as follows:
	Mrs. Jane Perry, President,
	"	Mary Young,
	"	Mary Perry,	Counselor ,
	"	Rebecca Thorn, Sec'y,
	"	Lucy Woodard, Assistant,
	Miss Francessa Campkin, Treasurer.
	There are about fifty members enrolled;
we meet every two weeks and enjoy a good 
degree of the Spirit of the Lord; our meet-
ings are well attended considering the
scattered condition of our little settlement.
Our Society is in a flourishing condition and
we can truly say, our Association has been
the means of uniting us together as a band
of sisters. Although we have not much
means we are blest with health and willing
to perform our part in every good work
requested by our president. I write also to
inform you of our success in tne grain
movement, we have nearly one hundred
bushels of wheat now on hand and intend
adding to it as fast as our limited means
will allow. Through the courtesy of Bro.
Richard Thorn we received storage for the
winter. We desire by the help of our
Father in Heaven to exert our energies to
secure all the grain we can, and be one
with our sisters in the organization in car-
rying out all the counsels of our leaders.
	Hoping for the success of your valuable
paper, we remain as ever your sisters.
