					CHORUS.

	Good girls rally, sustain the cause of truth,
	Remember your Creator in the days of early youth.

I'll never condescend to be a coquette or a flirt,
Allow my skirts when walking out to trail in filth and 
	dirt.
To chastity, and modesty I'll ever be inclined
Avoiding rude loud laughter which denotes the vacant
	mind.

I will not use the powder puff, nor daub my face with
	paint,
And never deign to eat or drink what would my system
	taint;
I never will be found among young men who smoke or
	drink;
Avoiding as associates rude girls who leer and wink.

The cause of God I will sustain with undivided heart,
All evil passions will restrain, from sinful ways depart:
At all times good examples set whate'er my lot may be,
Whene'er my pathway is beset, from all temptation flee
									WM. WILLIS
	Dedicated to the Young Ladies' Retrenchment Socie-
ties, Dec. 13th, 1876;
