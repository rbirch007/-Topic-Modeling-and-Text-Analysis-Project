			R. S. REPORTS.

	The General Retrenchment meeting, held
in the Fourteenth Ward Assembly Rooms,
Saturday, Jan. 6th, 1877, was the first of
the year 1877, and the sisters who spoke
were most earnest in their expressions of
renewed interest on the subject of advance-
ment in all good works and home improve-
ments. President Mrs. M. I. Horne pre-
sided, and the instruction and testimony
given through those who spoke were worthy
of the time and institution, and we sin,
cerely trust may be made applicable in the
lives of all who love virtue and righteous-
ness and are seeking after eternal treasures.

	The General Retrenchment meeting held
in the Fourteentn Ward Assembly Rooms
Saturday, Jan. 20, was crowded almost to
overflowing; there was not a vacant seat in
the room. Counselor Zina D. Young pre-
sided over the meeting, (President Mrs.
M. I. Horne being too ill to attend,) and
gave some excellent instruction. Counsel-
ors E. R. Snow, B. W. Smith, and E. How-
ard, Mrs. W. East, Mrs. E. B. Wells, Mrs.
E. Tuft, Mrs. M. E. Watmough and others
all spoke upon those things which are al-
ways inspiring and encouraging to the Lat-
ter-day Saints.
