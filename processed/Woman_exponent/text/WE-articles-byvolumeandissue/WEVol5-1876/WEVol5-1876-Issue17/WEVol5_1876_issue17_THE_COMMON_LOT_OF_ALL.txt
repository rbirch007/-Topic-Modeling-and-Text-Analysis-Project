THE COMMON LOT OF ALL.

	There is no heart but hath its inner anguish,
		There is no eye but hath by tears been wet,
	There is no soul but hath been known to languish
		O'er hours of darkness it can ne'er forget.

	There is no cheek, however bright its roses,
		But faded buds beneath its hue are hid;
	No eye that in its dewy light reposes,
		But broken starbeams tremble 'neath its lid.

	There is no voice with laughter ringing,
		However light and gay its words may be,
	But hath in hours of agony been seeking
		For power to solve its life's deep mystery.

	We are sojourners in this land of dreaming,
		Hands meet, hearts join, and eye to eye replies;
	Nor deem we that below a brow all beaming
		A hidden grief all unrevealed lies.
											E. R.
S. L. City, Jan. 5, 1876.
