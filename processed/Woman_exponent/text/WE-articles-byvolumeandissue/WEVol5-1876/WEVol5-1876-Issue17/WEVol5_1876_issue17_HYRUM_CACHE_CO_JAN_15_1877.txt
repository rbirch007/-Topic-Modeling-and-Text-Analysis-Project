		HYRUM, CACHE CO, Jan. 15, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	By request of our worthy president, Mrs.
C. Liljenquist, I address a line to you to in-
form you that we are still trying to honor
our calling as a branch of the Rellef Society,
and to do all in our power to assist the
needy, and comfort the afflicted either by
word or deed. We are one with our sisters
in Utah to follow out the counsel of our
honored President with regard to storing
up our wheat. Bro. Ira Allen was kind
enough to proffer us a room in his granary,
a committe was appointed who have already
bought between one hundred and one hun-
dred and fifty bushels of wheat and are still
busy. They are going to visit the brethren
and solicit from them according to counsel.
I am happy to say that our Bisbop and
many of the brethren are willing to assist
us in procuring and storing our grain, and
the sisters as a majority are united in this
important movement. We are also called
upon to donate means towards assisting in
building the Temple; and it is our desire
to be always ready to respond to the differ-
ent calls made by the servants of the Lord.
In conclusion I will say that the EXPO-
NENT is a very welcome visitor. It is ap-
preciated by both old and young, and may
sucess attend the noble women that spend
their time and talents in contributing to
make it interesting and instructive, is the
prayer of your sister in the Gospel,
				NANCY M. NEILSON,
								Sec'y.
