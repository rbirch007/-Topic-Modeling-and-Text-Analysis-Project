			MORONI, Jan. 15, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Agreeable to your request I write you our
proceedings relative to the storing of wheat.
We have in our possession between one
and two hundred bushels of wheat, well se-
cured in a good granary; and we still feel
to go ahead and do all we can to gather in
the staff of life. And by so doing we obey
the counsel of our beloved president, Brig-
ham Young, and ever feeling it our duty
and desire to do our part in all things per-
taining to the kingdom of God, standing
firm and unshaken in the cause of truth,
that we may hold out faithful to the end is
my prayer.
						D. P. IRONS.
	P. S. The reason that there are so few
that take the EXPONENT here is that two-
thirds of them are Danish and Swedish and
cannot read the English language.
							D. P. I.
