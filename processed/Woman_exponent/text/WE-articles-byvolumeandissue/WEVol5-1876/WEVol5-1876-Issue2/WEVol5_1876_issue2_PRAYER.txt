				PRAYER.

	What a sweet word is prayer; it fills one's
heart with such a sweet rest, and sooths and
comforts as only prayer can. How many mean-
ings has prayer? Comfort for the weary, the
broken-hearted and those in distress; it means
forgiveness of sins for the sinful and repentant,
with a better life ever after. When we feel
dispirited and think there is nothing on earth
worth living for, when our friends prove false
to us, when we meet with every disappoint-
ment that can befall human beings, when we
see our dear ones taken from us, and almost
think that God has forgotten us, and that He
has laid His chastening hand too heavy upon
us, and that our cross is more than we can
bear; then comes the sweet thought of prayer,
stealing into our hearts like the whispering of
an angel; then we can feel the soothing influ-
ence of that High and Holy One who has said
"Come unto me all you who are heavy-laden,
and I will give you rest." And verily He will
do so, for has He not said, "Heaven and earth
may pass away, but my word endureth for-
ever." Do we pray enough? We should al-
ways have a prayer in out hearts. "Pray al-
ways lest ye enters into temptation." And if
we ever feel that the way of life is thorny, and
we all know that there is more shadow than
sunshine here, let us lift up our voices to Him
who has said, "Ask and ye shall receive" and
we will find sweet consolation in prayer. The
sweetest words to me I ever read were these:
"Lord teach us how to pray."
									JUNE.
