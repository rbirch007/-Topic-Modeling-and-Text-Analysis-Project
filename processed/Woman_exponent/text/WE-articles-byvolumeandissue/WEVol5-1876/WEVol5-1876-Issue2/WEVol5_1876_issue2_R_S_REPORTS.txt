		R. S. REPORTS.

	Meeting of the Senior and Junior Re-
trenchment Association held in the Four-
teenth Ward Assembly Rooms, Saturday,
May 27th, was one of an exceptionally in-
teresting nature. Prest. Mrs. M. I. Horne,
Counselors E. R. Snow, Z. D. Young, S. M.
Kimball, and E. Howard each addressed
the meeting, being seemingly inspired to
speak of the subjects which are adapted to
the present time, the building of the tem-
ple, the coming fair, home-industries silk
manufacture etc.
	Meeting of the same Association at the
same place Sat. June 10, was largely attend-
ed, and not only were the minutes of the
young ladies specially interesting, but each
person who spoke seemed to dwell with in-
terest on the topics most appropriate to the
occasion. A great deal of business was al-
so transacted relative to the Territorial Fair
now in contemplation, which will be held
in the Old Constitution Buildings, Main
Street, instead of the Social Hall, as was
previously anticipated, and will be opened
on Thursday July 6th.

			MORONI, May 10th, 1876.
