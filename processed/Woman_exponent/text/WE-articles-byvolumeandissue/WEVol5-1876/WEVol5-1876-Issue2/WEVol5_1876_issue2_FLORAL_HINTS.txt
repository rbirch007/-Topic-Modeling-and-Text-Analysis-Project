FLORAL HINTS.

	PELARGONIUMS for winter should be cut
as early as June, and the new leaves pinch-
ed off for a few weeks before potting. You
may regret this treatment at first but they
will reward you with steady bloom.
	FOR Heliotropes which you design for
winter blooming keep them pinched off
during the summer months, then pot them
in September, and they will grow large and
bloom profusely; give them plenty of sun-
shine, and all the water they can absorb.
	PRIMROSES are best kept in the shade
during the summer, part of the time in a
cold frame, with brush on the sash to shade
them. In the winter put them in the sun-
niest spot possible; then they will grow and
bloom all winter, requiring very little wa-
ter.
	WHEN house plants are troubled with in-
sects, set the plant on the floor, place a
small fire-shovel near it with a live coal
from the fire on it, then sprinkle some fine
tobacco on the coal, cover both plant and
shovel with a large cloth and give it a good
smoking. When sufficiently smoked wash
it with warm suds, then in clear water.
	HOGA Carnosa: This plant is a native of
the forests of tropical Asia, where it is
partly parisitical, its roots penetrating the
bark of the trees that support it; we can
only give less water for rest, as it will not
bear a low temperature. It is easily rooted
by wrapping a branch in moss, and keep-
ing it moist until the adventitous roots are
an inch or so long when it may be cut off
and potted.
