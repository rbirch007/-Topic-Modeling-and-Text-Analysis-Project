PARIS, BEAR LAKE CO., IDAHO TER,
						May 21st '76.
EDITORY EXPONENT:
	Although we have had a hard winter and a
cold backward spring, we are still alive to the
interests and welfare of the kingdom of God.
	We were yesterday called upon to part with
our much respected and esteemed Sister Mary
Humphreys. She has been an invalid for sev-
eral years, and great sufferer. Her funeral ser-
vices took place to-day, President C. C. Rich
delivered the funeral sermon.
	Our Society meetings are good, and the sis-
ters seem to take a lively interest in doing
what they can towards helping to build up
Zion.
	The Young Ladies' Retrenchment Associa-
tion is progressing finely, Prest. Rich often
meets with them, and seems to take great in-
terest in their advancement; always giving
good counsel and encouraging them to dili-
gence.
	Although our climate is rugged and cold, we
are blessed with health; there is little or no
sickness at this time. The grain is beginning
to make its appearance out of the ground; but
never mind, we have our spring time up here,
if it is in July.
	Your paper is read with great interest, and
duly appreciated; I always feel when I take it
up to read, as though I cannot lay it down un-
til I have read every item in it. You have
my best wishes for your progress and welfare.
			Yours Truly,
