THE TIMES.

	In the present epoch of the world's history
may be recorded, that now and then, at almost
regular intervals, a decided lull seems to
have o'erspread the earth and its inhabitants.
Rumors of great disasters by sea and by land
are hushed; the hand of death is stayed; busi-
ness is be-calmed; even the voice of conten-
tion is for the instant quieted. At such a mo-
ment, if the wheel of Time does not itself be-
come clogged, there seems to be a kink, a pause
in the windings of the great chain of events.
How long the calm will last, or what results
will follow, the Allwise Ruler can alone define.
Even to the inspired among men, it is not
given to comprehend the exact time of day, or
what change will be next in order, succeeding
the silence of such an hour. But, the Lord
does not slumber, neither should His people.
The Saints have been warned of approaching
dangers, and forewarned against slothfulness
and procrastination.
	Let not a momentary subsidence decoy any
into the belief that the reign of peace has com-
menced. The heavy storm is always preceded
by a calm, and the longer the clouds are in
gathering, the more to be dreaded is the com-
ing tempest. Have we oil in our vessels, and
are our lamps trimmed?
