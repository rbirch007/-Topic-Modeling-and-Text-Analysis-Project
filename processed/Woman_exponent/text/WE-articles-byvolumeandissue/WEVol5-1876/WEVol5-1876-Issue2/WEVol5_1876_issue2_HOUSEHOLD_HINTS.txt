HOUSEHOLD HINTS.

	To whiten unbleached Valencicnnes lace, lay
on a cloth and cover with magnesia or baby pow-
der, leaving it over night. Blow off the dust and
repeat if need be.
	Clean cashmere lace by dipping or rinsing it in
gasoline, being careful not to rub it so as to get
the meshes and figures out of place. Dry it out of
doors, then lay it on a cloth in good form, and
place between the leaves of a book to press.
	Potato Balls for Breakfast.—To three cups of
cold mashed potatoes add one cup of sweet cream,
a little salt and two well-beaten eggs. Mould in-
to little balls; put on a baking-pan, and set in the
oven until done a delicate brown, or, if preferred,
fry in boiling lard.
	To Make Attar of Roses.—Gather the flowers
of some of the most fragrant sorts, but the com-
mon Blush or other June roses are the best. Put
the petals in a cask, or stone jar, with sufficient
water to cover them. Then put the vessel out of
doors where the sun will keep it warm. In a
week or ten days the oil may be seen on the surface,
and may be removed by the aid of a piece of cot-
ton.
	To clean kid gloves. Lay your glove in a
long, flat-bottomed dish, like a common vegetable
dish, and cover it with the gasoline; rub soiled spots
with a clean white rag, squeeze, not wring with
your hand, as dry as you can, lay it on a clean old
muslin, take one corner of the muslin and rub the
fingers and thomb out smooth, rub soiled spots if
any remain, inflate by blowing into it, then pin on
a cloth out-of-doors to dry. The process should
be gone through with quickly, as the glove is in-
jured by remaining in the liquid; not more than
ten minutes being occupied in cleaning the pair of
gloves. This will be found excellent economy.
