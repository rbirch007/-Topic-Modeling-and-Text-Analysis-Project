FLORAL HINTS.

	AUTUMN LEAVES:—Gather as large a num-
ber and as great a variety of these leaves
as you wish, use old books for drying, or old
magazines, or newspapers; put as many
leaves on a page as you can, only not let
them touch each other, then turn five or
six pages and arrange similarly until you fill
your book; place in a cool dry place under
a heavy weight for twenty-four hours, then
remove the leaves to a dry book; change
them thus three times, when they will be
in beautiful condition and ready for use.
Get some cake wax, such as is used for
"fruit molding," put it in a vessel and set
that in a pan of water on the stove; when
melted, add a few drops of turpentine or
fir balsam, to render the wax pliable; when
the wax is the right temperature, dip the
leaves in one by one, or spray by spray and
hold each until cool, then place them upon
newspapers to harden completely: Thus
prepared they will present the natural ap-
pearance of the leaf, but to give them a
glossy surface give them a thin coat of De-
mar varnish with a camel's hair brush.
The best time to gather ferns for winter use
is September and October, also all kinds of
grasses and varieties of autumn berries.
Ladies of skill and ingenuity may arrange
these so artistically as to beautify and adorn
their homes with very little expense.
