OUR PARENTS' LOVE.

	There is no love as true and faithful as
the love our parents give us, and for none
are we so ungrateful. We take this devo-
tion as something which is our right, or as
something we deserve, even though our
mothers toil and labor and deny them-
selves time after time for our gratification,
from our earliest infancy till our growing
to womanhood. We can never comprehend
the extent of her love and patience with
us; yet is there anything like it? Our lover
may desert us, our friends vanish, and in
our sorrow and distress leave us; but our
parents cling to us in all trials and troubles,
and we are dearer to them for any misfor-
tune or grief we may chance to have, or
meet with  in this life. Then let us do all
we can to make our parents hearts rejoice,
and that will add to their happiness in any
way, and show them by our actions that we
appreciate their kindness and love, and try
to lighten their troubles and labors; then,
when they shall leave us, we will have
nothing to reproach ourselves with on their
account; but to look forward eagerly to the
time when we shall meet them with joy
and gladness, and be assured that the meet-
ing will be a welcome one.
	M. J. R. R. HAMPTON.
S. L. City.
