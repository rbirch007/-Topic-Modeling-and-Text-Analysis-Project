	DEWEYVILLE, BOX ELDER Co.,
						Sept. 6th, 1876.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The Annual Meeting of the Relief Socie-
ty of this place was held in the School-
House, Aug. 9th. There were present, Pres-
ident J. C. Dewey, of this place, and Pres-
idents' Harriet and Minnie Snow of Brig-
ham City. They spoke words of encour-

[Column 3]
agement to the sisters and exhorted them
to be united and live their religion. The
report of our last years' proceedings was
read; after which Pres. Mary Dewey made
a few remarks. The present officers are
as follows:
	Mary Dewey, President,
	Heneretta Call,	}	Counselors
	Althea Lewis,	}
	Eliza Howard, Secretary,
	Matilda Lish Treasurer.
	There is a small amount of means in the
treasury, and we hope to do more in the
next year than we have done in the past;
some of the sisters have united and are
saving the eggs their hens lay on Sundays
for the Temple. We meet once a week and
have good meetings, and those who attend
them regularly, feel well and are desirous
of doing all they can to help the work along.
We have been organized one year and we
know how to appreciate the blessings of a
Society. Your excellent paper is read in
our meetings with great interest.
	With best wishes for the advancement of
every good work, we remain your sisters,
&c.
			MARY DEWEY, Pres.
