WOMAN'S RECORD.

	MADAME Louis Blanc, who died recently
in Paris, must have been very lovable, as it
is said tens of thousands followed her re-
mains to the cemetery.
	MISS Anna Oliver, the young preacher,
is filling with characteristic dignity, an en-
gagement at the First Place Methodist
Church in Brooklyn.
	MISS Tora Dutt, a young Hindoo lady,
has just published a book of translations of
French verse into English. Critics praise,
and esteem it as an item "in the history of
the progress of culture."
	"SHELLS FROM THE SANDS OF TIME,"
is the title of a book written by Lady Lyt-
ton, (widow of Lord Lytton the author) in
which she comments upon modern servants
"stuckapativeness."
