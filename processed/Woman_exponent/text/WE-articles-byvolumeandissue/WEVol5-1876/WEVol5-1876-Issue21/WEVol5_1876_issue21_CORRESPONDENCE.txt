			CORRESPONDENCE.

			CENTREVILLE, March 20th, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Believing that a few items from here touch-
ing the great work in which we are all engag-
ed will be gratifying to you, permit me to state
that we are blessed with a general time of
health in this place, with an increasing desire
made apparent to walk as near the line of duty
as is possible, when our circumstances are tak-
en into consideration. I am proud to say that
the Young Ladies Retrenchment Association
is in a flourishing condition, each and every
member thereof responding cheerfully to all
the requirements made of them, and I rejoice
to be associated with them and all such as love
and serve God in the Latt[-]r-day kingdom, and
that I have the privilege of doing something
though small to help roll it forth. A general
good feeling akin to inspiration prevails in the
Relief Society, and a desire to grow in grace,
and in the knowledge of the truth. The Young
Mens' Association is also i[-] a flourishing con-
dition.
	Praying for the success of your valuable pa-
per and every laudable endeavor in the king-
dom of God, we subscribe ourselves your sis-
ters in the Gospel,
					ELIZABETH WADOUP,
