	WELLSVILLE, Feb. 10, 1877.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I will give you a few items of a general
nature respecting woman's work and pro-
gress.  In our meetings many of the sisters
young and old arise and bear faithful and


earnest testimonies to the truth of the ev-
erlasting Gospel. I believe the sisters who
covenanted to eschew round dancing have
faithfully kept their covenants inviolate.
	The majority of the people are earnestly
striving to live by every word that pro-
ceedeth out of the mouth of God, and their
subscriptions to the Logan Temple, and all
other public and private benefits, bear am-
ple testimony. A few among us, when the
bridegroom cometh, may be caught nap-
ping and our lamps devoid of oil, but the
gospel net catches of all kinds.
	Considerable sickness has prevailed among
the people inthis valley—diphtheria among
the children, but we are in the hands of
God and have full faith in His guidance.
With prayers continually for your success
in your work of love, I remain your sister
and fellow-laborer in the cause of truth, and
for the redemption of Zion.	M. E. M.
