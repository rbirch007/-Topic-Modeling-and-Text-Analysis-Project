EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Our Society comprising several districts,
lying far away on either side of Morgan City,
deprived many sisters of the privilege of at-
tending meetings and of doing the good it was
in their hearts to do. Therefore, Bishop Smith
who cares for his flock with fatherly solicitude,
decided to organize a Relief Society in each
little branch of his ward; he therefore called a
general meeting of the sisters, for the purpose
of dissolving the society, and to divide the
stock on hand among the several branches.
The total stock on hand is two hundred and
eighty-eight dollars and thirteen cents.
	We met again on the 19th inst. to reorganize
the old branch of South Morgan. The elec-
tions were as follows: Mrs. Lydia Rich Presi-
dent, Mrs. Mary Ann Simmons, and Mrs. Ann
Fry Counselors; Mrs. Rawb Secretary; Mrs.
Annie Lesnell, Treasurer. Our beloved Bish-
op being very sick, E[-]ders R. Fry, W. Edding-
ton and D. Ball addressed the meeting and or-
dained the officers.
	I hope that you will hear oftener, and good
news from this corner of the vineyard, for I
feel vividly impressed that the harvest is close
at hand. And now, good-bye dear little EX-
PONENT, may thy pithy columns, impart as
much warmth and comfort of soul to every
weary head and heart, as they do to
			Your sister in the Gospel,
				ESTHER C. E. Francis.

	P. S.—Would it not be a good plan, for
every Society to keep a copy of the EXPONENT
smooth and clean, to be bound after awhile, in
a handsome volume? And so keep a Record
of every Branch of this great and glorious en-
terprise; namely, the Relief Society of Utah.
