HOUSEHOLD HINTS.

	To prepare a counterfeit cream for tea and cof-
fee, take one egg, one half cup of sugar, one half
cup of sweet milk, one half cup of cornstarch, beat
well together.
	White Fruit Cake.—Take one cup of butter, two
of sugar, one of sour milk, the whites of three
eggs, the whole of one egg, one teaspoonful of soda;
two of cream tartar, and one and a half cups of
raisins stoned and floured.
	Cookies.—Two cups of sugar, one of butter,
one egg, one half cup of sweet milk, two teaspoon-
fuls of cream tartar and one of soda, add nutmeg.
Take sufficient flour to mix, and roll out. Sprin-
kle sugar over the top and roll it in before cutting
them out. Put them on a paper to cool before
piling them up to store away.


Old-Fashioned Fried Cakes.—Take 1/2 cup sugar,
1/2 cup shortening, one pint sour milk, one teaspoon-
ful saleratus, season with allspice or nutmeg; mix
with sufficient flour, but not too hard. Have your
lard hot enough to make the cakes a darkish
brown. These cakes can be made very quickly,
and will keep a long time in cool weather and do
not get stale or dry.
	Home Prepared Citron for Cakes and Pud-
dings.—Take the citron, cut and prepare it in the
ordinary way except to boil the syrup until it is
quite thick, but instead of closing up the jars,
leave them open for the air to enter, taking, of
course, every precaution against dust or damp.
The preserve will then dry gradually, and you
have an excellent substitute for the imported arti-
cle, which will answ r the same purposes. Now
is the time of year, and there are plenty of citrons
for sale very cheap. Another year perhaps many
housekeepers knowing this recipe may raise their
own citrons at home.
