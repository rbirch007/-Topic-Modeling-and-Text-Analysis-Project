			MILL CREEK, Oct. 17, 1876.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Our Relief Society meetings are held
every second Thursday in each month.
Last month we had the pleasure of a visit
from some of the sisters from the city.
Miss E. R. Snow, Mrs. Zina D, Young,
Mrs. E. Davis, Mrs. P. Staines, and Mrs. E.
Howard.

[Column 2]
	Miss E. R. Snow spoke to us about home
industry, and of making substantial cloth-
ing such as we can manufacture ourselves;
she also spoke of the necessity of meeting
together often.
	Mrs. Zina Young spoke upon seri-culture,
showing how very easy we could raise the
materials, and that it would be for our good.
We had a good meeting and if we do not
do right it is not for the want of instruc-
tions.
	I will thank my sisters for their kindly
visit and good counsel, and may God bless
them in their labor.
	We are doing as well as we can in our
scattered condition. We have a good room
well carpeted and furnished. We take care
of the sick, and help the needy. The
Young Ladies Retrenchment Association
is doing well under the presidency of Mrs.
Isabella Carlisle. They take the EXPONENT
and read selections from it in their meet-
ings.
	With best wishes for the advancement of
every good work and home manufacture,
we remain the co-workers of those who la-
bor for the advancement of the Kingdom of
God,
			MRS. MARY MCALLISTER,
							Pres't.
		MRS. ELIZABETH CARLISLE,
							Sec'y.
