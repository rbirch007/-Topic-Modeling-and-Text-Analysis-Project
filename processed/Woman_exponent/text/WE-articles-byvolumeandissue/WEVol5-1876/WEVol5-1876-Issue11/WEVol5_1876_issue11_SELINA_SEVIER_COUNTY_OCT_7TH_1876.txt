	SELINA, SEVIER COUNTY, Oct 7th, 1876,
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The following is is a brief report of our
societies. The sisters are alive to their du-
ties and are trying to serve God in a right
and acceptable manner. Our Relief So-
ciety was organized Dec. 25, 1873, and Sis-
ter Margaret Rasmasen was chooson for our
president, but being called to move away,
Sister Margaret Anderson was chosen to fill
her place. We find her a good, kind presi-
dent and one who is trying to live her re-
ligion and help the sisters, and especially
the poor in all their needs for comfort or as-
sistance of any sort. A number of the
Saints here have been baptized for the re-
newal of their covenants, enjoying the
Spirit of the Lord, and He has blessed them
with an abundance of harvest this season.
The brethren have built a store-house to
store away the grain, and are very busy.
We meet every Fast day and every second
Thursday and converse upon the principles
of our religion. The teachers visit the dif-
ferent wards once a month and find out the
candition of the saints and where there
are any sick or poor that are in need we
give comfort and attend to their wants im-
mediately. We also have a Young Ladies
Society which was organized last Fall with
the following officers. Mrs. Mary Ann
Steele, President; Mrs. Hannah J. Spencer,
and Mrs. Sarah Casto, Counselors, Miss
Catharine Martin, Secretary and Mrs.
Martha Fenn, Treasurer. There are but
very few young ladies in our small place,
but there is a large num[-]er of small girls
from nine to twelve years of age, who love
to attend meeting, and we hope at some
future day to have a large and intelligent
Society.
	Wishing you success, I remain your Sis-
ter in the Gospel.
