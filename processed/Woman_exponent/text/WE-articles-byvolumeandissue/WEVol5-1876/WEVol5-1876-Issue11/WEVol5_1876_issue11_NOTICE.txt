NOTICE.

	The Woman's Commission House, recently establish-
ed under the auspices of the Relief Society Woman's
Mercantile Association, is now transacting business on
commission at the Old Constitution Buildings, opposite
Z. C. M. I. Their stock consists entirely of home-
manufactures. They are well supplied with plaid, lin-
seys and waterproofs for winter dresses, wraps etc; and
shawls large and small, stockings and socks of all sizes
knit with machine or by hand, baby's socks, crochet
cuffs or wristers large and small. A dressmaker is
there, ready to make up material to order. There is a
good supply of men's and boys wear, of all the differ-
ent varieties manufactured here; an excellent tailor to
do all kinds of work in that line. There is also a full
stock of hats, straw and felt, and a miliner to attend to
that part of the work. They have also various other
articles to wear and use. All the standard church
books. Homemade soap, Jamaica ginger, ink, black-
ing and vinegar: also butter, cheese, and eggs. Among
the things noticeable are the lace and silk productions,
some of which are exquisite. The patronage of the
people is solicited for this institution, that Utah may
sustain by united effort the industry which is the pros-
pective wealth of her people. The women of this Ter-
ritory are making this almost unprecedented movement
to concentrate their exertions in one grand enterprise to
become self-sustaining, that they may free themselves
as a people, as soon as possible, from the shackles of 
Babylon!
