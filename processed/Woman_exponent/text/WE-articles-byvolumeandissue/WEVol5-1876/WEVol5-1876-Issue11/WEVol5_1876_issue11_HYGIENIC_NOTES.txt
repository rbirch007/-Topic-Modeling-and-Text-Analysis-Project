HYGIENIC NOTES.

	Lime-water and milk is said to be a most useful
compound for sick or delicate infants, and also for
persons at a later period in life, when the functions
of digestion have been seriously impaired. A
tumbler of milk and four tablespoonfuls of lime-
water is about the requisite proportion for a grown
person, and will afford proper nourishment when
almost everything else fails.
	It is asserted upon the authority of persons of
great experience in sickness and disease, that in
many instances men and women who have suf-
fered from nervousness until their hands shake
like leaves on a tree in a windy day, and others
who have become so affected as to cause irregular-
ity of the pulsations of the heart which produces
undue excitement, have been effectually cured by
the daily use of celery eaten at meal times.
	A medical lady of some repute tells us that
thousands of people create lung disease from their
own willfulness and ignorance, from the effects of
a pressure on the lungs through the habit of sit-
ting, standing and walking with the head drawn
forward and stooping; this prevents the lungs from
filling with air naturally, as they otherwise would.
Stand erect and throw your shoulders back if you
would preserve your health and life.
	An excellent cough powder may be prepared
from skunk cabbage root, comfrey root and white
sugar. Take equal quantities of each, pulverized,
and mix together, and use one-half dram for a
dose in a little water three times a day, or morn-
ing and evening. The skunk cabbage is a peren-
nial plant growing in boggy swamps. The root
has anti-spasmodic properties and is very useful in
chronic colds, coughs and asthma, and should be
prepared as a powder.
