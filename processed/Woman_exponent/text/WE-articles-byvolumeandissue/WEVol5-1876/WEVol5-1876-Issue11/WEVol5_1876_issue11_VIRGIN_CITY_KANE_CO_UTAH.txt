	VIRGIN CITY, KANE CO., UTAH,
					October 16th, 1876.
EDITORS EXPONENT:
	When we last wrote to you, in May, our
Retrenchment Association numbered thirty-
two; since that time it has increased to for-
ty members, and more are joining almost
every meeting. We hold our meetings
once in two weeks; we employ our time in
speaking, reading from the EXPONENT and
other good publications, and in working at
different kinds of work. On Friday, Oct.
6th, the Association met at the school house
for the purpose of quilting a quilt, the first
that had been completed in the Association;
the young ladies worked very diligently
till 12 o'clock, when all brought in their
picnic, and a table was set the length of the
hall, and at one p. m. about forty (including
a few of our mothers who had been invited
in) were seated at a very good dinner.. Af-
ter dinner the tables were removed, and
the quilt finished. Afterwards the Asso-
ciation held a meeting, to which the Young
Men's Association had been invited, and the
girls had been requested to invite their
mothers. After singing and prayer, Sisters
Maria Spendlove and Alice Isom delivered
lectures, which were listened to with great
interest, and which were truly encouraging
and edifying to the young. At six p. m. all
met at the hall to enjoy themselves in the
recreation of a dance, which closed at ten,
when all expressed themselves well pleased
with the evening entertainment. A good
spirit prevailed throughout the entire day.
	Hoping for the success of your valuable
paper, we remain as ever your sisters
	LUCINDA STRATTON,	}	Secretaries.
