FLOWERS.

	THE best friends are those who stimulate
each other [--] do good.
	GREAT works are performed, not by
strength, but by perseverance.
	THE man who contents himself to-day
with that which he has will content himself
to-morrow with that which he may have.
	People who suppose that a good prayer is
preferred to a good act, doubtless imagine
that God has more hearing than eyesight.
The end, we fear, will show that they rea-
soned from false premises. The poor are
oftener prayed for than helped. The rea-
son is, we believe that air is cheaper than
bullion.
