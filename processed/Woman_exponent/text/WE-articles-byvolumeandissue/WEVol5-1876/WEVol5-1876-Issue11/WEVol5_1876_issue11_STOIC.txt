							STOIC.


THE thing which an active mind most
needs is a purpose and direction worthy of
its activity.
	TEAR from a woman's life all else that
she holds dear and leave her love, and she
is rich; load her with jewels, clothe her in
costly stuff, let slaves wait upon her and
courtiers bow, and she is but a pauper, if no
one loves her.
	POETS know, and statesmen ought to
know, it is by sentiment when well direct-
ed—as by sorrow when well used—great na-
tions live. When sentiment dies out and
mere prosaic calculations of loss and profit
take its place, then comes a Byzantine
epoch, a Chinese epoch, decrepitude and
slow decay.—Kingsley.
