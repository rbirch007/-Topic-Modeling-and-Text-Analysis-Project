			R. S. REPORTS.

	The meeting of the Senior and Junior
Retrenchment Association was held in the
Fourteenth Ward Assembly Rooms, Satur-
day, Oct. 14. President Mrs: M. I. Horne
was present and after the reading of min-
utes from different wards addressed the
meeting on the various subjects now under
consideration. She exhorted the sisters to
encourage all home institutions, and above
all to live their religion in an acceptable
manner; she spoke of the sorrow mani-
fested by the people now that they could no
longer have the privilege of officiating for
their dead, and advised all those present to
beware of procrastination lest the Bride-
groom should come and find them unpre-
pared. Mrs. E. S. Barney gave some ac-
count of the recent visit she had made to
some of the settlements, and the success
which had attended their efforts. Mrs. B.
W. Smith, Mrs. P. Woodruff, and Mrs.
Margaret P. Young all spoke [-]pon the prin-
ciples of the Gospel and the testimony of
the Holy Spirit. The meeting was an in-
teresting one and the Spirit of the gospel
seemed to abound in the hearts of the sis-
ters.
