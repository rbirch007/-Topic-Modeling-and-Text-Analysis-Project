				HOME AFFAIRS.

	SEVERAL OF the missionaries appointed at the last
Semi-Annual Conference, started to-day on their jour-
ney. Some had left previous and others will start on
the sixth!
	THE DISTRICT Court seems unable to dispose of
some of the cases on hand, as there are so many crooks
and turns taken that these cases are likely to drag on,
until they shall be ruled out of Court, or assume the
character of cases in chancery.
	THE UTAH Musical Times, No. 8, now issued con-
tains many excellent things. The editorial "Singing in
Schools" deserves to be widely read, and all interested
in the advancement of the young are sure to read it
with interest. The hints given on the subject are adapt-
ed to the situation of the people. The music is always
of a high order and tends to refine the taste. We hope
this journal will be well sustained. It has our best
wishes, especially as we believe it to be one step towards
the progression of Utah in musical art.
	THE MUSICAL and dramatic entertainment tendered
to Mr. O. F. Whitney, and given in the Fourteenth
Ward Assembly Rooms, Wednesday evening Oct. 25th,
was very well attended. The whole performance was
creditable, and there were several parts which were exe-
cuted in such a tasteful and artistic manner that they
deserve notice made of t[-]em. These amateurs ought to
be congratulated for their efforts to please their friends
and especially for lending their efforts in a good cause.
Mr. O. F. Whitney tenders his sincere thanks to Prof.
Thomas, the Salt Lake Orchestra, the Union Glee Club,
also the young ladies and gentlemen who so kindly took
part in the performance.
