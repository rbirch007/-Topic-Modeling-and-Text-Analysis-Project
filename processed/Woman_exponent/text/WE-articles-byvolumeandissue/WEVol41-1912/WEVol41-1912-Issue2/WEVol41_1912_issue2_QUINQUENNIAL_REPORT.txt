QUINQUENNIAL REPORT.
	Of General Secretary of Relief Society.
	Brief review of the work of the National
Woman’s Relief Society from October,
1907, to October, 1912.
	During the past five years the great work
of the Relief Society has been going for-
ward with energy and earnestness, through
the willing and untiring efforts of the Gen-
eral, Stake, and Local officers and the mem-
bers themselves. Many events of impor-
tance have taken place and many changes
have been made.
	It is with feelings of pride that we record
the establishment of Relief Society head-
quarters in the Bishop’s Building, in Decem-
ber, 1909, and for the first time in its history
the General Society has rooms for the
transaction of its business.
	Since the acquirement of these headquar-
ters there have been several notable birthday
celebrations: On May 3, 1910, a party was
given by the General Board in honor of the
eighty-eighth birthday anniversary of Presi-
ident Bathsheba W. Smith, in appreciation
of her life long labors, both in the Temple
