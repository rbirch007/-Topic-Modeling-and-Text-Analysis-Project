		CORRESPONDENCE.
	At present there are a number of young
women working in the missionary field, full
of zeal in their labors and with joy in their
hearts for the blessed privilege of bearing
the glad tidings to the people of the world,
as their brothers have been permitted to do
these many years past. The following let- 
ter and poem was written from one of these
girl missionaries for the occasion of her
great-grandmother’s ninetieth birthday an-
niversary, and as it breathes somewhat of
the freshness and devotion of youth’s en-
thusiasm, we have accepted it for publica-
tion.—Ed. 
			Kansas City, Sept. 25, 1912.
My Own Dear Grandma: 
	Please accept these few lines—hurriedly 
written—as my contribution for your birth-
day. 
	May you have many happy returns of the
day—may this birthday be the crowning
happiness of your life, thus far.
	I am so happy, grandma, I couldn’t write
anything very sad. And so busy I couldn’t
find much time to think.
	I am so thankful to be one of the spirits
permitted to come through your lineage
and I love you.
	I am sending you several hundred bar-
rels of love, C. O. D. Perhaps they will
not get there for a year, so take good care
of yourself, and I wil deliver them next
year in person.
	Once more wishing you a happy, happy
time, I must say good-bye. God bless my
dear little grandmother.
	Your far away loving (great grand)
child,
