HIS HANDICAP.
He wooed her when they both were poor,
	’twas then he won her, too;
She cheered him when the days were drear,
	and toiled to help him through;
She taught him things from books that he had
	failed to learn in youth,
She got him to avoid the use of words that
	were uncouth:
She took her jewel in the rough, she polished
	day by day,
And with a woman’s patience [-]round the
	worthless parts away.
She turned him from a stupid clown to one
	whose mein was proud,
She planted in his heart the wish to rise above
	the crowd;
She planned the things he undertook, she
	urged him on to try,
She gave him confidence to look for splendid
	things and high;
She bore the children that he loved and toiled
	for them and him,
And often knelt beside her bed with aching
	eyes and dim.
She cheered him when the days were dark, and
	when the skies were bright
She saw him rise above the crowd and reach
	a noble height;
Her brow is marred by many a line; she’s bent
	and wan and old.
He has a bearing that is fine, a form of noble
	mold;
And people say: “Poor man, alas! He’s grown
	beyond his wife;
How sad that such a load should be attached
	to him for life!”
		—From the Chicago Times-Herald.
