					MARY E. DALTON.


An order from Virginia for eighty thou-
sand leaflets, recently received by the liter-
ature department of the National Suffrage
Association, is one of the straws showing
which way the wind blows in the South in
these piping times of votes for women.


The late Cardinal Vaughan, Archbishop
of Westminster, when asked his views on
woman suffrage, replied: “I believe that
the extension of the Parliamentary fran-
chise to women upon the same conditions as
it is held by men would be a just and ben-
eficial measure, tending to raise rather than
lower the course of national legislation.”
