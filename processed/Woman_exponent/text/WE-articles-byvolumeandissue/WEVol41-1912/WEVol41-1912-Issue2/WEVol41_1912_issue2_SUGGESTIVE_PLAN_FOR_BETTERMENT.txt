SUGGESTIVE PLAN FOR BETTERMENT
				WORK.
1. Organization:
(a) A Central Betterment Committee—
	composed of six members, three from
	the Relief Society and three from the
	Y. L. M. I. A., appointed by the
	Presidents of the two General
	Boards.
(b) A Stake Betterment Committee—
	composed of four members, selected
	by the Stake Presidents of the two
	organizations and appointed by the
	Stake Priesthood President.
(c) Ward Missionaries—one or two se-
	lected by the Stake Committee, the
	Bishop and Presidents of the two
	local organizations.
2. The Work—Preventive:
Aims—To bring indifferent parents and
	their children into the organizations
	that will bring the spirit of the Gos-
	pel into their hearts and homes.
		To impress parents with the sacred
	duties and responsibilities of parent-
	hood.
		To encourage the downcast, stim-
	ulate the indifferent and reclaim the
	wayward.
3. Qualifications of the Workers:
	The Spirit of the Work—Wisdom—
	Spirituality — Charity — Patience —
	Love—Tact.
4. Duties of Missionaries:
	Keep in close touch with the Relief
	Society and Y. L. M. I. A. Visit
	homes. Hold neighborhood meet-
	ings, and labor unceasingly with
	great love, and much patience for
	the betterment of the home and its
	inmates.
