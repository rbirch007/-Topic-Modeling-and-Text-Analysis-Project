HOUSEHOLD HINTS.
	Plum Pudding— 1 lb. of brown sugar, 1 lb. of
suet, 1 lb. of bread crumbs, 1 lb. of raisins, 1
lb. of currants, 1/2 teacup of citron and lemon
peel, 9 eggs, beat whites and yolks separately,
1 pt. of milk, 1/2 oz. each mace, cinnamon and
nice stiff batter. Boil from eight to ten
hours first day, and four to six the day of
using.
	Fruit Cake— 1 lb. flour, 1 lb. sugar, 1lb. but-
ter, 1 doz. eggs beaten separately, 1 lb. raisins,
seeded and chopped, 1 lb. currants washed and
dried, 1/4 lb. lemon peel, sliced thin, 1 lb. in
shell of almonds sliced thin or pounded, 2 nut-
megs, a tablespoonful of cinnamon, 1/2 tea-
spoonful of cloves, a teacup of milk. Beat
sugar, butter and yolks of eggs together, beat
whites to a froth and add last, beat flour in
gradually and other ingredients, stirring a
long time or until thoroughly mixed. Bake in
moderate oven two hours.
	French Cream Cake—1 cup of sugar, 1 1/2
cups of flour, 3 eggs, 2 teaspoonfuls of baking
powder, 2 tablespoonfuls of cold water; bake
in two pie tins, cut open and spread with
custard before entirely cold.
	Cream Filling— Beat with a little milk 2
small tablespoonfuls of corn starch, add 2
eggs, 1 small cup of white sugar; stir into half
a pint of boiling milk, cook in double boiler,
and when nearly done, add one-half cup of
butter; flavor with vanilla or lemon.
