CONSOLATION.
In Memory of Lane M. Clegg, by Maud Ellen
			Baggarley.
On a far-off, lonely hill-side
	Our babe forever sleeps,
And the sun shines warm on his little bed,
	And angels vigil keep.
But Father, I think of the mother
	Who misses that little head
Which nestled warm on her bosom
	But now is lying dead.
And I grope for Thy purpose, Father,
	But mystery lies between
The familiar things about me
	And the vast and vague unseen.
Yet I know a vision beckons,
	Tho’ ’tis often hid by tears,
And I know that prayer unceasing
	Lights our path through all the years.
But Sorrow sets her seal upon thee,
	Chosen ones of God above,
Linked thou art with realms celestial,
	By a bond of human love;
Human love most high and holy,
	For thou, too, didst give a son.
Tarried thou within “the garden,”
	Knowest now the crowned One.
But knowledge lights the path of duty,
	And thy mission now is plain,
By the little child that leads thee,
	Go thou forth to ease the pain
Of the suffering world about thee,
	Tell them they shall live again.
Thou canst comfort those in sorrow,
	Thou canst whisper, “I have known,”
And shalt garner crowns of amaranth
	From the seeds that thou hast sown.


The movement throughout the world in
the interest of woman suffrage has stirred
the men and women of Russia to give more
thought to the condition of women. The
Douma recently passed a bill entitling
women teachers to almost the same pensions
as men. A bill is pending to provide for the
extension of the personal property rights of
married women and practically insists upon
an equality of the sexes.
