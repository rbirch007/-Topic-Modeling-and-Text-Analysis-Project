		TO CARE FOR WAIFS.
	Mrs. Williams took the initiative in this
work, and a society, which was at first
thought to be impossible, was perfected. The
object of this society is the relief of the
needy, but special attention is paid to babies
whose parents are destitute or who have
been left parentless and homeless.
	As a result of the visit here and of the
agitation made by these women, a new
baby home may be founded soon in Port-
land by the Mormon church, but not until
after the organization has seen to it that
the baby homes already established here are
able to care for themselves without the aid
of the Mormon society.
	During the conference held here, lectures
were given to the young girls and women
of the church, in which they were urged to
live right, and to take special care of their
dress, so that they need not attract unnec-
essary and uncomplimentary attention.
	The work in that direction will be made
a regular feature of the local society and is
a campaign that is being carried on by all
societies.
	In connection with the charitable work
that is done by this organization it should
be made plain that the women do not con-
fine their efforts to the people of their
church only, although they receive first
attention, but they work in all quarters.
During the visit of the two noted women
in Portland, many calls were made in homes
where it was known that help was needed,
and necessary aid was given.
