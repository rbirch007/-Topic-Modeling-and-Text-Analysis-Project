	PLAN MEMBERSHIP CAMPAIGN.
	The women make a regular business of
storing wheat as a source of income and
each year they receive donations of thous-
ands of bushels of wheat, which they sell
when the market is highest. The women

[Column 3]
own several very large granaries in Utah.
In case of a great disaster, the wheat is sold
to secure immediate funds, or if necessary
the wheat is shipped to the points where it
is needed.
	It was the Mormon women who sent the
first relief train to San Francisco at the time
of the earthquake, and it was the women of
this church who made the first big wheat
donations to the starving in China. The
attention of the society is now directed to
the sufferers in Mexico, who have been
made victims of the uprisings there. The
membership of this great organization num-
bers more than 30,000. An increase in the
local membership is the object of a cam-
paign to be started here soon.
