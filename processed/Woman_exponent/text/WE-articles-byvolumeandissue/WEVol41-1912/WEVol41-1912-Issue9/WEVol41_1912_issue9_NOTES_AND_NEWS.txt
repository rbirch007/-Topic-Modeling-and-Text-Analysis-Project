NOTES AND NEWS.
	The Pacific Coast Woman’s Press. Asso-
ciation open their meetings this year with a
splendid program, beginning Sept. 8. Much
attention in their papers and discussions is
given to the Panama Canal—that being for
the present the most absorbing topic for
the western press. Monday, Sept. 22, is
Members Day, when no guests will be per-
mitted, no papers read and all talks limited
to two minutes. There will be besides the
business of the association, discussions on
the following subjects: “Within the Law,”
“My Little Sister,” “The New Chivalry;”
and it is safe to predict a very lively time
among the women editors during these dis-
cussions.
