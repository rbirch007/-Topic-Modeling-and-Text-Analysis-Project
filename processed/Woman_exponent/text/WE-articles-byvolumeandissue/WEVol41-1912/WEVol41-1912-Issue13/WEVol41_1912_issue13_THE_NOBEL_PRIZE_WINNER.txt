THE NOBEL PRIZE WINNER.
	The Nobel Prize for literature for 1913
was awarded to Rabindra Nath Tagore, a
Bengali poet. The man thus honored is of
a notable Bengal family, famous both for
wealth and intellectual attainments.
	Born in 1860, Tagore’s first few years of
childhood were spent at Calcutta and in the
vastnesses of the Himalayas. At seventeen
he was sent to England, where he thorough-
ly acquainted himself with the English lan-

	Tagore’s early poetry shows marked ro-
manticism, probably caused by the influence
of the English romanticists, Byron and
Shelley. This sensualism became so great
in some cases that several of his early works
were discountenanced by the more conserv-
ative Bengali. We are told, and this is
probably one of the causes for his extreme
romanticism, that he returned from Europe
distinctly Epicurean; that he lived, for a
time, sumptuously, in the proverbial Ori-
ental magnificence, until the awakening of
his riper, greater powers revoked the sen-
sualist and called forth the higher spirit-
