NOTES ABOUT WOMEN.
	Mrs. Lillie Devereaux Blake, a pioneer in
the cause of woman suffrage, died in a san-
itarium at Englewood, N. J., December 30,
1913, aged 80 years. She had been ill for
several years, and recently had a turn for
the worse, when it was seen that because
of her advanced age her recovery was hope-
less.
	Mrs. Blake was the author of several suc-
cessful novels in her earlier years, but de-
voted the greater part of her life to cham-
pioning the cause of woman’s rights. She
was born in Raleigh, N. C.
	Another woman Mayor has been elected
in Oregon. A dispatch from Troutville
says that Mrs. Clara Larrson has been
chosen to that office by a majority of five
over the man who opposed her.	
	Oregon’s other woman Mayor is Miss
Clara Munson of Warrenton, who was
elected by the Citizens’ ticket last year.
	The town of Kanab, Utah, has a woman
mayor and the city council is composed en-
tirely of women. The executive ability of
women is gradually being recognized not
only in the suffrage states but the recent
appointments of women to Federal positions
shows that President Wilson realized, the
fact that women of the nation should
receive some public recognition.
