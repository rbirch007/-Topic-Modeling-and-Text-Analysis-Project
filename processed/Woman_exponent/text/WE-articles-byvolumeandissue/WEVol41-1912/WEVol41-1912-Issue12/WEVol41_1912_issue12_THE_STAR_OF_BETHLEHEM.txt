THE STAR OF BETHLEHEM.
Wise men discerned a change among the stars 
	In distant lands they had a wonder seen,
	An orb most luminous had come between
	The shining heavenly worlds, what could it
		mean, 
Its light a-streaming through the heavenly
		bars?

But as they watch the star in brilliance grow,
	It moves, and they, to learn its destiny,
	Prepare to follow where so e’er it be,
	To other lands or far beyond the sea, 
The stranger keep in sight where e’er it go.


O’er Bethlehem the guiding star stands still,
	And there in Mary’s arms they find the
		Child;
	Their hearts o’er fill with rapture wild,
	When kneeling round he on them smiled—
This Babe who came to do his Father’s will.

While shepherds wait without the manger door,
	Lo! Eastern kings make princely offering
	Of precious spices, such as camels bring
	Across the desert, to the new born King— 
Significant the gifts those wise men bore

The royal birth declared by angels’ voice,
	Brought love to all mankind, where e’er
		they be,
	Which thrills our hearts e’en though we may
		not see, 
	The blazing star in earth’s fair canopy,
Nor join the shepherds’ song when they re-
		joice.


Our hearts are touched with pathos of Christ’s 
		word;
	“How oft I would have gathered you,” he
		cried,
	And grieved that they companionship denied,
	Rejecting him and from him turn aside.
As if his pleadings they had never heard.

Yet now his natal day brings songs of love,  
	The Christmas bells with happy, noisy chime,
	Ring down the ages to remotest time
	E’er wakening chords of music most sub-
		lime— 
The song the angels sang in vaults above.
