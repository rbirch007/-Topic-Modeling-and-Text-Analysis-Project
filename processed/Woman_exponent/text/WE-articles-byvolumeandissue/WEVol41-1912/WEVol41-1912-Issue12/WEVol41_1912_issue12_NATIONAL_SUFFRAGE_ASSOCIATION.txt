NATIONAL SUFFRAGE ASSOCIATION.
	The meetings of the National Suffrage
Association, this month at Washington, are
considered the most important ever held
by that association. The suffrage women
have held annual gatherings in the different
cities of the United States, meeting every
alternate year at the national capital for the
past forty years. The results in gaining
recruits to the suffrage ranks, removing
prejudice, advancing social legislation for
the benefit of the woman laborer, and many
other achievements have marked the on-
ward trend of the work, but the fact that
in all that time only ten states have granted
equal rights to women, is not considered
sufficient progress and that the rest of the
women of the nation might enjoy the fran-
chise the pleading, petitioning and waiting
period is about over; patience is exhausted;
and the women now propose to make a de-
mand from the law-givers for more atten-
tion to this subject. The papers state that
the scene resembled a charge on a citadel,
when more than a thousand women marched
from the suffrage headquarters to the cap-
tol. The women moved in regimental
formation, in quick step order, and crowded
the hearing room long before the committee
appointed to hear them arrived. The re-
quest made by the women was that a House
committee on suffrage be appointed, equiv-
alent to a similar one in the Senate. The
request is made at every session of the asso-
ciation for an amendment to the Constitu-
tion in behalf of the enfranchisement of
women, and with special committees in each
branch of Congress the day seems not so
far distant for this result.
