RESOLUTIONS IN HONOR OF SISTER ELIZA ANN
			OKERLUND.
	Whereas, In the allwise providence of our
Heavenly Father it has pleased Him to call
from our midst Sister Eliza Ann Okerlund,
second counselor of the Relief Society in the
Loa ward.
	Therefore, Be it resolved that in the pass-
ing away of our beloved sister, we, the
officers and members of said association, are
bereft of a wise counselor and a cheerful and
loving associate.
	Sister Lida was ever ready at the call of
duty, no matter what her physical suffering
might have been. She carried not only her
own burdens but lightened those of others.
The needy and the suffering ones always found
in her “A Ministering Angel” in the truest
sense of the word. Her gentle presence filled
[-]he home with comfort and delight.
	Resolved, That we mourn the loss of Sister
Okerlund and appreciate the worthy example
set by her. We, her sisters and co-laborers,
extend to her bereaved husband and children
our heartfelt sympathy and pray God to com-
fort them in their great sorrow.
	Be It Further Resolved, That one copy of
these resolutions be presented to the family
of the deceased, one copy sent to her son Ivin,
who has just departed to fill a mission to the
Eastern States, a copy be sent for publication
to the WOMAN’S EXPONENT, and one copy be
placed on file in our Relief Society.
	We, the following, sign in behalf of the Loa
Relief Society.
				JAMIMA HOOD,
				EMILY BLACKBURN,
