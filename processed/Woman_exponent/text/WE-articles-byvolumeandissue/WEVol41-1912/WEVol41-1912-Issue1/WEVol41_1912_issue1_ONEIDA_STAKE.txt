		ONEIDA STAKE.
	Minutes of annual conference of the
Oneida Stake Relief Society held at Pres-
ton, May 19, 1912, in the Stake house, Pres-
ident Louisa B. Benson presiding.
	There were present Alice M. Horne and
Jennie A. Hyde of the General Board; Stake
President Joseph S. Geddes, other leading
brethren and 310 people.
	The singing was furnished by the Stake
choir under Prof. Charles Engar. Singing,
“Now Let Us Rejoice,” etc. Prayer, Elder
John Larson. Singing, “Our Mountain

[Column 3]
Home So Dear.” Roll call denoted 20
societies represented. President Benson
welcomed all and gave a brief general re-
port which denoted progress in the “Moth-
ers’” work and increase in enrollment. The
above welcome was beautifully responded
to by Meda Nelson.
	Statistical and financial report for the
year ending Dec. 31, 1911, read and ap-
proved. Secretary Phebe A. M. McNiel
reported the societies of the Stake as being
in satisfactory condition. Choir sang
“Count Your Blessings.”
	Mrs. Alice M. Horne addressed the con-
ference on the subject of the Relief Society,
bettering the conditions of the home, the
world, and of its power to give cheer and
comfort, and of the power of the individual
over self; and said: “Salvation will only
be obtained as we conquer self; the giant
‘greed’ must be banished and humility and
charity cultivated.”
	Mrs. Jennie A. Hyde gave greetings from
President E. B. Wells to the people of
Oneida Stake; was delighted to see so many
brethren present; spoke of the loving min-
istrations of the Relief Society teachers.
	President Joseph Geddes appreciated the
words of the sisters; spoke of the privilege
of living in this day; hoped all gloried in
the gospel and would live up to the light
within; advised all to be courageous and
prayerful.
	President Benson urged the Relief Socie-
ties to at once take up the work of assisting
the flood sufferers.
	Singing, “If There’s Sunshine in Your
Heart.”
	Benediction, Alice M. Horne.
