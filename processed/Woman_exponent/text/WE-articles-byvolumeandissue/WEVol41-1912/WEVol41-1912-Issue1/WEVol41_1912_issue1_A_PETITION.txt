A PETITION.
“For an acceptable sacrifice bring me a broken
	heart and a contrite spirit.”
When Thou didst send me here on earth
	awhile,
Free was my spirit from all sin and guile;
Sweet are the mem’ries of those early years.
Yet in their passing, gathered doubts and
	fears;
Fallen the idols on life’s way, 
Gone are the dear ones of a brighter day,
And desolate, I bow,
And plead that in Thy pity, Thou
Wilt reach and find me.

Then—if a broken heart atone for all,
In Thy compassion, wilt thou hear my call?
Then—might I hear in voice of love Divine,
“All that I promised unto thee is thine;”
Then as a bird loosed from captivity
Singing soars upward, so would I be free
From griefs that blind me now—
And wake from death in joy, and Thou
Receive my spirit.
