		AFTERNOON SESSION.
	Singing, “Awake, Ye Saints of God,
Awake.”
	Prayer, Elder S. H. Hale.
	Singing, “Sweet Is the Peace the Gospel
Brings.”
	Sacrament was next administered. 
	There were present 13 bishops and 695
people.
	Reading, “A Thread of Thought,” by
Melissa Maughan.
	Mrs. Jennie A. Hyde expressed joy at
seeing so many of the priesthood and such
a vast audience present; spoke of woman’s
power, the Savior breaking her estate of
thralldom, and advised her to live up to her
privilege and merit all blessings. Asked all
to seek the better part, the gifts of the
gospel and become broader and better.
	Solo, “Fear Not Ye, O Israel,” Sarah
Gerard.
	Alice M. Horne spoke of her early life;
her desire and struggle for an education;
said, “Every soul who comes to earth has
the power to progress and gain knowledge
if they will but try;” spoke of the blessings
our sorrows often are; how God-like it is
to reach out a helping hand to those in dis-
tress; advised all to seek for truth and
grasp all opportunities.
	State School Superintendent Nelson of
Utah spoke on education. Among other
good things he said: “Every individual
owes it to himself and his fellowman to de-
velop himself and find his true vocation.”
“Every true mother is a queen in the earth.”
	General and Stake officers were sustained.
	Singing, “Grant Us Peace.”
	Benediction, by Coun. Taylor.
			PHEBE A. M. MCNIEL,
							Secretary.
