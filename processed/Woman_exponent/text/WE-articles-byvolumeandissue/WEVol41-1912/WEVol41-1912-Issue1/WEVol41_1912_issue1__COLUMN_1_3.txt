THE TRAINING SCHOOL FOR NURSES.
	No finer training school for nurses can
be found anywhere than that offered by the
Dr. W. H. Groves Latter-day Saints’ hos-
pital. Miss Charlotte E. Dancy, superin-
tendent of the nurses at the hospital, is not
only a graduate of the Johns Hopkins hos-
pital school, but served in that hospital for a
number of years; and besides a thorough
knowledge of her profession, thorough
study and experience, she possesses the rare
quality of freely imparting to others.
	The students find her quite accessible and
are drawn to her by her cheerfulness and
personal magnetism, at the same time appre-
ciating the dignity of her position and re-
specting the rigid rules of the curriculum.

nurse training who never expect to become
professional, but merely to better qualify
