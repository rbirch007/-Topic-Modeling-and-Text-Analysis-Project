note for the future was in what Pres. Joseph
F. Smith had said in the last semi-annual

spoke on honoring the Sabbath day, form-
ing of habits, card playing, and cigarette

boys who do these things, the Lord would
help us; spoke on the great work of women,
and felt there was not another people who
had as much to live for as the Latter-day
Saints. May God bless you in your work in
this part of the Lord’s vineyard.
	Stake Pres. Oleen N. Stohl felt the most
important thing before us now was to im-
press our children with faith; thought we
ought to talk more to them, and speak more
of the beauties of the gospel; spoke of the
receiving of the Holy Ghost and the bless-
ing s that follow it; the mothers taking
more interest in the Parents’ classes in the
Sunday School, and participating in the
lessons taught there; felt to ask a
blessing upon the mothers and said they
could do much in instilling good principles
in the lives of our children.
	Pres. Sarah P. Stohl felt we had had
an enjoyable time, together, and thanked
the sisters who had visited us and given us
such good talks and counsel; felt we ought
to be friendly and neighborly.
	By request, the choir sang “Daughters of 
Zion.”
	Benediction by Sister Emelia D. Madsen.
			ANNIE H. LITTLEWOOD,
