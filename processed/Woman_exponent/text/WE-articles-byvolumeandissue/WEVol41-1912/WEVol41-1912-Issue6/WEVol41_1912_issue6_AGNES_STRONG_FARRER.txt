		AGNES STRONG FARRER.

		Read by Zella Farrer Stubbs.
	Agnes Strong Farrer is the daughter of
John Strong and Agnes Miller. She was

[Column 2]
born October 3, 1837, in Kendal, Westmor-
land, England. In 1846 she was baptized a
member of the Church by her father John 
Strong, who was president of the branch.
	Grandma with her parents left England
and walked across the plains and landed in
Utah October 28, 1855. In 1856 they moved 
to Provo and in 1857 she married Roger
Farrer, and they have resided here ever
since. Grandma is the noble mother of nine
children, six of whom are living. She has
always been a Church worker in different
organizations; a teacher in the Sunday
School and counselor in the Primary for
fifteen years.
	The first Relief Society meeting she at-
tended in 1870 she was called upon to sing
and pray, and this was the beginning of her
interest in this organization. She was set
apart as teacher and labored in that capacity 
many years. She was appointed president
of the teachers, also an aid on the stake 
board. She was for the last seven years a
member of the Utah Stake Relief Society
Board, acting as chorister for that organiza-
tion.
	She has always been a regular attendant
of Relief Society gatherings and an ardent
worker therein. Her labor has been one of
love and pleasure, and her influence for
good is always felt wherever she goes. She
is a strong, most beautiful character and 
can see the good that is in each person.
	By her smile, how soon the stranger and
friend knows, it is not of herself she is
thinking, not of herself but of others—an
example for us all. She is a Saint of cheer-
ful spirit, Saint of days and nights. She 
shall sit in heavenly places and have her 
heart's desire.
	God bless our dear, sweet and soul-inspir-
ing mothers and grandmothers whose di-
vinely human interest and love have given
us our glorious nation.
