Both in the St. George and the Juarez stakes
of Zion, she has traveled from one settle-
ment to another as the president of the stake
Relief Society, and many will know and
remember the words of wisdom that she
gave in counsel on these visits.
	Her husband was called to our heavenly
home February, 1902, and since that time
she has been perfectly willing to join him.
In the latter years of her life, thinking each
might be her last, she did not want to leave
her home near her husband’s resting place,
for she wanted to sleep beside him. So
when last July the colonists were forced to
leave their homes for fear of death and de-
struction from the Mexican rebels, it was
hard, indeed, for her in her eighty-second
year to go, leave her beautiful garden of
roses, her loaded vineyard and her comfort-
able home, the work and toil of years, to
travel again, when she had hoped to rest.
	She did not live long, for her ever kind
and loving Father sent for her and she went
to Him January 19th, 1913, in Ephraim,
Sanpete County, Utah.
				Theresa Snow Hill.
