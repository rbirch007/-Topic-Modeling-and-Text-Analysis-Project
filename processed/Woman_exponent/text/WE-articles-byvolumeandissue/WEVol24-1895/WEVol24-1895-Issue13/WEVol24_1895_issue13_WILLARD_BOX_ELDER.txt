WILLARD, BOX ELDER.
Editor Womans Exponent:
	THINKING you had not heard from us
for some time and it being advisable to
write a few lines to your valuable paper, I
do so with pleasure.
	Our Relief Society was reorganized on
the 12th of August, 1895, with our aged
President, Sister M. A. Hubbard, (nearly
eighty years of age) still acting as presi-
dent, she has been our president from the
first, and her trusty counselors and mem-
bers feel to try and make her responsibilities
as light as possible, and are trying to do all
the good they can. There has been some
severe cases of typhoid fever here this fall,
and some of a lighter form, but all are get-
ting better now, for which we are all very
thankful.
	Praying God to bless all who labor in the
good cause.
	We remain as ever your sisters in the
Gospel.
MARY ANN HUBBARD, Prest.
