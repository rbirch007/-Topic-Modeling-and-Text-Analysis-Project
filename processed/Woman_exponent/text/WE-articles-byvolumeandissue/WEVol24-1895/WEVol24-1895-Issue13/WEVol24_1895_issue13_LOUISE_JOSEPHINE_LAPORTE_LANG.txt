LOUISE JOSEPHINE LAPORTE LANG.

[Brief Sketch of Life.]
	LOUISE JOSEPHINE LAPORTE LANG daughter of
Joseph Marie Laporte and Louise Perret, was
born Nov. 8, 1828. at Cluses Savory, France.
	At the age of twenty-two, she went to Geneva,
Switzerland, where she was married to John
Daniel Lang in 1857.
	She was born and raised In the Catholic Faith,
but embraced the Gospel, with her husband, in
the fall of 1863, being baptized by Elder Conrad
Schweitzer, emigrated to Utah with her little
daughter (her husband following the next year)
arriving in Salt Lake City, on the 22nd of Sep.
1869, where she resided until her death; with the
exception of five months, living in Beaver duriug
the winter of 1882—83. Although an invalid for
nearly one year; she died suddenly of hemorrhage
of the lungs, at her residence in the 15th Ward,
Salt Lake City, July 18th, 1895; aged sixty-five
years eight months and ten days.
	She was the mother of three children, two sons
and one daughter.
	She leaves her husband, a daughter and a son
(who is on a mission in Europe) six grand-child-
dren and a host of friends to mourn her loss.
	She had been a member of the Relief Society
of the 15th and 17th Wards of the Salt Lake
Stake of Zion, where she had acted in the
capacity of a teacher.
	While in the “old world,” her door was ever
open as a home to the missionaries and in a hum-
ble way she led a faithful and exemplary life and
died as she had lived, in the hope of a glorious
resurrection.
	Written by her daughter,
	HORTENSE MARY  JONES.
Salt Lake City Aug. 11th, 1895.
