A HAPPY PARTY.

Publisher Woman’s Exponent:
	WE send this small article to you, hop-
ing that you will insert it in your paper.
It was a beautiful morning and the sun
sent forth its warm rays over the earth,
when the people of Farr West met with the
Relief Society for the purpose of surpris-
ing their much esteemed secretary, Sister
Christene Hegsted, President M. A.
Stephenson in charge of the affair.
	The people all marched into her residence
and were greeted very kindly, by the much
surprised sister, while tears of joy moisten-
ed the eyes of every one present. The
house of Sister Hegsted was filled to over-
flowing, so they proceeded to the meeting-
house, where a large table was spread with
many of the luxuries of life, after which a
program consisting of songs and recitations
was rendered in a very pleasing manner,
by some of the young ladies of the ward.
This was followed by a speech from Presi-
dent M. A. Stephenson, “sisters we have
met today for the purpose of showing our
respect to our secretary, who has so faith-
fully served for the past fourteen years, and
she may rest assured, that we do appreciate
her labor.
	She has been called to preside over the
Young Ladies Association, and we wish
her success in her future labors,” and a
hearty vote of thanks was tendered her.
A nice shawl was then presented to her, by
the members of the Relief Society, after
which she, herself, made a few remarks and
tendered her resignation as secretary, and
thanked the sisters for their kindness to-
ward her.
	Some very encouraging remarks were
made by Bishop W. F. McEntire and a few
brethren present. Benediction by P. C.
Stephenson.
Yours Truly,
	M. J. Rawson, Sec.
Farr West Oct. 28, 1895.
