EDITORIAL NOTES.

	A VERY pleasant party of ladies was
given by Mrs. Lydia D. Alder at her resi-
dence in this city, on the afternoon of
Thursday, Dec. 5. About twenty five sis-
ters were gathered in her spacious parlors,
which were artistically decorated with vines
and autumn leaves, and after some time
passed in social conversation a delicious
supper was served to the guests, and late in
the evening, the party took the form of a
meeting, Mrs. Lucy W. Kimball being
chosen to preside. Most of the sisters re-
sponded to the invitation to speak, and
many were the expressions of good will and
friendship for Sister Alder. Among those
present were Sisters M. Isabella Horne,
Bathsheba W. Smith, Julia C. Howe,
Elvira S. Barney, Elizabeth Webb, Emma
Woodruff, Lucy Jane Thurston, Elmina S.
Taylor, Elizabeth J. Stevenson, Maria Y.
Dougall, Margaret N. Caine, Annie T.
Taylor, Clara M. Cannon, Maria W. Wilcox
and others. The gathering was a very en-
joyable one, and the sweet interchange of
ideas and sentiments were specially gratify-
ing to all. These social visits help to unite
the sisters in ties that will be lasting in time
and continue throughout eternity.
