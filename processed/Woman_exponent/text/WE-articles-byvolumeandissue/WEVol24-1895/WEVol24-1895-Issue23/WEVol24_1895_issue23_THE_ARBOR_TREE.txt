THE ARBOR TREE.

On the school-grounds gathered
	On a smiling April day; 
Teachers, friends and scholars,
	All in holiday array.
There a little tree they planted,
	Straight and tall and slim,
Pretty verses there they chanted,
	As its roots they covered in.
Through the spring the children watched it,
	Watched its tiny leaves unfold,
And eagerly they guarded it,
	From all dangers manifold.
For they loved this tree they planted
	And they longed to have it be,
Free from every mark or blemish,
	A stately, perfect tree.
That to them ’twould be a symbol,
	Of a life upright and true,
To their hearts a happy memory,
	Of the childhood that they knew.
And this the tree the children planted
	Grew both beautiful and strong 
Like a sentinel it guarded,
	The happy childish throng. 
But the years bring many changes
	And as the children had to part
With the school friends and the school days,
	So dear to every heart.
The dear old tree remained there,
	Through the years that come and go, 
Through the springtime with its verdure, 
	Through the winter with its snow.
Ever watching o’er the children,
	As they play beneath its shade,
Ever listening to the childish vows,
	And the many games they played.
Oh may this tree forever,
	Through the years that come and go,
Be spared from wind and weather,
	From the woodman’s fatal blow. 
And live on and on forever,
	A mighty tree to be,
And children always love to gather,
	’Neath our own dear Arbor Tree.
