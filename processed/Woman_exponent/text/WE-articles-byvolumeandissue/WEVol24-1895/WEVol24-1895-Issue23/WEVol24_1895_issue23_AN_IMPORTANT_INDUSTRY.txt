AN IMPORTANT INDUSTRY.

	THERE is no doubt but all those who
have been in the past interested in silk, are
aware of the fact that a bill for the estab-
lishment of sericulture in Utah, was passed
by the last Legislature, and will make
themselves familiar with the provisions of
the bill. The Commission appointed are
very anxious to have just as much work
done as possible this year. The season for
raising cocoons is now upon us, and we
have decided the most we will be able to do
this year, will be to publish in the WO-
MAN’S EXPONENT, some instructions to
those wishing to commence work at once.
	We are also prepared to furnish a limited
amount of eggs to those who wish them.
Persons desiring to obtain eggs from the
Commission, will write at once to Mar-
gar t A. Caine, 732 S., 5th East, Salt Lake
City, and state the number of trees, and
age of the trees from which they can obtain
leaves; also the number of persons they ex-
pect to have assist them in the work. If
you are not a subscriber for the WOMAN’S
EXPONENT, we will send you the papers
containing information which will be of use
to you.
	The establishment of this industry will
also bring a new work for the Horticultur-
ists, as the raising of the best varity of mul-
berry trees will in the future be a very im-
portant matter. For the present old trees
should be well trimmed to cause the leaves
to grow thicker upon the branches, and
contain much more of the essential proper-
ties, than by letting them remain in the old
bushy state. In some parts of the State,


there is still time to set out slips; so that in 
a few years we will have thousands of new
trees growing in all parts of the State. We
are very sorry to hear of the damage done
by frost in the Southern part of the State,
but still hope the people of that part may
raise an encouraging crop of silk this year.
We will be pleased to give any information
to parties communicating with us, that will
assist them in raising silk.
