U. W. P. C.

	By special invitation the Utah Woman’s
Press Club held its April meeting at the
residence of Mrs. Syntitha Dickinson.
The meeting being called to order by the
president.
	Prayer was offered by Mrs. E. W. Hyde.
Roll call was answered by original senti-
ments. Minutes read and with corrections
were approved.
	Miss. S. L. Monroe read a most in-
structive paper on Ceramics. Giving the
origin history and present status of pottery.
She said "All other arts fade with time,
but the flowers on a Dresden vase will last
for ages, children grow up among beautiful
china with an innate love of beauty and re-
finement; the table ceases to be merely a
feeding place when set with beautiful
china.”
	This was followed by the recitation of the
“Drunkards Dream,” by Miss Edith
Rossiter. Mrs. Ruth M. Fox then read an
original poem entitled “Myrtle And
Violets.”
	Dr. Elliot was then introduced to the
club as the guest of the evening and enter-
tained the club with interesting facts in the
life and labors of Miss Barton. Gave the
origin of the Red Cross and a sketch of
Miss Barton’s work; she possesses great
executive ability; does everything in a
systematic and economical manner.
	Mrs. M. E. Shipp then sang “Sweet
Spirit hear my prayer.” A letter to the
club from Mrs. Brown, at Washington,
was read by the Secretary—and the
exercises closed with prayer by Lizzie
S.Wilcox.
	Then followed dainty refreshments
served by the charming hostess and a bevy
of lovely girls. Social interchange of
thought and pleasant converse followed and
all went home feeling benefited socially and
intellectually.
