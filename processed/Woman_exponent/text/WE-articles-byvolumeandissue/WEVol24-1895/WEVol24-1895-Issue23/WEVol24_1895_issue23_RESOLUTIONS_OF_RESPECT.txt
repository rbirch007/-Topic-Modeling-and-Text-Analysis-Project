RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.

	WE the Sisters of the Cottonwood Ward Relief
Society, do deeply feel the loss of our beloved
sister and president, Emma Morris. whom God
our Heavenly Father has in His infinite goodness
and mercy, seen fit to call from our midst.
	Be it resolved that we as a band of sisters, emu-
late her worthy examples, and her kind and
motherly counsel; she was indeed a mother to the
needy, a comforter to those cast down in trouble;
a true and sincere friend to all that knew her. We
sincerly sympathize with the bereaved children;
at the same time we ourselves feel, that we have
lost a mother.

	Memorial Services were held in honor and es-
teem of our departed Sister Emma Morris, who
died at Morgan, Utah, Jan. 6, 1896, that having
been her former home; but she had been a resi-
dent of Star Valley, Cottonwood Ward, for the
past seven years, and was also President of the
Relief Society of this place. She was very ener-
getic and faithful in all her duties, and a Saint in
very deed. She left here the first of last June, to
care for some sick relative, who resided in Mor-
gan. Her time was spent in doing good among
her relatives and friends, until she was taken sick
and died after an illness of about three weeks.
She was a kind and loving mother and sincere
friend to all associated with her. Aged 56 years,
11 months, 6 days; she leaves 7 children, 15 grand-
children and an aged father and mother, and a
host of friends to  mourn her loss. She died as
she had lived a faithful Latter-day Saint, beloved
by all who knew her.
	C.J.
