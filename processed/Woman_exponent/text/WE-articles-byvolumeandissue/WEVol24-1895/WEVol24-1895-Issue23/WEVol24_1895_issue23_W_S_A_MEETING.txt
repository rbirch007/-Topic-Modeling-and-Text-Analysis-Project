W. S. A. MEETING.
	MINUTES of Salt Lake Co. W. S. A. held
in fourteenth. Ward hall, March 17th, 1896.
Sang “O, Come, Come Away.”  Prayer
by Elizabeth Price. Sang: “The Reason
Why.” Minutes of last meeting read and
approved. Report of select committee.
Emily S. Richards had consulted with State
committee and several lawyers and found
that they disagree on the subject of Right
of Wife’s Dower, she had been treated
courteously and kindly by all and read a
written statement given by Judge Suther-
land. Her report was accepted and a vote
of thanks given Mrs. Richards for the in-
formation given: and committee released.
Mrs. J. Fewson Smith read from Roberts
Rules of Order. Elizabeth J. McFarlane
read a very instructive chapter on Rights
and Privileges and immunities of citizen-
ship. Mrs. J. R. Midgley said that the
study of Parliamentary Rules was very
essential if we were going to attend, and in
all probability conduct public meetings our-
selves in the future. Mrs. Holden resigned
as chairman of Executive Committee and
Mrs. J. Fewson Smith was appointed to fill
the vacancy, and Maria Holt appointed to
fill the vacant place on the committee.
Meeting adjourned for one month. Bene-
diction by Hannah Oswald.,
