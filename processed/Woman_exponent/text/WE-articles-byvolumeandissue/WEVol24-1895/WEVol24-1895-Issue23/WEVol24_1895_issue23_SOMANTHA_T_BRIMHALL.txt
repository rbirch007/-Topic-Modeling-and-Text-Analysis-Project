	SOMANTHA T. BRIMHALL.
BRIGHT is the morn and daylight gleams and
	sheds its rays afar,
ln silent reverence nature breathes the calm, soft
	morning air; 
Clouds resting on the Eastern hills are lit by early
	dawn, 
The bright sun through their curtain steals, again
	his light is born
To smile upon the vale beneath clothed by
	verdure’s hand
As it glitters there to form a wreath to crown a
	wasted land.
I leave the vale while all is still for silence reigns
	around
And wander to a neighboring hill, where food for
	thoughts abound,
For solitude has pleasant charms to lead the
	mind to good,
While I am cheered by nature’s forms that ages
	have withstood.
Serrated outlines rise and fall o’er columns stately
	grand
As an uneven lofty wall looking o’er all the land;
The mountains rising in the North to head the
	level plain
Roll off in grandeur in the South to meet the
	surging main;
Rich valleys gleaming down among the rolling
	foot-hills there,
Now smile with fields of waving corn and gardens
	rich and rare.
The foot-prints of an ancient race who lived upon
	this land
Are seen upon the mountains face, a sight pro-
	foundly grand;
Their ruins here are strewn around, we see them
	everywhere,
Upon the barren wasted ground which once was
	tilled with care.
I look toward the rising sun and on the moun-
	tains’ height
I see the works their hands have done, for there
	a wondrous sight!
A fortress stands, of olden time, upon the highest
	peak;
It seems to be above the line that man would
	ever seek.
And yet it stands a witness bold, its walls of solid
	stone, 
The mortal hands that made its hold forever now
	are gone.
This land has here a desert lain for many a dreary
	year
The ruined ‘Montezumas’ seen, for ages have
	been here;
No puny race could ever build those strong old
	walls so grand,
That now this mystery deep enfold “’twas once a
	favored land.”
While wrapt in muse and wonder here and think-
	ing of the dead;
There seems a low voice in my ear, "you in the
	footsteps tread”
Of ancient Lehi’s fallen race, once a chosen band
Whose record, Mormon here did trace with a
	prophetic hand.
How changed the scene, I turn again and gaze on
	vale below;
Once more the barren waste is green and pure
	bright streamlets-flow.
While leafy trees of fruit and shade hide dwell-
	ings from the view 
And cast their shadows o’er the - glade, where
	flowers bloom anew.
Or interspersed by loftier spires gleaming so
	proud above

Form arches there with twining vines, that in the
	breezes move.
Once more the feathered tribe is seen among the
	beds of flowers,
Once more their chambers high are green, their
	nests hang in the bowers:
The ancient trees whose trunks have lain per-
	haps since other ages,
Are sought by honey bees again and filled with
	choicest "dulces.”
Once more the harvest lap is filled with sheaves
	of ripened grain
And delicious fruits to redness turned by the
	summer sun and rain:
Once more the busy echo flies from the sound of
	workmens’ tools
And handsome modern structures rise for
	pleasant homes and schools.
Colonia, Juarez, Mexico.
