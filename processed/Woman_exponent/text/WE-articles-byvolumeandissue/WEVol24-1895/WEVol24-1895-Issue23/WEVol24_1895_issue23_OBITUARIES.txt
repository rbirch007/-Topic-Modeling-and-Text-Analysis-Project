OBITUARIES. 

	SISTER JANE HALL, died at Eager, Union
Ward, Apache Co., Arizona, April 5th, 1896, of
old age. She was born in North Carolina the 2nd
of March 1810. She joined the Church at an
early day and was with the Saints at Nauvoo and
was well acquainted with the Prophet Joseph.
Smith and his family. She was one of the mem-
bers of the first Relief Society, she came to Utah
in 1852 and settled at Ogden. She was the
mother of ten children five of whom are now
living to mourn her loss; she spent the greater
part of her long and useful life among the sick
comforting and blessing them especially those of
her own sex, and was ever ready and willing to
do all she could for their relief. She was a faith-
ful wife, a kind and loving mother, and a true
friend, a faithful and conscientious Latter-day
Saint, and died as she had lived strong in the
faith. She had a happy, cheerful disposition and
made many friends.
	We do recognize in her death the severe loss
of a faithful and true friend to the sick and needy
and a comforter in the hour of trouble. We also
recognize in her faithful labors and devotion to
the cause of Zion an example worthy of the
emulation of each member of our society.
