TWO LITTLE HERD BOYS.

Billy, I’m thinking of when we were boys,  
	Days that seemed made all of trouble and care,
Come back in memory laden with joys 
	Glowing with pictures all happy and fair,
Up the long canyon with whip and with pail
	Gathering flowers we followed the cows,
Gathering water cress close by the trail
	And hunting the birds nests far tip in the
	boughs.
And while our herd wandered over the hill,
	Idly we gazed on the valley far down,
While echoed around us the whistles so shrill
	Of U. P. and Denver in race for the town,
Long before dinner we emptied our pails,
	Thoughtless of hours ere the sun would go
	down,
Then homeward we hurried with songs and with
	tales,
	Two little herd boys, barefooted and brown.
Over the dry lake that shone in the sun,
	Down by the Jordan, the rushes and slough;
Wild fowl flew up out of reach of our gun,
	And fishes were plenty though we caught but 
	few.
Still we were happy, for glowing and bright
	The sun as it sank,threw its rays from the west,
And the windows of home answered back all
	alight,
And beckoned us there to a welcome and rest.
The coasting and skating! We boys were not
	cold
Though keen blew the wind and the icicles 
	grew,
’Till back by the fireside and stories are told—
	Then,Billy,’twas cold,with the chores yet to do.
O, heavy in keeping, the burden and strife!
	The riches and honors, they chain us around,
They flush and then fade. The best hours of
	life
	Were for two little playmates, barefooted and
	brown.
	AUGUSTA JOYCE CROCHERON.
April 30, 1896.
