IN MEMORIAM.

	In memory of Maria Jane Chaffin, beloved wife
of George E. Chaffin, and daughter of Moses
Wade and Mary Kirkbride Wade. She was born
Jan. 15, 1856, in Salt Lake City, died Feb. 22, 1896
in Teasdale, Wayne Co., Utah. She leaves a hus-
band and seven children, two daughters and five
sons, with many friends to mourn her departure
from this life, Our beloved sister was second
Counselor to the President of the Teasdale Relief
Society, and was ever desirous to do a good part.
She was very generous, and a friend to the poor.
She was highly intelligent, and had faith in the
principles of the Gospel; was ever ready to defend
the cause of truth she had embraced in early
youth, and taught the same to her family, and en-
couraged them to take an active part in the Asso-
ciations for the youth of Zion. She was favored
some years since, with a day vision; her spiritual
eyes were opened, and she beheld her father (who
was dead) in the spirit world preaching the Gos-
pel, surrounded by a large audience of people,
who were attentive listeners. This made a strong
impression upon her mind, and she realized the
vicarous labor being done in the life beyond; and
before her death was very desirous to attend to
Temple work. She was a devoted wife and a
loving mother, and was much respected by all
who knew her. May her family emulate her pre-
cepts and example, for she died as she had lived,
a faithful Latter-day Saint, true to the cause of
truth, and she will receive the pleasing and just
reward which is promised by our Heavenly Fath-
er to those who endure faithfully to the end.
	J.S.C.
