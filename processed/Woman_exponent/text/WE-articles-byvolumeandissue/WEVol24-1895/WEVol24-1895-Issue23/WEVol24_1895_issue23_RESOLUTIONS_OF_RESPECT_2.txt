RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.

	TO the memory of Sister Polly Carter, who de-
parted this life March 24, 1896, at her residence in
Provo City, after one year of lingering illness,
suffering with catarrh of the stomach. She was
the wife of Dominicus Carter, a Pioneer of Utah.
Sister Carter was born in New London, Huron
County, Ohio May 6, 1831. She came to Utah in
1852, with her mother Tamma Minor and the
family, her father having died Jan. 3, 1848, not far
from Boneparte, on the Des Moines River.
	She, with her parents, endured the buffettings
of the early Saints. Her mother was sick and
practically an invalid, and on her at an early age
devolved the responsibility of caring for the fam-
ily of five, as well as a sick mother, now,
	Therefore, we the members of the Relief Soci-
ety, wish to express the honor and respect enter-
tained by us for her as a true friend. She held
the office of a teacher in the Relief Society since
its first organization in this the 3rd Ward of Pro-
vo:
	She was also a member of the Y. L. M. I. A., in
which she was very much appreciated and held in

[Column 2]

high esteem, for her wise teachings and example.
	Whereas, we acknowledge her loss and her
readiness at all times to give a helping hand, and
use her influence for the furtherance of the great
Latter-day work,
	Therefore be it, Resolved that we deeply sym-
pathize with her bereaved family and friends, and
further
	Be it resolved that these resolutions be record-
ed in the records of the Relief Society, and the
Young Ladies Journal, and that copies be sent to
the WOMAN’S EXPONENT and to the family of the
deceased.
	HANNAH T. CLARK,
