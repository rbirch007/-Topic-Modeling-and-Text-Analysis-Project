			NELLIE M. LITTLE, Sec.


The citizens of Paris have named a
street after Maria Deraismes the able wo-
man suffrage advocate and leader in France
who died recently.
	The Racine, Wis, Times, has moved in-
to a new building of its own and made
great improvements in its outfit. Since
the death of the business manager, Mr.
Willis, the name of Olympia Brown Willis
appears as the head of the Publishing Co.
Mrs. Willis, known to our readers as Rev.
Olympia Brown, is a speaker of such ability
that it is a pity that she should be of
necessity tied down to business cares, al-
though she is making a success of her
present undertaking.—Ex.
	MRS. CHARLTON EDHOLM spoke on
“Traffic in Girls, and Florence Crittenden
Missions,” at the National Purity Congress
in Baltimore Md. Oct. 14th. Mrs. Edholm
asserted that of the two hundred and thirty
thousand erring girls in the country, over
half had been snared and bought and sold
into their lives of shame.
“Their average life is five years. Forty-
six thousand are carted out to the Potters’
Field every year. Over a hundred Ameri-
can homes have to be desolated every day
to recruit the ranks of shame. Twenty
million Christians can rescue 230,000 err-
ing girls, or surely the religion of the Lord 
Jesus is a failure.”
