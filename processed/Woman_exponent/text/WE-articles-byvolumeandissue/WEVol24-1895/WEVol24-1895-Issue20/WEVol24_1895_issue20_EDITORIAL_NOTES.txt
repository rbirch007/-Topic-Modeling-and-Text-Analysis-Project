EDITORIAL NOTES.

	IT is expected that at the Suffrage meet-
ing which will be held sometime during


Conference, there will be some interesting
talks by Mrs. Emily S. Richards and Mrs.
Sarah A. Boyer, who attended the Wash.
ington Convention last winter; also by
other women who have been invited to
speak, among them Mrs H. C. Lewis of
Montana who is a noted lecturer and Lucy
A. Clark of Farmington Electa Bullock of
Provo, and Mrs.Wm. Stewart of this City
have been invited to make addresses, also
Mrs. E. Crane Watson of Parowan.
	THE Woman’s Cooperative Store situated
on Main St. No. 44, is in the sense its
name implies a woman’s enterprise, incor-
porated and carried on in the interest of the
women connected with the Relief Society
organization, it has had a hard struggle in
the past but the prospect at present is more
hopeful. Having succeeded in getting a
suitable location on the Main Street where
it is more convenient, and having some
special articles not handled by large es-
tablishments. Burial clothing for the
dead is made a specialty, millinery and
boys clothes etc. There will be an opening
of Millinery goods Wednesday and Thurs-
day, April 1st and 2nd, to which the public
are cordially invited. New goods and
latest styles, prices reasonable. Remember
the Woman’s Co-op, 44 Main Street.
