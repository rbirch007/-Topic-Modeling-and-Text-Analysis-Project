LADIES’ SEMI-MONTHLY MEETING.

	MINUTES of Semi-monthly Meeting held
in Fourteenth Ward Assembly Hall, Jan.
11th, 1896, Mrs. E. Stevenson presiding.
Singing “All hail the new born year,” etc.
Prayer by Sister Elizabeth Webb. Singing
“Come let us anew,” etc. Minutes of the
previous meeting were read and accepted.
Sister E. Stevenson made a few opening re-
marks, said she felt very much disappointed
at not having our President Sister Horne
with us to-day, felt there were enough here
to have a good time. Since last we met
many things had happened, one very im-
portant thing “STATEHOOD.” We should
acknowledge the hand of the Lord in all
things. Said the Prophet Joseph Smith
told President John Taylor he would give
him a key word that would prevent him
from ever apostatizing. It was to each
morning dedicate himself to the Lord,
which he faithfully did. The speaker
strongly disapproved of speaking against
the Lord’s anointed. Prayed the Spirit of
God to be with us and go from heart to
heart.
	Singing “Reedemer of Israel.” Sister
Barnes felt it a dangerous thing to speak
against one of God’s prophets; was glad to
be numbered one with the Church.
	Sister Zina D. H. Young said it seemed a
long time since she had looked on the dear
faces of her sisters and that these meetings
were like a beacon light to her; many had
come here and been comforted and been able
to go home and bless their families. These
