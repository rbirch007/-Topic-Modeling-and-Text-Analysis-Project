A PLEASANT GATHERING.

	SISTER MARY BAXTER was given a
pleasant surprise, by a few of her friends on
the twelfth inst., the event being the seven-
tieth anniversary of her birthday.
	She was presented with a beautiful chair
as a token of love and esteem, and appre-
ciation of her good works.
	Sister Baxter acted as President of the
Relief Society of Spanish Fork for fourteen
years before the wards were divided. She is
now acting as one of the Vice-Presidents of
the fourth ward, and is honored and respect-
ed by all who know her.
	After presenting her with the chair, an.
impromptu program was rendered in
which were many congratulations and good
wishes given Sister Baxter. A grand ban-
quet was then given and about eighty
persons partook of the bounties of life.
	Among those present were the Bishop of
the fourth ward and his counselors, and a
few leading brethren of the ward, also
sisters who have labored with Sister Baxter
for a number of years.
	About five o’clock the company dispersed
feeling that they had a very enjoyable time 
one long to be remembered by those pre-
sent.
