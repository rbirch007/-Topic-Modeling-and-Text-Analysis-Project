	MINUTES of W. S. A. held in the 14th
Ward Hall Jan. 21st, 1896. Roll call,
Minutes of last meeting read and approved.
Song, “The reason why.” Prayer by Jane
Albrand. Song, “New America.” Mo-
tion of Mrs. Fewson Smith to adjourn the
meeting and attend the State Legislature to
see the election of the State Senators, lost
and the meeting continued. Rights of
States read by Secretary and reviewed by
President Kate Groesbeck. Elizabeth Mc-
Farlane requested to write an essay On
“What are the rights and immunities of
citizenship.’’ One lady spoke of girls being
dilatory in regard to taking part in politics
because their brothers and beaux objected
to women in politics. Amanda Hook
thought if girls understood the necessity of
informing themselves on questions pertain-
ing to women’s welfare, they would think
differently. Did she consider her brother or
her beau as something better than herself,
that he should enjoy more privileges than
she? and said the first duty of woman is
honorable motherhood which we must not
forget to impress upon the minds of our
daughters; for without mothers we cannot
have either home or state. We should
thoroughly understand the Constitution of
the State that we may know our rights un-
der it, and ask our Lesislators to pass laws
that will be just and benefit our women and
that will not be misconstrued. A commit-
tee was formed to pass resolutions and call
on the President of the Association and en-
quire into the law on “A wife’s dower, and
her right to hold and control property in
conjunction with her husband.” Adjourn-
ed for one month.
	Cassie C. Newman, Sec.
