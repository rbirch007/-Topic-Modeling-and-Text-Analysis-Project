U. W. P. C.

	By special invitation the Utah Woman’s
Press Club held their December meeting at
the residence of Mrs. I. Cameron Brown.
President E. B. Wells presided in her usu-
ally dignified manner, calling the meeting
to order, and for invocation by Mrs. M. A.
Freeze. The members answered to roll
call by original sentiments.
	Mrs. Lizzie Wilcox read in her sweet im-
pressive manner an interesting paper on
Margaret Fuller, which was followed by a
song by Miss Pearl Russell.
	Mrs. C. Louise Boyden then gave in her
inimitable style a humorous recitation.


By special request our amiable hostess
Mrs. Brown, favored the club with a unique
and entertaining paper on the life and
works of Frances Burney.
	The names of Mrs. S. O. B. Cummings,
Mrs. Rachel S. Miller, and Dr. Ola R. Curtis
were presented for membership and the
vote carried unanimously.
	In behalf of the club an expression of
thanks was given by the President to Mrs.
Brown, who has upon different occasions
entertained the club so generously.
	Following the literary exercises dainty
refreshments were served in the most artis-
tic manner, “intermingled with a flow of
reason and a feast of wit,” for the musical
hum of voices kept time with the rattle of
china and the pleasant reunion closed with
many a cordial grasp of hands that never
met before, and all felt that heart came close
to heart and soul communed with soul.
