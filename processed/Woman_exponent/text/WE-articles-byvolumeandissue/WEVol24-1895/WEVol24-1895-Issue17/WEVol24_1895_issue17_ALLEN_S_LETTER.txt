ALLEN’S LETTER.

	I HAVE enjoyed attending the sessions of
the convention more than I can tell you.
	Many of these women of national
reputation are more than one has believed
them to be. I was greatly delighted with
Mrs. Catt. She is a woman of marvelous
power. I cannot speak in detail of all of
them. To do it would simply be repeat-
ing what you already know better than I
do.
	On Utah night the church was packed as
I never saw a house packed before.
	Hundreds were refused admission and
hundreds of women and men stood during
the long exercises.
	Miss Shaw’s speech was short not over
twelve or fourteen minutes, but the most


eloquent I have ever heard which is saying
a great deal. Mr. Cannon’s reply was in
his usual happy vein. Mr. Allen spoke
briefly and not apparently unacceptably.
Mrs. Boyer followed with a talk that
shewed her a woman of ability. Mrs.
Richards read her paper last. It was a
thoughtful and well prepared history of suf-
frage in Utah. I never saw her look so
handsome. Her dress was beautiful and
became her well. The rest of the evening
was given up to state reports and brief ad-
dresses. It was amusing to see that even
here in this great convention there were wo-
men who would run over their time, and go
on after the bell had rung.
Washington D. C. Jan. 24th, 1896.
