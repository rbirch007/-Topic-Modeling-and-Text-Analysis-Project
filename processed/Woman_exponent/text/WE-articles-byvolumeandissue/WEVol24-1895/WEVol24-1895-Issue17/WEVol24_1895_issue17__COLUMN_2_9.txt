Of purest ray, and primal magnitude,
Shone forth a light of brightest radiance;
Which, as I gazed, enlarged, and as it grew,
Its brightness softened, and its mellowed tone
Assumed the form and substance of a man,
Of goodly parts, and beaming countenance.
A god-like man, who towered above the world,
As Gulliver beyond the pigmy tribe.

A gleaming pedestal beneath his feet,
Made up of stones, each sep’rate stone a gem,
Engraved, in shining letters with its name.
Fair Truth appeared as chiefest corner-stone
Flanked by Justice here, and Mercy there.
Faith shone serenely from her central place,
Love! Her sweet sister, snugly by her side—
While Hope, with smiling lips and eye serene,
Throughout the darkness sent long rays of light.

A standard held the Vision in his hand,
A silken banner of cerulean hue—
Enwrit,in letters like to burning fire,
With “REVELATION!”title most divine.
And with the other hand he held aloft,
A great golden key: and as I gazed,
I heard a voice of wondrous depth and power,
Proclaim this utterance to the startled world:
"The key is turned for woman: and henceforth
She takes her rightful place by brother man.
Her star is risen, and will brighter grow,
And go not out forever. Let it be!"

The vision faded, leaving in its wake,
The glorious light, the heaven-revealed, the true,
Which ever spread, and ever chased the gloom.
And from afar arose small points of light,
Which grew, and multiplied, and coalesced,
As though in sympathy with their great head,
Until, in God’s due time, the darkness fled,
And man and woman, true to his great plan,
Emerged into the glorious light of day.
