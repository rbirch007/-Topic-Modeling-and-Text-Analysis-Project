U. W. P. C.

	On Thursday, Jan. 30th, this interesting
organization met as per adjournment at the
office of the Woman’s Exponent. Presi-
dent E. B. Wells in the chair. Meeting
opened with prayer by Elizabeth J. Steven-
son. Roll called and Minutes read and
approved. Miscellaneous business being
next in order C. C. R Wells made a motion
which was seconded by Ella W. Hyde, that
the President appoint a committee to pre-
pare suitable resolutions congratulating the
Utah Woman’s Press Club that one of its
members, Mrs. Isabella Cameron Brown,
is at present the recipient of Congressional
honors. The motion was carried unani-
mously, and R. B. Pratt, E. R. Shipp and
L. D. Alder were named as a Committee.
Another motion was made and car-
ried that the same Committee should
make honorable mention of the 75th birth-
day of Zina D. H. Young, the honored
President of the N. W. R. S., and that
such Resolutions should be recorded in the
minutes. The financial report for the last
two years was then read by Treasurer C.
C. R. Wells. A motion was made and
carried that the report be accepted with
specifications. A motion that Antoinette
Brown Kinney be admitted to membership
was carried unanimously. The chairman
of the Program Committee, Ruth M. Fox,
reported work done for three months. Ella
W. Hyde moved, that as their labors were
not very onerous and as they were a very
efficient Committee, that they continue in
office another three months—motion carried.
	The regular program began by reading
of the 1st Chapter of Parliamentary Law by
Ella W. Hyde, followed by a discussion
and review of the Chapter by the mem-
bers. Phœbe C. Young then read a very
interesting paper on Herbert Spencer—the
merits and demerits of which were discuss-
ed by the Club. Single Tax was the sub-
ject of the next paper prepared and read by
