            TO AUNT ZINA.
Beloved sister! Honored friend!
Most loving greetings I now send
On this auspicious day to you;
The anniversary of your birth
When Father sent you here on earth,
A great and noble work to do.
Among the choicest spirits there.
Our Father sought, with all wise care,
To choose thee for this earthly sphere,
He knew thy true integrity,
And all thy grand nobility,
Hence gave to thee a mission here.
And through these long eventful years.
You’ve shared with Saints their joys and tears
’Mid scenes of carnage strife and blood,
When persecution’s raging hand
Spread devastation through the land,
For right and truth you firmly stood.
Predestined by a Heavenly power,
You came at the “Eleventh hour”
In these the glorious Latter-days,
Now Zion’s daughters bless thy birth
And useful mission on the earth,
And for thy virtues give thee praise.
How oft thy gentle hand hath smoothed
Life’s rugged path—and kindly soothed
With healing touch the sick and sad,
And by thy loving sympathy
Left burdened hearts from sorrow free,
And made the troubled spirit glad.
And now while thousands grateful raise,
Their loving voices to thy praise
With words of approbation.
I too would add my humble lay
To those who now their tributes pay
in fond congratulation.
O may you live while life is sweet,
And all your noble works complete
Both for the dead and living.
To see the triumph of God's cause,
And deep submerged all unjust laws,
While man grows more forgiving.
And in the twinkling of an eye
Be changed at last with angels nigh,
To drink of “Living water.”
Receive thou, blessed of the Lord;
Thy glorious and just reward.
“Well done my faithful daughter.”
E.R. SHIPP.
Ann Arbor, Jan. 29th, 1893.
