EDITORIAL NOTES.

	ERRATA—In the January number of our
paper the last stanza and ninth line of the
poem ‘‘Dying Yet Living” the word
“freedom” should read “guerdon.”
	MRS. EMILY S. RICHARDS Vice Presi-
dent At Large of the State Suffrage As-
sociation and Mrs. Sarah A Boyer President
of Springville Suffrage Association, left the
City for Washington on the U. P. express
train Jan. 19, (Delegates) to attend the
National Woman Suffrage Convention at
the Capital. A number of Delegates were
nominated at an executive session of the
State Association on the 18th of December,
1895, and credentials furnished, but
on accouut of circumstances they could not
all go to the Convention. Among those who
were nominated besides the two already
mentioned were Lucy A. Clark, Farming-
ton, President Davis County Suffrage As-
sociation; Corinne M. Allen wife of the
Utah representative who is already, in
Washington, Augusta W. Grant, Ella W.
Hyde, Margaret A. Caine, Martha B.
Cannon wife of the Senator (Frank J.
Cannon,) Margaret B. Salisbury, Nellie
M. Little, Elizabeth McCune and Mary E.
Gilmer. Our knowledge of the Convention
is very meager, only what the dispatches
have given, and one or two personal tele-
grams we have received.
