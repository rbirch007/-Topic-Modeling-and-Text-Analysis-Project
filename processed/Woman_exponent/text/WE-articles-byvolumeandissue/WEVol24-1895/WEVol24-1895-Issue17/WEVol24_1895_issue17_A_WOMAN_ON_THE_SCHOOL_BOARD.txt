A WOMAN ON THE SCHOOL BOARD.

	THE women of the new State of Utah
have good reason to be proud of the recent
appointment of Mrs. Cornelia Horne Clayton
upon the school board for Provo. Mrs.
Clayton is a most estimable woman and
well qualified for the position, both by
nature and education: her parents came to
the valley in 1847, and may with all due
propriety be termed pioneers. The lady
was born, raised and educated in Salt Lake
City, and was always in her girlhood con-
sidered strong-minded; later she matured
into a practical young woman with excellent
judgment and good executive ability; she
was a special favorite with the members of
the ‘Wasatch” a literary society of young
people that was very popular in the ‘70’s”,
and among them were some brilliant,
talented and clever young girls and boys,
who have since filled positions of honor and
trust.
	The editor of the Exponent has great
pleasure in offering congratulations to Mrs.
Clayton remembering with sincere affection
the many happy days when they were
associated together in the office, and the
agreeable companionship always maintained
and it is not surprising to the writer that
the former business manager has attained
to the distinction of filling this honorable
position, and fully expects she will yet
occupy other places of emolument and
trust in the gift of the people. Success to
the cause of equal suffrage, women as well
as men should receive some of the honors
in the new state.
