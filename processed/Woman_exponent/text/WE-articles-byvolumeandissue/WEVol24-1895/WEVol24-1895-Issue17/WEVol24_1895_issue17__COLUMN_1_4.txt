	David H. Cannon, President of the St.
George Temple, said that it was with pecu-
liar feelings that he attempted to address
the people, because of the separation from
Sister Ivins. He said he oft-times thought
we put off eulogizing our friends until they
pass away and then speak of their valuable
qualities when it will do them no good.
	We mourn because of our mortal separa-
tion, but we look forward to the glorious
resurrection, to that union which we look
forward to with such fond anticipation.
She always had a mantle of charity to throw

	Our meeting to-day shows how highly
she was esteemed by the people, and what
to us is a loss, to her is a gain and that
stepping from this stage of action is only
like stepping from one room to another, and
there is in reality no cause for us to mourn;
and, without a doubt, she has already been
greeted by some whom she has officiated for
in holy places. He asked that the bless-
ings of God might be poured out upon
Brother Ivins and the whole family.
	President D. D. McArthur said he could
not help but rejoice for the condition of
Sister Ivins. We have many of our old
people passing away in peace, having lived
faithful all their days. Spoke of the joy
we should have in laying our loved ones by
in their peaceful quiet graves. Spoke of
the way the saints when traveling, had to
lay away members of their families by the
way. He rejoiced that his father and mo-
ther had laid their bodies down in peace
among the saints of God. He was sure
that Sister Ivins had gone to paradise and.
God, and all is well with her.
	Her example has been of the very best—
let us follow it. The servants of God are
giving us good counsel and advise all the
time. Let us follow it that we may be
worthy of the blessings God has in store for
us. He rejoiced that Sister Ivins and our
old brethren and sisters, who are passing
away, are laying their bodies down with
the testimony of Jesus in their hearts.
