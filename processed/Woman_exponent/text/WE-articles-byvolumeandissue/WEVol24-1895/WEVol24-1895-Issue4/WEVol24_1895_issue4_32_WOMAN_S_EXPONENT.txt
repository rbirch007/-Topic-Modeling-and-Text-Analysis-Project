32    			WOMAN’S EXPONENT.


Relief Society Organized.
West Layton, July 12th.
	Editor Women’s Exponent:—Thinking
that it might be of some interest for you to
know that on the 13th of June, President
Susan Grant of the Davis Stake with her
counselors, also of the Stake Presidency
Hiram Grant and Joseph Clark organized
a Relief Society with the following named
officers:
	Mrs. Virginia F. Major, President; Mrs.
Emma Corbridge, First Counselor; Mrs.
Mary A. Egbert, Second Counselor; Mrs.
Antinett Dibble, Treasurer, and Mrs. Mary
Roberts, Secretary,
	Wishing you success, I remain yours truly,
							V. F. Major.


MRS. HANNAH J. BAILEY, in her recent
report as National Superintendent of Peace
and Arbitration work in the W. C. T. U.,
quotes Victor Hugo’s prediction that war-
fare would cease in the twentieth centnry,
and adds: “It will not be long ere the peo-
ple of the world will regard the wars of the
present day with the same feelings as we
now regard the gladiatorial combats and
other barbarities of former ages.”
