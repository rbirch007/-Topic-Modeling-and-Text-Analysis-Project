CURE FOR POISON IVY.

	AN excellent remedy for this poison is the
whey obtained by scalding sour milk. Dip
cloths in the whey and apply to the affected
parts renewing with fresh ones as fast as
they dry, until the fever subsides. Do not
use the same cloths more than once.


PRACTICAL inventions by women are
making a good showing at Atlanta. Em- 
ma Farrell, of Brooklyn, has invented a re-
volving frying pan, and Mrs. A. E. Taylor,
of Brooklyn, a bed-clothes fastener, which
is a handy contrivance to keep the clothes
over the children at night. Mrs. H. Ryan
contributes a metal liquid polish, which
polishes all metals, hot or cold.
