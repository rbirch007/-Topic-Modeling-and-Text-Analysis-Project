	In 1893 Colorado submitted the question
again whether women should be entitled
to the right to vote, and the women of Col-
orado made a most heroic struggle, the re-
sult of which was that the Republican par-
ty, the Democratic party and the Populist
party, put a Woman Suffrage plank in
their platforms, and my good friends the
result was that every meeting throughout
that campaign of 1893, the Woman Suffrage
question was discussed, and when election
day came and when the vote was taken
7,000 more of the Colorado men voted in
favor of woman’s enfranchisement than
against it. For the first time in the his-
tory of the world a majority of the class
holding absolute power over another class
went to the ballot box and voted “yes” to
share that power equally with the less
fortunate class of Colorado which turned
the tide for this question to be carried to the
Ballot box. Wyoming set the example for
the legislature to extend suffrage to the
women and Utah followed the example of
Wyoming: so those two states have the
credit and will have it through all time for
being the first legislatures of the Union to
extend full suffrage to their women, and
then Colorado stands as the first state in
the Union to give suffrage to women by
what we term the popular vote; and now
you are the second state in the Union which
shall have submitted to its people its consti-
tution with equal suffrage imbedded in that
fundamental law. (Applause). As Wy-
oming with full suffrage imbedded in its
constitution adopted that constitution, with
this justice to women, just so Utah’s funda-
mental law is giving to her voters, the men
of the State of Utah, the privilege of extend-
ing this great principle of justice to the
women of her territory: and just as sure as
the men and women of the State of Wyo-
ming voted for the ratification of that con-
stitution with justice imbedded in it, iust so
surely will the majority of the men of Utah
go to the ballot box and vote for the ratifica-
tion of your constitution. So friends, the tide
is turning in the direction of full enfran-
chisement to women. In several of the
states women enjoy the right of suffrage to
a certain extent: in Kansas they have
municipal suffrage established for women
and then we have Colorado, Wyoming and
Utah with full suffrage extended, for you
are just as surely there as if you were there
already, and the states of these old rock-
ribbed mountains are the first to embody
ustice in their constitution and are going to
set the example, which is to be followed by
the other states by knocking that little
adjective male out of the suffrage clause of
the Constitution. (Applause.)
