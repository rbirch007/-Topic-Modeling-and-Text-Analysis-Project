MISCELLANEOUS.

	OUR greatest glory is not in never fall-
ing, but rising every time we fall.—Con-
fucius.
	THE man who does his work well dig-
nifies it, but he does not usually wear gloves
while at work.
	ALL reasoning is retrospect; it consists
in the application of facts and principles
previously known. This will show the
very great importance of knowledge, es-
pecially of that kind called experience.—
J. Foster.
	As every thread of gold is valuable, so
is every minute of time and it would be
great folly to shoe horses (as Nero did) with
gold, so it is to spend time in trifles.—Mason.
	IT is not enough that we swallow truth
we must feed upon it as insects do on the
leaf, till the whole heart be colored by its
qualities, and show its food in every fiber.
—Coleridge.
	PROSPERITY has this property: It puffs
up narrow souls, makes them imagine
themselves high and mighty, and look down
upon the world with contempt; but a
truly noble and resolved spirit appears
greatest in distress, and then becomes more
bright and conspicuous.—Plutarch’s Lives.
