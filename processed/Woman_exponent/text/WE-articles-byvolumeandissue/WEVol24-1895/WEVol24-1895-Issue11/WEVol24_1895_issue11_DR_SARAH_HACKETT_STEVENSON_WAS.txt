	DR. SARAH HACKETT STEVENSON was
the editor of the woman’s edition of the
Chicago Evening Journal of Nov. 1st, per-
haps the most elaborate and successful
complication of newspaper work by women
ever issued.

	ONE hundred thousand men and twenty-
five thousand women in round numbers
voted for woman suffrage in Massachusetts
on the 5th day of November. This fact
will give a standing and an impetus to the
cause in this State which it never had be-
fore. We shall go before the next Legisla-
ture with an added claim to favorable con-
sideration.—Ex.
