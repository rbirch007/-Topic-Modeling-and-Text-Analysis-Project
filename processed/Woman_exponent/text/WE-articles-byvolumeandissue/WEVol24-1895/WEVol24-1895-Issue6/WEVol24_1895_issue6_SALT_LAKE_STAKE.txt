SALT LAKE STAKE.

	The regular quarterly conference of the
Salt Lake Stake Relief Society was held in
the Assembly Hall June 15th, 1895. Pres-
dent M. I Horne presiding. Present on
the stand of the Central Board, President
Z. D. H. Young, Counselors Jane S. Rich-
ards and Bathsheba W. Smith, also Mary
Ann Hyde, President R. S. Sanpete and
Sisters Lucy V. Kimball and Maria W.
Wilcox. Meeting commenced by singing
“Now Let Us Rejoice in the Day of Salva-
tion.” Prayer by Lydia D. Alder con-
tinued by singing “O! Say What is Truth.”
Minutes of the morning session of the pre-
vious conference read and approved. Pres-
dent Horne was glad to see so many pres-
ent and wanted to see them come close
together, the spirit of the Gospel was to
draw people together it is necessary for us
to pass through trials that we may become
perfect we should wake up to our many
duties. The suffrage has now come to us,
and with it new duties. We must not be
indifferent to those privileges which will
bring to ourselves a better government.
	Verbal reports of the different wards were
then called for. 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 5th,
7th, 8th, 9th, 10th, 11th, 12th, 13th, 14th,
15th, 16th, 17th and 18th were all reported
as doing well.
	President Horne urged the several
branches to pay up their dues, would not
continue the reports longer, as all would
like to hear President Angus M. Cannon.
	President Cannon felt pleased to see our
conference so well attended, and had been
much interested in hearing the reports
given of the work being done in the differ-
ent branches. Urged the Sisters to press
forward and be faithful in performing their
duties.
	Meeting closed by singing “Lord Dis-
miss Us With Thy Blessing.” Prayer by
President A. M. Cannon.
