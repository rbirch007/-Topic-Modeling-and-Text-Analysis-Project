“O SUCCESS! WHAT A TRIUMPH?"

	THE W. S. A. of Parowan held an ex-
ultation meeting the evening of theseventh.
It was a general time of rejoicing. The
house was filled to o’er flowing, as it gen-
erally is at the meetings of the W. S. A.
The room was neatly decorated for the oc-
casion. After the opening exercises yellow
ribbon badges were passed to all present.
The program consisted of speeches, songs,
music from the orchestra band. Many were
called on for sentiments or expressions of
their feelings on this joyful occasion, all
responded:
	One year and three months have elapsed
since the first organization was effected in
Iron Co. We have rapidly advanced gath-
ering profit with pleasure. Have often
been told that our meetings were behind
none in the territory.
	This is largely owing to our leader.
Who,mailed in all the dignity of her call-
ing bravely meets the unceasing opposition. 
Now that we are in a new world let us not
disband. But in the strength of our union,
live to advance the race. Be minute wo-
men, and ever survive for the right. 
						J. M. L.
			Parowan, Jan. 15th, 1896.


MISS FRANCIS WILLARD writes: "The
cry of humanity is, Let the Red Cross enter
Turkey! No other means is left to us by 
which we can help our starving brothers
and sisters in that country. It is an oppor-
tunity greater, more sacred, and more per-
emptory than any that has come to a phil-
anthropic society since history began.
