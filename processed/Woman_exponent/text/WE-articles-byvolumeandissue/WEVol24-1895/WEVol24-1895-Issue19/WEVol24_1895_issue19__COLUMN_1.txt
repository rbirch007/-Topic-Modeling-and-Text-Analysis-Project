KINDERGARTEN EDUCATION.
	THE movement all over the United States
to establish Kindergarten schools is gaining
ground rapidly, even among pedagogues, and
people are fast becoming converted to the
more recent ideas upon child culture and
methods.
	Fair Utah which abounds in children
ought not to be behind in progressive work
in this direction. Some extra exertions
have been made during the last two years to
give the children of Salt Lake City, Provo,
Ogden and some other localities the advan-
tage of this system of training, but it has
been almost discouraging at times, 
especially from a financial standpoint as fos-
tering the work has been entirely a philan-
thropic movement and it is difficult to make
it understood appreciated and recognized by
the community; therefore the maintaining
of the schools which have been established
has been by a comparative few who have
seen the good results and have been able to
comprehend the philosophy of the system,
and endorse it with zeal and earnestness for
the childrens' welfare and the good of the
race.
	Women cannot too soon urge upon
legislators and school boards the quality and
excellence of the Kindergarten.
	Froebel the originator of this system in
his trumpel-call to the mothers of Germany
says, "Come let us live with the children;"
and as every mother should live with her
children, and many mothers are severely
puzzled to know what to do with the chil-
dren and how best to train and manage
them, the mothers are the ones who should
become kindergarten educators, and the
young women of today can find no better
field or profession than the kindergarten
affords.
	In a lecture given upon this subject in
Chicago where there is a Kindergarten col-
lege, we find the remark "What becomes of
the girl graduates?" The answer is this,
"When a new and almost limitless field of
labor, which demands the highest and best
that women can bring to it stands open;
when salaries ranging from fifty to seventy-
five dollars per month, for only three hours
of laborin the school room during five days
in the week, go begging for lack of trained
teachers, when positions offering from one
to three thousand dollars a year for training
teachers, cannot be filled, we repeat where
are the girl graduates?"
