F. S. Richards made an eloquent address in
eulogy of President Zina D. H. Young,
referring to the influence she has exerted for
good in the world. He also referred to the
work of Eliza R. Snow as another illus-
tration of an unselfish and devoted woman,
but did not allow himself to wander far
from the subject of his address—Zina D.
H. Young—He also very strongly praised
the work of the Relief Society. His
address as pronounced by many to be a
master-piece, and it is hoped that it may
sometime be published.
	Mrs. H. C. Brown made a few well-chosen,
brief and instructive remarks, as did Mrs.
F. D. Richards, the president of the Relief
Society of this stake and the first counselor
of the president. In closing, Mrs. Rich-
ards expressed a hope that President Zina
D.H. Young should live and be president
of the Relief Society for at least twenty-five
years yet and asked that all so minded
should rise to their feet. Instantly the en-
tire assembly was standing.
	All joined in singing the Doxology, after
which Joseph A. West pronounced the bene-
diction.
	A noticable feature of the gathering was
that people of all sorts, the young and the
old, the rich and the poor, the learned and
the unlearned, were gathered together to
honor a character so beautiful in its strength,
so strong in its beauty.
Herald, Feb. 3, 1896.
