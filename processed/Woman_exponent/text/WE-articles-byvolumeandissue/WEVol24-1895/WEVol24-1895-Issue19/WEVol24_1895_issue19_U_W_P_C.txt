U. W. P. C.

	THE regular meeting of the Utah Wo-
man’s Press Club was held at the office of
the WOMAN’S EXPONENT Feb. 27th, 1896,
at 8 p. m. Mrs. E. B. Wells in the chair.
Prayer by Dr. R. B. Pratt. Secretary not
having arrived, C. C. R. Wells was chosen
Sec. pro. tem. Roll call responded to by
sentiments from Zina D. H. Young.
Minutes of former meeting read, revised and
adopted. A letter was read from the
Ninteenth Century Club of Provo inviting
the members of the Press Clup to attend a re-
ception given by them. Other miscellaneous
business was transacted and the Report of
the Committee on Resolutions was received
and read by the chairman of the Committee,
Dr. R. B. Pratt.
	The paper on Single Tax question by Dr.
Pratt was continued and followed by dis-
cussion opened by Phoebe C. Young who
had read Progress and Poverty by Henry
George and the Swiss Referendum. Mrs.
S. O. B. Cummings moved that the Single
Tax subject be continued for the next meet-
ing, seconded by M. A. Caine and carried
unanimously.
	Poem (original) read by Lydia D. Alder
on seventy-fifth birthday anniversary of
Mrs. Zina D. H. Young.
	The second chapter from Woman’s
Manual of Parliamentary read by C. C. R.
Wells. Mrs. Caine appointed to read the
next chapter at meeting in March. A
motion was made and carried that Dr.
Pratt send written copies of Resolutions and
Congratulations to Mrs. I. Cameron
Brown at Washington D. C. and to Mrs.
Zina D. H. Young of this City. Moved
and carried that the invitation from Provo
be properly acknowledged by the Cor. Sec.
Program read for the next meeting.
Moved and carried that the same Program
Committee, who have been very efficient,
continue in office three months longer.
	The President stated that she had re-
ceived a letter from the Countess Annie de
Montaigu asking for information concern-
ing the club, etc., to publish in an Eastern
magazine, and was pleased that the Club
was being so well known.
 Adjourned to March 31st. Benediction
by M. A. Cairie.
	C. C. R. WELLS, Sec pro tem.
