		IN MEMORIAM.
	OF Sister Laura McCleman Whipple, of Vernal,
Arizona, wife of Hyrum Whipple, and daughter
of William and Elsie McCleman, of Colonia Juarez,
who died of liver complaint, at Colonia Juarez
Chihuahua, Old Mexico, Dec. 12, 1895, while on a
visit to her mother. Sister Whipple leaves a
husband and two children, one an infant, to
mourn her loss and many sorrowing relatives and
friends, Sister Whipple was a faithful Latter-day
Saint, whose deeds were worthy of emulation.
We mourn a sister and a friend and deeply
sympathize with the family in their affliction and
resolve that we the officers and members of the
Relief Society do sincerely sympathize with and
seek to cheer the bereaved family in their hour of
trial, and that a copy of these resolutions be
inscribed on the Records of the Relief Society,
and that we publish the same in the WOMAN'S
EXPONENT. 
			Phoebe Fife, President.
			Hannah Smith Ballinger, Sec..

So, Sister thou hast left us too,
Thy Father called’st, well thou must go,
Thy mission, here below is done,
The victory over death thou hast won.

And now the battle of life with thee is o’er...
Thou wil’t labor here with us no more,
Thou wil’t,labor now in another sphere—
Not forgetting your kindred here.

Husband, children and parents, that loved yo u
well,
Will strive to be faithful,that they may dwell;
Where trouble and anguish and pain are o’er;
Where Satan and sin, can trouble no more.

So now, dear Sister, we bid you farewell, 
We long for the time, when with you we may
dwell. 
We’ll strive true Saints, indeed to be,
To share Celestial Joys with thee. 
	H.S.B.
Colonia Juarez.
