	We recognize in the death of Sister Teasdale,
the loss of a faithful, energetic worker, a friend
to the needy, a comforter in the hour of trouble,
we esteem her faithful labors and devotion to the
interests of Zion as worthy the emulation of every
member of our society. Therefore be it resolved
	That we condole and sincerely sympathize with
the bereaved husband, children and friends in the
loss of a faithful wife, loving mother and sincere
friend.
	Resolved that these resolutions be inscribed
in our Records of the Relief Society, and that we
publish the same in the WOMAN'S EXPONENT.

Gone, dear sister, thou hast left us,
Our Father in wisdom has bereft us;
Gone, to dwell in a higher sphere,
And left us awhile to sojourn here.

Bereft of thee, sister, causes anguish and pain;
But, as, our loss is your infinite gain,
We meekly bow to the chastening rod,
And, acknowledge the hand of the Lord our God.

Husband, children and friends deplore thy loss,
But are seeking nobly to bear their cross,
Knowing that thy salvation’s sure;
As thou didst unto the end endure.

’Tis true the trial is severe,
But we will seek your loved ones to cheer,
And with them bow beneath the Rod,
And seek to serve the Lord our God.

And now thou hast laid thy body down;
And gone to wear a righteous crown,
Our race also, will soon be run,
Our work on earth will soon be done.

What consolation, this doth give—
To know that thou didst nobly live,
O may we ever faithful be,
And share celestial joys with thee.
	H.S.B.
