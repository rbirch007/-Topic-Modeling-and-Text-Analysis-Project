FOREIGN LETTER.
	Nottingham, England, Feb. 13, 1896.
	Editor Woman's Exponent:
	There are a great many women in the
Church in England, both old, and young;
who would be highly delighted, and bene-
fited, could they have the opportunity of
perusing your excellent publication, many,
yes very many, of these sisters are unable
to subscribe, and are literally starving for
faith promoting and encouraging literature,
suitable to their sex. They are surrounded
day by day, with allurements and induce-
ments to sin, struggling on in the hope of
one day being able to gather to the body of
the church, fighting against the most dis-
heartening conditions, with nothing but
hope to buoy them up. Can the blessed,
yes thrice blessed sisters, in Zion not do
something to keep them, if only to have a
copy of the Exponent placed on file in
every conference house in the missions of


the world, where most of these sisters
visit, there it could be read and reread and
finally passed from one to another. Thus
doing a great amount of good. A little
money thus spent would be productive of
as much good as several Missionaries in the
field.
	Yours in the gospel of peace,
			H. A. TUCKETT,
President Nottingham Conference.
