DAYS THAT LIVE.

SOME fair days to each mortal are given,
	That shine as stars thro’ misty rain,
Breathing sweet melodious harmonies,
	A balm to soothe life’s bitter pain,
Time glides with ceaseless flight, days disappear,
	Sombre or bright, with flash and gleam,
Fresh with the dews of hope, or blanched with
		fear,
	They pass from sight a fitful dream,
Yet there are days in life’s long journeyings,
	Oasis ’mid the desert sands,
To which we turn as living memories,
	In spite of time’s destroying hand.
With eager zest—quaff its pure waters
	Elixir’s boon, to heart and brain,
Quick’ning the pulses with thrilling raptures,
	Forgetting for awhile earth’s heavy strain,
As aged pilgrims toward home returning,
	With ardent hopes of rest aglow,
So turn we oft with eager yearning,
	To revel in the days of “long ago.”
Fair days within fond memory holden,
	Light e’en through gloom of midnight—give,
And as through cloud-rifts gleam sun rays golden
	Love’s beacon light are “Days That Live.”
