	THE fifth annual Continental Congress of
the Daughters of the American Revolution
opened at Washington, D. C., Feb. 19.
The president general, Mrs. John W.
Foster, wife of the ex-Secretary of State,
reported that within six months, fifty-seven
societies had been organized, 4,700 applica-
tions for membership received, and 700 en-
rolled. The building of a continental
memorial hall in Washington was advocated.
Mrs. U. S. Grant was mentioned as one of
the new members. Mrs. Letitia Green
Stevenson, the wife of Vice-president
Stevenson, was elected president general.
