AFTER THE SAD NEWS.

My little boys, my darlings, stay with me!
The postman brought this letter which you see.
I've opened it, and all its contents read;
'Tis from aunt Lissie, Grandma Greene is dead!
Oh, Lee! my first born son, so large and tall,
You're old enough to comprehend it all.
Your loving arms, close, close around me twine,
And for a moment press your cheek to mine;
And you too, Willie, you can understand,
Sit here, dear boy, and hold your mother's hand.

In years to come, with tender, sober thought,
You will recall bright truths which grandma taught.
Evan and Heber are so young that they
Scarce now remember when she went away.
Her gentle face my babies will forget,
With all its love, but I shall see it yet. 
My brothers—sisters, we shall see her still,
Oft precious dreams will her sweet presence fill;
Soothing our cares with smiles and fond embrace,
Rich with the glories of her dwelling place.

She will be near us in each trying hour,
And father too, and we shall feel the power
Of their true love, united, pure and strong;
Strengthening, guiding, helping us along;
That we may find the rest which they have found,
In that blest home where peace and joy abound.
Let us be faithful, brothers, sisters mine,
That we may meet them in that home divine;
'Twill not be long, we will with reverence wait,
For their sweet welcome at the Pearly Gate.
									LULA.
