EDITORIAL NOTES.

	The Conference of the Relief Society of this
Ssake will be held in the 14th Ward Assembly
Rooms in this city on Thursday, June 21, commenc-
ing at 10 a.m. and at 2 p.m.  Presidents or their
Counselors are expected to report the Relief So-
ciety over which they preside.  The Y. L. M. I. A.
of this Stake will hold their Conference on the
Friday following June 22, commencing at the same
hours.  The Primary Conference will be held on
Saturday June 23 in the Assembly Hall.
	Mrs. M. Isabella Horne, her son Richard and
her sister Mrs. H. Ellis, lelt this city on the 12th
of May for England to gather up the genealogy
of their ancestry. They sailed from New York
on the Steamer Circassia on the 19th of the same
month, and have probably landed ere this on
England’s shores. Sister Horne’s departure
should have been mentioned before, as we know
the sisters in various places are interested in her
welfare, and will pray for the preservation of her
health and her safe return home, when the object
of her journey has beery accomplished.
