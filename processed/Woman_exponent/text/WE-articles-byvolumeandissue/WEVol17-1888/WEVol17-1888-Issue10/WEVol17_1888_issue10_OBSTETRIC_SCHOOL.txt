OBSTETRIC SCHOOL.

	The Summer class in midwifery held closing
exercises at Dr. E. R. Shipp's office, No. 16 Main
St., on the 22nd of September, when a number of
the ladies passed most satisfactory examinations,
and received certificates of study signed by Drs.
H. J. Richards and A. S. Bower. The names of 
the successful candidates are as follows: Mrs.
Adela Eardley and Mrs. M. J. Thompson, of 
Salt Lake City, Mrs. Dina Backman, of Hunts-
ville, Weber Co., Mrs. Fannie C. McQueen, of
Preston, Idaho, Mrs. Emma Smith, Cedar City,
Iron Co., and Miss Martha C. Hovey, Millville,
Cache Co.
	In the evening the students and teachers, with
a few chosen friends, enjoyed a feast of music,
social and intelligent converse, delicions ice
cream, cakes, fruits, and all the delicacies of the
season, parting at the close with mingled feelings
of satisfaction and regret, rejoicing over the cul-
mination of their hopes, after so many months of
earnest endeavor to prepare themselves for lives
of usefulness. But as "there is no rose without a
a thorn," neither is there one satisfactory experi-
ence without an accompanying regret. So as


these earnest workers bade adieu to each other—
"It may be for years and it may be forever," in
this life—a feeling of sadness crept in, despite the
melodious strains of "Home, Sweet Home," that
still lingered in our ears. But with the charac-
teristic fortitude of these women, thought reverted
to duty, and each mentally resolved to,

	Know it, love it, act it bravely,
		Wheresoe'er the path may lead;
	Thus obtaining greatest pleasure,
		And at last the riehest meed.
