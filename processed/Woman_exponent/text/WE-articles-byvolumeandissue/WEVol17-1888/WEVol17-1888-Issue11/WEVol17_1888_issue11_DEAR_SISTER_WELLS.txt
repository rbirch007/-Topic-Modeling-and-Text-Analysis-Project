DEAR SISTER WELLS:
	The Relief Society of San Juan Stake held its
quarterly conference at Monticello, Aug. 27th,
1888, commencing at 9 a.m., Prest. Jane M.
Walton presiding. A number of the brethren
were present, including Prest. F. A. Hammond
and Counselors, and the bishops of the various
wards. After the usual preliminaries of sing-
ing and prayer, the minutes of the previous
conference were read and approved.
	Prest. Jane M. Walton made a few remarks;
prayed that we might have the Spirit of God
in our meeting; encouraged the sisters to do
what is right, and wished the young  sisters to
attend the Relief Society.
	Prest. Mary Roberts reported the Mancos
Society; said it was in a thriving condition.
The teachers had done a good work among the
sisters. Felt like going forward and doing all
the good she could. Prayed the Lord to bless
us all.
	Prest. Jane M. Walton spoke of selecting a
Stake Secretary, and Sister Sarah Jane Roger-
son was chosen, unanimously sustained, and
set apart by Prest. F. A. Hammond. The
former Secretary was honorably released, hav-
ing done a good work.
	Sister Jane Perkins bore a faithful testimony
to the work of God. Exhorted the sisters to
keep the word of wisdom.
	First Coun. to Prest. F. A. Hammond en-
dorsed what had been said by the previous
speaker; gave some very valuable instruction 
to the sisters.
	The officers of the Stake were presented 
and sustained.
	Prest. F. A. Hammond made pertinent re-
marks concerning attending meeting. Said
not to let anything hinder us from attending
meeting; and when we came to conference to 
stay till it was over. Exhorted the Saints to
sustain those who were placed over them.
	Prest. Jane M. Walton made a few closing
remarks; advised the officers to be in union
with each other and with their bishops.
		JANE M. WALTON, President,
		SARAH JANE ROGERSON, Secretary.
