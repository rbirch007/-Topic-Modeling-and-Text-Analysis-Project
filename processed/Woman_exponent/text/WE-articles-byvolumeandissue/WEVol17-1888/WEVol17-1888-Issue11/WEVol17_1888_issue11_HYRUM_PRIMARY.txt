		  HYRUM PRIMARY.
	The Primary Association of Hyrum held its
annual meeting Oct. 2nd, 1888, at 10 o’clock
a.m. There were present on the stand Stake
Prest. Jane E. Molen, Acting Coun. Emma
Pike, Coun. James Unsworth, Prest. Martha
Allen, 1st District, Prest. Phenella Allen, 2nd
District, Prest. Maria Hanson, 3rd District,
Sisters Johnson, Stevens and others of the
Relief Society and Y. L. M. I. A. 
	Meeting opened with singing. Prayer by
Bro. C. F. Olsen. Singing, “In our lovely
Deseret.” Minutes of the last annual meeting
read and accepted. Reports of the 1st, 2nd
and 3rd Districts were read and accepted. A
well selected programme was rendered, which
consisted of songs, essays, recitations and dia-
[column 2]
logues, interspersed with short speeches from
the brethren and sisters.
	Coun. James Unsworth said, “I can see
 much improvement in  the Primary since my
last visit, which I am thankful for.” En-
couraged the children to attend their meetings,
for the time would come when they would be
called to fill responsible positions.
	Stake Prest. Jane E. Molen: "I am proud
of the exercises this morning; they are all
selections from our own Church works, and so
well rendered. I can see a marked improve-
ment, and am pleased to see so many of the
mothers come to meeting; it shows they are
also interested in their children.” Asked the
children to give them a vote of thanks.
	Sister Pike, of Logan, was very pleased to
see so many boys come up to the front, and
said, “Your exercises have been a credit to all
your Presidents, and your selections have been
very good.” Related an incident of childhood.
Said, “We should be willing to lend a helping
hand to the poor and needy; we should be
prayerful to God.” Spoke of the Fair in
Logan; thought it was very good. Said it
would be a good idea for the presidents to
visit each other’s ward. Bro. Pike also made
a few remarks. Singing. Benediction by
Bro. Orson Wilson.
