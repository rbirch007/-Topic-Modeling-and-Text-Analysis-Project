R. S., Y. L. M. I. A. & P. A. REPORTS
		      BEAVER P. A.
	The 10th anniversary of the Beaver Primary
Association was held March 23d, 1889, con-
sisting of a sumptuous dinner, and a programme
for the aged people; all those of seventy years
and upward were invited, and at 1 o'clock p.m.
the aged were seen wending their way to the
First Ward assembly hall, where a table
spread with the good things of the earth
awaited them; the blessing was pronounced by
Patriarch Johnathan Crosby; after which meet-
ing commenced. President Mary A. Lolton
presiding. Singing, "Beautiful Zion." prayer
by Bishop J. X. Smith. Singing. "Beautiful
Day." Minutes of the previous meeting were
read and approved. The programme was as
follows:
	Roy Sheppard recited "The golden keys,"
Carrie Bird sang. Lucy Jones recited "Good
resolutions." An essay on "Let us be useful,"
was read by Jane Grimshaw. Esther Clay-
borne sang. May Cox recited. Willie Ash
sang. Lillie Stoney recited "The dead doll."
A song was rendered by Willie Ash Wilford,
and Frank Burnett. Ella Pitts recited, A
dialogue was rendered by Cassie O'Donnell
and Nora Baker. Vina White recited "Be
kind to the aged." Lydia Hutchings and
Cassie O'Donnell rendered a duett. Sister
Jane Gillis made a few remarks; Counselor
Mary Fernley sang.
	Counselor Agnes Hutchings spoke a short
time, gave some excellent instructions to the
children. Meeting adjourned for one year
singing "O, come children, come," benediction
by Patriarch Daniel Tyler.
			MARY A. LOLTON, President.
			KATIE SMITH, Secretary-
