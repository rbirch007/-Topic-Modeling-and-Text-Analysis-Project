EDITOR WOMAN'S EXPONENT:
	Dear Sister—Last Sunday morning, the 7th inst.,
Sister Emma P. Toone died at her residence in this ward.
For the past six months her health has been very delicate,
but until the last five weeks she has not been wholly con-
fined to her bed; although at times during her illness, her
sufferings have been very intense, yet the cause of her de-
mise may be truthfully styled, General Debility.  In 
labors of love and devotion to the work of salvation for
mankind, her spirit was still willing, but the fleshy taber-
nacle was worn out.  Sister Toone would have been
seventy years old, had she lived until the 26th inst. Ever
since the organization of the Relief Society in 1868,
Sister Toone, until her death, has been a most efficient
and faithful laborer as a Teacher, and for many years has
presided over the quorum of teachers in the 20th ward
branch of the society.  For ten years she has also labored
with indefatigable zeal and most loving tenderness for the 
welfare of the children in the ward in the capacity of the
President of the Primary.
	At her funeral, held in the ward meeting house,
and very largely attended, several of the brethren testified
of the purity of her life, and the wisdom and energy which
characterized all her works; faithful in all things as
a wife and mother, and also as a benefactor to humanity in
the truest sense of the word.  Among the speakers were
Bishop John Sharp, President Francis, of Morgan Stake
and Bishop George Romney, who presided at the funeral.
	For several years Sister Toone's birthday has been cele
brated by many of her friends, who would congregat
about her, with tokens of loving appreciation and es-
teem on that anniversary.
	The following lines were written for and presented to
her on one of those occasions, it being her sixty-third
birthday.
