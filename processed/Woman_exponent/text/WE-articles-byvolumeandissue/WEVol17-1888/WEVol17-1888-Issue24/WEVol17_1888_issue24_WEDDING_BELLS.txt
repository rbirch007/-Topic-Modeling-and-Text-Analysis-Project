WEDDING BELLS.
"Hail !  Wedded love, 
Perpetual fountain of domestic sweets."  
	On Tuesday at half past 8 a. m., the 30th, of
April 1889, a marriage ceremony was solemnized
at the residence of Major W. W. Woods, in
Murray, Idaho, the aged Father Joset of the "Old
Mission,” officiating.  In this marriage the writer
has a particular interest, the bride being her
grand-daughter Verona May Woods, who only a
few months ago left the city with her parents who
moved from here to Northern Idaho.  The young
lady was for some time an employee of this office
and her face was familiar to those, who are in the
habit of frequenting it from time to time. The
bridegroom is Mr. Barry N. Hillard, of Murray,
formerly of San Francisco.  The young couple
started the morning of their marriage on their
wedding tour coming direct to this City where
Mrs. Hillard has many relatives and friends.
They were met at the depot on their arrival the
evening of May 2d. by a party of ladies, with a
warm welcome—greetings and congratulations
and a profusion of flowers.  They remained here
only a few days, the time being fully occupied with
visits and calls.  They left on the morning of May
7th, for Chicago where they will spend a few days
in sight-seeing returning home via the Northern
Pacific R.R. taking in St. Paul’s and Minneaopolis
enroute.  May every blessing possible rest upon
the newly wedded pair and the richness of love’s
fruition crown their lives.
	“But happy they!  the happiest of their kind!
	Whom gentle stars unite, and in one fate
	Their hearts, their fortunes, and their beings blend.”
