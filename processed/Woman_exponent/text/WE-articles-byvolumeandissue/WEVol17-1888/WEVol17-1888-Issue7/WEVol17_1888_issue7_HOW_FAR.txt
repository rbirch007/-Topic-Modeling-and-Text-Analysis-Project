HOW FAR?

	I have a daughter just launching into woman-
hood, and I feel, as every mother does, likely, 
that I should like it to be a noble womanhood
for her sake, also for my own, for I should like
to do my life's work well, and the moulding of 
characters seems to be the great work for the
mothers.
	An incident happened this morning that has
kept my thoughts busy all day; so, in the hope 
that an abler pen than mine will take up the 
subject, I submit these thoughts to the EXPON-
ENT.
	This daughter wished to wear an article of
dress, or ornament, that is certainly a fashion
of Babylon, to which I objected, when she re-
marked, that if she did not, she would appear
odd from all other girls, and she could not see
why I objected, for Mrs. P——, President of 
the Young Ladies' Association, wore one. If
the daughters of good women, and good women
themselves, follow some of the fashions of the 
ungodly, where can we draw a line so we can
know how far we dare venture and still be safe 
from danger of contamination from the spirit
that prompts these fashions?
	We want our daughters' lives to be as bright
and happy as possible, but should we not try to
instil correct doctrine into their minds, even on
the subject of dress. My faith is, that if ever
we become the instruments in God's hand to 
accomplish His great purposes, we shall even 
dress in "holiness to the Lord."
							E. T. G.
