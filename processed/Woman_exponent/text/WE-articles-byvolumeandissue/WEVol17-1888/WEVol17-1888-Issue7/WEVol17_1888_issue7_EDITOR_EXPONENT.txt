EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The officers of the Relief Society and Prim-
ary Association of Millard Stake having re-
cently visited the several branch societies and
associations, desire to express our satisfaction
with the good results and earnest labors of our
sisters, and we are pleased to note the vast im-
provement of each association under the able
management of these respective officers.
	We met with a cordial welcome, and our
meetings were well attended, and though feeble
our efforts, God's Spirit was with us, and we
rejoiced to unite with so many of the pure in
heart, for many such do we find, who, through
sacrifice and humility, are learning the truth
and wisdom of God's ways, which none can
know except by His Spirit.
	The Primary Association at Oak Creek was 
organized, with Annie N. Anderson, President, 
and Caroline Duston and Libbie Finelson,
Counselors, there having been no permanent
organization since the death of our late Presi-
dent, Eliza Lyman.
	The anxious endeavors of our Primary
children elicit great praise, and truly exhibit
characteristics of the noble spirits of this dis-
pensation, and through their early obedience to
the Gospel, are manifesting a true and solid
foundation.
	The officers of the Stake are zealous in their
labors and desire to act well their part as co-
laborers in the great mission of the Latter-day
Saints.
							D. K. O.
