EDITORIAL NOTE.

	It would, perhaps, not be amiss to state that the
remarks made by Elder F. D. Richards concern-
ing the Relief Society, at the Memorial Meeting
held in the Ogden Tabernacle on the 19th of July,
1888, were predicated upon the sermon of the
Prophet Joseph, given at a meeting of the Relief
Society in Nauvoo, April 28, 1842, and published
in the "Sermons and Writings of the Prophet
Joseph." Republished lately in the Utah Journal
at Logan, April 14th, 1888, and in the Deseret
Evening News of Saturday, July 28 (on its fly
leaf); also in the Semi-Weekly and Weekly News 
following; in the WOMAN'S EXPONENT of August 
15 and September 1, 1884, and in the "Contribu-
tor" August, 1882. The sisters who have any of 
these papers by them can easily refer to them, and
it would be well to reperuse them, as they contain
so much that is of importance to know. In the 
sixth paragraph President Smith said: "No matter 
who believeth, these signs, such as healing the 
sick, casting out devils, etc., should follow all
that believe, whether male or female. He asked
the Society if they could not see, by this sweeping 
promise, that wherein they are ordained, it is the
privilege of those set apart to adminisier in that
authority which is conferred upon them, and if
the sisters should have faith to heal the sick, let
all hold their tongues, and let everything roll on."
