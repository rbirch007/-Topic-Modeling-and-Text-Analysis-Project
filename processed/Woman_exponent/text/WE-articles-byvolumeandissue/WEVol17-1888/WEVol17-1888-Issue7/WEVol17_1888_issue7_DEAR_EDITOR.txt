DEAR EDITOR:
	With great pleasure I communicate to your
very interesting paper the following items from
Herriman:
	On the 12th of December, 1887, we were
visited by several of the brethren from differ-
ent wards.  In company with Apostle Heber
J. Grant, Prest. A. M. Cannon, Elder Taylor
and E. Morris, they held meeting that day.
During the time, they recommended the Relief
Society to be reorganized.
	We held another meeting on the 15th of
January, 1888, and elected the following offi-
eers:  Emma Jane Bodell; President; Sarah
Jane Butterfield and Emeline Butterfield,
Counselors; Nancy Jane Freeman, Secretary;
Hannah Stocking, Treasurer.  They were
blessed and set apart under the hands of Bishop
R. Dansie and Wm. C. Camp, who also made
some excellent remarks, and encouraged the
Sisters to be active in the duties devolving up-
on them in their high and holy calling. We
held our first meeting on the 2nd of February,
1888.
	Praying God to bless you in your labor of
love, I remain, with kindest regards,
		Your sister in the Gospel,
