R. S., Y. L. M. I. A. & P. A. REPORTS.
	CASSIA STAKE.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Our first Relief Society Conference, which
convened June 23 last, was quite an event in
the early history of this, the Cassia Stake of
Zion.
	Nearly all the Society’s officers were present,
also the Stake authorities and several Bishops
of wards. The several branches of the Relief
Society in the stake were represented with one
exception, and their presidents reported them
as doing well, being alive to their duties and
flourishing as well as ever circumstances would
permit.
	The Presidents of the.Y. L. M. I. A. and
Primary Association also represented their
calling although but  few organizations had
been effected as yet.
	The Conference was addressed in an interest-
ing manner by Prest. Louisa Haight and her
Counselors, also Prest. Hunter of the Tooele
Stake and Sister Todd of Utah. Prest. Hor-
ten D. Haight, his Counselors and other
brethren gave some good instruction concern-
ing the duties and privileges of the Sisters.
	The speakers expressed a hope of spending
many another such an enjoyable occasion as
was our first conference.
				Louisa Pickett, Sec.
