GLORY.

At the close of the sunny hours,
	As the moon rose in the sky,

I went to refresh my flowers,
	For the air was hot and dry.

"Why should this weed by growing
	Here at the top of the bed,
To drink of the draught I am throwing,
	To my sweet flowers?" I said.

Then sat down my empty bucket,
	And took hold of the weed,
Up by the roots to pluck it,
	Ere it should scatter its seed.

But I paused, for close around it
	A morning glory twined;
There, before I had found it,
	Two lives were in one combined.

In the weed, I seemed to discover,
	And the vine which around it clung,
A brave, though uncouth lover,
	And a maiden fair and young.

I could not commit the slaughter,
	So I said to the Red-root, "Live,
And drink still of the water
	Which I to my flowerets give.

Had I been more alive to my duty,
	And displaced you long ago,
This gentle, delicate beauty
	Had learned without you to grow."

Here's a lesson for many mothers—
	Look well to your daughers needs;
Don't leave them to cling to others,
	Who may prove to be but weeds.

The Red-root and Morning-glory,
	In this flower-bed of ours,
Repeat the old, old story—
	Parents, protect your flowers!

		LULA,
July 27th, 1888.
