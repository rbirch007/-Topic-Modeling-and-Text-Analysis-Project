OBITUARY.

	DIED, in Kanab, on the 31st of May, 1888, Sister Sarah
Harmon Crosby.  Sister Crosby, or Aunt Sally, as she was
familiarly called, both by old and young, was born March


4th, 1808, in Garret County, Kentucky. She was married,
to her husband, William Crosby, in March, 1832, and was
baptized by Peter Haw in 1843. She went with her hus-
band to Nauvoo, where they both worked in the Temple;
they also contributed considerable of their means to the
Church at this time. She came to the valley in the second
company of pioneers. Went with her husband on a mis-
sion to California in the spring of 1851. Returned to Utah
in 1858, and from that time until her death, she has lived
on the frontiers of southern Utah. When the Relief So-
ciety was organized in Kanab, she was chosen President,
and continued in that position until old age prevented her
from performing its duties. She was the mother of two
sons, nine grandchildren and four great-grandchildren. She
was a woman of most amiable disposition and benevolent
qualities of heart, and, having been blessed with an
abundance of this world's goods in times past, she dis-
pensed of her bounties to the poor liberally and unostenta-
tiously.
