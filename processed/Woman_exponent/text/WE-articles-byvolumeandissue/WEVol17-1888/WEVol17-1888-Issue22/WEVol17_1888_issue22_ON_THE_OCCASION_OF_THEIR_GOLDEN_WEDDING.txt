ON THE OCCASION OF THEIR GOLDEN WEDDING.  

Out on the brink of long years we pause,
	To echoing tread of distant day.
Thrilled with mysterious subtle cause,
	No human wisdom can e'er gainsay.

While o'er a chasm of five decades,
	Memory beckons with lamp aglow.
But who can fathom, or who can guage,
	Histories hidden in depths below?

Conflicts and victories, lost or won.
	Opportunities slighted, improved,
Burdens long carried, or work undone,
	Varying changes of ill or good.

Bright golden hours that swiftly flew,
	Time's precious jewels, recklessly strown.
Rejoicings, greetings, and sad adieus,
	Visions of beauty, and hopes forlorn!

We'll trust infinite wisdom alone,
	To read the record of years aright,
Thus far this merciful care is shown.
	Thro' morning noon-tide, to gray-haired night.

Now o'er time's chasm on FANCY'S wing,
	We roam again youth's fields of delight,
In bud and blossom of life's early spring,
	In rapturous love our pledges plight.

The years roll back like a misty dream,
	The pleasures of youth are ours once more. 
We muse again on the same sweet theme,
	Greet the same faces we loved of yore.

Blissful existence mem'ry doth hold,
	Firm health and courage, youth's strength and stay
She gives us back in measure full told,
	Voices, kind greetings, music's sweet lay.

As light breaks o'er the fallen snow,
	Cheering late travelers on their way,
So MEMORY, with her lamp aglow,
	Gilds over the pathway of decay.

And from this our "Golden Wedding" day,
	In humble faith we will bide our time,
Until called from earthly scenes away,
	Into life more perfect and sublime.
