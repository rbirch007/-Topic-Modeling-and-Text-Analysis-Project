REFLECTIONS IN FASTDAY MEETING.

	My dear Sisters, especially those whose whole
souls are centered in the interests, and redemp-
tion of Zion; I am perfectly satisfied in my
mind that there are many at the present time, that
are like minded unto myself, whose burden of
their prayer is the redemption of Zion, it seems to
be the theme and topic of our Tabernacle and
Ward meetings, and it is a generally acknowledged
belief, that, that event is not far distant. But it is
very possible that even among the saints, there
may be some to whom this prayer for Zion's re-
demption may become a matter of habit, like unto
the professing Christians of to-day; praying daily
for God's Kingdom to come, without ever once
seriously asking themselves the question, do I un-
derstand what I am asking for, and am I doing my
part towards bringing it about? Well if this is the
situation that we many of us are found in to-day,
it is high time that we were aroused out of our slum-
ber, lest that day should come upon us, as has been
foretold, like a thief in the night; therefore with
a view to this end, and an eye single to the glory
of God, (though I feel myself one of the least in
His Kingdom.) I feel led as a matter of duty to
write the reflections of my mind on this great and
important subject, namely the redemption of
Zion.
	It has for some time been the most attracting
sound that met my ear, so when the opening
prayer was offered at the fastday meeting, March
7th, 1889, in which a petition was offered, for the
time to be hastened when Zion should become a
power in the midst of the earth, it caused many re-
flections to arise in my mind. It seemed to
cleave unto my mind with such force that I could
not shake it off, and the more I looked into it, the
more real it seemed to be, that small as the chosen
few of the members of God's Kingdom set up in
these last days appear to be, compared to the
nations and kingdoms of the whole earth, it is
nevertheless true, that Zion will yet become a
power in the midst of the earth; hence, the
question arose in my mind, but how long will it
take to bring that about under present existing
circumstances? and especially with the spirit of
Babylon increasing so fast in our midst; for it
seems as if instead of Zion purifying herself for
that glorious time, the saints are daily manifest-
ing an increased desire for the things of the world
and the pleasures and vanities thereof, instead of
seeking after the things of the Kingdom of God.
	Such were the reflections of my mind, as
testimony upon testimony was added, more or
less bearing on that theme or subject, until a
brother arose and spoke in tongues, and I felt at
the commencement of his testimony, that there
was a mighty power of the spirit of God in the
place, which seized my whole frame, and just as
quick as lightning, the veil was withdrawn from
my eyes, and as the tongue was acknowledged
though uninterpreted, to be on the redemption of
Zion, I saw it right close at hand just as soon as
the saints would wake up to a sense of their situ-
ation, as in the light and sight of God, and turn
round and forsake the ways of the world, and do
His will and keep His laws and commandments
with all their heart; just so sure was he waiting
ready to stretch forth his mighty arm to deliver
them from their enemies; and also with attending
the Relief Society meeting in the afternoon, and
having the privilege of listening to the wise
counsel and instructions of Sister Higbee from
Salt Lake City, who so plainly and powerfully laid
before us, the follies and abominable ways that the
daughters of Zion had fallen into, which in the
sight of God, have been a great burden on my
mind ever since I came to the land of Zion.
	This caused me to reflect, and to say to myself,
would to God that I could do anything toward
bringing about a better state of things? and the
answer to the question came to me in this manner,

[Column 2]

I will ask all my sisters that are earnestly plead-
ing for and waiting for the redemption of Zion, to
oin with me in pleading with God our Eternal
Father that he may so inspire the hearts of His
servants, that have the leadership of His Church
and Kingdom, with such a measure of His spirit
that they may be led to see and realize the pride
and vanity that exists in His Church, in the light
that He sees it, and that they may be led to do as
is spoken of in the word of God, to one of the
Ancient Prophets, namely, "to lift up their
voices, and cry aloud and spare not, and show
Israel or Zion her transgressions, and His people
their sins. And I feel as sure as I live, that just
as soon as we as a people are led to see ourselves
in the light of truth and the spirit of God enlight-
ening us, and turn round and do His will, and
walk in His ways, will that be fulfilled, spoken of
in 102 Psalm and 13 verse, namely, "Thon shalt
arise and have mercy upon Zion, for the time to
favor her, yea, the set time is come." So as I
had suffered myself to be held back from testify-
ing of the light that was given unto me, I felt
prompted to write the feelings of my mind, in
hopes that it may do some good towards the up-
building and rolling forth of the work of God;
realizing that quenching the workings of His
spirit, gives Satan more power over us for the
future. I would not have ventured to dare to take
this upon me, feeling my littleness and insignifi-
cance in the Kingdom of God, but was encour-
aged by remembering the instance found in the
1st chapter of St. John, where Andrew after hav-
ing found the Saviour, and believing that he was
indeed the Christ, was anxious after his brother
Simon Peter, and it states, "he sought him and
brought him to Jesus," and in that act he took one
that became the most mighty and valiant in His
service. So in hopes that in like manner, though
this come out of weakness, it may be received by
some of the same spirit and power as the Apostle
Peter, that it may be carried forth to the honor
and glory of God; and that we my dear sisters,
may be led to cease not our supplications unto
God our Eternal Father, and keep not silence be-
fore Him, until He makes Zion a praise and a
power in the midst of the earth.
	Your humble sister in the cause of truth,
		M. A. C.
