A PLEASANT GATHERING.
	On the 28th day of March, the sisters of
Pleasant Green Relief Society wended their
way with baskets filled with the bounties of
earth,to spend a few sociable hours with one of
their co-laborers, Sister Nancy Deardon, Second
Counselor in the Society, who has been afflicted
for the last year and a half so that she could
not leave her home; and our good sisters think-
ing their presence would cheer and buoy her
up all came out enmasse for a few hours; and all
cast in their mite and presented her with a
handsome easy chair. Elder Samuel G.
Spencer composed a few verses for her in be-
half of the sisters, underneath them were en-
rolled the names of the members of the Society
these were put in a handsome frame and were
read during the entertainment. The sisters
also gave her a small sum of money. We had
a pleasant time and all felt well repaid and we
left Sister Nancy feeling that she was not en-
tirely forgotten.
			ALVIRA L. HIRST, President,
			FANNY H. JENKINS, Secretary.
