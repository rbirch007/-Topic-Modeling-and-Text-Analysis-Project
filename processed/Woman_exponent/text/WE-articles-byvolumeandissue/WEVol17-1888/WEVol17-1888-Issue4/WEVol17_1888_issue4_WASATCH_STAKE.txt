	WASATCH STAKE.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The semi-annual conference of the Relief
Society of Wasatch Stake convened in the
Stake House, Heber City, May 14th, 1888.
On the stand were Prest. Emma Brown and
Counselors, Sister E. S. Taylor and Miss Mary
E. Cook, of Salt Lake City, and several of
the brethren.
	Services began by the choir singing, "God
moves in a mysterious way." Prayer was
offered by Bro. J. R. Murdock, Singing.
Minutes of former conference, also statistical
and financial reports read and adopted.
	Prest. Brown, in her opening remarks,
wished the various presidents to give short
and concise reports, as she wished the visiting
sisters to occupy the greater portion of the
time.
	All the branches, eight in number, were
represented as in a prosperous condition. All
had contributed for the furnishing of the
Stake House the portion assigned them; had
tried to look after and minister to the com-
forts of the poor under their charge, and dis-
charge faithfully all the duties and require-
ments placed upon them.
	After the reports, Sisters Taylor and Cook
occupied most of the remaining time. Ex-
pressed pleasure at meeting with us in this
capacity, and also at the good reports. Spoke
strongly on the rearing of our children; also
in keeping them under our own influence. They
touched upon most of the requirements of the
mothers in Israel. Their remarks were listened
to most attentively by a large audience, and
all felt amply repaid for attending confer-
ence, by the wise and motherly counsel of the
sisters.
		JOSEPHINE C. JONES, Stake Sec.
