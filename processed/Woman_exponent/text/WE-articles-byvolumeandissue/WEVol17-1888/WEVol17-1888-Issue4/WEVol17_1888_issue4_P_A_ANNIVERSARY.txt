P. A. ANNIVERSARY.
	The Primary Association of Big Cottonwood
held their annual meeting on the 14th of June,
1888. A very enjoyable time was had. We
had present with us from the city, Sisters E. C.
Clawson, L. A. Wells and M. H. James, each of
whom expressed themselves highly pleased with
the progress of the children. The report for
the year showed the society to be in a flourish-
ing condition.
	The programme consisted of songs, solos,
essays, select readings and recitations. We
have a paper called the "Primary Trustworthy,"
to which the children write. There was held,
at the same time, a Primary Fair. The work
in it was very creditable, consisting of aprons,
quilts, lace collars, embroidery, bread, butter,
yeast, cake, and from the boys were tables,
ladders, hay rakes, cupboards, cabbage plants,
turnips, peas, bread and pies; and numerous
other articles, all of which showed great care
and thought.
	There were two meetings held, one at 10 a.m.
and one at 2 p.m. Each of them were largely
attended by both parents and children.
