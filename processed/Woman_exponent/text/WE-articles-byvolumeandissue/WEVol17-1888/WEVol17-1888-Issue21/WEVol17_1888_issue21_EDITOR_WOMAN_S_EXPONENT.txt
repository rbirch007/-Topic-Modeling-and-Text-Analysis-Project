EDITOR WOMAN'S EXPONENT:
     On the nineteenth of February, the Teachers
of the Relief Society of Santaquin, and a few
others met in the school house for the purpose of
showing their respect to Sister Sarah Jane Halli-
day, (wife of Bishop George Halliday), who has
been called away, in connection with her hus-
band to American Fork.  She has been a faith-
ful teacher in the Relief Society of this ward; and 
in waiting upon the sick, she was always on hand,
we shall all regret losing one that has been so
good.  
     After all were seated, a short address, and a few
appropriate lines were read by the Secretary of 
the Relief Society.  Sister Halliday was then


presented with a neat purse containing money
which was donated by the sisters.
     She responded in a very impressive manner,
thanking the sisters for their kindness towards 
her, followed by her husband, who made a short
and affecting speech, thanking them all for their
kindness towards his wife; exhorting all to be
true to their religion, live united, and have charity
for each other.  Closed by asking God's blessing
to rest upon all. 
     Our newly appointed bishop, Brother Eli Open-
shaw, made some very appropriate remarks,
touching upon the excellent qualities of Sister
Halliday, and the good she had done in our com-
munity.  Brother J. C. Stickney also spoke of the
loss we would sustain, in parting with Sister
Halliday, and asked the Lord to bless her in her
new home.  President E. J. Stickney of the Re-
lief Society; Counselor Wall, Bettie Boss, presi-
dent of the teachers, and Counselor Ann J.
Stickney, made a few encouraging remarks.
Each one expressing regret in parting with Sister
Halliday. 
     All joined in wishing her a pleasant journey,
and health and happiness in her new home.
			     NETTIE TIETJEN, Secretary.
Santaquin, March 17th, 1889.
