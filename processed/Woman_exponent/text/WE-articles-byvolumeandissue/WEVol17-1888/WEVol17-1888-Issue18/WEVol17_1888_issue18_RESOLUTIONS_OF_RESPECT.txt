RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT. 

	We, the undersigned members of the Panaca Relief So-
ciety, having been authorized to draft Resolutions of Re-
spect to the memory of our beloved sister, Mary Rice, who
departed this life Dec. 10, 1888, beg leave to submit the
following:
	Whereas, our Heavenly Father, in His infinite wisdom,
has seen fit to remove from our midst to a higher sphere
our worthy and beloved sister, Mary Rice, and,
	Whereas, the intimate relations held during a long, useful
life by her, with the members of the Relief Society, makes
it fitting that we record our appreciation of her, therefore,
be it
	Resolved, That we, the Panaca Relief Society, deeply
regret and feel really the loss of one so loved, faithful and
esteemed, and feel to extend our heart-felt sympathy to
the bereaved family and relations who are called upon to
part with an affectionate mother and friend, and with them
acknowledge an Almighty God in our bereavement; and
be it
	Resolved, That we recognize in the death of Sister Rice
the loss of a dutiful and faithful officer, and we look for-
ward to that time when the graves will open and the spirit
and body be again united, when the earth will be purified
and become the home of the faithful Saints.
	Resolved, That a copy of these Resolutions be tendered
the bereaved family; also that one be placed on our re-
cord.
		ELIZABETH WADSWORTH, Prest.,
