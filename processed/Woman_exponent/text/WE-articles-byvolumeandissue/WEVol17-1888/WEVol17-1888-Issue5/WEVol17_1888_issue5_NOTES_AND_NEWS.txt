NOTES AND NEWS.

	“The announcement that Louisa M. Alcott’s
home at Concord, Mass, is to be sold, leads to
the suggestion that it be bought by subscrip-
tion and converted into a home for poor child
ren, as a memorial to the author so well be 
loved by young readers.”		
	At the Chautauqua Assembly at Framing-
ham, Mass.. Prof. Townsend a few days ago,
warmly advocated woman suffrage, and spoke
in high terms of the valuable service rendered,
by the women of the Boston School Board. His
words were received with enthusiastic applause
and a general waving of handkerchiefs by the
assembiy.—Ex.
