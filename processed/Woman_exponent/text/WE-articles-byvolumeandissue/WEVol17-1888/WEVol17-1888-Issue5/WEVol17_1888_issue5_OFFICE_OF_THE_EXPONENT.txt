OFFICE OF THE EXPONENT:
	DEAR LADIES:—I have been very much grati-
fied with the literary attainments and pleasant tone
of the editorials in your paper.  I have certainly
a very different idea of the trials and struggles of
the Mormon Pioneers, from what I have had be-
fore.  I wanted to know the standing of the
women rights' advocates, among your people, and
I have been gratified indeed.
	I wish that I might help many who do not believe
in their good faith to feel differently, although I
could not myself believe in the Book of Mormon;
but the noble, loving hearts, whose self-surrender
is unknown, appeal to my sympathies.
						MRS. A. CLARK.
	Nantucket, R. I.
