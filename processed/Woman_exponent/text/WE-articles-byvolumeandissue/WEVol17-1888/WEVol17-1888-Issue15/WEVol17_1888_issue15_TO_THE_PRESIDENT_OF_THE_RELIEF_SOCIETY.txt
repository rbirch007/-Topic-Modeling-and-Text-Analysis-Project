TO THE PRESIDENT OF THE RELIEF SOCIETY
	OF THE CHURCH OF JESUS CHRIST OF
	LATTER-DAY SAINTS:
	Dear Sister:—I have just finished the report
of the Relief Societies of these islands to for-
ward to you, but, having had no previous cor-
respondence, I do not know where to address.
I have not received the EXPONENT for some
time, and, as I think that, perhaps you have
received a letter for the EXPONENT from the
islands ere this, concerning the work of the
Relief Society I will not write to it at present.
But I will say, we held our Fair at the time of
our conference at Laie, Oct. 6th, which was
attended with marked success in the ability of
our native sisters.  Seven handsome quilts
were on exhibition, a large variety of babies
hoods, little shoes and stockings, lace tidies,
underwear, dresses, aprons, etc. etc., All
kinds of useful and ornamental work; over
two hundred articles in number, and reflecting
much credit on the ability of our dusky sisters.
This work was done by the four Societies of
this island only.
	They take great interest in this work, and
it is an industry that should be continued
among them, as it will tend to help them to
renounce their slothful and indolent habits,
and make them more fit for the association of
white people.
	Our Hawaiian sisters wish to send their
alolia nui to yourself and the societies in
Zion.  Ever praying for the advancement of
the work of God, I have the honor to be,
	Your Sister in the Gospel.
