SISTER JULIA C. HOWE.

Dearest sister, fain I'd bless you.
Fain would drive the pain away.
Which so sadly does distress you,
Making dark the sunniest day.

But, take courage, God is near you,
Med'cine to His Saints He gives;
Then, my sister, never fear you,
Thro all His love forever lives!

May He send the balm of healing 
Quickly on your suffering head;
May you hear His voice revealing
Your spirit shall be clothed and fed.

Then look up, dear, passive be,
Soon a peaceful time will come;
Soon rejoicing days you'll see,
And fill with peace your blessed home.
	HANNAH T. KING.
June 1, 1882.
