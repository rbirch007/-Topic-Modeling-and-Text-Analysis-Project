SKETCH OF MARY G. CROOK.

	Mary Giles Crook, was born April 13th, 1833, in Calver-
ton, Nottinghamshire, England; the sixth child of Wm.
and Sarah Huskinson Giles. Baptized into the Church of
Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, A. D. 1850; emigrated
to the United States, 1854, arrived in Burlington, State of
Iowa the same year; stayed there one year. Then came
to Council Bluffs, resided there one year. Crossed the
plains in P. H. Merrill's ox team company, arrived in Salt
Lake City, in said company on the 15th of August, 1856,
settled in Provo City; married to John Crook, September 6,
1856, in said city by Bishop Jonathan O. Duke. Moved
to Provo valley in 1859; received her endowments in Salt
Lake City, July 27, 1861; was set apart as first counselor to
Sister Katie Foreman, President of Relief Society, West
Ward, Heber; also set apart as first counselor to Sister Sarah
Cummings, President of Y. L. M. I. A. of said ward.
	She was the mother of nine children, six sons and three
daughters. Age at death, 55 years and five months exact;
was five months to a day paralyzed. She died faithful to
the Gospel. A dutiful mother and faithful wife; always on
hand in case of sickness or death. When the dreadful
scourge diphtheria visited Heber City some years ago, she
was as a ministering angel with her companion Maggie
Hicken among the afflicted both night and day. The
Lord blessed her in her comforting rounds with health and
strength, with only one exception, having a slight touch of
the scourge for about twenty-four hours. She was a woman
of great endurance, never having had much sickness until


the last five months of her life. Peace to her memory; she
has gone to receive her reward with the just and faithful.
	Yours respectfully,
		JOHN CROOK.
Heber City, October 28, 1888.
