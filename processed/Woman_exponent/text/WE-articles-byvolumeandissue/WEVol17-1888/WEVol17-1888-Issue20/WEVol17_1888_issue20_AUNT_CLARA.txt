AUNT CLARA.

And is she gone? that gentle soul,
	Passed to another sphere?
The rapturous joy which greets her there!
	The bitter sorrow here!

No purer spirit ever dwelt
	Within an earthly frame;
Her charity reached every heart—
	It sought the blind the lame.

Did she e'er laugh? I do not know,
	But ever on her face
There shone a lovely cheerfulness,
	That lent it heavenly grace.

Aye, she could weep. Such tender tears,
	For others' woes they fell;
So keen she felt your every smart,
	Her own forgot to tell.

Generous? Our part we gladly tell,
	The poor could tell the whole,
And kind? Her very presence was
	A benediction to the soul.

Rest in earth, sweet rest in heaven,
	So let our sorrow cease.
Her ways were ways of pleasantness,
	And all her words were Peace!
