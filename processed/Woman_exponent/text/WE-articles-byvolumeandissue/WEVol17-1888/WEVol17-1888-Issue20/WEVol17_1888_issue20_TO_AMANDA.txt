TO AMANDA. 

Sweet friend of my childhood, kind friend of my youth,
Firm guardian of virtue, bright lover of truth,
Thy sleep shall be peaceful, unbroken thy rest,
Thy spirit, disburdened, shall sleep on God's breast.

Oh, well we remember, sweet spirit, the light
God lent us a while ere it sank in the night—
In the night we call death; yet in vain do we see,
For the day-spring of glory envelopeth thee.

Oh, many may envy thy happier lot,
To die in thy beauty, to live unforgot
In the hearts of thy loved ones, who ever will shed
The tear of affection, rememb'ring thee dead. 

In songs with the angels thou taketh thy part,
The glory of Heaven now filleth thine heart.
Earth's woes now may languish, no more for thy brow
Shall they weave cruel thorns—thou art slumbering now.

The river of Heaven now laveth thy feet,
Fair angels shall twine thee a bridal wreath sweet,
And am'ranth immortla shall crown thy fair head
Oh, in heaven they deem thee not, dear one, as dead!

Sweet, sweet be thy slumber, unbroken thy rest,
Sleep, sweet as a babe, on the Savior's kind breast;
God grant we meet thee on Heaven's bright shore,
To part with thee, darling, in grief nevermore!
