		SARAH M. POMEROY, Sec.

	At the Stake conference, 23d and 24th, which
was held at Mesa, Sarah M. Pomeroy was ap-
pointed to fill the place of Sister-E. G. Mac-
Donald, as Stake President, with Sarah E.
Russell and S. A. Robson, as counselors.
	We had a very pleasant reunion of the
Relief Society of Mesa, on the 27th at the
Society Hall, as a farewell party to our be-
loved president,  and a very sociable and
agreeable time was enjoyed by all who parti-
cipated. Dancing was indulged in by those
who desired, both old and young taking part.
In the course of the evening, our president was
presented with a beautiful maroon plush covered
toilet case, for a lady. It was presented by Sister
Sarah E. Russell, as a token of love and
respect, from the Relief Society of Mesa, in
a few choice words suited to the occasion, she
briefly reviewed her labors of love amongst us.
Sister MacDonald was deeply affected at this
fresh proof of the love and respect in which
she was held. She responded in a feeling
manner, said she should never forget the many
happy hours spent in the society of her sisters,
and wherever her lot might be cast, should
remember them with grateful affection.
	On the evening of the 28th, which was the
evening before her departure, a few of her
friends planned to surprise her with a visit,
and with pic-nic and social conversation, the
evening passed pleasantly away.	S. M. P.
