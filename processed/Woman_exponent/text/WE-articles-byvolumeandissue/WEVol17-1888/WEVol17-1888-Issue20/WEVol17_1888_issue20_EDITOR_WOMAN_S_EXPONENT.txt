EDITOR WOMAN'S EXPONENT:
	In justice to my wife, I must write you a
few lines, which you may publish if you like.
	Sister Dubois has had a severe sickness
during the winter, and has been attended to by
the members of the Relief Society in particu-
lar, and by the good people of Santaquin
in general, with such tender care and attention
that she feels to express her gratitude and sin-
cere thanks to all those who assisted in, or ad-
ministered to her during her prostration.
	During the three months of her illness—
which was caused by a premature confinement
—she was for three weeks lying at the point of
death, and a good many of my neighbors, like
myself, thought that I was going to lose her,
and it seemed that nothing short of a miracle
could restore her to health.
	But she is a very good Saint, and a strong
believer in the gift of healing by the laying
on of hands by the Elders of her Church, and
as soon as the brethren had administered unto
her she whispered to me that she would get
well again some day. Thank God! she is at
present able to resume her duties again and
take care of her eight children.
	Poor unbeliever as I am. I almost envy her
her faith in the Gospel. It puts me in mind
of a great French writer, Chateaubiand, who
in his "Geni du Christianisme," portrays the
powerful influence the mind has on the consti-
tution.
