RIGBY.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The R. S., Y. L. M. I. A., and P. A., of
Rigby, held their annual meeting on February
25th, 1889, Sister Annie Robbins, presiding,
singing by the P. A., "'Tis Meeting Day,"
prayer by Bishop Cardon, singing, "In Our
Lovely Deseret." Minutes of the different
associations were then read and approved, and
the annual reports of each association were
read, and officers sustained. A vacancy of the
second counselor having occurred in the Prim-
ary Association; Sister Ellenor Homer, was
elected and set apart to fill that position, the
 Primary Association then proceeded with
their exercises as follows. Opening address
Miss Edith Saxton; answers on the first prin-
ciples of the Gospel a class of the larger
children; song by Sarah E. and Laura P.
Call, "Put my little shoe away;" recitation by
Russell K. Homer; dialogue between Olive
Robbins and Edith Saxton, on housekeeping;
recitation by John Saxton, "Give the little
boys a chance;" duet by Thomas E. and Chas.
Doman, "Two little birds are we;" recitation,
Alfred R. Homer, "The first cigar;" the ten
commandments, in verse by the smaller chil-
dren; recitation by Betsy Saxton, "The swal-
lows and I;" song by Lillie May Saxton, "I'm
going to write to papa;" recitation by Omer
Cardon; recitation by Laura P. Call, "little
Miss Tidy;" recitation by Maria Call, "I've
got three kisses so sweet to give;" recitation by
Ellenor Basset, "Nobody knows but mother;"
recitation by Maggie Saxton, "Busy little
fingers;" singing by the Primary Association,
"Nice young Mormons;" benediction by Bro.
D. S. Robbins.
		MARY E. CALL, President.
		ANNIE DOMAN, Secretary.
