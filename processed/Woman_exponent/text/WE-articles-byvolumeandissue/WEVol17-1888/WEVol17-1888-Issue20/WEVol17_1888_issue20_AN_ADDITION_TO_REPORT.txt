AN ADDITION TO REPORT.

	In the report of the Union proceedings o the
Executive Session of January 24th, the statement
concerning the chronology of the proposed Na-
tional American Society ought, in order to give
the understanding of the committee on the Union,
to read—As the two Societies were organized the
same year, and as the difference of chronology
results from a slight difference in the way of
naming the first annual meeting, it has been de-
cided that it will be true to the spirit of the
chronology of both Associations, to call the next
year's Convention the 22nd.
	Also, Miss Shaw and Miss Stone Blackwell
were present, not as technically representing the


American Committee on Union, except in the 
sense that they could have a fair idea what their 
Committee would be apt to accede to.—Rachel
Foster Avery, Sec. of N. W. S. A. Committee
on Union.
	Philadelphia, February 19, 1888.
