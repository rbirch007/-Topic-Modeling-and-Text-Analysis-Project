VISIT TO FARMINGTON PRIMARY.

	In company with Sister Ellen C. Clawson, on
Saturday, Sept. 29, I went to Farmington to attend
the Primary Conference and Fair.
	In the morning we passed through the Court
House. It was surprising to see what the ingen-
uity and industry of the children had accom-
plished in the useful and ornamental articles they
had spread there for gratified parents and friends
to look upon. One room was devoted especially
to the offerings to the Deseret Hospital from the
Davis County children; the many cans fiiled
yearly, dried fruit, etc., and a nice cheese, made 
by a little boy, for which please accept thanks on
behalf of the board.
	Sister Aurelia Rogers, the County Prest. of P.
A., must have been highly gratified with her able
assistants and the kindly presence of the Bishop,
Counselors and parents. A good spirit prevailed,
and we had a very enjoyable time.
