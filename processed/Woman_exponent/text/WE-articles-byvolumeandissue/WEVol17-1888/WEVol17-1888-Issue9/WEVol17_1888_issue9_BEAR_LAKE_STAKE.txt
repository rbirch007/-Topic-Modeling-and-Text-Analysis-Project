BEAR LAKE STAKE.

	The Relief Society of the Bear Lake Stake
held their Twentieth Semi-annual Conference
at St. Charles, July 7th and 8th, 1888, Prest.
Julia P. Lindsay presiding. Saturday 10 a.m.
Singing, "Come, ye children of the Lord."
Prayer by W. M. Allred. Singing. Roll
called. Minutes of previous conference read
and approved.
	Prest. Julia P. Lindsay made a few opening
remarks. Reported the Societies as being in a
good condition, as far as she knew.
	The presidents of the different branches of
the Society gave in their verbal reports, which
were favorable.
	Coun. E. Collings made a few remarks, in
which she plainly set forth some of the duties
pertaining to the Latter day Saints, and showed
the good results of obedience to God's laws.
	Annie Laker, Stake Prest. of the Primary
Association, referred to some of the duties
resting upon the members of the Relief So-
ciety.
	Closing remarks were made by Mrs. Lewis
and W. M. Allred. Singing, "Love at home."
Benediction by Elder Noall.
	Sunday, 2. p.m.: Singing, "When trials
shall be no more," etc. Prayer by Prest. Geo.
Osmond. Singing, "I know that my Redeemer
lives." Sacrament was then administered.
	Prest. Julia P. Lindsay addressed the con-
gregation a short time. In her remarks she
spoke of the duties that rest upon mothers in
regard to teaching their children the principles
of the Gospel. Desired the sisters to be dili
gent in their labors to assist in building the
Tabernacle; also in storing grain.
	Prest. Geo. Osmond made a few very appro-
priate remarks.

[Column 2]

	Prest. James H. Hart said, "We are all
members of the Church of God, and there is a
labor for every one to perform." Spoke of
the progress the Saints are making, of the
beautiful cities and towns they have built, and
of the work there is still to be done. 
Choir sang an Anthem. Conference ad-
journed until the 2nd Saturday and Sunday in
January. Benediction by Bishop John Hunt.
