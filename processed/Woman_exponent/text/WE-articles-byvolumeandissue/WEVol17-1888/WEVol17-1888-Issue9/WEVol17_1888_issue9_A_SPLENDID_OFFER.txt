A SPLENDID OFFER.

	The Salt Lake Herald is adopting a novel
and praiseworthy method of securing to its
several editions subscribers. Arrangements
have been made to furnish all those who take
either the Daily, Semi-Weekly or Weekly or
Sunday editions of the Herald with a sewing
machine at one-half to one third the usual
prices. To assure the public that no fear may
be entertained of this machine, it has been
tested by W. H. Rowe, Esq., Superintendent
of the Z. C. M. I. shoe factory, with leather,
heavy cloth and fine goods, and has proven
thoroughly satisfactory. Each machine has
been thoroughly tested, and is supplied with
a complete set of attachments. Four styles
are carried. The cheapest, including the
Daily Herald for one year, is $27.50; including
the Semi-Weekly Herald, $20.50; including the
Sunday Herald, $20.00; including the Weekly
Herald, $19.50.
	If $6.50 more be added to each of these
figures, it will show the value of the dearest
machine, including the papers.
	Full particulars can be had on application
for information.
	It also appears that the Herald Company
have arranged for an extensive drawing of
valuable premiums in connection with the
Semi-Weekly during the coming week. Over
1,000 premiums are promised. The Herald is
evidently up to the times.
