OBITUARIES.

[For the EXPONENT.]
	Died, at Oceanville Long Island, N. Y., Nov. 6, '88,
of typhoid fever, Mary Eliza, wife of Wm. R. Sopher
and daughter of Richard and the late Amy Bower, born
June 15, 1834. Was baptized into the Church of Jesus
Christ of Latter-day Saints, by her father, in 1861; and
always remained faithful to her belief.

	To the memory of Barbara Morgan Maughan beloved
wife of W. H. Maughan. She departed this life Sept. '19,
1888, aged 53 years 8 months and 27 days; she leaves a
husband and family to mourn her loss. Her mortal re-
mains were followed to the meeting house by her family,
and the officers of the Ward Relief Society, where
Prest. Pitkin, Bishop Hughes and Bishop Molen spoke in
praise and eulogy of the pure life and many virtues of our
beloved friend.
	She was Counselor to the President of the Relief Society
in this Ward, being released on account of failing health,
with a benediction and a blessing from the sisters, as her
counsels were always gentle and loving. She was one of
the pure in heart, and died as she had lived, beloved and
esteemed by all who knew her, in full faith of a glorious
resurrection and reunion in the life to come.

	"Friend after friend departs,
		Who has not lost a friend !
	There is no union here of hearts,
		That find not here an end.
	She is not dead whose noble mind
		Lifts thine on high—
	To live in hearts we leave behind,
		Is not to die."
