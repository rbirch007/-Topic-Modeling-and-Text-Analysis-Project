RISON.

	On the 4th of this month the inauguration of
President-elect Harrison will take place. The
Republican party are jubilant over the victory
they have gained elaborate preparations are
being made for the event, and it is expected the


Capitol will be crowded with visitors to witness
the grand display and the imposing ceremonies,
and to be a part of the thronging multitude that
ever waits on greatness.
President Cleveland leaves the White House
with the good feelings of the people; his adminis-
tration has not by any means been a failure, and
he has certainly shown himself a man of strong
character and without fear, and has maintained
his own opinions and views, independent of favor,
or the prejudices of his own party.
Mrs. Cleveland has been exceedingly and de-
servedly popular.  Her youth and beauty, her
amiability of manner, and that modest grace
which carries with it a peculiar charm, has en-
deared her to all withwhom she has come in con-
tact.
And so one goes out and another comes into
power, and thus the wheel of life turns round, but
the destinies of men and of nations are in the
hands of the Great Ruler of the universe, and as
the rivers of water, He turneth them whitherso-
ever He will.
