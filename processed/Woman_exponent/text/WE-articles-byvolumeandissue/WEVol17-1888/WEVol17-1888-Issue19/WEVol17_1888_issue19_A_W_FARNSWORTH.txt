	A. W. FARNSWORTH.

Are not life’s burdens beyond endurance? 
Questions the mourner’s sad heart,
But a comforting voice breathes assurance,
Not long you’ll remain apart;
She has been called as one of God’s chosen
To labor in a brighter land;
’Tis by His will that she's been taken—
Called from our mortal band.

’Tis left for us to try and profit
By her life’s crosses so nobly borne;
It is left for us to strive to mend
The poor hearts by sorrow torn.
Be comforted, we kindly say,
And acknowledge God’s hand, of love,
Your dear one has been justly called
To the beautiful realms above.

Parents your child has left a name—
’Tis an honor to her and you,
Free from cruel guilt or shame, 
And ’tis loved by not a few.
As child and maiden she was true loved,
The favored of her parents’ home.


As life companion, a gentle, true,
Kind woman she was known.

As friend and associate she’s proven
So noble, pure and good,
As a member of our Mutual band
At duty’s post she stood.
On father, mother, brothers, sisters,
We ask God’s blessing to descend;
Yes, in the heart of every mourner
May a soothing solace wend.

And heal the wounded heart of husband,
He who’s been so tried and true;
Hover o’er her precious infant
Whom its mother scarcely knew.
Yes, place your strong arm round your babe,
Keep him close to you and God;
In the paths of truth and virtue,
E’en the paths his mother trod.

And ere his infancy hath faded,
Or to manhood he’ll attain,		
Teach his lips and heart to love
His mother’s sacred name;
And when this short life is ended,
And no more on earth you’ll roam,
At the pearly gates of heaven
Mona’ll wait to welcome you home.
