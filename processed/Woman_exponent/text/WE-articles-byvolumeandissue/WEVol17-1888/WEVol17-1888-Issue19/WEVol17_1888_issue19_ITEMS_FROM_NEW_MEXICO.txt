ITEMS FROM NEW MEXICO. 
EDITOR EXPONENT:  
	The Relief Society of this place was organ-
ized four years ago, since which time we have 
been laboring in a quiet way, doing all the 
good we can, visiting the sick, comforting those 
who mourn, and administering to the needy.


Monday, Jan. 21st, we held a special meeting, 
and were blessed with the wise and fatherly 
counsel of Coun. E. N. Freeman, of the Stake 
Presidency, on our duties as wives and mothers 
in Israel, and as members of the Relief Society;
also in regard to teaching our daughters the 
laws of health.  
	Bishop J. R. McNeil spoke of the duties 
resting upon the sisters in visiting and adminis-
tering to the sick; also many other duties. 
	We had a time of rejoicing; the Spirit of the 
Lord was with us, and we felt strengthened for 
our labors in this portion of the Lord's vineyard.  
	We are surrounded by the red men of the
forest, and our every action is closely scanned 
and criticised by them.  They compare our 
actions with those of other people with whom 
they come in contact, and we feel that as sis-
ters we are doing a good work among them by 
our home example.  
	Ever praying for the spread of the Gospel 
and the redemption of the tribe of Israel, I am, 
	Your sister in the Gospel,
