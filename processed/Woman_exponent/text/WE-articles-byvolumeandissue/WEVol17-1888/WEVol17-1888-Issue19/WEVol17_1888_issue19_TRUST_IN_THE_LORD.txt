TRUST IN THE LORD.

Thou mourning soul, bowed down with grief,
	Oppressed, bereaved and worn,
There is a source of sure relief
	For hearts by sorrow worn.
Our God is just and will repay
	The smiter and the smit;
He will wipe all thy tears away,
	And heal thee every whit.

It needs must be offences come
	To overflow the cup,
And prove the justice of the doom
	The wicked filleth up.
But thou, offend not; keep thy feet,
	Though bleeding, in the way,
The straight and narrow way, and sweet
	Will be thy coming day.

If God hath chosen thee for one
	His name to glorify,
To prove that justice will be done
	Offenders proud and high;
Shouldst thou repine, unwilling be,
	And weary Him with cries?
May, they who bravely bear will be
	Most lovely in His eyes.

Foretaste thy joy and lighten woe,
	By yielding all to Him;
"He chasteneth whom He loveth!" oh,
	That faith should e'er be dim!
No human love or mortal joy
	Can faintly shadow this,
Which seeks to cleanse thee from alloy
	And give thee endless bliss.

Oh, trust in Him with boundless trust,
	With faith unwavering go
Where'er He leads, though in the dust
	Thy heart be stricken low;
Take up thy cross and follow Him,
	Though seas above thee roll;
'Mid sunless depths or deserts dim
	He'll not forsake thy soul.

Oh, rest assured whate'er thy lot,
	Though lone, obscure, and low,
By Him thou never art forgot
	In welfare or in woe.
No tears of thine shall fall unseen,
	No sigh exhale unheard;
His love, thee and thy griefs between,
	Takes note of every word.

And in His own swift circling time,
	That soon will roll away,
He'll bring thy wounds sweet healing balm,
	And crown thy night with day.
Be not in haste, do not complain,
	Nor doubt His loving care,
But trust His word and bless His name,
	And yield not to despair.

For swiftly comes the fated hour,
	 The hour of joy and doom,
When broken falls the rod of power
	Which wrought thy days of gloom.
And grief's pale cheeks shall bloom with joy,
	The pure, the deep, the true.
Unspeakable without alloy—
	Take heart, it comes for your.
