SCATTERED THOUGHTS.

	Dear sister:  I have read so many useful and
instructive pieces in this valuable paper; I have
long desired to throw in my little mite with


the rest of the Women of Zion, for I think we
as mothers and daughters in Zion, should all
put our little mite together, and help roll on
this great and glorious work in which we are
engaged in these the last days, and to help fill
up these precious columns; but as for myself
I feel very weak and unlearned to try such as
this, but I have a desire to try and learn, and
accomplish some little good while I am per-
mitted to live, and to fulfill the mission I was
sent here to do, and I realize it is a great and
important work which we as young mothers in
Israel have resting on our shoulders, to know
how to train our precious children which our
Father in Heaven has blessed us with, and
which are our glory.  Then, dear sisters, let us
all strive to gain wisdom and knowledge, that
we may know the true way to train them, and
to set good examples before them, for I know
this is our greatest work we were sent here to
do, and let us strive to have them grow strong
in the faith so they will not be "as chaff before
the wind," but be as ripe wheat that is in the
bind, where neither wind nor storms can hurt
nor destroy them, and which our Father in
Heaven will love to claim.  And although we
are weak let us ever press onward, be faithful 
and true, and may we be as the wise Virgins 
with oil in our lamps.
	  Your sister in the Gospel,
						    K. L. C. 
Jan. 15, 1889.
