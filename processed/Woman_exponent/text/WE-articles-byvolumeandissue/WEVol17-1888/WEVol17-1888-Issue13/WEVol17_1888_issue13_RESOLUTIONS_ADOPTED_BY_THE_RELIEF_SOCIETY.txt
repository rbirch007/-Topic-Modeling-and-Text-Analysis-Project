RESOLUTIONS ADOPTED BY THE RELIEF SOCIETY.
	Whereas, in the dispensation of Divine Providence, our
beloved sister and counselor and co-laborer, has departed
this life, and ceased her earthly labors,
	Resolved, That we lovingly cherish the memory of her
peaceful influence, and her untiring efforts to promote and
sustain Relief Society interests.
	We commend her impressive example in imploring God's
blessing on the afflicted everywhere, and her pleadings in
behalf of the wayward.
	We bless the memory of her dear, diligent hands which
reached out in blessings for the needy, in administrations
for the sick, carried consolation to the bedside of the dying
and assisted in the last sad rites for the dead.
	That while we endeavor to bow submissively to this be-
reavement, we extend deep sympathy to the family and
more immediate friends.
	We would add that a life so rich in experience and so
beneficient in its labors for the good of humanity must reap
the reward of eternally reciprocated love, and be crowned
with unfading glory.
	That we have these Resolutions spread on our minutes
also preserved in our historical record books and published
in the WOMAN'S EXPONENT.
		SARAH M. KIMBALL,
	In behalf of the 15th Ward branch of Relief Society.
