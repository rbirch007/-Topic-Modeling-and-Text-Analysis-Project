DEAR EDITOR:
	With great pleasure I communicate to your very
interesting paper the following items from North
Point:
	On the 11th of September, 1888, we were visited
by Sisters M. I. Horne and Elizabeth Howard, and
by our Bishop and Counselors, assisted by Bishop
Schoenfield, of Brighton, we were organized into
a Relief Society. The following officers were
elected: Hannah Hanson, President; Margaret
Reid and Ann Williams, Counselors; Margaret A.
Hanson, Secretary; Rozina Rudy, Treasurer.
The officers were blessed and set apart under the
hands of the Bishopric.
	We had some good instruction given us, and
were encouraged in the duties we have to perform.
We held our first meeting on the 19th of Sept.,
1888.
	Praying that the blessings of God may be with
us all, I remain,
		Your sister in the Gospel.
	MARGARET A. HANSON, Secretary.
