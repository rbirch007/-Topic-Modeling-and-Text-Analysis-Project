   		    TIMPANOGAS.
DEAR SISTER WELLS:
At a quarterly conference of the Relief So-
ciety of Utah Stake, held Sept. 3rd, 1886, it
was announced that a branch of the Relief So-
ciety would be organized in the Timpanogas
Ward (situated on Provo bench). The name
of Caroline Daniels was proposed as President
of that branch, and accepted by the conference.
Sister Daniels stated that she had chosen Sister
Joanna Patten as her First Counselor and 
Sarah L. Partridge as her Second Counselor,
which was also accepted by the conference. 
On Sept. 23rd, 1888, Bishop P. M. Wentz
called a meeting of the sisters of the Timpano-
gas Ward, for the perfecting of the organization
of the Society. Prest. David John, of the
Stake Presidency, and Sister Mary John, Stake
Prest. of the Relief Society of Utah Stake,
were present. The names of the officers chosen
to fill the positions of the President and Counselors
were presented to the sisters and accepted.
	Prest. John explained to the sisters, that the
officers chosen to preside over them had been
presented to the Presidency of the Stake and 
approved by that quorum, next they had been
approved by a Stake conference of the Relief
Society. Hoped there would be union in their
approval of the officers so chosen to preside
over them.
	Sister Julia E. Loveless was chosen Secretary,
and Loraine Meecham as Treasurer, which was
also approved.
	At a future conference of the Relief Society
the officers were set apart to fill the offices as-
signed them, under the hands of Prest. A. O.
Smoot, David John and H. H. Cluff.
	Our Society only numbers eighteen members;
we have five teachers. Our settlement is
scattered over four miles of country, making it
inconvenient for the sisters to attend meetings
very regularly, but seem willing to do all they
can or are called upon to do. 
Your sister and co-worker.
