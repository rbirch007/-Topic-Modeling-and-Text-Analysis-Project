		  OFFICIAL NOTICE. 
To the Officers Of the National Wo-
        man Suffrage Association:

     The Secretary of the N. W. S. A. some
months since addressed a personal letter to each
officer of the Association, requesting her views
as to the most effective methods of work during
the ensuing year. This was done that from
the collective wisdom so garnered, the execu-
tive committee could formulate the best plans
for advancing our reforms.
     It was asked that replies should be in the
hands of the secretary by January 10, 1889, in
order to give time for their digest and due
arrangement; it is hoped this has already been
done. But should any official thus far have
failed to reply she is begged for an immediate
response—letters to be sent to Mrs. Rachel
Foster-Avery, Riggs House, Washington D.
C. All vice-presidents are requested to make
short succinct reports (replies to be sent as
above) of the work done in their respective
states and territories during the past year.
     It is through such general interest shown at
the time of our annual Washington conven-
tion, that our reform progresses. It is hoped
that a large number of delegates elected upon
the new Basis of Representation will be pres-
ent.
      Let this session of the Fiftieth Congress be
also made memorable through the large num-
ber of individual petitions for the removal of
political disabilities, as recommended in the
present year. Such petitions are in the highest
degree educational both to Congress and the
country, going through the associated press to
all parts of the United States.
				MATILDA JOSLYN GAGE,
				Chairman Ex. Com.

Training School for Nurses
     On February 4th, 1889, I will open a Training
School for Nurses; the object being to make edu-
cated nurses of women and girls, and prepare them
for competent work either in hospital or private
practice, so that skilled service can be furnished
and good pay demanded. The instruction will be
similar to that of the Boston Training School, and
will occupy a period of three months. Those not
prepared to enter at the beginning of the course,
may do so later, and preceding lectures will be
made up to them. Special arrangements can also
be made for obstetrical teaching.
