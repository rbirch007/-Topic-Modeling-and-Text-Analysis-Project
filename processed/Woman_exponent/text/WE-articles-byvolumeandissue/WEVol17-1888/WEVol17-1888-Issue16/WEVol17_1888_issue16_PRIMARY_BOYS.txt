PRIMARY BOYS.

	Christmas morning—bright and beautiful—
while I sat quietly thinking of the many
children that were made glad by kind old
Santa Claus, and wondering if the teachers of
the youth of Zion were doing much good—all
at once I heard sweet music and singing.  Open-
ing the door I saw five little boys, aged from
six to eight, with drums and banjo, and such
sunny faces, and they saluted me with, “A
Merry Christmas to you.” I felt and said,
“God bless all the dear children!” To me it
was like bread cast upon the waters, found on
a Christmas morning.  I want you to know,
Mrs. Editor, that these boys belong to the
Sixth Ward Primary of Logan, and their
names are, three little John Benches, Charley
Thorp and Willard Jones.
	How sweet it is to be remembered by the
boys of the Primary ! May they live long to
do good, and obey their parents and be kind to
the poor and the aged; and I want to express
A Happy New Year to all the Primary child-
ren, and also to the old and young, through
the medium of the EXPONENT.
	Our Primary is in a healthy condition, and
well attended by officers and children, and I
wish A Happy New Year to the EXPONENT.
