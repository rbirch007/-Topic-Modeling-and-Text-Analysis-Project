ARTICLE XI.
	All local suffrage associations that may be
organized throughout the Territory, by adopt-
ing these rules, or similar ones, and the Con-
stitution of the N. W. S. A., shall be considered
auxiliaries, and their representatives or dele-
gates will be expected to attend a Territorial
Suffrage Convention should one be called, and
entitled to the privileges pertaining thereto.

	A suggestion was made in reference to dele-
gates to the National Woman Suffrage Conven-
tion to be held in Washington, D. C., Jan. 21,
22 and 23, and Mrs. Kirby nominated Mrs.
Emily S. Richards as one of the delegates,
which was seconded and sustained by unani-
mous vote. Mrs. Margaret Caine was then
nominated by Mrs. Alder, and the nomination
seconded she was chosen unanimously. The
Credentials for the delegates were alluded to,
and the officers were reminded that the Execu-
tive Committee were authorized to prepare the
Credentials. The ladies came forward and
gave their names to the Secretary, and about
one hundred were enrolled members of the
Association.
	An adjournment was moved, as there was
no further business, and the motion being
seconded the meeting was adjourned for one
month, notice of time and place of meeting to be
published by the Secretary.
