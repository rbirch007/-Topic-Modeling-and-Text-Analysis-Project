RELIEF SOCIETY FESTIVAL.
	The Fifteenth Ward branch of Relief Society
gave a festival in honor of the widows and the
aged of the ward, in the ward hall, Jan. 8, 1889.
Sisters Binder, Dunkenson and Rudy, as super-
vising committee, were efficient in making the oc-
casion enjoyable. The Bishopric of the ward
honored the occasion with their aid and their
presence, and we were happified with the presence,
by invitation, of a few veteran sisters, Society
workers, from other wards. Some of Zion’s
sweetest singers graced the occasion, and en-
chanting music helped to banish care to the land
of forgetfulness. Bro. Elias Morris, ex-member
of the Bishopric, offered the opening prayer.
	Prest. S. M. Kimball said, “This gathering is
designed as a sociable;" she hoped a spirit o
sociability would prevail, and that we might have
a very enjoyable afternoon. A feast was prepared
for the body, after which she hoped we would
feast the soul.
	Seventy-nine surrounded the tables, who were
served by ready and willing waitresses. After
dinner came a season of social interchange of
thought, then music, interspersed with speeches,
wherein some interesting reminiscences were
made prominent. 
	Prest A. M. Cannon being present, related
some of his early remembrances. The hearts of
those convened seemed warmed and strengthened
to meet the vicissitudes and continue the journey
of life.
	The sisters regret that their accomodations were
not sufficient to reach more of the loved and re-
membered ones. We look forward, and with the
spirit’s eye see a time when, with unlimited ac-
comodations, we will enjoy a grand reunion amid
scenes of bliss unspeakable.
