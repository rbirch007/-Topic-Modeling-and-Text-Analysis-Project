AN ASSOCIATION FOR UTAH.

	An official call having been previously
issued, signed by influential women of the
Territory, for a meeting of suffragists, to be
held in the Salt Lake Assembly Hall, Thurs-
day, January 10, 1889, accordingly, in re-
sponse there was a large number of ladies in
attendance, residents of Salt Lake City and
vicinity, and representative women from other
parts of the Territory. Upon the platform
were several prominent ladies—Mrs. Zina D.
H. Young and Mrs. Margaret P. Young, Mrs.
Emily S. Richards, Mrs. Lena Wells, Mrs.
Nellie Webber, Mrs. Lydia D. Alder, Mrs.
Charlotte I. Kirby, Mrs. Maria Y. Dougall,
Mrs. Birdie Cummings, Mrs. L. Ardelle Cum-
mings, Mrs. Phebe Y. Beatie, Mrs. Julia C.
Taylor, Mrs. Edith Knowlton and others. At
the reporters' table were seated Mrs. E. B.
Wells, Editor of the WOMAN'S EXPONENT,
and Mrs. R. B. Pratt, M. D. Opposite the
platform were Mrs. M. I. Horne, Mrs. Sarah
M. Kimball, Mrs. Jane S. Richards, Mrs. B.
W. Smith, Mrs. Mary Pitchforth,Mrs. Elizabeth
Howard, Mrs. Nellie Little and other distin-
guished ladies.
	Mrs. Zina D. H. Young stated the object of 
the meeting, and that Mrs. Emily S. Richards
had been duly authorized to preside and to
organize a Woman Suffrage Association for
Utah Territory, having been appointed Chair-
man of the Executive Committee for Utah at
the last Annual Convention of the N. W. S. A.
in Washington, D. C., April, 1888. Mrs.
Richards then took the chair and said, "Will
some one nominate a Secretary?" Mrs. M. Y.
Dougall noninated Mrs. L. D. Alder as Secre-
tary pro tem. Mrs. Ardelle Cummings seconded
the motion. Carried.
	The meeting opened with singing by the con-
gregation, "Know this, that every soul is free;"
and an earnest prayer was offered by Mrs. M.
I. Horne.
	The Chairman then requested the Secretary
to read the Call, which was responded to, and
the call was read.
	The ladies of Utah Territory who are inter-

	Chairman said, "You have heard the Call
and understand its object; we will now proceed
to organize."
	Mrs. M. Y. Dougall nominated Mrs. Margaret
Caine, President of the Woman Suffrage Asso-
ciation of Utah; the nomination was seconded,
and the motion put by the Chairman and
carried unanimously. Chairman suggested
that some one nominate three Vice-Presidents.
Mrs. C. I. Kirby nominated Mrs. Lydia D.
Alder as First Vice-President; seconded by 
the ladies on the platform; Chairman put the 
motion; carried. Mrs. L. D. Alder nominated
Mrs. Nellie Webber, Second Vice-President;
seconded by several of the ladies on the plat-
form; carried. Mrs. Ardelle Cummings nomi-
nated Mrs. Priscilla Jennings Riter, Third
Vice-President; seconded and carried. Mrs.
Birdie Cummings nominated Mrs. Cornelia
H. Clayton, Secretary of the Association; mo-
tion seconded and carried, and Mrs. C. I.
Kirby Corresponding Secretary; carried. Mrs.
Birdie Cummings nominated Miss Margie
Dwyer, Treasurer of the Association; carried.
Chairman said, "The appointing of an Execu-
tive Committe is now in order." Mrs. Alder
nominated Mrs. M. Y. Dougall, Chairman of 
Executive Committee, and the names of Mrs.
Nettie Y. Snell, Mrs. Ann E. Groesbeck, Mrs.
Phebe Y. Beatie and Mrs. Jennie Rowe were
then presented as members of such committee;
voted upon and carried. Chairman asked the
secretary to please read the Constitution of the
National Woman Suffrage Association. Secre-
tary read as desired.
