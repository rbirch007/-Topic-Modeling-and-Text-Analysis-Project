MIXED PARTIES.

	This is the season when parties are many
and numerously attended. Social enjoyment
is sought by all classes and by all races of
people, and the people of this Territory have,
since its earliest settlement, encouraged,
especially in the winter season when out-door
work was often almost impossible, innocent and
recreative amusements and social gatherings.
When in each town and settlement everybody
knew everybody else, and was sure of meeting
only friends or acquaintances in whom he or
she had confidence, there existed no necessity
for strictness of inquiry as to who would be
present at a party. But things have changed,
and strangers are often now admitted to parties
in this Territory who are not proper associates
for innocent and pure-minded young people,
especially young ladies. We refer to what has
been called “mixed parties," where the price
of admission is placed at a certain figure, and
where every person is welcome who will pay
the money at the door. To such places every
character, no matter how vile, can claim
admission; and, there, simple and guileless
young girls may be thrown into the society of
men practiced in all the wiles and arts by which
maidens are lured to destruction. No wise
father or mother will allow their children to
attend such parties if they are aware of their
character; and as some of this kind have been
already gotten up, with the invitations headed
“select,” and as it is presumable that others will
be so gotten up, we earnestly recommend
parents to make the strictest inquiries where
the slightest doubt rests upon their minds, that
they may be satisfied beforehand their children
are not exposed to blighting evil, nor thrown in
the way of insidious and dangerous temptation.
