SPLINTERS.	
				—
	An agent has been sent to Germany in
the hope of securing 1,000 new hands to
work in the Lake Superior mines......For
the third time within nine years, the Jack-
sonville, Illinois, female college was lately 
burned......A Mrs. Kendall, wife of an officer
at Fort Davis, Texas, shot and killed a negro
corporal in the act of entering her sleeping
room, through the window in the absence
of her husband, and was justly applauded
......Black small pox has been widely spread
through Toledo, by the theft by some
students, of a body from the grave-yard
which had been taken off by that disease.
......Upon the first reading in the Spanish
Cortes, of the bill to abolish slavery in Porto
Rica, the vote was given in favor of the
ministerial measure, 180 to 6......Lotta, the
actress, lately reported dead, denies the 
report by a published card......Eight board-
ing house masters have been arrested by the
United States authorities in San Francisco,
for shipping seamen and demanding pay 
of the United States Commissioner......The
heavy frosts of this season in San Francisco,
have burst quite a number of lead water
pipes......The English Court of Queen's
Bench, awards an actress $8,000 damages
for a scar inflicted upon the face by a rail-
way accident......The way in which Irish 
school masters receive the school inspectors
is to meet them outside the door and give
them a sound drubbing; at least one was so
received not long since......There are fifty 
or fifty one counties in the State of Califor-
nia......In the suburbs of Paris, near the 
river Seine, houses are standing in vast lakes
of water from the overflow of that river......
A distinguished member of the French In-
stitute has been struck from the jury list for
declaring his unbelief in the existence of a 
God......A boy in Georgia is reported to
have refused $30,000 for a patent watch......
A recent ball given to the patients in the
lunatic asylum at Blackwell's Island, by
their physicians, is said to have resulted in
much good......A prize of one thousand 
dollars, for the best history of the United
States since the Civil War, is offered by the
King of Denmark......Mrs. M. J. Lamb, of
New York, is preparing a history of the 
Empire City......An old farmer states that
he has cured his daughter of the Grecian
bend, by pouring water on her and holding
her in the sun till she warped back again......
Streams in the neighborhood of Ghent, 
Belgium, have overflowed their embank-
ments and the country is inundated three
feet deep with water......Washington street,
Boston, had a $75,000 fire on the 23rd of
December which caused considerable con-
sternation......George Colton, a celebrated
American Indian painter and traveler, died
at Jersey City, New York, two days before
Christmas, 78 years old......A young lady in
Owensberg, while handling a pistol the
other day. accidentally shot herself through
the heart......All portions of the country
northwest, give accounts of almost unpre-
cedented cold weather......Three passenger
cars were recently precipitated from the
track, near Tonah, Wisconsin, by the break-
ing of a rail on the Milwaukee and St. Paul
Railroad, killing one man and injuring
several......Stokes second trial opened on
December 23, result like the first—nothing
definite concluded......A diamond was re-
cently found in the African diamond fields,
of 288 carats......The Pope severely de-
nounces the conduct of the Italian and
German Governments, and declares that
church property sold by order of the Italian
Parliament conveys no title to the purchaser
......A fire at Portland, Oregon, a few days
[new column]
since, destroyed $350,000 worth of property.
......Thirty persons were washed away and
drowned, from the packet Germania, which
went ashore on a sand bank at the mouth of
the Gironde, during a violent storm on the
night of the 21st of December......The horse
disease has been very prevalent at Toronto,
preventing the running of stages......Rail-
road trains on some of the northern routes
have been delayed by snow......Among the
specimens of the Army Medical Museum,
which number about 15,000, are 897 human
skulls and 34 skeletons......On the 23rd of
December Jersey City, New York, had a
$60,000 fire; Helena, Arkansas, a $30,000 fire,
and East Canaan, New Hampshire, a fire
worth $50,000, on the 22nd of December.
