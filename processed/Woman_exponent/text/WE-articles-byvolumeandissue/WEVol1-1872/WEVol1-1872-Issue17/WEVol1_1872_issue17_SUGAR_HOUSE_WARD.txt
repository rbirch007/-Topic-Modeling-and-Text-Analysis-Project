				SUGAR HOUSE WARD,
				       	January 15th, 1873
EDITOR WOMAN'S EXPONENT:—
	At our monthly meeting, held in the
School House, November 3rd, 1872, Mrs.
Sarah Gibson presiding, a unanimous vote
was taken to omit the word “Female” from
the name of the Society as suggested by
Sister E. R. Snow.
	Our Ward is widely scattered, nevertheless
we do the best we can with our surroundings,
administering to and blessing the poor.
There are always a few good, faithful sisters
on hand who are ready and willing to do all
that is required of them. Our President,
Mrs. S. Gibson, gives us good and valuable
instructions. May she long live to fill the
position she now occupies
	I have been a member of this Church for
18 years, and I still desire to do right and be
faithful to the end.
	With kind wishes for the success of your
paper, I am yours affectionately,
			SARAH WHEELER, Secretary.

			RICHMOND, Jan. 12th, 1873.
EDITOR WOMAN'S EXPONENT:—
	At our regular monthly meeting, held the
first Thursday in November, we unanimously
agreed to drop the word “Female” from the
name of the Society, according to Sister
Eliza R. Snow’s suggestion. This report
has been sent before, but as it has not
appeared in the Exponent, I suppose it has
not reached you, and I accordingly send
another.
	As a society we are doing our best to bless
those in trouble, sickness and poverty; of
the latter class, thank God, but few have
need of our assistance.
	Your excellent paper is a most welcome
visitor in my home, and it is highly appre-
ciated—as it seems—by many of our sisters.
God bless and prosper you in your noble un-
dertaking.
		SARAH A. MERRILL, President..
			OGDEN, Jan. 20th, 1873.
