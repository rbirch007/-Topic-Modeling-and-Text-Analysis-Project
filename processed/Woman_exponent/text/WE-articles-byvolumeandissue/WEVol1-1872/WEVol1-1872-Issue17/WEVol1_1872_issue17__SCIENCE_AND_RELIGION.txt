SCIENCE AND RELIGION.
			  —
	Truth is science, and science in its legiti-
mate form embraces all truth; and as “true
science and true religion walk hand in
hand,” nothing that is unscientific can be-
long to true religion, nor can anything that
is irreligious be classed with true science.
There are people whose ideas of religion 
lead them to pray a very great deal, and do
but little else, for fear of doing wrong, and
who dare not read other than spiritual
works; while many scientists, who demon-
strate great truths, being “unbelievers,”
take all the glory to themselves instead of
rendering it to God, the great Originator of
all intelligence. It may be unnecessary to
state here the obvious fact that these two
extremes are both wrong. In whatever form
the principle of divine truth is illustrated it
is sacred, and also scientifiic, and is an
established, fundamenal rule or portion of a
rule pertaining to the one true religion—the
Gospel of Jesus Christ.
	There are simple yet essential truths 
which appertain to daily, domestic life, as
lofty in their natures and more necessary to
be understood, than those of a grander and
more imposing character, which often startle
the world with their singular yet manifestly 
natural delineations. There are scientific 
and beautiful truths in the “Cookery Book” 
as well as in the Bible; and they all belong
to the true religion. It is quite as impor-
tant for present welfare and happiness to be 
considered, wherever it may be done in 
honor and wisdom, as that future blessed-
ness be sought for, regardless of the priva-
tions and inconveniences which may be
unnecessarily borne to-day, in consequence
of a lack of genuine faith and knowledge. 
Salvation is as good to-day as it will be in
days to come, if correctly viewed; and there
is no part of true science or true religion in
looking ever for future blessedness and
passing recklessly by those which should be
appreciated and enjoyed to-day.
