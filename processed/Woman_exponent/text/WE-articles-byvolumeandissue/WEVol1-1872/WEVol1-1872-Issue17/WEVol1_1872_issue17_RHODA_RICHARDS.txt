					RHODA RICHARDS.


THE spirit of true religion breathes gentle
ness and affability; it is social, kind, and
cheerful; far removed from that gloomy,
illiberal superstition and bigotry which
cloud the brow, sour the temper, deject the
spirit, and impress morosity on the man-
ners.
	REFORM, to be of any permanent value,
must be based on personal virtue, not force;
and the millennium will not be far off when
each individual shall set about reforming
himself, rather than society, and conforming
his life to the great law of loving God and
his fellow-men.
