	14 WARD ASSEMBLY ROOMS,
			          Salt Lake City.
	General Retrenchment Meeting, held
January 18th, 1873, presided over by Mrs.
Mary I Horne.
	After singing, and prayer by Counselor
Mrs. Zina D. Young, the minutes of the
preceding meeting were read by assistant
Secretary, Miss Maggie Mair, and approved.
The minutes of meetings of the Young
Ladies Retrenchment Associations, of the
9th and 11th wards, were also read. Presi-
dent Mrs. Horne spoke a short time. She
felt well in spirits, but on account of a bad
cold, contracted the evening before, was not
able to speak long; thought the sisters
should strive to be awake to their duties,
and be energetic in performing the same;
they should not trust to the Lord to do for
them what He had endowed them with
faculties to do for themselves.
	Counselor Mrs. S. M. Kimball spoke con-
cerning sympathy, true and false: When
she was in the midst of afflictions, she did
not want her friends to tell her how sorry
they felt for her, or to utter words of a nature
calculated to draw forth the emotions of
grief which she was striving to overcome;
she considered such, mistaken sympathy,
and preferred, when she paw a friend weighed,
down with sorrow, to offer something that
might serve to strengthen and build up
[new column]
faith and trust, rather than to incite and
prolong suffering, by speaking words, meant
for consolation, but only wringing more
effectually the heart already tried.
	Counselor Mrs. Elizabgth Howard read
the letter from Sister E. R. Snow, which
appears in this number of the Exponent,
addressed to President Mrs. Horne and her-
self. After which, remarks were made by
several members of the Association, and
meeting adjourned for two weeks, “Faith”
being chosen for the subject to be treated
upon at the next meeting.
