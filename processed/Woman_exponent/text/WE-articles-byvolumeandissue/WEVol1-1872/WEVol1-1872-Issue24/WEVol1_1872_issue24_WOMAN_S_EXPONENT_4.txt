WOMAN'S EXPONENT.


﻿[For the Exponent.
TO THE SISTERS OF THE RELIEF SO-
			  CIETY. 
			    ——
Sisters! let’s be up and doing, all our hands may
	   find to do,
Let us to the world be proving, to our trust we can
	   be true.
'Tis no light or trifling duty, 'tis no empty name we
	   bear,
The poor, the lame, the distant Saints, too, of our
	   time must have a share.

Let us not sit idly dreaming, while the precious
	   hours pass by,
Life with duties now is teeming let them not neglected
	   lie.
Let us seek the poor and needy, do them all the good
	   we can,
Knowing though each hour is needed, life at best is
	   but a span.

Let us seek to know each member, what they have,
	   and what they need,
Not alone their earthly comforts but to mental wants
	   give heed;
Some there are though having plenty,  yet are
	   stretched on beds of pain,
Wishing in their hours of anguish, some kind friend 
	   would “come again;"

And by words of love, and kindness, while the heavy
	  hours away,
Draw their thoughts from earth to heaven, till the
	  dove of peace holds sway.
It is thought a noble action, aiding sufferers in dis-
	  tress,
And to sooth the sick with kindness should be viewed
	  as nothing less.

Every good and worthy offering, always brings its
	  own reward,
“Every kind act to my brethren, is done to me,"
	  saith Christ the Lord.
Let us ever strive to walk in footstep of the pure
	  and true,
That the light of God’s good Spirit, may illume life’s
	  journey through.
						     MRS. A. RANDALL.
SPANISH FORK, April 22nd, 1873.
