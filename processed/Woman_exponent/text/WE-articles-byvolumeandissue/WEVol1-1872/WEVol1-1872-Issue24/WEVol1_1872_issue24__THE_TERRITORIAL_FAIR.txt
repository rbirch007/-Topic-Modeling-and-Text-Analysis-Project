THE TERRITORIAL FAIR.

	The following brief note from the Secretary of
the Deseret Agricultural & Manufacturing So-
ciety calls for a response from the various
Relief Societies, which we hope will be promptly
forwarded.  It is desirable that the ladies’ de-
partment of the forthcoming Fair should be as
well represented as possible, and reports are
solicited from the Societies that design contri-
buting to it, that proper arrangments for them
may be made in due time. Here is Elder
Campbell’s note:
Editor Woman’s Exponent:—
	In your interesting paper of the 1st, ult., the
Agricultural and Manufacturing Society issued
a circular to the Relief Societies throughout
the Territory, requesting their aid in contri-
buting to make the Territorial fair of 1873 a
marked success. President Woodruff and the
Board of Directors desire a report from each
Society that has taken the circular into con-
sideration, and that intends to contribute.
	They are requested to address the under-
signed,
			Robert L. Campbell,	Box	A.
						Salt Lake City.
Salt Lake City, May 8, 1873.
