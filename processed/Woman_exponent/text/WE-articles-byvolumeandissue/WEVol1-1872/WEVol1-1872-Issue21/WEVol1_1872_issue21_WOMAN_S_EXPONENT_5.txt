WOMAN'S EXPONENT


﻿LIBERTY is self government—not govern-
ment “by” ourselves merely, but govern-
ment “of” ourselves—strict and unrelent-
ing, healthful and cheerful.
	SCATTER flowers on the young maiden's
coffin, ye blooming friends!  Ye used to
bring flowers on her birthday feasts; she is
now celebrating the greatest of them, for
the bier is the cradle of heaven.
	LOCKMAN, the philosopher was once
asked, who had given him his first lesson
in wisdom.  “The blind,” he replied, who
do not move a step in advance until sure of
the ground with a stick.
