PEN AND SCISSORS.
				—
	A VETERINARY surgeon who lives at
Buffalo, N. Y., lately tried to “founder” his
mother-in-law by dancing a polka with her
until she got warmed up, and then giving
her all the iced lemonade she could drink.
	THE indolent habits of the Orientals are
well illustrated by the following “bon mot,”
which in the regions of the East has
assumed the character of an adage: “No
man should run when he may walk, walk
when he may stand, stand when he may
sit, or sit when he may lie down.”
	ALWAYS pleasant is a compliment to edi-
torial ears. “I saw three excellent things
in your last paper ” said a wag to an editor.
“Ah, indeed! what were they?” inquired
the delighted knight of the quill. “Mince
pies,” replied the wag, who escaped the
paste-pot by slipping out of the door. 
	A JOLLY fisherman was asked in the
presence of a spinster, what sort of a wife
he would choose, if he were going to marry,
to which he promptly replied “An 'Ale-
wife.’ ” The spinster increased the merri-
ment which this occasioned by dryly adding,
“I like wine best, and should prefer a
grape ‘Husband-man.’”
