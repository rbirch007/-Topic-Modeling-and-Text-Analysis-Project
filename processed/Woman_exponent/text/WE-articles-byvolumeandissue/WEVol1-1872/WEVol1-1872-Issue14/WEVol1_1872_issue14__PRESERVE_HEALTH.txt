PRESERVE HEALTH.
					—
	The worst spendthrifts in the world are those
who squander their health. For, without
health, success in any profession is uncertain
if not positively unattainable. But even should
victory crown the ambitious hopes and unre-
mitting labors of one who has outraged the
laws of nature, there is little enjoyment to be
realized when deprived of health, compared
with what might have been its accompaniment.
A great man once remarked to a young pro-
fessional, “I am certain I could have performed
twice the labor, both better and with greater ease
to myself, if I had known as much of the laws of
health and life at twenty-one as I do now.”
	No study can be of more importance to the
human family than that of the human physique
its self. For only with proper understanding
of the corporeal structure of a system, can the
promotion of its usefulness and longevity be
safely attempted or permanently secured. And
one of the most reprehensible features in modern
prodigality is the little attention paid to the
divine regulations of economy in regard to
health.
