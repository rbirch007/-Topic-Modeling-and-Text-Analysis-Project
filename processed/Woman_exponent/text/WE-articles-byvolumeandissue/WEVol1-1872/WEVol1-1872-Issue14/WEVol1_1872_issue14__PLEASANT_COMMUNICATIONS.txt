PLEASANT COMMUNICATIONS.
					—
					FOUNTAIN GREEN,
							Nov. 26th 1872.
EDITOR WOMAN'S EXPONENT:—
	I am always pleased to welcome your
paper, and truly glad that the women of
Utah have a chance to let their voices be
heard and their views known. I am sur-
prised that there are so many sisters who do
not take the EXPONENT. I have sent some
of mine to Manti to be circulated among the
sisters there. All parents who are desirous
of placing a paper of truth in the hands of
their children should have a chance to do so,
and the EXPONENT is certainly worthy to be
called such. I had much rather my children
would read but one paper like WOMAN'S
EXPONENT than all the novel stories that
were ever manufactured.
				MARIA J. WOODWARD.
				   —
				SALEM, UTAH CO.,
						Nov. 28th 1872.
DEAR EDITOR:—
	I am a reader of the EXPONENT, one of
its first subscribers; watch for its coming
with interest, delight in its perusal, think
every one the best, desire to see it prosper
and enlarge with choice matter selected from
the many who write for its beautiful col-
umns. May God bless the WOMAN'S EXPO-
NENT and all connected with it or who fee
an interest for it.
