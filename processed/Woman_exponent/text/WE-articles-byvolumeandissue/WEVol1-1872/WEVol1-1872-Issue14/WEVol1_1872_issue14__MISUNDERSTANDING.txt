MISUNDERSTANDING.
	“None are so blind ast hose who will not
aee.” How some people love to misconstrue
the words and motives of others! Why
can’t they have sense enough to realize
that they, like others, and others like them-
selves, are mortal beings, and all alike are
subject to mortal weaknesses and mortal
woes? I have but little charity to extend to
those extraordinary “good” people who
watch for iniquity in others and seek to
hide their own baseness under a cloak, so
black as to deceive almost themselves, and
make them fancy they are pure and


truthful when they are nothing but budgets
of the vilest cant and hypocracy! Only imag-
ine a woman who, over such a prayer as the
following, for an unfortunate fellow creature
who had become so desparate as to commit
suicide, and her friends, could write dark
misrepresentations concerning the teachings
of a man proven to be a firm maintainer of
truth, integrity, virtue and honesty! “May
the tender Father of us all, who saw all the
agony of her soul, gather and enfold her in
His merciful arms, and also extend to the
aged mother and sister, far away on the
shore of Michigan’s mighty lake, His tender
consolation in this hour of deepest sorrow.”
And the falsehoods told and written by
these foolhardy-people, in this instance—are
believed by many, in preference to the
truth! And yet we must not be annoyed,
because that is what they would like to
make us do and we must disappoint
them. Well!
