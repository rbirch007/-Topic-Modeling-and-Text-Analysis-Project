FEMALE PHYSICIANS.
			—
	At Steinway Hall last evening a very iutel-
ligent appearing audience assembled to
assist at the commencement exercises of the
Women’s Medical College of the New York
Infirmary. On the platform were several
well-known physicians of this city and other
distinguished citizens, among whom were
Judge John R. Brady and Nathaniel James,
Jr. Mr. Samuel Willetts, President of the
college, presided at the exercises, which,
after a voluntary on the organ by Mr. G. W.
Morgan, were opened with the conferring
of degrees of the following named graduates:
—Miss Abbie Luelle Fox, of Illinois; Miss
Charlotte Warner Ford, of New Jersey; Miss
Ellen Currie Leggett, of New York; Miss
Jeanette Taylor, of New Jersey; Miss Emily
Hellen Wells, of Pennsylvania, and Miss
Kate Cecilia Woodhull, of New York:	The
obligation was read to each of the graduates
by Mrs. Emily Blackwell, M. D., Secretary
of the Faculty; after which the diplomas,
were presented by Mr. Willetts.
	Prizes were then awarded as follows:—To
Miss Emily H. Wells, for the best report of
the medical clinique, “Nehmeyer’s Practice
of Medicine;” to Mrs. Sarah E. Crocker, for
the best report of the surgical clinique, a
splendid case of surgical instruments; to
the same for the best report of the eye and
ear clinique, a handsome opthalmoscope.
For report of the eye and ear clinique, Miss
E. Baldwin recived “honorable mention.”
	The valedictory was then read by Miss
Emily H. Wells, after which Judge Brady
delivered one of his characteristic humorous
and philosophical addresses, with which the
exercises closed.—“New York Herald,
March 26.
