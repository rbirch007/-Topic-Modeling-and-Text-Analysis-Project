	WOMAN’S VOICE.

	       	     Farmington, Davis Co.,
				         April 8, 1873.
Editor WOMAN'S EXPONENT:—
	As a lover of the true principles which
God in His great mercy has again restored
to the earth, I feel, with many of my sisters,
to offer my “mite.” I have been a member
of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day
Saints from my childhood, and can realize
that the hand of the Lord has been over His
Saints. He has caused streams of water to
burst forth, and the desert to blossom as the
rose. I was in Missouri at the time of the
mobbing and driving of the Saints from
that land and I realize they were miracu-
lously delivered from their enemies; and
the same God has led and guided us to the
valleys of the mountains. I have been in
polygamy for eleven years, and feel thank-
ful to my Father in Heaven, I have never
felt like saying anything against that pure
principle. We know, of course, it is a trial;
but has not God said He would have a tried
people? “After much tribulation cometh
the blessings.” I can truly say I am a hap-
pier woman since I have obeyed this com-
mandment. If we would strive to be
humble and prayerful, it will be a lesson in
the great school which will prepare us for
an exaltation in the Celestial Kingdom of
God.
	I take the EXPONENT and feel amply paid
in reading its interesting pages, especially
the spirited letters of Sister E. R. Snow.
				       	Mary S. Clark.
