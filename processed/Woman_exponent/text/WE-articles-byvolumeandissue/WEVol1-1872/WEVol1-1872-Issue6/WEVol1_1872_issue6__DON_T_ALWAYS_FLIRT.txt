DON’T ALWAYS FLIRT.
					—
Don't always flirt; dearest Bessie, believe me,
	You will repent it some day if you do;
Come, now, as some sage old mentor receive me,
	Take my advice, lest your conduct you rue.
Perhaps it’s good fun young men’s feelings to hurt.
Don’t always flirt, though, Bess—don’t always flirt.

True, you can count your fond slaves, dear, by
		dozens—
	All the young men you have got at your feet;
True, you are worshiped by all your male cousins,
	True, all the “belles” of the season you’ve beatl
Granted all this, Bess! you may be too curt,
Too great a flirt, Bessie—too great a flirt!

Time is going on, darling, seasons are hastening;
	Pardon me, but even you must grow old;
Think, now; if you are not good chances wasting;
	Don’t, now, I pray you, imagine me bold.
Really, though, I must my words assert,
Don’t always flirt, Bessie—don’t always flirt!

Young men who now gladly bear your caprices,
	Slaves who’re too happy to suffer your slights,
Perhaps as their knowledge of women increases,
	May not so willingly give up their rights!
Men may be ready to rudely out blurt,
You are a flirt, Bessie—you are a flirt!

Come, then, at present you have half society
	Only too glad to find grace in your eyes;
Have you not, then, had enough of variety?
	Cannot you settle to one, and be wise?
Don’t treat us always as though we were dirt.
Don’t always flirt, Bessie—don’t always flirt!

If I might venture—I know it’s audacious—
   To push my own claims, why, then, Bessie, ma
	chere,
I should be charmed, indeed, if you’d be gracious,
	And trust your dear self from henceforth to my
		care;
Promising only, all rows to avert,
That you wont flirt, Bessie—that you won’t flirt.
