F. R. SOCIETY REPORTS.
				  —
	The following, under date of August
18th, is from Mrs. Julia Lindsey, President
of the F. R. Society of Paris, Rich county,
Bear Lake valley:
	Money is very scarce in this part of the
country, owing to our crops having been de-
stroyed repeatedly, by frost and that terrible
scourge the grasshoppers, which has kept
the people in rather poor circumstances.
But we trust in God, and hope for better
days. We had quite severe frosts the latter
part of July, and it was at first supposed
our wheat crop was all killed; but time has
proved that it is not injured as badly as was
at first feared, which we are very thankful
for. Our society donated quite a sum to
the emigration fund, though small, I pre-
sume, in comparison with some other socie-
ties; but the fault was not in the heart but
in the purse. Our society is still alive, but
for the reasons stated above, we have not
accomplished as much as we should have
done, under more favorable circumstances.
We have our regular meetings once a fort-
night, which are not always very numer-
ously attended. But there are a number of
good, faithful sisters, who would be a bless-
ing to any society, who are determined,
with the help of God, to hold the society
together in spite of the slackness of the
few, and accomplish all the good they can.


A GOOD EXAMPLE.—A little girl, seven
years old, earned by her own labor two dol-
lars, which she has donated to the fund for
the emigration of the poor. She is the
daughter of Mrs. Cowley of the 14th ward,
of this city. This speaks well for mother
and child. Who will follow the example?
Boys and girls, remember the poor, and
God will remember you.		W. E.
