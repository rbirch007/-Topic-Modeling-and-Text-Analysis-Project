SPLINTERS.

	One hundred and eighty young and hardy
Scotchmen have lately landed at Quebec......
A new ocean cable has recently been laid
from the Suffolk coast in England to Hano-
ver......Gold medals have been sent to Stan-
ley, the explorer, from the St. Petersburg
and Florence geographical societies......Some
one now states that Horace Greeley, instead
of Wilkes Booth, was or should have been
Lincoln’s assassin......One hundred people
were lately thrown out of employment, at
Portland, by the burning of a rope factory...
About fifty delegates, from different parts
of the United States and Canada, were pres-
ent at the session of the Caledonia society
of North America......The Indians at Por-
tage la Prairie, Canada, refuse their annui-
ties, as last year provided by treaty, and
declare that unless better terms are granted
they will stop surveys......In the past two
years, twenty-three thousand miles of elec-
tric cables have been laid in the waters of
England, France, Spain, Holland, Turkey,
China, South America and Australia......A
late fire in the press room of the “Christian
Union,” Henry Ward Beecher’s paper, de-
stroyed about $10,000 worth of property......
Twenty-three cases of sunstroke in New
York, and seven in Brooklyn, occurred on
the 15th of August......John G. Saxe takes
the stump for Greeley and Brown......Half
the slate pencils used in the world are
claimed by Vermont to be made in the
Green Mountain State.....An English stock
purchaser has paid $13,000 for two short-
horned heifers in Kentucky......Carl Schurz
delivered his speech in Chicago in German...
It is believed that Charles Sumner and N.P.
Banks will take the stump in Maine......Gar-
rison tries to prove that Greeley was never
an abolitionist......Ludgate Hill, London’s
great thoroughfare, is to be paved with
American wood at a cost of $25,000......Kate
Field is engaged to write for the “Ameri-
can Register”......A New Hampshire paper
reports that twenty persons and a negro
were baptised on a Sunday, in its neighbor-
hood......Report says the prettiest girl about
Saratoga, is an Indian maiden, who sells
bead-work at the springs......Rev. Miss
Babcock has entered pastoral possession of the
pulpit of Warren street church, in Boston......
Last year, England bought from the United
States, $184,000,000 worth of cotton; and
manufactured from it L382,000,000 worth of
goods......Turin, Italy, boasts eighty women
over a century old, the youngest being 102
and the eldest 117......The Grant men think
to carry Maine, September 9th, by 49,000
majority; but Greeley men say they wont.
GREELEY is said to wear his trousers in-
side his boots to show he does not buy mili-
tary boots......“Colonel” Tennie C. Claflin
will take command of her regiment, she
says, as soon as it is recruited up to a thou-
sand strong.....Fanny Lewald, the German
authoress, intends to lecture in the United
States next autumn.......Mr. Boutwell is
now speaking for Grant in Maine......One
hundred and eighteen printers in Albany
have formed a Greeley and Brown club......
Chicago laid over a mile of sidewalk in
July......The female students who claimed
admission to the Scotch Universities, have
gained their point.....A Boston belle wears
the honors at Cape May......Bret Harte is
writing a melodrama......Two boats, one
containing two men, and the other a little
boy and girl, drifted over the Niagara Falls
on the 26th of July......The Emperors of
Germany and Austria met at Ischl on the
28th of August......The tomb of Abelard
and Heloise, at Pere la Chaise, is being
renovated at the expense of a noble and
[new column]
aged French lady......The Stadt Theatre,
New York, was lately sold at auction for
$12,200......The Grand Lodge of Odd Fel-
lows, of New York, reject the constitutional
amendment for admission of members ex-
pelled more than ten years......“Mark
Twain” has sailed in the “Scotia” for Eu-
rope......America is to be visited by a son of the
Khedive of Egypt......Juarez,the late Presi-
dent of Mexico, was a full-blooded Indian...
A cat was recently sold in London for
$255......The population of Russia is 81,-
500,000......Professor Agassiz is on his way
homeward......The father of the late James
Fisk, jr., has been horse-whipping general
J. W. Phelps, at Brattleboro, Vt......The
well-known and popular journalist of New
Orleans, Paul E. Roman, is dead......A pro-
ject for a tax upon domestic servants, was
recently brought forward in the French as-
sembly......A literary revolution is in pro-
gress in Poland......M. D. Conway says the
Prince of Wales possesses one good quality;
he is free from cant and hypocrisy......A
brother of Dr. Livingstone, residing at Lis-
well, Canada, has received letters from the
doctor, sent by Stanley......Miss Dickinson
is now said to be quietly pursuing her lec-
ture studies at home.
