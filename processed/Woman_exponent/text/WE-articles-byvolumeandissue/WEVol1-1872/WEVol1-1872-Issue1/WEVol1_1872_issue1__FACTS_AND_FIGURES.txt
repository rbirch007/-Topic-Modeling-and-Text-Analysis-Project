FACTS AND FIGURES.
		       —
TEXAS pastured 3,651,316 cattle in 1871.
THEY only use thirty-two books in the St. 
Louis High School.	
  LAWRENCE, Mass., employs 14,000 opera-
tives in her manufactories.		
  ENGLAND'S annual expenditure for alco-
holic drinks is $540,000,000. Estimate the	
“consequential damages” on that.		
IT is said that 40,000,000 pounds of tea 
are consumed yearly in the United States.
What tea parties—and nervousness !
  IOWA is awaiting a population of 9,000,-
000, which she claims to have capacity for
on her 55,000 square miles of surface.
  DELAWARE will yield 1,250,000 peach
baskets this year, the product of 5,000,000
peach trees.   Utah will do better, tree for 
tree.
OF lunatics sent to Taunton, Mass., dur-
ing the past eighteen years, the madness of
703 was caused by intemperance and six by
tobacco.
  LADIES, one of your inalienable rights is
to tell us where the 2,000,000 packs of pins
annually manufactured go to, especially as
each pack contains 3,660 pins.
 THE taxable property of Texas is $54,079,-
488 less than in 1858. Not that the State is
not prospering ; but the $85,000,000 worth
of slaves are not counted.
THERE are 343,102 Indians pastured on
87 reservations in the United States, at a
cost of $5,000,000 annually; including offi-
cial plunderings by agents and others.
  IT IS estimated that 2,000 persons anually
find peaceful graves as a result of the mur-
derous illuminating compounds now in use.
“Death in the lamp!” would be a legiti-
mate cry.	
  THE largest cities in the German Empire, 
according to the census of December 1,1871,
are Berlin, 828,012 inhabitants; Hamburg,
235,365 ; Breslau, 208,025 ; Dresden, 177',-
025 ; Munich, 169,612 ; Cologne, 129,251;
and Leipzig, 107,575.
  MICHIGAN has declared that any school
district having over 300 children can borrow
$15,000 to erect a Union school-house, arid
the cry is “Union Forever!” with slight
Mormon tendencies. Erection will be the
order of the day.—“ Pomeroy’s Democrat.”
  THERE is one mile of railroad to every
379 inhabitants in Iowa; to every 411 in Il-
linois ; to 467 in Indiana; to 699 in Penn-
sylvania; to 686 in Missouri; to 975 in
Massachusetts; and to 1,022 in New York.
And the life insurance rates are in propor-
tion.		
   A MAN incarcerated in the Tombs has
been figuring in chalk on the walls of his
cell.  It reads: “In New York City the 
spires of 342 churches, worth $41,130,000,
point heavenward. I’m here for stealing a
loaf of bread for my starving child.” Chris-
tian civilization !
 EUROPE, it is said, has nearly ten millions
of men trained to arms and kept from pro-
ductive industry, to feed ambition, gratify
revenge, and meet the demands of all the
baser passions of human nature. What a
commentary on existing governments and
the degradation of man’s noble powers !
 LONDON is the largest city in the world,
far surpassing all those of antiquity. Ac-
cording to Gibbon, the population of ancient
Rome, in the hight of its magnificence, was
1,200,000; the population of Pekin is sup-
posed to be about 2,000,000; that of London
is about 3,500,000, over one-twelfth of the
population of the United States.
