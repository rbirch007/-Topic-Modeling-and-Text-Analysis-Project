DEMORALIZING INFLUENCES.
					  —
	Atrocities of late occurrence, in this city,
serve to render the aspect for the return of the
“happy days gone by,” when purity and inno-
cence were in no danger of being contaminated,
or virtue outraged, in no wise auspicious.
There has been a time, since the settlement of
Utah by the “Mormons,” in which the modesty
and chastity of woman were here regardrd as
her chief adornments; and held more sacred by
her honorable and respectful protectors than
every other treasure, even life itself. But base,


villainous men have intruded their persons
and fiendish practices even here, as emissaries
of the evil one, to tempt, decoy, entrap, and, if
possible, to destroy the virtue of the young and
thus prevent the future progress and prosperity
of the pure minded and God-fearing among the
people. And this fact, standing prominently
forward, as it does, should be a powerful admon-
ition to the inexperienced, the young and the
virtuous to be circumspect in their associations,
their coversation and deportment. Not only
should woman seek to maintain her own
crowning glory, purity and integrity, but
mothers and sisters will also do well to wield
the benign influence of which pure and noble
womanhood is ever possessed, energetically and
ceaselessly over the minds of the young of the
opposite sex. There is danger for them as well
as for the beauty and simplicity of maidenhood,
and they cannot be too prudent in respect to
the company they keep and the inflnences
which they allow themselves to be made subject
to.
