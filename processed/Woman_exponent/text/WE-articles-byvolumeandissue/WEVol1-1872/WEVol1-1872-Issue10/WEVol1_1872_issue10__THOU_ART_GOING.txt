THOU ART GOING.
		     —
RESPECTFULLY INSCRIBED TO MISS ELIZA R.
	SNOW ON HER APPROACHING JOURNEY TO
	JERUSALEM.
					—
Thou art going dear sister, to th’ land long forsaken,
	Whose people, a hiss and a by-word, have been;
Who rejected the Savior, the way of salvation,
	And for centuries have dwindled in darkness and
	sin.

But the Spirit of God is now moving to gather,
	A people once favored, as scriptures record;.
Their cities and temples again there will flourish,
	And themselves live in peace, for a time, saith the
		Lord.

And thou, when thy feet tread the paths once familiar
	And dear to the Savior while dwelling below.
Thy soul will go forth in communion with Heaven,
	And glad strains of praise from thy full heart will
		flow.

Oh! what a rich feast, to a nature like thine,
	To view the paths trod, by that being divine,
And to feel that ere long He will tread them again,
	And be known to the Jews, as the Savior of men!

Thou art going, and with thee our blessings will go,
	And we’ll pray for thy safety thy long journey
	through;
We will not forget the rich counsels you’ve given,
	For they will refine and prepare us for Heaven.

May Heaven’s rich blessings rest on your companions,
	And Israel’s God be your guide and your friend;
May angels protect you, while absent from Zion,
	And guard you safe home, when your journeyings
	end.
						MRS. ANNE RANDALL.
	SPANISH FORK, Sept. 30th 1872.

And let us add, in friendship’s warmest tone,
	Servant of Zion’s daughters, not alone
We crave for thee the wage of peace and health,
	But new, bright stores of intellectual wealth;
Rich gems of thought, sweet words of solace, too,
	Thou’ll glean and bring to us, beloved and true.
										LULA.
