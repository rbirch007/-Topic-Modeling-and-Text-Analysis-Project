TO STRANGER FRIENDS. 
			    —
Stranger Sisters, kindly caring,
For a lone one in distress,
Neither time nor int’rest sparing
that would serve to soothe and bless;
May the light of Heaven never
Cease across your path to shine;
But the grace of God, forever,
Yield you strength and power divine.

Have ye wand’ring sons or brothers?
May they meet with friends as true,
Finding at the hands of others
That which ours receive from you. 
While this prayer is but a token
Relevant, deep thanks accord,
Other thoughts must rest unspoken
With the future and the Lord.
							L. L,
