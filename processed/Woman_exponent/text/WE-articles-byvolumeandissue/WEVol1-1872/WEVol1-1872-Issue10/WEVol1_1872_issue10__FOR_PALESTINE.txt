FOR PALESTINE.
				    —
	On the morning of the 26th ult. Sister Eliza
R. Snow, President of all the Female Relief
Societies, left this city by the early train, on
her way to Palestine, or the Holy Land. Her
brother, President Lorenzo Snow, of Brigham
City, Elder George Dunford and Bishop Jacob
Weiler, of this city, and Elder Anson Call, of
Bountiful, Davis County, formed the party
with which she traveled east, they all designing
to unite with President George A. Smith before
he leaves for Europe. Bishop Thomas Taylor
of this city, and daughter, left a few days be-
fore those who started on the 26th, and Elders
John Taylor and Paul A. Schettler still expect
to go. The entire party, when united, will
number some twelve or thirteen persons, and
may be absent for nearly a year. They will
bear with them the earnest prayers and best
wishes of the thousands of Latter-day Saints
in these valleys, for pleasant voyaging while
absent, and their safe return.
	We hope to be able to present the readers
of the Exponent with communications from
Sister Snow, as she can find time to write in-
cidents of the journey; and we need scarcely
say how deeply interesting such letters will
be, however brief.
