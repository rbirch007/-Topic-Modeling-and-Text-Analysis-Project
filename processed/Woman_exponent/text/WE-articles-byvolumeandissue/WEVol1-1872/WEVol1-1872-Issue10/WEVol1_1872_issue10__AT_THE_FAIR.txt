AT THE FAIR.
	The Relief Societies, from different parts,
manifested their interest in the progress of
home science, by contributing handsomely to
the many attractions, useful and ornamental,
exibited at the Territorial Fair, considering
the time allowed them after being notified that
they were solicited and expected to take part in
it. The Societies of the Third, Seventh,
Eighth, Ninth, Twelfth, Fourteenth and
Twentieth Wards of this city were represented;
also those of Ogden, Bountiful, and Lehi.
Articles belonging to individual members of
other Societies were presented, but so far as we
have been able to ascertain, the above named
were the only Societies specified. Premiums
were awarded to those Societies as follows:

	Several of the articles which drew prizes were
got up in very little time, and had the invita-
tion to the Societies been issued a month earlier


the exhibition of articles contributed by them
would have been much better.
	Taken altogether the Fair was quite success-
ful, which must be a cause of gratification to
the Board of Directors of the D. A. & M. Soci-
ety; and the ladies have reason to feel satisfied
with their share in its attractiveness. In the
department devoted to art were some very
beautiful specimens of ladies’ work in wax,
which drew prizes and were greatly admired by
all who saw them; and the specimens of differ-
ent kinds of work exhibited by individual
ladies, as well as by the Relief Societies,showed
highly cultivated taste and excellence of work-
manship in a very superior degree.
