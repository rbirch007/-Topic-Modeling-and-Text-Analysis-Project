ENJOYMENT FOR THE YOUNG.

	One of the most pleasant and interesting 
sights we have witnessed in a long time was the
Sunday School excursion from the north part
of Utah County, to this city, on Monday, the
21st ult. The Sunday Schools of Lehi, Ameri-
can Fork, Pleasant Grove and Alpine, to the
number of over thirteen hundred, accompanied
by their Superintendents and Teachers, and by
Bishop Harrington, of American Fork, Bishop
Brown, of Pleasant Grove, and Bishop Mc-
Cullough, of Alpine, and having along the
brass bands of Lehi and American Fork,reached
this city about noon. They visited the New
Tabernacle, heard the grand organ play, looked


through the building; and then marched to the
Theatre where they enjoyed an afternoon per-
formance. At about half past four o’clock in
the afternoon they left by the cars for Lehi, a
happy-looking, satisfied, intelligent company,
having evidently much enjoyed the excur-
sion.
	This thing of providing pleasure for a large
number, mainly of young and growing persons,
is worthy of all commendation. Youth seeks
amusement as naturally as the flowers seek
sunshine, and if denied it or deprived of it, will
droop and wither as a tender plant would be-
fore a keen frosty blast. But the amusement
should be carefully planned and arranged by
more mature minds, that it may be at once a
means of instruction and enjoyment; a medium
by which intelligence can be increased and the
desire for amusement be gratified. Because
this Sunday School excursion met these objects,
feasted the young eyes and gladdened the young
hearts, while giving them a more extended idea
of what is to be seen in places away from home,
we viewed it with so much favor; and trust the
mothers of the future hope of Israel will lend
every aid in their power to the repetition of such
seasons of enjoyment and pleasure for the
growing “olive branches.”
