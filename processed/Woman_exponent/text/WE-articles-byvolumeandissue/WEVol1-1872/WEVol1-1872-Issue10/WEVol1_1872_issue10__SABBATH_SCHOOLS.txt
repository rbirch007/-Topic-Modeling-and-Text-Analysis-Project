SABBATH SCHOOLS.
				—
Editor Woman’s Exponent:
	A great deal has been said and done of 
late by the First Presidency and others in 
behalf of Sabbath Schools. If the majority 
of parents would take greater interest in the
matter, and visit the Sabbath Schools
occasionally, it would be encouraging to
both teachers and scholars. Some may
think it onerous to go Sunday after Sunday
as a teacher to a class. But, speaking from
experience, I can say that to go with the
spirit of the Gospel is a delightful pleasure.
I have been a teacher some years, and have
never found it a loss but have been amply
rewarded. For while we thus impart the
blessings of knowledge to others we are
ourselves recipients of like blessings. If we
desire our sons and daughters to fill honor-
able positions in society, we must lay correct
foundations for them in their childhood.
Faith in God should be early and firmly im-
pressed upon the minds of our children,
that it may grow with their growth, and be
to them, in future time, a consolatory guide
to smooth life’s rugged pathway. The people
of the world seem to have lost the gift, so 
that when troubles overtake them they sink
in despair. Not so with the people of God.
Their tribulations serve only to consume
the dross, and elevate and refine the
character.
	The would-be sympathizers who draw
long faces and groan over the condition of
women in Utah, should reserve their 
sympathy for themselves and their associates.
We, as wives and mothers, honored and
blessed, seeking to ameliorate the condition
of all within the reach of their influence,
have no need of it.			   J. S. C.
