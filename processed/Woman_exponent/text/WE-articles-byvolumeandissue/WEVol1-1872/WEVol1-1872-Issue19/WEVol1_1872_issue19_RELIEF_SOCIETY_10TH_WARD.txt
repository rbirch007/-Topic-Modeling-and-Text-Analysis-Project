		RELIEF SOCIETY, 10th WARD,
		   Salt Lake City, Feb. 21, 1873.
EDITOR WOMAN'S EXPONENT:—
    At a meeting held November 7th 1872, it
was agreed upon by our Society to omit the
word “Female,” as suggested. Our report
comes thus late on account of our failing to
get the understanding that each Society
was desired to report.
    Although our subscription list to your
excellent paper is yet small, it will increase;
for many are pleased with it. Those inter-
esting letters from Sister E. R. Snow are
splendid. May God bless, preserve and
inspire her to write us many such is the
prayer of her faithful sisters. The Society
here is prospering. Our monthly business
meetings are moderately attended, and
good feelings pervade; our semi-monthly
meetings, for testimony, prayer and etc.,
are doing much good. Our Retrenchment
Society has been reorganized and our young
sisters have made a very good beginning.
We have the confidence of our Bishop and
his counselors who are well pleased with our
exertions. Our President has been very
sick but is now better.
    May success attend the EXPONENT and
may it go faithfully and steadily onward.
    Respectfully yours,
			SARAH STRONG, President.
    SUSANNA M. NAYLOR, Secretary.
