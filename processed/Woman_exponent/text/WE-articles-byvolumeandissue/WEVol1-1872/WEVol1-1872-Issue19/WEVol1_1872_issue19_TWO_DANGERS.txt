TWO DANGERS.

	There are two dangers to which the Latter-
day Saints are exposed. One is a want of 
that strong faith in the Almighty, necessary to
secure His blessings, often the result of a neg-
lect of duty, a want of prayerfulness, or a
disregard of revealed truths, in consequence of
which the Spirit of God is grieved. The other
is an over confidence in the protection of the
Lord, and acting on the belief that He will
preserve His people from all danger or affliction,
no matter what course they may persue. They
have the promise that He will sustain them
and bear them off triumphantly if they will be
faithful, humble, prayerful, and keep His com-
mandments; yet they have no promise of such
protection if they neglect their covenants and 
disregard the commandments of the Lord. The
Saints have been so visibly preserved from
many threatened dangers, and have so often
been borne in safety over the troubled waters of
affliction, that some seem to imagine they will
continue to be so sustained, under all circum-
stances. Yet the promised blessing is only to
those who seek to sanctify and purify them-
selves before Heaven.
	These are principles which mothers should
inculcate upon their children, upon their sons 
and daughters, that from childhood to maturity
the youth of the community may be trained
in ways and in the practice of principles of
righteousness, that will give them a faith and
a power only received and enjoyed as a result
of living honestly and uprightly before the
Lord, and observing every truth of the Gospel 
of salvation.
