				FRAGE.

	Congress adjourned without the bill directed
against Utah becoming law, although it passed
the Senate in a modified form, but still retain-
ing the provision depriving the women of
Utah of the ballot. This is a point in which
all the women of the United States should be
interested, as far, at least, as they desire to
occupy a political equality, not simply with
men, but such men as are to be found among the
most degraded class of the colored race,
or the most ignorant foreigners that may seek
our shores and desire citizenship. Does any
one think that if Congress were to deprive the
women of Utah of the elective franchise by
repealing the Territorial statute conferring it
upon them, that the same body would imme-
diately turn around and undo its own work,
by again conferring the suffrage on them
through any measure extending that right to
all American women? Those who are agitat-
ing the question, who are working for the
extension of the elective franchise to women
all through the United States, are directly
interested in this matter, for they may rest
assured that the Congress that strikes at the
principle of woman suffrage, by depriving the
women of a Territory of the right to vote, will
not stultify itself by passing or recommending
any measure, nor any Constitutional amend-
ment, recognizing woman’s right to this
political status.	
	The Woman’s Suffrage Association of New
York saw this, and passed a resolution, which
was presented to Congress, protesting against


the blow aimed at woman's rights in the Frel-
inghuysen bill. And advocates of woman’s
cause throughout the entire Union should be
alive to the fact that, if a similar attempt is
made next session of Congress, it will have a
double object, as it had before: to further the
plans for plunder of political adventurers in
this Territorty, and to strike a blow at
the woman’s suffrage cause.
	Most of the men who urge with the most
strenuousness the crusade against the Mormons,
are avowed enemies to the woman’s rights move-
ment in its every aspect; and while they are
without influence to effect anything against it
in the States, they know very well that if they
can get any measure through Congress in
opposition to it and attacking it in a Territory,
they will have successfully made a point.
	Every lecturer, every editor, every person
advocating or interested in the cause of woman,
should make this matter his or her own, in
the event of another attempt to deprive the
women of a Territory of the right of suffrage.
