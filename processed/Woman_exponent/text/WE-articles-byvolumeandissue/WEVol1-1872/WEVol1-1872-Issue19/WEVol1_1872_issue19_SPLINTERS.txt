SPLINTERS.
	Gail Hamilton has the small-pox......Mrs.
Stowe has a new book called “Palmetto
Leaves.”......Laura Keam is candidate for
the office of State Librarian in Indiana.
Mr. Conklin is alluded to as the thin-haired
Apollo-Belvidere of the Senate......The Arch-
Duke Charles of Austria has lately completed
his seventieth year......The income tax in
India is to be abolished......Nellie Grant
will visit Europe again this year......The
Crown Prince of Germany is an artist......
Fred Douglas talks of going to live in Eu-
rope......A Terre Haut, Indiana, belle stands
six feet one inch in her stockings......Mrs.
Henry Wood has a new story entitled “The
Master of Greyhounds”.......Barnum lately
had his pockets picked and a diamond pin
stolen......The balance of trade against the
United States last year was in round num-
bers $118,000,000......Michigan produced 118 1/2
pairs of boy twins during 1870, the other
half was a girl......A St. Louis paper says
that fashionable wedding trips in Chicago
consist of a foot race to the nearest
divorce court......The Japanese Embassy was
received with military honors at Calais......
The Sisters of Notre Dame have offered
their services as nurses at the small-pox
hospital in Boston......A premium was
offered at a late fair in Montgomery, Ala.,
for the “young woman who would make
the best wife for a poor man.”......The new
head-quaters of the Pullman Palace Car
Company, in Chicago, are finished and
occupied......Powell’s new painting of Per-
ry’s Victory has been placed in the Capitol
at Washington......A woman in Delaware
recently died at the age of 72, after serving
fifty years in one family as a farm hand.
The lecture field will soon be abandoned by
Olive Logan....Froude’s return to England
was much sooner than he expected.........
Oysters are being taken from Norfolk, Va.,
to be planted on the British coast......Ice at
St. Paul has been fourteen feet thick......
Chicago lost 800 horses by the distemper.
......Connecticut has 114 Baptist churches.
A locomotive shop in Russia employs 4,000
men......Small-pox is spreading in New
England......Brooklyn, Long Island, had
twenty-six fires in December......Lake
George was frozen over in December last, the
third time in thirty years at so early a date.
......Wisconsin is said to be more than two
million dollars in debt.....Lancaster, N. H.,
has had a touch of 42 degrees below zero......
The University at Geneva has, in a gentle-
manly manner, opened its doors to women.
......Philadelphia proposes a tower 1,000
feet high, to illuminate the grounds of the
great Centennial Exposition......The Chicago
public library is kept in a large iron water-
tank......A monster nettle of Australia
grows to the height of forty feet....Yale
College is to have a new chapel to cost
$100,000......The Boston flats are being filled
up with rubbish from the burned buildings.
......Massachusetts has $184,797,313:92 in
her savings’ banks......It is said that free
use of the Atlantic cable has been offered to
the Smithsonian Institute......Ohio men get
fined for opening their wives’ letters......
During the past year, within the limits of
the United States, 7,478 miles of railroad
have been completed, and 6,514 miles put
under construction, making a total of 13,992
miles on which work has been done.........
New Jersey has thirty-three savings banks.
......The abreviatory letters, M. C. for Mem-
ber of Congress, may now be appropriately
transversed, and read C. M. for Creditors
Mobilier......The amount of bonds which
the Government issued to the Union Pacific
Railroad Company, is pronounced $27,237,512
currency, bearing interest at the rate of 6
per cent per annum......Comodore J. D. Mc-
Kenny, of the United States Navy, lately
died, sixty-six years old, and leaving
property valued at more than $100,000......
Miss Rothschild, a daughter of the well
known family, was married in London, on
the 12th of February, to Mr. Elliott of New
York......Another Arizona diamond hunter
comes to Virginia City with several pounds
of what are pronounced “geuine rubies.”......
The President of Harvard, Mr. Elliott, has
given up his own residence to a student
who is down with the small-pox......Ken-
tucky wants to establish a law to have
separate schools for its white children and
colored ones.....Rose Porro, arrested in
Naples for child murdering, has confessed
the terrible crime of having starved and
poisoned, as near as she can recollect, about
forty infants, to satisfy her greed for money.
......More money is said to be made in
America by the cheap story-weeklies than
by the entire daily press.......Frauds, law-
suits, and false telegrams are so common
that they no longer create the slightest
surprise.
......A notorious Corsican—bandit, Antonio
Suzzoni, who has four times been
sentenced to be hanged and each time made
his escape, once from the very scaffold, was
lately shot and killed......Two gentlemen
in Virginia, Nevada, have adopted a plan
for prospecting for ore, similar to that em-
ployed in sinking artesian wells......A young
lady in Indiana, a few days since, fell into
the fire in a fainting fit, and burned to
death before the accident was discovered.
......Tennie C. Clafflin has lost her Colonel-
ship......Ben Butler is said to write sympa-
thetic letters to Mrs. Woodhull......Speaking
of the Credit Mobilier, a contemporary says,
“Who steals will lie!”....Newbern, N. C.,
eats shad at $1:80 per pair......In some
portions of France and Spain Woman’s
Rights are wonderfully recognized; she may
chop her own wood, fodder her husband’s
stock, and all that sort of thing, besides
doing the house-work......Colorado has
seven daily papers, six more than Florida.
......The euphonious name of a suburb of
Dubuque is “Dirty Holland.”
