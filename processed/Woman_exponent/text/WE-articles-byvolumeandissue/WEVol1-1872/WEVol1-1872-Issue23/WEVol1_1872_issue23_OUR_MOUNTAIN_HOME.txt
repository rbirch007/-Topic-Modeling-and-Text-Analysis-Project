OUR MOUNTAIN HOME.
				   —
	The following sweetly expressive lines, full of the
simplicity of nature, were written by the second wife
of an Elder in Israel, addressed to the first wife of
her husband.	

	In the old log cabin in the hills, Mary,
		We have spent many happy hours;
	Our children p'aying about the door,
		As bright as the summer flowers.
	And though we have sighed for social life,
		And the joy which friendship brings,
	We have found there are pleasures in duty’s path,
		Pleasures which leave no stings.

	Sometimes we have felt we were banished, Mary,
		And as days and weeks rolled by,
	We looked to the Eastern mountains,
		With longing, wishful eye.
	We sometimes feel as if shut out
		From friends and kindred dear;
	Yet this is but a moment’s thought—
		For duty calls us here.

	To teach and instruct our boys, Mary,
		To guide their footsteps aright;
	Will do much for us to accomplish,
		If we do it with all our might:
	How, then, can we ever be lonely,
		With this great mission to fill?
	By the Spirit of Truth directed,
		Teach them Jehovah’s will.

	How we may spend our future,
		Or where our lot be cast.
	We know not, may we ne’er regret
		The unforgotten past;
	And may memory turn with pleasure, Mary,
		To the lonely days we spent,
	In the old log cabin away in the hills, 
		On "every-day duties” intent.
										E. A. B.
April 16th, 1866.
