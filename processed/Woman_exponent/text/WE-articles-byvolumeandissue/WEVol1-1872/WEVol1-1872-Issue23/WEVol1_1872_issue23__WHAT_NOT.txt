WHAT-NOT. 
				—
	Nothing is so reasonable and cheap as 
good manners.
	A LOUISIANA operator makes soap out of 
cotton seed. 
	LOUISVILLE policemen are provided with
wheelbarrows.	
	TUNEFUL lyre—a music teacher who does
not keep his engagements.
	A CONNECTICUT horse has acquired the
habit of chewing tobacco. Poor horse!
	A DETROIT youth has no forehead, the
hair growing down close to the eyes. Poor 
boy!	
	A CROSS-eyed man in Newberg has
gathered around him seven cross-eyed
servants.
	THE stocking manufacturers of Newark
N. J., say that the largest sizes are shipped
to Chicago.
	A YOUNG lawyer in North Carolina was 
recently paid a pound of butter for legal 
services.
	THACKERY says that a critic is a Solo-
mon that sits in judgment before us and
chops up our children.		
	A LOUISVILLE man is so opposed to	
capital punishment that he can’t even
tolerate paper hanging. 
	If you should be asked when a cat is like a
teapot, you might reply, “When you’re
teasin’ it.”
	A MAN out West says he moved so many 
times during one year that when ever a 
covered wagon stopped at the gate his 
chickens would fall on their backs and hold 
up their feet, in order to be tied and thrown
in.
	A GENTLEMAN was complimenting a pretty	
young lady in the presence of his wife. "It 
is lucky I did not meet Miss Hopkins before 
I married you my dear.” “Well, yes, it is
extremely lucky—for her,” was the dry
rejoinder.
	WHAT a glorious world this would be, if 
all its inhabitants could say, with Shake-
speare’s shepherd: “Sir, I am a true laborer!  
I earn what I wear; I owe no man hate,
envy no man’s happiness, glad of other
men’s good: content with my farm.” 
	MRS. Moore, an industrious woman of
Topeka, Kansas, earned money enough to
support a good-for-nothing husband and
bring a niece to live with them. The
husband eloped with the niece and she is
now nicely rid of both.
	“Mother,” said a little girl, who was
making her doll an apron, “I believe I will
be a dutchess when I grow up.” How do
you expect to be a Dutchess, my daughter?” 
“Why, by marrying a Duthman, to be
sure,” replied the girl.
	SINCE their only schoolma’am eloped
from Green Bay, Wis., the school directors
advertise for a teacher that is over forty
years of age, has had the small-pox, with
poor teeth, and a glass eye, and wooden leg,
if possible. Any girl answering these re-
commendations can secure a permanent
job by applying early.
	A LITTLE boy was playing with a couple
of five cent pieces, the other evening, which
a friend had given him, and putting his
finger on one of them, said, “This one I am
going to give to the heathen.” He kept on
playing till at last one of the pieces rolled
away, and he could not find it.   “Which
one have you lost?” asked the friend.
“The one I was going to give to the hea-
then,” replied the cherub.
