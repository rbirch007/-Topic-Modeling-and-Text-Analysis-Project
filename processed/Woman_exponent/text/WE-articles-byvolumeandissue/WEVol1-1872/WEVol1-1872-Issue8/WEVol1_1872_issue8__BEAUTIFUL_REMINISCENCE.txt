BEAUTIFUL REMINISCENCE.
	The following sentiments were expressed
by a Scandinavian sister, in the 14th Ward
Assembly Rooms, on Saturday afternoon,
August 31:
	“I cannot express my feelings as I would
like to do; but when I hear you talk of the
influence which mothers have over the
minds of their children, I can hardly repress
the tears that will come into my eyes, for I
remember my mother! And her wise coun-
sel is stamped upon my heart; and has ever
been, through all the trials and temptations
of my life, a source of comfort and of re-
straint. Although I was not more than
seven years old when I had to leave my
mother, I remember when she used to say
to me, ‘Don’t talk so much, my daugther;
the Angels don’t talk so much. Don’t
laugh loud; it is not good to laugh loud.’
And many other instructions which she
gave to her children are fastened on my
memory, and have seemed to be a safe-
guard to me in many circumstances through
which I have passed. I, therefore, know
that the words and examples of mothers
have a great weight with their children, in
forming their determinations and practices
through life. I know it is from those early
impressions, formed upon my mind, that I
was prepared to receive the light of the true
gospel; and I want to live to do something
for my mother. I am thankful that I know
the truth, and I shall never cease to praise
God and my mother!”


MISS Alice Bird has twice been invited
to a professorship in the Iowa Wesleyan
University.
