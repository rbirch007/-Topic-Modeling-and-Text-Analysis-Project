POLICY VERSUS LOVE.

	The Princess Marguerite, cousin-wife of
Prince Humbert, of Italy, does not believe
in Iove for persons of her condition. She
is said to have remarked, not sentimentally
but with the common sense air of a woman
of her century, “There is no such emotion
for us.” The match between her and her
cousin-consort was merely one of State poli-
cy, and although the Prince is not an agree-
able husband, but sullen, and very often
reproachful, and the Princess sometimes re-
sents his rude snubs, she yet seems to con-
sider that it is nothing out of place. She
endeavors to be a patient, kind wife to her
arrogant liege lord, and to further the public
interest even at the cost of her own health.
But the Prince is fond of his boy, becoming
placid and amiable at his presence, no mat-
ter how inconsiderate and sullen he has
been during the day. It is a fine thing for
women so situated to believe as does Mar-
guerite of Savoy.
