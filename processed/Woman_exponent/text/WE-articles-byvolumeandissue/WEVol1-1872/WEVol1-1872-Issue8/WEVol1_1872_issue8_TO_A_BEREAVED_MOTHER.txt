			TO A BEREAVED MOTHER. 
Dear sister, the evils which life’s pathway strew,
Are striving for ever the good to outdo;
They vex and torment us, afflict and bereave,
And in every manner bewilder and grieve;
They load us with burdens we scarce can sustain,
They tempt us with pleasures or torture with
	pain!
Whoever undaunted would patiently stand,
Must see and acknowledge the Great ruling Hand.

Ag’d friend, I can sympathize deeply with you :
Though young, I have had some experience too;
I have tasted, though lightly, of life’s bitter cup,
And can feel for my fellows when sadly they sup:
Your late, sudden loss is a sorrowful one—
Hereft in old age of a dutiful son;
Yet wounded and rack'd, bowing low to the sod,
'Tis good to acknowledge the kind Hand of God.

May you answer at last, “Though worn and dis-
	tressed,
With reliance unshaken still onward I’ve pressed;
Though tempted and tried, yet my heart hath not
	fail-d,
For good o’er the evil within me prevailed;
The thick c ouds of darkness were forced to de-
	part;
Warm, beautiful sunshine stole into my heart;
And there, with the balm and the chastening rod,
I saw the just Hand of a merciful God.”
									L. L.
