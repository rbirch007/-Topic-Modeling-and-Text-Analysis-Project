KITES AHOY!
EDITOR WOMAN'S EXPONENT:
	You will confer a favor on the Telegraph
Companies by asking boys not to fly kites
in proximity to telegraph wires, as they
very often “foul” the lines and give serious
trouble, especially during wet weather. If
parents will urge their sons to keep away
from the wires with kites, and not to throw
rocks at the insulators, superintendents will
feel under obligations.
		Respectfully,
			A. M. MUSSER, Superintendent.
	Salt Lake City, Sept. 7, 1872.
