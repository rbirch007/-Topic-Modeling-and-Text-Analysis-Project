WOMAN’S EXPONENT.


﻿The ladies of the Congregational Society
of San Mateo, realized from their “Mother
Goose party” over $300.


﻿Ohio has enacted by law that eighty-
eight cubic inches of bituminous coal shall
weigh just sixty pounds avoirdupois.


A Connecticut company is putting up a
factory for the building of carriages made
entirely of India rubber, excepting the axles
and tires. A decided superiority is claimed
for the material over wood.


﻿In 1787 the Emperor of China issued the
following decree: “All persons of the female
sex are forbidden, upon any pretext what-
ever, to enter a temple or quit their houses,
except in cases of absolute necessity.
Fathers, husbands, sons or relatives are
commanded to keep them at home on pain
of being themselves severely punished.”
What a believer in equal rights! The men,
most likely, had as hard a time of it as the
women.


The Massachusetts Labor Union Conven-
tion met in Boston on the 28th ult. Wendell
Phillips offered a series of resolutions, which
were adopted after a long debate, approving
all labor associations, announcing the belief,
expediency and necessity of strikes, but
denouncing violence; advocating congres-
sional establishment of labor unions; declar-
ing that all dividend paying companies
should be obliged to divide half of the net
profits among employes, and that imported
pauper contract work should be dutyable.
