LET ME THINK THUS.
				
Let me not think that the world is all selfishness,
  Care’ess and grim and of dark, sombre hue;
Let me believe there are hearts still affectionate,
  Still undeceiving, unsordid and true.
Though often death’s undisputed authority,
  Claims the most gifted, the freest from guile,
Yet their dear spirits still make earth their dwelling
	place:
  Let me think thus, and he happy the while.
Let me not think that our God, unremittingly,
  Leads us poor mortals in ignorance here,
Thirsting for knowledge, expecting and wondering:
  One day with light He'll envelope our sphere.
No more dark shadows of doubt and disparagement;
   All will be sunshine, Jehovah’s glad smile;
Freely we’ll drink at the Fount of Intelligence:
  Let me think thus, and be happy the while
									L L
