FACTS AND FIGURES.
			—
  THE Wisconsin tobacco crop for 1872 is
estimated at 10,000,000 pounds.
  THE Odd Fellows of Indiana, in even
numbers, are 20,000 strong.
  THE biggest sale of real estate ever made
in Minnesota was that of 31,500 acres in
Todd and Otter Tail counties sold the other
day to a London firm for $80,000.
   KOOPMANSCHAP has gone to wreck at
last, on the Mongolian wave, with liabilities
of $260,000 and any quantity of assets in
Chinamen’s notes, quotable at a cent a pound
for old paper.
  IN 1871 the iron mills of Illinois rolled
40,026 tons of rails, iucluding 2,800 of sheet
rails, and rounded 51,152 tons, making a to-
tal production of 91,178 tons of railroad bars,
which places Illinois next to Pennsylvania
in the manufacture of rails.
   FRANZ ABT works a whole year in Ger-
many for about $1,500, while in this coun-
try twice that sum is lavished in his enter-
tainment almost nightly.  America must be
to him the Eldorado of his dream days.
  THE total membership of the Orthodox
Quakers at present in the United States is
57,105, being an increase of 715 in 20 years.
   AT THE Methodist General Conference it
was stated the other day that the church
has $70,000,000 of property, and that before
long it will amount to $100,000,000.
  THERE are 215 cadets at the Naval Acad-
emy, 39 of whom were appointed by the
President, four are naval apprentices, three
Japanese students, one is from the District
of Columbia, and 169 from the various
States.
  THE American Printworks at Fall River,
Mass., are arranging to suspend operations
for a season on account of the price of goods.
The amount of business done at the works,
requires $2,100 a day to carry it on, and
nearly $3,000,000 worth of cloth is in process
of printing from the time it goes into the
mill before it is finished.
   THERE are over 650,000 scholars in atten-
dance at the public schools in Illinois.
Teachers number over 20,000. School
houses, log, frame, brick and stone, 10,773.
Expenditures for school purposes for the
past year, $7,000,000.
   LEXINGTON, Ky., has nine hemp facto-
ries, employing an average of about sixty
hands each. The average amount of hemp
consumed per day the year round by these
firms, is from twenty to thirty tons, necessi-
tating the planting by the farmers to supply
this demand of about 30,000 acres of the
best land.
   THE following are the dimensions of the
great bridge now being erected between
New York and Brooklyn: Total length,
5,682 feet; length of central span, 1,600 ;
elevation above high water, 130 ; Width of
bridge floor, 80 feet; height of towers above
high water, 268 feet; base of towers at wa-
ter line, 121 by 56 feet.
  THE Vulcan Iron Works, Carondolet, Mo.,
employs 800 men in relays of five hours
each. The furnaces are turning out from
seventy to eighty tons of pig iron daily.
One of them is making pig iron directly
from pure. Iron Mountain (Mo.) ore, to fill a
contract with the Bessemer Steel Works at
Chicago. The rail mill is making forty tons
per day. The largest vertical, direct-acting,
blowing engine west of Pittsburg is here ;
its steam cylinder has a diameter of five feet
and stroke of nine feet; the blowing cylin-
der, a diameter of ten feet and stroke of nine
feet. Its capacity is 20,000 cubic feet of air
per minute.
