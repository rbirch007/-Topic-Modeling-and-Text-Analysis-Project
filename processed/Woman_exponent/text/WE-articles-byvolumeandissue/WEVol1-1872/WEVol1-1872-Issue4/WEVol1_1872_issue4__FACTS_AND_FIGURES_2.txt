FACTS AND FIGURES.
				—
	BALTA is a Russian city of 15,000 inhab-
itants, where, until recently, not a single
school was in existence.
	A TERRE Haute, Ind., professional sheep-
shearer says that he has not seen such fine
fleeces in the last fifteen years as this year’s
clip shows.
	THE managers of the New York and Erie
railroad have issued an order forbidding the
sale of spirituous liquors at the restaurants
along the road.
	AN English lady in the city of Birming-
ham has been made to pay a fine of L20 for
giving an undeserved “recommendation”
to a discharged domestic.
	THE model cheese factory of Utica made
last season 211,500 lbs.,. of cheese, which
sold for $21,776.81. The business will be
largely increased another season.
	THE Lincoln “Statesman” says: A care-
ful canvass of the political status of the
Lunatic Asylum taken yesterday gives the
following result: Grant, 39; G. F. Train,
4; Greeley, 1. Strickland is their unanimous
choice for Congress.
	A NEW cave in Schoharie County has re-
cently been explored for a mile. It has
statacutes, statagmites and all the rest of
those fellows. Near the centre is a dome
about a hundred feet high.
	IT is estimated that there are 400 saloons
and groceries in San Francisco, which dis-
pense annually 20,000 barrels of lager, 500,-
000 gallons of wine, and 2,000,000 gallons of
the more stimulating fluids, the total value
of which is $10,000,000.
	THE industrial and commercial growth of
Italy during the past few years has been re-
markable. Genoa alone, with a population
of only 150,000, has now twenty-four great
banking houses, with a capital of 162,900,-
000 francs; twenty corporations, with a capi-
tal of 42,328 francs, and forty-five insurance
companies.
	THE great pyramid weighs 12,760,000,-
000 tons, if anybody wants to know. Ac-
cording to Herodotus, it took the labor of
100,000	men twenty years to build it. To
show the mechanical value of modern im-
provements Dr. Lardner affirms that 480 tons
of coal, with an engine and hoisting ma-
chine, would have raised every stone to its
position.
	THE total wool product of the world for
1871 has been given at 1,121,519,000 pounds.
The largest contributing countries were as
follows: United States, 177,000,000 pounds;
England, 160,000,000 pounds; Australia,
152,000,000 pounds; La Plata, 138,000,000
pounds. These four countries furnish 627,-
000,000 pounds, or over 50 per cent. of the
total product.
	THE entire area of Manhattan Island is
14,500 acres. Taking out 1,800 acres set
aside for public parks, and 1,638 acres left
for streets and boulevards, and we have 11,-
062 acres left for building purposes. Of
these 11,062 acres, 7,253 acres are now occu-
pied by 101,000 houses, which leaves only
3,809 acres to be occupied! These 3,809
acres, at twenty building lots to the acre,
would make 76,180 more building sites on
the island. The last census gives our popu-
lation at 942,992. If we increase during the
next twenty years as fast as we have for the
last twenty years, our population will have
reached 1,600,000, or 100,000 more than
enough to cover every inch of ground from
the Battery to Harlem River.—“New York
paper."
