KIND WORDS.
				—
	The Utah “Pomologist" says, in the last
number to hand:
	THE WOMEN'S EXPONENT.—No’s 1 and
2 are before us, and we give it a hearty
welcome. Its editorials are plain, sound and
logical, and its selections pithy, newsy and
well chosen. The character, object and aim
of the EXPONENT is widely different from
that of the political, strong-minded wo-
man’s rights journals of the day. Every
family in Utah should give it support. Long
may it wave, an exponent of the sentiments
and feelings of the many good women of
Utah.	
	And the Council Bluffs “Record and
Farmer” has this in regard to our little
journal:
	WOMAN'S EXPONENT.—The above is the
title of a new semi-monthly publication, in
octavo form, now being published at Salt
Lake City, Utah, by ladies of Mormon faith,
in that Territory, who, in their Prospectus
for the paper, say: “It is better to repre-
sent ourselves than to be misrepresented by
others.” And it is to this end they have
begun the publication of the WOMAN'S
EXPONENT, a journal neat in typographical
appearance and replete with reading matter
of interest. Success to the enterprize of the
ladies.
