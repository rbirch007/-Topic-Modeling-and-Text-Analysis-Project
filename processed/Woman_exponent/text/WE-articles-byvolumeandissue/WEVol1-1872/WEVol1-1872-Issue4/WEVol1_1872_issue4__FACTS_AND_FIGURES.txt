FACTS AND. FIGURES.
			—
  IOWA marble is of superior quality.
  CARRARA, Italy, yawns with 168 marble
querrics.
  KEY WEST sends pine-apples by the ship-
load to Baltimore.
  MORE poets flourish successfully in Austria
than in England.
  THERE are over 500,000 affiliated Masons
in the United States.
  PORTUGAL, in a hundred years, has not
quarreled with any power.
  FIVE nations are working this year to raise
flags on the North Pole.
  A DISCOVERY of emeralds has been made
on the Guinea coast of Africa.
  THE latest foreign labor news is to the
effect that 12,000 German miners have
struck.
  TIGER TAIL, a son of the famous Seminole
Chief of the same name, peddles pumpkins
at Key West.
  THE annual expense of protecting the six
thousand inhabitants of Arizona from the
Indians is $4,000,000.
  A NATURAL spring that will intoxicate,
situated near Kern River, Cab, is delighting
the Indians of that vicinity.
  NEW JERSEY raised by a local taxation,
last year, for school purposes, $2,375,000,
which was an average of $14 a pupil actually
attending school.
  THERE is said to be $25,000,000 invested
in silk manufacturies in this country, and
the productions of these manufactures sell
at $30,000,000.
  AN East Saginaw' pastor declines an ad-
dition of $200 to his salary on the ground
that it is more than he wants to do to collect
the salary that he already has.
  THERE are over 650,000 scholars inatteiul-
ance at the public schools in Illinois. Teach-
ers number over 20,000. School houses, log,
frame, brick and stone, 10,773. Expend-
itures for school purposes for the last year,
$7,000,000.
  ST. VINCENT, Fla., is an island of 7,000
acres, owned and occupied for the past three
years by Mr. Hatch, a gentleman of intelli-
gence and fortune, formerly Mayor of Cin-
cinnati, who chooses to lead here the life of
a hermit, solitary and alone, save with a
servant or two, and seeing few visitors.
  IMMIGRATION into all parts of the State is
large. The influx is now setting in in full
tide. Not less than 75,000 people will be
added to the population of Nebraska in the
year 1872, and in less than ten years a mil-
lion souls will occupy this magnificent region,
whose arable area is fifty per cent, larger
than that of Illinois.
  THE library of the University of Stras-
bourg now numbers 200,000 volumes. The
old library had. from 300,000 to 700,000
volumes, and was so loosely managed that
no catalogue was ever made of it, nor is
there now a leaf of it extant. Any man in
Strasbourg or the province, of Alsace may
draw books. The city, however, is attempt-
ing to found an opposition  library, and is
soliciting aid through Europe.
  SAMUAL A. HITCHCOCK, of Brimfield, has
just given $100,000 to Amherst College and
$50,000 to Phillips Academy, at Andover.
Each of these institutions has at a previous
time received $75,000 from Mr. Hitchcock.
At Andover he established the Hitchcock
professorship of Hebrew language and
literature, and at Amherst the professor-
ship of natural theology and geology, also
bearing his name, besides six scholarships.
