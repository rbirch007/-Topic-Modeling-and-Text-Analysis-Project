﻿MORMON WOMEN.
Editor Exponent:
Under the above heading I find the fol-
lowing in the San Francisco “Pioneer” of
July 4th:
“We are pleased to see the intelligent
women of Utah coming to the front and de-
nouncing Polygamic life, and are giving the
lie to some former statements that these
women were happy under a system which
strikes at the root of womanhood, blighting
the affections and hopes, and making her a
slave to priestcraft—teaching a woman that
she is most happy when most oblivious of
every instinct of her nature. But this new
agitation among the Mormon women them-
selves, who are at last awaking up to a full
realization of their grave situation, will do
more than anything that has transpired
lately to shake the priestcraft in Utah to its
utmost depths. Let them open their mouths
and testify to the wrongs they suffer, and
their lives will no longer be but a dreary
I show.”
As a “Mormon Woman” I beg a little
space to notice this paragraph.
What definition the “Pioneer” may give
to the word “intelligent” I am unable to
say. If it is that element existing in the
composition of some narrow minds, which
protrudes itself into public notice in the
form of the grossest misrepresentations,
meddling with affairs in which it has no in-
terest but to injure those concerned, but
little need be said. Yet if my idea of in-
telligence be correct, that it is a quality of
true understanding, something which is cal-
culated to create good for its possessors and
their fellow men, to establish Truth and
Virtue and aid in uprooting error and cor-
ruption, then the “Pioneer” has been grossly
deceived, and its “pleasure” is grounded on
a great misunderstanding. For the “in-
telligent women of Utah” have not taken
the stand which, it seems, the “Pioneer”
would be pleased to have them take. On
the contrary, the intelligent Mormon wo-
men comprehend more clearly to-day than
ever before how elevated is their position
as honored wives and mothers, and the
partners of good and noble men. As for
their hopes and affections being blighted,
there is no intelligent Mormon woman that
could not say with sincerity of heart that the
better she learns to understand the religion
she professes, the brighter and purer do her
hopes and affections become. And instead of
being oblivious of any instinct of her nature,
she has a fuller realization of her true rela-
tionship to God and the works of His crea-
tion, than is possessed by those whose un-
appreciated and senseless winnings over
her wrongs she can only despise, while she
pities the ignorance, willful though it may
be, of such as seek to crowd themselves out
of their proper sphere into one which they
will fail to be satisfied with, or hold with
honorable dignity, should they ever obtain
it in the illegitimate way they have adopted.
Truth.
