NOT THE MATERIAL FOR A SHAKERESS.
—Mary Hogan was too pretty a girl to be a
Shakeress. They tied her hair and ears
back, and distorted her fair proportions by
uncomely dress, but she still refused to be
plain. Brother Jackson, warmed out of cold
faith by love and the weather, eloped with
and married Mary a few days ago. When
interviewed by some of the scandalized
brethren, Mary is reported to have said:
“You can make your apple sass and war-
rant it to keep; but gals ain’t apples, and
you.can’t bile ’em down so they won’t sour
on your old rules about marrying.”—“Wash-
ington Star.”
