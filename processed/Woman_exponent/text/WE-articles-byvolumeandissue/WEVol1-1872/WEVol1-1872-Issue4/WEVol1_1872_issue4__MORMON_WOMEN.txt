“MORMON WOMEN.”

  An extract from the San Francisco “Pioneer”
under the above title, and comments on it by a
lady of this city, will be found elsewhere in
this number. The editor of the “Pioneer”
evidently desires to be deceived relative to
the sentiments of the “intelligent women of
Utah,” those women who have proved their in-
telligence as organizers and colony-founders
along with men whose troubles and privations
they have shared and in whose prosperity they
participate. There are no people so difficult to
undeceive and as they who are self-deceived and
are satisfied with their assumed knowledge.
The “Pioneer” may be assured that the “Mor-
mon women” have long since been waked “up
to a full realization of their grave situation,”
and have solid reasons for holding that its editor
and those in whose agitation she so manifestly
sympathizes, would do well to pass through a
similar process of “awaking up;” for the con-
dition of women the world over is far from
what it should be, nor were the day-dreams so
often indulged in realized would it be greatly
improved.  But we desire to let this matter be
discussed by others of the “Mormon women” of
Utah, either over their own signatures or not
as best pleases them, satisfied that there are
hundreds of intelligent women in Utah who
can present facts and reasons on the subject
which the editor of the “Pioneer” would profit
by carefully considering and studying.
  The columns of the EXPONENT are open to
the “Mormon "Women” that they may express
themselves freely on this and other matters in
which they have been abundantly misrepre-
sented.
