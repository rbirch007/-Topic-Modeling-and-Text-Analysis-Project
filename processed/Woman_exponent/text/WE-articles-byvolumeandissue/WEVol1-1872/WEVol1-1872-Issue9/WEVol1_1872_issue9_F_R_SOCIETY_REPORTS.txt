F. R. SOCIETY REPORTS.
St. George, Sept. 11, 1872.
Editor Woman’s Exponent:
I transmit to you a report of our organi-
zation, which was established August 24th,
1868, by President Erastus Snow, assisted
by Bishops Robert Gardner and Henry Ey-
ring, and Elders William Fawcett, William
H. Branch, Franklin B. Woolley and James
G. Bleak; and consisted of twenty-five
members, with Anna L. Ivins, as President;
Emily C. Branch and Hannah E. Crosby,
Counsellors; Augusta B. Jackson, Secretary;
and Caroline A. Jackson, Treasurer.
During the past four years the people
visited by the teachers of the Society have
contributed liberally to Its fund, and we
have always had means on hand to supply
every call for aid.
In response to the call of President Young
for help to gather the poor from foreign
lands, we collected and forwarded one hun-
[new column]
dred dollars; and are still receiving money
for the same purpose. Our disbursements
during the time since the Society was or-
ganized have been upwards of two hundred
dollars, and we have on hand six hundred
dollars in goods and capital stock in the
R. V. M. Company, and Co-operative store
of St. George. Our present roll counts one
hundred and fifty members; with Anna L.
Ivins as President; Hannah E. Crosby as
first, and Minerva Snow as second Counsel-
lor; Hannah G. Perkins, President of Teach-
ers; M. C. McArthur, Treasurer; and
			CAROLINE A. JACKSON,
						Secretary.
