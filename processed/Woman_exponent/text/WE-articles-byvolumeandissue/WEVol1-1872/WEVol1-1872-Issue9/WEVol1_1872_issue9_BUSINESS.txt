BUSINESS.
	Many of the business men in the differ-
ent parts of the country, seem to consider
that if they do not murder a man for his
money, nor actually steal it from him, their
dealings must be honest and honorable. No
difference how great the advantage they
take of the necessitous circumstances in
which another is placed, or of his mistakes
or ignorance; it is the “Almighty dollar”
that they are after, and with them it is no
matter how it comes, so they get to clutch
it. But they would not like to be thought
dishonest. Oh, no; they are “our honor-
able business men!”
	And some women seem to think that it is
none of their business where the “money
comes from,” if they can only get to handle
a good share of it. They never question
their husbands as to the prospects ahead,
debentures or anything of the kind; such
affairs are no business of theirs, and they
do not trouble themselves about other peo-
ple’s concerns.
	The business of every just and upright
man, should be in all his dealings with his
fellow men, to see that they, as well as
himself, are benefitted and blessed. And
no true woman will allow her selfish propen-
sities to so far overpower her consciousness
of right, as to require means for her per-
sonal gratification which could be more
wisely spent, or which have not been ob-
tained in a legitimate way.
	It is everybody’s business to do as much
good and as little harm in the world as pos-
sible, and no one has a right to do anything
contrary to this rule.					L.
