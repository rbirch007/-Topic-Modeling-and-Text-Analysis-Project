BE KIND TO THE OLD.
Be kind to the old, back in the spring of life,
They may have been o’ertaken by the storms of
	strife,
And amid summer’s sweet buds, fruit and flowers,
They may have seen but few of pleasant, happy
	hours.

Be kind, then to the old, for in the scenes gone by.
Dark, foggy clouds have dimmed the once clear
	eye,
When sorrow’s trying flood did nigh o’erflow,
The hopeful promise of long, long ago.

Be kind to the old, for autumn cool has come;
The Reaper soon will gather the ripe harvest home;
Life’s winter neareth—Ah! how cold and drear!
With no warm, loving smiles the aged heart to
	cheer.

Be kind unto the old as you would blessing seek—
God loves the loving, the filial and the meek;
Parents will cherish, children praises sing.
And an approving conscience choicest blessings
	bring.
San Bernardino.						MINNIE.
