SPLINTERS.
	The Khedive has twenty-three palaces...
Rome has 230 convents......Santa Barbara
wants a gold note bank......High heels are
going down and spines may go up again......
The Queen of the Belgians rejoices over
the birth of a daughter......The Sutro tun-
nel is in 3,050 feet......Ristori’s daughter,
M’lle. Crisi, is to be married some time in
October......Virginia city, Montana, is full
of prize ring experts......Council Bluff’s
Spiritualists are said to exclude red-haired
people from the circle......The English
Government is considering the project of
purchasing all the railroads in Ireland at
the price of $150,000,000......It is reported
that the Siamese twins are at last divided,
one being for Greeley and the other for
Grant......The San Juan mines are still the
excitement in Colorado.......The Massachu-
setts Republican State convention has given
unequivocal and complete endorsement to
Woman Suffrage.......New Albany and Jef-
fersonville political clubs marched for hours
through a drenching rain, while being
drilled a few evenings ago, without a man
flinching......A grand ball has been given
at the California State prison........The
“Christian Union” thinks the American girl
has an easy life, compared with her
brother’s......Grant papers try to prove that
the present President was not drunk at Shi-
loh; but a journal of a different nature says,
to save his military reputation, they ought
to prove that he was dead drunk the entire
week in which Shiloh was fought......Two
Greeley and Brown flags have been raised
in Shirley village, Massachusetts, the first
anti-Radical flags that ever floated there......
A swarm of bees, In Iowa, stung a horse to
death lately In half an hour........Report
says, Blaine was as poor as a church mouse
when he entered Congress, but is now a
millionaire......Princess Thyra, of Italy, is
said to be the most beautiful woman in Eu-
rope......A Nantucket octogenarian recently
spent an entire evening at the piano, play-
ing waltzes for her fantastic-toed grand-
children......Thirty-one million dollars are
invested in the wine culture of California....
A Branford, Connecticut, girl, who has been
clerk in the post office, recently confessed
the triple crime of robbing the mail, for-
gery and passing a forged check......In
Bethany, Pa., which has always given de-
cided Republican majorities, it is said, not
one vote will be cast for Grant......Five dol-
lar counterfeit gold pieces, bearing the San
Francisco mint mark of 1867, are reported
circulating in Pioche......California fruits in
great abundance, are for sale in all the east-
ern markets......Eleven prisoners are the
present occupants of the Washington
Territory penitentiary......Charlotte Cush-
man opens the Bay State course of lectures
at Boston on the 3rd inst....It is reported
that bands of Gypsies are traveling through
western Massachusetts, stealing children
and robbing orchards and cornfields......A
Coventry workman, in England, has been
sentenced to ten years penal servitude, for
starving his little daughter, four years old;
he has been allowing his family only three
shillins a week for food, while he spent
eighteen in drink........Olive Logan has writ-
ten a play called “A Business Woman,”
which will be presented during the coming
season......A Far Western lady who was not
posted in history, and had forgotten her
geography, asked a friend who was going
to Utah, to bring her a couple of Mormons
for her aquarium......A Connecticut girl
has renounced Christianity, and embraced
Judaism and a Jewish doctor to whom she
was immediately married......Jenny Lind
is reported ill......A Keokuk man took so
much stock in the comet business that he
forgave his enemies, paid his debts and pre-
pared to die......Report says, a daughter of
the late Confederate minister, John Slidell,
will shortly be married to a German Baron...
A curious disease prevails at Barton, Ver-
mont, called by the physicians there
“typhoid dysentery.”.......Charles Whee-
ler Denison, editor of the “Nation-
al Harbinger,” Washington, “regret-
fully, conscientiously and positively asserts
that the ‘Nation’s chief magistrate drinks
too much’”.....The Philadelphia “Press”
says Grant will be elected by a majority of
four electoral votes, (!) but the Chicago
“Times” admits that Greeley may be elect-
ed President......A grand dinner has been
given by the Savage Club, London, in honor
of Stanley......In an Iowa college, the Pro-
fessor of agricultural science is a woman,
Mrs. Tupper......An attempt to organize a
Greeley and Brown club at Hillsboro is re-
ported a failure, not one person putting in
an appearance.....St. Petersburg has a
prominent female lawyer, Madam Krietzoff
......The Navy Department is believed by
some to be the most corrupt branch in the
government......In Europe and the United
States there are 150 manufactories of India
rubber goods, employing five operatives
each, and consuming over ten million
pounds of gum every year......A young lady
who has been lecturing on “Thoughts and
their Chariots,” is requested to discourse
next on “Whims and their Wheelbarrows.”
