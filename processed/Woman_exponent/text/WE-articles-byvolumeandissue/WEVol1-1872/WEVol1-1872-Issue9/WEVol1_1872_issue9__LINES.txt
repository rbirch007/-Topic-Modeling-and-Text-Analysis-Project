 LINES.
		[Written for the EXPONENT.]
Awake, my muse, so long thou’st slumbered,
	All thy laurel wreaths will fade;
Days have passed away unnumbered
	Since I last invoked thy aid.

I would have thee, while awaking,
	With bright laurel twine my brow,
For ambition’s morn is breaking
	Brightly o'er my pathway now.

Highest thoughts, bright aspirations,
	Oft my beating bosom swell;
Shall I banish such reflections !
	Shall I bid such thoughts farewell !

No, but rather let me cherish
	Thoughts so bright, so pure, and deep;
Every idle dream shall perish,
	Every lighter care shall sleep.

Life’s bread pathway lies before me—
	Shall I travel without aim ?
Guardian spirits hover o’er me !
	Help me strive to gain a name !

Hope, bright star, shall onward guide me
	Through night’s deepest, darkest gloom;
Shed its radiance o’er my pathway, 
	Light my passage to the tomb.
  Provo.								M. J. T.
