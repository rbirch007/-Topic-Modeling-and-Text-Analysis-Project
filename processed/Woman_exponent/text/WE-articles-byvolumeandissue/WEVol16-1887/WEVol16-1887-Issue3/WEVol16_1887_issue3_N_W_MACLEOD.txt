	N. W. MACLEOD.


Mrs. May Wright Sewall urged in behalf of
woman suffrage, at a recent convention:  First,
that working for woman suffrage commits
one only to a belief that the ballot should be
granted to women, and does not involve one in
the endorsement of any opinion which may be
entertained by leaders of the suffrage move-
ment upon any other question.  Second, that
the doctrine of evolution fortifies the expecta-
tion of the discovery of hitherto, hidden
spiritual forces, as well as of hitherto hidden
physical forces.  A generation that has found
a subtle, invisible vapor hidden in the pores of
deep buried rock, which can heat and light the
world, ought not to be incredulous when as-
sured that the capacities hitherto undreamed
of are discovered in the little-explored in-
tellectual and moral nature of women.
