	ALBION, CASSIA CO.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I have intended for some time to send you a
report of our Relief Society. We were organ-
ized in September, 1884, by Bishop Horton D.
Haight and Wm. C. Martindale, of Oakley."
Our Society is known as the Albion Branch,
of the Cassia Ward, Box Elder Stake of Zion,
with the following named officers: Ada I.
Brim, President, Mary Lewis, and Rosabel A.
Brim, Counselors, Fannie L. Harper and Mary
Jones, Teachers, Althea Lewis, Secretary, Louie
Brim, Assistant Secretary, and Faunie L. Har-
per, Treasurer. There are only a few Latter-

[Column 2]

day Saints here, and we are surrounded with
those that are not of our faith, but they are
peaceable people, causing us no trouble what
ever. We hold our meetings every two weeks,
and they are very well attended, considering
the scattered condition of the sisters.
	We are few in number, but we enjoy a
goodly portion of the Spirit of the Lord. The
sisters are united and are very liberal in their
donations. Our Society has progressed very
favorably so far. There are no poor among us,
for which we feel grateful to our Heavenly
Father; that He sees fit to bless us all with
means to take care of ourselves. Hoping we
may ever retain the Spirit of the Lord to guide
us in all our good endeavors, and ever praying
for the welfare of Zion, we remain your sisters
in the Gospel of Christ.
		ADA I. BRIM, Prest.
