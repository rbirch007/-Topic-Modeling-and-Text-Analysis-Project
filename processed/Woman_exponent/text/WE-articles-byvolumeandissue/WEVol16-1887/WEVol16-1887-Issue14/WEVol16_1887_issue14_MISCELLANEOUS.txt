MISCELLANEOUS.

	Miss C. Elbe has passed a successful examina-
tion in dentistry in Sweden.

	Mr. Blair, of New Hampshire, has intro-
duced in the U. S. Senate a bill for a constitu-
tional amendment giving suffrage to women.

	God openeth many hearts with gentle pick-
locks, while with others He useth the crowbar
of terrible judgments.— C. H. Spurgeon.

	Let grace and goodness be the principal load-
stone of thy affections. For love which hath
ends will have an end; whereas that which is
founded on true virtue will always continue.— 
Dryden.
	We can be thankful to a friend for a few
acres, or a little money; and yet for the freedom
and command of the whole earth, and for the
great benefits of our being, our life health and
reason, we look upon ourselves as under no obli-
gation.—Seneca.
	Pundita Ramabai is meeting with good suc-
cess in her effort to raise funds for a school to
educate Hindoo widows, who in turn shall
educate their countrywomen. “Ramabai
Circles” have been formed to raise money for
her during the next ten years, after which it is
hoped that the school will be self-supporting.
Rev. E. F. Hale, Phillips Brooks, and others
are at the head of the central organization, and
branches are forming all over the country. The
one at Cornell has already a large membership,
and there are circles in Chicago, Sioux Falls
and elsewhere. Everyone who has read her
heart-breaking book, “The High Caste Hindu
Woman,’' must wish the brave little lady suc-
cess in her work for her unfortunate country-
