EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The Primary Association of Spring City held
a fair Jan. 31st, 1888, under the management
of their most energetic President, Mrs. Emma
Robinson. There was a fine display of fancy
work, pastry and tools, such as plows, yokes,
potatoe-mashers, butter-ladles, etc., made by
both boys and girls. Among other things was
a nice piece of carpet, made by the children, to
be given to the Manti Temple.
	After the things were viewed by the company,
they were called to order, and a programme of
songs, recitations, speeches and instrumental
music was carried out.
	The singing of the children' under the train-
 ing of Bro. John Schofield was excellent. The
day’s entertainment concluded with dancing
and picnic.
