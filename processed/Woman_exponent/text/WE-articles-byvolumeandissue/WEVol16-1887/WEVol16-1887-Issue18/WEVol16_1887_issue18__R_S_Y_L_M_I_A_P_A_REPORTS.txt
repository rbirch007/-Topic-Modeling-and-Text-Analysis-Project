R. S., Y. L. M. I. A. & P. A. REPORTS.
		BEAR LAKE SRAKE.
	The Bear Lake Stake Relief Society held a
conference at Paris, Jan. 14th and 15th, 1888.
Meeting commenced at 10 a.m., Julia P. Lind-
say presiding. Singing. Prayer by Elder J.
E. Hart. Singing. Minutes of the last con-
ference read and accepted. Present on the
stand, Prest. Julia P. Lindsay and Counselors
E. Collings and L. M. Hart; the Stake Presi-
dency of Y. L. and P. A. were also present,
Presidents Budge, Hart and many other
brethren.	
	Prest. Julia P. Lindsay made some opening
remarks.
	The presidents of the different wards gave
verbal reports, showing that each Society was
in a good condition, and that all were trying to
do the will of the Lord.
	Coun. E. Collings made some excellent re-
marks; urged the sisters to look after the poor.
	Bro. Athey made a few appropriate remarks.
	Coun. L. M. Hart spoke on the great influ-
ence the mothers and sisters have in their
homes; said,  "As we have commenced another
year, let us renew our energies and improve
ourselves.”
	Conference adjourned until Sunday at 2 p.m.
Saturday afternoon was occupied by the Prim-
aries, Sunday morning by the Y. L. M. I. A.
Singing, “Lord dismiss us,” etc. Benediction 
by Bishop West.	
	Afternoon session: Conference, opened with 
singing and prayer. The statistical and finan-
cial reports were read.	
	Prest. Julia P. Lindsay said, “When, we ad- 
dress a congregation we should be influenced 
by the Spirit of the Lord, so that we may be
able to advance correct principles.” Urged the
sisters to look after the sick and poor in our
midst. Said the secretaries should understand
their duty, that correct accounts may be kept.
Spoke very impressively on educating the
young people, as they will have to hold respon-
sible positions. Spoke on the amiable qualities
of our deceased Secretary, Sister E. Pugmire,
and recommended her daughter; Sister L. Rich,
as being capable of holding this position. She
was then chosen and set apart as Stake Secre-
tary.
	Coun. E. Collings and L. M. Hart gave some
good instructions.	
	Prest. Budge spoke very encouragingly to
the sisters; approved of their labors very much;
thought we should try to become a more self-
sustaining people.				
	Conference adjourned for six months. Sing-
ing. Benediction by J. Hart.
			LOTTIE PRICE, Cor. Sec. of Stake.
