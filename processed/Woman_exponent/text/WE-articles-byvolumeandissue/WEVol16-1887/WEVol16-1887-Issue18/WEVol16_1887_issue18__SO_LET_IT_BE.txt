SO LET IT BE.

Ah yes, it must be better so I
The jarring chord too well 1 know,
Into the anthem, deep and grand,
Which my soul strives to understand,
It creeps, and drowns the heavenly strain,
And wakes to life the old, old pain.
And ’tis not fair that I should bring
Its echo in your ears to sing.
I thought that I might draw from you
A little strength, some courage true,
Born of a friendship nobly blest.
Forgive me, friend, God knoweth best,
No more my woes shall worry thee;
I'll ask not e’en thy sympathy.
						—Inter-Ocean-
