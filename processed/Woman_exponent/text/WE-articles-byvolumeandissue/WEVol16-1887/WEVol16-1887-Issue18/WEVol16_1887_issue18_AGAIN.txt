			AGAIN?

Yes, I believe in God; therefore endeavor
	To do His holy will from day to day;
And when the time shall come that He shall sever
	This vital spark of life from lump of clay;
Thanks be to Him, I know 'tis not forever
	There still remains a vital spark, a ray
That shall again re-animate this clod,
And in my flesh I know I shall see God.

And wherefore can a man such knowledge gain?
	Doth God reveal it? 'Tis written if we ask
In faith believing, we need not ask in vain,
	And yet how few that undertake the task.
Is not the great jehovah able to make plain
	All things pertaining to this, our mortal frame?
And can He not unite, as well as sever,
And let our souls, in bodies, live forever.
									A. A. TANNER.
Oakley, Idaho, Feb. 7, 1888.
