		GEORGE TEMPLE, JULY 17, 1887.

	A few short years have passed away.
		Since first thou cam’st to earth;
	Away down in the sunny south
		The land that gave thee birth.
	Those golden streets, and mansions fair, 
		Thou bad'st them all farewell,
	To tabernacle in the flesh,
		And with earth's mortals dwell.

	Thou did’st forget those rapturous scenes,
		Scenes of Celestial love;
	Thy father and thy mother, too,
		In regal courts above,
	Transporting joys of heavenly bliss.
		Lit with eternal flame
	Yea, all of these thou didst forget,
		And e’en thy angel name.
 
	God in His kindness gave to thee 
		An angel pure and bright,
	To guide thy feet while here on earth
		In paths of truth and right.
	That thy young heart impressed might be 
		With love of sacred truth,
	And yield obedience thereunto		
		While in the bloom of youth.

	And seemingly unknown to thee,
		Thy path led to the west,
	To mingle with God’s people there
		In Zion’s place of rest.
	And in His sacred temple courts
		He caused thy feet to tread,
	To minister in holy things
		For the living and the dead.

	The peace of God lit up thy soul,
		A heavenly joy serene;
	When at His shrine thou wast ordained 
		A priestess and a queen,
	And millions of thy sex have not
		Been blessed of God as thou,
	He honored thee ’fore thou wast born
		He honors thee e’en now.

	O, may thy works in temple courts
		Accepted be as thine,
	And shed a halo o’er thy life
		And after death still shine.
	And should we ever meet again 
		Let memories of the past
	Spring up like everlasting flowers, 
		Sweet-scented to the last.
