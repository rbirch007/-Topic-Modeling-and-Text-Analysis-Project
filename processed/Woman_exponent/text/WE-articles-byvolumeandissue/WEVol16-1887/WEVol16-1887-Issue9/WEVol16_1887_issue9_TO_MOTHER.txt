TO MOTHER.

Two years have rolled round,
	And a part of another,
Since I last saw the face
	Of you, darling mother; 
Now longing to see you,
	The fates still decree
Another six months
	We divided must be.

Although fate has denied me
	One glimpse of your face,
 I have ne'er found the one
	That could fill mother's place.
  In joy or in sorrow,
	In sickness or health,
A mother's dear love
	Is more precious than wealth.

Although for the present
	We divided must be,
My thoughts ever tenderly
	Dwelling on thee,
Will cherish your kind words
	And acts of the past,
And hopefully trust
	In a meeting at last.

May God's choicest blessings
	Forever rest on you,
May health, peace and happiness
	Never go from you;
May sorrow and trouble
	Keep far from your home,
And may you be prospered
	Through all time to come,

When the cold snows of winter
	Have melted and gone,
And the trees and the plants
	Their green robes have put on,
Then my hopes, like the flowers,
	Will spring up anew,
For I trust in the spring
	I'll again meet with you.
