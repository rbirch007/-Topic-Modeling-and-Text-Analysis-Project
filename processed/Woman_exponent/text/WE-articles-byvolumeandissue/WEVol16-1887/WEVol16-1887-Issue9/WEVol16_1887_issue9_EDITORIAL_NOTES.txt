EDITORIAL NOTES.

	WE have on hand reports from several Stakes
of conferences and meetings, that will appear in
due time. We have Juab, Box Elder, San
Juan and others. We have given rather lengthy
minutes of the sisters’ conference in Salt Lake,
on account of President Eliza R. Snow Smith be-
ing present, and speaking at each meeting, after
her long absence from among the people, because
of her delicate health.
	WE very gratefully acknowledge the gift of
some beautiful and delicate ferns, gathered in the
woods of New Zealand by one of the missionaries,
Bro. Davis, of South Cottonwood. They are a
most charming variety, and one with a fertile im-
agination might weave a pretty sketch of the
natural scenery of the country just from such hand-
some and artistic specimens as those which Sister
Elizabeth Davis brought to this office recently.
Though one may not have the means or the op-
portunity to travel, it is always pleasant to be re-
membered by one’s friends with little tokens from
other countries far away. Many thanks for the re-
membrance.
	WE had the pleasure of attending the sisters’
Conference of Juab Stake in company with Sister
E. S. Taylor. The meetings were held in the
Nephi meeting house, on Friday and Saturday,
the 23rd and 24th of September, and were very en-
joyable and very well attended, great interest
being manifested in each organization. The first
day, Friday, was occupied by the Relief Society
exclusively, and Saturday morning the Primary


Associations held their conference, and the Y. L.
M. I. A. in the afternoon. Our visit there was a
very interesting one, and we found the sisters who
labor there in Societies or Sunday Schools alive to
to their duties and the responsibilities resting upon
them in this important period of time.
