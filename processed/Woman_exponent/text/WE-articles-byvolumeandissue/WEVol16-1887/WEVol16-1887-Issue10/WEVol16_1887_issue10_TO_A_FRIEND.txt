TO A FRIEND.

	The world can never give thee to my arms,
	Nor from my heart thy image tear;
	It rests secure 'mid life's alarms,
	A shrine, and sacred image there.
	No human sounding lines can tell
	The secrets of that sunless well,
	Across the crowd our glances meet,
	With laughing lip, no long drawn sighs,
	But in our inmost souls we read,
	"The sad embrace of pining eyes,"
	Naming us kindred, fond and dear,
	In some past time, in some lost sphere.
