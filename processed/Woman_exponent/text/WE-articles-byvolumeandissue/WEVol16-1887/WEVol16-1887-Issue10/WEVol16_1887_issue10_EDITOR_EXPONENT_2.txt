EDITOR EXPONENT:

	At Richfield, on the 22nd and 23rd of Sep-
tember, we held our Annual Stake Fair for the
Primary Associations of Sevier County; the
Young Ladies also held theirs with us this year,
which was pronounced a great success. A
pleasant feature of the fair was the work-shop,
where children from six to twelve years, worked
at different work, some sewing, carding wool,
spinning, knitting, braiding straw, carpentering,
etc. It is really surprising to note the advance-
ment the Primary children are making, both
temporal and spiritual, and we feel to thank
God and Sister Eliza R. Snow Smith for the
organization.
	The articles that were contributed are too
numerous to mention; the hand work, baking,
and some very ingenious articles made by the
boys, were really excellent, reflecting great
credit on all. The Young Ladies’ tables were
not so well filled, it being their first fair, yet
the Young Ladies of Glenwood had their table
well-supplied both in fancy work and hand
sewing, for which they certainly deserve much
praise; there were also articles from other wards,
which showed great taste and patience. We
hope the Young Ladies will try again. 
								H.
Glenwood, Sept. 30, 1887.
