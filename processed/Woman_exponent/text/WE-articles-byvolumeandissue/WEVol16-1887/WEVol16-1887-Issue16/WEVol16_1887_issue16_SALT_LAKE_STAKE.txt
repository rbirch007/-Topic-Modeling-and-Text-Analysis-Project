		SALT LAKE STAKE.
	The conference of the Relief Society of
Salt Lake Stake was held in the 14th Ward
Assembly Rooms, in this city, Thursday, Dec.
8, 1887, Counselor Elmina S. Taylor presiding.
On the stand and platform were Sisters Rachel
R. Grant, Sarah M. Kimball, Rebecca Jones,
Zina D. H. Young, Bathsheba W. Smith,
Presendia L. Kimball, Minerva W. Snow, the
Secretary, Elizabeth Howard, and presidents of
of branches of the R. S. in the Stake. After


singing, prayer was offered by Sister Zina.
Continued by singing.  Secretary called the
roll; 20 presidents, 6 counselors and 1 teacher
responded.  Minutes of previous conference
were read and accepted.
	Coun. E. S. Taylor made brief remarks, al-
luding feelingly to the recent death of the
President of the Relief Society, Sister Eliza
R. S. Smith, and praying for an outpouring of
the Holy Spirit.
	First Ward represented by Pres. M. Steele;
Second Ward, Coun. S. McGregor; Third
Ward, Coun. F. Smith; Fifth Ward, Pres. Ann
Brimley; Sixth Ward, Pres. S. Fulmer; Seventh
Ward, Pres. M. A. Lambert; Eighth Ward,
Pres. E. Fletcher; Ninth Ward, Pres. S. Groo;
Eleventh Ward, Pres. M. McMaster, Twelfth
Ward, Pres. J. Druce; Thirteenth Ward,Coun.
L. A. Wells; Fourteenth Ward, Coun. M. W.
Wilcox; Fifteenth Ward, Coun. R. Jones.
	Remarks suitable to the occasion and in ap-
preciation of Sister E. R. S. Smith, were made
by Sisters Zina D. H. Young, Presendia L.
Kimball and Minerva W. Snow. These sisters
also urged the necessity of union and confidence
in each other, and exhorted all to earnestly en-
deavor to live near the Lord and be guided by
His Spirit.  Conference adjourned with singing
and benediction.
	Afternoon session: Opened as usual, and the
Seventeenth Ward report was given by Pres.
B. W. Smith; Sixteenth Ward, Secretary Sarah
Cumberland; Eighteenth Ward, Sister M. W.
Snow; Nineteenth Ward, Pres. Rachel Whipple;
Twentieth Ward, by a sister who had been re-
quested by the President to report; Sugar
House Ward, Pres. S. B. Gibson; Big Cotton-
wood, Pres. E. Stevenson; Union, by the Trea-
surer, M. Brady; Draper, Pres. C. Smith;
Granite, Pres. Ruth Despain; Mountain Dell,
Pres. Dixon; Sandy, by one of the Counselors;
West Jordan, Sister Cutler; North Jordan, Sis-
ter Webster; Granger, Pres. M. E. Porter;
Brighton, Pres. M. A. Hazon.
	After these verbal reports had been given,
very excellent instructions were given and testi-
monies were borne by Sister R. R. Grant, B.
W. Smith, H. M. Whitney, R. B. Pratt, M. D.,
Mary S. Clark of Farmington, and also Sisters
S. M. Kimball and E. Howard.  Their teach-
ings embraced a variety of subjects; among the
most important were remarks upon obedience,
the word of wisdom and the training of the
young people in the ways of the Lord.
	Pres. Angus M. Cannon read a letter from
Pres. Woodruff, in which some instructions were
given concerning certain ordinances and cere-
monies for the sick, and he also made instruc-
tive remarks.
	Sister Zina spoke of the words of the Prophet
Joseph concerning the afflicted and the sick,
and felt wisdom and the Spirit of God were
necessary, that there might be joy and satisfac-
tion in these labors.
	Coun. E. S. Taylor made some appropriate
closing remarks; felt satisfied with the confer-
ence.  Adjourned for three months, with sing-
ing, and the benediction was pronounced by
Sister Presendia L. Kimball.
