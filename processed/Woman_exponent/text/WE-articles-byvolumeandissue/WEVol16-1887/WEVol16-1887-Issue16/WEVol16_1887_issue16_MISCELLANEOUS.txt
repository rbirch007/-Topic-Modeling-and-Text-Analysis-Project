MISCELLANEOUS.
	Science removes ignorance but it does not
correct ill nature.
	Falsehood may have its hour, but it has no
future.
	A rich man without liberality, is like a tree
without fruit.
	Humility kneels in the dust but gazes on
the skies.—Archer Butler.
	The blue of heaven is larger than the cloud.
—E. B. Browning.
	Provided you do not tire of seeking, you
will find what you seek at last.
	Though we have a thousand friends, still we 
lack; but one enemy is too much for us.
	There is a pedigree of the body and a pedi-
gree of the mind,—Wendell Phillips.
	We speak of educating our children; do we
know that our children also educate us?—Mrs.
Sigourney.
	There is danger in keeping silent when we
should speak, but more in speaking when we
should hold our tongues.
	“My little son, you should not say your
prayer so hurriedly, but take time to think.”
“Why mother, it isn't me that has to think, it’s 
Claire; I can say my prayer without thinking.”


At the last meeting of the Hartford (Ct.)
Equal Rights Club, Mrs. S. G. Young read a 
letter from U.S. Senator Palmer, in which he 
said:
	“The enfranchisement of women, in my 
opinion, is not far off. Even conservative papers
concede that. Their right to vote should 
place the family as the foundation of the State.
The floating vote of nonstake-holders in soci-
ety would be neutralized.”
