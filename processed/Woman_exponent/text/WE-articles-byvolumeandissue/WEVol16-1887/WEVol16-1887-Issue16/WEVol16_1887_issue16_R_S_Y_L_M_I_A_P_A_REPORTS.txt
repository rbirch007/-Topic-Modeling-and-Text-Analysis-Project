R. S., Y. L. M. I. A. & P. A. REPORTS
		PRICE, EMERY CO.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We are doing the best we can in our scat-
tered condition in behalf of the Relief Society
here at Price. We have a good band of sis-
ters here who are working unitedly under the
directions of our worthy President, Sister Cox.
Our Relief Society has been organized two
years.
	On the 17th of November, being our Presi-
dent’s birthday, the sisters all gathered for
meeting and brought with them a selection of
dainties. The tables were spread and every-
thing in readiness for a surprise. When she
came we had a very enjoyable time.
	Sister Sarah L. Cox then gave a short
sketch of her life and showed in many ways
how she had been protected by the hand of
Providence which was very interesting.
	Others of the sisters spoke a short time in
behalf ot Sister Cox’s diligence. Sister Cox
selected a hymn, “’Mid scenes of confusion,"
etc. She was then given some very nice presents,
and adjourned to meet in the evening, which
we enjoyed, and all went home feeling we had
had a good time. 
		Your sister in the Gospel,
		MARIAH BRYNER, Assistant Sec.
