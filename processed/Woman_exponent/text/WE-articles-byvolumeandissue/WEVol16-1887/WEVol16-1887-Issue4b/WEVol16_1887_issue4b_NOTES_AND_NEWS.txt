NOTES AND NEWS.

	Mrs. Sawyer, wife of the Mayor of Lincoln,
was recently admitted to the bar of Nebraska.

	Mrs. Myra Bradwell has for the eighteenth
year prepared for publication the session laws
of Illinois.

	Miss Mary Tillinghurst, who has designed a
painted window for a church in New York, is
said to be the first American woman em-
ployed in ecclesiastical work. She is the same
lady who was paid a fabulous sum by Vander-
bilt for some tapestry hangings.

	Miss Phoebe Cousens, just appointed marshal
of the U.S. Court in St. Louis, is the first
woman who ever held such a place. She is
appointed by Justice Miller to succeed her


father, and it is thought that the President
will make her tenure permanent.—Ex.

	The Woman's Congress, of which Mrs. Julia
Ward Howe is President, will meet in Masonic
Temple, New York on the 26th, 27th and 28th
insts. Sorosis and the New York Local Com-
mittee to the A. A. W. are making great pre-
parations to entertain the Congress, and
an enjoyable as well as useful week
is anticipated. All women who expect to attend,
whether members or not, are requested to send
their names to Mrs. Geo. W. Townsend, 351
Pearl Street, New York. The publications of
tho Association are no longer mailed to those
whose dues are in arrears.
