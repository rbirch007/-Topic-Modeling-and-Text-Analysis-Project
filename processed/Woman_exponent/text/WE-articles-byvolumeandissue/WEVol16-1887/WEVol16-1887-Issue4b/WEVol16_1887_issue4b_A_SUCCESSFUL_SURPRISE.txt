A SUCCESSFUL SURPRISE.

	Last night the Fourth Ward school house
was made the scene of an enjoyable entertain-
ment given in honor of Mrs. Jane S. Richards,
President of the Relief Society in this County.
The affair was arranged by the Fourth Ward
Society, under the direction of Mrs Marianna
Stratford, President, and her counselors, Mrs.
Maria Chambers and Mrs. Elizabeth Parkinson;
and they were supported in the plan by Mrs.
L. W. Shurtlift, counselor to the Relief Sociey
stake presidency. The entertainment was a
complete surprise to Mrs. Richards and her
family. They were invited to be present as it
to attend a meeting, and when Mrs. Richards
discovered the real nature of the gathering she
was overcome by emotion
	Speeches, songs, instrumental music and
brief addresses filled the time except when the
company was at the supper tables. Every
feature of the picnic and entertainment was a
success. Apostle Richards was present and
made some instructive remarks.
	An eloquent and touching address was read
to Mrs. Richards. It was signed by the offi
cers and members of the Fourth Ward Re-
lief Society and it expressed the love and con-
fidence of all in Mrs. Richards. A tasteful
little memento accompanied the address. Mrs.
Richards responded toward the close of the
entertainment with words well-chosen and full
of gentleness and help.
	The surprise was a success in every way, and the
ladies who managed it are to be congratulated
upon the good taste with which the affair was
conducted.— Ogden Herald.
	Oct. 20, 1887.
