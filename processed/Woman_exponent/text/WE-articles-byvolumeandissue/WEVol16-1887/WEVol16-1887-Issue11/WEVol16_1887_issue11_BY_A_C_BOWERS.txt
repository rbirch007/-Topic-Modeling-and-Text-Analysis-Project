			BY A. C. BOWERS

A poet’s vision, clad in the fair guise 
	Of a lily, all in white and gold—
	Hers not the form for passionate arms to fold;
She loves, but loves in such angelic wise
As might some wanderer from the upper skies,
	Who wears, with rosy lips of tenderest curve, 
The starry purity of saintly eyes.
	But if some lofty purpose were to serve,
The fair and delicate figure then would seem
	One who could w lk, with straight unshaken tread.
	The flaming city of the unpardoned dead
(Shown to the Florentine in lurid dream),
	Serene and Scatheleess thro’ the infernal glow—
	Nor touch of fire upon her raiment know.
				—New Orleans Times-Democrat.
