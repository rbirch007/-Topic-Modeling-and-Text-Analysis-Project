EDITOR EXPONENT:
	I thought a few lines from St. Johns might
be of interest to the readers of the EXPONENT
to let you know how we are getting along in
this far off land. I am pleased to say that the
prospects look brighter than they have in the
past. We have a good Relief Society here
with our beloved Sister, Lucinda Richey, for
President, with her Counselors, Helena Jensen
and Hannah Arnold who are women always
on hand to do all the good they can for the
Society; we have, also, a good band of teachers
who are willing to do their duties as well as
the rest of the sisters; in fact, I think we have
as good a Relief Society here as they have
anywhere, as we have, in the past, had quite a
little to contend with; but we have yet re-
mained faithful to our mission; and I think
that I can truthfully say as one of our leading
men said, that those who stayed here were in-
deed Latter day Saints.
	We were organized as the St John's Stake
of Zion, on the 24th of July, by the visiting 
brethren. We have for Stake President of 
the Relief Society, Sister E. L. Udall, with
Sisters T. A. White and E. M. Patterson Coun-
selors, and Sister E. M. Farr, Secretary.
	The Brethren met with the sisters on the
25th and gave us some very good instruction
as to our duties as wives and mothers, and also
explained to us the necessity of living up to
all the principles of the Gospel.
	We have, for Stake President, D K. Udall
with E. Treeman and W. Gibbons counselors.
As Bishop, Willard Farr, with Erick
Larson and Niels P. Johnson Counselors.
	We feel to acknowledge the hand of the
Lord in having good and upright men to lead
and guide us. Ever praying for the welfare
of the EXPONENT, I remain,
		Your sister in the Gospel,
			CLAUDINA JENSEN, Secretary.
