DEAR MRS. WELLS AND OTHER MORMON
SISTERS:
	I was told the other day by an acquaintance,
that my “Mormon friend, Eliza Snow is dead,”
and he said that notices of her death were in
all the Cincinnatti papers. I did not see the
notices but I felt very much saddened to hear
of it. One’s life is made up of regrets, at
least my life is. I shall always regret that I
never had a fuller acquaintance with “Sister
Eliza,” as you all so lovingly called her.
	She was a noble, a wonderful woman, it
seemed to me, and I shall now always think
of her as being in heaven with the many who
were “first at the sepulcher,” and as always
busy and happy. I have been reading Miss
Phelp’s books lately and heaven seems to me
more like a real place, and that we shall know
each other there.
	One by one the older ones of your Church
are going to the other home. I wonder what
the future of the peculiar people will be. I
have long intended to write something for the
EXPONENT, but my time is so entirely used up
with home duties; and the public disregarding
the fact that children growing up into woman-
hood and young manhood increase the calls
upon a mother s time, makes many demands
upon me, which as a mother, a woman, I can-
not refuse to respond to. There is work in
church and moral movements for women now,
God grant we may all do our work faithfully.
How much I should like to have known Miss.
Snow personally, and how I should like to know
you all. May peace and love be with you here,
and everlasting joy in the home beyond is the
wish and prayer of your sister,
						EMILY SCOTT.
Dec. 18, 1887.
