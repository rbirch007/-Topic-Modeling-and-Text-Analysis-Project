				NANCY ANDERSON.

	DIED, at her residence in Wanship, at 10:30 a.m., Nov.
26, 1887, Carolina Dortha Peterson, born in Goderstof,
Holstein, Germany, Oct. 8th, 1835. She emigrated to
Denmark in 1847 with her parents Henry and Christine
Madeline Dabelstein and was there married to Andrew
Peterson on April 27, 1857, to whom she bore seven sons
and five daughters. One daughter has gone before her;
the rest, with their father, feel the great loss of a beloved
wife and mother. She received the Gospel, with her hus-
band, in 1851 and was baptised on the 4th of February in
that year. The Lord manifested great power in her be-
half. She emigrated to Utah with her husband and family
in 1866. She received her washings and anointings in the
house of the Lord in 1868 and had twice been in the
house of the Lord at Logan to perform Ordinances for her
dead relations and friends. She embraced all the known
principles of the Gospel. She was a member of the Wan-
ship Relief Society and had been a teacher for many years
performing all duties faithfully. She died, as she lived, a
faithful Latter-day Saint, which was shown by her last
charge to her family to be true to the Gospel for that is
true and all the principles thereof and listen to the counsel
of their father as she had done and God would bless them.
The funeral was conducted by Bishop Young at the late
residence of the deceased and appropriate remarks made
by Counselor William Crook. Henry Reynolds, Counselor
to the Prest. of the High Priest Quorum and Bishop Ma-
lin of Rockport. She was followed to the cemetery by a
large multitude of her relations and friends and dedicatory
prayer offered by Elder Henry Reynolds.	COM.
