EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Because the Chester Annual Primary Fair
held here a few days ago was such a joyous oc-
casion I write a few details.
	Stake President, Sister Helena Madsen, and
a Miss Wickham, favored the Fair by their
presence and most excellent remarks.
	The juveniles did wonders in making
things, the result of the inevitable jack-
knife from the little chair,through agricultural
implements, wagons, etc.
	Nor were the little ladies behind in the
dainty needle work, in all sorts, and sweet
culinary productions, and it was a real pleasure
to take their dainties so pleasingly offered you.
And Emma the president fairly bubbled over
in ecstacies as her little ones vied with each
other in song and recitation. Allusion was
made to Prest. Petersen’s suggestion of a
lOcts. subscription  by each member of the
Stake Primary the full name of each subscriber
to be recorded in the Temple Record.
	In response to this the little ones sent up
$9 45 in cash; also 25 yards of most excellent
rag carpet and a beautiful floral and animal
rug the labors of the deft fingers of the “little
toilers.” When you consider our limited set-
tlement the few who take hold of these things
“it was more than ye all” besides it is a most
gratifying evidence of the new regime of this
place.
	H.
Dec. 17, 1887.
