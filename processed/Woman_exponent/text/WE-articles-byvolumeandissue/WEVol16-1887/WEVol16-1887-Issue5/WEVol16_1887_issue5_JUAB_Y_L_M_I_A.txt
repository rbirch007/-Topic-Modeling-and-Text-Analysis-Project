	JUAB Y. L. M. I. A.
	The Young Ladies of Juab Stake held their
quarterly conference in the Nephi meeting
house, June 24th, 1887. The meeting was pre-
sided over by Sister M. E. Teasdale, Stake
President. After the usual opening exercises
of singing and prayer, the minutes of the pre-
vious conference were read and approved. A

[Column 3]
programme, previously arranged, was well exe-
cuted by the members of the several wards
throughout the Stake. This consisted of songs,
readings, recitations, organ solos, and the read-
ing of the “Young Ladies’ Manuscript Jour-
nal,” interspersed with short addresses by the
members.
	The meeting was also addressed by Sister
Mary Pitchforth, Bro. James Picton and Coun.
Charles Sperry of this Stake. Each gave ex-
cellent instruction to the young people, and
showed that these organizations were formed
for their special benefit, and encouraged them
to attend strictly to their duties in the same.
Verbal reports of the several wards were given,
showing a steady improvement of those attend-
ing the weekly meetings.
	Sister M. E. Neff, Prest. of the Nephi Asso-
ciation, tendered her resignation with regret,
on account of inability to attend to her many
duties. Her resignation was accepted, and
Miss Lizzie Schofield sustained to fill that posi-
tion.
	The Stake and local officers were then pre-
sented and sustained unanimously.
	After the close of the meeting, the officers
and members, and other invited guests, assem-
bled in the Relief Society Hall to partake of
strawberries and ice cream, prepared by the
young ladies in commemoration of the twelfth
anniversary of their organization.
	The days’ enjoyment closed with a ball in
the evening, under the auspices of the Y. L.
M. I. A.
