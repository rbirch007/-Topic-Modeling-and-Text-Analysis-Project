MISCELLANEOUS.

	It is a signal of honor to be an arrow in the
Almighty’s quiver.—Macduff.
	Always learn to think and act for yourself.
Learn to say no; it will be of more service
to you than to be able to read Latin.—John-
son.
	Resolve to edge in a little reading every 
day, if it is but a single sentence; if you gain
fifteen minutes a day, it will make itself felt at
the end of the year.—H. Mann.


Mrs. John Taylor, once a bride of the White
House, has been calling on Mrs. Cleveland,
and reciting some sparkling reminiscences of
her early married life as the wife of a presi-
dent. She has five children and a beautiful
young granddaughter.
