A SURPRISE AND A REORGANIZA-
				TION.

	On April 27th last the members of the Re-
lief Society of Graham, Arizona, met at the
residence of President Annie Jorgansen to
give her a surprise before she left, as she was
making preparations to join her husband, he
having been in Mexico two years. She was on
a visit to her daughter’s, ten miles distant, so
she was completely surprised on her return
home to find the house crowded with sisters, and
the tables loaded with good things.
	After all were seated, a few appropriate lines
were read by the Secretary, and Sister Jorgan-
sen was presented by the R. S. with a large
portrait of our late President, Brigham Yonng,
handsomely framed. She responded in a very
impressive manner, thanking the sisters for
their kindness to herself and family. After
an hour or two spent in pleasant conversation,
the company dispersed, wishing Sister Jorgan-
sen a pleasant journey and health and happiness
in her new home.
	May 4th President Wilmirth East, Counselor
Mary L. Ransome and Stake Secretary, Lucinda
Gustaverson, met with the sisters to assist the
Bishop reorganize the Relief Society. Sister
Pauline Freestone was chosen President, with
Vercy Stelle and Mary Peterson, Counselors;
M. E. Skinner, Secretary; Martha Mulanare,
Treasurer.


Presidents East and Ransome each made
very encouraging remarks after a few words of
counsel by Bishop Peterson. Sister Gustaver-
son gave some advice to the Secretary; the
meeting was dismissed, all feeling that we had
had a time of rejoicing long to be remem-
bered.
	We have had some sickness of late, and two
of our brethren and one little girl have been
laid to rest in this settlement. Previous to
last month we had three deaths in six years,
but we could not expect to be always so
favored.
	Ever praying for the cause of truth, and
success to the EXPONENT, I remain,
		Your sister in the covenant,
			MARY E. SKINNER.
June 27, 1887.
