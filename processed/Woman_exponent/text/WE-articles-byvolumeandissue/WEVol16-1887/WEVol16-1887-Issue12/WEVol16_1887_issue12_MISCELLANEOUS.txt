MISCELLANEOUS.

One seldom speaks of the virtue which one
has; but much oftener of that which fails us.— 
G. Lessing.

Whatever is the subject of faith should not
be submitted to reason, and much less bend to
it.—Pascal.

Tears are the softening showers which cause
the seed of heaven to spring up in the human
heart.—Sir W. Scott.

Poetry is music in words, and music is poe-
try in sound; both excellent sauce, but they
have lived and died poor that made them their
meat.—T. Euller.
