RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.

     The Y. L. M. I. A. of Minersville adopted the following
Resolutions Oct. 1st, 1887;
     Whereas, On the 25th day of September, 1887, it pleased
an Alwise Creator to remove from our midst by the hand
of death, Sister Maria E. Walker, born August 26th, 1858,
and,
     Whereas, She was elected President of the Y. L. M. I. A.
Nov, 24th, 1884, which office she held until the time of her
death, and,
     Whereas, She was an active member and teacher of the
Relief Society, also a teacher and Secretary of the Sab-
bath School,
     Whereas, She has filled these several offices with faithful-
ness and integrity, showing by her example and precept
that she was ever willing to do her duty, therefore, be it
     Resolved, That the community have sustained a severe
loss in the demise of one so good and useful; therefore,
be it
     Resolved, That we extend our heart-felt sympathies to
the bereaved husband aud relatives; also,
     Resolved, That a copy of these Resolutions be spread
upon the records of the Y. L. M. I. A.; also one sent
to the WOMAN'S EXPONENT for publication, and one pre-
sented to her husband.
