INTERESTING ITEMS.
Juarez, Mexico, Oct. 24, 1887.
Editor Exponent:
	Perhaps a few notes from this far away
place in regard to woman’s work may not be.
amiss:
	In July of 1886, though our camp was in an
unsettled state, Brother Teasdale thought it
best to organize a Primary Association, which
he did, with Sister Hannah H. Romney, Presi-
dent; Sisters Olive C. Moffet and Mattie Dil-
man, Counselors. Apostle Teasdale encouraged
the children and officers with his presence while
he remained with us, and quite a lively interest
was manifest in the training of our children.
On the 7th of last July an organization of
the Relief Society and Young Ladies’ Associa-
tion was effected, the President of the former
being Sister Elizabeth Hawkins, and of the
latter Sister Dora Pratt, both of whom have
labored faithfully in their respective offices,
and with good results. Sister Romney was
also at the same time given two new Counselors,
as her previous ones had moved away, Sisters
Mary E. Taylor and Annie Skonsen being sub-
stituted.
	Sister Minerva W. Snow was present at the
organization, and gave the sisters of the differ-
ent associations some excellent instructions in
regard to their duties, etc. She has endeared
herself to the sisters here by her active sym-
pathy and encouragement.
We are doing as well as we can in our un-
settled condition, but hope to do much better
when we have a comfortable schoolhouse to
meet in, which we hope will be in the near
future.
	The young people are anticipating, a nice
time this winter in their conjoint meetings, etc.
	There has been considerable sickness the last


two months, but the health of the people at
this date is much better, except that many of 
the children are having sore eyes just now, but
this does not deter them from turning out to
their Primary meetings, as nearly every child
in town was present last week.
			Yours as ever,
							KATE.
