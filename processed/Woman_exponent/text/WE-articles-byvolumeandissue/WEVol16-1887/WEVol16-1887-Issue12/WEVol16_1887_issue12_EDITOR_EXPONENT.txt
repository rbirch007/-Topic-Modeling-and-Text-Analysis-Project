EDITOR EXPONENT:
	October 20th, 1887, we held our Annual
Fair for the Primary Association of Millville;
the Young Ladies also held theirs with us this
year. Meeting held at ten a.m. Singing by
the Young Ladies. Prayer by Sarah Gamble.
Then singing by the Primary Association.
	V. S. Cummings addressed the meeting, and
spoke very encouragingly.
	Sister Jane Cummings said she was pleased
to see so many children, and all looked so happy
and well.
	Sister Jane Molen addressed the children in
her usual pleasant way. She said that she was
pleased to meet with the children, and also the
young ladies, and was pleased with the pro-
gramme; she thought they rendered their parts
well; she was also pleased with the articles that
the Young Ladies and Primary Children had
made; hoped they would still continue in well-
doing, and not forget these annual fairs, for
they are very beneficial to the young people.
	There was a dance for the children in the
afternoon; they all enjoyed themselves very
much, and felt well. We concluded the day
with a dance for the young folks at night
	With good wishes for the EXPONENT, etc.,
				SARAH GAMBLE, Prest.
