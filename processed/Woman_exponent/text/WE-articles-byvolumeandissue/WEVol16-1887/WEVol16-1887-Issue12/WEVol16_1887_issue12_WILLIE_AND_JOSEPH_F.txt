WILLIE AND JOSEPH. F.

I knew the first, a boy so bright and pure,
	So gentle and affectionate and good,
High in the angel’s records, I am sure,
	When his release was sent, he must have stood.

The little fellow when he was but three,
	Stoutly refused on Fast-day morn, to eat;
For, if his father fasted, so must he—
	And off to meeting went, firm, yet so sweet!

But Willie died, while yet a little child,
	Although he seemed so rosy, well and strong;
Ah! friends, be not by outward signs beguiled—
	The best and noblest oft remain not long.

Then, as a recompense, another son,
	Our Father gave, with tender, loving grace;
A child "peculiar" from his birth, and one
	They felt could almost fill the vacant place.

The parents, fond and hopeful, full of joy,
	That God remembered them, and heard them pray,
The name of "Joseph F," gave this dear boy,
	For the brave Prophet Exile of to-day.

Oh, Saints of God! we are so very slow,
	To understand our Father’s wise decrees;
It is not meet all should to manhood grow,
	Behind the vail there’s work for such as these.

Each has his part assigned, each knows his place,
	They come to earth, their bodies thus secure,
Return, and there before the Father's face,
	They minister in work sublime and pure.

Thus, friends beloved, your Joseph came and went,
	And you shall know, in God’s own time and way,
For what peculiar mission he was sent—
	Be faithful, and await the blissful day.
									LULA.
