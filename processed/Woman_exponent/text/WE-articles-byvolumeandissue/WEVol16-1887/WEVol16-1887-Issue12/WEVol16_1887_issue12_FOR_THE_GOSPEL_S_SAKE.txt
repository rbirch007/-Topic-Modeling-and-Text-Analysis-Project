		FOR THE GOSPEL’S SAKE.

	Adieu, sweet homes, we must bid you adieu,
	Dear wives and children; and hasten from you;
	The thoughts of a prison extorts a deep sigh,
	Our hearts sink within us, to high heaven we cry.

	The stripes in our clothes not long shall we wear,
	But robes of bright glory shining and fair:
	With a crown on our heads that will dazzle the sun,
	And from glory to glory eternally run.
						MRS. E. G. WILLIAMS.
	Farmington, March 1st, 1887.

	The lady is eighty-two years old, and belonged to the
first Relief Society that was organized in the Church by
Joseph Smith the Prophet.
