SUMMIT STAKE.
	The Summit Stake Relief Society held their 
conference at Kamas, September 29th, 1887.
Meeting commenced at 10 a.m., M. J. Atwood,
Stake President, presiding. Singing by the
choir. Prayer was offered, and the choir sang
again.  The minutes of the last conference
and reports from the different branches were
read and accepted.
	There were on the stand Sisters Zina D. H. 
Young, of Salt Lake, for the R. S., Sister
Rogers, of Farmington, for the Primaries,
the Stake Presidency of R. S., presidents of
Y. L. M. I. A. and Primary Associations, Prest.
W. W. Cluff and others, and a very good
attendance in the hall.
	Much good instruction was given by the sis-
ters and President Cluff, which was well appre-
ciated and enjoyed by those present.  Arrange-
ments were made for another Fair for the bene-
fit of the Summit Stake House, to be held in
May next at Coalville.  The reports from the
several branches show them to be in good work-
ing order.
	On Friday, Sept. 30th, the Y. L. M. I. A.
Conference was held at the same place, occupy-
ing the whole day, which was very interesting,
and they arranged to join the Relief Society in
the Fair.
	The Primary Conference was also held at the
same place, on Saturday, Oct 1st., occupying
the whole day.  The services were interspersed
with questions, which were creditably answered
by the children.  It was the fullest attendance
and the most interesting Primary Conference
ever held in Summit Stake.
	The three days’ conference was a feast seldom
it ever enjoyed before by the sisters of Kamas.
The blessings they received from the sisters of
Salt Lake Valley will long be remembered.
