		“THE KAURI TREE.
	"Stateliest of all the forest giants of New Zea-
land is the Kauri Tree, with its crown of
powerful branches twined into a dark, dense
green roof, and attains to the height of 140 to
150 feet, and even 200 feet Its trunk and
branches produce an abudance of resin or
Kauri gum, and which is found in the earth
from the surface to twelve feet deep, and affords
employment for some 10,000 to 12,000 people
in the neighborhood and north of Auckland,
which is sent away by the ship-load, and used
for varnish, etc., as well as many beautiful
ornaments, watch chains, brooches, eardrops,
etc. Everything in the New Zealand forest is
evergreen, and it is lovely and grand.
				"Very respectfully,
					"Eurini Ruihi Reweti,
					"Edwin Lewis Davis."
