R. S., Y. L. M. I. A. & P. A. REPORTS.
HUNTINGTON.
Editor Woman's Exponent:
Thinking you might find space for a few
lines in the columns of your little paper, I take
pleasure in writing them.
Our society is in a thriving condition con-


[Column 1]
﻿sidering that this is a new place. We have a
granary nearly completed which will hold
about one thousand bushels of grain, and hope
to have it filled some day. I think we will
have it all paid for this fall.
	There has been considerable sickness here
this fall with typhoid fever. But the sisters
seem to be doing all they can to help one an
other. There is a good spirit in our society.
Our monthly meetings are well attended con-
sidering there is so much sickness. Wishing
the EXPONENT the success it deserves, I re-
main,
			Your sister,
				Julia Whitney, Sec.
