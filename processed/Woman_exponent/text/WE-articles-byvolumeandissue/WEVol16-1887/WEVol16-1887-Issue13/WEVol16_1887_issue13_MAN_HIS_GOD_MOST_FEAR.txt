MAN HIS GOD MOST FEAR.

The signs of the times are conspicuous,
	The fig tree is beginning to swell,
And the time is speedily approaching,
	When but few will say, "It is well!"
Bloodshed, wars and pestilences,
	And coming advents are clear,
When jars, contention and strifes—
	And man his God must fear. 

Earthquakes and general commotions
	Are terrifying the hearts of men,
While labor and secret combinations
	Are united heart and hand
To destroy the peace of the nations;
	The judgments of God are near,
He will pour out His indignation,
	And man his God will fear. 

The rich in vanity and splendor
	In palaces gorgeously arrayed—
The poor have cause to murmur
	When their children cry for bread—
They have forgotten their wise Creator,
	And unto fables now give ear;
They will have to endure His vengeance,
	And man his God will fear.

They have changed the laws and covenants,
	And they are wafting to and fro,
And are worshipers of mammon,
	Endeavoring the truth to o'erthrow;
As well might they say to the sun, "Be still!"
	To the angry waves, "Stay there!"
The kingdom will continue to grow,
	And man his God will fear.

Zion's banner is now unfurled,
	The Gospel must be preached,
For the field is white to harvest,
	We will gather in the wheat;
We will call them to repentance,
And to oease their wild career,
	And teach the laws of heaven,
For man his God must fear.
