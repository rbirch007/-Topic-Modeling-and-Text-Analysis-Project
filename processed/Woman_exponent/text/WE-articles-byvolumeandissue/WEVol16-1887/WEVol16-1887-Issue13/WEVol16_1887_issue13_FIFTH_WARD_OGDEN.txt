		FIFTH WARD, OGDEN.
Mrs. Emmeline B. Wells:
	Dear Sister:—In compliance with your
request I send you condensed minutes of the
organization of our Relief Society.
	According to appointment a goodly number
of the sisters residing in the Fifth Ward, also
Prest. Jane S. Richards and Courselors, met
with the Bishopric of said ward, on the 13th
day of June 1887, to organize a Relief Society.
	After the usual opening exercises, the Bishop
said he bad consulted with his Counselors and
Prest J. S. Richards in regard to officers, and
they had decided on the following sisters,
whose names were presented and unanimously
sustained: Rachel F. Burton, President;
Maria S. Stevens and Martha H. Brown, Coun-
selors; Mary A. Watson, Secretary; Lydia Mor-
rison, Assistant Secretary; Sarah Watson,
Treasurer.
	After the organization was effected, the sis-
ters who had been elected, being called upon
expressed themselves willing to act in the po-
sitions assigned them, and desired the assist-
ance of the Holy Spirit that their labors might
result in much good.
President Richards, her Counselors, and
Counselor G.W. Larkin addressed the meeting,
giving much wise instruction and many words
of encouragment.
	After a few closing remarks by the Bishop
the meeting was adjourned for two weeks.
Singing. Benediction.
