OBITUARIES.
DIED, In Huntington, Emery Co., Utah, October 15th,
1887, Catharine A. Johnson, wife of Joseph E. Johnson,
and daughter of Peter and Catharine Johnson, of Hunting


ton.  Deceased was born in Ephraim, Sanpete Co., Utah,
June 28th, 1862, and was married to Joseph E. Johnson
January 18th, 1878.  She was of a kind and gentle disposi-
tion, and an earnest worker in the cause of truth.  She was
a member of our Society, and was chosen President of the
Y. L. M. I. A. when it was first organized in this place, and
retained that position until about a year ago.  The be-
reaved husband was on a mission to the Southern States,
but returned home a few days after her burial.  She leaves
a loving husband and two little children, and a large circle
of friends and relatives to mourn her departure.
										COM.

     DIED, in Mill Creek Ward, Salt Lake County, October
17th, 1887, after a lingering illness of more than one year;
Albertine Ariminta North, born Dec. 22nd, 1867.  De-
ceased was a young lady of exemplary character, and her
acts in life were those of a good and pure girl, and she
bore her long illness with patience and fortitude.  She was
a member of the Y. L. M. I. A. and a teacher in the Sab-
bath School, and was always willing to cheerfully respond
to all calls made upon her.  She lived and died a true Lat-
ter-day Saint.  She leaves a father and mother, brothers
and sisters, and a large circle of friends to mourn her loss.
Her friends feel to sympathize with Brother and Sister
North and family in their sad bereavement, and feel to ask
the blessings of heaven to rest upon them to comfort and
cheer their hearts.
						CLARA E. SNEDAKER,
In behalf of the Y. L. M. I. A. of Mill Creek Ward.
