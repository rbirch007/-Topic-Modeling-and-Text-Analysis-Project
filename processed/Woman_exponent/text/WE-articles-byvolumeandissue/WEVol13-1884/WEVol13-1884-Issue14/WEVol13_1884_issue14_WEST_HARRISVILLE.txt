	WEST HARRISVILLE.
	The Relief Society of West Harrisville, We-
ber Co., was favored with a visit from Sister
Jane S. Richards, Stake President and her
Counselor, II. C. Brown, also Sister Sarah S.
Richards, who advised, encouraged and in-
structed us in a very able manner on the prin-
ciples of the Gospel. Bishop P. G. Taylor
and his Counselor, W. Ransom, also gave us
some very good instructions.
Our prayer is that we may so live that we 
may have the blessing of ouur Heavenly Father 
to help and assist us in carrying on the 
work before us, and obey every commandment 
given. Ever praying for the welfare of the
Exponent.
			CHRISTINA HEGSTED, Sec.
SOUTH COTTINWOOD WARD.
Minutes of the Primaty Meeting of the
South Cottonwood Ward, held in the ward
house, Thursday, Nov. 12th, 1884. Called to
order by President E. M. Davies, at 11 o'clock.
Singing, "In our lovely Deseret." Prayer by
Sister Ann Wheeler. Singing, "Don't kill the
little birds." Roll called, showing an attend-
ance of thirty-five members. Minutes of previ-
ous meeting read and accepted. Next in order 
came the songs and recitations, etc, by the
members of the association, which were very
good. Prest. Davies followed in short remarks,
exhorting the children and thanking them for
their good behavior and for being so punctual
with their part of the programme.
SIster Bathsheba Smith occupied a short 
time; said she was pleased to see so many pres-
ent and such good order; told the children to
be prayerful and keep out of bad company.
The song, "Word of Wisdom," was sung by
members of the association, followed by Bro. T.
A. Wheeler, Sister Mary Rawlins and Sister
Ann Wheeler, after which Sister Davies said
there had been a quilt made by the association
and donated to the Hospital.
	Meeting adjourned till 2 o'clock the next
Saturday. Singing "Joseph Smith's first
prayer." Benediction by Bro. Job Welsh.
ELIZABETH DAVIES, President,
EMILY E. BRINTON, Secretary.
