THE DEAD YEAR

Dead, dead, old Year, are all thy happy days,
The flow’rs of Spring that gemmed thy pleasaht ways;
The birds that sang on many a clustered bough
In chorus sweet are hushed in silence now;	
	The year is dead;
The fruit is gathered in, the leaves are fled.

Oh, tell us,&nbsp;vanished joys, what way ye went— 
Ye moonlit evening hours, from Eden lent,
Ye fragrant morns half girt with silver mist,
With clouds of crimson, rose, and amethyst !
	Where are ye now,
Ye dreams that Time’s stern teachings disallow?	
Where are ye? For to-night strange ghosts do rise
And vex my loneliness with eager eyes,
With sweet pale faces, fair and full of light.
Ah, how they throng around my hearth to-night,
	And whisper low
In tones that death has silenced long ago !

Nor these alone; for there are deeper eyes,
With glances keen as stars in winter skies
In the last moments of the passing year
The guardian angel of our souls comes near,
	And, good or ill,
Of every life makes a faithful record still.

Look back, my heart, through all the crowded days—
Their acted falsehoods and uneven ways,
Their ceaseless care and strivings after naught—
Nor wonder at the change the years have wrought,
	Nor think good fruit
Can ever grow on folly’s bramble shoot.

O winds, the softest dirges ye can sing
To this unquiet&nbsp;soul no peace can bring,
No healing balm ! With our own hands we twine
The shackles ’neath whose weight we most repine.
	Through this new year
God grant us strength to go with conscience clear.
