EDITOR EXPONENT:
    I feel it a pleasure to pen a few lines to my
sister, the readers of the Exponent. We
often read of the good times enjoyed by our
sisters in associating together at meetings, so-
ciables and birthdays; so we thought we would
let you know we were not far behind in that
line here in Arizona. Not long since I had
the pleasure to meet and shake hands with an
old friend, sister and neighbor from your city,
who is at present the guest of Prest. Layton.
    The 16th of September was the birthday of
our highly esteemed sister, Elizabeth Layton;
quite a number of brethren and sisters took
their baskets filled with the good things pre-
pared for the occasion, and gave her a surprise.
About forty-five sat down to a sumptuous re-
past.
    Prest. Willmirth East, with several sisters,
came up from Pima, bringing with them a
handsome birthday cake prepared by their own
hands. We had a very pleasant time and all
enjoyed an excellent spirit. After we had par-
take of the good things, we were regaled with
music and singing by Brother Joseph East and
wife, also by Sisters Mary and Pamelia Ran-
som, and Lucinda Gustaveson; recitations by
Prest. W. East; songs by Sisters E. Lay-
ton and Halliday, also Bro. J. Moody; 
reading, the Prophet Joseph's last address to
the Nauvoo Legion, by one of the ladies;
speeches by Prest. and Sister E. Layton, after
which there was music and dancing until
about ten o' clock.			W. EAST.
