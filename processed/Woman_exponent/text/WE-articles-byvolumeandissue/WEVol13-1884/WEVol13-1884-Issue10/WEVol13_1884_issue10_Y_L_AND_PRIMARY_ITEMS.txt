Y. L. AND PRIMARY ITEMS.
Editor Exponent: 
	Knowing, as I do, that it is always a source of
gratification and pleasure to you to hear of the
progress of the Y. L. M. I. A. and Primary, I
venture to send you an account of the Y. L. &
P. A. of Fillmore and Holden.
	Being on a visit at these places I attended the
meeting of the Y. L. A. of both places; also an
annual Primary meeting at Holden, which was
a most interesting one in every way, the child-
ren seeming to be most attentive and one with
their President, Sister Kelson, who was unani-
mously re-elected.
	The Primary gave a concert on Friday even-
ing and acquitted themselves in a first rate
manner.  A little band of drummers in uni-
form being the novelty of the evening. The
singing and recitations were excellent.
		Your sister in the Gospel,
				M. A. Greenhalgh.
	Meadow, Sept. 18,1881.
