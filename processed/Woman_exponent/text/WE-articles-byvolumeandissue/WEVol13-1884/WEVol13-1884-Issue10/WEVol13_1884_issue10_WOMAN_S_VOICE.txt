WOMAN'S VOICE.

My Dear Sisters:
	I desire to thank you for your many words of
consolation and advice which I have received
through the medium of this dear little paper, the
Woman's Exponent. As I have not the pleas-
ure of listening to the counsels of the Saints
who have been gathered home to Zion, it is a
great pleasure to me to be able to read them.
	I, too, am indignant at the misrepresentations
of Mrs. Newman and others who have said so
much about the sisters in Zion.  Although I
have never lived in Utah, I have parents there,
and I have lived among the "Mormons" many
years of my life, and I know there are a great
many untruths told about them; but as it was
in the days of Jesus, when He said, "Let not your


hearts be troubled, for these things must needs
be;" but rather rejoice that you are where you
can be led and guided in the right path and your
Heavenly Father will never forsake you in the
future any more than He has in the past, if you
continue to do His will.
						ANN HARDY.
	Council Bluffs, Iowa.
