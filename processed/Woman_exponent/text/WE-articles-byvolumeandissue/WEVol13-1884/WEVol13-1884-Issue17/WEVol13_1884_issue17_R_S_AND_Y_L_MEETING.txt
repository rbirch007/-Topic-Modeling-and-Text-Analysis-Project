R. S. AND Y. L. MEETING.

	The Ladies Semi-monthly meeting was held
in the Fourteenth Ward Assembly rooms,
Satuaday, January 27, Mrs. M. I. Horne presid-
ing. Singing, "Lord, Thou wilt hear me when
I pray." Prayer by Counselor Phebe Woodruff.
Singing, "Glory to Thee, my God." Minutes of
previous meeting read and approved. Minutes
of Y. L. Associations from 20th 6th and 17th
wards, also minutes of P. A. from the 5th, 4th,
15th, 11th, 17th and 10th wards were read by
the reep[-]ctive secretaries.
	Prest. Horne then addressed the sisters.
Spoke of tha cultivation of our young people,
and thought we should all seek to learn those
things that would be for our best good and ad-
vancement. Said, we are living in a time that
requires us to be wise and humble, and when
we must live near to God and do His holy will,
if we would expect His blessings. The time
has come that our Prophet Joseph Smith spoke
of, when all the world is turned against us. Let
us try to be faithful and merit the Lord's pro-
tecting care, for we know not what may come
to us or ours. Let us also remember our breth-
ren who are being so tried, and offer up our
prayers in their behalf.
	Coun. Woodruff exhorted the sisters to be
faithful in the performance of every duty rest-
ing upon them. Wished those present to im-
prove the time in bearing testimony.
	Several of the sisters bore excellent testi-
monies.
	Sister Mc. Master spoke in the gift of tongues,
and Sister Lawson gave the interpretation.
Prest. Horne made a few closing remarks, in
which she gave a brief account of the trials the


Saints had to pass through in Missouri, and said
the Lord protected us then, and if we are faith-
ful He will protect us now.
	Meeting adjourned for two weeks. Singing,
Doxology. Benediction by Sister E. S. Tay-
lor.
