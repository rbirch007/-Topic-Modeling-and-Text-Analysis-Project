THE HEART DOOR.

There is a little grave, watered well with tears,
	Where I used to sit when winds were still,
Dreaming sad dreams of the might have been,
	Indulging in vain regrets until
The Savior entered, and shut the door,
And that old pain comes to my heart no more.

Did He not say, as He used to walk
	On the vine-clad hills of old Galilee,
The sweetest thing He said or taught
	Was, "Let the children come unto me."
So, when He entered in my heart's door,
Sorrow departed to come no more!
