CORRESPONDENCE.

	GARDEN CITY, Rich Co., Jan. 15,1883.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Thinking perhaps a few items from our north-
ern country would be welcome, I will try to pen
a few lines.
	I can truly say that your valuable little paper
is an ever welcome guest; we read it and enjoy
the instructions therein. And while we are en-
joying peace here, for which we feel thankful,
we do not know for how long a time. When I
read of what is termed legal proceedings with
our brethren in different places, many of them
born legal heirs to the Constitution for which
our forefathers fought and bled, I can but ex-
claim, Where, oh, where has liberty fled? They,
for the exercise of their religious faith, are put
into prison and fined; whereas, our glorious
Constitution guarantees unto all religions to en-
joy that right. It was written by inspiration,
and has prepared the way for the bringing to
pass the purposes of the Lord in establishing our
holy religion upon the earth in these the last
days. Then let us all, sisters, pray earnestly
unto the Lord to hasten the time when our
brethren may be removed from prison and re-
turned to their families and the bosom of the
Church, which may God grant.
	Our Relief Society in this place is in good
condition; we hold our meetings regularly, and
have the Spirit of God with us. The Young
Ladies' and Primary Associations are in success-
ful operation.
	Praying the Lord to bless you and give you
strength for your great labor, and for the differ-
ent societies and associations, I am your sister
in the Gospel,
