EDITOR EXPONENT:	
	Thinking that a few lines from our quiet
little town would not be uninteresting to the
many readers of your valuable paper, we en-
close the following:
	It is a general time of good health among
the people of this place. The holidays have
passed off quietly and pleasantly; especially so
did Christmas day, when a social party was


held under the auspices of the Y. L. M. I. A.
of Willard, which was a very enjoyable and
praiseworthy affair.
	At 1:30 o’clock p.m., the invited guests as-
sembled at the hall, which was tastefully de-
corated with mottoes, pictures, etc., for the oc-
casion. At 2 o’clock the afternoon exercises
began, and until 5 p.m those present were en-
tertained with speeches, songs, recitations and
instrumental music, all of which were credit-
ably rendered.	
	The afternoon exercises being over, all were
permitted to partake freely of the contents of
the baskets of the members of the. Y. L. M. I.
A., which were well filled with the bounties of
this earth, so tastefully prepared that none
could refuse partaking of the pleasant repast,
After picnic those present went forth and en-
joyed themselves in the dance until near mid-
night, when all wended their way home im-
pressed with the idea that the day was one not
soon to be forgotten.		
	Your sister in the Gospel,
	MARION.S. PERRY, Prest.,
