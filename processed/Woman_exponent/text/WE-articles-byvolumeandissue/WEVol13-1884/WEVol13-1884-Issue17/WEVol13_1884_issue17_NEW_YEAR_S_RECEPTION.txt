NEW YEAR'S RECEPTION.

	Sister E. R. S. Smith spent the Holidays iu
Brigham City. During her visit here she at-
tended several of our meetings and social gath-
erings and contributed much to their profit and
social enjoyment.
	On New Year's day a reception was held in
Prest. Lorenzo Snow's residence in honor of our
esteemed sister, under the supervision of Prest.
Minnie J. Snow, assisted by her counselor, Miss
Freddie Widerborg. One hundred ladies from
different wards of the city, members of the R.
S. and Y. L. M. I. Associations, in groups of
twenty five arrived at various intervals during
the day. As each party of guests appeared they
were cordially received, introduced and seated
in the parlor, where Sister E. R. S. Smith gave
expression to her surprise and appreciation of


this unexpected honor. Indulging a short time
in social conversation, many reminiscences of
by-gone days were related. At the expiration
of the time thus pleasantly employed, all re-
paired to the dining hall, surrounded the taste-
fully spread tables to partake of refreshments.
Here toasts, sentiments, etc., enlivened the
scene. Every succeeding company took Sister
E. R. S. S. unawares, as she supposed each de-
parture completed the day's surprises.
	One feature of the occasion was of peculiar in-
terest; the last party were Scandinavians, many
of whom were advanced in years, their counte-
nances radiant with joy as with faltering step
they advanced, to greet their esteemed friend.
At the table the kind wishes and pleasing senti-
ments offered in their native tongue, were inter-
preted by Sister Mary Stars, their President
and el[-]cted hearty and appropriate responses
from Sister E. R. S. S. and our generous hostess.
So passed the day: the occasion cheered and
made happy many hearts, and we all realized
the truthfulness of the proverb—"In blessing we
are blest."	M.
