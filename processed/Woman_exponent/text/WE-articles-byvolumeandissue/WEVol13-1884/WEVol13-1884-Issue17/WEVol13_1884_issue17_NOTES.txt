NOTES.
	During the debate on Wednesday on the
anti-church doctrine resolution, Mrs. Stanton
in her remarks tending to show that organized
religion had ever tried to keep woman in abase-
ment, asked what it was that bound the Hin-
doo woman on the funereal pyre of her dead
husband? "Religion! Religion it is that keeps
the harems of Turkey full; and what but re-
ligion could ever induce the Mormon women
to endure that abominable and destroying prac-
tice of polygamy?"
	The character of ladies who participated and
who were present was intellectual, at least in
appearance, if not in fact. There were many
young women present. There were not a
great number of men, but the number of males
increased each day. Mrs. John T. Caine and
Mrs. F. S. Richards of Utah were interested
spectators and listeners to a large part of the
proceedings.
	Several of the ladies who are attending the
convention have never attended the Washing-
ton convention before. Mrs. Gordon of Cali-
fornia makes her appearance here now for the
first time. Br. Chapin of Albany, Mrs. Fray
of Ohio, Miss Burr of Hartford, Mrs. Colby of
Nebraska and Mrs. Ellis of New York are also
here for the first time. Mrs. Halleck and Mrs.
Hull are Quakers, and there were several
other ladies from Maryland belonging to the
Society of Friends present to-day. Miss An-
thony is of Quaker ancestry; Mrs. Colby of
Nebraska is editor of a paper; Miss. Laura
White of Kentucky is sister of Representative
White of that state. She is a student of arch-
itecture, and proposes to make architecture her
profession. Mrs. Post of Wyoming is the wife
of Delegate Post. She has been the foreman
of a jury and served on a jury when she was
the only woman present.
