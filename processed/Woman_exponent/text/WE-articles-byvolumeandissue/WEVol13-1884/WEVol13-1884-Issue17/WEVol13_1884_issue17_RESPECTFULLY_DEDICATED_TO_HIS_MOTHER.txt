RESPECTFULLY DEDICATED TO HIS MOTHER.

In the spring the flowers will bloom again,
	The air be laden with perfume sweet
That the south winds bring of the bursting buds
	That are springing to life 'neath the zephyrs' feet;
The sun will smile on the ice-bound streams
	And send them dancing along their way;
And wonderful treasures of fragrant life
	Come out with the light of each opening day.

	Till it grew to a proud, o'ershadowing tree,
Is dead by the touch of the spoiler's hand—
	Spring cannot bring back its life to me.
I had thought to hide 'neath its branches strong
	When the withering heat of my day was high,
And to rest in its beautiful shelter long,
	As the sun of my life crept down the way.

But it fell, untouched by a slow decay—
	Its sweet strength crushed in a single hour,
While its stately beauty was fair to see,
	And its life seemed full of unmeasured power;
And we who trained it and loved its growth
	Stand here in the storm our hearts must brave
And see nothing above but a stormy sky,
	And nothing below but this precious grave.

Lionel—my boy—the hopes are not vain—
	The hopes that arise from thy tear-stained bed—
Of a purer life in an endless spring,
	But now I only remember—my boy is dead.
I strive to look up in our Father's face
	And read in its frownings a blessing hid.
But ah! can a blessing be given again
	Like that lost one under the coffin lid!

O, God make us patient, we two who walk
	With weary feet toward the gathering night,


With this strange, deep grief like a shadowy cloud
	Shutting away all of earth's sweet light.
We would ask no greater boon than this
	Of the Father who pitied our anguish deep,
Than to walk in the path His life has marked,
	Then lie down at His side and forget to weep.
