BY HIS WIFE.

Welcome, oh, welcome, to home and to me,
And thy children’s fond hearts have a welcome for
	thee!
Thy friends and thy neighbors will gather around,
And from brothers and sisters the welcome resound;
Thy mother will meet thee with welcome most kind
And from nephews and nieces a welcome thou'lt find.
My heart has been lonely while waiting to hear
The signal to tell me thy coming was near,
And, oh, how it beat when I saw by the press
That thou wert returning thy children to bless;
With thy precepts to guide them, thy fond love to
	cheer,
Thy presence to shield them from terror,or fear;
They will tell how they missed you when prayer time
	came 'round,
And they yearned for a look, or a smile, or a sound
Of thy voice to encourage, to soothe, or restrain.
To share their young joys and their faith to sustain;
Even baby has seemed for her father to mourn,
And daily looked forward to papa's return—
She was "going to Salt Lake to meet him," she said—
Twas her first thought at morn and her last going to
	bed;
And now she will see him—I scarce can believe
That thou art returning my cares to relieve;
To share in our pleasures, to chase our weak fears,
To brighten our smiles, and to dry up our tears,
To pray with us, talk with us, tell where thou hast
	been,
And give us an outline of what thou hast seen.
In the long winter evenings how good it will be
To feel I have round me my children and thee:
To see thy kind smile, feel the press of thy hand,
See thee mingle again with thy little home band
When their voices in praise to their Father on high
Are lifted in thanks that their papa is nigh,
And they ask to be granted this one prayer more,
That father’s long absence for some time is o'er,
And. across the broad ocean no more he may roam,
But his mission in future be nearer to home.
