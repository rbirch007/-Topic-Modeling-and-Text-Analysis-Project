EDITOR EXPONENT:
	On Thursday last Sister Julia Howe gave a
most enjoyable entertainment to the widows of
the 17th Ward.  Her parlors were thrown open
for their reception, and two long tables were set
in the dining-room, on which were placed the
substantials of the season, with many delicacies
abundantly garnishing the feast. Dinner was
served at four o'clock and the Sisters Reese,
Alder, Keetie Kimball and Miss Zina Hyde,
were the ministering spirits attendant upon the
tables. They greatly assisted, too, in the order
and satisfaction that every one preserved and
felt.
	Sister Hyde presided by appointment over the
assembly, and by her ever kind, affectionate, and
unassuming manners made all feel at home.
	After dinner, all adjourned to the large and
beautiful drawing room, where, after a time, the
guests were called upon to express their senti-
ments upon such subjects as they chose to


speak upon. Many arose and spoke in an inter-
esting and edifying manner of their experience
in life, of their happy associations and enjoy-
ments in social gatherings, of journeying across
the plains, etc. Songs and recitations were also
engaged in.
	All expressed their appreciation of Sister
Howe's kindness and benevolence in providing
search an entertainment for them, and called
down blessings upon her.
	At ten o'clock all returned to their homes re-
freshed and strengthened by "the communion
of Saints"
	Our Sister, in speaking, said President Taylor
o served at the last Conference that from that
time the widows should be blessed, and she be-
lieved that in this meeting that prophecy was
being realized.
