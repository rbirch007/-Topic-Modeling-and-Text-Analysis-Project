	HARRISVILLE. 
	Minutes of a Primary Conference, held at
the east school house, the Primary Association
of West Harrisville meeting with us, com-
mencing at 1 o'clock p.m., Prest. Mary E. 
Taylor presiding. Singing, "In our Lovely
Deseret." Prayer by Bro. L. J. Taylor. Sing-
ing. Minutes of previous meeting read and
approved.
	The President made the opening remarks.
and gave some very good instructions. She
was then followed by the children, with songs,
dialogues, recitations and select reading, which
was very interesting to those who were present
There was a goodly number of the parents
present, who seemed very much pleased with
the exercises of the Primary children. Prest.
Harriet Daybell felt pleased with the children
of the Association; thought they manifested 
great interest in their meetings.
	The speakers were Coun. Wm. Rawson, L.
J. Taylor, T. Brown. N. L. Shurtliff, Jane
Rawson, Lydia Martin, Lovina Hegstead; each
one made some very appropriate remarks, en-
couraging the children in well doing; thought
it a great privilege for the children in these
days to have a meeting where they can be 
taught the principles of the Gospel. It was
also remarked that we should be thankful for
our comfortable homes, when there are so many
out in the world without homes such as we
have. We should live prayerful, and pray for
each other; the Lord will hear your prayers
just as quick as He will your parent's. You
should honor your fathers and mothers and be
kind to every one. There were many other
good instructions given.
	The President said she had been well edified
in listening to the exercises, also the good in-


structions given by our brethren and sisters
that were present, and felt like thanking the
parents for coming out to see us. 
	Adjourned the joint meeting for three
months, to be held at the west school house
Singing, "Lord, dismiss us," etc.  Benediction
by K. D. Brown.  
	ANNIE E. SHURTLIFF, Secretary,
