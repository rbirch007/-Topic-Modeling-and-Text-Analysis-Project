A WORD FROM WEST WEBER.

	April the 1st the members of the Relief So-
ciety, Y. L. M. I. A., and Primary Associations,
 and a number of the brethren of the West
Weber Ward, met at the school house to pre-
sent Prest. Mary Douglass with a small token
of respect.
	Almost the entire company were there at the
appointed hour, which made it quite a surprise 
to her, not having the slightest idea of the pro-
ceedings. A beautiful easy chair was presented
to her by the sisters, and the following lines
were composed and read by Bro. Archibald Mc-
Farland:
To our honored President and fellow laborer, Sis-
ter Mary Douglass.
	We, The members of the Relief Society,
take pleasure in expressing goodwill toward 
you on this occasion, and request your accept-
ance of a small token of our confidence and es- 
teem, in which we trust you may often enjoy
the rest so justly due one as efficient in every
good work as you have ever been.
	Sister Mary Douglass returned her thanks
to the sisters for the handsome present, and
their goodwill toward her.	
	The West Weber Brass band was in attend-
ance, and played some lively airs. The tables
were spread with the good things of the earth,
and a bounteous dinner was partaken of, which
added much to the pleasure and comfort of the
party.
	After the dinner tables were cleared away,
Bishop J. I. Hart and Counselor James F.
Hunter and Bro. A. McFarland made some
excellent remarks, suitable for the occasion.
	After the speakers were through the Primary
children engaged in a dance. At seven o'clock
in the evening the aged brethren and sisters
convened in a dance and songs.
	A good spirit prevailed, and all enjoyed
themselves. In conclusion it was a day long to
be remembered by all present.
		PUAH S. HART, Secretary,
		FRANCIS C. HART, Ass't Secretary.
