FLOWER.

Curious flower-vine of sacred love,
Giving a history of one above;
Teaching a lesson of wondrous truth
To the aged, as well as the youth—
You teach that nature is truly kind,
Though false and wicked are mankind.
She gave a witness, who saw Him slain,
She sympathized with our Savior's pain.
Yes, little flower, you are a living proof,
A silent witness of eternal truth;
You show the nails that held His hands and feet,
And the hammers that drove them complete;
Also the blood veins so gory red,
With the thorny crown that bound His head;
And the long sharp spear beside,
Which pierced so deep His bleeding side.
The three days that He laid in the tomb,
Before He went to His heavenly home,
Are also numbered quite true by thee,
Sweet Floria's history of that tree.
And the twelve apostles we count in thy leaves,
That form the crown for which nature grieves;
And the grave we can find, also,
Among the leaves that round the cross do grow.
Sorrowfully up that cross you did climb,
To witness that awful deed of crime;
And though they killed the body dear
You ever live trusting souls to cheer;
Full of life, thou eternal evergreen,
Like His Spirit you so surely seem;
Ever with your green hand pointing above,
Where ever lives One who died for love.
"Passion-flower" is thy beauteous name,
Great and worthy is thy sweet, fair fame;
Among all the children of all men
You are the Great Author's pen.
Ah! how simple, how easy, how plain
To read Thy words of suffering pain,
How great thy mission, little vine—


How grand, how gloriously sublime!
Oh! who can doubt, when they gaze on thee,
That Jesus died for us upon that tree.
