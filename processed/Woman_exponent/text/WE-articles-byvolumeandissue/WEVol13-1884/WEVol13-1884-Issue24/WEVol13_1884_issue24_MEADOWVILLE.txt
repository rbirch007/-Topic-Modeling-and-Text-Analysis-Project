	MEADOWVILLE.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	For two years the Relief Society of this
place have not been holding meetings, having
no poor to attend to, until September 28th,
1884, Prest. Julia Lindsay, with other sisters
and brethren, also Bishop J. Kimball and
Counselors met with the members of the So-
ciety and advised them to resume their meet-
ings, since which time we have held meetings


monthly, on fast day, at two p.m. The Society
is presided over by Sister Ann M. Eldredge,
who is a good, faithful, energetic woman, and I
believe we fully appreciate the privilege of
living in this "out of the way place," and being
"nobody in particular.”
 We enjoy much of the Spirit of the Lord in
our meetings, and pray the Lord to bless those
of our brethren and sisters who are being per-
secuted for righteousness' sake, and hasten the
time when Zion shall be free.
  Wishing every success to your welcome little
paper we remain with you in all good works.
