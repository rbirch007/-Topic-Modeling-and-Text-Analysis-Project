IN MEMORIAM.

	Nellie Johnson, daughter of Heber F. and Lizzie
Johnson, was born May 13th, 1884; died March 17th,
1885.
	LITTLE NELLIE.
Tiny little fingers, but oh! so strong their hold,
Little waxen fingers now so still and cold;
Fold them very gently, for their work is done.
Her earthly sojonrn's ended, her heavenly home is won.

Just for one little moment sent from God above, 
Bringing a gleam of sunshine, lighting hearts with
	love;
Pure as any snowdrop, and as fleeting was her stay,
But her coming brought sweet flowers to life's weary.
	way,

To be remembered often, for their fragrance ne'er will
	die,
And she's waiting for her loved ones in that blessed
	home on high,
Beyond the grave’s dark portal, where all parting will
	be o'er,
Waiting in joy to greet them on the bright golden shore.

So lay it very gently, her little form to rest, 
The germ of life immortal is now with heaven's blest.
"Suffer the little children," said Christ, "to come to
	me,"
And we, too, must be like Him if His kingdom we
	would see.

Lay away the little tokens of her short life on the earth,
Think and speak about her often, as ye gather round
	the hearth,
For 'twill bring pure thoughts and feelings, turning
	them to God,
And His Spirit’s power will help you to bow beneath
the rod.

Ah! there are many households who have lost their
	treasures thus—
But no, not lost! in heaven there’s neither moth nor
	rust.
So dry the scalding teardrops, repress the weary sigh,
And prepare to meet the loved ones in the “sweet bye
and bye."		S. E. R.
