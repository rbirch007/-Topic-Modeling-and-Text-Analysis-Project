IN HER LEFT HAND A MIRROR.

Truth! thou pure and heavenly maiden!
	Daughter of a sire divine
Of a mother gospel-laden,
	Of a long, unsullied line.

By thy robe of purest whiteness,
	By the lamp above thy head,
By the mirror in its brightness—
	By all these may I be led
Through the oft bewildering mazes
	Human footsteps have to trace
Through the wondrous varied phases
	Met within life's complex race.

"What is truth?" was Pilate's query,
	Man of half awaken'd soul,
Little ken'd he one so dreary,
	He just pass'd His earthly goal,
Could be truth personified!
	The Son of man to whom the world
Yet should own Him deified
	By the motto He unfurled.

Truth!—how few its power defining
	Have the wisdom or the will—
Crowds we see it oft declining,
	Fondly nursing error still.

In this word, that has no equal,
	What a volume lies unread;
Eternity contains the sequel,
	By heavenly oil its lamp is fed.

Oh! may truth, by God's own Spirit,
	On my heart, my lips, my brain,
Be impressed by its own merit,
	And all its wealth may they retain.

Truth, God's motto, is eternal
	As the heavens eternal are;
Blooming bright, and ever vernal
	Like yon glorious Polar Star!

Let us guard it as the jewel
	That shall blaze around our crown
When we pass a life renewal,
	When we lay the earthly down.
