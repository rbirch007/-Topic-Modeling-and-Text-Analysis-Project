A QUESTION, OR WHY IS IT?
				------
   Why did our Savior teach His disciples to
pray, saying, "Our Father who art in heaven,
Hallowed be Thy name.  Thy kingdom come.
Thy will be done in earth as it is heaven,” etc.
	Would He have taught them to pray for
this if it would never come to pass?  For He


certainly had a prophetic view of the future.
Now, if this is to be, how will it be brought
about? Is not Christ's sermon on the Mount
the first stepping stone? Those who apply
their minds strictly and faithfully in observ-
ance of its mandates, will always find a happy,
peaceful spirit attending their efforts; also a
feeling of charity, a light and intelligence
which they did not perceive before, called the
good Spirit.
	This good Spirit will expand the mind and
enlarge the soul, if cultivated and exercised, as
it certainly will be by applying our minds to
learn this one, and great lesson. It is
such an important lesson that we cannot ad-
vance to any perfection without it; as also its
spirit attending our efforts.
	This sermon has had some attention given it
by the churches—just enough to hold society
together. But this will not be sufficient in our
day. Do you ask why? Because the day is
far spent, and the time is at hand when the
Bridegroom is coming to reign on the earth.
He has sent a message to this effect. We, as
a people, have received it. Those, who in the
sincerity of heart, believe in the Gospel of
Jesus Christ, are benefited by its precious
truths, and do realize that every word which
He has spoken is true and faithful. There
were some who followed because of the loaves
and fishes. They are doing the same to-day.
But such have not much depth of root, and
when the heat of the sun is felt they wither.
	Let us be faithful in learning this important
lesson, as also those given for this dispensation;
remembering, though heaven and earth pass
away, not one word which He (the Savior) has
spoken, shall fail.
