TO THE Y. L. M. I. A., BURNHAM.

To the Y. L. M. I. A. of Burnham Ward, New
Mexico.
DEAR GIRLS:
 	Having a feeling of love for you, and a de-
sire to be remembered by you, I endeavor to
write a few lines, which will be but little,


nevertheless we often show our true characters
by little acts, and our life is made up of little
things, therefore we should attend to all our
little duties promptly and faithfully, for the
little acts are the elements of true greatness,
and if we have any good acts they are gifts
from God, and should be cultivated, no matter
how small they are. Even a smile or a kind
word often heals a wounded heart; and remem-
ber, one unkind word often wounds a heart.
We should study never to speak one word to
hurt or injure another, and always try to speak
well of every one, and be not too hasty to
credit evil reports; wait and learn the whole
truth before you decide, and remember, an in-
jurious rumor originated, perhaps, in sportive
gossip, once attached to a person’s name will
remain beside it a blemish and a doubt forever.
But it is too much the nature of all to speak
and believe evil of another, and thus we go on
day after day throwing stones at each other.
	Then let us each one, as daughters of Zion,
shun such a principle, and if we have evil
spoken of us walk lightly by it, and try to
prove to the world it is not true; and let us
remember we make our own character, and
we should ever be ready to cultivate the facul-
ties God has given us.
	There are girls who choose truth and honesty
as a soul companion, and their words and
actions proclaim it, and their feet tread the
paths of virtue; it makes them pure and noble,
and they are loved by all who know them; and
when God looks around for His jewels they
are the girls His eyes rest on. From your
	Friend and Sister,
		CLARA.
Logan, April 17th, 1885.
