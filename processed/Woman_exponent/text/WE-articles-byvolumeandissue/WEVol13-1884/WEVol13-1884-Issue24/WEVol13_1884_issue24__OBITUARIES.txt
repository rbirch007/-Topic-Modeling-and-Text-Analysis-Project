OBITUARIES.

	DIED, at Mount Carmel, Kane County, Utah, on the
morning of May 1st, 1885, Brittanna E. Mage Jolley,
supposed to be born in the year 1814, in Pitt County, N.
C.; married to Henry B. M. Jolley October 31st, 1833, in
Weakly County, Tennessee. Moved to Illinois in the
year 1842; was baptized into the Church of Jesus Christ
of Latter-day Saints in the year 1842; arrived in the
Valley of Salt Lake 1852. Was the mother of ten
children, seven sons and three daughters, and two sons
dead; grandmother to thirty-eight living and five dead
grandchildren; was great-grandmother to thirty-one
living children and three dead, making a grand total of
one hundred and nine children. Was a faithful member
of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, and
esteemed and loved by all her friends.
	R. M. ENGELSTEAD.
Mount Carmel, May 4th, 1885.

	DIED, at her residence, in Vermillion, Severe County,
Utah, on the 13th of March, 1885, of heart disease,Anna
Maria Berg Dastrup, aged sixty-five years. Sister
Dastrup was born on the 22nd of January, 1820, in
Welsbek, Clesvig; came to Syalland, Denmark in June,
1828; was married to H. L. Dastrup October 16th, 1841.
She joined the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day
Saints in 1853. Was baptized by Elder H. I. Lund.
Came to St. Louis on the 9th of March, 1856, and started
for Utah July 3rd,1859, and arrived In Salt Lake City in
Dec., the same year.
	Sister Dastrup was a good Latter-day Saint, and a
faithful worker in the Relief Society, having been secre-
tary for several years. She was living in the celestial
order of marriage, and died in full faith of a glorious
resurrection.
	She leaves a husband,one son and eight grandchildren
and many friends to mourn her loss.
		REBECCA NEBEKER, President,
		VIOLET IVIE, Secretary.
	DIED, at Smithfield, Cache County, Utah, March 26th,
1885, Maria F. Smith. She was born October 13th,
1822, in the State of Florida. Was baptized into the
Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints in 1849.
Emigrated to Utah the following year.
	She died as she had lived, a faithful Saint, true to
every principle of her religion, prompt in the discharge
of every duty. She has gone to reap the reward of the
righteous, and though we now mourn her loss, we know
it is her gain, and we who are left live in the hopes of a
glorious meeting at the resurrection morn.
	COM.
