		WASATCH STAKE.
Conference of the Relief Society of the
Wasatch Stake of Zion was held in Heber
City Hall, Sept. 28th, 1884. On the stand
were President Emma Brown and her Counsel-
ors; also several of the brethren.
	Our conference being held on the Sabbath
day we had a very large attendance. The
choir favored us with some of their sweetest


songs, which made our meeting a very enjoy-
able one. Prayer was offered by Bro. Hicksen.
Minutes of last conference were read and ap-
proved. Written reports were read, which
showed the Society financially in a very good
condition. The Presidents of the several wards
gave in very favorable reports. Sitsers Rich-
ards and Taylor were with us, which made our
meeting more than usually interesting.
	Sister Richards, in her quiet, loving way,
gave us the very best of counsel; to mothers
in particular, to see after their children, their
daughters, to know where they are and who
they are associating with.
	Sister Taylor said without the Spirit of God
she would be unable to say anything to benefit
her sisters. Said we should always attend our
meetings if possible; we would feel better, and
make better wives and better mothers. Never
allow our children to be taught by those who
are not of our faith. Said we should try and
improve ourselves that we may become good
financiers.
	Sister Brown spoke a short time, felt to thank
the sisters for coming to visit us, and hoped
the sisters would profit by the good counsel
they had received. Singing, “God moves in a
mysterious way,” etc. Benediction by Bro.
John Murdock.
