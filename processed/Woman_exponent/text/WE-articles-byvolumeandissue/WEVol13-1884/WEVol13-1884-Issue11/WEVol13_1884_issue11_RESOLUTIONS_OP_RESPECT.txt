RESOLUTIONS OP RESPECT.

	To the memory of Maria J., wife of Bishop J. E.
Booth, who depared this life October 7, 1884, in Provo
City:
	Whereas our Heavenly Father has seen fit, in His Al-
wise providence, tn call from our midst our beloved sis-
ter,!
	Resolved, That we, the members of the Fourth Ward
Relief Society, recognize in the death of our beloved and
honored President the great loss of an able and efficient
officer, a very devoted Saint and co-laborer in the cause
of truth.
	Resolved, That we condole and deeply sympathize
with her bereaved husband and family in the loss of a
kind and affectionate wife and mother.
	Resolved, That we cherish her memory and regard her
virtues as ever worthy of emulation.
	Resolved, That these Resolutions be entered in the
Relief Society record; also that copies be presented  to 
the bereaved family and printed in the Territorial En-
quirer and WOMAN'S EXPONENT.
	M. M. WATSON,
	D. M, MEECHAM,	Committee. 
	A. SMART, 	
Those who have lived and suffered
	Within the Gospel pale
Ne’er feel such hopeless pain for those
	Who drift behind the veil.
We loved Maria for her worth,  
	Her meek and gentle ways;
We loved her for her willingness
	To serve God all her days.
Sweet soul! too pure to longer dwell
	Beneath the chastening rod;	
Although we mourn, the whisper comes,
	“She’s gone to dwell with God.’


[first column]
	DAVID JAMES & CO.,
