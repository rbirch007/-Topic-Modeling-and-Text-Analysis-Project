			EDITORIAL NOTES.

	THERE will be a rally of the People's Party in
this city on Monday evening, Nov. 3rd, at 7
o'clock, in the theatre in this city.

[Column 2]
	SISTERS M. I. Horne and H. M. Whitney at-
tended the Quarterly Conference of the Relief
Society of Davis Stake, held at East Bountiful,
Oct. 27.
	The Y. L. M. I. A. of the Eighth Ward had
an interesting time on Wednesday, the 29th. A
meeting and sociable to which many guests
were invited. These re-unions are calculated to
create a good feeling and promote harmony in
the work of improvement.
	We gratefully acknowledge a beautiful bou-
quet of choice late flowers, which came like a
sunbeam, from Mrs. Jane Holt of East Boun-
tiful; also some pretty, sweet-scented wild
flowers and gaily colored Autumn leaves, from
little Caroline Crocheron.
	THE semi-monthly meeting of the Senior Re-
trenchment Association was held in the Four-
teenth Ward Assembly Rooms, Oct. 18th, Coun.
S. M. Kimball presiding. The meeting was
well attended and the remarks and addresses by
the ladies were very interesting, so we are in-
formed, but as we were not able to attend and
have not seen the minutes, we cannot give a
proper report to our readers.
	Miss Mabel Sherman Crawford, who vis-
ited this city recently, spent a morning in our
office, and we had a most interesting interview.
She is an ardent advocate of woman's suffrage,
and is one of the leading women of the suffrage
party in England. She has landed estates in
Ireland, and gave us considerable information
upon the condition of the people in the Emer-
ald Isle. She is liberal and philanthropical in
her character and views, in a brilliant conversa-
tionalist and has traveled extensively.
	A MEETING of the People's Party was held at
Provo on the evening of Oct. 29th. The brass
band met the delegate and his party at the
depot and escorted them to the place of meeting,
where a large crowd were waiting. Opening re-
marks by Judge Dusenberry; John C Graham,
Chairman. Speeches by Hon. John T. Caine,
and James Sharp. The meeting was an en-
thusiastic one, and will, undoubtedly, be pro-
ductive of good, and induce many who are dila-
tory to turn out and vote at the coming election.
	"The Golden Gems of Life," or "Gathered
Jewels for the Home Circle," is the title of a
book now being canvassed for by Mrs. S. M.
White of this city. The book contaius 608
pages, is printed on tinted paper in large, clear
type and handsomely bound. the subjects are
such as recommend the book even before read-
ing it, and the essays, for such they seem to be,
are well written, simple in language and clear
in style, and adapted for home life. The sub-
jests first treated upon are Life, Home, Home
Circle, Father and Mother, Infancy Childhood,
Brother and Sister, Manhood, Womanhood, and
so on. It is decidedly a very good book, and one
from which much may be learned, and it ought
to have a wide circulation. The Editors are S. C.
Ferguson and E. H. Allen, Cincinnati, Ohio.
Morocco and gilt binding, $5.75; silver finish
cloth, $4.75; half morocco, $4.50; green cloth $3.75.
   Mrs. Emimy Pfeiffer, poet and author,
lately visited Salt Lake accompanied by her
husband. They remained about four days here,
seeing the places of interest. Mr. and Mrs.
Pfeiffer are both interested in woman's suffrage,
and the lady has written several articles as well
as poems upon the subject, that have attracted
some attention and been the subject of consider-
able comment in London society. Mrs Pfeiffer
writes for "The Spectator" and "Contemporary
Review." Her books have all gone through two
and some through three editions. The titles
are, "Gerard Monument," "Poems," "Quarter-
mass Grace," "Glan Alaeck," "Under the As-


pens,” “The Rhyme of the Lady of the Rock,”
a poem of two hundred.'and fifty pages—
“Sonnets and Songs.”  Mrs. Pfeiffer is much in-
terested in the subject of dress reform, enter-
taining the view that the, present style of dress
Is neither healthful nor graceful.  She was much
impressed with Sister Eliza R Snow Smith;
thought her a most remarkable character, and
expressed her admiration of the woman and her
wonderful work.
