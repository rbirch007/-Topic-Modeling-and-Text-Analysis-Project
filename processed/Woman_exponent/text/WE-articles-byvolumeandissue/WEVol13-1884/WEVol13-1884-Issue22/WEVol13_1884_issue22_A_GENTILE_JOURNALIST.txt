A GENTILE JOURNALIST.
				  ------
MRS. H. M. WHITNEY:
   Yours of the 13th, the papers, etc, at hand. I
deemed your letter of sufficient interest to
warrant publication, and therefore sent it to
Colonol C. T. Nixon, the editor of one of our
popular journals, and it will appear  week. I
trust the act will meet your approbation.
  I am now in an adjoining county, but will re-


turn home in a few days, and will write you at
greater length, and perhaps refer briefly to the
marked articles in the News and Herald. Any
papers or written messages from you will always
receive due attention, and will be used in the in-
terest of your people, who seem, just at this
time, to need the sympathy and assistance of
every person who possesses any conception of
honor and justice.  I am heartily disgusted at
the cruel and unjust course of our Government
towards the Mormons, and I should always op-
pose it to the bitter end.
			In haste, your friend,
							W. H. B.
