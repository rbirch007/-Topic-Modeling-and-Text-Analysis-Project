AN OVATION.
	A meeting of the -Young -Ladies’ and Primary
Associations of Juab; Stake convened in the
Nephi Relief Hall, March 23rd, 1885. The hall
was crowded, there baing representatives and
others present from the branch associations of
the stake.
	The gathering was in honor of Sister Hannah
Grover' sixty second birthday; also to express
the appreciation and gratitude for her labors, for
Sister Grover has been connected with the presi-
dency of the Y. L. and Primaries for a period of
ten years, being placed to act as superintendent
for the Y. L. at its first organization by Sister
Eliza, after which she was called to act as Stake
President over the Primaries, and acting at the
same time as First Counselor to the Stake Presi-
dent of Y. L. M I. A.
	The meeting commenced by singing. Prayer
was offered by Sister Grover. Singing. Prest.
M. A. Caz er mtde the opening remarks,followed
by an address from Prest. M. E. Teasdale, who,
In behalf of the Y. L. and Primaries, presented
Sister Grover with two beautiful presents, a
photograph aud autograph album. She ex-
pressed the regrets felt by all in having to part
with Sister Grover, for she had won the respect
and esteem of all with whom she ahd been asso-
ciated,
	Sister Grover was much affected when she
arose to -thank her friends for their tokens of
remembrance and regard shown her.
	The remaining time was occupied by short ad-
dresses from the ladies, and songs, and recitations
principally from the children. This enjoyable
meeting was closed by singing "Love at Home."
Benediction by M. E. Teasdale.
