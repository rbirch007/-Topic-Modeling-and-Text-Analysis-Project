BY RUTHINDA E. MONCH.

O, feed my lambs, the Savior said,
In ages past and gone,
To Peter, whom He dearly loved
And placed this trust upon;
Take care of them, the little ones,
And feed them day by day
On precious truths, eternal truths,
That none may go astray.
Yes, give them sips, refreshing sips,
From fountains from above,
And bind them to the laws of God
By silken cords of love;
Of such as these, the "little ones,"
The kingdom yet will be,
Around the throne of God they dwell
In peace and harmony;
The children then in robes so fair,
Or in their tattered gown
Were taken in His arms and blessed--
On none did Jesus frown.
His heart was filled with love for them,
And were they rich or poor,
The charge to Peter was the same--
To feed them ever more.


[Column 1]
And did the Savior of the world
Notice the children then?
Oh! yes, for well He knew the boys
Would shortly be the men;	
And were they trained in righteousness
Most noble ones they’d be,	
And Peter was the chosen one 	
To teach them purity,
Now thou art called as Peter was
The little ones to train
In paths of virtue, truth and love,
Thy mission's much the same.
So, sister, may the grace of God
Henceforth your work attend,	
That Zion’s youth may rise in power
And Zion’s cause defend, 
That when He comes on earth to reign 
He’ll find a noble band,
Worthy in thought; in word and deed
To stand at His right hand.

	We desire to present you with a slight
tribute and testimonial of our love and respect
for you, and of our appreciation of your valu-
able services for the benefit and building up of
these associations, and to thank you cordially
for your faithful labors in our behalf, hoping
and trusting that your good works and enno-
bling influence may continue,that you may be
a bright and shining light through many future
years of happiness and prosperity, and that
your pathway may be strewn with the blos-
soms of faith, hope, friendship and affection,
that will entwine in a fadeless wreath of im-
mortal flowers to bloom forever, while pleasant
memories of past associations will linger with
us in years to come, together with the bright
hopes that we shall all meet in a glorious re-
union beyond the shades of time; when we shall
see as we are seen, and know as we are known,
when the mists that obscure our vision her[-]
have rolled away beneath the rays of celestial
sun rise, when it enfolds the earth in its radi-
ant glory, while sweet echoes of songs of praise
from immortal lips float over the happy in-
mates of a world redeemed.
	
	You have labored well, dear sister,
		In the cause of love and truth,
	You have been a brave enlister
		To improve the minds of youth.

	Many years you’ve labored fruitful
		In the place you’ve now resigned,
	And your labors hav[-] been faithful,
		Bearing fruit each step you’ve climbed.

	[-]t were vain to wish you pleasure,
		Riches, beauty, fame of earth,
	God will meet you out your measure, 
		He will pay you for your worth.

	Diamonds, pearls and gold may glitter,
		Shedding beauty rich and rare;
	But pure gems of thought are fitter
		For God’s faithful ones to wear.

	We would wish thee all earth’s pleasures,
		All its beauty, wealth and dower,
	But we know for thee there’s treasures 
		Laid beyond man’s feeble power.

	Treasures to thy heart far dearer
		Than the richest earth can give,
	And thy labors draw thee nearer
		Every day white her[-] you live.

	Then we’ll but ask God to bless you
		With the crown you have won here—
	Of eternal wealth and honor,
		All the hopes you hold most dear.

	Still[-]progressing may you ever
		Keep in view the noble aim,		
	Ever trusting, doubting never,
		Till eternal rest you gain.
