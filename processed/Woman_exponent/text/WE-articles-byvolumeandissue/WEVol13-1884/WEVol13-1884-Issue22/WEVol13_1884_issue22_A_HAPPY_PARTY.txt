A HAPPY PARTY.
			       -----
   As the President of the Y. L. M. I. A. is in-
intending to leave the Eighth Ward, the officers
and members gave her a pleasant surprise, Fri-
day evening, April 3rd, in the meeting house of
that Ward.  It was in the form of a picnic and
meeting.  After meeting the Secretary present-
ed her with a nice center table and handsome
cover, in behalf of the Association, showing
how her actions have endeared her to us.  We
all feel sorry to lose our President.  As it was a
complete surprise to her, she could only ex-
press her thanks and good wishes.
    Our Association being in a very good condition
we intend to hold our meetings regularly until
we have another President chosen.  We meet
every week.
			S. H. HAWKINS, Secretary.
