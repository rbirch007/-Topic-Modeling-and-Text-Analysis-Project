		M. A. HARDY.

	It now becomes our painful duty to announce the
death of our beloved associate, Sister Sarah Mariah
Wadsworth, who departed this life March 5th, 1885,
after a brief illness of twenty-four hours. She was the
daughter of Thomas S. and Eliza J. Terry, born at
Hebron, Washington Co., September 13th, 1865, and
was married to George William Wadsworth November
16th, 1882.
	She was Second Counselor in the Young Ladies' Mu-
tual Improvement Association of Panacca, which posi-
tion she faithfully filled for one year. She was one of
the most amiable of her sex, and was beloved by all
who had the pleasure of her acquaintance.
	The deceased was a loving wife and mother. She
leaves a husband and two children, the youngest but
one day old, and a host of friends who deeply mourn
her loss.
	Resolutions of Respect to the memory of Sister
Sarah Mariah Wadsworth, by the Young Ladies' Mutual
Improvement Association of Panaca.
	Whereas, It has pleased our Heavenly Father to take
from our midst by the hand of death our beloved sis-
ter, we feel that we have lost a faithful and worthy
member of the Young Ladies' Mutual Improvement
Association.
	Resolved, That we esteem her past faithful labors and
great devotion to duty as worthy the fellowship and
highest respect of us all.
	Resolved, That we deeply sympathize with the be-
reaved family in the loss of a kind wife and affectionate
mother.
	Resolved, That these Resolutions be spread upon our
records, and a copy be presented to the bereaved family,
also the WOMAN'S EXPONENT for publication.
		MARY JANE LEE, President,
		CHRISSIE ZYPUS, Secretary.
