	THE DEATH OF HER PET DOG “BEAUTY.”

“Beauty,” of dogmatic race,
	Thy mistress weeps thy doom[-]
That cruel death should thus displace
	Thy loving form so soon.

Thou wert dogmatic in thy love,
	Devoted, fond and true;
And all her acts did fully prove
	She estimated you.

I pray another dog may find
	A niche within her heart,
As faithful, loving and as kind
As him she’s seen depart.

I own he was a pretty dog
	As e’er a dog could be,
One fit to hold in memory’s “log,”
	A cenotaph for thee.

But yet I own my love goes o[-]t
	Never to dog or cat:
I like to see them run about
	The house and grounds, “and that,”

But give me ever human love,
	Intelligent and kind,
Inflated richly from above,
	And interchange of mind.

The dogs I leave without the wall,
	Where Scripture says they’ll be,
When that grand order rests on all—
Eternal entity!

“Beauty” is still upon the earth,
	Where’er you turn your eyes;
Beauty has daily, hourly birth
	For votive sacrifice:

Then, dearest lady, look around
	And dry those eyes of thine,
For love like thine is bliss when found
	By those your sea[-] says—Mine
		HANNAH T. KING.
Salt Lake City.
