RESOLUTIONS.

	Whereas, It becomes our duty to part with
and accept the resignation of our esteemed
friend, Mrs. Moody, who has acted as President
of the Thatcher and Layton Wards during the
past year, and who feels the task too great to
do justice to both places, in her stead have
been placed Mrs. Layton, as President, Mrs.
Monteath and Mrs. Madsen, as Counselors.
	Resolved, That we, the sisters of the Lay-
ton Ward, esteem her as a good president and
wise counselor, and that we will ever appreciate
her kindness, and endeavor to practice her
good teachings. We are pleased to present to
her a beautiful picture of our much loved
Prophet and Patriarch Joseph and Hyrum
Smith. And now, dear editor, if you will be
patient with us for a few moments and follow
us in your mind's eye we will escort her to a
neighboring house,and when the door is opened
judge of her surprise and pleasure at beholding
a table spread with the good things of the land,
among which can be seen a dish of nice home
produced honey-comb, which, in this desert
land, is not only very pleasing to the eye, but
to the taste also.
	We will leave them to enjoy the surprise,
with a bevy of beautiful girls to wait on them.
		MRS. ROXANA WALKER,
		MRS. NANCY PEEL,
		MRS. HARRIET WARNER,
		MRS. ANNIE PACKER.
	Layton, Arizona, April 2nd, 1885.
