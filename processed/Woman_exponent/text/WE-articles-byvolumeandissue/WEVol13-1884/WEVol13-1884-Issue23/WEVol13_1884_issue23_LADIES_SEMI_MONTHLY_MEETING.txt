LADIES' SEMI-MONTHLY MEETING.

	The regular semi-monthly meeting was held
in the Fourteenth Ward Assembly Rooms, on
Saturday, April 17th, at 2 p.m., as usual, Mrs.
M. I. Horne presiding. Singing. Prayer by
Counselor B. W. Smith. Then singing. Minutes
of previous meeting read and approved; also
minutes from the P. A. of the 21st, 15th and
16th Wards.
	Remarks by President Horne, who expressed
her regret that there were no minutes of the Y.
L. M. I. A. Referred to the trials which the
Saints are now experiencing, and the necessity
of keeping the commandments of God. Ad-
vised the sisters to exercise charity and bear
with each others faults and shortcomings, and
be comforters to all. Be sure and obey counsel,
as it was the only path of safety.
	President Horne called upon the Presidents
of the several branches of the Relief Society
present to express themselves, beginning with
Sister M. Chase of the First Ward, who had just
recently returned from Vermont, where she had
been for some months on a visit to her relatives.
	Sister Chase made a few remarks; said she
had been absent one year. Gave a brief account
of her visit, and expressed her desire to be faith-
ful and true to God and the principles of the
Gospel.
	Remarks calculated to strengthen and en-
courage the sisters were made by Sisters Rhoda
Fullmer, Jane Lawson and Sinah Bishop; the
last named recited a poem of her own composi-
tion. Sisters Player and Louisa Horne made a
few remarks, and one or two other sisters. Dr.
E. S. Barney spoke a short time, and endorsed
the remarks which had been made by the sis-
ters.
	Sisters Norman and Watmough bore their
testimonies, and then President Horne made


concluding remarks, exhorting the sisters to be
wise and obedient, and remember all clouds have
a silver lining. Meeting adjourned for two weeks.
Singing, "Wake, O wake, the world from sleep-
ing!" etc. Benediction by Sister E.S. Taylor.
