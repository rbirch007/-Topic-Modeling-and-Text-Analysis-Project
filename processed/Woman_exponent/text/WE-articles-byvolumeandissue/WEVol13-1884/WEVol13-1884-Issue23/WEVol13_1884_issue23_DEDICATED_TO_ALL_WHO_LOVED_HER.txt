DEDICATED TO ALL WHO LOVED HER.

Adorn the dead with robes of white,
	Symbol of purity and peace,
With anthems sweet, and flowers most bright,
	Proclaim our sister's glad release.

A radiant rose, without a thorn;
	A type of queenly beauty rare—
I knew her in life's early morn,
	When all her future looked so fair;

Yet, clouded oft her days have been;
	A long and lonely path she trod;
Nor doubt we that she learned to lean
	For strength upon the Lord, her God.

No more for her such pain and strife,
	Conflicting fears, and hopes defer'd
The clashing cares of mortal life,
	By which the tenderest hearts are stirred.

No more for her the daily cross,
	That God's peculiar people bear—
The world's contempt, who count as dross—
	E'en Zion's daughters pure and fair.


A vile opprobrious outcast name,
	The royal badge of Saint's indeed;
A heritage of scorn and blame—
	This dower "the Christ" His own bequeath'd

Children of Zion, need ye weep
	For one who honored heaven's behests?
Nay, for a record angels keep
	Of all who stand the fiery test.

What if the world's reproach must be
	The need of all who're faithful found—
The just shall wake to ecstasy
	Soon as "the quick'ning trump" shall sound.

Rejoice! for with the dead 'tis well,
	Who nobly lived and faithful died;
Rejoice! that in God's crucible
	Even as gold the best are tried.

Heaven help us to be true and pure,
	To keep "the faith" what e'er befall,
And for the crown the cross endure,
	As did the mighty Lord of all.
