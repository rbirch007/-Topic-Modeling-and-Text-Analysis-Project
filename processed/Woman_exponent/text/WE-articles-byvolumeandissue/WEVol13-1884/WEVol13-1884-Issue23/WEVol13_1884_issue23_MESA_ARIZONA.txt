MESA, ARIZONA.
	Our Relief Society Stake Conference was
held to-day in the Relief Society Hall, in Mesa,
President E. G. McDonald presiding. After
the usual opening exercises a report of the
various branches was given, showing consider-
able improvement in good works since our last
conference. Quite a number of the sisters
were called upon, who cheerfully responded,
encouraging all to work works of righteous-
ness.
	Prest. E. G. McDonald then instructed the
conference in their duties as teachers, mothers
and Saints, after which the statistical and finan-
cial reports were read.
	Prest. Chas. I. Robson, Bishops Pomeroy and
O. M. Stewart and Elder J. M. Lewis were
called upon, all of whom encouraged the sis-
ters in their work of charity.
	The subject of the silk industry was dwelt
upon, which was of great importance to us, as
a number of the sisters propose engaging in
that pleasant and profitable labor this season.
	An excellent spirit prevailed during our
conference, and all felt well paid for attending.
		ADDIE PASSEY, Secretary.
