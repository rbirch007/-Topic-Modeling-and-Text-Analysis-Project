ORDER.

	'Tis a good theme, Heaven's first law, and
one which I delight in. Where'er it reigns
there's power; absent, chaos prevails. The toil-
some work is ended, eve of the day of rest is here;
on bended knee we thank the Lord for mercies
given in the past week, for bounties on us bes-
towed by His all-bounteous hand; all needful du
ties performed; procrastination is not thought
of; needful rest is sought, and all is left in "or-
der."
	The Sabbath morn is ushered in; at early
hour the nightly couch is left; prayer offered; all
things found at hand. The product of the pre-
vious evening, delight steals o'er the senses, for
it is felt that all is order. 'Tis getting church
time, and the hour has come for starting; the
spot is reached; the usual seat is gained and
taken, there to sit with thoughts collected and
wait the arrival of God's servants. Bowing in
humble adoration before the author of our being
we meet and join in songs of praise and offer
prayer to Him for mercies had since last we
met, and ask, in faith, our Heavenly Father to
bestow on us more mercies in the coming week.
	The everlasting Gospel is restored in all its
beauty, the holy priesthood is again on earth,
with prophets, seers, and revelators, through
whom the loving counsels of the great "I am"
are sent to His straying, erring children. The
end of time approaches, the six day's work is
nearly done, the great millennial day of rest is
near, eternity is before us.
	Oh! that vast word, "eternity," who can grasp
it? Our finite minds quail before it, for 'tis [---]


measurable. In the beginning all was "order."
The end of time will be in order. Order will
reign throughout "eternity," for it is God's law.
