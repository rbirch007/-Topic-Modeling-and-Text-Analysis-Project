EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We were organized as a Relief Society of
Mormon Ward on September 3rd, 1881, Sister
Sarah S. Richards, President; Ann Hale and
Anna Folkmann, Counselors. Since that time
we have done our best offering our mite wher-
ever it was most needed; we have donated to
the Logan Temple, also to the Deseret Hospi-
tal. We have some wheat on hand, and the
sisters all have a good spirit and try to go to
meeting as often as possible, though the people
are so scattered, and the men at present are so
busy with their summer's work, it is impossible
for the sisters to attend regularly. Apostle Mo-
ses Thatcher, Bro. Wm. Hendricks and Bro.
Parkinson held two meetings here last week,
giving the people some very good advice; they
appointed Bro. John B. Thatcher, Bishop, Alma
Hale and Arthur D. Young, Counselors, our
former Bishop, Solomon H. Hale being promo-
ted to the office of First Counselor to the Pres-
ident of the Idaho Stake.
	Our crops are looking well, and although
times are unusually dull, and money scarce, yet
we anticipate more prosperous times in the fu-
ture, and hope for the blessings that seem to
follow the saints generally.
	I will close, wishing you every success.
				MELLIE PECK, Secretary.
