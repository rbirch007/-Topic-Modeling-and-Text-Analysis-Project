TRUST IN GOD
O God above in whom I trust
	And worship day by day
Incline thine ear and hear my woe-
	Thou art my only stay;
For friends, though counted by the score,
	Are few when sorrows come,
And though they dwell anear my door,
	As well a thousand miles from home.

"So near, and yet so far!" I've heard it oft,
	But never realized before
The truth of it, but now I know
	The deep, full meaning of the term,
O Father!  Thou whom I adore,
	And in my weak and humble way
Desire to learn to know Thee well,
	Desire to live but to obey.

They "still small voice" still speaks to me,
	And bids me in the darkest hour
Call unto Thee in Jesus name
	For comfort, and Thou hast the power
To cheer and comfort on the way
	The weary, wounded, breaking heart,
But not to take the thorns away,
	For each must bravely bear his part.

They *servant to Thy Saints hath said,
	Call on the Lord with faith in prayer;
When in thy secret closet kneel,
	Ask of thy God thy heart's desire.
Oh precious promise!  priceless boon!
	To ask of God, and He will hear.
And hearing answer to our call;
	He seems so far, and yet is near.

"Nearer, my God, to Thee," I cry,
	Nearer to Thy throne I come;
Thinner the veil that hides from me
	The glories of my futnre home;
But ere I leave this mortal sphere
	My earthly task I would complete;
Oh! give me strength, and grace, and power
	To lay my faults beneath my feet.

When foes without, and foes within
	Combine, They people to enslave,
The weakness of the flesh would cry,
	Oh, give us rest within the grave!
O Father, heal our bleeding hearts,
	Help us to praise Thee while we've breath,
Help us to nobly bear our part,
	Oh, give us liberty, or death!

Thy will, O Lord, not mine be done,
	Oh! give me strength and grace to say,
As said Thy well beloved Son,
	Upon that sad and solemn day,
When weak as other mortals are,
	In deepest anguish cried to thee,
If possible, the bitter cup,
	O Father! let it pass from me.

And though He plead in angnish sore,
	And could "command a heavenly host,"
And though He bled at every pore
	God's Son could not desert His post;
Then meekly bowed His head in prayer,
	Drank of the cup with faith divine,
Crying, they know not what they do;
	Father, forgive!  Thy will not mine.

Can we, the children of our God,
	The brothers, sisters of His son,
Do less than give our lives to Him?
	Eternal lives we then have won.
There is no "Royal road to heacen;"
	The same rough road our Brother trod
We all must tread.  No pattern's given
	Save Jesus Christ, the Son of God.
									RUTH.

June 1st, 1884.
*President W. Woodruff
