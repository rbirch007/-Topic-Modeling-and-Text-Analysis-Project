EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The Y. L. M. I. A. of the First Ward of
Provo is in a flourishing condition. There are
seventy names enrolled as members. Average
attendance between thirty and forty. We have
a paper called the Diadem once in two weeks,
which the young ladies write pieces for. Our
President, Pauline Hardy, is always punctual
at her meetings, and tries to make them inter-
esting as well as instructive; in fact all the
young ladies are up and awake to their duties,
and bear faithful testimonies to this great work
in which we, as children of God, are engaged.
	In financial affairs we are not behind the
other associations; we have donated $50 to the
Stake Tabernacle, also some to the Salt Lake
Temple and Deseret Hospital, and are now go-
ing to help build the Brigham Young Acad-
emy by donation. The EXPONENT comes regu-
larly to hand, and we are always happy to re-
ceive it.
	Ever praying for the success of the EXPON-
ENT and the best interests of Zion, I remain,
		Your sister in the Gospel,
			MARY STEWART, Secretary.
