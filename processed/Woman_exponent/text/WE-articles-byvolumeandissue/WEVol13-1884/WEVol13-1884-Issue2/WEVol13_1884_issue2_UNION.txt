UNION.

	Union is something that ought to be cultiva-
ted and exist in every settlement of the Latter-
day Saints. And without it we cannot accom-
plish much good. We ought to try and be
united with each other as much as possible.
The Lord says, "Unless ye are one ye are not
mine." By that it seems that we must unite
or He will reject us. And without union our
meetings cannot be interesting. Then let us be
united in our meetings as well as in other
things. It seems that nothing worthy can be
accomplished without union. Without united
effort this stream of water that flows close by
cannot be controlled; one man's efforts would
be useless to control it, but the united efforts of
twenty or thirty have once controlled it, and
they will do it again.
	One grain of powder is harmless, but when a
barrel explodes it will tear the rocks to pieces.
Think of the great damage that was caused
by the united effect of the explosion of many
tons of powder in the magazine at Salt Lake
City. Who has not heard of the Indian chief
who, when on his dying bed, took a single ar-
row and broke it easily, and then said to his
sons, This is the way you will be broken after
your father is dead if you single out and di-
vide yourselves! And then he took a bunch of
arrows and told one of his sons to break them,
but be conld not; the father then said, This is
the way you will be if you all stay together
and remain as one; you cannot be broken.
Take a single match and light it and it is no-
thing, but unite it with other flames and its
power is wonderful. It will destroy property
of every kind. And where there is union al-
most anything can be accomplished.
