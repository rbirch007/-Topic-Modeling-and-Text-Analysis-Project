AN INTERESTING BOOK.

	Jos. H. Parry & Co. have just published the
third edition of "History and Philosophy of
Marriage, or Polygamy and Monogamy Com-
pared." The book is from the pen of a "Chris-
tian Philanthropist," who, having been strictly
brought up in New England, resided for many
years abroad in foreign countries, and had the
best opportunities of seeing and comprehending
the subjects and practices of which he has so
ably written.
	The careful reading of this book cannot offend
the most delicate or sensitive student of human
nature, or enquirers into the laws of cause and
effect. It is a work of 274 pages, and is written
in a purely conscientious and high, moral tone.
The late publishers have had the copyright
transferred to them, and have issued an edition
of five hundred. At the present time it should
be in every household of the Saints, that they
may have the opinions and testimony of men of
the highest intelligence in this enlighteded
nineteenth century for reference upon a princi-
ple which now involves so much, in the presen


crusade against the people called "Mormons"
	The book contains many historical and philo-
sophical facts with which every individual, who
is interested in the search of truth, should be in
possession and familiar with; that one may
reason intelligently upon vital subjects it is
necessary to be well armed with powerful argu-
ments, facts and figures. Every library should
contain one of these books. Price, $1, for sale
at Jos. Hyrum Parry's book store, Main Street,
opposite Z. C. M. I.
