	FOR THE PRIMARIES.
My Lord!  my glorious Shepherd--King!
	My wants He doth supply--
In pastures green He laid me down,
	Where gentle streams ran by.

He bindeth up my wounded soul,
	He guides my wandering feet,
In paths of righteousness He leads
	To happiness complete.

Yea, though death's shadow oft has bowed
My shrinking soul to ill,
Into his valley dark and drear,
Yet Oh! no evil will I fear,
For thou art with me still;
Thy rod I hold, Thy staff I grasp,
And they shall comfort me.

A table in the wilderness
	For me has oft been spread,
Where I, with loving guests, have sat,
	And all been richly fed.

Yes, truly this has often been
	In presence of my foes--
My head Thou hast anointed, too,
	My cup--it overflows! 

Surely Thy goodness and Thy love
	Shall tend me all my days,
And in the house of God I'll dwell
	With everlasting praise.
					HANNAH T. KING.
	March 25th, 1885.
