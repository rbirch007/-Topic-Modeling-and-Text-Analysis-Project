OBITUARIES.

	DIED, at her residence, in Wilson Ward, Weber Co.,
Utah, January 26th, 1883, aged fifty-three years, ten
months and five days, of dropsy, Pettra Nellie Nielson,
beloved wife of L. H. Nielson, born in Sweden—bap-
tized into the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day
Saints March, 1856; has passed through persecutions and
imprisonment in the old country for the Gospel's sake,
also many trying scenes in the Church and while cross-
ing the plains, but has ever been true and faithful,
never doubting the truth of the Gospel.
RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.
	We, the members of the Wilson Ward Relief Society,
of Weber Co., Utah, unanimously adopt the following
Resolutions:
	Whereas, God in His wisdom has seen fit to call from
our midst by the hand of death our much loved and
worthy teacher, and,
	Whereas, Her labor in our Society has been faithfully
performed, we feel that some respect due her virtues be
recognized among us, therefore, be it
	Resolved, That we do recognize in the death of our
sister, Pettra Nellie Nielson, the loss of an able, effic-
ient and faithful teacher in our Society.
	Resolved, That we esteem her past faithful labors and
devotion to duty as worthy the fellowship and emula-
tion of us all.
	Resolved, That we condole and deeply sympathize
with the bereaved husband, children, relatives and
friends, in the loss of a loving wife, affectionate mother
and true friend.
	Resolved, That these Resolutions be spread upon our
record, that a copy of them be sent to the bereaved
husband, and also that a copy be sent to the WOMAN'S
EXPONENT.
	GENET BINGHAM, President,
	AMANDA M. BINGHAM, Secretray.
