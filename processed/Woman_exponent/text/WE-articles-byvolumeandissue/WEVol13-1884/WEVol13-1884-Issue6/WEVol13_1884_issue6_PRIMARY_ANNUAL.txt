PRIMARY ANNUAL.
[line break]
	On the 15th of July, in company of Presi-
dents Louie Felt and Ellen Clawson, we attend-
ed an annual meeting of the Primary Associa-
tion of Sugar House Ward.  The meeting was
conducted by the President, Mrs. McGhie.
	The exercises of the children were very cred-
itably rendered, exhibiting the effect of careful
training.  We noticed a marked improvement
from one year ago, in a large increase. In num-
bers, and also in the very excellent order of the
children.  One highly pleasing feature in the
exercises was extempore speeches by s[-]ven or
eight of the boys in succession.  We felt in-
tensely interested in all of the exercises of the
girls as well as the boys, considering them, as
we do, true indications of future usefulne[--].
	It was plainly manifest that their President
and her Counselors have won the hearts of those
children, which is the key to success in mould-
ing and guiding in the right direction.  Love is
the ruling power in these associations.
	The children invited us to join in a picnic af-
ter the close of services; and with promptitude
tables were bountifully spread for aged, middle-
aged and every child, of which all partook in so-
cial order.	E.R.S.S.
