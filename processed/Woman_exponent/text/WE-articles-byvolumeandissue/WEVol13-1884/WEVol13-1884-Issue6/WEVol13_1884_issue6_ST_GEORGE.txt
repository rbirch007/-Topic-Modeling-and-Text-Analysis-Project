ST. GEORGE.

	The Relief Society of the St. George Stake
of Zion held their Quarterly Conference at the
Lyceum Hall, St. George, on Friday, June
13th. There was not so large an attendance
as usual, owing, probably, to the unfavorable
condition of the roads for traveling, from the
unusual amount of rain which has fallen this
spring, but a good spirit prevailed. The re-
ports read were satisfactory, showing an in-
crease in members, and a general feeling and
desire to improve in all things, and to attend
to the admonitions and carry out the instruc-
tions that are given.

[Column 2]
	Apostle Erastus Snow in his address re-
marked that the sisters had done remarkably
well in the way they had observed the word
of wisdom, and exhorted them to continue
faithful in all their labors, and they would not
lose their reward.
	Elder Henry Eyring encouraged the sisters
to persevere in their efforts to raise silk, as it
would no doubt become a lucrative employ-
ment, as silk was now being raised in Amer-
ica instead of being imported from France and
China.
	The Young Ladies' and Primary Associa-
tions are doing a good work. The minds of
the young are expanding, and in very many
cases they seem able to grasp the truths of the
Goepel and comprehend its first principles.
	May the Lord shower down his blessings
upon those who organized, and those who sus-
tain those institutions.
					M. A. G. B, Secretary.
