		MANTI, SOUTH WARD.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
     The Relief Society of this ward was organ-
ized February 2nd, 1882, with Sister Eliza-
beth Casto as President, Sisters Maria Jenson
and Sarah Jane Lowry, Counselors, who are
exemplary women, with the Gospel at the bot-
tom of their hearts, and ever ready to strength-
en the weak. They assist the bishop to take
care of the poor and lend a helping hand to
the sick and afflicted. We have also a num-
ber of good sisters who are willing to act as
teachers and we have no trouble to get them.
They all realize that it is a noble mission in
which they are engaged. We have quite a
number of poor and aged in this ward who
are looking for our charity, and to my knowl-
edge none are suffering for the real necessities
of life. Our means is increasing, therefore we
acknowledge the blessings of our Heavenly
Father.
	May every Relief Society throughout the
land of Zion prosper, and fulfill the charitable
duties devolving upon all, and let wisdom be
one of the counselors in each is my sincere
prayer.
		Your sister in the Gospel,
							A. R. K.
