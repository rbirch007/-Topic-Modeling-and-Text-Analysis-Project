SENIOR RETRENCHMENT MEETING.
				_____

	The Ladies' regular semi-monthly meeting
was held in the Fourteenth Ward Assembly
Rooms on Saturday, August 9, commencing at
2 p.m., Counselor Zina D. H. Young presiding.
After singing prayer was offered by Mrs. Mary
A. Freeze, and Sisters Howard, Horne and
Kimball prayed for, that they might be speed-
ily restored to health. Minutes of previous
meeting were read and approved, and the report
of an annual meeting of the four Primary As-
sociations of Mill Creek was read by the Secre-
tary. Sister Zina was the first to address the
meeting; she dwelt eloquently upon the bless-
ings and good influences experienced while en-
gaged in working in the Temple at Logan, also
of her visits to the Associations and among the
people in that vicinity, and made many very
excellent remarks upon the order and purposes
of our lives here upon the earth.
	Sisters E. B. Wells and E. S. Taylor made
some suggestions to the sisters on matters per-
taining to their particular labors, and alluded to
the need of improvement in spiritual intelli-
gence, and so forth. After which Sister Zina
called upon the sisters in the congregation to ex-
press themselves.
	Sisters Watmough, Paul, Freeze, Bird and
Dyer each made some remarks; and Sister Dyer
spoke quite powerfully in the gift of tongues;
Sister Elizabeth Paul was called upon by Sister
Zina and gave the interpretation. Miss Emma
Finch made a few very touching remarks upon
the subject of healing in a very impressive man-
ner.
	Sung Doxology.  Benediction by Miss Alice
Jenkins, in which two of the sisters who were
sick were earnestly remembered before the
Lord.
