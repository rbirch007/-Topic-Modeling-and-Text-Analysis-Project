RICHMOND PRIMARY.

	Minutes of the Fifth Annual meeting of the
Richmond Primary Association, held June
2nd, 1884, President Mary Allsop presiding;
singing, "Little Children should love Jesus;"
prayer by Joseph R. Thompson, aged thirteen;
singing, We all have a work to do." Minutes
of last annual meeting read and accepted, also
the yearly report. The exercises consisted of
songs, recitations and dialogues rendered by
the children, which they did with great credit.
	Sister Jane E. Molen, Stake President, met
with us and gave good instructions. Short ad-
dresses were made by Sister Williams, Presi-
dent of the Smithfield Primary, Bishop Wm.
S. Skidmore, Sister Myra Merrill, President of
the Relief Society, and Sister Hannah T.
Brower.
	Meeting adjourned for one year. Singing,
"Always Cheerful." Benediction by the child-
ren in concert.
	Statistical report of the Primary Associa-
tion, beginning June 1st, 1883, and ending
June 1st. 1884:
	Number of meetings held, 44; Number of
members enrolled, 195; Average attendance,
95; Cash remaining in treasury from previous
year, $1.72; Cash received from members dur-
ing the year, $5.08; Cash received from Pri-
mary entertainment, $56.00; Total, $62.80; Dis-
bursements, Paid to the new meeting house,
$50.00; Expenses for Primary entertainment,
$1.25; Paid to Emigration Fund, $5.00; Paid
for cards, $4.70; for papers, $0.60; Total, $61.55;
Property on hand, $5.00; Cash on hand, $1.25.
				MARY A. TRAVELLER,
							Secretary.
