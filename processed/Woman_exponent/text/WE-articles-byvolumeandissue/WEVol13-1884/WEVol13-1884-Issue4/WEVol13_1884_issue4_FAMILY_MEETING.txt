FAMILY MEETING

	From Sister Harriet Hunsaker, of Honeyville,
we have heard the part'culars of a very pleasant
party, which convened at the residence of Sister
Catherine Beckstead, senior, of South Jordan,
on Monday, July 7th, to celebrate the anniver-
sary of her birth, which took place 77 years ago
in Canada. The party consisted almost entirely
of the aged lady's own immediate family; four
daughters met on that occasion who had been
separated for several years. The Bishop of the
ward and his wife were present, also Sister Ann
Holt, President of the Relief Society of South
Jordan. Among the guests sixteen great grand
children were present, and those were not
all the great grand children of the ven-
erable lady, who was the surprised recipi-
ent of the honors of the day. Over seventy
sat down to supper, and one can better imagine
than describe the joy and pleasure of the happy
meeting.
	Sister Beckstead has been the mother of fif-
teen children, ten are still living, and many
grand children as well as great grand children.
She has been a widow more than fourteen years.
Sister Beckstead was a "Mormon" in an early
day, and traveled up to Missouri about 1837;
shared in the persecutions in Caldwell and De
Witt Counties, afterwards came to Illinois, left
Nauvoo in 1846 and journeyed with the Saints
to Winter Quarters. Her eldest son, Gordon,
was a member of the famous Mormon Battalion,
Sister Beckstead came with her husband and
family to Utah in 1849, and settled in North
Canyon, afterwards moved to what was then
known as the Jordan, and subsequenty called
South Jordan. The aged lady is held in very
high esteem by all who are acquainted with her.
and her many friends will no doubt join with
her children and relatives in wishing her con-
tinued health, happiness and prosperity. Such
family reunions seem to strengthen the chords
of love and unity which bind together friends
and families, and make life more desirable and
enjoyable.
