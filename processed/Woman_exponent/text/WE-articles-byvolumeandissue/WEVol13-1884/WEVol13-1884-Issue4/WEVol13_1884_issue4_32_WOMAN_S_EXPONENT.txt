32	WOMAN'S EXPONENT.


Emerson once said to Mrs. Helen Jackson
("H. H.") that she stood on the threshold of a
great achievement. The large and increasing
circle of her readers are doubtless satisfied that
Mr. Emerson's prophecy was fulfilled years
ago; if any doubt remained, it would certainly
be removed by the beauty, pathos and power
of Mrs. Jackson's story "Ramona," no being
published in the Christian Union, 20 Lafayette
Place, New York. For dramatic interest, nar-
rative skill and deep feeling no story of recent
years has equalled it.—Ex.
