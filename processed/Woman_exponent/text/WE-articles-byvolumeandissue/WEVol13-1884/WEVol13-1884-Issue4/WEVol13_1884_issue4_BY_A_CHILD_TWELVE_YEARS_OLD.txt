BY A CHILD TWELVE YEARS OLD.

	The Fourth of July, or Independence Day. I
wonder how many of us know the meaning of
this day. It is the day that our forefathers de-
clared their independence from the mother
country. And we find by reading the Declara-
tion of Independence, how the people of Ameri-
ca were tyranized over by the king of Great
Britain. He would keep among them in times
of peace standing armies without the consent of
their legislature, and would deprive them, in
many cases, of the benefit of trial by jury. He
would have them taken over the sea to be tried
for pretended offenses, and he would also sus-
pend their own legislature, and claimed that he
had the right of making their laws, just as Gov-
ernor Murray would like to treat our legislature.
 So I would advise all my brothers and sisters to


become acquainted with the Constitution of our
country, which was framed by good men.
	That we may always be true to our country as
American born citizens, is the desire of your sis-
ter in the Primary Association.
							AGGIE LEWIS.
	Written for the "Primary Star," Spanish
Fork, June 28th., 1884.
