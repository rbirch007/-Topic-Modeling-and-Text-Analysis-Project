TEMPE.

Little children, do you know
Why upon this Christmas day,
In a month of storm and snow,
When rought winds the wildest blow,
Human hearts are light and gay?

Why with gift, and smile, and song,
From the dawning's early grey,
We the passing hours prolong,
Giving love, forgiving wrong,
Till the light has passed away?

Why the rich, with generous hand,
Give good gifts to those in need
And a spirit through the land
Bids each passing brother stand—
Giving each to each "God speed!"

Why the proud head boweth low,
And each heart grows tender when
Voices on the night air ring,
And glad choirs the anthem sing,
"Peace on earth, good will to men!"

Eighteen hundred years ago,
Waiting shepherds saw a star,
Leading with a steady glow
To a stable poor and low,
Drawing men's hearts from afar

To that low and lonely shed,
Where the cattle, gentle eyed,
All unconscious stood and fed,
While the precious moments sped,
And the gates of heaven thrown wide—

Saw the son, the prince of power,
Helpless as a fledgeling dove,
Lower in his natal hour
Than the serf with Slavery's dower,
He, the Lord of peace and love.

Thus the earth received her king,
This the welcome that they gave
One who brought salvation near,
Burst the bands of sin and fear,
Won the victory o'er the grave.

Little children, ye have read
How He lived, the truths He taught;
How He walked by Galilee;
How men loved with Him to be,
And their babes the mothers brought;

How He took them in His arms,
Blessed and spoke the sweet decree,
While each sould was touched and stirred,
And the people wond'ring heard,
"Let the children come to me."

Follow Him, each little one,
Follow to that upper fold;
When your life's great work is done,
When your earthly course is run,
May your names with His be [-]old.
