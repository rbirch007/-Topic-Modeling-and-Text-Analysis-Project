EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Thinking that a letter from an obscure sis-
ter like myself might be acceptable, I proceed
to pen a few lines.
	I often wonder what I am here for, when I
see so many of my sisters hard at work, either
trying to spread the Gospel or trying to defend
it so nobly. And then it seems as though I
am ashamed of myself for allowing such un-
grateful thoughts to enter my mind. We are
all here to fill up the measure of our creation
in one way or another. A little poem I once
read comes to my mind. "He Knoweth," be-
ing the title of the poem.

	"There are flow'rets in the valleys low,
		And over the mountain side.
	Which were never praised by human voice,
		Nor by human eyes descried.

	Yet as sweet as the breath of the royal rose,
		Is the perfume they exhale;
	And why they bloom, or where they bloom,
		The good Lord knoweth well."

	Yes, the good Lord knoweth well. There is
no need for any one to feel dejected or cast
down, for our Creator has ordained all things
in His own wise way.
	Last evening as I sat reading an aged sister
living near me came in to while away an hour
or two. I read to her out of the little EXPON-
ENT; you should have seen her face as it lighted
up with enthusiasm as I read to her "Mormon
Women," by Aunt Hannah; Aunt Em's
"Reverie," also that beautiful poem, entitled,
"A Christmas Gift," and others that were alike
interesting. As I read I could not but make
the comment, "The degraded women of Utah,
Ind[-]ed!" One has only to take up the little
EXPONENT if they want to see such cruel as-
persions contradicted. As the sweet perfume
of flowers that are wafted to us by some soft
summer breeze, calms and soothes our turbulent
spirits, so do the grand and lofty thoughts ex-
pressed in the EXPONENT by our sweet sisters,
lull and soothe all intelligent readers.
