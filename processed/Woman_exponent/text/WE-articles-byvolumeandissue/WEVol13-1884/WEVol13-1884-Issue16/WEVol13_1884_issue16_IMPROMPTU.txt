IMPROMPTU

	"She stretcheth out her hand to the poor; yea, she
reacheth forth her hand to the needy."—PROV., xxxl.

I pray that health and strength be given,
	And worldly wealth to thee;
For well I know the breath of heaven
To thee in zephyr waves are given,
	That blessed the poor may be.

Long may you live, dear friend of mine,
	To bless the drooping heart.
To shed the balm of love divine,
That widow's hearts may "rise and shine,"
	And feel  you life impart.

Yes, 'tis a blessed boon to give,
	And blessed to receive;
And poor indeed must be the soul
That cannot own the sweet control
	That cometh as a blest reprieve
To bid the dying lives.

May many happy years be thine
	On this our mother earth;
Thy children rise and call thee blest,
Thy husband's soul upon the rest,
Thy "works within the gates" exprest,
	Thy presnce scattering dearth,
The hand of friendship here relates—
"Thy works shall praise thee in the gates."
	HANNAH T. KING.
Salt Lake City, New Year's day, 1885.
