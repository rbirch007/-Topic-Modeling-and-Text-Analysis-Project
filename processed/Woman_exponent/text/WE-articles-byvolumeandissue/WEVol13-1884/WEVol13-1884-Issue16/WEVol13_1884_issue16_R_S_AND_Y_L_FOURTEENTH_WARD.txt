R. S. AND Y. L. FOURTEENTH WARD.

	The sisters regular semi monthly meeting
was held in the Fourteenth Ward Assembly
Rooms, Saturday, Jan. 10th, 1885, at 2 p.m,
Mrs. Ellen C. Clawson presiding. Singing, "We
thank thee, O God, for a prophet," etc. Prayer
by Mrs. Mary A. Freeze. Singing, 'Come let
us anew." Minutes of the previous meeting
read by the Secretary, Miss Alee Jenkins, and
approved. Minutes of Y. L. A. were then read
from the 20th Ward, and minutes of P. A. from
the 21st and 15th Wards.
	Mrs. Clawson then made a few opening re-
marks, and expressed her desire to hear each of
the sisters say a few words. Mrs. Freeze, Mrs.


Mc. Master, Mrs. St[-]yner, Mrs. H. C. Young,
each  made a few remarks, and the remainder of
the time was occupied by the sisters in the con-
gregation, many of whom bore strong testimon-
ies of the truth of the Gospel, and related items
of personal experience, all expressing a desire
to live faithful, to be dilligent in good works, and
to prove worthy of their high and holy mission
upon the earth.
	The Spirit of the Lord was plainly manifest,
and each one who spoke seemed desirious of be-
ginning the new year with a firm resolve to do
better and live nearer to God. Meeting ad-
journed for two weeks. Singing, “Lord dismiss
us with Thy blessing.” Prayer by a brother in
the congregation.	
	After the close of the meeting Mrs. E. B.
Wells read the letter which had been addressed
to her from the Woman's Department of the
World's Exposition.
