THE WOMAN'S EXPONENT

Like to a summer butterfly,
	Or sunshine on a winter's day,
Dear friend, you ever welcome arc—
	Welcome as first flowers of May.

You fill my heart with Christian love,
	Teach me a fuller trust in God;
Tell me all hear say tales to prove—
	Weigh and measure by God's own world. 

Dear EXPONENT—aye! exponent
	Of mankind's higher, truer life;
Of noble work, of sweet content
	In duty's path midst joy or strife.

Across prairies, o'er the plains,
	You come, dear friend, as heaven sent,
Sweet as their own wild flower's perfume;
	Welcome, thrice welcome, dear EXPONENT.
		EMILY SCOTT.
Ohio, Dec. 15, 1884.
