RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.
				[line break]
	The following are Resolutions of Respect to our young
and much beloved sister, Catherine Stanifird, daughter
of John H. Stanifird and Mary Argyle, born in Bounti-
ful, Davis Co., Utah.  December 3rd, 1868.  Died in
Apache Co., Arizona, August 6th, 1884.
	Whereas, God in His divine wisdom has seen proper
to call from our midst to a higher sphere, one of our
members, who was greatly beloved by us all for her
kind and gentle spirit, her pure and virtuous life, her
faithful labors in our association, and many good quali-
ties of a Saint, which she possessed, and for the love
which we have for her, and the respect for her pa
rents,
	Therefore, Be it resolved, that we, as members of the
Y. L. M. I. A., do deeply mourn the loss of our beloved
sister and co-laborer in this association, and that we
condole with her parents in thier great loss, and that we
try to live so that we will meet our dear sister in the
resurrection of the just.
	And, Be it resolved, that we present a copy of these
Resolutions to her parents, and that a copy be sent to
the EXPONENT for publication, and that they be spread
upon our record book.
				MARTHA M. PERKINS,
				LOTTIE WALKER,	Committee.
				CEDENIA WILLIS,
	Taylor, Apache Co.

DR. ELLIS R. SHIPP
	Will begin her next term in midwifery on the 14th
of October next, the first Monday after the general
Conference. Her last class was large and very satisfac-
tory in its progress, and in the result of examina-
tions.
	Dr. Shipp offers fine inducements in the way of
thorough training.  Reasonable terms and free scholar-
ships.
	Those wishing further information should communi-
cate with her by letter.
