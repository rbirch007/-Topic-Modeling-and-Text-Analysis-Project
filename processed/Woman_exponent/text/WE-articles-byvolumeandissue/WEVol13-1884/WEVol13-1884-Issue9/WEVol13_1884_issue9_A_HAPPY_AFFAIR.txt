A HAPPY AFFAIR.

	On Tuesday, September 30, Miss Martha J.
Horne was united in marriage to Elder Joseph
S. Tingey; both of this city. Miss Horne was one
of the first girls who learned type setting here,
and set type for the EXPONENT from its first is-
sue. She made herself very proficient in the
art of type setting, and could compete with
any of the men in the office in this respect.
The bride is a very promising young woman; is
very much loved by her friends, and greatly
esteemed by all. She holds a prominent posi-
tion in the Y. L. M. I. A. She is the daughter
of Elder Joseph and M. Isabella Horne, both
widely and favorably known among the Lat-
ter-day Saints. The bridegroom is the son of
Bishop Tingey of the 17th Ward; he is a typo
in the Deseret News Office, and is a young
man in every way likely to make a good hus-
band.
	A reception was held at the residence of the
bride's parents in the evening, and the friends
were entertained in a very hospitable style. A
delicious repast, abounding with fruits, wed-
ding cake, ice cream and tempting delicacies,
was served to all the guests.
	Prest. John Taylor, Prest. George Q. Can-
non, Gen. Daniel H. Wells, Prest. A. M. Can-
non and ladies and many others were present,
and a whole bevy of young people. There
were many generous gifts bestowed, acompan-
ied by good wishes and congratulations. There
was some music by the young ladies, and a
speech by Prest. Geo. Q. Cannon.
	We tender our most sincere congratulations,
and would wish for the happy young couple
peace, prosperity and every blessing that the
most perfect union of heart and hand can
possibly bestow; long life and happiness here,
and a fulness of celestial glory hereafter.
