A TRIBUTE.
	A tribute of sympathy and condolence to
President A. O. Smoot.
	Whereas, It has pleased our Heavenly
Father in His infinite wisdom to take from our
midst our beloved president, Sister Magaret T.
Smoot, and knowing that she has always given
us good counsel, exhorting us to works of
righteousness, and realizing that her record
has been that of a well spent life, worthy of
emulation,
	Therefore, Be it resolved, that we as a so-
ciety, deeply sympathize with her husband,
President A. O. Smoot, and his family in their
great loss, and pray that the Spirit of God may
comfort and sustain them, knowing that our
loss is her gain, and that she has passed away
in the sure hope of a glorious resurrection;
trusting that we may live true and faithful to
our covenants, and be worthy to meet her and
enjoy her society through the endless ages of
eternity.
	MARY S. HINDLEY,
	CHARLOTTA SHELLEY,
	ALICE GREENWOOD, Committee.
	ELLEN D. CLARKE,
	EMMA FEATHERSTONE,
American Fork, Sep. 4, 1884.
