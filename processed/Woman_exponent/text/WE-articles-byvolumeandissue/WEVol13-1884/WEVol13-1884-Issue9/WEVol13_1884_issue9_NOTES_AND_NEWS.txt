NOTES AND NEWS.

	Anne Whitney, the sculptor, whose last work
is Harriet Martineau's statue, has a farm of
one hundred and seventy-five acres, over which
the White Mountain range towers, and through
which the Androscoggin runs, and which she
farms practically.
	Mary E. Shaffer, of New York, has secured
a patent for a new "non-inductive, electric ca-
ble;" Sue E. Smither, of Keen, Ky., has pat-
eted a "check-rein hook." Both these patent
have been granted during the week ending
Spet. 9, 1881.
	Miss Florence Finch, late of the Boston
Globe, is now on the editorial staff of the Troy
Telegraph. The Telegraph is to be congratu-
lated on the accession of an able editor who
brings scholarship and editorial experience to
her new post.
	Just now in the city of Mexico the subject
of woman's industries is one of considerable
interset, and an association, of which the Sen-
ora Rafaela Suarez is President, is actively en-
gaged in collecting and preparing and exhibit of
woman's work for the New Orleans Exposi-
tion.

	Bro. L. M. Olsen has been appointed agent
for the WOMAN'S EXPONENT at Ephraim,
Sanpete Co., and Sister Caroline C. Raleigh
for Bear Lake Stake.
	In the article from Sister S. M. Burgess pub-
lished in our paper, a mistake was made in
the date of her going to Kirtland; it should
have been 1834 instead of 1837.
