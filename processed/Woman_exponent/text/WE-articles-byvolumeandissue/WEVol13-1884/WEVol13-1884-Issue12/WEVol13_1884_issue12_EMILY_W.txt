	EMILY W.

	DIED in Fairview, July 11th, 1884, Nancy Emeline
Young, daughter of James M. and Mary A. Pritchett,
and wife of John Floyd Young. Sister Young was born
July 1st, 1842, in Rich Valley, Smith Co., Virginia, and
was baptised in 1858. Was married November 2nd, 1859-
came to Utah in 1865; moved to Fairview in Novem
ber, 1867.
	She was the mother or sixteen children, five of whom
died in infancy. She left a babe only a few hours old.
	She was a teacher in the Relief Society almost from its
organization; always cheerful and willing to perform
the duties of her office in ministering to the wants of
the sick and needy.
	As a society and as friends we deeply sympathize
with her husband and family in their great loss, and
feel to commend them to our Heavenly Father, who
alone can comfort and sustain them.

	DIED, in Fairview, Aagust 30th, 1884, Florence Jane
Rees Young, born June 17th, 1850, in Smith Co., Vir-
ginia. Was married to Parley R Young October 30th,
1870, and came to Fairview the same year, where she re-
sided until her death.
	Sister Young had been a great sufferer for about ten
years. She died in the hope of a glorious resurrection,
leaving a husband, two daughters and many friends to
mourn her loss.
	LOIS ANN WILSON, Secretary.

	Catherine Lewis was born February, 1818, in London,
England; was baptized into the Church of Jesus Christ
of Latter-day Saints August llth,l849, County Brackon,
South Wales. Immigrated to Utah July, 1871. She
died at her residence in Sandy, October 6th, 1884, at 6:10
a.m. She died as she had lived, a good, faithful Latter-
day Saint. She leaves a good husband, Phillip Lewis,
and eight children to mourn her loss.
	She attended Conference October 4th—was at two
meetings that day—took sick in the night, was admin-
istered to and came home Sunday evening and died
Monday morning, as stated.
	She was a member of the Relief Society, and held the
office of Counselor to the President from the organiza-
tion of the ward faithfully to the time of her death.
	W. O.
