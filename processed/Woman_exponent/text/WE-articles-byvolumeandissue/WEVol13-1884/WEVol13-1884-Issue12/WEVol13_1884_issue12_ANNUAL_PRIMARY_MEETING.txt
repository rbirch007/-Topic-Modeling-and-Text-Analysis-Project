ANNUAL PRIMARY MEETING.

	On the 30th of November I attended, by invi-
tation of the President, Sister Julia Howe, the
anniversary of the Primary meeting of the 17th
Ward. Many of the parents of the children
were also present, which was encouraging to
Sister Howe and her assistants. There were a
goodly number of children present, the girls
much predominating, all looking healthy and
happy. The order was excellent and the be-
havior of the children was pretty and winning.
They were very correct in answering a long list
of very important questions, and the songs and
recitations were admirably given, even by two
or three mere infants. A song by little Miss
Edna Hyde was sung and accompanied by her-
self in a sweet manner; her sister, Miss Laura
Hyde, is secretary, and though so young, filled
her office with ease and dignity, worthy of imi-
tation. I feel it must have been a proud day for
Sister Howe, for she has been indefatigable in
her labors in the Primary department, also Sis-
ter Dougall, who has been very energetic - and I
must not forget to name Miss Ella Dallas, who
is most faithful and praiseworthy in her con-
stant attendance wherever she can be an assist-
ent, or make herself useful; she is gentle, hum-
ble and ever lady-like. Such young ladies
should be appreciated by every one - and I ex-
pect they are. I felt it was a time of refreshing


to all those ladies, and that they were in a
measure sweetly repaid for all their labors.
	Among the delightful things was a "fish
pond," which created a fund of enjoyment for
all. This concluded the eventful day, and I
feel it will long be remembered by all present.
Sister Ellen Clawson, Sister Carrington and
Sister Crooheron each addressed the children in
their usual winning way, and I felt refreshed
and edified as a child of large growth.
								H. T. K.
