DEDICATED TO THE MEMORY OF
		MRS. L. W. ELDREDGE.
	The sad news that reached our ears announc-
ing the death of Sister Lizzie Winder Eldredge,
sunk deep into the hearts of relatives and friends.
I was personally acquainted with her, lived a
near neighbor to her for six or seven years, and
admired her traits of character as a true Latter-
day Saint. In all her acts of life she showed a
thorough good training of a wife and judicious
mother. In her short life she accomplished more
than one of the ordinary class of women.
	Her whole life was devoted to her religion;
she was ever present at her meetings, her child-
ren, neatly dressed, were always with her, and
her husband at the Sunday School and Sabbath
meetings. They went hand in hand together.
With a young babe in her arms she assisted her
husband in the Sunday School, acting as teacher
and reporter.
	Present at the Relief Society she there acted
as Secretary and co-worker and an encourager
of home industry. She also took an active part
in the Primary meetings; took untiring pains to
teach her young sisters to be useful. Helped
the secretaries and teachers, and spent a great
deal of time to revise and correct minutes,in the
midst of her family cares and duties.
	In her domestic life she was economical and
prudent. Her house was ever ready to entertain
her associates in, and adorned with the work-
manship of her own hands. She was a woman
of education, and had a tact for business, in
which she asssisted her husband. She attended
to all her household duties, and has gone from a
life of usefulness to a more useful one even than
this, a higher and broader sphere of action.
Her husband has a treasure eternal there—im-
mortal ones. May he emulate her example in
the culture of his children.
	Dear husband and children,
	Short is the time of our parting;
	It is near at hand, the millenium,
	I shall come in the morn, the first resurrection.
