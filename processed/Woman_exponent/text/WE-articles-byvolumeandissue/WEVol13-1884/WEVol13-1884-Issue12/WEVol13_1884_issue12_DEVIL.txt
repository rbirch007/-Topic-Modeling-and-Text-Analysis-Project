				DEVIL.

	“And when they say unto you, seek unto them who
have familiar spirits, and unto wizards that peep and
mutter, should not a people seek unto their God? For
the living to the dead?--ISAIAH, viii, 19.”
			If light we desire
			Oh, why not enquire
	Of the Lord, who is pleased to bestow it?
			Who shall bind you and me,
			Whom the truth maketh free,
			Bid the “Evil One” flee—
	Nor yield him an inch if we know it.

			Why bow to "the Prince
			Of the Air," or evince
	Respect for his pestilent legions,
			Or foolishly seek
			Those who mutter and peep,
			Or their company keep
	Who’re bound for the bottomelss regions?

			And wherefore be found
			On the arch-Tempter’s ground,
	Or bring ourselves down to his level?
			Lest “he sift us as wheat,"
			Or our ruin complete
			And esteem it a feat—
	Never hold out a hand to the devil;

			Because of the snares,
			Many sink unawares,
	And unwittingly fall in temptation.
			'Tis “the turn of the tide,"
			And the best must be tried;
			May we faithful abide,
	Nor stumble o'er ev'ry vexation.

			In the Mighty and Just
			May we evermore trust,
	He is equal to all that oppose Him;
			Let the vile do and say
			Whatsoever they may;
			For guidance we’ll pray,
	Till the Lord in His wrath overthrows them.

			Need we shrink from the storm
			Whilst we lean on His arm,
	Whose command, yea, whose whisper can quell it?
			Let the hurricane blow,
			For our pilot we know,
			When he wills thus or so
	There exists not a power can annul it.

			To mortals is given
			The priesthood of heaven;
	An organization efficient
			To triumphantly tread
			On the “Old Serpent’s” head;
			While we seek Him who said;
	“For the weakest my grace is sufficient."
