A BIRTHDAY PARTY.

	Sister Talitha C. Avery is our energetic and
worthy president of this place. The sisters of
the Relief Society got up a surprise for her on
the 22nd of Sept., it being the sixtieth anni-
versary of her birthday. At twelve o’clock a
bounteous dinner was prepared in the school-
house; when all was ready Sister Avery was in-
vited in; she was very much surprised to see so
many sitting down to four long tables filled
with the good things of the earth; about one
hundred partook of the sumptuous repast. Af-
ter dinner was over the tables were cleared
away and meeting was opened by singing and
prayer, after which songs, speeches, dialogues,
also a piece of poetry composed and read for
the occasion by Sister Esther Grange and an
acrostic by Sister Maria J. Wakefield; the
martial band also enlivened us with several
cheering tunes. A nice rug was then presented
by the sisters of the Society to Sister Avery,
with several other presents. The evening was
spent in dancing, social conversation, etc. It
was quite a joyful time, and will be long re-
membered by those who participated therein.
	Praying for the success of your valuable pa-
per as an exponent of truth and a vindicator
of our rights, I subscribe myself,
	Your sister in the Gospel,
	ANNIE M. ALGER.
Huntington.
