OBITUARIES.

	DIED, at her residence, in Tooele City, September 28th,
1884, Mrs. Mary C. De La Mar. She was baptized in the
[--]ll of 1849, by E'der William C. Dunbar in her native
land, the Island of Jersey, immigrated to the valley in
1852, and in 1853 removed to Tooele, where she has
been for many years identified as a practical Latter-day
Saint.
	She was called as teacher in the Relief Society in
1870, and in 1872 was chosen treasurer, which office she
held up to the time of her death, and by her integrity 
and uprightness won the confidence and love of all as-
sociated with her.
	She leaves a family of six living children and seven-
teen grandchildren, born and raised in plural marriage; 
and many times she has borne testimony of the truth
and purity of that holy principle.
	She has a son and son-in-law at present filling mis-
sions in Tennesee, but her faith was firm in God, and
she left them in His hands, knowing “all is well.”
