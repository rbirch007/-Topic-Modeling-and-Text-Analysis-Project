GOSHEN Y. L. AND PRIMARY FAIR.
	A feeling of delightful admiration came over
the people on entering the shoolhouse, which
was most beautifully decorated with the handi-
work of the members of the Y. L. and Primary
Associations. It was surprising to see the
beautiful articles made by the youth of this
place. At a glance one could note the im-
provement made on previous years. How can
the world say the sons and daughters of Zion
are not intelligent? We are sure they would
not believe that statement if they would visit
the fairs among the Saints and see the bright
faces of the children beaming with intelligence,
and see the many beautiful things made by
them.
	Some very handsome quilts were exhibited,
done by the elder members; also neatly done
patchwork and cradle quilts by our very small
children. There were rugs, mats of various
kinds, flowers on velvet, canvas and hair work,
shawls, infants' wear, stockings, brackets, laces,
tidies, embroideries, mottoes, slippers, wool, card-
ed and spun, window curtains, aprons, and
other clothing; also varions articles of food and
vegetables, walking canes, hay rakes, ladders, a
centre table, and many other things too numer-
ous to mention.
	Two meetings were held, one at 10 a.m. and
the other at 2 p.m. In the morning the time
was spent in listening to the remarks of our
Bishop, our Primary President, and other
members of the Ward. In the afternoon we
were highly interested in listening to a spicily
arranged programme, consisting of songs, reci-
tations and dialogues. In all, we had a pleas-
urable and enjoyable time.
LUCY TAYLOR.
Oct. 18, 1884.
