A BIRTHDAY PARTY.

	The 23th of July, the 66th birthday of Patriarch B. F.
Johnson, was celebrated in the following manner by
his wives and children: He was escorted to the bowery
by his sons, where, to his surprise, he found his family
seated in order at the table loaded with good things,
awaiting his appearance. He entered; took his seat at
the head, over which an arch had been formed and dec-
orated with fruit and flowers, and the flag of our coun-


ry behind. He pronouned it a complete surprise,
then gave thanks to our Father in heaven. 
	After partaking of supper, the Patriarch arose and
said that all who have entered into the celebration of my
birthday with love and good feeling, I say, God bless
you, and I bless you in the name of the Lord Jesus. 
	Of his children fifty-five absent. One hundred
and nine of the company belonged to his family. A
few useful gifts were presented, and a piece of poetry
written by a friend, expressing good wishes and that he
might always have the love of his children, was then
read.
	The evening was spent in dancing, himself cheerfully
joining in the excercise. All said the party was the most
enjoyable of any for many years.
	The Patriarch received the Gospel when sixteen years
of age, and has ever since been a lover of its principles,
and has instilled the love of the same into the hearts of
his children, for which now they return him thanks. He
is man of great enterprise and extensive labors: he has
a large and comprehensive mind and is a benefactor to
his people.
	Our Ward is fully organized with Relief Society,
Young Folks' Mutual and Primary, which are well at-
tended, and all seem to realize the importance of doing
their duty as presented. We are always pleased to re-
ceive the EXPONENT, and think everybody should read
it. Lovingly your sister in the truth,
								J. D. W.
	Tempe, Arizona.
