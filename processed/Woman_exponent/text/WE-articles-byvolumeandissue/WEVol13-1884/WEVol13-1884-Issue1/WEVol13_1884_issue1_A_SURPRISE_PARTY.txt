A SURPRISE PARTY.!

On the 29th of April the Y. L. M. I. A. of Minersville
gave a surprise party in honor of their president, Sister
Lydia Blackburn.  The Association, with a few elderly
people, met at the Relief Society house at six o'clock.
After the tables were spread and everything put in or-
der, the president was informed that her presence was
required at the R. S. house, her surprise when entering
the house where some fifty or sixty people were gath-
ered was very great.  A nice dress pattern was then
presented to her by the association.  After supper James
H. Rollins was elected chairman, and the remainder of
the evening was spent in a very enjoyable way, by sing-
ing, recitations, speeches, etc
	Bishop James Mc.Knight and Counselor George Eyre
made some very interesting remarks, also President Ly-
dia Blackburn expressed her gratitude for the honor
shown her on the occasion.  It was then motioned that
the minutes of the proceedings be sent to the Exponent
for publication.  The company broke up at twelve
o’clock, feeling that the time had been well spent.
IDA  MARSHAL,
HELEN STODDARD,

		H.S.
