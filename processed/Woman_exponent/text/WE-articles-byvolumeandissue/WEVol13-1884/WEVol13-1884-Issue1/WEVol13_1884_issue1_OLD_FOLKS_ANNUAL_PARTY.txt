OLD FOLKS ANNUAL PARTY.
			--------------
		 BLOOMINGTON.
			--------------
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We held our annual party for the aged of our settle-
ment on January 31st of the present year, at which up-
wards of a hundred persons sat down to dinner; it was
a very enjoyable affair.  After which in regular order
the following programme was carried out: Song, "We
thank thee, O God, for a Prophet," prayer by Bro. P.
Greenbalgh, singing, "O my Father," Opening address
by President J. H. Hart, in which he expressed his pleas-
ure at meeting with us, and also gave some excellent
instructions.  Bishop Wm. Hulme expressed his desire
to have a roll and minutes read at the next meeting.
Song, Sister Mary Roberts: address, Sister A. Womsley:
dance, Father Nelson: song, Bro. and Sister Greenbalgh
and Jarvis; recitation, "Bread upon the Waters," Sister
L. M. Hart; dancing; address, Father Nelson; Danish
song, Bro. Holtz; speech, Bro. Quinton; song "You've
been a friend to me," J. Haddock; song, Sister Roberts.
address, Prest. S. Greenbalgh; song, "Hard times come
again no more," Bro. and Sister Jarvis, song, "The
man behind the plow," J. H. Hart; speech, Sister C.
Long; Negro song, Bishop Hulme; song, Father Patter-
son; speech, Sister M. Roberts; song, "The bobbin-wind-
er," Bro. P. Greenbalgh; song, "The old Wooden Rock
er," Misses Mary and Adelia Greenhalgh.  Singing
benediction by Prest. Hart.
	Dancing was interspersed throughout the programme,
which was in two parts, refreshments being served at
the intermission.  A good spirit prevailed, and all
seemed to enjoy the entertainment.  We have plenty of
snow on the ground, but the weather is mild.
	Wishing your valuable paper success, I beg leave to
subscribe myself, your sister in the Gosepl,
				SARAH GREENBALUGH, President.
				E. C. PIGGOTT, Secretary.
