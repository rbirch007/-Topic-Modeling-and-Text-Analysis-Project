OBITUARIES.

	DIED, at the family residence, in the 17th Ward, Salt Lake
City, Mary Jenson, the beloved wife of Andrew Jenson,
after about ten weeks suffering.  She was born in Bjerggard,
Hellevad Sogn, Hjorring Amt, Denmark, March 7, 1859;
was baptized into the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day
Saints May 24th, 1873; enigrated to Utah in 1875, and was
married to Andrew Jenson August 30, 1875.  She leaves,
besides her husband, four children—two sons and two
daughters—and died in full faith of all the principles of
her religion, to which she had yielded obedience.  She was
a dutiful wife and mother, and was much beloved and re-
spected by all who knew her.

	DIED, at Manti City, Sanpete Co., Sister Cecelia J.
Hansen, born July 6, 1829, in the City of Aalborg, Den-
mark, and departed this life Jan. 6, 1887.  She was bap-
tized into the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints
Feb. 6, 1851 She buried her mother in June, 1852, who
was the first member of the Church who died in that city.
Sister Hansen left Denmark that fall with the emigration
Dec. 20, 1852, and arrived in Salt Lake City Sept. 29, 1853.
Lived at Israel Ivins' until she married Niel Hansen,
Sept. 10, 1854.  She was the mother of seven children,
three sons and four daughters; she had also two adopted
children; four have gone before her; she leaves a husband,
two sons and three daughters to mourn her loss.  She died
in full faith of the Gospel to her latest breath, and admon-
ished all her children, one by one, to faithfulness in the
latter day work under all circumstances.  She was a faith-
ful member and teacher in the Relief Society, and did
many deeds of charity among the poor.  We deplore her
demise, and pray that He, who doeth all things well, will
heal and comfort the mourning hearts.
							A. R. KELLER, Sec.
	In behalf of the Relief Society.
