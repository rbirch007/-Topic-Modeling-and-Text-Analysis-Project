	DAVIS STAKE. 

	The quarterly conference of the Relief So-
ciety of Davis Stake was held at Kaysville, 

[Column 2]
July 30th, Sister Susan Grant presiding. 
Meeting commenced at 10 o'clock. Singing, 
"O God be praised." Prayer by Sister Sarah
B. Layton. Singing, "May we who know the
joyful sound." There were present from Salt
Lake City, Sisters E. Howard and E. B. Wells. 
The minutes of the previous conference were
read and accepted. The reports from the
different branches of the Relief Society were
also read. Prest. Susan Grant said that she
with her counselors had visited all the different
branches in the Stake; gave a very satisfactory
report of each branch. 
	Sister E. B. Wells then gave us a brief ac-
count of some of the things she saw while in
the East, and at New York and Washington
in particular; also exhorted the sisters to be
diligent in teaching their children the princi-
ples of the Gospel. 
	Sister Mary S. Clark expressed her thanks
for the privilege of meeting once more in the
capacity of a conference; urged the sisters to
seek for the Spirit of God and live in a way to
claim His blessing. Meeting adjourned until
2 p.m. Benediction by Prest. Susan Grant. 
	Meeting convened again at 2 p.m. After 
the opening exercises, Sister E. Howard, Coun.
Lucinda Brown and E. B. Wells addressed
the sisters. Spoke at some length upon mothers
governing their children, and also to try and
induce our daughters to dress in a manner
that would be conducive to health. 
	Conference adjourned for three months. 
Singing, "The Spirit of God like a fire is 
burning." Benediction by Phebe Atkinson.
			RHODA HINMAN, 
	Secretary Davis Stake R. S.
