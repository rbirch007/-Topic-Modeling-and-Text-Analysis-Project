A LOVE SONG. 

O love, he said, if I might win
	Thy hand to clasp in mine alway,
O sweet the joy and strong my hopes,
	Though rough or hard should be life's way;
Thine eye should never miss my smile.
	Thy heart should never grieve a wrong,
Like light and warmth our lives should blend,
	Our language, one sweet, sacret song.

O words so sweet, how could they fail;
	They woke love's answer in her heart,
They made her present joyous, and
	Grew of her very life a part.
For her they lit the taper pure,
	That never dies while life endures,
And e'en became the star of hope
	That still her troubled heart assures.

For him, they died upon his lips
	As floweret's odors pass away.
But e'en as spring returning, wakes
	The flowers that sleep through winter's day;
O answer, by thy smile of love
	O lover! her true heart again,
And keep the vow that angels heard,
	For she is faithful now as then.

Fulfil thy troth, man may not break 
	So sacred bond at idle will,
Lest she a Saint in heaven stand
	Confronting thee and loving still! 
How shall thy changeful mind reply
	Her steadfast truth thou didst implore, 
For thou must meet it there and then, 
	Nor turn away, or falter more.
