HONOR TO WHOM HONOR IS DUE.

	At Pima, Arizona, a pleasant and enjoyable 
surprise was given to Stake President of Re-
lief Society, Wilmirth East, on Wednesday,
Nov. 17th, it being her sixty-second birthday,
by the Relief Society and Young Ladies' Asso-
ciations of the St. Joseph Stake. About 250
of the brethren and sisters were present, among
whom was President Layton, who spoke in
high terms of praise of the way in which Sis-
ter East had performed her labors as President
of the Relief Society of St. Joseph Stake; said
she was truly a mother in Israel; always on
hand to administer to the sick and needy, and
give words of encouragement to those who were
called to mourn, or to those who were inclined
to stray from the path of duty. Said he hoped
all would enjoy themselves, and be benefited
by coming together on such a pleasant occasion,
The remainder of the afternoon was spent in
singing and giving recitations, and in pleasant
conversation.
	Sister East was the recipient of some hand-
some presents from the branches of the Relief
Society of Pima, Central, Thatcher and Layton,
and from the Y. L. M. I. Associations of the
above named settlements. Sister East spoke
a short time; said it had been a complete sur-
prise; thanked all for the honor they had
shown her, and for the gifts bestowed, and
hoped with the help of God to be always
worthy of their love and esteem, after which
all partook of the plentiful repast that was
spread before them, and then returned to
their homes, feeling benefited by meeting to-
gether.
