	LAURA NUTTALL, Sec.


﻿The second "Mother's Meeting" of this sea-
son, at the Woman's Educational and Industrial
Union, 74 Boylston Street, Boston, will be
conducted by Mrs. Julia Ward Howe.
	One of the leading charities of New Orleans
is called the "Society of the Ladies Servants
of the Poor." It was established in 1861, the
first year of the rebellion, to assist destitute
families of respectability and provide for the
wants of old and infirm "gentlewomen."—Ex.
