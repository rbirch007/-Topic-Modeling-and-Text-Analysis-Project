EPITAPH.

Life was full of pain and tears,
	And hopes doomed to defeat,
Of grief-rent hours and bitter years,
	That death, at last, is sweet.
Dear Lord, a heap of helpless clay,
	Fettered and prison-walled,
Lies here until the promised day,
	Waiting to be called.

Sweet dreams of how I shall rejoice,
	And burst these prison walls,
When first that loved and loving voice
	In heavenly music calls.
Make every atom of this dust
	To thrill with deep delight;
Oh! call me with the pure and just
	To dwell with thee in light.
