CHRISTMAS NIGHT.

In all this boasted land of happy homes,
   How many circles are complete to-night?
How many round their festive firesides come
   Without a vacancy or sorrow’s blight?
Fancy paints many brilliant, happy throngs,
   Where beauty, wealth, and splendid grandeur beam;
While feasting, music, dance and joyous songs,
   Make bliss upon the earth, 'twould seem;
But could we look into each secret soul,
   Would we there find response to this display?
Would we not see behind this self control
   That bleeding hearts in agony hold sway?
Thus incompleteness seems to be to man,
   While on this earth a common legacy;
Let him seek perfect joy where’er he can,
   For from earth’s ills he never will be free,
Because our Father did not so ordain.
   That our brief lives should be devoid of ill,
Nor free from disappointment, care and pain;
   Ah, no! He had an object greater still.	
Here on this earth, created for man’s sake,
   He placed, with agencies, His chosen few,
That they might of the good and ill partake,
   And learn to judge between the two.
But there will come a Christmas holiday—
   The glorious natal day of heaven’s Son,
When all the world combined will stay
   To give their praises to that Holy One.
Then every land in perfect joy’ll abound,
   No mortal fears will then beset the soul; 
Nor will there in immortal hearts be found
   One weakness that demands control.
Then every nation, people, kindred tongue,
   With one accord will bow the pliant knee;
And glorious praise and anthems will be sung,
   Whilst Gods and angels shout, “Oh, Zion’s free!”
							     E. R. SHIPP.
Philadelphia, Dec. 25, 1876.
