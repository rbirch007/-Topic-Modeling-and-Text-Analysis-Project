BY M. J. TANNER.

	As I gaze upon the ocean,
	Thrilis my heart with fond emotion,
And the wind is blowing chilly on the shore;
	While the waves are secrets telling,
	And their bosoms gently swelling,
As they dash upon the breakers with a roar.

	And I think upon the rhymes
	I have read so many times,
For surely they are written by the score;
	And I think how grand a theme
	It must be for poets dream,
As I bring to mind some long forgotten lore.

	And I listen here and ponder,
	For my heart is growing fonder,
As I think of all the tales I've read of yore,
	And I know that when I leave thee
	My heart will often grieve me,
For I'll listen to thy beating never more.

	Oh, thou great, mysterious ocean!
	Holdest thou no warm emotion—
Holdest thou no secret sigh in mem'ry's store?
	Can your heart, without pulsation,
	Hold the secrets of a nation,
That we read not as we search your bosom o'er?

	Who shall tell the wondrous story,
	As the world with ages hoary
Binds the pride of many nations gone before,
	And the spirits o'er thee gliding,
	And the souls their time abiding,
When the trumps shall sound their requiem evermore.

	When thy depths shall not be hidden.
	Nor the argosies unbidden
Sent to sleep beneath thine ever sounding roar;
	And a great and mighty power
	Shall unclose thee in that hour,
When you will not keep your secrets any more.

Sea Plain, Monmoith Co., New Jersey, May, 1885.
