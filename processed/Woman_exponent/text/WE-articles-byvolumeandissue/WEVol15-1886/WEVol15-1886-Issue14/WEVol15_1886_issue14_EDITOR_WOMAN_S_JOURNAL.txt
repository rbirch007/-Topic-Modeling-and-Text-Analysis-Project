EDITOR WOMAN'S JOURNAL:
	Our city has been quite excited over the
ordination, of Miss Ida C. Hulten. A large
and fashionable audience gathared in the
Unitarian Church, on the 10th inst., to witness
her installation as pastor of that congrega-
tion.
	It was a beautiful and deeply impressive
ceremony. Beautiful, because a woman minis-
ter stood in the pulpit ready to extend the
right hand of fellowship. In the joy and glad-
ness of her heart she extended both hands,
clasping in hers the hands of another woman
minister, and showing by this loving clasp how
willing women are to help one another. Rev.
Mary A. Spafford, of Sioux City, was fitly


chosen for this pleasant duty, and Rev. Marion
Murdock made the ordination prayer, in words
so tender and trusting that every heart was
touched. The sight of three women conse-
crated to the ministry made the ceremony
doubly impressive. The suffrage workers take
new courage and grow strong under such an
inspiration as this.
	MARIA S. ORWIG.
Des Moines, Iowa, Nov. 19, 1886.
