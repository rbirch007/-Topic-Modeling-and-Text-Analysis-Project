A PLEASANT PARTY.

	A large number of the Pleasant Green Relief
Society met at the house of their President,
Alvira Spencer Hirst, on her twenty-eighth
birthday, recently, and gave her a thorough
surprise.
	When we were all seated to partake of the
bounties of the earth, the Bishop presented her
with a handsome book, entitled; "History of
Salt Lake City," in behalf of the Relief So-
ciety. Our President was almost overcome
with surprise and gratitude, but she arose and
made a short speech, in which she thanked all
the sisters for showing so much love and re-
spect to her, although she did not feel herself
worthy of so much respect. 
	We had a very happy time, spending most
of the day and evening in singing, recitations,
etc. We had some very good remarks from
the bishop and his counselors; also some of the
other brethren.
		FANNY BROWN,
		FANNY JENKINS, Committee.
