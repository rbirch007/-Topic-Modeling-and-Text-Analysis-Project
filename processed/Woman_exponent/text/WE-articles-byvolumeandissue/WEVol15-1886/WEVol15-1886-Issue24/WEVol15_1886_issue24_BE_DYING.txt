BE DYING?"

Ah, how long will the heart be dying?
	Is a question we often ask,
While the agonized cry unanswered
	Resounds through the universe vast.

How long, crushed with anguish unspoken,
	Under doubt's burden weight held fast,
Pulsating with torture and broken,
	Can its ebbing of life-blood last?

When glad, earthly hopes and bright visions,
	All along life's pathway have fled,
When its fairest flowers are blighted,
	Its dearest one's scatter'd and dead.

When friendships strong ties have been rended,
	And their fragrance of memory gone,
With love's fondly cherished and tended,
	How long will the tried heart live on?

How long with relentless fate striving,
	With darts from detractions pierced through,
Poisoned arrows, malice sent flying,
	From caldrons of envy's foul dew?

How long with ingratitude stricken,
	With censure and impious scorn,
Can poor human hearts hold resistance,
	O'er cruel oppression and wrong?

Aye, how long keep the signal flags flying,
	O'er	battlements tattered and torn,
Will the heart in its grief be dying,
	 How long ere it ceases to mourn?

A bleeding world calls forth in sighing,
	In travail of bitterest woe,
How long shall the tried heart be dying,
	Is the boon we crave to know?

From the far off hills of the morning,
	When the breezes are gently stirred,
Comes the call in echo returning,
	Like the notes of a wounded bird.

It rings through earth's mighty cathedral,
	From above around and below,
How long shall the tried heart be dying?
	Is the boon we crave to know:

Martyred graves, and the wreck of nations,
	With united throb and throe—
And ever the echo's repeating,
	Is the boon we crave to know?
