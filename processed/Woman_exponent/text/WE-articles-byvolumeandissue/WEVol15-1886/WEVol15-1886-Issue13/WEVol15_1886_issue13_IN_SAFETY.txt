		   IN SAFETY.
			
	   May we safely pass the "narrows,"
	      Is the prayer of many a heart,
	   Which cruel persecution harrows
	      With its bitter, piercing dart.

	   Very narrow now the path is
	      Which the true Saint has to tread,
	   Winding through courts and prisons, ’tis
	      Enough to fill the soul with dread.

	   Yet, nothing strange to us has come,
	      The former Saints were also tried; 
	   To suffer is the common doom,
	      Unless the "faith” can be denied.

	   But firm in truth’s great cause then stood
	      Those worthies of a former day,
	   Who "all things” suffered and endured
	      Rather than throw the pearl away.

	   The prize they sought was life eternal,
	      A life beyond the power of death;
	   Fraught with joy and biss supernal,
		That would not vanish with their breath.


They in safety passed the “narrows,”
	      For in Israel’s God their trust:
	   By faith in Him they burst the barriers
	      The wicked placed around the just.

	   What were bolts and bars to Peter,
	      When he was into prison cast,
	   Safely bound with chain and fetter,
	      With soldiers charged to keep him fast?

	   How very narrow was the road
	      For Daniel, made by wicked men.
	   When he must cease to pray to God,
	      Or go into the lion’s den.

	   Of this aware, he did not falter,
	      Although he knew the king’s decree,
	   Made by laws that none could alter,
	      On him must executed be.

	   The “narrows” he in safety passed,
	      Though hungry as the lions were,
	   And for this reason made to fast, 
	      They could not hurt—his God was near.

	   Straight and narrow was the pathway
	      By the Prophet Nephi seen;
	   And mists of darkness on it lay,
	      Nor ray of light scarce intervene.

	   But clinging to the Iron Rod,
	      Through all the turmoil and the strife,
	   Pressing along the narrow road,
	      Many did reach the “Tree of Life.”

	   Be the “narrows” e’er so narrow.
	      God can bring us safely through;
	   He who careth for the sparrow,
	      Careth for His children too.

	   And although the fiery arrows
	      Thick and fast around us fly,
	   All will safely pass the “narrows”
	      Who on Israel’s God rely.
						   HANNAH CORNABY.
Spanish Fork, Nov. 9, 1886.
