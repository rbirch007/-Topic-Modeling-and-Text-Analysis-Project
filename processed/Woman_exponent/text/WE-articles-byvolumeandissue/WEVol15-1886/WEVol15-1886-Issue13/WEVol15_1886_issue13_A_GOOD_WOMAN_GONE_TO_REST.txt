A GOOD WOMAN GONE TO REST.

	Sister Judith Bellows departed this life Sept. 30th, 1886,
at the residence of her son, John F. Bellows, Lake Shore
Ward. The deceased had been failing for some time, and
died without a struggle, at seven o’clock p.m. She had
her share in the mobbings and drivings of the Saints. Her
husband, father of her children, was poisoned, and died
from the effects of it, administered by the mob that came
up against the Saints; after which she, with her children,
suffered the privations in common with the Saints in those
terrible times, remaining true and faithful to the Church
and kingdom of God; she has borne many true and faithful
testimonies to the restoration of the Gospel; this knowledge
was her chief joy by day and night. Did considerable
work in the St. George Temple, in 1883. She became so
feeble in health that she had to leave, much against her
will, having enjoyed her labors so much. On every oppor-
tunity she would exhort all to faithfulness. Her integrity
to the truth and her many good qualities are worthy of
imitation.
	She was a member of the Relief Society, and as such
was highly respected and esteemed.	
						EMMA CURTIS,
						ANN NEBEKER, Committee.
						M. A. HARDY,
	We don’t feel to mourn
	For our sister departed,
	She has gone to the rest
	That	remains for true hearted;
	She has gone to her husband,
	Who long has been waiting
	To clasp her with joy to his bosom again.
	
	She has suffered, but borne
	All her sorrows in patience—
	Her troubles and trials
	Have all passed away;
	She has won the bright crown,
	By faith and endurance,
	Her reward is obtained, her joy is complete.

	Farewell, then, dear sister,
	We would not recall thee,
	We know thou art safe in the haven of rest;
	Thou hast finished thy work,
	Thou hast fought the good fight,
	Henceforth thou wilt dwell
	With the good and the blest.
