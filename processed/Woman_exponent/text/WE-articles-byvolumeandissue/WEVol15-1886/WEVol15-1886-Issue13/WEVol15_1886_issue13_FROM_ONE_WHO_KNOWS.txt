FROM ONE WHO KNOWS.

	Having read in the WOMAN'S EYPONENT
a request of the sisters to furnish some sub-
stantial testimonies upon the subject of plural
marriage I will here say I have had a long and
happy experience in that celestial order of
marriage.
	In the spring of 1851 my husband married
a second wife in obedience to the counsel of
those, that had a right to counsel him and be-
cause he sincerely believed the principle to
be true. I was willing and even anxious that
he should do so for I know he did it to be
obedient to the law of God and not from any
spirit of selfishness, and we lived together, the
second wife and myself, for two years; then it
pleased God to call her from this world and I
felt her loss deeply. I was glad when my hus-
band married another wife and although we
have passed through some trials during the
twenty-two years we have lived in this order
together yet we know it is the will of our
Heavenly Father that we should be tried in
all things and I am fully convinced that it is
right and that the principle is a divine one.
	In any of the trying scenes that I have
passed through I never have said I was sorry
we had entered into that order of marriage or
obeyed that principle for I have had testi-
monies in my own experience that have con-
vinced me of its truth. I cannot give the
event or the evidence  in writing that have
convinced me but the spirit of the Lord has
enlightened my mind and borne witness to its
truth. My husband has been dead now five
years and we, his wives, both of us, look for-
ward to the time when we will meet him in the
resurrection. We expect to be reunited with
our husband in the celestiel kingdom of God
having kept the celestial law.
		Your sister in the Gospel,
						C. H. Martin.
