			BY M. J. TANNER.

My spirit is with thee, my heart is oppressed
With the sorrows of Zion, my home in the west;
And the voice of thy mourning is with me to-day,
For the tears of His children the Lord will repay.

I will turn to the mountains, my footsteps once more
Shall tread in thy green grassy vales as of yore;
And my song shall arise as an anthem of woe,
For thy sons that are chased as the wild mountain roe.

He has hardened the hearts of the Pharoahs, to-day,
And their hands are upheld by Thy beloved ones to stay,
And the cries of thy orphans are borne on the gale
And the tears of thy widows no pity avail.

Great Spirit Almighty! how long wilt thou prove
The hearts of Thy children, the sons of Thy love;
How long shall the tears of Thy daughters arise,
And the prayers of the martyrs ascend to the skies.

How long wilt thou suffer their blindness and lust
To trample the hearts of Thy Saints in the dust;
How long will the light of Thy smile be withdrawn,
Ere the darkness of night be dissolved in the dawn.
	East Saginaw, July, 1885.
