EDITORIAL NOTES.

	THE conference of the Relief Society of this
Stake will be held in the Fourteenth Ward As-
sembly rooms on Thursday December 9th, com-
mencing at 10 a.m., and at 2 p.m.  A verbal re-
port from each branch in the Stake will be ex-
pected and it is most desirable that the President
or one of her counselors should be present to
make the report and clearly represent the con-
dition of the respective branch over which she
presides.  It is earnestly hoped that this advice
will be acted upon that the President may know
as much as possible of the spiritual as well as
the temporal condition of each of these organiza-
tions in the Stake.
	ON Friday December 10th the Young Ladies’
Associations of this Stake will hold their quarter-
ly conference at the same place,the meetings com-
mencing at 10 a.m., and at 2 p.m.  The presidents
are expected to make verbal reports personally
or through one of the counselors.
	ON Saturday the Primary Associations of the
Stake will hold their regular quarterly conference,
and it is expected there will be a good attendance.
The exercises will be interspersed with singing
and reciting.  All who are interested in these or-
ganizations are cordially invited to attend the
conferences.
	The regular meeting of the officers of the Y. L.
M. I. A. in this Stake, which occurs on the first
Saturday in every month, will be held hereafter
(for the present) in the Relief Society Hall in the
Fourteenth Ward instead of the residence of Mrs.
E. S. Taylor; the attendance at these meetings
is so large that Mrs. Taylor’s parlor proves too
small a place.  The Relief Society Hall is in
the upper part of the brick building just west of
the Fourteenth Ward Assembly Rooms on first
south street.  All those interested in mutual im-
provement are cordially invited.  Meetings com-
mence at 11 a.m. The next one will be December
4th.  The entrance to the hall is in the rear of the
building.
