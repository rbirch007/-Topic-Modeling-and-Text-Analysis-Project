			WASATCH STAKE.
	The Semi-annual conference of the Wasatch
Stake was held at Heber, Nov. 5th.  On the
stand were Prest. Emma Brown and Counselors
and the presidents of the different branches,
Coun. Thomas H. Giles, several bishops and a
number of the other brethren.
	The choir sang,  "Sweet is the work,” prayer
being offered by Sister Mary E. Cluff.  Minutes
and written reports were read and approved.
Prest. Emma Brown and her Counselors, Sis-
ters Daybell and Alexander, dwelt strongly on
the subjects of the word of wisdom, and train-
ing the minds of the young to fit and prepare
them for future use in the kingdom of God.
	All the branches, eight in number, were
most ably represented by their presidents; also
a large number of members from each place.
The verbal reports were as usual, showing no
decrease in either members, or energy on the
part of the sisters.
	Coun. T. H. Giles encouraged the sisters;
Bishop R. S. Duke also spoke well of the ener-
getic enterprise among the sisters.  Said, “We
must strive to fulfill every duty required of a
Latter-day Saint.”
	The meetings were well attended, the sing-
ing by the choir excellent, and a good spirit
prevailed  throughout.   Each  went  home
strengthened, edified and encouraged.
	Conference adjourned for six months.  Sing-
ing.  Benediction by Bishop R. S. Duke.
