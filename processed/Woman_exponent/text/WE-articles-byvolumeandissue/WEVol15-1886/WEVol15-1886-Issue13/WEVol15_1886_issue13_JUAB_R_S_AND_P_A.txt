			JUAB R. S. AND P. A.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	As I have never seen anything in the
columns of our valuable paper about the Relief
Society of Juab, I thought I would send you
a few items.
	The 30th of October was the third anniver-
sary of the organization of our Relief Society
and Primary Association.  We had a very en-
joyable time; the programme occupied two
hours, with songs and recitations, dialogues and
reports of the Associations, by members of
both.
	After the exercises were over, we had some
very good instructions given us by Sister Pitch-
forth and Bishop Taylor, and at one o’clock a
meeting was called of the sisters, and addresses
were made, in which encouragement and much
good and very valuable instructions were given
from Prest. Pitchforth and Brother and Sister
Bigler of Nephi.
	In the afternoon we had a dance for the
children, and for the older folks at night.
	The Relief Society here was re-organized on
the 16th of last April, with Sarah M. Boyack,
President; Christina Jenson and Mary E. Tay-
lor, Counselors; Frances Jackman, Secretary;
Bell Taylor Assistant Secretary; Christina Jen-
son Treasurer.  The Primary of this place
was re-organized on the 13th of last September,
with Ruth E. Chase, President; M. Boyack and
Annie Jennings, Counselors; Jane Olerton,
Secretary; Retta Olerton, Assistant Secretary;
May Wilson, Treasurer.  The Relief Society
have nineteen members enrolled, and the


Primary have twenty-five.  Both organizations
are in a prosperous condition at present.  I re-
main,
	Yous co-laborer in the cause of truth,
						S. M. BOYACK.
Nov. 8, 1886.
