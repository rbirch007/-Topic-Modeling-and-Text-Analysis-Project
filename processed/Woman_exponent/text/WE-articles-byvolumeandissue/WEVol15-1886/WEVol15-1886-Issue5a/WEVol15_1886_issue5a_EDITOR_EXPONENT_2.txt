EDITOR EXPONENT.
	As our voice has never been heard in your
paper, and as we think it is our turn to give
our ideas, we write, after having long talked of
it:
	Our associations are in a rather scattered
condition. We have ten fully organized, which,
as a general thing, are doing pretty well. We
have not visited as much as we would have
liked to, but there are so many inconveniences
that it is impossible at times to cross the
streams. However, our aim is to do good and
be a benefit to the young. Some say it is not a
woman’s place to preach the Gospel, but we
think, as sisters, that it is woman’s place to
know herself, to know her God, to be ac-
quainted with the principles of the Gospel, and
we certainly have the privilege to teach and
exhort all to do right, that by keeping the
commandments of God they may be blessed.
	We have a beautiful country up here; the
fields are beginning to look green, and the
health of the people is generally pretty good.
We have had a few deaths in our stake this
season, outnumbered many times, however, by
lots of fine babies.
				Your sisters,
				SUSANNAH R. LAWSON,
				MARY A. RICKS,
				MARY A. RAYMOND,
					Of the Stake Presidency.
