		ROSE ELIZABETH CLEVELAND.


"My dear," said an anxious wife to her hus-
band, who is running for office, "we must econo-
mize in every possible way."
	"I do economize," he replied.
	"Yes," she said, "you spend ten or fifteen
dollars a day in treating a lot of bar-room
loafers to beer and whisky just to get them to
vote for you. Do you call that economy?"
	"Certainly; that's political economy."—
Life.
