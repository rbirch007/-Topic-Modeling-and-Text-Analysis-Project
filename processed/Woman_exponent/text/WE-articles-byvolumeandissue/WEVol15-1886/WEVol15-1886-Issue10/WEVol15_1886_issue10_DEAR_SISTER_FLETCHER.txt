DEAR SISTER FLETCHER:

	     “May happy days be ever thine,
	     And heaven’s glory on thee shine;
	     And as the days glide swiftly past,
	     May joy attend thee to the last,"

is the sincere desire of the children.
	The officers of the Primary Association are
as follows: Sister Jane Cowan, President;
Sister Ann Cartwright and Miss Jennie Mor-
tensen, Counselors; Susie Keysor, Secretary;
Aggie McMurrin, Assistant Secretary; Sarah
McMurrin, Recording Secretary and Treas-
nrer.
	A short programme was rendered. consisting
of recitations, readings, songs, organ solos and
essays.  Prest. Jane Cowan and Counselors
each spoke a few words, expressing their desire
to do the best they could.
	Sisters Clawson and Wells, as well as Bro.
McMurrin, also spoke, giving the children
much good advice. exhorting them to be obedi-
ent and prayerful.  They also gave words of
counsel and encouragement to all present.
	Those who attended felt that they had in-
deed been well paid for so doing.  The meet-
ing was then adjourned for one week, at four
o’clock, and prayer was offered by Sister Claw-
son.
