REORGANIZATION.
	The Eighth Ward Primary met at the ward
meeting house, Sept. 21st, 1886, Prest. E. B.
Fletcher presiding.  Sisters Ellen C. Clawson
and Lydia Ann Wells were present; also Coun.
Joseph McMurrin.
	Sister E. B. Fletcher stated that the meet-
ing was for the purpose of reorganizing.  She,
through poor health, realizing her inability to
perform her duty in a creditable manner, of-
fered her resignation, which was accepted, and
a vote of thanks tendered her and her Coun-
selors, Miss Katie Spencer and Miss Lizzie
Chamberlain, for their efficient and much ap-
preciated labors in the past.
	Sister Fletcher was elected as President at
the organization, March 19th, 1879, and has,
since that time, been greatly loved and re-
spected by the children.  She felt it quite a
trial to part with the children, but circum-
stances were of such a nature that it was neces-
sary for her to resign, as she had so many du-
ties and labors to perform.
