EDITOR EXPONENT:

	Herewith I send you an account of the death of Sister
Annie Woods, at Woodruff, in this county, on the 15th of
last month. She was born in England, July 10th, 1837.
Her maiden name was Chandler. When sixteen years of
age she embraced the Gospel. She immigrated in 1853,
with her mother, two brothers and one sister. At New
Orleans her mother and youngest brother died of cholera.
With her remaining brother and sister she came to Utah.
In 1854 she married James T. Woods. Of their eight
children, only three reached maturity.
	She was Secretary and Teacher in the Relief Society of
the 14th Ward, Salt Lake City. In 1875 her husband was
called to Arizona, and she accompanied him thither. At
Brigham City, in said Territory, she was President of the
Young Ladies' Association. At Show Low she was Presi-
dent of the Relief Society, and was a Teacher in the Relief
Society at Woodruff. She sang regularly in the choirs
where she lived, possessing a voice of great richness and
purity of tone. Her husband was absent on a mission to 
England at the time of her demise.
				Very respectfully,
