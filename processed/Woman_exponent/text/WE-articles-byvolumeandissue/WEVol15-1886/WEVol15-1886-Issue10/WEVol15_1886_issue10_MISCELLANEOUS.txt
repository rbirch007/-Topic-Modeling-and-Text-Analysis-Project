MISCELLANEOUS.

	They are never alone that are accompanied
by noble thoughts.
	It is really of little consequence who we are
—it matters more what we are.
	Determine a person's worth by his actions,
not by what people say about him.
	Ideas are the seeds of thought, but they do
not produce flowers unless the soil where they
are sown is fertile.—Lady Btessingion.
	A good wife must be grave abroad, wise at 
home, patient to suffer, constant to love, friend-
ly to all, and provident for her household.—
Theophrastus.
	In every relation of life we must bear and
forbear; we must not expect perfection, and
each person should carry the cloak of charity
for the other—Rev. M. Huggins.
