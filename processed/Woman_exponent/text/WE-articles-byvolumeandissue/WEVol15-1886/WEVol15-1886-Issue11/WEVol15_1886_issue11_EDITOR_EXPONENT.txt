EDITOR EXPONENT
	I know that you will be delighted to learn
that the young of Chester met under the aus-
pices of dear Aunt Emma and her Counselor
Sister Amelia Allred in the capacity of a 
Primary Fair. On the 23rd again came troop-
ing this regiment of the little folks of Zion
obedient to the call, and with songs, recitations, 
speeches and good words, the day passed in the 
midst of joy to young and old present.
	To particularize would be tedious and es-
pecially where all was excellent. Specimens
of the Jack knife, the culinary department,
the garden, and the needle truly graced the
tables and the occasion.
	These are red lettered days for the young
and fraught with good.
