A NEW FASHIONED GIRL.

She'd great and varied knowledge, picked up at
	   a female college, of quadratics, hydrostatics,
	   and pneumatics very vast;
She was stuffed with erudition, as you stuff a
	   leather cushion, all the elogies of the col-
	   leges, and the knowledge of the past.

She had studied the old lexicons of Peruvians and
	   Mexicans, their theology, anthropology, and
	   geology, o'er and o'er.
She knew all the forms and features of the pre-
	   historic creatures—ichthyosaurus, plesiosau-
	   rus, megalosaurus, and many more.

She’d describe the ancient Tuscans, and the
	   Basques and the Etruscans, the griddles
	   and their kettles, and their victuals that they
	   gnawed;
She’d discuss the learned charmer, the theology
	   of Brahma, and the scandals of the Van-
	   dals, and the sandals that they trod.

She knew all the mighty giants, and the master
	   minds of science, all the learning that was
	   turning in the burning mind of man;
But she couldn’t prepare a dinner for a gaunt
	   and hungry sinner, or get up a decent sup-
	   per for her poor, voracious papa, for she
	   never was constructed on the old domestic
	   plan
							Hearth and Hall.
