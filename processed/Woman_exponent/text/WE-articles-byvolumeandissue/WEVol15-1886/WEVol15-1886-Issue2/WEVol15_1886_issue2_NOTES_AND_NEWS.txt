NOTES AND NEWS.

	Mary Anderson is to have her portrait
painted in character, as "Salammbo," by Alma
Tadema.

	Miss Mary F. Eastman will speak on the
Indian Qustion at the coming meeting of the
Progressive Friends, at Longwood, Penn.

	The Queen Regent of Spain will maintain
and educate at her private expense the chil-
dren of those who perished in the recent tor-
nado at Madrid.

	One of the newly-appointed directors of the
People's Bank in Stockholm is a women.

	The publishers of Miss Cleveland's book say
that it has had a larger sale than was se-
cured for Queen Victoria's book in England.

	Mrs. Helen M. Gougar sailed Saturday May
29, for Ireland to study the Irish question
among the people. She expects to return
about September 2.

	The sixteenth aniversary of Dicken's de-
cease was celebrated at Boffin's Bower (1031
Washington Street,) Boston, June 9.
