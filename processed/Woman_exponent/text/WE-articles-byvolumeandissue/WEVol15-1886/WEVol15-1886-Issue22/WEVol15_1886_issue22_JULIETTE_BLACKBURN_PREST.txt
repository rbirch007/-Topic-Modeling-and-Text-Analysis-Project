		JULIETTE BLACKBURN, Prest.


Mrs. Logan gives a striking instance of the
discrimination made in the pay of government
clerks on account of sex. She says: "No lady
is allowed the same salary in the higher grade
of clerkships, no  matter how well she fills the
higher salaried desks, and many of them are 
to-day in those positions, but do not receive
the pay. As an illustration, it has happened
that in cases of illness or disability of men
clerks, their wives performed the duty of their
husbands, drawing in their name the salary as
if they had performed the work. In one case
the wife kept up her husband's desk for three
years, during his illness, receiving the $1,800
per annum, and thereby supporting the family;
but from the day of his death, though she con-
tinued the same work, her salary was nine
hundred!"
