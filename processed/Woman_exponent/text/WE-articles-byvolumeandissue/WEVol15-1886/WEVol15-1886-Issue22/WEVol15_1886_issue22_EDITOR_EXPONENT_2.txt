EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We thought, perhaps, a small contribution
on our part might be acceptable to your valu-
able paper.
	Our Relief Society is steadily plodding along,
and as a general thing a good feeling prevails.
On the 29th of March last the sisters got up a 
party to surprise our beloved President, Sister
Adelia Kimball, on her fifty-ninth birthday.
Tables were set in the school house, and a good
assembly met.
	Sister Kimball was lured from her home on 
some pretext, and, accompanied by her Coun-
selors, Sisters Mills and Gay, entered the
house; she was greatly surprised and deeply
affected. On being seated, an autograph album
was presented to her as a token of friendship
from the sisters of the Relief Society. It was
a very happy gathering, and pleasure beamed
on every countenance.
	After all had partaken of the good things of
the earth, the tables were removed, and in the
evening songs, dancing and recitations were in-
dulged in, the ladies holding the dancing num-
bers. The invitation had been extended to the
brethren, and the house was well filled. At
the close, Sister Kimball expressed her grati-
tude to the brethren and sisters for the respect
they had shown her, and all went home feeling
the time had been well spent.
		ADELAIDE PAXTON.
	Kanosh, Millard Co., April 3rd, 1887.
