EDITOR EXPONENT:

	Thinking, perhaps, a few lines from this 
isolated place might be received with welcome
I thought I would drop you a letter.
	We are few in number but are trying to
live our religion. Our Relief Society was or-
ganized, Jan. 7th 1884, with Sister Elizabeth
H. Swapp as President, and Sisters Martha
Williams and Martha C. Swapp as Counselors.
Sister Williams being called to Old Mexico
Sister Gertrude Reynolds was called to fill her 
place.  We are trying to do all the good we
can and as ltttle harm; though our means are
limited and we are in  a scattered condition.
Our meetings are generally pretty well at-
tended and a good spirit prevails.
	We have been called upon to part with one 
of our members, Sister Eveline Lee, who de-
parted this life the 2nd of this month. She
was a faithful member and Latter-day Saint
and bore a strong testimony to the truth of 
this work.  She leaves a husband and three 
small children and a host of friends to mourn 
her loss. The EXPONENT is a welcome visitor 
and we look anxiously for its coming.
	Ever praying for the welfare of Zion, I 
will close. 
		Your sisters in the Gospel,
			ELIZABETH H. SWAPP, Prest.
