EDITOR WOMAN'S EXPONENT:
	Following closely on the receipt of the lovely
expression of respectful remembrance from my
dearly beloved sisters of Provo, a notice of which
appeared in your last issue, on the morning of my
eighty-third aniversary, I received a heart-cheer-
ing and soul-enlivening telegram, which, like the
Provo cake, speaks for itself what its words are
altogether inadequate to express.


My highest ambition in life, has been, and is,
to be worthy of the favor of God, and the love and
confidence of the Latter-day Saints. To me it
is wealth.						E. R. S. S.
			COPY OF THE TELEGRAM.
						Ogden City, Jan 21, 1887.
