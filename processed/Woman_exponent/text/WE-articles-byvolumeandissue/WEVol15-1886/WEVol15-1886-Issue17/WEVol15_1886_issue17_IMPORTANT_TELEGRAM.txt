IMPORTANT TELEGRAM.
	Washington, D. C., January; 21.—The support-
ers of the anti-Mormon bill are much concerned
at the interest which they say Mr. W. L. Scott is
manifesting in the bill, and in the efforts which he
is making, as they understand it, to induce the
President to veto it. Mr. Scott was active while
the bill was under discussion in the House, to
have certain amendments adopted, which should
preserve what he called the rights of the Mormons
and which should provide for the submitting a
constitution to them forbidding polygamy. Chair-
man Tucker, who has charge of the bill, and who
made a speech so strong in support of it that the
anti-Mormons have telegraphed here for 3,000
copies of it has said to some of the friends of the
bill that he does not fear the influence of any man
with the President in opposition to the bill, except
that of Mr. Scott.
