	BY GRANDMOTHER.

	Now, I suppose you will think, what has
grandmother got to say now; I should think
she had said enough by this time; but that is
all a mistake; she has got a great deal to say
yet, for I love the little ones that God has
given to the daughters of Zion, for bright and
shining lights in the kingdom of God, and I
want to see every mother noble and true, and
teach the little ones noble principles--to be
virtuous, honest and industrious, for the more
the mind is kept employed, the easier children
can be governed. How the mother's heart
will rejoice to see her children grow up without
a spot or blemish on their pure and good name.
Oh! how different to see them take the other 
course, and bring their parents' grey hairs
down in sorrow to the grave; and when it is
too late they can look back and see where
they might have done better. The time to sow
the good seed is in their infancy.
	Some will say, "Oh! let them come to the 
years of understanding before you teach them
too much;" but you leave them that long to
themselves, and they think then that they
know too much to be taught, and they will
have no respect for their parents, nor for the
aged of either sex. But the mother who
teaches her children correct principles, and
rears them in the fear and admonition of the
Lord, and teaches them to respect and honor
old age, they will respect their parents and be
good children; they will be loved by everybody
and they will make good Latter-day Saints,
and be good citizens.
					CLARISSA WILHELM.
From the Advocate manuscript paper Erastus.
