		E. R. UDALL,

	Dearest sister, we have loved you,
	We have loved you fond and true,
	And it fills our hearts with sadness,
		That we have to part with you;
	But a crown of royal brightness,
		Waits to deck your honored brow,
	And a robe of purest whiteness
		Waits your body to endow.

	Where your friends await to meet you,
	And your husband waits to greet you,
		For you have been good and true;
	You have been so kind and patient,
		Ever laboring for the poor,
	And for this the Lord has loved you—
		May you rest in peace secure.

	Dearest sister, we shall miss you,
		We shall miss your presence here,
	But we hope again to meet you—
		Farewell, sister, sister dear.

			S. K.
