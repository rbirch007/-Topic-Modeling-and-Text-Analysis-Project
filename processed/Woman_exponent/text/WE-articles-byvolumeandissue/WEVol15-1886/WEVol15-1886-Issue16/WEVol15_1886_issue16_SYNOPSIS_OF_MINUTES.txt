SYNOPSIS OF MINUTES.

	Semi-monthly meeting held Oct. 16th 1886,
Coun. D. H. Young presiding. After the
reading of minutes of previous meeting,
minutes of the P. A. of the 21st, 20th and 15th
wards, and Y. L. A. of the 21st, 2nd, and 3rd
wards were read; and Sister Zina then addressed
the meeting. Said, "This is the day that the
day the prophets foretold," and thought we
would have grace to stand if we lived near to
the Lord. Referred tenderly to the decease of
Sister Hannah T. King, and said, "Our older
sisters are gradually passing away, one by one,


but we should not  mourn  for  them, for,
'Blessed are the dead that die in the Lord.'
Sister King has gone triumphant and victori-
ous, and has left behind her a tangible record
of her life and labors." After many interest-
ing remarks, the meeting was given into the
hands of the sisters.
	Sister Harriet Woodbury spoke in the gift
of tongues, and the interpretation was given
by Sister Zina.
	Sister Sinah Bishop bore testimony that the
interpretation given had been dictated by the
Spirit of the Lord, and made many excellent
remarks.
	Sister Dyer spoke a few words in tongues,
and the interpretation was given by Sister
Bishop.
	Sisters Emily Stevenson, Grix and Morten-
son bore faithful testimonies. Sisters Evans,
Watmough and Brown each spoke, bearing
testimony and exhorting to faithfulness. Sis-
ter Mary Silver made a few remarks about
Sister King, and recited one of her poems.
	Sister L. G. Richards, after a few timely re-
marks, asked that the sisters would unite their
faith and offer up a prayer for our afflicted sis-
ter, Sister Wilkins. Sister Zina asked Sister
Richards to be mouth in prayer, and the sisters
all joined in the amen.
	Sister Zina then spoke a few words, and the
meeting was adjourned for two weeks. Closed
with singing Doxology, and benediction was
pronounced by Sister M. Snow.
