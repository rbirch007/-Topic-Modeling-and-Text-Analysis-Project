I AM WEARY. 

I am weary of the sin
	Of the pain, the care, the woe, 
That increasing come to greet me, 
	As the years that ebb and flow. 

Weary of the boasted pleasures, 
	Lurking 'mid the wild world's throng, 
Where the only queen is fashion,
	And the only law is wrong. 

Weary of the false ambition
	With the low, deceitful shoals, 
Overhung with crowns' of greatness,
	That have wrecked so many souis.

But through life's fleeting journey
	May we never, never know
Of the wondrous "Balm of Gilead,"
	Healing hearts of pain and woe. 

Far beyond there is a haven, 
	Golden sunset! peaceful, blest;—
Where the soul long tried and weary, 
	Seeks at last eternal rest.
