RE-ORGANIZATION
OF THE RELIEF SOCIETY OF THE 17TH WARD, 
	MAY 18, 1886. 

	On account of the death of our loved Presi-
dent, Mrs. Marinda N. Hyde, we were obliged to 
re-organize. 
	Bishop John Tingey, with his Counselor A. E. 
Hyde, assisted by Mrs. M. I. Horne, effected the
following organization. 
	Mrs. B. W. Smith was chosen President, with 
Mrs. Julia Pack, as first and Mrs. Harriet A. 
Preston as second counselors.  The secretary and
treasurer remained the same as heretofore. 
	Bishop Tingey added his blessing and hoped
the new organization would be as united and
faithful as the old one had been. 
	LYDIA D. ALDER, Secretary. 

	P. S. The Bishop and his counselor also set
apart the President, Mrs. Bathsheba W. Smith
and her First Counselor Mrs. Julia Pack. Mrs. 
Harriet A. Preston, the Second Counselor, not


being present. Remarks were made by Sister M. I.
Horne and others suited to the occasion. At a 
subsequent meeting in consequence of the need
of assistance, Mrs Julia Howe, the treasurer, hav-
ing gone to England on a visit and Mrs. Lydia
Alder, the Secretary being detained at home in
delicate health, Mrs. Emma Bull was appointed
assistant secretary and treasurer pro tem.—Ed.
