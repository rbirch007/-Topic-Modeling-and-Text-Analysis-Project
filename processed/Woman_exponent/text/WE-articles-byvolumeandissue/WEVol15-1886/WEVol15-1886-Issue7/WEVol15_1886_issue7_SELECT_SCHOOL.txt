SELECT SCHOOL.

	On the 6th of September 1886, Dr. Godfrey
Hessel of this city intends opening a select school
for special branches of instruction, in classes at
No. 56 N., 2nd West St. His terms are for
two lessons a week, 4.00 a month in English
Grammar, Arithmetic, Geometry, Latan, German,
French, Italian, Spanish, Organ, Piano, Guitar
and Vocal Music. Private lessons in languages
$1,00 a lesson.  Music lessons at the residences
of pupils two lessons a week $6.00 a month.
	Mercantile Arithmetic, Correspondence and
Bookkeeping, Mondays Wednesdays and Fridays
from 8 to 9 p.m. $6.00 a month.	
Dr. Hessel seems thoroughly qualified for the
work so far as education and ability are con-
cerned. He is a member of the Church, having been
converted and embraced the Gospel in New York
City through the teachings of Brother Hart, Emi-
gration agent.  He is the author of some of the
articles in the Utah Journal that have attracted
considerable attention.  We wish him success in
his labors to instruct the youth of Zion.  There
may be a number of young people who cannot
devote sufficient time to study for attend the uni-
versity who might avail themselves of this oppor-
tunity of Dr. Hessel's select school or private
teaching.
