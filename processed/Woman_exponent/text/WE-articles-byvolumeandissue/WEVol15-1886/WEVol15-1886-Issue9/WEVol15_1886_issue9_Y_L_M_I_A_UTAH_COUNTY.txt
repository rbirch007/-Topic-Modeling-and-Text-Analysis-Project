Y. L. M. I. A., UTAH COUNTY.

	Report of the meetings held by the presi-
deney of the Y. L. M. I. Associations of the
Utah Stake of Zion in the southern part of
the county.
	Minutes  of  the  Y.  L.  M.  I.  A. of
Springville, held in the meeting house, Sept.
13, 1886 with President Hattie Daily presid-
ing.  After the opening exercises, President
Jennie Tanner advised the young ladies to
preserve their health,the greatest blessing aside
from virtue; also to spend their time in reading
such literature as would refine and tend to
their advancement.
	Mrs. M. J. Tanner spoke of the beauties of
Zion, and closed by advising all to guard well
their actions, treat strangers with civility, not
to be given to forwardness and intimacy as
they would be led astray.
	Miss Evlyn Billings advised them to profit
by the instructions they had received and to
prize their virtue, a jewel that when once lost
could never be restored, and one false step
taken would lead them to destruction.
	Similar meetings were held throughout the
Southern part of the County.
	Spanish Fork, Tuesday, Sept. 14, President
Banks presiding; Salem, 3 o,clock p.m.,
Sarah Sabin presiding; Payson 8 o'clock p.m.
Ida Coombs, President; Santaquin Sept. 15,
Emma Wall presiding; Goshen, Sept. 16, M.
A. Price presiding.
	The visitors were entertained by the various
wards in a cordial and hospitable manner, and
found the Associations in a favorable condi-
tion although some of their meetings were not
as well attended in the summer as the winter
season, circumstances being such as to require
them to be elsewhere, yet they were energetic
in taking part in furthering the progress of
the work.
