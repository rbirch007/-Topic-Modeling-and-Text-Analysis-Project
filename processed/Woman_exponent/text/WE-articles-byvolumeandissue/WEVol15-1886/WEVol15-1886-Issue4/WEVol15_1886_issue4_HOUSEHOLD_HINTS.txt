HOUSEHOLD HINTS.

	Every one has a cure for sore throat, but simple remedies
appear to be most effectual.  A gargle of salt and water
is used by many, but a little alum and honey dissolved in
sage tea is better.  Cloths wrung out of hot water and
applied to the neck, changing as often as they begin to
cool, have potency for removing inflamation more than
anything we have ever tried.  This should be kept up for a
number of hours; during the evening is usually the most
convenient time for applying the remedy. 

   Ice Cream.—Dissolve five teaspoonfuls of corn starch or
arrowroot in a teacupful of milk.  Add to it the whites of
three eggs well frothed, and the yelk of one well beaten.
Sweeten with loaf sugar, and boil half a gallon of new milk.
As soon as it begins to boil, pour it in small quantities over
the mixture of eggs and starch until about half the milk is
taken from the kettle.  Then pour all back into the kettle,
and stir it a few minutes.  After it cools, add one quart of
rich cream; flavor to your taste, and freeze the cream.
