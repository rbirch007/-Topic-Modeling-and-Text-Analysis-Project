MRS. E. B. WELLS:
	Knowing they are not designed for publication, 
I take the responsibility of complying with your
request, and send you for the EXPONENT a copy 
of one of a series of descriptive letters addressed 
to his family by my brother in the  Utah Peni-
tentiary.  Without the preceding one, of which 
I have not the copy, the opening of this letter 
seems abrupt.  The style adopted is amusing. 
							E. R. S. S. 
	   ONE OF A SERIES OF LETTES.
