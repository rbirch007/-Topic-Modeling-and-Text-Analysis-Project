		BY IDA A. AHLBORN.

He was haunted night and day
	By a spirit clad in white,
That no exorcist could lay.

So he wrote a magic rhyme,
	Gave the subtle spirit form,
Sent it forth to war with Time.

He is freed, but this is done.
	Now the spirit in the song 
Haunts a thousand for the one.
						—Traveller.
