REMEMBRANCE.

Will my boy remember his mother,
	When, fresh in the dawning light, 
He springs from his bed of slumber,
	Prepared for the brief day’s flight?
Will he think of the morning greeting,
	The smile, the blessing, the kiss,
The happy home-talk and the children—
	Will he think of them only to miss?

Will my boy remember his mother,
	When gone to his school or his play,
When, over his bright head is flitting
	The hours of youth's happy day?
Will he think of the rides we have taken
	Through meadow and field all alone,
Our quiet talk, drifting from flowers
	Up even to God’s holy throne?

Will my boy remember his mother,
	When down the orb of the day,
Soft twilight is drawing her eyelids
	And folding her labors away,
Will he think of the romps in the garden,
	The swing ’neath the old apple tree,
Will he think of the prayers he has offered
	So oft at his fond mother's knee?

Will my boy remember his mother,
	When into his youthful ears
Is poured the soft voice of the tempter—
	Will he then remember her tears
When invited to smoke or drink liquor,
	Or passing some bright gambling den,
And companions persuade him to enter,
	Will my darling remember me then?

Oh, let him forget me at evening,
	Or during the day’s busy hours,
While reading, or romping, or singing,
	Or riding ’neath sweet country bowers,
Forget me in joy or in sorrow,
	But always remember me when
Assailed by the pleadings of Satan,
	My darling, remember me then!
							HOMESPUN.
[handwritten text on the right side] Susan J. Gates
