UNDER THE APPLE TREES.

My love, this orchard shade is full
Of breathing thoughts of thee;
'Neath every overreaching bough
Thy face I almost see.
'Twas here we met and sweetly talked,
'Twas here we came and gladly walked,
In the shade of these apple trees.

Thy footstep seemeth still to fall
Upon my listening ear;
That same dear voice my name to call,
I almost, almost hear.
O throbbing heart, one moment still;
Cease, breeze, my eager ears to fill
By rustling these apple trees.

Thy welcome presence liveth near,
Just only out of reach;
I catch, inaudible, but clear,
The spirit of thy speech;
I stand beside thee once again,
Apart from sorrow, fear or pain,
As I lean on this apple tree.

The whispering leaves to me repeat
The precious words you said;
The hopes we cherished, pure and sweet,
Their olden radiance shed;
And happy memories wake and smile,
As here I rest a little while
In the arms of this apple tree.

I know the angels hovered near
This Eden bower of rest,
And smiled, and lowly bent to hear
The holy thoughts expressed,
And treasured them, my king, my love,
To place on record bright above,
As we talked ’neath these apple trees.

I know, whatever may betide
In this world dim and low,
Our souls shall yet be satisfied
With love like a river’s flow;
And so good night, my love, till when
Elsewhere or here we meet again.
Good night, ye dear apple trees.
