DEAR EDITOR:

	The kingdom of heaven is for those who
seek for it; not many rich or noble, but the
humble and faithful and pure in heart. We
as a people are of the tribe of Ephraim, prone
to wander from the fold of God, but, oh! let us
return; now is the trying day, and we should
be willing to show our colors. Jesus said, “If
ye will not forsake all and life itself for the
Gospel and my name’s sake, ye are not worthy
of me.”
	Let us turn from our follies. Israel often
worshiped idols and went astray; are we not of
the same lineage? Let us not worship our
houses, lands, clothing or any property, for our
God is a “jealous God, visiting the sins of the
fathers on the children,” but if they walk not
in the path of their fathers he will turn away
His anger, reads the 18th chapter of Ezekiel.
Oh, what comfort to the erring ones! Let us
not be discouraged, but try more earnestly to
live our religion,keeping all the commandments,
observing the Sabbath day and fast days, for
there are great promises to those who do so.
Read the 57th chapter of Isaiah, and you
may understand the promises of the Lord
to the faithful; also the 3rd chapter of Malachi,
you will see the promises of the Lord to those
who pay their tithes and offerings, and those
who assemble to worship their God shall be re-
membered as a man will remember and reward


his own son that serveth him. “He will be
mine;” saith the Lord, “when I will make up
my jewels.”
	Let us read and study our Bible, Book of
Mormon and other Church works, that we may
be prepared when the Bridegroom shall come,
is the prayer of
			Your sister,
						E. JENSEN.
	Orderville.
