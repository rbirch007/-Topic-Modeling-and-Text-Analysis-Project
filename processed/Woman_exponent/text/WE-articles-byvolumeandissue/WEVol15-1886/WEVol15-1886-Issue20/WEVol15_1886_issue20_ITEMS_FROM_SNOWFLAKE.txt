		ITEMS FROM SNOWFLAKE.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Thinking that a few notes from this little
settlement might be worthy of room in our
valuable paper, we take pleasure in sending
them. It is seldom we are heard from, but we
are trying to bravely fight the battles of life,
and prove ourselves valiant to the cause of
Christ on the earth.
	As a stake, we are having a rest now from
persecution, but we know not how long peace
may prevail, and we deeply sympathize with
those of our brethren and sisters who are not
so favored, but we know all will be well with
those who are not faint-hearted nor weary in
well-doing.
	Our Relief Society in this ward is doing very
well; we try not to let any go needy or desti-
tute; we strive to visit and comfort the sick, and
soothe the grief of those who are called to
mourn.
	We are very thankful for the recent visit of
Dr. E. S. Barney to our land. She came as a
stranger to us, but when she went away we felt
she was our friend and sister. She spent her
whole time, while here, teaching us things for
our good, and we shall ever hold her in grate-
ful remembrance for the good she has done us.
	We have had a pleasant, open winter, and
recently a rain and snow storm, which moist-
ened up the earth nicely for putting in crops.
Our brethren are busy now, fixing their water
ditches, and making a resevoir to store water
in for the dry part of the seassn. With the
blessings of God upon us, we hope to raise our
own bread, and not have to import as much as
we have had to do in past years.
	We prize our little paper very highly, as it
comes to us laden with good, and we pray that
it may always be a comfort in Zion.
	Praying for the welfare of Zion and the
spread of truth in the earth, we remain,
		Your sisters in the Gospel covenant,
						MARY J. WEST,
						LUCY H. FLAKE,
