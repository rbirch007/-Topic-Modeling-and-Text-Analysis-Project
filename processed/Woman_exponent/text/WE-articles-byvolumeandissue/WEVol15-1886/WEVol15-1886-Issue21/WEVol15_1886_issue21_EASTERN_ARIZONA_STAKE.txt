	EASTERN ARIZONA STAKE.
	The quarterly conference of the Relief So-
ciety of this Stake was held at Snowflake,
Friday, March 11th, 1887, commencing at 2
p.m., Mrs. Emma S. Smith presiding. After
singing, prayer was offered by Bro. Oscar

[Column 2]
Mann. Singing again. Verbal reports of ten
branches were given by their respective presi-
dents.
	Prest. Smith expressed herself much pleased
with the good attendance, and was thankful
that we were permitted to meet under such
favorable circumstances, and also pleased with
the good reports.
    Sister Sarah Driggs made a few encouraging
remarks, and made some suggestions in regard
to conducting the working meetings.
	The statistical and financial report was then
read.
    Coun. Frances White said her prayers were
continually offered for the unity of the Saints.
Spoke of the good the Society of St. Johns
had done in aiding the poor, and urged all to
be diligent in keeping the commandments of
God.
    Sister Mary J. Minnerly felt that although
we have many difficulties to labor under, we
are continually being blest and prospered. De-
sired the faith and prayers of the sisters in
her behalf, and spoke upon the necessity of
children attending the Primary.
	Prest. Emma S. Smith exhorted all to be
faithful in the discharge of their duties. Sug-
gested that each Society have one of their 
members appointed to take charge of the busi-
ness, in order to lighten the burden of the
president. “Mothers should teach their child-
ren the principles of truth and honesty, and
should seek to guard them against infidelity.”
	Conference adjourned for three; months.
Singing. Benediction by Bro. L. H. Hatch.
	DELLA FISH, Secretary.
