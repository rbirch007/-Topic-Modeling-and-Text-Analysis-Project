SYNOPSIS OF MINUTES.

	Regular meeting held in the Fourteenth
Ward Assembly Rooms, Saturday, Oct. 2nd,
1886, Prest. M. I. Horne presiding. After the
usual exercises, minutes of the last meeting,
held in August (conference intervening) were
read and accepted; also minutes of the P. A.
of the 5th, 20th and 8th wards; also minutes of
the Y. L. M. I. A. of the 8th and Mill Creek
Wards.
	Prest. Horne then addressed the meeting for
a short time in her usual motherly and instruc-
tive way, after which Sister Eliza R. Snow
Smith occupied a short time. Said Latter-day
Saints ought never to look sad; that God would
overrule everything that is not for our good,
for He loves every one of us. Spoke of the
weaknesses we all have to overcome in our-
selves, and the progress we must make; that
there was no standing still in this kingdom,
but going onward; God will have a tried peo-
ple, and those that are of pure minds and con-
trite hearts. Sister Eliza made mention of
the family of Bro. Jacob Hamblin, who died
lately, and thought it would be well for the
sisters to give a trifle to help them.
	Coun. M. W. Snow spoke of the pleasant
time she had had recently visiting in the set-
tlements; thought the people were improving,
and rejoiced that she was a Latter-day Saint,
and associated with this people. Gave a few
items concerning the family of Bro. Jacob
Hamblin, and ended by praying that we might
all fill our missions. 
	Sister E. S. Taylor spoke of the necessity of
meeting together and getting comforted and
strengthened; that the Lord knows how to
mete out our trials, and will give us strength
according to our day. Exhorted all to set a
good example.
	Sister Emma Terry of Draper, Sister Julia
Pack, Sister M. Brown and Sister Nellie Cole-
brook of this city, each occupied a few minutes,
speaking upon interesting subjects to the edifica-
tion of all present.
	Meeting adjourned for two weeks with sing-
ing, and benediction by Sister M. E. Kim-
ball.
