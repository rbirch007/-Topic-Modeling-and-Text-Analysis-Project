	She was a devoted mother, teaching her child-
ren the laws of the Gospel from their earliest
youth. She leaves three sons and two daughters
each of these faithful members in the Church,
numerous grand-children, relatives and friends
to mourn her loss.
	She obeyed all the principles of the Gospel as far
as they were revealed to her. She was a mother
to the motherless, performing a noble part. All
through her long illness she was tenderly cared
for by her son, Brigham F. and his wife Sister
Margaret Price
	Our dear sister was a faithful member of the
Relief Society. She was always in attendance
whenever her health would permit. She was a
very fluent, intelligent speaker. She was firm in
her convictions for the advancement of women, a
supporter of the EXPONENT and all good works
in that direction.
	She has finished her work in this probation
and gone to meet those of her loved ones whom
she has officiated for in the Temple of the Lord.
	The funeral services were held at the family
residence, the house was filled to overflowing
with sympathetic relatives and friends. Bishop
J. C. Hanson and Counselors were present; many
consoling remarks were made by the presiding
officers of the ward, each bore testimony to the
faithfulness of Sister Price. Beautiful flowers
covered the casket, the hymn “O my Father,”
was sung by the choir. A large cortege followed
the remains to the ward cemetery. “Blessed
are the dead that die in the Lord for their reward
is sure.”
			ELIZABETH HILL, Pres’t.
