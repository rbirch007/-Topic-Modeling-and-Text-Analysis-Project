AURELIA

REST dear one rest! And O, may it be sweet
In the land of the blest thy Savior to meet!
Thy pathway of life was troubledand sad,
May this “Happy New Year,” make thy tremb-
	ling heart glad.

O, hard is the journey that leads to the height;
But o’er the bleak mountains the peak is in sight;
There too to the traveler green valleys appear, 
Where the tired feet rest, and the Father is near.

The streamlets are bubbling a beautiful song;
And the wind of the South plays the tall trees
	among;
And there where the skies are sunny and blue,
There is rest for the weary, and happiness too.

Bright song birds are warbling their rarest notes
Like vespers at eve, the sweet melody floats;
From the crystal clear fountains the pearl drops 
	are falling, 
So faint on the ear like angel’s voice calling.

O, there is the rest most soothing and sweet;
And there the dear Lord his ransomed ones greet;
O, they are not lost but forever more blest
To all whom He loves, He giveth sweet rest.

Then ring out ye bells! this Happy New Year,
And tho’ on thy page hath fallen a tear,
Yet spotless and fair it will fade from the sight,
Fast melting in mist to the land that is light.

Rest, O there is joy when, the wanderer comes
	home,
Though foot-sore and weary breathing many a
	moan;
Yet the Father who notes the wee sparrow’s fall,
Outstretches His arms to gather us all.

Then sweet be thy rest this Happy New Year !
Though stand we in sorrow around thy young
	bier;
Yet in the fair mansions where the glorious ones
	dwell,
At the feet of the Father you say “ It is well.”
   A member of our Sundav School class who
died New Year’s Day, 1897.
