A SONNET. 

How grand how beautiful is old age!
The crowning glory of a life well spent,
That looking back can see no rent,
But noble effort wr t on ev’ry page;
Truly it is a noble heritage,
That lovely,gentle grace which time has lent.
Revealing the softest beauty of each lineament.
’Tis fitting that to such a worthy sage,
The young should come for wisdom,
Those of riper years for love and sympathy,
The burdened and oppressed for consolation,
The poor and needy for sweet charity,
Oh, what a glorious consummation !
When the twilight cometh on.
							RUTH M. FOF.
	Written for Sister Maria W. Wilcox' birthday,
June, 17 1897.
