	Mrs. Annie S. Wells gave a delightful
party on Friday afternoon June 18, at her
pretty cottage in the 20th Ward of this
city. The rooms were fragrant with roses
and carnations of which the decorations
largely consisted, roses of many varieties
and artistically arranged. The conversa-
tion was cheerful and entertaining as it
generally is when so many charming wo-
men come together socially. Besides her
several Wells’ aunties, there were a num-
ber of the Wells family married and un-
married, Mrs. Isabel M. Sears, Mrs.
Frances Wells, Mrs. Abbie Young, Miss
Kate Wells, Mrs. Helena Wells, Mrs. Joseph-
ine Wells, Mrs. May W. Whitney, Miss
Emeline Wells, Mrs. Emily W Grant,
Mrs. Annette W. Culmer, Miss Lyde Wells,
Mrs. Annie W. Cannon, Mrs. Clara W.
Hedges, Mrs. Lillie Y. Wells, Mrs. Susie
R. Wells, Mrs. Edna W. Sloan. It was a
very happy afternoon, taking all in all
though some were absent on account of
illness that would have made the company
more complete, and it would have been
more gratifying. The repast was very
delicious and served on little tables in the
parlors, assisted by some dainty little girls
in white, Dessie Grant and Alice Wells
who were very polite and attentive to the
guests and made every one feel at home.
	The hostess may be assured that all
went away with the impression that she
knew just how to make visitors enjoy
themselves. These family parties are a
delightful social feature and promote union
and love in the family, than which there
can be nothing more desirable among those
connected by the ties of kindred.
