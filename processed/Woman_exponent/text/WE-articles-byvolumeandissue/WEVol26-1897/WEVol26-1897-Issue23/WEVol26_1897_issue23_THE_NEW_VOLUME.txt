THE NEW VOLUME.  

	How glad we should be if we could say
the new volume commences under auspici-
ous circumstances or with a hopeful out-
look, but unfortunately it is not so, and yet
if the women of the community could only
realize the value of having a paper of their
own it might be very different. We know
there is always a faithful few who are prompt
in payments,and who would rather do with-
out something else so that they might help
sustain a home periodical. All our sisters
must be aware by this time, having been
frequently reminded through these columns,
that there is great need of extra exertion
on their part just now in the midst of finan-
cial depression, to maintain their own
journal. 
	We will not recount all the disadvantages
that have brought about the present need
of money, (but they are all apparent to the
editor, yet would not look well in print)
but we would urge upon the sisters in the
Church, and especially in the Relief Society,
that great organization of women that was
founded more than fifty-five years ago, that
they strive with greater diligence to increase
the circulation of their own official organ; the 
President of every branch of the Relief
Society should consider it her duty to ap-
point a committee to canvas the ward in
which she presides, and to urge upon the
sisters the necessity there is of sustaining
the paper in the interest of women and of 
the Society at home and abroad.
	One sister from a distance writing recent-
ly to the editor, said, that “the EXPONENT
was like a light set on a hill.”
	Sister Young, the President of this great 
and grand Relief Society, has said over
and over and has published over her own
signature that no woman is expected to 
preside over the Relief Society who does
not have a copy of the paper which is the
official organ. If the President herself is
not able to pay for a copy, then the money
should be raised in the Society for this pur-
pose 
	Remember now is the beginning of the
twenty-sixth volume of the paper, and it is
time to renew subscriptions and for new sub-
scribers to commence to take it. We do
not believe any sister who will do so will 
ever miss the price of the subscription; at
any rate the editor will endeavor to do her 
part, and the reader must recollect that she
uses the best material that is furnished and
strives editorially to maintain a high stand-
ard of purity and moral excellence.
