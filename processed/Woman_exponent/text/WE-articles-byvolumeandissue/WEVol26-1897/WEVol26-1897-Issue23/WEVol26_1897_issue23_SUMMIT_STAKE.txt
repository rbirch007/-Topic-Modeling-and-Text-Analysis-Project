SUMMIT STAKE.
	MINUTES of the annual Conference of
Summit Stake Relief Society held in Hoyts-
ville meeting house, May 4th 1897, com-
mencing at 10 o’clock a. m., President
Mary J. Atwood presiding. Meeting
opened by singing “The happy day has
rolled on;” prayer by Elder James Wools-
tenhulme; singing “Sweet is the work
my God my King.” Present on the stand
were Counselors Ann Cluff and Ruth M.
Pack, President W. W. Cluff of the Stake,
President C. Y. Pack and others. Minutes
of previous Conference read and accepted,
roll called and nine presidents responded.
	President Atwood said “I am pleased to
say we have the annual reports from every
branch in the Stake, we number seventeen
branches, three of them being in Wyom-
ing.” Statistical and financial reports read,
also the Stake report, verbal reports were
given by the several presidents all the
branches being in good order and the sis-
ters that attended to their duties were unit-
ed and readily responded to every call and
were striving to live their religion, several
of the branches being small and the mem-
bers living far apart.
	President Atwood said, “I am well
pleased with the reports, and if I should
judge by your looks I should say you all
feel well. I love to see sisters feel well, it
shows you are doing your duty and by
doing right we have the good spirit to
help us.”
	President Elmina S. Taylor and Sister
Emma Goddard, one of her aids in the
Y. L. M. I. A, have arrived to attend the
Conference of that association tomorrow.
They will address us this afternoon, this
Conference will adjourn until 2 o’clock.”
Singing “Jesus once of humble birth.”
Benediction by Bishop Sargent of Hoyts-
ville.
