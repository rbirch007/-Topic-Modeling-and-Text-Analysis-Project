THE SAVIOR’S WORDS.

	AT the birth of our Savior a great part of
the world was subject to the Romans.
Augustus was the Emperor at that time.
Most nations except the Jews were idol-
aters, the greater part of their gods were
ancient heroes, likewise females who were
distinguished for their great deeds, also the
sun, moon and stars received worship from
many Others paid divine homage to the
earth, mountains, rivers, oceans, or at least
to the deities supposed to preside over these
objects.
	Towards the close of the reign of Herod,
(a tributary king of Palestine and a cruel
tyrant,) our Savior was born.
	Our Savior, the spotless Son of God!
During his brief life he set a perfect exam-


ple for man to follow but little heed was
taken. The early Christians were barbar-
ously treated.
	The teachings of Christ were so simple
that all might understand but how few did!
He gathered to himself disciples, instructed
them and sent them forth to preach his
Gospel. The Roman Empire asserted its
right to rule in all things, human and Di-
vine. To preach Christianity in Rome was
looked upon as high treason. The Romans
considered themselves not only the greatest of
all nations but the most religious.
	The great mass of the early Christians
were from the lower ranks of the people,
and down through the centuries the poor,
the meek and the lowly have been the fol-
lowers of Jesus. It is the same today.
	As Latter-day Saints how blest a people
we are who live in this day when the true 
Gospel of Jesus Christ has been revealed,
and what a work rests upon us for the liv-
ing and the dead!  Then let us be true to
our religion, doing our duty and all will be
well, for the Lord has ever been and will
ever be with those who do his will. We
may make grave mistakes, commit many 
errors, but if our desires are to do right our
Heavenly Father will be merciful unto us
His children.				E. H. L.
