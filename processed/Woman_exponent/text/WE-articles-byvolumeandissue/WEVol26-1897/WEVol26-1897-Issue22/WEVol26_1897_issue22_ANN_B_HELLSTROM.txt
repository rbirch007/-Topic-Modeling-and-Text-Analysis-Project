ANN B. HELLSTROM.
	WRITTEN in memoriam of Anna Bridget Hells-
trom who died at Colonia, Juarez, Mexico, June
30, 1897. Deceased was the daughter of Olof
Olson and Catherine Anderson She was born at
Gillberga, Sodermanlan, Sweden, March 16, 1824.
Sister H[-]llstrom embraced the Gospel in her
native land many years since and gathered with
the Saints, leaving husband and relatives but
bringing with her two children, a son and a
daughter. The daughter died leaving six child-
ren whom our beloved sister has taken care of,
providing for their wants by her faithful and un-
tiring labors. Few women have been more active
than was our respected and beloved sister in
carrying on the work God gives women to do.
She was a firm believer in industry, not in theory
only but in practice. She was a strict tithing
payer and was indeed a model Latter-day Saint,
and a woman in whom there was no guile. She
was a teacher in the Relief Society and faithfully
discharged her duties in that capacity for some
time. We can truly say of her that she was one of
God’s noble women, faithful, unassuming and
consistent, and her examp[-]e was worthy of imita-
tion.
	The funeral services were held at the ward
meeting house. Appropriate remarks were made
by Antony Ivins, president of the Stake, Bishop
George A. S[-]eley, Counselor Miles P. Romney
and Patriarch W. W. Stowell. A large cortege
followed the remains of our beloved sister to its
resting place.
	In behalf of the Relief Society we tender our
deepest sympathy to the bereaved family. May
we emulate her example that when we are called
home we may hear the joyous notes sounded
“Well done good and fafthful servant, enter into
thy rest.”
	Resolved by the Relief Society that a copy of
this sketch be sent to the family, a copy placed
on record and one forwarded to the “Exponent”
for publication.
	PHOEBE FIFE, Pres.
