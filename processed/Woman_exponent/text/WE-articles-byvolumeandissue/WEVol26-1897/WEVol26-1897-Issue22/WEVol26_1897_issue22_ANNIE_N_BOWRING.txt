ANNIE N. BOWRING.
	WITH loving remembrance we record the de-
mise of our sister, Annie Bowring of Brigham
City, who departed this life Sep. 14, 1897.
	Annie Nyman Bowring, d[-]ughter of Henry
G[-]orge and Jane Bird Nyman, was born at Lye
Hill Burbage, Wiltshire, England, Dec. 23, 1844.
She emigrated to Utah in 1863, crossed the ocean
in the sailing vessel Amazon, and crossed the
plains in Dan McArthur’s company with ox teams
and reached Salt Lake City in Oct. of the same
year. She was married to Elder Henry Bowring
the same year, and was the mother of three sons
two of whom are still living
	She was connected with the Relief Society of
the 15th Ward, Salt Lake City as secretary,
came to Brigham City in 1877 and has resided
here ever since. In 1878 she was elected secre-
tary in the 1st Ward Society which position she
fi[-]led until April 1885, also acted as teacher from
May 1881 until Feb. 1885. After having removed
to the 3rd Ward she was elected secretary for this
ward S[-]ciety, which position she faithfully filled
until June 1892, when she was elected assistant
secretary of the Stake board, which position she
held at the time of her death She also acted a
few years as corresponding secretary of the Y. L.
M. I. A. Stake board.
	Sister Annie Bowring possessed the love and
respect of her co-laborers, and although she has
for yea[-]s been in delicate health her death came
so unexpectedly, on the very morning of our Re-
lief Society c[-]nference, that a spirit of solemnity
and sadness prevaded the meetings of the whole

[Column 2]
day; still we mourn not in despair for we feel as- .
cured that all is well with our sister and that she
is in a happy place, associating with the faithful
loved ones who have gone bef[-]re.
	RESOLVED, That we place these sentiments on
our records, also publish a copy in the “Woman’s
Exponent,” also present a copy to her bereaved
family.
	OLIVIA WIDERBORG, Pres.
