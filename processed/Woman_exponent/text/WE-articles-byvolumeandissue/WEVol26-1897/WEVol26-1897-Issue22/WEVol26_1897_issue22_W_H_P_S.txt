W. H. P. S. 

	THE Woman’s Hygienic Physiological
Society met in the 14th Ward meeting
house Oct. 7, 1897. There was a good at-
tendance and the workers were very much
encouraged by the presence and remarks of
the brethren. President Angus M. Cannon,
President David Cannon, Bishop Keeler of
Provo, Brother Lucius Snow of Brigham
City, and many others were present. Each
of these brethren expressed themselves as
being pleased with the subjects treated,
they knew it would increase as it was truth.
	Sister Sorensen briefly explained the
work; she said the raising of children was
the most important part of her teaching,
that a woman must understand what is re-
quired of her as a mother, how to care for 
her body that she may bear the healthiest
and noblest of God’s children
	Sister Kirkham of Provo, treated the sub-
ject of “Child Training,” and said we must
be particular in the language we use to a
child and treat them as we wish to be
treated.
	Sister Salzner spoke of the necessity of
girls being prepared for marriage. Sister
Pine described a woman as a nurse in her
own home, what her qualifications should
be etc. Sister Eva Merril of Lehi read a
paper on “Round Dancing.” She said
we ought to obey the authorities of the
Church and not allow impure or immoral
men to be so familiarly associated with us,
such as are often met in the ball room.
Sister Allen prepared and read an essay on
“Prenatal Influence.”
