A TWILIGHT REVERIE.

I’M sitting in the twilight, the sweetest hour of all
The hour when day is dying, and dusk begins to
	fall.
I gaze in thoughtful silence, on the burning em-
	bers bright,
While the shadows slowly lengthen, in the tender
	fading light.
In the glowing coals of fire fantastic figures meet
	my gaze,
And in fancy pass before me, the scenes of other
	days, 
Fair forms and faces far away, are pictured in
	the fire;
And in the hrooding calm I hear, sweet tones of
	harp and lyre,
Before me comes my mother’s face the same
	sweet, tender smile,
That cheered my drooping spirits, when we part-
	ed for awhile.
My little sister laughs again at me in childish glee,
My brothers, friends and schoolmates all, once 
	more return to me.
The little, humble cottage where I lived when
	but a boy,
All trouble was a dream to me, life’s gold held no
	alloy.
The old house is deserted now, its inmates all
	have fled.
Some are in the churchyard, sleeping, numbered
	with the dead,
Some have gone to foreign lands, ’mid strangers
	they are cast,
I often wonder if they think of happy days long
	past.
When we built our castles in the air, of futures
	fair and bright,
But time brought changes, and the castles all
	vanished from our sight
The fire is dying, day has fled strange shapes no
	more I see,
The past within my memory locked, holds all its
	charms for me.
The future dim, uncertain, calls silently from
	afar,
With faith and love for a motto, and hope for a
	guiding star.
