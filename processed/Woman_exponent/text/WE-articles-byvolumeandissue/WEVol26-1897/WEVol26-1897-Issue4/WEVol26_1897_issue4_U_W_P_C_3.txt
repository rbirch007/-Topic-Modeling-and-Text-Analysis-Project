U.W. P. C.

	MINUTES of the U. W. P. C. held
Sep. 30. President Fox called the meeting
to order at the usual time. After prayer by
Mrs. Stevenson, the secretary called the
roll, members responding with sentiments
from Elizabeth Barrett Browning.
	Under “unfinished business” the amend-
ments to the Federation Constitution by the


Woman’s club, which was laid on the table
at the previous meeting, were brought be-
fore the club. Amendment to Art. III,
sec. 3 was read and discussed. A motion
was made by Dr. Ferguson that the amend-
ment be accepted The motion was lost.
The amendment to the by laws, Art. II.
was read and discussed. Motion by Dr.
Ferguson that this amendment be accepted.
Carried. The club voted that the annual
business meeting should be held Monday,
October 18, at 11 a. m.
	Next in order was the critic’s report by
Dr. Shipp. Dr. Pratt was appointed critic
for the evening.
	The evening’s program was as follows:
Topic—“Alaska,” Mrs. Aimee Schiller.
Original poem—“The Woman Preacher,”
Dr. Shipp. Mrs. Dickinson was unable to
be present and her paper on the “Effect of
Klondike gold discoveries on American in-
terests” was postponed.
	Mrs. Lawler at the request, of the club
described the vessel in which she crossed
the Atlantic, also a number of points and
places of interest in Paris. The program
for the October meeting was discussed and
suggestions made. A motion was made
and carried that at the October meeting
each member represent some prominent
character in fiction. A motion was made
and carried that each member be permitted
to bring an escort. Dr. Shipp graciously
offered her home for that meeting. The
club decided to have refreshments and
Pearl Russell, Mrs. Lovesy, Mrs. Wilcox,
Mrs. Hyde and Clara Ferguson were ap-
pointed as a committee to assist Dr. Shipp.
Dr. Pratt gave her report as critic, the
minutes were read and after benediction by
Mrs. Young the club adjourned for one
month.
