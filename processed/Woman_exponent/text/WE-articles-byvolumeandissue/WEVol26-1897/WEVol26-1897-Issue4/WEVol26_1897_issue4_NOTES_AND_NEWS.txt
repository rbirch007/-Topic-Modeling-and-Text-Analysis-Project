NOTES AND NEWS.

	MRS. MCKINLEY has secured the ap-
pointment of Miss Hattie Gault, her tutor
at Brooks Hall Seminary thirty years ago,
as postmistress at Media, Ohio.
	MRS. JOSEPHINE R. NICOLS, who had
charge of the W. C. T. U. exhibit and
headquarters at the Paris Exposition, and
also at the World’s Fair, recently died at
her home in Indianapolis. She was a
strong woman and a great help to the
W. C. T. U. 				—Ex.
	THE University of Wisconsin has creat-
ed a new office, that of “Dean of women,”
and has appointed as Dean, Miss Annie
Crosby Emery of Ellsworth, Maine, A. B.
Bryn Mawr College 1892—holder of the
European Fellowship of the class, and Ph.
D. 1896
	JEAN INGELOW, the poet and novelist,
died in London July 19. She was born in
Boston, Lincolnshire, England, in 1820.
Ten years ago Miss Frances Willard pro-
posed and Dr. Chauncey M. Depew seconded
the motion, that Queen Victoria should ap-
point Miss Ingelow poet laureate, but the
suggestion was not accepted.
	MRS. M. J. WHITMAN, president of the
State Suffrage association, is making an ex-
cellent record as a member of the board of
regents of the State University. One of
her fellow regents is reported saying that
Mrs. Whitman is the most diligent and
efficient member of the board. This will
not surprise anybody except a few fossils
who cannot get it through their heads that
women have brains of just as fine texture
as men.
	BOISE CITY is entering upon a municipal
campaign. The indications are that it will
be a spirited contest. This is an excellent
opportunity for the women of the capital
city to set an example to their sisters
throughout the state. The women of Boise
can no doubt absolutely dictate the result
of the election. Will they do it and do it
right?
