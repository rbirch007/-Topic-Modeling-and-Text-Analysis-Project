		RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.

	ROSINA MARION CARTER DUBOIS, the beloved
wife of Leo G. Dubois anddaughterof Wm.F. and
Elizabeth Howard Carter, born in Santaquin,
June 10, 1870, died July 9, 1897, aged 27 years
29 days. She was a beloved and efficient help to
her widowed mother, who always kept her by
her side from her birth until she departed this
life.
	She was born and raised in the Church and died
as she had lived, a faithful Latter-day Saint.
Though her days were not many upon the earth
they were well spent. As a daughter and wife
she was loving, persevering and kind, and would
often say to her aged mother "Mother you rest
and let me work.”
	She was a faithful member in the Relief Soci-
ety, and was always punctual in attending to her
duties. Her departure was sudden, being sick
but a few days, when her pure spirit returned to
the Father who ave it.


Our sister does not need our tears,
	She seems to say to all
“Be up and doing death may come,
	Be ready for his call."

We bow to God our Father’s will,
	And humbly ask that He
Shall comfort all the mourning friends
	That they His hand may see.

	WHEREAS, Our Heavenly Father has seen fit in
His alwise Providence to call from our midst by
the hand of death our much respected sister,
Rosina Marion Carter Dubois, therefore be it
	RESOLVED, That we the members of the Re-
lief Society of Santaquin do sympathize with the
bereaved husband, mother, brothers and sister,
and relatives in the loss of a kind and affectionate
daughter.
	RESOLED, That we cherish her memory and re-
gard her virtue as ever worthy in her energy and
perseverence to the canse of truth
	RESOLVED, That these resolutions be placed
upon the records of the Relief Society, also that
a copy be presented to the bereaved husband and
mother. Also the WOMAN’S EXPONENT for
publication.
ELIZABETH WALL.
HANNAH KIRKMAN,
NETTIE TIETJEN,
Committee
