LIST OF DEATHS ON BOARD THE SHIP.
	Feb. 14th the infant son of Joseph Nichols,
aged 2 years 18 days. Buried same day at 11
o'clock a. m. in Lat. 37 N., Long. 20 W.
	Feb. 20, 6 o’clock p. m. Elias Ensign died, aged
57 years, 5 months. Buried next day at 11 a. m.
in Lat. 19 30 N., Long. 26 W.
	Saturday Feb. 28, son of John R. Robbins d ed
at 10 o’clock p. m., of scarlet fever, aged 2 years
and ten days. Buried in Lat. 316 N. Long. 25 W.
	March 6, the son of John Fowler, aged 1 year,
7 months and 28 days.
	March 7, 6 o’clock a. m., of consumption, Miss
Eliza Ensign, aged 20 years, 8 months and 17
days. Lat. 3 S. Long 27 W.
	March 14 10 o’clock a. m., son of John R. Rob-
bins died of consumption, aged 1 year, 5 months
and 16 days. Lat. 15.30 S, Long. 32 W.
	March 26, Edward Miles one of the ship’s crew.
	March 27, 2 o’clock a. m., the daughter of
George R. Winner died of cankered sore throat,
aged 6 months 7 days.
	April 1, 2 o’clock p. m., Silas Aldrich died of
dropsy, aged 43 years, 8 months and 20 days.
Buried at a.m. next day in Lat. 43 S. Long. 47 W.
	May 6, Mrs. Laura Goodwin died, aged 32 years,
11 months and 23 days. Her death was occa-
sioned by a fall, she left seven children. Buried
Juan Fernandez.
	A male child born before doubling the Cape
was called Atlantic and a female born this side
was called Pacific. A. J. C.
