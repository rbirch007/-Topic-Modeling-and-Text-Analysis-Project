U. W. P. C.

	THE regular monthly meeting of the U.
W. P. C. was held Saturday evening, July
31, at the office of the WOMAN’S EXPO-
NENT. President Fox called the meeting to
order at 8 o’clock. After prayer by Mrs.
Lucy A. Clark the secretary called the
roll, members responding with sentiments
from various Utah poets. As there were
but few members present the evening’s
program was in order before business. The
chair appointed Dr. Curtis critic for the
evening.
	The subject for the evening was the
Utah Jubilee. The first paper was pre-
sented by Miss Annie Laura Hyde on “The
Pioneers;” this was followed by a paper
on “The Jubilee,” by Mrs. Ruth M. Fox.
This completed the program.
	Under the head of business a motion was
made and carried that the program be pre-
pared by the committee and read before the
club one month befere hand.
	In accordance with a letter to the club
from Mrs. Bradley, asking that the U. W.
P. C. appoint a special correspondent to
the Review, the secretary was appointed by
the club to fill that position. After bene-
diction by Mrs. P. C. Young the meeting
was adjourned for one month.
