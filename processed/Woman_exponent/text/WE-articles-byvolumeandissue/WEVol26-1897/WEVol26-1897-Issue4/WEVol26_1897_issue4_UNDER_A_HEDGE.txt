UNDER A HEDGE.

A FLORIST a sweet little blossom espied,
Which bloomed like its Ancestors by the road side,
Its fragrance was simple, its colors were few,
But the blossom looked fair in the spot
Where it grew. Under a Hedge. 

The Florist beheld it and cried “I’ll enchant
The Botanical world with this sweet little plant.
Its leaves shall be sheltered and carefully nursed,
It shall charm all the world tho’ I met with it first
			Under a Hedge.”

He carried it home to his hot-house with care 
And said “Tho’ the rarest exotics are there
My little pet plant when I’ve nourished its stem
In tints and in fragrance shall imitate them
And none shall suspect from the road side it
	came.
Rodum Sidus I’ll call it, a beautiful name,
And when Botanists look through their glasses to
	view
Its beauties they’ll never suspect that it grew
			Under a Hedge.”

The little pet plant when it shook off the dust
Of its own native ditch soon began to grow pert
And tossed its small head, for perceiving that none
But exotics were round it, it thought itself one
And forgot that it grew, Under a Hedge.

As a wild flower all would have owned it was
	fair,
And praised it though gaudier blossoms were
	there,
But when it assumes the hot-house airs we see
	through
The forced tints of its leaves and suspect that it
	grew
			Under a Hedge


In the by-ways of life oh how many there are, 
Who being born under a fortunate star, 
Assisted by beauty or talent grow rich, 
To bloom in a hot-house instead of a ditch.
And whilst they disdain not their own simple
	stem,
The honor they grasp may gain honor from them
But when like the pet plant such people grow
	pert, 
We soon trace them to their original birth
				Under a Hedge
