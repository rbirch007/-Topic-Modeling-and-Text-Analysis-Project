HISPANIA.

HISPANIA thy doom is sealed! The blood 
Of innocence cries for vengeance. 
								Blood and
Tears mark thy pathway for centuries,
And as thou hast sown so must thou
Reap,tears and blood for thy portion.
The noble Incas, trusting, unsuspecting, though
	long among the dead,
Cry for thy downfall.
Romantic thou in love, thy troubadours singing
	their tender passion 
To the tune of the light guitar, where silver
	moonbeams faintly fall,
Love’s soft light from shining lustrous eyes reward
	a lover’s kiss;
But under their smiling, base treachery
	Lurks, that to the death betrays,
In blackened crime or tortures cruel and slow.
Hispaniola as thy sins are great, so mighty must
	be thy fall,
Thy yoke, burdens thy subjects willing neck no
	longer,
But in multitudes, they rise against thee,  
Insurrection at home, wars abroad, thy crown in
	the balance trembles.
Thy power is broken, and strangers rule over
	thee.
O, Montezuma thou shalt now be avenged!
Thou, monarch of a princely race with subjects
True, possessors of wealth fabulous beyond
Compare, they did murder and pillage.
The debt must now be paid. Dost see them
Rising from their graves, the crimson blood
Oozing, dripping from ugly wounds?
Their sightless eyes, and ghastly hands
Raised heavenward, to invoke her vengeance?
Where are their riches, where their treasures?
	Gone
To Spain! Where their princely diadem, their
	works of art?
Gone, gone, stolen by those whose treachery
	is the spectacle of a world,
Ingrates, dealing death to friends in kingly halls,
At whose banquet they sup. 
							Centuries have
Passed away but the blot still remains.
Generations have been born, and acting their

[Column 2]
	part on the stream of Time
Are sleeping in the dust. Children
Reading of thy base ingratitude, shudder to read
	the tale 
Betrayers of friendship, alluring the
Guiltless to destruction, thou hast been.
							Haughty and
Proud art thou, but in the dust thou shalt lie,
An object of pity for tyrants everywhere.
Thy sins have reached to heaven, so clanging loud
	thy convicts chains,
Now read thy doom!
								And Cuba
Must be free! Deep groans to God, ascend from
This poor and stricken land! Brave Cuba thou
	shalt be free!
Thy murmuring winds low sound the dying’s
	groan. 
Thy graves the mourner’s sigh, while
Blood and tears bespread the land.
But freedom’s banner doth wave across
The sea, where float the stars and stripes, 
A mighty arm is raised for thee to break the
	tyrant’s band,
Lo! o’er the world the shout rings wildly out,
	rejoice! for Cuba’s free!
 
And shall the Maine forgotten be, though in the
	mud she lies?
Sent to her doom by treacherous foes, who came
	in f[-]iendly guise?
Her crew asleep, a night most dark, no tears of
	pity rise;
The signal came, and the Battle S[-]ip, upward dash-
	es toward the skies.
The wild waves hush the martyrs shriek and end
	their quivering pain;
Death closes o’er and deep they lie, to never rise
	again.
And sisters pray, and mothers weep for those
	among the dead,
And fathers too whose hair is gray their hope
	forever fled.
And widow’s hearts with anguish break, and or-
	phans cry in vain;
For the Sacrifice, who sailed away on the fated,
	ill-starred Maine.
Hispania! for thee no mourner weeps thy doom
	forever to die,
On history’s page thy name goes out, thy debt
	of liberty to satisfy.
