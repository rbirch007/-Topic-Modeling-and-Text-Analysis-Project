THURSDAY EVENING DEC. l6, 1897.
THE people of Boston once set an example,
	Which people of U[-]ah might follow, I think;
If the word and the coun[-]el of God are not ample,
	To teach them exactly the things they should
		drink.

If Boston went out with her sissors and axes,
	Rippe bags, split up boxes, and spilt all the
		tea;
And all that because of exorbitant taxes,
	A hint is there hidden, if Utah could see.

If the drinks of the Boston girl afterwards savored
	Of garden sage, catnip and rasberry leaves,
And yet with prosperity great she was favored,
	The Utah girl here a fine lesson receives.

Receives if she will—and I’d Iike her to take it—
	And take no more tea, as a pointer to wealth;
But remem[-]er God’s promise, (He never wil
	break it,)
Great trea[-]ures of wisdom, long life and good
	health.
