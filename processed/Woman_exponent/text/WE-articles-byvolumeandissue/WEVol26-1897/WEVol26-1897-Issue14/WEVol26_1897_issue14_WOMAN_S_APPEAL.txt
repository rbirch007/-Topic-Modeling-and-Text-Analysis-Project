WOMAN'S APPEAL.

O NEVER yet has freedom called in vain
On woman’s hand or woman’s brain,
Through shadowy stretch of fleeting years,
See her undaunted by her fears,
Set forth from kindred and her native strand,
To dwell thence forth in foreign land;
Then truth was loosed from angry bigots thrall,
Woman had answered Freedom’s call.

To seventeen hundred and seventy-six:
Now turn the page and read the tricks
Of guile and greed and royal lust of gold
That wrenched the States from British hold,
Then in that contest woman bore a part,
Sent forth her sons with cheerful heart
To fight for liberty, perchance to fall,
And thus she answered Freedom’s call.

The Nineteenth Century on roll of fame
Has oft inscribed a woman's name;
She had trod the field, the hospital, the tent,
Ever on kind mission bent,
She listened to the soldier’s parting breath
And eased his painful way to death;
With charity and helpful hand to all,
She bravely answered Freedom’s call.

When school and college opened wide their doors
Eager she drank from wisdom’s stores.
To liberty with outstretched hands she turns,
And begs the right she fairly earns,
That legislators break those chains disgracing
Ranking her with class degrading.
O brothers, let your deed our hope forestall;
’Tis yours to answer Freedom’s call.
