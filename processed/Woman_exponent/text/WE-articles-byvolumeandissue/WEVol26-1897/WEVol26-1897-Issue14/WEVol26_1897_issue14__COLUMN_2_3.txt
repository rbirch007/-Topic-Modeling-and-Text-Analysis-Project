	The children should be made as happy as
possible with nice and comfortable gifts
from those who love them, but meantime
remember the Lord gives us all things and
it is to him we must be grateful for all
blessings.
	When the Saints who settled these val-
leys came here they asked the Lord in
humility and in faith to bless this land and
make it fruitful, to prosper them and multi-
ply their blessings temporally and spiritual-
ly. The Lord heard and answered their
prayers, and the land overflows with plenty
and we and our children must not forget
that the Lord has given us these great
blessings.
	In the early days our children who are
now men and women with children of their
own, did not have the good things that are
so plentiful now, and the mothers and fath-
ers gave them any little, simple gifts at
such times and were very glad if they had
warm clothes and plain food to give the lit-
tle ones, and the children were as content
with rag dolls and molasses candy and 
doughnuts and pop-corn as they are now
with the abundance of sweetmeats and wax
dolls and ornaments of all sorts. Then we
used to tell the children stories for there
were few books to give them, now they 
have story books in abundance and papers 
for children full of nice little things fun-
ny and curious to please and delight them.
What good men and women they ought to
be when they grow up with all these condi-
tions and opportunities.
	On the blessed Christmas day let us hope
there will not be a hungry child or an un-
happy one in all Utah, but every one be
provided with plenty to eat so they may be
free from anxiety on that one day in all the
year, and if every one who has plenty and
to spare will give to one poor family, then
to be sure there will be no want in all the
land and every heart will be made glad.
