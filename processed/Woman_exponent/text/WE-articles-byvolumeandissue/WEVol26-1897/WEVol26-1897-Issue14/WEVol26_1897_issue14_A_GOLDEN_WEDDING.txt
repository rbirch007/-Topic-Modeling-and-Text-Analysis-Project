A GOLDEN WEDDING.

	ON Thanksgiving a golden wedding
anniversary was celebrated at Nephi in
honor of the marriage of Charles Price and
Ella Johnson at Council Bluffs in the year
1847, the bridegroom then eighteen, the
bride scarcely fourteen years of age. They
have been blessed with a large family, fif-
teen in number, eleven of whom survive.
	The family prepared a sumptuous feast for
the occasion. Five sons with their wives
were present and five daughters with their
husbands, one daughter was absent on ac-
count of sickness. Thirty-five grandchil.
dren were present, two were absent, and
thirteen great grandchildren, besides many
guests who honored them with their
presence.
	The tables were set in an upper room
where over a hundred were seated to par-
take of a bounteous repast. I may here
state that the wedding cake was decorated
with gold pieces amounting to $40.00.
Afterwards came music, speeches and reci-
tations, concluding with a dance which
was greatly appreciated. It was pleasant
to see the fathers dance with their little
daughters. All were made happy enjoy-
ing perfect freedom which will be to them a
pleasant memory.
						M. A. HYDE. 
	Nephi, NOV. 27, 1897.
