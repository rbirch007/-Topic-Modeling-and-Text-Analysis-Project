CALIFORNIA MISSION.

Dear Editor:
	THE 10th of February being the anniver-
sary of the marriage of President and Mrs.
Nye, the Relief Society tendered them a
dinner at the Mission house. Eighty peo-
ple were seated at the tables which were
laden with all the good things one could de-
sire. The guests were waited on by Miss
Lilly Boedefeld, Miss Flossie Sarll, Miss
Adle Boedefeld Miss Edith Sarll and Miss
Florence Rawlinson, young ladies of the
branch. They were kept busy nearly three
hours and in a most gracious manner waited
upon all who came.
	Many investigators hearing of the event
came to do honor to Brother and Sister Nye
who are ever ready to help in the cause of
truth, one would have thought he was in
Utah to look down the long banqueting
table and see so many well known Utah
people, among whom were Brother Lorin
Farr of Ogden, Don C. Musser, Sister Mary
J. Young and son Briant Young; also Willie
Mackintosh of Salt Lake and many other
former residents of Utah, and ten Elders
laboring in San Francisco and vicinity help-
ed to swell the number
	Sister M. A. Rattenberry, president of
the Relief Society, made a speech while the
guests were still seated at the table, in her
well known and pleasing style. The other
officers, Sisters Sewell, Simpkin, Sarll and
Woodward, followed in neat little speeches
full of loving words to Sister Nye, who
labors with them so faithfully to help in the
mission work in California. The evening
was spent in vocal music and recitations
by members of the branch, and a most en-
joyable time was spent.
	The Relief Society is progressing and the
sisters feel that God is blessing their work.
IDA M. SARLL, Sec.
San Francisco, March 17, 1898.
