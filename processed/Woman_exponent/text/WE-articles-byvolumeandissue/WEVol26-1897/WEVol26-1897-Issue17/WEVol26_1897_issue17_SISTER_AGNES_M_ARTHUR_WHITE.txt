SISTER AGNES M’ARTHUR WHITE.

	Sister McArthur White, daughter of James and
Elizabeth McA thur, was born May 13, 1842, was
baptized into the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-
day Saints November 1857 and emigrated to Utah
in 1859. She was married to Stephen White in
Salt Lake City on November 1, 1859, and lived in
Mill Creek ward until the spring of 1868 w en she
moved to Willard where she resided until her
death on Feb uary 4, 1898.
	Sister White was a teacher in the Relief Society
of Willard and a counselor in the North District
Primary Association, also a teacher in the
Sunday Schoo . She was a faithfu  Latter-day
Saint and was loved and respected by all who
knew her. She eaves a kind husband and eight
children, four sons and four daughters to mourn
her loss.
		RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT. 
	WHEREAS, Our Heavenl  Fa her has seen fit to
take from our midst one of our sisters, be it
	RESOLVED, That we the officers and members of
the Relief Society of Willard and of the Primary
Association of North District do extend our heart-
felt sympathy to the bereaved family in this their
hour of sorrow, and that we ever cherish her
memory. 
	RESOLVED, Tha  a copy of these resolutions be
placed on the records of the Relief Society, one
presented to the bereaved family and one sent to
the “Woman’s Exponent” for publication.
