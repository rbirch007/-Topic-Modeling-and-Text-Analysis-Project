ST. JOHN’S STAKE.
	Minutes of the St. Johns Stake Primary con-
ference held Feb. 28, 1898, President Emma
B. Coleman presiding. After singing pray-
er was offered by our young brother, Oscar
Freeman. Sister Coleman made same good
and appropriate opening remarks. after
which a short program was rendered con-
sisting of songs, music, recitations and our
paper read by the editor, Ethel Farr.
	Short reports were given by presidents or
representatives of the different wards, every
ward in the Stake being represented. The
statistical report was read and the officers
sustained.
	Sister Cornelia Horne Clayton of Provo,
Utah, then addressed the congregation
in a very interesting manner, giving good
and timely instruction and sang the chil-
dren a song.
	President D. K. Udall spoke for a short
time, followed by Celia Hamblin. After
singing, benediction was pronounced by
young Revel Jarvis.
	SARAH H. CROSBY, Sec. pro tem.
