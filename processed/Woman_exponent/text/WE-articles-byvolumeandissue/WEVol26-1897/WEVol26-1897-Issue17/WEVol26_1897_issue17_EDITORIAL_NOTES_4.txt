EDITORIAL NOTES.

	MADAME MOUNTFORD has arrived at
Jerusalem and is at present quartered at
the Howard Hotel. She says in her mes-
sage to the writer, March 24, that she has
not forgotten Zion and desires to be remem-
bered to all inquiring friends.
	THE Welsh prize singers gave their con-
cert in the large Tabernacle early in the
month. Judging from current opinions the
affair was a greal musical success. It seems
to be quite popular to give concerts, lectures
and such like entertainments in the great
“Mormon” Tabernacle.
	MME. MELBA and her grand opera com-
pany arrived in the city last night and will
appear in the large Tabernacle tonight. The
Tabernacle choir will also take part in the
concert to be given. Mme. Melba is an
Australian and takes her stage name from
the city of Melbourne where she was born.
Her bird-like voice has made her famous the
world over. Of her personality or history
very little seems to be known by local peo-
ple in Salt Lake City.
