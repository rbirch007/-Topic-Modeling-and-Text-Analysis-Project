	At the close of the literary exercises all
repaired to the dining room to partake of
delicious refreshments amid the cheerful
hum of voices, the perfume of fragrant flow-
ers and sweet strains of music floating
through the rooms, enlivening the hours
and exhilarating the soul.
	True poetry is the divine music of the
inner soul! To feel its instinctive power,
to listen to its silent whisperings, and more
than all, to be able to express its euphoni-
ous eloquence is to be wafted (so to speak)
from our materialistic beings into the realms
of the infinite.
	Those who peruse “Musings and Memor-
ies” must surely feel this mental exaltation.
How many useful lessons we learn from
these truthful touches of nature—“The
Spirit of Consolation,” Sorrow and Sym-
pathy,” “Faith and Fidelity,” the rambles
through woodland and glen and in “The
Dear Old Garden,” the voice from the
mountain and the plaintive, weird music of
the sea—all reach the inmost soul and lure
us back to heaven. The grandest thoughts
of human souls are thus immortalized and
live again within the heart of ages yet un-
born. And methinks in that blest home
where Father dwells, angelic hearts will
sing these songs of praise—these gems of
inspiration—that come to mortals here be-
low as beams of heavenly light from His
exalted presence.
