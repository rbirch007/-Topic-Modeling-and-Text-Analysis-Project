REORGANIZATION.
	I write to inform you of the reorganiza-
tion of the Relief Society of St. John’s
ward, which occurred March 25, 1898.
Anna D. Kempe who had served faithfully
for four years as president, being a widow
and having to work very hard to support
her family felt she was unable to perform
her duty in that position, and therefore
tendered her resignation which was accepted
and she was honorably released. She is a
very energetic woman and is well respected
by the members of the Relief Society as
president, also by the community at large.
	President E. L. S. Udall, Stake president of
the Relief Society being here at the time, a
special meeting was called and President
Udall together with her counselor, Emily
Patterson and the Bishop of the ward, select-
ed the following officers: Margaret Jarvis,
president; Anna L. Anderson, first counsel-
or; Eleanor Patterson, second counselor;
R. B. Berry, secretary; Sina Stradling,
assistant secretary; Sarah Berry, treasurer.
					R. E. BERRY, Sec.
ST. JOSEPH STAKE. 
	The quarterly conference of the Re-
lief Society of St. Joseph Stake, Arizona,
convened at Thatcher, March 11, 1898,
Counselor Mary L. Ransom presiding in the
absence of the president, Elizabeth Layton,
who was sustained in that position at the
late reorganization of the Stake, President
Wilmirth East who has filled the office so
long and so well, being honorably released
on account of her advanced age and feeble-
ness.
