	The District Court is in session at this
time, and as usual divorces are being called
for and granted. It is alarming, I think,
that so many divorces seem to be needed.
I hope that our young people will, and I
believe they do, appreciate the plain counsel
they are getting; truly the ax is being laid
at the root of the tree. I believe one cause
why so much petty jealousy and unpleasant-
ness arises in so many families commences
through lack of confidence in one another;
and why this lack of confidence ? If we
would trace it back sometimes we would find
it was because of undue liberty before mar-
riage; and therefore lack of confidence in
one another leads to disrespect and disre-
spect to hate, hence the only way they can
see out is to get divorced, and often this is
“jumping out of the frying pan into the
fire.”
	Are we profiting by the many sermons
we hear? A woman who was born in the
Church but had married into an apostate
family, said to me one day that she could
see little difference in church going people
or in their children who attended Sabbath
School, and those who did not; she failed to
see any difference in those who prayed or
those that were taught to pray, and those
who did not. Her children, she said, were
just as good as anybody’s, and they did not
pray. I told her that we who attended
meetings and prayed did not profess to be
perfect., that the Church was a school and
some of us are very dull and slow to learn
it is true, and profit very little by attending
meetings perhaps; but there are two natures
in mankind, human and divine: “To err is
human; to forgive divine.” Those who
pray cultivate the divine nature, they can
forgive. I said to her “You are not happy
when you treasure up some trifle which
you imagine is so bad that a neighbor or
neighbor’s child has done or said. You
may sometimes forgive one who believes as
you do, but one who is trying to be a Saint
you cannot forgive, you do not seek the
spirit of forgiveness.” She said, “Well I
am satisfied, I would not have my children
blessed nor baptized, because I will not
allow religion to interfere when their father
does not believe in it. He is a good pro-
vider and that is all I want.” “Well,” I
said, “we will not enjoy this life always
and we must prepare for an eternity. I
want my children to go to Sabbath School,
also to be baptized and confirmed, that they
may be able to cultivate, if they are dili-
gent, the higher nature, this is the object,
I believe, of all religious meetings whether
of individuals, associations, Sabbath School
meetings or meetings of worship; they are
for the cultivation of our higher natures. If
the partakers of all these benefits are not
any better for them it is not the fault of the
Church nor the teachers in the Church, but
those who are idle.”
	That we may still work for the building
up of the kingdom is the desire of
						GRANDMA.
	Willow Creek, Bingham, Idaho.
