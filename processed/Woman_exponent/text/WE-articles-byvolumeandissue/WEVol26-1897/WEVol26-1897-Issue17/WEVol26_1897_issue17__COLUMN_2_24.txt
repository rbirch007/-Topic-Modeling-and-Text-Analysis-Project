	Now as to the labor accomplished:
Masonry about				$115,00.
Rock hauling, 96 perches		57.50.
Clay, 10 loads					5.00.
Sand						12.00.

Total work on granary			$189.50.
	In addition to the above thirty-five loads
of shale were hauled into the Tabernacle
yard, nearly as many more at street cross-
ings and a number of loads of rock for the
new school building in course of construc-
tion. All this, with the good work of the
street crossing committee would foot up a
grand total, could we arrive at the exact
figures.
	An entertainment was tendered the gen-
tlemen, their wives and sweethearts, in the
evening in the theatre. A nice program
was rendered, tempting refreshments served
after which the remainder of the time was
spent pleasantly in dancing.
	Dear Aunt Em and the readers of the
“Exponent” pardon this long contribution.
I have given the particulars thus in detail
in the hope that it might inspire some
others having similiar desires, but a deficien-
cy of the wherewithal, to go forth and do
likewise, with this example of successful
effort before them.
	Our granary will have a capacity of two
thousand bushels, and we expect to have it
ready to receive the wheat at harvest time.
Our success has made us bold, and we will
be pardoned for asserting that we do not
anticipate any trouble in getting grain to
put into it.
	We trust that the end and aim of this
communication may speedily be realized in
learning of some other struggling Society
making an effort in this necessary direction.
			JULIA A. PACE, Cor. Sec.
	St. George Stake.
