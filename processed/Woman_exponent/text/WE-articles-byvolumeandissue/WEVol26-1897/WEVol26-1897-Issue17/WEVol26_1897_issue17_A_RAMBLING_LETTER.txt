A RAMBLING LETTER.

	WINTER is beginning to wane and the
farmers are commencing to prepare for seed
time. The school land is in the market,
and our friends and relatives are coming
here to make their homes and fortunes in
this land of “Uncle Sam.” The boom-
ers land is all that seems to be lying idle
now; perhaps when all the rest is improved
this will sell to someone who needs a home.
	We had a good attendance at our Relief
Society conference, which was held at Idaho
Falls on the 13th of last month. The house
was comfortably filled, especially in the
afternoon, although it was blowing a
very keen wind from the south. The new
meeting house is a very nice edifice and
does credit to the ward. I hope we will be
as successful at Willow Creek. We received
some good instructions from the presidency
of the Stake, also from Sister Bennett and
counselors, our lady presidency of the Stake
of Bingham. Our young people are looking
forward to a treat, their conference coming
on the 16th of this month. We are very
proud of this Association. Our officers both
of the Stake and of the ward are efficient in
their abilities and energy. We parents are
glad to see the young people growing up to
