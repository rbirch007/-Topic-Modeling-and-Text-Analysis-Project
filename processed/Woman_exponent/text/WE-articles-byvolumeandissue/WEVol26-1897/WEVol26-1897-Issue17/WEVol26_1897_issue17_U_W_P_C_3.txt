U. W. P. C.

	THE ladies of the U. W. P. C. met ac-
cording to adjournment Thursday evening,
March 31, 1898. Club was called to order
by President Romania B. Pratt. Prayer by 
Mrs. Lydia D. Alder. Roll call with re-
sponse from Spanish authors. Under un-
finished business the question of issuing re-
ceipts for membership fees was discussed.
A motion in its favor by Mrs. Emily S.
Richards wascarried. A motion was made
and carried that the club subscribe
$5.00 from the treasury for charter mem-
bership in the George Washington Memor-
ial Association, was carried.


In the General Council of Woman’s Clubs
the Press Club was given the appointment
of secretary from its membership. A letter
was read from Ruth M. Fox contain
ing a statement to that effect and a motion
by Mrs. E. B. Wells was carried to the ef-
fect that President Pratt make the appoint-
ment after time for deliberation.
	Mrs. Lydia D. Alder read an invitation
given by the Inqueires Club to the Press
Club to attend a lecture by Rev. Alfred
Henry, to be given Monday April 4th, at
2:30 p. m. at the M. E. Church. In behalf
of the club Mrs. Alder accepted the invita-
tion by letter.
	Mrs. E. B. Wells proposed the following
names for acceptance as honorary members:
Mrs. Emily H.Woodmansee, Mrs. Maria Mil-
ler Johnson and Mrs. Augusta Joyce
Crocheron. Motion was seconded and car-
ried.
	Mrs. Emily S. Richards then read a very
ably written paper on Portugal followad by
Mrs. E. B. Wells with a beautiful poem “Is
life worth the living?” written by Dr. Ellis
R. Shipp. A short and interesting sketch
of Spain was given in a well written paper
by Mrs. Phebe C. Young. Dr. Orielle Curtis
dealt with Cuba in written words, glowing,
stirring and eloquent.
	Program presented for April 30, as fol-
lows: Paper on Frances E. Willard, E. B.
Wells; Zolo the French author, Helen V.
Kimball; Poem, Lydia D. Alder; Alfred
Austin, England’s Poet Laureate, Edith
Lovsey; sentiments from noted American
women.
	Critics report given, minutes of meeting
read and approved and dismissed with prayer.
	At the close of the meeting a
a dainty lunch was served through the hos-
pitable courtesy of President R. B. Pratt
and Mrs. E. B. Wells, and a social half
hour was agreeably spent.
