SPRINGTIME.

AH! my love, come here.
Winter's breaking spring is waking,
Hark! a lark sings clear;
From the lakes the flocks are taking
Flight to northward, and I hear
’Neath the ice the ripples making
Music to the ear.
Soon the green will sprin[-],
Where the snow lay shall be pathway,
Sweet with blossoming;
From the glens a chorus ringing,
Will bear outward and away,
Message sweet in echoes singing
Of the coming May.
And the days shall gain 
Each one faire, bringing rarer 
Gifts than flowers that throw
Perfume to the winds, their bearer;
Colors, odors, changing so
Into gifts to each glad sharer—
Autumn’s harvests show.
So, for human hearts
From beginning, still is winging 
Where each lifeway starts,
From Above (sweeter than singing)
Flight to earthward—nothing parts  
Or can turn the angel, bringing
Help to waiting hearts. 
And a voice calls “Here.”
Earth-bands breaking, joy is waking—
Hark!  the song draws near—
From the death-clasp souls are taking
Flight up heavenward and they hear
O'er earth’s mourners angels making,
Welcomes sweet and clear. 
	AUGUSTA JOYCE CROCHERON.
Salt Lake City, March 31, 1898.
