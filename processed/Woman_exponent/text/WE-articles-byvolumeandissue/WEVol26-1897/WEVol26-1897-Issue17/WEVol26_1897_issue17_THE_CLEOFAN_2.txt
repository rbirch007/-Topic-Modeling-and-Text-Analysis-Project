THE CLEOFAN.

	ON Tuesday afternoon the Cleofan enjoy-
ed a topic on the Fairy Queen, by Mrs.
Clara M. Clawson. The Epithalamium
was given by Mrs. Mary J. Clawson. Spen-
cer as a Philosophic Poet, by Mrs. Margie
D. Jennings. All were given in a clear
and concise manner. The club adjourned
for one week.
	March 1, 1898.
	On Tuesday afternoon the Cleofan en-
joyed character readings from Richard III.
by Mrs. Susie D. R. Wells, Mrs. Annie S.
Young, Mrs. Margie D. Jennings and Mrs.
Clara M. Clawson. Mrs. Susie D. R.
Wells gave the story of the play in a most
nteresting manner. The club adjourned
for one week.
	March 8, 1898.
	ON Tuesday afternoon the Cleofan en-
joyed a discussion of Miss M. J. Evans’
paper “The school a moral faction in the
nation,”—by Mrs. Susie D. R. Wells.
Miss. May gave a charming talk on the
Kindergarten system. Reports for the pub-
lic schools were given by Miss Emeline
Wells, Miss Kate Wells, Mrs. Evelyn Y.
Davis and Mrs. Blanche P. Lee. The club
adjourned for one week.
	March 15, 1898.
CLARA T. FELT, Cor. Sec. pro tem.


THE bill to submit a suffrage amendment
was defeated Feb. 15, in the Upper House
of the Iowa Assembly, 50 to 47.
