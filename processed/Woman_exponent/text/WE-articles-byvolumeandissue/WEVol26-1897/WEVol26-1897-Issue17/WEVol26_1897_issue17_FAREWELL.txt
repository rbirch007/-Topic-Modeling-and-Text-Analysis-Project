FAREWELL.
The solemn thought which slumbers in my soul,
Of pain, despair, unhappiness,
Is born of that sad word.
The beams which heretofore have shed their
radiant gleams,
Have sunk to rest;
And in their place a shadowy darkness lurks,
That bids me long for what can never be.
Forgetfulness.
No word responsive to my least desire,
Softens the parting pang;
The oft times sundered thread of hope.
Is riven once again,
Farewell.
	HYACINTH.
Feb. 22, 1898.


A POMPOUS solicitor was one day called
upon at his office by a somewhat younger
member of the profession, who asked his
opinion on a certain point of law. The
elder gentleman drew himself up and said,
“I generally get paid for what I know!”
“Oh very well,”: the other replied taking a
half sovereign from his pocket—“tell me all
you know and give me three and fourpence
change."
