MRS. E. B. WELLS.

Published by request.
	THE pleasing duty has been assigned to
me to write a few thoughts of my esteemed 
friend Mrs. E. B. Wells. Behold this gather-
ing! Women associated in two clubs, the
Press and the Reapers, whose organizer she
is. Here are women of intelligence, poets, 
writers and thinkers, whose common aim is
to elevate themselves, to understand and
further develop the God given talents given
to them.
	On each one she bestows personal care
and encouragement. Each one can tell of
many times when by her counsel she has
been strengthened, and the resolution
formed to make another effort.
	Editor of the “Exponent” for many
years, her words are read the world over.
Its columns are open to the women of Utah
irrespective of nationality or creed. Being
the organ of the Relief Society one of the larg-
