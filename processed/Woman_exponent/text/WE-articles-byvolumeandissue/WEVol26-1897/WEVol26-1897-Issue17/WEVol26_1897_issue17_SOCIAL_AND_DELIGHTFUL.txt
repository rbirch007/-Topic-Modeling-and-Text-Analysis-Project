SOCIAL AND DELIGHTFUL.

	MRS. C. F. WILCOX entertained at her
home in this city a rather large party of
ladies (perhaps sixty) in honor of her
mother, Mrs. Elizabeth J. Stevenson, who
on that day reached her sixtieth anniver-
sary. It was refreshing to meet socially so
many who for years had been familiarlly
associated and to converse upon the vari-


ous experiences and incidents which make
the chapters of each one’s life of special
interest to those near and dear by ties of
friendship and sisterhood. Mrs. M. Isa-
bella Horne gave some interesting remin-
iscences of early days and of Joseph the
Prophet. The famous musician, Prof. J.
McLellan, gave on the piano Ave Maria
and an air from the Bohemian Girl, also an
"Inspiration." Prof. Wm. Done also play-
ed some of Beethoven. Camilia C. Cobb sang
in her sweet way the old song "Then you’ll
remember me." Lula gave the poetical
tribute to Mrs. Stevenson given below and
also recited another of her poems entitled
"My Father." Mrs. Minnie J. Snow sang
sweetly in a plaintive fashion "Far Away."
	Mrs. Wilcox read from Mrs.Wells’ poems
"The Dear Old Garden." There were very
dainty refreshments elegantly served and
highly enjoyed, and the hostess gave us
quite a display of old-fashioned capes of
muslin embroidery and other similar arti-
cles of fine needlework.
	Mrs Stevenson was the recipient of pro-
fuse and sincere congratulations and pretty
presents, tokens of affection and love, also
many good wishes and bouquets of violets,
carnations and roses. The writer adds her
congratulations to the many others wishing
her every joy and comfort possible for mor-
tals in this probation and eternal happiness
hereafter when sorrow and pain are no
more and we enter with our dear ones gone
before upon the life immortal.
