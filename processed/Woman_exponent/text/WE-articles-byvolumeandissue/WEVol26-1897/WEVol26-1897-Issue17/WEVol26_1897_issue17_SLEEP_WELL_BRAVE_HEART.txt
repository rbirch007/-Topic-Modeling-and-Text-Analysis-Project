SLEEP WELL BRAVE HEART.

	[Written while Mrs. Dickinson sat by the side
of Miss Willard’s casket Monday, Feb 21, 1898.]
SLEEP well, brave heart, beloved of Christ and
	crowned,
		He gives thee sleep.
The wide world’s love enwraps thy slumber round,
		God gives thee sleep.
His angels smile; His stricken children weep;
Yet smiles, nor tears, shall break thy blessed
	sleep.
Oh, wondrous face! whose mystic, solemn grace
		O’er-floods the gloom,
Till grief—in all this sorrow-haunted place—
		No more finds room.
Show us, dear God, what glory greets her eyes,
Let us, too, hear the voice that bids her rise.
Chide not our tears! So weak we are and blind,
		Yet she would share 
Her gladness with us who are left behind.
		Heed Thou our prayer!
Not yet. Not yet? Tarries the vision still?
Then, grant us, Lord—with her—to love Thy will
To work Thy will, to follow where she trod, 
		Without a fear;
To drink her cup; to climb the heights of God
		Knowing her near;
To make her joy more joyful by our strife.
So may we share even here her glorious life.
So may our homes, our land in shame so long, 
		Be cleansed from wrong;
So may our breaking hearts through love be 
	strong,
		Through love be strong.
Bless still, through us, earth’s sinning and op-
	pressed.
Thy work rests not, dear heart. Take thou the
	rest.
			MARY LOWE DICKINSON,
—Union Signal
