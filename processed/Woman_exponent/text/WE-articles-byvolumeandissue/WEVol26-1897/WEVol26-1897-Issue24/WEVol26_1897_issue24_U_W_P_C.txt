U. W. P. C.

	The ladies of the U. W. P. C. met ac-
cording to adjournment April 30, 1898, in
the "Woman's Exponent" office, President
Romania B. Pratt presiding. Opening
prayer by Dr. Ellis R. Shipp. Roll call re-
sponded to by quotations from noted
American women.
	Under unfinished business President Pratt
stated that Margaret A. Caine had been ap-
pointed delegate to the Council of Woman's


Clubs. Under new business President
Pratt reported a meeting of the Council
held Saturday morning April 30. General
discussion followed. The Curfew law was
discussed and on motion of Lydia D. Alder
was laid over for further consideration;
seconded by Mrs. E. B. Wells and carried.
	A very pleasing and interesting paper on
Frances Elizabeth Willard was read by
Mrs. E. B. Wells. One on Emile Zola by
Helen V. Kimball was listened to with
deep interest. A poem by Lydia D. Alder
"Hispania" was sympathetic, patriotic and
timely. President Pratt made some excel-
lent remarks on the Spanish-Cuban situa-
tion and the causes given in history leading
to the present trouble, referring particularly
to Spanish tyranny and cruelty. Edith R.
Lovsey was excused from giving a paper on
Alfred Austin, poet laureate of England.
	The program was presented for next meet-
ing, minutes read, corrected and approved,
and prayer offered by Ella W. Hyde.
	Program of U. W. P. C. for May 31,
1898. Patriotic original sentiments on
war. Short talk on Alfred Austin, Edith
Lovsey. The Red Cross, Annie W. Can-
non. Royal family of Spain and the pres-
ent generals, Luella Rhodes. Current
events, including the letters sent from wo-
men's organizations concerning the war,
and a mother's letter whose son was killed
on the Maine, Mary J. Lambert.
