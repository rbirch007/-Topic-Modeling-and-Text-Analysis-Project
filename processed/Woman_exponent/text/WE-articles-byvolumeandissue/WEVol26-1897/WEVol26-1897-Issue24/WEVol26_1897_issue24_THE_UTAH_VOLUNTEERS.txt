THE UTAH VOLUNTEERS.

	The hearts of the mothers, wives, sisters,
daughters and sweethearts, and of many
fathers, brothers and friends in this fair
State have been stirred to their inmost
depths during the last few days by the de-
parture from home of the Utah Volunteers,
who it is confidently expected will soon be
called into actual service in this cruel war
now in operation between Spain and the
United States. The leave takings and fare-
wells have been pathetic to say the least,
though all have tried to be brave and stout-
hearted when saying their goodbye and
“God bless you.”
	It is the first experience of the kind since
these mountain vales were settled, though
there have been Indian skirmishes in which
some have had to engage, yett hese difficult-
ies were near home and only a few were
called out and that only for a brief time;
