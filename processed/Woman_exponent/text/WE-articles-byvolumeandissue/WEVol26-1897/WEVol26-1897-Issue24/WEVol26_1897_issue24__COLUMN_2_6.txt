	Sister Armeda Young spoke of teaching
our children to be peace-makers instead of
the reverse, knew there was much responsi-
bility resting upon the mothers.
	Sister Maria Nebeker said that God had
promised to be a husband to the widow,
this she had seen literally fulfilled in her
own case. Spoke of the war and said God
had promised to fight our battles, felt if our
young men were called out they would be
looked after as they were in olden times.
Said we were all in the hands of the Lord,
and prayed He would look down in mercy
upon us.
	Dr. E. S. Barney spoke of a meeting she
attended of sisters and after the meet-
ing many of them came up to her for a
blessing, and how the Spirit of the Lord
was poured out upon her, also spoke on the
war. Spoke of patriarchal blessings, urged
the sisters to have them and said there was
virtue in them. Spoke of faith, said we,
should cultivate it more than we did and
made many instructive remarks.
	Sister Annie T. Hyde felt impressed to
read the 98th section of the Doctrine and
Covenants, felt it was applicable to the pres-
ent time and thought President McKinley
had unknowingly carried out this plan.
Felt that every mother who prayed earnest-
ly to the Lord that her son would be pre-
served from harm if he were pressed into
service, would have her prayer answered.
	Sister Clara C. Cannon bore a strong
testimony, felt impressed that our sons
would not be called upon for active service,
we need not fear for the Lord was with us
and would continue to bless us, felt to re-
joice through it all and prayed the Lord
would bless every mother and give her.
peace.
	Counselor Elizabeth Webb knew we
were greatly blessed that we could meet
here in peace when all is strife and war
outside, felt our sons would be preserved,
for the Lord’s care was over us. Testified
that there was a mighty power in prayer,
made many beautiful remarks and urged
the sisters to be faithful in their Relief
Society labors, it would benefit them.
	A sister in the congregation who was a
stranger said she would feel condemned if
she did not express her feelings. Although
a stranger in our midst she could feel the
same sweet spirit that always attends such
meetings.
	Counselor Stevenson felt we had had a
very enjoyable and beneficial time, made
some closing remarks, and meeting ad-
journed for two weeks by singing “Satan’s
empire long has flourished,” and benedic-
tion by Sister E. S. Taylor.
