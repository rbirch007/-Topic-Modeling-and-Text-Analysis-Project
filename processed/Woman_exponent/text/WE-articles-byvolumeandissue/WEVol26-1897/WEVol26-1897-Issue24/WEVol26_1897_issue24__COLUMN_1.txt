	President Horne said she was glad to see
so many sisters present in response to the
call published. The meeting would be a
little different than usual, as a matter of
business of great importance was to be con-
sidered. “Dr. Margaret Roberts has very
generously offered to instruct a corps of
sisters in the art of nursing the sick among
us free of charge. We have counseled to-
gether and estimate that about four sisters
from each of the wards of this Stake would
make a good sized class. We think if the
Relief Society presidents and those of the
the Young Ladies Mutual would consult
together they could get such sisters as
would make good nurses, then after they
had received their education we would ex-
pect them to devote some of their time to
nursing the poor when sick in their respect-
ive wards, or if the sisters in one ward
should be out nursing, then some could be
called from another ward. None would be
expected to leave where they were nursing
until such time as the sick could be left.
