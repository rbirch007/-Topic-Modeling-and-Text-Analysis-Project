LITERARY CLUB ELECTION.

	The Last meeting for 1897-98 of the
Ladies’ Literary club was held on Friday.
It was a business session only, and the
principal event of interest was the annual
election of officers. All retiring officers
made reports on the season’s work. The
election resulted in the following
roster: Mrs. Eugene Lewis president; Mrs.
William Ingleheart, first vice-president,
Mrs. L. E. Hall, second vice-president;
Mrs. Clara Colton, recording secretary; Mrs.
G. R. Hancock, corresponding secretary;
Mrs. E. B. Critchlow, treasurer; Miss Ethel
Paul, critic; Mrs. J. C. Royle, historian;
Mrs. S. F. Fenton, librarian; Miss. Emma
McCornick, Mrs. T. G. Webber, Mrs, H.
W. Brown, directors for one year; Mrs. W.
A. Nelden, Mrs. F. A. Vincent, Mrs. R.
C. Woodruff, directors for two years.
	The section chairmen had been previous-
ly named. Some changes in the sections
have been made, the art section being dis-
continued and two new ones created,
music and French. The sections with their
chairmen are: History, Mrs: J. H. Brown;
tourist, Mrs. R. C. Woodruff; Shakespeare,
Mrs. John Caine; home and educational,
Mrs. G. F, Stiehl; current events and litera-
ture, Mrs. S. W. Morris; music, Mrs. W.
A. Nelden; French, Mrs. Eugene Lewis.
