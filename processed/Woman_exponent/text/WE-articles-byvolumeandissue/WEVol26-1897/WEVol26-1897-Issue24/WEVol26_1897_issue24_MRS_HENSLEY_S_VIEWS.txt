MRS. HENSLEY’S VIEWS.
	Mrs. Sorensen’s paper was also discussed.
Mrs. Hensley who spoke from one of the
private boxes, was very earnest in her ap-
proval of the argument Mrs. Sorensen had
advanced. She said she had long been
amazed at the mock modesty which had
prevented the proper consideration of the
subject Mrs. Sorensen had so intelligently
treated.
	The Countess of Brazza, when the ap-
plause which greeted Mrs. Hensley had sub-
sided, gave a brief but interesting descrip-
tion of the advancement of practical educa-
tion of women in Europe.
