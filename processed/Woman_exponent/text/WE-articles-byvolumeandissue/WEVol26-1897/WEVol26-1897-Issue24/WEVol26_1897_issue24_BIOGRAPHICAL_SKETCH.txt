BIOGRAPHICAL SKETCH.

Eliza Wakefield Hawkins, daughter of
John and Annabella Park Wakefield, was
born in Fairford, Glocestershire, England,
Feb. 11, 1827. She married Wm. Hawkins
when she was eighteen years of age, re-
moved with him to Australia and remained
there two or three years. She was baptized
into the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-
day Saints in Sidney, Australia, in March,
1856, and started for Utah in 1857, arriving
in Beaver, January 1858.
	Although they had no posterity of their
own their home was never without children,
several orphans being made welcome and
regarding them as parents. These orphans
and their children who are very numerous,
deeply mourn their loss and will ever cher-
ish a loving remembrance of them.
	Sister Hawkins became a member of the
Relief Society when it was first organized
in Beaver, and labored faithfully as a teach-
er. She also filled the position of counselor
to President R. W. Tyler for several years.
She was ready to respond to every call, do-
nating liberally and was ever anxious to re-
lieve suffering being of a sympathetic nature.
	Her spirit took its flight on the 26th of
January, 1898. She had been a great suf-
ferer for a long time, and the summons to
join in the spirit world the companion who
had left her seven years previous, came as a
welcome message to her.
