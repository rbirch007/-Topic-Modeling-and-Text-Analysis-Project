A WOMAN’S TESTIMONY.

	How thankful we should be, as Latter-day
Saints, that we are permitted to live on the
earth in this our day, when the words of the
prophets of old are being fulfilled every day be_
fore our eyes, and that the power of Jehovah is
felt on the earth; for are we not the favored of
the Lord? Is He not stretching out his mighty
arm for our deliverance? Should we not be
proud of our name and station? What should
be our bearing before the world? We should
not give way to pride and folly, but seek to
serve the Lord our God with full purpose of
heart; a prayer should be ever on our lips.
Righteousness and truth should be our motto;
seek ever to draw unto Him who has said, “I
am the Lord thy God.” The earth is His and
the fulness thereof, the cattle on a thousand
hills; and wo are His Saints; and has He not
promised, in his own due time He would give
all to His people, but we must live faithful
before Him, Saints in very deed; shall we deny
any principle He has revealed for our exalta-
tion? No, verily, no! for have we not a testi-
mony within our hearts, that every principle is
heaven born; for many years have I lived in
plural marriage; as well might you tell me that
the sun would stand still at my bidding as tell
me Celestial marriage is not true, it is the grand
high school to exaltation in our Father’s King-
dom, where we shall meet with our Elder
Brother, even Jeses Christ, who sitteth on the
right hand of God, who has said, “Fear not,
little flock, for I have overcome the world.”
There is no happier lot than that of a true Lat-
ter-day Saint. Let us do the part assigned unto
us well, that it may be said unto us, “Well
done, good and faithful servant, enter thou into
the joy of thy Lord.” God bless you, dear Edi-
tor, and all your co-workers who write so good
for our dear little paper.			B. M. B.
	Richmond, April 4th, 1883.
