RESOLUTIONS.

	Resolutions of respect to our President, Margaret A.
Lambert, who departed this life January 6th, 1883,
aged twenty three years, ten months and eighteen days.
	Through the providence of God we are again called
to mourn, the loss of one of our members by death, two
others having died within the year, and this time our
beloved and respected President, whose loss we deeply
mourn for we loved her for her kind and gentle man-
ners, her faithfulness and integrity in the cause of
Christ, her prompt and punctual attendance to the
duties of her office, and the exemplary life she led
worthy of imitation in both her public and private
career.
	Therefore, be it resolved, that we condole with her hus-
band in his irreparable bereavement of a faithful and
loving wife and companion, and with her parents and
relatives and friends.
	May we long remember her saintly life, and emulate
her virtues, and her moral, religious and domestic life,
and may her infant daughter never know the want of a
mother's care, but live to fill up a life of usefulness.
	Resolved, that we present a copy of the foregoing reso-
lutions to the parents and husband of the deceased.
	Resolved, that we send a copy to the EXPONENT for
publication.
							ANNA LAMBERT,
							ELVA WOOLSTENHULME,
							EDITH ATWOOD,
											Councelors.
					ELIZA ATWOOD, Assistant Secretary.
Kamas, January 22nd, 1883.
	P. S. Since writing the above her infant daughter
has passed away.
