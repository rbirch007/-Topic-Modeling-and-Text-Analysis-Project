DEAR EXPONENT:
	I thought perhaps a few lines from this out
of the way place might find a corner in your
delightful little paper. Last Friday, the 26th
of the month, was the birthday of one of our
good mothers in Israel, Sister M. P. Killian.
She is President of the Reief Society of this
place. Having recently lost her husband she
was feeling quite low spirited and cast down.
The brethren and sisters turned out en masse
and made things lively in the shape of a sur-
prise dinner, Sister Killian not being aware
of their intentions until they came into her
house well laden with baskets, etcetera. Soon
the tables were fairly groaning under their
weight of good things and all sat down to par-
take of the delicious repast. After dinner the
Choir sung several hymns, Bishop S. E. John-
son, made a few remarks and spoke in praise
of Sister Killian, who was completely over-
come by this unexpected demonstration. Bro-
ther Johnson presented her a beautiful card
bearing a quotation from Psalms, 27th chapter,
17th verse. "Wait on the Lord; be of good
courage, and He shall strenghen thine heart:
wait, I say, on the Lord."
	the evening passed away, each one enjoying
themselves to the utmost.
