PATCHWORK.

'Tis not genteel, they tell me now,
	To make a patchwork quilt,
'Tis sign of poverty they vow
	And poverty is guilt

In worldly eyes—well let it be,
But you and I should clearer see,
I think it is a branch of art
That forms a good, important part
In home economy and joy,
And pleasant hours without alloy,
May thus be spent in social chat,
While fingers fly this way and that,
Forming designs of light and shade
Until a perfect piece is made;
And then the quilting, Oh how nice!
To meet in friendship and rejoice,
When work is done the circle spreads.
Mingling the white and golden heads
Of father, mother, sister, brother—
[If not your own, why then, some other,]
In pleasant play or may-be dance.
While jest and song the hours enhance;
From many a patchwork quilt I trace
Some well known name, some dear, dear face,
And from one star will spring anew,
Some time of love and friendship true.
Our parents loved those simple joys,
Shall we forget—their girls and boys,
And in the new-scenes scorn the old?
Ah no! there lies the purest gold,
And is not life all patchwork too,
Our thoughts and actions, false or true,
Pieces that go to form the whole
Great plan divine the Gods control;
Then be our station high or low
Let’s cut and fit the pieces so
That perfect shape and colors blend,
Praying that One above to lend
His wisdom where our own would fall,
Courage when our weak hearts would quail.
Then may our life work be replete
In all things perfect and complete.
									HOPE.
