DEAR SISTER WELLS:
	I am happy to say that our little meetings
are well attended, considering our scattered
condition. We have had one Primary fair.
A large table was covered with children's arti-
cles, such as wool flowers and fancy card work,
hoods, stockings, and many other things that
I will not mention. We have had a visit from
Sister Helena Madson and her Counselors.
She is our Stake President. She is a very
kind lady. Our much beloved President is
not at home at present; when she is away we
miss her kind voice and wise counsel. I am
but thirteen years old and this is my first at-
tempt to write to you. I send a New Year’s
greeting to all my Primary friends. Praying
that our Heavenly Father will bless us all,
that His Spirit will rest upon us is my prayer.
	I remain, your sister in the Gospel,
						MINNIE ALLRED.
	P. S. Sister Wells, I am one of the little
girls that helped make the hair wreath that
was sent to you from Chester three years ago.
I thank you for the little Primary Hymn
book you sent me.			M. A.
