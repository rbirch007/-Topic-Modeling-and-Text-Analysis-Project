A TRIBUTE
		TO HANNAH T. KING.
Once more, dear muse, I pray thee, come to me,
And tune thy lyre, and let thy music flow,
In praise of her, whose soothing melody
Hath lightened many a heavy load of woe.

Her themes are not the deeds of mighty men—
The heroes of the crimson battle-fields— 
She sings of those whose lowly lives have been
Crowned with the glory that all goodness yields.

She sings of those whom poverty and care,
And all the changing evils of their lot,
Have sought in vain their weary feet to tear
From that sweet rest they have so dearly bought.

She sings of those whose gentle lives are given
To deeds of mercy and to words of peace;
O, may she sing, when life’s frail bands are riven,
With those whose songs of joy shall never cease.
