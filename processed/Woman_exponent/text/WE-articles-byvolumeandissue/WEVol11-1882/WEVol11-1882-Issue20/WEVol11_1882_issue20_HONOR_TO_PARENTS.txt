HONOR TO PARENTS.

"Honor thy father and thy mother that thy days may be
long in the land which the Lord thy God giveth thee."

If thou with angry words would strike,
Or striking wound another;
O never let such venomed dart
Descend upon thy mother.

Remember all the days gone by,
When thou wert in her keeping,
When gazing on her love-lit eye,
Or on her bosom sleeping.

She gave thee of her life and heart,
And blessed the day that bore thee;
In love she ever bore her part,
And softly guarded o’er thee.

When helpless to her hand you clung,
Her warm heart fondly beating,
Upon your gentlest accents hung,
Its tenderness repeating.

But now to stalwart manhood grown,
Such scenes no more reverting,
Beware of words like arrows flown,
For who can tell their hurting.

When feeble stops yet bear her on,	
Toward life’s other border,
Still let the love she bears her son,
some balm in life afford her.

Let not thy tongue speak idle words,
Or thoughts to grieve or pain her;
But cheer her heart if lone or sad,
While you may yet retain her.

For soon her weary steps will pass
Beyond life’s busy portal,
And earth will hold one spirit less,
And heaven hold one immortal.

And then in all the coming years
That	hold	thy manhood’s glory,
You’ll see no more her smiles or tears,
Or listen to her story.
						MARY.
