A GIFT.
		______
Sweet souled and faithful one,
To-day I have no other
Dear gift, thee to bestow upon,
Than this—God bless my mother!

And with it, these fair flowers,
Emblems of peace and beauty,
Hoping they’ll cheer some lonely hours,
Of waiting and of duty.

A year ago—O how
Are earthly pleasures riven !
Father was with us then, but now, 
He sings God’s praise in heaven.

Never will be complete
Our peace and joys—O never !
Till in that holy, safe retreat,	
No kindred tics will sever.

But there, O. blissful hours !
When perfoct family chains,
Unite through Jesus’ love and powers,
And joy eternal reigns !
April 3rd, 1883. 					Lula.
