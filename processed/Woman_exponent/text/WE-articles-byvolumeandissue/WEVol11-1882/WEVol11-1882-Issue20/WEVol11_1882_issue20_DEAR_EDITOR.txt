DEAR EDITOR:
	As it is the contributions that in part com-
prise your valuable paper, I thought I would
throw in my little mite, hoping it will be ac-
ceptable. The EXPONENT has proved a great
blessing to the ladies in this vicinity, fo[-] as
yet we are in an out-of-the-way place, and can-
not get information of what is going on except
by what we glean from reading. What a great


blessing it is to be able to read! It proves in
this, as in all other things, that God in his in-
finite goodness' has provided for his children!
When I stop to meditate on the many good
books—some of them written by inspiration—
for the benefit of the human family, I cannot
bat have a greater reverence for my Heavenly
Father for the gifts he has blessed us with.
And do we appreciate it as we should? I fear
there are a great many of us who do not!
What are we placed on this earth for? Is it
to work; toil and eke out a miserable exis-
tence? I think not, and I think that the Lord
will hold us accountable for letting our talent
lie dormant; our minds need food as well as
our bodies. I believe the Lord intends that
his children shall enjoy the blessings and gifts
that he has blessed them with. We should avail
ourselves of every opportunity that comes in our
way, to improve our minds. What class of wo-
men is there on the face of the earth that
should be more noble, great and good than
those of the Latter-day Saints—with the spirit
of their religion to help them? Then let us,
dear sisters, seek for improvement, and prove
to our Heavenly Father that we are not uncon-
scious of the many blessings He has given on
this earth for us to enjoy. And let us place
such books before our children as will instil
in their little minds principles true and exalt-
ing, that when the Lord comes to make up his
jewels, we will not be so low and ignorant that
He will pass us by.		JOSIE E. CHILDS.
	Castle Dale, Utah.
