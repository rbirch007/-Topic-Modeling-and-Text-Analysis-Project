 REPORT TO APRIL CONFERENCE, 1883.

   Financial report of the Deseret Hospital from
October 6th, 1882, to April 6th, 1883, inclusive.
October 6th, 1882, to balance: cash, $1,858.00; or
ders, $103.20. Receipts during the six months,
cash,1,815.25; orders, $37.25; groceries, $47.50.
Received in fees per patients, cash, $483.10; or-
ders, $270.35; produce, $33.62 1/2; on a cow, $33.00.
Total, $825.07. Total cash to date, $4,101.85.
Total cash expense, $3,143.63; cash on hand, $1,
017.72. Total expense in orders, produce and
groceries, $129.68 1/2; orders on hand, $62.34. Total
expense, cash, orders, etc., $3,573.31 1/2; average
expense per month, $593.55 1/2. Fees to physi-
cians, $490.00; matron and helps,$734.00. Rent
for building, $360.00.
   In addition to the amounts donated in cash,
orders, etc., there have been donations of coal,
five tons, three stoves, a liberal quantity of wines
and brandy, towels, blankets, quilts, pillow-
slips and bedding of all kinds. One cow and
calf, one pig and quite a number of chickens.
One quarter of beef and one of venison and
many other useful articles. The names of the
donors would fill a pamphlet. Of the money
given, the Unity Club gave the largest amount
during the past six months. The Primary As-
sociation of the Eleventh Ward gave $41.00.
From party in the Fourteenth Ward by the
Relief Society and Young Ladies Association,
$20.00. Donation by Joab Lawrence, $100, at
New Year’s.
   The people have been liberal and the Execu-
tive Board feel encouraged in their labors. It is
expected that an annual meeting of the officers
and members of the Deseret Hospital Associa-
tion will be held on the 12th of May next in the
Council House in this city at 2 p.m, to transact
business pertaining to the Association. Re-
spectfully submitted.
						E.	B.	Wells,
								Secretary.
Apil 6th, 1883.
