A WOMAN’S LETTER.

Laurel, Franklin Co., Indiana,
March 28th, 1883.
EDITOR WOMAN'S EXPONENT:
	Having read your paper, sent me by an
Elder of your Church, who happened to be in
this section of country preaching, whom my
husband invited home with him to stay over
night, from a meeting held in this place in
October last, viz., S. R. Marks, I admit my
mind has been much enlightened and I must
say that I admire the tone of your paper, and
confess that it has done me much good. I
have always heard so much evil of your peo-
ple, that I was like many others, rather preju-
auced against your religion, but I am happy to
say through the preaching of Elder Marks and
reading of your paper, that prejudice is entirely
removed from my mind.
	I was always taught to believe that our sex
were very much abused, and kept in abject
slavery among the “Mormons,” but I am
pleased to learn from Elder Marks, and by
reading your paper, that it is the reverse; for I
find that your people are now fighting with the
arm of the law, and the word of truth, for the
rights of women; while those who have been
crying slavery, are the ones who are trying to
enslave you.
	I am not of your faith, but I must confess
it is Scriptural, and it must triumph over the
false systems of to-day, and I hope that all


those who fight against the liberty of your peo-
ple will come to shame and dismay; for liberty
is guaranteed to all by our glorious Constitu-
tion, and belongs to Utah’s people as much as
any in this, or any other section of our common
country, and should be enjoyed by its citizens
in common with ours.
	Wishing the greatest success for your valu-
able paper, and your triumph over the enemies
of liberty,
		I remain	respectfully yours,
