SILENT VOICES.

The voice of the heart, and the voice of the brain,
Their language is silent, but thrilling the strain!
What volumes they write! and what volumes they tell,
And the soul stores them all in its innermost cell.

At midnight, when sleep has forsaken the eye,
These voices come thronging, and sleep they defy;
And memory rising, obeying their call
Draws aside her sweet veil, or throws back her sad pall!

Alone on the mountains, these voices we hear,
Breaking sweet on the heart, on the brain, on the ear;
Transporting the soul to a land of its own,
And binding it round by a mystical zone!

In a voice without sound, they converse with the soul,
Defying restraint, and defying control;
'Tis a carnival sweet, that the spirit then holds,
And wraps the corporeal in magical folds!

'Tis a foretaste of that which in future will be,
A mirror upheld in which visions we see!
A picture presented—a realm of the soul,
Releasing the spirit from mortal control!

How we hunger, and listen to voices like these,
That come in the flowers, that come in the trees,
That come in the cataract, roaring and wild,
That has often our soul of its sorrow beguiled!

Let us often be silent, and watch for the time,
When such voices ring out in their mystical chime!
Such are often in mercy sent down from above,
Infused by the spirit of God!—who is love!

Oh life! earthly life! thy ENIGMA is grand!
Thy mystical units of elysian land !—
Thy whisp’rings bring balm to a sorrowing soul,
And it seeks not the magical charm to control.

'Tis like manna descending each day, as of old, 
And the same loving shepherd is watching His fold;
And His children are fed in a mystical way,
For the voice of the shepbord they love and obey.
								HANNAH T. KING.
	Salt Lake City, Feb. 21st, 1883.
