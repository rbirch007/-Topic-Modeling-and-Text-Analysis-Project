RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.
				______
	At our last choir practice the following preamble and
resolutions were unanimously adopted.
	Whereas, It has pleased an All-wise Providence to
call from our midst, by the hand of death, our beloved
friend and Sister, Therefore,.
Resolved, that we do recognize in the death of our


Sister, Mary Williams Dorlus, the loss of an able, effi-
cient and faithful member of the Ephraim Sabbath
Choir.
  Resolved, That we esteem her past, faithful labors
and devotion to duty, as worthy the fellowship and
emulation of us all.
	Resolved, That we condole and deeply sympathize
with the bereaved husband, father, mother, relatives
and friends, in the loss of a loving wife, kind and affec-
tionate daughter, and true friend.
Resolved, That these resolutions be spread upon the
records of the T. L. M. I. A. of Ephraim, Deseret News
and Woman’s Exponent.
			Yours Respectfully,	
				JAMES STEVENSON,
				HELEN E. ARMSTRONG,
				GEORGE JENSEN,
				J. E. CHRISTIANSEN.
		In behalf of the Ephraim Sabbath Choir.
March 17th, 1883.


﻿Died, at Ephraim, March 16th, 1883, of palsy, after
five days hard sickness, Mary Williams, wife of C. C. N.
Dorious, and daughter of Steven and Emma Williams.
	Deceased was born January 2nd, 1860, at Ephraim.
She died as she lived, a true and faithful Latter-day
Saint, and her early departure from us cast a gloom
over the people.						COM.
