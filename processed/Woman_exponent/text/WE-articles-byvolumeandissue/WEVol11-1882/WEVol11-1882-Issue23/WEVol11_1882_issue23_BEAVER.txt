BEAVER.
	A Conference of the Ladies' Relief Society
of Beaver Stake, was held in the Meeting
House at Minersville, on March 2nd, Sister
Lucinda Howd presiding. Sister Howd's 
Counselors and the Presidency of the different
wards were present. There was quite a good
attendance of the sisters, and a good repre-
sentation of the brethren. The presidents of
the several wards gave verbal reports, showing
them to be in a prosperous condition. Bishop
McKnight, of Minersville, expressed himself
as being well pleased with the reports he had
heard, and gave the sisters good instructions.
Mrs. S. M. Dell read an extract from a sermon
by Joseph Smith, published in the Contributor
of August last, calculated to give the people a 
proper understanding of the vexed question,
whether the sisters have a right to lay on
hands for the recovery of the sick. Remarks
were made by a number of the brethren and
sisters, that were instructive and pleasing, and
the conference throughout was thoroughly en-
joyable. The young ladies held their confer-
ence in the evening of the same day; on the
day following the Primary conference was
held, Mrs. M. A. Tolton presiding.
