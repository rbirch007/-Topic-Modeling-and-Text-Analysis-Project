.H. YOUNG, JANUARY 25, 1883.

I ne'er had counted till to-night
	The precious years since first we met;
They've flown so quickly, and each one
	Has grown in value richer yet.

My life at that time had not known
	Aught worth the name of sober care,
Though premonitions sometimes came
	(As night winds far-off sounds may bear).

And nearer coming also found
	These, rich in wisdom, near my side,
True friends, who lent their helping hands,
	And gave their loving words beside.

O, may I never lose the joy
	That I have met and won such friends,
But keep it also through the life
	That through eternity extends.

Go thou, my page, gently for me,
	And from my heart this message bear;
To-night, when friends in love surround,
	My spirit shall be also there.

And still for many year to come
	May truest friends their greetings bring,
And in unbroken circle join
	Their praises of thy lives to sing.
