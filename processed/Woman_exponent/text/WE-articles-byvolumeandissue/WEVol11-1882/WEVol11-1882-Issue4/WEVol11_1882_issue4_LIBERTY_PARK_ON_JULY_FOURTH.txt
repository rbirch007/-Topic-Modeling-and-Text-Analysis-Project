LIBERTY PARK ON JULY FOURTH.

	The Fourth of July was very appropriately
celebrated in Liberty Park, just south of this
city. The Park is a very suitable place for public


festivities, but will no doubt have more improve
ments made upon it from time to time, as the
city fathers shall deem wise and expedient.
His Excellency Eli H. Murray, Governor of
Utah, was President of the day. The orator of
the occasion was Judge P. T. Van Zile. His
oration was well written, comprehensive and
poetical. Responses to toasts were made by
the following gentlemen, Mayor Jennings pro-
posed the toast, "The President of the United
States." responded to by S. J. Jonasson. "The
Union, No North, No South, No East, No West
but one, inseparable now and forever," was res-
ponded to by Arthur Stayner. "The Constitu-
tion" by J. L. Rawlins. "Our Heroes Dead and
Living by Gen. McD. McCook. "Utah," by
O. J, Hollister. "The Press," by R. W. Sloan.
"The Industrial Arts.," by G. G. Bywater.
"Education: The Foster-mother of a Virtuous
Commonwealth," by J. M. Benedict. These
responses were, all of them, interesting and to
the point; if we had space we would gladly re-
publish them. The music was excellent and
the affair passed off most agreeably to all parties.
The fireworks during the evening, on the pub-
lic grounds, were undoubtedly the finest yet, in
Salt Lake City.
