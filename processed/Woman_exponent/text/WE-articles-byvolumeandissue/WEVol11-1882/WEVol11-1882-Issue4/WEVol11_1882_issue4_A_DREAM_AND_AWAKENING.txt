A DREAM AND AWAKENING.

I toiled 'till my weary fingers
	Groped over the long dark scam
Then dropped into fitful slumber,
	Of wild and feverish dream;
Where perplexities without number,
	Crossed ever my path between.

Just at the hour of midnight,
	At the last echoing chime
Of the church clock in the tower,
	I 'woke to bewail lost time,—
Time never restored hereafter,
	Though sought for with tears sublime.

Yet strove to resume my labor,
	To finish the task begun
That conscience might give the verdict
	Of 'well and faithfully done:
Most priceless reward of merit
	By tolling mortals won.

Then came through my curtained window
	Showers of silvery spray,
And all common things around me
	Rolled back with the dying day:
Leaving a halo of glory
	That gladdened my heart for aye.

The moonbeams flooded my chamber
	With glowing marvelous light;
While the rainbow hues of brightness
	Grew more dazzling to the sight,
A power unseen impelled me
	To take up my pen and write.

Sweet Love, the blessed Evangel
	Waved high her banner of gold;
'Till from brain and heart o'er burdened
	A torrent of feeling rolled.
Like pent up stream from the mountain
	No longer to be controlled.

And over a fair pure surface
	Swift arrows of thought sped on
Thro' the inky tide of blackness,
	'Till the golden light was gone,
And night had travailed with darkness
	Giving birth to coming dawn.
