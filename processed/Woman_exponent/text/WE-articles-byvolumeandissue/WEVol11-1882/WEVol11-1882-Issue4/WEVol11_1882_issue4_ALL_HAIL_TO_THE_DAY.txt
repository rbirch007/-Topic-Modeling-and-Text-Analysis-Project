ALL HAIL TO THE DAY.
	SONG FOR THE CELEBRATION OF THE FOURTH
		OE JULY, 1881, IN SALT LAKE CO. WHICH ON
		ACCOUNT OF THE ASSASSINATION OF PREST.
		GARFIELD DID NOT TAKE PLACE.
	All hall to the day when the Standard of Freedom,
		Bright Phoeix arose o'er proud Tyranny's grave:
	Loud cheers for the vet'rans whose hearts swelled with
			courage
		Ere th' conquest was won by the sword of the brave.
	All hail to our sacred, diving Constitution--
		That heav'n-inspired gift, stamp'd with virtue's life-
			lease;
	We crown with rich gems of true, loyal devotion,
		That bulwark of Justice--that trophy of Peace.
	Those statesmen knew not that Jehovah was guiding
		Their thoughts and their moves, in those soul-trying
			hours,
	When their names they aflix'd to the bold Declaration
		of national Rights and all national Pow'rs.
					Chorus.
	With Washington--know as the Sire of his country--
		In th' annals of honor, their names are enroll'd:
	Their mem'ries are dear to all lovers of freedom--
		Enshrined in true hearts with the sages of old.
						Chorus.
	Should traitors presume, with oppression's foul daring
		From th' dear Constitution, to sever one thread:
	The vengeance of God, shorn of m[x]rey's forbearing,
		Will follow with blight the perfidions tread.
						Chorus.
	The banner that waves o'er the mountains of Ephraim,
		Endow'd with insignia our fore-fathers won:
	We'll honor, preserve, and unsullied bequeath it,
		To bless generations while time shall roll on.
	All hail to our sacred diving Constitution;
		That heav'n-inspired gift, stamped with virtue's life-
			lease:
	We crown with rich gems of true loyal devotion,
		That bulwark of Justice--that trophy of Peace.
