					CHESTERFIELD.


Of vain things, excuses are the vainest,

	Justice is the Queen of all virtues. - Ciero.

	Habit is a cable. We spin a thread of it
each day.
	There is a remedy for every wrong and a
satisfaction for every soul.

	One grain of truth brings down the scales
against a world of error.

	Mrs. Sarah E. Franklin was a delegate from
Indiana to the state convention.

	More than half the newspaper in the world
are printed in the English language.

	Mrs. Helen M. Gouger, of Lafayette, and
Miss Florence Hardin, of Pendleton, spoke on
the Fourth of July at Anderson.

	A large number of young women graduated
from the Boston University this year than ever
before, From the School of Floral Arts there
were seven, from the Theological School one,
and from the School of Medicine thirteen.
Three were admitted to the degree of M. A.
- Miss Alice Leon Mumford, Miss Marion
Talbot and Miss Agnes Fitz Henry Williams -
and one to the degree of Ph. D. - Miss Marie
A. Molineaux. This lady is the second one
only who has taken this degree, Miss Helen
Magil being the other. - Ex.

	The National Woman Suffrage Association
will hold its annual meeting this year at Oma-
ha, September 26, 27 and 28. On the 29th
and 30th the same speakers will hold a mass
convention in Lincoln. Boyd's Opera House
in Omaha, and Church's in Lincoln, have been
engaged for these evenings, so the suffragists
will have no rival attractions. Following
these meetings the speakers will divide into
three boards, holding two days' meetings at
these other important towns, and then from
these localities will separate for individual ef-
fort. - Ex.
