RESOLUTIONS,

	Of the Young Ladies' Mutual Improvement
Association of the 5th Ward, Logan, June 30th,
1882:
	Inasmuch as it has pleased Almighty God to
take to Himself our dearly beloved and esteemed
President, Sister Libbie Hyde,
	Be it therefore Resolved, That while we
humbly bow in submission to the divine will of
Almighty God, we realize that we have lost a
loving President, a motherly adviser, and a firm
and faithful defender of the principles of the
Gospel.
	Resolved, That while life with us remains
that we cherish her past diligence and efforts in
teaching us the principles of the Gospel and
promoting our interest.
	Resolved, That we tender our heartfelt sym-
pathies to her bereaved husband and parents in
their bereavement.
	Resolved, That a copy of these resolutions be
spread upon our minutes and one presented to
her bereaved husband.
		MARY PARRY. Secretary.

	Died, in Centerville, Davis County, Utah, at
the residence of her father-in law, Charles Dun-
can, May 22nd, 1882, Harriet Maria Lucas, be-
loved wife of John Duncan; born at Daventry,
Northhamptonshire, England, February 18th,
1858; baptized into the Church of Jesus Christ
of Latter-day Saints, June 29th, 1866; emigrated
to Utah, November 12th, 1871; married to John
Duncan, December 25, 1876. She was a member
of the Relief Society and of the Y. L. M. I.
Association, ever ready to discharge any duty
incumbant upon her. She was a kind and an
affectionate mother, and leaves a husband and
two children, with numerous friends, to mourn
her loss.
	She was beloved by old and young,
		To whom her name is dear:
	Her praise now hangs on every tongue,
		Her record it is clear.
	As stainless as the driven snow,
	The record that she left below.
