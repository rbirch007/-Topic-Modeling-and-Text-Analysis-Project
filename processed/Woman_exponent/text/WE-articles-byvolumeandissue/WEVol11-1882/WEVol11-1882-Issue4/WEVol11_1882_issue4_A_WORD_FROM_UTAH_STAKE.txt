A WORD FROM UTAH STAKE.

	The officers of the Y. L. M. I. A. and P. A.
of Utah Stake have just returned from a visit
to the southern settlements, which was made in
the interest of the organizations they represent.
	Springville Associations were found in a
flourishing condition. The presidency are do-
ing all in their power to make them successful.
The equal attendance of boys and girls was
quite an unusual feature, as there is generally
a greater number of girls. Active operations
for the coming Fair have already begun.
	The Associations of Spanish Fork are all do-
ing well, and the officers seem deeply inter-
ested in those under their charge. The Presi-
dent, Sister McLean, being absent attending to
her duties in the Deseret Hospital in Salt Lake
City, Primary Associations there were ably
managed by the two Counselors, although her
absence was a matter of regret.
	In Salem, the P. A. having suspended meet-
ing on account of the President moving away,
we reorganized the same, feeling sure those
chosen would carry on the work assigned them
in an able and satisfactory manner,
	There are four P. A. organizations in Pay-
son, the Presidents of which preside alternate-
ly at their public meetings.
	We found several new and interesting fea-
tures among the Payson Associations, namely:
They meet together jointly once a month. On
Fast days, verbal or written reports are sent
from each district, stating the condition of
their meetings; day schools are suspended to
enable the children to attend Fast meetings.
Under the able management of Bro. Stark, a
martial band, composed of Primary boys, has
been formed, who, at the close of the meeting,
played several tunes in a very creditable man-
ner, which delighted the children and was a
source of satisfaction to all.
	After the meetings in Payson, the President
proceeded to Benjamin, where a society was
organized, as no organization had previously
been made.
	In Santaquin the Primary and Y. L. M. I.
Associations meet together and were both in
prosperous condition. The programmes for
their general meetings attracted our special
attention.
	Arriving at Goshen, we were met by the
children in holiday attire, carrying a banner;
they surrounded our conveyance and sang a
sweet song; after which we repaired to the
schoolhouse, having a most interesting time.
The progress of the organizations has been
very marked.
	The Bishops and people generally showed a
great interest in the associations, and seemed
to be doing whatever they could in their be-
half.
	The Bishops sustained us in our labors and
remarks, and conveyed us from one settlement
to the other. All the meetings held were
largely attended by young and old; and the
Spirit of God attended us in our labors.
	A sociable was gotten up by the sisters in
Springville and Goshen, where the company
expressed their feelings, recited or sang for the
amusement of those present.
	We feel to thank God that the children are
having a chance to become more spiritual
minded through the meetings of these associa-
tions.			ZINA Y. WILLIAMS, Prest.
