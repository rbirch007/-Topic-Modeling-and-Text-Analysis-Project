ST. GEORGE PRIMARY.

	The P. A. of St. George Stake held a Fair
here on June 30th, and July 1st, in the base-
ment of the Tabernacle. Articles of all sorts
and kinds were exhibited,representing the labor
of children from four years of age to sixteen
years. One table was covered with fancy ar-
ticles, one table with that which is useful, one
with boy's articles, such as hay-racks, rakes, a
trunk or two, wagons, traps, doll's furniture,
&c., &c. Another table was devoted to vege-
tables, another to cookery, while the room was
lined with bed-quilts and rugs. A table was
given up to the display of silk, which was very
good. Much satisfaction was given, and all
seemed repaid for time and labor spent. Prizes
were given, necessarily very simple, by the Pres.
to encourage the children. Pres. J. H. Ivins,
who had the full responsibility on her shoul-
ders, deserves great credit for the manner in


which the Fair was conducted. In the even-
ing an entertainment by the several wards of
St. George was given in the Hall. It was a
success, and reflected great credit on those who
took part therein. It was given under the
charge of Mr. and Mrs. J.F. Gates.
	The financial report of the Fair and Enter-
tainment is as follows:	By cash, $38.60; by
trade, $35.35: total, 73.95. To expenses cash,
$2.50; trade, $5.00; total, 7.50. Profit, 65.45.
J. H. Ivins, Pres. S. Y. Gates, Sec.
