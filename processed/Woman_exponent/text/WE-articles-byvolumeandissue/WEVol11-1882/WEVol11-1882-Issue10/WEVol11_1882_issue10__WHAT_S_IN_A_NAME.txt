"WHAT'S IN A NAME?"

Fragrant as air from Eden,
	Mingled with wild-birds' lay
The name that you have given,
	Your new-born babe to-day,
It comes with mem'ries laden,
	Stirring deep founts of thought,
With magic light revealing,
	The changes time hath wrought.

As notes from some chance singer,
	Recalleth sweetest rhyme
Of dear old songs so tender,
	Sang oft in days 'Lang Syne,'
When life was free and gladsome,
	With only April tears,
When hope's bright rainbow colors,
	Shone over future years—

So that charmed name just spoken,
	Turns back time's tidal waves,
Rich treasure-troves are opened
	From out its hidden graves.
Like *rock of feeble olden,
	Concealing boundless store,
Enchanted but to open,
	To words of magic lore.

Without it vain the effort,
	An entrance to secure,
Name it! lo! quickly swingeth,
	Outward the pond'rous door.
Thanks to the power potent,
	That doth this charm impart,
Ne'er may it be withholden,
	From one poor human heart.
