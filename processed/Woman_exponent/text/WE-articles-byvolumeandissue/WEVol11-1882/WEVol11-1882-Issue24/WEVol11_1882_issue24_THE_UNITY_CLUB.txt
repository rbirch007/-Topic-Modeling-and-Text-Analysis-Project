THE UNITY CLUB. 

	The Deseret Hospital gladly acknowledge
another liberal contribution from the Unity
Club of $200, the proceeds of the entertainment
given in the Salt Lake Theatre, Monday even-
ing, April 5th, after all the expenses were set-
tled. The Unity Club have certainly given
positive proof of their financial ability, as well
as talent and tact in pleasing the public and
giving full value for money received. It is
usual in entertainments or fairs for the benefit
of charitable institutions to charge extra for ad-
mission, and at fairs to ask fabulous prices for
articles on sale.
	This has not been the case with the Unity
Club, the public in every instance have been
pleased, and have only paid the usual prices.
Bishop H. B. Clawson has worked faithfully to
assist the ladies in these matters. An associa-
tion which has only been six months organized
to be able to donate $700 made by their own
exertions, is something exceptional indeed.
	The Y. L., Y. M. M. I. and Primary Associa-
tions of Spanish Fork, combined forces and
talent and gave a concert recently, and made a
donation of part of the proceeds ($15.00) to the
Deseret Hospital. They were ably assisted in
the management of the affair by Brother Wil-
liam Jones of that place, a man noted for his
musical ability.
