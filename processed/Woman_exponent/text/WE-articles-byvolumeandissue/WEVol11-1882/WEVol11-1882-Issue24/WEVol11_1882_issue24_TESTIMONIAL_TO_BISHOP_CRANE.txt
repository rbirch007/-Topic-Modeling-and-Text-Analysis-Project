TESTIMONIAL TO BISHOP CRANE.

	"Honor to whom honor is due." The good
people of Herriman celebrated the 52nd birth-
day of their Bishop, James Crane, in a very
suitable manner, expressive of the high appre-
ciation in which he is held; the people of his
ward feel that he is indeed a father and a friend.
He has labored faithfully to lead the young peo-
ple of his ward in the ways of the Lord, that
they may attain to honor. An excellent ad-
dress was prepared for the occasion, by Sister
Jane Bowen and was read at the party. The
sisters of the ward contributed the means and
presented the Bishop with a nice gold watch
and chain, which he received with happiness
and gratitude. The party was held in the
schoolhouse and about two hundred and fifty
people sat down to a sumptuous dinner. Sister
Bowen also composed a poem that was read and
highly appreciated. There were several
speeches made, and much good feeling ex-
pressed, calculated to happify and encourage the
Bishop. The evening was occupied in dancing
and social enjoyment.

	Died, in this city, April 2nd, 1883, of consumption,
Caroline Iverson, beloved wife of Soren Iverson, born
October 4th 1836. A pure spirit has left us, one well
prepared to dwell in a holier sphere, where she now
shines among the jewels of that glorious court on high.
