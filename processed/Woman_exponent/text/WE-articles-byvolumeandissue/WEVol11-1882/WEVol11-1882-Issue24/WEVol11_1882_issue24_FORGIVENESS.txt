FORGIVENESS.

	There is no virtue in the human heart which
so adorns the lips and character of an individual
as forgiveness. For proof of this, look at the
example of the Savior, who, while suffering on


the cross at the hands of his enemies, cried out
in his anguish of soul, "Father, forgive them,
they know not what they do." Shall man; cre-
ated but little lower than the angels, follow the
example of Him in whom there was "no guile,"
or shall he debase himself by failing to do so?
	So apt are we to stray from the path of duty
that we find ourselves often called upon to for-
give the faults of those who do us an injury,
and unless we forgive hatred will reign triumph-
ant in our hearts. But if we forgive those who
offend us we obey the injunctions of Christ, and
contribute to the enjoyment of the offender,
and advance our own happiness and hopes of
forgiveness.
	If slander is hurled to crush our hope we
should remember "to err is human," and freely
forgive the offender. The man with a revenge-
ful spirit is in a perpetual storm, he is his own
tormentor, and his guilt, of course, is his pun-
ishment; whether he is at home or abroad, in
company or alone, it still engrosses his thoughts,
and as Providence has ordained that whosoever
meditates against the peace of another shall
even in the design lose his own.
	My advice would be to banish all spiteful and
revengeful thoughts, for a spirit of revenge is the
very spirit of the devil. Let him whose soul is
darkened with malice walk through a field clad
with verdure and flowers, and nothing will
appear beautiful to him, but on the other hand
to the person whose heart is full of forgiveness
every thing appears beautiful. Hoping we will
all cultivate a spirit of forgiveness and charity
towards one another, I remain, etc,
	M. A. WELCH.
South Morgan.
