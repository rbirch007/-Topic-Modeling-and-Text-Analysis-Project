LAIE, SANDWICH ISLANDS.
DEAR EDITOR:
I take the liberty of writing a few lines to
you at the present time for the purpose of giv-
ing you a brief account of the condition and
labors of the Relief Societies which are now
organized on the different islands.
There are five on this island (Oahu), four
on Hawaii, three on Kauai, and two on Maui,
making fourteen in all. Reports were read
from all at our Conference, with the exception
of two; the verbal reports given by the Presi-
dents who were present and also from the El-
ders who have been traveling and laboring
among them for the past six months, are very
encouraging indeed.	
I send you the minutes of our Conference,
tsken by the secretary, a native sister, and
translated into English by President Partridge;
the report may not be correct in every respect,
as the secretaries are all inexperienced in
things of this kind, but they are willing to be
taught, and anxious to be instructed in all
their duties, that they may understand what is
required of them; they are energetic in their
labors, and very liberal in donating; they have
a very pleasant way of assisting each other;
when they wish to perform some work and
have not the means to do so, they invite the
other Societies to assist them.
The Society at Laie is intending to build a
house for the accommodation of the poor who
have not comfortable homes, and have called
upon the other societies to assist in raising the
necessary means, and have now on hand the
sum of $149, and promise of more as soon as
there is an opportunity to send it. Great
efforts are being made by the Elders to per-
suade the people to forsake their evil ways.
