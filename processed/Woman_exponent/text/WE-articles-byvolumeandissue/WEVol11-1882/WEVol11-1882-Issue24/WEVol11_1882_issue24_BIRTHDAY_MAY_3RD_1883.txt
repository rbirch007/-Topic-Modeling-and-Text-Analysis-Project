BIRTHDAY, MAY 3RD, 1883.

	Blessings be upon thee my sister, on this
the return of thy natal day; may blessings of
every kind be multiplied upon thee from this
day henceforth. Blessings be upon thee for
thy labors of love, adminstered to thy sisters in
holy places! Blessings be upon thee for thy
faithfulness in all the walks of life; like every
true Saint of God thou hast had sore trials,
but hast borne them meekly, and with calm en-
durance; thy spirit has bent as the willow to the
stream, and the spirit of our God has brooded
over thee, and He has sent His angels to watch
over thee, and to comfort and sustain thee;
thou hast realized their presence by the intui-
tion of thine own spirit, and the whisperings of
the Eternal! Thou hast been blest with the
friendship of the servants of God, and of thy
sisters, handmaids of the same. And to-day
we see thee standing like one of the handmaids


of old—erect, healthy, and with a countenance
bright and smiling with the reflex of the spirit
of thy Father—God! Long dear Sister may
you so stand, to bless and instruct your sisters
by your sweet words and, more—by your exam-
ple. 
	I need not say God bless you, for you bear
the impress of  His blessing on your person
and your habitation. Till you pass behind  the 
veil may you retain the same, is the prayer of 
your Sister,
	HANNAH T. KING.
	May 3rd, 1883, 17th Ward.
