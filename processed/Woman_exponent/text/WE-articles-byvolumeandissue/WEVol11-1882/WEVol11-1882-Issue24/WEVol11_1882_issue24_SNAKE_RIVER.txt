SNAKE RIVER. 

	CEDAR, BUTTE, March 30th, 1883. 
DEAR SISTER WELLS:
	After reading the WOMAN'S EXPONENT I 
feel impressed to write a few lines to put in it.
I think it a very excellent paper, one which
every mother should have in her house, for her
daughters and herself to read. It is a great
help to me, as I am President of the Y. L. M.
I. A., and think it ought to be patronized by
our sisters, as it has been printed for their 
benefit.
	On the 21st of December we were organ-
ized by President J. R. Pool, with the follow-
ing officers: Serreta Pool, President; Mary and
Annie Stephens, Counselors, Susie Pool Secre-
tary; Emily Pool, Assistant Secretary; Chris-
tena Pool, Treasurer. We have about twenty-
five members enrolled; though our numbers are
small we are striving to prepare ourselves to 
become useful members in the kingdom of
God. We realize that we have a great work
to perform, and unless we are diligent we can-
not expect to gain the reward, which our
Father has promised to his faithful children.
Our meetings are very interesting, and we are
all doing our part to help them along, mean-
while remembering, practice makes perfect. I
think it is a good thing we can have these
meetings, they are a great benefit to the young;
and should we not be thankful that we have
such Sisters as Eliza R. Snow Smith, Zina D. 
H. Young, and other sisters who travel from
place to place organizing the sisters? I think we
ought. I hope they may live a good many
years yet, and have health and strength, and I 
hope we may sometimes have them visit us up
here in our far away homes.
	Ever praying for the welfare of Zion, and the
success of the EXPONENT, I remain your sister
in the Gospel, SERRETA POOL,
	President.
