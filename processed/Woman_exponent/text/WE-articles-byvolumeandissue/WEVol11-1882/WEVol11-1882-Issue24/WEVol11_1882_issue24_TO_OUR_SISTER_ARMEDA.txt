TO OUR SISTER ARMEDA.

The following expression of affectionate regard was
presented to Mrs. Armeda Snow Young on her departure
on a mission to the Sandwich Islands with her husband. 

	We, the officers and members of the Y. L. M.
I. Association of the Third Ward of Brigham 
City, feel to tender our thanks and appreciation
of your interest in our behalf, and we do ac-
knowledge that we have enjoyed your efforts in
doing good in our midst, therefore we ask you
to accept this ring as a small token of "true
friendship."
	We present it to you because we LOVE you,
and wish you to remember, when you are in a
far distant land, that, no matter what comes,
there are still "true friends at home."
	May you go out into the world as a messenger
of "peace," that wherever you go that heavenly
influence will secure for you a band of true
friends We ask our Father to bless Sister Ar-
meda and guide her barge through the voyage
of this life; may your heart always be full of
"Faith, Hope and Charity," that you ever shall
keep in sight the bright "Beacon light of
Truth."
	We bid you "Farewell" for a short season; and
when you return, we pray, may not one link be
broken in the chain of friendship, but more so,
do we sincerely hope, that we may ALL prove
worthy to meet in the "grand reunion" in
Father's Home, where we shall meet those
"Loved ones who have gone before."
