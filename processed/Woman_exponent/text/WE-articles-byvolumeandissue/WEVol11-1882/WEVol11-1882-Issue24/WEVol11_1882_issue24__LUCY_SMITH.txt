LUCY SMITH.

The Prophets mother! On her breast
	The sacred infant slept,
From infancy to youth, her love
	Its faithful watchcare kept;
And from his lips—a gentle child's—
	Whose easliest words she taught,
Her ear and heart with joy received
	The wondrous truths he brought.

Unfaltering, when men amazed
	Their chosen master proved,
Through persecutions, aye, till death—
	Her love, her faith, unmoved.
Whose rapture like the mothers own
	That through all suff'ring smiled!
Whose woe like hers? who would have died
	For him, her Prophet child!

Prophet and Patriarch, her sons.
	Before the mother's eyes
Did visions of the gauntlet path
	And martyrdom, arise?
Or, answering prophetic fears,
	Did heaven in mercy bend
With strength and balm, and promise sure
	To lead her to the end?

Of women, it was she who first
	Received baptismal birth,
When from the throne of God, his work
	Was brought again to earth—
The life that here was one with them,
	Linked still with theirs will shine
With ransomed souls eternally,
	In courts and spheres divine.

Within the heart of ev'ry Saint
	Forever be endeared
The tender face, the noble life,
	The mother heaven revered.
Long hath she slept in silence sweet,
	And now her rest is won,
Awake her name, that younger Saints
	Forget not this dear one.
