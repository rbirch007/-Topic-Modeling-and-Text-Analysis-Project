LOVE'S BIRTH.

It had lain for years, like a frozen thing,
	In my soul's remotest deep. 
I drifted through life with a careless heart,
	Nor dreamed of the force alseep.

*		*		*		*		*		*

The storm was flown, and o'er the scene
	The moon's soft impulse glowed,
Casting o'er beach and shadowed hill
	It's silvered wreath untold.

The grassy path was smooth, was fair,
	That led us to the strands—
With restless arms the sea leaped up
	And smote the quivering sands.

A gentle touch—my wrap was laid
	Around me soft and warm;
And standing thus, your arm was staid
	Around my quiet form.

Oh! something stirs within my heart—
	Full, palpitating life.
Swift quickening throbs my every pulse
	With expectation rife.

You raise my lips to yours—my soul
	With passion throes is torn—
But with the lingering caress
	Behold! my love is born.
