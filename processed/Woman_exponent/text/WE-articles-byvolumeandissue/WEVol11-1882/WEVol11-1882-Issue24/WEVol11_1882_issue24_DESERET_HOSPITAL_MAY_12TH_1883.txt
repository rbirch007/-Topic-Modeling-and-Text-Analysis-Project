DESERET HOSPITAL, May 12th, 1883.
DEAR EXPONENT:
	I wish to express my sincere and heartfelt
gratitude to my numerous friends for their
kind attention and sympathy manifested
towards me during my sojourn in the Deseret
Hospital, where I have been made very com-
fortable.
	I feel, in my heart, to acknowledge the hand
of the Lord in the affliction which has over-
taken me, and am assured it will result in a
blessing, for I have learned by a long expe-
rience under the "New and Everlasting Cove-
nant" that all things are overruled for good to
those who love God. I know it is good to
serve the Lord, and I desire to devote my life
to His service. Nor do I forget my dear rela-
tives and friends in St. George, where the acci-


dent took place, after which I remained there
four weeks at the residence of my brother in-
law, Mr. R. Bently. Friends were on hand to
render what aid and comfort they could. The
hope was entertained that it was only a sprain
and would take time to regain strength. By
a surgical examination it proves to be a bro-
ken bone which will require an apparatus (sent
for to New York) to wear to strengthen the
limb and hold it in position to enable me to walk.
	I expect to remain at the Hospital until the
article named shall arrive, which will be, prob-
ably, two weeks longer. In the hope of again
being permitted to be among my friends, the
Saints of God.
			Respectfully,
