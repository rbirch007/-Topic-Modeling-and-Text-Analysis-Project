A SONG.

Free as the birds of the mountain,
Free as the rippling rill;
Free as the crystal fountain,
So free is the human will
Mighty and strong, for right or wrong,
To choose thagood or ill,
O, God above, send down thy love
To guide the human will. 

Dcar in the eight of heaven—
Of this vain world a part—
Great is the work that’s given,
To each poor human heart.
Nobly and true, its work to do,
Though good or ill betide,
O, God above, send down thy love,
The human heart to guide.

God's mercy, ever careth
For the immortal soul,
All its needs He knoweth,
It should his name extol.
Let songs arise to rend the skies
Let soul and heart and will
To God above give endless love,
Our mission here to fill.
E. S. D.
