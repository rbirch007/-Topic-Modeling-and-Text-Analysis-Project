EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The Primary Association of Ephraim City,
held a fair in the Relief Society Hall on the
2d and 3d of November, 1882. representing
the taste and talent of the little ones from
four years of age to fourteen years. The hall
was lined with bed quilts, rugs, embroidered
and crochet tablecovers, framed pictures of
hair flowers, also of flowers of Berlin wool on
velvet, and a variety of handsome wall pockets.
One table was covered with such things as
breakfast shawls, hoods, stockings, baby shoes,
pillow lace, edging, embroidery, mats, cushions,
shell box, etc. Another table, some pastry and
good bread. On the boys' side was one sailing
vessel, the word WELCOME carved on wood,
rakes, rollers, wagon and harrows; also some
fine beets and other vegetables. No prizes
were given, but we hope the favor of this re-
port in the EXPONENT will help to encourage
the little ones. In the evening, a meeting was
held in the meetinghouse; after the children
had filled the programme, Prest. H. Madsen,
of Gunnison, and her Counselor, addressed the
meetimg, also Sister Greer, President of Manti
P. A. The leading authorities were all pres-
ent. Much satisfaction was given, and much
praise is due to the officers of each ward for
their earnest perseverance, and also to the
children.
				EMMA J. WILLIAMS,
				AUGUSTA STEVENS,
							Presidents,
				C. THORPE, Secretary.
