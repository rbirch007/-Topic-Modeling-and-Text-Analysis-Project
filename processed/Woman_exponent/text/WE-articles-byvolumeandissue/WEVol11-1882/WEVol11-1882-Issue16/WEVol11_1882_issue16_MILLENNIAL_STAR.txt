MILLENNIAL STAR.

	THE Christmas number of "The Latter-day
Saints Millennial Star" closed the forty-fourth
volume of that valuable journal. It is the old-
est paper except one published in Liverpool.
This paper is edited and published at the present
time by Apostle John Henry Smith, now Presi-
dent of the European mission, though the
practical work of preparing it for the press, is in
the hands of Elder O. F. Whitney, and his
poetical ability is apparent even in his editorials.
He closes a most interesting editorial on Christ-
mas in this poetic strain:
	"Let smiles wrethe every face with gladness.
Let happiness, like a well, rise up from every
honest heart, and rippling laughter, like a sunny
stream, flow merrily forth from the lip that
speaks no evil. Let pleasure wait on purity,
and festivity go hand in hand with temperance.
Let faith, hope and charity—the white-winged
graces of the Christian gospel—encircle each
festive board, and hover over every family
hearth. Let kindness and brotherly love be
mutual mediator and monarch over all. Let
poverty praise generous wealth, and generous
wealth remember poverty. Let the song of the
jubilant be tuned by chastity and the laughter
of innocence be tempered by the inward prayer


of thanksgiving. And while Joy and gladness
abound, and thanksgiving and melody resound
from every heart and home, let every tongue
tacitly utter and every soul silently respond,"
"Glory to God in the highest, and on earth,
peace, good will toward men!"
	The value of this paper to the Saints In
Europe, cannot be estimated, and as a record of
the British mission, it has been very essential
indeed. May it continue to increase in popu-
larity and prosperity, until all the honest in
heart from all nations are gathered under the
Gospel banner, and there will be no more need
of the Elders In foreign fields of labor.
