HELEN AND VIRGINIA. 

My friend, when once you asked of me
My reason for the truth I bear,
I answered briefly, for my gift
Is not in language as with men
Accustomed through their lives to share
Conflicts in pulpit or by pen. 

And so unsatisfied, I turn
To answer further one who seems
Inclined impartially to hear
The weaker side, and to receive
Such truth therein she may discern
As by our Church taught and believed.

It has been argued, theories,
However plausible to view,
Fail when brought into daily life,
And our's requirements and restraints
Are, while its precepts we pursue,
Beyond the strength of even Saints.

And that 'tis better far to keep
The "golden rule" on every hand,
Leaving the rest to our dear Lord,
Avoiding thus all cause of strife,
Attaining so through all our land
An onward march of peaceful life.

For us who know we have been called
To rise and work, not sit and wait,
Such well meant counsel does not fit
Our cause and time on this world's stage;
The field is wide, the hour is late,
And none must loiter who engage.

For in the kingdom there's a place,
A name and work for every one,
According to the talent lent
By the great Master over all,
Who notes the humblest service done
In answer to His holy call.

So if I answer you by way
Of narrative culled from the lives
Of those whom I have known for years,
Accept the picture, you will see
Of "Mormon" maidens, mothers, wives,
In proof of what we claim to be.

Not seed of promise, bud or bloom,
Taken with hopes and anxious fears,
Lest it prove thorned and barren tree,
And life's long labor spent in vain;
This is the fruit, after long years,
The fruit we've wrought in faith to gain.

Not that we lacked the "corner stone,"
Whereby to build, or rule to guide;
Each spirit knew by inward sight
The system's truth, its course and goal;
And gathered close or scattered wide.
Labored as one harmonious whole.

If in this garden you should find
Fruit all unworthy of its name,
Remember, e'en in Eden grew
A thing of evil, bringing sin,
Where all was pure; nor choose to blame
All, for the few tares found therein.

This is our work: to root from earth
Evils that have for ages spread;
And not by words alone, by works
That shall be felt the whole world through,
Till vanquished sin with bending head
Yields unto Truth the honor due.

And who will do this mighty work?
Not men of war in war's array,
But gentler ones who armor bear
Against all selfishness of heart;
Of these I weave my rhyme to-day—
Women who bear an honored part.
