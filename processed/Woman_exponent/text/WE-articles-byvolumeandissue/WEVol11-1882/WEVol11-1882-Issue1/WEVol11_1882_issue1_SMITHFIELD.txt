SMITHFIELD.

	The yearly meeting of the Primary Associ-
ation of Smithfield, Cache Co. was held on
April 25th, 1882. A number of the Relief
Society sisters were present. During the meet-
ing a great many encouraging and instructive
remarks were given. Sister Adeline Barber,
in behalf of the mothers, presented Pres. Mar-
tha Williams with a handsome hat and cape;
also read a memorial of respect, composed by
the mothers for the Association, as a token of
respect and love toward her for her labors, as
she had been faithful in teaching their children
the ways of truth and righteousness. Prest. M.
Williams, with a few appropriate words,
thanked all for the kindness they had shown
her.
	On April 15th Sister Molen, Bishop Farrell
and Sister Barber being present, the Associ-
ation was reorganized, with the following
officers: Martha Williams, President; Lillian-
Jones and Emma Farrell, Counselors; Emma
Nelson, Secretary; Ghena Folsom, Assistant
Sec.; Hannah Christensen, Treasurer; Ida F.
Harper, her assistant.
	Sisters Williams, Molen and Barber, also Bp.
Farrell spoke encouragingly to the children.
	The Primary Association of Smithfield is
doing well, and great improvements have been
made since its first organization. The children
have held three concerts during this year; also
paid $12.00 to the emigration fund and have
wheat and beans stored away for a famine.
The Association proves to be a blessing to this
ward. A book of Questions and Answers, by
Sister Snow Smith, proves to be a great help
to the President.
					EMMA NELSON.
							Secretary.
