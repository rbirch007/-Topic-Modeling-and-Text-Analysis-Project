MOAN COPY, ARIZONA.

	The Relief Society of this place was organ-
ized May 11th, 1882, under the direction of
President Wilford Woodruff, with sixteen
members, of whom five only remained at the
end of a few months. Consequently we have
accomplished but little. We hope to do better
in future, as other settlers have now moved in;
we were reorganized April 6th, of the present
year, by Bishop A. L. Farnsworth and Coun-
selors; with the following officers: Harriet M.
Gardner, President; Elisabeth D. Gardner,
Caroline E. Walker, Counselors; Mary A.
Farnsworth, Secy.; Annie E. Farnsworth,
Treasurer. We have twenty-one members,
twelve of whom live near enough together to
attend meetings regularly. We intend meeting
once a month at present; working meetings as
often as necessary. We are few in number,
without either the affluent or the absolutely
needy in our midst. Our situation is isolated
in the extreme; the country, for many miles on
either side of us, is an irreclaimable desert,
with this small oasis in the midst, where the
people live by the blessings of the Lord, and
their own untiring industry. We are striving
to do all the good we can among the Lamanites
and fill our mission acceptably before the Lord.
We raise plenty of good corn here, but no
small grain as yet. The climate is rather too
warm for vegetables to do well. But the
people, in general, enjoy excellent health, for
which we are thankful. We have no regular
mail here, and the nearest post office is seventy-
five miles distant, at Lee's Ferry on the Big
Colorado. We have waited sometime for a
favorable change in our mail affairs, and are
tired of living without our invaluable Expon-
ent. We will now subscribe for two copies
for our Society. Hoping ere long to chronicle
something more creditable to ourselves as a
Relief Society, we remain your sisters in the
Gospel of peace.
				HARRIET M. GARDNER,
