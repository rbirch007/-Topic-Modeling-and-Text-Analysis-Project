ADVICE TO MRS. SCOTT.

	[Answer to letter in No. 16 of the Woman's
Exponent headed, "Some Facts."]

	To be good is to be happy. Is there any
thing in Mormonism that is not good ? Positive
good, comparative better, superlative best. I
think the best thing for you, is to embrace the
first principles of the Gospel, by being baptized,
by one having authority, and then you will
know for yourself the gifts and blessings do


follow all believers. It is very natural fo
mortals to feel after happiness. When I readr
the Bible and all good books, I find the Lord
in a very peculiar manner bestow great bless-
ings, upon men and women who lived in plural
marriage; we will notice Sarah, who told Abra-
ham to enter into that order, and after she was
very old the Lord told her that in her seed all
the nations of the earth should be blessed, then
we find her grandson with four wives, and they
had twelve sons; John the Revelator, sees the
city the New Jerusalem with twelve gates, and
their names were written thereon, and if you
read in Volume 7 of WOMAN'S EXPONENT, you
will find strong testimony of the happiness of
women living in plurality, and if you were well
acquainted with that paper you would find that
Mormon women went to President Hayes, in
the name of all the Mormon women of Utah,
that they might not be disturbed in their mar-
riage relations.
	My heart is full of joy and gratitude to my
Heavenly Father for the privilege of spending
the last twelve years of my life in that holy
order of marriage.		JANE COWAN.
