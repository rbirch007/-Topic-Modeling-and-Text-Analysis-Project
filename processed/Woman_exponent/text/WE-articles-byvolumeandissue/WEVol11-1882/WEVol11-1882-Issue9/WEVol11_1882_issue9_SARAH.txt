SARAH.

I'm glad I know thee, gentle, faithful one,
	And that I felt thy spirit blend with mine,
As kindred rivulets together run,
	Or sister branches 'round each other twine.

Thy memory shall remain with me, dear girl,
	Forever cherished, sacred and secure,
Like the soft lustre of a royal pearl,
	For thou wert good and innocent and pure.

And thou art gone unto the dearest place
	I know, or think, or hear of, anywhere;
Closing my eyes I often seem to trace
	The outlines of our many loved ones there.

Eliza! Oh how joyfully you met
	That bright young sister, eager first to greet!
My baby girls! say have you seen them yet?
	My precious lambs, so loving and so sweet!

I almost hear you tenderly respond,
	And listen breathlessly, but ah, 'tis gone!
I'm here, you're in the "beautiful beyond,"
	The veil is thin, but yet, not quite withdrawn.

										LULA.
