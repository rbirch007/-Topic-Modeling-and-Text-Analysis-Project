WEST JORDAN PRIMARY.

	My Dear Little Brothers and Sisters. As I
am appointed to write an essay I will try and
do the best I can, but I don’t think I can write
any thing that will be interesting to any of
you. I like to come to meeting and come in
time. I am willing to do any thing I am
called upon to do. We should be kind to those
placed over us, and listen to good counsel. We
are taught when we come to meeting, we should
be obedient to our parents and respectful to all
old people. We should pray night and morn-
iny, and in the day-time as well, for when any
of our little brothers or sisters are sick, we
should pray in our hearts, and have faith that
they will get well, and they will get well; inas-
much as we ask sincerely. I think if we would
attend meeting regularly we would feel a great
deal better than if we do not. My little broth-
ers and sisters, it we could only realize the
blessings that we are surrounded with. Kind
parents and teachers to instruct us. Let us
come to meeting and attend regularly and come
in time. Let us show by our actions that we
will do better in the future than we have done
in the past. Please excuse all mistakes, for
this is my first attempt; I will now close this
essay, wishing to do better the next time.
	Your Sister in the Primary Association.
		REBECCA GOFF.
July 8, 1882.
