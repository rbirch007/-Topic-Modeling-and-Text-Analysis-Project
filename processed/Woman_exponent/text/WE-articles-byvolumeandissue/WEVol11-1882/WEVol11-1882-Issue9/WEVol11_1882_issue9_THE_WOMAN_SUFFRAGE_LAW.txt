THE WOMAN SUFFRAGE LAW.

	In this number of the EXPONENT it will be seen
that the law of this Territory, giving to women
the right of suffrage has been fully sustained
from the charges made against it by political trick
sters, and discord creators, who have an exist-
ence and are kept before the public only by their
continual harangues and false and vehement
assertions. Both Judges Hunter and Emerson,
sustained the law in every particular, and in the
strongest language at their command, main-
tained the right of the Territorial Legislature to
pass the act which it was attempted to over-
throw, in the interest of a meager and unscru-
pulous minority. Judge Twiss also asserted the
validity of the law, but held that women must
be taxpayers before they could vote. In view,
however, of the rulings of the two Judges first
mentioned, it is most probable that this portion


of Judge Twiss' decision will be ruled against by
the Supreme Court, and that the right of wo-
men to vote under the existing law will remain
undisturbed. The result is no more than was
anticipated, but it is gratifying to know that
we have men on the bench who are not afraid to
breast the waves of wild and unreasoning
clamors of a party frantic for power to which
they are in no way entitled.
