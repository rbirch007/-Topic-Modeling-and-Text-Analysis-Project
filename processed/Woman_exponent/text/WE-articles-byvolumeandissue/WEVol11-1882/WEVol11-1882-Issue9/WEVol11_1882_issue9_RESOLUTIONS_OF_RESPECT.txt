RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.

	Resolutions of respect on the death of William
Knight, member of the 20th Ward Primary As-
sociation, who departed this life Sept. 1st, 1882,
with heart disease.
	As God in his wisdom has seen fit to call an-
other of his precious jewels, who has labored
faithfully in the performance of every duty, we,
as officers and members of the Primary Associa


tion, unanimously adopt the following resolu-
tions:
	We sincerely condole with his parents, brothers
and sisters, in their heavy bereavement, and we
tender them our heartfelt sympathy.
	May his many virtues be emulated by the
members of our Association, so that his noble ex-
ample may stimulate others to a life of virtue
and usefulness.
	Resolved, that we adopt these resolutions, as
a token of respect, that we present a copy of
these resolutions to his parents, and publish the
same in the WOMAN'S EXPONENT, and place a
copy on our record.

The angels bore him robed in spotless white,
To their peaceful home of light.
