RECIPES.

	GREEN TOMATOES PICKLED: 4 quarts of tom-
atoes sliced, 4 oz. salt, 1 tablespoonful of allspice,
3 of cloves, 2 of pepper, 4 of black mustard seed-
3 onions sliced. Put about half the salt into a
kettle with some vinegar. Put the sliced toma-
toes and onions into this and let them boil a few
minutes, skim them out, and let them drain.
Then put them into a jar with remainder of salt
and spices, and cover them with scalding vine-
gar; horseradish root improves them, making
them crisp.
	GREEN TOMATOES PICKLED SWEET: 2 gal. of
tomatoes sliced, 2 qts vinegar, same of sugar, 2
table spoonfuls of salt, 2 of pepper, 1 of allspice, 1
of cloves. Method: Put enough water in a
kettle with a teacup of vinegar, to cover the
tomatoes. Let them scald, then put in a jar,
then take spices and vinegar and let them come
to a boil, and pour over tomatoes. Horseradish
root placed on top of a jar of pickles makes them
crisp. All tried and good recipes.
	SWEET PICKLE PEACHES: 8 lbs of fruit, 4 lbs.
of sugar, 1 pt. of cider vinegar, 1 oz. each of
cloves, allspice, cinnamon. Method, make syrup
with vinegar, sugar and spices, except cloves
which you stick in the peaches, two to each
peach. Put in the fruit and boil till easily pricked
with a straw, take the fruit out, and boil the
syrup 20 minutes longer, then pour it over the
fruit, boil up the syrup three mornings, pour over
fruit and then seal up.
	APPLE JAM: 10 lbs. sugar, 10 lbs. good sour
apples; chopped fine. The grated peel of three
lemons, and a few pieces of white ginger root.
Boil till apples look clear and tender.
