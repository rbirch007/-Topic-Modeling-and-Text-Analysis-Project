BY HANNA T. KING.

I feel I must answer your beautiful lines
They come like the sunshine in winterly times
They come with a warmth, and a genuine ring,
That is sound—and such ever sweet music doth bring.
You only speak truth, I am all you portray,
And to you I spoke truth in the very same way;
I know you've a gift that no teacher has given
I know 'tis a gift from the Chancery of Heaven—
Oh! prize it! to you it will prove such a boon—
You'd not change for a gold mine—no not very soon;
Nor the wealth of a Rothschild, the Jew of the Kings!
By which you could purchase terrestrial things;
In extasy, No the fond poet exclaims,
No ermine—no di’monds, or jewel-linked chains
Could buy from my brain, nor indeed from my heart,
The jewel I hold of my soul a rich part—
My Lyre! my Lyre, gives oceans of wealth
I lave in its waters, abounding in health!
I drink of its streams fresh from Helicon's fount—
And into the regions of poesy mount
Parnassus I climb—from its glorious hight,
Earth recedes from my vision in mystical flight.
I'm away from the world, and its "Vanity Fair,"
Its tinsel, its emptiness—even its glare!
All these throw no charm o'er my heart or my brain,
On Pegasus mounted, with full scope of rein.
I pass into regions of gorgeous delight,
Where day is eternal, with sunshine all bright;
Is this not a boon no mortal can give?
Is this not a gift for the life we now live?
Yes, yes! 'tis a prism that gilds all we see,
A rainbow that arches the Heavens for me.

And will you not listen? and will you not say,
Friend give me thy hand—I am one in thy lay?
Yes poets are counterpart, twin in their soul,
And they passively bow to the mystic control;
And to Heaven they offer the prayer of the heart—
From Thy Lyre, Thy gift, may we two never part.
	Salt Lake City.
