HOME AFFAIRS.

	THE 13th Ward Y. M. and Y. L. M. I. A.
were re organized Tuesday evening, Oct. 17, by
Bishop Attwood and his Counselors.

	IN company with Mrs. M. I. Horne, we at—
tended the Conference of the Relief Society and
P. A. of Davis Stake, held at East Bountiful Fri-
day, Oct. 20,Will give some details in our next
issue.


Sisters E. R. S. Smith and Z. D. H. Young
left the city Thursday, Oct. 19, and visited
through Juab Stake; attended Conferences in
Nephi, and held meeting attended in their tour
through the Stake by Prest. Teasdale, Elder L.
A. Bailey and Sister Amelia Goldsbrough.
   We had the pleasure of attending the Confer-
ence of the Primary Association of the 20th
Ward Thursday, Oct 19th, but have not space in
this issue to give items in detail. The 12th
Ward Primary had a Fair on he same day,
which was, we are informal, quite a success in
a small way..
 Ladies, vote for the delegate of the People's
Party, the Hon. John T. Caine, who will repre-
sent all the people of the Territory. Those who
have been permitted toregister will certainly
not forget to go to the polls and cast a ballot on
the first Tuesday in November. No one should
excuse herself from this important  duty on
election day. The vote will undoubtedly be the
heaviest one ever cast in Utah, and women will
be largely represented in it.
  Mrs. S. M. Kimball made quite a political
speech in the 14th Ward Assembly Room Sat-
urday, Oct. 21 She gave her opinion of the
duty of the women of Utah in regard to the
"Declaration of Principles" of the People’s
Party; felt something was due from them as a
response to the sentiments therein expressed
towards woman. Thought women thus privi-
ledged had a responsibility resting upon them
which should be taken into consideration.
