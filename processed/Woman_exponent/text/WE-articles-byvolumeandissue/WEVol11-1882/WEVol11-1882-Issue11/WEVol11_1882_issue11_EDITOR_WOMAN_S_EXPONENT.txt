EDITOR WOMAN'S EXPONENT:
	We have now closed our subscription to assist
in emigration, aud thought we would mention
what we had accomplished. Our Primary Asso-
ciation donated $31,25. The Young Ladies, with
the assistance of the Young Man's Association,
donated $30.75; making a total of $62. Much
can be accomplished by perseverance, and it is
right for the young to take an active part to
carry on the work of God. Some little boys
twelve years old worked and earned their dona-
tion, and were proud they could assist to gather
the poor Saints. The Primaries are a great help
to the rising generation; through their agency
the principles of our holy religion will be im-
pressed upon the minds of the children, never
to be erased. Our Associations are in good con-
dition. The Primary Association held a Fair
during the summer, which was very creditable;
also a concert. President Mary A. P. Schow
takes great pains to train their voices to sing
their Primary Hymns.
	I often think it is good to have a medium like
the EXPONENT to exchange ideas and to stimu-
late to good works.		JANE S. COLEMAN.
	September 30, 1882.
