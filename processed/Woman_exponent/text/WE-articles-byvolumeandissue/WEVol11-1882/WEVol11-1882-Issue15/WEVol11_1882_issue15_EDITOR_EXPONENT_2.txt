EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We held our Fair on the 24th of September,
and although it was pretty good for such a
small settlement, still it did not quite come up
to our expectations. In our display might be
named, a box of charcoal, burned in the moun-
tains by a very little boy, some very nice
brackets, cornucopias, tidies, a very nice wind
mill by one of the little boys, a very nice
watch pocket done in wool, wool flowers, draw-
ings, lots of things too numerous to mention.
We had a visit from some of the sisters of
Payson and Santaquin, who gave us good in-
struction.
	We also held our annual meeting on the
21st of October, when the little folks were
treated to refreshments. They had some good
instructions from the sisters, after which they
joined in the dance for a short time, when all
went home in the best of humor.
	Wishing the EXPONENT every success, we
remain your sisters in the Gospel,
			SARAH ROBERTS, President.
			CHRISTINA HIGGINSON, Secretary.
	Goshen, Nov. 14, 1882.
