DEAR EDITOR:
	As we always desire to aid, in our humble way
the work of the Lord, we feel to mingle our
voices with the valuable efforts of your corres-
pondents, who so ably strive for the truth.
Every number of the EXPONENT seems to out-
vie the preceding one in interest and worth;
truly our sisters are becoming noble defenders of
righteousness and virtue; and we can say, "God
moves in a mysterious way, His wonders to per-
form." Truth is mighty and it will prevail, and


woman is nobly performing her part in the great
arena.
	On June 24th I sent a communication on our
annual meeting of the Relief Society of June 4;
as it has not appeared in your columns I presume
it has been mislaid; we may be deemed negli-
gent, I thought a few lines would not be amiss.
Our meeting was held as usual, the Bishop and
his Counselors being present; had an excellent
time, and good instructions were given us. Our
reports were good; the Relief Society house not
being completed, they were not as concise as we
should have liked. Considering all things, we
are doing well; there not many poor among us,
and the sisters are ever ready and willing to aid
and assist, with few exceptions.
	Yesterday was election day; we had a pleasant
time, the weather being fine and the brass band
making times lively, while peace and cheerful-
ness reigned within our homes. We felt that
our Father in Heaven was at the helm, guiding
the good ship Zion to her noble destiny.
					A. J. MERRIL, Pres't.
