RESOLUTIONS.

	Be it resolved, That we, as members of the Relief So-
ciety, deeply monrn the loss of our beloved sister and
co-laborer, and we sincerely sympathise with her hus-
band, children and relatives in their loss, intermingled
with the knowledge that it is her true gain, and rejoice
to know that if we follow in her footsteps, and are
faithful, we shall meet again where sorrow cannot
come.

She's gone, and our hearts feel the smart,
	Our friend from our bosom is torn;
The sweet ties that bound us are broke,
	And nature compels us to mourn.
But oh, transporting delight!
	To think of our meeting again,
When, clothed in our robes clean and white,
	We'll meet thee, Rosina, again.
						FRANCIS TRAVELLER,
						MARY ALLSOP,
						BETSY BROWER,
									Committee.
