IN MEMORIAM.
Salt Lake City, Feb. 14, 1883.
Resolutions of respect to the memory of Sarah Haigh,
who departed this life Jan. 29, 1883, aged 56 years.
Whereas an all-wise providence has called from
us by the hand of death, our beloved und respected
sister who was present with us at our last meeting;
therefore
 Resolved That we recognize in the death of our
sister Sarah Haigh the loss of an able, efficient and
worthy teacher and co-laborer in the 4th Ward Re-
lief Society.
 Resolved, That we esteem her past faithful labors
and devotion to duty us worthy the fellowship and
highest respect of he all.
Reaolved, That we condolo and deeply sympathize


with the bereaved family and friends in the loss of a
kind wife, affectionate mother, worthy teacher, and a
good and respected citizen.
Resolved, That these resolutions be Epread upon our
records, and that ft copy be furnished the bereaved
family, also the Woman’s Exponent for publication!
Yours respectfully,
Susan Sperry.
M. E. Taylor, Secretary.
“Dearest Sister, thou hast left us,”
Gone to meet the friends of yore;
Where your kindred and your loved ones
Have preceded you before.
Weep not for your wife, dear Brother,
You will meet her if you’re true
To the cause of truth and virtue,
And your onward course pursue.
And her children all will miss her,
That dear face they loved so well,
And her kindness they’ll remember.
While upon the earth they dwell.
Cecelia Kirk.
