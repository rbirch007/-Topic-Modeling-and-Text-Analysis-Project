PRIMARY PARTY AND FAIR.

	On the evening of the 15th of Dec., 1882,
the Primary Association of the 2nd Ward of
Richfield gave their President a delightful
surprise. It was planned entirely by the little
folks, to whom great credit is due for the man-
ner in which it was conducted.
	At the hour appointed every-thing was in
readiness, and a conveyance was sent for the
President and her Counselors, who were great-
ly surprised and pleased.
	After dancing awhile, a bounteous repast
was partaken of, and heartily enjoyed, after
which the President and her Counselors were
each presented with a beautiful shell box by
Bp. Joseph L. Horne, in behalf of the com-
mittee. Dancing was then continued until
12 o'clock, when the company separated, all
feeling amply repaid for their trouble.
	New Year's Day was celebrated by a Fair,
given by the Primary Associations of both
wards, under the management of the Presi-
dents and Counselors of the Associations. It
was held in the Society Hall, doors opened
at 9 o'clock a. m. and closed at 4 o'clock
p.m. A programme was arranged and
songs and recitations were delivered at in-
tervals during the day, by the members of
the Associations. The articles were useful
and ornamental, reflecting great credit on
the little folks, for their industry and in-
genuity. We mention a few: quilts, tidies, em-
broidery, pillow shams and slips, wool and
paper flowers, stockings, a framed picture
of French embroidery, a braided rug, mats
and pin cushions, a straw hat, card board
work and pastry, done by the little girls,
a street car, spinning wheel, roller, nutmeg-
grater, ladders, hay racks, drawings, whips,
halter, nose sack, hammer, a jumping jack,
corn; potatoes and beets, these last made
and raised by the boys.
	CELIA E. BEAN, President,
	MINNIE A. FARNSWORTH, Counselor.
Richfield, Feb. 12th, 1883.
