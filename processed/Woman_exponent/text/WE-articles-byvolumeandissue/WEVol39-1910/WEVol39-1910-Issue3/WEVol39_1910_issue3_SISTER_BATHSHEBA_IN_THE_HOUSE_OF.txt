SISTER BATHSHEBA IN THE HOUSE OF
			THE LORD.
	Great as was the love of this noble and
gifted woman, Bathsheba W. Bigler Smith,
for the beautiful works of charity which
pertain to the mission of our Relief Society,
her devotion to the special labors of salva-
tion and redemption for the children of God
in his Holy House seemed, if possible, even
more earnest and untiring, and more richly
and perfectly enjoyed.
	Having begun to acquire a knowledge of
the sacred ordinances of the House of the
Lord under the direct instructions of the
Prophet Joseph, unto whom the perfect and
wonderful plan was revealed by the Lord
himself, and having administered in those
ordinances in the Nauvoo, St. George, Lo-
gan and Salt Lake Temples, her understand-
ing and comprehension of the magnitude
and grandeur of the work might well be so
far reaching that her appreciation of and
love for it could not be otherwise than great
indeed. And so well was she fitted for such
angelic and loving employment, both by na-


ture and education, it is
not in any way surpris-
ing that from the time of
the first work which was
performed in the Salt
Lake Temple until the
beginning of the illness
which terminated her
mortal existence, she was
ever anxious to be in her
place in that holy edifice,
and missed there on very
few occasions during
more than seventeen
years, since the temple’s
dedication.—L. L. Greene
Richards.
