			R. S. REPORTS.
IN-HONOR OF RETIRING OFFICERS.
	The Relief Society of the Big Horn Stake
gave a banquet at Byron, August 10th, 1910,
in honor of the retiring presidency, Lucy F.
Grant, Avilda Dickson, Ida Sessions, Nellie
Jolly and Ella May. From 11 a. m. until
12 a. m. the time was spent in social chat-
ting and hand shaking. They then partook
of a most excellent luncheon which was to
the credit and honor of the sisters of Byron.
The tables were beautifully decorated with
flowers and fruits fresh from their own
gardens.
	At 2:30 p. m. the following program was
rendered: President Mary L. Welch pre-
siding. Congregation sang, “There is Sun-
shine in my Soul;” prayer was offered by
Brother Egan; Singing, “If There’s Sun-
shine in your Heart.” Address of welcome

[Column 2]
by Barbara Lyman. Verses composed for
the occasion, read by Nellie Jolly. Duet,
“What are the Wild Waves Saying,” by
Etta Wilcox and Margaret Holyoak; reci-
tation, “Slanders,” Rachel Briggs; senti-
ments, Retta Porter; recitation, “My Mis-
sion,” Mattie Neeves; vocal solo, “The
Beautiful City,” by Rebecca Jennings. Re-
marks, “Our aim in Relief Society work,”
Artie Willis; recitation, “John Davis and
Wife’s Argument,” by Kate Abram. Re-
marks were made by stake presidency, Chas.
A. Welch, Jesse W. Crosby and Byron
Sessions. Duett, “When you Bowed this
Morning,” by Millie Egan and Bertha Ses-
sions. President Mary L. Welch then pre-
sented the retiring officers with a token
of love, purchased by the sisters, namely:
Stake President Lucy F. Grant and Coun-
selors, each a silk shawl; Nellie Jolly and
Ella May a cracker bowl, after which they
arose and thanked the sisters for the love
and esteem they had shown them. Presi-
dent Mary L. Welch felt to thank all those
who had worked so zealously in preparing
dinner and making arrangements for the
occasion.
	Singing, “God be with you.” Benedic-
tion, Francis Crosby.
					LIBBIE SNELL,
					Acting Secretary.
