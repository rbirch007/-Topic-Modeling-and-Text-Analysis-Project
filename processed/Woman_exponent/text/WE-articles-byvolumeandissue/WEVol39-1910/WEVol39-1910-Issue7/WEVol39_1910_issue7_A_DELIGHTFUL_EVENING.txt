A DELIGHTFUL EVENING.
	February is the natal month of that es-
teemed and beloved Utah woman, Mrs.
Emmeline B. Wells, and during that month
her numerous friends, vie with each other
in showing her their love and appreciation.
	The first of a series of entertainments
was a most charming dinner party given on
the evening of Friday, the 17th instant, by
Mrs. J. S. Houtz, in her elegant apart-
ments in the Avalon.
	The guests were the charter members
of the Utah Woman’s Press Club, now res-
ident in the city, and the First Presidency
of the Church. The rooms were effective-
ly decorated in red and green, and a fine
musical program was rendered by Mrs.
Agnes Dalquist Beckstrand and Prof. and
Miss Nettleton. Mrs. Houtz, herself a
most delightful hostess, was ably assisted
by two charming daughters, Miss Pearl and
Eradne and a niece Miss Snow.
	The appointments of the table were very
beautiful; filmy Duchesse lace centerpieces
over green silk with a centerpiece of red
and white carnations mingled with ferns
and smilax, The place cards were red
hearts with the name of the guest and


“Utah Woman’s Press Club” thereon, and 
the service used was green Yeirakas ware 
inlaid with silver, while the dinner was all 
that the most fastidious gourmet could pos-
sibly desire, and the beauty and charm of it
was that the young daughters of the house 
prepared and served it themselves. 
	A beautiful little Miss of three years—
Channa Glory—assisted in the entertain-
ment of the guests with singing and quaint 
conversation. A ring with a setting of the 
Utah stone—the Amatrice, the gift of the 
Club members—was presented to Mrs. 
Wells, President Joseph F. Smith making 
a graceful and tender presentation speech. 
	The guests will ever feel indebted to Mrs. 
Houtz and her beautiful family for a most 
delightful and enjoyable evening.
