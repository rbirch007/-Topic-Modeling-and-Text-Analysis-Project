THE BRUSSELS CONGRESS.
	Miss Martina G. Kramers, editor of Jus
Suffragii, represented the International
Woman Suffrage Alliance at the World’s
Congress of International Associations held
at Brussells on May 9, 10, and 11, 1910.
During the sectional meetings, Miss Kram-
ers got a woman placed on one of the sec-
tional boards, and called attention to the
rights of mothers, married women, and self-
supporting women as a topic for discussion
in societies working for international juris-
prudence. A resolution was drafted.
