A TRIBUTE
	To Sister Celia E. Hunt Bean, who was called
home, May last 1910.
	I first met her as a young girl, many years ago
just budding into womanhood.
	She was modest, gentle, rather timid and shy at
times, yet, one could not fail to perceive on first
aquaintance, the unusual personality, the true
genuineness of soul and superiority of mind she
possessed.
	We were schoolmates, personal friends for a
number of years, and my being her senior, she
often came to me for advice. Along religious and
literary lines she excelled.
	She was descended from an excellent family, on
both her father and mother's lines.
	The dear old grandmother, Celia Hunt, (for
whom she was named) wife of Captain Jefferson
Hunt, of Mormon Battalion fame, told me thirty-
six years ago. that she was "first cousin to the
gallant General Montgomery, who fell at the bat-
tle of Quebec, early in the Revolutionary war."
	In Celia's family circle she was a leader, re-


fined, cultured, spiritual in temperament, she
ever wielded an influence for the uplifting the
moral strength of her home. As wife and mother
she was self-sacrificing and untiring in her labors
of love and devotion.
	In the communities which were fortunate in
claiming her as a citizen, all have felt her kindly
help and generous aid i[-] their time of sorrow or
necessity. Firm in her religious convictions, she
never wavered in her trust in God.
	We will all miss her magnetic presence, her
honorable, upright character, her gentle woman-
liness, the grace and loveliness of her life, but to
her husband, her children, her immediate family
associates, her loss is irreparable. May God's
mercy, His love, His care, guidance and protec-
tion be over and with them all the time, is my
faithful, my earnest prayer.
JULIA P. M. FARNSWORTH.
August 1, 1910.
