OBITUARY.
	In memory of Jennie Dixon Hickman, of Hinck-
ley, Utah, who was called home May 23, 1910.
	Sister Hickman was born in Payson, Utah, Feb-
ruary 24, 1879. She was the only daughter of Mr.
and Mrs. John H, Dixon of Payson. She was
married to F. Leslie Hickman in the Salt Lake
Temple, June 19, 1906.
	Since her marriage she has lived in Hinckley
and has won the love and admiration of the en-
tire community, She was in every respect an
ideal woman so pure, so gentle and kind, so tal-
ented and cultured—she was always on hand to
perform any task assigned to her. In her faith-
ful work as a member and teacher in the Hinck-
ley Relief Society, she was never found wanting.
	She leaves a husband and two little children,
also a multitude of friends to mourn her loss.
	The officers of the Hinckley Relief Society offer
the following
RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.
	Whereas, after a painful illness, our beloved
sister, Jennie Dixon Hickman, has been called to
the "other side", be it resolved that we, the Sis-
ters of the Hinckley Relief Society do sincerly sym-
pathize with the grief-stricken family, and we
mourn for one who was one of earth's choicest
spirits—a model for us all.
	Resolved that in her death we recognize and feel
keenly the loss of a true saint and faithful worker,
aud we are proud to have had her association and
example and we are sure that she will receive the
crown of one of the noblest daughters of Zion.
Farewell, dear, but not forever—
There will be a glorious dawn;
We shall meet to part, no, never!
On the resurrection morn.
'Tho thy darling form lies sleeping
In the cold and silent tomb,
Thou shalt have a glorious waking
When the blessed Lord doth come.
	Resolved that the resolutions be placed on the
record of the Relief Society and a copy be sent to
the bereaved family.
Phoebe Hale Mecham, Pres.
Catherine A, Allred, Sec.
