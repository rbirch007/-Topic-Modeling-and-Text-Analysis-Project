LAWN PARTY AND BANQUET.
	A most entertaining and enjoyable even-
ing was given by Mrs. Emma H. Jenson
at the home, 154 North 2nd West Street,
on Thursday, July 7th, in honor of Mrs.
Romania B. Penrose, with whom Mr. and
Mrs. Jenson and their daughter Eva traveled
in Europe in company with President A. H.
Lund and wife and daughter Eva, and to
celebrate the arrival of the hostesses in Salt


Lake City twenty-five years ago. Their
luxurious home was most artistically ar-
ranged and decorated with palms, sweet
peas and carnations. Their beautiful lawn
was a source of great enjoyment owing to its
shade and wealth of foliage. An elaborate
dinner was served, covers being laid for
fifty guests. The ornamentations were ex-
ceedingly pretty and delicate. A special
feature of the decoration being a silver cen-
ter piece recently received from Mr. Andrew
Jensen, being a presentation to him by the
L.D. S. choir of Copenhagen, Denmark,
where he is presiding over the Scandinavian
Mission, as a token of love and respect for
all his kindness and zeal. During the re-
past Miss Bessie Smith, accompanied by
Miss Eva H. Jenson, sang several songs in
her pathetic and graceful manner. During
the evening several vocal numbers were
rendered by Mr. Amer Hansen, Mrs. C. V.
Anderson and the inimitable George B.
Margetts. Mrs. E. J. D. Roundy made an
eulogistic tribute to the hospitality and
kindness of the hostesses. All regretted
when the hour of separation arrived and
were unanimous in their expressions of
pleasure at the enjoyable time they had
passed and looked forward to another simi-
lar occasion,and desired that the same com-
pany might be privileged to meet again.
