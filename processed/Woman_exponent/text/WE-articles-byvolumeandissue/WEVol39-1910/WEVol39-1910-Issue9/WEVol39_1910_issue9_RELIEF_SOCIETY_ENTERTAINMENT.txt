RELIEF SOCIETY ENTERTAINMENT.
	An entertainment was given by the
Twelfth-Thirteenth Ward Relief Society on
the 17th of March, 1911, in the amusement
hall of said ward, being the anniversary of
the organization of the Relief Society.
	Refreshments were being served, tables
being set for that purpose, and all was
beautifully decorated with ferns and pink
and white carnations. Having been com-
fortably seated at the tables, President Mary
Carrington spoke a few words of welcome,
wishing them all a good and enjoyable time
during the evening.
	Nearly three hundred persons were en-
tertained. After the banquet had been
served the tables were cleared away, and a
short program prepared for the evening was
rendered.
	Opened by singing, “America.”
	A solo was then rendered by Brother
Melvin Peterson. A piano solo by Dorriett
Evans.
	President Joseph E. Taylor gave a very
interesting talk on the work of the Relief
Society, also said that on the day of the
organization, sixty-nine years ago, there
were eighteen members, and today there are
possibly over forty thousand.
	A solo was then given by Mrs. Ethel
Leaver, followed by a solo given by Heber
K. Aldous. Sister Ellis Shipp, M. D., of
the Relief Society, then spoke for a short
time, her remarks being along the same line
as those of Brother Taylor. A solo was
then rendered by Sister Elma Young.
	Bishop F. B. Platt spoke a few words of
encouragement and asked God to let His
choicest blessings rest upon the Relief So-
ciety.
	After benediction, pronounced by Brother
Thomas Williams, an invitation was given
to all to stay and enjoy a good time in danc-
ing.
			HULDA C. ERICKSON, 
							Secretary.
