NURSE CLASS AND OBSTETRICS.
	Dr. Ellis R. Shipp’s School in Nursing
and Obstetrics closed the winter session on
Friday, the 31st inst. The next term will
begin on Monday, Oct. 9th, 1911, at her res-
dence, 713 Second Ave.,
	After many months of theoretical and
practical instruction, these ladies have
passed a most successful examination and
have now gone to their various fields of use-
fulness hoping to dispense health and hap-
piness to those requiring skillful and effi-
cient service.
	The importance of such knowledge can
scarcely be over-estimated, educational to
the wife and mother— to the daughter, as
well as to those wishing to make a pro-
fessional career.

To minister with tender care
	To sick and suffering;
To mitigate the pangs they bear
	Will greatest blessings bring.
Exemplify the love of One
	Who lived alone to bless,
Who “healed the sick” and walked the path
	Of lowly righteousness.
							E. R. S.
