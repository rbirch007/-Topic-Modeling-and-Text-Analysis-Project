RECEPTION AT THE McCUNE MANSION.
	The Genealogical Society of Utah gave a
grand reception and musicale at the elegant
residence of Hon. and Mrs. A. W. McCun[-]
on Wednesday evening, June 15th, 1910.
The elegant home opened its doors to a very
large number of the citizens of Utah. Three
thousand cards of invitation were issued
and many responded, the President of the
Society, the Right Hon. Anthon H. Lund,
and the Vice President, Joseph F. Smith,
Jr. were present in person, and Elder Ben-
jamin H. Goddard, the active agent of this
mportant work, was one of the committee
busy in anticipating the wishes of all to take
part in the pleasures of the evening.
	The music was exquisite and the floral
decorations were artistic as well as fragrant,
but the home in itself was the one attrac-
tion to those who had an opportunity to see
all the beautiful furnishings and adorn-
ments. One might fancy being in an old
castle or abbey of the 15th century so far as
elegance and sumptuousness, only that
everything bears the stamp of modern times.
The hall in the upper story is so unique in
tself that the dancing seemed a second con-
sideration though the music was charming.
	The committee of ladies were very atten-
tive and solicitous to have all the guests
meet the hostess and the principal people


interested in this wonderful society, and al-
together the evening entertainment was a
perfect success; but it is doubtful in our
mind whether those present comprehended
much of the vast magnitude of the work
this new society represents. 
	If an incentive to become members and 
enter into the spirit of this far-reaching sal-
vation-promoting organization has been
given through bringing together such a large
number of people, then the entertainment
has been a great step in the advancement of 
what is to be accomplished in work for the
dead in the Temples of the Lord.
