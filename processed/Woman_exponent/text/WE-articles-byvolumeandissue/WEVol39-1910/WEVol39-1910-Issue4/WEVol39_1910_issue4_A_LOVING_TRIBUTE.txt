A LOVING TRIBUTE.
	President Bathsheba W. Smith.
Methinks the angels must have wept when she
Left Eden’s bowers, put on the garb of earth.
Mortality, attendant of our birth,
Yet still the path to immortality.
Was intuition of her destiny
Felt by the mother-heart that gave her birth,
Cradled and welcomed her great soul to earth.
In latter days to stand, to act, to be?
With what majestic mien she trod life's way,
Leading to higher plane all womanhood,
Ornate with virtues gilding direst day,
Until High Priestess to her sex she stood;
Eden and earth enriched, she passed along
Through gates celestial, joined the victor’s
	throng.
					—Nellie Becraft.
