			ONEIDA STAKE.
	Morning Session.
	The Oneida Stake Relief Society met in
annual conference on May 7, 1910, Presi-
dent Louisa B. Benson presiding.
	Singing, “O ye mountains high.”
	Prayer, Julia Roper.
	Singing, “Prayer is the soul’s sincere de-
sire.”
	Address of Welcome, Lucy D. Parkinson.
President Benson expressed disappointment
at not having any of the General Board,
present, but joy in seeing and meeting with
her sisters. Explained that it was due to a
misunderstanding of dates that we had no
visitors.
	A detailed sketch, giving all dates of or-
ganization, etc., of the R. S. of the stake was

[Column 2]
compiled and read by Sec. Phebe A. M.
McNiel.
	A ladies’ chorus was then rendered.
	The General Relief Society conference
was reported in brief by President Benson,
also gave a description of the Relief Society
rooms in the Bishop’s building.
	General and Local Relief Society officers
were sustained.
	Singing, “Sing we now at parting.”
	Benediction by Counselor Hannah Mick-
leson.
	Afternoon Session.
	The Stake Presidency and members of
High Council were present.
	Singing, “Come, let us anew.”
	Prayer, Elder F. W. Pratt.
	Singing, “Sometime, Somewhere.”
	A lecture on Tithing was given by Linda
N. Benson.
	Piano solo, Georgia Dailey.
	Recitation, Enoch Nelson, entitled “The
Ship,” by E. R. Snow.
	Counselor Jos. S. Geddes took up the lec-
ture and spoke on Tithing, also spoke on
Temperance.
	Ladies’ chorus, “Count your blessings.”
	Counselor Chas. D. Goslind spoke on the
system in the Church, dwelling briefly on
the first principles, emphasizing the Word of
Wisdom.
	President Geo. C. Parkinson spoke of the
carrying out of God’s purposes on earth,
dwelt on the evils of intemperance and the
cure thereof, spoke of the power of the
Priesthood, and in what ways a woman en-
joyed the blessings of the same.
	President Benson endorsed all that had
been said, gave words of encouragement to
all.
	Singing, “God be with you till we meet
again.”
	Conference adjourned for six months.
	Benediction, Elder Crosham.
