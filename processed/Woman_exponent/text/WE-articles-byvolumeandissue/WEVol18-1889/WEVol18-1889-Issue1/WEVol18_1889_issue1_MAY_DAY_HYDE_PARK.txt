MAY DAY HYDE PARK.

	The Primary Association of Hyde Park,
Cache Co., had a May Day celebration
Wednesday, May 1st. A full house assem-
bled; the children and young ladies arrayed
in white and flowers, made a fine picture.
The singing and music were pleasantly ren-
dered. Of course they had their May Queen,
who carried her honors, and dispensed her
favors with graceful dignity. A few words
of approval to children, and thanks and coun-
sel to parents were spoken by Counselor Emma
Pike and Mrs. E. L. Crowther of Logan.
	The closing remarks were made by their
Bishop. A lovely dance was given the child-
ren in the afternoon, and one in the evening
for the older ones. Much credit is due Presi-
dent Kirby and her assistauts. The brass
band turned out, and made such good music,
that even the old folks wanted to dance.
