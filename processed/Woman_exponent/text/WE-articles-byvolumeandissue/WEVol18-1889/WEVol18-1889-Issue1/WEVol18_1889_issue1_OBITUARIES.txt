OBITUARIES.
	Died, at Payson, Jan., 21st, 1889.  Louisa Haag, our
much beloved sister, who was born in Stuttgart, Germany,
November 8th, 1839.  Embraced the Gospel, in the same
place, in 1881, and five of her children soon followed her
example.  Emigrated to Utah in 1883, with two of her
children, to join three others who had preceded her; leav-
ing her affectionate husband and one son behind.  She
was religiously inclined, seeking after truth from her early
childhood.
	She would steal away from her home to wait on the sick,
when only girl of fourteen.  It seemed to be her greatest
desire to give relief to the poor, sick, and needy.  She ex-
posed herself to wait on others; as a lady physician she did
a great deal of good among the sick.  She was an affec-
tionate mother and always set an example before her
children which was worthy of their imitation.
