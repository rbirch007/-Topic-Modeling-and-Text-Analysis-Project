NOTES AND NEWS.

	Rev. Mr. Pitblado spoke on woman suffrage
in Unity Hall, Hartford, Conn., Tuesday, May 14.
	It is reported that there are now six women
police officers in London, and seven more are
to be appointed.
	The Connecticut House of Representatives
has defeated the proposed conititutional amend-
ment striking the word "male" out of the consti-
tution.
	Miss Rose Elizabeth Cleveland is going
abroad in June, and is said to have accepted a
position in a young women's boarding school
in London.
	 Queen Christina, of Spain,, has promised
her support to the anti-slavery campaign in
Africa. The Spanish Society has work near
its own doors, as slaves of both sexes are
brought from the interior of North Africa and
sold in the open markets of Morocco.
	Glorious news from Michigan! The muni-
cipal woman suffrage bill passed the House of
Representatives on Wednesday, May 15, at
3.15 P. M., by 25 majority. A special dispatch
from Mrs. May S. Knaggs gives the vote as
yeas, 58; nays, 33. There is great rejoicing in
Lansing over this signal victory.—Ex.
	"Mrs. Emily Pfeiffer's new volume of poems,
"Flowers of the Night," the publication of
which was interrupted by the stroke which fell
upon her in the death of her husband in Jan-
uary last," was probably issued in London last
month. "It is known that Mr. Pfeiffer, who was
a man of varied accomplishments, was virtu-
ally the editor of his wife's works, which
passed out of her hands into his almost as soon
as committed to paper. In accordance with his
wishes, the issue of "Flowers of the Night" is
resumed by his widow at the earliest possible
moment."
