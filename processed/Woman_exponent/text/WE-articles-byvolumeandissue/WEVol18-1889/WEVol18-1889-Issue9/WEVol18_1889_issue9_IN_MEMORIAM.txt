IN MEMORIAM
	Mary Emerette daughter of John F. and Mary Annie
Rupp Gerber born in Granger July 30, 1870. departed this
life August 26, 1889. The cause of her death was typhoid
pneumonia,  after a few day's illness while absent from
her home; her remains were brought home to her parents
in Granger and the funeral services were held at the Ward
house and conducted by Bishop D. Mc'Rae.  Suitable re-
marks were made  to  console  the mourners  by  Elders
John C Mackay, George B.  Wallace, and the Bishop.  At
the first organization of the Y.  L.  M.  I.  A.  of the Ward
deceased was chosen as first counselor she also assisted in
the singing and in the Primary Association and Sunday
School faithfully till she went away into exile.  She was
a constant reader of the WOMAN'S EXPONENT always
willing to learn some good, from her childhood, she was
affectionate to little children and a kind nurse to her
younger brothers and sisters. Her demise caused her 
family and friends much sorrow, and has cast a gloom 
over all that knew her intimately; she was a happy dis-
position, cheerful and patient under the many trials she was
called to endure, while absent from  her  home and loved 
ones.  Her comforting words to her mother were at all
times,  'do not worry over me, I am all right." She
passed away like a child going to sleep, without a struggle
true to her God and the faith she had embraced, and it
can be truly said of her she sacrificed her all  for the 
glorious hope which was within her;  she dwells now in a
holier sphere.  Rest, sweet rest at home in heaven.

		Dearest Mary, "thou hast left us
		Here thy loss we deeply feel
		But 'tis God that has bereft us
		He can all sorrows heal."

		"Soon again we hope to meet thee
		When death's gloomy night has fled
		Then again with joy we'll greet thee
		Where no bitter tears are shed."
									M. A.  R. G.
Granger Sept. 13th 1889
