NOTES AND NEWS.

	The Woman’s Congress will be held in Den-
ver, October 8th, 9th and 10th.
	The annual meeting of the Pennsylvania
Woman Suffrage Aassociation will be held
October 24th and 25th.
	We have had the reign of man’s tyranny:
we have had the era of his chivalry and
generosity, now let people rise to the full
moral dignity of an American citizen and say:
“Let there be justice.”
	Women were admitted as delegates to the
Protestant Methodist Conference which met in
September in Wheeling, Virginia. It was
voted by a small majority. This is the denom-
ination of which Anna Shaw is a regular
ordained minister.
	Miss Anthony recently visited her mother’s
bridesmaid, Mrs. Annie Jenks Richardson at
her home in Cheshire Massachusetts. Mrs.
Richardson is 94 years of age but reads the
WOMAN'S TRIBUNE and keeps up her interest
in the progress of women.
	Probably the theory that voting would be
too arduous a duty for women to perform has
arisen from the fact that this seems to be such
an exhausting business for men. So many of
them after waking up and being lively on
election day are so used up, that they have to
sit around on dry goods boxes and sun them-
selves for months in order to recuperate.

	The 10th and 12th Sections of the New
Constitution of Montana reads as follows:	
	SECTION 10.—Women shall be eligible to hold the office
of county superintendent of schools or any school district
office, and shall have the right to vote at any school
district election.
	SEC. 12.—Upon all questions submitted to the vote of
the tax-payers of the state, or any political discussion
thereof, Women who are tax-payers and possessed of
the qualifications for the right of suffrage required by men
by this constitution shall equally with men have the right
to vote.”
