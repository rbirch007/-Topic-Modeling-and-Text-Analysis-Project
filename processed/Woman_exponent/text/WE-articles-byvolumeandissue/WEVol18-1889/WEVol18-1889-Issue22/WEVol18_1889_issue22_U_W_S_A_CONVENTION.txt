U. W. S. A. CONVENTION.

	The Annual Convention of the National Ameri-
can W. S. A. of Utah Ter.; was held in the Salt
Lake Assembly Hall, Thursday April 3, 1890


commencing at 2 p. m. Mrs. S. M. Kimball in
the Chair. Prayer by the Chaplain Mrs. E. B.
Wells, minutes read and approved. Report of
Emery Co. W. S. A. by the President Mrs. S. A.
Fullmer. Mrs. E. Y. Stanford President of the
Ogden W. S. A. gave a brief report. Mrs. Susa
Y. Gates reported Utah Co. W. S. A. and made a
telling speech. Referred to the former President's
death and the consequent necessity of reorganiza-
tion, said they had endeavored to organize a civil
government class and to study divorce laws and
those relating specially to women. They held
parlor meetings and discussed these subjects.
They were to have a public debate on the 23rd of
April, the subject "Shall woman have the fran-
chise?" 540 members enrolled.
	Mrs. S. M. Kimball commented upon the Com-
mon Law as it stood upon the Statute Books o
many of the States—that made woman the goods
and chattels of her husband etc., and the good
work accomplished by Mrs. Packard before
Legislatures, her success in getting this obnoxious
statute stricken out in many States and Terri-
tories.
	Morgan, Cache, Sanpete and Sevier Counties
not reported. Beaver Co. W. S. A. represented
by Mrs. S. C. Maeser, said they had songs
lectures, essays etc., members 40. Mrs. S. S.
Boyer of Springville W. S. A. reported 125 mem-
bers, they were studying municipal government,
with other subjects. She made a glowing speech
full of suggestions and pertinent with facts. Mrs.
Zina D. H. Young had recently visited Nephi and
gave a good report of the W. S. A. of Juab Co.
Lehi W. S. A. reported by Mrs. Josephine
Brown, 150 members, meetings held regularly,
study of civil government with an aim to gain
a political education.
	Prof. Alexander of Canada gave quite an elo-
quent address in which he set forth the absurdity
of women publicly ridiculing men etc. Thought
the ladies should strike out in new paths, said it
was impossible to legislate morals; the home
must be the center of these—home-rule is pro-
gressive.
	The Chairman Mrs. Kimball answered some
objectionable points in his remarks in a very
polite manner. Mrs. M. Y. Dougall gave an in-
teresting review of her trip to Washington with
Mrs. Kimball, related some pleasing incidents.
	Convention adjourned sine die.
		RACHEL EDWARDS, Sec'y.
	The officers of the W. S. A. the Delegates from
other places and some other ladies met after-
wards at Mrs. Phebe Y. Beatie's and partook of
refreshments, joining in informal conversation
and asking questions on the subject of suffrage
etc.—Ed.
