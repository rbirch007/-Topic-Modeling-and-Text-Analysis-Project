EDITORIAL NOTE.

	Miss Flora E. Finlayson of the "Bostonians"
who recently performed in the Opera here was
studying at the New England Conservatory dur-
ing the time Prof. Evan Stephens was there and
also Miss Daisie D. Woods. While she was here
in the City Prof. Stephens and Miss Woods ar-
ranged to have a sort of impromptu recital for her
in the large Tabernacle Thursday Apr. 3, and
let her try her voice in that large building.
Prof. Radcliffe who is always extremely obliging
consented also to play a few pieces and Prof.
Stephens played Miss Finlayson's accompani-
ment on the organ, and she sang those simple
ballads "Annie Laurie," and "Love's Old Sweet
Song," in a most unaffected style and excellent
voice, as great singers always do. She has a rich
contralto voice and is one of the soloists of the
Bostonians. She is very handsome and graceful,
a genteel figure and very pleasing in manner. She
is a Western girl from Reno, Nevada and at the
N. E. Conservatory in Boston was known as
"Nevada." Prof. Radcliffe rendered some choice
selections from Baptiste in his very best style,
and it is well known here what that is. He has
made many friends since his residence in Salt
Lake City. Miss Finlayson paid one or two visits
to this office and sang several of her best songs
in the parlor of the WOMAN'S EXPONENT. She is
rather new in opera but she is sure to win name
and fame, for she is not only a fine singer but has
winning ways, is modest, unassuming and also
beautiful and accomplished.
