						LYDIA LUND


The Value of Detached Ideas! That de-
pends—upon whose ideas they are: whether
the idea is one of Shakespeare’s myriads, or of
old Montaigne’s thousands, or Bacon’s or
Pascal’s or Goethe’s hundreds,  or Emerson’s
scores—an idea that can stand by itself, an
idea which is like a rose from your garden,
which does not need the garden itself to en-
hance its beauty; an idea which is like a beau-
tiful pillar from a Greek temple, more than
an ordinary temple itself, for a temple is only
a place for worship, while this is a thing to
worship—for its beauty. The value of this—
of beauty and the love of beauty—will stand
as long as the world stands.
