NOTES AND NEWS. 

	The estate of Robert Browning amounted 
to eighty-five thousand dollars—all earned by
writing poetry. 
	THE theme of the Japanese national anthem, 
as recently rendered in English, is: "Oh, that
the reign of our emperor might continue thou-
sands and thousands of years—until oyster
shells become rocks."
	The story that Mrs. General Grant will
publish a volume of the dead warrior's love
letters to herself is untrue. It grows out of
her intention to put into historical form much
of the valuable material that she necessarily
collected during her eventful life.
	The bill for the admission of the state of 
Wyoming with a constitution guaranteeing
equal suffrage to women passed the House of
Representatives on Thursday afternoon, March
27, by a strict party vote,—yeas 132, nays 119;
every Republican voting in favor and every 
Democrat against it. Welcome Wyoming!
	MRS. EMILY CRAWFORD, the noted Journalist
of London, says in answer to the question "what
do you think of journalism as a profession for
women?”  "Well.  I should say the first requi-
site was a giant constitution. A woman of 
delicate physique may do good by spells, but
the ceaseless grind of the regular journalistic
life requires the strongest constitution."
