WOMAN’S WORKS.

	I do not believe woman desires to rise above
man, or usurp any of his rights. And would
the opposers of "Woman’s Suffrage furnish
every lady with an upright intelligent, sober,
working husband, they would be willing to
stand by his side as his counselor and educator
of his children.
	But let us take a glance around the world,
and see her true condition. If it be across
ocean or in our own loved land, we find women
 not by the hundreds or thousands, but by the
millions thrown on their own resources for
support, young girls homeless with parents
unable to provide for them, wives widowed
by death or desertion, or worse still husbands
who fail to support them, and they, left to pro-
vide for their families. Then if woman must
take equal responsiblities with man, should
she not have equal rights. 
	There are many positions in which she gives
as good satisfaction as a man for instance in
the schoolroom, in the sales room, or
as type setter, and in many other places of
trust, but is she recompensed with equal salary?
No! for the same work, she receives from 1/3 to
1/2 less pay.
	But the majority of women are not prepared
for any of these positions and must eke out a
meager living by earning a mere pittance at
the hardest drudgery.
	Then is it to be wondered at, that her more
fortunate sisters seek to help lift her burdens,
to alleviate her woes and clamor for equal rights
and laws, that the training and education of
the child should be self suporting.
	For a woman who can face the world and
feel herself equal to any fate that may befall
her is richer, than if she posessed government
bonds.
	Woman’s cause is just and will eventually
succeed.
	Then let us as a band of sisters, not tire in
the good work we have just begun, but march
onward, ever onward until success crowns our
united efforts and right wins the day.
