HEPHZIBAH.
				VII.
	In youth’s sweet Spring time, O, how grand and true,
		The aspirations of a fresh young heart;
	And the bright vision, fancy opes to view,
		Strange forecasts of the future life impart.

	While Mrs.  H. was at the seaside with her
little daughter, she tried to make a study of the
disposition and temperament of the child, and
her maternal love for the little one deepened
more and more, as she carefully watched her
day by day and found her exceptionally bright
and knowing.  She took counsel with herself in
the matter, and considered it well, for she saw
plainly that an ordinary education would not
satisfy the intense longing, that Hepsie mani-
fested for something beyond what seemed to be
quite sufficient for her young companions.  The
