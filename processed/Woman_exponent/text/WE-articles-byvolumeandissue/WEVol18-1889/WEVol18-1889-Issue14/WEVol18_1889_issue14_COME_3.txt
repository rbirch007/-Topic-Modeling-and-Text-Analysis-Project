	With what little means she could gather after
selling her few treasures which she had managed
to keep from her husband, she gained admit-
tance to a hospital and while there confided to
her nurse, who seemed a kindly disposed wo-
man, the story of her father and the new faith
he had espoused. It happened that the nurse
had heard of this new Gospel and was in cor-
respondence with an Elder-at that time,and so
she worked with Emma to help her to find her
father. The nurse wrote to the Elder to come to
the hospital, and after hearing Emma's story he
wrote for her to the Presiding Elder in Eng-
land to find out when and where her family
had emigrated. Word was sent to her father
and he soon sent means for his daughter to
come to him. The thought of seeing her dear
ones and the hope that another warmer cli-
mate might possibly cure her cough, for it
was consumption, which exposure and over-
work had brought upon her, raised her spirits
to an unusual degree, and she seemed to revive
as though by magic. She stood the ocean
voyage and long journey to the West re-
markably well, but when at last the journey
was over and in her father's comfortable home
she was reinstalled as their own bright star,
her strength again failed, and she faded and
died in her mother's arms.
	And this Thanksgiving day the father had,
had ceremonies performed in the Lord's Temple
for that dear daughter, who could not live to
do them for herself, and he strolled to the grave
to offer there a prayer of thanksgiving for the
return of his loved one to him, to his people,
and to her Lord.
	This was the unwritten story beneath that
little stone marked Emma.
							A. W. C.
