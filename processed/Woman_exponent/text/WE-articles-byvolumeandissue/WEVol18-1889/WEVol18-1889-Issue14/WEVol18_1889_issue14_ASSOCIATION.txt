LUCY WEBB HAYES ASSOCIATION.

Mrs. E. B. Wells: 
						Washington, D. C.
						     Nov. 19. 1889.
My Dear Madam!
	The Woman's National Press Association Sept.
6th, 1889 carried the following motion:—Resolved
that we inaugurate a plan to erect a statue of 
Lucy Webb Hayes in this city—to further this
movement they called a public meeting of all
the woman’s organizations in the city. Discus-
sion has evolved and elaborated the plan into a
temple with a large hall for conventions, smaller
rooms for committees, clubs, etc. niches for
statuary or an art gallery where busts and por-
traits of great women could be placed, with a
drinking fountain and statue of Mrs. Hayes in
front of the building. The following committee
has been elected to correspond with prominent
women concerning a National Organization. Miss
Carrie Harrison, Miss Clara Barton, Mrs. Bishop
J. F. Hurst, Mrs. E. M. S. Marble, Mrs. D. S.
McKnight, Mrs. Annie E. Bovee, Mrs. Sarah D.
Le. Fetra, Mrs. Albertine S. Odell, Mrs. Sen. H.
M. Teller, Mrs. Dr. T. S. Hamlin, Mrs. E. A. V.
Anderson, Dr. Clara Bliss Hinds, representing
the Red Cross, Home and Foreign Missionary
Societies, W. C. T. U. Woman's Relief Corps.
G. A. R. Woman's National Press Association


and the Woman’s National Suffrage Association
We purpose having five thousand Charter Mem-
bers the same to elect the National Officers.
	The enclosed card filled and returned to the
secretary with five dollars will entitle you to a
Charter Membership Certificate by return mail.
Upon the cooperation of the women of the
country depends the success of this attempt to
organize the first Woman’s Monument Associa-
tion in America.
			Respectfully,
						Carrie Harrison.
						Chairman,
Dr. Clara Bliss Hinds.
1331 N. St. 				Secretary,
