Ere they join the body for earthly birth


If ﻿they meet the Gone Before.
And, if they talk of the struggle and strife,
That is sure to be  part of an earthly life,
	And trials that must be borne.

And I wonder if when behind the scene,
If they know what will be, and what has been,
	And all the mystery of life.
If so they know ere they enter the gloom
All the joy there'll be in a future home,
	When they've ended  the earthly strife.

As I lovingly clasp my babe so pure,
And think of what she must endure,
	My heart is filled with misgiving.
And I hardly know for which I should weep,
The innocent babe in my arms asleep,
	Or those that are not with the living.

A peace comes with the thoughts of the dead,
To the life beyond with hope we're led,
	And it is joy now understood
To know that forever they all are mine,
Both the living and dead that make the nine,
	If I'm true to my mother-hood.

E. T.
