WOMAN'S EXPONENT


The Ex-Empress of Brazil died suddenly of
heart disease at Oporto, on December 28. and
it is said that all Europe is in mourning for
this gentle spirit, whose only sin was the
crown she wore. Her heart was broken when
she was driven from Brazil, and change of air
and scene could not save her, she had been
Empress forty-seven years, and had spent her
long married life in assisting her husband in
contributing to the charities of her adopted
country, and watching over the education of
her daughters and their children. She always
held aloof from the affairs of the State; was
greatly loved by her people, for her charities
and good works were innunerable. In, every
way she was an exemplary woman and Prin-
cess. The news of her death came as a great
shock to the people in Brazil. Monarchists
and Republicans alike mourn deeply over her
untimely death. The regret is very general as
she was so greatly loved.-
