A SAD DEATH.
	Died in Salt Lake City, November 17th 1889. Sylvia 
beloved wife of Walter C. Lyman, and daughter of John 
and Ane Lovell, born at Deseret, Millard Co. Jan. 9th 
1867. She leaves with her stricken husband, two bright
little boys and in the same grave with her sleep two sweet 
little babes.
	She was well known and dearly loved at her home in Oak
Creek, Millard Co. She was an energetic and faithful 
teacher in the Relief Society of that ward and Counselor 
to the Y. L. M. I. A. for some time and in the ward 
choir her sweet voice was heard singing the songs of 
Zion.
	She is missed more in the home circle, where her kind 
and loving disposition endeared her to all and nothing


but the comforting influence of the Gospel can resign her
friends to their great loss.
	Bless and comfort, O, our Father
		Those who are so sorely tried
	For so many hearts are aching
		Since our own, dear Sylvia died.
	Bless and comfort, Thou, her husband
		And her little boys so fair;
	May they each prove true and faithful
		Till they meet her, "over there."
	Bless, Thou all the stricken loved ones
		Of dear Sylvia pure and fair
	Guide us all O Heavenly Father,
		Till we meet her, "over there,"
								A. E. L.
