	President J. M. Walton made some closing
remarks; hoped the sisters would all attend
the next conference.  Exhorted the brethren
to go and take them.  Spoke of the duties of
mothers to their children, and the amount of
influence a mother has over her children.
	President F. A. Hammond felt there was a
good influence prevailing.  Said the instruc-
tions were excellent at the late conference in
Salt Lake.  There is a movement on foot to
unite the sisters with the Woman Suffrage
society that we might gain strength.  Endorsed
the remarks in regard to taking the EXPO-
NENT; the sisters should take, and thoroughly
read it.  Urged all the society to get good
books; felt that it could not be impressed too
strongly.  Wanted the sisters to teach tithing.
Mothers! teach your girls to be help-meets;
find something for them to do; idleness comes
from an evil source.  Teach them to be self-
sustaining, and not wait for a man who is
able to keep them in ease and luxmry.
	Conference adjonrned for three months, to
meet at Moab.  Singing, "Now We'll Sing
With One Accord."  Prayer by Counselor
Wm. Adams.
	SARAH JANE ROGERSON, Stake Secretary.
