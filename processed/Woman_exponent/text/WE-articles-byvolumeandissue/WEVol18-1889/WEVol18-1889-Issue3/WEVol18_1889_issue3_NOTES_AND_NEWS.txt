NOTES AND NEWS.
	Alice Stone Blackwell, edits The Woman's
Column, No. 3, Park Street, Boston, Mass.  It
is a weekly and but twenty-five cents a year,

	Every woman ordained to preach the gos-
pel, by any church or ecclesiastical organiza-
tion, is a legally suitable person to perform the
marriage ceremony.

	May 20th, the House of the Connecticut
Legislature passed the bill giving women the
right to vote on the question of the sale of in-
toxicating liquors.

	A proclamation was issued by Governor
Warren, of Wyoming Territory, for an elec-
tion of delegates to a Constitutional Conven-
tion to meet September 8.
	
	Mme. Carnot, as the first lady of France, is
as popular as she is beautiful; but both she
and her husband, Count Carnot, the President
of France, never use their titles.  She says,
“no title can be higher than that of President
of the French Republic, because that name
cannot be separated from the history of
France.
	Among all the competitors for the first prize
in the schools of San Francisco, it was won by
a girl, Miss May Ayers. The prize was a
ticket to the Paris exhibition and return. She
has sailed with her mother for Europe,
from New York, visiting Chicago en route.
This is quite a victory for the girls, and proves
the oft repeated assertion, that the brains of
girls are not inferior to the brains of boys.
