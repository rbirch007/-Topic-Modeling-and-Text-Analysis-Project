A MISSIONARY.

How shall we express the great pleasure
	We take in now writing to friends?
O truly! No mortal can measure
	The blessings, which Our Father sends.

One year and a half since we parted
	From friends, with the shake of the hand;
'Tis just the same time since we started,
	From mountains and valleys so grand.

Though in a strange land now we wander,
	As young missionaries we roam;
We're growing the fonder, and fonder
	Of friends in our dear Mountain Home.

Duty called for a time, separation
	From those who are dear to our heart;
From Sunday School, friends and relations,
	From all duty called us to part.

The call was to go on a mission,
	To battle with error and doubt;
And overcome rank opposition
	To truth the world soon shall find out.

This labor and duty devolving
	On us to perform for the Lord—
We left our home firmly resolving,
	To trust in his promising word.

We went, ah yes, 'twas our duty
	To go and proclaim to the world,
The glory and transcendent beauty
	Of Zion's white banner unfurled.

To warn men of all ranks and stations,
	To show them the Gospel we teach,
