     On the return of the committee with the
Constitution and By-Laws, they were read and
submitted to the meeting by the chairman, and
were unanimously adopted.  An intermission
of fifteen minutes was then taken, for the en-
rollment of members, to which 45 responded
and paid the membership fee, which had been
fixed at 25 cents.
     The meeting was again called to order, and
the election of officers proceeded with.
     The association is to be called "The Millard
County Woman Suffrage Association,"  with
Mrs. M. A. Y. Greenhalgh as President;
Mrs. D. Olsen, Mrs. Eugene Dalbrook, Mrs.
Melissa Olsen, of Deseret, as Vice-Presidents.
Mrs. E. Henry, Treasurer; Mrs. Lois Hamp-
ton, Secretary; Miss Mary Anderson, Assistant
Secretary, and Miss E. Henry, Corresponding
Secretary, with an Executive committee of
nine, so that all the settlements in the County
are represented in the officers.  The Constitu-
tion adopted is the same as that by the associa-
tion in Salt Lake City.
     Mrs. Greenhalgh again briefly addressed the 
meeting; thanking them for the honor conferred
upon her, and urging upon them the necessity
of unity, for in this only is strength, which in
the end must prevail.
     Mrs. D. Olsen read a very interesting essay,
subject, "Woman's Right to Vote and Her
Ability to use It."
     Mrs. Yates, in a neat and interesting speech,
spoke forcibly upon the subject of "Woman,
Her Labors, and Her Ability, Together With
Her Present Disabilities," and called the at-
tention of the meeting to the work which had
been done and is still being done, by noble
men and women in the cause of freedom and
of right.
     The meeting was a success, all appearing
satisfied with the choice of officers, and the
proceedings generally.
     The meeting terminated at 6 p.m., and was
closed by prayer by Mrs. D. Olsen.
