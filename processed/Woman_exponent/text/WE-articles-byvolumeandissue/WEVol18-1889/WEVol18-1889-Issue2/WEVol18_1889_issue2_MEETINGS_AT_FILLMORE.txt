MEETINGS AT FILLMORE.

     On May 25 the Primary conference of
Millard Stake was held at Fillmore, commenc-
ing at 10 a.m., Stake President Mrs. D. Olsen
presiding.  The house was well filled with the
children and parents.  The exercises were well
given, and did great credit to the local Presi-
dents and those who had the training of the
little ones.  The Stake and local authorities 
were present, and President Hinckley in a 
brief speech expressed his great pleasure and
satisfaction with all he had heard; encou-
raged both teachers and children to go for-
ward in the good work, and prayed God to
bless them in all their efforts.
     At 2 p.m., the same day, the Relief Society
of Millard Stake held a quarterly confer-
ence at Fillmore; Mrs. E. Yates presiding;
assisted by her counselors, Mrs. Thompson
and Mrs. Robinson.
     The conference was well attended, and the
reports from the various wards were most
favorable.  All the wards were represented.
     President Yates congratulated the sisters
upon their zeal and good works among the
sick and the needy, and gave much excellent
advice, as did also her counselors, and Stake
secretary Mrs. D. Olsen.
     President Hinckley spoke highly of the work
the sisters were engaged in, and encouraged
them to continue zealous and faithful, that the
Lord might bless them.
     There was a large attendance, and the con-
ference was most interesting and instructive,
an excellent spirit prevailing throughout the
entire meeting.
     At the close of the Relief Society conference
at 4 p.m., A WOMAN SUFFRAGE MEETING was
held, the greater part of the women remain-
ing.  A preliminary meeting was held the
previous evening, when a committee was
appointed to consider and report upon a Con-
stitution and By-Laws for a Woman Suffrage
Association for Millard County.
     On Saturday afternoon the general meeting
was held and the Constitution and By-Laws re-
ported upon by the chairman of the committee,
Mrs. E. Henry.
     The meeting was opened by prayer, the
chaplain, Mrs. McBride officiating; the chair-
man of the day, Mrs. D. Olsen presiding.
The committee retired to decide upon the Con-
stitution, and during their absence, at the re-
quest of the chairman, Mrs. M. A. Y. Green-
halgh delivered the opening address and
stated the object for which the meeting was
convened.  Gave a brief sketch of the struggles
by women for their rights;  quoted Senator
