SUFFRAGE MEETING.

	The regular monthly meeting of the Woman's
Suffrage Association of Ogden City was held in
the County Court room, June 3rd, at 4 p. m.
There was a large attendance present and all ap-
peared enthusiastic over the object for which they
had organized and met—the franchise for woman.
Pres. Stanford presided,and Mrs. Sarah Richards,
chaplain, opened by prayer. The minutes of the
previous meeting were read and approved.
	Hon. C. W. Penrose of Salt Lake, was introduced
to the large audience and spoke on the privileges
that should be accorded women. The EXPONEFT
in its next issue will publish a synopsis giving the
principal points and subject matter of the speech
which was a most excellent and telling one.
At the conclusion of Mr. Penrose's speech
Miss Lizzie Ensign sang "The Brook" in a
charming manner, pleasingly accompanied by
Prof. L. D. Edwards.
	Hon. Lorin Farr gave a short address.
	A lively glee was sung by a number of young
ladies under the direction of Prof. Edwards.
	On call for members two new names were ob-
tained on the roll.
	Notice was given that at the next meeting Mrs.


E. B. Wells, editor of the WOMAN’S EXPONENT,
Salt Lake City, would address the society.
On motion the meeting adjourned for one
month.
