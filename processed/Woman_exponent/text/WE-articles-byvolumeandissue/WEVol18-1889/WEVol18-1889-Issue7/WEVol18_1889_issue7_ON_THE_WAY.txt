ON THE WAY.
	If you could see how long a distance I have
been, you would not wonder that I had not written.
  
	The team is now here and we start in a few
minutes for Panguitch, forty-five miles ride. We
held no meetings in Minersville, Sister Snow
stopped one day to rest. We stopped at Adams-
ville next, with the Sisters. Pres. Murdock was
there,also at Greenville; we had many good meet-
ings, they are small places but alive to their
duties. It poured down with rain as we entered
Greenville, at 3 p. m., but we got to Beaver before
sunset, distance 18 miles. Sunday went to meet
ing, Monday Sisters, Conference all day, a very
good time.
	In the evening the Y. L. and Y. M. I. Conference
also a house full and such lovely singing and some
of the most beautiful girls I ever saw in any
place.
	It is very healthy, I have not seen a feeble
looking child; the aged are mostly the suffer-
ers. 				We	have attended	twelve
meetings including the Young Ladies’and Pri-
maries; the children have all done very well.
Tuesday.
	I am at Pres. Murdock’s. The Stake President
Sister Howd goes with us to Panguitch. Sister
Snow has attended all the meetings here, her
wrist is some lame, that is the worst feature from
that fearful fall.
	God is very good, all has been done for us that
it was possible to do. 		We	stopped
one night with Sister R. W. Tyler, had a good
visit; she sent her love to you; am going to
canvass for the Exponent wherever we go, from
place to place.
			Your Loving Sister,
						Z. D. H. Young.
Beaver City, Aug. 14th 1889.
