THE LAND OF REST.
"Where the wicked cease from troubling, and the weary
	are at rest."
		Where is that land of love?
		Oh! mother, tell me where
		The wicked cease from troubling,
		And where no weary are?
		
		Mother my heart is faint,
		I'm longing to be there;
		The cares of life are pressing,
		Oh! mother, tell me where?
		
		Cease, cease, my child; there is no spot,
		That mortals know, where care is not;
		But sin and sorrow both are found, 
		On earth's most consecrated ground.

		And shall I hope in vain, 
		For climes of beauty rare
		Where pain and trouble never come,
		And where no weary are.

		No, not in vain my precious one, 
		The blessed Savior whispers come.
		Have faith in Him; then thou'lt be blest.
		He gives the weary spirit rest.

		Yet not to man has He e'er given
		To know the spot, which we call heaven;
		Be holy; Jesus dwelleth there,
		No trouble comes, no weary are.
									Selected.
