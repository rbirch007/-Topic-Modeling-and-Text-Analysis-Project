MASS MEETING AT LOGAN.
	Logan City Dec. 10th, 1889. At 10 a. m.
a Mass Meeting of the women of Cache Co.,
assembled in the Tabernacle for the purpose of
organizing a Woman Suffrage Association.
Mrs. E. Howard called the attention of the as-
sembly; singing, a fervent prayer was offered
by Lucy B. Young. Mrs. Howard explained
the object of the organization and read a por-
tion of an official call signed by influential
women of the County. Mrs. E. Howard was
elected Chairman and Mrs. Jane E. Molen
Secretary pro. tem. Speeches were then made
by Mrs. Adaline Barber, Mrs. L. B. Young and
Geo. Pitkin Esq., showing the necessity of a
Woman Suffrage Association in this county,
denouncing the action of the Utah Carpet Bag
Liberals, whose misrepresentations to Congress
deprived the women of this Territory of their
inestimable rights.
	The following ladies were unanimously
elected as officers. Mrs. William Sanders
Pres. Mrs. Julia Stewart, First Vice Pres. Mrs.
Mattie Hansen, Second, Mrs. C. I. Goodwin,
Third, Mrs. Jane E. Molen, Fourth, Mrs. E. J.
Jushaw Fifth, and Mrs. Julia Petty Sixth
Vice Pres. Mrs. Sarah Farr, Secretary, Miss
Mary Ann Thane Cor. Sec., Mrs. Marie
Thatcher,Treasurer. An Executive Committee
was then appointed. Mrs. Clara McAlister
Chairman, Mrs. Elizabeth Haines, Mrs.
Armenia Parry, Mrs. Martha Tarbett and Miss
Maggie Sloan as members of said committee.
78 names of ladies enrolled. Benediction by
Bishop Lewis.
