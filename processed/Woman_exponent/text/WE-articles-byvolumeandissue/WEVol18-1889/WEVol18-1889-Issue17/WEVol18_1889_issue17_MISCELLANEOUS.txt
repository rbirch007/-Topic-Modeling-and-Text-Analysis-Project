MISCELLANEOUS.

	God’s mills grind slow but sure.—George
Herbert.

	When Greeks joined Greeks,then was the tug
of war—Nathaniel Lee.

	Life is not so short but that there is always
time enough for courtsey.—Emerson.

	Act well at the moment and you will have
performed a good action to all eternity.—
Lavater.

	Those who live on vanity must not un-
reasonably, expect to die of mortification.—
Mrs. Ellis.

	Nature loves truth so well that it hardly
ever admits of flourishing.  Conceit is to nature
what paint is to beauty— it is not only need-
less,but impairs what it would improve.—Pope. 

	Compliments of congratulation are always
kindly taken, and cost one nothing but pen,
ink, and paper.  I consider them as draughts
upon good breeding, where the exchange is al-
ways greatly in favor of the drawer.—
Chesterfield.

	Conscience is too great a power in the nature
of man to be altogether subdued; it may for a
time be repressed and kept dormant, but con-
jectures there are in human life which awaken
it; and when once re-awakened it flashes on the
sinner's mind with all the horrors of an in-
visible ruler and a future judgment.—Blair.
