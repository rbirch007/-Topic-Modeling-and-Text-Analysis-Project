MARICOPA STAKE.	
	The Quarterly Conference of the Maricopa
Stake was held at Lehi, Dec. 18th, 1889.
Pres. Sarah M. Pomeroy presiding, her Coun-
selors were also present with, the President of
the several branches. Of the brethren, C. I. 
Robson, Stake Pres., Coun. H. C. Rogers, with
Charles Allen and others. The opening ex-
ercises were followed by verbal reports of the 
several branches by their respective Presidents;
showing that all were feeling interested in their
work and a general feeling of unity and faith-
fulness prevailed.
	Pres. S. M. Pomeroy and Coun’s. in turn ad
dressed the conference, and gave wise and
timely advice.
	Coun. H. C. Rogers said the remarks of the
sisters had called to his mind ideas which he
had never before thought of. It seemed
strange that we should be forgetful and care-
less of duties when such blessings were in store
for the faithful; but thought forgetfulness was
planted in our hearts that we might have it to
overcome.  Said, this was one of the waste
places of Zion, was flattered that he had the
privilege of assisting to build it up.
	Brother Allen felt a cheering influence in
our meeting, encouraged the saints to live
faithful.
	Brother Brimhall said our reward would be 
according to our diligence.  "Without the
spirit of the Lord we can do nothing, but with
it our yoke is easy and burdens light.” 
	Sister Valenzuela (a Pima sister) spoke in
her native language, Sister Susan Savage in-
terpreted. She bore a faithful testimony to
the good spirit working in the hearts of her
people.	
	Pres. C. I. Robson spoke briefly, gave some
instructions regarding the general fast day,
quoted from Paul on the subject. Said the
saints had been more faithful under trying
circumstances than when peace and prosperity
reigned.
	Closing remarks by S. M. Pomeroy, ad-
journed for three months.
					  F. E. Robson, Sec.
	 Mesa, Jan. 6, 1890.
