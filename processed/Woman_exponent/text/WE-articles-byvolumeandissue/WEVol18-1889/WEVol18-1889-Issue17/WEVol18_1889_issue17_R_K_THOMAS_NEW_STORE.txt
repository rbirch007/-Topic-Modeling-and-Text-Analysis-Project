R. K. THOMAS NEW STORE.

	R. K. Thomas the popular dry-goods merchant,
has removed from the Eagle Emporium building
to the new five story Culmer block 26, 28, 30, and
32 East, First South Street,where he has fifty feet
front of plate glass, and the room is not only well
lighted and elegant in appearance, but is fitted
up in the most commodious and handsome style,
with modern improvements in the way of arrang-
ing and displaying goods of all descriptions, that
are sold at retail in a dry-goods house; silk,
dress goods in great varieties, white goods of all
kinds, embroideries, laces, hosiery, gloves, muslin
underwear, shoes, slippers, ladies, childrens' and
men's furnishings, boys' clothing and fancy goods.
The basement is the same size as the salesroom
above, and fitted up conveniently for the sale of
heavier goods of various kinds such as carpets
rugs, etc, and is quite light enough for showing
the goods to advantage, in fact as light as many
stores. Mr. Thomas himself is certainly one of


the most genial and affable men in business and
that has much to do with his success. He has
had a large experience in the mercantile line hav-
ing been buyer in New York and Eastern markets
for a large and wealthy establishment in this City
for fifteen years. He is bound to succeed if push,
energy and enterprise coupled with honesy, af-
fability and those fine qualities of head and
heart that are recognized everywhere as the true
mettle.
