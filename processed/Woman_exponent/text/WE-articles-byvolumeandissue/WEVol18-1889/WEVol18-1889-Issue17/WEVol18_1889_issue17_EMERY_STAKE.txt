EMERY STAKE.
	The Relief Society Semi Annual
Conference of the Emery Stake was held at
Castle Hale, Oct. 18th, 1889. Two meetings
were held during the day, Sister Elizabeth
Howard, from Salt Lake City was present and
the Stake Presidency of the Relief Society
were on the stand, and representatives from
the different wards with a goodly number of
sisters filled the house.
	Sister Anne U. Larsen presided in the meet-
ings and gave a report of the Stake; as far as
she was acquainted with the situation it was
favorable.
	Presidents of the several wards gave reports
land it was learned that the “labor of
love is increasing and the poor and missionaries
are first and most considered and the little
mites are gladly given to forward the cause
of  “Relief.”
	Sister Howard occupied most of the time in
the afternoon and touched briefly but pointedly

[Column 2]

on prayer, going to meetings, Word of Wis-
dom, reading good books, keeping the Sabbath
day holy, to observe the Fastday, to preserve
health and shun the vain and foolish
fashions of to day; how to partake of the Sacra-
ment, keep away our children from sectarian
schools, to learn our boys a trade, advocated
woman suffrage and recommended the golden
rule of the Savior: “Love one another.” Pres.
C. G. Larsen and Counselor Wm. Howard
were also present, and gave some very good
counsel and encouraged the sisters not to be
weary in the work of charity. Beside the fol-
lowing sisters were the most prominents peakers:
Anne Pulsipher, Josie Childs, Anne U. Lar-
sen, and several others. The meetings were
well attended, the speeches spirited and all
agreed that they had enjoyed another feast in
our "lovely mountain home." 
		Kathinka Andersen, Asst. Sec.
