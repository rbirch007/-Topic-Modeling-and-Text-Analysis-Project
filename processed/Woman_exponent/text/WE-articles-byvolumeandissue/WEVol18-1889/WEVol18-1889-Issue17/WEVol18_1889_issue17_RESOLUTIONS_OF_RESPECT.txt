RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.

	To the memory of Mrs. Mary Jane Tanner, who de-
parted this life Jan. 8th, 1890.
	Sister M. J Tanner has been Pres. of the Relief Society
of the 3rd Ward Provo City since its organization in May
1868 being over 21 years.  She was recently sustained by
unanimous vote Pres. of Utah Co. Woman Suffrage Asso-
ciation which was organized Oct. 23, 1889.
	Whereas our Father in Heaven in His Divine wisdom,
and Providence, has seen fit to remove from our midst, a
good and noble woman, a useful and honored sister, a
mother in Israel and champion of woman's rights who has
endured many trials, and spent her life in His Church and
Kingdom on earth, and has gone to join her friends behind
the veil, —
	Whereas we the women of the W. S. A. of Utah Co.,
and of the 3rd Ward R. S. Provo City, mourn her loss in
these associations and her ability amongst her friends and
acquaintances,—
	Resolved that we recognize the infinite mercies of God
and feel that He doeth all things well, and that we hold in
remembrance her virtues and good qualities, in discharging
her duties and we deeply recognize her loss.
	Resolved that we the members of the W. S. A. and of
the R. S. Provo City sympathize with the husband and
family in their bereavement in the loss of a kind wife and
mother.
	And that a copy of these resolutions be placed on the
records of these Associations, and sent to the WOMAN'S
EXPONENT and the T. and D. Enquirer for publication.
						MARY JOHN,
						MARRILA DANIELS,
						ELECTA BULLOCK,
						CAROLINE PRATT,
									Committee.
