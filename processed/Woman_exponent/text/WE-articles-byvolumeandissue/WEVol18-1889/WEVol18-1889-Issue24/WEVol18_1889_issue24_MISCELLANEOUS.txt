MISCELLANEOUS.

	Duties are ours, events are God's.—Cecil.
	They never fall who die in a great cause.—
Byron.
	When joy's day is over faith shines through
the arctic skies like a midnight sun.
	Try to frequent the company of your betters
in books and life. That is the most wholesome
society.
	It is always good to know, if only in pass-
ing, a charming human being; it refreshes
one like flowers and woods and clear brooks.
—George Eliot.
	Hope nothing from luck, and the probability
is that you will be so prepared, forewarned,
and fore-armed, that all shallow observers
will call you lucky.—Bulwer Lytton.
	Great talents for conversation should be
attended with politeness. He who eclipses
others owes them great civilities; and what-
ever a mistaken vanity may tell us, it is better
to please in couversation than to shine in it.
	I have drunk deeply of the draught of
science; I have loved it with a passionate love,
and now I am becoming an old man; and I can
say that every discovery of science has bronght
me more nearly face to face with the love of
God.—Charles Kingsley.
