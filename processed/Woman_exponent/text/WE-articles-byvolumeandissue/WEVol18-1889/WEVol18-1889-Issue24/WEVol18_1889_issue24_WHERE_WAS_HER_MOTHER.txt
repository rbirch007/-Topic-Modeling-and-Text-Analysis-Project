WHERE WAS HER MOTHER?

	Edwin Booth has told how he lately took
occasion to reprove some silly young women
who were seeking his acquaintance, and now
George Riddle has a similar story to tell. A
gushing young girl in Chicago, a stranger to
Mr. Riddle, kept writing to him, begging an
interview. Mr. Riddle paid no attention to
her notes, till finally she called on him and
sent up her autograph album, with the request
that he would at least give her his autograph,
with a favorite sentiment. Mr. Riddle there-
upon gave her this,from "Romeo and Juliet:"

	Your love says, like an honest gentleman,
	Where-s your mother?

		—Boston Herald.
