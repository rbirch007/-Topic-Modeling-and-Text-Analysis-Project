NOTES AND NEWS.

	Pundita Ramabai is lecturing to crowded
audiences in Bombay on the education of
women.
	Boston University has graduated 478
doctors of medicine, nearly one-half of whom
are women.
	A constitutional convention for New Mexico,
with a view to Statehood, is to be held next
September.
	Louise Imogen Guiney, who is an oarswoman
as well as a poet, has a paper on "Rowing” in
the August Wide Awake.
	The Supreme Court of Michigan has just
confirmed the decision of the lower courts, that
in Michigan women may vote for school
officers.
	Memorial services in honor of Mrs Lucy
Webb Hayes were held last Sunday by the
local W. C. T. U.’s, by the request of Miss
Francis E. Willard.
	John G. Whittier writes: “I earnestly hope
the new-created States of the West will lay
their foundations on the rock of justice and
equality, and thus secure to their people the
full rights of citizenship irrespective of sex or
color.
