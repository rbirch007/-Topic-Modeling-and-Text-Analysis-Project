DEAR EDITOR:
     Thinking perhaps a few lines from this little place
will be acceptable, I take the liberty to write you
of a pleasant surprise and farewell party which
was given on July 17th to our highly esteemed
Bro. Hans M. Hansen, who departed on the 20th
on a mission to his native land, Norway.  He is
first Counselor to our Bishop.
     The Bishop and Bro Hansen went out for a ride,
and to call on some friends, during the afternoon.
At three o'clock the people of the Fremont, and
Loa Wards, gathered at the Fremont School
house, where tables were spread with the good
things of the earth.  At four P. M. the Bishop
came back, and seeing the crowd assembled drove
up to the School house, when several of the men
took Bro. Hansen from the buggy and carried
him into the house.  After his surprise and the
excitement of the moment was over, the choir
sang, "Adieu my dear brethren adieu."  Prayer
was then offered, and all partook of the boun-
teous repast. 
     The evening was spent in dancing intermingled


with songs, recitations etc, which had been pre-
pared by the people for the occasion.
	At a little after midnight benediction was pro-
nounced, all feeling to join in thanking our
Heavenly Father for His blessings, and asking
His divine care and blessings on our brother and
his family during his absence.
	Tickets for the party were isued at 50cts. each,
the proceeds of which with contributions from the
people made up a purse of over $80,00 to assis
Bro. Hansen. 
	The Lord is blessing this portion of His little
flock with peace and prosperity.
	I am an interested reader of your very valuable
little paper, and ever pray for the welfare of Zion.
									DELTA.
Fremont, July, 1889.
