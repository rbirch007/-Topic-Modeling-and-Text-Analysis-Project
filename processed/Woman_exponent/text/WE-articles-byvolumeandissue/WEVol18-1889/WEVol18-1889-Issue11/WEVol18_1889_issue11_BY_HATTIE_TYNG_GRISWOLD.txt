BY HATTIE TYNG GRISWOLD.

"O will the right not triumph at the last?"
We cry, as sick at heart we view earth's plain,
Where love and hate have grappled once again,
Renewed the ceaseless warfare of the past,
The unending struggle, still so fierce, so vast,
And still for answer comes the impassioned strain:
“When first the Universe sprang from God's brain,
And forth into the formless void was cast,
He said that it was good; and so to day,
In firmest faith we answer give once more:
The right must triumph, death and hell must die,
Though proud they stretch to clutch the sky away
Wherein the bow of promise, as of yore,
Proclaims the surety given by God Most High."
		Ex.
