BIRTHDAY GREETING AND RESPONSE. 

	To Brother William Willes, on his 75th Birthday, July
5th, 1889. written in Fast Meeting, while listening to
Brother Willes remarks.

	On this thy Birthday, Brother Dear, 
		I wish a wish for thee, 
	Prompted by feelings most sincere
		Of kindred sympathy.  

	Fasting not feasting, we today 
		In every temporal sense:
	Yet, the good Spirit, while we pray
		Gives more than recompense.  
			
	To listen to thy earnest tone, 
		To watch thy silvery hair. 
	Gives pleasure, to the world unknown, 
		And calls forth fervent prayer

	For, from thy lips proceedeth free
		Words of Eternal Truth; 
	In thee a man of God we see, 
		Faithful, e'en from thy youth. 

	The wish, the prayer I breathe for thee, 
		Dear father, brother, friend, 
	Is, that thy days may many be,
		And prosperous to the end.
			
	Thy little grandchild here today,
		A gift of choicest worth,
	Thou blessedst-many more I pray
		Such gifts be thine on Earth, 

	And in that world of peace and bliss
		Of which thou speakest well. 
	May we, in meetings such as this 
		God's power and mercies tell. 
			L. L. G. Richards. 

	Reply to Sister L. L. G. Richards' Good wishes, on
my 75th Birthday, July 5th 1889.

	Tis true, I'm seventy-five today
		I freely must confess;
	And with my heart and soul do say
		No language can express
	The gratitude I feel to Him
		Who lengthens out my days
	That I am healthy, bright and trim
		For which I render praise.

	But, most of all, my thanks are due
		To know the Gospel plan.
	And hope to stand the journey through
		And prove myself a man.
	And never falter; turn aside,
		Or e'en unworthy prove,
	By envy, lust, or worldly pride;
		Keeping my thoughts above.

	The kind regards your verse reveals
		Doth cheer and comfort me,
	And firm and strong our friendship seals
		And sets my soul quite free.
	Should those good wishes you portray
		By me be realized
	They represent a bright array
		By me most highly prized.

	I now reciprocate the same,
		The wishes you express;
	That, every blessing I can name
		For you to cheer and bless.
	And may the honor you bestow
		Upon my hoary head,
	In streams of blessings ever flow,
		Profusely on you shed,
			Wm. Willes.
