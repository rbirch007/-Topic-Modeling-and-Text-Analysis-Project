“JOHNY IS PACKING HIS TRUNK.”
TO MARY.
“Johny is packing his trunk as I write”—
Why comes this thrill to my being’s core, 
Why do I read it o’er and o’er,
And why do the tear drops start ?
“Oh I could weep for my first born goes,
Out from the old Home nest tonight.
My God, may their future lives be blest—”
What do I see as your letter I hold.
What does the past to my vision unfold ?
A sweet faced babe in my arms I clasp,
And gaze upon him in joy enrapt,
He soon gained a place in every heart
And grew of that household band a part,
like a ray of light from babe to man
He brought no Sorrow in all the span,
The home was brighter whene’er he came,
Oh will it ever be the Same?
Free of heart and open of hand
He won him friends throughout the land,
And the Bride who goes forth from her old home too
Where a cherished one she lived and grew,
To share with our boy on life’s ocean wide,
The Bark that must weather wind and tide,
To build a New Home where others may dwell,
Where that “Song of the heart,” may in melody swell.
Here in this far off land I raise,
A deep thanksgiving prayer of praise,
To God who kept him through the day,
When evil snares beset his way;
And may His power around him shine,
’Til home and love grow all divine.
Home should be the spot the nearest heaven,
And home ties the dearest our God hath given;
An altar of love in the Desert Wild,
A rest for earth’s weary toil-stained child;
Oh, sacred the light of the Old Home burns.
And often the Memory backward turns,
To seek a balm for some hidden pain
And gather strength for life’s work again.
God bless the Old Homes and the New,
And help to make them pure and true,
A haven sweet of peace and rest,
Of every spot on earth the best.
	S. E. RUSSEL.
Mesa Sept. 1, 1889.
