PRAYERFULNESS.
	We are bidden to pray without ceasing and
to continue constant in prayer.  Paul prayed
for “Timothy without ceasing night or day.”
Now how is it possible to pray without ceasing?
We must have three conditions; first, we must
acknowledge a want, and then we must ac-
knowlege God’s power to supply that want,
then comes the appeal to Him to put forth that
power.
	When we have fully learned the lessons of
our countless wants, the effect will pass into
our very being, and will make us gentle to
others and give a certain childlike meekness to
the whole temper.  Then we must have the
knowledge and belief in God’s power to supply
this; and who will dare to deny that this power
can go beyond any thing we conceive, or
feel.
	Ambition, shame, selfishness, or love, can lay
hold of the mind with a firm, unshaken grasp,
and yet you will refuse to believe in prayerful-
ness.
	When some unpleasant, or unholy thought
steals across us, and something we cannot
banish, try a fervent, though it may be unspoken
a prayer; send to our Father in heaven, one
swift but earnest appeal, and we shall find this
unhallowed thought will trouble us no more.
