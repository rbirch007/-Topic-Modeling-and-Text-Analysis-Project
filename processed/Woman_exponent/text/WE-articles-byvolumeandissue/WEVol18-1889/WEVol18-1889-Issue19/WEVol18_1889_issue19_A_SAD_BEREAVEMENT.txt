A SAD BEREAVEMENT.

	It is with deep regret that I tender you the sad news of
three sweet little members of our Primary Association,
whom, our Heavenly Father has seen fit to call into His
presence.  The dreaded diphtheria which quarantined
many homes here for two months past, was the cause of
their demise, Dec. 23rd, 1889 Sanford B. Lewis a lovely
boy in his tenth year and only son of Sister Martha Ann
Lewis—January 9th 1890 also his younger sister Amy in
her eighth year.  Also January 21st, 1890, Joseph F.
Skidmore seven years old, son of our good, kind Bishop.
The parents and families have the sympathy of the entire
ward, while our feelings of tenderness as an Association
cannot be expressed in words.


The dearest, faithful little members,
		Will be missed in all our meetings,
	They make less, our happy numbers
		Who used to meet in fondly greetings.
	They are gone but hope is left us
		Of meeting them all again,
	For 'twas God who did bereave us
		He gives the hope, we'll e'er maintain.
					MARY ALLSOP, Pres.
					HARRIET LAWRENCE, Sec.
Primary Association, Richmond.
