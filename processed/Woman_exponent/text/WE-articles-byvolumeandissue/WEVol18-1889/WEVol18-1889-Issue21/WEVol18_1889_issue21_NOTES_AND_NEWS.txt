NOTES AND NEWS.
	Mrs. Elizabeth Cady Stanton has an article
on the divorce question in the current number
of the Arena.
	A bill giving school suffrage to women
been favorably reported in the Iowa House of
Representatives.			
	The Duchess of Albany, widow of Prince
Leopold, has just received her diploma as a
hospital nurse, after taking the regular course
and standing the usual examination.
	WHAT a sad commentary on the boasted 
chivalry of men was the dying statement of
Mrs. Grace C. Henderson. Three months
search for honest work—plenty of dishonorable
opportunities—then despair and suicide.
	The Women’s Exchange of San Francisco,
Cal., have created a building fund of $10,000
by which they propose to erect a building for
the work of the Exchange, and for a Reading,
Lecture and Waiting Room for women and
their interests.
	Seventy-four woman suffrage petitions have
been sent in to the Massachusetts Legislature
this year, signed by 2,908 women and 1,603
men; in all 4,511 citizens of the State over
twenty-one years of age, and residing in fifty-
three cities and towns.
	An interesting feature in the Jewish Messen-
ger, of March 7, is a symposium on the train-
ing of women, in which a number of thought-
ful American Jewesses take part. They plead
for the broadest and most thorough culture—
religious, intellectual and physical.
	STATES which desire a representation of
women on the World’s Fair Commission
should immediately, by organizations and by
individuals, petition the President to so appoint
Chicago, which promised such generous recog-
nition to women, should now speak with no un-
certain sound. From Wyoming, the Dakotas
and Kansas all such acts of chivalry and jus-
tice are expected, and any state would honor
its history by demanding that one of its commis-
ioners should be a women.
