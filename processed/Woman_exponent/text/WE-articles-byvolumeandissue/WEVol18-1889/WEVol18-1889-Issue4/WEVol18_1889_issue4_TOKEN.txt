	Shall never come message or token,
		Ne'er a sound to break silence dread—
	Nor one tender word breathed or spoken,
		Assuring that love is not dead?

	Towards the far away snow-capped mountains
		And over its shadowly vale,
	Goes forth the sad cry, but the echo
		Weird, mournful, returns on the gale.

	I wait in the gloaming of eve’ing,
		Her bright banners fade into hlue.
	I question, can love that was golden
		Fade and die from heart leal and true?

	Ah! somewhere the sun must be shining,
		Somewhere, tho’ now hidden from sight,
	And tho' we see not through the darkness,
		We know that beyond there is light.

	In hope, we'll wait on thro' the gloaming,
		Tho' no voice replies as of yore;
	We know there must come a morning
		Where love reigns supreme evermore,
