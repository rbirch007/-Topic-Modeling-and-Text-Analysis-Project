NOTES AND NEWS.

	Florence Nightingale has written a letter to
the Johnstown sufferers.
	“Universal Suffrage,” said Charles Sumer,
“is the first proof and only basis of a genuine
republic.”
	The woman suffragists have scored a victory
in the Washington Convention in the election
of Judge Hoyt president, and Dr. John R.
Thompson, chaplain.
	The daughter of the Chief Justice of the
United States is to enter a law office and study
for the legal profession. She has just gradu-
ated from college.
	Mrs. Oliphant, the novelist, is sixty-three
years old and a hard worker, with no desire for
newspaper fame. She lives with her two
children near Windsor castle.
