THE EDITRESS OF EXPONENT:
	From the perusal of the two last numbers of
your paper I perceive one of your main objects is
to secure to women in general, and the Mormon
women in particular, those Rights which belong
to them, equally with men, politically and socially.
	Allow me to aid you in this matter, by drawing
attention to one of the most important rights con-
ferred on women by Nature, which cannot be
wrested away from them, but which has not been
sufficiently recognized, or acted upon by the sex
hitherto, although there are the possibilities within
its influence to obtain all, and more than all that
you can desire.
	Nearly every one has heard the old saying
“That every clever man that lives, must have had
a clever mother, or, the inference to be drawn is,
that he would not have won distinction by his in-
tellectual powers.
	This is a blind sort of way in recognizing a
truth which has had a mighty influence in the past,
and which will have a much stronger power in the
future, when the Maternal Influence of each
mother on the destinies of her child’s life, is fully
understood and acted upon.
	It is a well known fact in Physiology, and one
that has been acted upon by stockbreeders for
generations, "That the father gives the body, and
the mother gives the mind,” yet human beings
ignore that knowledge in the propagation of their
oun race, which they find acts so advantageously
in the animal kingdom generally.  Many men
have perceived this power which lies inherent in
women, ready for immense possibilities when
knowledge sets it moving, and which acts
naturally now, though not intelligently with every
female.  It requires a Lady Teacher like yourself
to present the subject to your readers in such
language, and in such a manner, as only ladies
can use, for these subjects have been so long
under the ban of prejudice that it would be con-
sidered highly indelicate for women to hear them
promulgated by any one of the opposite sex.
	But it may be stated in general terms without
being offensive that every child is indebted to its
father for its body, and constitution, or vitality and
those faculties which lie at the basilian, or lower
part of the brain, while the mother contributes the
upper part of the brain, embracing the moral, re-
ligious, and intellectual part of the children’s
future, and this principally during the last half of
gestation.
	When this fact is thoroughly understood and
acted upon, you can well understand how children
can be so moulded before birth, that there need be
no contest for Rights, either female or male, for
every mother can bequeath such conscientious-
ness to her child, that every right would be con-
ferred or granted without solicitation simply be-
cause wrong would be impossible to its nature.
	As the mother’s mind is during pregnancy,
whether operated upon by pleasant thoughts and
surroundings or the reverse, so will the child be,
if fear predominates the child will inherit it, if
ambition, pride, or egotism, it will be transmitted
to the child, the same as regards honesty in word
and deed, generosity, imagination, hope, or any
other of the faculties in the coronal region and
reflectives.
