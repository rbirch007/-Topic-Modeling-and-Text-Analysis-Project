OBITUARIES.

	Sister Dina Rollins was born July 26th, 1816, in
London, England, where she became a member
of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.
She emigrated to Utah in 1852, and soon after
settled in Mound Fort, where she remained al-
most continuously until her decease.
	After years of faithful labor as a teacher she
was appointed on Jan. 9th, 1882, First Counselor
to the President of the Mound Fort Relief Society
and faithfully magnified that calling, always being
on hand to strengthen the weak and administer to
the sick and needy.
	Declining health deprived the Society of her
esteemed labors of love, and feeling unwilling to
occupy a position she could not magnify, she
tendered her resignation which was accepted with
regret on Dec. 28th, 1890.
	After a lingering and painful illness she died
May 28th, 1892, a faithful and true Latter-day
Saint in the hope of a glorious resurrection.
	Two surviving children and many grand chil-
dren, together with a large circle of relatives and
friends mourn her loss. May she rest in
peace.
			ANNIE D. TAYLOR.
	MOUND FORT, June 19th, 1892.
