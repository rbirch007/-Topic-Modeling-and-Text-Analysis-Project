NOTES AND NEWS.

	THE Daughters of the American Revolu-
tion in Washington will have a portrait of
Mrs. Harrison painted for the White House
Gallery.
	THE political superiors of women in New
York are reported as registering some of
them eighteen times, some of them even
forty times apiece.—Ex.
	THE Woman's Century is the name of a
new weekly paper to be issued in Weimar,
Germany, in recognition and furtherance of
the progress of women in that country.


THE XXVth Annual Meeting of the
National American Woman Suffrage Associ-
ation will be held in Washington, D. C. in
Metzerott's Music Hall, January 15, 16, 17,
18, 19, 1893.
	REV. Anna Howard Shaw, Vice President
-at-Large of the National American Woman
Suffrage Association, was appointed Frater-
nal Delegate to the National Woman's
Christian Temperance Union Convention
which convened in Denver, Colorado, Oc-
tober 28th, to November 2nd.
	Various articles in the public press indi
cate a misconception of the work of the
Woman Suffrage in the country.  A num-
ber of newspapers speak of "The Woman
Suffrage Candidate for the Presidency of
the United States."
	The National Association of the Woman
Suffrage Workers, the National American
Woman Suffrage Association, though its
work is of a political nature, is not a politi-
cal party and has not nominated any candi-
date for the presidency.
			RACHEL FOSTER AVERY.
				Cor. Sec. N. A. W. S. A.
