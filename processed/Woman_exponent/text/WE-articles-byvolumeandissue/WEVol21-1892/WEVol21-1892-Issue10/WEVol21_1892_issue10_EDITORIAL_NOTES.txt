EDITORIAL NOTES.

	THE ladies’ department of the World’s
Fair in Utah County held quite a stirring
meeting in Provo a week or so ago at which
very able and eloquent speeches were made
and fine music rendered. Miss Thurman
we are told was glowingly eloquent, and
Miss Lillian Roberts sang patriotic songs in
patrioic voice and style.
	WE must beg the indulgence of our
readers for this once in not being able to
continue the story, “In Rural England,” on
account of so much space being used for the
Mass Meeting and other current topics.
We have also other matter on hand that had
to be deferred and again we have crowded
out the minutes of the General Relief
Society Conference.
	VERMONT women are rejoicing over the
passage of a bill in the Legislatnre giving
the women of that State numicipal suf-
frage; the wives of the most prominent men
in the State are giving receptions and enter-
taining in consequence, and altogether
jubilant over such a success and no won-
der, indeed it is a victory considering the
conservatism of New England.
	IMPRESSIVE memorial services were held
in the Tabernacle in this City on Sunday


Nov. 13, for David Hoagland Cannon who
died Oct. 17. while on his mission in Ger-
many, and also for all the Elders  who
have died while  absent  upon missions.
The Tabernacle was beautifully draped in
white suggestively emblematic of the
purity of life of the young brother whose
body lay there in the elegant metallic cas-
ket in which his remains were brought
across the sea and the continent,for interment
beside his mother and with the Cannon
family.
