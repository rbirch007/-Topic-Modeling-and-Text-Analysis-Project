							M. A. SMART.

	On the first of Oct. 1892 we were called upon
to mourn the loss of a dearly beloved sister and
member of our Relief Society of Samaria Idaho.
Sister Jane Price, was born in the Parish of
Handilorvan, Brecon Shire, South Wales, March
1822 and baptised into the Church of Jesus Christ 
of L. D. S. in the year 1850; was the wife of John
Price and emigrated to this place in June, 1874,
with her husband and one son.  In her native
country her home was ever open to the Elders on
missions, and many will remember the kind treat-
ment received from her hands. Being of a
sympathetic nature she was much beloved by all
who knew her.  For a number of years she has
been second Counselor to the President of the
Relief Society which position she honorably filled.
At her funeral the R. S. followed the remains to 
the meeting house, from which place it was fol-
lowed, after services, to the grave yard by numer-
ous friends and relatives from all parts of the
valley, there to sleep in peace until the resur-
rection day.
			MARY D. DAVIS,
				Pres. of the R. S. of Samaria.
