			WOMEN.

	At the business meeting of the National
Council of Women of the United States,
held in Chicago in May, the resignation of
the Vice-President, Mrs. Ella Dietz Clymer,
was presented and accepted with expres-
sions of regret. Mrs. Clymer’s condition
of health made it imperative that she should
accede to the advice, of her physician, and
resign all official positions in the various
organizations with which she was con-
nected.
	Miss Clara Conway, of Memphis, Tenn.,
was unanimously elected to the vacant of-
fice. Miss Conway has accepted the posi-
tion and looks forward to active participa-
tion in the work of the Council, in which
she is deeply interested.
