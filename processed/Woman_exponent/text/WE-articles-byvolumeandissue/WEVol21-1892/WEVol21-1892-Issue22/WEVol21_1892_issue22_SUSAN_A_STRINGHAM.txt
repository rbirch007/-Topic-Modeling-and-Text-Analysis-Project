				SUSAN A. STRINGHAM.

	MISS MARGARET WINDEYER has been
appointed by the government of New South
Wales as a commissioner to the World’s Fair.
She is the daughter of Sir William Windeyer,
the senior Puisne Judge of the Supreme
Court of New South Wales, and will
represent the colony of which her father is
so distinguished a citizen.
	LADY HENRY SQMERSET has been re-
elected president oi the British Women’s
Temperance Association by a majority
of sixty-nine. There had been a strong dif-
ference of opinion on the matter,  Lady
Henry being regarded by the conservative
element as too radical on some subjects,
especially on woman suffrage. Her re-
election is a triumph for the progressive
party. The defeated minority withdrew
from the meeting.
	THE old Liberty Bell was greeted with
cheers all along the way on its route to
Chicago. When it arrived, the great crowd
cheered itself hoarse. As we read this
account we wondered whether the men who
cheered for the deeds for which this old
bell rang would themselves uphold the
claims of justice for women, who are dis-
franchised just as the colonists were, and
do it so nobly that for centuries the world
will give them glad welcome? We hope
so.
