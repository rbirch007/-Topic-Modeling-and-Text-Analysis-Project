[CONCLUDED.]
	ON the Monday after we commenced our home-
ward journey, our company was enlarged by
several brethren and sisters going to Conference
at Moab, Mrs. Freeze and I got into Brother Wm.
Halls wagon with him and his interesting and in-
telligent daughter Hannah. We traveled a beau-
tiful and excellent road, passed Montezuma
Valley with the City of Cortez in the distance,
also saw the Chuck a Luck Mountains in Arizona,
crossed a good bridge over the long deep and ex-
pensive canal made to take water to Cortez from
the river Dolores. We traveled a long distance,
and camped when quite dark for the night, which
was cloudy and every appearance of rain; our
beds were made by fire light; as we could not see
any distance so I wondered what sort of a place
it was, we slept well and comfortable, on awak-
ing in the morning I saw an immense flock of
crows pass over our beds and said to Mrs. Freeze
we are going to have no rain the crows are flying
very high. On getting on my elbow and looking
around I was delighted with the sight of the field
of beautiful flowers on which we had slept, there
was nothing else in view, I pulled a bunch which
I keep for a relic from under our wagon cover
and mattress and I admired the place as a verit
able "Field of Ardath."
