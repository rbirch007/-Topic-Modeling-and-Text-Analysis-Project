RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.
	Resolutions of Respect to the memory of
Lydia C. Snyder, who departed this life March
30, 1892, aged 70 years, 8 months, and 23 days, at
the 4th Provo ward, Provo City, Utah.
	Whereas it pleased our Heavenly in his alwise
mercy to call from this sphere of action Sister
Lydia C. Snyder who for many years has been an
officer and a member of this association. There-
fore,
	Resolved that in her demise we have lost the
valuable association of an honest and successful
laborer in our noble cause of Relief Society work.
	Resolved, that her example is worthy of imita-
tion by all good women.
	Resolved that we offer our sympathy to her be-
reaved children with the full assurance however
that ours and their loss is less grievous from the
full faith we have in her present happiness and
glorious future through her faith and works and 
the mercies of our Savior.
	Resolved that a copy of these resolutions be
printed upon our minutes, a copy presented to


her family and be published in the Provo paper
and in the EXPONENT.
			M. M. WATSON,
			HARRIET BEAN,
			D. I. BOOTH,
