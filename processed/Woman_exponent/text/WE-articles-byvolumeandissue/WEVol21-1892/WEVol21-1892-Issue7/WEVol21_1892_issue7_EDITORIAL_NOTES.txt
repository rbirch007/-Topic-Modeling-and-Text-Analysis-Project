EDITORIAL NOTES.

	THERE will very likely be a meeting called of
the Utah Woman’s Press Club while Conference
visitors are in the City.
	WE must apologize for not giving in this issue
the article on mines and disturbances in Northern
Idaho, particularly describing our interesting
visit to the Gem mine. There are so many local


affairs to be mentioned that things of less im-
portance must wait.

	THE Territorial Fair to be held in this City the
present month promises to be the most varied in
kind and mammoth in proportion of any that has
preceded it. It ought to be in view of the repre-
sentation expected for the World’s Fair in ’93.
Certainly the management is in good hands and
the people are right in expecting the best results
of the enterprise. It opens on Tuesday October
4th.
	TME W. S. A. Ter. Convention will be held in
the 14th Ward Assembly Rooms in this City
Wednesday Oct. 5,—begining with an Executive
session in the morning. Delegates are expected
from all parts of the Territory and the President
Mrs. S. M. Kimball and an Ex. Com. are prepar-
ing an attractive programme of exercises. In the
evening a reception for the Association delegates
and friends will be held at the residence of Mrs.
Phebe Y. Beattie, Ch. Ex. Com., at 55 N. West
Temple—elaborate preparations are being made
and suitable decorations will be a feature of the
reception.
