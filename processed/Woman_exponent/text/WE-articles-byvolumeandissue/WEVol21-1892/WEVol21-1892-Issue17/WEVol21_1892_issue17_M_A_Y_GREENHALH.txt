				M. A. Y. GREENHALH,


THE Democratic girls of Mt. Holyoke
College seem to have entered into politics
as far as they can. Though in the minority
among the students, they are overflowing
with enthusiasm. They held a grand rally
when Cleveland’s election was declared,
and they held an inaugural banquet and
rally on the evening of March 4th. En-
thusiasm ran high; many toasts were res-
ponded to, and Mrs. Cleveland and Baby
Ruth were not forgotton.
	THE Arizona House of Representatives
has passed a bill giving full suffrage to wo-
men, by a vote of 16 to 7.
