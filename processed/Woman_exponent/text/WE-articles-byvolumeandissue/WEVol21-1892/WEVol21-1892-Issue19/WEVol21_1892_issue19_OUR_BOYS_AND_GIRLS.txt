OUR BOYS AND GIRLS.

	WHAT shall we do with our boys and
girls too, for that matter? is a question that
is agitating the minds of hundreds of par-
ents in Utah today. I mean the poorer
classes. We make every effort and sacrifice
to educate them to some extent, and when
they become fifteen or sixteen years of age,
we need their assistance and it is time that
they should commence to take a part in the
great battle of life. “For life is real, life is
earnest.” Our girls are not all fitted by
nature for household drudgery, though they
should understand house-keeping in all its
details. If we send them to Salt Lake City
to work at anything, our minds know no
peace, for there is every allurement and
fascination to lead them astray. Why
should one dollar lay idle, when there is so
much to be done? Why do not our capital-
ists invest? Why should we import our
gloves, cotton hosiery, ribbons, corsets,
underwear, and hundreds of other articles
too numerous to mention. May the time
speedily come when some enterprising, pub-
lic-spirited, magnanimous men will start
such industries in outside settlements to
afford employment for our young people
that we may keep them at home. We
have everything in Utah to make us self
sustaining, if Utah will work for Utah.
Suppose our capitalists do not amass thous-
ands of dollars, they will have the satis-
faction of knowing that their works will
live long after they have passed to that
bourne from which no traveler returns.
