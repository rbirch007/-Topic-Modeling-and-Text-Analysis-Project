PRIMARY REPORT, COLONIA
		JUAREZ, MEXICO.
MRS. LOUIE FELT:
	DEAR SISTER—Perhaps a few items in
regard to the Primary Associations of the
Mexican Mission will be of interest to you.
There is an association in each of the four
settlements of this Mission, and, judging
from the reports at our recent conference
they are all in a flourishing condition.
	The Mission organization was effected at
our February conference with Sister Ettie
Teasdale, President; Sisters Victoria Pratt,
and Abbie Young, Counsellors.
	Two Primary Conferences have been
held since the organization. The last one
was held on the 26th of August, President
Ettie Teasdale presiding. A good spirit
prevailed and many excellent instructions
were given by the leading sisters; also
Apostle Teasdale and others of the leading
brethren. There was a very fair represen-
tation from all of the associations, each of
which assisted in rendering a programme
prepared for the occasion, consisting of
songs, recitations, etc. The children all
took their parts in a very pleasing manner.
	During conference the primaries held a
fair in the basement of the schoolhouse,
under the management of the president.
	There was a very fine display of Child-
ren’s work, consisting of straw braid, hats,
quilt-blocks, stockings and various kinds of
fancy work, as well as many specimens of
cookery done by the little girls. While the
boys had a very fair showing of fruits and
vegetables, also a few specimens of their
mechanical genius.
	The whole affair was a credit, considering
the short time they had for preparation and
the busy season of the year.
	Ever wishing for the advancement and
welfare of the Primaries throughout
Zion, I remain your sister in the Gospel.
