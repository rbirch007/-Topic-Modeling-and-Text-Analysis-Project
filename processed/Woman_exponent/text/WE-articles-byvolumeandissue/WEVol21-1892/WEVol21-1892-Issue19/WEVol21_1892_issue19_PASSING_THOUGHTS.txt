PASSING THOUGHTS

	THE TEMPLE—The great event has
come—it is passing. Daily thousands of
people pass within the sacred walls, gaze
with wonder and admiration, join with
faithful hearts in the grand inspiring
prayer, hearken to the soul-stirring music
of the choir and the interesting and pro-
phetic words of the Apostles, join in the
hosannahs to God and the Lamb and pass
down the granite steps, without the portals, 
scarcely realizing that hours have passed,
for so rapt are they in the events of the 
moment. During the week of the dedi-
cation I have gazed from my casement
windows towards the great edifice and 
watching the tall graceful towers, like
angels in the blue sky have lived over 
again those precious momentsspent within
the walls.
	Some say that places so beautiful, so
chaste, so sacred are fit dwellings for
angels and that their forms may be seen
and their voices may be heard, so almost 
divine is the feeling that prevades the soul
while enjoying the services in this holy
place.
	They are the angels of love and mercy,
of good will and charity, and in our hearts
alone do we feel their presence. This 
dedication is to the Saints the greatest event
for many years. How long we have
watched the building of the Temple and
as stone has been laid upon stone our faith
and prayers have been offered for the safe
and perfect completion of the building and
now that it is so handsomely completed
well may we feel proud and happy. The
Temple is so dear, so sacred to us all. How 
as a little child I remember the sight of the
great stones one at a time being hauled
along the streets by two yoke of oxen and 
we would all stand for them to pass with a
feeling of awe and reverence, and so in 
later years when the trains brought the
white stones by car loads I always stood
