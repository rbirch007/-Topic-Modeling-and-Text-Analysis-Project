LOVE’S QUESTIONINGS.

IN the valley of the Temple
	Where God's work in beauty gleam,
From the snow capped peak or meadow,
	Rose-blown hedge or rippling stream.

In the shadow of the Temple
	On the hill-side fair to see,
When thy gaze was all enraptured,
	Came sweet tender thoughts of me?

Did you think of nature's lover
	How her eyes would feast the while,
Viewing o'er each scene of beauty
	'Neath the charm of thy sweet smile?

In the music of the Temple
	Swelling sweet with prayer and praise,
Came a thought of joys unmeasured,
	That we knew in other days?

In the glory of the Temple
	Where the angels met with man,
Great and precious truths revealing
	Of the gospel's glorious plan.

When the Holy Ghost in power
	Filled thy soul with perfect peace,
Did you long for me that hour?
	Did your love for me increase?

To my spirit comes the answer,
	In sweet accents soft and low,
Giving me a blest assurance,
	All my trusting heart would know.
