EDITORIAL NOTES.

	A BRIGHT French woman is seeking a
nomination to the Chamber of Deputies.
Of  course her election would not take
place even were she nominated, no more 
than the election of Mrs. Lockwood to the
presidency, but not long since the women of
France scarcely dared speak on political 
matters in fear of banishment and like
a treatment to that of Madam Da Stael and
Madam Recamer, but thanks to the free
institutions of a Republican form of govern-
ment the French Woman's wit and power
is no longer confined to the salon but she 
has many public privileges which she uses
not only for the advancement of her own
self, but for the benefit of all mankind.
Thus step by step woman's sphere is
broadened and her realm will yet embrace
the universe.
	WE feel sure the people of Utah will be
pleased to know that the two pictures sent
by Miss Kate Wells, of Salt Lake City,
were accepted for the Woman's Building.
The Indian Chief colored by her, hangs in a
very conspicuous place in the gallery up-
stairs, at the right hand of the staircase, so
that when you turn to the right it is the
first in the long line of these beautiful pro-
ductions. The colored photographs of the
six girls is not yet placed but will no doubt
be where it can be seen and admired. The
book of poems, ‘‘Songs and Flowers of the
Wasatch," has reached Chicago safely and
Mrs. Margaret Blaine Salisbury, to whom


it is dedicated, will take care that it is where
it will make the best showing.
	It is certainly a very beautiful book, and
Utah may well be proud of the work, both
poetical and artistic. It is excellent in
every respect.
