RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.

	Whereas the Allwise Father has seen fit to re-
move our dear sister, Orelia Hansen Edwards,
from this sphere of action, to a more exalted one
above, therefore be it
	Resolved, that we, her associates in the Relief
Society of the Diaz Ward, do deeply mourn her
loss and that we cherish her memory and emu-
late her many virtues.
	We can truly say that her failings “e’en lean
to virtue’s side.” She manifested that true
charity which flows from a sense of duty, and a
hope in God.
	Resolved, that we sincerely sympathize with
her many friends and colaborers as she was a
faithful laborer in the Manti Temple. Her whole
soul delighted in this work.
	Resolved, that we send a copy of this to the
WOMAN'S EXPONENT, and also place a copy on
our record.
	Farewell dear sister, rest in peace and may the
Lord grant that our future be as bright and hope-
ful as thine.
			LUCY E. JOHNSON, President.
			ANNA JORGENSEN,
			VERONA WHITING,
				Counselors.
			ADA EARL, Secretary.
