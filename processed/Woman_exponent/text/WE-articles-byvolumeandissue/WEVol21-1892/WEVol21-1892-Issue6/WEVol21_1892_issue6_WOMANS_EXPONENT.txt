WOMANS EXPONENT.


MRS. BELLE C. JOHNSON is editor of the
Salem (Ill.) Republican.
	MISS ZELIA NUTTALL, of San Francisco,
is making her mark as an anthropologist.
Just now she is in Dresden, Germany,
where she has a fine collection of books on
Mexico. She is remarkably well-informed
in Aztec history and arts. In the last num-
ber of the “Internationales Archivfur Eth-
nographic" she publishes an illustrated
article upon the feather shields of Mexico.
In a recent visit to Florence, Italy, Miss
Nuttall discovered in the library an Aztec
manuscript with pictures. It turned out to
be a treatise upon dress and ornament, and
contains a text in Spanish. This, reprinted
in facsimile with critical notes and an
English translation, Miss Nuttall will pre-
sent at the next Congress in October.
