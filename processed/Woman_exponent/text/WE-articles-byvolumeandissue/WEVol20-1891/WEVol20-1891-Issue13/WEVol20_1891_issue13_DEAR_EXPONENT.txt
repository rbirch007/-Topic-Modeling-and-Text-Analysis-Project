DEAR EXPONENT:
	I enjoy reading in the various publications
the beautiful sentiments and arguments of our
noble representative women of the nation for
the emancipation of our sex, from gross and de-
grading oppression by the stronger sex who
have so long retained the ascendancy and
arrogated to themselves the right of making
laws inimical to justice and humanity
which these most excellent and benevolent wo-
men are devoting their energies to rescue from
such thraldom so that woman may be placed
on a proper footing and have influence to
check the progress of inebriety and crime.
Instead, as in the past, women who have hus-
bands have had to toil and labor in poverty to
sustain their families while their husbands are
indulging in intemperance.
	On the other hand hosts of women, un-
married, are exposed to temptation and in-
duced to live lives of dishonor and consequent
misery. 

	Is it not true that the larger proportion of
women worthy to be wives of good men and
true have not the opportunity of becoming
wives and honorable mothers

	I would that all good women might receive
the benefit of the sublime teachings of the
martyred Prophet Joseph in regard to woman
whose friend he was and whose sayings I often
recognize emanating from the pen of writers
who have unwittingly adopted some of his
views. 

	My husband was considered worthy to per- 
form a historic mission to Palestine. In 1840
he was sent by the Prophet Joseph, and went
without purse or scrip, to dedicate that land
preparatory to the return of the Jews in ful-
fillment of prophecy and accomplished the
mission to the divine acceptance.
	Most of the Jews will gather in unbelief, and
by the force of circumstances, are compelled
to fulfill the prediction to return to Jerusalem
(laid waste) to the inheritance of their fore-
fathers.
