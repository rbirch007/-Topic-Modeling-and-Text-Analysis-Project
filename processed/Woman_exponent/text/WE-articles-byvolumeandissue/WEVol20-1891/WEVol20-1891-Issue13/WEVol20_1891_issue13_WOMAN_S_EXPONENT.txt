WOMAN'S EXPONENT.


The nine lady managers resident in Chicago,
called together by Mrs. Potter Palmer, have
decided to establish a model sanitary kitchen
in the woman's building at the Exposition.
An effort will be made, by a special entertain-
ment or otherwise, to raise the $4,000 neces-
sary.
	"The Woman's Real Estate Association"
was organized in Indianapolis, Ind., a few
weeks ago, and incorporated with $5,000
capital stock. Its purpose is to deal in real
estate, both as rental agents and as speculators,
and it already has considerable property
placed under its control. The members are
ladies of standing. Mrs. Leon Bailey, who
holds a prominent place in the literary and
musical circles of the city is president of the
association.
