U. W. P. CLUB.

	Minutes of the meeting of the U. W. P. Club
held at the parlors of the WOMAN'S EXPONENT
Wednesday Dec. 30th, 1891, at 8, p. m. Called to
order by the President, E. B. Wells. Minutes
read by the Secretary and accepted. An original
poem from the able pen of Julia A. Mac-
Donald entitled "One Phase of the Divine Mis-
sion of Woman" was then read.
	Miss N. V. Davis of the 14th Dist. school told
a very pleasing Christmas story in a happy man-
her. Gladys Woodmansee rendered some fine
musical selections on the piano and and Miss
Davis sang the German Christmas Hymn and
another German song in a very sweet voice.
Treasurer R. M. Fox sent a letter of regret for
non attendance and an original article relating to
the rights of wives and husbands. Dr. E. R.
Shipp read an original poem entitled "For Those
We Love." The President then read a very in-
teresting article on "Club Culture" and remarked
that we wished to obtain all good ideas for club
work as we wanted to do creditable work this
coming year, in order that we may be able to join
the Federation and meet with them at the World's
Fair which will surely prove benefical in the
interchange of ideas and the awakening of new
thoughts.
	The object of this club is purely literary and
all foreign topics will be excluded.
	Gladys Woodmansee read a poem from
Whittier entitled "The Watcher."
	Delicious refreshments were then served by the
President. After which benediction was pro-
nounced by Miss Woodmansee, and meeting ad-
journed until Jan. 30th, 1892.
