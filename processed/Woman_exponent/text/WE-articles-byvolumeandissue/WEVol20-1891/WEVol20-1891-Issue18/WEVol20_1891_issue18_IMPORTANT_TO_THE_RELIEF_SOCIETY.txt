IMPORTANT TO THE RELIEF SOCIETY.

	The President of the Relief Society, Mrs. Zina
D. H. Young, desires to obtain, for future use and
reference, historical sketches of each branch of
the Relief Society in all lands where there is or
has been an organization, the sketch of each to
include the amount of charitable work or dis-
bursements made and for what purposes, with
necessary data; also when organized and by
whom, the names of officers, and the changes
that have been made, if any. The President
wishes the minutes of Jubilee celebrations held in
each ward or place, whether in this country or in
other lands, accompanied with the sketch of the
Relief Society written for the occasion. Address
these manuscripts and communications to Mrs. E.
B. Wells, office of WOMAN'S EXPONENT, Salt
Lake City, Utah.


Professor John Gardiner, who is a graduate
of the University of London, and fills the
chair of Biology in the University of Colorado
takes strong ground in favor of the equality
of men and women. He says that the reason
Italy gave a position in Bologna University to
a woman, three hundred years ago, and that
Dante should depict such a character as
Beatrice, was because Italy was a commercial
nation. In peace is woman's opportunity.
Militarism is her destruction. No being has
so much to hope from peace as woman.
