PERFECT TRUST.
Take me! comes in plaintive accents
	From the babe close by my knee,
Wearied Now with toy and plaything 
	Comes she now, for rest, to me.
Never doubting, full well knowing
	I would shield her from all harm,
Mother love is all— so potent— 
	To the babe upon my arm.
Baby hands I know are reaching
	Unto me from Spirit Land,
Friends I love I know are waiting
	Until I shall join their band.
Oft I feel their presence near me
	Bringing solace oft midst pain,
Causing tears but leaving gladness,
	“Like the sunshine after rain.’’
When I plead unto the Father 
	For the strength I sorely need
Surely too The Mother listeth
	To a daughter as she pleads.
And with a perfect woman’s wisdom
	With the power born of right,
In mercy too will heed the daughter
	Struggling here amid the fight.
Though the darkness is appalling, 
	And the tempter's brave and strong,
Oft-times when we would be righteous
	We are tempted to do wrong.
But with heaven’s love above us,
	And with spirit-love beside,
We can hope to reach that haven—
	The perfect trust of a little child.
	E. T.
