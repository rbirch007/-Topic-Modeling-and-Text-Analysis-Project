WOMEN'S NEWS.

	On Monday March 28th at Union Square
Hall the ladies of the W. P. A. assembled to 
listen to one of the  most entertaining pro-
grammes of the series, there was music and 
reading of papers. During a short recess light 
refreshments were served, Mrs. James Neal
interested her listeners with the reading of a 
paper on Mrs. H. A. A. Beach who has com-
posed a Mass for the famous Handel and
Hayden society of Boston. Mrs. Beach has the
unique distinction of being the only lady who
has ever produced such a musical composition;
that it has worth is evidenced by its accep-
tation by this great organization. The pleas-
ant meeting closed till the next social, Apr.
11th. Some of the interesting numbers on
the programme were as follows: Vocal Duet
Mrs. Newlett and Miss Morse;Song, "A Rainy
Day" Miss Morse; An original Poem Mrs M.
R. Johnson.
						ALMA ALDEN
	San Fraucisco, Cal.
