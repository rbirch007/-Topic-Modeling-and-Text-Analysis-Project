		SOUTH BOUNTIFTL.
	At a meeting held in South Bountiful,
Davis Co., Utah, Feb. 11, 1892, a Woman
Suffrage Association was organized as follows:
Pres. Lucy A. Clark of Farmington was
chosen Chairman, Mary Ann Page appointed
Sec’y. pro tem.  After singing and prayer Mrs.
Dye of Salt Lake City addressed the assembly
stating the nature and object of the meeting.
	Adela W. Eardley and Margaret A. Caine,
also of Salt Lake City, gave further instruc-
tions. Mary Clark and Mrs. Stringam of
Farmington spoke encouragingly, giving some
forcible arguments in favor of woman suf-
frage.  Wm. Page of South Bountiful thought
the movement a good one, and also that, what
the ladies of Farmington had done, the ladies
of South Bountiful could also accomplish.
Samuel Howard of South Bountiful was will-
ing that his wife should stand side by side with
him in ‘‘equal rights.” G. W. Crocheron united
in good wishes for the success of the organiza-
tion. Charlotte Atkinson made her first
political address.  The organization was then
effected as follows:
	Pres. Sarah A. Howard, First Vice Pres.
Charlotte Atkinson; Sec. Vice Pres. Rose Ellen
Hatch; Third Vice Pres. Maria Hatch; Sec'y.
Lizzie Hatch Cor. Sec’y. Zina E. Crocheron
Chairman of Ex. Committee, Mary Ann Page,
members of Ex. Com., Louie Johnson, Jane
A. Hatch, Eliza Melson, Emma Moss; Treas.
Sarah A. Atkinson.
	Ten members then enrolled their names, six
ladies and four gentlemen besides the officers
making 22 members in all.
	MARY A. PAGE, Sec’y. pro tem.
