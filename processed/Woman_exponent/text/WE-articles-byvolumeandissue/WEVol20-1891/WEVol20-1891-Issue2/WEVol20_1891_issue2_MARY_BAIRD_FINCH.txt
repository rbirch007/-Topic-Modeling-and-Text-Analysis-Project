MARY BAIRD FINCH.

As blossoms wake 'neath sun and rain
I heard you sing love's tender strain,
With all love's sweet delight of pain.

Then softly on my heart it fell,
The story you stooped low to tell
Like spring-time's dawning miracle.

'Twas years and years we had not met,
Where 'mid the prairie grasses wet
You brought the first pale violet.

And we had grown so wondrous wise
To guard love's secret with our eyes—
How soon love saw the yain disguise!

And while his love was on us laid
That kept us both as half afraid
Love knew us for a pair betrayed.

Love came and smiled—I turned away—
Maids do the same this very day
The while they question love's delay.

Love weeping grieves and cries, "too late,"
And tender pity mourns his fate,
Yet lower now swings heaven's gate.
			Woman's Tribune.
