Forgetting and forgotten, so it seemed—
	My days went by, each other like the last,
A monotone of duty drawn by faith;
	Life's lightest moments scattered down the past:


'Till weary grown with "hope so long deferred,"
	O'erweighed with cares that faster seemed to roll,
I strove no more, but silent sat alone—
	Then—through the silence thou didst call my soul.

Thou didst not call me forth to joyous throng.
	To join in festal pleasures or in mirth:
Not praise or honor waited for my steps,
	Too feeble were my works to earn such worth,
But—for my soul's own sake thou didst awake
	My weary halting in the path of life:
That I should labor further to upraise
	Some better mark to shine above its strife.

And through my weariness a thought did steal,
	(As echoes follow after song has died,)
Perhaps—the Master too hath not forgot
	His pilgrim faltering by the long way side.
Perhaps—thy voice an echo of His will
	That shall throw open wide the hidden way—
Arise my soul! once more take up thy staff,
	Nor let the rough grown paths thy steps delay.
