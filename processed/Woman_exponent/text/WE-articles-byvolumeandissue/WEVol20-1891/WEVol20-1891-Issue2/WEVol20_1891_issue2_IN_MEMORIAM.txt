IN MEMORIAM.
		
	We, the sisters of the Relief Society of the Fourth Ward,
Provo City, unanimously adopt the following resolutions.
To the memory of our esteemed sister Mary S. Bennett,
who departed this life May 20th, 1891.
	She was born in Roland, England, March 2nd 1839.
She was married to John Bennett, Feb. 1st 1863, was the
mother of twelve children, ten sons and two daughters,
seven sons who survive her.
	She was a faithful member of our Society, and an active
teacher for several years, she had a kind and cheerful dis-
position, and was ever willing to help the sick and those in
trouble, she was an affectionate wife and indulgent mother.
	Whereas our Heavenly Father has seen fit to call from
our midst by the hand of death, our beloved sister and
friend,
	Therefore be it resolved that we recognize in the death
of our sister the loss of a faithful teacher and co-laborer in
the Relief Society.
	Resolved that we esteem her goodness and patience and
uncomplaining endurance worthy of our imitation.
	Resolved that we extend our heartfelt sympathy to the
family of the deceased, and with them acknowledge the 
hand of our Father in heaven.	Resolved that these resolutions be entered into the
Relief Society record, also a copy be presented to the be-


reaved family, and printed in the Utah Enquirer and WO-
MAN'S EXPONENT.
	M. M. WATSON,
		President.
	ADELAIDE J. MELDRUM,
	RACHEL ANN GATHERUM,
		Committee.
