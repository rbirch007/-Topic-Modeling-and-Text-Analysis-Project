DEAR SISTER WELLS:
	
	Your token of friendship has come safe to me
		O'er mountain and desert and plain,
	And the wish that you sent me, I breathe back to thee,
		For it calls up sweet memories again.

	This delicate spray in the corner so fair
		How like the sweet flowers of the heart,


Which if we would save we must garner with care
	And shield from the tempest's cold dart.

A "happy new year" ah, can happiness be,
	While Zion in fetters is bound,
While Satan still reigns on land and on sea,
	And sorrow is everywhere found?

Yet still we will sing a "Happy New Year,"
	For Hope springs afresh in each breast,
So faith all undying dispels every fear,
	And whispers the day dawn of rest.
				With love yours,
					HOPE.
Juarez, Jan 29, 1892.
