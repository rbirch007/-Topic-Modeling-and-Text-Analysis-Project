In the sacred name of freedom, for the sake of simple
	justice—
With the common right of Suffrage, can't a mighty nation
	trust us?
Oh ye favor'd Sons of Freedom—be magnanimous and
	just;—
Need the "Weaker Vessel" always be compell'd to kiss
	the dust?
			CHORUS.
Apathy, pretends to patience—while conditions harsh and
	hoary—
While oppressive senseless Statutes, mar a nation's name
	and glory;
Antiquated, wrongful Edicts—must be speedily repeal'd;
Woman's Right, must be conceded, Serfdom must to
	Freedom yield;
Join the ranks—and raise the Banner of the Woman Suf-
	frage Army?
Never mind the shafts of envy, never mind the tempests
	stormy;
