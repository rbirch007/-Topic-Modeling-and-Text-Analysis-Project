Liberty! is worth a struggle, worth the cost whate'er it

			CHORUS.
Wiser grows the world—and faster—much is done, and 
	much endeavor'd,
All humanity, rejoices, that the Negro's bonds are sever'd.
Now, in the behalf of progress, as a crowning act of grace—
Let, Consistency, forever, free the Mothers of the Race;
Doubtless, Woman's "Legal Status" is a farce, and noth-
	ing better;
Yet, withal, some "Foolish Virgins" would not loose a
	single fetter;
Wrest from man, the Right of Suffrage—an indignant hosts
	you'd see;
