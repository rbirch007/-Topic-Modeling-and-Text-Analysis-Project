THE SONG OF THE WORLD.

	Love, love, love!
Pleads the child heart hungering
For its heritage of heaven;
For its dower of holy leaven
From the All-Compassioning.
	Gently calling,
	Softly falling,
Love, the song the Angels sing.

	Love, love, love!
Beats the hopeful heart of youth,
Thrilling to that vital current
Of divinity inherent,
From the Fountain-head of Truth.
	Though the morrow
	Sob in sorrow,
Love, may speak the trouble smooth.

	Love, love, love!
Throbs life's whispered noontide prayer,
From the lives of toil it rises,
Through the mother's sacrifices,
Through the father's selfless care.
	Ah! to live it!
	Thus to give it!
Is to feel God everywhere.

	Love, love, love!
Sigh the old in underbreath,
'Tis the smile of the immortal
That makes warm the silent portal,
And 'tis all the silence hath.
	While life's gladness
	And its sadness
Slip away to darkling death.

	Love, love, love!
Is the hushed, the smothered cry
Of the world; through evil stealing,
Through the pain and wrong revealing
Man's redemption drawing nigh.


Naught can still it,
	Naught can kill it,
Love of love can never die.
			CARRIE RENFREW.
The Woman's Tribune.
