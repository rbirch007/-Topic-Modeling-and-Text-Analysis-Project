	She was always a faithful wife and mother,
seeking every opportunity to promote the hap-
piness and comfort of the family of her hus-
band, ever their true nurse in sickness, affliction
and trials, watching over them with all the
care and anxiety of a true mother and wife,
ready and willing at all times to sacrifice her
own comfort for their benefit. Together with
her husband, she entertained their friends and
neighbors in the most hospitable manner.
	Sister Allred will be greatly missed as a
representative woman in Sanpete Stake, having
been in the habit of traveling and meeting
with the sisters in the various localities for the
last ten or eleven years; but she has gone from
our midst, and we will see her dear face no more
in our assemblies; but let us, my sisters, emu-
late her noble example that we may be pre-
pared to dwell with her and the many loved
ones who have passed behind the vail. Rest
in peace, dear sister, and may we meet again
where there shall be no more sad partings.
