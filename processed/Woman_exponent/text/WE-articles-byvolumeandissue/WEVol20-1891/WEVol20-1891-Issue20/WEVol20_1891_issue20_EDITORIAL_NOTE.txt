EDITORIAL NOTE

	Mrs. Young, President of the Relief Society,
and Mrs. Taylor, President of the Y. L. M. I. A.
have received official notice of the Biennial Meet-
ing of the National Council of Women which is
to occur in May 1892, and will be held in Chicago
and at which time it is expected they will be
present, as they are by virtue of the office they
hold ex-officio Vice Presidents of the National
Council. Important matters are to be brought
before the Council relating to the International
Council of Women which will meet in Chicago
during the World’s Columbian Exposition, and
will in all probability be the most imposing body
of women from various countries ever gathered
in an assembly for the transaction of business
pertaining to the elevation of womankind through
the advancement of all that pertains to the higher
cultivation of the powers of the human soul. One
of the advantages of the connection of these As-
sociations with the National and International
Councils of Women is in bringing into contact
women engaged in the various pursuits, occupa-
tions and professions that are opening wider
avenues of employment and progression, and
helping to sound the key-note of woman’s free-
dom to all the world. Although perhaps neither
Mrs. Young or Mrs. Taylor can make it con-
venient to attend this year on account of the long
distance, yet they will probably send letters ex-
pressing their views upon the subjects under con-
sideration, and also pay their quota of percentage,
which it was voted should be paid at stated inter-
vals during the intervening four years instead of
paying so large a sum as one hundred dollars
down.
