THANKSGIVING AND PRAYER.

   President Woodruff and the First Presidency of
the Church have called upon the people to fast
and pray and give thanks, and have set apart a
day for that purpose, the Sabbath day, the first of
May and this day is to be generally observed
throughout all the Stakes of Zion, in the several
wards according to the direction of the presiding
priesthood. That the devotion and prayers of an
entire people offered up in unison and with fast-
ing will be pleasing to our Father in heaven there
can be no doubt. All who have the spirit of the
Gospel and of the times will gladly respond to
this call, and the congregations of the Saints as-
sembled for such a purpose will realize the need
of drawing near to God and putting away worldi_
ness, seek forgiveness and mercy, and renew
their covenants and resolutions to serve Him
more completely.
   Thousands of the Latter-day Saints will hail
this day as a happy omen for the prosperity of
Zion, the Lord has said He will be inquired of,
and He delights in the services of His Saints
and their gratitude and thanksgiving must be an
acceptable oblation at the throne of grace.
