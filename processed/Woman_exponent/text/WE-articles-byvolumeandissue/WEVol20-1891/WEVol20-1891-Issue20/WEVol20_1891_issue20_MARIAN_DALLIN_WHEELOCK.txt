	MARIAN DALLIN WHEELOCK.
Dear Sister Wells:
		It is with feelings of the deepest
sympathy that I communicate to you the death
of our beloved sister, Mrs. Marian Dallin Whee-
lock, who departed this life on the 13th of
March after a lingering illness of eight weeks,
which she bore with patient resignation. She
was a loving and affectionate wife and tender
mother, and was gifted with many noble and
redeeming qualities. Sister Wheelock was the
daughter of Tobius and Anthea Dallin, was
born in Newport, Monmouthshire, England,
May 27th, 1831. She embraced the gospel in
her girlhood and emigrated to Utah in 1852.
Her devotion to the cause of truth has been
manifested in her daily life. She was an earn-
est worker in whatever she engaged in, and
her loss will be greatly felt.
	For many years she was a faithful and
diligent teacher in the Sunday School, where
her labors will be greatly missed. During the
last five years she was president of the Primary
Association of the South Ward, Mt. Pleasant,
but in Aug, 1891, sent in her resignation on
account of failing health.
	She never was happier than when laboring
among the youth of Zion. She was held in the
highest esteem by not only the children of
Mt. Pleasant, but by a host of friends who
knew her throughout the Territory.
	She had the love of all she knew, and if she
had an enemy we knew it not. Her aged
husband has the heartfelt sympathy of the en-
tire community, as also her bereaved children.
	The funeral was largely attended by a host
of sympathizing friends and relatives.
