UTAH W. S. A.

	An executive session of the Utah Woman
Suffrage Association was held in the Four-
teenth Ward Assembly rooms October 3rd, at
ten o'clock. Mrs. Phebe Y. Beatie chair-
man.
	Mrs. Howard was appointed chaplain and
opened the meeting with prayer. The minutes
of the executive meeting held April 3rd, were
read and adopted.
	A compilation of the national and territorial
by-laws designed to govern the Utah associa-
tions were read and discussed by sections and
adopted as printed below. The duties of
honorary vice presidents were defined, on sug-
gestion of Maria Y. Dougall. The nomination
of territorial officers was held with the follow-
ing result; President, Sarah M. Kimball; Sec-
retary, Ada Croxall; Cor. Sec., Julia Taplor;
Ass't. Cor. Sec., Nellie Little; Treasurer,
Margery Dwyer; Auditor Dr. E. S. Barney;
Chairman Ex. Com., Phebe Y. Beatie; vice-
presidents; First, Emily Richards; second
Ann Groesbeck; third, Caroline Dye; Emmeline
B. Wells, chairman publishing committee.
	The following are the names of the Terri-
torial honorary vice-presidents, whose duties
and privileges are defined by the constitution:
Zina D. Young, Jane S. Richards, Bathsheba
Smith, M. Isabella Horne, Emmeline B. Wells,
Elmina S. Taylor, Mary A. Freeze, Maria Y.
Dougall, Emily H. Woodmansee, Augusta J.
Crocheron, Ellen Jakeman, Lula G. Richards,
Mrs. Haymond.

	The Territorial Woman Suffrage Convention
convened Oct. 3rd, 1891, at 2 p. m., in the
14th Ward Assembly Rooms of Salt Lake
City. The hall was decorated with stars and
stripes and above the stand hung two large
portraits of Elizabeth Cady Stanton and Susan
B. Anthony.
	Pres. Sarah M. Kimball presided. The
Convention was opened by prayer, offered by
Zina D. H. Young.
	M. A. Y. Greenhalgh was elected temporary
chairman while the Territorial officers who
were nominated in the Executive Session were
placed before the Convention. They were
then confirmed.
