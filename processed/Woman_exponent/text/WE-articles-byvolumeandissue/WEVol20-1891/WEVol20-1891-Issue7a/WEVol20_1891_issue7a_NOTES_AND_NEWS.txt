NOTES AND NEWS.

	Mrs, Julia Ward Howe, at the Woman's
Congress, at Grand Rapids, will read a paper
on "Aliens in America."

	A lady (Miss Coffin) has been appointed
Assistant Superintendent of Schools in Detroit
Michigan.

	Miss Susan B. Anthony and Rev. Anna H.
Shaw addressed an immense crowd on Wo-
man's Day at the Western New York Agri-
cultural Fair at Rochester, Sept. 23.

	It is reported that there are only ten persons
in the State Penitentiary of Wyoming.
Wyoming is the only place where women have
had full suffrage for the last twenty-two
years.

	Miss Vinnie Hall, a young woman of twenty
is editor and publisher of the Edgely (N. D.)
Mail.  She began by learning to set type, and 
while at Aberdeen, S. D., she was a member 
of the Newspaper Union for two years and a 
half, being promoted to forewoman of the com-
posing room.

	Mrs. Therese A. Jenkins of Wyoming,
National Superintendent of Franchise, has
been invited to present to the National W. C.
T. U., at its coming convention in Boston, a
banner to represent the Franchise Depart-
ment.  This will be one of the most interest-
ing incidents of the convention.

	Miss Florence Balgarnie gave three lectures
in Detroit, Sept. 20, 21, and 22, to full houses.
Her subjects were "Women in Public Life,"
"The Modern Factory Girl as I knew her in 
East London," and "Ourselves and Our Great-
Grandmothers."  The second subject proved 
especially interesting to those who are studying
the labor question, and elicited many inquiries
and comments.  Miss Balgarnie won many
admirers among the ladies of Detroit.
