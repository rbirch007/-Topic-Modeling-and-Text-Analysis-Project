FIRST ANNUAL CONVENTION OF THE
P. C. WOMAN’S PRESS ASSOCIATION.
	The lady writers of the Pacific Coast will hold
a three days’ Convention (instead of two as be-
fore stated) commencing Sept. 21st. There will
be a session each morning at 10 o’clock and
another in the afternoon at 2, for business only,
but the evening of Wed. 23rd inst. will be de-
voted to a literary and musical programme, when
the doors will be thrown open to the public. The
meetings commence at 8 p.m. and will include ad-
dresses by Mrs. Nellie B. Eyster, Pres. P. C. W.
P. A., Mrs. Emily S.Y. Parkhurst, Cor. Sec. P.
C. W. P. A., Miss E.D. Keith (Di Vernon S.F.
“News-Letter”), Mrs. Alice C. Waterman, Ade-
line Knapp, S.F. “Call,” Alice Kingsbury Cooley.
	Short poems will be given by Mrs. Emily B.
Powell, Charlotte Perkins Stetson, and Lillian
Plunkett. Those who will represent the musical
portion of the programme are, Henry Kirke
White Jr., Miss Jerome and Mrs. Marian Taylor,
L. C. M. Complimentary tickets may be obtained 
from Mrs. Parkhurst, 1419 Taylor St., or from
any member of the Association, but as the de-
mand for them is already very great, it will be
necessary—for those who wish to be present—to
make an early application, in order to receive 
their admittance.
