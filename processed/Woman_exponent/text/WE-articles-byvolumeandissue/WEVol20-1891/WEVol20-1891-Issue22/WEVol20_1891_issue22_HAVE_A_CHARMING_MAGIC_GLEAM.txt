	Have a charming magic gleam,
Passion's earnest voice is thrilling,—
	Ah, it is a happy dream!
A most brilliant, joyous dreaming,
	Fresh and pure as budding rose,
Fragrant incense borne from Eden,
	Solace sweet for cares and woes.
I am dreaming, I am dreaming,
	Of one brilliant Summer day
When with friends we took a ramble,
	Bidding them to lead the way.
But there seemed a reason mystic,
	Oblivious to define,
For the path they took we miss'd it—
	And sought not in haste to find.
So we talked of birds and flowers,
	Of the rocks, trees, flowing brooks,
And so quickly passed the hours,
	That no note of time we took.
On, on, over hills and valleys,
	Still straying we knew not where,
The sweetest flowers were gather'd
	Twined in wreaths most passing fair.
I am dreaming, surely dreaming,
	Of that bright glorious day,
How the stars came forth to greet us,
	As we turned our homeward way,
And I see an altar forming,
	Amid thoughts and dreams divine,
And I hear a voice calling,
	"Dear girl, you must be mine!"
All is pictured out before me,
	As bright meteors they gleam,
And the words, "I love, adore thee!"
	O, it is not all a dream!
Ah, how kindly to those  moments,
	My holiest thoughts reply,
In love's accents soft and tender,
	As balmiest zephyrs sigh.
With radiance pure and holy,
	Its light tinges every hue,
Would to heaven, after moments
	Had thus ever been as true!
O, break not the spell that binds me,
	Nor dispel this witching theme,
The glad light of love is round me,
	Yet it is not all a dream.
			LUCY.
