			S. L. CO. W. S. A.

	The regular monthly meeting of Salt Lake
County W. S. A. was held in the 13th Ward
Assembly Rooms, May 16th, 1892, Vice Pres.
Louise K. Spencer in the chair.
	Opened by singing, "Columbia's Daughters."
Prayer, Vice Pres., Phebe C. Young. Sing-
ing "Song for Equal Rights." Roll called,
minutes of meeting held March 15th 1892,read

[Column 2]
and approved, minutes of meeting held April
19th 1892, read and approved.
	A programme was rendered as follows:
Reading from The Woman's Manual of Parlia-
mentary Law, by Ardella Eardley. Reading
of a poem, "Martha Washington," by May B.
Talmage. Song, "In Old Madrid," by Maggie
Bassett. Reading, of "The first code of senti-
ments read at a convention held forty years
ago in Seneca Co., New York," by Mrs. Del
Felt. Reading of speeches of Isabella Beecher
Hooker and Miss Susan B. Anthony,by Maggie
Bassett.
	Elizabeth Price read several articles by emi-
nent writers on Equal Rights, and made some
remarks on them.
	Mrs. E. McFarlane read a few clippings on
the advancement of Woman's cause.
	Dr. E. S. Barney made a few remarks.
	Sarah A. Howard, President of the Bounti-
ful Association, made a few pleasing and in-
teresting remarks on Equal Rights.
	Ter. Pres., S. M. Kimball, said she had been
much pleased and edified with all she had
heard during the meeting, and was proud to
see so many young women take an active part
in the programme. Referred to the progress that
woman is making; encouraged the ladies to
press forward in this great work.
	Meeting adjourned until the third Tuesday
in June, at the same time and place.
