DEAR AUNT EM:
	The following extracts from a private letter,
I feel sure, will be interesting to many of
your readers. The writer is a widow with five
young children.
							L. L. G. R.
			*	*	*	*
"DEAR BROTHER AND SISTER:
	I received your very kind and welcome let-
ter a week ago. I should write to you oftener,
but it seems as though every moment of my
time is necessarily employed. We often talk
about you, and wish we could see you all.
Even little John remembers your coming to
see us, and bringing the baby. He talks of it,
and how good the baby’s father is for sending
us the Juvenile Instructor with such pretty
stories in. There is no other amusement he
takes so much comfort in as listening to read-
ing.	*	*	I know you must be kept very
busy with your family of boys, but what a
comfort children are to their parents. There
are trials for all of us to pass through, but our
trials are so much easier if we trust in the
Lord and acknowledge his hand in all
things.	*	*	*	*
	The money you so kindly sent me was grate-
fully received, and was quite a surprise. The
Sunday before it came, I went to meeting, and
the Bishop said that all the tithing of every
kind must be paid before next Saturday night.
I owed five dollars in due bills, for my earn-
ings and a calf; and I had it on hand, for I 
had saved for that purpose. But I was tempt-
ed not to pay it, and to use it for something
else. I overcame the temptation, and paid
the five dollars; and after I had done so, I
felt so rejoiced to think I had overcome. And
at night I received your letter and the money
you sent; then I felt ashamed of having doubt-
ed that The Lord would provide. I thank you
both, and the Lord also, and pray Him to
bless and keep you always.”
		*	*	*	*	*	*
								S. P. G.
