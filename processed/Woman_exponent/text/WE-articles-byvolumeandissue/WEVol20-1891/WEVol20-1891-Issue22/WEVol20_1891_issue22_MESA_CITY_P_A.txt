	MESA CITY P. A.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	Thinking a few items in regard to the Pri-
mary Association of Mesa City would be inter-
esting,  I herewith, at the request of Sister
Harriet Burton, President of the P. A., send
the following:
	It is presided over by Harriet Burton with
Laura Rapleye and Anna Phelps as Counselors.
The children take a great interest in their
meetings, which Sister Burton manages to make
very interesting. She has the faculty of win-
ning hearts, and so arranging the exercises
as to make them entertaining as well as in-
structive. A Primary Fair was held last sum-
mer or fall, which was very creditable to the
children. An account of it was sent to the
EXPONENT, but never appeared, much to the
disapointment of the officers and members of

[Column 2]

the Primary Association. It was the request
of the authorities of the Stake that funds be
contributed to assist in the completion and
furnishing of the Salt Lake Temple. The
Primary Association felt ambitious to take part
in such a noble work. They therefore inaugu-
rated an entertainment, which was a success
in every respect, and from the proceeds of
which they donated $15.00 for the Temple,
which is a credit to their heads and hearts.
They also have an interest in our Relief
Society Hall, furnishing a fine chandelier,
which is quite an ornament to the building.
Hoping you will make a little note of this in
the EXPONENT, and let the readers know that
Mesa is not a whit behind, in their Primary
organization, I remain your friend,
