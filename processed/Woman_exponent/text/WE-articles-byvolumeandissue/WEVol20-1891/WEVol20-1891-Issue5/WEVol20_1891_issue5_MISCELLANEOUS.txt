MISCELLANEOUS.

	Keep cool and you cammand everybody.
St. Just.
	The best armor is to keep out of gunshot.—
Lord Bacon.
	Reason and virtue alone can bestow liberty.
—Shaftesbury.
	Good manners are the small coin of virtue.
Woman of England.
	The greatest difficulties are where we are not
looking for them.—Goethe.
	The first step in debt is like the first step in
falsehood.—Samuel Smiles.
	True merit, like a river, the deeper it is the
less noise it makes.—Halifax;
	Our deeds determine us as much as we de-
termine our deeds.—George Eliot.
	The ways of the heart, like the ways of
Providence, are mysterious.—Ware.
	He that calls a man ungrateful sums up all
the evil a man can be guilty of.—Swift.
	My gems are falling away, but it is because
God is making up His jewels.—Wolfe.
	Youth is in danger until it learns to look
upon debts as furies.—Bulwer Lytton.
	Libraries are shrines where all the relics of
saints, full of true virtue, and without delusion
and imposture, are preserved and reposed.—
Bacon.
