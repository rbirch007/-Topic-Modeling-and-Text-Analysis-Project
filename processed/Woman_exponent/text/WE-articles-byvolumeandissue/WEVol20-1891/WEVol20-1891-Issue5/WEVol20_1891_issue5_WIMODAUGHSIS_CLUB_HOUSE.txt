WIMODAUGHSIS CLUB HOUSE.

	Our beloved Wimodaughsis has secured by
purchase a most desirable house, being 1328
I St., N. W. This house is a comparatively
new, press brick front, containing fifteen rooms.
It has a frontage of thirty feet, and the lot
runs back one hundred and thirty-four feet to
a wide alley, which gives ample opportunity
for additional buildings. The location is par-
ticularly desirable, as it fronts on Franklin
Square which is one of the most beautiful of
the many parks in the city. It and the
Lafayette Park are said to contain the greatest
varieties of trees and plants of any parks in
the world. The street is quiet and retired,
and yet it is near all the car lines and prin-
cipal hotels. The Board feels very much en-
couraged at having secured so excellent a
place, and have every confidence that the
friends of the enterprise which has been so
fully explained in your columns, will rally to
aid in making payments as they mature. There
is also need of many gifts to furnish the house
and build up the library. It is especially de-
sired that this should be a woman's library, in
which their words and works may be fully
recorded and represented. Articles that have
belonged to prominent women, and their
portraits and autographs will be specially
welcome now that a permanent place is secured
where they can be properly cared for. An
appeal will soon be sent out, and if it fails to
reach any one who is interested, it is hoped
she will not delay on that account, but send
whatever she feels the ability and inclination
to give.
	Cordially yours,
		EMMA M. GILLETT.
Woman's Tribune.
