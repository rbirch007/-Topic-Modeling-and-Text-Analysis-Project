PERSONALS.

	Di Vernon has just written an article on
"Sea-side resorts of California" for the Ameri-
can Press Association.
	Mrs. S. T. D. Evelyn has an article in the
August number of the Chicago Woman's Jour-
nal, on "Women Holidaying."
	Mrs. Gertrude Franklin Atherton has
written a new novel entitled "A Question of
Time."
	Di Vernon wrote an article in the Pacific
Rural Press on "How I built a greenhouse '
(Aug.15th).
	Louise Franci had an article in the Pacific
Rural Press Aug. 15th on "Take comfort in
canning fruit."
	Mrs. R. F. English of the W. P. A. has
been appointed to take charge of the training
school in connection with the Normal School
at San Jose.
	Mrs Barbara Ruell has discovered a fine
Bay-Salt Quarry on her property at Los
Guilicos.


Mrs. Romualdo Pacheco a distinguished
member of the W. P. A. will shortly rejoin her
husband in Guatemala.
	Miss Virna Woods author of "The Amazons"
read a strong original poem recently at Pacific
Grove reflecting great credit on the P. C. W.
P. A.
	Mrs. Alice Moore Mc.Comas of the Los
Angelos Express is at Chino writing up the 
sugar beet industry for the P. C. W. P. A.
	Clara Spalding Brown has just returned
from Mount Wilson, where she has been
writing up Wilson's Peak and photographing
its scenery.
	Mrs. E. S. Y. Parkhurst goes as Delegate to
the International Federation of Women's Press
Associations, which assembles Nov. 15th in'
Boston.
	Ella Higginson's poem "The New West'
read at the convention of the P. C. W. P. A.
in March last has been re-published in the
"Review of Reviews" London.
