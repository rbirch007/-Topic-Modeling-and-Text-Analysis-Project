EXPRESSIONS OF LOVE AND RESPECT.
	The members of the La Platta branch Relief Society
wish to express through the columns of the WOMAN'S EX-
PONENT, the love and esteem they felt for the late lamented
Sister Jane M. Walton, their Stake President.
	We feel that in her we have lost a wise counselor, a true
friend, and an agreeable associate.
	She was a meek and lowly follower of Christ, and what-
ever she was called upon to do she did it with that child-
like dependence on Him that impressed one at once that
she was ever guided by His will. We never saw in her
any marks of arrogance, but always leaning upon those in
authority for counsel, for guidance and for support.
	You will doubtless hear of her from those who have
been more immediately associated with her, and have been
blessed for so many years with her presence, in sorrow and
in joy, for she was always near when a word of comfort
was needed or help in sickness of any nature.
	She was certainly an enviable women for she lived a life
of usefulness and has gone to receive the reward of the
just, has left none behind but that feel to breathe blessings
on her name.
	A loving wife, a tender parent, a true friend. May the
Lord help us to emulate her example, that we may have
the privilege of associating with her where wicked men are
not permitted to prey upon the just.
	Members of the La Plata branch of the Relief Society
of the San Juan Stake. Farmington N. M. Aug. 5, 1891.
