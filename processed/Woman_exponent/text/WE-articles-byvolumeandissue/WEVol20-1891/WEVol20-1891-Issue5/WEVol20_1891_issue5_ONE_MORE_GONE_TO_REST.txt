ONE MORE GONE TO REST.

	At Eden Ward, Weber County, June 5th 1891. we were
called to part with a noble woman, Sister Margaret Mc'
Bride, President of the Relief Society of the Eden Ward.
	Margaret Howard Mc'Bride was born Dec. 21, 1814, at
parish of North Miles, Lancashire, England. Married at
the same place in the year 1833, Nov. 25. Baptized into
the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints on the
4th of Jan. 1838, by Heber C. Kimball, in Preston, Lan-
cashire, England; and emigrated on the 10th of May 1856.
Crossed the plains with handcarts, her husband dying upon
the way; he was found dead under his wagon covered with
snow and was buried in connection with 14 others all in
one grave.
	She arrived in Ogden, Dec. 24, 1856, with five small
children the youngest three years old: she was the mother
of ten children five of them are now living, and are mem-
bers of the Church; she has forty-six grandchildren.
	She was chosen as President of the Relief Society of the
Eden Ward on the 7th of June 1878, and has ever been a
faithful sister and an active President.
	Therefore be it resolved that we recognize in the death


of our sister the loss of a faithful President and kind
friend.
	Resolved that we esteem her goodness and patience and
uncomplaining endurance worthy of our imitation.
	Resolved that these resolutions be entered in the Relief
Society record,and also a copy be printed in the WOMAN'S
EXPONENT. 
MARTHA A. FERRIN,
MARGARET LARSEN,
	Counselors.
