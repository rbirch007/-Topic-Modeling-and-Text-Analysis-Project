MARICOPA.

	Report of the Maricopa Stake Conference
held at Mesa Dec. 12th 1891, Pres. S. M.
Pomeroy presiding.
	There were present C. I. Robson, Bishop
Passey and others of the leading brethren and
sisters.  After the usual exercises, Pres. S. M.
Pomeroy said she regretted the small attend-
ance, thought the extreme cold weather (for
this climate) might be the cause.
	Pres. Barbara Lang said the report of the 
Mesa ward was about the same as last confer-
ence, thought they were moving along towards
the right but some were careless.
	Melissa Hunsaker was surprised at being
called upon to report the Alma ward thought
there must be some good reason for Sister Klei -
man not being present, as she was at her post
when possible, had held their meetings regu-
larly.
	Pres. Susan Savage said the Papago ward
were doing a little better than at last report.
	Pres. Esther Openshaw had a band of good
faithful sisters to labor with in the Nephi ward
with the help of the Lord were doing their
duty.
	Lehi ward not reported.  S. M. Pomeroy
had visited all the wards and found things
moving along well.
	M. A. Hakes rejoiced to meet with the 
sisters,felt that the plan of salvation was broad
enough to take in all honest hearts.
	S. A. Robson, C. Averett and Lovina Brim-
hall made a few timely remarks.
	Brother Noah Brimhall and George F.
Wilson gave some good advice to the sisters as
well as to the saints in general.
	Pres. C. I. Robson was an advocate for short
spirited meetings, said we have had a good 
time together spoke briefly upon the plan of 
salvation and its benefits to the human family;
after singing meeting adjourned for three
months, benediction by N. Brimhall.
