	Why ere the snow and frost had fled,
		I should this gem of spring-time find.


A farther search revealed to view
	Two purple pansies,bright and rare,
Beneath last Autumn's leaves they grew
	And bloomed,and nestled snugly there

Burn's saw "within the harebells eye"
	The proof that nature had a God,
The violet showed me God was nigh
	And used his power beneath the sod.

I read a lesson from that flower
	A lesson old, yet ever new,
A proof of the Creator's power
	That can from death,young life renew.

I watched the plants at close of year
	And marked them wither, fade, and die,
And yet before the spring is here,
	They're looking upward to the sky.

So shall immortal spirits rise,
	From earthly gardens here below
To bloom 'neath heaven's eternal skies
	And through an endless summer grow.

There, guided by a Father's hand,
	And tended by His watchful care,
They'll live and progress in the land
	And life, immortal, with him share.

Thus, when we lay our lov'd ones down
	Beneath the snow clad cheerless sod,
We know they'll rise and wear a crown
	Fashioned by theirs, and natures God.

Oh, tiny flowret, sweet and pure
	I fain would linger at thy shrine,
Nor let one mundane care allure
	From works of love, and thoughts divine.
