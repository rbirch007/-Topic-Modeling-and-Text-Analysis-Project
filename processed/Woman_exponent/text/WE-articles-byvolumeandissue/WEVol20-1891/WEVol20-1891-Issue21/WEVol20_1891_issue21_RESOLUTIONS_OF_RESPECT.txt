RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.

	Died:—At her late residence in the seventh
ward of Salt Lake City, Sunday, April 17th 1892,
of apoplexy, after an illness of five days, Mrs.
Jane C. Richardson, wife of the late Dr. Darwin
C. Richardson.
	The deceased was born in Lisbon, New Hamp-
shire, Oct. 15th, 1819. Arrived in this city Sept.
26th 1847, the same year as the pioneers.
	Whereas—our sister has been taken from us by
the remorseless hand of death, we feel that a
noble woman has fallen, we have lost an honored
and loved friend, and our Relief Society, of which
she has been Treasurer for the past twenty-two
years, an efficient and faithful officer, and the poor
a sympathetic, generous provider, therefore be it—
	Resolved—that we deeply mourn for one who
was in every way worthy of our love and highest
regard; that we will ever strive to emulate her
estimable qualities, and follow her noble and self-
sacrificing example.
	Resolved—that we sincerely condole with the
family of the departed, and lovingly commend
them for consolation, to one who doeth all things
for our good, and whose chastisements are ever
in mercy.


Resolved—that this heartfelt testimonial of our
sorrow and sympathy be presented to the be-
reaved family, also placed upon the record of our
Relief Society and published in the WOMAN'S
EXPONENT.
			MRS. M. W. WILCOX, President.
			MAGGIE Y, TAYLOR,
			E. J. STEVENSON,
				Counselors.
			ELMINA S, TAYLOR, Secretary.
