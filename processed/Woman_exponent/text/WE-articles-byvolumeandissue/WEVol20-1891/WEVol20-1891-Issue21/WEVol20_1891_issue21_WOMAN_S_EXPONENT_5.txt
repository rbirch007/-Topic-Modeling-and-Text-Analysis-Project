WOMAN'S EXPONENT


One of the sublimest things in the world is
plain truth.—Bulwer.
	Temptations are a file which rubs off much
of the rust of our self-confidence.—Fenelon. 
	Virtue alone outbids the pyramids; her mon-
uments shall last when Egypt's fall.—E.
Young.
	Loving kindness is greater than laws; and
the charities of life are more than all ceremo-
nies.—Talmud.
	Scratch the green rind of a sapling, or wan-
tonly twist it in the soil, and a scarred or crook-
ed oak will tell of the act for centuries to
come. So it is with the teachings of youth,
which make impressions on the mind and heart
that are to last through life.
