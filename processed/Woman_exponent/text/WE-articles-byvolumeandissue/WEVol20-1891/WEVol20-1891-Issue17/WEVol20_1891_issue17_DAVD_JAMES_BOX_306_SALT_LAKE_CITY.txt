	DAVD JAMES. Box 306. SALT LAKE CITY.


Mrs. J. F. Russel, of Topeka, Kan., has
been appointed to succeed her husband, lately
deceased, as superintendent of the county
poor-farm.

	In the Iowa House of Representatives, the
bill to grant women presidential suffrage was
refered to the woman suffrage committee, who
on Feb. 25 recommended its indefinite post-
ponement, four Republicans and all the
Democrats voting against it.

	The Writers’ Club, London, was formally
opened at No. 190 Fleet, on Jan. 30. It
provides a place where women engaged in
literary and journalistic wotk can write in
silence, dine, see the newspapers, and meet
their friends.

	The committee for women’s work of the
English world’s Fair Commission are; Princess
Christian, of Schleswig-Holstein, president,
the Marchioness of Salisbury, the Baroness
Burdett-Coutts, the Countess of Aberdeen,
Lady Alfred Churchill, Lady Agnes Buren,
Lady Brassey, Lady Knutsford, Lady Galton,
Lady Jeune, Lady Roberts, Mrs. Carmichael,
Mrs. Fawcett, Mrs. Bedford Fenwick, Miss
Forcyth, Mrs. Priestly, Mrs. Roberts-Austen,
Mrs. Tyssen Amherst,and Miss Fay Lankester.
