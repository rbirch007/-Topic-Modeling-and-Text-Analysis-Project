A LOVING TRIBUTE.

				Juarez, Feb. 18, 1892.
DEAR SISTER WELLS:
	One by one the “Lights along the Shore” are
 being hidden from our view by the shadow that
lies over the River, one by one our pillars of help
and strength are taken from us, one by one God’s
acre gathers them in and one by one the ran-
somed of the Lord are called home to the Father’s
mansions, dear Aunt Presendia, must we learn to
live without her too? Faithfully and true has
she finished her mortal labors, but her words of
counsel, of comfort and blessing will live in the
hearts of the hundreds she has helped to pass
through hours of trial and darkness. No words
of praise for her are needed, her life and works
are before the people, since the earliest days of
the Church, her faith, her hope, her prayers were
for the triumph of God’s Kingdom. Who ever
sought a blessing at her hands in vain, when did
she ever neglect a duty, not even bodily pain and
suffering stayed her work of blessing her sisters
in Zion. Once I said to her—"How often I have
seen your hands laid upon others to bless them,
but how seldom have I seen this done for you, do
you never feel weak and in need of a blessing?”
she looked at me with one of her pleasant smiles
and answered, “In blessing others I am blessed
I have proved this promise of Jesus to be true.”
What she has been to me and mine words can
never tell, she came to us in a sad hour of sorrow
long years ago, and ever since has been a true
unchanging friend and mother. May the memory
of her counsel and example still bear fruit in
Zion. Peace and rest to her weary body until the
resurrection morn.
	Glory and honor and joy be thine for ever
more, Dear Aunt Presendia.
							S. E. R.
