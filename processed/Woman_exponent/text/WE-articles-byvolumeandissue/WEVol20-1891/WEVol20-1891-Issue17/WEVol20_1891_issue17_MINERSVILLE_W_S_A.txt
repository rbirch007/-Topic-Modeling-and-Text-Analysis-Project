			MINERSVILLE  W. S. A.
	If you will give us space in the columns of
your most valuable paper I will endeavor to
let the readers know that the W. S. A. of
Minersville is doing well, we have 13 names
enrolled on our books; our society is in the
third year of its organization, we take three
papers viz: the WOMAN'S EXPONENT, Woman's
Tribune and Woman’s Column; we have
monthly meetings the first Tuesday in every
month, and through the kindness of Bishop
Walker we hold our meetings in the meeting
house. At our meeting in December a motion
was made that we have a Leap Year party the
first day of January 1892 and also all the
members of the W. S. A. wear a badge of
yellow ribbon with the letters W. S. A. printed
on each. The flag for Wyoming was made
and graced the walls of the room, together
with the stars and stripes of the National flag,
also the representative women of the nation.
The woman’s flag looked lovely, was ten feet
long and the single star in the field of blue
looked lonely, with just the one star and no
more. A short speech was made by the Presi-
dent.
	Success to all the ladies in this New Year in


all their labors, and to the papers that send
their messages forth.
