A CHILD'S THOUGHT.

"Oh mother where is heaven—
	I’m drefful tired o' play,
want to see the angels,
	So tak me dere to-day?"

"But darling heaven is yonder
	A long, long way from you—
"Above the house tops mother,
	Above the sky so blue?’"

"Well guessed my fair haired laddie,
	But tho' so far away,
The great, good God can see you,
	And hear you when you pray."

"But mother how do people,
	Reach up to heav'n so high, 
I don't think I could det dere
	No matter how I'd try?"

The child's blue eyes were wistful
	And dark with unshed tears,


When like a bird of passage
	Away flew all his fears.

And gleefully he shoutedf
	"I know what I will do,
I'll det the big step ladder,
	And climb to heav'n won't you?"
