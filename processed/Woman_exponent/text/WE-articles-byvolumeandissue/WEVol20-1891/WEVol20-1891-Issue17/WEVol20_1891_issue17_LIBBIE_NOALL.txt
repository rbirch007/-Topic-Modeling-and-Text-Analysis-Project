			LIBBIE NOALL.

		LULA.
	If I'm washing or ironing or scrubbing,
		And as busy as busy, can be,
	I must stop and glance o'er the newspaper
		To read the lines written by thee,

	Thy "Holiday Thoughts" was a treasure
		And the lines to thine aged mother too
	And "The Red Root and Morning Glory"
		Drew my thoughts out responsive to you.

	I have but to see the word Lula
		To know the sweet strains will be there
	That will find in my own heart an echo,
		For thy lines are the products of prayer,

	May the Angels of heaven watch round, thee
		May thy beautiful Gift thus increase,
	For 'twill prove a solace in thy sorrows
		And through it will thy trials find release.
