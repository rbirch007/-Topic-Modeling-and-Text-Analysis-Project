NOTICES OF MEETINGS.

	A meeting of the officers of the Primary Asso-
ciation from the several Stakes of Zion will be
held at the residence of Elder Charles Felt, 155
N. Main St. this City at 7:30 Sunday evening
October 4th; a full and prompt attendance is de-
sired by the President Louie Felt.
	The General Conference of the Primary Asso-
ciation will be held in the Assembly Hall in this
City on Monday October 5, commencing immedi-
ately after the close of the afternoon session of
the Conference. Presidents of Stake Associa-
tions and of branches, and all officers and mem-
bers of the Primary who can, should be in at-
tendance of hear the instructions. Sister Aurelia
S. Rogers of Farmington is expected to deliver
an address on Primary organization, its origin
and what it has accomplished.
	The General semi-annual Conference of the
Relief Society will be held in the Assembly Hall
in this City October 7, commencing at 10 a. m. 
Officers and members of the organization from all
the several Stakes of Zion are most cordially in-
vited to attend the morning session as there will
be but the one meeting, the Y. L. M. I. A. hold
ing their Conference the same day.
	ZINA D. H. YOUNG, President.
	JANE S. RICHARDS,
	BATHSHEBA W. SMITH,
	Counselors.
	The First General Conference of the Young
Ladies Mutual Improvement Association will be
held in the Assembly Hall in this City, October 7,
meetings commencing at 2 o'clock p. m. and 7:30,
in the evening. It is desirable that there be rep-
resenttatives present from all the Stakes of Zion
and a general invitation is hereby cordially ex-
tended to the young people and to all interested
in the work of the Association.
	ELMINA S. TAYLOR, President.
	ANNIE M. CANNON, Ass't. Sec
