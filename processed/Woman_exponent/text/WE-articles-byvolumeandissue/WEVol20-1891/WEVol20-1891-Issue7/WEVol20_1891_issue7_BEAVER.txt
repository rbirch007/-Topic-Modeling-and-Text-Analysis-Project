BEAVER.
Editor Exponent:

   At the last quarterly Conference of this
Stake Apostle Lyman united the two wards
of Beaver City into one.  This necessitated a 
change in the organization which had been
divided. In a general meeting of the sisters
called for this purpose by Bishop Mumford, the
two branches of the R. S. of Beaver ward were 
consolidated and Sister Ruth Tyler sus-
tained as President with M. A. White and
Jane Bickley as Counselors, and Alice M. J. 
Tanner, Secretary.
   The last quarterly conference of the Relief
Society held Sep. 4th, was a very enjoyable
one although but few attended.
   There were present of the brethren C. D. 
White, Pres. of Beaver Stake, Geo. Mumford
Bishop of Beaver ward, and Patriarch Daniel
Tyler. 
   Our Stake President, Sister Lucinda Howd


<column 1>
﻿thought that on account of her age and in-
firmities it would be for the best interests of
the Society for her to resign which she accord-
ingly did. The sisters, however, could not
feel to give her up and after a spirited discus-
sion showing the long and faithful labors of
Sister Howd she was unanimously sustained
as President of the R. S. of Beaver Stake,
with full permission to throw the most of her
labors upon her Counselors which are not yet
chosen.
	The reports were encouraging showing that
notwithstanding the dull times, and general
slackness of the people, the sisters have kept
in line with their duties remarkably well,
caring for, and visiting the sick and poor.
	Much good instruction was given with re-
gard to our duties, raising our children, stor-
ing grain, sustaining the EXPONENT etc.
	Conference adjourned for three months.
Singing, benediction by Patriarch Daniel
Tyler. 
			Louisa Jones, Stake Sec.
