RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.

	To the memory of Mary Ostler Ockey, who departed
this life, Sept. 9th, 1891, of typhoid fever. She was born
Sept. 28th, 1849, in Bridgeford Dorset, England. She
was the daughter of John and Sarah Ostler, and the wife
of Charles B. Ockey and the mother of thirteen children,
ten of whom are living. Deceased was an active member
of Relief Society holding the office of teacher at the time
of her death.
	Whereas it has pleased our Heavenly Father to call
from our midst by the hand of death our beloved sister
and friend; therefore be it.
	Resolved, that we recognize in her death the loss of a
true friend and co-laborer in the Second Ward Relief 
Society of Nephi
	Resolved, that we the members of the Relief Society of
Nephi, tender our sympathy to the bereaved husband and
children who are deprived of the care and counsel of their
beloved mother.
	Resolved, that a copy of these resolutions be sent to the 
family, also published in the Ensign and WOMAN'S EX-
PONENT, and that the same be placed upon the records of 
this Society.
"Weep not that her toils are over, weep not that her race
       is run;
God grant we may rest as calmly when our work like hers
       is done;
'Till then we would yield with gladness, our treasure to
       Him to keep,


And rejoice in the sweet assurance, He giveth His loved
        ones sleep.
					MARY PITCHFORTH,
					REBECCA UDALL,
									Committee.
					EDITH CLAYTON, Ass't. Sec.
