EDITOR EXPONENT:
	We have been advised by Pres. Teasdale
to send you a report of the organization of
our Relief Society, and also a Statistical and
Financial Report. We were organized by
Bishop William Derby Johnson Jr. on the
5th of January 1888, with six officers and
twenty-four members. This was in the early
days of this settlement, when we were living


in tents and tabernacles like Abraham Isaac
and Jacob of old. Now our Society has
increased in numbers and we have been
steadily growing in experience, and love of
our pleasing labors, that have brought to
us so much benefit and profit. We number
now, officers and members, seventy-seven, and
our average attendance is forty. We have
most excellent meetings twice a month. We
meet on the afternoon of every Fast Day, the
first Thursday in every month, for testimony,
and are generally favored with the presence
of some of our leading brethren, who have
always encouraged us in our duties and
labors of love. Then once a month we hold a
work meeting. These meetings are well at-
tended by our earnest faithful sisters and are
exceedingly interesting. Sister Lucy Johnson 
is our President, with Sister Anna Jorgensen
as her first, and Verona Whiting as her
second Counselor; Sister Ada A. Earl Secre-
tary and Mary J. Johnson Assistant Secretary
Emma Barker Treasurer. We have seventeen
Teachers. Our report dates from our
organization, in the future we shall send semi-
annual reports. With kindest regards your
Sister.
