PERSONALS.

	The Say Diego Union, and Santa Paula
Chronicle, are using an ink made of crude
petroleum and find it very satisfactory.
	The New York Journalist gave more than a
column to news of the P. C. W. P. A. re-
cently.
	Mrs. M. O. Stanton of the P. C. W. P. A.,
has the honor of being the first lady to lecture
before the Faculty and students of the Toland
College, San Francisco; she having lately de-
livered three lectures there on "Phreno-
Physiognomy." An invitation has also been
received by the same gifted lady to give a
course at Jepherson College, Philadelphia.
	Mrs. Kate Douglas Wiggin of the P. C. W.
P. A., who is spending her vacation in a Maine
farmhouse, has completed a new work for girls
entitled "Polly Oliver's Problem, which may
be called the sequel of "A Summer in a
Canyon," and which like the latter has its
scene laid in California.
