THE WOMAN’S STORE.

	The Woman’s Cooperative Mercantile Institu-
tion established last January, has recently been
moved to 63 E. First South Street, three doors
west of the theatre. The location is much more
desirable than the former one and the announce-
ment made to the general public ought to bring
custom to the establishment and success to the
enterprise. The ladies keep on hand an ex-
cellent quality of goods, particularly in the line of
millinery. Feathers, ribbons, laces and various
kinds of hat trimmings, in fact a full line of
millinery goods, direct from one of the leading
houses in Chicago. A bright young lady ex-
perienced in the business and formerly employed
in the fashionable store of Madame Button has
charge of this department of work. The dress-
making department is under the supervision of a
young woman who has had great experience in
this direction and is thoroughly competent to un-
dertake the most stylish and fashionable work.
The Children’s Book by Augusta Joyce Croche-
ron, is also for sale at this place.

	This store keeps on hand Ladies’ Equipoise
Waists and Jenness Miller’s Underwear. All
sorts of wax flower materials and Ladies Fancy
and Furnishing notions. Stamping and Pinking,
Buttons covered and Feathers cleaned etc. Burial
suits kept on hand or made to order if preferred.
	There is still some Capital Stock to dispose of
and ladies are invited to take shares and co-
operate for mutual benefit.
	If we would succeed in any home enterprise
we should give it our patronage and seek to
secure that of our friends and interest them in
the good cause. The Woman’s Cooperative
Store fills an important place and fits in admirably
with the organizations of women that are so per-
fect and complete with us as a people. It is an
undertaking that should meet with the hearty
support of women who are looking to home in-
terests,and home industries that will give suitable
employment to those who possess ingenuity and
skill of various kinds.
