NOTES AND NEWS.

	The Ft. Scott (Kansas) E. S. A. will
present the Kansas Historical Pageant in that
city about the middle of October.
	The Trades and Labor Council of the
Dominion of Canada, at its meeting in Quebec
on Sept. 3, passed a resolution asking for the
appointment of women as factory inspectors.
	There are 108 ladies now serving as Poor
Law Guardians in England and Scotland,
and the number of women elected to this
position increases every year.
	Mrs. Julia Fillmore Harris, sister of Presi-
dent Fillmore, who lately died in San
Francisco at the age of seventy-nine; was a
woman of unusual ability. She fulfilled her
duties at the White House during her
brother's administration with courtesy and
kindness, and it is said that the president often
applied to her for advice on public questions.
She believed in suffrage for women, and was
an active worker with her pen in that cause.
	The American Social Science Association,
at its meeting last week in Saratoga, N. Y.,
elected two women among its vice presidents
for the coming year, Dr. Grace Peckham of
New York, and Mrs. John E. Dodge, of
Boston. Among the papers read was one by
Dr. Frances Emily White, of the Women's
Medical College of Philadelphia, on "Spiritual-
ism and Hypnotism," arguing that these
phenomena could generally be accounted for
by purely physical causes.
