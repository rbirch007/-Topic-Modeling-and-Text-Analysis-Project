THE MOTHER AUTHOR.

Beside the bed of her sick child she watches,
	With heavy, anxious eye;
While Time's cold chisel on his tablet notches,
	The hour of midnight, passing grimly by.

Low, bending, o'er the cherished form she measures,
	The hard-drawn, labored breath;
Sensing how near her darling is to pleasures,
	Across the narrow river, bridged by death.

But the morning breaks with a radiance bright,
And the mother's spirit is glad and light;
Broken the night's dark fetter,
Unloosed the icy spell.
For the father of her child hath said,
With hands placed on the precious head,
"We thank Thee, our boy is better,
And, that he will get well."

Oh! blessed boon, and best to mortals given,
	Who have for faith and strength and patience striven,
That they may seek the Lord in fervent prayer,
	And find Him willing all their griefs to bear.

The noon-day finds the mother busier yet,
Her house in working order to reset.
But in her heart and  brain there softly clings,
A bird-like something which forever sings.
A rhythm or a chant, half prayer, half song.
Hopeful and grateful, earnest, sweet and strong.
No time today, to grant expression power,
Too full of other things the present hour;
But there will come a time in future when,
Her girls to women grown, her boys grown men,
Will ask and will receive life pictures from her pen.
							L. L. G. RICHARDS.
Nov. 30, 1891.
