	CASSIA STAKE.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The Relief Society of Cassia Stake con-
vened in Conference at Elba, June the 19th,
1891.
	There were present a full attendance of the
Stake officers, representatives from all the
wards except one. Stake President H. D.
Haight, first Counselor and leading
brethren; also a large congregation.
	Minutes of two preceding conferences were
read; also statistical and financial reports
showing good work.
	Remarks from Prest. Louisa Haight were
followed by favorable verbal reports from the
various wards, showing how the majority of
the members endeavored against many disad-
vantages to keep up the spirit of their
duties.
	Counselors E. Tuttle and Ann Wilson each
addressed the conference in a forcible and in-
structive manner.
	During the afternoon session, Prest. H. D.
Haight and Counselor Moroni Pickett spoke
at some length upon the sisters duties and
wherein the brethren could and should render
them assistance in their labors.
	Remarks also by Sisters Priscilla Worthing-
ton and Louisa Read touching upon
the elevation of the young; dealing with traits
of character, self-education and other top-
ics.
	Prest. L. Haight extended to the brethren
a standing invitation to attend the conferences
of the Sisters.


General and Stake authorities were sus-
tained by the conference.
	The Elba choir rendered sweet music; every
one seemed blessed with a righteous and
friendly spirit.
	Conference adjourned to meet at Oakley in
three months.
