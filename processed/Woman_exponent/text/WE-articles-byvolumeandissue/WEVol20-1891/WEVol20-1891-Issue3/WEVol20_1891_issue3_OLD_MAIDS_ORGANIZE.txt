OLD MAIDS ORGANIZE.

	The young women of of Woodhaven, N. Y.
have organized an "Old Maids' Consolation
Association." They aver that they have been
neglected by the young men, and now propose
to retaliate. Not to be outdone, several young
men met and organized an "Old Bachelors'
Sympathetic and Consolation Society." Pre-
amble and resolutions were adopted as fol-
lows:
	WHEREAS. It having become known
that the young women of this city have orga-
nized an "Old Maids' Consolation Association"
setting up as a primary cause "the inattention
of the young men," and announcing their de-
termination to exclude from their society the
male offenders, and fearing that if such plan
were carried into effect it might cause too
great a devotion to the historic black cat and
strong tea; therefore
	Resolved, That we, as representative young
men of the community, deploring their hasty
action in assigning themselves so prematurely
to old maidenhood, earnestly request them to
reconsider their action. At the same time we
extend our heartfelt sympathy and penitently
promise to atone for past neglect.
	Resolved, That we appoint a committee to
attend the next meeting of the "Old Maids'
Consolation Association," and do all in their
power to effect a consolidation of the two socie-
ties under the title of the "Mutual Consolation
Association."
	After singing the hymn "Come ye Disconso-
late," the meeting adjourned, subject to the
call of the chair.—Ex.
