	THE FULFILLMENT OF A PROPHECY.

	Yesterday while reading, Section 130,
Doctrine and Covenants, I felt strongly im-
pressed with the idea that this prophecy was
fulfilled in the year 1890.
	It reads thus: "Joseph my son, if thou livest
until thou art 85 years old, thou shalt see the
face and the Son of Man; therefore let this suf-
fice, and trouble me no more on this matter."
In another verse, "I was left thus without be-
ing able to decide, whether this coming re-
ferred to the beginning of the Millennium or to
some previous appearing, or whether I should
die and thus see His face. I do not think He
will come before that time."
	I should say however, in another previous
verse it reads, "I was once praying very
earnestly to kno the time of the coming of
the Son of Man, when I heard a voice repeat
the following," as written above. This re-
minded me of a text from which I heard a
sermon preached in Utica N. Y. It was this:
	"Surely the Lord God will do nothing but
He revealeth His secret unto His servants the
prophets." He took this text from the Bible
Amos 3rd chap. 7 verse. I feel satisfied how-
ever that this has been, and will be the case
when there are people on the earth who have
this gift. And you know what St. Paul said:
'Seek earnestly the best gifts, but rather that
you prophesy."
	St. John the Revelator tells us that the testi-
mony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy. Ac-
cordingly those who will apply their minds to
the study of those principles, and live in ac-
cordance with the same, with humility of heart
can obtain this precious gift for their own bene-
fit, if no more.
