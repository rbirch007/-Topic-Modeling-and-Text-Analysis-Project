Earnest demonstrations made patriotism grow.
Every human creature the oldest to the babe,
Drank its inspiration, helped to swell the grand parade,
Then the cannonading, and. beacon fires glow,
Roused the whole creation, one hundred years ago.
"Our Fathers where are they?" who struggled on for years
Toiling for a country, 'mid blood and sweat and tears,
Sustained the declaration, only fought for right,
For God, for home and freedom, united in their might,
Firmly laid foundation, that liberty might grow,
Smile not now, in thinking their progress very slow
Of their sowing and reaping, sickle scythe, and flail,
While fleeing through the country; riding on a rail
Wondrons things have happened one hundred years now
	gone,
Quarrels and dissensions on Uncle Samuel’s farm.
Now the sheller and the reaper play an active part,
Then neither would been known from Noah's ancient ark,
Lightning speeding messages sent around the globe,
Great countries unknown then now opened and explored.
Steam power perfected and kingdoms overthrown;
Yet one thing is certain the world has not outgrown, 
Since the pair turned from Eden to toil, till and plan;
Old time has never faltered in reproducing man.
Still human nature lives tho' fashions come and go,
Query! has it improved, since one hundred years ago?
Roll down the magic curtain, we'll view the scene apart'
The question is before you; answer from the heart.
	L.M. Hewlings
	Atchinson, Kans.
