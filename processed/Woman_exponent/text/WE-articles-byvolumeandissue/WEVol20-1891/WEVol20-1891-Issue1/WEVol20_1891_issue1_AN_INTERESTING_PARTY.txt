AN INTERESTING PARTY.

	Thursday evening June 4, a very pleasant
party of ladies and gentlemen gathered at the
hospitable residence of Mr. and Mrs. J. W.
Eardley.  The affair was in the hands of a
committee who must have been very efficient
to make it such a complete success.  The en-
tertainment was of a most interesting character
throughout; refreshments of various kinds of
delicacies were tastefully served; conversation
brilliant and humorous; and later on vaired by
music and recitations; the singing of Mrs. Lizzie
Felt awakened tenderest emotions, her voice is
rich, clear and sympathetic.  Mrs.  Susie
Talmage recited "Only a Girl" most feelingly,
she has a sweet voice and pleasing address.
Mrs. Nellie Colebrook well and favorably
known on the Salt Lake stage, gave some
charming renditions of favorite poems, show-
ing well her dramatic power.  Brief speeches
on the present political situation etc., were
made by Apostle John W. Taylor, Counselor
Joseph E. Taylor, Elder B. H. Roberts and
Dr. C. F. Wilcox.  Altogether it was a charm-
ing entertainment and a credit to the Com-
mittee, of whom we believe Dr.  Maggie C.
Shipp was the able Chairman.  Among the
guests present were Bishop Samuel Woolley
and wife, Mrs. Bathsheba W. Smith, D. E.
S. Barney, Rodney Badger and wife, and
others, perhaps fifty in all.	
						E. B. W.
