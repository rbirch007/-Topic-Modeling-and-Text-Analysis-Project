RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.
	To the memory of our esteemed sister, Elizabeth Sabin,
by the sisters of the Relief Society of Payson Ward.
	She was born May 31, 1811, was baptized in the year
1842, emigrated to Utah 1850, and died Aug. 16, 1891.
Aged eighty years, two months and fifteen days.
	She was an active member of our Society, and untiring
in attending and blessing the sick, an affectionate wife and
kind mother, and,
	Whereas, our Heavenly Father has seen fit to call away
our beloved sister,
	Be it resolved that we esteem her goodness and faith-
fulness
	Resolved that we extend our sympathy to the family of
the deceased.
	Resolved that these resolutions be entered on the record
of the Society, also a copy sent to the bereaved family, and
printed in the WOMAN'S EXPONENT.
			M. A. HARDY,
			ANN PICKERING,
			ARTEMISIA WHITE,
				Committee.

	To the memory of Maria M. Jenson. Deceased was
born March 12, 1855, emigrated to Utah in the year 1872;
died in Salt Lake City, the 11th day of August 1891, leav-
ing a husband and six children, with a host of friends to
mourn her loss.
		IT IS NOT DEATH BUT SLEEP.
	Sister Maria Jenson has been a member of the Relief
Society of the Second Ward of this City since the year
1884; and has labored faithfully as a teacher of the same
for a number of years, and we deeply mourn the loss of a
loving sister, a kind and tender mother, and a true wife
and Latter-day Saint; always gentle and kind ever striving
to make peace and good-will among all with whom she was
called to unite with, and we feel to value her memory ever
among the sisters of the Relief Society of the Second
Ward, of S. L. City, and to say may God bless her hus-
band and family who are thus bereft of her society and may
we feel that all is well with the loved one who has gone,
and perhaps she  may be a stronger link in the chain that
binds us to our Father in heaven.
