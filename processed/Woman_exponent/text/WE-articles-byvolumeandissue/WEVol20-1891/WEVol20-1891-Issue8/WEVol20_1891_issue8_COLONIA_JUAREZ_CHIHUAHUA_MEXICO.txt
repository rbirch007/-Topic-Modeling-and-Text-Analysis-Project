	COLONIA, JUAREZ, CHIHUAHUA, MEXICO.
EDITOR EXPONENT:
	The Juarez Relief Society of the Mexican
Mission was first organized July 13th, 1887,
with the following officers, Elizabeth Hawkins
President, with Sarah A Corden first and
Phoebe Fife second Counselors; Hannah Croft
Sec., Selina Cordon Treasurer—60 members
enrolled. It was reorganized July 10, 1890,
with Phoebe Fife president, Mary B. Eyring
first and Susan Martineau, second Counselor
D. E. Eyring, Secretary, Keturah Baker,
Treasurer, with 73 members. We have held
some 50 meetings, average attendance 30. Our
finances or requirements have not been very
large. Cash donations $17.35; provisions and
merchandise $95.80. Disbursed cash $15.25;
merchandise and provisions $85.80. Cash on
hand $2.10, merchandise $10.00.
	The Society in the first years of its organi-
tion labored under great disadvantages. There
was not much aid needed, until we had a few
cases of small pox, when the Saints being
called upon donated $68.00 to the needy. Our
prospects as a Society are improving and we
trust in the near future to be able to make a
better report. The organization at present is
as above, with the exception of S. E. Russell
acting Secretary, and Cynthia Bailey Treas-
urer.
	With kindest regards, your sisters,
			MARY B. EYRING,
