DEATH OF JANE M. WALTON.
RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT.
	Resolutions of respect to the memory of Sister Jane M.
Walton, who was suddenly deprived of life in a most sad
and shocking manner by being accidently shot while
attending a social party on the 24th of July at Monticello
where she resided.


Sister Walton was born the 16 of July 1846 making her
45 years and 9 days old. She was one of the Pioneers of
the San Juan Mission being one of the first who landed
here in 1880. She lived in Bluff about 8 years when she
with her husband was called to help build up Monticello.
A husband and three grown up children are left to mourn
her loss.
	Sister Walton was President of the Bluff Ward Relief
Society as long as she lived there, and was also Stake
President of the Relief society from the time the Stake was
organized till the time of her death.
	She was an earnest and faithful worker wherever duty
called her, and was universally loved and respected where
she was known.
	Whereas:—Our Heavenly Father in His infinite wisdom
has suffered to be removed from our midst our beloved
Sister Jane M. Walton. Her faithful and zealous labors
as President of the Relief Society of the San Juan Stake of
Zion, also the great interest she always manifested for
the young, and as a teacher in the Sabbath School, both
by her presence and wise counsel, make it fitting that we
record our appreciation of her labors.
	Therefore:—be it resolved that we the officers and
members of the Relief Society of the San Juan Stake do
mourn her loss, realizing we have lost a noble and
benevolent Sister, her heart being willing and her hand
 ready to help the poor and comfort the sick.
	Resolved:—that a copy of these resolutions be placed on
the records of the Relief Society of the San Juan Stake
and that a copy be sent to the bereaved family and to the
WOMAN'S EXPONENT.
	MARTHA HAMMIND,
	JOSEPHINE WOOD,
	ELIZA A. REDD,
Committee.
Bluff, San Jaun Co. Utah, Aug. 6, 1891.
