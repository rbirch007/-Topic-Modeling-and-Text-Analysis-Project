AMERICAN FORK W. S. A.
July 31st was our monthly meeting we had
a very entertaining and instructive programme.
Father Spratly gave a speech on political
questions which was very instructive, though
the old gentlemen is over 80 years of age his
voice was as clear as a bell. Millie B. Garff
sang a song entitled “The Maid of the Mill”
Ray Robison gave a recitation entitled “The
Smitten Rock.” Misses Lee and Mc’Neil
sang a song entitled “The Old Tree in the
Lane.” Emily Keeperieck, read a poem “The
Woman’s Flag.” Instrumental music Ida Chip-
man.
We also displayed our new flag at meeting,
which is twelve feet long with a single star,
about in the center; golden, for Wyoming.
We intend to make each star golden as each
state grants the right of suffrage to women.
We also gave a ball and strawberry festival
from the proceeds we gave our home band
forty dollars to encourage home talent.
It was resolved by the band committee that
resolutions be passed, thanking us for our gift,
but for some reason it did not appear in our
weekly paper the Independent.
