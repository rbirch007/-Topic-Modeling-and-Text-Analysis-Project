SUNDAY SCHOOL SONG.

[Written for the South Bountiful Central Sunday
School.]
Ye children saints of Latter-days,
Assemble at the Throne,
And make your songs of joy and praise,
For mercies God has shown.
For heritage in this fair land,
The choicest spot on earth;
Where God led forth His chosen band,
And proved the Gospel’s worth.

Here desolation long had reigned .
O’er barren wastes and snows,
But God the desert has reclaimed
 blossoms as the rose;
And while astray from holy lands,
No hope the red man knew,
The Book of Mormon brought him joy 
And shaped his life anew.

And here to Him in Sabbath hour,
In gratitude we bend,
Oh may His Spirit’s quickening power,
Our listening hearts attend.
Help us to know Thy holy will,
And faithful servants be;
That rising from this life we’ll find
Our selves at home with Thee.
	Augusta Joyce Crocheron
