The death of Prince Albert Victor, eldest
son of the Prince of Wales, who was the
immediate heir to the throne, has softened
every heart towards the bereaved father.
Prince George Frederic, "Sailor Prince," now
becomes heir in the regular line of succession.
Other deaths of world wide interest are those
of M. Quatrefages de Breau, the well known
Director of the Museum of Natural History
in Paris; Professor Kuenen, the Biblical
scholar, at Leyden; and Charles A. White of
Boston, writer of popular songs and ballads.
A day later the death of Cardinal Manning is
announced, who, leaving the Church of 
England, became a convert to Roman
Catholicism in 1857. He was noted not only
as a writer of ecclesiastical books, but also for
his work in the cause of social and temperance
reform. On the same day, in Rome, Cardinal
Simeon, formerly Papal Secretary of State
died from La Grippe.
