MRS. E. B. WELLS,
		DEAR SISTERS:
	It is with pleasure that I improve this oppor-
tunity of penning a few lines to you. My hopes
and good wishes are that you have enjoyed a very
merry Christmas and happy New Year.
	Christmas passed very pleasantly here a
Christmas tree in our public Hall, loaded with
presents, and distributed by old St. Nicholas him-
self, made the hearts of the children glad, with a
dance for them in the afternoon.
	I myself passed a very agreeable Chrismas
with my family gathered around me, I also had
Pres. Robson and his family with some other
friends. My son Wm. married C. I. R's. daughter
and my eldest son Talmas married a daughter of
B. F. Johnson, formerly of Spring-Lake Villa. At
such a gathering an absent one is miSsed very
much, so I missed Zula, wife of S. F. Kimball.
	Ever having a desire for your happiness and
the success of our little paper, which is such an
able advocate of the rights of women, I will ever
remainlts well wisher.
	Yours with much love.
