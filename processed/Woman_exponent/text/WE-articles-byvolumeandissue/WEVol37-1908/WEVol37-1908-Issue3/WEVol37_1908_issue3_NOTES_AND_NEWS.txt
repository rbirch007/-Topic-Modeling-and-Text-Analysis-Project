NOTES AND NEWS.
	One of the results of the extension of the
franchise to women in Norway is that
henceforward women employed in the postal
service are to receive the same pay as men.
Norwegian women are greatly rejoicing over
this victory.
	Mrs. Zella Nuttall, who has received
many honors from American and foreign
scientific associations for her explorations
and archaelogical researches, is now in Mex-
ico engaged as field director of the Reid-
Crocker Mission, which is excavating the
Pyramids of the sun and the moon at
Teothuacan. This work is carried on large-
ly through funds furnished by two other
American women—Mrs. Whitelaw Reid and
Mrs. Crocker of San Francisco.
	Mrs. Emily P. Collins was 94 years young
on August 11. She received congratulations
at her home, 60 Newbury avenue, Atlantic,
Mass., where she lives with her grandchil-
dren. Mrs. Collins organized the earliest
womansuffrage association in the world, in
the Western part of New York State. In
the same year she sent to the Legislature
what she believes to have been the first
woman suffrage petition ever sent to the
Legislature of any State. It bore the sig-
natures of sixty of the more prominent men
and women in Bristol, New York, with a
few from neighboring towns.		Exp.
