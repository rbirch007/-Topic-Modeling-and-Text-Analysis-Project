IMPORTANT NOTICE.
	The Presiding Bishopric of the Church
have called upon the General Relief Society
to use its influence in securing a few more
pupil students of the L. D. S. College to
take the two years’ nurse course at the Dr.
W. H Groves Latter-day Saints Hospital.
	The Hospital has a training school for
nurses, and efforts are being made to affili-
ate this training school with the General
Church School System. A course of train-
ing is given at said Hospital covering a per-
iod of three years, and efforts are being
made to reduce it to two years in the near
future. The sehool year commences Sep-
tember 1st, 1908, and there is still eight
more pupil nurses needed to make the re-
quired number.
	The Relief Society desires to give this re-
quest publicity through the columns of the
Woman’s Exponent and letters will be sent
giving fuller particulars to Presidents of
Stakes, that they may understand the situ-
ation more in detail.
	This Hospital training will not in any way
interfere with the Relief Society nurse class
taught under the auspices of the General
Relief Society by Dr. M. C. Roberts, which
will commence later in September as adver-
tised in this nnmber of the paper, and is
especially in the interest of charity as its
first object.
	More concerning this subject will be pub-
lished in the next issue of the paper.
