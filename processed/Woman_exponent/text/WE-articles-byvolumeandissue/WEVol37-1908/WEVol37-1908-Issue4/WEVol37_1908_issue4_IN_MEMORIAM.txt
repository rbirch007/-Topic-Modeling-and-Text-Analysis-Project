IN MEMORIAM.
	Mrs. Elizabeth Hill Maughan, wife of the late
Bishop William H. Maughan, died at her home in
Wellsville, on October 3, 1908, after an illness of
about a year. She was born February 14, 1838,
in Upper Toronto, Canada. She immigrated to
Utah in 1852, and came to Wellsvile in 1856, where
she has resided ever since. She endured all the
hardships of pioneer life, she was the mother of
ten children, nine of whom survive her,, six sons
and three daughters, also fifty-four grandchildren
and seven great grandchildren.
		RESOLUTIONS OF RESPECT,
	Whereas our Father in heaven has in his infi-
nite wisdom removed from among us our worthy
and dearly loved sister, and
	Whereas the long and intimate relation with her
in the faithful discharge of her duty as a counsel-
or in the Society makes it fitting that we record our
appreciation. Wherefore
	Resolved that the wisdom and ability which she
exercised in our midst by service and counsel
will he held in grateful remembrance.
	Resolved that we sincerely mourn her depar-
ture from us, but appreciate her past faithful
labors and know that she has gone to receive the
reward for labors well performed. She held the
position of first counselor to Sister Elizabeth Sal-
isbury for the period of 18 years in the Wellsville
Relief Society, which duty she performed in an
energetic and exemplary manner and won the
love and respect of those with whom she associ-
ated in that capacity. 
	Resolved that we place these resolutions on our
records, present a copy to the family, and also
send one to the Woman’s Exponent for publica-
tion.
	Sarah A. Parker, Pres.; Elizabeth Salisbury,
Elizabeth Owens, Jerusha B. Maughan, Agnes
Allan, Mary J. Parker, Sec.
