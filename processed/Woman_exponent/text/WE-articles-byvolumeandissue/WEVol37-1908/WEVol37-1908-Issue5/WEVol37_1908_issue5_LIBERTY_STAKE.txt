		LIBERTY STAKE. 
	Liberty Stake Relief Society conference
was held in the 9th ward meeting house,
December 10th, 1908, President Annie S.
Musser presiding. After the opening exer-
cises, President Musser feelingly greeted the
sisters and spoke words of welcome, also
felt grateful for the many blessings she en-
joyed.
	Reports were made by the presidents of
the 9th, 31st and 33rd wards, all were in
the line of their duty, and performing a
good work.
	President Annie Wells Cannon of the
Pioneer Stake Relief Society, and a member
of the General Board, gave, by request-
a sketch of the life of her mother,
Emmeline B. Wells, the General Sec-
retary of the Relief Society, which
was very interesting and much enjoyed.
	A duet was rendered by Sisters Bailey and
Kirkman. After benediction by Sister
Blanche Earl, adjourned until 2 o’clock.
	Conference reconvened at 2 o’clock. After
the preliminary exercises, Sister Jennie
Lloyd gave a lecture on “The Training of
the Child in the Home,” which was very
instructive and helpful to mothers.
	Sister Strong rendered the solo entitled,
“Jesus Lover of my Soul.”
	Sister Julia P. M. Farnsworth, of the
General Board, felt proud of being a mem-
ber of the Relief Society and gave some
timely instructions to the sisters.
	Counselor Zurviah G. Eardley gave in-
structions to presidents in regard to looking
after the poor and infirm. Elder C. W.
Symons, of the High Council, made a few
timely remarks, and encouraged the sisters
to continue in the good work, and urged the
mothers to watch and guard their boys and
girls.
	President Musser made a few closing
remarks, thanking all who had helped to
make the conference a success. After sing-
ing, and benediction, conference adjourned
for six months.
	A delicious lunch was served, between the
meetings to all present.
Lizzie Robbins, Secretary.
