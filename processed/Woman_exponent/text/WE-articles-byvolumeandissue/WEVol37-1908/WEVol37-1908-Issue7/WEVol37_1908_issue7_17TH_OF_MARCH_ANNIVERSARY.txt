17th OF MARCH ANNIVERSARY.
	The seventeenth of March was a bright
and sunny day, in our Dixie land. The Re-
lief Society of the East and West St. George
wards assembled at 3 o’clock in the Lyceum
to celebrate the day, on which, 67 years ago
the work was begun, which has since played
so important apart in the lives of the women
of Zion.
	The meeting was presided over by Presi-
dent Esther Whitehead of the West St.
George ward.
	After the opening exercises Sister Ann C.
Woodbury gave a short talk on the early or-
ganizations of the society, after which Stake


Counselor Aggie Windsor sang, “Daughters
of Zion,” composed by Sister Annie Wells
Cannon. Two pieces were read giving inter-
esting items on the first organization and
short talks were given by Sisters Emma
Morris and Stake Counselor Elida Snow,
interspersed with some very sweet songs.
	A good spirit prevailed and the sisters felt
encouraged to press forward with renewed
energy in the good cause, for it certainly is
a glorious work in which we are engaged.
	The only thing to be regreted was the ab-
sence of the Stake President Cornelia Brooks,
who was unable to attend on account of
sickness.		LEONORA WORTHEN, Sec.
