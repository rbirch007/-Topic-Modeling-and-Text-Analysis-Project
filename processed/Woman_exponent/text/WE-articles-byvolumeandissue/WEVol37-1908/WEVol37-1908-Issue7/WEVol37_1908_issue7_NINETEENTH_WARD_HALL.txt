NINETEENTH WARD HALL.
	The Relief Society of the 19th wa[-]d of the
Salt Lake stake of Zion, completed a hall
for their use for meetings, also for sewing,
and socials, etc., on February 1st, 1909.
	The building contains a front rooms 20x20
feet, and a kitchen, pantry, toilet, coal bin,
tables and cupboards, all under one roof.
	The building cost, including plumbing,
wireing and fixtures ($2,031.25) two thou-
sand thirty-one dollars and twenty-five cents.
The Society still owe about $600.00.
	At the opening, on February 25th, 1909,
72 people called in the afternoon, 107 in the
evening and 75 children on the following
afternoon. A program was rendered and re-
freshments were served.
	Among those present were three members
of the High Council, also Sisters H. B. Harker,
C. S. Williams, E. B. Wells, the Bishop and
Counselors, Pres, of Young Ladies Associa-
tion, Pres. Primary Association, Pres. Re-
ligion Class, and nearly every organization
of the ward was represented.
	It was a very enjoyable time, exercises
well rendered and good music.
				AGNES G. BRANCH,
							Secretary.
