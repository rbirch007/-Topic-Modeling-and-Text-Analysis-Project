GRANDMOTHER’S FAN.
Grandmother’s fan I shall take today
Thus in my hand to the matinee—
Grandmother’s fan and my pink bouquet.

Graudmother’s fan—such a beauty! See!
Hand-painted satin and ivory!
Grandmother’s fan—and ’twill dance with me!

Daintiest, surely, of dainty things!
Look at the rose-wreaths and cupids’ wings!
Sweetest of odors around it clings.

Grandmother dear, with her girlish grace,
Once ’neath this fan hid her winsome face—
Once when the world was a diff’rent place!

Once when with grandfather first she met,
Daintily treading a minuet—
Grandmother’s fan may remember yet!

Oh she was gentle and blithe and true—
Grandmother dear, past whose cheeks’ soft hue
Fluttered this fan—and her secrets knew!

Let me be like her, if so I may—
who am taking her fan today
Thus in my hand to the matinee!
