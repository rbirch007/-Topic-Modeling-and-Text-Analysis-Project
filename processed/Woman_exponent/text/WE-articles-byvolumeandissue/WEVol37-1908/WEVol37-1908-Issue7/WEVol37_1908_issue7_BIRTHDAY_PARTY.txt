BIRTHDAY PARTY.
	At the handsome residence of Mr. and
Mrs. Ovando Beebe, Wednesday, March 24,
1909, a delightful afternoon party was given
in honor of Mrs. Beebe’s mother Sister Em-
ma S. Woodruff, whose birthday occurs
March 1st, but as that is also the anniver-
sary of the late President Wilford Woodruff’s
birthday and is generally celebrated by all
his numerous posterity, it is only fitting that
Sister Emma should have a select party of
her own intimate friends. It was the real
birthday of Sister Amelia McDonald and she
was a guest of honor with sister Woodruff.
After an hour or two spent in greeting and
congratulations the guests were called to the
spacious dining room, where two long tables
were set with elegant china service and cut
glass, each table for twenty persons;
beautiful flowers in profusion shed sweet
fragrance, but best of all were the dear faces
of old-time friends, and the hum of happy
voices made time fly swiftly. At each plate
a card with name and motto.
	Sisters Woodruff and McDonald were
seated side by side at the head of one of the
tables, and it is needless to add that they
were beautifully dressed.
	The menu was perfect in all its details,
and served by charming young girls which
added zest to the enjoyment of the delicious
repast. Toasts were given by some of the
ladies present to which the two guests of
honor responded in a few choice words. In
fact the affair might well be termed a festive
occasion, and one long to be remembered
for its good cheer and harmony of fellow-
ship.
	These social gatherings are essentially
restful to women who lead busy lives. Mrs.
Beebe is to be congratulated on the brilliant
success of the entertainment, and certainly
the ladies will agree that she is a charming
hostess.
