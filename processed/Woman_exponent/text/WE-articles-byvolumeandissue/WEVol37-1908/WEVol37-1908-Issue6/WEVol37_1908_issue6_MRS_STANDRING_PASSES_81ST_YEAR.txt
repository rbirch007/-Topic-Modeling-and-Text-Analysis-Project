MRS. STANDRING PASSES 81st YEAR.

	Saturday, February 20th, 1909, being the
81st birthday of Mrs. Rebecca Standring,
Stake President of the Relief Society of
the Alpine Stake, it was decided by the four
wards of Lehi, that they would honor the
lady by celebrating the day in a good old-
fashioned ball, luncheon and program.
	The ladies of the four wards, with their
husbands and the Stake officers of the soci-
ty met at the opera house at three p. m.
and listened to the following program which
was given by the four wards:
	Mrs. E. J. T. Roberts, master of cere-
monies.
	1. Singing by the congregation, “We
thank Thee, O God, for a prophet.” 2. In-
vocation—Mary Ann Webb. 3. “Oh, my
Father.” 4. Address of welcome—Kate
Allred. 5. Remarks—President R. Stand-
ring.
	6. Duet—Mrs. Lucy Whitman and Mrs.
Margaret Clark. 7. Narrative of a visit of
a stranger—M. A. Sorensen.
	8. Singing: “Count your Blessings.”
	9. Reading—M. J. Gray. 10. Remarks,
—Bishop Gardner, 2nd ward. 11. Vocal
solo—Mrs. Harris Hill.
	12. Presentation of a silk down quilt by
the members of the four wards of Lehi and
the Stake officers to Sister Standring, by
Sister Hindley of Am. Fork, counselor to
Mrs. Standring.
	13. Piano solo, Jennie Webb.
	14. Remarks by Mrs, Standring.
	15. Singing, “Spirit of God.”
	16. Benediction—Wm. Southwick.
	Luncheon was served iu the opera house
until 6,30 p. m. when the ball opened and
was enjpyed by the old and the young who
participated in the old time quadrilles and
other dances of by-gone days.
